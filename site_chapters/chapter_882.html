Chapter 882: Biological Program (4)

"We meet again."

Argones, who had been staring intently at the screen, sat in Pisho's chair and crossed his legs.

Although it was an event from a different time, Shirone felt as if he were right in front of him.

"Do you know me?"

So he asked, and surprisingly, he got an answer.

"Of course. I am Pisho."

The gelatinous substance of Argones quickly flowed over the surface and transformed into Pisho's body.

"I am also your father."

The skin turned liquid again and immediately changed into the face of Shirone's father, Vincent.

"I am also your woman."

This time, it was Amy.

Argones chuckled.

"Or was it this one?"

The faces of all the people Shirone had met flashed by quickly, as if the video were being rewound.

Shirone spoke.

"But I am not."

Therefore, it is not the truth.

As if he had heard, Argones returned to his original form and smiled.

"Hexa. A protocol outside the biological system. Indeed, you are beyond my control. But..."

Argones sprang from the chair, grabbed the video recording device, and leaned in.

"It doesn't mean I can't annihilate you."

Shirone's eyes widened.

In the still vibrating screen, Argones' face was rapidly pushing outwards.

"No."

Argones, who had seeped into the video recording device, was multiplying his cells and emerging again.

The synchronization was so perfect that it created the illusion of transcending time.

"Isn't that right?"

As Argones swung his arm like a whip, Shirone instantly moved.

When he looked up from the wall, Argones' arm had hardened like a blade.

"No need to be surprised. I was just checking what signals the Hexa protocol receives."

There was no way for Argones to escape the space sealed by the material barrier.

"Destroying the barrier of perception and expanding the unit of time. Quite unique for a human."

He seemed to have guessed that Shirone had already reversed time through temporal perception.

"...What do you want?"

Argones shook his head.

"I don't want anything. You know what I mean? An asteroid doesn't collide because it hates the planet. It just crashes. Living beings have their own inertia. Instinct."

Argones seeped into the floor and then emerged from the ceiling.

"All the desires of the biological world. The synthesis of those desires forms the world. My host seemed to understand that."

Pisho was an insect magician who studied the ecosystem in his mind.

"It was a useful conversation, but he chose heresy. The secrets of the world must not be revealed. They must be annihilated."

"By what right?"

"You still don't understand. Annihilation is also a desire of living beings. It's not about having the right. Cells self-destruct when the system is in danger. Pisho is the same."

"Apoptosis."

If Anke Ra prevents the collapse of the natural world, Argones seemed to prevent the collapse of the biological world.

"All the nature of living beings starts from me. And the further they get from me, the more the biological world collapses. Therefore, you, Nane, Havitz, and Miro are all dangerous elements."

It was surprising that Havitz, the embodiment of desire, was considered a dangerous element by Argones.

"When everything is unified, perspectives disappear. Extremes are dangerous. I don't know who will win, but it's better if no one wins. Only a complex world full of doubts will be safe for you."

"What if someone wins?"

"Apoptosis."

It meant he would annihilate all living beings when the unit of information transmission was set to the planet.

Shirone spoke.

"Goffin has left the photon system."

With a snap, Argones' body broke and landed softly on the floor.

And he was silent.

"Gaia has transcended you and Anke Ra. As long as that belief exists, we won't give up."

"You think I'm the villain."

Argones' body began to collapse as he placed his hand on the video recording device.

"Well, that's your limit. Belief? You can't understand Goffin with just that."

"What do you mean?"

The gelatinous substance seeped into the video recording device.

"You should think about why Gaia left humanity behind. Goffin may have left, but the fight isn't over yet."

As most of his body seeped in, Argones' finger pointed at Shirone.

"Save yourself."

The last voice leaked out.

"That's Goffin’s message."

As Shirone kept Argones' message in his mind, the video recording device twisted grotesquely.

With a pop, an explosion occurred inside, and the smell of burning cytoplasm filled the air.

Shirone, who had been silently watching the scene with nothing left, removed the material barrier.

And he fell into deep thought about how to handle Pisho's will.

When he made his decision and turned around, Shirone's eyes were filled with firm resolve.

"That's how it is."

When Shirone finished the story about Pisho's will, Iruki stroked his chin.

"Hmm, no one should win."

It was a story that couldn't be ignored from the perspective of humans opposing extreme evil.

"It's surprising that Nane is included. Didn't you say he swallowed Anke Ra's dream? So, Ra and Argones are enemies?"

"They said there are no sides. They just suppress phenomena that cross the line according to their standards. Both Ra and Argones."

Iruki nodded.

"It's tricky. We need to prepare, but the information will spread quickly. I understand how you feel."

"We can't reveal it, nor can we hide it. I'll go to the Ivory Tower and ask Great Star. Great Star might be able to make a more accurate judgment."

As they each fell into thought, Shirone asked.

"What will you do now?"

"Nothing will change. Havitz' army is right in front of us, so we have to stop them. I don't know if we can."

Iruki comforted Shirone instead.

"I'll leave the Argones issue to you. Don't worry about the kingdom. It's our job."

"...Save yourself."

The two looked up at the sky together.

In front of Pisho's grave, where people had left, Kaiden placed a chrysanthemum flower.

"Was it a satisfying life?"

He had deliberately arrived late to avoid mingling with the alumni, but someone had the same thought.

"You're late."

When he turned his head, Eden was standing with the sun behind her.

"You came too?"

"I had to. We're friends."

Kaiden, Eden, and Pisho had once fought as a team in Scramble Royale.

Kaiden slowly stood up.

"I heard you were in Zion."

"The spiritual realm is closed, and the monks have been dispatched all over the world. I stopped by briefly before supporting the Central Continent War."

"Good luck."

As Kaiden turned to leave, Eden asked.

"What are you going to do?"

"...What?"

"Won't you fight? You were born with the fate of the Red Cross Star. It's a chance to use your abilities."

Humanity needed Kaiden.

"I don't believe in fate." Honestly, he didn't know.

"I'm not a seeker. I'm not a priest like you. Why should I fight? For whom?"

Eden thought of many reasons.

But the one reason she couldn't say was, "For yourself."

If Kaiden became a hero who saved the world, it would mean his unhappiness.

"Well, I can't force you. Anyway, take care. Contact me if you change your mind."

Kaiden, who had been standing still, finally moved without answering.

At that moment, the alumni of Alpheas Magic School walked in groups from the entrance of the cemetery.

Familiar faces.

Shirone, Iruki, and Amy were there, and behind them, Screamer and Maya were walking.

As if time had stopped, only his heart was beating fast.

Screamer pleaded with Maya.

"Hey, come to my match. It would be even better if you performed."

"Perform?"

After a moment of thought, Maya turned her head with a huff.

"No."

"Do you still hate me?"

"I don't hate you, but I don't particularly like you either. Honestly, you were the one who hated me, right?"

"I'm sorry. I regret it. And you won back then, didn't you? Thinking about it, it's unfair."

Amy pointed ahead.

"Hey, isn't that Kaiden? And Eden is there too."

While Kaiden was still frozen, Eden ran over and waved.

"Hi? Long time no see."

As Eden talked with Shirone, Screamer approached Kaiden.

"Hey? What are you doing?"

Kaiden seemed soulless.

"What are you looking at?"

Screamer, who walked behind Kaiden to match his gaze, saw nothing.

"Maya."

Kaiden was trembling.

The moment he saw her, he realized the enormity of the harsh fate he had to bear.

"It's Kaiden."

Maya's heart fluttered for a moment, but she quickly regained her composure and shook her head.

"No, it's okay now."

She had decided to bear the cost of loving Shirone, so there was no need to ask about the graduation exam.

"Hey, say something! What's wrong?"

Knowing why Kaiden was frozen, Amy sighed and pulled his hand.

Kaiden, who took a step as if he were about to fall, finally came to his senses and looked around.

"Ah..."

Maya was smiling at him.

"What are you doing? Say hello."

As Amy coached him in frustration, Kaiden walked over awkwardly.

"Hi..."

"Yes, it's been a long time. Since the graduation exam, right? How have you been?"

When there was no answer, Maya tilted her head.

"What's wrong, Kaiden? Are you feeling unwell?" When Kaiden placed his hand on Maya's shoulder, everyone stopped talking and looked at them.

"Ma-Maya."

Amy's face turned pale.

'That's not it, you fool.'

Right now, the only thought in Kaiden's head was to confess his love.

'Destroy my fate.'

Without a word, Kaiden leaned in, bringing his lips close to Maya's.

"...What?"

As the alumni's eyes widened, Maya pushed Kaiden away in disgust.

"What are you doing! You pervert!" Amy held her forehead, and Kaiden, who awkwardly stepped back, looked at his friends' faces.

"I'm sorry."

Ignoring all the glares, Kaiden bowed to Maya and walked away.

"What's wrong with him? He was weird before, but now he seems completely out of it."

Amy said,

"He just doesn't know. He's never done anything he wanted to do before."

Whenever Kaiden desperately wanted something, the result was always the worst.

'But if it's the opposite...'

If he gave up his desperate prayer, at least Amy's imagination would be surpassed.

'It's a pity.'

As she was thinking like a soldier, Alpheas and Olivia walked over.

"You haven't left yet."

"Principal."

Shirone, who had finished talking with Eden, approached Alpheas.

"I've been wanting to ask, what happened to Professor Etella?"

"Why hasn't anyone told me? Did something bad happen?"

"Romi Etella..."

Alpheas said with sad eyes.

"She lost her mind."
