Chapter 1238: Exo Universe (1)

While the delegates waited in the Delta Annex for the official event to begin, the Holy War operations team entered first.

“Apologies.”

The operations team leader’s face was flushed with tension.

“It looks like the event will be delayed by about 10 minutes. The annex has been upgraded to Security Level 2...”

The moment those words were spoken, the heads of each country tapped into their direct communication channels to gather more information.

From Tormia, one of Dante’s subordinates approached.

“Chairman.”

“What’s going on?”

“There’s been a battle at the main entrance of Delta Headquarters. The involved parties are the southern warriors and the Anti-Demon Division of the Lami Church.”

Lupist repeated,

“Southern warriors and the Lami Church?”

“Yes, it seems...”

Albino cut him off.

“They’re the ones from the pyramid. So they finally made it into the Holy War? Since when?”

“The battle broke out at 9:45. Also, Yahweh and Havitz have joined the battlefield.”

‘They were there?’

That place was without a doubt the hottest zone in the Holy War right now—but there was still something unexplained.

“You’d best avoid it,” the informant said in a lowered voice.

“According to intelligence, there’s something strange going on. People coming into contact with those who arrived from the pyramid lose their will like they’re hypnotized, and then join the mob.”

Lupist said,

“The Wave of In. If they’re all OOPArts, then the magnetic influence can be mobile.”

“Yes. The annex is now a restricted zone under Security Level 2. The Holy War Secretariat has permitted up to two people per nation to leave the area.”

No one moved.

‘If the information from the future is true, the World Leadership Nation might be decided here. And even if it’s not, the vote happens tomorrow morning. If the king flees, they’ll lose all justification.’

Only the aides bustled quietly, while the annex remained cold and still.

Lupist asked,

“How dangerous is it? What are the odds the pyramid mob breaches headquarters and reaches the annex?”

Dante must have calculated that.

“Fifty percent. It depends on the decisions Yahweh and Havitz make.”

‘Fifty percent.’

The most human and meaningless number of all.

“No, it’s a hundred percent,” Albino said.

“Now it all makes sense. Why Fermi shared the future’s information with the world. What he feared wasn’t the Kingdom of Airon becoming the World Leadership Nation—it was the possibility that Maya wouldn’t die.”

The informant asked,

“What do you mean? Are you saying Keshia also wants Maya dead?”

“No. To be exact…”

Albino glanced at the vacant seat of the King of Keshia.

“It’s what God wants.”

Lupist’s eyes gleamed.

“The Wave of In. If those people are charging into the Holy War to kill Maya...”

“Exactly. Shirone won’t be able to stop it. And we too will be swept up in the Wave of In and end up killing Maya. Most people in this room will lose their will.”

“But I still don’t understand. Why would Fermi follow the will of God? We’ve never had intel suggesting Keshia made contact with the Kingdom of Paras.”

“Heh. Heheheh…”

Albino laughed like someone scratching an itch in their lungs.

“I see what he’s trying to do.”

His laughter abruptly stopped.

“Fermi doesn’t want to change the future. He believes information entropy should always remain at zero.”

“What does that mean in this situation?”

“Because he knows the future.”

Albino raised his index finger.

“The trap of updated information. Living life without knowing the future, versus knowing it and still not making any changes—those seem the same at a glance, but the quality of the information is entirely different.”

“Because one includes the power to choose.”

“Exactly. That’s the power of information. Imagine there’s a trap on the road. The first time, I fall into it not knowing it was there. Now let’s say I know it’s there—and I still fall in. The result is the same, but are they really the same?”

“Of course not.”

“Because in the second case, it was a choice. Fermi doesn’t want to change the future. If I knew about the trap and chose a different path…”

“Then you’re back in the unknown.”

“Right. A new trap could be waiting. But more than that, your ability to choose is taken away. The more entropy increases, the narrower the scope of control.”

Albino twisted his beard.

“At the heart of this setup is Shirone. Because he is the one capable of changing the world, information must be blocked from him. That’s the true identity of the unease we’ve felt in Shirone and Fermi’s actions.”

Lupist looked straight ahead.

“What are they waiting for? If they’re choosing to protect the future even as the world falls into chaos…”

It could only mean something far more terrible was about to happen.

“How audacious.”

Lupist nodded.

“Yes. But it won’t work.”

“We don’t know that. A liar who only tells the truth... I wasn’t sure what kind of man he was, but—”

Albino grinned as he looked up at the ceiling.

“Trying to fool God?”

King Fermi of Keshia did not go to the annex but instead visited Seriel’s room.

She was happy to be with him, but at the same time, she was worried.

“Are you sure it’s okay not to go? It’s an important event.”

“It’ll be boring.”

Seriel, who had been leaning against Fermi’s shoulder, sat up.

“You’re up to something weird again, aren’t you? Just looking at your face, it’s obvious.”

Seriel hesitated under Fermi’s subtle gaze.

“W-What?”

“Shall we... get married?”

“You’re really saying that in a situation like this? You’re smart, but sometimes you say the strangest things.”

She harshly rejected his proposal, but in truth, it was because a sudden fear had crept into her heart.

Seriel asked gently again.

“What’s going on? Are you in danger? Is that why you’re suddenly saying we should get married...?”

“Haha, this is why it’s exhausting dealing with capable people. It doesn’t mean anything. It was just a thought that crossed my mind when I looked at you.”

“No. You’re not the type of person to say something like that without a reason.”

Fermi felt genuinely hurt.

“Then tell me clearly. I won’t ask anything else. You’re okay, right? You’re going to be fine, right?”

“How could I know that? I might stub my toe on the corner of a table any second now.”

“Don’t dodge the question.”

“I’m not dodging it. This is a battlefield. Who knows what’ll happen while doing this and that... Ugh!”

Seriel pushed Fermi down onto the bed and climbed on top of his waist.

“Ah! That hurts...”

Her eyes were glistening with tears.

“Tell me.”

After hesitating, Fermi finally spoke.

“...There’s nothing in the Apocalypse records that says something particularly bad happens to me.”

“There you go again! Trying to trick me with the truth! I want to hear you say you’ll be okay, that after this war ends, you’ll still be with Seriel!”

Fermi never lied.

“See? I knew it... you—”

“I will.”

He spoke clearly, word by word.

“I won’t die. After this war, I’ll still be by your side.”

Seriel sniffled and added,

“...Alive.”

“Haha, yes. Alive.”

Finally relaxing, she collapsed onto Fermi’s chest.

“You keep saying unnecessary things. Say it one more time, and I’ll kill you.”

Was it really that upsetting?

“Sorry.”

Fermi turned his gaze to the window.

“...It’s raining.”

“What are you talking about? It’s not—”

The moment Seriel looked at him with a confused expression, the sound of a sudden downpour roared like a monsoon.

Mother, knowing the future is such a burden, Fermi thought.

He wished even this sound of rain was a lie.

The chaotic noise from Delta’s front gate seemed to be buried beneath the rain that poured as if into the very depths of the earth.

Drenched in an instant, Shirone and Havitz stood locked in each other’s gaze.

Shirone asked,

“Why didn’t you run?”

By the laws of the early commandments, Satan could eternally run from Yahweh.

“And why didn’t you kill me?” Havitz replied coldly.

If he’d truly wanted to bypass Vanishing, he could have just cut through Untara.

“Shall I guess?”

Havitz closed one eye and said,

“It’s because you only get one chance an hour. If I dodge your attack, that’s it for you.”

“Can you dodge it?”

“Don’t spout nonsense. I know why a coward like you is holding on.”

There were less than two hours left until the final confrontation of truth and lies would end.

I have to prove just one truth in that time.

Havitz’s condition: kill either Uorin or Shirone by midnight.

If I kill Shirone here, I gain the upper hand.

Of course, Wizard’s proposition could be fulfilled simply based on one’s mindset.

Either love me or hate me.

Depending on his heart.

If she loves me, there's no need to win. If she hates me...

Then Havitz could simply go wild.

“Coward, huh?” Havitz’s energy shifted, and violet killing intent began to seep out.

“Hell’s Prison.”

Twelve fanatics erupted from the ground.

“Satan.”

“Capture him.”

The moment Satan gave the order, the twelve encircled Shirone.

And I...

Vanishing.

Outside the law, Havitz aimed his sword at Shirone’s back while Hell Prison whispered:

“Yahweh.”

The twelve hidden codes—the power to mock the world.

“Wish.”

Whatever you desire—

Pleasure, longing, conquest, domination. Every wish a human could ever have is yours.

Shirone’s expression remained cold.

It won’t work.

As Havitz watched from behind, his grip on the longsword tightened.

But I’ll win.

Until now, Vanishing had been broken only because Havitz followed the rules.

And that’s boring. Right, Balkan?

Through a tunnel outside Shirone’s awareness, Havitz lunged with his sword.

Today, Yahweh’s back seemed broader than ever.

I can do this.

More than anything, there was the divine frequency.

Though something felt deeply off, the name “Havitz” wasn’t heard anywhere.

Still, I’m the closest to him among all humans in the world. He really is an irritating bastard.

Having finally slipped past Hell’s Prison, Havitz narrowed his eyes and thrust his sword.

It’s over.

And then, a shiver of terror surged down his spine.

“What...?”

Just as the blade touched Shirone’s back, Havitz’s elbow bent inward at the same velocity he was thrusting.

“Grrgh!”

Realizing it wasn’t enough, he tumbled past Shirone, rolling across the ground.

“S-Satan...”

Vanishing was broken, but Shirone remained standing.

So it’s true, after all.

Only at the end of the world did Havitz realize.

“Are you alright?”

Even Hell’s Prison’s voice didn’t seem to reach him—Havitz just sat there, dazed.

Why couldn’t I kill him?

The one feeling he had in that moment—

Run.

Havitz looked at his trembling fingers. It wasn’t even fear—because he hadn’t defined it that way.

“What is this?”

But it was definitely not a good feeling.

Gripping the hilt of his sword, he slammed it into the back of his left hand—crack, crack, his bones snapped.

“What the hell? Huh? What’s wrong with me?!”

“Havitz.”

The moment he heard Shirone’s voice, Havitz stopped moving, as if his mind were cleaved like stone.

“You can’t kill me.” Shirone said as Havitz’s nostrils flared with ragged breath.

“And that’s why... you’re running.”
