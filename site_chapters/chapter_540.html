Chapter 540:

Marsha bolted out of the engine room, dashing through the tunnel as a furious roar echoed behind her.

“I’ll kill you!”

She dove between the tracks and the platform, hiding her body.

At least I got one hit in.

So far, she’d used two of her Dagger of Depravity charges.

Once the dagger materialized, the tattoo vanished, and it would take 24 hours to regenerate.

Five left.

She slid her hand deep under her left thigh and pulled out another dagger.

The gas emission rate was 1% per minute—total exhaustion would take 100 minutes, but it was unrealistic to last that long.

I need to stab him at least twice more.

With a 3% emission rate per minute, even at maximum resistance, Malta would be worn out in 33 minutes.

As a flickering red light glowed ominously through the tunnel, Marsha sprang to the opposite side just in time.

A massive fireball obliterated a train behind her.

“Freaking psycho...!”

Her blood ran cold. Scrambling up onto the platform, she took off through the labyrinthine corridors.

“You think you can escape?!”

Malta teleported and reappeared atop the platform.

But at that exact moment, the trail of gas ceased. The black smoke vanished.

“What…?”

The last trail he saw had turned the corner, but he hesitated to follow.

The wound is still there.

That meant it was likely an advanced application of the Regulation Exception.

Just as Malta had guessed, Marsha had cut off her Spirit Zone and was hiding beneath an escalator.

When Spirit Zone is deactivated, the Dagger of Depravity disappears, and the gas emission halts.

Now four left…

She’d lost one without even using it, but at this point it was just another result.

“Come out! Just like a street rat—cowardly to the core!”

Like you’re one to talk.

To prove it was a taunt, Malta remained in passive defense mode, using only his Spirit Zone to scan the area, minimizing exposure.

He’s trying to boost his reaction time for a counterattack.

Marsha teleported forward and reactivated her dagger. The gas began streaming again.

She darted around Malta’s spells like a cat, slicing his ankle and opening a new wound.

She pierced through Earth Skin?

The dagger ignored physical defenses.

“Rrgh!”

A crushing air press slammed her to the ground.

The pain felt like her spine would snap, but with the searing fireball forming in Malta’s hand, she had no time to react to pain.

The explosion blasted through the room as Marsha tackled him at the waist, dragging them both out of the blast radius.

This close, he won’t be able to use area magic.

She stabbed the dagger deep into his side.

“You annoying bitch!”

Malta struck her in the jaw and tore her dagger-wielding hand away—but she reached behind with her other hand and drew another blade from the groove near her tailbone.

“Yaaaah!”

She stabbed frantically into his side. Four more sheaths formed in an instant.

“Fucking hell!”

Malta braced himself and cast a fire spell, blasting both of them across the tunnel in a burst.

“Ghhhhh…”

The pain felt like her skin was melting as Marsha trembled.

That makes seven total.

With 7% per minute gas emission, Malta would be done in about 10 minutes.

“UWAAGHHHH!”

To be drained like that, spewing black gas from every pore—it was a horrifying experience.

Malta’s Hellfire spell scorched the subway interior into a wasteland.

“Hah… Hah…”

Feeling his energy drain by the second, Malta finally realized the dagger’s true effect.

So that’s what it is…

Driven by urgency, he attacked even more viciously, eventually cornering Marsha at a dead-end wall.

“Die, you foolish woman!”

Two massive fire pillars twisted together above his head, charging.

“HURRAAGH!”

But Malta’s eyes widened as the spell dissipated.

“Time’s up.”

He’d exhausted all of his desire. Malta collapsed to his knees with a dull thud.

It felt like he’d run a mountain marathon—his body refused to move.

“Damn it… To someone like you…”

Marsha clutched her burned right arm, approached him, stared for a moment—and then delivered a brutal kick to his chin.

“Ghk!”

Without the strength to resist, Malta’s head slammed into the ground, and he passed out.

“Should’ve just kept your hands to yourself.”

Sighing, Marsha dragged his unconscious body back toward the engine room.

When the group reunited, Fermi healed her burns with recovery magic.

After Marsha reported what Malta had done, Miro ordered Rian to retrieve Malta’s wife from the locked room and bring her before her husband.

“Honey! What’s going on? Honey!”

Even if she was fake, it was too humiliating for Malta to speak about what he’d done in front of her.

“Looks like the husband got some dirty ideas about one of our own. Has this happened before?” Fermi asked casually.

Hearing this, the wife looked utterly dazed.

“Honey. Is it true? Did you… really… with that woman…?”

Malta screamed, eyes wide with rage.

“What the hell are you doing?! Just kill me already! I’m sick of living in this goddamn world!”

“That won’t be necessary. This will make for a good case study,” said Miro, glancing at Fermi.

“What do you plan to do?”

“Leave it to me. It’s a perfect chance to study how Minor Conception merges information. I’ll observe the wife’s response while I torture Malta. And vice versa.”

“No,” Rian said immediately, not even hesitating. “You can’t.”

“Why not?”

“Because it’s cruel.”

“Hahahaha!”

Fermi actually laughed, dumbfounded.

“You’re misunderstanding the point. This isn’t punishment. This world doesn’t even have laws. It’s just a valuable opportunity to analyze Shirone’s condition.”

“I don’t care. What matters is that the wife did nothing wrong.”

“Then think it over a little more. Don’t spout nonsense.”

Fermi tapped his own head in frustration.

“We know this world is fake. That means we’re in an ‘open’ state. But Malta’s wife is in a ‘closed’ state.”

Being trapped was, ironically, what made her state authentic.

“Depending on that state, the type of information you receive changes completely. So what about Shirone? He’s ‘closed’—he’s taking in the information of this world as if it’s real. Which means he’s likely quite different from the Shirone we know.”

Fermi pointed at Malta’s wife.

“Even living right beside her husband, her information wasn’t restored. Because she’s still trapped. So instead of just ‘encountering’ Shirone, we need to hit him with something even more extreme.”

He sighed like dealing with all this was a chore and grabbed the wife’s head.

“I’ll take her to the back. From there, I’ll—”

A massive sword suddenly thrust itself into view, cutting him off. Fermi turned his head coldly.

“No. No matter how I think about it, this isn’t right.”

“…Are you seriously that stupid? You’re not a microbe—how can someone in an open state think like someone who’s closed?”

“It’s not about state. Real or fake, I can’t justify torturing an innocent person.”

“You’re really making this difficult.”

“I’ll cooperate. I’ll do whatever it takes to bring Shirone back. Even if it costs me my life. So just trust me this time.”

The two stared each other down for a long moment.

“…Phew.”

At last, Fermi raised his hands and stepped back.

“Fine. You’re really frustrating, you know that? Are you even Shirone’s friend?”

“Shirone’s smart. Cold, too. He’s probably even sharper than you. But…”

Rian lowered his sword.

“I believe Shirone would’ve made the same choice I did.”

There was a brief silence.

“…Maybe so. I’ll apologize for doubting your friendship.”

Fermi began to turn away—then suddenly spun back around.

“But I’m not Shirone.”

With a flick of his hand, he cast Wind Cutter. Malta’s head flew clean off his shoulders.

“HONEEEYYYYY!”

Ignoring the wife’s scream, Fermi grabbed Malta’s severed head and brought the face close to his own.

“Now, do you see this? It’s your husband’s face. He died because of you. Does anything come to mind now?”

“Kyaaaa! Kyaaaa!”

“Scream all you want. But think. You’re the fake one. The real you is already dead.”

“N-no! That can’t be! I, I...”

The wife stared up at the ceiling, her face frozen in shock.

“Vegetative state...?”

Fermi’s eyes lit up.

That’s it!

As the wife suddenly lost consciousness and collapsed, Fermi rested his chin on his hand and muttered regretfully.

“A trauma equal to death—or beyond. Not easy to pull off, but for now...”

“You bastard!”

Rian came charging in with his greatsword, but Marsha ran up and grabbed his waist.

“Wait, Rian! Hold on!”

“Let go! Don’t tell me you’re siding with that bastard?!”

“I feel the same way you do! But it’s already happened! We need to be rational! If it’s irreversible, then at least—”

“RAAAAAAH!”

Rian shook Marsha off and lunged forward.

Calm! Rational! Efficient! Efficient!

The mage’s words were always right.

But then what was he supposed to do with this overwhelming fury threatening to explode in his chest?

“I won’t forgive you!”

Just as the greatsword came crashing down toward Fermi, dozens of phantom hands appeared and caught the blade midair.

Rian, flung across the room by Miro’s extended arms, landed with his sword still pointed forward.

“What is this? If you’re about to give me the cold logic lecture too—”

“No. I completely understand how you feel. But listen to this. If you kill Fermi, Shirone will never come back.”

“No. I’ll save Shirone.”

“Then all the more reason to stop. Time is running out.”

Rian lowered his sword.

“What are you talking about?”

“Even if Shirone’s information is fully restored, we can’t be sure the signature on the contract will still be valid. Isn’t that what you were thinking, Fermi?”

“Yes. What I realized when I arrived here was that—even in this virtual world—incarnations and laws are still separated. That gives us two urgent reasons to find Shirone as soon as possible.”

Fermi raised two fingers.

“First, if Shirone is harmed or dies, reintegration of his information becomes impossible. We could prove that by killing Malta’s wife.”

Before Rian could retort, Fermi continued.

“But that’s highly unlikely. In fact, even if we don’t look for him, Shirone will find us. As much as I hate to admit it, he’s freakishly fast at reading situations. The kind of guy who’d build a sandcastle to survive even if you threw him into a desert.”

That, Rian had to agree with.

“Apocalypse’s data is going to keep shaping him. Because he’s trapped. And since it’s a dangerous world, there’s a good chance magic will manifest. Maybe even to the level where he can cast it just like in reality.”

Marsha chimed in.

“But even then, he’s not the real Shirone, is he?”

“He’ll be using the situation to its fullest, surviving. That’s what I’d do. And if he gets breathing room, he might even start making plans. We’ll meet him eventually. But it’ll be Apocalypse’s Shirone.”

“Hoooh…”

Fermi glanced over at Rian, who sighed deeply.

“Now do you understand? Malta’s wife is valuable information. If Shirone’s fully reconstructed by the Apocalypse, the only way to save him is to make him realize this world is fake. But that... yeah, that’s basically insane.”

Miro added, “It’s easy to relate when you compare it to real life. Even if someone told us the real world was fake, we wouldn’t be able to accept it.”

Fermi shrugged.

“Yeah. That’s the kind of problem we’re dealing with.”
