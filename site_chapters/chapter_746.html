Chapter 746:

“When you told me to meet other guys, you meant it!”

Avoiding Isis’s nagging, Amy stormed into her room, fuming as she sat on the edge of her bed.

“Does she think I’m sitting still because I enjoy it?”

Her anger subsided quickly. Isis had a point, and her fears were entirely understandable for any normal person.

‘What’s going to happen to this world?’

The emergence of spiritual zones across various regions would shake the world far beyond the mere issue of demonic appearances.

‘Countless casualties, civil wars, conflicts with foreign nations…’

Soldiering would become the most dangerous profession.

Perhaps, for Amy—who had been preparing for the public certification exam—this might be the only chance to change her future.

‘Shirone.’

When they graduated, she had thought they would build their future together and live a happy life.

‘What am I to you?’

Exhausted from battling demons, Amy fell asleep without realizing how she had collapsed onto the bed. Tears silently slid down her cheeks.

“It’s been a while, Miss Amy.”

“Huh?”

Hearing a voice, Amy turned around, and for some reason, the person before her seemed vaguely familiar.

An elderly man with a kind smile and a bushy mustache.

Unlike the blurred dream world around him, he alone appeared vividly colored, exuding a clarity that pushed her into the deep sixth-level realm of lucid dreaming.

“Where is this?”

The moment she realized it was a dream, the landscape sharpened, revealing a vast field dotted with rare, vibrant flowers.

“Huh? What’s happening?”

She had occasionally experienced sleep paralysis during dreams, but never had she been so completely aware.

Now fully conscious, Amy turned back to the old man.

“Wait a minute… No way.”

“Yes, I am Luber, the Dream Guardian.”

Amy knew only those who had undergone special training and obtained a lucid dream certification could find Luber.

“Did you come here directly to meet me?”

“Yes. Normally, it’s against Teraphos Interstellar Law for a Dream Guardian to forcibly awaken someone’s dream. However, given the circumstances, I had no choice.”

Luber had already reported the matter to the Supreme Judge of Teraphos, and with Shirone acknowledged as humanity’s representative, permission had been granted.

“Why did you come looking for me?”

“There’s someone who wants to meet you. If you don’t mind, may I connect you to that person’s dream?”

The fact that Luber was speaking so respectfully made Amy a bit nervous.

“Who is it?”

“I cannot tell you. But I assure you, you won’t regret it.”

Amy, who had been pursing her lips in thought, finally made up her mind.

“Fine. It’s just a dream anyway.”

“Then please, enjoy your time.”

Luber’s body gradually turned transparent before completely vanishing.

“What was that?”

While Amy looked around in confusion, a familiar voice suddenly called out from behind her.

“Amy.”

Startled, she turned around slowly, only to find Shirone standing there with a gentle smile.

“You… You…?”

“I’m sorry for coming to you this way. But…”

Even though she knew it was a dream, the sight of his face brought forth a surge of emotions she had been holding back.

“You jerk!”

Grabbing Shirone by the collar, Amy shouted at him.

“How could you not contact me even once? Do you know how I found out about you? Through my dad!”

Shirone remained silent, only offering an apologetic expression.

“Do you have any idea how embarrassed I was? My mom told me to meet someone else, people whispered that I’d been dumped! And you… You just…”

Before she could finish, Shirone pulled her into a tight embrace.

“I missed you.”

Amy knew.

The overwhelming, desperate emotion he conveyed—no exaggerated words could describe it.

‘I know. I know it too.’

Hearts are vaster than the universe.

“Fine. Since you’re here, I’ll forgive you.”

As Shirone held onto her shoulders and stepped back slightly, tears welled in his eyes too.

“But! I need to clarify something first.”

What exactly did this guy think of her?

“You said you came here through a dream, right? Knowing you, you didn’t come just to see me, did you? How many people have you seen before me?”

“You’re the first, of course.”

Shirone answered hastily, but the guilty look in his eyes betrayed him as he cautiously added, “Actually… I went to see my parents first.”

Amy broke into a bright smile.

“Then that means I’m still first. Come on, you’re going to tell me everything that happened.”

Holding hands, Shirone nodded, and they ran across the grassy plains.

Ahead, they saw a small house surrounded by a field of flowers, butterflies flitting about like fragments of light.

The house was entirely pink, and realizing it revealed her subconscious, Amy covered her mouth in embarrassment and cleared her throat.

“Ahem.”

This was precisely why dream connections were forbidden by Teraphos law.

Yet, because it was Shirone, instead of feeling embarrassed, she felt an exhilarating thrill.

“...Let’s go inside.”

Amy spoke shyly, and Shirone followed her, wearing a mysterious smile.

“Amy, there’s something I need to tell you.”

“I know, it’s urgent…”

Knowing there wasn’t much time, Shirone began explaining everything she needed to know.

As the conversation progressed, the candles around them flickered and changed in various ways. By the time the full truth was revealed, the room had grown dim.

“So I…”

“I understand.”

Amy interrupted him.

“I understand why you couldn’t contact me and what you need to do now.”

The candles brightened once again.

“It’s okay. It can’t be helped. I’m glad I have a clear goal now too. We’ll work together to bring down Nane.”

“No. This isn’t something that can be solved at that level.”

Understanding this might be his last chance to approach her as a person, Shirone spoke with difficulty.

“Amy, I can’t be with you anymore.”

“What?”

Amy’s house fell into complete darkness.

“Being with you like this makes me so happy. So happy I can’t stand it, I feel like I’m going to go crazy.”

“Then what’s the problem? Why are you suddenly saying this?”

“This is the problem. I’m too happy. In the end, I’ll become obsessed with you. And if that happens…”

Shirone bit his lip.

“Nane will definitely exploit that. The more I love you, the more danger you’ll be in.”

“But isn’t Nane a Buddha? What kind of Buddha would do something so cowardly?”

“That’s also part of human emotion.”

Shirone looked toward Amy’s face beyond the darkness.

“To Nane, everything in this world is meaningless. He won’t hesitate to do whatever it takes to close the world.”

Because nothing truly exists to him.

“He has already transcended the concept of good and evil. After mastering all good and all evil, he has reached the realm of the divine.”

It would be a lie to say he wasn’t afraid.

“And I can’t fight someone like that while trying to protect someone. So, Amy, please… let’s end this.”

“Fine. Let’s end it.”

At Amy’s sudden cold response, Shirone’s heart sank, unable to continue.

“Hah, I knew this would happen. How can we even talk about love in times like this? You don’t need to worry about me anymore. Just fight Nane however you want, wherever you are. Got it?”

Her words came out smoothly, but the howling wind outside betrayed her true emotions.

“I’ll come to you.”

Like the wind, Amy’s voice trembled as she sobbed.

“I’ll become strong enough that you won’t need to protect me. So I’ll definitely, definitely come to you…”

Warm, wet tears soaked Shirone’s chest as he held Amy tightly.

“Don’t worry about anything. Just wait for me. I’ll come to you, no matter where or who you’re fighting.”

She would grow stronger—so strong that she could punch Nane’s face herself.

“Amy…”

Shirone couldn’t hold back his tears either, and the two of them gently caressed each other’s faces in the darkness.

If only he could hold her forever.

‘It’s just a dream. A dream for just the two of us.’

Their lips, almost touching, wandered hesitantly, lost, while only their overwhelming emotions stormed between them.

“I have to go. Stay safe.”

“No. Don’t go. Shirone… wait, just a little longer…”

Even though she thought she was prepared, facing reality made it unbearable.

“I’m sorry. I’m so sorry, Amy.”

Wouldn’t it be better to just love her madly until the world ended, and then face the end together?

As that all-too-human thought crossed his mind, Shirone clenched his eyes shut and bolted out of the house.

“Shirone! Wait, wait…!”

That was the end of Amy’s dream.

“Ughhh!”

Curled up and sobbing in the dream world, Shirone looked up as Luber approached him with a sorrowful expression.

“Do not despair.”

“Why… why me? Why only me… why!”

“Forgive me.”

At Luber’s calm yet resolute words, Shirone’s frantic breathing gradually slowed.

“Hah… hah…”

‘Once again, he accepts it. Truly, an indomitable spirit.’

Neither Shirone nor Nane had been born as gods. Their foundation was entirely human. Yet, even so, Shirone’s will was strong enough to aspire toward divinity.

“This is only the beginning. The Great Purification has just started. Without courage, you’ll end up destroying yourself.”

And there was no guarantee that wouldn’t happen.

“I’m sorry. You went out of your way to help me, Luber.”

“No, it is I who should apologize. I knew this would happen. But if Shirone becomes fully human in reality, the world will collapse.”

That was why Luber had chosen to test things through a dream.

“Are you ready to go back?”

“No. This is my last chance before I develop stronger attachments. I need to tell as many people as possible.”

The mission of the Five Great Stars had already begun.

“Please take me to my friends.”

Luber whispered in a somber tone.

“Have a pleasant dream…”

“Ugh… ugh…”

Hearing Neid groan in the early hours of the morning, Liz, who had been sleeping beside him, gently shook his body.

“Neid? A nightmare?”

They had spent the entire day fighting demons while trying to escape the range of the spiritual realm.

“Maybe you’re just exhausted?”

“Was that really just a dream?”

Even though Luber had explained everything, Neid, having never experienced Drimo, remained skeptical.

“Pfft! Who cares? Wow, it’s Shirone!”

Rushing toward Shirone with excitement, Neid’s expression immediately stiffened when he saw his friend’s tearful face.

“…What’s wrong, Shirone?”

Why are you crying?

“Neid.”

“What’s wrong? Did something bad happen?”

This was the first time Neid had ever seen Shirone cry so sorrowfully.

“Who hurt you? Tell me who it is! I’ll go beat them up right now!”

“Neid, I…”

It had been a long dream.

“Gasp!”

Neid woke up with a start, sitting upright and gasping for air.

“Are you okay?”

Hearing Liz’s voice, he glanced around, taking in the unfamiliar surroundings of the inn where they had sought refuge from the demons.

“Was it a dream about the demons? I tried waking you, but you wouldn’t budge…”

“It wasn’t a dream.”

Shirone had said that Nane was the enemy.

“Get ready, Liz. We’re heading back to Tormia right now. We need to meet Iruki. He’s probably waiting for me.”

“Tormia?”

When the spiritual realm engulfed the capital, Neid had crossed the border with Liz in tow.

While Neid himself wasn’t exactly patriotic, Liz, being an official member of the Magic Association, had done something unthinkable.

“Do you even know what the situation in the capital is? The moment we arrive, we’ll be arrested. And what’s this about Iruki waiting for you?”

“Shirone’s in trouble.”

“…”

Liz couldn’t argue because both of them owed their new lives to Shirone.

“Some lunatic is messing with my friend.”

High-level concepts and such weren’t Neid’s concern.

“So, you’re really going? Now?”

“I would abandon a country without hesitation to protect the one I love…”

Neid said as he pulled on his coat.

“But if anyone messes with my friend, not even a Buddha will be forgiven.”

That was Neid.

“Haah. Seriously, men and their sense of loyalty…”

Hoisting a backpack onto his shoulder, Neid gave Liz a sly grin.

“Let’s go. Buddha or not…”

If humans are the problem, he would rule over them.

“They’ll find out exactly what happens when you mess with Shirone’s crew.”
