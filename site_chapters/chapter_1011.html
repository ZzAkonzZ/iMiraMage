Chapter 1011: Shadow of God (3)

Ouroboros.

A concept symbolizing infinity, typically depicted as a snake eating its own tail and growing.

Even if it continues to swallow its tail, the body grows accordingly, so there is no end.

However.

"I'll devour it all!"

Shirone's teeth violently bit and chewed the tail, giving Gyorgi no chance to recover.

"Groooar!"

Gyorgi, with veins bulging in his neck, tried to amplify his evil power with all his might, but...

"It's impossible."

It was extremely impossible to overturn the inertia from the already destroyed balance.

Perhaps an understanding of infinity.

The technical difference between Gyorgi, who was born with it, and Shirone, who experienced it from nothing to everything.

"Just that much."

But that one difference collapses the balance and creates a cosmic gap.

Gyorgi no longer struggled.

As the cycle of 20,000 iterations proceeded in an instant, the light completely purified the darkness.

"Ahhh! Falling!"

The citizens who were floating in the air fell to the ground, and all the dust in the city poured down.

Fwoosh.

In the landscape dressed in gray, as if snow had piled up, Shirone twisted his body.

The Miracle Stream that swallowed the darkness circled around and slowly dissipated.

"Give up, Gyorgi. You can imitate my current self, but you can't imitate my experiences."

Gyorgi raised one corner of his mouth.

"That's right. I already know that I can't defeat Yahweh with straightforward methods. Maybe even more than you."

"Then why?"

Gyorgi, who expanded his senses, realized that Mio no longer existed.

"Well... let's say it's part of a strategy. And I wanted to clash at least once."

"Once?"

Shirone frowned.

"I don't quite understand. Do you think I'll give you two chances?"

"Don't be mistaken, Yahweh."

Gyorgi opened the Devil's Bible.

"This Bible is the only one in the underworld and is the source of all my abilities."

The pages turned by themselves.

"I don't have Hexa in the first place. The ability to confront you is only in Markina Chapter 16, Verse 5. And so chaos willingly becomes the shadow of God, trampled under God's feet, endlessly imitating his form."

Shirone noted that the Bible had thousands of pages.

"Yes, as you guessed. The Devil's Bible contains many abilities. Of course, not as many as you."

Hexa contained infinite possibilities.

"What do you want to say? So... you're going to use all your skills to confront me?"

"That's one of the possibilities. If you don't agree to the deal."

Gyorgi held up a page.

"If you let me go, I'll destroy this ability in front of you. To add, the ability to imitate Hexa is only in Markina Chapter 16, Verse 5."

Shirone frowned.

"Destroy it? It's probably the most crucial ability that allowed you to become the leader of the Catacombs."

If it were Shirone's demon, it wouldn't lie.

"And it's not a bad proposal."

Even if you can gain an advantage in the Taiji, it's certainly troublesome if the opponent can imitate Hexa.

"On the other hand, if you destroy Markina Chapter 16, Verse 5, you can actively respond no matter what the opponent does."

Shirone's eyes calmed down.

"This guy... really resembles me."

"From the beginning, he calculated everything and jumped in. The fact that he could make a deal if he failed in a power struggle, and that I would accept this proposal..."

It was impossible without deeply understanding Yahweh.

That's why it was bitter.

"If he can think and judge like me... can't he understand my heart too?"

Gyorgi pressed.

"It seems you've had enough time to think? Now it's time to make a decision. The choice is yours."

Shirone didn't get caught up.

"Anyway, it's a bluff."

Reading each other's thoughts was too easy.

After being lost in thought for a long time, Shirone took a deep breath and spoke.

"This is the last time. There won't be a second."

"...Deal."

With a snap, the page of Chapter 16, Verse 5 was torn.

"Remember, Yahweh. You may be stronger than me, but evil always wins over good."

Shirone quickly grabbed the paper flying in the wind as the departing enemy watched.

"The Devil's Bible."

The verses of Markina Chapter 16 reflected in Shirone's sad eyes.

Gyorgi, who left the city, found someone waiting 20 meters ahead.

"Are you alive?"

It was Gultan, a member of the Catacombs. He had changed into new clothes, but couldn't hide the scars from burns on his face and hands.

"...Not exactly a welcoming tone."

Gultan stood beside him, but Gyorgi didn't even glance at him and moved forward.

"No, it's fortunate that you prevented a complete retreat. I just... didn't think you'd be alive."

"Hmph, from that human girl."

Honestly, it was close.

The range within which Gultan could move in a quantum state was only about 100 meters.

To escape the mountain that Amy was burning down, he made seven retries.

The total exposure time to the heat was less than a second, but it was still a near-fatal burn.

"I need treatment."

Even as Gultan limped and followed, Gyorgi didn't slow down.

There was no camaraderie among demons, but Gultan felt bad for a different reason.

"By the way, what happened to Gyorgi? Didn't you meet Yahweh?"

"I did. He's strong."

Gyorgi's condition was too intact to say he had fought a fierce battle.

"Is that really all? Didn't you deliberately avoid the fight?"

"What do you mean?"

"Yahweh is certainly strong. But he should know how to win over good with evil."

Gyorgi fell into thought.

"Do I hate Yahweh?"

If an existence became two, they would do everything to kill each other.

"To become the only real one."

But can that be called hatred?

"It's just a retreat for perfection. Gather all the members of the Catacombs within three days. We need to revise our strategy."

"Understood."

Gultan was still suspicious but didn't have the courage to push Gyorgi further.

Using spatial movement, Amy, Caden, and Maya landed in front of Shirone.

Amy asked.

"What happened to Gyorgi?"

"For now... it's like a draw. We don't have to worry for a while."

"A draw?"

Can that be a conclusion against demons?

"Anyway, it seems to have worked out."

Seeing Maya and Caden standing side by side, Shirone roughly guessed the situation.

Maya approached awkwardly.

"Sorry, I got you involved in something dangerous. I have no face to see you and Amy."

"It's okay. I'm just glad you're safe."

Caden approached.

"Shirone, I..."

As soon as he was within range, Caden's right arm flew towards Shirone's face.

"Ugh!"

Shirone, who tilted his head, asked.

"What?"

But the surprised person was rather Caden, who started wrestling with his right arm.

"Damn it! Stay still!"

Shirone, who was watching blankly, asked.

"What happened?"

"That..."

After hearing the situation from Amy, Shirone nodded.

"It's like Alien Hand Syndrome."

As if it were a lie, Caden's right hand calmed down, and he turned back with shining eyes.

"Alien Hand?"

"It's a problem that occurs in the brain's information processing stage. In the past, it was mistakenly called that because it was thought to be possessed by a god. Most of it is known to be caused by trauma."

Caden's case was different.

"It's common for emotions to change the body. But for the mechanism of the thought circuit to change..."

Shirone could understand the level of stress Caden had experienced at that moment.

"Let's go to Vashka for an examination. I'll also look for a treatment method with Seriel."

"No, I like it as it is now. If I can protect Maya, I can give up anything."

Maya's face turned bright red.

Shirone, who was looking at them with satisfaction, corrected his expression and raised both hands.

"Miracle Stream."

The light smoke gathered into two in the air and permeated Caden and Maya's heads.

"Huff!"

As the light of Agape settled in their hearts, hot tears flowed down Maya's cheeks.

"Ah..."

It was Shirone's love.

"For the time being, emotional illness won't manifest. But there's a condition. I need your help, Maya and Caden."

Maya nodded.

"I'll do anything I can. No, I'll do even what I can't."

Seeing Maya, who had regained her vitality in front of Shirone, Caden felt a pang in his heart.

"I can't make Maya smile."

The reason his right arm was still might be because he couldn't even be jealous of Shirone.

"Don't be greedy."

Just because he found a way to protect her didn't mean he escaped fate.

"If I get obsessed, Maya will be in danger. Just think about protecting her safely."

Putting personal feelings aside, Shirone and his party left the city for their next mission.

"Take Maya to Vashka. I'll be waiting at the palace."

After hearing the explanation of the simultaneous events, Caden gripped the sword handle and spoke.

"Don't worry. Maya will be safe."

Probably, but Shirone was more worried about Caden.

"It's not time to reveal it yet. It's better this way for now."

The fate of the Red Cross Star, the true meaning contained in its law, was told by the history of Omega.

"Yes. See you in the capital."

Caden, who had escaped fate, drew his sword with his right hand and initiated spatial movement with Maya.

As Amy looked up at the flash of light curving into the sky, she smiled contentedly.

"Still, it's good. Caden gets to be with the person he likes." Shirone couldn't bring himself to say that it would have been better to give up on Maya.

Screeeech!

The noise of spatial movement, which had been fading into the end of the sky, returned with the flash.

"Huh?"

An old man with a benevolent face, hiding intense eyes, appeared with his hands behind his back.

"Mr. Lampa."

It was Arnold Lampa, the world's greatest information mage.

"Your Excellency."

The reason he, who was handling numerous matters, sought out Shirone and Amy's party was one.

"We have found Wena Wizard."

The only person with the purity to oppose Habitz as a natural awakener.

"So, they were alive."

"Yes. They are currently in the Kingdom of Stang. The Ten Mages of the Underworld are simultaneously monitoring and guarding them."

The System Inspection Department had five 1-star residents and an equal number of satellites.

These were called the Ten Mages of the Underworld, and unlike other stars, they were trained by Freed.

Amy spoke.

"Finally, a full-fledged mission. What kind of child is wizard? It's a girl, right? Is she cute?"

"Well..."

The words that Lampa, who had lived for 112 years, pulled out of his mind were just two.

"Well, yes."
