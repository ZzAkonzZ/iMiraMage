Chapter 1165:

Miro arrived at the port, the Tamo Syndicate’s territory, and hid behind a shipping container.

“Arius, go check how many are there.”

Woof.

Arius had always been clever, but lately, he seemed to understand human speech better than ever.

Behind a crate containing thirty bombs, Shirone sat with an indifferent expression.

“Brother, if we use our abilities to raid the armory, the adults won’t have to get hurt.”

“… Hmph.”

The only reason they could move crates weighing hundreds of kilograms was because of their supernatural powers.

Arius returned from his patrol and let out a small bark.

“Let’s go, Brother.”

The two of them activated their powers at the same time, moving the crate of bombs.

Among the twenty or so armories, they targeted the one with the weakest security.

‘That’s the one.’

Two armed Tamo Syndicate guards stood at the entrance, while a watchman was stationed in the tower.

‘That’s the problem.’

Miro turned to Shirone.

“Brother, I’ll climb up and push that guy off. You drop the bombs. Can you do it?”

She never wavered.

“… It’s dangerous.”

“It’s fine. By now, Dad must have figured it out. The merchant alliance will have no choice but to move. While the enemies are drawn to their base, the adults will seize weapons. And before they finish, I’ll end it myself.”

Arius let out a whimper.

The real Miro—the one from reality—was someone who would do anything to protect what was precious to her.

‘I’m the strongest one here, so I have to do it. If the adults step in, people will die.’

Of course, she was putting her own life at risk, but if her theory was correct, it was more than possible.

“I’m going. I’m counting on you.”

As Miro moved, Shirone slowly scanned the armory.

‘I have to do this?’

He didn’t want to think about the reason.

He had no memories, but it felt like he had once decided to accept the world in the most passive way possible.

‘Why?’

At times, it felt like life was all that mattered. But at other times, he didn’t want to dig too deeply into it.

Shirone silently sent the thirty bombs toward the targeted armory.

“Hmm?”

The watchman squinted as he noticed creeping shadows across the armory roof.

“What’s that?”

His confused expression suddenly changed as his eyes widened in alarm.

“Enem—!”

His voice was trapped in an air barrier as Miro reached the watchtower and struck with a melee attack.

‘Now!’

Her heart was made of steel.

No hesitation, no doubts, no inner turmoil—because of that, even in her twelve-year-old body, she could overpower an adult.

“Argh!”

Hit from behind, the watchman fell off the railing before he could react.

His voice had been cut off, but she couldn’t silence the dull thud of his body hitting the ground.

“Who’s there?!”

The guards at the armory turned toward the noise and spotted Miro.

“Shoot!”

They didn’t realize she was a child, but even if they had, it wouldn’t have changed a thing.

The moment bullets rained from all directions, Miro’s focus reached its peak.

‘I didn’t tell Dad about this, but…’

Above her head, a divine manifestation of Avalokiteshvara with a thousand arms appeared, bearing the face of a child.

‘I can see it.’

Time shattered into countless fragments, and as her consciousness expanded across every timeline—

‘Deflect them all.’

Each time Avalokiteshvara’s hands moved like an afterimage, sparks burst from thin air.

Ping! Ping! Ping! Ping!

She deflected the bullets, but a painful expression crossed her face.

“Ugh…!”

The impact was absorbed by the divine figure, but it felt as if her own palms had been pierced.

The Tamo Syndicate members were dumbfounded.

“What the hell? She… she blocked bullets…?”

At that moment, Miro shouted to Shirone.

“Brother! Now!”

The crisp ping of safety pins being pulled out echoed in succession, and the syndicate members instinctively looked up.

“Huh?”

The moment they realized it was a bomb, nobody needed to say anything.

“Ahhh! Run!”

With a thunderous explosion, flames engulfed the scene, and the armory doors burst open.

“We did it!”

Having already descended from the tower, Miro ran toward Shirone and Arius.

“Move! That hurt like hell!”

Even in a dream world, physical force operated under different principles—but the sheer power remained unchanged.

“What happened?!”

Syndicate members swarmed toward the explosion, and one of the survivors barely managed to speak.

“A bomb! And that kid… she deflected bullets!”

“What nonsense! Chase them!”

Realizing this wasn’t just an ordinary intrusion but an assault with firearms, the syndicate members were on high alert.

“Brother! Hurry!”

Woof! Woof!

Exposed, Shirone and the others bolted toward the Tamo Syndicate’s base.

They pushed their bodies forward with telekinetic force, but their pursuers were far faster than ordinary men.

“Stop right there!”

As dozens of men fired simultaneously, Miro’s heart sank.

‘Huh?’

It felt as if the bullets weren’t reaching her.

Sensing the anomaly, she turned back—only to see Shirone standing still.

“Brother! You’re in danger!”

The second volley of gunfire began, and Miro’s vision filled with countless flashes.

‘I can do this.’

Shirone was sure.

Whether it was thirty bombs or three hundred, in the realm of the mind, mass didn’t matter.

What mattered was perception.

‘Speed is the same.’

The human eye couldn’t track bullets, but the mind’s eye could.

‘I’ll catch them.’

The moment he thought it, hundreds of bullets froze in midair before her.

“Keep shooting! Shoot!”

The syndicate members emptied their magazines, but when they lowered their rifles in disbelief—

“What the…?”

A black wall of bullets floated in front of Shirone.

“Run!”

As soon as he turned and fled, the bullets clattered to the ground.

The bewildered syndicate members stood in stunned silence—until one man, his voice filled with frustration, screamed.

“See! I was right!”

Gaold and Kangnan arrived at the port.

Half of the Tamo Syndicate was chasing after Shirone’s group, while the other half remained to guard the armory.

“Damn it! Who the hell are these guys?”

“I know one of them. It’s that brat, Miro. She’s Sein’s daughter. Annoying little girl.”

“Damn it. If we catch her…!”

Before he could finish speaking, Gaold leapt from a container and drove a dagger into his throat.

“Who’s there?!”

A syndicate member turned and fired his rifle wildly, shredding his own comrade’s body into a bloody mess.

Gaold had already moved, slipping behind the shooter and slashing his throat with a blade.

“Ghhhk…”

Hearing the gunfire, more enemies rushed in.

“Intruder!”

Even with his life on the line, Gaold walked into the armory without hesitation.

“Catch.”

Kangnan caught a heavy rifle in both hands.

“Do you even know how to use this?”

Even in what was supposedly the "real world," she had no experience with guns.

“No idea.”

Gaold cocked his rifle and stepped outside, pulling the trigger and unleashing a hail of bullets.

“Damn it! Fire back! Shoot him!”

The enemy's counterattack riddled the container with holes, several bullets grazing his ear.

Kangnan yanked him back.

“Are you crazy? If you get hit, you’re dead!”

Gaold picked up another rifle from the armory, yanking the bolt back as he muttered.

“So are they. If we lose momentum, we’ll die before we even get a shot off.”

Gaold knew how to fight.

As he stepped forward and opened fire again, the enemies hesitated—this time, there was no return fire.

Kangnan suddenly realized something.

‘Right…’

He wasn’t fighting because he believed he could win.

“Ha, this is pretty good.”

Gaold quickly adapted to using firearms, grabbing any weapon within reach and firing at will.

“That bastard…!”

A Tamo Syndicate member hefted a bazooka onto his shoulder, standing atop a crate.

With a streak of fire trailing behind, a grenade launcher shell hurtled toward the armory’s entrance.

“Shit—!”

BOOM!

The explosion sent shockwaves through the area as the syndicate members opened fire en masse.

“Check the bodies.”

Two low-ranking members cautiously stepped through the smoke.

“What the hell? No one's here—”

In that instant, two ghostly hands appeared behind them, strangling their necks.

“Ghhhk!”

Gaold dropped one of them to the ground, causing the other to spin around in panic.

“H-Hey! What’s go—”

A dull thud landed on his solar plexus. Kangnan pulled his head down and drove her knee into his face.

Crack!

The sound of a broken neck echoed, and his body crumpled backward.

“Where’s Miro?”

Gaold pressed a knife to the fallen man's eye.

“G-Gaold! It’s me! Asto!”

Peering through the smoke, Gaold saw that it was a delivery boy from the marketplace.

“Where’s Miro?”

A dagger pierced into Asto’s left cheek, stabbing through his tongue and emerging from the right cheek.

“Hiiiiiik! I-I dunno! S-She ran away, fher real!”

Already gone?

“P-Please! Spare me, Gaold! I… I just—”

Gaold sighed.

“Asto, listen. If fighting today won’t change anything tomorrow, I don’t bother fighting.”

He hated unnecessary trouble.

“Uhhhhhghh!”

Desperate, Asto clawed toward a pistol lying on the ground, dragging it toward him with his fingertips.

“But if someone messes with my life—”

The moment Asto grasped the gun, Gaold ripped the dagger through his cheek, splitting it open.

“—I just kill them.”

“Ghhhk!”

His face split apart, and in the next instant, Gaold drove the dagger through his throat, severing his spine.

“Ghhh… Ghhk—!”

Asto’s entire body convulsed, his pupils rolling back as his life drained away.

人

Kangnan patted Gaold’s back.

“He was going to kill you.”

“I know. Doesn’t matter. I would’ve killed him anyway.”

Gaold flicked the blood from his knife.

“What pisses me off is… if you're going to interfere in someone’s life, at least be ready to die for it.”

As Kangnan silently watched him, the smoke from the explosion finally cleared.

Dozens of syndicate members stood with their guns trained on them.

“… Shit.”

Even if Sein had gone to summon the Merchant Alliance, they wouldn’t arrive for another twenty minutes at least.

“Who the hell are you? Actually, never mind. That doesn’t matter. What matters is the punishment you’re about to receive.”

One of the syndicate members jerked his chin.

“Bring the woman forward.”

A rifle-wielding thug stepped toward Kangnan, making Gaold scowl.

“Run. I’ll hold them off.”

“What? No way. I’m not leaving you—”

Gaold didn’t even listen.

Instead, he charged forward.

‘This is why you guys are useless.’

They didn’t know how to fight.

The moment Gaold initiated the charge, all the tangled emotions in Kangnan’s mind vanished.

“Khh—!”

She dashed sideways, while every gun barrel locked onto Gaold.

Ratatatatatatat!

The roaring gunfire echoed across the port.

‘Gaold!’

Kangnan’s heart nearly stopped as she turned back toward him.

But what she saw was—

“… Huh?”

Gaold stood completely unharmed.

“Krrrrrrrr…”

His hair had turned completely white.

He was stopping bullets.

“Keep firing! Keep shooting!”

A barrier of air surrounded him, deflecting every bullet in a wide radius.

Kangnan’s eyes snapped upward.

“… What the hell is that?”

Above them—

Countless eyes stared down from the darkened sky.

“KRAAAAAAAAH!”

With an overwhelming surge of pain, Gaold instinctively raised his five fingers.

Air Gun.

An onslaught of high-pressure air bullets exploded from his fingertips, ripping through the enemies ahead.

“Run! Someone stop him—!”

The syndicate members turned to flee—

But none of them escaped.

One by one, they were torn apart.

“Khhhhh…!”

Gaold, overwhelmed by the shock, collapsed to his knees.

The eyes in the sky slowly faded.

Kangnan finally understood.

“Awakening.”

The more their real-world abilities manifested, the weaker the barrier of the 1.5th floor became.

‘And outside…’

Beyond that fragile barrier—

Ymir was waiting.

Waiting to consume this world.
