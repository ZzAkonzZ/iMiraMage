Chapter 1183: The Meaning of the Incident(1)

Unusual phenomena occurring around pyramids scattered across the world were reported to the Holy War.

The chieftain of the Southern Tribal Union, Untara, spoke.

"The intervention of the gods."

"Due to the abnormalities in the pyramids, people's emotions have reached their peak. If powerful magic flows into the world, it will affect all systems."

Untara's expression was grave.

"The world will boil like a cauldron. All incidents will converge into one—before even humanity realizes it."

The unification of the Law signified the victory of the gods.

"What if we retrieve Elicia? If we use that power to attack Jive, then perhaps..."

"Hmph."

Untara took a sip of tea.

"That’s exactly why we fail. Trying to solve a problem without even understanding what it is."

When Shirone was asked what he would do with Elicia’s power, he replied,

"Nothing."

After a long silence, Shirone raised his head.

"I will do nothing."

It seemed like an underwhelming answer at first, but as Untara reflected on his words, realization dawned.

It was the answer he had been searching for.

Untara spoke.

"How arrogant it is to believe that a single human can change the world. Yahweh is not a belief—it is merely an immense wind. One must simply do what they are capable of."

"Is that why you let it go?"

"Saying 'I will do this' or 'I will do that'—in the end, nothing is ever perfect. Someone will always be sacrificed, and someone else will always be enraged."

Unification was impossible.

"There is only one Elicia, but Yahweh exists across the entire planet. The moment you start prioritizing, power becomes distorted. I believe Elicia has already found its rightful owner."

"Will his wish come true?"

Untara sipped his tea, lost in thought.

‘Countless events are overlapping. Everyone is trying to fulfill their own desires. Is God… saying that even human hearts can be rewritten by Law?’

He looked up.

"I do not know. But one thing is certain—every incident from now on will operate toward a singular purpose. And when we finally reach the end, the outcome that remains…"

Will it belong to humans, or to the gods?

"The end is in sight."

Untara forced himself to ignore the truth—that the unification of Law was progressing faster than the unification of humanity.

Shirone’s eyes snapped open.

"Huff—!"

He hurriedly sat up and looked around. He was inside a military tent, where a campfire was burning.

‘What happened?’

After being thrown from the Ivory Tower alongside Baal, Shirone had landed in the occupied territory of Huig.

‘Ah—?’

Just before he fell into the hands of the demons, he recalled Minerva appearing to save him.

"You're awake?"

Minerva entered the tent, pulling back the fabric.

"What happened?"

"You lost consciousness just as we arrived at the hideout. You were in a really dangerous state."

Even Shirone’s mental fortitude had been unable to withstand the impact.

"There's no time. We need to save Great Star."

Minerva remained silent.

"Why? What happened?"

"See for yourself."

The moment he stepped out of the tent, Shirone was shocked.

Just 100 meters away, demon forces and Corona soldiers were engaged in a fierce battle.

"We’ve been completely pushed back. We lost the war."

Among the soldiers, Shirone even spotted some of the Ivory Tower's Stars, making it even harder to believe.

"How…?"

"It happened suddenly. Less than twenty minutes ago. The demons feel significantly stronger than before."

Shirone’s mind raced.

As if awakening from anesthesia, his consciousness sharpened, and he connected the events together.

‘The pyramids.’

Incidents overlapped according to one's emotions.

"Mika."

"Yes?"

"How many people have been affected by the pyramids?"

"Eighty-three million, two hundred seventy-three. Just moments ago, twenty-eight more reached the death route."

It was far greater than Elicia.

‘Events unfolding under divine will… I don't know their exact purpose. But—’

Now, it was the demons’ world.

"Shirone, we need to leave now. If the Ivory Tower’s Stars are wiped out here, there is no future."

"Their target is Great Star. Without intellect, even the definitions of good and evil will vanish."

The Ivory Tower’s fate would be even worse.

"Either way, we must flee for now. The soldiers of Corona will buy us time. You need to get out—"

Shirone shook his head.

"No. There’s nowhere left to run. The magic of the underworld is crossing into reality."

Even Jin Seoug Eum could not stop it.

"First, we eliminate the demons here and return to the Ivory Tower. Rescuing Great Star is the priority."

"Easier said than done. Those things have basically been promoted by two whole ranks. It’s like turning a regular soldier into a company commander—no, even a battalion commander into a division general."

"I'll handle it. There aren't too many. I can hold them off for now."

"But you’re injured—!"

As Shirone leaped toward the battlefield, he glanced back at Minerva and smiled.

"You’ve become even prettier."

Minerva wasn’t the type to blush at compliments, but this time, her face heated up.

The changing Laws of the world had freed her from the fate of a witch.

"You little brat…"

Her appearance hadn't changed, but her expression seemed much softer than before.

‘But that doesn't change anything.’

With a bitter smile, Minerva pushed off the ground and followed Shirone.

‘I am still a murderer.’

Even if the Laws changed, she believed she must still take responsibility for her own life.

Meanwhile, Shirone, now at the battlefield, finally understood the full extent of the demons’ power.

‘We're being completely overwhelmed.’

Corona soldiers were falling, and the Ivory Tower’s Stars were struggling desperately.

Twelve Mini from the Unified Universe Department cast Scale Magic, enlarging her body.

"You bastards!"

She grew to 10 meters, 50 meters, 100 meters, and slammed her fist into the ground.

Boom!

The impact shook the earth, but the demons instantly crawled up her arms.

"Get off! You disgusting things!"

She tried to shake them off like ants, but they tore into her flesh in an instant.

"Aaaaagh!"

At that moment, the Miracle Stream surged, sweeping away the demons and slamming them into the ground.

Mini’s face brightened as she looked down at the ground.

"5 Star!"

From the valley between her breasts, Mini’s satellite, Ariana, suddenly popped out.

"Here, here! Come inside quickly!"

Shirone smiled awkwardly and said,

"Next time."

"Oh my!"

The joke ended there. With his eyes sharpening in focus, Shirone leaped into the battlefield.

"Step back."

As Mini took a step back, covering dozens of meters, Hand of God rose into the air.

On the radiant palm of light, a Photon Cannon began to form.

"Yahweh! Kill Yahweh!"

"Kuahaha! I’ve become stronger! Now, I can finally kill you, detestable—"

A beam of light struck down.

With a deafening explosion, a shockwave erupted, sending demons flying in a perfect radial pattern.

"Whew…"

No matter how many times he saw it, the power was always astonishing. However, the demons this time were different from before.

From within the massive crater, numerous demons that had survived began to emerge.

‘Is this all it took?’

"Yahweh, I will kill you."

As the demons crawled out of the rubble, Shirone closed his eyes.

"Whew."

And when he opened them again—

"You’re going to die…"

A massive sphere of light, incomparable to before, filled the sky.

"No, this can’t be!"

Like the break of dawn, the horizon of the Huig Occupied Zone swelled with brilliance.

"Advance! Push forward!"

A blizzard raged around the Ivory Tower, but the strengthened demons charged through without hesitation.

Approximately 200,000 demons, with even the weakest soldiers now possessing the strength of former division commanders.

As demons poured through the narrow entrance, Great Star and Xing remained motionless.

Umji and Yangji, 4-Star residents who assisted Xing, sensed the situation below.

"Xing, at this rate, we’re in danger."

The stars of the Law Department and Balance Department were defending the entrance, but the power of the demons was overwhelming.

Great Star spoke.

"Xing, it's time to let go of your misunderstanding. If this continues, the Ivory Tower will fall."

Xing already knew.

‘Shirone… do you think the same?’

As long as Xing remained still, Great Star, bound by the power of Law, would also be unable to act.

"Why?"

Great Star asked.

"You won’t accomplish anything. Binding me is nothing more than stubborn arrogance."

"You’re right."

The stars and residents had come together in unity, but in the end, it was Xing who had to make the final decision.

"I might be wrong. I won’t deny it. But I know this—if I step aside, there will be no one left to take my place."

If she was right…

‘If 99.99% of the world was wrong, and I was the only one who was right… if the moment I changed my mind meant the end of everything…’

Xing spoke.

"Let’s die together, Great Star. I’ve already accepted it."

Great Star’s eyes filled with sorrow.

"Climb! Overwhelm them with numbers!"

The demons launched a full-scale assault on the 23rd floor of the Ivory Tower.

Arte swung his fan, conjuring a massive gust that swept the demons away like an iron wall.

"Rrraaaaahh!"

But continuously casting magic had pushed Arte’s mental endurance to its limit.

"I can’t hold out for more than ten minutes. If we don’t do something within that time, the Ivory Tower will fall."

"You fools! More demons will arrive soon! You’ve already lost!"

Winning and losing were common in war, but this loss was different—it would be catastrophic for humanity.

‘Evil will no longer be evil.’

Arte gritted his teeth.

"Wind Fang."

The air on the 23rd floor spun into a razor-sharp vortex, tearing through the demons’ flesh.

"Steel Will."

At that moment, Arte felt as if his mind had split in two, along with the magic he wielded.

"Ugh!"

As he staggered, an enormous force of sword energy swept across the ground.

The Black Jiangshi dragged Arte backward, while a massive crack ran up the entire height of the 23rd-floor wall.

The demons, momentarily stunned, turned their heads—there, stepping forward, was Baal, the 1st Legion Commander.

"Commander!"

As the army knelt before him, Hokgangshi bit his lip.

‘Damn it.’

If division commanders had risen to the level of battalion commanders, then what had become of a Legion Commander?

"Stand aside."

Baal spoke to the Ivory Tower’s stars.

"Clear the way. You cannot defeat me. I am here to negotiate with Great Star."

Tanjura asked.

"You think we will step aside?"

"Do you think I am threatening you with your lives? Hardly. This is your last chance. The last opportunity to negotiate before I open the Demon Realm."

‘Deadly Cross.’

If Baal opened the Demon Realm, it wouldn’t just be the Ivory Tower that fell—humanity itself would face extinction.

"So, the battle of evil begins."

But Baal’s words only steeled the resolve of the Ivory Tower’s stars.

"Intelligence does not yield to threats. Whether humanity perishes or the universe collapses…"

Tanjura lunged forward.

"What is right is right."

An example must be made.

"The sin of denying righteousness—death."

Baal was bound by Law, and from the side, Daehou, one of the satellites, activated the execution guillotine.

"Heh."

In the next instant, everyone witnessed the severed bodies of Tanjura and Daehou falling to the ground.

"Intelligence is dead."

Baal, holding his blade at his side, turned to the remaining stars blocking his path.

"Will you oppose me?"

Shirone, flying at incredible speed from the Huig Occupied Zone, widened his eyes.

Demons swarmed around the Ivory Tower, forming a mass as vast as an ocean.

Minerva approached on jet.

"The entrance is blocked!"

The demons’ resilience had increased so much that breaking through with brute force was no longer an option.

‘Great Star, just hold on a little longer.’

Unleashing the Judicial Halo, Ataraxia, Shirone activated physical amplification and dived straight to the ground.

"That lunatic!"

As Minerva swerved, Shirone crashed right into the heart of the demons.

"W-What?!"

The demons, realizing he was Yahweh, bared their fangs in triumph—

But then—

"Whew…"

Shirone exhaled, releasing the Breath of Light—his face illuminated with an overwhelming radiance.

At that sight, the demons instantly turned pale.

"Move."

He hadn’t directed it at anyone in particular, but the demons felt as if the world itself had split apart.
