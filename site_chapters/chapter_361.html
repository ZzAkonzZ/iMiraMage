Chapter 361: Magic Society (7)

"Yes, that's why you're here. So, first... hmm?"

Gaold turned to look at Gando and Plu, who were standing awkwardly. After a moment of thought, he spoke in a puzzled voice.

"Why are you two here? Don't you know this is a Level 1 security area?"

"Ah, well..."

As Gando hesitated, Kangnan sighed.

‘He's forgotten again.’

Shirone was a key resource that could bring groundbreaking changes to this project. Even if he didn't show it, it was clear that Gaold was quite excited.

"Go back. We have some private matters to attend to."

"Yes, understood."

Gaold waved his hand dismissively, and Gando awkwardly walked back to the elevator. Even as a senior member of Gaold's elite guard, he had no choice but to follow orders.

However, Plu remained in place.

‘I was here the whole time. The Chairman brought me along.’

She knew Gaold had memory issues, but that didn't make it any less hurtful. After all, he still remembered Shirone, whom he had just met 10 minutes ago.

"Plu, what are you doing? Let's go."

Gando spoke in a worried voice, but Plu stood her ground.

For over two years, she had served in Gaold's elite guard, completing numerous missions. She couldn't accept being dismissed like this.

"Please let me stay."

Gaold, who had been walking towards Shirone, stopped abruptly. He slowly turned his head, his eyes swirling with something abnormal.

"What did you just say?"

Plu, emboldened, shouted even louder.

"Please let me stay! We are also your subordinates, serving you, Mr. Gaold!"

Gando, his face pale, shook Plu's shoulder.

"What's wrong with you, Plu? Are you crazy?"

Plu wasn't standing there calmly. Her heartbeat echoed in her ribs, and her fear-stricken eyes avoided Gaold's gaze, looking up at the ceiling instead.

"Heh heh, heh heh heh."

Gaold bent his knees, hunching his neck like a beast eyeing its prey. Heat waves seemed to rise from his shoulders and head.

"Plu."

"Y-Yes!"

"Since I became the Chairman of the Magic Association, I've never been involved in an assault incident. Do you know why?"

Plu slowly lowered her gaze to look at Gaold. Seeing his grotesquely grinning face, she felt a paralyzing fear.

"Because if I hit someone, they die."

Gando flinched and stepped back.

‘Oh no! It's starting.’

Plu's vision darkened, and she saw a hellish landscape of burning sulfur. The agonizing screams of tortured souls clawed at her brain.

"Ah, ah..."

Plu's pupils rolled back. Her knees wobbled like a beaten dog, and the tears streaming down her face felt as hot as blood.

‘So scared...!’

She felt she would do anything to escape this moment. But that would be a betrayal of Gaold. Everything she had built would crumble in an instant.

"Sob! Sob!"

With all her strength, Plu shouted with her eyes tightly shut.

"I respect you!"

The hellish vision vanished.

But her spirit, which had flown far away, showed no sign of returning. In a state of trance, words spilled out of her mouth.

"I respect you! I've admired you since I started learning magic! I'll do anything for you, Chairman! So please let me stay!"

‘Plu...’

Gando looked at his subordinate with pity.

Gaold was too far beyond reach to be a life goal. Even as a certified 4th-grade magician, Gando found it hard to suppress his trembling in Gaold's presence.

But this time, he mustered the courage to step forward for his cherished subordinate.

"Please, let us stay! Don't doubt our resolve!"

Gando bowed beside Plu.

Gaold stared at them, lost in thought. But in reality, he wasn't thinking about anything.

"Do as you wish."

His easy acceptance left Gando stunned. Plu, still not fully understanding the situation, continued to sniffle.

Kangnan clicked her tongue in annoyance.

‘Honestly... this is why people call him crazy.’

Gaold was bound by nothing. He acted solely on his emotions in the moment.

His brain's memory function was damaged, but he was still alive because no one in the Tormia Kingdom could harm him.

Gaold walked to the center of the bunker and lit a cigar, facing Shirone from a distance. Smoke drifted from his parted lips like a ghost.

"So, Kangnan brought you because she saw potential. Now I'll judge for myself. If you meet my standards, we might have a good relationship."

Being evaluated out of the blue wasn't pleasant, but it wasn't unacceptable given Gaold's position as Chairman.

What worried Shirone was what Gaold had done to Plu earlier. What had happened to make the usually confident Plu so terrified and tearful?

‘Is this really okay? He seems a bit strange.’

When Shirone didn't respond, Gaold spoke in a bored tone.

"I'm not forcing you. We don't need anyone with weak will. If you don't want to, you can leave now."

"What will you evaluate?"

"There's only one thing I expect from you. Show me Ataraxia."

Just as Shirone had expected. If a certified 1st-grade Grand Magician was interested in a mere student, it had to be because of Ataraxia.

"Do it at full power. This might be the only chance in your life."

‘Full power?’

At first, Shirone didn't understand. But seeing the aura emanating from Gaold, a troubling thought crossed his mind.

"Are you telling me to shoot it at you?"

"Yes. Show me your best skill."

Shirone's face turned incredulous.

To him, Ataraxia was a siege spell. It wasn't something to be cast on a person unless he intended to kill them.

"But this isn't the 2000 system."

"Of course not. That's why I brought you here. The true value of magic is best confirmed through the body."

Now Shirone was certain. Gaold was insane. Unless he intended to make him a murderer, how could he make such a request?

"It's too dangerous. You could get hurt."

Gaold squinted and exhaled smoke, then suddenly burst into laughter.

"Hahaha! That's the funniest joke I've heard in a while. Coming from a kid who hasn't even grown hair yet."

Shirone's face turned red with anger. Whether it was a joke or not, it was completely belittling.

Gaold was indeed the strongest magician in Tormia. But Ataraxia was also the power of an archangel.

"I'm serious. You could die."

Gaold waved his hand dismissively, as if brushing off the remnants of his laughter.

"Alright, alright. Then from that moment, you'll be the Chairman of the Magic Association. Everyone here will be witnesses. How about that? A super-fast promotion."

‘He's not even listening.’

Shirone focused his mind, determined to show Gaold what Ataraxia was.

A point of starlight appeared in the air, drawing a circle.

As the halo formed, Plu winced. A sharp pain pierced her temples.

‘This is the halo, the embodiment of the Immortal Function.’

The more sensitive one was, the more intense the shock. As expected, Gando seemed to be in considerable pain, squinting one eye and exhaling deeply.

Kangnan, on the other hand, checked the time with a calm expression. Gaold, looking bored, puffed on his cigar, blowing smoke rings as if to say, "Can you do this?"

‘He's the real deal...’

It was Shirone's first time meeting someone completely unaffected by the halo.

Calming his excitement, Shirone decided to go further.

As he concentrated Ataraxia, multicolored flashes converged, constructing concepts beyond human knowledge.

“Wow...”

Plu's mouth fell open.

She had only heard rumors about Ataraxia. She knew it was the power of an archangel, but she hadn't expected a student's spell to be so impressive. Even as the top graduate of the Royal Magic School, she couldn't comprehend even 10% of the concepts embedded in the halo.

‘How is that possible? How many cores does it use?’

Ataraxia was complete. Hundreds of small magic circles rotated like gears, emitting a brilliant light. Gaold, having seen enough, turned to Kangnan.

“How is it?”

Kangnan checked her pocket watch.

“One minute and two seconds.”

“Hmm, a bit faster than before.”

Flicking his cigar, Gaold sent the burning tip flying like a firecracker, hitting the wall. He pulled one leg back and faced Shirone diagonally, lowering his stance slowly.

“Shall we begin? Come at me.”

As Gaold took his stance, the atmosphere in the bunker changed. If a magician was omnipotent in the Spirit Zone, everything here was under Gaold's control.

Even so, Shirone hesitated. Ataraxia was a trigger-type spell. Once he fired the Photon Cannon, he couldn't control it.

Kangnan's voice reached his ears.

“Shirone, I told you. This is a matter of international importance.”

Shirone continued to look at Gaold.

“I can't tell you everything yet, but we're serious too. If your conviction is strong, you can leave now. But if you're bound by moral constraints, know that it's presumptuous.”

“Phew!”

Shirone exhaled deeply, as if expelling all distracting thoughts. There was no way out now. The only option was to rely on the title of First-Class Archmage.

"Let's begin."

As Shirone widened his eyes, a photon was born with a pop sound in front of him. The more the mass concentrated, the louder the sound in his mind became, but he resolutely ignored all the noise and increased the power.

'From now on, even I can't control it.'

Shirone gritted his teeth and unleashed the Photon Cannon.

As the sphere of light passed through Ataraxia, the bunker was bathed in white.

At the same time, Flu's mind went blank. It felt like being thrown in front of a phenomenon from a transcendent world untouched by human feet—a massive tidal wave of mass.

Bang!

A powerful sonic boom split the roar of the flash.

As the atmospheric pressure in the bunker explosively increased, the energy of the Photon Cannon was compressed in front of Gaold's palm. It was the highest level of air-based defensive magic, the Vacuum Press, which only Gaold could perform.

"How... how is this possible...?"

Shirone couldn't believe what he was seeing.

It wasn't that he endured it or dodged it. He literally caught the Photon Cannon with his hand.

The Photon Cannon, having already lost its energy, floated above Gaold's palm. When he clenched his fist, it vanished with a small pop.

Gaold turned to Shirone with a smile and said,

"Failed."

Silence fell. No one in the bunker could speak.

'Failed? Me?'

Shirone realized belatedly that he had never even considered the possibility of failing. He had only hoped that no one would get hurt by Ataraxia.

Gaold, with his coat blown off by the wind pressure, slung it over his shoulder and walked towards the elevator, looking disappointed.

"Kangnan, you were right. That guy is useless. Send him back."
