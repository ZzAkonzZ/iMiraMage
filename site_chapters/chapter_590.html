Chapter 590:

The Twelfth Island of the Mediterranean, the Kingdom of Roblang

With a population of 700,000, this small nation boasts beautiful scenery, with 70% of its citizens working in tourism. Known as a global resort and a tax haven for the wealthy, it hosts secret meetings of well-known politicians in its hotels.

Marika Hotel, Basement Tavern

"Paré!"

Fourteen people were drinking at a table made of six joined together. At the center was Miro, the most famous yet mysterious woman in the world.

"One more drink!"

"Are you challenging me?"

"Of course. I'm not done yet!"

As Miro raised her hand confidently, the men laughed.

"This girl can really hold her liquor. Alright! Kadun, pour another one."

"Leave it to me!"

A man named Kadun climbed onto the table, pouring liquor from two bottles into a 1,000 cc glass, creating a foamy mix. As Miro grabbed the filled cup, the room fell silent.

As Miro's throat moved with each gulp, the onlookers watched in awe. When she finished the last drop and slammed the cup down, everyone stood up and cheered.

"Paré!"

The shout echoed through the tavern.

"Living the good life, Miro."

"Huh?"

Miro turned to see Sane, who had entered the tavern, and her face lit up.

"Hey, my friend! What brings you here?"

Miro's face was flushed, seemingly from the alcohol.

"Paré!"

Someone else downed a drink, and cheers erupted from the other side of the wall. Miro flopped onto a bed.

"Ah, I'm getting drunk. Maybe I had too much polka."

Sane wasn't interested in what polka was.

"Is everything going well?"

Miro pointed to the ceiling.

"They're on the 14th floor. The discussion is taking longer than expected. It might take two more days to reach a conclusion."

Sane pulled out a chair and sat down.

"Enjoying yourself is fine, but be careful. Many people are after you."

"Haha, who would dare challenge me?"

Few in the world could harm the great Miro, but seeing her so relaxed today made Sane worry.

'Well, she was imprisoned for 20 years.'

It seemed Miro's naturally free-spirited nature had exploded after being confined for so long.

'Maybe the world's greatest troublemaker has been unleashed.'

Miro asked, "So, what's up? You came in person. Is there a problem?"

"A bit. Fermi visited me."

Miro smiled mysteriously.

"He took the plans for Istas. Specifically, the plans with Goffin's door."

"Ha, I figured."

"I sent them to Armin as you said, but are you sure it's okay?"

"Of course. What could go wrong?"

"An infinite loop of space-time. It's an event Goffin tried to protect. If external factors interfere and twist the event, the world could end."

Despite the ominous statement, Miro remained silent.

"What exactly happened at Istas?"

"I don't know."

Sane tilted his head.

"You don't know?"

"Something happened at Istas, but I don't remember. It disappeared with Goffin's erasure. Now, experiencing the world, I can only vaguely guess."

"Is that why you didn't get involved?"

"The upper echelon of Istas contains the me from 19 years ago. If the event is twisted in any way, the world will change. That's why I gave Fermi the plans."

Miro's eyes grew distant.

"If the world's history is twisted, it's up to those involved to destroy it."

"Does that include Shirone?"

"Possibly."

Sane, deep in thought, asked, "Who is Shirone? Goffin's descendant?"

"I don't know. Only the me from 19 years ago knows."

Sane poured a drink and handed it to Miro.

"You're not running away?"

Miro raised an eyebrow as she accepted the drink.

"Running away?"

"Yolga."

"I told you, I don't remember that time."

"But you remember before entering the upper echelon, right?"

Sane was persistent.

"Yes."

Miro recalled Yolga's face.

"She was lovely, kind, and just a good person. Who could hate Yolga?"

"Then she went missing."

Sane approached Miro.

"What's the chance you killed her?"

"Haha, do you really want to know?"

"I'm just worried about you. Why did Goffin choose you over Yolga if she was so good?"

"Because she was good."

Miro downed the strong drink in one go.

"Maybe I did kill her. But even if I could turn back time, my decision would be the same."

The drunkenness vanished from Miro's eyes as she looked at Sane.

"If I killed her, it was because she deserved it. I was meant to be Goffin's successor."

Early in the morning, the graduating class was bustling.

Participants of Scramble Royal rejoined the fierce competition, while a few left the school for personal reasons.

Notable among them were Nade and Kaiden.

The winning team was determined to make up for their seven-day absence, while the losing team had to find ways to lower their scores below Nade's.

'Just lower it before the graduation exam. Being one point behind Nade is the best outcome.'

The problem was not knowing when Nade would return.

"Don't worry. He just needs time to think."

Iruki, Nade's closest friend, reassured Amy.

Meanwhile, Shirone, who had refused the evaluation, left his dorm early and arrived at the meeting place.

Anchal, without her eyepatch, was waiting.

"You broke the seal?"

"That's why I brought the Majingan. Where's Armin?"

Shirone showed the Kubrick cube in response.

"Now tell me. What do you mean Goffin is in the upper echelon?"

"I'll explain on the way."

As Anchal turned towards Istas, Shirone objected.

"No. Explain first. I'll go with you after that."

Checking the time, Anchal turned back.

"Alright. It's a matter of life and death for you too."

Anchal reconstructed the possible events from 19 years ago at Istas.

"Goffin… was here?"

"Of course. You saw Goffin's door."

Even so, hearing it didn't feel real.

The gap between a figure erased from everyone's memory and a mage known worldwide 19 years ago was hard to bridge.

"It's assumed that among those who pursued Miro to Istas were Yolga and her group."

"Yolga?"

"Fermi's mother."

"Oh…"

"What's certain is that Tormia Kingdom secretly sent troops to kill Miro. The secret unit Mars from the Special Forces. Each one is a powerful assassin."

Shirone recalled the scenes he saw at Istas through Four Symbols Explosion.

"There were definitely Tormia Kingdom insignias."

"The upper echelon of Istas doesn't open unless very complex conditions are met. But your ability to manipulate time ignored those conditions and brought past events to the surface. This will help in some way."

Anchal asked one last time.

"Are you coming? My explanation ends here."

After a moment of thought, Shirone finally nodded.

Rumble! Rumble!

Amid the ever-changing structures of Istas, Fermi and Lycan were talking.

"The upper echelon only opens with a specific structure. At the end, you need Stop magic."

"Very meticulous."

"More than meticulous. It wasn't designed to be explored."

"An infinite loop of time."

Lycan still couldn't grasp it.

"If that's real, you could have everything in the world."

He was more interested in the time itself than the event at Istas.

"Yes. The event keeps repeating. We're no exception. So…"

Fermi threw a glowing red orb.

"You can replicate items endlessly."

Lycan caught the orb, looking puzzled.

"Didn't you say it was worth 10 billion gold?"

"It is worth 10 billion gold."

"This? It doesn't look like a jewel. Is it an object?"

"Correct. 'The Last Scream of the Jaeger Family.' I bought it at the Curia auction for twice its market price."

"Twice? That's a loss. The high price of an object is due to its rarity. If you replicate it…"

"True, but 'The Last Scream of the Jaeger Family' is an exception. If the owner is in danger, it sends a signal. It's a consumable closely tied to life, so replicating it hundreds of times would make a fortune quickly."

"Hmm, I see."

Lycan was finally satisfied.

"Move along the designated coordinates, then return to this warehouse and put it in the safe."

Fermi pointed to a sturdy safe.

"Then the event repeats, and it keeps accumulating. But be careful. The people involved in this event are highly skilled. It's best to bypass them."

"Hehe, no problem."

Lycan pulled up his rubber suit to cover his nose.

'He's reliable.'

Gunjou Lycan, the eldest son of Gunjou Bedium, was considered the strongest hitman in the Black Line.

With that trust, Fermi asked, "What will you do with the money?"

"Charity."

"Haha! What?"

Fermi thought Lycan was an even worse villain than himself.

"Isn't it fun? A villain philanthropist. Showing up the hypocrites with their fancy words."

Lycan cracked his neck.

"Do you know why people think they're always right? It's because of their damn imagination. They mistake things they've never experienced for reality."

Fermi listened with interest.

"In your imagination, you might think you could cut off your own foot to avoid death. But in reality, if you were given a saw, you wouldn't even bring it to your ankle. That's the flaw of imagination—it's devoid of pain."

Lycan doesn't imagine.

"I work as a hitman, but I honestly think it's a bad thing. If I kill someone, others can kill me."

"That's the social contract."

"But my father fed our family through hit jobs. If he hadn't killed people, we would have been worse off."

"That doesn't mean everyone commits murder."

"Nor does it mean they don't. The point is, if you're not willing to bear my pain, then shut up. Don't spout useless imagination."

Fermi smirked.

"You're a real villain, Lycan."

"Hehe, that's why I like you. I knew from the start that working with you would make us rich."

"Go, Electric Monster."

Electricity surged through Lycan's body, and with a crackling sound, he vanished.

"Let's get started."

Fermi confirmed that Istas's structures had stopped moving and left the warehouse.

It was time to cast the Stop magic.

"The structure has changed."

Arriving at Istas with Anchal, Shirone muttered with a serious expression.

It wasn't just that it had changed; it was a structure he had seen before.

'This is the structure when Goffin's door opened.'

"Fermi must have already entered."

After carefully examining Istas, Anchal looked at Shirone.

"Are you ready? Once we step in, we might never come back."

Swallowing hard, Shirone nodded.

"Alright. Let's begin."

The warehouse door opened, and the two slowly stepped inside.

This was an event that actually happened 19 years ago but was erased from everyone's memory.

A record of 2 hours and 48 minutes.
