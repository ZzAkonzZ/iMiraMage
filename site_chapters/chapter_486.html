Chapter 486:

"It is impossible."

Uriel tilted his head.

He had suggested to Raiel, the Archangel of Light, to release the Stop spell on Kariel, but surprisingly, Raiel refused.

"Why? With your ability, you should be able to reverse the stopped time."

Raiel's fundamental concept was light.

He could manipulate electromagnetic forces at will and was the fastest being in heaven.

"Let's call it the logic of power."

Raiel recounted the incident where he and three other archangels had clashed with Ikael.

"Ra has forbidden the activities of the angels, and Archangel Ikael follows Ra's will. For that reason, I cannot release Kariel."

If Ikael had made that judgment, Uriel had only one option.

"I will invoke the Council of White."

The highest executive body in heaven, the Council of White.

As the scenery disappeared and a white space filled the area, the archangels began to appear one by one.

Kariel, trapped by the Stop spell, naturally couldn't come, and this time, the Archangel of Annihilation, Paiel, was also absent.

The other archangels knew it was because the Archangel of Amplification, Ikael, had come, the highest authority in heaven.

"Uriel, why have you invoked the Council of White?"

Ikael's authoritative gaze, which had persuaded the archangels with the logic of power, was more commanding than ever.

"Kariel has been affected by human magic. Given the wartime situation, I would like to borrow Raiel's power."

Ikael thought for a moment and then spoke.

"Granted."

All the archangels' eyes turned to Ikael simultaneously.

"Is that alright? Kariel's rebellion has ultimately dispersed heaven's power. He must also bear responsibility for that."

"Victory in the war takes precedence."

The archangels fell silent.

Ra had forbidden the activities of the regular angels, and human resistance was much stronger than expected.

Of course, they still didn't think they would lose, but even having such thoughts was shameful.

"Why has Ra forbidden the activities of the angels?"

Even the archangels who handled fundamental concepts couldn't understand.

"Mirka is dead."

The Archangel of Union, Metiel, spoke.

Her hair, as thin as gold thread, hung down on both sides, and her beautiful face showed an expression of anger that seemed impossible.

"Shirone, the Nephilim, did it."

At the mention of a boy's name, the archangels' gazes sharpened again towards Ikael.

Mirka, the second-ranked fairy.

The news of her death had spread quickly throughout heaven, and Shirone had become the enemy of many fairies.

"Is he that strong, just a Nephilim?"

The Archangel of Disintegration, Satiel, spoke.

She looked like Metiel's twin, but her violent temperament made it easy to distinguish between them.

Sensing her energy, the archangels' halos all shone brightly.

If this weren't the Council of White, all surrounding matter would have been disintegrated into particles.

Reading the archangels' discontent, Ikael spoke.

"I already said I would handle Shirone."

Metatron spoke.

"That's the problem. Why are you so fixated on the Nephilim? Could it be that the boy is…"

As Metatron's words trailed off, silence fell over the Council of White.

Even the archangels, who feared nothing, didn't dare to speak.

Metatron's mouth disappeared.

It was as if his skin had covered it, leaving nothing below his nose.

There was only one possible explanation.

Anke Ra.

The archangels could only roll their eyes at the first manifestation of his will.

Metatron's question was one all the archangels shared, but now they couldn't afford to dwell on it.

"What is Ra's will…?"

The erasure of a fundamental concept would cause significant changes, even if the Akashic Records governed parts of it.

Ra had only erased Metatron's mouth, but the message was clear: any further defiance would result in complete erasure.

"Indeed, there is something. What is the relationship between Shirone and me?"

Ikael also felt Ra's will but knew that further interest would be dangerous.

He had already resolved to follow Ra's will before losing his memory, so adhering to it was the clearest solution.

"I will kill Shirone."

Ikael's declaration weighed heavily on the Council of White.

"As you can see, Ra's will is firm. The activities of the regular angels are forbidden. However, archangels are permitted to guard Araboth. This is my decision. We will now vote."

As expected, the result was unanimous.

In a deep cave, Miro, sitting cross-legged with her eyes closed, began to glow brightly.

As Archangel Raiel released the Stop spell on Kariel, the remaining 0.1 seconds until the explosion began to decrease again.

0.09 seconds. 0.08 seconds…

And now, in Miro's primal consciousness, her avatar and Arius were desperately trying to break the magic circle.

"It's connected!"

Even with his eyes blind, Arius accurately connected Miro's cerebral nerves.

It was possible because he dealt with microscopic spaces in Scale Magic.

As the line of light extended and successfully bypassed the circuit blocked by Kariel's magic circle, the magic circle suddenly vibrated as if it were about to explode.

Miro's avatar bit her lip in frustration.

"Is it already too late?"

She couldn't be sure, as she didn't know how much time had passed in reality.

Miro activated her Spirit Zone through the circuit Arius had bypassed.

As soon as she cast Scale Magic, the magic circle began to shrink rapidly.

Only when she felt the detonation of the magic circle on her skin did she realize she couldn't make it in time.

"No. If it explodes like this, the damage will be too great."

Although her head wouldn't explode due to the reduced scale, she estimated that about 15% of her brain would be damaged, preventing her from exerting her full power.

"Arius!"

"Yes!"

Arius, emitting light with his hands spread, turned his head and shouted.

At the same time, Miro ran over and shoved the now tiny magic circle into his mouth.

"Swallow it!"

Since everything here was part of Miro's mind, any explosion would directly affect her.

But Arius was a foreign entity in Miro's mind.

So, by placing a foreign object within a foreign object, the impact would be mitigated.

"Ugh!"

Swallowing the magic circle, Arius looked at the smiling Miro in shock.

"Hehe, better your heart than my head, right?"

'Ah.'

Arius shed tears.

'Beautiful. So lovely.'

Bang!

Arius's body jolted as an explosion occurred in his stomach.

Simultaneously, in reality, Miro, still sitting cross-legged, was thrown to the side as if struck by something.

"Ouch, my head!"

The explosion, mitigated by the shock absorption, spared her brain, but smoke was rising from Arius's mouth as he emerged from the door.

"Are you okay?"

Arius, clutching his stomach and drooling, nodded with a pitiful expression.

"Yes, I'm fine."

There could be no personal resentment towards the sacred Miro.

Even as a mage, it was right for him to take the hit if one of them had to.

'Ah… Lady Miro.'

Realizing this, Arius bowed his head.

The gratitude for her concern for his safety made him momentarily forget the pain that turned his insides upside down.

"Lady Miro."

"Hmm?"

"I would do anything if I could love you. Please give me a chance…"

"Do you want to die?"

"S-sorry!"

Feeling the pressure of the absolute, Arius quickly bowed his head.

As the only human to reach Miro's primal consciousness, Arius knew.

He knew the trauma she had discovered in the Temple of Drimo.

'As expected, you are…'

While Arius was lost in thought, Miro stepped outside the cave.

The sun had set, and the moon had risen.

'I can use magic, but with the barrier of time and space broken, I can't stop the war alone.'

"What will you do now?"

Arius asked, unable to hide his guilt, and Miro turned back with a beautiful smile.

"What do you mean? I'm going to explore heaven."

Miro wouldn't miss such an opportunity.

The war that had started early in the morning had only subsided around midnight.

Small skirmishes were still occurring in various parts of heaven, but they weren't as intense as during the day.

Summarizing the first day's battle, it could be said that the rebels had achieved a one-sided victory.

From the First Heaven to the Sixth Heaven, the distance the rebels had advanced towards Araboth, breaking through the outer gates, was enough to reach the second concentric circle when dividing heaven into three concentric circles.

Currently, the rebel headquarters, led by Crude, had established an advance base in the middle of the Fifth Heaven, Matei, preparing for the next day.

The surroundings were quiet as if everyone were asleep, but except for those who had passed out from exhaustion, everyone was lost in their thoughts.

Kanya and Lena, part of the headquarters, were no exception.

Sitting side by side, it was natural for them to see their parents' faces in the stars.

Lena leaned on Kanya's shoulder instead of crying.

Having family to rely on, expressing sadness was not appropriate for the other rebels.

Ching. Ching.

The sound of calm metal made Kanya's face contort.

Babel, having completed her patrol of the camp according to her programming, returned.

When not on a mission, she was programmed to take care of Kanya and Lena.

This showed Shirone's consideration, but to Kanya, it felt even more horrifying.

The machine that had pierced her father's body was now fighting alongside her.

"Shirone algorithm applied. Protecting Kanya and Lena. Mission in progress."

"Shut up."

The moment she heard Babel's mechanical voice, Kanya's patience snapped.

"I said shut up!"

"Logs are set to be disclosed."

"Logs? Do you have the log of killing my father?"

"Search complete. Data number 0342-24-6-46."

"Shut uuuup!"

Detecting abnormal vibrations in Kanya's voice, Babel switched to emergency mode and muted her sound.

"What do you know? Do you have a family? Can you feel pain?"

Babel remained silent.

Among the many data choices available, this was the best judgment.

"Answer me. Tell me the most painful way you can feel!"

"I understand pain. However, I do not have the neural mechanisms to feel it."

Kanya gave a murderous smile.

"Really? Just wait. When the war is over, I'll destroy you in the most humiliating, disgraceful way possible, in front of everyone!"

Babel responded after a moment.

"When the war is over, I will pay the price."

As Kanya ground her teeth and turned away, the commander's voice was heard.

"Don't get excited. Save that anger and unleash it tomorrow."

"Commander."

Kanya and Lena stood up and saluted simultaneously.

"Seems like you can't sleep. Well, it's understandable."

Tomorrow, all the rebels would attempt to enter Araboth, the heart of heaven.

No one expected to survive.

The moon they saw now might be the last, so sleep wasn't a priority.

"Commander, at this hour…"

Crude smiled bitterly.

"I can't sleep either. But it's best to get some rest. We're going to win, not to die."

Kanya nodded calmly.

She understood the meaning behind Crude's words, knowing that survival wasn't guaranteed.

Ching!

Babel's eyes suddenly lit up.

A red laser quickly scanned the front and immediately identified a suspicious figure, reporting it.

"A living entity detected 600 meters ahead. Heat sensor activated. Body temperature 40.1 degrees. Classified as ally. Name: Ravid Plu."
