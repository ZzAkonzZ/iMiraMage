Chapter 374: Senior Information Session (2)

The game of Truth or Dare had begun, and the first question was over. Even that was enough to create a subtle tension among the students.

Though it was an unspoken rule in the senior class never to bring up what was said during the game, the participants couldn't help but feel affected.

Everyone was fiercely engaged in a battle of wits.

'So this is how it is. If I hadn't known, it could have been a disaster.'

Shirone felt a bit more at ease and looked at Amy.

No matter what question came up, he just had to point to her, avoiding early psychological warfare.

Amy met his gaze and shrugged.

She had gone through this process last year in the senior class. For Shirone, a new senior, her help was more valuable than gold.

'Thank you, Amy. Really, thank you.'

The psychological pressure disguised as tradition.

Being able to avoid early psychological attacks from competitors was definitely an advantage in the senior class.

"Haha, alright! Let's move on. Pony, why don't you go next? Everyone has to do it at least once."

Pony, maintaining her icy expression, asked Neide, who stood opposite her.

"Who was the first person you saw when you got here?"

It was a simple question, leaving no room for psychological games. She seemed uninterested in Truth or Dare.

Screamer looked at her, disappointed.

"Really? That's it? Can't you make it more interesting?"

"Forget it. This is childish."

Neide looked at the Class Two students. He could find meaning in any question if he wanted, but since Pony had asked lightly, he answered honestly.

"The first person I saw was that guy."

All eyes turned to the person Neide pointed at.

A tall, lanky man with perfect curls and tanned skin from long sun exposure. His prominent cheekbones made him look even older.

Shirone realized Neide wasn't playing mind games. He had also noticed this person first upon arriving.

'That's Pisho.'

Pisho was the oldest in the senior class at twenty-five, though he looked thirty-five.

His specialty was insect magic, dealing with insects, a unique field among various magics.

Insect magic was more academically developed than magical. Most specialists in this field became scholars, but Pisho stayed in school until twenty-five to get a mage’s license for better opportunities.

Screamer, dissatisfied with the dull outcome, decided to take matters into his own hands to change the atmosphere.

He couldn't miss the chance to crush the new seniors.

"Alright, I'll ask the next question."

Screamer's opponent was Dante. As the top students of Class Two and Class Three, it felt like a showdown between leaders.

"Who do you think you could easily beat? Someone so weak you could crush them with a finger."

Even though the question wasn't directed at him, Shirone felt tense.

No matter who Dante chose, it would hurt someone's pride. Any choice would leave lingering resentment.

Dante frowned, deep in thought.

The Class Two students watched him with sharp eyes, daring him to choose them.

"Hmm, this is really tough."

Screamer smiled, showing his characteristic red tongue.

"Just be honest. It's an unspoken rule that there will be no retaliation for any choice."

"Can't I just say all of Class Two? They all seem about the same."

The explosive atmosphere deflated instantly. Class Two's expressions turned cold, and Shirone's eyes widened.

Only someone like Dante, once the kingdom's top prospect, could say such a thing.

'Your personality is something else, really.'

Screamer didn't get angry.

He understood Dante's strategy: to spread the animosity among everyone, thus diluting it.

"Sorry, but that's not allowed. You have to pick just one. That's the rule of Truth or Dare."

Dante casually pointed at Screamer.

"Alright, then I'll pick you. Since you're the top of Class Two, choosing you means everyone else is below me."

'This guy is really crazy.'

This time, even Screamer couldn't help but get angry.

Dante Airhein. The top prospect even at the Royal Magic School.

Even now, he was considered one of the top candidates for graduation.

But that was based on advanced class standards.

Once past the Iron Gate, advanced class rankings no longer mattered.

No matter how skilled, one mistake could lead to a downfall in the competitive senior class.

'This is why I hate rookies. They get pampered in the advanced class and think they're invincible.'

Dante's declaration had a significant impact. The smirking faces of Class Two turned serious.

"Can I ask the next question?"

Maya raised her hand.

She didn't need anyone's permission, but Screamer nodded as if he had the authority.

"Go ahead."

Maya smiled mischievously at Shirone.

Waiting for her question, Shirone swallowed nervously. What would she ask?

At first, he thought it wouldn't be a big deal, but now his heart was racing.

'I just have to choose Amy. We've already agreed on this. No matter the question, it's no big deal.'

Maya's eyes sparkled as she asked,

"Who's so adorable that you want to hug and kiss them right now?"

"Uh... what?"

Shirone's eyes widened.

Even Amy seemed caught off guard by this unexpected question.

Shirone studied Maya's playful smile.

This wasn't about choosing an answer. She had asked the question knowing the answer.

'What should I do? Should I pick someone else?'

He thought it over but couldn't find a better solution than sticking to the plan.

"I... I choose..."

Blushing, Shirone pointed at Amy.

Amy, unable to hide her embarrassment, turned her head to look at the distant mountains.

"Wow! Really? You two are actually dating?"

Eider exaggeratedly reacted, putting pressure on Shirone.

There were rumors in the senior class that Shirone and Amy's relationship was just a cover.

Senior students avoided dating within the school. The chances of a couple both making it to the final ten were slim.

Screamer clapped, trying to lighten the mood.

"Wow, you had the courage to choose honestly. Since it's out in the open, why not show us?"

Ignoring him, Shirone looked back at Maya, who was laughing as if she found it all amusing.

Why had Maya asked him such a question?

He might never get the chance to ask, but this question couldn't pressure a newcomer, nor did it seem like something she would care about.

The game continued, and finally, it was Luman's turn to ask Iruki a question.

Having finished a bag of chips while waiting, Luman stuffed the last crumbs into his mouth and asked,

"Alright, who in Class Two do you think is definitely not a virgin?"

Pony's golden eyebrows furrowed angrily.

"Disgusting..."

This was why she disliked the senior class traditions. Why should a royal like her be subject to such questions?

Regardless of Pony's thoughts, Luman elaborated.

"I mean, who looks like they'd sleep with any guy?"

The female students seemed to burn with indignation.

Despite his excellent crowd control skills, Luman's attitude was one reason he remained in Class Two.

"Hmm, I see."

Iruki rested his chin on his hand, trying to find a basis for his answer.

Of course, he couldn't tell just by looking. But he wasn't one to rely on intuition.

Excluding Amy, he considered the four Class Two girls: Pony, Maya, Suabi, and Dorothy.

First, he looked at Pony. She turned her head slightly, as if insulted.

'A royal...'

Pony's first impression was of a cold, noble ice flower.

As a royal, she likely didn't mingle with common nobles. Statistically, her pool of male acquaintances would be smaller.

'Okay, not her. Next, Maya?'

Maya watched with interest, curious about his answer. Though she was a candidate, she seemed to enjoy the situation.

'She's too openly happy. That's a bit...'

Iruki turned to Suabi.

With her short, braided brown hair, she hadn't spoken a word since the event began and avoided eye contact.

Her gentle, rabbit-like appearance matched her timid demeanor, and she seemed genuinely terrified.

'She's too shy. Isn't she a utility specialist?'

Utility magic supported other mages rather than acting alone.

While sound magic excelled in wide-area buffs, utility magic focused on individual enhancement with excellent effects.

In the professional world, utility specialists were highly sought after, but fewer mages chose it due to its dependency on others.

Given her timid nature, Suabi's choice of utility magic made sense.

'She's extremely anxious. On the other hand...'

Iruki finally looked at Dorothy.

Wearing large glasses and a vacant expression, she seemed oblivious to her surroundings.

Though naturally pretty, she didn't seem to care about her appearance.

Her specialty was manipulation magic, and she always carried a tin can doll, making her seem eccentric.

Shirone also took a special interest in Dorothy.

Having heard about zoners from Flu, he saw Dorothy differently this year.

'So that doll moves with an algorithm. I thought it was like a harvester.'

Iruki made his decision.

He pointed to Dorothy.

"I choose you."

Despite the attention, Dorothy didn't react. It was as if she lived in another world.

Iruki awkwardly lowered his hand.

After a long pause, she returned to reality and spoke.

"Why me?"

Iruki nodded, as if it were a good question.

"I observed resignation and resistance. If someone were truly promiscuous, they wouldn't want to reveal it. Based on that, you seemed the most emotionally detached."

Dorothy, as if wondering what to do, drifted off again. After ten seconds, she returned and spoke.

"You're right."

The boys looked at Dorothy in shock.

The boys looked back at Dorothy with surprised eyes.

There was no need to answer out of the courage of truth. But once she confessed, she was curious about what would come next.

"I love all men."

For the first time, Dorothy made eye contact with Iruki.

"Iruki. I love you too."

Iruki's eyelids opened and closed quickly.

"And I love women too."

"Huh?"

An exclamation of surprise came from someone.

Regardless, Dorothy hugged the tin doll and slowly lowered her gaze.

"But I hate humans."

"......"

The gazes that had been focused on Dorothy scattered in different directions. It was an instinctive reaction.

'What is she talking about? She's out of her mind.'

Shirone was equally bewildered. Competing with people like this in the future seemed daunting.

Especially since Class One hadn't even stepped up yet.

It seemed like it wouldn't be easy from the first day.
