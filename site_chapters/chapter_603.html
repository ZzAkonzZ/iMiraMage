Chapter 603:

The sight of Istas changing its form was spectacular.

“Isn’t it amazing? This is Istas, the pride of Alpheas Magic School.”

“Oh, come on. We had something like this at my school too.”

Liz mentioned her alma mater without hesitation, while Oscar referred to it as just the school he attended.

Even in casual conversation, prestigious and non-prestigious schools were distinguished.

Oscar was a native of the capital, but unless it was the Royal Magic School, it wasn’t among the top five prestigious schools in the kingdom.

However, Liz was proud of Oscar.

With this self-made man who had earned his certification, she felt they could overcome any hardship together.

“Come on, let me show you the research society filled with my memories.”

“Let’s just go back. We’ve already greeted the principal.”

“Oh, come on. Hurry up. It’ll be fun.”

While Liz was excited like a child, Oscar’s mind was preoccupied with his dinner plans.

“Nothing has changed here.”

Countless memories flashed through Liz’s mind.

“How do you find your way? It’s like a maze.”

“One of the former presidents had Savant abilities. That person created the master equation.”

“Really? How does it work?”

Liz turned around with her hands behind her back and stuck out her tongue.

“It’s a secret.”

The door to the Supernatural Psychic Research Society opened, and Liz’s face popped in.

“Ta-da! Oh, no one’s here?”

“Of course, they’re in class. Let’s go back. We have a dinner appointment with the association people.”

As Oscar turned to leave, Liz grabbed his wrist and turned him back.

He sighed and turned around, but Liz’s meaningful expression made his eyes light up.

“Are you really going to leave? You might regret it.”

“Ahem, what do you mean?”

Oscar pretended not to know.

“I spent more time here than in my dorm. You could say it’s filled with my essence.”

As Liz pulled him in, Oscar’s legs moved as if hypnotized, and soon the door closed.

“Ah, this is driving me crazy.”

Having finished his evaluation earlier than his friends, Neide entered Istas to find Shirone.

‘I can’t focus at all.’

His mind was filled with thoughts of Liz, leaving him unable to do anything.

‘If I had known, I would have avoided her.’

If he hadn’t left the Scramble Royal, if he hadn’t gone to the bathhouse that day, countless regrets flooded in.

But a part of him was happy to have reunited with Liz.

“Is Shirone here?”

Neide’s hand froze on the doorknob.

At first, he thought it was Shirone, but the voices coming from inside were clearly male and female.

“What the…”

Opening the door to the research society, Neide saw Liz and Oscar kissing.

“Liz, senpai?”

It was a shock that felt like his heart was being ripped out without anesthesia, leaving him unable to think.

“Liz! Mmph! Mmph!”

Oscar, seeing Neide, quickly pushed Liz away.

Realizing what had happened, Liz looked flustered but then awkwardly smiled and approached.

“Oh, Neide! When did you get here?”

“What are you doing?”

“Oh, this is embarrassing. Sorry. I got caught up in old memories and the atmosphere…”

“Get out.”

Neide’s stern tone made Liz’s mouth shut tight.

“Don’t ever come here again.”

Oscar frowned.

“How dare you speak to your senior like that? The research society isn’t yours, and Liz graduated from here too.”

Neide’s gaze lingered on Oscar for a moment before returning.

“Outsiders, leave.”

They knew nothing.

They didn’t know the battles he and his friends fought to protect the research society.

“Outsiders? Leave?”

Oscar couldn’t hold back any longer.

“You should think before you speak. The person standing in front of you is a certified professional mage in the kingdom. You, without a mage certification, have no right to speak to me like that.”

Neide didn’t even want to engage in conversation.

“This is your last warning. Leave if you don’t want trouble.”

“You little…”

Oscar took a step forward, eyes blazing.

But the moment their gazes met, Oscar felt his heart stop and couldn’t move.

‘What is this guy?’

A chill that could freeze blood.

Even during missions, he had never experienced such killing intent.

“If you’re confident, come at me. You’re a professional mage, right?”

Oscar was a professional, but he was frozen by an amateur senior.

No matter how he felt, he couldn’t back down, so he tried to move again, but Liz intervened.

“Stop! Don’t fight.”

She pushed Oscar away and turned to Neide.

“Alright. We’ll leave. I’m sorry if we upset you.”

“Liz, are you going to tolerate this? He only cares about his own feelings. He even speaks rudely to his senior.”

“Oscar, please stop. Let’s go.”

As Liz led her boyfriend out the door, Oscar spat out a curse.

“Trash. No wonder you’re last place.”

The door slammed shut, and Neide, feeling dizzy, sat on the sofa.

“Sigh.”

Contrary to his initial expectation of feeling relieved, only regret washed over him once it was over.

“Ahhh! Damn it! Damn it!”

Neide, hitting his head in frustration, buried his face deep into the sofa.

Two hours later, when Shirone and Iruki arrived, Neide was already a wreck.

“What’s wrong? What happened?”

Shirone was amazed at how much someone could change in just a few hours.

“It’s over. Everything is over.”

“What do you mean, everything?”

“Liz came by…”

After hearing the whole story from Neide, Shirone and Iruki remained silent for a long time.

It was a mistake to kick her out so harshly, but thinking about being in that situation themselves, they couldn’t just scold him.

“Maybe it’s for the best. She’s engaged anyway. Getting your hopes up would only hurt you.”

Iruki’s advice was practical.

“I know. But I can’t give up. I’m so angry, but I don’t want to lose her.”

Shirone tried a shock tactic.

“Even after seeing that, you still like Liz?”

“Yes! I like her so much it’s driving me crazy. Do you know why I wanted to open a magic shop? Because Liz works at the armory! I was going to confess properly, but now…”

Neide clutched his head.

“I was stupid! I should have played it cool. Now she must hate me.”

“Hate or not, she’s engaged. That means she’s going to get married.”

“Engagements can be broken. What if she changes her mind and comes to me? Iruki, what are the chances?”

“Zero percent.”

He didn’t even want to calculate it.

“Shirone, tell me. What should I do? Isn’t this what insight is for?”

“If you really can’t give up, there’s only one way.”

“Really? What is it?”

“Go and apologize to both of them right now.”

“Apologize?”

He might apologize to Liz, but he never wanted to bow to Oscar.

“You were harsh. If you can’t do it, you’ll have to give up.”

Neide knew it was the only solution.

“But I don’t know where they are. I can’t search every inn in the city.”

“The principal will know. I’ll ask him.”

Shirone ran to Alpheas and found out that Liz was staying at the Dolphin Inn.

Thanking him repeatedly, Neide left Istas, but by the time he reached the inn, he was a nervous wreck.

‘Calm down. You’re just here to see a senior. You didn’t act like this before.’

With the determination of jumping off a cliff, he raised his hand and knocked on the door. Liz’s voice came from inside.

“Who is it?”

“Uh… it’s Neide.”

“Neide?”

The door opened, and Liz appeared in casual clothes.

She wasn’t as beautiful as when she was dressed up, but the rarity made his heart race.

“How did you know I was here?”

Liz’s face wasn’t as friendly as he had hoped.

“Do you want to come in?”

“I’m sorry! Senpai!”

Thinking he shouldn’t waste any more time, Neide immediately bowed and apologized.

“I must have lost my mind. I didn’t even recognize you. I’m really sorry.”

This wouldn’t resolve all feelings, but there was no reason not to accept his apology.

“Alright. Thanks for coming. Honestly, I felt a bit uncomfortable. I wondered if I shouldn’t have gone to the school.”

“It’s not your fault. It’s all my fault.”

“Okay. Let’s meet again before I leave.”

“By the way, where is Oscar? I want to apologize to him directly.”

“He went out for a dinner appointment with the association people. Don’t worry. He’s not the type to hold a grudge.”

“Still, could you tell me where he is? I promise I won’t cause any trouble.”

Liz also thought it would be better for Oscar to let go of his anger.

“He’s at the Gold Ocean Restaurant. He said he’d be there. It’s an important meeting, so don’t interrupt.”

“Yes. Don’t worry. Thank you.”

Thinking he might make another mistake if he stayed longer, Neide hurriedly left the inn.

‘Got it!’

There was still some distance, but it was better than losing the connection.

‘Getting happy over something like this. She smiled at me just a few days ago.’

Why do people only realize the value of something after losing it?

‘I should be satisfied with just seeing her again.’

From across the street, Neide spotted Oscar through the restaurant window.

In a group of five or six, Oscar was confidently gesturing as he spoke.

“Ugh, lucky him.”

Does Oscar realize how blessed he is to be loved by Liz?

She could be annoying and sometimes a bother, but to someone, she was a noble woman whose gaze was hard to meet.

“When will this end?”

Waiting outside to apologize to his beloved’s boyfriend was the height of misery.

Wearing thin clothes, the autumn chill felt even colder, but it wasn’t just the weather.

‘End quickly. End quickly.’

After about an hour of waiting with his hands tucked under his arms, Oscar finally stood up from the table.

‘He’s coming out.’

Oscar opened the restaurant door himself and waited for his companions to exit, adjusting his clothes and bowing.

“Thank you for the dinner, Branch Manager. Next time, I’ll take you somewhere nice.”

“Haha! I’m glad you enjoyed it. I look forward to your recommendations.”

‘Wow. That man is the branch manager?’

Neide looked at the middle-aged man with a bulging belly, barely buttoned into his suit.

Earning a certification was impressive, but having dinner with the Creas branch manager of the Magic Association was extraordinary.

‘He must be capable. Well, to live with Liz… huh?’

A blonde woman in a red dress put on a fur coat and gently linked arms with Oscar.

“Dad, can I take a walk with him? To help with digestion.”

“Sure. Escort my daughter well.”

“Don’t worry.”

From their conversation, it was clear she was the branch manager’s daughter.

But what Neide couldn’t understand was why such a woman was linking arms with Oscar and taking a walk.

“What the…?”
