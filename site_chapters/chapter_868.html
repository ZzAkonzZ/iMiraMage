Chapter 868: Groggy (3)

Marin looked at Betherus, then back at Shirone with a shocked expression.

She had spent enough time on the battlefield to know many mythical stories.

'If that boy is Yahweh...'

And among those myths was the story of a knight who cut down demons while traversing Kashan.

'The Knight of Maha.'

Even with demons approaching, Rian was calmly observing the weather, showing no signs of tension.

'Why are these people here?'

They didn't look like defeated soldiers.

According to her common sense, whether it was the Holy War or another country's army, they should be revered and escorted.

As the thirty demons gathered in a circle, Shirone pointed to one of them.

"Come here."

"M-me?"

The demon looked around in denial, but none of his comrades met his gaze.

The reason minor purification was terrifying was that the particles of light fundamentally destroyed the demons' information.

The surviving demons already understood this, and Shirone appeared as a Buddha to them.

"I have a question."

The demon approached and squatted where Shirone pointed.

"How can I summon the Shiyok?"

"Shiyok?"

The demons were residents of the under world, but very few had even heard of the Shiyok.

As Shirone stared at him, the demon flinched and waved his hands.

"I really don't know! I've never heard of it!"

Shirone expected that.

"There's no need to be afraid. I'm neither Yahweh nor a Buddha. I'm just an ordinary human."

The demon's eyes filled with confusion.

'Not Yahweh or a Buddha?'

If it were true, the demons wouldn't need to fear Shirone anymore.

"R-really?"

"Yes."

The demon looked back at his comrades, shrugged, and extended a finger towards Shirone.

"You're not angry?"

Cautiously, as if handling an explosive, the demon poked Shirone's cheek, but no rain of light fell.

"See? I told you, I'm just an ordinary human."

The demon, lost in thought, suddenly grinned.

"Haha, so that's it!"

The killing intent from the demon merged with that of his comrades, flaring up like flames.

"Ugh!"

Betherus and Marin hurriedly took defensive stances, but they couldn't stop what was about to happen.

"What are you doing?"

Marin shouted at Rian, who was still watching.

"We need to stop them!"

Even if Shirone wasn't at Yahweh's level, he wouldn't fall to thirty demons.

But Rian had a deeper reason for not moving.

"Guys! This is our chance!"

The demons, baring their sharp teeth, stomped towards Shirone.

"Despicable Yahweh, no, human! We'll tear you apart!"

As the demon who had approached Shirone stood up arrogantly and raised his claws, Shirone spoke.

"And now."

Light radiated from Shirone's body, rising like a mirage.

"I am Yahweh again."

It was Yahweh's light.

The demons stared in disbelief at the light rising ten meters high.

"You bastard."

Rian chuckled at the sight of Shirone being cursed by the demons.

'The state of mind isn't something you lose.'

It's a specific mental state that can be reached anytime by flipping one's mindset.

Of course, flipping that mindset is the hardest part for some, but magicians are experts in handling the mind.

"Come here."

As Shirone, enveloped in light, gestured, the demons' legs trembled.

But they couldn't refuse, and the demon who had raised his claws sat down quietly.

As Shirone reached out towards the demon's red, menacing face, the demon closed his eyes tightly.

"I'm sorry."

At first, the demon couldn't believe his ears.

When he opened his eyes to the gentle touch on his cheek, he saw sadness in Shirone's eyes.

"I didn't try to understand with my heart. I judged based on the results without looking at the cause."

Just as Betherus had judged Shirone.

"The reason you were born lies with humans. Born from evil, you have every right to feel wronged."

The demon trembled.

"What are you doing? Saying such things won't change anything."

"Repent. Let's save the world together."

The demon bowed his head.

"...Thank you for saying that."

As the demon slowly stood, an overwhelming killing intent emanated from him.

"But we can't do that. Just as we can't live without eating, this is natural for demons."

Shirone's eyelids drooped halfway.

"This is your last chance."

"Yahweh, destroy us. If it can bring you pain..."

The demons attacked simultaneously.

"For us, it's the greatest pleasure!"

As the thirty demons pounced on Shirone, a blinding explosion of light occurred.

The curtain of light, striking hundreds of times per second, left no trace of the demons.

"My God..."

Marin couldn't comprehend what had happened, and neither could Betherus.

"This is magic?"

No magician on the battlefield could turn demons to dust in such a short time.

The survivors who had scorned Shirone slowly backed away, but Shirone was lost in thought.

'My pain is their pleasure.'

He had reached a stage of understanding them, but true compassion still seemed far away.

"Let's go, Rian."

As Shirone stood up, Rian followed without a word.

"Please wait!"

Betherus approached and knelt.

"Please take me with you! I beg you!"

Marin quickly joined in.

"Me too! I beg to follow the great swordsman."

Shirone didn't want to take them.

Until he apologized to the Ogent family, he couldn't accept anyone.

Rian spoke.

"If you follow us, what about the remaining survivors? Weren't you responsible for them?"

"That, that is..."

"Forget what happened today. It has nothing to do with what you did. We're going our own way."

Betherus bit his lip.

'Damn it! This opportunity...'

Rian said it didn't matter, but Betherus deeply regretted ignoring Shirone.

'I should have realized from the start. If I had done better, they would have taken me...'

Rian turned to Shirone.

"It's raining. Are you okay? Should we ask for a horse?"

"No. It's fine now."

As Shirone lightly tossed the "Law Slayer" and activated the material ability, metallic elements began to attach.

With a thud, a massive quadrupedal creature landed.

"Let's ride the Slayer."

Rian nodded and climbed onto the Slayer, with Shirone following slowly.

The survivors' astonished gazes were clearly felt on Shirone's back.

Shirone stopped and looked up.

"Rian."

Rian understood what he wanted to say.

"Do as you wish."

The responsibility of killing Guy was already too much, and Shirone didn't want to take on anything else for now.

'But...'

He couldn't do the wrong thing.

"Everyone, follow me. I'll take you to a city nearby."

The survivors looked bewildered.

"You're taking us?"

Shirone, not one to dwell on decisions, smiled.

"Yes. It's dangerous here. Let's go somewhere safe."

Shirone also spoke to Betherus.

"Gather only the necessary supplies and food. We'll leave in ten minutes."

"Why..."

Betherus was puzzled.

"Why do you care about us? We didn't even recognize the great magician and scorned you."

"You can't be serious."

Shirone placed his hand on the Slayer's body.

"This is the right thing to do."

He didn't give a detailed explanation because every human inherently knows.

"But no one lives like that."

That might also be true.

"Yes."

As Shirone enhanced the material ability, the Slayer's size began to expand enormously.

Turning back to Betherus, Shirone gave a sad smile.

"That's why it's so hard."

The state of Yahweh.

Even if he didn't understand a hundredth of it, he could grasp what Shirone was carrying.

"I'll prepare as quickly as possible."

As Betherus went to gather supplies, Marin hurriedly stood up to organize the survivors.

'Shirone.'

Rian looked at Shirone with affectionate eyes.

"You say I'm amazing, but I don't have the confidence to live like you."

That's why he became Shirone's sword.

"It's truly strange. Even when doing the right thing, everyone doubts you."

Shirone wasn't wrong.

"Hurry. Move quickly."

The 100 survivors, ready to leave, boarded the Slayer.

A normal beast's legs would break, but the metallic Slayer was unfazed.

As it walked, shaking the ground, even the magician Betherus turned pale.

"It's so big you might get motion sickness. But I'll increase the speed."

As the "Law Slayer" accelerated the Slayer, its legs moved as fast as a horse.

"Ahhh!"

The screams of the people echoed through the desolate city.

An emergency arose in the command center of the Holy War.

"Report! A 3 million-strong demon army is advancing from the southeast of the Kettler Mountains!"

"Reporting! The Altras region has been occupied. The damage is estimated at 400,000!"

Commander Iruki's expression darkened at the bad news pouring in from all directions.

"Damn it."

He had already heard through urgent intelligence that the Gustav Empire had been wiped off the map.

'It was too clean.'

Shirone hadn't intended it, but the complete annihilation left no need to turn back to the main camp.

Absorbing the remaining demons and waiting for the right moment was an option but, Balkan leading Habitz's army, didn't give them time.

"Report! The port city of Tostra has been hit. The damage is estimated at 1 million!"

At that point, Iruki slammed the round table.

"Ahhh! Ahhh!"

"Ahhh! Ahhh!"

Whether it's 400,000 or 1,000,000, it's just a number when spoken, but...

"Damn bastards!"

To recount the lives within those numbers would take a lifetime.

"What should we do? We can't stop them."

Iruki checked the entire map.

The armies of hell were converging on Tormia, and there wasn't much time left.

"There is a way..."

It's not that there wasn't a way.

But if Iruki were to implement this technology, humans wouldn't be safe either.

'Pandora's box.'

Another report came in. 200,000 had died.

'I'm sorry, Shirone.'

At humanity's last stand, Iruki suddenly recalled his days at Alpheas Magic School.

"I will go to Tormia."

The commanders turned to Iruki.

"Now? You mean you'll go yourself?"

"If we delay, we'll miss our only chance. I'll be back shortly. Assemble an escort team."

As Iruki headed for the door, he turned back.

"Oh, and call for Neid. Tell him I'll explain the reason in person."

It was time to call in favors.
