Chapter 1267: End of History (3)

Siena furrowed her brow.

"Pilot?"

Even if his mind was injured, calling someone who was still alive a corpse was unacceptable.

"I don't care what the Illuminati is. We’re not interested. So please leave."

No matter how sweet the offer sounded, the ominous feeling was too strong to ignore.

"Really?"

Crown pointed at Kuan.

"That human might be able to recover his mind, you know?"

"What?"

Siena's eyes widened.

And in that moment, she realized that, no matter the cost, she had to get involved with this person.

"Is there a way?"

"It's simple. You just fix it. Like... say the receiver is broken. The signal’s coming in, but it’s not processing. But if I pilot him, I might be able to fix it."

"And that ‘piloting’..."

Crown made a circle with her index finger and thumb.

"You just need to drill a tiny hole. Then cover it back up. It won’t even be visible—won’t look ugly."

She instinctively knew he meant the brain.

"Are you insane?"

"Heh heh."

Crown turned her head toward the battlefield.

Girshin and a human were clashing like titans on the side of a mountain, as if displayed on a massive screen.

In the sky, Shirone and Nane were locked in combat. On the ground, humanity’s elite were facing off against Ymir.

Screeeeech!

The temple, overlapping with the Underworld, was burning from the bottom up, screaming as it went.

"What a circus."

He had no interest in the conflict between good and evil.

What interested him was a fundamental biological question that persisted even in the outer world.

—Does love transcend death?

Why did life choose death as a system for passing things on?

—Even in the outer world, children are still being born.

Despite having attained eternal life.

So it couldn’t even be fully explained as a natural response to a virus.

Crown said,

"To enter this chaos, I’ll need a stronger body than what I have now. It might be an opportunity for you. Choose—whether it’s a fitting misfortune or a slim chance at hope."

Siena was silent for a long time.

Tap. Tap tap.

The area around Havitz continued to spread, turning to charcoal from where he stood.

Unable to stand, Kido held Uorin from behind and retreated some distance.

‘This is bad. Even if I activate Vanishing, if I can’t land an attack on Havitz, it’s useless.’

What Satan wanted was only Uorin.

"Running away?"

Havitz’s mouth, which hadn’t existed a second ago, split wide open.

"What a joke."

Boom—the fire engulfed his body, and he shot toward Uorin at terrifying speed.

"Ugh!"

Kido instinctively raised his spear but flinched as the memory of that horrifying pain returned.

‘If I get hit by that again...’

His legs were already shaking.

"Damn it!"

Like Gaold, he erased his hesitation and stepped into the pain.

"Let’s gooooo!"

The moment they clashed, the spear began to burn, but this time, he didn’t let go.

‘I have to protect her.’

"Hahaha!"

Just as Havitz grinned wide, a smack landed on his cheekbone, bursting it and sending shards flying.

"Huh?"

He stumbled to the side and turned—Natasha was standing with her fist clenched.

"Run."

Hearing the thunderous pounding of her heart, Kido looked at her fist.

It had turned pitch black, like a lump of coal.

‘That must hurt...’

In that moment, Natasha's avatar forcibly injected strength into her hand.

As her five fingers stretched open—pop—charred skin cracked and tender pink flesh emerged.

She said,

"I’m the one who has business with Havitz. Get the others out of here. If you stay, you'll experience hell."

"Why?"

Kido had to ask.

"Weren’t you on the same side as him? Revenge? Why care now if others die?"

"…Because we’re friends."

It felt like she’d just spoken an incredibly difficult word, and Kido could no longer question her.

"Go."

"Don’t talk nonsense!"

As Havitz reignited and charged again, Natasha instantly blocked his path.

In front of Kido’s eyes, a sonic flurry of punches exploded.

"Go, now!"

Seeing Natasha’s face twisted in agony, Kido didn’t hesitate any longer.

"Raaah!"

Carrying Uorin on his shoulder, he sprinted down the corridor as Havitz screamed from within the flames.

"Graaaaaaaaagh!"

Kido felt dizzy.

‘Please! Please!’

Don’t follow.

The pain from just losing a piece of his body was enough to make him lose the will to fight.

‘If we get completely swallowed by Death Field...’

There will only be eternal pain.

Havitz had become a being feared not for strength or skill, but simply because fighting him was terrifying in itself.

‘We have to run. All of humanity, if possible. As far away from Satan as we can get.’

When they emerged from underground, humans and demons were fiercely fighting for territory.

What meaning did it hold?

"Everyone…"

Kido shouted with all his strength.

"Get out of here!"

"Grrrraaaah!"

Having lost Uorin, Havitz turned his terrifying gaze toward Natasha.

Her right arm and left leg had turned to charcoal. Sweating coldly, she bit her lip.

"Hurts, doesn’t it? Didn’t I tell you not to piss me off? Who the hell are you to block me?"

"Ughhh..."

Natasha couldn’t focus.

‘Feels like I’ve turned into a human-shaped lump of coal.’

But despite her will, the avatar of the Reaper forced her body to rise.

"Aaaaaargh!"

Natasha, driven by superhuman determination, screamed—and Havitz found it amusing.

"Pathetic. I never liked you from the start. You were only pretending to be close because of our families, weren’t you?"

Natasha thought she could almost hear something near her ear.

—Havitz, Havitz. The Dusk One.

She didn’t remember clearly, but even back then, Havitz had probably been hard to deal with.

"Havitz."

She muttered with vacant eyes.

"Grow up already."

Crunch. Havitz’s face contorted, and black ash crumbled downward.

This damned world. If I really wanted to, I could burn it all down right now.

Natasha said,

"Let’s stop here. Our friends are gone. Whether our memories are real or not—let’s end it here."

Havitz didn’t respond.

‘Wizard.’

Even if the memories were real, he wouldn’t leave her behind.

"No. If you want to go, go alone."

"...Fine."

Even in unbearable pain, Natasha activated the Dance of the Reaper.

"Then I’ll send you myself."

Havitz's mouth curled open.

"Hehehe."

Five minutes later.

…

In a hallway scorched black like the scene of a fire, the charred corpse of a victim lay alone.

Twisted limbs, burned into charcoal—its identity was unrecognizable, but—

Creak. Creak.

As her joints snapped like a malfunctioning machine, the avatar revealed that she had been Natasha.

"I told you. You go."

The corpse trembled intermittently, its fingers curled inward.

No ears to hear. No mouth to speak.

It was eternal pain.

"How is it? You wanna die? Want me to kill you?" Havitz, watching the trembling corpse in annoyance, stomped on her face and shattered it.

"Tch."

Creak. Creak.

Her combat doll folded like a piece of paper and collapsed—then vanished completely after a few seconds.

"Next time, know your place."

Was freeing Natasha a gesture of kindness toward a friend—or mockery?

"Wizard."

One thing was clear—Havitz would never again show mercy to the world.

"It doesn’t matter where you are."

If he turned the entire world to ash, the only one left would be her.

"Hehehe."

As Havitz’s body reignited, the already burned-out building flared up once more.

"GAHAHAHAHA!"

The Death Field was expanding at an incredible rate.

Screeeeech!

Screams echoed from Delta Headquarters, and the ground forces all turned their heads.

"W-what is that?"

In an instant, the building was engulfed in flames, and even the land beneath it turned pitch black.

"Satan."

Proving the birth of true malice, a massive ship appeared high above in the sky.

The High Judge announced to the world:

—The Day of Judgment has arrived. Humanity, the culmination of all your history will now be placed on trial. In the battle between good and evil...

"UAAAAHHH!"

Shirone roared as he charged forward.

"Nane!"

A massive barrage of Photon Cannons fired, but all of them were destroyed by Nane’s Sermon.

"Why did you kill her?!"

"Shirone, look at the world."

"Why! Why did you kill her?!"

"Look at what humanity has done! Look at the Satan they’ve created from ignorance, evil, desire, and attachment!"

As the Death Field expanded, all the humans within it turned to charcoal.

Awooooo...

They had no mouths, but it felt like all their souls were wailing in agony.

"What more do you want from this world? They can’t even die! Living with hearts burned black, forced to keep living until death. That’s all this world is!"

"YAAAHHH!"

Finally reaching Nane, Shirone grabbed him by the collar.

"Why."

Tears fell.

"Why did you kill her? We can’t go back. That’s something you can’t undo! Why! Why!"

"I’ll take responsibility."

"…Yeah?"

Shirone lifted his head, eyes full of murderous rage.

"Then die."

When Photon Cannon Infinity activated, a wave of particles spread at light speed.

"Foolish child."

Simultaneously, Nane’s Sermon, imbued with the greatest truth, split through the particle wave and pierced Shirone’s chest.

"Guh!"

As he plummeted toward the ground, Nane looked on with sad eyes and raised a hand.

"I will save all living beings from suffering."

A massive golden blade began spinning in the sky.

"Shirone!"

Spotting him falling, Rian shoved Ymir aside and leapt into the air.

Sliding along the ground, he caught Shirone and quickly checked his condition—no visible critical injuries.

However…

"Wake up!"

There was no sign of consciousness returning.

With Shirone’s defeat, not only the elite forces but all their allies came to accept the reality.

"We lost."

Humanity had been defeated.

"Take Shirone and leave."

Miro approached, surveying the Death Field.

"Without Yahweh, we can’t stop Nane. Even if we fight Satan now, the result will be the same."

Cradling Shirone in one arm, Rian gripped his greatsword with the other and asked,

"Where should I go?"

Miro had only one thing to say:

"As far as possible."

Rian nodded firmly and launched himself off the ground.

He was prepared to run to the ends of the planet.

"Where do you think you're going?"

Ymir blocked his path.

"Damn it!"

"It’s all over anyway. Let’s settle this once and for all."

Even if the world closed now, he wanted to know who the last one standing would be.

Boom! Boom! Boom!

Every time Ymir’s attacks clashed with the greatsword, it felt like Rian’s bones were shattering.

"Unbelievable."

Such an extreme defense.

"So this is all you amounted to? That reckless momentum, that wild power—it was all just bluff?"

"Guhhh!"

It wasn’t about recovery ability.

What he feared wasn’t dying, but leaving the world behind without having protected Shirone.

‘I will protect him.’

As strength returned to his trembling hand, Ymir narrowed his eyes.

‘This...?’

It wasn’t Rian—it was as if the greatsword itself was standing before him.

"YAAAHHH!"

The greatsword, guided by Rian’s conviction, sliced through all three of Ymir’s right arms.

"Huh?"

Ymir stared blankly at his shoulder, then turned his head again.

"Haa… haa…"

Rian’s hand, reduced to bone with his muscles torn off, still clutched the greatsword.

"You…"

In that moment, Rian kicked off the ground and retreated.

"No."

He couldn’t let it end like this.

‘You’re a Yaksha. A demon. You made it this far, and now you’re going to run? You?’

Do you want to see the giant lose his mind?

"Come back."

Come...

The moment he realized Rian had already vanished from view, Ymir’s face twisted.

"GRAAAAAAAAH!"

Nane spoke.

"Sermon."

Annihilation Wave.

As the golden blade stabbed into the earth, all signs of civilization turned to dust and crumbled away.

"UAAAH! Retreat! Re—!"

Even someone’s desperate shout was swallowed by the rising clouds of dust.

Rumble…

And thus, the history of humanity came to an end.

Omega: terminated.
