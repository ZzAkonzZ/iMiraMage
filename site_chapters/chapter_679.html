Chapter 679: Sensory Emergence (3)

Judging by the size of the bioweapon, if it were operating at maximum output, even Wangseong would fall within its range.

From the moment they first began managing Radum, they had set limits on the interception radius, but if Ra Enemy was directly involved, it was a situation where those limits could easily be lifted.

*‘This is the Great Sanctification. Born 19 years ago, I let my guard down.’*

If Ra Enemy governed past events, uncovering the release code would be trivial.

Rufist infiltrated the bioweapon alone, without waiting for the others.

*‘You must resolve this, Jane.’*

All he could do was hope she would seize control of the bioweapon in time.

***

With each thrust of the rapid-fire swords, the horrific screams of the non-human races erupted.

Every member of Spectrum’s lower ranks was attacking Shagal, while Etela and her companions could only watch in stunned silence.

*‘That’s not human. That’s a demon.’*

Even Jordic, a war fanatic, trembled at the sheer carnage.

“Aaaah!”

The screams grew closer until Shagal finally broke through the crowd and emerged.

“Huuuuh!”

His face was contorted like a hieroglyph of murder, his eyes—whites fully visible—glinting with an oil-like madness that defied approach.

*‘Kill! Eliminate all life!’*

With his purpose clear and doubt erased, his Schema exhibited its highest functionality yet, reinforcing his body.

“Stop! Listen to me!”

Etela’s shout only twisted Shagal’s face further.

*‘Tia!’*

Why did she have to die?

*‘Don’t think! If all life disappears…!’*

Then there would be nothing left to think about.

“Die!”

The destructive force of the rapid-fire swords transcended combat, reaching calamitous levels as beings of all races turned on Shagal.

As if born solely to erase life, anyone who entered his radius fell in an instant, their bodies pierced and collapsing.

*‘Trapezius! Clavicle! Deltoid!’*

“Gyaaah!”

A blade sank into a goblin’s left neckline, blood spraying from the rapid-fire sword’s sheath.

*‘Sternum! Pectoralis major! External oblique! Rectus abdominis!’*

“Gueeehk!”

Pidu screamed as a blade pierced his torso.

*‘Rectus femoris! Adductor! Vastus medialis!’*

“Ughhh!”

A dagger sliced diagonally through Tubbo’s thick thigh, starting at the groin and rising to strike vital organs.

*‘Bladder! Kidneys! Stomach! Lungs! Heart!’*

—The key is to thrust swiftly.

“Shut up!”

Ignoring the ghostly voice of Raiden that refused to fade, Shagal twisted his upper body madly, stabbing daggers in all directions.

A juggling act of slaughter.

After each thrust, he twisted the blade to retrieve the sheath, eyeing the next wave of enemies.

A goblin’s jaw trembled as their eyes met.

“R-run awaaay!”

The moment the word “run” left the mouth of beings whose aggression was unmatched, the battle was as good as over.

“Damn it!”

As Shagal closed in at an inhuman speed, Arkman activated teleportation.

*‘Fire Snake…!’*

The moment he arrived, he tried to cast a spell, but pain surged from his feet, making his pupils shake.

“Ghk!”

A dagger from the rapid-fire swords had already pinned both his insteps.

If he teleported again now, his ankles would be severed—but that was better than dying.

“Aaaah!”

Tearing off his ankles and rolling across the ground, Arkman saw a rain of daggers descending.

“Idiot! Dodge!”

Jordic swung an ice whip forged from Ice Globe, but seven sheaths had already pierced Arkman’s heart.

*‘Dammit! Where is he?!’*

Jordic could track Shagal’s movements through Spirit Zone, but that was only perception.

His nerves took an eternity to produce a logical response, and Shagal exploited that gap.

“Ugh, aaaah!”

Abandoning thought, Jordic swung the whip wildly, but it found no purchase.

*Pik.*

The sound of flesh being pierced was as clean as a bird’s chirp.

“Ghk…!”

As Jordic staggered like a crab with a dagger sheath embedded in his nape, Shagal flew through the air.

Thrusting three more times in rhythm—*stab stab stab*—Jordic’s body was flung sideways.

“Aaaah!”

Blood sprayed from the pressure.

*‘At this rate, we’ll all die!’*

Etela ambushed Shagal from his blind spot, but he caught the scent of the incident and immediately shifted his focus to Ibian.

“Huhp!”

The dagger trembled inches from Ibian’s wide eyes, refusing to advance further.

*‘What?’*

The hilt of the rapid-fire sword was metal.

“Now! Quickly…!”

Before Ibian could finish, Shagal’s kick struck her ribs.

“Huuuh!”

Her ribs shattered, but if she released the magnetic force, the dagger would plunge in.

“Hmm.”

Landing gracefully, Shagal grabbed the back of Ibian’s head and shoved it toward the dagger.

*Thud.* The blade embedded itself in her forehead.

“Master!”

Elwi cast a spell, releasing a potent toxic gas.

With the masters of the three great guilds dead, this was her only means of vengeance.

“Huuuh!”

After holding her breath for nearly five minutes, Etela exhaled slowly as the fog cleared.

“Ugh! Ugh!”

Shagal, hugging Elwi from behind, slumped against a wall, covering her mouth and nose.

No matter how potent the gas, it wouldn’t blow back at the caster.

At first, he pressed the blade to her throat, but when she refused to dispel the spell, he simply sealed her airways.

Etela spoke as she watched Elwi, trembling and tearful.

“Wait. I need to talk to you…”

“Ugh! Ugh!”

Shagal ignored her, stabbing Elwi’s torso repeatedly with the dagger.

*Stab stab stab stab. Stab stab stab stab.*

It was a mechanical repetition, as if stamping a seal for fun, and with each thrust, Elwi’s body convulsed as if electrocuted.

When her life finally faded, Etela closed her eyes in silent prayer.

*‘Where this man goes, only corpses remain.’*

Over a hundred bodies—human and non-human alike—littered the ground, blood flowing like rain.

*‘But why did Master…?’*

Did he willingly give his life to this man?

“Do you hate me?”

Shagal asked.

“If you despise me, then come at me.”

As Elwi’s trembling ceased, Shagal rose, gripping the last remaining dagger.

“You’re strong. Stronger than me.”

Etela admitted it honestly.

“But if you were nothing more than evil, you couldn’t have killed my master, Archbishop Raphael.”

“I am evil.”

Shagal could never forget Raphael.

“Were you his disciple? Your master died by my hand. Take your revenge.”

“I looked into you. And I learned why my master willingly gave himself to you.”

“Hypocrisy.”

Shagal’s lips twisted bitterly.

“If you want to dress it up like that, go ahead…”

“The group called ‘Grass Leaf Circus’ never existed in this world.”

“What?”

To honor Raphael’s dying words, Etela suppressed her overwhelming urge to strike Shagal down with superhuman will.

“Your entire life is a lie. You were nothing more than a vessel for a brain.”

***

Shirone, leaping over Radum’s buildings in his search, spoke.

“Rian, let’s split up.”

Time was short, so Rian agreed.

“Then I’ll take this side. You head the opposite way. Meet at the bioweapon’s entrance.”

“Yeah. Be careful.”

Rian chuckled as if amused by Shirone’s concern, then launched himself forward.

*‘Even if I wanted to die, this body wouldn’t let me.’*

After jumping down from the building, Rian sprinted at full speed, meticulously scanning the area.

Though he couldn’t fly like a mage, the ground bore its own traces.

*‘There.’*

Following the trail of non-human corpses, Rian stopped.

Wig of the Gale.

He lay dead, his waist severed.

The expression on his face—capturing the emotions of his final moments—clearly conveyed sheer bewilderment, as if he hadn’t even realized how he’d been cut.

*‘This wasn’t a combat injury.’*

His right arm was missing below the elbow, as if torn off by force.

*Thud. Thud.*

At the eerie sound, Rian looked up to see a bespectacled goblin in a red cloak chewing on Wig’s arm.

Rian raised his greatsword as he watched the goblin’s jaw work, swallowing chunks of flesh.

“Come down. I’ll carve you up.”

“If I may argue…”

The goblin licked his teeth and spoke.

“He hunted me first.”

Wig’s arm swayed.

“Kill me for money, buy food with it. How is this any different?”

The goblin’s identity was Kido the Devourer, leader of the Speedkillers.

He was the “taste” of the Great Sanctification.

***

Rian didn’t deny Kido’s words.

He had experienced enough to know how human nature manifested in extreme situations.

He had simply endured more than others, not because his nature was different.

“Come down. Let’s settle this.”

He would cut him down simply because he was an enemy.

Erasing countless meanings through simple goal-setting was Rian’s strength.

“Humans are so arrogant.”

Kido jumped down from his perch, landing lightly and spinning his spear, producing a sharp whistling sound.

*‘He’s agile.’*

Rian, who had fought Wig directly, knew better than anyone how fast the Gale was.

*‘I’ll assume he’s even faster.’*

Kido vanished, and Rian twisted his torso, raising his greatsword vertically just as the blades clashed.

*Clang.*

But only the sound remained.

Kido’s speed, disappearing again, sent chills down Rian’s spine. He realized his miscalculation.

*‘Not just faster.’*

Far faster.

Though he hadn’t witnessed the fight, it was clear Wig had been defeated without landing a single counterattack.

*Whistle whistle. Whistle whistle.*

The spear’s whistling, though a fatal flaw in revealing his position, was now so fast it was distracting.

“Keke, as expected of the Mach Knight.”

*‘He knows me?’*

Kido, clinging to his spear like a wheel, rolled across the ground, sparks flying where the blade struck.

The intervals between sparks grew—4 meters, 5 meters, 6 meters—until they erupted around Rian as if piercing him.

*‘I’ll cut here!’*

Unconsciously rolling forward, Kido swung his spear, but Rian kicked off the ground and soared into the air.

“Hmph, walking on two legs like the rest…”

Kido, reaching Rian’s landing point first, slid across the ground and spun, unleashing his spear technique.

“What…?!”

From Kido’s spinning perspective, Rian remained suspended in mid-air.

*‘Magic?’*

Impossible!

*‘Why isn’t he falling? Unless…?’*

He had transcended gravity.

A being who left the embrace of the earth—mother of all life—to soar toward the ideal.

The realization sent a shiver down his spine.

*‘Cut!’*

As Rian’s divine transcendence denied the law of gravity, the sky behind him split in two and surged forward.

“Guhhh!”

Kido halted his spin and flung himself aside just as the greatsword struck like lightning.

*Kwaaaaang!*

The ground split with a deafening roar, and Rian emerged through the dust.

“Wait! Stop!”

Kido shouted, but Rian’s resolve was unshaken.

“Shirone!”

Kido squeezed his eyes shut at the sight of the greatsword.

“…needs me…”

Realizing his tongue still moved, he cracked his eyes open to see the blade halted inches from his face.

*‘He stopped it? But how?’*

The sound of Rian’s muscles twisting echoed as he negated the colossal momentum.

“What about Shirone?”

It was the only keyword that could stop Rian, and Kido slumped in relief.

“Whew, so this is the power of Mach. I underestimated you because you lack a Schema, but you’re a real monster.”

The greatsword’s tip pressed into Kido’s stomach.

“I’ll hear one more statement.”

“Shirone needs me.”

The words spilled out without pause.

“And I need Shirone.”

“…What nonsense is this?”

“Shirone is looking for Ra Enemy. So am I. That’s why I left the Speedkillers and came here.”

Now that he mentioned it, Kido was alone.

“And you expect me to believe that? Weren’t you part of Spectrum? Serving Ra Enemy?”

“It’s complicated.”

Kido shook his head.

“I don’t think he’s real. He only exists through events. Of course, he came to me too. Offered to make me king of goblins, even king of humans. But I refused.”

“Why? Isn’t that what you all want?”

“Because I knew he killed my sister.”

Rian frowned.

“You’re not making sense. First, you say Ra Enemy only exists through events, now you claim he killed your sister?”

“He eats.”

Kido pointed at his throat with a long finger.

“After my sister died, I ate her corpse.”

It wasn’t unusual in goblin society.

“When you eat a being, their memories surface. Not all, but specific ones. I call it ‘the taste of memory.’”

Rian slowly lowered his greatsword.

“At first, I thought all goblins were like that. So I lived without much thought. Ate people, ate all sorts of things. Then I realized: I’m the only goblin who likes reading books and whistling music.”

Kido stood, brushing himself off.

“When I ate my sister, the strongest memory was of Ra Enemy. I don’t know how, but she seemed to have died from shock in utter despair. He probably killed her to provoke my rage. But as you can see…”

Kido shrugged, spreading his arms.

“I’m not your average goblin.”

“So what? You’re here for revenge?”

“I’d like that. But it’s probably just the emotions of something I ate. Goblins don’t seek revenge. Then I learned about you and Shirone.”

Through Wig’s flesh.

“And after eating your flesh, I learned about Shirone’s connection to Ra Enemy. According to those memories, the senses of hearing, touch, and smell are gathered in Radum. And if I’m the sense of taste…”

Then it was almost certain.

“Ra Enemy is designing something. Colliding variables to induce specific events. Our gathering is no coincidence. I don’t know about sight, but it’ll probably appear when the time comes.”

“Hmm, so variables colliding…”

*‘I don’t get it.’*

Kido cut to the chase.

“Either way, we have two choices. Live as Ra Enemy designed, or reject it.”

For Kido, who had awakened to transcendence, reclaiming free will was as vital as life itself.

“To choose the latter, we first need to know what he’s designing. I can do that.”

Kido pointed at his sharp teeth.

“If we materialize Ra Enemy through the five senses, I’ll eat him. Steal his design through the taste of memory.”

*‘That would help Shirone.’*

Before deciding, Rian glanced regretfully at Wig’s corpse.
