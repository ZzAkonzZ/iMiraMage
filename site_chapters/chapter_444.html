Chapter 444:

"Humans are amusing. Their emotions are so visible," Garas sneered, sticking out its long tongue as it pinned Plu down.

Born late and having all its breeding targets taken by its siblings, the human before it was the perfect prey.

As Garas said, Plu's eyes were filled with fear, but not of the horrific events to come.

In truth, she had a vague suspicion.

The persistent headaches and the feeling of something being disconnected.

She was almost certain that even if she were the original, she would have made the same judgment.

Death is a deeply personal experience. The fear of annihilation is the same for both the original and the clone.

Plu's ability to smile dryly in the face of death came from the satisfaction of fulfilling her duty as a mage.

"Take this."

Garas didn't understand the meaning of a human raising their middle finger.

In that brief moment of confusion, Plu's eyes slowly closed.

'Shirone, become a great mage.'

The documents Shirone had seen likely contained evidence that she was a clone.

'Thank you for saying that.'

He had promised to come and save her.

She was happy that he had recognized her as Plu, not just a cloned being, until the very end.

A clone's lifespan was only a week.

But it had been a wonderful life.

Plu's hand fell limply to the floor, and Garas realized that the life beneath it had become an object.

Breeding is ultimately a desire for species preservation. It had no interest in a being that couldn't produce offspring.

But the emotional drop from the peak of excitement to the bottom was unbearably frustrating.

"Raaaargh!"

Garas grabbed Plu by the neck and threw her against the wall, then raised its upper body and let out a deafening roar.

A living creature! It needed a living creature!

Its highly evolved sense of smell, specialized for breeding, detected another creature far away.

"This time, I won't miss it!"

Garas's shadow stretched down the corridor like a black wind as it followed the scent.

Raycis struggled with all her might.

But as her muscle fibers were broken down by the plasm, her limbs moved in directions beyond her control.

'No! I have to reverse this!'

Shirone, trapped in glass sphere 1, was a Type B equipped with the Ultima System.

With the main type set to him, if the Elixir of Legends proceeded, all organic matter would merge around Shirone.

'How did he survive?'

Even seeing Plu with her own eyes, she couldn't believe the situation.

Could she escape the glass sphere? How had things come to this? Was the woman before her really Plu?

She thought she needed to think, but her mind was stuck in a loop of unanswered questions.

Plu, who had modified the Fuzix Machine, slowly turned around. Almost simultaneously, the plasm filled to the top, covering Raycis.

As Plu approached the glass sphere with calm steps, Raycis's palm hit the inside with a thud.

Raycis pressed her face against the glass. Her eyelids were gone, giving her a startled expression, and her eyes were filled with unresolved questions.

Plu raised the ring finger of her right hand. The spot where her Cubrick should have been was much lighter in color than the rest of her skin.

'So, the original? But since when…?'

Plu had removed the Cubrick a week ago, during the time she had investigated the underground lab while Shirone was away.

After examining the clone isolation room, she had intuited Raycis's intention in bringing Shirone to the command center.

To mass-produce Shirone's clones, she used Plu, who shared the same world's genes.

Adding Garas's traits made it possible to rapidly grow the clones to adulthood.

After investigating the Fuzix Machine, the memory transfer device, and the traits of various creatures, she devised a plan.

Transfer the original's memories to a clone in the isolation room.

By cutting off the last memory and giving the drone and Cubrick to the clone, she entered the isolation room herself, waiting for a chance to counterattack. It was physically and psychologically grueling, but highly effective.

The most perfect way to defeat an opponent is to stab them in the back.

Using the psychological blind spots of the entire command center, it was a strategy based on the assassin's adage that even gods can be killed if caught off guard.

'Could she have been hiding in the isolation room?'

Raycis stared at Plu with a blank expression.

Why hadn't she suspected it even once?

The Cubrick, always worn during physical exams. The unconscious belief that translation was impossible without the drone left no room to accept new situations.

"Stop making that dumb face and disappear, you demon."

Raycis couldn't hear Plu's words clearly through the liquid. But the emotions she felt were much stronger than what she heard.

'How dare you…!'

Raycis gritted her teeth. Her rubbery teeth shattered instantly.

"It doesn't change anything! Everything will still become one! I will achieve perfect beauty! I have won!"

Ignoring Raycis's muffled shouts, Plu ran to glass sphere 1, where Shirone was, and banged on the wall.

"Shirone, wake up! Shirone!"

When she had escaped the isolation room and arrived here, the Elixir of Legends was already well underway.

Her first thought was to cancel it, but that was too emotional a decision.

If she canceled it and Shirone was more than half dissolved in the plasm, there would be nothing to gain.

Instead, she decided to proceed and change the main type to Shirone. Even if he became a monster, the project could continue.

Shirone would understand.

Because he was a mage.

"Shirone! Shirone!"

But in her heart, she desperately prayed for Shirone to wake up and do something.

As Plu's voice traveled through the liquid medium, Shirone's stopped heart twitched.

Unlike Raycis, who was 70% dissolved, Shirone's body, protected by Armand, was still intact.

Armand, integrated with Shirone through the Ultima System, attempted a final method based on the basic instinct for life.

A powerful shock strong enough to burst his heart jolted Shirone's body in the water.

Another shock, and as his heart was compressed again, his pupils flew open, and air escaped his mouth.

"Gasp! Gasp!"

The shock of escaping death made Shirone thrash his limbs wildly.

A dull thud echoed from the glass wall, and he faintly heard Plu's voice.

'Senior?'

She was alive. Realizing that, he quickly regained his composure.

If he hadn't experienced the second stage of the survival test, Ice Prison, he wouldn't have recovered his senses so quickly.

'Stay cold. The coldest state of mind.'

Finally, he felt Armand merging with him. That meant he was trapped in the glass sphere, and if this continued, he would be absorbed by Raycis.

'Should I cast Rampage…?'

As he considered breaking the glass sphere to escape, the memory of his near-death experience connected, and he quickly reversed his decision.

Raycis was a four-sided Mara, something he had never heard of.

Having already lost one battle, escaping now would only make him prey for an even stronger Raycis.

Though the process was different, Shirone reached the same conclusion as Plu and withdrew his Spirit Zone, instead strengthening Armand.

'Endure. Just endure!'

He didn't know which consciousness would dominate when the Elixir of Legends ended, but with Armand equipped with the Ultima System, he had a chance against the four-sided Mara.

As Armand's organic material regenerated to its limit, it protected Shirone's body from the plasm.

Finally, after enduring for a minute, the plasm began to drain from the bottom of the glass sphere.

"Grrr!"

Shirone broke through the shell and emerged from the water, pulling Armand down with him.

He had survived, but the outcome was uncertain.

If Armand lost control to Raycis, what would emerge in the central glass chamber would be a demon, not a magic sword.

Kuuuuuu!

Raycis opened her eyes to the sound of burning air. Everything was clear in the liquid state.

The undulating fluidity, the electrical stimuli triggering life, Ikasa's Valhalla Action, the magic of the Jinma species Galtomic, the hard armor of Ringer—all of it merged and surged in at once.

Everything in the world became one.

The beautiful and the ugly, didn't they all originate from one?

As a blue light descended from afar, turning the world silver, Raycis smiled happily.

In the reflected light, she saw herself before meeting Satan.

Pure white skin, a slightly more developed chest than others, firm thighs…

'Haha. My feet are still ugly, though.'

Raycis shed clear tears.

It was the most beautiful form she had ever wanted, so desperately desired.

'Now… it's done.'

The blue light flared like a flash, and everything returned to one.

"Cough! Cough!"

With Plu's help, Shirone emerged from the glass sphere, vomiting the murky liquid.

The pain was immense, as if his stomach had dissolved.

"Shirone, are you okay?"

"No. It hurts so much. But how did you…?"

At that moment, the central glass chamber flashed, signaling the end of the Elixir of Legends.

Shirone and Plu stopped talking and watched with tense expressions as the glass chamber opened with a clunk.

Armand, glowing in a rainbow of colors, stood proudly.

"Success, it's a success."

The Ultima System in Armand had acted as the main type, integrating the other traits.

Without time to celebrate, Shirone walked forward. His stomach was in agony, and he needed to activate Diamond Armor as soon as possible.

Boom!

The lab door shattered as countless Garas of various forms poured in. With no more breeding targets left in the underground lab, they all flooded into this room.

"Kiiik! Kiiik!"

Hundreds of Garas, driven to the peak of frustration, were in a state of extreme excitement.

As Shirone grabbed Armand, Plu activated Phoenix.

Even without Phoenix, the power of the Phoenix Flame was immense, but the burning Garas attacked relentlessly, undeterred by death.

"Kikiki! Found you."

Plu, struggling to fend off the Garas, turned her gaze to the door.

All the Garas stopped attacking and made way for a newly arrived Garas.

Plu's upper body bent backward under the immense pressure of the Dawkins algorithm.

All she could see was a dark blur passing before her eyes. In terms of speed, it surpassed her.

"Kikiki! This is fun!"

Seeing a human identical to the one who had raised their middle finger before dying, Garas's excitement soared even higher, activating its cells.

The real-time evolution finally bridged the gap of the missing link, achieving its ultimate form.

Its curved spine straightened, and its face became more human-like, resembling the bald man.

As Plu felt the air around her ripple, she enveloped herself in an air shield, but the air expanded explosively, pushing her back.

'Ugh! Spirit Force…!'

The affinity unique to the Norin. Although Garas was born from the bald man, its power was far greater.

"I should tear off your limbs first!"

Garas turned into a black blur and charged at Plu, who braced herself for the impact.

The Dawkins algorithm might allow her to dodge, but she had to be prepared for her spine to snap.

"Raaaargh!"

At that moment, Garas stopped. Its shoulders hunched as if tied by a rope, and it let out a scream, raising its upper body. Plu quickly turned around.

Shirone, having activated Diamond Armor, was glaring at Garas with a terrifying look.

"Antithesis?"

It was a form of Diamond Armor unlike any she had seen before.
