Chapter 749:

Holy War

The Holy War is a supranational organization formed by the Tri-Imperial System, the Seven Star Kingdoms, and the Two Kings, operating the Valkyrie unit, which is a force against Heaven.

Founded by Terraje and supported by 24 countries, including the Holy War, it is the strongest military force of humanity.

With the activities of Heaven quiet outside the Immortal Community, countries around the world were suggesting that the Valkyrie should be the vanguard against the 72 Demon Legions.

"Kashan, enter!"

To the tune of Kashan's national anthem played by the Cotria Republic's military band, Uorin walked with a dignified stride.

The guards, divided into inner and outer circles, spread out in a wide radius, crossing their gazes like a net.

Behind Uorin, the strongest swordsmanship group, Pungjang, walked with identical strides, not an inch out of sync.

'I thought I would get used to this by now...'

Gando served as Uorin's aide.

'But I never do.'

The Holy War's timing and location changed each time, but it was always a grand event where the world's leaders displayed their national power, incomparable to any other meeting.

"Welcome the state guests!"

Along the golden bridge, the Cotria Republic's honor guard spun their heavy spears, welcoming the queen.

'They've prepared thoroughly.'

The Cotria Republic, part of the Seven Star Kingdoms, is a peninsula nation extending like a dolphin from the southern part of the Gustav Empire.

With no contact with other countries except the northern Gustav, it should have been swallowed by the Empire, but Cotria managed to protect its territory under the proud name of the Seven Star Kingdoms.

'They must have done their best to be proud. But the biggest reason is...'

Because they possess the ancient weapon Ex Machina.

It is presumed to be a type of map weapon, and Uorin had mentioned that it might be a law-based weapon rather than a power-based one.

"Gando."

Uorin called quietly.

"Have you heard from Shirone yet?"

Since becoming one of the Five Great Stars, Uorin had sent messages to Shirone, who was wandering the world, but had not yet received a reply.

"No, Your Majesty."

Gando thought for a moment and then added.

"Shall I send someone again?"

If a polite letter was coldly rejected, the Empire would have no choice but to use coercive means.

"No, it's fine. He must be busy."

Uorin's voice was filled with disappointment, which surprised Gando.

'She seems less composed than usual today.'

Uorin called again.

"Gando."

"Yes, Your Majesty. What are your orders?"

"Sit next to me today."

"Pardon?"

He had accompanied her to the Holy War several times, but she had never given him a seat next to her.

"The Emperor of Gustav has changed."

Gustav Habitz XVII.

According to the memories of the previous Mistra, he was an intriguing human, almost on par with herself.

'He must be quite old by now. I wonder what kind of person he has become.'

"Do I deserve such an honor? If you're worried, should I give separate instructions to Pungjang?"

Although he took care of all the queen's conveniences, in terms of combat power, Gando was among the top of the inner guards.

"It's fine. Just stay by my side."

"...Understood."

If Uorin's concern wasn't about force, then Gando was a suitable choice.

After the Tri-Imperial System's entrance, the remaining six countries, excluding Cotria, displayed their military power as they entered the castle gates.

The Middle Eastern Paras, the Eastern Wen, the Central Continent's Jive, the Southern Continent's Iron, the Southern Tribal Union, and the Ten Kingdoms of the Southern Continent Sea represented by Arakne.

The status of a country is relative, and its national power cannot be calculated by numbers alone, but none of the Seven Star Kingdoms were weaker than Shirone's homeland, Tormia.

"Two Kings, enter!"

After all the human troops had entered, the representatives of the Dragon and Fairy races appeared.

Unlike the human rulers who reached the peak of display, there were only two of them.

The Dragon race had pride in being the strongest in any situation, and the Fairy race disliked ostentation.

"It's been a while, Apostle of Time."

Enoch, the representative of the Fairy race, greeted the approaching dragon with a body as large as a house.

First-class dragon Blitz.

With a sharp snout like a sword, a sleek body, and blue scales that shimmered as if electricity flowed through them.

"Thunderwing Dragon, I didn't expect you to come."

He was the best in combat among the Twelve Apostles but disliked getting involved in such political affairs.

"I don't like it, but I have no choice. I don't care about the Holy War, but there's an important issue today."

"An important issue for the Dragon race... Hmm."

Enoch, who had been lost in thought with his chin resting on his hand, smiled broadly at Blitz.

"Is the sun going to explode?"

"Not funny."

Enoch's face immediately turned cold, and he pursed his lips.

"Yes, yes, would a noble being like you laugh at the joke of a lowly fairy like me? Must be nice to be so noble."

"..."

Blitz, who had been walking silently for a while, roughly turned his neck in front of the castle gate.

"How can you not fix that damn bipolar disorder even after 4,000 years?"

"Mind your own business. Should someone like you be talking to me?"

Fairies, being pure, had extreme emotional swings. If humans were creatures of anger, fairies had their own form of melancholy.

'In some ways, I dislike them more than humans.'

There were humans who fell in love with and married fairies, but most ended badly due to this personality.

"Thank you for coming. Please, come in."

As the minister waiting at the castle gate bowed, a broad smile appeared on Enoch's face again.

"Hello. Is His Majesty well?"

Even from a distance, when he saw Uorin, Enoch's appearance was a different kind of beauty from humans.

"Ah, yes. He is very well."

When Blitz raised his head, the soldier on the castle wall was so startled that he fell on his backside.

"Eek!"

"The castle gate is low."

Should a dragon have to lower its head to enter?

"I'm sorry. It was built to human height..."

It was Blitz's first time participating in the Holy War, but the minister couldn't say that.

"Hmm..."

As Blitz looked down at the minister with dissatisfaction, the sound of bones twisting began to be heard from his body.

As Enoch frowned, his body gradually compressed and transformed into a human form.

It was a transformation only possible for a first-class dragon.

"This should do."

The sharp blue scales replaced his hair, and the eyes of thunder flashed under the protruding eye bones.

"Ah, of course. Thank you."

To the music of the military band, the trumpeter shouted.

"Two Kings! Enter!"

The Holy War began.

Three thousand demon troops surrounded Zulu's pyramid, standing alone in the land of death.

"Go in! Kill everyone you see!"

Every time Division Commander Gassias swung his saw-like weapon, the monsters emerging from the dungeon were cut to pieces.

"Pitiful creatures."

Without a heart, Zulu muttered empty words and raised his hand from the top of the pyramid.

"Erga."

Endless abyss of darkness.

A black bird flew towards the sky and was crushed with a cracking sound.

Just as Zulu had done long ago.

Screeeech!

The crumpled black mass spread into a giant figure, and the 10-meter-tall reaper unleashed thunder.

"Charge! No retreat!"

The curtain of lightning rippled like waves, but the demons attacked the pyramid, ignoring their bursting flesh.

"Shall we, Erga?"

As the lightning disappeared, the lich raised both hands to the sky, and a ghostly smoke swirled.

"Wrath of the Dead."

A death magic that the living cannot use, a terrible disaster that only Zulu could manifest.

"Huff! Huff!"

Kangnan, holding two machetes, was tearing through the pyramid's labyrinth.

'How many are there?'

He had occasionally encountered demons in the desert, but this was the first time facing such a large army.

"Die! Suffer, humans!"

As soldiers with blue veins like corpses charged with spears, Kangnan threw himself into the blades.

"Yaaah!"

Swinging his machetes wildly, the enemies' heads fell one after another.

"Rabe! Nabera Um!"

As he cut through the demons, Division Commander Gassias swung his saw-like greatsword.

"Ugh!"

Kangnan crossed his machetes in an X to block, but the saw teeth pulled, causing him to lose his grip.

"I know how to make women suffer!"

Gassias charged with his shoulder.

"But I only kill!"

"What...!"

Having lost his weapon, Kangnan twisted his upper body into a stance unique to the Ram Muai.

"Shut up!"

A powerful high kick struck the armor, sending the heavy body rolling back and crashing into the wall.

"Commander!"

Gassias shook his head, unable to believe the impact from a human, especially a woman.

"You won't get past here!"

Every time Kangnan swung his shin like a whip, the demons' knees shattered, and their heads exploded.

"...Is that so?"

To demons, cruelty was as natural as humans chewing pigs to fill their bellies.

"Advance, all troops!"

All the demons parted to the sides in front of Kangnan and began running deeper into the labyrinth.

"No! Not there...!"

As Kangnan, imagining the worst, shouted with a pale face, Gassias swung his greatsword.

"Uoooooo!"

When the saw blade struck her abdomen, sparks flew in Kangnan's eyes.

"Graaaah!"

With a wolf's howl, Kangnan pulled her abdomen in and caught the blade with her elbows and knees.

Ram Muai Strike Technique - Arang's.

Gassias, glaring at the wolf's illusion, swung the sword, collapsing the wall.

Rumble!

"Ugh!"

As Kangnan rolled on the floor, clutching her abdomen, the saw blade of the greatsword snapped with a sharp sound.

'She broke a sword made of hellsteel.'

Though he acknowledged her as a warrior, demons could be as treacherous as needed to achieve Satan's goals.

"Sorry, but this is the end."

Kangnan sprang to her feet.

"Gaold!"

No matter how complex the labyrinth, with this many troops, someone would eventually reach the lowest level.

"Commander! Over here!"

Hearing the voice from the floor below, Kangnan descended the stairs to find Gaold's door wide open.

"Noooo!"

As Kangnan shouted from the end of the hallway and launched herself with a powerful push off the ground...

Boom!

The pyramid shook violently up and down.

"......"

The surroundings were silent, and only the sound of demons running upstairs echoed through the walls.

"Oh..."

Kangnan cautiously entered Gaold's room.

All the demons were crushed into bloody pulp, and in the corner lay a chunk of meat presumed to be Commander Gassias.

Surveying them all, she finally fixed her gaze on Gaold, who was so emaciated that only bones remained.

"Oh..."

She wanted to say something, but seeing Gaold's face twisted in pain, fear overwhelmed her.

"Mutt."

When Gaold raised his hand, the nerves on the back of his hand twitched, and his fingers trembled uncontrollably.

"Do you...?"

With a hoarse voice, Gaold struggled to speak, slowly folding his fingers except for the index and middle fingers.

"Got any cigarettes left?"

"Ugh!"

Before the hot tears could blur her vision, Kangnan, on the verge of tears, ran to Gaold.
