Chapter 1196: Omega Year 999 (1)

The more humans were affected by the pyramids built across the world, the stronger the Maga of the Outer World became.

That amplified Maga was directly transmitted to the demons, contributing to their assault on the impregnable Ivory Tower.

"Charge! Charge!"

Demons, appearing as if all the creatures of the world had been fused at random with humans, stormed forward.

"Crush intelligence! We are victorious!"

As befitting the title of the 1st Legion, their sheer force was overwhelming—even the Ivory Tower's Stars struggled to hold them back.

Minerva shouted.

"Where is Shirone?"

Having activated Ataraxia Close Combat, Shirone had already disappeared deep into the demon army.

"I can’t see him! Either he made it into the Ivory Tower, or…"

Or he didn’t.

At that moment, an explosion of light erupted near the Ivory Tower’s entrance, sending a pillar of radiance into the sky.

‘There he is.’

The sheer power of Close Combat was undeniable.

‘He’s risking everything to save Great Star. But he can’t keep this up for long. We need to send reinforcements.’

The Stars of the Ivory Tower focused their power to push forward, but the resistance of the demons only grew stronger.

They had tried to drive in a wedge to break the enemy’s formation, but instead, they had gotten caught in an unbreakable stone.

‘A frontal assault won’t work.’ Minerva spoke.

"Mimeon."

From a pool of human blood, a woman’s face emerged, swelling outward.

"Yes, 5 Star."

She was a 1-Star resident of the Ivory Tower, possessing the ability to liquefy her body like slime.

"Forget the battlefield and head for the Ivory Tower. Help Shirone. Someone has to get inside."

"Understood."

As Mimeon melted back into the blood, a familiar voice called out from behind Minerva.

"5 Star."

Golden-haired and brimming with vitality, Ariana stood with Mini nestled against her chest.

Minerva turned fully to face them.

"Can you two ever act normal?"

"Don’t you know? A pair is like one body. The demons are getting stronger. In an hour, their power will surpass the Ivory Tower’s forces. We need to break through now."

"Do you have a plan?"

"We push the wedge strategy to the extreme. Using Mass Teleport, we displace the demons in a triangular formation. The moment that gap forms, we hit them with everything we have."

It was a solid strategy.

"A wedge of normal size won’t do. It has to be massive. And if they’re teleported away, they’ll be trampled by the demons the moment they arrive."

Mini had already steeled herself for death.

"That’s why Ariana and I will do it. Corona Kingdom doesn’t have the means to leave only allies behind."

Ariana added,

"More importantly, Shirone is fighting on the front lines. Our division should handle this."

"Sister."

A voice called out from the defensive line.

"I’m sorry."

The bodies of 3-Star resident Gale, satellite Moskotch, and Mimeon’s satellite Mari were torn apart.

"Kuahahaha! Victory is ours!"

The remaining Stars fought with everything they had, but they wouldn’t last another five minutes.

‘Yeah. You trash fought hard.’

Most of the Stars in the Human Safety Enforcement Bureau had once been threats to humanity themselves.

"Fine, we go with that strategy," Minerva said.

"But I’ll be the one to execute it. I’ll send the demons flying—you break through the entrance and help Shirone."

"But—"

Minerva shook her head.

"It needs to be done properly. And if I do it, there’s at least a chance I’ll survive."

It was a painful lie.

"Understood. I’m sorry."

"Get ready."

As soon as the command was given, the Stars dispersed. Minerva turned to look at the Ivory Tower once more.

‘Shirone.’

"You’ve become prettier."

‘Thanks for saying that.’

With the changes in Law, she was no longer a witch who manipulated people’s desires.

‘But…’

It was far too little to heal the scars engraved in her heart.

‘I’m still a witch. The Great Witch of an era that led countless people to their deaths.’

Minerva’s body shrank into that of a ten-year-old girl.

‘There’s no way to wash it away.’

The memories remained.

The childhood of suffering, trapped in the hands of adults, her body forever in that form.

—It hurts! It hurts!

—It’s okay.

—I’m not the one in pain.

"Kh…!"

Tears streamed down the young girl’s face.

‘That’s when I realized.’

When you move your jaw to chew food, you never really think about it.

‘Just repeat, over and over, until the hunger is satisfied… again and again…’

What is humanity?

‘Nothing but machines made to torment others.’

She was ready to pay for her sins. But there was one thing that would never change.

‘I do not regret it.’

One fact remained unshaken—she could never forgive humans.

‘So, Shirone.’

Do not forgive me.

Minerva’s Spirit Zone took the shape of a wedge, growing at an incredible speed.

In an instant, she identified over ten thousand entities, distinguishing friend from foe, and her body began to glow brightly.

"Mass Teleport."

A triangular light bloomed within the demon army, and in an instant, they vanished as if they had been erased.

Mini shouted.

"The entrance is visible!"

Though it was no wider than a needle’s eye, it was the only opening they could reach.

"Charge!"

The Corona Kingdom forces roared, and the remaining Stars gave their all as they surged toward the entrance.

A dull impact echoed from a snowy mountain 20 kilometers away from the Ivory Tower.

"Ugh!"

Minerva, using her jet to shield herself from the attack, found her body embedded in an ice cliff like an engraving.

The demons were furious.

"You wretched thing."

Given their abilities, at least 20 kilometers of distance was needed to buy time.

But they were the elite of the 1st Legion, warriors forged in a global war.

"Don’t think we’ll just kill you quickly."

The demons had analyzed the situation the moment Mass Teleport was activated and had already begun closing in on Minerva.

As cracks spread through the ice cliff, Minerva’s body fell ten meters down.

"Whew…"

Leaning against the wall, the 10-year-old girl looked at the demons with a resigned expression.

‘Wait for me, you trash. I’ll be there soon.’

The demons approached.

"Heh, looks like you’ve given up. Well, there’s no one here to save you now."

Her body convulsed.

"You won’t even be able to lift a finger. We can’t have you killing yourself now, can we? Let’s see how long you last."

"… Suicide?"

Minerva scoffed.

‘That would’ve been better. If I had just died and disappeared alone, this catastrophe never would have happened.’

The Walking Dead she had unleashed had killed countless people.

‘Afraid of dying?’

She had once tried to hang herself, but the reason she couldn’t go through with it was…

‘Out of sheer frustration.’

She was so angry that she wanted to die, but even more furious at herself for not being able to do so.

‘And that made me want to die even more!’

Even with the noose around her neck, she could still see the faces of those wretched people, alive and well.

‘I’ll kill them all!’

And so, she became a witch.

"You bastards think you’re something special? When you’re nothing but puppets, swayed by pathetic human emotions."

As Minerva’s eyes burned with killing intent, the power that had restrained her shattered.

"Do you know who I am?"

She bit down on her pipe as she watched the demons charge toward her, filling the landscape.

"I am the one who created Lawslayer."

"Kill her!"

A thick, toxic smoke spilled from her lips.

Walking Dead.

20 minutes later.

"Ugh! Gah!"

Both her legs were broken, her right shoulder dislocated. Minerva crawled across the snow, dragging herself forward.

"Stubborn bitch."

Only seven demons remained.

"Haah… Haah…"

As she struggled to the edge of the cliff and leaned against it, the demons sneered as they approached.

"A shame. If luck had been on your side, you could have pulled off one of the greatest operations and lived."

Minerva spat out the blood pooling in her mouth.

"And what of it? Dumbass."

Had she not been a Grand Magician, her life would have already been extinguished by now.

"Heh, that’s the spirit. But I hope you’re ready—you’ll have to suffer in place of your fallen comrades."

She twitched her left fingers, still sprawled out on the ground.

"Yeah, come on. Give it a shot."

Even in the face of death, an overwhelming aura of bloodlust radiated from her, making the demons grimace.

"What the hell is this? She’s more twisted than us."

"Forget it. We’re running out of time—let’s end this quickly. She’ll scream soon enough once we start peeling her apart."

The demons reached out and grabbed her arm.

‘I won’t scream.’

She looked up at the gray sky.

‘Yeah, I’ll be joining you soon, you bastards. I’ll probably suffer for a few hours first, though.’

"Let’s start here."

The blade that touched her wrist was as cold as ice.

Just as she registered the sensation, a gust of wind swept through.

Thin lines traced across the demons’ bodies.

And then, they were cut to pieces in an instant.

"W-Who…! Urk!"

The last demon’s head was severed, and as it hit the ground, a familiar silhouette flickered before her eyes.

"Shirone?"

She shook her head.

‘No, different.’

He looked exactly like Shirone, but the icy gaze was completely detached from Yahweh’s presence.

‘Perhaps…’

She muttered.

"… Saint Brain."

The full name was unknown, but Minerva had a vague idea.

"What brings you all the way here?"

As if the conversation was burdensome, the brain erased Shirone’s image and returned to its original form.

"Answer me. You never participated in the faction wars, so why the sudden change of heart?"

The brain cast another illusion.

She had seen many of his forms before, but this one was new.

Which meant—this was his true form.

And more than that…

"You’re Gaian, aren’t you?"

His metallic hair cascaded down his back, his face calm, without eyebrows.

The Sacred Brain spoke.

"I am Adam. The first of all humans, and the last Gaian remaining in this world."

Then, he corrected himself.

"Technically, just a brain."

"Yeah, sounds about right. But that doesn’t matter to me. Why are you here?"

"I came to say goodbye."

"Goodbye?"

Minerva scoffed.

"I know how Great Star determines 4-Star rankings. Even if we’re in the same department, you’re not part of our inner circle."

Adam looked up at the sky.

"I lived as one of the Stars of the Ivory Tower, mimicking the lives of countless humans—to decipher Law. I feel nothing but regret for your life. It is my responsibility."

"For what?"

"For everything. We ruined it. It wasn’t just her fault. I should have stopped it."

"Uorin?"

Adam did not answer.

"The end is near. My brain is the only one capable of computing Law. I will undo what was done wrong."

"Don’t give me that crap. If you think you can talk your way out of this, you’re dead wrong. Kill me. That’s the only way to atone."

"It’s too soon for that, my daughter."

Adam rose into the sky, shrouded in radiant mist.

"Death is not the only form of atonement. Existence is bound by necessity. One day, you will understand your purpose."

If Adam truly could compute Law, then his words could not be ignored.

"Where are you going?"

Adam turned his gaze beyond the heavens.

"To Eve."

A single streak of light shot across the sky.

Minerva watched it disappear, then let herself collapse to the side.

"Ugh! Ugh!"

Crawling forward, she reached for her pipe, lying in the snow, and took a deep drag.

"Whew…"

The gray clouds above looked like the faces of her fallen subordinates.

"Sorry… but I think I’ll live a little longer."

At that moment, as if by magic, the blizzard cleared, and sunlight bathed the snowy mountains.
