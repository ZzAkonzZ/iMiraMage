Chapter 1240:

“Do you know why I hate evil?” Shirone said.

“It’s because people are under the illusion that happiness and suffering are somehow equal. If someone hears ten compliments and ten insults, are they supposed to feel completely neutral?”

Shirone’s fist struck Havitz’s face.

“Ugh!”

“...It’s perfectly normal to feel like crap.”

The human heart is not neutral.

“Stop trying to balance it out. No human being considers happiness and suffering to have equal value. There’s not a single person in the world who wants to suffer. Even you—who found pleasure in using countless people as your prey—”

As particle destruction magic rained down, Havitz writhed on the ground.

“Aaaaagh!”

“Isn’t this one moment of pain far more terrifying?”

Why?

“Why do you keep using faulty logic just to hurt others...”

Shirone grabbed Havitz’s head with both hands and cast magic again.

“Raaaaagh! Raaaaagh!”

Sparks flew from Havitz’s eyes, and blood streamed from his eyes, nose, and mouth.

“Why should I have to forgive you?”

Shirone was on the verge of tears.

“Say it! You were happy all this time, so you can handle a little pain, right? You laughed at everyone else—go ahead, laugh now!”

To reach Ultima, he would have to accept even evil into his heart... but Shirone couldn’t bring himself to do it.

“S-Satan...”

Twelve members of Hell Prison, their faces blackened and burnt, crawled toward Havitz.

“Graaagh! Graaaagh!”

Havitz let out inhuman cries as if his soul had left his body, gazing deep into his own mind.

A sense that light was tearing through at a speed unknown to the world.

“Wh... what...?”

As he reached a point where thinking was no longer possible, an involuntary word escaped his lips.

“...Mom.”

Startled by his own voice, he quickly snapped back to his senses and triggered Vanishing.

I have to run.

Anywhere would do—as long as it was a place without Yahweh.

I know how now. I just don’t have to kill him. If Giyorgi kills him, it’s fine.

Shirone stood up.

“You’re too late.”

He had already anticipated Havitz’s Vanishing, and so he murmured:

“Elikia.”

As the brilliant light exploded outward, Havitz’s jaw trembled as his Vanishing was forcibly undone.

“Huff... Huff...!”

He didn’t even dare look back—he simply ran with all his might.

Mom.

Was it a dream? Or a near-death hallucination caused by dying brain cells?

Havitz ripped at the shredded flesh on his ruined face, laughing hysterically.

“Hehehe...!”

In truth, he hadn’t seen anything.

He had only felt, and realized—what kind of existence he truly was in this world.

Why chaos?

Because there was nothing he needed to protect.

“Mom.”

Unbothered by his skin peeling off, he grinned like a child and said:

“Let’s play something fun.”

At the same time, the flash of Elikia surged toward Havitz’s back.

“Protect Satan!”

As Hell Prison blocked the path, the flash spread like lightning and burned them all to ash.

Kyaaaaaaaa!

The charred remains of Hell Prison hovered in the air for a moment, untouched even by the rain, before vanishing.

Shirone’s expression remained calm.

“Hoo...”

He had suspected, even before coming here, that he wouldn’t be able to kill Havitz.

Satan can escape Yahweh by transcending the laws. If he truly wills it...

Had he used Elikia from the start, Havitz likely would have fled without a second thought.

“I hope it brought you at least a little peace.”

He hoped Havitz might have come to understand—even a sliver—of the suffering endured by the victims of the Ivory Tower’s stars and the claws of Satan.

He said ‘Mom’...

That one word had stirred a few thoughts, but they were still just hypotheses.

More importantly...

The Holy Knights of the Rami Church and the southern warriors were still locked in a bloodbath.

“Yahweh.”

Chieftain Untara, supported by his men, approached.

“Chieftain.”

“I saw you fighting Havitz. It seems Elikia’s power wasn’t enough.”

“He would’ve fled no matter what. The law between me and Satan makes a direct clash impossible.”

Which is why Wizard was necessary.

“Is that so? Either way, the situation is urgent. Those fanatics seem intent on storming the Holy War.”

Shirone turned toward the mind-lost People.

Those who’ve lost their minds and now follow the will of God. Their target is likely... Maya.

Though the reason was unknown, Maya’s death was noted as significant in the future information.

Shirone remembered what Yucus Man had said:

—The song was too loud. We had to stop it. There were too many songs. We had to cover the planet. Thicker, and thicker, and even thicker...

Could it be connected to that? But it’s still too early to conclude anything.

Shirone said:

“I’ll stop them. Please help me.”

Satan had fled, and Hell Prison had been wiped out. All that remained was the wave of People.

“Of course you must...”

Untara looked toward the main road and said:

“But can you stop them all?”

Shirone turned—and saw an endless sea of People filling his vision.

It felt like the entire population of the world was marching toward them.

“...I have to.”

Shirone activated Miracle Stream and swept the front ranks of the mob with Hand of God.

“Grrgh!”

An immense force struck him.

A magnetic field...

If they were just objects, they could be changed by Yahweh’s will—but O-Parts were different.

If the People are God’s pawns...

Their presence neared the extreme of law itself. Each one felt as powerful as one of Nane’s sermons.

“Yaaaaah!”

Clenching his teeth, Shirone swept his arm, gripping handfuls of people with Hand of God.

Hundreds were pulled away, but like water slipping through his fingers, even more escaped.

...This won’t be easy.

If he resorted to destruction, it might end in mass slaughter.

The People all spoke in unison:

“Yahweh.”

It was a message from God to Shirone.

“You are an error.”

And perhaps... that was true.

In this cold, indifferent world, living things were meant to follow the law.

“No.”

Shirone’s eyes flared open.

“We will define it.”

To build a world without injustice, without needless sacrifice, without horrific suffering.

“The true master of this world is the heart.”

Spreading Miracle Stream to its limit, Shirone swept through the wave of People.

Ooooooooh!

As if the world itself was screaming, the streets trembled from the clash between emotion and law.

“Raaaagh!”

While all laws denied the heart, a radiant light exploded in Shirone’s eyes.

“With this, all official events of the Holy War come to a close…”

Applause spread through the annex.

No one clapped half-heartedly, yet their expressions remained stiff.

Everyone was thinking the same thing.

The clash near the main gate hasn’t reached us. Maybe it can be stopped. And if so…

Does that mean Maya has to die?

If it goes to a vote, Tormia has an overwhelming advantage. But nothing’s certain. If something unexpected happens here, the outcome could be reversed.

The ambition to become the World Leadership Nation stoked the murderous intent of each country’s monarch.

“And now, we begin the finale performance in hopes of peace for the Holy War. Please welcome them with your applause.”

Clap…

A few people lightly clapped, but as if it had been planned, silence quickly returned.

It was hesitation—reluctance to give a welcoming gesture to someone who might have to die.

“Um…”

The host was flustered but quickly recovered.

“Introducing! A performance by the world’s finest artists—the Panier Orchestra!”

Trying to make up for the earlier awkwardness, applause finally broke out as 36 artists stepped onto the stage.

Lupist spoke.

“Shirone’s holding the line well. We should be able to last until the performance ends. Does that mean the future has changed?”

Albino’s eyes narrowed.

“Hmm. There are still some variables. First, the King of Iron—Basac. If the prophecy is correct, he kills Maya and seizes the seat of the World Leadership Nation. He’ll make his move.”

To seize the destiny laid before him.

“But now everyone knows the future. The other nations won’t stand by. If we can stop Iron here, the future might be altered.”

Whatever Fermi might be thinking, Lupist made his judgment for Tormia.

“I agree… but I’m sure it’s already been calculated.”

“What do you mean?”

“Fermi and Shirone. The two share the same information but act in completely opposite ways. Fermi knows but refuses to change it. Shirone knows, and tries to change it.”

“That’s just their nature.”

“That’s the key. Their opposing actions seem to split the divine computation in two. Shirone will do everything he can to stop the wave of People. But since even that is within the God’s calculations, He circumvents Yahweh to bring about His desired outcome. And the one who enforces that outcome…”

Lupist realized it.

“Fermi… rewrites it again?”

“That’s likely the strategy. Shirone must’ve known. He probably found a way to control the information somehow. That’s what it truly means to ‘deceive God.’ They’re both out of their minds. They’ve come up with something outrageous.”

Lupist felt the same.

“Whatever the case, the future lies just a layer above what we think. No matter what happens right now, it might not be the true future.”

As long as Fermi controls the information.

Then…

Lupist scanned the line of performers on stage, bowing together.

What future do we still not know?

Pope Constantine walked the corridor.

“Oh my, Your Holiness.”

Even among those not of the Rami Church, he was a globally renowned figure, and the maids greeted him with reverence.

“Yes, thank you for your efforts.”

“What brings you here, Your Holiness? Someone of your status should be at the annex…”

The maid’s face went blank.

“Oh.”

Her eyes briefly flickered with a blue light, and her expression went emotionless.

“Follow the truth of the Rami Church.”

“…Yes.”

The maid turned and headed toward the dormitories where Holy War staff were housed.

Within a few hours, most of Delta’s population would likely become followers of the Rami Church.

“What’s the problem?”

And with that, Pope Constantine finally made his way toward the annex.

“What God desires.”

As the artists performed under Panier’s direction, El Kiana stepped forward first.

Her voice, singing of world peace, was so beautiful it seemed to flow like light.

But the audience remained unmoved.

When do we kill her?

What they were waiting for was the moment Maya stepped into the spotlight at center stage.

According to the prophecy, Iron killed her after the performance. So we have to strike before that.

Panier couldn’t see the audience, but he felt a chill down his spine.

What the hell is this?

He had conducted many performances before, but never felt anything like this.

Next is… Maya.

Regaining focus, Panier glanced at Maya and gave her a small nod.

The spotlight shifted, and Maya walked calmly to the center of the stage.

The eyes of officials from every country widened.

Is it now? Now?

Turning to face the audience, Maya raised her gaze slightly and slowly parted her lips.

“I…”

The impact of that moment—

“When I was very young…”

—was enough to shake people’s hearts in a way that eclipsed any ambition or murderous intent.

“I met a boy…”

A silence deeper than silence itself spread through the hall.

“…and I loved him.”

As she finished the first verse with a soft smile, many eyes in the crowd welled up with tears.

Why was she killed after the performance?

Albino realized it.

Maybe… it was because they couldn’t bring themselves to kill her before.
