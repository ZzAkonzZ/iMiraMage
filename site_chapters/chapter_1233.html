Chapter 1233:

Holy War.

As the maids walked through the corridors of Delta’s main building, their faces turned pale at the sight of a man.

“Kyah!”

There, sitting with his back against the wall, was Havitz—Satan himself—whose name alone now caused people to scream in terror.

He had given up on the 9 o’clock murder game.

It doesn’t matter anymore.

When Shirone stepped in front of Wizard during the 8 o’clock round, it was such a deflating moment that Havitz lost interest.

Like a parent suddenly showing up in a child’s fairy-tale playground and ruining all the fun.

Because Wizard’s parent… is Shirone.

Havitz had tried to hear Wizard’s heart through the divine frequency, but in the end, he couldn’t.

She’s not an easy opponent either. That’s why he still couldn’t be sure—did Wizard love Shirone, or… was it him?

“Kehehe…”

What a joke.

How could I not know? Of course she loves Shirone. Just look at how she acts. I’m just being used.

But on the other hand…

What if I don’t know?

He had never actually looked directly into her heart.

What if she does love me? Maybe she’s only acting sweeter toward Shirone to throw me off…

He knew it wasn’t true.

But if he didn’t let himself believe it, even just a little… He might kill her on the spot.

Why doesn’t she love me?

At last, Havitz could admit what it was he truly wanted with Wizard.

“Hey.”

A voice called out from down the hall. Havitz turned his head and saw a familiar face.

Balkan.

He looked like a wreck, but the fire in his eyes hadn’t gone out.

Havitz turned his gaze back forward.

“What is it?”

“You look like hell. Got your heart broken or something?”

“...Probably.”

Balkan slumped down beside him.

“Feels like I won’t last much longer. I don’t even have the strength to walk anymore. Guess this is goodbye for us.”

“Yeah. Take care.”

Havitz spoke casually—not to be cold, but as if he were saying goodbye to a friend he saw every day.

“Heh, sure. Anyway, how’s it going? Heard you had someone you liked.”

“...Not great.”

Balkan could tell how much Havitz was burning up inside—he was never one to say much.

“Why? Did she reject you?”

“She acts like she’s interested, then turns cold again out of nowhere.”

Balkan looked at him quietly.

“You’re a strange guy. Some people might hate you, but no one ever finds you boring. If someone did get bored of you…"

Blood ran down Balkan’s chin.

“That means you’ve become boring. That you’re not being you.”

Havitz blinked.

“You told me once—if you define nothing, eventually you’ll get everything you want. You said we should play freely. Live however we want.”

That was chaos.

“Yeah.”

Havitz stood up.

“I’m going to kill them all. Not one per hour anymore. Everyone I lay eyes on.”

He had been too distracted by Wizard—and Shirone had used that against him.

Balkan smiled.

“You’re finally ready to beat Yahweh.”

“Do your best,” he rasped. “We’ll be trying to kill you too. It’s gonna be rough.”

“Haha. You think so?”

Havitz drew his longsword and pointed it at Balkan’s throat.

They exchanged silent, cheerful smiles.

“Goodbye, Balkan.”

He would kill everyone in sight.

Havitz's blade sliced through the air—smooth and swift. Balkan’s head flew from his shoulders.

Even as his head rolled across the floor, Balkan was still calculating the odds of this gamble.

Heh. Now this is fun.

Havitz would hesitate no longer. He would become a true demon and tear through the Holy War.

“Havitz…”

A hoarse voice whispered from Balkan’s severed throat.

“Twilight child…”

But Havitz walked away, his steps echoing down the hall.

He didn’t ask.

He didn’t question why Balkan, knowing he would die, had still offered him comfort in the moment of heartbreak.

Was it out of friendship—their bond as closest comrades?

Or had that too been part of some deeper calculation?

Havitz didn’t ask.

Because that was the one and only joy he and Balkan of Gustav’s Fourth Host had shared.

“I’ll kill them all.”

Ten minutes after Havitz disappeared into the hall…

Quick, catlike footsteps approached Balkan’s body.

Natasha knelt beside the corpse, where the head lay a few meters away.

She cradled his smiling head in her arms.

“…So cruel.”

Then, as she slowly raised her head—

“Havitz.”

Her lidless eyes reflected the deepest abyss in the world.

The Holy War had become a bloodbath.

“There are already more than 20 dead. We believe it’s Satan—Havitz.”

Having killed Balkan, Havitz went on a rampage, indiscriminately butchering humans.

Security forces were fully deployed, and elite guards from every nation formed a tight perimeter.

“Huh?”

But no one remembered Havitz.

“Why are we even doing this? Who are we trying to stop again?”

None of them realized it.

That Havitz was already cutting down their comrades, one by one, heading straight for them.

“This is weird. What’s going on—”

The guard with a sword through his heart coughed up blood as he spoke.

“Is this really happening…?”

As the death toll rose to one person every minute, the five department heads of the Holy War convened an emergency meeting.

“Havitz is rampaging.”

“What is Yahweh doing? Hasn’t he been able to locate Havitz until now?”

Since Elikia had already been activated at the Ivory Tower, it would take time for Ultima to recharge.

Dorothy from the Military Department spoke.

“We’re aware of Havitz’s location for now, but we don’t know when Vanishing will activate again.”

“Why did he even drop his ability?” asked Mito Shirano from the International Tribunal.

“It might be for mockery,” she continued, “but more than that, I think he’s trying to instill fear. Dying without even realizing it—there’s nothing more terrifying than that.”

“This isn’t the time to be speaking like it’s someone else’s problem. If we don’t come up with a plan, the vote is over.”

The chairperson, who was on Gise’s side, tried everything to ensure the vote proceeded.

That’s when Constantine, head of the Religious Department, finally spoke.

“How long are we going to rely on Yahweh?”

“Your Holiness…”

“The root of this entire disaster is Yahweh. He provoked Satan under the pretext of saving humanity—and now he can’t even provide a solution.”

He stood up.

“I, and the Rami Church, will no longer stand by.”

“Do you have a plan?”

“Bring them in.”

At the Pope’s command, the Holy Knights of the Rami Church’s Demon Suppression Division entered once again.

They come and go like it’s their own house.

While this technically violated protocol, no one dared object with Havitz on the loose.

“Yahweh pretends to be God and deceives the people. But God does not negotiate. God is omniscient and omnipotent by nature. From this point on, I will prove the true hand of divinity.”

A radiant light shone from Constantine’s body, and Shirano narrowed one eye.

It’s definitely a sacred aura. Most demons wouldn’t stand a chance against it. But…

She didn’t underestimate Yahweh.

Whatever his power may be, he’s only barely managing to neutralize Havitz’s Vanishing once every hour. What is the Pope really thinking?

Just then—

“Emergency!”

A senior aide burst in, pale as a ghost, which immediately annoyed the chair.

“What’s with this ruckus?! Do you realize where you are?!”

“O-Outside…”

“Outside? Are you talking about the protesters?”

“It’s not at that level.”

The aide hesitated before finally spitting it out:

“It’s a riot.”

Though it was past 9 PM, the number of demonstrators outside the Delta headquarters had only grown.

Opposition and support groups clashed in a deadlock, voices raised on either side of the divide.

“Impeach Gis! Reveal the truth!” “Make the King of Jive the leader of the Holy War! Let’s make Jive the World Leadership Nation!”

Then, a new wave surged between the two factions.

“Lord Kreia, look upon us with mercy!”

The Demon Suppression Division, who had left to investigate the pyramid, returned chanting prayers.

Their faces were solemn—but their eyes were lifeless, as though their souls were gone.

"Kill Maya."

That single mission alone propelled the mass of humans forward—colliding directly into the crowd.

“What are you doing?! Even if you’re from the Rami Church, this is no way to disperse a protest—”

But the words didn’t last long.

“O Lord…”

People near the Suppression Division lost focus in their eyes and all turned in the same direction.

“Save us.”

The wave of “humans” spread too fast. In moments, the entire crowd had lost their minds.

Their unified will was now focused on one thing:

Kill Maya.

The will of a god.

With 30 minutes left until the finale performance, thousands surged toward Delta.

“Damn it! What’s wrong with them? It’s like they’re hypnotized!”

The head of the gate security shouted orders.

“Stop them! Don’t let them within 100 meters! You’re authorized to kill!”

But faced with a mob that moved like a single organism, there was no shame in killing civilians.

“Charge!”

The guards clashed with the crowd.

“O Lord…”

And then the bewitched people turned and ran toward Delta once again.

The guard captain realized the gravity of the situation.

“Report to the higher-ups.”

“Yes, sir!”

Even though it might not help, it was all he could do.

“Tch!”

He began to pull his forces back, adjusting to the slow pace of the mob’s advance.

We can’t fight this. The moment we touch them…

Death. That was the feeling.

“God commands it.”

At the front, the Demon Suppression Division raised a holy sword.

“Advance!”

“UWOOOOHHH!”

With a deafening roar, they charged. The guard captain clenched his eyes shut and raised his spear.

“Damn it all!”

At that moment, dark-skinned warriors rushed past the guard line from Delta’s direction.

The Southern Tribal Alliance had arrived.

“Everyone, hold your ground! We follow Yahweh’s will!”

As they clashed with the Demon Suppression Division, a proper battle finally began.

The guard captain’s eyes went wide.

How…?

Could they really withstand the overwhelming mind control that had turned even his loyal men?

“Stand aside.”

Chieftain Untara of the Southern Alliance approached, flanked by his guards.

“Sir!”

Untara waved him off.

“I’m not someone worth bowing to. Nor do I want to be. Send your troops back to the barracks. We’ll take it from here.”

“I can’t do that! If this gate falls, I might as well be dead!”

“I doubt you can even die right now.” He instinctively understood what that meant.

“Listen to me. The fact that they’re here means there’s something they want inside. If we waste our strength now, we might lose it at the critical moment.”

“Lose what? Who would even—”

“God.”

The guard captain’s face went blank.

“The indifferent god, and the god with a heart. And the two beliefs that follow each. Understand? From this point on, the battle here is no ordinary war.”

Untara declared:

“This is a Holy War.”
