Chapter 379: Entering the Competitive System (3)

Iruki landed in the training ground. Smiling, he approached Binder.

"Ugh!"

Even as Binder stepped back, Iruki didn't rush. For him, everything was already over.

"Your specialty is certainly neat. It felt like reading a textbook."

Cancellation analyzes the changes occurring in the opponent's Spirit Zone and reverses all vector quantities.

The effect of resetting omnipotence is tremendous, but the humiliation the opponent feels is also significant.

"Don't be ridiculous! My specialty is perfect!"

Binder, unwilling to lose, tried to cast magic again. But now, his omnipotence was being reset even before he could attempt it.

"I know. It's perfect. Perfect."

Binder's upper lip curled like a gorilla's. Then, suddenly, he looked down with a startled expression.

An explosive reaction was occurring below.

'Damn!'

"It's okay. It's just 10 kilobusters."

As Iruki turned around, an explosion occurred.

In the second training ground system, there was no "knockback" judgment in interpersonal combat, so Binder didn't get blown away. However, his mental strength gauge dropped straight to zero.

He could have withstood it if he had cast a defensive spell, but there was nothing he could do in front of Cancellation.

Iruki came down from the training ground, making a victory sign.

At one time, he was jeered for being disrespectful to the loser, but no one in the senior class laughed at him now.

"Cancellation... on the first day."

Their expressions were serious. Iruki had revealed his most powerful weapon among the spells he could use.

'Is he confident he won't lose? Or does he have another spell prepared?'

Revealing his trump card so boldly made their thoughts even more tangled.

"Hmm..."

Fermi watched the battle with his hands in his pockets. Then, smiling, he asked his companion.

"How many seconds?"

"2 minutes and 11 seconds."

"2 minutes and 11 seconds to restore a broken glass."

No matter how orthodox Binder was, analyzing a senior class-level Spirit Zone within 3 minutes was impossible without a servant's brain.

'If interpersonal combat is included in the graduation exam, that will be quite tricky.'

Calculations related to business quickly ran through Fermi's mind.

The door to the dormitory closed with a thud.

It was past 11 PM when Shirone finally returned to the dormitory, collapsing onto his bed.

"Ugh, I'm exhausted. So tired."

The senior class schedule had already reached the fourth week.

Unless there were special circumstances, most evaluations ended around noon, but there was still no time to rest.

The senior class was literally a wilderness.

No one taught you anything; you had to learn it yourself if you wanted something.

Shirone also continued training with his friends in the training ground all day after evaluations.

He was still doing well in interpersonal combat, and cognition and judgment were favorable subjects for him.

Even in terrifying situations, his mental endurance remained unshaken, and his excellent insight led him accurately toward the exit for survival.

However, other students also had professional-level skills in at least one area.

Binder, aiming for Yongroe, scored full marks in omnipotent implementation, and Arin and Dorothy, specializing in mental activities, were noted for their performance.

Those with unique specialties like Pisho steadily earned points in specialty enhancement, maintaining their rankings.

Thus, even after rotating through the six subjects four times, the fluctuation in rankings was still minimal.

"It's much harder than I thought."

Considering the 30-week schedule, it was still the early stages.

But the fatigue Shirone felt was beyond imagination.

He had to take evaluations every day, where even a single point slip could drop his ranking. The tension from that carried over into his afternoon personal training, leaving him completely exhausted by the time he returned to the dormitory.

"I want to sleep. I want to sleep. Just one hour of sleep."

Despite his muttering, Shirone got up from his bed.

While other students might be sleeping by now, he had another task to complete.

Fulfilling Gaold's conditions.

The reason for his grueling daily training was not just to raise his ranking but also to avoid a meaningless death in the potential journey to Heaven.

He had to become stronger.

And he had to develop it.

A magic powerful enough to destroy Heaven.

"Me?"

Himself, struggling through the senior class evaluations?

"Haha!"

Shirone let out a hollow laugh and sat at his desk, opening his notebook.

It was filled with ideas he had come up with over the past month.

But none had hit the core yet, so they were still just scribbles.

'Let's approach it conceptually today. A magic to destroy Heaven. What could it be?'

First of all, it had to be a long-range magic.

Casting a wide-area destruction spell at close range would kill the magician as well.

'Or maybe I should consider that too...'

Shirone wrote "Possibility of caster's death" at the edge of his notebook.

'If I can die, would more paths open up?'

Deep in thought, Shirone grabbed his head.

"Ugh, I really don't know."

The key was to use Ataraxia's amplification power.

That much was certain.

But it was extremely difficult to reach the sub-light speed range of light-based magic with a Photon Cannon that had mass.

Someday, it might surpass the speed of sound, but even then, it wouldn't have the power to destroy Heaven.

"I'm just running in circles."

Shirone wrote "Ataraxia" and added "Trigger" next to it.

Ataraxia's strength was undoubtedly its tremendous amplification power, but it had other advantages too.

First, it was particularly threatening to the beings of Heaven.

Even sensitive humans were shocked by the halo, but the Maras, born from the concept of angels, felt instinctive fear on top of that.

"Hmm, it can cause a delay."

Shirone wrote "Delay 1-2 seconds" in parentheses next to Ataraxia.

"The most important thing is the trigger."

Magic that passed through Ataraxia was no longer governed by the magician's Spirit Zone but by Ataraxia.

That was why Shirone defined Ataraxia as siege magic.

"Amplification. Delay. Trigger."

These were the three divine attributes of Ataraxia.

"So, the magic I can use is..."

Shirone listed them sequentially.

For attack magic, there were Photon Cannon, Homing Photon Cannon, and Laser.

For defense magic, there were Radiant Burst and Dark Sphere.

For control techniques, there were Shining Impact, Shining Chain, and Slow. For movement techniques, there were Teleportation, Spatial Movement, Mass Teleportation, Scatter Movement, and Radiant Wings could also be classified as a movement technique.

"That's a lot. I've worked pretty hard."

Feeling proud for a moment, his expression soon darkened.

No matter how many spells he had, none could completely destroy Heaven.

No, it was impossible from the start.

How could he, not even a formal magician, destroy Heaven?

"No! This won't work, that won't work, nothing works!"

Shirone drew a big X in his notebook and buried his face in the desk.

After a month of research, all he had was a faint, tickling feeling in his mind.

Even if he learned a new spell, there was a limit to a single type within a year.

'Amplification. Delay. Trigger. Time division. Force. Fusion. And the spells I've learned so far.'

He had to combine all these to create something completely new.

It might be wrong, but he had a strange feeling that it was the right answer, flowing through the channel of insight.

'Find it. Find it. Find a way.'

Repeating the only thought left in his mind, Shirone fell asleep.

For the senior class, a holiday was invaluable.

But Shirone had to wake up early, rubbing his sleepy eyes.

The fourth week's evaluations were over, and today was one of the six annual overall evaluation days.

It was an evaluation where they directly experienced the regular subjects of the graduation exam, so they had to do their best.

The winner would gain 20 points, and the loser would lose 20 points.

With a value of 40 points at stake, even those who had been playing psychological games would be determined to give their all.

In terms of interpersonal combat, it was equivalent to 13 consecutive wins.

In terms of specialty enhancement, it was the same as passing the master level in four items.

'Today's rankings could change significantly. No, they will change.'

Today's overall evaluation was the survival subject.

Since there had to be losers, there was a chance for a significant rise in rankings.

Especially in survival, which was the graduation exam version of Shirone's specialty in cognition and judgment, it was a match he couldn't afford to lose.

The students would enter the massive Virtual Zone together.

They would be evaluated on their mental strength to see how long they could endure in extreme situations.

Judgment was important, but mental endurance was essential.

For Shirone, failing to earn points here would put him at a disadvantage in the future schedule.

Meeting Nade and Iruki, Shirone headed to the training ground with the large Virtual Zone.

Many students were already gathered.

They were still competitors, but the initial awkwardness was gone.

They had also somewhat identified friend and foe.

It was hard to get along with Screamer, Pony, and Luman, but he got along well with Maya and Aider.

He had a neutral relationship with Dorothy, Suabi, and Pisho, and the Class One students didn't even look at Class Three.

When the entire senior class gathered, Collie lined up the students by class and checked each person's eyes.

Since survival was known to be the most dangerous and harsh subject in the overall evaluation, his face was filled with a sense of solemnity.

"Today's overall evaluation subject is survival. Before we start, I'll explain the rules. First, we'll divide into teams. The line drawn over there marks the right side as the red team and the left side as the blue team."

A line was drawn in the wide open space leading into the Virtual Zone.

"Once the teams are decided, everyone will enter the Virtual Zone. The survival program consists of seven stages and lasts for a total of 50 minutes. The team with the last survivor wins, regardless of the remaining results. In other words, the team with the final survivor wins overall, and the losing team loses 20 points."

Boyle raised his hand.

"What's the criterion for deciding teams?"

"Go to the team you want."

Pandora asked.

"Um, shouldn't it be 15 versus 15?"

"It doesn't matter. Even if all 30 are on one team, that's fine."

The atmosphere in Class Three became awkward. Some students looked around as if to say, "Is this for real?"

'If all 30 are on one team, there's no competition. What kind of rule is this?'

Of course, no one would suggest forming a team of 30 to live or die together.

The senior class was a place where everyone was desperate to gain an advantage over their opponents.

But they needed to think about the meaning of the rule.

Considering the senior class's operating principles so far, it wasn't just a competition to earn points and climb the rankings.

It was designed to determine how well you could position yourself among competitors and how accurately you could assess others' abilities.

Iruki said.

"A magician's position in a real party is absolute. It seems the overall evaluation is no exception."

If a magician falters, the party is doomed. Even third-rate adventurers know that.

Just having powerful magic and an excellent Spirit Zone doesn't guarantee passing the graduation exam.

How intelligently you solidify your position among competitors was also an invisible evaluation criterion.

"Now, we'll decide the teams. You have 10 minutes. If you don't decide within the time, you'll be considered to have forfeited. Go to the team you want."

As soon as Collie finished speaking, the 30 students flinched as if stunned.

Someone must have flinched first, but it was an unpleasant reaction speed that couldn't identify the culprit.

They needed to think carefully.

The rule was that even if only one team member survived, the team would earn 20 points.

Therefore, for students who weren't confident in the survival subject, the key was which team they joined.

In a sense, it was a true test of survival ability.

So, those confident in their mental endurance needed to move first.

Some were already thinking of taking the lead.

But choosing between the meaningless red and blue teams was subtly nerve-wracking.

'What was the win ratio between the blue team and the red team so far?'

It was a kind of jinx.

"I'll go first."

Shirone moved first.

Since his goal was to be the final survivor, he didn't need to worry about others' choices.

When Shirone went to the red team, the crowd finally stirred. The second to move were, of course, Nade and Iruki.

Shirone's group was on the red team.

With this crucial information, the remaining 27 minds began to work quickly.

"Hmm, then I'll go to the blue team."

Everyone turned to Fermi at his voice. His group followed him confidently as he walked.

Reaching the center of the blue team, Fermi looked back at Shirone and gave a playful smile.

Shirone didn't smile. He couldn't tell what thoughts lay behind those narrow eyes.

Finally, the moment the remaining students had been waiting for arrived.

The formidable newcomers of Class Three and Fermi's group, who had never missed the top spot in the final evaluations, were divided into teams.

Which side had a higher chance of winning?

Now it was a matter of choice.
