Chapter 279: The Trap of Choosing Between Two Choices (3)

"Hmph, your mouth is still lively as ever. What's the reason for this clown show? Did Father say he'd make you king later or something?

"I'm sure I told you clearly. I'm not interested."

Zion turned away without even listening. He walked along the wall, taking in the scenery of the waiting room.

In fact, today's demonstration schedule was unexpected even for him. He hadn't expected Orkamp to propose a real showdown so quickly.

But it also meant that things were quite urgent on their side as well.

Zion, who arrived in front of Shirone again after circling the waiting room widely, made a tempting proposal.

“I will send you home.”

Shirone asked back with a puzzled expression.

“What are you talking about?”

"You said you're not interested in the position of the first prince? Then what you gain from this demonstration is freedom. I'm saying I'll give that to you. No matter how much you avoid the eyes of Kazra Royal Palace, you can't avoid Terraje's gaze. We have a global reach. But if I send you off? Kazra will become mine, and you’ll gain your freedom. It's a win-win for both of us, don't you think?"

It was a sweet temptation, almost impossible to refuse. But it wasn't something to accept easily.

It was said that Empress Terraje had planted Zion to swallow Kazra. Therefore, if Zion becomes the king, Orkamp and Eliza will die. Without cutting off the royal family's legitimacy, they can't plan for the future.

“What do you want me to do?”

"It's simple. Ruin the show. Whether it’s the power of the archangel or whatever, just mess it up. And tell the nobles. The rumors were exaggerated, it succeeded because of luck, it's not magic that someone as insignificant as me can do, like that."

Shirone nodded in understanding.

Today's demonstration was a political stage. Whether the nobles believed the lie or not, once Shirone raised the white flag, the game would be over.

However, what weighed on his mind was the death of Orkamp and Eliza. Was it really okay to do that?

No matter how much they abandoned their child, they at least kept him alive. Does he have the right to repay that tiny bit of grace with death?

"I'll think about it. No, give me time to think."

"Take as much as you need. Anyway, I'll hear your answer at the Colosseum. Whichever way you choose, Kazra will end up in my hands. Make a wise decision.”

Shirone, with his head lowered, heard the sound of Zion leaving. But his mind was too complicated to pay attention to it.

If he helps Orkamp, he'll be chased by Terraje Faction for life. But if he sides with Zion, Orkamp and Eliza will die.

'What should I do? What on earth do they expect me to do?'

Uorin, who had recovered her pet cat that had been wandering around the waiting room, finished preparing to leave and said goodbye to Shirone.

"Oppa, stay strong."

Shirone looked up at Uorin with lifeless eyes. She probably knew about his dilemma. But she offered no advice other than words of comfort.

He tried to convey his urgent feelings with earnest eyes, but she just tilted her head and smiled as if she knew nothing.

Shirone looked at the ground with a resigned expression.

What on earth is he hoping for? There's no way there could be someone to help him in the royal palace, is there?

At that moment, Uorin sighed.

"Haah."

And then, in a calm and lowered voice, she said.

"Do you want me to help you, Oppa?"

Shirone raised his head again.

Uorin was still beautiful. But the bright feeling that was evident just a moment ago had disappeared.

"Of course, I could help you."

Uorin stepped back a couple of steps as if reestablishing the distance.

"But I could also choose not to. What's important is justification. For example, let's say there's a wealthy person who owns 100 billion gold. He wouldn't mind giving 1 gold to anyone. But he doesn't do that. Not because he's stingy, but because there's no justification. If he were to give 1 gold to someone for no reason, he'd eventually have to give 1 gold to everyone in the world. You don't have justification yet, Oppa. So you have to create a reason why I must help you."

"What... should I do?"

“That’s also up to you, oppa. You might lose a lot in this demonstration. But it's also an opportunity to gain something more precious. I hope you make a wise choice."

With those final words, Uorin headed towards the door. Shirone couldn't see the beautiful smile he had hoped for until the end.

Left alone in the waiting room, Shirone fell into thought. He was analyzing her words left at the last moment of choice.

'I see.'

Losing a lot meant the material loss he would suffer if he gave up the title of first prince. On the other hand, gaining something precious referred to the lives of Orkamp and Eliza.

If Zion becomes the king of Kazra, Uorin will also have justification to help him.

In other words, she's saying this: Give the throne to Zion, and she'll guarantee the lives of his birth parents.

'Yes, that will do.'

Shirone made up his mind to ruin the demonstration.

Perhaps Orkamp would be disappointed, but ultimately, he would be saving his life, so he would have fulfilled his responsibility as a son.

The lamp on the ceiling lit up and a noisy siren sounded.

Having organized his thoughts, Shirone entered the elevator installed on the wall. When he pulled the switch, the elevator ascended to the ground with clanking sounds.

What he saw upon arriving at the ground was only enclosed darkness and iron bars installed 10 meters ahead.

The sunlight was as faint as it was before sunset, but the champions of long ago would have rekindled their hope for life while looking at the intense sun of midday.

The iron bars went up, and Shirone walked to the center of the Colosseum.

Zion's words about it being a clown show were right. As numerous gazes focused on him, he felt as if he were stripped naked.

The target was standing in the direction of the setting sun. As if found in haste as a last resort, it was an extremely shabby wooden board.

It's not even a stone slingshot, but they expected him to cast magic on that? If the intention was to mock, it was perfect.

He snorted, thinking about how even choosing one target would have caused a power struggle.

"We will now begin the qualification test for the first prince candidate."

A qualification verification for the first prince candidate. It was certainly a fitting excuse to put on a clown show.

Shirone paid his respects to Orkamp and said,

“Your Majesty, magic is a powerful force. If we're not careful, there is a risk of damaging property. I would like to ask for your understanding regarding that first."

It meant that the precious target might be destroyed. It was a sharp jab at those mocking him.

Orkamp quickly grasped his intent and supported Shirone's point.

"Do not worry. The target is merely a pretext; as long as you can showcase your abilities, it doesn't matter what you do."

Shirone felt delighted as if he had given them a blow. But in the end, this was all he could do.

Feeling a bit of self-loathing, he turned towards the target. As everyone held their breath, he concentrated and cast Halo.

An intensely bright light, like a star, pierced the space and began to move slowly, drawing a gigantic circle.

He had to draw it as large as possible.

He had heard from Amy after Heaven’s Trial duel that Halo could shock those with sensitive senses. If so, it would surely work on someone here.

It might be a meaningless resistance, but without doing what he could, he couldn't create any variables.

Finally, as the Halo was completed, a brilliantly shining light ring appeared.

Amy quickly surveyed the surroundings.

First, Reina frowned. If she felt pressure from the Halo, it meant her Schema sensitivity was quite high.

On the other hand, the nobles were calm. Most of the nobles at the center of power were from the administrative branch, so they were far from Schema or Spirit Zones.

But those who guarded them were different. Without exception, they were all biting their teeth desperately.

'Hmm, they're certainly strong ones...'

Zion and Uorin didn't seem particularly shocked.

Since she hadn't expected much from them anyway, Amy looked toward where Orkamp was.

To be exact, it was the tall man standing behind Orkamp.

There are many experts in this place, but none were as highly tense as that man. Moreover, he was covering his face with his right hand, as if he still couldn't adapt.

'Damn it! That startled me.'

Arius decided to believe in the existence of the soul. Because at the moment the Halo was born, he felt as if something inside his mind had flown outward. It was that much of a terrifying shock.

For him, who hadn't been mentally stunned for more than a second since joining the Seven Magical Sins, it was astonishing.

'He has materialized the Immortal Function into a form. Is this the basic mental system of the Nephilim?'

Having finished analyzing the Halo, Arius waited calmly. Through this demonstration, he planned to figure out the mechanism and find a way to break the encapsulation. As Ataraxia began to accumulate, his eyes shone intensely.

'It has begun! First is a binary choice. Is it based on binary numbers?'

Starting with the first magic circle embedding itself in the center of the Halo, a shower of light surged in.

The nobles marveled at the spectacular sight, but Arius organized all the concepts accumulating in Ataraxia without missing a single one.

‘Algebra. Musho Geometry. Multi-Operation Equations. Vanessa's Theorem. Spherical Integration. Eisen's Relativity. And that, Hegel's Paradox?'

As the concepts accumulated within Ataraxia, his thoughts began to overload. His head felt like it was about to explode.

As the accumulation process passed the halfway point, even the Zeta Function, the cutting edge of human intellect, appeared. It was a kind of hypothesis that was thought to contain the blueprint of the universe, a formula for discovering patterns in prime numbers.

Arius stopped thinking. No, he couldn't.

From the Zeta Function onwards, everything was a concept he had never seen before, and it was being embedded into the Halo at an incredible speed.

Unanalyzable.

'This fucking….'

As the accumulation ended, Ataraxia was born as an iridescent, integrated concept. It was the reality of a supreme magic amplification circle that humans could never realize.

Arius shuddered with a sense of defeat.

He had only managed to understand 65% of the entire circuit. Compared to Shirone, who gave up at the 17% point when he was possessed by Ikael, it was an astonishing level of intellect, but not knowing even 1% is the same as knowing nothing.

Shirone stared at the Ataraxia that appeared before his eyes and fell into thought. From now on, his life would change depending on what he chose.

After about 10 seconds, the nobles who had been watching closely gradually lost interest.

Knowing how important one second was in real combat, they were only disappointed by Shirone's inexperience.

"What are you doing? If you're going to do it, do it, if not, don't."

"He's indecisive. Or does he need some more preparation steps?"

Nobles who commanded archmages wouldn't have the patience for a student-level demonstration. As complaints arose here and there, Zion added his voice.

"Haha! Rumors are often exaggerated. How many high-level mages are there in the royal palace, yet a mere aspiring mage is being verified with this?"

Then, those presumed to be part of Terraje Faction chimed in one after another.

Leaving them to set the atmosphere as they pleased, Zion arrogantly looked down at Shirone.

Now the stage was all set. All that remained was for Shirone to confess his inadequacy.

Shirone listened to the voices coming from all directions. As expected, the majority were Zion's subordinates.

'Okay, let's stop. From the beginning, he was too strong of an enemy for me to deal with.'

Shirone dropped his shoulders powerlessly and opened his mouth.

"I…"

The first word of a crucial announcement came out, but the nobles' murmuring showed no signs of stopping.

Realizing his voice was too small, Shirone took a deep breath and filled his lungs with air so that everyone could hear.

'Wait...'

Then suddenly, a strange thought occurred to him.

Was giving up really the right decision?

It was a thought that suddenly emerged, pushed up not from his head but from his body.
