Chapter 1148: Trigger (3)

Even as the drinking session progressed, Uorin and Munryong kept their true intentions hidden.

"Hahaha, horseback riding is great for health and also a perfect remedy for a troubled mind."

"I've heard that the horses of the Mun Kingdom are fast and strong. Horses are quite rare in Kashan."

While casual conversation flowed, the captains of the two nations' guards were engaged in a fierce battle of wills.

'We can't lose.'

The only ones allowed to attend Code One, besides the kings, were the captains of the guards.

Losing balance here was akin to losing a war, creating a tense atmosphere.

'She's no ordinary woman.'

Kido glared at Ryongryong, the captain of the Mun Kingdom's guard.

Her face was as pale as a corpse, with only her blood-red lips showing any life.

'They call her the Red Jiangshi?'

Ryongryong met Kido's gaze without flinching, gently tucking her hair behind her ear.

As her blue trumpet sleeves fell, ominous red characters tattooed on her wrist were revealed.

'The Mun Kingdom is known for its Jiangshi techniques. Inscribing laws onto the human body.'

Not knowing what laws were inscribed on Ryongryong's body, Kido couldn't let his guard down.

"The Mun Kingdom has a famous horse named Bifeng that can run a thousand miles a day and isn't afraid of arrows. I'll send you a stallion as a gift soon."

"Thank you for your kindness."

Uorin covered her mouth and smiled brightly, but her inner gaze was as cold as a snake.

'Dragging this out. What's the deal?'

Kashan had no allies.

It was strong enough not to need them, and weak alliances could lead to future troubles.

'What card does the Mun Kingdom have?'

They wouldn't have invoked Code One without confidence in their persuasion.

While hopeful, she also felt uneasy, as if being led by the opponent.

'Damn time waves.'

The Paras Kingdom's manipulation of the law rendered her future vision useless.

"You're very patient."

Munryong smiled, but Uorin responded calmly.

"Haha, I just lost track of time. It's already late."

Munryong looked down at his glass and said,

"Let's form an alliance."

"What do you have?"

"What you want."

"And what is that?"

"Well, disaster? Shockwave? Great fear? Something that can disrupt the current situation."

Uorin, who had been leaning forward aggressively, slowly leaned back in her chair.

Taking a sip from her glass signaled her willingness to listen.

"I will send a curse to the kings of the Holy War."

Listening! Listening!

With her ears perked, Uorin listened to a 20-minute explanation.

"The curse is ready. It just needs to be released. But at this point, I want to propose something. Havitz."

Munryong leaned forward.

"I've heard that using the under world's system can further enhance the curse. If so, we could assassinate all 11 kings of the Holy War."

Uorin scratched her nails.

'Crazy bastard.'

Her heart pounded, and she felt blood coursing through her veins.

'There's always a way out.'

Suppressing her rising smile, she looked up for the first time.

"It can be done through Siok, a subordinate of Havitz. So, can I avoid the curse?"

"You're underestimating me. Now that I've explained, threats are meaningless. I proposed it because you won't refuse."

Indeed.

'There can only be one world leader, and a weak alliance could leave enemies behind. A double-edged sword. But Munryong's proposal is tempting.'

If they could bring 10 nations to the brink of collapse, the alliance would be worth it.

"When will the curse be released?"

"At midnight."

Uorin thought it was the right answer.

'The value of information decreases over time. The sooner, the more destructive. The downside is the lack of time to consider variables.'

Kido, feeling the same, glanced at Uorin.

'Hey, this isn't right.'

In about two hours, they planned to kill the world's top 10 leaders simultaneously.

'Too rushed. Let's think this through.'

Uorin smiled.

'You're wrong, Kido. It's effective because it's rushed.'

Teraje's true strength wasn't memory genetics or history search.

'Load a single bullet into a revolver. Then hold the barrel to your head…'

The world looks different.

'Unnecessary emotions disappear, and thoughts become clearer. You can distinguish between what's important and what's not. As a result… you understand the world as if absorbing it.'

Of course, you have to pull the trigger.

'One in six times, it fires. If the bullet goes off, it's over.'

About 16.6 percent.

'You survive with an 83.4 percent chance. It's an incredibly easy gamble. But people hesitate because life is precious.'

But Teraje was different.

'He kept pulling. Realized it, spun the revolver, and faced 16.6 percent death again…'

Click.

'Kido, can you do it? You have to. Everyone in the Holy War is like that. Those who can't overcome 16.6 percent fear aren't fit to conquer the world.'

Uorin spoke.

"Alright, I'll accept the Mun Kingdom's proposal. I'll ask Havitz to enhance the curse. But I'd like to modify the strategy a bit. Now that we're allies, I have the right to voice my opinion, right?"

"Of course. I'm listening."

"Assassinating the leaders of 10 nations could have significant repercussions. Whether successful or not, nations not hit by the curse will be suspicious."

"Indeed, I thought of that. But it seems a waste to miss this opportunity."

"Instead, if we can frame a specific nation as the culprit, the benefits will outweigh the risks. It's a double strategy: kill the enemy's king and frame the enemy's king."

"Hmm."

It made sense.

"Can such a scheme be executed immediately? Even astrologers can't predict human minds."

"You know me."

Staring into the eyes of Mitochondria Eve, Munryong nodded.

"Very well. Decide now which countries will be hit by the curse and which will be spared."

They had to finalize the plan within 10 minutes.

With Kido and Ryongryong swallowing nervously, Uorin spoke.

"The first country to be hit by the curse… I've decided."

"Tormia."

The temporary barracks vacated by the Mun Kingdom were quiet, with only the flickering candlelight.

It mirrored Uorin's state of mind.

"Are you nervous?"

Kido asked.

"Honestly, yes. Why did you accept such a proposal? Kashan's position is still strong. We had a good chance even with a stable approach."

"Being 'good enough' isn't enough."

Teraje's intuition was clear.

"Kido, 'good enough' means we're actually declining. Humans aren't satisfied with just being okay."

"We have to keep pushing upwards. If things continue, Jive will become the leading nation. I need the power to shake things up. A strong force."

"And that means assassinating kings?"

"The Mun Kingdom will send the curse anyway. If we can't avoid disaster, it's better to be on the side causing it. We can control the information."

"Well, I'm sure you know what you're doing."

Kashan's queen didn't need advice, but Kido felt it as a warrior.

'Once someone dies…'

A cycle of blood vengeance would follow.

Uorin's eyes sparkled with life.

"By the way, where has Havitz been all day?"

As night fell and political maneuvers were in full swing, the four knights of Gustav ventured into the city.

"Havitz came here?"

Balkan looked up at a building lit by hundreds of bulbs along its exterior.

Zetaro tilted his head.

"A casino. Was he that bored? He's not the type to gamble now."

Smoto said, "He wouldn't come to kill anyone. He made a pact with the Kashan Empire."

From their conversation, Balkan realized.

"No, he's here to kill."

Natasha asked, "Really? Havitz doesn't care about contracts, but he doesn't break them if it's disadvantageous."

That was his nature.

"It's not a breach of contract. He has no responsibility for what happens outside the Holy War. Even if he did, Kashan's power could enforce it."

Smoto stroked his chin.

"Hmm, then it's even stranger. No one's died. No one's vanished."

"…Let's go in."

As the four entered through the main door, they saw many nobles gambling.

"Yes! One million gold!"

As a girl raised her hands in victory at a card game, people applauded.

'Rebecca.'

Reading the atmosphere, Balkan nodded.

"Havitz didn't come by chance. There's a VIP. Gis's daughter, Rebecca."

"She's bold. I heard there were injuries, yet she's still gambling."

"Her eyes are glazed. Must be drugs. But she doesn't seem to know her position."

Zetaro said, "I think Havitz came to kill Rebecca. But why did he change his mind?"

"Over there."

Balkan pointed to the bar.

"Welcome."

The bartender, wiping a glass, changed his expression upon seeing the four knights' fierce faces.

"What brings you here?"

"I'm Balkan, Emperor of Gustav. I heard Havitz came here today. Did you see him?"

The bartender looked blank.

"Who did you say you were?"

Zetaro banged on the bar.

"The Emperor! I'm the Prime Minister. Answer the question. Did you see Havitz or not?"

'Are these people crazy?'

Many important figures had sought him out, but never an emperor.

"Uh, well…"

The bartender recited what he had told everyone else, word for word.

His life depended on it.

"A girl… you say?"

The unexpected information shook Balkan.

"Yes. She looked about seven years old. The atmosphere was strange. Havitz drank whiskey, and the girl didn't touch anything."

Natasha asked, "What did they talk about? Be honest."

"I really don't know! The guests started vomiting, so I went to get ice from the storage."

Smoto, with his keen sense, confirmed the bartender's expression.

'He's not lying.'

The remaining question was who the child, more interesting to Havitz than Rebecca, was.

"We'll find out, no matter what."

No one disagreed with Balkan, and the four knights stood up, ready for action.

"Interrogate everyone. Even if they didn't see Havitz, someone must have seen the child. Grab anyone you can."

The Holy War wouldn't stand idly by, but the four knights of Gustav had nothing to lose.

"No need."

The bartender pointed ahead with a shocked expression.

"There! That child!"

The four knights turned to see Wena Wizard standing there.

'Alone? Where's Havitz?'

Balkan quickly scanned the surroundings but soon fixed his gaze on Wizard.

'No, more importantly, is this child…'

They all had the same thought.

'Is she really human?'
