Chapter 1264:

"Ariana."

As Mini returned to an average size, her satellite, Ariana, joined her.

"Let's go."

"Okay."

As Mini approached Girshin, her scale began to grow exponentially.

And finally, upon reaching the same size, she unleashed a full-powered punch.

BOOOOM!

A massive amount of atmosphere was pushed away, and with a thunderous impact, she struck Girshin.

Girshin’s upper body staggered and bent.

"Grghhhh!"

With their sizes equal, the balance of power was even—

What remained was a difference in technique.

"A mere human..."

As Girshin slashed his sword, the atmosphere ignited, forming an arc of fire.

"Hmph!"

Mini spun gracefully behind him and wrapped her arms around his waist.

"Now!"

Ariana, riding on Mini, vaulted over Girshin’s face and landed on his forehead.

"Go!"

Mini leaned back and executed a German Suplex.

"Hiyaaaaaa!"

As Girshin's legs lifted off the ground and he was flipped upside down toward the earth—

"Phantom Press."

Ariana's projection appeared and slammed Girshin’s face into the ground.

RUMMMMBLE.

The earth shook, and clouds of dust spread across the city.

"Aaaaagh!"

Girshin sprang up, swinging his fists, but passed right through Ariana’s projection.

‘It’s not a physical body.’

It was her incarnation technique—Childlike Manifestation (telekinesis).

"Over here, idiot!"

As Girshin lowered his head to look for Ariana, Mini charged again.

"Holle holle ho!"

And the moment Girshin looked forward again, she struck his solar plexus with a dropkick.

"Urgh!"

He was sent flying by her massive weight, and Ariana immediately activated her incarnation technique.

"Hrrrgh!"

Her projection ripped a mountain peak from the earth and smashed it down onto Girshin’s crown.

WHACK.

"Ugh!"

Trees, soil, and all manner of flora and fauna poured onto Girshin’s face like sludge.

‘What the hell?’

The projection was just a conduit for power—Ariana was moving matter with her mind.

‘To lift the mass of an entire mountain...’

Before the thought could finish, Mini locked Girshin in a chokehold and performed a twister move.

"Aaaargh!"

Girshin was now pissed off.

"Gotcha!"

Ariana's projection dove in, clasped its hands together, and hammered the giant's stomach.

BOOOOOOM!

The citizens inside the city were stunned, as if the battle playing out wasn’t even real.

Shirone felt the same.

‘This is getting interesting.’

"Yahweh, the giants are moving out of the city. If we’re going to move, now’s the time."

Euntara’s words snapped him back to his senses.

"Right. Let’s get the people to the Holy War. Mini and Ariana will hold this place. And I…"

Suddenly, Shirone flinched.

"What is it?"

Euntara asked, but Shirone gave no reply. He just stared toward the sky and muttered:

"What… is that?"

The law was changing again.

At the core of the Sun, Ikael was holding Amy from behind as she endured.

Though it was only in the form of spirit, she would be able to protect Amy to the end.

“Ikael! Don’t do this!”

“It’s alright. You’re not an archangel. If someone must die in the end…”

…it should be me.

“Ikael…”

As Amy spoke with a sorrowful voice, the divine law that had bound them was released.

“Huh?”

The moment they regained consciousness, they found themselves separated, having returned to their original forms, and looked around.

“What happened?”

Amy thought she knew.

“Shirone did it. He stopped God’s law!”

They still didn’t know that Satiel had helped in the process.

Ikael looked out with a divine gaze.

“Ahh.”

Even on the scale of an archangel, it was a breathtaking sight—a completed artificial structure.

And beyond that, they could see Shirone breathing a sigh of relief.

“Well done.” She couldn’t help but see Goffin’s image flicker over him.

“Let’s go to Shirone.”

As Amy drew forth the full power of her fire incarnation, Ikael nodded happily.

“Alright…”

Kuuuuuuu!

In that moment, Ikael saw the world’s law twisting through a signal of light.

“Why?”

The reason she didn’t leave right away was because it didn’t feel like it came from God—it was something entirely different.

‘This is… a heart.’

A quantum signal.

Though the feeling wasn’t exactly pleasant to them,

…it wasn’t cold either, and it was full of biological variables.

“Amy! Get out now!”

Perhaps that made it even more dangerous.

“Huh? What are you—”

Amy, who started to ask, felt it too.

“…Ah.”

She turned her head, following a faint sense of nostalgia, and her body was engulfed in a halo of light.

“Amy!”

And then Ikael too was swallowed in white light.

Shirone frowned.

“Guh!”

The just-completed Shirone Sphere was unraveling in reverse time.

‘Tachyon?’

Shirone was the one who made the Sphere.

So for it to be undone like this, someone would have to directly interfere with Shirone’s own cause.

‘You think I’ll let you?’

Activating Ouroboros, Shirone entered the battle to protect his own past.

As if caught in a wave of In, countless events bubbled up like foam.

“Ughhh!”

It was different.

‘This thing… isn’t God.’

Unlike the God who manipulated causality with cold logic, this one felt undeniably human.

‘No, not just that… it feels like…’

…like it understands me.

Having sensed a rough outline of its nature, Shirone poured his strength into restoring the past once more.

Within the clash of tachyon signals, the Shirone Sphere shimmered like a mirage.

It was a matter of who would push through.

‘Don’t be ridiculous.’

That’s mine.

The moment Shirone locked onto the decisive event and forced the Sphere through—

—Right?

He felt the illusion of hearing a voice.

And then…

“Huh?”

A fierce solar wind blasted toward the direction of Shirone’s planet.

Just seconds later, the skeleton of the Shirone Sphere was scorched by the strike.

‘Miracle Stream.’

Shirone, too, had to desperately erect a barrier to preserve his life.

“Grrrrrgh!”

An enormous heat wave swept over him, but what was worse was the planet.

“Damn it!”

Shirone looked back at his homeland, now twinkling like a small star.

‘It’ll reach in about ten minutes.’

At this intensity, even with a magnetic field, half the hemisphere’s organisms would be burned.

‘They caught me off guard. I didn’t expect a solar wind attack…’

A being skilled in both photons and quanta.

“…Huh?”

Suddenly, it came to him.

“Amy.”

He turned his attention toward the Sun, but nothing remained in the core.

“Amy!”

Shirone, who had been flying toward the planet, soon realized something.

‘I’m already on the planet.’

Shirone vanished.

And with that, the simultaneous events that had been unfolding across the world—excluding the Holy War—came to an end.

Rumble. Rumble.

On the mountainside where aftershocks still trembled, Tess stood and let the tears she had held back finally fall.

“Rian!”

It might have been a delusion.

No matter how skilled a master of Schema was, even gathering light couldn’t provide a clearer vision than daylight.

But she knew it by instinct.

Ymir’s attack had ended. It was a shock so immense and overwhelming that it brought certainty.

Tess climbed onto her horse.

“We have to go!”

This time, even Clump couldn’t stop her.

‘Is this really how it ends…?’

That the final moment of the first swordsman in human history to reach the realm of the divine could be something so hollow?

“I’m coming too.”

With the determination to see it for himself, Clump spurred his horse.

“Hyah!”

From a distance, Rai watched them ride away.

“…Hoo.”

The fist that had struck the ground was dozens of times larger than Ymir’s three-meter-tall frame.

And Ymir’s expression… was not good.

“That was fun.”

It was the emptiness that follows the most thrilling moment in an eternity of lifetimes.

Crack.

The enormous arm shrank, returning to its original size.

Left in the crater where the fist had struck was a mangled, unrecognizable body.

‘He’s dead.’

That’s what he thought.

When the blow had reached Rian’s will, Divine Transcendence had been shattered. And when it reached his body, even his conviction had been broken.

The scattered remains were proof, and in that death, Rian…

‘…He’s dead.’

Ymir agreed with himself.

‘Unbelievable, really.’

His journey flashed before his eyes like a lantern parade.

‘After everything I went through… I was called a fool from all sides, thrown into hell, suffered every kind of suffering…’

And when he finally arrived at the pinnacle—

‘I thought I could finally fight properly.’

But he’d been utterly crushed by Ymir.

‘Come to think of it…’

A memory surfaced.

‘Didn’t someone tell me this would happen?’

A girl prophet at the desert oasis of Vanguard had once predicted his future.

‘Hah!’

He’d thought she was just a scam.

‘Whatever. It’s fine now.’

There was nothing more he could do, was there?

‘I’m completely shattered. I’m exhausted. I just want to sleep. Damn it, how do I sleep? Even my brain’s been smashed to bits.’

Why am I still thinking?

After a moment’s thought, Rian gave up and waited for true death.

He had lived like fire and now was simply worn out.

Time passed.

‘Why am I not dead yet!?’

Frustration flared within Rian.

Was he going to exist as nothing more than thought forever?

‘No.’

He knew the truth.

There was still one thing left—something he had wanted to ignore, even after his resolve had been broken.

‘Shirone.’

He had failed to protect his liege.

‘I’m a broken sword.’

– Smille.

‘Can I fight again? Can I protect my lord while carrying the shame of defeat?’

Ogent said:

– Smille.

Ymir’s brow twitched.

“Huh?”

Lines of light began to flow over the flesh and blood resembling Rian’s remains.

Will filled the signal of Idea, and the scattered flesh began to stir.

“Heh…”

Ymir laughed.

“Hehehe! Hahaha!”

Rian’s body slowly rose.

‘In my heart…’

Let a kingdom of conviction be built.

Reborn not as a battle-crazed swordsman, but as a true knight—his spirit would never…

‘…break again.’

Yes.

Rian and Ogent spoke in unison.

– Idea cannot be destroyed.

Everything inherited from his forebears fused together, and the Great Blade was once more gripped in Rian’s right hand.

“…Hoo.”

The moment his clearly shining eyes opened, Ymir felt a chill.

‘Incredible regeneration…’

But it wasn’t the body he was talking about.

‘Even I wouldn’t have come back. After burning everything, was there really anything left?’

It had been that kind of battle.

“So… what now? Doesn’t look like you’re giving up.”

The beast within subdued, Rian remained calm.

‘This is the end.’

His resurrection ability was gone, now that his Idea had fused with Ogent’s.

“I’ve fought enough.”

He had no lingering attachment to life.

“But I will protect.”

He had crossed the river of death to protect Shirone, humanity, and the world.

“…Ho?”

Ymir stroked his chin.

“Sounds impressive, but how? You can’t protect anything if you can’t beat me.”

“I’m not alone.”

“Rian!”

Tess and Clump, having reached the battlefield, were shocked to see Rian still alive.

“You… are you okay?”

Ymir shrugged his shoulders.

“Keh, yeah. You’re definitely not alone. Thinking of hiding behind meat shields now?”

Just then, space twisted, and a different landscape unfolded past a clear boundary.

Jin Seong-eum stepped across the border.

“Transport complete.”

Behind her stood everyone who had experienced the simultaneous events with Shirone.

Nade stepped forward, cracking his knuckles.

“Alright, so that big dumb oaf is first? Hey, come on. I’ll smash you into pieces.”

Ymir just stared.

When neither Miro, Gaold, nor even his friends reacted, Nade turned back with a confused look.

“What, what…?”
