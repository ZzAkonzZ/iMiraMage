Chapter 839: Depression (3)

When Xing from the Law Department made her cryptic statement, the eyes of the Ivory Tower residents changed immediately.

The Law Department had only five members but possessed the highest number of stars, totaling 17.

This was because they had two 4-star members, while some departments had none.

'What a powerful law.'

As Shirone focused his strength, Xing's presence began to fade.

Following suit, Freed, Amanta, and Minerva overcame Xing's law, with the 4-star and 3-star members following.

'Ugh! This won't do!'

However, residents below the 2-star level were still overwhelmed by Xing's presence.

Freed asked Xing, "Are you planning to side with the Integrated Universe Management Department?"

"No matter what decision I make, it has nothing to do with you. Though saying this is meaningless."

Xing added, "I haven't changed my mind yet. I just said that the illusion called Shirone is unique."

She turned to Shirone.

"Why do you try to save humanity? Are you a program designed to protect other illusions?"

Shirone wanted to say, "I am human," but he couldn't bring himself to say it.

'There's no way to change Xing's logic. But it's not delusion. She's a human who has reached the highest level in the law.'

Yet, she considered everything an illusion.

Conversely, Shirone thought that Xing might be an illusion to him.

'Everyone except me is an illusion... Humans are truly lonely beings.'

'I hate that.'

Today, he envied the Gaia beings who had achieved an integrated mental system.

"Whether it's fake or real doesn't matter. If we leave things as they are, the army of hell will destroy the world. And the Ivory Tower is part of that world. We must stop it."

"Do you really think so?" Shirone flinched.

"I don't understand. Everyone here is designed to understand the world quite well. How did you reach that conclusion?"

Freed agreed.

"You're lying."

Shirone's eyes sharpened.

"You can push any opinion in the Grand Assembly, but you can't lie. Where do you think the army of hell came from? Murderers? Rapists? Sure, there's some of that, but no. They came from us."

Freed spread his arms.

"You probably worked incredibly hard to reach the level of Yahweh. But so did everyone else. So what's left? Love for humanity? No, we know better than anyone. The reason we wanted to reach the top of the pyramid was just one."

Freed slowly turned his head.

"Because there are no humans there."

"Dreams? A sense of achievement? No matter how you dress it up, it's the same. You don't want to be entangled with humans or hurt by them. The top of the pyramid looked the brightest to you because no one lived there."

That was why the Ivory Tower was created.

"How many trivial arguments have you had? How many times were your thoughts denied and ridiculed? But up there, no one is there. The merit of the summit is enough. You can live without feeling the nature of humans."

Shirone bit his lip.

"You know it too. If you weren't disillusioned with the world, you wouldn't have come this far. Save humanity? We're just taking responsibility for ourselves. As Nane said, it's a great purification."

The Grand Star spoke.

"Freed, Nane's philosophy has already been..."

"Grand Star."

Shirone interrupted.

"I'll continue."

Feeling a chill, the Grand Star closed her mouth, and Minerva swallowed hard.

'This doesn't look good...'

Shirone fixed his gaze on Freed.

"You're right. I can't say I never had those feelings. But I've already forgiven them. Because I'm just like them."

"That's your business. I'm not Yahweh."

Shirone nodded.

"Even so... I got angry."

Freed finally turned to Shirone.

"Why do I have to go through this? Why can't I be happy? I was so angry and frustrated that eventually..."

Shirone pointed to his chest with his thumb.

"I created a terrible monster here."

Even without opening his chest, Shirone's precarious aura spoke volumes about the size of his anger.

"The reason I could endure again was because I love this world. If I'm not happy here, it will be the same anywhere."

"So that's your business..."

"I'm not asking for understanding."

Shirone's eyes changed.

"I don't know why I was given the chance to save everyone, but this is truly the last chance. If I let go of the level of Yahweh, it will be..."

A tiny bit of anger lurking within Shirone's heart.

"No one can be happy."

The Great Stars of each department frowned.

'What is this?'

Suddenly, a black monster tore through Shirone's clothes and screamed at the stars.

Screeeech!

"Ugh!"

The 2-star residents recoiled in shock.

'No, it's not a monster.'

As they regained their composure, the countless thorns that had overwhelmed them disappeared, and a single vibrated.

Minerva clenched her teeth.

'He activated with just his aura. I couldn't even do that in my prime.'

With killing intent directed at the entire world, Freed asked with a displeased expression.

"Are you threatening us?"

"Yes. But it's not my will."

To prevent the destruction of humanity, Yahweh made the final choice.

A terrible inner monster.

'The only way to contain that anger was...'

Extreme philanthropy, where if no one could be happy, then no one could be happy.

'Just one more time...'

Shirone's voice trembled.

"Can't you give me one more chance? The fact that you came this far because you hated humans means you loved them that much. You really want to live together, don't you?"

Xing, with her lips tightly closed, looked at Shirone.

'Together?'

Her wounds would never heal.

'Where is that? Humans are illusions that torment me with all kinds of emotions.'

But there was one exception.

If Shirone wasn't an illusion, if an absolute being appeared and guaranteed he was human.

'Maybe I'll think differently...'

As all the stars focused on Freed, time passed relentlessly.

Ordinary humans couldn't withstand the atmosphere for even a minute, but the stars held their ground for over an hour.

"I have something to say."

At that moment, Lampa, a 3-star resident of the System Inspection Department, raised his hand.

He was over 100 years old, and a yellow bird emitting light had landed on his wrinkled hand.

'Lampa. The world's greatest information mage.'

His information transmission magic, Telebird, had a range that covered the entire planet.

Freed, still deep in thought under the watchful eye of , asked gruffly.

"What is it? At such a critical time."

"I thought you should know before making a decision. Just now, the emperor of the Kashan Empire has changed."

Shirone's head snapped around.

"The emperor has changed?"

Lampa waited for Freed's instruction.

"Continue."

"The succession ceremony has just ended. The new emperor is Gando, the biological son of Teraje Mistra. Also, Kashan has withdrawn from the Holy War and seems to be siding with Gustav."

'Uorin... has given up on Kashan.'

In other words, Gustav's power would become significantly stronger.

'What's happening?'

As Shirone turned his head, the Grand Star used Gaia's ability to show Kashan's territory.

"Hiya! Run! Run!"

Habitz led the army of hell, galloping across the vast territory of the Kashan Empire.

On the distant horizon, Kashan's defense forces surged like a tidal wave.

'They always have all the fun.'

Torturing prisoners in occupied territories and sitting in a high chariot all day was unbearably boring.

"I'm going!"

Smodo, startled by Habitz's sudden action, quickly mounted his horse and followed.

"Damn it! What are you thinking?"

Even if he was outside the law, he was still human. If stabbed, he would die.

Smodo shouted to Balkan, who was riding alongside him.

"Is this okay? He'll get himself killed!"

"No."

Balkan smirked.

"He won't die."

As the 30,000-strong Kashan defense force and the army of hell clashed, a clear boundary formed.

Habitz was buried in the midst of the weapons.

"Let me kill some too!"

Running around the battlefield like a child, swinging his sword, Habitz's skills were average.

Yet no one could kill him, and tasting blood, he ventured deeper.

"What? That's the emperor?"

The commander of the archer unit sharply turned and ordered his soldiers.

"Shoot! If we take him down, the war is over!"

A thousand soldiers fired their arrows simultaneously.

As the arrows arced through the sky and fell, high-grade magic bombs exploded.

The area was devastated, and the massive demons were torn apart, rolling on the ground.

"Is it over?"

Hope gleamed in the commander's eyes.

But at that moment, Habitz emerged unscathed from the smoke.

"Damn it! Everyone, reload... No, I'll take him down myself!"

As the commander drew his sword and charged, Habitz ran to meet him without hesitation.

'Fool. He's asking to die.'

Despite the many demons fighting around them, no one stepped in to help Satan.

'I can do it! I'll capture him! I'll be a hero!'

Just one clash revealed that Habitz's swordsmanship was barely intermediate.

'This is real! I'll take down the emperor...'

His heart pounded wildly.

"Yaaah!"

He delivered the best strike of his life.

"What..."

The blade grazed Habitz's throat as he leaned back, but it only cut through empty air.

'Was he just lucky? That can't be right.' The commander swung his sword repeatedly, but only managed to barely nick Habitz's collar.

'Damn it! He's not even wearing armor! Is he actually a master swordsman? But these movements... It doesn't feel like he's dodging by sight.'

'What?'

When the commander saw Habitz's face, his body froze in shock.

'His eyes... are closed?'

Balkan smiled.

"It's not a miracle."

When all of humanity rolled the dice of history, Habitz was simply the last one standing.

'It's probability, just probability.'

Someone had to be left standing.

'It becomes inevitable.'

Just as it seems inevitable that we were born after surviving billions of competitors.

"Your Majesty! Are you having fun?"

Balkan laughed heartily as he beheaded the commander with a single stroke.

"Not bad. But I want to see Uorin soon."

"You'll have her in your grasp soon."

As Smodo arrived late, Balkan looked over the army of hell advancing towards Aganos.

In the distance, a white flag fluttered.
