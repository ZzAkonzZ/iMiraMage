Chapter 992: Karma Chain (2)

Is this what it feels like to become a bomb?

As energy that wasn't his own surged exponentially, Rian clenched his teeth.

"Ugh!"

When hot steam started spewing out between his teeth, he realized he couldn't endure it any longer.

Regardless of the pressure, demonic energy was surging through the chains of Gehenna.

"Aaah!" Rian opened his upper body wide and released the annoying pressure outward.

Rumble!

Hot steam spread along the ground in a reverse spiral around him.

"Huff! Huff!"

Although he was breathing heavily, he didn't feel particularly tired.

Shirone's voice was heard.

"Gehenna."

When Rian slowly raised his head, he saw Shirone walking from beyond the horizon.

"Shirone, what is this?"

"An eternal flame. Also known as Hellfire. It's something that shouldn't happen to a living soul."

Shirone looked around and said.

"Usually, one would burn in Hellfire and become the essence of demonic energy. But you kept regenerating your body endlessly even in reality. That's why you became a living Hellfire, Gehenna."

"So, I have to carry this around forever?"

"Even though you've fallen into hell, your mind is still tied to reality. It's because you're a living soul. This is called karma, and the chains tied to your heart represent your lingering attachment to reality. If you purify that karma, the chains will disappear."

"Then I will die."

Shirone nodded.

"Yes. Complete annihilation. Your body has already disappeared, but your soul will evaporate as well."

"I understand. But how do I purify it?"

"Hmm, according to my memory..."

Shirone recalled the records of Omega.

"The under world is a realm of emotions, so I don't know exactly. I only indirectly experienced it by following the records of living souls. When Goffin met Lucifer, a voice was heard from the sky."

"The sky?"

Rian, looking up at the sky filled with flames, shouted loudly.

"Hey! Say something!"

Rumble!

The sky only spewed angry flames.

When the red light on the signal device turned on, the informant twisted her waist anxiously.

"What should I do? What should I do?"

Basically, at least one informant is assigned to each purification target.

There was an abundance of labor in this world, and most of them had nothing to do.

"He broke the Karma Chain by himself." As far as she knew, this was impossible, especially with the Hellfire of Gehenna.

Beep! Beep!

Every time Rian shouted, the buzzer rang, but she didn't have the courage to respond.

"I need to tell him the purification time..."

In the under world, the time of the photon system is used as a measure of the size of emotions.

In other words, the greater the pain, the faster time flows, and the lesser the pain, the slower it flows.

"Then why?"

Why can't it be measured?

The signal from the blood in the hemispherical bowl read "unmeasurable."

It was the first time in her life, and she didn't even know such a signal could appear.

"Boss!"

Eventually, ignoring the red light on the signal device, the informant went straight to find Lete.

"What?" Lete raised an eyebrow.

"Unmeasurable? What do you mean? No being can receive such a signal."

"I'm telling you, that's the signal that came out."

At the informant's frustrated expression, Lete buried her elbows on the desk and touched her head.

"The chains of Gehenna..."

The Fire Resource Management Corporation is an independent organization, even though it receives funds from hell.

It is a group dedicated to its original purpose, purification of demonic energy being its top priority.

"An unusual guy?"

It was a bland reaction, but there was no other way to explain it.

"Even Satan can't defy Gehenna. He was born from Hellfire too. In short, it's a system malfunction."

"What should we do? We can't just honestly say it's unmeasurable. The signal device keeps ringing..."

"Don't make a fuss. It's a simple error. The Karma Chain is meant to keep the purification target bound to hell in some way. It just got temporarily disconnected. It will reconnect."

"What if it disconnects again? It's dangerous for a living soul to roam freely. They could come to the Fire Corporation."

Lete, with dark circles under her eyes, called out.

"Monoras, come in."

After waiting for 10 seconds, there was no sound of footsteps in the hallway.

"Sigh."

She lowered her head deeply, hesitated for a moment, then let out a trembling voice.

"Ah, ah..."

Bang, the door opened.

"Boss! What's the matter...!"

Monoras, who entered with wide eyes, saw a scene completely different from what he expected.

The informant was looking back, and Lete was staring coldly from behind the desk.

A long silence followed.

"Hmm."

Monoras smacked his lips, turned around, and headed for the door without a word.

"Hey!"

Lete slammed the desk and stood up.

"I called you! Please do some work! Every time I see you, I feel like I've aged thousands of years!"

Monoras asked indifferently.

"What is it?"

Knowing that shouting was pointless since the early days of the under world's creation.

"Go on a business trip. There's a problem with the purification system, so we need to call an expert. The city of magical engineering, Laviet. Tell the Grand Duke to come here."

Without a word of acknowledgment, Monoras was about to leave the room when Lete quickly added.

"Don't take bribes. Got it?"

"Yes."

"Don't accept hospitality. Don't hit the demons, don't harass the residents, don't abuse your power. No, never mind."

Lete waved her hand.

"Just do it all. Do it all, but please do the job properly. Don't get sidetracked. Okay?"

"Don't worry. Who do you think I am?"

Who are you?

Monoras turned around at the door, gave a charming smile, and winked.

"Fire Corporation Secretary, Monoras."

Lete, staring blankly with her mouth open, slumped into her chair.

"...I'm really going to go crazy."

By the time it was well past noon, Shirone and Amy had arrived in the southern part of the Kingdom of Tormia.

Although the damage from the demons wasn't severe, the aftermath of the emotional plague had spread to this area.

As screams echoed from every house, Amy pointed the way to the noble district.

"If I remember correctly, it's that building."

It was a massive structure, comparable to the mansion of the Carmis family.

Shirone confirmed the name "Cross" engraved on the nameplate.

"Kaiden."

Amy nodded.

"He should be at the main house. I checked the Valkyrie unit's roster just in case, but he hasn't enlisted."

"Does he still like Maya?"

"That's why he's holed up. If he likes her that much, he should just confess. It's so frustrating."

"But Kaiden..."

"Yes, he was born with the fate of the Red Cross. He can never achieve what he truly desires, but he can exert 100% of his potential in everything else. At the time, I thought it was nonsense, but..."

Now that I've approached the truth of the world, I fully understand Kaiden's feelings.

"A fate that reaches the extreme of the law in exchange for giving up one's heart. That's probably the mechanism of the Red Cross."

"That's why I asked you. With Miracle Stream, there might be a way. If Kaiden joins, it will be a great help to humanity's strength."

Shirone had no objections to that.

"Alright, let's go in."

They knocked on the door from outside, but neither the guards nor the butler came out to greet them.

Being one of the top families in the kingdom, they must have been managing the emotional plague well, but it still seemed difficult.

"Who is it?"

As they considered climbing over the wall, a man in martial arts attire walked over from the garden.

It was Kaiden's father.

"Hello. I'm Amy. Carmis Amy."

Kaiden's father, who had been watching her, instead looked at Shirone with a gleam in his eye.

"Yahweh?"

"Hello. We'd like to see Kaiden."

The door opened, and Kaiden's father, with his hands behind his back, walked towards the mansion.

"I knew someone would come. If my son isn't a complete fool, that is. Come in."

The mansion was desolate, and even the air was cold.

It couldn't be blamed on the emotional plague, as it felt like a very old chill.

"It's been a long time since we've spoken. After attending Pisho's funeral, he hasn't come out of the basement."

His father knocked on the iron door.

"Kaiden, your friends are here."

There was no response, and with a sigh, he climbed the stairs.

"From now on, it's up to you. He might be dead. It's a pity. The genius of the family..."

The sense of loss that he couldn't do anything until the world ended was evident in his voice.

As his father left, Shirone exchanged glances with Amy and knocked on the door again.

"Kaiden, it's Shirone. Amy is here too. Can we talk for a moment?"

"Go away."

It was a voice that could only be heard with an ear pressed to the door, but it was definitely Kaiden's.

Amy spoke.

"We'll leave soon. We have something to tell you about Maya."

They tried baiting him, but there was no response even after five minutes.

"Shirone, open it."

Amy pointed to the doorknob.

"Can you open it? Open it."

"Using force won't help persuade him. If Kaiden doesn't want to..."

"He didn't react even after hearing Maya's name. If he's trapped inside himself, it doesn't matter either way. Someone has to go in and pull him out."

Shirone found her words reasonable and cast Miracle Stream on the doorknob.

As the lock quietly released and the door opened, the sound of a pen scratching paper was heard quickly.

"No, this isn't it!"

In the dimly lit room, Kaiden was drawing with a single light on his desk.

"...Oh my."

Seeing the room covered with drawings of Maya, Amy got goosebumps.

"This isn't it either!" Kaiden threw the paper he was drawing on the floor and pulled out a new sheet.

At first, they thought he had failed, but when Shirone stepped in to check, it was a perfect drawing of Maya's face.

'What a peculiar technique. How did he draw this...'

Shirone stopped thinking as he looked at Kaiden.

"Huff. Huff."

Could that even be called a drawing?

Kaiden was just moving his pen from left to right at an incredible speed.

'It's just straight lines.' But as those lines accumulated and formed a surface, a perfect intaglio drawing emerged.

"No! This isn't it!"

Kaiden threw the paper away again and this time grabbed a pen in each hand.

As both hands moved simultaneously, another new drawing of Maya emerged from the symphony of lines.

"Damn it! Why! Why!"

Kaiden, who had completed the drawing, threw it on the floor, grabbed his head, and cried.

"Why can't I draw Maya?"

Shirone watched him with sad eyes.

'Technically, there's no problem. No, he's already surpassed human limits. But...'

To Kaiden's eyes, it wasn't Maya.

'Because he loves her.'

No drawing could capture the essence of the woman named Maya.

"Kaiden."

Shirone placed a hand on Kaiden's shoulder.

"Go to Maya. She's in a dangerous place right now. She might even die."

"...I can't."

Kaiden said with a look of despair.

"I can't draw Maya. If I can't draw her, I can't protect her. I can't do it."

Amy finally understood why Kaiden had been drawing like a madman.

"Maya is now..."

Shirone, who usually kept silent about simultaneous events, shared information this time.

"She's in as bad a state as you are."

For the first time, Kaiden's gaze turned to Shirone.
