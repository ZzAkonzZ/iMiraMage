Chapter 597:

Event Coordinates

Time: 2:32 AM

Location: Warehouse No. 19

The fateful Warehouse No. 19.

If anyone remembered the records of Istas’ upper echelons, they would call it that.

After Hexa died at Lucan’s hands, Yolga searched for Miro, who had left.

‘It’s not over yet.’

Miro would find a solution somehow.

‘That’s the kind of person she is.’

Some might place Miro and Yolga on the same level, but to Yolga, Miro was a sister she could respect.

Miro will save this world in the future.

That was why she could remain calm even after hearing the confession of a boy she met in the upper echelons.

“There you are.”

The warehouse door opened, and Mars’ captain Nickel entered.

He was alone, but his body was covered in blood.

“Nickel.”

“The child is confirmed dead.”

“Yes. If the event concludes like this, peace will return to humanity.”

Unlike Yolga, who only worried about Miro, Mars had entered with the mission to protect the kingdom.

“You’ve done well.”

Therefore, there was one more event Nickel had to complete before the event concluded.

“It’s thanks to the sacrifices of Mars’ troops. It wouldn’t have been possible otherwise.”

Yolga smiled, but her eyes were still sad.

“…May I ask, is there really no way out?”

There is.

If Goffin orchestrated all this, Miro achieving her goal and returning to the world was part of his plan.

“The coordinates are all mixed up. I don’t know if we can find an exit.”

If the closed loop event completed a cycle, they would all become ghosts of Istas.

“I see.”

Lost in thought, Nickel placed his hand on his chest and spoke solemnly.

“Thank you on behalf of the kingdom.”

Yolga looked into Nickel’s eyes for a long time.

“You’re welcome.”

But she wasn’t someone who calculated her emotions.

“Rather, I should be…”

As she bowed to Nickel, a dagger plunged into her heart.

“The royal artifact Baekjado. Even you won’t survive.”

Once inside the body, the blade melted, turning all the blood white and solid.

Yolga’s last words were a gush of blood.

“Yolga!”

Edgar struck Nickel’s head to the side, and Mustang caught the falling Yolga.

She was already dead.

In the horrific situation where Yolga’s sincerity was repaid with murder, her comrades’ eyes filled with rage.

“You…!”

Nickel’s face was half gone, but he was still breathing, befitting Mars’ captain.

“Why did you kill her?”

Nine gripped his sword and approached.

“She was dangerous. I’m a soldier. I follow the king’s orders.”

Yolga’s ideology was a threat to the monarchy.

“You bastard!”

As Edgar approached to finish him off, Mustang saw Nickel’s eyes and shouted.

“Get back!”

Nickel pulled out another artifact, the magic bomb Expos.

“For the Tormia Kingdom!”

Before Nine’s thrown sword could reach him, the glass orb of Expos shattered, unleashing a localized magic storm.

The life-consuming heatwave was created from the blood of countless species and the sacrifices of wizards, priceless in gold.

As the terrifying blue light swept through and disappeared, Yolga’s comrades lay on the ground with grief-stricken faces.

“…So that’s how it is.”

The warehouse door opened, and Fermi entered.

Even in the face of his mother’s death and the deaths of his beloved uncles and aunts, Fermi remained unshaken.

‘Calmly, coldly.’

He turned towards Warehouse No. 44, where the vault was.

“To bypass time…”

Event Coordinates

Time: 1:48 AM

Location: Supernatural Psychic Research Society

Returning to Istas through Goffin’s door, Miro held the sleeping prince of Kazra.

Shirone, following behind, still felt uneasy, but a choice had to be made.

“Shirone, don’t overthink it.”

Miro said.

“To you, the present is my future. Take the secrets of the upper echelons back. Do what you can there.”

At that moment, the door opened, and Yolga entered.

‘It’s happening after all.’

Shirone was tense, wondering if he would meet another version of himself, but there was no one else with her except her comrades.

‘The event twisted again.’

So who sent Yolga here?

“Miro, who is the child you’re holding?”

Miro’s eyebrows twitched.

‘She knows. But who?’

Even if they met in another timeline, it wasn’t something to reveal to Yolga.

“He’s the prince of Kazra. Hexa no longer exists here. So give up, sister.”

“No.”

Yolga’s gaze turned to Goffin’s door.

Until the closed loop fully closed, the dimensions would remain connected.

“We can still reverse it.”

“We can’t. Not while I’m here.”

“Miro, don’t say it’s just a few sacrifices. This denies the value of all humanity. If we can’t choose our future, we’ll eventually perish.”

Yolga’s insight spanned 100 years, 1,000 years, over 10,000 years.

“We can’t create a history where everything relies on one transcendent being.”

“Goffin fought for humanity.”

“But he’s not our god. Let us choose. That’s the only way humanity becomes infinite.”

“If we don’t stop now, there’s no future. I’ll sacrifice everything in Goffin’s place.”

There was no compromise, and Yolga spoke firmly.

“I won’t let that happen.”

Miro turned and said.

“Guard Goffin’s door.”

As Yolga’s comrades moved, Shirone and Anchal blocked the door.

‘Is this the right thing?’

Shirone saw merit in both arguments.

Life is like that, and choices might be a punishment.

‘Don’t waver. Do what you believe is right.’

Gaold did, Zulu did, and Plu did.

‘That’s what a wizard is.’

Shirone decided to trust Miro.

For reasons unknown, she didn’t trap Hexa in the dimensional wall but sent him back to the original world.

That was the only light of hope guiding Shirone.

“It’s too late, sister.”

Miro floated the prince of Kazra in the air and placed him in a corner.

“Are you daring me to kill him?”

Having switched Hexa, Miro had no reason to protect the prince at the cost of her life.

“That’s the sacrifice.”

Miro’s eyes gleamed, and a massive Avatar of Kannon, unlike any seen before, emerged.

“From now on, it will be different.”

A Maria avatar also emerged from Yolga’s body.

“Even Kannon can’t break my avatar.”

“Not Saint Maria.”

As Miro’s hands crossed, Kannon’s palms overlapped hundreds of times and fell on Yolga’s head.

“Keep taking the hits.”

Boom!

As the warehouse shook, Yolga’s comrades charged towards Goffin’s door.

“I’ll handle the woman. Guard the door.”

Anchal lured Mustang away, and Edgar and Nine attacked Shirone from both sides.

Using Armand’s keen senses to track their movements, Shirone took a deep breath.

“Phew!”

The wizard’s spirit, adding one more opinion for humanity’s future, was unwavering.

‘Antithesis!’

As his artificial brain turned into Akamai’s eye, Edgar and Nine’s bodies froze just inches away.

“Grr!”

Their will to act was momentarily bound, and the boundary of Shirone’s Spirit Zone disappeared.

Elysion-Sona Explosion.

Light was born inside Edgar and Nine’s bodies, rapidly expanding.

‘Impossible!’

No Spirit Zone could cause phenomena inside a living being with its own mind.

But Shirone, with no mental boundaries, could cast it on the space itself.

‘This is instant death if it hits!’

No matter what, an internal explosion was fatal, so Edgar and Nine barely escaped Antithesis with desperate Skima.

Even that was impressive, but they couldn’t completely avoid the exploding mass wave.

Two consecutive booms sent them flying into the wall.

Their bodies didn’t explode, but Edgar’s mechanical arm was shattered, and Nine suffered severe internal injuries.

Mustang, fiercely fighting Anchal, looked at Shirone in disbelief, and Yolga trembled in her Saint Maria state.

“How…”

It wasn’t because she saw something different.

It was the shock of seeing a familiar but inimitable phenomenon.

“Goffin’s Ultima System?”

Miro shrugged as if it were obvious.

“That child is the future Hexa.”

Yolga’s eyes shook with shock as she looked at Shirone.

With Lycan’s change in behavior after being betrayed by Fermi, Lucan again died with his lower body crushed by Miro.

Yolga’s kiss was a bonus.

Leaving Lucan’s body behind, they set out to find Miro again, and Fermi appeared before them.

Event Coordinates

Time: 1:10 AM

Location: Warehouse No. 65

“Who are you?”

“I’m a merchant. I smelled money here.”

Yolga stared at the young man’s familiar face and finally smiled.

“Merchants are everywhere.”

Mustang said.

“You look like Fermi.”

At the pointed remark, Yolga asked.

“May I know your name?”

“Coincidentally, my name is also Fermi.”

Fermi scratched his head and approached.

“Do you know a Fermi too?”

“Yes. He’s the most precious person to me.”

Fermi scratched his head and approached.

“Do you know someone named Fermi too?”

“Yes. He is the most precious person to me.”

Fermi secretly bit the inside of his lower lip.

As long as Yolga remained steady, he couldn’t afford to waver either.

“So, did you find anything valuable?”

Fermi took out dozens of "Last Scream of the Yeager Family" from his bag.

“Yes, quite a bit. This should be worth billions of gold.”

Yolga stared intently at Fermi’s smile.

“How long have you been here?”

“There’s no clock here, so it’s hard to tell. But it feels like quite a long time biologically.”

Though it didn’t seem like a question, Yolga always hit the mark, so Fermi hurriedly changed the subject.

“Here. It’s a deal.”

When he handed over the bag filled with objects, Yolga accepted it and checked the contents.

“These are good items. Replicas, I see.”

It was no coincidence that Fermi chose to bring "Last Scream of the Yeager Family" to the upper echelons.

Though unspoken, Yolga likely knew this too, and she shook her head with a troubled expression.

“I’ve received the lives of dozens, but I can’t pay that much.”

“I don’t need money. I have more than enough. I’m quite resourceful, you see.”

Yolga laughed as if she were amused.

“That’s impressive. So, what do you want from me?”

“There’s something I want to ask. Getting advice from a great wizard is worth a lot.”

Yolga tilted her head like a curious cat.

“If it’s something I can answer.”

Fermi dropped his smile and asked.

“Am I… doing well?”

For him, it was a question worth more than any amount of money.
