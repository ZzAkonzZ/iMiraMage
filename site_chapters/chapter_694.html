Chapter 694: Reunion Hall (3)

“Still, something feels a bit….”

Since Shirone was likely the only one among the three candidates to have encountered Ra Enemi, he thought the Ivory Tower should add special rules for him.

“I really don’t know. The test’s criteria are based on the domain where Taesung-nim’s Karma operates.”

Tzuoi hesitated for a moment before speaking.

“Since you asked, I’ll tell you—I heard the other two competitors are already close to passing.”

“What? Already?”

It hadn’t even been a month since the test began.

“Why are you so surprised? It’s not like it’s particularly hard, right? You just need to find Ra Enemi and come to the Ivory Tower.”

‘I can’t let my guard down.’

Perhaps because the incident in Radum was so significant, they thought they’d already achieved something?

But the earthly task was ultimately about meeting the Ivory Tower’s standards.

“I think Nane will receive the title of the Star.”

Shirone spoke frankly, but it only made his heart ache.

“Why do you think so?”

“First, his combat power is overwhelming, and above all, his judgment is destructive. Some residents point to Jin Seong-eum, but I think her Karma is lacking. She’s upright but feels a bit rushed.”

Just from the description of their Karma, he could roughly guess what kind of person she was.

“Do the residents also speculate who will pass?”

“Of course. Not long ago, they even conducted a survey among the Stars. I’ve been swamped with work.”

“A survey? What were the results?”

Tzuoi pouted.

“Do you really want to know?”

“Yes. I do.”

He felt uneasy, but he couldn’t back out now.

“Out of 27 votes, Nane came in first. A whopping 17 people supported him.”

“…I see.”

“Jin Seong-eum was second, with 9 votes.”

Shirone’s eyes widened.

“Then I… got one vote?”

Tzuoi patted his shoulder as if comforting him about someone else’s problem.

“Don’t worry too much. We’re not prophets—it’s just what people think. Isn’t Karma about asserting yourself?”

Of course, he wasn’t hurt, but the vote ratio felt strange.

“So, one of the Stars thinks I’ll pass?”

“That’s right. But I don’t know who. It was an anonymous vote. However….”

There was a rumor.

A rumor that the one who voted for Shirone was Taesung, the greatest Star in the Ivory Tower.

‘Is it true? But he rarely disagrees with the Five Great Stars.’

“However, what?”

‘It’s not confirmed, so….’

Tzuoi shook his head.

“Never mind. Just do your best. I’ve taken a bit of a liking to you.”

People who saw through his facade were disgusted.

“I’ll cheer for you. Of course, the voting’s already over.”

As Tzuoi and Musso flew away via spatial movement, Iruki scratched his head and approached.

“Looks like you’re the one with the most extreme job among us.”

“It’s fine. What I’ve lost, I’ve gained in other ways.”

Though he might be behind his competitors in progress, Shirone’s Karma level had risen compared to before.

Rian placed a hand on his shoulder and said,

“We’ll win, Shirone.”

Tzuoi’s words were already gone from Shirone’s mind.

“Of course.”

***

While admiring the beautiful winter garden scenery, Madlen came running breathlessly from the direction of the royal palace.

“Iruki-nim! Iruki-nim!”

For a researcher of Yongrae’s rank to skip a meeting was a matter of attitude, not just work, and Shirone was worried about Iruki.

“Are you okay? You’re not being punished because of me, are you?”

“I’m fine. I just got someone’s approval earlier. Besides, this isn’t school. If they don’t utilize a high-level talent like me, it’s their loss.”

His confidence was unwavering.

“What are you doing here? Yongrae is in an uproar!”

Iruki turned around and said,

“I’ll attend now. It’s not over yet, is it?”

“No, it’s not about the meeting!”

“Huh?”

Madlen’s rabbit-like eyes widened as she shouted.

“Albino-nim is furious and demands your immediate presence! I’ve never seen him so angry in my life!”

A shadow fell over Iruki’s face.

“Sorry, I’d better go now.”

“No, I’m the one who should apologize.”

“Don’t worry. If it’s Father, there won’t be any punishment. Damn it, I was planning to take a few days off this time.”

Shirone’s eyes narrowed.

Back in school, Iruki would often cause trouble on purpose to get suspended when classes bored him.

“Don’t tell me you did it on purpose….”

Iruki cut him off and turned to Rian.

“It was nice meeting you. Take care of Shirone.”

“Sure. Let’s grab a drink next time.”

Kido added,

“Take care, brother.”

The joke meant he’d recognized Iruki’s true nature, so Shirone laughed.

‘I knew you’d like it.’

“Shirone, make sure you pass. I’ll be keeping an ear out for good news.”

Despite his relaxed smile, Iruki turned and sprinted toward the royal palace as if his backside was on fire.

Shirone looked at Rian and Kido and said,

“Alright, let’s get going too. There’s still a chance to turn things around. From now on, we’ll be faster too.”

Kido asked,

“You said you had something in mind?”

“Yeah. I’m thinking of going to Kashan.”

“Kashan? That’s the Empire, isn’t it?”

Since the taste of memories didn’t convey everything, Kido didn’t know about Shirone’s connection to Kashan.

Rian said,

“You’re planning to meet Terraze, right?”

“She’s someone I’d rather not owe a favor to, but….”

Wuorin wasn’t an easy person to deal with.

“Given the situation, it seems I’ll have to play this card. Wuorin would know about Ra Enemi.”

“So our next destination is Kashan. Let’s see….”

Rian unfolded a rough map.

“The routes out of Bashka are the Kazura Kingdom and the Across Kingdom. But Kazura has closed its borders, so….”

The only option was to go through Across, which ran parallel to Tormia along the coastline.

“Then we’d pass through Across, Märhen, and Zaib to reach Kashan. But is this map’s scale accurate? The distance from Kashan’s border to the capital is longer than crossing three kingdoms.”

Kashan was the largest empire in the world.

“Will it be okay? Even if we ride nonstop by carriage, it’ll take two months.”

“No. We won’t take the Across route. Let’s go to Kazura. We’ll jump straight to Kashan from there.”

“Hmm, that’s one way.”

Wuorin was the daughter born between Kazura’s King Orkamp and Terraze Mistra.

After the paternity scandal, Kazura cut off diplomacy with other nations, but that only proved it was under Kashan’s rule.

“There must be a direct spatial channel between Kazura and Kashan. Using it would save time.”

“But we won’t get approval. If we’re caught sneaking in, it could lead to international conflict.”

“Orkamp will want to meet me.”

Shirone’s eyes darkened with sorrow.

“Because the queen’s son died in my place.”

Guffin used Kazura’s prince to eliminate Hexa’s cause.

“I see.”

“Saving time is one thing, but I also think he should be told about his son’s death.”

Every day, he must be living in despair, clinging to the hope that his child might still be alive.

“So you planned to stop by Kazura from the start.”

Though he spoke of efficiency, the latter reason was likely stronger.

‘That’s why he’s in third place.’

Rian realized why Shirone had only received one vote in the Ivory Tower’s survey.

Though Tzuoi didn’t say it, she probably evaluated Shirone’s Karma like this:

Grand, but too slow.

‘Because he tries to embrace everything.’

But it was precisely because Shirone was like this that Rian had entrusted his entire life to him.

‘Whoever gave you that one vote won’t regret it.’

Having made up his mind, Rian said,

“Alright, let’s go to Kazura!”

Kido raised his hand.

“Sounds good, but how do we get there? You said the borders are closed.”

“Huh?”

Rian blinked.

***

“You want to go to Kazura?”

Aria, sitting at the royal foreign ministry’s desk, repeated the question.

“Yes. The borders are closed, but I thought there might be a secret diplomatic channel.”

“Hmm.”

Though she had a soft spot for Shirone, her work left no room for personal feelings.

“Is Kazura absolutely necessary? If it’s about reaching Kashan, I can send a cooperation request to the nearest country.”

“That would still take too long. If we can’t jump directly to Kashan’s capital, we’ll lose time either way.”

And currently, the only way to reach Kashan’s capital was through Kazura.

“If there’s a channel connected to Kazura….”

Aria took off her glasses and placed them on the desk.

“I’d lie and say there isn’t. That’s my job. But the reason I don’t need to lie is because there really isn’t one. The channel is completely blocked.”

It was the most honest answer a diplomat could give.

“Is there really no way?”

“There are three main ways to reach Kazura. First, cross the border. But the security is tight. Second, use the Red Line’s channel. Since Kazura is part of the Red Line, you can go through the Magic Association. But this would let every Red Line country track your movements. Third, pass through the demilitarized zone designated by the kingdom’s treaty. Usually, there are secret meeting places between nations that aren’t publicly disclosed. I assume you came to me for the third option, but even that’s blocked now.”

“Hmm.”

Shirone stared intently at Aria.

Though she said there was no way, she had just listed all possible methods—except one she couldn’t voice.

“The location of the secret meeting place is known only to high-ranking nobles, so the security is naturally lax. Not nonexistent, but easier to bypass than other places. But if caught, Tormia would face massive reparations.”

“Hmmmm.”

As Shirone’s hum grew longer, Aria’s gaze sharpened.

“The magic circle’s location is a secret, but….”

She picked up a pen and scribbled eight numbers intermittently, then filled in the gaps to form pi.

Shirone memorized the original numbers.

‘XY coordinates. Behind the Crete Royal Palace in the Jijeo Mountain Range.’

Aria clasped her hands again.

“It’s completely closed off now, so no one goes there. Even if you do, the security code won’t work.”

Shirone could decipher any code.

“I see. I’ll give up on that method.”

As Shirone smiled, Aria’s expression softened.

“Good thinking. It’s better to go through Across.”

“Yes, thank you.”

“Don’t mention it. Just don’t pretend not to know me when you join the Ivory Tower.”

She wouldn’t reveal secrets without a price.

“Of course. I’ll definitely visit you again.”

Shirone turned and took a carriage out of the royal palace, disembarking at the capital’s outskirts.

After confirming the carriage had left, Kido looked at Shirone.

“Do you know where it is?”

“Yes. The coordinates are precise, so it should be easy to find.”

Rian asked,

“What are you talking about? Weren’t we going through Across?”

“……”

Kido’s eyes squinted.

“No, we’re taking the spatial magic circle to jump to Kazura.”

As Shirone explained while climbing the Jijeo Mountain Range, Rian finally understood.

“I see. So we’re becoming spies?”

“If we’re caught, yes.”

The coordinates Aria provided led to a dense forest.

Pushing through artificially transplanted trees, they found a hidden passage leading underground.

The reason they couldn’t enter immediately was that the stairs were covered in dust, showing no signs of use for over a year.

“It’s better not to step on them, right?”

Shirone agreed with Kido’s opinion.

“Convection magic would be too noticeable. Let’s teleport.”

Grabbing Kido and Rian, Shirone turned into a flash of light, bypassing the stairs.

Descending flawlessly through narrow, winding paths, they arrived at a large space on the third underground level.

A massive magic circle was drawn, and alchemical devices lined the walls to control it.

Generators hummed, and intricate circuits lit up.

“Can you do it?”

“Yeah. I’ll try.”

Carefully analyzing the circuits, he saw a spherical cluster of rotating code.

‘This is the firewall.’

Deconstructing it with the Ultima System, a single starlight appeared at the junction of all circuits.

‘This is the core code.’

Activating it, a majestic sound echoed through the cavern as a cylindrical beam of light erupted from the magic circle.

“Done.”

But even though it worked, no one could predict what lay on the other side.

“Kazura’s side might be heavily guarded. Their situation is different from Tormia’s.”

Prepared for the worst, Shirone stepped forward, followed by Kido and Rian.

“Then….”

Before he could finish, a blinding light engulfed them, transporting them to the other side of the magic circle.

“……”

It was an instant, but Rian cut off his words and surveyed their surroundings.

Like the Tormia side, it was a dark underground space, but this one was a natural cave.

“No guards nearby.”

Kido looked around.

“Is this really Kazura? Hard to believe.”

“It’s probably the border region. Tormia and Kazura must share it. There’s no control device here, so they likely gather here before heading to the meeting place. Let’s go.”

The cave was a natural maze, with magical and physical traps scattered throughout.

“…We must have come a long way.”

Even without getting lost, they’d been walking for an hour straight.

“Shh, someone’s here.”

At Kido’s warning, they crouched and approached, finding a vast space 10 meters below.

Twenty people in identical robes bowed to a man on an altar, pleading.

“Great Teraphos, guide our path through your oracle!”

‘Heretics….’

And not just any heretics—it was the cult of Teraphos, known as the Evil God.

“The god has granted me an oracle.”

The elderly man on the altar raised his hands, rolled back his eyes, and trembled.

“Karomaropa, erodaimer, udiraknaom.”

‘An oracle? Shirone could decipher it.’

As Rian turned his head, Shirone frowned.

“What’s wrong? Is it serious?”

“That’s not an oracle. He’s just spouting nonsense.”

“Nonsense?”

The Ultima System was translating the man’s words.

“Ah, so boring. That newbie looks good. Tonight, I’ll have her. These thoughts have nothing to do with oracles.”

A fraud among heretics.
