Chapter 1251:

Arius muttered.

“Tachyon, you said?”

The darkness blooming inside the Miracle Stream was a signal of nothingness that should not exist in this world.

Shirone's eyes widened as the ominous light of the tachyon expanded like a sun.

As negative and positive energy annihilated each other, the black hole began to evaporate.

“O-Oh!”

Through the tearing rift of the darkness, Arius recognized the familiar landscape beyond.

‘Ymir’s primal psyche.’

As the fractures of light multiplied exponentially, gravity rapidly weakened.

Zzt, zzt.

Noise crackled from the Ultima in Arius’s hand.

“Remember this.”

As the connection to the outer world was severed again, God’s voice faded into the distance.

“Imaginary time… even so… all things… results… God’s arrangement…”

The final words vanished pitifully, and at last, the darkness of the black hole completely lifted.

“Shirone!”

On the distant surface, Miro, Gaold, Sein, and Kangnan were waiting.

Sein whispered.

“The Ultima?”

As if hearing her, Arius held out the Ultima he had extracted from the outer world.

God remained silent, and the most powerful intelligence passed over into the inner world as a single phenomenon.

On the far side of the universe, across the five planets bound by the Number of the Law—5—Ymir came to realization.

‘Heh heh heh…’

His sense of a body—so vast it could not even be felt—began to return.

‘It’s happening.’

His limbs, hands, and feet became clear, muscles began to contract.

‘It’s been far too long.’

The five stellar bodies of the Five Stars, once compressed into a speck smaller than a body, began to distort rapidly.

‘Just wait. I’ll crush everything in my path.’

The rhythm of power he had been gathering for so long finally reached his brain.

“Raaaaaaah!”

With a roar spreading at near-lightspeed, the Number of the Law—5—was destroyed.

Ymir’s Deep Layer, Phase 1.

Shirone felt a wave of simultaneous events rushing in all at once—events that had been blocked until now.

‘So that’s how it was…’

So many things had happened.

Since “Zhuangzi’s Butterfly” had been activated, the link with other simultaneous events had been severed, because the information from the outer world that Shirone had accepted was too alien.

But now the barrier had been broken, and even the Shirones from other simultaneous events had come to understand tachyon.

Rumble, rumble...

Ymir’s Deep Layer shook as if hit by an earthquake, and Arius cried out:

“This is bad! Ymir is breaking free!”

Shirone felt it too.

‘There’s no time.’

If the King of Giants returned to Shirone’s planet, nothing would remain.

“Let’s head down first.”

When Shirone and Arius landed where Gaold and the others were, Miro approached.

“The Ultima?”

Arius, despite having blasphemed God in the outer world, was nothing more than a mouse in front of Miro.

“H-Here…”

As she watched the pulsating blue energy, Miro finally smiled.

“I told you, right? You just had to do it.”

“Haha, yeah…”

Overcome with emotion, Arius lifted his head—but suddenly blood gushed from his mouth.

“Urgh!”

“Arius!”

Since they had traveled the outer world together, Shirone was the first to catch him.

Miro asked,

“What happened?”

Arius’s face rapidly turned pale, and his breathing weakened.

Shirone answered with a pained expression.

“To extract the Ultima from the outer world… he connected. With a human body, to a god’s intelligence…”

There were no precise words to explain it, but Miro nodded as if she understood.

“I see.”

“Ghk!”

Arius vomited more blood.

“I’m okay.”

Though he sensed his end was near, a peaceful smile formed on his lips.

“Here, take this.”

Arius held out the Ultima to Miro.

Only Shirone, among those present, understood what he had truly achieved.

‘It was greater than risking his life.’

He had understood the void of the outer world, and still, for his heart—for Miro—he fought to the very end.

“So what? Why should I take this?”

Looking down at the dying Arius, Miro spoke coldly.

“You’re not really saying you’re dying from just that, right? That’s just running away. Stop talking nonsense and get up.”

“Hehehe…”

Arius burst into laughter.

‘I’m a lucky guy.’

How many people in history had been born human and pierced into the mind of a god?

‘Still, the greatest moment in my career was…’

Seeing Miro’s mind.

‘Don’t worry. I won’t regret it or be afraid. You think I don’t know you?’

With one final spasm, blood clotted and spilled from his mouth.

“I’m sorry.”

He wished he could’ve stayed a little longer by her side, upholding the dignity she pretended to maintain.

“Now… I really have to die.”

Miro’s nose scrunched slightly.

“Really?”

She could not allow herself to be sad.

No matter the situation, she had to maintain absolute composure. There was no blind spot for the Thousand-Armed Avalokiteśvara.

She struck Arius’s arm, still holding out the Ultima, then knelt beside him.

Burying his face in her chest, she whispered softly.

“You’ve done well.”

Arius’s blood-slicked lips curved into a grin.

‘Isn’t it strange?’

Arius, the grave robber.

A man infamous enough to be listed among the world’s top 100 most dangerous individuals, so far from justice.

‘How… did I end up here?’

He’d never thought about saving humanity or living virtuously from now on.

‘It was just… fun.’

Good or evil—it wasn’t something worth worrying about for someone simply trying to live.

‘Still…’

He was glad to be able to rest in a sanctuary of good.

‘What will I become after I die? Will that world really be waiting for me?’

He pictured himself learning it had all just been a dream and laughing in defeat.

“Heh heh…”

Screw it.

‘Even if I live twice… no, even if I reincarnate hundreds of times… I will always serve Miro.’

‘Got that?’

Arius’s head slumped to the side, and only then did Miro take the Ultima in her hands.

“So this is…”

“Hand it over.”

Everyone turned their heads at Ymir’s voice.

“That belongs to me.”

Many rebuttals came to mind, but none made it past their lips.

“Madman.”

The reason was clear—the demonic grin on Ymir’s face and the overwhelming aura radiating from him.

Miro handed the Ultima to Shirone.

“Go. I’ll hold him off.”

“But—”

“That’s our only hope. I’ll stop Ymir. You go make it into an object.”

If they entered Drimo and used Luber’s authority, the Ultima could be perfectly objectified.

‘Can they hold him off?’

Miro and Gaold—both masters in Shirone’s eyes—but Ymir, now threatened at the source of his power, was on another level.

“I propose a deal.”

Despite his words, Shirone and the others felt sick to their stomachs.

“My physical body is about to regain freedom. When that happens, none of you will survive. Someone worse than me will come.”

The sovereign of minds—Super Ego.

Like before, Shirone assumed Ymir’s Super Ego was in a weakened state due to restraints.

‘But now it’s different. If Ymir regains power…’

Sein asked,

“What are you trying to say?”

“If you leave the Ultima here, I’ll let you go peacefully. Let’s settle this outside.”

“Hah, how can we trust that—”

Sein stopped mid-sentence.

‘We have no choice but to believe it.’

If the strongest ego in the universe had to stoop to such pitiful lies, that would be even sadder.

“Decide. You have 30 seconds.”

RUMBLE—

The Deep Layer shook violently, and a massive hand tore through the sky’s veil.

KRAAAAH!

As it spread both arms wide, a giant loomed so vast it took their breath away.

‘Ymir’s Super Ego.’

“20 seconds.”

Ymir’s countdown made everyone glance at one another.

They didn’t need words. They could read each other’s thoughts.

“Run!”

At Sein’s shout, the entire group bolted without looking back.

Sein felt no shame.

‘This is right. Why fight a monster like that when there’s nothing to gain? Once the Ultima is extracted…’

Ymir’s consciousness would shatter into ten billion pieces.

“It’s over. We’ve won.”

“10 seconds.”

Ymir’s eyes thinned to slits as the Super Ego raised its fists toward the sky.

No matter where they fled, a single blow would kill them all.

‘There’s no way to dodge—’

“Five Star!”

Luber and Monga were waiting with a portal into Drimo.

Holding out the Ultima, Shirone shouted,

“Hurry! There’s no time! If we don’t convert it into an object—!”

“5 seconds.”

RAAAAAAGH!

The Super Ego’s shriek numbed their eardrums. Luber pointed at the gate and shouted something—

But…

No one could hear him.

At the top of the Ivory Tower, where the sky could be seen—

"Graaaaah!"

Argones let out a more violent roar than ever before, his expression twisted with rage.

"So, in the end, you've trespassed?"

By making contact with the Outer World, Shirone had triggered Argones’ authority to bring about the end.

"I will annihilate humanity."

Just as Argones was about to activate the Cell Buster, Great Star struggled to open her mouth.

"Wait."

"Wait? What are you saying? No matter how much you love humanity, they've already crossed the line."

"That's not true. I can persuade him. Shirone, answer me. Do you truly want to keep fighting to the end?"

Shirone, still within the Ivory Tower, now understood the truth about the Illuminati.

"I only want to protect my world."

"I see."

Great Star turned to Argones.

"Nothing has happened yet. Give us a chance. You can, can’t you?"

Then she turned to Xing.

"Please, stop. Do you really want the Cell Buster to activate? Is forcing your belief more important than the survival of all humanity? Trust me. As humanity’s representative, I will protect you."

Xing's face twisted in turmoil.

"Grrr…"

Even someone with the strongest personal conviction in the world couldn’t find her footing in this moment.

‘What am I supposed to do?’

"Xing."

Shirone spoke.

"I’ve obtained Ultima."

"...What?"

"In the Outer World. At the cost of someone's sacrifice. Humanity can now be unified. We don’t have to fight alone anymore."

Was it true?

"Maybe, as you said, everyone but me is an illusion. But can’t you believe in me? Let me take the lead."

"Ugh…"

Xing’s face turned sorrowful.

‘He’s incredible.’

Shirone wouldn’t know this, but faced with a question that held humanity’s survival in the balance, even Son Yujeong had turned gray within ten minutes.

"Unity…"

Xing whispered with a hoarse voice.

"You’re saying we can live together? Without suspicion or fear of one another anymore…?"

Shirone looked at her with eyes full of desperate hope.

"Yes."

Xing didn’t move for a long while, but everyone in the Ivory Tower waited patiently.

"I understand."

And finally… Xing’s arm, which had been restraining Great Star, slowly began to lower.

"I’ll believe in you."

Her conviction was broken.

But her face looked peaceful, and she smiled—a smile no one had ever seen from her before.

‘So she can make a face like that too.’

It may not mean much now, but with this, the Stars of the Ivory Tower had become one again.

Shirone held out his hand.

"Let’s go. I’ll need your help for what’s to come."

"Hmph."

Just as Xing reached to take his hand, Shirone’s eyes suddenly widened, and his body trembled.

"Gah!"

"Sh-Shirone?"

Even as she saw it happen, Xing couldn’t move.

The eyes of the woman who had stabbed Shirone through the heart from behind were far too cold.

"How arrogant you are, human."

It was Great Star.
