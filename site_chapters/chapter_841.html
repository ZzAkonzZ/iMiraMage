Chapter 841: Participation (1)

Uorin left the imperial city of Aganos, wrapped in a shabby robe.

There were no attendants to serve her, only Kido, who was there to protect her life, followed beside her.

A distant explosion was heard.

“Did the negotiations break down?”

Kido removed his hood and looked over the sky towards the main gate of Aganos.

Uorin did not stop walking.

“They probably didn't even get a chance to try. To be precise, Habitz is doing whatever he wants.”

Habitz's chaos is the pinnacle of unpredictability, rampaging freely outside the world's laws.

Kido followed Uorin and asked.

“If it's impossible to predict Habitz, then what is a predetermined future?”

“The law is a collection of ideas. Outside the law is chaos. Ideas and chaos intertwine to produce specific results. Therefore, even Habitz has a predetermined future, but…”

“But the way he reaches that future cannot be calculated by the law.”

Uorin nodded.

“The world has already lost control. The wheel of good and evil is spinning rapidly around humans. The end of the world is not far off.”

“But why are you…”

Kido stopped speaking and closed his mouth.

‘The world is ending.’

In a world of destruction where there is nothing more to gain, what does Uorin want to leave behind?

A woman's voice was heard from the sky.

“Your Majesty.”

A figure wearing a white owl mask circled in the air and landed in front of them.

When the mask was removed, a woman's face with a pale complexion and thick eyelids was revealed.

It was Yula, the leader of the Wind Guards.

“How did it go?”

“We are blocked on all sides. Over ten million of the army of hell have completely sealed off the capital.”

Currently, the 99 members of the Wind Guards, except for her, were searching for a way out around the capital.

“Is that so…”

As a shadow fell over Uorin's face, Kido tried to act strong.

“It doesn't matter. We don't have to face all ten million. Let's break through the weakest point.”

“That's already a meaningless number. There's no place with a gap…”

Uorin interrupted.

“Find it. We will break through there.”

“Understood.”

Yula immediately flew into the sky.

“Let's go too. We need to get as far away from the imperial city as possible. We have no choice but to blend into the crowd.”

As they left the alley, demons were brutally ravaging the citizens of Kashan.

‘This is hell.’

Demons with black wings filled the sky, blocking out even the sun.

“Let's go.”

Kido, who had hidden his spear in his robe, led Uorin.

‘It's fortunate that there are many humans in the capital.’ The demons' actions were horrific, but if they were killing machines, they wouldn't have had time to escape.

“Please save us! Please…!”

There was no one to help them.

But for Uorin, who once ruled Kashan, those desperate cries pierced her heart.

“Don't think about anything. I'll do the thinking, you just focus on walking.”

Kido's movements, as he moved out of the demons' sight, were also stiff with tension.

“Hahaha! This is mine!”

A two-meter-tall demon, holding a blood-stained axe, charged at them.

“Run!”

They started running, but no matter where they went, it was full of demons.

“They're running! They're running!”

The demons, who had spotted the only lively prey among the resigned ones, pounced all at once.

“Damn it!”

Kido stopped running, flapping his robe and pulling out a hidden double-edged spear.

“Stay still!”

Kido shouted to Uorin and rolled on the ground, circling at an incredible speed.

The demons were instantly chopped into pieces.

“What, what is this?”

Realizing they were not ordinary fugitives, the demons collectively charged, narrowing the encirclement.

“Grrr!”

Like a machine grinding, the demons' flesh splattered mercilessly outside Kido's radius.

‘There's no end.’

Even a machine would have its blades dulled by this number.

“Kido!”

At Uorin's voice, Kido spotted the rear through the rotating afterimages.

Three demons had broken through Kido's blade hell and were attacking Uorin.

“Damn it!”

Sliding forward, he sliced their legs, and the demon holding Uorin's clothes screamed.

“Argh!”

As her clothes were torn off, revealing Uorin's face, the demons' movements stopped.

“The Empress, it's the Empress!” The sacrifice Satan so desired.

“Contact the main force!”

As the winged demons kicked off the ground and flew into the sky, Kido's eyes widened.

“Yaaah!”

As the law of the earthbound spirit activated to the extreme, dozens of demons were pulled to the ground.

“Ugh! This, this is…!”

Kido, who had pulled the demons with gravity, spun his double-edged spear at a terrifying speed.

Vroom!

The afterimage of the blade revealed a spherical shape.

“Argh!”

Like moths to a flame, the demons' bodies were ground up with a thud as they collided with the sphere of blades.

“Hah! Hah!”

When he stopped spinning and looked around, the nearby demons were annihilated.

Kido checked on Uorin.

“Are you okay?”

“Yes. But they'll come again.” Kido said, taking off his robe.

“Wear my clothes for now. If your face is known…”

“Your Majesty.”

Citizens of Kashan, who had been hiding in the streets, approached with blank expressions.

“Is it really… Your Majesty?”

Uorin, who had been looking at them with a pale face, bit her lip and averted her gaze.

The citizens were shocked, having heard rumors that Uorin was being handed over to Gustav to save Kashan.

Kido narrowed his eyes and looked around.

‘They can't bring themselves to speak directly, but their expressions say it all.’

They seemed to be asking with their expressions.

Where are you going?

Dressed in shabby clothes without even an attendant, where are you going? The answer popped into their heads.

‘You're running away.’

Sensing the gravity of the situation, Kido blocked Uorin's path and shouted.

“Move aside! Anyone who blocks the way will be killed!”

Having witnessed Kido's prowess in tearing apart demons, the residents hesitated and stepped back.

Yet they couldn't run away, driven by the anger boiling deep within their hearts.

“Hahaha! They're gathered over there! Have fun!”

A group of demons with sinister eyes was approaching from the opposite block.

“Follow me! Quickly!”

Kido grabbed Uorin's wrist and walked towards the people blocking their way.

‘I have no choice but to kill them.’

Just as he was about to swing his double-edged spear to cut their throats, the crowd parted like a lie.

“Go. We will buy you time.”

Kido didn't understand their decision, but Uorin left without a word.

‘Because I am the Empress. Because my life is noble.’

Their judgment, seeking to find some meaning at the end of despair, was so foolish.

‘I stand above that foolishness.’ If so, use it thoroughly. If everyone has a reason to live, then I will think and act only for myself.

“Long live the Empress! Long live Kashan!” As Uorin fled, the voices of the citizens being trampled by demons were heard behind her.

‘Habitz.’

Uorin gritted her teeth.

‘Habitz!’ All of Teraje's legacy was being destroyed.

“Argh!”

Uorin clenched her fists and screamed at the sky.

As Shirone descended through the atmosphere, the vast territory of Kashan came into view.

‘There's no time. As close as possible.’

The glass sphere, which was being restored by Material's power, finally melted away from the heat.

‘That's it!’

At that moment, Shirone, with wings of light, flew at full speed towards the imperial city of Aganos in Kashan.

‘It's immense.’

Even though there were still 20 kilometers to the capital, the sight of the army of hell completely covering the sky and ground was vividly visible.

“Commander! An aircraft is approaching!”

At the sentry's report, the eyes of Vanessa, the commander of the 3rd Division of the 7th Legion, lit up red.

Clad in heavy armor reminiscent of a rhinoceros, she dragged a two-ton iron ball connected by chains as she moved.

“Yahweh…”

The moment she confirmed Shirone's face, her decayed face twisted grotesquely.

“All troops, deploy! Eliminate the detestable Yahweh!”

“Kiaaa!”

The demons, their faces twisted with hatred and loathing, all sprang out, baring their fangs.

Shirone realized it from the shaking ground even before the horizon was visible.

‘They're coming.’

A massive force, occupying the ends of his vision, was charging with tremendous momentum.

‘Philanthropy.’

He wanted to believe there was no evil that couldn't repent.

‘A thousand times, ten thousand times, I can forgive. I can give a hundred thousand, a million chances.’

But if that blind love had twisted a child named Habitz…

‘I must teach him!’

Shirone's eyes widened as the incarnation of the Archangel soared high into the sky.

‘What he is doing wrong.’

The angel's punishment.

As the archangel finished the motion of throwing the spear, a spear of light struck the ground.

Boom!

A massive crater formed at the impact site, but tens of thousands of demons quickly reclaimed the space.

“Khehehe! It's Yahweh! The foolish Yahweh!”

As the demons' voices got close enough to hear, Shirone drew the Law Slayer.

‘I won't go berserk.’

With Yahweh's heart that loves everything.

‘So, no matter how angry I am. Even if I can only handle a fraction of this anger…’

Even just a fraction of that fraction.

“Endlessly endure, split, and split again.”

A fragment of anger smaller than dust, a single particle of that anger…

“Even just a fraction of that fraction!”

The Law Slayer, released from Shirone's hand, began to vibrate with a width of over 10 meters.

“Try to understand!”

The Law Slayer, leaving Shirone's side, let out a scream that seemed to tear the sky apart.

Kiaaa!

A demon was the first to notice it.

“What is that????”

The moment they felt something flying towards them, the sound of demons being pierced was heard from all directions.

Pop, pop, pop, pop, pop!

No one could see the trajectory of the Law Slayer.

“Argh!”

Only the belated screams of annihilation echoed endlessly like a four-dimensional space.

Vanessa gripped the handle of her mace and shouted.

“What is happening!”

Every time the black afterimage zigzagged, demons were falling.

Watching her subordinates being erased by the thousands per second, Vanessa's shoulders trembled.

‘What is that?…’

A black monster, seemingly reflecting Yahweh's wrath, was roaming the battlefield.

The trembling voices of the brigade commanders were heard.

“Yah, Yahweh…”

Vanessa's eyes grew serious.

‘We are scared.’

Of Yahweh, not Buddha.

“Don't talk nonsense! This is proof that Yahweh is no different from us!”

“Ah, ahhh!”

All the demons lined up behind Vanessa began to flee in terror.

Kiaaa!

As the Law Slayer howled for the second time, Vanessa's hearing was permanently lost.

“Grrr!”

As the blade that had swept the front flew straight towards her, Vanessa swung her mace with all her might.

“Ugh!”

But before the iron ball could even lift off the ground, the Law Slayer pierced through the center of her chest.

As Vanessa fell to her knees, she saw Shirone with wings of light.

“R-run…”

In an instant, Shirone passed by her.

“Strike.”

Vanessa, with her face buried in the ground, muttered weakly.

“Satan…”
