Chapter 642:

Everyone remained silent, yet the atmosphere grew unbearably heavy as if weighed down by their collective thoughts.

To reject a royal decree, fully aware of its implications, was nothing short of a challenge to the authority of the state. Lyman’s shoulders trembled with fury.

“You insolent fool! Do you even know what the crime of insulting royalty entails? Even execution would be too lenient!”

With Amy’s approval already secured, Shirone had nothing left to hold him back.

“I’m not being insolent, nor am I opposing the kingdom. I’m simply stating that I’ve done nothing wrong.”

“You…!”

Just as Lyman was about to explode, Pony could no longer bear it and turned away, leaving the room.

“Lady Pony!”

With the very person owed an apology walking out, Lyman was left at a loss, unable to regain control of the situation. All he could do was direct his resentment at Shirone, the one who had orchestrated this mess.

“You will pay dearly for your choice! Wait with your neck exposed!”

Though Lyman wanted nothing more than to tear Shirone apart on the spot, he couldn’t act recklessly due to the expectations of the royal palace.

When Lyman stormed off, the students swarmed around Shirone.

“Senior Shirone! Are you sure about this?”

Shirone simply turned to Amy, and her supportive smile filled him with gratitude.

Amy.

Could one even conceive of the mind without the body?

Regardless of what came next, the distance between them would inevitably grow.

In the conference room of Alpheas Magic Academy, teachers exchanged uneasy glances as Sade abruptly stood up.

“I’ll take the criticism. I believe we must pass the motion to delay Shirone’s graduation by a year.”

While it was the most pragmatic option, Siina’s sense of justice wouldn’t allow it.

“Shirone has achieved a record-breaking score in the kingdom’s history. If we revoke his graduation now, Alpheas Magic Academy will become a laughingstock in history!”

“But this is a royal decree. Ignoring it could bring serious repercussions to the academy.”

“That doesn’t mean we can sacrifice a student for political convenience! Teachers are duty-bound to protect their students!”

As Siina slammed her hand on the table, Sade raised his voice in turn.

“Shirone can still graduate next year! When a storm approaches, a ship must seek shelter. Trying to push through will only sink us all!”

Alpheas let out a long, weary sigh.

‘How did it come to this?’

Though Shirone had achieved the highest scores in the kingdom’s history, his extraordinary talent had turned into a curse.

Olivia chimed in.

“Commoners lack a strong foundation, so they’re easier to unsettle. Plus, their sense of loyalty to the state is often weaker than that of nobles. That’s what’s making the royal court uneasy. If Shirone had been a noble, this could have followed normal negotiation protocols.”

“So, we just sit back and watch?”

“What choice do we have? Rationality is shaped by the system we’re in. Once a royal decree is issued, our options are limited.”

While Alpheas technically had the authority to graduate Shirone unilaterally, the fallout would be catastrophic.

“This is giving me a headache.”

With a heavy sigh, Alpheas stepped out of the heated debate in the meeting room.

“Where’s Shirone?”

When Nade arrived at the dorm, he found only Amy and Iruki inside.

“He said he wanted some fresh air and went out.”

“You don’t think he’s planning anything rash, do you? Like leaving the school entirely?”

“He’s not that reckless. Nothing’s been decided yet. Let’s wait and see what stance the royal palace takes. They might propose a better compromise.”

As the day filled with discussions about Shirone drew to a close, Pony sat silently, her thoughts tangled, even neglecting her water.

‘I just wanted to be a mage.’

The moment she realized that the qualifications could have been hers at any time, her life seemed to lose all meaning.

Seated at her desk, Pony picked up a pen and began drafting a farewell letter, ending it with a plea for Shirone’s graduation to be approved.

Standing on a chair, she looped a rope over a beam and tied it into a noose, slipping it around her neck like a necklace.

For a moment, she questioned if this was truly the best solution.

“I’m sorry, Mother.”

Without hesitation, she stepped off the chair, her weight pressing onto her slender neck.

‘What’s happening?’

Her tightly shut eyes opened slowly.

Her heart raced as signals of death fired through her brain, and only then did she realize someone was holding her legs.

Looking down, Pony’s eyes widened.

“Shirone?”

Shirone looked up at her with a gentle smile.

“This isn’t the way, Pony.”

Was this really Shirone? For a moment, she wondered if she had already died and was seeing an illusion. But as she shifted her body, the painful reality of the rope around her neck brought her back.

“Ugh!”

Just before the noose could tighten further, a wind cutter spell severed the rope, and Pony collapsed to the ground like a broken doll.

“Are you okay?”

Propping herself up with her hands, she turned away from Shirone.

“Why are you here?”

“I was worried you might do something like this.”

“Why did you come?! I was trying to end it all. If I die, you can graduate. So why did you save me?”

Shirone didn’t answer.

“Go away. I might still have one more chance to die. If you miss this, your life will be over.”

“This isn’t your fault, Pony.”

Pony’s face hardened.

“It’s not my fault? Do you really think I’d believe such a lie? Just say it! Admit that you wish I’d die, that everything would go back to normal if I weren’t here!”

“I didn’t say I wasn’t angry.”

Shirone squatted beside her.

“I want things to work out, too. But it’s not your fault, so there’s no reason for you to die.”

The shame of showing her weakest moment, coupled with the comfort Shirone offered, made Pony feel wretched.

“What do you want from me?”

She turned her head sharply.

“What should I do? Money? Power? A title? Take it all. Take everything from me if you must, but just end this already!”

“I don’t want anything from you. What I want isn’t something anyone can give me. And I’ve already gained it—the person I am now, who can think and act like this.”

Shirone placed a comforting hand on her shoulder, and Pony’s lips quivered.

“So don’t feel guilty. I don’t need anyone to give me qualifications. I’m already a mage.”

“I….”

Finally, the words she truly wanted to say burst forth, and she threw herself into Shirone’s arms.

“I’m sorry! I’m so sorry, Shirone! I… I…!”

As Pony sobbed in his arms, Shirone patted her back and whispered.

“It’s okay. It’s not your fault.”

“I wasn’t myself. I’m so sorry!”

Being a mage wasn’t something that could simply be handed to you.

‘If I can’t accept it myself, I haven’t truly earned it.’

Just then, a staff member from the Magic Association entered the room, startled by the commotion.

“What’s going on here? How did you get in?”

Upon noticing the open window, the staff member prepared for combat.

“Damn it! What’s the outer guard even doing?!”

“Stand down.”

Pony stepped out of Shirone’s embrace and addressed the staff.

“But Lady Pony! Shirone has been strictly forbidden from entering the Association….”

“Are you seriously invoking the Association’s authority in front of me?”

“Urk!”

The staff recoiled at Pony’s newfound presence, which radiated an authority born not of training but of royal lineage.

Turning to Shirone with a gentle expression, Pony spoke.

“Shirone, you should go now.”

“But you….”

“I’m fine. I won’t falter anymore. I’ll stay at the academy and become a mage, no matter what.”

Understanding Pony’s resolve, Shirone finally smiled with relief and headed for the window.

“Good luck, Pony.”

“You too.”

As soon as the words left her lips, Shirone leapt out the window.

The staff rushed to the window in reflex, but no one could spot him—the traces of his Time Explosion vanished without a trace.

"Graduate Arian Shirone! The students cannot tolerate the kingdom's unjust treatment!"

Members of the emergency committee, formed around the student council, gathered at the teachers' building to protest.

Even Pony, the person involved in this issue, stood with the committee, giving it significant impact, and the teachers seemed to subtly align with the students' opinions.

"We understand your sentiments. We are currently preparing the necessary documents…"

As Olivia, the auditor of the teachers' council, came out with the teachers to convey the school's position, Lyman arrived with soldiers.

"What is this commotion?"

"Please allow Arian Shirone to graduate!"

"This is a royal decree! Do you all want to be arrested for treason?"

As some students flinched, Pony stepped forward.

"If you want to arrest someone, start with me."

"Miss Pony! Why are you doing this?"

Lyman couldn't understand. If she had just kept quiet, Shirone's graduation would have been canceled, and she might have secured a position in Tio.

What I want cannot be given by others.

Recalling Shirone's words, Pony crossed her arms.

"Step back. I will demand a special investigation system in the royal castle using the royal family's authority. I will thoroughly reveal who is involved in this matter and how much slush fund has been exchanged."

"Wow!"

The students cheered in support of Pony's declaration.

'Still young.'

As the blade of judgment tightened around his neck, Lyman became calmer.

"Demand whatever you want. However, Shirone's graduation will be postponed until the trial is over."

At this point, the only option was to drag it into a mud fight and buy time. If Shirone couldn't graduate, the backlash would eventually subside.

"Miss Pony. Regardless, the royal decree is still valid. If you don't disperse the students, we will have to arrest everyone."

Since it wasn't just a threat, Olivia stepped forward.

"Everyone, please return to…"

"Excuse me."

Hearing a voice from behind the teachers, they turned their heads, but the owner of the voice was already walking towards the committee.

'An incredible master.'

Even Olivia, a certified second-class mage, couldn't sense his movement.

The man had a kind appearance matching his pure white robe, but his eyes were sharp, and his long black hair was tied up on top of his head.

With his left hand behind his back, he lightly waved a small fan with a piece of paper attached to it and spoke.

"Um… So, what was the name again? It was something like Shi…"

"Shirone. How many times do I have to tell you?"

From inside the man's robe, a small creature with a tiny frame like a newborn appeared. Its skin was crimson red, its eyes were black like a panda's, and it had a long tail like a spear.

"Is there an Arian Shirone here?"

For a moment, they tilted their heads at the strange speech, but the name Shirone snapped them back to reality.

"I am Arian Shirone. Who are you?"

The man, who had been staring at the approaching Shirone, hurriedly fumbled through his belongings.

"Uh, well, I am… Where did I put it?"

The crimson-skinned creature sighed and handed over a badge with three overlapping pentagrams.

'A star. And a three-star one at that.'

Everyone who knew the significance of the three stars trembled in shock.

"We are from the Balance Department of the Ivory Tower. We have a message for Arian Shirone."

"The Ivory Tower?"

As the students murmured, the man with the fan extended his hand to Shirone.

"Nice to meet you. I am Arte."

Arte, a three-star resident of the Ivory Tower, known as the Ghost Goblin.

"And I am Satellite Tokey."

Tokey, grinning like a mischievous child, placed a badge with an orbital path drawn on it next to Arte's badge.
