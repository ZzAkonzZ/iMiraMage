Chapter 534:

Woodga didn’t dwell on the name and simply pointed ahead.

“Now we have to go collect from the Tree of Life. If you want to keep living here, you’d better do what I say.”

When the boy didn’t respond, Woodga clicked his tongue.

“Well, once you receive Ra’s Blessing, you’ll start to understand. Anyway, let’s go. We don’t have time.”

The boy looked around the ruined world with a troubled expression.

Even so… for the world to end up like this.

From what fragmented memories he could recall, no matter how massive the catastrophe had been before stasis, the world hadn’t looked this devastated.

No use getting anxious. The memories will come back with time.

As they got closer to the city center, Woodga’s expression grew increasingly grim until he finally came to a halt, his face pale.

“Brother… it’s the Contaminated Zone.”

Skyscrapers—dozens of stories tall—still stood in eerie contrast to the crumbled surroundings, but even more striking was what covered everything: a slimy, mucus-like substance coated the buildings and even the ground.

Overall, it shimmered a reddish hue, but the colors shifted like bile—sickly and foul.

“Mucus…?”

“Yeah, mucus. If you touch it—huh?”

Woodga paused, realizing that wasn’t Hamei’s voice. He turned back to the boy.

“You… you know what mucus is?”

The boy didn’t answer but frowned deeply.

He didn’t remember seeing anything like this, yet the reddish slime felt strangely familiar.

—The fallout from the nuclear war depleted global resources…

—We’re maximizing biomass efficiency.

—Eliminate the mucus! It’s the root of ecological collapse!

Fragments of memories flashed through his mind.

“Ghh…!”

The boy clutched his head again, pained, but the children had no time to coddle him now.

“Let’s go. I’ll go first—just follow me.”

Woodga walked along a narrow, winding path not yet overtaken by mucus.

This is bad. The path’s gotten even narrower than before.

No one knew the true origin of the mucus—how many sources it had, or even if it had a source—but one thing was certain: it was expanding.

Its average spread rate was just one centimeter per hour.

Extremely slow, and yet more than half the world was now classified as Contaminated Zone.

“Brother, look—up there!”

In the sky, five Razettes—floating fish—were spewing jetstreams in panic, trying to escape a pursuing predator.

Just as the carnivorous beast caught up and swooped in, a mucus strand stretched like a frog’s tongue and latched onto its leg.

KIEEEEEEK!

The beast screeched as it was violently reeled into the wall, its body twisting grotesquely as it was engulfed by the mucus.

They were far away, but just watching the creature twist and writhe unnaturally made it feel like they could hear its bones shattering.

It was clear why most life in Apocalypse had abandoned the ground for the skies.

“Brother… what if we…”

“No. The mucus doesn’t react to slow-moving creatures. So just walk slowly. Got it?”

Though his voice was firm, even Woodga’s face was tense.

The path was about a meter wide—plenty of room—but it felt like crossing a tightrope.

“We’re clear. From here on, it’s safe.”

They exited the Contaminated Zone and followed an old road for about an hour before finally reaching the edge of a lush forest.

The greenery—seen for the first time since waking—moved something deep in the boy’s heart.

“In that forest is the Tree of Life. From here on, you really need to do as I say.”

At the forest’s edge, Woodga looked around cautiously.

“Hamei, do you hear anything?”

“Yeah… a low vibrating hum.”

“That’s Ra’s warning. Only Children of the Sun can hear it. When you hear that sound, never enter the forest.”

“What happens if we do?”

“I’ve never tried it myself, but the older kids say it gives you a splitting headache.”

Their conversation faded into the background as the boy stared at the lush forest in awe, his body trembling slightly.

A forest… it’s a forest.

Not a memory from before cryostasis, but something else—some other recollection stirred faintly in his mind.

Why are my memories so jumbled?

He wondered if it was a dream he’d had during stasis—but at minus 273 degrees Celsius, that was impossible.

“Brother, the sound stopped.”

“Good. Then let’s go in.”

Inside the forest, not a single creature stirred—no birds, no bugs—only plants.

That alone was eerie, but the bizarre scene ahead crushed all sense of normalcy.

“This is…”

At the center of a field stood a colossal tree.

But could it even be called a tree?

It pulsed rhythmically, like it had a heartbeat. Though brown, the trunk and branches were woven like muscle fibers, not bark.

But what shocked the boy the most were the “fruits” growing from it.

They weren’t fruits at all—they were fetuses.

All at different stages of development, the tree bore human offspring.

This was how the Children of the Sun were born.

“Remember, Hamei? You were born there too. The older boys collected you.”

Hamei just stared blankly at the Tree of Life.

“The tree only opens twice a day—right after Ra’s warning stops. The others say the energy it emits wards off other life, even the mucus can’t come near.”

Each fetus was tethered to the tree by a stem attached to its crown. Once the fetus matured, the stem would extend to near ground level.

Woodga crouched near a particularly chubby infant whose stem was almost touching the earth.

“Alright, it’s close enough. Time to harvest.”

Leaving a hand’s width of the stem, he snipped it with scissors and cradled the newborn as it began to cry.

“Waaah… waaah.”

“Haha! What a cute little guy. Hamei, here—he’s yours. This harvest is your task.”

Hamei gently wrapped the baby in a cloth and held it in her arms.

A child born from sunlight—one of the Children of the Sun.

“Hehe… he’s really cute.”

This is insane.

The boy could hardly believe what he was seeing.

Even among bizarre creatures, humans growing from trees defied everything he understood. It was impossible by any natural evolution.

How does this even work…?

As he stepped toward the tree, a chilling sensation crawled up his spine.

A low vibrating hum—like the twang of a thick string.

A magnetic field…?

His senses suddenly sharpened, and even things outside his field of vision became vivid.

He felt like he could count every fetus hanging from the tree if he focused.

“Hey… don’t you hear something?” the boy asked.

Woodga frowned. Something was definitely off about this ancient one.

“Step back from the Tree of Life. And stop acting so weird. You’re coming with us either way.”

He waited until Hamei wrapped the baby securely, then turned.

“Let’s go. We have to leave before Ra’s warning comes back.”

Reentering the forest, the boy felt the flow of information overwhelm him—but even that didn’t seem like enough.

What’s happening to me? Why is this all happening?

His senses grew ever sharper, expanding outward until everything within fifty meters could be felt through synesthesia.

Heh heh heh… so they really came this way.

Seven Underfolk—lying in wait—spotted Woodga and the others and silently loaded their rifles.

They didn’t have the hearing organs needed to detect Ra’s warning, but they did know the Children of the Sun would be retrieving fetuses here.

If they scatter, it’ll be a hassle. Take them all out at once.

The leader signaled with his eyes, and the Underfolk hiding in the trees nodded.

“Now!”

As the target entered range, the leader grabbed a branch and swung down like a monkey.

But Woodga and Hamei had already been shoved into the underbrush by the boy.

“…What the—?”

How did they know?

Did they sense our ambush? But if they did, why take this path at all?

“Boss, what do we do!?”

The leader’s face twisted in frustration.

“Open fire!”

“Kiyaaaaaaaah!”

The Underfolk shrieked like monkeys as they swung from branches and fired their rifles.

With bullets raining from every direction, Woodga fell into the bushes, screaming.

“Uwaaah! Help me!”

“Stay calm! We need to get out of here!”

The boy helped Woodga and Hamei to their feet and led the way, sprinting ahead with movements far too confident for someone who’d never been here before.

What the—? He’s completely changed!

Despite the Underfolk ambush, the boy hadn’t lost his cool. The composure he showed reminded Woodga of the Consul—the ruler of their enclave.

“No, he’s just a slave! Hamei! Let’s run too!”

The Children of the Sun were fast, no slower than the boy, but the real problem was the Underfolk—their physical capabilities far surpassed theirs.

“Yoo-hoo! Found ya!”

An Underfolk hanging from a tree branch pulled the trigger, but the boy twisted his body and ducked behind the trunk.

Tat tat tat tat tat!

The tree trunk shook with the impact of bullets, vibrations crawling up the boy’s back. Still, his eyes scanned his surroundings, sharp and calculating.

The enemy’s movements were too swift and erratic to get a precise headcount.

Don’t let fear take over. The only way to survive is to stay aware until the very end.

You’re not dead until you’re dead.

Bracing himself like stepping off a cliff, the boy sharpened his focus, and his senses became even clearer.

Seven enemies with superior physical abilities. Ranged weapons powerful enough to shatter tree trunks. Escape won’t be easy.

“Brother! Help me!”

He turned toward the source of Hamei’s scream—three Underfolk were closing in on her.

Woodga stepped between them, but there was no way he could withstand bullets with nothing but his body.

What do I do?

Papapapapow!

Splinters flew from the tree as a bullet hit near the boy’s head. He quickly pulled back.

“Damn it!”

The Underfolk weren’t even bothering with careful aim—they were burning through entire magazines without hesitation.

So inefficient…

It was clear: the Underfolk weren’t cold, calculating soldiers—they were emotionally driven warriors.

There’s no way out of this…

The gunfire that should’ve continued suddenly stopped. The boy turned his gaze toward Hamei.

The Underfolk were keeping their distance, aiming their guns but not firing.

Why aren’t they shooting?

The Underfolk hunted for food—nothing more. If the prey was helpless, it made more sense not to damage the meat.

“Kekeke. One shot to the head, coming up.”

“Wait! I like heads! Just shoot her in the heart! Hearts are too tough to eat anyway.”

“Are you crazy? Do you know how tasty the organs in that area are? Just blow the head off!”

“I’m starving! I’m doing it!”

Unable to control his hunger, one Underfolk raised his rifle.

No!

The moment the boy thought only of saving them, his body exploded into a flash of light and shot forward.

By the time he regained awareness, he was already standing in front of Woodga and Hamei.

“Wh-What the hell?! Shoot him!”

Panicked, the Underfolk fired their rifles, but the boy’s eyes snapped open wide.

BOOM!

A veil of light erupted with a thunderous pulse. The bullets twisted violently off-course, thudding into the trees behind them.

The deafening harmony of the blast drowned out the infant’s cries. When the magazine was finally empty, a ringing silence fell over the forest.

“Wh-What the hell? The bullets…?”

The boy stood frozen, staring at the ground as new memories echoed through his mind.

—Spirit Zone. A state of hyper-sensitive consciousness.

—The God Particle. Mass is energy.

—Clash your thoughts. That’s what we call ‘Force.’

“Ro… Roshine?”

At Woodga’s voice, the boy snapped back to reality.

“…I’m not Roshine.”

Then he turned and walked toward the stunned Underfolk, his voice calm and resolute.

“My name is Shirone. Arian Shirone.”

Shirone Information Restoration Rate: 48%.
