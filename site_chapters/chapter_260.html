Chapter 260: The Revealed Truth (5)

She was the principal of the Royal Magic Academy 10 years ago, but now she's the vice principal of a rural academy.

She’s not the type to lie in front of a reporter, so it must be true.

But why? Why would she make a decision that would shatter a career built through years of constant success in an instant?

What on earth is in this academy?

Alpheas also had doubts.

Although they had recently let go of their old animosity and grew closer, she was still reluctant to take on the role of vice principal.

“Olivia, you...”

“Just stay still. We can talk about that later.”

As Olivia looked at him as if frustrated, Alpheas quickly shut his mouth.

Meanwhile, Killain's eyes were darting around. Profit and loss calculations were racing through his mind.

A trivial misunderstanding had brought about this situation. No, could this even be called a misunderstanding?

It's just Olivia's whim.

But if a second-rank archmage has a whim, you have no choice but to accommodate them.

Killain hastily knelt and prostrated herself on the floor.

“I’m sorry! I overstepped and made a mistake. I should have informed you in advance, but I didn’t realize that Olivia was thinking that way….”

There was no other option but to beg.

When she could crush someone logically, Olivia became the most terrifying entity in the world.

Hundreds of her disciples were seated in leadership positions in various fields of society. With a mere word, she could ensure that one wouldn’t even set foot in Teachers’ Association, not to mention possibly being permanently ousted from society.

“I will immediately issue a correction article. Please, just give me one more chance! I will do everything I can to clean up this situation.”

Olivia let out a long sigh.

If it were that simple, she wouldn’t have created such an atmosphere from the start.

The magazine had already spread across the world, and it wasn’t possible to retrieve them all. Even if she could, she couldn’t erase the memories from people’s minds.

“Releasing a correction article will only make the situation bigger. Don’t create unnecessary issues.”

“Then what should I do?”

Killain raised her head, now crying.

The confident woman who had entered with her head held high was now reduced to the greenhorn reporter she had been when she first joined Teachers’ Association within just ten minutes.

“For the next two months, publish a series of special articles. Distract the public with sensational stories like corruption in academies, student harassment by teachers, and organ trafficking in the magic society. I’ll give you the sources. And Shirone and Dante’s names must never appear in the magazine.”

“Yes, yes, I will do that.”

“And... apologize to Shirone.”

Still on his knees, Killain turned toward Shirone.

Shirone’s face openly showed his complex emotions.

He was undoubtedly angry, but seeing Killain fight to defend his livehood and then abandon his pride was pitiful.

Perhaps it was because she was an adult. An adult’s life could end with just one mistake.

“I’m sorry. I’ll offer whatever compensation I can, so please, just give me one more chance...”

Shirone didn't want to hear it.

He wanted to shout that none of this should have happened in the first place, but even that would be pointless.

Seeing someone 15 years older than him crawling on their knees, head pressed to the ground, was so uncomfortable he could barely look.

“It’s fine. Just leave. It’s already in the past. You can’t undo it. But if you get the chance, I’d appreciate it if you apologized to my parents.”

“Yes, I’ll visit them right away. I’m truly sorry.”

Olivia gave a bitter smile.

Any student would be similar, but Shirone was especially honest. If he had been a little more shrewd, he wouldn’t have dismissed Killain so easily.

'Well, he's a child who is far removed from things like desire for fame or material wealth.'

Those who have mastered Immortal Function tend to have a metaphysical mindset. They have an extreme lack of desire for worldly things, a pursuit of ideals, and a deep interest in worlds beyond imagination.

Regardless of Shirone's disposition, Olivia did not want to cast Killain aside.

Even in a monarchy, the power of the press cannot be ignored, and she was smart enough to recognize that.

Moreover, Killain was well-regarded in the world of reporters, so keeping her close was much more beneficial than raising a new one.

"Killain, you're a competent child. Make sure this kind of mistake never happens again. Once this is over, let's have a meal together."

"Thank you! I promise this will never happen again!"

"Alright, you may go."

Killain bowed repeatedly as she retreated.

With this, Alpheas and Shirone didn't have much to say.

They had chosen the best solution in the current situation, and since Killain had already experienced a taste of hell, they felt somewhat comforted.

Olivia looked at Shirone and spoke.

“I’m sorry. It was my fault. Of course, strictly speaking, it was the old man’s fault.”

Alpheas, feeling embarrassed, covered his mouth and cleared his throat.

Shirone forced a smile.

Although not all of his feelings had been resolved, the presence of Olivia and Alpheas was a reassuring support for someone like him, who had nowhere else to rely on.

Alpheas comforted Sirone.

"This too shall pass soon. The final exams are coming up, so don’t think about anything else and focus on your studies. In time, everything will return to normal."

"Yes, thank you. I'll be going now."

Shirone sincerely expressed his gratitude and left the principal's office.

Although all the main characters of this incident had disappeared, tension still hung in the principal's office.

Olivia closed her eyes, lost in thought. Then she looked at Alpheas and asked,

“Do you really think so?”

Alpheas had no answer.

If it were a moderately serious matter, time would heal it. But Shirone's case was different.

What they were thinking about wasn't Shirone's humble origins or him being unfit to attend magic academy.

"We can only watch over him. Perhaps... nothing will happen at all."

"Perhaps. But..."

Olivia stopped mid-sentence.

As Alpheas had said, no matter how much noise was made now, nothing would change. All he could do was hope that the terrifying bomb hidden beneath the surface of this incident would not come to light.

@

The final exams were over.

Last semester, vacation began immediately after the exams, but there was one more thing scheduled for the second half of the semester.

It was the graduation exam for the graduating class.

As it was the biggest festival of the academy, where all students participated, the atmosphere in the academy was lively.

Shirone's final exam results placed him sixth in Class Four.

Dante came in first, followed by Savina, Closer, Boyle, and Pandora in that order.

Below Shirone were Iruki and Nade.

Shirone, who had drastically improved in omniscience, which had been his biggest weakness, over the past year, was satisfied. On the other hand, Iruki and Nade were in shock, as they had fallen behind in the theory exams and seemed dazed for a while.

When combining the practical exam scores with the theory exam, Shirone ranked third overall.

Dante was first overall, followed by Iruki.

In any case, since Dante, who took first place overall, was defeated by Shirone in Heaven’s Trial, there were many students who sided with Shirone among the top students expected to graduate by a narrow margin.

However, his reputation wasn't as strong as it used to be.

Due to his background being revealed, some of the students who used to follow Shirone began distancing themselves.

Some even openly hurled derogatory remarks at him.

Each time that happened, Shirone felt hurt but didn’t show it.

Killain kept her promise and no longer published any articles in Spirit magazine.

According to Olivia, Killain had gone to Shirone’s parents and kneeled to beg for forgiveness.

Shirone thought that, in life, there are bound to be days when one falls into a pit, so he saw this as just another passing storm.

Maybe that could have been the case.

However, the article about Shirone, published just once in Spirit magazine, returned with an enormous backlash that exceeded everyone’s expectations.

Fifteen days before the graduation exam, a commotion broke out in the academy.

Clank! Clank!

Forty fully armed knights passed through the noble district of Thormia, escorting a single carriage.

The carriage, adorned with gold crowns, was so large that one could eat, sleep, and even relieve oneself inside.

Even the most prominent nobles didn't dare frown at the marching military forces occupying the road.

So it was only natural that the guards at the academy’s main gate were utterly terrified.

When the carriage stopped at the main gate, the academy’s security system fell into complete disarray.

The head of security came running in a panic, and after reading the document handed to him by the lead knight, he turned even paler than when he had rushed over and sprinted toward the principal's office.

“Principal! We’re in trouble!”

As soon as the head of security knocked, he opened the door of the principal's office and entered.

Olivia’s expression soured, as she disliked people with no manners.

However, seeing the head of security, who was nearly in tears at almost fifty years old, she decided to set her feelings aside for the moment.

“What's going on?”

“Uh, uh… Someone came looking… No, look at this!”

Instead of explaining, the head of security placed the document on the desk.

Olivia, more curious than tense, picked up the document and read it carefully.

"Haah…."

Letting out a deep sigh, Olivia placed the document back down and rubbed her forehead.

“What, what should we do? Should I let them in?”

“Of course we should. How could we turn them away? Lead them this way. I’ll go out to greet them as soon as I’m ready.”

“Understood!”

The head of security didn’t even bother with a formal farewell and ran out in a hurry.

Olivia thought about chasing after him to scold him, but she understood his feelings. His knees were probably weak, and his mind was likely blank.

“Eventually… things blew up.”

Olivia rested her chin on her hands, propped up by her elbows on the desk.

The situation she had feared had finally come to pass, though she hadn’t expected the opposing party to be this formidable.

'Well... I suppose there was no way they wouldn’t act, after seeing that article.'

Perhaps, in the long run, this might be a good thing for Shirone?

Based on his nature, he might actually hate it, but considering his future and life, this was undoubtedly more of a blessing.

“In any case… I should go meet them.”

Olivia quickly changed her clothes and left the principal’s office.

@

As the massive carriage crossed the garden, students stopped walking and stared.

Not only was the size of the carriage surprising, but what shocked them even more was the sight of fully armed soldiers entering the academy for the first time.

“What’s going on? What’s happening?”

“Did a war break out? Why are knights entering the academy?”

There was a commotion here and there, but the knights walked on, paying no attention to the stares.

Finally, the carriage stopped in front of the central building of the academy.

The knights stood on alert, hands on the hilts of their swords, as if ready to draw at any sign of trouble.

Hearing the rumors, students began to gather, and soon, hundreds surrounded the carriage.

Yet the carriage remained completely still, like it was rooted in place.

Alpheas, who received Olivia's message, came out first to greet them.

However, despite the appearance of the former principal of the academy, the knights did not lower their vigilance.

“Principal, what’s going on? Did something happen at the academy?”

Alpheas could not say anything to his student.

Even though he had been half-doubting the situation after receiving Olivia’s message, seeing it with his own eyes revealed a situation far beyond his expectations.

On the roof of the carriage, decorated with golden crowns, stood a statue of a lion carved from gold.

There was only one country on the continent that used the lion as a symbol.

Alpheas, who had just arrived himself, didn’t know all the details yet, but the reason for their arrival was clear without needing further explanation.

Shirone.

It was the mistake made by Killain that ultimately led to this situation.

'No, should I call this a situation? Perhaps for Shirone….'

At that moment, the door of the carriage burst open, and an old man with sharp eyes descended. The knights parted to make way for him as he walked forward, his lips tightly pursed with determination.

Though his steps were unsteady with age, the fierce expression on his face was anything but ordinary.

Once he stepped out from the protection of the knights, he scanned the crowd of students with a commanding and arrogant gaze, before pulling out a scroll from his robe.

The scroll itself was made of the finest paper, with golden borders.

“I am Ordoss, Administrative Officer of the Kingdom of Kazra and envoy of the King!”
