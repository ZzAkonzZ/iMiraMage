Chapter 1158: The Five Major Systems

Etella pressed her stomach firmly.

This is bad.

According to the structure of the Law, this place was where illogical demons flowed in.

It’s reacting.

Every time she heard the crying, it felt like a knife was stabbing into her abdomen. But more than the pain, it was fear.

At this rate… I’ll have a miscarriage.

It was most likely Shagall’s child. The child of the man who had cast her, a follower of Goodness, into Hell.

That’s why she was so confused.

I don’t know… What should I…

It was still difficult to make a decision, but her womanly instincts were screaming at her to get away from this place.

For now, I have no choice but to protect it.

Even the thought of saying it aloud was embarrassing. Just as she was trying to gather her courage, Shagall stepped forward.

"Tch! What the hell is this?"

Everyone turned to look at him.

"They're just the same wretched souls. Starving ghouls clinging to life when they should already be dead. I came here to break these damn chains. I don’t want to waste my time in a place like this."

It was not a sight one could stomach for long. Shirone’s group quickly followed Shagall out.

Only Etella remained behind for a moment, silently absorbing the emotions transmitted through their chains.

On the way up to the 12th floor, Venistov explained the Fire Management Corporation’s system.

"The Under World, as managed by the Fire Management Corporation, is divided into three major sectors. First is the corporation’s subsidiary branches, second is the cities where demons reside, and lastly, the logistics warehouses controlled by the Hokseung."

Shirone and Rian had already experienced all three firsthand.

"What makes the Company special is the Flesh Brain, the Central Processing Unit you saw earlier. Emotions may be illogical, but they are not without causality. While reality operates based on events, we handle emotional causality. This is what we call karma."

Shirone asked, "If the Flesh Brain is purified, will Jin Seong Eum be freed?"

"Hmm… The Under World would be paralyzed. What happens after that, I cannot say. But this is only hypothetical. In reality, the Flesh Brain cannot be purified."

"Why not?"

"The **fetuses that make up the Flesh Brain are classified as the Limbo race. They have no karma. Therefore, they cannot be purified."

Venistov raised a finger.

"Not all Limbo are aborted fetuses. The criterion is karma. A child who was born but never developed logic also lacks karma. Such Limbo drift outward in search of sustenance. You can see them in Baramaon’s Seventh District."

When Etella and Shagall first fell into Hell, they had seen them in the Graveyard of Spines.

"It’s best to be cautious. They may not be strong, but they are cruel. Or rather, they don’t even possess the concept of cruelty. The only driving force behind Limbo is their obsession with life."

Shirone asked again, "What if we destroy the Flesh Brain?"

Sometimes, understanding the target requires brutal thoughts.

"Limbo, like Hell Prison, is part of the Law. Even if the individuals disappear, the concept remains. Burning the fetuses would only lead to the creation of a new Flesh Brain. Of course, a major system error would occur, but as a system expert, I strongly advise against it. What Yahweh wishes to achieve will not happen."

Venistov’s tone became firm.

"Destruction is not a good solution. Especially when too much is at stake. If you break both reality and the Under World, what remains? Would you take a hammer to a delicate machine in the hopes that it might fix itself? Sure, maybe you get lucky. But in my opinion, the odds are much higher that it turns into useless scrap. There’s no turning back from that."

Shirone fell deep into thought.

He’s not lying. If it weren’t true, they wouldn’t have shown us the Flesh Brain so easily.

But… something feels off.

It was that enigmatic feeling, as if the most important piece was still being hidden.

The moment they reached the 12th floor, any complex thoughts were blown away by the sheer noise.

"Worship Satan!"

It was a grand, coliseum-like space, packed with people on the 10th and 11th floors.

A demon, holding a microphone embedded with multiple human ears, raised his hands and shouted.

"Ohhh! Lord Satan! Grant us joy! Grant us pleasure! Grant us success!"

His voice boomed through the hundreds of mouths mounted on the walls.

Below the altar, a crowd of thousands shed tears of emotion and chanted after him.

"We believe! We believe in Satan!"

Shirone asked, "This place…?"

"A Satanic Mass. The followers gathered here are humans who have made contracts with Satan."

In other words, living humans who had cast their souls into Hell.

There were about six thousand of them—every one a candidate for Hell Prison.

"Submit! Desire with all your heart! Satan will answer you!"

The crowd roared with fervor, raising their hands.

Each of them was longing for something, but to Shirone, it all sounded like meaningless screams.

The demon stretched out his hand.

"I hear it!"

Like magic, the noise vanished. The demon’s long fingers pointed to someone in the crowd.

"The grief of a mother who lost her child… I hear you, Marie."

"It’s me! It’s me!"

A woman clutched a dead infant in her arms, jumping up and down.

"Come forth. You, who submit to Satan, have the courage to do so."

With tears of joy, Marie climbed onto the stage. The demon accepted the child's corpse.

"This child will live again. Do you believe?"

"Yes! I believe! I… I believe in Satan! If only my child can be brought back…!"

"There is no condition! Do you believe?!"

The woman screamed.

"Yes! I believe!"

"Good! In Satan’s name…!"

With both hands, the demon lifted the corpse high into the air and slammed it onto the ground with all his might.

"Come back to life!"

A dull thud rang out as the child’s body crashed onto the floor.

The entire congregation erupted into thunderous applause.

"Praise the greatness of Satan!"

Shirone felt like he was watching a sick joke, but what came next was even more horrifying.

"Ohhh! My baby!"

Even the mother—holding her dead infant—danced with joy.

"Come here, my child."

She kissed the cold, blue corpse.

Shirone’s group was filled with dread.

Shirone whispered.

"What… the hell is this?"

The demon continued.

He called forth a soldier who had lost a leg and an elderly woman who had gone blind.

"Walk properly!"

The soldier, filled with ecstasy, hobbled onto the stage on his one remaining leg.

He took one step before collapsing, but he kept picking himself back up.

"My body is healed!"

With another loud crash, he fell again.

"Look! I can walk just fine now! I’ll get my revenge on those bastards!"

Once again, she collapsed.

"I can see! I can see now! My checks! No one will steal my checks ever again!"

The old woman flailed her hands, wandering endlessly in circles.

Shirone clenched his teeth.

"… A fanatic."

Lethe spoke.

"That’s right. This is how Hell Prison is born. A blind faith so extreme that it shatters logic. Hidden Codes are special phenomena that emerge from such contradictions."

She turned her head with a sympathetic look.

"I don’t think you’re underestimating this. But if it’s Jin Seong Eum—if it’s her sheer mental fortitude—then you must have thought she could take a place in Hell Prison, didn’t you?"

Shirone didn’t answer.

"This is a colossal desire. To what extent must one’s emotions be pushed to make a contract with Satan? A mother who lost her child in a horrific accident—she could be a standard case. That old man whose family robbed him of his wealth—his resentment is just as strong. Jin Seong Eum cannot do this. She is a human who sacrificed herself for the world."

Lethe was right.

As the demon sent the worshippers back to their seats, twenty individuals stepped onto the stage.

"They are the most prominent candidates for Hell Prison."

They weren’t as frenzied as the other believers, but their despair was far more wretched.

"Behold, everyone! Thanks to my faith in Satan, I have become wealthy!"

From his pocket, garbage continuously spilled onto the floor.

As the congregation applauded, his microphone was passed to a woman beside him.

Her face was horrifically disfigured.

"Hello, everyone. I am Mortasinger, one of the final twenty candidates for Hell Prison."

Shirone’s eyes widened.

That woman… is Mortasinger?!

As far as he remembered, she was a beauty who could have competed in a world pageant.

Even Son Yu-jeong, who had been desperately searching for her, was left staring blankly in shock.

"Satan has bestowed upon me the most beautiful appearance. Look! Aren’t I beautiful?"

The crowd whistled.

"Oh… Uh… I am so happy. I will dedicate myself entirely to Satan…"

As she awkwardly continued speaking, the Command Room on the 13th floor was observing her closely.

The 1 o’clock seat of Pride spoke.

"She’s useless. She handles Hidden Codes decently, but she’s not 100% in sync."

Following Havitz’s orders, they were trying to complete Hell Prison as quickly as possible.

The 6 o’clock seat of Wanderlust added,

"Her early development was fast, but her conviction is lacking. Probably because she was a rule-defier."

The 8 o’clock seat of Jealousy scoffed.

"Even if she distorts logic, it’s still a form of logic. She tried to understand Hidden Codes through cheap tricks like rule-defying, and now this is what happens. What an idiot."

"More importantly… look at the 12th floor."

The 11 o’clock seat of Climax, a man with a gentle appearance, jerked his chin downward.

"Yahweh is here."

The moment they laid eyes on Shirone, a murderous intent surged through the air.

"Because of that damn Yahweh, we’ve suffered so much. Didn’t he also raise Wizard?"

If they could kill him now—

"… Should we go?"

"You sure about this? Lethe is right next to him. She’s obviously planning something."

"Who cares? We exist only for Lord Havitz. That is the Twelve Temptations of Satan."

They were the zealots of Hell Prison.

Once the twenty candidates had finished their introductions, the demon took back the microphone.

"This is the power of Satan! A god whose power knows no bounds! Let us pray!"

He likely recited verses from the Demon’s Bible, which Gyorgy was believed to possess.

After the gathering ended and the believers dispersed, Lethe led the group to the 10th floor.

"Mupa!"

A demon, who had been resting on the stage, noticed Lethe and hurried over.

"Ah! Madam President! What brings you here?"

"I was giving these people a tour of the Company. Introduce yourself. You know them, don’t you? Yahweh, and his companions."

If he were any other demon, his eyes would have gone red with rage, but Mupa simply smirked and extended a hand.

"Nice to meet you. Mupa, at your service."

Shirone scowled, making it clear he had no intention of shaking hands.

Mupa awkwardly pulled his hand back.

"Tch! So harsh. Aren’t we in the same business?"

"The same business?"

Hearing the cold edge in Shirone’s voice, Lethe quickly spread her arms wide.

"Alright, alright! Let’s move on. You know Mortasinger, right? One of the Hell Prison candidates. Where is she now?"

"Probably praying. She’s really into it lately. But why do you ask—"

"Son Yu-jeong!"

At that moment, a door connecting to the auditorium swung open, and Mortasinger burst in.

"It really is you! I thought I saw you earlier, but I wasn’t sure! It’s really you, Son Yu-jeong!"

She ran straight to Son Yu-jeong, throwing her arms around her neck.

But even as she embraced her, Son Yu-jeong couldn’t believe it.

"You…"

They had roamed Heaven together, stirring up all kinds of trouble—a friend with a bold and carefree personality.

"Haha! Well, I guess it’s only natural you’d be surprised. I used to be ugly, but now I’ve become this beautiful!"

But the thing standing before her now—

It was something so grotesque that even a skeleton would have been more pleasant to look at.
