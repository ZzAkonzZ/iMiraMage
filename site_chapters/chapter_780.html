Chapter 780:

"If good and evil are defined by humans, then who decides who's good and who's evil?"

Uorin continued.

"How could anyone know? The good who score 100 say those who score 10 are evil, and the evil who score 10 say those who score 1 are evil."

The world feels like it's overflowing with bad people.

"Everyone just thinks they're right. The moment someone realizes they can’t be absolutely right themselves, they begin to lean from neutrality to good..."

That’s intelligence.

"But even the enlightened commit wrongs."

That’s why human life, when seen up close, is a tragedy—when seen from afar, a comedy.

"Then what about the perspective of Extreme Evil? If someone watched human life from the farthest distance possible..."

From far enough away, our lives lack logic or consistency.

"It’s just one big, trashy comedy."

"Puhaha! That was amazing! As expected from Zetaro! You truly deserve the title of one of Gustav’s Four Banners!"

Havitz couldn’t stop laughing.

"Someone just died. And yet you laugh? Show some restraint."

When Zetaro spoke sternly, Havitz’s laughter stopped abruptly.

"…"

Caught in the middle, Natasha smiled with her massive, eyelid-less eyes rolling gently.

"Puhaha! That’s hilarious! The ultimate comedian! Isn’t that right, Natasha?"

"No question about it."

Zetaro restrained his laughter to the very end.

A true comedian must never laugh at his own jokes.

Rosica Zetaro, one of the Four Banners of Gustav.

Doctor, master craftsman, comedian—his skills were countless. But Havitz liked him for one specific reason.

"Zetaro’s funny."

Because he was the most like Havitz.

"Hey, Smodo! Come here quick! Guess what Zetaro did today—"

Smodo met them at the gates of the imperial capital.

"This isn’t the time for that. Aren’t you going to attack the Cotria Republic? Balkan’s bored to death waiting."

Havitz dismounted.

"You haven’t attacked yet? Do it now. War’s Balkan’s favorite thing, so let him go wild."

"We’ve already deployed one division. It’s just a probe. They’ll take over the border city of Pasifa."

"If Balkan’s leading them, they’ll win. Do what you want. Handle the spoils yourself."

"Speaking of spoils—sure, the soldiers will take the women for themselves, but I was thinking we could sell some off to citizens for gold."

"Why? Out of money? Just steal from another country."

"You know how it is. Young men need fun if you want them to fight. If we turn slaves into livestock and distribute the profits to families, we can increase taxes."

Havitz gave a thumbs-up.

"As expected of our domestic policy king. Oh, by the way, Zetaro killed a guy during his show. Pick ten handsome, strong men and send them to the widow. They'll be more useful than her husband. Give her a billion gold."

"Understood."

Uorin tapped her head.

“All they think about is how to make themselves happy.”

They have no regard for others' emotions.

“Killing, hurting others—sure, you could call that evil. But that’s not the definition of evil.”

So what is evil?

“Ignorance.”

According to Uorin:

"Not stupidity or lack of education. It’s having no idea what’s right, and therefore no basis for judgment."

Thus, ignorance is chaos.

"And of course, no guilt. Because they don’t even realize they’re evil. Lawslayer is powerful, but it’s hard to wedge into that gap."

Because they exist outside the bounds of law.

"Are you serious right now? If you keep this up, I’ll flip the table on everything."

Ardak, Deputy Minister of the Environment in the Gustav Empire, had a habit of coercing maids into bed by making them owe him money.

"Please stop. I’ve told you no."

But Mari never fell for it.

"Come on, it’s a win-win. Think about your poor mother. I could get her treatment."

She’d already heard countless horror stories from other maids about what Ardak had done.

"I don’t need your money. My mother’s illness is a family matter. I’ll earn what we need myself."

"You insolent little—!"

Ardak raised a hand, and Mari flinched in fear.

"You think I can’t do anything to you? I’m the Deputy Minister! I could have you strung up in the square today!"

Ministers of Gustav were notoriously tyrannical. Killing a maid was hardly worth noting.

"P-Please, spare me…"

"Just borrow the money. Make it official, okay? It’s good for both of us."

Just then, a soldier shouted:

"His Majesty, the Emperor!"

Ardak jumped and backed away immediately. The maid bowed as Havitz approached, scratching his crotch and dressed in rags as always.

"Everyone working hard, I see. So what’s going on here? Sniff."

Mari instinctively grimaced—then met Havitz’s gaze, and her face went pale.

"A-Apologies…"

Havitz held out his hand, and Zetaro pulled a small hatchet from his bag.

As Mari’s heart dropped, Havitz buried the hatchet into Ardak’s skull.

"Justice served."

"GYAAAAARGH!"

As the blade lodged in his skull, Ardak’s eyeballs popped out of their sockets with a grotesque pop.

“H-hhh—huh—!”

Gasping, Mari stood frozen in fear. Natasha walked up, plucked one dangling eyeball off the ground, and examined it.

"…"

Then, casually, she put it in her mouth and sucked the blood out.

"Eye."

She half-spat the eyeball from her lips and turned to Havitz, who let out a chuckle.

"Enjoy life. Give her a billion gold, too."

He patted Mari’s shoulder and walked off. Natasha followed behind and casually spat the eyeball onto the ground.

"I feel stronger already."

Raising both arms, she turned the corner. Mari collapsed to the floor and vomited violently.

"Bleeegh! UUEEEGGGH!"

“Evil comes from ignorance.”

The fact that mages—symbols of intelligence—have always become the era’s paragons of good was no coincidence.

“And yet, sometimes evil can seem… charming. People mistake evil for good. Humans are creatures of illusion—once they like something, even its flaws seem fine…”

Uorin shrugged.

"But the truth is, none of it means anything. Good deeds, bad deeds—they’re just fragments tossed out from chaos."

Shirone asked:

"How can someone like that exist?"

"Because they’re extremes. And by that logic, paragon good—Extreme Good—is just as bizarre. Your compassion, Nane’s merit, all of it is just being as far from humanity as one can get."

Minerva asked:

"What about childhood trauma or something?"

If they could find the cause of Havitz’s descent, maybe they could find a way to deal with him.

"I ran a history search," Uorin said. "But there's almost nothing."

Ness asked:

"Almost?"

"When Havitz was three, that’s the age where kids start getting curious about bodies… he blinded a maid by gouging her eyes out."

"…"

"The Gustav bloodline is violent, but you can’t discount environment either. If the maid had pulled his hand away, she would've been executed."

For resisting the emperor’s son.

"The maid screamed, covering her eyes in pain. Little Havitz looked around with blood on his hands. And you know what happened next?"

With everyone’s attention on her, Uorin twirled her finger.

"Everyone in the room clapped and praised him. With terror in their eyes. That’s absolute power."

Shirone muttered:

"He didn’t know what was right."

"Exactly. He never had the chance to learn. But not every powerful child becomes Extreme Evil. Maybe there’s something else we’re missing…"

Uorin shrugged again.

"Or maybe there’s no reason at all. No matter the upbringing, Havitz might’ve turned out this way regardless."

Gudio spoke up.

"A child. That’s the key, isn’t it?"

Uorin snapped her fingers.

"Bingo."

As they entered the Grand Hall of the imperial palace, Balkan was already there, standing before a massive world map.

With all four of the Gustav Empire’s Four Banners now gathered, Havitz stretched lazily as he ascended the throne.

“Asker.”

Zetaro, who had instantly concocted a powerful narcotic, handed over a pipe. He took one for himself as well, puffing deeply.

While Smodo rolled around with one of the maids on the floor, Balkan spoke.

“We’ve taken Pasifa. The city mayor was captured, and approximately 70,000 civilians have been detained as new conscripts.”

Havitz, high on Asker, shuddered with euphoria.

“Kill them.”

Zetaro, eyes glassy, staggered over and yanked Havitz by the hair, dragging him off the throne and onto the floor.

“Heheheheheh, that’s hilarious.”

As Havitz lay sprawled out, chest rising and falling with laughter, Zetaro seated himself on the throne like an emperor and shouted:

“Kill them all!”

“Bury them alive.”

Havitz’s pupils suddenly refocused.

“Dig a massive pit and bury everyone. Only the ones who crawl out get to live.”

Smodo turned his head, still lying atop the maid.

“You know how much it’d cost to bury 70,000 people?”

“That’s what makes it fun.”

That was the end of the discussion. Balkan stood up.

“Then I’ll give the order. It should be quite the spectacle—come watch once preparations are complete.”

Natasha chirped with excitement.

“Let’s go! Let’s go! I wanna see!”

But Havitz, who had suddenly sobered from his Asker high, stared at the ceiling and muttered,

“…I’m not going.”

Smodo froze mid-motion.

“You’re really not gonna go watch? After spending all that money?”

“That’s what makes it even funnier.”

Zetaro slapped the armrest.

“Puhaha! I get it! I totally get it!”

Balkan and Smodo blinked. Natasha, lacking eyelids, tilted her head in confusion.

“Heheheh…”

And then, realization hit. Laughter erupted.

“Oh god, my stomach! I’m gonna die laughing! This is gold! Not going? Actually not going? Puhahahaha!”

The maids in the hall trembled in fear.

Madmen…

They didn’t want to know what was so funny, but the way these people laughed—tears and all—made it look like they were genuinely… happy.

“Keh keh keh! Keh keh keh keh!”

Natasha sprinted across the hall, twirling gracefully into a spinning ballet pirouette.

Havitz whispered to himself.

“…This world is fun.”

Like a playground.

Even if, when Mom calls you in for dinner, you cry your way back home.

“You won’t want to admit it…”

The very thought was revolting.

“But Extreme Evil is also as pure as the mind of a newborn. They simply pursue a different direction.”

They have no concept of how they should behave.

“Miro’s Goodness. Shirone’s Compassion. Nane’s Emptiness. Gaold’s Balance. Now, surely you understand what all of this means.”

Shirone had traveled far and wide.

“But the last thing we must understand is Havitz’s Evil. So we have to accept this horrifying truth.”

A new era of chaos was upon them.

“Evil is pure.”

Those who had accepted it now looked on with sharp, glinting eyes.

“And only in that purity of Evil…”

Uorin nodded.

“Yes. That’s the only place we can wedge ourselves into its cracks.”

A method to kill Havitz had been decided.
