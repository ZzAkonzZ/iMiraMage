Chapter 226: Waiting for the New Term (4)

“We lost. I have a favor to ask—please keep this matter a secret.”

“Huh? Didn’t you just say you’d flatten our ass and expel us?”

Despite the humiliation that made his body tremble, the leader of the mercenary group was someone who could face reality.

He needed to endure for the future of himself and his comrades.

“I’m sorry! Just help me this once! You’re going to be a mage too. But I’m your senior, so there’s no need to break my rice bowl, right?”

Dante shook his head as if finding their pleas insufficient.

The mercenaries blushed as they realized what the boy was asking for. Eventually, even the four standing behind the leader bowed politely and apologized.

“Consider our situation. We need to make a living too, don’t we?”

Dante, finally satisfied, put his hand on the leader's shoulder.

“Don’t worry. I was just bored during the vacation and decided to have some fun. By the way, aren’t you being a bit too careless? I wonder how you got the official license?”

At that moment, the door opened and a woman came in. The mercenaries instinctively straightened their bent backs and pretended as if nothing had happened.

“Oh my! It’s over already. Ah, what a pity.”

The woman had curly, chin-length hair sticking to her cheek. Her playful face looked as if she were in her early 20s, but she was actually over thirty.

“You are... Kylline?”

She was a woman who worked in the academic journal editing department under Teachers' Association and was a correspondent who traveled around the kingdom to write reports on magic academies.

The articles she writes are published in a magazine called <Spirit>, which is distributed to magic academies across the kingdom, so it was only a matter of time before she would become famous once they were published.

“I came after hearing rumors, and you certainly lived up to them. Dante, is this miserables scene all created by your hands?”

As Kylline struck an exaggerated pose, the accompanying photographer took pictures with an optical camera.

The mercenaries were at a loss as to what to do in this embarrassing situation.

For them, <Spirit> magazine was just a memory of the past, but it was also a major magazine distributed nationwide. If their disgrace was published in an article, it would be a blot on their family's name.

“No, it wasn’t. How could I have defeated them alone without the help of my seniors? I asked for practical training, and they kindly agreed. I learned a lot. Thank you, seniors.”

When Dante turned around, even the most perceptive mages quickly adjusted their expressions. (단테가 돌아보자 눈치로 둘째가라면 서러운 마법사들이 얼른 낯빛을 고쳤다.)

“Haha! This much is nothing. Still, it was impressive. Truly, you are the greatest star of the kingdom, aren’t you?”

Kylline turned around with a gleam in her eyes.

“Oh my! Do you know Dante? As expected, the kingdom’s proud promising talent is something else. Even though he’s still a student, rumors about him are spreading among the active mages.”

“Of course. I sometimes talk about Dante with my colleagues. I believe that one day Dante will become a first-class Archmage.”

Kylline meticulously took notes during the interview with the leader.

<Spirit> has been a popular magazine to begin with, but on days when an article about Dante is published, sales increase by 1.5 times.

Dante's appearance was beautiful enough to capture the hearts of girls, and the jealous attention of men must have been no exception.

‘Oh, how cute. You've grown up so well, my Dante.'

It would be safe to say that Kylline, who had been interviewing Dante since he was young, had risen along with Dante. It was also thanks to Dante that she was able to rise to become the best reporter in the kingdom.

“Allright, now that the interview is over, let’s go have a party. Teachers’ Association will be treating us generously!”

As Dante left the hideout, the butler of Airhain Family, who had been waiting outside, approached with a box.

“Young Master Dante, Lady Olivia has sent you a package.”

“From my mentor? What’s this all of a sudden?”

“I’m not sure myself... But I was instructed to deliver it personally.”

Even Dante, who had nothing to fear in the world, would be humbled by Olivia’s words. She was his teacher and benefactor who recognized his talent from a young age and had him admitted to the Royal Magic Academy. Of course, the same was true for his classmates, Closer and Savina.

“I see. Give it to me.”

When he received the parcel and opened it, a thick form came out. Dante read it one page at a time, and his eyebrows furrowed.

“What’s this? It’s an application for transferring academy?”

Closer and Savina peered over his shoulder. The application included not only Dante’s profile but also theirs.

Kylline clung to them.

“What’s this, Dante? Huh? What’s going on?”

“Um, it’s… a transfer application. It seems that Master has been temporarily appointed as the principal of a certain academy. If we want to study there together, we’re invited to join...”

Closer said.

“Then of course we should go. If Master is going, how can we not follow?”

Savina asked.

“Where is it? Does it not say which academy is it?”

“No, Alpheas Magic Academy. But where is this academy located?”

Students from Bashka had little interest in regional information. Dante’s group only knew that it was one of the top five prestigious academies in the kingdom.

Kylline, sensing a scoop, brightened up.

“Oh my, Alpheas Magic Academy? And with Miss Olivia as the temporary principal? This is a huge event. Alpheas Magic Academy is ranked 4th among magic academies. It’s located in the city of Creas. The most famous alumnus is the current head of the Magic Association, Mr. Micaea Gaold.”

“Ah, so Mr. Gaold is from there.”

The mention of the 4th rank didn’t impress them much, but hearing the name Gaold changed their expressions. If it was the academy where one of the five certified Archmages in the kingdom had studied, they would be able to save face even if they transferred.

Closer suggested.

“Let’s go and see. Anyway, school is starting nationwide now. We can transfer to match it.”

“Yes. Master will also be taking up her post then.”

Dante took the letter and left the application form to Savina. They could discuss the details when they met; for now, he was just happy that something interesting had come up in his otherwise dull routine.

Dante, who was watching the sunset falling down the mountain, looked back at his friends and smiled.

“Sounds fun, right? Alpheas Magic Academy.”

Where is Shirone? (1)

The second half of Alpheas Magic Academy has begun.

From the morning, the main gate was crowded with carriages carrying students.

A bizarre scene was created where young students accompanied by their parents were crying and begging because they did not want to go to academy.

On the other hand, the adolescent boys crossed the main gate with indifferent expressions, as if they were embarrassed to even be attended to by the butler.

The entrance ceremony was scheduled for 11 a.m., so the students returned to their accommodations and unpacked their bags.

Shirone, who arrived early in the morning, put the books he brought on the bookshelf and laid out his clothes and daily necessities.

“Shirone!”

His friends burst through the door and came in.

“Nade! Iruki!”

Shirone, with a big smile, ran over to them. It had only been 30 days since they last saw each other, but it felt like a year.

There were so many things happened. How would they react if they knew he had been to Heaven?

They held each other's hands and shouted in unison.

"I have something amazing!"

Then they stared blankly at each other.

Though they didn’t know what each other’s stories were, they anticipated that today would be too short to share them all.

"Okay. Let's take turns. Shirone, you go first."

“Allright. I went to Galliant Island with Amy…!”

Nade suddenly grabbed him by the collar.

“What! You… No way… That…! What the hell! You traitor! I’m so jealous!"

Shirone's face turned red.

"No! That's not it. Just listen."

“Euaaaa! This is ridiculous. I thought that no matter how long I stayed single, I'd at least beat Shirone. How could this happen...!"

Nade was on the verge of losing his mind.

Even Iruki’s pupils were shaking, indicating that this issue was indeed a significant dilemma for boys.

Only after Shirone firmly denied the assumption could he start his story.

An hour quickly passed. However, none of them realized how much time had gone by.

“Wow. You really… survived. That was a very dangerous situation.”

“That’s true, but the final war. It’s, what can I say, so serious that it doesn’t even sound serious.”

The impression of Heaven was so strong that Nade and Iruki fell into deep thought for a long time.

On the other hand, Shirone, who felt relieved after sharing his story, wanted to hear how his friends had spent their time.

“Now you tell me. What amazing things happened during your vacation?"

The two looked at each other, and Iruki spoke first.

“I ate crayfish-flavored ice cream.”

"A giant spider the size of a fist appeared in my workshop."

"Haha! You really had fun."

Just imagining it was amusing. Ice cream that tasted like crayfish? And the thought of a fist-sized spider right in front of him was creepy.

"Damn it!"

Nade pushed Shirone's cheek with his fist.

“Ouch! What's that for?"

"You had such an exciting vacation all by yourself! And with the super beautiful Senior Amy!"

"I told you, I almost died! And what's with 'super beautiful Amy'? That's childish! Ugh!"

While Nade was slapping Shirone's face again, Iruki was pounding the ground in regret.

"Really a shame. I shouldn't have pretended to be crazy and gone back to my parents' house. I’m also curious about that magic, Ataraxia or whatever. Can you show it to me sometime?"

“Huh? Ataraxia?”

He could have done a demonstration at any time, but the situation wasn't favorable. The depletion of mental strength was one issue, and performing it in front of others would only complicate matters.

"I'd like to, but maybe next time. I'll show you when there's a chance."

"Oh? Saving it for later, huh? Fine, then I'll keep my new magic a secret for now too."

The mention of new magic made Shirone’s eyes sparkle. If it was Iruki with his Savant Syndrome, the new magic he had learned would certainly be on a different level than what the other students were learning.

“What is it? Hm? What kind of magic is it?”

“It’s a secret. Let me give you a hint. Should I say it’s a secret weapon that can defeat you?”

After saying that, Iruki turned to look at Nade and asked.

"By the way, what about you? You didn’t just play around during the vacation, did you?"

"Oh, I was insanely busy. I’m studying alchemy these days. I’ve been reinforcing some of the magic needed for that field.”

Although alchemy is a major branch of magic, it wasn't a subject taught at the magic academy. Alchemy is a vast discipline, and alchemists are trained separately at the Alchemy Foundation.

Iruki was concerned. After all, it seemed Nade had focused on something unrelated to their classes during the vacation.

"Are you sure about this? Getting a mage’s certification would be more advantageous if you want to become a magic engineer."

"Of course, but I also want to learn materials science. Functions begin with forms, and the beginning of forms is the essence. I thought it was important to understand the basics of alchemy."

They heard the dormitory supervisor’s voice from the hallway.

"The entrance ceremony is about to begin. Students, please gather in the central hall."

All students from Class One to Class Ten gathered in the training grounds.

Shirone looked for Alpheas on the platform, but for some reason he was nowhere to be seen.

“Huh? I can’t see Principal.”

"He’s on a six-month suspension. An interim principal is coming today. I don’t know who it is, but I hope they won’t make us study too hard."

Nade closed his eyes and prayed. Although he aspired to be a magic engineer, he first needed to graduate from magic academy, and tougher classes would not be welcome.

As the newly appointed principal appeared on stage, murmurs spread through here and there.

Iruki's face grew serious.

“This… of all people, it had to be that person."

“Huh? Do you know who it is?”

"Jerome Olivia. Former principal of the Royal Magic Academy. Probably a certified second-grade Archmage."

Shirone looked closely at Olivia. She looked much younger than her age, and above all, she was beautiful. The teachers were lined up on both sides, and they all looked tense.

'What a great person. Principal of Royal Magic Academy....'

Olivia waited until the students' commotion died down before she began her speech.

"Hello. My name is Jerome Olivia, and I will be serving as your new principal starting this semester. Although my position is temporary, I promise to do my best to teach and guide you during my term."
