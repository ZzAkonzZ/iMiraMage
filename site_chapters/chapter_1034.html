Chapter 1034: The City of Magical Engineering (2)

Boyd, the deputy director of the Alchemy Guild, left the Marquisate of Picaras with Monoras.

"The president has instructed us to go to Laviette as soon as possible. If we hurry..."

Monoras, whose belly was bloated from all the bribes he had taken, changed direction.

"Let's do this. I'm too heavy right now, so I need to lighten up a bit."

Monoras's gaze was fixed on the gambling city of 'Unlucky.'

"Deputy Director! We don't have time for this!"

Boyd shouted, but Monoras's body was already flying towards Unlucky.

Meanwhile, at the Alchemy Guild.

"It's done."

Lethe, who had worked through the night, looked up at the ceiling with a tired face.

Her head was spinning, and she was overwhelmed with sleepiness.

"...I have to work."

Although she had sent Boyd, she couldn't leave this matter to someone else.

As she left the president's office in rags, the employees who had just arrived for work bowed their heads.

"President, where are you going?"

"On a business trip. I'm going to Laviette. I've processed all the pending documents, so it should be fine for a while."

"You look very tired. You didn't sleep at all last night, did you? You should get some rest..."

"It's okay. Everyone is working hard. Oh, right, let's stop by the restaurant. I need to get some snacks that Monoras likes."

"Yes. I'll let them know."

The employees liked the president of the Alchemy Guild.

"By the way, you're amazing. I don't mean to be rude, but I don't think I could handle the deputy director's personality."

"Haha, but he's a good guy at heart. As the deputy director, he must have his own difficulties."

'It doesn't seem like it at all.'

Sometimes she wondered if she had amnesia.

Morning in hell.

Flames filled the sky with the smell of sulfur, evaporating the cold snap from the night.

"We've arrived."

The grand castle where the duke lived was one of the largest buildings in Laviette.

Unlike the bleak cityscape, it was neat and, above all, resembled a building from reality.

"I thought you would like it. Now, let's go inside. The duke is waiting for you."

Following Bielman's guidance, they entered the castle, where a luxurious feast was prepared.

"Welcome, Yahweh. And your companion."

The duke, who was eating at the head of the table, put down his utensils and smiled.

The old man had a white beard that reached his chest and a kind appearance.

'Duke Marguerite.'

The fact that Rian had no special reaction was a clear sign that he was tense.

'He is indeed impressive.'

Compared to the army of hell, it felt like facing a commander ranked in the top ten.

"Would you like to join me for a meal before we start?"

The food, which had just been prepared, was steaming.

Rian swallowed his saliva.

Unlike the food in the restaurant, it looked like something humans would eat, and the utensils were familiar.

"Do you usually eat like this?"

As Shirone sat down opposite, Rian also quietly took a seat next to him.

"At my age, there's nothing particularly important in the world. But I did put some effort into this."

Even his proficiency in the common language of the continent raised Shirone's wariness towards Marguerite.

'He's no ordinary person.'

He might not be as strong as a commander, but he was a key figure in the current situation.

As they ate the food, Shirone asked,

"I'd like to hear directly about what happened yesterday. The demon you sent used a strange ability."

"It was just entertainment. Do you think I would dare to touch Yahweh with something like that?"

"So, what is it?"

For the first time, their gazes collided, and the atmosphere rapidly cooled.

"Desire, a demonic material I created."

Marguerite took a step back.

"When impure magic is refined with special techniques, you can obtain a special substance that reacts to desire. About 0.1 grams can be extracted per human who falls into hell."

How many humans would need to be ground up?

"War advances technology. Humans are desperate, but we can't just sit back and take it. I was convinced this substance would bring victory to the demons. The problem is..."

Marguerite adjusted his expression.

"The spiritual realm has been closed. Before Desire could be commercialized and supplied to the army."

In the end, Seong-eum was right.

"If it were just a matter of destroying the system, it could be fixed, but she used the system. Once inside, she closed it, and it can't be reopened until she is purified."

Shirone's eyes, stripped of pretense, sharpened.

"How long will it take?"

"I don't know. The Alchemy Guild might know, but I didn't feel like asking. Since then, I've been neglecting technological development, just passing the time. Then I heard Yahweh had arrived, so I played a prank."

Marguerite leaned forward.

"Now, let me ask. What brings someone busier than me to this hell?"

"To destroy the demonic realm."

A catastrophic system, possible only for a high-ranking commander, was tormenting reality.

"I'm thinking of negotiating with the Alchemy Guild. You might need to use your power."

"Hmm."

Marguerite stroked his beard.

"Of course, the power of the demonic realm is based in hell. If you can't crush the sprout, you'll burn the roots?"

"Yes. I will purify hell. Even if it means all demons disappear. But if it's you... you might find a better way."

"If this is a threat, it's too much. Even if the spiritual realm is closed, demons are born endlessly. The army of hell, the commanders, will be restored over time."

"You want to end it too. The tiresome cycle of reality and the hidden world."

Marguerite's eyes half-closed.

"Do you believe in the legendary lesson that not all evil fails to repent?"

He stood up.

"Of course, there was such a thing. A long time ago. But don't be mistaken. I'm not someone who has reached such a noble realization. I'm just one of the common demons who hate Yahweh and dislike humans."

"Is there no room for negotiation?"

"Haha. Do humans negotiate with dogs or pigs?"

Rian's eyes glinted with murderous intent.

"Don't misunderstand. If you were offended, let's say demons are dogs or pigs. Anyway, we are entirely different species. We can never have the same goals."

Marguerite turned and walked away.

"It was a pleasant breakfast. I hope you achieve everything you want in hell..."

Despite the mocking tone, Shirone responded calmly.

"What if you open the spiritual realm?"

Marguerite's steps halted.

"...What do you mean?"

"Exactly what I said. If you open the spiritual realm that Seong-eum closed, can you help us?"

Marguerite turned to Shirone.

"Yahweh lies too. No demon understands the system of hell better than I do. Jin Seong-eum, that woman, will never find freedom until her karma is fulfilled. In other words, opening the spiritual realm is impossible."

"I can break the karma chain."

"No."

"Rian."

At Shirone's words, Rian stood up and infused his entire body with powerful energy.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

The chains of Gehenna burst from his chest and struck the ground, causing Marguerite's brow to furrow.

In the next moment, the four chains snapped and were sucked back into Rian's chest.

"...How?"

Shirone smiled and said,

"How about it? Don't you want to analyze it? It seems there's a hidden code you don't know about."

A system error?

Marguerite, who had been glaring at Shirone with sharp eyes, raised his right hand to his chest.

At the same time, a chain emerged from Rian's solar plexus and entered his grasp.

"Ugh!"

As Rian exerted his strength again, the heat, like molten iron, was transmitted to Marguerite's hand.

Sizzle!

Even as the sound of burning was heard, Marguerite lowered his gaze to examine the chain.

With a serious expression, he looked at Rian and then threw the chain to the ground, where it stuck with a thud.

"Tch!"

After confirming that Rian had broken it again, he clasped his hands behind his back and walked to the window.

A long silence followed.

'This is no ordinary problem.'

The first person that came to Marguerite's mind was the president of the Alchemy Guild.

'Lethe must know. Yet she hasn't come. No, the fact that Yahweh reached me first means...'

Is hell also on the verge of collapse?

'If it's the natural order, it can't be helped.'

How naive to think that only hell would remain safe in the grand cycle.

"Prepare lunch."

With that, Marguerite snapped his fingers at Bielman and moved on.

"Open the underground laboratory."

When they arrived at the duke's laboratory, a young succubus, about one meter tall, ran up.

"Hello, Duke. Huh?"

Seeing Shirone, the succubus's face turned pale.

"Y-Yahweh!"

Instinctively, she activated her dream demon abilities, but instead of seduction, she only looked cute.

"He's a guest. Treat him with respect."

As the succubus's abilities were released, she wore a dazed expression while the duke turned to the central device.

"This is Mercede, the manager of the laboratory. This child will assist Yahweh for the time being."

Rian looked around.

There were over 200 large glass tubes containing human samples.

"Are they all dead?"

"In reality, death is 'an unending sleep,' but in hell, death is only purification. If you mean 'an unending sleep,' then yes."

Shirone asked,

"Why did you bring us here?"

"Just breaking the chains of Gehenna isn't enough to conclude a system error. First, we'll examine Rian's body. And if possible, Yahweh should assist as well. It will provide useful data."

As Marguerite activated the device, two capsules opened with a whooshing sound.

"You can enter here."

Rian thought of the 200 humans sleeping in the glass tubes, but Shirone moved forward without hesitation.

"Wait, Shirone. It could be a trap."

"It's okay."

Shirone didn't stop.

"The duke enjoys a battle of wits with his enemies, but he doesn't cling to their weaknesses."

The duke that Goffin knew was such a demon.

"Right?"

Marguerite avoided Shirone's gaze and turned to the device.

"Please enter. It won't take long."

As Shirone and Rian entered the capsules, the doors closed, and transparent water filled them.

As the water rose to his chin, Rian turned his head, but Shirone remained calm.

The capsules filled with water, and five seconds later, the water level quickly receded.

Mercede reported,

"Scan complete. Transmitting to the Underworld screen."

"Hmm."

Marguerite examined a rectangular steel plate shallowly filled with blood-red liquid.

Rian's information was extracted.

"This, this is...!"

Numbers that had never been seen in any demon were engraved on the red plate.
