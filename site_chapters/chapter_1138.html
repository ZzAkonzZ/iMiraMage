Chapter 1138: Key Keywords (1)

Uorin got up from the carpet, gulped down some cold water, and took a seat at the table.

As she pressed her temples and fell into thought, Kido approached and asked, "Are you feeling better now?"

Realizing Kido's presence, Uorin turned her head and waved her hand dismissively. "Get out."

Kido frowned. "Wow, you really know how to say thank you." He had rushed over upon hearing about the crisis, yet she couldn't even thank him.

'Friend, huh.'

At least, he thought they were that close.

"Fine. Do whatever you want."

As he turned coldly to leave, Uorin's expression soured.

"Kido."

When he turned back, she was smiling warmly. "Thanks for protecting me. Want to stay for some tea?"

The goblin's lips pouted. 'Lies… I know the heart of someone who has endured countless years to meet Shirone.'

'Someone like me wouldn't even catch her eye.'

Kido turned away again. "I'm leaving. I'm the captain of Kashan's guard. I have a lot to do. Call me if you need anything."

As the door closed, Uorin's expression vanished as if it had never been there.

Bang, her hand hit the table.

"Kaiden."

It was the key future keyword she had found through a combination of history search and future vision. Now it was a matter of thought.

'Kaiden is the biggest variable, which means it's related to the prison break.'

At Arachne's banquet, Kaiden's right hand had almost made a big mistake.

'Even if the law is twisted, he maintained the initial inertia to attack Marcus. That's the prison break. But why? How does he affect the future?'

Her thoughts raced.

'Of course… it's Maya.'

A famous singer from Tormia, only she could influence Kaiden's heart.

"Hmm."

Uorin crossed her legs and rested her chin on her hand.

"But why?"

She's just a singer.

'Giant pyramids have been built in various countries. Humanity is steadily heading towards extinction. But Maya? In this situation, one human's voice…'

Uorin's thoughts came to an abrupt halt.

"The Ultima System."

She had become human over many generations, but she was once a Gaian.

'Yes, it must be related to the integrated mental system.'

She had analyzed the meaning of the keyword, but this was as far as she could infer.

'I can't figure out the process. There are too many logical gaps. This is just delusion.'

Uorin's eyes glowed golden.

'Future vision!'

As she endured the pain and examined the timeline again, a shock surged through her brain as if it would explode.

"Ugh!"

The time wave was vibrating like an earthquake due to the changes in the law led by the Zodiac.

Unable to withstand the nausea from the overlapping events, she cut off her ability.

"Annoying."

In this state, she couldn't even find keywords, let alone predict the future.

'Anyway, Maya. And Kaiden.'

Where are they and what are they doing now?

In the rehearsal room at Delta headquarters.

The world's most famous musicians were gathered, practicing intensely. In the place preparing for the grand finale performance of the Holy War, many emotions clashed.

As each represented their country, there was bound to be a competitive spirit.

The performance manager said, "There will be a full rehearsal soon. Let's take a 10-minute break."

The artists, who had stopped practicing like clockwork, scattered as if they had never been there.

"Whew."

As Maya relaxed and sighed, Reina approached and patted her shoulder.

"Good job. You did well."

"Reina, sis."

Among those from Tormia, only Maya and Reina were selected for the finale performance, indicating their high level.

"I thought I was going to die from the difficulty. The others are amazing. Not a single mistake."

"Hehe, it's not like that. Everyone is doing their best."

Kaiden brought water.

"Maya, drink this. Reina, you too."

Since Maya's agency representative, Palmers, couldn't attend the Holy War, Kaiden volunteered to be her manager.

Maya returned the cup and said, "Thanks, Kaiden. Honestly, I'm really nervous since it's my first ensemble, but I'm glad you're here."

"Don't worry. I don't know much about music, but your voice sounded the best to me."

As a blush appeared on Maya's cheeks, Reina, who was watching, chuckled.

'Affection is scary.'

At that moment, a voice came from the sofa.

"Hey, you."

The woman sitting with her legs crossed arrogantly was El Kiana, the world's top singer.

"Bring me some water too."

Kaiden, meeting Reina's gaze, took out a new cup from his bag and approached.

"Oh, okay."

"Wait."

Maya stepped in front of Kaiden and said, "Kaiden is my manager."

"So?"

"If you're going to ask for a favor, you should ask for permission. And don't speak informally."

El Kiana's eyebrows narrowed, and the foreign artists watched with interest.

'I thought she was mild, but she's tough. Not many come out unscathed after clashing with El Kiana.'

El Kiana looked around.

"Whoever's manager he is, he's not a real manager anyway. We're preparing for the finale diva performance, and besides him, what other man is in this room?"

"Kaiden isn't hired. He's my friend and volunteered to help."

El Kiana covered her mouth and laughed.

"How childish. Do you think I don't know? Bringing a man into a place like this is obvious."

Maya didn't back down.

"What are you thinking? Kaiden is just a friend! We're not anything more! Ah…"

Maya quickly looked at Kaiden, but he smiled without showing any sign of discomfort.

"Maya, we're all performing together anyway. I can handle fetching water."

"But they're treating you poorly."

That was true, but Kaiden didn't want to cause any trouble here.

'If my right hand moves, it'll be a disaster.'

The biggest issue was the legal changes that had been happening to him for the past few hours.

'It's not clear yet. But if I swing my sword now, it might not have the same power as before.'

'There's no need to make unnecessary enemies.'

With that thought, he handed over the water bottle, and El Kiana looked him up and down.

"Hmph."

She snatched the bottle and said, "You're lucky. The people here, even royalty, have to wait months to see me. And I destroy the bottles I drink from in front of everyone. Who knows what you might do with it…"

"El Kiana." Just as Maya was about to argue again, the door opened, and a stern-looking man entered.

Even the overwhelmingly famous El Kiana couldn't hide her tense expression upon seeing him.

'Panier? Is he in charge?'

As the world's top performance planner, he had produced countless musicians, including El Kiana.

Everyone in the industry knew him, so the rehearsal room's atmosphere froze instantly.

Panier slowly scanned the artists' faces and spoke in a cold voice.

"What are you doing? Not practicing?"

'Still the same.'

Those familiar with Panier's notorious reputation gathered on stage without a word.

Even Reina took a deep breath to calm herself as the finale song began.

There were five divas, each with a unique voice blending together.

'Duet part.'

El Kiana and Maya faced each other, decorating the climax of the song.

Their eyes were filled with tenderness, as if they had never had an emotional conflict. Reina analyzed objectively.

'El Kiana. Her resonance is incredible. Her emotional expression is top-notch, and above all, her voice is outstanding.'

If Maya had an edge over El Kiana, it was in her slightly more delicate emotional expression.

'Of course, Maya's technique is excellent too…'

None of the divas gathered here were lacking in technical skill.

At the end of the song, El Kiana's calm, monologue-like voice permeated the air.

Panier's eyebrows twitched.

"Hmm."

The performance was qualitatively different from ten minutes ago, but all the artists watched his reaction.

"Well done. You've practiced a lot."

"Haah."

El Kiana looked up at the ceiling, and the others sighed in relief.

'We really did well.'

His standards were notoriously strict, but he wasn't unreasonable.

"The flow is good. Let's change the concept in a few sections. It feels like the impact is leaking."

Wearing pince-nez glasses, he checked the names on the sheet music and matched them to faces one by one.

"Hmm, Shinbi Maya?"

"Yes."

"You captured the emotional contrast well. I'd like to hear El Kiana's final part in your voice too."

"Wait a minute!"

El Kiana raised her hand out of habit from her childhood.

"Changing that part changes the roles too. Doesn't that alter the interpretation of the song?"

"That's how it should be. What's the problem? I'll decide after hearing it."

That's why she was upset.

'I admit Maya's emotional expression is good. But to experiment to the point of changing the interpretation…'

She didn't think she had done poorly.

"Did I make a mistake? Just tell me, and I'll fix it."

"El Kiana."

Panier took off his glasses.

"Don't be arrogant."

She felt like she was back in her lesson days, unable to move a finger.

"Just because you made some money, you think you're invincible? Do you think you became famous because you're great?"

His gaze swept over the artists.

"Society determines what abilities are needed. In the past, someone who caught rabbits well was a genius. But now? Just a hunter."

"El Kiana, you told me you wanted to be the most famous in the world."

"…Yes."

"If you still think you're great and want to sing your own songs, I'll give you the chance now."

El Kiana bowed her head and stepped back.

"No, thank you."

"Why not?"

Maya's sudden question made the artists turn their heads in surprise.

Panier put his glasses back on.

"What did you say?"

"I understand what you're saying, Mr. Panier, but you're pushing too hard. Isn't everyone working hard because they want to create their own art?"

El Kiana whispered.

"Hey, are you crazy?"

Arguing with a master offering a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity.

'What's wrong with her?'

Contrary to the artists' worries, Panier looked at Maya with calm eyes.

'Their own art…'

He remembered a man who loved music and guitar but was clumsy in life.

"Troubling."

In a secret room in the Keshia Kingdom sector, Fermi checked the information on the Apocalypse.

When the law changes, the content of relics also changes, but some information remained the same.

Opening a triple-secured box, Fermi read the contents of a memo.

4, as expected…

A short phrase flashed on his retina.

'This hasn't changed.'

Thinking of Shirone's face for a moment, Fermi shook his head and took a deep breath.

"Let's get to work."

Wearing a magnifying glass, he picked up tweezers and began piecing together scraps of paper on the desk like a puzzle.

'Cell Buster.'

One of the worst future events.

'Hmm?'

A piece of paper with a name written on it fit almost perfectly next to the torn part.

'This guy is one of the Seven Magical Sins…'

Guitaruman.
