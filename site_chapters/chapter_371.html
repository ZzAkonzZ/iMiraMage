Chapter 371: Iron Gate (1)

The day of the magic school's opening arrived.

Shirone woke up early, folded his blanket, and packed his things.

The Kubrick ring was convenient for everyday use. He distributed various items across its six slots and placed Armand in a separate slot, just in case.

As he came downstairs, Olina greeted him with a smile.

"Are you all set? Where's your bag?"

"I've got it right here."

Shirone showed her the ring on his palm.

Olina, looking at her son's playful face, didn't ask any questions and turned away.

Since the Kazra incident, she had realized her son was a mage.

"Come eat quickly, or you'll be late."

Shirone watched his mother for a long time. She was the only one in the world, his mother.

"I love you, Mom. And thank you."

When Shirone hugged her waist, Olina's hand paused.

At home, he was a doting only child. But a mother's intuition sensed the heavy burden in her son's voice.

'Something is happening again... God, please protect this child.'

Regaining her smile, Olina turned and playfully smacked Shirone's head.

"You rascal."

"Ouch!"

"You're too old to be acting like a baby. And don't touch a lady's belly so casually."

Vincent, having finished washing his face, wiped it with a towel and said,

"That's right! Shirone, I won't give up Mom to anyone. So hurry up and find your own Amy at school..."

"Stop babbling and eat your breakfast."

"Yes, ma'am."

Vincent marched to the table like a soldier. Shirone's laughter filled the kitchen. He wished this happiness would last forever.

Arriving at school in the Ogent family's carriage, Shirone saw the front gate crowded with people.

It was the start of a new semester, and parents were bending down to scold their undergrown children.

As the announcement for the entrance ceremony began, many students rushed past Shirone.

But Shirone was in no hurry. The graduation event was at 2 PM at the Iron Gate, and his dormitory was already arranged, so he planned to spend time with his friends at the research club.

"Hello?"

Hearing Amy's voice, Shirone turned with a smile, but his expression quickly changed to surprise.

"W-What?"

"Why are you so shocked? It's like you've never seen a red-haired girl before."

Amy's red hair was cut to shoulder length. It framed her cheeks and was styled asymmetrically in the front, making her look completely different.

Amy twisted her lips as if holding back laughter and ran her fingers through her hair.

"How do I look?"

"Uh, well... you look pretty."

She playfully hit Shirone's shoulder and laughed.

"Is that all? Do I seem more determined?"

"Uh, yeah. You look sharper."

"Hmm, really?"

Amy, looking satisfied, got lost in her thoughts before snapping back.

"How did it go? Did you finish your work at the association?"

"Yeah. I asked about Heaven and did some tours."

Shirone gave a vague answer, avoiding any lies. Even with his closest friend, he couldn't reveal sensitive information.

Amy smiled subtly and didn't press further.

After two months, she seemed more confident.

"It suits you, but why did you cut your hair?"

"Just felt like it. It was annoying during training, and I wanted a fresh start."

Amy was a strong candidate for graduation, having ranked fifth in last year's senior class.

To break the second-year jinx, she seemed to be starting anew.

"You're early. The event is at 2 PM. Going to meet the idiot duo?"

"Yeah. I'm meeting Iruki and Neide to go up together. What about you?"

Amy pointed her thumb at the distant uphill path.

"I'm heading to the Iron Gate. Got to prepare to welcome you all. Oh, and make sure to say 'me' later."

"Me?"

When Shirone pointed to himself, Amy shook her head.

"No, not you. Just say 'me.' Got it? Just say 'me.'"

"What's that supposed to mean?"

"You'll see at the event. You'll be really grateful to me. See you at 2 PM at the Iron Gate."

Amy winked and walked away. Her personality seemed brighter with the new hairstyle, but Shirone still wasn't used to it.

As she walked away, she turned and said,

"Welcome to hell."

"Haha! Thanks a lot."

Amy giggled and walked off, clearly amused.

Shirone shook his head, smiling.

'Finally, we're standing in the same place.'

He understood why Amy was in such a good mood.

Supernatural Psychic Science Research Club.

Iruki stared at the chalkboard filled with equations, lost in thought.

He had finally reached his senior year. But honestly, he had just ended up here somehow.

"Becoming a mage? Me?"

He always thought he could come here whenever he wanted. At least, that's what Iruki believed.

What had held him back was the fear of how his monstrous intellect would be used in the world.

"In the end, I followed Shirone here."

It wasn't a bad feeling. Even with the graduation exam a year away, he felt calm because Shirone was like a giant control mechanism installed in his brain.

"Yeah, Shirone is meant to be a mage. I wonder if he's preparing well."

Honestly, there was nothing to worry about with Shirone. With a clear goal, he would never stop.

"But Neide..."

He probably hadn't done anything again.

He was likely holed up in a storage room, tinkering with strange devices all day.

It was unfortunate, but there was nothing to be done. Neide didn't want to become a mage.

"Has it really been six years?"

Six years ago, Iruki was expelled from the Royal Magic School and transferred to Alpheas Magic School.

His personality was still difficult, so no one wanted to associate with him.

Iruki also had no interest in making friends. But one person caught his eye—Neide.

A boy who always sat at the back of the classroom, glaring at the chalkboard with a fierce look.

"Hmm, an interesting guy."

After observing Neide for a few days, Iruki shamelessly asked his classmates about him.

"Neide? I don't know him well. He doesn't talk. His grades are okay, I guess."

"I don't like Neide. He's creepy. Kind of perverted."

"Why are you curious about Neide? It's best to leave him alone. His eyes are too scary."

Iruki could roughly guess what Neide's school life was like.

A few days later, Neide approached Iruki's desk with a cold look.

Iruki raised his hand casually.

"Hi. What's up?"

"Are you the one snooping around about me?"

If you have a long tail, it gets stepped on. Rumors about Iruki asking about Neide had reached him, so he came directly.

"Snooping? Why would I investigate your butt?"

Someone snickered, and Neide's fist flew into Iruki's jaw.

Iruki fell with his chair and couldn't move.

"I'll ask again. Are you the one snooping around about me?"

"Spit."

Iruki spat out blood and slowly got up.

But it was a feint. He immediately lunged, trying to poke Neide's eyes with two fingers.

"Ahcho!"

Iruki's arm stretched out, stopping just in front of Neide's eyes as he leaned back.

Even so, Neide's expression didn't change.

"......"

Iruki withdrew his arm and took a stance mimicking a snake, ready to fight.

The atmosphere quickly chilled, and the lower-grade students turned pale.

"Hoo."

Neide sighed, looking at Iruki's ridiculous stance.

He didn't know how this idiot got into magic school, but it was best to avoid him.

"I'm warning you, don't take an interest in me again. I won't let it slide next time."

"I don't want to. Why should I listen to you?"

A fierce look flashed in Neide's eyes.

"You... will really die."

"I know. I'm human, so I'll die someday. Are you stupid?"

Neide heard the sound of his reason snapping. He didn't punch immediately because he might kill Iruki.

"Meet me after class. Come to the place I mentioned."

"Oh, is this the after-school duel I've heard about? But are you sure? I'm pretty strong."

Neide didn't respond and turned away.

If Iruki was too scared to show up, that was fine.

But if he did show up... Iruki wouldn't be able to attend school from the next day.

"Ouch, that punch was strong."

Iruki set his chair upright and sat down, rubbing his lips.

Class started, but Neide didn't return.

The weather was gloomy, and by the end of class, gray clouds covered the sky.

Iruki rubbed his swollen cheek and looked out the window.

"It's going to rain."

Lightning flashed, followed by thunder that tore through the sky.

In the torrential rain, lightning and explosions chased each other relentlessly.

"Huff, huff."

Iruki lowered his head, breathing heavily. The rain pounding on his head felt hot, and water dripped from his soaked hair.

Only his eyes were alive, piercing through the darkness of the rain.

Through the curtain of rain, Neide's silhouette appeared.

As the lightning flickered, parts of the silhouette regained color, but the monstrous face he saw at first never fully emerged.

"This is serious."

Iruki had to admit his calculations were off.

He knew Neide wasn't ordinary, but this was beyond normal.

Iruki's voice cut through the rain.

"What happened to you?"

Splashing through the water, Neide approached. The terrifying expression was gone, replaced by a look of resignation.

"What is it? Why are you interested in me?"

"It's not interest, it's exploration. I've been looking for something recently."

"Looking for what?"

"As you can see, I'm a genius. A real, extraordinary genius. Depending on how my abilities are used, they could become a disaster. I might even kill many people. So, I need someone who can control me."

Neide neither laughed nor took it seriously.

"Kill many people?"

"You should know. With my abilities, it's entirely possible."

"Ridiculous."

Neide spoke coldly.

"Have you ever killed anyone?"

A massive thunderclap shook the world.

Iruki turned away from the chalkboard with a bitter smile.

The opponent he had fought to the brink of death had now become his closest friend.

Of course, Neide's personality softened after that incident and some time had passed.

But at least from that moment, Neide accepted Iruki as a friend.

"Hey, comrade. How long has it been?"

Neide burst through the door, stood straight, and saluted. The memories from six years ago made Iruki chuckle.
