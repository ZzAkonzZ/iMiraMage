Chapter 554:

D-36 Days.

From the day after Shirone's sequence method surpassed 1 quadrillion, his skills grew at an astonishing rate.

With his eyes wide open, the numbers he expanded from 1 accelerated endlessly, entering the quadrillion range.

'2 quadrillion!'

Although he was counting numbers, it no longer felt like he was reading them.

It was as if the numbers flowed endlessly through his thoughts, like blood through his veins.

'3 quadrillion!'

Through a great realization that allowed him to take the first step into a new realm, Shirone's numbers raced without end.

'4 quadrillion!'

As the numbers grew, so did the overwhelming emotions, and Miro, who was observing the training, felt a moment of confusion.

'Huh? What's this?'

Shirone's incarnation took the form of an angel with wings of light.

As the sequence method progressed, the light's afterimage disappeared, and the outline became clearer.

However, after surpassing 4 quadrillion, the outline began to distort, and parts of the form even collapsed.

'Is the change starting now?'

When one reaches the state of unity of mind and body, the spirit of Prajna can influence the laws of objects.

But just the collapse of the form didn't give any clue about the uniqueness of Shirone's incarnation.

"Gasp!"

Shirone, who exhaled roughly, trembled with a shocked expression and fell to his knees.

Miro approached him and asked, "How far did you get?"

"I stopped at 5 quadrillion."

Exactly half.

'A psychological brake.'

He had internalized a technique of the sequence method that could be mastered at the quadrillion level and quickly surpassed 4 quadrillion, but the thought of reaching only half of the goal created a new limit.

"As I said before, you mustn't be obsessed. Only flexible thinking like water can break limits."

"Yes."

Of course, if it were as easy as saying it, there would be no failures in the world.

"I'll try again."

Miro was satisfied that Shirone no longer complained.

'The desire for success has surpassed the fear of failure.'

Failure, failure, endless repetition of failure.

But if you don't give up, it's not failure but countless experiences needed to reach success.

'You're doing well, Shirone. No one who hasn't fallen can walk.'

He wasn't training to earn sweet titles like 'genius mage' or 'monstrously powerful mage.'

'Fail countless times. It's not shameful. It's something to be proud of.'

A 'trained' mage who can handle any variable.

'That's your pride and the highest honor a professional can have.'

Shirone's nose wrinkled as he focused intensely on the world of numbers.

'I will become a mage! I will definitely become one!'

D-34 Days.

Dorothy's room was hot with the heat of anger and dizzy with the smell of pleasure.

On the bed, Dorothy lay curled up under the blanket, and a sound that was hard to distinguish between joy and crying seeped through the cotton barrier.

"Ah! Ah!"

Next to the bed, the scrap metal doll Hickory lay completely shattered.

"Ugh! Ugh!"

She thought it was almost complete, but at the last moment, all sorts of errors erupted.

'Why now! Why do they all have to happen at once!'

Her anger reached its peak, and her mind twisted.

"Aaah! Ugh!"

As the blanket, curled up like a grub, shook violently, the door suddenly opened, and her younger sister entered.

"Hey! What was that noise just now? Did you break something again?"

The blanket stopped moving, and her sister, looking around the room, frowned as if she had expected this.

"Seriously, psycho! What have you been doing all vacation without showing your face? Do you even know how worried Mom is?"

As the nagging continued, Dorothy uncurled and sat up on her knees.

"…Get out."

Her face, wrapped in the blanket, was covered in cold sweat.

"Before you tell me to get out, you should be quiet! Think about the people living next door! All day in bed…"

"Get out!"

Her sister flinched at the loud scream.

Her sister was always abnormal, but this hysteria was no joke.

"Crazy…"

As her sister left the room in a hurry, closing the door, Dorothy covered herself with the blanket again and curled up.

"Ugh! Aaah!"

The sound of her anguished groans escaped from under the blanket.

D-23 Days.

For 20 minutes, electricity had been sparking from Iruki's eyes.

As the vast amount of data calculating nuclear fusion backflowed into his brain, the blood vessels in his eyes began to burst.

Even as he shed bloody tears, Iruki's mouth was smiling.

'I can do it. I can because I must.'

If you can't succeed, keep knocking until you do.

Data began to crash into the iron wall blocking the perfect equation at an incredible speed.

'It will eventually open.'

Even if he couldn't open it, it didn't matter.

The already successful version of himself beyond the iron door would open it and come out to greet him.

D-21 Days.

'Why? Why can't I progress from here?'

Amy's face twisted with anger as she rotated the Spirit Zone in sniper mode.

For days, her sniping accuracy had not risen above 90%.

Checking her state backed up in the red-eye, Amy found the stagnation point in her psychological data.

'Stupid. You're satisfied.'

She couldn't reach perfection because her subconscious felt it was unnatural.

'My thinking is rigid! I need to destroy myself!'

As she rotated the Spirit Zone, the 2-kilometer sniper zone spun at an incredible speed.

Every time she fired a Fire Strike at the target, the air groaned.

The flame accelerated through the air tunnel reached the target in just 1.3 seconds.

'If it's not 100%, it doesn't mean anything!'

Dozens of targets exploded simultaneously.

D-19 Days.

"Good, Screamer! Smash it!"

"Aaah! Aaah! Aaah!"

Screamer screamed in pain, but his body showed no signs of stopping.

He had reached a runner's high.

Pyroca, who was watching his son unleash a flurry of punches, muttered in a hoarse voice, "Yes, Screamer. That's it…"

"What? What did you say?" Screamer, who hadn't heard, snapped irritably, but Pyroca was simply proud of his son.

'You've finally surpassed it.'

Wanting to defeat someone, to trample on the defeated and feel superior, has its limits.

'No, you can't become strong that way.'

Such base pleasures are too easily crushed by the immense pain that must be endured in the future.

'Screamer, do you know why so many people risk their lives to compete?'

Because they don't want to lose.

Because they can't accept the fact that they couldn't achieve something, they endure any pain.

'Become a mage, Screamer! Become a champion! That will be the greatest happiness in your life!'

"Dad! What are you doing!"

Pyroca snapped out of it and wiped his tears.

In a state as painful as death, he wouldn't be able to endure without some encouragement.

"Good, Screamer! You've finally got him on the ropes! The champion is completely out of it! Finish him off!"

"Aaah!"

D-14 Days.

The psychological brake of reaching half of 10 quadrillion didn't disappear easily.

The sequence method still lingered at the limit of 5 quadrillion, and several days passed in that state.

'It's okay. I'm doing my best.'

Shirone wasn't anxious.

If he couldn't do it, he couldn't do it, and that was the cold reality he had to face.

"Haha!"

Sitting on a rock by the stream, Shirone suddenly burst into laughter.

He noticed that while most of the leaves on the thirty-second branch of the tree across the stream hung down, one leaf pointed upward.

'That's really funny.'

The fact that he could laugh at the direction of a leaf that no one else would care about was proof that his thinking had become more flexible.

'The spider has built a big web. Good for you.'

A spider web, which must have been destroyed five times by rain and wind during the training period, was strung between the branches.

'Really persistent. That's right, it was windy last night too.'

The sight of the spider stretching its thin legs and moving around its web made him feel happy.

'You endured.'

At that moment, his body tingled, and he made a tearful face.

"Huh? What?"

Overwhelmed by an uncontrollable wave of emotion, tears streamed down his face.

'Ah, I see.'

Nothing is insignificant.

'Good for you. Good for you, spider.'

Even a mere creature, a spider that others find repulsive and don't even look at, has a story as grand as his own if you look inside.

'There is a way in everything.'

As Shirone's Spirit Zone opened, the angelic incarnation began to emerge.

With Elysion activated, the boundary between the world and himself disappeared, and the sequence method raced at an incredible speed, forgetting even Miro's instructions.

3 quadrillion, 4 quadrillion, 5 quadrillion.

As the limits he thought he had crumbled, the numbers piled up to a higher level.

'6 quadrillion! 7 quadrillion!'

As the angelic incarnation further collapsed, Miro, watching from a distance, smiled.

'Everything in the world converges to life.'

No state, no transcendence can surpass living.

'You want to become a mage. That too is a grand life incomparable to others.'

Thinking that he might reach it in time, Miro observed the changes in the incarnation.

'What is this?'

Miro's smiling face suddenly hardened.

As the sequence method, which had been racing at 7 quadrillion, entered 8 quadrillion, the form of the incarnation completely collapsed, and particles of light began to swirl in numerous vortices.

The dazzling sight was overwhelming, but it couldn't be called beautiful.

It was a bizarre mirage that felt honestly grotesque.

'No, it's not at that level. It would be the same even if I stood before the sun.'

Feeling it was grotesque was a human standard.

Whatever uniqueness the incarnation had, it was a phenomenon that transcended all human experience, making it feel frightening.

'What kind of singularity is this?'

As if space-time was distorting, the scenery refracted, and numerous afterimages overlapped.

'The change is becoming clear.'

Having realized with his body that there is a way in everything, it was safe to say that he was close to the state of unity of mind and body.

'Just a little more.'

As curious about Shirone's incarnation as he was, Miro was cheering for the sequence method when suddenly the light mirage disappeared as if it were a lie.

"Gasp! Gasp!"

Shirone, who had come out of his trance, was breathing heavily and quickly turned around, sensing Miro's presence.

"Did you see that? I broke through! I reached 9 quadrillion!"

"9 quadrillion… The demon of perfection."

Contrary to his expectation of praise, Miro bit her lip, and Shirone realized he had disobeyed instructions.

"Oh, I'm sorry. I did the sequence method without realizing…"

"No, you did well. Even if you broke through 5 quadrillion, I thought you'd hit another wall. Let's do our best to succeed in the remaining time."

"Yes!"

Gaining confidence, Shirone walked energetically to the training hall.

Meanwhile, Miro remained in place, looking back at the rock where Shirone had been sitting.

It was a situation where they were almost there, making it even more regrettable, but wanting more was greedy.

'We'll find out soon enough. But what kind of incarnation is it?'

The change in the laws revealed when approaching the singularity was a mysterious phenomenon that even the strongest Prajna, Miro, couldn't analyze.
