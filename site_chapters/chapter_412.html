Chapter 412:

Shirone and his companions followed Mahatu through a complex maze.

Upon learning that he was a messenger of the gods, the warriors all knelt, and even after entering the communal area, they were warmly welcomed by the tribespeople.

Unlike Kadum, who offered massive tributes to the messenger of the gods to gain eternal life, Mahatu transparently managed Kergo's funds.

The lives of the tribespeople improved, and their faces were full of vitality.

Although Shirone hadn't fought for Kergo, opening Goffin's Gate and heading to Heaven made him their savior.

"I'd love to hold a festival, but it seems you're in a hurry."

"Yes. We need to get to Heaven as soon as possible."

Mahatu didn't ask about the situation in Heaven.

The Kergos still worship Anke La and revere the angels, but what mattered most to him was the well-being of his people.

Sein asked, "How many are waiting? I heard it's at full capacity."

Anyone could go to Heaven if an unlocker opened Goffin's Gate, but Miro created the Hall of Achievement and Sacrifice to prevent indiscriminate travel to Heaven, allowing only chosen warriors to pass.

If it was at full capacity, it meant many were waiting for an unlocker to arrive.

Mahatu awkwardly licked his lips.

"Actually, there aren't that many. About seven."

"That's strange. Then why block entry?"

"Well... it's not something I can explain. But you'll understand when you see it."

Gaold didn't care.

The only thing he needed in Kergo was Goffin's Gate.

If there were skilled people among those waiting to go to Heaven, he would take them, but matching the high level of his team would be difficult.

"Fine. Let's go see and get it over with."

Mahatu quickened his pace, showing his sincerity.

They arrived at a massive iron door engraved with a giant's carving.

"This is the Hall of Warriors. The warriors who wish to go to Heaven are waiting here."

When Mahatu opened the door, Gaold entered immediately, followed by the others.

The seven warriors Mahatu mentioned were scattered around, watching Gaold sharply.

A swordsman sitting with a longsword asked, "Are you a warrior or an angel?"

Gaold didn't respond. After assessing the seven, he turned to Sein and said, "They need to be at least certified 6th level. If not, they're not worth evaluating."

A mage in the corner frowned.

"You talk as if you're already a certified 6th level."

"And if I am?"

"Haha! Is that why you're so arrogant? I've killed countless certified mages. Evaluating skill by rank is outdated. Life and death are determined by how much hell you've experienced."

The mage revealed five rings on his right hand and stepped forward.

The swordsman holding his sword spoke irritably.

"Hey, I thought I was first?"

"Doesn't matter. Anyone with skill can go. Isn't that the rule?"

The mage standing in front of Gaold beckoned him with his right hand.

"I'll show you the level beyond ranks."

Gaold pointed his finger at him.

With a bang, the air gun fired, and the mage's body slammed into the wall. Spitting blood, he collapsed to the floor, unable to move.

"Next."

Shirone frowned.

Gaold hadn't intended to kill, but Shirone knew the power of Gaold's air gun better than anyone.

"I also agree with what that man said."

An elderly mage stretched his neck and walked to the center.

"There is a level beyond ranks. But without skill, theory is just delusion. I'm a bit different..."

As the mage's face twisted to one side, Gaold, who had teleported, struck his face with the back of his hand.

The mage's head wobbled like a punchball before he collapsed like a log.

"Next."

The five remaining people, having seen two fall in an instant, didn't move.

This wasn't a team dreaming of quick riches on the outskirts of Heaven.

It was clearly a task force with business in Heaven.

When no one stepped forward, Gaold pointed at the swordsman sitting with his sword.

"Hey, didn't you say something about being first?"

The swordsman glared at Gaold with his mouth shut.

Then he suddenly relaxed and raised his hands in surrender.

"Haha, I give up. Acknowledging skill is also... Ugh!"

Gaold, who had rushed like a dog, punched him.

As the press magic exploded on the swordsman's jaw, his face slid across the floor in the direction he was hit.

"All talk, no action."

The atmosphere in the room rapidly cooled.

Gaold glanced at the remaining four, then turned his head towards the door.

"Get out. If you don't want to die."

Without hesitation, the four scrambled out the door.

With only the unconscious and Shirone's group left, a chilly air filled the Hall of Warriors.

Gaold dusted off his hands and turned to the door.

"That's enough. Nice and clean."

"There's still one more."

Mahatu entered the Hall of Warriors.

While his skill was certainly above those present, Gaold still thought going to Heaven was too much for him.

"What? Are you challenging too?"

"No. Actually..."

Mahatu hesitated, then looked outside the door.

"There is another messenger of the gods. He asked not to let any more warriors in..."

"Huh?"

As Mahatu made way, a familiar man entered.

It was Armin of the Radiant Eyes, the eternal contemplator.

"Oh."

To catch a big fish, you need good bait.

Siena was perfect for catching Armin.

Siena's eyes turned cold.

How long would he treat her like a child?

But when she saw Armin's face, her words came out less harsh than she intended.

"Why are you here? Did you forget my request?"

"No, I remember. I didn't come to interfere in your life. I'm here to keep a promise to myself."

Gaold spoke.

"Sorry, but that's not possible. We don't work with the White Line. They're too unpredictable."

"I've left the Ivory Tower. Isn't that enough?"

Siena's eyes widened.

If he had thrown away the magical utopia that only the world's greatest mages could enter, his resolve was no ordinary one.

"Why? And what about Keira?"

Armin didn't explain the details. His expression briefly showed a hint of heaviness.

"Siena, nothing in the world is more important than you. Heaven is a dangerous place. I can't let you go alone. Not for the sake of the Olifer School that raised me."

"Siena is a certified 5th-level mage."

"Mr. Kuan..."

Siena was surprised that the usually quiet Kuan spoke up.

"I can guess your relationship, but it seems you're underestimating her. And you don't need to worry about Siena's life. No one will die before I do."

A mage's position is to survive until the end of the party.

Regardless of skill, it was a matter of pride for a swordsman if they died first.

'Kuan...'

The sword demon who had cut down two members of the continent's strongest swordsmanship group, Feng Zhang.

Such a person seemed to think highly of Siena.

In a situation where they had to follow the madman Gaold, it was a comforting fact, but as her brother, he couldn't help feeling hurt.

'Then again, what right do I have...'

Except for Siena, everyone welcomed Armin's inclusion.

The master of time magic who had conquered the speed of light.

Especially his stop magic, the world's strongest crowd control.

"Fine, if you're not with the White Line, you're in. But I'll be in command. If you disobey, you're out."

Armin had no complaints.

With Siena as a hostage, fighting for the team was the best option.

Thus, the final team of ten was assembled to reclaim Miro, with Heaven in their sights.

'Wow, seeing them all together is really impressive.'

Shirone couldn't believe he was part of this team.

A task force of the best talents from the White, Red, and Black Lines.

Mahatu provided the remaining rooms.

The ten members, each with their own room, had dinner and gathered in the largest room for a meeting.

The time to open Goffin's Gate was set for 6 AM the next day.

Considering the association's pursuit, leaving immediately was an option, but they would need to sleep first anyway.

Since resting in Heaven could be a battle, it was better to spend the night here.

Gaold spread the map Kangnam had given him on the table.

It was a spray chart of Heaven, with colored dots marking various zones.

"Heaven is currently undergoing a great upheaval. There's a revolution among the citizens, and rebels are active. This is a real-time threat map showing zones of level 2 and above. Well, not real-time, since it's from three months ago."

Gaold's loyalists, who had been on an espionage mission in Heaven, sent this map as their last communication.

Sein spoke.

"After confirming Istas, we need to revise our strategy. The priority is to check Miro's condition. Depending on the situation, changing the coordinates of the Akashic Records might be meaningless."

Etella spoke.

"If Heaven kidnapped Miro, shouldn't we reassess the enemy's state? It means they're already prepared."

"Right. A preemptive strike through infiltration is difficult. We'll have to see the situation, but we'll use a strategy of causing as much chaos as possible before infiltrating."

Armin spoke.

"Causing chaos in Heaven? There aren't many ways to do that. Are you thinking of using the rebels?"

"That's definitely something to keep in mind. But the rebels won't move easily. That's why we need the ancient weapon, Tagis. If we can neutralize Heaven's interception system, Aegis, they might reconsider."

Armin shook his head.

"No. Even if we break through Aegis, the chances of the rebels launching a full-scale war are slim. Most of them were once citizens of Heaven, so they know its power better than anyone. If Mecca and Nor were united, Heaven couldn't ignore their strength, but the problem is fear. Deep-seated fear isn't easily forgotten."

"Fear can be removed. Anyway, Heaven's situation will be far from what we expect. We'll adapt as we go. We have the masterpiece, after all."

Siena asked, "What do you mean by 'masterpiece'?"

Sein looked at Shirone.

"It's you, Shirone. If you succeed, it could have the same effect as mobilizing the entire rebel force. So, let's hear it. How exactly do you plan to blow up Heaven?"

With everyone's eyes suddenly on him, Shirone swallowed nervously.

He had to explain this before they left for Heaven, but the atmosphere was different from when he told Gaold.

From now on, every word would be part of their strategy.

Gaold nudged him, as if to reassure him.

"Hehe, no need to hesitate, Shirone. From what I see, it's a brilliantly insane spell."

'That's what makes it even more unsettling...'
