Chapter 784:

The place Amy arrived at was the parade ground, within sight of the female recruits’ dormitory.

On the other side of an iron fence, male recruits were packed together, but they were too far away to make out clearly.

“Are we training separately?” If so, it was a bit disappointing—she had expected more from the Valkyries.

“The commander is entering!”

A massive man, his face marked with dozens of scars like badges of honor, walked in with a drill instructor by his side.

“Salute the commander!”

As salute commands in various languages rang out from the recruits, a few of them remained still.

Either way was acceptable.

Even so, Commander Gomez of the Valkyrie recruit unit growled like a tiger.

“What a bunch of disorganized fools! Huh? You’re all a mess!”

Among the nearly 100 women standing in formation, not a single one showed signs of displeasure.

“From now on, you’re going to experience brutal gender discrimination! And why? Because I’m a raging misogynist! Simple as that—I’m a man!”

Gomez shoved his face right in front of the first recruit.

“Does that piss you off? Huh? Answer me!”

“No, sir!”

A sharp, clear voice rang out.

“You’re out! Disqualified! No pride at all, huh? What’s someone like you doing crawling into a warzone?”

Before the first recruit could even respond, Gomez moved to the next in line.

“What about you? Does it piss you off?”

“Yes, sir! It does!”

“Shut up! So what if it does? I’m your commanding officer, and you’re just a grunt! If it sucks so much, you become a commander! Then go ahead and discriminate against all the men! Got it?!”

“Yes, sir!”

Gomez turned toward the instructor.

“You all know this already, but this place is hell! Especially for you women! I’m not just some garden-variety male supremacist—I’ve got a full-blown rage disorder on top of that!”

He tore open a box containing recommendation letters and waved a thick stack of them at the recruits.

“You know what this is? Royal recommendation letters with the king’s seal. Of course I didn’t read a single one! What, you think this is kindergarten? You toss me a scrap of paper and expect me to care?!”

With that, he shredded the recommendation letters over his head.

“Don’t need this crap! You know what demons are? They’re exactly like me! The moment they see someone soft and pretty like you, they lose their minds and want to rip you apart! So either rip them to shreds, or—!”

In the middle of his tirade, Gomez suddenly froze, staring at someone with a baffled expression.

“Hey. You.”

At first, no one reacted.

“You! Red hair!”

Amy turned her head and realized he was pointing at her. She stepped forward.

“Recruit Carmis Amy, reporting!”

After a long silence, Gomez asked:

“…Why are you wearing makeup?”

Amy was briefly flustered but quickly regained her composure and lifted her chin.

“I’m not wearing any, sir!”

“Oh yeah?”

Gomez moved in close and scrutinized her face.

“No makeup, huh? Incredible! The demons are gonna love you! What are you doing here, trying to seduce me with that face?!”

“No, sir!”

Sparks lit up in Gomez’s eyes as he swung his fist, landing a blow square on Amy’s face.

She collapsed to the ground, trying to grasp the situation—but her body rose instinctively before her mind could catch up.

“I’ll ask again! Are you trying to seduce me?!”

Amy had come to become a general.

“No, sir!”

Gomez’s face twisted in a sneer as he hit her again, harder than before.

“Get up!”

Amy bounced back up like a punching doll.

“One last time! You’d better answer right if you don’t want to be the first one eliminated! Are you trying to seduce me?!”

His voice exploded, practically a roar of rage.

“No, sir!”

She clenched her jaw, one eye squeezed shut from the impact. Gomez stormed back to his spot, shouting as he walked.

“Listen up from now on! There’s only one thing I want from you—just die! That’s our job!”

The recruits snapped to full attention.

“Who the hell wants to die? That’s why we’re needed! We get paid to die for them! That’s what it means to be a soldier! So do your job! Death is the highest honor!”

All 100 women had fought fiercely to make it here, but their eyes now burned differently than when they first arrived.

“That’s all! Head to your quarters and receive your supplies! Once you finish lunch, the official induction ceremony begins!”

As Gomez made his way to where the male recruits were, the instructor followed behind and asked,

“What do you think, sir? I was worried about the quality of foreign recruits, but after reading their recommendation letters, they seem far better than expected.”

“What do you think I’d say? War is the enemy, not people. You think girls like that came here because they’re just dying to die?”

Even Gomez, for all his bluster, was a loving husband and father of two daughters at home.

“These are the future backbone of the Valkyries. Fighting demons is nothing like fighting humans. Drill them until it makes their eyes bleed. Because if we don’t—”

A deadly glint flashed in Gomez’s eyes.

“—they’ll be the ones bleeding on the battlefield.”

Once the instructors left, the women finally relaxed, releasing long-held breaths.

“I was curious what a global military would be like. Turns out it’s nothing special.”

“Discipline can only go so far. What kind of training we get—that’s what matters.”

Amy, rubbing her sore, swollen cheek, quietly listened to the other recruits’ chatter.

“I saw in the last Valkyrie recruitment letter—they’re accepting not just officers and noncoms, but even general-level candidates. It’s a great chance to learn under the best.”

As expected, Amy was steeling her resolve, thinking there wasn’t a single weak person among them—when suddenly, someone tapped her on the shoulder.

“Wha…!”

Her eyes widened at the sight of a familiar and unexpected face.

“Tess! You applied too?”

Tess of the Elzine family.

Rian’s friend, and part of the trio alongside Amy and Arin during their time in Heaven, was now beaming with a bright smile.

“Wow, Amy. You really made an impression on day one, huh?”

Amy let out a bitter laugh.

“Yeah… I think I’ve been marked already.”

“That guy’s totally a pervert. If he’d hit you one more time, I would’ve stepped in and slammed him into the ground.”

Knowing Tess, she absolutely meant it.

“It’s fine. At least it was a punch. If he’d slapped me… I probably would’ve flattened him too.”

I’ll become stronger.

If she rose high enough—became a general—then she could meet Shirone without shame.

Seeing the quiet intensity in Amy’s voice, Tess gave her a warm smile, sensing her resolve.

“Yeah. Let’s both pass and show them what we’re made of.”

Then Amy blinked, realizing something.

“But what about your swordsmanship school? What made you suddenly apply to the Valkyries?”

Tess’s expression darkened.

“My father died. He was guarding a colony during the last altar opening… He was killed by the demon army.”

Amy felt the ground fall away from beneath her.

“Oh…”

“It’s okay. I’m not sad. He just did his duty as a commander of an occupied territory. But I can’t forgive the demons. That’s why I applied as a non-commissioned officer after I saw the notice.”

“A non-com? Not an officer?”

With Tess’s swordsmanship, she could’ve easily entered the Valkyrie Army as an officer.

She wants to fight, Amy realized.

She wanted revenge—for her father.

“What about you, Amy? Officer?”

“Yeah. The Valkyrie magic corps doesn’t take non-coms. But officer or not, we’re still going to be deployed the same.”

Since magic was considered a specialized field, its units were smaller than regular infantry.

Promotion came faster, but even a lieutenant colonel often ended up in frontline melee combat.

“By the way…”

Tess hesitated before asking.

“Have you heard from Rian?”

“No. Total radio silence. Even the Ogent family doesn’t seem to know anything. He hasn’t contacted you either?”

Tess answered only with silence.

“How heartless. He could’ve at least sent word…”

Rian was probably the only one who could ease the grief of losing her father, which made his silence all the more painful.

“It’s fine. That’s just how he is. He’s gotten stronger, so wherever he is, I’m sure he’s eating well and kicking ass.”

Amy, too, had a boyfriend who was hopeless when it came to understanding a woman’s heart.

“Hmph! Who needs a blockhead like that anyway? I’ve got you now, right? If the nights get lonely, I’ll just date you instead.”

Tess wrapped her arm around Amy’s and pressed in close, causing Amy’s face to go pale.

“Wait—what did you just say…?”

“Hey now, no need to act shy. We’re in the army, after all. We’ll just have to make do among ourselves.”

As Tess tugged her along with surprising strength, Amy walked nervously and asked,

“Tess, that’s a joke, right? …Right? It better be a joke…”

“Hohoho! Who knows? I might surprise even myself!”

Between the still-awkward recruits, Tess’s cheerful laughter rang out across the training field.

“Who’s laughing so joyfully like that?”

A window from the command tower opened, and a general, attending the induction ceremony in person, looked out over the parade ground.

“Who are those girls?”

The world’s greatest fire mage, Luda Garcia.

Former head of the South Aymond Magic Association, now serving as the commander of the Valkyrie Magic Corps’ 2nd Division.

“Who’s acting so relaxed?”

“General,” the duty officer responded, flipping through a file after spotting the two laughing girls.

“Carmis Amy and Elzine Tess. Both from Tormia. Looks like they’ve known each other for a long time. Also…”

The officer licked his lips and smirked.

“According to intel, their lovers are none other than the Ivory Tower’s Five Star, Saint Arien Shirone, and the Knight of Maha, Rian.”

Garcia’s eyes narrowed with interest.

“The Knight of Maha? The one cutting through the northwest of Kashan and sweeping aside demons as he heads south?”

Rian’s location was top-level classified information.

“Yes. The Valkyrie offered him a general’s position, but he declined.”

“Which one of the two is the lover of the Five Star?”

Garcia owed a debt to Shirone.

“That red-haired one, sir.”

Garcia fixed his gaze on Amy’s face.

“Can you compile and send me everything? Training results, performance records, specialty details—everything.”

The duty officer snapped to attention.

“Yes, sir!”

It was a direct order from a lieutenant general.

Elsewhere…

In exchange for handing over the Ex Machina, the Cotria Republic received full support from the Holy War.

The Holy War, comprising 30% of the Valkyrie forces, had struck hard at the western front of the Gustav Empire.

Faced with overwhelming numbers, even Balkan’s famed discipline faltered—just enough to give the Cotria Republic a chance to breathe.

“It won’t last long.”

Flying toward Aganos, Shirone glanced sideways as Minerva flew beside him in her jet.

“Even with the Eastern Jincheon Empire, the Holy War in the west, and the Cotria Republic attacking from the south, all they’ve done is slow the expansion. Balkan’s military power really is something else.”

“They are fighting the entire world. The fastest way to end it is still to eliminate Havitz.”

And now, word had reached Shirone and Minerva that the first button had been fastened.

“…Who do you think it is?”

The Ex Machina team had found the Alpha Fish.

“Whoever it is, they can’t be in their right mind.”

Malice. Chaos. Ignorance.

The one who truly loved Havitz—who embodied every vile concept imaginable.

“Is that even possible?”

Even Gustav’s 4th Holy Oracle had only been drawn by Havitz’s scent as a fellow demon. It wasn’t love.

“You got here fast. What about the altar?”

When they arrived at the inner city through a priority pass, Iruki was already waiting at the gate.

“I came in the middle of the sealing. They said it was urgent. Has the Alpha Fish arrived?”

“Yeah. They’re waiting in the room.”

“What kind of person is it?”

“Well…”

Iruki hesitated, lips twitching, but then gave up on explaining and turned toward the palace.

“Come on. Better if you see it for yourself.”
