Chapter 1107: Rules of the Holy War (4)

"High Priest."

Seeing High Priest Maximus's face, Lucia was overwhelmed with sorrow.

I’m so sorry.

He was the priest she admired most after the Pope, and yet she had inflicted grave injuries upon him.

I never meant to hurt him.

She believed it was because she had lost control of her emotions—anger when he opposed her relationship with Glenn had triggered her unholy powers, a sin she thought she bore alone.

This is my trial.

She resolved to subdue the demon within her left hand and prove her innocence to High Priest Maximus.

As memories of the past unfolded, Lucia saw the younger Maximus staring at her 17-year-old self with a blank expression.

Did that happen?

She couldn’t recall. At the time, she had no idea how important Maximus would become.

But now, knowing more about him, she noticed something unsettling in his gaze.

That expression doesn’t suit him.

Suddenly, time twisted again, and Lucia found herself witnessing not her own past but Maximus’s.

"Aileen."

The scene showed a young Maximus blushing in front of a woman.

Who is she?

Though not identical, the woman bore a striking resemblance to Lucia.

Time rewound further.

"Eat your soup, sweetheart," said a woman as she fed the toddler Maximus.

Why?

The woman resembled Aileen and Lucia.

Why am I seeing this?

The scene shifted back to the seminary.

"Who’s that?" Maximus asked, pointing at Lucia.

The supervising nun answered proudly, "She’s the most outstanding candidate here, with extraordinary divine power."

"I see," he replied.

This time, Lucia remembered.

Maximus had spoken briefly with her, encouraging her to work hard so she could one day serve the Church.

Those words changed my life.

That night, Lucia saw Maximus tossing and turning on a makeshift bed.

I don’t want to see this. I shouldn’t be peeking into someone’s private moments.

"Aileen," he murmured.

He had loved her deeply but had been driven by ambition.

If only I could turn back time, he thought. If I could return to the moment she confessed her love—the first and last time.

Lucia watched in shock as the memory showed Maximus heading to the female trainees' dormitory—a forbidden area for men, even high priests.

Cautiously, Maximus opened the door marked with Lucia’s name. Inside, the 17-year-old Lucia was fast asleep.

He stood silently, gazing at her, before taking a deep breath.

Why does God take only my soul, leaving my body trapped in this world?

When he had first seen Lucia in the chapel, Maximus had wondered, Could this be undone?

As his hand reached toward her, the present-day Lucia was overcome with dread.

No! Don’t do it!

Even if he could turn back time, she believed he would have still chosen to become High Priest.

Don’t make foolish choices just because you’re now burdened by regrets.

Maximus caught his reflection in a mirror. He was no longer the vibrant youth he once was but a revered Church authority, possessing everything the world could offer.

Furious with himself, Maximus turned and returned to his chamber. He knelt, took a wooden needle, and stabbed his thigh repeatedly.

"Lord," he prayed, as his blood stained his robes. "Deliver me from the devil’s temptation. Purify my soul."

Even as dawn broke, he continued his self-inflicted penance.

"Stop it," Lucia whispered, clutching her head. "I don’t want to know. I don’t want to know any of this."

Lord, forgive my desires, echoed voices from the seminary.

Grant me the strength to serve you. Make me a High Priest.

Cause.

The man with the pyramid-shaped head spoke: "If life is the sum of all causes accumulated from birth… does humanity truly have the power to determine its outcome?"

"One without cause," he continued, "are you trying to become a god?"

"You’re wrong," Shirone replied firmly. "I may lack a cause, but my will endures."

It endures through the heart.

"I, too, have wrestled with countless doubts through the flow of time," Shirone said. "Through those choices and mistakes, I’ve arrived at this moment."

"Yet you remain unshaken. Do you deny the foundations of your life?"

"It’s not denial," Shirone replied. "The causes of everyone who’s ever existed are etched into my memory. Yes, they’re confusing and often murky, but…"

The man called Human extended his hand. "Understand your insignificance," he said.

A wave of temporal energy surged toward Shirone, who countered with a radiant light of Agape.

"I forgive you," Shirone declared.

The explosive brilliance filled the pyramid, only for darkness to fall again.

Human was gone.

"Never underestimate the human heart," Shirone muttered, his gaze steady.

Meeting Human had confirmed one thing for him:

God exists in this place.

The High Priest Maximus lay in his chambers, resting while reminiscing about the past.

Lucia.

When had everything started to fall apart?

I shouldn’t have gone to the seminary that day. I ended up hurting that child.

But if he had chosen Aileen, would Lucia have remained just another student?

How fragile humans are in the face of life.

A loud knock at the door broke his thoughts.

"High Priest! High Priest!"

Maximus frowned. "Enter."

The door opened immediately, revealing a pale-faced priest.

"What is it?"

"Well… it’s… rumors are spreading that Yahweh has clashed with the Inquisition."

Maximus's eyes briefly betrayed his surprise.

"Yahweh… clashing with the Inquisition?"

"Yes. That’s the word going around. The Pope is away, and you’re the only one with the authority to intervene."

Maximus’s gaze darted about before a faint smile formed on his lips.

"Understood. I’ll head to the Inquisition. Summon the paladins."

"Yes. My apologies for troubling you when you’re unwell."

Although Lucia's attack had caused significant injuries, his divine power had healed most of it.

"Don’t worry. This is why I’m here."

After the priest left, Maximus let out a deep sigh.

"Damn it!"

He had already deduced why Yahweh remained in the Vatican.

What a troublesome man.

As he prepared to leave, a voice sounded from the corner of the room.

"My, you seem busy today."

Maximus froze, his body stiffening. Moments later, a slender hand rested on his shoulder.

"Feeling neglected? I was going to make you feel so much better today."

"…I told you, if we’re caught, it’s over."

The woman's face pressed against his cheek.

It was Aileen.

But it wasn’t really her.

Though identical in appearance to a younger Aileen, he knew it was Elka, a member of Yahweh’s assassination squad, Catacomb, and an evangelist for the Satanic cult.

This demon had the power to manifest a person’s deepest desires into visible form.

"Hoho! Yes, you’re right. I’m just a filthy demon. But who was it that let me take their purity, hmm?"

"Ugh."

Maximus’s face twisted in anguish.

"Get out. I won’t succumb to a demon anymore."

Elka pushed him toward the bed.

"Fine, I’ll leave. But are you sure you’ll be okay? Your emotional sickness might relapse."

Maximus had far more to lose than most. Among those things was his position as High Priest.

"You’re the one who chose to follow the Satanic teachings. You’ve suppressed your desires for a lifetime to achieve your success. Can you really throw it all away now? Without the Vatican, you’re just another old man."

"No! I…"

"This is your reward for all your hard work. You’re still the High Priest of the Church. And now, you’ve even found love."

As Elka pinned him to the bed and leaned in, he turned his head away.

"God, please. Please."

"It’s fine. You’ve waited long enough. Sixty years, right?"

Evil knows how to exploit human weakness.

"Enough already."

Tears streamed down Maximus’s face.

"I was wrong. Please, stop this."

"I don’t want to hurt you…"

With Aileen’s identical face, Elka gave a sorrowful smile.

"But I haven’t done anything yet."

Maximus felt his mind fracturing. Elka’s lips brushed his neck.

The Entrance of the Moon Kingdom

"Moon Kingdom! Enter!"

The procession’s entrance drew the attention of everyone at the Delta capital.

So that’s the Moon King.

Unlike the others who arrived in carriages, the Moon King rode alone on horseback, his dragon-like presence dominating the scene.

He stroked his long beard, sneering.

"Hiding in your buildings, peering like rats. No kingly dignity to be seen."

If Paras was the origin of Kashan, the Moon Kingdom was the cradle of Jincheon.

Like Paras, their specialty was astrology and divination.

"They both practice dark arts," Plu remarked. "Without more intel, we’ll need to tread carefully."

"The biggest threat is if Paras and the Moon Kingdom join forces. Warn Yongroe to intervene if necessary."

Shirone remained silent.

The Holy War hasn’t started yet.

Compared to the chaos surrounding simultaneous events, the Holy War preparations felt calm.

But not for long.

Another nation entered the city, catching Shirone’s attention.

"Gustav Empire! Enter!"

Though Havitz had defected to Kashan, Gustav remained a formidable nation.

Their procession rivaled Kashan’s in length, and soon it reached Delta’s central building.

Havitz is gone. So, who leads them now?

The throne had been vacant recently, and participating in the Holy War without a representative seemed implausible.

"Who is that?"

As the group approached, national leaders began murmuring.

"The Four Knights of Gustav? Weren’t they dead?"

Inside the emperor’s carriage sat Balkhan, wearing a crown, flanked by three others with unrefined manners.

Shirone’s expression grew grave.

Smode, Zettaro, Natasha.

With their abilities, they could certainly control Gustav. Politically, they have enough credibility, too. But…

The mystery lingered:

Why did they come to the Holy War?

Everyone turned to Havitz, who was gorging on food in the banquet hall.

He smirked, approaching the window with a grape in his mouth.

"I can hear them, you know," he said.

Through God’s frequency, he picked up on the murderous intent of the Gustav Four Knights.

"Fools," Havitz muttered, laughing darkly.

Balkhan pointed at him from his carriage.

"There you are."

Havitz laughed louder as the Four Knights rose to their feet, their gazes locked on him.

The tension thickened.

"You’re here, Havitz."

The Four Knights’ voices echoed, dripping with animosity.

Havitz’s face twisted into a manic grin.

"Ha! HAHAHAHA!"

Everyone in the central building turned pale. The shapes of their fears took grotesque form in Havitz’s laughter.
