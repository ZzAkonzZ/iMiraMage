Chapter 860: Singularity (3)

Seongeum's sacrifice activated Gwangcheonseong.

The spatial distortion brought the borders of Gustav and Jincheon together, but the sea itself had not disappeared.

Fierce battles continued in the western sea of Jincheon, with demons endlessly pouring onto the sandy beaches.

"Hold the line! Just a little longer!" The goal was to buy time until Jincheon's elite forces could capture Gustav.

In the battle for the survival of the two empires, the army of hell was overwhelmingly dominant.

"Hahaha! Watching humans struggle like this is disgusting. They look just like insects."

A massive demon with a protruding lower jaw sat cross-legged on a giant clam.

It was Mitura, the commander of the 8th legion of the army of hell.

His realm was the tidal wave, and beside him was a giant esophagus that a normal human couldn't even lift.

"I'm hungry! Let's eat something!" At Mitura's command, demons brought up creatures from the depths of the sea.

"Ahhh!"

A mermaid with the upper body of a beautiful human and the lower body of a fish was caught.

"Please, spare me!"

Living in an environment with pressures unbearable for humans, mermaids were rarely reported in academic studies.

The mermaid's tearful plea was pitiful, but to Mitura, she was just food.

"Shut up! I can't miss such a delicacy."

Grabbing the mermaid by the hair, he slammed her onto the clam's back and raised his esophagus.

"Please! Please!"

As he pressed down on the mermaid's head and struck with the esophagus, her tail was severed.

"Ahhh!"

Her upper body writhed in pain and fear, while the detached lower body flopped around as if in shock.

"Let's see..."

Mitura grabbed the tail fin and took a bite out of the bleeding stump.

The cold flesh of the fish melted on his tongue.

"Not bad..."

The mermaid, watching her body being eaten, rolled her eyes back and fainted.

She wouldn't live long.

"Pretty good."

Mitura quickly chewed and swallowed half of the tail, then threw the rest into the sea and stood up.

"Time to get started."

He didn't need to open his realm to create a tidal wave that could sweep the beach.

"Hoooo!"

The vast area began to ripple, and the seawater started to rise with the waves.

It was weak now, but by the time it reached land, it would be a 200-meter-high tidal wave.

"Commander! Over there...!"

The Jincheon army fighting on the beach lost their will to fight at the sight of the approaching tidal wave.

"Damn it!"

Cutting down house-sized demons was hard enough, but now a natural disaster was upon them.

"Is it all over?"

If the western sea was taken, the demons of the 8th legion would sweep through the capital, Yeomra, in no time.

"Ahhh!"

As the soldiers, overwhelmed by the force of nature, screamed and fled, a bell rang.

Ding.

Despite the noise of war, the soldiers clearly heard the clear, delicate sound.

"What is that?"

The commander turned to see a neatly dressed man with a bandaged head holding a fan.

It was Arte, a three-star resident of the Ivory Tower.

On his shoulder was a small, red-skinned creature with a long tail.

"Over 200 meters? Can you really stop it?"

It was Arte's satellite, Tokei.

"A demon?"

As the commander stared in shock, Arte began to move.

"200 meters. It's a bit difficult."

Arte raised his fan above his head.

"Just a bit?"

As he waved his sleeve like a dance, time seemed to slow down.

The sight of water flowing down the surface of the massive tidal wave was vividly clear.

"Scenery."

Arte's magic was unique in that it used differences in atmospheric pressure rather than air flow.

As a pressure trough formed, the atmosphere in the vast area of the tidal wave began to twist at incredible speed.

Boom!

The sound of the world splitting echoed, and the soldiers, their hearing numbed by the shock, saw the sea wide open.

"The tidal wave..."

The water barrier was destroyed as if hit by a small typhoon, and heavy rain poured down like hail.

"Hoo."

As Arte finished his dance, the clear sound of the bell rang again.

The commander, regaining his senses, asked,

"Who are you? How did you..."

"I'm from the Ivory Tower."

Arte's expression was not good.

"Saving lives and making the world better. I have no objection to the ideal of humanity, but..."

It was unprecedented for all the stars of the Ivory Tower to join Shirone's cause in a full-scale war.

"It can't be helped."

Tokei found the current situation quite interesting.

"Stagnant water is bound to rot. The new flow called Shirone will change the Ivory Tower."

That's why the Five Stars participated in the war to prove their philosophy again.

"I know."

The commander approached.

"What should we do now? We stopped the tidal wave, but more will come."

Arte walked towards the sea, holding his fan.

"Ghost Demon Arte, joining the war against the demons."

Tokei's large eyes spun rapidly, calculating the elements of the wind.

"320893. 23091. 3494539. Elevation angle 23 degrees..."

Arte's magic targeted not the army of hell but the Gustav Empire across the sea.

"At least category six wind power. Can you do it?"

"I told you."

Arte's usually emotionless face twisted like a ghost.

"I'm joining the war."

As the fan moved from right to left, the soldiers felt the air being pushed away.

"Climate magic, Great Scenery."

Boom!

A vortex created by the pressure difference sucked up water from the middle of the sea.

The wind grew stronger, and soon the demons around were sucked into the vortex.

"What is that?"

The vortex grew exponentially, but even its speed was nothing compared to the final result.

"It's a typhoon."

The Great Scenery would travel thousands of kilometers and hit Gustav with the force of an empire.

Arte raised his fan again.

"Here we go again. I'll blow it all away."

Watching Tokei's eyes spin, the commander swallowed hard.

"Crazy. They're attacking across the sea?"

Massive storms were heading towards the Gustav Empire, where Shirone and Rian were.

Feeling Shirone's gaze on the back of her head, Melania spoke more earnestly.

"If it can calm your anger, I don't mind being torn apart here. So please, forgive us."

She didn't expect this to fully calm Shirone's anger.

"But it should have some effect."

When driven by rage, one doesn't feel exhaustion, but the moment reason returns, the backlash is unavoidable.

"He's thinking. That's enough. After destroying the world for over a day, he can't be fine."

Melania raised her head.

"Yahweh...!"

Shirone kicked her in the face.

A magician's kick was as light as a feather, but her eyes were filled with shock.

"Enough."

If he was going to be swayed by such words, he wouldn't have abandoned Yahweh in the first place.

"I don't believe it. No matter what you say. Even if it's the truth, I'll deny it."

"Is it not possible?"

In the end, Shirone was human, and if this continued, he would lose his life.

"But then there will be no demons."

Shirone raised a photon cannon above his palm.

"Just die."

In the critical moment, a thought flashed through Melania's mind.

"It can be reversed!"

"What?"

For the first time, Shirone's expression changed.

"The woman who sealed the spiritual realm is wandering in hell. But she can be brought back."

"Seongeum..."

The photon cannon disappeared.

"You can save her?"

A single mistake could ruin everything, so Melania spoke the truth.

"I can't guarantee it. But if it's about freeing her from hell..."

Shirone grabbed Melania by the collar.

"Tell me. How?"

"By breaking through the Shiyok."

"Shiyok."

The twelve apostles who guard every 0.666 seconds.

"The spiritual realm is closed, but if you can pass through the time not covered by the law, you can extract her spirit."

"How do you break through?" Even Shirone couldn't perceive the Shiyok.

"Even the legion commanders don't know the true nature of the Shiyok."

There was only a theory that they were the counterparts to the twelve apostles who guard time.

"Then it doesn't make sense."

As Shirone raised his hand again in anger, Melania hurriedly spoke.

"But they definitely follow Satan's orders. And now is the critical moment."

"There is someone in the human capital with the authority of the Shiyok. If you meet him, you'll know."

Shirone looked into Melania's eyes.

They were beautiful and without a hint of deceit, but demons had deceived with such eyes before.

"Who is it?"

"It's the supreme commander, Ogent Guy."

Rian's older brother, whom he had met before, crossed his mind briefly.

"A human has the authority?"

"Satan, Habitz, is human. The Shiyok is not controlled by the legion commanders. It was left by Balkan and his army as a contingency."

Shirone released her collar.

"Lead me to Ogent Guy."

No matter what the truth was, there was no choice but to pursue any method to save Seongeum.

"Yes, yes."

Melania stood up, bowing her head, and gave orders to the demons.

"All of you, retreat. Anyone who fights humans from this moment will be killed by me first."

The demons were visibly displeased, but they couldn't disobey the legion commander's orders.

The demons scattered in all directions like cockroaches hiding in corners.

"Let's go. I'll guide you," Melania said.

"If you're lying..." Shirone's eyes hardened.

"I'll make you suffer the worst fate imaginable."

Such immense emotions were difficult to express in words, and Melania swallowed hard.

'It doesn't matter.'

If she could stop Yahweh's rage for even a second.

In the commander's office of the capital, Marsak.

Ogent Guy was lost in thought as he donned his armor, preparing for battle.

'Family.'

Black hair and blue hair, each with different personalities, but he remembered them having a good relationship.

'Rian.'

He had no talent for the sword and was quite immature, but there was something endearing about him.

'Is it now the era of blue hair for the Ogents?'

The reason his heart swelled was because of family, but even such thoughts were a luxury.

'I want to become stronger. Isn't that natural for a swordsman?'

He had abandoned family, country, and even his own character to pursue strength on the side of the demons.

"I must end this war."

As he turned, fastening his sword to his waist, a red vortex appeared before his eyes.

"What is this?"

When the second legion commander, Melania, emerged from the vortex, his brow furrowed.

"Why is a demon...?" Shirone followed behind her, his eyes cold.
