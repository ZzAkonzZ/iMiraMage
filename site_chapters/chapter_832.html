Chapter 832: Breaking the System (4)

Rian entered the cabin, quickly packed his belongings, and finished preparing to leave.

"What will you do?"

Although the Jaive soldiers had retreated, the hunters' lives would be in danger once Rian left.

"There are no more vampires."

Although one Lord had not yet awakened from hibernation, Xenia was confident she could handle it.

"We'll gather the hunters and form a death squad. We won't leave a single vampire in this world."

When that happened, Xenia and the hunters would finally be able to live in peace.

"Take care."

"You too. Jaive isn't the only one targeting you. You might have to fight the whole world."

Rian smiled confidently.

"It doesn't matter. I was prepared for this."

Having witnessed his prowess firsthand, there was no need for further words.

"Rian..."

Although they would walk their separate paths, Xenia was certain they would never meet again.

"Why don't you come with us?"

Xenia mustered the courage to speak.

"Come with us. The captain of the Shinjang said a great war is coming. How about we join forces and fight together?"

Rian shook his head.

"I have somewhere I need to go."

Xenia recalled what Rian had told her before.

"Someone you really want to protect? You said that person is much stronger than you."

"Of course. But now, I can be of help. And..."

Rian looked back at the sunset.

"He's going through a lot right now."

Unlike Rian, who could throw everything away to become stronger, Shirone had many things to protect.

Now was the time to share the hardships as Shirone's sword.

"Although the descendants of the true demons are gone, there are still many demons in the world."

Rian placed a hand on her shoulder.

"Let's meet on the battlefield."

Understanding the significance of his words, Xenia smiled and lightly punched his chest.

"Don't go breaking too many things."

It was a promise he couldn't guarantee, but Rian nodded and left the hunters' hideout.

'Although I was going to return home... the place the strongest great swordsman heads to is the Ivory Tower.'

Heaven.

After the war that claimed the lives of many angels, Anke Ra disappeared.

The one who was given full authority was the archangel Ikael, but she had remained silent for a very long time.

Then one day, Nane's voice reached them.

"I permit the breaking of the System for all beings."

Angels, Maras, fairies, giants, and immortals all knew what impact this would have on Heaven.

And now, for the first time since the war ended, the assembly of archangels, the White Mirror, was held.

The archangels gathered in the pure white space were Ikael, Uriel, Satiel, and Raiel.

"Exactly half are gone."

At Uriel's words, the archangels fell silent.

The eight fundamental concepts that supported this world.

But now, each of them had lost their counterpart.

Amplification lost to annihilation, destruction to creation, decomposition to combination, and light to gravity.

Raiel, the archangel of light, asked.

"For an archangel to be resurrected, Anke Ra's power is absolutely necessary. But now Ra is gone. What is the reason for summoning a half-formed White Mirror?"

"Ymir has broken the ice barrier."

At Ikael's words, the halos of the angels stiffened.

"Didn't you say a special law was in effect?"

As far as Satiel knew, the ice sea of Niflheim was an absolutely immovable world that could freeze even souls.

"There was never a place that could contain Ymir. The reason he stayed quiet wasn't because of Anke Ra."

Uriel spoke.

"Because it was meaningless."

If there was no one who could destroy him, Ymir had no reason to do anything.

Raiel looked at Uriel.

"Then now, does it mean there is a reason?"

"Something is happening. Not in Heaven."

Only Ikael could answer, but she remained silent.

"So..."

After a long pause, as Satiel began to speak, the space of the White Mirror started to vibrate.

"What is it?"

The halos of the archangels expanded into rings, and their sharp gazes scanned the surroundings.

"He's here."

Only Ikael remained calm, but her eyes were colder than usual.

As the vibrations intensified, two hands suddenly appeared in the space of the White Mirror.

Satiel stood up abruptly.

"Who are you!"

The White Mirror was a place born from the purest minds of the archangels.

No impurity could tear through that mind.

"Ugh!"

As the rough hands tore open the space of the White Mirror, the halos of the angels shook left and right.

"Who else would it be?"

Finally, a voice flowed in, and at that moment, everyone realized.

"It's me."

Ymir, the king of giants, tore open the White Mirror and pushed his body inside.

"Ugh!"

The wall healed instantly, but the intrusion of the foreign substance, Ymir, was extremely unpleasant for the angels.

"Are you insane, Ymir?"

The reason Ymir could physically impact the angels' minds was because he was a fusion of Gaia beings.

To him, power was pure energy beyond the physical strength of living beings.

"I knew you'd be here."

Ignoring Uriel's provocation, Ymir looked at Ikael.

"No matter how much I called, there was no answer."

Finally, Ikael's lips opened.

"Why did you want to see me?"

"War."

Ymir approached Ikael.

"Send me. You can do it, right?"

The human organization Zion was still preventing Heaven's invasion.

Of course, the person with the strongest defense was Adrias Miro, who guarded the space-time of the labyrinth.

"Don't be arrogant, Ymir."

Ymir's eyebrows twitched.

"I know what you're thinking. But starting a war for your personal reasons is unacceptable."

"Personal reasons."

Ymir placed his hand on Ikael's shoulder.

Three angels stood up simultaneously to kill him, but Ymir spoke first.

"Isn't it the same for you?"

Satiel was the first to release her hostility, and the other two archangels looked at Ikael.

"Anke Ra's Akashic Records have passed to Nane. Therefore, Heaven must find Nane as soon as possible. Yet you can't make a decision."

Ikael couldn't refute.

'Shirone.'

The child with Goffin's will.

"I know roughly through Suoi. Ikael, you've regained your memories, haven't you?"

Anke Ra erased Ikael's sins but restored all her memories with immense amplification.

"That's why you can't go. Well, that's fine. But I want to ask you one thing. Why did you have a human child?"

With the three archangels sitting down again, Ikael was obliged to answer.

Satiel glared at Ikael with hostility.

'I loved Goffin too.'

Although she no longer knew who Goffin was, the longing for him still remained in her mind.

'But even so, having a child is unimaginable.'

Angels are concepts.

As beings of Idea itself, they had no reason to have children, mere shadows of humans.

"First, Shirone is not my child."

Ikael declared.

"The child is already dead. At birth. It wasn't even given a name."

"But it has some connection, doesn't it?"

Ikael didn't respond to that.

"The reason Anke Ra forbade breaking the System is... no one should know the secrets of this world."

"Are there secrets we don't know?"

"That's why they're secrets. Even Anke Ra. It's something that shouldn't be approached."

Ikael raised her index finger.

"We are concepts, and thus we exist forever. Humans, on the other hand, are material and mortal. Therefore, they are subject to our rule and obey for eternal life."

That was Heaven's law.

"But what if it's the opposite?"

"Opposite?"

"What if we were created by humans, and humans are the ones who have realized the secret of eternal life?"

The halos of the archangels shook.

"We might just exist for human convenience. We might be nothing more than components supporting this world. If that's the case, what happens?"

The atmosphere of the White Mirror became explosively tense.

"We become mortal. If this world closes, we become nothing. Conversely..."

Ikael looked around at everyone.

"Humans are the gods."

Satiel stood up abruptly.

"What nonsense is that! Those weak humans are gods? Humans are the ones struggling desperately because they hate this world!"

"That's why they can break the System."

"Ah..."

Satiel stepped back.

"Breaking the System is an exception to the rules. Some humans ignore certain rules of this world. And that wouldn't be possible without an outside world."

Longing showed on Ikael's face.

"This is Goffin's hypothesis."

Satiel her his lip.

"Goffin."

"I don't know. As an archangel, I fought fiercely with Goffin. But after hearing his words, I thought about it. If we are just information, do we have a reason to exist?"

"So you had a child?"

"I just... wanted to continue."

Like the Gaia beings who left the photon system.

"But I couldn't."

The child died, and Anke Ra took away Ikael's full authority for committing a great sin.

Ikael, emerging from her recollection, informed everyone with a solemn face.

"Anke Ra never permitted breaking the System. Even when Heaven was on the brink of destruction."

But now the situation had changed.

"Anke Ra asked Hexa, 'Why can't I become a god?' But Hexa's answer was unexpected. Despite coming from the outside, he wants to protect this world."

Perhaps it was because he came from the outside.

Raiel spoke, "So that's why Ra chose Nane."

Nane is a being trying to go from inside to outside.

"At first, we waited, believing he would lead us outside. But he was blocked by Hexa's barrier."

"Enough with the explanations," Ymir said gruffly. "In the end, we have to go. Whether we destroy all humans or follow Nane outside."

"Satan is there."

A being outside the law.

Satan, born from all human chaos, was the worst enemy for angels.

"But Nane has permitted breaking the System," Uriel said.

"We won't be as easily defeated as before."

Raiel, who had been listening, turned to Ikael and said, "It's time to make a decision. If anyone other than Nane enforces this world, Heaven has no future."

Ikael knew they had to go, but the reason she still hesitated was...

'Shirone.'

Going to the mortal world would inevitably mean clashing with Hexa.

As everyone awaited Ikael's judgment, she closed her eyes and pondered. Finally, she spoke.

"Organize the army." Heaven began to move.
