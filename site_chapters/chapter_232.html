Chapter 232: Battle Simulation (2)

The moment the target entered Spirit Zone, the target trembled as if it had been electrocuted.

Then, as Dante’s mental state changed, it began to bounce off into unpredictable orbits.

As the target tried to escape his Spirit Zone, Dante employed the Four-Way Zone technique to secure the space.

Then, using the momentary focus of Mental Pulse, he quickly pulled the target back.

As the target returned to the center, it started to thrash around again.

Each time, Dante skillfully adjusted the shape of the Four-Way Zone to prevent the target from escaping.

As time passed, the target's movements became faster, and its trajectory changed in increasingly bizarre ways.

In some sections, it vibrated as if it was trapped in a wall, and in some sections, it stretched out at full power for more than 10 meters.

The students felt tense just watching.

When the target flew so fast that even the longest-range target type couldn't catch it, a gasp escaped them.

"Whoa?"

At that moment, Dante expanded his Spirit Zone using the Sequencing technique.

As the target-type distance increased, the target was locked in again. His Mental Pulse rose without fail and the target was drawn in.

Those watching his Spirit Zone operation, which was close to a miracle, were left speechless.

After a minute passed, Olivia called for a stop.

"That's enough. I think this demonstration is sufficient."

Dante drew the target back and suspended it above his head before releasing Spirit Zone.

The students were left in awe.

Even for a brief moment, the ability to move the target in the desired direction was a level of skill impossible with ordinary mental strength.

"This is the Lifting Test. If you're in Class Four, you should be able to maintain it for about a minute. Now, how about we have some other students give it a try? I heard there's someone named Shirone..."

Boyle raised his hand.

"I'll do it."

It was a strategy to embarrass Shirone, but when another student volunteered unexpectedly, Olivia was caught off guard.

Maintaining the Lifting Test for a minute was already a level much higher than the average for Class Four.

Even Dante, who had been practicing since the test was introduced, found it difficult to maintain it for two minutes.

"I am the top student in Class Four. I'm confident."

Olivia's eyes lit up.

Although it wasn't Shirone, a comparison with the top student in the advanced class would provide objective data to pressure Ethella.

"Alright. Since it's your first time, just think of it as getting a feel for it."

Boyle confidently walked into the Image Zone.

As he ascended the steps, he glared at Dante, who merely shrugged and stepped aside.

With the students watching intently, Boyle's Lifting Test began.

Upon entering unfolding his Spirit Zone, the target reacted strongly.

It was much harder than he expected, feeling like a rubber ball bouncing inside his skull.

As expected from a talent that ranks first in the advanced class, he was maintaining the Lifting, but the intervals of pulling the target with Mental Pulse has become noticeably shorter.

Eventually, the speed of his Four-Way Zone transformations couldn't keep up with the target's movements, and the target broke free from the Spirit Zone.

“Huff, huff.”

Boyle gasped for breath. He thought he had done reasonably well for his first attempt.

But the expressions on the students' faces were grim.

When he checked the scoreboard, his eyes widened in shock.

21 seconds.

His performance hadn't even reached half of Dante's time.

He could have expected a higher score if he practiced, but even so, it seemed like it would be difficult to maintain one minute.

"Damn it! Damn it!"

Boyle returned to his seat with his head bowed.

He felt ashamed to face Ethella.

His failure seemed to prove that Ethella's students had weaker fundamentals compared to Olivia's students.

But Olivia was not pleased and scolding Ethella.

Although it started out as a childish motive, as someone who has spent her entire life in the teaching profession, she was sincere when it came to academy work.

"Boyle, you did well. Twenty seconds on your first try is very respectable. I’m not one to ignore the basics. What I'm trying to convey is that no matter how solid your basics are, they must still function effectively. In a time when students across the country are pursuing specialized courses, sticking strictly to the basics will only lead to being left behind."

When they heard it, it made sense.

For the first time, the teachers considered the possibility that they might be wrong.

Had they unconsciously fallen into a rut?

To keep up with the changing times, fresh stimuli were necessary.

Perhaps that is why Alpheas himself went out of his way to bring Olivia.

Olivia flashed a smile at the students.

When the cold-faced woman smiled, her beauty seemed to stand out even more.

"Now, I'm sure you're all eager to try. So, let's make today's class a free practice session where you can work on your Lifting skills. Since it's the first day, it's important to get a feel for it, so try whatever you'd like. Let's begin the practical training."

The students swarmed toward the practice area, excited as if they'd just received a new toy.

Olivia watched the students practice happily without leaving her seat.

Feeling that she had nothing more to do today, Ethella headed toward where the other teachers were gathered.

She approached Thad and Sienna with her shoulders slumped.

"I'm sorry. It feels like trying to crack a rock with an egg."

Thad appeased Ethella.

"Haha! Don't think of it that way. Still, Ethella said what needed to be said, and it was refreshing. Besides, Dante is ranked number one in the kingdom. Boyle's fundamentals are by no means inferior."

Sienna also thought similarly.

There may be a difference in level, but if Boyle had practiced Lifting, it would have been a good match.

Considering that most summoners only come into their own after their thirties, Boyle, who had become the top student in the advanced class at such a young age, was undoubtedly a rare genius.

"What worries me is what lies ahead. Since Dante won, the principal's influence will likely grow stronger. If we're not careful, it could destabilize the foundation of the academy."

"Ei, come on. I doubt that. There's only so much that can be done in the advanced class."

With the graduation class in place, there was no way to impose rigorous training on advanced class students.

Above all, it wasn't as if the curriculum was poorly designed.

Still, Sienna felt uneasy.

She thought there might be some intention behind Olivia's actions with Dante at the forefront.

The three of them watched the students as they practiced their Lifting techniques.

After some adaptation, most of the students did well, and Boyle and Pandora showed off their skills befitting the top of the class.

Sienna pointed to Shirone and his group.

“Huh? What are they doing?”

Shirone, Nade, and Iruki had teamed up and were lifting a single target together.

The other students, intrigued by this novel approach, paused their own practice to watch.

One person would trap the target, bounce it off, and then another person would catch it and lift it again.

“Oho, oho! This is fun.”

As Nade clumsily tried to trap the target, Iruki's Spirit Zone came in and intercepted him.

The next moment, Shirone snatched the target away and moved it behind him.

The two without the target rushed toward Shirone, who held on for a while before finally losing control and letting Nade take over again.

By now, all the students were watching Shirone and his group.

Although they had been known for their mischievous antics since their days in Class Five, it was enviable to see them enjoying themselves without the pressure of competition.

"By the way, was Shirone's Spirit Zone always that large?"

"Huh, you're right. It's gotten enormous?"

The students in Class Four immediately noticed the change in Shirone.

It's common sense to do special training during vacation, so it wasn't surprising that every student had made some progress, but Shirone's improvement was nothing short of astonishing.

Dante and his crew also had no choice but to notice Shirone's Spirit Zone.

"Wow, it's really huge. That size would put him in the top 30 at the Royal Magic Academy."

“Tch! It’s about the same as Dante’s. Shirone's just avoiding a head-on confrontation because he knows he can't beat Dante in Lifting. Typical loser mentality, right, Dante?"

But Dante didn’t respond. He was simply watching Shirone and his group perform their Lifting exercises.

“Kukuk, kids. Shall we show these kids how it’s done?"

Closer said, as he headed towards the Image Zone with Savina. The other students stepped aside to make way for them.

The lifting skills of the two were not inferior to Dante's at all.

Savina, in particular, showed off her whirlwind technique by momentarily twisting her mind. The students exclaimed in admiration as the target, which was swept in a flashy manner, returned just before it was about to escape.

Thanks to this, teachers were also able to see the abilities of the transfer students.

Olivia's disciples were certainly great.

Even Thad, who had suffered the most from her, could not help but admit it.

"They truly are the kingdom's finest talents. What do you think, Teacher Ethella? How would you evaluate Dante's skills as a Zoner?"

"Um, it's hard to judge based on Lifting alone, but he certainly has some outstanding qualities. Kids these days seem to have an incredible sense of magic."

Sienna said.

"But it's a bit disappointing. While their talent is undeniable, they seem shallow? It feels like today's kids see magic more as a tool for showing off rather than something deeper."

"Given that they've grown up with magic as a part of their daily lives, it's not surprising. But the spirit of a mage, or passion? I think that’s fading. They pursue glamour and fame. In that sense, Shirone, Iruki, and Nade have a more classical approach, huhu.”

Ethella smiled as she looked at Shirone and his group.

There was a time when magic was a child's dream. But for the kids in the capital, it seemed more like an accessory to make them stand out.

There was no stopping the changing tide of the times.

Still, it was nice to see Shirone and his friends still having pure passion.

When their game of tag ended, Shirone descended from the Image Zone, panting heavily.

Having worked up a sweat, he felt refreshed.

While Shirone was wiping the sweat off with a towel handed to him by a female junior, Dante approached him.

Although no one told them to, the students paused their practice and fixed their gazes on them.

This was the moment when the two finally faced each other.

Dante stared intently at Shirone's face.

'So this is the guy Master told me to defeat?'

Just by looking at Shirone's appearance, no one would think he was strong.

This was the reason why Closer and Sabina didn't feel nervous even after receiving Olivia's special mission.

But you don't fight with looks.

In addition to Dante’s natural talent, his thoroughgoing nature and unwavering determination also played a part in his achievement of being undefeated at the Royal Magic Academy.

"Pleased to meet you. I'm Dante."

Dante reached out first.

The students were surprised by his demeanor, which was completely different from what they had seen in the classroom. The person whose pride was hurt the most was Boyle.

But unaware of this, Shirone accepted Dante's gesture with goodwill and shook his hand.

"Yeah, I've heard a lot about you."

"Of course you have. I've heard about you too. You’re an Unlocker, huh? You’re a perfect person to step on. Oh, I’m not trying to disparage you.”

"Haha! Sure. Let's get along well."

Despite the active provocation, Shirone did not look offended.

Dante tried to analyze Shirone’s inner thoughts, but gave up because he thought it would only make his thoughts more complicated.

"Anyway, let's get along. If you have any questions, feel free to ask. There are no teachers or friends when it comes to learning. If you want, I’ll teach you properly.”

"Huh?"

Dante became displeased when Shirone tilted his head.

Even if he was a country bumpkin who was ignorant of the current situation, it was natural for him to keep a close watch when someone who threatened his position transferred to his academy. Dante found it hard to believe that Shirone truly didn't understand the situation.

"You don't get what I'm saying? You're specializing in Photon Magic, right?"

“Oh. My specialty is Photon Magic.”

"So is mine. They call me the 'Shining Boy.' You didn’t know?"

Only then did Shirone realize his mistake and stuck out his tongue.

“Oh, I’m sorry. I really didn't know. I don't read the academic journals."

“You didn’t know?”

Dante was the kingdom's top prospect in the field of Photon Magic.

Every student attending the magic academy knew this, so Shirone claiming he didn't because he didn’t read the journals seemed like a weak excuse.

"You're pretty good at provoking. You’ll have no shortage of opponents for me.”

"No, really, I didn't know. I wasn't trying to provoke you. I'm really sorry."
