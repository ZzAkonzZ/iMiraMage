Chapter 838: Depression (2)

'Damn bastard.'

Under Freed's cross-shaped helmet, his eyes glowed fiercely.

Even if time was reversed, the events that occurred weren't erased from his mind.

But he was still a Great Star.

Suppressing his shame and analyzing the recent events, he noticed something peculiar.

Meanwhile, the stars and satellites of the Ivory Tower were speechless, alternating their gaze between Freed and Shirone.

'Freed was hit.'

Although it was an event in a non-existent timeline, no one present was ignorant of the concept of breaking time.

'Or was he really hit?' The event had returned to the original timeline when Freed was struck.

As a mage and swordsman, Freed's durability was far superior to other stars, leaving questions.

And only Freed, who had directly received Shirone's attack, knew the answers.

'Strange.'

The first thing that came to Freed's mind was a sense of incongruity.

'Breaking time. I understand pulling the future into the past by vibrating the timeline, but...'

The trajectory of the photon cannon, all the variables when Shirone's teleportation reached his proximity.

'Why couldn't I dodge?'

Even if it was a surprise attack, someone at the pinnacle of both swordsmanship and magic shouldn't have been unable to dodge.

'It wasn't just once.'

It felt like another time within time was vibrating.

'The event of me being hit by his attack is just one of countless possible events.'

How many times had the same segment repeated?

'There's no need to think about the unknown.'

What he realized about Shirone was that he could perform calculations on future possibilities in an instant.

'A mage who searches for the most perfect conclusion and delivers an unavoidable strike.'

That was Shirone.

'But casting a spell, teleporting, and casting again. What other variables could there be?' The changes were so minute that an ordinary mage wouldn't notice.

But those subtle differences gave Freed a sense of nausea.

'Surprise attacks don't matter. What's annoying is...'

Freed had never been bested in either swordsmanship or magic.

'Is there a tiny crack in my level that he can exploit?'

Breaking time was a secondary issue; what bothered him was Shirone's ability to find a way to ambush him.

"Interesting."

Freed grinned, exuding a menacing aura.

"Unexpected. I didn't think the Great Star of the Integrated Universe Management Department would strike first."

The Great Stars who guarded the Grand Star had always sat quietly like enlightened beings.

"What are you talking about?"

Shirone asked nonchalantly.

"I didn't do anything."

"Heh."

Veins bulged on Freed's temples.

'So that's how you want to play it.'

He never intended to follow Shirone's philosophy, but this exchange made it clear.

'Whatever you think, you'll never succeed.'

The atmosphere in the Grand Hall grew tense.

The stars and satellites of each department glared at each other, making the air ripple with killing intent.

Even with the Grand Star watching closely, the name "Grand Assembly" carried immense weight.

'Shirone.'

The Grand Star looked at Shirone with unfocused eyes.

Breaking time, unlike Gaold's direct planetary impact, pressured her differently.

'I'm dizzy.'

From her perspective, Shirone had vibrated time approximately 40,000 times to successfully ambush Freed.

Reversing time was likely to anticipate Freed's counterattack after the successful strike.

'Freed isn't someone who goes down easily.'

While Shirone had taken the lead, his methods were unusually aggressive.

Minerva observed Shirone.

'No, that was the right decision. The System Inspection Department will be our most persistent obstacle in this Grand Assembly. But...'

This wouldn't be enough to break Freed.

"The Integrated Universe Management Department will speak."

Shirone stood up.

"If demons were born from human evil, now is the time to eradicate that evil. We request the cooperation of all departments of the Ivory Tower to fight the demons."

"Why should we fight the demons?" Freed asked, arms crossed.

"The strong devour the weak. If they're strong, why not let them have this world?"

Shirone's eyes hardened.

"If demons take over the world, all human nations will fall. Is that the outcome the System Inspection Department wants?"

Freed remained calm.

"You're mistaken. We're not lackeys of royalty. The current world system is a state system, but if demons introduce a different system, we'll manage that."

As long as the system was maintained, it didn't matter who ruled the world.

"Let's convey the System Inspection Department's opinion to the Grand Star. We don't act for the benefit of any specific nation. If demons are seen as a new nation, this is just another war. Wars have always existed and will continue to exist."

Minerva bit her lip.

'They're fully prepared.'

Freed was unpleasant, but the System Inspection Department held significant influence among the five departments.

"Freed's words have some merit."

Shirone nodded. "Systematically, there's no reason this world must belong to humans. But there's one thing I don't understand."

"Heh, ask away. I'll explain it to our rookie in detail."

Shirone looked directly at Freed.

"You said you'd manage the demons' system, but I don't think your department has that capability."

The eyes of the eight stars in the System Inspection Department burned with different kinds of killing intent.

"This arrogant brat..."

Freed scowled.

"Just because you have one trick doesn't mean you can act up. There are plenty of ways to kill you."

Shirone responded calmly.

"Really? Like what?" Freed's mouth remained shut for a long time, and then his shoulders shook roughly.

"Heh. Heh heh."

His body disappeared from view, and the ground where he had been sitting caved in a semicircle.

"Damn it...!"

The Grand Hall shook as Freed, sword drawn, appeared before Shirone.

'Damn it! Not again!'

Seeing Shirone's afterimage twisting into a counterattack stance made him nauseous again.

'Time has already vibrated.'

It was the second time, but the feeling was indescribably filthy.

'But can he find a gap this time?'

Unless it was another surprise attack, he was confident there were no gaps in his prepared stance.

'Reverse it all you want.'

The only event that would be decided was Shirone's head falling.

'If I win this time, it's 1-1. But...'

Freed's sword swung at the highest speed within the law's limits.

'My victory means your death!'

At that moment, a wheel spun rapidly at the midpoint of the clash between sword and magic.

'World Wheel? Damn it!'

It wasn't strong on its own.

But maintaining absolute balance by circulating external forces didn't require strength.

Boom!

As the power rotated around the World Wheel, Shirone and Freed were flung in opposite directions.

"Ugh!"

Where they glared, Amanta stood, spinning the World Wheel between his palms.

"Stop. This fight is meaningless."

Freed sneered.

"Meaningless? You heard that brat mock me."

"Both of you are wrong." Amanta turned to the Grand Star.

"Shirone, who centers on humanity, and Freed, who centers on the system. Both have failed to prevent today's situation. Let me use the World Wheel now."

The Grand Star asked.

"To what extent can you balance it?"

"By eradicating 80% of humanity, I can reduce the number of demons by 30%."

Minerva protested.

"80% of humanity? Are you joking?"

"It wouldn't have been this extreme. If you had listened when I suggested using the World Wheel, humanity would have only suffered 30% casualties."

Shirone countered.

"30% is still a large number."

"Yes. But that 30% is already gone. They're all dead."

"Do you understand? Your idealism, even if unintentional, was tolerated once. But it led to greater sacrifices. Face reality."

"I have something to say."

A cold, sharp voice came from the outermost part of the Grand Hall.

It was Xing, the Great Star of the Law Department.

Her black hair was tied tightly on top of her head, and her pale face was fierce yet beautiful.

"Wait. I'm not done..." As Freed tried to intervene, Xing's right hand moved gently to the side.

'What is this?'

Shirone's eyes widened.

Everyone except Xing felt as small as dust on the floor.

'Incredible law.'

Minerva gritted her teeth.

'Xing of the Law Department. If Freed is annoying, she's truly strong.'

Xing spoke.

"There is no standard in the law. If I become stronger, others become weaker. Therefore, my thoughts without a standard might be helpful."

The Grand Star asked.

"What are your thoughts?"

"...I also question whether humanity deserves such respect."

Freed smirked while Shirone's brow furrowed deeply.

Minerva sighed.

'It's natural, Shirone. Everyone here has reached the pinnacle of humanity.'

The Great Stars, those with the title of Star.

'Their commonality... is that they've been hurt by countless humans.'

'You know how terrible humans can be. That's why you're going mad.'

The feeling of not fighting together was the root of the Great Stars' depression.

"In this world, there is no humanity. Only humans, humans, humans."

According to Xing's theory, a human is a being alone in a place created by someone.

"Honestly, I don't believe multiple humans live in this world. Only I am human, and you are all illusions."

Everything except me is a puppet.

Even if applied universally, no one could prove Xing's words wrong.

"Therefore, I am the only human in this universe. I fight to cultivate my world. I don't care about the suffering of illusions."

Shirone looked horrified.

'What kind of life must she have lived to think like that?'

But it was precisely that philosophy that allowed Xing to manipulate the law so extremely.

"So I tried to align with the illusion created by someone named Freed, but..."

The still imposing Xing looked back at Shirone, standing on the vast floor, and smiled.

"The illusion called Shirone is quite unique too."

'What is she?'

He couldn't analyze the woman named Xing.
