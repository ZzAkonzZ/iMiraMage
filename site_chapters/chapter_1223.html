Chapter 1223:

It was approaching 9 p.m.

From the Holy War facility’s engine room, to the storage areas, and even the underground hideouts of the gutter rats—there was a distinct stench of blood in the air.

Not human blood.

It was the scent of goblin blood.

“Kiiieeek!”

Kido, gripping his double-edged spear tightly, rolled across the ground with a flash of his amber eyes.

The way his spinning body cut through space looked like an antlion pit made of blades.

“Jincheon Sorcery.”

Anchal’s Magic Eye flared.

“Titan's Might.”

The bricks in the ceiling dented like droplets before forming into a massive foot that came crashing down toward Kido.

His eyes widened as if about to pop from their sockets, and his body was slammed into the ground as if stamped into mud.

Natasha moved.

Blocked.

But her target wasn’t Kido—it was Uorin, who was pressed flat against the wall.

Just need to kill her, right?

As she charged in at sonic speed, aiming a hand strike at Uorin’s neck—

“Guh!”

Kido had already forced the giant’s foot aside and now blocked Natasha, grabbing her wrist.

Though he didn’t get pushed back thanks to the strength of his spirit-body, the shockwave from her speed hit him squarely.

“Urgh!”

Natasha turned her head to avoid the spray of Kido’s blood.

“…You're one fascinating creature.”

Kido, now soaked in his own blood, bared his sharp fangs, his killing aura surging.

“Grrrrrr!”

“You’re slower than me—so how did you catch me?”

At that moment, Uorin’s face turned pale. She dropped to the ground, gasping for breath.

“Haa… Haa…”

She thought she was dead.

The attack was so fast she could’ve sworn Natasha’s hand had pierced her throat—she had felt the pain.

But… it was just a misjudgment?

Even after experiencing it dozens of times during this battle, she still couldn’t get used to it.

Death.

I can’t die here. I have to live.

Shirone…

“Kido.”

Her voice barely escaped her lips through gritted teeth.

As she spoke, Rai slashed at Kido’s side.

“Guh!”

Kido’s pupils shook, but Rai frowned at the dull sensation of the blow.

Didn’t cut through.

Kido had used incredible flexibility at the last second to deflect the sword’s trajectory.

More responsive than me?

No—he had blocked the strength of a giant and even tracked Natasha’s movements, which were hard to follow with the naked eye.

A monster… in a different sense.

Uorin asked, “Kido, are you okay?”

“…Yeah.”

“Save me. I have to live. You know that, right?”

“…Yeah.”

Her heart was filled only with thoughts of Shirone, so she didn’t feel disappointed.

But—

Water…

Her throat was dry again.

Why is this happening?

Just like when they defended Andre’s Labyrinth, she felt like something inside would dry up if it wasn’t filled.

“Haah!”

Almost on instinct, Kido opened his mouth wide and bit down on Natasha’s arm.

“Chomp. Slurp.”

As he tore through flesh and sucked the blood flowing from it, Natasha didn’t move.

Then, as Kido looked up with blood-stained lips, she slowly stepped back and asked,

“…Did it taste good?”

“Foolish.”

Rai approached, letting his sword hang loose.

“He consumes the memories of others. That guy’s skills are built on the talents of countless people. And you just handed over your manifestation without hesitation. Our battles are going to be even harder from now on.”

“Oh really?”

Natasha shrugged.

“But I was curious. I wanted to know what it felt like to be eaten by another living creature.”

They were from different nations, so there was no reason to cooperate—but Rai found her insufferable.

“You gave away your talent out of curiosity?”

“Haha! Maybe.”

Deciding he couldn’t reason with her, Rai sighed and turned back to Kido.

So, he did absorb it.

Even his gaze had changed. Natasha’s blood was flowing through his veins, carrying her memories with it.

This woman…

Kido’s brow furrowed.

“A genius…”

…Before anything else.

“How much did you train?”

To perform the Dance of the Reaper with a severed spine—how many walls did she have to break through to get there?

“No idea.”

What she had achieved wasn’t something Kido could casually absorb.

“I just kept going until I made it.”

Rai’s face twisted.

Until you made it…?

Wasn’t that a contradiction?

Keep going until it works? That’s not how the world works. That’s why people talk about talent in the first place.

Suddenly, Rai’s thoughts stopped.

Talent, talent, talent…

Rian… that fool…

A swordsman with not even a speck of talent.

Natasha said, “What? Are you done already? I was kind of hoping to see a goblin dance. Well, should we start again?”

“Urgh!”

Blood spurted once more from Kido’s throat.

“Kido!”

Uorin’s sharp voice snapped him back from the edge of death.

I know. I know…

He couldn’t die—not even if he wanted to.

“Grrgh…”

Kido clenched his fading consciousness together and prepared to lunge forward again—when Natasha suddenly stopped.

“Aha, I see now.”

She pointed at Uorin and asked,

“You. Do you love this girl?”

The tone in her voice suggested disbelief—like she was mocking the idea that a goblin could possibly love an empress.

“Come at me.”

Kido’s heart felt even drier.

And at that moment—

“Yes.”

Anchal's voice rang out.

“Yes, it’s fine. Go ahead and speak.”

Her bell-shaped earring trembled slightly. It was the only sign she was seemingly having a conversation with someone.

“I haven’t handled it yet. But soon… Yes. Understood. I’ll proceed as instructed.”

She had just received orders to return to the Jincheon Sector.

Letting the catch of a lifetime slip away…

The Empress of Kashan would’ve been a huge prize. Still, Anchal had no complaints about the new mission.

Is it true…? Using the Grand Demon Spear… If it’s to save Princess Jin Seong-eum, nothing could be more important.

“Your Majesty.”

She was about to say something to Uorin, but then shook her head as if it no longer mattered and stepped back.

Please wait just a little longer, Princess.

A ghostly sigil spun into being beneath her, and her body vanished into the spirit realm like it had been pulled in.

Breaking the silence, Natasha said,

“I’m done too. Balkan’s time limit is up. The Empress can go with Tormia.”

Rai remained silent.

The 4 Gustav’s were always a bit unhinged, but he was still a soldier who followed orders.

The problem is…

He had no way of defeating Kido on his own.

“Let’s go. We have to run now.”

Kido tugged on Uorin’s arm, eyeing the others warily as he started to slink away.

Natasha waved.

“Good luck. Honestly, you’re not much of a looker, but hey, the heart matters most, right?”

She had no eyelids, so she couldn’t smile—but her expression somehow gave that impression.

“Kiiiii…”

Kido let out a threatening hiss as he disappeared into the darkness, but Natasha felt nothing but relief.

“Well, guess I should head out too.”

“Wait.”

Rai called to her.

“Hm?”

“You said, ‘Just keep going until it works,’ didn’t you?”

Natasha realized what Rai meant, and her expression turned sheepish.

Daphne.

Ever since she'd been cut down by Rian, she'd begun to understand what defeat truly meant.

“Sorry. I didn’t mean it like that. I was just saying… for me, that’s how it was.”

Rai’s tone sharpened.

“No need for pity. I just want facts. The truth. Do you really believe that?”

“Hm. If you’re asking that seriously… then yeah. I do. I just kept going until it worked. And after fighting him, I’m even more sure of it.”

“Him?”

“His name’s Ogent Rian…”

Rai’s eyebrow twitched.

“Right, you’d know him. He’s kind of a big deal with a sword.”

“Of course I know him. He’s my younger brother.”

“Oh?”

Natasha pursed her lips.

“You’re Rian’s brother? That’s wild. You two are totally different.”

“What’s that supposed to mean?”

“Exactly this. You put meaning into every word. That guy was the complete opposite. He didn’t even think about that stuff. You have different hair too. I just couldn’t picture it.”

She pointed to herself.

“I lost to Rian.”

So that’s it.

Having seen Natasha’s strength firsthand, Rai realized just how far above Rian must be.

“It was the first time I truly understood what it meant to lose. Maybe the reason he beat me… is because he was reaching for something higher than I was.”

“The best swordsman—everyone dreams of it. But not everyone can become it. I don’t think I worked less than him. So if it’s really just about keeping at it until it works… why doesn’t everyone become strong?”

Natasha chuckled.

“You really don’t get it, do you?”

“What?”

“Don’t take it the wrong way. I’m not trying to belittle you. I mean, I’m a dancer, so my field’s a little different… but ‘keeping at it until it works’ isn’t the same as just working hard or working efficiently.”

“Then what is it?”

Natasha held up her index finger.

“It means doing everything you possibly can.”

Rai stood in a daze.

“If you’ve truly learned something, it means you’ve engraved that skill into your body. And what do you use to engrave it? As far as I know, there’s only one tool. Pain.”

She continued.

“People are selfish. They tend to make the same mistakes. That’s why you use pain to carve it in. Because…”

She recalled her long years of hardship.

“Humans never repeat the exact same pain.”

Rai furrowed his brow.

Are you saying I’m lazy?

Maybe. But ever since Rian surpassed him, he hadn’t once sought comfort or taken it easy.

“I…”

“I know.”

Natasha already knew what he was about to say.

“But you keep repeating that hard process. If you can do it again, it means it wasn’t painful enough. Sure, your skills improve—but that alone won’t let you break through the wall.”

The wall.

Rian had one. Rai didn’t.

Is that it?

That guy had been pushing past his limits every moment of every day.

“Do you want to break through the wall?”

Natasha asked.

“Then engrave your skills into your body—with such excruciating pain that you never want to do it again. So painful that you're sure you'll never be able to repeat it…”

She gestured a graceful curve with her fingers.

“That’s when humans go beyond their limits.”

Every moment…

Rian had been hurling himself into territory no one could repeat.

Seeing Rai’s vacant expression, Natasha turned away.

“Well then, bye.”

“Wait.”

“What now?”

She turned back with a half-irritated look.

“Why are you telling me this? Tormia is an enemy of Gustav, and in the Holy War—”

“Why make it so complicated? It’s nice to share what you know. You like swords, don’t you?”

When Rai still looked puzzled, she sighed.

“You two are really different. Even though you’re brothers.”

Then, a bittersweet smile crossed her lips.

“…Maybe it’s because you don’t feel like a stranger.”

The jealousy and anxiety in Rai reminded her of an old friend—Daphne.

Though Daphne had shattered Natasha’s spine, Rai… Rai still made her hope for a different ending.

“I wish you well. Really. Bye for real this time, Mr. Sensitive.”

As she too vanished into the darkness, Rai was left alone in the empty underground and muttered,

“Mr. Sensitive…?”

Then, with a scowl, he drove his sword deep into the stone floor.

“Damn it! She thinks I’m a joke!”
