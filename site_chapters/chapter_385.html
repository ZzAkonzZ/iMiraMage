Chapter 385: The Meaning of Survival (7)

Alpheus gave a bittersweet smile.

"The vastness of the universe might be no more than a speck of dust to an external being. So what significance do we hold in this universe? That terrifying truth is what Void aims to convey. Shirone has taken his first step towards that truth."

The two remained silent for a while, knowing they were thinking of the same scene.

"How close has that child come to the truth?"

Nineteen years ago, the first student in the school's history passed the seventh stage of survival.

An Unlocker who had grasped the truth of scale and transcended the essence of humanity.

"Adrias Miro. When I founded the school, I hoped for at least one genius to surpass me. But when I saw Miro, what I felt was fear. Perhaps that child should never have been born."

Alpheus, once a renowned genius in the kingdom, found Miro different.

A student whose brilliance and peculiarity couldn't be fully described by the word "genius."

"Not long after the survival test, there was the Judgment of the Twenty. I can't say it didn't have an impact. Everyone wants to be a genius, but for Miro, it was a curse."

Olivia's eyes calmed.

"Does she... resent me?"

"It was a difficult issue. Miro's sacrifice saved us all. And now, 19 years later, Shirone stands here."

That's why it was unsettling.

History doesn't repeat itself just because of some similarities, but the Judgment of the Twenty was a primordial sin of humanity that should never be repeated.

"Don't jump to conclusions. Just because she broke through Void doesn't mean the 'Holy War' will move. At that time, Miro was already a fully developed scale magician. Even if we had sent her into society, she would have been among the best in the world."

"True. But ultimately, levels are drawn to realms."

The limits were not yet visible. Therefore, there was no guarantee that the feared events wouldn't occur.

Shirone's smiling face, receiving praise from his friends, resembled the clear face of Miro from 19 years ago.

'Miro held the lives of all humanity in her hands. Shirone, what will you hold in that vast vessel?'

The survival evaluation ended on the last holiday of the fourth week.

Week 5: Mental Activity Evaluation.

The mental activity evaluation, which assessed changes in the Spirit Zone, saw a significant increase in difficulty from the fifth week with the introduction of the advanced technique 'Joint.'

As an element opposed to endurance, Shirone was focusing more than usual, performing warm-up exercises.

Shirone was quick and rational in his thinking.

But thinking of this as an evaluation of human-level traits would be a big mistake.

The mental fluidity of magicians specialized in mental activity was unparalleled.

For them, belief meant having no belief.

Their minds had no qualms about assimilating with others, even monsters.

"Arian Shirone. You're next."

At the teacher's call, Shirone entered the Image Zone.

The evaluation standard for the Spirit Zone was a diameter of 20 meters. As the floor became transparent, a sphere of mental energy appeared around Shirone.

A signal sounded, and the first problem was presented.

It was a figure-eight filter with two circles attached vertically.

The difficulty of the fifth week was to create a symmetrical Spirit Zone by 'jointing' specific sections of the mind.

'What? This is really hard.'

Watching others, he thought there would be enough time, but experiencing it himself, he realized he was mistaken.

Shirone put all his effort into jointing his Spirit Zone. Meanwhile, the filter approached indifferently.

10 meters. 5 meters.

His heart pounded harder as the distance closed.

With just 1 meter left, the center of his Spirit Zone tightened, and the filter passed through Shirone.

A green light of success lit up ahead.

'Phew, that was close.'

The subsequent problems were variations of the figure-eight filter at different angles.

Unlike the first time, Shirone operated his Spirit Zone smoothly, passing the filters.

Though his mental fluidity was lower, he had another weapon: exceptional insight. Once he grasped the core, his application skills were unmatched.

Having passed all ten problems, Shirone earned 5 points, but his face wasn't bright as he left the Image Zone.

By the fifth week, no student had scored 4 points.

The high level of the students contributed, but mental activity was a learnable skill through training.

Class Two students had already experienced the 30-week evaluation, so they were well-prepared for the fifth week's difficulty.

But that fact wasn't comforting. Class Three also had some exceptional talents.

"Next, Arin. Please come to the Image Zone."

The chatting students fell silent.

It was rare even in society to see a mental magician with the weapon of 'super sense' perform.

The lights in the Image Zone dimmed, and Arin's Spirit Zone became visible, glowing blue.

Among the five patterns prepared for the fifth week, the figure-eight filter Shirone had faced appeared again.

But Arin didn't react.

Even when the filter was just 5 meters away, she simply watched.

"What is she doing?"

As the filter approached her nose, Arin's Spirit Zone rippled like water and tightened instantly.

"Wow..."

There were gasps from Class One. Even Shirone, familiar with Arin's skills, was astonished.

'Unbelievable. How can a human mind change like that?'

For Arin, the world was filled only with sensations.

The murky desires emitted by all kinds of people.

If her thoughts weren't as fluid as water, she would have gone mad, buried under the anger, lust, and hostility directed at her.

That's why Arcane had also rated Arin as the greatest talent in the mental field.

After her near-miraculous performance, the gauge showed a filter volume ratio of 99.9%.

It meant she had shaped the Image Zone's proposed form with no margin of error.

'This senior class isn't easy.'

With all the students' evaluations completed, almost everyone scored 5 points, except Aider, who missed two problems.

The first 4-point scorer in mental activity this year. Shirone felt that dishonor wasn't far from him.

'He seems to have a similar disposition to me.'

Despite his young age, Aider had shown remarkable endurance, reaching Stage 5, Insect King, in the survival evaluation. He had a similar disposition to Shirone.

The difference was that Aider lacked Shirone's insight.

'But he's still young. With 1-2 more years of training, he might actually graduate.'

Aider came down from the Image Zone, looking dejected. Then he gave Shirone a pitiful smile.

"Man, that was really hard."

"You were close. If you had jointed a bit faster, you would have made it."

Aider sighed.

"If it were that easy, I would have done it. I'm not a genius like you."

The implication that he was a decent genius was the youngest's wit. And he wasn't actually incompetent.

"What are you talking about? You're the youngest student in the senior class. That's proof you're on par with us. Plus, mental activity improves with training."

"Honestly, I thought so until last year. But seeing Class Three this time, that thought vanished. Arin and everyone else are incredible."

Shirone also thought the current Class Three squad could rival any prestigious school in the kingdom.

"Plus, my major is in the air element. It's the most common. People say it's for those without talent."

Affinity with an element is closely related to omnipotence. But nothing is absolute.

"That's not true. There are many strong air element users. Sabina is strong, and..."

Shirone stopped.

To boost Aider's morale, Gaold, the head of the Magic Association, would be a perfect example, but he didn't want to risk revealing confidential information.

"Hey, Aider! What are you doing there? Get over here!"

Screamer shouted from afar. Already annoyed by losing 20 points, seeing Aider chatting with Shirone made him furious.

"Oh, I have to go. You know, survival strategy for the youngest."

"Haha! Sure, go ahead."

Aider turned and hurried away. Being still inexperienced, he aimed to benefit from good relationships.

In a way, it seemed sly, but his honesty made it hard to dislike him.

'Do your best, Aider.'

After the evaluation, the students headed to the dining hall.

The senior class had flexible meal times, so the food was prepared buffet-style.

Shirone's friends sat across and beside him, continuing the conversation Nade had started.

"Then I got a cramp in my calf. It hurt so much that I curled up, but I squeezed so hard that I farted."

"Hehe, that must have been awkward. What happened then?"

"My leg hurt like hell, but I couldn't stop laughing. So I laughed and cried! Hahaha!"

Shirone clutched his stomach and stomped his foot.

"I can imagine. In that situation, you'd cry."

"Hey, why are you talking about that while we're eating?"

Despite her protest, Amy had a smile on her face. She had found more peace of mind this year with Shirone's group joining the senior class.

"Hi, mind if I join you?"

Maya from Class Two approached with a tray.

"Oh, of course."

Nade pulled out a chair for her, and Maya bowed slightly before sitting down.

"Thanks. It looked so fun, I wanted to join in."

Iruki said,

"You might not want to. Nade's humor isn't exactly dinner-friendly."

"Haha! It's okay. I have a strong stomach. What were you talking about?"

Shirone watched Maya blend naturally with his friends, thanks to his unique ability to connect with people.

Her uncommon brown skin and the fact that she always ranked last in the graduation exams were all he knew about her.

Sensing Shirone's gaze, Maya turned her head.

"Hey, why are you staring at another girl when your girlfriend is right here?"

"Huh? No, I was just..."

As Shirone stammered, Amy scoffed.

"Hmph, girlfriend, my foot."

Maya playfully glanced at Amy and took a big bite of her meat, washing it down with a gulp of water.

Shirone's group simultaneously paused their knife work to look at her. It seemed like she was forcing it down rather than enjoying it.

Realizing everyone's gaze, Maya quickly apologized.

"Oh, sorry. That must have looked bad."

Shirone waved his hand.

"No, not at all. It's just a bit unusual. You can't taste the meat if you drink water with it."

Maya nodded bitterly.

"You're right. But I have no choice. I have to eat to maintain this body. I naturally don't gain weight easily."

Nade asked,

"Is there a specific reason you need to gain weight?"

"Of course. My major is sound magic, specifically in the buff category. Increasing my volume expands the effect range and improves efficiency."

Iruki, nibbling on some vegetables, asked,

"Does gaining weight necessarily increase your volume?"

"It might not, but in my case, it seems to help. Honestly, it's because I lack talent. So I'm desperately trying to build up my body."

Understanding her reason, Shirone casually continued eating his salad and said,

"You'd look really pretty if you lost weight."

This time, everyone focused on Shirone.

Seeing his friends' surprised faces, Shirone realized his mistake and quickly explained.

"I mean, you're already pretty, but you'd be even prettier if you lost weight... That's what I meant."

Maya, who had been staring blankly at Shirone, changed her expression to a pleased smile.

"Thank you. Shirone, you're really kind. Amy is lucky."

"Hmph, lucky indeed. You'll find out soon enough how frustrating he can be."

Maya smiled with her eyes and resumed cutting her meat.

"Really? I like him..."

Caught between Maya's and Amy's contrasting attitudes, the three boys could only bury their faces in their trays.
