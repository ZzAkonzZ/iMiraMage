Chapter 677: Sensory Overload (1)

After Rian deliberately stepped aside to give the two men some privacy, Shirone let out a deep sigh and spoke.

“Go.”

As soon as the permission was granted, Kargin and Joshua jerked their heads up to look at Shirone.

His face wasn’t as benevolent as they had hoped, but it wasn’t filled with condemnation either.

“If you manage to leave Ludum safely, go find Mr. Brooks and rewrite the contract. Return the commission fee as well.”

Overwhelmed by the thought that he might not die here, Kargin bowed his head repeatedly.

“Thank you! Thank you so much!”

Shirone, forcing himself to remain cold, turned away and flew up to the building, calling out to Rian.

“Let’s go.”

Rian followed Shirone’s rapidly retreating figure, stepping off the wall and climbing the building until he landed on the rooftop.

“Are you going to find the other members?”

“I have to.”

They might choose to leave Ludum like Kargin and Joshua, but for now, they were still Shirone’s team.

As they silently leaped across buildings, Shirone asked,

“……What do you think?”

“You know me. I don’t judge.”

“Not as a sword, but as my friend, Rian. Do you think letting those two go was foolish?”

After a brief silence, Rian chuckled.

“Don’t be stupid.”

Someone who doesn’t value even one life would never care for ten thousand. That idea itself is delusional.

Shirone was, and always would be, Shirone.

***

“What the hell? What’s going on?!”

Brooks hurried down the hallway after receiving reports from his subordinates.

From the room at the end of the corridor, the screams of the Tricerebrate Venetia seeped out.

“Stop! Please, stop!”

When Brooks arrived, he paled at the sight of Venetia’s eyes, consumed by madness.

“Hey! Snap out of it! What’s happening?”

Brooks existed in the present.

***

—Wasn’t this what you wanted?

The Ra Enemy speaking to Venetia was from the past.

“No! Not like this!”

When the Ra Enemy had visited Brooks’ mansion, Venetia had resolved to face him directly.

“What are you scheming?”

Having relinquished her position as the head of Spectrum, she believed she wouldn’t be used by him anymore.

“Why not see for yourself?”

Memories of the Ra Enemy began flowing into Venetia’s brain, the one responsible for her past.

‘This is…….’

An incident.

A story from the Ra Enemy’s distant past, so remote it couldn’t even be called history.

“Kyaaaah!”

Tears of blood streamed from Venetia’s eyes.

‘How, how could this happen?!’

Was this a calamity or a blessing?

One thing was certain: this was an event so anomalous that even the Tricerebrate’s brain couldn’t analyze or predict it.

“Ah, ah……”

Venetia’s face, twisted in agony, suddenly relaxed into a dazed, euphoric expression.

‘I see. God. So this is…….’

The shocking truth God had concealed.

‘We can’t win. No matter what we do, we can never defeat Ra.’

The Ra Enemy approached with a sad smile.

“That’s right.”

As he slowly reached out and closed Venetia’s eyelids, she could feel her life fading away.

“That’s who I am.”

Venetia prayed desperately.

‘Get out of there, Shirone.’

Her body collapsed forward, and her three brains shut down one after another.

‘You must not meet the Ra Enemy.’

No one should try to save humanity.

“Hey, Venetia! Snap out of it!”

Brooks flipped Venetia over and shook her shoulders, but her body was already cold.

“Damn it! Do you know how much she’s worth? Call a doctor! Now!”

The Ra Enemy from the past gazed at Venetia’s corpse before slowly turning and leaving the room.

Brooks shouted again.

“Damn it! Do you know how much she’s worth? Call a doctor! Now!”

***

The explosion of the Golden Jade scattered the Shirone Mercenary Corps in disarray.

While it didn’t matter much to Rufist, Aria’s absence was unsettling.

“Where did she go?”

Jane, who had followed Rufist, soared into the air and scanned the surroundings.

“It seems she’s gone deep inside. She might be approaching Bioflora on her own. Let’s wait.”

With Quan as her bodyguard, nothing major should happen, but the code imprinted in Aria’s subconscious was a matter of utmost importance.

“Find her. I’ll head to Bioflora first.”

Splitting up would ensure perfection.

“Understood.”

As Jane boarded the Cosmic Rail to depart, Rufist added,

“Jane.”

“Yes?”

“I won’t tolerate a second mistake.”

Nothing other than reclaiming Bioflora as a national asset should interfere with the mission.

“……Understood.”

As the Cosmic Rail sped away and disappeared beyond the buildings, Rufist glanced at the spire of Bioflora.

“It’s quiet.”

He muttered as he walked,

“What are they plotting?”

***

“Kyaaah!”

Aria, crouched beside a building, screamed as she clutched her head.

Members of Vega, a subordinate organization of Spectrum, were battling Quan.

The Tuvos, a race with bovine faces and abnormally developed lower bodies, were among the most formidable fighters in the lower ranks, but not a single one could surpass Quan’s blade.

“Where the hell is she?!”

None of the Tuvos had even seen Quan’s face.

The movements of the Pierrot were too precise, and those who did see his face didn’t live to tell the tale.

A gust of wind blew, and Quan appeared before Aria.

When she slowly opened her eyes, she saw countless bovine corpses strewn about.

“Are you alright?”

As long as Quan was guarding her, she was safe.

But the heat from the Golden Jade, which had ignited Bioflora, was intense, and without special defenses, her wrists and ankles were covered in severe blisters.

“Yes, I’m fine.”

Despite her words, Aria’s face was pale.

‘She’s infected.’

Quan’s sword was useless when it came to saving lives, though it excelled at taking them.

“I can go. The code……”

Aria’s condition was deteriorating rapidly.

Ludum was a filthy place teeming with various demi-humans, making it hard to identify the cause of her illness and treat her.

“Quan.”

Jane descended vertically from the sky, leaping off the Cosmic Rail.

“Why are you still here?”

“As you can see, the subject is injured.”

As Aria shivered from chills, Quan offered an excuse that wasn’t really an excuse.

“I can’t cut germs.”

It wasn’t Quan’s fault.

“Jane, please…… take my code……”

Aria pleaded with desperate eyes.

If she lost consciousness, it was better to transfer the code to Jane as soon as possible.

The problem was that transferring the code without accessing Bioflora’s main system would result in Aria’s death.

“It’s okay.”

She had already made up her mind.

“I’m also a diplomat of the Tormia Kingdom. I know that in a national crisis, I’m the first to be sacrificed. Take the code.”

Jane looked at Aria with pity.

‘A tool of the kingdom.’

According to Rufist’s philosophy, humans were nothing more than expendable tools to maintain the system.

***

—I won’t tolerate a second mistake.

The guild master’s voice seeped into her mind like a hypnotic suggestion.

“There’s one way.”

Aria stared blankly at Jane’s words.

“What way?”

“I’ll connect my mind to yours and infiltrate Bioflora. If we transfer the code in the main system, there won’t be any issues.”

As a mental mage, Jane could maximize her Spirit Zone’s tendrils to connect with Bioflora.

“But then you’ll be in danger!”

In that state, she wouldn’t be able to fight at full strength.

Moreover, the place she had to reach was the heart of Bioflora, where the upper-class demi-humans resided.

“You’re right, Aria. We’re tools of the kingdom.”

Jane placed a hand on Aria’s shoulder.

“But we’re not suicide squads. If there’s a way, it’s only human to take it.”

“Jane……”

As Aria’s eyes welled up, Jane turned away.

“Protect Aria. It won’t take long.”

Recognizing the gravity of her resolve, Quan bowed respectfully.

‘Cosmic Rail.’

After connecting her Spirit Zone to Aria, Jane soared back into the sky on the Cosmic Rail.

“Impressive.”

Despite the mental burden, the speed at which the rail unfolded was unchanged.

***

In a secluded corner of the concealed area, Maylay knelt with her left ear covered.

***

‘They said to gather at Bioflora, right?’

Wig of the Storm ran straight toward Bioflora as Rufist had instructed.

On the way, he encountered a Pidow unit, but as a swordsman recognized by the world as divine, his dazzling twin blades left only pig heads rolling in his wake.

“Hweee. Hweee.”

At first, he thought it was the sound of the wind, but upon realizing the melody was intentional, he immediately stopped and assumed a combat stance.

“Hweeee. Hweee.”

The mournful tune fit perfectly with Ludum’s desolate landscape.

“Who’s there? Show yourself.”

Whoooosh!

At that moment, a deafening engine roar came from Bioflora, and all of Ludum’s concealment devices shut down.

“What the?”

As Wig looked around in confusion, he spotted a single goblin in his line of sight and raised his twin blades.

‘What’s with that guy?’

A goblin in a tattered red cloak, with a distinctive hooked nose and goggles, sat on the rooftop railing, whistling.

The whistling faded into the air, and the goblin adjusted his goggles with a finger before looking up at the overcast sky.

“Is there a need to fight, comrade?”

It was Kido, the leader of the Speedkillers.

“For what purpose have you set foot in this ruined world?”

“Tch.”

Wig spat on the ground and pointed his twin blades at Kido.

“Stop acting all profound, you ape. Come at me. I don’t have time for this.”

Kido leaped down, his cloak fluttering, a 1.5-meter-long spear with blades on both ends in hand.

“Life must eat, and to eat, it must fight. Life itself is battle. Pitiful human, are you also trapped in the cycle of consumption and reproduction?”

“Don’t compare me to you savages. You’re ugly creatures who cannibalize your own and rape indiscriminately.”

“Without reproduction, there is no consumption.”

As Kido swung his spear, a whistling sound cut through the air.

“If you eat, and eat, and eat, until only you and one other human remain in the world……”

The spinning afterimage halted abruptly, drawing a horizontal line.

“Answer me. Will you reproduce with that human, or consume them?”

Irritated for no reason, Wig shot forward like the wind.

“Who the hell are you preaching to?”

His twin blades flashed as Kido’s spear scattered afterimages.

***

Goblin Spear Arts—108 Torments.

The sharp whistle pierced Wig’s eardrums.

***

“It seems everyone’s gathered.”

Arkman, the master of the Silver Ring Guild, spoke to Etella.

Behind her stood Yordic of the War Chariot Guild, Ivian and Elwi of Bloodrose, and the heavily armored swordsman Vaikon.

Arkman looked bitterly at the corpse of Kon, a former guild member.

Amid Spectrum’s subordinate organizations searching the area, who to save first was purely a matter of luck, and Kon had been the unluckiest of all.

‘Stupid bastard. You should’ve survived somehow.’

“I’ll head to Bioflora now.”

The lack of response was because they knew the reality of this operation.

In a mission where an ancient weapon would strike the surface, there was little they could do.

“I won’t ask you to come with me. No, all of you should just go back now.”

It was Shirone’s decision to make, but Etella already knew what choice he would make, as he had once been her student.

“I’ll take responsibility. You won’t suffer any losses.”

She believed they had already earned their 100,000 gold just by coming this far.

Contrary to her expectations, the members exchanged glances.

‘100,000 gold isn’t enough to retire on.’

As leaders of their respective guilds, mission failure would deal a fatal blow to their reputations.

Arkman glanced at Bioflora, barely 300 meters away.

‘We’re almost there anyway.’

With their core members’ skills, they could probably hold their own once they regrouped.

“If the Silver Ring stays, so does the War Chariot.”

As Yordic spoke, Ivian didn’t back down either.

“I’ve spent my life building this guild. I can’t give up here.”

As Etella sighed at their resolve, a group of dwarves with childlike faces emerged from the path.

The magic race, Filkers.

“Found them! They’re here!”

As a Shining Spell activated above the Filkers’ heads, goblins, Pidows, and Tuvos swarmed in.

With Bioflora nearby, the enemy numbers were bound to grow, and the path was soon packed with foes.

“Tch! Let’s break through!”

The united mercenaries prepared for battle as demi-humans surged in like a tide.

With the masters of the kingdom’s three great magic guilds present, their firepower was terrifying.

As Ivian bound the enemies’ weapons with a Magnetic Field, Vaikon charged in with his massive frame and swung his sword.

“You’re worse than insects!”

Driven by the thought that he hadn’t pulled his weight until now, he moved faster than usual—until a dagger flew from the side.

“Guh!”

The blade, shaped like a shell, bounced off with a clang, and Shagal, clearly abnormal, thrust his face forward.

“Shagal!”

Etella shouted, but the wave of demi-humans quickly cut off Shagal’s figure.

‘He’s dangerous!’

As she punched through the demi-humans to clear a path, the distinctive sound of rapid slashes echoed.

“You think I’ll die after coming this far?!”

Vaikon twisted his body left and right, deflecting Shagal’s dagger off his heavy armor.

‘Thick.’

Shagal’s interest ended there, and with a shing, three slashes aimed for the gaps in the armor.

“Gyaaaaah!”

Vaikon writhed as shells embedded themselves in both his eyes and the left side of his neck.

“Use Schema to tighten your muscles! If you leave it like that—!”

Before Etella could finish, blood spurted from Vaikon’s neck.

The speed of the attacks meant there was no time to consider the enemy’s condition.

Twelve more shells pierced Vaikon’s armor gaps, and his corpse convulsed before collapsing.

“Vaikon!”

Etella tried to rush forward, but Shagal’s dagger flew at her first.

Unlike before, this strike was aimed squarely at her forehead, forcing Etella to halt and lean back.

‘What the hell are you?’

Why was Tia still alive?

‘What’s real, and what’s an illusion?’

As Shagal blocked the mercenaries, the Filkers behind him whispered innocently.

“Huh? Are they on our side?”

“Whatever! Just fight already!”

The demi-humans, their morale blazing, charged at the mercenaries—just as Shagal drove his rapid slashes into a goblin’s forehead.

“Guh!”

“This bastard’s an enemy!”

The Speedkillers swiftly changed targets, and countless daggers filled the air.

‘Just kill them all.’

Only when no life remained in this world would the truth become clear.

Horrific screams erupted one after another as over twenty bodies collapsed, blood gushing.

Shagal reappeared, and Etella trembled as she stared at him.

“I’ll kill every last one of you.”

Shagal was a born killer.

And he was evil incarnate.
