Chapter 565:

As the moon rose, a man and a woman met behind the graduation building.

Dorothy had arrived first and was waiting, and five minutes later, Iruki appeared.

"You're late. Five minutes."

Dorothy chided in her characteristic tone, but Iruki's mind never forgets the time.

"You came five minutes early."

"That's right. My watch is five minutes fast."

Dorothy turned her head shyly.

"But I always forget."

Iruki couldn't analyze what this conversation meant to Dorothy.

"Then let's get to the point."

As Dorothy nodded, Iruki explained about the Scramble Royal.

She didn't interrupt until the explanation was over.

"The Scramble Royal will take place every day from noon to midnight. You can attend the morning evaluations, and if necessary, you can also attend the afternoon evaluations. It's not a bad proposal since you can bring down many who are ranked higher than you."

"Anything else?"

It was Dorothy's only question.

"Anything else?"

"Is that why you called me? Nothing else to say?"

After some hesitation, Iruki spoke.

"There is. Honestly, if you participate in the Scramble Royal, you'll have to give up the afternoon evaluations. It's not a restriction, but realistically, that's how it is."

"……"

Dorothy looked at Iruki for a long time before gently placing her hand on his cheek.

"Slap."

She made a sound effect with her mouth, but Iruki's expression remained unchanged.

"Alright. I'll do it."

"Thank you. On behalf of the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Group."

"It's not a bad proposal, as you said. I just need to go to the research group on holidays, right?"

Before Iruki could answer, Dorothy turned and headed back to the dormitory.

The sixth day's evaluation of the week ended, and the day for the final negotiation of the Scramble Royal was just one day away.

Neid arrived at the research group with hollow eyes, a shadow of death looming over him.

"I couldn't find anyone. I'm dead."

Despite his loud boasts, most of the students he had approached showed no interest.

The risk of being hindered during the participation period was greater than expected.

"No, that's not it."

Iruki, who had already recruited Dorothy, was the only one with a relaxed expression, wiggling his fingers.

"They calculated the benefits of not participating, in addition to the risk."

Shirone nodded gloomily.

"While we're participating in the Scramble Royal, they can gain more points than us."

"Ugh! Is that the problem? If we just win, we can surpass the Class One kids!"

Unlike the excited Neid, Iruki remained calm.

"Low risk, low return. It's not a bad choice. Besides, with that kind of determination, they wouldn't be much help anyway. It's time to consider realistic alternatives."

Since the realistic alternative was ultimately Sabina, the distressed Neid kept silent.

Honestly, at this point, he would welcome anyone's help, but he hoped not to get entangled with her.

"Alright. I'll go talk to her."

"Hurry. Go now. We really don't have much time. I'll go see Amy again."

As Shirone left the research group with a gloomy face, Neid followed and looked back at Iruki.

"What about Shirone? It's true that it's an unreasonable schedule. Both the one asking and the one refusing must feel bad. Won't it become awkward again?"

"Amy will join. Don't worry, just do your best."

"Huh? What do you mean?"

Iruki, as if he hadn't heard, muttered while looking at the wall.

"At this point, we have no choice but to use the final card."

"Sigh, I'm really going crazy."

Neid, who had turned back from going to Sabina, sat on a park bench and sighed deeply.

'I know. I know too.'

As the president of the research group, he had to take responsibility, but there were some problems he hated more than death.

"What are you doing here?"

"Don't talk to me. My head is a mess… huh?"

Realizing it was Sabina's voice, Neid quickly turned his head.

"How did you find me here?"

"Iruki told me to come. He said you were looking for me."

He imagined Iruki's sly smile.

"What? Not even inviting me to sit? Weren't you looking for me? Should I just leave?"

"No, don't go, I mean…"

Sabina tried to hide her bubbling expression.

"Hey, do you dislike me that much?"

"Who said I dislike you? Don't say unnecessary things…"

Realizing he was being cautious, Neid suddenly changed his expression and shouted.

"Why do you keep bothering me? I don't understand your intentions. You can't possibly like me, right?"

"Why do you think that?"

"Huh?"

Sabina sat on the bench.

"Yeah, honestly, I admit it's strange. Since the moment I saw you that day."

Neid didn't move.

He was frozen in time, and Sabina felt a chill from the absolute hostility she had never experienced before.

"Ugh, it's really scary…"

After last year's duel with Neid, she had been tormented by nightmares of him choking her every day.

'How did it come to this?'

When fear is too intense, one might surrender to it, and Sabina might have unlocked her heart to Neid in a similar way.

"It was too scary. Honestly, it still is. So please… stop doing that."

"Oh, sorry."

Neid grabbed his face.

'Damn, I messed up again.'

His true face revealed itself without him realizing.

He hated his face even more because it resembled his mother's.

"But it's not because I'm scared. I've been watching you. Laughing and chatting with your friends, it's different from the Dante team I'm in. It looks really fun."

Neid, sitting on the bench, spoke in a cold voice.

"It's all fake. That's not my true self."

It was the first time he had confessed so honestly, except to Iruki.

"What do you mean by true self?"

Neid looked at Sabina.

"Whether you're a scary person or pretending to be nice, it's still you. There's nothing fake about your appearance."

Sabina, with a kind smile, said,

"Neid, who desperately tries to hide his scary side. That's the only you, and that's the Neid I've seen."

Neid pressed his lips together tightly.

He felt like he would burst into tears if he relaxed his facial muscles even a little.

"I just want to know you a little better. So don't worry. Of course, if my skills are lacking, I won't be of any help."

As Neid remained silent, Sabina stood up with a resigned expression.

"If you change your mind…"

"Scramble Royal."

"Huh?"

Neid, as if making a decision, raised his head.

"Will you join? If you help, I think I can do it properly."

Sabina smiled with her eyes.

Finally, the day of the negotiation arrived.

Neid, who had recovered remarkably in a day, now looked healthy, but this time Shirone looked like a corpse.

Even his friends had never seen him tremble so much.

"What should I do? I couldn't persuade Amy after all."

"Ugh, she sure is stubborn."

Neid clicked his tongue.

"There's nothing we can do now. We just need to negotiate well and balance things out."

"But even that means giving up a negotiation card. I'm sorry! Because of me…"

As Shirone was about to cry, Neid patted his shoulder.

"Don't say that. If you can't persuade her, no one can. It's not your fault."

"Ahhh! It's all my fault."

As Shirone covered his face with both hands, Iruki stood up.

"I'll be right back. Get ready to meet our guests."

"Where are you going? We only have a few hours left."

"I have a prior appointment."

Saying that, Iruki left Istas and headed straight to the store.

It was quiet because it was a holiday, and Amy, who was waiting while drinking coffee, raised her hand.

"How's Shirone?"

"He's about to die. There's no hope."

Amy asked with a guilty expression.

"Is there really no other solution?"

"There is. You can join."

"That again? I told you, I can't. This is my second graduation exam. I can't afford to make mistakes."

Iruki nodded.

"I know. So I won't ask anymore."

"Then why did you want to see me?"

"To threaten you."

Amy raised an eyebrow.

"How are you going to threaten me?"

"You know Kaiden, right? He's the president of the Women's Body Research Group."

"Ho, I didn't see that coming."

Amy consciously tried not to show interest.

"You've probably heard from Shirone, but the Scramble Royal is similar to extrajudicial rules. Once the rules are set, they manifest."

"So?"

"Kaiden has liked Maya for a long time. And Maya likes Shirone."

"So?"

She started to feel she shouldn't listen any further.

"Kaiden holds a grudge against Shirone. He might propose a condition like this: If he loses, he can never date Amy for life."

"So…?"

"Just saying."

Amy slammed the table.

"Are you kidding? What kind of threat is that? What's between me and Shirone? We've already talked about it on the day we returned."

"I don't know about that. I just thought I should tell you since this is the last chance to turn things around."

Iruki stood up and said,

"That's why Shirone was desperate. Don't be too upset that he didn't understand your situation. We also support your graduation more than anyone."

As she watched Iruki leave the store, Amy drank the remaining coffee in one gulp.

"No! Absolutely not!"

Back in the dormitory, Amy checked the time and saw that the Scramble Royal negotiation table would soon be set up.

If they lost, there would be no future with Shirone.

"Hmph! Who said we're dating? Men are so funny. They get the wrong idea with just a little attention."

Participating in the Scramble Royal would result in a significant loss in the graduation evaluation.

"Even if there's a chance of that happening, so what? Not graduating and just dating—what a stupid thing to do."

Sitting at her desk, Amy opened a book.

'Yeah, forget it! Just study! Shirone isn't the only guy, and there's no need to ruin my life over this. Besides… Shirone's team might win, right?'

The pages of the thermodynamics theory book flipped quickly.

'Kaiden.'

Amy slammed the book shut and jumped up from her chair.

"No, but this guy really…!"

The clock struck noon.

As Istas activated, the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Group descended to the first floor, and the six members of the alliance team arrived at the appointed time.

Hershey first counted the numbers.

'Five.'

His expectation that Amy would be included was wrong, but it was actually a good thing.

'Istas is ours.'

With both sides seated, the negotiation began.

"Let's set the negotiation deadline to midnight today."

"Alright. Twelve hours is tight, so let's outline the main points first. Game rules, rewards and penalties, and prohibitions…"

At that moment, the door burst open.

"Hey! Kaiden!"

Everyone turned to see Amy standing there.

"Amy?"

The most surprised was Shirone, but she didn't even look at him and walked straight to Kaiden.

"Do you want to die? Who gave you the right to interfere in someone else's marriage, no, love life?"

Kaiden closed his eyes as if ignoring her.

"I don't know what you're talking about. Nothing has been decided yet."

"Huh?"

Only then did Amy come to her senses and look around.

The participants of the Scramble Royal were staring at her with blank expressions.
