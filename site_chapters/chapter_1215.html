Chapter 1215:

For Code One to be finalized, the process was complicated—but there were less than 12 hours left until the vote.

The diplomats of each nation showed flexibility, and thus Albino came to meet Phedra, the Prime Minister of Arachne.

“Well, well. We’ve got quite a rare guest here.”

Though not a king, Phedra acted with the arrogance befitting the real power of Arachne.

“Tormia must be in quite a rush. What’s the matter? If it’s urgent, we can move to a more private location.”

Albino, knowing Arachne’s specialty was using seduction, shook his head.

“No need. Are you trying to kill an old man?”

“Kekeke! What’s with all the fuss, Minister? Just leave it to me. I’ll know how to treat you right.”

“I’m here to request Code One with Arachne.”

Phedra’s smile vanished.

‘Has this old man lost his mind?’

Code One—with Arachne, a nation already allied with Jive, the top candidate for World Leader?

And besides, even if they met with their ceremonial king, it wasn’t as if that would make negotiations possible.

“There’s no need to make things complicated. If you’re unhappy about something, you can speak to me directly. And from what I hear… isn’t Queen Pony in poor health?”

“I won’t hide it. Her Highness won’t be attending.”

“Then who?”

“Yahweh.”

Phedra’s eyebrows twitched.

“Yahweh will be negotiating on Tormia’s behalf. You do know he’s originally from Tormia, yes? Of course, the negotiation won’t be with you. It’ll be with your king.”

Phedra had no idea what Albino was talking about.

‘You’re bringing Yahweh into this? At a time like this?’

If there were even half a brain left in Tormia, they had to know the momentum was no longer on their side.

‘You should’ve played this card before the Jive scandal. The Pope will never approve of Yahweh.’

Therefore...

‘What kind of bullshit is this?’

It had to be a last-ditch effort from a country that had fallen out of favor with Jive’s alliance.

“Heh…”

Phedra spoke.

“Honestly, I’m disappointed. You’re trying to threaten us when you should be offering everything you’ve got? Do you really think I’ll just say, ‘Fine, let’s go with Code One’ if you keep pushing like this?”

“I don’t care.”

Albino didn’t blink.

“I’m telling you that Yahweh wants to meet your king. That’s all. And this is just the beginning. If you think you can handle it, feel free to get up and leave.”

The world’s strongest mage. The representative of humanity. One of the Five Stars of the Ivory Tower. A symbol of an age.

Yahweh was Yahweh.

‘You’ve got to be kidding me.’

Albino said this was just the beginning.

‘What is he planning to do? Go to war against every country? Even Yahweh wouldn’t win that.’

But at the same time…

‘…I can’t say he’d lose, either.’

As Phedra’s conflict began to show on his face, a sharp glint appeared in Albino’s eyes.

‘He has no choice.’

Being the weakest among Jive’s allies meant Arachne was extremely sensitive to shifts in public opinion.

‘All I have to do is push with momentum. There’s a difference between someone who’s strong, and someone who pretends to be.’

“Why not negotiate with me? Surely you know nothing happens in this country without going through me.”

“You don’t have a vote.”

“Listen. You might think the game is already won, but each country only gets one vote. Rom, Garto, Temica… On voting day, Arachne is just one of twelve countries.”

Albino lowered his voice.

“And you’re not the one casting the vote. Whether your king is a puppet or not, it’s still the king who must enter the voting room, sign the papers, and drop the ballot in the box.”

In the end, the king’s choice wasn’t governed by law but by quantum uncertainty.

Phedra bit his lip.

‘So nothing’s certain until the box is opened.’

“Think carefully. If you’re really confident, then go ahead. But if things don’t turn out the way you want, I don’t have to tell you what’s going to happen.”

Phedra lit a cigarette.

Then, with smoke in his mouth, he simply stared blankly at the floor beside his chair.

Albino smiled inwardly.

‘Dante. You did it perfectly.’

Shirone was the ace in this game.

‘Even if the political winds are against him—even if the odds technically worsen because of it…’

Dante already knew.

‘No nation can ignore a hand offered by Shirone.’

The magical allure of being the best.

‘It’s not just because he’s strong. The concept of “the best” ignores all relativity.’

So now, the most desirable card had landed in Tormia’s hands…

‘It’s over.’

Albino smiled internally.

‘Humans don’t spend more than one minute making a decision. The subconscious has already chosen. The rest is just convincing yourself you made the right choice.’

Phedra stayed silent until he finished his cigarette, then muttered a quiet curse.

Then he chuckled and turned his head.

“I know this is all a bluff. You’re trying to rope me in. And if I fall for it, I’d be a real idiot.”

Albino remained calm.

“Phew! Yeah.”

Phedra nodded.

“The stakes are too high. I know I’m being played, but I can’t place the bet. I’m out. But if you win the pot—leave me out of it.”

“Agreed.”

Crushing his cigarette in the ashtray, Phedra leaned forward.

“Let’s proceed. Code One.”

Meanwhile, Fermi was organizing Omega 999 using the diplomatic documents gathered by his ministers.

‘We’ll worry about value judgments later.’

For now, the key was to arrange events logically, build a framework, and let the outline reveal itself.

‘The country that will become World Leader is… the Kingdom of Iron.’

Fermi’s cold eyes focused on the name, and he moved his pen swiftly.

Time was tight.

‘Where do I need to cut the info? I didn’t think the corrupted files would be this critical…’

With intense concentration, he finished the work and sealed it in an envelope.

‘It’s done for now.’

A faint smile crept across his lips.

‘I wonder how much this document is worth.’

A file containing future data—every relevant item regarding the next World Leader.

‘About half the planet…’

He didn’t need to know the exact amount.

“Haha…”

But it wasn’t just a funny thought.

“I’ve become the richest man in the world.”

He’d always vaguely imagined it might happen someday, but now that it was real, it felt strange.

“Hm.”

With a gentle smile, he counted to ten.

“All right. That’s enough.”

Just as Shirone had become the Infinite Mage…

Fermi, too, had finally realized his dream.

‘Now, only one thing remains.’

And once that’s done, he might finally be satisfied for the first time since he was born.

“Your Majesty, a representative from the World Health Organization, is requesting an audience outside.”

It was Seriel.

“Let her in.”

After passing the security checks, Seriel entered with a smile—though her eyes were full of fear.

“What brings you here at this hour? Missed me that badly?”

“Fe… Fermi…”

Her face twisted, and a tear ran down from one eye.

“Run!”

As if something had been lifted from her, Seriel’s true emotions surfaced.

At the same time, a silhouette shimmered beside her, and a woman with a snake-like appearance revealed herself.

“Tsk tsk. A lady shouldn’t raise her voice like that.”

Shura, 7th in the Council of Ten, wrapped an arm around Seriel’s neck and cast a cold gaze.

“I’m sorry, Fermi. I never intended to let this happen. But this woman messed with my mind—”

“It’s okay.”

In fact, it was a relief.

If one of the brutes had kidnapped Seriel, she would’ve fought to the death.

Fermi turned to Shura.

“Want some tea? Let’s see… what kind of tea should I serve you? That part’s not in the records.”

“So, you really do know the future. Even the fact that I’d come here with her.”

“No need to bring the future into it. I’m not the only one who’s been to Apocalypse, after all.”

That was why, when Basak demanded the world, Shura had thought of Fermi.

“I know you’re digging for information. Hand it over. Otherwise, this girl dies.”

Seriel shook her head.

“No, Fermi!”

She couldn’t understand what they were talking about, but she instinctively knew it wasn’t good for him.

“Let’s go back to the beginning…”

Fermi walked to the table and poured some tea.

“There are two types of tea in my room—caffeinated and decaf. So what kind did the future me pour?”

“That’s not the point. What really matters is: even if you knew the future, would you still have poured the same tea?”

Shura understood.

“Which country becomes the World Leader?”

“Iron.”

Here’s where the problem starts.

“Okay then, why Iron? Option 1: Because that’s the future. Option 2: Because you came and took the future’s information. Option 3: Because even the fact that you came to take the future’s information is already written in the records.”

“The answer is…”

Shura responded.

“Option 3, isn’t it? Otherwise, there’s no way you’d know I was coming.”

“Right. Even if you know the future, the very act of obtaining that information becomes part of the future itself. So you’re wrong. The correct answer is…”

Fermi held up the folder.

“Option 4: Because I, knowing even that it becomes part of the future, decided to give this document to you.”

That was the most accurate explanation.

“In other words, the moment I destroy this document, the World Leader changes.”

“Can you do it?”

“It doesn’t matter. If I destroy the file, only Iron loses. No other country even knows I have this information.”

Shura’s eyes narrowed as she began choking Seriel.

“I’ll really kill her.”

Just as Fermi calmly opened his mouth to respond, Seriel spoke first.

“Kill me.”

Now she understood everything.

‘It’s future information.’ In a world where even knowing that fact could alter the future, Fermi had been fighting alone.

‘That’s enough.’

Because she now knew the truth, she could smile.

“It’s okay, Fermi. Do what you have to do. Don’t give up because of me.”

“Shut up. I really will kill her.”

Even as Shura choked her, Fermi’s expression didn’t waver.

‘Seriel… Amy will die soon. I told Shirone that. She can’t be allowed to live.’

Then what about Seriel?

“Kii!”

With a sinister expression like a snake, Shura moved to snap Seriel’s neck—

“Here.”

Fermi held out the file.

‘No more running.’

Even if the whole world condemned him.

“Gah! Cough!”

As the grip loosened, Seriel collapsed to the floor, and Fermi flicked the sealed document like a shuriken.

“Take it. That’s the future.”

Shura snatched it quickly, tore it open with her claw, and scanned the contents.

“...Hmph.”

After glaring at both of them in turn, she vanished into thin air like a puff of smoke.

“You okay?”

As Fermi bent to check on Seriel, his collar was suddenly grabbed.

“You crazy bastard! How could you give that to her?!”

“I predict… you’re about to slap my right cheek—ugh!”

Seriel punched him in the gut, and Fermi staggered back.

“You said it yourself—Option 4. That it wasn’t 1, 2, or 3. You can’t give it to Shura.”

Because it would make it part of his chosen future.

“If you do, you’ll die.”

“It’s a minor thing.”

“You just gave away the one and only chance to change the future! You should’ve just killed me instead!”

“I can’t kill you…”

Holding his stomach, Fermi gave a bitter smile.

“Because it’s the future.”

There was no version of the future where Fermi killed Seriel.

“You idiot…”

Overwhelmed by a mix of awe and fear, she buried her face in his chest.

“...What are you going to do now?”

“Well, since you hit me… I’ll have to pay you back twelve times over.”

Seriel looked up.

“You have a plan?”

Fermi winked and flicked a single chip into the air with his thumb.

“A depreciated trade.”
