Chapter 636:

'You're the hardest to handle!'

Ignoring the silent cries of the eliminated, Nade approached Shirone.

"I'm sorry."

"It's okay. Let's fight with all we've got."

"No, that's not what I mean."

The electricity surrounding Nade's body dimmed, as if it were falling asleep.

The fact that he had perfectly controlled his magic transformation was astonishing, and the power it unleashed was beyond imagination.

"You are blessed, Shirone."

Shirone didn't respond.

"You could do anything you wanted. No one would criticize you for failing, and everyone around you would support you."

Nade turned and began to walk.

"But do you know? While you were accumulating victories through fierce battles, someone else was screaming in agony, deprived of even the chance to fight."

Nade stopped walking.

"Effort? Experience? If you think that's all it takes to do your best, you're a fool."

Nade had made up his mind.

"I'm telling you this because you might not hear it otherwise. If you fight me, you might die."

As his resentment towards the world and his nameless anger grew, Nade awakened the monster within him.

"Give up. This is your last warning. You can't beat me."

"Fine. I'll give up."

The colosseum buzzed with murmurs.

"If that will ease your pain, if it will make you happy, I can give up."

Nade gritted his teeth and lowered his head.

"…That's why you're a fool."

Closing the distance with Spark Magic, Nade reached out his hand in front of Shirone.

"You said you had no roots. But you don't realize how beautiful what you have is. Is being first in the graduation exam nothing compared to helping a friend?"

"No. This exam is important to me too. Maybe even more important than my friends. But…"

Shirone was sincere.

"It's not more important than a friend's pain."

'That's why I hated it.'

That's why he hated fighting Shirone so much.

Being with Shirone made his disgusting jealousy and anger towards the world so clear.

"Don't talk nonsense!"

Nade struck Shirone's face, sending him spinning to the ground.

"You're always like this! Do you think I'll be moved by your words? Do you think everything goes your way?"

"If you put your mind to it, you can do anything!"

Shirone shouted as he got up, pushing off the ground with both arms.

"So fight with all you've got! Don't ruin your life over that anger!"

"That anger?"

As Nade's reason snapped, his body surged with power, transforming into the form of the thunder god.

"That anger!"

Kaaaang!

The electric roar made the audience cover their ears.

-I'll kill you all!

As Nade's hatred manifested through his magic transformation, Shirone also activated his incarnation technique to counter.

When the thunder god and the Light Angel clashed, the analysts' minds raced with simulations.

"Similar, but different."

Light and electricity.

In the dazzling magical battle that reflected their natures, the audience and scouts were mesmerized.

The thunder god's hostility was so violent that even the Light Angel had to concede, but Liz felt she could hear Nade's scream within the roar.

'Nade, you must not lose hope.'

The fact that he had perfectly controlled his magic transformation showed unprecedented genius.

But he didn't shine like Shirone.

-Die! Friends, family, humans, all disappear from my sight!

"I won't let that happen!"

Shirone, retreating to the edge of the colosseum, planted his feet and activated Quantum Superposition.

"I will become a mage!"

The 300-fold Photon Cannon bombarded the colosseum like a meteor shower.

-Mage, my foot…!

Nade, shedding tears of blood, released his electricity.

"I am a monster!"

Seeing Nade's face emerge from the barrage of flashes, Shirone's eyes welled up with tears.

'No, Nade. You're not a monster.'

As the audience held their breath at Nade's self-proclaimed monstrous cry, Baikal spoke.

"If he had received proper education."

If he had been given the chance to hone his magic with everyone's encouragement and support.

"He would have eventually reached his potential."

Who made Nade a monster?

'I'm sorry, Nade.'

Bolum couldn't lift his head, feeling like everyone was staring at him.

"…No."

Teria, on the other hand, still glared with terrifying eyes, denying her son's pain to the end.

"It's not my fault."

The thunder god, now unrecognizable, projected Nade's hatred, forming a massive electric net.

"I thought he was always a bright child."

Olivia's words made Alpheas smile bitterly.

"He wasn't always like that as a child. He endured well. The fact that he lived a normal life while embracing his magic transformation is a miracle."

"It's a pity. If only we had known sooner…"

"No. I want to believe it's not over."

Alpheas didn't give up.

"When getting angry becomes a hassle, a person becomes an adult. But youth doesn't know compromise. Being angry means there's still longing in the heart."

The pure idealism of youth, wishing for a life without blemish.

That's why Nade, who had ruined most of his life with hatred, might be in more pain now.

-I am a monster!

Just the movement of the electricity filling the colosseum was draining Shirone's mental strength.

'Grr! What incredible power!'

-Who can understand my anger!

The young Nade, captured by the bandits, had to dangle above a pit lined with spikes, his hands and feet bound.

With only a single rope clenched between his teeth, the relentless torture gradually drove him to the brink of madness.

By the time even the mocking voices faded into the distance, the half-conscious Nade dreamed he was swimming in an endless sea.

A distant shore appeared on the horizon, but no matter how desperately he flailed his arms and legs, he couldn’t move an inch closer—a horrifying nightmare.

Nade. Nade.

The nightmare that had been buried in Nade's subconscious began to resurface through the thunder god’s body.

Save me, Nade.

Teria, emerging from the water like a specter, climbed onto Nade’s back, pressing him down beneath the surface.

‘Swim! Come with me, Mother!’

I don’t want to do anything. So, you have to save me.

‘I can’t! I’m too tired!’

It’s fine if you’re tired. As long as I’m happy. My happiness is what matters most.

“Rrrrrghhh!”

As if still biting down on the rope, Nade clenched his jaw so hard his molars felt like they would shatter.

‘I want to live! I want to live, too!’

Then keep swimming! Save me!

‘I won’t do it anymore! I won’t swim anymore!’

Then we’ll die together. Your life ends here, too.

“Aaaaaaahhh!”

As Nade waited for his absent parents, he felt an overwhelming urge to let go of the rope clenched in his teeth.

He truly wanted to let go…

“You don’t understand anything!”

Shirone couldn’t possibly know.

The torment of living a lifetime shackled to rotten roots you couldn’t sever.

“How could you possibly understand?!”

As the thunder god’s lightning grew fiercer, the shockwaves from anti-magic surged with unimaginable force.

There was no escape now.

“Maybe I don’t understand! But you can’t give up!”

Shirone’s heart ached as Nade’s life, projected through the thunder god’s voice, played out vividly before him.

“Fight to the end! Struggle to the end!”

Nade’s face twisted grotesquely.

‘Shut up!’

Who wouldn’t want to live?

But no matter how fiercely he fought, all he would achieve was Teria’s satisfied smile, and his heart would rot further.

“I’m going to die! I’ll destroy everything and die in the most excruciating way possible!”

He would die.

If causing suffering to those who had abandoned their responsibilities could be achieved, then nothing else mattered.

“And you still want me to struggle? You don’t know anything, and yet you tell me to fight harder?!”

“Yes! Fight!”

“Why?! Why should I?! When it only brings me pain, why should I?!”

As the thunder god’s lightning reached an unprecedented level, the shockwaves shook even Shirone’s resilient spirit to its core.

“You idiot! Why can’t you see it?!”

Still, Shirone held on. If he faltered, no one would be able to stop Nade’s descent into destruction.

“If you don’t fight… if you don’t at least struggle…”

Tears streamed down Shirone’s face.

“You’ll sink into the abyss forever!”

As Shirone’s voice rang out, a spell of unprecedented magnitude began to unfold. Countless versions of Shirone started to overlap at a single coordinate.

Quantum Superposition—Wheel of Endless Time.

It began with two overlaps.

Shirone expanded into the Fourth Dimension, layering another version of himself, which immediately activated Quantum Superposition again.

What would be the result?

Miro, who knew the answer, widened her eyes in shock and shouted, “He’s insane! What is he thinking?!”

Events unfolding in the Fourth Dimension were independent. Even a single layer of feedback would spiral out of control.

It was, in essence, a Fractal of Eternity.

‘How does he plan to control this?’

Even Shirone himself could no longer grasp how many versions of him were overlapping.

Some Shirones would stop the superposition, but even if a single one among thousands made a different decision, the fractal structure would begin anew.

He was trapped in a quantum foam, able to do nothing but layer himself further.

“Rrrrrghhhh!”

As the weight of endlessly overlapping events drained all the Shirones of their vitality, they clung desperately, waiting for the moment of absolute control.

‘I can do this. I can bring it all together.’

The more events multiplied, the more variables arose, and those variables exponentially increased the number of future possibilities.

‘Now!’

When Shirone reached the threshold of controlling all possible variables across time, his eyes snapped open, and he glared into the world.

Ultima System—Elysion.

The moment Shirone, now unified into a single entity, stopped his Quantum Superposition and raised his head, Miro trembled violently.

‘So there was a way after all.’

To others, he looked like a single Shirone, but Miro could sense the overwhelming presence contained within him.

‘Perhaps… this is the true ability of McClain Goffin.’

Quantum Oscillation.

‘Let’s live. Let’s live, Nade!’

Shirone ascended into the sky, compressing photons into a massive, pulsing orb of light that tore a hole in the heavens.

The divine power of particles drawn from the Realm of Endless Time rivaled the transcendence of Miro’s achieved state of Samadhi.

The scouts, having glimpsed the realm of the extraordinary, were rendered speechless, accepting the monumental phenomenon with awestruck silence.

‘Oh, God. Is this what it means?’

Alpheas wept tears of awe.

‘Am I… enough as I am?’

The Light of the Mirhi Family.

The mistakes of his youth had left him with a lifelong guilt for betraying the divine gift of his talent.

But in this moment, the genius he had waited for since founding the magic academy had finally appeared—a student greater than himself.

The true light does not falter in darkness. I am but a tool to illuminate that light.

Beyond the veil of a world bathed in white, it felt as though the hand of God was reaching out.

Your sins are forgiven.

After decades of waiting, the voice resonated like a divine certainty, and Alpheas knelt, lifting his gaze.

‘Oh, is this why you showed me, O God, the masterpiece you created…?’
