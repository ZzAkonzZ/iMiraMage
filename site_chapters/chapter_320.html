Chapter 320: Fleeting Rift (2)

Cariel wore an uncharacteristically vicious expression for an archangel. His eyes gleamed with murderous intent, as if he had suffered an unbearable insult.

"How could the leader of the archangels do such a thing? It's like mating with a dog if you compare it to humans! And you want me to forgive her? Could you?"

Uriel couldn't answer.

In a way, Cariel was like his twin. Both were born from the primordial knowledge of the first archangel, Ikael.

But their personalities were as different as their given primordial concepts. And above all, Cariel... truly loved Ikael.

Cariel retracted his halo. But he turned away coldly, clearly hurt.

"If you don't intend to help, then leave. I will break through Miro’s Time and Space. I will annihilate all the arrogant humans who defied God's will!"

'...So petty.'

Uriel looked at his back with pity.

The radiant wings, a symbol of the archangel, spread brilliantly, but in some ways, he resembled a human.

Just like Ikael.

'I'm sorry, Ikael. But we still need you, especially Cariel.'

Uriel placed a hand on Cariel's shoulder.

"Alright. I misspoke. I'll join you in this endeavor. So, I just need to break this Celestial Wall or whatever it is?"

Cariel turned back reluctantly, but soon regained his bright expression and continued explaining.

"Yes. But it won't be easy. Even for you, it will take your greatest strength to break it. I've created a material far stronger than anything in this world."

Nodding, Uriel approached the Celestial Wall, passing Cariel.

Up close, it appeared as nothing but a black barrier. Examining its molecular structure, he saw it wasn't formed through heat and pressure. Likely an explosion. This wouldn't be easy.

"You remember, Cariel, from long ago."

Uriel transformed his halo into a radiant form.

As information accumulated in the four-meter-wide halo, a glowing club materialized beside him. It was four meters long, with a one-meter shaft and triangular ends, forming the Celestial Club.

"Of all the things you've made, there's nothing I couldn't destroy."

Taking a stance, Uriel spun the Celestial Club rapidly, creating a wheel-like afterimage. As the rotation speed surpassed Mach, the air screeched like metal being torn.

Celestial Club Destruction Technique - Celestial Wheel.

"Uoooooo!"

Archangel Uriel, the angel of destruction, held a primordial concept opposite to Cariel, the angel of creation.

In the beginning, the world was born from an amplification of something. Creation and destruction intertwined, bringing forth all things.

Cariel constantly created, and Uriel destroyed. It was the destiny of the two brothers born from Archangel Ikael, the angel of amplification.

"Here I gooooo!"

Twisting his torso and swinging his arm, Uriel sent the ten-meter-wide Celestial Wheel flying. As he launched his punch, the powerful wheel of light struck the Celestial Wall.

Kuuuuuuung!

A massive reverberation filled the Great World War.

But the Celestial Wall didn't budge, and the rebounding Celestial Club flew to the end of the Great World War before returning.

"Haa! Taa! Uoooo!"

Uriel continued to crash the spinning Celestial Wheel into the Celestial Wall.

Cariel, knowing its power better than anyone, winced with each impact.

'Tsk, tsk, so brutish...'

After about ten attacks, Uriel didn't throw the returning Celestial Club again. No matter how hard he hit, it showed no signs of breaking.

"Hmm, this one is quite something."

Cariel smiled broadly, trying to console him.

"No need to overdo it. Even this much will have a tremendous impact on Miro’s Time and Space. It's a shame we can't see that woman's terrified face."

Though meant to be comforting, it only fueled Uriel's determination.

Uriel swung his wings forward, retreating far back. After measuring the distance, he spoke.

"Stand back. This time will be different."

Cariel shrugged and flew to the side of the Celestial Wall, signaling for him to go ahead.

"Huuuu."

Uriel took a stance like a heavy infantryman about to charge, exhaling deeply.

As his halo expanded to 20 meters, the information of destruction accumulated. Golden sparks crackled around the halo, causing the Great World War to tremble slightly.

Even Cariel, usually quick with a joke, looked worriedly around his lab.

"Uooooooo!"

Uriel charged at the Celestial Wall.

The 25-meter-wide Celestial Wheel followed, increasing its rotation speed. The atmosphere of the Great World War was sucked into the wheel's center, creating a fierce wind.

Ten meters from the Celestial Wall, Uriel twisted his torso with all his might.

The Celestial Wheel flew in a curve, colliding with the Celestial Wall. The disc's afterimage vanished, and the Celestial Club embedded itself in the surface.

But the rotational inertia was fully contained in the Celestial Club. The massive Celestial Wall shook violently, and white cracks spread across the black surface.

Kaaaaaang!

With a powerful explosion, the Celestial Wall shattered.

Though he lost the bet again, Cariel's face was filled with joy. It was the destiny of their primordial concepts for Uriel to destroy what Cariel created. Cariel had merely applied that destiny constructively.

What mattered was that it broke.

And according to his calculations, it was enough to destroy Miro’s Time and Space.

Miro’s Time and Space.

Miro had built a temple in an endless, meaningless space. It was an act of squeezing meaning from a vast void.

The temple existed, so she existed. Based on that existence, she erected a massive barrier between Heaven and humanity.

In the grand temple, Miro existed alone.

Sitting in meditation, her biological clock had stopped long ago. In this new time and space, she inherited the destiny of endlessly maintaining the dimensional wall.

'What are you plotting, Cariel?'

Attempts to bypass Miro’s Time and Space had always existed, but recently, the approaches had become blatant. She felt cornered.

When Heaven attacked, Miro blocked. It had always worked. But now, she sensed something ominous.

'What are they thinking?'

A powerful impact struck Miro’s Time and Space. Electromagnetic waves hardened the dimensional wall. Miro's eyes snapped open.

A second and third impact followed.

As Miro’s Time and Space shook, the same intensity of shockwaves hit Miro's mind.

'They're directly striking the space-time. Is this a new technique?'

Even with humanity's advancements, they couldn't match Heaven's technology. They must have found a way to break the dimensional wall with a mecha system, not magic.

Kneeling hastily, Miro formed complex hand signs. Crossing her left hand, with the index and thumb touching, and her right hand, with the middle and ring fingers pressed by the thumb, she created a cross.

As she reinforced the dimension with scale magic, the wall thickened.

'This power... Uriel?'

Miro's face contorted. Cold sweat dripped, and her crossed arms trembled.

How much time had passed? Suddenly, the impacts stopped.

Breathing heavily, she collapsed to the floor. Even with the reinforced wall, she couldn't hold out in this state. A new method was needed.

"Ah!"

As the Celestial Wheel struck the Celestial Wall, an unprecedented shockwave hit Miro’s Time and Space.

Miro was thrown tens of meters as if directly hit.

Sliding across the floor, she gritted her teeth and endured. Her eyes trembled with confusion.

The impact had cracked Miro’s Time and Space.

'At this rate, it'll break. No, it's already broken.'

Miro decided to use her last resort.

Sitting cross-legged, she floated her fist above her palm and closed her eyes. There was no time to deliberate. If the dimension remained empty for just ten more seconds, Heaven's army would invade without resistance.

'Entering Samadhi.'

Once in Samadhi, she couldn't escape on her own. It was a state of absolute concentration, devoid of any thought.

As the sole guardian of the space-time, she had to remain vigilant. But now, it was time to fall into a deep sleep.

She focused on focusing. And focused again. Repeating the process endlessly, her ego faded, and she reached a state of no thought. Only the inertia of descending into the abyss remained.

In return, she gained immense mental energy. The dimensional wall thickened infinitely, sealing the cracks. Even Uriel couldn't break it now.

The temple's tremors subsided, but Miro remained motionless, like a stone.

Lost in infinite concentration, she forgot her existence. And unless someone came to wake her, she would remain in that state forever.

"What happened?"

At the top of the central control system, Cariel and Uriel analyzed the experiment's results. Cariel watched the screen with interest.

Miro’s Time and Space had cracked. But it quickly recovered before Heaven could exploit it. Miro's response was impressively swift.

Most remarkable was that Miro’s Time and Space had become significantly stronger after the recovery. The durability readings on the screen made even Archangel Cariel shiver.

"It's still getting stronger. What did she do? Can a human really achieve this?"

Uriel simplified the complex results.

"Did we fail?"

Cariel smiled wryly.

"But we achieved something. About three seconds by Earth's standards. If we had three more seconds, we could have completely broken it."

"I see."

Uriel didn't feel regret. In fact, he wasn't sure. Maybe he even felt relieved.

"The technology of the Celestial Wall itself can be considered a success. But we underestimated Miro. She truly represents the Nephilim of Earth. How did she strengthen it? Now, even a decent Celestial Wall won't make a dent."

"Humans are strange. They seem infinitely weak but can become infinitely strong. Goffin was like that. Miro is a formidable human too. Maybe that's why Ra blocked access."

"Hmph, they're still just humans."

Cariel didn't acknowledge humans.

They were unclear, chaos itself. That's why Ra controlled their lifespan under the guise of immortality.

"So, what now? The strategy to destroy the dimensional wall failed. It will be harder to attack in the future."

Cariel wasn't worried.

"We'll find another way. What's important is that Miro’s Time and Space cracked. Interesting things will happen. This was a good experiment."

Cariel pointed to the monitor. The process of the Celestial Wall experiment was displayed in a 3D image.

A reference line was drawn at a specific point on a large sphere, showing durability values as numbers. When the Celestial Wheel struck, cracks appeared.

Such cracks wouldn't allow Heaven's army or any creature to pass through. But that was within the realm of living beings.

On the outskirts of Heaven, there are all sorts of strange entities that defy human definitions. And at this moment, something had slipped through the cracks into the Land of Earth.

"What is that...?"

Cariel couldn't answer.

It wasn't due to a lack of knowledge. It was because whatever had entered the Land of Earth existed in an 'unknown state.'

After the magic school's graduation ceremony, Shirone took the carriage sent by the Ozent family back home.

Reflecting on the journey, he realized it had been a year since he enrolled. It was a short time, but so many events had occurred.
