Chapter 844: Participation (4)

‘What is evil?’

Is chaos this lonely?

A being that cannot communicate with anyone outside the law, not even understanding itself.

So lonely that it cannot feel anything even if it kills the entire world.

“Love? Who? This goblin?”

It was a question only Havitz could ask, from the perspective of whether a human could love a beast.

“Shirone.”

Sadness filled Kido's eyes.

“Shirone?”

Smoto spoke.

“The wizard from the Ivory Tower I mentioned before. Balkan defines him as the philanthropy of this world.”

“Ah, that guy. Hmm.”

Havitz looked up at the sky.

What was he thinking?

Did the word "love" remind him of Abella, whom he had killed with his own hands?

Uorin shook her head.

‘No. He has no thoughts.’

That is chaos.

Everyone assigns meaning to Havitz's actions, but in reality, what moves him is random chance.

“Why did you do it?”

Havitz lifted Uorin's chin with his toe.

“Because you're from the Three Emperors, because you're the Empress of Kashan, did you think you could beat me? Did you really think you were stronger than me?”

She thought she had a chance.

But upon reaching the future Havitz had arrived at, she realized how futile that hope was.

‘Almost reached? Nonsense.’

Even if the Law Slayer had chased Havitz to his chin, failure was still failure.

‘Probability means nothing to Havitz.’

Even a 0.0001% chance, he exists by breaking through it.

‘A monster born of the universe.’

That's how Uorin defined Havitz.

“I didn't know my place. I can't beat you. Please show me mercy.”

Uorin spoke sincerely.

“I want to live.”

In Teraje's life, there had never been a more humiliating moment, but she felt no resentment.

‘This is defeat. That's all.’

This world is structured so that not everyone can win, and at the extreme, there is only one victor.

Teraje had come close to the top but had not crossed the final barrier.

‘The one who rules the current world is Havitz.’

Havitz spoke.

“Fine, I'll let you live.”

Uorin lifted her head, but Havitz had already turned away, seemingly uninterested.

“Let's go.”

Smoto asked in disbelief.

“Where are we going?”

“To another battlefield. We need to finish conquering the world before we're called back, don't we?”

Who on earth was calling Havitz?

“I'm going to the central continent. Leave this to Balkan. He likes this kind of thing, right?”

As Havitz whistled, the beast circling the sky quickly landed on the ground.

As he mounted its broad back and was about to take off, he pointed at Uorin and said,

“Do your best. Enjoy it.”

Having her life spared by her greatest enemy, Uorin felt an indescribable strangeness.

“Why…”

Even though she had abandoned her pride, as a woman bearing the name of Teraje, she had to ask.

“You're letting me live?”

Shame washed over her.

“You were my greatest enemy. Your wife died because of me. Why?”

“You said you wanted to live, didn't you?”

The simplicity of the answer made her thoughts even more complicated.

“I am the Empress of Kashan. I might come after you in the future, and also—”

“I don't understand.”

Havitz was indifferent.

“Whether you're an empress or not doesn't matter. There's no one stronger than me anyway. If you come after me, I'll just fight again.”

The epitome of self-confidence.

Because he didn't understand the good and evil defined by humans, he didn't even know if what he was doing was good or evil.

That chaos itself was extreme evil.

“It was fun. Goodbye.”

As Smoto stared blankly at the beast disappearing into the sky, Uorin fell into thought.

‘Goodbye? It was fun?’

What did this world mean to Havitz?

At that moment, a fragment of information stored in Teraje's vast database surfaced.

‘McClain Goffin.’

Already erased, not even remembered.

But hearing Havitz's words, a memory, like a delusion, came to mind.

‘It was fun. Goodbye.’

Wasn't that Goffin's last words before leaving the photon system?

‘It must be a mistake.’

Even she, who had the background on Goffin, couldn't remember the exact words.

‘But why did I think of this?’

If it were true.

‘If there is a god.’

Does it really care about this world?

If the world we live in is just one of infinitely many creations.

‘Havitz is…’

He would be the human closest to god.

“Don't think it's over yet.”

Smoto drew his sword and approached.

“His Majesty spared you, but to end this war, your head is necessary…”

“Smotoooo!”

Balkan, with bloodshot eyes, stopped the beast and looked around.

“Havitz! Where is Havitz?”

Smoto frowned and said,

“He left. He didn't kill the Empress and went to another battlefield. What is he thinking?”

Balkan looked at Uorin.

Seeing her kneeling, he sighed deeply.

“Ha, it's over. It's over now.”

Even with arrows raining down, there was no future where they could catch Havitz.

‘It was a close call.’

That's why Shirone's law, which made Havitz leave from a place 8 kilometers away, was even more terrifying.

‘The difference between 1 centimeter and 8 kilometers. That's the strength of Shirone's law.’

Balkan gestured for Smoto to get on the beast.

“Let's go. Leave this to the army of hell and follow Havitz. It's best to leave this place.”

The fact that Havitz had completely left Kashan was like a prophecy that a great disaster would soon unfold here.

As Smoto mounted the beast, Balkan grabbed the reins and spoke to Amon.

“Stop Yahweh by any means necessary.”

“Yahweh?”

Knowing that the demons of the 7th Legion were already being massacred, Amon immediately turned around.

As everyone left, Uorin, with Kido unconscious on her lap, fell into thought.

‘Shirone, you came.’

She wanted to see him.

“Your Majesty.”

The 20 Wind Guards created a sound of wind as they quickly circled around her.

“We found a weak spot. We will escort you.”

Uorin shook her head.

“Return to the imperial city. There should be a healer before it falls. Treat Kido.”

“But…”

“I need to see the end of this war.”

Roar!

Finally, Shirone's weapon, Salsu, entered the imperial city.

Every time the Law Slayer, powered by Yahweh's wrath, moved Salsu, the demons exploded.

Terrified screams were heard from all directions, but not a single voice of repentance.

‘Why!’

I want to ask, humans.

Can't you stop what you're thinking right now and practice love?

‘It's impossible.’

As long as even a single evil exists, good is a foolish, pathetic, and inefficient means.

“Argh!”

So people think the methodology of evil is cool, smart, and the privilege of the enlightened.

‘So… are you satisfied now?’

Then why, in a world where absolute evil has descended, do humans cry out for salvation with tears of blood?

‘I can forgive. I will save you.’

But sometimes.

‘It's not too late! Save the people next to you! Then we can win!’

Even that desperate voice sounded like an empty echo, making Shirone extremely angry.

“Ugh…”

Shirone's roar.

Roar!

As it turned into Salsu's scream, the surrounding demons exploded in an instant.

“Yahweh!”

With a loud shout, Amon, clad in black iron armor, collided head-on with Salsu.

“Grrr!” Grabbing Salsu's spikes with both hands, he pressed it to the ground, causing the dirt to exponentially pile up.

“What is that?”

For the first time, the engine of the Law Slayer met resistance, and Shirone narrowed his eyes.

‘A legion commander.’

Even as he was pushed back tens of meters, Amon didn't lose his stance, and Salsu was finally buried deep in the ground.

“Is this all you've got, Yahweh?”

Arriving at the spire of Aganos on the Wind Guard, Uorin surveyed the battlefield as soon as she landed.

“Shirone…”

She had no doubt about the abilities of the Ivory Tower's Great Star, but seeing Amon's skills with her own eyes, he surpassed the stars.

“Die, Yahweh!”

As Amon, moving at incredible speed, swung his sword, Shirone teleported back.

“What?”

Amon, who instantly closed the distance, swung his sword and beheaded Shirone.

This was within one second.

“Die, Yahweh!”

The same event repeated.

As Shirone began to learn from the repeated events, his response methods increased exponentially.

‘Block!’

Death.

‘Counterattack!’

Death.

With each accumulated experience, Shirone's reactions became faster.

‘I found a counterattack route!’

Finally, after surpassing 2,000 iterations, he could see the trajectory of the sword.

‘Strange.’

To Amon, it was still one second.

‘It's not a coincidence. This is some kind of technique.’

Though he couldn't be sure, there was a strange unease in Shirone's movements.

‘It's not a physical-based movement. It can't move like this. Yes, it's like…’

A movement that felt like it was pieced together from countless fragments of the best feelings.

Even though it was a human moving, it didn't feel human.

Seeing movements accumulated from as many as 12,000 experiences made the world feel like it was rippling like oil.

‘I see.’

Amon realized.

‘That's why Satan…’ After approximately 40,000 iterations, Shirone found an opportunity to strike.

‘He is certainly strong.’

In terms of swordsmanship alone, he was not much inferior to Freed.

A photon cannon, containing immense power, was fired.

‘I'll end it here!’

It was clearly a direct hit.

But to Amon, it felt like a nauseating distortion passing through all events.

Approximately 98,000 iterations.

That was the number of times Shirone had practiced to achieve the current result.

“Ugh!”

As the photon cannon struck Amon's abdomen, his armor exploded before his body could even be pushed back.

“Argh!”

Amon's body, pushed back while screaming, collided with numerous demons, sending them flying.

Boom!

The moment he crashed into the wall, a loud noise erupted, and all the demons in the midst of battle stopped moving.

“Commander…”

For the subordinates who had followed Amon through the battlefield, this was an impossible event.

Shirone, breathing heavily, glared at the pile of stones where Amon was buried.

‘I couldn't penetrate him.’

His physical durability was on a different level from other creatures.

“It's done! We can win!”

Watching the battle from the spire of Aganos, Uorin cheered and clenched her fist.

“If we can just take down the commander…”

At that moment, a tremor was felt from the ground, and soon the entire imperial city began to shake.

“Your Majesty! Evacuate!” The Wind Guards shouted, but Uorin's gaze remained fixed on where Amon was buried.

‘It's there.’

Something terrible was stirring.
