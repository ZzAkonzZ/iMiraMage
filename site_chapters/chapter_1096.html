Chapter 1096: The Final Holy War (2)

The desert heat was rising day by day, as if a lens had been placed over the continent.

"This is brutal."

Kanis shielded his eyes from the sun with his hand.

Due to the scorching heat of the Fifth Legion Commander Marbas's demon realm, even the oases had dried up.

Shirone spoke.

"Frigi and Argan are restoring the oases in each city. But it won't last more than three days…"

Zulu pointed to the horizon.

"There it is."

A massive oasis shimmered like a mirage.

"Nagnak. It means the Holy Grail of the Gods. It's a royal territory directly managed by the Kingdom of Paras."

Arin stuck out her tongue.

"Water."

Although they could create water with magic, the sight of an oasis was refreshing in itself.

Kanis spoke.

"The area within a six-kilometer radius around the Pyramid of Truth is a restricted zone. That's why pilgrims, travelers, and explorers all gather in Nagnak. It's the closest point that ordinary people can access."

Shirone took out a permit.

"If we submit this to the National Treasure Management Department in Nagnak, the soldiers will guide us."

While they were talking, they arrived in Nagnak.

It was a community of about 200 households, with many outsiders roaming around.

"Wow…"

As a cool breeze blew, Arin took off her cloak and dried her sweat.

"Kanis, look over there. The oasis hasn't dried up. It didn't rain while we were staying in the royal palace."

"That's because…"

Just as Shirone was about to explain, two familiar apostles walked in from the village.

It was the Ice Dragon Frigi and the Earth Dragon Argan.

"Dragons."

When Arin mentioned what she saw, Kanis's shadow lengthened sharply.

Frigi approached.

"We've been waiting for you, Messiah."

With silver hair and a cold appearance, she never showed emotion, even in front of Shirone.

"The oasis is holding up well," said the Earth Dragon Argan.

"We used our breath to work on it three days ago. We were planning to tour other villages, but we received a message from Kira and are on standby in polymorph form."

Kira was a one-star resident of the Unified Universe Management Department and a master of undead magic.

"Speaking of which, where is Kira?"

"She was supposed to wait with us, but she received an urgent telegram from the Ivory Tower and left."

"Telegram?"

Shirone raised an eyebrow.

Kingdom of Stang.

When Shirone called, a beam of light flew through the sky and landed in front of him.

"You called for me."

It was Lampa, a three-star resident of the Ivory Tower and an authority on information magic.

"I heard Kira from the Kingdom of Paras received an urgent telegram and left. What's going on?"

Unless it was a special case, all matters were reported to the Five Stars.

"Well…"

After hearing the explanation, Shirone's expression became serious.

"So they've finally shown themselves."

"Yes. The army of hell, led by the First Legion Commander Baal, is engaged in battle with the Kingdom of Corona. The situation is tense. All the stars except for you have been summoned to the Ivory Tower."

"The Kingdom of Corona is not weak. What the Great Star is concerned about is… the demon realm?"

Lampa nodded.

"Deadly Cross. Baal, or rather Havits, intends to bring down the Ivory Tower."

"But they haven't opened the demon realm yet. There must be a deeper motive."

"They're likely considering the Holy War. Since Corona is participating, they want to hold the Ivory Tower hostage and gain an advantageous position in negotiations."

Shirone's head was already throbbing.

"But why wasn't I called? I could handle it with a simultaneous event."

Lampa spoke with difficulty.

"It was the Great Star's order. The reason is unclear, but the atmosphere in the Ivory Tower is different from before."

"Mr. Freed and Mr. Amanta. The System Surveillance Department and the Balance Department have lost their Five Stars. Losing ten stars in the entire Ivory Tower is a significant loss. For now, we have no choice but to absorb and integrate, with Ms. Sing at the center."

"Sing."

The Five Star of the Law Department.

"Mr. Sing's perspective on the world is different from yours, Shirone. Although it hasn't surfaced yet, the stars and residents of the Ivory Tower are likely making their own decisions. While Ms. Minerva is upholding Yahweh's banner, many are presumed to follow Ms. Sing's perspective."

"That's unexpected."

It wasn't arrogance.

'I believed we had shared many interactions and understood each other, even if not fully.'

What caused the sudden change in perspective?

"The atmosphere is not good. A grand assembly is necessary, but with the Holy War and the intense battle in Corona, it seems the Great Star has only blocked your summoning."

"Is it that serious? Ms. Sing is strong, but I haven't been idle in the Ivory Tower either."

"That's because…"

Lampa's forehead wrinkled deeply.

"Ms. Sing has approved the Law of Taeguk. This has shocked the stars and residents."

"Taeguk."

Two people came to Shirone's mind.

"Umji and Yangji."

Ivory Tower.

In Sing's room, twin sisters who looked identical entered.

"You called for us."

Umji and Yangji.

They were four-star residents of the Ivory Tower, and their completely different atmospheres made them easy to distinguish.

"Yes. What's the situation in Corona?"

Yangji answered.

"Nothing significant. Baal's army is the most elite force in hell, but I can wipe them out. Please send us immediately."

Umji spoke.

"We must be cautious until all the stars have gathered. First, we need to strengthen the internal unity of the Ivory Tower…"

Yangji interrupted.

"If we use our Taeguk, Sing's law will reach the absolute pinnacle. Let's just go. We'll wipe out the demon army and tear Baal apart."

"That's nonsense," Umji retorted.

"If Baal dies, Havitz will come. If one dies in Taeguk, it will lead to the annihilation of the Ivory Tower. The demons will take over the world. They will torture humans, and we will be captured by them…"

She shuddered.

"We'll suffer all kinds of things. They won't kill us easily. We'll end our lives in agony, writhing and screaming. In the end, there will be only pain. It's over. This world is doomed!"

Yangji shouted.

"It's not doomed! We have eight stars with the law of Taeguk. No Five Star is higher than us! Ah, Sing, I'm sorry. I didn't mean to offend you."

"So what! We're just humans in the end! No different from worms in the ground. Living beings are insignificant! We can never, ever defeat Satan!"

"Enough of this…" Sing interrupted.

"Stop."

Umji and Yangji's expressions reset simultaneously, and they stood side by side like twins again.

"I understand your thoughts. But I won't make any decisions. First, we need to win over the residents of the Ivory Tower. Start from there."

Umji asked.

"With all due respect, why are we splitting up? I thought you liked Shirone."

Sing's eyes darkened.

"I don't trust anyone. I accepted you to enforce my will. Convey my intentions to the stars of the System Surveillance Department. It will be fine if you both go."

As Umji and Yangji left the room, Sing turned to the window overlooking the atmosphere.

"Shirone."

If she had to choose, she definitely liked him.

"Understand me."

Nagnak Oasis.

Regardless of the situation in the Ivory Tower, Shirone focused on what needed to be done in Paras.

The simultaneous event was his unique sense, and sharing it would only unsettle everyone.

"Let's get going."

The group headed to the government office in the center of the village.

It was the only stone house, and across the table, a junior staff member was arguing with some pilgrims.

"We're not asking to go inside! You can't even see the pyramid from here!"

"It's the king's order. The number of people visiting this place has increased tenfold in the past two months. Go back."

"Then at least let my family go! We'll return as soon as we cure the emotional illness…"

"Do you know how many people I deal with daily who talk like you? This isn't a hospital! Go back before I call security!"

Shirone looked at the carving on the building wall.

'The Pyramid of Truth.'

The image of a large eye floating above the pyramid was engraved in relief.

'The All-Seeing Eye.'

Did the ancients know about the eye of the outer world?

'The ancient civilization isn't recorded in Omega. If we're not careful, all the data could be lost. But…'

The fact that the ancients traveled to the Pyramid of Truth was evident from the symbol.

When it was his turn, Shirone approached the table with the pass given directly by the king.

"Hello. The Pyramid of Truth…"

The staff member sighed.

"Didn't you hear what I said? How many times do I have to repeat myself? Do you think working in the middle of the desert is a joke?"

"No, that's not it…"

As Shirone handed over the pass, someone at the end of the table shouted.

"What are you talking about? I got permission four months ago! From the Minister of Land Management!"

"That was four months ago. Don't you know the world situation? The king's name has changed. This document is useless."

"Four months is a tight schedule! An archaeological expedition isn't a joke, with all the manpower and equipment…"

"Enough. If it's not allowed, it's not allowed. Go back."

The staff member turned back to Shirone, smirking.

"See? Even the minister's influence doesn't work here. So…"

As he was about to return the pass, his eyes widened.

"What? This…"

The seal of King Kitra of Paras was prominently stamped in the middle of the pass.

"My apologies!"

The pale-faced staff member stood up abruptly, shouting, and everyone turned to look.

Even the explorer at the end of the table looked at Shirone's group but quickly turned back to the staff.

"Anyway, if this isn't approved, I'll formally sue the Kingdom of Paras…"

The staff member assigned to Shirone spoke.

"Please, come this way."

As they left the table and guided the group, the explorer suddenly realized.

"Hey! Those people! They're going to the Pyramid of Truth, aren't they? What's going on? I thought it was off-limits?"

"What are you talking about? The king…"

Ignoring the staff, the explorer turned and followed Shirone.

"Hey, you there! Wait a minute!"

When Shirone turned around, the explorer with a beard down to his chest grabbed his shoulder.

"You got approval, didn't you?"

"Well, that's…"

"Tell me! No, take me with you! Our team has been preparing for a long time! We can help."

Since it was a dangerous mission, Shirone didn't want to involve civilians.

"Sorry. We're…"

"I'll pay! Is it because of the emotional illness? Or the ancient relics? Please…"

The explorer, half out of his mind, suddenly scrutinized Shirone.

"Shirone?"

Hearing his name, Shirone also looked at the face hidden behind the beard and was stunned.

"What?"

At that moment, the explorer's eyes glowed red.

"Haha! Yes, Shirone! It's really you!"

"Mr. Ares?"

It was Amy's second brother.
