Chapter 999: Tomb of the Gods (4)

Ymir's Mind.

Upon reaching the fifth deep layer, Shirone's group couldn't descend further for seven days.

"This is driving me crazy."

Arius grew anxious.

As expected, the fifth deep layer was a highly rational environment, but the lack of differentiation was the problem.

"Hello, Shirone." A resident, resembling a wooden doll with a white egg on top, bowed.

Then, another identical resident greeted them.

"Hello, Shirone."

Shirone passed by without responding.

'I can't tell who they are.'

The residents had no names, ages, or genders, and the scenery around them was the same.

Simple structures like circles, triangles, and squares were randomly placed in the world.

"This is unsettling." Miro couldn't help but frown.

The residents and scenery they saw were likely how Ymir viewed the world.

"No matter how you look at it, does it make sense for all the residents to be the same? Surely there must have been someone memorable?"

Sein said, "It seems there isn't. He doesn't even care about the background. The world we live in so intensely is this simple to Ymir?"

"He mentioned the Tomb of the Gods," Shirone said.

"At least those were beings Ymir acknowledged. So it's not that there are no memorable beings. At the very least, Rian should be here."

Miro grabbed a passing resident and shouted.

"Hey! Where's Ymir?"

Using the name of the ego in the mental world was taboo, but the resident didn't care.

"Hello, Shirone..."

Miro's fist went through the resident's face.

Sein sighed, but since it was just a mental projection, he was honestly relieved.

As Miro withdrew her fist, the resident wobbled and fell like a puppet with cut strings.

"Do you know what's really annoying? We've met this guy 3,000 times."

No one could prove it, but Miro's point was valid.

"Let's get rid of them all. At least reducing their numbers might increase the chances of meeting new residents."

Shirone said, "We've already destroyed one city, thanks to Gaold."

They had tried everything possible in the fifth deep layer.

Arius said, "The key issue is that no resident responds to keywords related to Ymir. This might be a world ruled by someone other than Ymir."

"We used the names of all the gods Ymir fought, using Shirone's Omega. We even tried 'Hello, Shirone,' the most popular phrase in this world."

Arius suddenly stopped.

"Hello, Shirone?"

Miro glared at him.

"Do you want to get hit?"

"No, this is worth considering. Why do the residents greet Shirone? They don't respond to Gaold, who broke through the nightmare."

Shirone said, "We thought it was related to the Ultima System. But all our attempts were in vain."

"Indeed. What I want to say is that while it appears structured like consciousness, this place is undoubtedly the unconscious. It's not about finding specific keywords like on the surface. That's the mistake."

Miro said, "So you were mistaken?"

"I admit the failure, but calling it a mistake is hurtful to an expert. You can't mistake what you don't know. This is my first time encountering this type of deep layer."

"Alright. Keep talking."

"If this were the surface, the lack of individuality in the residents could be interpreted as Ymir's perspective on the world. But the unconscious has many overlapping elements."

"Ah."

Arius spread his arms.

"In the end, it's everything. All of them, this entire world, are the keywords."

It made sense.

"But knowing that doesn't change anything. We've tried everything."

"Maybe..."

After a moment of silence, Arius spoke.

"There might be something we haven't tried."

Shirone asked, "What is it?"

"Reverse integration."

Arius raised his index finger.

"The residents are Ymir's perspective, but they're also Gaia. The unification mentioned in the nightmare. These are the simplest representations of the Ultima System as Ymir sees it. And Shirone couldn't break it."

Watching the busy residents, Shirone admitted.

"I can't do it alone."

"Exactly. That's why there was no response. But what if Shirone integrates with them?"

Blending in with those who wander the streets without meaning or purpose.

Shirone, who had trained his mind to the extreme, could move in a state of detachment.

"Probably. The residents are integrated, but they're just empty shells without real minds."

Only the concept of integration remained.

"Alright."

As Shirone erased his mind, Sein felt a chill.

'Perfect detachment. Shirone now exists only through the interaction of mind and body.'

Shirone said, "Let's begin."

Though he spoke, his tone was emotionless, like a machine.

As he walked towards the residents, they bowed and greeted him.

"Hello, Shirone."

Listening indifferently, Shirone eventually spoke softly.

"Hello, Shirone."

All the residents in the world froze.

Shirone's group anxiously watched the world, trapped in perfect silence as if time had stopped.

Arius said, "Ymir's unconscious is reacting. Something is..."

At that moment, golden light burst from the residents' bodies and shot towards the sky.

"Idea?"

It was a massive hexagram created by the 10 billion Gaia that made up Ymir.

A giant voice echoed from the sky.

-You who do not belong to us, we deny your existence. Leave this world.

In reality, it would have shaken the heavens and earth, but here, only Shirone's group suffered.

"Ugh!"

-Denial! Denial! Denial!

As the entire world pulsed and pushed them, Shirone's group was endlessly driven back.

They quickly regained their senses but had already been pushed several kilometers away. Miro deployed her incarnation technique.

'Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara.'

The incarnation of Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara, growing limitlessly, clapped its hands.

The tremendous sound wave erupted, but it couldn't stop the defining power of the entire world.

"Shirone! Break the Ultima!"

Shirone reacted mechanically and regained his mind from among the residents.

'Deny the denial.'

Instantly, the Ultima System split in two, and the resulting explosion was enough to devastate the world.

As the scenery radiated like thorns, all structures crumbled into dust, and the residents screamed.

"Kieee! Kieee!"

The forms, halfway between humans and dummies, clutched their heads in agony, terrifying enough to make one's legs tremble.

"It's broken! What now?"

Shirone had caught up with Miro's group, but the residents were still overwhelming.

"Fight!"

Arius, the weakest, took the center, while the others surrounded him in a defensive formation.

Countless residents began to charge at them.

"Get out! Get out!"

A resident with a white face rushed forward, raising a crude fist.

'How strong are they?'

Since it was Ymir's mind, it would be as powerful as he felt about Gaia.

Boom!

Gaold's form, blocking the resident's blow head-on, scattered in the shockwave.

"Gaold!"

As Kangnan tried to approach, Gaold, holding the crude fist, revealed himself.

"I'm fine. I can handle this."

"The Tomb of the Gods."

Arius said, "This world is just the shell of Ultima. In Ymir's mind, there's no being stronger than Ymir."

From then on, it was a continuous battle.

Air Press crushed them, and Hand of God slapped the residents with its palm.

'There are too many. This won't end.'

Even as fatigue appeared on their faces, Luber, who hadn't acted, spoke.

"I'll do it." Flying into the sky, he spread his arms, and rings of light formed in front of his palms.

"Dream Machine."

In the mental world, the Dreamwalker could convert any dream into an object.

However, the further from the REM domain, the lower the grade of the object.

'It should be possible in the fifth deep layer.'

From just below the REM domain, Luber summoned a small wooden box.

"The Butterfly of Zhuangzi."

Though it looked unimpressive, if extracted into reality, it would be an extremely dangerous item.

"Awaken from the dream."

As he opened the box, all the Gaia in this world began to dissolve into particles of light.

"W-what?"

The Butterfly of Zhuangzi was one of three dreams given special treatment by Drimo's administration.

A kind of enlightenment.

If it came into reality, its effect would be 'momentarily escaping the photon system.'

"Ugh!"

As the shock of the summoning shook Drimo, the impact hit Luber, the incarnation.

Even so, he didn't close the box.

'Not enough. Just a bit more!'

The Butterfly of Zhuangzi actually attacked Ymir's mind, not the residents.

But even the unconscious fifth deep layer couldn't be massively shocked.

"Luber! It's too dangerous!"

Shirone shouted, but Luber, determined, held the box open.

"Shirone! Look!"

Miro pointed to the sky, and everyone turned.

The excited particles of light moved erratically and began to gather into a single point in the sky.

A giant voice echoed.

-Denied.

As everyone crouched and covered their ears, Luber screamed and fell.

"Aaah!"

The box closed on its own, ignoring his grip, and a pillar of light struck the ground like a spike.

Boom!

For the first time in the fifth deep layer, Shirone felt a vibration and looked ahead.

"What?"

With steel-like hair and a beastly fierce impression, McClain Goffin stood.

"Goffin?"

As Shirone muttered, the group turned to look.

"What?"

"It's McClain Goffin."

Miro stared intently at Goffin's face.

She didn't remember, but she found her heart pounding with fear.

'Does my heart remember?'

"Focus."

Gaold interrupted.

"He's moving."

As Goffin slowly approached, Miracle Stream surged and shot up into the sky.

"Hand of God."

A massive hand, different from what Shirone had deployed, dominated the entire sky.
