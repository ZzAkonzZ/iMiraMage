Chapter 945: Hidden Code (4)

The war between the coalition forces and the demons in the flower field resembled a life-erasing game of bingo.

Brilliant movements and powerful magic simply calculated the outcomes through simple reduction.

"There's no end! It's not an exaggeration!"

No matter how many demons were killed, they seemed to reappear in their places instantly, and the coalition forces sensed an impending defeat.

"Mwahaha! I'll wrap your intestines around!" shouted a horned demon, aiming a spear at a soldier's abdomen.

"Gack!"

The air erupted with a loud noise, and the demon’s torso twisted and snapped apart.

"Oops, sorry."

Appearing with an afterimage, Natasha grabbed the demon’s spinal column and stuck out her tongue.

"Why. Why..."

The demon, whose torso had fallen to the ground, raised its head in confusion, but Natasha had already turned her gaze away.

'Where did he hide?'

In a combat too fast to be called merely "high-speed," mistaking one’s target was common.

It meant she was focused, and her consciousness was wholly fixated on Rian’s location.

"Yaaah!"

As Rian shouted, the landscape exploded around him, slashing through dozens of demons within a radius of several meters.

Amid the cleared view, Rian’s demon form was visible.

"There he is."

As soon as she finished speaking, Natasha and her avatar simultaneously disappeared from the space.

Rian widened his eyes.

"Time seesaw."

A phenomenon known to humans as "life flashing before one’s eyes," detaching the time of the senses from brain time.

While the body remains in reality, the brain is processing a massive cross-section of signals.

'Normally, you die. If you feel this, you die.'

Except in cases of extraordinary luck, it’s nearly impossible to extricate oneself by one’s own power.

'But I am…'

As Divine Transcendence activated, Rian’s body began to move beyond its limits.

'It’s Ogent Rian!'

As Natasha’s fist struck a broad surface in the instant Rian pulled his greatsword up to his chest, he grunted.

"Guugh!"

Through repetitive training, Rian managed to push his greatsword forward before the shockwave penetrated.

The force enough to overload his shoulders was more about supersonic speed than brute strength.

'Time to counterattack!'

Overcoming inertia, Rian stopped midair and stepped forward, swinging his greatsword.

Instead of her upper body, Natasha bent her knees and rose up like a shadow, shaking her upper body.

'Incredible flexibility.'

Even in a strobe of moments, her upper body seemed to split into two, and a burst of punches surged forward.

'I dodge.'

Rian swiftly twisted his body, pulling in his greatsword to strike her, when suddenly—

"Ugh!"

A dull impact surged from an unguarded flank, and he heard the sound of bones snapping.

"Arghhh!"

Pushed to the side, Rian tried to overcome the inertia but couldn’t muster strength in his legs.

'What the…'

Where and how had he been hit?

As Rian stooped and stopped, turning forward, Natasha was already approaching him.

'This woman, she really can fight.'

It wasn’t so much that humans were strong—though they were—it was more that she had a knack for pushing things to the extreme.

'A genius in combat.'

That was why she was known as Gustav IV.

"Hmm."

Resting her chin on her hand, Natasha examined Rian’s condition and frowned slightly.

"I've felt this before…"

Even Rian, usually indifferent to others' assessments, swallowed and waited for her next words.

"You really have no talent, do you?"

Her expression remained unchanged, but something heavy settled in Rian’s heart.

"Why do you use so much strength? You should move by calculating time and space. Your rhythm is a mess, and you lack creativity. Fighting you is really no fun."

"I'm not fighting for fun."

Natasha easily conceded.

"Of course, you're not. But that’s an important part, isn't it? Do you want to spend your life doing something you find no joy in?"

Rian fell silent again.

"You are a strong person but… I can't find any other strengths in you."

Rian was strong.

But he was probably the person furthest from being good at anything to the extreme.

"That’s why it’s a pity. Want to learn how to fight from me? Just a few adjustments might do you good."

"Shut up."

Rian’s ribs reconnected.

"I don't discriminate when it comes to learning, but you're an exception. There's nothing to learn from a villain of the underworld."

"What if I wasn't a villain?"

Silence lingered for a moment.

"What do you mean?"

"You don’t want to learn from a villain, right? Then, for the duration of your training, I'll be on the side of humans. You have good physical abilities. It would be a shame to waste them."

Natasha remained an enigma, but there was something inherently definitive about her.

"I see."

Rian smiled bitterly.

"You must have no flaws."

Among the many geniuses Rian had met, she was the closest to perfection.

"I’ll take your word for it. Although you're an enemy, I can tell it's sincere. But you can't teach me. If it could have been fixed, it would have been fixed long ago."

"That’s why I'm offering help."

"To someone else, the lack you see in me might just be a minor flaw. Like finding the exact right part to fix a machine."

"Isn't it?"

"You wouldn't understand."

This time, Natasha was the one who fell silent.

"How many times I've swung my sword to overcome that one flaw. What I’ve tried. How long I've resented and cursed myself."

"That's why it's a pity."

"Ever thought about it? Not the flaw itself, but the efforts of the person who has that flaw."

"A genius dancer was born in the Gustav Empire!"

Natasha had stood out in the dance world since she was just seven years old.

Even in front of the empire's most powerful figures, her dance was a perfect art.

"Amazing. That child doesn’t even seem nervous."

She didn’t need to be nervous.

'Rotation.'

With her hands above her head and pivoting on the tip of one foot, her center of gravity was perfectly aligned with the direction of gravity.

'Leap.'

Jumping high with an elasticity uncharacteristic of a child, Natasha’s body traced a beautiful line.

"Wow!"

Even the spectacle of the audience widening their eyes seemed visible to her.

'It’s fun.'

Being good at something.

'I want to be better. Then it'll be more fun.'

The angle of each of the 10 fingers, the bending of the knees, the position of the chin, the degree of gravity, the distance from the ground, air resistance, sightlines, facial muscles.

'I can feel it all.'

Not checking and adjusting each one, but her entire body becoming a brain.

During her time in the air, countless thoughts passed.

'And upon landing...'

As the music sped up, Natasha’s toes began to cross rapidly.

Music flowed in her mind, and her body continually broke the rhythm.

Rhythm.

A measure of how many events can fit into a single moment.

"She's a genius! No need for further words!"

Although her peer Daphne was there, only Natasha was seen in the eyes of the officials.

Even the head of the dance troupe felt the same.

'Rhythmic deviation. At least one-thousandth of a beat.'

When some rhythms are cut on the beat, someone else taps the rhythm 0.1 beats slower.

If that's called a tenth of a beat, a skilled person can feel up to a hundredth of a beat.

But being off by one-thousandth of a beat from the beat is beyond human perception.

The sense itself that rushes in feels almost like goosebumps.

"Bravape!"

Applause burst out as the Gustavian word for "amazing" was uttered.

Natasha became a star overnight, but in her mind, she only wanted to excel in dance.

"Daphne."

In the practice room, Natasha called out.

"Hmm?"

Though they were close peers, Daphne felt overshadowed by the world's attention on Natasha.

"Your center is a bit off,"

"Really? I didn’t notice when I looked in the mirror."

"About two degrees. We're young and our muscles are weak, so it's crucial to precisely find your center. When you rotate, don't focus on the axis of rotation but imagine a circular band."

"A circular band?"

"Centrifugal and centripetal forces. Instead of focusing power, imagine that the power gathers by itself."

"Ah..."

Realizing something, Daphne bounced her bent knees and spun on her toes.

"That's it!"

Surprised at herself, Daphne briefly wore a dazed expression, then grabbed Natasha’s hand.

"Thanks, Natasha. I thought you'd become famous and wouldn't care about someone like me."

"What are you talking about? I hope everyone can know the joy of dance. Let’s work hard and stand on the same stage."

"Yeah."

Tears welled up in Daphne’s eyes.

As time passed, Natasha and Daphne reached adulthood.

Daphne tried hard, but ultimately, Natasha, not just in Gustav but perhaps the best dancer in the world, was unmatched.

"Daphne, don’t listen to the music, try to play it in your mind. While doing that, push slightly like this. This way with your fingertips..."

Even Natasha’s hand movements were mesmerizing.

"Amazing."

While it wasn’t visually apparent what was different, Daphne certainly felt it.

"A tenth of a degree, no, a hundredth of a degree difference?"

Daphne forced a smile.

"Yeah, I’ll try."

"Good. Fight on!"

Returning to her spot to resume practice, Natasha watched as Daphne’s emotions became complex.

"She never rests."

Unlike other young people who frequented cafes or dated, Natasha had no interest in those things.

Only dance.

When dancing, Natasha always wore the happiest expression in the world.

As Daphne bowed her head for a moment, she grabbed her bag and passed by Natasha.

"I'll head out first. I’m not feeling well."

"Huh? Suddenly? Where do you feel bad?" Without answering, Daphne opened the door to the practice room, her eyes filled with despair.

And so, the years went by.

One day, Natasha was abducted by a thug, resulting in a broken spine.

It was the end of a genius dancer’s career.

Coming out of her reverie, Natasha spoke.

"I’ve never thought about it, but if that’s really why you hated me..."

Natasha’s cheeks puffed up.

"That's a bit unfair."

"I don’t know what you’ve been through, but I do know this. To you, it's just a flaw, but for them, it was a lifetime burden. Unless you understand this...

Rian aimed his greatsword.

"Your kindness will never be understood."

Rian’s words, oddly enough, hurt Natasha more than a threat to kill her.

"I didn’t try hard enough."

For the first time, a chill filled the eyes that were always bright like a cat’s.

"I didn’t try as hard as me."

"That’s right. I don’t know who you’re talking about, but it definitely isn’t you."

The puppet master controlling Natasha moved.

"You took away the most important thing." Natasha never bothered to find out whether Daphne, who hired a thug to beat her up, was actually responsible or not.

Rather than dwelling on irreversible events, her tendency was to strive to be the best in her field.

That extreme tendency itself was genius...

'I want to dance.'

The days when she was simply happy to let her body flow with the music were gone.

"I kill. That is my job."

Dance of Death—Rhythmic Concerto.

In the seesaw of time, the moment Natasha moved, a shiver ran down Rian’s spine.

'What?'

It was a thousandth of a beat.
