Chapter 1030: Black Chains (2)

Boyd, Deputy Director of the Energy Management Corporation.

After receiving the call, he arrived at the Karma Management Department and bowed respectfully to Lethe’s orders.

“Yes, yes.”

Unlike Monoras, Boyd was efficient in his work and had no desire for material wealth.

“When you arrive in Raviette, meet with the chief of staff first. It seems they’ve been delayed due to an issue... Anyway, after that, go to the duke and deliver the message exactly as I told you.”

“Understood.”

If it were up to her, Lethe would have gladly entrusted everything to Boyd.

“Ugh, that insufferable pig.”

But thinking about the humiliation Monoras would suffer made it impossible for her to harden her heart completely.

“Proceed as planned. Support the chief of staff well, and make sure there are no errors.”

“...Yes.”

Thinking about working alongside Monoras darkened Boyd’s outlook, but how could it be worse than what Lethe endured?

‘I’ll help you, Director.’

To Boyd, Lethe was the most rational figure in Hell and the mother of all demons.

‘Monoras is the only one who doesn’t get it.’

As Boyd left with a cold gaze, Lethe sighed and brushed her hair back.

“Phew, now I’ll try to get some sleep...”

“Director!”

Vivian shouted.

“Classification number 4984! Incident at the Wall of Lamentation! That’s where Rian is!”

Lethe snapped awake, twisting her body around.

“What’s happening?”

Without needing further explanation, the screen showed an explosion at the Wall of Lamentation.

“Yahweh.”

Though it hadn’t collapsed entirely, thousands of holes were punctured through it, with humans pouring out.

“That bastard’s gone insane! If he wanted to leave, he could have done it quietly! Why destroy the warehouse and cause chaos? How much is the damage?”

“It seems like a clash with the Black Monks. The damage is significant, but worse than that, there’s a system error...”

Vivian pointed at Rian.

“What should we do about this?” Lethe’s face gradually turned pale as she stared at the screen.

“Just a little further!”

Rian, who was climbing vertically along the left side of the wall, finally found an opening and leaped.

BOOOOM!

A powerful explosion echoed as he twisted his body to see the Wall of Lamentation riddled with holes like Swiss cheese.

“Rian!”

Shirone shouted.

“See you at the end!”

Crossing the Wall of Lamentation was the only way to reach the Energy Management Corporation.

Unfurling his wings, Shirone avoided the Black Monks and surged forward, while Rian scanned ahead.

Far more Black Monks than Shirone had faced were now blocking his path.

“Gehenna?”

They were targets for purification.

“You, bound by the agony of flames, are not meant to enjoy freedom. By what arrogant trick have you broken your chains and wandered here?”

Rian pointed his greatsword forward.

“Because I decided so.”

Karma, being a consequence of actions, was an immutable code.

‘Is this possible?’

For the Black Monks, this was as shocking as a dead man returning to life.

“...Purify him.”

The Black Monks surged forward like smoke.

“Punish the sinner!”

Their black cloaks rippled like liquid before billowing out into smoke, enveloping the space.

Black chains shot toward Rian, and a scythe hidden within the cloak gleamed for a brief moment.

“Yaaah!”

Rian swung his greatsword, unleashing an erratic, rhythmic slash that tore through the advancing darkness.

–Krrrgh! How...

By perfectly cutting off their unpredictable movements, Rian caused the Black Monks' wariness to skyrocket.

“Be careful. That’s the pinnacle of a single-strike technique.”

It resembled the zeta function.

Just as patterns in prime numbers are difficult to discern among infinite values, so was Rian’s sword rhythm—impossible to define.

‘This rhythm doesn’t even exist in Hell. Even the gods would frown at such chaos.’

Beilouk, a sword technique that encapsulated every possible form into a single linear slash.

‘Approaching him recklessly will get us sliced instantly.’

It was a technique of time, not space, born not from talent but sheer effort.

“Yaaah!”

Slashing in every direction, Rian moved forward without hesitation.

As the Black Monks struggled, human hands suddenly emerged from the ground.

“Don’t go.”

Gripping his ankles, the hands were torn away, but soon more began clutching at him from every direction.

“Now! Finish him off!” Those who wished for misfortune recalled Shirone’s words as Rian stomped the ground forcefully.

“Out of my way!”

Wherever Rian ran, hands rose up, swaying like a field of reeds.

Eventually, pieces of human bodies detached from the wall and began climbing up Rian’s body.

A torso-only figure, its spine dangling loosely, shrieked.

“Don’t go! You cannot leave!”

“Aaaah!”

As Rian tried to shake them off and run, he suddenly felt intense heat rising from his chest.

“Of all times... Damn it!” At that moment, chains from Gehenna burst from his solar plexus, anchoring him to the Wall of Lamentation in all directions.

WOOOOO!

A scream loud enough to burst his brain echoed around him.

“Urghhh!”

The scream splintered into countless emotions, piercing deep into Rian’s mind.

–Why are you the only one free? We’re suffering, so why should you be happy?

Like that...

You are...

“Shut up!”

–Die with us. Suffer with us.

If you are free,

I cannot endure it.

The chains of Gehenna were ripped away.

“I said shut up!”

Like sweet potato vines being pulled, hundreds of human figures were dragged along with the chains.

–You’re just like us. You’re no different...

Their voices infiltrated the deepest recesses of Rian’s mind, awakening a hidden part of himself.

“Ughhhh!”

Clenching his teeth, veins bulged on Rian’s neck, and blood trickled from his eyes.

‘They called him Ogent Rai, right?’

When his elder brother succeeded in sword mastery, every acquaintance of their swordsmanship family said:

“Compared to him, Rian... lacks talent.”

“No, it’s worse than that. He has no understanding of swordsmanship at all.”

Returning home after sword training, Rian overheard them murmuring.

“The difference is too great. The family head must be struggling. Usually, when this happens, the younger sibling ends up rebelling. It would be better to give up now rather than drag his brother down later.”

A hallucination echoed in his mind.

–You hated Rai, didn’t you? You wanted to kill him. Why was he given talent while you were not?

“Rai is just Rai.”

The person Rian truly wanted to kill...

As Rian slashed through the Black Monks and dashed forward, three thousand humans were being dragged behind him.

"Yaaaah!"

More and more humans clung to the chains, endlessly increasing in number.

It felt like they weighed over 6 tons.

‘I won’t give up!’ Rian couldn’t shake off those clinging to him, so the only thing he could do was something incredibly simple.

‘Move. Keep moving your legs…’

Something that any human could do.

‘So, there really are geniuses in this world.’

When Rian was defeated in a live duel against Shirone at the Ogent family training ground, he realized something.

‘I’ll never be able to reach this level.’

It was a bitter emotion he had no choice but to accept.

‘It stings… just a little.’

Unlike with Rai, this feeling of defeat was undeniable, and Rian had to make a choice.

‘Let’s do what I can.’ Something that anyone could do.

A voice echoed in his mind.

—You’re jealous of Shirone, aren’t you? It’s just that Shirone shines so brightly, and you want to be near that light.

“No.”

—But deep down, you want to kill her, don’t you? If someone gave you the power to kill Shirone, you’d take it, wouldn’t you? Because you’re jealous of him.

“Shirone is just Shirone.”

The person Rian truly wanted to kill...

"Graaaa! Don’t go! Don’t go!" As Rian sprinted another 14 kilometers, a massive cluster of humans continued to be dragged behind him.

"Let’s die together! Come on, die with us!"

Estimated weight: 94 tons.

"Say whatever you want! I will never stop!"

The person Rian truly wanted to kill...

‘Is myself.’

All the pain, all the training, all the despair—Rian endured it all to reach this point in his future.

"Grab him! Don’t let him escape! Everyone, get up! Grab him, now!"

Estimated weight: 187 tons.

"Graaaa! Get buried here! Become one with the wall like us!"

Rian kept moving his legs, tirelessly.

‘Go ahead, keep trying to stop me! Beat me as much as you like! No matter how many times you chant for me to die, curse me, or stomp me down!’

In the distance, the future version of Rian waited, holding his greatsword, pointing it at the present Rian.

“I will never stop!”

Estimated weight: 302 tons.

Despite the increasing weight, his speed didn’t falter, causing the Black Monks to hesitate.

—Attack him! Now’s our chance to take him down.

The Black Monks charged from all directions, but they were swept away by Rian’s erratic, rhythmic swordsmanship.

"Come on! Try whatever you want!"

If his momentum slowed, it would be over.

Feeling the exponentially increasing weight of the chains, Rian clenched his teeth.

“I...”

I grow stronger every single day.

“I...”

I keep failing until there’s nothing left to fail at.

‘I keep doing what anyone can do, over and over, filling my days with it.’

And then, I compress all those days into a single second...

“I...!”

Leap across the vastness of time!

"Yaaaah!"

The chains of Gehenna turned red-hot, igniting the bodies of those clinging to them.

"Graaa! No! No!"

Desperate to hold Rian back, they clung to the chains with all their might, but...

"Aah! It burns!" The burden Rian bore was far too intense for them to carry.

‘It’s here!’

The future version of Rian, as if acknowledging him, nodded before charging toward him.

Rian raised his greatsword.

“I will kill you.”

That place now belongs to me.

—Run!

The humans clinging to the chains turned to ash, and the Black Monks scattered in every direction.

"Divine Transcendence."

At last, in that critical moment, the two versions of Rian met, and their greatswords clashed.

...

The future Rian was cut down, and everything in front of him split in two, top to bottom.

—Kieeeh! Th-this...!

As the Black Monks dissolved into smoke and vanished, the end of the Wall of Lamentation finally came into view.

‘I’ve made it.’

Using the last of his strength, Rian kicked off the rooftop, and from his left, he heard Shirone’s voice.

“Rian!”

Although he was in terrible shape, Shirone smiled brightly, waving at him.

“Shirone.”

He is the unreachable sun of my life.

‘But still.’

Smiling back at Shirone, Rian turned his gaze to the clear horizon ahead.

‘We’re running side by side.’

The half-destroyed Wall of Lamentation and its burning remnants stood as witnesses to their journey.

The Karma Management Department was engulfed in silence.

Lethe, watching the screen in a daze, wandered aimlessly in place.

“Ah...?”

Her mind finally returned to reality, but the splitting headache was anything but welcome.

“What’s the damage? How much is the damage?” She rubbed the back of her neck as she asked, and Vivian, looking slightly frightened, turned her head toward her.

“Approximately 83 billion Des.”

“Oh.”

Lethe nodded calmly, but once she moved her head, it showed no signs of stopping.

“Eighty-three billion. Eighty-three billion Des... that’s... let’s see... about two duchies wiped out?”

“Yes, more or less.”

Lethe stomped her foot on the ground with a thud.

"These damned humans! Just when the company’s finances were already on the brink, they deal us this kind of blow? This won’t do! I have to go there myself! Call the deputy director back!"

Vivian hurriedly intervened.

“Shouldn’t we focus on damage control first? If you leave during a crisis, the company will fall into total disarray.”

Breathing heavily, Lethe pondered for a moment before pulling a pained expression and shouting.

“Does God even exist?!”
