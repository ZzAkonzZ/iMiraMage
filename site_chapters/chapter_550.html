Chapter 550:

Apocalypse Reset.

Countless symbols began to accumulate toward the final destination of information.

In the void, a blue light surged, sketching a three-dimensional overview and restoring life to the desolate landscapes.

The city's information was restructured.

Aside from Shirone’s disappearance, nothing had changed in the ruined scenery.

The world was reborn in this way. Meanwhile, a man walking along the vast trench where the ocean had dried up suddenly lifted his head.

“What’s this?”

His name was Gaold.

He was a man instinctively drawn to the end of the world.

“It feels like I’m walking a path I’ve already traveled.”

His hair was wild, his body emaciated, and he wore nothing but dirty rags to cover himself.

“It doesn’t matter.”

Gaold struck the ground with the staff he held in both hands and took another step forward.

He was searching for something, though he didn’t know what.

“Where is God? I need to kill him…”

“Shirone will never come back.”

Rian’s heart was heavy.

The Shirone he had met in the Apocalypse was merely information, but that information was identical to the Shirone in reality.

If a similar situation were to occur in the real world, Shirone would undoubtedly sacrifice himself again.

“And at the end of it all lies the ultimate void.”

“Yes, an open state. Just as we perceive the Apocalypse as meaningless, such things could happen in reality as well.”

As Rian’s expression darkened, Miro tried to lighten the mood.

“Don’t worry. It’s not an easy thing. The Apocalypse is a world built from human thoughts, but reality isn’t like that, right?”

“None of what Shirone has accomplished has ever been easy.”

Miro pursed his lips at the pointed remark.

‘This kid has a sharp tongue in strange moments.’

If the Immortal Function were expanded infinitely, the self would dissolve; if perfectly controlled, one could leave the world by their own will.

It was assumed that Gauffin was the only one to have succeeded in the latter, but since it was Shirone, there was always cause for concern.

Breaking the somber atmosphere, Arius appeared.

“You’ve returned safely.”

He had been waiting in the Under Coder, blocked from entering the Apocalypse.

“Well, if I don’t make it back safely, who would?”

Arius nodded as if acknowledging the obvious and asked, “Did things go well?”

Miro turned his head toward Fermi, who produced the depreciation deal contract.

“I’ve obtained the signature, at least.”

Though the signature was forged, it was based on Shirone’s fully restored information, making it viable.

Arius meticulously inspected the contract before tucking it into his pocket.

“I’ll hold onto this from now on. To bring it into reality, it has to be treated as stolen goods.”

For the forged signature to work in reality, it needed to bypass Dreamo’s censorship and be transformed into an object.

By Dreamo’s rules, this was outright fraud, but for the tomb raider Arius, it was second nature.

“Can you manage it?”

“No problem. I’ve stolen more complicated items and sold them to Terraje.”

“True enough. Then let’s move.”

Miro’s group retraced their steps through the Under Coder and arrived at Dreamo.

If they were caught by the Dream Keeper, they could be trapped in the dream world forever, but Arius used his extensive experience to move the process along smoothly.

“I want to leave the dream.”

At Dreamo’s exit, Arius lied without hesitation.

The dreamer, Luber, scrutinized the group with glowing eyes but soon smiled warmly.

“Did you have good dreams? Farewell.”

Even Marsha, the leader of a band of thieves, felt her heart race as she passed the Dreamer.

Ascending to the surface of their psyche, the group stepped out through a door Arius had installed.

Their physical senses returned faintly, and a blinding light engulfed them aggressively.

“Gah! Gah!”

The first to awaken was Rian.

Overwhelmed by immense pain, he sat up, causing the others who had been lying beside him to gradually open their eyes as well.

“Ugh...”

“Rian, are you okay?”

Marsha looked at Rian with concern.

The carpet was stained red from the blood he had coughed up while unconscious.

“He took a serious beating from the subterranean weapons. And was slashed by blades too.”

Rian took deep breaths, fighting the immense pain.

The sensations he had experienced in the Apocalypse had felt real, but now, upon waking, it was clear they were merely transmitted information.

Arius, who had been sitting cross-legged, approached.

“You had severe convulsions. If not for your regenerative ability, you would have died.”

Regardless, since he had survived, Miro turned to the most pressing matter.

“Let’s confirm it first. Can you do it?”

Rian, still in pain, looked at Fermi.

“Let’s give it a try. Phew…”

Fermi took a deep breath and began casting a spell.

As the depreciation deal contract materialized, everyone’s attention focused on the signature line.

“It’s there.”

The signature “Arian Shirone” was clearly inscribed.

“It’s not over yet. Whether it actually works…”

Fermi licked his dry lips and focused with utmost concentration.

Depreciation Deal—Valhalla Action.

A golden sphere shimmered in his palm, solidifying into a chip that shot upward.

“It worked!”

Marsha’s eyes widened in shock, her mouth agape.

Though they had fought to the death for this, none of them had been certain Shirone’s magic could truly be extracted.

Before the chip could even fall, Fermi snatched it mid-air and placed it in his palm.

A red-and-gold pattern circled its edges, with two curved arrows at the center chasing each other’s tails. Unlike the usual design, a diagonal black line ran through its center.

“As expected, it’s marked as unusable. It means the price hasn’t been paid yet.”

“What about Shirone?”

Rian looked at Shirone, but the Armand robe still hadn’t dissolved, and his breathing remained steady.

“It seems Shirone still bears the debt.”

Fermi agreed with Miro’s statement.

“Extraction alone hasn’t changed anything. Once it’s transferred, Shirone should wake up. Probably.”

“Give it to me.”

When Miro held out her hand, Fermi tossed the chip to her and added, “There’s plenty of time left before it expires, but just in case, it’s better to transfer it quickly.”

After all, that’s when the 1.9 billion gold would start being deposited in installments.

“No need to worry. Terraje has probably already hired a Grand Mage. If ten of them take turns paying, it should be enough. You’ll still be needed for each transfer.”

Providing that level of service was a broker’s basic responsibility.

“Still, it’s remarkable. Sacrificing ten Grand Mages just to wake up a single student from a magic academy.”

“Terraje must believe Shirone is worth that much,” Miro replied, though she shared the same belief and chose not to disclose further details. Instead, she turned to Marsha.

“I’m leaving immediately. Are you coming with me?”

Though Marsha had been promised support for the Parrot Thieves if the mission succeeded, she had no intention of going to Kashan.

“No, I’ll trust you—not Terraje, but you. I need to return to the hideout and discuss matters with my crew.”

Respecting Marsha’s decision, Miro turned to Rian.

“What about you?”

If Rian wanted to come along, Miro was open to it. Not only was he Shirone’s steadfast ally, but the determination he showed during the Apocalypse battle would undoubtedly be valuable in the future.

After a brief moment of contemplation, Rian asked instead of answering directly.

“What will happen to Shirone now?”

“First, we’ll take the chip to Kashan. After that, I’ll look after him for a while.”

Fermi pouted.

“You’re taking him in?”

“Of course. Do you know how much time, money, and effort went into waking this guy up? I’ll teach him a few things and then send him back to the magic academy. He should at least get his mage certification.”

Fermi, realizing something, smirked meaningfully.

“A mage certification, huh? That won’t be an easy feat.”

Though they belonged to different branches of the family, Miro, sharing the same bloodline, responded with a knowing smile.

“That’s why I’m taking him.”

The geopolitical landscape was about to shift dramatically, and Shirone would inevitably be swept into the storm’s vortex.

‘Even in the Kingdom of Tormia, the head of the Magic Association has changed.’

Fermi shrugged indifferently.

“Teach him well. Though with me around, Shirone won’t even graduate.”

“Haha! That sounds amusing,” Miro said with a laugh before turning to Rian.

“So, what’s your decision?”

“I’ll resume my knightly training.”

Marsha was surprised by his response.

“Why? You’ve only just reunited with Shirone, and you should see him wake up. He’s alive because of you, after all.”

Rian shook his head bitterly.

“In this battle... I died a thousand times.”

The atmosphere grew solemn.

“Though I survived thanks to sheer willpower, I still failed to protect Shirone. All I did was fight back—I couldn’t defeat them. That’s how strong they were.”

Rian’s memory flickered with images of Paknyeo charging like a beast.

“In other words, it means they’re an opponent I couldn’t beat even if I died a thousand times. I want to train harder. By the time Shirone graduates, I want to stand before him as a proud knight.”

Miro respected Rian’s resolve. It was a worthy aspiration.

“Alright. Now that decisions are made, let’s set off.”

And so, the group departed from the Ardino family estate.

Before leaving, Miro gave some parting advice to Fermi, who had come to see them off at the front gate.

“Make sure you graduate this time. Stop giving your dad such a hard time.”

“Haha! I’ll think about it.”

Miro glanced at Shirone, who was being carried by Arius.

“Well, I guess it’s not like Shirone is begging to graduate either. After this, you should have a clear understanding of his level.”

“It’s not about levels,” Fermi replied. “The Valhalla Action won’t be lifted before graduation, and as for the rest of his magic—well, I have this, after all.”

Fermi flicked a gold coin into the air with a ting.

“... Why on earth did you create such a custom technique? Isn’t it time you told me?”

“For now, let’s just say I wanted to buy some magic.”

When Fermi dodged the question, Miro decided not to press further and turned away.

“Fine. I’ll pass along your regards to Shirone.”

“The sooner the payment is processed, the better,” Fermi replied in his typical style, even for a farewell.

Upon arriving in Kashan, Miro delivered the Valhalla Action chip and explained what was believed to be Shirone’s collateral.

With the contract fulfilled, she left Kashan again, assigning Arius a separate mission before taking Shirone to a cabin deep in the mountains of Tormia.

After delivering the chip to Worin, nothing happened for two days.

Each night, Miro checked Shirone’s condition, choosing to observe with patience.

And so, another day passed.

- Restriction lifted. Will to act accepted.

At the faint, dreamlike voice, Shirone’s brow furrowed.

The Armand robe dissolved into organic material, merging into the form of a cursed sword. Shortly after, Shirone’s eyelids fluttered open.

“...”

Staring at the wooden ceiling, Shirone blinked slowly as memories of his time in the heavenly realm flooded back.

“Gasp!”

Instinctively springing upright, his battle-honed reflexes kicked in as he scanned his surroundings with sharp eyes.

“What... what’s going on?”

Everything around him was unfamiliar.

There was no sign of the Arabot spire or the fearsome archangel Faiel.

‘What happened? Where am I?’

A creeping fear, as if he had awoken in a distant future, took hold of him.

But his body, realizing it was back in the real world, first registered an overwhelming hunger.

As his stomach growled, Shirone clutched it and doubled over.

“I’m starving.”

As his sense of smell returned, the scent of boiling herbs drifted in through a crack in the door.

‘I need to eat something...’

When Shirone opened the door, he saw a small kitchen where a woman with long blue hair, wearing an apron, was stirring a pot of porridge.

There was something familiar about her, but from behind, he couldn’t quite tell who she was.

‘Who is that?’

She tasted the porridge with a ladle, smiled in satisfaction, and muttered to herself.

“Ah, as expected, I’m a genius. Even something this bad tastes amazing thanks to my brilliant palate.”

“...”

Shirone, staring blankly, finally spoke.

“Excuse me, who are you? And where am I?”

The woman turned as if it was the most normal thing in the world. With a bright smile, Miro said:

“Oh? Did you sleep well, Shirone?”
