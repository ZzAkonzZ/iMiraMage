Chapter 794:

“It’s over. I totally blew it.”

Palmer clutched his head as Maya remained frozen even as the intro neared its end.

‘Did the nerves get to her?’

The sudden mental blanking that hits rookies was a common phenomenon.

As the time came for Maya’s voice to begin, Palmer couldn’t bear to look anymore and squeezed his eyes shut.

“Like a sudden shower…”

A crisp, refreshing voice, like the dawn breaking.

“Huh?”

Palmer opened his eyes—and as the powerful drum beat exploded, the song began.

“Light falls from the sky!”

An explosive vocal tore through the sound of all the instruments, pushing forward like a forceful wave.

‘She hit the downbeat perfectly. She’s better than during rehearsal!’

Maya’s voice, now flowing naturally with the rhythm, was the most sincere it had ever been.

‘I’m not afraid.’

When you sing from the heart, there’s no room for mistakes.

‘Because I practiced endlessly.’

Though her mind was blank, the rhythm, the melody, the flow of the song all moved through her subconscious.

“Like a sudden downpour!”

As her senses began to return and the world came into focus, she saw—just like in her lyrics—Shirone smiling at her.

“Unavoidable light pours down!”

No one could explain precisely how sincerity transforms technique, but the audience was clearly feeling it.

“That singer’s really good.”

“Right? Even with all the big-name guest performers, she’s a rookie and still this good.”

Hearing the praise, Shirone’s lips itched with pride.

‘That person’s my classmate, you know.’

He had told himself to stay back and simply watch from afar until she soared even higher.

“You’re amazing, Maya!”

It was a dazzling debut performance.

Fourteen kilometers from the venue, a group was speeding forward.

Some flew low using the power of wind, others sprinted with sheer muscle strength, and others rode mechanical beasts.

The methods varied, but their speed—like arrows—was nearly equal.

“Lady Mortasinger, we’re nearing the venue.”

The voice was in the language of Heaven.

“Good. Hold position at two kilometers out.”

At the center of the group, a woman cloaked in black fabric marked with a cube symbol on her face spoke.

“All those pretty girls should just die.”

Tenth in the Council of Ten, Mortasinger (age 10,048).

“They’re not going to go down easily. The World Beauty Contest is a major enterprise for Arachne.”

“So what? It’s just a country of the Lower Earth. We who once ruled over the citizens of Heaven have nothing to fear.”

Ra Enemy had pulled in the Council of Ten and the Immortals’ Community to erect an altar.

Their goal had been to infiltrate the human world easily through the Reset, but Ra's final choice had been Nane.

‘Tch, that guy has no sense of responsibility.’

Beron and Paknyeo were dead, and there were rumors that Shura had become Nane’s disciple and was now studying the Way of Enlightenment.

‘If I’d known this would happen, I would’ve brought Yujeong from Heaven too. That would’ve ended it already.’

Second in the Council of Ten hierarchy.

But at the time, breaking their vows had been forbidden, so she’d left it to her clone while she slacked off.

‘Whatever! I’m going to do what I want now.’

The other executives were all just as temperamental. Once Nane gave up his role, most scattered around the world with only a few loyal followers remaining.

‘Just try to come back crying later!’

Now left solely responsible for the community, Mortasinger moved her forces based on personal emotion.

‘As if being ugly weren’t bad enough, they have to hold a beauty contest? Lower Earth humans really are stupid!’

A potbellied man with a tiger-like beard stepped beside her.

“Does this fight have any real meaning?”

He was Bodhidharma, a man who had reached the state of Vajrayana. Behind him trailed the one-armed monk Hyega.

“……”

They weren’t traditional Heaven-born beings, but Heaven had always been home to many outer races.

More importantly, their spiritual states far exceeded Mortasinger’s, despite her age.

“Meaning? You find it or you don’t. If you’re not here to follow my will, then leave.”

Most other Immortals had already left with the Council of Ten executives.

“Enlightenment doesn’t lie elsewhere. We will remain by your side.”

Mortasinger was the only Council of Ten executive who relied on forbidden techniques rather than cultivation.

“No need for your pity. I’m plenty strong.”

The Council of Ten was powerful.

‘That’s exactly why I pity you.’

A perfectly twisted human being.

Not even Vajrayana practitioners could break her forbidden technique.

On the first day of the competition, the identities of the veiled contestants from each nation were finally being revealed.

“Everyone! Please welcome Miss Baiden!”

Unlike her bored, yawning self backstage, she now stepped onto the stage like a bashful girl.

“Woooo! She’s the best! That’s Baiden for you!”

With her head slightly tilted and cheeks flushed, she charmed the crowd, while the other contestants bit back irritation.

‘Ugh, such an act. My mouth is itching to call her out.’

After her brief introduction and interview, it was Miss Tormia’s turn.

“Oh? I’ve got to see this.”

Patrolling the venue to monitor threats, Shirone halted and stood on tiptoe.

“You’ll be surprised! This year’s Miss Tormia is a royal, representing her country’s beauty!”

“Royalty?”

“Introducing Alfred Pony!”

As the magnificently dressed Pony walked onto the stage, the audience erupted in cheers.

“Pony?”

Shirone’s eyes widened.

“What about school?”

All kinds of thoughts crossed his mind, but in the end, he had to admit—she looked far more beautiful than in their school days.

‘Maya and Pony… this is complicated.’

Lastly, the host nation Arachne’s representative stepped forward to the loudest applause yet.

“Langi! Langi!”

A man, hearts practically in his eyes, pushed through the crowd to stand just below the stage.

“I love you!”

It was the same guy who’d cornered Shirone during the parade with his overly passionate speech.

“Oh?”

Langi, experienced on stage, didn’t flinch and simply looked down calmly, but security quickly apprehended the man.

“Get him out of here! What the hell do you think you’re doing?!”

But as she first laid eyes on him, Langi had felt it—like fate.

“You’re the one I’ve been looking for!”

As Shirone watched the absurd scene unfold, a chill ran down his spine, and he spun around.

“What was that?”

From a distance undetectable to the human eye, a sharp killing intent had shot forward like a beam.

“A sniper!”

From the mountains, countless metal spheres were hurtling toward the venue.

‘Those are…’

Shirone’s eyes didn’t lie—it was the Arkra, a weapon used by Mecha citizens.

“Get down!”

Only one person had been watching the ridgeline—Jaestin. She reacted first, shoving the contestants off the stage with both arms.

But Langi, standing in the center, didn’t react in time.

“What the…?”

The explosive spheres were already within 200 meters, and their overwhelming bombardment blotted out the sky.

“Langi!”

The passionate fan broke free from the guards, climbed the stage, and tackled Langi out of harm’s way.

“Kyaaa!”

As she fell back, Langi saw hundreds of metal spheres spinning like a vortex.

‘Is this a dream?’

As her mind triggered a time-slowing reflex in the face of imminent death, particles of light floated through the scene like soap bubbles.

‘Shi…ro…’

Even faster than thought, light began to assemble into Shirone.

‘…ne?’

The spheres, encountering a powerful resistance, began to ignite like popping corn, bursting into flames.

‘As high as possible!’

The vortex of spheres twisted upward, exploding simultaneously in midair.

“Aaahhh! It’s a terrorist attack!”

As the explosions shook the ground, the audience scattered in chaos, and the guards activated emergency protocols.

“Langi, are you okay?!”

Shirone turned around. Langi, still dazed, nodded without realizing it.

“Jaestin! How’s it looking on your side?”

Jaestin scratched her ear as she climbed up onto the stage.

“Everyone’s safe. But this is…”

Pony suddenly leapt up.

“Shirone! Why are you here?!”

“And you? What about school?”

After a moment’s pause, Pony curled her lips into a faint smile.

Jaestin shouted.

“They’re coming!”

From the same direction where the sniper attack had occurred, around 200 enemies were now charging forward.

Still debating her answer, Pony’s eyes sharpened as she looked directly at Shirone.

“Tactical decision.”

“Oh yeah?”

A smirk tugged at Shirone’s lips.

“Then I don’t need to protect you, right?”

Water surged around Pony’s hands, whipping into the air and forming into a dense mass above them.

“You’re asking me that?”

At the same time, Shirone teleported off the stage—and the Immortal Community rushed in.

“Kill them! Kill all the pretty ones!”

‘What the hell is this?’

They were clearly Heaven's citizens—mechanical augmentations and all—but their motive sounded unbelievably petty.

“Terrorists! Execute them on sight!”

Security forces rushed to fight back, but the Immortals were overwhelming, each like a one-man army.

“Ha ha ha! Pathetic groundlings!”

Those with metallic frameworks bolted around like springs, smashing through armor with brutal kicks.

‘Must be strength-enhancement rigs…’

As Pony began drawing in more power from the surrounding water, a slicing wind flew toward her.

“Aaaagh!”

But Maya’s voice soared from behind, and Pony’s focus spiked. Her signature spell, Water Dragon, crashed forward in a massive flood.

“Whoa…”

Langi stood stunned as the roaring tide swallowed the bladed winds and swept the mechanical attackers clean off the stage.

“What are you gawking at? Didn’t you used to be a soldier? Grab something and get ready to fight!”

Pony barked orders, then glanced back at Maya—the one who had just cast the sound-based magic.

“Uh… hey. Been a while?”

“Tch.”

The reunion was awkward—after all, Maya was the one who had caused Pony’s graduation failure firsthand.

“Right. Let’s catch up after we clean this up.”

A few had regained composure, but most of the contestants were frozen in terror.

“Eeeek! Help! Where are the guards?!”

“Save me first! Do you know who I am?! Fight later—get me out now!”

Watching this chaos unfold, Langi moved closer to Pony.

“Those guys are strong. Really strong.”

Despite the multi-layered security detail around the stage, it looked like it wouldn’t hold long.

“You— You already knew Shirone, didn’t you?”

Langi hunched her shoulders with a guilty expression.

“Consider it a miracle. I don’t know which of us he came to see, but…”

Pony’s guess wasn’t quite right, but—

“At least we won’t die here.”

Her eyes locked on Shirone, who was already charging at the enemy commander.

“What do you think you’re doing?!”

A woman with her face veiled in a cloth bearing the emblem of the Mecha rose into the air, laughing wildly.

“Kekeke! What’s wrong? Upset that I messed with the pretty ones?”

‘One shot.’

As he prepared his Photon Cannon, Bodhidharma and Hyega flanked her from both sides.

‘Strong…!’

Bodhidharma dropped like a meteor, driving a fist straight forward.

“Flowing Vajra Fist!”

A shockwave shaped like a giant fist struck Shirone, heavy and solid like steel.

‘Avatar Technique.’

Shirone, with Yahweh’s sphere floating in his palm, struck upward.

“Unravel.”

Boom! The Vajra Fist unraveled, dissolving into the air.

“…Huh?”

Bodhidharma’s eyes went wide with shock.

‘Vajra Fist can’t be undone… And yet…’

Hyega charged in.

“Diamond Body.”

His skin crystallized into razor-sharp diamond, converting anything he touched into unbreakable gemstone.

“Stop, Hyega!”

A flash of light slammed into Hyega’s solar plexus. The diamonds shattered like glass.

“Gah!”

As Hyega’s face twisted in pain, Mortasinger’s laughter suddenly cut off.

“Hyper-Unified Force?”

The ultimate power sought by the Mecha.

And the Norin call it—

Yahweh.
