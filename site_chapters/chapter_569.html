Chapter 569:

Alpheus Magic School. Warehouse No. 3.

At dawn, Shirone's team of six gathered at the entrance of the grounds where Istas was visible.

Despite the ongoing graduation evaluations, no one was absent due to pride and the personal stakes involved.

"Everyone might be anxious, but let's focus on the battle for now."

They were all prepared.

"The battle period is seven days. After today, we'll have a rough idea of our capabilities. We'll think about the rest later."

After Neid's opening speech, Amy asked, "What's the strategy?"

Iruki replied, "It's impossible to control all six of us based on the reactions of the six opponents. For now, it's a reconnaissance battle. But don't let your Master Card be taken."

Shirone added, "We've decided that leaving the battlefield counts as a forfeit, so make sure to check the boundaries."

Sabina asked, "Shouldn't we decide on the first Scramble? Black, White, or Random?"

Shirone said, "The alliance team will all choose Random. Even if we get White in the first stage, if the opponent also gets White, it's a draw. Conversely, if we choose Black, we'll be at a disadvantage for two hours."

Dorothy added, "From the second stage, we need to consider the team's combination. Even if we open two Random Cards (ⓡⓡ), there's a 25% chance of getting Heretic (●●). So, with six of us, at least one should get Heretic."

Shirone summarized, "The most important thing early on is not to miss any Scrambles. The coordinates might put us at a disadvantage."

"I can locate the opponents," Dorothy said, expanding her Spirit Zone.

Iruki asked, "Jonah, how far can you extend it?"

"If I completely reduce the density, the maximum is a diameter of 1 kilometer. But I can barely distinguish between humans and non-living objects."

Even as a Jonah, she was a manipulator, prioritizing the precision of the zone over its size.

"For reference, when I manipulate Hickory, the Spirit Zone's diameter is about 50 meters. I thought I should let you know."

It meant she couldn't manipulate Hickory without the precision to reduce a 1-kilometer diameter to 50 meters.

Switching to sniper mode, Dorothy rotated her linear Spirit Zone to locate the enemies.

Unlike Amy, who could rotate dozens of times per second, it took Dorothy 20 minutes to scan 360 degrees.

But only a Jonah could transform a 1-kilometer diameter Spirit Zone into a linear one.

"The alliance team has Hershey, the sonar. Their search will be faster than ours."

While Iruki was lost in thought, Dorothy spoke.

"Six people are gathered 7 kilometers to the east."

"Then let's go in the opposite direction. We must collect the first Scramble. The more test subjects, the better."

"How will we track each other's locations?"

"Scrambles are summoned every two hours. By checking the status of collected cards, we can roughly determine locations."

Sabina sighed.

'It would have been nice if Dante had participated.'

Having a communication mage would be ideal, but it was too much to ask for everything unless they were at Cage level.

"Alright, let's go."

As the six of them simultaneously cast spatial movement, the gathered flashes scattered in all directions.

Scramble Royal waiting time: 10 minutes.

'About three minutes left.'

Arriving at the start coordinates, Shirone calmed his anxious mind with a sequence of exercises.

Since he might need to use his time manipulation early on, it was good to prepare.

'The primary goal is to collect the nearest Scramble. But depending on the situation, I might have to run a long distance.'

At that moment, Ruru's voice resonated in his mind as an electric signal.

"Scramble Royal has commenced."

Shirone's eyes flashed.

Grasping the newly summoned Master Card, he swiped the screen with his thumb to reveal the coordinates.

Exactly 12 coordinates were recorded in XYZ format.

After quickly inputting the 12 coordinates into a mental map, Shirone identified the nearest one.

'X-271, Y-373, Z-8. It's near Training Ground No. 9.'

With the destination set, his body moved automatically, and his mind began analyzing the information.

'The shape is a slightly distorted circle on the right. But the distribution is close to average.'

He couldn't guarantee that the Scramble locations would be fair in the next round.

But if there was a specific pattern, Iruki could find the equation in about three days.

Checking his Master Card, Shirone saw that two coordinates had already disappeared almost simultaneously.

'Already collected?'

Arriving at the safest landing point for spatial movement, Shirone ran towards the coordinates while checking his Master Card again.

'Five.'

Five of the 12 coordinates had already vanished.

'It's been less than five minutes. Is this the human element of probability?'

Swiping the screen, he saw that Iruki, Amy, Hershey, and Anchal had ⓡ (Random Card) status.

'As expected, they all chose Random.'

Seeing another coordinate disappear, his anxiety peaked.

"Damn!"

Kicking off the ground, Shirone cast teleportation, and a bright flash cut through the dense forest.

"That concludes the morning evaluation of the combat training. Enjoy your lunch, and I'll see you in the afternoon."

Binder raised his hand.

"What happens to those who didn't attend today's combat training?"

A top student with a perfect score in theory, he had fallen to 26th place due to the fierce competition among this year's graduates.

Especially since his opponent today was the formidable Anchal, he had almost given up.

Assistant teacher Collie entered the combat training ground and said,

"If they don't attend by the afternoon, it's a forfeit."

Some students' eyes lit up.

'Please don't come.'

Eleven students had missed the morning evaluation. If this continued, most students would gain three points by default.

"Fermi, do you know anything?"

Collie asked, but Fermi just shrugged.

"Why are you asking me?"

Collie knew that two members of the Golden Wheel were absent, but there was nothing he could do if Fermi played dumb.

'Participation in the graduation evaluation is up to the students. In this case, it will be recorded as an absence…'

"Maybe they ate something bad yesterday? It looks like they all got food poisoning, haha."

Richard, the alchemist, chuckled.

"Go have lunch."

With that, Collie turned and headed to the principal's office.

'Scramble Royal.'

"Found it."

Shirone discovered a glowing Scramble, the size of a fist, hanging from a tree on the outskirts of the training ground.

'Remaining coordinates…'

After the initial rapid disappearance of coordinates, four still remained.

'If I collect this, there will be three. I'll choose Random first. But before that…'

Grasping the light sphere, it transformed into a Pure Card.

-Once a Scramble is collected, it cannot be transferred to another participant.

Shirone mentally shouted,

'Random!'

There was no reaction, and after one second, it transformed into a Random Card as per the rules.

"As expected, it doesn't work."

It seemed he couldn't arbitrarily choose Random in the Pure Card state.

Although it automatically transformed after holding it, this was crucial for Shirone.

If he could choose Random at will, he could use his time manipulation to revert it to the Pure state.

'That's confirmed. Next, I'll try reverting an Open Card to Random.'

As he quickly raised his sequence of exercises to use his time manipulation, a voice echoed in his mind.

"All Scrambles have been collected or destroyed. 1 hour, 38 minutes, and 12 seconds until the next summoning."

Shirone postponed the experiment and checked his Master Card.

"Phew, thank goodness."

All his teammates had obtained one card each and had chosen Random as planned.

Shirone then checked the alliance team's collection status.

'As expected, they also chose Rand… huh?'

While five names had ⓡ symbols, Eden's name had a ○ (White Card) symbol.

'She chose White. It's the strongest in the first stage. But it will be reversed in 1 hour and 30 minutes.'

A thought struck him.

'Could it be…?'

Before he could finish his thought, someone emerged from the bushes.

"Finally, we meet."

Shirone's eyes glinted with murderous intent.

"Kaiden."

Kaiden of the Cross Star family approached, dragging his family's weapon, the Cross Sword.

Having quickly collected a Scramble, he had clearly been searching only for Shirone, dragging the sword's tip along the ground as he approached.

"You chose Random. My card is also Random."

Even though both had Random cards, Shirone was at a disadvantage since he hadn't opened his yet.

'Kaiden must have opened his card immediately after collecting it. There's no reason to keep it Random.'

Shirone, however, had to conduct his time manipulation experiment, causing a slight delay.

'Should I open it now? But then I'd have to wait over an hour for the next experiment. What happens if I receive a Kang in this state?'

Would it automatically open, or would it be destroyed?

He considered this as another potential experiment when Kaiden said something unexpected.

"Do you think I'll call a Kang?"

"…"

"It's just the first card. It doesn't matter. That's why I found you. I can beat you up regardless of the hand."

Kaiden pointed his sword at Shirone.

"Let's start with a beating."

Shirone was willing to take the bait because he was equally angry.

"I told you."

Kaiden's nose twitched as he scrutinized the Photon Cannon that appeared around Shirone.

Even at a glance, the compression of photons was far greater than before.

"I won't forgive you if you interfere with me."

The Photon Cannon transformed into a flash and shot out, while Kaiden dodged to the right, leaving afterimages.

'He's incredibly fast. Is it Schema?'

Kaiden's movements, as he circled the training ground as if on rails, were hard to follow with the naked eye.

"Uoooo!"

The Cross Sword swung wildly, but each trajectory was clearly lethal.

'Photon Cannon!'

Kaiden gritted his teeth and lifted his head after being hit by the light curtain.

Sixteen red dots had appeared on his face.

"Homing Photon Cannon."

"Ugh!"

Kaiden ran with swift movements like a swallow, but he couldn't escape the seeker's pursuit.

"Shirone…!"

As he stopped, pushing off the ground, red energy rose and condensed into the shape of a cross.

The greatest talent one can obtain at the cost of sacrificing fate to reach their goal.

"Shironeee!"

As Kaiden shouted and swung his sword, the Homing Photon Cannons bounced off with a bang.

Each time he deflected one, his legs slid back a meter, and after deflecting all sixteen, he was 20 meters further from his original position.

"Ughhh!"

Kaiden, his face twisted in pain, glared fiercely at Shirone.

'What monstrous power. This is the Photon Cannon.'

The blade of the Cross Sword reflected the light, but the impact of the mass felt like his finger bones were shattering.

'How dare you…! My muse!'

Recalling Maya's face, Kaiden's eyes turned white as he gripped his sword tightly again.

"I'll kill you!"
