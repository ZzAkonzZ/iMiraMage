Chapter 739:

Minerva's description of the Kingdom of Corona as the terrace of the Ivory Tower was no exaggeration.

The tower, with its 468 floors, was a marvel of magic, law, and engineering, equipped with perfect stealth capabilities that no creature could reach on its own.

Shirone calmly observed the scenery outside the elevator as it ascended until the sky darkened.

Minerva asked in a casual tone,

"Not very impressed, are you?"

Though it was his first time seeing it with his eyes, the emotions Shirone felt upon returning from the realm of omnipotence were calm.

"I don't know. It's mysterious, but not particularly…"

"Whoo."

Minerva blew into his ear, causing Shirone to jump back in surprise.

"What are you doing?"

"Well, your Kar is so low I thought you might have lost your personality. I wondered if you were a psychopath."

Shirone recoiled, rubbing his ear.

"How could that be? And what does blowing in my ear have to do with being a psychopath?"

"Really? I don't get it."

When Shirone looked into Minerva's eyes, he felt his heart burn.

'This is…'

No one in the world could pressure Shirone, who had mastered the scale of the universe, with mere emotions.

'It's not just a killing intent. It's slaughter.'

How many lives must one end with their own hands to convey such a feeling?

The fear pheromones emitted by creatures at the brink of death clung to Minerva's body like an indelible scent.

"Now you're finally seeing me."

She had tried countless times since the Kingdom of Corona, but she couldn't make the presence of super cognition tense.

Now, she was at her 100%.

If anyone other than Shirone had been in this elevator, they would have died from shock.

"Why didn't you accept it?"

For the first time, the smile disappeared from Shirone's face.

"I don't want to fight. You wouldn't understand."

During the time when Mirach Minerva terrorized the world, she was known as the Grim Reaper.

Although not officially recorded, she was the person who committed the most murders in human history.

"Right. You'll never understand me."

A witch has no personality, and thus no taboos.

"For me, the best way to solve a problem is to eliminate it. If the conflict between Nane and Shirone is the problem, killing both would end it. Or at least one of them."

It was a simple solution.

"But I couldn't do it. If there had been a 51% chance of killing you, I would have done it in Corona."

She didn't see it as even 51%.

"The stars have clear convictions. They don't want violent conflicts, but such things happen occasionally. Before you go to Great Star, there's one last thing I want to confirm."

Minerva's broom, Sweeper, made a loud noise as it shook.

"Forgive me."

The aura of death emanating from Minerva's body filled the elevator, as if it would explode.

'Death.'

She had never counted how many she had killed.

Calculating by population density, the number of humans she had killed was approximately 42 million.

The pheromones of 42 million beings, collected only from those facing death, struck Shirone directly.

Could he forgive?

He might collapse, vomiting blood, unable to accept the need to forgive.

"Yahweh."

Shirone embraced Minerva with a warm light.

"We've arrived."

At the last floor of the Ivory Tower, leading to the Great Sanctuary, the stars who had conversed with Great Star awaited the elevator.

As the elevator, which had traversed dozens of floors in an instant, slowly stopped before them, Tzuoi approached.

"Congratulations. Who would have thought you'd become a star?"

Given her history of suppressing Shirone's spirit in the Kingdom of Tormia, it was fitting for her to offer the welcome.

"Gasp!"

As the elevator doors opened, the color drained from Tzuoi's face.

The scent of catastrophe wafted out of the elevator, causing the other stars to groan.

"This is… Black Death?"

It was the same black magic Fermie had used in the graduation exam, but on a different level of intensity.

'Who could have done this?'

Inside the elevator, Shirone and Minerva stood side by side.

"Miss Minerva, when did you arrive?"

Minerva, stepping out first, did not look pleased.

As she slowly scanned the surroundings with cold eyes, no one could meet her gaze.

'This is the person called the Grim Reaper…'

The mere scent from her body induced a fear that made one's legs tremble.

"Wait…"

It was Tzuoi's first time seeing Minerva, and she turned to Shirone with a shocked expression.

'He rode the elevator with someone like that?'

Shirone's expression wasn't particularly good either, but there was no trace of fear.

As the brain's illusion watched expressionlessly, Dreamer Luber slowly approached and asked,

"It's been a while, Miss Minerva. But using such murderous magic inside the Ivory Tower…"

"It's decided."

Interrupting Luber, Minerva glanced at Shirone behind her and turned away.

"The Human Safety Enforcement Division will kill Nane."

Shirone had forgiven Minerva.

"Let's go, Brain."

Saint Brain, who could be considered her direct subordinate, nodded and followed Minerva.

"Suddenly, what is…"

Tzuoi muttered in disbelief as she turned back, and Shirone approached with a smile.

"It's good to see you again."

Through Great Star's ability, Gaia, they already knew Shirone had reached the realm of infinity.

'But this…'

It felt like a newborn baby had suddenly become an adult in the blink of an eye.

"Are you really that kid from back then?"

Even then, Shirone was a promising talent in the world, but he had fallen far short of Tzuoi's standards.

"I was the same height back then too."

"Don't joke around! How did you reach such a level and still fail to stop Nane?"

It was also why Minerva had been puzzled.

"Indeed."

Only those who had faced Nane directly could understand his strength, so Shirone found no reason to explain.

"Welcome, Shirone. Do you remember me?"

Seeing Dreamer Luber, Shirone narrowed his eyes, but there was no significant emotional disturbance.

"You were a resident of the Ivory Tower."

"I work in the Integrated Universe Management Division. There's no hierarchy among the stars, but a leader is necessary. It seems we'll be following your lead from now on."

This was because Mahagaruta, the former leader of the Integrated Universe Management Division, had attained enlightenment.

"Hello, I'm Twelve Mini."

Mini, who had been sitting on Borbor's head, stood up and waved her tiny hands.

"I'm a two-star, specializing in scale magic."

Her size alone indicated her level, but the scale of the person standing before her was infinite.

To her, Shirone had reached the pinnacle of her field, and even as a fellow star, her eyes were filled with admiration.

"When I heard about you from Great Star, my heart raced. If you could teach me your secrets…"

"We're short on time."

As Luber interrupted, Mini immediately fell silent.

"Nane will activate all the altars within 48 hours. You must receive the title of star from Great Star before then."

"If it's that urgent, shouldn't we deal with Nane first?"

Even though receiving the title of a five-star in the Ivory Tower, where the world's greatest magicians gathered, was significant, Shirone was indifferent.

Luber, as if expecting this, smiled kindly.

"Of course, but filling the vacant position of the Five Stars is equally urgent. Great Star will agree. Follow me."

As it was an event for the Integrated Universe Management Division, residents from other departments dispersed.

Walking alongside Luber, Shirone looked down at the sound coming from below.

"Huff! Huff!"

Mini, who had jumped off Borbor, was running at full speed to keep up with Shirone.

"Are you okay? You look tired."

"Well… If I release the scale, I become too big. Don't worry, I'm a fast runner."

Shirone knelt on one knee and extended his hand.

"Hop on. Let's go together."

Luber glared, but Mini couldn't suppress her burning desire.

"Then, excuse me."

As Mini settled into Shirone's palm with a grin, Luber shook his head and continued walking.

"But… I thought I could choose the department where I would work."

According to Arte, whom he had met earlier, the Ivory Tower had five departments, and one chose based on their philosophy.

"Usually, yes. But the Five Stars are an exception. The departments don't really matter."

Mini added,

"The Integrated Universe Management Division is like the command center of the Ivory Tower. It coordinates with Great Star on matters from the other four departments."

"Mr. Mahagaruta also worked closely with Great Star. You don't have to, but…"

As they reached the top of the Ivory Tower, Luber pointed upwards.

"You should know how to get up there."

When Shirone looked up, all he saw through the glass ceiling was the blue sky.

"Is Great Star up there?"

"It's a place you can't reach without Great Star's permission. Only Mr. Mahagaruta had unrestricted access. Now you must prove your worth."

Since it was space, even a magician couldn't fly there.

'I can go.'

But that wasn't the answer.

'Unrestricted access means Great Star doesn't need to open the door.'

Placing Mini on the ground, Shirone said,

"I'll be back."

As the light of Yahweh gathered in his hand, it meant that all the information of the world was in his palm.

"Open."

With a short command, Shirone's body transformed into light and disappeared.

"Wow, this is…"

Mini's eyes widened, and Luber nodded as if he had guessed something.

"He is different from a Buddha who practices righteousness. It seems the entire universe willingly abandons its truth for him."

That is why it is a miracle.

This universe loves Shirone enough to deny the existing laws.

"…How is that possible?"

Luber looked up at the sky.

"Because he gave everything first."

Great Sanctuary

Crossing space, Shirone arrived at Great Star's sanctuary and calmly looked around.

A woman with a pure and delicate appearance, as if she had stars in her eyes, descended the stairs, her white dress fluttering.

"You've come, Hexa."

Sadness filled Shirone's eyes.

"I'm sorry I couldn't call you my child."

Every living being born from the stars was Great Star's child, but Shirone was an exception.

"Is there a more pitiful existence than you in this world? But think of this universe as your parent."

Great Star's hand, caressing Shirone's cheek, was cold but didn't feel cold.

"So, how does it feel to be a god?"

It was a joke to lighten the mood, but Shirone, who had experienced the 19,000 worlds of Andre, couldn't laugh.

"I have something I want to know."

A troubled expression crossed Great Star's face.

"Am I a user?"

This was the truth beyond infinity.

"Or am I just a ghost existing within the system?"
