Chapter 1149: Trigger (4)

It felt bizarre, like Havitz.

"No, the revulsion from this child is much stronger. It's so alien that it makes me nauseous."

It felt like seeing a fifth-dimensional creature.

"I don't know what that is, but if it exists, it would feel like this. I understand how Havitz feels."

Balkan looked back at Natasha.

"Can you catch it?"

Receiving a silent signal, Natasha performed the Dance of Death and dominated Wizard from behind.

"I'll break its arm."

But the child had already disappeared.

"Fast?"

No, it's different.

Without eyelids, there are no blind spots in the field of vision, but it felt like blinking.

"A blink. Is that it?"

In the inertia of moving at high speed, twisting her body, Natasha saw it in front of her.

Hoooooo!

A long, tree-like fetal face was sticking out its tongue and sobbing.

It was truly creepy, but the feeling suddenly disappeared along with the form.

"This is it!"

The identity of the blinking feeling.

"The moment that disappears…

As if the world had closed and opened once, Wizard was suddenly in a different place.

"Annoying."

Natasha, feeling the need for a strike, activated the Dance of Death - Battle Machine.

Then the Void God roared again.

Hoooooo!

"It's really a terrible dissonance."

Whether time flows fast or slow, the movement of the Void God seems unchanged.

"No. There is another movement within the movement."

A fractal structure.

Even if you take a part of the movement, it repeats the same movement as the whole.

"This strange vibration dominates the surroundings."

Hoooooo!

The moment the long tongue from the fetal face wavered, Natasha's fist shot out.

"Hoo…"

As the sobbing abruptly stopped, the form of the Void God seeped into the air.

"When it becomes perfectly quiet."

As the dissonance disappeared, the world lost exactly one frame.

Blink.

After the frame was omitted, the scenery Natasha saw was reversed.

"Huh?"

Checking the state with a delicate schema…

"Waist throw?"

Wizard grabbed Natasha's waist and threw her with all her might.

"Ugh…"

Thrown with inertia, Natasha hurriedly raised her arms.

Even though she reduced the impact with a fall technique, her body bounced like a ball and slid across the casino floor.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

Rolling 20 meters in that state, she took a crouching position and braked.

"Phew."

It was a sigh mixed with admiration.

"That one frame is really tricky. Once activated, there's nothing I can do. It's nothingness to me."

Hoooooo!

As the Void God stuck out its tongue again, Natasha twisted her head and her eyes gleamed with madness.

"Dance of Death…"

"Stop."

Balkan said.

"Stop it now. There's no point in us fighting each other."

"Us?"

The battle between Natasha and Wizard lasted only a moment, but it was enough. Balkan looked at Wizard and asked, "Is Havitz… dead?"

An adversary.

Whoever sent it, it was a combat weapon that countered all of Havitz's abilities.

Wizard shook her head.

"No."

"Why?"

Balkan, who knew best what Havitz was, was puzzled this time.

The child gave that feeling.

"…Because I'm weak."

While Smodo and Zetaro pondered those words, Natasha returned to her place.

Feeling the gazes of the casino people, Balkan approached Wizard and asked, "We seem to have something to talk about?"

Havitz arrived at Delta Headquarters an hour before midnight.

The fact that he had visited the casino had already been reported through various intelligence agencies.

The managers had uneasy expressions.

"What the hell did he do? Seeing that Rebecca is safe, there must have been another reason."

No one knew about the roughly six-hour gap after he left the casino.

And those six hours…

"Kekeke."

Every second was so boring that it made Havitz's mind completely excited.

"Interesting."

Wena Wizard.

All the creative results she produced in battle thrilled him.

"I don't know what she's thinking. I can't predict even one second ahead of her. Who is she?"

He was so engrossed in Wizard that he didn't notice the Delta knights approaching.

"Hey! Havitz! Stop!"

Although he didn't use Vanishing, Havitz's magic alone was suffocating.

"Ah…"

When Havitz came to his senses and turned his head, dozens of knights were positioned.

As anger filled Satan's eyes, the knights' instincts screamed.

"Ugh! If you're thinking of fighting…"

"Sorry."

As if it never happened, Havitz's anger disappeared, and he smiled with his eyes.

"I thought it was the Kashan sector."

It would be convenient to use Vanishing, but I didn't want to upset Uorin anymore.

"Be careful from now on."

Since no knight wanted a direct confrontation, the situation ended anticlimactically.

Havitz headed straight to Uorin.

"Hey! Where have you been!"

As soon as the door opened, a tearing voice was heard, but the voice of the heart was different.

Havitz smiled and said, "Congratulations. You hit the jackpot after a long time. Is the real assassination war starting?"

Uorin sighed. "Where have you been?"

"Just… getting some fresh air. Is there anything to worry about? I'll come when you need me."

If it were Satan's law, that would certainly be the case, but this time, she wasn't sure.

'The feeling has changed from before.'

Now that future sight was blocked, the Havitz she felt with her intuition was not just chaos.

"Anyway."

Time was running out.

"I know everything, so I'll skip the explanation. Can you enhance the curse that the Moon Kingdom painstakingly created?"

"It's possible. Karma is controlled by the system control branch of the Fire Management Company. But the spiritual realm is closed. To handle the work, we would need to send Siok."

"That's why I'm asking. There's only an hour left until midnight. Can you make it?"

She knew how dangerous it was to use the word "ask" with Satan.

"Alright, I'll do it."

But surprisingly, Havitz agreed without much interest.

"I have a favor to ask too."

"Huh?"

Uorin blinked.

Like Under Coder and High Gear, the time of the Apocalypse was also different from reality.

"Mining. It's tough work."

They had been mining for over two weeks, but finding meaningful information was difficult.

Marsha said, "It's still okay. Finding keywords from a specific time period is like digging for fossils. Once we find the events of that era, information will come out in bulk."

Operator propped her chin.

"Hmm, it would be nice to find a place where various eras are accumulated. Like a museum, a government document storage, or a similar database," said Number Seven.

"The problem is that we can't see ahead because of mucus. The air layer hasn't come out for 17 hours."

The mining team aboard the giant excavator 'Extra' watched the screen in the control room.

The laser drill endlessly repeated the scene of digging out the mucus.

Feeling frustrated, Operator grumbled, "Isn't the machine too slow?"

"Are you kidding? Do you think it's easy to dig out rock at 40 kilometers per hour? It's made of Titan alloy specially crafted by Metal Genome Company. The engine is…"

"Stop it, it's creepy. Anyway, it's all a fictional company you made up."

Number Seven pouted.

"Tsk! This kind of reality is the motive for creating masterpieces. You don't even know…"

While chatting for about 30 minutes, the Extra's body shook violently.

"What is it?"

"The forward speed has increased. The resistance is weakening. It seems like it will break through soon."

As everyone's eyes focused on the screen, the frustrating limestone suddenly disappeared.

"It's out!"

The joy was short-lived as enormous gravitational acceleration pulled Extra down.

"Aaaah! Aaaah!"

They had experienced the same situation several times, but the fear was still overwhelming.

"Wait."

Shirone, who had gone outside, grabbed Extra's body with the Hand of God.

"Phew."

Looking down in that state, they saw a small village with military facilities.

'It's a government agency.'

Shirone, with shining eyes, flew to a large spire and landed Extra.

"It looks like a monument."

The mining team, equipped with gear, searched the surroundings and returned.

"This is a war hero monument. The bodies seem to be placed elsewhere, but there should be belongings here. Let's check just in case."

The mining team opened the iron door installed on the ground and went down to the underground with thousands of shelves.

Shirone randomly inspected.

'Identification tags. Family photos. Notebooks. Diaries…'

His gaze fixed on one place as he slowly scanned the items on the shelves.

"Huh?"

It was a toy pyramid.

After briefly checking the hallway left and right, Shirone took out the pyramid, which fit snugly in his hand.

The inside was empty and light, but when shaken up and down, a piece of paper moved.

'A note.'

Opening the lid at the bottom and unfolding the twice-folded paper, a precise eye was drawn.

"The All-Seeing Eye…"

The eye of God that sees everything.

When he brought the note to the upper part of the pyramid, the lines matched perfectly.

"Shirone! Here!"

Hearing Marsha's voice, Shirone packed the pyramid and ran there.

"What's going on?"

"We found it."

What Marsha was holding was a thick book covered in mold.

'Ancient Sorcery Guide.'

Shirone decoded it with Ultima, but Marsha referred to the pictures rather than the language.

"While skimming through, suddenly this…"

On the page she opened, there was the mark of the Holy War and the flags of the 12 participating countries.

'The sorcery of the Moon Kingdom, curse. It is presumed to have been used for the assassination of royalty. The deceased at that time…'

Shirone immediately closed the book.

"What time is it in reality now?"

The bell announcing midnight.

When the solemn sound wave spreads through the capital at night, the lights in every house will go out.

"Screeeech!"

In a cave 4 kilometers away, it was the sound that would soon wake the ghost.

"Your Highness! Please escape!"

The exorcist, tearing apart the barrier, retreated to the entrance with Munryong.

"This can't be…"

The vision of a woman scratching her face with her nails was flashing like electricity.

The shaman jumped.

"Resentful spirit! Ghost wandering the nine heavens! You must resolve your grudge. You must resolve your grudge!"

The ghost, glaring at the pictures of kings on the floor, screamed in anger.

"I'll kill you! I'll kill you all! Everything alive, I'll kill them all!"

'It's so vivid. This is… too much.'

The exorcist's face turned pale, but Munryong didn't even twitch an eyebrow.

"What are you doing? Shoot."

As the trigger was pulled, the exorcists cut the final barrier with their swords.

"Go! Go! Your grudge… Ugh! Your resentful grudge…! Ugh, ugh!"

As the shaman, who vomited blood, collapsed, something white like frost shot out of the entrance.

"Hmph!"

Munryong, staring straight at the ghost's face flying towards him, said, "I am the king."

Aaaah…

The essence of the grudge that escaped the cave became a faint scream and decorated the night sky.

Ding. Ding. Ding.

The bell rang twelve times.
