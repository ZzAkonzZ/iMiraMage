Chapter 1137: Major Adjustments (5)

When Shirone asked, the dry voice began to sound like an echo again.

"Borbor called me that. But my real name is not Mika."

'Then?'

"I am Borbor."

Shirone's brow furrowed.

"Of course, the body that just died was also Borbor. I am her analytical mind."

'You created yourself?'

"Yes. Due to the extreme stress on her brainwaves, I was separated."

At twelve years old, Borbor was watching the people on the street and also realizing something.

'They are not looking at me. They are looking at themselves looking at me.'

The boundary of self and others collapsed, and from then on, Borbor did not observe people.

Her gaze turned inward.

"The realization that day helped Borbor in her life. By looking into herself, she could understand the nature of all humans."

'Knowing oneself is knowing humanity. It requires long contemplation and deep insight.'

"But her life was not happy. She was still weak, and she had to be wary of her mother, who was tired from work, and her father, who was constantly violent."

"Alcohol! Hey! Bring me alcohol!"

Borbor could only get a little money when her mother was in a good mood.

With eyes that could see through others' emotions, it wasn't particularly difficult, but…

"You ungrateful brat!"

The daily verbal and physical abuse gradually sickened Borbor's heart.

'Misantropy.'

Since her stepfather's tendencies were also within her, hatred for humans was soon hatred for herself.

"This wretched family!"

Her mother, bleeding and collapsed, and the broken bottle. Borbor was brutally beaten that day.

"Die! Die!"

As her consciousness gradually faded, her stepfather's face suddenly turned pale.

"Who… are you?"

Feeling as if the person lying on the floor was himself, he was engulfed in an unknown fear.

"Well. Who could I be?"

"You, you crazy brat…"

As her stepfather tried to escape, Borbor pulled him close and whispered in his ear.

The feeling of having one's innermost thoughts, which couldn't be spoken aloud, conveyed through someone else's mouth.

"Sob! Sob!"

And when morning came, he had hung a rope from the ceiling and hanged himself.

Her mother, regaining consciousness, drank without a word, and Borbor curled up in a corner, thinking.

'I killed someone. No, am I the one who died?'

With the boundary of self and others collapsed, her mind was quickly dying.

And then.

"Hello? Nice to meet you?"

A spark occurred in her brain, and bluish electricity began to appear before her eyes.

Borbor asked.

"Who are you?"

Her mother turned her head but soon lost interest after frowning for a moment.

"Well? What could I be? Hello?"

Borbor smiled brightly.

"Mika."

Shirone nodded.

'Just before the boundary of self and others collapsed, you created yourself to escape.'

"Yes. In reality, I am a very weak electrical signal. Born from Borbor's brain."

'Then why are you telling me this?'

Mika got to the point.

"Borbor respected you for sacrificing yourself for humanity. She always wanted to help."

'Really?'

Shirone felt melancholic.

"Although the main body is dead, I remain. I might be able to help you."

'Huh?'

"I don't know how this electrical signal is defined in reality, but it must be a very unique phenomenon. Now I will merge back into the world and return to my original state, but I know I can come back."

'Hmm.'

If electricity that sees everything in the world exists beyond phenomena as an entity…

"Don't worry. I won't inhabit anyone other than Borbor. Existence doesn't mean much to me. I just want to help you."

'What are you thinking?'

"There will come a moment when you need me. Borbor believed that, so it must be true. Of course, it would have been better if she could do it herself, but that's impossible now."

It might be an illusion, but Mika seemed sad.

"If you need my help, call me Mika. I will respond to your voice."

'But… you are Borbor.'

Perhaps through Mika, he could feel Borbor's life.

"I like the name Mika."

Reading the long resonance in the short answer, Shirone decided to follow her words.

'Okay. Got it.'

As the current at his fingertips disappeared, Shirone stood up and looked around.

The stars were not visible, and a cold wind blew through the hole created by the Three Thousand Worlds.

"Mika."

Suddenly, a weak current flowed through his body.

"Yes. Did you call?"

Shirone blinked and cleared his throat.

'No, I was just mumbling. Sorry. We just parted, and it's quite embarrassing.'

"It's okay. Borbor was human, but I, already merged with the world, have no concept of time. To inform you, Baal has reached the 76th floor. The positions of the other stars in the Ivory Tower are…"

Mika transmitted the critical information happening within the Ivory Tower through brainwaves.

'This is… truly amazing.'

Borbor's legacy, Mika, could process all the information in the world simultaneously.

-Call me again if you need me.

Shirone knew Mika had left again when the current flowing through his body disappeared.

Savoring the feeling for a moment, Shirone looked up at the ceiling and muttered.

"Clairvoyance."

He remembered the conversations he had with his friends in the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Society during his magic school days.

'At the end of the world…'

A bitter smile appeared on Shirone's lips.

"But I found it, Neid."

In the forest outside the Vatican.

Shirone and his group, along with Eden, watched the horrific ritual taking place in the village hall.

Seina felt anger that one of the people she respected, Maximus, was involved.

'Selling his soul to Satan!'

But the Holy Eye did not activate, and the Holy Sword Astasia did not shine either.

'Really?'

Seina became dejected.

'I have no right to punish evil. From the beginning, my faith was fake.'

Eden, observing her, spoke.

"Let's move for now. It's not safe to talk in the middle of enemy territory."

Shirone's group hid on the outskirts of the village and sat in a circle in the shade.

"Eden, how did you get here?"

"After the assassination of Havitz failed, I was lost. So I went to find my master."

A blue light flickered in her eyes.

"The Holy Eye. I followed the evil energy with these eyes. There is immense evil here."

Seina was shocked.

"How?"

Did she mean that Yora could possess the Holy Eye, which only the Paladins of the Lami Church could open?

"This is nonsense! The Lami Church believes in Cria! The Yora Church doesn't even have a god."

Eden replied.

"Fighting against evil is the same. But the method is wrong. The Lami Church says that God's messengers need three things: holy weapons, divine power, and a trained organization."

"But you saw it on your way here. By fixating only on what is visible, things get twisted. The Paladins are brainwashed, and the Inquisitors torture themselves to torture others."

"Don't judge by parts. It's true that mistakes were made, but it's a church that has fought evil for a long time."

"Then one day, the Satanic Church infiltrated the Lami Church."

Eden continued, ignoring the interruption.

"Of course, it's understandable. No matter how devout, clergy are not free from emotional illness. In fact, it would be harder because they have more to protect. The demons exploited that gap in their hearts."

Seina bit her lip.

"It's not just here. Many cardinals in various countries have fallen to the Satanic Church. The Lami Church is the world's largest religion, so how many people under it believe in the Satanic Church? Soon, the whole world will…"

"Stop it!"

Seina shouted.

"Is it wrong to be the world's largest religion? Then what about the Yora Church? Surely there are those among the Yora who have fallen to the Satanic Church."

"No, there are no such Yora."

"Lies! Do you think I'll believe that?"

"The moment they fall to the Satanic Church, they are no longer Yora. Then tell me, are the priests there still priests to you?"

Seina couldn't answer.

"Do you understand now? What is visible: positions, ranks, purity, divine power. Of course, noble beliefs are needed to obtain them, but they are not the measure of goodness. Instead, evil exploits the rigidity of such things."

Eden had once been the same.

"The voice of Satan's heart that I heard was terrible, but it was never rare or special. Rather… it was the most human and the deepest resonance."

"I know."

Seina admitted honestly.

"I became a Holy Knight and fought evil. But the more I did, the less I understood. What is goodness? I thought it was good to destroy evil, to slay demons…"

Her hands were stained with blood.

"The founder of the Yora Church, the First Yora, said this: The power of goodness is not to eliminate evil, but to turn evil into good. Therefore, that power can never take the form of destruction, and can only be achieved through self-sacrifice."

"Sacrifice?"

Eden made an awkward expression.

"Actually, I heard this from my master. Of course, it's still hard to fully understand and even harder to emulate, but it's the foundation of my strength. I can feel the power of goodness in you. You will surely regain your divine power."

Seina smiled.

Although she was comforted by a Yora from another church, she had now realized something.

'What is visible is not important.' It's the heart.

"Oh, and…"

Eden turned to Shirone.

"I knew you would be here. The reason I was dispatched to the Vatican was to deliver a message. Three hundred Yora gathered and discussed it. From now on, the Yora Church will fight against the Satanic Church worldwide, following Yahweh's will."

"But I don't intend to create a religion."

"I know. But the Yora Church doesn't distinguish between sects. That's why we can help. Only those who stand alone can love the whole world. That's the teaching of the First Yora."

Iruki said.

"You have something in common with Shirone."

Eden hesitated for a moment before speaking.

"After hearing the tradition, I thought the same. There are many commonalities. Practicing love and philanthropy. Valuing the invisible more."

Seina asked.

"Then did he also reach the level of Yahweh?"

"No. According to the tradition, he didn't even have divine power. He had no special power to fight evil."

"But he fought and defeated countless powerful evils, so the tradition continues to this day, right?"

"It's not described in detail, but when I heard the tradition, one thing came to mind."

Eden looked at Shirone.

"The Demon Sword Armand."

"Huh?"

"The object you carried. I think the first owner of that sword was the First Yora."

"When I asked who Armand was… the face of a woman sharpening a sword came to mind."
