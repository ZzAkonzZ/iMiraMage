Chapter 891: Desperate Measures (5)

200,000.

That was the scale of the human army Havitz had rallied when invading Central Continent.

Though integrated through ideological education, the impact of such training was minimal.

"It's a miracle they even survived..."

Joining the army of Hell, on the brink of world domination, was actually what they desired.

"If we win the war, we could gain immense wealth. No, perhaps even become kings."

With the reduction in human numbers, competition decreased.

"200,000 of us will share what 6 billion used to enjoy. A simple calculation shows that each person's share is..."

Everything that approximately 3,000 people enjoyed.

"That's just for an ordinary soldier." The face of the man who had risen from battalion commander to general lit up with excitement.

"My blood is finally boiling."

The reason humans don't want to go to Hell is simply because there are no pleasures there.

"It was good to join the army of Hell."

If it involves money, humans would sell even their souls.

When Iruki and Balkan strategized, everything was clear.

There were rules, and variables were controllable.

Among them, strategies were either perfect or lacking, never exceeding their limits.

But when you look away from the board and out into the world, reality is a game of numerous probabilities.

It could be good or bad; one could gain everything or lose it all.

"If it's just about competing over who's luckier, I’m ready anytime."

Iruki looked down at the map.

"After all, it's a fifty-fifty chance."

His gaze landed on the Tatetra Mountains, etched in his memory from long ago.

Deep in the Tatetra Mountains.

A tall man, gaunt as a skeleton, and a girl with black hair covering one eye were walking along a mountain path.

The man wore round sunglasses and had a horse-like long face with a slightly crooked nose.

The girl was no less sinister in atmosphere, but her skin was as white as porcelain, and she was strikingly beautiful.

The man's nickname was Guitaruman.

A member of the famous Magic Seven from the Black Line and one of the world's top 100 dangerous individuals.

He was also considered one of the best mental magicians in the world, alongside Sane.

He was called Guitaruman because he possessed a D-class object, "Gitaru."

Presumably, "Gitaru" meant guitar, which, though not a powerful object in combat, could play the sounds of all musical instruments simultaneously, making it the ultimate equipment for a musician.

Silence, endless silence.

The two had not spoken for four years, but their silence had now become their language.

The girl suddenly stopped walking.

While Guitaruman checked the time and sat down to chew on jerky, the girl went into the woods.

She casually looked around, pulled down her panties, and squatted to urinate.

Shh.

After finishing and emerging from the woods, all of Guitaruman's jerky had already disappeared into his mouth.

U 99

??????

The girl stared intently.

Then Guitaruman spat what he was chewing into his palm and offered it to her.

A mass of squished jerky.

But the girl gladly picked it up and chewed it with relish.

The two still did not speak. Guitaruman picked up his guitar case and stood up, and the girl, still chewing on the jerky, followed him.

An endlessly sprawling plain.

Coincidentally, 500 meters apart, Valkyrie and Hell's armies faced each other.

"Damn! To meet here."

While the Valkyrie unit, instructed to avoid engagement, was annoyed, Hell's army was eager to make their mark as soon as possible.

"Ran away like rats, and now we finally see them. Boys, get ready to sweep them away."

With their forces outnumbering the enemy by four times, Hell's army was filled with confidence.

And then, a man and a girl entered the middle of the standoff on the plain.

"What's that?"

The Valkyrie commander shouted, eyes wide.

"Hey! Get out of the way! This is going to be a battlefield soon!"

Ignoring the warning and continuing their walk, jeers poured from Hell's army.

"Hahaha! Came here to die on their own! The girl's quite pretty, huh? Hey, if you want to live, come this way." Even the worst villains instinctively try to save someone falling off a cliff.

Even the Valkyrie commander, who normally disregarded civilian deaths, felt oddly troubled by the situation.

"Hey, scare them off."

Clearly, there were lunatics in the midst of war.

An archer from the elite unit raised his bow to the sky and fired an arrow.

With a twang, the arrow traced a parabola and landed precisely in front of the two people.

Guitaruman and the girl stopped walking. As if pondering the arrow's meaning, he slowly sat down and opened his guitar case.

"What? A madman..."

In the middle of the battlefield, after tuning his guitar, Guitaruman slowly rose and plucked the strings.

Zing.

Unlike a regular guitar, it emitted a thick, lingering sound, electrified.

Title: First Night.

Lyrics and music by Guitaruman. Although there was only one guitar, it played numerous tones simultaneously to a fast beat.

The girl began to sing.

Sold to the emperor

Her voice was a deep alto, unusual for a woman, and her expression was like a cat that had tasted something bitter.

Valkyrie and Hell's armies stood dumbfounded, listening to her song for a moment.

It's my first time, but I'm not scared because you are the king

You can swing me around as you like

Guitaruman added a deep bass chorus.

Love me

At the same time, soldiers randomly scattered between the two armies flipped their eyes back in their heads and died.

"Urgh!"

Precisely 111 died as if by design.

I wish what tears into my body was enormous The girl turned her head to the left and danced like a soldier, and hundreds of thousands of soldiers mimicked her movements exactly.

Even as horses reared up in fright, the soldiers' movements were controlled as if puppeteered.

"Damn! What is this! Snap out of it!"

The general of Hell's army shouted, but in reality, he too was dancing to the music.

'It's magic. It can be resolved with Schema. But why can't I even twitch a finger?'

'Could it be?'

A figure flashed through the general's mind for a moment.

"Damn it! All troops retreat! It's Guitaruman!"

Once among the world's nuisances, Guitaruman from the Magic Seven was shrouded in secrecy.

Especially in crowd control technology, he was said to be unparalleled in history.

'It's the death rune!'

The ability to artificially generate the electrical signals a brain emits just before death through sound waves.

The girl sang out.

The morning will never come!

"Uuuurgh!"

8,888 more died.

Love me

As the chorus followed, a staggering 22,222 humans vomited blood and collapsed.

Salute to the emperor's greed!

The girl picked up a cane and saluted, and all soldiers placed their palms beside their eyebrows.

Your child won't be happy

As the girl turned her head to the right and moved her feet in a dance, soldiers beneath her feet fell like dominoes.

"These, these bastards...!"

The Valkyrie commander, stepping on the corpses of his subordinates, gritted his teeth.

"You bastards! What do you think of human life... Ack!"

Exactly 40,000 died, and the commander was not exempt.

From my arms...

The girl softly concluded the song.

I'll drown you

After 3 minutes and 48 seconds of song, the only ones standing on the plain were the two of them.

248,247 dead.

The girl raised her chin proudly, and Guitaruman put his guitar back in the case.

And just like when they arrived, they crossed the plain in silence.

Two hours later.

The generals of humanity and the demons received reports of their units being annihilated almost simultaneously.

"Are there no survivors?"

A terrified soldier trembled as he reported.

"Yes. But the enemy suffered the same fate. The figure appears to be from the Third World, and according to experts..."

"According to?"

The soldier raised his head.

"The highest probability is that it's Guitaruman."

Balkan blinked.

"Guitaruman? The Guitaruman of Magic Seven?"

"Yes, that’s..."

At that point, Balkan shut his ears and thought.

'Has he come out into the world? No, we must have summoned him. Damn, to overlook that.'

As Hell's army swept the world, his hiding place had also disappeared.

"What should we do? Should we chase him?" Balkan gave no response.

'His combat ability isn't concerning.

He's extremely vulnerable in one-on-one combat.

That's why he was hiding, but his ability with the death rune cannot be ignored.

"Forget it. No need to chase."

"Sir?"

"It was an act of God. Think of it as being struck by a typhoon, earthquake, or lightning."

Balkan slammed the table, and the soldier quickly shut his mouth.

"Damn! Just our bad luck."

Or was it really bad luck?

'I was too intoxicated. The fact that Valkyrie's forces didn't even respond to my moves...

Initially, I thought it was because they couldn't respond.

"I'm as mad as they come."

The messenger entering the Valkyrie headquarters looked unusually flushed.

"Report! On the plains of Gera...

Swapping our 50,000 troops for 200,000 of the demons' forces could be considered a victory.

"Guitaruman?"

As the commanders exchanged opinions, one looked at Iruki.

"Did you know?"

"No."

It was a strategy possible because he didn't know.

"If we had known, we would have prepared. We would have included it in the strategy, and so would their military. But...

Balkan knew why he could guess the weakness: the overwhelming disparity in force.

"To Balkan, 10,000 soldiers might be insignificant, but to me, even 100 are precious. To overcome the asymmetry of power, we must conserve every single one."

In the Tatetra Mountains, such negligible losses of forces occurred frequently.

"I suspected there was something. Of course, I couldn't be sure it would be helpful. It was a gamble."

"So, you're saying you gambled without knowing which side it would benefit?"

"Under normal circumstances, our chances of winning were less than 10 percent."

Iruki raised a finger.

"But if Balkan gambles, the odds jump to 50 percent. There’s no way I wouldn’t take such a favorable deal."

If they had lost, everything would have been over.

"Only gods and numbers can bear the weight of human responsibility. A quote from the Servant Magician Veronique. I simply chose the side with higher odds." It was still an incredible risk.

"Now that Guitaruman has appeared, I guess the world has really gone as far as it can."

Iruki agreed.

"Probably an outlier. I guess that in one-on-one combat, he’s extremely vulnerable. The more there are, the greater his power amplifies."

Yet it was still formidable.

"Secretly track Guitaruman's movements. A small elite team would be better."

"Yes, leave it to me."

"It’s not yet time to relax. We've also lost nearly 50,000 troops."

Iruki examined the map.

'But...'

Balkan's mistake had created a crack through which countless strategic opportunities to buy time were born.

"One day."

Iruki raised his index finger.

"Just one day, if the Second Corps can hold, it would be like strapping an unprecedented bomb to humanity."
