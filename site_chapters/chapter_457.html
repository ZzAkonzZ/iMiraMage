Chapter 457:

In the meeting of the research society, Miro revealed everything to Gaold that she had previously told Sein.

After passing through the dimension of emptiness in last year's survival test, an organization called the Holy War began to move.

They revealed the secrets of the world to Miro and even informed her about the existence of Anke Ra.

Miro mentioned that she had been to Heaven, and that experience had trapped her in a loneliness worse than death.

“Why…”

Gaold stood up and shouted.

“Why did you accept such a proposal?”

“If I don't, everyone will die. I will become someone's successor and protect this world. It's an honorable thing.”

“No, it's madness! What do you gain from it?”

“Ha, a follower of the Yore faith shouldn't say such things. Isn't it wonderful to be able to give my love to the whole world?”

“But what about my love?”

“Gaold, love isn't something you can do alone. I don't love you.”

“You can love alone. You pretended to date Sein just to deceive me, didn't you?”

Miro sighed.

Normally, such emotions would be embarrassing, but compared to her situation, they were nothing.

“Hah. How did we end up like this?”

Gaold pointed a finger at her.

“It's all your fault!”

“Ha ha ha! What did I do? I was just standing still. This popularity…”

Even in a situation where her life was at stake, Miro's tendency to joke infuriated Gaold.

Didn't she care about the feelings of the person who had to send her away forever?

“Fine! Do whatever you want! I'll forget you from now on.”

Gaold left the research society abruptly, but his heart was more inflamed than before.

Miro's tomb.

The design of Istas, created by Sein and Miro, was like crafting her own coffin while waiting for her death.

‘There must be a way. I can save Miro.’

There was no way.

When he started to realistically consider solutions, Gaold realized how enormous the situation Miro was involved in was.

Gaold prayed through the night.

“God, why her? Why does the person I love have to suffer such pain?”

As Gaold grew more emaciated, the world continued to turn, and finally, Sein informed him that the committee of twenty had been convened to decide Miro's fate.

Gaold recalled the names of the committee members.

Miro's mentor, Mirhi Alpheas, Olivia from the Tormia Kingdom's teaching council, King Adolf of Tormia, and the queen.

Having four people from his own country among the twenty might be an excuse for the secret history that would surface later, but Gaold wasn't satisfied.

The committee included the highest authorities in various fields such as state, religion, society, humanities, history, philosophy, and art, as well as the emperors of the three realms and top-level reapers.

‘Gustav Havitz. He will never forgive Miro.’

So Gaold had only one person to rely on.

The religious figures invited were the pope of the world's largest sect, the Lami Church, and Yora Amium, known as the living saint of the Yore faith.

‘Yes, if it's him…’

Though less influential than the Lami Church, Amium's character was recognized worldwide.

If he could influence the committee, he could sufficiently check Havitz.

Thus, the Judgment of the Twenty was being prepared discreetly, away from the world's eyes.

Near Istas at Alpheas Magic School, the space-time dimension of a scale mage opened, and the world's highest authorities gathered there.

After several sleepless nights, Gaold spotted Amium, the symbol of Yore, walking surrounded by his knights.

“Amium! Amium!”

As Gaold rushed out, the knights' blades flew towards his neck.

“Stop.”

Caught between crossed blades, Gaold couldn't move.

Amium ordered the knights to lower their swords.

As the living saint, it was unthinkable for him to kill someone, so the knights obediently followed his command.

“Who are you? How did you get in here?”

“I am Gaold, a follower of the Yore faith.”

Amium silently watched the kneeling Gaold.

“Please save Miro! I have lived my life as a servant of God without shame! I will give my soul! Please, just save Miro…!”

“Did you say Gaold?”

“Please… grant this humble servant God's grace! Show mercy to Miro…!”

“God will forgive us as well.”

Gaold's eyes widened.

It was a shock that shattered the faith he had built his entire life on.

Amium intended to send Miro away.

“Yora…”

“Let's go.”

Amium walked away with his guards without another word.

“Security is lax.”

Those were the last words Gaold heard from Yora.

“Sixteen votes in favor, one against, three abstentions. The Judgment of the Twenty approves Miro's Moon Project.”

No one applauded.

Whether they felt guilty or not, everyone knew it was an unforgivable act.

“So it has been decided.”

Even after hearing Sein's words, Gaold stared blankly at the wall.

“Forget it. Miro made the best choice. Her sacrifice gives us all new life.”

“New life?”

Gaold slowly turned his head.

“No, we've all lost our light. Because one person took everything and disappeared, we've gained an indelible original sin. We will forever rely on the second and third Miro.”

“What's wrong with that?”

Sein looked at the coordinates of the space Miro would leave.

“What's wrong with living with original sin? If that's how it turned out, then we live that way. That's humanity.”

“I will go to Miro.”

Gaold stood up.

“Don't. She's the one suffering the most right now. We can't do anything for her.”

“To Miro…”

Gaold gritted his teeth.

“I will go.”

The night before Miro was to leave the world, Gaold called her to the park.

Contrary to his expectations, she came willingly, smiling brightly as if she had already made up her mind.

“Wow, I'm so glad. I was bored to death with no one visiting. Sein is really heartless.”

As Gaold stared at her in a daze, Miro laughed and patted his shoulder.

“Why so serious? This is a good thing. I will become a myth beyond history. What could be more glorious for a mage? Living like this is boring anyway.”

Though she was always talkative, she was even more so now.

Was it because this was her last conversation with someone?

When Gaold realized this, he hugged Miro.

“Let's run away.”

Miro remained calm.

“Let's run away somewhere! Leave with me right now! I'll protect you somehow!”

“How will you protect me?”

Gaold grabbed her shoulders and pushed her back. He looked straight into her eyes and said,

“I'll give my life, do whatever it takes…”

“Gaold, this isn't like you.”

“I know! But I don't care anymore! Why you? There are so many people in the world! If it were me instead…”

“Because you're weak.”

Gaold looked at Miro with sad eyes.

“So I have to do it. Gaold, understand.”

“No. Now I can only see you. I can't do anything without you!”

Gaold held her hand and pleaded.

“Let's just run away! Even for a day, no, even for a few hours…”

Miro roughly shook off his hand.

“Why are you doing this to me! You can't do anything for me!”

Tears welled up in Miro's eyes as she raised her voice.

“Then… save me…”

Seeing the slight resentment in her eyes, Gaold trembled as if struck by lightning.

His mind went blank, and an indescribable flash of emotion flowed through him.

Miro suddenly regained her composure and turned away coldly.

“Forget it. I said that because your selfish thoughts were so pathetic.”

Even after Miro left and dawn broke, Gaold stood there as if nailed to the spot.

On the morning Miro was to leave, Gaold looked up at the altar of the Yore Church and put on his pendant.

“God.”

It was Gaold's first prayer in his life.

“For the first time, your servant, who has obeyed you all his life, is asking for something. I don't care if I fall into hell. I don't care if I cry out in eternal pain. Just please, give me Miro.”

Gaold took his first step towards Miro.

In front of the Istas magic warehouse, the committee of twenty, historians, and high-ranking officials from related departments were waiting.

There was no need for words about the already decided matter, and Miro slowly walked towards the tomb she had created for herself.

Having already mastered the essence of scale magic, opening the space-time dimension was no problem for her.

Sein watched her pass by in silence and then spoke.

“Goodbye, Miro.”

Miro's steps halted.

Thank you.

Sein wanted to say.

My feelings for you were genuine.

But he remained silent.

Thus, Sein let go of the woman he loved most coldly from his heart.

Just when it seemed everything would end easily, someone shouted.

“Miro!”

Sein's face turned pale as he looked back.

Gaold, wearing the Yore faith's uniform, was running towards them.

“That madman, does he want to die…”

Before Sein could stop him, the highest-ranking knights drew their swords.

“Deal with him.”

“No.”

The imperial official overseeing the matter intervened.

“This is a highly sensitive issue that could change the direction of humanity. There must be no deaths recorded in history.”

“Then?”

At the official's signal, mages and law enforcers cast anti-magic spells and laws to restrain Gaold's movements.

‘I can do it! I can reach her!’

As Gaold got closer to Miro, his heart pounded faster.

Tick. Tick.

At that moment, powerful anti-magic spells attacked his mind, and the laws of the enforcers pressed him to the ground.

“Ugh! Miro!”

With only his head raised, Gaold called out to Miro, but she didn't turn around.

No, she couldn't turn around.

“Argh!”

Using all his strength, Gaold tried to stand, but the mages and enforcers of the committee were the best in the world.

“Grrr! Grrr!”

As the restraints tightened, Gaold's face twisted in pain.

Tick.

“Crush him. As long as he doesn't die, it doesn't matter.”

As the power of the laws increased, Gaold screamed under the crushing pressure that felt like it would break his spine.

“Aaaagh!”

Thud! Thud!

Blood vessels burst in Gaold's eyes, causing tears of blood to flow, and blood seeped from between his teeth. His facial nerves twitched uncontrollably, making his skin ripple.

Pain Amplification 2x.

“What is this?”

The mages and enforcers finally sensed something was wrong.

No normal human could withstand such intensity.

Gaold, with blood-filled eyes, looked at Miro.

She was only 20 meters away.

Tick…

But those 20 meters felt like an eternity.

…Tock.

Those damn 20 meters!

“Aaaagh!”

Crack! Crack!

Feeling the pain of his entire body being twisted, Gaold continued to step forward.

Pain Amplification 4x.

The closer he got, the further Miro seemed to be.

And at that moment, Gaold's clock stopped forever.
