Chapter 406:

Rumble. Thud, thud.

The buildings of Istas stacked up, transforming into a massive structure. It looked like a giant with thick legs rooted in the ground.

Sein, who had opened the warehouse on the giant's right leg, spoke to Alpheas.

"Please reactivate Istas once we go inside."

Only those who go to Heaven have the right to verify Miro's spacetime.

At least, that's what Alpheas thought, and he intended to do what he could.

"Let's begin."

The playful look on Gaold's face had disappeared.

Finally, he would meet Miro.

It was fortunate that she was in deep meditation. The anger he had bottled up for 20 years might otherwise have shaken him.

Once everyone was inside the warehouse, Alpheas reactivated Istas.

Following the pattern set by Sein, the cube-shaped buildings began to intricately combine and separate repeatedly.

Thud. Thud. Thud.

The heartbeat of Istas could be heard.

With each step, Sein's amber irises ticked.

The left iris tilted left and right at a 15-degree angle, while the right iris rotated at different speeds in each section.

Sein varied his stride instead of his walking speed, controlling space to manipulate time.

"This way."

Sein turned at a right angle from the center of the warehouse and opened a door, connecting it to another warehouse that rose up, forming a passageway.

As soon as they crossed the boundary, the previous warehouse fell below and was overtaken by other blocks, moving away.

This process repeated countless times.

'Amazing. Only a servant could pull this off.'

The warehouse moved, Sein moved, and time moved.

It felt like the concept of space he knew was collapsing. Shirone was truly walking through four-dimensional spacetime.

When Sein's steps stopped at the final destination, Shirone's eyes filled with nostalgia.

Paranormal Psychic Science Research Society

The familiar sign was hanging there.

Always tilted, ignoring balance, as if it would tilt on its own.

When they opened the door, the traces of the agents' ransacking were still there.

The formulas Iruki had written on the blackboard were partially erased, and the picture books Nade had cherished were torn to shreds and left scattered.

Sein walked to the sofa.

"In 12 seconds, we will reach the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Society. The upper floor of Istas is at those spacetime coordinates. Armin, it's your turn now."

Armin stepped forward, passing Sein.

As a scale mage who handled time and space, he could guess what the upper floor of Istas was without lengthy explanations.

"7 seconds. 6 seconds. 5 seconds."

As Sein began the countdown, a strong wind, like being caught in a storm, could be heard.

It was the phenomenon occurring as the spacetime coordinates of the upper floor approached them.

The objects in the research society blurred, melting into the density of the strong wind, and a stone door, similar to the one seen in Galliante, overlapped as an afterimage on the table in front of the sofa.

Whoooooosh!

"4 seconds. 3 seconds."

As time passed, the scenery faded, and the color of the stone door became more vivid.

The objects flickered and became transparent in the receding spacetime coordinates.

"2 seconds. 1 second."

When all the scenery disappeared, and only the stone door reached 100% materialization.

"Now."

Armin cast his spell.

Stop.

"Oh my..."

Siena's eyes widened in amazement.

Etella and Shirone felt the same.

The upper floor of Istas, hidden in a specific space and time, was revealed, captured by the stop magic.

Twenty years ago, a genius girl who shocked the world appeared. Humanity imprisoned her at a coordinate in spacetime where no one could find her.

This was the truth of the upper floor of Istas, which had been only a rumor at Alpheas Magic School.

'So this is what it was.'

Shirone finally understood Sein's words.

The origin of the cube. The place countless agents couldn't find.

Armin turned from the stone door and spoke.

"As long as time is stopped, the door will remain. But it's best to hurry. If the magic is released, you'll be trapped in Miro's spacetime."

It seemed unlikely, but with their lives at stake, caution was necessary.

Shirone had also escaped Miro's spacetime before, but that was because he was an unlocker.

Moreover, Miro was currently in deep meditation, so they couldn't rely on her help.

Gaold didn't think about future problems.

Despite being a first-class grand mage, his heart was pounding, and his face was more flushed than ever.

"Let's go, Sein."

"Yes."

Sein's irises rotated in opposite directions as he followed.

A phenomenon that occurred when calmness and excitement acted simultaneously.

This ability to perfectly separate emotion and reason was why he could reach the pinnacle of anti-magic.

"Shirone, was it? You should do it."

The door to the upper floor was sealed with an immortal function, similar to the door of Goffin.

There were two unlockers in the team anyway. But since Armin needed to focus on the stop magic, the opening was Shirone's task.

When Shirone unlocked the immortal function, the letters on the stone door glowed, disassembling into small cubes and spreading into a sphere.

As the material melted, a massive black hole formed.

"Um... who goes in first?"

For some reason, it felt like they needed to decide the order.

It didn't matter now, but it could be interpreted as a significant historical event later.

"The one who unlocked it should go first. Shirone, you're the first entrant."

Armin gently pushed Shirone with a kind smile.

Siena, watching this, felt her mind grow more complicated. She felt like she couldn't even remember who Armin was anymore.

"Then, I'll go in."

After a moment of darkness, a view of the center of a massive temple unfolded.

Columns 2 kilometers high supported the ceiling, unmistakably Miro's spacetime.

'I never thought I'd come back here.'

People began to emerge from the black hole one by one.

Just like when Shirone first came here, everyone seemed speechless at the enormous scale of the temple, which defied human comprehension.

"Huh? But..."

Shirone quickly turned around. Everyone else was also looking around.

"What? What's going on?"

Miro was nowhere to be seen.

"Shirone, is this place..."

"No. This is Miro's spacetime. It's exactly the same as when I came here a year ago."

Gaold cast a fly spell.

With a pop, his body quickly flew from one end of the temple to the other.

It was a 'jet' type spell that flew by exploding air pressure, not by lift.

Gaold quickly flew around the vast temple.

It was similar to Shirone's flight, but the level of movement was on a different scale.

When Gaold returned to his place, his face began to twist horribly. It looked like the inferno of hell was about to unfold.

"Those damned bastards..."

"Calm down. Nothing has happened yet. It's just that Miro isn't in Miro's spacetime."

Miro wasn't in Miro's spacetime.

It was a fact, but it was something that shouldn't happen.

First, no one could enter Miro's spacetime. Also, if she was in deep meditation, it was impossible for her to leave on her own.

No, if that were possible, Gaold wouldn't have waited 20 years.

"It's hijacking."

Sein concluded.

"It seems Heaven has kidnapped Miro."

Gaold asked.

"Is that possible? Nothing has ever crossed over from Heaven before."

"No, it has."

Armin bent down and picked up debris from the floor.

It was rubble that had fallen from the ceiling when Miro's spacetime was impacted.

Rubbing it between his fingers to check the texture, he spoke.

"I heard Miro's spacetime cracked recently. A lawmaker crossed over to the Kingdom of Tormia. I understand a mage from the Akeanis Temple handled it and reported it."

"But it was just a mental body. A crack isn't enough to kidnap a whole person."

"The Ivory Tower agrees. But that's just human thinking. If it's Heaven, especially the archangels, they might have found a special method."

Sein propped his chin and pondered.

"Why did they take Miro?"

It was a puzzling question.

Heaven had been planning the final war and had consistently shown intentions of infiltration since Miro's spacetime was created.

Shirone asked.

"Isn't it to invade this world?"

Etella spoke.

"No. I don't think that's possible."

Sein agreed.

"Taking Miro alone has no effect. Even if they kill Miro, Miro's spacetime won't be destroyed."

Shirone asked in confusion.

"It won't be destroyed?"

"Miro's spacetime is fundamentally a dimension based on the void. It continuously creates new dimensions within the dimension. It uses that infinity to create boundaries in spacetime. And Miro accelerated the void's speed through deep meditation. Even if Miro dies, the inertia of deep meditation will continue to strengthen Miro's spacetime. It's a theory, of course, but you wouldn't understand."

As one moves further into the mental world after death, the control of consciousness weakens, and one is influenced by the most intense inertia experienced in life.

Even if the virtual zone is turned off, if one can't escape the void, they will be eternally buried in their mind.

"No, I think I understand."

Sein snorted.

"That's just a delusion. It's a beginner's mistake to think you've grasped the essence just because you understand the context."

How many people in this world truly understand Miro's spacetime?

Probably not many, and certainly not a novice attending a magic school.

Siena spoke up.

"Shirone has passed through the void."

Sein's eyebrow muscles twitched. It was the maximum expression of surprise he could show.

The only person who had passed through the void at Alpheas Magic School was Miro. He vividly remembered the uproar when a global genius had appeared.

'Hmm. Indeed, Gaold has found someone worthwhile.'

Gaold summarized simply.

"Anyway, they can't kill Miro. If they took her, it means they've found a way. We don't have much time. Once the team is ready, we'll head to Heaven."

Sein had no objections. He had been navigating the Black Line for 20 years for this day.

'But... is this really normal?'

Something felt off.

It was just a feeling.

Could today's outcome be merely a series of small coincidences, an unfortunate event that could happen to anyone?

If so, what was this unsettling sense of discord that wouldn't leave his mind?

If things were going to go wrong like this, they should have gone wrong somehow over the past 19 years, a highly irrational thought.

'Why now? Why is this happening? Am I thinking normally? The world doesn't flow as we wish. Attributing transcendental meaning to coincidences is no different from superstition.'

He wanted to conclude that way.

But Sein couldn't shake it off.

It wasn't an illogical thought. It was a subtle disturbance occurring in the realm of intuition beyond logic.

'Something is definitely...'

Something was out of place.
