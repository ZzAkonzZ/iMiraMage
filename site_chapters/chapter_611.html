Chapter 611:

"Tormia!"

The herald shouted.

"Royal! Magic School!"

The reason the chant for the Royal Magic School was longer than for other prestigious schools wasn't just because it had more syllables.

All eyes turned to the road leading to the north gate of the royal castle, where a massive carriage, pulled by eight horses, occupied the entire width of the road.

"Wow!"

The cheers were much louder than before.

The king-sized carriage was used only in the capital, and it couldn't be operated without the road control support of the Royal Castle Traffic Management Department.

"Tch, so this is the Royal Magic School?"

It was a school established by the king's order to train mages to lead the nation, and most of the key positions in the association were held by graduates of the Royal Magic School.

The four doors on the sides of the carriage opened simultaneously, and 30 students from the Royal Magic School stepped out in a line.

Their eyes were filled with pride as the kingdom's top elites, and they remained unfazed by the applause of the people.

The students from other prestigious schools looked at them with disdain.

'There's definitely an unfair aspect.'

Graduating 20 students.

No matter how competitive the Royal Magic School was, having two-thirds of the students pass was a significant advantage.

"Dante, don't you regret it?"

Sabina asked.

Dante and his group had been in the top 30 ranks at the Royal Magic School before transferring.

If they had stayed and made it to the final 30 for the graduation exam, the competition would have been much easier.

"No need to be swayed by the name."

Dante observed the students from the Royal Magic School.

Unlike the students from other prestigious schools, who were wary of notable figures like Shirone, Dante, and Fermi, they wore confident expressions.

"The place where I fight is the top school. Be confident."

Sabina and Closer smiled.

'Yes, Dante, because you're the best.'

"Students, enter!"

Following the herald's command, the barricades opened, and 150 students in formal attire began to march into the central plaza in formation.

Although they were students, they were graduating students, and the energy of 150 aspiring mages was enough to form an army.

"All the capable ones in the kingdom are gathered here."

From a point 2 kilometers away from the central plaza, the current head of the Magic Association, Lupist, watched the undulating black wave while casting Fly Magic.

With a pale face and sharp features, a scar on the right side of his mouth, and eyebrows that looked like they had been clawed by a beast, Lupist was an imposing figure.

Jane, the chief secretary assisting Lupist, spoke.

"If we consider only their brains, it's no exaggeration to say they are the top 150 in the kingdom. However, they are ultimately just parts of the state."

With her red hair tied up and wearing a silk robe, her face was fierce like a wildcat, and her artificially removed wrinkles made her age hard to guess.

"That's true."

Lupist didn't acknowledge individual worth.

"But they are key parts."

Thus, his current statement was the highest praise he could give the students.

"It's time. Let's go. I'll head to the association to prepare."

As soon as Jane finished speaking, the two flew off in different directions.

Although flight magic was prohibited within the capital, no one could stop Lupist from flying to the central plaza.

"Look…!"

People pointed to the sky, watching Lupist.

Under the gaze of nearly a thousand people, Lupist landed on the platform, his black coat billowing.

'Flight magic in the capital.'

Collie realized that all of Lupist's actions were political maneuvers.

"Greetings, everyone. I am Lupist, the head of the Tormia Magic Association."

As his voice echoed through the plaza via the sound amplifier, the eyes of the graduating students changed noticeably.

'That man…'

First-class Archmage Raphael Lupist.

Known as the Steel Archmage, he was one of the core strengths of the Tormia Kingdom.

'He's definitely different.'

Although Shirone had lived with Olivia, a second-class Archmage, the presence of one of the kingdom's only four first-class Archmages was on a different level.

'Another Gaold.'

That was Shirone's first impression.

"Before we begin the inspection, I feel I should explain something. Mages don't act unless they understand."

His rough speech was uncharacteristic of a head of the association, but it was tolerated because he was the head.

"In truth, there's no special reason for calling you here. I just wanted to see my future family with my own eyes as I take office."

As Lupist's gaze swept across the plaza, the students' hearts pounded.

'He's looking at me. He definitely saw me.'

Everyone thought the same, and Lupist's gaze finally returned to Shirone.

"You are the top 150 in the kingdom. Out of you, 60 will pass. Of those 60, about 10 will obtain certified mage licenses. And out of those 10, only 5 will be able to work at the Magic Association."

The atmosphere grew solemn.

"Of course, some will leave the Red Line, and some will dig into dungeons and never come out. You don't know it yet, but mages are a difficult profession to manage. To work at the association, you need patriotism."

Lupist spread his five fingers.

"I want to form a team of the top 5 elites. I don't know who among you will pass, but if you think you're the best, aim for the Magic Association."

It was a kind of propaganda.

"Why the Magic Association, you ask? First, you'll have beautiful women and handsome men following you."

While the citizens laughed, the students remained serious.

"And you'll receive significant bribes. Illegal funds."

Even in front of a large audience, Lupist was unabashed.

"Of course, I don't encourage it, but who knows? There are thousands of ways to get away with it, right? You're smart."

That was also true.

"I don't control mages because they can't be controlled. The only standard I demand from you is this."

Lupist's index finger pointed straight ahead.

"Can you solve problems?"

The students' eyes burned with determination.

"Whether you're conscientious or a scumbag, I don't care. Need to beat someone? Then beat them. The king will remain silent. Want to kill someone? Then kill them! The king will close his eyes!"

Lupist's voice rose.

"Do whatever you want! The only rule you must follow is to make the kingdom strong!"

He pounded the platform with his fists.

"Everyone complains about this and that, but you must not! You're smart! Mages are not problem raisers; they are problem solvers! That's why you are given public power, and that's why you are given a license to kill!"

The air in the plaza trembled with the boiling enthusiasm.

"Who is the best mage here?"

Me.

'It's me.'

Everyone thought the same.

"Eliminate all factors that hinder the kingdom's development! If it's a god, then kill the god! That's the kind of mage the association wants! Understood?"

"Yes!"

The students' voices were so loud that the citizens were startled.

"Chant it! Absolute power!"

"Absolute power!"

The students' voices echoed in the quiet plaza.

"That is the greatest gift the Magic Association can give you. If you want it, come to the association."

As Lupist cast spatial transfer magic and flew to the Magic Association, a thousand eyes followed him.

It was a common sight for the graduating class but a miraculous phenomenon for the general public.

"Now, we will proceed with the inspection. Each school, please stand in four rows with a 5-meter interval."

The students moved in perfect order, and Shirone lined up with Iruki and Nade.

"Whew, that was intense. I was really nervous."

"Yeah. I've met Gaold, but Lupist's charisma is just as strong."

Iruki said.

"Raphael Lupist. In the political world, he's considered a representative of the far-right. Showing the nation's strength unites people, but his statements about public power were dangerous. If he didn't have that level of charisma, the citizens would have revolted."

The inspection began, and as the 150 students, starting with the Royal Magic School, marched along the road, the citizens applauded and welcomed them.

'Still, it's nice to be treated like this. Maybe being a mage isn't a bad job…'

Even Nade considered it for a moment, so powerful was Lupist's impact.

"Huh?"

Shirone noticed first, and Nade followed his gaze.

'Senior Liz.'

Liz stood in the crowd, smiling gently.

Though he thought he had forgotten, seeing her face made his heart race again.

'But why…?'

Even as Nade looked at her with questioning eyes, Liz couldn't give a clear answer.

'What am I doing here?'

She had pushed him away harshly, so what right did she have to hold onto him now?

It's much better to regret something you did than to regret something you didn't do.

Recalling the craftsman's words, she steeled herself and clapped vigorously.

"You're amazing, Nade."

Her voice was drowned out by the cheers, but Nade felt as if he had heard it.

'Senior Liz is watching me.'

Only me.

It felt exhilarating, like being in a bathtub filled with drugs, but it wasn't enough to heal his emotional wounds.

'I'm sorry, senior.'

It was too painful, and he was afraid to step back into that place.

"Nade…"

Liz watched Nade walk away with a sad expression but soon smiled.

'Okay. I'll take the pain now.'

She finally understood the craftsman's words.

Following the military band's lead, the students arrived at the Magic Association, and its grandeur immediately captured their attention.

The 18-story building, towering as if to pierce the sky, gleamed in the sunlight, and the top mages, whom Lupist had emphasized so much, stood guard at the entrance in a rigid stance.

"This concludes the inspection. Please submit your exam applications in a single file."

As they passed through the revolving door, Shirone saw the familiar front desk.

Due to the large number of students, they moved via the stairs, allowing them to get a better look at the Magic Association they had dreamed of.

"It's even more impressive than I heard."

It was on a different level from the buildings that wealthy nobles built just to show off.

"It's no exaggeration to say this is the pinnacle of humanity's advanced technology. This is definitely motivating."

Although Shirone had not only toured but also lived here before, his desire was no different from that of the other students.

'It's no longer a dream.'

There were times when he worried about surviving and returning from Heaven, let alone graduating, but now, with the submission of the application, only the graduation exam remained.

'It's Senior Plu.'

When they arrived at the External Affairs Department, Plu, dressed in uniform, was standing at ease.

Her professional demeanor, with her gaze fixed straight ahead and not on anyone, exuded a sense of dignity.

Top graduate of the Royal Magic School.

Shirone could now feel how much blood, sweat, and tears she must have shed to earn that title.

'Should I greet her?'

As he got closer and his thoughts deepened, Plu's left hand, which had been behind her back, slowly slipped out.

'Senior.'

Smiling, Shirone gave her a high-five as he passed by.
