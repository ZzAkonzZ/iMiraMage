Chapter 1263:

The desire to die might stem from rage, but the lack of will to live came closer to emptiness.

As Shirone watched the line of people who had regained their minds, he found himself thinking:

‘Which is sadder?’

“Aaaah! Why is this happening to me? Put it back! Put everything back the way it was!”

Those who had said they wanted to live moments ago were now consumed by fury.

What exactly is a human being?

“I don’t know,” Euntara said.

‘Is there really anyone who truly wants to live? Are we living… or just being kept alive?’

“Yahweh, humans are a burden. No matter how much you try to fill them, it’s like pouring water into a bottomless jar. They don’t care about the sacrifices of others—because deep down, they expect it.”

“I’m human too.”

Otherwise, he would be nothing more than a signal.

“Of course you are—a human who sacrifices. That’s what I mean. Are you really okay with that? Sure, if no one takes out the trash, the world becomes a garbage heap… but maybe that’s fine too? We all have the option to let it all burn.”

Shirone knew it too.

That after such a massive sacrifice, what returned would only be greater expectations, the disappointment that comes with them, criticism… and perhaps even higher standards.

“There’s someone I love.” If he could finish every task given to him, maybe—just once—he could smile.

“That’s enough for me.”

Just another human being.

‘I’ll believe I’m fighting for them.’

This wasn’t something one could endure by telling themselves they were doing something grand.

Euntara looked around at the people.

Those who had experienced countless futures through the wave of In, those who had eventually felt the hollowness of life.

‘Can they come back?’

Even if they say they want to live… they’ll want to die again.

Could they truly invest their hearts in reality again, with the rage of life rekindled?

“We can do it.”

Shirone didn’t doubt it.

“They’ll start living again. Even if it’s just being kept alive… eventually, they’ll rediscover it.”

Something to love.

“First, let’s set up a barrier toward Delta. Once the bat burns completely, the underworld and reality will merge. That’s when good and evil will truly clash. And more importantly…”

From where Shirone turned, a booming explosion erupted.

“Ymir.”

An indescribably massive monster was growing over the rooftops of buildings.

Euntara stared in a daze.

“Ymir… you say?”

It was beyond describing its shape—it invoked despair the moment you laid eyes on it.

“Yahweh, tell me. Is that good… or evil?”

“I don’t know,” Shirone replied.

He couldn’t define it.

But maybe… that very feeling was what made it closest to ‘existence’ itself.

Five minutes earlier.

Ymir let out a roar.

“Gaaaah!”

The tears of the Giant King were unlike those of any other creature.

Not even the slightest sympathy could be felt—as his nature was growing increasingly savage.

“More…”

Ymir hunched over.

“Mooooore!”

When the two collided again, every hit sparked blinding flashes before their eyes.

“Grrrk!”

Rian too, began surrendering himself to instinct as his consciousness blurred.

“Aaaaaah!”

Ymir struck as Rian’s great sword hit his ribs.

“Huff!”

Ymir held firm, and Rian twisted his head back into place with divine transcendence.

His face was—

“Grrrr!”

A demon, nothing less.

Ymir felt a chilling shiver… and ecstasy.

“Kuhuhu.”

An epiphany?

“Bullshit.”

This is just fighting. That’s all we’re doing.

‘Victory? What do you even get from beating someone? Wealth? Power? Fame?’

You didn’t need to grow this strong for garbage like that, you idiot!

The moment his punch slammed into the great sword, a shockwave blasted out in all directions.

“Yaaaaah!”

As Ymir’s blows landed, for the first time since the fight began, Rian began to be pushed back.

‘Strong.’

Even in his haze, one word floated in Rian’s mind—Ymir was strong.

And in a voice he didn’t even realize was his own…

‘It’s okay.’

The voice of a Yaksha, perhaps muddy and monstrous, echoed in his mind.

‘I’m stronger.’

Divine Transcendence — Asura Balbalta.

As Ymir’s punch struck, the afterimages of Asura multiplied into even greater numbers.

“Hehehe.”

It was terrifying.

And in the next moment, a monstrous flurry of blows blew away even that fear.

Ymir’s vision went black.

‘Fun.’

This was what all his training had been for. And what remained between them now was…

An overwhelming sense of respect for an equal who had reached the same height…

And…

‘I’m stronger.’

A base, animal desire to trample that incredible opponent into the dirt.

‘We’re the same.’

Nothing but beasts.

Ymir thought that this feeling he had now—maybe this was what people had always been talking about.

Orgasm.

“Ooooooh?”

As Rian’s great sword struck him again and again from every angle, something began to build up.

“Oooooh… oooooooh!”

Something began to boil up from deep inside, promising an explosion at any moment.

Divine Transcendence!

As Rian spun, he smashed Ymir’s bent right arm with his great sword.

Crack, crackle.

The blade crushed Ymir’s arm and caved in half his torso, sending him flying.

And Ymir—

“GAAAAAAH!”

Let out a scream of joy for the second time as he was hurled hundreds of meters and slammed into the earth.

From a distance, Tess cried out.

“That’s it! Rian won!”

It was a massive blessing for humanity—but no one around said a word.

Because they, too, were swordsmen.

“…Is it real?”

Feeling the disbelief in the air, Rai watched Rian.

‘What does it feel like?’

To be the best at something—

Clump spoke.

“Rai, everyone has their own life. Don’t make yourself miserable.”

“Miserable, huh.”

Well, if anyone could become a great swordmaster, then being a great swordmaster wouldn't mean anything at all.

“Why is that, Grandpa?”

Rai asked.

“Why couldn’t I even dream of it? Why was it Rian and not me?”

Luck, effort, talent, limits?

“Hmm.”

Clump chose his words carefully, since Rai was a precious grandson to him.

But no matter how much he thought about it, the only conclusion that remained was...

“Love.”

Rai turned around in disbelief.

“Huh?”

“You don’t love the sword.”

“More precisely, you don’t like fighting. Sure, you’ve had your moments of joy, but now you feel worn down. Why? Because of your limits? Because training is exhausting? Because luck didn’t follow you? No.”

Clump continued.

“You’re just… tired of it. Of the sword.”

That was all.

“You’ve grown dull to it. Your current position, your current fame, the attention you get… That’s because you craved status, fame, and people’s attention. The sword was never your end goal—it was just a means to achieve something else.”

Clump pointed at Rian.

“Look at him.”

Even while gasping for breath, he hadn’t taken his eyes off the spot where Ymir had crashed.

“Talent? Effort?”

Clump smirked.

“He’s got the look of someone who’s madly in love with fighting.”

That’s what it meant to be a Yaksha.

Rai turned his head with an unsettled heart, after listening to Clump to the end.

“…Huh?”

And at that moment, ominous heat haze began to rise from the place where Ymir had crashed.

“GRAAAAAH!”

Ymir’s monstrous roar pierced the air and stabbed at their eardrums.

“Ugh!”

It was a kilometer away.

But the visual impact far outweighed the pain in their ears.

“W-What is that?”

Ymir’s body was rapidly undergoing cellular proliferation, morphing into a grotesque form.

It must have had some color, but it only appeared pitch black—as if it absorbed light itself.

“…The end, maybe.”

That’s what it felt like.

Ymir, who continued to grow endlessly, looked down at Rian as he trembled with excitement at his new level.

‘Thank you.’

Reaching a limit for the first time in his life had taught him why humans strive to grow stronger.

‘Because they can.’

At last, having found what felt like a true life, Ymir stopped growing near the skyline.

“Fwoooooo…”

In the next moment, his body rapidly compressed into an Idea-like form.

“Here I come!”

His only untouched right arm launched down toward Rian on the ground.

“Grrrrrr!”

Within the massive shadow cast over the ground, Rian raised his sword with the stance of a demon.

Their eyes locked.

The Buddha of the Battlefield.

Ymir acknowledged Rian.

‘Enlightenment attained through countless battles. Yeah, that really is something.’

However—

‘But has Ymir experienced it?’

Ymir's strength, having surpassed all limits, was unprecedented—even for the Buddha of the battlefield.

“I’m going to win.”

BOOOOOOOOM!

As Ymir’s massive fist struck down toward Rian, the ground shattered in rippling waves.

Rumble rumble rumble!

An earthquake struck the city of Jaive, and citizens rushed outside with whatever they could carry.

“Ahhh! Save us!”

But they barely moved a few meters before freezing in place.

Thud. Thud.

Giants the size of mountains stepped over the walls of the city and entered.

“Wha…”

Someone tried to speak up—

THUD.

In one stomp, dozens of people were crushed like ants, followed by screams.

“Aaaaah! Help!”

Under the giant’s legs, Girshin—still a somewhat human size—drew his sword.

“Wipe them all out.”

The contract Ymir had made with Luber was theirs to fulfill, and the giants destroyed the city in an instant.

“Gah!”

They crushed people underfoot, tore buildings from their foundations like toys, and hurled them into crowds.

“Eek!”

As a house came flying toward them, the people froze in terror—

Hand of God.

A hand made of light caught the building and hurled it back at the giant.

Thwack!

The giant took a direct hit to the forehead and finally glanced around the ground.

Shirone stood there with the southern warriors.

“It’s Yahweh!”

Recognizing his face, the citizens cried out and rushed toward him.

“Outta the way! I saw him first!”

Their panic was understandable, but in this situation, there was no way to properly protect the civilians.

“Back off!”

The southern warriors shouted.

“Retreat slowly! Just because you’re here doesn’t mean you’ll survive! You have to run!”

“Shut up! Who even are you?!”

How do you escape from a giant who walks dozens of meters in a single step?

More than anything, Shirone was still deflecting all of the giant’s thrown attacks.

“Yahweh…”

Girshin scowled.

Ymir might have said it wasn’t fun anymore, but Girshin didn’t care for fun.

“I’ll kill you.”

As the only 7th-tier giant began to grow, his body expanded without limit.

The screams of the citizens fell silent.

What they witnessed was a creature so large it defied imagination.

WHOOOOOOOM.

Even the mere movement of its legs stirred up windstorms—an unreal spectacle.

“Sir Five Star!”

A familiar voice called out.

“Huh?”

Shirone quickly turned, but saw nothing but scenery.

“Over here! Over here!”

When he looked down again, Shirone’s eyes lit up.

“Mini!”

Mini, tiny like a squirrel, came running, waving a hand energetically.

And in the next instant, she passed Shirone—her body beginning to grow rapidly.

“I’ll take care of it.”

Resident of the Ivory Tower, Two-Star Ranked: Twelve Mini.

Nickname: The Super Giant.
