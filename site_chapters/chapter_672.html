Chapter 672:

Existence and non-existence.

Like the front and back of a coin, they are defined as opposites, but Pakhji-sense perceives them only as a single coin.

As the world of Minor World Possession faded, the Lord of Terror, Igor, reappeared.

“How are you still alive?”

The sight of countless Igors, each filling the horizon and speaking in unison, was truly unsettling.

But Shirone was no longer focused on Igor—his eyes were on Ra Enemy, the one who had orchestrated the entire situation.

‘What are you thinking…?’

If even Igor, a division commander of the Under World, was within his calculations, then Ra Enemy’s ambition was far greater than expected.

“Can I escape again this time?”

The blue-flamed spears once again filled the space and surged toward him, but Shirone no longer avoided them.

‘So this is what it was.’

Ever since the first Igor drove his spear into the ground, the coin had already been flipped.

‘Then I’ll flip it back.’

The spears pierced Shirone’s face, torso, and limbs—but like the moon’s reflection on water, ripples spread, and his body instantly restored itself.

‘This is inside my mind.’

He had expected it, but actually experiencing it through perception was a different matter entirely.

With the Seventh Sense, the form of Igor became vivid—like grasping an object with one’s hand.

“Weak being!”

Igor hurled his spears in a frenzy, but none could reach Shirone anymore.

The spirit of Golden Resolve clearly belonged to the realm of non-existence.

However, Shirone could control his mind as if it were his body, and the expanded range of his Spirit Zone was unparalleled.

“Begone.”

When Shirone extended his hand, a Halo was born. A multicolored flash flew like a dagger and injected 100% information in an instant.

“GRAAAAAAH!”

Overwhelmed by the perfect presence of the circle, Igor let out a scream as if being torn apart, and a Photon Cannon, enhanced by a magic amplifier, hummed as it pierced through Ataraxia.

As the world turned pure white, Igor’s screams faded into the distance.

Ahhhhhh...

As the space they occupied shrank, it was drawn endlessly into Shirone’s mind, and the events unfolding in his retina within the Under World also disappeared.

“……”

Shirone blinked and, as if checking the battlefield he had just fought on, looked down at his chest before raising his head.

The blue-flamed spear was still embedded in the ground, and Igor sat silently on his horse.

“Pull out your spear. You should remember—you too can perish here.”

“Karr de Suma (I am the only one).”

Igor slowly grasped the spear with his right hand.

“Anke Ra.”

“He ordered you to instill fear in me, didn’t he? To take control of my past.”

Goffin had said clearly that Hexa had no cause.

Then why was Shirone remembering a past he had never lived?

Igor said:

“Ra Enemy is merely an event that exists in the past. But that event is dangerous enough to collapse the boundaries of the Under World. We are companions of space, but also enemies who must never meet. If you disappear, so too will Ra Enemy.”

That was why Igor had personally come to eliminate Shirone.

“That could be true. But if the domain of existence falls under his control, this world won’t be safe either, will it?”

“...You're right.”

“Release my companions. I’m not giving up.”

Igor studied Shirone’s face closely.

“Can Ra truly be stopped?”

“This isn’t about whether it’s possible.”

“Ra Enemy will eventually tear down the world’s boundaries. Do you think you can handle that?”

The countless non-existent entities that could previously only interfere would become real and wander the world.

‘Even just looking at what’s happening now...’

Even the trained mercenaries were being forced to relive their past traumas.

If the boundaries collapsed and Igor was unleashed into the world, it wouldn’t matter where it was—hell would unfold in a single day.

“You already know there’s no avoiding it. If we don’t stop it, everything ends.”

After a long pause, Igor reached into his robe and drew out a small blue flame, softly glowing.

“To the one who has conquered fear—receive the mark.”

When Igor extended his hand, the blue flame flew like a flower seed and landed on Shirone’s palm.

Perceived through the Seventh Sense, the blue flame took the form of a necklace in the realm of existence.

“Where there is fear, I shall be.”

The Necklace of the Lord of Terror, Igor.

As Shirone placed it around his neck through Pakhji-sense, Armand immediately sent a signal.

—New brainwave detected. Paralysis effect confirmed for fear-related mental activity.

It forcibly suppressed fear.

For Shirone—who had almost had his past dominated by Ra Enemy—it was an essential item.

“This will define your place and mine. Come find me when the time comes.”

Turning his dead horse, Igor vanished through a vertically split rift in space, just as he had arrived.

With Igor’s power lifted, the mercenaries freed from their nightmares groaned and collapsed unconscious.

In contrast, the core members quickly understood the situation with Igor’s disappearance.

“Did we… defeat him?”

“Well, about that…”

Just as Shirone was about to answer, Shagal’s voice rang from outside.

“DIEEEE!”

The deafening clash of steel-on-steel echoed with terrifying speed, and everyone rushed out of the structure.

“What is that?”

Outside, free from their traumatic visions, Kuan and Shagal were locked in a fierce battle, each displaying extraordinary martial prowess.

“It’s Shagal.”

At Lupist’s words, Etella’s eyes sharpened with fury.

‘He’s the one who… my master…’

No one could deny his skill.

He stood his ground even against Kuan, one of the strongest members of Heaven. His juggling of dozens of daggers was nothing short of overwhelming.

‘He’s the one who…!’

But even so, it didn’t justify the murder of the Archbishop of Karsis.

“I’ll take him!”

Without waiting for permission, Etella launched herself forward.

Had she lacked the skill, she would’ve lost her head the moment she entered between the two fighters—but she slipped into the narrow opening between them with incredible precision, far beyond normal limits.

‘I won’t kill you…’

Grinding her teeth, Etella unleashed her Yin-Yang Pulse Fist.

‘But you will answer for what you’ve done!’

Sensing her killing intent from the flank, Shagal turned his gaze.

But his hands moved faster than his eyes—three daggers crossed like fangs and stabbed into her forearm.

In that fleeting moment, seven dagger shells embedded into her arm, but no blood flowed back—Etella pushed in even deeper and drove her fist forward.

Resonance Strike.

Shagal widened his eyes and twisted his body backward, narrowly dodging the blow.

As the pulse reverberated through the air and struck his abdomen, his lungs compressed and his eyes flew open.

“Urgh!”

To avoid the follow-up barrage from Kuan, he had no choice but to swallow his pride and retreat ten meters—dozens of daggers he had retracted followed him.

‘What the hell? Suddenly—?’

He didn’t feel it was cowardly—but being interrupted just when the blood was boiling again after Igor’s disappearance left him irritated.

“I’ll kill you first—!”

Shagal reached out and snatched a dagger. The moment he turned toward Etella—

His face turned ghostly pale, as if his soul had left his body.

Because this was the first time he had actually seen her face during the battle.

And except for the glasses she wore—

She looked exactly like Tia.

Shagal’s expression was so disturbing that even Kuan lowered his sword and turned to Etella.

“You knew each other?”

“No.”

Etella firmly shook her head—but Shagal couldn’t dismiss it, not even for a second.

“Tia… Why are you…?”

It wasn’t just a matter of resemblance.

Even if a hundred people stood before him looking exactly like her, Shagal would pick out the real one without hesitation.

His heart couldn’t deny what it saw—even if his eyes did.

‘No, it can’t be. She’s definitely dead.’

Hadn’t he held her cold corpse in his arms, weeping?

Hadn’t he felt it with every one of his five senses?

Sniff sniff… sniff sniff…

He tried to take in the scent to confirm the memory—but in the Jarred Brain trap, his sense of smell was blocked.

‘Now that I think of it… Tia…’

What did she even look like?

His head spun. Dizziness overtook him.

“What’s wrong with him?”

Kuan muttered as Shagal staggered alone—and at that moment, Etella lunged again.

‘I won’t let this go!’

He had to be caught and forced to explain why he killed Raphael.

“Surrender peacefully, murderer!”

“Uaaahhhhhh!”

As Shagal swung dozens of daggers with his right hand while covering his face with his left, Etella froze mid-motion.

It felt as if she were trapped within a vibration.

None of the blades flew toward her, and doing nothing was the only way to avoid being struck.

Feeling as though she were sealed within a mold of death, Etella swallowed hard.

Shagal denied everything.

‘No! This can’t be happening!’

—Forgive me.

Raiden’s voice echoed in his mind.

“Uaaaaah!”

Etella tried to pursue Shagal as he twisted away to escape, but he wasn’t someone she could catch after giving up even a moment’s opening.

“Damn it…!”

Etella clenched her teeth in frustration as Lupist approached.

“Let him go for now. Whatever’s happening seems serious—and we’ve got bigger problems.”

Igor was gone, but with everyone still trapped in the Jarred Brain, continuing the mission was impossible.

“I can deactivate the Jarred Brain.”

At Shirone’s words, Lupist turned to him.

“How?”

“We just need to remove the trap.”

Using Pakhji-sense, Shirone gazed beyond the realm of existence.

“It’s over there.”

He led the group to a serpent statue wedged between two structures.

The two-meter-high snake, with its hood fully spread like a king cobra, appeared as a man’s corpse through Pakhji-sense.

‘I’m sorry.’

Using Elysion, Shirone cast a miniature explosion inside the skull of the man, and with a muffled pop, the face trembled.

To the others, however, it simply looked like the cobra’s head fell off with a clean snap.

“Oh?”

Gradually, the familiar landscape returned.

The pulsating, fleshy structures turned back into solid stone, and the bright morning sun rose in the sky.

Even so, Shirone could still feel something else.

‘It was this complicated all along…’

Countless spiritual entities—undetectable by human senses—were now vividly perceived through Pakhji-sense.

‘If I were to reduce this vivid sensation to a faint level… would that be what people call intuition?’

Shirone turned to Lupist.

“Let’s wake the others. We were caught off guard this time, but it won’t happen again so easily.”

Jane asked, “What exactly happened?”

Explaining it all would take more than a day, so Shirone chose a simpler method.

In the realm of existence, the necklace had become a physical object whose weight pressed slightly against the back of his neck.

‘Ah, so this is how it’s used.’

When Shirone raised his hand into the air, Igor’s necklace burst into a blue flame.

Everyone watched in stunned silence as the air around his palm burned away, scattering like ashes.

“You… opened Pakhji?”

Because they understood what Meirei’s words meant, the onlookers were at a loss for how to react.

‘Come to think of it… even the prayer feels different now.’

As Lupist frowned in deep thought, a rough voice echoed from the direction of the Under World.

“Raktas Sagar Vero Dem (Do you need fear?)”

“No way…”

The ground trembled as Igor, the Lord of Terror, reappeared, leading his dead horse.

As he approached Shirone and turned his head toward the group, Rian pointed with wide eyes.

“The Lord of Terror?”

Behind Shirone stood Igor, dragging his blue-flame spear, casting a strange contrast against the morning sunlight.

Arian Shirone — Karr value: 90.2%.
