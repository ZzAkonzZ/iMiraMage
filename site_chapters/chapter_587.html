Chapter 587:

Eden felt fear for the first time as the immense mass of the shockwave converged on a single point.

'Fear? Me?'

It was an emotion she couldn't accept, being invincible under God's protection.

'This can't be!'

Eden resisted with all her might.

For humans, even a planet is vast, and they might never experience such a terrifying phenomenon in their lifetime. She desperately clung to her faith.

"Huuuuh!"

Tears flowed because she couldn't overcome the fear.

If God existed beyond human reach, then Shirone's Quasar was also a god.

'No, no!'

God had to reside far above that.

'Is this all it was?'

The faith she believed encompassed the universe was ultimately just the arrogance of a creature that hadn't experienced its limits.

"No! My faith, my belief…!"

As the number of flashes traveling through the 4D path increased, Quasar's energy soared exponentially.

"Aaaargh!"

Finally, a scream burst from Eden's mouth.

Though Absolute Barrier still protected her, she couldn't help but feel shaken.

'Give up, Eden!'

If she couldn't accept Gaold's suffering, then her God couldn't transcend humanity.

'It's not absolute! That's not God, Eden!'

It was crumbling.

She felt cracks forming in Absolute Barrier.

'This can't be happening!'

It also meant her faith was collapsing.

"God…!"

Tears streaming down, Eden prayed.

"I believe!"

Trapped in light, unable to move, she clasped her hands and knelt, and the barrier's durability soared to its highest level.

'Amazing. Truly amazing.'

How strong could the human heart become?

"Stay with me! Watch over me forever!"

Eden wouldn't be destroyed.

God existed, and she believed in that.

At the extreme of human faith, Quasar relentlessly hammered the solid wall.

'Why are you angry? Why are you angry with me?'

She heard the sound of cracks forming in her mind.

'What did I do wrong? What is wrong?'

Crack, crack! As the barrier began to break, the shock reached her.

"Huff!"

Reality, which humans couldn't help but feel, was closing in.

'God! Why are you punishing me?'

The light of Quasar, over 100 meters in diameter, suddenly expanded like an explosion.

"I acknowledge the Servant's ability. But we won."

Hershey smirked.

"You might think you can reshuffle cards without wasting them by not accepting Kang, but you're actually losing out."

It was like throwing collected cards into the trash.

"It's not meaningless. Thanks to that, we secured a draw."

"That's your mistake."

Hershey pointed at Iruki.

"You should have taken a chance. You shouldn't have used all the scrambles on me. Because we have Eden."

If they couldn't retire Eden, a draw was meaningless.

"Giving credit to your teammates? That's emotional, Iruki."

"So, what are you trying to say?"

"First."

Hershey raised a finger.

"You failed to accurately assess your opponent's abilities. That was your mistake."

"Iruki, look!"

Dorothy pointed at the sky in shock.

Iruki, seeing it, and Hershey, who turned her head late, both widened their eyes.

"What is that?"

Would it feel like this to see the sun up close?

Thousands of light sources flickered, rapidly growing to fill the night sky.

It was the birth of Quasar, seen from afar.

"Aaaargh!"

Even as Eden's throat-tearing scream echoed, Shirone could only watch.

'This is bad! I can't stop it!'

The reason flashes were coming from all directions was that space-time had completely folded around the coordinates.

The amplification power might not differ much from 2D Ataraxia, but the energy focusing on a single point was on a different level from existing magic.

'She'll die at this rate.'

As he realized this, Eden's barrier began to tear apart.

"Huff!"

Eden's eyes widened, and her body arched like a bow.

Like a newborn baby, the shock of reality vividly transmitted through her skin.

'Dark Sphere!'

Shirone slammed the Dark Sphere, amplified by Ataraxia, into Quasar.

There was nothing else he could do.

As the light energy was absorbed by the darkness, Quasar's brilliance visibly dimmed.

'Please, please!'

Even so, Eden's survival was uncertain.

Finally, Quasar dissipated, and Shirone saw Eden falling from the sky.

Teleporting, he barely caught her and checked if she was still breathing.

"Phew."

Her chest was faintly rising and falling.

"Put me down."

Eden's voice was weak.

Maintaining consciousness showed incredible mental strength, but her body couldn't move a finger.

"It's dangerous like this. Let's get you treated."

"Put me down."

She tried to infuse her words with emotion, but even that felt weak.

Laying Eden on the grass, Shirone stepped back slowly.

"Why…"

A tear rolled down her cheek.

It wasn't just about losing the battle; her lifelong faith had been shattered, making it hard to speak.

"Why did I lose?"

"Eden…"

Cutting off Shirone, she cried out in frustration.

"This can't be! Why am I inferior to Gaold? I've never defied the will of Yor! Gaold abandoned God!"

"Yes."

Shirone said.

"But it seems God didn't abandon Gaold."

Eden's eyes shook with shock.

"Eden, I don't know if God exists. But the moment you tried to prove God through Gaold, the God you believe in couldn't be God."

Shirone approached her.

"Faith isn't like that. Being a great Yor and spreading love is your faith."

'God…'

Eden's eyes burned with realization.

She finally understood why God had been so angry with her.

'Forgive me for doubting you.'

After a prayer of repentance, she slowly opened her eyes.

"Take me."

"Huh?"

"The master card. It's not here. It needs to be destroyed, right?"

"Oh, right. Where is it?"

"You can't go alone. I'll guide you."

"That's a bit…"

Only after the battle did he realize she wasn't wearing clothes.

"Can't we go after you get dressed?"

"Are you teasing me? My clothes are there, and I can't move my fingers."

"Then maybe my clothes…"

As Shirone started to take off his shirt, Eden stopped him.

"Let's just go. That would be weirder."

Realizing his mistake, Shirone quickly put his shirt back on and picked her up.

"Where to?"

Avoiding eye contact, Shirone asked, and she smiled weakly.

"West. Near Training Ground 19."

"Huff! Huff!"

Amy walked wearily.

Dragging Kaiden, whose ribs were broken, she pulled him by the collar.

The mountain was burning, and if left there, he might suffocate.

After leaving the fire zone, Amy irritably dropped Kaiden to the ground.

"Ugh! The more I think about it, the angrier I get! You're really an unlucky guy."

It was her honest feeling but also a compliment to her opponent.

"I've heard that a lot. But I'd rather not hear it from a Karmis."

Panting, Amy sat down.

"Yeah, what's the point? Neither of us is in a position to envy others."

"Why didn't you kill me?"

"What?"

"Why did you bring me here? Don't you want to kill me? You hate me."

Amy pouted.

"Why did I save you?"

There was only one answer she could give.

"Because it could have been me."

Kaiden turned his head.

"I fought fiercely at the magic school. I defeated many competitors to get here. You probably did too."

"……"

"No matter how much I hated them, when I won, I didn't feel the joy of victory. Just relief that I survived."

Amy looked down at her feet.

"Everyone tries their best, so it's scary. Today, I stepped over you, but it could have been me lying here."

Hiding her sadness, Amy smiled.

"That's probably why. People who risk their lives for something can't be hated, no matter how much you dislike them."

Kaiden looked at the stars in the night sky.

"I can't understand the mindset of mages."

"…I've been meaning to ask, why did you come to the magic school?"

"Not much. I just came. I wasn't worried. I'd do well here too."

"Want another punch?"

"Until I met Maya, I planned to stay briefly and leave. But now it's different. It's comfortable."

Kaiden looked at Amy.

"Mages don't pity. Like you."

As Amy fell silent, his gaze returned to the sky.

"They see things as they are. Whether it's the Red Cross of sacrifice or anything else, they call it unlucky if it's unlucky. It's different from sword schools."

"It might be presumptuous, but have you thought about rejecting your destiny?"

"That's really presumptuous."

"I know! I'm just asking! A loser with a big mouth!"

Kaiden opened up.

"There's no way to reject the destiny of the Red Cross. But I could live ignoring it. Maybe I'll try swordsmanship again."

"Because I beat you?"

"That's part of it."

If Kaiden resumed swordsmanship, he would quickly become the best.

For one reason: he didn't want to be the best in swordsmanship.

"I should forget Maya too. I can't achieve anything I want. That's my life."

"Can you ignore it?"

"Being the best at something might not be a bad life."

Thinking of something amusing, Kaiden smiled.

"In that sense, making a knight's vow to you would be perfect. I could protect a woman I don't like and use swordsmanship I don't enjoy."

"Haha! Just imagining it is truly awful!"

Even Kaiden couldn't help but laugh despite his pain.

'A knight's vow…'

Amy thought of Rian.

Compared to Kaiden, Rian was the complete opposite as a swordsman, but remembering his spirit made her somewhat envious of Shirone.

"I know you dream of being an artist, but I wish you'd become a swordsman."

"Sending off a competitor, huh?"

"Don't be ridiculous! You can't beat me!"

Amy's outburst quickly turned calm again.

"I don't know much, but… there might be a big war coming."

That's what Shirone had told her.

"Wars always happen."

"I suddenly thought, if destiny really exists, there must be a reason you were born with the Red Cross Star."

The price of not achieving what you desperately want is the greatest talent.

"Sacrifice, huh."

Maya's face suddenly came to mind.

"If a war happens and I can help, then I'll be protecting her too."

Maybe that would be the closest he could be to her.

"…But I won't make a knight's vow."

"Hoho! Who would accept it anyway? Stick a sword in front of me, and I'll kick your butt in front of everyone!"

"Anytime. That won't happen."

They were both confident in their declarations.
