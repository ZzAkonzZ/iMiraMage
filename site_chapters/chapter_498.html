Chapter 498: Despair (1)

Life is pain.

That's what Gaold said.

"Sir."

It was when Gaold had returned to Tormia and was struggling to raise the ranks of the association.

Sixteen-year-old Kangnan, who was walking in the association's garden, picked a red flower growing by the railing and showed it to Gaold.

"Isn't this pretty?"

It was a beautiful flower that couldn't be found in the desert, where not a single blade of grass grew, or even in her southern homeland.

Gaold looked at the flower with indifferent eyes.

His magical skills had reached the top tier for his age, but politics required another kind of talent.

Growing weary of the internal power struggles within the association, he had become indifferent to everything.

Kangnan, understanding Gaold, didn't wait for an answer and held the flower petals up to his face.

"The best thing about Tormia is the many beautiful flowers. What's this flower called?"

"Pain."

Kangnan looked up at Gaold.

"Pain? Is that the flower's name?"

"I don't know its name. But whatever it's called, it's pain."

Gaold stepped closer and looked down at Kangnan.

He had become irritable lately, but today his expression was colder and more frightening than ever.

"But this flower is beautiful."

Gaold snatched the flower from Kangnan's hand.

"Kangnan, all living beings can only feel the world through pain. Pleasure is just another way of accepting pain."

"Sir, you seem very tired lately. Maybe you should rest a bit."

Kangnan expressed her concern indirectly.

She knew better than anyone how much pain Gaold had to endure to live in this world.

But she didn't want to see him like this, as he had never shown his suffering before.

Gaold cast a spell, sharply cutting the end of the flower stem.

"Flowers are beautiful because they pass on their lineage, but they never know their mate or see their offspring. Kangnan, life is pain. Our senses and the nerves under our skin are just functions created to accept life's pain."

"Life is… pain."

It wasn't a comforting thought, but Kangnan thought Gaold might be right.

To varying degrees, the world each being perceives is filled with pain.

Pain Amplification 100 Million Times - Air Press.

Poof!

All the fallen angels surrounding Gaold vanished, leaving only a few drops of liquid behind, as if they had evaporated.

It felt as if the sky had collided with the earth.

Among those within Gaold's radius, the only one still standing was the archangel Uriel.

Uriel's halo expanded to a diameter of 100 meters, spinning terrifyingly.

Though he didn't appear shaken, the rapid acceleration of his mind showed Gaold's immense power.

'Is this truly human power?'

If one were to name a human who had transcended humanity, Uriel would only think of Miro, but Gaold's extreme endurance had already surpassed human limits.

"Raaaargh!"

But this wasn't something Gaold had always possessed.

The aftermath of 20 years of hatred exploding at once was immense.

All he felt was pain.

With his eyes rolled back and his body trembling, Gaold screamed, losing his sanity, and eventually, his lower jaw dislocated, leaving him to scream through his throat alone.

"Raaaargh! Raaaargh!"

Gaold ran madly.

Almost instinctively, he dashed towards Kangnan.

Wham!

As the second Air Press unfolded, the fallen angels and Maras vanished simultaneously.

"Raaaargh! Raaaargh!"

Life is pain.

The 100 million times amplified pain Gaold felt conveyed all the world's information as agony.

The shock was too great for a human made of flesh and blood to bear, and blood tears flowed from Gaold's eyes as he looked up at the sky.

It was as if his soul, trapped in his body, was desperate to escape the miserable prison of his flesh.

Thus, Gaold struggled within the confines of the pain bestowed upon living beings.

Kingdom of Tormia.

Isabel, a certified 3rd-rank archmage and former librarian of the Magic Association, was hospitalized in the association's exclusive hospital, 1 kilometer from the capital Baschka's Magic Association.

A recognized scholar and mage, she had been caught in the association's political schemes and tortured for 48 hours for supporting Gaold.

The torturer, Sakiri, had not used the extrajudicial "Scales of Truth" as promised.

But his hands were so cruel that they felt beyond emotion, almost malicious, and Isabel endured every second of agony.

As a result, Isabel held out for 48 hours, buying time for Gaold's team to reach heaven.

She had been unconscious for over a month, relying on life support, and woke up only yesterday morning.

Though the healing mages and surgeons asked her many questions, she hadn't spoken a word until today.

She just blinked indifferently, staring at the white ceiling.

Knock, knock.

Even when someone knocked on the door, her gaze didn't shift.

But when she heard the man's voice entering the room, her pupils trembled, and she slowly turned her head.

"Are you alright? I heard you woke up early yesterday morning."

Sakiri, the 5th-level mage who had tortured Isabel for 48 hours, entered with a calm expression.

As the memories resurfaced, Isabel's heart rate skyrocketed.

But soon, her trembling pupils steadied, and her expression began to stabilize.

He was a man she never wanted to see again.

But Isabel didn't stop Sakiri from approaching.

What she had sought, at the cost of her body, was an answer to her long-standing question about Gaold.

"What happened?"

Sakiri pulled a chair next to Isabel's bed and slowly sat down, asking again.

"What do you mean?"

"Did Gaold… go to heaven?"

"He did. But he hasn't returned yet. He may never come back."

Sakiri sighed deeply, looking at the contemplative Isabel, and continued.

"The association sent Team Cage B, but they also lost contact. The situation is complicated."

Sakiri's words didn't reach Isabel.

What she truly wanted to know wasn't that.

"…Did he transcend?"

Sakiri didn't answer.

"Gaold…"

Clenching her right hand, she trembled with shame and despair.

In the agony of torture, Isabel had cursed her existence as a living being thousands of times.

She had been broken.

It felt like endlessly falling towards something less than human.

If she could stop the pain, she would do anything vile, shameful, dirty, or monstrous.

That was the fate of living beings.

The karma of humans, bound to pain.

'But how… Gaold, how did you…'

Isabel raised her clenched fist to cover her eyes. Tears streamed down her cheeks.

"Gaold…"

Isabel cried out.

"Did he truly transcend! Did he transcend pain, the human condition, the fate of living beings!"

If life is pain, then birth is a curse.

But if it were Gaold, perhaps he could overcome that curse and become something truly free from pain.

If so, Isabel could endure.

She could overcome her ingrained hatred for humanity and live again.

Sakiri, drawing on his experience as a torturer of many, answered honestly.

"Who knows. You can forget pain. But transcending it is impossible for living beings."

Turning to the window, he looked far beyond the sky and spoke.

"But if anyone could, it would be Gaold."

Poof!

With the third Air Press, the number of fallen angels and Maras was halved.

Now the enemies realized they would be annihilated upon approach.

"Raaaargh!"

Gaold, now a mere embodiment of pain, had forgotten even Kangnan's existence and rampaged wildly.

With his lower jaw dislocated and his arms flailing, blood tears streaming, Gaold looked like a demon.

"Sir…! Sir…!"

Like the girl who had sent Gaold to hell ten years ago, Kangnan was terrified.

Life is not pain.

Gaold's true face wasn't like that.

"Then why do we exist?"

As Gaold walked to the flower bed, posing another question, Kangnan set aside thoughts of pain and refocused.

"It's strange. Humans experience pain from birth to death. Does that mean life is a cursed bastard of the universe?"

While Kangnan pondered, Gaold dug up the soil and replanted the flower.

"Can it survive without roots?"

"Maybe it will take root again. Though it will be a painful process."

Gaold looked back at Kangnan, who had approached the flower bed.

"But Kangnan, that's what life is. Because it was born, it blooms even in pain."

Gaold smiled warmly at the struggling flower, then turned to Kangnan.

"That's why life is the most beautiful flower in the world. Like this flower, our lives are beautiful."

For the first time, Kangnan saw it.

Gaold's face, smiling so kindly and beautifully, was his true self before he became a madman.

"Raaaargh! Raaaargh!"

Gaold screamed.

With his tongue hanging out and his face twisted like a demon, he seemed to embody only the emotion of pain.

Watching Gaold writhe in agony, Kangnan closed her eyes and cried.

"Ugh…! Aaaaah…!"

Gaold, who had to live with a self-replicating mutation, perhaps a curse from God.

But he had said life was beautiful.

The man she loved most in the world, the most beautiful man, was falling apart.

'Release! Please release!'

Kangnan struggled to free herself from .

"Release! I said release!"

She didn't care if her arms broke as she struggled, but the device absorbed her strength, preventing any impact.

'I can't let him go like this!'

Even if her body shattered, she had to get down. She had to jump off the wall and reach Gaold.

"Raaaargh! Raaaargh!"

Every time Gaold screamed in pain, Kangnan's heart tore into a thousand pieces.

"Sir! I'm coming! Just wait a little longer!"

Kangnan cried and struggled even more, but showed no sign of opening.

In the helplessness that her entire life was collapsing, she hung from the cuffs, letting out a miserable scream.

"Aaaah! Aaaah!"

Watching the person she loved suffer was an infinite pain that words like "pain" couldn't describe.

Kangnan finally understood what Zulu had meant.

"Raaaargh! Raaaargh!"

The human heart is a hell.
