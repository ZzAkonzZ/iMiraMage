Chapter 762: Lord of Ruin (1)

As she flew toward the entrance of the presidential palace, Minerva looked back at Shirone.

“I win, don’t I?”

Jet had arrived first, but Shirone was already fully focused on the battle.

“I’ll take the lead.”

“Suit yourself. But getting in won’t be easy.”

Without answering, Shirone flared his wings of light and flew toward the far end of the palace, while Minerva landed at the entrance.

The moment she pulled the jet from where it had embedded diagonally, a dreadful venom spread from her body.

“Alright, let’s start cleaning up.”

Piercing through the poisonous mist, demons over two meters tall, brimming with muscle, surrounded her.

“We’ll devour you alive!”

Even with their flesh rotting, the demons showed no fear. That’s what made this army of eight million the strongest of its kind.

‘Honestly, I’m a little nervous.’

A stiff smile crossed Minerva’s lips.

The palace grounds, saturated with the dictator’s greed, were among the largest in the world.

Ignoring the teeming demons on the ground, Shirone flew straight toward the leader’s chambers.

‘I’ll have to cut off the head.’

Eight million.

Easy enough to say aloud, but how many humans could truly imagine the scale of an eight-million-strong army?

And the ones gathered here weren’t even human—they were demons, far more powerful than any human average.

‘Maybe this was never a winnable war to begin with...’

That thought crossed his mind for a moment.

Satan’s army numbered two billion. Even if each demon killed just two humans, humanity would be annihilated.

It was terrifying to imagine what might have happened if Zion hadn’t shrunk the Spiritual Zone.

“What is that?”

From far across the sky, a sinister flame flickered in Shirone’s flight path.

“Grrrrrrraaaaaah.”

A face, like it had been chewed through by insects, pushed its way into reality as space tore open.

Captain Renault.

A demon responsible for transporting hell’s armies—he could carry up to four hundred demons inside his mouth.

“Yahweh...!”

As he saw the squirming demons packed inside Renault’s open jaws, Shirone’s face twisted.

‘Berserk!’

An explosive surge of divine light burst forth, piercing through the demon’s mouth and tearing the beings inside apart with shrieking cries.

“KYAAAAAA!”

A blinding flash. Blood and shredded flesh filled the air before bursting like fireworks.

Remaining forces: 7,999,231.

...It was endless.

He had disintegrated 400 demons in an instant, yet not a single one expressed grief or hesitation.

‘I’m sick of this...’

Yahweh loves all that exists and transcends good and evil...

But demons know no mercy.

‘Ataraxia!’

A massive halo bloomed, unleashing a torrent of prismatic information.

Shirone, who had already completed the incantation, aimed the spell circle at the ground and fired a Photon Cannon.

GROOOAAAAHHH!

The air howled as a beam of light dozens of meters wide struck diagonally downward, carving the earth as it went.

“GRAAAAAAAAH!”

The demons, stronger than humans by far, were flung in all directions—but they couldn’t escape Shirone’s scale.

273, 398, 113, 198... An average of 200 demons died per second.

By the time the beam’s amplification finished, the casualties exceeded 2,000.

Brigadier General Sodas looked up into the sky.

“So beautiful…”

Remaining forces: 7,996,973.

“This is driving me insane.”

Shirone grimaced.

It didn’t feel like they were shrinking. Feeling the overwhelming weight of sheer numbers, he began to worry about Minerva.

He looked back toward the palace entrance but saw only clusters of demons at various heights.

Some were more than ten meters tall.

‘Can I really do this?’

While Shirone was lost in thought, the 44th Brigade of the 7th Division in the 24th Corps aimed their gazes skyward.

“There’s Yahweh! Fire!”

Maw-faced demons with flat noses and no hair opened their jaws so wide they looked dislocated, launching streams of filth like mortars.

‘Annihilate the wicked!’

Yahweh’s light expanded in Spirit Zone form, turning the venomous filth into ash.

Shirone worried about Minerva.

‘Just once. Let me do this just once.’

As one who had reached the realm of Yahweh, the maximum number of clones Shirone could simultaneously sustain in a Quantum Superposition was 100,000.

‘It’s nowhere near enough, but...’

If every one of those 100,000 used the angel’s divine punishment, they might actually weaken the army.

‘Just a moment is all I need.’

The problem: Shirone’s main body couldn’t be protected while performing Quantum Superposition.

But Minerva wouldn’t last long like this either.

‘Quantum Superposition!’

100,000 overlays.

A sudden dizzying rush hit him, and Shirone’s body plummeted toward the ground at terrifying speed.

All the main body could do was cast a spell to soften the landing.

“Now!”

100,000 Shirones shot upward like flashes of light, each one invoking an Avatar of the Archangel.

At the same time, Brigadier Sodas lunged and grabbed Shirone’s main body.

“Got you!”

Incarnation Manifestation – Angel’s Judgment.

Remaining forces: 5,668,227.

Like a rainstorm of light, the attack washed over the palace, and every demon within lost their hearing.

Remaining forces: 4,340,976.

The sound was beyond deafening—it wasn’t even clear what it was. But everyone realized that a cataclysmic shock had hit the earth.

Remaining forces: 3,365,763.

“GRRAAAHHH!”

As the cycle continued, the number of casualties decreased—but the plaza was already left with crater-like bald patches.

“Guh!”

Sodas gripped Shirone’s neck with both hands, and all 100,000 clones ceased fire at once.

‘He aimed for the perfect opening.’

Shirone had sensed the approach through Spirit Zone—but not quickly enough to react.

‘He’s strong.’

And he was beautiful.

Too lovely to be a demon. His skin smelled sweet, like fruit.

“What are you waiting for? Keep going.”

Shirone furrowed his brow.

“...What?”

Sodas pressed his body tightly against Shirone’s, then moved a hand down to caress his chest.

“I’ll protect you. So go ahead. Kill them all.”

Shirone wasn’t sure what kind of trick this was—but Sodas’s eyes held no hesitation.

“No matter how many of the others die, the brigade commanders like me won’t go down. And those other guys... they’ll just be reborn anyway.”

As long as humans didn’t give up on demons, that was true.

“But they’re your soldiers.”

Tears rolled down from Sodas’s eyes.

“Honestly, I... I want to repent. I’m sick of living as a demon. Please... just this once, give me a chance...”

‘They always say that.’

But Igor had said this:

A demon who repents does exist.

“This’ll be the last time. If you can purify my wretched body with your light... maybe even I...”

Sodas’s eyes rolled back.

“This... this is Yahweh’s body...”

But while his left hand moved down gently, his right—the one on Shirone’s throat—suddenly surged with force.

‘Die!’

Just as he tried to snap Shirone’s neck—

“Huuugh!”

Yahweh’s light flared out, and the hand clutching Shirone’s throat began to crumble into ash.

“Ggghaaaaah!”

Quantum Superposition had already ended.

“Again...”

Sodas grimaced in pain as Shirone spoke, his eyes lowering with difficulty.

“You lied.”

Who would ever believe a twenty-time felon and con artist?

A demon that repents?

Parents believe.

If a child cries and promises, Mom, I won’t do it again, even if they’re a hundred-time felon, a parent has no choice but to believe.

That they’ll change, that this time it’s real...

So Shirone, while extinguishing the pain-ridden child of humanity—the demon—felt only sadness.

“No.”

With sorrowful eyes, Sodas looked down at Shirone and caressed his face with a hand turning to ash.

“I really did want to repent.”

“AAAAAAAH!”

As Yahweh’s light expanded, Sodas and every demon around him burned to cinders.

“Huff... Huff...”

While practicing the Breath of No-Mind to regain his composure, the enemies continued to pour in without end.

“Can you still manage?”

When Shirone turned around from his kneeling position, Minerva had already arrived behind him.

She, too, showed clear signs of exhaustion, and her clothes were shredded from being clawed at by demons.

With the hyperqualia senses that allowed him to perceive the mind like matter, Shirone swiftly calmed himself.

“I’m holding on. What about you, Minerva?”

“Me? Well… good enough.”

It wasn’t the time for a battle of pride.

“Cover me. If I cast Quantum Superposition again and sweep through the demons—”

“It’s no use. You still don’t get it?” Minerva raised her pipe and pointed it forward. “Yahweh’s light is a powerful weapon against demons, yes… but only because they’re non-corporeal. It hurts them, sure—but it’s just a matter of time. You kill them, and they keep coming back.”

“Then are we just supposed to let them go?”

“No.” Minerva exhaled a plume of green smoke.

“There’s a more constructive way.”

Shirone blocked the smoke from reaching him and used Angel’s Judgment on the surrounding enemies.

“What exactly are you planning?”

Remaining enemies: 3,363,305.

“I’m going to use a forbidden spell.”

Shirone was already growing anxious.

“If it’s forbidden, you’re supposed to not use it. What’s the point of calling it forbidden if you’re just going to throw it around?”

“The Integrated Cosmic Administration will cover for me. Even if you don’t believe me, you know the Great Star finds you terrifying. I’ll give you three veto tokens for wishes.”

“This isn’t something to bargain over.”

“Really?”

Minerva inhaled deeply.

“Then I guess I’ll take the punishment.”

Catastrophic Magic – Walking Dead.

Her cheeks puffed out as she swept her head side to side, breathing out a thick green smoke that rolled like clouds.

“You’re unbelievable—”

“I already did it,” she cut in.

Shirone immediately assessed the situation and looked around frantically.

“Grrrrraaah…”

Dozens of demons who had inhaled the smoke stood dazed, their pupils unfocused, before suddenly going berserk.

But their target wasn’t Minerva—it was the demons right next to them.

“What is this…?”

Minerva explained.

“A spell of civil slaughter: Walking Dead. Once infected, they feel only hatred toward their own kind. They lose consciousness, but unless their heads are destroyed, they won’t die.”

“Walking Dead…”

A name recorded in history books as one of the most horrifying disasters.

“Exactly. And the true terror of this masterpiece,” she added with a deadly smile, “is infection.”

Infected: 48.

“Grrraaaah! GRAAAAH!”

A demon bitten by another flipped his eyes back and bit a third nearby.

Infected: 127.

“Follow me. We’ll break into the leader’s palace through this.”

As demons began turning on each other, a widening corridor formed around Minerva as she advanced through the open plaza.

“You idiot! Why are you biting your own kind?!”

Infected: 897.

Shirone stared blankly at the witch’s back as she walked forward down a path that widened all on its own.

Mirac Minerva.

A member of the Great Five of the Humanity Safety Enforcement Division, yet perhaps more accurately described as a walking catastrophe.

“Those who side with humans are no longer demons!”

Infected: 2,483.

“Heh, you think it’ll be that easy?”

The higher the population density, the more devastating the infection.

“Aaaagh! No! Just kill me instead!”

Even before Nane, the worst blight to nearly bring humanity to extinction was now spreading through the presidential palace.

Infected: 6,323.
