Chapter 1254: The Human Obstacle (1)

Fire Management Corporation.

Even though they knew Lethe wouldn’t return, the employees couldn’t go home.

“The system is collapsing.”

Rian’s purification of the Valley of Wailing was shaking all of Hell.

Vivian, Rian’s assigned informator, reported what was being transmitted through the Hidden Code.

“Lady Lethe is protecting Yahweh. The demons… they’re absolutely furious.”

Leona replied.

“Of course they are. They were like fire and water. But it’s Yahweh who’s incredible. I thought he’d never compromise. Working this job for so long… I’m finally seeing something like this.”

“It won’t be easy. Even if Lethe manages Hell, the head of the demons is still Satan.”

Rumble...

The ground was collapsing beneath them.

There’s not much time left.

Once this battle ended and the system was destroyed, they too would vanish into nothingness.

So… what will you do?

Leona wanted to witness the end of the journey for Shagall and Etella—the ones she oversaw.

“Hah! Hah!”

Shagall kept running.

“Aaaaagh!”

Even though the karma assigned to the two had already been drastically reduced,

“I’ll kill her! I’ll tear her limb from limb!”

—the pain Shagall felt was just as enormous.

“Why can’t I reach her?”

No matter how far he ran, Etella remained just out of reach.

“Wait! I have something to say too! I have things I need to tell you!”

He was sure that if they just saw each other face to face, he could change her mind.

No—even if he couldn’t.

“I’ll kill her!”

He felt like he would go mad if she wasn’t by his side—even if he had to sever her neck to keep her there.

“Damn it!”

No matter how fast he ran, the distance between them never closed.

“Why?!”

He wanted to give up, but his sense of smell kept telling him—her scent was close enough to grasp.

“Aaaagh!”

Shagall’s heart was being torn apart.

Just let me see you.

He’d carve into everything in sight.

Just once, just let me catch you…

He would torment her and torment her again until she screamed and begged for forgiveness.

“…Please.”

Shagall realized something.

“Please… just give me one more chance.”

He might really lose Etella forever—right now.

“You son of a—!”

Screaming at the sky, Shagall stopped running and staggered forward.

“I…”

And finally, falling to his knees, tears pouring down his face, he whispered:

“I was wrong.”

At that moment, Shagall’s soul plummeted into the abyss—and an immense amount of karma was purified.

Leona’s voice came through.

—Time remaining until purification: 24 aeons, 8,341 kalpas…

Shagall didn’t hear it.

It no longer mattered how much karma remained.

“I ruined everything. I won’t ever do it again. I don’t care if I suffer in Hell for eternity. Just…”

Shagall looked up with tear-filled eyes.

“Let me see her. Just once.”

His only wish.

“I was wrong. I can’t let her go like this. Do whatever you want to me—just let me see Etella smile… one more time.”

Sigh.

The sound slipped through the broadcast, a mistake—but Leona calmly turned off the mic.

Resting her chin on the table, she looked upon the repenting greatest evil with mournful eyes.

“See… why did you do it?”

You didn’t have to go that far.

“There were so many chances to turn back. So many opportunities to be happy again…”

You had so many of them in your life.

But blinded by rage, you couldn’t see a thing—you just blamed the world.

“And in the end, all that’s left is regret.”

Vivian approached.

“It’s really the end now, isn’t it?”

“Yeah. For humans, time can’t be reversed. Regret might just be the most miserable, most painful feeling there is.”

“They never change. Even when they know the right answer, they stubbornly write the wrong one. Self-destructive to the end.”

The human obstacle.

Vivian and Leona followed Shagall with their eyes—he was running again.

“He has no choice.”

It was the only hope he had left.

A river of fire flowed through the Valley of Wailing.

As rich in golden lava as before Jin Seong Eum’s purification, the source of that flame was…

“Kill Lethe!”

The collective wrath of the demons who had gathered here.

“We’ll slice off her head and show it to the world! We’ll tear the flesh of that vile Yahweh’s ally!”

Countless demons threw themselves into Lethe’s fire, fully prepared to be annihilated.

“Graaagh!”

Lethe’s heart ached every time one of her children vanished.

“Pitiful children… why are you so angry? The end of rage is always regret.”

“You betrayed us! You abandoned us!”

The demons screamed through their tears of blood.

As if it didn’t matter what happened to them, so long as they could make Lethe feel pain—even a little.

Lethe’s eyes dimmed with sorrow.

Well… I suppose that’s true.

You were born of humans, after all.

So many demons were being purified that the river of fire began to flow backwards—

—toward where Rian and Jin Seong Eum were. Where Shirone still lay collapsed.

Rian poured everything into feeding Gehenna’s flame.

“Aaaaaagh!”

The valley shook, and the reversed river of fire evaporated the instant it reached Shirone.

I will protect Shirone.

Even on the brink of unconsciousness from the pain, that single thought remained.

—You fool…

The Black Chains, circling above, pierced Rian’s immobilized body.

“Ghhhhh!”

The skin on his face split apart. His skeletal structure became visible.

I can’t hold out like this…

Even with divine transcendence pushed to its limits, his Idea’s recovery speed wasn’t what it used to be.

With both muscles and bones now dissolving, it felt like even his soul was being burned.

I…

A man without any special talent.

“The world calls me a swordmaster, but honestly… it doesn’t matter anymore. Admire me, envy me—whatever. I don’t care.”

Do I feel proud to have reached this level?

Of course not.

If some grand “Swordmaster” status had just dropped from the sky, maybe I’d have bowed down in thanks.

But how could this possibly feel good?

It’s a title I bought with pain. That’s all.

Want to know how to be good at something?

There’s only one answer.

Even if you’re tormented by failure, selfish motives, unfairness, sadistic suffering, and despair before the impossible… Even if everyone mocks your pain…

“Endure it.”

That’s all.

“Aaaaagh!”

You endure it.

“I will never break!”

A crimson light surged through the chain embedded in Rian’s body.

—Danger! Retreat immediately!

The Black Chains scattered too late. Gehenna’s flame surged faster—up the chain.

BOOOOOOM!

The cluster of Black Chains exploded in a massive blast.

“What… what is that?!”

Even the demons who hated Lethe were left stunned by the fire that engulfed the sky.

Lethe turned to look back.

“It’s being purified.”

Rian’s body, burning like fuel, was melting the chains that bound Jin Seong Eum.

Her soul screamed.

Aaaaaaaaah!

If she continued to be purified like this, she would soon melt away entirely—disappearing forever.

What do we do now, Shirone?

To save Jin Seong Eum, the Underworld’s system would have to crash—for just a moment.

And that final timing… was in the hands of the Jincheon Empire, who were preparing to fire the Pama-Gwangcheon Core.

Anchal, drenched in cold sweat, shouted urgently.

“Your Majesty! Entering the final stage!”

The energy being focused into Etella was past 99% and rapidly approaching 100%.

“Khugh!”

Jin Kang, slumped in his chair, was bent like a willow, barely able to stay upright.

“Y-Your Majesty…”

He should’ve died long ago.

With unfocused eyes, he slowly rose from his chair and staggered forward.

No one supported him—because this was the most meaningful moment in the Emperor’s entire life.

“My daughter…”

My daughter, Jin Seong Eum.

It was only after sending you to Hell… that I realized. That even I, Jin Kang, who never knew regret…

He sobbed, weeping tears of blood.

“Hell, is it?”

His face twisted like a demon’s.

“You dare… do this to my daughter?!”

A red light flickered through the lens of the Pama-Gwangcheon Core.

Meanwhile, Shagall’s head snapped up.

“Etella?”

He suddenly sensed her scent—spread throughout the underworld—drawing close.

“She’s nearby.”

Shagall sniffed like a madman.

“Etella! Etella!”

Maybe it was because he had truly repented?

“Yeah! Just wait a little longer!”

He didn’t want anything more.

If he could just see her face one last time, he needed nothing else.

“Etella!”

Etella slowly turned, having heard his voice travel through the Taeguk Chain.

The reason Shagall could finally pinpoint her scent… was because her journey had come to an end.

“We’re here now, my children.”

The immense energy radiating from her body would soon reduce all of Hell to ashes.

“Mom! Mom!”

Entangled with countless Limbo souls, Etella waited for the end—her final moment—thinking of Shagall.

“I’m sorry.”

The reason she had to turn her back on him… was because she knew exactly what the karma of the Taeguk Chain meant.

“This can’t be undone.”

If liberating the Limbo and saving Jin Seong Eum was the karma granted to the righteous…

“Then your sins…”

…were endless karma, filled with regret.

“Etella! Uaaaah! Etella!”

Shagall ran, howling, searching in every direction.

He was closing in—but the karma remaining between them was vanishing fast.

“No! No!”

Don’t purify me. Even if I stay in Hell… please…

Unable to bear disappearing like this, Shagall inhaled so sharply that his nose bled.

Etella closed her eyes gently.

He’s coming.

As the explosion within her soul swelled—Shagall’s eyes widened.

There she is!

He had found her. With everything he had, he launched himself toward her.

—Time until purification: 4,232 seconds. 3,511 seconds. 2,382 seconds…

Shagall prayed.

“God…”

Please… have mercy.

“You thought you’d walk free after throwing my daughter into Hell?! The Princess of Jincheon! My daughter!!”

Coughing up blood, Jin Kang clutched his chest and roared:

“UAAAAAAAAAAAAHHH!”

It was a father’s final strike, delivered straight into Hell.

Etella’s body became wrapped in light.

And from afar… Shagall’s voice rang out.

“No!”

He was running straight toward her, but Etella already knew how it would end.

“We can’t meet.”

No matter how much space he crossed—time would not allow it.

Shagall held his breath.

There!

If he could just reach that point, he could see Etella.

It didn’t matter if she was already part of the process—his eyes didn’t work anyway.

—Time remaining: 5 seconds. 4 seconds. 3 seconds…

As if mocking him, the karma count decreased in exact distance-based increments.

Shagall reached out his hand.

“Just a little more!”

And when his fingertips came within one meter of Etella—

—1 second.

“Hhhhkk!”

That’s when he understood.

Just one missing second.

But in spatial terms, that missing second was equivalent to an infinite distance.

“Etella…”

Shagall choked out.

“I was wr—”

At that moment, a massive explosion erupted from Etella’s body, completely decimating the process.

RUMBLEEEEEEEE!!

As the seismic shockwaves spread across all of Hell, the informator’s final announcement came through:

—Purification… complete…
