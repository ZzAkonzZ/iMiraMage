Chapter 1171: The Killing War (1)

As Havitz’s words sank in, everyone in the conference hall was left in shock.

Everyone except Uorin.

‘Havitz will kill one person every hour. Of course, until Shirone dies. But why now? Until now, Satan had avoided direct confrontation with Yahweh.’

Such were the laws, such was the precedent.

‘Benevolence and Malice. They both fight over the world, yet they despise direct involvement with one another. Like divorced spouses. They hate each other, yet they don’t want to face each other.’

If they had no choice but to directly collide—

‘Would it be because of a child…?’

Yesterday afternoon, Havitz had met a young girl at a casino in the capital.

‘From the moment he met that child, something happened in those few hours… Something that made him abandon History Search and drove him to madness.’

Who was it?

Uorin put her thoughts aside and rose from her seat.

“This is unacceptable! Havitz’s remarks are an act of aggression against the peace treaty and a declaration of war against the Twelve Nations! Kashan will not back down, and we hereby officially declare Havitz a public enemy!”

No applause followed.

Instead, Albino stroked his beard.

“…The Empress just lit the match.”

Lupist agreed.

“A storm of blood will soon sweep through the Holy War.”

“Havitz is serious. As long as Shirone is alive, he will kill one person every hour. It might be a low-ranking official, or it might be a nation’s ruler. But the biggest problem…”

“…is Vanishing.”

“Exactly. Even if Havitz kills someone, we won’t be able to detect it. And if another nation commits assassinations, they can just blame it all on Havitz.”

Anything could be justified.

“In short, this isn’t just a threat. On the contrary— Havitz has given us something.”

Albino’s eyes narrowed.

“…A license to kill.”

A declaration that he would take all the blame—

And that the nations should kill to their hearts’ content.

Satan’s intentions…

left the minds of the Twelve Nations utterly speechless.

“…We will adjourn the conference for a short break.”

As the officials filed out, Albino’s gaze locked onto Uorin.

‘Did you calculate all this? Of course, you did. But you know this isn’t a perfect strategy.’

You’ve set your own trap.

‘Thormia will be the one to cut your throat.’

With Balkan absent due to sick leave, Smoto and Zetaro attended the Holy War in his place.

Smoto spoke.

“This… This is brilliant.”

“He’s an expert at breaking the rules. Well, the rules have changed now. The question is… How do we use this?”

Zetaro’s face darkened.

“What’s wrong? Can’t think of a plan?”

“I have a plan. To be honest, I thought of an amazing plan… And Havitz would probably love it.”

“You want to kill him yourself, don’t you?”

The only thing Zetaro desired—

Was for Havitz to be in his most ecstatic state.

“Balkan and Natasha both think you have the right to do it. That you’re Havitz’s closest friend. Not Wizard.”

A paradox.

A love so strong, it could justify murder.

It was why Satan mirrored Yahweh’s emotions.

“…Yeah. Thanks. But we need to tell Balkan first. Without that brat, this plan won’t work.”

Until Havitz died, they both desperately wished for Balkan to stay alive.

Though the Grand Conference was adjourned, the subdivisions of the Holy War were still running at full throttle.

Seriel made her way to the Subcommittee Hall.

‘Assassination…?’

Multiple heads of state had suffered attacks.

Even Kessia was among them.

‘So that’s why Fermi…’

She regretted not treating him a little more kindly.

“Excuse me.”

As she entered the subcommittee hall, around 200 officials filled the seats.

The Small Conference.

Also known as the Executive Summit.

If the Grand Conference handled major resolutions, then the Subcommittee fine-tuned the details.

Of the five major divisions of the Holy War—

The Grand Conference, Military Affairs, Economic & Social Affairs, Religious Affairs, and the International Tribunal—

Each division head was present.

Additionally, experts from the Holy War’s specialized agencies—

World Climate, Telecommunications, Public Health, Heritage, and Education—

Were also in attendance.

“The Holy Pope, Constantine, will be about ten minutes late. Please wait a little longer.”

Seriel recognized some familiar faces.

‘Dorothy, the Military Affairs representative.

Liz, the co-founder of Nade Group.

The Alpheas Magic School…’

Both Alpheas and Olivia were present, as well as Siina.

“My apologies for the delay.”

The Holy Pope, Constantine, entered.

The Holy Knights of the Exorcism Division followed in full battle gear.

‘Armed guards at an Executive Summit? He must’ve been delayed because of legal procedures regarding security.’

After Havitz’s announcement, the Pope had no choice but to tighten his precautions.

The meeting began.

“Before we continue from yesterday, let’s summarize today’s topics from the Grand Conference. Last night, the Moon Kingdom attempted to assassinate multiple nations.”

The room turned cold.

“Some nations have suffered severe losses. One kingdom has even lost its ruler. As stated in the report, we must discuss the legal charges against the Moon Kingdom and the suspicion of Kashan’s involvement. Let’s begin with the International Tribunal’s stance before opening the floor for discussion.”

The two people sitting side by side were none other than Socrates and Shirano.

Both had resigned the moment the Holy War began.

Their contrast was striking—

Socrates, weighing over 150 kilograms.

And Shirano, nothing but skin and bones.

But the real problem was—

“Why are you looking at me?

I resigned ages ago.”

“…Actually, Shirano, you were never officially removed from the records. Which means, you are still the official head of the International Tribunal.”

Shirano’s eyes blazed as she turned to glare at Socrates.

“…You fat bastard…”

“Kekeke! What else can I do? The Chairman of the Red Line Society has always been the Tribunal Head. Aside from you and me, there’s no one else. If you don’t like it, you should’ve taken the job first!”

“…I’m going to kill you.”

As befitting her title "The Swamp Witch," long claws extended from Shirano’s fingertips.

“Oh? You wanna fight me? I’ll warn you now—my explosion magic has no mercy.”

Socrates’ eyes narrowed.

“Kuh…!”

The Holy Knights of the Exorcism Division tensed.

Even the non-mages in the room could feel the bloodlust rising.

‘This is bad!

Someone needs to stop them—!’

A Second-Class Archmage or higher can bypass even the most advanced magical security devices.

In the tense silence, where an explosion seemed imminent, Socrates suddenly burst into laughter.

“Puhahahaha! Just a joke! What, do you really think I’d do such a thing? I was just humoring an old spinster’s hysteria—”

Before he could finish, Shirano smacked him across the face.

A deafening bang echoed through the chamber—a sound that defied all logic.

Not only had she resorted to violence, but the fact that a giant like Socrates had collapsed from a single hit was truly bizarre.

Shirano shook off her wrist.

“Tsk. Try to be nice to you, and you only listen after I beat it into you. Well? Get up, you pig. Or do you need another slap?”

Socrates immediately sprang back up and took his seat.

Shirano turned to face the room.

“The paperwork will be processed by the end of the day. I will not be involved in this matter.”

“You knew from the beginning, didn’t you?”

All eyes turned toward Dorothy.

“From the moment the Holy War was convened, you knew this would happen. And so you resigned in advance, didn’t you?”

“…Hmph.”

Shirano crossed her legs.

“That’s right, Minister of Military Affairs. The International Tribunal represents Red Line, and the position of Head of the Tribunal has always been held by a retired Chairman of the Association. Do you know why it’s always a retired Chairman?”

Dorothy fell silent.

“Because active ones never know when to step back. And that’s exactly what a mage should do. So, tell me—what kind of ruling do you expect from me? Now that Moon Kingdom is weakened, should I just side with Kashan?”

“Hey, Shirano…”

For the first time, Socrates’ expression turned serious.

But she raised a hand, cutting him off.

“The Holy War is a den of snakes. They swallow first and think about digestion later. Not a single person in this room truly understands what’s happening. If I make a rash decision, the consequences will fall squarely on the next generation.”

Dorothy, too, was a part of Red Line.

“And honestly— Aren’t you all dumping everything on the International Tribunal because you don’t want to take responsibility? Fine. I’ll take responsibility. You can dismiss me, you can arrest me, I don’t care.”

The room fell into a heavy silence.

“In a world ruled by snakes, the elders are here to clean up the filth for the younger generation. The International Tribunal will not be issuing a ruling for the time being.”

There were no objections.

“What the hell is going on?!”

Rebecca, daughter of King Gith, stormed into Delta Headquarters, flanked by the Captain of the Royal Guard.

“Do you have any idea how much I was about to win?! Because of you idiots, I lost everything!”

She had been gambling all night.

“I apologize, but Jive’s First-Class Emergency Alert has been issued. You must refrain from leaving for now.”

“Tch! Of course. For a second, I thought my father had summoned me.”

“The alert can only be issued by the Supreme Commander of the National Army. So yes—your father did summon you.”

Even though the result was the same, it felt different to her.

‘Ugh, I’ve had enough of this. Might as well pop a Dream Star and go wild in Under Coder.’

High Gear was still operational, but ever since Yahweh left, the fun had all but vanished.

‘…Wait. Speaking of which…’

Shirone was in Delta Headquarters.

‘That’s it!’

Her pace quickened, and her bodyguards adjusted their steps to match.

‘She seems determined.’

Upon reaching the Grand Conference Hall, Rebecca spotted Shirone walking in the distance.

‘There he is!’

A man fighting for the world.

And she—

The daughter of King Gith of Jibe.

‘If I capture Yahweh…’

She had no real interest in politics or world affairs, but…

Wouldn’t this trigger something huge?

“Shirone!”

Rebecca shouted.

“Huh?”

Before his own name, Shirone first thought of his code name.

‘Death Princess.’

To him, the connections he made in Under Coder were just as precious as those in reality.

He smiled and raised a hand.

“Well, well, look who it is!”

With her bodyguards flanking her, Rebecca confidently strode toward Shirone.

“Haha! We’re actually meeting in real life? This is totally—”

The next moment—

Rebecca’s head was severed cleanly from her body.

“…Whoa…”

Her severed face bounced on the ground.

Blood gushed from her open throat.

As she fell—

Havitz casually flicked the blood off his longsword and walked past.

“That’s one down.”

59 minutes and 59 seconds until the next victim.

Even as Havitz disappeared down the hallway, Rebecca’s bodyguards had yet to register that she was dead.

“…Huh?”

A single tear streamed down Shirone’s face.

‘…Why am I crying?’

Because the misery of all humanity was also his own.

Right now, in this very moment—

Yahweh felt as if he had died.

The one reason Yahweh could never kill Satan…

Ironically—

Was because Satan absolutely refused to confront Yahweh.

Shirone’s gaze turned deadly.

“Ha—vi—tz…!”

His voice boomed through the hallway.

Rounding the corner, Havitz’s fur stood on end.

‘…That was close.’

For a brief moment, he had stepped beyond the threshold of divine law.

And it felt like being struck by lightning.

But…

Wena Wizard.

‘It begins now. Every human being will come for your head. Feel free to look forward to it.’

The Killing War had begun.
