Chapter 1122: Identifying Friend or Foe (2)

Ikael could already anticipate the situation.

“This must hurt.”

For Wizard, Shirone was not only her sole teacher but also her only source of emotional support.

“It’s been only seven years since she started experiencing the world. Yet, as a biological entity, her abilities are already exceptional.”

And yet, she was capable of love.

Although she lacked the desires of an adult, her feelings were pure and unwavering.

“What Shirone provides her is something her parents couldn’t. It was inevitable that this kind of bond would form.”

For an ordinary child, it might have been something to laugh about and move on from.

But Wizard was no ordinary child. Soon, she would have to stain her hands with the blood of the most evil being in existence: Havitz.

“I hope her emotions remain unchanged. I hope that when she kills Havitz, she is in a state of purity.”

Like a machine.

“If thoughts like fighting for someone or being forced into killing seep into her mind…”

They would create complications.

“Shirone must know this. Which means he knowingly accepted the risks.”

Shirone could have made Wizard into nothing more than a weapon to kill Havitz.

“But he wasn’t ruthless. He couldn’t bear to ruin a child’s life.”

It was Yahweh’s greatest weakness.

“I have to help.”

As an archangel ruling over heaven, Ikael understood the cost of resolution.

“This isn’t something he can handle alone. Sometimes… one must be cruel.”

Goffin might be angry, but Ikael couldn’t watch her son bear humanity’s suffering alone anymore.

In the awkward silence on their way to Jive, Wizard suddenly raised her head.

“If Satan dies, will peace come to the world?”

Her gaze turned toward Amy.

“Well, of course,” Amy replied. “The chaos in this world is all because of Satan.”

Ikael listened quietly.

“But wasn’t the world chaotic even before Satan came? I heard there were many wars back then too.”

“You heard that from Shirone, didn’t you?”

Amy understood what Wizard was trying to say.

“Yes. Shirone said Satan was born from the human heart. Even if we kill Satan now, if humanity can’t form a unified consciousness, the world won’t change.”

It was Yahweh’s philosophy.

“That might be true,” Amy said. “But we still have to do what we can. Wizard, you’ll be the beginning of that change.”

Wizard let go of Amy’s hand.

“If I kill Satan and peace comes to the world, will you marry Shirone?”

“That’s something I don’t know yet. I love Shirone, but marriage is a matter of the distant future.”

“And that’s okay?”

“What?”

Wizard furrowed her brows.

“If you love someone, don’t you want to be with them all the time? You seem so confident about it.”

Amy couldn’t explain the trust she had built through countless battlefields with Shirone.

“You’ll understand when you grow up.”

“Well, I’m not sure about that. I don’t have much time to grow up, after all. I have to kill Satan first to even have a future.”

Sensing the weight in Wizard’s words, Amy turned to her, only to hear something surprising.

“If I kill Satan, if I succeed in my mission, will you promise me something? That you won’t marry Shirone until I’m grown up.”

Amy, too, had once been a child. She had done impulsive things in a desperate attempt to gain love from her individualistic family.

“Shirone must be her only hope. Her certainty that no one else could be better.”

When Amy didn’t respond, Wizard continued.

“I was just curious about what you think. Don’t take it too seriously. Feelings are…”

“Wizard.”

Amy stopped walking and crouched down, meeting Wizard’s eyes, though Wizard hesitated and looked away.

“I know how much responsibility you’re carrying. Your feelings for Shirone, and the fight you’re waging for humanity. I’ll do anything I can to help you.”

The child’s lips quivered.

“When Satan is gone from this world, I won’t marry Shirone until you’ve grown up.”

Rain fell in Wizard’s heart.

“That’s not what I meant.”

She had dreamed of a way for everyone to be happy, even though she knew it didn’t exist.

As tears welled up in Wizard’s eyes, Amy smiled.

“Let’s…”

At that moment, a triangular horned Mara, Ashur, appeared beside Ikael, drawing his sword.

“Get down!”

Before he finished speaking, Ikael leaped forward.

Boom!

A massive impact struck the earth 23 kilometers from Delta.

Amidst the molten crater, Ikael’s group reappeared.

Amy checked on Wizard.

“Are you okay?”

The child, already in battle mode, was glaring at the towering archangel ahead.

“Who is that?”

Amy turned to the same direction.

“Uriel.”

The Archangel of Destruction, Uriel, rose with a powerful vibration that shook the atmosphere.

“Ikael, how long did you think you could keep running from me?”

Ikael knew immediately.

“He’s resolved herself.”

And this was the one situation Ikael had least wanted to face.

Ashur bowed his head.

“I apologize. I blocked all signals that could expose our location, but the narrowed range…”

No matter what illusions they used, Ikael’s arrival in Jive was inevitable.

“It’s not your fault. I share the responsibility for failing to sense his approach.”

The fact that Uriel had penetrated even the amplification of their senses showed just how strong he was.

“Take Wizard with you. I’ll hold him off here.”

“But…”

Ashur hesitated.

“Can she really win? Uriel has never fought Ikael with his full strength before.”

Uriel had always respected Ikael more than any other angel.

“As the source of all concepts, Ikael is a force unto herself.”

But now, Uriel, the Archangel of Destruction, had decided to break free from that respect and unleash his true power.

Ikael spoke.

“This is a wound I caused. I need to speak with Uriel. Leave this to me.”

Ashur bowed his head before materializing beside Wizard using his signal ability.

“Let’s go. We’ll move to Jive.”

With Ikael’s stealth compromised, it was only a matter of time before Satiel also discovered her location.

Amy stood up.

“It seems one more will have to stay behind.”

As Ashur looked up, another figure in a martial uniform approached decisively.

It was Uriel’s second mara, Aruta.

The sole reason for existence lies in the understanding of combat, and his mastery far surpasses that of any human.

Aruta spoke. “Uriel wishes to face Ikael alone. Anyone who interferes will be eliminated.”

“Wizard, go,” Amy said, but Wizard shook her head.

“No, I’ll do it. I can handle this.”

“You can’t win. Go.”

Wizard was strong, of course, but all her training had been designed with Havitz in mind.

“Even with her talent that surpasses experience, she picked the wrong opponent this time. This one is strong.”

When asked what kind of strength it was, it would be hard to put into words.

If someone had trained in a single discipline for tens of thousands of years, this is the kind of aura they would emit.

“Even a 1% chance that Wizard might fail to complete her mission cannot be allowed.”

Wizard was stubborn.

“I can win. If I can’t handle someone like this, I won’t be able to kill Havitz either.”

“Don’t talk so arrogantly.”

The only reason Wizard was unique was that she alone could break through Vanishing.

“You still have a long way to go. Focus on defeating Havitz.”

“At least I’m better than you—!”

As Wizard began to yell, Amy’s Crimson Eye flared up, and she glared at her.

“I said go!”

Wizard flinched.

Thunder roared in her heart, and before she could say another word, Ashur activated his Signal.

“Be careful,” he said as his final words before Wizard disappeared.

Amy exhaled deeply.

“Don’t even think about chasing her.”

“Don’t misunderstand,” Aruta said. “This is by Uriel’s decision alone. We don’t stoop to cowardly tactics.”

“Is that so?” Amy scoffed.

“Big talk, coming here specifically when Shirone isn’t around. Was Yahweh that frightening?”

Aruta’s muscles tensed.

“I’ll say it again—”

Uriel had said he wanted to talk with Ikael, but explaining further would only diminish their dignity.

Aruta stopped speaking, lowered his stance, and brought his right fist to his side.

“I’ll end this in an instant.”

In that moment, his aura transformed overwhelmingly.

“That’s it.”

It felt as if every principle necessary to subdue an opponent with a fist was converging into a single point.

“One-hit kill.”

Failure would collapse his stance entirely, but his confidence in not failing was absolute.

“Not a good match-up.”

The Crimson Eye’s ability only peaked after the second exchange, making dodging the first strike critical.

“Your expression doesn’t look good. Are you scared?” Aruta asked.

Amy smirked, lifting the corners of her mouth.

“Not at all.”

Flames erupted from her body, burning the air as she unleashed the energy of a warrior who would never retreat.

“Come at me.”

The battle would be over once she dodged and countered his attack, and Aruta knew it.

“Interesting.”

Amy’s wavering, flame-like aura seemed to follow the natural order itself.

Both of them were aware of the simplest strategy for their confrontation, standing at a tense stalemate.

“Ikael,” Uriel said, rising over the crater.

“I know I am not worthy. But Satiel will destroy humanity. She is not in a state to make sound decisions.”

“I know,” Uriel said, landing on the ground and stepping closer to Ikael.

“This isn’t what you wanted, was it? You abandoned the angels and heaven for the sake of a single human you loved.”

“I didn’t abandon them. I simply—”

Before she could finish speaking, Uriel’s fist slammed into Ikael’s face.

A thunderous roar echoed as Ikael was sent flying, carving a V-shaped fissure into the ground.

Standing amidst the radial destruction, Uriel stomped the ground, creating another shockwave as he landed directly before Ikael, who knelt beneath him.

“You could have killed me.”

Even just now.

“Since the day you left, I’ve thought countless times about whether I should erase you completely. The origin of all angels… you.”

“It seems you’ve made up your mind.”

Though Ikael still had strength, she remained on the ground, unmoving.

“Uriel can destroy everything.”

The only reason he had been controlled until now was that Ikael was the very concept from which all angels originated.

“It must feel like destroying part of himself.”

This was no mere clash of egos or mutual opposition. It was a single, devastating blow imbued with the intention to truly kill.

“Get up. Let’s have a proper duel. Pleading like this won’t change my mind,” Uriel said.

“What do you want?”

When the two of them clashed, the world would shake, but the outcome wouldn’t change.

“I can’t let myself be destroyed just yet. At least not until Shirone fulfills his will.”

Ikael raised her head.

“If you demand an apology, I will give it. Even more than that, I will do as you wish. Just give me time. I still have work to do here.”

“All I do is destroy,” Uriel said, lifting his foot slowly.

“I’ve thought about this more than I can bear. Since the day I was born, I have thought more deeply than ever…”

His expression, as cold as metal, locked onto Ikael’s.

“Let’s end this.”

The Judicial Halo, Ragnarok, activated as his foot hurtled toward Ikael’s face.

Boom!

His foot stopped just inches from her nose, but even that motion obliterated the landscape behind her.

Had there been even a flicker of fear in her expression, he would have destroyed her face without hesitation.

But the unwavering eyes that stared back at him, her expression, her poise, and her dignity…

“Kariel.”

She reminded him of a mother.

“I finally understand you.”

Confronted by the unique strength only Ikael possessed, Uriel found himself conflicted once more.
