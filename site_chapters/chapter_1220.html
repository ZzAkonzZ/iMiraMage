Chapter 1220:

The crooked merchant asked,

“What are you supposed to be? You think you can take us on?”

“Of course. I’d rather go to prison than hand over my points to garbage like you.”

They looked stunned.

“Kid, you must not know… Do you even realize what happens to people in prison?”

Even without hearing the details, it was easy to guess just from seeing Curtis’s condition.

Nade smiled.

“Good. I was curious what it’s like. Not confident? Then stop hovering around and get lost.”

“Haa…”

The merchants' eyes turned cold.

“Oh, you think we're easy ‘cause we were being polite? One more thing—Even if we clean you out, we can still attack you. The more points we have, the more brutal the crimes we can commit. I’ll leave it to your imagination what we’re capable of.”

“Go ahead. If you’ve got the guts.”

As Nade raised his right hand and summoned lightning, the thugs flinched and stepped back.

“A m-mage?!”

“Yeah. You guys are nothing in the real world. Are you starting to get it now?”

They stood frozen for a moment, then suddenly burst out laughing.

“Pfft—Hahaha! Total noob! Do you even know where you are? This is Melkidu, kid.”

“So what?”

Player-vs-player combat wasn’t allowed.

“Forget it.”

Their leader raised a hand to step forward.

“Alright, I’ll admit it. We know how to treat customers. Let’s settle this. Ten million points, and we’re done.”

Of course, that wasn’t their true intention.

‘A mage, huh?’

The kind of person who likely got him locked up countless times in the real world.

‘So that was the plan. He was gonna teleport out. But he picked the wrong mark today.’

He crept closer.

“Fine, you win. Then I’ll shave off a little more from the ten million and—”

Suddenly, just as he lowered Nade’s guard, he twisted his body and swung a longsword with full force.

Nade leaned back, and a few strands of his hair scattered as the blade grazed the front of his head.

“Hahaha! Got you! You’re finished—”

Warning. System-detected hostile act. The Guard is now tracking the Brika Team.

The message echoed in all accomplices’ minds, but the Brika Team didn’t understand what was happening.

“What? What’s this about?!”

The next moment, guards in full armor began pouring in from all directions across the capital.

“Wh-what?!”

Despite the confusion, one thing was clear: they couldn’t stay here.

“Run!”

But the exits were already blocked, and the guards quickly overpowered and arrested them.

“Let go! Dammit! I said let go, you bastard!”

Within a minute, the entire Brika Team had been handcuffed and forced to the ground.

“You bastards are insane! We didn’t commit a system crime! We attacked a player, that’s all!”

Instead of responding, the guards saluted Nade.

“Salute! All seven criminals apprehended!”

“Mm. Good.”

Nade nodded, hands behind his back, and the Brika Team’s faces turned to disbelief.

“…Huh?”

Brika shouted.

“You… what the hell did you do?! How are you a guard?! You’re a user!”

“I told you, didn’t I? If you’re not confident, stay out of the way.”

“You son of a—!”

Brika’s face turned red with rage, but the cold steel of the cuffs made his heart sink.

‘No… not like this. I’m really going to prison.’

They had neither the power to break the cuffs nor the strength to fight off the guards.

“I’m sorry.”

Brika begged.

“We were wrong. Just give us one more chance, okay? We’ll do anything you want.”

“This is Melkidu,”

Nade said with a smile.

“Try using your points. That’s all you’ve got, right?”

As he turned back to his friends, the guard captain approached.

“What should we do with them?”

Nade waved a finger with an annoyed look.

“Lock them up. Now.”

“Yes, sir!”

As they were dragged off, the crooked merchants shouted.

“Let go, you bastards! What crime did we even commit?! You’re all making a huge mistake!”

Only Curtis, who had already experienced prison, looked at them with a touch of sympathy.

“So, how’d I do?”

Nade flashed a V sign with his fingers, and Eden said,

“You… kind of enjoyed that, didn’t you?”

“So what? It’s all ending today anyway. Guys who abuse their point wealth need to be taught a lesson.”

Curtis asked,

“That item you mentioned—Is it true that with a registration badge, a player can become a guard?”

“Yes. Let’s get out of here first. I’ll explain everything.”

Shirone’s team headed to a high-end restaurant where the discount coupon applied and ordered a late lunch.

“It’s packed.”

The wide interior was bustling with customers.

Curtis was probably the hungriest of them all, but he seemed more interested in the items.

“Hm… So if you wear this necklace, you can pass through physical barriers? That means you could enter the inner track too.”

Iruki replied,

“Yeah, but isn’t everything over now? If we were staying in Melkidu longer, it might be worth exploring.”

“…True.”

Pena asked,

“But even if you convince the king, Curtis and I aren’t accomplices. How are we supposed to enter the Core?”

That was a good point.

“Since Parme allows point transfers, we’ll just redistribute them after Shirone does the persuading—”

As they were discussing, a group walking out of the restaurant suddenly stopped.

“Curtis?”

Everyone turned at the man’s voice.

“Where is he?”

“That’s him, right? Curtis. What happened to him? He’s not the type to get beat up.”

“That’s not the issue. Block the entrance. Circle behind and take him from the back.”

As the group moved with trained precision, Curtis picked up his fork and spoke.

“How about this? We use this item to enter the royal castle. That way we don’t have to wait until tomorrow. Maybe we’ll find another solution in there. If we can steal a registration badge, getting into the Core becomes possible too.”

“Hm, that might really be the only way…”

Just then, a shadow fell across the table, and Curtis instinctively turned his head.

“Curtis!”

As soon as he saw the figure, his pupils trembled—then the man's fist slammed directly into his jaw.

“Guh!”

–Warning. System hostile action detected. The Guard is tracking the Liver Team.

“What? What is this…?”

Curtis rolled across the floor, flipping the table and sprinting toward the exit.

The man guarding the door shouted,

“Calm down! It’s over—Ugh!”

The guard burst through the door, knocking him down, and the restaurant descended into chaos.

“Don’t let him escape! Get him!”

The ones who hadn’t been caught yet were herding Curtis into a corner.

“Hey, Curtis. It’s me, Liver. Let’s just talk.”

As the encircling net closed in, Curtis turned toward the wall.

He charged with full force, arms covering his face, and threw himself at the wall—passing straight through it.

“Huh?”

Those left standing were soon tackled from behind by guards.

“Salute! Eight suspects apprehended!”

As the guards saluted, Nade approached Liver.

“And who are you supposed to be?”

“Shut up! Do you even know what you’ve done? You let the suspect get away!”

“Don’t talk nonsense. Curtis is a detective.”

“So are we.”

Nade was speechless for a moment. Shirone asked,

“If you’re also detectives, why are you chasing him? Curtis is working to solve a cold case.”

“Edrina, right?”

Liver frowned.

“He’s the prime suspect in that case. He’s accused of murdering four women in their twenties.”

Pena said,

“I don’t trust people easily. But I know how to read them. Curtis might be unlucky, but he’s no psycho.”

“I know what kind of guy he is too. He’s my colleague and friend. But… we have evidence.”

Shirone asked,

“Is it true one of the four victims was Curtis’s daughter?”

“Yes.”

“How did she die?”

“She was hanged.”

“Then wouldn’t that be suicide—”

“That’s why it’s a cold case. Curtis and I found Edrina in the underground storeroom of a villa. The rope had snapped, and she was lying on the floor, having fallen from the pipe on the ceiling.”

Nade asked,

“Then it was suicide, wasn’t it?”

“The problem is, she couldn’t have done it alone. The ceiling was four meters high. There was nothing to climb on, and no structure to scale the wall. The steel door had a manual lock and was locked from the inside. What, did she fly? No—Edrina was just an ordinary person. Not a mage.”

It was indeed strange.

“There’s no other conclusion except someone else hanged her. We thought the broken rope might be a clue, but there was nothing definitive. Honestly, it’s not that rare. Struggling right before death can exert enough force to snap a weak rope.”

Liver continued,

“More importantly, there were three other deaths. All signs pointed to homicide. We started tracking the suspect… then one day, we got a letter. From Edrina. After she died.”

“She delayed the delivery.”

“Exactly. She sent it before her death. It went through the international post and took a while to reach us.”

“What did it say?”

“One sentence. ‘It wasn’t my father’s fault.’ Do you get it now? This isn’t just a cold case. Curtis knows something. But he vanished before we could piece it all together. And then… he came to Melkidu.”

Having understood the gist, Iruki asked,

“What now, Shirone?”

“Let’s find Curtis. He’ll talk to us. Especially if he thinks he has to go to the Core.”

Nade gave the order.

“Take them into custody—but don’t put them in prison.”

“Yes, sir!”

The guards led the Liver Team out, and Shirone’s group left the restaurant.

Pena scanned their surroundings.

“Where did he go? If he used a Rapid Recovery Pass Necklace, there’s no way to track him.”

“It’s okay. There’s only one place he could’ve gone.”

Eden turned her head.

“The royal castle.”

Shirone said,

“Curtis probably realized we’d hear the truth from the detectives. Now that he can’t stay with us, he’s probably trying to reach the Core alone.”

“Do you think he’s really the killer?”

Shirone didn’t give a clear answer.

“Let’s head to the castle. The event is scheduled for tomorrow. Going in now could be considered a system violation. Just because we have a Guard registration doesn’t mean we’re safe.”

The group infiltrated the castle using the Rapid Recovery Pass Necklace and flight magic, arriving at the Grand Hall.

Curtis was kneeling, held down by knights.

“Curtis!”

The king turned his head.

“My, how the laws of the kingdom crumble. So, are you lot ruffians come to threaten the king as well?”

"Nothing to lose."

Shirone stepped forward.

“I’m Shirone. I came seeking Your Majesty in order to reach the Core of Melkidu.”

The event hadn’t officially begun yet, so the king wouldn’t have read the recommendation letter.

“Shirone, you say…”

And yet, the king showed a strange kind of amusement and curled his lips into a smile.

“The philanthropist of the era, here in the sanctuary of a murderer? How curious.”

“…What?”

“To think I’d meet Yahweh here. Well, if it’s fate, then so be it.”

Shirone’s expression turned cold.

“…Who are you?”

This wasn’t an NPC.

“Well. Rather than explain…”

A strange distortion flickered across the king’s face, and his appearance began to shift.

A man with black hair. Mid-40s, with a mischievously handsome face.

“Nice to see you again, Shirone.”

Shirone’s eyes widened in shock, the truth hitting him through Omega’s records.

“Cain.”

The first murderer of humankind.
