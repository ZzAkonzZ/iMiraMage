Chapter 278: The Trap of Choosing Between Two Choices (2)

“Shirone, even something beautiful is just a monstrosity when seen up close. The world is not beautiful to anyone but you, and showing emotions is never good. Especially if you want to protect the people you love."

Shirone took a deep breath to calm his excitement. Now was the time to devise a solution rather than get angry.

“What should I do from now on?”

"Show them your strength. If they want a clear reason to choose sides, providing them with solid evidence could be one approach."

Orkamph finally revealed what he had truly wanted to say.

"I'll arrange a place. Give a demonstration of Ataraxia. If you show them your power there, at least you’ll force them to distinguish friend from foe. Then, at the very least, they won't touch your family."

Shirone's gaze lowered. As his mind settled calmly, a hot sensation came up his throat and got stuck in his throat.

"So that's... the real reason you called me here."

Orkamph let out a small breath. As expected, lies didn't work on Shirone.

“Have you heard of the Valkyrie? It is an army organized by Terraje in preparation for the Final War. The reason Kazra Kingdom was able to survive economically was that we secured contracts for weapons from them. But the situation isn't optimistic. The reason Terraje planted their children here is to eventually drive me out and swallow Kazra one day. We need to strengthen our influence within Valkyrie even more. Your Ataraxia is fully capable of doing so.”

Shirone felt the strength drain from his body. He had vaguely guessed, but tears welled up in his eyes when he heard these words from someone he thought was his birth parent.

In the end, blood ties weren't important. What they wanted was Ataraxia, the ability of an archangel.

"I’m asking you. Show them. Just by demonstrating it before the nobles, the impact will be immense. It’s an opportunity to sway the neutral faction to our side."

Shirone forced himself to swallow the tears that were threatening to spill. Orkamph wasn’t crying, so neither could he.

"Did you think I would obey readily if you said that?"

"On the contrary. That's why I'm thinking of making a proposal. You said before that you wanted to go home. Does that still hold true?"

"No, it's not. I want to go back even more than before."

Orkamph wasn't displeased by Shirone's sarcastic words.

"If you perform the demonstration, I’ll let you return home as soon as the paternity test is finished. You can go to the magic academy or do whatever you wish. You won’t even have to come to the royal palace if you don’t want to. What do you think?"

Shirone couldn't just pass up this proposal either.

Now that he had experienced the royal palace, he realized that returning home wouldn’t be as simple as he initially thought. In that situation, Orkamph's proposal was definitely tempting.

“But then Ataraxia won’t be available for you. I’ll be needed eventually, so how do you plan on convincing the nobles?” (“하지만 그렇게 되면 아타락시아는 이용할 수 없을 텐데요. 결국에는 제가 필요하게 될 텐데, 대신들을 어떻게 설득시킬 생각이죠?”/I don’t know why Shirone mentioned minister out of nowhere so I changed it to noble.)

Orkamph felt uneasy as if his true intentions had been exposed. The reason he proposed the demonstration was for a much greater purpose than drawing in the neutrals.

Arius had said that if he saw Ataraxia directly, he could find a way to replicate it.

Once replication succeeds, Shirone would be unnecessary. No, rather, it would be better for him to leave the royal palace. Even for Shirone's own life...

"That's why a secret agreement is necessary. I’ll announce to the nobles that you will return after graduating from the magic academy. Would one year suffice? During that time, I will purge Terraje Faction. After that, we can each go our own way."

In the end, it meant that if he couldn't purge Terraje Faction, the deal would be nullified.

However, Shirone nodded as if that level of risk was natural. As long as he was guaranteed one year, whether the promise was kept afterward didn’t really matter.

He was planning to move his residence once he graduated from the magic academy anyway. It's just a matter of thinking that the moving distance has become a bit farther.

At least until he had gained enough power to stand unshaken by royal authority, he could live that way.

"Who will be attending the demonstration?"

"I've already sent word. All the high-ranking nobles will gather. They're expecting a grand show."

Shirone looked at Orkamph with sorrowful eyes.

"You knew from the beginning that I couldn't refuse."

“Shirone, it was you who first requested this proposal three days ago. I merely created a justification to allow it."

Orkamph remained cold to the end.

But thanks to that, Shirone regained his composure. He would do this not for Kazra, but for his family.

"Fine. Let me know when the preparations are finished. For now, I need to go see my 'parents.'"

He wanted to just leave right away. He also knew that even if he did so, Orkamph wouldn't reproach him.

But Shirone maintained his composure and exited with courtesy.

After all, Orkamph was the king of a neighboring kingdom.

@

Upon arriving at the quarters, Shirone first checked on his parents' well-being.

Their eyes were sunken as if they hadn't slept all night. Though they were forcing smiles so as not to worry their child, Olena's face was as pale as a blank sheet.

Even for Shirone, who had trained in magic, his heart was racing. For her mother, who had lived her life without causing harm to others, the stress must have been unbearable.

'I'm sorry, Mother.'

Amy, who greeted him, neither forced a bright smile nor showed signs of tension. She just raised her hand in greeting with a bitter smile.

Shirone felt a strong sense of camaraderie in her appearance.

Reina, who had been in a meeting with the attendants in the next room, greeted him with a bright expression.

“You’re here, Shirone. We've been waiting."

Despite everyone looking worn out, Reina alone seemed full of energy. As expected of a court musician, she was clearly accustomed to the palace atmosphere.

While it was refreshing to see this new side of her, Shirone couldn’t shake the discomfort in that moment.

It's not that he disliked her. But no one would understand how he felt, having been a fool for a day while his family's lives were in someone else's hands.

“I met Orkamph.”

Reina's expression hardened.

Not only from Shirone's cold voice, but also from the way he addressed the king by name, one could guess how angry he was about this incident.

"I'm sorry for not telling you in advance. But I was afraid you'd worry..."

"I understand the reason. But everyone was in danger."

Reina swallowed hard. It was the expression that appeared only when Shirone was truly angry, which she had seen once at their family estate.

Now that she was on the receiving end, it felt like her heart was freezing. Had her father felt this same chill back then?

"It's okay now. We've strengthened the security procedures. We've requested the guard to further enhance security..."

“You should have told me.”

Reina’s mouth shut tight like a clam. Amy, watching the situation unfold as if she were observing a fire, let out a sigh.

'See? I told you we should have said something.'

This mess was a result of underestimating how much Shirone cared about his family.

But honestly, Amy was in no position to say anything either. Even though she had clearly anticipated Shirone's reaction, she had followed Reina's judgment because she knew Reina’s decision was ultimately the right one.

Even now, with Shirone fuming like this, if he had found out earlier, the situation could have spiraled out of control. There's no way Shirone wouldn't have known this fact. That's why, in the end, it was Reina's victory.

Vincent, feeling the awkwardness, tried to console Shirone.

“Shirone, Ms. Reina had no choice. Moreover, it was a decision made after seeking our consent. If you're upset, be mad at me."

Shirone lowered his head. He had known that from the beginning.

He was just angry.

The thought of his family being threatened with assassination, his so-called birth parents being obsessed with Ataraxia, and now even Reina shunned him. The sheer frustration of it all made him want to scream.

"I'm sorry. I know Reina Noona saved all of us. I just... I felt so frustrated, and it got the better of me."

Reina heard the faint tremor in Shirone’s voice. What on earth did Orkamph say to him that this usually strong child returned so broken?

"No, I’m the one who should apologize. You had every right to be upset. Informing you was also one of my duties. From now on, I'll report everything without omission."

As Shirone managed a forced smile, Reina finally relaxed a bit.

As expected, this child was the most beautiful when he was smiling. It made Reina even more determined not to see him sad.

In order to leave the palace safely, Reina knew she needed to remain level-headed. Even if the same situation arises, Reina intends to make the same judgment.

Once the tension between them subsided, Amy asked.

"So what did you talk about when you went to the audience chamber?"

“Ah, that’s….”

Shirone said what Orkamph had proposed. Amy sank into careful thought, then nodded.

"Hmm, it's a decent proposal. Of course, assuming he keeps his promise."

“It doesn’t matter. We have to move after graduation anyway. We can think about the next problem when we get there. So I accepted the proposal for now."

"That makes sense. Same for me."

The concept of a "main family" for nobles exists because their place of residence changes depending on their profession.

Amy’s older brother, Diane, was the head of the archers' division at the royal palace, and her second brother, Ares, who was an explorer, was always wandering remote places, so his current whereabouts were unknown.

Globally, the employment rate for mages is about 80 percent, and the hiring rate is close to 99 percent. The reason the employment rate is lower than the hiring rate is that there are quite a few mages who refuse jobs.

But competition was fierce, and most students, upon graduating, moved to the capital, Bashka, to find work or prepare for certification exams. If those options don’t pan out, many went abroad for further studies.

Although there are magic institutions in Creas too, Shirone didn't want to end his magic career in a provincial region.

In the future, when he reach the certified fifth class, he might be appointed as the vice-chairman of the Magic Association Creas Branch, but right now, there were many things he wanted to accomplish.

Alpheas Magic Academy was a kingdom-designated institution, and upon graduation, you receive a non-official tenth rank mage certificate. Once you obtain the certificate, guilds give you work, so you can also live as a mercenary. This way, he wouldn't even need to buy a house, making it more convenient to move around.

He thought that maybe, after living like that for a while, he might meet someone special and eventually settle down somewhere.

Amy, as if supporting Shirone's resolve, clapped her hands and cheered him on.

"Alright! If we're going to do this, let's do it right!"

The Colosseum was built in a basin area 2 kilometers north of Kazra Royal Palace. It used to be a huge gambling den where warriors fought wild beasts and nobles placed bets. However, due to financial issues in the royal palace, it had been indefinitely closed.

The unkempt sandy ground still bore traces of blood. No matter how much the workers swept with their brooms, the red bloodstains wouldn't be erased.

The nobles entered with their guards, and Amy and Reina also took their seats in the third-floor stands.

Orkamph had considered Shirone’s feelings and allowed his parents to attend, but since Olena's health had worsened, it was decided that Vincent would stay behind at the quarter with her.

Shirone was waiting for his time in the waiting room underground the Colosseum.

Around him, there were exercise machines and sandbags once used by champions, and various weapons were displayed on shelves.

As the iron door of the waiting room opened, Zion and Uorin entered. It seemed to be Uorin's first time in the underground waiting room, as she curiously glanced around.

Shirone no longer welcomed anyone. After learning of last night’s events, everyone seemed like an enemy to him.

Zion approached with a waddling gait and said,

“What a desolate place. I wonder what thoughts went through the minds of the warriors waiting here. Were they praying not to be eaten by the lions? Or perhaps they were planning how to break down that door and escape."

"No, they must have been determined to kill their enemy. Because that's the only path left for a warrior."

Zion smiled crookedly. As expected, when it came to words, he was no match for a mage.
