Chapter 961: The Right Choice (4)

The Royal Castle of Bashka

"A monster!"

Giants over 80 meters tall had broken through the castle walls and surrounded the royal castle.

Even seasoned politicians wet their pants at the sight of the giants peering through the windows with their bulging eyes.

Was this a sign of the kingdom's downfall?

In the king's chamber, King Adolf XII, who had become king in his youth, was on his deathbed.

"Haa. Haa."

Pony felt sorrowful that the last sight for the king who had ushered in a new era of magic was this.

"Your Majesty, please do not worry too much. The glory of Tormia will surely continue through your descendants."

"Gasp! Gasp!"

Surrounded by the royal family, Adolf XII lifted his head to deliver his final words.

"My, my everything..."

And at that moment, he made eye contact with the giant peering through the window.

"Ahhhhh!"

Adolf XII convulsed in terror and, with a final death rattle, passed away.

"Your Majesty! Your Majesty!"

The King of Tormia had died.

While the royal family gathered and wept, only Pony remained calm.

'This is a king.'

Just a mere human.

'What should I do now?'

Thanks to risking her life to plead with Lupist, she knew about the disaster that would soon befall Bashka.

An elemental bomb would soon explode over Bashka, and no one would survive.

"Listen carefully from now on."

"Your Majesty! Your Majesty!"

As none of the royal family paid attention, Pony shouted with wide eyes.

"Everyone, listen to me!"

The royal family frowned and turned to look at her.

But upon seeing the giants wandering outside, they were brought back to reality.

"Run!"

As they reached for the door handles, a torrent of water swept them all away.

"Pony! What are you doing!"

As the royals, pushed against the wall, glared and shouted, Pony spoke coldly.

"Where do you think you're going without even securing the throne?"

"Well, that..."

For those who had staked their lives on their lineage, securing the throne was as important as life itself.

"Of course, the next king is Adolf XIII, who is here! Why are you asking the obvious?"

Each of the three princes had their own names, but the eldest had already become Adolf XIII.

The royal family was tense.

'What is that girl Pony thinking?'

The castle guard had already been sent to the flower field on Lupist's suggestion.

There were a few guards left, but for Pony, a graduate of the magic school, killing someone would be a matter of moments.

"If Brother Tenar is to become the next king..."

As everyone watched with tense expressions, Pony spoke.

"How about holding a brief succession ceremony? The legitimacy of the throne must be maintained."

"Huh?"

The royal family blinked.

"Look outside. The nation is in a state of crisis. We must quickly restore the royal authority and lead the people."

Relieved that she wasn't planning a rebellion, they sighed in relief.

"That sounds good. The throne must not be left vacant for even a moment."

The eldest member of the royal family brought Adolf XII's crown and placed it on Tenar's head.

"It's done. Now let's go. Quickly!"

The succession ceremony ended in an instant, and the royal family hurriedly left, leaving Pony behind.

Watching her relatives hastily retreat, Pony smiled with relief.

'At least they have some courage.'

When she first heard Lupist's suggestion, she had dreamed that she might become queen.

"What does that matter?"

With the world in this state, she realized that what truly mattered couldn't be given by others.

"You were right, Shirone."

She would survive.

At least her end would be different from Adolf XII, who died with a miserable expression.

The giant, who had been curiously observing the inside as if watching a world of tiny people, let out a roar.

"Graaaah!"

The giant's desire to crush small things was far greater than that of humans...

"I'm not that easy."

Through the gap in the wall smashed by the giant's fist, a flash of teleportation escaped.

Codename Lucky Boy

"There it is!"

The aircraft Nade was piloting was a self-powered, mage-specific vehicle.

Drawing power from external sources, it was lightweight and small, perfect for maneuvering missions.

'Bashka.'

From high altitude, Bashka was no bigger than a thumbnail, but he could see the swarming demons within it.

It was disgusting, and if he could, he would crush them with a solid boot.

'Soon it will be.'

Attached to the underside of the aircraft was a single elemental bomb.

'Just one.'

If it failed to detonate, the operation would fail, and humanity would be destroyed by the demon army.

Yet Iruki insisted on just one bomb.

'He's thorough...'

Who could blame him?

'Don't worry. I'll succeed.'

Looking down again, most of the demon army was concentrated inside or near Bashka.

'I'll drop it here.'

As the first realistic signal reached his brain, his heart sank.

'Really? Am I really doing this?'

It felt as if all the simulations he had imagined were absurd illusions.

'Everything I see will disappear. In an instant.'

Thinking about life, civilization, and all the emotions within, the weight was unbearable.

"Huuu."

Though his heart wasn't moved, he strangely began to sob and tears flowed.

"Timer. Timer."

So he decided not to think at all.

'Just do it. Like a fool, like a machine. Like a psycho, just do my job.'

The panel of the elemental bomb displayed 60 seconds, and as it separated from the aircraft, the countdown would begin.

'According to calculations, it will explode 200 meters above Bashka, and once it does...'

No life would survive.

"Here goes."

As Nade's trembling hand reached for the button, a sharp voice rang out.

"Catch that human!"

A fairy army was flying towards him.

As he saw them, a pure electrical signal flashed through Nade's mind.

'Press it.'

The elemental bomb detached from the aircraft with a click, and the fairies sharply changed direction.

"Catch that object! By order of Ikael!"

Time until explosion: 59 seconds.

Miro rampaged through Bashka's streets.

"Like cockroaches!"

Every time the avatar of Avalokiteshvara used its techniques, buildings crumbled.

"There!"

It was hard to tell who the real thugs were, but Miro's group had no time to worry about such things.

"Hahaha! You think you can catch me?"

Paimon laughed, moving freely among hundreds of points in the air.

'Annoying ability.'

Regulation Ban.

An ability to isolate a specific space and use the mobility of those trapped within.

And currently, Bashka had an abnormal population density that didn't need the world's best label.

Paimon's voice echoed from all directions.

"Haha! Moths drawn to the flame! The greatest festival in this world will soon...!"

The roof where Paimon stood was crushed by a palm, and the techniques of Avalokiteshvara struck repeatedly.

"...begin."

Paimon, having escaped the space, looked up at the sky with ecstatic eyes.

The elemental bomb was falling.

"What?"

The sun and moon halos created a magnifying effect in the atmosphere, and Miro's group looked up.

"Damn!"

Even though they were prepared for death, the reality of the bomb about to explode was dizzying.

'How much time is left?'

Sein checked the timer: 38 seconds.

Nade's aircraft, dodging the fairies' attacks, increased its output.

"This is driving me crazy!"

Even when casting lightning magic, the aircraft absorbed the power, making counterattacks impossible.

'There's no way to escape in time.'

Finally, Nade left the aircraft, spreading plasma around him and casting magic in earnest.

White electricity exploded.

"Eek!"

The fairies' screams were cute, but the results were horrific.

The fairies struck by lightning glowed, revealing their squirrel-sized skeletons.

'What are these things?'

According to the information Nade had as the head of the Nade Group, they were the army of heaven.

The dynamics of good and evil were complex, but they were not necessary for humans.

'Too many.'

The number of fairies in the main force was over 20,000, and there were at least 1,000 around him.

"I'll handle the human. You retrieve the bomb!"

At the command of the fairy captain Tanote, hundreds of fairies chased the bomb.

'No!'

Though he had already given up his life, the elemental bomb had to explode at the designated location.

"Stop right there!"

As Nade headed towards the ground, a powerful binding force enveloped him.

"Ugh!"

Tanote smiled coldly.

"The Triangle of Justice. Triangle Field."

Nade quickly scanned the red lines surrounding him.

He was trapped in an acute triangle, with ominous flames burning at the vertices.

"When three points connect in a straight line, that area becomes mine."

'Damn it!'

Time until explosion: 23 seconds.

"Iruki! Iruki! Open the door!"

Iruki, alone in the command room of the Holy War, sat on the floor, unresponsive.

Empty bottles lay around him, and his eyes, having finished everything, were unfocused.

"Iruki, please."

Dorothy's voice was followed by the commander's shout.

"Step back! He said he wants to be alone!"

"How can you say that? What if he has dangerous thoughts? You know what Iruki is feeling right now!"

"Because we know! No, no one knows! We can't interfere!"

The sound of their argument seemed distant, as if from another dimension.

"...I made the right decision."

According to the calculations of the Lucky Boy's performance, it would reach the skies above Bashka in five minutes.

Iruki gave a faint smile.

'I did it. I really did it. It couldn't be more efficient than this. Because of that...'

Is it the right thing?

"Ah..."

As the fear trapped in his heart burst forth, he began to sob uncontrollably.

"Ah... Ah..."

Grasping his head and trembling, Iruki was certain.

"It's over."

He wouldn't be able to live with a sane mind.

"I'm finished."

"Iruki! Please! Talk to me! Okay?"

Dorothy's pounding on the door sounded like the footsteps of a grim reaper approaching.

"Ah! Just leave me alone!"

When the noise outside the door ceased, Iruki clutched his head and wept again.

"If I die, if I just die..."

At that moment, a different, calmer knock was heard.

"Iruki."

The moment he heard the voice, his eyes widened.

Thump! Thump!

His heart raced as if it would burst, and despite knowing it was a hallucination, he lifted his head.

"Iruki, it's me. Can I come in?"

It was Shirone's voice.
