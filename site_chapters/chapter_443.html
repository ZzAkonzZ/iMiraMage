Chapter 443:

The First Command went into emergency mode.

The headlights of the Guroi, lined up in the warehouse, burst into light one after another as they began to move.

Infantry equipped with pipers carried arcs and flanked the Guroi as they exited the warehouse.

In the midst of the chaos on the battlefield, only Babel and Sein stood still, glaring at each other.

Physically, Sein was no match for Babel.

That was the conclusion Sein reached after seeing Babel's first attack.

'Algorithm.'

But her movements were not human.

If the enemy operated on a probabilistic algorithm, it wasn't entirely impossible.

'There are gaps. They're just hard to find within human capabilities.'

Babel Program: Engaging Anti-Personnel Combat System.

The panel protecting Babel's lens emitted a red light, using all its functions to analyze Sein.

Target: Programmer.

Combat Pattern: Unrecognizable.

Lethal Response Methods: Infinite.

Babel's joints moved independently as it fixed its gaze on Sein, twisting its lower body to the side.

Just that movement reduced the number of lethal response methods from infinite to approximately 32 quintillion.

In reality, if there were 32 quintillion ways predicted to be lethal, it might as well be infinite for a human.

The battle would proceed by blocking future probabilities with specific actions, and Sein prepared countermeasures.

'It will likely aim for the optimal move.'

So what is the optimal move?

The difference in value judgment on that point would determine the outcome.

Babel moved first, and Sein reacted.

As the flashes of teleportation moved three-dimensionally, Babel's metallic sound spread like cold moonlight.

Lethal Response Methods: 2.78 billion.

The number had dropped to a more humanly conceivable figure, but Babel still had more than enough ways to kill Sein.

Amid the faster-than-expected physical movements, Sein blocked her movements with her iron wheel eyes, frowning.

The calculations were much faster than expected.

When Babel made a specific judgment, Sein had to make a new judgment to neutralize it.

Time, space, environment, and state—every variable gave meaning to each coordinate on the battlefield, and Babel calculated all of it.

In contrast, Sein abandoned the method of calculation.

Responding in the same way to a machine performing calculations in the quintillions was madness.

Insight to find the most aesthetically perfect vital point in the tangled coordinates.

Judgments formed by experience and intuition filled the gaps in Babel's algorithm meticulously.

"Damn it! What are we supposed to do?"

Two hundred Guroi surrounded the battlefield in a circle.

All the soldiers could see were flashes and the metallic afterimages reflected in those flashes.

But the trajectories were so sharp that even a layperson could sense the danger, and an engineer could guess what was happening.

"Hold position. If we intervene, that man will die."

In reality, Sein was desperate.

One mistake and his head would be separated from his body.

Every time Babel's movements blocked her path like a net, her vision blurred.

Lethal response meant an algorithm that moved in a way to eliminate the enemy with 100% certainty.

If Babel's program took even a slight risk, the battle might have already ended in Sein’s defeat.

But conversely, it also meant that Sein was delaying a 100% fatal situation caused by Babel.

'It's only attacking in perfect situations. That means it still has a way to eliminate me with 100% certainty?'

A chilling sensation ran down his spine.

Was this how an ant felt in an antlion's trap?

Amid the excitement, Sein smiled.

He had defeated many strong opponents, and some he hadn't, but this was the first time an opponent overwhelmed him in the same style.

'Interesting.'

His iron wheel eyes rotated, forming two halos.

The realm of emotion, intuition, and the realm of reason, calculation.

As the two halos overlapped perfectly, Babel and the world it encompassed began to waver.

Iron Wheel Eyes - Sun and Moon Halos.

Mental Transcendence - Unity of Mind and Body.

Shirone manifested Armand, connected through the Ultima System, into the realm of his incarnation, integrating it with his own.

Just as the physical Armand strengthened Shirone's body, the ideal Armand strengthened Shirone's mind.

As the limit of his mental transcendence soared and his incarnation emitted a powerful light, Satan's incarnation was obliterated.

Simultaneously, in reality, Raycis released Shirone's neck and was flung back like a cannonball.

"Huff! Huff!"

Shirone gasped for breath.

Armand extracted oxygen from the air, quickly restoring his oxygen saturation.

Regaining his senses, Shirone rubbed his sore neck and glared at Raycis.

Flames and electricity alternated between her teeth.

"Grrr!"

Raycis writhed in pain.

Her incarnation, formed from the fusion of countless creatures, was collapsing from the impact of Shirone's incarnation.

"Craaa!"

Her red skin melted like blood, and even her grotesque form began to crumble.

The blood that had completely melted and spread on the floor defied gravity, rising and transforming into a robust 2-meter-tall body.

Her body was blood-red, with sharp fingertips, and thick horns curved forward from her temples.

But what caught Shirone's attention was the red triangles floating above the monster's head.

They were similar to those seen on Mara, but there were four of them.

'A four-sided Mara?'

Satan's Law of Evil.

If an angel's halo represented endless perfection, Mara's triangle symbolized perfection within imperfection.

Thus, the highest rank of Mara was a three-sided Mara with three triangles.

But Satan had added a fourth triangle, making him the only Mara to escape the Akashic Records.

Even Anke Ra couldn't eliminate the Law of Evil permeating the universe.

Just as Shirone had temporarily merged with Behemoth's incarnation at Kazra Castle, Raycis was now imbued with the extreme Law of Evil.

"A mere Nephilim dares to summon me. Has the Law of Evil weakened this much?"

Shirone gritted his teeth, enduring the pain.

Even Armand recoiled, assessing the opponent's power.

It was a presence he had experienced before.

The aura emanating from Raycis now was comparable to that of Archangel Kariel.

'I can't lose! If I die, everything ends!'

Compressing photons before his eyes, a massive sphere of light exploded into existence.

In a state of unity with Armand, Shirone's mental transcendence far exceeded human limits.

The power gathering was enough to penetrate any obstacle, yet Raycis watched with arrogant red eyes.

Photon Cannon!

As Shirone gritted his teeth and cast the spell, a thunderous roar tore through the air.

Simultaneously, Raycis vanished.

"Huff, huff."

Plu's body was drenched in sweat.

Leaning against the wall, her head tilted back to the ceiling, she couldn't move, her knees bent as if she might collapse at any moment.

Blood flowed from her shoulder and calf.

The bleeding was a problem, but her muscle fatigue had already rendered her body nearly useless.

She had already fended off four waves of Garas.

But the forced movements of the Dawkins algorithm were a double-edged sword, capable of destroying her body if overused.

'Is this… the end?'

"Kik! Kik!"

Ten small Garas, crab-like in appearance, gathered in a semicircle around Plu.

They might have been unlucky enough to combine with weaker creatures, but their reproductive drive was no different from other species.

'I just want to stop now.'

Finding a weaker creature, Plu was overwhelmed by a strong desire to give up.

She knew this was her last chance to die as humanely as possible.

"Kiii!"

The Garas leaped at Plu.

Ponifar Algorithm.

As the counterattack algorithm activated, Plu's body shook, and she swung Phoenix in all directions, striking the Garas.

Fragments shattered like crab shells, and Plu's knee hit the floor with a thud.

This attack had left her entire body in tatters.

Of course, she knew that killing the current Garas wouldn't improve the situation.

But a mage could only do what they could.

'Haha, I can't even commit suicide now.'

Plu used all her strength to lean back.

Thud! Her head hit the wall, and she leaned against it, her eyes slowly losing focus.

"Kikiki."

A grotesque laugh that grated on her eardrums.

Plu couldn't even turn her head, only moving her eyes.

A bizarre creature, 1 meter long, approached slowly, crossing its four legs repeatedly.

It was entirely black, making its features indistinguishable, but she could clearly feel it was laughing.

"Human… woman…"

Garas was more intelligent than humans. But this was the first time she had encountered a species that could speak.

The Garas approached the heavily breathing Plu, observing her for a moment before splitting its mouth open.

"Found you."

Shirone was dragged across the floor by Satan's hand and thrown into a glass sphere.

His heart had stopped, and his eyes were rolled back, his face exposed as his robe was pulled back.

"Phew."

Satan, Raycis, finally felt that everything was over and sighed in relief.

Her muscles were torn in places, and smoke still rose from her temple, where she had taken the last hit.

She had underestimated the Nephilim.

"Truly impressive."

Raycis smiled in satisfaction.

Everything was in place.

A Nephilim to integrate traits, an angel to provide a beautiful body, and creatures to perfectly combine everything.

"Today, I become a god."

Stepping over the bodies of her subordinates, Raycis walked to the Fuzix Machine and activated it.

As the engine of the Fuzix Machine roared to life, the central glass chamber lit up.

It was time to begin the Elixir of Legends.

Raycis entered the last remaining glass sphere, closed the door, and sat down.

Soon, plasm began to fill the eight glass spheres.

Raycis slowly closed her eyes.

This was the grand unification, the journey to completion.

When everything melted into one and was reborn in the central glass chamber, she would become the most perfect being in the world.

The level of the black liquid rose quickly, soon covering her face.

The fragmented body of Ikasa and the fallen form of Shirone were submerged in the liquid, no longer visible.

'It's over. With this…'

Wiiiiiing!

At that moment, a siren blared from the ceiling.

Raycis's eyes snapped open at the faint sound piercing through the plasm.

'What is that?'

She tried to move her legs, but her body's durability had already weakened rapidly in the plasm, like bait dissolving in water.

This made her even more uneasy.

"Grrr!"

Raycis mustered all her strength to stand.

She managed to rise to the top of the glass sphere, gaining a few centimeters of height to see.

'That, that is…!'

Raycis's eyes widened in shock.

Plu, dressed in Norin clothes, was manipulating the controls of the Fuzix Machine.

'How is she here?'

If she had stolen the clothes of the subordinates, it was likely a clone from the isolation room, but the clones there had no intelligence.

'No, I already ordered them to be killed with gas!'

She couldn't make sense of it.

Was the Plu here a clone? Or the real Plu?

The siren blared again from the ceiling.

Warning! Warning! Main type reset! Changing main type from glass sphere 1 to glass sphere 8!

Gurgle!

Bubbles rose from Raycis's mouth.

The creature in glass sphere 8 was none other than Shirone.
