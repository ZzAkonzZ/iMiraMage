Chapter 997: Tomb of the Gods (2)

Ymir had left.

He was likely waiting for Shirone and his group in the deepest part of the first layer of the subconscious.

"But..."

Kangnan's voice was heard.

"Is this really a nightmare? Ymir seemed too calm for that."

Arius replied, "He wasn't calm. You could tell by how quickly he left. He wasn't crying in fear, but the situation was definitely unpleasant for him."

Miro asked, "What made him uncomfortable?"

"First, let me explain," Monga said.

"The dream isn't controlled right now. Ymir isn't defining us as anything. It's a sort of text state. It's a challenge."

"Arius, is there a way?"

"We need to find a keyword connected to the unconscious in the fifth deep layer. But the problem is it's only eyes, and there are over 10 billion of them."

Gaold muttered, "Isn't there a special eye among them?"

Everyone looked around, but only about a third of the eyes blinked at any given moment.

"Fear. Fear."

Arius examined the eyes again.

"This dream is our only hint. Why do 10 billion eyes make Ymir afraid? The dismantling of the Ultima System? The fact that he's being defined?"

"Could it be something other than the eyes?" Shirone suggested.

"More precisely, this dream isn't just about the eyes. There's the vacuum-like state and us, the undefined ones."

"Hmm, that's true."

Arius, deep in thought, suddenly looked up.

"The Tomb of the Gods."

Muttering as if receiving a revelation, he spoke to everyone with an excited voice.

"Yes, this is a nightmare. Ymir said his memory is the Tomb of the Gods. I overlooked it because it seemed impossible, but it makes sense for Ymir."

"Explain so we can understand."

"At the sixth layer, the REM domain, consciousness and the unconscious are separated. Conversely, this place is a middle ground where consciousness and the unconscious mix. But look at this dream. There's nothing here. It means he's not conscious of anything."

Sein asked, "Because he's too strong?"

"Yes. To Ymir, all matter is lighter than air. There's no special stimulus transmitted through his senses. It's a state resembling a vacuum."

"So it's all about hearing. But why is that a nightmare? It's the same in reality."

"In reality, there are things. Ymir has defeated countless strong enemies since his birth. They would be beings revered as gods in any civilization. For Ymir, sensory experiences are at most that level. A handful of strong enemies."

Images of Ymir's past opponents flashed through Shirone's mind.

"Usually, such things are placed in the realm of consciousness. You know, memories you can't forget for a lifetime. But for Ymir, it's the opposite. The unconscious, lacking in sensory input, absorbs those stimuli like a sponge."

"Then what's in the realm of consciousness?"

"Only the shell remains. There might not have been much on the surface. That's why it's more serious. Probably below the REM domain, starting from the fifth deep layer..."

Arius spoke in a solemn voice.

"The strongest enemies who gave Ymir the greatest stimuli of his life are waiting."

The Tomb of the Gods.

"They exist in reality. They must be somewhere in the universe. But they don't exist in this dream. That's why it's a nightmare. All the strong enemies are gone, and he can't feel any stimuli. That's why he provoked us."

Miro said, "I understand. So how do we go down?"

"In the end, it's those eyes. The way to escape the nightmare is to give Ymir a stimulus comparable to his past opponents. We need to enter the Tomb of the Gods."

A silence fell.

Normally, there would be plenty of volunteers, but since the opponent was Ymir, they needed time to think.

Miro asked, "So... who will do it? And what exactly do we need to do? Hit really hard?"

Arius said, "Those eyes don't even define our bodies. Kangnan's schema might have a slight chance, but magic seems better. Shirone or Gaold should do it."

"What about me?"

Arius avoided a direct answer to Miro's question.

"Let's try attacking. Whoever has the highest single-target destructive power between Shirone and Gaold should do it."

When neither of them answered, Miro chuckled.

"Good point. Gaold, who hits harder between you and Shirone when you go all out?"

Gaold replied indifferently, "We're not kids. This is childish..."

Of course, but it was surprising that Gaold didn't give a definitive answer.

"What, really? Does Gaold also hold back in front of Shirone? Why? Are you not confident?"

"Are you Satan?"

In the end, Gaold decided to step up, and soon Air Press was cast.

"Ugh!"

The immense pain surged, but the 10 billion eyes didn't define the magic.

"...It didn't work?"

Everyone, who had been waiting confidently because it was Gaold, finally realized the seriousness.

"I'll try."

When Shirone's voice was heard, Gaold said, "Wait."

Kangnan advised, "Don't let your pride get in the way. Ymir is the strongest enemy. We need to work together..."

"That's not the issue." Gaold's voice was serious.

"Even though I cast the magic, I can't gauge its power. It will be the same for Shirone."

Miro tried it herself.

"You're right. There's no sense of reality. We need to give it our all, but it's like just imagining it. We can't set a standard like this."

"But I can."

Gaold said.

"Use pain as the standard. Keep increasing the power until it reaches the realistic maximum, our full strength."

Kangnan was worried.

"Are you sure? Even if you reach the realistic maximum, if the nightmare doesn't break..."

Only Gaold had a standard, and the Gaold she knew wasn't someone who would give up.

"A god, huh."

Gaold grinned.

'Let's see your face.'

As Air Press was cast, Gaold's pain perception soared endlessly.

"Ugh!"

He had already reached full power, but the nightmare still didn't budge.

'So you're the master, huh?'

As Kangnan feared, Gaold ran faster towards the path of blades before him.

"Aaah!"

Hearing Gaold's desperate scream, Kangnan activated her schema to the extreme.

"Gaold! Gaold!"

She felt a slight sense of physical sensation, but it was still far from reality.

"Stop! You'll die before you even fight!"

"Quiet, wait."

Miro said coldly.

"We can't help anyway. Let Gaold handle it."

Kangnan was furious that she couldn't grab Miro by the collar right then.

"Aaah!"

Gaold's scream was the nightmare itself, and finally, Sein spoke up.

"Miro, it's too long."

Gaold, who usually subdued opponents in an instant, had never prolonged Air Press this much.

"He'll die like this."

Until Miro told him to stop, Gaold would never stop.

"If he dies, he dies. Are we going to live here?"

Sein also loved her, but at times like this, he couldn't help but feel a chill.

Finally, Shirone stepped in.

"Anyway, for now..."

At that moment, the eyes seemed to shake, and a faint vibration was felt from everyone's bodies.

"Sensation..."

It was being defined.

Gaold's form, howling like a beast, began to be reflected through the nerves passing through his body.

"Aaah!"

Finally, with a thud, a hole hundreds of meters in radius appeared in the nightmare's floor.

Monga, now in form, spoke.

"The nightmare is over."

At the same time, Gaold collapsed to the floor, gasping for breath.

"Huff! Huff!"

"Gaold!"

As Kangnan leaped, Miro reached him first and patted his back.

"Wow, that was impressive. Well done. Really well done."

Seeing Gaold's shoulders trembling, Kangnan momentarily lost her composure.

"At least you did it. It was worth bringing you..."

Before she could finish, Kangnan grabbed Miro by the collar and yanked her up.

Without stopping, she raised her arm, and Miro's toes pointed to the ground like spikes.

"Are you crazy?"

Miro's gaze, looking down at the growling Kangnan, was icy cold.

"What is it, suddenly?"

"I've known you were a lunatic for a long time, but listen carefully. If you mess with Gaold again, I'll destroy you."

Miro glanced at Gaold.

"I've known for a long time that you're an idiot."

"What?"

"We came to fight for the world, didn't we? We found a way through. Do you think this would have happened if I had stopped Gaold?"

"Is that... the ultimate good?"

"Yes. Because there are people like you who get beaten by evil while being swayed by weak emotions, I fight. I carry everything and hold the line until the end."

Kangnan lifted Miro higher.

"Say it. You won't use Gaold again."

"No. Do you even have the right to say that? Besides, Gaold said he'd do it."

Kangnan raised her fist.

"You might really get hit."

As the situation threatened to turn violent, the group approached, but Miro's killing intent came first.

"Let go."

The incarnation of Avalokiteshvara rose high, with thousands of arms aiming at Kangnan.

"Last chance."

In the tense atmosphere, where Avalokiteshvara seemed ready to move at any moment, Gaold stood up.

"Miro."

From his back, which now seemed larger, a fiery energy radiated.

"You're right. We came here to fight. I won't fall from just this. But..."

His low voice carried strength.

"Don't touch Kangnan."

Kangnan's head snapped around, and Miro's eyes wavered for a brief moment.

'He was like this even in heaven.'

Gaold had risked his life to save Miro and then headed into danger again to save Kangnan.

"Gaold..."

Gaold gave a dry smile.

"Heh, don't worry. We've come this far, we have to catch the god."

Miro didn't know.

How a fourteen-year-old girl had protected Gaold when he lost consciousness after falling into hell.

'She'll never know.'

As human emotions pierced her heart, Miro desperately steadied herself.

'I mustn't break. I mustn't break.' Her eyes quickly cooled, and she raised her arms with a bright smile as if nothing had happened.

"Alright. I'm sorry. Can you let go now?"

Kangnan was still angry, but she was more concerned about Gaold's condition.

She released Miro's collar and supported Gaold as they moved away.

"Are you really okay?"

"I'm fine. You really have a bad temper. When will you fix that habit?"

"Who started it? Why? Are you scared I might actually hit you?"

"Haha! It's a miracle you're not dead. Well, you need that kind of guts to say such things."

"Hmm?"

Gaold, lying on Kangnan's lap and catching his breath, pointed at her with his index finger.

"You said you wanted to become the strongest woman in the world, right?"

"Oh."

She remembered clearly, but had she ever told Gaold that?

"Do you know how old I was then? When did I even say that..."

"You will be."

Gaold's eyelids slowly closed, and he soon fell asleep, breathing evenly.

Miro placed her hand on Gaold's forehead and looked at Kangnan, who was smiling.

She thought Kangnan was beautiful.
