Chapter 793:

“Work! We've got work!”

When Jaestin burst into the guild and threw open the door, her subordinates were sprawled out asleep on the broken wooden floor.

“Get up, you lazy bastards!”

Six men and one woman groaned, still dazed, and slowly raised their upper bodies.

“Ugh, my stomach… Why so loud first thing in the morning?”

The stench of last night’s vomit still lingering, One-Eye grimaced and looked at Jaestin.

“What? Morning already?”

Jaestin loaded a magic round into her gun.

“Is that something someone who eats off this guild should be saying?”

The moment they saw the barrel of the gun, the men’s sleepy expressions vanished and they flung themselves in all directions.

“Run! She's gonna kill us!”

Bracing for the sound of a magic round, everyone shut their eyes tightly in panic.

“I said we’ve got work. For crying out loud.”

With a sigh, Jaestin lowered her gun.

Inside the drafty guild patched up with wooden boards, the entire Jaestin Mercenary Corps gathered around the round table.

“So, you're saying we’re going to be guarding the most stunning ladies in the world?”

One-Eye asked in disbelief.

“Yes. Though only I’ll be allowed inside the waiting rooms. You six will handle external security.”

With the threat still unresolved, the Arachne government had mobilized everything for security. Even Jaestin being a woman played a key role in their selection.

The woman at the counter asked, “How much are they paying?”

“It’s performance-based. But the advance alone is enough to pay for the guild’s repairs. And if we pull this off, our name will spread far and wide.”

One-Eye's single eye gleamed.

“Does that mean… we’re finally stepping back into the world?”

The rebirth of the Jaestin Mercenary Corps.

“Exactly. It was a last-minute decision, but we’ll be ready by rehearsal. Briefing in ten minutes at the open lot.”

At the World Beauty Contest venue, participants and staff had separate waiting rooms.

“Haaah… Haaah…”

About to debut, Maya sat in the guest performer’s waiting room, taking deep breaths.

‘Why am I like this? My heart’s racing way too fast.’

Her manager Palmas came in and handed her a mild sedative.

“Still nervous?”

“Yes… but I’ll be okay.”

So many people had worked so hard for this day—she couldn’t afford to falter now.

“Just stay calm. You’ve practiced hard. You nailed the rehearsal. You’ll do fine.”

‘I almost wish I had made a mistake.’

The fact everything went too smoothly made her more anxious—it was her first professional stage.

‘Shirone…’

Maya opened the sheet music titled Light Rain.

‘Someday, you’ll hear my song.’

The song's title symbolized the feeling of light pouring down from the sky—her love for Shirone.

Watching her calm down as she read the lyrics, Palmas clicked his tongue internally.

‘Whoever he is… he’s lucky.’

Everyone involved had realized from the lyrics that they weren’t just words—they came from a real place in her heart.

‘This is only the beginning. Just throw your heart in, Maya. That’s all it takes.’

Palmas quietly left the room.

In the contestants’ waiting room, representatives from 32 countries gathered—a silent battlefield.

The female security officers all shared the same thought.

‘This won’t be easy to manage.’

Miss Baiden, lounging in her underwear with her legs spread wide, grumbled.

“Sigh, I’m so bored.”

No one responded. A social butterfly by nature, she felt suffocated.

‘If only I could sneak out for a cigarette…’

But with so many eyes watching, even that was impossible.

As she yawned and stretched, she looked over at Miss Tormia.

She had a tall forehead framed by golden hair and eyebrows like pure gold—strikingly beautiful.

“Hey, isn’t it uncomfortable to sit in your dress this early? You’ll have to change soon anyway.”

Alfred Pony closed her book and replied,

“A royal must maintain her dignity regardless of time or place.”

“Oh, are you a royal?”

Having narrowly lost to Maya in Tormia’s magic school exam, Pony had taken a leave of absence.

‘This was the best choice I had left.’

Failing the exam was tough, but what opened her eyes was her royal family’s political meddling against Shirone.

‘If I win, I’ll join the elite class too.’

In any country, a beauty queen receives political privilege, often linked directly to the monarchy’s influence.

“So… are you really a royal? Do handsome guys throw themselves at you?”

Pony saw no need to respond. Just as she was about to reopen her book, a hand entered her view.

She looked up to see Miss Arachne, Barho Langi, greeting her with a bright smile.

“Nice to meet you. I’m Barho Langi.”

Arachne had produced many previous winners, and Langi was a top contender this year.

“Alfred Pony. Nice to meet you… I guess.”

“Haha, straight to the point. You really are a mage.”

Everyone probably had the profiles of all the participants, but to Pony, this felt like a jab.

“I’m not a mage. I didn’t graduate.”

Yet stating the fact only proved she still viewed herself through the lens of magic.

“I know. Still, I think you’re pretty cool.”

Langi had a way of making people feel at ease.

“Hmph.”

Sensing goodwill, Pony softened, and Langi pulled over a chair and sat in front of her.

“You went to Alpheas Magic School, right? Then you must know Shirone?”

For the first time, emotion flickered across Pony’s cold face.

“How do you know Shirone?”

“Of course I know him. Who doesn’t know the Star of the Ivory Tower?”

“Well… that’s true.”

The Ivory Tower might be obscure to the public, but to a mage, it was sacred ground.

“What’s he like, Shirone?”

“What’s he like?”

Resting her chin on her hand, Pony recalled their school days, her eyes clouding with sorrow.

“We weren’t that close. Just exchanged a few words in the cafeteria. We fought more often than not.”

“You fought Shirone? No way!”

Langi was oddly delighted.

“Back then, we were rivals. But things went south after the final exam. Shirone’s from a commoner background.”

She added,

“Of course, magic doesn’t care about status. It’s a world where only skill matters.”

But her sharp eyes dimmed again.

“My family—our royal house—made a mistake. They tried to pressure the school into failing Shirone and passing me. Just because he was a commoner.”

‘Politics…’

Do they mass-produce these people in a factory or something?

“I was ashamed. So I stayed out of sight. The scandal got worse. Eventually, I…”

Pony gave a bitter smile.

“I decided to end it all.”

Langi’s eyes widened. Miss Baiden leaned over and whispered urgently,

“Hey! You can’t say stuff like that out loud. Everyone’s listening.”

It could be a PR disaster. But Pony wasn’t fazed.

“I tied a rope to the ceiling and put my neck in it.”

No one responded, but all sounds in the room ceased.

“I kicked the chair and let gravity take me. But right as I fell—suddenly, I stopped.”

Langi swallowed hard.

“I slowly opened my eyes and looked down. Shirone was holding my legs. He’d caught me. He looked me straight in the eyes and said…”

Pony’s tone was calm.

“‘It’s not your fault, Pony.’”

Everyone was looking at her now.

“He wasn’t just trying to comfort me. He meant it. Even though he was about to lose not just valedictorian, but his entire qualification as a mage… he still said that to me.”

Pony still couldn’t quite believe it.

“What kind of person is Shirone? It’s simple. A mage. And the greatest mage of our era.”

“You still love magic, don’t you?”

“I wanted to keep going to the end. But now, I just want to believe in myself again. I think that’s the first step.”

An endless world of battles and competition, a cold frontier where no one appealed to emotions.

Yet looking back, that might have been the most comforting time in Pony’s life.

“I miss it. School…”

For the first time, a smile touched Pony’s lips, and the other contestants turned their heads to look.

‘Tch, can’t use that.’

If it had been a shameful secret, they could’ve exploited it. But something that dramatic only served to make Pony more captivating.

The Archmage Who Slays Dragons.

Langi still had the novel Shirone had left behind at the hotel.

“He’s an amazing person, Shirone.”

Pony laughed softly.

“Yeah, when he’s a mage… I can’t really deny it. But if you meet him in person, you’d be surprised. He’s just a total countryside boy most of the time.”

Their situations were different, but Langi felt like she understood what Pony meant.

A mage, huh… I wonder what he’s like when he’s casting magic.

Just as Langi was lost in her thoughts, the security team that had been surveying the area returned.

The head of security introduced Jaestin.

“This is a sniper specialist we’ve deployed for the emergency. With her, your safety is even more assured, so please relax and enjoy the competition.”

Only a handful within the royal capital knew about the terror threat.

“A sniper expert? Come on, who’s gonna shoot us from afar? If anything, they’d try to kidnap us.”

Jaestin spoke firmly.

“Evil doesn’t only exist in men. This is a precaution to ensure your safety—please cooperate.”

“Ugh, come on! They’re already controlling what we eat, drink, even how we breathe! Now this too?”

Secrecy was top priority—convincing them would be impossible.

“And now, let the greatest global celebration begin! The World Beauty Contest finals officially start!”

As the MC exited to thunderous applause, the opening performance began.

“Maya, nerves are poison, but don’t let go too much either. Trust me. You can do this.”

It was Maya’s turn.

“Introducing! A voice of outstanding talent from Tormia—please welcome Maya!”

She took a deep breath and stepped onto the stage, where she could see the entire audience. Her head spun.

Stay calm. You’ve been through worse than this.

She tried recalling her days at the magic academy.

That’s right. I’m not alone.

The sight of musicians setting up their instruments reminded her of Shirone’s team during hilltop sieges.

Drums—Iruki. Crimson-haired Amy on keys. Neid would fit right in with electric guitar. And on bass…

Shirone, always the one to center the team.

Someday… will we be able to perform together?

The intro of Light Rain, the song she had written, began to play. She could feel a thousand eyes focused on her.

“Whew! Good. I’m not too late.”

Shirone had arrived. The moment he sat down, Maya began stepping to the rhythm.

You did it, Maya. You made your dream come true.

She couldn’t hear Shirone’s voice, but the second he arrived and sat in the front row, Maya’s movements suddenly froze.

“W-What? What’s going on?”

Watching from backstage, Palmus instantly realized something was wrong.

Shirone…

At first, she thought it was a hallucination. But no—sitting in the VIP section was unmistakably Shirone.

It really is Shirone.

She could no longer hear the music, nor the audience’s cheers, not even her own heartbeat.
