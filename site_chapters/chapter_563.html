Chapter 563:

Hershey, who had recruited Kaiden, did not return to her room but left the dormitory.

She had detected the heartbeat of someone watching him from a certain distance for some time.

It was only after entering a quiet park that Hershey stopped walking.

Judging by the sound of the heartbeat, it was more likely to be a woman than a man.

"Come out now. Who are you?"

A girl emerged from behind the trunk of a street tree.

"Ho?"

It was an unexpected person.

"Eden…"

A devout follower of the Yore Church and a specialist in defensive magic.

As far as Hershey knew, she was not affiliated with any research group, making her an unlikely participant in the preliminary round of the Scramble Royal.

"What brings you to follow… no, summon me at night?"

Eden knew she couldn't follow Hershey.

"Let me participate in the Scramble Royal."

"The bigger the alliance, the better. But you're not part of the underground research group, are you?"

"I will be. If I take over Istas."

'So it's the upper echelon?'

Hershey's guess was half correct.

The reason Eden, a follower of the Yore Church, came to the magic school was solely because of Micaiah Gaold.

Once the president of the Tormia Magic Association, he is still considered one of the most powerful mages in the kingdom.

But he was a heretic who betrayed the Yore Church and abandoned God.

'Unforgivable.'

She recalled what Yora had told her before the second half of the graduation schedule began.

"Do not hate Gaold. He is a pitiful child."

Eden shuddered with displeasure at hearing Yora, whom she respected, defend a heretic.

"Why do you say that? He abandoned the Yore God. Isn't he a monster who worships violence and has committed numerous killings?"

"Yes, he is a monster. But he did his best."

"No. That is proof of his lack of faith. No matter what pain befalls us, the Yore God is protecting us."

Eden was recognized within the global Yore Church not just for blindly following God.

She was highly rational, understood the clash between reality and doctrine, and accepted human desires and limitations.

The combination of faith and logic was indestructible, making it impossible to persuade Eden.

"Yora is deluded by Gaold's strength. If he were just a worthless destroyer, would she still defend him? I know what pain he went through to get there. He has surpassed human limits. But in the end, he is just a heretic. I cannot tolerate a force that defends a murderer in this holy temple ruled by the Yore God."

"I also think Gaold's decision is regrettable. But he is being punished. And he will continue to be."

"Punished?"

Eden sneered at Yora.

"Do you think I am weaker than Gaold?"

Yora did not answer.

"I'm not talking about being the best combat mage. I have no interest in such barbaric titles. But I am stronger than Gaold. A heretic is just a heretic."

Eden's eyes were intense as she spoke.

"As long as the Yore God is watching over me, I have no enemies."

Eden's eyes were more intense than ever as she came out of her recollection.

'I will shatter the legend that began with Istas.'

Hershey, hearing Eden's rough heartbeat, smiled and said,

"Fine. I'll report to the boss. You can expect good news."

Hershey patted Eden's shoulder and disappeared into the darkness.

The first holiday after the start of the semester.

Shirone, Iruki, and Neid gathered at the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Group for a reunion party.

Although they only had snacks and drinks, they lost track of time chatting after a long time.

"The problem is always the group evaluations. Especially strategy and tactics. With three absentees, the last group has to do it with just two people."

With only 27 members, the last group of six had to exterminate monsters with just two people.

Although the difficulty is adjusted according to the number of participants, it was true that they couldn't expect the synergy effect of five people's firepower.

Iruki poked at Neid's complaint.

"That's why everyone is trying to raise their rank. You should hurry up too. If you just surpass Binder and Pisho, you can be in the fifth group."

"Sigh. The gap in scores is too wide…"

At that moment, the research group vibrated, and Istas started operating.

"What is it? A teacher? It's a holiday today."

Unlike Shirone, who was looking around, Neid's expression had already turned serious.

"It's here. Let's get ready."

As the mechanical device stopped operating, Shirone and his friends moved to a position to greet the guests.

A familiar voice was heard from outside the door.

"It's the underground research group."

"Come in."

As the people entered through the open door, Shirone blinked.

Most of them were top-ranked seniors expected to graduate.

Hershey, who entered with his hands behind his back, turned his body at a right angle and declared,

"I propose a Scramble Royal."

Neid, lounging on the sofa, propped his chin on his hand and looked at the people standing in a line.

The third-ranked senior Frings, seventh-ranked Kaiden, ninth-ranked Anchal, tenth-ranked Eden, thirteenth-ranked Hershey, and twenty-fifth-ranked Pisho, a total of six people.

Representatives of the underground research group had gathered in one place.

'It's war from now on.'

Scramble Royal.

A set of rules where the winner takes everything by competing under certain conditions.

It was an absolute rule that took precedence over school regulations in the underground research group, affecting even graduation exams.

Three members of the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Group and six members of the underground research group alliance sat facing each other across a table.

Although Shirone was not familiar with the underground rules, he could feel the completely different atmosphere from the graduation evaluation.

It was a matter solely for them, with no interference from teachers or school authority.

"No need for introductions. We know each other."

Hershey, representing the Golden Wheel, continued.

"Of course, you already know why we are here."

Neid arrogantly crossed his legs and said,

"Indeed. Then let's just set the schedule and rules."

'Schedule? Rules?'

Everything was still new to Shirone, so he just listened.

With a snap, Hershey placed an iron box on the table.

It was an unfamiliar object, but the reason it felt familiar was because it had the pattern of Henna seen in heaven.

"Let's start the Scramble Royal, boasting a 30-year tradition."

Thirty years meant it was before Miro was active.

As Shirone subtly observed, Neid explained.

"This is a tool called 'Judge.' By setting rules through mutual agreement and inputting them into the Judge, it rewards the winner and penalizes the loser. It's the perfect tool for competing with stakes because it can set rules for breach of contract."

Hershey added,

"Any rule is possible under mutual agreement. If you set a rule like 'death if the contract is not fulfilled,' you will actually die."

'Extrajudicial.'

The first word that came to Shirone's mind.

He also thought it was an ancient device from heaven.

The reason it didn't fit the expression of a weapon or armor was because he had seen the lifestyle in heaven.

It was a dangerous device that could cost lives, but considering the out-of-the-ordinary scale, it might have been a toy for angels or fairies.

"The game is, of course, 'Black and White,' right?"

"It hasn't changed in 30 years. There's a manual, so it's easy to start the match."

Although curious about the game, asking immediately could show weakness to the enemies.

"The alliance has six participants, as you can see. Match your numbers. We won't stop you if you want to do it with just three."

The number of participants was also determined by mutual agreement.

"Hmm."

Even Neid, who boasted about the elite few, was cautious this time.

He didn't want to involve non-research group members, but the alliance members were too strong.

'We definitely need allies.'

Hershey, Anchal, and Frings, as well as Pisho with insect magic and Eden with defensive magic, contributed to the perfect balance.

'And Kaiden… huh? Kaiden?'

Neid raised his eyebrows.

"Wait. What's going on? Why are there two representatives from the Golden Wheel?"

Although it was fine if they set the rules that way, he had no intention of agreeing to it.

"Don't misunderstand. Kaiden is not here as a representative of the Golden Wheel."

"What do you mean? Then…"

Neid realized something and stared blankly at Kaiden.

"Could it be…?"

"Yes. I am the president of the Women's Body Research Group."

Shirone was stunned by Kaiden's shocking declaration, whom he had considered the most honest among Fermi's group.

"The president of the Women's Body Research Group?"

Kaiden nodded as if he had nothing to hide.

"I've drawn quite a few nudes of Amy too."

Shirone frowned at the immediate provocation.

'Why bring that up suddenly…?'

Suddenly, he recalled Maya's painting in the red book, and Iruki, who had the same thought, said,

"You really can't judge a person by their appearance. Did you join despite knowing your identity would be revealed out of jealousy? Or are you going to set a rule for Shirone to give up on Maya?"

"Don't demean me with such cheap emotions."

Sure, here's the translation:

Kaiden leaned forward, glaring.

"What drives me is anger. Shirone, I will bring you the greatest pain."

Although his words were filled with hostility, Shirone did not back down.

"Come at me as much as you want. As I said before, I will show no mercy to those who hinder me."

As the tension rose, Anchal stepped in.

"I'll give you a week. Gather your members. We'll decide the detailed rules then."

Iruki interjected.

"No, there's something we need to agree on beforehand. To recruit allies, we need to outline the overall framework since they will also need rewards."

Anchal had anticipated that they wouldn't just let it slide, given it was at the graduation level.

"Fine, speak."

"What the alliance wants is for us to give up the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Group if we lose, right?"

"To be precise, to give up Istas. We don't care what happens to the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Group."

"Well, fine. We'll accept that. But I have one proposal."

Iruki exchanged glances with his friends and presented their condition.

"If we win, the entire alliance team must rank lower than our team until the graduation exam."

The alliance members' expressions hardened, and Shirone's eyes lit up.

'That's right. This is absolutely necessary.'

To match the level of the enemies, allies must be at least at the graduation level, but none of them were related to the underground research group.

No one would join the Scramble Royal at the cost of disrupting their graduation schedule.

However, if they could rank higher than those in Class One, it would provide a definite motivation.

"Hmm, rank…"

As Hershey pondered, Neid smiled and said,

"Don't pretend to hesitate. I don't think you'd hesitate over ranks when you haven't even graduated because of Istas. You've just easily secured the most difficult negotiation point."

Hershey also thought that negotiating the price for giving up Istas would be the biggest challenge.

'That's true. It feels like we've given it up too easily. No, it must be that difficult to find allies. Anyway, they don't seem to think they'll lose.'

The alliance felt the same way.

"Fine, I agree. Other representatives, speak up."

Anchal immediately said,

"I don't mind."

Kaiden didn't need to be asked, and the remaining members agreed, leaving Neid feeling a bit regretful.

'Hmm, did I concede too much? Pisho's rank is low anyway. Well, even if we can just bring down Frings and Kaiden, it would be a success.'

Hershey packed up the Judge and stood up.

"Then gather three more people. We'll finalize the details in a week."

"Sure. You won't have to go far."

Neid didn't lift a finger as he saw them off.
