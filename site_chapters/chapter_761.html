Chapter 761:

"The Law of Lawslayer isn’t perfect. As long as your target isn’t an idiot, they’ll try to avoid its causality."

Minerva spoke calmly.

“Cause and effect are endlessly intertwined. That’s what Axing distorts to the extreme. But if there’s an Axing that can cut through anything… then there must be one that blocks everything too, right?”

She crossed her index fingers.

“When those two collide, a new law is born—a law formed by the overlap.”

Lawslayer doesn’t simply enforce causality—it must interact with the target’s law as well.

“Statistically, circling the planet takes about four months.”

“Oh? That’s surprisingly fast.”

Compared to having to follow the threads of causality, it was a remarkably short world tour.

Minerva nodded.

“Yeah. It’s a powerful Axing. But sometimes it can take over a year. For example, what if the target of Lawslayer keeps moving nonstop across the world?”

It would become an endless game of tag.

“There was a case like that. The target was a diplomat. What I learned from that was no matter how fast Lawslayer’s causality is, it can’t beat someone who’s constantly on the move for a specific purpose. So what do you think I did?”

“You delivered it personally.”

“Correct. That’s why ‘resetting’ is important. You erase the target, then artificially design the event to happen.”

“But… is that really possible? Even if Lawslayer’s wielder dies, the law’s already been activated, right?”

“There is one way. I call it ‘target substitution.’ If you use it, you can reset the law.”

“Hm...”

Shirone fell into thought.

“It’s a pretty complicated weapon.”

“That’s what makes it an S-class Object. Not complicated—just so effective that the potential for amplification is huge. Take Armand, for example. Anyone can become stronger, but to unleash 100% of its capabilities, you need complete understanding.”

Shirone had fully mastered Armand.

‘And the only person who can truly wield Lawslayer best… is her.’

The one who created the weapon—Mirac Minerva.

“Alright. Let’s work together for a while. If I need to use Lawslayer, explain it to Uorin.”

If anyone could design an optimal plan from Minerva’s explanation, it would be the Empress of Kashan.

Meanwhile, as Shirone and Minerva were discussing Lawslayer, Jayce stood with a hollow expression.

“It’s… really possible?”

With the demons wiped clean from the landscape, the laborers were joyfully reunited with their families.

“If that’s true… then why… why on earth…!”

Why did my husband have to die?

“If someone has power like that! Then why? Why didn’t anyone save him?!”

Shirone turned his head toward Jayce’s voice, and Minerva’s gaze turned cold.

‘Possibility…’

Just by showing the possibility of saving everyone, Shirone had become something close to a god.

‘But you still can’t save everyone.’

Someone would always suffer, and that suffering would follow the karma of reincarnation endlessly.

‘This time it was me…’

Minerva had become a witch.

‘Tomorrow, it’ll be you.’

Whether from an incurable disease, a natural disaster, or a sudden accident—life is simply the endless passing of a bomb called “suffering.”

That’s why, from the end of time, the world looks like nothing but pain.

And still, Uorin is right.

‘Those who think they got lucky today won’t understand her.’

Jayce was likely one of them.

‘Do you realize? The Jayce standing here now…’

And Minerva?

‘She resents the world more than anything.’

Jayce cried out in anguish.

“Why me?! Why?! Everyone else is happy—why am I the only one suffering like this?!”

Shirone bit his lip.

‘Because of me.’

Because he had stood in Uorin’s way, this pain had arisen. And there would be more bombs to come.

‘I want to grow stronger.’

No—if he could just beg on his knees and change Uorin’s heart, he would.

But because he couldn’t do that, Shirone clenched his fists tightly and steeled himself.

“It’s not your fault.”

Minerva placed a hand on Shirone’s shoulder.

“It’s not Uorin’s fault either.”

A contradiction.

What happens when the Axing that cuts through all and the Axing that blocks everything collide?

The law says:

“It’s simply a state.”

A state of paradox.

“Just because we can’t comprehend it doesn’t mean there’s no result. This is the result. Accept it—and move forward.”

Minerva laid Jayce, who had cried herself to exhaustion, onto her lap and put the stem of her pipe to her mouth.

“Sleep for a bit. It’ll help.”

As the witch’s breath entered Jayce through her lips, her pupils softened in peace.

“If I leave her like this, she might make a bad decision.”

Floating Jayce with a spell, Minerva turned to Shirone.

“Let’s go to the capital. We need to wrap this up.”

The witch’s broom, Jet, swept Godan’s corpse off and shot in with incredible speed.

“Wanna ride together?”

There didn’t seem to be enough space with Jayce on board.

“No, go ahead. I’ll follow you.”

Even knowing Jet was a record-breaking aircraft, Shirone insisted on keeping up, which made Minerva laugh.

“Alright. But if you’re too slow, I’ll handle things my way.”

The moment Minerva leapt onto Jet, a powerful burst of gas launched it skyward.

With the shriek of tearing metal and a sonic boom, Shirone turned to Verdi.

As if she understood what he wanted to say, she nodded and bowed deeply.

‘Thank you.’

Steeling his heart that had nearly collapsed, Shirone looked to the sky and bent his knees.

Radiant Wings.

Wings of light extended tens of meters, and everyone watching widened their eyes in awe.

“T-That’s...”

For the mere act of flying, it was well over the required output. But to follow Minerva, this was the bare minimum.

As the pair of wings stretched toward the sky, a blazing beam of light crashed down at terrifying speed.

BOOM!

With a deafening roar, Shirone’s body pierced the clouds in under a second.

“My god…”

Moments later, another sonic boom echoed.

‘She’s still faster—even though I’m flying at Mach speed.’

Each time Radiant Wings slapped the air, Shirone was propelled hundreds of meters forward like a cannonball.

Only after doubling the flight cycle could he finally spot the trail of smoke left by Jet in the distance.

“Oh my? You really followed me?”

As if bumping into an old acquaintance, Minerva looked over and waved.

It was too loud to talk, so she and Shirone read each other’s lips midair.

“Are you bringing her?”

Jayce dangled like laundry, looking like she could fall at any moment.

“Don’t worry. Surprisingly comfy ride. As for Jayce...”

Minerva glanced down at the mountain range speeding by, then released Jet’s law.

“I’ll leave her here.”

Shirone quickly turned his head, but Jayce was already just a dot in the distance.

“She’ll be okay, right?”

“Who do you think I am?”

With no retort, Shirone turned back to the front—and Jet’s engine kicked into high gear.

“Loser grants a wish.”

Jet extended to ten meters, and Minerva began overtaking Shirone.

“Anything I want.”

She winked before shooting ahead like a streak of lightning, and Shirone’s competitive spirit flared.

“So that’s how it is...”

He didn’t have a wish in mind, but letting her win was not an option.

As the mass of Radiant Wings solidified and slammed the air like steel, Shirone launched forward like a missile.

‘There it is!’

The capital of North Aymond came into view.

The capital, Pasia.

The presidential palace where the head of state resided was a multi-functional fortress made up of 780 interconnected buildings.

Revolution Square was one of the largest man-made structures in the world, large enough to hold all 3 million residents of the capital.

“Squeezed in” would be the more accurate term, but Igor believed every citizen should praise him directly.

That was the mindset of the human Igor, but even now that the president had become a demon, that belief remained unchanged.

“Commander, preparations are complete.”

The 24th Legion Commander, Maraduke the Destroyer.

A man with strikingly handsome features and lavender hair that flowed to his shoulders—enough to turn any woman’s head.

Golden engravings traced the edges of his tight-fitting black armor, and a longsword leaned toward its master from the left of the throne.

“How many have gathered?”

“The total number of forces withdrawn from the Spiritual Zone is eight million.”

Though it was dozens of times larger than the military force of any nation, it still wasn’t enough to be considered a proper legion in Satan’s 2-billion-strong army.

“It’s more than enough to annihilate the humans.”

As Maraduke strapped the sword to his waist and descended the stairs, the four division commanders stepped aside in unison.

Despite the room’s perfect soundproofing, the glass windows trembled under the weight of the roar outside.

“I shall be the vanguard who brings ruin to the world.”

As he opened the window, the pent-up sound exploded inside like a cannon blast.

“Kill the humans! Kill the humans!”

A wave of madness echoed across the 8-million-strong army gathered beyond the presidential palace.

Shirone looked down at the capital of Pasia.

“...What is that?”

While most of the city streets were deserted, the area around the palace was swarming with demons, more numerous than ants.

Minerva spoke.

“The capital’s finished. Not even a trace of resistance. The Capital Defense Force must’ve been wiped out already.”

“Are there no survivors?”

“There probably are. But not near the palace. Anyone who got caught in that mess was devoured instantly.”

“We need to find the survivors.”

“That’s a fine goal. But now’s not the time. If those demonborn stationed at the palace cross the border, things will get ugly.”

They’d infiltrate society and begin operating covertly.

“Sure, the closed-off society allowed them to conduct experiments—but that also gives us an opening. For now, we strike first.”

“Strike? Just like that?”

Even at a glance, the number of forces gathered around the palace was well over five million.

“Where are we even supposed to start?”

Minerva curled her index finger.

“Riiight there.”

As Shirone followed her finger, Minerva’s body floated upward, separating from Jet.

“From beginning to end.”

The moment resistance kicked in around her body, Jet surged ahead and plunged toward the palace gates.

‘Is she actually serious?’

Taking on an 8-million-strong army with just the two of them was unthinkable by human standards.

“Snap out of it! We’re going in!”

Though she had detached from Jet, the massive inertia still carried Minerva toward the presidential palace.

SCREEEEECH!

At the supersonic crack of the air, the demons at the entrance looked up.

“A surprise atta—!”

Before the sentence could be finished, Jet crashed into the ground, sending the nearby demonborn flying into the sky.

“GAAAHHHHH!”

Remaining forces:

7,999,954.
