Chapter 670: Lord of Terror (1)

The "Jarred Brain" was Ra Enemy’s unique ability that awakened human senses to perceive the true form of the Under World.

A phenomenon of the Seventh Sense.

Under the overwhelmingly higher-dimensional sense, the original five senses became meaningless.

‘Where are you?’

The stray dog that had lost its sense of smell still wandered through hell.

‘Show yourself. Reveal yourself before me.’

Dragging a bag full of daggers behind him, Shagall trudged endlessly down the blood-soaked path.

He had already taken down dozens of Gallopers, yet their flesh did nothing to quench his thirst.

“Ra Enemy.”

Shagall had killed Ra seven times.

Without a doubt, he’d crushed its windpipe, even mangled the corpse beyond recognition.

‘Why can’t I kill it?’

The more times he killed Ra, the clearer the scent of the incident became—and paradoxically, the pleasure dulled.

By the seventh time, he’d lost his reason. He couldn’t even remember how he’d killed it.

—Because someone had already forgiven it.

Raiden’s words resurfaced.

‘Anyone can make mistakes because they’re human. If that’s true…’

Then humanity itself is evil.

If even the potential to choose evil counts as evil, then there’s no one in this world worth keeping alive.

‘Kill, kill, and kill again—’

When all possibility of evil is erased, the world will at last achieve perfect stability.

Thus, Shagall became a murderer.

Even knowing the final person he must kill was himself.

“What’s that now?”

At the sound of something being dragged, Kuan turned his head.

Even in the grotesque anomalies of the Under World, tension flashed across his usually composed face.

“Who are you?”

With a single motion of drawing his sword, he halted Shagall’s approach. And Shagall, too, sensed something.

‘He’s like me.’

A genius, yet twisted.

“How many have you killed?”

When Shagall asked how many corpses had been stacked to reach this level, Kuan smirked.

“One.”

As always, only the one standing in front of him mattered.

“You’re Shagall, aren’t you.”

Shagall didn’t answer.

“Bad luck for you. I don’t know how you ended up here, but I can’t just let you walk away.”

Kuan hadn’t formed any special bond with Etella, but if he had to choose a side, it would be hers.

“Huuuu.”

Shagall rolled his shoulders left and right.

A simple movement anyone could imitate—but it was infused with a deep mastery of pendulum motion.

Kuan felt the world sway.

‘So this is what it was.’

Always the one fooling others, he found himself intrigued even as he was caught in Shagall’s technique.

“Sorry, but…”

Kuan’s body soared like a hawk.

“That technique belongs to me.”

The pinnacle of asymmetry—Clown Pierrot.

The air warped as his movement twisted, and Shagall staggered, feeling like the world was tilting.

‘What the…?’

Startled for a brief second, he sensed a sinister bloodlust and leapt back.

Kuan’s sword grazed his shoulder.

‘Circus-type. And the strongest acrobat, at that.’

The cells Shagall controlled via schema burned hot.

How long had it been since he’d felt this vivid a desire to kill?

“Kuhuhu…”

As he landed, Shagall twisted his waist and hurled his bag into the air. Dozens of daggers rained down like a storm.

Within that storm, his arms moved faster than insect wings.

Kuan’s brow twitched at the vibration of the air.

‘Folding.’

A technique where part of the schema is compressed to amplify a specific function.

There were even more extreme variants like “crumpling” (over-face) and “tearing” (abandoning physical feedback), but those had harsh drawbacks.

‘This one’s tricky.’

As Kuan was infiltrating Shagall’s rear, his trajectory twisted, looping around him again.

Shagall grinned, swinging both arms.

“Come at me, acrobat.”

The daggers danced in midair, unleashing shells of rapid-fire slashes in every direction.

‘Tch, he had ranged techniques too?’

Judging that delay would be a disadvantage, Kuan unleashed twelve waves of external force and slithered into Shagall’s zone like a snake.

‘He’s here.’

Shagall’s eyes flared. At last, the greatest acrobat and the greatest juggler clashed in a contest of skill.

“The sense that breaks the boundary of space…”

Hearing the explanation of Bakji, Shirone asked again.

“What kind of sense is that, exactly?”

Meirei shook her head.

“There’s no way to explain it. The sixth sense, Time Burst, is the integration of the five senses—so ordinary people can sometimes feel it. But Bakji is the integration of the sixth sense. It’s impossible even to imagine for beings of only five senses.”

Jane added, “But technically, Shirone should be able to do it, right? Since he already fully integrated the sixth sense.”

Ordinary people might sense the seventh sense with help, and under special conditions, so might Shirone.

If so, it could be a major hint for escaping the Under World—but for the person himself, it was hard to even grasp the concept.

“Even if that’s true, how can I attempt something I can’t understand?”

Holding a sliver of hope, Shirone looked to Meirei, but she seemed to recall nothing further.

“What I can say for sure is that the Under World is layered over our own. It’s not that our world disappeared—it’s just that it’s separated by sensory perception.”

If higher senses saw both as one, humanity must have already been subject to the Under World’s interference.

Shirone recalled times he’d felt a chill while alone, without reason.

Can we truly say the visions of artists are merely born in their heads?

If somehow influenced by the Under World, then this place was the very source of human inspiration.

‘A single space containing past, present, and future simultaneously.’

That is Time Burst.

‘A single moment containing both existence and nonexistence.’

That is Bakji.

‘So if I can perceive both the real world and the Under World at once, I might escape. The question is how…’

While Shirone was lost in thought, the heart-like structure around them suddenly began beating rapidly.

“What’s going on?!”

The mercenaries jolted awake in panic, and the core members all rose at once.

“What’s that?”

A vertical slash tore space apart, and another master of the Under World appeared.

Three meters tall, clad in black iron armor, with a void inside his helm.

He rode a black horse with gouged-out eyes, and from the tip of his lowered spear, blue flames flickered like living fire.

“I remember now.”

Meirei trembled as she spoke.

“Lord of Terror, Igor. The reason the others died in fright… this is his territory.”

The Jarred Brain transformed human senses into raw exposure—and Igor’s terror directly attacked the brain.

Lupist asked, “Is he strong?”

“His rank is Division Commander. I don’t know his strength firsthand, only through the oracle.”

If the Under World’s hierarchy mirrored their own, that rank was not low.

“Laqtas Vera Imera!” (Submit to Fear!)

Igor raised his arm high and slammed his flaming spear into the ground.

"Huuu."

Kargin exhaled a long trail of smoke from his cigarette.

"How about it? Not bad for an old guy, huh?"

Joshua, having straightened her outfit and meticulously adjusted her breastplate, snatched the cigarette from his hand.

"Cut the crap and stay alert. I didn’t sleep at all thanks to you."

"You won't die from missing a night of sleep. And now that I’ve cooled off a little... hmm?"

As Kargin reached to take the cigarette back, he froze in shock—Joshua’s fingertips were gripping the burning stub, and it was smoldering away.

"What are you doing? Doesn’t that hurt?"

He slapped her hand to knock the cigarette loose, but she didn’t even flinch. She was staring intently at something in the distance.

"Leo..."

Her four-year-old son, who had died of heatstroke, stood at the end of a narrow alley, looking at Joshua.

"Mom... it hurts so much. My body really hurts."

"Leo!"

Joshua shot up and ran toward the alley, Kargin throwing on his robe and chasing after her.

"Wait! What are you—"

But he, too, froze in place when he saw the woman standing in the alley.

"Marianne?"

The woman he’d met during his rookie years, with whom he had once planned a future. She had died during a mission when a monster ambushed them.

Kargin had fled at full speed while she was devoured alive, and from that moment on, he had known that the rest of his life would be filled only with despair.

"You were so young... too young. I was terrified! I’m sorry! I’m sorry, Marianne!"

"Terrified? More than I was? Do you remember what I went through that day? Then watch closely!"

The scene from forty years ago unfolded in Kargin’s retinas—the horrific scene of that day laid bare.

"No, no!"

The fear buried deep in the vault of his mind was triggered.

"NOOOOO!"

"I’m not a coward! I’m not!"

Baiakon, clad in heavy armor, curled up his massive frame like a bear, trembling all over.

Memories of childhood, of being bullied and isolated, soaked the swordsman's mind in terror.

Everyone makes horrifying mistakes at some point in life.

Only the core members remained silent, watching the visions unfold on their retinas with solemn composure.

"What the hell is this now?"

Without bothering to analyze the current phenomenon, Rian slung his greatsword over his shoulder and walked forward.

"Murderer!"

Corpses rose from the surface of an endless sea of darkness.

"I accepted my defeat. I never once intended to stab you in the back. But you still cut me down without hesitation."

That’s right.

"To get stronger? Is that why you killed me? How many lives did your pride destroy?"

That’s right.

"My family starved to death. Because you killed me. It’s all your fault! You’re a murderer!"

Hundreds of corpses Rian had slain formed a ring around him, screaming accusations.

"Die! Pay for your life of mistakes! Repent with your death for all the lives your errors destroyed!"

The decayed, tattered corpses lunged at Rian with desperate movements.

"This is the tragedy you created!"

Rian scowled, then bared his teeth as he swung his greatsword in a wide arc.

The corpses were cleaved in half and fell to the ground, faces contorted in grief, hands reaching toward him.

"Why..."

Blood-tears streamed down from their rotted eyes.

"Are you not ashamed to live as a human being? After robbing so many of their lives, do you not feel even a shred of guilt?"

If Rian had truly been a cold-blooded killer, he would never have experienced these memories to begin with.

"It’s conviction."

Not something to be understood or analyzed.

"Whether they’re nightmares, wraiths, or really resurrected..."

Rian stomped down on the face of the weeping corpse. With a wet pop, the brain matter burst out.

"If I cut you down once, I can cut you down as many times as it takes."

Fwoooooosh!

As the sea of corpses screamed and rushed in from all directions, Rian plunged into their midst and swung his sword.

"Die! Die! Pay for it with death!"

"Wait for me in Hell, you bastards."

Rian’s face twisted in fury as he hacked apart already-dead bodies.

"I’ll die as many times as it takes—after I build the Kingdom of Conviction."

And behind Rian, who cleaved through the tsunami of corpses, a massive path was opening.
