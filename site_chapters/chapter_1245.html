Chapter 1245:

As Shirone’s group reached the exit of the hall, Cain turned around.

“This is it. Follow this path and you’ll see the church. Gyorgy is there.”

They looked beyond the door.

Outside the Hall stood a beautiful forest, and above it rose a steeple with a bell.

Cain continued.

“I built the Melkidu system, but the real owners of this place… are them. The Church of Satan.”

Nade asked, “What are you planning to do?”

After all, he was the first murderer. It would be reckless to just leave him alone.

“Don’t worry. I may be evil, but like I said earlier, I’m more like a scholar who studies evil.”

Cain stepped back and said, “I’ll be watching—the final battle between good and evil, born from me and my mother. If evil wins, I won’t need to hide in a place like this anymore.”

“And if good wins?” Iruki asked.

Cain thought for a moment.

“…Who’s to say good ever really wins?”

Leaving behind those cryptic words, Cain disappeared into the corridor.

Shirone turned to his friends.

“This is it. From here on out, it’s not Melkidu’s system anymore. It’s a real-world battle.”

Iruki, Nade, and Eden’s eyes sharpened.

“Let’s go.”

From a distance, the Satanic monastery didn’t look different from any other.

But as they got closer, the ornaments, statues, and murals were grotesque.

“Disgusting,” someone muttered as they passed a fountain.

‘Usually it’s a little kid peeing…’

But here, it was a squatting pregnant woman screaming, hands on her swollen belly.

“Ugh!”

A fountain of blood spurted.

Nade flinched but quickly turned with a cold glare.

The blood reeked, but none of them stepped back.

“Gyorgy.”

The high priest of the Church of Satan emerged through the chapel doors, flanked by his four demon generals.

Gyorgy, Gultan, Marit, Euphrapus.

Specialists in anti-Yahweh combat.

And beside them was the person Shirone had come to find.

“Seina…”

Her eyes, already swayed by the Church of Satan, gleamed with a red hue like blood.

Gyorgy said, “So, you’ve finally come. How was the tour of evil’s sanctuary? Not bad, right?”

“Give Seina back. That’s all I want. There’s no point in fighting anymore.”

Gyorgy, too, knew the end was near.

“You’ve come far. Seems your time in Melkidu wasn’t wasted. But you’re still wrong.”

He opened the Bible of Demons.

“Askar Covenant, Chapter 7, Verse 2. Turn away from the heavens, and crawl below. Lust lies there.”

His shadow split into two massive hands that reached toward them.

“It nullifies light…” Shirone realized, adjusting with Hand of God to balance the energy.

Then Marit, one of the Satanic council members, stepped forward.

“Emblem—(Mark of the Demonic Flow).”

It was a form made of concentrated human demonic energy—so obscene it was hard to look at.

Eden furrowed her brows.

“I don’t know what that is, but I hate it.”

Still, after learning about the birth of good and evil, she understood the sensual undercurrent.

“Is that the form of the emotion Lilith felt when she crossed the forbidden line…?”

Shirone, on the other hand, focused on the emblem itself.

“It collects the energy of Satanic cultists. Then it’s fired using Euphrapus’ ability…”

He recalled the time the Emblem’s spear pierced his side, breaking through the Hand of God.

A weapon that rivals Yahweh…

Because it wasn’t made by demons—but by human emotion that rejected Yahweh.

“Mika.” Yes.

Melkidu’s core was reality itself, and therefore could connect with Mika.

“How many Satanic cultists are there?”

—As of now, 1,273,018,388 followers worldwide.

Back then, the spear’s power was equivalent to about 30 million people.

“This time, I won’t survive it.”

Even if he lived, the divine aspect of Yahweh would be torn apart.

—Due to the emotional plague, Satanic cultist numbers rose steeply into the 1.1 billion range. However, due to a recent wave, growth has stalled.

The wave of In.

Euphrapus, the elder, spoke.

“Yahweh, admit your defeat. You couldn’t stop the emotional plague or the shift in laws. The world isn’t ruled by good and love—it’s ruled by evil and utility. Why don’t you understand?”

Iruki snapped back.

“You can’t defeat utility either. If this continues, everything ends.”

Gultan smirked.

“Doesn’t matter. If it ends, we go with it. If someone stops it, whatever. You’re the ones with regrets, not us.”

As the Catacomb members sneered, only Gyorgy looked visibly displeased.

He said,

“Yahweh…”

The one who abandoned me.

“This is your last chance.”

As if on cue, Seina stepped forward, standing directly in front of Shirone.

“She has received Satan’s baptism. She is closer to us than to any human.”

Eden shouted, “Don’t be ridiculous! You brainwashed her!”

“And you didn’t?”

“What?”

Gyorgy said little more.

“If you want to save Seina, do it. But you’ll die to the Demonflow Spear.”

Nade spoke.

“Hah! You think we’ll just let that happen?”

“Who knows. What I do know is that time is short. If you lose here…”

Gyorgy opened the Demon Bible once more.

“There is no Ultima.”

At that moment, Seina charged forward, and Shirone’s team scattered left and right.

Damn it…

1 hour left until the end of humanity.

The final legacy dream.

Though Yorahan’s spirit remained in Dreamo, his body continued to wander the mortal world.

How many years had passed?

“Sanji! Sanji!”

Their journey had twisted and turned across the world until it finally reached a secluded village deep in the eastern mountains.

“Kyaaaa! I’ll get my revenge! You filthy scum! How dare you kill me? You think you can?!”

The face of the seventeen-year-old girl was distorted with rage, and her cheeks were lined with popped capillaries.

“Sanji! Please, snap out of it!”

As her mother sobbed in despair, the village’s elder woman frantically shook a rice jar.

“Oh guardian spirit of the land, please help this child! Deliver her from the grip of the evil ghost!”

“Grraaah!”

Sanji’s mouth twisted into a broken grin as a sickening laughter crawled out of her throat.

“Kehehehe… kehehehehe!”

Terror filled the old woman’s eyes.

“Gyaaah!”

She collapsed as though she’d seen a vision, eyes glazed as she muttered.

“Such an evil spirit…”

“Someone, do something!”

Sanji’s mother cried out desperately, but the villagers gathered in the yard remained frozen.

“Even the old woman with the strongest spiritual sense ended up like that. How can we deal with a ghost?”

“Then… my daughter is…”

“Excuse me.”

Everyone turned to see an unfamiliar figure clad in iron armor, standing silently.

“Aaah! A demon!”

“There’s a dark presence here. If it’s not too much trouble, may I take a look?”

Though the voice was male, it had an androgynous tone—calm, with no trace of malice.

“Please help us!”

Though fearful, the mother was willing to grasp even the faintest hope to save her daughter.

“She’s been possessed! Please, please…!”

Clank, clank.

With heavy steps, Armand entered the worn-down house.

“Keheee! What now?!”

She had traveled the world and soothed many evils, but this time was different.

Evil evolves… This is an endless fight.

Armand spoke.

“Leave that girl. The place where you belong lies beyond the river of fire, beneath a sky of brimstone.”

“Kekekeke! Spare me the nonsense! You and your little lover buried me alive—and now what?”

A grudge spirit…

A demon caught between the world of the living and the beyond, bound by a deep-seated resentment.

“I understand your pain. But it happened long ago. Those who wronged you are nothing but bones now.”

“What do you know?! My rage, my hatred…!”

Armand placed a hand on Sanji’s chest.

Small World Resonance.

“Gyaaaaagh!”

Sanji let out a horrible scream that startled the onlookers—until she slowly drifted into slumber.

“Ah! My daughter!”

As her mother crawled toward her, Armand said calmly:

“It’s not over yet.”

She murmured and stepped into the spirit realm to confront the grudge spirit directly.

In that dark, vast mental space—

“Kihihihi… So you’re dead too. Can you possess others like I can?”

The grudge spirit had the form of a rotting body, with dozens of tentacles where arms and legs should be.

No doubt a manifestation of the terror and desperation of being buried alive.

“Well, that’s perfect. The living keep trying to exorcise me. Now this body is mine.”

As tentacles slammed down into the void and reached Armand, the spirit’s one good eye widened.

“What…?”

Across from her was a woman sharpening a blade—and beyond that, a host of nightmarish creatures.

“You’ve come.”

Though grotesque in form himself, the grudge spirit shrank back in fear at the sight.

“What the hell are you…?”

“Let go. Cross the river of fire. The Lethe of forgetfulness will give you peace.”

“Let go?!”

The spirit’s face twisted in fury.

“Who are you to tell me that?!”

And as he lunged, tentacles stabbing forward, a wave of overwhelming emotion crashed into him.

“Ugh!”

“Forgive them.”

Armand’s voice was gentle.

“And I will forgive you.”

She accepted the spirit’s rage and obsession calmly, within that vast mental space.

It was a vile, nauseating process—but she bore it with a smile.

Isn’t that right, Yorahan?

When the connection dissolved, her spirit returned to reality.

“It’s over. Your daughter is safe now.”

As the armored knight rose and turned to leave, the villagers dropped to their knees.

“Oh heavenly one! Protect our village!”

Heavenly one…

From within the gaps of the armor, Armand extended the tentacles that had once belonged to the grudge spirit.

“Gyaaah!”

The villagers recoiled in terror, parting instinctively.

“I am but a wandering soul.”

And so, more centuries passed.

“Haa… haa…”

On a barren plain devoid of a single blade of grass, Armand fell to one knee.

This is my limit.

She had consumed demons to grow stronger, but now she could go no further alone.

“I… shall become a sword.”

She engraved the truth into herself: she would now become an object.

“The one who possesses the demon sword shall gain the world. Saints, villains, even ghosts may wield me…”

Please.

“…Let it be a good person.”

That quiet prayer would drift on the wind, guiding her, one day, to Shirone.

You did well, my love. I think… I can finally let you go.

Farewells to one’s beloved are always steeped in sorrow.

As the Adamant Armament was released, a single demon sword embedded itself firmly in the cracked earth of the wasteland.

And then, the kneeling corpse crumbled to dust and scattered on the wind.

Goodbye…

Yorahan.
