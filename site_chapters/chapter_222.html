It sounded like viscous liquid splashing.

The physician turned his head and stared blankly at Rian's arm, just like everyone else.

The blood flowing down from his shoulder was forming a pattern.

This was impossible. Even the greatest healing magicians couldn't alter the form of blood.

For three days after returning from Heaven, Shirone and his friends received the utmost hospitality from Jis and Yuna.

They averaged 16 hours of sleep a day, and their routine consisted of sleeping, eating, and then sleeping again.

When they gathered for dinner in the living room in the early evening, Shirone would recount their experiences in Heaven.

But he kept quiet about the final war.

Rian frequently broke things with his right arm due to the immense strength he couldn't yet control. Tess explained that it would take time for him to develop the fine muscles needed to precisely control his newfound power.

Tess cleaned and maintained the drone whenever she had time. She never let it out of her sight, and even while sleeping, she would stroke it, making eerie noises that frightened Amy and Arin at night.

Canis and Arin spent a lot of time talking alone. While it seemed that Canis had obtained what he desired from heaven, he didn't share what that was with anyone. However, he did confess that he was the one who had reduced Kadum's lifespan.

Amy wandered around with the ancient spirit Jack O'Lantern lit up. When she commented on how cute the bouncing flame was, Rian said Amy was even cuter.

When the boys and girls hung out separately, Amy often stared at Shirone for long periods. However, true to her meticulous personality, she never let her friends catch her.

Shirone often sat on the sofa, lost in thought. At such times, the face of Archangel Ikael would come to mind first.

The insight from Ataraxia was still vividly etched in his mind, retaining its warmth.

All those who went to Heaven achieved their desired results, but none received as great a gift as Shirone.

Canis threw a tantrum and demanded to know the secret, but Shirone genuinely didn't know how to explain it.

'Ikael... Are you doing well?'

But at the last moment, Kariel could not resist Ikael's advice. As long as she took the side of humanity, the final war would not occur.

On one hand, he wanted to meet her again. Thinking of the grumpy yet soft-hearted Peope made a wave of longing wash over him.

“Shirone, let’s go to sleep. It would be best to get some rest today.”

Amy stood there in her pajamas, looking beautiful in the moonlight.

Both had been feeling the need to talk about something for the past few days. But in the end, they just smiled at each other, resolving the tension for the moment.

“Right, let’s go to sleep.”

Shirone entered the room where the boys were sleeping. In the small attic-like space, Rian's snoring echoed. Next to him, Canis slept with a pillow pressed over his ears.

Shirone sat in the corner, staring into the darkness. A shiver ran through him as he thought that everything he had experienced was real. Starlight filled his eyes as he smiled tensely.

That night.

“Brother! Kaios! Kaios!”
