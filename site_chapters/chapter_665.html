Chapter 665:

Through the Ultima System, Shirone interpreted Meirei’s language—and a chilling shiver ran down his spine.

She hadn’t identified anyone specifically, but it felt like Ra Enemy was whispering directly to him.

‘The hell within me.’

That’s where Ra Enemy was waiting.

The interpretation left room for nuance, but it was clearly a critical clue for passing the Ivory Tower’s test.

“What exactly is the ability you call ‘God’s frequency’?”

“Theraphos, the god worshipped in the Kingdom of Gallon, is a conqueror of countless universes. According to their doctrine, he came from a place far beyond the universe we live in.”

It wasn’t something Shirone could dismiss as myth—not when he himself had once visited Heaven.

‘Could they be Gaians?’

It wasn’t an unreasonable thought—but he shook his head.

‘No. It can’t be them.’

The Gaians had already left the Photon System, and with the departure of the Goffin, their history as a species had come to a close.

“Theraphos delivers divine revelations through dreams. I started receiving them when I was seven.”

Meirei spoke, gesturing with her hands.

“The place I see in the dreams is always the same. It’s dark. Nothing is visible—until a vertical slit of light opens, and within it, the silhouette of a god.”

She lowered her hands.

“When I wake up, I remember almost nothing. Just a few fragments of information. The clergy collects that information and delivers it to the people of Gallon, and they follow it.”

“But in the end, it’s still just a dream, right? How can you be sure it’s real?”

“Because religion isn’t about proof. Even in Gallon, there are heretics rebelling against the revelations. The papacy is powerful, so it isn’t much of a threat—but the real issue starts here.”

Meirei brought everyone's focus back.

“The reason I became a heretic and left Gallon is because my ability exceeded the Pope’s authority. One night, just like always, I felt I would receive a revelation. The dream came. That dark space. And when the door of light opened…”

Her expression darkened.

“I woke up.”

“You… woke up from the dream?”

“Maybe it was still a dream. But every part of me told me it was real. That’s when I realized—those dreams I thought were divine messages were actually real experiences.”

Shirone swallowed hard.

“The door of light opened fully, and they came. I don’t remember what they looked like—it’s all blurry. They studied me closely and began to communicate using strange signals. I didn’t understand it then, but now I think it meant something like, ‘This one is unusual.’”

Meirei’s voice began to tremble.

“They did a lot of things to me. I screamed in fear and eventually blacked out. When I came to, I was lying on something like an operating table. I cried for help, and they said again, ‘Truly unusual. Why does she wake up?’”

She calmed herself again.

“Then I woke up in my own bed. But by then, I already understood what was real.”

“It still could have been just a dream, couldn’t it?”

Meirei tilted her head and lifted the hair above her ear.

In the area where no hair grew, there was a faint surgical scar.

“I checked as soon as I woke up. I don’t know what they did to me—but I think they implanted something.”

She pressed her hands against both ears.

“That’s when the ability started. When I block my hearing, I can pick up sounds on specific frequencies.”

That was the true identity of Meirei’s Regulation Exception: God’s Frequency.

“Of course, covering my ears doesn’t completely block out sound, so some noise gets mixed in. I think if I cut my ears off, I’d hear more clearly. But Mr. Lupist said no—because I wouldn’t be able to communicate with others.”

Even Shirone agreed—it would be a waste to lose her ability to communicate for slightly better signal clarity.

“Did Mr. Lupist tell you about me?”

“He helped me defect from Gallon to Tormia. He said that if I worked with you, there might be a chance to eliminate Ra Enemy. And maybe… maybe the reason I have this ability is because it was designed by Theraphos.”

Shirone thought that last part would be better discussed with Lupist himself.

“I understand. Thank you.”

Meirei didn’t say anything else. She already knew she’d passed, so she left quietly.

Aria took off her glasses.

“What an unbelievable story. Ra Enemy, Theraphos—all of it.”

It was all information completely beyond the reach of the average person.

“But it’s real. If we don’t stop it, everything we know could be at risk.”

As an official of Tormia, Aria understood the gravity of the matter.

“Right. For now, it looks like we have enough to form the mercenary unit. Let’s notify the successful candidates and hold a meeting this evening.”

That night.

The key members gathered in Shirone’s room.

With Rian and Aria already present, Etella and Kuan entered one after another, followed by Meirei.

Lastly, applicants 28 and 29 entered and removed their masks—unsurprisingly, no one was shocked.

“You selected well. The list of successful candidates looks solid.”

Lupist, praising Shirone first, began to explain.

“You probably already have a rough idea, but there are two core objectives to this mission. First: seal off Radum. Second: uncover Ra Enemy’s intent, and if possible, eliminate him.”

Etella raised her hand.

“Where’s Shagall?”

“We’re still searching on the association’s end, but his whereabouts remain unconfirmed. He’s either left the kingdom—or he’s in Radum.”

“He’s in Radum.”

Shagall was the sensory-type of the Great Purification.

If he had followed Ra Enemy’s trail all the way to Tormia, there was only one place he could be heading.

“I’ll handle Shagall. There’s something I need to confirm.”

She might have to kill him—but Etella never once used the word “revenge.”

“Permission granted. Just don’t forget—he’s still a valuable asset. I don’t need to spell it out.”

“I do have a question about that.”

Shirone spoke up.

“You said ‘eliminate if possible’—but what does that entail?”

“I’ll explain now.”

Lupist stepped forward where everyone could see him.

“We’re protecting Venezia, the head of Spectrum. According to her, Ra seems to influence Radum’s organization by manipulating the past. That’s why the kingdom has taken the risk of shutting it down.”

That much made sense.

“So how do we find Ra? First, Ra only exists in past events. Shirone, how would you define the past?”

“Um… events that occurred before a specific point in time?”

“Not wrong—but I want to define it more precisely. The past is sensory memory.”

“Ah…”

“Everything we experience is reconstructed by the five senses and recorded in the brain. Therefore, Ra Enemy’s manipulation of the past also can’t extend beyond the range of human sensory experience.”

“Because it would exceed human limits.”

Lupist nodded.

“Now imagine: what if past sensations could be felt in the present? Then they are real. After all, time is just the brain’s way of interpreting reality.”

Which is why something like the Sibu Temporal Expansion Technique is possible.

“Venezia, using the abilities of the Tri-Brained race, pointed this out. In short, depending on how it’s perceived, the past can absolutely become reality. So Ra isn’t just some phantom with no form. He exists. In the past.”

Lupist raised his index finger.

“He simply exists in the past. Shirone, Ra may have been in this very room just now. Do you agree?”

“Yes.”

“Then why can’t we be sure?”

“Because we have no sensory memory of it.”

“Exactly. Because it’s not in the present—or more precisely, because we don’t perceive it in the present—we conclude it doesn’t exist.”

He lifted the cup on the table.

“Now imagine that humans aren’t what we appear to be—but are actually brains floating in a special liquid. How would you feel?”

Shirone tried to picture it.

“It doesn’t matter if it’s true. The point is—if it were true, we’d have no way of knowing. The brain is everything. So, does this cup truly exist? Or is it just a fabrication of the brain?”

No one answered.

“When dealing with Ra, distinguishing between existence and nonexistence is meaningless. If we can feel Ra Enemy through our five senses—then he exists to us.”

‘I see… So when they say to find Ra…’

Shirone finally understood the intent behind the Ivory Tower’s exam question:

—Can you prove existence and nonexistence?

That was the question every candidate of the stars had to answer.

Kuan asked, "Bringing the sensory memory into reality to kill Ra—the idea is good, but how exactly do we make it real?"

"Jane," Lupist said.

At his signal, Jane handed him a box.

"This is an artifact specially procured with royal funds. Inside this box is a clue to Ra’s power."

Rian, who had been spaced out like a fool up to that point, showed his first sign of interest, eyes lighting up.

"What kind of artifact is it?"

"Nemesis."

The moment Lupist said the name, Shirone slapped his knee.

"Ah, Nemesis!"

It was the A-class artifact Uorin had told him about during the paternity incident in the Kingdom of Kazra.

"So you’ve heard of it. It’s quite famous in this field. When someone comes into contact with Nemesis, their imagination manifests into reality within a 20-meter radius."

Because it often led to ruin for those consumed by their desires, Nemesis was one of those artifacts that frequently changed owners.

"There’s no need to explain how imagination becomes reality anymore. In any case, the Association suspects that Ra’s ability functions similarly to Nemesis."

Etella spoke. "So we’re planning to turn Ra’s power against him. But doesn’t Nemesis only manifest reality for the one who touches it?"

"That’s why we broke it."

When Lupist opened the box, it revealed eight rings made of a pinkish, jade-like material.

Shirone pointed to them with an intrigued expression. "Those are... Nemesis?"

"Yes. When we first acquired it, we wondered what would happen if two people touched Nemesis simultaneously. The experiment showed that imagination is shared—because if it’s perceived by the senses, it becomes reality."

Lupist picked up one of the rings.

"Also, the strength of Nemesis’ power appears to depend on the concentration of a special compound within the ore. We discarded nine-tenths of the original mass to process it into ring form—for ease of transport and wear. From the moment you put on the ring, you must control your mind. But of course, only those confident in doing so have gathered here. Once the mission is over, the items will be reclaimed."

Kuan spoke. "I understand using Meirei’s ability to draw Ra into reality. But we’re still lacking in sensation. Relying solely on hearing to track him isn’t enough. How exactly do we kill Ra?"

Shirone muttered as something came to him. "Time Explosion?"

As everyone turned their eyes toward him, Lupist looked at Shirone and said, "Exactly. You."

Shirone’s Time Explosion allowed him to perceive the past as the present, and that meant he could act as the touch capable of making contact with Ra Enemy.

"When Meirei locates him—you kill him."
