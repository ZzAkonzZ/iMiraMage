Chapter 1272:

The Supreme Court of Theraphos.

Though the Chief Justices had departed, a few judges remained who refused to back down.

“This is too hasty! The battle between good and evil isn’t over yet! Doesn’t the last light still remain?”

The reason Theraphos had rushed to confirm evil’s victory was because of the fate of the universe.

“If we waste time here, the universe really will close! Judges, is that what you want?”

“It hasn’t happened yet. The arbiters of the universe must judge based only on the present.”

“Then look at the present. What is left for humanity? No user can survive on this land. Civilization has vanished—resources, life, all extinct!”

Only suffering remains.

“Objection! I formally appeal!”

“There’s no time! All enforcers, carry out the Supreme Court’s final ruling! Any who resist will be detained!”

The doors of the court burst open, and enforcers equipped with plasma swords swarmed in.

“Judge! Are you really doing this?!”

A formal appeal against the Supreme Court's ruling—this was unprecedented even in the history of Theraphos.

“...There’s no other way, is there?”

Even if they possessed standards beyond human comprehension, they had not reached the 11th sense.

“We must follow the grand principles of Theraphos, set by the Creator. We are here to restore balance.”

They, too, were creations of the gods.

As silence fell, the enforcers surrounded the opposing judges.

“Detain them.”

The Prison of Balance—a space that isolates anything that defies the balance of the universe. All of them vanished.

Over the scorched earth, a burning dust storm blew.

“Haa… Haa…”

Cutting through the hazy curtain, a woman with her nose and mouth wrapped in cloth appeared.

It was Miro, the last guardian of light.

“Please save me!”

Hearing a voice carried by the wind, she stopped walking as a young girl ran toward her.

A survivor.

Though 99% of humanity had perished, some had still remained on the surface.

Not many, certainly.

“Hehehe! Get her!”

A wagon drawn by skeletal horses, carrying demons, was hunting the girl.

They could’ve killed her already, but they seemed to be toying with one of the last remaining playthings.

“Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara.”

As Miro’s divine manifestation rose high, the demons in the wagon looked up.

“Huh?”

From a shimmering gray curtain above, the divine form of Avalokiteshvara looked down at the earth.

“Lightning Strike.”

BOOOOM!

A vertical divine strike crushed both the demons and the wagon into the ground.

The girl groaned with dry lips.

“Ahh…”

“Are you okay?”

Whether the one before her was enemy or ally, dream or reality, there was only one thing to say.

“Water… Can I have water…”

Miro conjured water with magic.

Once her thirst was quenched, the girl’s eyes finally focused—and then she began to sob uncontrollably.

“Waaaah! Waaaah!”

There was no need to ask what had happened.

“Lucky… Do you know where the demons are gathering? Are there any other survivors?”

“No… I don’t even know where I came from. I wanted to die… It was too scary…”

At that moment, the wind of the storm shifted, and Theraphos warships appeared in the gray sky.

“What now?”

“Bearer of Light.”

The voice came from nearby. Miro turned her head, but all she saw was a flickering, transparent silhouette.

“A Chief Justice?”

When the stealth dropped, the figure of an alien lifeform appeared, and the girl shrieked in fear.

“Hiiiek!”

Miro asked calmly.

“What’s going on?”

“The verdict of the Supreme Court has been rendered. As of now, humanity is ruled to have lost the battle between good and evil.”

Given the state of things, it was understandable—but still felt unfair.

“This was your decision? Aren’t you the Chief Justice?”

“I issue the sentence, but the judgment follows the three principles of the Theraphos species. It was unavoidable.”

“You couldn’t stop it?”

“I could’ve stalled. But I judged this to be the better choice than staying to fight.”

Had he fought in court, the Chief Justice himself might’ve been imprisoned in the Prison of Balance.

“Quick judgment is fine—but why?”

“There are survivors attempting to overturn the situation. And… this is my personal belief…”

After a moment of thought, the Chief Justice spoke again.

“There’s merit to it.”

“Although the odds aren’t high. In fact, if they fail, the universe will collapse. Meaning humanity’s failure would bring about the extinction of the Theraphos race as well.”

“And yet you’re betting on us?”

“Ultima.”

The Chief Justice nodded.

“Theraphos is superior to humanity, but we never reached Ultima. And maybe… I saw the reason why. Because Ultima isn’t something achieved through intelligence alone.”

Miro pondered those words.

“What do you want me to do?”

“Endure.”

There was no time for lengthy explanations.

“Bearer of Light, you cannot defeat evil alone. Good and evil, emptiness and love—all stem from one origin. When one dominates, humanity loses direction.”

“Then how do we win?”

“Winning is also evil’s methodology. When good and evil, emptiness and love, are all perfectly aligned, humanity will be forced to make its final choice. You must hold out until then.”

Far in the distance, the Theraphos warship approached.

“Never forget—you are the Bearer of Light. If you fail to maintain that position, balance will tip. Love will lose its power, evil will rise, and emptiness will prevail.”

A Theraphos enforcer dropped its stealth and approached.

“Let’s go.”

“…Remember. Good exists to oppose evil, not to hate it.”

As they vanished, a blaring siren wailed in the sky.

The girl stared in a daze.

“…My God.”

Plasma flashes rained down on the earth.

BOOOOOOM!

Theraphos's planetary strikes were felt even by those sheltering underground.

Inside a room filled with residents of the Ivory Tower, the vibrations made hearts tremble.

“How rash.”

The players of the card game sat around a round table, dealing out hands.

“They want to end the war before the universe closes. It’s not a bad strategy. The demons will vanish too.”

“But that also means Theraphos is betting on emptiness’s victory, doesn’t it?”

Silence fell.

“Victory… Is that even possible? Good and evil, emptiness and love—they’re all absolutes. There’s no hand higher than that.”

Like the four suits of aces.

“This is a matter of combination. Good and evil can choose between emptiness and love. And emptiness and love can also choose between good and evil. The meaning changes entirely based on the combination.”

“That’s right!”

Gudion slammed the table.

“The winner of this game isn’t good, evil, emptiness, or love. They’re just making hands. The one who chooses the winner is someone else.”

“…So it’s not just four players.”

“Exactly. It was a five-player game from the start. The last hand to be revealed—that is the heart. And the last player we need…”

“…Is a human.”

Maice clutched his head.

“Damn it! I should’ve realized when Minerva bet her entire fortune. That wasn’t a variable—it was part of the game all along. We just didn’t know the rules.”

Of course, Minerva didn’t know either—but that’s what made the game valid.

Clunk! Just like planting a coordinate, Ness drove a dagger into the center of the table.

“One more! Anyone! Hurry!”

“May I join you?”

Even these gambling maniacs straightened up as the 3-Star mage of the Ivory Tower, Rampa, approached.

“Ah, Rampa.”

“I sold even my powers, and all I have left is money. That makes me perfect for this, doesn’t it?”

“Please, take a seat.”

Gudion explained the rules.

“We each build a hand. Place your bet on who you think will win.”

“Heh heh…”

Rampa laughed.

“Now that’s an interesting game.”

Then, Shura arrived.

“Let’s begin.”

Nodding, Shirone followed her up the stairs toward the surface.

"Ah."

Amy’s eldest brother Diane and second brother Ares were waiting for Shirone.

“Hello.”

Shirone had met Ares before, but this was his first time seeing Diane since he visited Amy’s home.

“It’s been a while.”

Shirone spoke stiffly.

“I’m sorry. I couldn’t protect her. But this time, I’ll definitely bring Amy ba—”

“Don’t push yourself.”

“Huh?”

“I don’t know much. About time, or the universe. But this much is certain—you're not going to retrieve a lost object. You're going to meet a living person.”

“My sister simply fought her own battle. So, no matter what happens…”

Diane lowered his head and continued.

“I just hope you’ll respect what Amy thought, and what Amy chose.”

Shirone thought to himself.

‘Of course… The Carmis family.’

“Yes. I’ll fight with everything I have.”

At the start of a journey on which he would stake his entire life, Diane’s words eased his tension more than he expected.

Ares gave a wink and a thumbs-up, and Shirone finally arrived on the surface.

It was his first time stepping outside since regaining consciousness—and it was far worse than he had imagined.

No—there was nothing left.

“This way.”

At the ruined altar, just as Nane had left it, stood a man with a horn on his forehead.

“Huama.”

Ranked fourth in the Council of Ten—the Diamond Yaksha, Huama.

He had guarded the altar ever since Veron passed away, waiting for this very day.

“You’re late, Shura.”

“How’s the situation?”

“Not good. I hear the roars of the demons, and there are strange flying crafts in the sky.”

His gaze sharp as he looked at Shirone’s face, Huama gestured with his chin and turned toward the entrance.

“Come in.”

Uorin and Kido had already arrived.

“Kido.”

Knowing what it meant for him to be here, Shirone felt even sadder.

Shura explained.

“Everything within the altar’s radius is part of the Iron Axis. But we chose this place to keep external interference away. While you’re reliving the past, we’ll protect Uorin and Kido.”

More precisely, they would protect their powers.

“This place won’t hold for long either. Once the Annihilation Wave of the Earth hits, it’ll collapse quickly. The resistance will hold out to the end, but…”

In other words, there wasn’t much time.

“I understand.”

Shirone walked up to Kido.

“I’m sorry. I’ve always just taken from you. I wish there was something I could give you in retur—”

“That’s not true.”

Kido shook his head.

“To find something in life worth devoting your entire being to… that’s a gift in itself. Don’t you think?”

There was once a time when all he knew was eating and breeding.

“I’ve received a lot.”

Even if it meant endless thirst.

“Thank you.”

He’d lived a wonderful life.

“Ki…”

Feeling like he might burst into tears, Shirone stopped mid-sentence and suddenly pulled the small goblin into a tight hug.

Kido smiled and said,

“We’re friends, right?”

Shirone nodded, and with a gentle push, Kido stepped aside and cleared the path.

Uorin was waiting.

“Come back safe.”

There was a hint of emptiness in her eyes, but the ferocity of old had been replaced by gentleness.

“Yeah.”

He realized just how much he owed her.

Interaction.

In the end, humans are fragile creatures who can only live by relying on one another.

“I’ll be back.”

But he would not rely on the gods.

Tachyon.

Shirone, too, had staked everything to plant a single thought into this universe.

"I will change it."

The future.

His consciousness unraveled into imaginary particles and vanished beyond time and space into the boundless void.
