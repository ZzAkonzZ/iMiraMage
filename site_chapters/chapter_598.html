Chapter 598:

Fermi waited endlessly for an answer.

‘If the event is twisted, Mother can live.’

But to Fermi, Yolga was more than just a mother, and denying her death felt like an insult.

So he wanted to know.

Why did Yolga have to enter the upper echelons? Why couldn’t such an exceptional mother return to her family?

“There are many times in life when you lose your way.”

Yolga finally spoke.

“But Fermi, it’s okay to ponder what’s right and wrong. When you stop doing that, the value of human existence disappears.”

“Even if I’m the worst villain in the world?”

Yolga gave a kind smile.

“Humans choose between good and evil; good and evil don’t choose humans.”

She placed her hand on Fermi’s grown-up shoulder.

“Always remember that you still have a choice. If you do, you can find your way again, even if you get lost.”

All parents protect their children, but Yolga’s words embraced Fermi’s entire being.

‘My mother. My only mother.’

No, she was the mother of the whole world.

“Did that answer your question? I hope it’s a fair exchange for the good items I received.”

“It’s more than enough.”

As Fermi turned to leave, Yolga hurriedly asked.

“Where are you going now?”

“Forward.”

Fermi turned his head and smiled.

“I’ve made my choice.”

Location: Supernatural Psychic Research Society

It was a fierce battle.

Mustang, who freely manipulated fire, ice, and air, seemed to have developed unique magic, while Anchal, the director of the Jincheon Astronomical Bureau, defended with all possible illusions.

Shirone’s Elysion cast magic on the space itself, pressuring Edgar and Nine, while Miro’s Kannon, now seemingly engulfed in madness, relentlessly attacked Yolga.

‘You’re strong, Miro.’

It wasn’t just a matter of skill; Miro’s very existence transcended humanity.

‘I understand why Goffin chose you.’

If Yolga had reached the state of Prajna from the lowest position in the world, Miro looked down on the world from a height beyond human thought.

‘I want to rely on you.’

Feeling reassured by Miro’s presence was entirely different from Yolga’s blind embrace of others.

‘If I am the mother of humanity…’

Miro was the father of humanity.

At the forefront of crisis, protecting humanity by sacrificing herself without showing any pain.

‘Yes, Miro. We are not wrong.’

But the world demanded that only one remain.

In the face of humanity’s end, what did humans need more, a father or a mother?

Yolga smiled contentedly.

‘You’ve grown well.’

A young man’s face flashed through her mind.

So it wasn’t the end.

“Miro, I can’t hold back any longer.”

The kindness disappeared from Yolga’s face.

Prajna-Break Maria.

“Really? Now it’s getting interesting!”

Knowing that Break Maria could destroy, Miro accelerated Kannon’s attacks.

After Anchal crushed Mustang with the Jincheon Astronomical Bureau’s hammer and Shirone defeated Edgar and Nine.

‘She must think she can’t waste time.’

That’s what Miro thought, but Yolga was smiling.

“What…!”

Just as Kannon’s hands flew in from both sides to crush her, a flash of light snatched her away.

“Grr!”

Released from the photon state, Fermi gritted his teeth as he slid across the floor and hit his head against the wall.

“Fermi?”

Yolga looked at Fermi in surprise.

He was covered in blood, with severe wounds all over his body.

“How did this happen…”

“Heh. The people targeting you are very strong.”

To prevent Yolga’s death, Fermi fought Mars and managed to take down 14 members.

Thanks to his numerous high-level spells, but he couldn’t avoid fatal injuries while eliminating the kingdom’s top assassination unit.

Laying Fermi on the floor, Yolga looked around the research society.

In front of Goffin’s door lay the bodies of Edgar and Nine, and beside the kneeling, bleeding Anchal was Mustang’s corpse.

Finally, she turned to Miro and spoke.

“Don’t let me disappear. Instead, grant me a favor.”

“No, don’t ask for favors. Don’t disappear. Just come out with me.”

“Dieee!”

At that moment, Mars’ captain Nickel entered the research society.

Covered in the blood of his fallen subordinates, his eyes were filled with the determination to complete his mission.

“I’ll kill you!”

‘Fool! That’s not Hexa!’

Unaware of the current situation, Nickel naturally saw the prince of Kazra as the child Miro was holding.

“No!”

As Yolga ran towards the child, the warning sound of the "Last Scream of the Yeager Family" rang out.

“Danger!”

A girl’s scream meant imminent death.

“Danger!”

She would die.

“Danger! Danger! Danger!”

She would die. Die. Die.

Ignoring dozens of chances to live, she moved towards a future filled only with death.

Nickel’s sword pierced her back.

“Gasp!”

Yolga’s head snapped back, and Nickel twisted the blade, pushing it further in.

‘I must kill the child!’

The trained soldier’s mind was as cold as a machine.

Thousand-Armed Kannon Strike.

Just before the sword tip reached the prince, Kannon’s palm flew in and separated Nickel’s head from his body.

Kneeling, Yolga fell, and Fermi stood up, clutching one shoulder.

“Mom…”

But Miro spoke first.

“Why?”

It was incomprehensible.

“This child is destined to be trapped forever! Why do you keep doing foolish things?”

“That’s also something to choose for oneself.”

Miro saw Yolga’s extremity.

“Miro, believe in us. Believe that we can protect the future with our strength.”

“Even if you say that, you won’t remember once you leave here. You’ll be erased! You foolish sister!”

“No, I’m sure we can remember.”

Yolga smiled with a burning vitality.

“You’re not foolish.”

Remember.

Miro clenched her fists.

She would remember what Yolga conveyed through her death.

Yolga closed her eyes in satisfaction, and her body fell back. Fermi hurriedly caught her.

‘Mom.’

He felt he had to say it.

“Mom…”

“Fermi, thank you.”

Yolga raised her hand and caressed Fermi’s cheek.

“Your choice changed the world. My proud…”

Her hand fell limply.

“My son.”

And so, Yolga left Fermi’s side.

Just like 19 years ago.

“Thank you, Mother.”

But he no longer grieved and sent Yolga off with a smile just like hers.

“Fermi! Get down!”

As Shirone shouted, powerful electricity surged around Fermi.

Hugging Yolga’s body, he leaped away as a dark figure grabbed the prince of Kazra and dashed to the corner.

“Ha! Ha!”

Bleeding from his head, Lycan gripped the child’s neck and glared at Fermi.

“Traitor. How dare you use me?”

“Get it straight. I didn’t use you; you were used.”

Knowing Fermi’s personality, Lycan asked bluntly.

“Where’s the object? Hand it over.”

“I don’t have it.”

Electricity flowed through Lycan’s hand as he gripped the child’s neck.

“If you don’t want to kill an innocent, bring the object.”

Knowing Yolga’s identity, Lycan also knew the child she protected with her life was useful for threats.

“Even if I wanted to, I can’t. They’re all destroyed.”

“Then find a way. That’s your specialty, isn’t it? Make one if you have to. Otherwise, this child dies.”

No one could take the child from the electrified Lycan.

“Lycan, you should think carefully. Making me angry won’t end well for you.”

“Heh, maybe before. But not anymore.”

Lycan’s gaze shifted to Yolga’s body.

He thought replicating objects using the incident of his mother’s disappearance was everything.

But when he opened the lid, he found Fermi was human after all.

“You idiot. Missing your mommy’s embrace…”

It was a natural misunderstanding for Lycan, who couldn’t grasp how the complex events of the upper echelons would change the future or what Fermi truly wanted to protect.

“I don’t need to explain to you. But refusing me will make your life miserable.”

“Do you know, Fermi?”

Lycan’s eyes gleamed with malice.

“I’ve always hated you.”

With a crack, electricity burst, silencing the child’s cries. Lycan threw the body and used electric magic to escape the research society.

“Lycan!”

As Fermi shouted in anger and chased after him, Shirone quickly checked the prince of Kazra.

“He’s dead. Let’s change the event again.”

It was unfortunate that Yolga’s will was broken.

“Do as you please. I’m leaving.”

But Miro had to uphold Yolga’s teaching, the importance of choice for humanity.

‘I’ll carry your last memory, sister.’

To do that, she had to leave now.

“When I leave Istas, the closed loop will fully close. This will conclude the event.”

If the event twisted, Yolga’s death would unfold differently, and Miro’s resolve would become a non-event.

If Hexa was trapped in the dimensional wall, Shirone wouldn’t be here either.

“I don’t know when, but if we meet again, remember today.”

Miro crossed the threshold of the research society.

“Think carefully! If this leads to the worst possible event…!”

“What?”

Shirone’s eyes sharpened as he pressed the completion button.

“I’ll meet Goffin.”

“What are you talking about? How will you meet Goffin?”

Goffin definitely existed.

In the world before Miro entered and the events began at 12:00 AM.

‘Goffin, I don’t know what you’re thinking…’

Hearing the sound of Istas vibrating as it changed its structure, Shirone left the warehouse.

‘You must convince me!’

If she left Istas like this, time would be isolated, and only the events she experienced would continue to cycle.

With the exit in sight, Miro stopped abruptly.

“Don’t come. Whatever you’re thinking, don’t come here.”

It was a warning to Shirone, who had just arrived and was watching from behind Anchal’s illusion magic.

Rumble!

As soon as she finished speaking, Istas vibrated, and the structure began to change.

“You fool…!”

Turning around, Miro saw Shirone running towards her.

‘I will meet Goffin!’

It might be possible.

If he could reach 12:00 AM.

As Shirone’s illusion, burning with time energy, appeared, time began to flow backward.

The starting point of the 2-hour and 48-minute record.
