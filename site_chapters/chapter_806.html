Chapter 806: That Winter (2)

Reversing the world wheel is certainly possible for Yahweh. However...

If he was already trapped in the prison of balance, he should have been completely isolated.

"Not an error?"

A being free from the law, even Therapos, who guards the law, couldn't define him.

"It's not just about Yahweh's strength."

What this incident told Amanta was that perhaps this world isn't just a space for living.

In other words, it suggests that there might be a specific intention from the being who designed this world.

"Why does Hexa exist?"

Shirone couldn't answer that question.

"Anyway, fine."

When Amanta extended his hand, the wheel buried in the snow returned with a cold wind sound.

Tiriririri!

The law turning again.

"Even if the law acknowledges philanthropy, your actions still cause massive killings."

Minerva said.

"But we can't let extreme evil go unchecked. Even Therapos destroys civilizations when evil taints the world."

"That's why I want to stop it."

Amanta stopped the world wheel.

"With the Buddha absent, if even philanthropy fails to destroy extreme evil, this world will be drastically tainted by evil."

As a result, Shirone's philanthropy would cause numerous casualties in the world.

"We just need to eliminate extreme evil."

"Even if Gustav and the Ivory Tower wage a full-scale war now, it would be a difficult battle. Can you do it with Law-Killer? If I turn the world wheel, both good and evil will subside to some extent."

Assuming both Buddha and Yahweh are absent, Amanta could reduce the numbers of good and evil to about 30% of their current levels.

Then the world would be livable.

"Certainly, neutralizing both sides to delay destruction is one way. But that's also an extreme of balance."

Amanta looked at Shirone.

"I don't think Yahweh can handle it. If you break the balance, Shirone-kun, I will fight you anytime."

"It might be unmanageable." Shirone was firm.

"But I will manage it."

Amanta slowly shook his head but knew that further words were meaningless.

"Don't expect human emotions from me. I am one who realized the center of the universe. I only maintain balance." The world wheel spun rapidly in his hand and absorbed Amanta's body, making him disappear.

Shirone sighed.

"Phew, I really thought I'd be trapped."

Minerva glared at where Amanta had disappeared and approached Shirone.

"He's not someone to take lightly. The only intact part of the world wheel is its center of rotation. If inertia leans to either side, Amanta will cut off the protruding part."

"Even if it's good."

Minerva nodded.

"That's balance. He knows that half-hearted good incites evil."

That was the philosophy of the Five Stars of Balance Department.

"No matter what Amanta thinks, we just need to do our job. We've recovered Law-Killer, so let's go back."

Half-hearted good incites evil.

'Am I half-hearted?'

If a day comes when Yahweh truly can't handle it as Amanta said...

"Yes, let's go back."

Although Shirone stood at the top of the world, his doubts about extremes only grew larger.

Every three days, snow poured down heavily.

"Advance! Advance!"

All soldiers except for 70% of Gustav Empire's forces fighting in the holy war advanced towards Cotria Republic.

The difference this time was that they intended to occupy Cotria Republic for real.

To show Balkan's determination, 30% of their army included elite infantry units from the 1st Army.

"We must break down Cotria's capital walls in Bashken."

At Balkan's command, a man over 2 meters tall with spiky black hair bowed deeply.

"I will definitely capture it."

First Commander Ozant Guy.

Rian's eldest brother was originally a certified 6th-grade swordsman from Tormia but realized something through long espionage activities.

"A truly strong sword doesn't need miscellaneous beliefs."

He abandoned his homeland with a single-minded focus on swords and settled in Gustav Empire four years ago.

"The essence of a sword is only to cut." Nicknamed Berserker,

As Gustav's certified class-1 swordsman and commander of 600,000 troops, he was proving his swordsmanship.

Balkan understood Guy.

"Even if it's family, there will be no mercy from Berserker's sword."

He had no doubt that Bashken would fall under Guy's command

While Cotria Republic fought its final defensive battle, Kashan secretly moved Ex Machina.

The location was a small village 84 kilometers away from Bashken; Iruki and others arrived there playing card games the next day.

"Did Law-Killer get delivered properly?"

Iruki replied,

"If it's Shirone, he won't make mistakes. He'll be ready to snipe from Haigang Hill."

Haigang was a low hill area 142 kilometers away from Bashken.

"It's an optimal spot within Ex Machina's range. Shirone and Minerva agreed to assist with detailed work."

Ness said,

"The real battle begins once Law-Killer passes 80 kilometers. There must be no margin for error."

"Although Havitz has set out to conquer Cotria Republic, their defense is still strong."

Normally, Law-Killer wouldn't be able to pierce through numerous obstacles and reach Havitz's heart.

"The sniping time is 7 days 3 hours 31 minutes 28.463 seconds. This coordinate is our only found gap."

The card players nodded,

"We still have time. Let's check until the end if we missed anything."

"More firepower! It's almost over! Do you see those Cotria cowards trembling?"

As winter reached its peak coldness, Gustav Empire's 1st Infantry Unit was on the verge of capturing Bashken Castle.

Cotria Republic sent their great swordsman for a duel but lost his head to Ozant Guy in just twelve moves.

"A monster!"

Guy wielding his heavy iron armor and long sword looked like a demon,

Out of a thousand soldiers, two hundred died by his hand alone; eventually Cotria locked their castle gates tightly,

"Damn! Why delay the time?" It was normal to wave a white flag when the situation was tilted this far.

"Is there another strategy?" It was strange that there had been no report of Exmachina being activated by Balkan.

"It doesn't matter."

Standing over a sea of corpses, Guy sheathed his sword and turned towards the camp.

"Just pierce through everything."

Though he had forsaken everything for his sword, his temperament as a swordsman was still that of the Ogent family.

The bureaucrats of the Gustav Empire, who had taken control of Vashken's noble district, were busy collecting spoils.

"Kyaa! Save me!"

They scooped up everything wholesale, regardless of whether valuable items were broken, and young women were enslaved on the spot.

The highest nobles were gathered in the square, kneeling, as Havitz and Abella appeared.

"This wicked creature! Do you think the world will bend to your will?"

A high-ranking noble bellowed, but was immediately speared by the army's lance.

Abella's gaze as she watched this scene was calm.

"This is my destiny."

After being baptized by Havitz, it was true that the tightrope in her mind had snapped.

But even amidst this desolate scene, her heart was less perturbed, likely due to her childhood experiences.

"Abella."

Havitz affectionately wrapped his arms around Abella's shoulders.

"Once we conquer this castle, I'll give you everything that comes out of this country."

Since Havitz was everything to Abella, she wanted for nothing.

"That's okay. Just one beautiful flower from this country will do."

"Okay. Then..."

Havitz walked towards a dead noble.

"Daddy! Daddy!"

Young children clung to their father's corpse, crying, but Havitz’s expression remained unchanged.

"This wicked child! I must..."

Before he could finish his sentence, a lance pierced his chest.

"Hmm, what now?"

Since marrying Abella, Havitz had been happy, but his cruelty had not dulled in the slightest.

Balkan smirked.

"It's not out of anger that he kills."

For Havitz, the world was merely a passing stage, a public square and playground.

"No! Let my daughter go! Rather, take me..."

As someone's family died, became enslaved, and he watched the spectacle, Havitz sang.

"My haven of love..."

His voice was hauntingly beautiful.

"Become my flower and bloom in my heart."

The delicate vibration of his quivering lips seemed to mock the world...

"You're not human! You're a demon! A demon!"

The families of the victims' eyes burst with veins.

"Impudent things, where from!"

Once again, spears flew, and by the end of the song, half the nobles were dead.

"Good."

After finishing his song calmly, Havitz looked around the square and pointed to an alley.

"Bring that man here."

A disabled beggar, unable to escape, trembled against the wall.

"Ugh! Please save me!"

Havitz knelt down and placed his hand on the beggar's head.

"From now on, you are the king of this country."

"Ugh... Ah..."

Then he personally strapped his treasured sword onto the beggar's waist and gave him instructions.

"A king rules everything. Prove your worth with this sword, and everyone will worship you."

Though he lived a hungry life, the beggar was quick-witted and looked at the nobles with fearful eyes.

'If I can't rule, I'll die.' Limping towards the nobles, he was met with glares of disgust from all directions.

The beggar drew his sword and pointed the tip at the chin of an elderly woman.

"Worship me. Kneel before me and swear allegiance."

"Ptui!"

The elderly woman spat on the beggar's face.

"You!"

Losing his temper, he brought the sword down vertically, splitting her face perfectly in half.

No one screamed, and the beggar, panting heavily, looked around and shouted.

"Bow your heads! Obey me!"

"Ugh..."

Everyone bowed their heads towards the beggar.

"Our king!"

Seeing the highest nobles worship him gave the beggar such ecstasy that he almost lost his mind.

"Hahaha! Yes! I am the king! I am now... Ugh!"

Havitz, who had approached unnoticed, embraced him from behind and patted his shoulder gently.

"How is it? Being a king is fun, right?"

"Yes? Ah, yes! It's great! Your Majesty! I will serve you forever!"

The beggar was thrilled.

"No need to serve me since you're the king. Just have fun."

Havitz pointed at the nobles.

"They're all yours. Enjoy."

As he returned to the soldiers, Havitz threw off his emperor's cloak high into the air.

Zetaro asked,

"Why did you do that? Are you uncomfortable?"

"No, I just thought this would be fun too. I'm tired of being an emperor. From now on, I'll be a beggar."

"Hehehe! That sounds fun!"

When Zetaro turned his head, Smoto and Natasha also nodded with bright expressions.

Balkan said,

"Don't get too carried away. The war is still..."

"Let's go! The beggar's rebellion!"

As the three people already immersed in their game ran towards the houses, Balkan spoke to Abella.

"Can't you stop them?"

Abella shook her head with a bitter smile.

"Who can stop that personality?"

The beggar, carried on a noble's back, swung his sword.

"From now on, women will bathe me. Men will find clothes suitable for me! Got it?"

"...Yes, understood."

It was utter chaos.
