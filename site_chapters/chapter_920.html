Chapter 920: A Cold War (4)

After the banquet with the royals, Lupist and Plu left the castle in silence for a long time.

Words, words, words.

The stress of having to interpret all kinds of words according to others' standards weighed on them.

"I'm sorry."

Plu frowned.

"For what?"

"Just."

He could explain, but repeating the royals' vulgar jokes was annoying.

"Director."

Plu sighed and turned her head.

"Aren't you becoming too sentimental these days? I know we're on the brink of a major event, but..."

"Sentimental?"

Me?

"It doesn't mean anything special. I just did you a favor because you didn't deserve that."

"That makes me feel worse." Plu said with a displeased expression.

"If the previous secretary-general had gone through the same thing, would you have apologized?"

Jane's face came to mind.

He wouldn't have even mentioned an apology.

‘We were a perfect team.’

Realizing his mistake, Lupist returned to his usual demeanor and asked coldly.

"What's the progress?"

A smile finally appeared on Plu's face.

"With the royals' approval, we can now deploy an additional 200,000 royal guards. If we place the drum line at the forefront, we can lure the demons."

"When is the expected start time?"

"According to Dante, we have 16 hours left. We need to complete all arrangements within that time."

"I'll inform the command. Anything else?"

Plu brought up something she had been holding back.

"I think Pony has noticed. She had the same smile as us."

Lupist was aware too.

"She's different from the other royals. She attended the magic school and has quite a bit of social experience, right?"

If participating in a world beauty pageant counts as social experience.

"What should we do?"

It was a short but chilling question.

‘If she has noticed...’

If she tells other royals, the strategy will be ruined, so they need to act now.

"Your thoughts?"

"It's best to do it. She's an excellent magician, but we can't rely on probabilities."

She was destined to die anyway.

"...I'll send someone."

She's smart but not exceptionally skilled in magic, so a hitman would be better than an assassin.

"Yes. Then next..."

Just as Plu was about to present the next agenda, Pony appeared at the crossroads.

Their steps halted abruptly, but their expressions remained unchanged like machines.

"Are you heading to the Magic Association?" Lupist asked politely, showing respect to the royal.

"Miss Pony, what brings you here?"

Pony, looking back and forth between Lupist and Plu, seemed to make up her mind and bowed.

"Please save me."

A royal bowing was a national shock, but Lupist remained calm.

"Save you? What's the matter?"

"I don't know."

Pony bit her lip and raised her head.

"Because I can't even imagine what will happen, I'm asking you to save me."

‘She's certainly smart.’

Realizing she couldn't respond to the danger and giving up all her power took only 10 minutes.

‘The king is an illusion, but...’

She was undeniably a key figure who could unite the nation in the shortest time.

‘It should be fine.’

Rebuilding with a puppet king.

"How much are you willing to give up?"

Having already made up her mind, Pony hesitated, realizing this was the end.

"Everything."

To inherit the throne in a new era, Pony was willing to give up her humanity.

‘Mental murder.’

To survive in a hell ruled by demons, this level of madness was necessary.

"I'll inform you separately."

Lupist said as he passed Pony.

"It won't take long."

"Oh, what a disaster."

Packing his belongings in the royal barracks, Daris felt like the sky was falling.

‘Damn royals. Deploying the royal guard, including the military band, to the front lines?’

His comrades felt the same.

"It's really strange. We didn't hear anything until yesterday. Suddenly calling us..."

A sense of foreboding filled the air.

"Everyone, gather!"

At the battalion commander's order, the grumbling soldiers grabbed their drums and ran to the parade ground.

"Listen up! From now on, we will march rapidly for four hours to reach the Denan Plains. There, we will lure the army of hell!"

The soldiers murmured.

"I have a question! Why is the royal guard being deployed? Each division has its own military band."

"All the military bands under Tormia's command will gather at the Denan Plains."

The murmuring grew louder.

"The entire military band?"

There were nearly 3,000 members.

"For that reason, we are also deploying, but don't think too seriously. As soon as we lure the army of hell, we will retreat, and the infantry will take over."

It was reassuring to hear, but the danger of being on the front lines still loomed.

‘The death rate is about 5%, right? So out of 3,000, 150 will die...’

Everyone had the same thought.

"Now, return to the barracks and pack your gear. Regroup here in 20 minutes! Dismissed!"

The soldiers quickly returned, and Daris packed his gear with a troubled heart.

"Daris."

He turned at the voice at the entrance to see Hanea, a royal maid.

"What? How did you...?"

They were childhood friends.

Daris confessed first, and Hanea politely declined after a day of thought.

He had suffered from lovesickness, so his comrades were surprised by her appearance.

"Can we talk for a moment?" The squad leader patted Daris's shoulder. "Go ahead. I'll pack your gear."

Putting off his gratitude for later, Daris took Hanea outside.

"What's up? It's work time."

"I heard you're deploying."

As a royal maid, she was well-informed.

"Haha! Just my luck... No, it's something I have to do. It's a bit dangerous, but..."

"I'll wait for you."

"Huh?"

Hanea blushed slightly as she glanced at Daris, who asked in disbelief.

"I've thought a lot, and you're right. Starting anew might be good."

"So, you mean... you and I..."

Hanea nodded slightly.

"Come back quickly. Don't be a hero, and do whatever it takes to survive."

Daris took a deep breath.

After their brief meeting, he returned to the barracks, and all his comrades turned to look at him.

"How did it go? Did you..."

Seeing the fiery determination in Daris's eyes, they all gaped in astonishment.

"I will come back alive." Definitely.

After a four-hour rapid march, the royal military band joined other bands. A drum line of 3,000 formed on the vast Denan Plains, and the sky was silent.

Daris desperately tried to calm his trembling hands holding the drumsticks.

‘Stay calm. It's nothing. Just play the drums for an hour, then run.’

The band leader raised the flag.

"Everyone!"

The sound of drumsticks hitting the drums echoed in perfect unison.

"March!"

Rat-a-tat-tat-tat-tat!

The fastest drumbeat thundered across the plains.

As the leading drum line moved one kilometer, the infantry finally followed.

‘The distance to the lifeline is one kilometer.’

When luring the demons, they would have to run at least 500 meters like mad to reverse positions.

After 30 minutes of drumming until their hands cramped, the result appeared.

Demons with colorful skin approached, shaking the ground beyond the horizon.

‘There are so many!’

They filled the entire horizon of the Denan Plains.

‘We can't win! We have to run!’

Everyone felt the same, but the band leader's retreat flag remained still.

Rumble!

The ground shook like an earthquake, and comrades began to cry.

"Aaaah! Aaaah!"

Daris shouted too.

"Damn it! Retreat now! We have to go!"

As the killing intent of 3,000 soldiers focused on the band leader, the retreat flag finally waved.

"Damn it!" Daris threw off his drum and ran towards the distant infantry.

The race for life and death began.

"Hahaha! Shoot! Shoot!"

Arrows shaped like spines flew from the demon horde, raining down.

Though still far, some arrows from powerful bows pierced the soldiers.

"Ugh!"

Seeing comrades fall around him, Daris gritted his teeth and pushed his legs harder.

‘I can survive! I will survive!’

The band leader's decision seemed good, as there were fewer casualties than expected.

‘5%, no, 3%. I won't be in that number!’

Hanea was waiting.

‘Let's get married. Go on vacation together, have dinner at a nice restaurant! And do lots of fun things!’

Tears streamed down his face as the infantry came into view.

"Uwaaah! Uwaaah!"

At that moment.

"Ugh!"

A thick bone arrow pierced Daris's neck from behind.

Falling forward, Daris clawed at the arrow embedded in the ground.

"Ugh! Ugh!"

In shock, he looked up and met the eyes of his squad leader, who had turned back.

"S-save... save me..."

As he reached out, the squad leader's pupils wavered briefly.

"Please..."

But then he turned away and ran towards the front with all his might.

"No, no...!"

God is indifferent.

‘Why me? Out of all people, why me? Hanea, Hanea spoke to me!’

What grudge would God have to punish a young man who had just found love?

‘I don't want to die! Please, someone...’

The arrow was shot, and he was just in its path due to the world's inertia.

‘I can't die! It can't end like this!’

He simply hadn't run fast enough.

"Ugh!"

He tried with all his might to pull out the arrow, but the barbed arrowhead wouldn't budge from his neck.

"H-Hanea."

A cold world.

"I want to see you..."

As the army of hell advanced, they trampled Daris's body until it was nothing but pulp.

"Kahahaha! Kill them! Kill the humans!"

Just before the last piece of his brain shattered, Daris had a sudden thought.

‘If only I hadn't come here.’

Even if it meant losing his job, if he had given up everything and left with Hanea.

‘Why?’

Why did he think he could survive?

‘Because the woman I love accepted my confession? Because the chance of dying was only 5%?’

No matter what he wanted or hoped for, the wheel of law turned mechanically.

‘There was a chance...’

A chance to give up everything.

Just before losing consciousness, Daris thought he heard a voice in his mind.

Save yourself.

Whether it was a hallucination from his brain or a message from God, only he would ever know.
