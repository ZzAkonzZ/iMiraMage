Chapter 496:

"Ha ha ha ha ha!"

Laughter erupted from the twisted, collapsed form of Satan, whose eyes, nose, mouth, hands, and feet were all jumbled together.

A powerful shockwave rippled through the air, and in an instant, Satan's body returned to its original state.

"Damn it…"

Satan's massive hands grabbed the throats of the angels.

With a force that seemed like it could snap their necks at any moment, the faces of the two beautiful angels twisted in pain.

'Has it come to this?'

An angel's physical durability is not something that any creature can easily destroy.

Moreover, the secondary application of the law they used had indeed been effective.

However, Satan's law, having annihilated countless ordinary angels and even the archangel Metatron, had grown much stronger than Metiel and Satiel had anticipated.

If the Akashic Records represent the entirety of the universe, Satan's law is its counterpart.

In other words, if the seesaw of law is balanced to some extent, even physical impacts cannot be fully conveyed.

"Two archangels."

Satan alternated his gaze between the two suffering archangels and smiled wickedly.

"Is this enough to end it?"

As Metiel and Satiel's halos shook violently, about to explode, someone rushed in from behind Satan and struck him.

The sudden loss of strength in Satan's body allowed Metiel and Satiel to break free.

But Satan paid no attention to the already defeated angels.

"Who the hell…?"

The impact wasn't that strong, but it was enough to momentarily neutralize Satan's law.

Turning around, Satan finally understood the reason.

Etella, the bishop of the Karsis Monastery, who possessed the perfect law of good, was glaring at Satan with burning eyes.

'Just a little further…'

Plu caught a glimpse of Aravot's walls.

But like a mirage, they quickly vanished, replaced by giants.

"Sob! Sob!"

Tears of frustration streamed down her face.

She had come to heaven with pride as a professional mage, but it was an overwhelmingly difficult place for her.

The giant guards of Matei were so massive that her magic barely affected them.

What could she possibly do in such a place?

Yet she kept running, because stopping here would mean never being able to do anything again.

Even her lifelong pride as a mage would crumble.

'I can still fight!'

Thud! Thud! Thud! Thud!

The giants noticed Plu running beneath them and repeatedly stomped their feet.

Plu, rolling on the ground, felt like a helpless insect, biting her lip in despair.

A majestic voice boomed from above.

"Humans are so annoying. They're weak and small."

Annoyed by Plu's persistent evasion, a giant brought down his greatsword with all his might.

Boom!

To Plu, it felt like the world was splitting apart.

Massive debris flew in all directions, and the shockwave pushed her body dozens of meters away.

As she lay on the ground, momentarily losing focus, a rock the size of a human head struck her shoulder.

"Aaaah!"

Hearing the sound of her bones shattering, Plu knew it was over.

She screamed and fell to the ground, unable to roll due to her shattered right shoulder.

Seeing the giant's hand descending from the sky, Plu trembled as if electrocuted.

Like a child thinking of hundreds of ways to torment an ant, she couldn't even imagine how the giant would kill her.

"Plu!"

Before she could turn towards the voice, someone rushed in, grabbed her, and leaped away.

"Aaaah!"

Clutching her injured shoulder, Plu screamed and widened her eyes at the face of her rescuer.

"How are you here?"

Crude, who should have been leading the rebels in the middle of the battlefield, was here alone.

And he was equipped with only the first-stage mecha weapon, Piper, instead of Titan or Guroi.

"Why are you here? What about the rebels?"

"The war is over. We lost."

"That's ridiculous!"

Even if they lost, shouldn't a commander fight to the end?

But Plu fell silent.

She had no right to criticize Crude, having informed him that divine punishment would strike Aravot.

'He must resent me.'

She knew Crude hadn't told the rebels the truth until the end.

She had said it wasn't her concern, but she couldn't imagine what he must have felt.

That's why Crude had come.

'Maybe it's for the best. If my death eases his anger…'

"But."

Crude, estimating the height of Aravot's walls, continued.

"Given the situation, wouldn't it be satisfying to at least see heaven's destruction?"

Plu was speechless at his unexpected words as Crude carried her towards the wall.

Using Piper's functions to zigzag, Crude dodged the giants' stomping feet as they began their insect hunt.

Each time a giant's foot fell, the ground shook like a rubber mat, and the noise was deafening.

'I can do this.'

Even in the overwhelming fear, Crude clung to his rationality.

He was the skilled pilot who had precisely operated the massive Titan and Guroi with millimeter accuracy.

Though he couldn't match the giants' strength, his understanding of the equipment was comparable to that of an immortal.

"Humans are worse than insects!"

But as the giants' stomping tempo increased, the scale's limits became apparent.

Even running a hundred steps was within a giant's single stride.

Reaching this far with Plu was already a miracle.

"There! Aravot!"

Plu pointed, and Crude saw it too.

But climbing the wall head-on would only result in being crushed by a giant's hand.

"Do you know why I came for you?"

In the dire situation, Crude's question seemed out of place, but Plu felt a strange sense of understanding.

She couldn't comprehend why he had come to save her, the one he should resent the most.

"To kill me…"

Plu's honest answer made Crude pause, then he smiled as if making a decision.

"Make sure you survive."

"What are you saying…"

She hadn't heard his answer yet.

But before she could ask again, Crude removed Piper's voice recognition device and issued a command.

"Limit release."

Clank! Clank!

Piper read the user's movements at nerve speed to enhance strength.

But no matter how much the function was increased, the human body had its limits.

With the limit released, Piper was no longer a strength-enhancing device.

It was a trap, a killing machine that would destroy the user's body.

'I have to do this at maximum output.'

Crude knew this was his last chance.

As he twisted his waist, pulling Plu to his side, Piper detected the movement and rotated at maximum output.

Crack!

His waist rotated half a turn, and his spine made a crushing sound.

"Ugh!"

Crude didn't care.

He focused all his strength on twisting his waist again to launch Plu over the wall.

"Aaaah!"

The small engine roared as Crude's waist snapped back at incredible speed.

As the disks crushed, the shock nearly knocked him out, but Plu's body soared like a cannonball.

"Catch her!"

The giants reached for Plu, flying past Crude.

Though she flew at tremendous speed, the giant hands stretched towards her, overwhelming the space.

As the palm blocked her view, Plu saw the five fingers closing in.

Dawkins Algorithm.

Plu slipped through the fingers and hastily cast a fly spell, landing on Aravot's wall.

In the midst of fear, with no time to feel relief, Plu looked back at Crude.

"No, no…"

She thought he might be smiling when a giant, arriving late, grabbed him.

The giant, bending over, straightened up and roared in anger at missing Plu.

"Raaaargh!"

If she jumped into Aravot, the giant couldn't reach her.

The only remaining option was to throw Crude at her.

The giant swung his arm, and Crude's body flew in a straight line towards Plu.

Bang!

Crude's body missed Plu by a hair and slammed into the wall's edge, bouncing off.

"No…"

Plu stared at Crude in shock.

Seeing his twisted limbs, she had a selfish thought.

'I haven't heard his answer yet.'

Crude's body, following the thrown momentum, fell over the wall into Aravot.

"Noooo!"

Unable to hold back her sobs, Plu jumped off the wall after Crude.

Clang! Clang!

Cracks began to form in the iron, known for its supreme strength.

The sword of Girshin, the giant corps commander who had reached the 7th stage of the Art of One, finally started to destroy Babel's body.

Kanya trembled, holding the unconscious Lena.

The battle between Girshin and Babel was too fast for her ordinary Mecha tribe eyes to follow, but the sparks and explosions made it clear.

The other giants had already wiped out the rebels and left to push the second wave, leaving only Kanya and Lena as survivors in the desolate battlefield.

"No, this can't be…"

The reason they survived while everyone else died.

Though she didn't want to think about it, Kanya had to know.

Babel, calculating the war's odds at zero percent, moved solely to protect Kanya and Lena.

And now, even the third possibility in Shirone's algorithm was converging to zero percent.

"Kekeke, impressive for a tin can. But that's the limit of machines. No one can surpass a giant's strength."

To Girshin, Babel was a worthy opponent, but the excitement ended there.

He had always won, so no being held more value than a mere rival.

There was only one being who could dominate him.

Not even Anke Ra, but the strongest of all creatures in this world, the giant Ymir.

"Taahaaa!"

Girshin's battle cry was completely drowned out by the ensuing roar, and a flash of light severed Babel's right arm.

For Babel, whose program was specialized in combat, losing a part of its body was a critical blow.

That's why the Gaian designers prioritized durability when creating Babel.

However, Girshin's strength surpassed that durability, and Babel's computation unit finally calculated a win rate of less than 0.1%.

Dodging Girshin's next strike, Babel leaped to shield Kanya and Lena.

Thud! Thud thud thud!

As her shoulders opened, the iron armor emerged and embedded itself in the ground, protecting Kanya and Lena.

Almost simultaneously, Girshin charged and struck Babel's body with his sword.

Boom!

The tremendous impact exploded from Babel's back, causing Kanya's shoulders to shake from the nearby shock.

Yet Babel's expression remained unchanged.

With a face as cold as a machine, she continued to endure Girshin's attacks, looking indifferently at Kanya and Lena.
