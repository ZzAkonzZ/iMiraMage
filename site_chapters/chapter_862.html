Chapter 862: Rain (1)

"It's closer to impossible than difficult."

Nane continued.

"What a great contradiction it is to be able to destroy what you love the most." The more you love, the further you are from emptiness.

"That's why, although I've almost reached it, it will still take an immeasurable amount of time to become a Buddha."

Shura asked.

"Then will Shirone reach it first?"

The ultimate.

"He's still far from it. I can vaguely guess what he might be feeling in a world dominated by evil."

The greater the love for all things, the greater the sense of betrayal, and his anger would be driving him towards emptiness.

"Then will Shirone end the world?"

Like Nane once did.

"Possibly. But I know how difficult that is. If there's a difference between us..."

Nane looked up at the sky.

"It's that this world loves him."

Even if Yahweh's anger were to lead to the end of the world.

World Climate Organization.

With the help of the Holy War, the organization had relocated its headquarters, housing over 300 magicians.

Their mission was to manipulate the climate to increase agricultural production in areas not invaded by demons.

The surplus food became the rations for the Valkyrie army, making this department indispensable in the world war.

"Manager! Look at this!"

Amira, a first-year employee, collected signals from observation devices worldwide and shouted.

"What is it? Did a disaster strike somewhere?"

The documents were filled with numbers and diagrams, but the manager saw a picture.

"What is this?"

It was a typhoon.

A massive typhoon, unprecedented in history, was moving between Gustav and Jincheon.

"And there are seven of them! How is this possible?"

Amira pointed to the detailed information.

"The speed is on a different level from existing typhoons. It will make landfall in Gustav in a few hours. Its power will exceed at least category six."

It wasn't a natural occurrence.

"It's magic. But who could..."

Even the World Climate Organization found it difficult to create typhoons of this scale consecutively.

"What should we do? If it makes landfall, the civilian damage will be enormous. We have to dissipate it."

"Can we dissipate it?"

Amira stammered.

"Well, if we plant the seeds before it grows any bigger, we can slowly subdue it."

"Even so, it's impossible to eliminate all seven. It's better to prepare evacuation measures."

The fact that the typhoons were heading towards the evil stronghold of the Gustav Empire also played a part.

"Manager! Over here!"

Another employee shouted with a pale face, and the manager hurried over.

"Damn it! What now?"

Amira bit her lip as she stared intently at the documents the manager had thrown down.

'What's happening?'

It was a series of disasters.

Jincheon Sea.

Mitura, the commander of the 8th legion, stood up from the giant clam, watching the typhoons move beyond the horizon.

"Here they come again. How many are they going to send?"

"What should we do? If this continues, Gustav will be destroyed. Satan will be angry..."

Mitura glared, and the demon flinched.

"Of course, I trust you, Mitura."

The demons suggested opening the demon realm before things got worse.

'Annoying.'

Only the ten legion commanders had this privilege, but it also symbolized eternal annihilation.

'You can't even be resurrected from the fires of hell.'

Opening the demon realm was a last resort, and Mitura wasn't ready yet.

"Commander! Over there!"

Beyond the horizon, four of the Five Stars of the Ivory Tower approached, causing rough waves.

Minerva, aboard a jet, spoke.

"Don't worry about the others. Our job is to eliminate the commander."

Freed, running on the water's surface, gripped his sword, eyeing the demons.

"You learned from Kashan, right? The best option is to finish it before they open the demon realm."

Mitura's realm was the tidal wave.

Once opened, it would destroy all coastal cities worldwide.

Mitura stood up, holding the giant esophagus.

"Alright! Let's go, everyone! Let's have a proper fight!"

A murmuring voice was heard from afar.

"Just open the demon realm."

As Mitura swung the esophagus horizontally, the heads of the demons lined up dozens of meters away flew off.

"Eek!"

There were no more complaints from the demons.

"Consider that a greeting!"

Mitura lowered his stance and slashed the water's surface, raising a massive wave.

"Here they come!"

Freed's ice magic froze the wave, dozens of meters high, with a loud crack.

Boom!

Minerva burst through the ice wall, stopping in mid-air and firing jets.

Gustav's capital.

"Ahhh! Save me...!"

The voice disappeared before the scream ended.

As people covered in insects fell like charred wood, Rian dashed through.

"What a huge number."

The insects of the hidden world, Palapinas, endlessly multiplied, covering the entire city.

'How do I get rid of this?'

He could cut down powerful enemies, but against countless insects, the sword was the worst tool.

"Yahhh!"

Rian's sword, imbued with the power of Maha, split the scenery, but the fly-like insects kept clinging to him.

"Ugh!"

His entire body was soon covered in bugs, and the sensation of his blood being sucked made him dizzy.

'It's okay.'

The Idea wouldn't break from this, but ordinary humans would die within ten seconds.

Moreover, Palapinas kept multiplying, and if not suppressed early, it could lead to the world's destruction.

"Damn demon realm."

Rian accelerated, breaking through a wall, and struck the greatsword with all his might. The atmosphere split with a roar, and for a moment, the sky was visible.

"What..."

Unable to even think of raising his sword, Rian stared blankly at the sky.

"What is that?"

It was probably an incarnation, but he didn't want to know what it was.

Shirone's body was weightless, like luminous smoke.

Above his bowed head, anger was endlessly rising.

Melania muttered.

"Yahweh's anger."

The black smoke rose at a terrifying speed, piercing the sky and spreading like a mushroom cloud.

It expanded without slowing down, like black paint spreading across the sky.

'It's out in the world.'

Yahweh's rotten, festering resentment had burst through his body.

'What will happen?'

Melania snapped back to reality and shouted.

"Kill him! Kill Yahweh!"

"Ugh!"

Guy immediately ran and swung his sword, but it didn't cut Shirone.

'It doesn't seem to be material.'

Following the sword's trajectory, Guy couldn't see it, but hexagonal particles of light were floating.

'Information?'

Meirei stood up and covered one ear.

"Chief Justice."

The voice of Chief Justice Theraphos was still deep but urgent.

"The Hexa Program has been activated."

"Hexa... Program? What's that?"

There was no time for explanations.

"I'll send the Ark."

The communication was cut off.

The manager of the World Climate Organization shouted.

"Is this even possible?"

The thrown documents slid across the floor, and the surrounding employees hurriedly picked them up.

"Manager! What's wrong?"

Amira approached, but the manager was too agitated to respond.

"Amira, look at this."

Amira quickly checked the figures on the documents from the Gustav Empire's observatory.

The documents fell from her hands again.

"Impossible."

The employees thought the same.

"But it's true. This isn't a natural phenomenon or magic. It's something unknown."

Even if all the water on the planet were used, clouds of this mass couldn't be created.

"It's like..."

An employee imagined the sky beyond the ceiling.

"Like iron heavier than the planet is floating."

Guy's sword slashed through Shirone's body at a speed that left afterimages.

'Why can't I kill him?'

Maybe because he was already dead.

Realizing this, Guy finally stopped attacking and looked up at the sky.

"It's grown bigger."

The cloud was large enough to cover an entire kingdom.

"What kind of incarnation is that?"

"Yahweh."

Melania approached.

"That's Yahweh? Isn't he supposed to love all things? This is more like..."

It was darker and more sinister than the demons.

"Yes. Truly despicable."

The amount of evil a human had to suppress to reach the state of compassion.

"Why wouldn't he want to be angry? He'd want to be jealous, to desire, to act on his whims."

Yahweh is not good.

"Compassion is created through sacrifice. The unrecognized sacrifice accumulates deep inside, becoming an unbearable monster..."

The black cloud covered the entire empire.

"A being who must live forever, imprisoning himself to prevent the monster from escaping."

Melania touched Shirone's particles.

"To demons, that hypocrisy is horrifying..."

She grasped the light in her hand.

"It's detestable."

As she slowly opened her hand, the hexagonal light fluttered up to the sky.

Despite her words, Melania's eyes looked ecstatic as she watched it.

"We did it. Yahweh's anger is gone."

Rumble.

A foreboding thunder rumbled from the black cloud that covered the sun.

"Shouldn't we get out of here?"

"Where to?"

Melania, with disheveled eyes, turned her palm towards the sky.

"It looks like it's going to rain."

As soon as she finished speaking, particles of light began to fall from the sky.

The light, containing divine particles, was a gloomy gray and thousands of times heavier than raindrops.

Swoosh.

The light droplets fell almost simultaneously.

However, at the extreme of observation, simultaneity cannot be achieved, and the leading droplets of light headed towards the capital, Marsak.

The moment the first droplet of light struck the highest spire of the capital.

Thud!

With a powerful burst, a fist-sized dent was formed.

Thud! Thud! Thud!

And then...

"Damn it!"

Infinite.

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

An infinite number of droplets struck the entire empire.

"Ahhh!"

Tens of millions of demons screamed simultaneously, but even their cries were drowned out by the roar.

The capital's height was being reduced by four meters per second under the continuous bombardment.

"What is this! This...!"

The force was pounding the entire empire equally, destroying everything on the ground.

"Yahhh!"

Guy swung his sword, deflecting the particles of light.

After about three seconds, nothing remained of his sword except the handle.

"This...?"

A dull sound echoed through his skull.

"Ugh!"

As thousands of droplets of light pounded his body, Guy's form crumpled to the ground.

"How unfortunate."

Melania, holding a black umbrella, looked down at the shapeless Guy.

"It seems the Ark did not come for you."

She turned to look at the capital, where millions of bluish domes had formed.
