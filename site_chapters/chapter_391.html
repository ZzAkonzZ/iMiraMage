Chapter 391: Combat Readiness (4)

"Photon shaping. It's advanced beyond fractals."

Canis muttered with a tense expression.

Creating wings from light was proof that shaping had transcended the realm of photons.

Shirone had already realized the art of incarnation.

"How much does he know?"

Harvest spoke through the mental channel.

-If he's at the stage of using specific magic, we have to consider him ahead of us. It seems the reason he passed the void wasn't just the immortal function.

That would explain the sense of incongruity Canis felt at the time.

"It's become clearer."

Canis looked at Arin.

"What do you mean?"

"Shirone's wings have become clearer."

Canis tilted her head. It was rare for her to refer to the shape of a specific object.

'Come to think of it...'

On Galliant Island, Arin had asked if Shirone was blonde.

At the time, it didn't seem significant, but now, hearing Harvest's words, it couldn't be ignored.

"How exactly do you see Shirone? Can you explain?"

"Shirone."

"Yes?"

"Shirone is Shirone. I had a vague idea before, but now I know, I was seeing Shirone's incarnation."

Arin couldn't analyze the form reflected in the first sight. But the form of the incarnation born from the law never changes.

"It's not the art of incarnation. Shirone is the incarnation."

That was Arin's conclusion of mental transcendence.

"Hmm."

Canis looked at Shirone with a serious expression.

There would certainly be a difference between the art of incarnation and the incarnation itself. It was impossible to predict the outcome if they clashed head-on.

"What do you mean by the wings becoming clearer?"

"They were there back then too, on Shirone's back."

"What?"

When they met on Galliant, Shirone didn't have the technique called Light Wings. So how did she see the wings?

"Is it like a feeling?"

Arin shook her head.

"There's no feeling in an incarnation. It's just an incarnation. But it wasn't as clear as now. It was a transparent form, like made by the wind. But now it's tinged with golden light."

Arin wasn't a precognitive. Therefore, the wings she saw on Shirone were likely those of the incarnation.

'Wings on an incarnation?'

If Shirone continued to strengthen the power of the incarnation, would wings really sprout from his shoulders?

There would probably be no one in the world who could answer that.

Shirone bent his knees, focusing on the navigation magic fixed in front of him.

'One, two...'

Finally, the arrow pointed to the sky.

'Three!'

At the same time, the wings of Light Wings flapped, and his body soared.

The acceleration created by plunging mass at near-light speed was tremendous, and everyone looked up with astonished expressions.

Confirming direction, distance, and speed within 0.8 seconds and casting teleportation was a matter of intuition.

The most challenging part was that the navigation's suggestion changed during teleportation.

Ultimately, judgment was only possible after the turn ended, resulting in a loss of about 0.2 seconds.

That 0.2 seconds was the time the association suggested could be shortened with the Divine Particle.

'Now it begins!'

Light Wings met that standard precisely.

Bang!

As the wings flapped during teleportation, the direction of the flash turned vertically.

Bang! Bang! Bang!

With explosive sounds, Shirone executed the navigation's suggestions without error.

"Ugh!"

The pressure from rapid direction changes due to gravitational acceleration was entirely Shirone's burden.

Every time the direction changed, the blood in his veins rushed to one side, and his muscles felt like they were tearing.

If he had Armand's Diamond Armor, it could mitigate the risk factors, but he was currently unarmed.

The stronger the pressure, the more intense the wing flaps became. He couldn't stop, even if his body broke.

It was a flight to become a magician.

"Wow, it really works?"

The 0.2-second difference was also conveyed to the students on the ground. It was a completely different trajectory from ordinary teleportation.

Knowing how strenuous it was to change direction during casting, they felt a chilling sensation.

"Won't he break apart in the sky? He must be enduring several times the gravity."

"But if he can withstand it, it's on a different level from teleportation."

The rhythm of the movement was different.

Such a syncopation was unprecedented, and for the opponent, it would likely be perceived as an entirely new tactic.

Even numerically, it was a significant advantage.

Assuming a 100-turn battle, it meant he could reach the desired coordinates 20 seconds faster than the opponent.

That was the true meaning of the 0.2 seconds Shirone was currently performing.

'When will this end?'

The navigation was persistently tormenting Shirone as if it had cognitive abilities. It demanded reverse directions as a basic, and continuous vertical drops and ascents.

How far had he flown?

The magic school's grounds looked small enough to fit within a circle made by his fingers.

No movement evaluation of any series required such a vast space. What was the association thinking?

'No, this is right!'

Shirone believed blindly. The Divine Particle was his, but there would be no error in the standards calculated by professionals.

Finally, the arrow pointed towards the magic school.

The speed suggested by the navigation was an average of over 300 kilometers per hour. The distance was a whopping 5.4 kilometers.

'Get there in 65 seconds?'

A distance achievable by linking 540 teleportations. The problem was that inertia reset, so he couldn't benefit from acceleration.

It wasn't the photon series from the start. What the association demanded was the dynamics of mass, that is, movement with acceleration.

"Whoo!"

Starting with teleportation, Shirone swung Light Wings at maximum speed. Every time the wings slashed through the air, the sound of breaking through the air flew towards the school.

180 kilometers per hour. 220 kilometers per hour. 280 kilometers per hour.

The moment he covered the 2.4-kilometer distance, he finally exceeded 300 kilometers per hour.

But because he lost time in the initial acceleration phase, he had to fly even faster to match the average speed.

"Ugh!"

The situation, where only the navigation was visible, felt no different from falling off a cliff.

Shirone focused solely on speed. The navigation wouldn't crash a student into a cliff.

340 kilometers per hour.

He doubled the area of Light Wings. The acceleration soared as the power equivalent to the photon cannon flapped.

440 kilometers per hour.

He heard the sound of fire burning in his eardrums. The magic school was approaching at an incredible speed.

'Wait a minute...'

A sudden thought occurred.

Who guaranteed that the navigation wouldn't crash a student into a cliff?

That was just the vague expectation of someone cornered, not a rational judgment.

The only reliable information was that the navigation was merely pointing to the school's coordinates.

'This is driving me crazy!'

Shirone pulled Light Wings towards his body.

The acceleration was already at its peak. But if he lost speed, he would fail. The only safety measure to protect his life was air resistance.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

Shirone felt intense vibrations as he pierced through the body of the air. It was the first time he realized how solid air was.

The students who spotted Shirone stood up abruptly.

"Get out of the way! If he crashes here, it's over!"

But where could they go? Unable to predict the exact landing point, they could only sway their upper bodies left and right.

The navigation made its final suggestion upon reaching the evaluation area. A dot. It meant it was best to stop here.

"Huff!"

Shirone widened his eyes and swung Light Wings forward.

Bang!

A sound like his eardrums were tearing erupted. The dust in the training ground was blown away, spreading a hazy mist ahead.

The students turned their heads, grimacing at the ringing in their ears.

But soon, they moved their gaze to the sight of the dust settling with fearful expressions.

Imagining Shirone's body turned into a bloody mess, they would have preferred the dust not to clear.

"Sh-Shirone?"

Maya moved with a pale face. But her body was stiff, and she couldn't move forward.

"Shirone! Shirone!"

Amy and her friends ran past her towards the evaluation area.

Flap!

A slow, massive sound scattered the dust in all directions. Nade, who had swallowed dirt, spat and looked ahead.

Shirone, floating 5 meters in the air, was slowly flapping his wings and descending to the ground.

"Shirone, are you okay?"

Shirone responded with a smile and turned his gaze to Maya. When she sat down with a relieved expression, a thrilling joy surged.

It was undoubtedly a paradoxical emotion.

"I'm fine. Let's finish the evaluation first."

As he folded and dissipated Light Wings, golden fragments spread brilliantly for a moment before disappearing.

The evaluator began recording.

"Shirone, you passed the master difficulty for movement control. The total score for today is 30 points."

Although it didn't reach Fermi's 100 points, the minds of all the graduating class became even more complicated.

Fermi, the all-master. Shirone, who surpassed Fermi's capabilities without being an all-master.

The rivalry between the two was still neck and neck.

'This has made things more difficult. Is Fermi really going to break?'

Except for Fermi's group, it was natural for those who staked their lives on this graduation exam to have conflicts.

Electric monster Lycan spoke with cold eyes.

"This is the first time I've seen someone so tenacious. Fermi, it's about time...!"

Lycan's face froze as he turned his head.

Fermi's eyes were not normal. His right pupil was rolled up, and his left pupil was shifted to the side.

It was the true face of a monster hidden behind the mask of a businessman.

'Damn, this is like a mutual destruction scenario.'

A deviant. No matter how rational his thinking, he was a twisted monster inside.

It was unlikely, but if Fermi started acting emotionally, it would cause massive losses.

'How much do we earn in a month...'

He thought it might be better to just let Shirone go.

"Hey, Fermi. You..."

"An emergency meeting is called. All executives gather at the Golden Wheel."

Fermi gave a short order and walked away.

Like seeing a ghost with its features erased, Lycan couldn't remember Fermi's face from a moment ago.

The hill capture evaluation was two days away.

Students were secretly gathering for strategy meetings, and those who hadn't decided on a team were now at the point of making a decision.

As a mock evaluation for the graduation exam, everything from team composition to matchups was autonomous.

You could designate the team you wanted, and if you were designated by multiple teams or not designated at all, it was decided by drawing lots.

So far, the only team with all members revealed was Shirone's team.

On the other hand, the Screamer team was still shrouded in mystery, at least to Shirone's team.

'Will she be in the music room today?'

Shirone entered the graduating class building. At Iruki's request, he went to find Maya earlier than their scheduled strategy meeting.

As expected, the sound of singing flowed from the music room, and the emotions from that sunset day resurfaced, making his heart race.

He slowly opened the door and entered, and Maya turned her head with a bright smile.

"Oh, Shirone! What brings you here? We were supposed to meet later."

Shirone felt a mix of emotions. Sometimes he felt distant, but at moments like this, she felt closer than anyone.

"Iruki asked if we could start a bit earlier. He came up with a tailored strategy for the Screamer team."

"Oh, I see. Where are we meeting?"

Maya closed the piano and stood up, walking past Shirone towards the door. She smelled like milk, like a child.
