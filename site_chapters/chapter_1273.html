Chapter 1273: Final Chapter (1)

The void of infinity.

‘Everything is an illusion.’

And yet, because the heart moves toward it, it cannot simply be called an illusion.

‘This is where I began.’

The first illusion Shirone chose was the moment when the Iron Axis was first planted in this world.

He didn’t need to verify it—but after such a long journey, he wanted to be sure.

The Grand Earth Temple of the Ivory Tower.

When he opened his eyes, the planet beneath his feet was blue and beautiful—his own world.

‘And…’

Veron had just finished embedding the Iron Axis and was receiving Nané’s sermon from the ground.

Nané’s sword had grown endlessly massive, grazing the planet and leaving a straight scar across the earth.

Great Star asked, “How is it? You fought him before, didn’t you? If it were Shirone, could he stop that sermon now?”

Her question was proof.

‘I’ve returned.’

Great Star furrowed her brow. “…Shirone?”

More than the lack of a response, it was the look in Shirone’s eyes—like he already knew everything—that concerned her.

“Great Star.”

“I… I…”

Like a system crash, she appeared visibly flustered—something entirely unfamiliar for her.

‘If I speak the truth, history will change.’ Of course, the Triumvirate of the Twelve Apostles, Uorin, and Richard would maintain the original timeline, but…

“I’m heading off now.”

It didn’t matter. It was a meaningless concern.

Shirone’s body dissolved into the light of Miracle Stream, and in an instant, he arrived at the altar on the surface.

Veron, on the verge of death, said to Shura: “Go to Hexa. Don’t let the meaning I left in this world be in vain.”

“I won’t! You know how much I hate that little brat! What about the Council of Ten?!”

In this slightly twisted version of history, Shirone was able to hear their conversation for the first time.

‘Did he know?’

In the end, Shura had come to him, and the Iron Axis he left behind became humanity’s only hope.

Shirone looked up at the sky.

‘Nané.’

The moment he saw Nané’s face—detached, observing the world passively—rage began to boil within him again.

‘If I defeat Buddha…’ Then the tragic future won’t happen.

Channeling the full power of his will, Shirone sped toward Nané.

‘Hand of God.’

They had clashed over the truth of the universe once before, but looking back, how immature he had been.

‘That wasn’t it. The truth… isn’t something you define with logic alone—it’s the collective heart of all humanity, and the pain that comes with it.’

“Nané!”

A Photon Cannon was born in the palm of a giant hand of light, and Nané’s eyes widened.

“That is…”

What sort of enlightenment could bring this forth?

But the surprise lasted only a moment. Nané’s body shuddered, and his eyes became hollow.

“Shirone.”

His voice held a slight note of anger.

As the Photon Cannon tore through the atmosphere at an incredible speed, Nané extended his hand.

Great Sun Tathāgata.

A Buddha’s realm equal to Yahweh. Nané’s body glowed like the sun.

Boom—

The Photon Cannon was effortlessly destroyed, and Nané looked down with a sorrowful expression.

“Yahweh, is this something you truly cannot give up?”

Tachyon.

Nané too had arrived at this point by transcending time and space through imaginary signals.

‘As expected… this won’t be easy.’

Shirone replied,

“Then you give it up. Try to understand the desperation of humanity that forces me to go this far.”

“That heart of yours is also an illusion, once it awakens.”

Nané’s arms moved.

“If you cannot free yourself from delusion, you will endlessly repeat the cycle of reincarnation in the Saha world.”

Ku-ku-ku-ku-ku…

The avatar of the Great Sun Tathāgata rose above the horizon and began to press its palms together as if to encompass the world.

A painful memory returned.

‘No.’

“Namu…”

Even Nané’s chanting triggered Shirone’s trauma, leaving him dizzy.

“Gwanse…”

Shirone turned away.

“Amy!”

The two massive palms of the Tathāgata gently covered the world—and met.

“Sound (Om).”

A single life was extinguished.

Though he hadn’t seen it with his eyes, the image burned vividly in Shirone’s mind.

“Wha…?”

He turned his neck stiffly, and saw Nané’s face—crying tears of blood.

“Don’t avert your eyes from the truth, Yahweh.”

“No… no—”

The next moment, Shirone, consumed by killing intent, shot toward Nané like an arrow.

“Nanéeeeeee!”

And then—

“Sermon.”

The world flashed in a blaze of light—and Shirone’s consciousness was severed.

“Haaah!”

In the infinite void of nothingness, Shirone opened his conscious eyes.

‘Illusion.’

It felt like his heart had been ripped apart.

‘I’m sorry, Amy.’

And so, the first journey ended—without even being able to see the one he loved.

‘But now I understand.’

The moment Shirone tried to change Nané’s life, Nané began tracking him too.

‘There has to be a way. If Amy’s alive, I can fight at Yahweh’s level. If I can just take down Nané…’

It might be easier than expected.

“I’m going.”

As he committed to his second illusion, a new universe began to form once more within the void of infinity.

As long as the Iron Axis existed, there was no fear of losing his way.

Uorin furrowed her brow.

“Ugh!”

As Shirone’s Tachyon interacted with the Iron Axis, she was fully exposed to the time waves.

“The first cycle already ended?”

Kido didn’t look well either.

Being hit by waves faster than real-time felt like his life force was being drained.

“How fast was it?”

Uorin shook her head.

“I don’t know. Since it’s a waveform, there’s no fixed value… but about 8 hours passed per second.”

An insane rate of acceleration.

Rumble—

As they heard the vibration rushing in from outside, Huama looked toward the entrance.

In the distance, they saw the horde of demons approaching.

“It’s starting.”

The victors of the battle between good and evil had come to claim their final spoils.

“We’ll begin the count.”

As humanity entered the countdown to extinction, the World Governance Council vote wrapped up quickly.

It would take about ten minutes to finish the count—but truthfully, everyone already knew the outcome.

As Lampa and the others played cards, Armin sat deep in thought.

‘Siena…’

She hadn’t been listed among the survivors, but even before that, her identity had never been confirmed.

‘Please… let her still be alive.’

Keira walked up to Armin, who had his hands clasped together.

“Here, drink this.”

Armin accepted the tea with a small smile.

“Thanks.”

“You don’t have to pretend everything’s fine. Seriously, the way you love your sister—it’s kind of sweet.”

Armin repeated softly, “Thank you.”

Keira let out a small laugh and sat beside him, staring up at the ceiling.

“So, what are you gonna do once the war’s over?”

It wasn’t a question that suited the situation—but maybe, just maybe, thinking like that wasn’t so bad.

“I’m going to paint.”

“Hah! Like back in your intel days? Well, I’ll admit it—you weren’t half bad. But you sure? You’d earn more keeping me fed as a mage, you know.”

“There’s something I want to put on canvas.”

“What is it?”

“I don’t know yet.”

“What?”

“I can’t say yet. I’m going to wait. For the scene, the moment, the vision I want to show to all of humanity. I’m waiting so I can paint that.”

“…That’s so you,” Keira smiled.

The doors opened.

“The vote has concluded.”

Shirano stepped up to the World Governance Council podium and made the announcement.

“With this, all remaining kingdoms will rally under Tormia for the final stand. We hereby declare Queen Pony of Tormia the leader of the World Governance Council.”

The plaza erupted in applause.

Even as the ruler of the world, it was likely the harshest position of all—so there were no objections.

Even the Papacy of Lami, which once vied against Yahweh, couldn’t defy the truth revealed by Melkidu.

“And now, we will hear her inaugural address.”

Shirano stepped aside, and Pony took the podium.

“I…”

She remained silent for a long time, simply looking at the crowd.

‘I’m the leader of the world.’

Even if all that remained was the burden of responsibility… no—especially because of that, she was content.

‘I made my dream come true.’

So now, she could say what she truly wanted.

“As Queen of Tormia, I’ve always tried to be humble toward other nations.”

The crowd fell silent.

“It wasn’t because Tormia was weak, or because the others were stronger. It’s because behind me stood people I deeply love and respect—their lives, their very breath.”

If that’s what it meant to be responsible…

“And now, standing in a position that bears the weight of all humanity, I will remain just as humble. Because the power I now hold was not given by the gods—but by you.”

Pony opened her palm.

“If the end of this war can come by someone’s death, then I will gladly offer this life!”

As she clenched her fist and shouted, a thunderous cheer erupted.

“Let’s fight! Let’s fight!”

Morale soared. In everyone’s eyes burned a determination not to lose.

‘It’s drawing near.’

Ultima.

And in that moment, only one person remained silent with a bitter expression.

Zetaro, the last surviving member of the 4 Gustavs.

“It’s not a matter of immortality.”

It was Crown speaking from within Kuan’s body.

“Once you reach the end of reincarnation, death doesn’t hold any special meaning. So why keep on living?”

Siena listened in silence.

“This is about existence. How long can we continue to exist? If we stay too long—and there’s nothing new anymore—we too will collapse.”

It was the law of the universe.

“The Missing Link. Forgetting is necessary. The Illuminati is fighting against ‘nothingness.’ If there were truly nothing to begin with, then it would just be… nothing.”

Siena didn’t care.

“What about Kuan? You promised, remember? That you’d fix what was broken.”

Crown limped and muttered, “This body’s incredibly inconvenient. How did he even fight with it? Wait… was I scammed?”

“Stop dodging the question!”

Crown chuckled.

“I restored the severed nerves with mine. But the mind? That’s beyond my reach.”

“Liar! You’re the scammer! If you’re suppressing Kuan—!”

“Well, there’s no way to prove otherwise, is there? But it’s the truth. Honestly, I wish I had some kind of manual. How am I supposed to control this? I can’t fight like this.”

“Who exactly are you fighting…?”

Siena was about to press the issue when a massive boom echoed from the sky.

“…Hmm.”

Twelve dragons soared to intercept the incoming Theraphos battleship.

“It’s begun.”

To the Illuminati, neither Emptiness nor Love mattered.

They merely wanted to find something—anything—in this miracle-like anomaly of a world.

‘A world that refuses to forget.’

Maybe the outer world had simply reached this point too easily.

‘A way to surpass infinity…’

Siena froze—just for a second—as Theraphos warriors suddenly appeared on all sides.

“Humans. The verdict has been made. We shall judge you by the will of God.”

“Do something!” Siena snapped. “You created the gods—can’t you persuade them?!”

Crown shrugged. “That doesn’t make me a god. Gods, you see, are entities that calculate multiverses—”

The warriors charged.

“Eliminate them!”

“Tch!”

Siena unleashed a freezing spell, but their alien tech easily overpowered her magic.

“Theraphos is strong.”

“You—!”

As Siena glared at Crown, enraged by his indifference—

“Do not resist. There will be no pain.”

A warrior slammed her down and pointed a plasma sword at her throat.

“Kuan!”

Crown’s eyes widened the moment he heard her voice—and something inside him began to unravel.

“…Huh?”

Something massive surged into his mind.

“Wha…?”

–Protect Siena.

The signal struck his consciousness—and the world around him shattered like glass.

“…Whoa.”

Only a member of the Illuminati could sense what was truly happening.

‘This guy’s thoughts… they break the symmetry of the universe.’

The ultimate asymmetry.

In the next moment, the entire battlefield tilted violently—and all the Theraphos warriors went tumbling to the ground.
