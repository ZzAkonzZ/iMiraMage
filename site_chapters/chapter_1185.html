Chapter 1185:

Ymir’s Mind, Deep Layer 1.5

"Live."

Kangnan spoke.

"You don’t have to stay by my side. Just do what you want. I will never resent you."

When Ymir’s Ultima, with its ten billion eyes, infiltrated Layer 1.5, she realized something.

‘This is a man who has lived a life I can’t even imagine.’

Gaold’s abnormal level of self-restraint and his obsession with Miro.

‘Sein must have thought the same thing.’

Since Miro had chosen Gaold, the father-daughter relationship was now nothing more than a hollow sentiment.

Sein’s family entered Kangnan’s home.

“There you are.”

After dealing with the Tamo organization, the market had stabilized, but they had closed their business.

‘Spot the difference.’

The boundary between dreams and reality had become clear, and now only a choice remained.

“I’ve decided to live here,” Kangnan said.

"After much thought, this is my conclusion. I’ve realized how painful reality is. I will live here."

Sein had no objections.

As Arius whimpered, Miro approached Gaold, who was sitting at the dining table.

“Are you feeling any better?”

Gaold had only tasted the pain of reality briefly, but he had been in shock for a while.

“... Yeah.”

"Now that Kangnan and my dad have given their approval, it’s over. You must be happy—you’ve got a pretty little bride like me."

Little bride.

Gaold, who had been holding a cigarette between his lips, asked,

"Aren’t you going to ask for my opinion?"

“What’s there to ask? I now understand just how persistent you are. Why, do you hate it?”

It was awkward to think of Miro knowing the real Gaold from reality.

"Aha, I get it. You're going crazy having to wait until I grow up, aren't you?"

Gaold put out his cigarette.

“Don’t be ridiculous, kid. If you knew how I lived, you’d just shut up.”

Miro knew.

That Gaold had endured twenty years in the hell of unbearable pain.

‘Yeah, this is right.’

By compromising with Miro, the world had reached perfection, and Ymir would no longer be able to infiltrate.

Sein asked.

“By the way… what about Shirone?”

Since that day, Shirone had remained in a deep sleep, as if he were dead.

Standing beside Luber, Monga spoke.

“We’ve tried several methods, but none worked. Even in a dream within a dream, we should still be able to make contact…”

“There’s a condition,” Luber said.

“The only case where even a Dream Keeper cannot access a dream… is if the Dream Keeper themselves has granted approval.”

Kangnan asked.

“Did he approve it?”

"A long time ago..."

Back then, he had accepted a proposal from a man now referred to as the first Yora.

“The condition was simple. He wanted to pass on a message to those who shared the same ideals. It’s called a Legacy Dream. Through this dream, his will may be revived, but…”

No one knew for sure.

"When will he wake up?" Kangnan asked.

“No idea. Time doesn’t matter in dreams. It’s about how much information is being processed. When all the Legacy Dream data has been transmitted, the dream within a dream will end.”

At that moment, Shirone’s body suddenly arched like a bow, and his arms trembled violently.

“Five Star!”

Monga checked his condition, but he remained unconscious.

“What’s happening?”

Luber studied Shirone, his expression growing serious as he observed the near-seizure state.

‘In a dream, the Five Star isn’t affected by simultaneous events. That means the thing being influenced is…’

The Demonic Sword Armand.

The power known as Materialization was exerting a massive influence in reality.

“A seizure?”

Just how immense was this power for its unconscious wielder to be suffering this much?

Louver looked up at the sky of the 1.5th-layer world.

‘What are you doing?’

Meanwhile, at the Sun, Shirone clenched his teeth.

“Kh…!”

From between his outstretched palms, an enormous amount of material gushed forth.

A substance that surpassed human knowledge—something even the harsh conditions of space could not destroy.

‘I can’t complete it all at once. First, I need to construct the skeletal framework in the shape of a ring around the Sun’s diameter…’

Construction progress: 0.00134%.

The estimated workload was around 64 billion man-months, and even reaching 1% completion seemed like a distant goal.

‘I have to do this.’

If divine law were fully enforced, this universe would meet its end as just another disposable bubble.

A solar storm surged toward him.

“Kh!”

Once again, the laws twisted, and their effects were immediately felt through simultaneous events.

‘It’s okay. Mom will buy me some time.’

If he could finish within that window—

As he increased Materialization’s output, the boundary where the material emerged glowed red-hot.

Born and assembled at the speed of light, the gigantic mechanical structures flew across space.

The impact of the Final Adjustment directly struck the Pyramid of Truth in the Kingdom of Paras.

The inscriptions on its walls were replaced by new concepts, and the Baptism of Benevolence surged in once more.

"No! No, this isn't happening!"

Glen, a priest of the Lami Church, clung desperately to his sanity as it teetered on the edge of collapse.

"I… I love Lucia…"

Lucia had dismissed it as a mere memory, but for Glen, it had been an event that changed the entire trajectory of his life.

Born into a poor family, Glen had faced a harsh life from an early age.

A sliver of light had appeared when his parents embraced the faith of the Lami Church.

By sheer luck, he was admitted to a monastery, where he met Lucia’s family.

"Hello? I'm Lucia."

"Uh… yeah. I'm Glen. Nice to meet you."

At seventeen, the two of them quickly became close, though their parents' circumstances were more complicated.

It was a day of missionary work at the monastery.

"Glen, Mother. Thank you for coming despite your busy schedule. May the grace of Creia be with you."

Lucia's mother, a priestess, was kind to everyone, and Glen’s mother liked her as well.

"Oh, no need for thanks. It’s our duty."

At the time, Glen and Lucia were paired together, going from house to house to spread the faith.

Lucia’s mother smiled.

"You two are always together. It’s nice to see. Glen is a wonderful young man."

He should not have heard that.

‘That moment… I should never have heard those words.’

No parent dislikes hearing praise for their child, and Glen’s mother beamed with pride.

"Lucia is such a kind and upright girl. I hear she’s considered a future High Priestess at the monastery."

"Oh, no, that’s too much."

"Both Glen and Lucia have such bright futures ahead. If they were to end up together someday…"

The sentence was abruptly cut off.

For the first time, Glen saw something unusual in Lucia’s mother's eyes—a cold, sharp glare.

"Oh, I’m sorry… I overstepped…"

"Glen’s mother, please never say such things again. Even among fellow believers, there are lines that must be respected."

"I’m so sorry. I didn’t mean—"

Unable to bear seeing his mother bowing in shame, Glen turned away.

Lucia asked, "Glen, what’s wrong?"

"It’s nothing."

"Nothing? Your face is full of anger. Did I do something wrong?"

"Lucia…"

Glen clenched his fists.

"I don’t feel well. I’m going inside to rest."

Though time had dulled his emotions, the scar etched into his heart had never healed.

"No!"

Glen grabbed his head, his thoughts spiraling.

"Was that really all it was? Did I try to take Lucia just because of that? I… I loved her! I really loved her!"

Love. A beautiful word.

"Why are you trying to deceive me?! Do you think I’ll waver because of something like this? I am a priest of the Lami Church!"

How many things do we turn away from simply because they are wrapped in words that sound beautiful?

"O Lord, look upon me! Banish this demon’s deception from my soul!"

As he prayed, Glen heard whispers.

Glen. Glen.

"I believe in You. Please save me."

There are no demons.

"Huh?! Have mercy on me… Please… I offer everything to You…"

Is this not simply the result of your own actions?

"Nooo! Nooooo!"

Glen buried his face in the ground, tears and mucus spilling from his eyes.

That night, back at the monastery.

"Glen, what’s wrong? You haven’t spoken to me at all. Just tell me what’s going on."

"Go away! Just leave me alone!" Glen shouted.

"… Alright. Let’s talk tomorrow. Good night."

Hearing Lucia’s retreating footsteps, Glen swept everything off his desk in a fit of rage.

"Damn it all!"

He was still seething.

"So, I’m poor. That’s it, isn’t it? The idea of someone like me being with their daughter disgusts them. But guess what? Lucia likes me. If I wanted to, I could…"

Glen lifted his chin.

"I could ruin her. Make it so she could never dream of becoming a High Priestess…"

"No!"

The moment he screamed, the walls around him lit up with countless glowing inscriptions.

"You bastard! Why are you doing this to me?! I really love her now! Back then, I was just so angry…!"

And then—events began to overlap.

"Aaaaagh!"

He slammed his head into the wall, but he did not die. That was his final act of defiance.

"Hhhhg! Hhhhg!"

No matter how many choices he made, his life only continued to fall apart.

"Hhhhhh!"

And at last, Glen understood.

‘I am disappearing.’

He was no longer a person. He was becoming just an object.

As if his skull were splitting open, he suddenly heard a familiar voice from the hallway.

"Glen!"

Lucia was running toward him.

"Glen! There you are! Do you know how long I’ve been looking for—"

"Aaaaagh!"

With an inhuman screech, Glen lunged up and wrapped his hands around Lucia’s throat.

"Die! Just die!"

"G-Glen…"

Pinned to the ground, she stared up at him. His face was twisted like that of a beast.

"You never loved me! You regretted it, didn’t you?! That night, when we were together, you only accepted it because you had no choice!"

"That’s not true."

Lucia had also been baptized in the Blessing of Humanity. She knew exactly what Glen was saying.

"We were too young! I was scared! But now… now I love you! I really do, Glen!"

"Die!"

As his grip tightened, Lucia grasped his wrists.

‘The Right Hand of God.’

A hand that could stir the waters of the human heart—yet it found nothing to grasp.

‘He’s lost his heart.’

"Die! Die! Die!"

Glen threw all his weight into his hands, and Lucia had no choice but to use her last resort.

‘The Left Hand of the Devil.’

With just a brief touch on his shoulder, Glen’s body twisted like a corkscrew.

Falling to the side, writhing in agony, Glen trembled violently. Lucia knelt beside him.

"Glen! Are you okay?! Wake up!"

"Urrghh…!"

As his grotesquely contorted body let out a choked groan, footsteps echoed from the darkness behind them.

"Euphra."

Lucia turned. The Great Swordmaster Euphra was approaching with vacant eyes.

Raising his sword slowly, he spoke.

"All is as God wills…"

A person who has lost their reason becomes nothing more than a relic of fate.

"God awaits us!"

The sword came down to sever Lucia’s head—

But in that instant, a mass of light struck from the side.

With a thud, Euphra was slammed against the wall, and from around the corner, Shirone emerged.

"Lucia! Are you okay?!"

"Ah…"

The moment she met Shirone’s eyes—eyes filled with human emotion—tears streamed down Lucia’s face.

"Yahweh…"

A heart untouched by the Law.
