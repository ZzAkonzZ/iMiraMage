Chapter 492:

Thud! Thud!

The giant guards who had reached the 6th stage of the Art of One began to mobilize from the castle of Matei.

Standing nearly 80 meters tall, their heads touched the sky, and from their perspective, humans and machines looked like mere insects.

As their massive feet stomped the ground, numerous rebels and Gurois were simultaneously crushed flat.

The only ones who could see the giants' faces were the pilots of the Titan, a Mecha 3rd stage system.

Crude swallowed hard as he watched the 20 giants stride over the rebels like grains of sand.

Even when he was a citizen, he had never seen such giants up close.

When he saw them, he finally realized the truth.

This was a war they could never win from the start.

"Don't be ridiculous!"

Crude forced himself to deny the fear rising within him and advanced the Titan.

The enormous giants used the power of Schema to nimbly leap towards the Titan like small humans.

Boom!

As the giant's fist struck the Titan's body, all the joints in the waist twisted, causing it to stagger.

"Ugh!"

He momentarily regained the balance of the machine, but as he stepped back, his allies were crushed.

Crude didn't even register their deaths.

In the midst of the battle between giant creatures and machines, both rebels and giants were dying alike.

"Damn it!"

Using the recoil of the machine, Crude launched a powerful attack at the giant.

The Titan's output was beyond imagination, but the Schema of the giants at the 6th stage of the Art of One was layered to produce an impossible power for living beings.

Boom!

The thermal flash cannon fired at close range grazed the giant's shoulder.

The giant let out an earth-shattering roar as the flesh on its shoulder disappeared, but the wound quickly healed.

The process of the thick nerves and muscles rising and intertwining like vines was reminiscent of the birth of nature.

As the enraged giants pounded the Titan, the rebels on the ground felt as if thunder was crashing from a clear sky.

As the Titan's mechanical parts exploded, tons of debris fell, striking both Gurois and giants.

Boom!

Finally, the engine exploded, paralyzing the controls, and the Titan began to slowly topple over.

"Huff! Huff! Huff!"

Crude felt himself plummeting tens of meters and timed his move.

Just before crashing into the ground, he pressed the emergency escape button.

As the cockpit opened, a Guroi popped out and fell vertically.

Crude briefly lost consciousness but then came to.

The Mecha 2nd stage system Guroi was also inoperable due to the impact of the fall.

He sat in the cockpit, looking up at the sky.

It was only the sky seen through the vision, but the sun rising towards noon was blindingly bright.

Under that sun, endless giants were destroying another Titan.

"This place… is hell."

A giant's foot was descending from tens of meters above.

It wasn't specifically targeting Crude, but it was clear what would happen if he stayed lying there.

Pressing the emergency escape button again, Crude ejected from the Guroi and landed on the ground using the power of the Mecha 1st stage system Piper.

Having come this far, there was no turning back.

The rebels had no choice but to advance, and advance they would.

"To the end."

To Aravot, where Anke Ra awaited.

As he finished his resolve and lifted his head, Crude's eyes filled with fear.

"What… is that…?"

A tremendously ominous aura was swirling from Aravot beyond the giant's castle.

It was as if the entire Aravot was burning in black flames, and a strange wind that seemed to call for death could be heard.

Whoooosh!

Crude's face twisted in despair.

Only death.

The aura was so powerful that it melted his iron will like molten metal, making him certain that entering Aravot meant certain death.

What on earth was there?

The fleeting question vanished in the face of the fear of annihilation.

'We must retreat. Everyone must flee. It's the only way we can survive.'

Crude decided to acknowledge their defeat in the war as he reached for his personal communication device.

"All troops…"

Once spoken, it could never be taken back.

Even if the words could be retracted, the will that admitted defeat would never rise again.

'There's no way we can win!'

As Crude took a deep breath and was about to issue the order to the entire rebel force, he saw a woman.

Plu was breaking through the giants' offensive, running straight towards the terrifying flames of Aravot.

So, she had come this far?

"It was a war we could never win from the start."

But why was she heading towards the center of annihilation, where only death awaited?

"Damn it! I won't be swayed by the likes of you!"

With a fierce glare, Crude set Piper's functions to maximum and kicked off the ground.

11th floor underground in Zebul.

In the underground of Zebul, the pinnacle of the intelligence of countless angels was located.

The heavenly munitions factory, Zion.

Everything produced in heaven came from here, including Kariel's Great Law Book and Uriel's Paradise Staff.

Countless machines, whose functions were unimaginable, clanked and splashed molten metal as they operated beneath the endlessly extending steel bridge.

"The path is complicated."

Zulu surveyed the maze-like paths of the steel bridge from a distance, looking for a way down.

A small black bird with a broad beak sat on his shoulder.

It was Erga, a 10-tier monster Zulu had first successfully summoned at the age of three.

Though it had no special abilities other than mimicking human speech, Zulu always kept Erga by her side when walking alone in dark paths like this.

"Suck. Suck. Suck. Suck."

Erga jumped off Zulu's shoulder, passed under the steel bridge, and mimicked the sound of sucking on a pacifier.

"Yes, it's there."

Zulu chose the third path from the six branching bridges.

The stairs leading down were hidden behind the railing.

-Why do you want to go alone?

When Zulu said she would find the Metagate alone, Sain couldn't understand her decision.

Etella had to meet Frankwine, so they had to separate, but Inglis and Zion were both in Zebul.

It would have been better to join Armin's group and move together to increase the chances of success, but Zulu insisted on going alone.

-It's better for me this way.

Sain, pondering Zulu's words, no longer objected, and thus she found the Metagate production factory at the southern end of Zion.

Numerous Metagates were moving around on belt-like trailers.

It was a place no human could easily enter if not for the special wartime situation.

Even thinking about the high value of Metagates in the land of the earth, the sight was enough to make one covet, but Zulu's gaze remained as calm as ever.

"After causing all this trouble, are you now planning to run away?"

Hearing a woman's voice from across the trailer, Zulu stopped reaching for the Metagate and turned her head.

It was a woman with four arms, her skin dark and her slit eyes like a snake's, with pupils that absorbed light, making her look like a shadow from a distance.

Zulu checked the number of red-glowing triangles above the woman's head.

Three-sided Mara Kali.

She was a direct subordinate of the Archangel of Annihilation, Faiel.

'Strong.'

Zulu's eyes turned cold.

She had personally experienced the prowess of the three-sided Mara since coming to heaven, but the Mara before her exuded an aura that was on a different level from other Maras, regardless of strength.

"Those who come must also go. It's just to follow the natural order. Do not interfere."

It wasn't a slightly comical continental language, nor was it a voice that put people at ease.

"Interfere?"

Kali spread her four arms wide, revealing a sword, shield, whip, and skull in each hand.

"No creature can defy the god of death."

As Kali's sword slashed, a black line cut through space, rushing towards Zulu.

Zulu evaded with teleportation, dispelled Erga, and summoned a 3-tier monster, Cougar.

Roar!

The giant beast approached as if to devour Kali, swinging its sharp claws.

Clang!

As Kali's shield blocked the claws, black smoke rose and enveloped Cougar's body.

"Roar!"

Cougar's fur evaporated, black spots appeared on its skin, and pus swelled as if it were decaying.

Kali's four weapons all embodied the concept of death, and among them, the shield caused the fastest death by inducing disease, making it impossible for any creature to survive.

"All living things shall bow before me."

As Kali swung her sword, Cougar's massive body was sliced in half.

Cougar was a top-tier summoned monster and the most physically powerful among the monsters Zulu could summon.

Suck. Suck. Suck. Suck. Suck.

As Kali subdued Cougar in one blow, the sound of Zulu sucking on the pacifier quickened.

"Now, choose. What kind of death suits you?"

With an evil smile, Kali spread her upper body wide, and her black legs dissolved into smoke, lifting her body.

"…This is dangerous."

Zulu truly thought so.

"Huff. Huff."

Numerous angels were clutching their stomachs and sobbing along the corridors of Zebul.

At the end of that humiliating scene stood Frankwine, who had made a pact with Satan, wearing a smug expression.

Behind him, the grotesquely transformed subordinates of Yameng were looking around with vicious eyes, searching for another angel.

"Kekeke! More, more angels…"

Although Satan was free from the abilities of angels, he knew from the dawn of history how powerful they were.

Even if he could break the concept of angels, if they united and attacked with their physical abilities alone, the outcome would be uncertain.

Therefore, Satan sought the easiest and most convenient way to kill angels.

The result was the subordinates of Yameng, combined with Garas.

"Ugh! I'd rather not exist like this…"

Feeling the seed of Garas growing inside her, the angel tightly closed her eyes and reversed her halo.

With a pop, the angel's body turned into light and vanished.

Shame.

For angels who prided themselves on their noble spirit, this was a more terrifying emotion than annihilation.

Following the first angel's self-destruction, all the angels lying in the corridor reversed their halos and erased their own existence.

"Kekeke, Laisis was a fool."

Using the nature of Garas to gain great power?

"No, just becoming Garas is enough."

As all the angels before him committed suicide, Satan's presence grew even stronger through the seesaw of law.

"Uoooooo!"

His body grew larger, and horns sprouted and twisted from his temples.

As his thick chest muscles twitched like armor and he clenched his fists, flames of hellfire erupted.

"Not yet. I need a bit more."

Satan, with fangs extending down to his chin, looked at his fist and then shot flames down the corridor, shouting.

"Go! Find every single angel! Ravage them! From now on, heaven is our world!"

"Kiaaaaaa!"

Dozens of subordinates, transformed into the form of Garas, ran towards the blazing flames at the end of the corridor.

The angels' banquet hall.

"Sob. Sob."

While the angels who had been attacked by Garas erased their own existence, one angel had hidden in the empty banquet hall.

But even she could not defy the law, and her belly was gradually swelling.

Adio, the angel of fulfillment.

She was so happy to exist in this world that she couldn't bring herself to leave, even knowing the outcome.

"I don't want to disappear. I have so much to do."

"Great angel."

Among the three Maras, from one-sided to three-sided, who were guarding Adio, the leader, Mara of Regret, Rubel, spoke.

"Please endure a little longer."

It was the only comfort they could offer.

Even if the seed of Garas was conceived, the angel's durability would prevent complete annihilation.

But what meaning would that have?

Beyond the physical damage, the mental shock of an angel, who dealt with concepts, giving birth to a monster's child would be immense.

"No, no! Aaaaaah!"

As Adio screamed with wide eyes, her belly exploded with a pop.

Her halo shook wildly, and according to the law of her kin, the three Maras disappeared from reality.

"Sob, sob…"

Adio collapsed to the floor, shedding miserable tears.

What she realized now was that she should have chosen annihilation like the other angels from the beginning.

Gurgle. Gurgle.

The creature that emerged from Adio's body ignored its mother and slipped out of the banquet hall.

It was the birth of a Nephilim, something no one wanted.
