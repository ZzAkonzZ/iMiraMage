Chapter 438:

Rebel First Command.

Commander Crude stood outside, gazing at the full moon.

The memories of losing a unit every time that moon rose stirred a sense of vengeance within him.

'I will win, for the sake of my fallen comrades.'

"You seem to have a lot on your mind."

Crude turned his head.

Seeing Sein approaching, he looked back at the moon and spoke.

"Not really. There's been no report of angel patrols yet. It looks like we'll get through this full moon night safely."

Sein's gaze followed Crude's.

"Indeed."

Crude turned around and suddenly remembered something.

"How's your research going? Is it finished?"

Sein had been tirelessly observing planetary movements day and night to obtain the precise coordinates needed for divine retribution.

"To some extent."

"Astronomy, huh. What does that mean to a mere creature under the sky, especially during a war?"

"It means nothing yet. But soon, it will be the only thing that matters."

Crude raised an eyebrow.

"I don't understand what you're saying."

"I can't reveal it yet. Anyway, it's not bad for the rebels. At least we won't be hit unilaterally."

'A kind of self-destructive tactic?'

Crude could only guess that much from Sein's research on planetary movements.

Even Gaold and Sein had found Shirone's method absurd when they first heard it.

"Salute!"

Kanya, who had come out of the barracks, saluted.

Next to her stood Lena and her father, Witcher.

Having left his deceased wife and come to Heaven, Witcher was now a military officer teaching maintenance skills to Mecha members.

Crude returned the salute.

"So, what brings you here at this hour?"

"Well, it's just that… it felt a bit stuffy in the barracks."

While it wasn't encouraged for soldiers to take nighttime walks with their families, Crude didn't reprimand them.

Every rebel had the right to feel uneasy on the night of the full moon.

Kanya asked Sein.

"Any news from Shirone?"

"Gardrak visited a few days ago. It seems things are progressing smoothly."

The story of Shirone overturning the Second Command with his Nephilim abilities was a secret known only to a few in the First Command.

Like the other officers, Crude was displeased with Shirone being incorporated into the Second Command but didn't show it.

What truly mattered wasn't that.

"Now that I think about it, I don't believe Raycis genuinely accepted Shirone."

Sein turned around.

"There was conflict, right? A difference in views on biological experiments?"

"On the surface. But you don't abandon half your forces over biological experiments during a war. What she did back then didn't make sense to the Mecha or the Nor."

"She crossed the line? What did she do?"

Crude's face twisted cruelly.

"Raycis…"

Screeeeech!

A tearing sound from the sky swallowed Crude's words.

"Ugh!"

Crude grimaced as the sound waves struck his skull directly, and Kanya and Lena clutched their heads and fell to the ground.

Witcher, pressing his two daughters down with his arms, looked up at the sky where electricity flowed in a dome shape.

"It's ultrasound. It collided with the magnetic field."

Sein, with an emotionless face, raised his head, his iron wheel eyes gleaming.

Something reflecting the moonlight was flying in rapidly from the other side of the sky.

Search algorithm terminated.

Babel turned off its search function and transmitted the results of its scan of all of Purgatory to Cariel via wireless communication.

Magnetic field detected. Target probability 92.7%.

All she could see was a dense forest, but the program detected that 0.3% of the overall landscape had the same structure as other landscapes.

An artificiality as if light had been copied and pasted.

It was the rebel command center.

Switching to attack mode.

Babel's eyes glowed red.

Strike approved.

As Babel, flying straight with a sonic boom, entered the command center's airspace, Sein cast mass teleportation.

Boom!

A massive crater with a diameter of 30 meters formed at the point where Babel struck the ground.

Emergency sirens blared throughout the command center, and lights began to turn on in the barracks.

"Damn it! What the hell is this?"

As Crude gritted his teeth and moved towards the crater, a tall woman clad in black walked up the crater with clanking footsteps.

Kanya's voice trembled.

"I-it's a machine?"

Eliminate the subjects.

A short command was issued to Babel's program.

Infinite Judicial Halo - Valhalla Action.

Ikasa's unique ability, Valhalla Action, has the power to reverse cause and effect.

When she desires something, the result is realized on the condition that she repays the time and effort required for the action later.

For example, if she desires to go from point A to point B, Ikasa can reach the distance that would normally take 10 seconds instantly.

However, if there are obstacles between points A and B, she must also pay the cost of bypassing or destroying them.

If it takes 5 minutes to destroy the obstacles to reach point B, Ikasa, upon arrival, will be incapacitated for 5 minutes and 10 seconds to repay the cause.

Calculating this causality is the core of Valhalla.

When you press a balloon with your finger, the opposite side bulges. When she sets the desire A, the result B automatically follows.

Therefore, desires beyond Valhalla's calculation ability cannot be realized in reality.

For example, if she desires to enter a rock, it is impossible because there is no space inside the rock.

However, if there is any space inside the rock, even if Ikasa is unaware of it, it will be realized.

This means that Valhalla's computational system is influenced by the Akashic Records, encompassing the whole rather than mathematical truth.

Similarly, desiring to cut off a human's head cannot be realized.

Valhalla cannot calculate a human's future actions, thus preventing the "action."

'I just need to eliminate Shirone.'

Ikasa's eyes, looking down at the rising dust cloud, were filled with cold murderous intent.

To her, who wished to be reinstated as a regular angel, Shirone was a personal revenge target and a necessary kill.

She had identified Cariel's suspicious movements in Heaven, but as someone who had committed two serious crimes, Ikasa saw Shirone as her only lifeline.

'They're coming back.'

22 minutes and 42 seconds.

The time it would have taken Ikasa to bring down a mountain with just her fists under normal causality.

Even considering the full moon, it was a celestial feat.

During those 22 minutes and 42 seconds, the rebels on the ground struggled to clear the dust cloud.

They used the group magic Mahagart to blow away the dust, but the smoke from the collapsed mountain didn't dissipate easily.

Shirone and Plu, who had barely escaped before the cliff collapsed, stayed alert without moving.

Plu, who had taken out Phoenix from Cubrick and swung it around, applied the Dawkins algorithm to her Spirit Zone.

A function that automatically reacts when an object penetrates the Spirit Zone at a certain speed.

Following this, Shirone also took out Armand and activated Diamond Armor, and Plu immediately noticed the change.

The ragged robe was now clean, and the artificial brain emitted a blue light similar to the Ultima System seen in Babel.

"What happened?"

"I integrated with the Ultima System."

Plu thought about what that meant but quickly dismissed it.

That wasn't the most important thing right now.

"Does it have any combat functions?"

He wasn't sure yet.

He hadn't fought with Armand since integrating with the Ultima System.

"I don't know. It changed from sharing thoughts to integration, but how it will work…"

Finally, the dust cloud cleared, and the bright full moon illuminated the ground again.

The revealed landscape was devastating.

The command center cliff was shattered beyond recognition, and Norin corpses were scattered under the debris.

The result of a single surprise attack.

Such abilities that defied common sense were why the subjects feared angels.

"That is…!"

Shirone's eyes widened as he saw the angel floating in the sky. At the same time, unwanted memories resurfaced.

'Ikasa.'

The fallen angel who had made him helplessly watch Amy, Tess, and Arin being kidnapped.

Her movements were so fast that even now, the thought of it sent chills down his spine.

"Shirone, what's wrong?"

"I've seen her before. She's a fallen angel."

Though better than a regular angel, it didn't make him feel any more likely to survive.

Ikasa crossed her arms and descended.

The ominous vibration made the rebels' hearts pound.

"Hehehe, you should have come out when I called. Did you think you could defy me, you worthless creatures?"

As her words, transmitted through the angel's power, reached their minds, one of the surviving officers shouted.

"Focus on me! We'll counter with group magic!"

The officer prepared the grand magic Aruope.

A magic known for its supreme cutting power, utilizing the essence of air.

Ikasa, watching the dozens of mages connecting their Spirit Force to the officer, sneered.

"Fools."

Ikasa moved.

It was like the movement of an electron existing as a probability in space.

Appearing like an afterimage before the dozens of soldiers, she knocked them all down with her delicate wrist.

From lower-ranking mages to officers, the pyramid of ranks collapsed quickly, and the coup ended in an instant.

"Ugh!"

As Ikasa's fist deeply embedded in the officer's abdomen, blood spurted from his mouth.

"Kekeke! Killing humans is always fun!"

Ikasa was strong.

For someone who could bring down a mountain in 20 minutes, a few dozen human mages were no match, even without Valhalla Action.

But she adhered to the concept of origin, and Shirone clearly understood the function of her halo.

'Equivalent exchange of cause and effect.'

The Ultima System accurately sensed this.

As Ikasa, having wiped out the rebels, turned her head, electricity ran down Shirone and Plu's spines.

Her eyes were filled with the desire for revenge.

"Shirone, do you know how long I've waited for this day? You ran away like a rat back then."

"I haven't forgotten what you did."

"Oh, really? You couldn't even move a finger and let the women be taken. Do you think it will be any different now?"

Shirone furrowed his brow and lowered his stance.

"Be careful, Senior. That angel's halo seems to reverse cause and effect."

Plu, having seen the rebels fall, quickly understood.

"Let's go on the defensive. We need to buy time. There are probably rebels surviving underground."

Ikasa puffed her cheeks and laughed.

"Puh! Do you know why I hate humans? Because they are fundamentally delusional. They think they can achieve something, even though they're just human1, human2."

Ikasa charged, swinging her fist in front of Plu, causing the air to vibrate.

Plu's upper body bent like a willow branch against the gale-like attack.

It was the effect of the Dawkins algorithm, but her spine felt like it was about to snap, and she couldn't hold out for long.

"Ugh!"

As Ikasa's left hook landed, Plu's body flew like a laundry line.

She had used Phoenix to cushion the impact by striking her hand, but the shock was still unbearable.

"Hahaha! Struggling is a bug's characteristic!"

As Ikasa laughed and turned, a massive tentacle appeared before her eyes, grabbing her face.

The tentacle arched into the sky and then slammed Ikasa vertically into the ground.

Boom!

Lying spread-eagle, Ikasa blinked, her face still buried in the tentacle.

'What is this?'

The impact was so strong it made her head throb. The speed was so fast she couldn't even react.

It was a strange and heavy blow that couldn't be excused as a mere surprise attack.

"You insolent…!"

Ikasa cut the tentacle with a knife-hand and sprang towards Shirone.

"Watch out!"

Plu shouted, but the scene that followed was unexpected.

Shirone dodged all of Ikasa's brutal attacks by swaying his upper body.

'I can see it! I can see Ikasa's attacks!'

If Armand's role was to maximize the user's physical abilities, now Armand itself was Shirone's body.

Everything he could do in Diamond Armor was as easy as moving his fingers.

"A mere human? I am an angel's body!"

Ikasa threw a powerful punch.

As her fist reached its target, Shirone's body spun quickly.

A heavy tentacle shot out from his robe, striking Ikasa's cheek and sending her flying horizontally.

Boom!

Ikasa crashed into the rubble of the collapsed command center, and Plu stared in disbelief.

In front of Shirone, who was glaring at Ikasa, Ataraxia was gathering at a speed several times faster than before.
