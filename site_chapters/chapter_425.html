Chapter 425:

Raesis returned to her office, draped in her red cloak, deep in thought.

Her aide, who had followed her in, cautiously asked, "Commander, what should we do about Shirone?"

If Shirone had shown his Nephilim abilities from the start, it wouldn't have been an issue. Nephilim were such beings.

But Shirone had already surpassed Raesis's potential.

This would inevitably affect the Nor rebels, who placed great faith in Spirit Force.

For Raesis, it was a tricky problem.

'I knew they didn't trust me, but I didn't expect them to stab me in the back like this.'

"Commander, your orders…?"

The aide, growing impatient, pressed again.

Outside, the atmosphere was filled with curiosity about Shirone's rank.

The longer Raesis delayed, the more her absolute authority would be questioned.

"Ha ha ha ha ha!"

Raesis threw her head back and laughed, leaving the aide bewildered.

When she stopped laughing, she looked at the aide.

"I've finally found the perfect element to complement my flaws."

"So, Shirone…"

"Yes, I will absorb him. Shirone's immense Spirit Force will be a vessel for any biological trait. I can't miss this opportunity."

The aide's eyes darted around.

He had seen Shirone's Spirit Force with his own eyes, so he had no objections.

But Shirone was a symbol of the rebels.

If public opinion turned against them, everything they had built could collapse in an instant.

Raesis smirked.

"I know what you're thinking."

"I'm sorry, but it's dangerous for you too, Commander."

Raesis swirled her cloak and turned away.

"We'll cultivate a clone from Shirone's genetic sample. After clinical trials on the clone, I'll absorb it once we're sure."

"Is that possible? Shirone isn't a citizen. Human cloning is a sensitive field, and even slight differences in genetic sequences can lead to failure."

Citizens and non-citizens were both human, but they had evolved separately for a long time.

Even with the same number of chromosomes, differences in genetic sequences could make successful cloning unlikely.

"Even if it's possible, it could waste Black Elixir."

"Don't worry. Humans are like machines. There are always ways to design them. I'm already excited about what abilities I can give Shirone."

Raesis's face lit up with excitement, then turned stern again.

"Make a deal with the military community. We need the genes of powerful monsters to combine with Shirone's clone. Only the strongest traits will be selected."

"Understood."

After the aide left, Raesis sat on the sofa, crossing her legs and licking her lips.

Experimenting on a Nephilim's body was unprecedented, but the potential rewards were limitless.

"This will be very interesting."

It was the gamble of Raesis's life.

Shirone was promoted to the leader of the Special Forces under the 2nd Headquarters.

His unit, the 3rd Mobile Unit, carried out direct orders from the commander, unlike the 1st and 2nd units. The members were elite warriors with Spirit Forces over 20%.

Plu, now the deputy leader of the 3rd Mobile Unit, glanced at the three members.

Although her status had risen, she couldn't shake the feeling of being watched.

"They're annoying, aren't they?"

"We expected this from the moment you opened the Immortal Function."

Raesis had promised an alliance with the 1st Headquarters if Shirone joined the 2nd Headquarters, but there were still concerns.

It was wise to prepare a foundation to shake the power balance in case of an emergency rather than fully trusting her.

"Still…"

When Plu turned, the three members watching Shirone quickly looked away.

It was clear they had received specific orders from Raesis.

'It'll be hard to shake them off during missions. Maybe at night?'

As Plu pondered, one of the members approached.

A tall man with a long philtrum, his eyes were intimidating despite his lower rank.

"Deputy Leader, we've received our first mission from the higher-ups. At the Plateau of Corruption…"

"Wait. Why are you receiving the mission?"

Plu interrupted.

"We haven't heard anything. The reporting system is a mess. Shirone is the leader."

The man remained expressionless, then reluctantly bowed his head.

"I'm sorry. There was a mistake. I was the leader before, so I received the orders. I'll report to the higher-ups to prevent this from happening again."

Plu couldn't push further since they had to work together for now.

"Alright. Continue. At the Plateau of Corruption?"

The drone translated Plu's words.

It wasn't fast enough for urgent wartime commands, but it worked for regular conversations.

"There's an ancient ruin at the Plateau of Corruption. The rebels call it the 'Tomb of Angels.' It was once frequented by angels but is now abandoned."

Heaven's history was ancient, even mythical to humanity.

But even in Heaven, there was an ancient past, which intrigued Shirone and Plu.

"Hmm, what do we need to do there?"

"The ancient ruins are sealed by angelic magic, so only angels or Nephilim can enter. Currently, Shirone is the only Nephilim among the rebels."

"Ah, I see."

Raesis intended to monitor them while fully utilizing them.

Plu didn't know the relationship between the Nor and magicians, but Raesis's approach was certainly magician-like.

"Alright, let's go."

Exploring ancient ruins in Heaven could provide valuable information for their project, beyond personal interest.

'I have to do this. The seniors are on more dangerous missions.'

As the light spirit Elizer activated, the 3rd Mobile Unit was transported to the Plateau of Corruption.

Armin, Siina, Etella, and Kuan.

The four of them camped in the forest and entered the mainland early in the morning.

The mainland reminded them of a world's fair from their own world.

There was no uniformity, and strange creatures roamed the streets.

The buildings along the road varied widely, from huts and wooden houses to brick buildings and mechanical structures, reflecting different eras.

"Wow, this is amazing. You won't see this anywhere else."

Etella smiled innocently, but there was no response.

With only four members left, the distance between Siina and Armin became more apparent.

Siina was the most troubled.

She had expected Armin to explain why he had followed her to Heaven.

'He left the Ivory Tower?'

If it were that easy, why hadn't he mentioned it before?

Before she could finish her thoughts, Siina couldn't hold back her frustration.

"Are you okay?"

Etella, who had been trying to lighten the mood, fell silent, and Armin finally spoke.

"What do you mean?"

"Keira. What happened?"

"……"

It was something he could never tell Siina.

But even beyond that, staying silent was a matter of respect for Keira.

"You're leaving the Ivory Tower?"

Keira glared at Armin.

She wasn't shocked, as she had expected it. The problem was that Armin was bound by a pact with the Ivory Tower.

The pact, a power of the Regulators, would activate if the agreement was broken, preventing him from ever using magic again.

So Keira brought out the pact.

At the bottom of the document was Armin's signature, etched in his own hand.

He had signed it under duress, with Siina as leverage, hoping never to have to tear it up.

"If you leave, our relationship ends here. If you're okay with that, go."

Armin smiled gently.

He rarely smiled, so Keira knew it was a farewell.

"Thank you for everything."

Armin knew Keira had supported him in many ways, even if she didn't show it.

"Don't try to convince me. Don't act like you're the only one suffering! Or what? Are you going to stop time and run away again?"

Armin shook his head.

"I can't use Stop Magic like that. I'm just a burden now."

"So what? If I tear up the pact, you're nothing. A blind man who can't even go to the bathroom alone!"

"Then that's how it is. I've caused enough trouble, so I'll let you have your way this time."

Armin walked towards the door.

As he grabbed the doorknob, he heard the sound of paper tearing.

Armin turned in surprise.

The pact's conditions were met, but the target wasn't him.

"Keira…"

One of the torn pacts on the floor bore her signature.

Another pact.

Keira had to sign it to monitor Armin for the Ivory Tower, and now it was destroyed.

"Go. It's settled now. If you care so much about her, go!"

"Keira, I…"

"Don't come near me!"

Keira shouted.

She didn't want to be pitied in her final moments.

"Go to Heaven and protect her. But…"

Keira, her face streaked with tears, looked up.

"Come back alive. Protect her, and come back alive, you bastard!"

Armin turned away silently.

Keira was also a renowned magician of the Ivory Tower.

He didn't think her achievements were any less than his.

"I'll repay you."

Armin cast Flicker Magic and disappeared.

Armin's team arrived at the entrance of the military community, known as 'Yamaeng,' on the outskirts of the mainland.

The massive complex lived up to its name, with smoke rising from every chimney beyond the walls.

"No need to ask further."

Armin walked to the gate.

They had brought a drone for emergencies, but Armin had learned Heaven's language, so it wasn't necessary.

"Excuse me. Is this…"

The guards at the gate closed ranks, blocking the path.

They wore tight suits and dark glasses, unlike anything seen in their world.

"Stop. Who are you?"

"We have a request to make here. Can we speak to the person in charge?"

The gate opened, and about ten guards in similar suits blocked Armin's path with expressionless faces.

"Go back. The boss is in a bad mood today."

"That's unfortunate. But we don't have much time either. At least pass on our message."

A burly guard approached, cracking his knuckles.

"Do you even know where you are?"

As the ten guards simultaneously exuded a menacing aura, an unsettling energy enveloped Armin's group.

'This…'

All four of them realized it at the same time.

It was an aura they could never feel in their original world.

"Be careful."

Kuan drew his sword and said, "These beings are not human."
