Chapter 1103: Beginning of Destruction (4)

The Tormia delegation arrived at the royal castle of Jive, across the moat of Delta.

"Lower the drawbridge!"

The sound of gears turning echoed through the sky as the bridge lowered over the moat.

Lupist, entering the castle, remarked,

"Well built."

Unlike naturally occurring castles, this one was constructed according to urban planning.

Shirone commented,

"It took twenty years to transform like this. It's proof of political stability."

The main building of Delta came into view.

It was modern in style, a flat rectangular structure when seen from above.

Kashan and Corona had arrived first but didn't seem to pay much attention to Tormia.

'That's reality.'

Pony reviewed her thoughts.

'To become a leading nation, we need a convincing card to persuade the world. The technology of elemental bombs is excellent, but other countries have similar cards.'

It was a simple issue.

'Kashan can control demons, Corona has the intelligence of the Ivory Tower, and then there's Tormia.'

Which country would the world want as the leading nation?

'Probably not Tormia.'

Yet, the reason they could compete with other countries was because of Yahweh.

'Without Shirone, Tormia would just be a mediator with one vote. I also have to consider which country to sell this vote to and for how much.'

Even in encounters with superpowers, her heart raced.

'With Shirone's help, it's not impossible. That's why Lupist is putting everything on the line…'

But Yahweh's stance would be different.

'Shirone doesn't need Tormia to become the leading nation. He's here for the unification of humanity.'

Of course, as long as Tormia supported Shirone's beliefs, their alliance wouldn't break.

But would that be enough?

'Politics involves getting your hands dirty. And Lupist won't hesitate to use any means.'

No, she couldn't just pass the responsibility.

'I'm the same.'

For Pony, who had been pushed out of the line of succession early, power was something to overcome.

'There was a time I just wanted to escape.'

It didn't matter if there was no destination.

'I entered the magic school, faced scorn and contempt, even participated in beauty contests…'

Her disillusionment with power allowed her to become a puppet queen.

'In truth…'

Hadn't she desired it more than anyone?

'No.'

Pony shook her head.

'It's just a momentary desire. Focus on helping Shirone. As a mage, for humanity…'

When she first became queen,

The first thing that came to mind was the expressions of the royals who had ignored her.

'Now aiming to be the world's queen.'

The thrill of her self-esteem, which she couldn't show anywhere, exploding brilliantly.

'I don't want it to end here.'

Something inside her began to whisper.

'Bind Shirone to Tormia. If Shirone moves only for me…'

You can get what you want.

Pony looked at Shirone's profile, deep in thought.

'Amy.'

She didn't think she was lacking compared to her.

'If the person next to Shirone was me instead of Amy, would he help? Would he prioritize me over humanity…'

Would he make me the queen of the world?

'Haha.'

Power was terrifying.

'Yes, that's why I can't do it.'

Amy wouldn't have such repulsive thoughts.

At that moment, a sound came from beyond Delta's walls.

"Kingdom of Kessia! Enter!"

The mere act of Kashan and Corona's officials turning their bodies showed the nation's prestige.

"Hmm, Kessia. A formidable opponent."

The southern continent's powerhouse was still Iron, but Kessia had also swallowed seven nations.

'And at the center is…'

Ardino Fermi.

As the procession led by a golden carriage entered, Lampa of Corona instructed Chongyi.

"Don't lose sight of the Kingdom of Kessia. They're the most unpredictable variable in this Holy War."

"Because their think tank is Golden Wheel. They're the organization that took over seven southern nations with Angel. Their leader is the drug king Fermi."

"Ardino Fermi."

The definitions differed between the young and the old.

"Master, I know you're old, but isn't this too old-fashioned? You said updating information is crucial. Since Yolga's death, the Ardino family has been completely overshadowed by Adria. Fermi is outstanding, but compared to Miro, the epitome of goodness…"

"The depth of Adria."

Lampa said.

"There was a saying, 'The breadth of Ardino.' When the two heroines coexisted. Modern mages see Ardino as a pushover, but have you seen Yolga? Don't update unless you're sure."

"But…"

"Yolga's vision, reading the flow of the times, far surpassed human limits. Though her records were erased, it's believed Miro's philosophy was influenced by her. Underestimating Fermi could endanger Corona."

Chongyi smacked his lips.

"Who's underestimating? I'm serious this time."

"Why? Is there someone you fancy?"

"No, well…"

Information mages were central to all wars but rarely in the spotlight.

'But the Holy War is different. Here, I can stand out. In other words…'

He could appeal to women.

'The world might end, but I should experience love before I die, right?'

Lampa knew his student's thoughts as clearly as his palm but didn't scold him.

'Maybe that's all there is.'

Do humans need grand reasons to live?

Meanwhile, Uorin felt bitter.

'They finally came.'

The Apocalypse faction had likely set its sights on keeping Shirone in check.

“This won’t be easy, will it?”

Between Keshia's wealth, Angel, the drug capable of treating the Emotion Sickness, and the buried information about the Apocalypse, Keshia held cards no one else could play.

“He knows how to exploit every filthy angle.”

Though Keshia's tactics were shrouded in shadows, this made them even more dangerous in the bid to become a leading nation.

“It should work.”

Shirone shared this sentiment.

“Fermi prioritizes extreme efficiency. In other words, he’s inclined to resolve problems in the simplest way possible.”

People naturally gravitate toward simplicity.

“At the very least, they have the power to overturn the game once. That’s Keshia’s greatest weapon.”

As Fermi approached Delta’s headquarters, all eyes were on him, and he smiled.

“Am I really that popular?”

Behind his composed demeanor, he was quickly analyzing the key figures.

“Corona seems formidable, with the Stars in their ranks. Kashan is rallying around the Wind Guards… wait.”

He noticed a man leaning against Uorin’s back.

“Extreme… Havitz.”

Their gazes met momentarily before Havitz yawned and looked away.

“Haaaah.”

The divine frequency whispered something indecipherable.

“...Interesting fellow.”

While Satan’s observation was brief, the others were fixated on Fermi’s every move.

“We need to use him.”

Those who understood the essence of politics knew they could manipulate Fermi’s cards to their advantage.

“Tormia, Corona, and Kashan are lined up in order. He’ll probably align with a faction he considers friendly.”

Fermi whispered something to the king, setting Keshia’s unexpected course.

“What’s this?”

Instead of taking an available position among the main groups, Keshia positioned itself on the outskirts next to Tormia, a smaller contingent.

As Keshia’s delegation settled, Fermi’s eyes gleamed with cunning.

“This is the weak spot, you fools.”

By bypassing Kashan and Corona, Fermi threw the leadership into confusion.

Rampa stroked his beard thoughtfully.

“Hmm. Not positioning themselves in the middle is a clear show of favor toward Yahweh. It means they prioritize practicality over pride. It makes sense—they’re willing to take losses to build trust when necessary.”

But if it was more than a calculated decision...

“If there’s something only Ardino can see, then Kashan and Corona may have made a critical mistake.”

Fermi smirked and greeted Shirone.

“Hey, Shirone! We keep meeting like this. We might end up becoming best friends.”

Shirone responded curtly. “This is a formal setting. Watch your words.”

“Haha! What’s the harm? I’m from Tormia, too. It’s been a while, Pony. How have you been? Let’s keep it casual since we’re on the same side.”

Pony hesitated before replying. “Oh, uh… yeah. It’s been a while.”

Even knowing Fermi’s motives were calculated, Pony couldn’t ignore the olive branch Keshia was extending.

Shirone narrowed his eyes. “Clever bastard.”

For now, having Fermi focus on Tormia wasn’t a bad thing.

Pony turned to greet Keshia’s king, but both she and Shirone were stunned into silence.

The King of Keshia, Manolka, looked like a mummy, his emaciated body covered in wrinkles, mouth agape like a corpse.

“He looks... dead.”

Shirone glared at Fermi, his eyes blazing with anger. “Fermi, you bastard…”

Fermi feigned innocence, realizing the misunderstanding. “Oh, right.”

He shook the king’s shoulder. “Your Majesty, wake up. Stop dozing off.”

The king stirred and blinked groggily. “Huh? Are we there already? Well, well. Greetings, young ones. I’m Manolka, King of Keshia.”

Shirone asked cautiously. “Are you all right? You don’t look well.”

Manolka chuckled. “People say that a lot. It’s just that I’m naturally skinny. Haha!”

Fermi shrugged. “Why blame me for everything? This is just how he is. Seventy-two years old and still in his prime!”

‘He looks at least 172.’

The same thought crossed everyone’s mind.

The Grand Hall

The main doors of Delta’s headquarters opened to the sound of music.

“Welcome, leaders of the world!”

A well-built man with a clean-shaven face and a cleft chin entered without an escort.

“Gith.”

The King of Jive stood before them.

“Although the full ceremony will take place tomorrow, I felt it was inappropriate to keep you waiting. So, I’ve prepared a special prelude.”

“A prelude?”

As confusion spread, a towering woman emerged from the hall.

“...What?”

It was Satiel, Archangel of Disintegration.

“Allow me to introduce Jive’s new strength that will guide our future—the Heavenly Army.”

The rooftop of the headquarters sparkled, and hundreds of radiant beams ascended into the sky. Each beam was an angel, their divine halos radiating unique, awe-inspiring power.

“Damn.”

The overwhelming force shook the ground, leaving the officials pale with fear.

Gith spread his arms wide. “Welcome to Jive.”
