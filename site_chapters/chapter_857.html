Chapter 857: Ugly Woman (4)

The Valkyrie forces stationed at the defensive walls along the borders of Jive, Molten, and Bornai had retreated entirely.

Amid the southern advance of Hell's army, Harvitz strolled through the ruins of the Jive Kingdom.

Natasha, who accompanied him as his guard, asked, "There’s nothing here but corpses. Why are we even here? Wouldn’t it be more fun to head to the battlefield instead?" By now, Balkan had already marched south with the army of Hell.

"Not everyone will be dead," Harvitz replied, adding, "We need to kill them."

Natasha tilted her head with her doll-like, charming face. "Harvitz, you’re so cruel."

Though the army of Hell had left this place, survivors might now be clinging to a fragile hope for life.

"Why not just leave them be? Isn’t it pitiful?" Her tone carried no particular emotion.

"Natasha, you’re really strange." As Harvitz continued walking, Natasha followed closely, matching his exact pace.

"I’m strange?"

"Balkan, Smode, Zetaro—they’re all entertaining in their own ways. But you, you’re different. How should I put it..." Harvitz turned to look at her.

"You seem like a good person."

Perhaps a benevolent soul.

"You’re kind-hearted, too. Like when you didn’t seek revenge against the thugs who broke your back."

Natasha propped her head up with both hands.

"What’s the point of revenge? It’s not like it’ll let me dance again," she said, holding up her index finger to emphasize her point. "And for the record, I’m definitely not a good person! Do you know how many humans I’ve killed?"

"That’s true."

Harvitz nudged a corpse with his foot, checking if any survivors remained.

"That’s why I find you so peculiar. You’ve killed countless humans, yet…"

A man was lying under a wooden plank.

"If you were on humanity’s side, everyone would love you. You’d probably save many lives and fight against me."

That’s the impression she gave.

"Hmm." Natasha frowned, lost in thought.

"What does that matter? Anyway, I’m here with you. I have no intention of switching sides."

Harvitz lifted the plank and said, "I enjoy hanging out with you, too. All I’m saying is, you should do whatever you want."

"I already am."

Ending the conversation, Harvitz looked down at the man lying on the cold ground.

"Get up."

As if by magic, the man’s body stirred.

"Please, spare me," he begged.

Harvitz unsheathed his longsword and plunged it into the man’s heart without hesitation.

"Argh!"

He then peered into the dying man’s eyes.

"Resignation. Regret. Resentment."

The gaze seemed to consolidate the entirety of one man’s life.

"It’s coming."

In the fleeting moment before death, a sensation he couldn’t quite analyze flared up like a flame.

"What could it be?"

It was something never found in the eyes of a corpse—a fleeting glimpse of life itself evaporating.

Natasha asked, "Why are you so obsessed with that?"

"It’s just fascinating. No matter how many times I see it, I never get tired of it. It’s like an addiction. That’s why I keep killing."

Natasha showed little interest.

"If you want to kill more, let’s move somewhere else. Doesn’t seem like there are many survivors here."

"Then that village over there..."

Harvitz pointed toward what should have been a village, but instead, there stood silhouettes of people.

When Natasha turned her head, over a hundred individuals had encircled them.

Some bore strange mechanical devices, others wore robes like mages, and still others, bare-chested, had sun-darkened skin.

It was the Immortal Community.

Though the sun had set, making the surroundings dim, the fervent prayers of each individual burned as brightly as the sun.

Three figures emerged from the crowd of immortals.

"What’s this? What’s going on?"

They were clearly different in demeanor from the rest of the hundred.

"A unique aura," Natasha noted.

Her instincts, honed over 10,000 years, weren’t wrong—these were executives of the Ten Lords.

Nohre’s nihilist and fifth in the Ten Lords hierarchy, Vanishing, stepped forward, holding a massive brush on his back.

"Who are you? Jive has already fallen. What are you doing here?"

"No need to explain. I’ll check myself."

Osilante, the eighth-ranked Mecha engineer of the Ten Lords, lifted a drone the size of an egg to her eye.

Through augmented reality, data on Harvitz appeared.

"Oh? This guy’s the Emperor of Gustav."

"Ho?"

Itoka, the sixth-ranked warrior of the Ten Lords, raised a thick blade.

"The Emperor?"

The porcupine-like aura emanating from his body made Natasha’s eyes gleam.

"Strong."

Itoka was a master of physical combat, known as the strongest warrior of the Kergo tribe and superior in swordsmanship even to the ninth-ranked warrior of the Ten Lords, Parknyeo.

"What a magnificent trophy. I came looking for Buddha, but who would’ve thought I’d run into someone this significant?"

Since Anker La’s disappearance, the Ten Lords executives had scattered across the globe.

Eventually, they united to revive the world by finding Nane—a shared purpose for the current leadership.

Vanishing, the team leader, gazed beyond the horizon.

"Being with Shura makes finding him difficult. For someone so weak, he sure knows how to hide."

Harvitz twirled his mustache and asked, "Are you enemies of ours?"

Osilante answered, "If you’re the Emperor of Gustav, you’re one of the worst evils of this era. Not exactly an enemy, but definitely not an ally."

"Then can’t you just let us go?"

The immortals burst into laughter.

"Haha! What a joke! I expected more from the leader of Hell, but without his demons, he’s nothing!"

Harvitz remained calm.

"I don’t want to fight for now. I prefer killing those who have already lost the will to fight."

He wanted to see the final sparks of life burn even brighter.

"Is that so, you bastard?"

Though not pacifists, even the immortals found Harvitz’s atrocities detestable.

"Had fun killing all this time? Let’s see if you enjoy being on the receiving end."

Harvitz turned away.

"Natasha."

"Hm?"

"I’m leaving. This is a hassle."

Natasha nodded.

"Okay. Go ahead."

The immortals drew their weapons.

"Running away and leaving your companion behind? So the leader of Hell is nothing but trash."

Despite the jeers, Harvitz didn’t respond, and their eyes shifted to Natasha.

"The Dance of Death."

Natasha moved into position, raising her arms and twisting her knees inward.

Just before her knees touched the ground, a skeletal avatar appeared 10 meters high, connecting its limbs to hers with glowing threads.

"Avatar magic?"

It was a high-level skill, but for immortals who had lived over a millennium, it was standard fare.

"No need to drag this out. Kill her."

As the Kergo warriors lunged, a chilling light flickered in Natasha’s eyes.

Dance of Death—Killing Machine.

A whirlwind of movement followed—a madly dancing puppet.

"Aaah!"

While the world seemed on the brink of collapse from the cacophony, Harvitz merely walked toward the village, ignoring it all.

Finally, the noise stopped.

"Ugh!"

As Natasha’s fist pierced Osilante’s solar plexus, blood gushed from her mouth.

With that, the battle was over.

From start to finish, it had taken less than four minutes.

"What on earth…?"

On Osilante’s retinas, the augmented reality data her drone had sent earlier flickered faintly.

The fact that she had no time to analyze Natasha’s physical abilities was more regretful than death itself.

"Is God insane?"

How could such a being exist?

It was either a creature created by a mad god or something not made by any god at all.

"In my opinion…"

Unable to reach a conclusion, Osilante collapsed onto the cold ground like the others.

"Phew."

Natasha wiped the sweat from her forehead with the back of her hand.

Though the fight had been brief, the area within a few hundred meters looked as if it had been bombarded.

"They were impressive."

Even with her vast experience, Natasha considered them among the strongest in the current world.

"I should’ve left when Harvitz told me to."

That was the extent of her sentiment.

"Harvitz."

She hopped as if on springs, quickly catching up to him.

"Is it over?"

"Yes. I killed them all."

To Harvitz, the only being defined by "strength" was Natasha herself.

"You don’t seem to have enjoyed it."

"What’s fun about fighting? It’s tiring and annoying. At least I can dance during it."

Even killing three of the world’s strongest had been nothing more than moonlit choreography to Natasha.

"So when do you have fun?"

Natasha, gazing at the full moon, turned to Harvitz after a moment’s thought.

"What about you, Harvitz? Did you enjoy it when I killed them?"

"Yes. Winning is fun."

Natasha smiled contentedly.

"Then I’m happy too."

Harvitz blinked.

"You’re truly a strange child."

The Gustav 4 generals were all strange, but Natasha’s nature was uniquely different.

Unlike the others, she had no particular reason why it had to be Harvitz.

"This place has burned down too."

Entering the village, the two surveyed the already ruined scenery.

Harvitz, eager to kill any survivors, showed a hint of disappointment.

"This is too much. Even though they’re my subordinates, killing everyone and leaving no one behind?"

Shouldn’t they have left something to enjoy?

Just then, the cry of an infant carried through the night air.

"A survivor!"

The two locked eyes and ran to a dilapidated shack on the verge of collapse.

"Only the baby survived?"

As Harvitz spoke, the infant’s cries abruptly stopped from within the shack.

"Hmm."

Harvitz kicked open the door, and a rancid smell hit his nose.

Under the moonlight streaming through the broken roof, a plump woman was nursing a baby.

She had a snub nose, irregular teeth jutting out of her lips, and eyes narrowed to slits.

"Are you here to kill me?"

Harvitz stepped inside.

"Yes."

"Wait. Let me finish nursing the baby."

Harvitz waited.

After soothing and feeding the baby, the woman raised her head.

"If you kill me, you’ll have to take care of this baby. Otherwise, it’ll starve to death."

Harvitz looked at the infant’s face.

"Is it your child?"

"Yes."

"And the father?"

"I don’t know. This is a desperately poor village. I sold myself to mercenaries for a piece of bread. One of them is probably the father—dead or alive, who knows..."

"Seems to be eating well."

Harvitz put down his sword and crawled toward the baby in her arms.

The infant’s frantic sucking sounded like it was gasping for breath.

"Is it that good?"

As Harvitz stared intently at the nursing baby, the woman asked.

"Do you want to try?"

Harvitz’s gaze shifted upward.

"Me?"

"I have two breasts. If you want to, go ahead. But you’ll need to give me bread. Do you have any?"

Harvitz nodded.

When he crouched like a beast and waited, the woman turned her body and offered him her other breast.

"Here, this side."

Staring at the foul-smelling breast, Harvitz pursed his lips and closed his eyes.

Slurp. Slurp.

As the sucking noises grew intense, Harvitz’s eyelids quivered.

"Life."

It was connected.

Slurp. Slurp.

Natasha silently watched as two children buried their faces in the woman’s chest.
