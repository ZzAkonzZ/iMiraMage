Chapter 846: At the Edge (1)

The laser passing through Ataraxia pierced through the stratosphere and relayed information at an altitude of 6,000 kilometers.

The force generated when a mass of over 10 tons crashes to the ground could devastate a city.

It was such a dangerous spell that even Shirone, the developer of the magic, had cast it only once, and that from heaven.

'What exactly is he planning?'

Minerva tilted her head as she watched Shirone launching the laser.

The impact of divine punishment falling would be beyond human endurance.

'So it's all the same in the end?'

Shirone concentrated all his nerves on the heavens.

'Magic that can't be controlled is no magic at all.'

Just another disaster.

Despite its unprecedented destructive power, this was why Shirone had never used it until now.

'But now it's different.'

Information accumulated in space tilted a spear toward the earth. The difference now was that a triangle-shaped seer had been attached to the middle of the spear.

Three lines of lasers were fired from the seer.

"That's it!"

Ascending above the clouds on a jet, Minerva confirmed the lasers piercing through the atmosphere.

'Laser guidance system.'

Looking down, a red dot marked the top of Argantis, who couldn't move a muscle under Sing's law.

'The mental exhaustion must be tremendous.'

Launching it is just the trigger, which is not a problem.

However, attaching the seer to divine punishment requires the spirit zone to be active at an altitude of 6,000 kilometers.

Truly a last-ditch effort with everything at stake.

'But this allows for precise strikes. If it falls correctly, the damage can be minimized.'

Now the remaining issue was time.

According to her calculations, it would take at least 10 minutes for divine punishment to reach the ground.

If Argantis breaks free in that time, the precise strike would be pointless, and the capital would be destroyed.

'Can Xing handle it?' Xing said about five minutes.

But she, too, one of the Five Great Stars, could probably buy a few more minutes if she threw all caution to the wind.

'It’s still a close call.'

Minerva turned the nose of the jet and dived vertically.

'Shirone is defenseless.'

When she reached the ground, Shirone was looking up at the sky, his eyes half-visible, shaking from side to side each time he precisely manipulated the seer's lasers.

The smallest strategic weapon in the world.

A bittersweet smile appeared on Minerva's lips.

'... Good thing he’s a kind person.' At heart, he's still human.

Nothing should disturb him until the magic is successfully cast.

"Capture Yahweh!"

Though Argantis had become a disaster, the commanders, led by a brigade commander, were still intact.

It was only natural for them to attack Yahweh, their most hated enemy, whether they knew of divine punishment's existence or not.

"Come."

Creatures with an average height of over 4 meters blocked the view like a wall.

"Sons of bitches."

Minerva's eyes widened, and her body transformed into that of a naked young girl.

Her white skin was marked with various bruises, blood spots, and vivid rope burns as if the flesh had been rubbed raw.

"Kraaaaa!"

As the demons raised their claws, the illusion of the girl disappeared, and she emitted the pheromones of a witch.

"Krrrrr..."

The demons, reminded of the fear before they became demonic, stiffened as flames spewed from the jet.

Propelled by the thrust, the jet swung full circle, and the demons' heads snapped in the same direction.

"You liked playing with me, didn't you?" Minerva deeply inhaled a cigar and exhaled smoke towards the front.

Catastrophic magic, Walking Dead.

Demons, having lost their reason, began attacking each other, and the flow of the assault reversed outward.

"Kyahaha! Die! Die!"

As Minerva slammed the jet down, smashing the demons' skulls, it was a true display of her essence.

Running atop a roof, Freed clicked his tongue.

"He's always a fighter," knowing Shirone's methods.

"I feel bad about this."

Lifting his head, Freed looked up at Argantis, grotesquely twisting under the law.

"A tree, of all things..."

As he formed blue lightning in his right hand and was about to leap from the roof.

"Please save us!"

A scream came from below the building.

A family surrounded by demons was hugging each other, tears streaming down their faces.

'Well, if humans die, they die.'

'It's tough for everyone to live. Do you know how much blood I've shed to get here? You don’t? Then just die.'

A demon's voice rang out.

"Kahaha! The woman is mine!"

"Mom! Mom!"

The daughter's voice also rang out.

"Honey! Ugh, you bastards!"

Listening to the gradually fading cries, Freed soared into the sky.

A lot was on his mind.

"Damn it."

Freed's face turned gloomy.

"I'm really going crazy!"

Just before a spear pierced the chest of a husband standing in front of his wife, a speed-of-sound sword strike flurry ensued.

"Huh, what?"

Demons were decapitated in an instant, and the family looked up in shock.

Freed stood there with an annoyed expression.

"... This is why I hate coming down here." Because he was no longer just a human.

"Kahaha! Is there still a champion of justice? But you will regret this!" As the brigade commander gave orders, countless demons aimed their spears at Freed.

"Please save us! Please save our family..."

Freed looked bitterly at their daughter.

"Champion of justice my ass. Living and dying is everyone's own responsibility. Do you think I'm doing this to save anyone?"

He coldly dismissed the man and stepped towards the demons.

"But don't touch the family. Would you feel good if I grabbed your daughter and kicked her?"

"Huh? Daughter?"

As the brigade commander looked around, his subordinates shrugged their shoulders.

"We don’t have that," but Freed did.

A man always says a sword is best, arguing daily with his magical father and son.

"That's why you don't know, you fools." Freed gripped the sword handle tightly.

"What a human can do."

"Kill them."

As the demons lunged, Freed swung his sword, creating a gust.

As if throwing something that was originally separate into the air, each flash split the demons.

"Such trivial things."

Having quickly resolved the situation, Freed flicked the blood off his sword as the family bowed.

"Thank you! Thank you so much!" He couldn't leave them behind.

Then what about the lives of other families dying at this very moment?

'It's really difficult. That's why I hate you, Shirone.'

Why do you live so complicatedly?

'But...'

When Shirone looked him straight in the eye and revealed his plan, the emotion that flickered through Freed's mind was,

'I wish my son would grow up like you.'

He would have been proud.

"Ah, damn."

Wiping his eyes, he looked back at Argantis.

Still caught by the law, but now with increased activity compared to before.

"... Still shitting himself."

Taking a deep breath, Xing clenched her teeth and glared at Argantis.

'It's an illusion!'

Not real.

'Only I am real. The thinking me. The existing me. All laws but mine...

Her bracelet, the legal artifact 'Genuine', vibrated, suppressing the demonic will.

"Kneel before me!" Kraooo!

As Argantis screamed in frustration, wrestling with her constrained body, Xing's face flushed with veins.

'I can't let go yet!'

The five minutes she had told Shirone had already passed.

"Xing."

Amanta's voice was heard.

As the World Wheel, which had grown to a diameter of 20 meters, spun rapidly, the surrounding humans began to disappear one by one.

Without turning her head, Xing asked,

“How many did you save?”

“We should be able to save as many as the number of the dead. I don't know how large the shelter in Terafos is.”

It should be enough.

“What about Shirone? If this continues, even my law will be twisted.”

“He's casting the spell that destroyed Aravot. It takes quite a bit of time. Because of the laser guidance, he's also in a groggy state.”

“Is that so?”

Xing's face turned serious.

“I'll release the law in 30 seconds. The shockwave will be tremendous. Evacuate the people near the Makai Tree.”

“What are you planning?”

“We can't match the timing as it is. The point is to directly hit the Makai Tree, right?”

Amanta immediately nodded.

“Exactly.”

From the moment the World Wheel moved away, Xing began counting.

When the count reached 30, she released the law that was suppressing Argantis.

Hohohohoho!

As if venting all its pent-up frustration, the black branches waved like seaweed.

The air was torn apart like a whip, and all the buildings in the area were swept away.

‘No need to block everything!’

Xing quickly formed hand seals.

As her hands moved, thousands of rings formed from the roots of Argantis.

‘Just hold the main body!’

The ultimate demon-binding spell, Exorcism of Three Thousand Worlds. The rings coiled endlessly around the trunk, finally spreading to the thick branches.

It was as if they were holding a human's arms from both sides.

‘Now I can hold on!’

However, the smaller branches that the Exorcism of Three Thousand Worlds couldn't bind were attacking the ground in fury.

“Damn it!”

Freed blocked the branches in front of the family.

“Take it easy!”

Though much smaller than the main body, each branch was enough to pierce through a house.

“Ahhh! Mister! Mister!”

Just as Freed cut down dozens of branches, the ground began to shake.

“What now?”

Woohohohohoho!

Argantis, unable to absorb nutrients for a long time, could no longer support its massive body.

“I want to eat!”

The sound leaked from tens of thousands of mouths.

“I want to eat! Give me food… Grrraaah!”

From the mouth at the bottom of the Makai Tree, strong acidic vomit gushed out.

As soon as it touched the ground, toxic gas rose.

“Damn it…!”

It was regurgitating.

Like a bottle with a hole, the green liquid continued to spew along the trunk.

‘If it reaches the top…’

When the regurgitating liquid reached the top, it would rain down with the force of a volcanic eruption.

“Ahhh!”

The family covered their faces in agony as the toxic gas carried by the wind hit them.

“Get inside the house!”

As Freed blocked the wind and stood in front of the family, the roots climbing the building twisted like pretzels and charged.

“Mister!”

When he turned around, the roots, now sharp as awls, were already in front of him.

Boom!

The family, who had closed their eyes tightly at the loud noise, slowly opened their eyelids as they realized the silence.

“Ha, these things…”

Freed, who had blocked the attack with his sword, grimaced and cut down the roots.

Amanta's voice was heard from the air.

“The situation has worsened.”

Glancing up, Freed began to dismantle his armor piece by piece.

“I'll handle it. Take the family with you.”

“What are you going to do?”

“If it's spewing, I have to plug the hole. I'll climb up while burning it with electricity.”

“Can you do it? It's quite high.”

Freed looked back as if to say, "What are you talking about?"

“Can't you see I've taken off my armor?”

With his body trained to the extreme, Amanta spun the World Wheel and said,

“Hurry. Something huge is falling.”

“Who cares?”

Gripping his sword, Freed charged at Argantis with a speed comparable to flying.

“I've already switched modes.”

It was the mode activated when someone he really wanted to beat up appeared.
