Chapter 319: One-Third (5)

"If he helped someone for compensation, it's illegal. But what if Fermi just helped someone on a whim? Can we call that unfair?"

Olivia couldn't answer.

"The graduation exam candidates range from first to thirtieth place. But the gap between students isn't consistent every year. Some years, students of similar levels compete, while other years, a standout talent sweeps the competition. Fermi's case isn't different."

"Even so, deliberately failing is a problem. It goes against the purpose of the graduation exam."

Alpheas shook his head.

"Think about Shirone from Class Four. He'll probably join the graduation class next year. If Shirone went pro right now, what level do you think he'd be?"

Olivia put a finger to her lips, pondering.

"Hmm, if we're only talking about combat ability, he could probably compete at level eight. Honestly, below certified Class 6, it's all pretty similar. They just keep overtaking each other."

"We can say that from above, but for students, even certified Class 10 mages are a huge barrier. And that's normal. But there are always students who surpass the typical level. You can't argue fairness just because a few exceptional students are included."

"That's true, but..."

"In terms of competition difficulty, next year's graduation class will be much tougher. The squad moving up to the graduation class next semester is arguably the strongest ever. But will they complain just because the competition is tougher than other years?"

"But those kids won't deliberately fail."

"You can't be sure. I'm not saying they'll deliberately fail, but it's hard to set clear standards for these things. Whether there's bribery or not, the free competition system doesn't change."

Olivia groaned, resting her chin on her hand.

There was no way to overthrow Fermi's regime. What was a mage with professional-level skills, suspected of unregistered practices, thinking by doing this?

"So there's no way to help Amy. It's unfortunate."

"Are you sure Amy would have passed 100% without the collusion?"

Olivia's eyes narrowed at Alpheas's leading question.

"Based on talent alone, yes."

"Karmis's daughter is renowned across the continent. She absorbs everything like a sponge. A jack-of-all-trades, you could say. And she's actually beautiful too."

Olivia glared at Alpheas with cold eyes. But he wasn't wrong, so she sighed.

"Being too pretty is a curse."

"I've watched her since she was twelve. She'll get through this."

They both looked out the window. The school, which had seen countless events over the year, was now as quiet as a stage after a performance.

Thus, the year at the magic school came to an end.

Heaven, Sixth Heaven, Zebul.

The city of angels, Zebul, has two main systems.

One is Inglis, where the Akashic Records are kept, and the other is the Great World War, Archangel Cariel's laboratory.

The Great World War, which had been devastated by Shirone's Photon Cannon, was now fully restored.

The central control system, a massive pillar, was the tallest structure in the Great World War. Next to the pillar emitting multicolored light, a galactic telescope smoothly observed the movements of the stars.

The Great World War had regained its former appearance. But Heaven had not.

Since Shirone's departure, a wind of change had blown through First Heaven, Shamain. The citizens no longer believed in the absolute correctness of the Doctrine of Unity, and some radicals had emerged.

In response, the angels mobilized all their resources to break through Miro’s Time and Space.

Leading the charge was Archangel Cariel.

The Archangel of Creation, who had tried to cast the Elixir of Life on Amy, Tess, and Arin, who had been captured by the fallen angel Ikasa.

The most intelligent of the angels, he analyzed the Meta Gate Shirone had left behind when he returned to his original world.

Finally, he found the coordinates to the Land of Earth. But to get there, they had to break through Miro’s Time and Space.

"Even with the coordinates, we have to break through. But it's tricky."

"Then what's the point of recovering the coordinates?"

Cariel turned at the majestic voice.

Recognizing the voice, he smiled. Another archangel, Uriel, clad in pure white heavy armor, floated in the air.

Angels are taller than humans, but Uriel was the tallest among them, standing at three meters. His chest armor was so thick it reached his chin.

Cariel flapped his wings of light once, rising to the top of the central control system. A large screen was installed 40 meters high, displaying numerous numbers.

"Not necessarily. There are quite a few Nephilim in the Land of Earth now. Even if we break Miro’s Time and Space, we can set up a firewall. But with the coordinates, we can bypass it."

"Break Miro’s Time and Space? Wasn't that strategy dismissed long ago?"

Only Anke Ra could dismiss an archangel's proposal. Indeed, Anke Ra had forbidden investigating the Land of Earth after the Final War was blocked by the Maze.

The archangels didn't know why, but they couldn't defy Anke Ra, who governed the Akashic Records.

But Cariel didn't give up.

The decisive factor was Shirone's appearance. His presence changed the citizens. And that change was rapidly amplifying over time.

"I don't intend to defy Ra's will. It's a precaution. The Land of Earth will be conquered someday. It might be too late to prepare then."

"So you're not certain about this experiment either."

Cariel smirked.

He had never been uncertain before. But every attempt had been thwarted by Miro’s quick response. It felt like he was playing a game with her from an unseen place.

But today, she would have to give up.

Miro’s Time and Space would be breached. Anke Ra wouldn't want it, but... what could he do? It was fate.

Cariel flew past the galactic telescope to the western wall. Uriel watched for a moment before creating a terrifying gust of wind and catching up in an instant.

"Here it is, my masterpiece for today."

"..."

Uriel couldn't find words to describe it.

Cariel's masterpiece was so massive it occupied a quarter of the Great World War. A rectangular wall, 40 meters wide and 70 meters high.

Uriel, who had seemed like a giant earlier, now felt small in front of the wall.

"It's quite large."

Uriel's acknowledgment made Cariel even more proud.

"I wondered why we couldn't break through Miro’s Time and Space. It's because the dimensional wall is inherently unbreakable. But with this Celestial Wall, it's possible. Even unbreakable liquid shatters when frozen. We'll strike Miro’s Time and Space directly."

"A direct hit. So this Celestial Wall represents Miro’s Time and Space?"

It was a simplistic but accurate interpretation.

Well, it didn't matter. He had called Uriel for his strength, not his intellect.

"If the Celestial Wall breaks, so will Miro’s Time and Space. I wanted to add an amplification circuit, but it's impossible without Ikael's help."

Uriel avoided responding.

Ikael wouldn't help. She was imprisoned for an unforgivable crime, and Shirone's actions had solidified suspicions that she sided with humans.

Cariel tapped the Celestial Wall.

"So, want to give it a try?"

Uriel pondered whether he should break it.

If the Celestial Wall broke, so would Miro’s Time and Space. And that went against Anke Ra's will.

"No one can defy Ra's will. Not because he's strong, but because he's the whole."

Whatever the future held, Ra would remain Ra. Cariel knew this, which was why he was taking such a reckless action.

Uriel decided to be honest. From the beginning, it wasn't Ra that bothered him, but Ikael.

"Why... did Ikael abandon us?"

Angels had long conveyed and executed God's will in the Land of Earth. Uriel, in particular, had excelled in destruction. His power overturned the earth and split the sea.

But even Uriel, who had blindly slaughtered humans, questioned his righteousness when he heard rumors that Ikael might side with humans.

Ikael was always right.

No, it wasn't about right or wrong. Even if she wasn't right, he wanted to follow her. If it was his nature to do so, he would dedicate himself to it, even if it was wrong.

Cariel probably felt the same way throughout his existence.

Angels had no concept of a mother. But if they had to choose one, it would be Ikael. The archangel who had cared for them since their birth.

Uriel glanced at Cariel. Seeing his innocent smile reminded him of long ago.

Cariel hadn't always been like this. He was still sly and perverted, but he hadn't been obsessed with creating things. He used to make fun and ingenious items to give to Ikael, who would laugh and pat him.

"Cariel, I can destroy it. You know that. Do you really want this?"

"Haha! This time won't be easy. No, honestly, it has to be destroyed. I want to annihilate the Land of Earth as soon as possible. If we break Miro’s Time and Space, today will be that day."

"Why are you in such a hurry? Ra hasn't given any orders. Ymir is still asleep. Is this really necessary?"

Cariel looked out of Zebul. The rebellion that started in First Heaven, Shamain, had spread to Second Heaven, Rakia, where citizens and fallen angels were fighting.

"Shirone has changed Heaven. And this is just the beginning. We must break through the Miro’s Time and Space and eliminate the humans on Earth as soon as possible."

Cariel's concern was understandable.

But it didn't make sense for an archangel who had destroyed countless earthly nations to be so anxious about a mere rebellion.

"Do you think destroying humans will bring Ikael back?"

Cariel's face suddenly hardened.

"...What do you mean?"

"Ikael's crime has already been erased. The incident itself no longer exists. Goffin disappeared as a result. Shouldn't we at least forgive her?"

Cariel's halo expanded at a terrifying speed.

As the 40-meter-wide halo spread, the Great World War's mecha system sounded an emergency alarm, reflecting Cariel's current state of mind.

"Erased? Then what are these filthy memories left with us? I don't care about Goffin! It's Ikael who disgusts me! Don't ever mention her again!"

"Are you really going to fight her? Cariel, I'm asking again. Are you really going to fight her?"

Cariel's expression was a mix of sorrow and anger.

"She gave birth to a human child!"

The halo sent out wave-like ripples. As the mecha system flickered on and off, the Great World War blinked.
