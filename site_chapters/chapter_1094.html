Chapter 1094: What is God? (4)

Operator's Meeting Room

“It’s over.”

The "Dr. Martin’s Counterattack" episode had concluded with an unprecedented victory for the users.

The Black Circle Mark remarked, “It was a decent episode, considering your incompetence.”

The screen for Number Seven, which had been disconnected until now, suddenly lit up. It was on Wuorin's directive.

“Tch! So, what now? Do whatever you want. Revoke my operator rights or something.”

The Smile Mark responded, “Is it so easy for you to say that? Because of you, High Gear almost got shut down.”

“I know! That’s why I’m asking—what do you want me to do? I’ll transfer all my shares in Under Coder. Satisfied?”

The Lollipop Mark suggested, “Apologizing might be a good start.”

“You think I don’t want to? I just want to take responsibility. My career’s already ruined, and if I leave here, I’ve got a metaphorical blade at my throat. Just decide how to punish me. Use your creativity. Mess me up as much as you want. I don’t ever want to think about High Gear again.”

Time couldn’t be reversed, and punishing someone in High Gear had its limitations.

The Gear Mark thought, ‘But that’s the charm of Under Coder. We’re all drawn here by that sense of freedom.’

The Yo Mark chimed in, “Fine. Just get lost. What are you even doing here? Anyway, it’s always us sane ones cleaning up after people like you. Go ahead, live your pathetic life and die like that.”

Number Seven burned with frustration but gritted his teeth, knowing he had to answer for his actions. “I said I’d take responsibility.”

“So how will you take responsibility? Asking us what to do won’t help! You should never have made that stupid mistake in the first place! You thought you were something special just because you got a title?”

“Ugh, seriously!” Number Seven shouted. “You can insult me all you want, but let’s get one thing straight! You think this is because I had a title? I didn’t have a choice!”

“What gives you the right to raise your voice?”

“Why wouldn’t I? Who here worked harder than me? High Gear’s creatures? I handled everything! I ran the physics engine tens of thousands of times based on those ridiculous proposals the Operator gave me!”

If he could turn back time, he’d wish he’d never met the Operator.

“But what did she do? She flirted with some weirdo, and as if that wasn’t enough...”

His words trailed off as the vacant screen flickered, and a system message appeared:

-Operator has entered the room.-

The mark symbolizing High Gear blinked, and Number Seven felt his chest tighten.

‘The Operator.’

She was the ultimate designer for whom High Gear meant everything.

“Number Seven.” Her voice was cold.

“What, what?”

“Apologize.”

“...Sorry.”

As soon as the words left his mouth, something he had desperately tried to protect crumbled inside him.

“I’m sorry. I got angry seeing you with that Yahweh guy and wanted to destroy everything. That’s the kind of person I am. You know me. I’m petty, selfish, trash.”

The room was silent.

“Yo was right. I ruined everything. I have no right to be angry. Curse me all you want until your anger subsides. I’ll take it all.”

The Operator spoke. “This isn’t something you can resolve with an apology. Do you realize how much you hurt me?”

“I know. So tell me—what should I do? I’ll do whatever you say. Honestly, I don’t know what else to do.”

After a moment of thought, she replied, “Let’s meet.”

“What?”

“Outside. All the operators. Let’s call it our first official meeting. I also have some things to say.”

The Lollipop Mark exclaimed, “Wow! That sounds fun!”

Everyone else stayed silent, but their silence was filled with apprehension.

“Uh… that’s probably not a good idea.”

“Anyone who can’t come doesn’t have to. Some of us aren’t human, and not all operators can leave. But Number Seven—you must show up. That’s my condition.”

Number Seven’s voice trembled. “I-I don’t want to.”

“Are you sure? I heard you’ll die if you don’t get unanimous approval to stay out.”

“I’d rather die! How could I face you? No, I just don’t want to.”

“Is that so? Then this meeting is over. I’ll report that we couldn’t forgive you.”

Number Seven whimpered. “Hey! Just this once, let it go. The people out there are terrifying. I’ll do anything else you want, okay? Please?”

“You have three seconds.”

“Ah…”

At Gold Wheel Headquarters, Shirone woke up in the recovery room.

A white ceiling greeted him. As he slowly sat up, he noticed over 200 beds, all empty. Fermi was nowhere to be seen.

‘Even Marsha’s not here.’

The texture of the air he breathed reminded him of where he was.

‘I’m back.’

Though it might have only been a day, he had experienced so much in High Gear.

Yet… it all felt like a fleeting dream.

‘An illusion.’

As Shirone reflected on what had happened in the outer world, the door opened, and Fermi entered.

Shirone frowned.

‘We took the same dose, so why is he up before me? He’s so irritating.’

Fermi smirked. “You look priceless. Maybe you should pull the blanket up. Don’t worry, I’m not going to eat you. Come on, we’ve got things to settle.”

Sticking his tongue out at Fermi’s back, Shirone got out of bed and adjusted his senses.

“Where’s Marsha?”

“She woke up four hours ago. They all went drinking. They’re not coming back tonight.”

“Is that okay? Drinking right away?”

“People live to give their brains pleasure. Sure, some need to save humanity to feel satisfied, but others just live for a good drink.”

There was a sharp edge to his words, but Shirone ignored it.

When they reached the top floor of the Golden Wheel building, where Shirone had been a few times before, Fermi gestured to a seat.

“Sit down.”

Shirone knew what “settling” meant.

“You’ve made deals with Wuorin, too, haven’t you?”

With the condition of sharing what was obtained from High Gear, Golden Wheel provided full support.

"I'm just receiving my share of the information. I think I'm free to use it however I want."

"Who said otherwise? I was just asking if you made a deal with Uorin. No need to get defensive."

Fermi twisted his lips.

'We just don't get along.'

There was a reason they had clashed since their school days.

'He's straightforward and smart. He knows what information can shake me.'

It might sound funny to say it was payback for teasing him in the recovery room, but he was definitely that kind of guy.

'Anyway, it seems like he's going to settle things properly. Well, that's why we make deals.'

Fermi nodded.

"It's not the same ratio. I need to think about what the Empress can do for me."

"Hmm."

Fermi and Uorin were individuals with immense influence on the world.

'Pony has to deal with such people in the Holy War. Of course, there's Mr. Lupist, but…'

From Shirone's perspective, who fought for humanity, all skilled individuals were potential trading partners.

"He went out into the outer world."

Since it was a story often told in the world of the Void, the explanation proceeded quickly.

Fermi's face, which had been smiling, hardened by the end of the conversation.

"…So that's how it is."

After a long pause, Fermi's mouth opened.

"That's shocking."

The fact that he showed emotion meant his mind was still full of thoughts.

"So, in the end, it's a battle between two universes. The universe of gods and the universe of humans."

"It's not a battle. It's a complete takeover."

Fermi acknowledged.

"Indeed, it wasn't a battle from the start. Their will acts as a result for us. Also, to maintain that status, this side's universe becomes a multiverse."

"That's a reasonable view. In short, all universes are signals sent from the outer world. An infinite number of multiverses all digging into the Void."

"Because it's the same signal, your senses didn't disappear in the world of High Gear and acted the same."

"That's right."

Fermi ran his hand through his hair.

"But is it possible? In the outer world of High Gear, you could have been mistaken at any time. That means even your mind was processed as a signal. To compute all that infinite universe, what kind of technology would it take?"

"The reversal of cause and effect."

"Right. Whether it's possible or not doesn't matter. Whatever we dream of, whatever this universe aims to achieve, the final result is…"

Shirone pointed to the side.

"It's already in the minus universe from the beginning."

"…A god?"

The moment he perfectly understood it in his mind, Fermi could imagine a god for the first time.

"What are you going to do now?"

It wasn't the time to be lost in thought.

"You have to find out. The outer world. You must have felt it in High Gear, but that's the key to Ultima."

The paradox of being able to plant a child in the world of the Void.

"Right. My simultaneous event is staying in the Kingdom of Paras. Once the king's approval is given, I'll explore the ancient ruins. They say it's a giant pyramid."

"You're talking about the place where the signal came from in the outer world. Are you sure about this? If your theory is correct and the god is the result, no matter how much you struggle, you won't stand a chance."

"Not necessarily."

As Fermi narrowed his eyes, Shirone raised his head.

"Goffin wanted to become a god with a heart. And he shot Hexa into this universe. What do you think that means?"

"…He shot it from the outer world."

"Exactly. He broke the perfect law of the gods and sent a single heart. I don't know how, but my existence is proof. That's why I don't give up. If I can return from the outer world and speak of hope…"

"Humanity will be united by love."

It was Yahweh.

"For now, we need to focus on what's necessary. The operators of High Gear will gather tomorrow. I'm planning to go. With their help, we'll mine the Apocalypse."

It was a plan laid out by Uorin.

"Alright, it's settled. The Empress too, let's not distinguish between allies and enemies for now and dig in. Leave the operator matter to me. I'll make a mining plan with the Parrot Mercenary Corps."

Fermi stood up, pushing his chair.

"Fermi."

"Yes?"

When he turned his head, Shirone, still sitting, had a serious expression.

"Why? Do you have more to say?"

"About the lost memory. I won't ask about the deal. But shouldn't I know the reason you accepted it?"

Fermi smiled.

"Ah, you want to hear that? I told you I'd tell you from the beginning. Anyway, the decision is yours…"

"I trust you."

The smile disappeared.

"I can't judge. So I have to rely on your judgment. If I know the reason I made the deal even at the cost of losing my memory, what happens then?"

A silence long enough to feel lengthy passed.

"Don't be mistaken. I'm not your friend. Trust me? With that statement, thousands of ways to use you are popping into my head."

"And for me?"

Shirone didn't give up.

"If I know, I have a chance to prepare. Now that the secret of the outer world is revealed. Considering all that, what do you think? Even now, shouldn't I know?"

As Fermi, who was staring straight at Shirone, walked to the door, he muttered.

"…No."

Before the door closed, a voice was heard.

"Trust me, you fool."
