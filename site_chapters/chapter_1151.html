Chapter 1151: 12 o’clock, 12 Nations (2)

Fermi did not give up.

'It won't be too late.'

Among the regrets one experiences in life, there are not many that are truly too late.

While running through the Keshia sector, he encountered fewer than three people.

They were all likely immersed in Angel.

As he increased his speed, time seemed to slow down, and Fermi had countless thoughts.

He had received support from Keshia.

Even the crazy idea of concocting drugs was accepted by King Manolka without suspicion.

It was not a rational judgment but a blind trust in Fermi.

"Tsk!"

In less than a second, Fermi opened Manolka's door.

"Old man…"

His gaze fixed on the old man sitting at the desk, who looked like he was asleep with a skeletal frame.

How many times had he been fooled by that appearance?

But the blood dripping from his bowed head was no lie.

Suppressing the thought that he was too late, Fermi approached Manolka.

As he reached out to touch his shoulder, Manolka flinched and raised his head.

"Gasp!"

Fermi's eyes widened.

"Old man!"

The first thing Fermi did upon confirming Manolka's survival was to cast a healing spell. As the warm light filled the room, Manolka's pained expression gradually stabilized.

"Don't make a fuss. I just dozed off for a moment."

"Let's go. I have a doctor friend."

If he asked Seriel, he could get help from the World Health Organization.

Manolka shook his head.

"You know this isn't a physical illness. It's a sickness of the heart. It's irreversible."

As Fermi was about to speak, Manolka coughed up a large amount of blood again.

'What the hell?'

Though invisible, the chilling aura of the under world was felt deep in his bones.

"Fermi."

Manolka smiled.

"We had quite a good time. Now it's time for me to go. That's the natural order."

"Don't talk nonsense. This is an assassination."

"Do what you need to do. Don't let emotions cloud your judgment. That's really not like you."

"What do you think I am? An emotionless machine? A drug lord who contaminated the world with drugs?"

"A capable guy."

Fermi fell silent.

"Yeah, you're not a good guy. But when you live as long as I have, even a rascal looks cute."

"I…"

As Fermi was about to speak, Manolka grabbed his collar roughly.

"End it as a rascal. Don't become a demon. There's nothing worth achieving by losing yourself."

Manolka's eyes burned with a fierce intensity.

"What's the difference between a rascal and a demon?"

"…There's no difference."

Manolka released Fermi's collar and relaxed, smiling faintly.

"You're a decent guy. It seems like you're desperately trying not to get caught."

In front of Manolka's eyes, which were losing their vitality, Fermi gave up arguing.

"I have to reach the top of the world."

"The top…"

Manolka gazed at the ceiling as if he could actually see it.

"I'm glad you're here."

With those words, Manolka's eyes closed, and his last breath left his lungs.

The King of Keshia had died.

In the lonely room where no one had entered, Fermi slowly stepped back.

'Time was too tight.'

He couldn't respond immediately to the attack from the Kingdom of Paras and the changes in the world's laws.

'Shirone was late. If I had stayed in the Apocalypse…'

Would the mining have been faster?

'I'm short on manpower. From planning to design, it's all my responsibility. Everyone is high on drugs…'

Thanks to that, he suppressed the expansion of the Satanic Church.

'Angel should be used moderately. I clearly instructed them to patrol in shifts every two hours. Why are they so stupid…'

Fermi stopped thinking.

'Enough.'

He shouldn't blame anything but himself.

'Get a grip, Fermi.'

Stop whining, kid.

'It's not that I couldn't do it. If I had given up sleep entirely, I could have patrolled.'

He thought it was efficient to secure about 40 minutes of sleep a day to maintain brain function.

'It was a misjudgment. I shouldn't have done that. I'm incompetent. I need to squeeze more time, collect information, classify it, manage personnel, and understand the situation…'

After finishing his self-criticism, Fermi put an imaginary revolver to his head.

'Pull the trigger.'

Click, click, click. With each pull of the trigger, his anger cooled.

'Think.'

That's what primates do.

'What I can do and what I can't. Among what I can do, what I need to do first. Identify all the immediate problems and solve them one by one…'

He was so angry that his head spun.

"Ah, seriously."

Fermi lit a cigarette and exhaled a long plume of smoke, muttering.

It was a dirty curse.

In a secluded alley in the capital of Jive, the midnight bell rang.

"Hmm. Shirone…"

Gustav's Four Knights had heard how Wizard had come this far.

From Wizard's perspective, there was nothing to lose if she could get their help.

'Kill Havitz.'

When focused, Wizard was colder and calmer than any other mage.

Natasha thought.

'She doesn't seem like a child. Will she become a mage with this kind of presence when she grows up?'

Even without hearing it, she could guess what price Wizard had paid to create this mindset.

Balkan spoke.

"Now I understand. The moment you harbor hostility, Satan escapes the law. Therefore, to kill Havitz, a murder without killing intent must occur."

Even the Law Killer had failed, but Wizard was different.

"Shirone created a Law Killer that can think. That's you. But you lost to Satan, and now you can't face Havitz in a pure state anymore."

"That's right."

That was why Wizard couldn't act.

"Even with the slightest logical thought, Havitz escapes my perception. I still can't read the voice of my heart. The initial strategy needs to be revised."

Balkan propped his chin.

"Hmm. A way to penetrate Vanishing. I do have one…"

At that moment, Wizard activated Void God.

Before Gustav’s Four Knights could react, she fixed her gaze forward and shouted.

"Get down!"

The moment Balkan turned around, the air around him felt deathly cold, as if plunged into subzero temperatures.

He didn’t know what it was.

‘What is this?’

But Wizard, possessing Hyper-Sense, detected a chilling presence that felt like it could freeze his very bones.

‘An emotion beyond imagination.’

A scream as red as blood, rage that reeked of decay, a heart shattered into fragments.

"Kuh!"

Balkan coughed up a mouthful of blood, and the color drained from Zetaro’s face.

"Balkan!"

The giant warrior, whose body had been tempered through Schema, collapsed with a thunderous crash as if he were a tree cut down by an axe.

"Balkan, stay with me!"

Natasha turned to Wizard with a sharp gaze.

"What the hell was that?"

"I don’t know," Wizard replied calmly. "It’s some kind of force. It doesn’t belong to this world, but it’s not from the afterlife either..."

A horrifying grudge lingering at the boundary.

"Guh! Guh!"

Zetaro quickly applied emergency healing, and Balkan gasped for breath as he regained consciousness.

"Kuh…!"

But his body, already affected by the unknown force, was rapidly deteriorating.

"It’s fine. I’m okay now."

Balkan tried to shake Zetaro off, but Zetaro firmly held onto his wrist, checking his pulse.

He tilted his head, listening intently, and his expression darkened.

‘Damn it…’

Balkan was strong, so he could hold on for a while. But if it were an ordinary person, they would have already died from this.

Balkan could feel it too.

"Something… attacked me."

As Natasha’s killing intent flared toward Wizard, Balkan raised a hand to stop her.

"No, it wasn’t her. She gains nothing from killing me."

Smodo asked, "Then who?"

"It must be someone from another nation. The only reason to take me out is because I’m the emperor of Gustav. Something infiltrated me… an extreme, unfathomable rage…"

Balkan coughed up blood again.

"It’s a kind of energy. If I’m lucky, I’ll last a month. If not, maybe a week or two at most."

There was no reason to grieve.

Natasha asked, "Havitz?"

"Well, if it’s an attack like this, he would know about it. But that’s not the main issue right now."

Balkan spat out a glob of blood onto the ground and looked up at Wizard.

"If you’re willing to join us, there’s a way to kill Havitz."

Wizard remained expressionless.

"What way?"

"A game."

Balkan’s eyes sharpened.

"A method of killing without direct intent to kill. We just need to set up such a system—a game of sorts. The question is whether Havitz will be interested. But if it’s you, that won’t be a problem."

He was certain.

‘Havitz didn’t kill Wizard. This isn’t just chaos. It’s emotional and logical.’

That meant there was a way to take him down.

"Fine."

Wizard responded.

"Approaching it as a system rather than an emotional battle… That could work. But in the end, this isn’t just a game. Killing Havitz will be real."

No matter how much they turned it into a game, in that final moment, emotions would come into play.

"Don’t worry."

Balkan turned to Zetaro.

"We have a game expert on our side. He’ll make sure we slit Havitz’s throat for good."

A paradox. A man who lived only for Havitz’s pleasure was, ironically, the perfect person to kill him.

"The moment we drive Havitz to the brink of death, we’ll undoubtedly face Vanishing. But Zetaro can handle it. Right?"

Zetaro only gave a sorrowful expression.

"And as for me…"

Balkan grinned.

"I’ve never lost a game."

"Are you insane?"

Upon hearing Havitz’s request, Uorin looked at him in disbelief.

"You seriously want to do that?"

"Does it matter? The dice are already cast. And they weren’t just any dice—the strongest ones possible. By tomorrow, the entire Holy War will be in chaos."

Havitz watched Uorin’s stiff expression.

"Why so serious? Isn’t this what you wanted? If you can’t control something, turn it into complete anarchy. That way, the initiative comes back to you."

It was true.

Ever since Satan had made his so-called request, disaster had already been set in motion.

‘No… This is far more logical than I expected. It’s even rational. If anything, it benefits Kashan. There’s no real downside to this strategy.’

The reason she hesitated was because it involved Shirone.

Havitz smirked.

"Relax. It’s just a game. A really fun one. I have something else I want to do."

Wizard, who loves Shirone.

‘I want to see her again.’

Even while talking with Uorin, Havitz couldn’t stop thinking about her—not even for a moment.

‘I want to meet Wizard… So first, I have to meet her. And then… huh?’

Suddenly, he realized something.

‘I…’

What exactly did he want to do with her?
