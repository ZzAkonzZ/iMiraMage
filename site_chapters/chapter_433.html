Chapter 433:

The ice of Niflheim trembled.

From a high vantage point, one could see the rhythmic movement of ice molecules spreading out like concentric circles on the surface of water.

Gaold stood at the center of that wave.

He stood there, and nothing else existed.

"Ughhh!"

Gaold gritted his teeth like a demon.

The veins that ran up his neck to his temples throbbed like annelids.

The guide, kneeling on the ice, stared blankly at the spot where the Grim Reaper had vanished.

"This, this is impossible."

At that moment, the ice couldn't withstand the wave and cracked, spreading in all directions.

A single crack zigzagged and curved towards the guide as if it had a mind of its own.

"Eek!"

As Kangnan grabbed the guide's collar and threw himself, a 4-meter-wide crack quickly passed through the spot where they had been.

The crack stopped, and the area was completely devastated.

It was Gaold's presence etched into Niflheim.

Whoooosh!

The Grim Reapers attached to the ice began to ascend, vomiting out the life stream they had accumulated throughout their lives.

"Huh? Huh?"

The guide's eyes shook with shock.

It was the first time in his life that he had seen the spectacle of ten or so Black Elixirs simultaneously soaring into the sky.

Just seeing it made his heart feel like it would stop from the intense pleasure, and his consciousness became hazy.

Seeing the Elixirs rolling on the ice with a clattering sound, he ran like a madman, flailing his limbs.

He didn't even notice his palms being cut by the cold ice as he frantically scooped up the Elixirs.

Holding them in both hands, they felt quite heavy.

They felt quite heavy, but it was as if he was holding the heaviest thing in the world.

'If all of this were mine…'

The guide's eyes were filled with desire.

But the moment he saw Kangnan's cold face, he snapped back to reality. Words he hadn't even thought of spilled out of his mouth.

"N-no, I mean, half of it is mine…"

"Pick them up. All of them."

Confirming that the guide was hurriedly picking up the Elixirs, Kangnan turned to Gaold.

'What a fool!'

Pain sensitivity increased by 100,000 times.

An experience unimaginable for ordinary humans. But for Gaold, it was just a common reality.

After all, pain is just a special signal transmitted to the brain by the nervous system.

It's just that.

A human who has to live a lifetime enduring extreme pain that he can't escape or reject.

That was the curse placed upon Gaold.

"Are you okay?"

Gaold, who had regained his former self, wrinkled his nose like a mischievous child.

"Heh, what if I'm not okay? Will you give me a kiss?"

Kangnan snorted.

If such a thing were to happen, how would Gaold take it?

The pain of his sensitive sensory organs? The greatest thrill of his life?

"Sorry, but I don't have such hobbies with old men."

"What's this? When we first met, you were so shy you couldn't even lift your head."

That never happened.

But Kangnan reminisced with a wistful expression, recalling that time.

'Did I… really?'

Seeing his bold demeanor, she felt relieved, but the more she did, the more the flames of anger rose within her.

Her already sensitive senses soared a hundredfold in an instant.

Even an ordinary human would scream and roll on the ground if the pain of a cut finger was amplified a hundredfold.

But Gaold was a human with a basic amplification of a thousand times.

It was obvious that he couldn't possibly be okay, even without looking inside.

"Listen carefully. When you increase the pain sensitivity in the future…"

"Now I see a path. Let's go quickly."

Gaold interrupted Kangnan and headed towards the guide.

The guide, facing reality, was completely terrified.

At the beginning of the journey, he had thought he could make a good profit.

But seeing the power that had instantly devastated 40 Grim Reapers, he finally realized where he had been dragged to.

It wasn't a matter of betrayal or backstabbing.

In Niflheim, where there was nothing but wraiths, Gaold was like a god holding his life in his hands.

'He'll kill me. He definitely will.'

The condition of taking 50% of the harvested Elixirs.

In terms of ratio, it was high, but in reality, if he obtained two Black Elixirs, he would get one of them.

At least, that had been the calculation so far.

But as the number of Elixirs increased, the 50% ratio began to feel overwhelmingly significant.

From the perspective of having to pay an enormous amount of Elixirs, there was no reason to keep him alive.

"How many have you collected?"

The guide flinched and opened his pouch.

"Uh, let's see…"

Including the 24 he already had, he had obtained 14 more this time.

Thinking that he had a total of 38 Black Elixirs in his possession, he felt like he was dreaming.

"As you can see, there are 38."

The guide hastily added.

"I, I don't have such great greed… So please…"

"Go back."

"What?"

The guide, who was about to beg for his life, looked up in confusion.

"Leave half and take the other half. From now on, it's beyond your level to guide."

If 40 Grim Reapers appeared simultaneously, there was no guarantee that more enemies wouldn't appear as they got closer to Hell.

It was in the guide's best interest to give up his greed at this point, and there was also the calculation that the proportion of 50% of the large amount of Black Elixirs would continue to grow.

'Go back? Is he serious?'

He was probably serious.

If they wanted to, the strong ones who could kill him at any time had no reason to backstab him.

As the most feared problem was resolved, the guide's mind began to spin quickly.

He would return to the mainland with 19 Black Elixirs.

He could live doing whatever he wanted for 38 years.

'No. Rather, let's use about 10 Black Elixirs to establish a community. Then I can live a life of leisure, commanding subordinates.'

But his heart didn't move as easily as his mind.

If he established a community with 10 Black Elixirs, he would be left with 9.

But the human heart is so peculiar that it felt like he had lost 10 of the 19 he originally should have had.

Moreover, the number 9 felt somewhat insufficient to keep for a lifetime.

'Damn it, if I had just one more, I could give up without regret. What's 9 compared to 10? It's annoying.'

The feeling of having the whole world with just one Black Elixir had already disappeared in the face of greater greed that came after achieving it.

'Yeah, just one more. It's just as dangerous to go back now anyway.'

He had memorized the paths with low Grim Reaper occurrence rates, but the data wasn't 100% accurate.

"How can you break the contract suddenly? I can't back down like this. I'll guide you to Hell."

Gaold stared intently at the guide.

It was a desperate look, as if he was grasping his last chance in life.

"Heh, yeah, a man should go big. Lead the way."

"Absolutely."

The guide quickly stood up and took the lead.

Patting the bundle full of Black Elixirs, he recalled his parents' words from his childhood.

Strike while the iron is hot.

"Get up. It's over."

Shirone and Plu removed the goggle-like devices and got up from the bed.

They had been isolated for seven days, undergoing daily physical examinations, and today was the day for brainwave measurements.

"In my opinion, there are no particular abnormalities. We will lift the isolation measures as of now, so you can return to your quarters."

Shirone sighed in relief.

The situation of being under strict surveillance, unable to even go to the bathroom properly, was finally over.

Shirone and Plu walked side by side towards their quarters.

Shirone, who had been careful with his words until now, spoke up.

"Don't you think it's strange? Why are they so persistent in examining us?"

They had suspected from the beginning that the infection by an unidentified pathogen was just an excuse.

Even so, they hadn't shown it because they didn't want to lose the opportunity to counterattack while their hands and feet were tied.

"I wonder about that too. The promised integration of the rebels is also dragging on. Now that the isolation is lifted, there should be some changes. I'll look into it bit by bit while you focus on external activities."

"Is that really necessary? It might have nothing to do with us."

Shirone's team's primary task was to integrate the rebels.

Even if there was a secret involving Raesis, if it was an internal matter of the command, there was no need to stir up trouble unnecessarily.

'No, there's definitely something.'

Although they had been completely cut off from external information during the isolation, the small changes she noticed while going to and from the examination room made her uneasy.

'I need to investigate without getting caught.'

A Norin passing by in the corridor glanced at Plu.

Raycis entered the secret laboratory of the Second Command.

As usual, she was wearing a red cloak, and her expression was highly excited.

"You sent the requested item to Yameng?"

"Yes. It's being disassembled now. Please confirm."

On the experiment table, a chunk of meat with thick fat attached to grayish skin smeared with mucus was laid out.

On the opposite table was the part corresponding to the creature's face.

Raycis shuddered as she confirmed the elongated nose like a drooping snake and the sharp teeth embedded in the circular mouth.

'It's certain. I've finally obtained it.'

The Bakyo species Isermon.

A creature classified as Rank A in the Purgatory Hunt, possessing the ability of Antithesis, which binds all targets except its own kind.

In other words, it was a law that rejected laws, presumably a unique mechanism of the home planet where Isermon resided.

"We underestimated Yameng. To think they brought back an Isermon so quickly."

"I was surprised too. Even the elite soldiers of the command were completely helpless against the Antithesis."

"They must have found a hunting method or hired an exceptional individual. Maybe both. Anyway, let's begin. Activate the Fuzix Machine."

Raycis turned to a device with a 4-meter-high glass tube at its center, with large glass spheres placed at each vertex of an octagon.

The Biological Mixer Fuzix Machine.

A device that creates new organisms with complex traits by mixing two or more species.

'I must enhance the Isermon somehow.'

The Bakyo species Isermon already possessed excellent abilities, but combining various traits that could utilize the advantages of Antithesis would make it an even more perfect weapon.

'An Antithesis powerful enough to suppress the actions of angels. If it can't achieve that level of power, it won't be useful in the war.'

After perfectly dissolving various Elixirs, adding water, and then adding Black Elixir, a black, elongated liquid flowed down the hose into the container.

It was 'Plasm,' used in the Elixir of Legends.

Plasm, which only reacts to organic matter, dissolves organisms and absorbs their genetic information.

When mixed with other Plasm and subjected to electrical stimulation, a new organism with the combined characteristics of the two entities is born, a mechanism identical to that of the Elixir of Legends.

However, since it doesn't use only humans as materials, the more types involved, the higher the failure rate.

The reason Raycis had her eye on Shirone was to control the variables of interspecies combination with the powerful mind of a Nephilim.

"Commander, the Plasm of Isermon is ready."

Raycis's eyes gleamed with joy as she saw the Plasm sloshing in the container.

The stench was so bad it made her want to plug her nose, but to her, it was the sweetest scent in the world.

'I will evolve endlessly.'

It was the water of life that would lead her to the position of a god.
