Chapter 604:

“Should I have given him the location?”

Liz pondered why Neide had been so angry at the research society.

He still hadn’t let go.

When Neide had formally confessed, she had drawn a firm line, but looking back, she realized she had been flustered.

‘What was I afraid of?’

He wasn’t an easy junior to be around.

Though they were close, Liz always felt a delicate balance of emotions when with Neide.

‘The timing wasn’t right.’

For a woman who had just graduated from a prestigious school and was dreaming of her future, a junior’s confession was like stepping into an uncertain darkness.

She simply couldn’t choose.

‘I didn’t know what my future would be.’

And now, she had Oscar, who promised a happy future.

‘Yes, this is how it should be. The past…’

Liz shook her head to erase the past.

“Neide.”

But the reason his name kept coming up was because of that unforgettable day four years ago.

“What’s going on?”

Neide looked around the cafeteria when Liz, who had been talking to him for nearly three months, was nowhere to be seen.

‘Hmph, let her be.’

After months of awkward interactions, it was time to give up.

“Where’s Liz? She went out yesterday and hasn’t come back.”

Despite himself, Neide’s ears perked up.

“Yeah, she said she had something to take care of for the research society.”

“She didn’t get caught, did she? I heard those alchemy guild guys are really nasty.”

The mention of the alchemy guild caught Neide’s attention.

“Those bastards. They took advantage of her being a student and charged exorbitant interest.”

Neide slammed his utensils down.

“Shut up.”

As the students turned to look, Neide stood up and approached Liz’s friends.

“If you’re so worried, why don’t you go check? Why sit here whining?”

Her friends grimaced as if they had seen something disgusting.

“What’s it to you? Bored without Liz? You should have treated her better.”

Neide pulled out a chair and sat down without responding.

“Tell me exactly what happened.”

Neide left the school and arrived in a dark alley in the city of Creas.

“Idiot.”

According to her friends, the Supernatural Psychic Research Society had conducted a soul transmission experiment.

They had used financial products from the alchemy guild to procure equipment but had fallen into the hands of a swindler.

Though he had no clues, Neide, familiar with the underworld, had no trouble finding Liz.

After walking for a while, he saw some low-level gang members smoking and chatting.

Neide approached them without hesitation and asked arrogantly.

“Hey, come here for a second.”

The gang members turned their heads nervously, then looked puzzled when they saw he was just a kid.

“What’s this brat? Do you know where you are… Gah!”

All they saw was a flash of blue lightning, and all but one lost consciousness.

“Where’s Liz?”

Neide grabbed the remaining man by the throat, and the terrified man’s eyes darted around as he shouted.

“I don’t know! I don’t even know who that is!”

Of course, he wouldn’t.

“Then find someone who does.”

Neide infiltrated the underworld and learned that Liz was being held at the alchemist Wiz’s house in the forest.

“Alchemy guild, huh.”

After sneaking into the house, he saw a warehouse 200 meters away guarded by two burly men.

As Neide approached, they shouted.

“Who are you? How did you get in here?”

“Give me the girl.”

The experienced guards immediately sensed the situation and charged at Neide.

Boom!

The sound of thunder made everyone in the warehouse turn towards the door.

“What’s going on?”

The warehouse door shattered, and Neide entered.

“Neide?”

Liz, looking haggard, muttered in surprise.

‘She’s still safe.’

Seeing the table cluttered with documents, it was clear they had been negotiating all night.

Neide guessed that from Liz’s perspective, the terms were unacceptable.

These were people who would casually hand over ridiculous loans and even body forfeiture agreements.

‘Professionals.’

Unlike other loan sharks, the alchemy guild dealt with large sums of money, so their employees were all mages.

Neide surveyed the seven mages standing menacingly and let out a hollow laugh.

“What a reckless senior. Did you even know what the guild was before borrowing equipment? And you’re just a student.”

Liz looked aggrieved.

“I paid all the rental fees! But they keep saying the equipment is defective and I have to buy it!”

Knowing this was a typical scam, Neide examined the equipment Liz had brought.

“Ultrix, huh.”

A high-priced device that discharged high-voltage electricity in one go, difficult to obtain without a related company.

But its durability was guaranteed.

“Defective? Open it up. I’ll check.”

As Neide approached, Wiz raised his hand.

“What are you? A kid who hasn’t even dried behind the ears inspecting Ultrix? Do you know how much this equipment costs?”

“You’ll see. You know the guild’s internal regulations, right? Article 7, Section 3. If caught scamming, you’re permanently expelled.”

Wiz’s eyebrow twitched.

“Are you an alchemist?”

Neide didn’t have an official certification.

But having studied magical engineering since childhood, his skills were on par with professionals, and he knew the industry’s taboos.

“If you’re worried, have it inspected by a company. Choose. Take the rental fee and leave, or take the equipment to the guild.”

“Surround him.”

At Wiz’s command, the mages moved.

“Neide!”

Liz tried to approach, but flames formed concentric circles around her.

“Don’t move, or you’ll be roasted.”

If they were hired by the guild, they were veterans in intimidation.

Wiz glared and asked.

“You seem to know a lot. Who knows you’re here?”

Neide sneered.

“Unfortunately, I have no friends…”

‘You idiot!’

Despair filled Liz’s eyes.

Even a lie involving someone else would have helped them escape safely.

“Really?”

As expected, Wiz’s face turned sinister.

“Take care of him.”

Six mages attacked simultaneously.

Even without certification, they had honed their skills in real combat, and their attacks, maximizing their attributes, flew sharply.

Boom!

In an instant, the warehouse flashed blue, and with the sound of thunder, dust filled the air.

“Cough! Cough!”

Wiz, coughing, opened his stinging eyes and looked ahead, his face hardening.

In a single strike, six professional mages lay electrocuted, their limbs twisted.

“Grr!”

Watching the mages grit their teeth and endure the shock, Liz’s face turned pale.

‘He’s really strong.’

She had thought he was just a difficult junior.

Seeing Neide emitting blue electricity over the fallen mages was a powerful shock.

“Is he doing okay?”

Shirone, still in the lab after Neide left, muttered.

“He’ll manage. He wouldn’t be unable to apologize, would he?”

As Iruki said, Neide wasn’t foolish, but the bigger issue was his emotional wounds.

“The graduation exam is coming up. He needs to get it together by then.”

Iruki glanced at Shirone and closed his book.

“Do you really think so?”

“What do you mean? Of course, he needs to get it together.”

“I told you. Neide was captured by bandits as a child and tortured with electricity. Very cruelly.”

“I know. That’s why he ended up like that. Honestly, I still don’t understand how he can control it.”

“That’s what I mean. Maybe it’s better for Neide to wander like this. For your sake, Shirone.”

“For my sake?”

“You’re giving up the evaluation because you think you can’t become a mage with just good grades.”

It was an uncomfortable topic between friends.

“Iruki, I…”

“It’s okay. Once the graduation exam starts, I’ll have to fight you too.”

Iruki stood up.

“But the problem is Neide. He probably can’t become a mage. No organization in the Red Line would hire a mage in ‘that state.’ Unless he drifts between guilds or falls into the Black Line.”

“Neide would never do that.”

“I think so too. But Shirone, Neide is completely different from you.”

Iruki looked at Shirone.

“You have endless potential, but Neide has clear limits. He was already the strongest at eight and still is. And in my judgment…”

Iruki’s eyes turned cold.

“At your current level, you can’t surpass Neide.”

“I don’t care if Neide is stronger than me.”

Shirone was sincere.

“But we can’t be sure. If we both do our best…”

“You’ve never seen Neide’s true face, have you?”

Shirone fell silent.

“I have. And so have a few others. They’d agree with me. Neide isn’t a mage. He’s just magic.”

Iruki sighed.

“I think it’s for the best. Neide has no future as a mage. So for your success…”

“I don’t like that.”

Shirone gritted his teeth.

“Sacrificing the future for a friend? If Neide is idle because of me…”

“It’s not just that. You know?”

“Even so, I can’t accept it. Sacrifice in competition? That’s nonsense!”

Iruki couldn’t decide what was right.

Shirone was a precious friend, and Neide’s power was almost unfair at the magic school level.

“I’ll go see Neide. I’m worried about him.”

Understanding Shirone’s feelings, Iruki stood up and followed him.

“Let’s go together.”

There was a loud knock on the door.

“Oscar? You’re back early?”

Liz, snapping out of her thoughts, quickly went to open the door, only to find Neide standing there with a troubled expression.

“Senpai, I’m here again.”

Perhaps because she had been reflecting on the events from four years ago, Liz’s demeanor was softer than it had been a few hours earlier.

“Come in. Did you meet Oscar?”

“Well… Oscar might be a bit late.”

Confused by his words, Liz opened the door wider and gestured for him to come in.

Seeing Oscar’s neatly folded clothes inside made Neide’s heart ache.

“Have a seat. What do you mean he might be late?”

Sitting on the chair, Neide still couldn’t decide whether to tell her what he had seen.

“Senpai, do you really love Oscar?”

“Haha! What’s this all of a sudden? Of course, I love him. That’s why we’re engaged.”

This was a different kind of torment.

‘I wish I hadn’t seen it.’

If he had remained ignorant, he might not have felt this burden.

“Senpai, I think…”

Neide hesitated, unsure about the future.

“I think Oscar might have another woman.”
