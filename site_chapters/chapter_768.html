Chapter 768:

“Well then, that settles that cleanly!”

As Minerva tucked the national bonds into her coat, Shirone quickly grabbed her wrist.

“Hold on a second.”

“Alright, alright. I was gonna split it with you anyway.”

“What do you even need this kind of money for?”

It was an amount that far surpassed the limits of personal luxury.

“You’re new to managing a department, so you wouldn’t know yet, but do you have any idea how much money it takes to run the Ivory Tower? The Corona Kingdom’s revenue isn’t even close to enough.”

“And that justifies openly asking for money?”

“We didn’t ask. It was a fair deal. And South Aymond didn’t lose out in the slightest.”

Cutting 40% of a national budget would be crippling in the short term, but in the long run, they had practically seized an entire nation for free.

“You’re the one who liberated North Aymond. Calculating the consequences of that action is also the job of a Star.”

If they handed over North Aymond without checks, the balance between nations would collapse.

‘Even giving it to the Holy War wouldn’t change that.’

Shaking off Shirone’s grip, Minerva waved the bond in front of his eyes again.

“If you’re not taking it, I’m keeping all of it.”

“Go ahead.”

From the perspective of the Universal Administration Bureau, all they hoped was that the money would be used for humanity’s safety.

“Let’s go back. We need to report to the Great Star.”

Just as they were heading toward the exit of the government building, someone blocked their path.

“What now?”

A young man with the left side of his head shaved like a chestnut, with scratches carved into his temple. The other side was braided into dozens of strands, each ending in fangs or beast-tooth-like ornaments.

‘A mage.’

Shirone immediately felt the aggressive, unfiltered energy of a Spirit Zone that disregarded the emotions of others.

“Greetings. I’m Sky Wigo, a certified mage of South Aymond.”

Minerva fired back without missing a beat.

“Don’t know you. What do you want?”

Wigo’s face flushed—he was a well-known mage in South Aymond.

Tch. Do they really not know me?

He turned hopefully toward Shirone, but Shirone only blinked innocently.

“Disappointing. Well, Miss Minerva is a senior, so fine. But Shirone, I expected more from you.”

Minerva looked at him, baffled.

“‘Miss Minerva’? ‘Shirone’?”

Not even Garcia, the President of the South Aymond Magic Association, spoke to them so casually.

Wigo must’ve realized he’d crossed a line, as he quickly bowed his head in apology.

“I’m sorry if I came across as rude. You’re both... not strangers to me, that’s all.”

“How so?”

“Well… I was a candidate for the Ivory Tower myself.”

Shirone raised an eyebrow.

“You were a candidate?”

The candidate list Arte had once shared included only Ra Enemy, Nane, Jin Seoug Eum, and Shirone.

“The Tower chose ten candidates this round. I was ranked ninth.”

If that was true, his frustration was more understandable.

“Ah, sorry. I didn’t know about the other candidates—”

Minerva cut Shirone off.

“Ninth-ranked Sky Wigo. You’re an Unlocker who’s opened the Immortal Function, and a Scale-type mage.”

Wigo’s lips curled slightly. She remembered after all.

“It’s an honor to be remembered.”

Shirone was surprised too.

“You knew?”

“Not exactly. But the Sky family? Of course they’d stand out. They’re one of the world’s greatest Seeker families—rivaling the Adrias family of Miro.”

Hearing the name Miro gave Shirone a sense of scale.

“Before Miro emerged, the two families were neck-and-neck. Adrias is defensive; Sky is offensive. Think of it as the difference between convergence and divergence.”

The Ardino branch of the Adrias family, like Yolga, was known for the world’s best defense.

“Seeker families study the principles of the world from birth, so they have a high chance of accessing the Immortal Function. At Wigo’s level, the government’s probably supporting him directly.”

“Hm.”

While Shirone was thinking it over, Minerva spoke up again.

“Then why come to us?”

Miro’s greatness lay in the fact that he wasn’t just a genius among the Adrias—he was utterly transcendent.

Even with the Sky name, there was no reason for the Five Stars of the Ivory Tower to change their stance.

Wigo’s face briefly twisted, but then turned into a wide smile like a reflex.

“Ha! Right. I’m just a mage who didn’t make the Tower. But did you know? We never even got the chance to test.”

He pointed to Shirone.

“I heard you weren’t much different from me, Shirone. If not for the presence of Ra Enemy as the absolute frontrunner.”

Back then, Shirone had ranked fourth.

Grouped with Nane and Jin Seoug Eum in a similar Karr value tier, but he’d undeniably had luck on his side.

‘So that’s why he’s angry.’

The fact that Karr value alone wasn’t absolute had been proven by Shirone ascending to become a Star.

‘He surpassed Nane and Jin Seoug Eum to reach the top.’

Though he now stood at the Yahweh level, Shirone’s power during the candidate tests had been nowhere near this strong.

‘If I’d been given the same chance…’

Wigo truly believed that had all ten candidates been tested equally, the outcome would’ve been different.

“Aha, I see.”

A mischievous glint appeared in Minerva’s eyes.

“Sometimes, opportunity is everything. So what now? A duel to the death? Winner becomes a Five Star?”

She had always loved cruelly crushing sprouting seeds before they could bloom.

“I’m not that foolish. And I have no lingering attachment to being a Five Star.”

Wigo was irritated at being treated like some brat.

‘The gap’s already too wide.’

He had missed his greatest chance at growth, simply because he didn’t surpass five of the world’s best students.

‘Father…’

When Shirone reached the level of Yahweh, Wigo’s father Cassia, who was now part of the Zion Project, had said this:

“Think of Shirone as if he doesn’t exist.”

Because the moment you acknowledge him, every mage in the world would lose their desire to strive for the top.

‘So I’m being left behind again?’

Just as a generation of mages had despaired once before because of Miro.

“I’m not here to make a fuss. Shirone’s strength is already proven by being a Star of the Ivory Tower.”

Minerva sounded disappointed.

“Then what do you want?”

“How about this? A contest—not in magic, but through Avatar Arts.”

The depth of one’s realm.

“Oho? Now that’s interesting.”

Minerva’s eyes lit up with renewed interest at Wigo’s unexpectedly bold proposal.

‘Very aggressive. As expected of the Sky line.’

Like a beast waiting for its leash to come off, Wigo was visibly anxious for Shirone to agree.

“Shirone, let’s do it. I mean, look how politely he asked. Wouldn’t it be inhuman to ignore him now?”

“Go on, do it.”

Egged on by Minerva, who was clearly dying to see a good show, Wigo added calmly,

“Just to make it clear, this is not a challenge. But if your vessel is truly vast, Shirone, perhaps it can even encompass the pride born from the narrowness of mine.”

Which was, in essence, an admission that he still couldn’t accept things as they were.

‘At the very least, when it comes to the depth of my realm, I’m not behind. I’m the strongest seeker-weapon my family has ever produced.’

Of course, it wasn’t something Cassia had instilled in him directly.

But as the third generation born into a family that had spent decades under Miro’s shadow, Wigo felt the pressure more sharply than anyone.

“I understand how you feel…” Shirone finally spoke after some thought. “But I’m sorry. I don’t want to fight you.”

Minerva looked even more disappointed.

“What? Why not? Don’t you care how unfair this is for Wigo? It’s not every day someone gets to fight a Star. Just go along with it!”

Her true motives were as transparent as glass.

“Stop interfering and stay quiet. Since when do you care so much about other people’s feelings?”

Wigo stared with intensity.

“…Then can I take that as running away?”

“Yes, that’s a fair way to put it. I don’t want to win, and I don’t mind if I lose to you.”

Wigo ground his teeth.

“Of course you’d say that.”

You’d already climbed to the highest peak and feasted your fill on the view—what did it matter to you now, stepping down and handing the throne to someone else?

‘That’s what I can’t stand.’

Being at the top wasn’t something you handed over. You pulled people down from it.

Concession could never replace conquest.

“I’ll ask as well.”

When Shirone turned, he saw Garcia deeply bowing—an action that looked almost unnatural coming from him.

“I’m sorry. But I truly don’t believe anyone here needs to experience defeat today.”

Garcia stepped forward.

“Wigo is a global talent. Though he was not chosen as a Star, the Republic holds great pride in him.”

“President…”

Even for someone ranked far below him in official status, Wigo couldn’t help but soften at Garcia’s words.

“Our enemy right now is not each other—it’s the Ma within us all. I fully understand the deep meaning behind your refusal, Shirone.”

He looked to Wigo.

“And I believe Wigo’s concern for the world is no different. But as a human, he stands before a wall he never had the chance to face. Helping him climb over that wall—that is something only someone like you, one of humanity’s greatest assets, can do.”

Minerva nodded with eyes closed.

“Well said. Wigo—or rather, someone of Wigo’s caliber—certainly has potential for the future…”

Shirone interrupted her.

“I can’t promise it’ll be helpful.”

“It will be,” Garcia said.

Whether Wigo lost and accepted it, or perhaps even won—it would resolve the frustration he held.

‘And if it’s a contest of Avatar Arts… the latter might not be impossible.’

Garcia, more than anyone, knew the nature of Wigo’s Avatar.

“Alright.”

At Shirone’s consent, the three pairs of eyes locked on him gleamed with intensity.

“However…”

Now, Shirone was serious too.

“If this is your reason for the duel, then I won’t hold back.”

“That’s exactly what Wigo and I hoped for.”

And Minerva said inwardly:

‘Me too, of course.’

As if hearing her thoughts, Shirone let out a soft sigh and began walking.

“Where should we do this?”

“The government building has too many eyes. The Sky family estate would offer more privacy.”

It was a respectful offer in consideration of a Five Star’s reputation—but also meant stepping onto enemy ground.

Shirone didn’t care.

“Alright. Let’s go there, then. We’re short on time, so it’s best we start right away.”

As Shirone exited the building first, Wigo exchanged a glance with Garcia and clenched his fists tightly.

‘This is it!’

The Ivory Tower, the gathering of the world’s greatest mages—and he now had a chance to defeat one of its very peaks.

‘I have a shot at winning!’

As Garcia followed Wigo out of the building, Minerva lightly tapped the mage president on the back.

“Well done, very well done. What’s gotten into you, huh? Actually doing something helpful for once.”

Garcia gave no response.

“Wanna bet on it? Loser grants the winner a wish. Let’s pick up some snacks on the way—these kinds of shows are better with food.”

‘I really hate this woman…’

Garcia still said nothing.
