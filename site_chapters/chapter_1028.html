Chapter 1028: The Circulating World (4)

Fire Resource Management Corporation

Sitting at the desk in the president’s office, Lete made meaningless sounds as she stared at the wall.

‘Why do I feel so uneasy?’

The fire resources were stable, and the circulation of the tributaries was running smoothly.

“Monoras.” All her anxiety stemmed from the fact that the lowest-ranking demon in the underworld was nowhere to be seen.

“No news since he left for Laviette? Did he get sidetracked again?”

She slammed the desk and shook her head.

“No, it can’t be.”

Despite being deceived by Monoras a billion times, she believed in him.

“Surely, even in this unprecedented situation, he wouldn’t… He’s a manager, after all. Yes, it can’t be.”

Rian, a living spirit, was wandering the underworld with the karma chain broken.

‘Every being in the universe bears its own karma. It can’t be surpassed.’

Thus, a system error.

And only a handful of demons in the underworld could solve such a problem.

“Annoying. As if I didn’t have enough work already.”

Feeling uneasy, Lete left the president’s office and headed to the Karma Management Department.

On both sides of the corridor, numerous informers were monitoring the living spirit through visions.

“Hah! Hah!”

Among them, an informer was making excited noises and busily touching herself.

“Riona.”

In charge of Etella and Shagal, she turned hurriedly at Lete’s voice.

“Oh, President.”

Lete placed a hand on her shoulder.

“You’re working hard. These are the ones, right? I heard they made the rankings this time.”

Riona spoke excitedly.

“Yes, they’re ranked seventh in history. Purification time is 87 years. How immense must the combined good and evil be? Just imagining it makes me feel so hot I can’t stand it.”

Lete felt dizzy as well.

“Taiji is the signal that birthed the universe. The circulation of yin and yang contains immense power. It combines two opposing concepts into one.”

Riona looked at Lete.

“Then the first universe was also born through circulation. What concept circulates?”

“Probably… electricity.”

Only a manager could guess this.

“Energy generated by the circulation of positive and negative poles. Thus, every concept born from that energy principle is divided into two, and that’s our universe.”

Lete raised her index finger.

“Reality and the underworld. The reason two completely different worlds can coexist is because they were designed with the principle of Taiji. But when applied to humans, it’s very dangerous.”

“Why?”

“It has the property of reversing all definitions of the universe. What happens when good and evil, black and white, material and immaterial, up and down, all concepts are integrated?”

The first one.

“The only concept that can transcend the universe is born. That’s what we call the First One. It’s no wonder those two bear immense karma.”

Riona swallowed hard as she looked at the screen.

“…An incredible connection.”

“Hoho! This is just the beginning. Those bearing karma can never be at ease, so it’s best to keep watching.”

Humans couldn’t understand, but the combination of good and evil evoked a strange romance in demons.

‘I hope it turns out well.’

She didn’t know what the outcome would be.

“By the way.”

Lete licked her lips and asked.

“Any news from that pig? No informers spotted him?”

“No. The chief secretary is also a manager, so tracking him through the karma screen has its limits. Anyway… there’s no report of him being found near Laviette.”

Lete grabbed her head.

“Hah, what is he doing? He’ll do well, right? Yes, he’ll do well. I sincerely asked him, so he’ll handle it properly this time. Right?”

Riona twisted her lips.

‘I doubt it.’

Perhaps Lete was the person who knew Monoras the least in the underworld.

River of Depravity, 78th Tributary

The village along the snake-like river of fire was a marquisate in the underworld.

The marquis was Picaras.

As the manager of a tributary with an average daily purification time of 3.5 million hours, he was currently entertaining Monoras.

“Come, come! Our chief secretary, who does great work for the underworld. Have a drink.”

The marquis, with a snake’s face, poured a drink for the pig-headed Monoras.

“Good, good! Quack quack quack!”

The Marquisate of Picaras was still less than halfway to his business trip destination.

The marquis gave orders.

“Come on, you lot! Why aren’t you moving quickly? We’re hosting such a magnificent and handsome guest.”

As twenty succubi got to work, Monoras drooled.

“Quack! Quack!”

As the atmosphere heated up, the marquis subtly handed over some documents.

“Chief Secretary, this…”

“Huh? What’s this?”

The title read ‘78th Tributary Expansion Development Report,’ but he didn’t read the contents.

“The number of demons in the marquisate has increased significantly. And with more demons, there are more mouths to feed, right?”

“Yes, yes.”

Monoras just wanted to focus on his senses.

“So I’ve come up with a solution. If our influential chief secretary from the Fire Corporation would just sign…”

As he reacted sensitively, Picaras turned and clapped.

An attendant with ram’s horns bowed and brought in a small box.

“You’ll understand when you see this…”

Opening the box revealed a golden key the size of a human palm.

“A key made of pure flame…”

Monoras slammed the table, scattering the succubi.

“Picaras, you scoundrel!”

With a voice full of tension, Picaras quickly bowed.

“Yes, yes!”

“Are you insulting me? Do I look like someone who would handle official duties for such a trinket?”

Understanding perfectly, the marquis, half-rising in haste, shouted.

“Bring it in!”

This time, the door burst open, and burly demons brought in a huge treasure chest.

Thud! Thud! Thud!

There were four in total, and Picaras hurriedly inserted the golden key.

Opening the lid revealed a blinding pile of treasures.

Turning awkwardly, Picaras pointed to the treasure chests.

“The one who holds this key is worthy of owning this treasure, or something like that.”

Staring intently at the chests, Monoras suddenly threw back his head and laughed.

“Guffaw! Quack! Quack!”

“Haha! Hahaha!”

As Picaras laughed along, the succubi clapped and flew back in.

“You’re generous! That’s why I recognize you!”

“Of course. It’s all thanks to you, Chief Secretary, for handling official duties so well.”

Returning to the table, Picaras subtly handed over the documents.

“Quack! I always do well. Now, what were we talking about? Something about the tributary?”

“Oh, it’s nothing to worry about. Honestly, someone who controls the fires of the entire underworld shouldn’t be concerned with such minor tributaries. The underworld wouldn’t function otherwise. There are demons who handle the big picture.”

“That’s what I’m saying! You know it, but the president, Lete, doesn’t. Isn’t that frustrating?”

“It is.”

Picaras snapped his fingers.

“Now I see why our chief secretary looks so troubled. At times like this, subordinates should take proper care. I’ll start by paying attention!”

“Guffaw, well, you’re doing well.”

As Picaras quietly handed over the pen, Monoras signed the approval line with a bloody signature.

“Bravo! Truly straightforward!”

As the succubi laughed and clapped, Picaras waved his hand.

“What are you doing outside? Bring it in quickly!”

Carts loaded with food and drink endlessly filled the table and then left.

“Have a drink. There are succubi lined up outside the castle.”

“Guffaw! Really?”

In the next hour, the marquis passed seven more approval documents.

“Now, this is a dispute over control of the fire dam with the Marquisate of Mydron.”

“Hey! Let’s have some fun now!”

Picaras, nodding madly, leaned towards Monoras.

“This is the last one. Really! The last! I swear!”

Thus, the 78th tributary dispute, a hot topic at the Fire Corporation, was resolved.

“Ow.”

Lete rubbed her left shoulder and muttered.

“Why does my shoulder hurt so much these days?”

Riona turned her head.

“Aren’t you overworking? You haven’t slept since the spiritual realm closed, just working. Should I massage it?”

“I’m fine for now. As long as nothing else goes wrong. But the Grand Duke needs to come soon.”

Watching Lete’s tired face, Riona felt bitter.

‘She’s really capable.’

Without her, the underworld would have fallen into chaos after the spiritual realm closed.

“Can the Grand Duke fix it?”

“I don’t know. But he’s the best at handling hidden codes in the underworld. Speaking of which, I should check. Who’s the informer in charge of Rian?”

“Vivian, I think.”

“Really?…”

Lete patted her shoulder and headed to Vivian’s station.

“Chains of Gehenna. What’s the status?”

“Continuing without rest. Judging by the direction, the Fire Corporation seems to be the destination.”

She sighed.

“Where are they now?”

“They’re about to pass the Wailing Wall. But President, once they get past it…”

Vivian raised the altitude of the screen.

“They’ll soon reach Laviette, the city of magical engineering. Isn’t that where the Chief Secretary went on his business trip?”

“Huh?”

Lete’s eyes widened.

“Oh! Right! If Monoras corrects Rian’s error there, it’ll be over!”

As she clapped her hands like a girl, Vivian continued to monitor the high-altitude screen.

‘They’ve arrived.’

A massive black wall stretching thousands of kilometers awaited Shirone and Rian.

Woooooo!

Even before arriving, the dreadful wailing was enough to make one want to cover their ears.

‘This is the Wailing Wall.’

No matter where they looked, the wall’s end was not in sight, and its thickness was likely similar.

Rian stopped walking.

“It’s immense.”

Compared to the wall’s size, their existence was smaller than dust.

“It’s painful… Get me out of here… How many are there?”

The wall was made entirely of human bodies, without a single exception.

“Horrifying.”

Even the pus had rotted, turning black like oil, and the surface was grotesque with protruding faces and limbs.

“I’m hungry… Thirsty…”

Though none had the strength to shout, the sheer number made it noisy.

“To reach the Fire Corporation, we must pass through this gate.”

A crack, smaller than a fissure compared to the whole, vertically divided the wall.

“Alright. Let’s go, Shirone.”

Though the Chains of Gehenna could activate at any moment, Rian’s compass always pointed straight ahead.
