Chapter 372: Iron Gate (1)

"You're here?"

Neide threw his bag aside and jumped onto the sofa.

"Ugh, I'm exhausted. The road from the central region to the south is so rough."

"I came from the capital."

"Do you think I came in a family carriage? I traveled without food and holding in bathroom breaks. If it had been a Merkodine family carriage, I could have eaten and... well, you know."

"Or done both at the same time."

"Enough! Anyway, how have you been? You didn't betray us and do some special training, did you?"

Iruki didn't deny it.

"Well, now that I'm here, I should aim to graduate, right?"

Neide jumped up from the sofa.

"Really? Damn, if you do that, what about me? What kind of magic is it? Better than Cancellation?"

"It's still at the theoretical stage, so it's not really magic yet. As long as I complete it before the graduation exam, it'll be fine. I'll tell you once I make some progress."

Every senior plans their physical rhythm to peak on the day of the exam.

No matter the process, the result is everything, as seen with Amy.

"Ugh, lucky you. I just played around during the break."

Iruki looked at Neide with a bittersweet expression. Despite his words, he didn't seem particularly upset.

Even as a senior, it was clear Neide still had no interest in becoming a mage.

"But you..."

"Hmm? What?"

Iruki shook his head and swallowed his words.

"Never mind, it's nothing."

"Ugh, you're so bland."

The door swung open more violently than before, and Shirone entered.

A smile bloomed on his face as he reunited with his friends.

"Hey! What's that smell?"

But his good mood was short-lived, and he quickly covered his nose in disgust.

The research club room was a mess, littered with trash. How could these guys stand the smell?

Neide and Iruki looked at each other and laughed.

"Sorry. I sprayed some cologne after a long time. Did you like it?"

"Shut up! Let's clean up first!"

The three of them grabbed cleaning tools and started a major cleanup.

By the time the place, which had looked like a giant trash dump, was somewhat clean, it was almost time for the event.

Exhausted, Neide lay on the sofa and looked at the ceiling.

"What time is it?"

"One o'clock. We should start getting ready."

Shirone suddenly remembered and jumped up from the sofa.

"That's right! I haven't picked up my uniform!"

"I got it this morning. Yours and Neide's."

Neide gave a thumbs up.

"You're always so thorough. But do we really have to wear that? I really don't want to."

The uniform was the magic school's special attire, only worn for significant events, but students found it so ridiculous that they avoided wearing it even once a year.

"Why do we have to wear uniforms for the entrance ceremony? I'd rather watch myself apply hemorrhoid cream in the mirror."

Shirone looked at Neide and asked,

"You have hemorrhoids?"

"It's just a saying! Anyway, let's get ready. Where's my uniform, I mean, my hemorrhoid cream?"

Iruki pointed to the corner. Three boxes were stacked like a tower.

They each took a box with their name on it and unfolded the uniforms. The white robes with stripes along the shoulders fluttered like ghosts.

While they might look magely once worn, it would be better for their mental health to wear masks in crowded places.

"Ugh, we have to meet the Class Two kids in this? They don't wear uniforms. We'll be a laughingstock."

"We have no choice. It's the rule."

Iruki didn't care much. As long as the clothes covered the important parts, like the head, eyes, and heart, he was fine.

The three of them stood in front of the mirror, wearing their uniforms over their clothes. They looked like three blobs of liquid monsters.

But contrary to their initial thoughts, their expressions were solemn.

"Will we ever get to wear these again?"

With the busy schedule of senior year, there were few opportunities to wear the uniform. If they didn't pass the graduation exam, this would be the last time they saw themselves in these robes.

Iruki flapped his sleeves, which were longer than his arms, and said,

"It won't be easy. We lack information."

Neide looked surprised.

"Really? I think you and Shirone will definitely pass."

"It's just a feeling. Mathematically, it's one-third. If Shirone, Dante, and Amy are there, two will fail. If Shirone passes, Dante and Amy can't graduate."

Neide couldn't imagine any of them failing.

"No way. All three could pass."

"That's because you know them. People always think their information is the most accurate. But if that were true, no one would fail. Even on average, only 3.3 students per class pass. And we're in the lowest Class Three."

Neide couldn't argue.

They were at the pinnacle of the magic school. The skill gap was narrower than in any other class, and no one thought they would fail.

Shirone smoothed the wrinkles of his robe and said,

"If we all plan to pass, we don't need probabilities. Let's start with a fresh mindset."

Shirone was right.

All the processes they went through from the lower to the advanced classes were reset. It was a final showdown among 30 students aiming to become mages.

Twenty minutes before the graduation event, ten students were walking towards the central park from different paths.

They all wore their uniforms, and their eyes were more intense than usual.

Shirone's group, the first to arrive, greeted their competitors for the next year.

These were the top ten students based on combined practical, theoretical, and performance evaluation scores.

The aura of the gathering students made the air feel heavy.

Shirone (ranked 3rd in the advanced class) looked down the eastern path.

Canis (ranked 6th) and Arin (ranked 7th) were walking together.

Shirone had fought them during the Arcane attack and in Heaven. He knew better than anyone that their skills were formidable.

Canis met Shirone's eyes but didn't speak and turned away.

He knew that the ten gathered here wouldn't all pass together.

Arin approached instead.

"Hi, Shirone."

"Hi, Arin. How was your break?"

"Just... okay."

Arin smiled shyly.

She remembered Canis's warning not to reveal what they had achieved during the break.

"Hey, you're already here? You're not nervous, are you?"

Recognizing the voice, Shirone turned around.

From the southern path, Boyle (ranked 8th) and Pandora (ranked 9th) were walking together.

Pandora, who had once tried to cozy up to Dante's group but was eventually cast aside, seemed to be back with Boyle. Despite their frequent arguments, their bond from studying together since childhood was undeniable.

'Boyle, the summoning mage, and Pandora, the scent mage.'

Boyle had lost to Dante before, but his summoning magic was not to be underestimated.

Pandora's scent magic was also tactically challenging to deal with.

"Shirone, look over there."

Neide pointed to the western path.

Dante (ranked 1st) led the way, followed by Closer and Sabina.

Shirone chuckled at Dante's rolled-up sleeves, pinned in place.

Even at the old castle ruins, Dante's desire to stand out was evident.

But today, Shirone was more concerned about Closer and Sabina.

They had undergone intense training under Vice Principal Olivia during the break, and their eyes showed it.

Closer could freely manipulate rocks, and Sabina could scatter dozens of sharp wind blades per second.

They were undoubtedly stronger than during the 2000th duel.

"Why the serious faces? It's a good day. Smile a bit."

As Dante raised his hand and approached, Boyle frowned.

"What's with your outfit? Don't you know the uniform rules?"

Before answering, Dante scrutinized Boyle's style. As usual, Boyle's hair was slicked back with oil, and his uniform was crisply ironed.

"Isn't that a bit too much? You look like you'd bleed if someone brushed against you."

"What's wrong with it? It's neat and tidy. You might lose points for wearing it like that."

"So what if I lose a few points? As long as I pass the graduation exam."

While Dante and Boyle argued about fashion, Closer and Sabina approached their targets.

Closer (ranked 4th) was most eager to confront Iruki (ranked 2nd).

"So, the day has come for us to face off again? Get ready. Every day might feel like hell."

"If you don't know, there's no need to say it. Are you asking me to teach you?"

"You...!"

Closer's eyes filled with anger. He had never met someone who provoked him so much.

Meanwhile, a different tension was brewing elsewhere.

Sabina (ranked 5th) hesitated before finally speaking to Neide (ranked 10th).

"Hi. It's been a while."

"Oh, yeah."

Sabina felt hurt by Neide's indifferent attitude. Or maybe she didn't know what to feel.

She had dated many men in the capital, Bashka, but this was a different kind of thrill.

'My heart must be broken. Or I'm a pervert.'

Regardless, she couldn't stop looking at Neide and couldn't retreat now.

"The uniform... looks good on you."

But Neide only responded with a bitter smile.

For someone who had no intention of becoming a mage, being told the uniform suited him was just painful.

Pandora, watching from a distance, smirked.

In the past, Pandora had flattered Sabina, but after intense training, she now felt confident and unashamed.

'She's cunning as always. Already planning ahead, huh?'

Pandora, who had witnessed Sabina being beaten by Neide, suspected that Neide would be the biggest wildcard among the ten students here.

Though currently ranked 10th, if Neide revealed his true potential, the rankings could shift dramatically.

"Looks like everyone is here. Let's get going and get this over with."

Shirone wasn't the type to take the lead, so Dante took charge.

His position as the top-ranked student in the advanced class helped him command the group.

Of course, once they passed through the Iron Gate, the advanced class rankings would no longer matter.

The Class Three students headed towards the senior building.

At the top of the slope stood the imposing Iron Gate.

Below it, ten Class Two students stood in a line, waiting.

"Hey, juniors! Hurry up! You're so slow!"

"Nice uniforms! Are you going to a school play? Hahaha!"

The taunts from Class Two were a long-standing tradition and felt even more intense than the rumors suggested. But none of the Class Three students showed any reaction.

"Hmph! Seniors, my foot! Once we're in the senior class, we're all the same!"

Boyle gritted his teeth and muttered, trying to psych himself up.

Closer swung his broad shoulders and quickened his pace.

"Yeah, keep talking, losers. I'll cook you all one by one."

Most students glared to avoid being intimidated, but Dante was simply bored.

As he watched his peers' reactions, he turned to look at Shirone.

'He's not nervous at all.'

A slight smile played on Shirone's lips, and his eyes sparkled with passion and excitement.

How could he not be thrilled? It was the scene he had imagined even in his dreams.

The life of a mage he had always wanted. The final gateway was now just ten steps away.

His heart pounded, and his mind felt like it was soaring to another world.

"Come on in! Do you think you can graduate? You're just fertilizer for me!"

"This is hell, you bastards! Your lives end here!"

Shirone clenched his teeth and fists.

A mage. A mage. He would become a mage!

'Fine, I'll enter hell or whatever it takes.'

With each step, the Iron Gate, a symbol of wisdom, seemed to tremble.
