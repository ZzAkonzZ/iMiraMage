Chapter 318: One-Third (4)

"I'm sorry. But I'm so upset. I know it's wrong for me to say this to you, who passed..."

"It's okay. I would feel the same way. This isn't the only exam. I'm the one who should apologize. And I don't think I can go to the party."

After the graduation exam, it's a tradition for the successful candidates to have a graduation party. Anyone can attend, but no one who failed has ever gone. There's no need to show up and dampen the spirits of those who passed.

"What's the point of a party now? I'm not going either!"

Amy looked at Seriel with loving eyes. Despite her early elimination, she was proud of her friend who fought through the turmoil to achieve her dream.

"Promise me, Seriel. Promise you'll go to the party. If you don't enjoy this moment, I won't be able to be happy even if I pass next year. I don't want to take away this once-in-a-lifetime joy. Smile proudly for me."

Seriel bit her lip and nodded.

For Amy, who had to fight for another year, this was the most important thing. She could easily set aside her own feelings for her friend.

"Alright. I won't think about anything else. I'll enjoy my success to the fullest. So you have to pass next year."

"That's the spirit. Thank you, Seriel."

A teacher called out from the shadows.

"All successful candidates, gather now! There will be an orientation! Hurry! There's no time!"

The reality of passing would sink in with the sunrise. And with the graduation party late at night, now was the best time for the orientation.

"Go on. See you at the graduation ceremony."

"Okay. You get some rest too."

Before leaving, Seriel looked back at Shirone.

She knew Amy was consciously avoiding eye contact with Shirone. So he was the one who could take care of her until the end.

Shirone nodded reassuringly. Only then did Seriel feel at ease and ran to join the other successful candidates after greeting Amy's parents.

Amy watched her friend's retreating figure with satisfaction and then let out a deep breath.

"Phew, I'm tired. Mom, Dad, you should go too."

"Are you sure? How about we go out for a bit? I can talk to the school."

"No, it's fine. I'm not the only one who failed. Why make such a fuss? We'll see each other soon during the break anyway."

Shakora, understanding his daughter's feelings, said, "Alright, we'll go. We'll send a carriage for the graduation ceremony. Get some rest tonight."

"Okay. You too, Dad."

Isis turned first, unable to hold back her tears any longer.

Following his wife, Shakora looked back at Shirone.

"Shirone."

"Yes. Don't worry, I'll take care of her."

Shakora conveyed his gratitude with his eyes and walked away. Parental comfort wouldn't help in this situation. It was fortunate Shirone was there.

Left alone with Amy, Shirone said nothing. He would answer if she asked something, but if she remained silent, he would respect that silence.

"Damn it! I failed again!"

"Damn it! How many times do I have to take this graduation exam?"

The silhouettes of disgruntled students emerged from the Colosseum. Shirone recognized Fermi's group by their voices.

Seeing Amy, Fermi changed direction and approached. Despite failing the exam for the seventh time, he showed no sign of disappointment.

"Well, meeting another failure is awkward. Don't be too upset, little lady. That's life. I'm in the same boat. How about joining us..."

Shirone stepped in front of Fermi. The cold look in his eyes made Fermi scratch his head and joke.

"That's a face I'd like to punch. But I’ll let it slide today. I'm sore all over because of that little lady. When top contenders clash early, it always ends up like this. My life is a mess too."

Fermi had already put up a defensive barrier and watched Shirone's reaction. But Shirone said nothing.

Fermi turned back to Amy.

"Haha! Anyway, Amy, how about..."

Shirone blocked him again.

This time, Fermi didn't stay silent. His eyes turned cold for a moment as he looked down at Shirone with a domineering gaze.

Shirone calmly met his gaze. Anything Fermi said would only hurt Amy. He didn't want Fermi to approach her.

"Having a boyfriend is nice. Well, enjoy your time together."

Fermi turned and walked away without hesitation. Rejoining his group, he shouted loudly.

"Let's go! Let's drink, you pathetic losers!"

"Haha! Yeah! Let's drink till we drop!"

Shirone looked at Amy. She had turned away and was staring into the distance, seemingly uninterested.

"Let's go, Amy. I'll walk you back."

"...Okay."

As they walked to the graduation dormitory, a heavy silence hung between them.

Amy, staring at the ground, frowned and turned to Shirone.

"Sigh, this is unbearable. Hey!"

"Yeah?"

"Why do you look so down? It's making me feel weird. If you have something to say, just say it. What's bothering you?"

"But I don't have anything to say."

"That's the point. I just failed the graduation exam. I'm not the only one who failed, and even if I were, my life isn't over. So why are you acting like this?"

Amy's words gave Shirone courage.

"Haha! You're right. So, I get another chance too?"

"Hmph, you should be grateful. I won't make this mistake again."

"One mistake is a stepping stone to growth, two mistakes are a disgrace to the family?"

"Of course. And tell your silly friends that next year's graduation class will be brutal. I'll crush their spirits from the start."

To Shirone, Amy's words sounded like an apology for disappointing them.

But she had nothing to worry about. Neide and Iruki were confident Amy would bounce back.

They returned to their usual selves, chatting as they reached the dormitory. Although she had failed the exam, the tension of the past year had lifted.

"Sigh, I'm tired. I need to wash up and sleep."

"Yeah, don't think about anything tonight. Just rest."

"Okay. See you tomorrow. Thanks for walking me back."

With a tired face, Amy said goodbye to Shirone and entered the dormitory. The hallway felt different from the morning, with a slight chill in the air.

Entering her room, Amy closed the door and sighed. No matter how tired she was, she needed to wash off the sweat.

But as she headed to the shower, she stopped. Transparent tears streamed down her face.

"Sob! Sob..."

She was devastated.

So devastated she felt like dying.

Why her? One-third of the students passed, and ten of her study mates had become mages. Why was she the only one left behind?

She had been in top condition. Why was she still at school? Why did she have to endure another year of this hell?

Collapsing onto her bed, Amy clutched her blanket. She couldn't stop her face from contorting into a crying expression.

"Ah... Ah..."

Shirone let go of the doorknob and leaned against the wall. He realized he couldn't see her anymore tonight.

"Ah... Ah..."

Amy's sobs were harsh and breathless.

"..."

But Shirone just looked up at the ceiling with deep eyes, staying there for a long time.

Long past lights-out, the lights were still on in Alpheas's annex. Olivia's angry voice leaked out through the window.

"How could this happen? How did you manage the students?"

It was Olivia's first time supervising the Alpheas Magic School graduation exam, and she was shocked. After the exam, she immediately investigated the students' profiles in the records room.

"These kids here!"

Olivia shook the profiles and threw them onto the table where Alpheas sat.

The papers spread out, revealing several students' names.

It was Fermi's group.

Alpheas glanced at the profiles briefly before speaking disinterestedly.

"Calm down. The exam is over. It can't be overturned, and there's no reason to."

"Why not? The participants colluded!"

"Collusion is part of strategy. Situational judgment is crucial in combat."

"Who doesn't know that? The problem is that this collusion was separate from the tactical flow. They had already formed alliances before the exam. Why? What do they gain from it?"

Alpheas couldn't answer that. It had been happening every year since Fermi joined the graduation class.

In a battle royale, there's no particular benefit to targeting a specific person. The strangest part was that they ended up failing too.

"The graduation exam isn't just a school issue. It's a national certification exam. Tactical flow is part of it. A teacher's personal judgment can't change the criteria for passing."

Olivia scoffed.

"Do you think I'm blind? Fermi didn't even show half his skills in this exam. He's already good enough to be a professional. He could graduate from the Royal Magic School immediately. How can he not pass for six years?"

"So what should we do? Just let Fermi graduate? Then why have a graduation exam? That would only cause backlash from the students."

Frustrated, Olivia paced the room before collapsing onto the sofa.

"I don't get it. Why doesn't he graduate? What's the benefit of staying in school?"

"I asked him before. He said this place makes money."

Olivia sat up abruptly.

"That's a diploma broker! If he's taking money to help others graduate, it's not just a school issue. It's a crime against the state."

"I investigated but found nothing. Not a single penny."

Olivia rested her chin on her hand, deep in thought.

If there were no records of bribery, it would be a case of no evidence. But based on intuition, it suggested there might be something else going on.

"Fermi... he's an irregular exception, right?"

Alpheas nodded.

"Probably. I don't know how he's making money, but if he's an irregular exception, it's hard to investigate. Unless Fermi uses his magic, there's no way to uncover it. But in seven graduation exams, I've never seen him use it."

"Then shouldn't we start there? Whether he's taking money or using magic for compensation, it's the same. Honestly, Samuel's performance today wasn't great, but he passed. Amy should have taken that spot."

"I had similar thoughts. But it's just something we can say because no one else is here. Without solid evidence, it's impossible. What if we start an investigation and find nothing? Would any noble stand by if their child was accused of a crime?"

Olivia leaned back on the sofa, cooling her heated head with her palm.

"So what will you do? Do you have any ideas?"

"No. As always, we conducted the graduation exam, and the successful candidates became mages. Whether there was bribery or not, that fact doesn't change."

"But we can't say we accurately measured their abilities."

"Can we really say that?"

Olivia raised an eyebrow.

"What do you mean?"
