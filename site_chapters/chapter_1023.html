Chapter 1023: Electric Civilization (3)

After the battle with the Jakra Pirates ended, the refugee ships reorganized their formation.

Thanks to the quick capture of Jakra, Arachne's ship narrowly avoided capsizing.

“The treasure is safe,” Cairns said first.

“We owe you again. It seems Arachne's knights weren't much help.”

He avoided political statements, having already understood Shirone's disposition.

“The result is the sum of all causes. It's not a good idea to judge based on parts.”

Shirone turned around.

“By the way…”

Jakra, bound with ropes, knelt with a blank expression and his mouth agape.

Using the mental technique of confinement, Shirone had trapped Jakra's consciousness deep within his brain.

“You should think carefully. You might end up living forever in the prison of your own body.”

Jakra's eyes trembled with shock, but that was the only reaction he could show.

“It’s useless. The only person Jakra fears is his brother, Kalt.”

The King of Dionas, known as the Great Pirate.

“What kind of person is he?”

Shirone's memory of Kalt through Omega was up to when he was twenty-four, a combatant in a mid-level pirate group.

Even then, he had the heart of a snake, but compared to Jakra's madness, he seemed relatively ordinary.

“I would define him as a strange person. He calls himself an enlightened one. He lives buried in his own doctrine, making him the most diplomatically challenging type.”

“An enlightened one, huh.”

Ancient civilization, electric technology, an enlightened one.

The fragments of mystery formed a shape, but it was still a vast enigma.

“Being someone who doesn't calculate profit and loss has made dealing with Arachne difficult. Wanting Langi was perhaps fortunate. But if they act like this, it's a different story.”

Poine spoke.

“I don't know why there's a blank in the Messiah's Omega. But I can tell you that Maika is a hypothetical continent called an ultra-ancient civilization by humanity. It was said to be somewhere in the South Pacific among the Ten Kingdoms of the Archipelago, but it supposedly sank into the sea.”

“A hypothetical continent. Does that mean it couldn't be verified, even with a dragon's abilities?”

“The Omega guarded by Karator, the Silent Dragon, was only delivered to the Messiah. We couldn't access it, so we couldn't identify any transmission errors. However, when the Core was present, information exchange among the Twelve Apostles was possible. Based on that, a few clues come to mind, but Sea Dragon Kaos would know more.”

Even though they shared the same information, Poine deferred to Kaos because it was a subtle feeling that couldn't be conveyed without direct confirmation.

After resting and waiting, Kaos returned from preventing the tsunami and landed on the deck.

“Messiah, I have returned. Based on the analysis of the tectonic plate movements, the next earthquake will be in 48 hours.”

“Good job.”

Shirone recounted the battle with the pirates and the discussion about Maika's artifacts.

“So that happened. I apologize for not being there. If I had been…”

“That's enough. What I want to know is about Maika's ruins. If the continent sank, you, who travel the world's seas, must have confirmed it.”

Kaos spoke heavily.

“To get to the point, the Maika civilization is presumed to have existed.”

“Presumed?”

“Yes. The abilities of the pirate group you’re curious about are likely influenced by OOPArts. The term means out-of-place artifacts, and it's quite accurate. The chronology of the ultra-ancient civilization I investigated…”

Kaos's expression grew serious.

“It's at least 2 billion years old. In other words, it predates even the existence of dragons.”

Shirone blinked, comparing it to Omega's records.

“Then why don't I remember? And if OOPArts exist, doesn't that mean Maika was real?”

“That's the problem. While it's believed to have sunk in the South Pacific, over time, the ruins have scattered across the seas. I've confirmed over seventy sites. But these ruins share a common feature.”

“What feature?”

“There are no traces of humans or living creatures.”

A silence fell.

“What do you mean? OOPArts are…”

“Yes, they are artificial artifacts. Objects containing technologies like spatial jumps or magnetic field shields can't occur naturally. The ruins show traces of civilization. But no one existed there.”

“It’s truly strange. Creating something requires a subject. But Maika is the opposite. There are civilizations, sites, and tools, but no subject.”

“Outside causality…”

Shirone suddenly thought of fish in an aquarium.

‘Fish just swim in their world. But if a human drops a ring into the aquarium…’

Wouldn't the fish perceive the ring as an artificial artifact without a subject?

‘Even if the fish had the Omega of the aquarium, they wouldn't understand the ring.’

Kaos continued.

“The reason I lost interest in ultra-ancient civilizations is that what you see is all there is. Without a subject enjoying the civilization, no imagination can be applied.”

“What about OOPArts?”

“It's hard to say. They are tools with a clear purpose, but I couldn't figure out how to use them. In short, I couldn't grasp what they were for.”

Poine interjected.

“If the intelligence of living beings is limited by the universe, perhaps concepts not present in the universe need to be integrated. All Twelve Apostles tried but ultimately failed.”

Shirone looked at Jakra.

“But he used it, didn’t he? And how were you sure spatial jumps were an OOPArts ability?”

“Because of electricity.”

Kaos said.

“Objects without a subject can only be interpreted as they are. The only conclusion I reached after obtaining OOPArts was that they emitted a faint electric current.”

Shirone listened again.

“It was astonishing. A 2-billion-year-old object still holding a charge. So I tried to delve deeper into that part, but…”

“But?”

“It disappeared.”

“What? The OOPArts?”

“I don't know. It could be the OOPArts, or it could be my memory.”

Poine's eyes darkened.

“Messiah, we don't know about OOPArts. We only know they are tools using electricity. When we tried to go further, we found that the mental network of dragons suffered severe damage.”

Kaos continued.

“We suspect a strong magnetic field. While the OOPArts might have truly disappeared, it felt very alien. As if it never existed, a phenomenon where the brain rejects the sensation.”

Poine spoke.

“After repeated incidents, Karator forbade further investigation of OOPArts. It could harm the Omega logs he guards, and for the Apostle of Time, who protects human history, a 2-billion-year-old artifact isn't that important.”

Shirone asked.

“What do they look like? The OOPArts?”

“They vary in form but aren't particularly special. Some look like cubes, others like trays. Of course, their uses are likely very different from what we think.”

“Hmm, can we get one?”

“Why?”

“Dragons couldn't figure it out, but maybe human insight could.”

Shirone knew someone whose insight into forms was at the pinnacle of humanity.

‘Arin.’

Her unbiased perception penetrates the true nature of all forms.

‘She’s probably exploring archaeological sites with Kanis. I need to find their location.’

Through simultaneous events, Shirone asked Lampa about Kanis's whereabouts and continued.

“Anyway, Jakra used OOPArts. Even without knowing its function.”

Poine looked at Jakra.

“Why not ask him directly? Leave it to me, and I'll make him talk in two hours.”

Shirone looked troubled.

“He seems genuinely unaware. Confinement is a terrifying state for humans. We'll reach Dionas tomorrow, so it might be faster to ask Kalt.”

Poine smiled kindly.

“Let's give it a try.”

That night.

“Ahhh! Ahhh!”

Piercing screams echoed across Arachne's ship.

As the promised two hours neared, the first human words were heard.

“I don’t know! I really don’t know!”

Poine grabbed Jakra's sweat-drenched head.

“Hoho, I see. You don’t know. Then let's start over from there. This time…”

Jakra burst into tears.

“I really don’t know! My brother knows! I swear! I don’t even remember!”

“Don’t belittle yourself. You’re smart. You’ll remember soon.”

“It’s true… He put something in me. My brother put something in my head. I don’t remember anything after that. I just don’t know! Damn it!”

Poine's gaze turned cold.

“It seems to be true.”

If, as Shirone thought, the magnetic field of OOPArts affected humans…

“Try to endure for another two hours. If you can still say the same thing, I’ll believe you.”

“No, wait. You crazy… Ahhh!”

The screams that had filled the evening ceased, and as everyone fell into a deep sleep, dawn arrived.

Shirone stood alone on the deck, gazing at the stars.

‘What is there?’

Or perhaps the answer humanity seeks is fundamentally different.

‘Why is it there?’

He sensed someone approaching.

Turning, he saw Langi, still damp, approaching in a robe.

“Langi, you’re not asleep?”

“No, I woke up. Had breakfast, exercised, and just finished washing up.”

It was 5 a.m. by the stars.

“There’s something important in Dionas, right? That’s why you’re negotiating with the king.”

Shirone remained silent.

“I’ll go to Kalt. I didn’t feel good about it, but now I’m okay. If I can help you, Shirone…”

Langi frowned.

“What’s with that smile? I’m serious. I’m going for you, Shirone.”

“There’s no need to worry. Nothing will happen to you, Langi.”

Shirone looked at the horizon as dawn broke.

‘It doesn’t matter what it is.’

Whatever lies beyond the sea, what matters is doing what can be done in this moment.

‘Only that…’

is the reason we exist.

“Look over there.”

Langi pointed with a blissful expression at the shimmering golden land in the distance.

The land of pirates, Dionas.
