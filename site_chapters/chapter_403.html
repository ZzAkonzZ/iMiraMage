Chapter 403:

After completing the omniscient implementation evaluation, Shirone headed to the cafeteria with his friends.

Although he scored full marks, there were quite a few uneasy moments, perhaps because his mind was unsettled.

His friends had already noticed, and the atmosphere at the table was different from before, more subdued.

Shirone wasn't in the right state of mind to care about others' feelings.

When would Gaold contact him? Even if he did, what choices could he make?

Everything felt uncertain.

"Shirone, is something bothering you lately? You don't look well. Are you sick?" Amy asked, putting down her utensils.

Even if it wasn't just Shirone, all the students seemed down, but today his face was especially pale.

"I'm fine. Just not feeling well."

"Sometimes, before midterms, some students can't manage their condition. I was like that last year. Just hang in there a bit longer. The holidays are coming soon."

Shirone forced a smile.

Amy, having already experienced the final year, was probably worried about her own wavering as they approached the 15-week mark.

"Shirone, here's some water."

Maya poured water and handed it to Shirone.

Seeing him barely eating and just playing with his fork was pitiful.

"Thanks."

Shirone accepted Maya's kindness.

He usually measured the distance in relationships before showing emotions, so his attitude towards Maya was unusual.

Amy pouted and watched Shirone and Maya exchange words.

Maya was kind and cheerful. It wasn't bad for Shirone to get close to her.

But she felt strangely uneasy.

Was it the feeling of losing her place, something she hadn't even realized she had?

Shirone noticed Maya's tray and asked, "Huh? Maya, why aren't you eating?"

"I already ate. I don't eat as much as I used to these days."

Since the end of the high ground capture evaluation, Maya had reduced her food intake.

To be precise, she had returned to the amount her body originally required.

Realizing she had no talent for singing, Maya decided to strengthen her sound magic with her natural voice, following Iruki's advice.

Becoming a mage, her primary goal, was necessary for her future.

But more important than her career was the desire filling her heart.

She wanted Shirone's love. She wanted to appear before him more beautifully.

Even just by not overeating, Maya's appearance had noticeably improved.

Her face had slimmed, making her eyes look bigger and deeper, and her nose more defined.

Her once plump body had slimmed down, making her clothes loose, and her shoulders had dropped to her arms.

Even the senior students couldn't openly ignore Maya anymore.

It was a bittersweet reality, but the human tendency to grant power to beauty was undeniable.

Nade and Iruki were also amazed at Maya's changing appearance day by day.

"She's changed a lot. She's starting to look like the face I saw in the picture book."

The strange tension grew stronger.

Amy was always sharp, but Maya was poor at hiding her feelings, so everyone knew she had fallen for Shirone.

Amy suddenly raised her voice.

"Can you eat right now? Even today's omniscient evaluation was like that. You almost got one question wrong. Class Two is competing with Class One. If you make a mistake in the first half, it will be harder in the second half when your stamina drops. Get your head straight."

Amy, who had already failed the graduation exam once, knew this was the most important time.

Even if everyone was discouraged by the Magic Association incident, they had to have a clear sense of purpose.

Skipping a meal wouldn't kill anyone.

If they didn't stay focused now, they would miss the most significant part of their lives.

Amy didn't want Shirone to regret it.

"Shirone, try this too."

Maya cut the meat Shirone liked into bite-sized pieces and placed it in front of him.

It was an action that seemed to block out the nagging, and it was indeed a bit emotional.

Being close as a team, Amy wondered if she truly loved Shirone.

Of course, Maya also wanted Shirone to become a great mage, but she thought what he needed was comfort, not advice, when he was stressed to the point of having nightmares.

"You're too much, Amy. If I were you, I'd be so happy I'd go crazy. I could do anything Shirone asked, but you treat him like this."

Shirone picked up the meat Maya had given him and put it in his mouth.

He chose it because it was in the best condition to eat, but he didn't even know who had placed it there.

His head felt like it was going to explode with all the thoughts tangled up.

Had Gaold met with Alpheas?

If he got caught by the National Intelligence Service, continuing the evaluation could be dangerous.

Just as a navigation system doesn't guarantee a student's safety, entrusting his life to someone else and being optimistic was foolish.

Shirone put down his utensils with a clatter.

Nade and Iruki flinched and turned around, sensing the tension between Amy and Maya.

"Sorry. I'm going back to the dorm first. I'm not feeling well."

Maya immediately stood up, but Shirone left the cafeteria without making eye contact with anyone.

Amy felt frustrated.

She knew Shirone had gone to the association. She wanted him to tell her first.

Like when he went to Heaven, like when he went to Kazra, like when he went to the old castle ruins, she wanted him to come to her first.

"What's the reason? Why aren't you saying anything this time?"

Maya bit her upper lip and looked at Amy as Shirone left.

She had no reason to hate Amy. What made her angry was the current situation where she had to get Amy's permission even for her own feelings.

Feeling Maya's gaze, Amy looked up. Then she quickly turned her head away.

Seeing the firm determination in Maya's eyes, Amy finally realized the inevitable had come.

"Amy, can we talk for a moment?"

Amy's heart sank.

She thought it couldn't be, but deep down, she knew this moment would come, but when it did, she thought, "Just a little later."

"Can we do it later? I'm a bit busy today..."

"Just one minute. I'll wait outside."

As Maya walked away with her tray, Amy's mind went blank. But eventually, she stood up with a shred of rationality, knowing the future was coming.

Nade and Iruki shrugged as they watched Amy follow Maya as if in a trance.

The book "Seeing the Darkness" tilted at a 45-degree angle and automatically slid into the bookshelf in the principal's office library.

With the sound of gears turning, the lock clicked shut inside the closed library.

A secret place designed long ago by Alpheas to store Erina's memories.

But now all her relics had been moved to the annex, and the room on the 3.5th floor was empty.

Alpheas and Olivia descended the stairs side by side.

Unlike Alpheas, who had a bitter smile, Olivia's expression was fierce. She even seemed tense.

"Hehe, it's been a while, old man."

Gaold, sitting cross-legged on the floor, raised his hand in greeting. Flue was standing next to him.

"Hello. I'm Flue, a certified 8th-level mage."

"It's been a while, Flue."

At Olivia's greeting, Flue lowered her gaze.

She had no shame in front of the sky, but socially, she had become a traitor, so she couldn't face his teacher.

"She was a brilliant child, but why did she end up with someone like Gaold..."

She had been a child Alpheas had kept an eye on since her days as the principal of the Royal Magic School.

But now her bright future was blocked. No, she was a fugitive who couldn't even guarantee her life tomorrow.

All because of Gaold.

How many people had one madman exhausted and how much chaos had he caused in society?

Gaold looked around with an amused expression.

"So this is the panic room that was just a rumor. I didn't think it actually existed. You're quite sly, old man."

Alpheas skipped the small talk.

"Why have you come to see me?"

"Well... Is it too much to ask for a warm welcome for a disciple visiting his alma mater?"

"Because of you, the school has been turned upside down. Agents come every day and ransack the school. If you had come a few days earlier, everyone would have been affected."

"Hehe, even the great Alpheas has changed. The kids here probably don't even know the agents came and went, and if there were still agents, they wouldn't have let me in."

"You know well. So I'll ask again. Why have you come to see me?"

Gaold sighed.

If he had come to make a difficult request, he should have lowered his stance, but he couldn't feel any affection for this school.

"I'm thinking of entering Istas."

"So, you want to meet Miro?"

"Hehe, did you think I came to retrieve something I left behind?"

"Gaold, your actions are causing chaos in the world. Miro doesn't want to see you anymore. She accepted everything and went in on her own."

"How do you know that?"

Gaold's eyes widened.

Sensing the extreme restraint, Flue squinted one eye, but Gaold's face only changed to a fierce expression without emitting any killing intent.

"You know how many people I've sent to their graves trying to approach Istas. Not a single person has entered in 20 years, including me. So how do you know Miro's thoughts?"

"Gaold, Miro..."

"Enough. I'll enter Istas myself. After all, we created it. Actually, I have one more favor to ask."

Alpheas asked in a resigned voice, "Alright, what is it?"

"As you know, I've lost all my limbs. The association captured everyone. Although the key figures are still intact, I can't do anything with just my head. Lend me some people. Competent ones."

No matter how outstanding Gaold was, it was impossible for him to carry out all operations alone.

He needed someone with a powerful strike, someone to devise tactics, someone to execute them, and someone to support them—a minimum party was necessary.

"There's a bishop from the Karsis Monastery at this school."

Olivia burst out, "Do you think I'll send valuable school personnel to their deaths? And what you're planning goes against the survival of humanity. Isn't it enough that you're a traitor? Now you want to throw the world into chaos?"

"Shut up."

For the first time, Gaold looked Olivia directly in the eye since entering the room.

His restraint didn't explode, but the energy from his gaze alone seemed capable of piercing her body.

"Do you know why I haven't killed you yet? Huh? Do you? Answer me."

Olivia clenched her mouth shut and glared fiercely.

"Because you're not worth killing."

Gaold's teeth ground audibly.

"Abstain? A member of the faculty council, letting a student of the kingdom be dragged into the dimension of terror, and you abstain? Do you know what people like you are called? Hypocrites. You don't like this, you don't like that, holding one of the twenty cards that can determine humanity's survival, and you choose to abstain?"

Olivia's lips trembled. Her gaze, which had been fixed on Gaold, slowly lowered.

"It was... unavoidable."

No matter what she chose, it wouldn't have been the result Olivia wanted. No, in fact, that thought was just an excuse.

She was simply scared. She was so terrified that she couldn't choose anything.

Transparent tears welled up in Olivia's eyes, vividly recalling the helplessness of that time.

Gaold turned away with a grim expression.

Recognized as a second-class archmage by the world, but unable to shed the shell of a woman. That's why she was only a second-class archmage.

'This is why I didn't want to come to this school.'

This place was full of the past.

No matter how much he tried to erase it, the past couldn't be erased.

Gaold couldn't remember what happened an hour ago, but he could vividly recall the scene on the day of the Judgment of the Twenty, down to the antennae of the bug on the leaf.

"Sigh."

Gaold looked back at Alpheas.

It was probably the first time anyone had seen such a deeply human expression on his face.

"Please, help me, Master."

"......"

There was a day known as the Judgment of the Twenty.

On that day, twenty people representing the world and Miro's position gathered to decide the future of humanity.

The result was 16 votes in favor, 1 against, and 3 abstentions.

The only person who valued one life more than the lives of all humanity was Mirhi Alpheas.

The only one who stood by Gaold's side, he willingly marked his card with a vote against.

"You know. I don't trust anyone but you, Master. Help me. We need to find Miro. She's not dead. She's still alive over there, so why won't you look for her?"

Alpheas was lost in thought.

Perhaps Olivia's choice during the Judgment of the Twenty was the most rational decision that good could make.

But he knew the feeling of losing someone precious right before his eyes, someone irreplaceable.

It wasn't a choice made for the world. Alpheas was also an inevitable madman.

"I'll ask Etella..."

Etella would probably grant permission.

A bishop of the Karsis Monastery and the previous successor of the Yin-Yang Wave Fist.

If he hadn't lowered his body and mind with the belief of One Thought Three Thousand, he wouldn't have the talent to be in his current position.

He was a suitable candidate for Gaold to use.

"Stay here for now. Olivia and I will handle the agents. Let's prepare to open Istas."

As Alpheas turned with a weary face, Gaold finally regained his smile.

Then, as if he had just remembered something, he raised his hand.

"Oh, wait a moment."

Alpheas turned back at the stairs.

"Since I'm asking, could you recruit one more person?"
