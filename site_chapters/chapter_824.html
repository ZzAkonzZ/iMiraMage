Chapter 824: Smile, Smile (4)

As Rian ran somewhere, losing consciousness, he suddenly wondered, 'What am I angry about?'

He saw a building rapidly approaching.

He saw it, but his thoughts were filled with something that wasn't his experience...

'Or is it my experience?' Every time someone's memory passed by, vivid stimuli were transmitted at the cellular level.

Without even thinking about breaking through the obstructing object, Rian collided head-on while running.

'Where am I going?'

According to Nike's memories, the Genocide team was captured and taken to the mansion of the tycoon Miguel.

Jinma Faust, the Elixir of Life, the Vampire Lord, and another Lord who had already awakened.

"Xenia."

The moment he uttered her name, a hallucination exploded as if it had come from the end of the universe.

"Smile."

Bang! Bang bang!

The walls crumbled like cookies, and the mansion of the tycoon Miguel appeared before his eyes.

At that moment, his thoughts leaped back ten thousand years, placing him in some location in heaven.

'Ogent.'

A long time ago, there lived a timid, self-centered, and irritable swordsman.

He didn't save the world or cause any significant events that symbolized a particular era.

'What am I angry about?'

The beings of heaven, who endured more than ten thousand years, recalled that swordsman this way.

Yacha.

Late at night, Ogent entered the training hall where his tribe practiced swordsmanship.

"Hoo. Hoo."

Every time he exhaled roughly, the candles illuminating the surroundings flickered as if they would go out.

'Endure. Just endure.'

What is anger?

It felt like the flames rising from his heart melted his mind and body, mixing them like molten metal in a furnace.

"Ah...!"

His tightly clenched teeth naturally parted, and he wanted to scream with all his might.

'Swordsmanship.'

Ogent needed a place to escape.

In the hell of his mind, blocked by flames on all sides, there had to be an escape route.

'Become the best.'

No one said it, but it felt like if he reached the pinnacle of swordsmanship, Smile would come to him.

'Thinking such delusions is why Smile calls me creepy.'

But it didn't matter.

'Take ten steps. Then it'll be okay.'

Ogent measured ten steps ahead with his eyes and slowly moved forward.

Although he actually moved, his feet didn't leave the ground for ten minutes.

'Slowly. Extremely slowly.'

To an observer, it would seem like he was just standing still, but Ogent was sensing countless movements throughout his body.

'Endlessly disassembling.'

His nerves were so finely divided that he could feel even the particles of air on his skin.

'It's the universe.'

Only what was contained in his body was his, and thus he was infinitely free.

"Brother?"

Smile peeked through the door of the training hall.

'Smile.'

His keen senses detected her, but the immense speed of his racing nerves swept her away to the edge of oblivion.

It was a familiar occurrence, so Smile sat in a corner of the training hall and watched her brother.

'What kind of training is that?'

Unlike the tribe's swordsmen, Ogent's training was so tedious that she often got bored and left.

But today, she had something to say, so she waited patiently.

In less than 30 minutes, she felt sleepy and soon dozed off, her head nodding.

"Huh?"

When she woke up, she heard the birds announcing dawn outside the training hall.

As her broken memories reconnected, she realized Ogent was no longer there.

"Wow."

She turned her head and saw Ogent standing with his legs together, having moved ten steps during the night.

"Brother."

She thought he was just standing, but Ogent was moving to achieve perfect stillness.

'Stop.'

He hadn't stopped once while taking ten steps, so his acceleration was beyond human measurement.

'Stop.'

Controlling it with his body was as difficult as feeling the speed of light.

As the candle flame was pressed down and extinguished, Smile swallowed hard.

Perfect stillness.

Her brain couldn't analyze it, but her eyes clearly saw it.

"Smile."

Ogent turned his head.

"What's the matter?"

Smile, who had been staring blankly, came to her senses and stood up.

"Oh, I have something to tell you, Brother." She knew what she wanted to say and what she wanted to hear.

"It's morning now. Let's talk later."

He couldn't let Smile go, nor could he hold onto her, and he felt pathetic.

"Fine. But if you don't say it now, promise you'll never say it."

As he turned, Smile looked at him with unusually angry eyes.

"You have something to tell me, right? The Mountain Tribe people will be here soon. If you don't say it now, you might regret it forever."

She was probably right.

"I don't know why you've been so cold to me lately. It started after the marriage with the Mountain Tribe was arranged."

"Smile, I..."

"Do you not want me to get married?"

His heart sank.

"Of course not. The River Tribe and the Mountain Tribe have cooperated for generations. This is an important marriage."

"I'm talking about you, Brother. You have a lot of complaints about this marriage. Should I guess?"

Ogent's face turned pale.

"Brother, could it be..."

Just as he felt dizzy with tension, Smile put her hand on her waist and said,

"You're upset because you won't have anyone to play with if I get married?"

"What?"

His cold sweat dried quickly.

"You lack social skills and have no friends. That's why you're leaving."

The vanished tension was replaced by disappointment.

'What was I expecting?'

Did he think there would be a powerful blow to break the precarious balance?

"That's not it."

As he tried to speak through his rough breathing, Damian opened the door to the training hall.

"Here you are."

Ogent felt guilty.

"Oh? Father? You're up already?"

Damian, who had been watching the siblings with complex emotions, sighed and turned away.

"The Mountain Tribe people will arrive soon. We need to prepare for the pre-wedding ceremony, so come home quickly."

As Smile watched Damian walk away, she looked at Ogent and waited for a moment.

Her eyes said to speak quickly if he had something to say, but hearing Damian's sigh, Ogent just kept his mouth shut.

"I'll go in."

Smile left, and Ogent closed the training hall door and closed his eyes.

'Slowly. Even slower.'

If he became the best, if there was no higher place to go, it felt like Smile would miraculously understand his feelings.

'Become stronger. That's all I need to do.'

It was the only way Ogent could fight the world.

The Mountain Tribe arrived.

There was no superiority between tribes, but it was a traditional ceremony for the groom's family to stay with the bride's family for seven days to foster harmony. They frequently exchanged goods between the river and the mountains, so most were familiar with each other.

But Ogent, who had always been secluded in the mountains training, met Smile's fiancé, Lead, for the first time.

A large man with a handsome face.

His hearty laughter with strangers was typical of the Mountain Tribe.

'What's so great about this guy?'

If he had been terrible, he wouldn't have said anything, but Ogent blamed everything on political reasons.

'Smile is being sacrificed.'

Smile, with her calm and broad mind like a great river, wasn't shaken by any major event.

It was something Ogent could never have, and perhaps he revered her for it.

'This marriage is ridiculous.'

Seeing Smile smiling while talking to Lead made him even more upset.

"By the way, where is your brother?"

Looking around, Lead noticed Ogent frowning and approached him.

"You must be Smile's brother I've heard so much about. Nice to meet you. I'm Lead from the Mountain Tribe."

He wanted to slap away the extended hand, but he couldn't take a single step out of line in front of the tribe.

"Nice to meet you."

"I heard you're the best warrior. Let's have a match sometime."

Ogent replied coldly.

"I don't think that will happen. I'm leaving on a long journey soon."

"A journey? Are you going to purgatory?"

"Excuse me."

Lead looked around at the River Tribe with a puzzled expression, but they didn't seem to care.

"What's with him? He's supposed to be her brother."

Lead defined Ogent this way.

"He's a complete fool."

Except for Smile's cold brother, the pre-wedding ceremony was harmonious.

With abundant food from the mountains and the river, the drinks flowed, and everyone was pleasantly drunk by early evening.

"Ha ha ha! So I took down three mountain kings at once!"

As the father sending off his daughter, Damian enthusiastically supported Lead's tales of valor.

"That's impressive. As expected of the strongest warrior of the Mountain Tribe."

"That's true, but..."

Lead, who had been drinking heavily, turned his gaze to Ogent, sitting at the end of the table.

Whether he was listening or not, Ogent sat there sulking like a pouting girl, staring at the ground.

'What an unpleasant personality. Well, he's not even a real son...'

If he was going to purgatory anyway, it might be fun to teach him the true charm of a man.

"Have you ever caught a mountain king, Brother?"

When Ogent didn't answer, Smile intervened.

"My brother is really strong. He's a genius. A few days ago, he cut down dozens of sea creatures."

"Ha ha ha! Smile, you're praising your brother too much. It's impossible to catch dozens of sea creatures underwater."

No human could do that.

"It's true. Right, brother? The thing you showed me while we were swimming. The technique to fly in the sky."

"Fly in the sky?"

Since no one had reached that level, not only the mountain clan but also the river clan paid attention.

"Is there really such a technique?"

Ozent couldn't help but respond.

"What would you do if you knew?"

But as expected, his words were cold, and the atmosphere quickly cooled down.

"Brother, from earlier, what exactly..."

Leard lifted his head and burst into laughter.

"Hahaha! It seems we've got a formidable brother here. Well, it's still better than a coward."

"Coward?"

Ozent glared at him, but Leard smoothly brushed it off.

"There's such a thing. By the way, if you have such a cool technique, why not teach it to me as well?"

Without waiting for an answer, Leard stood up, grabbed his wooden sword, and walked to the open space.

"Surely, the brave river clan isn't running away, right?"

As he struck a blow to the clan's pride, Ozent finally realized his mistake.

'Damn it.' He slowly stood up, gripping his wooden sword, when Damian put down his glass and spoke.

"Ozent, come see me for a moment." While everyone was waiting for the duel of the century, Damian spoke in the shade.

"He is the one who will spend his life with Smile."

"I know. I'm sorry. I'll apologize personally and make it as if it never happened."

"Just don't kill him."

"What?"

Ozent looked up in disbelief, and Damian patted his shoulder.

"Feel free to beat him up as much as you want. Until your anger is gone, until there's nothing left in your heart."

It was the only consideration he could give his son.
