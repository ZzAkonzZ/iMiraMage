Chapter 692: Reunion Scene (1)

“Wait. I have something to say.”

“Let’s talk later. I’m a bit busy right now.”

“I love you, Aria.”

“Huh? What did you say?”

“Let’s get married. I want to share a future with you. Have children and everything.”

Aria stared blankly at Brooks, who was standing with his arms wide open, then coldly turned away.

“Focus on raising the child you already have, human.”

“…….”

With a thud, the door closed.

***

The incident where the masters of the three major magic guilds of the Tormia Kingdom died simultaneously was the talk of the capital.

After disbanding the mercenary group and submitting reports to their respective guilds, Shirone was finally able to set off on his next journey.

“Whew, this is exhausting. Putting a team together is no easy task.”

Kido, his face hidden under a deeply pulled hood, chewed on street food as he spoke.

“Humans are a species that attaches too much meaning to death. Honestly, once you’re dead, you’re just food.”

Shirone glanced at the lamb skewer in Kido’s hand.

“Does eating that bring back memories?”

“No. Cooking it over fire erases the memories. It’s not like a curse or anything. Just digesting the memories stored in the cells of a living being.”

Rian looked at Kido.

“Isn’t that basically a curse?”

Kido swallowed the skewer in one bite and licked his fingers.

“What does it matter? Anyway, where are we headed next? We don’t even know where Ra Enemi is hiding.”

“I’ve got an idea. But before that, let’s make a quick stop. There’s someone I really want to meet. I also have a favor to ask.”

“Huh? Who is it?”

Shirone smiled and pointed toward the royal palace.

“The Mercodain family.”

***

The head of Yongrae (Dragon Thunder) has the duty to provide the king with the clearest and most rational answers.

The types of questions range from the metaphysical to the physical, varying endlessly.

While questions about the secrets of the universe are troublesome, Albino once said the most difficult question to answer is:

“What should I have for lunch today?”

This anecdote reveals just how much the king of a nation relies on the head of Yongrae.

Thus, it’s no surprise that the Mercodain family’s residence is located within the royal administrative district, unlike other nobles.

“Hmm, so the head of Yongrae lives here?”

Kido, who had heard the name “Yongrae” countless times since his days as the leader of Speedkiller, asked.

“Not the ‘head,’ but the ‘leader.’ But it seems the repairs still aren’t finished. How badly was it destroyed?”

Rian, towering over the others, peeked inside to see dozens of workers busy with construction.

“This is the royal administrative district, right? Was there a civil war or something?”

“Haha! No. Iruki blew it up. That spell he used at the end of his graduation exam.”

Arganes, Iruki’s mother, hurried across the garden after receiving a report from the butler.

“Welcome, Shirone.”

“Hello. You’re as beautiful as ever.”

Arganes covered her face and twisted her body shyly.

“Oh my, such words are embarrassing. My husband is on duty right now and won’t be back until very late.”

Shirone smiled.

“I don’t think that matters much.”

“Hoho! True. Iruki also works for Yongrae now. Like his father, he barely comes home.”

It was easy to picture.

“Could I ask for a Yongrae access pass? I need to meet Iruki and discuss some things.”

Though Yongrae wasn’t a place one could visit just to chat with a friend, Shirone’s involvement in the Radum incident provided sufficient professional justification.

“I’ll write you a pass for the inner palace. Iruki is probably waiting for you too. The times are unstable these days.”

As the lady of the Mercodain household, she couldn’t avoid hearing classified information, no matter how much she disliked it.

“Thank you.”

With the inner palace pass in hand, Shirone and his group crossed the threshold into the heart of the royal palace.

The beautiful garden was dotted with buildings for various departments, and the people moving about were high-ranking nobles.

Shirone spotted his friend sitting on a bench in the garden, staring blankly at the sky with messy hair.

“Iruki!”

In the quiet street, Shirone’s cheerful call made Iruki pause mid-sip of his tea and turn his head.

“Over here!”

His vision must have been blurry, as he couldn’t spot Shirone until he waved his hand to indicate his location.

Iruki, who had been craning his neck like a turtle, finally smiled and raised his teacup.

“Hey, Shirone.”

***

Mercodain Iruki (20 years old).

A junior researcher in Yongrae, the king’s advisory agency.

Though he had assumed his friends would remain unchanged, reuniting with them in the real world filled him with indescribable emotion.

“I’ve been waiting. You’re an hour early. Come in.”

Shirone tilted his head in confusion, but fully understanding Iruki was always a futile endeavor.

As Iruki strode down the hallway as if it were his own home, soldiers and officials alike saluted him respectfully.

“Yongrae really is something. You’ve made it big, huh?”

“Haha.”

Iruki’s eyes widened as if he’d heard a terrifying joke, and he let out a dry laugh.

After leading the group to his research lab, Iruki served tea and stared intently at Kido’s face.

“By the way, who’s this? No offense, but has anyone ever told you you look like a goblin?”

Kido shot back at the lame joke.

“I am a goblin! Are you my long-lost half-brother or something?”

Shirone introduced him properly.

“This is Kido. He’ll be traveling with us.”

“Ah, Kido the Devourer. Leader of Speedkiller. Ranked 179th in Radum’s political hierarchy, 7th in combat power, with the special ability ‘Taste of Memory.’”

Shirone was surprised by Iruki’s Yongrae-like demeanor, but Kido seemed unimpressed.

“My combat rank is only 7th? There wasn’t a single non-human who could beat me except for Draker. Humans really have terrible intel.”

“According to that ‘terrible intel,’ you’re now ranked 1st.”

The higher-ranked executives of Spectrum had all reportedly died.

Kido, with nothing left to say, made it obvious he disliked Iruki, but Shirone felt oddly relieved.

“You haven’t changed. How have you been?”

A sigh heavy enough to sink the earth followed.

“Predictions, just predictions. My job is to collect data from the palace, analyze it, and write prediction reports for 1-hour, 12-hour, and 24-hour intervals. For example, right now, Madeline will come in and…”

The moment he finished speaking, a maid opened the door and entered.

“Iruki, there’s an emergency meeting for junior researchers.”

“Got it. I’ll be there on time.”

After Madeline left, Iruki pointed at the closed door.

“See? That’s how it is. She has a 64.8% chance of getting a stomachache within 72 hours. There was a water contamination incident in the palace’s ice supply yesterday, and low-quality ice is distributed to lower ranks first. Plus, today’s dessert is fruit shaved ice. And according to my data analysis, she’s only skipped fruit shaved ice twice in five years!”

Iruki slammed the table, and an awkward silence filled the room.

“……You’ve got it rough.”

Iruki, now slumped on the sofa, covered his face with his hands.

“It’s not as simple as that. I had to check Madeline’s medical records, water supply reports, medical encyclopedias, and personal observation logs. Ah, why did we endure such grueling training in magic school? Big data this, big data that—I’m so tired I could die.”

Though it seemed like pointless analysis, all information begins with small details.

This was also part of the training for Yongrae members, and as one’s rank rose, so did the quality of the information they handled.

The pinnacle of this system was Iruki’s father.

Judging by Iruki’s workload, the sheer volume and quality of information Albino Mercodain handled was beyond imagination.

“Hang in there, Iruki.”

Iruki, who knew exactly what Shirone meant, propped his chin on his hand and pouted.

“Well, things will get better eventually. I’m not the only one suffering. At least I’m better off than Neid.”

“Speaking of which, how is Neid?”

“He’s working for an alchemy guild in the provinces. Apparently, he’s in the transport team, and it’s brutal.”

“But didn’t he say he was going to open a shop?”

“He is, but he’s short on funds for materials. He and Riz are saving up to start small.”

“If it’s about money, why not just…”

Though Shirone disliked the idea, given Neid’s skills, there were plenty of brokers willing to pay a fortune to hire him.

“You know how he is. He won’t use magic. I even offered to lend him money, but he said he needs to learn the basics if he wants to become a merchant. Not wrong, I guess. I’m doing the same now.”

“I see. Everyone’s working hard.”

Seeing his friends giving their all was the very value of humanity Shirone had risked his life to protect.

“How about you? I read the Radum report in my father’s study, but is there any chance of passing the test with this?”

“I’m not sure. That’s why I came to you for advice. I also wanted to tell you what happened.”

Shirone briefly explained the events in Radum, with additional context from Rian and Kido.

“Hmm, so Ra Enemi is gone. Definitely a gray area.”

The test’s passing condition was to find Ra Enemi and safely reach the Ivory Tower.

But the Ra Shirone met was part of a past event, and now even that place had vanished.

“Alright, I’ll solve this for you. Why not just ask the person involved directly?”

“Huh? The person involved?”

Iruki stood up and unlocked a secret safe under his desk.

“What are you doing? I’m getting really nervous.”

“Did you think I was just researching stomachaches? The best part of being in Yongrae is ‘borrowing’ top-secret files at will. Let’s see, calculating the trajectory…”

Leaky buckets in school were nothing compared to the leaks in the real world.

“I don’t know what you’re thinking, but is this really okay? It’s a violation of the kingdom’s security laws.”

“For civilians, yes. For colleagues, it’s just file sharing. Worst case, I get reprimanded for overstepping. Alright, calculations done. Let’s go.”

As Iruki put on his coat and stepped into the hallway, Madeline, holding fruit shaved ice, jumped in surprise.

“Oh my! Where are you going? The meeting is about to…”

“Something came up. Tell them I won’t be attending.”

“Junior researchers don’t have the authority to skip meetings!”

“Make something up. Tell them my mother is giving birth to my second sibling and I have to go in my father’s place.”

“What?”

As Madeline stood frozen, Iruki pointed at the shaved ice.

“Throw that away and eat mine instead.”

***

By the time Iruki left the research lab, the various departments of the Tormia royal palace were facing a crisis.

“They’ve entered through the outer gate!”

A first-level alert was issued, and the royal guards, fully armed, secured the grand hall like an iron fortress.

King Adolf of Tormia sat on the throne, but unusually, the queen was absent—likely to preserve the kingdom’s dignity.

“They’ve breached the inner palace!”

The reports of the intruders’ swift advance made Adolf’s face darken with anger.

“Your Majesty, should we activate the magic suppression device?”

“Don’t. No need to provoke them. And anyway…”

He couldn’t bring himself to say it likely wouldn’t work.

“They’ve reached the palace!”

As the report came in, a woman’s voice, amplified by sound magic, echoed through the halls.

“Don’t look at my panties!”

“……Huh?”

The ministers, frowning at the noise, all turned toward the grand hall’s entrance.

“Avert your eyes! Control your optic nerves!”

A dark-skinned girl strode down the hallway, holding the Ivory Tower’s emblem in one hand and lifting her short skirt with the other.

Behind her was a towering dark-skinned man wearing sunglasses and carrying a massive demon-slaying sword.

“Don’t look at my panties!”

Maids and high-ranking officials alike bowed deeply and stepped aside. Suddenly, the girl stopped.

The man crouched, stretching one leg forward and adjusting his sunglasses with a finger.

“You! You just saw my panties, didn’t you?”

“N-No! I didn’t! I would never…!”

“You imagined it, didn’t you? Don’t think at all! Or I’ll tear out your brain before you can blink!”

The deafening voice shook the hallway, leaving the official feeling like his eardrums had burst.

‘Damn it! If you don’t want people to see, just don’t show them!’

What kind of logic was it to flaunt her panties while demanding people not look?

“If I say don’t do it, then don’t! Understood?”

“Y-Yes! Understood!”

The girl coldly turned and kicked open the door to the grand hall.

“King Adolf, bow before me! The Ivory Tower’s System Inspection Department has arrived to investigate the truth of this incident!”

The guards drew their weapons, but King Adolf slowly raised his hand to stop them.

“I am King Adolf.”

The girl snorted and thrust the star emblem forward.

“I am Tszoi, a 2nd-class resident of the Ivory Tower.”

Her alias was Black Harmony.

“And this is…”

Tszoi pointed her thumb at the man, who crouched again, stretching one leg and pressing his sunglasses with his middle finger.

“…….”

Tszoi’s satellite, Kal Musso (Dog? Pig).
