Chapter 977: Hell of The Demon World (3)

"By the way..."

Tess, in a drunken state, asked,

"What's the world situation like? The simultaneous events, I mean. After all, you're not just here, but in other places too, right?"

"That's right."

Many things were happening all over the world, but Shirone's answer ended there.

Iruki asked,

"Is ignorance bliss?"

"Not necessarily... but what would change if we knew? Right now, these people need rest, and talking about unnecessary things would only add to their fatigue."

Amy said,

"Yeah, if you don't want to talk about it, we won't listen. This is normal, after all."

Forcing them to process unmanageable information would be nothing more than a mental attack.

'But also...'

Shirone looked out the window into the darkness.

'This is the reality happening in this world.'

The Gustav Empire, Havitz's homeland, was the country with the second-largest surviving population after Jincheon.

It was natural since it was the country that started the war, but now that Havitz had become even more distant from humanity, Gustav could not escape the demons' attacks either.

"Messiah, it's over there."

Although it was still daytime, the landscape in the snowstorm was as dark as night.

Beyond the curtain of snow, only the flickering lights of the castle, like ghosts, indicated the direction.

"Masarba."

It was a strategic stronghold and a large city that had to be passed before reaching the capital of Gustav.

Wearing a gray coat with a hood, Shirone exhaled white breath as he glared at the city.

The sounds of battle between humans and demons mixed with the howling wind.

"Let's go."

As Shirone moved forward, the smoke of the Miracle Stream extended thin branches ahead, transforming into a giant hand that swept the snowstorm aside.

"Are you sure it's here?"

The light dragon Phaeton and the black dragon Abiris, who were following behind, looked at each other before answering.

Abiris answered,

"Yes. After surveying the entire Gustav Empire, we obtained information that the 6th Legion Commander of the Hell Army, Siri, is here."

Her face, resembling a black panther, contrasted with the snow, exuding an eerie presence.

"Subduing Siri won't be particularly difficult, but I can't guarantee we can stop the Demon Realm from opening."

The Demon Realm was a matter of determination, a mental action that would transcend photon signals and open.

"Yes. The probability is fifty-fifty. Let's do our best."

Phaeton said,

"Stay sharp. If you try to keep up with my speed, your brain might explode."

"Hmph, speed isn't everything. I found the information about Siri this time too."

"You were just lucky."

How much they helped the Messiah was the most important thing among the 12 apostles.

After a moment of choosing her words, Abiris couldn't contain her irritation and lightly hit Phaeton's shoulder.

It looked like a joke, but the force of a dragon's strength was enough to make bones ache.

Phaeton, his face flushed, glanced at Shirone and then hit Abiris's thigh.

Abiris immediately retaliated, and soon the two apostles' arms were crossing at incredible speed.

Hearing the sound of bones breaking, Shirone frowned and turned around.

"Stop it."

The two apostles knelt simultaneously.

"We're sorry!"

"How many times has this happened? If you fight one more time, I'll go alone from now on."

At the chilling words, they quickly bowed their heads, but they also felt wronged.

'Why do I have to be paired with this guy?'

The fact that the light dragon and the black dragon were natural enemies was known to the 12 apostles and the Messiah.

Abiris asked,

"Messiah, I understand pairing us for the harmony of yin and yang, but I really don't get along with Phaeton."

"It's not because of the harmony of yin and yang."

Phaeton raised his head.

"Then, Messiah, is there some deeper meaning in pairing us..."

Shirone answered immediately.

"Everyone else didn't want to be paired with you."

In other words, they were a discarded combination.

To the other apostles, both the light dragon and the black dragon were personalities they didn't want to get involved with.

'These guys...'

As Phaeton and Abiris recalled the other apostles, Shirone chuckled.

"But you have me."

Relieved that the Messiah's anger had subsided, Phaeton bowed his head.

"Of course. And it's an excellent choice. We'll achieve the greatest results."

"Naturally. Let's take them all down."

The fierce determination in the two apostles' eyes was the real reason Shirone had brought them along.

As the snowstorm intensified, it turned into small, sharp ice crystals as they entered the city.

"That's dangerous."

Despite his words, Shirone calmly raised his hand and compressed the smoke of the Miracle Stream.

With a flash, the snowstorm momentarily ceased but soon engulfed the space again.

Abiris, with ice clinging to one side of her face, approached while surveying the surroundings.

"It's magic."

"Yes, Siri's Demon Realm is a cold wave. Even though it hasn't fully opened yet, it's this powerful..."

In any case, the likelihood of Siri being here had increased.

"Are there no survivors?"

It was unclear whether the citizens had been annihilated by the demons or had taken refuge in the castle at the city's center, but there were no people around.

Windows of houses were broken everywhere, and snow had piled up inside.

Help us.

Hearing a voice that human ears couldn't detect, Shirone stopped abruptly.

He leaped towards the sound coming from the end of a row of twenty stone houses.

The windows were all broken, and the interior was dark, but a faint light source shone from deep inside.

"Ugh. Ugh."

Following the sorrowful groans into the house, he found a woman holding a child and crying.

"My baby, my baby."

"What happened?"

As Shirone approached, the woman reached out with a crazed look in her eyes.

"Help! My baby isn't breathing! Please, please..."

Seeing that her husband wasn't there, it seemed she was isolated, too scared to flee.

The cold was too severe for a child to survive, but Shirone reached out with hope.

'The heart is beating.'

Feeling the vibration through his fingertips, Shirone quickly embraced the child and activated the Miracle Stream.

'If there's life, I can save it.'

As the light energy permeated the child, a cold smile appeared on the woman's lips.

Just as her hand, transformed into sharp ice, flew towards Shirone, a small magic circle appeared in front of Phaeton's eye.

Flying at the speed of light, he kicked the woman's face, shattering it like porcelain into powder.

The ice powder scattered from her neck down, and a few steps back, she transformed into a woman with an opaque icy appearance.

It was Siri, the 6th Legion Commander.

"Hohoho! Too bad! I thought I could land a solid hit."

As Phaeton attacked the woman, Abiris wrapped her arm around Shirone's neck.

It was a move to protect Shirone's vital points in case Phaeton's attack failed.

"I knew."

Shirone stood up, looking down at the snow powder slipping through his fingers.

Siri sneered.

"Sorry if I hurt Yahweh's pride... but I can't believe you knew."

"When I felt the heartbeat, the first thing that came to mind was that it couldn't be."

He wasn't foolish enough not to know biological limits.

"But I tried to revive the child because a mother was watching her child die. Even if there was the slightest chance, even one in a billion, that I was wrong...

If a life was lost because of that.

"The pain would be incomparable to the universe, Siri. You can't do that. No matter how much you hate me, you can't deceive me like this."

As the Miracle Stream flared up, the smile disappeared from Siri's lips.

The last bit of life in her eyes also cooled, like the city.

"That's why I hate you. You always throw tantrums. Do you know how many parents have lost their children simply by accident, by bad luck? Just say you hate me like Buddha. Then at least I'd scream in fear."

"Even if it doesn't make sense, that's the heart. Siri, realize love and repent."

"Nonsense. Benevolance is hypocrisy. You're just watching to see when I'll open the Demon Realm. You have to protect the humans you love. Why don't you try? See if you can kill me faster than I can open the Demon Realm."

"Philanthropy has no priority."

"Then prove it."

Siri knelt down with a thud.

"I know I can't win against you in a fight. So from now on, I will repent and disappear."

She had already heard that Yahweh had pushed out Buddha in Bashka.

"But can you trust my words? I might open the Demon Realm. Can you watch until I disappear?" Siri laughed.

"Hehe! See! You can't! You're actually suspicious, right? You hate me for making this offer, don't you? Want me to guess what you're thinking? What should I do, kill her now? But what if the Demon Realm opens first..."

"Siri."

Shirone, who had taken a step forward, looked down at her with kind eyes.

"Alright. If that will make you trust me..."

As Phaeton and Abiris swallowed nervously, Siri returned with a serious expression.

"Yahweh, I repent of my sins."

As Siri's body scattered into ice crystals, her Demon Realm, Ice Age, activated.

As the temperature dropped to a level that could be felt on the skin, Phaeton shouted in anger.

"How dare you deceive the Messiah!"

As she scattered into powder towards her annihilation, Siri glared at Shirone and burst into laughter.

Shirone waited for her as he had promised.

"Now everyone will realize how foolish Yahweh is! This feels great! I... I..."

At that moment, Siri saw her reflection in Shirone's eyes and realized.

"I won... I... For the first time in my life, hot tears are flowing from my eyes..."

"Why... why...?"

Seeing only pity instead of anger in Shirone's eyes, she finally understood everything.

'Is it true? Did he really...'

In the moment when the great chaos that made up the demon transformed into something clear and distinct,

"Ah..."

Everything became clear.

'I see.'

Perhaps it could have been undone.

"Yahweh..."

Siri's hand reached out urgently, but her body turned into crystals before she could finish.

As Shirone watched the spot where Siri had disappeared, he turned with the same expression as before.

"...Let's go."

Watching his footsteps, which carried a lonely weight, Abiris spoke to Phaeton.

"We must remember what happened today."

"...Yes."

They had to follow the Messiah, who was fighting the greatest battle in the universe alone.

And that was the last will left to the 12 apostles by the Dragon King Karatorusa.
