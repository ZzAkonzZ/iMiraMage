Chapter 1056: Free Gene (3)

While brushing her teeth in front of the mirror, Amy lifted her short hair.

She didn't dislike it.

She was a soldier and knew how useless appearances were in battle.

But when she returned to everyday life from the battlefield, she was still a woman.

"Sigh."

As she finished washing her face and came out, there was a knock on the door.

"Amy, are you in?" It was Shirone's voice.

She had imagined this countless times, but when it actually happened, she felt a sudden fear.

"Just a moment."

After drying her face with a towel, she opened the door to find Shirone with an awkward smile.

"What brings you here at this hour?"

"Well, it's just…"

Amy sighed.

"Come in."

After briefly checking the hallway, Amy closed and locked the door.

Click.

The sound seemed louder than thunder as Amy spoke first.

"You came because you were thinking about what happened this morning, right?"

"Well, Amy…"

"It's okay. I was upset, but I've come to a good conclusion. I was too sensitive."

She thought he had a really strong heart.

'That's why I like him.'

Amy smiled as she turned around.

"Thanks for telling me. I thought you didn't care at all, but you were thinking about it?"

"Haha, of course."

The misunderstanding from the morning was cleared up, but Shirone couldn't bring himself to leave now that he had made up his mind.

"So… should I just go now?"

"No, since you're here, would you like some tea?"

"Do you want to sit here for a moment?"

As Shirone patted the spot next to him on the bed, Amy finally realized.

"Why all of a sudden…"

As she sat on the bed, Shirone leaned in slightly.

"Wait!"

Shirone froze as if time had stopped.

"W-why?"

Amy got up from the bed, turned off the light, and returned to Shirone's side.

Time resumed for Shirone as he hadn't moved a muscle until she returned.

"Amy…"

As they heard rustling in the darkness and their breathing grew heavier…

"Great Star!"

Someone knocked on the door loudly.

Both of them jumped up simultaneously, and Shirone rolled off the bed.

"Who is it?"

As he turned on the light and looked back, Amy was fixing her clothes under the blanket.

"Great Star! Are you inside?"

It was Principal Nikolai's voice.

If he had come looking for Shirone himself, it would have been a serious breach of etiquette, but there was no time to think about that.

"Just a moment."

When he opened the door, Nikolai stood there with a pale face and sweating profusely.

"What is it?"

"I'm sorry. A guest has arrived… but you weren't in your room, so I came here."

"A guest?"

A certified class 4 mage wouldn't be shaken by most things.

Knowing this fact, Amy kicked off the blanket and approached the door.

"Who is it?"

There was an unusual mix of anger in her voice, and Shirone sided with her.

"Well…"

Nikolai wiped his forehead with a handkerchief and stepped back. Only then did Shirone realize.

"Oh!"

A promise suddenly came to mind.

"Shirone."

As the door slowly opened and the guest entered, Amy's mouth dropped open in shock.

It wasn't a human; it was someone with an otherworldly beauty not seen in the human world.

"Mom."

Archangel Ikael entered with a smile.

"Mom? Mom?"

The conclusion that quickly formed in Amy's mind hit her like a hammer blow to the back of her head.

"What? Mom?"

Ikael's eyebrows rose in surprise at the unexpected atmosphere.

"I came immediately because I heard it was urgent. Did I interrupt something?"

It was because of Wizard's situation. Also, due to the nature of simultaneous events, there couldn't be two Shirones in one place. They had arranged to meet Ikael beforehand and separated in another country.

'I didn't expect her to come at night…'

After sending Nikolai away, Shirone introduced the two women awkwardly.

"This is Amy. Amy, this is my biological mother…"

It felt awkward to say it out loud.

"This is Ikael."

"Hello. I'm Amy."

Ikael looked at Amy with gentle eyes as she greeted politely.

'So this is her.'

When Shirone first came to heaven, she was one of the women he had desperately tried to save.

"I've heard a lot about you from Shirone. You're very pretty. I'm Ikael."

The reason she didn't say "biological mother" was because of her lingering guilt towards Shirone.

Sensing the subtle nuance, Amy got straight to the point.

"So what's going on?" Shirone explained.

"Well… Archangel Satiel has become the new angel leader and is leading heaven's army. At first, the archangels were scattered, but Rayel seems to have sided with Satiel."

There had been many events within heaven's army as well.

"My mom and I traveled around trying to persuade ordinary angels. But we had no success. It seems every single one decided to follow Satiel."

"Every single one?"

Ikael explained.

"Angels are slightly different from humans. Unanimity is extremely rare in human society, but angels act based on absolute standards. When opinions differ, decisions are made by the archangel council called the White Hall."

Ikael used to be the final decision-maker there.

"The reason ordinary angels decided to follow Satiel is because they agreed with an absolute standard: restoring heaven's authority."

Shirone added,

"The White Hall was an institution with supreme authority in heaven. The fact that two members agreed played a significant role. If Uriel sided with my mom, it could shake heaven's army…"

Uriel intended to kill Ikael.

"Hmm."

Amy rested her chin on her hand and asked,

"What is Satiel planning? If she united heaven's army, something should have happened by now. But this is my first time hearing about it today."

"We don't know exactly either. At some point, tracking became impossible. We know they're on this planet for sure. According to Rampa's investigation, their trail ends in Jive Kingdom."

"Hmm, Jive. A powerful nation."

"Yes. It's likely related to the Holy War Council. The giants and fairies are also showing independent movements. Tormia has many cards to play but can't be complacent given heaven's situation."

"Indeed. Fermis side is also suspicious." Ikael said,

"I'm sorry we couldn't help more by persuading angels." Amy shook her head,

"No need to apologize. The angels left because I supported Shirone and humanity. I'm not representing anyone but I'm grateful for that."

Seeing Ikael regain her smile made Amy feel better too.

"But… why are you here today?"

Shirone said,

"Oh, I asked her to come because of Wizard. She'll help open temporal perception."

"Aha."

Shirone had also learned Ataraxia by resonating his mind with Ikael's before.

"But isn't it dangerous? You said one could lose themselves if not careful."

"That's why personal will is important. She'll be fine; she's talented and young which helps in opening senses."

"Mom, what’s wrong?"

Although Shirone had grown much more comfortable over time, there was something about Ikael’s presence at moments like this that made him tense.

Ikael softened her expression and said, “It’s late, so I’ll head back for now. You two should spend a bit more time together. I didn’t mean to intrude.”

Ikael was one of the few angels who had experienced how humans love.

“No, it’s fine. I was about to go to bed too. I just called Shirone because I needed to discuss something briefly, right, Shirone?”

Clenching her teeth, Amy stared daggers at Shirone, signaling him to get out.

“Oh, right. That’s what happened. Mom, you should go now. I’ll check if there are any empty rooms available.”

“No need for that. I’ll step out for a bit—I have something to take care of. See you both in the morning.”

Since there was no task that Shirone didn’t know about, he figured she was just being considerate.

“Good night. Sleep well, Shirone.”

After seeing Shirone and Ikael out, Amy closed the door and leaned against it.

“Phew.”

At least, she wouldn’t be having any unnecessary thoughts for a while.

In the dead of night, while everyone was asleep, blue sparks gathered on the rooftop of the dormitory. They swirled and took the form of a crouching figure.

Fwooo…

Exhaling deeply, the man, clad in a tight black bodysuit, stood still.

“…This is going to be tricky.”

Electricity coursed over the white mask covering his face, forming a neon expression—a childish, cartoonish display as if drawn by a kid. The eyes were marked with X’s, and the mouth was a wavy, jagged line resembling static.

“But I’ve been paid, so…”

Gunjo Vedium.

The top assassin of the Black Line and the father of Gunjo Lycan, who had been killed by Shirone.

When he received news of his son’s death, Vedium had displayed an expression of tears on his mask. That was the extent of his reaction.

‘Let’s test the waters first.’

Had Ikael not visited earlier, it would have been the perfect opportunity to kill Shirone.

‘Amy…’

Given how things had turned out, his immediate goal was to rattle Shirone emotionally.

Bzzt!

Once again, Vedium’s body dissolved into free electrons, and a wide grin appeared on his mask.

Like sinking into the ground, he vanished, only to reappear in a room filled with a pleasant fragrance.

Amy was sound asleep.

Sensing her brainwaves through the free electrons, Vedium gave a small nod.

‘Utterly stable.’

In his hand was a blade of crackling electricity shaped like an elongated S.

‘No emotions.’

If he struck her with this, the current would course through her organs, stopping her heart instantly.

‘Life’s just like that, isn’t it?’

Reciting a silent incantation to suppress his killing intent, he plunged the electric blade toward her chest.

Crackle!

At the same time, a surge of electricity erupted from his chest.

‘What?’

Realizing a fist had pierced through his body, Vedium quickly moved to the corner and lifted his head.

‘An archangel…’

A towering, stunning woman over two meters tall was standing over Amy, gazing down at her.

‘But why now? Could she have sensed my presence?’

Slowly turning her head, Ikael asked, “Who are you? Why are you targeting this child?”

Vedium’s mask displayed a playful expression, scrunching his eyes cutely and sticking out his tongue to the side.

‘This is… troublesome.’

A single bead of sweat blinked in and out of existence.
