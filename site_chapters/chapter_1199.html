Chapter 1199:

"Big Sister, You’re Amazing!"

As the Fire Tribe applauded, Yorahan stood there in a daze, having just witnessed a new technique of the Dimensional Barrier.

"You can do things like that?"

"Armand is the best among the Fire Tribe at handling metal. We call it Precision Adjustment."

Armand drew her sword and inspected the blade.

The weapon quivered as if alive, emitting a sharp, vibrating hum.

"Let’s go."

As Yorahan awkwardly stood up to follow, one of the Fire Tribe warned him.

"Be careful. There are carnivorous beasts where Armand is headed."

For a species to survive, an ecological system was essential.

"But don’t worry too much. Armand is incredibly strong. So stay close to her."

Yorahan flexed his arm.

"What are you talking about? I’m pretty strong myself!"

He threw a couple of unconvincing punches, then ran after Armand, making the Fire Tribe laugh.

"He's such a kind person. Maybe he's actually one of us?"

They had taken a liking to Yorahan.

"Hey, how far are we going? Let’s take a break."

After an hour of climbing the mountain, Yorahan was already exhausted.

"Pathetic. And you’ve been wandering the world with that level of stamina?"

Of course, he was confident in his ability to climb, but Armand’s speed was truly remarkable.

"At your pace, no one would be able to keep up with you."

"Hmph."

Despite turning away coldly, Armand sat down on a rock and handed him a water bottle.

"Drink."

"Ah, thanks."

As he drank, Yorahan glanced at Armand from the corner of his eye.

Unlike her usual fierce expression, she was gazing at the scenery with a serene smile.

"Cough! Cough!"

When he choked on the water, Armand’s expression immediately disappeared.

"What’s wrong?"

"Nah, just… I realized you can smile. I’ve never seen you smile before."

He was certain—having spent so much time with her.

"That just means you’re a danger to the village. If you’ve realized that, then leave."

"Why do you hate me? I swear, I have no intention of harming the Fire Tribe."

"Because you're human."

Armand spoke.

"The humans I’ve met weren’t evil from the start either. Most of them approached us with goodwill, just like you. But over time, they changed. As if they got bored of pretending to be kind."

Yorahan couldn’t deny it.

"You want to know why I hate humans? It’s because I resemble them."

"Unlike carnivorous species, the Fire Tribe absorbs energy from the sun. There’s no need for anyone to take more or share less—it’s an infinite resource. But you and I are different. If we eat something, it means some other life form has to starve."

She added,

"I don’t hate you. I just don’t trust the violent nature of carnivores. No matter what you think now, in the end, you'll want to own us too."

"... I’m sorry."

Yorahan admitted.

"You're right. That’s the nature of humanity. To be honest, even I felt my heart race when I looked at you just now."

Armand didn’t respond.

"But I would never hurt you. I will always act according to what is right. I believe that’s where humanity’s hope lies."

Good and evil weren’t what mattered.

What mattered was that they had the ability to choose.

"So please, won’t you read my heart just once? I want to show you."

"No."

"Why not? If you want to know whether I’m a bad person, don’t you have to understand me first?"

"That’s not the problem…"

Armand hesitated slightly, pouting.

"I’ve never connected with a human before."

"Huh?"

"At first, I tried it on animals. It was horrible. Resonating with something I had to eat…"

"Ah…??????"

"You keep asking me to read your heart, but the Dimensional Barrier isn’t like that. It doesn’t just let me see—it makes us truly one. Passive Fire Tribe members can handle it because they accept any thoughts that enter them. But I’m different. If a human’s ego invades me…"

Her body trembled.

"It’s disgusting. Having to accept the desires of someone I don’t even like."

Yorahan thought he understood.

"Don’t misunderstand. It’s not that I hate you. But honestly, I don’t want to connect with you."

It stung, being rejected. But he fully understood her feelings.

"I was too careless. The Dimensional Barrier must require more courage from you than a human giving up everything to another person. I’m sorry."

For the first time, Armand’s eyes showed emotion. But she quickly masked it and stood up.

"Let’s move. We have to finish hunting before sunset."

Just then, the bushes rustled.

Both Armand and Yorahan turned simultaneously. A pack of wolves was closing in.

"Shh."

Armand drew her longsword.

'The Fire Tribe’s scent doesn’t attract beasts. This is because of Yorahan.'

She couldn’t blame him—he was the one she had brought here.

"Back away slowly. I’ll handle them."

The chief’s rule was that they must not kill unless they intended to eat the prey. But this was different.

"The Fire Tribe helps humans."

As those two beliefs clashed within her, the wolves bared their fangs and lunged.

"Run!"

Armand charged into the pack, buying Yorahan time to escape.

‘So this is a predator…’

Even as a carnivorous plant, Armand was still a plant. She marveled at the sheer aggression of the wolves.

"I can’t hold back."

With that resolve, she spun, slicing through the beasts. Each time her blade moved, lives were cut short.

"Huff… huff…"

She looked at the bloody forest around her, her heart sinking.

"Damn it! Because of him…!"

Then—

"Aaaaaah!"

Yorahan’s scream rang out.

Through the Dimensional Barrier, she sensed his footsteps—heading toward a cliff.

"Damn it!"

When she arrived, she found him cornered at the edge by the remaining wolves.

Grrraaaah!

The wolves lunged—and as Yorahan lost his footing and fell, Armand leaped after him.

"There’s a raging river below."

The greater concern was the jagged rocks jutting out.

"Dimensional Barrier!"

Lashing out with vine-like tendrils, Armand caught hold of a branch and extended her hand.

"Grab on!"

Yorahan reached, but the gap was too wide.

"At this rate, he’ll die."

Crashing against the rocks meant certain death.

"A human…"

Acting on sheer instinct, Armand activated the Dimensional Barrier on Yorahan.

"Gasp!"

Her face burned red.

"That bastard!"

He claimed he was confident?!

All of his buried thoughts about her came rushing in like a flood.

"This is why I hate this! Why do I have to go through this humiliation? And yet, he acts so proud…!"

But then—she saw the core of his desires.

Patience. Conviction. Consideration.

All the effort he had endured to protect the emotions of others.

"Ah…"

For the first time, she felt it.

This is what it means to be loved.

When you put someone else before yourself, desire shed its murky shell and revealed something truly beautiful.

"I’m not afraid."

As long as he gave his all to protect her.

"Yorahan."

She reached out with her heart.

"Accept me."

The moment they became one, Yorahan experienced something beyond words.

"So this is it."

A connection that transcended dominance and submission—something no human could ever experience.

"True unity."

His body moved instinctively, dodging the rocks. But the rapids were unavoidable.

"Ugh!"

The moment he hit the water, the Dimensional Barrier shattered.

Armand let go of the vine and dove in after him.

"Yorahan!"

Her body slipped into the water, without even a ripple.

While Shirone’s Transcendent Dream unfolded, in Gaold’s world, more than a decade had passed.

In a world where compromise had been achieved, life was monotonous, and the amount of data to process was minimal.

Except for today.

“Congratulations, Gaold.”

“To think you’d get such a young bride. Well, I suppose that’s a kind of talent too. Eat well, my friend. Hahaha!”

After receiving the guests' congratulations, Gaold entered the waiting room and stood before a mirror.

A gaunt old man in his sixties stared back at him.

"Are you alright? What's with that expression?"

Kangnan approached and adjusted his attire.

"Relax. Today is the day your dream comes true."

"My dream."

"I know. It must feel strange. But in reality, you gave up everything for Miro. There’s no more excruciating pain here, no more wars. Isn’t that enough?"

"Excruciating pain."

Ah, that’s right.

Endless suffering that he could neither escape nor die from. He had endured it every moment.

"Come on, let’s go see your bride. Smile a little! No one’s going to say anything to you."

As the groom arrived at the bride’s waiting room, the people inside quietly stepped aside.

"Go ahead."

As if there was no wall, Gaold pressed himself against the door and knocked lightly.

"Yes, come in."

What kind of future awaited him?

When he opened the door and stepped inside the waiting room…

"Old man."

Everything he had ever wanted was waiting for him.

Miro, dressed as a bride, was so beautiful that he wished time would stop right then and there.

"What? Why are you looking at me like that? Do I look weird?"

He had waited over a decade for her to come of age, but in another way, it felt as if it had only been yesterday.

"You…"

Gaold smiled bitterly.

"You look younger than ever."

In reality, weren’t we just classmates at the Magic Academy?

"Haha."

Understanding the meaning behind his words, Miro walked closer to him.

"What’s wrong? You still look handsome. Besides, at a wedding, the bride is the star. As long as I look pretty, isn’t that enough?"

She truly was beautiful.

"Oh my! Your eyes are filled with impure thoughts. But I’ll let it slide today."

"… Won't you regret this?"

Miro smiled gently and asked back,

"Why? Do you think you'll regret it?"

"No."

At a moment like this, regret was an impossibility for any human being.

"Then I won’t regret it either. Because I love you."

It was the truth.

As long as this was the Extreme Good version of Miro, with no more reality left for her to protect.

"The ceremony will start soon. I’ll see you later."

Gaold nodded and left the waiting room, where an elderly Arius, now a loyal old dog, greeted him.

Seeing the expression on Gaold’s face, Sein asked,

"What are you thinking about?"

Even in this world, they weren’t particularly close. But Sein’s keen insight was hard to fool.

"What? Nothing at all."

"You’re not… thinking of something foolish, are you?"

Gaold shook his head and stood up.

"Don’t worry."

Sein watched him silently as he walked away.

With all the guests gathered—except for Shirone—the Tormia-style wedding began.

"Groom, enter."

As Gaold walked up to the altar, the applause and whistles never ceased.

"And now, for today’s main event—let’s bring in the bride!"

Miro, veiled, took Sein’s hand and walked in. The crowd murmured in excitement.

"The most stunning bride."

Old man.

Miro slowly lifted her gaze, and at the end of her vision was Gaold, smiling radiantly.

The guests erupted into laughter.

"Hahaha! The groom looks like he’s about to die of happiness!"

But then—

Tears welled up in Gaold’s eyes, and his face twisted into an expression of sorrow.

"Krrrhh!"

The wedding hall shook violently.

Countless eyes began to flood the ceiling.

Gaold clenched his teeth and focused straight ahead.

"Miro."

He had been dreaming.

A brilliant, beautiful dream.

"Let’s go back."

Miro removed her veil.

"We have to protect the world. I won’t settle for mere compromise. I can endure for as long as necessary…"

Gaold’s hair turned completely white.

"Let’s go back."

To a life where every moment was suffering.

When the will to protect others transcends desire, the heart loses its limits.

"Gaold."

For the first time, Miro called his name.
