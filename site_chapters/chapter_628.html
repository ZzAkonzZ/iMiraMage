Chapter 628:

Iruki calculated the detonation equation as he watched the Fire Strike expand in an instant.

'Nuclear Fusion!'

The body of the Fire Strike swelled like a balloon and then exploded powerfully.

"Ugh!"

The anti-magic shock hit all the participants who had already moved away.

'Even though I dodged…'

The power was so strong that the 2000 system judged it as an attack on the entire colosseum.

"So, you want to go all the way?"

The massive explosion marked the start of a chaotic melee, but Fermi remained calm.

'It's almost boringly easy.'

Having deliberately held back at the final hurdle of graduation every year, he found it easier now that he had lifted his limits.

'Wait, I just need to take down 16 more to graduate?'

Why did other students find this so difficult?

Blue lightning flickered in front of Fermi's eyes as Lycan approached with Spark Magic and swung his hand.

Fermi, who had widened the distance with his incredible reflexes, blew on his singed bangs and said, "Isn't it too early? If you fight me, you'll miss graduation."

Hershey of Sona approached from Fermi's side.

"That's your strategy."

Given Fermi's dominance in the graduation exams so far, it was natural for participants to avoid direct confrontation.

"But your era is over. If we corner you, everyone will attack you."

'Annoying.'

Hershey's analysis was accurate, and Fermi, who had been hit where it hurt, smiled insincerely.

"Give me a break. We have some history, don't we?"

"Nonsense. Do you even have such emotions?"

As Hershey activated Sona and Lycan pulled a rubbery hood over his face, Fermi's shoulders shook.

"Heh heh heh. Heh heh heh."

"Don't bluff. All that's left for you is a miserable end."

The laughter abruptly stopped.

"Pathetic."

Fermi's face, devoid of emotion, was as cold as a human's could be.

"Kill him!"

Hershey, feeling a chill, shouted, and Lycan charged at Fermi, raising his electric charge.

'Magma Stream.'

The ground where Fermi aimed his palm rapidly melted, and an enormous amount of magma surged up.

"Advanced fusion magic."

Combining atmospheric and fire elements required not just magical skill but veteran proficiency.

"An irregular eater. Buying experience with money."

The massive amount of magma clumped together in the sky and poured down towards Hershey and Lycan.

"Dodge!"

As the lava that hit the ground quickly spread, engulfing the entire colosseum, everyone flew into the air.

Caught between Shirone and Fermi, the two systems controlling the graduation exam, the participants' minds were in turmoil.

'Which side should I join?'

Canis didn't hesitate.

"Attack Shirone."

Harvest spread his giant hand.

"Heh heh, we must avenge Arcane, right?"

Arin thought it was dangerous.

"Canis, fighting Shirone now…"

"I know what you want to say. I'm stubborn and not great at judgment."

"That's not what I mean. Why do you keep belittling yourself?"

This side of Canis hurt Arin the most.

"That's why I'm fighting. If I back down here… I won't be satisfied with anything I do in the future."

Watching Canis's determination, Arin couldn't help but agree.

"Okay, let's go. This is our fight."

As always, Harvest took the lead.

"Ha ha ha! We're here, Shirone!"

Seeing Canis, Shirone's eyes tensed.

'As expected, Canis.'

A mage who honed his magic solely for combat and a top contender in mental strength among the graduating class.

"It's a rematch, Shirone."

"Bring it on."

Photon Cannon and the power of darkness clashed head-on.

As the black-and-white contrast became clear, the scouts murmured in surprise.

"How is that possible?"

Most dark magic was overpowered but had a single weakness to light that overshadowed all its strengths.

'My darkness overcomes light.'

The Book of Light and Darkness - Incarnation Reversal.

As the heavenly battery that once led Arcane to become an Archmage was equipped, the power of darkness began to drive out the light.

"Ha ha ha! Yes, this is it!"

Harvest's body twisted like a pretzel and transformed into a sharp spear, rushing forward.

-Pierce through, Harvest!

Although numerous Shirones simultaneously cast Photon Cannon, the shadow spear didn't budge.

"It's swallowing the light. That Number 12 has incredible power."

Elizabeth searched Canis's records.

"He's a disciple of Viltor Arcane. He also showed exceptional strength and stubbornness."

If the superior force in an elemental match was strength, there was no one like Arcane, who rose to an Archmage with dark magic.

"Indeed, elemental matches are separate from power. It's a battle of who can enforce their will."

"This time, I'll win, Shirone!"

As Canis focused, Harvest's speed, transformed into a shadow spear, increased.

"No, Canis."

Shirone stopped moving, and the numerous Shirones superimposed in the Force Dimension evaporated.

"I won't lose to anyone."

Lowering his center of gravity, Shirone extended his right hand backward, and dozens of Shirones began to overlap, creating afterimages.

'Concentrate all events on one coordinate.'

As if an earthquake had occurred, Shirone's body trembled, and a Photon Cannon formed outside his palm.

"That's it."

Miro pointed to the sky with her index finger.

"The pinnacle is always met at the top."

The ability to inscribe time into space was akin to the incarnation technique of the Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara.

The difference was that Shirone's Quantum Superposition superimposed the events themselves, and the magic concentrated on one coordinate expanded into a giant sphere.

Quantum Superposition - 128 Overlap.

The power of 128 Shirones simultaneously throwing Photon Cannons was realized as a 2-meter diameter flash, extending towards Harvest.

"Take this, Harvest!"

"Kieeeek!"

The shadow spear extended sharp spikes backward and rotated, but the giant flash swallowed it whole.

Countless eyes watched the clash of light and darkness.

A ringing sound.

"What?"

The beeping sound seemed to connect both eardrums.

He couldn't see anything, and soon concluded that he had lost all sensation in his body.

"…Canis!"

Arin's voice came from high above.

'What? Why is the sound coming from above?'

He tried to recall, but his mind went blank as if his brain had detached from his body the moment Harvest collided with the Photon Cannon.

'Where am I now?'

He shook his head, but wasn't sure if his body was actually following suit.

Fortunately, after a while, his vision slowly returned.

It took more time for him to realize he was lying in the middle of a cooled lava field formed by solidified magma.

'I fell? Me? Why?'

"Canis! Watch out!"

As Arin's voice became clear, a flash of light rushed down from the sky.

"Ugh!"

Harvest appeared in front of Canis, who had just sat up, and took the Photon Cannon with his body.

"Aaaargh!"

As his nerves awakened, a wave of real pain hit him.

'This can't be.'

In other words, the impact he felt before losing consciousness wasn't real at all.

-Canis, are you okay?

Harvest's signal came through the mental channel.

-What happened?

-I couldn't break through. I was overpowered. When you lost consciousness, I couldn't move either.

"I… was overpowered?"

Even though he neutralized the elemental advantage by deciphering the Book of Light and Darkness, he was completely defeated.

"Now! Attack Shirone!"

With Canis's start as a signal, the participants who had been waiting for an opportunity rushed in simultaneously.

Screamer, Conger, and Prings launched a joint attack while Arin's mental assault targeted Shirone. Yet Shirone's gaze only grew sharper.

"A mage who can respond to all variables?"

Though each participant took pride in their specialty, Shirone faced them all one-on-one without being pushed back.

Elizabeth spoke in a trembling voice.

"His mental strength is recovering. It's already over 30%. It's runner's high."

A spark ignited in Shirone's eyes.

'Immortal Function!'

The mental energy flooding in from the infinite realm further sharpened his focus and boosted the efficiency of the Force Dimension.

"36%! 40%! It's still recovering!"

Ataraxia, Elysion, Time Explosion, Immortal Function—all aspects that made up Shirone were circulating seamlessly.

"…A flawless system."

Even amidst enemy attacks, Shirone's mind became clearer.

'Strange.'

Logically analyzing it was impossible, but it felt as if everyone's movements were unrelated to his situation.

'I don't feel like I'm going to lose. I don't feel any danger.'

"His mind has completely opened up."

Albino murmured while stroking his chin, and Miro's eyes sparkled as if she had discovered a gem.

"He's starting to hear his heartbeat."

Poltar turned as if seeking an explanation, but Miro only saw Shirone's fluid movements.

'The time perceived by the brain is split by the heart. That's the essence of rhythm.'

Therefore, those who can hear their heartbeat…

"Can split all time into their own rhythm."

'Damn it! I just need one good hit…!'

Screamer pushed Shirone with all his might but couldn't even graze him despite using every trick he knew.

He was trapped in Shirone's rhythm.

"For God's sake, just get hit!"

As Screamer's body created afterimages and charged fiercely, Shirone's rhythm became even more finely divided.

'Dodge here.'

A punch brushed past with perfect timing, and the offbeat that split that timing became another beat that further segmented time.

"Uoooooooh!"

No matter how fast Screamer moved, he was already a puppet trapped in Shirone's rhythm.

"No chance."

Baikal said.

"A one-hit kill technique is useless if it doesn't hit."

The feeling of having lifelong martial arts training rendered useless was the worst humiliation in Screamer's life.

"This can't be! How can I not land a single hit…!"

Other participants also attacked Shirone with violent magic, but to Shirone, each moment was just another event split into individual segments.

'Ah, I see. I…'

Shirone's movements in a state of selflessness looked like a dance; it wasn't just technique but art without wastefulness.

"For this brief moment of dance…"

Albino realized what kind of life Shirone had lived.

"He must have filled most of his life with experiences of pain."

Miro muttered,

"…The privilege of arrogance."

"I am…"

A light of certainty exploded in Shirone's mind.

"The strongest!"

Quantum Superposition - 240 Overlap.

Drrrrrr!

Following Shirone's twisting body, 239 other Shirones concentrated their Photon Cannons on a single coordinate.

As he swung his arm in that state, a massive flash extended and struck Screamer's abdomen.

"Gah!"

The impact felt like an explosion in his brain; Screamer's eyes bulged as if they would pop out. He was already unconscious by then.

-Participant Number 10 has exited the 2000 system.

Seeing Screamer sprawled out on the ground sent chills down the spines of other participants who turned pale-faced away from him.

"The strongest?"

It was hard to believe such a statement came from someone humble. Yet dozens of charging Shirones showed no signs of stopping.

"Yes. This is your festival, Shirone."

Miro believed he deserved it.

"Run wild like a mad colt."
