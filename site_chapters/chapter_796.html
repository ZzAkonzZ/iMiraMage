Chapter 796: Complex (1)

“Excuse me.”

Just as the Jaestin Guild members were finishing breakfast and preparing to head to the pageant venue, an unfamiliar voice made them turn their heads.

“Well, if you have business, then excuse yourself properly. And who might you be?”

A sharp-featured woman stood at the door, wearing oversized glasses—clearly for style—and sporting a bob haircut that suited her aloof expression.

“This is the Jaestin Guild, right?”

Eyepatch stepped up as their representative.

“Of course this is the Jaestin—urk!”

He had been about to crack a joke when a pale man with shaved eyebrows walked in, and Eyepatch quickly backed off.

“What are you all doing in a place like this?”

As Freeman glanced around, four top officers of the Parrot Mercenary Guild filed in behind him.

‘They’re the real deal.’

Each one gave off a pressure that couldn’t be ignored.

Finally, Jaestin stood up and demanded, “Who the hell are you people? Barging into someone else’s guild this early in the morning.”

Marsha smirked.

“Honestly, if you’re going to summon people, you should organize the ranks beforehand. Girl, do you know who we are?”

“No, but I’ve got a pretty good idea. I’ve never met anyone who says that line and turns out to be decent.”

Marsha raised a finger.

“Fair point. But let’s make an exception this time—assuming you all live in the same world we do.”

“Pfft, says the—”

Just as Jaestin started forward, Shirone stepped through the back door, freshly bathed and toweling off his hair.

“Huh? Sister Marsha, when did you get here?”

Marsha narrowed her eyes as she looked at him.

Hmph.

Though she’d only seen a copy of him in Under Coder, it had been four years since they parted ways in Galliant.

‘He’s grown a lot.’

He still had the innocent aura of a seventeen-year-old, but his temperament had sharpened immensely.

Shirone hung his towel on a chair and hurried over to grab Marsha’s hand.

“It’s been so long.”

Marsha threw an arm around Shirone’s neck.

“You! Calling me here with that weird method! What the hell?! I couldn’t even decline because I couldn’t reply!”

“Wahhh! I had no choice!”

Pulling him close like a little brother, Marsha turned to Freeman and burst into laughter.

“‘Had no choice,’ he says! This kid’s all grown up and talking big now.”

As the others watched the noisy reunion, the Jaestin members narrowed their eyes, finally snapping to attention.

“No way… Parrot Mercenary Guild?”

Marsha looked to Jaestin.

“Yeah. Heard someone was dying to see me, so I came. Who was it?”

“I asked you to come. I have a favor to ask.”

As Shirone explained the situation, Jaestin kept her eyes fixed on Marsha.

‘This woman…’

The Mother of the Forsaken.

‘And what a nasty attitude.’

Whatever her relationship to Shirone, someone who could yank around one of the Five Stars like a thug didn’t discriminate by title.

“I see… So that’s the situation.”

Marsha approached the table, and everyone quietly stood to give her space.

Sitting alone, she lit a cigarette and held it out toward Shirone.

“Light.”

He owed her from Under Coder, and he was the one asking the favor—there was no refusing.

‘She hasn’t changed one bit.’

Lighting it with Yahweh’s fire, Marsha took a long drag and exhaled a cloud of smoke.

“Alright. Jaestin Guild, welcome aboard. Havitz or whatever—we’re family now. That means we go all the way.”

“Thank you. Hehe.”

Marsha chuckled at Shirone’s casual grin—something she hadn’t seen from him before.

‘He’s grown up, huh.’

Still, he was like a little brother to her.

“But we have a condition too. Nothing big, but it’s quicker to go through you.”

“What is it?”

For the first time, Marsha’s expression turned serious.

“Have you heard from Fermi?”

It wasn’t strange that Marsha would know him—after all, they’d fought together in Under Coder.

“No. I just heard he left after the graduation exam. I haven’t heard anything since…”

“Do you know what they call him in the underground?”

Marsha stood her cigarette upright.

“The Crimson Drug King.”

Shirone’s mouth hung open.

“He’s already taken over the central continent. It’s spreading to the south. The weaker the addiction, the higher the markup. And surprisingly, it works. Even regular folks are using it. He’s exploiting the fear of the demons.”

“But why drugs? If he needed money, he could’ve done depreciation trading…”

“You idiot. You just don’t get how this world works. He’s way past that level. The contract payment he got from the Empress of Kashan is long gone.”

Still, Shirone couldn’t wrap his head around it.

‘Has he broken?’

Did the trauma of the graduation exam destroy both his convictions as a mage and his will as a disciple of Yolga?

“How did you find out?”

“As Parrot’s influence grew, all kinds of people joined up. Some of them apparently started using. You know we have a strict no-drugs policy.”

With a signal from Marsha, a dark-skinned, broad-shouldered man stepped forward.

Parrot Mercenary Guild’s 3rd Unit Leader: Seike (Rat, Tiger).

“Nice to meet you. I’m Seike.”

He rolled up his sleeve and revealed a hieroglyph for ‘rat’ tattooed on his left arm.

“I’m a warrior of the Mun clan. Chosen warriors can engrave two animals from the twelve onto their bodies.”

It was a kind of shamanism.

“One from the six combat types, and one from the six support types. The rat gives me Tunnel Sight. I can bend light’s direction.”

That was impressive.

“For example, I can see inside this ceiling—not through X-ray vision. I follow the gaps in the floor, ride the drainpipes, and watch through the ventilation systems. Anyway, I started monitoring the suspects, and I found the coins used in the drug trade. They look like this.”

As Seike held up a coin, Shirone immediately recognized it.

“Golden Wheel…”

With a flick, Seike sent the coin spinning before catching it mid-air.

“I figured you'd know. Took me a whole month just to find where that insignia came from. Turns out it was used by Fermi back at the magic academy in Tormia—his alma mater.”

It had to be Fermi.

‘No one else could have built a drug route this fast.’

Shirone clenched his fists.

“Fermi… seriously…”

No matter how pragmatic he was, Shirone never imagined Fermi would trade in something that destroyed lives.

Marsha spoke.

“So our condition is this: We don’t know where the factory is or where Fermi is. But if you find him—help me get a meeting.”

“What are you going to do when you meet him?”

“Convince him. Or maybe partner up. Not for drug smuggling or anything, of course… but Fermi’s a genius.”

That much had been clear during Under Coder.

“Alright.”

If not for Yolga, Hexa wouldn’t exist. Shirone knew he had to face Fermi at least once.

“Then it’s settled. Jaestin Guild, start packing. We’ve got something we want you to handle anyway.”

Shirone stopped her.

“Not yet. I’ve still got unfinished business in Arakne. Let’s go after that.”

He had accomplished his original mission—finding Beta Fish—but there was no way he could leave with the World Beauty Pageant still going on.

“Unfinished business? What is it?”

Shirone let out a sigh.

“Well…”

When Shirone arrived at the venue, there seemed to be more security guards than spectators.

After the terrorist attack, the event’s commercial success was already a lost cause—but finishing the pageant was crucial if they wanted to plan for next year.

‘That guy’s here again.’

The man from the parade was once again handing out flyers, volunteering his support.

“Representing Arakne—Barho Langi! Please give her your love! Vote for Langi!”

Though, his slogans had changed.

‘This isn’t the time to worry about others.’

The contestants had been moved from the bunker to the venue early in the morning, and now, their safety rested on Shirone’s shoulders.

‘I’m not great at calming people…’

He took a deep breath at the door and knocked, but no reply came.

“Uh, it’s me—Shirone.”

Still no response. When he turned the handle, the door opened slightly on its own.

“I’m coming in.”

As Shirone cautiously poked his head in, he was greeted by a room full of women.

“Surprise!”

Pop! Confetti exploded overhead, raining down on him like snow.

“Were you surprised? We prepared a little welcome party! Come on in!”

Miss Oldhill, whose skin was pale as snow, grinned and pulled him in.

All the contestants were gathered, and some were dressed only in their underwear.

“Uh, um…”

Shirone’s eyes met Pony’s, and she let out a sigh.

“Sorry. It’s my fault. I was talking to Langi yesterday and your name came up…”

Langi approached with an apologetic smile.

“I just felt like we couldn’t relax unless you were here. So… I was honest.”

“That’s okay. Nothing’s more important than safety. But… this is a little…”

Then came a staff member’s voice.

“Twenty minutes until the event begins.”

And as soon as the announcement ended, several contestants began changing into swimsuits.

“Uwahhh!”

Terrified, Shirone spun around as the girls burst into laughter.

“Shirone, you’re so innocent! It’s okay, really. This is our job—and we work with male designers all the time.”

Apparently, it wasn’t just magicians who trained themselves not to look.

“Of course, not just anyone can be in here. But you’re not creepy, Shirone…”

Langi turned shyly to start changing clothes.

“I’m not okay with this! Hey! Get out!”

Pony, her face red as a ripe persimmon, pushed Shirone toward the door—when he suddenly froze.

A killing intent?

He whipped around, but the feeling vanished like a lie.

“Hey! Don’t just spin around like that!”

‘Pony didn’t sense it?’

It had been directed solely at him. The women, unaware, continued to laugh and chat.

“Hehe, see? Not such a big deal once you’re in here. But since you got a good look, now you’ve gotta protect us till the end, okay?”

It was definitely a big deal, but there was only one thought occupying Shirone’s mind now.

“…Ah.”

Hearing his groan, Pony shouted even louder.

“Get out already!”

How had he not considered it before?

‘We didn’t see the terrorist’s face. And if her Regulation Exception allows her to steal body parts…’

That meant she could have swapped her own face with someone else’s.

‘There might be traces…’

He carefully examined the women’s necklines, but found nothing out of place.

‘Any mental changes…?’

If only the face had been swapped, then the terrorist’s brain would still be inside—but without knowing the contestants’ personalities, he couldn’t verify that.

‘The killing intent earlier… must’ve been a slip.’

If the terrorist really was here, then the further he got from them, the greater the danger.

“Don’t worry, Pony,” Shirone said seriously.

“You can’t truly see with just your eyes.”

The contestants turned to look at him, and Pony shivered slightly, confused.

“What kind of nonsense is that…?”

Grabbing him by the ear, she yanked open the door and shoved him out.

“Get out, you perv! Hurry up…!”

“Wait.”

Shirone grabbed her wrist and, as he closed the door, whispered into her ear—

“Since this morning… has any contestant seemed a little off?”
