As she let out a sigh—

“Lethe.”

“Geez, don’t scare me like that.”

Lethe flinched at Shirone’s voice, then hesitated before finally turning around.

With the Under World having been reset, the darkness Shirone had embraced had now been fully purified.

Lethe said, “You look better. Seriously, why would you take on something you couldn’t handle? Did you think darkness would play nice?”

“What about you?”

Shirone smiled. “You said you’d fight for humanity.”

“Don’t flatter yourself.”

Lethe’s hesitant expression turned sharp.

“It’s for the Under World. I couldn’t just sit back and watch Hell vanish.”

“…I see.”

The Administrator of Hell had chosen demons over the gods.

“It’s fine. I’ve embraced the darkness too. I’ll handle the rest.”

“Hmph.”

Lethe raised her chin. “You sound confident. But you do realize—you can’t kill demons anymore.”

That was the kind of realm he had stepped into.

Shirone simply smiled in silence, and Lethe dropped her pretense too.

“Tch. Fine. You can use tachyons now. You’re basically a god. I can’t touch you anymore. Not like it matters—now that you’ve accessed the outer world, there’s nothing left to hide.”

Shirone asked, “Was it really all a lie?”

Lethe knew what he meant but waited for him to continue.

“Great Star, and Mr. Luber—did they deceive us from the start? Was their affection for humanity all fake?”

“We’re just programs. We act according to protocols for specific situations. It might look like a heart to you, but I’m no different. There’s just a quantum variable thrown in the mix.”

“But Great Star—he…”

“Ugh, I don’t know!”

Lethe shook her head.

“Even though we’re tied to the same core—‘God’—we each have our own sectors. Each with massive authority. One wrong move and you cause a system crash. That’s why we’ve got responses turned off.”

“...I see.”

As Shirone sank into thought, Rian approached, supporting Jin Seong Eum.

“Thanks—for saving me.”

Her words were short, but her voice trembled.

She hadn’t expected to see Shirone again.

“What are you talking about? I should be thanking you. If it weren’t for you, the world would’ve ended.”

Lethe, feeling the hot wind blowing from far off, said,

“We don’t have time for long chats. Say what you’re going to do. This place is about to turn to ash.”

Shirone looked at Rian. “I’ll take care of the Simultaneous Event. Ymir’s going to resurrect soon. Depending on how he moves, the entire game could change. Rian, head to Delta HQ with Seongeum. Use the Ether Wave. It’ll be instant. Can you handle it?”

Seongeum nodded. “Of course. But before I bring Rian—I need to stop somewhere first.”

Jin Kang.

She still didn’t know her father’s condition, so Shirone immediately agreed. “Alright. Go.”

He couldn’t say why, but from his urgency, she seemed to understand.

The Ether Wave activated, crumpling the barrier of the rift, and her figure disappeared.

“Guess I’ll vanish too. See you at the Holy War.”

As the Simultaneous Event was lifted, Shirone’s form began to fade. Rian gave him a thumbs-up.

“Don’t worry. I’ll catch up soon.”

Watching him for a moment, Shirone recalled the time they had spent together in Hell.

“Rian.”

It was awkward, but he had to say it.

“I’m glad you were here.”

Light particles fluttered like petals. Rian gave a smirk and turned away.

“You sap.”

Lethe, arms crossed, asked, “By the way, how are you planning to get back?”

“Huh? Obviously—”

A tidal wave of fire was racing across the Valley of Wailing.

“Oh, come on—!”

The inferno engulfed them.

“Yaaaah!!”

And Rian’s voice, charging through the flames, marked the final moment of Hell.

Jin Kang’s eyes stared blankly at the ceiling.

“Haa…”

His face pale as death, he had no more blood left to shed.

“Your Majesty.”

Anchal approached and held his upper body.

“Did Hell… feel my rage?”

“I’m sure it did.”

“And my daughter…”

Jin Kang gasped several times before he could finish.

“…Did she return?”

Anchal didn’t know. But if she was truly alive, he prayed she would be there, in what little life Jin Kang had left—barely a minute now.

“Your Majesty, I’m sure the Princess—”

“It’s alright.”

It was time to let go.

“If she’s alive, that’s enough. But even if she’s not…”

A soft smile crept onto his lips.

“…I’ll go bring her back myself.”

Just then, tears slipped silently down Anchal’s cheek.

“Father.”

A beautiful voice echoed like a dream.

“Prin—”

Anchal’s one eye flew open, and every servant dropped to their knees as though their legs would shatter.

“We greet the Princess!”

With a stunned face, Jin Kang froze as Jin Seong Eum stepped gently to his side and sat beside him.

“Father.”

“Ah… ahh…”

Only after feeling the warmth in her hand did Jin Kang accept this was real.

“I’ve returned, Father.”

“Yes. Yes…”

He nodded repeatedly.

But the emotion only lasted a moment—then he remembered his duty as Emperor, and his expression hardened.

“Anchal.”

“Command me, Your Majesty.”

“From now on, the Emperor of Jincheon… is Jin Seong Eum.”

Long live the Emperor! Long live the Emperor! Long live the Emperor!

“My daughter… help Shirone. A Jincheon emperor must never betray trust.”

Seong Eum bowed deeply.

“I will honor your command.”

“Heh…”

And when he looked up again—

Jin Kang was smiling peacefully, staring at the ceiling.

“Hrk…”

Seong Eum, who had held back her tears, finally broke down.

“Father! Father!”

Jin Kang, Emperor of Jincheon— A ruler who defined an age.

Was dead.
