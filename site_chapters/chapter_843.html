Chapter 843: Participation (3)

The elite troops of the 7th Legion of the army of hell were guarding the entrance to the imperial city of Aganos.

"Kill them."

Gando, who had already suffered severe injuries, lay against the city wall, glaring at his enemies.

"Kill?"

Balkan pushed through the demons and approached.

"Do you really think you have that honor?"

The throne had been passed down, but no one in Kashan regarded Gando as the emperor.

'We must keep Uorin alive until we catch her.'

"Damn it..."

Realizing the grim reality, Gando leaned back against the city wall and looked up at the sky.

"Mother."

It was strange that even in the face of death, he did not resent her.

'That is what it means to be an emperor.'

Born a mere mortal, yet regarded as someone who naturally deserves everything that others cannot have.

'I could never be like that.'

Had Uorin escaped safely?

'Smodo has gone. It's only a matter of time before they catch up.'

The Wind Guards and Kido had followed, but the demon Amon was different in temperament from other legion commanders.

'He's not just strong. There's something strange about him...'

Before Gando could finish his thought, a strange noise surged from the direction of the outer city gate.

Kraaaang!

The elite demon troops shivered involuntarily as the echo resonated through the heavens.

"What's this?"

Balkan turned around.

Although not visible yet, the rapidly changing mood of the crowd alone indicated the severity of the situation.

"Damn it. What the hell is this?"

Crowd spirit, a massive flow that reads the collective will on the battlefield.

And the flow Balkan was seeing now was the kind one would expect in a natural disaster.

'Has a volcano erupted?'

Even if so, it was unheard of for the demon's crowd spirit to be so terrified.

'It's not a disaster. It's emotion.'

Anger.

A massive wave of emotion was swallowing the demons' fighting spirit like a tsunami.

"Leave only the guards and deploy everyone else. Whatever it is, do not let it reach here."

An excellent decision.

Being able to read the flow of the battlefield is such a powerful advantage.

However, Balkan soon realized his mistake.

'It has transcended the realm of emotion.'

As he tried to visualize the crowd spirit, Balkan's head gradually tilted back to the sky.

'Is it this big? No, it's bigger.' The monstrous form was too vast to fit even against the sky.

'Even more tenacious.'

Armed with a resolve that seemed unbreakable by anything.

His subordinate shouted.

"Sir! Look there!"

As Balkan hurriedly looked down, the actual form of the huge illusion came into view.

The beast, with Law-Slayer mounted as its engine and Shirone on its back, was charging toward them.

Its dark spines stretched out like spears, and demons exploded upon impact.

'Can it be stopped?'

Looking back, Balkan would blush at how foolish the question was.

"Damn it!"

If it could have been stopped, such an abnormal crowd spirit would not have been detected in the first place.

Shirone shouted towards the castle.

"Havitz-!"

As the multiple explosions of light from the mind of Elysion were cast, layers of light curtains overlapped around them.

"Krraek! Krraek!"

The bones of the demons twisted randomly before compressing into chunks of flesh.

'That cannot be blocked.'

Balkan climbed onto the beast's back and shouted.

"I'll go to His Majesty! Lead the way!"

A winged demon set out to find Havitz, and Balkan kicked the beast's flank.

"Run! We have no time!"

His stomach churned as if he were about to vomit.

'Keep calm. Havitz won't die.' No matter how many dice are rolled, how many arrows fall, or how powerful the enemy blocking the path.

'An event of death simply does not occur!'

Then what exactly is the terrifying crowd spiritchasing Balkan now?

Kraaaang!

As he turned his head at the echo of the monster, a darkness as sharp as a lance was encroaching all around.

'The laws.'

Balkan's eyes narrowed.

'The laws are changing.'

"Without Buddha, Yahweh is also useless?"

"He’s consuming Buddha! Damn it...!"

This is dangerous.

"Have I really gone mad? Seems like our actions are not to their liking."

The laws of the world and the chaos of Havitz were now wrestling over a single future.

"I can't guarantee this. This is not something I can guarantee."

The beast carrying Balkan galloped even faster.

"Kido!"

At Amon's strike, Kido crashed against the wall with a bang.

"Ugh..."

He struggled to stand, but the impact had permeated his body, causing his legs to tremble.

"It's okay. I can still hold on." It was a lie.

"He's really strong. I can't handle him alone. Unless all the Wind Guards rush in... The remaining 80 Wind Guards were currently scouting the capital for escape routes.

"I need to buy time."

"That's strange."

Amon, resting his sword on his shoulder, said,

"I've killed countless creatures that look like you. Goblins, was it? They had more guts than humans. But you hate humans, don't you? Or were they just food? Then why are you protecting humans?"

Why indeed?

Uorin had brought him to Kashan, but there was no reason to be on a sinking ship.

'Why am I fighting for this woman?'

For a goblin, humans are just food.

No matter how they dress up in clothes or jewelry, in the end, they're just wrapping paper around the meat.

'Such utter pretense.'

For Kido, whose life revolved around feeding and breeding, Uorin's existence was foolishly lavish and yet brilliant...

'Do you think you're something special? You know, you eat and shit just like any other animal! What's the difference?'

She was so beautiful.

'Dare I, the Goblin King, to wear a dog collar for you.'

Sometimes when bored, Uorin would put a rope around Kido's neck and tease him.

"Kido, bark for me, bark."

Honestly, it wasn't a bad feeling.

'Humans are just that, after all. I just sat there because it was so absurd. What's so fun about that?'

Kido recalled Uorin's crawling with a bitter smile.

"Damn it."

It seems I might just die here.

"Kiiii!"

As Kido rolled on the ground and activated his illusion magic, the earth turned swampy and spread out.

'I'll bind him with the earthbound spirit first...!'

While Amon's legs were stuck to the ground, Kido thrust his spear straight at him.

'I'll stab the heart!'

As Amon grasped the spear blade, sparks flew from his iron armor.

"Do you still have strength left? Give up. You can't beat me with your skills."

The corners of Kido's mouth lifted.

"Really?"

Illusion Magic - Dominion of the Origin.

The tightly held spear blade began to advance, ignoring the force holding it.

"This is??????"

"Nothing touches."

The atoms of the blade split the atoms of Amon, and finally reached his solar plexus.

"Ugh!"

A look of dismay contorted Amon's face.

'Got him.'

Confident as he pierced through Amon's armor, a chilling sensation washed over Kido.

'What is this?'

An indescribably twisted demonic energy was being transmitted through the spear.

"Kraaaa!"

Breaking free from the earthbound spirit, Amon plunged his sword into Kido's abdomen.

"Ugh!"

As the sword withdrew, Kido flinched and clutched his stomach, kneeling down.

"Not bad. To wound my magic directly. You could certainly surpass a brigade commander."

As Kido's face hit the ground, he scraped the earth with his nails in terrible pain.

'Damn it!'

As Kido fell, the color drained from Uorin's face.

"Is it over now?"

Haritz moved indifferently.

"Is this the first time since we met in the Holy War? Before, I was almost indebted to you. Too bad."

Uorin was known to have tried to kill Havitz using Law-Slayer.

And as a result, he had become Satan by sacrificing the person he loved most.

'Havitz. Utter evil. Chaos.'

Feeling the demonic energy emanating from Havitz, Uorin's shoulders trembled.

Like being swallowed by a giant snake's maw.

"What do you think I'll do to you? Think about it. What do you think I'll do?"

'Everything possible.'

But what truly frightened her was that whatever she could imagine, it would surpass that.

'I can't just die like this.'

From the dawn of human history to now, she had lived only to meet Shirone.

Havitz rummaged through his pocket as Uorin's determined eyes watched.

"But now, I wonder what's the point."

Confusion crossed Uorin's face.

"So let's create some meaning. Let's see which of us is stronger."

Havitz pulled out two six-sided dice and threw them at Uorin's feet.

The dice added up to 3 with a 2 and a 1.

"Roll them. If you roll higher than me, I die; if lower, you die."

It was a highly favorable chance for Uorin, but she looked pale as she gazed at the dice.

If a 2 had come up, she would have certainly thrown them.

'It's over.'

No matter what humiliation she might endure, as long as she was alive, she could meet Shirone.

'Human pleasures are of no concern to me anymore. Such things cannot satisfy.'

What Havitz wanted from Uorin was not merely physical pain.

'I can't win.'

Even without rolling the dice, she already knew through foresight what side would come up.

"Roll them. Before I just kill you."

As a look of despair crossed Uorin's face, Kido clenched his teeth and stood up.

"Stop talking nonsense."

"Kido?…"

"I can still hold on. Just stall until the Wind Guards arrive... Ugh!"

A bucketful of blood poured from Kido's mouth.

Realizing that waiting for death was all that remained, Havitz drew his sword.

"Last chance."

Uorin slowly bent over and reached for the dice.

'The moment I roll these dice... I die.'

Because no will or law outside of chaos can change that.

'The only way is...'

To follow the will of chaos.

Uorin's movement abruptly halted, and she knelt on the ground.

"Please save me."

Kido's eyes widened in surprise.

"You..."

"I was wrong. Please forgive me for being so rude all this time."

Kido's face contorted.

'What are you doing? Why are you begging for your life from such a guy? Aren't you the most noble woman in the world?'

Havitz asked.

"Why?"

Uorin raised her head.

"I have someone I love. I want to meet him. Please show mercy..."

"Stand up!"

Kido, vomiting blood, shouted.

"Stand up! I won't let you die! I'll protect you no matter what!" Smodo also said.

"Discarding your pride is admirable, but it won't change anything. No matter how much you beg..."

Havitz cut him off.

"Love, you say?"

"Your Majesty."

Uorin looked up into Havitz's eyes.

'The cold, terrifying eyes of chaos.'

If translated into human emotions, absolute loneliness was embedded in his pupils.
