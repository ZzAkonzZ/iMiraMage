Chapter 576:

Even after Shirone was called away by Siina, the Black and White game continued fiercely.

At the training ground behind the graduation class, Amy and Anchal clashed over a Scramble, both holding the same hand, ●● (Heretic), resulting in a draw.

Jincheon Sorcery - Serpent's Path.

A giant serpent coiled around Amy's body, biting her upper torso.

"Ugh!"

Though it was an illusion, the mental shock was intense, almost life-threatening.

The most troublesome part was that it was impossible to avoid.

'Red Eyes!'

Amy's eyes blazed red, resetting her mind, causing Anchal to frown.

'Self-image memory. This is tough.'

At school, the only person who could pressure her was Vice Principal Olivia, but Red Eyes, which nullified illusions, was the worst possible matchup.

Jincheon Sorcery - Sword Field.

Thousands of blades sprouted from the ground, swaying like grass, making Amy feel like her flesh was being shredded.

However, once Red Eyes was activated, any illusion would be reset.

As Amy punched through the Sword Field, Anchal caught her fist between her elbow and knee.

"Ugh!"

Though not Schema, it was the martial art of a trained expert.

'Eastern martial arts. Who is she really?'

As Amy regained her composure and created distance, Anchal extended her hand.

"I'll yield the Scramble."

"Don't be ridiculous!"

"If you want, I'll forfeit the game."

"What?"

Amy's movements halted.

"Further fighting is pointless. I just want to investigate Istas. I have no interest in school life."

Amy couldn't grasp Anchal's intentions.

'Come to think of it, she also proposed a private meeting with Shirone if she won the negotiation.'

If she lost, she agreed not to unseal her Magic Eye even in the graduation exam.

'There must be a clear purpose. Does she really not care about graduating?'

Amy asked bluntly,

"Who are you? Why is someone with your skills at this school?"

"I collect dolls…"

"Shut up! Who would believe that now?"

Anchal sighed.

"I can't reveal my purpose. But I want to make a deal. As I said, I don't mind forfeiting. You can discuss it with your team. But if you win…"

"No."

Amy cut her off.

"Everyone is fighting fiercely, even giving up their graduation evaluations. I don't know what you're up to, but I won't allow secret deals. So, fight with all you've got."

"Is that so?"

Lost in thought, Anchal muttered in her native language,

"I can't understand Western thinking."

"What are you mumbling?"

As Amy charged with her Red Eyes blazing, Anchal waved her hand.

Jincheon Sorcery - Mental Image Manifestation (Self-Portrait).

'Oh no!'

Flames erupted from Amy's body, forming a giant female figure.

It was a tricky ability that manifested the target's mental image to attack its owner.

'Is this my self-portrait?'

Amy looked up at the giant flaming woman.

The figure was intensely fiery and had a voluptuous form.

"…Self-images can be distorted by complexes."

Amy's face turned red.

"That's ridiculous! I'm fine as I am!"

At that moment, the flaming self-portrait swung its fist at Amy.

'Red Eyes!'

Amy was the only one who could nullify the self-portrait upon its manifestation, but Anchal had already retreated.

"What is this…"

The fact that Anchal disappeared without even taking the Scramble added credibility to her words.

'I can't figure out what she's thinking.'

"…I believe in your existence. I believe your love will change the world."

In the depths of a secluded forest, Eden prayed.

The contract with the alliance team was to create and protect Infinity until the last day of the seventh day.

In return, she was given a free role and was pushing her faith to the extreme for the decisive moment.

"God is omniscient, omnipotent, transcendent, and unique."

Thus, her body, imbued with Yore, was invincible.

"Lord! Use me according to your will."

Incomprehensible prayers poured from her mouth, and her body radiated a holy light.

Bang!

Siina slammed her hand on the desk and shouted,

"Do you have any sense? Fighting at school!"

"It wasn't a fight, it was a Scramble…"

"Quiet! You've been spoiled, and now you can't tell right from wrong?"

Siina was usually rational, but this time she left no room for argument.

"You're a walking strategic weapon! Don't you know that countries around the world are watching you? Did you come back from heaven just to play games with your friends?"

Siina was also part of the task force involved in the Heaven Project, which made Shirone feel hurt.

"It was a trap set by Frings. He tried to kill me, what was I supposed to do?"

"You'd die anyway."

Siina said coldly.

"You should be grateful that your actions affected the whole world. If there had been even a hint of humanity in your actions, you'd already be dead. Whether by the kingdom, an organization, or something hidden beneath the surface, you'd have been assassinated by now."

As Shirone hung his head, Siina's tone softened slightly.

"You know, right? You're not alive because something is protecting you. It's because everyone is your enemy that they can't act immediately. You're standing at the center of a hurricane with an incredibly narrow eye. If you deviate even slightly, you'll be swept away instantly."

Returning to school and spending time with friends felt like a dream.

Did he think this time could last forever?

"I'm sorry. I'll make sure this doesn't happen again."

Finally calming down, Siina crossed her arms and turned her chair.

"End it quietly."

"What?"

When Shirone asked, Siina frowned in displeasure.

"The principal knows about the Scramble Royal. While Olivia has significant influence in the teachers' council, she can't block all student channels. If it's within acceptable limits, the school will tolerate it. So, handle it quietly without causing more issues."

Blinking, Shirone understood her intent and replied,

"Yes, I understand."

"Now go. And remember, don't let this happen again. Understand?"

Bowing at the door, Shirone quietly left the room.

When Shirone returned to the battlefield, Iruki gathered everyone at the hideout.

At the halfway point of the fourth day of the Scramble Royal, several peculiarities were noted.

"Anchal proposed a secret meeting. I refused because it felt off, but I thought I should report it."

"As expected, Kaiden is out of the battlefield. I haven't seen him all day."

"He's probably receiving healing magic. He'll be back by the seventh day."

"One unusual thing is that Eden hasn't been active. She hasn't collected any Scrambles."

"She was wrecked by Shirone yesterday. Maybe she gave up?"

Iruki shook his head.

"Optimism isn't good. The enemy isn't stupid. They must be planning something."

Sabina said,

"Two members of the alliance team have stopped, but Pisho has started collecting Scrambles in earnest. Using insects, he's quickly securing them, so the card collection speed isn't much different despite the numbers."

"Since we confirmed that Scrambles can be moved by other factors, they'll use that actively. But we have Dorothy. Now that the second half has started, we need to consider the total number of cards we can collect. We'll keep storing Scrambles using Hickory. Can you do it?"

"…Yes."

Dorothy answered in a small voice.

Noticing her lack of enthusiasm, Iruki glanced at her but decided to focus on Shirone.

"So, what happened? You were called by Siina, right?"

Shirone shook his head bitterly.

"I can't enter any places where classes are being held."

Neid gritted his teeth.

"Damn, that's too much. They must have planned this."

"No, it's my fault. I can't cause trouble for the juniors."

Since Shirone accepted it, his friends had no choice but to agree.

"We'll have to adjust our actions. Knowing that Scrambles can be moved, the alliance team will target us persistently. Should we redesign our routes?"

"It's fine."

Shirone smiled.

"We'll finish before entering the training grounds."

That night, Etella visited Siina late.

"How is Shirone? He must have been really shocked today."

"I talked to him and sent him off."

Siina rubbed her feverish forehead.

"Sigh, I'm the worst teacher. I incited a fight."

"It couldn't be helped. It's not just a school issue."

Though they had returned from heaven, it was only a truce.

That's why world leaders were watching Shirone's actions.

"It's frustrating. I can't find a solution because I don't understand what's going on."

"Just do what you can, Siina. The experts are already moving."

-Do what you want. Become a great teacher.

Armin's voice echoed in her mind.

'He said that and then…'

Since then, there had been no contact.

Honestly, it wasn't Armin but Kuan who was driving her crazy.

'How could this happen? I sent so many letters.'

Without a single reply, she received a letter from Kaizen Swordsmanship School.

The polite language essentially told her to stop stalking since Kuan had left the school.

'Kuan said I could send letters! He's making me look like a weirdo!'

Though stressed, Siina's eyes softened.

'Where could he have gone?'

She didn't know, but one thing was certain.

He was likely in the middle of the world's most intense battlefield.

"Oh."

Realizing she had been lost in thought in front of someone, Siina adjusted her expression and spoke.

"Sorry about that. So, what brings you here? Is it about Shirone?"

"Partly, but there's also something I need to tell you."

"What is it?"

Etella's expression turned serious.

"I heard from Head Teacher Collie that Fermi didn't attend the evaluation today."

"Fermi? That's really unusual."

As the leader of the Golden Wheel, Fermi rarely moved personally, at least not in Siina's memory.

"Is it related to the Scramble Royal?"

"It might be, but there's a bigger issue—it's likely about Istas. The Golden Wheel members participating in the Scramble Royal are Hershey and Kaiden. And just before the most intense period, the fifth day, Fermi disappeared. I think this might be a personal issue for Fermi, not just the Golden Wheel."

"So, it's something secretive enough to divert the members' attention to the Scramble Royal."

"Yes, that's the strong impression I get."

"Hmm."

Siina, sitting on the desk, pondered for a moment before turning back to Etella.

"What exactly is in Istas? I thought it was over when Miro's dimension disappeared."

Seeing Etella hesitate, Siina pressed further.

"Please tell me. I'm not part of the task force anymore, but I need to know something to protect Shirone, don't I?"

Etella seemed to make up her mind and spoke.

"Alright. But don't get too deeply involved."

Siina asked the question that had been on her mind the most.

"The rumors from the higher-ups. Are they true?"

Everything that shouldn't exist in this world does exist.

"To put it simply, they're not entirely false."

Siina's expression turned blank.

"Really… such things exist? What are they?"

Etella recalled what she had heard after returning from heaven.

"According to what Miro told me…"

A long story that would stretch into the night began.
