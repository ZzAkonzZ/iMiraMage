Chapter 951: The Age of History (2)

The army of heaven toured the planet.

Once along the axis of rotation, twice along the equator and the prime meridian...

'Can't find it.'

There was no Buddha anywhere.

'Unless we're incompetent...'

Shura's gestalt dominates lies, but in the face of amplification, it's like child's play.

'Is it avoiding us on purpose?' That was a sufficient possibility.

When memories were amplified 200,000,000 times, Ikael regained all lost memories.

Even what Anke La did to her.

'Of course, I resent it too.'

But I didn't feel the need to tear it apart or seek revenge.

'Because I have no heart.'

All of Anke Ra's judgments were just cold means to maintain the world.

'But Anke La has now become human. If I meet a human named Nane, what should I do? No, how will he treat me?'

Satiel watched Ikael, who was deep in thought.

'She definitely regained his memories.'

Of course, other archangels had some idea about Goffin's erasure.

The mental state of an angel is the purest, and even the slightest crack in the world causes discomfort.

'The whole is just the whole, but the first reset is definitely different.'

Also, the fact that angels felt discomfort was evidence that another entity had reset in Anke Ra's world.

'If this feeling is true, I would be an enemy to Ikael.'

Satiel used the power of nostalgia to break down memories to the smallest units and collect fragments.

'McClain Goffin. Jealousy. Ikael's sin.'

Just connecting the words at the level of delusion made her shiver and gave her goosebumps.

'But why? Why doesn't she say anything to me?'

Even without clear memories, it was obvious that she had done something very bad to Ikael.

'Did she really regain his memories? If amplification makes it possible, then it's not impossible for me either.'

So it was a question.

Why is it that only Ikael can remember everything about Goffin's erasure?

"Stop."

At Ikael's command, the army of heaven stopped.

Photon signals reflected the common sky scenery, but to Ikael, it was a childish lie.

"Come out."

The sky bulged, and Shura appeared.

"I came under Buddha's command."

"Shura."

Ranked 7th in the Council of Ten.

Even though the immortal of heaven couldn't be unaware of the archangel's authority, Shura did not show respect.

'Anke Ra is not here.'

The administrator of the world was a human named Nane.

'Just to understand the heart.'

The determination of Anke Ra, who had fallen from the status of a god to a mere creature, gave her goosebumps again.

"Under Buddha's command? You should choose your words carefully. The only one who can command us...

"Follow me."

As if there was no need to consider Ikael's opinion, Shura turned coldly.

The chief of the flower tribe, Protea, was closing her eyes in pain.

'We keep losing flowers. We can't hold out for long.'

The strategy of the flower tribe was this.

If 300 flower tribe members controlled 300 flowers, Protea would oversee the whole from the center of the flower field.

Therefore, the priority for the demons was to eliminate Protea, but in reality, they didn't care.

"Hahaha! I caught it! It's mine!"

They were engrossed in tormenting the humans, flower tribe, and elves in front of them.

"Damn it!"

Despite the overwhelming numbers, Lupist eventually gave instructions to Plu.

"Pull back the defense line! Protect Protea!"

The allied forces quickly moved and reestablished the defense line 300 meters in front of Protea.

Creaking sounds of breaking steel flowers were heard from all directions, and the ground shook with a thud.

"Elves! Catch the elves! They're a delicacy!"

Elves were the most popular prey for demons.

"No! Go away!"

Although they were more immune to fear than humans, once their hearts were broken, their emotions were fully exposed.

"You bastards!"

Seeing her kin being ravaged, Enox raised her sword and charged.

"Huh?"

A fireball flew from the sky.

The fireball, resembling the sun up close, did not arrive even as the demons watched.

"Oh?"

And only when their skin began to melt did they realize it was a large sphere over 20 meters in diameter.

"Ahhh! It's hot!"

As the fireball struck the ground, Enox retreated with the elves.

"Are you trying to kill us all?"

At the same time, Lupist blocked the rear of the elves and erected a 40-meter-high iron wall.

"Roar!"

The hot air was temporarily blocked, but soon the iron wall began to turn red like lava.

"Water magic!"

At Enox's shout, water spirits cast ancient magic.

"Ascol!"

The blood of demons and humans evaporated in an instant, and a torrential downpour fell over the area.

Sizzling sounds were heard as the iron cooled, and the temperature dropped enough to breathe, causing the iron wall to collapse.

The power was so great that it endangered even the allies, but thanks to it, the demon's advance line was pushed back 700 meters.

Lupist's eyes deepened.

'As rumored.'

There was only one person here who could use such powerful fire magic.

Garcia arrived through spatial movement.

"Are you okay?"

Feeling that the name of the world's greatest fire mage was not in vain, Lupist nodded.

"Thanks to you. How is the situation?"

"Not good. We lost 20% of the Valkyrie forces."

Considering that the allied forces lost 50% and the elves 30%, they were indeed the world's elite.

"But the biggest loss is that the first legion commander was taken down. We won't be able to defend the flower field like this."

Lupist's eyebrows rose.

'Pidero is dead? The swordsman known as the reincarnation of the great sword master Kadel?'

It was certainly shocking news, but on the other hand, anything could happen on the battlefield.

"It's up to the chairman to decide. Whether to retreat and plan the next strategy or to defend this place."

Garcia had a higher operational rank.

But he thought it was better to leave it to his country's commander when the fate of Tormia was at stake.

"Hmm."

Lupist rested his chin on his hand.

'If we retreat, we save our forces. But if we withdraw from here, do we have a future left?'

The flower field was the reason they could confront the demons despite the asymmetry in power.

Which is more efficient, humans or machines?

"All troops!"

Lupist shouted.

If they gave up the ancient weapon, they would never be able to inflict such damage on the demons again.

"Retreat!"

Without humans, there is no system.

That was Lupist, and Garcia respected his decision.

"Valkyrie! All troops retreat!"

The demons drooled as they watched the allied forces flee.

"Hahaha! This is the best part!"

The thrill of seeing the despair in the eyes of fleeing enemies was exhilarating.

"No! No!"

The screams of their allies, the familiar voices of despair, pierced the hearts of the allied forces.

"Roar!"

Their jaws clenched so tightly their teeth almost broke.

"Run! Keep running!"

But in the end, someone had to be caught for others to escape.

"Just wait! You bastards!"

As they retreated with tears of blood, the roar of spatial movement cut through the sky.

"What?"

Judging by the length of the sound wave, it was magic cast from an incredible distance.

Lupist's gaze followed to the depths of the demon's camp.

"Why?"

The distance was too far.

If the coordinates were wrong, they could only hope for a painless death.

The demons surrounding the spot where the flash fell also looked bewildered.

"Who's this kid?" A slender girl with short hair like a boy muttered quietly.

"Fire system."

Burns everything.

Contrary to her soft voice, a massive flame rose around her, piercing the sky.

"Ahhh!"

As the fire vortex danced amidst the screams of agony, the allied forces were stunned.

"No way?"

As Garcia's eyes shone with familiarity, the flames dispersed, revealing a scorched land.

"Amy????"

Amy stood there, untouched by the firestorm.

Only Garcia, the greatest fire mage, could guess what it meant.

Complete combustion.

'It's not magic. It's a mental state. It requires an extreme understanding of fire.'

It's a very difficult thing.

Knowing the essence of fire is akin to saying only those who have burned to death understand it.

Amy's body seemed to ignite for a moment, then she appeared in front of the allied forces' camp.

'Magic that uses concepts, not phenomena. It requires a unique understanding of fire...

It was also a privilege of the great mage.

Why fire magic needed spatial movement was unknown, but it had its uses.

"Lieutenant Karmis Amy, returning to the battlefield."

As Garcia watched seriously, Tess ran over with Rian.

"Amy!"

Tess hugged Amy so tightly she could hardly breathe, tears streaming down her face.

"I thought you were really dead! What happened!"

"Wait, wait, Tess...

"And why is your hair like this? You look like a boy. But you're still pretty."

"Lieutenant."

Only when she heard Garcia's voice did Tess come to her senses and step back in surprise.

"Oh, I'm sorry!"

On the other hand, Amy met Garcia's gaze with a meaningful smile.

'Master’

She knew that he was the one who had been waiting for her return more than anyone else.

"If you've survived twice, it can't be just luck."

Garcia, after briefly scanning the enemies ahead, turned back to Amy and said,

"The position of the first legion commander is vacant. Take it."

"?????? What?"

Amy, unable to process what she had heard, asked again, but Garcia's words remained brief.

"From this moment, you are the commander of the Valkyrie First Legion."

"Gasp!"

Tess covered her mouth, and the soldiers around them murmured in shock.

"The first legion commander... that's a major general, isn't it?" Even though the world was on the brink of destruction, it was an extraordinary promotion.

Amy's voice trembled.

"Commander..."

"As you know, it might just be an empty title. This place could become our grave. But the weight of the responsibility a commander must bear is the same. There's nothing to gain and much to lose. Will you still do it?"

Tears welled up in Amy's eyes.

"Yes!"

As she clenched her fists, her pupils began to burn with a red light.

'I did it. I really did it.'

In that brief moment, her entire life flashed before her eyes.

She had failed the first graduation exam at the magic school, graduated fourth out of ten in the second exam, and joined the Tormia army and the Valkyrie with a less-than-promising report card.

The training was brutal, and Garcia's guidance was impersonal and harsh.

'Thank you, Beric.'

Even if she had overcome all that, without Beric, today would not have been possible.

'And...'

Amy pulled out the precious memory she had kept even in extreme pain.

'Shirone.'

As the commander of the Valkyrie First Legion, she could earn the title of Arch Mage just by applying her reputation points.

Since she was actually a Arch Mage who had mastered the fire system, the Red Line grading system was reasonable.

'I can meet him.'

The night she had to let Shirone go right before her eyes, how much she had resented her own weakness.

'I can fight alongside him.'

As Tess watched Amy, whose red eyes were burning, she sent her thoughts,

'Congratulations.'

Chapter 952: The Age of History (3)

To Goffin, who had lived for eons, Miro was just a child, but he acknowledged her exceptional insight and unwavering will towards righteousness.

'She is someone who can be entrusted with the world.'

He liked that her self-esteem was sky-high and that she had a completely open mind.

'The only problem is...'

Is Miro prepared to sacrifice everything for the world?

"I'll do it. After all, there's no one else but me. Unless there's another way."

If it's the only way to solve the problem, a magician can lay down their life.

"No, that's not enough. It's not a role you can take on just because you can or want to."

She might have to endure alone for a lifetime, perhaps much longer than a human lifespan.

"The human heart has no limits, but it can also break easily. If you regret it even a little, humanity will fall into the worst situation."

"Even if it's something that requires the heart, not the head..."

Miro, with her arms crossed and supporting her chest, swayed her upper body from side to side.

'It's in her nature.'

He liked how she didn't lose her sense of humor even in desperate situations.

"Who would willingly take on such a task? I'm doing it because there's no one else. It's a matter that needs to be approached coldly, and I can endure it."

"For how long?"

Miro closed her mouth.

"I know you're amazing. 100 years? No, you could endure for 1,000 years. If I told you to hold on until then. But this is different. You have to fight the thought that you might be alone forever. No matter how vast the space, it will feel like being trapped in a coffin, and in that state, your sanity won't remain intact. I guarantee you'll go mad within 10 years."

Miro, unable to deny it, asked,

"What do you want me to do?"

When Goffin extended his palm, a hexagon of Hexa appeared and turned into a metal box.

"Metagate. It's equipment from heaven. It stores coordinates and bends space. Currently, this planet is distorted by the dimensional wall, so you can't return without this."

"Are you telling me to go to heaven?"

"I'll give you a year. You need to see and feel it yourself. It's not enough to just have reason. Without a mission to accomplish it, I can't make you my successor."

As Goffin handed over the Metagate, he added,

"Never let it be taken. Of course, it won't be easy to break through the dimensional wall, but a system without a manager will eventually be conquered. That's why someone has to be trapped in eternal solitude."

Miro, staring intently at the Metagate, looked up.

"I'm not complaining, but why can't you do it? You could be the manager."

"I have a greater mission."

It was a short answer, but Miro didn't ask further.

"Then, I'll go. Where should I go?"

"Galliante."

A year later, a black sphere appeared in the warrior's resting place prepared in Galliante.

Miro, who had returned home through space, looked more emaciated than a year ago.

Goffin, who was sitting on the bed, closed his book and asked,

"Was it an enjoyable trip?"

Goffin, observing Miro's eyes staring silently ahead, realized that it wasn't just her appearance that had changed.

'She's ready.'

A sense of mission that transcended reason was imbued in her.

"I'd like to hear your impressions."

The first words Miro uttered in response to Goffin's question were,

"No one should approach heaven. At least, humanity cannot handle the truth of the world right now."

Goffin nodded obediently.

"Have you made your decision?"

"I'll do it. I'll manage the dimensional wall and prevent the existence of heaven from entering here."

Miro turned her body outward.

"The Goffin's gate here is the same. If someone unqualified goes to heaven, it could provide an excuse for invasion. At the very least, I should set up a trial gate."

Goffin extended his hand as if to encourage her.

"Do as you wish. From now on, the world is yours to protect. The method is also your freedom."

Miro created a trial gate to judge the qualifications of those going to heaven.

Goffin prepared to leave.

"Then, see you on the promised day. I'll take care of Istas. And this might be meddling, but..."

After hesitating for a moment, he said,

"If there's something you think you'll regret later, don't hesitate and do it now. If there's someone you have feelings for, convey your heart. Share love."

"That's really meddling."

Goffin chuckled.

"I don't think you'll make a mistake. It's just... leaving regrets behind would be too sad."

It would be unbearably lonely.

'Regrets.'

A face of someone appeared in Miro's mind.

When she didn't open her mouth for a long time, Goffin asked as if he knew.

"Is there... someone?"

Miro, snapping out of her thoughts, answered firmly,

"No."

Although the judgment of the 20-member committee had not yet been made, the result was already anticipated.

The world would push her into cold solitude, and if that was her fate...

'I'll just accept it.'

This time, Miro asked,

"Don't worry about Istas. There's a capable helper. But what about you?"

"I have to prepare to leave."

Unlike a year ago, this time Goffin turned his body first.

"It won't take long."

The first place Goffin visited was the lifeless zone in the southern hemisphere of heaven.

The name was given because no living creatures were there, but in reality, it was a place where forgotten beings resided.

"Karatorsa."

In the darkness without sunlight, a huge presence passed by, and soon after, a radiance was born.

The majesty of the dragon without horns, looking down at Goffin with its massive body, was always awe-inspiring.

"Goffin."

Behind the dimming light of Karatorsa, the silhouettes of the 12 apostles were faintly visible.

'They are still a strong race.'

The fact that they were so powerful without any special evolution since the early days of Omega.

'It means they are perfect.'

At least, they were the most perfect creatures that Argones and Gaia could create.

"What is the reason for stepping into the forbidden realm?"

The dragons waited in the lifeless zone because they were allowed to continue their species by Argones.

"Don't be so cold. You were able to rest because we prevented the reset."

Karatorsa wanted to get to the point.

"So? Why did you come?"

"I was thinking of doing the reset this time."

Karatorsa's eyes gleamed with hostility.

"Human, I dislike jokes. If you're serious, it means you've come to fight us."

"On the contrary, I want to make a deal. But first, I want to ask, does the apostle of time forget?"

"Hmph, don't compare us to the imperfect thoughts of humans. We are the coldest race. We don't distort any information."

All information is stored as a combination of 0s and 1s.

"Then you can view our history neutrally. Help me, help humanity."

Karatorsa brought his face closer.

"We are not on the side of gods or humans. We only prevent the intentions of specific beings from interfering with the flow of time."

"That's what I want. A world that never changes, no matter what I do."

"Your understanding is lacking."

Karatorsa was frustrated.

"If you succeed in intervening, it means Anke Ra can do it too. The existing time will collapse, and it will be uncontrollable."

"What if I told you I could transmit the log of the Akashic Records to you?"

The eyes of the 12 apostles lit up, and Karatorsa, who had been staring blankly, asked again.

"The log of the Akashic Records? Are you talking about all the events and movements of every atom since the beginning?"

"Yes."

"How?"

Karatorsa's voice was provocative.

"I know you can implement external signals. It's called Idea. But in the end, Idea is also a signal created within this world. And the Akashic Records belong to the one who dreams of this world, only Anke Ra. You can't reach it."

"I can."

Goffin pointed to the sky.

"I'll shoot it directly from outside."

"...Outside?"

Karatorsa, lifting his head vertically, imagined the universe at the end of the dark sky.

After a long silence, he muttered,

"Go outside the universe... and from there... transmit the log to me?"

"Yes. I don't know what kind of world it is, but I'll make it happen. I'll hack the log and send it to you. In return, if I succeed, you must protect the flow of time based on the log of the Akashic Records. No variables."

"Photon system departure. If you disappear..."

Karatorsa's brain began to spin rapidly.

"Anke Ra will have the only obstacle removed. Then he will attempt a reset and try to correct all the mistakes he made."

"Yes. You have to stop him."

"But there's a question."

Karatorsa looked down at Goffin again.

"Even if we protect time, who will stop Anke Ra? You won't be here."

"Hexa."

Goffin smiled.

"Ikael and my child will be in this world. No name. I won't give one."

Because they don't even deserve to be parents.

"When the child is ready to handle the truth of the world, I want you to pass on everything to them. The beginning and the end of this world. The Alpha and the Omega."

Karatorsa became more realistic.

"Anke Ra won't leave it alone. Hexa will be the first to be eliminated in the reset."

"That's why I want to do it first. Hexa has implemented the signal from the outside world. If we initialize it, we can remove the cause itself. Then Anke Ra won't be able to touch it. The preparations are almost complete. The dimensional wall and the successor to guard it..."

"Wait. Hold on."

Karatorsa interrupted.

"If you attempt a reset while detached from the log, you won't exist in the second era. There will be underlying events. Users will experience déjà vu, and angels will feel a sense of dissonance."

"Yes. There will be cracks in the Akashic Records. I've left ruins with my name on them. It's not just for this, but it will help you resolve the contradictions of history."

"Hmm. So, rather than confusing the users, you want to make them accept the reset itself."

"That's the only solution. Even if you try to stop me, I will reset. It's better to have the log I transmit than to head into it blindly."

A long time passed.

"...Understood."

The 12 apostles descended to the ground in human form, and Karatorsa stood up fully.

"The apostle of time has promised."

"Thank you."

As Goffin turned and Hexa's light shone, Karatorsa asked,

"Are you confident you won't feel empty?"

"Looking at this world from the outside might just be a single signal. Can you still love this place, even if everything that was precious to you turns out to be a lie?"

"I can."

As the last descendant of the brilliant race that achieved an integrated mental system.

"Even if it's a lie, even if there's something more void beyond it, I can love it."

Goffin placed a hand on his chest.

"Because everything I experienced, loved, and felt here is real. It will remain within me forever."

It's not about leaving to leave.

"I'm ready. It's time to return with what I've realized in this world. And that is..."

Goffin's body began to dissolve into particles of light.

"A god with a heart."

As his voice lingered in the air, Karatorsa fell into deep thought.

"A god with a heart..."

He thought the world was continuously converging into endless voids, creating new worlds.

"Going up in reverse."

Beyond infinity.

So that a god with a heart can dwell in every world.
