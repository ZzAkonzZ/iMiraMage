Chapter 506: Wholehearted Resolve (5)

“Hoo.”

Plop.

As the Spirit Zone surged toward Shirone, the surrounding landscape refracted inward as if it had been trapped inside a droplet of water.

“Haa.”

Plop.

As she exhaled, the Spirit Zone expanded outward, reaching into the world. Objects around her stretched like rubber bands, creating a palpable tension.

‘Inhale.’

Plop.

‘Exhale.’

Plop.

With each of Shirone’s breaths, the world pulsed in unison. With every rhythmic beat, the mental energy that had been rushing toward detonation, triggered by the Ataraxia Incarnation, began to gradually stabilize, regaining a proper form.

“This… this can’t be…”

Ikael trembled, overwhelmed by an unnameable emotion. For a human’s mind to resonate with the objects of the world was characteristic of Elysion. But what truly pacified Shirone’s explosive mind was his own unique awakening.

This was clearly a realm beyond ordinary human comprehension.

‘He has reached Prajna.’

Just a year ago, when Ataraxia was first taught to her, Shirone had been a mere beginner, unable to delve deeply into her mind. Of course, even the Vajra state of mind was a level unreachable by ordinary teenagers, but Prajna was on an entirely different plane of mental mastery. By the standard of what Ikael deemed an average human’s potential, it was a realm requiring over a thousand years of disciplined practice—yet Shirone had crossed that chasm in a single leap.

Plop. Plop. Plop.

The contraction and expansion of the Spirit Zone accelerated. Drdrdrdrdrdr! The pulses grew faster and faster, striking the world repeatedly as if driven by centripetal force.

Finally, Shirone’s eyes snapped open.

Mental Transcendence—Prajna.

As the Spirit Zone vanished, golden heat waves burst from Shirone like flames. His entire incarnation manifested into reality, transcending mental and spiritual form.

‘That’s…!’

The first thing to catch attention was Armand’s incarnation. Unlike its previous monstrous form, Armand’s now-merged form with Shirone appeared as smooth, gleaming golden armor that looked as though molten gold had been poured over it.

Radiating even brighter than Arman, Shirone’s incarnation glowed brilliantly, and colossal wings of light unfurled from her shoulders. A being who had shed all desires and comprehended their true incarnation—this was Prajna.

Hoooom!

At the sight of Prajna, black aura surged from Faiel’s body, shaking the skies above.

‘Prajna?’

It was a state so profound that he had never considered the possibility of a human reaching it. This was also why Ankera had been so determined to suppress it. While Prajna was rare even among the ageless subjects, those few who did attain it were strictly controlled by Anke Ra’s will. They had formed a resistance group known as the Council of Ten, leading the Third Heaven, Shehakim.

‘Prajna must be eliminated.’

Faiel’s body dissolved into black smoke and shot forward, but Ikael, despite her weakened state, stood in his path.

Even as Faiel’s face loomed before her like a void ready to devour everything, and though she knew there was no chance of victory, Ikael’s feet remained firmly planted on the ground.

Screeech!

As Faiel’s attack came to pierce Ikael’s torso, a golden flash twisted through the air, intercepting him. Shirone’s incarnation, layered over his body like a phantom, cast a spell toward Faiel.

Kaa-booom!

A massive photon cannon erupted forward, blasting through the ground like waves. The solid earth rippled as if liquid, and Faiel’s silhouette flickered like ash within the white brilliance.

“Grrr…”

When the light cleared, Faiel stood with his hands crossed in front of him, groaning in pain. The energy that had once threatened to explode had now fully concentrated within Shirone’s incarnation, amplifying its power to an even greater degree.

“Faiel! Stop this madness! We can’t afford to lose another archangel!” Satiel arrived belatedly, shouting, but Faiel’s mind had already been consumed by the singular goal of Shirone’s destruction.

Hoooom!

Faiel’s white robe scorched from the heat as his body turned into pure darkness, lunging at Shirone. No longer an angel, what remained was only Faiel’s will of annihilation, bearing down on Shirone’s incarnation, which flickered as if about to extinguish.

‘I won’t forgive you.’

Shirone charged head-on into the death-laden darkness.

Ataraxia was not an option. The concept of amplification still worked in Faiel’s favor.

‘I won’t forgive you!’

But he had one last weapon.

Valhalla Action!

With the unfolding of his halo, the trigger that reversed cause and effect was activated.

‘I will end this…’

Shirone gritted his teeth. Ikael’s scream, her tears, her pain—all of it flashed through Shirone’s mind in an instant, fueling her resolve.

“I will never forgive you!”

As his fading incarnation reignited, the darkness swirled and let out a ghastly howl.

Hoooom!

An incarnation holds the sum of one’s entire existence. As Arin had once discovered with her first awakening, Shirone’s incarnation embodied light, and its law of illumination tore through the darkness.

“Grrraaah!”

As Shirone thrust his hands forward, black smoke trailed behind, gathering between his fingers. In moments, the smoke condensed, reforming Faiel’s body clad once more in angelic robes.

“Heh… heheheh…”

Even as Shirone’s grip tightened around his neck, Faiel let out a grotesque chuckle.

It was a laugh born of disbelief.

‘So, this is Prajna…’

Before today’s war, he could not have imagined such a situation.

Who could have predicted that he, the archangel most adept at guiding souls to death, would be driven to this point by a mere human?

‘But in the end, you’re still human.’

A twisted grin spread across Faiel’s face.

“You look frustrated.”

Shirone’s expression contorted at his mocking tone.

Even now, Valhalla Action awaited an input for a definitive outcome, but no matter how he thought about it, she couldn’t find a way to annihilate Faiel.

“Yes, that’s your limit.”

Even Faiel couldn’t deny Shirone’s power. His abilities—the archangel’s Ataraxia, the angelic Valhalla Movement, the cursed sword Arman enhanced through unified will, the immense mental strength of Immortal Function, and her mastery of divine particles unique to unlockers—all combined into a form that reached Prajna.

Yet, it still wasn’t enough to destroy Faiel.

“You’re only human. Impressive struggle, but that’s all it is—a struggle.”

Raising his voice as if to restore his bruised pride, Faiel sneered.

“Even if you give your all, you’ll never reach the mind of an angel!”

At Faiel’s uncharacteristic outburst, Shirone’s eyes grew cold, sharper than absolute zero.

“No.”

His golden incarnation expanded further, enveloping his entirely.

“Not yet.”

Valhalla Action resumed its rapid rotation. With blazing eyes, Shirone clenched his teeth and entered the final result.

‘I need more power.’

Valhalla Action accepted his boundless desire without hesitation.

‘Stronger! More! More! More!’

As his desire resonated with the Akashic Records, a result capable of annihilating Faiel emerged. The price required for this result was a staggering 127 years, 9 months, 17 days, 43 minutes, and 5 seconds of her life.

Even with such an inefficient exchange rate, Shirone remained unwavering.

‘I don’t care.’

As soon as he approved, Valhalla Action began reversing cause and effect.

“I don’t care… as long as I can destroy you.”

That was Shirone’s wholehearted resolve—his wholehearted devotion.

Valhalla Action—Photon Cannon.

Kuaaaaaaaang!

A blinding flash erupted in front of Shirone. From afar, it would appear as a beam of light streaking across the heavens, but to those nearby, it was more like a massive wall advancing forward.

“Graaah!”

With his neck gripped tightly, Faiel raised both hands in defense. But faced with the overwhelming force of the photon cannon, even an archangel had no choice but to be overwhelmed.

Hoooom!

First, his left arm fell away, followed by his right arm and legs, torn apart in an instant. Even as his entire body disintegrated, the dark energy comprising him writhed in futile resistance before it, too, vanished.

Without even being pushed back 100 meters, Faiel was reduced to nothing more than a speck of dust, and thus, he was obliterated from existence.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

The photon cannon fired from the central Aravoth of Heaven stretched toward the fairy city of Makonom. From there, it continued to devastate numerous districts, breaking through the outer walls of Heaven and disappearing into Purgatory.

“Fa… Faiel…?”

Satiel shuddered with dread. Within the remnants of the blinding light that had severed Heaven, not a trace of Faiel’s presence could be found.

A human had obliterated an archangel. A being classified as no more than a subject in Heaven’s hierarchy had utterly annihilated one of its supreme entities.

‘So that’s it… In the end, Ra’s will…’

Only now did Satiel understand what Metiel had been trying to convey in her final moments before her death.

‘Humans have climbed all the way to our level.’

There was a time when even Mara had been worshipped as a god by humans. Every event that occurred in Heaven had once been their mythology. But now, the veil of that myth was beginning to lift.

‘Satan.’

Satiel snapped back to her senses and turned toward Zebul. With Faiel’s annihilation, the Law of Evil had undoubtedly grown stronger.

“We must find Raiel!”

Kariel was on the brink of destruction, and Ikael was incapacitated. They needed to gather as many angels as possible, if only to prevent the situation from worsening.

‘Before Ra’s will is invoked…!’

Having made her decision, Satiel shot a brief glare at Ikael before spreading her wings of light and soaring upward.

Rumble.

As if to confirm her fears, structures in the distant Zebul were crumbling and breaking apart.

“Those wretched demons…!”

Satiel’s eyes gleamed with killing intent as she spotted something.

Metiel’s nemesis. Amid the billowing gray smoke, an enormous mass of red flesh was rising like a towering monstrosity.
