Chapter 1080: Fully Armed (2)

The End of Probability

When you assume rolling a die infinitely, what exists beyond that number.

'Now!'

It was a solid and clear certainty, as if the whole world was converging into a single point.

'Michelangelo!'

The sphere of light stretched into a flash and shot straight into Maginus' open mouth.

"Kaaa…!"

The time until the next change felt like an eternity, then Maginus convulsed violently.

It was 10 meters in front of Yahweh2.

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

Internal chain explosions began, causing the machine to writhe unrealistically.

As the mafia group watched in awe, Maginus suddenly changed direction.

"Chase it! It's the final pattern!"

Maginus, creating an ice tunnel, finally disintegrated under the sea.

As cold air sprayed in all directions, a huge ice cavity formed, and an item dropped there.

"Whoa!"

The mafia group, arriving at the cavity, checked the augmented reality and felt their hair stand on end.

Unidentified item drop: Legendary grade.

"Again, it's here!"

If it was a legendary item obtained by hunting Maginus, it was undoubtedly Donatello.

Knowing the priority system, Shirone turned away instead of taking the box.

"Shall we draw cards again this time?"

The mafia group had a hunch.

'It's not a coincidence.'

Though they couldn't guess the method, it was clear Yahweh2 could control probability.

Therefore, if he wanted, he could draw legendary items as much as he wanted.

'Once equipped, it can't be traded, so the price fluctuation would be minimal. He'll rake in the galaxy.'

The mafia, finishing their thoughts, spoke.

"Priority doesn't matter anyway. You take it. But we need to change the conditions."

This was the last chance since they couldn't trade after the event ended.

"Change the conditions?"

"We don't need the settlement. Instead, let's go one more round. You can draw legendary items, right? Can't you match one for each of us? We've been together all this time, so it's only fair."

"Sorry, that's not possible."

Sona asked.

"Why? It's so easy for you! It's even better than giving us the galaxy."

"I'm an outsider. I don't want to disrupt the balance of this world. I'll leave after the Solar War."

Dex Sensibility retorted.

"That's ridiculous. We're doing this because of the Solar War too. Do you know how much the charging fee difference is?"

Though the West Country wouldn't lose in this Solar War, there was no need to reveal it in advance.

"I'll compensate you adequately. It will benefit you too. Getting too involved will be troublesome."

Too many forces were already entangled in the confrontation between Shirone and the operator.

'Tch! Is it because he's from the East Country?'

Though it was obvious they were concerned about the Solar War, it was better to endure until the settlement was over.

"Fine. Then contact Golden Wheel as soon as we get out. Settle it immediately."

"I don't have a long-distance communication option. How about we meet in the neutral zone first?"

"Are you kidding? Why say that now?"

Was it necessary to mention it?

"Well, you all know my level. I thought it was obvious."

"Ugh."

If they didn't know, it would hurt their pride.

Sona suggested.

"Then we'll contact them. Meeting in the neutral zone is impossible. If a large guild like Golden Wheel takes action, we'll be helpless. It's too disadvantageous."

"Okay. Then contact them once we're out. You can reach Yolga's son or Aegis."

Of course, they knew the ranker's code names.

As the mafia group designated Yahweh2 as the priority, Shirone obtained the box.

Everyone gathered.

"Look."

As the box opened, a smooth porcelain bottle was in Yahweh2's hand.

"Oh! This is…"

The event has ended.

The sound of waves was heard.

The starting point was a beautiful white sand beach on the outskirts of Moon Island, not the freshwater lake.

Shirone sighed in relief.

"Phew, it's finally over."

Regardless of the difficulty, hunting in the West Country itself was a burdensome journey.

Sona approached.

"Congratulations. Will you equip it right away this time too?"

Shirone looked at the bottle in his hand.

"Yes, I should."

Though he didn't know about other users, it was mentally comforting to equip it immediately.

"Equip."

The porcelain shook and broke on its own, and a clear liquid metal like mercury floated in the air.

"Wow…"

As everyone admired, the liquid metal fiercely rushed into Yahweh2's mouth.

It was an irresistible system, and his whole body felt cold as if he had entered icy water.

Of course, it wasn't a bad feeling.

Installing new program.

A message appeared in augmented reality, and the existing scenery felt entirely different.

"Huh? This…"

It was a familiar feeling to Shirone.

'Spirit Zone.'

It was as if all the surrounding light was bending in, leaving no blind spots in his vision.

Sona asked.

"Are you okay? It was hard to tell from the video. It made me dizzy."

'Of course.'

The brain had to adapt, not the eyes.

'The recorded video only outputs what the eyes see. It would be an image of fragmented scenery overlapping in layers. If such an image moved in real-time, even I would get dizzy…'

In Shirone's brain, it was organized into a perfect spherical landscape.

Of course, this was far from the usual concept of 'seeing.'

Sona asked.

"Can you move? In the video, people staggered and fell as if they were drunk, even those over level 300."

Dex Sensibility added.

"I saw that too. It caused Donatello's price to drop temporarily. Legendary items vary greatly in efficiency depending on skill, but this was extreme."

Shirone understood.

It was like giving Spirit Zone to an ordinary person whose concentration couldn't handle it.

"Still, there are people who can handle it well, right?" Sona said.

"Since the number released is so small, one of the representative users in the West Country is Yagwi. I saw him dodging barrages in the Solar War, and it was amazing."

'Yagwi.'

He was a member of Ascension.

'Could he be a real-life magician? Well, if someone has high concentration, they might adapt.'

The mafia sneered.

"You've been standing still for a while. Are you dizzy? I told you not to equip it recklessly."

"Hmm."

As Shirone took a step forward, the mafia group, who had been mocking him internally, was startled.

"Let's see…"

As he sprinted, the machine sped across the white sand beach at high speed.

The visual information was overwhelming.

But for Shirone, who could handle not just Spirit Zone but also Elysion, it wasn't a hindrance.

'This is good. This should offset the output difference with the operator to some extent.'

At least he wouldn't lose his blind spots.

Turning again, Shirone tested precise movements beyond just sprinting.

As he performed circus-like dazzling maneuvers, the mafia group was momentarily stunned.

"What? He's running so well! Is this really the scene we saw in the video?"

"Probably. Yagwi does that too."

As silence continued, Shirone suddenly braked and stopped in front of the group.

"Phew, test over. This is really good."

The mafia felt a surge of anger.

"Well, I don't know about you, but Donatello is a difficult part to handle. The reason people go crazy for it is because of its special function."

"Special function?"

"It can detect stealth."

"Ah…"

"Of course, it's not perfectly visible. It's like seeing a shimmering outline."

Even in front of a device that refracts light, stealth can't be perfect.

"But that's still amazing. You can remove the low-frequency detection option. It's like gaining an extra slot."

"What does it look like with that option?"

"You can't see it. Only the coordinates are marked as dots. That's why stealth option users are hard to deal with."

"Then wouldn't everyone use stealth?"

"Certain selective options require specific parts to implement. To use the stealth option, you need parts that match that option, but their combat performance is extremely low. At high levels, once you're caught, you're dead."

"Kill or be killed. It's a binary choice."

"Exactly. That's why Donatello is amazing. It saves a slot and actually detects the form. Now, the explanation is over. Will you contact them as promised?"

As Shirone nodded, the mafia tried to contact Yolga's son.

"Will he answer?"

Rankers usually block communication from all but specific users.

As the group waited anxiously, the mafia's expression darkened.

Then suddenly, his eyes widened.

"Oh? He answered."

The group gathered around.

"He answered? Really?"

The mafia, gesturing for them to stay back, bent awkwardly and spoke.

"Hello? Hello?"

"Speak."

"I'm a user from the West Country, Mafia…"

"I know. I saw the news. What about Donatello?"

Though the dismissive tone annoyed him, the other party was a ranker and, more importantly, from an enemy country.

"We just obtained it. We want to settle. Yahweh2 promised. The settlement amount is…"

"Verification first."

The mafia relayed Fermi's words.

"Yahweh2, they want to verify first. Get close to the camera."

Shirone brought his right eye close.

"Zoom in. Zoom in."

As his pupil filled the screen, circular letters were engraved around the iris.

"Verification complete. Next, they want you to strike a victory pose to show you're really okay."

"Just send it quickly."

Though Shirone wasn't one to fall for Fermi's joke, the mafia was at a loss.

"Just send it! Sending now."

After the recorded video was sent, about 30 seconds later, Fermi's voice was heard.

"Seven people. Settled."

The communication was cut off.

The mafia, standing in disbelief, shouted as if the communication was still on.

"Hey! We haven't even adjusted the price yet! If you keep doing this, we'll…"

"M-Mafia."

As his colleagues stared at the air in a daze, a message appeared in the mafia's augmented reality.

"The galaxy has been deposited."

Entering the link, an unprecedented amount blinked in his personal vault.

"What? What?"

As he followed the zeros, his body trembled, and Sona raised her arms and shouted.

"Yay! We did it! We did it!"

Even as his colleagues high-fived and celebrated, the mafia couldn't believe it.

'Crazy people.'

In front of an amount that defied common sense, he didn't even feel the greed to ask for more.

Sona shook the mafia.

"Hey, hey! Let's go to the shop! There are so many things I want to buy! We'll sweep up all the limited editions."

Seeing them celebrate, Shirone smiled but also felt a bit bitter.

'Fermi.'

It wasn't just bravado.

An amount that could handle things most cleanly, based on a perfect understanding of humans.

There are many strong people in the world, but he was among the top ten he didn't want to fight, whether ally or enemy.

'I don't know what he's thinking. If he's an enemy, I'll have to defeat him eventually.'

Suddenly, he wondered.

'Why did Fermi choose the code name Yolga's son?'
