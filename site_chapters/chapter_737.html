Chapter 737:

Nane said,

"Shirone, as long as you deny me, I cannot become a god, but the same goes for you."

This meant that neither Shirone nor Nane could liberate the world from suffering.

"From now on, the world will face terrible suffering. I will end this fight as quickly as possible."

Shirone was determined to bear it.

"Even if the end of life is emptiness, as long as we love something, I will never give up."

Nane pointed at Shirone.

"Then the one who will suffer the most is you."

Perhaps.

"The Great Purification. The sins we have created will become reality and overwhelm you. It is too much to bear alone."

Nane's body dissolved like smoke.

"I hope the nightmare isn't too long."

A sandstorm blew away the traces of where Nane had stood.

"A nightmare?"

Though balanced at the extremes of the law, Nane was still the freer one.

'No matter what, it's a losing battle.'

Nane, who defined the world as emptiness, could commit any atrocity, while Shirone had to protect them all.

"You are not alone, Shirone."

Rian approached and placed a hand on Shirone's shoulder.

"Even if the whole world turns away from you, I will follow you."

No matter how distant the fight, Rian would be there at the very end.

"I agree."

As Sung Eum approached Shirone and extended her arm, Rian’s greatsword crossed space and landed in her hand.

Throwing it to Rian, she said,

"Humanity will have to face a great enemy. As the princess of the Jincheon Empire, I cannot stand by. I will return home and inform my father."

Even the emperor of the Jincheon Empire, whom even Sung Eum could not move, stood silently behind her.

"Go to the Ivory Tower. You have earned the right by postponing our lives."

"The Ivory Tower."

None of the candidates had been obsessed with becoming a star of the Ivory Tower, but now the situation had changed.

'We cannot win unless we all fight together.'

Even Shirone, who had reached the unprecedented realm of the Infinite Magician, could not completely stop Nane's grand plan.

Unless one side won, countless sacrifices were inevitable.

"I will go to the Ivory Tower."

Sung Eum nodded.

"Good decision. I will take you there."

With Ether Wave, they could go directly to the Ivory Tower without worrying about distance.

Kido spoke.

"I will not go to the Ivory Tower."

Shirone had already anticipated this from Kido's eyes.

"You plan to leave, Kido."

"I want to understand what I have realized. It's too confusing right now. You understand, right, Shirone?"

The journey from knowing to feeling, and from feeling back to knowing, requires time.

"Of course. It's your life."

As Rian, deep in thought, sheathed his greatsword on his back, he said,

"Shirone, I won't go to the Ivory Tower either."

Shirone nodded again.

"I thought you might say that."

"I want to go home and sort out my thoughts. I need to do what I can."

Though he had defeated Ymir, it was just a molar. To protect Shirone in the final war, he needed to become even stronger.

"Shirone, you will become a star of the Ivory Tower."

Perhaps Shirone, the only one in this world to open super cognition, could leave Rian’s side for a while.

"Then I must become a knight worthy of you. It feels like I'm always chasing after you, but give me time again."

Mun Kyung thought as he looked at Rian’s back.

'Does he still see a higher realm to reach?'

Rian’s power, which had shattered Ymir's body, was beyond what even Mun Kyung, a Jincheon swordsman, could match.

"Alright. I'll be waiting."

Rian and Kido were reliable comrades, but to stop Nane's grand plan, Shirone had to endure the longing.

'The world will change faster than ever.'

Sung Eum said,

"If the discussion is over, let's go. I need to return to the Jincheon Empire as soon as possible."

Kido stepped back from Sung Eum and said,

"Shirone, we are friends, right?"

Kido would travel the world and reach some conclusion, which might even deny Shirone's conclusion.

"Of course. You are my most precious friend. No matter the situation, that will never change."

Finally reassured, Kido turned and quickly ran towards the desert horizon.

"Good luck, Shirone!"

Shirone watched Kido's retreating figure, his old cloak fluttering, for a long time.

"Have a good journey."

Then he turned to Rian and asked,

"Will you be alright alone? How about I go with you to the nearest village? It might be hard to find food here."

Rian shrugged the greatsword on his back.

"As long as I have this, I can survive even in hell. Don't worry about me and come back as a star."

After the Ivory Tower test, Shirone felt like he had traveled a lifetime and missed home.

'Amy…'

Thinking he would see her soon made his heart race.

"I will leave now."

Mun Kyung bowed respectfully to Rian and stood behind Sung Eum's three steps as the Ether Wave unfolded.

As Sung Eum pulled space towards the North Pole and cast the spell, the three of them disappeared without a trace.

As Rian watched the spot where Shirone had vanished, a fierce fire suddenly ignited in his eyes.

"Ymir."

The giant's phantom shimmered on the desert horizon.

Kingdom of Corona

The Kingdom of Corona, located at the northernmost limit where humans can live, was the only country in the Arctic.

As a country built on eternal snow, ice crafts were developed, and it was almost the only market for the Ivory Tower.

People living in a country where blizzards hit daily felt death closer than life.

In the precarious cityscape where even the torches on the walls seemed unstable, people wore expressions as cold as ice.

"Where are we?"

It didn't seem like the Ivory Tower, so Shirone turned his head, and Sung Eum bit her lip with a troubled expression.

"It's probably the Kingdom of Corona."

To Sung Eum, who crossed vast spaces, the city-sized Corona felt small, but in the Arctic, the only inhabited places were the Ivory Tower or Corona.

"I couldn't reach the Ivory Tower."

As soon as she cast the spell, the feeling of the Ivory Tower disappeared, and she lost the coordinates.

It felt like an emergency escape, and this was the only place her senses could reach.

'Is there someone who controls space?'

In the past, it would have been unbelievable, but after facing Shirone, Nane, and Ymir, her thoughts had changed.

'It's entirely possible.'

It also meant Sung Eum's horizons had broadened.

"I could try again, but the result would be the same. They wouldn't have the Ivory Tower sign without such precautions."

Shirone agreed.

"Yes. But we've shortened the time by reaching the Arctic. I'll find my way from here. Thank you."

As Shirone, now calm, smiled, a blush appeared on Sung Eum's cheeks.

'He's a kind person.'

Anchal's words were not a lie.

'I don't want to fight him.'

She had much to say, but the current situation, constrained by time, was not favorable.

"How much time can you give me?"

"Haha."

Shirone laughed at Sung Eum's words, which seemed to measure time like distance.

"I can give you as much as you need. Without you, it would have taken over ten days to reach the Arctic."

"Ten days, huh."

Sung Eum smiled shyly and stepped back.

"When you become a star, visit Jincheon. I'll use the ten days you gave me then."

"No need to wait. If you want…"

"You promised. Ten days."

Knowing Sung Eum's personality, Shirone agreed without further comment.

"Alright. I'll give you my ten days."

It was a satisfying answer.

"I'll give you my zero steps."

"Huh?"

Shirone asked, but Sung Eum, with a meaningful smile, disappeared with Mun Kyung.

"…Zero steps probably means."

It likely meant to be close friends without barriers.

"Shall I get going?"

With that conclusion, Shirone walked the streets of the Kingdom of Corona with a lighter heart.

It was already late at night, and only the taverns were lit. The fatigue from fighting for days without rest finally caught up with him.

'Something feels off.'

At some point, he felt that Armand couldn't restore his fatigue, but he didn't dwell on it now.

"I want to sleep. I want to sleep."

With heavy eyelids, he found an inn with warm steam rising.

'I need to sleep.'

Even though he had reached the Arctic, he didn't know where the Ivory Tower was, and there were no competitors left.

As he entered, a middle-aged woman greeted him.

"Welcome to the White Inn."

Looking around, he saw a group playing cards at a corner table.

Passing the common scene, Shirone's gaze fixed on a table in front of the counter.

A red-haired girl, about ten years old, was drinking strong liquor alone.

'Do kids drink here?'

It seemed possible in a cold region, but on second thought, it didn't make sense.

"Are you here to stay?"

The middle-aged woman asked, and Shirone quickly replied.

"Oh, yes. If possible, I'd like some food too. I haven't had dinner yet…"

"Oh dear. We have some leftover bread and soup. But what should we do? There are no vacant rooms right now."

"No rooms?"

Shirone knew little about the Kingdom of Corona, but it wasn't a country frequented by tourists or travelers.

"Yes. Since yesterday, residents of the Ivory Tower have been staying here in groups, so all the rooms are full."

'Ivory Tower residents? How did they get here?'

Shirone squinted and looked at the four people playing cards.

There was no conversation; they just drew and placed cards like machines, collecting their winnings.

'It looks like four Iruki are sitting there.'

The middle-aged woman spoke.

"The blizzard is severe today. If you don't mind sharing a room, I can ask the awake guests."

"Oh, if you could…"

"You can share a room with me."

The girl, who had downed a strong drink in one gulp, turned to Shirone with a smirk, and the middle-aged woman muttered, resting her chin on her hand.

"Hmm, but mixed-gender rooms are a bit…"

"What does it matter? As long as both parties agree. Besides, I've missed having a man around. I won't charge for the room."

Shirone frowned.

"Kid, you shouldn't say things like that. And drinking at your age isn't good for you."

"Well, considering your opponent was Nane, you must be tired."

"What? What did you just say?"

As Shirone blinked, the girl, staring at her drink with unfocused eyes, turned her head.

"Even an Infinite Magician can be taken down if hit properly."

Shirone immediately opened his senses.

'She's not a child.'

As the Ultima System combined with super cognition, the false information surrounding her vanished instantly.

She had an attractive appearance, as if she were wearing makeup, with a slender figure and long limbs.

"Oh, right!"

The middle-aged woman clapped her hands.

"To most people, she would appear as a girl. This is Saint Mirach Minerva."

"Saint?"

"She's a resident of the Ivory Tower. Let me see, what rank was she?"

Minerva held up five fingers.

"Oh, a five-star. So, Miss Minerva, do you really intend to share a room?"

"I said I was fine with it. It's up to him."

Shirone heard nothing else.

'Five-star? Wait… Minerva? Could it be the one from the book…'

Mirach Minerva.

One of the world's top 100 dangerous individuals and the great witch who plunged the world into despair 700 years ago.
