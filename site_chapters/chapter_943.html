Chapter 943: Hidden Code (2)

Still grappling with doubt, Eden spoke up.

"Sorry, Liria. I can see what’s happening, but honestly, I can't trust you."

Despite reassurances, endless suspicions of her betrayal swirled around.

Meirei said, "That's enough."

Once the divine frequency intercepted Liria's thoughts, doubt vanished from the minds of the group.

Sein exhaled deeply.

"Reading someone’s mind dispels prejudice. Theraphos's technology is indeed impressive."

The right to judge good and evil isn’t granted without reason.

"Now."

At Kuan’s signal, Liria maximized the laws of the foolish harlequin.

Even their allies couldn’t find him, and Kuan's chosen target was Pride’s First Hour.

"I won't miss this time."

Circling behind a woman to aim at her heart, as if reacting, the Hours of Sloth moved in an orderly manner.

"It's Sloth’s Fourth Hour."

Something undesirable won’t happen?

"Let's see you endure it."

Given the asymmetry at its extreme, he’d have the upper hand in a clash between cheats.

Kuan's sword lunged towards the woman’s back.

"Die."

Since no one knew Kuan was there, no regrets would follow.

"Krrraaaah!" Wrapped in a sinister sensation, Pride’s First Hour clenched her upper body.

It was merely a desire.

"There’s definitely something."

Sloth’s Fourth Hour would mobilize all possible laws to avoid the impending future.

"You must realize."

Once realized, even if terrible things happen, the choice can be changed by the emotion of regret.

"Too late."

In a moment stretching out like eternity, Kuan inwardly scoffed.

"So-called hidden codes are but human inventions. That’s your downfall."

Before Pride’s First Hour could turn, his sword would have pierced her heart.

"What?"

A chilling murderous intent was felt from the side.

"Danger!"

Before Meirei could shout, Kuan had already retracted his sword and fled.

Where he had been, a fist entered and exploded the air with a bang.

"Who is that?"

As if influenced by centripetal force, Kuan scrutinized the identity of the fist while circling grandly in the air.

A young man with horn-rimmed glasses and his hair parted 2 to 8 was unwinding and laughing.

"Wow, that was sharp! I got the timing right, huh?"

That's what Kuan wanted to say.

"The attack just now was as fast as mine. Is there a master of Schema among the Hours too?"

The young man joked.

"You must have trained really hard. How did you do that move? I want to learn."

His admiring exclamation was sharp, but the gull-winged eyes behind the horn-rimmed glasses clearly mocked.

Landing on the ground, Kuan said.

"What’s the use of knowing? Even if I told you, it’d be beyond you for a lifetime."

"Ha! Of course."

The young man stuck out his tongue like a snake, emitting a chilling glint as he spoke.

"Who would do such stupid things, you fool?"

As Kuan remained silent, Meirei picked up the thoughts of the young man.

"Wanderer’s Sixth Hour. Hidden codes assume all actions necessary for achievement have been taken.

"Are you kidding?"

Everyone bristled upon hearing this.

Especially Kuan, whose eyes now bore disgust, but Wanderer’s Sixth Hour just shrugged.

"I’m human too, but watching your world is just hilarious. It's like a comedy. Desperately trying and then crying if you lose in a competition. Hahaha! Just..."

As Wanderer’s Sixth Hour turned and flexed his arm, the robe burst with a bang.

His right forearm was thick as a log.

"Isn’t this how it’s done?"

Although expected, seeing it with their own eyes darkened the expressions of Sein's group.

"No need to train."

Just assume and it’s done.

"Knowledge is the same. Digging into books every day, memorizing, trying to understand, pulling all-nighters..."

Wanderer’s Sixth Hour showed no special change.

However, Sein’s group could clearly read a subtle difference in the young man’s eyes now.

"Uh, so about 20 years’ worth of intelligence?" He smirked wickedly.

"Let's say you studied hard."

The fundamental displeasure about life’s purpose wasn’t just Sein’s feeling.

"How far do you plan to mock?"

To advance a second, lift an extra kilogram, score an extra point.

"Devoting a lifetime is what being human means."

Even as life felt negated, the pulse of the Hours continued to surge.

"Injustice’s Seventh Hour." A child.

He looked gloomy and expressionless, with undeniable malice in his eyes.

"So, what's the hidden code?" It takes no responsibility.

Now Sein’s group could easily recall the exact content hidden within the code.

"Even if you incite others to kill, push someone off a cliff, or poison a river!"

No one thinks it’s his responsibility.

"Yeah, let’s see how far you can go mad." As they waited for the next wave of mental pulses, a blonde woman reached out her hand.

"Hidden Piece."

The landscape around Meirei's group fractured like a puzzle, and Meirei's space completely detached.

"Give me your ear!"

As the blonde woman raised her finger tipped with flame and smirked evilly, trying to grasp Meirei's ear, Sein manipulated the spatial coordinates.

"Sun and Moon Halo!"

The blonde’s hand grazed Meirei's ear, and the tangled space reverted.

"Is this an exception?"

A capability Sein had read about in numerous reports entering the sanctuary.

"Mortasinger’s ability."

Tenth in the hierarchy of the Council of Ten.

According to Sirone, she had succumbed to Satan’s temptation and was dragged to hell.

"You, are you Mortasinger?"

The blonde woman covered her mouth and laughed.

"Hohoho! That’s unpleasant. To mistake me, mere fodder, for her."

"Fodder?"

Knowing that the Hours had no secrets because they read minds,

"Hidden codes need ingredients. Extreme emotions. I’m telling you the fun part, extreme emotions aren’t commonly felt in reality. Maybe 0.1 out of 100 people might experience it once in their lifetime?"

He roughly understood.

"But in the underworld, it’s commonplace. Mortasinger is still screaming in hell. If it were going to be like this, she’d rather be purified. Kakkakak! But purification means pain. What does that mean? She’s asking to be tortured more."

The blonde’s mental pulse was collected.

"Envy’s Eighth Hour. Hidden codes make it possible to possess the unpossessable… I see."

Money, lovers, positions can be stolen.

"But talents, looks, happiness are inherently non-transferable. Yet for Envy’s Eighth Hour, it's possible."

The condition is destruction.

If you are envious of someone's face, just pour acid on it.

"So that’s why Meirei’s ear..." Whether or not Envy’s Eighth Hour knew the group’s thoughts, he was busy talking.

"Ah, and thanks for sending that Jin Seongeum kid. The spiritual realm might be closed, but the hidden codes have been strengthened. She's caused quite a stir, and our demons are just loving her. I doubt we'll run out of ingredients anytime soon."

"Ingredients."

Sein suddenly wondered.

"You said extreme emotions are necessary to activate hidden codes. Then why Kuan..."

Sein turned to look at Kuan and stopped.

"Ah."

Kuan stood there expressionless, his asymmetry and amputated arm visible, his jacket flapping in the wind.

"Asymmetry to the extreme. The code obtained by removing one arm while the ankle ligament was cut..."

Kuan had activated it using his own emotions as the material.

"I roughly understand how extreme emotions work in hidden codes."

A living proof was right before them.

As Shiok took a step forward, Sein mirrored the distance and commanded.

"Maintain the gap."

"Hoho. Why? You can read our minds, can’t you? What more advantageous battle could there be?"

Sein felt humiliated.

"I thought so at first."

The problem was Shiok’s hidden code, knowing it didn’t mean it could be stopped.

"Delusion’s Ninth Hour is also tricky."

Sein’s gaze moved to a simple-looking old bachelor.

"Imagination becomes reality. A truly terrifying hidden code. Like a completed version of Nemesis."

Delusion’s Ninth Hour pointed at Sein’s ring.

"That smell is familiar. Is it Nemesis?"

Sein’s hypothesis was correct.

"In the end, that's what it is."

Like humans dream, the emotions of demons also flow into Drimo and float there.

"Sometimes seen."

If you've ever seen something while being pressed by sleep paralysis, it means a temporary portal had opened.

"And then it's looted by spiritual mages and escapes into the human world." Many artifacts exist, but those that have terribly disrupted human lives are mostly from the otherworld.

"Sein."

As Sein turned, his eyes widened.

"Miro?"

The scene shifted instantaneously, unfolding the landscape of Alpheus Magic School from long ago.

"Why are you standing there dumbfounded? You said you’d help with my research today. Forgot?"

"Ah?"

Sein hadn’t reached the conclusion that Delusion’s Ninth Hour had activated the hidden code.

Imagination becomes reality.

"Ah, sorry. Was just thinking something else."

Though memories of Shiok still lingered, the cheat reversed the senses of reality and imagination.

"Damn! I didn’t even say I’d help!"

A frail Gaold from those days followed, carrying three boxes of documents.

"Gaold..."

On the day of the judgement of 20, the only man who risked his life to save Miro.

"That’s right."

All memories were clear and he knew why, but there was no sense of discord.

This is reality.

"Miro."

Caught in the contradiction of knowing the future yet living in the present, Sein was overwhelmed by a powerful impulse.

"Hm?"

He liked her.

Just once, he wanted to honestly express his feelings to her.

As Gaold had in the fiery hell.

"What? You called me, why aren’t you speaking?"

It wasn’t a lack of courage.

He just wanted to know that no man could possess Miro.

"But I was wrong."

It all started...

Miro was seen walking towards Istas.

"Gaold will come."

His voice, cursed with self-consuming mutations to shout for Miro, had caused small cracks in her perfect mind.

"I’ve regretted it my whole life, even now. Why didn’t I have the courage like Gaold?"

As Miro passed by, tears welled up in one side of Sein’s mechanical eyes.

Farewell, Miro.

That was all he could choose for a final greeting.

-Choose. The future can be changed.

"Miro."

-Before Gaold comes, protect Miro. Shout to the world that Miro is my woman!

-Forget the process. You know, don’t you? If you choose here, Miro becomes your woman.

"I have to do it now! I can change it! I can live without regret! I’ve held it all my life!"

But Sein’s reason, unknowingly, blocked the voice.

"Huuuuh!"

The gears in both pupils madly moved as if stuck between one degree.

"No! It shouldn’t be like this!"

Prejudice’s Fifth Hour placed a hidden code on Sein.

-Actually, Miro likes you too. A lot.

"Aaaaaah!"

As his eyes burned with the clash of intellect and emotion, Sein screamed.

-She’s still only thinking of you.

"Please! Plea...!"

As Sein shouted, something snapped in his mind, and a giant crack appeared in the scenery.

"What...!"

As the positions of imagination and reality reversed, a chilling shudder ran down Sein’s spine.

"Damn! Stupid kid!"

As everyone escaped from Delusion’s Ninth Hour, they all looked around with the same expression as Sein.

"Kuan?"

Only Kuan was kneeling, gasping for breath in a sword-wielding stance.
