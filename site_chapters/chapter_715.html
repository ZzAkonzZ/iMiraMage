Chapter 715:

Labyrinth Andre-World No. 1.

Unaware of what was to come, Shirone spoke to Mitochondrial Eve.

"Even if you're not the one I know, I will never regret it. So let's live our best lives."

With a Karr value over 90%, there was no lingering doubt about the decision.

"Uga. Ugagaga."

Mitochondrial Eve's muffled voice was interpreted through the Ultima system.

"I don't know. I might not be."

She could barely speak properly unless she desperately pushed her brain functions.

Therefore, even she couldn't be sure who would be the protagonist of the foundational events to come.

"It's okay. You will definitely become the Empress in the future."

Mitochondrial Eve shyly pulled away and turned her head in embarrassment.

"Am I ugly?"

There was no precise answer to such a question.

"Well, um…"

"I know. You're from a different era. I can tell. And I know what I need to do."

Mitochondrial Eve took Shirone's hand.

"I'll help you. I'll find a way for you to return."

Although she didn't know what the other world was like, it was true that gathering information in the primitive era was limited and daunting.

'Still, it's a relief to have someone to talk to.'

As they smiled and held hands, screams were heard from outside the cave.

"Ahhh!"

It was the voices of those who had fled earlier.

"What's that?"

"Roar!"

At the dragon's roar, Mitochondrial Eve jumped into Shirone's arms.

"They found me…"

As she finished speaking, the dragon's face filled the cave entrance.

It had a long snout covered in blue scales, and vertical amber pupils crackling with electricity.

'It's different from other dragons.'

While its ferocity was undeniable, it had a certain dignity that set it apart from wild dragons.

"There you are."

As the electric gaze fixed on Mitochondrial Eve, Shirone suddenly realized.

'Maybe…'

Perhaps Mitochondrial Eve was never destined to die here from the beginning.

"And you are?"

The dragon's face turned emotionless and lifeless as it noticed Shirone.

"Roar!"

The cave shook with the thunderous roar.

"Roar! Roar!"

The dragon's roars were not singular; they struck like an unbroken echo.

"Ugh!"

Shirone gritted his teeth against the sonic assault and held Mitochondrial Eve tighter.

"Come out, human from another world."

Honestly, he didn't want to go out.

"If you refuse, I will breathe lightning into the cave. You won't be able to avoid it. You don't want that, do you?"

"Let's go."

Holding hands tightly, they walked out of the cave, and the dragon's face slowly moved aside.

'Out of the frying pan into the fire.'

He had considered fighting as a last resort, but hundreds of dragons already filled the sky.

'This is too much.'

Even in reality, it was known that there were over a thousand dragons, but it was rare to see more than one together.

"Submit. Our core is coming."

As the lightning dragon spoke and flew away, a massive shadow loomed on the horizon.

"It's not a shadow."

It was covered in black scales, and its size dwarfed the other dragons.

Boom!

The ground shook with the impact of its landing, and a dragon with a neck about 20 meters long looked down from a great height.

"Human from another world…"

The power in its voice transmitted through the Ultima system made Shirone's body tremble.

The language's force was on a different level, overwhelming everything.

"I am Karatorusa, the rankless dragon. Why do you disturb the world's information?"

"No way…"

Karatorusa, the rankless dragon.

In Shirone's world, it was a legendary myth, the king of dragons.

Labyrinth Andre-World No. 847.

"Power?"

Codename 97, Panika, explained things to Shirone on the way to the temple.

"Yes. Depending on how suitable you are for divine power, your rank is determined, and a code is engraved on your forehead."

"So, is there duplication? For example, if I'm number 50, could there already be someone with that number?"

"You, number 50? Hahaha!"

Panika burst into laughter and covered her mouth.

"Sorry, it was just too absurd. Anyway, if that happens, everyone behind you will be pushed back one rank."

"So it's not like a tattoo. The code, I mean."

Panika pointed to her prominent forehead.

"Of course not. This is a mark engraved directly by the gods. That's why rankers show their foreheads. It's a symbol of authority."

The style of shaving the front hairline was ridiculous, but it was a natural trend in this city's system.

"So, you can use powers according to your rank?"

"Yes. The higher the rank, the better the powers. Below 400,000, there's no power, but once you get into the 300,000s, you can amplify your strength and lift heavy rocks."

Understanding that physical strength varied by rank explained why there hadn't been a system overthrow.

"In the 200,000s, you can control elements. Create fire, freeze water. In the 100,000s, you can use two elements simultaneously."

Imagining the endlessly rising pyramid of abilities, Shirone understood why people were afraid of number 97.

"So, rankers are…?"

"From 1st to 100th. They use the ultimate power called 'Divine Knowledge.' I'm one of them."

Panika demonstrated.

"Rankers can use all the abilities up to 101st, but they have one special power. Mine is materialization."

An apple appeared in Panika's hand.

"It's the ability to create objects from thoughts."

Panika handed the apple to Shirone, who bit into it skeptically.

'It's a real apple?'

"I can make anything I want. Jewels, weapons, even explosives. But they wither over time, so I'm really into shopping these days."

It was no wonder people were surprised to see a ranker in a middle-class neighborhood.

'This is strange. Really strange.'

The powers called abilities were different from the magic Shirone knew, even from the irregular types.

There might be plant mages who could create apples, but only gods could make everything just by thinking.

'Is there really a god? Or maybe…'

As he considered a possibility, they arrived at the temple, where the city's upper class strolled.

"Everyone above 500 spends time here. It's a place anyone can visit, but…"

Those with lower ranks might get into trouble if they lingered.

He saw numbers 467, 311, and 109, and a man with the now shocking rank of 37 leisurely reading a newspaper.

"Hey, Panika. Who's this, your boyfriend?"

A sharp-looking man with short front hair and long side hair approached the temple entrance.

'Number 9…'

He was 9th among over 400,000 people.

"Codename 9, Gale. Be careful with him."

Gale glanced at Shirone as he approached Panika.

"What's with this guy's hair? Not a ranker? Where did you pick him up? I didn't know you had this taste."

Gale couldn't imagine an uncountable in the temple.

"What's it to you who I date? Move, I'm busy. Is Mr. Drine in the temple?"

Panika was visibly tense.

"What's the rush? You need to clock in."

Gale grabbed Panika's chin and kissed her, making Shirone's eyes flare.

"Stop it! What are you doing…!"

Panika held out her hand to stop Shirone, and Gale spoke without breaking the kiss.

"Haha! Why, does it bother you? Then take revenge. By the way, what's your number? In the 100s?"

Sensing danger, Panika pushed Gale's chest with all her might.

"That's enough! Stop it!"

"You're so prickly in front of your boyfriend. Want to fight? Maybe you could take my 9th place."

Despite the hierarchy, the reason for possible friendships between ranks was the 'exchange and acquisition' system for higher codes.

The higher up the pyramid, the smaller the skill gap, so rankers naturally avoided conflict.

'But not Gale.'

Even so, Panika had to endure Gale's advances because he was not just any ranker but a legendary ranker in the top 10.

Rankers in the top 100 could use the ultimate power, 'Divine Knowledge,' but those in the top 10 received an additional power, 'Divine Grace.'

'If we fight, I'll definitely lose.'

Ultimately, the most powerful force was the code, and Panika could only resent her fate with Gale.

"Isn't it too much just because of a number?"

Gale turned to Shirone with a bewildered expression.

"What… did you just say?"

"I mean the ugly number on your forehead. You're not number one, are you? Don't you think it's too much?"

"Hahaha!"

Gale laughed heartily.

"Well, low-ranked guys like you wouldn't understand. Panika gets a lot of perks. She's probably played with every handsome guy she met on the street. Including you."

"I'm not like that!"

Panika shouted, her face red.

"Well, that's a matter of taste, but having only 96 people above you is a very comfortable life. Even those in the 10,000s do whatever they want. You're not someone to pity, being Codename 97."

Is Panika unhappy?

If she is unhappy, how should we define the lives of the 427,000 people below rank 97?

"Even so, I can't accept it. Whether strong or weak, it's wrong for humans to torment other humans."

"Ha. Just listening to you is really annoying."

Gale, who had been touching his forehead, suddenly approached at incredible speed and lifted Shirone's bangs.

"What rank are you to be so arrogant… huh?"

Seeing that there was no number, Gale turned to Panika with a furious look.

"You… dared to bring an uncountable into the temple?"

"What's the commotion?"

A voice from the temple made all the rankers stop what they were doing and turn their heads.

As a man with a kind face and neatly trimmed bangs descended the stairs, Panika's face brightened.

"Mr. Drine!"

Codename 1, Drine.

He was the only absolute authority in this city who did not take orders from anyone.

"It's been a while, Panika. Tell me what's going on."

After listening to Panika's explanation, Drine nodded as he looked at Shirone.

"I see. An uncountable, that must have been difficult. Sometimes people cross over with no codename. Follow me. The gods will determine your place."

Gale sneered at Shirone as he turned to follow Drine.

"Prepare yourself. Once you get a code, not even Mr. Drine will interfere. Then I'll crush you."

As they climbed the stairs with Shirone, Panika whispered closely.

"Don't worry too much. Even if you get a low number, I'll be your friend. It won't be too hard to get by."

"Thanks."

At this point, the number didn't seem to matter much, but her words warmed his heart in the cold, hierarchical world.
