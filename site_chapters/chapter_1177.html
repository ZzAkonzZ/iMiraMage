Chapter 1177:

‘So, you really do want to kill me.’

There was nothing like true friendship.

Moved by their determination, Havitz finally turned his gaze to Zetaro.

"Explain it to me."

He already knew the rules through divine frequency, but this wasn’t a game he could win with logic alone.

Zetaro spoke.

"You and Wizard will play the game. The rules are simple. You state one truth and one lie."

Havitz chuckled.

"But neither of us will know which is which, right?"

"Exactly. You must state one undeniable truth. If you fail to prove it, you lose."

"And the consequence of losing is death?"

"Well… As much as I’d love that, I’d rather avoid a situation where the final outcome disrupts the rules. If you were to say, ‘I will die,’ and then prove it as truth, you’d actually win the game. That would break the system, wouldn’t it?"

"Does it really matter? You’re trying to kill me anyway."

"Then it wouldn’t be a game."

Zetaro’s subtle smile sent a rare shiver down Havitz’s spine.

"Heh, you’re right."

Satan was unpredictable.

If the structure of the game collapsed, he would survive under any circumstance.

‘Zetaro really is interesting.’

Feeling a thrill rise within him, Havitz asked,

"And what’s the price of losing?"

Wizard answered.

"The winner gets to make a wish, and the loser must grant it.

Neither side can refuse."

"Hah. Any wish, no matter what it is?"

Even as the glint of madness flickered in Havitz’s eyes, Wizard remained composed.

"Yes. Anything."

Though their desires were different, Wizard’s resolve was no less intense than Havitz’s.

"Alright, let’s go over it. For example— In one hour, I will eat an apple. In one hour, I will eat a strawberry. One of these statements is true, and one is false."

Zetaro nodded.

"Right. You must eat either the apple or the strawberry to prove your truth. If you eat both, or neither, you lose."

One truth, one lie.

"Hmm."

After confirming the finer details of the rules, Havitz unexpectedly remained cautious.

Smodo narrowed his eyes.

‘So, he sensed it after all.’

He knew just how dangerous this game really was.

The longer Havitz took to think, the more anxious everyone became—except Zetaro, who was certain.

‘Havitz will accept.’

He recalled their conversation from an hour ago.

"Havitz will accept."

At those words, Balkan, his face pale as a corpse, had fallen into deep thought.

"Are you absolutely certain? Playing the game means following its rules. That weakens his greatest strength—chaos. He’s already playing an assassination game. If he accepts a second game, it’s practically suicide."

Zetaro nodded.

"That’s true. Unless the game is absurdly fun, no game could possibly interest him. But this time, it’s different. Because Wizard is here."

Zetaro turned to Wizard.

"The rules themselves are simple. The real key to this game is psychology. And if there’s one thing that can strike Havitz, it’s psychology. He must state at least one undeniable truth."

"Divine frequency."

"Right. Havitz can’t read Wizard’s mind. The moment I heard the assassination game’s rules, I realized it. Havitz is sick of everything. But if Wizard is the only one whose mind he can’t read, then all we need to do is center the rules around her."

Balkan smirked.

"That makes sense. This game isn’t just about the rules—it’s about the people. If he were playing against someone he had no interest in, it would just turn into a meaningless word game. But he wants to understand Wizard. He’ll participate willingly."

"As long as Havitz is entertained, that’s enough."

Zetaro looked somber.

"That’s why…

He will definitely play this game."

"Alright."

Havitz spoke.

"I accept."

Even Wizard, who had remained eerily composed, showed the slightest flush of excitement.

Havitz checked the time.

"I still have to continue my own game. But first, let’s do this— In this assassination game, I will kill Zetaro."

Truth or lie?

"And one more. In this assassination game, I will kill Smodo."

One of them had to be true.

"If you can, try to survive. If both of you live, I lose."

"Hmm."

Smodo rested his chin on his hand.

‘Has he already decided? Or is he just keeping both options open until he makes a choice? Either way, one of us will die.’

Zetaro also fell into deep thought.

‘It’s too simple. If Smodo and I just kill ourselves, the game ends immediately. But Havitz…’

His eyes widened.

‘Ah.’

Havitz was engrossed in this game.

‘Even if Smodo and I die, if Wizard loses, it ends in a draw. This is a declaration— He will make sure Wizard fails. No, more than that— A much greater desire is at play here.’

How would their opponent respond?

"Understood. I accept."

Then Wizard spoke.

"Before this assassination game ends, I will kiss Shirone."

Truth or lie?

"And before this assassination game ends… I will kiss you."

Zetaro nodded inwardly.

‘As expected. Wizard understands the essence of this game. Now there’s no need to worry.’

She wasn’t a child.

Through countless simulations, her mind had matured to an adult’s level.

‘If she doesn’t falter in this psychological battle, she can defeat Havitz. It’s not about what is true or false. The real intent of this game is—’

The same thing he was feeling now.

"This is fun."

Havitz grinned.

"Alright, I accept. Now then…"

He checked his watch.

"I’ve got about eight minutes before I start my next game."

He put his watch away and said,

"Run. You have eight minutes."

Smodo and Zetaro exchanged glances before immediately fleeing.

"Tick, tock."

Havitz mimicked the sound of a clock as he turned to Wizard, who remained standing before him.

"Are you planning to kill me now? The moment you harbor murderous intent, Vanishing will activate."

Havitz smirked.

"Or what? Are you planning to kiss me?"

"I don’t need to kill you."

As Wizard activated Transfiguration, the ominous remnants of the Void God manifested in the air.

"I just need to stop you."

"You’re making the game boring."

Of course, it didn’t matter how she played.

As long as he won, the reward would make up for it.

‘She’s stronger now.’

From the flickering distortions of the Void God,

Havitz instinctively understood the level she had reached.

"Heh."

His eyes gleamed with killing intent.

"That’s unfortunate."

Twelve shadows appeared beneath Wizard’s feet as Hell’s Prison emerged.

‘I’m at my full power, too.’

The moment Havitz lunged forward, Wizard clenched her teeth and unleashed the Void God.

Reversal of the White Path.

"For Satan—!"

Before Hell’s Prison could even finish their chant, the 0.666-second cycle shattered, and time rewound.

"Tch!"

She sprinted down the corridor,

but Havitz was already nowhere to be seen.

‘Where did he go? Smodo? Zetaro?’

A 50% chance.

‘I can’t track him with Spirit Zone. He must have activated Hell's Prison. Do I gamble on the odds… Or do I find Shirone and finish my mission?’

Only five minutes remained.

Zetaro ran until his lungs burned.

“Haa…! Haa…!”

It wasn’t fear of death driving him—it was the desire to entertain Havitz to the very end.

‘Havitz is going to kill me.’

Three minutes ago, he had told Smodo exactly that.

“Havitz is going to kill me. If it’s more fun to kill someone he likes more, then that person is obviously me.”

That was his pride.

“I’ll lure him away, so you run in the other direction. From here on, we leave it to fate.”

“Will you be alright?”

“In this game, we’re just tools. Wizard will do what she needs to do.”

“Alright then… Be careful. Heh.”

Smodo chuckled at his own words, making Zetaro laugh as well.

“Heh. Maybe I should slap you just once for that.”

Only Wizard could perceive Havitz’s Vanishing, so he might not even realize when he died.

“Well, goodbye.”

And so, the two parted ways at the fork in the corridor.

Zetaro ducked into an empty office, locked the door, and hid beneath the desk.

“I can’t run anymore.”

He resigned himself to waiting for death, trying to recall old, happy memories when—

“Hm?”

Vanishing activated.

‘Why am I here again? I know I came in here to hide from something, but…’

One minute remained.

Smodo stood blankly in the hallway.

“What did I forget?”

Havitz approached, his longsword dragging across the floor.

“With this, my statement is true.”

Three seconds left.

Two.

One.

As he twisted his body to behead Smodo—

“Hrk—!”

A burst of Hexa light engulfed Delta Headquarters.

In that instant, Smodo’s mind registered Havitz’s presence.

Through Elikia’s quantum signal, the entire building became aware of what was happening.

A vision of Shirone flickered through Smodo’s mind.

"Yahweh…?"

The assassination game operated on an hourly cycle.

"KIYEEE!"

Havitz’s face twisted into something grotesque as he drove his sword toward Smodo’s heart.

“Guh!”

Smodo barely twisted his body, but the blade still pierced through his lung, exiting from his back.

Wizard arrived, having locked onto Havitz’s position.

“Havitz!”

“…Smodo?”

Still sitting under the desk, Zetaro muttered vacantly.

“Why? Why wasn’t it me…?”

Even Wizard had to acknowledge it—but losing to Smodo was a shock.

“No…!”

Zetaro clutched his head.

“That’s impossible! Havitz told me I was the most fun! Why… why wasn’t it me?!”

As the sword slid free with a slick sound, Smodo coughed up a flood of blood—yet he smiled.

‘Zetaro… That’s a relief.’

He collapsed against the wall, gasping for breath.

‘This round… must not have been fun for Havitz.’

Havitz turned away.

“I won.”

Spreading his arms wide, he declared,

“You didn’t meet Shirone. And I killed Smodo. My victory.”

“It’s not over yet.”

Wizard pointed at Smodo.

“He’s still alive.”

Smodo, clutching his stomach, nodded weakly, surviving on the last dregs of air in his punctured lungs.

“Hm. Then should I finish it?”

As Wizard activated the Void God, Havitz raised his sword.

First Movement—Weightless Creation.

For a brief moment, a single frame of time disappeared.

By the time Havitz came back to his senses, Wizard’s lips were pressed against his.

Even as she silently landed on the floor, Havitz stood frozen, sword in hand.

“With this, it’s a draw.”

Smodo used the last of his precious breath to burst into laughter.

“Kuhaha…!”

And with bloodied teeth bared, he spoke.

“I’m going first. Have fun.”

With that, Smodo finally died.

Havitz touched his lips as he turned to Wizard.

‘She kissed me.’

That was an undeniable truth.

But… did that mean Wizard had feelings for him?

‘I don’t know. I can’t read her mind. Was it just part of the game? Or was it something more…?’

He wanted to know.

He wanted to know so badly it was maddening.

Zetaro thought to himself.

‘Yeah… I don’t know either, Havitz. Why did you choose Smodo over me? But that’s the essence of this game.’

Truth or lie, words or actions, proof or denial—

‘We can never truly know another person’s truth.’
