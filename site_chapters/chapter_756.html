Chapter 756:

“Get in there! Do you want to die?!”

At the heart of North Aymond’s 48th Military Facility, a group of soldiers stood threateningly with blades drawn, barking orders at the laborers.

“I-I went in just yesterday! Please, have mercy!”

“If the draw says you're chosen, then that’s it! Are you saying you're refusing a mission from the Republic?!”

Only when the blade pressed against his throat did the man bow his head in resignation and clutch the burlap sack.

If he resisted, he would die here and now. But if he went into that warehouse, there was at least a chance of survival.

Out of ten people, eight came back alive. The odds were surprisingly high.

The problem was the other two. Their fate didn’t just end in death.

“Next. Who drew the X?”

“That’s me.”

Baseto, Verdie’s father, stepped forward, showing the stick marked with an X.

“Get in.”

He already knew resistance was futile, so he dragged the remaining sack and headed toward the warehouse.

“H-Hey, Baseto. Who’s going first? Huh? Me? Or you?”

His companion, equally selected but terrified out of his mind, annoyed him—but Baseto had long grown numb to this.

“You do as you please.”

The man’s eyes darted as he forced a weak smile.

“T-Then you go first. Alright?”

Eight out of ten survived—but of the thirty-nine who had died so far, thirty had gone in first.

“…Fine.”

The moment they opened the warehouse door, a repulsive stench stabbed at his nose.

Even shoving one’s face into a corpse wouldn’t compare to this sickening smell.

“Grrrrrrr…”

Inside the warehouse, behind metal bars, loomed massive silhouettes—monsters bigger than men.

Their shapes, temperaments, and behaviors all differed, but the soldiers simply referred to them as Garas.

Stay calm. Just drop the sack and walk out.

As Baseto opened the gate and stepped in, the Garas turned their heads and slowly backed away.

“Hrkk. Hrkk. Hrkk.”

They let out eerie growling laughter. Baseto, not daring to breathe, dragged the sack across the floor.

Don’t provoke them. Don’t make a sound.

He had learned exactly one skill here: how to drag the sack with such force it nearly tore his arm off.

“Chiiiiiik.”

Something sharp, like a claw, jabbed into his side, and Baseto involuntarily wet himself.

Damn! Did they pick me?

It still wasn’t over.

“…”

His hyper-aware senses picked up on the Garas retreating, even without turning his head.

Bastards. Are you toying with me?

The Garas didn’t break out of their cage because the 48th Facility provided them with the finest “ingredients.”

You think you’re emperors now?

Igor was prepared to pay any price to satisfy the sexual appetite of the Garas—beings a hundred thousand times stronger than humans.

To build the strongest army.

Whether they could be controlled was uncertain. But even getting them to accept the cage had been a victory.

I’m going back to my family.

These Garas were already full. Unless something caught their interest, they wouldn’t even look at him.

Almost there!

He dropped the sack, but he’d seen people caught because they’d tried to run.

Slow. Slower.

Repeating "Those who seek death shall live" over and over in his mind, Baseto finally made it out—and the Garas let out mock congratulatory laughter.

“Hrk! Hrk! Hrk!”

The fact he survived made everything feel worth it. Meanwhile, the man behind him had gone pale.

“C-Congratulations. That was amazing.”

There was no reply.

“R-Right. You’ll be fine too. Please, could you maybe drop mine in too…?”

When Baseto turned and glared at him with eyes filled with fury, the man dropped his gaze.

“Right… no one wants to die…”

Baseto’s heart had softened for a moment, but he soon regained his composure and pressed himself against the warehouse wall.

Creaaak. Unfortunately, the lock that had been working just fine until now let out a squeak as it opened—and the man’s reason snapped.

“Huff… huff…”

The ragged breathing made Baseto frown, but he didn’t dare speak.

Calm down. If you do as you’ve been taught, you’ll be fine.

“Uhhhhhhhh…”

A quiet whimper. One of the Garas in the corner licked its lips, intrigued.

Its thick, wet tongue lashed out and wrapped around the man’s neck, tightening.

“Please… don’t kill me…”

Baseto’s eyes shot open.

You idiot! Stay still! Don’t say anything!

“Please! I’m begging you! I was here yesterday too! I can’t die like this—AAAAAARGH!”

The tongue yanked the man away—and Baseto turned on his heel and sprinted for the exit.

“P-Please help me! AAAAAAAH!”

Without even glancing back, Baseto slammed on the door of the warehouse.

“Open up! Open the damn door, you bastards!”

A small shutter opened, revealing a soldier’s cold eyes scanning the room.

“I did it! I completed the mission!”

“The cage is open. Close it.”

“You sons of—!”

The curse burst out on instinct, but even that faded under the desperate will to survive.

Slamming the door with both hands, he turned back toward the cage—where the man was pinned under a hulking Garan, his hand reaching out.

“Please! Help—AAAAAAARGH!”

Baseto watched his eyes roll back before locking the cage with shaking hands.

“Kyaaaaah!”

A frenzied Garan slammed its massive black shadow against the bars, nearly knocking him out.

He didn’t even know how he moved, but suddenly his back hit the opposite wall, and slimy, snake-like appendages slithered out from the bent cage.

“Grrrrrrgh!”

Gritting his teeth, Baseto bolted toward the door—just as it opened and light flooded in.

The light of life.

“AAAAAAARGH!”

Terrified the door might close on him, he launched himself through and tumbled across the ground.

Someone save me. Please… my love, Verdie…

The horrific screams from within the warehouse echoed behind him. Covering his ears, Baseto broke down in sobs.

“Hhhkk… Hhhhkk…”

Another life had been lost.

In the deep reaches of the Galchen Mountains surrounding the capital of North Aymond, Pasia, Major Jacey—having escaped from the 48th Military Facility—arrived at the rendezvous point.

They’re not here yet? Five minutes early…

Though she operated as a North Aymond officer, she was in truth a spy who had infiltrated from South Aymond six years ago.

I endured all this just for today.

It was a mission only a non-registered Class-3 Arch Mage, specialized in espionage, could have survived.

“Jacey!”

Three soldiers emerged from the bushes.

The one leading them was Jacey’s husband.

“What took you so long?”

“Sorry. Getting through the border took longer than I thought. I’m just glad I made it.”

After a brief kiss, Jacey reached into her coat.

“There’s no time. Two days ago, the North succeeded in controlling a Garas for the first time. If we don’t act quickly…”

“Those monsters! They really did it?”

“Fortunately, they haven’t yet reached the level of taming higher organisms. But the fact that they succeeded at all is what's dangerous.”

As time passed, technology would inevitably improve.

“Deliver this to the capital. It’s everything I’ve gathered about the Garas. I’ll cross over in three days.”

Leaving now would put the entire country on alert and likely result in her husband’s capture.

“You’ll be headed to the torture chamber.”

As soldiers from North Aymond’s military police pushed through the brush, Jacey’s eyes filled with shock.

“How…?”

She thought she had hidden her trail perfectly.

“The world’s changed. You need to understand—there are eyes even in the places you don’t know.”

The Underworld.

No… that’s impossible. The residents of the Underworld would never cooperate with humans…

“Don’t even think about coming quietly. I’m in a bad mood.”

As the military police captain drew his sword and approached, Jacey cast an invisibility spell and shouted:

“Scatter! Run!”

She vanished from sight, and from an unexpected direction, she fired an electric bolt, sending the soldiers diving for cover.

So she’s not just some amateur.

Invisibility was one of the highest-tier spells in the illusion school of electric magic.

“…Things just got interesting.”

As the spies fled, the military police captain gave the order.

“Call the Beast King Unit.”

A flare shot into the air, and three minutes later, twenty soldiers arrived, each holding the leash of dogs nearly two meters tall.

Grrr…

One was covered in iron-plated fur, another was riddled with holes across its body, and another dripped venom from its fangs…

These grotesque creatures were created using Garas, and they moved in perfect formation under silent command.

Garas-Hybrid Mutant Hounds.

A terrifying success.

To control Garas, North Aymond’s biologists had turned to canine genetics.

Though it was still in its early stages, if these creatures could be trained, the empire would soon possess terrifying military strength.

“Do they really obey? If we rely too much on the dogs and let the targets escape, our heads won’t be safe.”

The captain of the Beast King Unit replied confidently.

“Don’t worry. These are the Republic’s dogs—they do even the most dangerous tasks without fail.”

“How’s the performance?”

“Outstanding. You’ll just have to see for yourself. Just give the order.”

The military police captain swept his cold gaze across the forest.

“Find those southern scum. Bring them back alive.”

Twenty minutes later—

Screams of agony echoed from the mid-slopes of the Galchen Mountains.

“Noooo!”

The hybrid hounds, with their advanced sense of smell, quickly tracked down the spies, and Jacey’s husband was beheaded by the captain’s blade.

“You bastards! Kill me too! Just kill me already!”

Jacey, incapacitated by the venom of a hybrid hound that relaxed major muscles, had missed her chance to take her own life.

“How unpleasant. Getting called a villain by the ones trying to steal our country’s secrets.”

“Shut up! Even war has rules! Do you even understand what you’re doing?!”

“Of course I do. We’re at war.”

The military police captain stomped on Jacey’s face.

“Ugh!”

Even with her cheek pressed against the ground, she glared at him defiantly, which irritated him even more.

“You know exactly how the Republic interrogates criminals. You’d better confess everything—how far does this conspiracy go?”

“Kill me.”

It was a resolve one could only have after surviving six years in enemy territory.

“You’re willingly throwing away your own human rights.”

The captain turned his head, and a black-furred hybrid hound came drooling toward her.

“You’re about to experience ten minutes of hell. After that, we’ll talk again. This is your last chance to stop it.”

“…Kill me.”

A researcher from the Beast King Unit opened a file.

“Experiment Data 38-7. Subject: Vestark Jacey. Age: 36. Beginning record.”

“AAAAAAAH!”

As Jacey tightly shut her eyes and screamed, the military police captain suddenly sensed something and spun around.

Even with his heightened sensory schema, he hadn’t noticed the person approach until they were right next to him…

“…Who the hell are you?”

A little girl stood there, wearing a wide-brimmed pointed hat and holding a broom in one hand.

“Human, human.”

The mature, chilling voice shattered the illusion, and the soldiers stepped back in shock.

Was I dreaming?

As if the scene had been a hallucination, the child’s image faded, and an enchantingly beautiful woman appeared before their eyes.

“Why is it… no matter how many of you I kill, you never stop coming?”

One of the Five Stars of the Ivory Tower.

Head of the Human Safety Enforcement Department— Minerva Mirac.
