Chapter 815: Dark Clan (3)

The man in the fedora and glasses took one last look around before entering the back door of the blood bank.

The man, who matched the description given by the gang leader, was named Tempest.

Like the vampire who attacked the Bark family, he was of the Almas rank.

Rian watched from the corner as the man passed through the wall without opening the door.

'Confirmed, he's a vampire.'

After staying in place for about a minute, Rian slowly moved towards the back door.

'This is tricky.'

Rian didn't have a key.

Gently gripping the steel door handle, Rian slightly increased the strength in his arm.

"Hup."

With a creak, the steel twisted, and the door opened.

'Did he hear that?'

He carefully pushed the door open and checked inside, but there was no sign of anyone.

Relying on his honed instincts, he entered and found a hallway stretching to the end of the building.

Only a few of the crystal lamps were lit, and as he followed the dark hallway, he saw a light in the distance.

'Blood refrigerator.'

Reading the sign, he peeked inside and saw specially treated blood packs stored by type.

As the vampire turned towards the door, Rian quickly pressed his back against the wall.

'He didn't notice.'

Speed alone isn't the measure of strength.

'There was no sign.'

Rian's stealth was so slow that it didn't even disturb the air, a skill of considerable level.

'Not the best yet.'

Though he wasn't satisfied with his level, it was enough to deceive the enemy.

When Tempest opened the lid of the steel box, he saw neatly stacked blood packs.

After scanning them with his eyes, he placed a bag next to him and began transferring the packs one by one.

"What are you up to?"

Finally, Rian turned and stood in front of the door, causing Tempest to freeze.

But he soon regained his composure and continued his work without looking back.

"You must be the one who interfered with Masker."

That must be the name of the vampire who attacked the Bark family, leading to one conclusion.

"Are you also an Almas?"

"They call me Tempest. I've been around for quite a while, of course, in the darkness."

"I have no intention of getting involved in vampire society."

Rian gripped the greatsword on his back.

"But harming humans is unacceptable. Surrender peacefully, and you won't be harmed."

"Harm?"

Tempest smiled.

"Just as humans eat something to live, we crave blood to survive. To put it in a distasteful way, like mosquitoes. Humans kill mosquitoes but don't feel guilty about it."

Rian had clashed with many beliefs while crossing the desert.

'It's always like this.'

There were very few times he found the right answer, and the outcome always belonged to the victor.

"I don't want to complicate things. You've killed humans and will continue to do so, so I'm arresting you."

"Really?"

After placing the blood packs in the bag, he turned around.

"I heard about you. They say you fight like a hunter. But do you know where you are?"

"The blood bank."

"Exactly."

Tempest's pupils contracted vertically.

"How do you plan to catch me?"

Throwing the remaining blood pack at Rian, he moved with lightning speed.

As Rian's sword sliced through the pack, blood splattered horizontally along the sword's trajectory.

'I won't lose him.'

Without any interference, even an Almas rank was a step below Rian.

With a flash, the greatsword cut through, and Tempest's left arm fell with a thud.

"Ugh!"

In that moment, Tempest realized the gravity of the situation.

'What a strange sword.'

Vampires in a semi-spiritual state have 50% immunity to physical attacks.

But as Masker said, Rian's sword wasn't affected by their transformation.

"But...!"

Biting into a blood pack, Tempest's lips turned red as blood poured out.

'Rapid regeneration!'

The mucus-like blood at the severed arm quickly regenerated the body.

"In this place, I'm invincible!"

As Tempest charged forward, the inside of the refrigerator room was turned upside down.

'This is annoying.'

No matter how many times Rian cut him, the blood-absorbing body wouldn't break.

'Damn! Is he really human?'

Meanwhile, Tempest also felt that Rian wasn't human, as Masker had said.

'I need to retreat for now.'

As Tempest's body dissolved into black smoke and headed for the door.

'Now!'

With a burst of energy, Rian thrust the greatsword into the center of the remaining upper body.

"Argh!"

As Tempest's smoke regained its physical form, the sword pierced his heart and pinned him to the wall.

"Blocking blood circulation makes regeneration impossible."

"Heh heh."

Tempest grabbed the greatsword with both hands.

"But you can't kill me. There's plenty of blood, so keep trying all night if you want."

A voice came from outside the door.

"How about this?"

With a flick, something stuck into Tempest's neck.

"Ugh! This is... a small syringe."

"What?"

As Rian turned his head, a middle-aged man with a thin mustache entered, carrying a medical bag.

"Don't move. If you pull out the sword, his heart will start beating again."

The middle-aged man warned as he cleared the blood packs from the table and opened his bag.

While he mixed three substances on the spot and drew them into a syringe, two more people entered the storage room.

A large man and a silver-haired woman.

Rian noted the woman's silver hair.

"It's ready. Now, just the injection..."

As the middle-aged man approached, removing air from the syringe, Tempest realized and shouted.

"Hunter?"

He struggled violently.

"No! Not that!"

With his heart stopped, he couldn't muster any strength.

"A special vampire blood coagulant. It only takes five minutes to detoxify, but in this state, it's enough."

"You bastards! Let me go! I'll kill you all! I'll drain all your blood!"

"Now, now! Stay still. The pain is intense."

As the middle-aged man roughly injected the syringe into his collarbone, Tempest screamed.

"Argh!"

As the injection took effect, visible changes occurred.

'He's disintegrating.'

Purple veins spread like a web across Tempest's pale skin, which then became brittle and flaked off like dead skin.

"Argh..."

His entire body burned as if cooked, turning to ash and falling to the floor. The large man who had entered with the middle-aged man looked at Rian with a massive gun slung over his shoulder.

"Who are you? How did you know to come here?"

Before Rian could answer, the silver-haired woman raised her rapier and charged.

"Move aside. I need to kill him."

As Rian quickly retreated using his acceleration ability, the others narrowed their eyes and watched.

"What's with his movements?"

The silver-haired woman pointed her sword.

"He's a vampire. Kill him."

Rian roughly understood the situation.

'This is getting complicated.'

Indeed, his acceleration ability resembled a vampire's movements, and she had confirmed Rian's death.

"It's a misunderstanding. I'm human."

"Nonsense!"

As the silver-haired woman ran along the wall, the large man aimed his gun.

"Is that so?"

With a loud bang, bullets were fired, and Rian raised his arm to block them.

With a thud, the bullets embedded in his muscles.

"Ugh!"

"He blocked it with his body?"

Seeing this, the large man lowered his gun, and the woman who had been running along the wall landed midway.

"Hmph!"

Hearing Smiling's hallucination, Rian tensed his arm, and the bullets fell to the floor.

They were made of silver.

"What? Are you enduring it?"

Even with Almas-level rapid regeneration, it would take several minutes to recover from silver bullets.

"They don't affect me. I'm human."

The large man shook his head.

"No, if you were human, you'd be dead."

The middle-aged man who had returned from checking outside gestured to his companions.

"Let's get out of here. The 'Shinjang' is coming."

Rian frowned.

"Shinjang? The royal palace guard?"

He had heard it would take time to form a task force.

'There's something I don't know.'

The large man pointed at Rian with his thumb.

"What about him?"

The silver-haired woman approached Rian closely.

"You're not a vampire?"

"As you can see... No, I can't prove it, but I hate drinking blood."

Even to her, Rian's face looked healthier than when his heart had stopped the previous day.

"Xenia, are you mistaken? If he were a vampire, you could sense the aura."

The silver-haired woman, Xenia, bit her lip.

"Not sensing it is the problem."

The middle-aged man made a decision.

"Then he's human. Are we taking him or not? If we get involved with the royal palace, things will get complicated."

Though they were vampire hunters, they didn't always use legal methods.

Xenia turned to Rian.

"If you're truly innocent, come with us. We'll find out at the headquarters."

"Alright."

Rian had no intention of staying here.

"But if it's revealed that you're a vampire..."

As she crossed the threshold, Xenia said coldly,

"I'll kill you myself."

The royal palace guard surrounded the blood bank.

"The door is broken!"

"Go in! Exterminate the vampires!"

Ten minutes later, a squad that had searched inside reported to the third division commander of the Shinjang.

"We found traces of vampires. But... they had already turned to ash."

"Damn it! Another hunter?"

"How do they always know and get here first? This operation was supposed to be completely confidential."

"Who knows? There are rumors of another vampire. Anyway, we're pulling out. Close the blood bank for now."

"Yes, sir!"

From the rooftop of a building 100 meters away, Rian watched the blood bank.

'So it was a secret operation.'

He could already picture Benoff losing his temper the next morning.

The middle-aged man asked,

"You don't seem to be from the royal palace. How did you know about the blood bank?"

"Just from here and there."

There was no need to explain further.

"We're hunters. We're called the Genocide Team. The last vampire hunters left in Rodenin."

'Genocide.'

The team name was quite telling.

"I'm an expert in drugs, Katain. This big guy here is Gunner Fowler. And the leader of Genocide, the swordswoman Xenia."

Xenia, with her silver hair cut short, had a sharp chin and thin eyebrows, giving her a stern look.

"Well, we don't know your identity yet, but—" Katain interrupted.

"What's your name?"

"Rian. Rian Ogent."

Xenia looked at Rian in surprise.

"The Knight of Maha?"

In this case, it was helpful that his name was known.

"That's what they call me."

Since the appearance of demons in the world, there had been rumors about a swordsman cutting down demons while crossing the desert alone.

'This man...'

He was much younger than she had imagined.

"But!"

Xenia's voice trembled.

"You were hit by the Impact Wave! Your heart shouldn't have survived. You weren't even breathing..."

"It's a long story." It wasn't something to discuss before dawn.

"Let's head back. We'll leave it to Dr. Grain."

Dr. Grain, who studied biological anomalies, might have an answer.

Xenia sighed and turned around.

"Follow me. I'll show you our hideout."
