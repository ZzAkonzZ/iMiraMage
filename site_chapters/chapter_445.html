Chapter 445:

Avant-garde.

By human standards, the design of the Diamond Armor was bold and innovative, yet beautiful.

The organic armor clung to the body's curves, with asymmetrical metallic bands running across it, and a fiery red cape flowed from head to toe.

The gloves, made of bone, were jointed like an insect's segments, covering the fingers, with nails as sharp as spikes.

The artificial brain had eyes clearly from Akamai, glaring at Garas.

'Perfect! This will hold it!'

Armand's additional functions were immediately integrated into Shirone's mind through the Ultima System.

Although he had already pushed the limits of his mental transcendence in his battle with Satan, this time he had a variety of options that were incomparable.

"Grrr!"

Unable to escape the Antithesis, Garas released aggressive pheromones.

As the sour smell spread, the Garas waiting nearby, following the king's command, all charged at Shirone.

"Watch out!"

Plu created a fiery barrier with Fire Mist, a natural enemy of living creatures, but Garas's obedience was almost mechanical.

Their sole target was Shirone, and they attacked from all sides, ignoring the burning of their flesh.

Rampage!

As Shirone cast the spell, slamming his fist down, a curtain of light exploded outward.

The power of Rampage, further enhanced by the law of the S-rank creature Jinma species Galtomic, was comparable to the durability of steel.

The Garas struck by the 40 hits per second had their bones crushed and were torn apart like whips.

Seeing this, Plu stopped fighting and retreated behind Shirone.

Garas had likely assimilated all the creatures in the command center's underground.

Given their exponential reproductive ability, defeating the lab's enemies would only result in endless waves.

'Phoenix Flame won't hold. This is beyond my capability.'

Her role in the project was always as a supporter.

While she could assist her team with versatile skills, she was no match for countless Garas.

Shirone, too, was part of the project as a terminator to destroy key factors and facilities, but now he was far beyond that level.

A biological weapon with all kinds of traits combined.

Garas had evolved into a form so powerful that it had no natural enemies among the thousands of species it had diversified into.

"Raaaargh!"

A giant lizard spewed fire, melting Armand, but the armor was restored by the regenerative ability of the Infinite Cell Proliferator Kenser.

Realizing the difficulty, the Garas horde sent feline-like monsters with sharp teeth and claws to attack.

In response, the metal of the Armored Mineral Species Ringer, with its supreme durability, formed, turning Shirone into an impregnable fortress.

Clang! Clang! Clang!

Unmatched hardness.

Garas's weapons shattered, and painful screams echoed through the lab.

The silver metal turned back into organic material, and the ends of the cape twisted into red tendrils.

The tendrils, which lashed at the enemies, had the teeth of the Devourer Kuzan, generating volatile energy by consuming organic matter.

As the teeth, locked like ankle traps, bit into Garas, explosive power surged into Shirone's body.

He launched himself into the Garas horde, linking scatter movements to unleash attacks.

With increased size, power, mass, and speed, the flashes that passed by caused Garas's flesh to burst.

"Kiaaa!"

The Garas king, who had been waiting for an opportunity amidst the deaths of its subordinates, emerged and cast a spell.

Compressed air surged like blades sharper than knives.

Ting-ting-ting!

The wind left dozens of silver lines on Shirone's armor.

The metal of Ringer, which hadn't broken under Etella's Yin-Yang Wave Fist, was impervious to the wind.

"I'll swallow you whole!"

Garas pressed down on Shirone's shoulder and opened its jaws to the limit.

As it tried to bite Shirone's face, the artificial brain activated Antithesis.

"Raaaargh!"

With Garas immobilized, tongue extended, Shirone focused his mind and prepared to counterattack.

Galtomic's ability amplified the law, and with a pop, a massive Photon Cannon, nearly a meter in diameter, was compressed.

"Take this…!"

The energy of the flash, created by mass and speed, burned Garas's body to ashes.

"Kik! Kik!"

With the death of their king, the Garas horde fell into disarray.

While Garas would risk death for the sake of reproduction, the predator before them now transcended living creatures.

Only a few continued to fight Shirone, while most turned and fled out the door.

"This is bad! We can't let them escape!"

If Garas left the underground lab, the local ecosystem would collapse.

Without a species to counter their influence, the damage would be unimaginable.

'There's no choice.'

Shirone activated Armand's final option.

A halo of black and white rotated above his head, calculating his desire.

Valhalla Action.

The time for his desire to reach the result was conveyed as a vague feeling.

Once approved, Shirone wasted no time deploying the magic circle.

Ataraxia skipped the accumulation process and appeared before him, automatically generating a Photon Cannon that fired forward.

Boom!

Plu closed her eyes and turned her head as the event unfolded without warning.

The bright flash, paralyzing her eye muscles, exploded, and a thunderous roar surged forward.

The flash lasted for a full three seconds.

During that time, the extreme mass wave swept away everything in its path.

Plu, who had been covering her ears and unable to move, slowly opened her eyes and turned her head.

It was a breathtaking sight.

The lab, once blocked like a lump of clay, was now pierced by the flash.

It was so natural in scale that it was hard to believe it was man-made.

Light streamed through the torn ceiling, and there were no Garas corpses in sight.

"Grrr!"

Shirone, with a twisted expression, glared forward.

To fill the cause for the result, Shirone would be incapacitated for about 23 seconds.

Thanks to the heightened limit of his mental transcendence, achieved through unity with Armand, he had gained this time.

Plu muttered in disbelief.

"To absorb even an angel's ability…"

Even with the equivalent exchange of cause and effect, reversing causality was a monumental event for a being that couldn't defy the flow of time.

Especially if he could instantly deploy the Archangel's super magic amplification array, the incapacitation was a minor drawback.

Once the Photon Cannon passed through Ataraxia, few creatures could survive its power.

After waiting comfortably for 23 seconds, Shirone exhaled deeply as the restraint lifted.

Armand, reverting to its sword form, emitted a strange glow.

Using precise control, Shirone floated the sword before him and examined it closely.

It looked as if blood flowed through the metal, a slightly grotesque but mesmerizingly addictive hue.

Ultima Version (Functions Installed: 6)

Ikasa's Valhalla Action: Equivalent exchange of cause and effect. Automatic calculation of exchange possibilities.

Ringer's Skin: Generates ultra-strong metallic material. Automatic response.

Galtomic's Magic Amplification: Explosive enhancement of the law.

Kuzan's Metabolism: Consumes organic matter to generate volatile energy.

Kenser's Absolute Restoration: Maximizes cell regeneration speed.

Akamai's Antithesis: Reverses the law to suppress the target's actions.

As Shirone mentally organized Armand's functions, he realized something he had overlooked.

Eight creatures had been combined, but only six options were added.

Excluding Armand, the main type, the missing trait belonged to Raycis.

'What happened? The Elixir of Legends should have been perfect. Is it because she's a four-sided Mara?'

Plu placed a hand on Shirone's shoulder as he pondered.

"Are you okay? After all that fighting, you're not hurt?"

Her voice trembled with excitement, having witnessed Shirone's incredible prowess up close.

With Armand's abilities, Shirone's combat power was formidable enough to face Heaven.

The most encouraging fact was that he was the terminator meant to deliver an unprecedented blow to Heaven.

The greatest fear was Shirone being killed before he could execute divine retribution.

But with the power to protect himself, the success rate of his mission would significantly increase.

"I'm fine. My lungs have healed. But… are we sure Garas is completely wiped out?"

Plu examined the area where the flash had passed.

"We were the only breeding targets in the lab, so they must have all come here."

"But there could be exceptions."

"No, I don't think so. Could a starving person resist the smell of meat after ten days? Garas's desire is much stronger than that."

Faces appeared through the cracked ceiling.

They were survivors buried in the debris.

Shirone sighed in relief.

If there were people to testify to Raycis's atrocities, today's events could be the starting point for rebel unification.

"Are you safe? What happened to the commander?"

"Dead, all of them."

The soldiers, hearing the translation from Shirone's drone, nodded with bitter expressions.

It was fortunate that the mad commander who used even his subordinates as test subjects was dead, but the damage to the command center was irreparable.

As Shirone emerged to the surface, the battered survivors gathered around him in a circle.

With the commander and officers dead, they had no one to rely on but the Light of Zone 73, Shirone.

"Nephilim, what should we do now?"

"Please become our commander. Only you can do it."

Honestly, he didn't want to.

It was a strange irony that those who had rejected Anke Ra and gathered in Purgatory were now looking to deify someone else.

'It might be easier that way. But…'

It was also dangerous.

When people relinquish their judgment to someone else, they can commit atrocities beyond human comprehension.

"Alright. I will carry out the rebel unification operation, but first, return and receive treatment."

It wasn't the way he preferred, but seeing the once despairing soldiers' eyes light up made him think it was enough.

Most importantly, Gaold's project was crucial, and sticking rigidly to his own ideas could be a dangerous idealism.

'It's over.'

Thus, the events at the Second Command came to an end.

Though they had successfully completed their mission, a heavy silence hung between the two.

There were still weighty matters in their hearts.

Life, creatures, humans.

In this place called Purgatory, where humans were just another species, what did their existence truly mean?

'Raycis…'

How could he explain that she, who preached the meaninglessness of the body, was actually the one most obsessed with it?

Is reason a product of evolution beyond animal instincts, or is it just another desire created to hide true desires?

Exhausted by endless questions, Shirone sighed and looked up at the sky.

A fly that had escaped from the underground flew up into the air.
