Chapter 1186:

Euphra, slumped against the wall, glared fiercely at Shirone.

“Grrr…”

With a low, beast-like growl, he raised his sword again and charged.

Shirone fell into thought.

‘Is the human heart truly free from the Law?’

The Veron Problem.

Faced with gold lying on the ground, an alluring partner before them, absolute power within their grasp…

‘How many would remain unshaken?’

A world controlled by the Law.

And now, the cold-hearted God had begun directly interfering with causes, manipulating humans.

‘OOPArts.’

A person who loses their heart is nothing more than an object.

“Yahweh, you are an error.”

Thus, even these words were being conveyed to Euphra as manipulated causes dictated by God.

A cold sweat ran down Shirone’s back.

‘Within the overlapping events, Euphra must have chosen life countless times. Yet in the end, he produced no results beyond becoming a mere tool of God—a mere OOPArts.’

And the reason was singular.

‘Because he is a creature of desire.’

Because he could not choose suffering, because he could not choose to die, because he could not yield.

‘As his desires clashed with others, that process ultimately became a predictable Law…’

And God computed it.

‘You claim you can manipulate the human heart? That we are nothing but tools of the Law?’

At the moment the blade touched Shirone’s neck, a surge of radiant light shoved Euphra aside.

With a thud, he crashed into the wall, and the luminous mist gradually morphed into a hand that pinned him down.

“Don’t talk nonsense. I am not an error. As long as I exist, the Veron Problem remains valid.”

Because it must be valid.

Euphra remained silent.

The Law manipulated him, but it had not granted him the ability to interpret his own essence.

“Yahweh.”

The light in Euphra’s eyes faded.

“You are an error.”

Like a machine ceasing operation, his head dropped completely downward.

‘Dead.’

Or rather, connection severed.

As the Hand of God vanished, Euphra’s body collapsed where he stood.

Having witnessed the fate of an OOPArts firsthand, Shirone turned his gaze toward the fallen Glen.

“Please… save Glen.”

‘He is not an OOPArts.’

He had come close, but Lucia’s arrival had kept him from losing his heart completely.

“I’ll try.”

His joints had twisted from the Left Hand of the Devil, but since the touch had been brief, no bones had broken.

Shirone used Miracle Stream to restore Glen’s vitality, then infused him with the light of Agape.

“Ghh…! I’ll kill you, Lucia. I’ll kill you.”

Shirone shook his head.

“Even Yahweh cannot change Glen’s cause. He has lived through countless events, and through his own will, he arrived at this state.”

Still believing in the strength of the human heart, Shirone refused to accept that Glen had simply been controlled by God.

“It is difficult to deny the life you’ve lived. But in the end, only you can change your own heart. All we can do is believe in Glen.”

Lucia wept.

“I saw it too. Glen’s past. The way High Priest Maximus looked at me.”

Shirone shared the news that Maximus had passed away in the Village of Heaven.

“I see… Now, I don’t know anymore. I believed in Glen. But love… it’s not always something beautiful, is it?”

“It is beautiful.”

Shirone supported Glen as he spoke.

“It doesn’t matter what name you give to the emotion. What matters is the heart that carries it through.”

“A heart that carries it through…”

“If you truly love Glen, then don’t give up. No matter what it is, if you see it through, it will be beautiful.”

Something clicked in Lucia’s mind, and she stood up.

“I understand now. The reason I didn’t become like Glen or Euphra. I was meant to come here.”

Now that she thought about it…

Events had overlapped within the pyramid, yet Lucia’s cause had not changed.

“That’s right.”

And there was only one reason.

“To God, you are already a completed result. Meeting Ares, Glen bringing you here—none of it was mere coincidence. But… why?”

Why did God need Lucia?

“The Left Hand of the Devil.”

A sudden transformation that had come upon someone who once performed miracles with the Right Hand of God.

Lucia cried out.

“I think I know how to meet God! We need to gather the others!”

That must have been why God brought Shirone here.

‘You intend to use me as well. But things won’t go according to your calculations.’

It felt like he was hearing a distant whisper.

— Save yourself from this dreadful truth.

“Alright, let’s go.”

As she followed Shirone, Lucia looked down at Glen’s unconscious face.

‘No one else can do this for you. You have to do it yourself, Glen. I won’t waver, either.’

Green Ocean.

The elves’ weapon against the invading fairies was none other than nature itself—the boundless sea of trees.

Giant trees swung their branches like whips, and stone golems trampled the fairies underfoot.

Boom!

A terrible crash made the fairy troops hesitate.

“Damn! Their resistance isn’t easy to break.”

“We expected this. More importantly, we need to focus on capturing the specimen.”

Crown wanted an elf.

“That’s only if we can break through this bizarre forest. Cowards, hiding behind nature.”

“These fire creatures or whatever… They have an incredible ability. They’re controlling the entire forest.”

At that moment, a grand vibration resonated through the air.

“Is it time?”

The fairies turned to see the Archangel of Destruction, Uriel, striding forward.

“Oh, Archangel!”

Spinning his Paradise Staff, he spoke.

“Step aside.”

Before waiting for an answer, Uriel charged into the forest and struck the ground with his staff.

"Celestial Wheel."

A massive explosion consumed hundreds of meters of the forest.

“Ugh…!”

Protea, the leader of the fire tribe, curled up just as the seismic waves reached them.

The elven leader, Enox, approached.

“What’s happening?”

“The Subworld Creation Field has been momentarily severed. The power of an Archangel is too monstrous.”

Most of the fire tribe had perished in the Demon War, leaving only a handful of survivors.

Flarrino, who had been controlling the river to douse the fires, spoke in despair.

“We will be pushed back at this rate. Why does Yahweh not come? Are we truly… unworthy of entering the realm of humanity?”

“That is not true, Flarrino.”

Enox spoke firmly.

“We must save ourselves. If we do not fight and struggle as humans do, then even if Yahweh comes, nothing will change. We would remain nothing but livestock.”

“But…”

“Humans were afraid too. They suffered too. If we do not overcome that, we cannot reach the center of the world.”

She raised her voice.

“Now! Forward, elves! How long will we bear the disgrace of being called cowards by the fairies?”

Three hundred elves exchanged determined glances.

“Full army, adva—”

Before Enox could finish, a tremendous shockwave swept forward, pushing them back a hundred meters.

“Gah…!”

Clearing the dust away with wind magic, they saw the towering figure of Uriel approaching.

‘Such overwhelming power…’

Among those with will, he was likely one of the strongest beings in the universe.

“Why do you interfere in a war between species?”

Just like Yahweh, an Archangel had no reason to meddle in the war between elves and fairies.

Uriel smirked and crouched slightly.

“Perhaps because I, too, am a species?”

Simply lowering his stance radiated power like an impending explosion.

The elves turned pale, and even Enox felt dizzy.

“This war ends here.”

The fairies cried out.

“Lord Uriel! The elves must be spared—”

Before they could finish, Uriel had already swung his Paradise Staff and charged.

‘Is this the end…?’

As Enox sank into despair, a man suddenly crashed into Uriel’s side.

Boom!

Uriel was sent flying, leaving the elves and fairies stunned.

“… Who are you?”

A handsome man in a gray coat stood there, his curly hair and metallic eyes making him stand out.

“Gold Dragon Metira.”

A split second later, a sonic boom echoed as the remaining Twelve Apostles landed simultaneously.

“… Ah.”

Tears welled up in Enox’s eyes.

‘So Yahweh has not abandoned us.’

The Poison Dragon, Poine, smiled.

“A different species interfering in a battle for survival? That’s cheating, don’t you think, Uriel?”

The ground trembled as Uriel approached.

“Unwanted guests.”

Seated among the Twelve Apostles, the golden-haired boy Light Dragon Phaeton sneered.

“What, getting cold feet all of a sudden? Don’t worry. Someone like you? I can handle you by myself.”

Uriel raised his right arm.

“A single arm should be enough to balance the fight. I could even discard my staff if you’d like.”

“Hah! Hahahaha!”

Phaeton’s face froze in a twisted grin as one side of his face morphed into a dragon’s.

“You. Get over here, you bastard.”

Uriel beckoned lazily, and Phaeton’s eyes gleamed with light.

“This son of a—”

Before he could move, Infernal Dragon Infercus streaked toward Uriel.

“Let’s take a beating.”

Her leather-clad body ignited, engulfing the battlefield in flames.

Enox clenched her teeth as she summoned water magic.

‘This is just the beginning.’

The war between elves and fairies.

The victor of this war would be absorbed into humanity and shape the future of the world.

‘Who will carve their will into history?’

The results of this war were already being processed through the filters of the Law, accumulating at the terminus of all information.

Apocalypse.

Suddenly, the ground trembled, and Shirone’s excavation team looked around in alarm.

“What was that?”

Though the quake wasn’t strong, it felt as if the entire world was changing.

“Shirone! Look at this!”

Marsha held up an artifact, which began rusting and rapidly decaying in her hands.

“A new future is taking shape. At an incredible speed.”

Shirone bit his lip.

‘That means the change in the Law is drastic. Has a major variable been introduced?’

Sedimentation of time.

As present events were forcibly pushed into the past, the excavation team tensed up.

“Is it… over?”

The world of the Apocalypse had completely transformed.

“… It’s clean.”

Where there had once been ruins and debris, an ultramodern mechanical city now stood.

Marsha asked anxiously,

“So… who won? The elves? The fairies? Who built this city?”

From between the buildings, seven androids emerged.

“Huh?”

Their heads were egg-shaped with black visor-like screens, and their bodies were made of silvery metal.

‘They resemble High Gears…’

They all wore identical monk’s robes, with electronic prayer beads fastened around their necks and wrists.

“Identifying sentient beings.”

The androids simultaneously turned toward them.

“Damn, they spotted us.”

As the Parrot Mercenaries tensed, a Sanskrit inscription scrolled across the androids’ screens.

"Form is emptiness. Emptiness is form."

Shirone wasn’t startled by their cold mechanical voices.

He was shocked by the depth of enlightenment displayed on their visors.

‘Buddha?’

Marsha, looking as if she were about to panic, stammered.

“Shirone, are they enemies? Please say they are. So I can turn around and run without hesitation.”

"Form is emptiness. Emptiness is form."

Final Evolution of Humanity – Final Type Z.
