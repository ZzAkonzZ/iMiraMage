Chapter 614:

"Quite a sight," said Fjord, sitting at the party table with Pisho.

At school, Fjord was known as a sinister figure who always wore a robe and indulged in wicked amusements, but today he was dressed neatly. Anyone who didn't know him would never guess that his specialty was in the deadly poison series of toxic magic.

"Kaiden will graduate this time," Pisho said.

Through the Scramble Royal, Pisho had learned that Kaiden was the president of the Women's Anatomy Research Society. But now that the Golden Wheel was disbanded, Kaiden was preparing to enter the world.

If it weren't for the cost of the trial, Kaiden would have easily made it into the top 10, but Fjord had never missed the upper ranks either.

"I don't care about someone like Kaiden."

Fjord cut his wrist with a sharp thumbnail and let blood drip into his glass.

"…What poison is that?"

"Kedraphusa."

A plant that grows only on a small uninhabited island in the equatorial sea, its pure extract could kill an elephant in under a minute.

After swirling the glass and taking a sip, the taste was so intense it made his tongue tingle.

"Heh, that's the stuff."

"Your body is full of poison."

"The human liver is a huge chemical warehouse. Once you adapt, even poison becomes medicine."

But most people couldn't adapt, which is why it was called poison.

"Eat well. During the graduation exam, everyone's liver will melt."

Roscas Fjord (Final Rank: 9th in the graduating class).

Major: Toxic Magic, Deadly Poison Series.

Special Notes: Massacre magic that does not distinguish between friend and foe.

"Excuse me…"

A female student from Bermin Magic School approached.

With her hair up, her dark circles were visible under her eyes, and her lips were purple.

"Fjord, right?"

The world of toxic magic was small, so Fjord knew who she was.

"The Witch of Decay, Windia."

She nodded expressionlessly.

"Yes. Did you put Kedraphusa in your drink earlier?"

"So what?"

For the first time, Windia showed emotion.

"You detoxified Kedraphusa?"

"Well… I've been consuming it since birth."

The Roscas family developed chemical weapons for war with the kingdom's approval.

Blinking, Windia cautiously asked, "Can I try it?"

"Go ahead."

Fjord offered, and she gently took the glass and touched her tongue to it.

"Ugh!"

The moment it touched her tongue, the intense sensation hit her brain.

She had trained her liver with many poisons, but this was beyond her tolerance.

"Wow, that's amazing."

Crawling back from the brink of death, she kept exclaiming.

"I've never tasted such delicious poison in my life."

"I'm glad you like it."

Looking at the wound on Fjord's wrist, Windia licked her lips and grabbed her hair.

"Want to try mine? It's Taropascal."

With a crunch, her hair broke off in the middle.

"Taropascal?"

A poison that rots the skin.

Fjord neatly arranged the hair and chewed it.

"Hmm, this is…"

"How is it?"

"Well made. A bit bland, though."

If Kedraphusa was a spicy dish for Fjord, Taropascal was like a side vegetable.

"Right? I want to synthesize it with something."

"Try adding the juice of the Pustan mushroom. It might reduce the decay time…"

As their discussion on poisons continued, Pisho sighed and left the table.

With nowhere to rest, he wandered until a student from the Royal Magic School cautiously approached from behind.

"Senior, long time no see."

A disciple who had studied under Francis Kaylor, an authority on insect magic.

"Yes. Are you prepared for the graduation exam?"

"It's not particularly important to me. The master emphasizes the achievement of learning over results. But this time, he seems to have made a big decision."

Pisho tilted his head slightly.

"What do you mean?"

"He wants you to graduate and come back. You've been here too long."

The disciple took something out of his inner pocket and handed it over.

"He has authorized its use."

A transparent vial containing a wriggling, dark red parasite.

"See you in the capital next year."

As the disciple left, Pisho put the vial deep in his inner pocket.

'…I must graduate.'

Maranov Pisho (Final Rank: 24th in the graduating class).

Major: Insect Magic, Evolution Acceleration Series.

Special Notes: Disciple of Kaylor.

As night fell and the party reached its peak, conflicts began to arise among the students who had been engaging in psychological battles.

Alpheas Magic School students were no exception, and the Royal Magic School students started picking on Suabi.

"Hey, you. What's your rank at school?"

Suabi, who was not very sociable, hesitated and stepped back when confronted.

"What's it to you? Move. I don't want to talk to you."

As Suabi tried to bypass them, a huge student over 2 meters tall blocked his way.

"What did we do to make you not want to talk? We're just asking."

"I'm 18th. Satisfied?"

She tried to pass, but the huge student felt like a black wall blocking her way.

"Haha! Just a mid-rank utility magic user? I told you, the Spirit magazine was lying."

The Royal Magic School students believed that Olivia, the vice principal, was the reason Scout Baikal was taken by Alpheas Magic School.

As a former principal of the Royal Magic School and a member of the teachers' council, she had considerable influence.

"Listen up. No matter how hard you try, you'll never match the Royal Magic School."

Suddenly, Suabi's eyes sharpened.

Even with perfect mental focus, being intimidated by students from another school could affect her performance in the exam.

"Oh! Did you see that glare? Scary!"

"Hey, you guys, knock it off."

The students surrounding Suabi turned to see Screamer standing with his hands in his pockets.

"Who are you?"

"Do I need to say? From what I hear, you seem to know the Spirit magazine by heart."

Hyde Allen, ranked 3rd at the Royal Magic School, walked over.

"Kids playing in the lower ranks are fun to watch, but I don't remember your face."

"Suabi, come here."

Ignoring them, Screamer gestured, but the huge student didn't move.

"If you're confident, come and get her. Crawl between my legs, and I won't do anything rough."

The Royal Magic School graduates burst into laughter.

"Seriously…"

Given Screamer's personality, he would have flipped out by now, but he held back and walked over.

"Suabi, let's go. Don't bother with these idiots."

"Idiots?"

"Maybe you're relaxed because you get 20 passes easily, but we don't have that luxury."

"Haha! Of course. That's the difference between the Royal and regular schools."

"I heard your average ability is 3.8 times lower than ours."

The comment hit a nerve, and the huge student's eyebrow twitched.

"Do you believe that nonsense? It's a lie."

"I think so too. The magazine must have added a dot. Looking at you, it seems like it's at least 38 times."

"You bastard!"

The huge student grabbed Screamer's collar and pulled him close.

"Do you think you can talk like that because of the magic control device? You're nothing."

"Really? You can't even lift me?"

"What?"

The huge student looked down and realized Screamer's feet were still on the ground.

"This…"

He tried harder, but Screamer didn't budge, as if rooted to the spot.

"I remember now. That guy…"

Before Allen could speak, Screamer grabbed the huge student's collar.

Despite the height difference, Screamer's strength quickly reversed the situation.

"Ugh!"

As Screamer pulled the huge student down, it was as if a hole opened in the ground, and he fell.

As the huge student knelt, unable to believe it, a massive fist stopped just before his face, creating a powerful gust of wind.

"Ugh!"

The sound of the punch echoed, and the huge student couldn't help but remember.

'The Fist Mage Screamer.'

Screamer released the huge student's collar and twisted his hand away.

"I get that you're upset, but if Royal Magic Academy is pathetic, that's your problem. None of our school cares about you."

Logan Screamer (Final Rank: 10th in the graduating class).

Major: Photon Magic, Fist Mage Series.

Special Notes: Completed the Iron Fist Project of the combat fanatic Pyroker, capable of one-hit kills.

"These guys dare to look down on us!"

The Royal Magic School students all stood up and charged at Screamer.

Alpheas Magic School students didn't back down and matched their numbers.

The party atmosphere turned into a power struggle between the two schools, with the remaining three schools watching the situation seriously.

"Do you want to fight? Just because some country bumpkins got a little praise, you're getting cocky!"

Electric Monster Lycan grinned wickedly and gestured for them to come.

"Haha! Cocky? Fine, if you're confident, come on. I'll make you the biggest fool who died before the graduation exam."

"Hmph! Do you think we don't have Schema users? Without the magic control device, you'd all be dead today!"

As the shouting grew louder, Maya stomped her feet.

"This is bad. We can't fight today."

Kaiden gently blocked her view and said, "It's okay. Nothing will happen."

At least for Maya.

"Come out now! Let's see who's the best in the kingdom!"

As the Royal Magic School students provoked, Fermi eagerly stepped into the scene.

"That's it. In that case, I have a fun game to propose…"

"Fermi, shut up!"

Everyone who knew Fermi shouted.

"Don't even think about getting involved!"

Fermi had a knack for turning everything into chaos.

"Hmm, a magic control device."

Dante, sitting at a nearby table, swirled his drink and pondered. A man with curly red hair from the Royal Magic School approached him.

"Long time no see, Dante."

It was Curtis, who had once competed with Dante at the Royal Magic School.

"You're here too?"

"Haha, don't pretend you don't know. It's all over Spirit magazine."

"You think I read that magazine?"

A year ago, Dante was overwhelmed with interview requests from all directions.

After a moment of silence, Curtis regained his composure and spoke.

"Do you know what I regret most as graduation approaches? Not having faced you at the Royal Magic School."

"Sounds like you're sure you'll graduate."

"Of course. It's not a boast, but I'm ranked number one at the Royal Magic School."

It was a boast.

"And you're number two. Even the great Dante has his limits."

Who would have thought that Dante, once considered the kingdom's top talent, would lose to an unknown like Shirone and be dethroned by a long-term student like Fermi?

"Well, maybe. If you're confident, try to stop me."

Dante pointed to Fermi with his drink.

'Haha, his pride is deeply wounded. That's the price for ignoring me, Dante.'

Curtis, thinking this, walked over to the scene.

"Everyone, stop."

With the arrival of the top-ranked student, the Royal Magic School students fell silent. Fermi turned to Sabina and said,

"See? Even among top ranks, the treatment is different."

"You know, you're best when you're quiet."

Curtis approached and extended his hand.

"On behalf of the Royal Magic School, I apologize. Let's stop this here. There's no benefit in causing a scene at the association."

Fermi shook his hand.

"I'd like to, but as you can see, our school has a tradition of ignoring the top rank."

"Are you saying you want to continue?"

"No. I'm saying you should talk to someone who listens."

"Who would that be? Dante?"

"He's coming now."

As Fermi pointed, all 149 students turned to look at the door.

"What's going on? Isn't this a party?"

Shirone blinked innocently, looking around.
