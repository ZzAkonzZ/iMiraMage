Chapter 421:

A desperate cry echoed through the Great World War.

Arius, shot by electricity, flew 10 meters and rolled on the ground.

The magic Apoptosis, which dives into one's thoughts, trapped him in an eternal concept where time did not exist.

It was a kind of mental death, and the shock upon returning was incomparable to the fear of a newborn baby.

"Aaah! Aaah!"

Arius couldn't stop screaming.

Everything was confusing and unsettling.

Only the faint remnants of memories vaguely told him who he was.

At the boundary of that oblivion, a thin thread of intelligence flowed in and connected with his memories.

The scene of casting magic to avoid Captain Walker's sword in the Kazra Palace came to mind, and the screams subsided.

'Damn, I can't keep doing this.'

Wiping the drool from his chin, Arius stood up, feeling suspicious of the unfamiliar surroundings.

"Where is this...?"

If someone had pulled him out of Apoptosis, it would be one of the Seven Magical Sins, and it should be their secret hideout, but the scenery was entirely different.

Everything was in a light purple metallic color, with bizarre machinery emitting light and operating.

"Are you the grave robber?"

Arius turned around with an unpleasant expression.

But he had to look up, and his pupils contracted in surprise.

A halo above the head, wings of light several times larger than the body.

An angel.

'Why is an angel here...?'

As the question filled his mind, he quickly suppressed it with the Flicker magic battery.

He had to escape.

Thinking came next.

Just as his body turned into light with a flash, Uriel's hand moved faster than a moment.

"Ugh!"

Arius, grabbed by the neck, struggled three meters above the ground.

It felt like his neck bone was being held, not just his neck.

"S-save me...!"

When Uriel slammed Arius to the ground, he arched his back in pain and groaned.

Then, struggling, he knelt on one knee and looked up at the angels.

"Damn, who are you?"

"I am Uriel, the Angel of Destruction."

"U-Uriel?"

One of the eight archangels in Heaven.

Arius quickly scanned his surroundings.

He recognized the objects, devices, and the galaxy mirror, realizing it was the Great World War.

'Then the one next to him is Kariel...'

With two archangels present, even Arius of the Seven Magical Sins had no chance of leaving this place alive on his own.

"I have something to show you."

Kariel, who had lifted Arius into the air, flew to the galaxy mirror and threw him again.

"Ugh!"

Falling from his solar plexus, he gasped for breath and then realized someone was in front of him, struggling to lift his head.

A woman was sitting cross-legged.

"A human?"

Uriel spoke.

"Adrias Miro."

Arius's eyes widened in shock.

"M-Miro? This woman?"

To a scale magician, Miro was an unforgettable name.

But even the grave robber Arius had never seen her face before.

So this is what she looks like.

An ordinary girl you could see anywhere in the world. The kind that might catch your eye for a moment if you passed her on the street.

But the adjectives attached to her were all superlatives: best, last, ultimate, strongest.

So Arius couldn't help but stare at the young girl's face, mesmerized.

Beautiful, just by existing.

Quickly regaining his senses, Arius analyzed the situation based on existing information.

Archangels, Great World War, Miro.

'Damn it. So it's come to this?'

Miro being in Heaven could be interpreted as the final war being imminent.

'What the hell is going on? Do they know in the original world?'

He wanted to talk to Miro, but she showed no signs of waking up.

Arius looked at Kariel and asked.

"Did you put her to sleep?"

"No. Miro is in the dimension of emptiness. You can bring her back to her original state."

'Dimension of emptiness...'

Arius's face twisted.

'Damn! It's Samadhi.'

Now he was starting to get the picture.

As long as Miro was in Samadhi, she couldn't be killed. And that was why they had summoned him, the grave robber.

"Destroy Miro's dimension of emptiness."

Arius snorted.

When Uriel had grabbed him, he instinctively begged for his life, but now that he had regained his intellect, death was not a priority.

If he pulled Miro out of the dimension of emptiness, her spacetime would be destroyed, and humanity would face extinction.

'I don't really care, but I don't like the idea of doing what these guys want.'

Still, he had no intention of giving up.

He was about to explore the mind of one of the greatest magicians in human history.

He wanted to know what was inside this woman's head.

What she liked, what she hated, what made her feel shame, and what secrets she would take to her grave.

"Destroy Samadhi?"

Arius frowned.

"Are you kidding me?"

"If you can't, you will die."

"You don't think that's a threat, do you? This woman is the strongest scale magician in humanity. Anyone who dives in will be crushed by the egoist."

"Then you will die."

When Kariel extended his hand, Arius raised both hands as if to calm him down.

"Wait. I didn't say I wouldn't do it. I'm just saying we need to find a way around it."

"A way around? Her mind?"

"If I dive in now, I'll be swept away by Samadhi. But what about Drimo? In Drimo, where any thought flows, I can collect Miro's mind. There, I can find a strong enough keyword to break Samadhi."

"Keyword?"

"Every human has trauma. Trauma so severe that just thinking about it stops their breath. We create a powerful net with those and throw it into the abyss. Then when we pull it, we catch the big fish, Miro."

Kariel stared at Arius, who was excitedly explaining.

Is he sane? How can he be so enthusiastic about destroying humanity?

'I can't understand humans at all.'

Arius set a condition.

"But Drimo is a multidimensional structure, not a place you can investigate at will. So give me some information. You must know something."

"Hmm."

Suddenly, Kariel's halo expanded into a ring.

As Arius's face turned pale, a black sphere emerged, tearing through space.

"Come forth, Brahma."

"Kiiiii!"

A hooked nose appeared first, followed by a red-skinned face.

The eyes were slanted unpleasantly, and the teeth were irregular.

Three glowing triangles floated on the forehead, and the brain extended beyond the crown, flowing like hair down to the small figure's feet.

Kariel's familiar, the three-horned Mara Brahma.

In some parts of the human world, he was called the god of creation.

"Lowly Brahma greets Archangel Kariel."

"Follow this human to Drimo. Assist him in whatever he investigates."

Arius was dumbfounded.

'Damn, Brahma?'

It was shocking for a magician to see the god of intelligence from mythology looking like that.

But he quickly regained his composure.

This was an opportunity.

With the knowledge of the three-horned Mara, he could access the deepest parts of Drimo, where humanity had never reached.

'Hehe, the god of magic is smiling upon me.'

Arius approached Miro.

He would uncover everything about her.

And when he returned, he would be the god of the world.

"Let's go. Heaven seems quite busy."

Brahma extended a strand of his brain through Miro's mouth into her brain.

As the brain area governing Drimo activated, a small spacetime tunnel appeared before him.

Arius leaped in without hesitation, followed by Brahma.

"Whew."

Only after the spacetime tunnel closed did Kariel sit down.

Summoning the three-horned Mara had drained all his remaining power.

"Are you alright?"

"My strength will return with time. Once Miro wakes up, humanity is finished. I'll have the right to initiate at the archangel council."

"It might not succeed. Brahma is not in a normal state due to the law of familiars."

The combined abilities of the three Maras an angel commands can never surpass the angel's abilities.

This follows the principle that no acquired concept can appear without a preceding concept.

This is the law of familiars.

With Kariel's weakened presence, the three-horned Mara Brahma couldn't exert his full power.

"It doesn't matter. Brahma was born by mimicking my intellect. He can make decisions in a second that would take ten thousand cautious humans to reach a compromise. It will take time, but he can do it."

It would take time. But what did it matter?

After all, it was the last time humanity had left.

The strategy room was engulfed in silence.

Niflheim.

A place even the mainland's border guards were forbidden to approach, inhabited only by evil spirits that once terrified humans.

Crude gave realistic advice again.

"Let me be clear, it's an extremely dangerous place. Considering the value of Black Elixir, many have tried, but no one has set foot there for thousands of years. The reason is simple. If you go, you die. So choose wisely."

Sein asked.

"Do you think we have a choice in this situation?"

"Even if you don't go to Niflheim, we can maintain our alliance. The opportunity isn't just now. But for the 1st Headquarters to engage in full-scale war, these three conditions must be met."

Choosing the safe route would take a long time. On the other hand, the quick route was too dangerous.

"Gaold, what will you do?"

Sein left the decision to the commander.

But in reality, it was just a formality. With no way of knowing Miro's fate, there was only one choice he could make.

"We're going to Niflheim."

"Hmm."

Crude let out a long sigh.

"I hold you in high regard. That's why I want to dissuade you. Using the light of Sector 73 to expand the rebel forces is also a good strategy. Why are you in such a hurry? Is there a special reason?"

Haste leads to mistakes.

Gaold knew this too, which is why he was desperately trying to stay calm.

But objectively, time was not on their side.

They couldn't kill Miro in Samadhi, but they wouldn't be doing this without a plan either.

"You don't need to know that. We'll meet the three conditions as quickly as possible. Is that satisfactory?"

"Of course. If it's possible."

Crude remained skeptical until the end.

"Now we'll split into teams. Kangnan, Zulu, and I will go to Niflheim."

Sein asked, "What about the rest?"

"Armin, Siina, Etella, and Kuan will handle the military community. Shirone and Plu will persuade the 2nd Headquarters. This is the only way."

Sein agreed, considering the difficulty of the tasks and the distribution of personnel.

"Then I'll stay here and work on the strategy. I also need to understand planetary movements."

To accurately strike Aravot with divine retribution, precise predictions of the landing point were necessary. Understanding planetary movements was a prerequisite.

"Are you really going to Niflheim?"

Crude couldn't understand Shirone's group.

Even with ten people, or even with the rebels' support, it wouldn't be enough, yet they were splitting up.

"Are you out of your mind?"

Gaold replied as he left.

"No."
