Chapter 282: Late Night Ball (3)

"Well, I'm just the seller. Since you've seen the demonstration directly, shouldn't the buyer make the offer?"

Zion bit his lip.

No matter how much he tried to deny it, the reality was clear. Ataraxia was far more valuable than Armand, the cursed sword. No, beyond value, he wanted to have it by any means necessary.

"4 billion. That’s nearly my entire fortune. That should be enough, right?"

"Hmm, four billion."

Four billion was a massive amount, enough to build four magic academies at current rates. But Arius still wasn't satisfied.

Honestly, it wouldn't have mattered how much was offered.

Demon Sword Armand was sold for 3 billion, but if it had gone to the Curia Auction House, it would have easily been classified as at least S-grade, with buyers offering blank checks for it.

The reason it couldn't be auctioned was because it was stolen goods.

Of course, most Objet are stolen goods, but putting something directly stolen up for auction risked being tracked by pursuers.

Since money didn’t hold significant importance to Arius anyway, selling it to the safest option, Zion, was the most profitable deal.

When Arius showed that he was not satisfied with even 4 billion, Zion grew impatient. Desire only intensifies when something was out of reach.

However, even if he scraped together all his assets, he could only add another seven million at best.

If it were Uorin, she would have at least ten times the 4.7 billion Zion could gather, but wasting a favor from her for mere money was foolish.

Knowing that Arius wasn’t someone particularly driven by material wealth, Zion decided to play his final card.

"On top of that, I’ll place you in the Terraje Faction."

As expected, Arius's eyes lit up with interest.

Terraje, Empress of Kashan, as one of the three most powerful emperors on the continent. With her protection, Arius could stop living as a fugitive.

However, Zion had no authority to grant such favors. The only gateway to Terraje Faction was Uorin.

Zion glanced sideways at his sister’s expression before asking her.

"Uorin, you can make that happen for me?"

This was his first time asking her for a favor, and he knew it would be the last. With Ataraxia in his hands, he could rise to the top on his own power.

Just once, just this once.

“Then Shirone Oppa would die?”

"Probably. There’s no guarantee decapitation will kill him, though."

Knowing that his sister had some fondness for Shirone, Zion mimicked the crazy logic Arius had used. But it was a decent enough excuse.

“Hmm.”

Uorin placed her finger on her lips, looking up at the ceiling.

She had no interest in getting involved in Kazra’s politics, but seeing Zion struggling lately made her feel a bit sorry for him. In a way, his incompetence was pitiful. Since this seemed like his last chance, she figured it wouldn’t hurt to show a bit of kindness.

"Fine. I’ll speak to Mother for you."

Zion breathed a sigh of relief.

It was ironic to feel anxious over something that should be natural for family, but now that he had her approval, nothing else mattered.

"Good, the deal is done. 4 billion, and Terraje Faction. Tonight, cut Shirone’s head off and bring me Ataraxia."

Arius blinked and raised his hand.

“Hold on a second. You’re asking me to do that job?”

“What do you mean? If the Grave Robber doesn’t do it, who will?”

"No, well, Your Highness doesn't know, but this is a specialized profession. I'm an expert in grave robbing, not combat. Especially, I really hate seeing blood."

Zion stared at him in disbelief.

No matter how many non-combat mages there are in the world, they all have enough combat power to overcome dangerous situations.

How could someone who was just raving about extracting brains say they don't want to see blood?

“Does it make sense that you can’t fight? But still, you could best Shirone, right?”

"It's not about winning or losing. I may be cursed all over the world, but I'm an authority in psychoanalysis. At one point, I was even named one of the top 100 minds in the world. you imagine someone like me holding a sword and cutting off the head of an eighteen-year-old boy?"

"Then what am I supposed to do? Go and kill him myself?"

"If you could, that would be best."

As Arius made a gesture of passing the task onto him, Zion gritted his teeth. But since Arius refused, he couldn’t force him to do it.

“Fine. I’ll do it myself.”

"Oh, as expected of the Prince. Very manly."

When Arius egged him on, Zion snorted. There were plenty of people who could secretly cut off the head of an eighteen-year-old boy without looking outside.

“But I’m not going to dirty my hands with blood. We’ll need to bring in a specialist for this. Zenogre.”

With that, Zion turned his gaze to the air in front of him.

His gaze, which had caught something, slowly lowered and fixed on something beyond Arius's shoulder.

Feeling the sharp chill of murderous intent brushing against the back of his neck, Arius forced a stiff smile.

“Huhu, now I’m too scared to even turn around."

Just as Arius finished speaking, the murderous intent vanished as if it had never existed. Moments later, a man descended before Arius, hanging upside down.

A thin thread, seemingly coming from his waist, was connected to the ceiling. He had his legs crossed as if sitting, and his hands were gathered at his lower abdomen forming a circle.

His face was gaunt, with a sharp, thin nose that jutted down from his brow in a straight vertical line. His lips were so thin they were nearly nonexistent. Especially, his eyes were not the usual almond shape but perfectly round, and even those were small.

“Have you called for me, Your Highness?”

Zion glanced back at Arius and introduced him.

"Say hello. This is my personal bodyguard, Spatur Zenogre. Zenogre, this is Arius."

Still hanging upside down, Zenogre twisted the thread to face Zion and gave a slight nod. He then turned to Arius.

“Of course, I know him well. We’ve crossed paths many times. Though this person is seeing me for the first time, of course."

Arius cracked a thin smile.

It was definitely his first time seeing Zenogre. But Zenogre must have been watching him every time he came to the gallery.

“Wow, impressive skills. Where were you hiding?”

Zenogre responded with a deadpan face, devoid of any emotion.

“Hiding? I was behind you the whole time?”

“Oh, such joke….”

Arius’ bird-like eyes narrowed even further.

Perhaps it might be true. Without eyes on the back of your head, the world behind you practically doesn’t exist.

But since it's not easy to trail one of the Seven Prodigious, it would be better for mental health to think of it as a joke.

“Do you think I’m lying?”

As he spoke, the flesh on both sides of his cheekbones and temples bulged out. Then, tearing through the skin, 6 additional eyes, 3 on each side, emerged.

They were eyes without pupils, glowing purple like grapes.

A total of 8 eyes. His blind spot would probably be at most 15 degrees.

'A mimicry ability user...'

As Arius suspected, Zenogre was a master of Spider Mimicry, a form of Schema body technique.

Mimicry refers to the rapid transformation of cellular characteristics through Schema to create organs that are entirely different from those of a human.

However, to achieve such extreme transformations like Zenogre, genetic enhancements were necessary.

The Spartur Clan, who have been responsible for the safety of Terraje Family for generations, have transformed their bodies into spider-like traits for nearly a thousand years. As this has been passed down through inheritance, they have acquired the ability to perfect the art of Spider Mimicry.

Arius turned his gaze away from Zenogre and looked at Uorin.

Terraje's children always have personal bodyguards. Therefore, Uorin's bodyguard must surely be here too.

Arius expanded his line of sight as much as he could without drawing suspicion. Yet, no matter where he looked, no assassin was visible.

Perhaps... they might be standing behind him.

The thought gave him a slight chill.

Engaging the Spirit Zone in the presence of royalty could raise suspicions, so he refrained. Even if he used his enhanced senses to scan the area, it was likely they wouldn’t be detected.

Skilled assassins often mastered techniques that rendered them invisible to Spirit Zone.

'Haha, this is why I can't give up on this life.'

As the strange introductions ended, Zion returned to the main topic.

“Now, let’s come up with a plan. Zenogre will handle the task of killing Shirone—no, separating his brain from his body. Once his head is severed, you can step in and extract Ataraxia.”

Arius no longer had any complaints.

"Of course. Mobility won’t be an issue. But the second thing we must consider is stealth. No one outside of this room must ever find out about this operation."

Zion immediately understood and asked Zenogre.

"You can ensure no one will find out, right?"

"That won’t be a problem. When you tail someone long enough, there’s always a moment when they’re alone. Even if that's not the case, I can simply kill everyone present, right?"

"Of course, that could be one way. But I'd like to minimize variables if possible."

Arius said.

“The tricky ones are the two women. Reina can be easily distracted since she has her duties, but that girl Amy will stick close to Shirone. She’s also the daughter of Carmis Family. If we leave her free, she could create unexpected variables.”

Hearing Arius’ words, Zenogre’s face once again grew eight additional eyes.

Activating his mimicry ability, he became the perfect spider. Even though he was standing right in front of them, it was as if his presence had blurred.

"Mistakes do not exist for me."

Arius gestured with both hands to calm him down.

"Whoa, whoa, it's not that I don't trust your skills. I'm just saying hypothetically."

Zion, who had been resting his chin in thought, made his decision.

“Fine. While you two handle the job, I’ll keep Amy occupied. It should only take about five minutes to kill him, right? So I just need to keep her busy for, what, an hour at most?”

“Hoo, that’s your preference, huh?”

Zion didn't deny it.

"Well... she's pretty, isn't she? I suppose it would suit my taste to play with her for a night.”

Arius felt bitter about the words coming from the boy's mouth. Well, growing up in the palace, Zion’s experiences were certainly different from those of other adolescents of the same age.

Anyway, he wasn't interested in royal bedtime stories. Robbing the ability of an archangel. Only that excited him.

“Then let’s finalize the time and place.”

While the three meticulously planned, Uorin spent a boring time isolated in a corner.

She stroked the cat, which meowed in response. Uorin smiled back, replying with her own playful “meow.”

The palace was brimming with a festive atmosphere.

As the first prince candidate, Shirone, displayed his mighty power, the vassals were overcome with emotion and shed tears of joy, even if it was forced.

A grand ball was held, and even after nightfall, the Grand Hall was as bright as day.

Long tables were set along the perimeter of the hall, adorned with lavish delicacies. As the court musicians performed, nobles and aristocratic ladies engaged in pleasant conversation.

Nobles, drawn by the food to their liking, came over to pay their respects to Shirone.

"We witnessed the demonstration well. It was truly a splendid display. Although the test results aren’t out yet, judging by your prowess, it’s clear you are indeed of His Majesty’s blood. Haha.”

Shirone said nothing, merely listening, prompting another quick-witted noble to chime in.

“Is there even a question? A reunion after 18 years… It’s all thanks to His Majesty’s virtue reaching the heavens. Wouldn’t you agree, Shirone?”

Shirone remained silent this time as well.

He wouldn’t be fooled anymore. The rules and manners of the palace were nothing but masks to hide their true feelings. And once the masks were removed, everyone’s face was as cold as his own.

'Are these people enemies, or allies?'

It seemed that royalty was fated to live their entire lives pondering such thoughts.

The loudest voice in the banquet hall belonged to Eliza.

Though she had never raised him, her praises were endless as if she knew everything about Shirone.
