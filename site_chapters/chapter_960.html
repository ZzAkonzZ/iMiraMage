Chapter 960: The Right Choice (3)

The Great Star's eyes lost focus.

Her eyelids fluttered as she absorbed the shock, and after a moment, she let out a long breath.

"Haaa."

The recent shockwave was likely the cause.

'The ground shook even here.'

The expressions of the Five Great Stars watching her grew serious.

'Is she really okay?'

The Great Star is a star.

But in her avatar state, she takes on the status of a living being.

'To manage planetary changes.'

While the star wouldn't split immediately, accumulated shocks of this magnitude could destabilize her.

'Earthquakes, tsunamis, volcanic eruptions. Regardless of the war, countless humans would die.'

Therefore, the Great Star used a method to separate herself from the star system.

She absorbed most of the shock impacting the planet to stabilize the system.

Freed asked,

"Why go to such lengths?"

"Humans can die. If humans disappear, so will war. Life will flourish on the planet again, and everything will return to its place."

"Nature is beautiful because... humans have told us it is."

Freed fell silent.

"When this universe first existed, all things simply stayed in place. Grass consumed the sun, insects ate the grass, animals ate the insects..."

Even in her struggle, the Great Star smiled.

"There was no reason. It was just a system. A very cold system. But one day, humans said, 'This is beautiful.' From that moment, amazingly, my system became beautiful."

The Five Great Stars listened intently.

"Humans give meaning to many things. This is comfortable, that is unpleasant. Giving a flower with a special name conveys love..."

Perhaps it started then.

"To me, a system within a system, the truth of the outside world doesn't matter. We call humans users because they give meaning to everything. The beings who define this world are the ones who use it. If all humans disappear..."

If there is no one to define her.

"Nothing is beautiful. There will only be a cold system that follows the law."

Before the maternal love of Gaia, the Mother of the World, the Five Great Stars were momentarily moved.

"But they are using it incorrectly."

Amanta spoke.

"Humanity can never be unified. If they had such a high-level spirit, they wouldn't have reached this state. The Great Star has difficult expectations."

"It's not an expectation. It's hope. Humans must reach Ultima again, and without that power, they can't stop Anke Ra or Buddha."

The Great Star added,

"Human survival is important in that sense. When humanity achieves an integrated mental system, the greater the destructive power, the more the law will change."

"With all due respect..."

Xing spoke.

"They probably won't reach it. I myself perceive others as illusions. Goffin is gone, and humanity is divided. Ultima ended with the Gaia era."

"No."

A sliver of hope.

"It is definitely continuing. The will of Gaia, through Goffin, to Shirone."

Minerva asked,

"Can Shirone lead all humans to Ultima? You know how self-centered and lacking in understanding humans are."

"A single moment is enough."

The Great Star spoke with determination.

"It will take a long time for humanity to reach Ultima. But if they can be perfectly unified for just one moment..."

They can strike the world.

"The Messiah left by Goffin, Shirone, will achieve it. Before that, as many humans as possible must survive. That's why I want to meet Rian."

If the demon commanders open their realms simultaneously, if those who have reached the state of destruction engage in full-scale war...

"The current shock is just the beginning. Soon, I won't be able to endure it either. Before that... Ugh!"

The Great Star curled up in pain.

"Great Star! Are you okay?"

The Five Great Stars rushed to check her condition, but she was more puzzled than hurt.

'Why?'

Even though she bypassed the shock signal, the residual shockwave was transmitting pain to the planet.

By human standards, it felt like her bones were vibrating.

'It's Ymir.'

She knew because she had experienced a similar shock long ago.

"It's okay now. Let's move quickly."

Despite her words, seeing the pale Great Star, Freed clicked his tongue and looked back at the forest where he had thrown Gaold.

'Tch! Because of one lunatic...'

Gaold, who lost his memory whenever he was bored, had gone berserk, delaying their progress.

But leaving him behind would mean he would destroy the planet, so they couldn't abandon him.

"Hey, get up. We're leaving."

As Freed walked into the forest to carry Gaold, his brow furrowed.

"Damn it..."

Minerva asked,

"What are you doing? Hurry up and bring him. We're almost there..."

"He's gone."

"What?"

Freed turned to the group.

"He's gone."

The warmth of Gaold's body still lingered in the grass where he had been lying.

To escape the endlessly spreading shockwave, Miro linked mass teleports.

They finally arrived at a mid-slope of the underground mountain range overlooking Bashka.

"Whew. That maniac."

Even Miro was appalled by Ymir's destructive power.

"Huh?"

Hearing a white noise-like uproar from far below, everyone's gaze turned in that direction.

"My God..."

Like an isolated island, the demons formed a sea beyond Bashka's walls.

Amy spoke.

"Now tell me. What don't I know about this operation? What's the intention behind the flower field?"

"The order came from the Holy War."

Dante's explanation was shocking, but Amy's eyes grew calm.

"...I see."

More than any explanation, the fact that it was Iruki's decision was significant.

'If he said it couldn't be done, it couldn't be done.'

He was colder than anyone but not someone who would weigh efficiency against human lives.

Lupist spoke.

"In summary, the flower field is destroyed, and the demons are gathering in Bashka. When the elemental bomb drops, this entire area will be engulfed in flames. But the elite demons are no longer here. Also, the army of heaven is pursuing us."

As they listened to the screams from Bashka, the leaders fell into thought.

'I want to go into the capital and fight. But what does that mean? When the elemental bomb explodes...'

"We're pulling out."

Miro spoke.

"We don't need to fight directly. If we're lucky, the elemental bomb might even take out the army of heaven. From now on, we'll move stealthily and get out of the blast radius."

There were no objections.

But no one could take their eyes off the tragedy unfolding in Bashka.

'We're not running away. We're fighting for more.'

As they steeled themselves to turn away, two halos spun in the sky.

"No, we can't leave."

"Sein."

As the sun and moon halos dissipated, Havitz's assassins appeared one by one.

Meirei was not visible.

'Did Havitz take her?'

Miro's thinking encompassed all directions, but she couldn't predict the future.

'But...'

In her assumptions, the current situation was entirely possible.

'Kuan lost one eye.'

This was also the same, so Miro coldly asked, bearing all her emotions.

"What do you mean we can't go?"

Sein spoke.

"I read Havitz's mind. The 9th Legion Commander Paimon plans to open the demon realm when the elemental bomb explodes. And when the demon realm opens, something terrible will happen."

"What kind of demon realm?"

"A hospital. But it's not just a disease. It will sicken both the body and the mind of humans. There's no time to explain in detail. We need to go into Bashka right now and deal with Paimon."

Dante stepped forward.

"Wait a minute. If they're going to open the demon realm, why do they need to time it with the elemental bomb's explosion? Why is that important?"

Sein spoke.

"Havitz wants to enjoy it. What if humanity's best judgment leads to the worst outcome? Humanity will fall into despair, and the demons will grow stronger."

"Satan will be thrilled."

Miro nodded at Sein's words.

"Right. We need to eliminate Paimon before the elemental bomb explodes. We have no choice but to enter Bashka."

Even if it meant getting caught in the explosion.

"I see."

Although the risk of death had suddenly increased, everyone's expressions were rather relieved.

Miro's ears twitched.

"They're coming."

The sound of giants moving echoed from the other side of the underground mountain range.

Miro looked around at everyone.

"We're going into Bashka."

No one objected, and soon the flash of mass teleportation shot towards the capital.

The demon army, without any discipline, cruelly trampled the visible humans.

"Help! Someone help! Aaaah!"

It was hardly an army, but this was their true nature.

"Give it to me! It's mine!"

"No! I caught it first! It's mine!"

Two demons argued over the ownership of a human woman's torso and legs.

"Aaah! It hurts! It hurts!"

Unable to withstand the demons' strength, the woman was torn in half, her spine dangling.

"Ugh! Ugh!"

The demons watching her in shock licked their lips and immediately turned away.

"It doesn't matter! There are plenty more! Hahaha!"

The demons didn't discriminate between the strong and the weak, nor did they care about social status.

"I found one! It's mine!"

Just as humans have preferences for livestock breeds, demons had their own special tastes.

Young women.

"Please save me! Save me!"

Children.

"Waaah! Mommy! Mommy!"

Of course, there were some who didn't care about youth or beauty.

"Hahaha! Come here! I'll pluck your eyes out first!"

If you know where demons are born, there's no need to question their preferences.

The smell of blood, entrails, and filth.

As the insides of humans burst out, the city of Bashka was filled with a stench.

Paimon raised her arms and shouted,

"Hahaha! This is perfect! This is what hell should be like! It feels as comfortable as being home!"

Her gaze turned to where giants, taller than the city walls, were peering in.

People were having fits.

"Ahhh! What is that!"

"Where is the city guard! Kill those monsters now!"

Most of the troops had been dispatched to the flower field, leaving no army to protect the citizens.

In the midst of the horrific scene, Paimon looked up at the sky, igniting the will of the demon realm.

"This is the place."

As a strategist, she knew the allied forces had hidden intentions.

"Hehe, fine. Do as you please."

She was ready to show them what unimaginable horror truly was.
