Chapter 1188:

The assembly hall, where the Hell’s Prison selection had taken place, was left in ruins from the aftermath of battle.

Through the hole Son Yoo-jung had used to escape with Mortasinger, the noise from outside came rushing in.

‘Is it the Black Chains?’

There must have been a reason why they hadn't entered the sanctuary, but it seemed that the outside wasn't safe either.

Lethe spoke.

“Yahweh, you should yield. This is the last time we’ll treat you as the highest guest.”

As the magic of reality amplified, the Agents' power grew beyond imagination.

Even the administrators, despite their authority, were bound by hidden codes that were also based on magic.

“My thoughts remain unchanged. Tell me how to purify the Six Brains and the central processing unit.”

It was a device that calculated the karma of the underworld, formed from the souls of aborted children.

“It’s impossible...”

Lethe let out a sigh before glaring fiercely.

“How many times must I tell you?”

As the flames of purification flared from her body, the board members trembled in fear.

“Then there will be no compromise.”

“… Fine.”

The doors of the assembly hall opened, and an overwhelming number of demons sealed off the exit completely.

“From today, this place will become the sacred ground of demons. Yahweh, this will be your grave.”

As time passed, the magic would only grow stronger, eventually becoming insurmountable.

‘There must be a way.’

What was Lethe hiding...?

Etella gazed at the brain, formed from fetuses, clustered between the city-sized cliffs.

‘Poor children.’

There was no sin in Limbo.

They had been erased before they could even attempt to exist—solely because they had been conceived.

And yet, the reason they remained in Hell was their relentless attachment to life.

‘They must want to live.’

But even if they did, what could they possibly do with such a life?

The only way to purify the Six Brains was for them to relinquish life of their own accord.

‘Can they do it?’

They were struggling so desperately to exist.

“Ugh…”

A sharp pain rose from her lower abdomen, and Etella grimaced, doubling over.

‘It’s afraid.’

Though still just a small seed of life, it was desperately resisting becoming part of Limbo.

‘I’m sorry.’

My child.

‘But I must resolve their resentment. That is why I came here.’

Through Raphael’s sacrifice for Shagall, Etella had learned the meaning of love.

‘I can do this.’

Now that she, too, was a mother—now that life was growing within her womb…

‘I can console the grievances of Limbo.’

If the underworld was purified, then humanity’s existence would become much more bearable.

Etella took a step forward.

“Huuuh…”

At the sight of the abyss below, where scorching flames raged, a groan escaped her lips.

Death was not something she feared.

Bound by the shackles of the Taeguk, she was fated never to die, no matter how much she wished for it.

‘How much will these children—no, the ones who came before them—despise me?’

Becoming the mother of the forsaken, embracing their pain, and vanishing along with them…

‘I can do this. No, I must do this.’

If everything in this world had a purpose, then bearing Shagall’s child was also fate.

‘Shagall…’

They were connected by the chains of Taeguk, yet he did not respond to her emotions.

Instead, what she felt through their bond was his endless, half-mad rage.

‘You, too, are pitiful.’

If he had not been manipulated by divine law, they might have been good friends.

‘But speculation is meaningless.’

Divine law carved reality into existence like a printing press.

“But still…”

She wanted to imagine.

‘Humans have the ability to imagine. To throw their hearts into uncertainty—that is what makes a heart, after all.’

Shagall could have been a good person.

‘I will carry that thought with me.’

As she took another step, an unbearable pain surged from within her womb.

“Hngh!”

The tiny life inside did not want to die.

“I’m sorry…”

Tears trickled down Etella’s cheeks.

Now that she understood love, how could she not ache for the child in her womb?

“I won’t let you live in this place.”

Extinction was all that awaited them.

The pain in her abdomen was nothing compared to the agony that shredded her heart, yet her will did not waver.

“Let’s go, my child.”

There was no other way.

He had simply been swept away by a storm of raw emotions, living his life consumed by vague hatred for something.

“Don’t do it.”

He would never see her again.

That one thought cleared the ever-present storm clouds in Shagall’s mind.

“Stop.”

He realized—he had not been in his right mind.

“Kehaha! Look at this guy’s face! He’s completely terrified. Well, I guess I can’t blame you. It is scary.”

Indeed, Shagall’s face was drained of all color, his lips trembling violently.

‘The baby. The baby inside my belly.’

Unlike its trash of a father, the child would have grown up happy under a truly good mother…

“Hngh… Huuuuuuugh!”

Tears poured from his tightly shut eyes as he sobbed uncontrollably.

Then, the administrator’s voice echoed in his ears.

- Remaining purification time: 87 aeons, 2,875 kyung, 3,241 trillion, 63 billion, 220,000 hours.

Roughly minus 10 billion hours.

The agents watching Shagall’s breakdown pointed at him and laughed.

“This is too good to keep to ourselves. Let’s cut off his head and hang it on the wall. We’ll bury it… whenever we feel like it—urk!”

A rapid-fire sword pierced the agent’s neck, and in an instant, dozens of holes riddled his body.

“…… Move.”

Shagall charged forward, his eyes rolling white, forcing the remaining agents to hurriedly take up battle stances.

“Where the hell did this guy get so much pow—?!”

Before they could finish speaking, a barrage of rapid-fire swords rained down like scattered gunfire, tearing their bodies apart.

“Move out of my way!”

As Shagall pushed forward to where Etella was, his muscles began to tear apart.

‘Please…’

He had to make it in time.

Meanwhile, Etella had steeled herself.

“… I’m sorry.”

Though Shagall’s emotions transmitted through the chain that bound them, her resolve did not waver.

Their relationship was like a tangled thread—the more they tried to unravel it, the worse it became.

‘This is the only way.’

The time they had known had been reset, and the child in her womb was not born of love.

“For the greater good.”

As tears streamed down her cheeks, she stepped past the edge of the cliff.

Shagall grabbed the chain.

“Wait!”

He pulled with all his strength, but the Taeguk’s cycle did not favor either of them.

At that moment, Shagall’s eyes widened.

“A-ah… Ahh…”

His entire body convulsed as if struck by lightning, and his knees hit the ground with a heavy thud.

“Uaaaaaaaah!”

- Remaining purification time: 87 aeons, 2,875 kyung, 3,241 trillion, 50.28 billion, 70,018 hours.

Nearly 130 billion hours had been purified.

“Why?! Why?! WHY?!”

If, in all the countless hours he had tormented her, he had shown kindness even once—

‘You idiot!’

No, even if he hadn’t lost control before their separation and had just stayed by her side—

“No! This is a lie!”

He could have saved Etella.

“Uaaaaaaaah!”

They could have been happy.

“The chain, the chain…”

Desperately, he fumbled for the only hope he had left. But Etella’s emotions no longer flowed through it.

“Why… Why?!”

Inside the main system that governed karma, every program had shut down.

Dragging his broken body upright, Shagall ran down the corridor, his face twisted in terror.

‘I’m not too late! I can’t be too late!’

- Remaining purification time…

An emergency message rang through the assembly hall.

- Warning! The core data of the engine room is being disrupted! Resolve the issue immediately!

Lethe’s head snapped up.

“What?”

If there was an issue with the central processing unit, all of the underworld’s karma would be reset.

“That’s impossible. Anyone bound by karma can’t access the Six Brains. Even if you destroy the physical structure, the law remains intact—so there’s no way the core data could be disrupted.”

- Romi Etella. Limbo.

Lethe clicked her tongue in irritation.

“You keep spouting nonsense! That woman’s karma is—”

And then, realization struck her.

“… Ah.”

Limbo.

A life that knew nothing, sleeping within the womb—how could it have any karmic burden?

‘Etella, professor…’

Hearing the message, Shirone finally found the answer to the riddle that had eluded him until now.

‘Shagall’s child…’

The implications were clear.

‘If the computation system disappears, karma can no longer be calculated. We can retrieve Jin Seong Eum.’

The only problem left was how to break through an army that had nearly all of the underworld’s forces gathered.

“Give up, Yahweh. Even now, we are growing stronger. You will never escape from here.”

“You knew all along, didn’t you? How to erase the Six Brains. How to destroy the aborted fetuses.”

“… I never lied. But if I had known Etella was pregnant, my response would have been different.”

She would have killed her without hesitation.

“Die.”

As Lethe ignited the flames of purification, a sharp wind suddenly whistled down from the 13th floor.

“… Huh?”

A thin line sliced through the landscape. Along that line, the bodies of the demons began to burn.

“Ge-Gehenna…?! AAAAAAH!”

The demons, now consumed by fire, were absorbed into the great sword. And beyond the flames, standing at the doorway, was Rian.

“… Sorry, Shirone.”

Boom. Boom. Boom…

The severed outer wall realigned itself with a grinding sound of stone against stone.

“They’ve all run away, Hell Prison.”

“Haha.”

Because of Havitz and Wizard’s clash, an unexpected variable had emerged in the underworld.

‘This is insane…’

Lethe grimaced in frustration.

‘That thing was still left…’

No matter how powerful the demons had become, the Gehenna Blade would devour all of their magic.
