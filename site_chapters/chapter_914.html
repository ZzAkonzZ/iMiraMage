Chapter 914: The Brain of God (3)

In the year 201 of Omega.

Hundreds of angels and thousands of Maras had taken over the sky.

Every time the judicial halo flashed, the law shook, and the violence of the Maras was inflicted on the ground.

"Ooooooooh!"

At the same time, the eyes of the Gaia people lit up with a golden glow.

‘Ultima System.’

As the quantum signals of 300 million people unified into one, the veil of the world was lifted.

"What is this...?!"

A massive stream of light, like the Milky Way, swirled and rushed towards the angelic camp.

"Attack!"

The angels, flying with their wings of light, threw themselves into the signal with all their might.

Boom!

The shockwave, larger than a planet, spread through the universe, shaking the white hall of the archangels' meeting room.

"Damn humans!" Metiel, the archangel of union, bared her white teeth.

"How long are we going to wait? Why don't we just go out and crush them?"

Despite her pure face with long blonde hair, her eyes were filled with murderous intent.

Kariel, the archangel of creation, agreed.

"I think the same. If we stay like this, we'll be overthrown before we even attempt a reset."

While Goffin showed independent actions, the Gaia people also strengthened the Ultima system.

To prevent another cosmic destruction, Anke Ra's conclusion was to reset.

Uriel spoke.

"Let's wait a little longer. If the reset succeeds, the future where the Gaia people existed will disappear."

They planned to redesign from the beginning of the universe to prevent such a situation.

Boom!

As the white hall shook again, Satiel, the archangel of disintegration, raised her eyebrows.

"Still, it's really amazing. It's hard to believe this power comes from humans."

Union and disintegration were sister concepts.

Thus, Satiel's appearance was as heavenly beautiful as Metiel's.

The distinguishing features were her short hair and her large, innocent eyes.

Metatron, the archangel of gravity, spoke.

"Be careful with your thoughts, Satiel. Does an archangel praise inferior creatures?"

Satiel pouted her lips.

"We should acknowledge what needs to be acknowledged. You know, when you disassemble any being, they all become the same?"

Metiel showed her displeasure.

"So, are we the same? No. Your mind is too childish. You can't rule humans like that."

"Okay, okay."

Satiel quickly bowed her head.

Born with the concept of disintegration, she had a tendency to treat all beings equally.

This made it difficult for her to get along with Metiel, the archangel of union, who symbolized individuality.

As the atmosphere calmed and the archangels remained silent, Ikael spoke.

"The angelic defense line has collapsed."

Her eyes, having fought many battles with the Gaia people, overflowed with archangel authority.

"Is it that bad?"

From Uriel's body, white steam like milk flowed and permeated the white hall.

"I will go out. Today, I will destroy Ultima."

"No. Everyone will participate."

The moment even a slight crack appeared in Ultima, Anke Ra would reset the world.

‘Not being able to get close means the Gaia people's spirit surpasses the angelic army.’

The only answer was for everyone to sortie.

"Is it necessary for even me to go?"

Faiel, the archangel of annihilation, couldn't accept that the situation had deteriorated this much.

‘Faiel.’

As Shirone passed through Omega's records, he took an interest in the archangel he had personally annihilated.

‘He was really strong.’

It took over 127 years of Valhalla action to defeat him.

Even considering the debt of time, it was terrifying to think it had been less than five years since then.

"Everyone will sortie."

Ikael warned Faiel.

"Don't underestimate the enemy, Faiel. That's why we're paying the price now."

"I acknowledge the enemy."

Ultima is strong.

"What I want to say is, do we really have to move according to your will?"

Raiel, the archangel of light, asked.

"What do you mean? Are you doubting the archangel who directly carries out Anke Ra's orders?"

"McClain Goffin."

Ikael's brow furrowed.

"When the Gaia people first tried to leave the Photon System, the archangel couldn't stop them."

It was an event from a distant past, but angels never forgot.

"So what? Are you saying you could have stopped the Gaia people if it were you?"

Only Faiel, with the concept of great honor, could confidently argue with Ikael, the leader of the angels.

"Of course, Ultima is strong. What I'm suspicious of is why Goffin saved you."

The incident where Goffin flew in to block Ikael as soon as he fired the Photon Cannon was talked about among the angels.

"Saved? Even if he hadn't blocked it, I wouldn't have been harmed by such an attack. And Anke Ra has already given sufficient reprimand for failing to stop the Gaia people. It's not your place to intervene."

After Anke Ra's stern rebuke, Ikael's spirit became even stronger.

Anyway, Ikael was the archangel, so Faielhad no more grounds to argue.

"My command is Anke Ra's command. All archangels, listen. Deploy immediately and stop the Gaia people."

"We obey."

Eight archangels simultaneously merged into the white hall's barrier and disappeared.

"Strike! Push harder!"

Lightning split the sky into thousands of pieces and struck vertically into the Gaia people's ranks.

"Weak."

Looking up at the electricity dispersing in all directions from 2 kilometers above the ground, Maishin raised the corners of his mouth.

As the eldest and representative of the Gaia people, he was fighting heaven with leadership comparable to Goffin's.

"This is more like it," he said, pointing forward with two fingers, and the Gaia people's minds unified into one.

"Lightning."

Boom!

As the world opened and lightning struck, the screams of angels were heard.

"Aaaaaaah!"

Even the conceptual bodies of angels had their holy bodies destroyed by the energy of destruction.

"Heh, reset, huh."

Among the crowd walking towards Anke Ra, a man sat on a rock, smoking a cigarette.

With a huge build and a wolf-like impression, he was Leo, the battle commander of the Gaia race.

"Anke Ra. You're dreaming a foolish dream." As he muttered, blowing out white smoke, a woman with iron-colored hair down to her ankles passed by.

"Hey! Everyone's moving, and you're just sitting there? Now that you're the commander, you're slacking off."

"Hey, would it make a difference if I slacked off?"

"If everyone thought that way, who would walk? Get up already."

Although they were exchanging banter, their conclusion was the same.

"That was good."

Leo threw his cigarette and put his hands in his pockets, walking next to the woman.

"Penny, if you're free tonight—" At that moment, a high-pitched scream interrupted him.

"Insignificant humans!"

The Gaia people's gaze turned to a fierce-looking angel glaring at them.

"How dare you defy the command of God! A great disaster will befall you!"

Leo's expression turned cold.

"Who is that angel?" Penny asked.

"Ikasa. The angel of desire."

With the judicial halo Valhalla action, she could reverse causality and was prepared for annihilation.

"Let's go, angels! We can't let these insignificant creatures take our world!"

Seeing the angels' eyes change, Ikasa rushed towards the ground.

"Great Anke Ra!"

As black and white information accumulated in her halo, a massive result surged.

‘I can't let them take it!’

The winner becomes God.

Conversely, the loser would taste a level of existence far worse than slavery.

"Die, humans!"

Shirone thought.

‘Impossible. Even if you reverse causality, you can't bring about a result that seizes life.’

Knowing this because he used the same judicial halo, he understood it perfectly after realizing the void.

‘It's because the codes are different.’

This world is made up of the photon code, which is the physical background, and the quantum code, which is the mind that resides in it.

‘Time belongs to the photon code. On the other hand, the subject that triggers actions, the mind, belongs to the quantum code.’

Therefore, the photon code cannot access the quantum code.

‘Kill! Even just one!’

But Ikasa's emotions were genuine.

The future she drew in for that result was all the punches she could throw until her body broke.

"This world is ours!"

Leo's eyebrows twitched.

"You're greatly mistaken."

As he raised his shoulders and clenched his fists, the eyes of 300 million Gaia people lit up.

At the same time, Ikasa activated Valhalla action.

"Dieeeee!"

As causality reversed, countless shockwaves of punches struck the ground.

"You say it's your world?"

Lowering his center of gravity, Leo took a stance to punch the sky with his right fist.

"No, we use it."

The world's signals can't affect the mind.

"Divine Transcendence."

The extreme manifestation of the mind imbues the world's signals, causing powerful phenomena.

"Instant Strike!"

In front of the phantom of the fist pushing through the landscape, the shockwave of Valhalla action shattered.

"What...?"

As space overlapped, time became meaningless.

The will of divine transcendence pierced Ikasa's torso, who had a blank expression.

"Aaaaaah!"

As Ikasa fell with only her upper body remaining, the wings of the angels following her stopped simultaneously.

"We can't win." The trembling holy bodies of the terrified angels emitted a sorrowful vibration.

"Do you understand now?"

The voices of 300 million people simultaneously shattered the angels' vibrations.

"We are one."

As 300 million sound waves resonated, the sound stretched as if time had slowed down.

"Th-there..."

Where the angels pointed, a procession of over 100 million Gaia people was joining the main force.

"With integrated thinking."

The angels bit their lips as they watched the procession cross without even brushing against each other's clothes.

"Beings with different individualities."

The Gaia people suddenly stopped and struck 400 million different poses, each unique.

"We are the universe."

A procession of at least 100 million Gaia people was approaching from all directions.

"We are the truth."

Everyone's eyes were lit with fire, and vertical beams of light from their bodies connected to the sky.

"That is humanity..."

The final evolutionary form of the society created by the users.

"Sob. Sob."

With only her upper body remaining, Ikasa looked at the procession of Gaia people passing by and shed tears.

Friends chatting as they walked, children holding their parents' hands and laughing brightly.

Their actions were different, but the golden glow in their eyes contained the same emotions.

"No."

We are the masters.

"No! Absolutely not! I am a god! I am a noble angel!"

Some of the Gaia people who had been passing by stopped and looked back at Ikasa.

"Ugh."

Facing the gaze of the Gaia people, Ikasa was terrified and stopped speaking.

"Ah..."

And she realized.

‘Good and evil, merit and love, none of it matters.’

If the Gaia people call evil good, it is good, and if they call merit love, it is love.

‘Because they are everything.’

If all beings see a black stone and call it white, is the stone black or white?

‘Akashic Records or Ultima System?’

‘Humans define it.’

Therefore, if the child now looking into her eyes decides to kill her mercilessly...

‘That is also the truth.’

She waited for her fate with terrified eyes, but the child just smiled and passed by.

"Sob!"

Ikasa buried her face in the ground and wept.

"Aaaaaah! Aaaaaah!" The moment she was defined as an insignificant being, cracks began to form in her holy body.
