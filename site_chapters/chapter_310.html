Chapter 310: The Wheel of Causality (2)

'What's going on? Archangel Ikael and Goffin. Is there a connection between the two? Or is it merely a coincidence?'

It was definitely related to the Reset. Once outside the fortress, I would need to research it.

If the Reset were true, there was a high likelihood that traces of it would remain in the depths of another person's psyche. I would kidnap at random and dissect them.

I slowed down as the city gate came into view. Guards holding torches were patrolling the walls.

Arius carefully attempted a Flicker. Disappearing with a fizz, he reappeared thirty centimeters ahead. The time distortion was gone.

'Have I shaken them off?'

Armin had spent considerable time in the food storage, making it difficult to pinpoint his exact location. Expanding the Spirit Zone to warp the entire radius was the only way.

Moreover, considering his timeline was short, it was quite possible that he had already escaped.

"But somehow, it feels unsettling."

Arius keenly observed the movements of the guards.

Realizing there was a two-minute gap in the guard coverage, he waited for the right moment, then sprinted forward, planning to break through the wall with a Flicker and escape.

'Take that, annoying Kazra.'

"Where are you rushing off to?"

Arius' run came to an abrupt halt.

The voice came from behind him. The presence was so silent that it indicated remarkable skill.

'Damn it!'

Arius turned around slowly, knowing that any hasty action could cost him his neck.

Kazra's chief bodyguard, Litni Walker, was lowering his long sword, grinning like a beast.

'The worst-case scenario.'

Of all people, it had to be him.

Kazra might be weak in magic, but not in swordsmanship. Moreover, Walker was a swordsman who adhered most closely to the textbook of great magicians.

The best strategy for a swordsman against a magician is 'cut them down before they can cast a spell.' And Walker was the best at that in Kazra. It was something he had read about in the basic court swordsmanship guide that Walker had written.

Walker leaned his sword on the ground and looked up at the night sky.

Even as Arius turned his gaze away, he was unable to move, as if Walker's nerve had lashed out and bound him like a noose.

"It's not the time for people to be wandering around. Even the bars are closed tonight."

"Ha! I had a sudden urgent appointment. And what about you, Captain? Are you on patrol?"

"Hehe, what's this, playing the player?"

Walker's eyes gleamed like those of a nocturnal beast.

"You, a grave robber? Arius of the Seven Great Magicians."

"I don't know what you mean. How many Ariuses do you think there are?"

Walker was neither from the faction of Teraje nor from Orkamp. He worked solely for the prosperity of the kingdom.

He had been tracking Arius for a long time.

Being a member of the Seven Great Magicians was irrelevant. For a kingdom like Kazra, weak in magic, it was an inevitable choice. But if he disrupted the kingdom, he couldn't just be ignored.

"Know what's scarier than a tyrant king? A betrayer. I'll give you the choice: surrender peacefully or be split in two by my sword."

"Just when I thought things were going well."

Arius slowly lowered his hand.

If he acted without trying to escape, Walker wouldn't move either. It was wisdom gained from experience.

A mage equipped with teleportation can't escape a swordsman due to the humanly time it takes to activate it.

Once the magic is activated, the swordsman can't catch the mage. But as long as it happens within human realms, a master of schema senses surpasses a mage.

The exact moment when the activation occurs remains a mystery to mages. According to swordsmen, they feel a change in temperament.

Arius kept a close eye on Walker's every move. From Walker's perspective, it might seem like no more than an ordinary person watching, but still, as a Habari mage, Arius had gathered some information. Walker's momentum felt like a volcano on the verge of erupting.

Arius sighed with a look of despair.

"Ugh. Annoying."

Bang! A long sword struck the ground where Arius had been standing, as if it had followed him a moment too late, in a vertical slicing stance.

"Damn it all..."

Arius was gone. He had been a half-beat too slow.

Or perhaps not. The timing had been exact.

The speed of perception, sensation, nerves, action—everything had transcended his opponent. Arius surely hadn't even seen his own movement. Perhaps there was as much difference as the time a human and a mosquito perceive.

'But how did he escape?'

It wasn't a teleportation-type magic. Nor was it Flicker. He simply vanished.

Invisibility magic? Even if so, the increased casting speed didn't make sense.

But it was a possibility that couldn't be ignored, so Walker looked around.

"Captain! What's going on?"

The guards had heard the sound and rushed over.

"From now on, we search for Arius. Strengthen the inner city's defenses and assemble all available troops."

"Arius? Isn't he His Majesty's technical advisor?"

"It's incitement of rebellion. The more time we waste, the harder it gets."

The guards scattered in an orderly fashion.

Walker watched his subordinates' movements and then entered the forest. If invisibility magic had been cast, there would still be traces somewhere.

After everyone had left, a Flicker magic was cast, and a man appeared like a ghost. It was Armin.

"Apoptosis. A rational decision."

Apoptosis is known as one of the most difficult scaling spells in the realm of scale magic. It makes sense because it's a spell that involves diving into one's own psyche.

If the body enters the psyche, the boundary between thought and reality collapses, turning into a concept entity. There are no emotions and even thinking becomes impossible. The chances of Arius returning on his own were very slim.

'It's better than dying.'

Certainly, it was better than dying.

Every magician has a critical technique.

Indeed, Walker's response was flawless. However, Apoptosis has almost no casting time compared to other spells because it's a feedback from the Spirit Zone.

Armin had ended the time distortion in case Arius was caught by Walker. It wouldn't matter if he died, but being captured and potentially spilling secrets would complicate things.

'No need to worry for a while.'

It's unlikely he attempted Apoptosis without a way to return. He must have made arrangements or formed a pact with someone for such a situation.

A year, or perhaps ten, or even a hundred years might pass without him returning. But he was certain to return someday.

'The collaborator is likely. That's where I should start my investigation.'

The most likely candidate was the mages of the Seven Magical Sins.

Remembering the members, Armin frowned. Arius was among those whose sanity had completely turned.

'Maybe it's time to get going.'

Armin looked back at the fortress.

He wanted to stay by Shirone’s side a little longer, but there was no time to delay. He had to return to Keira as soon as possible.

As he cast the Flicker magic, his form disappeared with a fizz. Walker's shouts faintly echoed across the empty space.

Zenoger was running through the underground emergency exit.

Every ten steps, a trap was triggered. A trapdoor opened, arrows flew, and a log studded with nails swung like a pendulum.

Zenoger maintained his speed, dodging the traps. He jumped over trapdoors and caught the studded logs in webs he spit from his mouth, stopping them midway.

"Ha! Ha!"

Leaning against the corner of the underground maze, he took a short break. Sweat dripped from his forehead as he looked up at the ceiling.

He couldn't save Zion. Therefore, he had fled. Teraje wouldn't spare a failed assassin.

If it were only his own death, he could accept it, but it would also mean the end for the Spatur family, who depended entirely on Teraje's support.

'I must escape. And find a faction to rely on.'

His clan had been assassins for a thousand years. Even if they didn't meet Teraje's standards, there were plenty of forces that needed them.

Zenoger restored only the energy at his core and resumed running through the maze, aiming to escape the fortress before dawn.

The floor suddenly swung open like a saloon door. Using his spider senses, he leaped just before falling, sticking a web to the ceiling and swinging in a parabola. At the same time, a dagger flew towards him.

"Argh!"

While swinging like a pendulum, he twisted his waist to dodge the dagger.

Though intended to be thrown by a person, the dagger flew as swiftly as an arrow and embedded itself in the wall.

Zenoger landed and immediately took a defensive stance. His chest swelled, sprouting four arms, and six eyes opened on either side of his temples.

"Who's there? Come out."

A petite woman turned around from the shadowy corner.

Her black hair was cut in a neat bob, and she squinted her closed eyes slightly, wearing a straight, narrow skirted dress that seemed more like wrapping than clothing.

Her hands, demurely clasped together, held the sheath and handle of the dagger, the blade partially exposed.

"Are you a hitman? Who hired you?"

"I have nothing to tell someone who's about to leave this world."

Her voice was refreshingly clear, even if only to the ear.

But Zenoger wasn't fooled. Her voice was highly trained.

"Hehe, well... that's just the way we live."

As Zenoger finished speaking, the dagger flew towards him.

He leaped to the ceiling and spread webs in all directions, transforming the environment to his advantage. Then, he whipped a whip-thick strand of web.

It seemed the woman's form multiplied briefly, then she moved swiftly behind him as if riding a rail.

'An unusual walking method.'

The narrow straight skirt seemed utterly unsuitable for combat. Even with her legs spread as wide as possible, she could only stride half as far as a normal person. The strange sandals between her toes were also annoying.

However, her walking speed was unimaginably fast. Plus, the acceleration phase was incredibly dense, creating an eerie movement as if chasing afterimages.

At first, it appeared slow, but with explosive additions over short distances, she suddenly appeared right in front of him. It was really troublesome. It wasn't ordinary acceleration, so timing was impossible to measure accurately.

'The rhythm is completely different.'

This must be how she made her living.

If categorized, she belonged to the circus. A hitman who uses athletic ability to deceive the opponent.

A fleeting curiosity about who she might be passed through his mind, but it was just a momentary thought. For an active assassin, being known by name meant they were essentially done for.

Zenoger cast a web curtain to block her approach. As he got used to the rhythm, her movements began to become predictable.

From then, the tide turned.

Zenoger moved like a spider between the walls, approaching the woman.

Despite the irregular rhythm, the last dagger did not unsettle him. He caught it in his mouth, twisting his neck.

"Hehehe! Not bad for a circus performer."

Diving into the woman's arms, Zenoger lifted his upper arms in reverse joints. Climbing over her shoulder with one hand, he grabbed her face and pushed her against the wall, smashing her head.

A thud followed by a crack as her face split like porcelain, with shards falling to the floor.

Zenoger quickly moved his left three hands. One hand strangled her neck, and another hand reached under her skirt. There were no genitals.

The middle hand grabbed her breast and tore it apart. The silk material ripped away, revealing a cushion-like mass resembling fat.

The bare skin of her breast was flat, and below it, her emaciated ribs were visible.
