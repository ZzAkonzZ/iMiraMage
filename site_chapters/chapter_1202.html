Chapter 1202:

The Underworld.

Son Yoo-jung's Yeoui, having attained the enlightenment of Shatter Evil and Reveal Righteousness, turned Lethe’s body into a mangled mess.

“Ugh!”

Lethe had just been about to transfer her weight into the ground.

‘Why is she so fast?’

The fact that she couldn’t react in time shocked Lethe just as much as Son Yoo-jung was surprised at her own breakthrough.

‘Why?’

Because it was right.

If one sat alone in a quiet room, closed their eyes, and reflected, they would always find the same answer—what is truly right.

‘This is wisdom.’

If the methods of evil are easy yet complicated, then the ways of good are difficult but simple.

And once that simplicity is fully accepted into one’s way of life, goodness can no longer be shaken.

This was something…

That evil could never possess. A sacred realm that existed only for the righteous.

As divine flames rose from Son Yoo-jung’s body, Lethe regenerated her damaged eye and lifted her head.

The golden form of the Great Sage, Equal to Heaven stretched across the sky of Hell.

‘Annoying.’

Lethe bent her knees, searching for an escape route.

‘If I stay here, I’ll lose track of Yahweh. I have to stop him from reaching Jin Seong Eum.’

Even if it meant abandoning the System Control Branch.

‘Now!’

The moment Lethe launched herself like a bullet, Mortasinger activated Rule Eater, pulling Lethe’s space back to its original position.

As Lethe twisted her body, Son Yoo-jung came hurtling toward her, shrouded in steam.

Lethe muttered under her breath.

“…You’ll regret this.”

“I won’t.”

Not anymore.

Yeoui split Lethe in two, slamming into the ground with enough force to send a shockwave tearing through the branch’s warehouses.

“Boss!”

Just as an agent tried to rush forward, flames erupted from the earth, taking on a colossal shape.

“What is that?”

A demon? Or a god?

A monstrous entity, the size of a building, let molten flames drip from its body as it let out a thunderous roar.

“You pathetic creations dare to interfere with Hell’s overseer!”

Anything caught in its heat was instantly purified, forcing the agents to retreat.

‘Is this the true form of Hell’s Mother, Lethe?’

Lethe clenched her teeth as she lifted her head.

‘Yahweh, binding me here won’t help you achieve your goal.’

At the farthest visible edge of the sky, a pitch-black cloud surged forward at an overwhelming speed.

It was likely every single Black Chain in Hell, all rushing toward Shirone and Rian.

“Yoo-jung!”

Mortasinger, looking terrified, ran toward her, but Son Yoo-jung remained calm as she gazed up at Lethe.

“It’s fine.”

The divine incarnation of the Great Sage, Equal to Heaven exhaled golden smoke, raising her staff toward Lethe.

“Because now, I can finally say it.”

No matter what happened before her eyes…

“The one who is right—”

A streak of light, carrying her Idea, pierced straight through the monster’s core.

Ymir, Deep Layer 1.

Miro fixed her gaze on the black sphere before her, a void darker than anything she had ever seen.

A black hole.

The light flowing along its edges could not send a signal anywhere.

If the sun symbolized law, then the ultimate extreme of the mind—Ultima—would be a black sun.

Arius spoke.

"If light cannot move, it means there are no signals. In other words, the law has been completely imprisoned."

Ymir looked up at the sky.

"That’s right. Every form of matter asserts its existence within the backdrop of this universe."

This was the primal subconscious. Ymir’s words were the fundamental information he had carried since birth.

"How is existence expressed?"

Ymir stood up.

"Weight?"

That was merely a human perspective.

"Hardness?"

Not a perfect definition.

"Existence."

Ymir turned toward them.

"How much can one assert their existence within time and space? If we were to rank it, the most prominent would be stars. But…"

Ymir flexed his body, and the scenery around them seemed to warp.

"There are things even stronger."

The assertion of existence.

No law could prevent Ymir from doing whatever he wanted.

As he took a step forward, the light of the sky was sucked into the black hole, turning the world into shades of gray.

"My existence reaches beyond the veil of the universe."

The outer world.

"Everything makes sense now," Arius said.

"The black hole is a phenomenon that represents Ymir’s unique mind, the fusion of ten billion Gaians' consciousness. That entire structure is Ultima, but to be precise, what we are looking for is at the end of the black hole."

The singularity—an infinite density with no volume.

"In the nightmare Monga created, Ymir was defined by ten billion eyes. Why was that so terrifying? Because if the gravitational force collapsed even slightly, Ultima would shatter. In other words, Ymir’s very existence would be denied."

Miro spoke.

"To put it simply, if even a single eye turns away from him, we win?"

"Yes. It may seem like ten billion Gaians are defining Ymir, but at the same time, Ymir is controlling all ten billion. It’s a mutual reinforcement of law, a dual principle. Ymir is both the sum of ten billion and a single Ultima."

Arius turned toward the black sun.

"What we seek is inside."

"Can you do it?"

If Arius could understand the mind of a target, he could plunder it—but Ymir’s mind was an exception.

"I don’t mind dying if I go in, but even then, I doubt I can steal it."

"Do it," Miro said.

"If you don’t do it, then it truly is impossible."

Something in her voice made Arius lower his head.

"Understood."

What was there to fear?

Miro’s mind was a perfect work of art.

Even if Gaold had taken her flesh, her blood, and even her heart, Arius had no regrets about losing his eyes to witness her mind.

"Alright. Time to go in again."

Like a diver who feels an ache in their joints on cloudy days, Arius gazed up at the black hole.

"Arius."

Miro called his name.

"Come back alive."

He knew she wasn’t the type to say things like that unless she truly meant them as a farewell.

Arius smiled.

As he soared toward the sky, Ymir did not move.

"I’ll let you go."

For a being who had assimilated ten billion Gaians, a mere fly was nothing to concern himself with.

There would be no pursuit.

Arius was relieved about that, but he was terrified of what awaited him beyond.

Inside that black void, which had pierced even Ymir’s primal subconscious…

What would be waiting for him?

Clenching his teeth, Arius opened a door into the immeasurable distance at the heart of the black hole.

"Aaaaaah!"

As he plunged into the deepest depths of the universe, he felt his consciousness unravel.

"It’s fine. This isn’t my physical body."

This was still a dream.

But he could say with certainty that if he fell for just a few more seconds, his identity itself would be destroyed.

"Endure. Just hold on."

If he could glimpse Ultima’s true form, his chances of plundering it would increase exponentially.

"Implant."

All he had to do was embed a distinct shock into Ymir’s primal subconscious, which longed for sensation.

In a state where his body had been broken down to its smallest components, Arius experienced something beyond reason.

"Ah…"

His sense of self blurred as he finally reached the singularity.

There, waiting for him—

Light.

Paradoxically, because he had no eyes, he could see it more clearly than ever.

A massive, overwhelming radiance.

"Aaaaaah!"

His soul screamed in ecstasy.

Meanwhile, the group that had sent Arius off was now facing Ymir.

"I see," Ymir said as he stepped forward.

"You are the first to reach Deep Layer 1. No… not all of you."

His gaze shifted toward Gaold, who lay unconscious on Miro’s back.

"Heh. What’s this? Did he pass out from just that?"

The group finally acknowledged the reality before them.

Gaold had broken through the realm of numbness and opened Deep Layer 1, but from Ymir’s perspective, the battle hadn’t even begun.

"Tch."

Miro gently placed Gaold on the ground and walked toward Ymir.

"You talk big, but not feeling pain isn’t something to brag about. You were just born strong. But Gaold is different. He made it here with a human body."

Ymir pondered this for a moment before asking,

"What’s the difference? In the end, strength is strength."

"That’s what I’m going to teach you."

As the avatar of Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara manifested, Luber and Monga carried Shirone out of range.

"We will protect Five Star."

"Understood."

Ultima was what Shirone needed, so their priority was clear.

As Kangnan and Sein moved behind Miro, Ymir raised an eyebrow.

"Just you lot?"

Extreme Good, the Southern Wolf, and the Black Line’s greatest mental mage.

But the only one who had ever made Ymir feel anything at all was Gaold.

"Don’t bother."

As Ymir’s fist shot into the air, the atmosphere of desire ignited, bursting into light as powerful as the sun itself.

In an instant, a new sun was born at the flick of Ymir’s hand.

"I’ll wait until he wakes up. Fighting you people would just ruin my appetite."

Sein had to admit it.

"This is Ymir’s mental world. Here, his identity is no different from that of a god."

Gaold breaking into Ymir’s subconscious had been the anomaly, not the other way around.

Ymir sat down, resting his chin on his hand.

"What are you waiting for? Sit down. You have nothing else to do, and you can’t even attack me. Are we just going to have a staring contest?"

It was a reasonable offer, but it didn’t sit well with them.

"Damn it! He’s mocking us."

Miro turned around.

"How’s Gaold?"

Kangnan checked his condition and shook her head.

"He’s not waking up anytime soon."

"Is that so?"

Nodding, Miro turned back toward Ymir.

"That’s a relief."

"Hmm?"

Ymir’s eyebrow twitched just as Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara spread its arms.

Time compressed to infinity, and sensing the danger, Ymir shot up from his seat.

Given Kangnan’s unmoving eyelids, Ymir’s speed was unimaginable, but—

"You bastard."

Miro was faster.

As the strikes of Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara slammed into Ymir, the entire world shook in unison.

"Take this."

With the final strike landing on his right side, Ymir tumbled across the ground.

Kangnan and Sein gawked.

"The attack landed."

As they looked up, they saw the avatar of Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara scowling like a demon.

"Anger…?"

Was such a thing even possible for one of Extreme Good?

As that thought crossed their minds, Miro walked toward Ymir.

"Listen carefully. If you ever make Gaold shed tears of blood again…"

Ymir, recovering from the impact, narrowed his glowing eyes.

"I don’t care if you’re Extreme Good or not. I will rip you apart."

Sein finally understood.

"Miro is not Yahweh. Even the death of others does not shake her perfect mind."

But Gaold was different.

If he had transcended death itself, then Miro could only respond in kind.

And the intensity of that rage—

"Come on. You need more of a beating."

It was enough to blow the lid off her mind completely.
