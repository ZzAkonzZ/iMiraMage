Chapter 476:

"It went in!"

Gaold was certain.

The moment the thrilling explosion of the Vacuum Press occurred at his fingertips, he clearly felt that Uriel had been shaken.

It was a certainty delivered by ten million times the pain.

There wasn't a speck of dust left nearby, but the wrinkles in the atmosphere had completely obscured his vision.

As the air spread outwards, a backdraft incomparable to a typhoon swept through the battlefield, which had momentarily fallen into a vacuum state.

The air swirled and gathered, turning into turbulence and creating hundreds of vortices.

In the movement of that wind, Uriel, who was crouching and defending his body with the Nirvana Shield, was visible.

"Is this really human technology?"

Uriel knew that superiority in martial arts alone did not guarantee victory, having fought countless battles.

Even if it was just a hypothesis, it wouldn't be shameful for a human with technology that even angels couldn't predict to be temporarily pushed back.

While Miro was a representative figure, Gaold had reached this point solely through martial arts.

"Ugh!"

Gaold, with his white hair flying, charged repeatedly.

The Vacuum Press was a powerful defensive technique, but depending on its use, it could also become a bomb that exploded with ultra-high-density air pressure.

"Interesting."

The Nirvana Shield clashed with the Vacuum Press with a loud noise.

As the frictional heat burned the air, a massive fire cloud rose as if a bomb had exploded.

The intense heat reached those on the fortress walls, and Sein, who was breaking Erg's magic circle, became even more anxious.

"Damn it. He might be dead."

The air literally turned into fire.

It was a power that even the great Gaold might not survive.

Sein activated the Sun and Moon Wheel and shouted.

"Cover me!"

As two gears penetrated Erg, Sein's consciousness was completely cut off from external information.

To protect the defenseless Sein, Zulu and Armin arrived.

A slow magic field unfolded, and Zulu's summoned cougar swung its sharp claws at the approaching enemies.

But as time passed, the number of fallen angels only increased.

"We can't hold out much longer here either."

There was no way to confirm Gaold's life or death, and the battle on the fortress walls was gradually being pushed back.

"Is this the power of an angel?"

The gap between angels and fallen angels was like heaven and earth, but their abilities, derived from their concepts, had a distinctiveness that set them apart from any mage's magic.

"We were never meant to win from the start."

Zulu said, commanding the cougar.

"We just need to focus on buying time. There's still one more chance left."

"Do you think Gaold is alive?"

"No."

Zulu looked at Kangnan, who was fighting the fire giant mage Urotas at the forefront of the battlefield.

"It's not about living or dying. It's about whether you can give up or not."

Boom! Boom! Boom!

Every time Urotas's fist struck the ground, Kangnan felt her heart sink.

The last remaining fire giant.

But he was a deputy commander and a unique mage among giants.

"The Seal of Fire!"

When Urotas, who had stopped his attack, clasped his hands and cast a spell, a burning seal of fire was engraved on Kangnan's forearm.

"Damn!"

The fire immediately materialized and began to burn, draining the strength from Kangnan's arm.

Urotas's magic was seal magic.

It was the ability to engrave a seal on the target with the gaze of fire and burn the control right.

Once the seal was engraved, it was as if the nerves were cut off, making it impossible to move.

The conditions for determining the application range seemed quite tricky, but considering that the opponent was a mage with the strength of a giant, it was undoubtedly an annoying technique.

"I will avenge my clan!"

Realizing that this was not an opponent she could fight with stubbornness alone, Kangnan twisted her body flexibly and dug into the opponent's chest.

When the straight and domineering attack unique to the Rammoai was launched, Urotas hurriedly widened the distance and cast the seal magic again.

As fire burned on her right thigh and she lost control, Kangnan stomped the ground with her opposite foot as if striking it.

She darted forward like an arrow and struck Urotas's side with a whip-like kick, pushing the giant's body back.

"Ugh!"

Urotas's eyes burned with fire as a seal was engraved on Kangnan's solar plexus.

"Ugh!"

As the fire burned and the control right was momentarily burned away, her heart stopped for a moment.

When Urotas's fist flew in, Kangnan covered her face with both arms.

It was a reflexive movement she had reached after countless repetitions every day.

As the impact pierced through her guard, Kangnan's consciousness blurred.

"Ugh!"

When she came to, what she saw was the towering fortress wall.

Tears flowed from her eyes.

"I am…"

She couldn't become like that person.

Even though she had risked everything, she couldn't even stay at the edge of where he had reached.

"Hmph!"

Kangnan clenched her fists and stood up.

Emotions didn't matter.

She had come here, abandoning everything, to achieve one purpose.

"I will definitely… definitely bring him back."

By now, the battle on the fortress wall had reached its climax.

When Shina's ice magic bound the Maras, Kuan flew in and cut down their frozen bodies.

Etella was blasting the giant Mara's body with Yin-Yang Wave Fist, and Zulu and Armin were using all their skills to block the bizarre abilities of the fallen angels.

And Sein… finally reached the central processing unit of the first-class Mara Erg.

As he passed through the tangled circuits like a net, he deactivated all connections with the Sun and Moon Wheel.

As the light of the magic circles in reality gradually dimmed, Kariel's face twisted slightly.

They were holding out much longer than he had expected.

For someone who regarded humans as insignificant as insects, it was truly unpleasant.

"I want to sweep them away right now, but…"

But Kariel's way of thinking was fundamentally different from Uriel's.

"There is a much simpler solution."

Realizing it was time to execute the second plan, Kariel approached Miro and extended his hand.

If he detonated the magic circle implanted in her head, her face would explode entirely.

Even after guessing Kariel's intention, Miro silently watched the battlefield.

In front of the Hall of Corruption, the ground was uneven with large and small craters created by Gaold's atmospheric pressure, and deep valleys formed wherever Uriel's touch reached.

"You've gotten stronger, Gaold."

Looking at Gaold, who was sprawled on the ground, Miro smiled for the first time.

Gaold was strong, but he couldn't defeat an archangel.

That was the fundamental concept.

Not the destruction of one, not the destruction of a thousand, just destruction.

The universe flows from beginning to end, and humans, who are the latecomers to this principle, cannot surpass the preceding concept.

"Why am I doing this? I'm just a woman too."

Just the fact that Gaold could fight an archangel for over a minute showed how far he had come.

"Still, honestly, it was a bit touching."

As the magic circle in her head prepared to detonate and sent vibrations, Miro slowly closed her eyes.

"Goodbye, everyone."

Gaold looked at the sky with empty eyes.

"Let's stop."

Had he ever poured out so much of himself in his life?

It was a sense of accomplishment that made him feel like he had achieved something, even though he had achieved nothing.

"Yes, let's give up. It was never mine from the start."

When he thought about it, it was simple.

Why couldn't he forget her? It was just a matter of changing his mind, like flipping his palm.

"I should become the chairman again. I'll meet a woman much better than Miro. I could become incredibly wealthy. I could have the most powerful authority in the world. I'm sick of it."

Gaold shed hot tears.

It was the end.

If he could just forget Miro… the world would be his.

Just Miro…

"Ugh! Ugh!"

Gaold gritted his teeth.

"Damn it! Because I can't do that…!"

He needed nothing.

Not a woman, not wealth, not honor, not even happiness.

"I'm going through this hell because of that!"

Only Miro.

If he could laugh with her for just one day, no, even for one minute, he would pay any price.

"Damn it. Why can't I even have that…?"

The dark thoughts reignited his hell, engulfing the world in flames.

"Are you still going to try?"

Gaold stood up from his lying position.

With his entire nervous system writhing like a monster, he bent his upper body and compressed the Vacuum Press as he sprang forward.

Boom!

As the scorching wind that seemed to melt his skin swept through, Miro's eyes opened again.

Kariel also looked at the battlefield with interest but soon widened his eyes and turned his head.

Clang! Clang! Clang!

Erg's magic circles shattered in succession, and Sein's consciousness returned to reality.

"Now! Go in!"

"Damn it…!"

Kariel accelerated the detonation of the magic circle implanted in Miro's head.

As the loud noise from her brain echoed, Miro's brow furrowed, and sweat dripped from her temples.

"Run! Quickly!"

As Sein's group rushed towards Miro, the fallen angels hurriedly ran in to form a barrier of flesh.

The angels' desperation was evident from their single-minded focus on just filling the space, without considering either attack or defense.

Confirming Miro's condition, Sein urged, "Break through! There's no time!"

Kuan, Etella, and Shina gave their all to open a path.

As soon as a very narrow path was opened, everyone infiltrated through it.

Boom!

A fallen angel descended from the sky at top speed and blocked the path again.

She landed almost crouching, raising her red eyes, which lacked pupils, and grabbed her wrist.

"Pulse Barrier!"

As everyone's biological rhythms synchronized and their movements stopped for a moment, Sein's face turned pale.

The explosive light of the magic circle was leaking through Miro's skull.

"Too late!"

"Yaaah!"

At that moment, a piercing battle cry was heard from below the fortress wall.

Kangnan, with one knee raised, flew like a cannonball and struck the fallen angel's cheek, sending her flying.

There were about 0.8 seconds left until Miro's head would explode.

In that brief moment, the only person who could finish all the calculations was Sein.

Sein, who had widened the gap with his nerve reaction speed, finally broke through the path and reached Miro.

"Hmph, it's already too late."

Kariel, who was thinking even faster, smirked.

"0.4 seconds! Can I make it?"

There was barely enough time to equip the Sun and Moon Wheel.

0.3 seconds.

The iron wheel turned, and Sein's consciousness began to focus on Miro.

0.2 seconds.

At that moment, the servant's calculation made a definite decision.

"About 0.03 seconds too late."

As emotions surged faster than light, his chest tightened.

"Damn it…!"

0.1 seconds.

The magic circle attached to Miro emitted a radiant glow and burned intensely.

And… time stopped.

Stop.

When Armin, who had crossed the distance with the Flicker magic that transcended time and space, cast the Stop magic, Kariel's mental action stopped, and the detonation situation froze at 0.1 seconds.

"Huh? Huh?"

A tear fell belatedly, and Sein, despite being a servant, momentarily lost his composure.

But no one could blame him.

Even Miro, who had maintained her composure until now, showed emotion in her eyes.

"Hurry! Escape!"

As Armin shouted, Sein and Miro, who were looking at each other, moved simultaneously.

Miro reached out her hand, but Sein hugged her waist.

"Sorry, Gaold. Please understand this."

And without a moment to look back, he cast a teleportation spell and flew outside the fortress wall.

"Gaold!"

As Gaold, who was on the ground, looked up, Sein, overwhelmed with emotion, shouted with a contorted face.

"Let's gooooo!"
