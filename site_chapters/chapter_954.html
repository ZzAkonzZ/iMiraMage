Chapter 954: What Goffin Left Behind (1)

Karatorsa nodded.

Shirone's Miracle Stream was on par with the abilities of Goffin that he knew.

"It has been properly passed down."

"Yeah. Thanks."

For Shirone, who had experienced Omega, there were no more mysteries left, and naturally, his speech became more relaxed.

"If it weren't for you all, the spirit of Gaia wouldn't have been passed down to this era." That didn't mean his respect had disappeared.

"I'll convey my gratitude on behalf of Goffin." They were the ones who had protected time from Goffin's erasure to the Great Purification.

"We just fulfilled our mission."

Even though he said that, Karatorsa looked at the ceiling with a distant gaze, feeling deeply moved.

"...Just because you know Omega doesn't mean it's over. As I said before, I can't tell you the history of the era you exist in. You will have to continue to fight the world with your own judgment."

"I'm prepared."

"But..."

Karatorsa's head slowly lowered, and his eyes fixed straight on Shirone.

"There is one more thing. The history of your time that Goffin alone permitted."

"The history that Goffin permitted..."

Shirone swallowed hard.

"Yes. It's an event that happened just before Goffin left the photon system and at the time you were just born. Very few people know that Hexa was born, so it won't have a significant impact on you now. But Goffin asked for your consent. I don't know the details of the event either. If you don't allow it, I will erase the log I have."

He didn't want to convey his emotions while leaving Hexa behind.

'It's a parent's perspective.'

Still, he wanted to know.

'He says he's my real dad, but there's not a single memory.'

Since Shirone didn't appear in Omega, there was no connection between him and Goffin.

"What Goffin left behind."

Shirone muttered softly and took a deep breath.

"Yes. I want to know." As Karatorsa's eyes lit up with a golden light again, some record from Omega flooded in.

With Goffin's gate installed in Istas and the judgment of 20 approaching, Miro visited heaven.

"Is that the child?"

The Plateau of Corruption.

In a landscape swept away by the aftermath of war, Goffin was holding a child.

Goffin looked down at the child and met its eyes with an indescribable emotion on his face.

"Let's see."

When Miro pulled down the child's swaddle, a bright child like a full moon was smiling.

"Hmm."

Goffin chuckled.

"If you think it's cute, just say so honestly. It's only natural that our child is handsome since he's Ikael's and mine."

"Your smile is reaching your ears. How can you give him away and leave if you're so reluctant?" Goffin smiled bitterly.

'Even if you see through everything in the universe, children are still heart-wrenching.'

Feeling sorry, Miro looked at the child again to change the atmosphere.

"Well, he looks 90% like Ikael. Baby, you're lucky. If you looked like your dad... oh dear."

Goffin shouted in disbelief.

"What are you talking about? Anyone can see he's my son! Compare properly. How is this only 10%?"

"No, his face is 100% Ikael. The remaining 10%, hmm, maybe here?"

Miro's finger slowly opened the swaddle towards the child's lower body.

Shirone's shoulders trembled as he accepted the log.

'Miro, please...'

Since it wasn't just a video but felt through 11 senses, even her thoughts were clear to him.

'She's really trying to look.'

At that moment, Goffin quickly twisted his body.

"What are you doing? You can't have my son."

Miro snorted.

"What? You said he's the unique successor and the last defender of the world. Was it all just a bluff?"

"I don't know. Anyway, my son is absolutely not allowed! He'll have a normal relationship with a normal girl."

Miro's eyes widened.

"Mister, he won't even remember soon anyway?"

If Goffin erased the log after leaving the photon system, he wouldn't remember what happened here either.

"Coochie coo, good boy."

Ignoring her words and playing with Shirone, Goffin suddenly showed a gentle gaze.

"He looks like Ikael..."

As Goffin's emotions flowed through empathy, Shirone's eyes welled up with tears.

'How much must he miss her.'

Seeing Goffin's gaze, Miro softened her expression and patted his back as she spoke,

"Let's go. We need to start."

...Huh?

After advancing 100 meters, there was a giant bell-shaped structure rising from the ground.

"Is this... the Tower of Babel?"

Goffin burst into laughter.

"I understand your disappointment. But once, it was tall enough to pierce the sky. And it was enormous."

It had approached the divine.

"The Battle of Babel. In the Second Resistance, Gaia officially lost to the gods. It's a historic place."

"Do you want to show it to the child?"

"Thinking about it, Gaia doesn't really have a hometown. Since realizing Ultima, they rejected the void and challenged the gods. Now, with even the roots disappearing, perhaps this place could become a spiritual home."

Goffin looked down at the child.

"Son, remember the spirit of Gaia that resides in this tower. Although we were defeated, we endlessly challenged. We never succumbed to the world. We never despaired and protected our free will until the end."

Goffin and Miro entered Babel and descended endlessly underground.

Shirone realized.

'They really were here.'

When he entered Babel during the Heaven Project, he felt a sense of déjà vu.

Miro asked,

"Why is the security so strict? Didn't Gaia live here before?"

Goffin, disabling the security devices with Miracle Stream constantly activated, answered,

"It's Heaven's doing. Judging by the code, it's Kariel. Originally, Heaven's technology was Gaia's. Now, the vassal state Mecca continues that legacy."

"...Is it safe?"

As Miro looked around warily, Goffin smirked.

"It's just technology. They can never imitate Gaia's unique spirit."

As the smoke of Miracle Stream spread, the red warning lights in the corridor turned off.

"We can do something here."

Entering the control room, the first thing visible was the cone-shaped pillars installed on the ground and ceiling.

Beyond them were various crude-looking mechanical devices made by Kariel, which seemed crude to Goffin.

"The Tower of Babel operates on the Ultima system. It's a pie in the sky for angels."

When he placed his hand between the cones and activated the Ultima system, powerful electrical energy condensed.

Simultaneously, all the devices lit up, and Babel activated, bringing the screen to life.

"Main system access. Codename Babel Project."

As Miro watched, the logic circuits of the operating program appeared on the screen.

"What's the point of activating it now?"

"Well, still." Goffin felt a bit embarrassed.

"But since we're here, I feel like I should leave something behind. After all, I'm the last Gaia."

We existed in this world.

"Of course, if Hexa can't defeat the gods, this record will also return to nothingness..."

That's why it's Omega.

"This time, I'm just throwing it out there. My heart."

As his thoughts converted to text, characters that had never existed before began to appear on the screen.

Miro couldn't read them either.

'The number of the law 1.'

Thus, it was only Goffin's language.

"Gaia used different characters according to their individuality. But communication was never a problem. After all, the signal that penetrates this world is one."

That is empathy, the Ultima system.

Therefore, if Shirone comes here in the future, he will be able to read Goffin's record.

'When Anke Ra attempts the second reset... it will be around Omega 412.'

Goffin, who recorded a specific era of Gaia in Omega units, filled the last 999 years.

'The last Gaia, McClain Goffin. Departure from the photon system.'

The name would be erased anyway, but with this, Gaia's history ended.

'The future...'

Goffin, lost in thought for a moment, muttered.

"Beyond infinity."

A sentence sent to someone was recorded again, and the screen turned off.

"Is it over?"

Miro asked.

Behind her, a dimensional door connected to Istas had opened.

"Then give me Hexa. If you swap him with the prince of Kazra and initialize, Hexa's cause will be removed forever. Then Anke Ra won't be able to touch him."

"That's right."

Since it was a matter of humanity's future, Goffin handed over the child without hesitation.

However, his hand seemed to tremble slightly.

"...The child will be safe. I can't persuade Yolga, but I won't show mercy either."

"Yes. Please."

As Miro was about to take Hexa, she looked intently at the child's face and then raised her gaze.

"As a father, any last words?"

Parting forever.

Knowing that leaving room for hope would be more painful, Miro felt she couldn't leave like this.

"Last words."

Advice for a son who would have to fight the world alone...

"If you ever."

Goffin stroked the child and said,

"If you ever have to fight evil so strong that you can't protect yourself, it's okay to follow the methods of evil."

The seal of Behemoth, engraved in Shirone's inner self, glowed red for a moment.

"If you become strong enough to protect your family, stand in the neutrality of good and evil."

There's nothing greater than protecting your family.

"Dad couldn't do that."

As Goffin's eyes reddened with a hollow laugh, tears flowed from Shirone's eyes as well.

"If you become strong enough to protect your family and more people, uphold good."

That is righteousness.

"And if... you become strong enough to protect the whole world..."

Goffin lifted Hexa high.

"Listen to everyone's stories."

Nothing born from one is insignificant.

"Agoo. Aah."

The child smiled brightly, seemingly pleased to see Goffin's face from a high place.

As if swallowing his tears, Goffin lifted his head to the end and slowly lowered his arms.

Miro, now reverent, gently took the child and turned towards the dimensional door.

"How much time do I have?"

"Istas's time must be linked with space. When events diverge, the relativity of time reaches its extreme. One second here could be decades in Istas."

It was a closed loop of time.

"Don't worry, I'll definitely succeed. If I can't return in time, just close the time. I won't resent you."

"No. You'll resent me anyway. If I disappear, you'll just be a scapegoat for humanity."

Goffin, lost in thought for a moment, continued.

"I'm sorry to you, and I'm prepared to be hated. Just protect Hexa."

Nodding, Miro stepped into Istas and muttered.

"Yolga."

The next moment, Miro, covered in blood, was seen walking down the corridor.

In the closed loop of time, space was time, so from Goffin's perspective, Warehouse 72 was the same time zone.

'If I create an independent time within the world's time and leave the photon system...'

In another warehouse in Istas, Miro might have already been affected by Goffin's erasure.

In the closed loop, it was the future, but in the overall time, it was actually the past, the mystery of Istas.

'The prince of Kazra... is not here.'

It was the first time seeing Miro so murderous, but she nodded silently.

'Thank you.'

There must have been a tragic event, but Goffin didn't ask and closed the dimensional door.

With this, Miro left the event of escaping Istas in the closed loop of time.

'All preparations are complete.'

Goffin muttered, looking up at the ceiling.

"This time, you can't stop it, Anke Ra."

Because he's not alone.

As Ikael's tears flowed through Hexa, a vast Ataraxia unfolded.

"Roar!"

Amplification.

Goffin's body itself became a Miracle Stream and began to compress infinitely.

'There's no more Ultima in this world, but...'

He left it behind.

'A single hope.'

As his body gradually spread into light, a faint smile appeared on Goffin's face.

"It was fun."

His body disappeared as if blinking.

'Pierce through.'

As he passed through the boundary of the photon system, an unknown layer without even the concept of time and space.

'What's that?'

A blond boy, looking just like Ikael, was seen running towards him.

"Cheeky kid."

Goffin understood it all. "You've turned out to be a cool guy.” See, you do look like me.
