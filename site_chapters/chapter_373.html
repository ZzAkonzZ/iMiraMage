Chapter 373: Senior Information Session (1)

The special tradition of Class Two students welcoming the new Class Three students began.

As Shirone crossed the Iron Gate, the jeers abruptly stopped, and the students parted to create a path for him.

Shirone walked down the path to the sound of applause. Though it was meant to be welcoming, it didn't bring a smile to his face.

'So these are the Class Two students.'

When ten students graduate from the senior class, the remaining twenty are ranked based on their graduation exam scores. Therefore, the ten students welcoming him were those who had been eliminated relatively early in last year's graduation exams.

Amy was among them, clapping.

She had been the third to be eliminated last year, placing her in Class Two and 18th in the senior class rankings.

Shirone fixed his gaze straight ahead, observing their faces from the corners of his eyes.

Men, women.

That was all he could discern for now.

Although he had observed last year's graduation exams and remembered some students' specialties, they felt like strangers to him now.

'If I can't get past this, I won't make it to the top ranks.'

Class Two students were the core of the senior class, marking the boundary between the upper and lower ranks.

This year's senior class had an average age of 20.6 years.

Shirone was 1.6 years younger than the average, but considering the senior veterans like Fermi, most were around his age, with some even younger.

After passing through the path made by the Class Two students, the Class Three students circled back to the Iron Gate. They stood face-to-face with the Class Two students lined up in front of them.

"Welcome to the senior class."

A confident-looking young man stepped forward.

Shirone recognized his voice as the one who had taunted them the most before they crossed the Iron Gate.

He was a handsome man with slicked-back blond hair, wearing a blue tracksuit that fit him perfectly, though it was not typical mage attire.

He was about 175 cm tall, with a lean but well-balanced physique that didn't look weak at all.

His sharp eyes and high nose gave him a wolf-like appearance, and his tongue, oddly red, looked as if it were stained with blood when he smiled.

"I am Screamer, ranked first in Class Two."

As soon as Screamer spoke, the same thought crossed the minds of the Class Three students.

'He's 11th overall.'

Screamer's specialty was light magic, but his field differed from Shirone's photonic magic or Dante's information magic.

To be precise, he was a magic martial artist.

Like a hybrid of a gunner, he used various light magic to confuse his opponents and employed physical combat skills to deliver critical hits.

Another student in the senior class who could use schema was Amy.

However, unlike Amy, who had only briefly learned it as a child, Screamer's schema was quite advanced.

His flashy teleportation movements and ability to finish opponents with his fists had drawn attention during last year's graduation exams.

He was expected to enter the Colosseum to learn martial arts after graduating from the magic school. If not for these complex circumstances, he could have graduated earlier.

After Screamer, Pony stepped forward.

Her calm, golden hair flowed down her sides, framing her pretty crescent-shaped forehead. Her expressionless face seemed to freeze her beautiful features in place.

With her white face, crescent-shaped eyebrows, sharp nose, and pointed chin, she looked like a doll from a distance.

"I am Pony, ranked 12th. My specialty is hydromancy, in the torrent series."

While Screamer's introduction was lengthy, Pony's was brief.

Even so, the Class Three students couldn't take their eyes off her. She was not only beautiful but also had an important detail missing from her introduction.

'Oh, so that's Pony.'

The only royal member of Alpheas Magic School.

Though her maternal lineage kept her away from the core of power, she was still a royal by their standards and not someone nobles could treat lightly.

Of course, competition in the senior class was fair. Pony didn't mention her lineage to avoid unnecessary animosity.

Pony's eyes were filled with royal pride, reminiscent of Prince Zion of Kazra. Though not as intense as Uorin's absolute gaze.

After Pony, the introductions continued.

Shirone listened with interest to each one. Then he felt the gaze of the girl standing in front of him and turned his head.

While everyone else focused on the current speaker, she had been watching Shirone with playful eyes from the beginning.

'Her name was... Maya, right?'

With her brown skin and plump figure, her round face and pleasant features made her look cute.

Shirone remembered her distinctly.

She was the first to be eliminated in last year's graduation exam. He had heard she was also the last in the previous year's exam.

Her specialty was sound magic, using buffs and debuffs through sound, but she hadn't left a strong impression.

In short, she lacked talent.

Her sound magic couldn't penetrate a mage’s mind, and she relied on basic magic learned in the advanced class.

Her fundamentals were solid, but she wasn't skilled enough to compete with geniuses. She was on the borderline between the advanced and senior classes.

When Shirone looked at her, Maya smiled and raised her hand.

"Hi."

Shirone glanced around. It wasn't polite to talk during introductions.

But she didn't care and leaned in to speak softly.

"I know you. Arian Shirone."

It was an awkward situation to respond to. Shirone hesitated before whispering back.

"Oh, I see."

"I saw you at last year's research presentation. That ghost incident. It was amazing! How did you come up with that? It was so clever. I've been a fan ever since."

Maya still vividly remembered that day.

She had been lying in bed after hearing the midnight bell when a ghost appeared from the ceiling, scaring her out of her wits.

Following the fleeing students, she ended up in the central park. There, the three members of the Supernatural Psychic Science Research Club appeared on the rooftop.

Their presentation, which exploited psychological gaps, was so entertaining that she laughed until she had stomach cramps.

"I also enjoyed the play. You looked great in a dress. What did you use for the chest?"

Screamer frowned and turned around.

"Hey, Maya. We're in the middle of introductions."

"Oh, sorry. I was just excited."

"Ugh, no sense of decorum..."

Maya stuck her tongue out at Shirone with an embarrassed expression.

It was still unclear what kind of person she was. But the Class Two students didn't seem to think highly of her.

Always finishing last, some didn't even consider her a senior class member.

Screamer was one of them.

Missing Class One by one rank, he was annoyed to be in the same class as Maya.

After the introductions, Screamer, now in a good mood, spoke brightly.

"Now that we know each other, let's start the Iron Gate tradition of 'Truth or Dare.'"

Pony muttered with a frown.

"Tradition, my foot... It's so tacky."

"Truth or Dare?"

Shirone raised an eyebrow, waiting for an explanation. Screamer, with his characteristic grand gestures, began to explain.

"Each Class Two student will ask a question to the person opposite them. For example, 'Who's the most handsome?' Then you point to one of us."

'Hmm, so that's how it is.'

There was no better way to intimidate newcomers.

Though the example was about the most handsome person, no one expected such a pleasant question.

'So that's why Amy told me to always pick her.'

Screamer turned to a young boy.

"Eider, you go first. You're the youngest."

"Ugh, even here, it's about age?"

Eider was seventeen. He was the reason Amy didn't get the title of youngest senior last year.

With his big cat-like eyes and dimples when he smiled, he looked much younger than his age. His short stature also contributed to that impression.

'Why emphasize age?'

Shirone tilted his head.

What did being the youngest mean in a competitive senior class?

Even so, Eider only pouted slightly before complying with Screamer's request.

'Being the youngest must have its advantages.'

Acknowledging his youth might bring minor inconveniences, but the benefits likely outweighed them.

'Even I let my guard down around Eider.'

Eider's target was Arin. The Class Two students watched with interest.

Arin, the first menstruating girl. Whatever question he asked, it would yield interesting results.

After some thought, Eider grinned mischievously.

"Who's the ugliest?"

"Huh?"

Arin hadn't expected that question.

She couldn't properly perceive shapes. This information had spread through the school, so the seniors likely knew.

The Class Two students tensed.

To her, ugliness might reflect inner distortion.

"The ugliest person?"

"Yes. Someone so ugly you wonder how a human could look like that."

"Hmm."

Arin stared at the grinning Eider.

Then she tapped her lips and carefully scanned the Class Two students.

Some avoided her gaze.

'Why ask this? Is he testing me?'

Arin raised her index finger and pointed to someone on her right.

"I'll pick you."

The student was so fat that just looking at him made one feel hot.

Despite the graduation event, he held a bag of potato chips, seemingly addicted to food.

His eyes were buried in his chubby cheeks, and his hair was a messy mop.

Wearing tight clothes that revealed his body shape, he looked awkward.

'That's Luman.'

Luman, ranked 13th in the senior class.

His specialty was trap magic, in the crowd control series.

Though skilled, his unpleasant personality often put him at a disadvantage.

Screamer clapped rhythmically.

"Wow, a woman's intuition is sharp. Even if she can't see well, she can still judge appearances accurately. Who knows, if the outside is a mold, maybe the inside is just as ugly."

Some students laughed.

Among them was Boyle from Class Three. However, his laughter didn't seem genuine.

The psychological battle had already begun, and it seemed they had decided to be aggressive from the start.

Despite being mocked, Luman just kept munching on his potato chips. Then he pointed at Boyle and asked,

"But why are you laughing? Shouldn't you not be laughing?"

Boyle exaggeratedly frowned.

"What? What does it matter if I laugh or not?"

"It doesn't matter. But you're fat too. If you laugh, isn't that self-deprecation?"

Boyle, looking at Luman in disbelief, retorted with the words his mother had always taught him.

"I'm chubby, and you're fat. There's a big difference. I have an air of nobility, and you're just a pig."

Luman paused his chewing for a moment, then quickly resumed. That was his only reaction.
