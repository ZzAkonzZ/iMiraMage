Chapter 719:

Labyrinth Jessica.

In the giant city divided into ten final stages, Jin Seong-eum arrived at the boundary of the sixth stage.

It was an achievement that even the most renowned organizations couldn't accomplish despite ten years of preparation, and the 200 members of the Volcano Expedition, who had preemptively occupied the area, were humiliated.

"Form a perimeter! Don't give them a chance to counterattack!"

Their skills were undoubtedly the best in the industry, holding the record for the highest human exploration in Jessica, one of the world's three great labyrinths.

"Princess! Danger… Ugh!"

The members of the Three Treasures scattered in all directions as thermal beams rained down from the sky.

'Damn it! Ancient weapons!'

The shoulder armor worn by the Volcano members was an ancient weapon called Kit, which fired thermal beams from the central lens.

'What's more annoying is the gravitational collapse.'

With about 100 Volcano members flying around and bombarding the area, the entire place was devastated.

'I must find the princess! I must protect her!'

The ancient weapons buried here were not primitive technologies like the Signa or Exd used by the citizens of Heaven.

In a situation where phenomena that modern technology couldn't replicate threatened Seong-eum, Mun-gyeong's heart pounded.

Although she was evaluated as the most powerful among the candidates for the Ivory Tower, she lacked real combat experience.

Having spent most of her life in the Imperial City, it was uncertain if she could withstand the professional fighters.

"Princess!"

Pushing through the smoke, Mun-gyeong found Seong-eum standing alone in the middle of the Volcano's camp.

'How did she get there…?'

She had clearly entered on her own, and it was a fatal mistake.

"Now! Finish her off!"

As 200 members simultaneously flew into the sky and fired thermal beams at Seong-eum, Mun-gyeong felt dizzy.

'No! That's…!'

Having followed Seong-eum from a distance all her life, Mun-gyeong knew she had never encountered an attack faster than her senses.

"It's over!"

As the Volcano officer shouted, a calm light flickered in Seong-eum's eyes.

Ether Wave - Great Bend.

"W-what is that?"

The beams, moving like objects on rails, naturally bent and swirled around Seong-eum.

Seong-eum raised one corner of her mouth slightly.

"…I'll allow up to two steps."

As she extended her hands, the grandly swirling beams all gathered between her palms.

The world's scenery twisted and tangled like a knotted thread, eventually shimmering in natural colors.

"Ahhh."

In the entangled space that the human mind couldn't analyze, Mun-gyeong felt as if she had lost her sense of direction in life.

'That is… the princess's, Jin Seong-eum's two steps.'

No one would ever enter.

'No, it would be the same for anyone. It's a realm humans cannot step into.'

"The Great Bend's oscillation."

As Seong-eum spread her arms, the particles of the thermal beams began to seep into the scenery like osmosis.

"Dodge, dodge!"

The Volcano leader shouted, but he and the members already knew.

'We can't dodge.'

In Seong-eum's space, directionality didn't exist.

"Ugh!"

As the powerful heat struck the sky, the 200 members evaporated in an instant, leaving no trace of blood.

"I should leave one alive."

Seong-eum reached out to the leader, who was flying around, having lost control of the Kit.

As she crossed the space and grabbed his ankle, she pulled him down at an incredible speed, slamming him into the ground.

"Ahhh!"

Approaching the leader, who was writhing with a broken leg, Seong-eum hid her expression and asked.

"Tell me everything you know."

The leader glared at her with tears of blood.

Labyrinth Pytharos.

In the labyrinth where the laws of the seven deadly sins formed layers of different densities, Nane reached the final layer.

"Hungry! Hungry! Hungry!"

Listening to the cries of the hungry ghosts would drive any human insane…

'Why do we suffer?'

They too were once human.

'If the equivalent exchange of pain and happiness is impossible, then there is no reason to exist from the beginning.'

Nane never walked quickly, but she arrived at the labyrinth's destination before any other candidates.

The only exit of Pytharos, the Gate of Annihilation.

The moment he crossed that gate, time would stop, and only the eternal continuation of nothingness would remain.

"Please take us with you! Please end the suffering of this wretched existence! We beg you!"

Despite this, countless hungry ghosts crawled on the ground, bowing their heads at Nane's feet and pleading.

They too had experienced all seven layers of hell and knew what Nane was.

"Poor beings…"

Nane looked at the hungry ghosts with pity.

"Are you human?"

The masters of the underworld guarding the Gate of Annihilation approached, stomping their massive feet on the iron plate.

Their horns grew wildly like tree vines, and their muscular flesh bulged red, inciting the lust of the hungry ghosts.

"Amazing. You've passed through all seven hells and still haven't lost yourself."

Nane extended his hand and commanded.

"Demons, open the gate and save these beings."

Passing through the Gate of Annihilation into the world of nothingness was the last refuge left for the hungry ghosts.

"This gate was not made by you."

The demons approached, snorting flames.

"But wasn't this hell made by you?"

The hungry ghosts fled in terror, and dozens of demons surrounded Nane.

"There is no exit for you. You too will perish here over the eons and become a hungry ghost."

Nane raised her hand and formed a circle with her thumb and index finger.

"Destroy the demons and save the beings."

As the sword tattoo on Nane's tongue burned golden, numerous swords of light floated around her.

Sermon - Tongue of Truth.

Nane was almost right.

Thus, the sound created by her tongue, with a Karr value of 99.999%, became the truth.

"Form is emptiness; emptiness is form."

The swords of light, manifested through sword transformation, pierced the essence of the opponent with the same intensity as the enlightenment gained through Zen dialogue.

"Ahhh!"

As the swords of light radiated in all directions and pierced the demons, their red skin turned to ash and burned away.

"Closing your eyes marks the boundary between existence and non-existence."

Nane was almost right.

"Follow me, wretched beings."

As Nane opened the Gate of Annihilation, a space of pitch-black darkness, devoid of sound or sensation, was revealed.

"Seeker…"

The hungry ghosts, shedding tears of overwhelming emotion, knelt simultaneously and called out to Nane.

"If we enter there, will everything disappear?"

"Yes. You will be freed from the pain of existence."

The hungry ghosts clasped their hands and pleaded.

"If this is the end, could we taste meat one last time? Just for the last time…"

Sadness filled Nane's eyes.

'Attachment. Attachment to life.'

The gap between the fleeting memory left after pleasure and the sensory void as if it never happened was the karma of living beings.

'Is it something that cannot be severed?'

Realizing that the hungry ghosts couldn't cross the gate, Nane sat down and extended her hand.

"Eat."

The hungry ghost, already drooling, trembled and bit Nane's finger.

"Since it's the end anyway. This is the last time…"

All the hungry ghosts pounced and began to savagely devour Nane's body.

"Delicious! Delicious! This is the best!"

The crazed hungry ghosts quickly consumed Nane's body, and when there was nothing left to eat, they scratched their necks with their nails.

"Hungry, hungry! I want more! Hungry!"

No one jumped through the Gate of Annihilation.

'With this, I give everything and leave.'

Having lost his physical body in the underworld, Nane crossed the Gate of Annihilation in a spiritual state.

In the realm without time or space, the only existence that could be born from nothing awaited her.

It was Ra Enemy, who dreamed of this world.

"…"

Reincarnated in human form, but with no muscles, blind eyes, and a crushed nose.

Unable to speak or hear.

In that withered body, Ra Enemy, existing as a past event, entered Nane's memory.

"Do you resent me?"

Ra Enemy smiled beautifully.

"Anyone can dream."

Even if it wasn't La Enemy, someone would dream, and if that was all there was to the world we lived in, nothing would change.

"So… what did you realize?"

Nane hesitated for a moment.

For someone who could think fast enough to stop time, it was an almost eternal contemplation.

"Only one thing."

Nane raised his index finger and said.

"Emptiness."

Labyrinth Andre-World No. 9999.

The towering buildings were made of neither stone nor iron.

There wasn't a speck of dust in the air, and despite the absence of wires, a perfect power grid provided light.

"As expected… there's no one awake."

According to the information obtained through the Ultima system, this place was a far future compared to where Shirone lived.

From what he had explored over the past few days, all the humans in this world were in human-sized glass tubes.

'They're either all dead or asleep.'

As he was lost in thought, a small sphere silently flew in from the sky, opened in the middle, and a lens popped out.

A laser scanned Shirone's retina, and a voice came from the speaker on the side.

"Searching for non-registered entity. Unable to find code. Danger level zero. Will you reincarnate?"

Shirone asked excitedly.

"Are you human? Where is this place? Where can I find people who are awake?"

The sphere tilted at an angle.

"Assumed temporary memory loss due to post-mortem stress. Would you like to undergo a detailed examination at the center?"

"A detailed examination? But I don't have memory loss…"

Shirone stopped speaking and nodded.

"Yes, I'll take the examination. Just take me somewhere I can talk to people."

"Transferring unregistered code to the center."

Hundreds of identical spheres flew over from the other side of the building and wrapped around Shirone's limbs and waist.

"Huh?"

Shirone's body floated up and quickly flew through the sky, carried by the drone spheres.

"Arrived at the center."

Gracefully changing direction, the drones brought Shirone to the first floor of the tallest tower in the city.

"A detailed examination will be conducted. Estimated wait time: 3 minutes and 2 seconds."

Shirone looked around the room, but there were only rows of machines where people could lie down.

"Wait! Is there really no one awake?"

Shirone shouted in frustration, but the drone spheres left the room without responding.

"What am I supposed to do here?"

"Please lie down on table number 1."

The voice from the ceiling was clearly not human.

'I need to investigate this myself.'

As Shirone tried to leave the room, another drone flew in and blocked his way.

"Sorry, but please let me go. I don't have memory loss…"

A light shot out from the lower lens of the drone watching Shirone, and a hologram appeared.

"Amy?"

It was Amy, with perfect features.

"Hello. I am Administrator Argo."
