Chapter 1157: Unsolved cases (4)

Under World.

If the headquarters of the Fire Management Corporation was the heart of Hell, then the System Control Division was its brain.

Commonly referred to as The Company, it was the institution responsible for processing all incidents within the system and correcting any errors.

"It's so quiet."

A man in a suit yawned as he glanced at Melkidu’s system log.

Even though his rank was that of a low-level employee, he was still an Agent, a type of administrator.

"What do you mean quiet? We’ve got a mountain of work to do. We’re just not doing it."

There was no such thing as being fired from the Company, but…

"Tch, do you know how long I’ve been doing this?"

There was no quitting either.

"Anyway, why is it so noisy today? The security staff have it good. They can wander around freely."

"They say Yahweh has arrived."

"Hmm, Yahweh…"

It took him a while to reactivate the part of his brain he hadn’t used in a long time.

"Wait, what? Yahweh?!"

The man shot up in shock, but his colleague remained indifferent.

"Calm down. Do you know what will happen if this facility gets discovered? Yahweh is already in Melkidu."

The man sat back down and checked the monitors.

"… What’s going on?"

"The team leader said that dozens of Agents were taken down at the entrance. If they had charged in the same way here, the entire Company’s industrial sector would have been wiped out."

"You’ve got to be kidding me. And yet they let Yahweh into the office? Wait, hold on—did the branch director approve this?"

"You don’t seem to understand. This is beyond that. The President gave the order directly."

"Lady Lethe…?"

"Yahweh is probably here because of the Jin Seong Eum issue. Fortunately, the President was near the Valley of Wailing, so she was able to intervene in time. She ordered us to stall for as long as possible until she arrives. That’s why Yahweh was allowed to enter without resistance."

The man furrowed his brow.

"Lady Lethe herself? Is that really necessary? The Under World has the most administrators in the system. If we mobilized all of Black Chains and Agents from around the world, we could stop anything."

"They killed Monoras, the Chief Secretary."

The man’s jaw dropped.

"You’re lying."

"I thought so too, at first. Lady Lethe can revert everything in Hell back into demons, while Monoras can consume all demons in Hell. And what he ate was none other than the strongest lifeform in the material world—Ymir."

His colleague turned his head.

"Who do you think took him down? Yahweh? No, it was a human traveling with Yahweh. He can control the Chains of Gehenna. That makes him, in a limited scope, just like Lady Lethe."

Only now did the man grasp the severity of the situation.

"Stay quiet until Lady Lethe settles this. It won’t be resolved so easily."

"And if it isn’t resolved?"

"Then, well…"

His colleague turned back to the monitor.

"One of us—either them or us—will be completely wiped out."

"Why is this taking so long?"

Shirone’s group had been waiting for about two hours in the guest room after passing through the industrial sector into the Company’s headquarters.

"I can’t take this anymore!"

Son Yu-jeong suddenly jumped to her feet.

"I’m going to see Mortasinger right now. If anyone tries to stop me, I’ll take them down!"

Shirone spoke in a clipped tone.

"Son Yu-jeong."

Her shoulders flinched.

"Stay put. Don’t ruin everything."

"Ugh. Fine."

She turned with a deep sigh and was about to sit back down when—

"… As if!"

Suddenly, she pushed off the ground, dashing forward while thrusting her right staff toward the door.

At that moment, the door opened.

"You are to remain he—"

The moment Son Yu-jeong saw Lethe’s familiar face, she bared her teeth.

"Move!"

Shirone’s eyes widened.

"Ugh!"

As soon as her Golden Staff shrank, Son Yu-jeong collapsed as if her strings had been cut.

"Owww, I’m sorry! Please…!"

Lethe glanced at the squirming Son Yu-jeong—or rather, at the face of Ricera attached to her waist.

Annoying.

If not for that escapee’s extensive knowledge of Hell, Shirone wouldn’t have made it in time.

Was this fate too?

Lifting her gaze, she scanned Shirone, Rian, Etella, and Shagall one by one.

Yahweh, Gehenna, Taeguk. How many karmic debts do you all carry?

They were ultra VIPs.

"Greetings. I am Lethe, the President of Fire Management Corporation. So we finally meet like this."

There was no response.

Stepping over Son Yu-jeong in one long stride, she seated herself across from Shirone at the table.

"Alright, I’ll make a concession."

A long silence followed as they locked eyes, until Lethe finally closed hers as if resigned.

"I’ll offer myself instead. Being the most beautiful woman in the Under World, that should be acceptable, right?"

"I’m not in the mood for jokes."

Lethe slammed the table.

"Then what do you expect me to do?! Jin Seong Eum cannot be freed! It’s systemically impossible!"

"It’s possible. If Hell is purified."

"Then all the demons would be wiped out! Do you have any idea what you’re saying? Do you know how hard I work to maintain this place?"

Lethe was livid.

"Yahweh, Buddha, whatever—you people aren’t exactly well-treated in the material world. You wander aimlessly between ideologies and beliefs, yet when it comes to Hell, you act like gangsters! Why are you wreaking havoc here?!"

Fighting humans was Satan’s job. She just wanted to keep the system running.

"Think about it from my perspective. Just as Great Star exists for humanity, I exist for demons. All you’re saying is that you want war."

Taeseong exists for humanity.

Shirone liked the sound of that.

"Let’s make a deal. I’ll erase the Demon Realm in reality, but in return, you must take responsibility for your karmic debts here. The amount of karma you all carry is enough to feed every Mara in the Under World for the next ten thousand years, so there’s plenty of room for negotiation."

"The Demon Realm can go. But Jin Seong Eum is non-negotiable. And I heard there is a way."

Lethe blinked.

"Huh?"

"She can escape through the Hell Prison, can’t we? There should be an empty slot right now. Because I killed one."

Technically, Wizard had killed another recently, meaning two spots were now open.

"Haah…"

Leaning back in her chair, Lethe let out a deep sigh.

"Unbelievable. Sure, the Company designs the Hell Prison codes, but do you even know what the Hell Prison is?"

"A fanatic."

"So you do know… No, forget it. I’ll just show you directly. What do you think?"

Son Yu-jeong, who had been sitting in the corner with a sullen expression, spoke.

"We'll be able to meet Mortasinger too, right?"

"Mortasinger? Oh, of course. She's in the Hell Prison candidate pool. Though, to be honest… she doesn’t seem to have much talent."

Lethe stood up.

"Let's go. It’s not every day you get a chance to tour the Company, the System Control Division."

Lethe’s mind was racing.

She needed to buy time.

Yahweh, with his heart of Agape, was a tyrant in the Under World, but he was different in reality.

Satan’s law would take care of it. If they could end him in reality, the Under World would remain safe.

Gyorgy had a plan.

"Then, let’s depart."

Hiding her thoughts behind a pure smile, Lethe opened the door and stepped into the hallway.

Men in suits were waiting.

Even though they were Agents, their presence was on an entirely different level compared to the ones Shagall had taken down at the entrance.

"Who are they?"

Lethe introduced them.

"They’re the board members of the Fire Management Corporation. The Company’s branch director is also among them. They will be guiding you."

A white-haired old man gave a polite nod.

"I am Venistov, the branch director. It seems you have an interest in Hidden Codes. I will be your escort."

Leading the way, he walked ahead while Shirone’s group and the board members followed in an orderly line through the corridor.

"Agents…"

Shirone muttered, prompting Lethe to respond.

"This is the most heavily managed of the five major systems. Handling emotions is extremely difficult. But Agents can do it. From the moment they come into existence, they understand Hidden Codes."

"So emotions are just another form of logic to them."

"Yes. If you define Agents from the perspective of the material world, they are autonomous processes that carry out specific tasks. To them, Hidden Codes are as natural as gravity is to humans."

She brought up the material world’s perspective to earn Shirone’s trust.

But all he felt was danger.

"This is where the combustion levels are regulated…"

Sticking to the purpose of the tour, Venistov explained each department as they passed through.

It was clearly Lethe’s doing.

If Shirone understood the Under World better, negotiations later would be much easier.

Venistov gestured toward a staircase.

"Let’s go straight to the 12th floor. That’s where Hell Prison candidates undergo testing. Once we get there—"

"Wait."

Shirone interrupted.

"What’s that door?"

It was an unmarked door, standing alone without any identifying signs.

"These doors are installed on every floor. There’s one just like it on the 12th floor."

His attempt to divert attention was evident—lying recklessly in a situation like this would only backfire.

"So, what is it?"

"… It leads to the Engine Room."

"Engine Room?"

Venistov glanced at Lethe for approval, and after a brief pause, she silently nodded.

"I will guide you. Please follow me."

Instead of explaining further, he opened the door and stepped into a spherical elevator that descended underground.

As the sphere carried them downward at terrifying speed, the sound of a heartbeat reverberated through the air.

At the entrance of a long, straight tunnel, a horrifying white noise seeped through the walls.

It sounded like hundreds of thousands of voices, each screaming in a different tone.

"Beyond that wall is the Central Processing Unit, where all the demons of Hell are processed."

"I see. So it’s really nothing special."

Shirone shot her a glare, and Lethe feigned an indignant expression.

"That’s why I brought you here. I had no hidden intentions. While it’s a critical facility, it’s just an information-processing mechanism. The only reason I didn’t want to show you was for your own sake."

The devices of the Under World were biological in nature, and they weren’t exactly beautiful to look at.

"I need to see for myself."

With a sigh, Lethe nodded, and Venistov proceeded without hesitation.

"This is it."

Suspended between the cliffs of what could have been an entire city was a massive, brain-like structure.

No, it wasn’t a brain.

Attached to it were countless fetuses, ranging in size from a fingernail to an adult’s forearm.

They wailed in a chaotic chorus, emitting an eerie cacophony that even Rian found disturbing.

"What… is this place?"

Lethe answered.

"I told you, you’d regret it. As you can see, these are fetuses—specifically, aborted children. They don’t possess human logic, but they do have emotions. Their fear of death draws them into the Under World."

Shirone’s face hardened.

"The Central Processing Unit…"

"Yes. Only fetuses can process demons in the Under World. The Company was built here precisely because demons naturally flow into this space, shaped by the structure of the Law. They have no logic—only raw, chaotic emotions."

The group watched in silence as the underdeveloped bodies squirmed, struggling not to fall.

Below the cliffs, there was fire.

Poor children.

Etella, who had been standing in the back with a sorrowful expression, suddenly flinched.

"Ugh!"

A sharp pain shot through her lower abdomen, causing her hand to instinctively clutch her stomach.

No way… This can’t be…

Shagall, who was connected to her through the Chains of Taeguk, turned to her with a stiff expression.

"You…"

"It’s nothing. Don’t worry about it."

Shutting down his concern coldly, Etella found herself confronted with a terrifying truth.

Ever since arriving in this world, her menstruation had stopped.

She had assumed it was because she had already died.

But if there was another reason…?

If… it was actually possible?

Because she had fallen into Hell while still in a state of life.

The abandoned fetuses continued to wail.

"Ah… ahh… aaaaah!"
