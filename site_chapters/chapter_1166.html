Chapter 1166:

Kangnan approached Gaold.

"Are you okay?"

His eyes were wide open, as if he had suffered a tremendous mental shock.

'Of course he has.'

Kangnan didn’t fully understand Gaold’s pain, but she knew how vast the gap between imagination and reality could be.

‘Those eyes must have been the Ultima of the Gaia people, just as Luber explained. In that moment, Gaold was defined.’

Along with an unbearable pain no human could withstand.

“Hey! Is everyone safe?”

Sein arrived, leading the Merchant Alliance.

“You actually did it?”

The merchants were shocked to see the armory destroyed, but Sein’s expression remained serious.

“Those eyes just now…”

“Yeah. It seems the merchants couldn’t see them. The compromise has been broken. Shirone and Miro have awakened…”

Now, it was Gaold’s turn.

Sein watched Gaold, who was still lying there in a daze, for a long time.

‘So that’s what happened…’

If the real world was truly reality, then it made sense why Gaold couldn’t stand back up.

‘It must be terrifying.’

Kangnan spoke.

“The more we awaken, the more this world collapses. Ymir is already waiting to consume us.”

Should they have just done nothing?

“Let’s go.”

Gaold stood up.

“There’s no need to overthink it. First, we need to find Miro. She must have gone to the headquarters.”

Kangnan asked, “Will you be okay?”

Would he be able to awaken again?

Even Gaold himself couldn’t be sure, but he didn’t stop walking.

“My awakening means that Miro and Shirone’s state is different from what we know. We can save them.”

Kangnan felt a nostalgic pull.

‘That’s just the kind of person he is.’

Even the kind of pain that could never be forgotten—he erased it from his memory and kept moving forward.

He was the ultimate form of human resilience.

The Tamo Syndicate’s headquarters was a four-story building.

Gunfire rained down from the second and third floors, and Miro deflected the bullets with a skill bordering on the divine.

‘I’m starting to get the hang of this.’

She wasn’t just deflecting bullets—she was redirecting them, sending them back at the enemies.

“Damn it! What the hell is that?!”

The pursuers from the port opened fire from the rear, but they couldn’t penetrate Shirone’s ability.

Miro felt her confidence rise.

“Nice, Shirone! Let’s keep going just like this!”

“Those little…!”

One of the furious syndicate members pulled the pin on a grenade and hurled it toward the ground.

“No!”

A senior officer shouted, but the grenade was already arcing toward Miro.

"Nice!"

Miro snapped her fingers, deflecting the grenade with Avalokiteśvara’s power. It soared up to the third floor and exploded.

“Aaaargh! Damn it!”

From the impregnable safe zone, Arius stood up on two legs, clutching his head before twisting his waist.

“What the hell is that mutt?!”

The psychological damage was immense.

“Boss! At this rate, we’re finished!”

From the fourth-floor window, Tamo watched the battlefield intently.

‘They’re using some strange abilities.’

They were deflecting bullets at supersonic speeds—and even erasing their kinetic force entirely.

“… Beautiful.”

The subordinates, who knew their leader was asexual, were shocked to hear such words from his mouth.

‘The boss just…?’

Rumor had it that back in his mercenary days, Tamo had once been captured by enemy forces.

They had tortured him in every way imaginable, even castrating him.

Perhaps it was from that moment that his ruthless and superhuman combat abilities emerged.

“One hundred people entered the 1.5th layer, but not all of them were created with the same program.”

Luber had once explained this.

“There must be differences in their combat levels. And the leader, Tamo, is probably pain-insensitive.”

Monga asked, “You mean… he can’t feel pain?”

“Yes. That’s likely a parameter built into him. Either he was born that way, or he suffered an injury that made him that way. But the important part is this—Tamo is similar to Ymir.”

Monga rested his chin on his hand.

“Hmm. The giant is neutral, after all. And Ymir is so overwhelmingly strong that it can’t even perceive resistance.”

“He has no sense of life. We can infer what that means through Ymir’s demons. Though they’ve been erased, they were described as incredibly greedy beings.”

“… A complete lack of desires?”

Luber nodded.

“Unless he experiences an immense impact, he can’t even feel that he’s alive. That’s why he wanders from battlefield to battlefield, constantly seeking it out.”

Like a living organism in an endless search for reproduction.

“He never misses. That’s why he can smell the battlefield perfectly. And that’s also…”

Why he was the leader.

A subordinate asked, “Boss, who exactly do you mean by ‘beautiful’?”

Tamo’s gaze landed on Miro.

“… Who knows.”

Then, he turned his eyes toward Shirone before stepping back and beginning to arm himself.

“Everyone, get out.”

“What?”

“I’ll handle things here. Go fight outside. The Merchant Alliance is probably on its way.”

There was a chance of that happening, but when the subordinate looked outside, the streets were still engulfed in darkness.

“How do you know? They might have given up.”

Tamo tapped his temple lightly.

“Instinct.”

Miro suddenly stopped using her transformation technique.

“Huh?”

At some point, the gunfire ceased, and the syndicate members were withdrawing from the building.

“Are they trying to surround us?”

But instead of encircling them, they left the premises entirely, even taking the forces stationed at the port with them.

A realization hit Miro like a bolt of lightning.

“Oh no! Dad!”

As soon as she figured out their plan and tried to turn back, a gunshot rang out from the fourth floor.

Shirone blocked it, but the real impact was aimed far beyond them.

Miro clenched her teeth.

“… Tamo.”

There he was, perched on the window ledge of the fourth floor, dual-wielding machine guns, smirking down at them.

“What’s with that look of surprise?”

His voice dripped with amusement.

“Aren’t you here to kill me?”

Miro shouted back, “You bastard! If you lay a finger on my dad or Aunt Kangnan, I’ll really kill you!”

Tamo chuckled.

“Hah… What a sweet, sweet threat.”

Tamo, sitting on the fourth-floor railing, leaned back and dropped over ten meters, landing smoothly on both feet.

Shirone and the others froze.

“You…”

For a normal human, the impact would have shattered their legs or at least sent them rolling across the ground.

“That’s right. Are you finally ready to take this seriously?”

He raised his machine gun.

“Let’s have some real fun.”

The moment he pulled the trigger, Miro’s mind split time into infinite fragments.

‘Hah! All he can do is shoot!’

The phantom hands of Avalokiteśvara flicked the bullets away, making dull thuds as they struck Tamo’s limbs.

“Huh?”

Despite the impact, he moved as if nothing had happened. That’s when Shirone and the others realized—

“He… doesn’t feel pain.”

“Puhahaha! Yeah, this is it! This is exactly what I’ve been looking for!”

Had he been just a fraction slower in dodging, his head would have been blown off. But that was precisely what proved he was alive.

“I’ll catch him.”

Shirone imagined grabbing Tamo’s right arm and activated his mental technique.

“… Huh?”

Tamo sensed his speed slowing, and when he turned his head, his right shoulder was completely dislocated.

His arm stretched unnaturally like rubber, yet his expression remained unchanged as he advanced.

“Urgh!”

Shirone’s mental technique shattered.

Rolling twice across the floor, Tamo crouched with a serious look before getting back up.

Grabbing his dislocated shoulder, he forcefully shoved it back into place with a grotesque crack.

“Hm.”

Rotating his shoulder a few times, he extended a hand, and the machine gun on the ground flew into his grip.

“What? How…?”

“Aha. So that’s how it is.”

Tamo’s lips curled into a wicked grin.

“This means I can go all out, right?”

As much as the members of the 1.5th layer had compromised, his combat program had upgraded accordingly.

“Let’s play some more.”

The machine gun roared, and before the bullets could even reach their target, Avalokiteśvara’s hands deflected them.

“Ow!”

Miro grimaced, staggering back a few steps.

The impact felt like being struck by a hammer.

“Watch out!”

Shirone pushed Miro aside as they both tumbled down, just as Tamo unleashed a storm of bullets.

“Kahahaha! This is incredible!”

The bullet trajectories twisted unnaturally, shooting in from every direction at once.

‘This…?!’

It was the same realm of mastery.

Meanwhile, a battle erupted one kilometer away from the Tamo Syndicate’s base.

“Fire! Don’t back down!”

The Merchant Alliance clashed with seventy syndicate members.

Bright flashes crisscrossed over a mere seventy meters of distance.

“Damn it! These bastards…!”

A gun may not distinguish its user, but experience made all the difference.

Kangnan shouted, “What do we do? The entry path is too narrow! Should we flank around?”

At that moment, Gaold threw himself forward.

‘I can do this.’

The memory of excruciating pain was burned into his mind, yet he didn’t hesitate.

‘Pain means nothing…!’

Gritting his teeth, he awakened once more, and Ultima’s eyes opened in the night sky.

“Aaaaaaah!”

Screaming, he fired Air Gun wildly, obliterating the enemy’s cover.

“What the hell kind of weapon is that?!”

“Guhhh…”

It hurts.

Even a single second of awakening had left Gaold curled up, his body going into shock.

“Gaold!”

As Kangnan tried to rush out, Sein grabbed her and issued an order to the Merchant Alliance.

“Cover him!”

Bullets rained down as Sein lunged to retrieve Gaold, clenching his teeth.

‘Damn it! Why can’t Kangnan and I…’

Why can’t we awaken?

“It’s because you don’t want to break the compromise,” Luber said.

“It’s not about courage. The real issue is—if it were so easy to break, then the 1.5th layer wouldn’t have existed like this in the first place.”

“But Gaold is—”

Luber interrupted Monga.

“Time changes things. But more importantly, some things only become stronger. Beliefs, obsessions, desires. Gaold is different from Shirone or Miro. He hasn’t understood this world—he just wants to break it.”

For what he desires.

“But that must be unbearable pain. No human would willingly go through that.”

“Of course. The idea that Gaold would choose to endure that pain again is completely irrational. But maybe we underestimated his desire.”

“You mean Miro?”

“You can’t define it that easily. A self-amplifying will… an unimaginable obsession to see it through. We can’t even fathom how immense Miro is in his heart.”

It was overwhelming.

“Isn’t this the terrifying depth of human obsession?”

“Gaold! Get out of there, now!”

Feeling the suppressive fire weakening, Sein grabbed Gaold’s collar.

“Grrrhhh…!”

Gaold’s body twitched violently, his blood vessels writhing unnaturally.

“Aaaaaah!”

Gaold shoved Sein away and charged toward the enemy’s gunfire.

“That idiot!”

Within his hazy consciousness, Gaold thought:

‘Who cares?’

The excruciating pain that was still vivid in his memory—he refused to acknowledge it.

‘Nothing happened.’

It doesn’t hurt.

‘Of course, it’ll hurt again…’

His body, already foreseeing its future agony, screamed, but Gaold stepped forward once more.

Into the abyss of pain.

‘I’ll save her.’

Sein’s desperate voice calling his name faded into the distance, replaced by the sight of dozens of gun barrels aimed at him.

Gaold grinned.

“Wow. Pain.”

Air Press.

The entire landscape trembled.

The Tamo Syndicate soldiers were crushed flat to the ground.

Back at the base, Miro grimaced as she struck Tamo with Avalokiteśvara’s twin palms.

‘He’s ridiculously tough.’

It was like hitting solid steel.

Tamo slid back several meters before swinging his machine gun onto his finger and standing upright.

“Nice, nice. So I really can go all out?”

“… He got stronger.”

Tamo’s tongue extended unnaturally long.

“I can’t even feel it. Did you really think this pathetic attack could kill me?”

“GRRRAAAAAAAH!”

Meanwhile, Gaold lay sprawled on the ground, his limbs twisting grotesquely as he wept tears of blood.

“Gaold! Get it together! Gaold!” Sein shouted, but one look at his face made it clear—he wasn’t hearing them.

There was only pain.

“Aaaaaaah! AAAAAAAAH!”

The countless eyes in the sky watched Gaold struggle.
