Chapter 556:

Even though they were both incarnations of Prajna, the depth they contained was entirely different, and just facing Miro made Shirone feel pressured.

Shirone, who had just reached the quintillion level, could not be on the same level as Miro, who had surpassed the unfathomable.

"Don't be nervous. You won't die."

The implication that anything was possible except dying made him even more tense.

'Time Explosion…'

Whether she knew his feelings or not, Miro glanced up at the solemn face of the Archangel.

No incarnation could manifest something beyond itself.

'The intimacy of light, the particles of God, Ataraxia, Valhalla Action…'

Countless experiences and techniques formed the Archangel, but Miro couldn't help but think of the influence of someone who no longer existed.

'McClain Goffin.'

If Miro could split a second to its extreme, Shirone would break the boundary of a second.

Incarnation techniques that encapsulated the particle and wave properties of light.

'If he reaches the level of Paramita…'

Even she couldn't imagine what the result of Time Explosion would be.

"I'm starting."

As Miro slowly twisted her body into an attack stance, Shirone also stepped back and prepared to receive it.

As the stance of the incarnation changed accordingly, the tension soared to its limit.

'Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara Thunder Strike.'

Before Miro's expression or stance could change, the incarnation moved.

In the space of a second, divided into thousands, the Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara's thousand strikes overlapped and struck Shirone's head.

'Wow…'

Seeing Avalokiteshvara's attack for the first time, Shirone trembled as if electrocuted.

Could the human mind fall this deep?

'I have to dodge!'

As the Archangel merged with his body, Shirone quickly twisted his body.

'It hit!'

As Miro widened her eyes with certainty, a thousand strikes slammed into the ground.

And Shirone had already moved to the right, following a gap in time that Miro couldn't perceive.

"Gasp! Gasp!"

Though she had controlled her strength, Miro, who had been serious, smiled approvingly at Shirone, who was breathing heavily.

"Pass. Congratulations, Shirone. This concludes your training."

As the word "concludes" hit his mind like a switch, Shirone's eyes rolled upward.

"Ah, thank you…"

Exhausted from the long ordeal and the shock of witnessing Avalokiteshvara's attack, he had no strength left to stand.

With a thud, Shirone collapsed and couldn't get up, and Miro, relieved, carried him back to the cabin.

It was around 4 a.m.

"Ugh."

Shirone opened his eyes, trying to gather his hazy thoughts as if he were drugged.

'Training!'

His body tensed from the daily repeated training, but recalling Miro's words at the end of his memory, his muscles relaxed.

"That's right, it's over."

His body felt heavier than usual.

It was probably his actual weight, which he hadn't felt until now.

"I did it, really…"

Shirone raised his arm and clenched his fist.

He had completed the seemingly impossible training and gained powerful incarnation techniques.

But the greatest achievement from this training was the experience of overcoming himself countless times.

'This experience will lead to more success.'

And so, he would continue to progress day by day.

"Where's Miro?"

Having sufficiently recovered, Shirone slowly came out to the kitchen.

Miro was nowhere to be seen, and only a pot of morning porridge was left.

"…"

It was only now that he realized there was a taste he couldn't get used to.

Creating a new bitter taste every time she cooked was nothing short of divine.

"I'm not hungry yet."

Muttering to himself to deny reality, Shirone went to find Miro.

Opening the door to the training hall under the midday sun, he found Miro meditating with a few candles lit.

"You're awake?"

"Ah, yes."

He worried she might ask if he had eaten the porridge, but fortunately, Miro just offered him a seat.

"Sit."

As he sat cross-legged and waited, her eyelids slowly opened.

"You did it, Shirone."

"Yes. Thanks to you, Miro. Thank you."

"You worked hard. The vacation is almost over. You'll descend the mountain tomorrow."

The thought of seeing his family filled Shirone's heart with joy.

"But as you know, nothing has changed. Many trials await you in your life as a human Shirone and as a mage."

Shirone recalled what Miro had said before the training began.

"I'm prepared."

"Good. I promised to tell you once the training was over. As a senior at the magic school, as someone who received the world's attention before you, I'll tell you what you need to do at school."

As Shirone took a deep breath and waited, Miro raised her index finger and began to explain the plan.

"First, when you return to school…"

West Family in Central Tormia

Nade, who had spent the entire vacation in the warehouse, fell asleep with an excited heart as the start of the new school term approached.

He could meet his friends.

Although Shirone's fate was still uncertain, just the fact that he could leave home made him feel like he was flying.

But perhaps happiness didn't suit him, as that night, an old memory resurfaced as a nightmare.

"Hahaha! This kid is interesting!"

"Aaah!"

A notorious gang of thieves, with hair cut like a rooster's comb, had tied Nade up tightly and were shocking him with electricity.

"Do it! Do it again!"

"Please, spare me! Please!"

The electric shocks he endured at a young age were not only painful but also terrifying beyond what his brain could comprehend.

"This kid is fun! Is this magic?"

"What kind of mage is a little kid? Kid, did you really make this?"

"Aaah!"

As another electric shock was applied, Nade's body, dreaming, convulsed violently.

He was drenched in cold sweat, clutching his chest in agony.

"Stop… stop…"

As bluish electricity emanated from Nade's body, the metal parts scattered in the warehouse began to tremble and then floated into the air.

"Die! Die!"

The gang persistently tormented Nade.

'Mom, Mom… why?'

Blood flowed from the burns etched all over his body.

It felt like his body was burning, but in the inescapable pain, he resented the world.

They were going to kill him.

The most sorrowful thing was knowing that no one would come to save him.

The people who had abandoned him and fled would never return.

"Alright! This time, maximum power!"

Nade could only watch as the clamps of the generator he had made were attached to his body.

The switch was turned up to the maximum, and an unbearable current flowed through the child's body.

"Aaah!"

Nade woke from the dream with a scream, sitting up abruptly.

At the same time, powerful electricity spread in all directions, and the warehouse exploded with a bang.

"Gasp! Gasp!"

Sitting on the burning bed, Nade glared ahead with fierce eyes.

Then, realizing the situation, he hurriedly threw off his burning clothes.

"Ugh!"

Even after more than ten years, scars from the burns still remained all over his body.

The only person who knew it wasn't an accident during an experiment, besides his family, was Iruki.

"What happened! What's going on!"

People who had heard the explosion from the mansion rushed over and stared at Nade in shock.

This was why he had stopped entering the mansion.

He was used to people looking at him with fear, but there was one gaze he could never get used to.

His mother stood among the crowd.

Nade thought his expression must be as twisted as her terrifyingly distorted face.

The day of the new term at Alpheas Magic School arrived.

After spending a day with his family, Shirone slowly set off for school in the Ogent family's carriage.

The graduation class had to pass through the Iron Gate separately from the opening ceremony, and Shirone had time since he had skipped the test.

As the advanced class noisily dispersed, the graduation class students began to pass through the Iron Gate one by one.

Collie, the head teacher of the graduation class, who had been guarding it for a long time, greeted them with a smile, welcoming them to hell.

Amy, Canis and Arin, Iruki, Nade, Dante, and others climbed the hill with a chilling gleam in their eyes.

'Haha, this is why I'm in the graduation class.'

Anyone who had completed the first half of the graduation class schedule realized the intensity of the competition was beyond imagination.

This led to intense training, and by the time the new term began, they exuded a fierce aura like a well-honed sword.

'No one has given up. Everyone did their best. But only ten of them will graduate.'

Even he got goosebumps, so how much more would the students feel?

The atmosphere was quiet despite the long-awaited reunion.

Iruki and Nade approached Amy to catch up, but it wasn't as lively as before.

'Shirone isn't here. I was looking forward to a rematch.'

Canis, who had returned from intense training during the vacation, was disappointed by this.

"Long time no see, Amy."

Canis raised his hand, and Amy greeted him calmly.

"Yes, long time no see. How have you been, Arin?"

As Arin shyly waved, Canis pretended not to know and looked around.

"By the way, where's Shirone?"

"Shirone? Why are you asking me?"

Canis blinked at Amy's irritated voice and then smiled as if he understood.

"Don't misunderstand. I just asked because you're close. I don't think you were rejected or anything."

Amy's eyes flared up.

"What nonsense are you talking about? What does it matter to me where Shirone is!"

"Alright, alright."

Canis, who usually prided himself on being Amy's nemesis, thought it best not to provoke her this time.

But the atmosphere was already like a funeral.

'That jerk. How could he just leave like that? I don't care anymore. He can die wherever he wants.'

As Amy left with a sulky face, Iruki and Nade looked at each other.

"What happened? Iruki, didn't your father say anything?"

"Not at all. I barely talked to him because of training. Even if I asked, he wouldn't tell me."

"Yeah… Is he not planning to return to school? Or is he even alive?"

Fermi walked between them and said.

"He's not someone who would die easily."

"What's with him? He acts like he knows something."

Nade grumbled at Fermi's back, but Iruki remained silent.

"Twenty-seven. It seems everyone is here."

As Collie said, Aider was still in the hospital, and Maya and Shirone had refused the evaluation, so they didn't need to attend.

"Now, a new competition begins today. I'll introduce the second half schedule, so follow me."

As the students started to follow Collie, Screamer, who was looking back, pointed towards the Iron Gate.

"Hey, there's one more person coming!"

"Huh? That, that is…"

The graduation class students' eyes widened in shock.

A blond boy, carrying his belongings in a cubic, was leisurely walking up the hill without even a bag.

"Shirone?"

Amy's heart began to pound as she realized it was real after hearing Nade's words.

"Hello? Long time no see."

No one spoke, but as if he had expected it, Shirone calmly looked around at his friends and said.
