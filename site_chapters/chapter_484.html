Chapter 484:

When Gaold emerged from the cave, Kangnan was nowhere to be seen, but footprints on the ground led toward the forest.

Kangnan, wandering through the thickest part of the forest with a severely scrunched expression, was clearly distressed. Suppressing the groans escaping through her tightly closed lips, she anxiously stomped her feet while scanning her surroundings.

Finally, unable to hold out any longer, she dashed into the bushes, yanked down her pants, and squatted down just in time. Having held it in for two entire days, her bladder felt as if it would burst.

The relief was mixed more with pain than pleasure, and only after the sharp sting of the pain subsided and the comfort of release washed over her did her expression regain some calm.

However, the fleeting sense of ease was quickly replaced by sharp vigilance as her eyes warily scanned her surroundings.

The world had taught her a harsh lesson since she left her homeland: never give anyone an opening from the start. Be more ruthless, like a beast—bite before doubt becomes certainty.

That was the way a descendant of the last remaining wolf tribe survived in this world.

"How long were you holding it in?"

Just as Kangnan was finishing up and preparing to stand, Gaold’s voice came from somewhere in the forest. A moment later, he emerged from the bushes, looking disheveled and worn.

Though the moonlight barely reached through the forest canopy, his eyes seemed to glow faintly on their own.

Kangnan’s nose crinkled, and a chill of hostility crept up her spine. Like other men, this one surely harbored some ulterior motive. The only difference was that he was a master so skilled that she hadn’t even sensed his presence.

"If you hold it in too long, it’s bad for your health. Did some men mistreat you?"

A cold smile curled on Kangnan’s lips. In her experience, men who asked such questions never truly cared for her.

"I’m a wolf. Treat me like a person, and I’ll kill you."

"Kangnan, you’re a woman."

Gaold took a step forward.

His silent, gliding movement made it seem as though he wasn’t even touching the ground, causing Kangnan to instinctively step back.

"And I’m a man."

Without thinking, she backed away until her back hit a tree trunk.

Before she realized it, Gaold was right in front of her.

"Anything can happen."

The moment his wild eyes grew cold and composed, Kangnan understood that staying like this would mean the end for her, and she launched an attack.

She swung a low kick, her most confident move, but when it landed on Gaold’s leg, it produced a muffled thud.

‘What?’

It felt like striking an airbag filled with compressed air—there was no impact at all.

"Ugh!"

Gaold placed a hand on her shoulder and pressed down.

Completely overpowered, she collapsed to the ground, and with a light shove from Gaold, she fell onto her back. He straddled her abdomen and pinned her shoulders with both hands.

"L-let me go! Or else…"

"Stop running from yourself."

Kangnan fell silent.

"Whether you’re a woman or a man, don’t waste time worrying about what’s already decided. What matters is what you can do."

When Gaold leaned in closer, Kangnan flinched.

It was fear that had been etched into her unwillingly.

"Now, tell me. At this moment, what can you do?"

Nothing. Her body was completely restrained, and Gaold’s abilities far surpassed anyone she had ever met.

‘I can’t win by running away.’

Though she wasn’t as strong as Gaold, Kangnan was smart, and his words quickly sank into her mind.

"Um, I… haven’t pulled up my pants yet."

Just as Gaold glanced down to check, Kangnan clenched her teeth and headbutted him.

Crack!

A loud sound rang out as Gaold fell backward.

"Aaahhh!"

Kangnan stood up, a triumphant smirk on her face.

"Ha! How’s that—"

But something was off about Gaold’s condition. Even though she had used all her strength, someone of his level shouldn’t be reacting this way.

"Argh! Aaaargh!"

Gaold clutched his head, rolling on the ground in agony.

Far more sensitive to pain than normal, he felt as though his brain was being stirred with a metal rod.

As his agonized screams continued, Kangnan realized the situation was serious and rushed to him.

"What’s wrong? What happened?"

"Arghhh! Aaaargh!"

"Say something! What hurts?"

No matter how much she shouted, her voice couldn’t reach Gaold. He would spend the entire night trapped in his agony, and this marked the beginning of a long bond between the two.

As they wandered the world together for training, Kangnan gradually learned more about Gaold. Though he wasn’t talkative, she pieced together what kind of life he had led from the occasional things he said.

He was training to meet someone—Adrias Miro. When Kangnan heard that the world was being protected by a single woman, even she couldn’t help but feel admiration.

"So, is Miro the strongest woman in the world?"

"There’s no such thing as being the best in combat. But officially, yes. Miro represents humanity, and no one can defeat her."

Kangnan casually tossed out her question and watched Gaold’s retreating back.

The strongest woman in the world.

Was this man in love with someone like that?

"What if I become the second strongest woman in the world?" she asked suddenly.

Gaold, who hadn’t expected the bold remark from the young girl who barely reached his waist, turned to her with a curious gaze.

"If you truly desire it, you might reach that goal. But in my opinion, it’s unlikely."

"Why? I’m not afraid of anything. I’ll do whatever it takes to get stronger."

Gaold acknowledged her tenacity—her determination was something he respected. She also possessed exceptional physical talent as a fighter. If she continued to grow, her body would undoubtedly become a formidable weapon.

But having wandered the world himself, Gaold knew better than to fill her head with false hopes.

"It’s not a matter of talent or effort."

"Then what kind of person is Miro?"

"She’s…"

Gaold gazed out toward the desert winds swirling beyond the horizon.

"Something beyond human. We just call her human because we have no better word to describe her."

Kangnan paused, taking in his words, then pouted and followed him with a determined step.

"Well, I’ll beat her someday anyway."

Their journey led them to the Akkad Desert in the Middle East, within the kingdom of Paras.

There, Gaold said he would train to overcome pain through extreme endurance. The desert was a land of death, and to survive, they needed more than strength—they needed resilience.

In the underground caves they called home, Gaold’s nightly screams echoed through the air, and Kangnan tirelessly scavenged for food day and night to sustain him through his suffering.

Deep within the desert, a madman and a wolf survived by clinging to each other’s warmth.

Even now, Kangnan struggles to define what their relationship was during those days. Family? Friends? Comrades? Lovers? Perhaps enemies.

All the emotions ordinary people experience with many others, they experienced solely with each other. It was the only way they could survive.

One day, Kangnan experienced the most horrific tragedy of her life.

“Ugh, I’m exhausted.”

Carrying a bundle of desert insects, she trudged into the depths of the cave, weary from her efforts.

Just then, Gaold’s piercing screams echoed through the cave. She was used to hearing his cries every day, but this time, something was different.

There was an unmistakable ominous tone mixed into the sound—enough to make her certain that something was wrong.

“Hey, old man!”

As her bundle fell apart, the insects scattered rapidly in all directions.

By the time Kangnan reached the end of the cave, the screams had stopped. Her eyes, trembling in fear, fell on Gaold lying motionless on the ground.

“Old man! What’s wrong? Say something!”

“Ughhh…”

Gaold’s eyes were fixed on the ceiling, and drool dripped uncontrollably from his mouth.

Knowing his condition, a rare form of self-perpetuating mutation, Kangnan immediately understood. After continuously pushing the limits of his pain threshold, his brain had finally reached its breaking point.

He was now trapped in a living hell—a state of perpetual torment with no end in sight.

“Please, old man! Wake up!”

For the first time in her life, Kangnan felt true fear.

The world consisted only of the two of them. A world without Gaold was unimaginable. Thus, the fourteen-year-old girl could do nothing but call out his name for days, screaming until her voice gave out, hoping he would come back.

Eventually, she collapsed into Gaold’s arms, unconscious from exhaustion. She remained in a deep sleep for three days before finally opening her eyes.

It felt as though she had been asleep for a thousand years.

But when she confirmed that Gaold showed no signs of waking, a grim determination replaced her fear.

Rustle.

Her keen hearing picked up the sound of an insect crawling across the ground.

After a brief moment of thought, she swiftly reached out, grabbed the insect’s body, and shoved it into her mouth. Chewing the thumb-sized insect, she pressed her lips against Gaold’s and carefully fed him the mashed remains.

She then took out a water flask and gently brought it to his lips. With a firm tone, as if making a solemn vow, she whispered:

“Wait for me, old man. I’ll save you. No matter what it takes, I’ll bring you back.”

Kangnan ventured into the city of Paras.

Healers, medicine, magic, information—whatever she needed to obtain, one thing was essential: money.

Would Gaold ever know?

Would he ever realize what kind of life Kangnan had to endure during that year while waiting for him to wake?

The hardships she faced, caring for an immobile adult who could do nothing for himself?

Yet Kangnan never once spoke of those days. She endured it all, waiting for the day Gaold would open his eyes again.

And when, as if by a miracle, Gaold finally woke, every bit of resentment and anger she had harbored vanished without a trace.

“Whew.”

The first thing Gaold saw when he regained consciousness was not the dark, oppressive cave where he had suffered in torment but the humble ceiling of a small inn.

A moment later, the door creaked open.

Returning with food and medicine, Kangnan dropped her bundle in shock upon seeing Gaold lying awake and blinking.

“Old man…”

Tossing the bundle aside, Kangnan threw herself into his arms, sobbing uncontrollably.

“Old man! Old man!”

At that time, Kangnan was fifteen years old.

Only a year had passed, but to Gaold, the girl who had grown physically and mentally in that short time felt unfamiliar.

“You saved me.”

Kangnan shook her head, wiping away her tears.

He was back. She was no longer alone.

“Don’t ever do that again. Promise me. Never leave me behind.”

Gaold gazed into Kangnan’s eyes.

There was no need to ask; he already knew.

He knew of the hellish time Kangnan had endured, fighting alone to protect him.

Gently, Gaold wrapped his arms around her.

“Let’s go back to Tormia. I won’t leave you behind again.”

Having emerged from hell, Gaold’s endurance and resolve were on a whole new level.

Now, it was time to begin in earnest.

They would return to Tormia, where Gaold would become the head of the Magic Association. It was the only way to reclaim Miro, and Kangnan would play a crucial role in his plans as his right hand.

Buried in Gaold’s embrace, Kangnan kept nodding.

Her tears wouldn’t stop as she recalled all the hardships she had endured, but she couldn’t let him see her cry.

“Old man, I’ve made up my mind about something,” Kangnan said, lifting her head as if suddenly remembering something.

Gaold smiled and asked, “Oh? What is it?”

With a bright, innocent smile, Kangnan declared:

“I’m going to become the second strongest woman in the world.”
