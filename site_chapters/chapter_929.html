Chapter 929: Even If It’s An Illusion (1)

The Elixir of Life, a spell that melts humans into giants using Black Elixir.

Its original purpose was to dismantle the Ultima System, which challenged the authority of the gods.

Due to the nature of the spell, its advancement depended more on quantity than the quality of experiments. Under Kariel's leadership, the number of Gaia people gradually decreased over a very long time.

"Move quickly! This is a sacred ritual! Cleanse the sins of the law and be reborn with new life!" The Maras urged, but the subjects of the Elixir of Life walked neither slowly nor quickly.

None of the Gaia people had offspring, and now they were down to their last 100.

After today, the Elixir of Life would proceed through a new humanity called Shinmin.

A truly historic day.

Ikael watched from afar as the Gaia people walked towards the bronze giant statue.

They showed no resistance or resentment.

She knew why.

The more they upheld Goffin's will, the clearer it became who was truly close to being a god.

'But it's futile.'

Ikael's halo emitted a dry light.

'I am the only one in this universe who knows about Anke Ra and Goffin's meeting.'

As long as she kept silent, no one would know, and it would be as if it never existed.

'Foolish humans, why don't you understand that this struggle is meaningless?'

Satiel shouted from the center of the square.

"Stop the Elixir of Life! You've done enough already! Let the lineage of Gaia continue!"

With the emergence of giants, fairies, and Shinmin, Heaven's hierarchy became more segmented, and the rank of archangels soared. But no one paid attention to her words.

It wasn't that they ignored her; they had heard it so many times that it automatically muted itself.

'Pathetic. Do you still believe in humans?' Ikael had never intervened during any Elixir of Life ritual until now.

She thought today would be no different.

'It's just 100 humans disappearing and five giants being born.'

The giants, having lost their identities, would return from Jotunheim with new laws bestowed upon them.

'This will erase Gaia's history forever.'

Forever.

The word "last" seemed to have some magical spell on it, making Ikael's nerves stand on end.

'Erased?'

All Gaia people who knew Goffin would vanish from Heaven.

The thought that she could never ask or hear about Goffin again struck her mind.

"Aren't you afraid?"

Before she knew it, Ikael was speaking to the subjects of the Elixir of Life.

"Archangel!"

The Maras were startled by something unprecedented in Heaven's history.

They hurriedly made way for her, but Ikael didn't even turn her head as she approached the Gaia people.

"Do you know what it means to become a giant? Your minds will be crushed and obliterated without a trace. Then new laws will be inscribed on your blank slates." It was a chilling statement, but the Gaia people showed no emotional change.

That fact made her even angrier.

"Why don't you say something? Aren't you supposed to be great, even if you're the last Gaia people? Aren't you really afraid? Or are you resentful? What are you..."

An old man interrupted Ikael's words.

"The weather is nice. On days like this, I should hang out my laundry."

The absurdity from someone about to die made Ikael's eyes narrow sharply.

"What do you mean?"

"What do I mean?"

The old man looked back at Ikael.

"I just answered meaningless chatter with meaningless chatter."

Understanding his point made Ikael's lips tremble slightly.

"Are you talking about feelings? Fine, I'll tell you. It's an illusion and an error. Goffin is a fraud. Do you want to know where he is and what he's doing now?"

The old man clicked his tongue.

"You're not in your right mind."

A murderous aura emanated from Ikael.

"What did you say? How dare a mere human insult an archangel?"

But the old man seemed too tired to argue and apologized as he hurried his steps.

"Enough. I'm sorry."

"Wait!"

Ikael grabbed his foot.

"This cannot be overlooked! You must be punished separately from the Elixir of Life!"

"Sigh."

The old man sighed deeply before speaking again.

"What do you want to hear from me?"

Ikael's eyes wavered as she tried to continue speaking but then closed her mouth.

"Are you curious about Goffin? Fine, I'll tell you. As you said, Goffin is a fraud. Are you satisfied now?" Ikael remained silent.

"Or should I say this? Goffin is not a fraud. He could never be such a person. Does that make you feel better?"

She couldn't respond either way.

"Stop playing word games and do your job properly. Get a grip, Archangel."

Human rudeness no longer registered in her mind.

"Goffin was to me..."

"No, no."

The old man shook his finger with a pitying expression.

"You're wrong. You should say 'to me, Goffin was.'"

"No."

Ikael's voice trembled.

"He didn't say anything. Even though he was going to leave like this, he didn't say a single word to me."

The old man looked at Ikael with deep eyes.

"Did you ever speak to him?"

"That..."

As Ikael hurriedly tried to speak, the old man continued first.

"Yes, explaining it would take forever. There must have been reasons. Because of complicated situations or fear of not getting the desired answer. If there were so many reasons that made it hard for him to speak..."

After pondering for a moment, the old man spoke again.

"Wouldn't Goffin have felt the same way?" The shock hit Ikael like her soul had been sucked out as she stood there with her mouth open in disbelief.

"Why do you think only you suffered? Why can't you understand that others exist too? The essence of the Ultima System is..."

The old man placed his palm on his chest.

"Others have hearts just like yours. So what is hard for you..."

Shaking his head with a sad smile, he continued,

"...is also hard for Goffin."

Ikael felt chills all over her body and couldn't stop her upper body from trembling violently.

"So we say we throw our hearts out there. The heart is thrown out. It may come back or nothing may return at all. So maybe it's an illusion as you said. But."

Following behind the procession, he added,

"It feels good inside, doesn't it?"

After chewing over his words for a long time, Ikael reached out her hand as if waking up from a trance,

"Oh, um..."

"Don't interfere."

The old man spoke firmly,

"Whatever Goffin did, it was my judgment and choice."

The Gaia people knew what was right.

"A fraud."

The old man threw back his head and laughed heartily in front of the bronze statue.

"Hahaha! What an incredible joke to hear before I go!"

He didn't stop laughing until the Black Elixir filled the glass sphere completely.

In Ikael's eyes, it was clear that he had chosen death without any doubt.

Just like how Ikael's heart waxed and waned countless times.

Curled up in her quarters, crying endlessly, Ikael's shoulders shook uncontrollably.

"Sob!"

Fear.

She imagined herself being crushed between the two colossal laws dividing this world.

"Oh God, what should I do?"

She had to throw her heart out there.

And that price might include losing everything, including herself.

‘It feels good inside, doesn't it?’

The old man's words were comforting, yet it seemed there was no better way to express it.

It was maddening.

After some time had passed, many archangels visited to report on resolutions from the council.

But Ikael, filled with the conflict of choices, couldn’t clearly remember.

"Huff!"

One day, as if someone had planted the thought in her, an impulsive conviction surged.

"Goffin..."

She muttered unconsciously and when she regained her senses, she was already flying towards the sanctuary in Aravot.

'Open the door!'

She flung open the door of the sanctuary in one motion, because the fear of her will breaking was scarier than disappearing.

"Ikael, what happened?"

Anke Ra opened his giant eyes and looked, but Ikael’s gaze was fixed on Goffin, floating in the air.

"Ahh."

Tears welled up.

"Answer me, Ikael. I did not permit you to enter here, did I?"

She feared that even a single word with Anke Ra might change the future she desired.

Ikael immediately flew over and severed the nerves connecting Goffin to Anke Ra.

Anke Ra’s eyes flared with anger.

"You…!"

An outcome not written in any law.

The first physical shock inflicted on an absolute being confounded even Ikael's mind.

"Ikael!"

"Huuk!"

Withstanding the piercing noise that shook the sanctuary, she fled Aravot at top speed.

'Farther! Even farther!'

There was no place in the universe hidden from Anke Ra’s eyes, but she sought a place that could offer peace of mind, not just physical distance.

Discovering a mountain range rising 6,000 meters high, she immediately descended into a secluded cave.

The celestial body dispelled the darkness, and as she laid Goffin on the ground, his focus gradually returned.

"Goffin!"

Ikael’s voice was fervent with everything she had risked to retrieve him, but Goffin struggled to regain his senses.

It was natural, given the long time he had been under Anke Ra’s influence.

"Where is this?"

As Goffin's voice finally leaked out, Ikael quickly supported him.

"Are you conscious? It’s me, Ikael. Do you recognize me?"

Goffin’s unfocused eyes groped Ikael’s face partially.

"Ikael?"

Ikael barely held back her tears.

"Yes."

"Why did you… save me…"

His words pierced her heart like thorns.

She wanted to ask.

Why hadn’t he told her the truth, what was the deal with Lucifer, and whether their kiss meant nothing.

"Goffin..."

Ikael gently stroked his cheek.

"Lo..."

Slowly, as she uttered the sound, tears of light streamed down her cheeks like a waterfall.

"I love you."

She threw her heart.

"It doesn’t matter what you thought. I love you. I want to be connected with you. It has to be you."

She might not come back.

Ikael bowed her head as if she had given up everything and closed her eyes.

"Hmm."

As she suddenly lifted her head, Goffin was faintly smiling.

"Out of a hundred points..."

Even amidst his incomplete senses, Goffin articulated clearly.

"You get a hundred."

Color returned to Ikael’s face, but she soon composed herself with a bittersweet smile.

"Is that your answer?"

Goffin slowly shook his head.

"No. That would be impolite."

Struggling, he lifted himself up and whispered into Ikael’s ear.

"I love you too."

Electricity sparked in Ikael’s eyes.

"I can’t say since when, but I too wanted to be connected with you. Truly, deeply."

Ikael, who had been holding back her emotions, finally burst into tears.

"Ah. Ahhh."

That hearts can be connected.

'How wonderful.' O Anke Ra, O God.

'I'm sorry.'

Even if the heart disrupts the law, even if all this might be an illusion.

'I am in love with this illusion...'

Ikael embraced Goffin tightly, her face blooming with a smile as pure as light.

'I like it so much.'
