Chapter 1241:

As she made her way to the annex, Constantine evangelized every person she passed.

“Believe in the god Kria.”

“Yes…”

Everyone she touched became People—expressionless, robotic, and immediately changed direction.

I am…

Constantine was not an Ooparts.

I am the agent of God.

That she, untouched by the pyramid’s wavelengths, had gained such power—perhaps that too was destiny.

Forgive me, O God Kria. I still had lingering doubts. I harbored suspicions even I didn’t recognize.

Tears streamed down her face.

She had believed in God despite all the world’s skepticism—but in the end, she had been human, too.

But now, everything had changed.

I saw it with my own eyes. I heard it with my own ears. The workings of God—His radiant proof.

If it exists, it can be believed. And that belief, to her, was absolute enough to merit the title of Pope.

From the divine perspective, her belief had become an unshakable law.

“Heh… a new age of God…”

Not a world built by humans, but one where they knelt before a transcendent being.

Just imagining it made her heart race.

I will cover the world in the name of God…

And just beneath that name, Constantine herself would stand tall.

The World Leadership Nation? Everyone in the Holy War was trying to earn legitimacy through Constantine’s approval.

“Nonsense.”

She had no interest in human nations.

“One hour, forty-nine minutes, twelve seconds… eleven… ten…”

At the sudden voice, she turned to see a wanderer emerging from around the corner.

Wearing tattered clothes, with unkempt hair hanging past his shoulders, he looked like a corpse.

“Kitra?”

When Constantine saw the shriveled snake coiled around his neck, she understood instinctively.

Why is the King of the Paras Kingdom here?

But what did it matter?

“One hour, forty-eight minutes, forty-two seconds… forty-one… forty…”

“Kitra.”

To create one more believer, Constantine extended her hand toward Kitra.

“Praise be to the god Kriya.”

“Thirty-six… thirty-five…”

The counting stopped.

“Kriya?”

When Kitra turned his head, Constantine felt her soul freeze with shock.

“Ah… ahhh…”

Overcome with awe, she trembled—but Kitra’s face remained indifferent.

“God has no name.”

With a thud, Constantine collapsed to her knees and prostrated herself—more pitifully than ever before.

It wasn’t the Rami Church’s formal rite. It was pure instinct.

“Please save me.”

…This was God.

The moment you encountered Him, all imagined shapes, doctrines, and creeds became meaningless.

Kitra asked,

“You haven’t lost your mind?”

“My heart belongs only to You. Use me as You wish.”

“Kill Maya.”

Maya…?

A moment’s doubt flickered in her, but she couldn’t muster the courage to look up and ask.

“I shall obey.”

Rising to her feet, Constantine left down the hallway. Kitra turned in the opposite direction.

Until the universe collapsed…

“One hour, forty-six minutes, fifty-five seconds… fifty-four… fifty-three…”

“Aaaaaaaah!”

Satiel’s spiritual form screamed.

No…

Even though the angels had guarded their minds amidst the cosmic shifts in law, nothing had changed.

God would circumvent infinite causes to enforce His desired outcome.

What God, what the whole cosmos wants…

Void.

By the photon system’s reckoning, in about an hour and a half, the universe would scatter into subatomic fragments.

After that: eternal nothingness—a state so devoid of form that even the concept of “nothing” could not exist.

It can’t end like this.

They had always fought with annihilation in mind, but the pressure now was of another magnitude.

It wasn’t that she feared death.

Even if I disappear…

As long as the universe existed, someone’s memory would keep it alive.

But I’d become… nothing.

Her hatred for Ikael, her grudge against Goffin—all would become passing delusions.

Ooooooooooo!

As overwhelming law surged in, the mental forms of the seven archangels began to dissolve into particles.

Ikael resisted with everything she had.

If we collapse here, it’s over.

If the original concepts of the Seven Archangels broke down, the universe would unravel instantly.

Uriel’s absence hurts. With all seven, we could at least slow down time…

But even that would only last so long.

“Ikael.”

A familiar voice came just then.

“Amy?”

“I’m here.”

Amy, fused into the sun as the incarnation of fire, connected with their mental forms, stunning Ikael.

Ho…

She was incomparably stronger than before.

“I’ll help.”

Thanks to Amy’s arrival, the archangels avoided collapse—but they only bought about an hour.

Ikael spoke plainly.

“I’m sorry. We can’t stop annihilation. Miss Amy, if you stay here, you’ll be in the same danger.”

She had hoped Amy would spend her last moments with Shirone.

“No. We’re going to survive.”

“What…?”

As the archangels’ minds focused on her, Amy smiled and said:

“Shirone is building something incredible.”

“Mika.”

—“Construction progress: 87.9546 percent.”

It was a magnificent sight.

Whatever it was, it was on a scale that no human could ever comprehend.

The Shirone Sphere had fully surrounded the sun, with only the fine assembly remaining.

Only 1 hour and 30 minutes left.

At the current speed, it would be completed in 3 hours—but by then, the universe would already be gone.

I need to more than double the speed.

Simultaneous Events were like ten fingers coordinating to play an instrument.

In other words, just because one finger disappears doesn’t mean the others can move faster.

But Material is different.

It was like the score suddenly changing, and having to perform in real time with razor-sharp focus.

In fact, after the Apocalypse, the speed of Sphere construction had increased dramatically.

That’s how I gained these remaining three hours, but this time it’s different. If the Simultaneous Events collapse, it’s all over.

Shirone was cautious.

If I can guarantee the final result, there’s no reason to hesitate. But the problem is...

Can he make it in time?

If he focused too much on Material and the Simultaneous Events failed, nothing would work.

But wasn’t this something he had no choice but to do?

“Faster.”

As Shirone made his decision and increased the pace of the work, the space where material was being formed began to glow red-hot.

“Grrrrhhh!”

His mind blurred in the trance-like unity of man and machine, and the foundation of the Simultaneous Events began to crack.

It’s shaking...

“Urgh!”

At the entrance of the Delta Main Hall, where Shirone was blocking the march of the People, he suddenly clutched his head.

“Yahweh!”

The southern warriors cried out in concern, but Shirone quickly raised his head again.

I won’t give up.

If the People's march entered Delta, Maya would inevitably die.

Maya...

Imagining her singing, Shirone opened his eyes wide.

“Hand of God.”

A radiant hand infused with the will to protect swept the marching crowd back, instantly creating a 20-meter gap.

“Yahweh, you are an error.”

Even as they were shoved and tumbled, the People repeated the same words without any change in expression.

Feeling a chill down his spine, Shirone focused his wavering mind into a single point.

Combining two focus points into one—

“I am not an error.”

It was as hard as performing an unfamiliar musical score at lightning speed.

“It’s the heart.”

According to Reina, when they were kids, Shirone had a gift for playing by ear.

Ooooooooooh!

With another clash between law and heart, the People’s march began to be pushed back once more.

“Shirone…”

From the window, Fermi spoke.

“He’s going to do it. No matter what happens, he’ll find the best solution and carry it out.”

Seriel smiled.

“He’s always been like that, even in school.”

“It’s not just about skill. It’s about cool-headed judgment, the courage to gamble, the awareness to assess the stakes, processing speed, a range of strategies, and the flexibility to switch when one path is blocked.”

Fermi turned around.

“There’s a lot packed into what we call ‘being good.’ Solving problems is the essence of human ability. And from what I know, he’s the most talented problem-solver among all mages.”

“You’re praising Shirone an awful lot today. That’s not like you. What’s really going on?”

Fermi turned his head back to the window.

“Maya dies in ten minutes.”

“What?!”

Seriel shot to her feet.

“And you’re telling me that now, you lunatic?! You’ve known all this time and just sat here?”

“It’s not something that can be stopped. God’s will enforces results. I’ve done everything I could.”

Fermi pointed to the front gate.

“Shirone will stop the People’s march. That’s why I can deceive it.”

“Deceive who?”

“God.”

Sometimes, when talking to someone with too vast a perspective, you think they must be insane.

“If Shirone succeeds, God will try to reroute. That’s why this strategy only works if Shirone pulls through. Even now, there are probably countless unknown causes converging. And the moment God reaches the result He wants…”

Fermi smacked his open palm with his fist.

“I’ll throw the counterpunch.”

“I don’t get it. I don’t even want to get it. Is Maya really going to die?”

“No idea.”

Imagining Maya’s song reaching its climax, Fermi spoke quietly.

“Not even God knows that. It’s up to Kayden.”

Even after Maya’s voice vanished, her echo seemed to linger in the air of the annex.

The only sounds breaking the silence were soft gasps from the audience here and there.

Panier lowered his baton.

Well done, Maya.

He gazed at Maya’s still-breathless figure, excitement yet to fade from her expression.

Now you’re the best in the world.

“Bravo!”

Someone stood and shouted, and soon a thunder of applause erupted from all sides. Only then did Maya’s expression relax.

It’s over.

In truth, she couldn’t hear the clapping, or the whistles, or the words of praise.

I can go home now.

Just knowing she had fulfilled her responsibility made her feel like she owned the world.

“You were amazing, Maya!”

When she turned at the voice from backstage, Kayden was applauding.

Yeah. You were always by my side.

Only after the performance ended did everything that had happened begin to feel real.

Hhhk…

Tears finally fell, and Maya bowed to the audience.

“Thank you.”

As the applause grew louder, her fellow performers took her hands and led her to the front.

“Go ahead, Maya. You’re the star.”

Watching her companions smile and cheer her on, Panier smiled as well.

She’ll never want to go through this again.

Fighting countless fears for this one fleeting moment of joy—it was too cruel.

Heh, what a joke.

No one ever leaves.

She’ll come back. Because the thrill she just felt is more addicting than a lifetime of happiness.

Meanwhile, Kayden, backstage, was clapping just as enthusiastically as the audience.

You did it. You really did it, Maya.

As he looked at her standing proudly among the world’s finest performers, his heart swelled.

“Hm?”

His clapping stopped, and his hand instinctively reached for the hilt of his sword.

No one noticed.

What’s that?

From the unlit side of the stage… a man was aiming a crossbow.
