Chapter 547:

Death Knight.

It wasn’t a common sight, but for Paknyeo, who had lived over ten thousand years, there were a few anecdotes that could explain what was happening to Rian.

In the history of war, there were knights who had fought even after transcending death.

Their lingering desire to live burned so strongly that even in death they couldn’t close their eyes—an unwavering will that could only be described as a kind of personal Law.

‘I’ve never seen anything like this.’

She had once seen a man continue swinging his sword for over three minutes after being decapitated.

But what Rian was doing was on a whole different level—an intensity far beyond mere willpower.

“Rian…”

Watching his desperate struggle, Shirone was overcome by a nostalgic emotion that no memory could ever fully replace.

‘That’s right… he was always like this…’

Rian was that kind of friend.

His charge, even at the cost of his life, was never to harm Shirone.

‘I don’t understand anything anymore. What am I supposed to believe?’

If, as Rian said, this world was a lie, then how was she supposed to explain the friendship in her memories?

“He won’t last much longer.”

Paknyeo reached her own conclusion.

“He’ll die before he gets here.”

It wasn’t humanly possible to survive that many gunshots. Shirone bit her lip and let go of the thought.

If the world could be restored by the sacrifice of one person…

Then this world must be the truth.

Because if not, then what were the love, the passion, the happiness, and the joy of all those who had lived here?

To Shirone, trapped as she was, compassion was the best choice she could make.

A thunderous shockwave shook the ground behind her, drawing Paknyeo’s eyes to the battlefield where Shura and Miro clashed.

“She’s really getting beaten up.”

The sight of Miro’s Kannon avatar hammering Shura like a mad storm was enough to make one’s knees buckle.

“Ugh, so annoying!”

But it was Miro—continuing the offensive—who was scowling.

Everywhere except the place where the two Panyas fought was now covered by Mucus. It had already climbed from the south, was flooding in from the east and west, and was steadily reaching the pyramid.

‘So time’s not on my side, huh?’

Miro grit her teeth as she glared at Shura.

Her Kannon avatar, expressed in three dimensions, gave the illusion of overseeing the entire world from the heavens.

But in Miro’s mind—her spiritual realm—it existed in four dimensions, a 360-degree field with no blind spots.

In that extreme scale, space and time were open concepts.

Thus, Kannon’s attacks could come from anywhere in space, and her rapid-fire strikes weren’t bound by time.

‘Kannon’s Crushing Field.’

When Miro swept her arm, thousands of overlapping Kannon palms came crashing down from the sky.

Shura, her snake-like eyes flashing, formed a seal and activated her ability.

Gestalt of Fusion – Divine Strangulation.

Around her, massive inverted crosses as tall as people twisted like rubber, fusing into a seamless sphere with no gaps.

Then Kannon’s barrage rained down with such force it bored holes into the ground.

“God, this is so irritating.”

As the crosses inlaid with crimson gems shattered, Shura emerged, licking her long tongue with a wicked smile.

“What, stuck in the dimensional wall too long? You’ve lost your edge, Miro.”

‘This wretched creature, just spewing nonsense…’

She hated to admit it, but this was Shura’s home turf.

Even Miro, formidable as she was, was like a lion diving into the ocean to fight a shark when going up against a Law-wielder of deception in the world of Apocalypse.

‘I need to destroy the pyramid.’

The real key was breaking the Law barrier surrounding it—but that wasn’t something Miro could do herself.

“I know what you're thinking.”

Shura, her snake avatar coiling around her, spoke.

“You plan to hold me down while someone else breaks the Law, right?”

Miro didn’t answer.

“But we’re not the only ones here. You think you can pull that off in time?”

She pointed, and Miro turned to see—the entire desert was completely consumed by Mucus.

“Damn it!”

Marsha cursed aloud.

It was already overwhelming just dodging the Guardians’ attacks, but now Mucus was erupting from the ground to trap her as well.

Though she’d managed to stab a few Guardians with her Depravity Dagger, the speed of Mucus’s expansion meant she didn’t have the luxury of waiting until they were exhausted.

“Don’t interfere with our mission.”

Exxer, with batlike powers, flew over Marsha and slashed with twin blades.

She rolled across the Mucus surface, only for it to spike up like spears, following her trajectory.

“What the hell am I supposed to do?!”

As Marsha gritted her teeth and rose to her feet, Targang, who had explosive powers, opened the crater in his fist.

“It’s over!”

BOOM!

Gas exploded, launching Marsha’s body through the air.

“Ngh!”

Just before she hit the ground, Mucus formed a wall and caught her mid-fall.

It surged up, binding her limbs and then rising like a tide to suspend her dozens of meters in the air.

“Grr! Let me go!”

Marsha thrashed wildly and turned her gaze across the pyramid.

There, Fermi was trapped in the exact same state and met her gaze with a shrug.

“Unbelievable…”

With everyone else immobilized, only Rian remained.

“That’s…!”

Marsha turned north—and her eyes trembled in shock.

‘I will not stop!’

Seeing the base of the pyramid swallowed in Mucus, Rian pushed his speed even further.

But the Mucus now followed like a tidal wave, lapping at his heels.

“Shoot! Even if he won’t die, shoot! Blast him to pieces!”

The enemy forces unleashed a full-scale attack.

“UAAAAAAH!”

Rian’s sword flashed like lightning, slicing full-bodied Undergrounders into chunks. (164 kills)

BOOM! BOOM! BOOM! BOOM!

Grenades exploded from all directions. Dozens of fragments embedded into Rian’s left palm as he shielded his face.

Bullets grazed the ground in zigzags—one struck and severed his ankle.

“Ghhh!”

Rian grimaced and slammed his right foot into the sand.

He launched forward as if pulled by a magnetic force, and the slaughter resumed.

“Gyaaah! A ghost!”

His face drenched in blood, Rian looked every bit the perfect Yaksha.

“Oh, God…”

High Priest Bebeto stood in disbelief, mouth agape.

An immortal body.

The body said to be granted only to those acknowledged by the Sun now stood before him, fully realized.

“Get ready, Shirone.”

As Mucus flowing from the east and west reached the summit, Paknyeo spoke.

But there was nothing Shirone needed to prepare.

All that was left was to savor the sensation of the Mucus wrapping around her legs… and accept the end of her life.

All the confusion and torment that had plagued her vanished the moment the Mucus touched her.

She would create a beautiful world.

‘Goodbye… everyone…’

From Rian’s eyes, now fixed on Shirone, tears of blood flowed.

‘I’m sorry, Shirone!’

The blood raining from his enemies mixed with his own tears as it ran down his face.

‘Even in the real world, you must have fought, sacrificing everything just like this!’

The vessels in his heart couldn’t handle the blood flow and began to rupture.

‘That’s why you ended up here!’

Smille’s hallucinated voice rang clearer than ever.

‘I couldn’t protect you!’

“Stop him! I said STOP HIM!”

As Rian neared the pyramid, the concentrated firepower surged to an all-time high.

A grenade exploded behind him, blasting shrapnel into his back and sending him airborne.

He barely landed, but his left ankle was severed, and he collapsed to one knee, unable to support himself.

“Now! Grenades! Grenades!”

“Let there be…”

Rian rose, eyes turned white with strain, and charged forward.

At the foot of the pyramid stood King Kong, pounding his massive chest like a balloon and roaring:

“Come! Let’s see who’s stronger!”

“Ghhk!”

It brought despair to Miro and joy to Shura.

“Ghhh…!”

—Smille. Smille.

‘Shirone! Shirone!’

And finally, just as the pyramid’s peak came into view, Rian pushed off with his right foot and leapt up the steps.

“SHIRONEEEEEE!”

Watching him ascend into the sky, Bebeto sobbed, eyes and nose pouring tears.

“Ahh… O God…”

The sun was wrapping itself around Rian.
