Chapter 656:

Cause does not exist—only the result.

That is Hexa.

“I see.”

Lupist rested his chin in his hand and sank into thought.

Though he wasn’t sure if Anchal had kept her promise to share information after investigating Istas with Shirone, he had already come across the report on Hexa through the Holy War.

If Shirone was Hexa, he would be the natural enemy of Ra Enemy—and there was no way the two hadn’t come into contact.

“And Shagall smelled that, didn’t he?”

Brooks didn’t understand.

“What in the world are you talking about? What’s this ‘incident,’ and who is Ra Enemy?”

Instead of answering, Lupist stood up from his seat.

“Alright. We’re going to meet Venezia now. Lead the way.”

“Wait a minute. Isn’t this too much? Even if you're the Association Chief, you can’t just demand someone else’s property.”

Radum was the kingdom’s greatest headache, and Venezia was the leader of its most powerful faction.

“Name your price—I’ll match it.”

Brooks let out a dry chuckle.

“You’re underestimating me. I’m a businessman. I know that any money you can actually see is pocket change.”

He had never even tried to put a price on Venezia’s worth.

“I’m in a hurry. If you’ve got something to say, say it now.”

Lupist, as always, was reasonable—but that meant no tricks would work on him.

“You said earlier that Radum would be shut down.”

Brooks spoke seriously.

“I did.”

“I want to be the one to manage it.”

“Hmph.”

“Once the terrorist organizations are wiped out, the kingdom will need a new department to oversee it anyway. If you designate Brooks Mercenary Brokerage as an official private partner, I’ll provide the mercenaries needed for managing the demi-humans.”

If he could monopolize Radum, it would open a playing field entirely different from any business he had done before.

Rian recalled how Shirone had bought up all the information cards at the Silver Ring branch.

“Anything visible is just pocket change.”

The same applied to swords.

More frightening than a visible blade was the vast potential it contained.

By that logic, Ra Enemy was a blade aimed at the world.

“Fine. Radum will be monopolized by Brooks Mercenary Brokerage.”

“Deal.”

Brooks drained the glass of liquor and headed for the door.

“Come on, I’ll show you the way.”

Venezia was being held in a punishment cell beneath the mansion.

The room was barely one square meter, with no torchlight even on the walls. She sat in a shabby wooden chair.

So this is the Tri-Brained woman.

When Shirone first heard of her, he had imagined a three-headed monster and had braced himself accordingly. But when he actually saw her, she looked perfectly human.

Only her irises were entirely blue with no whites, and her forehead bore a water-drop-shaped gemstone—not as decoration, but as a biological organ.

“You are Venezia, leader of Spectrum?”

At Lupist’s question, her lips curled into a slight smile.

“You must be Lupist.”

As far as he could recall, this was their first face-to-face meeting.

The two of them spoke at the same time.

“How do you know me?”

Lupist remained silent, and Venezia continued.

“Yes. It’s short-term precognition. I analyze the present based on the past and predict the future.”

That was how she’d unified numerous terrorist factions and protected the demi-humans from the kingdom.

Rian asked, “Even so, can you really predict what someone will say next?”

“That’s because you think like a human. What I analyze isn’t events, but time. If you can distinguish between past and present, you can distinguish between present and future as well.”

Lupist asked, “When did you know I was coming?”

“About a minute ago, by your standards.”

“Then you know why I came.”

“To understand Ra Enemy’s intent.”

“You met him?”

Venezia furrowed her brows.

“That’s a terribly vague question. If I were sure of that, I wouldn’t have allowed myself to be locked up, would I?”

Shirone stepped forward.

“Is it true Ra Enemy exists only as an event?”

Venezia looked blank.

“Who are you?”

“I’m Arian Shirone—”

“No. When did you get here?”

Lupist’s eyes sparkled.

“She didn’t foresee him?”

Shirone’s time explosion destroyed time itself, creating a completely open-ended future.

It made sense the Tri-Brain couldn’t perceive it.

“So you’re the reason I’m here.”

“No, I—”

Lupist interrupted.

“Explain it plainly. What is Ra doing?”

“No one in this world truly understands Ra’s intent.”

Venezia pointed to her head.

“Tri-Brains perceive the past, present, and future with three separate brains. It seems that Ra is engraving extraordinary experiences into countless people. These events truly happened—but they only exist in the past.”

“You mean they’re already over.”

“Yes. Humans who live within the flow of time can’t distinguish that. But my brain structure is different. In the brain that perceives my past, Ra Enemy remains vivid. But in the brain that perceives the present, I can retrieve no information. The biggest issue is that my future brain still performs predictions. Do you know what that means?”

Shirone said, “It means the present doesn’t exist.”

“Exactly. From a Tri-Brain’s perspective, if the past changes the future without going through the present, it means I have no choice. Nothing in this world can be changed by our will.”

Human will exists only in the present.

So if you rob humanity of the present, the world will move entirely according to Ra’s will.

And that was exactly why Yolga gave her life to persuade Miro.

“All I can do is imprison myself so I won’t be used by Ra. But my fellow comrades in Radum can’t do the same. They are acting on causality from incidents that did happen—but they believe they’re making their own decisions, when in fact they’re following Ra’s design.”

“What’s happening in Radum?”

“A regime overthrow.”

Venezia spoke with a sorrowful expression.

“The resistance will fight for freedom. Ra contacted many of our comrades—some were given hints, some were filled with rage, others with hope. When they’re all united under Ra’s will, the Kingdom of Tormia will be rebuilt as a nation of demi-humans.”

Shirone clenched his lips.

“How can this be…?”

Now the reason the Ivory Tower was searching for Ra Enemy, despite not lifting a finger even in the war with Heaven, became clear.

Ra is like air.

Air that has permeated every part of human life.

“Remember this. All who come into contact with Ra will eventually move according to his special design.”

“Now it makes sense.”

Lupist turned to Shirone.

“You’ve gotten information about Ra Enemy, haven’t you?”

“Yes. I stopped by the guild and bought information cards—”

“Then this will be easier. The Association also gathered a lot of intel. But none of it had anything in common.”

“It was the same for me. Some say he’s blind, others say he’s deaf.”

“Right. But that’s not the issue. Every informant we tracked has either died or gone missing. Twelve of them committed suicide.”

“They took their own lives?”

Shirone shook his head right after he said it.

“No, I guess we can’t say that.”

“Exactly. It’s Ra’s design. We don’t know how he led them to suicide, but he did influence them somehow—”

Lupist suddenly turned to Brooks, eyes sharp.

“Where’s Shagall right now?”

At the same time as the question, a scream erupted from the first floor of the mansion.

The brief storm of screams came to an abrupt halt. Realizing something had gone terribly wrong, Brooks dashed up the stairs with Lupist right behind.

“Shirone, stop Ra Enemy.”

Just as Shirone turned to go, Venezia spoke.

“He is the tragedy of humankind. Only a rare few can critique Ra’s incidents with the human brain.”

For anyone less than a Kar of considerable strength, it would have been impossible.

“But you are one who has escaped the fetters of causality. Find a way. If you don’t succeed, the world will spiral toward destruction.”

Shirone gave a short nod and went upstairs with Rian.

The incident had already ended, and the ground was strewn with countless corpses.

Brooks stomped the floor with his heel and spat.

“Damn it. He killed twenty-three people in under three seconds.”

It wasn’t an exaggeration—if the screams were all the last words they could leave behind. More importantly, these weren’t ordinary thugs; they were far stronger than the average bandit.

So this is Shagall’s true strength.

Rian examined the bodies, confirming the so-called fingerprints of a killer.

Every one of them had been stabbed, and blood was spurting from the wounds under abnormal pressure.

“The bleeding looks strange.”

“It’s a signature of the Rapid Blade.”

Lupist approached a body slumped against the wall.

As he slowly extended his hand toward the jetting blood, a translucent blade finally came into view.

“That’s his weapon?”

“It’s my first time seeing it too. Shagall’s weapon is a dagger made of dozens of layered thin sheaths, like you’re seeing now. Every time he stabs, a sheath peels off. That way, even if the muscles contract around the wound, the main blade pulls out clean, leaving the sheath behind. That makes follow-up attacks much easier. But that’s not even the scariest part.”

Lupist pointed to the tip of one of the thin blades.

“There’s a hole at the tip of each blade. So the moment it punctures, the difference in pressure causes extreme bleeding. No matter where he stabs, it’s a critical hit. Of course, with Schema abilities you can stop some of the bleeding, but…”

He tossed aside the sheath and gestured at the corpse.

“If he jams it in like this, there’s nothing to be done.”

Rian stared at the body, blood gushing from dozens of wounds.

There were even three sheaths embedded in the shape of a triangle near the heart.

Shagall of the Rapid Blade.

Next time they met, Rian knew he couldn’t rely on that sewer battle as any measure.

Shirone asked, “Why did he leave? Was this also Ra’s design?”

“Most likely. Shagall’s troublesome. If he were a typical psychopath, he’d be easier to catch. But he’s a developed killer—he wasn’t born this way. You don’t get labeled as one of the world’s top 100 most dangerous individuals just by being evil. You need a philosophy.”

“So you’re saying Shagall isn’t evil?”

“Of course he is.”

Lupist nodded without hesitation.

“He’s the worst kind of evil.”

“…”

“In any case, that settles it. Radum will be shut down.”

Brooks spoke up.

“But… with all my men wiped out, I can’t gather mercenaries immediately.”

And it wasn’t like they could officially assign personnel from the Magic Association.

“This is unofficial. That’s how Radum will be handled.”

Lupist, having reached a decision, turned to Shirone.

“Shirone, let’s make a deal with the Association. If you help us with this, I’ll owe you one.”

In terms of skill alone, Shirone was better suited than many official members.

“Alright. What do you need me to do?”

He was already planning to investigate Radum, so their interests aligned.

“Form a mercenary unit of about twenty and have it absorbed under Brooks. The Association will cover all costs.”

As Lupist moved toward the front door, Shirone quickly asked,

“What kind of people should I recruit?”

“You’re the leader.”

Lupist, stepping outside, closed the door behind him.

“You decide.”
