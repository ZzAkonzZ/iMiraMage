Chapter 819: The World They Live in (3)

Meanwhile, in the seats where the officials were, the head of the Bark family and his only daughter, Irene, were also present.

"Irene, go back. This is a serious matter. It's not something for someone your age to watch."

Irene was stubborn.

"I know everything now. And since it's the guard that will protect me, it's only natural that I watch."

The hunters gathered today were to be classified according to their skills and assigned appropriately.

However, it was well known that the most skilled ones were likely to become personal bodyguards for high-ranking officials.

'Didn't he come? Foolishly...'

The person Irene was looking for was Rian.

'This is a chance to make an impression on our family. I was going to specially select him if he came.'

She felt a surge of frustration.

"Huh?"

As she looked around in disappointment, she spotted a familiar face among the guards.

'There he is!'

Rian was watching the interview from the top of the colosseum.

"Mister, no, brother!"

As Irene waved her arms above her head, Xenia, who was next to Rian, shouted.

"You bastard!"

Without giving Rian a chance to stop her, she dashed down the stairs and jumped into the colosseum.

'I'll kill you!'

The image of her parents' bodies, torn to pieces by Nike's whip, was vivid in her mind.

'Because of you, because of you...!' She had also been dragged to an unknown laboratory and subjected to unspeakable experiments.

"Yaaah!"

As Xenia swung her Dark Blade at Nike, a whip cracked, and half her vision went dark.

"What...!"

Rian, who was coming down the stairs, shouted.

"Xenia! Step back!"

She might not have realized it, but her left eye had been ruptured by Nike's whip.

'Incredible reaction speed. He's strong.' The fact that it was daytime, dominated by the sun, made it even more terrifying.

"Rian!"

Benoff blocked his way.

"Did you find the spy?"

Rian glanced towards Xenia, but she was already obscured by the numerous hunters.

'Damn it!'

Changing direction, he ran with Benoff towards the officials' seats and said,

"There are two vampires. The secretary in the shadows and the old man in the VIP seat."

"Shovel."

If the head of Jive's intelligence department was a vampire, any strategy would be within his grasp.

"I'll arrest them. You save your comrade."

That was the agreed plan.

"You can't do it alone. They're Almas too."

Knowing Rian was right, Benoff could only close his mouth helplessly.

"Vampire! Kill him!"

In the colosseum, hunters had formed a circle around Nike.

'It's a chance. If I show my skills here...'

I could gain the king's recognition.

'Wrong, fools.' Xenia, regaining her senses after being struck by Nike, gritted her teeth.

The fact that they were driven by a sense of duty meant they were unqualified as vampire hunters.

'Nothing can comfort us.'

Those consumed by anger towards vampires kept a cool distance.

"Nike?"

An old man with a goatee approached Xenia, who was healing her eye with Blood Q.

"The priest who wields twin iron rods. They say he's the strongest Almas. We don't stand a chance, even if we all attack."

"So... are you going to run?"

"When I was seven, a vampire killed my mother."

The old man looked over sixty.

"Even at that young age, I thought it wouldn't take more than thirty minutes to kill a person."

The old man's bloodshot eyes filled with tears as he looked at Xenia.

"There's nowhere to run, is there?"

From this terrible hell of the mind.

"Boring."

As Nike swung his twin whips, the air hummed, and the hunters' bodies were torn to shreds.

"Now!"

Using the vanguard as a sacrifice, the hunters charged at Nike, filled with rage.

"No!"

Nike was strong.

"You're a Silver Born, right?"

The old man next to Xenia took out a drug device and gave a faint smile.

"Stay alive. Please."

Though he threw himself into the fray out of anger, someone had to survive to end the vampires.

"Mother!"

With a scream that seemed to tear his throat, the old man charged, eyes bulging.

"Don't expect mercy without pain."

The 3.8-meter-long whips moved as if alive, slashing at the hunters.

"Ugh!"

Bones shattered and flesh flew as if dozens of saws had struck at once.

"This can't be."

Xenia's shoulders trembled.

"Is there such a difference?"

Even with drug devices, firearms, and metal weapons, they couldn't even scratch Nike.

'Fighting in sunlight...'

With her hood off, Xenia felt like she was inhaling fire with every breath.

"Haah."

As Nike turned and swung his whip, hot steam billowed from his mouth.

"Oh, God."

Every time this hot air seeped into his lungs, he felt the illusion of being alive.

"Don't forgive me."

If you can, that is.

"Kill him! Kill the vampire!"

By now, the ground was covered with pieces of flesh.

"Your Majesty, you should leave."

King Meyer of Jive stood still, guarded by the royal guard 'Shinjang.'

There were too many eyes watching for him to flee because of a single vampire.

The high-ranking officials, also staying put, were internally anxious.

'Damn it. Let's just go inside. Let the hunters fight.'

The first to move would be seen as a coward, and their rivals would use it against them later.

Seeing the king's pale face, the officials sneered inwardly.

'Hmph, at least you have the Shinjang.'

They thought that as long as the royal guard was there, they wouldn't die, even if not as strong as the Wind Guards.

"The civilian casualties are severe. Send the third army to subdue them."

Perhaps realizing the officials' unease, Meyer pointed to the colosseum and gave the order.

"But Your Majesty, dispersing our forces..."

"They came to help the kingdom. Letting them be slaughtered would disgrace my name."

Unable to defy the king's order, the captain of the guard turned to the third army and gave the command.

"Capture the vampire."

As twenty armored soldiers landed in the colosseum, the ground shook with a thud.

"Prepare!"

With legs together, torsos slightly twisted, and hands on their sword hilts.

"Target!"

As they analyzed the battlefield with their honed drawing techniques, the third army captain shouted.

"Charge!"

In an instant, they disappeared from their positions, bodies leaning forward at the same angle.

"Attack!"

As the four-stage chain, designed to end any enemy in one strike, completed, twenty flashes formed a vertical net over Nike.

"Ugh!"

As the whips disappeared, the deafening sound of static electricity filled the air.

"What...!"

The officials watching from the colosseum widened their eyes, and even King Meyer gasped.

"Even the Shinjang can't do it?"

The twenty royal guards, their armor shredded like paper, fell to the ground in pieces of flesh and blood.

"Haah."

As the battle paused, Nike raised his head and exhaled white smoke.

Even a 1-centimeter movement of his hand made the whip's tip twitch like an eel.

"Your Majesty! You must flee!"

The captain of the guard felt ashamed but prioritized the king's safety.

"Ugh."

Reluctantly, Meyer stood up, and the officials, glancing around, slowly moved towards the exit.

Benoff shouted,

"Don't move! There's a vampire!"

Everyone froze as Shovel, the head of the intelligence department, suddenly stood up and shouted.

"Stop him!"

A secretary emerged from the shadows to block Benoff, but Rian's single strike decapitated him.

'They really are weak during the day.'

Nike was the abnormal one.

"Ah!"

At Irene's scream, Rian turned to see the head of the Bark family held by Shovel.

"Father! Let go of my father!"

"Shut up! You humans...!"

As Shovel dissolved into black smoke, he flew towards Irene, holding her father.

"Die."

Just before the impact, Rian's greatsword pierced Shovel's heart and pushed forward.

"Ugh!"

As the smoke was drawn to the sword, Shovel reverted to his human form.

"Hup!"

Running into the sunlight, Rian drove the greatsword, with Shovel impaled on it, into the ground.

"Argh!"

Unable to regenerate and exposed to the sun, Shovel's skin began to burn rapidly.

"Let go! Let me go!"

He clawed desperately with steel-like nails, but didn't get a scratch.

"Brother! Over here!"

As the officials' attention focused on Rian, who had quickly dealt with two Almas, Irene seized the moment to speak.

Rian looked back at the colosseum.

"Xenia."

Amidst the blood-teared hunters, he saw her fighting while inhaling Blood Q.

Even with her right ankle severed, she ran, stabbing the ground with her bone.

"Take your daughter and flee."

The head of the Bark family shook his head.

"No, you come with us. Protect my daughter. I'll reward you handsomely."

The person taking Rian was a powerful figure.

"Brother, you'll protect our family, right? Right?"

Rian looked at the officials, each with hopeful eyes fixed on him.

"From what?"

"What?"

Now he understood.

'What true strength is.' True strength is.

'Those fighting right now.' Anger.

"The only thing I must protect is one thing." My inner anger.

"I haven't lost anyone yet...

Because I wasn't born a god.

"I am always angry."

Only that anger can numb fear and drive humans to fight against greater foes.

"Ugh!"

As Rian clenched his teeth, the rage inside him surged, growing endlessly.

'Yes, this is it.'

What he had been searching for while crossing the desert was the source of the emotion that could topple Ymir.

'It doesn't matter what I become now.' He was no longer afraid of anything.

"Now, you."

A few strands of Rian's hair stood on end, and his shirt burst apart.

'This isn't a human body.'

The muscles Rian had thought perfect twisted like ropes, forming a bizarre shape.

"Haah..."

Reborn as the embodiment of rage, in the body of a Yacha, Rian twisted his upper body, crushing the ground.

'The law of the fist.'

He extended his fist towards Nike.

Divine Transcendence - Mind Strike Maha.

Shovel, impaled on , felt the law's tremor and screamed his last.

"Run!"

When Nike turned his head, what unfolded before his eyes was...

'What is this?'

The world's scenery crumpled towards a single point, rushing in.

'Can I... avoid it?'

The law of the fist approached faster than the most pessimistic prediction, and Nike's body exploded in a circular blast.

"Argh!"

Hundreds of bats scattered from the severed torso.

Bang! Bang bang! Bang! Bang!

The energy of Maha shattered the colosseum walls, creating an intangible tunnel as it moved away.
