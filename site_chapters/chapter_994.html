Chapter 994: Karma Chain (4)

The sailors' supplies were far from enough to last a day, but the people gained confidence.

In some situations, killing someone might be the only way to survive.

But at least they were not alone.

This atmosphere wouldn't last forever, but Shirone smiled contentedly.

'Even for a single moment.'

If everyone could care for each other's hearts, this world would never be hopeless.

Amidst the people dazed by the disaster, a warm breeze of human warmth circulated.

However, from a distance, perhaps from a higher place than them, Desika watched the situation with displeasure.

"Who is that guy?"

As the Minister of the Interior of a country, he might have heard the name Yahweh, but recognizing the face was another matter.

The knight hinted.

"There's always someone like that wherever you go. Those who act childishly, unaware of the impending tragedy. I'm curious to see what he'll do when there's truly no water to share."

Desika snorted.

"He'll probably beg and cry. He doesn't know how hard life is. It gets tough when someone like him gets involved."

If people started helping each other, naturally, the blame would fall on Desika, who monopolized the food.

"Deal with it."

"...Understood."

Desika's knight bowed with cold eyes and walked towards Shirone.

"Hey, you."

Shirone looked up.

"Me?"

"Yes, you. I'll let it slide this time, but from now on, this kind of behavior is forbidden."

"I understand that you didn't touch the minister's food."

"That's not the issue. If we start sharing supplies, we'll all end up dead. Resources are limited in any world. You should know that unless you're a fool."

For the first time, Shirone's eyes showed displeasure.

'This is it.'

What really made him angry.

"Sorry, but giving my share to someone else is my freedom. I can't follow your words."

The knight sighed deeply.

When he looked back at the minister, Desika nodded as if he understood.

"Kid."

The knight's sword, drawn with a splash, dug deep towards Shirone's neck.

"Do you know where you are right now?"

Unlike the calm Shirone, Poine's eyes were filled with formidable malice.

'Insignificant creature...'

Just as she was thinking of twisting the knight's neck 180 degrees, Shirone asked.

"Where are we?"

"In the middle of the sea. Food is scarce, and we don't know when a big wave will hit. Killing you is nothing. It might even be helpful."

The knight's expression turned cruel.

"You'll make good food."

The moment the blade touched Shirone's skin in a realm beyond human perception.

"I've decided."

Shirone slowly stood up.

Desika's expression twisted, but the knight, with his sword aimed, was frozen in place.

'What?'

His vision blurred, and it felt like his body was trapped in his brain, losing control.

Mind Technique - Imprisonment.

'Help me! Someone...!'

All his senses were still alive, but it felt like none of it was his own experience.

As people looked on in confusion, Shirone turned to Desika and spoke.

"The desire to live is a natural instinct of living beings. I don't want to deny that."

Desika remained silent.

"But some people share even the smallest things. It doesn't matter what you think..."

Shirone's eyes sharpened.

"Don't mock that heart. Even if it seems foolish, it's a great instinct for survival."

People swallowed nervously as they watched Shirone rebuke the Minister of the Interior.

"Hmm."

Desika rested his chin on his hand and asked.

"Who are you?" When Shirone didn't answer, he smirked and made a proposal.

"You seem to be a wizard. How about working under me? I'll give you half of my share."

A knight guarding the Minister of the Interior would be of high caliber even by global standards.

If he couldn't move such a skilled person, he had no choice but to use a conciliatory approach.

"Foolish."

Desika's eyebrow twitched, but Shirone turned away as if he didn't need to deal with him anymore.

"This is the middle of the sea." In the distance, the sea's back was rising.

"Save yourselves."

As soon as Shirone finished speaking, the sailors rang the emergency bell and moved busily.

"A big wave! Everyone, get inside!"

As people watched with anxious expressions, the captain came out and shouted at them.

"What are you doing! Get inside...!" Rumble!

The deafening sound made the captain stop and turn towards the sea.

"Damn it."

A wave of unprecedented height was about to swallow the ship.

"Kihehehe! Humans, humans!"

Amphibious demons were submerged in the tsunami, each holding their weapons high.

"Save yourselves."

Muttering that, Shirone wrapped himself in Miracle Stream and prepared to fight.

"Ugh!"

The knight, whose senses returned as if by magic, grabbed his head and looked at the tsunami.

'...We can't withstand this.'

It was beyond human capability.

"Minister! This way! We have to escape!"

Even in the end, the knight's dedication to the minister showed he had some qualifications as a knight...

"Messiah."

"Leave them."

Shirone knew what happened to those who lost their hearts in the prophesied end of the world.

"Aaaah!"

Desika and the knight turned pale as they saw the tsunami approaching 400 meters away.

At that moment, dozens of massive water columns shot up from the back of the ship.

"Roaaar!"

With a roar that shook the heavens and earth, the Water Dragon Kaios flew up to face the tsunami.

The Water Dragon's breath, boasting a 24-hour recharge time, exploded through dragon words.

As the astronomical amount of water twisted and struck the tsunami, the sea surged, lifting the ship hundreds of meters high.

"Aaaah!"

The deck was in chaos, and only Shirone and Poine watched the demons descending from the sky.

"Poine, I've decided."

At least he saw hope.

Miracle Stream condensed strongly in Shirone's hands, exploding into a brilliant light.

"Kieee!"

The demons engulfed in light turned to ashes and vanished, while the people felt a purifying emotion.

"Aaah..."

The feeling of falling from the sky was dizzying, but even that was soothed by a warm heart.

'Hand of God.'

A hand of light descended from the sky, supporting the ship and gently lowering it onto the water.

The waves were still rough, but as the Hand of God stirred around the ship, they quickly calmed.

"This, this can't be..."

Desika, the knight, and even those who had received help couldn't approach Shirone.

"Yahweh."

According to the news from across the sea, a blond boy was fighting for the world.

The Minister of the Interior knew the boy's identity and physical characteristics.

"This is unbelievable."

Blaming himself for not recognizing him sooner, the current situation was beyond belief.

'Yahweh is here? Why?'

The savior of the world couldn't possibly be on a refugee ship carrying only a few dozen people.

"Desika."

"Y-yes!"

Desika, understanding the situation, prostrated himself.

"When you reach the Kingdom of Dionas, set up a provisional government. Soon, people from the Kingdom of Tormia will come. Cooperate with them to help the flood victims as much as possible."

Desika wasn't a foolish politician who couldn't read the situation from Shirone's words.

"...Understood."

A massive tectonic shift was occurring, with the world being reorganized around Tormia.

"But the journey is still long. If Yahweh uses his power, we could reach land quickly..."

Desika flinched as he looked into Shirone's eyes.

"Ugh!"

No sweet words could work in the face of a mind-penetrating gaze.

"If it were just the people on this ship, we could reach land immediately."

Shirone looked back and said.

"We're all going together."

Following his gaze, people turned their heads and gaped in astonishment.

Countless ships filled the horizon, following the flying Kaios.

Underworld.

The Valley of Lamentation was originally a river of the hottest Hellfire in hell.

But after the spiritual realm was sealed, the lava dried up completely, leaving a cliff hundreds of meters deep.

Sliding down the rolling pebbles, Etella and Shagal reached the bottom of the valley.

'We need to find food.'

As living souls fallen into hell, they were hungry, but finding food was uncertain.

As Etella looked around and tried to move, a sharp pain struck her thigh.

"Ugh!"

Turning around with one leg bent, she saw Shagal stabbing his own thigh with a knife.

Glaring at his sneering face, Etella stood up again.

"Why do you keep doing this?"

"Do you really not know?"

Shagal pointed to the chain.

"You and I are trapped in this chain of hell. Pain, emotions, sensations, all shared through the chain."

As the chain connecting them shortened, Etella gritted her teeth.

"Ugh."

She tried to endure, but the uncontrollable force affected Shagal as well.

When their bodies were completely pressed together, Etella turned her head away in disgust.

"Understand? This is punishment. You're paying the price for daring to forgive me. You'll never escape me."

There was no rebuttal.

She had chosen this to remove Shagal from reality, so she had to take responsibility.

"Let's find a place to rest first. We don't know what demons might come out at night."

When Etella pushed Shagal away, the chain lengthened again, but its limit was about two meters.

When Shagal stabbed his own thigh with a quick-draw knife, Etella, who was ahead, collapsed.

Watching her back, which no longer responded, Shagal thought.

'And this is the punishment given to me.'

To torment Etella, he had to harm himself first.

'Hmm?'

After walking for a while, Shagal's superhuman senses detected a chilling presence and relayed it to his brain.

"Stop."

Etella, belatedly stopping, saw the illusion of Shagal's quick-draw knife flickering around him.

The eerie presence was still far away, emanating from the darkness at the end of the valley.

Etella's face turned pale.

'I can feel it. Overwhelming emotions, anger, sacrifice, pain.'

Don't come.

The woman's voice echoed loudly, as if it were resonating directly in their brains.

There was no way Shagal hadn't heard what Etella did, but he continued to advance slowly.

"Hmph, hell is just another place where people live, after all."

Since they were connected by the chain, Etella had no choice but to follow if Shagal moved.

The deeper they went, the stronger the emotions grew, until the fear became unbearable.

"What's that?"

The two stopped and looked up.

Hundreds of chains were embedded in the cliffs on both sides, binding a woman.

"D-don't come..."

It was Jinseong-eum, the princess of Jincheon.
