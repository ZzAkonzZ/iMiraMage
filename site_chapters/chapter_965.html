Chapter 965: Face to Face (4)

As 12 figures knelt around Shirone, murmurs could be heard from the allied forces.

"Who are they?"

Very few people knew their identities.

"Those guys..."

Zulu, feeling the fire in Miro's eyes, spoke.

"Calm down. The battle isn't over yet."

Even though the Hand of God had scattered the heavenly army across the world, they could regroup in an instant due to their abilities.

Among the 12 apostles, a tall, muscular black woman spoke.

"Sorry for being late. We were trying to find where the Messiah was going..."

It was the black dragon, Abiris.

She wore a gypsy-style skirt adorned with accessories imbued with magical meanings.

She called herself the Black King Wild Dragon, an arrogant personality always clashing with the light dragon, her elemental nemesis, but always respectful in front of the Messiah.

Phaeton, with a twisted smile uncharacteristic of a boy, said,

"Hmph, that's just slow. You need to stay sharp and observe."

Shirone's quantum transmission surpassed the speed of light barrier, so Phaeton could only track it belatedly.

The earth dragon, Gaitan, sternly rebuked,

"Don't be arrogant. Competition is always welcome, but teamwork is crucial to fulfill the Messiah's will."

He was a giant with dark skin and massively developed shoulder muscles, the strongest in terms of physical strength among the 12 apostles.

Shirone apologized,

"Sorry. If we didn't unfold the simultaneous events, there would have been disruptions. But now that we've bought time, we'll proceed as planned."

The 12 apostles bowed their heads.

"We follow the Messiah's will."

Miro, shaking off Kangnan's hand, approached.

"Isn't there something we need to resolve first? If it weren't for me, Shirone wouldn't have been able to stop the elemental bomb. Doesn't that mean your calculations were wrong?"

The fire dragon, Infercus, pouted.

"So what? We let you go, didn't we?"

Miro, instead, raised her head excitedly.

"Hohoho!"

She stopped laughing abruptly and glared fiercely.

"You had fun tying me up and beating me, didn't you? Want to try being on the receiving end this time?"

To prove her sincerity, the incarnation of the Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara appeared with a mad smile.

The energy dragon, Argan, raised both hands to stop Miro.

"Everything worked out well, so let's not fight. Everyone has their circumstances, right?"

He was a tall man with a white face like a clown and black eye makeup.

His lips were slit long with black rouge, giving a creepy impression.

"Shut up. Your hit hurt the most."

"No, that was... just part of the job."

As Argan, who was bad at arguments, scratched his head, the white dragon, Asraiker, stood up.

"Sorry."

The hall fell silent for a moment.

"We apologize for the previous incident. Now that we follow the Messiah, we will show respect according to the Messiah's relationships."

People around Miro were more stunned than Miro herself.

'Beautiful.'

No, it was a charm that couldn't be described with such words, enveloping her entire body.

'It's not charm. It's magic.'

Even the cold-hearted Lupist felt dizzy, and even Tess and Amy, who were women, couldn't take their eyes off her.

They were focused on Miro at the moment, but if they made eye contact, anyone would collapse with weak knees.

"?Hong."

Miro had already experienced the extreme charm of the white dragon.

Although she didn't have the ability to dismantle magic like Shirone, the Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara, with no blind spots, endured by blocking all senses.

'But in this state, delicate combat is impossible. Can this be considered an attack?'

Anyway, if the 12 apostles followed Shirone, it aligned with Miro's purpose.

"Alright, let's get to the point. Shirone, there must be a reason you scattered the heavenly army, right?"

"Yes. For now, that was my limit."

Shirone confessed honestly.

"The Hand of God is an aggressive use of the Idea Link. It forms the user's will into the shape of a hand."

As everyone looked up at the sky, a giant hand of light made an OK sign with its index finger and thumb.

Miro, lowering her head, asked,

"What does that mean? Of course, it takes time to prepare, but there's no special defense method."

They could only fight back.

"No."

Shirone shook his head.

"From now on, we will defeat the heavenly army individually."

"Huh?"

Everyone blinked.

A carriage that had left the sanctuary was speeding towards Bashuka.

Iruki sat in the upper seat, with Dorothy on the left and Aromi on the right.

And on the opposite bed...

"Quantum transmission?"

Shirone was there.

"Yes. It's Hexa's ability."

As Shirone raised his index finger, the hexagon spun rapidly.

"It's the first ability I created."

In fact, Goffin could have done it too, but it wasn't necessary.

"In the era of Gaia, everyone was strong. Not just in terms of force, but... how should I put it? Everyone was like Goffin."

Since it took time to reach Bashuka, Iruki could listen calmly.

"After receiving Omega from Karator, the first thought I had was that I couldn't do that."

"Current humanity isn't that strong."

"Unfortunately. So I thought about the most efficient way to use the Ultima System."

"Is that the ability you explained earlier?"

Iruki couldn't believe it.

"Yes. Quantum transmission is... Oh, the 12 apostles just arrived. Anyway, quantum transmission is..."

Dorothy raised her hand.

"Wait. I don't understand. So, there's you in Bashuka, and there's you here, and you exchange information?"

Shirone shook his head.

"No, I'm not split into two. There's only one of me in this world."

"But you are now..."

"Yes, I'm in Bashuka too. Hmm, how should I explain this easily? Right."

Shirone snapped his fingers.

"In fact, the city of Bashuka doesn't exist. It doesn't exist in this world."

"Hmm."

As Iruki nodded as if realizing something, Shirone pointed to the window.

"Dorothy, what do you think is outside now?"

The window was covered with curtains.

"Well, mountains or forests? Animals or something..."

Dorothy, who had stopped talking, blinked.

"There's nothing?"

"Yes. At least to us, the observers."

As Shirone pulled back the curtain, a vast meadow was revealed.

"And the moment we observe like this, the world is born."

Aromi, who was an outsider, asked an innocent question.

"What if you pull back the curtain incredibly fast? So fast that you can't even see it. Would you confirm that there's nothing?"

"Haha."

Shirone laughed but didn't feel bad.

It was probably because it felt like a good question.

"That's a common imagination. But if you expand the realm of thought, everything in this world can never surpass the background signal. It's trapped in a solid wall."

"What's that wall?"

"Time."

"Huh?..."

"It's called the speed of light barrier. The basic signal that makes up this world is photons. It's not because light is fast, but because it's fundamental, it can't be surpassed. Therefore, the speed of light is constant, and no matter how fast you pull back the curtain, you can't see the background."

Aromi nodded repeatedly.

"Oh, I see. No matter how fast an arrow is, if time stops, it can't move forward. Is this example correct?"

"Exactly."

A smile spread across Aromi's face.

"Haha, now I understand. In the end, everyone trapped in the photon world can't surpass the speed of light."

"Yes."

Shirone nodded and added,

"But I can transcend it."

As Aromi's mouth hung open, Iruki chuckled.

"Is that quantum transmission?"

"Yes. The mind can transcend the speed of light. Imagining the end of the universe is incomparably faster than light."

Dorothy asked,

"But that's just imagining, right?"

"Even so, nothing changes. In reality, Bashuka doesn't exist even now."

"That's our perspective. So... you're the only one here, and we can't know if you're in Bashuka until we observe it. I get that. The problem is you. It doesn't seem logical to exist in different spaces simultaneously."

"That's because humans have one body for one mind. In fact, creatures on other planets have one brain that moves millions of creatures. Their logic would be different from humans."

Shirone spread his palm and raised his index and middle fingers vertically with the other hand.

"These two fingers are in different spaces, but I can instantly know which finger moves. Of course, it's not an exact example. This is transmitted through nerves, but I'm connected by quantum signals."

"The mind."

Iruki said.

"An integrated mental system."

"Yes. I found a way to maximize the Ultima System's abilities. Of course, since I'm one person, the system doesn't get stronger. But I can defeat enemies individually. And most importantly..."

Shirone's eyes sharpened.

"This method is the fastest way to integrate humanity's mind."

Iruki realized.

'This guy really...'

He intended to revive the Ultima System.

"Does the range matter?"

"No. There's no restriction on space. That's the mind. But."

Shirone raised his index and middle fingers on his palm again.

"In the end, it's the mind. If I observe my existence in both spaces, one will disappear."

As the fingers touched, Shirone quickly withdrew his middle finger outside his palm.

"I see."

Iruki's mind was racing.

"Even so, it's strategically sufficient. So, if you exist in areas where you can't observe each other, the phenomenon you described is possible, right?"

"Yes."

Shirone, who was in two spaces, said the same thing.

"Simultaneous events."

Dante, Amy, Neid, and even Miro had blank expressions.

"What nonsense is this?" Rian had already given up trying to understand, while the poison dragon, Poine, burst into laughter.

"Haha! Have you been deceived all this time, young lady? Our Messiah's abilities move the heavens."

Miro glared at her, but she was right.

'Hmm, so that's why they forcibly disbanded. The basis of the idea is the Ultima System. It's definitely a good method.'

Miro, having finished thinking, asked,

"What are the criteria for transmission? If space doesn't exist, you can't set coordinates."

"That's right. The observer's quantum signal is necessary. In other words, you can't go to places without people."

"Well, that's..."

With Shirone's mobility, it was a barrier that could be easily overcome.

"Then from now on..."

After explaining the simultaneous events, Shirone looked around at everyone and said,

"I'll form teams to fight with me."

No one stepped forward.

Because Shirone would be in every team.
