Chapter 932: Even If It’s An Illusion (4)

Sadia, Central Thormia

In the city of Sadia, located in the central plains of Tormia, lies the estate of the Neid family. Once on the brink of collapse, the family business, Neid Munitions, founded by Neid, experienced explosive growth due to the war, establishing itself as a dominant force in the central region.

Despite this reputation being shaped by envious gossipers, the company initially focused on munitions but expanded its business scope significantly by handling the core of the Elemental Bomb Project. Now, it stands as a global giant, fittingly called a conglomerate.

Ranked third among global corporations with assets totaling 780 trillion gold, this is the current status of Neid Group.

"Chairman, we will arrive at the main house in 10 minutes," said the coachman, opening the window of the luxurious and secure carriage.

"Got it."

Neid, the young chairman of Neid Group, pulled up his coat collar and buried his neck into his shoulders.

Liz, sitting on the opposite bed and quickly reviewing a stack of documents, spoke up.

"The company's rank has risen by one. Congratulations."

"It's not my achievement. The war, blood, life, and demons created this money feast," Neid added. "It's fortunate to be Iruki's friend."

Liz knew what Neid wanted to say but remained silent. It was a battle of patience.

"Yes. Supplying to the Holy War was a growth driver, but Iruki is also a military commander overseeing the war. Your weapons were chosen because they were the best. And... you brought Neid Group to where it is now with your skills."

Normally, such praise from his beloved would make him proud, but this time he saw through it.

"I know what you want to say." Neid finally gave in. "But Liz, I have to go."

He had to ascend to the high skies above Bashka and drop the Elemental Bomb at precisely the right time.

"Sigh."

Suppressing her near-maddening anger, Liz sighed deeply.

"I just can't understand. You've led Neid Group well so far. Many people have survived thanks to your weapons. Why do you want to do everything? Are you some kind of hero?"

"It would be fortunate if I were a hero." Liz clutched her chest in frustration. "Think carefully. You'll die. Not even in battle—it's suicide."

"I'll escape as soon as I finish the mission. Theoretically, I can survive."

"What if something goes wrong? The equipment isn't 100% reliable yet. What if something worse happens than theory predicts?" When Neid chose silence over mentioning death, Liz's face turned sorrowful.

"Don't do it. I'll give you anything you want if you don't do this. Let's give away all our money. We'll find someone else to do it—there must be someone."

Neid's eyes turned cold.

"Liz, you can't buy a person's life with money."

"Then what about your life?"

Liz's voice echoed through the perfectly soundproof carriage interior.

"And what about me? Your family? The thousands of employees who rely on you? Their families? Why are you so selfish?"

Liz slammed her hand on the documents on the bed and approached Neid with determined steps.

"No! I can't let you go! Find someone else! If you can't find them, I will!"

"You know it's impossible. The aircraft is perfectly personalized. No one else can pilot it."

"No, it's possible. Do you think you're the only mage who can handle electricity?"

"Liz."

Neid frowned at her accurate point, but Liz didn't back down.

"I know why you're doing this. Shirone, Iruki, and you, the troublesome trio from Alpheas Magic School. Do you think you're indebted to your friends? Do you think you're living comfortably while they struggle? Or do you want to shine because they shine so brightly?"

Neid remained silent.

"Get a grip, you fool. You've become great in your own way. You don't need to fight like Shirone or control wars like Iruki, you play a significant role."

"I don't care about shining." He was sincere.

"We've known since school what we had to do. I was never meant for a shining role—that's Shirone's part. Iruki knows that too."

"Then why try to shine now? Leave it to Shirone or Iruki."

"Shine..."

Neid smiled faintly.

"When you graduated and I became president of the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Society, our only members were Iruki and Shirone."

Those were happy times.

"The three of us once held a presentation on paranormal psychics..."

"I know. I've heard it over a hundred times."

Neid reminisced about their school days without embarrassment.

"That night after everyone fell asleep, we lay in Central Park looking at stars—the North Star."

Neid burst into laughter.

"We were so childish then, talking about eternal friendship and graduation... But at that moment, it felt like we owned the world."

Rubbing his lips with his hand as if cutting off nostalgia, Neid spoke with a sad smile.

"Why did we feel that way? Because we successfully finished our presentation? Because we saw hope for our future?"

Liz listened silently.

"No. It was because it was us three together."

Recalling that thrill made Neid's lips tremble.

"I don't know why but being together felt like a miracle then—and now one of those friends is struggling."

As a participant in the Elemental Bomb Project herself, Liz knew how heavy Iruki's burden was.

"If we leave this task to someone else, it will only increase Iruki's burden further, why me? Because I want to, but really because..."

Neid's eyes reddened.

"Because Iruki wants me to."

Liz couldn't say anything more.

"He's smart but foolish—trying to carry everything alone, never asking for help, but I know, that's my role, to lighten his burden, I'm the only one who can do that now."

If only he had asked for help...

"But ignore him? Pretend not to see his pain because he didn't ask for help? I'd rather die, Liz, I can't do that, I love you so much, and I'd give my life for you, but..."

Neid had already made up his mind.

"I have to go."

Liz's shoulders slumped in defeat.

"Sigh."

Returning weakly to sit on the bed, she brushed back her hair and swallowed her tears.

The window opened—and the coachman spoke,

"Chairman—we've arrived at the main house."

Having bought all nearby mansions and renovated them, it took some time even after arriving at their destination.

Calming herself, Liz turned and puckered her lips,

"Someone even got married because of this mission."

Neid finally regained his smile,

"Haha, that couldn't be helped, you were included among survivors too."

"Who's talking about that now?"

"I love you Liz."

Though it felt like receiving thanks after prodding, it didn't feel bad hearing it,

"Hmph."

As they arrived at their main mansion, Neid's parents Volum and Terea came out to greet them,

"My son."

The terrifying mother who always scowled at Neid was gone,

Endless jealousy and envy seemed trivial before their immense wealth,

Neid tried not thinking about it,

"Yes, I’m back.”

Whether it was money that changed her or she rediscovered her true nature in a life of abundance, Neid didn't want to delve deeply into it.

Volum extended his hand for a handshake.

"It's been a while. You've been busy?"

"Yes. I need to head up immediately due to urgent work. But Liz will stay here for a few days."

Survivors of this project had to maintain confidentiality, and Liz was an excellent actress.

"Hello, Mother. I'm sorry I haven't visited sooner. Let's go to many nice places while I'm here."

"Hoho, of course. I've reserved the best restaurant. Let's have a cozy meal together."

It was fortunate that Terea liked Liz.

"Well then, I'll be going. Liz, take care."

After chatting for about 10 minutes, Neid bid farewell and turned towards the carriage.

"Mother, wait a moment."

Liz hurriedly followed Neid and stopped him.

"Wait. Your coat collar is wrinkled."

Knowing it was an excuse, Neid let her do as she wished.

"Sorry."

Though she choked up, Liz soon smiled.

"Promise me one thing."

"Sure."

Brushing off the wrinkles from his coat, she firmly gripped his collar with both hands and said,

"If you're going to do it, finish it perfectly. Shine brilliantly. Don't hesitate like you did in school. Got it?"

Surprised by her unexpected words, Neid nodded firmly.

"Thank you."

For letting me leave without regrets.

After sharing a brief kiss, Neid walked towards the carriage with flames of determination in his eyes.

'I'm coming, Iruki.'

The ballistic name for the Elemental Bomb was Lucky Boy.

Goffin and Ikael were spending time hidden in a remote area, utilizing Hexa's powers.

"Why isn't it working?"

While Goffin lay sprawled out on a mat, murmuring to himself, Ikael returned with some fruit.

"Are you still worrying about that?"

From all around, her retainers, Three, Two, and One horned Mara, rushed over and knelt before her.

"I will carry it!"

Ikael stared blankly at the six hands offered to her.

Ashur of Signal, Lethe of Augmentation, and Rasmus of Lightning.

These were Ikael's three guardian spirits, rushing to carry the basket, which was somewhat pitiable.

'I am indeed fortunate.'

Even as the direct servants of the angel, they couldn't ignore Anke Ra's authority, but they had chosen Ikael.

"It's okay. Leaving the White Hall has left me without much to do. This is all I can do."

Ashur bowed his head.

"Just your existence is a blessing, Ikael. Your beauty couldn't be recreated by any chance."

"Oh, Ashur, you really know how to flatter."

Ikael covered her mouth and laughed, while Goffin rolled over and scratched his belly.

'That cunning guy.'

Though there might be a hint of jealousy in his remark, it was still at a cute level.

Lethe extended both hands.

"Please give it to me. I will increase it to a hundred million so that Ikael will never have to do such rough work again."

Lethe could increase the number or quantity of specific objects.

"It's okay. Goffin will eat it anyway. Honey, fruit for lunch today."

Goffin, sitting up, said with droopy eyes,

"Fruit for lunch again today."

"Want something else?"

Just as Ikael's celestial body seemed to expand, Goffin bit into an apple.

'It's my fate.'

But his stomach was still cold.

"I understand you don't know the joy of eating. But tell me, what energy will you get from this?"

"I manage just fine, somehow."

Countless years had passed, but still, Goffin and Ikael had no children.

'The problem is different. Very different.'

It was fortunate that there was no morphological disharmony, but the mechanisms of birth were different.

"Are you that worried? About having descendants. Of course, I understand what that means."

Everyone can become a god.

'Ikael is a spiritual being. The physical has its limits. It requires using signals.'

They had twisted the laws with Hexa, and Ikael had brought many changes, but to no effect.

'The connection between Ikael and me is through the heart. But no matter how much we use Hexa to merge, no change occurs. It's not that I don't desperately want it.'

Watching the Hexa spin on his finger, Goffin clenched his fist.

Clang! And the Hexa shattered.

"Heart?"

Ikael, who was preparing the fruit, turned her head.

"Honey, what's wrong?"

"It's not the body."

Merely connecting their hearts into one signal was not enough.

"Let's go."

Goffin grabbed Ikael's wrist, who was kneeling and looking blankly, and pulled her along.

"What's this all of a sudden?"

"It's okay." Goffin turned to look at Ikael, who was reluctantly following, and smiled meaningfully.

"You'll like it too." The most noble love. Agape.
