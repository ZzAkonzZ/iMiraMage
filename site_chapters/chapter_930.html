Chapter 930: Even If It’s An Illusion (2)

Sein's team, tasked with assassinating Havitz, raced across the barren plains where the army of Hell was advancing.

'Timing will be tricky.'

Light rings appeared in Sein's eyes, and two massive halos formed in the sky.

'Iron Wheel Eye, Sun and Moon Halos.'

As the two halos merged into one, the world began to ripple.

As the scenery ahead rapidly approached as if space itself was being pushed, Eden took a deep breath.

'This is the Iron Wheel Eye.'

In the dark sky, only the ring of light spun at a terrifying speed.

'I can do it. This will stop Havitz.'

The ability to control all physical and mental factors within the radius of the halo.

Havitz might not die even if a rockslide fell on him, but...

'What if I adjust gravity?'

The future of immortality would be shattered.

Of course, to enhance certain factors, Sein would have to sacrifice his own mind and body.

'That's why I'm on the team.'

Eden, a specialist in defensive magic, clearly prioritized the protection targets.

'Protect the control tower.'

As Eden steeled her resolve, Armin approached Kuan, who was running on the other side.

"Are you okay?"

"A swordsman doesn't consider probabilities."

"No, I mean Shina."

Kuan's eyebrow twitched.

Olipher Shina was still working as a teacher in the city of Creas.

A closer distance compared to Zion.

But Kuan had never visited her.

"Why haven't you gone to see her? This battle could be your last."

"That's unpleasant."

Kuan glared at Armin.

"I handle my own affairs. And you're not the only one who cares about her."

Knowing that Armin was involved with Keira from the Ivory Tower made it even more so.

"If this mission fails..."

Kuan said,

"The Kingdom of Tormia will fall anyway. But if we kill Havitz, the war ends. That's all there is to it."

Sein pointed ahead.

"They're coming."

The army of demons filled the horizon from end to end, charging forward.

"Where's Havitz?"

Meirei no longer needed to cover her ears.

"I'm already listening. Put on the Nemesis. Oh, let me confirm one last thing."

Meirei turned to the group.

"The target for eavesdropping is Havitz. Don't expect normal sounds."

Understanding immediately, the group put on the Nemesis rings with tense expressions.

The sound of Havitz's mind that Meirei was hearing echoed in everyone's ears like a hallucination.

Eden's face turned pale.

"This, this is..."

Eavesdropping on a human's mind was a strange experience, but having seen many dark aspects of humanity as a seeker, he was somewhat prepared.

'This can't be.'

If the sound they were hearing was truly coming from within a human's mind...

'It's hell.'

This world was a mountain of suffering where one wouldn't feel wronged even if they cut their own throat right now.

Everyone had the same expression of shared emotion, and even the cold Kuan squinted one eye.

"No."

Realizing what Havitz desired, Eden shouted to everyone.

"We have to go back!"

They needed to stop Havitz's thoughts, not Havitz himself.

"It's too late."

Kuan insisted on increasing their speed, and Sein quickly made a decision.

"We push forward. We can't solve every problem. We just need to do what we do best."

They had to trust the Tormia army and the Holy War left in the flower field.

As soon as they felt the ground shaking from the enemy's advance, Sein gave the order.

"Now."

Light burst from Armin's eye, hidden under his eyepatch, activating the stop magic.

Time froze within a 4-kilometer diameter area, but it didn't trap the entire army.

The demons who collided with the boundary of the Spirit Zone froze, causing the following units to brake.

"What the...!"

The massive inertia pushed an additional million troops into the Spirit Zone before dissipating.

Armin spoke.

"We couldn't trap them all. We need to finish this quickly."

Sein weaved through the frozen demons.

'We have no mass right now. We can't exert physical force.'

In terms of information, they moved at the same speed as the signals entering this world.

"Found him."

Havitz, surrounded by demon guards, was frozen on a horse, ready to charge.

Lilia pulled several birch totems from the container on her back and leaped.

'We need to isolate Havitz.'

At that moment, Sein saw it clearly.

'What is that?'

Something was moving in one of Havitz's lifeless eyes, like a wax figure.

"A fly?"

Realizing it, twelve black holes appeared on the ground, and robed figures emerged.

'Shioh.'

It was the first time seeing it with his own eyes, but he could bet on what it was.

'So this is how it comes.'

It was proven through the Shirone case that the stop magic activated independently of Havitz.

Here began the experiment on the first hypothesis that hadn't been verified yet.

'Shioh is a time outside the law. Therefore, it isolates Havitz from all events, but...'

Why could they see Shioh now?

'Because time has stopped.'

If physical attacks on Shioh were possible, the success rate of assassinating Havitz would increase dramatically.

"Kuan."

As Sein gave the order, Havitz, at the center of Shioh, moved his lips.

"Stop."

In an instant, Shioh was sucked underground, and the stop magic regained control of the space.

"Why?"

Before Sein could understand the situation, Lilia threw the totems to the ground.

As the stop magic released, twenty totems formed a large circle around Havitz.

"It's done!"

As Lilia clasped her hands, the totems vibrated and dug into the ground.

As the ground shook, the surface of the formation became smooth like a mirror, reflecting the scenery.

But the scenery was not the Denan Plains; it was a vast wasteland covered in reddish-brown rocks.

'The law of space. Wonderland!'

As Sein's group surrounded Havitz, the formation began to flip as if it had an axis.

"Enemies! Kill them!"

The demons realized too late and charged, but the formation was already vertical.

Yet gravity still acted downward, making it seem like the world was turning upside down to Sein's group.

"Satan! Satan is..."

As the law of the formation, Wonderland, flipped 180 degrees, the demons crossed through empty space.

"Damn it! What's going on!"

No demon knew what had happened in the frozen time.

"Commander! Commander!"

The current general was Paimon, but the human messenger reported the situation to Balkhan.

"It's a serious situation! Lord Havitz has left the front lines! He seems to have fallen into a trap."

Balkhan stroked his chin as he listened.

"Hmm."

"What should we do? Should we pursue?"

"No, leave it."

"What?"

"Havitz moves by desire. He doesn't calculate in his head. It's something we can't stop anyway. And..."

Balkhan raised one corner of his mouth.

"Maybe it's for the best."

All Havitz could see was the horizon touching the sky in the wasteland.

Even if he survived and escaped, it was a place where he would die of dehydration and starvation.

"Who are you?"

Sein's group, surrounding Havitz, remained silent.

Havitz's choice was that shocking.

'I know what chaos is.'

But why did he come here, even stopping Shioh from activating?

"Why?"

Sein couldn't hold back and asked.

"Why did you withdraw Shioh? What's the reason?"

"Just because."

Havitz twirled his mustache.

"This seemed more fun."

It was the expected answer, but it confirmed something for sure.

'As long as Meirei is here, information is perfectly controlled. So he can't know about the elemental bomb.'

A predetermined future.

'If following us out of space is the only chance to save Havitz?'

If he chose the law that would take him as far away from Bashka as possible...

'Is our judgment right or wrong?'

He realized anew the vastness of Havitz's innate ability, the predetermined future.

"Meirei."

Kuan spoke softly.

"Eavesdrop."

At some point, Havitz's mind's voice had stopped being heard by everyone.

Turning her head, Eden saw Meirei, her face pale as a ghost.

Though her face was still expressionless, her skin color revealed her emotions.

"Meirei, let us hear it."

They had to react immediately if Havitz activated Vanishing.

"No."

Meirei spoke coldly.

She wouldn't make an arbitrary decision with the assassination target, Havitz, in front of her.

So the reason she blocked the eavesdropping, even ignoring Sein's command, was one.

She believed it would almost certainly lead to mission failure.

Sein spoke.

"Meirei, if you don't do it, we'll be annihilated."

"If you want to see your comrades die without knowing why, do as you wish."

Half-accepting, Meirei's nose wrinkled.

"I don't know what he's muttering, but..."

Havitz approached, dragging his longsword.

"Who should I kill first?"

Feeling the chilling murderous intent, Meirei closed her eyes tightly and shouted.

"I'll open it!"

As if a blocked hole had been pierced, Havitz's mind's voice reached Sein's group.

Waves of malice.

"Aaaah!"

Lilia screamed first, and Armin and Sein flinched as if shocked.

'Damn it! You should have told us!

Meirei had clearly warned them.

However, Havitz's mind was terrifying beyond their imagination.

"What's wrong?"

Havitz's first target was Eden.

"You seem to know something."

"Aah, aah..."

As Havitz approached, Eden felt dizzy, and her legs trembled.

'Stay calm. I'm Yora now. I'm not the same as when I was a follower of the Yora religion.'

Eden's faith in good was renowned even in Zion.

Even if a bomb fell in front of her, she wouldn't flinch because of her belief in God.

"Sigh."

But now, she was just a human, paralyzed by fear and falling on her backside.

"What's wrong?"

Havitz spoke.

"Are you afraid of me?"

You're just like me.

True fear doesn't come from outside but rises from within.

A murderer butchering people and cutting off limbs doesn't create fear.

Unless they do it to you.

'No. I am a messenger of God.'

Tears of fear flowed.

She thought she had expelled all evil from her mind, but there was still impurity left.

'I can't win. Never...'

The fear Havitz induced touched the essence of humanity, reaching the core of living beings.

'Extreme good.'

Only Miro, the perfect embodiment of purity, could respond to Satan's voice.
