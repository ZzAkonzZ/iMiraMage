Chapter 583:

All the Scramble participants' eyes were fixed on a single point in the sky.

Electricity generated from the ground discharged into the atmosphere, causing lightning to strike back down from the sky.

"What is that?"

Hershey was so captivated by the spectacle that he delayed analyzing it.

The only time he had seen such lightning in a real battle was when the Electric Monster Lycan faced a professional mage.

'That's where Nade is.'

The lightning was so violent that he couldn't even think of using Sona to search.

Finally, a massive light flashed as if cracking the world, followed by a thunderous explosion from the peak.

Then, silence.

'Nade! Wait!'

Sabina quickly retraced her steps.

Even she hadn't expected Nade to possess such magical power.

'The biggest problem is…'

Once magic is cast, its effects are real, even for the mage.

If the lightning was powerful enough to devastate the mountain peak, Nade couldn't have survived unscathed.

"Nade…!"

Returning to the battlefield, Sabina couldn't call out his name and stood frozen.

Everything had turned to ash and crumbled, leaving only Nade standing on the ground.

'It was like this before…'

The fear she felt when Nade had beaten her resurfaced, striking her heart.

It wasn't just because she was weak-hearted.

What was happening to Nade was clearly some 'phenomenon,' a real and present danger.

Where Nade walked, Frings lay sprawled with his mustache singed.

As Nade, with lightning crackling in his right hand, reached out to finish him, Frings spoke.

"…Spare me."

The reason his short words carried a lifetime of sincerity was that Nade truly intended to kill him.

If there were levels of murderous intent, Nade's was pure savagery.

It was different from animals killing for food or humans killing for possession.

'There's no purpose.'

It was like a force of nature.

Just as hurricanes, earthquakes, and lightning kill without meaning, Nade was a predator at the top of the killing pyramid.

"Why should I spare you?"

Frings, looking at Nade, immediately averted his gaze to the sky, unable to bear even a second of eye contact.

Conversely, Nade observed everything about Frings as if he were a prey's corpse.

The trembling of his fear-stricken body, the desperate breaths of a living being.

Then, noticing the bandaged fingers, Nade stepped on Frings' hand.

"Aaaargh!"

"Don't make a sound."

The sound vanished as if cut by a knife.

"Huff! Huff! Huff!"

As Nade approached his face, Frings' nostrils flared with each terrified breath.

"Don't breathe."

His breathing stopped.

"Yes. Die like that."

'Don't breathe!'

The reason he couldn't think of anything but not breathing, even though he would die anyway, was that Nade was scarier than death.

'Am I going to die…?'

"Tell me. Why shouldn't I kill you?"

As he began to lose consciousness, Frings' eyes snapped open.

'There's still hope!'

The second question hinted at a trace of humanity.

'He hasn't completely crossed the line. Or is he desperately holding back?'

He didn't know how it was possible, but it was clearly his last chance to live.

"If you kill me… you'll be expelled. You'll go to prison. The kingdom's powerful people will kill you."

"I don't care."

The humanity disappeared from Nade's voice again.

"Huh! Huh!"

Tears leaked from Frings' eyes.

"Think. Use your brain. Because I don't know. So convince me."

Nade declared.

"You have three seconds."

"Huuuuh! Huh!"

Frings squeezed his eyes shut and sobbed.

'Think! You have to think!'

The brain of a creature facing death sought answers at unprecedented speed.

The brain, nerves, and cells of his entire body coordinated, releasing all their energy.

'There must be a reason he can't kill me.'

A unique neural pattern flashed in his brain, bringing all his knowledge to the surface.

When he couldn't find an answer there, he began to trace back through all his past experiences.

The day he decided to become a pervert, Overflow, his infancy sucking his mother's milk…

He searched through his fetal, embryonic, and even the weak experiences of being a sperm and egg, finally reaching…

"Die."

'Found it!'

Frings' eyes snapped open.

"If you kill me!"

The electric afterimage flickered before his retinas.

"You'll never come back."

"…"

Frings used all the air in his lungs to speak.

"You're holding back desperately. If you cross the line here, you'll… never come back. So…"

The words crawled out of his throat.

"Spare me…"

The electricity in Nade's hand fizzled out.

"Take out your master card."

Frings, not even knowing how his broken fingers twisted, fumbled in his pocket.

"Tear it."

With a snap, the card split, and Nade stood up and walked away.

With each step, Frings' body convulsed.

'Nade…'

Sabina reached out to the retreating Nade.

She was relieved he hadn't crossed the line.

'But…'

Today, she realized she couldn't get close.

No one in the world could stay by his side unless he allowed it.

"Sigh."

Sitting on a burnt rock, Nade covered his cursed face with his hands and exhaled.

Anchal's mind projection had revealed his true face.

Having vented his rage, he regained some sanity, but what followed was intense regret and self-loathing.

'This face. This face!'

Even without a mirror, his mother's image in his mind told him what he looked like now.

"Damn it!"

His hands, covering his face, rose like spikes.

As his nails scratched his skin, red lines appeared on his face.

"Aaaargh!"

Just hearing Nade's scream made Frings feel like he was suffocating.

"Huff! Huff! Huff!"

Tears welled up in his bloodshot eyes as he gasped for air.

Nade, Frings.

Retired.

As the sixth day of Scramble Royal ended, Shirone's team gathered at the hideout.

"Iruki, what happened? Why is Nade…?"

Shirone asked as soon as he arrived.

According to Lulu's signal, Nade had left the area and was disqualified.

Iruki waited silently for the other team members.

Sabina appeared, looking defeated, and shook her head at Iruki's silent question.

'Hmm, so it happened.'

They wouldn't see Nade for a while.

'It was the same before.'

The bloody battle on a rainy night flashed through his mind.

'At least no one died. You held on till the end. Well done, Nade.'

"Iruki, tell me. What happened to Nade?"

Iruki hesitated.

But he didn't want Shirone to know, at least not yet.

"Shirone, Nade likes you more than he likes me."

"What are you talking about?"

"So trust him. Everyone has secrets they want to keep. You did too."

"But I…"

"Yes, you told us. So hear it from him. Nade will come back to us. Let's focus on the match for now."

Shirone clamped his mouth shut.

A moment later, Dorothy arrived, followed by Amy, trudging through the bushes.

"Amy? What happened to you?"

She was covered in dirt, just like Shirone had been after the first day of Scramble Royal.

"Kaiden is no joke."

She collapsed into a seat, sticking out her tongue.

"He chased me like a rabid dog… Shirone, how did you endure it?"

"Did he change his target to you? That's cowardly…"

Amy shook her head firmly.

"No. This is between us. We have personal issues to resolve. I'll handle Kaiden tomorrow too. Is that okay?"

Since Shirone had to deal with Eden, Amy was the best candidate to handle Kaiden.

"Yes. We're ahead in numbers, so it's good to keep one person tied up."

"By the way, Anchal retired too. I couldn't attend the mid-meeting because I was fighting Kaiden. What happened?"

"She made a deal with me."

"With you?"

Given Shirone's personality, it was unlikely he would agree to such a deal.

"There must be a reason?"

"Yes. It's about Istas, but it's complicated. We'll know for sure if we win Scramble Royal."

Iruki summarized.

"We've collected 28 scrambles. The enemy has 30."

"Sorry. I wanted to at least match them."

Iruki shook his head at Dorothy's apology.

"Against insect magic, this is a good result. We have one day left. It'll be a showdown between me and Hershey."

Sabina asked, "What's the plan?"

"Dorothy and I will team up. Hershey will pair with Pisho. We'll use today's scrambles and collect tomorrow's to form the best hand."

"So it's a real card game now."

"That's how it is. But there are many external variables. Amy will handle Kaiden, and Shirone must break Eden's Infinity."

"Should I reshuffle my hand now?"

To face Eden, Shirone also needed to create an Infinity.

"There's something to check first."

Iruki asked.

"You used Ataraxia today, right? How was it?"

Everyone looked at Shirone.

"Honestly, it's a really strong barrier. Even if I increased the output, I probably couldn't break it."

Having experienced the power of the Photon Cannon that passed through Ataraxia, his friends were shocked.

"That strong…"

"That's how strong it is…"

Iruki came up with a new idea.

"To give Shirone Infinity, we need to use 12 of our current scrambles right now."

They needed one Infinity (○○○○○○) to replace Shirone's Oblivion (●○●●○●) and another Infinity for Shirone to hold.

"So, we'd have two Infinities? Isn't that good?"

"Then the enemy would also make two Infinities. It would turn into a war. But this isn't meaningful. As long as we have scrambles, we can always create Infinity, which gives the allied team with Pisho an advantage."

"So what do you suggest?"

"From now on, it depends on Shirone. Can you really break Eden's barrier?"

As Shirone hesitated, Amy asked, "What does that mean?"

"If Shirone can break the barrier, I won't reshuffle his hand."

"But Eden might call Kang, bringing the master card."

"That's why it's a one-time chance. We'll do it in the sixth scramble, the last one. If Eden doesn't have the master card, she can't call Kang, and the game will end when the barrier is broken."

Dorothy added, "That way, we can save 12 scrambles. If Eden has the master card, we can create two Infinities then."

"Exactly. Tomorrow will be a battle of combinations. Using 12 scrambles on Shirone now would be wasteful. So I'm asking, Shirone, if you can break Eden's barrier, I'll use the 12 scrambles in the card game against Hershey. Can you do it?"

"Hmm."

After thinking carefully, Shirone looked up.

"I can do it."

He had no other choice.
