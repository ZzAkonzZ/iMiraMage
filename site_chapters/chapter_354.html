Chapter 354: Swordsmans Conviction (6)

After spending a day at the mansion, Shirone headed to the dining room with Rian and Tess. Clump was seated at the head of the table, reading a newspaper, while Reina was busy preparing breakfast.

Shirone rubbed his sleepy eyes and greeted them with a bow.

“Good morning.”

“Morning. Let's eat.”

Rian, rubbing his hungry stomach, sat down and asked Reina,

“Where's Rai?”

“He left at dawn. Do you think he's as idle as you?”

As usual, she started with a joke, but this time there was a hint of concern. As an older sister, she couldn't help but worry about her younger brother leaving on a knight's quest.

“Hey, I only dropped out yesterday! And I'm leaving right after breakfast.”

Reina served chicken stew onto plates and said,

“Why don't you rest for a few days? Shirone is here too.”

Shirone, carrying a plate, said,

“No, I need to head back soon as well.”

He couldn't explain the details due to his promise with Kangnan. Moreover, he was still curious why the chairman wanted to see him.

Since it was the day Rian was leaving, the atmosphere was quite solemn. However, Rian himself devoured three plates of chicken stew and hurriedly packed his things.

His belongings were simple: a backpack with some clothes and a day's worth of food. With a leather scabbard strapped to his body and the greatsword on his back, he approached Clump in the living room.

“Grandfather.”

“Yes, are you ready?”

There was one thing left to address.

The fact that he had defeated Rai would give him great courage for his journey. But there was something he needed to clarify.

“What did Rai mean yesterday?”

Clump looked up from his teacup at Rian.

“Why didn't he use swordsmanship? You know, don't you?”

“Oh, that.”

Clump smacked his lips and put down his teacup.

He had been putting it off to avoid giving Rian false hopes, but now that Rian was leaving on a knight's quest, it was time to tell him.

“The sword you carry is not an ordinary one. Have you heard of an object?”

“An object?”

Rian’s eyes widened as he drew the greatsword. The functions of precise control and Vajra Armor that Shirone had demonstrated the previous night flashed through his mind, making his heart race.

Shirone and Tess also came into the living room, intrigued by the conversation, and focused on the greatsword. It was a sword with no special features other than its imposing design.

Rian held the greatsword vertically and looked up at it.

“So this is an object? Is that why it wasn't destroyed? What abilities does it have?”

Clump crossed his arms and legs.

“The official name of that sword is . Its abilities are as impressive as its name. To put it bluntly, ...”

“In any situation?”

Clump's eyes sharpened as he spoke clearly.

“It cannot be destroyed.”

Silence filled the living room.

“And?”

“What do you mean, 'and'? It can't be destroyed. Whether it falls into lava, gets cut with a saw, or hit with a hammer, it won't break. Isn't that amazing?”

In a way, it was a swordsman's dream. But compared to Armand, which had dozens of functions, it seemed disappointingly simple.

“Well, that's... amazing. But it's still an object. If a sword breaks, can't you just buy a new one?”

“Foolish. Do you know how important a weapon's durability is in real combat? Even in yesterday's duel, it would have been much harder without .”

Rian acknowledged this. In fact, he had already been saved by once. It was when he blocked Ymir's straight punch.

Ymir had crumpled the steel-made Signa and Exde like paper, but he couldn't destroy Rian’s greatsword. If not for , Ymir's punch would have gone through the sword and shattered Rian’s body.

“That's the kind of sword it is. Rai didn't avoid using swordsmanship; he couldn't. When he was young, he really wanted that sword.”

Rian pouted. He now prided himself on being a respectable swordsman, but as a child, Rai was the family's pride and joy.

“Then why didn't you give it to Rai?”

“He doesn't like crude things. It wouldn't suit him.”

The answer was different from what Rian had expected.

As Rian’s expression turned sour, Clump laughed.

“Heh heh, what did you expect? That I prepared it especially for you?”

“Who said that? Stop pretending to read minds.”

Clump pretended otherwise, but Shirone could guess.

Rai probably really wanted it. Beyond its functions, inheriting the former head's sword was a great honor. But the sword was given to Rian.

Even so, Clump's jokes were his way of not burdening his grandson.

“Since we're on the topic, let me tell you a story about .”

“A story?”

Rian’s eyes sparkled with interest. Despite his complaints, he didn't dislike 's unique ability.

“Yes. When I was young, I went on a swordsmanship quest like you. I once single-handedly defeated a band of mountain bandits. They were quite formidable. I managed to annihilate them, but I was mortally wounded. I dragged my injured body down the mountain and found a strange village that wasn't on any map. It wasn't much different from other villages, but the people were oddly quiet and gloomy.”

Shirone and the others held their breath, captivated by Clump's story. It was about the man who would become a general of Tormia and his fateful encounter with an object.

“There, I met an old man and his granddaughter who saved my life. When I regained consciousness, my sword wounds were completely healed. I don't know how they treated me. All I remember is the old man repeatedly chanting something like a spell. 'Smile, smile,' he kept saying.”

Rian, who had been mumbling the word, asked,

“Smile? What does that mean?”

“I don't know. It could have been a spell or a type of magic. Anyway, the old man chanted it all night. When I came to, I explored the village. There was an antique shop with many strange items. The most impressive thing was that they sold glass bottles filled with colored smoke. When I asked what they were for, the owner wouldn't tell me.”

Clump took a sip of tea, signaling he was getting to the main point.

“Then I saw a greatsword hanging behind the counter. That was . I knew at a glance it wasn't an ordinary sword. I approached the owner as if possessed.”

Rian swallowed hard, his expression tense.

“And then?”

Clump blinked and casually said,

“What do you mean, 'and then'? I paid for it and bought it, of course.”

The tension turned to disappointment, and Rian blurted out,

“Are you kidding me?”

Clump raised an eyebrow in displeasure.

“What? You wanted to hear a story, didn't you?”

Rian scratched his head.

He had been fooled by his grandfather again. Was it so fun to tease an eighteen-year-old grandson?

“You made me nervous for nothing. I thought you had some life-and-death experience.”

“Hahaha! Do you think I'm crazy to risk my life for a sword? Rian, remember this. A true sword is held not by the hand but by the heart. Anyway, I made a big decision to buy it. Treat it with care.”

“Ugh, you sound like an old man. By the way, how much did you pay? Since it's an object, maybe around 2 million gold?”

“That? 80 million gold.”

Clang! Rian dropped the greatsword.

“Damn! Are you crazy? You paid 80 million for a sword that just doesn't break? You could buy hundreds of fine swords with that money!”

“It's an object, you fool! And it's my money, so why do you care? If you're going to treat it like that, give it back! Or pay me 80 million!”

“No way! You gave it to me, so that's it. No take-backs!”

Rian hurriedly picked up the greatsword and hugged it, afraid someone might take it.

“What kind of swordsman throws his sword on the ground? That sword is too good for you! Hang it in my room!”

“Why? Afraid it might break? Didn't you say a true sword is held by the heart?”

Shirone and Tess watched the argument in disbelief.

“They're so alike.”

“Indeed.”

After arguing for a while, they finally stopped, exhausted.

In the end, Rian won. He sheathed the greatsword and tied the leather strap tighter than usual, as if to show Clump.

After a moment of huffing, Clump smiled, remembering something.

It was a moment he had waited 18 years for. Seeing it with his own eyes made the old man's heart swell with emotion.

‘The more I see him, the more he resembles me.’

He had little talent for swordsmanship, but Rian was always meant to be the owner of .

He hadn't paid 80 million gold for its functions. An indestructible sword was the perfect symbol of the Ozent family's conviction.

And in this moment, all his philosophy was passed to Rian through .

‘It's strange. When I went back after paying 80 million gold, the village had vanished.’

Of course, if 80 million gold had come to a mountain village, the entire village could have moved to a better place.

However, there was a subtle feeling Clump couldn't share with Rian.

Perhaps he had only been lying injured in the mountains, and the month he spent going back and forth between the village and the house was actually a dream he wandered through in a state of unconsciousness.

In any case, it was one of the most inexplicable events in Clump's life. Moreover, the realization he gained that day led him to rise steadily, eventually becoming a certified third-grade swordsman general.

“Grandfather, I'm off now.”

Rian had already composed himself and said his farewell with a fiery gaze.

“Yes. The most important thing is to protect yourself. Remember that what you gain from real combat isn't always victory.”

It was advice to avoid fights when possible.

Taking his grandfather's advice to heart, Rian bowed sincerely.

‘Thank you, Grandfather, for believing in your foolish grandson until the end.’

Rian left the mansion, accompanied by his family's and friends' farewells. A carriage was waiting at the front gate.

After briefly contemplating his destination, Rian made up his mind and turned to Shirone.

“Shirone, do your best. Even though I dropped out, just think of it as me graduating first.”

“Sure. Take care of yourself. Keep in touch.”

Rian looked at Tess, who was on the verge of tears. She had held up well, but now that he was truly leaving, she couldn't suppress her emotions.

Rian gave her a gentle smile. Tess, despite not being a typical swordsman, was someone he cherished. One day, he would stand proudly before her.

“Tess, I'm off.”

“You jerk. You didn't even discuss it with me...”

“Sorry. I didn't think it through.”

Tess understood Rian’s decision. She was the one who needed to understand it the most. But she was also angry at herself for not being able to harshly reject this indifferent man.

“This is the last time I'll tolerate it. If you treat me this coldly when you return...”

Rian embraced Tess with his broad chest.

“I'll come back stronger.”

Tess bit her lip and held back her tears.

‘You don't have to become stronger. Just come back alive...’

She gently patted Rian’s back and said,

“Alright. Come back stronger.”

Having said his goodbyes to everyone, Rian got into the carriage. True to his nature, he didn't look back once.

As the carriage departed, the four left behind felt an emptiness. That was Rian’s presence.

Shirone watched the receding carriage and prayed.

‘Come back as a great swordsman, Rian. I'll do my best too.’

May good fortune be with Rian on his journey.
