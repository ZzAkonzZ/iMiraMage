Chapter 713:

"Rank…!"

Someone shouted.

"A ranker!"

The moment they saw the number 97 on the woman's forehead, everyone around fell to the ground.

"Your Grace!"

Even the pursuers, realizing the situation, fell to the ground at a distance, trembling.

Shirone estimated that the city's population was 427,620, and the girl before him was the 97th most superior among them.

"What's going on, Panika?"

Codename 97, Panika.

A girl of similar age, who had come out of the shop late, approached Panika and looked around in surprise.

"Oh my, why are there so many people?"

On her forehead was the number 113, lower than Panika's, leading Shirone to speculate.

'Do they still have some humanity?'

Of course, if Panika wanted to harm 113, she could do anything without it being a crime.

"I don't know. This man suddenly…"

Panika pointed at Shirone and stopped speaking when she saw there was no number on his forehead.

"Huh? An uncountable?"

Although an explanation was needed, those around them remained silent, barely breathing.

'It's over if you get caught by a ranker.'

Panika was known not to kill people for fun, but who could say for sure?

Maybe her hair was frizzy today, the food tasted bad, or she was just in a bad mood for no reason.

"You."

Panika pointed at a random man, who flinched and quickly raised his head.

"Yes! Yes! Number 97!"

His face, marked with the number 324,801, looked as if he were terminally ill.

"Explain what's going on."

"I will obey!"

As he stood up, he gathered his thoughts and swallowed before explaining what he knew.

"Hmm, so an uncountable entered the city, and that person is…"

Panika finally spoke to Shirone.

"That's you, right?"

"You could have asked me from the start."

Since he had encountered a high number, Shirone decided to gather information.

"Huh? You speak differently? Are you from another city?"

Sometimes, foreigners who crossed over from other cities became uncountables in Codename.

Even though the world was different, the thoughts were the same, so Shirone summoned the artificial brain (language interpreter version).

'Will it work?'

When Shirone gestured for her to hold it, Panika grabbed it without fear.

'She has immense confidence.'

It seemed there was nothing to fear besides the code.

"Yes. This is my first time in this city. So I want to know. Why do people have numbers on their foreheads?"

As the device vibrated and translated the language, Panika's eyes filled with interest.

"Oh, a unique power."

It was an ability used by the residents of Codename.

"Panika, let's just kill him. A human who rejects numbers is filthy just to look at."

Number 113 glared at Shirone, but Panika didn't even consider it.

"No. If he's from another city, it's better for him to receive a codename. It's better than dying as an uncountable."

"Let's do that. That's better."

From 113's quick change of stance, Shirone realized that the code was still supreme.

"What do you think? If you want to receive a code, I'll guide you."

'It's certainly simple and easy.'

If he could somehow receive a high code, he could obtain all the information he needed in this world.

"Okay, guide me."

When Shirone agreed, Panika looked around and spoke.

"Everyone, disperse."

As if a natural disaster had occurred, people jumped up and ran like mad.

Unless numbers 1 to 96 appeared and gave special instructions, her command would stand.

"Follow me. Let's go to the temple."

Under the pastel-colored sky, a temple stood.

In the labyrinth Andre-World No. 1583.

Shirone arrived in a village called Whiteblack, with about 2,000 residents.

There was a special rule here: anyone over fourteen had to choose between the White and Black zones.

"Do I have to decide too?"

"Of course. You're over fourteen."

The White zone decided everything by vote, while the lawless Black zone even allowed murder.

Surprisingly, unlike the rest of the world, most residents chose White.

'Well, who would want to live in Black?'

Shirone also chose White, and the next day, the village chief visited his home.

"There is an agreement between White and Black. Each zone selects ten representatives for a survival test."

"Why do we have to do that?"

"Because no one in Black works. The winning zone takes 80% of the village's production."

"That's too cruel."

"Black proposed it. Without this agreement, Black would plunder the White residents."

Although few chose Black, those who did were extremely brutal and a source of fear for White.

"And you have been selected as one of White's representatives. You will leave for Anarchy Mountain at midnight."

Shirone was taken aback.

"But I just learned about the rules…"

"It was decided by vote."

The moment White denied the result of the vote, its distinction from Black would disappear.

"This is how we live. You can appeal, but nothing will change. There are 1,600 White residents."

"What if I refuse?"

"You'll be exiled to Black. But even there, they'll select ten people, and you'll likely be chosen by force."

'Whether I'm chosen by vote or beaten into it, it's the same. Maybe I should investigate Black instead?'

Shirone was confident he could win in either zone but decided to delay his judgment.

'I still have to participate.'

If this world was also isolated by Goffin's special criteria, understanding his intent was the key to breaking the seal.

"What are the odds of winning?"

"The survival game has been held 843 times historically, with White winning 51% of the time."

"51%? That's higher than I expected."

It was an achievement against the Black zone residents, who killed as easily as eating.

"It's a matter of desperation. Black has fewer people and the strong monopolize, so 20% of the production is enough. But if White doesn't get 80%, their families will starve all year."

That was the issue.

"Black is just jealous. They bring despair to peaceful White this way. Sometimes truly strong individuals emerge, but the opposite is also true. We must win."

After the chief left, Shirone pondered alone.

'White can be peaceful because Black exists, and Black cannot exist without White.'

Like good and evil defining each other.

'Does it mean it's the same no matter what I choose?'

Time flew like an arrow, and as midnight approached, Shirone appeared at the designated place.

"Whew, you came."

Hearing the sighs of relief from the anxious residents, Shirone entered the square.

'Including me, there are five men. The gender ratio being equal must be the result of the vote.'

The nine representatives standing in line looked at Shirone with various emotions.

In the labyrinth Andre-World No. 1.

Hearing the roar of a volcanic eruption, Shirone flew quickly over the dense forest of a primitive planet.

The sulfur smell was so strong it made him nauseous, but the world was so vast there was no time to rest.

'It's a human.'

Except for using weapons and covering their genitals, they looked like monkeys.

"Uga uga! Uga uga!"

They were hunting with sharpened sticks but scattered in fear when a beast counterattacked.

'They're still weak.'

Seven men gathered again where the beast had been.

"Ugh ugh! Ugh ugh!"

Like children, they stomped the ground and eventually burst into tears.

Shirone opened the Ultima system.

"We have to starve again today! I'm hungry! I want to eat meat!"

A man, baring his teeth like a monkey, hit the chest of the person next to him and shouted.

"Why did you run away! You said you'd stab it!"

"When did I say that!"

A fight broke out, and unlike with the beast, they exchanged fierce punches.

"Raaah!"

Trees snapped, and the ground shook as a massive dragon landed.

"A dragon! A dragon!"

The fighting humans drooled and wet themselves, then flattened to the ground.

"Please spare us! Please spare us!"

To the humans of this world, dragons were gods.

"Pathetic creatures…"

As the dragon opened its mouth wide for a meal, a photon cannon struck its face.

"Ugh!"

The dragon, experiencing a physical shock only encountered in battles with its kind, quickly changed direction.

"Hu…man?"

Seeing Shirone, the dragon's eyes sparked with blue electricity, and its mouth shut tight.

"…"

Flapping its massive wings, the dragon rose several meters.

"So you are the one who came from another world."

"How do you know me?"

The dragon's mind was integrated into a binary network, and the experiences of specific individuals were stored on the web.

"Our king will summon you."

Leaving those words, the dragon disappeared into the sky, and the trembling humans looked up in disbelief.

"Uga uga! Uga uga!"

The joyful shouts of survival were clear without needing the Ultima system to analyze.

Humans gathered around Shirone, dancing and sniffing him.

"Wait… I…"

As Shirone recalled how to use the artificial brain, a man touched his lower body.

"What are you doing?"

Confirming he was male, they laughed and pointed somewhere, dragging Shirone along.

"You saved us. We'll do something good for you."

"Something good? What's that?"

Even without special abilities, the humans understood Shirone's words perfectly and moved their hips back and forth.

"No, wait! I don't need that."

Shirone waved his hand, but they dragged him along relentlessly and soon arrived at a nearby cave.

"Here! Here! We caught it!"

"Eek! Eek!"

Shirone's eyes widened in surprise as he interpreted the woman's angry screams with Ultima.

"Let me go! You stupid monkeys!"

If she called the humans of this world monkeys, it meant she was either from another world or a special sage.

'Who is she?'

Inside the cave, a precious campfire was burning, and in the corner was a woman tied with vines.

She was as hairy as the other humans and struggled without any dignity.

'Was I mistaken?'

Just as disappointment washed over him, Shirone realized something from the woman's eyes, and his heart skipped a beat.

"Eek! Eek! Eek…"

The woman, also shocked to see Shirone, stopped screaming and barely managed to speak.

"Shi…ro…ne…"

Though her appearance was entirely different, the energy in her eyes was unmistakably that of Uorin.

'No, she's not Uorin.'

She was the first Terraje, Mitochondrial Eve.
