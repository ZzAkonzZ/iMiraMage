Chapter 577:

Scramble Royal, Day 5.

As soon as the first Scramble was summoned, Iruki teleported to the nearest coordinates.

Fortunately, the Scramble wasn't far, and he arrived at Training Ground 2000.

It should have been empty during lunchtime, but someone was waiting for him.

"Eden."

She hadn't shown herself the previous day.

Iruki had suspected she was up to something, but he couldn't see any noticeable changes.

Except for the intense faith burning in her eyes.

"Did you wait on purpose?"

Being a nemesis to defensive magic wasn't just because of Cancellation; Iruki's magic was extremely offensive.

Meeting her in the first round suggested a special purpose.

"I needed to confirm something before meeting Shirone."

'So I'm the test subject.'

With Hershey's ability, finding locations was easy.

"So… are you going to take it?"

Iruki pointed at the Scramble.

"Probably… yes?"

"Heh heh!"

Iruki's eyes sparked with Overdrive.

'She's really pushing my buttons.'

Cancellation!

By the fifth day, there was nothing to hide, and an overwhelming amount of data flooded Iruki's mind.

'Do whatever you want.'

There was no need to understand the magic's mechanism.

Servant Syndrome allowed him to analyze the entire omniscience and reverse the vector quantity.

"I believe in your existence."

Eden began to undress.

"I believe that God's love resides within me."

Even as she walked towards him naked, Iruki didn't stop analyzing.

"That love will once again fill the world."

'Got it!'

As soon as the analysis was complete, he cast Cancellation magic.

"Thus, transcending faith…"

Eden spread her arms.

"I know of your existence."

Proof of God's Existence - Absolute Barrier.

There was no visible effect, just a naked girl, but Iruki was shocked.

"…This is impossible."

"What's wrong? Do what you can. That's why I came to you."

Cancellation wasn't working.

'The vector isn't moving.'

Immobility and slight movement are completely different concepts.

Even a slight movement would eventually cancel the magic, but now, change was impossible.

'It's not an omniscience issue.'

Absolute omnipotence.

Eden's extreme perception, transcending faith to feel God, had strengthened her omnipotence.

Her Spirit Zone clung to her body like vacuum packaging, but it was enough to protect her.

"Then, I'll take it."

'I have to stop her.'

As Eden walked towards the Scramble, Iruki activated the Servant's unique Double Spirit Zone.

'I have no choice.'

The power of a nuclear fusion explosion would be unimaginable, but if he couldn't stop Eden here, the game would be lost.

'It takes about 11.73 seconds to walk to the Scramble.'

Iruki designed a 10-second nuclear fusion equation and moved away. As Eden reached for the Scramble, he cast,

'Nuclear Fusion!'

A brilliant light gathered where Eden's hand reached, expanding rapidly and obliterating the landscape.

Boom!

The explosion from the mountain turned all the students' heads, and Siina rubbed her temples.

"What was that? An accident?"

Even from a distance, the mountain's shape had changed from the massive explosion.

'I created this magic, but it's really something.'

Landing on a branch, Iruki confirmed that Training Ground 2000 was completely gone.

The financial loss would be enormous, but that was a problem for later.

"Anyway… huh?"

Iruki's eyes widened as he saw Eden holding the card, unharmed.

It was a White Card.

"Just one more."

She looked at Iruki from afar and said,

"Infinity will be completed today."

"Do you know how much Training Ground 2000 costs?"

Iruki felt a sense of injustice, even though he was the one who destroyed it.

"I don't know."

Eden neither got angry nor argued.

As long as God protected her, even if the universe was destroyed, she wouldn't be harmed.

"I believe in your existence. I believe that God's love resides within me…"

Watching Eden walk away, reciting her prayer, Iruki muttered weakly,

"We're going to lose this."

Dorothy hid behind a tree, trembling.

Even after yielding the Scramble, Frings was relentlessly following her.

"Dorothy, where are you? Hiding here?"

As Frings pushed through the bushes, Dorothy's shoulder flinched.

"Not here? Then maybe over here?"

His voice was nauseatingly slick.

'He's mocking me!'

Though she thought that, her body couldn't move.

'Pervert.'

Dorothy knew what would happen if Frings caught her.

She would reveal everything.

Her intimate secrets, even the horrible ones she didn't want to remember, would be exposed.

"Aha, there you are?"

Dorothy's heart sank.

"Just kidding. Don't be so tense."

Her legs, weakened, trembled.

"Scared to death, aren't you? That's what makes hide-and-seek fun. Enjoy it, Dorothy. I'm thrilled too."

'I know. He knows where I am.'

Even without entering each other's Spirit Zones, they both knew.

"I can smell it. The scent of Dorothy's frustration."

Hearing his voice just a meter behind her, Dorothy's mind went blank.

"Now, let's meet our princess…"

Bang! Bang! Bang!

At that moment, Hershey's gathering signal went off.

Looking up at the sky, Frings spoke to the tree where Dorothy was hiding.

"Hershey's calling. You're safe, Dorothy. How do you feel? Relieved to the point of madness? Huh? Huh?"

"…"

"If you beg me to leave, I will. Beg, while crying."

'Ugh…'

Dorothy clenched her trembling jaw.

"…See you next time, miss."

As soon as the sound of teleportation faded, Dorothy collapsed forward.

"Huff, huff!"

Relief was brief, and unbearable misery washed over her.

"What… am I doing here?"

Hickory watched her as tears fell.

'Religion (○○○).'

Eden had already collected three cards.

'One per round, consistently. There's no way to stop her.'

Iruki pondered.

So how could they stop her?

With such a vast difference in omnipotence, Cancellation was out of the question.

'Even if I could exploit just 0.1%.'

There was no point in worrying about what he couldn't do, so he sought a second option.

'External force?'

Extending the nuclear fusion explosion equation to about a minute might work.

'Would she wait that long? Even if strategically induced, the aftermath would be catastrophic.'

Unlike the destruction of Training Ground 2000, it could cause casualties.

"I need to gather opinions. A gathering signal…"

At that moment, Dorothy emerged from the bushes.

"Dorothy? What's wrong?"

As the one collecting their Scrambles, she had no reason to seek out Iruki.

"Sorry. I want to quit."

"What happened?"

"I'll take the consequences. I'll repay everything, no matter what. Just let me quit."

Seeing her tears, Iruki sensed the gravity of the situation and stood up.

"Tell me what happened. I need to know to decide."

"I'm scared of Frings."

Sitting under a tree, Dorothy recounted her two days of torment by Frings.

"…I see. But we can strategize to avoid him."

"That's not the issue."

For the first time, Dorothy opened up about herself.

"I'm a pathetic girl. While my friends were busy with diplomacy and politics classes, I was under the covers, imagining strange things. Is there a God? An afterlife? Where did we come from? I collected data, made games alone…"

A sad smile appeared on Dorothy's lips.

"When I was little, everyone found it fun. But now, when I talk about it, do you know what they say the most?"

Iruki waited for her answer.

"How will you live?"

Dorothy spoke as if possessed by those words.

"Will you become a magician? Isn't it hard to be certified? Have you thought about how much you can earn in a year? If you're serious, you can't stay like this."

Anger filled Dorothy's eyes.

"What do they know! That stuff!"

Fantasy was her only reality where she could be happy.

"These thoughts are important to us too. Someone has to live thinking like that. So why do they look at me with pity?"

Iruki realized why he had chosen her.

"Of course. They're afraid of you."

"Afraid of me?"

"I was too. Because we're afraid of staying children, we all become adults. Then we realize."

Iruki raised his index finger.

"Being an adult is useless."

"…"

"There are no adults in the world. Only children and children pretending to be adults. Because we're all scared, we pretend to cope."

Iruki patted Hickory's shoulder.

"But you made this. A tin doll filled with wild imagination, fighting on par with the graduation class. Only the final hurdle remains. When you become a magician…"

Iruki helped Dorothy stand.

"When you become a magician, you won't have to fear anything."

"A magician…"

"Yes. Let's win the Scramble Royal."

Beyond rewards and penalties, it was a game they had to win.

"But Frings is too cruel."

"Avoid the filth. Everyone has secrets they want to hide. I do too."

"What is it?"

"Huh?"

Iruki, looking at Dorothy, shook his head.

"It's a secret because I don't want to say it."

"But I told you. You should too, to be fair."

"Well…"

Iruki pouted.

"I dreamed about you the other day."

Dorothy blinked and then laughed, covering her mouth.

"Haha! What is that?"

As the fifth day passed its midpoint, darkness began to fall.

Nade, who had infiltrated the senior girls' dormitory with her face covered, entered the laundry room with his eyes tightly shut.

"Ahhh!"

The expected scream erupted, but he had no intention of stopping his infiltration.

'Of all places…!'

A light was shining from the basket containing underwear.

"Move! Move!"

Nade pushed aside the juniors and rummaged through the basket like a starving ogre.

"Pervert!"

"Who's a pervert!"

By the time he collected the scramble and left the laundry room, the hallway was already filled with female students.

"Catch her! He's an underwear thief!"

Nade, his face burning, shouted.

"I am Fermi! Fermi of the graduating class!"

"Shut up, Nade!"

Although Nade was now officially in the graduating class, there was no title to be given to an underwear thief.

"Damn it! I'll get you all!"

Shouting such meaningless words, he exited the dormitory, clutching a random card to his chest and breathing heavily.

"Whew, at least I got one."

With Pisho gathering scrambles with insect magic and Eden destroying scrambles with absolute defense abilities, a single card was precious at this point.

"I need to go get praised quickly, right?"

Just as he was about to head towards his friends, shining magic shot up repeatedly from the sky.

Confirming the number and tempo of the flashes, Nade muttered.

"Rescue signal?"
