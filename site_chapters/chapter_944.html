Chapter 944: Hidden Code (3)

Shirone swiftly absorbed the records of Goffin having spoken to a lotus for 40,000 years.

As the end of Omega approached, he felt anxious, but there was no faster way to build omniscience.

'After all, it’s the history of the entire universe.'

His interest was in Goffin, but the actual data incoming was about every event that had occurred across the whole world.

Even the record of the 3,241st rabbit on a remote planet mating with the 8,790th rabbit and giving birth to the 17,653rd rabbit was being transmitted.

"I've decided."

From where Shirone's consciousness was focused, Goffin's voice conveyed with an eleventh sense.

His appearance was unchanged from when he first arrived, and the lotus that had become his home had not withered.

"I must leave."

'So suddenly? Where are you headed?'

Goffin stroked the lotus.

"Thank you for listening to my long, tedious story. I'm sorry..."

'The story about humans, right? Humans are fascinating. Truly amazing. Actually... I wish I could become human.'

"I lost my wife and child."

Goffin looked up at the sky.

"What's the point of becoming a god? Living forever just means losing someone else again. I might not be able to bear it next time."

'You've suffered a lot. I know.'

"If only there had been nothing from the start. If this world had never existed..."

For a moment, Goffin's eyes filled with emptiness.

But the void was brief, soon replaced by affectionate eyes looking down at the lotus.

"Yet, I still want to go out into the world."

'Why?'

Goffin did not hear the lotus’s voice, but coincidentally, he responded.

"Because it continues. It must go on endlessly."

Goffin stood up from the lotus, leaped across a wide lake, and landed on solid ground.

"I think I have told you too much. It must have been painful."

Though he couldn't communicate with the lotus, he didn't assume the plant had endured 40,000 years unchanged.

"If you can hear me now, and if you understand, then you must have a heart too."

Goffin paused in thought and added.

"It means you're connected to the outside."

'The outside?'

Goffin smiled bitterly.

"I’m not really sure. Maybe it’s time to close the doors to this world."

A world full of pain.

"But still, if you want to continue..."

Grateful for 40,000 years of companionship, Goffin bowed his head.

"Help humans."

Humans.

Beings that could tilt toward good or evil, affection or indifference.

The lotus pondered.

And as Goffin turned and walked away, golden smoke rose from the petals, forming a human shape. A girl radiating the fresh scent of flowers slowly bowed her head in Goffin's manner.

"Farewell."

Leaving behind the birth of the Hua, Goffin walked calmly through the forest of the wilderness.

'How much time has passed?'

He hadn't kept exact count, but it felt like about 40,000 years.

An unfathomable amount of time for humans.

But for Goffin, who had lived through an eternity, it was no more significant than a human timing their lunch.

"40,000 years. I wonder if anything has changed?" When he first arrived on this planet, there were primitive tribes, but they likely didn't last long.

The survival of a species is determined by the superiority of its systems, and building a system that can handle many variables comes at a cost.

The cost of failed experiences.

'Perhaps humans have vanished.'

Either that or unimaginable monsters rule the world, or perhaps it’s empty.

'If so, I’m alone again.'

Contrary to his despairing thoughts, Goffin’s steps quickened as he found an exit.

"Don't get your hopes up. You might end up crying with disappointment."

Trying to soothe his heart, he climbed to the top of a hill in the forest, and the world that unfolded before Goffin’s eyes was...

"Haha..."

Brilliant gold reflected by the sun.

'Of course.'

With a towering castle at its center, human civilization stretched to the horizon.

That's why he said sorry.
