Chapter 585:

After passing through a deep underground tunnel, Fermi found a fist-sized orb emitting blue electricity floating in midair.

Fermi stared at the orb.

It was an electrical encryption device, rare even in Radum, and used for security levels above grade 2 at the Magic Association.

"Money is draining fast."

Regulation-External Transaction - Depreciation.

A chip containing the 1.7 billion gold information magic, Indicator, popped into his palm.

Swallowing it and casting the spell, the orb's electricity spread into a curtain, revealing its internal circuitry.

From here, it was purely a matter of brainpower, but the encryption system wasn't overly complex compared to its security level.

'No information magic specialists, I see.'

It wasn't easy, but after about 30 minutes of fumbling, Fermi finally succeeded in decrypting it.

The orb glowed red, revealing a completely different scene.

The massive cave interior looked as if it had been carved out, with skulls embedded in the walls, and at the end of the snake-like cave, several figures were conversing over drinks.

They were aware of Fermi's presence but didn't seem to care.

'This is chilling.'

Even without the security device, the cave's atmosphere was as sharp as a blade.

Having experienced many battles, Fermi could tell that anyone here was at the pinnacle of their field.

'Well…'

It wasn't surprising.

The person waiting for Fermi was a man at the pinnacle of time magic.

"You finally came. I acknowledge your resourcefulness."

"Even though I'm on the run, I didn't expect you to be hiding in a place like this."

Smiling, Fermi walked towards the man with bandages over his eyes.

"Armin, the Eternal Seeker."

Armin, who had left the Ivory Tower, had joined forces with Miro and was on a mission with several others.

"How did you find me?"

Fermi, the son of the formidable Yolga and a genius, still found the situation puzzling.

"Honestly, I heard from Sane."

'Sane.'

Also known as Snake in the Black Line, he was helping Miro.

'If Sane sent Fermi, it means approval?'

"First, about the deal we discussed…"

As Fermi approached the table where Armin sat, he suddenly stopped.

One more centimeter, and he would have been dead.

'Wow.'

The person exuding a type of murderous intent he'd never experienced before was a one-armed swordsman leaning against the wall behind Armin.

Once an instructor at Kaizen Sword School, Parka Kuan was now working with Armin, preparing for the upcoming war.

"Sit."

Only after Armin's permission did the murderous aura recede.

"…Yes."

Fermi answered honestly and pulled out a chair.

'Phew, adults.'

Though Fermi feared nothing in the world, it was best to act like a well-behaved magic school student at this moment.

'These are my aunt's friends.'

The so-called Miro's lineup.

Including Sane and the former Magic Association president Gaold, who was currently in hiding, it was a formidable squad.

'My mother had them too.'

Yolga's colleagues, whom Fermi called uncles, were also there.

'If not for that incident…'

Feeling Fermi's cold gaze, Armin asked.

"You're here for the deal, right?"

"The promised funds are ready. Just need your signature."

Fermi pulled out the depreciation transaction contract.

"The term is one week. The deal amount is 100 billion gold. Three-year installment plan."

Armin looked at the contract.

Fermi's talent was just as Miro had described.

'But…'

He wasn't sure if it was the right thing to do.

Most of all, he didn't understand why Miro had allowed Fermi to investigate Istas.

"You must keep your promise."

The anxiety in Fermi's voice, uncharacteristic of him, indicated he was in an uncontrollable situation.

"Miro knows about this."

Though the promise between Fermi and Armin was made long ago, Miro had been informed.

"Are you prepared to take responsibility? You could die. Even if you survive, having Miro as an enemy won't be good."

Fermi's smile faded.

"Could you have said the same thing 19 years ago?"

"……"

"I just want to know for sure. Who originally owned what my aunt had."

After a moment of thought, Armin picked up a pen and signed, making Fermi's eyes sparkle.

"As promised, I signed."

The deal, which seemed impossible when a much younger Fermi had proposed it, was now complete.

"Thank you."

Casting the regulation-external spell, a golden chip was summoned.

It was the time-stopping magic, Stop.

As the fourth scramble was summoned and Iruki and Hershey's hands dwindled, the probability of random cards fluctuated wildly.

'Why are they spreading their cards?'

Hershey's question was answered when she opened the next random card.

'Another black.'

It was supposed to be a 78% chance for white.

'Of course, I can't ignore the 22% chance. But if this keeps happening…'

It was clear probability manipulation.

"I see. They're not accepting Kang."

"Dorothy, Kang."

As Iruki called Kang, Dorothy's hand, ○○○ (Religion), was reshuffled after a second.

"If you don't choose Kang or Pao, the reshuffled cards don't mix with the random cards."

Hearing Hershey, Pisho checked the status of the master card.

"Sabina is destroying scrambles. Our speed is superior, but we won't gain much this way."

"We're about even. There are about 40 scrambles left, including those collected."

The final decision would come at the very end.

"No. If that's the case, we win. Eventually, the probabilities will stabilize."

As the chess pieces dwindled, so did the variables.

"The winner of this game is Kaiden."

A smile spread across Hershey's face.

Though the fifth scramble had been summoned for a while, it no longer mattered to Amy and Kaiden.

"Uooooh!"

Each time Kaiden's cross sword spun like a windmill, a cold wave swept around.

Amy surrounded herself with Fire Mist, but this time, a blade-like wind similar to Air Cut sliced through the fire barrier.

A master who could incorporate all attributes into swordsmanship.

'Even with such talent…'

Was it the fate of the Red Cross Star to be frustrated?

"Don't talk nonsense!"

Amy's Red Eyes glowed, and her firepower exploded.

'One Path.'

Piercing through concentration again, she entered a temporary state of samadhi, her body blazing like a torch.

"What?"

No mage could withstand temperatures over 1,000 degrees Celsius.

'Vacuum.'

A magic combining air and fire attributes.

Amy's secret technique, Flame Seal, created a vacuum inside the flames, blocking heat convection.

"But you can't block radiant heat."

Amy could only maintain Flame Seal for about 10 minutes.

"That's enough to take down an idiot like you."

"Idiot?"

"Don't you think you're selfish? Everyone has a burden in life. But that doesn't mean you should give up and ruin others' lives."

"Maybe."

As Kaiden placed his hand on the cross sword, cold air rose.

"But do you know? The weight of my destiny is heavier than that of all humans."

'That's what makes you an idiot!'

Amy's charge looked like a giant fireball rolling towards him.

"Foolish. Trying close combat with a swordsman."

It was the first time Amy had closed the distance in their battles.

"Really?"

Amy's flames surged uncontrollably, becoming a giant of fire that engulfed Kaiden.

'Not magic?'

The cross sword cut through the giant, but the freezing energy vanished without resistance.

'Ifrit.'

Another battery equipped to Amy.

The ancient spirit Jack O' Lantern had evolved into the fire demon through the energy of One Path.

"Fire Fist!"

Ifrit's fists, made of fire smoke, split into dozens and swung.

Boom!

With the explosion of flames, the sixth and final scramble of black and white was summoned.

'It's done.'

Shirone calmly opened his eyes.

As midnight approached, the time energy of Four Symbols Explosion distorted the scenery beyond recognition.

'100 quintillion.'

This was the final position Shirone's sequence formula had reached.

He had hoped for something extraordinary, but as expected, nothing earth-shattering happened.

The number 100 quintillion was great only on a human scale, just a tiny segment of natural numbers.

But it was enough time energy to subdue Eden.

As he stood, the scenery returned to normal, and the incarnation of the Light Angel rose to the treetops.

"Eden…"

Checking the master card, Shirone searched for Eden in areas without scrambles.

Using the spirit zone to narrow the radius like hunting prey, he finally sensed Eden.

She likely sensed him too, but she neither ran nor hid.

"…You're here."

Shirone checked Eden's status.

'No master card.'

Eden also looked up at Shirone's incarnation.

"Is this the true nature of your time control ability?"

"I've thought a lot."

Shirone approached Eden.

"And I've concluded. Eden, your omnipotence isn't absolute. It's just strong."

"Humans tend to belittle what they can't reach. They fear the unknown. That's why people deny the existence of God."

"If God truly exists, why try to prove it?"

"Is revealing existence proof?"

"You deny Gaold."

"He denied God first."

"That's why it's not perfect."

"I don't understand. If you have another plan…"

"You might die."

Shirone interrupted.

"The reason I'm saying this is because I don't want to kill you. Give up now. Your omnipotence isn't absolute. If you take my attack in that state…"

Shirone was sincere.

"You could really die."

Eden shook her head with a pitying expression.

"Is this the best you could come up with? Trying to shake my resolve?"

"Listen carefully, Eden."

"No, fine. Let's say you're right."

Eden's barrier glowed with a holy light.

"Let's say my omnipotence isn't absolute. Even so, God is watching over me. There's no arrogance. Even if I'm not perfect, there's no problem in defeating you."

Eden spread her arms wide.

"If I'm not perfect, I'll dodge. That's another solution God has given me."

"You won't be able to dodge."

Shirone gritted his teeth.

"Once I start something, I guarantee you won't be able to escape. This is your last warning. Give up now."

"How arrogant humans are."

Despite Shirone's earnest words, Eden's gaze remained calm.

"Do you think controlling time makes you a god? Not at all."

Eden's barrier flared to its brightest ever.

"I have an invincible space granted by God."

"…Understood."

As soon as he finished speaking, the Light Angel merged with Shirone's body, and time energy began to spread.

"Please don't resent me."

Before the name of God.

Time and space were about to clash.
