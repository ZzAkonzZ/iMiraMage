Chapter 1060: Graveyard of Information (3)

Marsha returned to the main topic.

"Anyway, it's impossible to mine in this state. Even if we drill down 480 meters, finding evidence from a specific time period is nearly impossible."

Shirone rested his chin on his hand.

'Fermi is right. This is a gamble worth taking. If we can predict the future…'

Suddenly, he thought of something.

"Then what about the drug Angel? Is there another way to treat the emotional disease?"

Fermi replied.

"According to the evidence I've collected, the World Health Organization hasn't released a cure for the emotional disease."

It was a despairing statement.

"But…"

Shirone looked around and waved his arms.

"A cell buster occurred. If humanity was wiped out by the emotional disease, why would such a disaster be necessary?"

"Listen to the end. It seems humanity didn't go extinct because of the emotional disease. In other words, they didn't fail to develop a cure; they no longer needed one."

Shirone finally realized.

"They… gave up on emotions?"

"Yes. Future humans have a completely different emotional system from us. You know about the Giyorgi, right?"

Shirone nodded.

"What's taken from others isn't precious. Therefore, if you live with plundered goods, the emotional disease doesn't manifest. Future humans own nothing and just take. In this state, human unification is impossible."

"That's like beasts."

Wild dogs.

"But they're free from the emotional disease. That's what pain is. And this is the landscape of the end of the century that humanity will reach."

Fermi continued.

"The drug I made based on that information is Angel's Tears, Angel. It's a kind of hallucinogen. Why a drug? Because we need to lower the current human mind to the level of beasts."

Shirone remained silent.

"We went through countless trials and errors to find the golden balance. It took astronomical funds just to establish a distribution network on the southern continent. Accept it. This is reality."

"But… we can't report it that way."

The World Health Organization can't announce that all humanity must become wild dogs.

'No, even if they did, it wouldn't go as planned. It's thorough natural selection. Those with emotions will be eliminated over a long time.'

Fermi was preventing that process.

'Angel's Tears.'

Shirone imagined an angel shedding tears while injecting drugs into a human's arm.

Was that angel Fermi?

Or was it just the naming sense of a genius businessman who could sell sand in the desert?

Shirone couldn't tell.

"There must be a way. After all, it's not an incurable disease. Let's go down. We'll mine everything."

Marsha shrugged, and Fermi, exchanging glances with her, said,

"I knew you'd like it. But how do we go down? The mucus keeps regenerating."

Shirone cast the Hand of God.

"We'll dig out the exploration points. You can search while I handle the mucus."

"Mucus isn't just a simple substance. It's operated by Digital Ra, a quantum computer capable of thought. If you remove one ton of mucus, it will instantly fill with one ton. Or maybe even ten or a hundred times more."

Fermi raised his index finger.

"And one more thing. This is the world of information. Unlike reality, we can't deploy massive manpower or set up large-scale construction facilities. We barely managed to get this excavator by commissioning an Under Coder expert."

Marsha interjected.

"We were just talking about that. As long as the mucus regenerates with intent, it's difficult and dangerous to succeed in mining through external factors."

"External factors."

As Shirone bit his lip and fell into thought, Fermi crossed his arms and rested his chin.

"A way to deceive the mucus…"

Fermi suddenly looked up, and Shirone quickly turned to him.

They spoke simultaneously.

"Virus."

Marsha blinked.

"Huh? Virus? Like a cold?"

Not entirely wrong, but Shirone knew another use of the term.

"It's a kind of program. It infiltrates the main system and disrupts data. If we can plant a virus in Digital Ra, mining might be possible."

"Hmm, attacking from the inside, huh? But is it possible? It's supposed to be an incredibly capable machine."

Fermi said,

"We only need to change a few minor data points. For example, slowing down the mucus regeneration rate…"

"Aha."

Shirone added,

"Or freezing or hardening it. We don't need to paralyze the system. Just a minor error that Digital Ra won't notice."

Freeman asked,

"Is that really possible? This is the end of the world, but it's technologically superior. Who could create a program to disrupt Digital Ra?"

Fermi squinted one eye.

"There's one person who comes to mind. But it's a bit tricky. We're not personally acquainted."

Shirone jumped in.

"Is that the problem now? Who is it? Let me meet them. I'll persuade them somehow."

"You?"

Fermi stared at Shirone for a long time before suddenly puffing his cheeks and bursting into laughter.

"Pfft!"

Shirone's ears turned red.

"What's with the mocking laugh? I'm offering to help."

"No, sorry. I just couldn't help it, thinking about you, always 100% serious, doing this."

"What are you talking about? What is it?"

"Hmm."

Fermi calmed his ticklish lungs and smiled as he spoke.

"Have you ever heard of High Gear?"

Virtual Memory Zone.

In a dark space, two bluish circles appeared, and light rose in a cylindrical shape.

Shirone and Fermi emerged from each circle.

"Where is this…?"

As Shirone looked around, Fermi stepped into the darkness.

"It's a special exit for Abyss Walkers. If I had known earlier, I wouldn't have had to go through all that trouble."

"This is an exit?"

Shirone had only pressed the button on the pocket watch Fermi gave him twice quickly.

"It's one of Dream Star's new features. Pressing the button sends you to Under Coder's virtual memory. In reality, it's like recalling a memory, but it's not an actual memory. It's a false memory created by the hallucinogen's effect."

"So I'm in a hallucination right now. And the person who developed all this is…"

"The Operator. The first designer and operator of High Gear, and also the top-ranked user. Actually, I'm not even sure if they're human. Under Coder can be accessed through Dreamo, and with mental transmission, you can come from the end of the universe."

"But you said it was a person?"

'Sharp guy.'

It wasn't information that needed to be hidden, but he didn't like having his thoughts read this way.

"There are a few clues to assume. Most importantly, the combat base of High Gear is in human form. Non-logical forms are possible, but the balance is particularly immature. Of course, some specific stats rise tremendously, making it popular among enthusiasts."

"What exactly is High Gear?"

From what Shirone had heard, it was all about modifying the body to fight in a virtual world.

"It's better to see it for yourself. No matter how much I explain, the core won't be conveyed."

Shirone puffed his cheeks at the condescending tone, but Fermi held the initiative for now.

As they exited to the middle layer of Under Coder, Fermi looked back at Shirone.

"High Gear content isn't in the middle layer. We have to go much deeper."

"Deeper? The Abyss?"

"Not exactly. I mean it's illegal. We call it Floor Coder."

The fact that it wasn't in Omega's memory meant it was created after Shirone was born.

"Illegal? Who cares about that here?"

"The Dreamers."

"Oh…"

"All Floor Coder content uses Dreamo's modules. It's incredibly convenient for developers. It not only feels like reality but also automatically corrects difficult-to-implement parts."

The mystery of dreams.

"Actually, much of Floor Coder's content is provocative and decadent, with low completion. On the other hand, High Gear is a masterpiece that incorporates all of Floor Coder's strengths. It's hard to access, requiring a special Dream Star for Under Coder. If synchronization isn't perfect, it shocks the brain."

Fermi placed his hand in a gap between mirrors installed in the middle layer.

"Information magic, Information."

As the magic circle rotated, a rectangular gap appeared on the wall, and a red door emerged.

Shirone watched from a step behind.

'It's not a mirror.'

Just the different communication protocol hinted at what kind of place Floor Coder was.

Inside, the hallway branched out randomly, with various doors installed.

"But can we just go? You said High Gear needs a special Dream Star."

"It's fine. That's what I gave you."

Shirone's eyes narrowed.

"You knew this would happen from the start?"

"Of course. You've followed me to the Abyss before. You were just as passionate as today. Though it's your first time in Floor Coder."

Fermi stopped and puffed his cheeks in a smile.

"Curious? Want me to tell you?"

Shirone scoffed and turned his head.

"No thanks. I'd rather trust myself, even if I don't remember."

"Haha."

Fermi stopped in front of a sliding door reminiscent of an old castle in the real world.

"Here it is."

He opened the door to reveal only darkness, and Shirone followed him inside.

As the door closed, the interior of a remodeled castle unfolded before their eyes.

-Welcome! Welcome to the battlefield of High Gear, the playground of the universe!

Shirone looked around.

Except for someone sitting at a distant counter, it was eerily empty.

"There's no one here but us? You said it was a hit simulation."

"It's because it's your first time. The first person to link to High Gear can use the tutorial content. Usually, they go to a previously saved battlefield."

"Again, I'm not here for this."

"I know. But it's good to know. Talking to the Operator is like pulling teeth. They never have personal conversations unless it's about system-related suggestions."

"You said they're a top-ranked player, number one. Can't you talk to them in the battlefield or something?"

"I'd like to, but they're on the opposing faction."

Shirone didn't understand why that mattered.

"See, I told you the core wouldn't be conveyed. Anyway, I don't know them personally either."

"Then how do you know them?"

"The conditional auction market of Under Coder. You can't run such a precise world for free, right? I bought a lot of the Operator's items."

"How much?"

"Hmm, in real-world value, about 2,000 trillion gold."

Shirone's eyes widened.

"What? 2,000 trillion?"

"Haha! Why so surprised? Do you still believe money actually exists?"

"Not exactly, but…"

"Anyway, the reason for the beginner link is to meet the tutorial manager. It's also a transaction, so maybe they'll connect us to the Operator."

Fermi's work was always swift, but Shirone still had questions.

'…Why does it matter that they're on the opposing faction?'
