Chapter 1194:

Since the vagrants were already being hunted by the Three Great Gangs, they had nothing to hide.

"The most powerful faction is the Desperado organization…"

For the next ten minutes, the leader detailed the gang territories, their hierarchy, and even the names of their bosses.

Eden commented,

"The gang leaders are wanted criminals in the real world. But with all these constant battles, how do they never run out of food? The corpses of the dead shouldn’t be enough to sustain them."

"I don’t know the full details," the leader admitted. "But they seem to have a source of supplies."

He pointed toward the ocean.

"This whole area is filled with trash and pollution. You can only catch rotten fish. There’s no real open sea beyond here. But strangely, from time to time, large ships dock at the island. I think that’s where they get their supplies. In fact… you could say the entire gang war is about controlling that."

"Ships?"

Since the outer track was spatially separated, there was never a need for transportation between locations.

‘The underworld… As expected, someone here is in direct contact with the administrators.’

The leader continued.

"Honestly, food is secondary. The real supply is weapons—things called machine guns."

"Machine guns?"

"Yeah. They’re weird as hell. They make a loud noise, and when you pull the trigger, it just…"

He mimicked a rattling sound with his mouth.

Watching him, Shirone recalled High Gear.

‘Firearms…’

They didn’t match the essence of magic, but they could kill effortlessly.

And they made it easier to suppress guilt.

‘When objects change, so does the philosophy of an era. These things fit Melkidu perfectly.’

Nade asked,

"Alright, I get the situation. But why did you guys keep betraying gangs?"

"I used to have it all," the leader admitted bitterly. "Before I lost everything at the gambling house. I sneaked in here to escape the guards, but if you don’t join a gang, you’re just food. So I had no choice but to join. At first, I joined Desperado but—"

"But?"

"It was terrifying, so I ran away," the leader confessed. "Those guys aren’t human. If they don’t like something, they just pull the trigger. So I switched to Red Union, but it was full of lunatics too. Then I went to Johan Cartel…"

His face turned pale.

"It’s just… hell. All three gangs are hell. You don’t get it. I didn’t betray them—I escaped. If you join a different gang, they protect you, but now I have nowhere left to go. That’s why I live here."

"That’s even more pathetic," Nade muttered.

"Haha…"

Perhaps reminiscing about his former status, the leader suddenly straightened his back.

"You’ll understand once you see them. I’m warning you—those guys…"

He leaned in toward Nade.

"They’re real monsters."

As if he was trying to convince himself, his eyes sharpened with intensity.

Nade responded by slapping him.

"Ow! Why’d you hit me!?"

"Are you serious? You’re no different. You were about to mug us just a minute ago."

Nade dusted himself off and stood up.

"So, what’s the plan? Should we hit Desperado first since they’re the biggest?"

Eden interjected,

"That’s fine, but we don’t know where Penna is. Let’s head in and assess the situation first."

"Good point."

Nade turned back to the vagrants.

"You guys should go. And don’t get into trouble again. Just roll the dice and get out of here."

"Uh…"

The leader, still kneeling, crawled forward slightly.

"I have an idea. Would you consider forming a gang? I’d gladly serve you as my boss."

Even though machine guns were powerful, they were nothing compared to magic, especially electricity-based spells.

‘If we use these guys, we could take over all three gangs easily. Then I could…’

Nade stomped on his hopes.

"Forget it. We’ll handle this ourselves."

"Wait, just hear me out. With my knowledge of the three gangs, we could—"

"Listen carefully," Nade cut him off.

"Don’t ever let me see you again. We’re letting you go because we have things to do. Now get lost."

Knowing they had nothing to lose, the leader tried one last gamble.

"This isn’t a good idea. We have nothing to lose. Are you prepared for that? We’ll use every trick and strategy to—"

"Are you still here?"

Electricity flickered in Nade’s eyes.

"Ahhhhh!"

The vagrants screamed as their bodies convulsed and they scrambled away, practically crawling across the sand.

Relaxing, Nade sighed.

"These weaklings only learn the worst habits. If you’re going to threaten someone, at least pick the right target."

Nade, Iruki, and Eden were all strong, but Shirone was the strongest magician in the world.

"Alright, let’s go. I’m starving."

As Iruki led the way, Eden muttered behind him.

"I’m not eating any meat."

Past the heaps of trash, the heart of the island revealed itself—a grim cityscape.

Buildings were riddled with bullet holes, some half-collapsed and abandoned.

The main roads were controlled by armed enforcers, while beggars and vagrants huddled in the alleys.

"Each gang must control different areas. But Red Union’s emblem is the most common."

"Yeah. Some guys have two or even three tattoos. Must be a lot of deserters like those vagrants earlier."

"What about that place?"

Eden pointed to a neon-lit sign flickering even in the daytime.

Her friends nodded in agreement.

"A bar. Probably one of the gang’s public spaces. Hopefully, they sell food."

"But we can’t use points here," Iruki reminded them. "We’d have to trade items or offer labor."

"Let’s check it out. We might be able to trade something cheap."

As they entered the bar, the gang members, all shirtless, turned their heads simultaneously.

"What the hell is that?"

Seeing new faces, especially young ones, in Corkolas immediately lowered their suspicion.

'It's my turn.'

A rookie from Red Union, who had just joined recently, raised a machine gun at the group.

"Hey, who the hell are you? Which gang do you belong to?"

"I just have a question," Shirone stated bluntly.

The rookie smirked maliciously.

"Heh, then you came to the right place. Ask anything. But the price might be steep."

His gaze turned toward Eden.

"Alright, what I want is—"

"Hah…"

Realizing that this wouldn’t be solved with words, Nade was about to step in when a voice called out from the second floor.

"Stop."

A Red Union enforcer, looking irritated, stared down at the hall.

"O-oh! Boss! You're here."

The rookie bowed respectfully, but the enforcer’s eyes flashed with hostility.

"Boss, why—"

"Enough. Bring them up."

"Come on up."

As Shirone’s group climbed the stairs and entered a private room, the rookie tilted his head in confusion.

"Why is the boss so pissed?"

Whack!

A senior gang member smacked him across the back of the head.

"Ow! What was that for, senior?"

"Shut up and sit down. Drink your damn booze. You just used up the luck of your entire life."

Rubbing his head, the rookie asked,

"Do you know them?"

"Nope. Never seen them before. But I can tell that messing with them would be a disaster."

"They just seem like harmless kids, though."

"Don't look at their faces. Look at their eyes."

The rookie blinked.

"Uh… What?"

"Guys like us do whatever we need to survive. We have no pride, no shame. Do you know what kind of eyes we have?"

"Uh… Gangster eyes?"

"Beast’s eyes."

The senior downed a strong shot of liquor in one go.

"Our eyes are murky but sad, cold but filled with despair. But they’re different. Those are the eyes of people who never broke, who didn’t give up when everything went to hell. Instead, they climbed over it."

He let out a bitter chuckle.

"Guys like us need to be careful around those eyes. We might not be afraid of anything, but we should be afraid of the world itself, you dumbass."

The rookie turned his head.

"What does that even mean?"

The senior just poured himself another drink in silence.

"Forgive my rudeness."

In the VIP room, the Red Union enforcer poured drinks for Shirone’s group.

"This is the finest liquor in the house."

Shirone ignored it.

"Has anyone named Penna come here? Or has your gang recruited a woman recently?"

The enforcer raised an eyebrow.

"Are you with the police?"

As Shirone’s group remained silent, he continued.

"She is not part of Red Union. And according to my sources, none of the Three Great Gangs have recruited a woman recently. Unless she disguised her gender."

"Hmm… So she’s not with a gang."

The enforcer nodded.

"I will prepare dinner. Our boss will be arriving soon. You should meet him before you leave. We will treat you properly."

They didn’t have time for that.

"That won’t be necessary. I just have one more question—about the bribery program."

The enforcer smiled.

"I have nothing to tell you about that. But our boss might."

That might have been a good approach, but Penna’s safety was more important.

'If she’s not with a gang, that means she’s on the streets. If she’s been hiding for three days…'

Shirone stood up.

"We’ll be leaving for now. We’ll come back later."

Knowing there was no way to stop them, the enforcer bowed slightly.

"Please do return. And one more thing… Do not make deals with any other organization."

He lifted his head.

"I know you are strong. But moths always choose the flame over their own lives."

That meant they were willing to fight to the death.

"We’ll think about it."

Not bothering to listen further, Shirone’s group left their untouched drinks behind and exited the room.

As they left, the enforcer leaned back and lit a cigarette.

"Dammit…"

A message that had been passed down in Corkolas for a long time suddenly echoed in his mind.

"Why now…?"

Shirone’s group walked through the streets.

"What the hell happened to Penna? Are we sure she even came here? Maybe she got scared and turned back."

It wasn’t impossible.

"It’s too early to jump to conclusions. If she didn’t join a gang, then we should—"

"You guys!"

Someone in a hood suddenly latched onto Shirone’s arm.

"Ahh! What the hell!?"

The overwhelming stench made Shirone instinctively recoil, but then he saw the face under the hood.

"…Penna?"

Her expression crumpled into an ugly pout.

"Hng, why were you so late!? I was scared to death!"

"… Are you alright?"

She had probably been hiding in trash piles the entire time. Not only did she reek, but her entire body was covered in grime.

"Do I look alright!? I haven’t eaten in three days! I couldn’t even… use the bathroom properly!"

Nade looked at her in disbelief.

"Why the hell would you do that? At least find a toilet."

"And risk getting caught!? The best thing to do in this kind of situation is nothing at all. If you guys didn’t show up, I was dead either way! I didn’t move a single thing except my fingers and toes."

It was the ultimate survival tactic for the physically weakest—but the fact that she could actually pull it off proved she was tough in her own way.

"You did well. Let’s get out of here."

Penna sniffled.

"I'm so miserable… You owe me a meal! I overheard a lot of good intel too! I can order something expensive, right?"

Nade muttered,

"Shouldn’t you take a bath first? You’re killing my appetite."

"You’re so rude to a lady!"

Penna threw her cloak at him, wrapping Nade up like a struggling ghost.

"Ahhh! Why won’t this thing come off!?"

"Puhahaha!"

More than anything, they were relieved that Penna was safe.
