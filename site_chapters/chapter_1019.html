Chapter 1019: Underwater Work (3)

Shirone was not in a good mood.

‘An unprovoked attack?’

Honestly, it was a chilling aura, and the destructive power contained within it must have been beyond imagination.

‘The ship almost sank.’

It was fortunate that he suppressed it before the battle started; otherwise, the passengers' lives would have been in danger.

Shirone's unusual display of killing intent was partly due to his anger over this.

‘Foreign Minister Cairns.’

Although he knew roughly through Omega, Cairns was quite young.

‘The records I remember of Cairns are up to when he was twelve. Of course, he wasn't ordinary even then.’

He couldn't even imagine how he had evolved over the next 22 years.

Shirone massaged his eyes.

“I'm tired.”

He had no intention of getting deeply involved in politics, but Langi was on his mind.

‘He called her an object. Referring to a person.’

Even during the World Beauty Pageant, she entertained high-ranking officials, so it wasn't strange in itself.

However, the smile she showed at the end was genuine, without a trace of falsehood.

‘Throwing one's heart.’

Isn't that reason enough to get involved in politics?

Arachne's sailing ship.

“I'm sorry.”

In a large room decorated like a palace, Cairns was drinking with dancers.

“Do you even know how to say that?” Among the dancers with friendly smiles, Cairns' eyes were as cold as ice.

Benahar bowed his head again.

“I was mistaken. Shirone's prowess in person is beyond imagination.”

“How do you know? You didn't even fight him.”

Half of it was true.

If a battle had actually taken place, it might have been a good fight, but now it was meaningless.

‘His spirit is broken. That man's mind… is not human.’

No matter how many life-threatening situations, compressing a human's lifetime into war, it was an impossible level of mental strength.

Regardless of skill level, those who could ‘fight’ Shirone were mentally trained to an extraordinary degree.

‘Trained? No, insane. Only in the realm of madness could one confront Shirone.’

“Anyway, it's fine.”

Cairns didn't dwell on a lost cause.

“We'll just have to wait for the right time. You can go back now. And call Langi.”

“Well…”

Several facts collided in Benahar's mind, making him speak with a troubled expression.

“Things got a bit complicated.”

Cairns, who had been looking at Benahar with one eyebrow raised, gave a meaningless sneer.

That evening.

Shirone left the work to Poine and headed alone to Arachne's sailing ship.

Flying through the air, he easily crossed the 700-meter distance and saw the deck where a banquet was taking place.

They were all officials, and a feast of delicacies that couldn't be found on other ships continued.

Landing among the people drinking under the cozy moonlight in the windless zone, Shirone sighed.

“Phew.”

They weren't surprised by the magic.

They were just displeased by the fact that he had broken the mutual treaty and invaded the ship through the air.

“What are you? Do you know where you are…”

As a man with a medal tried to move, a guard magician stopped him.

“Deputy Minister, that's Shirone.”

“The Five Great Stars?”

Though his abilities were far inferior to Cairns, the deputy minister had the weapon of experience.

“Hmm, I see the star of the Ivory Tower.”

Even if he was a thorn in the side of politicians, there was no benefit in antagonizing the Ivory Tower.

At least not until the Holy War began.

“I came to see Cairns. Where is he now?”

‘A young man calling the minister by name?’

He wasn't one to be provoked by trivial matters, but he had to address official business.

“He's not attending the banquet, so he must be in his quarters. But what is this about?”

“I have a few questions. If you won't guide me, step aside.”

“Phew.”

As the wine he had enjoyed rose, the deputy minister exhaled deeply, releasing his intoxication.

“Well, fine. I shouldn't interfere with the star of the Ivory Tower's business. But as I understand it, the maritime exchange was first proposed by you, Shirone. And Arachne gladly accepted. But now…”

“Who ordered the banquet?”

The deputy minister frowned.

“The agreement on the inventory check is concluded. Do you have a problem with consuming our own food?”

“On the deck?”

The deputy minister raised his glass and looked at the night sky.

“Well… the weather is nice, and the moon is bright.”

As Shirone's eyes hardened, he straightened his posture and continued.

“I understand what you mean. Other ships can't eat as abundantly. But we are an elite force on a national mission. We have our routines and clear goals. This kind of emotional approach doesn't excuse breaking the treaty.”

As expected of the deputy minister of foreign affairs, his psychological warfare and eloquence were strong.

“A treaty.”

Thinking of what Benahar had done earlier that day, Shirone was exasperated.

“Fine. I apologize for breaking the treaty.”

The nobles murmured.

“Well, no.”

The deputy minister tried to suppress a smile, but his shoulders tensed.

“This minor issue, right? The relationship between the Ivory Tower and Arachne isn't…”

Seeing Shirone's gaze, the deputy minister's heart sank.

“Then let's hear from the minister how well the treaty is being kept. There shouldn't be any serious issues, right? But if there's a severe breach of trust…”

The nobles couldn't even blink.

“Don't expect this ship to be afloat by tomorrow morning.”

Turning to leave, Shirone saw the deputy minister's hammer-struck expression and extended his hand.

“No, wait. Star, no, Shirone.”

“Enjoy the banquet.”

Shirone turned his head and scanned the people.

“Don't let anyone leave. Stay here until I return to avoid any trouble.”

Benahar came out, opening the door to the quarters.

“What’s going on?”

The moment he saw Shirone, memories of the events from earlier in the day came flooding back, causing him to break out in a cold sweat.

“Oh, Shirone.”

“As I mentioned earlier, I’m here to see the minister. I heard he’s in his quarters.”

Benahar glanced at the deputy minister behind Shirone.

‘No, no!’

The deputy minister silently mouthed words while making a throat-slitting gesture with his hand.

‘Don’t let him see the minister. Make up any excuse—illness, anything!’

Benahar understood the gist but had no choice.

“Come, I’ll guide you.”

The deputy minister, knowing Benahar’s personality, looked at him in disbelief but couldn’t meet his eyes.

As the door closed, the remaining nobles felt a chill despite the warm southern climate.

“...Damn it.”

“Please forgive what happened earlier. I didn’t recognize who you were and acted rashly.”

Benahar made sure his mistake wouldn’t interfere with Cairns’ work.

But as a magician, he also held a certain respect for Shirone.

“Someone could have been hurt. If you have a problem with me, call me separately next time.”

“...That won’t happen again.”

When the door opened, Cairns and Langi were sitting side by side at a table in a luxurious dining room.

“Langi?”

Shirone asked, but Benahar just bowed and held the door handle.

“Have a good time.”

As Shirone approached, Cairns stood up.

“Welcome. It’s an honor to meet the star of the Ivory Tower. As the rumors say, quite a handsome man…”

“Langi, what’s going on?”

Shirone didn’t intend to ignore Cairns, but it was clear that calling Langi wasn’t out of goodwill.

“Haha! You mentioned having a prior engagement. Since Langi and I aren’t strangers, I thought it would be nice to have her join us. I apologize if it upset you.”

“Shirone…”

Langi looked more beautiful than usual, but her heart’s window was already closed.

“Let’s hear what he has to say first.”

As Shirone took a seat opposite Cairns, staff brought in food as if they had been waiting.

“There’s a banquet outside…”

“I apologize!”

Cairns placed his hands on the table and bowed, causing the staff to freeze.

“It’s all my fault. I apologize for what happened earlier, the banquet, and breaking the treaty. I’ll take full responsibility.”

It was clear he understood Shirone’s intentions without being told, which was impressive, but…

“How will you take responsibility?”

Words are meaningless without action.

“We’ll share our food. We’re also guests here, so we should share. We’ll agree to an inventory check. You can use our forces for security if needed.”

“Hmm.”

Shirone crossed his legs.

“What is Arachne aiming for in this voyage?”

“I’ll be honest. As you know, Dionas is a nation founded by pirates. The king rules with an iron fist, and most of the population survives through maritime plunder. We want to officially recognize Dionas as a state of Cartesia in exchange for one thing.”

“And that one thing is?”

“Artifacts from the ancient civilization of Maika.”

The moment he finished speaking, Shirone felt a chill.

“What is that…”

When he searched the 5D cube containing all atomic movements since the beginning of time,

“What?”

There was no record.

“I don’t know the details. No one does. It’s a civilization that existed somewhere in the South Pacific long ago and is believed to be submerged now.”

“And Dionas knows about it?”

Cairns continued without showing his excitement.

“Cartesia is a remote part of the world, so it might be unfamiliar to you. But the Ten Kingdoms of the Archipelago have been investigating Maika’s artifacts for a long time. However… since the world war, Dionas has been suspicious. Even if they weren’t affected by tsunamis, it’s strange that they haven’t suffered from demon attacks either.”

How should he take this?

‘If it’s not in Omega, it doesn’t exist. Therefore, the Maika civilization is just a myth.’

…he thought, but then,

‘But even myths should be recorded. Why don’t I know about it?’

If the general public knew, but Shirone didn’t, it meant it was removed only from Omega.

‘Erased? No, that’s impossible. Even if reset, the erasure log remains.’

It wasn’t a logical deduction.

‘The outside world.’

The only thing left after removing all categories existing in Omega.

‘It’s somehow related to the outside world. Otherwise, it couldn’t bypass Omega.’

Having thought this far, Shirone asked,

“Even if Dionas found artifacts or traces, would they share them?”

“That’s why I’m going. Armed with immense treasures, weapons, and the most beautiful women.”

Things that would come out in an inventory check.

“You think that’s enough to persuade them? Even if they’re pirates, isn’t that underestimating them?”

“That might be true. But what if I told you that King Kalt of Dionas…”

Cairns pulled Langi closer by the waist.

“Is obsessed with Miss Arachne? To the point of attempting an armed invasion.”

After all, they were pirates.

“Of course, Langi is a valuable asset. But with the Holy War approaching, we must do our best.”

‘This is why I didn’t want to get involved in politics.’

To achieve what you want, you have to see many things you don’t want to.

“Let go. Langi is my friend too. I won’t forgive you for doing that in front of me.”

“She’s still mine for now.”

Cairns, pressing closer to Langi, spoke with a challenging look.

“She’s necessary for negotiations. But… if the Five Great Stars help, we might not need her.”

He thought it was over.

‘Nothing is impossible in this world. This way, even the Five Great Stars will come under me.’

“Cairns.”

Shirone let out a hollow laugh.

“Have you ever heard of the name Fermi?”

Cairns undoubtedly had exceptional abilities in understanding humans and techniques to disrupt systems, but…

“No, I haven’t.”

Compared to the drug lord who erased even the memory of their deals, he was like a newborn.

“You have a lot to learn from him.”
