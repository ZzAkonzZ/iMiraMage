Chapter 919: A Cold War (3)

Dante looked around the village.

Instead of walls and roofs, they followed a unique residential style called the Sun Path.

When the sun shone through the tangled trees, the ground sparkled like stars.

‘They eat food, but in extreme situations, they can survive on just water and sunlight.’

Of course, they never ate meat. As they walked along the Sun Path, where the ceiling was open, a smile bloomed on Plarino's face.

"Oh, look at this sunlight. How beautiful the sky is. Everyone, as you walk along this Sun Path..."

She wanted to share the joy of the Flower Tribe, but all she received was Lupist's cold gaze.

"S-sorry."

Contrary to her expectations, Lupist looked up at the sky.

"Do you like the sun?"

"What? Oh, of course. The sun is the greatest gift to the Flower Tribe."

"I like it too."

Plarino's mouth hung open.

"But I don't look up without reason. How many humans live thinking there's a sky?"

"Why? It's so nice. When you close your eyes and bask in the sunlight, you feel happy."

"Happy?"

Lupist sneered.

"Do you know what we first discarded to build the current system? Individual happiness. We use the suffering of the entire population as fuel."

A Flower Tribe man asked,

"What does that mean? If no one can be happy, why do it?"

"To dominate you."

"If we can be superior, humans will even give up happiness. It's madness for the highest. But now, a system more mad than ours has appeared."

The Flower Tribe didn't leave their territory, but they understood the situation through the small world window.

"The winner of the system will be determined by who is more insane. Understand? Humans live in such a world. But you talk about the sun and happiness."

Plarino lowered her head gloomily.

"We know we're a failed race. That's why we live in hiding."

"It's too early to conclude."

Plarino looked back in surprise as Lupist pointed to the sky with his index finger.

"Anyway... the weather is great."

How long had it been since he realized there was a sky and he could look at it?

‘Is it time for me to retire?’

When did he start getting sentimental?

"You're here."

In a sunny spot, a woman with black hair sat cross-legged.

‘That woman...’

Lupist stopped, smelling the rich scent of lotus flowers in the air.

"Is she a direct descendant of the Flower Tribe?"

The woman smiled and bowed her head.

"Yes. I am Protea of the Lotus lineage. Thank you for visiting our humble abode."

He had provided a place to live in exchange for the deal, but he hadn't paid much attention to who the chief was.

He had only heard reports that some Flower Tribe members had found their way to the village on their own.

‘The Lotus lineage. They survived.’

The Flower Tribe's origin was known to be the lotus flower.

"Long ago, an unknown Buddha sat on a lotus flower and meditated for 40,000 years."

Protea spoke.

"The lotus flower neither withered nor died, and it listened to the Buddha's teachings about human life."

Lupist continued.

"And on the day the Buddha left, the lotus flower also attained enlightenment and became human. That's the origin story of the Flower Tribe."

Though it's just a legend, it explains the diverse lineage of the Flower Tribe.

‘It couldn't have happened naturally. The first Flower Tribe must have spread through the small world window...’

Thus, the Lotus lineage before him was a direct descendant of the Flower Tribe who attained enlightenment from the Buddha.

‘Annoying.’

If it were any other Flower Tribe member, he could just intimidate them, but Protea was a formidable opponent.

"Yes. Mr. Lupist, our benefactor, what brings you here?"

"You know why."

With her direct lineage abilities, it wouldn't be an exaggeration to say she controlled the entire forest beyond the village.

"Hehe. Yes, actually... I understand your philosophy about the system. It seems you need to precisely control the living flower."

Dante admitted frankly.

"Yes. It's a supermassive structure. We've built an interface, but dozens of people are needed for precise work."

"On the other hand, with the small world window, one Flower Tribe member can control it down to the nerve level."

Lupist stepped forward.

"Then you understand quickly. If we lose, you won't survive either. It's best to cooperate."

"The Flower Tribe's decision is..."

The smile disappeared from Protea's face.

"Rejection."

Unexpected, but Lupist didn't hesitate.

"Then die."

As blades rose around her, Plarino screamed and jumped back.

"Your death won't change the strategy. If I carry your head around, the other Flower Tribe members will join."

Dante asked,

"Why? Half the world has already fallen to the demons. If humans lose, you won't be safe either."

"We'll die anyway."

The Flower Tribe's gaze focused on her.

"The living flower is an ancient weapon. If it requires the entire Flower Tribe, there must be at least hundreds of them. If such a powerful weapon can't stop the enemy, aren't we just sacrifices?"

Lupist didn't deny it.

"Humans will pay the same price. Betting everything to gain everything, that's war."

"It's a human war. Present a means to ensure the Flower Tribe's survival. Otherwise, cooperation is impossible."

The Flower Tribe members listening in the forest revealed themselves and activated the small world window.

The forest shook, and the sensation of nerve bundles wriggling beneath the ground was transmitted.

"Hmph."

As Lupist snorted, a blade larger than a person rose beside him.

"Ugh!"

Some Flower Tribe members frowned, but Protea didn't even blink.

"Intimidation is useless. You've controlled us so far, but we'll choose our own deaths."

"I-I'll do it."

All eyes focused on Plarino.

"I'll control the living flower, so don't fight. I've done it before, so I can do it well."

Protea glared.

"Plarino, what are you doing? This is a fight for the survival of the entire Flower Tribe. There's no choice."

"If there's no choice, we must fight. If humans lose, the Flower Tribe will be destroyed too."

"No, if we're going to be destroyed anyway, we won't help humans. Think about what they've done. They've toyed with your body and violated your mind! Humans are the most selfish!"

"I know."

How could she forget?

"But I think Mr. Lupist is different. He seems cold and rational, but if he were truly like that..."

Plarino's gaze turned to Lupist.

"He wouldn't have provided a village for the Flower Tribe from the beginning. This place is exactly what we wanted."

It was the perfect place.

"You're being deceived. He prepared for this situation to use us..."

"He said the weather was nice."

Plarino continued.

"I don't think someone who understands the warmth of sunlight doesn't know true happiness."

Her eyes welled up with tears.

"We have no choice but to fight. Not everyone can be happy, so we become colder, colder... But in truth, we all want everyone to be happy, right?"

"No. I'm naturally cold. And humans are evil. I'll do anything for my goal."

That's why Lupist found it difficult to deal with women.

‘They're emotional in strange ways. It's annoying.’

With an enemy approaching that he couldn't kill fast enough, what did it matter if someone was good or bad?

‘We have to fight first. Set the optimal strategy and execute it.’

But on the other hand...

He felt good that she understood the reason for fighting.

"Sigh."

As Lupist deactivated the Spirit Zone, Dante also withdrew his killing intent shortly after.

"Fine, let's resolve this through dialogue. We'll do our best to ensure your survival. Is that enough?"

"No. We need a definite guarantee."

Just as Lupist's temple veins bulged, a woman's voice came from the forest.

"I'll take care of that."

As Lupist and Dante looked towards the voice, their pupils moved rapidly.

‘Fast.’

Just the speed at which she moved out of sight raised their alertness to the limit.

Protea's eyes shook.

"You are..."

The woman had pointed ears, a pale face, and eyes as clear and pure as a spring.

"Hello?"

As she turned and waved brightly, Lupist recognized her face.

"Enox."

She was one of the Fairy Kings of the Holy War.

"Yes. The commander sent me, saying I could help."

Dante's eyes darkened.

‘If this was the plan, they could have told us in advance.’

As Protea knelt, the remaining Flower Tribe members followed suit.

"Master of the forest."

"It's been a while. The Flower Tribe still has a lovely scent."

Enox raised her index finger and said,

"Don't worry about the war. We elves will protect the Flower Tribe."

"...Are you saying the elves will join the war?"

"Of course."

"Why do you side with humans? They destroy the nature we've cherished."

"That's exactly it."

Enox caressed Protea's chin and said,

"Humans are thrilling."

That night.

In the royal castle of Tormia.

With King Adolf on sick leave, Lupist and Plu attended a royal banquet.

With Pony, an alumnus of Alpheas Magic School, present, various small talks were exchanged.

"The world situation has become dire. Many who invested in other countries have gone bankrupt."

Even in the midst of the war with the demons, the royals were obsessed with increasing their wealth.

‘The most unrealistic place.’

Lupist continued his meal silently.

‘Such things are necessary for the system.’

In a place completely detached from reality, where only ideals are shouted by components.

"By the way, this new secretary-general is quite cute. The last one was too fierce for my liking."

Plu smiled sweetly.

"Thank you for the compliment." Taking advantage of the lightened mood, Lupist made a difficult request.

"The demons are going to invade Tormia. We need to mobilize the royal guard."

"The royal guard?"

The air grew heavy as the royal guard was the last line of defense for the royals.

"Yes. Don't worry. We have the Flower Garden. We just need troops for support missions."

Everyone would die anyway.

If they were going to be annihilated, Lupist wanted to use everything he could.

"Well, if we have the Flower Garden, it's safe. Tormia has the world's strongest defense."

Lupist had achieved that.

"That aside..."

A royal with thick eyebrows pointed his fork alternately at Lupist and Plu.

"So, how's the bedroom compatibility? There were rumors before, you know. They say you're a secretary-general killer. Hahaha!"

The royals burst into laughter.

Regardless of the truth of the rumors, they just wanted to see the reactions of the head of the Magic Association and the secretary-general.

"Not particularly..."

Lupist's expression didn't change.

"It's not very fun. The previous secretary-general was much better."

"Oh? Really? Well, Plu is too skinny. Eat this and put on some weight."

Plu puffed out her cheeks.

"This is quite hurtful. The director's taste is strange. I can't even prove it."

"Why not? Leave it to me. My stamina is renowned worldwide."

"Eek! That's a bit scary."

As the royals laughed, only Pony remained silent.

‘It's a lie.’

Having attended the magic school, she knew.

The cold emotions hidden in the eyes of the two magicians disguising their laughter.

‘Professional magicians. But they're not political enough to cater to the royals. And the royal guard.’

A chill ran down Pony's spine.

‘Something huge is about to happen.’
