Chapter 790:

"Who are you?"

When Shirone opened the hotel room door at the sound of knocking, a woman he had never seen before stood there smiling.

"Who… are you?" He had to ask again. Her appearance demanded it.

"Hello, Mage! I'm Barho Langie, this year’s Miss Aracne."

"Miss Aracne?"

Her bright greeting slightly eased his guard, but her identity brought a new wave of tension.

"And what brings someone like you to me?"

Langie pulled out a copy of Spirit, a magazine from the Kingdom of Tormia, from who-knows-where and held it out.

"I'm a fan! You graduated top of your class from Alpheas Magic School and now work at the Ivory Tower, which only the greatest mages in the world can enter."

Shirone looked stunned.

"Hehe, I’m really interested in magic. If it's alright, could I please get your autograph?"

In the magazine she opened, Shirone’s face was printed in large format next to a headline about his victory over Dante in the 2000th duel.

Did I really look that dorky?

He looked like a mountain kid through and through—but honestly, even now, he wasn't sure how much he’d changed.

"Alright. Come in."

Shirone invited Langie into the room and started searching for a pen. Meanwhile, she looked around the suite.

This is my first time in here, too.

The Royal Aracne Hotel was reserved for VIPs. The floor below the top level, where VVIPs stayed, already had over 40 rooms.

But the room Shirone was using? He had the entire top floor to himself.

This suite was usually reserved only for receiving royalty. As far as Langie knew, its doors opened less than twice a year.

A mage from the Ivory Tower…

One of those supernational figures the politicians she called "vipers" feared and cursed behind closed doors.

He seems innocent, though.

He hadn’t touched the premium alcohol in the suite. Instead, there was just a single, worn-out book lying on the wide bed.

He’s modest, too. So what is it that people fear so much?

Watching him run frantically around the room looking for a pen, she even found him… adorable.

"Ah, right!"

Shirone stopped in the middle of the floor, looked up at the ceiling, and trembled.

"If you don’t have a pen, you could use mine—"

Before she could finish, Shirone smacked his own head.

"Ahh, I’m such an idiot!"

"…Idiot?"

With a sigh, Shirone walked over to the table and held out his hand.

“Material.”

With astonishing speed, magical information assembled—and suddenly, a signing pen appeared in his hand.

“Oooh?”

Langie’s eyes lit up.

Is that even possible?

She’d seen high-ranking members of the Magic Association flaunt their powers before, but never a mage who conjured a pen.

"That's pretty cool. It feels like a magic trick."

She didn’t know.

It may have been just a pen, but the theory behind that magic represented a core principle of all universal intelligence.

"Hehe, yeah. I always forget where I put things, so I tend to rely on this."

“Oh? Then can you make other things too?”

Shirone gave a sheepish smile.

“In theory, yes.”

Yahweh hated tampering with the system, but technically, Shirone could mass-produce diamonds if he wanted to.

But Langie, unfamiliar with magic, took his humility at face value.

Well, yeah. If he could make anything, he'd basically be a god, right?

Having never given anyone an autograph before, Shirone’s hands trembled and his mouth dried out.

I can’t mess this up. If it’s bad, I’ll just reset it…

Fortunately, with the help of his trusty sigil compression, the autograph came out looking quite good.

“Here’s your autograph. I hope you like it.”

“I love it,” Langie said, hugging the magazine tightly.

Now it’s my turn to get your signature, isn’t it, Mr. Mage staying in the royal suite?

Langie knew exactly why she had been sent to this room, even if Shirone didn’t give off the vibe of someone expecting "entertainment."

It’s not your fault.

If anything, the fact that he didn’t act like he was receiving hospitality was what made him so disarming.

You’re a man. It’s only natural for you to want me.

So then—

I’ll forget. I’ll forget everything.

She didn’t want to remember anything about tonight—not a single moment.

Just as she was thinking that, her stomach growled.

Langie laughed awkwardly.

“I’ve been starving the past few days preparing for the pageant. I’m at the point where I can barely move. Would you… like to have dinner together?”

“Just a moment. I think I still have some bread I bought on the way.”

Shirone quickly dug through his bag, feeling bad for her—but ended up in an awkward situation a few seconds later.

“Huh? This…”

It was a traveler’s hard biscuit—half-eaten, with his bite marks clearly visible.

“I’m sorry. I’ll order something else—”

“It’s fine. We’re both human. And I love these. They’re great for dieting.”

Never in a million years did Shirone imagine he’d be feeding Miss Aracne leftovers.

Langie snatched the bread, broke it in half, and handed the other piece back to him.

“I’ll just eat half. There should be some strawberry jam. Ah, and it’s pretty dry, so…”

Efficiently, she found the items and placed them on the table, even pulling out a fine bottle of wine from the shelf.

“A little wine too.”

The impromptu dinner began, and by the time they finished the bread, the wine had gone to their heads.

“Shirone, do you have a girlfriend?”

“Yes, I do.”

Langie blinked in surprise—she had asked assuming the answer would be no.

“She’s a friend I met in school, but we’re apart right now. She’s in the military.”

“Ooh? A soldier, huh.”

Langie ran the compatibility numbers in her head. It looked like she’d have to push a bit harder.

“Not just her magic skills—her willpower is incredible. During our graduation exams, she actually threw a punch at Nade—”

The way Shirone excitedly talked about this girl made it obvious how much he genuinely liked her.

Still, how could he sit in front of another woman and go on and on about his girlfriend? Is he just naïve? Or stupid?

After listening to the end, Langie downed the last of her wine and stood up.

“Oh, right. Want me to give you a shoulder massage?”

As she stepped behind and reached for his shoulders, Shirone hunched his neck like a startled turtle.

“Uhh, I’m fine…”

“Relax. I’m good at this. Or what—can’t let anyone touch you unless it’s your girlfriend?”

“No! It’s not that, I just…”

Langie’s eyes gleamed.

Ah, I get it now.

He was the type who didn’t want to hurt others.

"Massaging the shoulders of the most famous mage in the world... I must be the luckiest person alive."

"You're the most famous one here, Miss Langie. You're Miss Aracne and competing in an international pageant."

"Hehe, you're right. Then shall we make a promise? Let's keep tonight just between the two of us forever."

"Huh? Oh, uh, sure."

Langie didn’t want to risk rumors.

So, are we finally getting to it? How long are you going to keep tormenting me like this?

She gently massaged Shirone's shoulders and leaned in close to his ear.

"I'm so happy."

If Shirone turned his head, she'd kiss him. From there, no man could escape.

Congratulations, Your Majesty, she thought. The king will get what he wants.

"Shirone..."

But Shirone didn’t turn. He stared straight ahead like a stone, not moving an inch.

"Shirone, hey… look at me for a second."

"Miss Langie."

His voice was calm but firm, breaking the mood instantly.

"I'm sorry. I don’t know why you're doing this—maybe I’m just misunderstanding, but..."

Shirone stood, his eyes now clear, his intoxication gone. He turned to face her.

"The moment I saw you, I thought you were incredibly beautiful. But… I have someone I love. And unless we part ways, I would never betray her."

"It's not betrayal. I'm not asking you to date me."

Langie tried a final line of defense, but Shirone shook his head emphatically.

"Then I really can't accept this. I think you’ve had a bit too much to drink. Please stay here tonight."

As he began to leave, panic surged in Langie.

"No! Please don't do that. I can't sleep here instead of you."

"You promised, remember?"

Shirone smiled as he opened the door.

"That we’d keep this just between us."

"Ah…"

Langie sat frozen until the sound of the door closing snapped her out of it.

“Seriously? That’s really it?”

It wasn’t a bad result. In fact, his words about keeping it their secret gave her some peace. So why did she feel so annoyed?

“Idiot! Who’s going to reward you for being a nice guy? You’ll regret that for the rest of your life!”

Langie, who believed that getting what you want was winning, threw herself onto the bed in frustration, sprawling across the silk sheets.

“Ugh, this is amazing! Fine! Right now, I’m higher than any king!”

Fluttering her limbs like a butterfly and savoring the luxury, she turned her head and saw the book Shirone had been reading.

“He left it behind. What is it?”

She opened it near the middle—it was a novel.

Langie tilted her head and checked the title on the cover.

The Archmage Who Slays Dragons.

“Pfft...”

Her arms dropped in disbelief, and a moment later, she burst out laughing toward the ceiling.

“Puhahahaha!”

Maybe Arcane had been right all along.

“Slays dragons…! Puhihihi!”

A mage is forever a boy at heart.

The next morning.

Standing on the hotel rooftop, deep in thought, Shirone soared into the sky as the sun rose.

With the hint he'd gotten from Langie’s visit, Shirone arrived at the royal palace more determined than before.

"Welcome, Five Great Star."

King Paronica of Aracne bent his massive upper body in greeting.

"About what I asked yesterday. Have you found someone named Jaestin?"

"Ah, the records were far more extensive than we expected. It’s going to take a bit longer."

Just as expected.

"I see."

Another Star might have grabbed him by the collar already. Paronica, thinking Shirone might be easier to handle, took the chance to speak again.

"Actually, due to a recent incident in the kingdom, we're short on manpower, which is slowing things down."

Shirone remained silent.

"A terrorist threat, to be exact. They’ve declared that unless the Miss World Pageant is canceled, they'll turn the venue into a bloodbath."

To make this request of a Star from the Ivory Tower meant the kingdom’s security forces had failed to catch the perpetrator.

"So it’s shameless of me to ask, but… if the Star could personally assist us in resolving this—huh!"

Paronica fell silent as he felt an overwhelming murderous intent radiate from Shirone.

W-What is this aura…?

He’d met Stars before, but this pain—like frozen blood tearing through his veins—was something else entirely.

"The person I’m searching for..."

If not for his promise to Langie, Shirone would have pressed Paronica even harder.

"...is someone who holds the fate of the world in their hands. Even as we speak, thousands of lives are being lost."

He’s going to kill me!

Only now did Paronica truly grasp what it meant for this young man to be one of the Ivory Tower’s Five Stars.

"I—I apologize! I’ll bring you the profile on Amery Jaestin immediately!"

If he didn’t already have it ready, there wouldn’t be any negotiation at all.

Hoo... this job is exhausting.

Relieved, Shirone relaxed his aura and turned away, now thinking about the terror threat.

I should look into it myself.

Preventing a major disaster was important, of course—but he didn’t want Maya’s precious debut performance to be ruined.

And I’m worried about Langie too.

Still, the first priority was finding Beta Fish.
