Chapter 980: The Grand Premise (2)

Miro, who had maintained a critical attitude, couldn't hide her disappointment when she heard Arius's words.

"Isn't there any way?"

"The human mind is exclusive and detests forcibly implanted concepts. That's why divers endlessly circumvent using familiar concepts as keywords. Yet, they can't avoid egoists. After all, it's impossible for someone to fully understand another's mind. Excavation is relatively harmless because forgetting is a natural flow for humans. But engraving something into the subconscious is just like what Ikael did to Shirone. It can drive someone mad or even kill them from shock. Usually, divers don't mind, but this situation is different."

"Then it's pointless from the start. Even if we expand perception with Agape and implant Ultima through Drimo, if the person can't accept it..."

"I have thought about that."

Shirone spoke.

"If accepting another's concept is met with resistance, we make it natural for them to accept it."

Most didn't understand, but Arius wore a serious expression.

"Hmm."

He found it plausible.

"Lowering the threshold through repeated experience."

As Shirone nodded, Miro looked down at Arius and snapped.

"Explain."

"Yes."

Arius straightened up for the first time and began to explain.

"Humans are adaptive creatures. The more they repeat an experience, the more they accept it as natural. For example, no matter how delicious food is, if you eat it every day, you'll get tired of it. The opposite is also true."

Arius raised both index fingers.

"Let's say Shirone's Ultima System is a kiss. And the person to be implanted has never kissed before. Naturally, when the concept of a kiss is implanted, there will be resistance. It's something they haven't experienced being defined in their mind. But what if the experience, the memory of having kissed before, is implanted along with it? The resistance would drop by more than half."

Miro's mouth fell open in astonishment.

"So you're saying..."

"Yes. Shirone wants to conceptualize the experience of the Ultima System as well. It's certainly possible. Familiar concepts can indeed be implanted. Even if you implant a different memory of pedaling to someone who rides a bicycle, there might be some confusion, but they'll eventually accept it as their own memory. In that sense, the mind is as exclusive as it is strongly self-assimilating."

Miro spoke.

"But this isn't just a kiss. It's implanting a level no one in human history has reached."

Arius countered.

"There's only one experience in the Ultima System. Any additional information is integrated. Once conceptualized, the implantation itself will be easier. And don't speak so lightly. Even the master hasn't kissed yet."

"Why bring that up now..."

Miro sighed, seeing Gaold lost in his thoughts.

"Alright. So where do we get that experience? Even Shirone hasn't integrated with anyone."

Minerva asked.

"Four-star resident Saint Brain. He's a Gaian, right?"

Shirone admitted.

"Yes. But he lost Ultima. He won't be of help in this matter."

Through Omega, Shirone knew what had happened between Adam and Eve.

Miro asked in frustration.

"Then what do we do? There's no way?"

"No."

Shirone looked around at everyone.

"There is one. Someone who already has a perfect Ultima System and doesn't need further integration."

"Is there such a person?"

Miro quickly ran through everyone she knew in her mind.

After a moment of confusion, she realized and her eyes widened.

"...Ymir."

The room buzzed, and even Rian, who had been meditating silently, opened one eye.

"Yes, Ymir. The only being in the universe with the experience of integrating 10 billion Gaian."

As no one spoke, Shirone turned to Luber and concluded.

"We'll enter Ymir's subconscious through Drimo and excavate the Ultima System. Then we'll implant it into all of humanity. That will end the war."

"Hehe. Hehehe."

A half-mad voice echoed through the Grand Hall, drawing everyone's attention.

Arius was shaking with laughter.

"Hehe... Hahaha."

Even though Sein was the top in the mental field, he couldn't match Arius in excavation, so everyone waited for him to exhaust his emotions.

"Hah."

For the first time, Arius, who had become mentally degraded as Miro's dog, raised his head like a human.

"So this is how it ends?"

He looked in Miro's direction with his blind eyes and raised his index and middle fingers.

"Master, may I have a cigarette?"

"Shut up and speak quickly."

"...Yes."

Arius licked his lips and spoke.

"I have entered the subconscious of both Shirone, known as Yahweh, and my master, Miro."

Though he had approached both with ill intentions, it certainly gave him authority.

"It was truly amazing. Yes, amazing. But in the end, it's just one human's mind. Not that Shirone's or the master's mind is shallow, it's just that. What I really want to say is... 10 billion minds."

Despite trembling with fear, Arius's mouth showed a hint of happy madness.

"And their integration. I can't even imagine what kind of world that would be. Are you really going to do this? If you want to call it the only hope, let me tell you this. It's a suicide mission. It's giving up without a fight."

Shirone smiled and asked.

"So you're not going?"

"I'll go."

Arius regretted for the first time that he had gouged out his eyes in his resolve to devote everything to Miro.

"I'll go no matter what. But as the leader, I must tell you, we're not going to survive. I'll do my best, but please keep that in mind."

"Alright. I'll join through simultaneous events, but we need more collaborators for the Ultima System project. Anyone willing to volunteer..."

Before he finished speaking, many hands in the Grand Hall went up.

There was no need to check Rian, and Iruki, Nade, Amy, and Tess's eyes were burning with determination.

Nade approached.

"Shirone, we're going too. You know how stubborn I am, right? I'm definitely going, okay?"

Shirone gave a troubled smile.

He was moved by his friends' resolve, but considering the difficulty of the mission, it was an impossible team.

"Kids, stay out of this."

Nade and Iruki glared but then shrank back like turtles.

Miro, exuding the aura of Avalokiteshvara, walked towards Shirone.

"What about me?"

Though she didn't need to be evaluated, it made forming the team easier.

Shirone, sensing the ironclad aura of Avalokiteshvara, nodded.

"You're enough."

Even Miro sought permission, so Iruki, Nade, and Tess quietly stepped back.

However, two people still hadn't given up: Rian and Amy.

Amy spoke.

"Shirone, I'm going too. I've experienced Drimo and played an active role there."

Shirone owed his life to Amy's quick thinking.

'Amy is strong.'

Her power, having conquered the fire realm, was beyond imagination, but Shirone hesitated to answer.

'It's a bit... just a bit ambiguous.'

That level of difficulty.

Shirone turned his gaze to the person he had in mind when he first mentioned volunteers.

'Mr. Gaold.'

He thought Gaold would naturally follow if Miro went, but he was still lost in thought.

As if Shirone's gaze carried invisible power, people made way.

Miro walked down the path.

"What, you're not going?"

There was no answer.

"Are you really a has-been? If you can't fight, just leave. Stop lingering in front of me."

Kangnan glared at her.

"That's harsh. He never ruined his body for himself. It was all for you."

"Really? Sorry. But what can I do? It seems there's nothing left to ruin. Foolish wreck."

Though the insult was directed at Gaold, Kangnan's eyes reddened with sorrow.

"Who do you think you are..."

Why was Miro so harsh to Gaold?

"I have a condition."

Ignoring Miro and Kangnan's conversation, Gaold looked at Shirone.

"Go ahead."

"I'll go, but Miro stays here. If that's decided, I'll destroy Ymir or whatever."

Gaold's statement came from the same cold analysis as Shirone's, but to Miro, it was an unbearable insult.

"You bastard!"

As Miro rushed and swung her leg at Gaold's chin,

'Huh.'

Everyone watching realized it was an illusion and quickly blinked.

Miro was breathing heavily.

"...Just die."

She wanted to kick him for real, but the moment her mental seal loosened, All-Seeing Projection would break.

"Just die. Get out of my sight."

Shirone had a sense of why Miro was so harsh to Gaold.

At that moment, Lupist entered.

"I am Lupist, the head of the Magic Association and the chief officer of the succession ceremony. Please follow me."

He bowed politely and looked at Pony.

"It's time for you to become queen."

The news of the succession ceremony reached even the farmlands on the outskirts of Bashka.

"To think I'd attend such an event. It's true, living long enough lets you see everything, right, dear?"

Farmer Klein's wife looked at him disdainfully as he tied a bowtie over his worn clothes.

"Are you really going? What can a farmer like you do at such an event?"

"We won the war. There are few families left who survived. Anyway, the city is in ruins, so I'm going to see if there's anything profitable."

"If you had that kind of sense, we wouldn't be living like this. Just don't get ripped off."

Klein scratched his cheek.

"Why has my whole body been itching since last night? Is there something weird here?"

"The only weird thing around here is your face."

"Dear, the way you talk..."

As her husband turned his head, not really annoyed, their son ran out from the kitchen.

"Dad! Are we going into the castle?"

My beloved son.

"Haha! Yes! Today, Dad is going to..."

At the moment he turned his attention to his son, a sharp pain, like being stabbed with a needle, shot through his entire body.

"Argh!"

Startled, his son stopped in his tracks, and his wife grabbed her husband's shoulder as he collapsed.

"Honey! What's wrong?"

"It hurts! Don't touch me! It hurts!"

He was on the verge of losing his mind.

"Where does it hurt? Here? Here?"

"I don't know! I don't know! It just... hurts everywhere... Argh!"

It felt as if his pain sensitivity had increased a hundredfold, and even breathing made his airways feel like they were burning.

Farmer Klein.

He was the first reported case of Emotion Sickness in the Bashka region.
