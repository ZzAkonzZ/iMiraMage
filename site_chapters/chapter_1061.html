Chapter 1061: Graveyard of Information (4)

When they reached the counter, a woman with long hair down to her shoulders greeted them.

"Hello. This is the High Gear user registration page. Are both of you new users?"

She looked human, but her face had seams and small bolts symmetrically placed on each side.

It was crude, but perhaps the mechanical look was part of the appeal.

"This friend is new, but I have a user code. Can I register now?"

"Yes. Please provide your code name."

"Son of Yolga. East Country."

Shirone turned his head in surprise, and Fermi shrugged.

The woman's eyes sparkled.

"Oh, you're a ranker. East Country Rank 7, Son of Yolga. It's an honor to meet such a great person."

Fermi smiled slyly with his eyes.

'Her acting is top-notch. Then again, with a mechanical face, she probably doesn't need to try hard.'

Tutorial manager Magneti.

As someone who managed real-time connections for High Gear, she couldn't be unaware of Fermi's identity.

Magneti manipulated the screen and spoke.

"According to High Gear regulations, a special privilege is activated for Son of Yolga for registering a new user. A 'New Face' bonus of 1 billion galaxies will be awarded."

Shirone glared at Fermi.

"Hey."

"We should take what we can get. Don't worry. I'll use the 1 billion galaxies for you."

"Forget it."

Shirone didn't want to get more involved.

"I'm not here to play High Gear. You said we could contact the Operator here."

"You're so impatient."

Fermi asked Magneti,

"Can we relay a message to the first designer of High Gear? It's urgent."

"Pointing out a specific operator is against regulations. If you have any suggestions, please tell me."

"As expected…"

As Fermi seemed ready to back off, Shirone approached Magneti.

"It'll only take a moment. If you hear me out, the Operator might change their mind."

"I'm sorry, but I can't approve that. High Gear operators adhere to their own regulations."

"Operator? So you're not an AI. Are you human from my planet?"

"…That's beyond my authority to answer."

Her voice was monotonous, but Shirone noticed her slight delay in response.

"You know what situation humanity is in right now. The fate of the world is at stake."

"The fate of the world…"

As she pondered those words, Magneti's mechanical face twisted oddly.

"Pfft…"

And finally, she couldn't hold it in.

"Pahahaha! The fate of the world? That's hilarious! It's even more serious than I thought!"

"What's so funny?"

As Shirone's expression grew darker with anger, Fermi sighed.

'As expected… In Under Coder, where fakes and anonymity reign, 'sincerity' is just a joke.'

"I asked what's so funny?"

As Shirone glared at her, Fermi said,

"Calm down. This is a virtual world. No one cares if the real world ends or not."

"How can you say that? Do you know how many people have died fighting? Is this reality funny to you? No way. You're just running away from reality."

Fermi felt hopeless about how to make him understand.

"Sigh. So this place…"

At that moment, Magneti's laughter disappeared.

"You can think whatever you want, but insulting an operator can result in penalties. You might be banned from High Gear for a certain period or permanently expelled."

"Go ahead and try."

Light from Miracle Stream flared up around Shirone's body.

"Would you still laugh if I attacked this place? Would you realize what reality is then?"

"You can just reconnect."

Shirone's eyebrow twitched.

"But you'd be permanently expelled and never see me again. That's the worst that can happen here. So do as you wish." "Shirone."

Shirone had never revealed his name.

"It's funny saying this, but I'm so angry I'll play along this time. This isn't reality. You don't know who I am outside. I could be a fighter for justice or the villain you despise."

Fermi agreed.

"And I don't know who you are either. Sure, I've heard your name before. But how would anyone know if you're really saving the world or just enjoying yourself using your power when you wake up from this dream?"

"I…"

"It doesn't matter if it's true or not. The important thing is no one cares here. Under Coder is that kind of place. So please leave reality behind and mind your manners here. Your seriousness is making users uncomfortable."

Fermi nodded repeatedly.

'Yeah, that's what I've been saying.'

"I don't get it."

Shirone clenched his fists.

"Whether it's reality or Under Coder, we're still people talking to each other now. Why ignore that? Your world is being destroyed!"

"…You're really persistent."

Shirone felt frustrated.

"What do I need to do to prove it? Will you believe me if I kneel here?"

Magneti scoffed.

"Kneel? There are countless people who become dogs and bark or feel like they're being dissected because they want to experience it here. Serial killers in reality take care of the sick here. Don't you get it yet? It's all fake."

"Fine."

Miracle Stream surged up to the ceiling.

"Then I'll destroy this fake world. Go back and open your window and look outside."

Magneti's hand moved towards the screen.

"…Attacking an operator results in permanent expulsion. You've been warned." In the tense atmosphere, Shirone lunged at Magneti.

"Wait…"

As Fermi reached out his hand,

"Please."

Shirone knelt on the floor in front of the counter.

"I want to save the world. So many people are suffering. I don't know how to convey my sincerity, and maybe you're being deceived by me… But even so…"

Even if that's the case.

"Can't you just believe me? Maybe this could save someone, even just one life."

Magneti glared at Shirone.

The emotion-filled mechanical face turned cold again.

"Sigh."

Finally, she spoke.

"What should I tell them?"

Shirone looked up in surprise.

"Just so you know, even though I'm an operator, I don't have the authority to command the Operator. If it's a strange message, they might not even respond."

Shirone stood up quickly.

"That's fine! Just tell them that Shirone, or rather Yahweh, wants to meet them…"

"Fermi."

Fermi interrupted.

"Tell them Ardino Fermi has a big business proposal. They'll be satisfied."

Then he shrugged at Shirone.

"It's better if I do it since I've dealt with them under my real name. The name Yahweh doesn't mean anything here."

"Alright. I've sent it."

Magneti finished sending the message, but after five minutes, there was no response.

As if expecting this, she said,

"Still no response…"

Beep-beep.

At that moment, an alarm message appeared on the screen embedded in the counter.

'They actually responded.'

Even she hadn't expected it.

"There's a response. I'll play it now."

As Shirone and Fermi remained silent, a heavily distorted voice came through.

"Get lost."

That's it? That's the end?

As Shirone looked bewildered, Magneti leaned on the counter and said,

"This is unusual too. Under Coder is full of various concepts, but the Operator's personality is genuinely quirky."

Fermi agreed.

"If even my name doesn't work, there's no other way. Let's go inside High Gear and find a way."

"But they responded."

Shirone didn't give up.

"If they really weren't interested, they wouldn't have told us to get lost. Try again."

"No, that's…"

As Shirone nudged him, Fermi raised his hands and moved back to the counter.

"Alright, alright."

Fermi looked at Magneti.

"Can we send one more message?"

Thinking Shirone had a point, she nodded willingly.

"Alright. What should I send?"

"Hmm."

Fermi's eyes changed.

"I'm thinking of putting a recording device containing data from the outside world up for conditional auction…"

Shirone quickly turned his head.

"There are three conditions. First, the bidder must have the ability to decode the record. Second, they must share the viewed data with me. Third, the auction will be on a first-come, first-served basis."

Tapping the screen, Magneti swallowed hard.

"Since we're not strangers, if you're interested, we can make a personal deal. That's all."

Shirone's eyes narrowed as he looked at Fermi.

'I knew he'd have a way.'

He always needed a push to listen.

"I've sent it."

As soon as she finished speaking, a loud bell rang, and Magneti blinked.

"Code name Operator is requesting a voice conversation with operator privileges. Do you accept?"

There was no need to ask.

As Fermi nodded, an agitated voice came through, even distorted.

"Hey, who are you? Using the operator channel for personal use? I thought you were smart."

"Anyway, it's an enticing offer, right?"

"Don't get it wrong. I'm connecting because I'm annoyed. Do you think I'd lose in a first-come, first-served auction?"

"Actually… that's not confirmed yet."

"Where are you selling that? Mentioning an auction means you haven't found a way to decode the recording device. To prevent a drop in value, you chose an extreme competitive method like first-come, first-served, right?"

'So picky.'

Fermi's expression didn't change.

"Of course, but the situation is delicate. If you don't accept the offer, it might go to some random guy. There's a thug next to me holding a knife."

"What are you talking about?"

Shirone stepped in.

"Hello, Operator. I'm Shirone. I've heard that in the Abyss…"

"Shut up. I didn't connect to talk to you."

Shirone felt a surge of anger.

"Shirone? Do you think Yahweh joining will change anything? I don't need that recording device."

Fermi smiled with his eyes.

"Come on, that's not true. Just looking at the items you buy at auctions, I can guess what you're obsessed with."

"Let's work together. It's a win-win, right? Just make a program for use in the Abyss."

There was a brief silence.

"No. I have to prepare for the Solar War. Aren't you going to? Otherwise, you'll be swallowed by the West Country."

Shirone stepped in again.

"Operator, I don't know what the Solar War is, but…"

"If you don't know, shut up. Why do you keep butting in? And how can you not know about the Solar War? Idiot."

A vein bulged on Shirone's neck.

"I don't know. But is that more important than people's lives?"

"Haha! You talk well. Do you think you're Yahweh here too? If you're confident, come and find me. If you beat me in a duel, I'll grant any request."

Fermi sighed.

'I don't like this.'

Defeating the Operator, the top ranker in both the West Country and overall, in a one-on-one duel was nearly impossible.

"Alright!"

Shirone shouted.

"You, wait right there! I'll smash High Gear or whatever it is to pieces!"
