Chapter 448:

As the aura of death dissipated, the force meter transported Zulu and Kangnan back to their original time and space.

Kangnan stared blankly at the ice field where the darkness had vanished.

Only Gaold was panting heavily, his shoulders heaving.

‘Foolish human.’

Even she, who always accompanied him, had not anticipated the extent of Gaold's rising power.

It wasn't due to special training or enlightenment.

It was the Miro he encountered after 20 years.

The closer he got to her time and space, the exponentially greater the pain he had to endure.

This was what Kangnan feared.

Even if Gaold had surpassed the limits of pain due to his self-mutating abilities, a creature's endurance is not infinite.

Just like the day 15 years ago when Gaold first fell into the Great Inferno.

“What are you trying to do…!”

“It’s not over yet.”

As Kangnan approached, Gaold spoke.

As if waiting, darkness surged up from beneath the ice, forming the shape of Hel.

‘An incorporeal entity?’

In Gaold's world, ghosts are a prime example.

They traverse the boundary between mind and matter, unaffected by physical influences in their mental state and unable to exert influence.

However, in their material state, they can exert physical force and be affected by it.

Hel had also narrowly escaped being crushed by Gaold's air pressing by shifting from a material to a mental state.

While this ability was troublesome for humans to deal with, it was a blow to the pride of Hel, the ruler of Niflheim.

Hoooooo!

Hel's hollow pupils swirled like a vortex, pulling Gaold into the depths of the abyss.

The world plunged back into darkness, and the battle between the undying and the immortal began.

The air magic, amplified 500,000 times, had already transcended the realm of the atmospheric attribute.

Air bullets from the air gun were fired at an incredible speed.

Despite the rapid fire, Hel's torso was riddled with holes as if hit by buckshot.

The tattered Hel charged at Gaold.

“Human! I will scatter your ashes over the corpse of the despicable Miro!”

“Grrrrrr!”

Hel's provocation backfired.

Hundreds of wrinkles formed on Gaold's distorted face, and hot steam escaped from between his clenched teeth.

Gaold's extreme endurance unleashed the Great Inferno.

In the dark space like the night sky, flames of incandescence surged, mingling with the screams of death and the wails of hungry ghosts, blending extreme cold and heat.

Even Hel, the ruler of the dead, could not remain silent before Gaold's powerful endurance.

‘Is there such a human?’

Hel immediately revised his thoughts.

‘No, is he really human?’

The pain amplified 500,000 times.

No one could imagine the hell Gaold was experiencing.

No one could even guess.

But for Gaold, it was the only reality.

‘Miro….’

No matter how painful, he would not die.

‘Miro.’

The reason did not matter.

On that day 20 years ago, when he had to helplessly watch Miro being taken away, Gaold had given up on becoming any kind of being.

Thanks to experiencing countless shocks, even his memories were tangled, but the only thing that never faded in that chaos was Miro’s voice.

-Because you are weak.

Twenty people judged. The whole world clung desperately. And Miro walked into Istas on her own.

But the truth that only Gaold knew was Miro’s voice on the eve of the judgment by the twenty.

-While you can do nothing!

Tears of blood flowed from beneath Gaold's upturned white eyes.

‘Miro!’

Pain amplified 1,000,000 times - Air Pressing.

Hel was crushed with a pop and then reformed in the air.

Had he been a little slower in shifting to a mental state, he would have been annihilated.

A light of confusion passed over Hel's face, as if his soul was escaping.

Hel wished Gaold would die.

For the first time since his existence, he desperately wanted someone's death.

‘Am I afraid? Me?’

The ruler of the dead does not fear death. Thus, what Hel felt was the endless acceleration of fear.

Gaold's endurance, with no measurable limit, seemed like it would accelerate forever, as if on a rail with no end.

“Kieeeee!”

As Hel exerted all his energy, all sorts of dead spirits swam through the air, trailing black smoke.

Seeing the spirits rushing towards the ground, Kangnan and Zulu scattered in different directions.

They could defend with the force meter, but to displace three seconds in time required nearly four years of omnipotence in real time.

Even Zulu, the strongest summoner, needed more than 30% of his mental strength for this magic, and his current usable limit was just one time.

Using it now would not be a good decision if they were to prepare for a decisive moment.

“Ugh!”

Kangnan raised her arms and curled up as the spirits' attacks tore through the space like a vortex.

Every time their claws scratched her, black scars were etched into her skin.

Her body, trained for a lifetime, attempted to counterattack, but it merely passed through the spirits' bodies, which had returned to a mental state.

‘Attack and defense must be simultaneous. The only chance is when the enemy attacks.’

Kangnan assumed the stance of Ram Muai.

Her sharp gaze pierced the enemy through the arms raised in an 11 shape.

Hoooooo!

As the spirits attacked, her body moved.

Every time her straight-line attacks, covering the shortest distance, exploded in succession, the black smoke of the spirits shattered into pieces.

Further away, Zulu was summoning the giant worm Odd Worm to suck in the materialized spirits.

The mindless spirits did not give up, and as their numbers dwindled, the view began to clear.

“Where is Gaold?”

He was in the sky. His face had become so grotesque that it was hard to tell who the ruler of the dead was.

Life and death, endurance and despair collided in the sky.

“Annoying…!”

Gaold's Great Inferno spread on an unprecedented scale, extending beyond Hel's darkness.

Vacuum Press!

At that moment, Gaold's specialty, which even compressed Ataraxia's photon cannon, was activated.

As the vast atmosphere compressed into a single point, Hel's entire body began to tighten rapidly.

‘Can't escape?’

Even in a mental state, Hel could not penetrate the shrinking vacuum force.

Endurance.

Finally, Hel realized why he, the ruler of death, felt fear towards a mere human.

It was because Gaold was not afraid.

“Kieeeee!”

Hel continued to be compressed.

From a small sphere to a smaller sphere, to a point, and finally to nothing…

At the moment of annihilation, Hel exploded, scattering countless spirits in all directions.

Their roars were powerful enough to spread throughout Niflheim, and any creature that heard them would lose its life.

As Gaold floated defenselessly, Zulu and Kangnan arrived behind him and used the force meter's power to transport all three to another time and space.

The lion's roar spread, compressing the air in waves, and perfect silence descended like the eye of a storm.

Gaold, who had landed on the ice, raised his head. He listened to the sound of the wind swirling in the transparent void.

Even if you refuse mortality, there is nothing you can grasp forever. Go to Jebul. There, the despicable Miro awaits. She will drive you to true death.

It was the sound of the wind.

“……”

As everyone remained silent, about a hundred black elixirs fell from the void like rain.

No one in Purgatory could gather so many black elixirs.

Gaold did not smile.

After the terrible pain, the only reality left was the sense of still being alive.

Some memories were erased, and some remained, but what value is there in memories that cannot be arranged in chronological order?

Gaold threw all his memories into the trash bin of oblivion and turned back, holding only Miro.

Since that day 20 years ago, he had lived like this.

“Pick them all up. We’re going back.”

Gaold turned coldly and began to walk.

Kangnan, watching Gaold pass by, bared her teeth.

It was fortunate that the goal was achieved, but the reckless increase in pain could potentially halt the project.

She could no longer watch the thunderous, naked fighting devoid of tactics and strategy.

“Wait. Not like this…!”

As she turned with fierce words, Gaold staggered.

Kangnan, who had unconsciously taken a step forward, stopped.

She must not hold him back. No one could understand his pain.

He had walked alone for 20 years.

‘Yes. Whether he returns alive or is buried dead, it ends here.’

Kangnan resolved. She would take Gaold there by any means necessary.

Shirone returned to the First Rebel Command immediately after integrating all the forces under the Second Rebel Command.

Armin's group, who had been allowed to run the munitions factory, was the first to return, followed by Gaold's group, who delivered the black elixirs to Yameng and returned.

All three divided parties perfectly completed their assigned tasks, but Shirone could not smile.

She heard shocking news upon arrival.

The command had already been devastated.

The culprit who massacred countless people was Babel, the metal angel Shirone had unsealed.

She could not bear to face Kanya and Lena.

Clove and Gardrak, who died in the collapse of the Second Command, and now Kanya's father.

All the deaths felt like her fault.

‘But… I have to speak.’

Shirone mustered the courage to head to the room where the two were.

She had to confess to them that she was the one who unsealed Babel.

The sisters, who forced a smile upon seeing Shirone, lost their expressions again as they listened to her confession.

Even after hearing the shocking truth, Kanya and Lena did not react.

Shirone couldn't tell if they were angry or simply too exhausted to be angry.

“I’m sorry. It’s my fault. If I hadn’t unsealed Babel… Everything is my fault.”

Lena couldn't hold back her tears and started crying.

Honestly, she didn't know how to react.

Kanya glared fiercely and spoke.

“If Dad had been killed by another angel, not Babel, would it still not be your fault?”

Shirone couldn't answer.

“No, it’s all your fault, Shirone. Every death in Heaven is your fault.”

Kanya got up from the bed and approached Shirone. She buried her face in his chest and said,

“So fight for us.”

Shirone felt his chest getting wet.

“I couldn’t do anything. Not even revenge… I was too powerless…”

Eventually, Kanya also broke down in tears.

In the face of an unimaginable enemy, she felt that humans were mere insects.

But Shirone was different. And those who came with Shirone were different.

How could they endure such overwhelming might?

Shirone took a deep breath.

He had come to Heaven to meet Ikael, but now that was no longer possible.

“Let’s go to Babel.”
