Chapter 1055: Free Gene (2)

The students felt as if they had taken a stimulant, their minds becoming incredibly clear.

'I surpassed my limits.'

Their bodies trembled with exhilaration, a sensation they had never experienced with any other buff magic.

Rampa spoke.

"The Five Great Stars are indeed considerate."

Shirone's buff was dangerous.

It was so powerful that it could even change the recipient's mind.

Amy said.

"He doesn't use buffs that increase power. It's an excellent educational decision."

As the demonstration ended and the buff's effect wore off, the teachers and students blinked.

It felt like waking up from a dream.

"Ah…"

The world seemed strangely unnatural without the refreshing sensation.

Shirone explained.

"It's a buff that increases the density of the Spirit Zone. If you recall the feeling from a moment ago, you'll achieve good results."

How much training would it take to experience that state again?

Even so, being able to briefly experience a certain level was an incredible privilege.

Unlike the excited students, Wizard was deep in thought with a serious expression.

"How about it, can you do it?"

"Yes."

The answer came immediately.

"But why do I need to learn this?"

For the first time, she raised a question, and the students and teachers turned to look at her.

"Brother, I mean, teacher."

During class, the term "brother" was forbidden.

"I know time is short. What you want is for me to grow strong enough to kill Habits."

It was to block emotional fluctuations, but this time she had crossed a line.

"Wizard."

"I'm sorry. I wanted to convey it properly. But… it's the truth."

"I want to focus on the mission's success rather than survival. I know he's not a weak opponent I can kill while worrying about my life."

Shirone remained unfazed.

"If you really think that, solve my task as quickly as possible. That's the fastest way."

Their gazes clashed for a moment.

"Alright."

Wizard sighed and cast vision magic, drawing a cross similar to the one Shirone had shown.

"Force."

Effects burst above the heads of the people around.

The buff's effect was astonishing, but the speed at which she collided thoughts surpassed Shirone's.

"Incredible…"

It seemed like Wizard's desire to be fully recognized as a wizard was visible.

'Let's apply it here.'

As numerous diagonals formed at the center of the cross, the shape of the thoughts began to construct a sphere.

The eyes of the Ten Wizards shook with shock.

"That's… three-dimensional?"

"Truly impressive."

Even Rampa couldn't help but acknowledge it.

"Three-dimensional thinking might be due to innate awakening, but applying it to Force is a completely different matter."

That's why Shirone's reaction was even more intriguing.

'A mental battle between master and disciple…'

Those who had the same thought remained silent even after the Force demonstration ended.

"Huff. Huff."

Wizard was breathing heavily but didn't take her eyes off Shirone.

"Is this enough?"

"Excellent skill. Very well done."

"Then teach me how to fight. Not running away from Satan, but how to fight."

"I intended to from the beginning."

As Wizard looked puzzled, Shirone cast vision magic.

"What…?"

Shirone, who had constructed the same three-dimensional thinking as Wizard, spoke.

"The reason I taught you utility was, of course, to increase your survival rate. But…"

Wizard's face froze.

"What I really wanted to show you is this."

The teachers, who hadn't noticed due to the rapid Force, suddenly realized.

"What? What?"

The buffs that were supposed to collide in order were randomly changing their sequence.

"Four-dimensional…"

Wizard murmured.

"Yes. Changing the sequence of batteries by transcending space means transcending time."

It was the realm of temporal perception.

"Habits always has Siok by his side. If you can break the barrier of time, the mission success rate will increase dramatically. The reason I chose the utility series is also because this method is the best for training temporal perception."

Wizard finally understood Shirone's deep intention, but she couldn't be sure.

"I don't know. Can I do it?"

Opening a new sense was not a matter of talent but of species.

"You might fail. That's why it's a gamble, but I think it's worth investing time."

For Wizard, who absorbed teachings like a sponge, even an hour was precious.

'But I can't send her off without addressing Siok. It's a crucial issue.'

Wizard, who had been thinking seriously, looked up.

"How long do I have?"

Shirone looked at the sky and said.

"I'll give you a day."

"What?"

Wizard's eyes widened, but Shirone was firm in such matters.

"By tomorrow morning. If you find a way, come to me."

Wizard, rolling her eyes, suddenly asked.

"Can I use any method?"

When Shirone nodded, she left the training ground without looking back.

With Wizard's training concluded, Shirone walked over to Amy.

"Did you sleep well? Your hair has grown a lot."

"Ah."

Amy looked up and rubbed her short, invisible hair with her fingers.

It wasn't a buzz cut, but compared to other female teachers, it was still unimpressive.

"I've been too busy to care. Shall we go out for lunch later?"

Amy shook her head.

"No. Wizard might change. It's better to stay at school today."

She still cared for Shirone, but her emotions were more restrained than usual.

"Then at least at the school cafeteria…"

"Alright."

Amy turned around hastily.

"See you then."

She disliked the contradictory feeling of rejecting him and then feeling disappointed, and she hated showing that emotion even more.

Rampa approached Shirone from behind.

"Amy seems to have a lot on her mind. The Five Great Stars are always getting attention."

"That's not true. I've never thought that way…"

"Haha! Why explain to me? Everyone wants to show their best side to their loved ones. Maybe she's bothered by her short hair?"

"No way. I'm very proud of Amy's short hair. It's a mark of saving humanity."

"…Maybe that's the problem? Anyway, even Buddha doesn't understand a woman's heart."

Shirone couldn't deny he felt a bit guilty.

"Actually, I haven't met Amy separately since coming here."

"Do you doubt your feelings?"

"No. Quite the opposite. My heart races every time I see Amy. But…"

If he gave his heart to her, the world's and humanity's problems would seem trivial.

"Indeed, it's strange to focus on work while leaving your loved one behind. Do what you think is best, but don't be too hard on yourself. Controlling your heart doesn't make the pain go away."

Watching Amy's retreating figure, Shirone pondered Rampa's words.

That night.

Shirone, Iruki, and Neide cleared out the remnants of demons in a remote village.

"Oh, Yahweh."

Since the pilgrimage began, Yahweh's name spread rapidly through people's mouths.

As rumors often do, the events were exaggerated and glorified, but at least Shirone's sincerity was conveyed.

"A ray of light in a chaotic world, please save us."

The fact that they knew without being told deeply impressed Shirone's group.

'The spread is accelerating.'

One person speaks, but countless ears listen.

Neide raised his voice.

"It starts small but ends grand. This must be a big deal in the city."

Creating a focal point for humanity was the core of the Ultima Project, but Shirone remained cautious.

"We can't be complacent. The Vatican's opposition won't be easy. It's late, so let's stay here and move to the next location at dawn."

Still, Shirone was in a good mood, ordering a drink, which was unusual for him.

Neide got tipsy.

"Ah, it feels good to drink after a long time. I feel so good, I miss our Liz. Don't you, Iruki?"

Though they traveled by choice, it was like a permanent separation for their partners.

"Why would I miss your girlfriend?"

"Idiot, I mean Dorothy. You act cold but you like her."

Iruki changed the subject.

"How's Amy? Still busy with Wizard's training?"

Neide realized.

"Oh, right? You're not lonely? You should tell us what you're doing. If you say something like 'events outside perception don't exist,' I'll hit you."

Shirone chuckled.

"Yes, she's fine. Training is going smoothly."

Neide put down his beer and spoke seriously.

"But shouldn't we clarify something? Like, do you want to find treasure in a dungeon or dig a tunnel…"

Iruki looked at him disdainfully.

"Why do you care?"

"It's not about curiosity! There shouldn't be secrets between us. What about you?"

"Well…"

As Iruki answered blandly, Neide turned to Shirone.

"Huh? What?"

Neide realized from Shirone's evasive reaction and opened his mouth in shock.

"Don't tell me…"

No attachment to the secular world?

"No, what about Amy? Did you fight again?"

Even Iruki looked surprised.

"Haha, it's not that. There were various reasons. Plus, I've been very busy…"

"Reasons, my foot." Neide stood up and pulled Shirone's ear.

"Ouch! What are you doing?"

Neide's voice boomed.

"Hey, Shirone! Listen to me! Just follow your instincts! If you rely on this innocent body, you'll die a lonely old man!"

"What body?"

Shirone pushed Neide away and rubbed his ear.

"Do you think this is some communication device? It's all me. And this isn't how it's conveyed."

But.

"Sigh, you don't understand."

Neide's voice reached Shirone in the Stang Kingdom, hundreds of kilometers away.

'Do they think I'm not human?' Instincts.

'Maybe I was cold.'

Both Lampa and his friends were more worried about Amy, who had been waiting all this time, than about himself.

"Alright! Let’s just give it a shot."

Without even knowing what was so good about it, Shirone left the dormitory, feeling half excited.

The light in Amy’s room was on.
