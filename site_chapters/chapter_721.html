Chapter 721:

"An endless world."

Perhaps it was the result of living beings perfectly adapting to the system of the universe.

Shirone looked at the people still dreaming in the glass tubes.

And perhaps…

'The world we live in too.'

It wasn't something he could be sure of, but if he assumed so, one question remained.

"If everyone enters the next simulation, won't your role end? No one will wake up."

"That's not the case."

Argo shook her head.

"Living beings are truly strange, and there are parts that cannot be controlled by the system alone. It's the mind. Even if the same sensations are experienced, the degree of those sensations varies, and that cannot be controlled."

This is called qualia or sensory qualities.

"These subjective experiences accumulate, raising the Karr value and triggering numerous insights and non-experiential knowledge."

As a red light shone on Amy's face, the atmosphere turned hostile.

"Sensations are electrical signals, but qualia, or the mind program, is transmitted through quantum transmission. Therefore, it cannot be controlled. Quantum signals cannot be controlled. Eventually…"

Amy's red eyes flared up.

"They awaken. They break through the eons and escape. Subjective experiences strong enough to destroy the system's coherence. This creates doubt about the world and provides an opportunity to escape the system."

Amy approached Shirone.

"Escaping on your own means rejecting the system. Because you could harm the dreaming residents of Utopia, I must continue to exist here. And this is…"

Numerous drones flew around Shirone.

"The reason I invited you here."

Shirone's presence was a threat to Utopia.

"Your code does not exist in Utopia. I don't know where you came from, but you didn't arrive through normal causality."

Andre disrupted space-time, which raised another question for Shirone.

"Are you saying the world we live in is also part of a simulation, endlessly converging through the eons?"

"I don't know. The greatest human delusion is thinking that knowing something means knowing everything. There are things that cannot be known. Things outside the system."

As Shirone's emotional focus wavered, Amy's face in his brainwaves began to distort.

"To me, the whole is just Argo's world, and I cannot think beyond that. I am not a god. You humans created me."

Argo erased the hologram and spoke from the void.

"Of course, the world you live in could be the start of the simulation. The true reality. When calculated through the eons, it's a one-in-infinity chance, but it can't be completely ruled out."

"There must always be a beginning."

"Yes. So now, make your decision."

"About what?"

With a loud noise, the lid of a glass tube installed three stories high detached.

Argo's mechanical voice echoed coldly.

"Whether to reincarnate forever or leave this world."

Eons or infinity.

It was time to choose.

Labyrinth Andre-World No. 1583.

At the top of the mountain under the Whiteblack moon, Shirone comforted the crying child in his arms.

"It's okay now. No one will hurt you."

"Ahhh! Save me."

Shirone patted the child's back and fell into thought until the frightened cries subsided.

'Whiteblack. Yin and Yang. A world created through the definition of the number 2 in the law.'

Shirone found himself increasingly understanding the true nature of Andre as time passed.

'Every world has its own inherent intention.'

The ability to infer the whole of Andre from exploring a single world was the power of insight.

This meant that Shirone's exploration of the 19,000 worlds of Andre was entangled in a quantum spin that transcended space-time.

"Are you from the White side?"

Shirone snapped out of his thoughts and looked down at the child.

"Yes. But I won't hurt you."

The child nodded, but it seemed more out of a lack of choice than trust.

Since it would take time, Shirone didn't rush and asked for the details.

'Hmm, a child born in the Black zone…'

The eight-year-old's name was Kai.

His parents had already passed away, and he was participating in the survival test to activate the harmony of Whiteblack.

"They told me they'd protect me if I lured the White zone people. But they all ran away. They tried to kill me…"

As Kai began to cry again, Shirone spoke.

"It's okay now. You just need to win the survival test. I'll help you. First, let's check our job cards."

As the child, now composed, searched his pockets, Shirone also took out his job card.

'Seeker, right.'

The card measured approximately 5.2 centimeters wide, 8.6 centimeters long, and 4 millimeters thick.

The back of the card had several circles overlapping like a wheel, which seemed to symbolize reincarnation.

'What abilities does it have?'

Since he didn't feel any immediate changes, Shirone checked the front of the card, divided into three sections.

The top section had the card name and grade, the middle had a picture, and the bottom had the description and effect.

'Seeker. Oh, an S grade?'

It was an unexpected grade since he didn't feel any special changes.

'The description…'

Seeing the picture of a seeker sitting cross-legged and floating in the air, Shirone read the text at the bottom.

Seeker: One who has realized the truth of the world.

Special Ability: Definition of the Law.

Effect: You can choose one of two laws.

Currently chosen law - White.

"Oh? This means…"

It meant he could freely move between the White and Black zones.

"Wow! A Seeker!"

Kai's eyes widened as he saw Shirone's card.

"Do you know about job cards?"

"Of course. Everyone in Black learns about them! We even make practice cards."

Since they didn't engage in productive activities, they had plenty of time.

"Seekers are really good. They can join any side, so they can always survive."

Given that the survival test required the extermination of one zone's participants, the value of a Seeker was high.

"But if I do that, my zone changes. If I choose Black and win, I can't go to White."

"Is that a problem? At least you won't die."

Shirone suddenly realized.

'Is that so?'

Whether White or Black, what could be more important to a living being than surviving today?

"Kai, what's your job card?"

When Shirone asked, Kai slowly turned over the card he was holding with his small hands.

"I'm… this."

It was a B grade, and the card name was Magician.

Magician: One who deceives almost everyone.

Special Ability: Magic Card.

Effect: You can change one of Whiteblack's weapon cards to a random card. However, the effect follows the original card.

Currently held magic card - Job Card (Magician).

Kai looked disappointed.

"This is useless to me. Changing the card doesn't create an effect, and no one will be scared of my tricks. I need a job that boosts physical abilities."

Shirone asked,

"The person who attacked us earlier, what do you think their job was?"

"Probably a Clown. They have the acrobatic ability to move quickly."

"Hmm, job cards can't be transferred anyway."

Shirone patted Kai and stood up.

"A Magician can still be a good strategy if used well. I'll be with you, so don't worry."

"But you're from… White."

Shirone took out his job card.

'Define the law.'

The card briefly glowed, and the chosen law changed from White to Black.

"How about now? We're on the same side, right?"

"…."

Using a Seeker to always side with the strong was common, so Shirone's decision was unexpected.

Although he could change the law at any time, it was enough to give Kai a minimum level of trust.

"Yes!"

As Kai stood up with a determined expression, Shirone smiled and pointed to the forest.

"Let's go. We need to gather weapon cards too. There should still be some left."

No matter how superior their physical abilities, Anarchy Mountain wasn't a place that could be completely searched in 30 minutes.

"Weapons reflect strongly in the Whiteblack moonlight."

As they searched the mountain, Kai explained.

"They don't reflect in regular light, but if we go to places with moonlight, we can find one or two."

Kai, who knew a lot, was helpful to Shirone even without combat abilities.

Following the moonlight, they saw a card sparkling like a star among the green grass.

"A weapon!"

Kai quickly ran over but hesitated to pick up the card, looking at Shirone.

"Oh, sorry. But weapons can be transferred…"

Relying on Shirone for his life, Kai couldn't readily check the card.

"It's okay. You pick it up and check it."

Since Shirone could protect himself with magic, he was willing to give up the card if it wasn't strategically important.

With permission granted, Kai's eyes sparkled with the excitement of a child on a treasure hunt.

"Oh, too bad. There's something better than this."

"What's wrong? Is it not good?"

"It's not trash. See for yourself."

The card was the same size as the job card, with a crude round shield drawn in the middle.

'Old Copper Shield. D grade.'

Old Copper Shield.

Description: Before smelting technology advanced, shields were made by casting copper.

Effect: Reduces damage by 10%.

"Just holding it reduces damage?"

"Yes, that's how Whiteblack's law works. It's the most common shield card. It would have been better if it were a High-Grade Copper Shield. It's a C grade and reduces damage by 20%."

"Still, it's something. You should keep it."

If they gathered several shield cards, Shirone would have a level of defense he could feel secure about.

Kai shook his finger side to side.

"Even if I keep it, it won't help much. A 10% damage reduction is almost nothing. You saw the Power Bomb earlier, right? That was just a B-grade card."

"Then what do you plan to do?"

"I'll use the Magician's special ability. The Magic Card. I'll change this to an S-grade and trade it with other participants' cards."

"Does that happen often?"

"A lot. It's common within the same zone, and near the end of the test, they even trade with enemies. After all, you have to survive to win."

It was the advice of a veteran who had practiced many times.

"Activate Magic Card!"

Kai raised his hand and shouted, and the Old Copper Shield card sparkled and transformed into an S-grade card.

Cardio-Pulmonary Resuscitation (Consumable).

Description: If you do your best, the heavens will help you.

Effect: You can revive a deceased participant in Whiteblack.

However, it cannot be used on yourself.

"Ta-da! This is the most popular CPR card. It can revive a teammate once, significantly boosting combat power. If traded as a team, it can be exchanged for several cards of the same grade."

"That makes sense with the law of resurrection. But it's made by magic. In the end, its effect is still the Old Copper Shield."

Kai pleaded with a desperate expression.

"I can't do it alone. They'd definitely kill me and take it. But if you support me, I can use this to get a lot of high-grade cards."

'Clever…'

No matter how young, Black was still Black.
