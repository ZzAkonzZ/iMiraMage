Chapter 458:

“Miro!”

Gaold took another painful step forward. He didn't know how it was possible.

All his senses had become extraordinarily sharp, and with that, the pain intensified.

Physical pain turned into mental anguish, which then seeped into his heart, knocking on the gates of hell.

The human heart is hell.

“Miro!”

As the restraining force grew stronger, Gaold's pain threshold continued to rise.

Even in the midst of excruciating pain, Gaold couldn't feel the changes in his body.

It hurt too much.

The heart has no substance, so it has no limits.

“Aaaagh!”

Pain Amplification 100x.

Gaold's face was so contorted it was barely recognizable as human.

Even the world's greatest mages and law enforcers felt a grotesque horror beyond humanity as they watched Gaold break free and move forward.

“H-how is this possible…”

Sein shouted,

“Gaold! Stop! You'll die!”

Gaold didn't give up.

His heightened nerves were focused solely on the receding figure of Miro.

-You can't do anything for me!

‘God.’

Pain Amplification 400x.

Thud! His nerves burst through his skin.

It felt like he had become pain itself, composed only of nerves.

Thump! Thump!

Each step felt like his bones were shattering, and shocks struck his brain.

Being alive had never felt so cursed.

Was this what it felt like to amplify an extremely unpleasant sensation a hundredfold?

“Stop him! Don't let him get close!”

The committee's eyes were filled with shock.

Even under anti-magic, his mind didn't collapse, and he continued forward, rejecting the laws.

“How can such a human…”

“Grrr!”

As his pain threshold exponentially increased, Gaold gritted his teeth.

His eyes lost their focus and burned, and the gates of hell began to open with his rage and hatred for the world.

“Maximize the pressure!”

At the committee's command, the mages and law enforcers poured all their power into restraining Gaold.

An unprecedented level of restraint, the outcome of which even they couldn't predict, was applied to Gaold.

“Grrr!”

At the same time, Gaold's mind shattered like glass.

His pupils rolled back, and blood continuously flowed from between his clenched teeth.

‘God.’

Crack. Crack.

With each movement, Gaold's bones cracked.

‘I said I love Miro.’

Pain Amplification 10,000x.

‘But how dare… someone like you…’

“W-what…”

Everyone watching turned pale.

Gaold's hair stood on end and began to turn white from the scalp.

“Someone like you…”

Pain is an intensely personal experience.

In pain that transcended the limits of living beings, Gaold was left with only himself.

It was a stark reality without gods, ideals, or aspirations.

That was all there was.

Screeeech!

As the gates of hell in his heart opened, demons that fed on Gaold's pain emerged.

These demons, summoned by pain beyond mortal limits, descended into the world, and everything around began to burn.

It was the beginning of Gaold's Great Inferno of Extreme Endurance.

Boom!

The anti-magic spells restraining him were nullified, and the laws were shattered, causing the law enforcers to collapse.

“Heee!”

Gaold, his thoughts scattered, opened his mouth and spat out blood-mixed saliva.

All he felt was pain.

Even breathing, even his heartbeat, just being alive was pain.

Yet he didn't stop.

He moved towards Miro.

To close that damnable 20-meter gap he could never reach.

Miro stopped moving, as if thinking or regretting something.

But without looking back, she continued walking towards Istas.

‘Miro! Miro! Miro! Miro!’

With his memory shattered by shock, the only concept left in Gaold's mind was Miro, and that blind emotion was the only painkiller in his agony.

“Activate the Royal Formation!”

At the command of the committee's highest authority, the sealing formation, prepared for emergencies, was activated.

A sealing formation was drawn under Gaold's feet, pulling the great evil to the ground.

“Aaaagh!”

The shock of Pain Amplification 10,000x was beyond the comprehension of any human or creature.

But Gaold didn't die. His time had already stopped.

From the wide-open gates of hell, a massive avatar of hatred, burning with fire, emerged.

Gaold screamed,

“Grrraaaagh!”

He unleashed his fury upon the world.

Gods, humanity, the world disappeared, and his hatred for all humans except himself, who was in pain, spread, turning the world into a hellish landscape.

“Grrraaaagh!”

It was such an extreme emotion that it felt almost pure.

‘Damn it!’

Sein gritted his teeth, holding back tears.

Even as a Servant, Sein's heart wavered before Gaold's extremity.

Like gas from molten lava erupting after thousands of years, his steel-like reason was pierced, and he had to acknowledge one thought.

‘Yes, Gaold! It's not over yet!’

Gaold's dislocated arms were pinned to the center of the sealing formation, and his legs twisted unnaturally, but his face still turned towards Miro.

At this rate, his body would be destroyed.

Before that could happen, Sein leaped forward.

Unable to hold back his tears, he grabbed Gaold's face and said,

“Live, Gaold! Not now!”

“Grrraaa! Miro, Miro…”

“It's not over yet. I'll do something! I'll get you to Miro no matter what! So please, live for now!”

“It doesn't hurt… Pain… I…”

“Accept my magic. Just a little longer, just a little more!”

Sein injected a sleep spell into Gaold's mind.

He knew that with Gaold's current endurance, even his teeth wouldn't work, but Gaold had to believe his words.

If it was Gaold, perhaps the purest and most foolish person in the world…

Sein cast his spell with all his might.

“Gaold, hold on. Just hold on. No one else can save Miro.”

“Grrr…”

Gaold trembled as he looked at Miro.

Just twenty steps.

The frustration of not being able to close that gap was nothing compared to the pain of losing Miro again.

‘Miro, I'll come for you. I…’

Gaold screamed in agony,

“Aaaagh!”

At the same time, Sein's magic finally began to take effect.

From the outskirts of the hellscape linked to Gaold's mind, shimmering light like an aurora closed in, gradually erasing the hellish landscape.

As the sealing formation's power weakened, Gaold's elongated arms fell limply to the ground.

Thud! Gaold collapsed, hitting his head on the ground.

The twenty judges, their guards, and the execution committee were all in a daze.

Even the world's top knights realized they were drenched in cold sweat only after the Great Inferno had disappeared and sighed in relief.

Gustav Havitz of the Three Realms said,

“He's dangerous. We should eliminate him now.”

Though he had been struck by Gaold while trying to monopolize Miro, he didn't hold a grudge.

It was an absurd thought, but perhaps Gaold could erase Miro's dimension.

“Let's observe a bit longer.”

As Havitz's subordinates moved, a woman's voice interrupted.

It was Terraze Mistra, the biological mother of Uorin and the queen of Kashan.

Even Havitz couldn't treat the queen of Kashan lightly.

Especially if she was Mitochondrial Eve, her decision wouldn't be based solely on personal motives.

Mistra watched as Sein carried away the unconscious Gaold.

‘Gaold…’

She thought that perhaps, in the distant future, his existence could bring about significant changes.

Gaold was urgently transported and treated.

He remained unconscious for nearly a month, and even after waking up, he was tormented by extreme pain transmitted through his hypersensitive nerves.

“Are you feeling any better?”

Gaold lay in bed, clenching his fists and trembling.

The pain was so overwhelming that he couldn't hear any words.

“Rest.”

Sein turned away.

He didn't know when it would be, but for the project to proceed, Gaold needed to be able to function somewhat normally.

“I had a dream.”

Sein stopped in his tracks at Gaold's words.

“I dreamed of going to Miro.”

A story that might come true in the distant future.

Sein accepted it as fate and asked,

“Really? How was it? Did you feel like you were flying with happiness?”

Gaold didn't answer that.

“I will go. Keep your promise.”

“Not for you. For Miro.”

That was Sein's last visit to Gaold.

It took Gaold another month to get out of bed.

The pain still tormented his nerves, but he couldn't stay lying down forever.

The graduation exam was approaching. To do anything, he had to become a mage first.

Gaold donned his uniform and went to the Yore Church.

As he silently looked at the circular symbol, Yora approached from behind.

“Gaold, it's been a while. How are you feeling?”

“…”

Yora looked at Gaold's back with pity.

She didn't know the details, but the story of a follower who had abandoned his faith had spread like a rumor.

She didn't know the details, but the story of a follower who had abandoned his faith had spread like a rumor.

It was clear that Gaold was that follower.

“Gaold, you must not blame God. There is a purpose in everything He does.”

Gaold finally turned to look at Yora, giving her his usual beautiful smile.

“No, Yora. I will never blame God.”

As Gaold walked past Yora towards the exit, there was a snapping sound.

Gaold's face twisted into a demonic expression as he clutched the pendant around his neck and tore at his chest.

‘From the beginning…’

His nails dug into his flesh, drawing blood.

The immense pain contorted his face, but his mouth stretched into a grin that reached his ears.

‘God never existed.’

Even after Sein finished his story, Shirone and the others silently stared at the burning torch.

The flames flickering before them seemed to mirror Gaold's heart.

“And so, Gaold abandoned God and wandered the world, seeking strength. He had no limits, so he couldn't adapt. Training in such extreme pain was sheer madness.”

Shirone shuddered at the thought.

Even if his pain threshold increased tenfold, he wouldn't want to walk, let alone train in magic.

“But humans are truly strange. He did it.”

“Did he overcome the pain?”

Sein shook his head.

“No. He accepted the pain itself. The pain he felt in his body became nothing compared to the pain of not being able to save Miro. Whether it was a thousand times or ten thousand times the pain, to Gaold, it was just a sensation traveling through his nerves. But Miro was different. She was an event, a phenomenon. When you dream of the impossible, the gates of hell in your heart open.”

Shirone pondered Sein's words.

Humans created the concept of impossibility, yet they also desired it.

The human heart is hell. Shirone finally understood Zulu's words.

Boom.

A distant explosion echoed through the night sky.
