Chapter 447:

The Sun and Moon Halos that emerged at the core of Babel's system conveyed all information to Sein.

It was as if Sein's consciousness had flowed into the system, and there he discovered a single blue flash piercing through the circuits tangled like a spider's web of light.

'This is…'

The Ultima System was just a single signal, but Sein couldn't extract any information from it.

It was a highly condensed concept, different from complexity or intricacy.

It was so vast that it was an extreme insight that human cognition couldn't grasp all at once.

'Who on earth created this?'

Sein followed the Ultima System endlessly upward.

Where the blue flash passed, a magic circle formed by an angelic system blocked the center as if obstructing the path.

The concept of the magic circle was also difficult for humans to read, but if it was complex, it was fortunate for Sein.

Analyzing the signal of the magic circle with the equalizing of the Sun and Moon Halos, Sein's eyes widened in shock.

'Damn! So that's what it was.'

From what he confirmed, no matter how perfect the angelic magic circle was, it couldn't disrupt this perfect single unified signal.

The only thing the magic circle's algorithm changed was the purpose of the metal angel's mission.

'Someone tampered with the enemy detection settings. It's not the rebels. Rather, this is…'

A weapon to kill angels.

Then Sein had only one task: to disable the angelic magic circle and revert the settings.

If he could do that, he could strike a counterblow to Heaven.

Sun and Moon Halos!

The halo, so clear that it didn't even seem to be spinning, began to dismantle the angelic magic circle.

"Grrr!"

Sein was overwhelmed by the far greater amount of information than expected.

'Damn it! This is just the information needed to change one enemy detection setting.'

He could feel how profoundly intelligent the blue flash penetrating the system was.

'No. At this rate, it's too late.'

Even with Sein's equalizing as a Savant, it would take at least 200 hours to completely dismantle the magic circle.

'There's no chance like this. I have to destroy it from the inside.'

The moment he embedded the Sun and Moon Halos into the center of the angelic magic circle, the halo's rotation stopped.

The sudden stop sent a powerful shock through Sein's head, almost shattering it.

"Grrr!"

The core logic of the magic circle was overwhelming Sein's logic.

Then the magic circle emitted a brilliant light and began to attack Sein's logic in reverse.

As the Sun and Moon Halos rotated in the opposite direction of the caster's logic, blood flowed from Sein's nose.

'Is this even possible? How can such a proposition exist?'

The angelic magic circle was completely negating the thought processes humans had developed over thousands of years.

Cracks appeared in the Sun and Moon Halos, drawing unpleasant lines.

Sein's eyes gradually rolled back, and blood seeped from between his clenched teeth.

'Is this the end…'

Sein's consciousness fell to the depths of the abyss.

At that moment, the Ultima System expanded its flash and permeated the magic circle.

The reverse rotation of the Sun and Moon Halos stopped.

Awakening from the brink of death, Sein shuddered at the unimaginable phenomenon.

'How can this be?'

The single signal was rapidly severing the complex connections of the angelic magic circle like light.

'What is this system?'

A single signal that perfectly encapsulated the entire universe.

Even the grand logic of angels was nothing more than a fool's babble before the only insight that could oppose the Akashic Records.

Reconnecting Ultima System.

Activating Babel's primitive system.

The small change Sein created made Cariel's logic system slightly inaccurate, and Babel began to reset.

As the magic circle shattered into fragments, Sein activated Equilibrium.

The two gears meshed, grinding Cariel's broken logic back into the void.

The Ultima System remained simple but had grown vast.

As the light expanded, everything finally unified into one.

Wooooong!

Babel's photon cannon ceased as if turning off a faucet.

Then its legs and arms, embedded in the ground, rose, and its upper body stood, returning to its pre-transformation state.

Boom! Boom! Boom!

As the pilot's tension eased, the power transmitted through the piper decreased, and dozens of Guroi knelt.

"What happened? Is it over?"

During the 20 seconds of photon cannon fire, the rebel command center was completely devastated.

Crude's Titan was damaged, and half of the command center's forces were dead.

Resetting Babel's program.

Babel, which had been in a combat stance, lowered its hands.

Then its electric wings disassembled into points, and its head and knees slowly lowered.

No signal.

Awaiting user registration.

Babel knelt, just as it had when it lay dormant in the ancient ruins, waiting for the next user to appear.

To the rebels, it was an infuriating sight.

The cockpit of a Guroi opened, and Kanya ran out with a signa.

"Aaaargh!"

Bang! Bang! Bang!

Every time she struck Babel's face with the sword, powerful explosions occurred, but not even a scorch mark appeared on the metal surface.

If it could withstand heat flash cannons, a mere signa couldn't harm it.

That made her even angrier.

Babel's unmoving form, as if accepting its punishment, plunged Kanya into despair.

"Sister, stop!"

Lena ran over, crying, and tried to stop Kanya.

Taking out her anger on a metal lump would only hurt the person hitting it.

"Let go! I'll kill it! Let go!"

"What if it starts working again? It's just a machine!"

"Aaaargh!"

Kanya threw the signa and screamed at the sky.

She was furious that she couldn't even avenge her father's death.

"Hoo!"

Sein wiped his nosebleed and slowly stood up.

Crude, who had descended from the Titan, approached calmly.

"You did it in the end. Thank you. You helped reduce the damage."

The damage was immense, but Crude acknowledged Babel's prowess.

"No, the system corrected its own error. I just unlocked it."

Crude looked at Babel with a serious expression.

"I've met many Norins, but I've never seen anyone as skilled as you. If you say that, what is that machine?"

"I don't know the details. The only thing I know is that it wasn't made to fight humans. It's an ancient weapon for archangels."

"An ancient weapon…"

Crude approached the inactive Babel.

Looking up at her face from below, he saw the red light on the panel still blinking.

"The power isn't off. Is it in sleep mode?"

"Probably. It seems the system reset when the angelic magic circle was destroyed."

"I see. Hmm."

Crude's eyes gleamed as he stood up.

"Then… how about we use this?"

Sein hadn't dismissed the idea.

But as someone who had directly explored Babel's system, he could confidently say it was nearly impossible.

"Babel's system is made of an integrated signal beyond human comprehension. We can't decode it."

Babel's capabilities surpassed those of the Titan.

If they could bring it to their side, it would be a tremendous asset, but what human could decode that signal?

'How can we use this?'

As Sein pondered, the cries of the victims seeped into his thoughts.

It wasn't darkness that covered the sun. It was the aura of death accumulated over eons.

As Hel, the ruler of Niflheim, grew in size and displayed its presence, Gaold's party trembled.

Death might be most akin to cold.

The aura of death, which couldn't be measured by temperature, penetrated to the bone, suppressing vitality.

"Hoo!"

Kangnam widened her eyes and infused strength into her body.

She had to overcome it with life.

If she couldn't activate each cell and prove she was alive, she would end up like the guide.

At that moment, Gaold's face twisted like a demon, and a powerful atmospheric pressure slammed into the ground.

Pain Sensitivity 100,000 times.

Boom!

The darkness was swept away like peeling wallpaper, but the aura of death soon reclaimed the space.

Even so, it was a strange phenomenon for Hel.

The aura that even blocked the sun couldn't be erased by mere air pressure.

'The will to live?'

It was a will that surpassed imagination.

"Arrogant one, why do you reject death?"

Hel raised its body and spoke in a solemn voice, but Gaold didn't respond, lost in thought.

'This is definitely tricky.'

Estimating the pain sensitivity needed to suppress an aura stronger than the Grim Reaper's was not easy.

When there was no answer, Hel spoke again.

"Did you walk into death seeking the detestable Miro? But what awaits you is only the god of the dead."

Miro.

The moment Gaold heard that word, he became a completely different person.

His pupils quaked, and all his blood vessels stood on end.

"How… did you know?"

"The dead know everything. They just don't interfere."

"Kekekeke! I see."

The whites of Gaold's eyes showed.

As the aura of extreme self-discipline spread from his body, even the aura of death recoiled in fear.

Sensing the danger, Kangnam approached.

She didn't know the limit of his pain sensitivity, but it had already surpassed the previous limit.

"Do you understand what you're doing? I told you not to…"

"Shut up."

Kangnam's mouth snapped shut.

There was no stopping him now. Gaold's mind was filled only with thoughts of Miro. Nothing else would be on his mind.

"Where is Miro? You'd better tell me. Unless you want to die again."

"Miro is…"

Hel's voice rose to a terrifying pitch.

"Kieeeee!"

The following words were a fierce wail that could stop a heart.

As the robe fell away, Hel's face, a composite of all deaths in existence, was revealed.

As the aura of death sharpened like a blade and surged forward, Zulu stepped in front of Kangnam and cast a spell.

"Phosmetary."

A giant bird made of light enveloped the two of them.

The dark aura struck them the next moment, but it passed through without causing any harm.

The Space-Time Bird, Phosmetary.

Though Zulu was famous as a lich summoner, Phosmetary was a 2nd-tier monster that only she could summon.

Phosmetary appeared once every four years on the equatorial island of Kanarika, flying for three seconds before disappearing, presumably to another dimension.

It took Zulu 12 years to successfully summon Phosmetary, having collected records of its sightings over millennia.

Considering it appeared for only three seconds every four years, the actual time spent was just nine seconds, making it a testament to Zulu's genius, celebrated alongside her lich summoning.

Boom! Boom! Boom!

Phosmetary transported the two to another space-time for three seconds, during which nothing could affect them.

For the first time, Kangnam could clearly see the aura of death passing before her eyes.

What she had thought was darkness was actually an infinite pattern of the faces of the dead, all screaming in agony.

"Three seconds. I can't hold it any longer."

There was no need to listen to Zulu's words.

As Hel revealed its true form, Gaold was already charging forward with a demonic face.

Miro! Miro! Miro! Miro! Miro! Miro!

Only one word flashed through his mind at the speed of electricity, and that blind conviction drew in even greater pain.

"The god of the dead? You're just an empty shell…!"

Gaold raised his right hand high and swung it down, casting a spell.

Pain Sensitivity 500,000 times - Air Pressing.

Hel's form disintegrated, falling like tens of thousands of water droplets.

Boom!

The delayed shockwave made the entire landscape tremble as if the planet itself was shaking.
