Chapter 1049: New Humanity (4)

"I'm back."

Barbelve carefully laid the unconscious Borbor in front of Piri.

"Yay, meat."

The girl's eyes, as black as obsidian, glowed with a small light as she examined Borbor.

'Piri's eyes.'

Barbelve shivered.

'Her gaze doesn't count as an act of observation. Therefore, she is completely free from the law.'

She could control quantum signals.

"Now, where should I start eating?"

Just as Piri was about to tear into Borbor's arm, the inner door burst open.

"Lady Piri."

The locust-humans were crying.

"We are outraged. Our comrades were brutally killed by that woman. We beg for a chance to avenge them."

Piri, looking back and forth between the locust-humans and Borbor, readily agreed.

"Fine, take him."

No matter how exceptional a human was, they were just food.

"We are the New Humanity. We are not like humans who fight each other over food."

"Thank you! Thank you!"

As the locust-humans dragged Borbor away, Barbelve shrugged awkwardly.

"I thought you'd like it."

"You need to learn to share. Humans have ruled by consuming everything on the planet. Now, we are just making humans our livestock."

To the New Humanity, humans were just that.

"Argh!"

At the scream from outside, Barbelve approached the window and looked at the castle wall.

"I'll catch a better one."

As a breeze swept through the room, the locust's body vanished without a trace.

"Growl. Growl."

Tanju Ra shivered as he watched Shirone, surrounded by shimmering light.

'Is this Yahweh's power?'

The destruction of over 300 locust-humans was the result of a single strike from the Hand of God.

'The state of seeing the principles of the universe as a single form. It could be called the pinnacle of enlightenment, but…'

The White Dragon Asraiker spoke.

"Don't be too surprised. The Messiah is the one who will save humanity. It's natural for him to be on a different level."

Tanju Ra waved his fan.

"Hmph, surprised? I was just confirming the Great Star's skill with the eyes of a magnanimous person."

He couldn't bear to look at Asraiker, whose extreme admiration made him uncomfortable.

"Locusts…"

As Shirone muttered with a serious expression, the Earth Dragon Gaitan stepped forward.

"Is something bothering you?"

"In the East, locust swarms are a symbol of disaster. They destroy crops. So this mutant form isn't a coincidence. It's a message to humans."

"That interpretation is possible. But they don't seem very threatening."

"The problem is that the mutation is controlled."

That was the key.

"Hexa can change the law, but she can't control mutations. Humans have minds. Their quantum signals would collide, creating an average."

They would mutate into countless forms based on their individuality.

Asraiker stepped in.

"Are you saying our enemy can perfectly control the human mind? That's impossible. There's no way to observe both the position and speed of an electron simultaneously."

Gaitan added.

"The mind is a quantum signal. The trajectory of a thrown stone is determined by the law, but what a human will feel in a second only exists as a probability. Even if you offer bread to a starving person, you can't guarantee they'll eat it."

Shirone was still deep in thought.

"The quantum signal embodies the uncertainty of the mind, and that's why humans define the world. If there's a being that can deny this fact…"

Gaitan fell silent at the chilling conclusion.

'The outside world.'

The only phase that can observe both the position and speed of an electron simultaneously is outside the universe.

'And that means…'

Even the human mind can be precisely measured there.

"Messiah."

"I know."

Shirone finished his thoughts.

"Are we minds or programs? It doesn't matter. Minds make mistakes, and programs have errors."

The Miracle Stream shimmered and transformed into the massive Hand of God.

"What do you think, Tanju Ra?"

Tanju Ra stopped fanning and asked,

"Yes? What do you mean?"

"The mutant that felt like it came from beyond the heavens. Do you think the Hand of God can subdue it?"

The answer came after a long pause.

"…Probably not."

The two apostles showed displeasure, but Shirone nodded calmly.

"I understand. I'll give the orders now. Tanju Ra, report to the Ivory Tower. Gaitan and Asraiker, rescue Borbor."

"But we can't leave you, Messiah…"

"The difficult task is yours. Borbor's mental fluidity is a crucial weapon for humanity. We can't sacrifice him now. Use any means necessary to save him."

As the two apostles showed reluctant expressions, a massive figure fell from the sky with a thud.

Tanju Ra's body stiffened at the familiar locust form.

"I am Barbelve."

"…We'll go ahead."

Asraiker and Gaitan leaped into the sky and disappeared.

Watching them with his eyes, Barbelve turned his gaze back to Tanju Ra.

"You're the one from before. Did you bring stronger humans? It was a good idea to let you go."

His pride was deeply wounded, but there was no time to consider his emotions.

"Ugh!"

As Tanju Ra kicked off the ground and fled, Barbelve's vocal organs trembled.

"Hehe! I knew it. But I don't like letting you go twice."

Barbelve vanished without a trace and reappeared behind Tanju Ra like an afterimage.

'I can kill him and take him.'

Just as the locust's claws were about to tear into Tanju Ra's neck, a massive force was felt from the side.

"Huh?"

When he came to his senses, he was enveloped in light, and his consciousness vibrated.

Boom!

Barbelve tumbled roughly across the ground, crashing through all the buildings in Bukjeon.

With his head buried in the cold dirt for a long time, he turned and asked,

"Who are you?"

Shirone was walking towards him.

"That's what I want to ask. Where are you from? Why are you in this world?"

He didn't want to talk about Piri.

"Are you the king of humans?"

With his limbs on the ground and his back arched, Barbelve looked like a perfect locust.

"Hehe, if that's the case, then it's great. I can show our king the best taste."

Before he finished speaking, Barbelve leaped and swung his claws at Shirone.

Quantum movement activated.

"Ugh!"

All of Shirone's probabilities were caught by the locust's claws, causing hundreds of shockwaves per second.

'As expected, this guy…'

As the force penetrated the Miracle Stream's barrier, Shirone realized.

'He's different.'

It was like cutting out a part of one picture and pasting it onto a completely different one.

'He's not a being created by the constants of this universe. He's an entirely different signal.'

When the universe was born, the total amount of energy was essentially fixed.

In other words, the mathematical and physical limits of the universe are set.

'If the outside world exists, our world is blocked by the barrier of rational numbers.'

For example, if the limit of the most powerful explosion or the fastest speed in the universe is set at 99.

'No phenomenon can exceed 99. If it does, it becomes an error, and the system will correct the world.'

But Barbelve seemed to be crossing that limit.

The total amount of numbers might be similar, but in certain aspects, he overwhelmed Shirone.

'Especially his time perception ability.'

The temporal perception that penetrated even the quantum signals of the senses.

'This is dangerous.'

Feeling the threat from its early form, Shirone increased his power.

"Kieee!"

As the Hand of God struck the ground with a thud, Barbelve's body tightened as he blocked it with his face.

'Amazing.'

Even if he was livestock, such a level deserved applause.

'No. We are the rulers.'

Recalling the philosophy of the New Humanity, Barbelve moved even faster and attacked.

Light flashed in all directions, and the tiled houses evaporated from the heat.

'Here.'

Barbelve broke through the barrier and punched Shirone.

A mushroom cloud rose.

In the cleared area, hundreds of meters in radius, Shirone exhaled deeply.

"Hoo!"

Barbelve's shoulders trembled.

'He blocked it?'

A physically impossible phenomenon.

'Could he be like Lady Piri? No, that can't be. Livestock can't have such abilities…'

Hand of God - Technique of the Hand.

If Barbelve was sure of something, Shirone could deceive his mind.

"Where did you learn such childish tricks!"

As Barbelve raised his other hand, a sonic boom was heard from the sky.

A sword emitting white light pierced the locust's side.

"Aaah!"

Shirone's eyes widened.

'This is…'

Sermon - Gate.

As Barbelve screamed and was pushed back dozens of meters, his body trembled.

"It hurts! What is this!"

Moments later, a man fell from the sky and landed next to Shirone.

"Nane."

"You're working hard as always, Shirone."

They weren't the type to shake hands and laugh, so Shirone asked cautiously.

"Why are you here suddenly?"

"That."

Nane pointed at the locust-human.

"I was watching from the moon, and I didn't like the feeling. I also have something to discuss with you."

"Discuss?"

"The outside world. Things have gotten much more complicated because of your meddling."

Shirone frowned.

"That's your perspective. If you had thought differently from the start, none of this would have happened."

"These worthless livestock!"

Barbelve shouted, trembling all over.

"How dare you harm the one who will lead the future of the New Humanity! I'll tear you to pieces and chew you up!"

Their conversation was interrupted.

"I retract my promise to deliver you alive to Lady Piri! Instead, I'll give you pain! Immense pain! The worst pain a living being can feel…!"

"Quiet."

As both Shirone and Nane turned their heads simultaneously, Barbelve's shouting abruptly stopped.

"Let's deal with this first, then we'll talk."

"Agreed."

Shirone turned first, and Nane followed him, walking slowly.

'What, what is this?'

Barbelve involuntarily stepped back.

'I'm feeling fear from mere livestock? No, that's impossible.'

Yet his legs wouldn't move.

'What are these beings?'

Yahweh's Hand of God covered the sky, and Buddha's teachings poured down like rain.

"Kiaaa!"

Born to be a king.

And at this moment, for the first time, Barbelve regretted choosing life.

'No, no!'

As the flash exploded, what shouldn't exist in this world returned to nothingness.
