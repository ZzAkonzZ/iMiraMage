Chapter 1020: Underwater Work (4)

Benahar hurriedly packed his belongings.

‘I have to leave.’

He didn't know where to go in the vast ocean, but it certainly wasn't this ship.

His attendant, watching with a puzzled expression, asked,

“Chief Secretary, what’s going on?”

“It’s over. It’s all over.”

He stuffed the expensive watch he had bought into the depths of his bag and zipped it up.

“You should pack quickly too. In the worst-case scenario, this ship might sink.”

The Chief Secretary wasn’t one to talk nonsense, but this time it didn’t make sense.

“The ship will sink? Is it the demons? Or is a tsunami coming?”

Benahar hesitated.

Could Yahweh be more dangerous than the demons or a tsunami?

“Ugh…”

The memory of Shirone’s gaze from earlier in the day made his metabolism go haywire again.

How narrow is the human brain?

‘I couldn’t even imagine it until I felt it with my whole body.’

“Chief Secretary.”

As Benahar trembled like a madman, the attendant became even more suspicious of his behavior.

“Are you unwell? Should I call the medical officer?”

“Yahweh…”

Benahar gritted his teeth.

“Yahweh is on this ship. He’s negotiating with the Foreign Minister right now.”

“Isn’t that a good thing? As you know, Cairns’ negotiation skills are top-notch. Of course, Yahweh is also under the protection of the Ivory Tower, but in this case…”

“Haha. Hahaha.”

It was so absurd that he laughed even in his terrified state.

“Do you know what Yahweh is?”

“He’s one of the four superhumans and a symbol of philanthropy. He’s here to help the refugees, and I personally support him.”

Supporting one of the Four Virtues was once a trend among politicians…

“Do you think it’s possible?”

Only when one experiences the extreme truth like Benahar did, can they realize it.

“Loving all humanity, do you really think that’s something a human can do? No way. That’s madness at the extreme of some thought. It’s not sane. It’s monstrous.”

The attendant blinked.

“Extreme evil is bad, and Yahweh is good? No, none of the Four Virtues are normal. They’re outside the human category, and they fight to enforce their own thoughts.”

“But… Yahweh is a good person.”

“I saw it.”

Benahar’s hand trembled.

“That gaze. That mind. The moment I thought it didn’t matter if I blew up the ship, Yahweh…”

It wasn’t murderous intent, hostility, or anger.

“Yes, deprivation. At that moment, Yahweh clearly felt that I was just ‘unnecessary.’ No matter how you think about it, that’s not a human emotion.”

It was closer to divine logic.

“When that happens, your mind goes blank. You can’t think of anything. Negotiations, deals… those are things humans do in the human world.”

If he could save all humanity, Shirone didn’t care if he wasn’t human.

“A world where common sense doesn’t apply. But I want to be human. I want to make money, meet women, and live boasting even fake honor. That’s what we’re all trying to do. I’m leaving.”

Without waiting for the attendant’s response, Benahar opened the door and left.

The door slammed shut, leaving the room as chaotic as his mind.

Cairns was displeased.

“Hmm.”

Shirone’s smile was annoying, but crushing his strategy was even more irritating.

“Even if you say that, I don’t know who this Fermi person is. Is he famous?”

“A classmate from magic school.”

‘Unbelievable.’

If it was a personal acquaintance from the start, any answer would make him look like a fool.

“Sigh. Shirone, let’s not do this between professionals. Emotions don’t…”

“Cairns.”

Shirone interrupted.

“Let go.”

Cairns, still holding Langi’s waist, moved his jaw and engaged in a staring contest.

A suffocating silence.

After a moment, the hand coiled around her waist like a snake slowly withdrew.

“I apologize if I made you uncomfortable.”

Cairns avoided a confrontation for now, but his gaze remained cold.

“I didn’t mean to upset you. I just wanted to emphasize that you have a choice.”

“That’s why you’re third-rate.”

His tone was calm again, but his words were harsh.

“I mentioned my classmate’s name because he knows me better than anyone. I’ve never met anyone who understands humans more deeply than he does.”

After seeing Cairns’ negotiation style, Shirone could guess Fermi’s strategy.

“The fact that you’re offering a choice means you don’t know anything about me. That guy…”

He didn’t remember, but…

“He wouldn’t have given me any choice. He would have created a situation where I had no choice but to follow. He knows that.”

Otherwise, Shirone wouldn’t have dealt with the drug lord.

“I’m starting to get angry.”

He was already angry.

“I don’t mind giving Langi to the pirates or having the Five Great Stars help me. Neither is a loss. That’s the art of negotiation.”

Cairns extended his hand as if offering.

“The rest is your choice, Shirone. Keep Langi or abandon her. What’s left?”

“Your resolve.”

As Shirone’s eyes turned cold, the entire dining room began to shake.

“Th-this is…!”

Cairns stood up awkwardly, and Langi’s face turned pale as she looked around.

“Don’t worry. I’m not trying to intimidate you with force. I’m just… in a bad mood.”

Are you prepared to accept even that?

“The real reason I’m upset is that your options completely exclude the possibility that I won’t choose anything.”

“Well, that’s…”

As Cairns tried to walk around the table, Shirone’s eyes widened.

“Sit.”

Even if he didn’t obey, his legs trembled, and he couldn’t stand any longer.

Shirone continued.

“So now I’m going to change positions. If I choose one of your options, I’ll feel defeated. I might lose my temper because I’m in such a bad mood. The odds are fifty-fifty.”

“Now that we’re on equal footing, let’s continue the negotiation. If you ask me to choose, fine. I’ll choose. But what happens afterward, I don’t know…”

Shirone spoke calmly.

“Can you handle it?”

The public’s view of Yahweh.

If he thought he could gain an advantage in negotiations by taking a hostage, he only knew half of Shirone.

‘Nothing will change. If I say it, Shirone will choose. But what happens afterward…’

Can he handle it?

‘No, he wouldn’t really sink the ship. It’s just a bluff. I’ve dealt with threats like this before.’

That was true, but the person in front of Cairns was entirely different.

Scary.

‘What if he really does it?’

It was an honest feeling, and he realized what it meant to negotiate on equal terms with the star of the Ivory Tower.

“Langi.”

He tried to hide his emotions, but Cairns’ face was already twisted with humiliation.

“Go to Shirone.”

It was annoying to treat people like objects, but the key to hostage negotiation was composure.

“Well, that…”

After hesitating for a moment, Langi hurriedly gathered her things and moved to Shirone’s side.

The ship’s trembling stopped, and Shirone’s smile returned as if nothing had happened.

“Thank you for your consideration. You’re a good negotiator. Arachne’s future is bright.”

It was a genuine compliment.

“Haha! I couldn’t win. I tried desperately to gain the upper hand, but I got hit.”

Watching Cairns cool himself with a hand fan, Shirone raised his evaluation of him.

“No. If the result is good, it’s good. With the big issue resolved, the small ones will be over soon.”

“Of course. Hey, is the food ready yet? It’s cold. Bring something warm.”

The staff quickly replaced the food, and Cairns stood up and poured the wine himself.

“Let’s toast. To the prosperity of the Kingdom of Arachne and the Ivory Tower. I’ll do my best.”

You can’t always know a person’s true intentions, but sometimes you don’t need to delve too deeply.

“Yes. Then.”

Clinking glasses in the center of the table, Shirone brought the sweet wine to his lips.

‘…This is hard.’

The dazzling chandelier on the ceiling seemed like something from another world.

‘Where am I… right now?’

The Next Morning

The emergency alarm of the sailing ship Shirone was on echoed across the horizon.

The sound was deafening for those on the ship, and everyone covered their ears as they rushed to the deck.

“What’s going on?”

As those in charge of security calmed the people, Shirone entered the captain’s cabin.

“What’s happening?”

“We’re under attack! Pirates!”

Looking at the northeastern sea pointed out by the captain, black dots shimmered on the horizon.

“Dionas?”

“Most likely, but they haven’t entered territorial waters yet.”

Poine spoke.

“If they aimed for this, they chose the right moment. If caught later, they can deny it. The problem is…”

“What?”

Shirone tilted his head.

“We finished talking with Arachne. Why come all the way out here with such a large force?”

There was only one possible explanation.

“They never intended to negotiate with Arachne.”

“I think so too. They plan to steal all the treasures Arachne brought as tribute and pretend otherwise. It’s a pirate-like strategy, but…”

Poine spoke softly.

“It doesn’t seem like a strategy to use against a powerful nation like Arachne. Do they have some assurance?”

“Maika’s artifacts.”

It was the first thought that came to mind.

“Execute the evacuation program. Then we’ll distance the civilians at maximum speed. We need to finish before the enemies get within range.”

“I’ll do my best.”

The captain grabbed the loudspeaker and shouted.

“To all passengers on board! This is an emergency! All women and children, except trained personnel, immediately board the A7 lifeboats. I repeat!”

As people evacuated to the rear, Shirone and Poine arrived at the bow of the ship.

“Miracle Stream.”

The light smoke gathered and transformed into a giant lens, revealing the distant ships.

“Armed ships.”

As Poine muttered, smoke rose from one of the ships, and a cannon was fired.

“What?”

The distance was too far for the cannonball to reach, so Shirone stepped out of the lens to see it in reality.

An incredibly fast object approached in a straight trajectory, seemingly ignoring gravity.

‘Faster than the speed of sound?’

The fleeing people looked back and pointed at the sky in shock.

“Cannon fire!”

Before the words were finished, the cannonball approached, and Poine stepped in front of Shirone.

A loud explosion erupted.

“Well, well.”

Poine, who had been pushed across the deck, caught the cannonball near her temple with one hand.

“Interesting device.”

Smoke rose from the crumpled iron ball.
