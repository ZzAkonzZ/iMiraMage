Chapter 759: Possibility (1)

Minerva and Jayce, who had infiltrated the 48th Military Facility, cleared a path as they ruthlessly cut down the stationed forces.

Jayce had initially volunteered to act as their guide, but it proved unnecessary—no one could even approach Minerva.

"Please... spare me...!"

The aura of death Minerva exuded was enough to bring soldiers to their knees, groaning in agony as they died.

The Five Stars of the Ivory Tower.

Jayce herself was an unofficial Class-3 archmage and had always been confident in her abilities—but this was on another level entirely.

She hadn't even seen Minerva use magic yet.

Minerva, who had crushed the army with aura alone, headed toward the laboratory in the central command building.

"Wait!"

Jayce cried out hastily.

"This is where the original Garas is kept! If we open it, all hell will break loose!"

"Garas has no original. Or rather, no one knows what the original is. They're creatures that destroy biological systems."

"That’s what makes it even more dangerous. They're completely different from the modified hound beasts that were bred to species level. They'll latch onto anything they see and start reproducing."

"Doesn’t matter. Open it."

Jayce couldn’t defy her and reluctantly released the lock.

Another steel door appeared, and beyond that, the prison holding the Garas was revealed.

"Grurrr! Grurrrr!"

As Minerva stepped forward, grotesque sounds erupted from every direction.

They had never been this agitated before.

Jayce trembled as she followed, and Minerva came to a stop in the center of the chamber.

"They smell the witch. For them, that’s the most powerful aphrodisiac there is."

"Should we open the cells one by one and deal with them?"

"No."

Minerva narrowed her eyes, then waved her Jet. Her body transformed into that of a young girl.

"Come now, my sweet little ones."

The moment the words left her lips, dozens of steel doors exploded open with a bang.

"Kyahhhhhh!"

Jayce's face turned pale—she had seen the Garas through experiments, but this was beyond reason.

They had overpowered even Minerva’s killing aura.

And yet, Minerva simply stood there with her eyes half-lidded, calmly watching.

A fate destined to become a witch.

By the time she accepted that escape was impossible, her body and mind had already been shattered.

"If this is my fate, then I have no choice but to accept it."

Dozens of Garas swarmed over her.

"Minerva!"

Jayce turned to flee, certain it was too late—but something incredible happened.

"Graahh! Graaaaah!"

The entangled mass of Garas bubbled like crude oil, and their numbers began to dwindle.

"The Witch’s Breath."

Now with her pipe between her lips, Minerva exhaled smoke as she emerged from between the Garas.

"What did you do?"

"I let them enjoy themselves—to the fullest. With each other."

From what Jayce knew, the Garas’ reproductive drive was the pinnacle of biological instinct, but they never coupled with their own kind.

"Ten times human libido? Then right now, it must be a hundred times that. At that level, they lose all inhibition."

"A hundred times..."

The fused mass resembled a black, oily blob, and countless mutants were being born and erased within it.

Minerva watched idly and spoke.

"A biological system is composed of reproduction and consumption. Garas maximize reproductive compatibility and expand their ecosystem. But when they mate with their own kind..."

Jayce turned as Minerva raised a finger.

"Eventually, evolution becomes impossible. After countless mutations, they’ll choose stability. Which means..."

"They return to the original form."

Minerva gave no reply, just watched.

What appeared before them was a black sphere about one meter in diameter.

Like a frog egg, it trembled within a translucent membrane, then split into two.

"A cell..."

As the fission continued, the size of each sphere shrank, but their number grew exponentially.

Once the number of cells passed 40 trillion, the shape of a person began to emerge.

"I've always wanted to meet you, Argones."

The father of all living beings in the universe.

Great Star, the mother figure, once called Argones a biological program.

Argones opened its mouth, the mucus receding from its throat to reveal a voice.

"You wanted to meet me?"

The tone was utterly flat, as if it had no concept of pitch or inflection—like someone mimicking speech without understanding language.

Minerva didn’t care.

"Anke Ra is gone. That couldn’t have happened without your involvement. What are you planning?"

If it truly was a biological program, then it must have been Argones who provided Anke Ra’s body.

"I plan nothing. I spread life. I fill empty space with more entities, even where there is no sun."

"A diligent bureaucrat, aren’t you? This world might close completely soon, you know."

"That doesn't matter, my daughter."

Minerva’s brow twitched.

"You are the lowest probability and also the key probability created from me. Kill as you please. Give birth as you please. That is the meaning of the probability you possess."

"I wasn’t created to serve your needs."

"Think whatever you want. I didn’t create you either. You’re just the result of probability."

"Bastard."

A murderous glint filled Minerva’s eyes.

"Is that supposed to explain the pain I went through?"

"Mutations are eliminated."

Argones’ body turned to a watery mush and seeped into the ground.

"It’s dangerous!"

Argones re-emerged from every wall in the form of mucus, attacking Minerva and Jayce.

"Mucus."

Where waves of slime came crashing down, no one remained. Minerva had leapt into a corner and slammed her Jet into the ground.

"Breath of Destruction."

Time seemed to accelerate around her. Black smoke surged outward at a terrifying speed.

Jayce, standing behind her, sensed the magnitude and cast a barrier spell just as the explosion launched the smoke forward.

Krrrrrrumble!

From beyond the black smoke came the sound of buildings collapsing, and the destruction spread uncontrollably.

"How far will it go…?"

When the magical smoke finally cleared, Jayce trembled.

The first spell she had ever seen from Mirac Minerva had turned everything in sight to ash.

"This isn’t magic. It’s a disaster."

For two hundred meters in front of them, nothing remained above ground. Only blackened hills of ash.

"Tch. So annoying."

Minerva, now back in her adult form, spun her Jet and glanced at Jayce.

"Let’s go. There’s still one more thing we need to confirm."

Jayce couldn’t find the words—she just nodded over and over.

If it was this woman...

She would deliver the perfect revenge against the Republic of North Aymond for killing her husband.

“Let me go! Let me out! I just want to go home!”

Baseto realized what kind of situation he was in the moment he laid eyes on the structure before him.

A shabby hut.

To the laborers, it was known as the “Eternal Hut”—a place no one ever returned from once they were thrown inside.

“I did everything you told me to! Why—ugh!”

A soldier struck the back of Baseto’s head with the hilt of his saber.

“When you’re told to go in, you go in. Quit whining and get inside.”

Even doing your job too well was a problem.

The soldiers had seen Baseto keep his cool and make sound decisions under pressure. That alone had sealed his fate.

“You’re fixed here starting today.”

“You bastards! What did I even do wrong?!”

Another dull thud. The soldiers grabbed him by the scruff and threw him into the hut.

“Consider yourself lucky. A private room, regular meals—not something the other laborers get to enjoy.”

They were telling him to rot alone in a cell with no means of suicide and beg like a dog for scraps.

“Goddamn bastards...!”

If he were still among the other workers, he could’ve at least begged someone to strangle him in his sleep.

“Let me go home. Please, let me go...”

Curled up in the corner of the hut, sobbing, Baseto suddenly sensed someone and looked up.

“What the—?!”

As if stairs had opened under the floor, Shirone was rising from beneath the hut, piercing through it from below.

“Whew… Finally found you.”

Zion’s Law, which covered the entire world, had intentional gaps scattered throughout for efficiency. Shirone had used one.

“Eek! A g-ghost!”

Baseto scrambled back against the wall, kicking his legs wildly in fear.

He’d suffered so many horrors, he thought the reaper himself had finally come for him.

“Shhh.”

Shirone placed a finger to his lips to calm him, then checked the area outside before speaking.

“Are you Verdi’s father?”

The fear in Baseto’s eyes disappeared instantly, replaced with fury as he lunged.

“How do you know Verdi?! My family—where are they?! What did you do to my daughter?!”

He must’ve thought his family was being used as hostages.

“They’re safe. I came here after talking to Albas. I’ll get you out of here.”

“Albas...? He’s alive?”

Relief washed over Baseto’s face.

“I’ll take you to them. While I finish what needs to be done here, I need you to hide somewhere safe.”

“Finish what...? Do you even know where you are? This is a dictatorship ruled by Igor.”

The Five Stars of the Ivory Tower were beyond any nation’s authority.

“I have a way. But first, we need to leave. If the soldiers show up—”

Shirone paused, ears pricking up.

“What was that?”

There was a blast outside the hut, followed by screams echoing through the area.

“Hold on—no, come with me!”

Judging by the scale of the explosion, staying indoors was more dangerous.

The moment they stepped outside, demons had already overrun the area, slaughtering soldiers and laborers alike.

“Verdi! My wife!”

As Baseto panicked and stomped his feet in a frenzy, Shirone grabbed him and activated teleportation.

“It’s dangerous!”

A fireball exploded.

Shirone turned his head to see a woman in North Aymond uniform casting a spell.

‘Why is she attacking her own side?’

It was Jayce—the infiltrator from South Aymond.

“Tch! Not bad...!”

She caught a glimpse of Shirone dodging the spell and flew at him using Fly magic.

To a spy, there were no real allies. Everyone here was her enemy.

‘I’ll kill them all! You disgusting bastards...!’

Six years of her youth spent undercover, and in the end, all that awaited her was the death of her husband—her only family.

“Feel what I felt!”

With Minerva at her back, Jayce feared nothing. At close range, she cast Invisibility.

Anyone relying solely on vision would already be as good as dead—but…

‘This way!’

Shirone’s senses were three levels above human norm. His Photon Cannon locked precisely onto her.

‘How did he...?!’

Jayce’s head spun in disbelief.

‘Press!’

Her experience as a third-rank archmage activated, compressing the air into a dense shield at the last second.

Boom!

The Photon Cannon’s impact shook the shield like steel and smashed into her abdomen.

“Guh!”

The invisibility broke. As the shockwave hurled her through the air, Jayce realized—

‘He’s not just any mage.’

It was a level of impact she had never experienced in her life.
