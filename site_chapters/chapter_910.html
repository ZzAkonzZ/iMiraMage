Chapter 910: Ultimately Human (3)

The Abyssal Cliffs.

Where an earthquake of magnitude 12.4 had swept through, there were no longer any valleys, just endlessly deep cliffs formed by the torn earth.

"Arghhh!"

From the depths of that deep chasm, Yoojeong leaped high into the air with a mighty kick.

Her eyes, smeared with black dust, fiercely glinted gold.

"This damn—cough! cough!"

Air hissed from where her throat had been cut.

'Where is it?'

The corpses of demons filled half the landscape, but there was no sign of Satan.

Shaking off the dirt, Yoojeong plucked a single hair from her shiny, mane-like hair.

'Line of Healing.'

The hair she tossed into the air started vibrating rapidly, emitting a golden light.

The string of light created a new phenomenon, gradually healing Yoojeong's throat.

"Huff!"

The moment the hole in her neck closed, the first thing she did was take a deep breath.

"You son of a—"

She glared furiously.

"Come out, don't hide!"

Her curse spread in all directions, but the demon corpses remained silent.

"Damn! Did he escape?"

As Yoojeong twirled her staff, preparing to chase after Satan, she heard footsteps.

"Huh?"

She turned her head nonchalantly, an unknowing tension marking her face.

Across a 20-meter wide chasm torn by the earthquake, Tess was watching Yoojeong.

"Is there a survivor?"

As the daughter of the Spy family Elzain, she was suited to assess the earthquake's magnitude and the movements of demons, but…

'That woman isn't right.'

The person who sent chills down Yoojeong's spine was Rian, who had just landed beside Tess.

"Huh? It's a human?"

Yoojeong blinked, staring intently at Rian, who was observing her.

'Fire Eye Gold Seal.'

Seeing through the world's truths with that eye, she finally understood the source of her unease.

'She's just like me.'

She had realized the Idea.

"I'm not human."

At Yoojeong’s statement, Rian tilted his head.

"Then, a demon?"

"A stone monkey."

Tess turned her head with a perplexed expression.

"What to do? She seems to be in shock."

"No, it's the truth."

The woman, exuding an enormous presence that made any statement believable, asked.

"Did you cause the earthquake?"

Hearing Rian's question, Tess frowned.

"What nonsense…"

Magnitude 12.4 earthquake.

With the entire Abyssal Cliff collapsed, it was impossible to determine the movements of demons.

'It's not something a human can do.' Yoojeong said.

"Yep. I did it." Tess’s face went blank, and both Rian and Yoojeong simultaneously asked.

"Who are you?"

Silence again.

This time, Rian spoke first.

"We need to investigate what happened here. Can you cooperate?"

"Hmm, cooperate."

As Yoojeong scratched her waistband, Tess’s face reddened.

'Really a monkey, huh?'

Yoojeong smiled.

"I don’t want to."

"Then we have no choice but to force it."

The moment Rian grabbed his greatsword, Yoojeong’s figure blurred into an afterimage.

'So fast...'

Before he could finish his thought, Yoojeong’s staff was hurtling towards Rian's forehead.

Rian dodged the blow by turning his head and aimed his greatsword at the enemy’s waist.

Rrrrumble!

A mach-speed strike split the earth horizontally.

'I dodged it.'

Rian looked up.

Not as fast as Natasha, but her reflexes seemed to surpass hers.

Yoojeong's eyes sparkled.

'She attacks with information.'

She realized why she, immune to physical force, had her throat slashed by Havitz's sword.

"If that’s the case…"

Yoojeong plucked another hair, blew on it, and a string phenomenon occurred, creating numerous clones.

"What's that now?"

As countless images filled the sky and plummeted down, Rian gritted his teeth and raised his sword.

A massive flash passed, and through the clearing view, the real Yoojeong was falling.

"Kiiiii!"

The moment Yoojeong swung her staff vertically, extending its length, it struck Rian's greatsword.

"Kuuuuugh!"

Realizing the collision had the weight of tens of tons, and was still increasing, he scoffed.

"Is that all?"

As his legs were buried up to the knees in the ground, Rian lifted his greatsword.

'There’s no weight.'

Yoojeong, who had reduced her staff to the speed of light, landed and spun around.

"Mystic Lotus."

Spreading her legs wide, she thrust her staff forward, and like buckshot, afterimages sprang forth.

Pop! Pop! Pop! Pop! Pop! Pop!

Dozens of holes burst through Rian’s flesh.

An enigmatic smile flickered across Yoojeong’s face as she glanced at Tess, but then her expression froze.

Despite witnessing a colleague's death, the fleeting oddness in her face quickly faded.

"Hufffff!"

Feeling a chill, she turned to see Rian, already regenerating.

Creak! Creak!

His muscle fibers twisted as Rian’s body transformed into a demonic figure, and he slammed his greatsword down vertically.

'Must block…'

Yoojeong retracted her staff to defend her head.

'Huh?'

As if in a dream, time slowed, and her arm wouldn’t rise.

'What? What? What?'

Behind Rian, the world opened up, and a cutting will surged forward.

Yoojeong screamed.

"Kiyaaaa!"

Just in time, her staff’s core was struck by Rian's greatsword like lightning.

"??????r"

Yoojeong’s pupils shook, and an indescribable force surged through her arms.

Crack.

In a thousandth of a second, split into a thousand parts, she saw her staff cleave apart.

'Ah.'

A strike so beautiful it was enchanting.

'If it hits...'

She felt a tingling sensation in her thighs as the greatsword halted just before her forehead.

"Why didn’t you dodge?"

"Huh?"

As if a monkey, she had her lips parted and suddenly stepped back.

"Achoo."

Caught up in the moment, she had forgotten that Rian's sword could destroy her.

"Creepy."

Yoojeong awkwardly bent her waist and rubbed her thighs back and forth.

After roughly patching things up, she discarded half of her split staff and aimed at Rian.

"What’s your name?"

"Ogent Rian. You?"

"I’m Son Yoojeong."

It sounded like a name from the Jincheon Empire, but such a powerful figure couldn’t have been unknown.

"Where are you from?"

Yoojeong pointed to the sky.

"Heaven. Since I like you, I'll tell you. The army of heaven has come down…"

A tremendous voice exploded in the sky.

"Son Yu-jung!"

At the piercing voice, Yu-jung looked up to see Archangel Ikael frowning.

Yu-jung raised her head and bared her fangs.

"Tch, why is that woman here? I was just about to have some fun."

"Son Yu-jung. While I can understand indulgence, breaking the laws of Heaven is unacceptable."

"And what if I don't accept that?" As Ikael's eyes widened, Yu-jung's body began to stiffen like stone.

"Ugh!"

"There are many ways to restrict your freedom, even without Buddha's intervention. Defy me one more time, and I will strip you of your right to exist."

Yu-jung's gaze shifted to Rian.

"How embarrassing...!"

Should I fight here?

"So that's how it is."

Rian looked up at Ikael and asked.

"If Heaven's army has infiltrated, does that mean Ymir is here too?"

At that moment, Yu-jung's restraints were lifted.

"Kiyaa! Just..."

As her body heated up and she was about to soar into the sky, Ikael spoke.

"Are you Rian?"

"Huh?"

Yu-jung, releasing her murderous intent, asked.

"Do you know this guy?"

Ikael recalled the blue-haired boy who had physically blocked Ymir's avatar.

'He's grown.'

Back then, he was just a mere human, but now he was the top priority for Heaven's army.

'I'd like to deal with him now if possible, but...'

Making a hasty decision before finding Buddha could have negative future consequences.

"Wait for the right time, Rian. Ymir will come for you, even if you don't rush."

Yu-jung's head snapped around.

"Ymir? Are you going to fight him?" There was no answer, but the determination in Rian's eyes was clear.

"Oh, I see."

With a meaningful smile, Yu-jung flew up to stand beside Ikael.

"Let's fight again next time, interesting human."

"Those...!"

Tess stepped forward to shout, but Rian raised an arm to stop her.

"Let's let them go for now."

As Ikael's wings flashed and her body shot forward at terrifying speed, Rian watched the long cloud Yu-jung created and turned to Tess.

"Are you okay? Any injuries?"

"At least I didn't get caught in the crossfire. Anyway, let's go back and report."

"Yeah. By now, the Holy War should have already assessed the situation. What's left is a full-scale war."

Ymir is coming.

"Will it be okay? Especially with the army of Hell..."

Tess, seeing Rian trembling with excitement, stopped speaking.

'Well, we've been chasing his shadow all this time.'

As a fellow swordsman, she could understand Rian's complex emotions.

"Don't worry. You are the world's strongest swordsman. Even the king of giants wouldn't stand a chance."

Rian gave a chilling smile.

"Come."

For the first time, time seemed to flow slowly.

"How was it?"

After leaving the abyssal cliff, Ikael, who hadn't spoken a word, asked Yu-jung.

"Huh? What?"

"That human, Rian. You, who are obsessed with fighting, must have some thoughts."

Yu-jung's face lit up.

"Of course, it was amazing! It's been a while since I felt that thrill! Ah, I want to fight again. I wouldn't get tired of fighting that human for 100 days straight."

That wasn't what Ikael wanted to hear.

"Of course, it would be. He's a descendant of Ogent."

"Huh? Ogent?"

After a moment of recalling, Yu-jung suddenly widened her eyes and shouted.

"What! Ogent!"

The one who stood equal to Ymir as a human.

"That's right! Now that you mention it, he did say his name was Rian Ogent! Ah, I see. Wow, a descendant of Ogent?"

Yu-jung was proud of her own lineage, but Ogent was on a different level.

"That's why I'm asking. You've fought Ymir before. What do you think, if those two clash?"

If Rian had surpassed his predecessor's power, Ymir could be in danger.

"Was he really Ogent? Wow, the legendary swordsman. What a shame! If I had known earlier, I would have listened to Ogent's tales..."

Ikael frowned.

"Son Yu-jung!"

"Huh? Oh, what?"

"Aren't you listening? Who do you think would win if Rian and Ymir clashed now?"

Yu-jung blinked.

"What are you talking about? Of course, Ogent was the strongest in Heaven's history, but..."

After considering Rian and Ymir, she laughed and raised her index finger.

"Of course, he wouldn't stand a chance against Ymir. He'd be dust in three seconds. Well, if it's the real Ymir."

"...I see."

The humiliating history with Ogent made Ikael cautious, but it seemed there was no need to worry.

"To add, I'm the only one in the universe who can defeat Ymir. Of course, I'm just staying put because I don't feel like it now, but if I wanted to..."

Despite Yu-jung's endless chatter, Ikael urged her to move on.
