Chapter 1193:

Iruki turned to his friends and asked,

"Then, could that information be..."

"No, no! I can’t say anything more. If I reveal this..."

She cut herself off, shaking her head.

If even love couldn't make her talk, it was likely a matter of personal safety.

Iruki ran a hand through his hair.

"Still, isn’t there any way you could tell us? Trust me. If you’re in danger, I’ll protect you."

Kara cupped her cheeks with both hands.

"Oh no… you're too charming."

While Nade pouted, Shirone waited with hope.

"I’m sorry… I..."

Seeing her eyes fill with tears, Iruki took a step back.

"I guess this is the limit. External factors can’t influence the dice."

Since he had no real affection for Kara, he remained unfazed even while speaking directly to her.

Eden commented,

"Still, this went well. We only spent 2 million points. If we were unlucky, it could’ve cost more than 10 million."

"Curtis didn’t go bankrupt for no reason. What now? Should we check out another store?"

Shirone spoke up.

"Let’s do it here. Like Eden said, just getting someone to warm up to us costs a lot of points. This is an opportunity. Whether it works or not…"

They had to settle it here.

"Kara, we want information about the bribery program. If you know something, please tell us."

Kara’s expression changed.

"It’s true that I have information, but I absolutely can’t reveal it. If you want to persuade me, the rule is designated numbers, and the cost is 10 million points per attempt."

Half the price had been cut.

It was likely a byproduct of Iruki’s persuasion attempt.

"Alright, I’ll persuade you."

"I’ll use two dice. Pick a single number between 2 and 12. If I roll that number, you win."

"Just one number?"

The difficulty was on a whole different level.

"In emotional terms, it means she really wants to keep this secret. Honestly, this is giving me chills."

Even if the rules seemed the same, there was a loophole in this match.

"Even if it ends in a draw, the administrator loses nothing. In the end, this is a game designed to be nearly unwinnable."

Of course, for an ordinary person.

"Alright. I choose 7."

"I’ll take 4."

Inside the subspace, both rolled their dice.

"Quantum collapse."

Shirone’s manipulated probability took effect, and the result was 3 and 4.

Kara rolled an 11.

He won.

As his friends clenched their fists in victory, Kara sat frozen behind the counter, overwhelmed by shock.

"Haah."

Her legs gave out, and she collapsed into her chair, her lips trembling.

"Alright, alright… If you’re going to play it this way, I have no choice. Your friend… he’s really something."

"My friend is pretty tough," Nade responded.

Shirone had only rolled the dice, but since he controlled the outcome with his ability, the statement wasn’t exactly wrong.

"Fine. I don’t know all the details, but… even saying this much puts my life at risk."

A blue light flickered in Kara’s eyes.

"What I know is…"

Her demeanor shifted.

A real-time keyword, something not stored in the standard database, slipped from her lips.

"Corkolas."

Shirone nodded.

"I see."

Kara’s face returned to normal as if nothing had happened, and she smiled.

"That’s all. Sorry, dear. I wanted to help more. So… are you leaving now?"

Even if her emotions were mechanical, it was hard not to be thrown off by her behavior.

"Let’s go," Iruki said, turning coldly.

"Wait, shouldn’t we at least say goodbye? I feel weird just leaving like this."

"That’s exactly why we’re leaving."

In the end, it had all been a game of dice. The only ones who got hurt were the humans involved.

"Let’s go on a date next time."

None of them looked back, afraid of what kind of expression she might be making.

At the entrance to Sector 7, Shirone spoke.

"Corkolas. That’s the name Curtis mentioned for the garbage dump. The place where Penna is waiting."

Nade nodded.

"I think this is huge. Curtis probably never got this far in persuasion. Either way, from now on, let’s not try convincing hot-tempered types. Their rules are ridiculous."

Iruki agreed.

"Without quantum collapse, you’d need twelve attempts to have a real chance. That’s 240 million points wasted."

Eden tapped her lips in thought.

"Hmm, but that’s not an impossible amount for a points tycoon. If someone was lucky enough—wait."

Luck.

From Eden’s stunned expression, Shirone realized it too.

"Right. There are two ways to enter the Core. Collect 10 billion points to buy a key, or… be lucky."

Nade added,

"So from the start, there was never a third route. This is one of the official methods."

Iruki leaned on his chin.

"That makes sense. Gathering 10 billion points and persuading administrators with points—those are the key challenges of the outer and inner tracks, respectively. Looking at the difficulty, they’re probably balanced."

Eden added,

"But for us, persuasion is heavily in our favor. If success depends on luck, we can control that luck. I bet no other team has tried bribing their way in for just 200 million."

Nade grinned.

"Curtis really did us a favor. We should treat him to a meal when we see him again."

That is, if he wasn’t suffering too much in prison.

"Yeah, let’s get him out soon. First, we strengthen our dice. If we use persuasion, we can buy more items. The first things we need are ‘Loss of Will’ and ‘Royal Entry.’ And of course, we have to keep 50 million points saved."

With the plan set, Nade shouted,

"Alright! Let’s move out!"

Corkolas.

The so-called garbage dump was an island where all of Melkidu’s waste was discarded.

Of course, there was a reason why people called it the garbage dump instead of using its actual name.

It was the place where the worst criminals and human trash gathered within Melkidu.

"Hooooo!"

A golden streak of light curved through the sky and landed on the white sands of the island.

As Nade’s voice faded, Shirone and his team looked around.

"Ugh, the smell."

Trash was scattered everywhere.

"Looking at the coastline, this is a pretty big island. It’s going to take time to find Penna."

"We have to stay at least a day now that we’ve arrived. Depending on the information, we might be here longer."

The path leading inland was piled high with heaps of junk.

"Do you think there are shops here? We’ll need food, but I don’t see any fishermen."

Iruki spoke.

"Penna said that if we came too late, we’d find her at the dinner table. That means…"

They were selling human flesh.

"Eugh…"

As Eden shuddered, a group of men emerged from the piles of trash.

"Heh heh. Look at what we have here."

They were covered in tattoos, wearing tattered, hole-ridden shirts.

"Do you even know where you are?"

"The garbage dump," Shirone replied.

"No, no. This is Corkolas. In other words, this is the place where you get stripped of everything you own."

They didn’t look particularly strong, but Shirone’s group had already spent a significant amount of their points.

The leader smirked.

"I’ll give you one minute. Drop all your items and kneel. If you have something valuable, I might just let you live."

Eden studied the various emblems tattooed on the man's arms.

‘Those marks…?’

The vagrants shouted,

"What are you waiting for? Start handing it over! Or do you really want to die?"

They gripped their knives, ready to lunge at any moment, but Shirone’s group remained unfazed.

"Heh… Heh heh! Hah! Hahaha!"

The leader, who seemed unaccustomed to laughing, scratched his throat and then burst into a rough cough.

"If you’re not just overconfident, then you must be rookies. Judging by how you don’t even know the infamous name of Corkolas."

The vagrants moved closer.

"If you’re relying on your points, that’s a shame. Every special area has its own unique rules. Corkolas doesn’t allow the use of points. Instead, you can kill as many players as you want. This is a free player-kill zone."

"Points don’t get deducted?" Shirone asked.

"Hah! Exactly. I could carve you up right here, and it wouldn’t matter. Now then…"

Nade cracked his knuckles.

"Dammit! So that’s how it is? And here I was getting nervous for nothing."

As the Avatar of Thunder God enveloped his body, the vagrants stared in shock.

"Hey, this guy… his body’s sparking."

"I know," Nade replied casually.

A brilliant flash of light erupted over the western shore of the island.

One minute later.

"P-please spare us! We were wrong!"

Five men lay sprawled on the sand, their limbs twitching violently from electrocution.

"You lot," Nade said as he approached them.

They flinched and squirmed like freshly caught fish.

"Eek! W-we’re sorry!"

"I just have one question. Have you seen a woman named Penna here? She’s a bit shorter than me, with a slim build. She should have arrived about three days ago, in this island’s time."

"N-no, we haven’t seen any women," one of them answered.

Even after replying, the leader nervously lifted his head.

"Really, we haven’t seen any. But… there’s another arrival point. She might have gone there."

"Another arrival point?"

"Y-yes. The eastern shore."

Shirone asked,

"Who controls that area?"

"N-no one. Both the eastern and western shores are under our control. There’s no one else."

Eden narrowed her eyes.

"And what exactly do you control?"

"Uh, well… the trash."

Shirone glanced at his friends.

"They must be low-level bandits who prey only on newcomers. There were people like this in High Gear too—Ten Men."

Nade frowned.

"You see them everywhere—those losers who stomp on anyone trying to make something of themselves. Pathetic."

The vagrants looked humiliated, but none dared to show their frustration in front of Nade.

Shirone asked,

"Why live among trash? Isn’t there a settlement further inland?"

"That’s a lawless zone. It’s controlled by the Three Great Gangs. If you wander in there carelessly, you won’t even leave behind bones."

Eden muttered,

"Red Union. Desperado. Johan Cartel."

Nade turned to her.

"You know them?"

"The emblems tattooed on both of that guy’s arms. Those are from well-known criminal organizations in the real world."

"Never heard of them," Nade admitted.

"Of course not. You guys operate on a global scale. These are just corporate thugs, really. They control small cities and act like big shots. Shirone might know about them, though."

"Yeah. I’ve never encountered them directly, but I’ve heard of them."

In Omega, at least.

"Since the Yora Church assists law enforcement, I’ve come across some of these groups. But don’t compare them to the world’s top ten guilds, like Parrot Mercenaries. These gangs don’t have many strong fighters. Their best mages are probably uncertified Class-10s."

"A bunch of wild dogs," Nade scoffed.

Eden looked back at the leader.

"But they’re ruthless. If he has three emblems, it means he’s switched allegiances at least twice. That means they betrayed their former groups and fled here."

Iruki smirked.

"This might actually work in our favor. If we take out the gangs, we might find the information we need."

"Oh, right!"

Nade suddenly turned back to the kneeling leader and placed his hand on his head.

"You probably know everything about the Three Great Gangs, don’t you?"

"Y-yes…?"

Feeling an electric current already building, the leader squeezed his eyes shut and stammered his answer.
