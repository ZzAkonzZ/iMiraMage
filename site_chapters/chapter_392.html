Chapter 392: Combat Readiness (5)

Shirone didn't look back. No matter how much he thought about it, he couldn't understand why she was acting this way.

"Maya."

"Can we have our meeting while eating something delicious? I've always wanted to do that."

"Maya."

Maya turned around at the second call.

"Yes?"

Shirone closed his mouth.

When their eyes met, it felt like the words in his head scattered in all directions. He couldn't talk about something that wasn't clear. The problem was that it wasn't clear.

"If you're fighting the Screamers because of us, you don't have to. It seems like you've been worried lately."

He felt it was cowardly to borrow Iruki's words, but if it solved the issue, it didn't matter.

"Oh, no. It's the overall evaluation anyway. I'm actually sorry because I feel like I'm a burden."

As Maya turned the doorknob to leave, Shirone realized he couldn't hold back any longer.

"Then why are you acting like this?"

Even though Shirone's voice was raised, Maya wasn't surprised. She just smiled awkwardly and scratched her cheek out of habit.

"No matter how much I think about it, I don't understand. Why have you been avoiding me lately? I can feel it."

"Did I... do something wrong?"

"That's not what I mean."

"Then why do I have to be kind to you?"

Shirone stopped in his tracks. It felt like a sharp blade had suddenly pierced his chest.

"What do you want from me? How do you want me to act? Should I always look at you and smile?"

Shirone couldn't understand why Maya was acting this way. If there was a misunderstanding, it could be resolved. But this wasn't a misunderstanding.

"How you treat me is your choice, but..."

Maya gave a bitter smile. She felt she understood a bit of what Amy meant when she said it was frustrating. But she had no intention of going back on the answer she gave that day.

"Then Shirone, can you love me?"

Shirone's face hardened.

Seeing the expected reaction, Maya lowered her gaze with sad eyes.

She too had held the wind that blew in the music room that day in her heart.

'Shirone, I'm different from Amy. I'm neither cold nor strong. If I can't be with you, every day will be like hell. I'll only think of you. If you can't love me, we have to end it here. We have to let it go.'

Shirone's trembling eyes gradually calmed down. His face, which had looked startled like a rabbit, became composed.

Maya prepared herself. Even so, the unavoidable anticipation made her heart race.

What would Shirone say?

I love you? Actually, I like you too?

All sorts of exciting words came to mind, but she already knew the result.

I'm sorry.

She already felt terrible. She wouldn't be able to get over it for a while. She would spend nights crying.

'But this is it. It ends here.'

Having made up her mind, Maya lifted her head. But the moment she saw Shirone's determined eyes, her heart sank.

"Maya, I..."

"Ah, hahaha!"

Shirone's face turned bewildered. As if it was really funny, Maya approached, wiping tears with her index finger.

"I'm joking, joking! Oh, you're so naive."

"Joking?"

"You did this at the graduation event too. If you keep acting like this, I'll tell Amy."

Shirone felt lost. It felt like he was standing in the middle of an unreal world.

"But you haven't been talking lately..."

"Ah, my head's been so full that I didn't have time to think about anything else. My family has high expectations, and my grades keep dropping. I'm sorry. From now on, I'll never! Ever pretend not to know."

Shirone felt drained. He thought he needed to sort out his thoughts, but Maya didn't give him any time.

"Come on, let's go! Iruki is waiting. Hill capture! We're going to win, right?"

"Uh, yeah."

Maya pushed Shirone out of the music room. As she crossed the threshold, she clenched her teeth and closed her eyes tightly.

'Ha, I'm in. What do I do now?'

It felt like crossing the threshold of hell.

In the northeastern mountains of Alpheas Magic School, there is a cave that goes 20 meters underground.

And if you navigate through the complex maze there, you come to the Golden Wheel, where Fermi is the president.

It was a room so large and luxurious that it could be called a mansion.

The table was filled with all kinds of delicacies, and waiters in tuxedos walked around with wine, filling empty glasses.

In the center, beautiful dancers performed, and a seven-member band played gentle music.

The Golden Wheel had 40 top-tier staff members.

The labor cost alone exceeded 700 gold per day, and all of this was for the five members.

Fermi sat on a sofa, enjoying his wine. A white-furred tiger with a collar lay sleeping on the carpet.

On the four sofas placed around him sat the members of the Golden Wheel, known as Fermi's group.

"Finally, the monthly profit has exceeded 7 million gold. Breaking 10 million is now within reach."

It wasn't an amount a student could handle, but Fermi was indifferent.

The perceptive members kept their mouths shut. On days like today, when an emergency meeting was called, they had to choose their words carefully.

"Arian Shirone..."

As Fermi opened the conversation, the members began to speak the prepared words they had been waiting for.

"Do we really need to worry about him? We'll have to deal with him eventually, but why not focus more on business?"

"I agree. He's just a naive kid. Why not just let him graduate?"

You can't tear down the house to catch a fly.

Since Fermi had shown his emotions, there was no guarantee that such a thing wouldn't happen.

"That's why we can't just leave him alone."

"Fermi, aren't you stressing too much? You can ignore provocations."

"You can ignore provocations. But this is a threat. A threat to the existence of the Golden Wheel."

They would follow anything Fermi said, but they couldn't agree with classifying Shirone as a threat.

"Of course, Shirone is an impressive rookie. But you know? We're professionals. Not just because we make money, but because of our skills. We can control him in the graduation exam."

Fermi coldly swirled his wine glass.

"Do you know why you can't make big money? Because you don't realize that the problem you're facing now started a long time ago. Or you overlook it even if you know. Today's threat becomes an unmanageable threat in the future. If you don't prepare now, there will be no solution when it hits."

The members fell silent. Once again, Fermi was right.

"From now on, Shirone is classified as the highest level of threat to the Golden Wheel. We'll analyze him thoroughly. He's going up against the Screamers, right? Who are their team members?"

Sona's Hersy reported the results of the eavesdropping.

"Screamer, Pony, Luman, Suavi, and Aider. The team was completed yesterday."

"Hmm, Luman and Suavi. A 2-utility, martial artist killer combination."

As expected, it was a team formed by mobilizing Screamer's connections. It was surprising that Dorothy was missing, but she probably refused. She's a woman with an incredibly sharp intuition.

"As proud as they are, they'll want to prove themselves. It's completely different from strategy and tactics. No matter how good Shirone is, it's not easy to defeat the Screamer team with Maya."

Unless it's a teamwork-centered battle like an annihilation mission, the martial artist killer strategy is quite powerful.

In the strategy and tactics situation, Shirone's team annihilated over 300 monsters in 3 minutes and 28 seconds, but Screamer's team, with Screamer alone, killed over 150.

They strengthen one person extremely to cause a balance collapse.

This strategy will shine even more in a 5-on-5 battle, and for Shirone's team to win, they need to build a perfect balance. In such a situation, Maya's presence could be a poison.

"That's not enough. This time, we'll figure out all of Shirone's strengths. We'll scrape out everything he's hiding."

"How are you going to do that? The overall evaluation has high scores, so the participants will do their best. The Screamer team is also fully prepared."

Fermi tapped the sofa a couple of times. A bluish energy shimmered from his body, and under his raised hand, black things quickly moved back and forth with a rustling sound.

Deviant - Depreciation Deal.

With a ting! sound, a casino chip spun and flew up.

Fermi caught the vertically falling chip horizontally and threw it to Hersy.

The center of the chip had a whirlpool pattern, and the edge had a black and white alternating pattern.

"This is..."

Hersy looked at Fermi, surprised.

It was a very precious chip to use for something like this. Even converted to market value, it was worth over 400,000 gold.

'He's serious.'

Shirone would definitely become a future enemy. It was a chip that could only come out with such certainty.

"Make the deal with that. The contract condition is to make Shirone go all out in the hill capture. If you provoke him, he'll reveal his true colors."

"Got it. Anything else to convey?"

After thinking for a moment, Fermi's lips twisted into a wicked smile.

"Tell them not to worry about the aftermath and go all out."

Hill Capture (1)

It was the day of the hill capture evaluation.

The first thing the 30 students did upon gathering at the training ground was to check the members of the opposing teams.

Shirone also confirmed the Screamer team.

Pony was strategically allied with Screamer, and Suavi and Luman were key factors in the martial artist killer strategy. Including the reliable Aider, they had formed a solid team.

Among the six teams gathered, the evaluation instructor explained the rules.

"Today is the hill capture. You should already know your opposing teams. Six teams will be evaluated simultaneously, with no time limit. The first team to score 5 points wins, and the losing team will have 20 points deducted. The battle will take place at the main hill capture battlefield, number 2000."

Shirone examined the battlefield.

It was a rectangular area 200 meters long and 50 meters wide, surrounded by 20-meter-high glass walls. Inside, each side had a 10-meter-high hill with a flag planted in the center.

The evaluation would proceed in rounds, and once a flag was captured, the round would end temporarily, moving on to the next round.

"To deepen strategic tactics, spatial movement is prohibited. Flight height is limited to 20 meters, and exceeding this will result in disqualification. If you touch the flag, you won't be able to use any magic due to the built-in magic control device. Remember this well to avoid complaints later."

While holding the flag, one would be defenseless and vulnerable to enemy attacks.

Therefore, capturing the flag required at least two people to occupy the hill and cooperate.

The instructor continued the explanation.

Upon entering the battlefield, the teams would be divided along the central 100-meter line, with each team member assigned a position to perform their duties.

There were four positions: forward, winger, linker, and guard.

The forward started at the 10-meter mark in their own territory, closest to the center line, and their role was to charge towards the enemy's flag.

Wingers moved along the left and right lines, typically starting at the more defensive 20-meter mark. However, they often moved faster due to less defensive pressure.

Linkers started at the 50-meter mark in the center line, balancing offense and defense.

Depending on the opponent's strategy, they could assist a winger, charge with the forward, or retreat to defend the flag with the guard, making them versatile players.

Finally, the guard protected the flag on the hill. Even if the enemy forward infiltrated, the guard could defend the flag, as the forward couldn't use magic while holding it, making it a defensive position.
