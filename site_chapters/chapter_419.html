Chapter 419:

Heaven's 6th Heaven, Great World War.

There is an angel with a face as transparent and beautiful as light.

Standing at 2 meters and 30 centimeters tall, with a slender body, he is the pinnacle of intelligence, born knowing all the principles of the world.

Among the many angels, only eight are archangels, and among them, the most beautiful is the Angel of Birth, Kariel.

But now, his face was as pale as a sick human's.

His eyes were sunken, and his expression was irritable.

The kind and clear facial lines that once overflowed with intellectual beauty had recently become frequently distorted.

In fact, he was in a state similar to being ill.

If an angel's power comes from clear concepts, his current clarity was only a tenth of what it usually was.

All this was the result of the labor he endured to bring one human.

"Kariel, are you really okay?"

Uriel, the Angel of Destruction, looked worriedly at Kariel, whose presence had become faint.

Standing at 3 meters tall, the tallest among the archangels, with a solid body, and a thick breastplate that reached his chin, a golden halo spun angrily at its center.

"I can still endure. I have to. This is just the beginning."

The fatigue Kariel felt could be compared to a human not sleeping for about 40 years.

Any creature subjected to this level of fatigue would have already had all its cells dried up.

But his eyes still burned with emotion.

Uriel turned his head to where Kariel was looking.

Under the galaxy mirror that governed the world's operations, a woman of human standards of beauty was sitting cross-legged.

Adrias Miro.

Could there be a more detestable concept to the beings of Heaven?

Even Anke La, the incarnation of the Akashic Records, couldn't penetrate the barrier she set up, blocking humanity's last bastion against the conquest of the universe.

But even she couldn't withstand Kariel's persistence.

"You finally did it."

Anke La, who had abruptly canceled the war just before the rebels were annihilated, prohibited any investigation into humans.

All angels obeyed the message, but only Kariel continued with the plan to exterminate humans.

What he obtained was Miro's 'body' right in front of him.

"How did you do it? Didn't you say Miro's spacetime couldn't be destroyed?"

A faint smile appeared on Kariel's tired face.

"When we attacked Miro's spacetime with celestial force, Optros slipped through the crack. Its electromagnetic pattern contained information about Miro's spacetime. I analyzed that."

"But Miro's spacetime healed immediately. You couldn't have brought her through the dimensional barrier."

"The dimensional barrier, in fact, has no substance. The reason Heaven's army can't go to the land of Earth is because the protocols are different. It's an independent world without even quantum entanglement."

Uriel didn't try to understand.

A being born for destruction had no obligation to understand its target.

"So?"

"I decoded the protocol with the information from Optros. Then I transmitted Miro to the Great World War through quantum superposition."

"Hmm, so?"

Kariel realized Uriel didn't understand.

"Simply put, I reached into a barrel and kept pulling until I got what I wanted. The barrel's size could be said to be the entire universe."

Uriel understood why Kariel had become so weak.

"So you searched the universe and kidnapped her."

"Well... exactly."

Kariel decided to let it go.

"Hmm."

Uriel looked at Miro's face, lost in meditation.

It was the same young face that had stood alone against Heaven's army, spreading the dimensional wall just before the final war broke out.

"The human mind, in some sense, is formidable."

Currently, Miro's mind, which constitutes her spacetime, is endlessly being sucked into the dimension of emptiness.

It was a mental state and concept that a perfect angel from birth couldn't understand.

"Hmph, that's proof of their imperfection. Don't have any sentiments for such insignificant humans."

Uriel didn't respond.

For him, anything he couldn't understand was simply to be destroyed.

But was it the same for Kariel?

The concept of birth is based on the premise of understanding everything.

For Kariel, who handles the elixir of life, not understanding humans might be the greatest contradiction in this universe.

"Humans are truly strange beings. Even now, one human is holding back all of Heaven's power."

Kariel's expression twisted.

Humans. Humans.

Starting with Anke La, everyone applied different standards only to humans.

"That won't happen. I'll destroy them."

Uriel changed the subject. He didn't want to further irritate his twin-like angel, born on the same day but with a different concept.

"So, what will you do now? Even if you kill Miro, her spacetime won't disappear. Why did you bring her here, exhausting her mind to the brink of destruction?"

"Hmph, of course, to destroy Miro's spacetime. I'll pull Miro out of the dimension of emptiness. Once she regains consciousness and is killed, her spacetime will disappear."

It was a good method, no, the only method.

"But we can't understand human imperfection. None of the citizens can understand Miro's mind. The Nephilim disappeared after Goffin's erasure."

"They don't exist in Heaven, but they do in the land of Earth."

Uriel tilted his head.

Of course, there are those who awaken as Nephilim in the land of Earth.

But with Miro's spacetime blocking it, bringing someone from there was impossible.

"Are you going to search the entire universe again? You might completely disappear this time."

"There's no need. I don't have the strength left for that."

Kariel flew up to the pillar-shaped central system and connected to the Akashic Records.

Uriel followed and looked at the screen from behind Kariel.

"I found something interesting while searching the Akashic Records."

When Kariel stepped aside, a human's name appeared on the screen.

Reading the description below, Uriel nodded.

"Indeed, interesting."

Coincidentally, it was a human who understood the human mind best.

No, it was correct to say that he had changed to his current state because he understood the human mind best.

"I will summon this person to the Great World War."

When Kariel spread his hands, a law book was born on his palm with a flash of light.

The law book, containing the principles of the universe, had a high-strength metal hardcover, and the pages made of light began to turn.

As the central processing system operated at full capacity, bluish electricity flowed like waves on the floor and gathered in the arch-shaped machinery.

Uriel watched for a moment, then turned back to the central processing unit's screen.

"A grave robber..."

A name that existed only as a concept was flashing.

Morrigan Arius

Rebel 1st Headquarters.

Shirone's group received enthusiastic cheers from the rebels.

Shirone's exploits, subduing hundreds of giants with the power of an archangel, quickly spread through drones and had already become a new myth among the rebels.

Amidst the overwhelming white noise, Shirone's face was solemn.

He was puzzled by the number 1 attached to the headquarters, but any doubts disappeared upon seeing the much larger-than-expected scale of the rebel base.

Dozens of buildings, presumed to be supply warehouses, surrounded the site, and a massive building over 30 meters tall stood in the forest.

'An incredible force. If they move...'

Gaold thought differently.

'It won't work.'

Of course, the headquarters' power was formidable.

But there was no doubt about the battalion commander's words that they were on the brink of annihilation.

It wasn't a matter of asymmetric power.

From the beginning, the beings of Heaven were something different from humans.

If thousands of ants bit a human, they would die, but angels were not humans who would let that happen.

"Hey, there's Shirone. Hey, Shirone! It's me, me!"

A familiar face popped out from the middle of the procession.

It was Clove and Gardrak, who had guided Shirone and his friends to Nor's shelter a year ago in Heaven.

Although the memory of Clove ignoring Amy still lingered, it was nice to see a familiar face at such a moment.

But Shirone couldn't approach them. The crowd, hearing Clove's words, was all trying to rush out.

"Wow! It's true! Shirone really came!"

Clove and Gardrak were buried in the crowd and couldn't be seen.

Except for the people of Sector 73, very few knew Shirone's face, causing this phenomenon.

Shirone widened the distance with a startled expression.

Getting closer could potentially harm people.

Watching Shirone move away, Clove tore at his hair and lamented.

"Ah, who knew that kid would become such an important figure. If I had known, I would have gotten closer to him earlier."

Gardrak smacked his disciple's head.

"Is that the issue now? We're on the verge of being annihilated by angels."

Clove replied with a resentful expression.

"But Master, you also said that if Shirone came, we would be treated well here."

Gardrak remained silent.

Shirone had returned. And at a very opportune time.

The halt in Heaven's attack was certainly a boon for the rebels, but it wasn't enough to bridge the deepening rift between the Mecha and Nor tribes after repeated defeats.

The headquarters, unable to reach a consensus, eventually split into the 1st and 2nd Headquarters, and although there were no battles, the situation was already bleak.

At a time when everyone was waiting for self-destruction, the light of Sector 73 began to shine on them again.

'Why now? Did he know and come back?'

A series of events connected by invisible links made it impossible to dismiss Shirone's return as mere coincidence.

"Hehe, I'm so happy to be walking with you, brother."

Meanwhile, Lena, who was holding onto Shirone's arm, did not leave his side for a moment on the way to the command headquarters.

Watching Shirone's stiff back, Plu clicked her tongue and said, "What is she doing? Did she come to Heaven for a romance?"

Gaold didn't mind at all. "Hehe, let it be. It helps with the parade."

For the rebels, who were on the brink of annihilation, the miracle of Shirone's arrival was welcome, and a bit of theatrics wouldn't hurt.

However, Kanya was embarrassed by her sister's behavior.

Unable to bear it, she whispered to Lena, "What are you doing? Let go. Don't you know what Shirone means to the rebels? What if people misunderstand?"

"What misunderstanding? We're not just acquaintances. It's been a year since we met, can't I at least link arms?"

Lena looked at Shirone and said, "Brother, let sister link arms with you too."

Shirone couldn't find the words to respond.

His heart was already racing; how could he walk with two women on his arms?

Kanya's face turned as red as a beet.

"W-what are you talking about? Why would I link arms with Shirone?"

"Oh, come on. You're too weak in these matters. We need to look close to Shirone to get things done at the headquarters. Your Guroi is damaged, right? We need to get a new one here to become pilots again."

Kanya was at a loss for words.

Despite being younger, her sister was more calculating.

Or perhaps, in this case, cunning?

Shirone reassured her.

"Kanya, don't worry too much. I destroyed the Guroi, so I'll make sure to tell the commander."

Kanya, smiling at Shirone, then turned her squinting eyes back to Lena.

'Oh, that fox...'

Whether she knew her sister's feelings or not, Lena, holding Shirone's arm, just smiled contentedly.
