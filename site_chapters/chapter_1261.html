Chapter 1261:

“What the—?”

As Havitz glanced around, Uorin fell back, landing on her rear in fear.

“Hrk…”

Once Vanishing deactivated, the pain returned.

‘My leg…’

Blood was flowing from the split muscle in her calf.

“Hm.”

Havitz, on the other hand, didn’t seem fazed even after seeing the wound in his own side.

He simply felt a strange sense of familiarity.

The fact that he could sense this—because they shared the same Vanishing—conveyed the severity of the situation.

‘That’s a power similar to mine.’

Havitz pointed his sword at Uorin.

“Come out. I’ll kill you.”

The second Vanishing deactivated, and only then did Havitz realize Kido was standing next to Uorin.

“Heh?”

As someone who had always been the aggressor, Havitz found it fascinating to suddenly be in the victim’s shoes.

‘So this is what it felt like… for the ones I killed.’

Havitz lowered his sword and asked:

“How’d you do it?”

Kido stayed silent, but Uorin, thinking back on the recent moment, came to a realization.

‘The taste of memory.’

By consuming Havitz’s severed ankle that had been torn off by Earthbound Spirit, Kido had absorbed his memories.

“Kh…”

Kido’s eyes were half-glazed over.

‘Freak.’

He had consumed the memories of many people, but he had never encountered anyone this utterly insane.

‘I feel like I’m going to throw up.’

Every time he tried to trace Havitz’s memories, his mind felt like it was unraveling into chaos.

‘How does anyone live with a mind like this?’

Even animals had more sense.

‘It’s extreme intellect driven by primal instinct—a desire for pleasure that has nothing to do with survival.’

In other words, a complete degenerate.

Kido glanced at Uorin’s wound.

‘That’s a serious cut. She won’t be able to escape on her own.’

When Havitz froze due to Gando, Kido made a once-in-a-lifetime decision.

‘Even if I protect Uorin from one attack, I’ll eventually die from Vanishing.'

So instead, he let Uorin go and devoured Havitz’s severed ankle.

‘In the end, I absorbed Vanishing.'

It wasn’t the result of hard-earned training like Natasha, but this time the natural talent was overwhelming.

‘If insanity counts as talent, he’s a genius.’

The problem was, while Vanishing was active, making rational judgments was nearly impossible.

Kido said,

“If you want to kill the Empress, then I’ll protect her. If we both activate Vanishing at the same time, no one can say who’d win. It’ll be a dice roll.”

And that was true.

Facing a different kind of crisis than anything before, Havitz gave it serious thought.

Of course, there was no thought.

“Kehehe…”

Only an insatiable hunger for pleasure.

“This sounds fun.”

At the entrance of Delta, rows of “In” users were collapsing one after another, choking themselves.

“This… this can’t be happening.”

As Euntara gasped in horror, Shirone realized it.

‘God.’

If He couldn’t use them, He’d kill them.

‘So that’s your answer?’

No murderer in the world had ever killed billions at once.

‘Do you really think this is okay?’

God would say:

Void.

From the perspective of the Outer World, this reality was nothing more than a fleeting bubble.

‘But we…’

We are beings who live eternity in that fleeting moment.

“Mika…”

Global population rapidly decreasing.

10.43 million.

4.89 million.

22.7 million.

44.29 million.

They were all committing suicide.

“Hrrk…”

The numbers made Shirone’s jaw tremble.

More than half of humanity hadn’t yet died, but most had already given up on life.

‘Why?’

Was God a murderer?

“Beep! Beep-beep!”

In the burning corridors of Delta, flickering with the waves of “In,” Kitra completed two operations.

The algorithms he ran had two paths:

Repeat the calculation until it yields 100% within the current system.

Change the system through learning.

The result of the first was this:

God was transferring full authority to the Illuminati.

62.7% of users request logout. Initiating system shutdown.

The universe could now be closed.

“Beeee…”

But in that moment, Kitra—God—evaluated the number 62.7%.

‘Is that enough?’

While it surpassed the threshold set by the Illuminati, 37.3% of users had rejected it.

Even for God, blocking a user's master code required sufficient logic.

Causality is weakening. Probability: 100.000000%. Probability: 100.0000%.

Though the probability remained at 100%, a trend of declining perfection was detected over time.

Probability: 100.00%.

Approved.

Driven by a purely mechanical logic to serve the greater good for more users—

System shutdown.

God entered the termination code into one of the countless worlds of the multiverse.

Outer space.

As Shirone’s Sphere surpassed 98% completion, the solar winds surged in.

“Guh!”

Even Shirone, stationed far away, could barely endure it.

‘The laws are changing.’

The rapidly expanding sun would engulf the entire system and then contract into a tiny point.

How long would it take for that point to absorb the universe?

Ten thousand years? A hundred thousand? A hundred million?

‘No.’

In the Outer World, it would be an instant.

Like popping a soap bubble, or turning off a machine, it exists—then it vanishes.

‘Please...’

—Current progress: 98.356%.

Upon receiving Mika’s cold update, Shirone calculated the time and despaired.

‘I won’t make it in time.’

The Shirone on Earth was using Tachyon to alter causes, but so was God.

‘I have no choice but to focus on the Sphere.’

Shirone looked toward the core of the sun.

‘Please.’

Please hold out.

Amy squeezed out her soul.

“Huuuuu…”

She maximized her incarnation of fire, but the sun’s laws seemed ready to burn even flame itself.

“Amy, go back.”

Ikael spoke.

“This is the end. We will soon dissolve into the world’s laws. But you are not an angel. I can’t let you die in a place like this.”

Once the sun exploded, even a god of the Outer World would not be able to reverse it.

‘A unique, irreversible event.’

It could truly be called the end of Omega.

“We can’t stop the end, but the universe won’t disappear immediately. There’ll be some time left.”

After the sun explodes, maybe ten minutes—or for them, maybe ten days—they might still survive.

Knowing Ikael wasn’t lying, Amy felt devastated.

‘Shirone…’

She wanted to see him.

If the end couldn’t be stopped, then at least she wanted to face it together.

‘I’m sorry, Shirone.’

With tears of fire streaming down, Amy said,

“No. I’ll stay here.”

“Amy.”

“Don’t try to talk me out of it. It’s not like there’s a way, and yes, I want to see Shirone but…”

How should she die?

“I’ll fight to the end.” Amy seemed resolved.

“Amy will die.”

Fermi’s words made Seriel’s eyes widen.

“What?!”

Immediately, she grabbed his collar.

“You crazy bastard! How can you say something that important only now?! Does Shirone know? Does he?!”

“No.”

Seriel was even more shocked.

“You’re insane! You knew and didn’t say anything?”

“Didn’t you say you understood?” Of course she did, but this far exceeded what she’d expected.

Drained of the will to hit him, she released his collar and staggered back.

“How could you… Fermi, this isn’t right. Sure, one life isn’t worth more than all of humanity, but still, this isn’t it. You…”

Seriel sobbed and shouted.

“You’re his friend!”

Fermi approached the window.

“I’m not his friend.”

“Fermi!”

“But I do think it matters. After all, Amy is Yahweh’s core emotion.”

“If I told Shirone the truth, the future would’ve changed completely. Even if many people cooperated, this future is one Shirone led us to.”

If Shirone suddenly took a drastically different path, all information about the future would become useless.

“So you sacrificed Amy?”

“No.”

Fermi turned back with a cold expression.

“I’m going to save Amy.”

“At this point, all I can say is, to save her—she has to die first.”

Seriel stood in a daze.

“And…”

Fermi gave a bitter smile.

“It’s not time yet.”

The archangels were vanishing.

‘So this is it?’

The feeling of heading toward endless nothingness, an unrecoverable state, was utterly horrifying.

‘To think it would end like this…’

As her mental form melted, Satiel recalled a distant memory.

‘There were good times…’

No—in hindsight, there were far fewer bad times than good.

FWOOSH! FWOOSH!

Metatron, Faiel, Metiel, Kariel disappeared, followed by Raiel.

“Satiel.”

His consciousness reached her.

“Remember me…”

Satiel choked up.

The only angel who came to her when everyone else had left—when she’d screamed in despair.

“Sniff!”

Why had she…

‘…been so close, yet…’

…done something so foolish?

“Ikael.”

Satiel smiled sadly.

“I’m sorry.”

At that moment, Ikael felt it.

From within Satiel’s consciousness, an indescribable emotion began expanding infinitely.

‘Heart.’

The heart itself is not an error.

‘But love…’

…is.

“BEEEEEEEEEEP!”

Kitra let out the most intense mechanical scream as his limbs trembled.

?Probability: 99.999999999……

‘The calculation is wrong.’

To correct the error, Kitra immediately accessed Satiel’s consciousness. The process sped forward at lightspeed.

THUMP... THUMP...

In a dark space where familiar heartbeats echoed, Satiel slowly opened her eyes.

“Satiel.”

With huge eyes set into a massive brown mass, the sun’s avatar Anke Ra was there.

“Anke Ra…”

Though no time passed during the signal exchange, Anke Ra got straight to the point.

“Your quantum signal is interfering with mine. Take immediate corrective action.”

“I’m sorry. It’s my fault. But, Anke Ra, I cannot comply.”

That’s what the heart is.

“I will give you eternal life.”

And Anke Ra knew how to alter quantum signals.

“I will place you in another universe with your current signal. There, you may do whatever you want.”

Satiel bowed her head shyly.

“Whatever I want?”

“Yes. Everything you desire will be granted.”

“Will Goffin be there?”

“Anke Ra, I’ve always been your loyal servant. I reported Ikael’s betrayal, and I even pushed the child born between her and Goffin to death.”

Satiel slowly stepped forward, sat before Anke Ra, and stroked his rough skin.

“I thought it would be enough. That if I followed your will, I would gain what I treasured. But what happened? They all left. Ikael, Goffin, even the angels who followed me. Even you.”

“I never abandoned you.”

“…Sure.”

Satiel’s eyes widened.

“You never cared from the beginning, you bastard.”

She drove her curled fingers into Anke Ra’s eye, pushing her arm in up to the shoulder.

“KYEEEEEEEEEEEEE!”

With an ear-piercing shriek that felt like the world would explode, the connection between God and Satiel was severed.
