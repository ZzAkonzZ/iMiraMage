Chapter 811: Age of Chaos (3)

The capital of the Kingdom of Jive, Rodenin.

As a free city, the gates were wide open, and even at dusk, many people were entering the capital.

"Stop right there!"

But Rian was an exception.

"Are you talking to me?"

"Is there anyone else here as filthy as you? It's fine if you become a beggar after entering, but we don't allow beggars to enter."

The rain had washed away the smell of blood, but the problem was that he wasn't using an umbrella.

"I'm not a beggar; I'm a wandering swordsman."

The guard burst into laughter.

"A wandering swordsman? Can you tell the difference between a beggar with a sword and a swordsman who looks like a beggar?"

It was oddly convincing.

"I'm a noble. I come from Tormia, and I'm a swordsman of the Ozente family."

When Rian finally showed his identification, the guard's expression changed immediately.

"Oh, I'm sorry. It's just that your appearance is far from that of a noble."

Rian smiled bitterly.

"I understand."

"When you enter Rodenin, you should wash up. Even with an ID, you'll face many inconveniences."

Rian thought it was advice anyone would give.

"Thank you. Well then, take care." As expected of a kingdom with many cloudy days, Jive had various nighttime activities, and quite a few people were out and about.

'It's not all lively.' Rian’s keen eyes detected the darkness in the small alleys connected to the main street.

Hidden in the shadows, people were gathered, and they weren't all beggars.

"Mom! It's a monster!"

Two women walking with umbrellas quickly stepped aside as soon as they saw Rian.

"...I really need to wash up."

With his large build, hair down to his shoulders, and a greatsword at his side, he looked every bit the villain.

As he walked the rainy night streets looking for a bathhouse, he heard the sound of hooves approaching from behind.

"There he is!"

One of the women who had run away earlier was riding.

"Hyah! Hyah!"

The carriage, which had been speeding as if to collide, came to a sudden stop in front of Rian.

As he waited, feeling the horse's rough breath on his face, a group of soldiers got off the carriage.

"We need to conduct a search."

It was bothersome since he had already proven his identity.

"What is this about?"

"We received a report. A suspicious person has been wandering the streets at night, targeting women."

Rian had just been walking.

"There seems to be a misunderstanding. I'm a wandering swordsman. Here's my ID."

When he presented his ID as he had learned at the gate, the expressions of the security team softened.

"Tormia. You've come from quite a distance."

The soldier who had initiated the conversation continued.

"Recently, there have been gruesome murders in the capital. It's a serial killing, and the methods are perverse. So..."

"Are you saying I'm the culprit? I just arrived in Rodenin. The gate guards can confirm that."

"That's not what I mean. But as you know, the security headquarters has issued a top-level alert. When a report comes in, we have to take the person in and record their movements."

He now understood why the gate guard had advised him to shave first upon entering the capital.

'It's still a hassle.'

The security team hadn't even caught a whiff of the serial killer and needed to show they were working hard.

"Understood."

Rian didn't argue.

He knew that such performance-driven labor was what maintained the city's security.

'This might be better.'

Rather than looking for a bathhouse at night, he could wash up and shave at the security headquarters.

"I'll cooperate with the investigation."

"Thank you."

The guard had the woman who reported Rian get off the carriage.

"We'll take it from here. Don't worry and get home safely."

The woman glared at Rian.

"He tried to kill me! This murderer! What were you planning to do by kidnapping me?"

Shaving would reveal a clean appearance, but the aura of a warrior who had fought demons couldn't be easily erased.

'It's inevitable.'

If even swordsmen felt a chill from his presence, it was natural for the woman to instinctively feel fear.

"Do you provide lodging?"

The guard, understanding Rian’s intention, smiled.

"Of course. It won't be as good as a nice inn, but you'll need to shave for the montage anyway."

The carriage carrying Rian sped down the winding road.

Rodenin's 2nd Security Headquarters.

Entering the three-story building on the west side of the capital, the warmth made him sleepy.

"I'd like to wash up first."

"Understood. I'll show you to the bathroom."

Entering the public bath, he saw sturdy guards who had finished their night patrols washing up.

Their muscles were well-developed, and their chests looked impenetrable, as expected of those who wore heavy armor.

"Phew, how long has it been since I had a bath?" As Rian undressed, recalling a past he couldn't remember, the eyes of those in the bath widened.

"Wow."

Though they prided themselves on their bodies, Rian’s physique was on another level.

'How can a person look like that?'

Rian’s body, which had cut down over ten thousand demons, was beyond biological; it was engineered.

'This isn't a body built by exercise. It's a body sculpted by the sword.'

The kinetic energy visible when his perfectly V-shaped body twisted was impressive.

Though it felt strange to have men sneaking glances at his body, Rian ignored it and washed up.

As he shaved off his long beard, it fell to his feet, and he scrubbed off the grime with a stone-like soap.

"Phew, refreshing."

As he shook his hair and stepped out, many guards who had heard the rumors had gathered.

"Are you done? Ah..."

The guard who had escorted Rian handed over his greatsword and widened his eyes at Rian’s body.

'The Knight of Maha. To see his entire body.'

It was a lifelong honor.

"I'm sorry I didn't recognize you. The rain made it hard to see your name clearly."

It was partly his fault, but confessing was easier since the other party was the Knight of Maha.

"It's fine."

As Rian, dressed in the security team's supplies, waited for the next step, the guard acknowledged his mistake.

'That woman reported such a big shot as a murderer. Well, even I felt a chill...'

Though Rian’s post-bath appearance wasn't gentle, it looked honest enough.

"Let's go. We'll finish the investigation as quickly as possible."

With the changed treatment, Rian thought he should stay clean from now on.

"But what about the murders? What exactly happened?"

He had heard the methods were perverse.

"Women have been kidnapped every seven days for the past two months. The next day, their bodies are found, and shockingly, they're completely drained of blood."

"Drained of blood?"

"Exactly. I've seen all kinds of corpses, but this was extreme. The bodies were completely drained, leaving only the skin, crumpled."

It was horrifying to imagine.

"Anyway, you don't have to worry about being a suspect. Now, this way..."

As they entered the room, the night patrols were interrogating the troublemakers in the chairs.

"Well, well, who do we have here? Isn't it the Knight of Maha who once shook the world?"

In a corner, a large middle-aged man over two meters tall approached.

It was Benoff, the 2nd Security Chief.

"Chief, this person isn't a suspect..."

"Step aside, kid."

Though he spoke playfully, an order was an order, and the guard quickly moved aside.

"Yeah, I know. The lone warrior who slays demons without belonging to any organization."

The Knight of Maha was a global issue, more than Rian imagined.

"After cutting down so many, you arrived in the capital of Jive, Rodenin. Hmm. Good, good."

"What do you want to know?"

Benoff bared his teeth and asked.

"Why our kingdom?"

"To go home." He came straight down, so he would go straight home.

"The security team is in bad shape these days. The higher-ups are pressuring us, and we can't catch the culprit. The stress is killing me."

Benoff lightly stepped and tapped the corner with his gloved fist.

"Let's make it simple. If you come up and fight, you'll be released immediately. Otherwise, you'll be investigated all night."

He wanted to test his skills against the Knight of Maha.

"Chief, but this is..."

Benoff interrupted again.

"Just stay put. What do you say, Knight of Maha? Forget the sword, let's fight like men with our fists?"

The guard whispered.

"He's on edge. Just refuse. Despite his appearance, he's the Rodenin Colosseum champion."

"I can hear you, kid."

As the guards and the troublemakers watched with interest, Rian spoke.

"I don't fight to defeat someone."

If defeating someone could make him stronger, what was the emptiness in his heart?

"Then why do you fight?"

"I don't know. I just think strength isn't about that."

"Hahaha! As expected, knights with a reputation are all talk. Just fancy words."

"Such provocations don't work on me." The laughter disappeared instantly, and Rian swung his shoulders as he walked to the ring.

"But if it's a way to quickly deal with this hassle, I don't mind going up."

Benoff's grin reached his ears.

"That's the spirit. Come on up."

Thus, an unexpected martial arts match was prepared, and the guards gathered around the ring.

"Ah, really! Let us watch too!"

The sound of junior guards dragging the troublemakers to the temporary detention center could be heard.

As Rian climbed into the ring and took off his shirt, a guard who volunteered as his second put on his gloves.

They were open-finger gloves with the fingertips exposed.

As he tightened the straps on Rian’s wrists, he said,

"I'm sorry it turned out this way. The captain is a martial arts fanatic. He was supposed to compete in the Central Continent Colosseum as the Rodenin champion, but it was canceled due to work. If it weren't for the security team, he would have gone global."

It was a roundabout way of telling him to be careful.

"It's fine. Fighting is my job. But if I win, let me sleep right away."

"Haha! Martial arts are different from swordsmanship." Though he had heard countless rumors about the Knight of Maha, the captain's skills were proven through his own strength.

As Rian walked to the center of the ring, tapping his gloves, Benoff approached as if he had been waiting.

"Let me explain the rules. Even if you die fighting, no telling your mom. Attacking vital points is a foul. It's better to die than to do that, right?"

Rian nodded briefly and stepped back to the side of the ring.

"We will now begin the 2nd Security Headquarters Martial Arts Competition. The allotted time is 10 minutes. Fight as much as you want."

The guard acting as the referee crossed his arms in an X shape and then quickly jumped out of the ring.

Benoff charged forward with a thud, but Rian raised his guard and only moved his upper body.

'Let's see the champion's skills.' The sound of jabs hitting Rian’s guard echoed.

Bang! Bang bang bang!

The combination of jabs continued to rhythmically tap his arms.

'This is fun.'

The corners of Rian’s mouth lifted slightly.
