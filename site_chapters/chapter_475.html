Chapter 475:

Gaold's Vacuum Press struck Uriel's face directly.

Boom!

The tremendous shockwave exploded, pushing the air out of the hall and then sucking it all back in.

Even those on the walls couldn't escape the storm's aftermath and turned to look in the shaking atmosphere.

"What… what is that…?"

Everyone's eyes were filled with shock.

Despite taking Gaold's powerful Vacuum Press head-on, Uriel stood in the same spot as before.

Looking at Gaold's twisted expression, Uriel spoke calmly.

"Is this the despair of humanity you spoke of?"

Uriel's halo flashed and expanded into a radiant ring.

"Then it is indeed a pitiful despair."

As Uriel's golden halo spread, the Celestial Galaxy Wheel was born.

The Celestial Galaxy Wheel.

As the wheel spun with a thunderous roar, Gaold widened the distance and applied immense atmospheric pressure around him.

As the Galaxy Wheel scraped the ground and charged forward, the earth split open, threatening to swallow Gaold.

Gaold's eyes widened.

It wasn't a collision of objects.

Everything that touched the Galaxy Wheel disintegrated like foam in its path.

Boom!

As Gaold's press crushed the ground, the shockwave from the Galaxy Wheel split his defensive barrier.

Boom!

The ground split beside Gaold as he twisted his body, and the indirect shockwave alone shook his insides, making him cough up blood.

"Ugh!"

Gaold's power was ineffective.

This realization was despairing for Sein and his group.

"Damn it! Hurry!"

Sein quickly regained his composure and led the group into the Hall of Corruption.

Judging by the power gap, Gaold could only hold out for a minute at most.

If they didn't reach Miro within that time, they would all be annihilated.

It wasn't the fear of death.

What truly terrified them was seeing their lifelong project fail right before their eyes.

As some archangels and Maras blocked their path in the Hall of Corruption, Kangnan took the lead.

She would reach Miro.

She would meet the woman who had driven a man's life to ruin after chasing her all his life.

'Uncle…'

Kangnan's nose wrinkled like a wolf's as she spotted a massive one-horned Mara charging from the front.

"Ugh!"

"Not a step further from here."

The three-meter-tall one-horned Mara, with a gorilla-like upper body covered in fur, swung its six arms simultaneously.

Kangnan bent low, taking the lowest possible stance, and twisted her waist.

As her right foot swung and struck the Mara's shin, there was a crack, and the leg broke.

"Aaaargh!"

Before the scream ended, Kangnan moved behind the Mara, spun twice, and kicked again.

Boom!

With the Skima of the Wolf Tribe in action, the Mara's neck broke, and its body flew several meters.

'Miro…'

Kangnan looked up to see where Miro was.

From the outside, it was just a sheer wall, but inside, it was a complex structure with numerous intersecting stairs.

The sky was already occupied by various Maras, and several fallen angels guarded key points on the stairs.

They weren't as strong as the enemies they had faced so far, but with Gaold fighting Uriel, they were still formidable opponents.

"Let's go!"

Kangnan gathered strength in her legs and leaped, her stockings tearing as her body rose vertically.

Numerous Maras swarmed her, but soon they were flung away, screaming in their death throes.

"She's impressive."

Kuan and Etella knew better than anyone how formidable Kangnan's martial prowess was.

They were also skilled in Skima, but Kangnan's body itself was a weapon.

Watching her fight, it seemed as if every part of her body was made solely for destruction.

"She fights well. Is that why she's the secretary of the Magic Association?"

"That's not it."

Zulu, following beside Sein, spoke.

"If Gaold brought us this far, now Kangnan must lead us."

Sein pondered Zulu's words.

Since the Heaven Project began, they had walked different paths and hadn't kept in touch.

But at some point, Kangnan had been by Gaold's side.

There must be a story behind it, but Sein quickly dismissed the thought.

In a situation where one mistake could ruin the operation, they had to use everything at their disposal.

Even if it was a precious human heart.

"Damn it!"

Kuan spun and flew, sweeping away enemies in all directions.

Finally, their view cleared, revealing the top of the Hall of Corruption.

Nothing had changed from what they had seen below.

Kariel remained composed, and Miro continued to watch Gaold and Uriel's battle without turning her head.

But Gaold was in a dire state.

Pushed back a kilometer, his body was battered and bruised from Uriel's relentless attacks.

All that remained were muscles ground like meat, protecting his bones.

"Huff, huff."

Gaold, with his arms hanging limp, glared at Uriel, breathing heavily.

Muscles weren't particularly important for a mage, but for someone whose power source was pain, the additional suffering was no help.

The problem was that his Air Press wasn't as powerful as before.

'Damn, bad luck.'

Miro muttered.

"Gaold can't win."

"Ugh?"

Arius, lying beside her, groaned and looked at her for an answer.

"It's not a matter of his body."

Miro narrowed her eyes and gritted her teeth.

'That's why you're a fool, Gaold…'

It was the catharsis of releasing what had been condensed for 20 years in one burst.

Humans could do anything to release that one emotion, but once it was out, it took time to reach that level of passion again.

'It's not your fault.'

She had pushed Gaold away.

So Gaold wouldn't have expected anything.

He hadn't come thinking he would be welcomed like a prince on a white horse, or that she would kiss him with tears of joy.

He had come because his heart ached so much that he would go mad if he didn't see her.

It was selfish to the extreme, but because all his emotions were directed at Miro, it was also selfless.

'If only I could…'

There was only one way to reignite Gaold's extreme endurance.

"Miro!"

Boom!

Covered in Mara's blood, Kangnan blasted away a group of enemies and reached the wall.

"Say it!"

Miro didn't even glance at Kangnan, keeping her mouth shut.

Kangnan took a deep breath.

She had finally made it here.

What kind of woman was she? What kind of woman could drive a man to such madness? The figure she had imagined every night was now in front of her.

"Tell Gaold…"

Kangnan shouted with all her might.

"Tell him you love him!"

Her voice echoed, reaching the battlefield a kilometer away, where shockwaves raged.

Gaold stopped fighting, and Uriel responded.

For Uriel, the Archangel of Destruction, war was as familiar as life itself, but he wanted to know.

Why were humans so chaotic?

As if time had stopped, everyone turned to Miro.

Under the silent pressure, Miro finally opened her mouth.

"The one I love is myself…"

Her head slowly turned to Kangnan.

"…and no one else."

Since she had thrown herself into existence for humanity 20 years ago, she had let go of everything.

She was just a wall, a system, a phenomenon.

Kangnan ground her teeth.

"What do you know?"

Yes. What did Miro know?

Could she be so shameless just because she had held a man's heart 20 years ago?

She knew nothing of his life, his pain, or how he had come this far, yet all she could say were those words?

'I will take you. I will make you kneel before Gaold!'

As Kangnan kicked off the ground and charged, Kariel raised his hand.

"Come forth, Erg."

A black sphere expanded in front of his palm, and the one-horned Mara Erg appeared.

Unlike other Maras, Erg resembled a giant insect with a metallic body, and a single metal lens was embedded in the center of its long, horse-like face.

Its nickname was the Computational Device of All Principles.

Kariel mainly summoned this Mara when he went into full defense mode.

Bzzz!

Electricity flowed through the dozens of glass bells on Erg's back, and numerous magic circles spread around them.

The 420-layered magic circles created barriers against all attributes, and Kangnan had to stop in front of the physical magic circle.

"What are you dreaming of, foolish human?"

Kariel spoke with a mocking tone.

"The only thing left for you is the punishment for angering the divine."

Kariel's finger pointed to the vast sky.

Darkness fell over everyone's faces as they looked up.

Countless fallen angels were flying towards them.

"Is this the backlash?"

The enemies they had bound with stop magic had regained their freedom.

It didn't feel unfortunate.

In fact, thanks to the absurd ability to stop time, they had reached Miro so quickly.

But the situation had reached its worst.

Sein quickly gave orders.

"I'll break the magic circle. Hold off the enemies in the meantime. But no stop or force meter magic."

Even if they destroyed Erg's barrier, there were still two archangels here.

The only magic that might give them a chance to escape with Miro was stop and space-time force meter magic.

No one failed to understand, and the group quickly found advantageous positions and prepared for battle.

Seeing this, Uriel turned to Gaold.

"It's unfortunate. You've been rejected again by the one you longed for so desperately."

Uriel's words were dignified but lacked sympathy. And that was precisely why Uriel was not human.

"Heh. Heh heh heh."

Gaold's lips twisted into a grin as he lifted his head.

"As expected... you're the best, Miro."

When he heard Miro's words through the archangel's resonance, it felt like the world was collapsing.

It was probably true, but it was also effective.

"Are you going to keep struggling? Is that what it means to be human?"

"You know nothing about humans."

Rumble, rumble, rumble!

The air around Gaold began to swirl.

It was different from before, or rather, the same overwhelming force that had swept away countless enemies was swirling again.

Boom!

A vacuum sphere formed in front of Gaold's hand.

His muscles were already shredded, and he couldn't even swing his arm, but he pushed forward with all his might.

"What would you know?"

'Danger!'

Uriel quickly raised his arm and swung the Celestial Galaxy Wheel in front of him.

Through the rapidly spinning afterimages, Gaold's ghostly face, swinging his limp arm, came into view.

Yes, what would he know?

The more unattainable something is, the more one desires it...

"The vile desires of humans."

Pain Sensitivity 10 Million Times - Vacuum Press.

Boom!

The torn atmosphere thundered and shot up into the sky, covering a radius of several kilometers.
