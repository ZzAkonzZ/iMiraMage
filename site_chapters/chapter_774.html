Chapter 774:

“Ughhh…”

How much time had passed?

It began with the groan of Arius, who was bleeding from his ears—signaling that their nerves were finally returning.

“Haa…”

Sein’s entire body throbbed as if he’d been beaten all over.

‘Miro?’

But there was no time to check himself. As soon as his vision returned, Sein scanned the battlefield.

Dozens of surrounding peaks had been shaved into a circular crater, as if to make the shape of the impact clear.

“Oh, Buddha!”

As the reverse cross, once assembled into a sphere, unraveled, Shura was the first to leap to her feet and run.

“We’re going too.”

Arius couldn’t hear, but recognizing Sein was moving, he immediately followed.

Shura stopped atop a peak 400 meters from the battlefield, checking the ground below.

Sein felt uneasy.

‘Why…?’

And when he reached her side, that unease solidified into reality.

‘Could he really be… a god?’

Nane stood before Miro, who knelt, still in a state of samadhi.

“If I hadn’t had a greater mission…”

His lips were torn and bleeding, but his expression was calm, showing no sign of shock.

“…I would have offered you praise.”

“……”

Miro couldn’t hear him.

As he gazed at her, her shoulders slumped and head bowed, Nane extended his hand.

“Sermon: Shout.”

A blue sword shot into the sky, then struck downward like a bolt of lightning, smashing into the crown of her head.

Miro shuddered briefly, then slowly opened her eyes.

“Kill me.”

Not words of defiance—just the voice of someone too drained to lift even a finger.

“You were a fraction of a second off.”

At the highest level, even the smallest cause could shift the outcome entirely.

“You might have fulfilled your goal.”

Miro sneered.

“Are you pitying me? You're not a god either.”

“No. But I am a little further from humanity.”

There was no excuse for such a defeat.

“A speck of impurity smaller than dust… Why didn’t you discard it? What still binds you to humankind?”

Nane wanted to know.

“Kill me.”

But Miro had no intention of answering. She simply closed her eyes, silently awaiting death.

“…Sermon: Death.”

Twelve red swords burst from behind Nane, curling like claws as they arced toward Miro.

“There will be no pain.”

Even from 400 meters away, Sein could feel the murderous intent so sharp it seemed it would pierce Miro at any moment.

“No. We have to rescue her—”

“It’s already too late.”

Shura did not smile before the erasure of Avalokiteśvara.

“The one closest to the Buddha has knelt. No one can surpass the Buddha’s time.”

As Sein launched himself forward, Nane’s sermon reached Miro.

“Damn it! Damn it!”

In the end, all he could do was slightly delay Miro’s death—not stop it.

Nane did not wait.

“This is the end.”

The twelve swords shot toward Miro.

As Sein and Arius braced themselves for a massive heartbeat, shrinking in tension—

“Phostmetery.”

A radiant, massive monster-bird wrapped itself around Miro and shot into the sky.

As Miro disappeared into another dimension, Nane’s sermon, unable to release its intent to kill, exploded on itself.

“Zulu’s summoned beast?”

Frantically scanning the surroundings, Sein spotted Miro—now relocated dozens of meters away from Nane.

“So… you really came after all.”

Zulu and Kangnan sat atop Kaidra, and on the ground, Gaold was standing, holding Miro.

“Now’s our chance!”

As Sein and Arius cast teleportation toward Kaidra, Shura clicked her tongue and flew toward Nane.

Miro stirred with a strange sensation.

‘What…?’

The information came in all at once—that she was still alive, and that she was being held in someone’s arms.

“……”

The moment she recognized Gaold’s face, her head spun, and a curse slipped from her mouth.

“…Damn it.”

Gaold gave a wry smile.

“It’s been a while.”

That was all he said. He turned his head again, back toward Nane.

“Is that the Buddha? Looks like he gave you a hard time.”

Miro grit her teeth.

“Put me down. Why are you even here? This has nothing to do with you anymore.”

Exhausted, she couldn’t even stand on her own. Sein came over and supported her.

“Gaold, you…”

“Later. Let’s talk later.”

Something in his tone made Sein fall silent.

‘Well… makes sense. He went through a hell worse than hell—not once, but twice—and came back.’

As Gaold focused solely on Nane, Miro stole a glance up at his face.

‘You’ve gotten so thin.’

All the muscle he’d built through endless battle was gone, leaving behind only scars.

‘What a terrible reunion.’

She’d sworn there wouldn’t be a second time… and yet once again, she owed her life to Gaold.

‘Lady Miro…’

Crouched beside her leg, Arius, relieved to see his master alive, bowed his head.

‘I can feel my heart racing.’

The diver who had once infiltrated Miro’s mind—

Arius knew about the one trauma engraved into her otherwise flawless psyche.

“Mikael Gaold.”

A name she could never bring herself to let go of.

“Let’s go back. Heaven is sealed for now, so we can regroup in Zion and—”

Gaold interrupted.

“Why is the Buddha trying to kill Miro?”

Sein frowned.

“What do you even know? We don’t have time for explanations. Just help us get Miro out of here safely.”

“No need. I’ll ask him myself.”

Gaold walked straight toward Nane.

“You idiot! Do you even understand the situation—?!”

Getting angry wouldn’t help, so Sein bit back his frustration and turned to Kangnan.

“Go get him. If you don’t, he’ll really die.”

Zulu answered instead.

“If we could stop him, he wouldn’t have come this far.”

“Then he’s going to die.”

Zulu stared at Gaold’s retreating back.

“To Gaold, this world is nothing but pain. But even after clawing through hell twice, he came back.”

The mood grew somber.

“We don’t know what Gaold went through. So we don’t have the right to stop him.”

“Buy me some time.”

Miro stepped away from Sein as she spoke.

“I’ll recover my strength soon. When I can move, I’ll run with him. Just protect Gaold until then.”

This was their final option.

Clicking his tongue, Sein turned back toward Nane.

“Tch. This just keeps getting messier…”

Watching Gaold closely, Nane’s eyes sparkled.

“Shura.”

“Yes?”

“Withdraw. As far as you can.”

“What?”

She asked, but no answer came.

“…Understood.”

Even after Shura left, Nane calmly waited for Gaold to approach.

‘He reminds me of my old self.’

After experiencing all the pain the world had to offer, Nane had surrendered his body to hell—and became the Buddha.

“Are you an ascetic?”

Gaold didn’t think the question was worth answering.

“Why are you trying to kill Miro?”

“To close this world.”

“What for? What did the world ever do to you?”

“Pain.”

Nane raised his index finger and pointed it at Gaold.

“And you—you understand this world better than anyone. Why not join me in severing the chains of suffering?”

“Understand this world?”

Gaold stepped forward, twisted his body, and swung full-force—

Right into Nane’s face.

“How the hell would I know, you bastard?!”

Sein’s eyes shot open in disbelief.

“That lunatic…!”

When Nane turned his head again, Gaold was stomping toward him, eyes rolled back.

“What, did you do a little soul-searching or something? You’re the Buddha, huh? What the hell do you know about me, running your mouth like that?”

“It’s Gaold!”

Seeing that same old fire in him, Sein turned to the group.

“It’s starting! Everyone get ready!”

Miro shouted, “Wait—hold on!”

As Gaold’s air pressure crushed the atmosphere, the ground wobbled up and down like a rubber mat.

“GRAAAAAAAHH!”

With all ten fingers curled like claws and a monstrous scream ripping from his throat, blood streamed from Gaold’s eyes.

“W-What is that…?”

Seeing Nane, unable to move under the crushing pressure, Kangnan’s face went pale.

‘How much pain must that be…?’

Pain sensitivity: 1 trillion times.

Since returning from Hell the second time, Gaold’s pain threshold had risen far beyond what it once was.

‘He’s going through that much pain?’

A strange glint flickered in Nane’s eyes.

‘And yet… he’s not dying?’

Zulu muttered, “He should’ve died already. But Gaold didn’t. Once something like that happens…”

…it just happens.

“And from that point on, he can’t even die anymore.”

With hair now fully white and blood-tears still flowing, Gaold continued walking toward Nane.

“Want me to tell you what real pain is?”

He was completely unhinged.

“It’s not something you can endure. You just take it.”

“Sermon: Destruction.”

A sword of light formed above Nane’s head, vibrating violently as it disturbed the air, which had hardened like steel.

“No! Someone stop Gaold—!”

Miro shouted just as—

“Pathetic, isn’t it? Trapped in this narrow body, all we can do is scream. And someone like you dares…”

Unable to contain the pain, Gaold screamed again, stomping his feet into the ground.

“Speak of pain!”

Pain sensitivity: 10 trillion.

“AH! AH! AH! AH! AH! AH! AH! AH!”

Pain sensitivity: 100 trillion.

“O foolish being!”

Nane amplified the power of his sermon to its limits.

“I will erase your pain!”

“GRAAAAAAAAAAAH!”

Gaold’s consciousness had already shattered. The only thing left in his mind was a single thought: pain.

‘1 quadrillion… 10 quadrillion… 100 quadrillion… DAMN IT!’

What kind of nonsense is that?

‘No matter how much someone screams in agony…’

How could anyone else feel that?

The greatest pain in the world is always your own, and for that reason, pain weighs the same for everyone.

‘But still… people live on.’

Even knowing nothing will change, hoping that maybe tomorrow will be better than today.

‘You grit your teeth—!’

And you keep living, because that’s what life is.

“So you did a bit of suffering, huh…”

Only Gaold could say this.

“And you think you can talk about someone else’s pain?!”

Pain sensitivity: 1 quintillion.

First, all the towering peaks nearby collapsed like dominos, crushed all the way to the horizon.

‘Ah… so that’s it.’

In that infinitesimal moment, where friction reached astronomical values, Nane realized.

‘So that’s why I couldn’t cry…’

As heat intense enough to melt any substance expanded the air around them, tears streamed down Nane’s cheek.

KWAHHHHHHHHH!

Jungcheon-dong exploded.

“KYAAAAAH!”

Across the Divine Earth, the Great Star’s scream echoed.

‘Another one… has transcended “the system.”’

Even with eyes wide in shock, her mind was racing with thoughts.

‘The ones who shatter the world’s balance…’

…are always humans.

The sacred land of the monks had vanished from the map.

“Oh my god…”

Shura, looking down on Jungcheon-dong, saw that it was now just one massive crater—as if a god had scooped the earth with a spoon.

Kangnan, having absorbed the lingering heat, began to cough.

“Cough! Cough!”

Had Miro not used all her mental strength to fly up in time, they all would have melted away.

“Is he… still alive?”

Zulu pointed to the center of the crater.

“He’s there.”

Everyone who looked down at the ground turned pale.

“N-Nane is…”

He lay sprawled out at Gaold’s feet, coughing up blood over and over.

“He’s down?”

Even as they saw it with their own eyes, it didn’t register. Only Miro truly accepted the reality.

‘Gaold… you…’

You brought down the Buddha.
