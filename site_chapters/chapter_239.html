Chapter 239: Battle Simulation (9)

“Ugh! This is ridiculous. How could this be….”

Boyle was shocked to learn that Dante's mental strength had not been reduced by even one percent.

He didn't fight an archmage. He was just a classmate of the same age, and he himself was a genius who also excelled in summoning magic.

In the end, it all came down to the details.

Dante had taken control of the thousands of tiny variables that arose during the battle, widening the gap to an extreme degree.

"Stop sulking and get it over with, will you? We had a deal, remember?"

Dante, with his hands in his pockets, waited smugly.

It was a situation where Boyle, who was officially ranked first in the advanced class, had to kneel publicly.

Boyle felt a surge of anger matching the weight of his position as the top student. But a promise was a promise, and he couldn't think of a way out.

"I… I lost."

The moment Boyle knelt down and admitted defeat, a torrential downpour poured down on the students' hearts.

Shirone and his friends, who had been cheering for Boyle, also felt heavy-hearted.

But it was a defeat without any room for excuses.

Iruki said.

“I guess we have to admit it. Information magic, that would be really tricky to deal with.”

Savina intervened.

“No, it’s possible because he’s Dante. We’ve been fighting under Teacher Olivia since we were ten. You guys haven't had nearly as much real combat experience. Dante's official record is 472 wins out of 472 battles. And in unofficial matches, he's never lost—he's a legend of undefeated battles. He’s not an opponent that a mere country bumpkin academy’s top student can beat.”

472 official matches. If you include the unofficial ones, he's fought in over a thousand battles.

Any mage will have practical skills if he fights that much interpersonal battle.

But what made Dante truly remarkable was that he had never lost, not even once, throughout all those battles.

As Dante's background was revealed, the students began to buzz with excitement. In an instant, he went from being a disliked classmate to a star of the kingdom.

No matter how bad his personality was, if his skill was that extraordinary, fame would follow. No, perhaps that is why they are more enthusiastic about it.

All the students flocked to Dante. Though they didn't show it outwardly, it was clear that they were all trying to get a word in with him.

Where the students had left like an ebb tide, only Canis and Arin remained.

As the crowd of students dispersed, only Canis and Arin were left standing alone. Although it was their first time watching a Heaven's Trial duel, it didn't particularly impress them since they had fought countless battles themselves.

"This is interesting. So magic academies train like this too. Canis, should we give it a try?"

“Not really. I don’t feel like it.”

“Aren’t you afraid of losing to Dante? Hehe.”

Although she said that, Arin's feelings were no different from Canis's. He wasn't keen on the idea of Heaven's Trial.

"What’s the big deal? Shirone's not making a fuss, so why should I?"

"Shirone's keeping quiet because he naturally dislikes fighting. You're different from him, Canis."

"Arin, it seems like you've been siding with Shirone a lot lately?"

Arin stuck out her tongue.

Of course, anyone who is at odds with Canis is also at odds with her. If the two clash, she will do his best to help Canis, but Shirone was someone she just couldn't bring herself to dislike.

“Anyway, what do you think? Information magic, it seems to be a new trend. Do you think you can win?”

Canis snorted.

He wasn't a fan of Heaven's Trial, but Dante's skills were undeniably real. The interesting part was that Dante was a genius in a way that mirrored Shirone.

Iruki was also a genius, but he was a bit too one-sided in his approach.

"Hmm, if I were to face him on the battlefield…"

"If you were to face him?"

Canis' face, which had been silent for a long time, gradually became serious.

Sensing Canis's wavering emotions, Arin nudged him in the side, urging him on.

"Well? If you faced him? What would happen?"

Canis turned his head away as if to avoid Arin's gaze and spoke in a small voice.

“It’s, it’s going to be a great match.”

Match Point (1)

“Yay! I won. Hey, get on your knees, quick!"

"Damn it! Let's go again! I would've won if I hadn't messed up!"

At some point, a catchphrase started to spread throughout the advanced class: "Get on your knees," a phrase popularized by Dante.

Even in Class Seven, similar scenes were happening frequently. Though it was just a game, the competitive atmosphere was no joke.

If A fights B, who would win? And if the winner fought C, would they win too?

Everything was turning into a system that ranked every student in the Royal Magic Academy.

“Tch! I don’t like it. Anyway, that weird principal came in and ruined the school."

Mark, lying on the lawn, grumbled as he watched his juniors engage in hand-to-hand combat at Heaven’s Trial training ground.

Currently, his match point ranked him as the top of Class Six. He had crushed every challenger that came his way. In the past, he would have walked around with his shoulders hunched, but after he came to respect Shirone, the ranking of the lower classes had no meaning to him.

Maria, sitting next to him, said while plucked at the grass.

"Mark, even if the principal is annoying, you shouldn't call them weird. What if someone hears you?"

"What am I supposed to do? I'm frustrated! I can't stand Dante, or whatever his name is. Especially Closer, that bastard used his passive magic when he fought me. If we fought fist to fist, he wouldn’t be a match for me.”

Maria also vividly remembered the scene where Mark got beaten. Closer, despite being younger, had even mocked him.

“I’m sure Senior Shirone will take care of them. After all, they’re in the same Class Four. If they push you too far, I’ll talk to Teacher Thad about it. What do you think?"

Mark was too proud to even voice his opinion. He wanted to get back at the guy who had bullied Maria, but he knew their skills were genuine. Of course, as a self-proclaimed member of the "Shirone line," Mark had plenty of monstrous seniors like Shirone, Amy, and Iruki…. He could ignore Dante and his crew because of said reason, but on the other hand, the fact that he couldn't handle the situation himself was driving him crazy.

"I really don't like this… not at all…"

Since the love for battle knows no age, the students in Class Four were also keenly interested in the ranking battles.

However, since Dante had organized the hierarchy of Class Four, their gaze was focused on higher places.

“If Teacher Ethella and Teacher Sienna fought, who would win?”

“Hey, of course, it’s Teacher Etella. She’s someone well-known throughout the kingdom.”

“But you don’t fight based on fame. Even though Teacher Ethella has achieved Triple, Teacher Sienna is an authority on freezing. In a real battle, specialists are usually the best."

"If you put it that way, then Teacher Thad would win. If both are at the same 6th rank, fire has the advantage over ice, right?"

Shirone stepped forward at the sound that could be heard even when trying to cover his ears.

"What are you all talking about? They’re all excellent teachers. They wouldn’t fight each other just to prove who’s stronger."

The students had indifferent expressions. After Boil kneeled, the center of attention in the advanced class shifted from Shirone to Dante.

At first, the students thought that Shirone would step forward and restore the academy's tarnished reputation. But that didn’t happen. In fact, it even seemed like he was avoiding confrontation. Recently, there were even rumors that he was dodging Dante because he didn’t want to fight him.

"So what if we're just talking about a safe, hypothetical fight? Honestly, you’re the same. You’re too scared to even join the Heaven's Trial duels against Dante."

“I’m not scared. I just don’t want to fight for no reason. I don’t know about tactical training, but one-on-one combat has nothing to do with our grades.”

“Tch, grades this, grades that. In the end, you’re only good within the academy walls. A true mage should be strong in real combat.”

Shirone shrugged, realizing it was pointless to argue further.

The classmates who had placed their last hopes on provoking him turned away, disappointed when even that didn’t work.

“Tch, Shirone’s no different after all."

“What can he do? In front of the star of the kingdom, Shirone is just a star from a small town.”

Shirone couldn't figure out what the point of ranking students was. The strength he was pursuing was different from the strength that could be achieved through fighting and subduing.

On the other hand, there were bound to be students for whom that was the only thing that mattered. A representative example was Pandora, who was ranked second in the advanced class.

After Dante subdued Boyle, she actively followed Dante and his crew and was now practically their servant.

"Hey, Pandora! Hurry up! We’re dying of thirst here!"

At Savina's angry voice, Pandora hurriedly ran over with a glass of water.

"Sorry, the line was too long."

"You should’ve just said it was for me and cut in line. You’re so inflexible."

“Hehe, are you tired? Should I massage your shoulders?”

"Sure, why not? I ran too hard with Haste, and now I’m sore."

Pandora earnestly massaged Savina’s shoulders. It would be a lie to say it didn’t hurt her pride, but she held onto the hope that soon they would recognize her true worth.

Getting close to Dante's group would instantly place her among the kingdom’s most promising talents. A rise in status from second place in a provincial academy to fourth in the entire kingdom. Isn't it a gamble worth taking?

As Closer fanned himself, he spoke.

“Hey, Pandora. I’m a bit thirsty too."

"Huh? Oh, okay! I’ll go get some water."

Pandora quickly ran to the makeshift water station. The sun was particularly hot today, and many students were waiting in line.

Though she could cut the line using Dante’s influence, that would be beneath her. Her specialty was to annoy the person in front of her and make them choke.

"Hey, what are you, an elephant? Drink your fill and move along! Think about the people behind you."

"Here, take mine."

Boyle held out his cup of water. Pandora looked at him with displeasure, but she couldn't help but accept it, thinking of the waiting Closer.

“Ugh, thanks.”

As Pandora returned to Closer, Boyle called out to her.

"Hold on. You’re not seriously going to give that to him, are you?"

"What business is it of yours? Don’t tell me you’re holding a grudge just because you lost to Dante?"

"I’ve accepted my defeat. But what’s up with you lately? Sure, getting close to Dante isn’t bad, but do you really have to grovel like this?"

“So what does it matter to you? Why, are you anxious already? Are you afraid that I will overtake you?”

"That’s not it! You’ve been my rival since we were kids! It just bothers me to see you bowing down like this!"

"But that’s not the case anymore. I’m joining Dante’s group. Soon enough, I’ll be featured in academic journals, and when I graduate, people will be lining up to scout me. Just admit you’re jealous. Anything you say now lacks credibility after losing."

Boyle fell silent. He had plenty to say, but none of it would reach Pandora. It was the unavoidable fate of a loser.

“Damn it! Do whatever you want!

Shirone’s group, watching from a distance, understood how Boyle felt.

All the relationships in the advanced class now revolved around Dante. No one dared challenge the absolute number one, and Shirone increasingly wanted to distance himself from the current situation.

He believed that if he kept refining his own abilities, success would naturally follow without needing to fight.

What was currently occupying his mind was how he could use Ataraxia as quickly as possible. In that state, Heaven's Trial duels didn’t even cross his mind.

Day after day, Shirone dedicated himself to lifting practice, and finally, he was able to hold the target in place for a full 20 seconds.

At this point, it was practically a feat. Maintaining a mental state that others couldn’t sustain for even a second for a full 20 seconds was remarkable.

“Wow, as expected from Shirone. His Spirit Zone is growing bigger and bigger. No matter what, he’s an incredible talent.”

“Well, that’s just because he’s an Unlocker. He can’t even look Dante in the eye."

“Honestly, Dante is too strong. I understand Shirone’s position as well.”

"Why do you assume Shirone is running away? When it comes down to it, Shirone is terrifying in competition. And don’t forget, there’s still Canis and Iruki."

"Hmph! If he’s so confident, why is he avoiding it? If I had that kind of skill, I’d challenge him out of sheer frustration. Since he’s running away like that, the girls are going to go to Dante.”
