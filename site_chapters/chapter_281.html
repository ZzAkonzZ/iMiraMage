Chapter 281: Late Night Ball (2)

Most of the Black Line are wanted criminals. The Seven Sins in particular were branded as world-class atrocious criminals, so they absolutely need a safe hideout to stay in one place.

Working under such a lacking master, it’s only a matter of time before one finds their neck on the guillotine. As this thought crossed his mind, he couldn't help but worry about his own safety.

"Hmm, maybe it's time to wrap things up here?"

The reason bounty hunters from the Red Line, who had been tracking him with crazed eyes, had never caught Arius until now was thanks to his beast-like instinct for knowing exactly when to strike and when to flee.

His intuition, honed by over a decade as a fugitive, was warning him that if he didn’t leave now, it would be dangerous.

'But it's a shame to end it like this....'

Ataraxia. The biggest catch in his grave robber career had been ensnared in his net.

Though material wealth played a part in why he dove into grave robbing, the greater motivator was to satisfy the intellectual hunger of an Unlocker.

In that sense, the power of Archangel Ikael was certainly something that piqued his curiosity.

'Should I just act crazy and dive down to the first level? No, it's impossible alone. Especially since a mage's unconscious is much stronger than ordinary people's. I can’t rely on Orkamph either.'

As Arius propped his chin and sank into thought, he slowly raised his head. Glancing down the empty hallway, he flashed a thin smile and spoke.

"What’s the matter?"

When an unknown official close to Zion revealed himself from the corner, a sneer naturally formed. The official was neither a mage nor an assassin. But as always, his presence couldn't be felt until he showed signs of himself first.

"There's someone who wishes to meet you, Sir Arius."

Arius knew who Zion was. But the official refrained from mentioning his master’s name.

It wouldn’t do anyone any good if it were known that Arius, Orkamph’s close aide, was secretly meeting and dealing with Zion, a key figure of Terraje Faction.

"That’s a welcome proposition. Lead the way."

As the administrator turned around and took a step, Arius stared at the old man's Achilles tendon.

He walked briskly, not making a sound.

Despite appearing as light as a cat, the calves hidden beneath the robe were undoubtedly highly developed.

However, that didn’t necessarily mean he was a martial arts master. It was more like the craftsmanship of someone who had spent a lifetime tending to their master’s needs.

'Haha, what an interesting place. The Royal Palace.'

@

"Damn it! That bastard made a complete fool of me!"

The glass bottle Zion threw smashed against the gallery door. The shattered glass flew everywhere, startling the cat sleeping on the floor into the air.

Unbothered, Uorin, sitting at the table, raised a teacup to her lips and took a sip.

"Why? I thought it was great. It was quite spectacular."

Zion glared at his sister.

Her nonchalant personality was usually cute, but in a situation like this, it's so infuriating he could die.

"I'll have to step up myself. I can't leave Shirone alone."

Uorin stroked the chin of the cat that climbed onto her lap and said.

"He was threatening though. What if he had cast magic towards the audiences' seats?"

Zion flushed as he recalled the situation at the time.

As Uorin said, it was a terrifying force. No matter how top-notch the guards were, there would have been casualties.

"That's exactly it! It was a declaration of war! It must have been revenge for me pointing a sword at him!"

"Isn't that a bit of an overstatement? He could have just done that because he was angry."

“It’s the same either way! That lowlife ignored my offer! I won’t let it slide!”

Just then, someone knocked on the door.

Zion, who thought it was Arius, calmed his excitement and returned to the table where his sister sat.

“Come in.”

Arius opened the door and entered. It was the only place where he could meet Zion away from prying eyes, but the atmosphere today was unusually tense. He gracefully leaped over the glass shards on the floor and greeted them politely.

"It's been a while, First Prince. Ah, you were here too, Young Lady."

Zion snorted. In the current situation, even the title of the first prince was nothing but a facade.

"Sit down for now. There’s something I want to discuss.”

As Arius gestured for understanding and sat down on the chair, Uorin poured him some tea.

Receiving the teacup with both hands, he looked at Zion and asked,

"What brings you to summon me? Have you grown tired of the Objet I procured last time?”

Uorin waved her finger and corrected him.

“Ei, you didn’t ‘procure’ it. You sold it to us, fair and square.”

"Haha! If we're being precise, that's right. But you’re well aware that it’s not something that can simply be bought with money, yes? The Objet."

Zion wasn’t interested in idle chatter.

"The Objet isn't the issue."

“Oh? Then what is it?”

"Don’t play dumb! It’s clear now. The reason father summoned Shirone to the palace. It's to rob Acraxia, right?"

"It's not Acraxia, it's Ataraxia. And grave robbing, really. If you're going to say it, please call it extraction."

Zion took a deep breath and calmed his mind. Getting dragged into Arius's sly banter would mean never reaching the point, even if a whole day passed.

“So... how far has the plan progressed?”

“Hmm, even for the First Prince, this is a bit difficult. After all, I am a subject of His Majesty, so I can’t just reveal classified information.”

Zion extended his hand to the north. Armand flew into his hand as if sucked in by a magnetic force, and in one swift motion, he pointed it directly at Arius's throat.

“I’m not joking around right now. It’s best you answer properly.”

Arius took a leisurely sip of tea, as if only now feeling at ease with the sword aimed at him.

But many thoughts were passing through his head.

Zion was nothing more than a brat without manners. Armand, on the other hand, was a troublesome weapon. He had retrieved it himself, but it was truly a shame for such a precious item to be in the hands of an immature royal.

"If you're asking how far it has progressed... about 49 percent, I'd say?"

It wasn’t a lie. After all, starting is half the work, and since they haven't even started yet, 49 percent is appropriate.

Uorin let out a small chuckle as she realized the meaning of the words, but Zion seemed to think that Arius had made considerable progress and continued to press him with a surprised expression.

"Stop it at all costs. No, just eliminate Ataraxia completely. If you're a grave robber, it should be possible, right?"

Arius exhaled through his nose.

What a pathetic bastard. How could he think of eliminating the power of an archangel that has crossed into the world just because a mere king deems it necessary?

Ataraxia, even if he didn't excavate it, was a treasure trove for humanity that had to be analyzed at some point.

"I'll say it again, as a body employed by His Majesty..."

“I’ll give you as much money as you want.”

Arius closed his mouth.

He had earned a huge fortune while dealing with Zion so far. Of course, he had a tendency to enjoy grave robbing itself rather than money, but he intends to part ways with Orkamph anyway. If he made a big haul one last time and left, wouldn't he be able to secure something like a retirement fund?

“Hmm.”

Lost in thought, Arius finally revealed his true nature.

As his cold, snake-like eyes emerged, Zion, despite having a sword pointed at him, couldn’t help but shiver.

However, the pupils hidden by Arius' narrow eyes were not directed at Zion, but at Uorin.

‘Beautiful.’

It wasn't an emotion one would normally feel for a fourteen-year-old girl, but she was truly stunning. Her mysterious appearance, combined with the halo of being a Terraje, made her a truly tempting fruit.

He had analyzed the minds of countless individuals, but Uorin remained the only one whose thoughts he couldn’t fathom at all.

When he first began his work as a Diver, he indulged in the amusement of directly peering into humanity’s innermost desires.

Such filthy, grotesque, and even horrifying desires.

But after diving over 800 times, no human mind could feel novel anymore.

In that situation, Uorin was material that could show him a new world he had never experienced before.

If he could dive one last time before he died, the target would be Uorin, not Ataraxia.

"First, let me tell you the exact progress. Currently, it's impossible to extract Shirone's Ataraxia. It is encapsulated. However, if you wish, I can extract Ataraxia in its entirety and present it to you, Prince.”

"Present it to me? Ataraxia?"

"Yes. The power of an archangel would belong to Your Highness."

Zion swallowed hard. At this moment, his face looked exactly like Orkamph’s.

No, anyone who had witnessed the power of Ataraxia would react the same way.

But Uorin, apparently unaffected, immediately caught the flaw in his words.

“But you said that extraction was impossible a moment ago?”

“With normal methods, yes. Ataraxia is presumed to reside in the deep sea of Shirone's consciousness. In short, it’s too deep. The deeper you dive into the psyche, the stronger the ego's resistance becomes. At that depth, the moment you enter, you'll be buried immediately upon entering."

Zion retracted Armand and asked,

"But how do you plan to grave rob it?"

“There is but one way.”

Arius was thinking of offering the same proposal he had made to Orkamph.

But unlike before, he took his time.

If there was any chance to turn things around, this would be the last opportunity. The moment he presented this idea, he would be permanently at odds with Orkamph. Also, he himself had to leave Kazra as soon as the job was done.

“Speak! How can I make Ataraxia mine?”

As Zion urged him, Arius spoke in a cold voice.

"By extracting Shirone's brain."

Not only Zion, but even Uorin were visibly shocked.

"Extracting... the brain?"

“To be precise, it’s about destroying Shirone’s unconscious mind. Consciousness is governed by the brain, but the unconsciousness is connected to the body. Therefore, by separating the brain from the body, even if you dive deep into consciousness, the resistance of the ego will be significantly weakened. Of course, you don't need to open up the skull. Just need what's above the neck will do."

Arius made a gesture with his thumb, as if slicing his neck.

Zion, seeming to understand, nodded.

"So, in short, you're saying we need to kill Shirone."

“Well, I’m not a fan of inductive reasoning. Cutting the neck doesn’t necessarily mean death, does it? I'm not saying we should kill Shirone, I'm just telling you a way to safely infiltrate the unconscious mind."

Zion was dumbfounded. He couldn't believe this suggestion was being made without acknowledging that it involved assassinating a crown prince candidate.

"Cutting off the head doesn't mean death? You're completely insane."

Arius smiled as if he had been complimented.

"While living as a grave robber, I learned one thing. There's no one in this world who isn't crazy."

Zion, tired of the madness, skipped the banter and got to the point.

"If I bring you Shirone's head, can you extract Ataraxia?"

"If you're asking whether it's possible, yes, it is. But it must be done quickly. The moment the head is severed, I need to Dive immediately. Because 'if by any chance' it leads to death, the mind also disappears."

“That fast? Life would only last about 10 seconds after decapitation.”

"Time flows slowly in the unconscious. The door has been installed, so as soon as Shirone is decapitated, I will Dive. I’ll Dive to the first level, and from there, I’ll excavate the true Ataraxia. Then it will belong to Your Highness.”

Zion puffed his cheeks and let out a long sigh, pacing around the gallery.

No matter what, an outright assassination. Isn't that too hasty?

No, this was the right move. As time passed, the number of neutrals siding with Orkamph would only increase. If he had to make a move before the paternity test results came out, today was the perfect time.

Arius read the murderous intent that appeared in Zion's eyes and smiled with satisfaction. He was definitely closer to a king than Orkamph.

As if he had already heard the answer, Arius asked,

“So, how much are you willing to pay?”

Zion, not offering a rebuttal, gave his answer through silence.

Now the negotiation table was set. After pondering for a while, he proposed a suitable bid price.

“2.7 billion gold.”

Arius asked back with a slightly displeased expression.

“Hmm, wasn’t Armand 3 billion gold?”

"So what? Are you saying that bastard's magic is more valuable than my magic sword?"

The 2.7 billion that Zion proposed was calculated based on that reasoning.
