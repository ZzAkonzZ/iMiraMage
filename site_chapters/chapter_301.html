Chapter 301: Secrets of the Abyss (2)

"This is truly amazing."

Shirone had perfectly categorized his unconscious mind. Honestly, it was beyond impressive; it didn't seem humanly possible.

Armin's group turned towards the sound of footsteps at the entrance. Shirone, looking paler than before, was walking in, breathing heavily.

"I'm feeling a bit better. Let's go down quickly."

Amy wiped her tears and approached Shirone.

"Are you okay? Let me help you."

"Go away. I don't want your help."

"But we need to go. Come on."

Despite Shirone's strong resistance, Amy supported him.

Shirone couldn't hold out any longer and had to accept her help.

Reina watched them with a bittersweet smile.

'So that's how it is.'

At first, she couldn't understand how they could ignore their feelings and remain friends.

But now she understood. They were bound by a strong trust that transcended emotions.

'I... could never be in that position.'

"She's an amazing girl."

Armin approached Reina. He also thought Amy was holding up well.

The avatar's hostility was pure emotion, making it 100% reliable. Yet, Amy was sacrificing herself for Shirone, something an ordinary girl couldn't do.

A relieved smile finally appeared on Reina's face.

"She's been holding on for a year."

Shirone's Mental Stage 3.

It was a world of extreme cold.

As the freezing wind that could freeze blood blew, Shirone's group crossed the frozen sea, huddling their shoulders.

There was no sun, but the ground reflected the moonlight, allowing them to see ahead.

However, all that lay before them was a vast darkness.

Amy imagined this was what the Arctic night would be like.

So cold and lonely, yet mysteriously serene.

In the extremely short visibility, occasionally passing by were crystals as tall as a person.

Each had a different color and emitted a soft glow like bioluminescent organisms.

"Love. Friendship. Desire."

Armin spoke.

"These are probably jewels containing such fundamental emotions. We can't access them unless we approach directly. He's handling the unconscious as if it were conscious. There's no need to worry."

Amy sighed in relief.

Since she hadn't encountered his inner emotions, it wouldn't be awkward to face Shirone again.

Of course, that was assuming they survived this place.

As they descended deeper, Armin felt he understood Shirone even less.

Even just looking at the 3rd stage, it was a landscape that couldn't exist without defining and establishing all inner aspects oneself.

'How deep is this child's insight?'

As he pondered, the lights of the crystals began to go out one by one. Shirone looked as if he could die at any moment.

Amy didn't look back at Shirone.

He wouldn't want her to see.

She just kept moving forward, unable to wipe away her flowing tears.

The avatar's weight had become so light it could no longer be called human.

'Just a little longer, Shirone...'

Even as he struggled to take a step, Shirone glared into the darkness with determined eyes.

Finally, the curtain of darkness lifted, revealing a small, lit cabin.

"That's it. The 2nd stage."

Everyone knew by now.

Armin moved his frozen legs quickly and explained.

"We've taken the shortest route through the mental world. We might have caught up to Arius by now. Be prepared for anything."

He was telling them to be ready for battle.

Amy began to warm up her mind with a series of mental exercises.

Reina took out an arrow covered in frost from the cold and nocked it, leaning against the door.

Armin quickly opened the door and inspected the inside of the cabin. Though not a Zoner, his panoramic vision without visual direction allowed him to scan the area faster than an ordinary person.

Next, Reina swiftly entered the cabin.

Following Armin with her bow drawn, she covered all directions but saw no enemies.

When Armin signaled that it was clear, Amy brought Shirone inside.

Their skin, blue from the freezing wind, thawed in the warmth of the cabin.

When the door closed, the eerie wind noise disappeared.

"Over there..."

Shirone pointed to the living room sofa with a trembling hand.

Amy helped him over, and he collapsed onto the sofa like a puppet with its strings cut.

"I just need to rest a bit..."

"That would be best."

Armin, checking Shirone's condition, agreed.

Then Reina, taking on the role of the villain, spoke up.

"We don't have time to waste. If Arius isn't here, it means he's already gone down to the 1st stage."

"Even if he found Ataraxia, extracting it will take time. Even if we stop Arius, Shirone will die if we don't imprint something here."

"Ah..."

Reina recalled the situation in reality.

Zenogor's strong line was cutting Shirone's neck.

Having reached the final stages of the depths, it was time to find a solution.

"The 1st stage is Shirone's earliest memory. It's called the primal consciousness. Since it involves events in the womb, it's impossible to imprint a clear concept."

"So this is Shirone's treasure trove."

Reina looked around.

The precious place Shirone had chosen was his cozy home. It showed how much he had felt his family's warmth.

Amy put dry firewood in the fireplace and cast Ignite.

Damn Ignite.

She thought to herself, feeling the warmth of the fire with her hands before turning to the group.

"The method is one thing, but where do we record it?"

"It varies from person to person. But based on what we've seen, it might be in the form of a book. Let's check the library."

The three of them headed to the library.

Shirone's thoughts throughout his life were categorized in the form of books.

There were even books with people's names as titles.

Vincent, Olina, and of course, Reina and Armin were all there.

As Amy scanned the names of the magic school students, her eyes fixed on one book.

Carmis Amy.

It had a red cover.

Everything was in that book.

How Shirone thought of her. What emotions he felt towards her.

Amy, as if possessed, took out the book and caressed the leather texture of the hardcover.

Armin and Reina watched from behind.

"I won't stop you, Amy. If that satisfies you."

"Amy has guided Shirone well up to this point. She deserves it."

Reina looked at Armin in surprise.

She understood the woman's heart and allowed it despite knowing it was wrong, but she didn't expect Armin to approve as well.

One thought crossed her mind.

'So that's it. The odds are much lower than we thought.'

Armin was preparing for the worst-case scenario.

If they couldn't find a way to save Shirone, this would be Amy's last memory with him. At least she should know his feelings before saying goodbye, Armin thought.

"No. I won't do it."

Amy only looked at the cover and then put the book back on the shelf.

Nothing was over yet. Shirone would survive. He would continue to compete with her and become a magician.

"It doesn't seem like there's a way to imprint something on Shirone here. Let's check somewhere else."

As Amy left the study without hesitation, Armin and Reina exchanged smiles.

Amy met Shirone's gaze, who was staring intently at her. But now, she was beyond caring.

"Go ahead, hate me all you want. When we get back to reality, I'll beat you up good."

"I hate you. Really."

"Hmph! Do whatever you want. I hate you too! Bleh!"

As Amy stuck out her tongue, Shirone coughed.

When a mouthful of blood gushed out, Amy's face turned pale, and she rushed over.

"Shirone! Are you okay?"

Even as the mental world collapsed, his eyes had remained clear, but now they were unfocused.

The avatar had become so light it could no longer feel its weight.

Shirone struggled to lift his trembling eyelids and spoke.

"Why... do I... hate you..."

"Okay, I get it. Just hang in there."

"...No."

Shirone spoke in a dying voice.

"I hate seeing you dance... with someone else."

Amy felt like she had been hit with a hammer.

She thought it was disappointment over not being able to save his life. She never imagined it was because she danced with Zion.

"That... that, you know, Shirone..."

It was an unavoidable event.

Or was it really?

Was she entirely blameless for Shirone's resentment?

"Idiot, it's not like that."

Amy spoke in a trembling voice.

She knew Shirone would be hurt. Yet, she couldn't help but look away.

She was too scared.

"It's really not like that. I... I..."

Shirone squeezed out his fading voice.

"Run... take everyone... and get out of here. I can't... everyone will die."

Amy shook her head and hugged Shirone.

No matter what happened, she wouldn't give up. She would never let him go.

"No one will die. I'll save you! I will definitely save you!"

Shirone was losing consciousness.

No matter how loudly Amy shouted, it seemed he couldn't hear her, his blurry eyes staring at the ceiling.

"Run... Amy."

Reina, who had been searching Shirone's room, ran out to the living room and shouted.

"Amy! Over here! I found it!"

Amy laid Shirone on the sofa and ran to the room.

She didn't even think to wipe her tears. They had reached the final moment, and it was all or nothing now.

On the table in Shirone's room, a notebook lay open.

Armin was waiting, and Reina was anxiously pacing.

Amy pointed to the notebook as she approached.

"Is this it?"

"Yes. It seems to be a notebook recording the flow of consciousness. But no matter how much I think about it, I can't figure out a way to save him. What should we do, Amy?"

Amy approached the table and examined the notebook.

At the top of the last page, the name "Amy" was written.

With a sorrowful expression, she turned the pages to understand Shirone's thoughts.

Her name was written there too.

The entire notebook was filled with her name.

She turned another page. It was the same.

No matter how many pages she turned, she couldn't find where the name "Amy" began.

Tears welled up in Amy's eyes again, but she quickly shook her head to control her emotions.

She had to save Shirone. By any means necessary.

But how?

No matter how hard she thought, she couldn't find a way to safely free Shirone from the strong line strangling his neck.

At that moment, the cabin shook like an earthquake.

Amy grabbed the table to steady herself.

If the 2nd stage of the depths was shaking this much, it meant Shirone's life in reality was almost over. If they didn't catch up to Arius, they would all perish here.

"Amy! We need to go down now! I'll bring Shirone!"

Reina ran out of the room, but Amy couldn't move.

Even if she died here, she had to think of a way to save Shirone.

'No! I don't know! What should I do?'

But reality was different from the mental world.

What couldn't be done, couldn't be done.

Reina and Armin weren't fools.

They knew it was physically impossible, so they were looking for a way to save someone else first.

Amy's hand, gripping the pen on the table, trembled. Each passing second felt like a curse.
