Chapter 1248:

A village near the Pyramid of Truth.

Most people had left, swept up by the Wave of In, but a small number remained in the village.

“Dad! Dad!”

Eighteen-year-old Arti was brimming with excitement at the realization of the story she’d heard since childhood.

“You were right, Dad! The pyramid—! The Pyramid of Truth is glowing like crazy!”

“Arti!”

Her broad-shouldered father stood up.

“I told you, didn’t I?! Never go near the Pyramid of Truth! That place isn’t meant for people!”

“But we’re fine, right? Just like you said—we know the incantation. We haven’t even left the village.”

“Haah, you’re impossible. You’re going to make me old before my time.”

His wife, looking out the window, spoke gently.

“It seems the time of prophecy has come. Dear, shouldn’t Arti know the truth now?”

“Yes. Yeah…”

After a moment of thought, her father went into a room and returned with an old wooden box.

“Come here.”

As he opened the box in front of Arti, she saw an old parchment scroll inside.

“What’s this?”

Her slightly disappointed tone made him chuckle.

“A guild oath.”

“A guild?”

He turned back to glance out the window.

“No one knows when the Pyramid of Truth was built. But the king who founded ancient Paras—the Pharaoh—apparently knew a secret passed down by word of mouth. That’s why he built his tomb to look exactly like the Pyramid of Truth. He believed that if he rested there, he would one day resurrect.”

“Did he come back to life?”

“Arti, death is the privilege of the dead. Eternal sleep, bodily decay—those are ideas of the living. Resurrection is probably no different.”

“Hmm…”

It made sense… kind of.

“The important thing is that the ancient Pharaoh knew of God’s existence. The knowledge must have been passed down in secret. But there were others besides the Pharaoh who knew, too.”

He picked up the oath scroll.

“The stonemasons who explored the Pyramid of Truth and built its replica—that’s who formed the ancient stonemason guild, the Freemasons. This is their confidentiality oath.”

Arti examined the emblem stamped at the top of the scroll.

“Whoa…”

Inside a triangle resembling the pyramid, a single eye gleamed with radiant light.

Her father continued.

“That’s the All-Seeing Eye. Also called the Eye of Providence. The eye of God that sees through all things. Your father’s father, and his father before him… if you trace it back far enough, we descend from the masons who built the pyramid.”

“So the incantation you taught me…”

“Yes. The ancient mason guild possessed mysterious powers. Most of it has been lost now, but who knows? Maybe somewhere, it still survives. All I know is what my grandfather taught me.”

Even that was enough to make the girl’s heart race.

“So that’s why we never left the village… even when everyone else changed.”

“That’s right. But…”

A flash of light lit up the house, and her father turned his head.

“Now it looks like we have to leave.”

Electricity surged around the pyramid like lightning striking the sky, and space itself ripped open.

Arti rushed to the window.

“...The All-Seeing Eye.”

One eye stared out over the world.

“Aaaaah!”

Inside the pyramid, now wrapped in crackling electricity, Lucia was flung back with a loud pop.

Her body twitched on the ground, her eyes trembling as if in shock.

“Lucia! Stay with us!”

What had she seen?

Her mind had already crossed into the realm of the infinite—and there was no returning by any means.

“Damn it!”

The group looked up toward the ceiling, where tendrils of energy surged upward from the machine.

The bricks soaked in electricity had turned transparent like glass, and above them hung the Eye of God.

“I’ve seen that before…”

Shirone turned to the group.

“I’ll go.”

Canis spoke up. “I’m going with you. I’ve got business with that thing.”

“No.”

Shirone was firm.

“I’m going alone. We don’t even know what’s up there—I can’t risk your lives too.”

“Don’t act so high and mighty. Sure, you’re strong, I admit that. But I have my own fight to fight.”

“Canis.”

Shirone spoke gently.

“If you go, Arin will insist on going too.”

“Leave it to me. If that eye really is God, maybe I can reason with it.”

He had realized something back at the Micah Ruins.

“Maybe it’s just a delusion, but I know it was trying to communicate something. About Goffin…”

“Shirone,” Canis said. “If it doesn’t listen to reason, grab it by the collar and drag it down.”

“Yeah.”

Shirone was grateful—more than anyone, he understood how Canis felt, and appreciated his willingness to yield.

“Phewww…”

The Outer World.

He had a vague idea of what to expect, but this would be his first time confronting it directly.

“I’ll be back.”

As Miracle Stream enveloped his body, he turned into particles of light like dandelion seeds and passed through the wall.

Watching Shirone’s body manifest outside the pyramid, Ares murmured,

“Yeah. You better come back.”

He couldn’t let his little sister become a widow before she even got married.

The members of Catacomb charged past the enemy before them, rushing toward Shirone.

“Detestable Yahweh!”

What Shirone intended was clear.

‘The purification of Melkidu.’

In other words, he was choosing to embrace the sins of everyone present.

Gyorgy wondered,

‘Is he serious?’

If he’d been capable of forgiveness from the start, he wouldn’t have vomited out the darkness like something repulsive.

“Hypocrite.”

Gyorgy’s voice rang out as he stepped forward.

“You really think you can do it?”

That utterly selfish, pleasure-seeking evil that shirks all responsibility onto others.

“Hrghhh…”

With the Immortal Function activated, Shirone took all of Melkidu into his heart.

It was a horrible feeling.

‘Evil is evil.’

So hateful, so loathsome, so repugnant.

‘It’s a contradiction.’

Shirone didn’t know Yorahān, but how could one’s dream of great forgiveness make any of this easier?

‘Sacrifice.’

Just like Lucia once did.

‘If you love, you can sacrifice. You can even give up your life. But why do I…’

…have to embrace even the evil?

The moment he faced that contradiction, his heart began to fill with murky rage.

“Die, Yahweh!”

Just as the members of Catacomb charged, lightning crashed down around Shirone.

“Judgment of the Thunder God.”

Nade, having summoned the Incarnation of the Thunder God, glared at them with a terrifying expression.

“Come any closer, and I’ll kill you.”

Eden approached with Iruki supporting her.

“Something’s wrong.”

Shirone’s Spirit Zone, which should have expanded infinitely, was instead collapsing inward.

Iruki said,

“He’s caught in a logical contradiction. He can’t swallow Melkidu whole. If it goes on like this, he’ll be consumed by evil.”

Shirone’s heart screamed.

‘Why?’

Was I really just a hypocrite?

‘I don’t know. They’re not doing anything. I’m always the one who has to sacrifice. I’m the one who carries the burden, who forgives—

Me, me, me, always me…’

Shirone’s thoughts came to a halt.

“…Ah.”

Havitz had once said:

—Go spend your life picking up trash alone, you idiot.

‘I see.’

The reason he had to forgive evil…

‘There’s only one.’

Because that is what good is.

“Hrk!”

His face twisted with tears, but his Spirit Zone began expanding rapidly once more.

“Aaaah! No! Don’t do it!”

Sensing the change in temperament, the demons of Catacomb dropped to their knees, clutching their heads.

‘Is he really…?’

Gyorgy didn’t believe it.

‘With a heart of perfect goodness, without a single trace of evil… he truly intends to forgive?’

The union of good and love.

‘Fine then.’

Shirone’s gaze fell on Gyorgy.

‘I don’t resent anyone. I’m not bitter. I do this because I can.’

With not a hint of malice left, Shirone was no hypocrite—he was true good.

So forget pretense.

‘Let’s truly…’

…forgive them.

Through an ultimate act of sacrifice, he embraced it all, and his Spirit Zone surged outward at tremendous speed.

Rumble…

As Melkidu’s system was purified, the island housing the Hall of Evil began to tremble.

Plink. Plink.

Fragments fell on Curtis and Pena’s heads.

The moment Shirone’s heart was transmitted through the Spirit Zone, they felt the weight of an immense forgiveness.

Pena asked,

“So… what happens to us now?”

“I don’t know.”

Curtis turned his head.

“It’s not something we get to choose.”

And in silence, the two shared a bitter smile and bumped fists.

In the Hall of Evil, Cain sat collapsed in Lilith’s room, quietly looking up at her portrait.

Rumble…

Even as the legacy of a lifetime was erased, his thoughts remained fixed on the past.

Silent tears flowed.

“Mother…”

He tried to smile, but only the corners of his mouth moved.

“I was wrong.”

The Hall of Evil collapsed.

“Hrk! Hrggh!”

As the light of purification grew stronger, members of Catacomb began to weep.

“Yahweh… Save us…”

Because not once—not a single human—had ever truly forgiven evil from the heart.

“Deliver us…”

In a wave of massive catharsis, the demons vanished, and Nade looked at the gradually fading Shirone.

“He’ll come back.”

“Of course. He’s the Infinite Mage.”

At that moment, the last remaining demon—Gyorgy—opened the Devil’s Bible.

“Revelation of Satan, Chapter 32.”

The final page.

“Satan spoke: Fear not the good. For all are hypocrites. Rise up the forces of evil.”

“That bastard, even now—!”

Nade stepped forward, but Eden stopped him.

“Wait.”

Gyorgy’s voice was calm.

“The hypocrites will persecute you. Prepare for the holy war. Claim victory by the ways of evil.”

Is that it, Yahweh?

“Be not persuaded. Persuade not. There is no mercy for those who abandoned us.”

Can we too become like you?

If all humanity achieved a perfect, unified moral consciousness…

“Eradicate the seeds of good.”

Then we too, rather than being wicked, vile, or repulsive—

“Erase the name of good.”

Could simply be human—capable of certain thoughts, of self-restraint. Sometimes joyful, sometimes the most wonderful and fulfilling part of life—

By feeling.

“Remember this, my children.” Could there be salvation?

“As long as even one seed of good remains in this world…”

Clack.

Closing the Devil’s Bible, Gyorgy calmly shut his eyes and awaited his end.

“There is no sanctuary for demons.”

And in a blinding flash—Melkidu vanished.

At the same time…

Shirone’s consciousness, which had been soaring toward Jin Seong-Eum of the Under World, suddenly spun.

“Ghhk!”

Caught in the state of Choeni Bardo, straddling the boundary between reality and the underworld, the emotional unification hit Shirone like a real shock.

It was only half the impact, of course—but even that was a force strong enough to make someone lose consciousness.

‘No… just a little further…’

With his eyes fixed on the Valley of Wailing at the edge of the horizon, Shirone collapsed to the ground.

The sky swarmed with Black Chains as they flew in.

—Now! Restrain Yahweh!

Countless chains shot out toward Shirone, and just as the black cloud began to engulf the earth—

Graaaargh!

With a blinding flash, the tangled mass of darkness split apart with a ripping sound.

“Phew!”

Rian appeared, cradling Shirone with his left arm and swinging his greatsword with the other.

“Hey, Shirone. What’s going on all of a sudden?”

There was no reply, but Shirone’s eyelids were trembling as if he were locked in battle with something unseen.

Yahweh’s mind was in the process of integrating good and evil—love and sorrow—but Rian couldn’t possibly understand that.

“…What the hell is happening?”

From deep within the Valley of Wailing, someone’s scream echoed out.
