Chapter 1179: The Law Shakes (1)

Shirone mulled over Curtis's words.

"It's a kind of system exploitation, isn't it?"

"Exactly. Some criminals despised Melkidu. Crime only holds value when it remains a crime. If anything you do is considered innocent, even psychopaths will eventually lose interest."

Curtis extinguished his cigarette.

"So, what would be the best way to make yourself stand out here? Attack the system itself. Some criminals deliberately got themselves imprisoned and searched for ways to escape. Melkidu has an ancient history, meaning all sorts of strategies have been attempted. And then, they discovered it—a hidden mission embedded in the system."

"Bribing the guards."

"Right. The method is surprisingly simple. There are certain areas in the inner track that have ‘backdoors’—shops run by the underworld. They aren’t visible on the surface, but if you pay the right price through those channels, you can bribe the guards."

Shirone and his group fell into deep thought.

"Why does such a method even exist?" Shirone wondered. "It could be a system bug, or it could be an intentional design by the original creator."

"I believe it’s the latter," Curtis said. "If a place calls itself a ‘Sanctuary of Murderers,’ then it must understand the nature of murderers well. The creators probably anticipated that someone would try something like this."

Penna spoke up.

"I don't know if I should be interrupting… but does bribing the guards get you into the Core?"

"That, I can't say. Even if you buy a key for 10 billion points, there's no guarantee it grants access to the Core. If someone knew for sure, it wouldn’t be an unsolved mystery."

"Data erasure, then," Shirone thought.

While he was lost in thought, Curtis continued.

"Once you establish ties with the underworld, most of the system’s restrictions can be bypassed. That means you don’t need to follow Melkidu’s rules to reach the Core. So, what do you think? Doesn't this plan sound viable?"

There was no room for denial.

"Does it have to involve bankruptcy?" Shirone asked.

"This is the information I gathered after extensive investigation. To activate the bribery program, you must break someone out of prison. It requires at least 50 million points. The inner track is home to an underworld faction closely tied to the guards. But as for my points… you don’t have to worry."

Curtis let out a weary sigh.

"I'm nearly bankrupt already."

Penna blinked at him in surprise.

"Seriously? But you were doing pretty well."

"How do you think I got hold of such high-level intel? I’ve been here for two years, at least in Melkidu’s time. But even after all that, 10 billion points is still a distant dream. Then, I came across a small piece of information, and my detective instincts told me, ‘This is it.’ So, I dug deeper."

"It’s a difficult strategy," Iruki commented.

"Most people here are criminals. You need at least two people or two teams to pull off the bribery, but trust is hard to build. Plus, one side has to go bankrupt."

Nade added, "And the mission difficulty is on a different level. This isn’t about dealing with non-player entities created by the system; you’re going up against the system itself. And if you don’t reach the inner track entrance, you’ll have to circle the outer track for at least another half lap. That’s a ridiculous amount of time."

Curtis nodded.

"That’s why I came to you guys. While gathering information, I heard about your progress. Your mission completion speed is insane. If it’s you, I believe you can handle the inner track missions."

Penna smirked. "And what do I do? You must have had a reason for calling me here too, right?"

When she was busy ordering food earlier, it seemed like she wasn’t thinking about anything at all.

"Hmm, when I go bankrupt, I’ll be thrown into prison. After that, I’ll be transferred elsewhere through the judicial process. The scale of my prison sentence will depend on the crimes I’ve committed. Your job is to track me and relay my whereabouts to them."

"So, I’m a messenger."

"Exactly. Meanwhile, you guys will handle the missions in the inner track. Oh, and if you have spare points, invest them in upgrading your dice. When dealing with the underworld, dice matter more than points."

"Does that mean we might have to fight?" Shirone asked.

"Oh, right. You guys haven’t been to the inner track yet, have you? In the outer track, points are king. But in the inner track, dice rule. And the underworld operates within the inner track. It wouldn’t hurt to be prepared."

"Got it. So, where do we go from here? Attacking other users is prohibited here."

"Melkidu is a haven for fugitives. No item can track a target. You have to rely solely on dice rolls. However, there are designated items that allow access to mission-free areas."

Curtis pulled out a card.

"This is a teleportation item—‘Royal Entry.’ It will take us to Parme, a city often referred to as the capital of Melkidu. It’s the largest and most bustling metropolis, with an active marketplace where users trade unwanted goods and items."

"Sounds like there will be a lot of people."

"Yeah. Honestly, a lot of people who give up on reaching the Core end up running businesses there. As long as you have Crime Points, you can commit any crime you want."

Iruki asked, "Is there an item that takes you back to Vital as well?"

"Of course. You think I just waited around hoping for a miracle? There’s a teleportation item called ‘Killer’s Resolve.’ It’s as expensive as Royal Entry, but I’ve already used mine. The issue is how we meet up again…"

"One in four."

Shirone decided to reveal an important piece of information.

"When I roll the dice, I can manipulate the outcome. That means I can land on a specific tile whenever I want."

Curtis and Penna froze.

"…What did you just say?"

There was no need for a long explanation.

"It’s a special ability I have. It’s free when used for gambling or rewards, but for movement, it costs Crime Points."

"Hmm, so it’s magic. How many points does it consume per use?"

"Three hundred thousand."

"What?"

Penna’s eyes widened in disbelief.

"That’s insane! Are you blowing up an entire town or something? The highest cost I’ve ever heard of was 100,000!"

"Well, it’s… a pretty complicated spell. But that’s not the important part."

For the first time, Penna showed a crack in her usual composure and quickly regained her expression.

"Hmm, yeah, I guess it’s not. I mean… it’s not like I’ve never heard of magic like that before."

Her voice was trembling.

Curtis, too, was shocked, though more than anything, he was overwhelmed by the sheer efficiency of the ability.

‘In Melkidu, dice determine everything. No item can manipulate dice rolls.’

Which meant… Shirone’s ability was system-breaking.

"I've made my decision."

Curtis spoke.

"This will significantly shorten our time. You guys don’t need to follow us. First, go to the inner track and upgrade your dice. With your abilities, you could probably complete all 468 sections of the outer track in one go—especially if you use items."

With the ability to maximize dice rolls and apply doubles, it was entirely possible.

Curtis turned to Penna.

"In the meantime, you and I will go to Parme. Once I’m transferred to prison, track me with the dice. You’ll be the one searching for the person who takes bribes in the prison, and after that, meet up with these guys."

"But where should we meet? I only have one ‘Royal Entry’ card. Once I leave, that’s it."

"Anywhere that allows a rendezvous is fine. We might as well just meet back here in Vital."

"Hah! Like I'd ever buy something as ridiculous as 'Killer’s Resolve.' I got here using dice. Honestly, I felt lucky, so I took a gamble on it."

She didn’t want to dwell on it too much.

"But you must have other movement cards, right? Someone as meticulous as you wouldn't leave without a backup plan."

"I do, but..."

She pulled out a card with a grim expression.

"This is it—'Loss of Will.’"

Even without reading the description, the dark, despair-filled background featuring figures screaming in agony spoke volumes.

"Fantastic," Curtis muttered.

He explained, "Loss of Will is a literal garbage dump. The most rundown… no, it’s just a landfill."

"I see," Shirone replied.

"It’s only useful for emergency escapes, nothing more. Though, admittedly, that's a huge advantage. The problem is, you can’t leave on your own. You have to roll doubles with the dice once a day to escape."

Penna added, "Do you know why it’s called the garbage dump? Because the people there are trash. Spend just one day there, and you’ll go insane. If you guys ever come looking for me, you might just find me sitting at a restaurant table."

Iruki asked, "Then why did you even buy that item?"

"Because it was cheap."

"Are Crime Points a joke to you? For the price of one ‘Killer’s Resolve,’ I could buy three ‘Loss of Will’ cards!"

Curtis interrupted before the conversation could escalate.

"Let’s set aside unsolvable problems and finalize everyone's movements."

With that, they updated their map, noting the location of the capital.

Penna sighed, slumping her shoulders.

"Ugh…"

"Don’t be too down about it. I’m the one going to prison. Compared to that, the landfill is practically paradise."

"Yeah, yeah. I'll be sure to lock you up tight."

Shirone spoke up.

"Miss Penna, if there’s anything you need, let us know. We’ll get it from the shop. We’re working together now, after all."

"Heh. Why are all of Curtis’s acquaintances like this? Do you know what I hate most? Sincere pity. I can get my own gear, thank you very much."

That was unexpected.

"But how did you even end up in Melkidu, Miss Penna? It only reacts to the strongest yin energy… What kind of fraud did you commit to end up here…?"

"Fraud? Ha! Nonsense. If I have a single crime, it's that I was born too beautiful."

Shirone’s group looked bewildered as she propped her chin on the table.

"I just fell in love. I talked to someone because they talked to me. I accepted gifts because they bought them for me. It was nice while it lasted. I thought, ‘Wow, even someone like me can experience love.’ But then, turns out…"

She chuckled bitterly.

"Every single one of those ‘good men’ was either married or already taken."

The pieces started coming together.

"Their wives came after me, dragged me by the hair, threatened me, and in the end, sued me. Of all the possible charges, they always went with fraud. As if calling me a con artist would make our love a lie."

Penna lifted her chin.

"I didn’t even bother to appeal. I didn’t have the money, and the sentence wasn’t that long anyway. Honestly, the scariest part wasn’t going to prison—it was how suddenly those men changed. Had they always been so cold?"

Curtis remained silent.

"Then I got caught in something big. Turns out, one of them was someone high up. Forget a lawsuit—he just straight-up tried to have me killed. I barely managed to escape from the guys with knives, and somehow, I ended up here."

Penna packed up some food.

"But I like Melkidu. Everyone's suspicious of each other, so there’s no betrayal. Plus, since everyone’s already a bad person, I actually feel like an ordinary person here. Hehe!"

Shirone could only respond with one thing.

"We’ll come as soon as we can."

"Yeah, we need to get out of Melkidu anyway. But don’t worry, I’m good at dealing with people."

Leaving the restaurant, they gathered at an isolated part of Vital’s entrance.

Curtis pulled out his card.

"You’ll need to save at least 50 million points. I’ll send updates through Penna."

As Curtis and Penna departed, Shirone spoke.

"Let’s get moving too. First, we enter the inner track and upgrade our dice."

"Right. We worked like dogs to earn it, now let’s spend it like dogs. So, is it my turn now?"

Nade rolled the Crime Dice in his palm and hurled them into the air.

A 4 and a 4—double.

"Yes! Did you see that? My ski—aaahhh—!"

Their bodies vanished at the speed of light.
