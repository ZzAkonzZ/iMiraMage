Chapter 731:

Royal Capital Aganos.

A steward of the 3.8th floor, where the grand mage resided, followed Kando into Uorin’s chamber.

“Your Majesty, all of Shirone’s debts have been paid off.”

When no response came, the steward added cautiously, “Not a single debt remains.”

It was Uorin’s directive. ‘But it might not have been Terraze’s decision,’ thought Kando.

Under normal circumstances, it would have been proper to leave a one-second gap in Shirone’s debt to seize a critical moment. In return, it wouldn’t have been a waste to keep an outstanding grand mage under control indefinitely.

‘What has changed Her Majesty’s mind?’

Uorin spoke in a soft tone, “I see. There will be a reward soon.”

The steward frantically waved his hands in refusal. “No, Your Majesty, I didn’t do it expecting a reward…”

“Take it. You’ve worked hard.”

“I am deeply honored by your grace.”

No matter how loyal the steward was, it was impossible for his heart not to race at the prospect of receiving a reward from Uorin, known for her generosity.

“Your Majesty, may I ask why you issued such an order?”

Once the steward had left, Kando posed the question. Even an empress of the empire might fall in love, but allowing emotions to cloud judgment didn’t suit Terraze’s image.

“Nothing has changed. I’m merely resetting the board.”

Kando still didn’t know. He didn’t know the pain Uorin endured while waiting for this day after Shirone accepted the offer of the Wudeng Dragon Karatorza.

‘This is the world Shirone lives in.’ At last, they could meet.

“Kando, the world is about to change. Everything we once deemed insignificant will come crashing down on us.”

What truly mattered was…

Finally turning, Uorin smiled warmly, a sight Kando had never seen before. “This marks the true beginning of the Great Purification.”

Ymir’s punch tore through the air with a trembling force.

‘What’s going on?’

Even though he unleashed sim-quan at the speed of thought, the opponent’s body remained intact, without a single crack.

‘Sim-ta…?’

Still, the mental blow from his actions not aligning with their expected outcome was significant.

‘An angel’s power?’

According to his memory, the magic circle the mage had cast earlier was Ica’s Valhalla Action.

Clutching his chest and rolling on the ground, Shirone grimaced. “Ugh!”

Even after drawing upon the collective time of 19,000 individuals to surpass the supreme boundary, the shock his body endured was beyond imagination.

‘So, this is Ymir…’

He had faced countless powerful foes before, but never had he encountered someone capable of twisting space and time with sheer physical strength.

“Your Highness! Are you all right?”

As the etheric wave dissipated and the space returned to normal, Munkyung rushed to check on Seongeum.

“Your Highness…!”

What he saw was Shirone lying atop Seongeum.

‘H-How…?’

While Munkyung stood frozen in disbelief, Seongeum’s eyelids fluttered open. “Ugh…”

“You’re awake? You were seconds away from disaster.”

No one understood the dangers of fully opening the Immortal Function better than Shirone.

“Huh?”

Before Seongeum could process her memories, she blinked at the surreal sight before her.

Shirone’s face filled her entire vision. ‘That’s strange. Why is he so close?’

The moment she realized that Shirone’s weight was pressing against her in an awkward position, her mind went blank.

“W-What… W-Whaaaat…?!”

For the first time, Munkyung witnessed Seongeum stuttering so severely. “H-How dare you! Y-You touched my body…!”

Before she could finish speaking, Shirone picked her up and teleported away, increasing the distance from Ymir. “Get a grip. It’s not over yet.”

In the brief moment during teleportation, Seongeum recalled Ymir’s presence and pushed Shirone away as she stood up. “Hmph, I simply underestimated the enemy.”

Her judgment was fitting for an Ivory Tower candidate, though her flushed face betrayed her.

‘What a disgrace…’

She had never allowed anyone’s touch before, and the sensation of Shirone’s weight lingered vividly. ‘To accept help from a rival…’

But she couldn’t repay the person who saved her life with ingratitude, leaving her deeply frustrated.

“Such absurd creatures.”

Ymir’s overwhelming presence snapped Seongeum out of her thoughts.

Hunched over like a beast ready to pounce, Ymir radiated an aura that surpassed the limits of any living being.

Shirone, keeping his eyes on Ymir, warned, “Be careful. If we don’t stop him here…”

“I know,” Seongeum replied, her gaze regaining its usual sharpness. “I’ll take responsibility. I won’t let that monster loose upon the world.”

Though merely a tooth in the grand scheme, Ymir’s strength was enough to destroy an entire city in an instant.

“You don’t seem to be the ones who awakened me…”

When it came to battle, Ymir’s animalistic intuition was rarely wrong.

“Sim-ta, is it?”

The moment Ymir’s eyes widened, Shirone and Seongeum felt their entire bodies being compressed.

‘Such immense pressure…!’

It was a technique on a completely different level from anything they had experienced before.

“Die.”

Just as the ground trembled and collapsed with a deafening roar, Ymir’s body vanished.

‘He’s coming!’

The speed was too great to avoid by ordinary reflexes, and the Valhalla Action magic circle appeared above Shirone’s head.

‘What?’

Though the computation to reverse causality had completed, an overload caused his Spirit Zone to waver.

‘No! If this breaks…!’

The accumulated experiences of 19,000 Shirones would be erased in vain.

“Shirone!”

Before Rian’s voice could reach him, Ymir changed direction at terrifying speed.

‘This one!’

For the first time in a long while, Ymir’s combat instincts were fully awakened, and he felt every cell in his body light up.

‘A swordsman?’

As their eyes met, Rian thought, ‘I’ve been cut.’

The fact that his mind registered being cut before any physical action occurred meant that the swordsman before him wielded a similar technique to his own.

“Hrrgh!”

Ymir raised his arm to block the greatsword, and veins bulged on either side of Rian’s temple.

‘Damn! He blocked it!’

It was his first experience with the shock of Sim-ta.

“Who are you?”

Though Ymir found Rian’s presence familiar, he couldn’t place him in his memory.

“Ymir?”

Just as Rian realized who he was facing, Ymir delivered a powerful kick to his abdomen.

"Annoying."

With a cracking sound, Rian's elbow broke, and his body flew like a cannonball.

"Rian!"

Kido arrived just in time to catch him but couldn't withstand the impact, rolling across the ground.

"Aaah!"

Barely regaining his balance, Rian knelt and glared at Ymir. His arms dangled from the handle of the greatsword embedded in the giant's arm.

"Ymir."

Memories of countless times striking down Ymir since their encounter in heaven flashed through his mind.

"Hmm, a Mind Sword. Even so..."

Ymir pulled out the greatsword and dropped Rian’s arms, then raised vertically.

"There aren't many swords that can cut my muscles."

Holding the sword horizontally, Ymir gripped the handle and blade ends, exerting force as if to bend it.

"Phew!"

As unimaginable strength was applied, space around Ymir began to ripple.

"Here I go!"

Finally, air was sucked in and exploded, shaking Andre.

Boom!

Mun Kyung turned his head back towards Ymir and paled at the sight.

'That can't be.'

Despite the strength capable of killing a person with mere wind pressure, showed no signs of deformation.

"Kekeke."

Nodding, Ymir looked back at Rian.

"Now I understand why I woke up."

The greatsword was likely an object. But only one person could imagine an indestructible concept according to Ymir's knowledge.

"You, what's your relationship with Ogent?"

Bending his broken arms and standing up, Rian replied,

"I am Rian Ogent."

"Huh? Ogent?"

Raising an eyebrow, Ymir saw no nostalgia for Ogent in Rian's rugged masculine appearance.

"No memory but..."

Unlike other parts, Ymir's molar had been independently buried in ruins long ago.

"Well, we'll find out through a clash then."

That was Ymir's way. Gripping the greatsword like a spear, he threw it with immense power.

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

Watching his sword pierce the wall and disappear, Rian's expression twisted.

Dusting off his hands, Ymir said,

"It's not a sword one can wield by mere imitation."

Without strength comparable to the King of Giants, one couldn't realize the true terror of .

"First, let's deal with nuisances."

As Ymir turned around, Mun Kyung guarding Sung Eum drew his sword and shouted,

"Shut up! Who said you could..."

His words were cut off as both Shirone and Sung Eum looked over Ymir's shoulder in shock.

Crack! Crack!

Feeling eerie sounds of something breaking, Ymir slowly turned his head.

"R-Rian..."

Kido, who had fallen on his butt, trembled as he looked up at a goblin that resembled Rian.

"I've been waiting for this day, Ymir."

At the tip of the sword—there was always Ymir.

"Yeah? I don't even know who you are."

Rian’s back burst open with an explosive sound, and the torn muscle fibers fluttered like shimmering heatwaves.

"Don’t take a single step from there. Come to me. If you don’t... you’ll regret it."

Rian’s incarnation technique was powerful even on an ordinary day...

"Interesting. I’m curious to see what happens next."

But his current opponent was Ymir, the king of giants—reputed to be the most powerful creature in existence.

"Grrrrrhh!"

Rian’s eyes rolled back, and the fluttering muscle fibers twisted rapidly, pulling inward into his body.

The muscle formation, unlike anything human, crafted solely for combat—it resembled a goblin’s grotesque form.

Divine Transcendence – Rebirth.

"A cheap imitation...!"

As Rian surged forward, shattering the ground beneath him, Ymir twisted his upper body with terrifying momentum.

On the see-saw of time suspended in a horizontal balance, their bodies moved so slowly it felt as though they might never reach each other.

"YAAAHHH!"

At the speed of thought, fists were exchanged.

'Mind Fist! Mind Strike! Mind Fist! Mind Strike! Mind Fist! Mind Fist! Mind Strike! Mind Fist!'

‘Mind Fist! Mind Strike! Mind Fist! Mind Strike! Mind Fist! Mind Fist! Mind Strike! Mind Fist!'

The sound of meat being shredded exploded in rapid succession, shaking the air around Andre, and Moonkyung shivered.

'What the hell is that...?'

Just hearing the sound conveyed the sensation of pain.

'What kind of freak is he?'

For the first time, Ymir felt curiosity stir.

'Ogent Rian, huh? Ogent... wait.'

Amid the flurry of punches, Ymir locked eyes on Rian’s face, and a sharp realization hit.

'Blue hair.'

He wasn’t an Ogent.

'I see.'

So that’s how it was connected.

'Smile!'

Fixating on Rian’s rapidly extending Heart Fist, Ymir burst out with joyous emotion.

"It’s been a while, Ogent!"

CRACK — their jaws collided simultaneously, and those watching involuntarily shut their eyes tight.

"Guh!"

A molar flew from Ymir’s mouth, shooting through the maze with terrifying force.

BOOOOM!

The sight of the cave wall being completely blown open made Moonkyung gasp in horror and snap his gaze back to Ymir.

"Ptoo!"

Spitting on the ground and wiping his lips, Ymir muttered,

"...That’s the second time."
