The central city of Sadia is the region where most of Thormia’s local nobles reside.

As a famous granary, its finances were solid, and while external forces were few, internal competition for territory was fierce, causing the hierarchy to change frequently. The West Family, to which Nade belonged, was no exception.

During the break, Nade practically lived in his workshop. His eyes were hollow after staying up for two nights straight.

The workshop looked like an extension of the Supernatural Psychic Science Research Group. Tools of unknown use rolled on the floor, and engine parts of some mechanism lay on the table.

“Phew, should I call it a day?”

Though the season had turned to autumn, the workshop was stuffy. When he took off his shirt, scars crisscrossing his back were revealed. It was an old wound, but sometimes it stung so much that it’s unbearabale.

Nade pulled a tarp over the table. If his family found out, there would be an uproar, saying he was spending money on something useless.

Though the family had fallen, just three generations ago, half of the Sadia granary belonged to the West Family.

Nade never learned the reason for their decline. However, after living for 17 years, he felt there wasn’t any special reason. They had simply spent it all.

“Nade, nade.”

Returning to the mansion, he heard a voice that made his heart stop.

It's ironic that the voice's owner is his mother, but to Nade, she was the scariest being in the world.

“Yes, Mother.”

“You said you were good with machines, right? Go to the Jones Family and fix something for them.”

“Let me just take a quick nap and then I’ll go.”

His mother’s expression twisted horribly like a monster's. It was like a lock that made it impossible for Nade to move.

When he was young, he even talked back to his mother. But she instead do self-harm, he became the world's greatest delinquent.

You can’t win against a mother who harms herself. No child can win against their parents.

“I’m just tired. I haven’t slept in two days.”

“What do you evendo? You don’t go out and earn money, nor do you help around the house. I didn’t tell you not to sleep, so why can’t you go?”

Nade clutched at his distorted facial muscles. He didn't want to end up with the same face as his mother.

“Do you not see how hard your mother working? Do you know how much it costs to send my son to that prestigious magic academy? If you’re going to be like this, just disappear from this house. I don’t think this mother can support you anymore.”

Neid tried to remember when she had ever supported him. But there were no such memories. Only the excruciating scars on his back ached.

No one in West Family ever tried to start even a little business with their wealth. They did nothing because they never knew how to do anything.

“I’m sorry, Mom. I understand. I’ll go.”

The burning pain from Nade's scars was radiating. But he couldn't show it. The family couldn’t defy her. No, they avoided her because they didn’t want to deal with her.

Then, on days when she commits self-harm, all the family's frustration is poured out on Nade.

When he was young, he didn't understand why it had to be him. But as he grew old, he realized that there is no such thing as a reason.

Nade's family put blame on everything to others and they knew he was the only one who couldn’t do the same. (네이드의 가족은 모든 걸 남에게 미루었고 유일하게 자신만이 그래서는 안 된다는 걸 알고 있을 뿐이었다.)

“Hurry up and go before sunset. The Jones family is looking for someone to check their boiler. Why don’t you consider becoming a repairman?”

“I think I can graduate soon. Please let me finish the academy first.”

“That damn school is a parasite that just sucks money without teaching anything. I might as well just die.”

Nade wanted to understand her mother.

Marrying into a wealthy family only to see her family squander money would naturally cause her great distress.

But no matter how much he thought about it, he couldn't find a reason why he should become a boiler repairman for them.

As his mother returned to the mansion, hot breath escaped from between Nade's teeth.

He felt like murderous intent was seeping into each cell of his body. Just a little more... if it built just a little more, and he would explode.

“Young master, there’s a letter... Eek!”

The butler who had been running towards him turned pale. Only then did Nade come to his senses and shake his head. The second wave of pain hit him as he realized that his mother’s face was embedded within him.

“Ah, I haven’t slept. What’s the matter?”

“A- A letter … It’s from Arian Shirone.”

“What? Shirone?”

Nade's face brightened immediately. Snatching the letter, he began to read Shirone’s handwriting.

The moment he saw the phrase “I miss you,” his heart swelled with sadness and tears welled up in his eyes.

“Just wait a bit, Shirone! I’ll be there soon!”

Nade clutched the letter and left the mansion. The Jones Family wouldn’t need a boiler repairman for the next ten years.

The main house of Seriel’s family, Fortress, was in Creas City, just like Amy’s.

Although their noble rank was not very high, they were a prestigious family known for producing many great doctors, and they were well recognized in the kingdom.

At school, she is Amy's best friend and a model student, but at home, she is a wild and unruly brat who puts her younger brother, who is an aspiring doctor, take the front while she stayed in the background.

Today, she was hiding for the third day to avoid her mother's nagging. Her younger brother's room, who was expected to become a great doctor in the future, was a nagging-free area where her family didn’t go.

“Uwaah! What do I do? It’s so moving.”

Seriel, who was reading a romance novel under the covers, couldn’t hold back her tears any longer and started crying.

Her younger brother, Vale, who was sitting at his desk, was about to lose his mind.

“Ugh, be quiet! I’m trying to study! Go back to your room! Or at least stop crying so loudly.”

Hugging the novel that ended in tragedy, Seriel savored the lingering emotions. If she were to rate it herself, it was one of the best stories she read during vacation.

“It was such a sad love story. Oh, where can I find a man like Joseph?”

Vale paused his writing and looked up.

"There is."

“Really? Who is it?”

“Joseph.”

“You’re asking for a beating! Are you teasing your sister?”

“Why not? If there’s no one like him in reality, you might as well live with a fictional character, kekek.”

Seriel glared at her brother’s back, her lips twitching. She hadn't gotten angry because she had been indebted to him for a few days, but now he was trying to climb all the way to the top of her head.

Just then, as the door opened, a letter fell out with a thud, along with his mother's nagging.

“You’ve got a letter! I won’t nag you to study, so come out and stop bothering your brother. If you’re going to play, at least take a bath. How are you going to get married like this? Tsk, tsk.”

Pouting, Seriel crawled under the bed to retrieve the letter.

She thought it was Amy, but the sender was an unexpected person.

“Huh? It’s Shirone! Shirone!”

Seriel quickly opened the envelope and read the letter. Her eyes instantly filled with affection. She smelled the scent of the paper and held the letter in her arms, murmuring.

“Ah, where can I find a man like Shirone?”

Vale's eyes, which had been diligently reading a book, became wide open.

‘Shirone is a real person, you idiot. Just go date him.’

These words were on the tip of his tongue, but he knew the woman wouldn't listen to him. She had been so engrossed in novels for days that she was clearly not in her right mind.

Creas City Municipal Training Ground.

Mark paid the expensive entrance fee and entered the training ground. He was already excited about being assigned to Class Six next semester, having succeeded in advancing early with Maria.

‘Finally, I’ll have junior students in the advanced class.’

If he hadn’t met Shirone, this day wouldn’t have come. After losing at the Uncrossable Bridge, he took Shirone as his role model and worked hard to achieve his goals.

“Hey, Maria.”

“You’re here? You’re earlier than yesterday.”

Mark laughed when Maria, who had been waiting at the training grounds, made a playful comment. She had been so depressed that she had endangered the students by operating the machinery at the Bridge of No Return, but her personality had brightened considerably after becoming Sade’s disciple.

“Sorry. My father noticed. He asked why I’m going out to train when there are good teachers at the Magic Association.”

“That’s right. The Slider Family is deeply involved with Magic Association. Are you sure you’re okay with continuing this?”

“No problem. I’m a man of my word.”

Mark winked and gave a thumbs up. In fact, he had barely escaped after starving until morning. At some point, the fifteen-year-old boy began to see his nineteen-year-old sister as a woman.

'I'm crazy, really crazy. To think I’m bullying such a cute sister.’

“Hey! Have you been waiting long? Sorry, I’m late.”

Mark's expression turned sour as Sade ran toward him at the entrance of the training ground. It was none other than his greatest rival. Though it was ridiculous for a teacher and student to compete over a woman, Sade, at twenty-six, and Maria, at nineteen, were a suitable match, and Saad didn’t seem to mind it either.

Maria greeted Sade with a more affectionate smile than anyone else, whether she knew Mark's feelings or not.

“No, we just got here.”

“Yeah, I was seeing off the principal, so I’m a bit late.”

“Oh, where did the principal go?”

“Well, there are some matters. Anyway, sorry to keep you waiting.”

Mark felt uncomfortable being left out of the conversation. He had experienced Sade's personality of being a womanizer enough, but he was particularly affectionate towards Maria, which irked him.

At that moment, Maria clapped her hands and took out a letter from her bag.

“Oh, right! Have you seen this? There’s a letter from Senior Shirone.”

“Huh? Senior Shirone? I didn’t get one.”

“You probably did. I received mine on the way out. I haven’t read it yet.”

Maria proudly waved the envelope and then opened the letter to read it. Her expression softened as she read the affectionate content.

“Ah, he’s such a wonderful person. Strong yet kind. How lucky is Senior Amy to be dating Senior Shirone?”

There was a strange tension on Sade's face.

To be honest, Sade was about seventy percent sure that Maria was completely smitten with him. But hearing this made him think it might just be admiration.

Seeing Sade’s gloominess, Mark felt triumphant.

Most of the female juniors talked about Shirone like Maria, so he didn’t feel jealous of Shirone.

Above all, isn't there an insurmountable barrier standing by Shirone's side named Amy?

‘Great! My goal to follow in Senior Shirone’s footsteps has become even stronger!’

The anxious Sade has stepped in to make amends.

“Haha! Yes, Shirone is impressive. But don’t most people prefer older partners? If not, at least the same age. Younger men are no fun, right?”

Mark took the blow and countered.

“Where did that come from? They say love knows no borders, so what does age have to do with it?”

“Age is more important than borders. Should we ask Maria? Which would you choose, an older or younger partner?”

Maria propped her chin and thought for a moment, then playfully stuck out her tongue and evaded the question.

“Huhu, who know?”

Mark and Sade, who had high expectation, were disappointed. Perhaps she was the true master of push and pull.

Regardless, they couldn’t just chat after paying such an expensive entrance fee. Mark and Maria began their serious training by warming up their minds with Sequencing. Meanwhile, Sade looked up at the sky and thought of Shirone.

‘The break is almost over. What kind of trouble will he cause when he returns?’

The path that Shirone has taken since entering the magic academy has clearly been unique compared to the history of the academy.

But that's why he was anxious.

It depends on what kind of answer the principal gives, but if he approves, it will be completely different from Shirone’s academy life up to now.

'It won't be easy this time, Shirone.'
