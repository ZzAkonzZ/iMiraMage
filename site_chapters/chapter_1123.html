Chapter 1123: Identifying Friend or Foe (3)

After lunch, Shirone walked through the corridors of Delta's main building, lost in thought.

'I'm a bit uneasy.'

It wasn't that he doubted the abilities of Ikael and Ashur, but the future of humanity was at stake.

'I have no choice but to trust them. Now I need to focus on strategizing with Tormia.'

As he hoped for Wizard's arrival, Satiel approached from the end of the corridor.

"Shirone."

Their relationship was strained.

"Where is Ikael?"

Shirone remained silent, but Satiel had expected no answer.

"You know, I have unresolved issues with Ikael. If you tell me where she is, I won't harm you. I might even help."

"Why?"

Shirone asked.

"Is it because you still have some affection for me? Or because you already killed me once?"

Satiel's eyes hardened.

"Say what you want. I don't regret my decision. I'd do the same again."

"I know, you had no choice." Her expression didn't change, but her aura wavered.

"The only archangel who sided with the Gaians. When Goffin chose Ikael, your sense of betrayal…"

"What are you trying to say?"

"Because it was irreversible, one mistake led to total ruin. But now it can be undone. I'm alive now."

"It can't be undone. Even so, it doesn't change the fact that I drove you to death."

"I don't blame you."

Satiel bit her lip.

"Can't we start over? The death of a child must have been a great pain for Ikael, but I'm here now. We can fix this."

The air vibrated.

Satiel's vibration was a reverent, supernatural sound wave.

'Undo it?' It would be a lie to say Shirone's words didn't shake her, but it was too late.

'The way back is just as far.' She was tired, and now what moved her was a will hardened into a mission, even her anger petrified.

'Goffin.'

If it could be undone.

'Even so, Goffin won't return. Ikael took everything from me.'

Shirone called out firmly.

"Satiel."

His voice resonated like thunder, and Satiel frowned.

"What do you want…"

At that moment, two-horned Mara, Gallio appeared beside Satiel.

"I found Ikael!"

His eyes, possessing extreme disintegration vision, swirled like a vortex.

"You found her?"

Even as Satiel turned to him in haste, Shirone couldn't understand the situation.

'How?'

Gallio locating her meant Ashur's signal was broken.

"Ikael!"

Satiel's expression filled with malice again, and without waiting, she broke the window and flew out.

'Simultaneous event…'

Shirone, who intended to use Amy's perception to go there, bit his lip.

"Ugh!"

Due to the nature of not being able to exist in the same space, the signals were colliding.

"Blitz!"

As he shouted, Thunder Dragon Blitz appeared, kneeling with a thunderclap.

"You called, Messiah."

"Right now…"

As Shirone was about to give instructions, he saw hundreds of flashes rising outside the window.

They were ordinary angels.

"Follow me."

As Shirone leaped out the window, twelve apostles followed.

Aruta and Amy didn't move, but they had already exchanged many moves.

'He's coming.'

The imaginary Aruta threw a punch, and Amy twisted her body to counterattack.

'Failed.'

Amy's head exploded.

In thousands of simulations, she had never succeeded in counterattacking.

'I can't win.'

Uriel's Second Mara was naturally not weak, but his strike surpassed the category of Mara.

'I'll die.'

She hurriedly grasped her faltering energy.

Aruta couldn't attack because Amy still had the potential to counterattack.

'The moment I waver, it's over.'

Only the tight balance, with no margin for error, extended her life.

'There's no way to avoid his strike. The energy is so strong it feels certain.'

Cold sweat dripped.

"Huuu."

Even as she barely held back tears, time passed.

In her weakening energy, Aruta found an opening.

'There it is.'

His insight, based on a lifetime of understanding martial arts, guided his body.

'This is the end.'

As Aruta kicked off the ground and charged, Amy felt her consciousness fade.

"Ah…"

Her mind went blank, and the unavoidable strike approached.

With no thoughts, even if she died, she couldn't think of the word death.

'Therefore…'

She was no different from being dead, and Aruta's strike carried that meaning.

At the boundary where the world returned to nothingness, Amy heard a voice that wasn't hers.

Mahaprajna.

When she first opened the realm of fire and lost consciousness, Nane had chanted sutras beside her.

Her consciousness opened, and her red eyes ignited.

"Immortal Function."

In her state of forgetting even her own existence, she had become pure fire.

Aruta's fist was inches from her face, but the distance no longer mattered.

"Ignition."

The crimson flames coiled around Aruta's arm, snaking into his mouth and incinerating his insides.

"Graaahhh!"

Before he could even register the pain, his body evaporated, leaving a massive vortex of fire in his place.

"Aaaaahhhh!"

Amy wept with overwhelming emotion.

“So this is what it was.”

She finally understood Shirone's heart, the meaning he had tried to convey.

“Burn it all.”

The flames, which scorched the vast radius to ashes, exploded outward before disappearing entirely.

At the center of the destruction stood a beautiful figure, her entire body transformed into fire, with her eyes gently closed.

Realizing Aruta's obliteration, Uriel slowly lowered the foot he had aimed at Ikael.

"Stand up."

He hadn’t intended to kill her, so Ikael carefully rose to her feet.

"I thought I had thought about this as much as anyone possibly could… but it seems I was mistaken."

"Uriel, that’s what it means to have a heart," Ikael said.

"Thoughts are just thoughts. All angels think according to the laws of the universe. But you didn’t follow Satiel."

That was why Ikael had placed her hopes in him.

"Because you have a heart. You have the will to change things. Isn’t that why you came to me?"

"Yes. To destroy you."

"That anger is part of your heart as well. But you’re hesitating, aren’t you? That hesitation means you haven’t fully committed."

Goffin had taught her.

"When you can throw everything away, that’s when you’ll find your path. Until then—"

A deafening voice echoed from the sky.

"Ikael!"

Leading a host of angels, Satiel descended at terrifying speed, her voice full of wrath.

"Die!"

As soon as she landed, she lunged forward, her fist raised high. Uriel drew his Paradise Staff.

"What—"

Caught off guard, Satiel attempted to brake, but her fist smashed into the staff.

The impact unleashed a massive shockwave that swept across the land.

Satiel glared furiously.

"What are you doing?"

"I was speaking with Ikael," Uriel replied calmly.

"And what were you thinking? I am the archangel now. If you do not obey my command, you too will be considered a traitor."

Uriel glanced skyward.

Hundreds of angels hovered, activating their Judgement Halos, ready to bombard the ground.

Looking back at Satiel, he said simply, "Do as you please."

"You—!"

As Satiel unleashed her Judgement Halo, Uriel's holy aura spread, forming rings of light.

Between the two archangels, light spiraled, triggering another massive shockwave.

Satiel was hurled back, and Uriel swung his Paradise Staff, casting Ragnarok.

"Is this what it takes to satisfy you?"

Even as lightning crackled and exploded, Satiel didn’t attempt to block. Instead, she issued a command:

"Kill Ikael!"

BOOOOM!

White lightning erupted above Satiel’s head, and hundreds of angels descended to bombard the ground.

The battle of the angels began.

Their clashes, far more advanced than anything humans or Mara could comprehend, shook the earth with each collision of abstract concepts.

Soaring through the sky at incredible speed, the Twelve Apostles called out to the one leading them.

"Messiah!"

"Understood," Shirone replied, his brow furrowing as he assessed the situation.

The angels' battlefield resembled a disaster one might expect to see in the depths of space.

"I’ll take care of this. Confirm Wizard’s safety first. And if possible… check on Ikael and Amy as well."

"Understood."

Blitz, acknowledging Shirone's orders, bowed and led the remaining Apostles as they scattered in all directions.

Meanwhile, Ikael was surrounded by hundreds of angels, fending off their relentless attacks.

"Respected Ikael."

Though she was no longer the archangel, the fact that Ikael was the origin of all angels hadn’t changed.

"Why did you abandon us?"

Ceriel, a form angel, used an infinite hexagonal construct to bind Ikael’s feet.

The binding force, infused with the concept of confinement, was beyond any physical strength.

"Break."

But as Ikael unleashed her Ataraxia, her body surpassed any limitation imposed by such concepts.

Breaking the shackles, she stepped forward, extending a slender arm. Her fist halted in front of Ceriel, but angels swarmed from all sides.

"Strike," Ceriel commanded.

Seeing Ceriel’s holy aura, prepared for annihilation, Ikael felt her heart ache.

“Angels who have lost their origin.”

In their hollow, empty gazes as they rushed toward her, she saw only despair.

“Because of me.”

For a brief moment, she hesitated.

“No, I can’t allow this. This is what I’ve resolved to do. I fight for Shirone.”

Her thoughts flashed, but the angels' attacks came even faster, pressing in around her.

Shirone dashed forward at near-light speed.

"Ikael!"

In the frozen landscape, as if time itself had stopped, a single spark ignited.

“What is this?”

At the same time as realization dawned, Amy arrived in front of Ikael, her Crimson Eye blazing bright.

“Ruby Flame.”

A massive sphere of fire, as radiant as the sun, engulfed the area around the angels.

"Guhhh!"

Shielding his face with the Hand of God, Shirone flinched at the overwhelming heat.

"Amy?"

The flames undeniably took Amy’s form, but their level of power was far beyond anything he had ever seen.

“This isn’t a physical fire. This is an idea.”

Manipulating the very concept of fire, she had seemingly mastered an advanced form of incarnation.

As the sphere of fire dissipated, Amy, back in her human form, turned to Ikael.

"Are you okay?"

Ikael didn’t respond, staring blankly ahead.

"Grrrr…"

Dozens of angels, caught in the flames, sat collapsed on the ground, their wings of light melted away.
