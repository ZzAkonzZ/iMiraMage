Chapter 801: Law Killer (2)

The imperial palace of Gustaf, Marsack, was bustling with women who had come to become imperial maids.

“How can this be…”

Despite Havitz’s notorious reputation spreading worldwide, over 300 people had applied.

“Do you know what kind of person His Majesty the Emperor is?”

A sharp-featured, cold-looking woman waiting in the left row was asked.

“Hmph, you’re the same. I’d prefer we keep quiet about it. From now on, we're competitors.”

“I’m not…”

Unlike the other candidates, Abella had come to kill Havitz.

“Is it for the money?”

The woman looked back coldly.

“What else? Regardless of background, the pay is unbelievable, and the employee benefits are excellent. Above all, you become a maid at the imperial palace.”

A commoner’s worth is determined by the nobility, and being a maid at the palace was a position even the nobles couldn’t look down upon.

“But he’s a tyrant, isn’t he?”

Everyone turned to look at Abella, and the atmosphere at the scene froze like the Arctic.

“Hey, what did you just say?” The captain of the guard, leading the maids, approached menacingly.

“It’s okay. I’ll be fine.”

The most crucial part of the project for Abella was pretending to love Havitz.

If Alpha Fish loses its original purpose, the law twists, and then everything ends.

“Repeat what you just said. You'd better pray my ears are deceiving me.”

“Havitz is a tyrant.” The guard captain drew his sword at her disrespectful reply.

“If you kill me, you die too.”

The blade stopped abruptly near Abella’s neck.

“What do you mean?”

“I didn’t come here to serve the emperor. I came to meet Havitz personally.”

“You know His Majesty the Emperor?”

“If you’re suspicious, just report it to your superiors. Tell Havitz that Abella is here.” He didn’t have the courage to do that.

“This is troublesome.”

Moreover, if her words were true, it would be imperative not to commit any rudeness.

“Let someone else handle it. Getting involved could be deadly.”

The captain sheathed his sword and returned to his position, muttering.

“Stay quiet. Everything has a procedure.”

He was clearly retreating, and Abella stuck out her tongue at the captain’s back.

A woman watching the absurd scene close by spoke with shocked eyes.

“Are you crazy? Why would you tell such a lie?”

While the candidate was quick-witted, if the truth came out, she would be executed on the spot.

“For now, I need to survive. And that captain won’t check, he’s too cowardly.”

The woman smirked and extended her hand.

“Let’s formally introduce ourselves. I’m Catenia from Manaka, thirty-two years old. I guess I’m your older sister?”

“Yes, I’m Abella, twenty-three.”

After introducing themselves, Catenia asked.

“So, you really have guts? How can you insult the emperor of the empire so easily?”

Honestly, I don’t know.

“Havitz.”

When I hear about his nearly criminal acts, it makes my blood boil, but somewhere inside...

-It’s okay. It’s okay.

When he patted my back and said it was okay, it was the first time in my life I felt I had a fence.

“Well, it’s the truth.”

Abella shrugged nonchalantly.

“I mean... I’m honestly scared too. But being older, I hope I won’t be made a concubine. Oh, sorry.”

Unlike Catenia, Abella was of an age to potentially share Havitz’s bed.

“It’s okay. I actually plan to become a concubine.”

“What?”

Even if the money was good, Catenia would have reconsidered if it meant being close to Havitz.

“Group four, enter!”

The guards opened the gate, and thirty people rushed through the arch.

True to being the largest man-made structure in the world, the palace's path seemed endless, and they finally stopped in front of a spacious central office.

“We’re here.”

Another spy from Kashan had secretly infiltrated the location of the first interview.

The assassin of Queen Woorin, El Crouch.

Once a doll maker, he had learned black magic from a doll maker in the East and transformed his body into a doll.

“I have two lives left.” Having distributed his life among porcelain dolls, he had lived for over 200 years, but now he was approaching his end.

“Well, I’ve lived long enough.”

On the day he left Kashan, Woorin had said.

“I’m sorry, Crouch.”

Crouch, who had no flesh, lifted his head, and the mechanical organs inside him whirred.

“It’s inappropriate for the Queen to apologize.”

Woorin smiled bitterly.

“We’ve known each other a long time. No one but you could transfer the Law-Execution to Marsack.”

“It’s an honor.”

Crouch’s mission was a transporter.

“The biggest problem is still Balkan. We can’t send multiple assassins because he can read the will of crowds.”

“Multiple. He can’t read the intent of one person, but he notices if there are two or more.”

“Right. The more there are, the clearer the military spirit. Currently, Alpha Fish is training to eliminate any hostility towards Havitz. So, you infiltrating should pose no problem.”

“The real problem is on the day of the plan.”

If Alpha Fish reveals its intent, adding Crouch makes two, allowing Balkan to read the military spirit.

“When Alpha Fish kills Havitz, I should be dead.”

It had to be singular, and only Crouch, who had multiple lives, could do it.

“You might need to die twice.” That would mean eternal death.

“For the Law-Execution to be transferred to Alpha Fish, it must go through ‘initialization.’ The process is dangerous.”

“I understand, Your Majesty.”

“So, Crouch.” Woorin approached him.

“It’s not because you have two lives that I entrusted you with this. I believe you can escape at least once. Don’t die.”

Crouch’s face, made of wire, twisted, and his mouth formed a wry smile.

“This life was given by you, Your Majesty. I won’t resent it even if all the porcelain dolls I carry break.”

“That’s not what I meant.”

Crouch smiled, but without eyelids, it just looked like his eyes were wide open.

“You don’t need to make excuses like a girl.”

Woorin puffed her cheeks.

“What?”

“Until the day you return, preserve your royal body.”

After leaving Kashan, Crouch assassinated the maid of Marsack and took over her role.

“My name is Orphe.”

As a master of disguise, he perfectly mimicked not only her appearance but also her personality and even her minor habits.

Abella, who knew the plan to be at the first interview site, couldn’t find Crouch.

“He must be watching me.” As she looked around and their eyes met, Crouch discreetly turned his gaze away.

“It’s better we don’t know each other.”

The interviewers approached the women of group four for a physical examination.

They checked whether they were men or women by feeling their bodies, and women with tattoos were disqualified.

“Pass. Proceed to the next gate.”

Six women were disqualified, and the remaining 24 followed the guard to a small waiting room.

“Congratulations on passing the first interview.”

A manager with a goat beard stood on the podium with a register.

“Of course, you still have to go through second, third, and fourth interviews and a final test.”

The candidates’ eyes grew tense.

“Thirty will be finally selected. It’s a competition where one in ten is eliminated. But we’ll select some special admissions from this gateway.”

“Special admissions?”

The candidate maids murmured among themselves.

“Is there anyone among you who would like to serve directly beside His Majesty the Emperor, in the Imperial Palace?”

No one wanted to serve the murderer, and a cold silence followed.

“It’s not compulsory. But ultimately, the final selection will be by draw. If you’re not confident of passing…”

“I’ll do it.”

All eyes turned to one place, and Catenia stammered in astonishment.

“You, you…”

Abella shrugged.

“I told you, something like this.”

The manager checked her tag number and quickly flipped through the register.

“Hmm, Abella Yuth. Gypsy origin…”

Nothing seemed out of the ordinary.

“Go through that door. The maids will re-examine you, and if nothing special is found, you’ll directly pass.”

“Yes. But I have one request.”

As if granting the wish of a dying person, the manager closed the register and raised his chin.

“Speak.”

“Could you possibly hint to His Majesty the Emperor that he might remember me?”

“The Emperor?”

The manager looked around nervously.

“You, are you unwell? If so, I can’t pass you. It’s a matter of life and death.”

“It’s your life that’s at risk, manager. If you leave me unattended, who knows what disaster might come later.”

“Damn! Why me...!” The manager felt the same frustration as the captain had.

“So, what is your relationship with His Majesty?”

As the candidates watched with pale faces, Abella grinned widely.

“Childhood friends.”

“Your Majesty! The head housemaid requests an audience.”

The doors of the grand hall opened, and an elderly woman over seventy crossed the hall with a deadpan expression.

Balkan snored on the throne, and a military tactical map was spread on the floor.

Havitz and the other generals sat there, playing with military figurines.

“Boom! Bang! Cannon fire!”

The comedian Zetaro was horrified.

"Aaah! My eyeball fell out! Quickly, put it back in! Eyeball! Collect the eyeballs!"

The battle doll Natasha moved a soldier figurine.

"Die! Die! Die! Die!" The Interior King Smodo crumpled the map.

"All the women of the Paris Kingdom are mine! Wait, just selecting the fertile women aged 10 to 20 would be about 4.47 million..."

The head housemaid coughed.

"Hmm-hmm, Your Majesty the Emperor."

Havitz threw a model ship and turned his head.

"Why? I'm busy now. It's a state council meeting."

"By any chance, do you remember a friend from long ago named Abella Yuth?"

"Who? Hmm."

If it were a lie, he could be beheaded, but the head housemaid just kept her mouth shut like a mute.

"If I die, I die."

She didn't imagine the future.

This was indeed the first qualification a head housemaid of the Gustaf Empire should possess.

"I'm not sure."

The head housemaid bowed her head.

"Understood, then..."

Balkan mumbled as if talking in his sleep.

"That, you know, the girl you met before. Something about setting up a flower shop or something..."

Havitz’s eyes widened as he stood up.

"Abella Yuth!"

The generals turned to look at him simultaneously.

"Right! It was Abella! But why? Why are you suddenly looking for her?"

He vaguely remembered the name, but he had never forgotten the promise he made to the girl at that time.

"It's not me looking for her, sire. It's a girl who has come as a maid this time and wishes to have an audience with you."

"Abella has come looking for me?" Havitz’s shoulders shook.

"She must have completed the flower garden!"
