Chapter 1120: Depths of the Abyss (4)

Gaold asked, "Is there any chance Miro will regain her memories?"

"There is," Monga replied, looking towards the beach.

Miro, with Arius, was talking to Shirone.

"If her lost memories manifested as a child, we could wait until she grows up. I think it might take about 30 years."

"30 years."

In the dream, Gaold could wait as long as needed.

'I don't know. What's the right thing to do?' Since there was no way out, he had plenty of time to think.

"Brother. Brother."

Miro waved her hand in front of Shirone's eyes.

"Say something. Is watching the sea that interesting?"

Gaold and the others watched.

"Master…" Monga's face was filled with sadness.

"He did so much in reality. Nothing was easy. He sacrificed everything and fought for humanity."

Sein said, "Now he's doing nothing."

"Yes. Usually, the most earnest desires are fulfilled within the dream barrier, but Yahweh's dream is quite dry. It's proof of how intense reality was."

Monga's shoulders slumped.

"It's a pity. This place is like level 1.5 of the abyss. Just a bit further, and we could have reached Ymir. He had the ability."

Shirone's strike against Leviathan in the second layer had been conveyed to Monga.

'He definitely surpassed infinity. In a normal state, he might have been able to defeat Ymir.'

Of course, since this was Ymir's dream, nothing could be said with 100% certainty.

"Hey, he's not saying anything."

As Shirone remained silent, Miro sat next to him.

Seeing Arius sniffing Shirone, Gangnan asked, "Why is he a dog? He was a person in the dream."

"He lost his reason. It's a good judgment. If memories are melted away, he focuses on instinct. That dog probably senses where we are. Not in clear human language, but instinctively."

As Miro drew in the sand, only the waves endlessly crashed.

"…waiting."

Hearing the voice beside her, Miro turned her head.

"What?"

"Waiting."

For the first time, Shirone spoke, and Gaold's group hurried over.

"Master! Are you conscious?"

Monga asked, but silence followed.

"What are you waiting for?"

In response to Miro's question, Shirone pointed to the endless horizon over the sea.

"Something huge."

And probably…

"The most powerful thing in this world."

Monga looked at the sea in shock.

"Ah…"

An unexpected variable was that they were near the end of the abyss.

"Ymir is coming."

The next morning.

Unlike the busy workers, the officials arrived later than the previous day.

"Ugh, my stomach hurts."

Since the incident where people's positions changed in the banquet hall, the schedules of various countries had been disrupted.

Most countries held emergency meetings to understand what had happened.

However, some leaders enjoyed the festivities provided by Arachne.

'Last night was amazing.'

Countries that didn't attend had no idea what was discussed in the pleasure spots.

Nations were tense.

"Arachne seems to be focusing on Jive."

Lupist, hearing the report from his aide, drew arrows on the relationship chart on the blackboard.

"I heard the banquet lasted until dawn. There must have been some deals. From today, sides will be drawn."

Albino asked, "How was the hospitality?"

"I didn't go."

Lupist turned around.

"I was analyzing the incident in the banquet hall. But some Tormian officials attended. They said there was no special information exchange…"

"VIPs are different."

At Plu's words, Lupist nodded.

"To get their information, we need to send our people. But securing allies comes first. There's a country trying to make contact."

Shirone asked, "Which one?"

"The Southern and Mun Kingdoms. The Southern is very interested in Yahweh. Meanwhile, the Mun Kingdom has shifted from hostility to friendliness. Last night's incident must have influenced them."

Lupist pointed to the blackboard.

"But this isn't enough to decide the outcome. Alliances can break anytime. The country Tormia needs most now is Corona. The probability is quite high."

Corona is managed by the Ivory Tower, and Shirone holds the highest authority there.

"There has been no contact yet. The factional strife within the Ivory Tower must be severe."

"They arrived this morning."

Everyone turned to Shirone.

"I'll negotiate directly."

The Kingdom of Corona

Even after the Holy War began, the army led by the First Legion Commander Baal did not cease its attacks.

The demons' motto was to surrender to Satan if they wanted to live, but Corona was not weak either.

"Phew."

The snow piled on the plateau was stained with the blood of both demons and humans, creating a repulsive color.

The man who had annihilated 3,000 demons had a cold expression and a long scar running along his lips.

Thud. Thud.

He irregularly stabbed the bodies with a knife to check for signs of life and then cut off a demon's arm.

Sitting on the corpse, he bit into the flesh.

The sound of chewing was followed by him sucking the blood from the severed end.

It was a way to replenish both food and water.

"Ah, that's good."

The man's name was Laska.

He was a three-star resident of the Human Safety Enforcement Department, where Minerva was one of the Five Great Stars.

The stars of the Human Safety Enforcement Department, except for the Holy Brain, were all former notorious criminals.

They were experts in war and murder, and even demons shuddered at these battlefield fiends.

"Captain."

Laska's aide, Staff, walked through the knee-deep snow.

"Big sister wants you to return to the base. You've been out too long. We're running out of supplies."

Big sister referred to none other than Minerva.

"Supplies, huh."

Laska, chewing on the flesh, offered the severed arm to Staff.

"Want some?"

Staff, watching it closely, scratched his beard with a smirk.

"No thanks. You've already had a bite."

"…You're not normal either."

As they teleported down the mountain, Corona's soldiers were engaged in skirmishes.

Minerva shouted.

"You idiot! Where have you been?"

"I was tracking the remnants. What's all this?"

Minerva's jet shot out at incredible speed, piercing through the demons.

"Can't you see? Baal has started pressuring the Ivory Tower. We can't hold out for more than three days."

The number of demons in the First Legion far surpassed the human army.

Laska said.

"Let's make peace. What's the point of this? If we fight alongside the Law Department, we can manage."

"That's why I called you." Minerva gestured behind her.

"Go. Shirone is here."

"The Great Star?"

Entering the tent, Laska found Shirone sitting there.

"Welcome, Great Star."

While all the stars respected Shirone, Laska's greeting felt somewhat irreverent.

"Yes. I want to meet the Grand Star. Minerva said you could do it, Laska."

'This woman…!'

Of course, with his ability, it was possible, but it would likely cost him his life.

'How did I get involved with such a woman?'

Sighing, Laska said.

"Meeting the Grand Star is very difficult right now. She's imprisoned within the law."

"Imprisoned?"

Shirone's eyes widened.

"Who could do that? How could anyone imprison the Grand Star?"

"Sing."

Laska's expression darkened.

"With the combined power of Yin and Yang, Sing is invincible. She enforces everything."

'The Grand Star is imprisoned.'

That was why the factional balance was so tense.

"Let's try anyway. I thought there was no solution as it is. Please resolve this, Great Star."

Laska took Shirone to the Ivory Tower.

Due to the wartime state, they had to use different magic circles, and security was tight.

'The atmosphere is bad.'

The Corona officials, who usually greeted them with smiles, were just watching cautiously.

Reaching the 230th floor of the Ivory Tower, they found a steel door with various magic circuits.

"The Grand Star is here?"

"Yes. As you can see, it's a self-destruct circuit. If you try to break it by force, the Grand Star will be in danger."

Shirone frowned.

'Sing. She's decisive but not cruel. Why show such hostility to the Grand Star?'

As Shirone activated Miracle Stream, Laska stopped him.

"Don't. The moment Hexa's power touches it, it's over. The only way is to deactivate the magic circuits from inside."

Laska took a deep breath.

'The pinnacle of thievery, Stealth.'

With the ability that once robbed countless vaults worldwide, he became immaterial while holding his breath.

'Impressive.'

Laska, now as hazy as smoke, walked towards the steel door with puffed cheeks.

Like a lie, his form entered, and moments later, the circuits disappeared.

"Phew."

As the door opened, Laska, emitting smoke from his entire body, collapsed.

"Are you okay?"

"More or less. Even as immaterial, all I could do was avoid triggering the self-destruct circuit. The mental shock is considerable."

His mind was shredded, and he wouldn't be able to move for a while.

"Grand Star."

Passing Laska, Shirone approached the Grand Star, who was sitting neatly at the end of the room.

"You finally came."

She smiled sadly but also had a welcoming light in her eyes.

"What happened? Sing imprisoning you is a rebellion."

"To explain…"

A cold voice interrupted.

"Stop, Shirone."

Turning around, Shirone saw Sing guarding the door with Yin and Yang.

Shirone coldly spat out.

"Sing, what are you doing?"

"Don't be foolish. This is for you. For humanity."

"How is imprisoning the Grand Star for humanity? The Ivory Tower has protected humanity through countless crises. The Grand Star is at the center of that."

"The situation is different now."

Sing looked at the Grand Star.

"When the outside world opens, Argones will activate. And the Grand Star is a manager of the same level as Argones. Do you really think… she will protect you until the end?"

When Shirone didn't answer, Sing asked the Grand Star.

"Grand Star, tell us yourself. After Argones activates, will you still side with humanity?"

The Grand Star didn't hesitate.

"Yes. I am the avatar of the planet and the mother of humanity. I will protect humanity until the end."

"Lies."

Sing said.

"I trust no one. In this world, only I am real. All other beings are illusions. Therefore, Grand Star, you are the same."

"Sing, I know your philosophy. But you're wrong. You are wrong."

"If you continue to lie…"

As Sing extended her hand, Yin and Yang pushed their power into her with the law of Yin and Yang.

As the Ivory Tower shook, Shirone blocked with Miracle Stream.

"Stop. Don't do something you'll regret."

Sing shouted.

"You fool! Don't you understand? Do you know how much danger your judgment could bring?"

Shirone's expression remained steady, and Xing finally decided to use force.

"Forgive me."

Boom!

At that moment, a loud noise erupted from below the Ivory Tower, and Araka from the Internal Affairs Department rushed in.

"There's trouble!"

The Grand Star asked.

"Araka, what's happening?"

"Baal! Baal has led the army of hell and entered. They're currently up to the 12th floor."

Exhausted, Laska blew his bangs up.

'I knew this would happen.'
