Chapter 223: Waiting for the New Term (2)

“Isn't it already too late? You said we would only stay for about 10 minutes.”

“That’s why I told you to get up early. I was so busy taking care of you that I forgot. I realized it because the number of tourists didn’t decrease even at night.”

“Ah, please.”

The Sea Dragon Kaios is known to traverse the three oceans crossing the equator over a span of 10 years.

On Galliant Island, it stays for about 10 minutes, and the number of tourists coming to see the dragon amounts to a whopping 2,000.

Upon arriving at the port, it was so packed with people that there was no space to squeeze in. It seemed impossible to walk to the dock.

Someone shouted that there were 10 minutes left.

Canis, who was gauging the proportion of darkness in the surrounding space, grabbed Arin’s hand.

“Well then, we’ll take our leave now.”

The two disappeared into the shadows. Casting Dark Port allowed them to reach their destination without being detected.

Shirone’s group was stunned. Especially, Amy trembled with her fists clenched, cursing Canis’s attitude. Judging by the names and parts of animals described, she seemed quite enraged.

“What should we do? Is there no way to get inside?”

“I’ll try talking to the people at the port. Wait here.”

Amy stopped Jis, who was about to dash toward the management office.

“It’s already too late for that. Do we just have to watch from here?”

Someone shouted that there were 5 minutes left.

At that moment, flashes of light shot up from the edge of the crowd and began to embroider the night sky. They were mages from the continent who had come to view Kaios.

Shirone and Amy looked at each other and grinned.

“Alright! Let’s go!”

In a flash of teleportation, Shirone’s group blended naturally into the flashes. Who could tell who was a student and who was a mage in such a situation?

Contrary to the expectation that the breakwater side would be the most crowded, most people were gathered on the outside of the dock, wary of it.

Although Kaios did not harm anyone on GalRiant, it seems that dragons were still feared.

On the other hand, mages and knights accustomed to battles were gathered inside the dock.

One could truly grasp the extent of the dragon’s popularity by the density of seasoned individuals, usually seen only in the capital's mercenary market.

“Hey, I’ve saved a spot.”

Canis, who had arrived first, greeted his friends without changing his expression. Amy rolled up her sleeves and approached.

“You traitor! You ran away to save yourself?”

“Is there a need to talk about life and death? Since you’re here, you can just watch.”

At that moment, the crowd erupted in cheers.

Shirone and his friends realized what had happened without even seeing it, then immediately turned and ran to the end of the dock.

A massive column of water was surging in from the open sea. Judging by its size and speed, it could easily submerge the port.

“Hoh? Hoooooh?”

Shirone’s group was stunned with horror. As the tidal wave approached 200 meters away, their breaths were caught in their throats.

Just when they thought they would be swept away without a trace, a huge object rose vertically from the water.

The wave collapsed and a massive water stream weighing thousands of tons gushed out, surged up toward Kaios.

“Euaa! Euaaa!”

Shirone’s group screamed as if electrocuted. The dragon lifted the entire sea. It was a magical power that seemed to have no end.

The rising waters spread out like a fountain 300 meters into the air, creating dozens of arches.

Kaios, who had confined himself in an arch with a radius of over 1 kilometer, displayed an overwhelming figure.

Kuooooo!

A mournful and beautiful cry adorned the sea.

At that moment, a light burst out from behind Shirone. People were aiming and shooting light at the Sea Dragon with cylindrical devices.

“Why are they doing that? Are they attacking it?”

Jis laughed at Shirone's innocent words.

“No, that’s an optical camera. It’s a device that can capture light as it is, and it’s incredibly expensive. But on days like today, it sells like hotcakes. There are even professional photographers who travel the world just to take pictures of Kaios.”

As Kaios performed its graceful, underwater flight, the sky was filled with large fireworks prepared by the GalRiant autonomous government.

According to Jis, they spared no expense because the tourism revenue was tremendous.

Watching the dragon swim through the arch of water columns, filled with fireworks, was a sight to behold.

Amy looked at Jis with sparkling eyes.

“Thank you, Jis. This alone makes the trip to this island worth it.”

“Hehe, I promised, didn’t I? I said I’d give you a proper tour.”

Tess was inspired by the sight of Canis and Arin holding hands affectionately. She couldn't miss this great opportunity, so she quietly grabbed Rian's arm.

“What, what’s this? What are you doing all of a sudden?”

As Rian grimaced as if bug had stuck to him, Tess’s eyes widened in disbelief.

“Hey, isn’t that too much? No matter how hideous I am, am I worse than your arm?”

“N- No, that’s not it. I was just really surprised.”

Normally, she would have felt disappointed and upset, but the spectacular and beautiful scene over the sea was enough to make her forget such emotions.

The sea dragon turned to the audience as if saying goodbye. The arch quickly spread toward the open sea, and twelve bursts of fireworks exploded around the moon, which looked like a silver platter.

Tess began to sob.

“Sob, I don’t know anymore...”

Her friends turned to her in surprise. Especially Rian, who felt guilty, couldn’t hide his confusion.

“Wha- What? I told you it’s not because I hate it. It just happened so suddenly…”

“Shut up, you idiot. I've gotten attached to you guys. What am I going to do now?"

Her friends felt the same way as Tess.

“We have to go back tomorrow. I don’t want to part. Maybe I should switch to a mage? Then I could always see you at school.”

“What are you talking about? Tess, you’re obviously going to be a swordwoman. We can meet anytime after graduation.”

Tess nodded repeatedly at Shirone's consolation. Then she called Amy and Arin to join hands.

“Let’s write letters to each other. Okay? We’ll stay in contact.”

Arin pointed at herself.

"M- Me too?"

"Of course! You’re a member of Heaven's Crew! Where else in the world can you find such a tight-knit friendship?"

Amy remembered the day she first met Tess.

There were awkward moments, but now she loved doing things together with her. No one in the world could dislike Tess, who had a righteous and warm heart.

“Yeah. We’ll definitely meet again. Next time, just us girls.”

"Alright! With this, Heaven's Crew is officially formed!"

Shirone’s friends turned around and headed home.

As it was a special day, the shops were open 24 hours, but they wanted to spend the last moments of their vacation intimately with friends.

When Jis arrived home, he found a letter in his mailbox.

"Huh? A letter from the captain? It’s addressed to you."

“Sister Marsha?”

On the day they came back from Heaven, they weren’t in good shape, so she couldn’t even properly say goodbye. When they woke up from a deep sleep, the Parrot Mercenaries had already left the island.

Shirone unfolded the letter and read it for a long time.

“Haha! As expected from Marsha Noona!”

Amy's eyes narrowed sharply.

“What is it? What makes it so interesting?”

"Huh? Oh, it’s nothing."

“This! Can’t you give it to me? What, is it a love letter or something?"

When Amy lunged to snatch it, Shirone readily placed the letter in her hand.

Surprisingly, Amy was the one who was flustered.

"What, what’s this? Why are you giving it to me for real? If it’s important, I don’t need to read it..."

"No, it’s fine. Probably."

If it's fine, then it's fine. What does “probably” mean? Amy grumbled as she read the letter, then tilted her head.

There was nothing written on the paper.

"What’s this? It’s just a blank sheet."

“That’s right. But she left a letter.”

Tess smiled as if she knew.

"That’s right. She left a letter."

Shirone looked up at the distant sky. The blank sheet of paper contained all the words she wanted to convey.

This was Marsha. They would surely meet again someday.

‘I should write her a letter when I get back.’

He wished Marsha the best in her future endeavors.

Waiting for the New Term (1)

After returning home, Shirone spent a pleasant time with his parents and wrote letters to his friends whenever he had free time.

Though Marsha conveyed her feelings with a blank sheet, Shirone couldn’t match her wit. So he filled his letters with even more care.

“All done. Now I just need to send them.”

He planned to go to the city tomorrow to mail the letters. By the time the letters reached his friends, the vacation would be nearly over. After returning from Heaven, he realized the value of everyday life. He wanted to go back to academy as soon as possible and see his friends.

"Shirone, come down for dinner."

“Yes, I’m coming.”

At her mother’s voice calling from the first floor, Shirone blew out the candle and left his room.

@

The capital, Baska.

The Mercodyne residence, located in the royal palace management district.

On the humble dining table, unfitting for a first-class noble, there were unknown greens that even an ascetic might chew on.

Though Iruki’s mother despised it, the Mercodyne father and son enjoyed their simple meal, because they did not like the feeling of fullness after eating meat dulling their thoughts.

Iruki ate rice with a fork and occasionally chewed on the greens.

Across from him, a man who looked exactly like Iruki, but with only a few more wrinkles, eating his meal.

Mercodyne Albino, Iruki's father and the leader of the Dragon Thunder. Although he was not a Savant, he was a man so knowledgeable about everything that it was said that no one in the world knew what he did not.

Albino asked at an odd moment.

“How is the entropy problem going?”

“I think I somehow figured it out.”

Iruki answered without even raising his head. Their relationship wasn’t bad, but he thought that having ears and a mouth was enough to hear and answer questions.

After finishing his meal, Albino put down his utensils and asked,

“Iruki, what is 1 plus 1?”

“Two.”

“No. It’s 3.”

For the first time, Iruki looked up. This was a moment when eyes contact were necessary.

“No, it’s 2.”

“Then make it three.”

"Why?"

Albino shrugged.

“Does it matter?”

"Does it?"

“Prove it and bring it to me. If you can do this, you’ll get closer to the answer you’re seeking. This is your final assignment for the break.”

“It’ll probably take at least six days.”

Albino's eyes lit up.

“Hoo, are you already getting a sense of it?”

“No. Father, you’re leaving for a business trip in five days.”

Iruki resumed eating his rice, one grain at a time. As long as he had the minimum energy to keep his brain functioning, satiety was the enemy. He had been working his brain non-stop during the break, to the point where even a savant’s brain might feel overloaded.

Albino finally spoke.

“I won’t confirm. I’ll just go and believe that you did it.”

“This is the first time I’ve known you trust me, Father.”

Albino smiled. Even if Iruki couldn’t solve the Akashic Record within six days, it didn’t matter. He was simply proud of his son for calculating his business trip schedule first.

“No knowledge can surpass wisdom. Let’s say you’ve won.”

"I really enjoyed the meal."

Iruki bowed his head and cleared the table. As Albino drank tea and read the newspaper, Iruki finished washing the dishes and was about to leave when the butler entered.

“Young master, a letter has arrived. It’s from someone named Arian Shirone.”

“Huh? Shirone?”

As Iruki’s face lit up and he ran over, Albino pretended to read the newspaper while secretly observing his son.

Arian Shirone. This was the word that came out the most from his son's mouth after he returned from this semester, a total of 3,742 times.

In Albino’s opinion, Iruki was a good prospect. He had the talent of a genius and was rich in human emotions.

Though he suffered because of this nature when he was younger, the son who returned after this semester was different.

He was no longer afraid of his own talent, and the boy named Shirone must have played a significant role in this change.
