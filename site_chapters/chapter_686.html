Chapter 686: Incident Occurrence (1)

The irritation was still directed at Shirone, who was lingering in the lower levels.

“What are you doing? I told you to take control of the main system! Where’s Jane?”

“This is the control room.”

Shirone, who had already arrived, pointed toward the device with sorrowful eyes.

“By the time we got here, it was already…”

Lupist, not understanding Shirone’s words, frowned and turned his head, only then noticing Jane lying there with a smile on her face—dead.

Though he was usually expressionless, now he seemed utterly devoid of humanity.

“Jane.”

Her abdomen was split open, and the muscles around both her wrists were completely torn.

There was no need to confirm her death, but Lupist approached her anyway.

Shirone followed behind and spoke in a mournful voice.

“It seems her heart was forced to beat after cardiac arrest. That’s why her wrist muscles…”

“I know.”

Lupist knelt on one knee and examined Jane’s hands.

“When did you arrive?”

Armand relayed the information to Shirone.

“1 minute and 32 seconds ago.”

“Nemesis was taken.”

They wouldn’t have stored it in a cube.

They cut off her fingers and took it.

Shirone suddenly realized.

“Don’t tell me… Chagall?”

As someone who succeeded the Grand Purification Weapon, he must have infiltrated the bioweapon under La Enemi’s design.

“Extending your life through mental transcendence was fine, but the final handling was lacking. You should have destroyed it somehow.”

Shirone scowled.

“Is that what you’re saying in this situation? Jane did her best.”

“And what does that change? Should I pat her corpse on the head and say ‘good job’?”

“Chairman.”

Shirone’s voice was laced with anger, but Lupist coldly turned away from Jane and stood up.

“I’ll process her as having died in the line of duty, but if she were alive, I’d have slapped her. Even as one of the Three Directors of Spectrum, she wasn’t a woman who would die so easily. In the end, it was her damn emotions that were the problem. This is why women can’t be relied upon.”

“Don’t speak so carelessly when you don’t even know what happened.”

“I know.”

He knew her better than her own parents did.

“Go. Head to the upper seed chamber and retrieve the bioweapon’s seed. Plarino has control now, but the Ultima System should handle it.”

“But…”

“We’re out of time.”

Since Lupist was right, Shirone gave up arguing and turned away.

“Let’s go, Maylay, Kido.”

As they left the control room, Shirone vented his frustration.

“He’s a cold-blooded bastard. It’s like he has no blood or tears.”

Kido, who ran up beside him, said,

“That’s not true, Shirone.”

“We’ve worked together for over ten years. And he dismisses her like that? I could never do something like that.”

“Didn’t you notice?”

Shirone looked at Kido with confusion.

“Huh? Notice what?”

“That chairman guy saw a woman’s corpse, right?”

Kido was staring at Lupist.

“He hasn’t blinked once since then.”

“Blinked?”

Kido mimed poking his own eyes.

“That’s not something you can do naturally. Of course, I don’t know what’s going through his mind. But it doesn’t seem unconscious.”

As Shirone ran while staring at the ground, Maylay spoke.

“Either way, we need to stay alert. Jane’s death is one thing, but the fact that La Enemi is silent is also concerning.”

Kido wrinkled his nose.

“They’re hiding. Not even a breath. We need scent. Nothing beats it for tracking.”

“But scent is Chagall’s specialty. Can we persuade him?”

“No need to persuade.”

Shirone said.

“Taking Nemesis means it’s already decided.”

“…….”

It felt like they had become puppets strung up by the threads of fate.

***

Meanwhile, Lupist left the control room in the opposite direction of Shirone’s group.

As the automatic door opened and he stepped into the hallway, Plarino was standing near the stairs.

“How does it feel to lose someone precious to you?”

Lupist sensed an alien energy pressing against his back.

Soon, black smoke billowed up, and the pureblood vampire Raika blocked his retreat.

“Inputting the primal code to reclaim the bioweapon was a good idea. The attempt was fine, too. But you didn’t anticipate the gap in ability, did you?”

Finally turning around, Lupist’s eyes were bloodshot, unblinking.

Raika smiled, as if enjoying it even more, and raised his hand.

“I finished her off with this hand. Well, not completely, but close enough. Given how things turned out, I think it’s for the best. She must have died in agony.”

Lupist wasn’t displeased.

That wasn’t why he was displeased.

“If you fear pain, you shouldn’t do such stupid things in the first place.”

“Heh, so you’re human after all. Your voice is trembling even as you say that. Was she precious to you?”

Plarino spoke.

“You’ve failed. The bioweapon’s control has returned to me, and soon the royal capital will be destroyed.”

Lupist let out a sigh.

“No. I don’t care about that anymore.”

Knowing how meticulous the chairman of Tormia’s Magic Association was, the two of the Three Directors looked puzzled.

“You must be angry, huh? So you’re human after all.”

It wasn’t sympathy.

What Plarino wanted was for Lupist to taste the same feelings of despair and defeat.

“Don’t misunderstand. That woman was nothing to me. She was an aide, a chief of staff. A bit eccentric, but now that she’s dead, there’s no need to think about her.”

The air grew heavy, as if it had turned into heavy metal, and Raika and Plarino’s expressions stiffened.

“She was competent.”

A woman so capable she had helped the single-minded Lupist rise to become chairman of the Magic Association.

“The only reason I’m angry is because of that. That talented woman, that genius who was almost impossible to replicate no matter how much we invested in education, wasted it all on the lives of some half-breed vermin. That’s what infuriates me to no end.”

Beyond a feeling, actual iron began to seep into the air.

“How will you compensate for this? The countless problems she was supposed to solve while alive. Not even a hundred of your deaths would be enough.”

“How dare you look down on vampires!”

Raika, dissolved into smoke, charged forward, and Plarino manipulated the surrounding iron walls to press in.

“…Iron Armor Wave.”

As the words left his mouth, the air around Lupist rippled, and iron barriers began to spread.

“Guhhh!”

Raika pierced through the first iron wall with his half-materialized ability, but second and third barriers formed, pushing him back.

“Ughhh!”

Plarino, fused with the bioweapon, crouched and screamed.

“You’d break it? Isn’t this flower precious to you, too?”

“Of course it is.”

The ancient weapon’s value was immense, rivaling more than half the kingdom’s national power.

“You should’ve behaved, then.”

The iron barriers surged even more violently, spreading toward the bioweapon’s massive outer wall.

“You’ll regret this! You!”

A cold smile formed on Lupist’s lips.

“What wouldn’t I regret?”

Creeeeak—BOOM!

The bioweapon’s vibration, growing toward the sun, became so intense that Shirone’s group stopped in their tracks.

“What’s happening?”

Kido grabbed the wall and shouted.

“It’s tilting! Is it trying to stop us?”

“No, this is beyond that.”

The angle of tilt exceeded 20 degrees.

Given the bioweapon’s height and weight, recovery was nearly impossible.

***

—We’re out of time.

Shirone suddenly realized what Lupist had meant.

“I see.”

It wasn’t about a preemptive strike on the capital.

“He’s going to break it.”

“Break it? But we haven’t even gotten the seed yet. Isn’t that important to humans?”

“You were right, Kido.”

Since when?

Probably since the moment they found Jane’s corpse.

“Lupist had already lost his rationality.”

***

The 47th Iron Armor Wave crushed the internal mechanisms, spreading halfway through the stem’s diameter.

The ancient weapon’s durability surpassed steel, but the iron barriers, unable to overcome its resistance, vanished—only for the 48th, 49th, and 50th waves to surge forth endlessly.

“You pathetic humaaaaan!”

Raika, having pierced through a staggering 87 iron barriers, grabbed Lupist’s shoulder and bared his fangs.

‘Just one bite!’

A single bite would make the Magic Association chairman his puppet.

“A mere bat.”

For the first time in his life, Lupist drew mana beyond the limits permitted by his society’s system.

Steel Magic—Adamantine Tsunami.

The steel waves, pulsing ten times per second, swept Raika’s half-materialized body away.

“Gyaaaaah!”

“Stop! How can you do this to a flower you cultivated? Are you insane?”

Plarino’s voice echoed through all the bioweapon’s speakers.

‘Lost his rationality? Far from it.’

That wasn’t their style.

‘Right, Jane?’

The strategic value of the bioweapon if handed over intact, the astronomical price of its seeds, the countless potential profits derived from them—

‘I’ll take care of all of it.’

He would grow strong enough to surpass even the ancient weapon and reverse all those losses.

‘I’ll fight your share, too.’

Lupist never closed his eyes.

‘So rest easy, Jane.’

Finally, the 362nd iron barrier reached the bioweapon’s outer wall, and Lupist unleashed the strongest power of his life.

‘This is…’

The Iron Armor Wave shattered the outer wall and burst through, cleanly severing the bioweapon’s base.

‘…the flower I lay on your grave.’

“Kyaaaaah!”

Plarino’s scream perfectly conveyed the agony of the voiceless bioweapon.

***

“It’s falling! He really did it!”

Kido ran along the tilting wall of the bioweapon, while Shirone and Maylay floated weightlessly, searching for an exit.

“Let’s escape that way! If we get caught in this, we’re done for!”

It would be like the impact of falling from space.

“I can’t fly!”

“I’ll catch you! Just jump!”

As Shirone and Maylay escaped, Kido leaped with a grimace.

“Damn these insane humans!”

***

Creeeeak—BOOM!

Ethela, her face wet with rain, looked up as she felt the ground tremble.

The world’s most expensive flower, 600 meters tall, was collapsing toward the south of Radum.

“Humans… humans.”

Beings who pursue human happiness from a human perspective.

A broken flower withers, but that flower is placed before someone’s grave to mourn their death…

And we call even that beautiful.
