Chapter 948: All Histories (3)

The hallucinatory flashes began to fade.

Natasha fell to the ground, and the few eyes that noticed her drew in countless gazes.

The noise of the war created ripples as it subsided.

‘Did that human woman… get defeated?’

Though demons despise humans, they could not easily dismiss Natasha’s prowess.

“Rian!”

Tess, having completed her mission in the biochemical area, returned and froze at the sight of Rian’s condition.

“Haah! Haah!”

Rian’s breaths were ragged, and his arms were gone from the shoulders down, vanished without a trace.

Only the divine transcendence, floating in the air—the Idea—spoke of his will.

Natasha lifted her head.

“Did you say your name was Rian?”

The skeleton harbinger was inserting its hand into her abdomen to treat her, but it could only stop the bleeding.

A kind of surgical ability.

But like the laws of manifestation dictate, the skill was only sufficient to mend a broken doll.

“Save your breath. You might die.” Rian held no particular philosophy of war.

To proclaim any banner would be hypocritical, given the number of humans his sword had slain.

“It’s okay, even if I die.”

Rian nodded and asked.

“Do you have any last words?”

“I lost.”

She wanted to make sure he knew.

“Your sword shattered all my rhythms. Whatever it is, from now on, it’s your technique, your style. I’d like to call it Irregular Beat, but it’s up to the master to name it.”

“Names don’t matter to me.”

“Is that so?”

Natasha relaxed and collapsed.

“You were impressive, you know.”

She had said all she wanted, but Rian still had something left to say.

“You mentioned discarding evil.” “It doesn’t erase your sins. But if you regret it, atone now. Rejecting demons here, where humanity judges you, is the only recompense for those you’ve killed.”

The demons’ faces twisted.

“You can torture and kill me. I’ve done many bad things.”

Natasha remained calm.

“But I don’t regret it. If regret were possible, I wouldn’t be here.”

For someone whose talent was always striving for the best, looking back was an impossibility.

“When I said I discarded evil, I meant it didn’t matter. Whether it’s on the side of humans or demons. But still, my answer is…”

Natasha lifted her head.

“I became friends with Havitz first. Sorry.”

For her, lacking a purpose in life, that was reason enough.

“Kill her! Kill the human!”

Realizing that seizing Natasha first was advantageous, the demon charged ferociously.

“Charge, everyone!”

The allied forces surged forward, and the area where Rian and Natasha were became crowded with troops.

“Kraaaaah!”

It was a scene of utter chaos.

“Satan…”

As Havitz’s severed head hit the floor, the demons all bore the same expression.

“O Satan!”

Though they differed in age and appearance, at that moment, they all seemed to have the same twelve faces.

“No, no!”

Pride's harbinger clutched its head in anguish.

“Time can’t be rewound! Why, why don’t you regret?”

Because it was unrecognized.

“I’ll take revenge!” As the harbinger charged, a massive figure emerged, causing the twelve to sink.

“Kraaaaah! Remember, humans! We will return! We will have our revenge…!”

The cries faded into the ground.

“Haah.”

It was a long while before Armin released the stop spell and collapsed.

“We did it.”

Kuan, bleeding from one eye, was visible.

The stab was perfect, making regeneration impossible, and even if it were, he’d refuse it.

“Kuan, about your decision…”

“Do you see now?”

Kuan interrupted coldly.

“Why I don’t go to see her.”

Kuan's hidden code thrives on the emotions that arise when he is driven into a horrific state.

The more his body approaches the abnormal, the more pronounced the asymmetry of extremity becomes.

‘When this war ends…’

What state will Kuan be in?

And will Siena be able to accept it?

“God, do not forgive me.” Eden, once overwhelmed by Satan’s influence, wept bitterly, offering prayers of repentance.

Sein tried to think positively.

‘At least we won.’

But upon inspecting Havitz’s severed head, a near-terrifying doubt arose.

‘What’s this?’

Havitz's lifeless face.

Yet, from one of his dead eyes, something small and dark wriggled.

‘A fly.’

The sight of it tapping at the retina with two legs sent chills down his spine.

“It’s not over yet!”

Honestly, he wasn’t sure.

If Havitz was alive, the divine frequency should have been heard through the vibrations of the mind.

“Kiaaaaa!”

As Havitz’s eye shattered like glass, blotchy tentacles burst from the broken socket.

“Dodge!”

The round mouth at the end of each tentacle opened like an umbrella, rushing towards Mei Lei.

‘You think I’ll let you?!’ Everyone activated their abilities, but for some reason, they had no effect.

‘What’s going on?’

Around Mei Lei, twelve figures appeared, heralding the return of the harbinger.

“Kah kah kah! Fooled again! You’ve been fooled!”

As the twelve rotating shadows spread, all laws within the circle were nullified.

Without making a sound, Mei Lei’s body was sucked into the tentacles’ maw.

Sein’s divine eyes could not trigger any event.

‘A predetermined future.’

Was it merely coincidence that Sein’s group gathered and Havitz left the battlefield?

“Kukuku.”

As Havitz plucked the ten-meter-long tentacles from his eyes, his face gradually regained vitality.

“Finally ate.”

The tentacles, swollen like an anaconda, rapidly digested Mei Lei.

‘No way.’

It wasn’t the sorrow for a comrade’s death.

Such human emotions had no place in the direst of circumstances.

“Kraaaaaah!”

Above Havitz’s head, a violet fan of Satan’s energy spread.

“More.”

He wanted more.

The voice of desire sounded as if it was whispering in his ear…

‘The hidden code of the Eighth Hour of Jealousy.’ The fact that Mei Lei’s divine frequency had been stolen by Havitz was clear proof.

“Cough! Cough!”

As something like innards spilled from under Havitz’s throat, they formed in the air into an abominable human physique.

“…Satan?”

“No. I’m Havitz.”

Havitz, whose face and body didn’t match, tapped his head with the monster’s index finger.

“Just the brain.”

While the rest of his body, made from others’ flesh and organs, might be cursed…

“Hoo.”

Havitz didn’t care.

“This is interesting.”

As Armin’s thoughts reached him like an echo, Sein’s group’s faces paled.

“Were you listening through the object?”

As Havitz spat out his tongue, Mei Lei’s blood-stained Nemesis was revealed.

“Ptui!”

The moment the ring was spit out and the mental wave cut off, Sein grimaced.

‘Damn it. Of all the abilities to lose, it had to be to the worst possible person.’

Havitz turned to Liria.

“Ah? You were thinking that? Surprisingly. Or should I say, cute?”

Liria’s face fell.

“Please.”

Realizing what had befallen this world.

“Don’t do this.”

If begging could somehow reverse the situation…

Sein spoke.

“Havitz, come at us. You want to kill us. Because we’re your only natural predators.” If Havitz’s face was his and his body Satan’s, then this time it truly was a single life.

“Hehe. Hehehehe.”

Enthralled by the divine frequency, Havitz ignored Sein’s words.

“I don’t care what you babble. I hear it all. The voices of your hearts.”

‘He’s truly gone mad!’

Havitz twirled his mustache in an old habit.

“And I’m done killing. You’ve used this power, so you know, right?”

Mei Lei had been eavesdropping on Havitz’s heart.

“No, your strategy will never succeed. Better to just kill all humans! Overwhelm and torture them! Isn’t that what you wanted?”

Arguably, that would have been better.

“The taste of humans.”

Havitz spoke.

“Humans torment, torture, and kill each other. There’s nothing tastier in the world. The problem is, it’s too addictive, and the threshold keeps rising.”

His sharp nails scraped his temple.

“So I thought, is there a part of humans tastier than life itself? And there was. The heart.”

That’s why he needed the divine frequency.

“Now it’s time to cook it. Extremely tasty, and you can enjoy it for a long, long time…”

Havitz’s one eye ecstatically trembled, and the tentacle on the other side stood erect.

“The taste of humans.”

No one responded.

“…They’re gone.”

When Havitz released the banishing, not even his footsteps remained.

Armin asked.

“Where could he have gone? Perhaps to Tormia…”

“No.”

Sein shook his head.

“Not Tormia. Miro is coming, or perhaps he’s already here. When good and evil clash, the judgment of Terraforce activates. And if that happens…”

Kuan took over.

“The divine frequency disappears.”
