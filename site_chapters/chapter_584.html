Chapter 584:

Back in his dorm room, Shirone sat on his bed.

With the final day of Scramble Royal approaching, the pressure of rewards and consequences resurfaced.

'Yes, I have to do it.'

He had to break Eden's Absolute Barrier.

'But how?'

Though he spoke confidently in front of his friends, he still had no clear solution.

'I need to figure it out. If it were Senior Plu, he would have found a way.'

A mage trained to handle all variables.

Miro said that was what made a professional, the highest title a mage could achieve.

'First, the laser.'

If he could keep accumulating energy, Eden's barrier would eventually break.

'But what I'm thinking, Eden is also thinking.'

This is where the problem starts.

If the opponent isn't a scarecrow, they are also desperately seeking solutions.

'She'll dodge before the energy accumulates. Do I really need to overpower her? What if I push her out of bounds?'

Using a high-powered mass wave to push Eden to the edge of the area was an option, but the countermeasure was no different from the laser.

'Eden is too light. That's the key issue.'

He could destroy a mountain, but Eden would fly away like a feather in a strong wind.

'Come to think of it, it was the same in heaven.'

It took reversing 127 years of causality with Valhalla Action to generate enough output to destroy the archangel Faiel.

Recalling that time, Shirone's eyes grew hollow.

'I fought so inefficiently.'

It was fortunate he achieved results, but his teammates might have thought he was still an amateur.

Feeling embarrassed about past decisions was a sign of growth.

"The solution is this. Somehow, I need to make Eden immovable against the mass wave. Then Ataraxia can destroy her barrier."

What if he used Shining Chain to fix her in place and bombarded her with Photon Cannon?

'The Shining Chain would break first.'

Frustrated, Shirone leaned back and banged his head against the wall.

No matter how he tried, he couldn't think of a way to break Eden's barrier.

'Should I give up? Maybe I should go back to Iruki for a new strategy…'

Shirone shook his head violently.

'No. There must be thousands, tens of thousands of ways. I just haven't found one yet.'

Coordinates.

He needed to find a way to fix Eden in place even under Ataraxia's powerful output.

'There's no way to fix her! No, there is! No, there isn't!'

Feeling like his mind was being wrung out, Shirone tore at his blanket in frustration.

'How do things stay fixed? No, nothing stays fixed! That was the problem from the start. Coordinates are…'

Shirone's eyes widened.

"Ah. Ah."

Staring at the ceiling, a small voice escaped his lips.

"Time and space."

All answers are hidden within the problem.

"There are no problems."

Even late at night, Eden didn't return to the dormitory but prayed in a deep cave.

"As long as you protect me."

On the eve of the final day of Scramble Royal, her omnipotence was at its peak.

She couldn't afford to lose to Shirone, especially to restore the honor of the Yor Church that Gaold had tarnished.

'He'll definitely come up with a different strategy today. He's smart, after all.'

But Eden had no intention of relying solely on God's name.

Her recognition in the Yor Church was due to her fervent faith being grounded in rationality.

When someone gave her bread, she didn't refuse, saying God would feed her.

'Ataraxia can't break Absolute Barrier. Even if he finds a better way, I'll just dodge it.'

If the solution God gave her was to dodge, it wasn't denying God.

'Tomorrow, I become a true daughter of God. And I'll erase Gaold's name from the records.'

Eden's prayers echoed in the cave.

'It's impossible to fix objects in place.'

Shirone was now sitting cross-legged.

As his sequence formula raced at incredible speed, he entered a state of awe, and a golden light began to shimmer around him.

'But I can do it. This will work!'

As the particles of the Light Angel's incarnation swirled like a vortex, everything in the room twisted as if being wrung out.

The next morning.

Before returning to school, Fermi visited Radum, the underbelly of Vashka.

Though it was where Canis and Arin grew up, the darkness they experienced was just the tip of the iceberg.

Radum was a ticking time bomb in the capital, hiding high-level concealment facilities where numerous non-humans plotted to overthrow the human world, including the Black Revolutionaries who recently bombed the Gold Tower.

As such, the eyes watching Fermi were filled with malice.

"An outsider."

A voice spoke as he entered a deserted area.

But when Fermi turned, only the shadows of buildings were visible.

"I'm lost. Where am I?"

"You can't just wander into Radum."

The entrance to Radum was guarded 24/7 by royal forces, strictly controlling access.

"Knowing the back door means you know where you are."

Fermi tossed a gold coin into the shadows.

The coin disappeared instantly.

"Let's go. I'm late for an appointment."

"…How much do you have?"

Fermi smiled.

"Who knows? About 10 billion gold?"

The shadow was momentarily silent, seemingly taken aback.

"I could spare a billion gold."

Shadows poured down from the buildings, surrounding Fermi.

'Shadow People.'

They were invisible in the dark.

"Maybe you really are lost. Or just a complete fool."

The voice's origin was unclear.

"This is Radum. Money won't protect you here."

The shadows rose and rushed at Fermi.

"Really?"

A tiny particle of light floated above Fermi's palm.

"What about a billion-gold spell?"

Time slowed.

"Wha…t…"

In the time it took the shadow to utter two syllables, the wave of light expanded rapidly through Radum.

"Aaah!"

As the darkness lifted, a woman's scream was heard.

"Being Radum, I came prepared."

Fermi looked down at a woman holding twin swords.

With gray skin, pointed ears, and deep-set eyes, she bit her lip and crouched.

"It's my first time seeing a Shadow Person. So this is what you look like. I heard your skin sells for a high price."

"You… how…"

Shadow People had skin that rendered them invisible in the dark.

"Shall I peel it off?"

As madness swirled in Fermi's eyes, the Shadow Person flinched and turned her upper body into the darkness.

"Run and you die."

"Ugh!"

The Shadow Person froze.

Though they wielded the laws of darkness from birth, Fermi's light magic was overwhelmingly powerful.

'What kind of magic is this?'

As expected, Fermi held the tiny particle of light he had just cast.

"Interesting, isn't it? It's called Big Bang. A spell developed by the mage Alpheas to subdue the archmage Arcane."

"Arcane…"

Arcane's name was well-known among Shadow People as an authority on dark magic.

Though Arcane himself downplayed its significance, it was registered as a standard spell for future generations.

'Of course, I didn't learn it directly.'

He simply bought the spell from someone who had.

"What do you want?"

"As rumored, your concealment is thorough. Honestly, I'm lost. So…"

Fermi grabbed her wrist and pulled her up, bringing his face close.

"Ah…"

"Shadow People are good at espionage. Can you guide me? I'll compensate you."

The Shadow Person stared at Fermi's mischievous smile, entranced.

'What is this man…?'

She had met many humans, but never one like him.

"I'll take care of Amy. I'll settle it today."

As noon approached, Kaiden declared to Hershey while grabbing his cross sword.

It wasn't a bad proposal if he could retire Amy, but it was doubtful she would be easily caught up in it.

"Even if you refuse, I'll go after Amy."

Realizing she couldn't stop Kaiden, Hershey adjusted her strategy to be as beneficial as possible.

"Wait."

As Kaiden turned, Hershey reached out.

"If you want to go after Amy, there's a condition."

Iruki and Dorothy teamed up to collect 12 scrambles.

Even with retirements, the number of scrambles summoned remained the same as the initial participants, leaving 72 scrambles.

"Pisho's collection speed is slower than yesterday."

It meant he was moving with Hershey.

Expanding the spirit zone with Jona's ability, Dorothy twitched her eyebrows.

"They're coming this way. We'll clash in three minutes."

'Master card.'

Hershey's status, previously ⓡⓡ, now had additional random cards.

'Three random cards (ⓡⓡⓡ)?'

With the first scramble of the seventh day summoned, counting was impossible.

Thus, the probability of drawing black or white from three random cards was still 50%.

'I can't read the hand. I need to raise it one more level.'

Iruki took scrambles from Hickory, turned them into random cards, and opened them.

His current hand was ●●●○ (Dictator), and Hershey added another card to reach the fourth level.

'Another random (ⓡⓡⓡⓡ)?'

As Iruki and Hershey arrived at the scramble location, they simultaneously called Kang.

'What's her hand?'

Hershey's status showed ○●○● (Democracy).

'She drew Democracy from four random cards? Damn! It's a complete gamble!'

Iruki, abandoning the scramble, led Dorothy back into the forest.

'Creating Infinity by designating cards is suicidal unless you're Eden. The best approach is to mix random cards.'

Receiving four scrambles from Hickory, Iruki opened four random cards, revealing Monarchy (○○○●).

'As expected, I can't create Papacy (○○○○) without counting.'

Iruki extended his hand.

"Give me one more."

Choosing a white card, Iruki's hand upgraded to Emperor (○○●○○).

Of course, Hershey's status would show ⓡⓡⓡⓡ○.

'From now on, it's a game of probabilities.'

Iruki's lips curled into a slight smile.

At 2 PM, the second scramble was summoned, but Shirone remained in the forest where he had settled in the morning.

Although his confrontation with Eden would be in the final round of Scramble Royal, he was also unaware of the passage of time.

'16 quadrillion.'

This was the current position of Shirone's sequence formula.

'I need to push it to the limit.'

If he didn't break his limits in advance, he wouldn't be able to respond to immediate changes in his battle with Eden.

At some point, the flow of time twisted grotesquely, distorting the surrounding scenery beyond recognition.

A silent distortion.

…!

…!

All the insects within a 5-meter radius, having lost their sense of time, emitted distress pheromones.
