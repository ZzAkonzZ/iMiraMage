Chapter 1219:

The warden slumped back into his chair.

“…Impressive.”

Then he burst into laughter.

“Ha ha ha! Truly impressive. You actually managed to persuade me. Alright, I’ll release this Curtis fellow.”

His disposition shifted instantly to friendly.

“Thank you. And one more thing—we want to go to the Core of Melkidu. Do you know how we can get there?”

“Hm, a Core access permit can only be issued by His Majesty the King. But this works out. I was planning to introduce you anyway.”

It was part of the process.

“It’s an honor. When would that be possible?”

“The procedure’s a bit complicated. Still, I’ll pull some strings. You should get a message by tomorrow morning.”

“Tomorrow morning…”

Melkidu’s time wasn’t the same as the real world’s, but they didn’t have much time left in reality either.

Iruki spoke.

“They’re probably processing the data. If this is an event, we can’t change it freely.”

Nodding, Shirone asked again.

“What about Curtis?”

“That’s simple. I just need to sign the release papers. Wait for him outside the prison—I’ll send him out.”

With that promise, the group exited the guard barracks.

“We’re finally out of the mid-layer.”

The dazzling and beautiful cityscape of Melkidu’s capital, Parme, came into view.

“So this is the play zone, huh? We’ve been sneaking through back doors so much, this feels weirder now.”

As Nade spoke, the iron gate opened and Curtis appeared.

“Curtis!”

Pena shouted.

He was limping, his face bruised as though he’d taken a serious beating.

“Are you okay? What happened to you?”

Ignoring Pena, Curtis approached Shirone with a tense expression.

“Did you find a way?”

“Yes. We’re scheduled to meet the King tomorrow. I think this is the final gate to the Core.”

“…I see.”

Curtis relaxed, though the feeling was more relief than joy.

“Let’s go for now. You need rest.”

“Yes, please, let’s just go. I don’t want to be here a second longer.”

As they headed into the city, Pena asked,

“Come on, tell me already! I’m dying of curiosity. What’s it like in prison? Do they feed you? What kind of place is it?”

“If you’re that curious, why not go yourself?”

Pena looked horrified.

“Are you insane? Me? Go there? Wait… don’t tell me… did you actually lose your virginity or something?”

“Only my virginity?”

Curtis didn’t say more, and Pena didn’t want to know any further.

Shirone offered comfort.

“You went through a lot.”

“It’s fine. I was prepared for it. It’s meaningless now anyway. As long as we can get to the Core…”

His resolve to catch the one who murdered his daughter made everyone go quiet in respect.

“Alright, now you tell me. You said you’re meeting the King tomorrow? What happened while I was gone?”

Shirone explained everything that had occurred.

“I see. You’ve all been through a lot. But in that case… how much crime point do you have left?”

“Almost none. We used most of it persuading the warden. We’ve got about a million left. But if we have to persuade the King too… that’s basically nothing.”

“Hm. I see.”

As Curtis leaned his chin in his hand and thought, Pena cleared her throat.

“Um… I’ll pay.”

“Huh?”

Looking away, she muttered,

“Well, Curtis just got out of prison, so he should at least eat something decent and get some rest. I’ll cover food and lodging, so don’t worry and go wherever you want.”

Curtis laughed.

“Getting a meal from Stingy Pena? Must be the biggest incident in Melkidu.”

“Shut up! I spend when I need to! Besides, you helped me too… so… just take it!”

“Well, fine. Pena’s covering food and lodging. But it’s still a problem that Shirone’s team is out of points. He’s the only one who can guarantee a successful persuasion. Who knows what kind of challenge the King will throw at us.”

Even the warden battle had been difficult.

“So, I have an idea.”

“Huh?”

Everyone looked toward Curtis as he continued.

“Come one, come all! Miguel’s Luxury Shop—stocked with all the hottest new items from just a week ago!”

“Lowest prices in Parme! We sell for 10% below market average! Rush sales welcome!”

The western plaza, with a clear view of the royal palace, was swarming with people.

“Whoa, are all these people vendors?”

Curtis explained,

“Parme has the highest foot traffic in all of Melkidu. It’s because you can trade points here. People come and stay for a long time to make back what they spent on the Royal Entry item.”

“So that’s how the market stays so active.”

“Exactly. You guys have some items, right? Sell them. You’ll earn enough points to cover your persuasion costs. Just sell them fast—we don’t have much time. I know you want to get the best value, but with the stuff you have, you’re basically VIPs. If you take too long, you’ll attract scavengers. You don’t want that, right?”

The group understood.

“There’s no such thing as a charitable merchant. It’s safer to go with a major dealer. Let’s go to that guy.”

He pointed to a sharply dressed man in his thirties.

“Welcome! This is Osman’s Luxury Shop. If you see anything in our catalog you like—”

“We’re not buying. We’re here to sell.”

Osman nodded immediately.

“Of course, you’re very welcome. No one offers higher rates than we do. Shall we move to the office?”

“No, let’s do it here. Most of the items are cards anyway.”

There had been expensive weapons at the port, but they didn’t have the means to carry those around.

“Understood. Then, please show me your items. Is this the person in charge? I’ll initiate the transaction.”

Osman pulled out a card.

“Activate. Shop.”

As the card lit up, a system message appeared.

—A trade has begun between Team Shirone and Team Osman. You may exchange points for one hour. Once both parties confirm the trade, the effect will end automatically.

‘A fraud prevention system. And… he’s an accomplice too.’

There were probably others in the area, as well as back in his office.

“If you open a shop with an item, does it use up points for each transaction?”

“Yes. That’s a cost you have to accept if you want to do business. It’s not too expensive, but it does add up.”

Of course, that would be factored into the commission.

“The real problem is people who come to trade without any items or points. Half are just messing around, and the other half are competitors trying to sabotage us. That’s why there’s a one-hour limit. If the person who activated the item doesn’t confirm the trade, they can’t trade for an hour.”

“Ah, I see.”

“Don’t worry. As long as the shop is active, your items are protected by the system.”

After confirming Curtis’s nod, Shirone handed over the items.

“Yes. I’d like to sell these.”

“Hmm, let’s see…”

As Osman calmly flipped through each card, his expression gradually began to shift.

“Oh…”

Stroking his chin in thought, he eventually handed the cards back and spoke.

“I’ve seen Mr. Curtis and Ms. Pena before, but this is my first time meeting you, Mr. Shirone. You collected all these cards without ever stopping by Parme?”

Using system messages, merchants were able to remember users’ names.

“Yes. It just turned out that way.”

Osman understood.

‘The backdoor, huh.’

There had always been rumors, though nothing concrete—but occasionally, there were customers who just felt different.

“Alright. Then let me make an offer. Including top-tier cards, there are thirty-seven total…”

He looked up.

“120 million points. How does that sound?”

It was more than they expected.

‘That means they’re worth even more normally… well, we didn’t reach the premium shops in the internal track.’

Osman continued.

“You didn’t come to haggle, right? That’s why I offered the highest price possible. Some of these are crafting items too. Which means—we’ll take care of any consequences.”

“How so?”

The conversation sidestepped the core issue.

“I don’t know the details either. If a problem arises, there are people who handle it. We outsource it to them.”

‘Gangs, huh.’

“But what I can promise is that no one will know where these items came from. That alone will save you a lot of trouble.”

They’d likely reach the Core tomorrow, but just imagining the hassle of the day ahead gave Shirone a headache.

“Alright. Let’s go with that.”

“Thank you. Then let’s begin.”

—120 million points have been transferred to Team Shirone.

Shirone passed over the cards.

“Thirty-seven in total. Confirmed. Activate, trade approval. Please repeat after me.”

“Activate, trade approval.”

—Trade complete.

The shop card vanished.

“Thank you. If we get the chance, we’ll be back.”

Osman handed over a card.

“A premium restaurant discount coupon. It’ll reduce costs by twenty percent.”

“Haha! Nice.”

Pena, touched, clutched the coupon tightly.

“And one more thing…”

Osman added in a cautionary tone.

“As I said earlier, there won’t be any direct trouble, but things might get a bit annoying. I suggest you leave Parme as soon as possible.”

“We will.”

Having seen enough of the world, Shirone didn’t need to hear any further details.

Leaving the merchant district, the group headed toward a restaurant, holding their hungry stomachs.

“What’s this discount for? Do they have steak?”

A group blocked their path.

“If you wanted meat, you could’ve just said so. We’ve got more than enough to treat you.”

Curtis frowned.

“Predatory merchants.”

“These guys are merchants?”

They looked thuggish from the start, and their weapons were clearly top-tier.

“In Parme, users can trade points directly. That’s why scumbags like them thrive here.”

“Haha! That’s right. Now choose—fight us and get stripped clean, or hand over your points.”

PvP battles between users were decided by draining the opponent’s points to zero.

“Better to give it to us than waste it. Be cooperative and we’ll only take half.”

Shirone stepped forward.

“What makes you so confident? If we have more points, you’ll be the ones stripped bare.”

“Keh. Is that so? Then let’s see what you’ve got.”

Shirone instantly understood.

‘Point tycoons.’

They hoarded enough points to win any battle and used threats to extort others.

“Oh? Isn’t that Pena? Hanging onto another bunch of rookies? Must mean they’ve got some points.”

“Cut the crap! Who do you think I am?!”

“What do I think? Your specialty, obviously. Leeching off rich guys, and hiding like a maggot when things get rough.”

As Pena’s face turned red with rage, Shirone stepped up.

“Watch your mouth.”

“Ooooh!”

The thugs exaggerated their reactions mockingly.

“Wow, tough guy. So what now? If you traded with Osman, you’ve got at least 50 million, right? Let’s call it even with that. Or you can lose it all and go to prison.”

‘These bastards are annoying.’

They wanted to retaliate, but if they triggered a battle and lost, their points would be gone.

“Shirone, let me handle this.”

“What are you going to do?”

As Nade started walking toward the thugs, he turned around and gave a wink, pulling down his collar.

“Leave it to me.”

Hidden beneath was a Royal Guard officer’s badge.
