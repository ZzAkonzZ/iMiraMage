Chapter 723:

The Battle of the Law: Whiteblack.

Survivors: 9.

White's survivors: 4.

"We still have a chance."

Among them was Marco, who had forced Shirone to activate the harmony of the law in the carriage.

"Four Black participants died simultaneously a while ago. Three of them were the only ones moving as a team."

This information was confirmed through Marco's weapon card, the Periscope, so it was accurate.

"Black will fight individually until the end. If we act together from now on, we have a chance."

Breeze, a hairdresser from the White zone, said.

"Hmm, that's probably best. But first, we need to decide by vote whether to reveal our cards."

Even among teammates, it's natural not to want to share essential weapon cards for survival.

Initially, White's participants aimed for minimal cooperation, but eventually, six sacrifices were made.

"I disagree. It's too early to combine cards. After all, Black only has one more person than us."

The dissenting voice came from Ballard, a man in his mid-50s and a guard in the White zone.

Being trained, he had superior combat skills compared to other participants, but in Whiteblack, weapon cards were everything.

'He must have good cards.'

Marco wanted even more to combine the cards.

"Alright, let's decide by vote. Breeze, Atria, and I are in favor of combining cards."

Atria, a 16-year-old girl, raised her hand.

"I haven't said anything yet."

Although she didn't have high-grade cards, if she agreed too easily to combine them, she would be underestimated.

"Well then. How will it be? Team or individual?"

Atria lowered her head sullenly.

"Team."

"You were going to agree anyway…"

Marco sighed deeply and looked at Ballard.

"Alright, it's settled. Three votes in favor. One against."

Ballard's face twisted in frustration.

"Damn vote."

"Hey, if you ignore White's rules…"

"I know. I'm a guard. My job is to enforce the rules and beat up anyone who doesn't follow them. Don't try to teach me, kid."

In the peaceful White zone, Marco had good relationships with the residents.

'I must return alive.'

Thinking of his family waiting at home made it easy for him to be shameless as a father.

"So let's share our cards now. We'll redistribute them according to roles: combat, support, exploration…"

As Marco rummaged through his pockets and spoke, Shirone landed with the noise of spatial movement.

"What the…!"

All of White's participants jumped up.

"How is he alive?"

Marco felt like he had seen a ghost since his Periscope card confirmed there were only four White survivors.

Shirone held out his Seeker card and spoke.

"I'm Black. I came with a proposal."

"A Seeker…"

Breeze's eyes sparkled as she looked at the job card while Marco's mind raced.

'A Seeker appeared? So it's 4 vs. 5? If Shirone joins White, we'll not only form a team but also gain numerical superiority. We've won.'

Shirone asked with a sneer,

"Why are you so surprised? Did you think I'd die activating the harmony of the law?"

"No way. I told you it wouldn't be particularly dangerous. We were looking for you too."

Everyone knew it was a lie but couldn't admit it honestly.

"Forget the past and join us. If you do, victory will be on White's side…"

"I'm Black."

Shirone interrupted.

"As I said, I'm here with a proposal. If you accept it, I won't harm you."

Ballard's eyes filled with killing intent.

"Hey, kid. Do you think being a Seeker makes you invincible? This is a battlefield where the law harmonizes. We can act like Black too."

Marco stopped Ballard.

"Wait. He might become an ally; there's no need to fight. How about deciding by vote?"

This time Shirone snorted.

"Vote? What nonsense."

As photons from the Photon Cannon floated above Shirone's palm, the participants' faces turned pale.

"M-magic…?"

When Shirone threw the vibrating light sphere away like trash, it exploded with a tremendous sound against a rock.

'How can a Seeker use magic? Is it a weapon card? No, he didn't draw any cards. Then…'

He had been a Magician from the start.

"Did you kill the Black team?"

There was no other reason why four Black participants died simultaneously unless it was something significant like that.

"Yes. It doesn't matter if it's Black or White. If you don't accept my proposal, your four will end up the same."

At Shirone's chilling threat, Marco fell silent while Breeze asked fearfully,

"What proposal?"

Shirone extended his hand and took a step forward.

"Hand over all your cards."

Labyrinth Andre-World No. 1.

'A dragon. A real dragon.'

Although he had seen Sea Dragon Kaios on Galliant Island before, this was his first time conversing so closely with one.

'And it's Karatorusa.'

Known as the king of dragons, Karatorusa was believed not to exist anymore in Shirone's world.

"When no one else can bear it, I will bear it."

Only these words remained from when he supposedly entered eternal sleep in an unreachable remote place.

"Karatorusa, we have found one who remembers reincarnation."

The Thunder Dragon that had dragged Shirone out of the cave landed next to Karatorusa and spoke.

'Definitely different.'

Unlike other dragons, its sleek body and sharp beak resembled a sword.

"It is indeed so. But this likely belongs to the first reincarnation cycle, Blitz."

Shirone's eyes widened in surprise.

'Blitz? That dragon?'

While Karatorusa became legendary after entering eternal sleep, Blitz was the most famous dragon in Shirone's world.

Thunderwing Dragon Blitz.

Among the top-tier dragons known as the Twelve Apostles for their combat prowess, Blitz was considered unrivaled in strength.

Supersonic flight and lightning strikes symbolized Blitz; Shirone had imagined those scenes through countless artists' works.

"…I see. The first incident."

Blitz absorbed Karatorusa's experience and looked down at Shirone with cold eyes.

"A drop of poison burned by time's flow. It must be removed before it spreads further."

Although Shirone had only saved a few primitive people, it would eventually lead to a completely different history.

"You understand," Shirone said.

This meant that the dragons fully understood the concept of a reset.

Karatorusa spoke, "You who come from the future, we are the apostles who guard time in the cycle of reincarnation. But because of you, hundreds of millions who would exist in the future will disappear."

If some transcendent being tried to change the system through a reset, the dragons were the antithesis to that.

"I accept responsibility. But I can't ignore people in danger."

That was Shirone's Karr.

"We can't delay any longer. For the hundreds of millions you might erase again, you must disappear."

Plasma breath began to spread chillingly between Blitz's clenched teeth.

"No, no."

Mitochondrial Eve stood in front of Shirone, spreading her arms wide.

"Step aside, one who remembers reincarnation. If we don't eliminate him, your blueprint will become useless."

Mitochondrial Eve's foundational events were essential information for the apostles guarding time.

"I can do it."

Mitochondrial Eve was doing her best to speak.

"Shirone… can do it. He can stop it."

"He can stop it?"

Karatorusa, lost in thought for a moment, turned to Shirone and widened his eyes.

"Ugh!"

The feeling of his mind filling with light was similar to Eye's Clear technique but on a different level of intensity.

'My memories are being forcibly opened.'

It was a form of mental hacking, and Shirone desperately blocked Karatorusa's access.

'With the Ultima System…!'

As he dismantled the initial dragon language composed of binary code, Karatorusa's head shot up to the sky.

"Ugh!"

"Karatorusa!"

Blitz shouted sharply and created a sphere of lightning in front of Shirone.

"What are you doing…!"

Karatorusa's voice stopped Blitz.

"The ultimate language."

The dragons turned their heads to where Karatorusa was looking up at the sky with his enormous eyes wide open.

"Indeed… is that so?"

If dragon language was the perfect signal that implemented the world, then the Ultima System was the higher signal that implemented that dragon language.

"So that's how you came here. You used the ultimate language to break the seal of time."

To be precise, it was opening Goffin's prison of space-time with the Ultima System.

"It can be done, human. If your signal is added, we can correct the distorted history."

"How?"

Karatorusa's explanation was bizarre.

"Unless time before now is not sealed, we cannot reverse events that have already occurred."

This was World No. 1.

"In that case, we must forcibly correct history from an irreversible state. We must predict all future variables and eliminate them."

"Is that… possible?"

"With the computational power of the apostles of time and dragons, it is possible. Approximately 673 years from now, history will flow as if you never came."

It was the time needed for a drop of poison to be purified.

"The problem is that it must happen simultaneously rather than following the flow of time. Therefore, we need a master code, an ultimate language. You must directly permeate the world and apply our derived results."

"It's an immortal function."

It meant infinitely expanding one's self and inputting new variables that transcend space-time through the Ultima System.

The insight he had vaguely guessed at some point…

'This is how to break Andre's seal.'

Even if exploring 19,000 worlds simultaneously, each Shirone was an entity with unique life.

'I don't want to die.'

Therefore, what was needed to break Andre's seal was something that would willingly make him sacrifice himself.

"Human, it may not be you now, but seek me in your world in the distant future. Blitz will guide you."

Blitz met Shirone's eyes and nodded with unwavering determination.

"Sacrifice yourself. For the hundreds of millions who will disappear because of you, give yourself up."

For those who will live in the future…

"For the future…"

Mitochondrial Eve's eyes filled with tears.

'I can wait. I can endure.'

It wasn't an eternal farewell.

But she knew how long she would have to wait until they met again.

Possibly tens of thousands of years…

The immense longing she would have to bear alone for eons until Shirone appeared again…

"You must choose now, human."

Taking a deep breath, Shirone looked up at the sunset sky and fell into thought.

"I…"

Hundreds of eyes awaited his decision.
