Chapter 1010: Shadow of God (2)

From the sky, the mountain appeared as a colossal ember, twinkling with hundreds of thousands of lights like stars.

Amy exhaled calmly in a temperature so hot that it could burn any living creature to death just from the heat.

"Yahweh's assassination organization."

Certainly, Gultan's ability, Psychocraft, would have been more lethal to Shirone than to herself.

"Maya."

Pushing aside numerous thoughts, Amy looked towards the forest-dense mountain across from her.

"Is it already too late?"

If the one who attacked Maya was on a similar level to Gultan, then hundreds of opportunities for death would have passed.

"Huh?"

Her senses flared red.

"What's that?"

Deep within the forest, she detected unnatural tremors — not of natural origin.

It meant the fight was still ongoing.

Amy immediately cast a space teleportation spell towards that location.

Mio, whose arm had been severed, was trying to comprehend the recent events without even thinking about stopping the bleeding.

"What just happened?"

The possibility of her arm being severed without her even realizing it was unfathomable.

"I'm a division commander. And that weakling couldn't even stand up a second ago."

Mio turned to look at Cayden, unable to explain the vast disparity even with her understanding.

"What are you?"

Cayden couldn't respond.

"Why can't I move my arm?" His right arm remained outstretched horizontally, fixed in space as if cast in a mold.

"Lower it! I'm telling you to lower it!"

As he focused his consciousness, the sensation of the handle and the weight of the blade became vividly clear.

"It’s not a sensory issue."

Yet, the arm still wouldn’t move, and he felt as if he had forgotten how to issue commands.

"The problem is in the brain. It must be related to the tearing sound I heard just before."

"Don't think about it." Like his right arm was encased in a cast, Cayden turned away from it.

"Protecting Maya comes first." As he glimpsed Mio, his sword began to wildly slice through the air.

Cayden remained calm.

His senses were alive, but his brain wasn't processing them.

"What are you doing?"

Startled by Mio's question, Cayden looked at his own arm, moving on its own accord, in shock.

"What is this..."

As he pushed his consciousness in, his thoughts dissipated and his body lunged forward.

"Kyaaaa!"

Mio's remaining arm was severed, and a terrible scream embroidered the mountains.

"You bastard!"

With her arms drastically shortened, she opened her mouth wide, baring her teeth.

"I'll chew your heart out!"

Cayden struggled to regain control.

And when he finally regained control over his body, he felt all strength draining from him.

"Damn it!"

Mio charged headfirst.

Even a simple headbutt from a division commander could crush bones.

Simultaneously, Cayden’s right arm thrust his sword into the ground, raising a thick wall of steel.

Thump!

Mio’s pupils violently shook.

"Awk!"

She kneeled, clutching her head as the steel wall cracked.

Cayden then realized.

"Two brains."

It wasn't certain, but it seemed like the networks between his left and right brains had severed.

"The brain controlling my right arm isn't sending signals to me anymore. That’s why it’s uncontrollable."

If that hypothesis was correct, then his complete focus on the strike earlier could explain why his thoughts evaporated.

"If the brain that doesn’t send me signals momentarily took over my body…"

Cayden wouldn’t receive any signals.

"Hehe. Hehehe."

What does it matter?

If he could be free of the cursed fate of the Cross Star, even if only one arm was free.

"Maya."

If he could hold her hand.

"Now I can finally..."

Before he could finish, Mio, who had been crouched, lunged towards Maya.

"Kiiii!"

Having hit her head against the iron wall, it was a fatal blow.

Then at least part of the strategy laid out by Catacomb should be fulfilled.

"If I can scratch even a little bit of Yahweh’s heart by killing Maya…" Cayden shouted.

"Maya!"

But the brain he could control was trapped in the fate of the Cross Star, immobile.

"Dieeee!"

Mio’s chest split vertically, and vicious ribs sprouted like jaws.

Simultaneously, a flash fell.

Amy, who had arrived via spatial teleportation, instantly assessed the situation and launched herself into action.

"It’s serious!"

Her calculations from stored memories indicated she was 0.43 seconds short of saving Maya.

"Kyah!"

Maya, with reflexes like a sensitive civilian, screamed belatedly.

"Kekeke! Too late!"

Mio, with her grotesque innards fully exposed, clenched her numerous ribs and pounced on her.

Smack!

Amy's stride halted.

Mio's body split in two as she passed beside Maya and crashed into the ground.

"Huuk! Huk!"

Maya, holding her hands up like a puppy, slowly opened her eyes, realizing the anomaly.

Cayden, in a stance of lifting a reverse-gripped longsword, glared at Mio.

Amy’s mouth dropped open in astonishment.

"Magic Sword Skill, 'Frostbite.'"

A lethal technique in itself, but compared to his school days, his sword speed was in another dimension.

"Cayden, what happened to you?"

There were many things she wanted to say, but something else had to be addressed first.

"Dreaming was once a luxury, but now I can."

With determined eyes, Cayden stepped forward, and Maya, with a smile, reciprocated.

"Thank you, Cayden. Without you, I..."

"Maya."

Cayden cut her off, her shoulders flinching as her face paled.

"I’ll confess properly. From the first time we met, you were the only one in my heart."

"Hey, Cayden."

Amy interjected, but Cayden ignored her.

"It’s okay if you don’t like me. Just know that I love you. Then I can do anything. I’m not afraid of hell or death." Maya couldn’t speak, overwhelmed by Cayden’s earnest words and gaze.

'Why is he doing this? Is he mocking me?'

Had she not seen how much Cayden had sacrificed to save her, she would have turned away from him.

"May I ask you one thing?" Cayden smiled warmly.

"Will you sing for me just once? That's my only wish. Of course, if you don’t want to..."

"Hey! Cayden!"

Finally, Cayden turned his head.

"Why keep interrupting? Can’t you see what I’m doing?"

"It’s too visible! What are you doing to Maya? Your hand! Your hand!"

"Huh?"

Cayden blinked momentarily, tracing from his shoulder to where his right hand was.

It was on Maya's chest.

For about a second, Cayden watched his hand’s actions, then his eyes widened in horror.

"Aaaaah! What is this, dammit! What the hell is this?"

"How should I know, you lunatic! Just take your hand off!"

"Oh God."

Cayden stomped his feet, but couldn’t think of how to move his hand.

"Argh!"

Finally, after pulling his wrist away, the dreadful incident was resolved.

"Why? Why in the world?"

He had never made such a mistake in his life; how could his hand commit such a foolish act?

Only his brain would know.

"Maya!"

Turning his head, Cayden saw Maya avoiding his gaze and frowned.

"It’s over. It’s all over."

Was the fate of the Cross Star so cruel that it wouldn’t even allow his first and last confession?

Imagining how she must view him now felt like falling into an abyss.

"I'm sorry, Maya. This wasn’t my intention. I don’t even know what my hand was doing."

It sounded like a typical pervert's excuse, but Maya, realizing his genuine regret, nodded.

"Okay, I understand."

That didn’t mean she was ready to close the distance with Cayden again.

Depressed, Cayden's eyes gleamed as he drew his sword with his left hand.

"Damn it! Then I'll punish myself!" Amy and Maya both shouted.

"Wait! Cayden!"

But before Cayden could do anything, his right hand automatically struck his face.

"Ouch!"

Struck by his own fist, Cayden lost his reason.

"Aaaaah!"

He threw the sword with his left hand and began to fight his right hand, and the contest was evenly matched.

But ultimately, the right hand overpowered the left, twisting it forcefully outward.

The two women watched in disbelief as Cayden finally admitted defeat.

"Aaaaah! Alright! I lost! I admit it!"

He may be stubborn, but what could he possibly do against his own right hand?

Maya murmured.

"I knew he was strange from the start..."

"Damn it! Damn it!"

Powerlessly slumped on the ground, Cayden stared fixedly at the sword lying beside him.

"Should I really cut it off?"

Amy said.

"Come on. You don’t know the whole situation, but you did protect Maya. Thanks to that right arm."

"Damn! If you keep saying that, how can I protect Maya's side? Do you know what this hand might do?"

Maya realized the real reason Cayden was disappointed.

"I'll tell you." She approached Cayden and sat beside him.

"It's not sincere. Honestly, I don't know what's causing this, but the Cayden I know isn't like that. So, I'll remind you every time this happens."

"Ma, Maya."

"Thank you, Cayden. Because of you, I can keep singing. I'll also grant your earlier request."

Cayden bit his lower lip, but he couldn't hold back the tears.

'He's surprisingly tender-hearted.' Of course, I know.

How much Cayden had dreamed of this moment, how he had screamed in madness at his cursed fate.

'Congratulations.'

Even if it was just his right arm, it was the first thing he had ever gained by tearing apart his fate with his own strength.

How much time had passed?

After the emotional storm had passed, the three of them sat side by side on a hill, watching the city.

"Anyway..."

The sight was so overwhelming that even Cayden, who had fought fiercely, felt drained.

"Incredible."

The gray space created by the Taiji spread towards the horizon.

'What could be happening inside there... I can't even imagine.'

Amy said.

"Time, space, laws, and minds. All of that is endlessly cycling in a collapsed state. The moment you enter there, your thinking will be destroyed, and even your identity will be lost."

They were beyond the level of being able to interfere.

The space of the Taiji was like a waterfall, with fierce energy rapidly flowing backwards.

Shirone and Giyorgi faced each other.

Just 2 meters apart, a tiny core was generating the power to swallow the world.

Giyorgi said.

"How long do you plan to hold out, Yahweh? You know. The Taiji is endlessly expanding."

If neither stepped back, it would eventually engulf the whole world.

"The odds are fifty-fifty. I wouldn't stop you from betting the whole of humanity against me..."

Yahweh wouldn't do that.

"Giyorugi."

Shirone spoke for the first time.

"You seem to overestimate yourself too much. Whatever being you are, you're just a shadow. You can't surpass me."

"Maybe so."

Giyorugi's eyes flashed as he bent forward, revealing a demonic face.

"But what if I do this?"

As the power of evil grew, the Taiji's space, which had been at a stalemate, began to expand again.

"Kekeke! Will you take it or will you back down? Are you, Yahweh, going to gamble with humanity?"

"That's why you won't make it."

Amid Giyorugi's contorted expression, Shirone dramatically twisted the distribution of power.

"This is what you call Taiji?"

As the light's energy weakened, the dark energy began to infiltrate at a terrifying speed.

'Twist it again...'

Harnessing the force pushed by the darkness, he rotated it, and the speed of light increased significantly.

Giyorugi realized.

"Damn it!"

The endlessly cycling light and darkness of the Taiji.

'Using the power of darkness to amplify the light. As a result, Yahweh is faster than me.'

"If you want to play this game, bring someone at least as good as me." In the fast-spinning game of yin and yang, the light finally bit the tail of the darkness.
