Chapter 795:

“Arian Shirone.”

That was the name of the only person in this world currently capable of wielding Unified Force.

‘Is it really possible?’

The moment when the universe resets into an activated state—also known as the Planck era—Unified Force was the collective sum of all known laws presumed to exist during that time.

‘None of them will be able to match him.’

This wasn’t to downplay the strength of Bodhidharma and Hyega, but Unified Force was poison to seekers of truth.

‘Not even the most righteous Buddha could destroy it. But what about a Regulation Exception?’

Ranked 10th in the Council of Ten, her level was not comparable to those at the top, but the universe inside Mortasinger’s mind was entirely different.

“There is no Yahweh inside me.”

Landing on the ground, Mortasinger charged toward Shirone, casting her magic.

“Hidden Piece.”

As ether waves spread out, a giant mechanical cube dropped down over Shirone’s head.

‘I can’t dodge it.’

Such was the law—an equivalent exchange.

“Gah!”

Shirone looked on in disbelief as his right hand vanished.

“What…?!”

Suddenly, the world fragmented like a puzzle, and his senses twisted into chaos.

‘It’s not the world that’s breaking.’

It was Shirone’s body—academically referred to as his schema—that was being broken apart.

“It’s begun.”

Glancing at Hyega’s condition, Bodhidharma watched Shirone, whose body had been twisted like a Rubik’s Cube.

The Regulation Exception: Hidden Piece.

Using ether waves as a base, Mortasinger could split a target into 98 cube segments. By removing a single block, the puzzle becomes “unsolvable,” scrambling the space like a cube.

‘Istar… that’s what this is!’

Shirone recalled both the mechanical structures he saw in Heaven and the cube puzzles from the magic academy.

With one eye shifted to where his ankle should’ve been, he focused his awareness, and the blocks began to realign.

‘The nerves are still intact.’

He didn’t have the clinical clarity of a sword master’s schema.

But the human blueprint—where each part of the body should go—was embedded in his subconscious.

“Hohoho! It’s useless!”

By the time Shirone managed to get one of his eyes back into place, his lower body had become even more of a mess.

While Mortasinger scrambled the puzzle, Shirone steadily pieced it back together—his thinking accelerating.

‘Got it!’

Blocks surged toward Mortasinger like waves, regaining their original form.

‘What is he thinking…?’

Shirone’s brain, honed over a lifetime of cognitive discipline, worked at the highest level in the world.

“Give me back my hand.”

Though he’d reassembled the puzzle, Mortasinger still held onto the block containing his right hand.

‘How do I undo it?’

As expected of a Regulation Exception, there was some condition—but before he could figure it out, Mortasinger swapped pieces again.

From the 98 fragments surrounding Shirone, the section above both his eyes disappeared.

“It’s over!”

Just as Mortasinger declared victory, a Photon Cannon shot toward her.

Right before Shirone’s brain—now part of a Hidden Piece—sank into darkness, he emitted a unique brainwave.

‘You won’t be able to dodge this.’

The speed of the Photon Cannon froze Mortasinger in a state of zero movement.

‘It’s the ultimate speed.’

There was barely a moment left before impact, but Shirone fell into darkness, trusting the law that had already been set.

“Kyaaaa!”

In a panic, Mortasinger released the Hidden Piece, twisted the space with ether waves, and barely deflected the Photon Cannon.

‘As expected.’

As Shirone’s vision returned, he saw Mortasinger collapsed, clutching her shoulder.

‘Same ether wave, but her realm is shallower than Seong-eum’s.’

Even a non-believer couldn’t twist the laws of the world at will.

‘Rian would’ve dodged it.’

Rian’s convictions bent the very rules of existence.

“Shirone!”

A shout came from the stage. Shirone’s Spirit Zone flared wide.

‘Am I reaching my limit?’

Even mid-battle, he’d been monitoring the area. The guards could no longer protect the stage—it was open now.

“Kyaaa! They’re coming! They’re coming!”

As immortals charged forward, Jaestin stood to block them while beauty pageant contestants screamed.

“What the hell are you doing?! You took all our money! You’re supposed to protect us!”

It didn’t matter how loud they screamed—the guards, utterly crushed, couldn’t help them now.

“Get it together! We’ve got to protect ourselves!”

With Maya’s support, Pony summoned hydropower and fought fiercely, but the Norin's group magic overwhelmed her.

“Why should I protect myself?! I worked hard to look this good for someone else! Get up and do your job!”

As immortals stormed the stage, chaos erupted, and even Jaestin couldn’t hold them back.

‘These guys are seriously strong…’

They weren’t just pageant terrorists—they could pull off a national-level attack.

“Gyaaaaah!”

As the Kergoin raised their curved blades and screamed, Langi launched herself into the air.

“No!”

Langi gritted her teeth.

‘I know schema magic too…’

But as she flew in for a kick, the Kergoin’s torso bent like rubber.

“...Huh?”

Even at point-blank range, she couldn’t follow the movement. Her face froze.

“Pathetic earthborn.”

His glaive swept like a flash, and Langi was thrown back violently.

When the Kergoin looked ahead with a frown, Shirone stood shielding her.

“You okay?”

“Huh? I-I think so…”

It had only been a few days since she’d last seen him, but the cold intensity in his eyes was something she’d never encountered before.

“Hey! Shirone!”

Pony shouted.

“You said I should stop guys like this! This isn’t some thug—it’s a war criminal!”

“Well, you stopped him, didn’t you?”

Gritting her teeth, Pony raised a massive wall of water that crashed down in a torrent.

“Now it’s your turn.”

As nearly 200 enemies stormed the stage, Shirone dashed forward.

His Flash Movement burst in a wide net, instantly shattering the immortals’ formation.

“Whoa…”

People who hadn’t even been scratched by security forces were suddenly collapsing en masse.

Flashes rained down, and their numbers dropped from 200 to 100… then to 50.

‘Pony, you lied.’

Langi watched, wide-eyed, as Shirone tore through enemy lines like living lightning.

‘You said you couldn’t deny it…’

When Shirone was a mage, he radiated the most beautiful light in the world.

“Lady Mortasinger, we should retreat.”

Bodhidharma approached with Hyega leaning on his shoulder.

“I don’t know why he’s here, but that’s Hexa—whom even other Council of Ten members couldn’t handle. We can’t win.”

“Hmph! Who said I wanted to win?”

Being the lowest-ranked in the Council of Ten, she didn’t care about realms or magical strength.

“Just kill those pretty things already!”

All the beautiful things in the world…

“Hidden Piece!”

The Regulation Exception activated again, and the entire stage fractured into 98 blocks.

In the middle of the scrambled puzzle, the block containing the contestants was drawn toward her.

“I’ll burn those faces off!”

As she charged forward with flames in both hands, Shirone's eyes, watching from afar, sparked sharply.

Boom!

The fire exploded across the stage, engulfing the contestants.

“Kyaaa! Somebody help...!”

That was the first second.

‘Time Vibration!’

And when that one second began to tremble endlessly, it was Mortasinger who now clutched her head and screamed.

“Wh-What’s happening?!”

As the repetition of a single second began to fold within itself, the world fragmented into countless blocks in the contestants’ eyes.

Massive waves of information danced and swirled until, slowly, the world began to reassemble into form.

‘Found it. The final piece.’

With his back to Mortasinger, Shirone’s body rippled with a shockwave and flipped perspective—back becoming front.

“Who are you?!”

If beauty was the root of her trauma, then the condition to release the Regulation Exception was to reveal the face.

A Wind Cutter flew toward Mortasinger’s face, and as it made contact, the Hidden Piece unraveled, restoring the scenery.

“No! Don’t look at me!”

Losing the will to fight, she took off toward the mountain, Bodhidharma and Hye-ga following closely behind.

“Now! Capture them!”

As the recovering security forces began arresting the terrorists, Shirone looked toward the mountain.

‘Immortal Community… Did they lose their place in the world?’

With Anke Ra gone, it must have felt like being abandoned by their god.

‘It was like they no longer knew why they were fighting.’

Except for one.

‘Given the sheer strength of her ability, she’s at least on par with the Council of Ten… But why would she do all this?’

The more he thought about it, the less sense it made.

The evening papers ran special editions for the first time in a while.

Every major newspaper splashed headlines about the terrorist attack at the World Beauty Pageant, and public attention was fixated on the event.

“Whew. What a mess.”

When Jaestin returned to the guild, Shirone—who had been eating with the other members—looked up and asked,

“How’d it go? What did the organizers say?”

“They’re continuing the pageant. Apparently, the higher-ups were satisfied with how most of the terrorists were captured.”

Shirone was dumbfounded.

“But one’s still out there! And she was the ringleader! They’re still going to continue?”

“Because it’s profitable. You know how it is.”

“What about the contestants?”

For the first time, Jaestin showed signs of exhaustion.

“My ears are still ringing. Some want therapy, others want prescriptions for anxiety meds...”

Shirone didn’t need to see it to imagine it.

“Are they really going through with it?”

“What choice do they have? This isn’t just about picking the prettiest girl. Their slogan is all about making the world more peaceful through beauty. If anyone bails now, the backlash will be brutal.”

Shirone thought back to the protestors.

“There are plenty who already criticize the competition. This would be the perfect chance for them to pile on.”

“Exactly. That’s why the show goes on but...”

Jaestin sighed.

“They insist you handle their security. Officially. They said, ‘If that handsome guy’s not guarding us, we’re not doing it.’”

“I don’t really have a choice, do I? I’ve got someone I care about in there, too. Count me in.”

Jaestin shook her head.

“No, I mean they want you specifically. Not as part of the team—they want you personally watching over them. That means being in the waiting room, the bathroom, the showers, even the dining hall.”

Shirone’s eyes widened.

“What?! That’s insane! I’m a guy! I can’t just agree to that! And tomorrow’s the swimsuit segment!”

“Then go tell them yourself! They were sobbing like little girls, calling for their moms! They don’t trust the guards or anyone else! They’re in total panic!”

Shirone let out a groan.

“I’ll… try to explain things.”

“Good luck with that. But I doubt it’ll help. Word’s already out. Everyone in the pageant knows who you are. Who’s going to say no to the world’s greatest mage guarding them?”

Shirone already felt a headache coming on.

“They’ll all be sleeping in the bunker tonight, but the real problem is tomorrow when the event resumes. What’ll you do? Even the guards are hoping you’ll step in. From what I heard, these terrorists are part of the group that unleashed demons across the world.”

It was an organization capable of crushing elite special forces from every nation.

“...Fine. I’ll do it.”

Even if the participating countries took swift action, for now, Shirone was the only one who could defend the stage.

“Hey, kid.”

Eyepatch placed a hand on Shirone’s shoulder.

“Uh… any chance you need an assistant?”

When Shirone turned, he saw the other guild members staring at him with sparkling eyes.

“No. I don’t.”

Shirone answered firmly and brushed Eyepatch’s hand off.
