Chapter 742:

The planet that Great Star and Shirone looked down upon was divided into five major continents.

First, there was the Arctic, where the Ivory Tower was located.

Below it was a continent that included more than half of the world's 70 countries.

The continent was divided into the northern, central, southern, and Middle Eastern regions. In the northern continent, the Kashan and Gustav Empires, part of the Tri-Imperial System, occupied the west and east, respectively, sharing a border.

The central continent, with the highest density of countries, had 21 kingdoms, including Shirone's homeland, the Kingdom of Tormia.

The southern continent had relatively larger territories than the central continent but was less powerful.

However, the Kingdom of Iron, located at the southernmost tip, was a powerful nation representing the south as one of the Seven Star Kingdoms.

The only kingdom in the Middle Eastern continent, Paras, was also one of the Seven Star Kingdoms, sharing control of the Akkad Desert with Kashan.

Crossing the sea from the Middle East led to the second-largest landmass in the world, known as the East.

In the north of the East was the Jincheon Empire, part of the Tri-Imperial System, with six kingdoms below it fiercely competing for the second position.

Crossing the equator past the island nation below the East led to the South, which was also Kangnan's homeland.

The South had 14 kingdoms, but their control was weak, with thousands of tribes maintaining their unique traditions.

Lastly, there was the Antarctic, the coldest place in the world, where no life could survive.

And now.

The grand project that had been steadily progressing since Anke Ra opened the gates of the Great Purification was approaching its final moment.

The 3,600 altars installed in the 69 countries of the world, excluding the Kingdom of Corona in the Arctic, were about to open.

The Kingdom of Iron.

The Kingdom of Iron, one of the Seven Star Kingdoms, had a painful memory of Marsha's Parrot Mercenary Corps falling into banditry.

But that was now in the past.

Marsha, who had rescued Shirone from Under Code, activated her contract with Uorin, and the Parrot Mercenary Corps was now a distinguished S-class mercenary group receiving special treatment in the Kingdom of Iron.

"What on earth are they doing?"

Freeman, looking down at the altar from the cliff, rested his chin in thought.

Although he was no longer the second-in-command in terms of skill, he still served as Marsha's deputy.

A sleek man of medium height reported.

"According to our investigation, over a thousand altars have been discovered worldwide, with more than 200 in the Kingdom of Iron alone."

And they had just been completed.

"Is that possible?"

Even a national project would take at least a year to build such massive altars simultaneously.

The civil engineering work alone, which involved completely leveling a mountain, was beyond human capability.

"Could it be the power of magic...?"

It was the closest answer to the truth, but Freeman still couldn't readily accept it.

"Perhaps a god could do it."

As his pale face, devoid of eyebrows, twisted, the members gripped their weapons nervously.

"Shall we infiltrate?"

Since Marsha had given the direct order, they had to go, even if a god resided there.

"We're going in."

As soon as Freeman gave the order, the members lowered their stances like birds of prey and rushed towards the cliff.

"Stop."

The voice was quiet, but the 20 members with superhuman senses halted without a moment's hesitation.

"Commander."

Clay Marsha, the commander of the Parrot Mercenary Corps, walked through the forest, holding a pipe.

"What's the sudden matter?"

Freeman asked, but Marsha only squinted and looked down at the altar below the cliff.

"I was short-sighted. This isn't something we can handle."

The ominous rumors circulating in high society were pushing the Kingdom of Iron into an end-of-the-century atmosphere, but the mercenary group's value was always about money.

A member asked.

"Commander, what do you mean? Is there anything in this world we can't handle?"

It was unusual for Marsha, an excellent strategist, to withdraw a plan midway.

"We received urgent intelligence. The special forces of the Kingdom of Iron, investigating altars in other regions, have been annihilated."

"Annihilated?"

It was extremely rare for the forces of Iron to be annihilated within their own country, except in a war with another nation.

"Who on earth..."

Just as Freeman was about to speak, Marsha frowned and pointed below the cliff.

An old man with a hunched back was walking towards the altar, leaning on a cane, on a cleanly paved path.

When Freeman, who was observing closely, looked back at Marsha, she swallowed hard, her white neck trembling.

"Withdraw immediately."

"What's going on?"

"No time to explain. That isn't human."

"If it's not human, then what is it?"

"I don't know."

It was an honest impression.

When Marsha had encountered Miro's skills in Under Code, she thought no human could reach a deeper level.

"But that..."

Surpassed Miro.

The reason she was sure was that she felt as if all the scenery of the world was being drawn into the frail old man.

"No matter how great Miro is..."

He couldn't be heavier than the old man.

"An uninvited guest has arrived."

The top-ranked member of the Ten Elders, Veron.

When the shiny eyes of Amita Banya turned towards the cliff, the members finally realized.

'We're going to die.'

There was no falsehood in Marsha's words.

"Run!"

In an instant, the entire Parrot Mercenary Corps disappeared from the cliff, and Veron clicked his tongue.

"Young people these days lack spirit."

Veron struck the ground with his cane again.

"How long are you going to keep leeching off an old man?"

Veron, who had reached the lowest level of the altar, sat down and fanned himself.

"Come to think of it, I've lived a long time."

All the executives of the Ten Elders had lived for over ten thousand years, but no one knew Veron's exact lifespan.

'Why can't I go anywhere?'

What remained in this world?

As Veron closed his mouth like a toothless old man and fell into thought, a red light flared up before his eyes.

"Chairman."

The seventh-ranked member of the Ten Elders, Apostle Banya Shura, appeared.

She had a sleek body like a snake, and her black eyes became long and cold when dealing with lies.

"The preparations to open the altar are complete."

"......"

"We built the altar according to Anke Ra’s instructions, but there is much talk even within the society of the immortals."

Anke Ra accepted Nane to become a god, and Nane accepted Anke Ra to save all beings.

"We wanted Ra to become a god and make this world unique. But now..."

A great void was approaching.

"Some attain enlightenment, some reincarnate, and now someone wants to close it all."

For Veron, this world was everything.

"As always, we must find meaning. For an immortal, the meaning of life is like the life of a mortal."

"Then what meaning does Nane's merit have?"

"None."

Veron stood up, leaning on his cane.

"So now I intend to create it."

As Veron climbed the steps of the altar, he asked Shura, who followed him.

"What do you think of fate?"

"A delusion created by the harmony of time... perhaps? Nothing escapes causality."

Veron nodded.

"The human mind is also just a grain of sand swept away by the flow of time. Fish swimming upstream don't change the flow of the sea."

Shura listened attentively.

"If you plant a seed in the present, it will sprout in the future. Humans mistake this for a great ability..."

Veron caught his breath midway up the altar.

"In fact, it's already determined the moment the seed is planted. It's not about changing it. It's just flowing that way."

We don't create the future; the already created future comes to us.

"If human free will truly had the power to change something..."

Veron turned his head just before reaching the top.

"Shouldn't it be able to destroy the future, escaping the delusion of changing it?"

Even if you plant a seed, it should be able to not sprout.

"That is truly living the future."

"Chairman."

Even Shura, who had reached the level of Apostle Banya, couldn't grasp Amita's intentions.

"The master of this world wants to close it. Then it should be accepted."

Veron looked up at the sky.

"But it's ultimately human will."

Attachment to the present world, the immortals.

Perhaps this was the meaning of his existence, having been part of this world for countless ages.

"Justice."

Veron thought.

Amita's Incarnation Technique - Iron Pole.

"Ugh!"

Feeling the concentration of the law, Shura jumped straight down from the top of the high altar to the ground.

'Tremendous concentration of thought.'

Veron's wide-open jaw trembled, and a hoarse voice escaped roughly.

"Haah!"

As the laws of the world twisted, they began to gather at the tip of Veron's cane.

His will would be planted here with such strong binding force that it wouldn't be swept away by the waves of time.

"Foolish, Amita."

A giant voice spread from the sky.

Then all the clouds in the sky gathered and transformed into Nane's face.

"Ugh!"

As Shura activated Apostle Banya's Incarnation Technique, numerous crosses formed a spherical shield.

Gestalt of Union - God's Strangulation

Just as one might see meaningful shapes in wall stains, her law combines falsehoods to create phenomena close to reality.

'It will never be destroyed! Even Miro couldn't break it!'

Even though it was a world specialized for her abilities in Under Code.

Do not be afraid.

Sermon - Truth

A white sword, like a blank canvas, shot from Nane's forehead at the speed of light, striking God's Strangulation.

"What...!"

The meticulously assembled crosses disintegrated like a chemical reaction, revealing Shura's face starkly.

'This is Nane.'

The nemesis closest to righteousness in this universe, having swallowed Anke Ra’s dream.

"Ah..."

As Shura fell on her backside, trembling, Nane's gaze shifted to Veron.

There was no visible phenomenon, but the iron pole was already completed, nailed into space-time.

Do you think I cannot break it?

Veron's eyes, looking up at the sky, held both pride and reverence.

"It is your world, but there is no way to deny just one like me."

Nane's karma score was not perfect.

It will be swept away eventually.

No matter how many resets it withstands, it is destined to be eroded by the waves of time.

The only thing that could endure was Hexa.

"Then just accept it. I merely want to know if the human spirit is truly so insignificant."

This was Veron's final question to the world.

"Even if everything returns to nothing and no records remain, fighting to the end is the life of an immortal."

Shura suddenly realized.

'So, before the altar opens...'

He had driven the iron pole into the only point where there was still room for reversal, creating an immutable standard.

'Even if hundreds of futures are mixed, the beginning is always here.'

Veron had precisely targeted the 0.1% of Nane created by Shirone.

Perhaps nothing had been decided yet, Shura thought fleetingly.
