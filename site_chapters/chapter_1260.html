Chapter 1260:

A tearing sound came from Kitra’s throat.

“Kiiiii!”

The cause Shirone was trying to alter for In was completely opposite of what the god desired.

‘Cause revision! Cause revision! Cause revision!’

The god has no heart, but perhaps in that moment, it may have felt sorrow—not because it was angry, but because it couldn't even feel anger.

‘Tachyon detected.’

A being capable of manipulating imaginary time now existed in two instances—meaning there were two omniscient gods.

And the clash of the two, whether intentional or not, could only lead to humanity’s destruction.

‘Objective unachievable.’

Who would win?

‘At this rate, the error cannot be eliminated.’

The side uninterested in humanity's destruction—cold and mechanical—finally made a decision.

To In…

A new command was issued.

—Die.

Pyramid of Truth.

Suddenly, Glenn’s eyes lit up, and he let out a wild scream as he began to strangle himself.

“Graaagh!”

Ares shouted.

“Get a grip! What’s going on?!”

He was choking himself so hard that it didn’t seem like he’d last another minute.

“Damn it! How strong is he?!”

Ares grabbed his wrists and pulled, but Glenn’s arms didn’t budge.

Canis said, “It’s beyond just muscle strength. It’s probably a neural signal. Try breaking his wrists instead.”

“Will that be okay? What if it makes things worse?”

“There’s no other choice.”

Lucia got up.

“I’ll do it.”

“No. You’ve lost too much blood. If you push yourself even a little more, you could go into shock.”

If it weren’t for Zulu, a top-class utility specialist, she might’ve already died.

“The fact Glenn wants to live means half his heart has returned. That should be enough for my ability to work.”

She would touch Glenn’s heart directly with the hand of God.

“You could die.”

“I’ll be fine.”

Lucia placed her hand on Glenn’s chest.

“Save yourself. Isn’t that right?”

As the hand of God activated, all the color drained from her face.

“Hrrrk!”

Everyone was tense, but they could see the strength slowly fading from Glenn’s hands.

Ares shouted, “Just a little more!”

And then, focus returned to Glenn’s eyes.

“Hah!”

As if waking from the grave, he gasped for air and looked around.

What did I just do?

“Glenn…?”

Lucia gave a beaming smile and collapsed. Glenn hurriedly caught her.

“Lucia! Lucia!”

Ares checked her condition.

“She’s okay. Just exhausted. But what happened? Why did you strangle yourself?”

Glenn shook his head.

“I don’t know. It’s not that I wanted to die. But… it felt like it didn’t matter if I did.”

Emptiness.

“Void. A vast emptiness. There’s nothing in this world. It’s all hollow.”

He suddenly looked up.

“There’s no time. Everyone will make the same choice I did. Then this world will end!”

Zulu asked, “Why? Even if someone dies, the world doesn’t end.”

“No, you don’t understand.”

Glenn covered his face.

“I… I saw it. We can’t stop it. We’ll never beat the god!”

Canis replied, “You regain your mind only to turn into a coward?”

“You don’t know! This isn’t about life and death! You don’t understand what the god is trying to do to us!”

At that moment, the scenery shifted.

“Zulu.”

As Glenn stared blankly, Seongeum stepped through the boundary of space.

“Shirone is calling for you.”

Yahweh seemed intent on fighting to the end.

“They call it the user code,” Lethe said.

“The heart itself isn’t an error. If it were, I wouldn’t exist either. But it’s not special, either. I’m just a quantum signal processing random codes.”

Sitting within a crater landscape, Great Star looked like a broken machine.

“Humans want to live. It's slightly different from animal survival instinct. And because they want to live, this world continues to exist.”

By quantum signal.

“That’s the essence of love. But when attachment to life becomes too strong, malice amplifies, and the world grows muddied. Theraphos exists to prevent that, but it’s limited by its good-vs-evil framework. Only emptiness can suppress love.”

Great Star still didn’t react.

“A human life starts with love and ends with void. Between those, there’s a multitude of good and evil. A world where all four harmonize would be Ultima—but even gods struggle to balance that. Still, this world is user-friendly.”

God, too, was made by humans.

“It seems the Illuminati believe that a life controlled by machines is as good as dead. They’d rather sleep eternally than reincarnate endlessly across multiverses. That’s why this world is so dynamic. Random data constantly clashes, and there's a system to process it.”

It was the under world.

“The Missing Link is the core of the reincarnation system. To prevent data from accumulating too densely, the Illuminati receive forgetfulness codes whenever entering a new world. In a way, it’s true immortality. But that's exactly where the error occurs.”

When love becomes too strong.

“Some users seem to love this world more than the Idea. That’s what both the god and the Illuminati define as the error—the function of the heart.”

Why is the heart dangerous?

“Even if this world is an illusion, the user code is connected to the Idea. If someone decides to stay in this world, not even a god can shut it down easily.”

Lethe approached Great Star.

“Of course, the god’s authority is vast. It can kill users, torment them, or make their lives hell. Not because it's emotional—just due to the laws of computation.”

Just a matter of law.

“But even such a god can’t touch the user code. That would be considered a hostile act against the Illuminati. So if all users choose to remain in this world…”

If they are united by Ultima—

“The god won’t be able to close the world by any means.”

Great Star blinked once.

“The Illuminati must be losing their minds. People don’t want to wake from a world they think is fake. And if this world’s outcomes surpass those of the god’s, real and fake switch places—and their world becomes the illusion. That’s why the heart… is the most critical error in the universe.”

Lethe asked again.

“So how do we stop it? What’s clear is the Illuminati can’t intervene directly in this world. That’s why Shirone couldn’t reach the outer world. But if they grant the god the authority to destroy the master code…”

Humanity might be erased.

“Looking back, the god we follow is nothing more than a machine designed to bridge two universes. With imaginary and real time connected, the Illuminati have reached infinity.”

Because two makes infinity.

“The god is trying to close the world using every method it can. Cause manipulation. Cell Burster. In’s wave. And now, it’s injecting the code of emptiness to encourage death. It wants people to commit suicide and exit to the outer world.”

Lethe looked up at the moon.

“18 minutes and 40 seconds until midnight. I wonder… will we still exist after that?”

The doomsday appointed by the god.

“It won’t be easy. You know Cayden escaped, right? That must’ve slightly shifted the god’s expected outcome. Even a 0.1% difference is massive.”

A god must be perfect.

So even a 0.1% error could be a reason to overwrite the entire system.

Which means… it’s really 50/50.

God and humanity have equal chances of winning.

As Great Star remained unresponsive, Lethe turned to leave, as if giving up.

“I’m going to fight. Take care.”

“…Me.”

Great Star finally spoke.

“Will Shirone forgive me?”

Lethe turned back slowly, thinking.

“…Hehe.”

Delta Headquarters – Underground Passage.

“Huff! Huff!”

Havitz let out strange groaning sounds as he stumbled through the dark corridor. The moment he was exposed to the Photon Cannon Infinity, what flashed through his mind was a fragment of memory.

“Mom.”

Or was it even a memory?

Because once you believe it, that’s enough.

“Somehow…”

Living had just become too easy.

“I’m free.”

In the feeling of liberation that anything was permissible, Satan’s chaos surged toward its peak.

“Kehehe! I’ll do whatever I want.”

As he always had.

“Haah! Haah! Haah! Heek! Heek! Heek!”

Staggering and colliding with the walls, Havitz’s appearance surpassed mere grotesqueness.

“Hehe! This is fun!”

Midnight was approaching, and only one round remained in the game of “Truth and Lie.”

To prove the final proposition as truth, he had to kill Shirone… or Uorin.

“You bastard!”

Now that he had confirmed he couldn’t kill Yahweh, the only choice left was Uorin.

‘No, it’s different now. I’m going to kill her. That bastard. I’ll tell Mom everything.’

Havitz let out an empty giggle.

“Hehehe…”

He had never lost any kind of game before, and this time would be no different.

Wizard.

He realized why he liked her.

Noeulja…

Vanishing activated.

Uorin suddenly stopped.

‘What am I missing?’

Even after engraving his name into her head thousands of times while fleeing, the name Havitz wouldn’t come to her.

“Hrrrrk!”

His determination to restrain any foe locked down a perfect two-meter radius like a swamp.

And Havitz…

“Hehehe! Hehehehe!”

First his ankle joint popped out, followed by torn muscle and skin as he stepped forward.

Thunk!

“I am free.”

Just as his longsword came down toward Uorin’s nape—

“Urgh!”

Vanishing broke.

“Havitz?”

The sight of the longsword inches away from them made their skin crawl—but the question that dominated their minds was:

‘Why didn’t he kill us?’

Havitz’s face twisted as if something were stuck, and a strange voice came out of his mouth.

“R… run…”

Gando.

“Graaaaagh!”

Havitz let out a monstrous shriek.

‘That’s Gando’s body.’

Maybe it wasn’t—but Uorin felt certain she would never see him again.

Gando saved me.

Trusting her instincts, Uorin threw herself backward.

The instant Havitz’s longsword fell vertically as he reactivated Vanishing…

‘What am I missing right now?’

Two people.

‘No… one of them wasn’t human, was it?’

“Something’s strange…”
