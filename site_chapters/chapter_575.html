Chapter 575:

It was as if time had sped up thousands of times, causing icicles to form in the sky and crash to the ground.

Thud! Thud! Thud!

Frings appeared between the thick ice pillars.

'Frings? Why?'

Even if he ambushed me, a battle was impossible.

No one on the alliance team had a higher hand than Shirone, so he could just call a Kang.

"This isn't fun, but…"

Thin Ice Ridge.

As Frings swung his arm, the air froze thinly and surged towards Shirone.

The ice was so sharp it was nearly invisible, slicing through the forest trees.

"Frings! Kang!"

Dodging the Thin Ice Ridge, Shirone called a Kang, his eyes filled with shock.

'What?'

Ruru's electric signal didn't come through.

"Did you think it would be that easy with the Judge? You need to understand the rules properly."

As Frings continuously cast Thin Ice Ridge, the scenery began to look as if it was being cut apart.

'What's going on?'

Rolling on the ground to escape the slashes, Shirone shouted again,

"Frings! Kang!"

Again, the Kang didn't work, but with two attempts, he identified the problem.

'He disappeared.'

Frings' presence suddenly vanished from the sensory perception of the Spirit Zone.

'I see.'

-To call a Kang on a specific target, the Spirit Zone must be in contact.

In battle, overlapping Spirit Zones are common, but it's different in the Black and White game.

If the Spirit Zone is avoided and the range is exited when calling a Kang, the Judge won't activate.

'That's the theory. But is it really possible?'

Unless in an Image Zone, the Spirit Zone's form can't be seen with the naked eye.

Avoiding the Spirit Zone through combined sensory perception requires extreme sensitivity.

"I'm a bit sensitive."

Shirone had fought many mages, but this was the first time encountering a tactic to avoid the Spirit Zone.

'Graduation class rank 3, Frings. He's not there by luck.'

It made sense why Frings hadn't been interested in collecting cards.

'He can't be Kanged. He has absolute confidence in avoiding it.'

Unless it was Arin's level of mental activity, it was difficult to catch Frings with just spatial transformation.

'But is that really the case?'

As Shirone's competitive spirit flared, Frings raised both arms and cast a spell.

"Ice Soul Chaos."

Hundreds of tadpole-shaped ice clouds spread from Frings, piercing through the forest.

It was as if an ice age had arrived, with frost forming on everything.

'This is dangerous!'

This wasn't Training Ground 2000.

Fire and ice are considered the best attack magic in real combat because they create environments uninhabitable for living beings.

"Let's begin."

Frings, with the spikes of the Ice Demon, charged.

"Today's integrated class is on spatial transformation. The Spirit Zone changes according to the mage's mind, but it's inefficient in real combat. So, it's important to master the basics of spatial transformation."

In front of the advanced class students, Etella explained spatial transformation as usual.

The newly promoted advanced students listened intently, while the class five and four students waited impatiently for practice time.

"Ugh, this is boring."

At Mark's grumble, Maria put a finger to her lips.

"Shh. The teacher will hear you."

"Sorry. But I feel like I'm in a slump."

"You're training hard, aren't you?"

Mark and Maria, receiving personal guidance from Saad, had improved significantly, currently ranking first and second in class four.

"That's the problem. I'm working hard, but it's boring. I think it's time to leave the advanced class."

Maria had been having similar thoughts recently.

"Yeah, our seniors also considered advancing to the graduation class at this time. But the slots haven't been announced yet."

Aider's status in the infirmary, whether he would drop out of the graduation class, was still undecided.

"It's frustrating. With a full-body fracture and trauma, he should just give up this year instead of blocking the juniors' path."

"How can you say that about a senior?"

"Tch, I heard he messed with Shirone and got what he deserved. Anyway, as soon as the slot opens, I'll apply first. Is that okay?"

Maria shrugged.

"Do as you like. I'll follow the proper procedure."

She saw no merit in joining the graduation class immediately, other than experiencing the graduation exam early.

"Wow!"

As Etella entered the Image Zone and quickly demonstrated spatial transformation, the class seven students cheered.

"Everyone understand? Now, let's split into classes and practice spatial transformation…"

Boom!

Suddenly, an explosion echoed from the forest, and broken trees shot into the sky.

"W-what?"

The advanced students looked on in shock as Etella quickly turned to the forest.

Without needing to sense it with the Spirit Zone, two graduation class students entered the integrated training ground.

"Huh? Shirone?"

Chased by Frings' indiscriminate attacks, Shirone realized he had entered the integrated training ground.

"Damn…!"

With no time to regret his mistake, the ice spikes of the Ice Demon flew straight for his vital points.

'The precision is incredible. In terms of senses, he's on par with Dante.'

As the forest cover disappeared, Shirone and Frings began using attack magic in earnest.

Reflected flashes, exploding ice shards.

The advanced students watched in awe at the high-level battle.

But their admiration was short-lived as the escalating magic threatened their surroundings, causing the lower-class students to jump up.

"Ahhh! It's dangerous!"

Frings, retreating in a horse stance, bent his ten fingers and cast a spell.

'Ice Wave!'

As the freezing wave scraped the ground, thousands of ice spikes shot forward, and Shirone cast Photon Cannon.

Boom, boom, boom!

The ice wave fragments scattered like stars in the sky, glittering like frozen dust.

"Stop! It's class time!"

Etella shouted, but Shirone and Frings, half-entranced, didn't stop fighting.

As the Homing Photon Cannon circled the training ground, even the top advanced class students turned and fled.

"Run!"

Though the distance was far, the speed of the Homing Photon Cannon made them think it was coming their way.

"Move aside. What's going on?"

Hearing the sound of Pisho's insect magic explosion, Siina and Saad teleported in and pushed through the crowd of students.

"Huh? That's…"

As Saad blinked, the lower-class students shouted,

"Shirone and Frings are fighting! Saad, stop them!"

Saad frowned.

'This isn't a kids' fight.'

It wasn't just the power; it was the deadly tactics and strategies seen only on real battlefields.

Unless they joined the fray, they couldn't intervene immediately, so Etella and Siina waited for an opportunity.

'Light Wings!'

As Shirone, with wings of light, flew low, ice spears followed his trajectory.

The Homing Photon Cannon, completed in split seconds, approached powerfully, but Frings used ultra-precise movement to create a misfire.

'The difference in precision is clear. It's a bit annoying.'

As Shirone cast the Photon Cannon directly, a one-meter-thick ice wall rose in front of Frings.

The Photon Cannon pierced the ice wall with a roar, but Frings remained indifferent.

'The power has already diminished.'

As he gathered reflective ice in his fist and swung, the Photon Cannon deflected.

"That's amazing…"

The class four students murmured in admiration.

Approaching graduation, they understood the precision and sensitivity of the current battle.

"Shirone…"

Mark, in particular, was shocked beyond imagination.

'I thought I was desperately catching up.'

He was confident no one trained harder than he did.

'I'm not the only one running. Everyone is running.'

In such a race, what more must he give to run faster than others?

"Maria, let's double our training from today."

"…Okay."

In the increasingly fierce battle, Frings prepared his finishing move, timing it carefully.

"Let's end this."

As he gathered freezing mist at his right side, it began to compress at a bizarre speed.

Simultaneously, Shirone prepared to counter with a Photon Cannon.

"Super Ice…!"

As Frings was about to cast his spell, a rough vortex rose from beneath him and froze.

Siina's Ice Tornado.

Sharp ice spikes crossed Frings' body, but no wounds appeared.

Meanwhile, Etella approached Shirone from behind and locked her arm around his neck.

"Ugh!"

Feeling the pressure of a steel whip, Shirone finally canceled the Photon Cannon.

But his gaze remained fixed on Frings, and his breath was heavy with excitement.

"Frings, what are you doing?"

As Siina approached, Frings, unable to move, turned his eyes to her.

"Sorry. We had a minor disagreement."

Having been briefed on the Scramble Royal in the teachers' meeting, Siina wasn't amused.

"Stop immediately if you want to stay in school."

'Nice body.'

Frings didn't listen.

"I can't leave school. But in this case, mutual fault seems appropriate."

Siina's face turned red.

'These guys…'

The underground research group leaders never listened to teachers.

Even when sent to the graduation class, they hadn't matured and had been causing headaches for years without graduating.

"Shirone, stop."

Etella released her grip and advised.

"But…"

Frings had initiated the fight.

"The juniors could have been hurt."

Only then did Shirone look around and see the students, who had been his classmates last year, watching in fear.

"…I'm sorry."

The Scramble had likely been collected by Pisho, and with the teachers intervening, further fighting was pointless.

Siina, having dispelled the Ice Tornado, spoke.

"Return to the graduation class immediately. If you cause another incident like this, there will be no forgiveness."

"As you command."

Frings bowed politely and headed towards the forest, but in a direction unrelated to the graduation class.

"Ugh, this is so frustrating. Such a headache."

Pressing her temples with her fingers, Siina called out to the dejected Shirone.

"Shirone, come with me for a moment."

"Now? But the Scramble Royal is still going on…"

Siina's eyes narrowed sharply.

"Now. Even if you're in the graduation class, this is still a school. Or do you think advanced class teachers are a joke?"

"No! I would never think that…"

"Alright, then follow me."

As Siina led Shirone down the mountain, Etella smiled and tried to calm the atmosphere.

"Alright, everyone! Now you see why it's important to diligently practice spatial transformation? The key to transformation is…"

As the students stared blankly, Saad shook his head and sighed.

"Teacher Etella, that's not it."
