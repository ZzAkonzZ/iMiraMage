Chapter 589:

"Scramble Royal has ended."

Graduating class head teacher Collie entered the principal's office.

Alpheas stood with his hands behind his back, looking out the window, while Vice Principal Olivia sat on the sofa, legs crossed, deep in thought.

"Shirone's team won."

Olivia sighed in relief.

"That's a relief. At least we've blocked the underground research group's approach."

Alpheas spoke.

"It's fortunate the situation didn't escalate. But Shirone's team's victory means they'll get closer to the core of the incident."

"How do you know that? We don't even know what Istas's core is."

Turning from the window, Alpheas sat at his desk.

"True. No one knows until Miro speaks. Even those with voting rights in the Council of Twenty don't know the upper echelons' secrets."

"But Shirone was with Miro. Maybe he told him something."

"If that's the case, Shirone won't investigate Istas. He's curious but cautious. He's not foolish enough to dig into global secrets recklessly."

"I hope so."

Alpheas's agreement left a bitter taste.

'If possible, it's best to leave it buried.'

As a mage, he was eager to know Istas's true nature, but too many forces were involved to approach it out of mere curiosity.

"I'll be back shortly."

Olivia rose from the sofa and headed to the door.

"Where are you going? Don't cause a commotion. It's best if Istas remains closed."

"I know. That's why I'm going."

Olivia's eyes gleamed with determination as she stepped outside.

'There's just one person I need to see.'

In her dorm room, Anchal sat cross-legged on the floor, meditating.

As the clock struck midnight, she slowly opened her eyes, sat at her desk, and took out a small brush.

The brush handle was thin, and the bristles even thinner.

On a scroll of paper, the brush moved, writing in the language of Jincheon.

"Your Majesty,

I am now before the secret of the 'Fragment.'

I do not fear death, but I worry I may fail in my duty due to the mission's difficulty.

Therefore, I intend to break the seal of the family's treasured artifact, the Majingan, bestowed by Your Majesty. Please forgive my humble skills.

I will do my utmost to bring back the 'Fragment.'

Long live Your Majesty.

Long live the Jincheon Empire."

Carefully placing the brush down, Anchal waited for the ink to dry before rolling up the scroll.

"Honggyu."

At her call, two large eyes appeared on the ceiling, as if drawn there.

"Yes, Director."

Anchal, the director of the Jincheon Space Agency, a top-secret division of the Jincheon Empire's External Inspection Department, and an advisor to the Valkyrie's reset system analysis institute.

"You must deliver this personally."

"Leave it to me."

As Anchal tossed the scroll upward, a tongue emerged from the ceiling, catching and swallowing it.

"Forgive my boldness, but please be careful. The future of Jincheon rests in your hands."

"…Understood."

As the ceiling's image faded like drying ink, Anchal clasped her head in thought.

'Istas.'

By now, Scramble Royal would have ended.

Though it didn't matter who won, Anchal preferred Shirone's team to win.

'With the power of Four Symbols Explosion…'

Initially, she hadn't planned to involve Shirone, but realizing he could manipulate time made Scramble Royal meaningless.

'Fermi will start moving soon. He likely has the plans for Istas.'

Anchal needed help from both sides but was prepared to discard either if necessary.

As she walked to her bed, someone knocked on the door.

"Anchal, are you in?"

'Vice Principal Olivia.'

Expecting this, Anchal quickly composed herself and opened the door, feigning ignorance.

"Vice Principal? What brings you here at this hour?"

"I need to talk. May I come in?"

Though her room was spotless, she didn't want to let a certified second-class archmage inside.

"Or should we talk outside?"

Olivia's voice was sweet but had a sharp edge tonight.

"Yes. Let's talk outside. I need some fresh air."

As soon as they stepped outside, Olivia teleported away without warning.

A normal student would be bewildered, but Anchal followed without complaint.

They arrived at the bottom of a deep ravine on the opposite side of the mountains surrounding the school.

"What is this about? Why bring a student here?"

"Anchal, how old are you?"

"……"

Realizing this was a confrontation, Anchal saw no reason to lie.

"Thirty-two."

"I thought so. People from the East look so young."

"Not particularly in the East. So, what do you want to know? The secret of Istas? My identity?"

"Everything, if possible."

Anchal shook her head.

"I didn't think you were foolish. It's best for you and the school to stay uninvolved."

The Jincheon Empire was ruled by the Three Sovereigns.

"I don't intend to get involved in imperial politics. But Istas is private property. I'm entrusted with full authority by the owner."

"I'll ensure the school isn't harmed."

"That's for me to decide after hearing you out."

"Sorry, I have nothing to tell you."

"Really?"

Olivia's lips parted as she cast a sonic spell, emitting the highest frequency sound a human could hear.

The amplified echoes from the cliffs assaulted Anchal's eardrums.

'Loud noise. That's why she chose this place.'

As Anchal's mind reeled, Olivia recited a book's worth of incantations and cast a spell.

'Great Expansion.'

As the air expanded, the ravine, tens of meters high, collapsed inward.

Rumble!

When the spectacular landslide ended, no ravine remained within visible range.

Landing on the rocks that filled the ravine, Olivia's eyes flashed.

Jincheon Sorcery - Giant.

As she leaped, a 20-meter-tall giant appeared and slammed its fist down.

Boom!

The giant's arm pierced the rocks, burying itself up to the shoulder, making Olivia break into a cold sweat.

'Destroying objects with an illusion…'

Like finding a ring buried in the dirt, the giant's arm swept through the ground, pulling Anchal out from deep below.

Her leather eyepatch was torn, revealing a bluish eye.

"Is that the Majingan?"

The giant vanished after placing Anchal on the ground.

"What are you doing? Do you really want to fight me?"

"I'll do whatever it takes to ensure the school's safety."

"Don't be arrogant, Olivia. I'm a soldier. Even an archmage can't defeat me."

As Anchal tore off the eyepatch, Olivia added.

"I don't care about imperial secrets. This is about the students. Explain why Shirone and why Fermi is interested in Istas. That's my limit. If you refuse…"

Olivia loosened her lips.

"I'll take you on."

"……"

After a long pause, Anchal extinguished the Majingan's light and sat on a rock.

"I'm an agent of the Jincheon Space Agency."

Olivia lowered her hands and leaned against the cliff.

"My mission is to gather information on cosmic resets. The universe has undergone three resets so far, starting with someone named Goffin."

Anchal looked up at the sky.

"Nineteen years ago, Goffin protected humanity from heaven. But he entrusted the mission to Miro and disappeared. Here's the question."

Anchal raised a finger.

"Why did Goffin have to erase himself? Given he chose Miro as his successor, it's hard to believe he lost affection for this world."

"Hmm."

"Through the Valkyrie's hypothesis, we concluded that Goffin had to finish something through Miro that he couldn't do himself. And we believe Shirone is somehow involved."

"Shirone?"

Anchal kept the confidential details to herself and moved on.

"Goffin's erasure was a disaster for humans. The barrier protecting them from heaven's army vanished."

"There must have been strong opposition."

"They don't remember, but it's predictable. And we suspect Fermi's mother, Yolga, was at the center of that opposition."

"Shirone and Fermi."

"Is that enough reason? We need both of them to investigate Istas."

"Alright. But why Istas?"

From here, the security level was much higher, but Olivia's question was valid.

"Do you know about the rift field verification experiment?"

"Roughly. It suggests the total amount of time and energy in our world is slightly off."

"Correct. Through the Anke Ra Erasure and the Great Purification, we kept calculating the rift field. We found that the rift value during Goffin's erasure was always constant. The more resets, the more complex the rift should be."

"What does that mean?"

"It means there's an event that never changes, even through three resets."

Anchal drew a cross in the air.

"Why Istas? The door Goffin installed there is fixed at a space-time coordinate, unlike other ruins. A coordinate you can't reach without stopping time. The Jincheon Space Agency separated that coordinate into time and space using the rift field value."

Anchal mimed pulling a thread apart.

"Connecting spatial information to time, we concluded that an event lasting about three hours occurred."

That event was the source of the rift during Goffin's erasure.

"In short, Goffin fixed a specific event. Why? It's different from the second and third resets."

"Is that event the upper echelon of Istas?"

"You know the rumors about the upper echelon? Everything that can't exist in this world exists there. It refers to a specific event. And that event isn't affected by resets."

"Is it really possible for an event to remain unaffected even if the world is reset?"

"It's possible."

The answer was immediate.

"Of course, it's not easy. But if the event itself becomes an independent world connected to ours, it can happen."

"Like another universe?"

Anchal nodded.

"The Jincheon Space Agency collected all the information and simulated the world of Istas's upper echelon. We needed to understand the world to gauge the difficulty of infiltration. The results were astonishing."

"What were the astonishing results?"

"There is no beginning or end."

Anchal twirled her finger as she looked at Olivia.

"The event that occurred in the upper echelon of Istas, that roughly three-hour event, is repeating endlessly."
