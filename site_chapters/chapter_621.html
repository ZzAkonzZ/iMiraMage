Chapter 621:

Once you return to school…

On the day the training of the "Time Explosion" ended, Miro explained the plan.

"You will have to refuse the evaluation."

"Refuse the evaluation?"

"It's too late to catch up with the scores of the graduating class. You'll only give scouts a bad impression. Instead, it's better to give up the evaluation and bet on the graduation exam."

Shirone thought it was a correct diagnosis.

"But just graduating shouldn't be the ultimate goal."

Miro emphasized with a strong voice.

"Even if you graduate, there aren't many associations willing to embrace a ticking time bomb. If you want to become a mage, you have to show that you are valuable enough for them to take the risk."

Miro's strategy was simple.

"When you think of the graduation exam as a long-distance race, you have to start at full speed from the beginning. Then…"

Miro pointed to the destination.

"Finish the race at that same speed."

Entering the 5th stage of the endurance survival.

As the participants' faces began to show signs of fatigue, a 6th-tier creature was summoned.

"From now on, there will be many dropouts."

Even the professionals who used summoning magic in real battles turned pale when they saw the lineup of monsters.

Growl!

The monsters included not only werewolves, which were much smarter than wolves, but also Cerberus with three heads and a massive body like a cow, Bugbears weighing up to 300 kilograms, and Gargoyles with skin as hard as stone, all capable of killing a human with a single blow.

'This will definitely create a distinction here.'

Should they cooperate to move to the next stage or hide until others drop out?

While most participants were contemplating the same thing, Shirone dashed to the front without hesitation.

The strategy of tearing through the formation with scatter movement and defeating each monster with photon cannons was simple but incredibly powerful.

When the flashes pierced through the bulging belly of the Bugbear, it flew away with both feet off the ground, and the Gargoyles attacking from the sky couldn't penetrate the violent barrier and were pushed back.

"Movement, attack, and defense are all balanced. Even though he's an unlocker, he's well-trained."

"That's what makes it even more puzzling."

Baikal stroked his chin.

"He must have studied the endurance survival event, right? There's no way he can complete it anyway."

In the endurance survival event, which selects 15 people, if even one person can complete it, the evaluation criteria itself would be shaken, so the association sets an impossible difficulty level.

"Yes. From the 5th tier, the lineup consists of creatures that even professionals mainly summon."

They had to fight hundreds of such creatures.

Lara raised an objection.

"Simple comparisons are dangerous. The simulation is just a simulation. Even if the reactions are designed considering the intellectual level of the species, there are limits. Especially, creatures controlled by mages move according to the mage’s intellectual level."

"Of course, it's different from real creatures."

Baikal acknowledged that point.

"But the abilities reflect reality. Even the 6th tier is a life-threatening difficulty for students. Quickly eliminating participants is also a reasonable judgment for the second evaluation. What's the intention?"

Elizabeth analyzed Shirone's battle.

"Magic power level 2.03 million. Average single power of photon cannon 2,847 crash. Firing speed 267 rounds per minute."

"Crazy."

Baikal couldn't help but speak.

"He has already far exceeded the master difficulty level set by the association. The noteworthy part is that the impact and firing speed were classified separately. But now Shirone is achieving both simultaneously."

It meant he was delivering more than four strikes per second, which was thought to be possible only with the highest concentration.

'Has he surpassed the association's expectations?'

As Baikal pondered, Lara asked.

"Evaluator, your overall assessment…"

"F."

Elizabeth turned her body, demanding an explanation.

"The 29 participants are already out of his mind. It means he doesn't have a proper grasp of reality."

"But Shirone's abilities are clearly…"

"I know. I'm disappointed with the strategy set by number 27. With the current stats, he might be able to handle up to the 6th or 7th stage. But that's the end."

Baikal shook his head and continued.

"Scouts remember every detail about the creatures in the exam. Even a 3rd-tier creature wouldn't be affected by that. If 15 people are selected before he reaches the end, his current power is just a display. F."

"…Understood."

Lara's pen inscribed an "F" on Shirone's page.

'I know.'

If Shirone had heard Baikal's explanation, he would have nodded.

'I know.'

It wasn't a strategy built with a clear success possibility in mind.

He knew that running at full speed from the start and completing the race was his only remaining option.

"I will never stop!"

Shirone's photon cannon fell like a comb, breaking Cerberus's spine.

"If you come out like this, it's troublesome."

As Prings, who had lost the leading role, cast ice magic, sharp ice spikes divided the colosseum.

Taking advantage of the gap, Eima sniped the enemies from all directions.

'87 monsters. 88 monsters.'

Elizabeth was watching.

"Firing speed 67 rounds per minute. Considering the sniper mode, it's an amazing speed."

"The accuracy seems to be a bit off. How many shots missed?"

Elizabeth, connected to the core system, checked the exact numbers.

"The current accuracy is… 89.4 percent."

It was excellent by graduation standards, but not satisfactory for Baikal.

'Red Eye!'

As Amy, who had backed up her memory, started shooting more precisely, the Bugbears began to burn.

"It's going up. 90.2 percent. 91.6 percent."

"Karmis should be at that level. For now, B."

After finishing the cold evaluation, Baikal's gaze moved to the next participant.

'Atomic Bomb!'

Iruki's detached spirit zone quickly flew and exploded on the monsters' faces.

"It looks like an instant explosion."

Boom! Boom! Boom!

It was as if an invisible iron ball was bursting the monsters' heads.

"In reality, it's a very high-level detonation equation. And it's accurate. For Savant No. 5, objects are perceived as fractal structures. It's mathematical, so there are no errors."

Baikal closely observed Iruki's movements.

"Reaction speed 0.024 seconds, currently the top among participants."

"Hmm."

Lara's note had "Hmm" written on it, and then the pen stopped.

"Isn't it a bit… lacking in intuition? Just because it's accurate doesn't mean it's efficient. The response method is too mechanical."

"It seems to focus more on eliminating variables than increasing efficiency. Even so, the annihilation speed is above average."

"For now, B."

Lara recorded it, but Elizabeth pouted.

"Aren't the standards too high? Even if the participants' level is high, at this level, there are no shortcomings."

"Just because there are no shortcomings doesn't mean there are strengths. For example, Plu would have made a different judgment."

Comparing with the top graduate of the Royal Magic School itself meant the standards were high.

"It's a mess."

"Uoooo!"

Screamer, who cast scatter movement, punched the Bugbear with an iron fist.

"A guy whose mind is paralyzed by his own power."

"Maya! Don't fall behind me!"

Kaiden, rotating around Maya, cut down Cerberus with his sword.

"A guy who forgets his mission and clings to his beliefs."

Shirone's photon cannon bent the werewolf's back and extended.

"Throwing tactics to the dogs and focusing solely on firepower."

Now even Elizabeth was silent.

"There are cases like this. Their abilities are far superior to other schools, but their mental state is not normal. What do they think the exam is? They're doing whatever they want."

No matter how perverse their personality, the kingdom wanted mages who acted like mages in front of their mission.

"According to the Red Line regulations, graduates have the first negotiation rights with their national association."

If the first negotiation fails due to treatment, salary, or other issues, other countries can propose scouting.

"The standards are important to them, not to us. Our job is to find the most competent ones and report to the association."

"…Understood."

Lara had no choice but to accept Baikal's opinion.

"Still, some are quite useful."

Fermi's various magic was adding firepower at the right places, and Dante was also leisurely handling enemies by applying instant magic circles.

Also, Prings' ice magic was sharp enough to be called a masterpiece.

"Number 1. Number 2. Number 22. Grade A."

As the actions of all participants were graded, the 5th stage annihilation time was almost over.

'One minute left.'

With the number of monsters still exceeding two hundred, Shirone's mind grew anxious.

'58 seconds. 57 seconds.'

If this continued, the 6th-tier creatures would be summoned to the colosseum without even 30 seconds to recover.

'I must finish it!'

For Shirone, who had increased his firepower from the beginning, those 30 seconds were desperately needed.

Boom! Boom! Boom!

The scattered photon cannons struck the monsters all around.

The Bugbear with a broken shoulder, the Cerberus with one head blown off, the Gargoyle with cracked skin—all the enemies started rushing towards Shirone.

"Ugh!"

Shirone gritted his teeth and focused his mind, firing the photon cannon even more, but the number of approaching enemies kept increasing.

"Isn't that a mistake?"

Elizabeth asked.

"He has been firing magic at that power for nearly 10 minutes. It's inevitable that his concentration would waver."

Even without being a scout, everyone could see that Shirone's accuracy was dropping.

"Isn't that dangerous?"

At Reina's words, Olina started crying again.

"You're so clueless. If you don't know, just watch."

Reina, who was scolded by Rian, patted Olina's shoulder and spoke with a hint of frustration.

"But even I can see that he's visibly exhausted."

Rian bit his lip and looked towards the colosseum.

'Shirone…'

Only those who have experienced it firsthand can understand how many creatures a number like 100 represents.

"It's okay. There won't be any problems."

"Really?"

Now, Reina also relied on her brother's judgment.

"I'd rather die than…"

Rian's voice was firm.

"He's not the type to lose focus just because he's tired."

'Now!'

Shirone, who had drawn in numerous monsters, gritted his teeth and focused his mind.

'Elysion!'

Raising his concentration again at the end of a 10-minute sprint consumed much more mental energy than usual, but it wasn't a bad trade if he could succeed in annihilation.

'30 seconds. If I can just have 30 seconds to recover!'

With that thought, he cast his wide-area destruction magic, multiple explosions.

Boom! Boom! Boom!

"Unbelievable…"

The sight of seven overlapping explosions with a diameter of 10 meters blew the minds of the spectators, and the monsters caught in the wave of mass had their limbs broken and were flung around between the spheres of light.

'9 seconds! 8 seconds!'

With 7 seconds remaining, the creatures dominated by the multiple explosions were reduced to their smallest pixels and disappeared.

"That's it! I told you!"

As Rian raised his arms and shouted, Olina's shoulders shook.

"Magic power level 2.93 million magicles! Heart rate 237 beats per minute!"

"What? 237 beats?"

"Average impact of 6,800 crash at four points! 13,000 crash at five points!"

As the VIP seats became noisy as if a commotion had broken out, everyone turned to look, and the embarrassed Rian quietly lowered his hands.
