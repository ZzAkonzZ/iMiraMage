Chapter 912: The Brain of God (1)

In the year 187 of Omega.

Goffin, who had returned from the hell of the under world, gathered the Gaia people and prepared to leave the Photon System.

"It will be an interesting journey."

The hearts of those welcoming the new era were excited.

"There is only one way to leave the Photon System."

Goffin began his final speech to the thousand Gaia people gathered.

"To escape the control of Anke Ra, we must all become the managers of this world."

Everyone knew this, but the Gaia people silently watched Goffin on the high tower.

"We have no more controversies or doubts. We are the true masters of this world. Therefore, we will escape Anke Ra's control and transcend infinity."

Applause echoed.

Although they were few compared to the total population, the number of individuals was not very important to the Gaia people.

‘Of course, today is different.’

To destroy Anke Ra's authority and leave the Photon System, the more personal codes, the better.

As Goffin descended from the tower, his metallic hair fluttering, people gathered around him.

"The time is approaching. Your role is important."

Although the Gaia people were integrated into Ultima, the key figure was still Goffin.

"Magic."

The principle of phenomena.

Understanding the truth of this world and changing phenomena through that understanding were separate issues.

Only Goffin, the infinite magician, could unlock the final lock at the end of the universe.

"We choose."

The pride of the Gaia people, who had regained the meaning of perfect existence at the end of truth, pierced the sky.

"Uncle Goffin!"

A woman with golden steel hair twisted like a vortex and attached to her head approached.

"Milla?"

She was the first historical child born when Goffin returned from the under world.

"It's been a while."

Despite the sharp impression unique to the Gaia people, a bright and soft atmosphere was felt.

"Of course. I'm a grown woman now. You have no idea how long I've been waiting for today."

Milla's face blushed slightly.

"Since the day I fell in love with you."

"Yes. I'm sorry."

Goffin smiled and patted her head, and Milla stuck out her tongue and laughed brightly.

"It's okay. When we leave this world, everything will start anew. I might have a chance too."

"Haha! I hope so."

Although Milla had been rejected by Goffin, her heart was not hurt. She knew that mutual consent was necessary for love.

‘Knowing what is right and doing it.’ She was fortunate to be born as a Gaia person.

"Goffin, the preparations are complete."

At the elder's words, Goffin opened his 11th sense, the sense of the palace.

‘Ultima.’

He felt all the Gaia people on the planet recognizing him through the sense of the palace.

"Then..."

Goffin smiled and looked back at Milla, who nodded with an excited expression.

"Let's go."

As the Spirit Zone opened and they shot out at superluminal speed through the realm of infinity.

Bang!

Milla's face exploded.

Her body, left only below the neck, staggered left and right before falling with a thud. Goffin turned his head.

"Archangel."

In the distant sky, Ikael was looking down at the ground with her halo greatly expanded.

"Foolish humans, why do you forsake the authority given by God and invite death?"

Goffin muttered.

"Ataraxia."

The concept that gave birth to this world.

‘The signal closest to the outer world. The range of amplification can be said to be from nothing to existence.’

Even for Goffin, the infinite magician, it was difficult to deal with a concept at the highest level of the universe.

The Gaia chief examined Milla's condition.

"She's dead. The attack was faster than the sense of time explosion. It couldn't have been blocked."

"I hope it was a good dream." Goffin looked up at the sky.

"Archangel, can I interpret this interference as the will of Anke Ra?" Ikael arrogantly raised her chin.

"It is not interference but law. I, Ikael, the archangel, will destroy those who disturb the law."

She extended Ataraxia to the end of the sky and reached out to the ground.

"This is your last chance. Follow the will of God."

With a snap of her fingers, the ground would push and the mountains would split.

"God?"

Goffin smiled.

"Don't be mistaken."

As Goffin's eyes burned golden, the eyes of all the Gaia people lit up.

"How dare you!"

As Ikael reached out her hand, the surrounding atmosphere began to drop at an enormous speed.

"Ultima System."

A thousand Gaia people created a single thought.

"We refuse."

Boom!

The dome-shaped barrier spread on the ground blocked the descending wind pressure that was crushing the mountains.

‘What is this?’

Ikael, her pride hurt, furrowed her brow.

‘What is that light?’

What is it connected to that it exerts power that even ignores the laws of this world?

"Ikael, do not doubt."

Ikael, who had captured Goffin flying into the sky, wrinkled her nose.

‘If it's just one person...’

As she thought that, a sphere of light was born in Goffin's hand and swung fiercely.

"Photon Cannon."

The beam of light containing mass rushed towards Ikael at near light speed.

‘Ataraxia!’

Amplifying her sense of time, she dodged by leaning back, and the beam struck the plateau behind her.

Boom!

The power that blew away 20,000 tons of earth was comparable to the earlier Ultima.

‘I must eliminate that human.’

Feeling a sense of duty as an archangel, Ikael gritted her teeth and flew towards Goffin.

As the leader of the angels and the representative of the Gaia people collided head-on, the world shook.

‘How can such power exist?’

As the beams of the Photon Cannon rained down, Goffin's voice was heard.

"I told you not to doubt."

As Ikael turned her head, Goffin, who had transcended time and space, was right in front of her.

"The holy body is shaking."

The light approached Ikael's wide-open eyes at an enormous speed.

"Ugh!"

As she tried to use the power of amplification, Goffin blocked her path faster than the Photon Cannon.

"What?"

Boom!

The roar shook the heavens and the earth, and Goffin, who was hit directly by the beam, flew away with Ikael.

Thud! Thud! Thud! Thud!

Ikael, who had pierced through four peaks and crashed to the ground, immediately raised her head.

Goffin, holding her waist, didn't move an inch.

‘Why?’

As Ikael slowly reached out her hand, Goffin suddenly raised his head.

"Just as I expected."

Goffin raised one corner of his mouth and spoke.

"Angels don't have hearts."

It was natural for spiritual beings like angels, but Ikael felt strangely uncomfortable.

"There's no reason for angels to imitate the inferior human organ. We are the highest intelligence."

"Really?"

Goffin slowly stood up, grimacing, and knelt on one knee.

"Ugh!"

Through her downward gaze, Ikael saw Goffin's back, charred black.

It was a choice that couldn't be made without madness.

"Why did you do it?"

Goffin didn't answer.

"Even though such an attack wouldn't destroy me, I'm still your enemy who killed your kin?"

"I made a promise."

If Lucifer hadn't perished in Goffin's place, today's opportunity wouldn't have come.

"I can't harm you." Attacking and defending oneself was a fundamental way of thinking for Gaia people.

"What do you mean? A promise?"

Goffin scratched his sharp, wire-like hair.

‘This is awkward.’

Lucifer's face, who had asked Goffin to find the meaning of existence, flashed in his mind.

‘Aha.’

Discovering a possibility, Goffin suddenly brought his face close to Ikael's.

"Can you reproduce?"

As soon as he finished speaking, Ikael's eyes widened, and she swung her fist at an incredible speed.

"Ugh!"

Goffin, hit squarely on the chin, flew dozens of meters and crashed into a huge rock.

"How dare a lowly human insult an angel?"

Even if they borrowed a human body to produce Nephilim, it was far from an animalistic act.

Goffin approached, holding his dizzy head.

"Ugh, what a temper..."

"Shut up. This must all be your cunning. But it won't work on me."

At that moment, Goffin's eyes lit up.

"Who created this world?" Ikael, facing the unique gaze of the Gaia people, furrowed her brow and answered.

"The eternal Ra. The only god who gave light to this world and created angels."

"Do you really think so?"

Ikael's eyebrows twitched.

‘I really hate that gaze.’

It felt as if he considered not only the archangel but all the universe's creations as insignificant.

"Are you saying you're God? That Anke Ra, I, and everything is a lie?"

"No."

Goffin shook his head.

"We're no different. Even if humans created this world, who created those humans?"

The eternally repeating cycle of creation and creation.

"So I'm going to find out."

"I won't allow it."

As Ikael raised her will to attack, Goffin extended his hand and spoke.

"Want to come with me?"

"What?"

"We won't know what comes first until we go. Let's check it out together. Who knows? If we transcend infinity and reach the end..."

Goffin smiled.

"You might find the meaning of your existence there."

"The meaning of existence?"

As an archangel, she had never doubted her existence.

‘I am the first. I am the beginning.’ But looking into Goffin's eyes, she felt as if she might really escape somewhere.

‘The outer world.’

Ikael frowned and shouted.

"Nonsense! Do you think I'll be swayed by a human's words? I am Ikael, the leader of the archangels."

"Really? Then there's no choice."

Thinking he had done enough, Goffin sighed and activated the Ultima system.

Although there was a brief delay, countless Gaia people had finished preparing to leave the Photon System.

"Let's go."

As Goffin's body transformed into light, Ikael witnessed an astonishing sight.

‘Really...’

As the blue sky disappeared, signals crossed against a black background like the universe.

‘Does it really exist?’

She had to stop it.

Moreover, if it was just Goffin, she could have stopped him with the activation of Ataraxia.

"But..."

But.

As millions of beams shot up towards the sky, Ikael was stunned.

"Is it there?"

As Goffin's body dispersed into light and faded, the holy body trembled anxiously.

"I, I too..."

Ikael reached out her hand.

Anke Ra's eyes snapped open.

‘What is it?’

Before she could realize, the sensation of being electrocuted surged, and the giant eyes shook rapidly.

A voice mixed with electrical noise spread.

"Detected full-scale destruction intent towards the universe. Target, Gaia people. Code allocation, 99.8% of total. Searching for optimal response plan."

Silence fell.

And after a moment, Anke Ra, who had regained its original gaze, recited in a majestic voice.

"Activating Code Argones."
