Chapter 561:

As Maya filled her tray with food, the men's gazes followed her steadily.

It wasn't just her beautiful appearance; the extent of her transformation was astonishing.

"She's really become beautiful. The picture in the red book wasn't a lie."

Amy turned to Nade and asked, "Red book? What's that?"

"Huh? Haha! It's nothing."

It was something he could never tell Amy.

"Why is she here? Is it because of Shirone?"

Amy thought that was the only reason.

Shirone had gone to heaven, but Maya had stayed at school without showing her face even once.

She looked lonelier than before, and Shirone felt guilty for contributing to her current state.

As if reading his thoughts, Amy sighed and said, "Invite her to join us."

"But…"

Shirone wasn't so oblivious as to not realize that Amy's forgiveness was a final chance.

"It's really okay. We have 15 weeks left; we can't keep going like this."

Seeing the logic in Amy's words, Nade tried to ease Shirone's burden by shouting, "Maya, come over here! Join us!"

Maya, who had been picking at her food, put down her utensils and walked over to Shirone's group, but she didn't bring her tray.

"It's been a while, everyone."

Maya's smile was still kind, but her changed appearance made her seem completely different.

"Yeah, it's been a long time. How have you been?"

Maya just shook her head and spoke to Amy.

"I'm sorry. I didn't mean to make you uncomfortable. But I felt I had to meet and say hello at least once."

"No need for formalities between us. Come on, sit with us. We always ate together before."

"No, today will be the last time. I won't be coming to the dining hall anymore."

The entire graduation class focused on Maya's words.

"You won't come? Why?"

"You know why, Amy."

Amy's brow furrowed slightly.

"That's a bit unreasonable. You're an adult. Saying things like that just burdens us. If something's bothering you, why not face it and resolve it?"

Even after all this time, Amy's unchanged demeanor made Maya smile bitterly.

"I'm envious, Amy."

"What? Why suddenly?"

"I'm sorry. But I'm not as strong as you. Even if it burdens you, this is the only way I can cope. I know too well that I can't endure otherwise."

Maya added in a small voice, "So please bear with me. I know it's selfish, but I really intend to graduate this time."

It was clear what kind of resolve had brought her here.

"And Shirone."

"Yes?"

"Honestly, it was really hard. It hurt a lot."

"Oh, well…"

"It's okay. I can endure it now. But that doesn't mean I've completely forgotten you."

The dining hall buzzed, and amidst the noise, someone clenched their fist in anger.

'She still has feelings for Shirone…'

"So if you ever change your mind, you can come to me anytime. I'll be waiting."

"Whistle."

A whistle sound passed by.

"But if that's not the case…"

Hesitating, Maya gathered her resolve and said, "I hope we don't run into each other until the graduation exam. Can you understand my feelings?"

Shirone nodded, pushing aside his complicated emotions.

"Yes, I understand."

He felt sorry for Maya, but his heart didn't pound as it had when Amy gave him her ultimatum.

Then a thought crossed his mind.

Maybe the tears he shed last night weren't just for Feope.

Maya's eyes, expecting a desperate reaction, filled with sadness.

'What was I expecting?'

Did she think that by cornering him like this, Shirone would cry and hold onto her?

Maya shook her head inwardly.

She thought she had endured, but seeing Shirone's face made her heart race, and her months of resolve seemed to crumble like a sandcastle.

"Well then… take care."

Saying her farewell, Maya returned to her seat, handed her barely touched tray to the kitchen, and left the dining hall.

Even among the many gazes following her, Amy's was particularly distinct.

'What? You're the strong one…'

Watching Shirone chew his bread with a bitter expression, a sigh escaped from deep within Amy's chest.

Before classes started, Shirone and his friends spent time at the school store.

Shirone shared what had happened at the research group, and Amy, who had heard the full story the night before, had no trouble understanding.

However, the underground research group was new to her.

"Hide-and-Seek Research Group? There's such a group at school?"

Nade answered.

"There are a few more. As long as they have enough members, they don't need to hold presentations like us, so most people don't know about them."

The Paranormal Psychic Science Research Group had become known largely due to their presentations.

"But those juniors, I sent them back, but why were they tailing me?"

Iruki spoke.

"They probably think like I do. With graduation approaching, they're starting to move. To uncover Istas's secret."

"But it was quiet last year."

Nade stroked his chin and said, "When you refused the evaluation, rumors spread at school. Istas was off-limits for a while, even to us research group members."

"Oh…"

It was during the time when the National Intelligence Service was undercover at the school looking for Gaold.

"They're incredibly sensitive, so their switch was flipped. There's definitely something in Istas. Today it was the Hide-and-Seek Research Group, but other groups might have already approached."

Shirone spread his arms in frustration.

"Who is the president of the Hide-and-Seek Research Group? You must know."

"Yeah, I have a pretty good idea. The Hide-and-Seek Research Group runs the school's black market. I've even sold a few inventions there. The most likely candidate is…"

"It's me."

A voice interrupted, and they turned to see Prings, currently ranked third in the advanced class.

"So… it was you?"

Nade asked with a disdainful expression.

Prings had long eyelashes like flower petals and a thin mustache. He was a typical genius with a very quick mind but had a perverted and sinister personality, so he never got along with Shirone's group.

Shirone glared at him and snapped, "Why were you tailing me? And ordering juniors to use attack magic? Are you out of your mind?"

"Hehe, why so serious? The top star of the graduation class, getting worked up over some juniors' antics."

His voice was as slimy as his appearance.

"This is just the beginning. The Hide-and-Seek Research Group is everywhere. For example, we know Amy went to your room last night for a secret meeting."

Shirone's eyebrow twitched.

He wasn't careless enough to be spied on, but there was no way to hide Amy visiting his room.

'It's fine. They couldn't have gotten too close.'

While Shirone felt relieved, Amy bristled at the misunderstanding.

"Secret meeting? How can you be so vulgar in your thoughts?"

"Hehe, Amy. You still smell wonderful. No perfume can replicate your natural scent."

Prings inhaled deeply, his nostrils flaring, and his eyelids fluttered, revealing the whites of his eyes.

'Disgusting…'

His voyeuristic tendencies, obsessive focus on scents, and slimy voice made him a man Shirone would rather escape from than share the same sky with.

"Anyway, you should look forward to it. Fifteen weeks until graduation. Most of the underground research groups are gunning for you."

Nade snorted in response.

"Yeah, you'll need to gather as many as you can. We're an elite few."

Prings pointed at Shirone's group with his finger under his nose.

"A mediocre Class One, the bottom of the graduation class, and a dropout. I don't see any elite higher ranked than me."

"You little…!"

Nade stood up abruptly, but Shirone grabbed his arm to stop him.

"Calm down. If we're confident, it doesn't matter."

As Prings walked away, Nade clenched his fists in frustration.

"That guy makes me want to punch him so badly. Even more than Iruki used to!"

Iruki ignored the last comment.

"Anyway, it looks like a war is about to start. We need to be fully prepared."

Amy raised her hand.

"Wait. Why are we involved? Let me be clear, I have no interest in your 'stupid whatever' research group."

"It's Paranormal Psychic Science."

"Whatever it is, I'm not joining. Unless it's about Shirone's supernatural sense, my priority is graduation."

Shirone got to the point.

"Let's summarize. How many research groups are targeting Istas?"

"Hmm, probably all the underground research groups. The most dangerous one is Fermi's Golden Wheel."

The Golden Wheel was the core of the underground economy and had five powerful graduation class members.

"Fermi is the president, with his right-hand man, the Electric Monster Lycan. The strategist is Sona's Hershey, the operations leader is the alchemist Richard, and the fifth member is…"

Iruki pointed to the window of the store.

"Over there. Kaiden."

Kaiden of the Cross Star.

With neatly combed reddish-brown hair, he was a handsome and honest-looking man.

Nade, who knew the positions of the Golden Wheel's executives, knew little about Kaiden.

"Yeah, Kaiden makes up the five-member team. Their ranks have shifted, but they dominated the top five until the middle of the first half."

Shirone asked, "What's Kaiden's rank now?"

"He's seventh."

"Wow, still in Class One."

When Kaiden suddenly turned his head, Nade quickly looked down.

"Ugh! He's looking. Be careful. His intuition is sharp. If Dorothy has instincts, he has intuition."

As Kaiden, who had been glaring at Shirone's group, left, Nade pretended to wipe sweat from his brow.

"Whew, that was close."

Iruki asked, "Why are you so nervous? That's not like you."

"That guy is worth being nervous about."

"Oh?"

It was rare to hear such words from Nade.

"I don't really know. Kaiden doesn't act much within the Golden Wheel. He's straightforward, so I don't get why he's with Fermi."

"So you have a strange feeling about him."

In Nade's language, that meant he was dangerous.

"Well, all the Golden Wheel members are noteworthy. And Fermi made a huge amount of money recently."

'That's right.'

Shirone recalled what Miro had told him.

Fermi had earned 190 billion gold by forging signatures in the Under Coder.

Even with installment payments, over 10 billion gold was coming in, so the level of magic he could use was on a different scale.

"Alright, we've covered the Golden Wheel. What's next?"

Nade held up two fingers.

"The second strongest group is definitely the Hide-and-Seek Research Group. They run their own market, and Prings's skills are on par with the Golden Wheel. You know about Pisho's Ant Language Research Group, and the Doll Collection Research Group is also significant. The president is none other than Anchal."

"Anchal? She's in an underground research group?"

Shirone looked at the long-haired woman drinking coffee and reading a book on the terrace.

She wore an eyepatch over her left eye, but she wasn't blind.

It was said she wore it to seal the energy of the magic eye accumulated by her family over generations. If she had unsealed it during last year's graduation exam, she would have graduated at the top, according to a scout's interview.

"What's Anchal's rank now?"

"Ninth."

'Class One.'

It was impressive that she held a high rank without using her trump card.

"Golden Wheel, Hide-and-Seek, Ant Language, Doll Collection. Are all their members our enemies?"

Iruki shook his finger.

"No, there's one more. It wouldn't be right to discuss underground research groups without mentioning this one."

"Huh? What did we miss?"

Nade spoke with a serious expression.

"The Women's Anatomy Research Group."
