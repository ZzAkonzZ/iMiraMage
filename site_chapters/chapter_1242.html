Chapter 1242: The Weight of Existence (1)

While processing the Apocalypse’s information, the heads of each nation all had the same question:

If Maya must be killed…

How far does the method of her death apply?

What if the assassin is an agent of a specific country? Or if she dies by indirect means during chaos?

With unclear criteria, most countries concluded that the cleanest way would be to kill her with a blade.

The crossbowman.

The Kingdom of Iron, originally destined to become the World Leadership Nation, played its strongest card.

Kayden was cautious.

Why is this happening? This is dangerous. Why isn’t anyone stopping him? Am I the only one seeing this?

Fermi thought to himself:

If I hadn’t obtained the information about the future, Shirone wouldn’t have stopped the march of the People.

By now, the audience would’ve been swept away by the wave of the People, and in that chaos, Maya would be killed—by the Kingdom of Iron.

Fermi also thought:

But I knew, and then the Kingdom of Iron knew. So the Kingdom of Iron won’t kill Maya.

Many others likely sensed the dissonance in the prophecy that said Iron would kill Maya and become the World Leadership Nation.

And now, every country knows the truth.

If stepping back means defeat, then Iron must be preparing for both outcomes.

Since Shirone blocked the wave of the People, Iron will now try to kill Maya. And because Iron tries to kill Maya…

Kayden escapes.

That was the future Fermi had anticipated when he released the Apocalypse’s information.

Kayden hesitated.

No one else seems to see it. Or do they see it and choose not to act? Is this some kind of setup?

You can’t blame someone who doesn’t know the future for being afraid. But his right hand didn’t hesitate.

“Guh!”

As his dark sword energy ignited, a sharp arc of force sliced clean through the crossbowman’s neck.

He really killed him.

The man might not have been an assassin, but even so—Kayden’s hand would have moved.

“Kayden?”

As the applause died, Maya turned toward the back of the stage in disbelief.

And then the screams erupted.

“Ahhh! Someone’s dead! What happened?!”

El Kiana shouted,

“What is this?! A murderer?! Where are the guards?! Get that man—!”

Then she turned—and froze.

“What…?”

Hitmen from various nations had stormed the stage, drawing their weapons and charging forward.

“Get out of the way!”

“Ah—ah…”

El Kiana desperately wanted to move—but couldn’t even twitch a finger.

When she saw the lifeless gleam in the hitman’s eyes, she understood in an instant:

I’m going to die.

Just as the blade was about to strike her neck, Reina tackled her to the ground and rolled them both offstage.

“You idiot! Run!”

“Eek!”

As El Kiana fled toward the exit, Reina scanned the stage, now turned into a war zone.

Was this what Rai warned me about? But why are they targeting us? No—

She suddenly remembered the crossbowman’s position.

“Maya!”

Just as she shouted, the hitmen swarmed Maya and swung their blades.

Kill her first—

“Yaaah!”

At that moment, Kayden shouted, and a tornado erupted where Maya stood.

Maya, eyes shut tightly, peeked one eye open—and saw the broad back of Kayden in front of her.

“You okay?”

“Yeah. Who are these people?”

“I don’t know. Just moments ago, they were…”

Tearing up—cheering her with applause.

“Dark swordsman?”

A hitman wielding twin daggers in reverse grip said,

“A bit sloppy.”

They were likely top-class hitmen, but Kayden’s silence wasn’t because he feared them.

The fate of the Red Cross Star has weakened.

He could feel it—his suffocating fate, which had followed him all this time, had vanished.

Yet his dark sword energy still worked—because of his uncontrollable right arm.

I’m fighting with one arm. One I can’t even control.

Kayden decided what he had to do to protect Maya in this situation.

“Maya, run.”

Even with the enemy listening.

“I can’t win.”

Only by throwing away his pride and being honest could he get Maya to act.

“Kayden…”

“Go! Don’t make this harder!”

He was never one to say such things, so Maya, holding back tears, cast her teleportation spell.

“After her!”

The hitmen moved, and Kayden stood in their way.

“Yaaaaah!”

He shouted fiercely, but that was all he could do.

I’m the one who has to act.

Even if he didn’t fully realize it.

Please…

That he had loved Maya far more deeply than he thought.

When Lightning Impact struck the stage with a surge of blue electricity, chain explosions erupted.

The hitmen finally realized something was wrong.

If we hesitate, we’ll lose her.

But if they fought Kayden, someone else would land the kill first.

“Let’s do this together.”

As masters of efficiency, they negotiated quickly.

“We kill him first, then compete. Agreed?”

No one was weak—so they all agreed with a glance.

Kayden rejoiced internally.

Good.

His dark sword energy was strong, but his body—except for his right arm—had to move by sheer will.

If they all attack me, I’ll die. But while I’m dying, Maya can escape.

He truly prayed.

“Why don’t you know?” Seriel asked.

“You never fight battles you can’t win. There’s no such thing as something not even God knows.”

“That’s exactly it.”

Fermi turned from the window.

“Because even God doesn’t know—no one knows. No being in this universe could know.”

What Kayden would do.

“But the problem is…”

When Fermi checked earlier, Kayden’s escape applied only to his right arm.

The man himself hasn’t escaped. It’s not just about using one hand.

True escape—

Only happens when one breaks free from the prison of the body itself.

“The prison of the body…”

“Yeah.”

Fermi gave a bitter smile.

“No one knows.”

And that’s what made Kayden terrifying.

What the hell...?

Twelve of the world’s best hitmen attacked simultaneously—yet Kayden’s right arm was effortlessly blocking every strike.

This arm… what even is this thing?

Even though it was attached to his body, he couldn’t control it. It didn’t feel like his.

The hitmen’s expressions gradually twisted in frustration.

"Impressive."

These elite assassins, masters of ultra-fast kills, hadn’t been able to end the fight in over three minutes.

Is he a sword prodigy? No, something’s off. I’ve never seen swordsmanship like this. It’s unpredictable.

While the hitmen were beginning to acknowledge Kayden’s skill, he himself only felt more disturbed.

Damn it!

It felt like being a slave to a part of his own body.

No, I’m the one moving it. The hand doesn’t have eyes...

He was clearly reacting based on what he could see with his own eyes.

But still...

Kayden grimaced.

Why don’t I understand how I’m doing it?

If the movements went through his brain, and the arm could do it, then logically, he should be able to do it too.

Whatever it is—it’s inside me. I’m just refusing to face it. Desperately running away from...

The fate of the Red Cross Star.

Even if the laws had changed, Kayden’s heart was still bound to it.

Damn it...

He hated it so much.

I never cared about magic or swordsmanship. I just wanted to be a painter. I just wanted to paint Maya at her best!

In that instant, a hitman’s dagger slashed across the back of Kayden’s right thigh.

“Ugh!”

“...Got it.”

It’s just the right arm.

Outside the exit, Maya froze.

El Kiana was suspended in midair, dangling from Pope Constantine’s grip.

“M-Maya! Help me!”

As Maya took her first step toward her, she met Constantine’s eyes.

This man… isn’t human.

She felt like her breath had stopped.

“How peculiar. You and this girl. So there’s no particular regret you wish to undo?”

Who could live without regret?

But every experience had shaped her song, and Maya was grateful for her present.

“It doesn’t matter.”

That she had arrived here, in front of Maya, was surely the will of God.

“Die.”

As divine light burst from Constantine’s hand, El Kiana kicked Maya out of the way.

The light, originally meant to destroy evil, only grazed Maya’s bangs.

El Kiana shouted.

“Run!”

Maya, now on the floor, blinked and asked:

“...Why?”

El Kiana wasn’t exactly kind. She wasn’t someone who would save another while being held hostage.

Why did I do that?

Even she didn’t know.

I hate her. She’s like a perfect little angel. So annoying. She even stole my part…

El Kiana muttered.

“Kill me instead. She can’t die—she’s the star of the Holy War’s finale.”

Do you even understand what that means?

“She’s the world’s greatest... diva.”

If sound carries both emotion and information, then her voice was the most powerful wavelength of all.

Maya’s eyes welled with tears.

“El...”

The Pope interrupted.

“A diva?”

Then he began walking toward Maya.

“A song that does not praise God is heresy—a wicked spell.”

“Kayden!”

Kneeling on one leg, Kayden was still swinging his right arm when he heard Maya’s voice.

“Maya!”

Suspended beside the Pope, Maya and El Kiana were bathed in divine light.

“Behold, all of you! Witness the proof of God! The power! The age of God shall now begin, fallen humans!”

The kings of each nation had already left their seats, but key officials remained to watch the situation unfold.

That’s the Pope, isn’t it? He’s gone mad.

But Albino realized—it had finally arrived.

So this is it.

No matter how you alter the cause, the result God wants will always come to pass.

“Maya will die.”

No cause could stop it.

“No!”

Kayden shouted, but his body—except for his right arm—was already falling apart.

“O God! Accept my faith!”

Constantine screamed. His eyes glowed, and Maya’s own rolled back as if being pulled from within.

“Maya! Maya!”

Kayden once again cursed his own fate.

Why...

Why can’t I ever have what I want?

“You stubborn bastard.”

The hitmen were now within striking range of his neck, but Kayden didn’t see them anymore.

It’s not like I asked for much. Just to stay by Maya’s side. That’s all.

Was it really just that?

“I...”

As Maya’s body began to tremble, Kayden gritted his teeth and stood tall.

“I truly want this.”

Maybe what he wanted wasn’t so small after all.

“Maya.”

He wanted her whole heart.

As a hitman went in for the final strike, Kayden gripped his sword with both hands.

Dark Sword Technique – Seomhae (Flash Divide).

In a single instant, a dazzling, geometric slash carved across the stage like light itself.

“What the...!”

Before they could react, the hitmen were torn into pieces.

“Kugh!”

Even the Pope’s arms were severed at the elbows, and Maya and El Kiana dropped to the ground.

Albino’s eyes widened in shock.

“How...?”

How could the result change?

“Huff…”

As the afterimage of the slash faded, Kayden exhaled deeply and slowly turned his body.

The Pope staggered back.

“You… you...”

The core keyword of Omega—Cross Kayden.

“Step away from Maya.”

He had escaped the prison of God.
