Chapter 983: The Unchoosables (1)

"Rian, huh..."

Given that Rian, a swordsman who had reached the level of Divine Transcendence with Idea, was the standard, Nade had no choice but to give up.

Tess spoke up.

"But how will he enter the Underworld? Doesn't he have to be nearly dead?"

"Yes, exactly."

Tess's face turned pale, but the Great Star remained calm.

"In fact... being nearly dead isn't enough to enter the Underworld. People often say they hover between life and death. But even then, they only straddle the boundary between reality and the Underworld; they don't fully enter."

Shirone explained.

"Reality and the Underworld are as thin as a sheet of paper. That's why it's called the sanctuary. But the mental distance is vast. Ancient people called it the river of no return."

"Wait, Shirone! Are you saying Rian can't come back?"

"Seekers call crossing the boundary between life and death 'Cheoeni Bardo.' But even I can't do it now. Jinseong-eum, the princess of Jincheon, completely sealed the passage from the Underworld to reality."

Miro added.

"Thanks to her, humanity has hope."

"Yes. If demons born from the fires of hell kept crossing into the real world, victory would be impossible. It was an incredible achievement, but the price she paid was..."

Eternal suffering in hell.

"So you're saying Rian should endure it too? Just because Jinseong-eum did it, Rian should be prepared to die?"

Shirone understood Tess's feelings.

"Don't misunderstand. While that's partly what I meant, there is a way to come back. If we free Seongeum from hell, the barrier will disappear."

"How do we free her? Is it even possible? Has anyone ever purified hell?"

Even Goffin couldn't do it.

The Great Star spoke.

"The problem comes after that. Shirone can cross into the Underworld through the sanctuary. But Rian has to use a brutal method. The best way is to burn him."

Tess was on the verge of fainting.

"Rian has a strong mind. He can withstand most shocks. By burning him endlessly, we can concentrate emotions to a level that reality can't accept."

"No, how can anyone do that..."

"It's impossible."

When Rian interrupted, Tess quickly turned to him.

"Right? You think so too, right? Setting a living person on fire..."

"No, it's the opposite. I can't guarantee it since I haven't experienced it, but even if I'm burned, my mind won't waver. Something stronger is needed."

While Tess stood with her mouth agape, the Great Star smiled in satisfaction.

"That's why we need you, Rian. If you fall into hell without your sanity, you'll just be devoured by demons. The method doesn't matter. Fire helps gather emotions, but what's truly important is your will."

"I understand."

Rian looked around the Grand Hall and then directly at Shirone.

"I'm going too, Shirone."

The two exchanged a look of unwavering trust, and Tess bowed her head sadly.

Pony spoke.

"It's not just Bashka; other areas are also in trouble. The hell army has spread out, but there are still many demons in the central and southern regions."

Shirone turned to her.

"Yes. I was about to mention that."

Amy asked.

"You followed Dante. Did you reach Creas?"

Amy's hometown, Rian's hometown, and most importantly, Shirone's adoptive parents were there.

"Not yet."

"You didn't make it? What happened?"

"Well..."

Shirone began to speak.

From the mountain peak overlooking the city of Creas, Dante looked horrified.

"It's overwhelming..."

Hundreds of thousands of demons were besieging the city walls, waging a siege.

Giant monsters led the charge, breaking through the iron-plated gates, and demons followed through the gaps.

"Let's go. There's no time."

As Dante spoke, Shirone looked back.

Thousands of refugees rescued from various regions stood there, looking exhausted.

"Go there...?"

An old man with a cane spoke on behalf of the refugees.

"We won't be of any help. We might handle bandits, but how can we deal with those monsters..."

Eden spoke.

"I'll stay and protect the people. You go and help the others."

With such a large group, it was only a matter of time before flying demons spotted them.

'Eden's defensive magic. Even in the worst situation, it should hold for a few hours.'

Having made his decision, Shirone spoke.

"Alright, let's go. Dante, assess the situation at the Creas branch of the Magic Association. Lyria, support the city walls. I'll go inside the city and rescue people."

Even as he spoke, flames were rising within the city.

As Shirone prepared to cast a teleportation spell, Dante called out urgently.

"Shirone."

"Yes?"

"Visit the Ogent family first."

Shirone couldn't respond.

"Go. You have every right. If you don't save your parents first, no one in the world will help you with a good heart."

"...Thank you, Dante."

Dante nodded, and Shirone's body transformed into a flash of light, flying towards the city.

Landing precisely in the center of the Ogent family's estate, Shirone heard a familiar voice.

"Take that! Take that!"

In front of the main building, Reina was valiantly shooting arrows at the demons.

Her rhythm was excellent, and the demons fell one by one, but when a commander-level demon approached, her arrows were ineffective.

The commander, covered in scales, deflected the arrows with his forearm as he advanced.

"Keke, a human daring to challenge demons. It's been a while since I've found such an amusing toy. I'll tear off your limbs one by one."

Reina calmly drew her bowstring to its limit.

A powerful arrow capable of piercing scales was shot, but the demon caught it mid-air and charged.

"Kahaha! A mere human dares to...! Ugh!"

Before he could finish, the Hand of God flew in and struck the commander with the back of its hand.

Simultaneously, the hand of light swept the ground, capturing the demons and flinging them into the sky.

It was literally the hand of a god.

Reina, who had been watching in a daze, turned her gaze forward.

With a thud, her bow fell to the ground.

"Shirone!"

Tears welled up in her eyes as she ran to him, grabbing his hands and shouting.

"What happened? Why are you here?"

"It's a long story. Where's Uncle Bishoff?"

"He's fighting at the city walls. Your parents took refuge at the magic school. It's probably the safest place."

'Indeed, the army can't hold them off.'

On the other hand, the Alpheas Magic School had a rare certified second-class magician from the association.

'Vice Principal Olivia.'

Reina continued.

"I took your parents to the magic school and came back briefly, but I got surrounded. I had to protect the family's seal."

"I see."

In a knight's family, the seal was as important as the heart.

Shirone was grateful that Reina had prioritized evacuating his parents over such an important item.

"That's really fortunate. Honestly, I was half giving up. Let's get to the magic school quickly..."

At that moment, an explosion sounded from the other side of the city, and a mushroom cloud of fire rose.

"What's that?"

It was where the magic school was located.

The Alpheas Magic School had a campus as large as a small village in other regions.

Demons that had crossed the mountains gathered at the central building, which had turned into a fortress, and launched a siege.

Despite the high level of the teachers from the Five Great Families, the demons were visibly taken aback.

But being a warlike race, they began to launch a massive offensive.

Saad, who was bombing the ground with fire magic, looked back at Alpheas and shouted.

"Principal! The defensive line has been breached!"

The wrinkles around Alpheas's eyes deepened.

"...Teachers, evacuate the students and citizens. I'll buy you some time."

Siena spoke.

"I can't leave you behind, Principal. I'll stay here instead."

"This is no time for stubbornness. Shouldn't the students survive before the teachers?"

"Then I'll stay."

Olivia, with a cold gaze, stood next to Alpheas, looking out the window.

"What can an old man do to buy time? Leave it to me. I'll get out alive."

Everyone knew it was bravado, and Alpheas smiled faintly.

"Keke, I may be an old man, but..."

As he walked to the window, a terrifying killing intent emanated from his body.

"Have you forgotten who I am?"

Mirhi Alpheas.

Seeing Alpheas's back overlapping with his younger self, Olivia's expression softened.

"...Foolish."

Even at this age, she wanted to spend her last moments with the man she loved.

As she approached Alpheas, she spoke to the other teachers.

"Go. We'll handle this."

The noble atmosphere from their relationship left the teachers with no room for argument.

"We'll come back."

With those words, Saad left the room, and the other teachers quickly followed.

As they opened the iron door to the basement, the advanced students waited anxiously.

Behind the only graduating students, Mark and Maria, were Shirone's parents.

Saad looked at the students and spoke.

"From now on, stay calm. We're leaving the magic school. We'll take you to a safe place."

If such a place existed in the world.

Even the advanced students, aspiring magicians, followed the teachers without faltering.

As they were about to exit through the back door, Siena, who was scouting ahead, raised her hand.

"Demons are coming."

A small but elite infiltration team was approaching, trying to bypass the building.

"Damn it! At this time..."

"I'll distract them. Take the kids and run."

Planning to divert their attention before they found the door, Siena cast a teleportation spell.

There was no time to stop her, and even if there was, the teachers' roles were clear.

"There! After her!"

The demon team chased after Siena, firing explosive spikes from their backs.

Hundreds of spikes, trailing flames, bombarded the ground, shaking the entire area.

"Ugh!"

She spread cold to block the heat, but the shockwave left her ears ringing.

'What kind of firepower is this...'

She tried to counterattack with ice magic, but the demons' physical abilities were beyond imagination.

More spikes were fired, this time homing in on her.

'Homing missiles.'

Biting her lip, she waited for the right moment to teleport.

The spikes collided and exploded, sending her flying and rolling on the ground.

'It's frustrating.'

Before the world fell into this state, she had a certain pride.

-You'll be a great teacher.

Armin had known.

The enemies that would appear in the future were beyond what mere humans could handle.

"Don't be ridiculous."

Gathering strength in her trembling legs, Siena raised the Absolute Zero barrier.

Though it was a localized spell, it could at least take a few enemies with her.

"Come at me, you bastards!"

How many times in her life had she uttered such harsh words?

Despite her resolve to die, the demons only lunged at her with greedy eyes.

"I won't kill you. There's a lot I want to hear."

As Siena raised her hand and hundreds of spikes flew in from both sides,

'Huh?'

The world suddenly tilted, and the spikes fell to the ground.

'What?'

Had the planet flipped?

'No...'

She was the one moving.

By the time she realized it, there was no one around, and she was standing alone on the ground.

"Argh!"

A short scream from a demon.

Turning her head, she saw the entire demon team lying on the ground, decapitated.

"What...?"

And at the center of the bloody scene, she saw the back of a man.

He had one arm.
