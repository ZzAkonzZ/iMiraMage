Chapter 947: All Histories (2)

"Freedom!"

Smodo’s gaunt body was fully exposed, and Tess’s face went pale.

His entire body was adorned with dangling piercings, his skin bluish and decaying like a corpse.

"Freeeedom! Freeeedom!"

What had she even expected?

Tess despaired, realizing no form of revenge could rid her of the suffocating sense of futility.

"Hahaha! I am free!"

Blood trickled down as Smodo ripped out his piercings one by one with his uninjured pinky finger.

"Die."

Even without her personal hatred for him, the sight was something Tess wanted eradicated from the world.

At that moment, the wall of the control room was ripped apart.

"Why are you so late?"

The brigade commander and a group of demons had arrived to check the sluggish battle progress.

‘What is this?’

Noticing the states of Zetar and Smodo, the commander’s focus shifted to the absence of their guard detail.

‘These fools...’

Even though Satan had favored Havitz, demons inherently despised humans.

Especially now, with military control shifting from Balcan to Paimon, there was no reason for the demons to care about the safety of the two.

‘Tch, still, they are Satan’s favored pawns.’

The brigade commander decided it would be troublesome if they were killed and tucked Zetar and Smodo under each arm.

Smodo had already lost consciousness.

"Return to the base! Prepare those with regenerative abilities!"

"Stop!"

Tess pulled her saber from the ground and charged, but the commander had already exited the biochemical structure.

"Damn it!"

Though Smodo’s injuries would delay any further attempts to seize the bioweapon, Tess felt no satisfaction.

‘They’re not human.’

The sight of Smodo and Zetar slung over the demon’s arms appeared monstrous to her.

All Histories (2)

A rhythmic sound of cracking ribs filled the air.

If they weren’t his own bones, Rian might have found the sound pleasant.

‘Unpredictable.’

Natasha's fluid, unpredictable movements relentlessly exploited Rian’s blind spots.

The Polyrhythmic Concerto.

A rapid-tempo dance piece by Rastani, infamous for its sudden, jarring shifts in rhythm.

Thud. Thud. Thud.

What bothered Rian more than the sound of his bones breaking was that his regenerative speed was noticeably slower than before.

‘No, perhaps this is normal.’

The abnormal strength of his right arm was gradually equalizing with his left.

This confirmed that Ymir's arm, which he had consumed, was no longer within his body.

‘Why would Ymir…’

Even knowing the answer, Rian couldn’t help but question why Ymir had given up her arm to regenerate his.

‘Thanks to that, I survived countless deadly encounters. And now, I’ve reached Idea.’

If this was what Ymir had intended, then…

‘Very well.’

Rian’s eyes sharpened as he dashed forward.

‘From now on, it’s my path alone.’

He wasn’t one for deep thinking, but his unyielding optimism, even in dire circumstances, was his greatest strength.

"Here I come!"

As his massive blade fell vertically, Natasha spun mid-air, narrowly dodging it.

The blade grazed the tip of her nose, the taut thread of tension between them stretched to its breaking point.

Rian felt no disappointment.

‘Her talent is extraordinary, but it’s her manifestation technique...’

Manifestations reflect a person’s internal traits in physical form.

Natasha’s skeletal avatar suggested that something within her had already died.

‘Does she see no meaning in life?’

As her afterimages flickered left and right in Rian’s vision, dizziness overwhelmed him.

It was as if she was on his right one moment, then on his left the next.

‘She’s ridiculously skilled.’

Natasha’s combat skills, amplified by her manifestation, seemed to push her physical abilities to the limit of natural law.

But the cost was apparent:

A puppet.

Natasha was a puppet unable to dance on her own.

Three frames later, Natasha’s fist struck Rian’s solar plexus.

"Ugh!"

Even as he brought his greatsword to his chest, he was already too late.

Natasha asked, "You can see my movements, can’t you?"

Even the ability to consciously activate a phenomenon like momentary flashes placed Rian among the elite.

"Then do you know why you can’t evade?"

She raised a finger.

"Rhythm."

Momentary flashes existed in the realm of perception.

Even if one saw an arrow flying in slow motion, grabbing it required a separate skill.

Though Rian’s divine transcendence allowed his body to move as fast as his thoughts, that alone wasn’t enough...

"You have no sense of rhythm."

If an opponent’s unfamiliar rhythm was mixed in, it rendered Rian’s perception meaningless.

‘The Seesaw of Time isn’t magic.’

If his body couldn’t follow the rhythm, it would remain incapable even within a flash state.

Natasha tilted her finger sideways, flicking her wrist as if keeping time.

"Feel that?"

Her finger… moved up and down in rhythm.

She introduced irregular beats into the rhythm.

"What about now? Notice any difference from before?"

When Rian stayed silent, Natasha nodded as if her analysis was complete.

"Do you know why rhythm matters? Every event in the world unfolds along the flow of time. Someone without rhythm merely reacts to events as they come. But with rhythm, you can do much more. If you divide an event into three beats, you think on the first, prepare on the second, and act on the third. But you only react when it’s right in front of you."

Rian couldn’t argue.

"Another advantage of rhythm is that you can draw your opponent’s rhythm toward yours. After about five minutes of fighting, even the strongest adversaries reveal their rhythm. That’s where the real mind games start—the fun part of combat. But…"

Natasha hesitated before continuing.

"You lack rhythm entirely. Honestly, fighting you feels like wrestling a mindless beast. Just overwhelmingly strong, but…"

Rian muttered, "That’s my way."

"And it’s wrong. You’ve survived this long probably because you overpower most opponents before they can grasp your rhythm. But once someone adapts to you—like me—your combat skills feel like a child’s nursery rhyme. A tone-deaf one, at that."

Her words stung.

But Rian tightened his grip.

"Even so, that’s my sword."

Natasha raised her guard, a bewildered look on her face.

‘Is he an idiot?’

Despite her pointed critique, he didn’t attempt to analyze or adapt.

‘Not everyone is like Daphne.’

Natasha had known rivals who, unlike Rian, let their resentment fester into sabotage and betrayal.

"Very well. Let’s finish this."

Her gaze turned deadly.

In the most ferocious and rapid dance she knew:

Night of Frenzy.

Natasha lunged, her kick smashing into Rian’s side.

"Ugh!"

"If you can't keep up with the rhythm, you have no choice but to catch up with strength!"

As I twisted my waist with all my might, Natasha finally came into view.

"I see her."

In the extreme rush of thoughts, her body flickered like a light.

"What is this?"

It meant that Natasha was repeatedly moving in and out of Rian's perception.

1/40,000th of a beat.

That was the rhythm she dealt with a single event.

"I have no choice but to strike!"

The moment Rian swung his sword vertically, Natasha turned her body and approached the side of the blade.

Ting. Tingting.

Even with the trick of flicking the blade with her fingernails, Rian couldn't react.

The rhythm was still there.

Natasha was staring intently at Rian, who was tracking her...

"Change the rhythm here."

She suddenly twisted her body in the opposite direction.

"Damn it!"

For the first time, despair bloomed in Rian's heart as he lost Natasha again.

"Is it really impossible to win?" To hold onto his crumbling heart, Rian swung his sword while screaming like a beast.

It was the end of brutality, but for Natasha, who fought with rhythm, it was rather tricky.

"Even a graze would be fatal."

1/80,000th of a beat.

By breaking the rhythm into even smaller pieces, Natasha regained her composure and launched a series of attacks.

Rian's body was covered in fist marks.

"Damn it! Damn it!"

Feeling the frustration of not being able to respond even while seeing it, Rian finally admitted.

"I don't belong with the sword."

No talent.

"Someone like me stopping Ymir? No, it's better to leave it to this woman."

Whoever wins will show a great fight.

"Why me?"

If Brother Guy were alive, or if Brother Rai had received the idea instead.

"Even Sister Reina is better than me!" She even had blue hair.

"A guy who can't do anything! What am I trying to do here..."

When Natasha's fist destroyed Rian's left eye socket, half of his vision disappeared.

"Why, why does it have to be me!" Because it's you.

...

It seemed like every cell in Rian's body was speaking.

Whether it was a positive thought like a flame or a conclusion drawn by the Ogent in his cells, it was unknown.

But what passed by faster than a rush of thoughts was the image of Ogent racing through heaven.

Even losing the human form, shouting Smille's name while wielding the sword.

"Not beautiful at all."

Ogent saved Smille against the entire heaven.

"I see."

Rian's dream was to become a swordsman who guards the brightest shining magician in human history.

"It was that simple."

With a face twisted like a demon, Rian grabbed the sword with both hands and twisted his body.

Natasha's pupils shook.

"What is this?"

Rian was still facing away, but the face of a demon that seemed to have already turned appeared translucently.

"Rrrrr!"

As guided by the will of divine transcendence, Rian's body began to absorb rotational force.

"Whether I have talent or not!"

"Incredible speed...!"

Natasha leaned back and moved away.

"Whether you mock me or not!"

Following the afterimage of the sword, the surrounding air shattered like glass.

"Whether human or not!" What does it matter?

"If I can do it, that's enough!" Natasha found the sword approaching her waist and got goosebumps.

1/160,000th of a beat.

As she split the rhythm even more sharply, she felt relieved that she still had room.

"Thunk?"

She heard something snap in her head.

The rhythm was being destroyed.

"Unorthodox rhythm."

What kind of rhythm is it that isn't contained even when split into 160,000 pieces?

"That can't be!"

Natasha, whose pride was hurt, felt the rhythm even faster.

1/320,000th of a beat.

But what she heard in her head was not a rhythmic beat.

Tututututututu! Tututututututu!

Only the perfect noise of unorthodox rhythm that destroyed the rhythm entirely.

"This guy."

Natasha realized.

"Really has no talent."

If having no talent is considered a talent, is Rian the nemesis of geniuses?

"Does that make sense?" Having no talent as a talent.

As if answering, the tip of the sword penetrated deep into Natasha's abdomen, sending a tingling sensation.

"Oh, I see."

To Natasha, it was just a flaw.

"But to this man, it's life."

Something he had to carry in his heart for a lifetime, endlessly swinging the sword to overcome...

"Yaaaaaah!"

Because it was his life itself.

"Huh!"

As blood spurted from her stomach like a fountain, Natasha let out a groan without realizing it.

Suddenly, this thought crossed her mind.

"I..."

Are we overthinking things too much?

Are we running away with countless thoughts because we can't do just one action to the best of our ability?

"See, Daphne."

A friend who dreamed together.

"You can do it."

Natasha smiled bitterly.

"Still..."

If she had eyelids, she would have smiled kindly.

"I'm sorry."
