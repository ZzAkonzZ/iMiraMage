Chapter 528:

Northern Aymond Republic.

The Northern Aymond Republic, ruled by the iron-fisted dictator Igor, was infamous for its reign of terror.

The society was extremely closed off, its citizens were aggressive, and their tendency to carry out suicide attacks in matters directly related to national interest made them the "problem child" of the world—too dangerous even for superpowers to easily conquer.

Although the country enforced brutal laws, such as cutting off a thief’s hand, crime was rampant.

With "combat ability" considered the foremost national virtue, even street children could often be seen carrying small knives.

The only port city open to foreigners, Dormica, was a ticking time bomb in the western continent—where all sorts of war weapons were traded freely.

Mercenaries, pirates, and retired soldiers had made the city their home, and law enforcement was practically non-existent.

Even Igor’s absolute power couldn’t eradicate the dark forces within, because the so-called security forces were no different from the other factions.

They often allowed illegal arms deals in exchange for money, and occasionally, open war broke out between the guards and rival organizations.

Most ships docking at the port were loaded with weapons, and not a single tourist could be seen anywhere.

So it was only natural that all eyes turned to the woman with blue hair who had just arrived at the port.

"Wow, so this is Northern Aymond."

Miro looked unmistakably like a tourist.

She had braided her long hair like a scarf, wore light makeup, flashy accessories perfect for attracting trouble, a long sweater with sleeves past her wrists, and a skirt short enough to expose her thighs—altogether giving off a cheerful, naive impression.

In Dormica, women dressed like that were usually either sex workers or total airheads.

"Whoo-wee! You’ve got guts, lady. Walking around with that much skin showing in a place crawling with hungry wolves? You asking to be eaten?"

Some dockworkers—fish suppliers—approached her.

They reeked of fish in their waterproof overalls and had a mess of kitchen knives hanging from their belts.

A bald man threw his arm around Miro’s shoulder, bringing his face right up to hers.

"How about hanging out with us? We know a place that’s real fun."

"Oh, really? I was looking for somewhere like that. Where is it?"

"Where else? Right here."

The man grabbed Miro’s hand and shoved it toward his groin.

"Ha ha! What do you think? Puts a blue whale to shame, right? Once I catch a fish, I never—GAAAAAH!"

The man’s eyes bulged in pain as he collapsed to his knees, clutching between his legs.

"M-My...!"

The other workers stared in disbelief, then drew their knives and surrounded Miro.

"You crazy or what?! You know who we are? You mess with James Fisheries, you’re fish food by sundown!"

Miro wiped her hand on her clothes like she'd touched something disgusting and calmly held it out.

"Yeah, yeah. So, any of you happen to know where the Osto’s Tavern is?"

The men’s steps halted.

Osto’s Tavern was known as the headquarters of one of the three major forces that upheld their own laws in lawless Dormica.

"Who the hell are you? If you think name-dropping’s gonna save you, you’d better imagine real hard what’s about to happen to you."

Sometimes, desperate nobodies invoked the tavern’s name to avoid getting killed—using the tavern’s "no attacking outsiders" policy as a shield.

"I’m new to the area, that’s all. Heard they’re based at the Osto’s Tavern."

Miro showed not the slightest fear, just looked around casually as she approached them, prompting the dockworkers to rethink.

"Who are you looking for there?"

Miro glanced at the storefronts and turned back with a smile.

"The Fallen Madonna."

Following the directions she got, Miro arrived in front of a four-story building.

The first and second floors were the tavern, the third a lodging house, and the fourth a gambling den.

But Miro headed for a place not open to the public—though everyone knew it existed—deep beneath the tavern.

Descending to the second basement floor, she found two swordsmen standing guard.

Even above ground, there’d been tension, but here the air was thick with killing intent.

"Hmm. Not bad at all."

If these were the gatekeepers, this place must’ve bounced back from ruin to nearly its peak strength.

And it had all been accomplished in just a year—that made it even more impressive.

"Hey, who are you? Who let you in?"

"No one let me in?"

The guards glanced at each other before drawing their swords.

"State your identity. This isn’t a place for games."

"Really? I’m being serious, though."

Miro walked up and raised both hands.

A gust of wind-like pressure surged forward—the manifestation of the Avalokiteśvara of the Prajna—and blew the guards aside, forcing the door open.

Inside the 40-pyong room, around ten people were scattered about, killing time however they pleased.

The stone-walled space had the gloom of a crypt, but the most striking thing was how no one reacted to the intruder.

Three men at a table didn’t even glance her way as they played cards and smoked.

"Agh! We’re under attack!"

One of the guards crawled in to report, but still, there was no response.

A man lying in a hammock, chugging booze, belched loudly and turned his head.

"We know, dumbass. We’ve got brains too! Whose guest is it this time? Snyde, yours?"

A burly man holding a thick cigar drew another card.

"Beats me. Never seen a hooker that pretty around here."

A brief silence followed.

Then the man in the hammock shouted irritably.

"Dammit! Would someone just do something?! They said we’re under attack!"

"Then why don’t you do something?"

"Pwahahaha!"

Miro simply watched as the lieutenants all continued doing their own thing.

Just then, a woman’s voice came from behind the burly man.

"So, who are you?"

He shifted to reveal a short-haired woman at the table, flipping an abacus like a cat.

Next to her stood a pale man with no eyebrows, his expression blank as a wax figure.

"You the Fallen Madonna?"

"That’s what some idiots call me. But I asked who you are."

Miro ignored the question and walked forward.

"I thought you’d learned your lesson after what happened in Galliant. Still playing at bandits, huh?"

Click. Click.

The moment she finished speaking, everyone in the room raised their weapons at her.

It was so swift, it felt like two frames of a movie had swapped in an instant.

Miro glanced to her right.

The eyebrow-less man had closed in, pressing a magic bullet-loaded gun to her temple.

"This is your last chance. Identify yourself."

"Who knows? Any guesses?"

As his finger started to squeeze the trigger, the woman spoke.

"Stand down, Freeman."

Miro gave her a passing grade.

If someone couldn’t at least assess her abilities, they had no business entering the Abyss.

"You mentioned Galliant. So you already know who I am, huh?"

"Of course. You’re Clay Marsha, leader of the Parrot Bandits."

Previously defeated in a fierce battle against Shirone’s group on Galliant Island.

According to Teraje’s intelligence, she was a master strategist and a Regulation Exception user.

She once used trauma related to her adoptive father to extract others’ magic, but after being hit by Shirone’s "Catharsis," she likely had a different ability now.

Marsha finally pushed her paperwork aside, turned toward Miro, and crossed her legs.

She pulled out a long pipe and put it to her lips; Freeman returned to light it instantly.

"Yeah, I’m Clay Marsha. What, were you expecting a winged angel?"

"Yikes. Shirone would be hurt to hear that."

Marsha’s eyebrow twitched slightly at the name but she calmly puffed her pipe again.

"Oh, that kid? I don’t know how you know Shirone, but real life’s not so simple. Banditry’s all I know."

Some strength could only be proven by cutting away what mattered.

Realizing she wasn’t dealing with just anyone, Miro got to the point.

"Shirone’s in a bit of trouble. I think your help would really turn the tide."

Marsha didn’t even pretend to think about it.

"Sorry. Busy these days. Find someone else."

"It’s actually a pretty serious problem. He might never wake up again."

Even without knowing the full story, Marsha could picture it clearly.

Once again, he must have thrown everything away for someone else—not for himself.

"That’s unfortunate. But as you probably already know, Shirone’s not really my type."

After leaving Galliant, she had roamed the world, building up her power. She’d gathered powerful subordinates and settled in the port city of Northern Aymond, where she fought fiercely day after day. Over time, her memories of Shirone had gradually faded.

"Alright. Looks like I came to the wrong place. Sorry for wasting your time."

Without hesitation, Miro turned on her heel and patted the shoulder of the guard still grinding his teeth, then stepped out of the room.

Fermi had asked for someone who had a deep emotional bond with Shirone, and Marsha—who had once experienced Catharsis—had been the top candidate. But if her heart had already moved on, there was no need to waste time.

"Wait."

Miro paused and turned back at Marsha’s voice.

"Why me? If someone like you is out looking for support, there must be plenty of people to choose from."

"Hmm, I’m not really sure either..."

Miro shrugged.

"Maybe because you’re someone Shirone held close in his heart."

"..."

Marsha narrowed her eyes and exhaled a long stream of smoke.

"Clear the room."

The lieutenants filed past Miro like water flowing around a rock and vanished from the room in an instant.

A restaurant in the Kingdom of Baiden.

Having finally left the village of Rafne and reached a place that could properly be called a city, Rian was stuffing himself with meat like he was trying to make up for every missed meal.

With his solid build, the greatsword strapped to his back, and his voracious appetite, he drew the attention of everyone in the restaurant.

"Mister! One more steak, please!"

The owner, clearly frightened, grilled another piece of meat and placed it in front of Rian.

It was already his fourth steak.

From the looks of him, he didn’t seem particularly wealthy, but the heavy aura he radiated kept the owner from saying anything.

"Uh, excuse me, sir..."

"Yes?"

"I-I’m sorry, really sorry, but..."

The restaurant owner looked like he was about to cry.

"You will pay, right?"

Rian blinked at him for a moment, then finally realized what was going on and put his utensils down.

"Ah, I’m sorry. I guess I do look suspicious like this. I’ll pay in advance."

He handed over all the travel funds he had, and only then did the owner finally relax.

Perhaps emboldened by the friendly gesture, the owner carefully asked,

"By any chance... are you that famous knight of Maha?"

"The Knight of Maha?"

News about Rian had spread quickly thanks to Lambda’s informants, but having just arrived in the city, Rian hadn’t heard a word of it.

He didn’t even know the name of the man he’d cut down—Lambda.

"Yes, the Knight of Maha! According to the rumors, he’s a giant like you and wields a massive blade with one hand. A fearsome warrior."

"Hmm..."

Rian thought for a moment and shook his head.

"I’m just an unknown swordsman. Sounds like it’s someone else."

When he answered honestly, a few customers groaned in annoyance.

Rumors were often exaggerated, and seeing Rian’s quiet demeanor had made them overly cautious.

"Tch, so he’s a fake?"

"Seriously. Who carries around a sword that big and doesn’t know how to use it?"

"This place is crawling with wannabes. Someone gets famous, and everyone starts pretending to be them."

"Still, how could he compare to the real Knight of Maha? They say he single-handedly took out a bandit gang of over a hundred!"

While conversations buzzed around him, Rian remained entirely focused on his meal.

Then the restaurant door burst open, and in came a hairy brute, a head taller than Rian, with a massive double-edged saw blade strapped to his back.

"Hey! Where’s the owner? Bring your finest steak and best booze for the Knight of Maha!"

"The... the Knight of Maha?"

Everyone’s eyes turned to him.

Now that he’d heard the name, even Rian paused mid-bite to glance over.

‘So that’s the Knight of Maha? He’s definitely big... I wonder if he learned swordsmanship similar to mine.’

Satisfied with the attention, the brute cocked his chin arrogantly and jabbed a thumb at himself.

"That’s right! I’m the future greatsword master who cut down the entire Red Spear Bandits—the Knight of Maha!"

PFFFFFT!

Everything Rian had been chewing exploded out of his mouth.
