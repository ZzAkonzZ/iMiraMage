Chapter 1117: Depths of the Abyss (1)

Kaden put all his strength into it, but his fist ended up slicing through empty air.

'Huh? Empty air?'

His movement stopped, but the people in the banquet hall were busy looking around.

"What? What's going on?"

Not only Marcus, but the positions of everyone in the banquet hall had changed.

Uorin frowned.

"Ugh."

Shirone noticed her expression but focused on assessing the situation first.

"Did something just…?"

"I don't know."

Lupist shook his head.

"Something definitely happened, but I don't know what. Was I standing next to you?"

Shirone retraced his steps.

'The process of getting here. I met Lupist in the hallway after leaving the room…'

As he finished recalling, he felt a chill.

'I definitely moved.'

He had shifted about 3 centimeters from his remembered position.

Moreover, among those whose positions had changed, his was the shortest distance.

'I wasn't forcibly moved.' If something moved, it was probably her mind.

'I came here of my own will. Something happened that changed my mind.'

It felt like one of two events had been chosen, like a quantum superposition.

Thus, those without temporal perception would feel an awkward sense of déjà vu.

Lupist asked.

"Can you explain it?"

"I have a guess. But what's hard to understand is the scale; it's too large."

Quantum superposition only affects where Shirone's actions occur…

'But now, everyone's positions have changed. In other words, the entire event has been twisted.'

It's beyond the level of temporal perception.

"This is the result of hundreds of wills being twisted simultaneously. Who could do this?"

"Indeed."

Lupist rested his chin on his hand.

"As you said, it's not spatial movement. I'm standing here of my own will. But I have no memory of getting here. I was definitely over there."

He pointed to a corner of the banquet hall.

"Plu was next to me, and I was meeting with an official from the Kingdom of Mun. The memory is clear. But the problem is, despite that, I feel like I came here of my own will."

"That's the key."

Is it possible to accept a sudden change in position without memory?

"There is a similar case."

Shirone thought of a man's face.

"Shagal of the Swift Sword. His life was filled with memories implanted by Ra Enemy. That made him a murderer, and now he's fallen into hell."

"So, we've all experienced the same phenomenon as Shagal, to varying degrees."

"Yes. Shagal's entire life was taken. Anyway, changing the cause to control the result requires authority at the level of Anke Ra."

Lupist summarized.

"So, the current situation involves a power that changes the mind and quantum signals. And its power is…"

His gaze turned to the floor.

"Enough to push Yahweh's mind by 3 centimeters?"

Considering everyone's positions had changed significantly, Shirone had almost stayed in place.

'A mind that doesn't waver under any variable.'

But the fact that even Yahweh's steps had moved slightly was serious.

Lupist murmured.

"The system has changed…"

Shirone thought the same but remained silent this time.

'This is definitely the work of the outside world. It's too soon to share this information.'

Of course, some knew, and one of them might have reached the correct answer.

'Uorin.'

When Shirone turned his gaze, Uorin was hurriedly leaving the banquet hall.

"Still, questions remain."

Lupist asked.

"Yes. It's the result of everyone's will changing. Minds twisted, and I'm here. But then…"

He pointed at Kaden.

"Why did he swing his fist? If Marcus wasn't in front of him, his right hand shouldn't have moved."

'Yes, this is strange.'

As Shirone turned his head, Kaden looked at his right hand, frowning.

'What did I do?'

He remembered Marcus harassing Maya, but he understood that Marcus wasn't in front of him.

'It was just a delusion. That guy never actually approached me. So why did I…'

Why did he try to attack Marcus?

'Was I so angry just thinking about it? Is that really it?'

His right hand gave no answer.

As everyone felt similar confusion, Fermi's cold gaze focused on Kaden.

'So that's it.'

It was one of the unanalysed keywords obtained from the Apocalypse.

'Breakout.'

A phenomenon of escaping the prison called the body, which allowed no gaps.

Uorin's face was covered in cold sweat as she left the banquet hall.

'This is impossible. How could this happen…'

When the positions of the people in the banquet hall changed, her foresight twisted sharply.

'The future has changed.'

The lines of light swayed to the point of blinding, and a tremendous shock hit her brain.

'The system. Changing it itself is beyond human capability. If it's possible…

"Uorin—"

She flinched.

When she turned around, she saw Shirone quickly walking down the empty hallway.

"…Hmph."

She knew he would follow her, but she still felt a bit disappointed.

'He only seeks me out at times like this.'

Shirone spoke.

"You know what happened just now, don't you?"

"I don't. And even if I did, I have no reason to explain it to you. We're competitors."

The irony was that to win Shirone's heart, she had to defeat him.

"From a god's perspective."

He had shared the secret of the outside world with Fermi, so Uorin must have heard it too.

"This is something only a god can do. But I don't know exactly what happened."

"Sigh."

Uorin sighed.

"Follow me."

She led him to the Kashan area, checked the surroundings, and entered her room.

"No one will come for the next 35 minutes."

There were no traces of any significant events in the hallway, as seen through her foresight.

"In other words, we only have 35 minutes. I'll explain briefly. I also have information from you."

Uorin organized her thoughts.

"Twelve countries gathered in the banquet hall. Some, like mine, had their kings attend, but a few didn't. The culprit is likely from one of those countries."

"Mun. Southern. And…"

Shirone spoke in a low voice.

"Paras."

"Yes. If a god's power was at work in the banquet hall, Paras, with the Pyramid of Truth, is a strong candidate. You can guess that King Kitra entered the pyramid."

"It seems the emotional disease was cured too. The question is, how did they change the system?"

"The Zodiac."

Uorin raised her index finger.

"The movement of the stars."

Some people had left the banquet hall, including Jin Kang, the emperor of Jincheon.

"Explain."

He asked bluntly, and Anchal, who had approached, bowed and spoke.

"The Jincheon Space Agency is investigating. Nothing special has happened. But…"

"But?"

"The position of the stars has changed."

Jin Kang's expression turned displeased, and Anchal quickly continued.

"Actually, it's not enough to say they've changed. They might not have changed at all, but…"

"Explain properly."

Anchal understood Jin Kang's frustration but had no better explanation.

"As Your Majesty felt, some event was suppressed into the realm of delusion. But if it was an event that might have actually happened…"

"The position of the stars is different from before?"

"Yes. It's not visible to the naked eye. It's about one part in an astronomical number. But my magical eyes, which deal with the mind, definitely sense that something has changed."

"Hmm, the position of the stars is different."

Jin Kang stroked his beard and asked.

"What does that mean?"

"The first universe."

Uorin continued.

"All the actions of atoms from that moment created the current celestial bodies. Humans, and all beings in this universe, are bound by that law."

She opened the window.

"Fish exist because there is water. Birds exist because there is the sky. Ancient humans realized this and named it fate."

Stars twinkled in the night sky.

"Stars themselves don't have special power. It's just that the celestial bodies created by those initial conditions determine how certain humans live. Kaden was born under the fate of the Southern Cross…"

Uorin turned around.

"To be precise, Kaden's birth was determined from the beginning of the universe, and the result is the constellation we see now."

A specific pattern created by the position of the stars.

"The Southern Cross is in the southern sky, with seven red stars forming a cross. But what if… the position of the stars changes?"

Shirone finally realized.

"The law changes. The beginning of the universe, the initial cause, changes, so the result changes too."

"Yes. Moving a planet would cause a big problem. But if it's different from the start, there's no sense of incongruity. Human logic is based on causality. I was born, I have parents, my parents have parents…"

Uorin rotated her hand.

"And finally, when you reach the beginning of the universe, human logic ends. Conversely, because human logic starts from there, no matter what result comes out now, it doesn't seem strange."

"Because it's a result reached by strictly following causality."

That's why everyone in the banquet hall believed they were there of their own will, despite having different memories.

"Yes. There are memory errors. But that's not all. The change in people's positions in the banquet hall is a universal-scale change. The change might be infinitesimal, but Paras likely has the ability to make fine adjustments."

Shirone thought of the leader of the New Humanity, Piri.

'Nane said it was just the beginning. But did Fermi know that beginning would be Paras?'

"Shirone, I can't figure out what Kitra is thinking. We need to uncover the secrets of the outside world faster than Paras."

"We're exploring the Pyramid of Truth, but it will take time. We need to resolve it before the Holy War ends…"

"There's one more thing."

"Hmm?"

Uorin crossed her arms and asked.

"In Ymir's dream. The quantum signals being blocked means it's connected to the outside world, right?"

Shirone fell silent at her cold analysis.

'Come to think of it…'

What are those who entered the dream to excavate Ultima doing now?
