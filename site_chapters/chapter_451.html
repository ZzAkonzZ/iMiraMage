Chapter 451:

Past midnight.

As the war with Heaven approached, Kuan felt a heavy weight in his chest.

It wasn't the fear of death.

What he truly feared was breaking.

To be remembered as a broken sword in the minds of the living and the dead.

‘This won't do. I need to become stronger.’

This realization came from his battle with the mineral species Ringer.

Though he had captured it with Etella's Yin-Yang Wave Fist, he should have been able to cut it in two with his own sword.

What bothered him most was Shina.

Failing to demonstrate his skills in front of her weighed heavily on his mind.

“Well done. Let's start the operation tomorrow.”

Shina emerged from a door at the end of the hallway.

While those in combat positions had time to rest, strategists worked through the night.

Kuan watched Shina, who was conversing with the rebel commanders with a smile.

He had thought she was cold, but she could surprisingly smile beautifully when handling business.

‘She's different from me.’

Dealing with people was always tiring, and the only connection he had was his childhood friend, Kiyora Ellis. Shina's unexpectedness was a refreshing pleasure.

Shina, who had been smiling, met Kuan's eyes.

She raised her eyebrows in surprise at seeing him awake at this hour, then smiled and nodded slightly.

Kuan also bowed politely.

Just as he was about to speak, Armin of Guang'an emerged from the conference room.

Though his relationship with Armin hadn't improved despite wandering through many hunting grounds in Purgatory, it was different with Shina.

Through their shared experiences and late-night meetings, the cold relationship between them seemed to have thawed a bit.

Even with his dull emotions, Kuan could tell from his keen swordsman's eye that Shina's expression was different when she looked at him.

A slight distance.

But even that was a look of trust between them.

Following Armin, Shina turned back to Kuan and nodded.

At that moment, he didn't want to receive her greeting.

Kuan had a restless night.

The nightmare of losing his achilles to the Wind Guards once again paralyzed him.

“Gasp! Gasp!”

Returning to reality, Kuan clutched his head.

“Damn it!”

Hadn't he given up everything? Hadn't he come to make Heaven his grave and swing his last sword?

‘My resolve has dulled. This won't do.’

Kuan strapped on his sword and went outside.

Though it was a time when everyone was asleep, he found a place where people were unlikely to come and drew his sword.

Only the sword.

He thought he could live his whole life swinging a sword.

Some say it's just about swinging it. They ask if it's not just about cutting quickly and strongly.

Kuan had no intention of explaining to them.

He didn't want to prove the harsh pain contained in the metal, the training of a thousand hardships, and the life of endurance to those who had never held a sword.

A gust of wind cut through the darkness. He advanced, cutting through the air.

His limping leg felt as heavy as a thousand pounds, but he gritted his teeth and swung the hilt.

‘Move! Move!’

No matter how hard he tried, the leg without an Achilles tendon wouldn't respond.

“Damn it!”

Kuan's breathing grew rougher.

As his convulsing leg failed to support his body, he was thrown into the air.

-Kuan handles the sword well.

He had picked up the sword for a trivial reason.

At first, it seemed like he could do anything. His ideas were extremely free, and his movements were the pinnacle of strangeness.

To him, the sword was a dream, a mission, and a hope that could achieve everything.

“Grrr!”

As Kuan landed and spun, a sharp whirlwind swept in all directions.

Only the sword remained. In his empty grip, there was still a cold piece of metal.

That's why he couldn't let go.

The only thing he could do was cut, and he lived believing that was his only value.

‘I am the sword. Only the sword.’

The more he tried to deny it, the more the face of a woman flickered, and Kuan swung his sword irritably.

Why?

It was the first time the sword had seemed so insignificant.

‘What is this?’

For the first time in his life, as he distanced himself from the sword, paradoxically, Kuan transcended a level.

The outward and inward forces alternated faster than the fastest key a human could strike, creating turbulence as hundreds of forces intertwined.

As rotation added to rotation and more rotation, Kuan's sword whirled.

The storm-like wind pushed the atmosphere, and as air cut air, a sonic boom erupted.

‘Olifer Shina…’

The level soared endlessly. Yet, he didn't feel the same joy as before.

The more he wanted to let go of the sword he couldn't release, the more new paths opened, but he couldn't find the same joy as before.

‘Get a grip. Abandon false hope.’

With a bleeding hand, Kuan gripped his sword.

Without the sword, he was nothing.

Even the beautiful ghost smiling in the air had approached because of his sword.

“I am the sword demon.”

Gritting his teeth, Kuan advanced.

The whirlwind he created, dozens of meters in diameter, swept the clearing as it moved.

Whoooosh!

The massive whirlwind split into hundreds of smaller whirlwinds, spreading in all directions.

In the compressed wind like a mirage, Kuan stood still and lowered his sword.

Whoooosh!

The sword marks on the ground were revealed.

The place where the wind had disappeared was completely devastated by the traces of the sword.

“Haaa…”

Kuan let out a weary sigh and looked up at the sky.

The blade was absorbing the blood flowing from his torn palm.

He could faintly sense the sun beyond the mountains.

In the time when no one had yet opened their eyes, Kuan looked towards where Shina was sleeping.

That night, Kuan transcended three levels, and Shina slept peacefully, unaware of the level a man had reached for her.

“The production of Tagis is complete.”

Mitgun reported to Frankwine, the leader of Yameng.

Just three days.

Though Yameng produced all kinds of munitions, no one had expected them to produce large Tagis in just three days.

Frankwine took a deep drag on his cigarette and said,

“They're crazy. 138 black elixirs? What are these guys doing?”

Mitgun was equally baffled.

“It's not just the number of elixirs, but also using them all for Tagis production. With 100 black elixirs, one could become the strongest even in the homeland.”

Frankwine clicked his tongue.

“That's why you don't get it. That's a theory that works in tens, but once you go over 100, you're just the strongest. They must have bigger dreams than the homeland. Like me.”

Mitgun paused and then asked,

“What will you do now? The death of Raysis has caused significant losses for Yameng.”

“If Raysis is gone, we'll get it from someone else. Send the Tagis first. There's no point in provoking them. Pick the best people, and negotiate afterward.”

“Understood.”

Following the orders, Mitgun selected only the strongest subordinates to transport the Tagis to the First Rebel Command.

The rail installed in the mountains behind Yameng's munitions factory was used only for handling special items, and the transport team numbered 30.

As several large containers ran in a line, the iron rail suddenly tore with an explosion.

One container derailed and fell to the ground, and Yameng's guards sprang into action with superhuman speed.

“What is it? An attack?”

“No way. Who would dare mess with Yameng?”

The guard captain gave orders.

“Hold your positions. Securing the goods is the priority.”

The contents of the containers were top secret, even to the transport team.

If there were attackers, they were likely just bugs hoping to open a treasure chest.

“Well, this is a big catch.”

But the guards' expectations were completely off.

The attackers didn't care whether the containers held treasure or dung. More importantly, they were on a different level from mere bugs.

“Sorry to interrupt your busy work. We have some business with you.”

An old man walked slowly with one hand behind his back.

He seemed skilled, but the guards, being elite selected by Yameng, looked puzzled.

But the next moment, all 30 guards raised their killing intent and glared ahead.

From the surrounding forest, figures emitting not just strength but auras appeared.

Exactly 21.

They were Cage B Team from the Tormia Magic Association, tracking Yameng to eliminate Gaold.

“Prepare to fight.”

At the guard captain's words, all the guards grinned fiercely.

Their necks thickened abnormally, and their bodies began to swell accordingly.

As their clothes tore, their skin colors were revealed, and various biological weapons from human experiments appeared.

Humans resembling crocodiles, wolves, and snakes, each with unique traits etched into their bodies.

Aroella of the Flame bent her neck as if to break it and stepped forward.

“What is this, a zoo?”

“Grrr! Attacking Yameng without fear. We'll swallow you whole.”

As a man with a snake's face lunged with his mouth open, numerous beastly bodies followed.

Through a deal with Raysis, their physical abilities had been enhanced to Telomere Level 5, equivalent to the Kergog tribe's chiefs.

As Aroella ignited her flames, the guards scattered in all directions.

Though they were well-trained, none of Cage B Team wavered.

Metal mage Roche stepped forward with a sneer.

Though friends with Tarvan, he looked much younger, with a red dragon tattoo symbolizing a dragon on his cheek.

Iron Cutter!

As the metal magic, a particularly unique branch of magic, was cast, iron blades whirled in all directions.

“Graaah!”

At the start of the battle, over ten guards were cut down, and the remaining members of Cage B Team began their activities.

“What are these…”

The guard captain couldn't believe what he was seeing.

The top warriors of Yameng were being taken down without even putting up a fight.

‘Even Telomere Level 5 can't handle this?’

If so, he was no match for them either.

If anyone could face them, it would be Yameng's leader Frankwine or Mitgun.

‘I need to report this…!’

The guard captain turned, using his subordinates as shields.

Ignoring the screams behind him, he ran, but it was already too late.

“Hey~!”

He had vowed never to look back.

But the guard captain turned his head. He sensed death in the man's voice.

The first thing he saw was all his subordinates, now corpses. Then, Tarvan's mocking laughter as he flew through the air.

“W-wait a minute!”

As the guard captain reached out, Tarvan's hands extended towards him.

Kaiser Blast.

“Ugh…!”

As the guard captain's breath was suddenly cut off, his body exploded from within.

Boom!

As the body burst like fireworks, Tarvan lit a cigarette.

“Well, we've cleaned up. Do you really think Gaold will come alone? No matter how crazy he is, he's not stupid enough to face Cage B alone.”

“He will come.”

Team leader Rose said, checking the contents of the container.

“Because this is everything to him.”
