Chapter 1155: Unsolved cases (2)

Realizing the impact of Crime Points, Shirone’s group looked up at the sky and asked,

"What’s our current points?"

- Your current Crime Points are 91P.

Nade spoke.

"We spent 9P on food. Knowing this is useful, but we need to gather more points. If we can’t figure out a way to get food, we won’t be able to do anything."

Eden added,

"Let’s explore the village first. If this place works in a loop, we might return here later. We should gather as much information as possible before attempting a mission."

As they walked through the village, Shirone noticed that he couldn’t make eye contact with any of the townspeople.

"We have to kill three people."

Dorian was a small village, and in just thirty minutes, they had gathered all the information they could.

Back at the village entrance, the group sat under a tree to exchange opinions.

Eden was the first to speak.

"They feel like real people."

Iruki responded,

"But they’re not. More importantly, we’re missing the key detail—they resurrect."

Nade agreed.

"Yeah. If the consequences of our actions reset, is there any reason to hesitate? If we don’t complete the mission, our points will keep dropping. We’ll end up in prison."

Iruki turned to Shirone.

"You decide. We’re here to support you, whatever you choose."

Shirone spoke thoughtfully.

"Melkidu must be a world governed by a hidden code—a kind of simulation."

The group listened attentively.

"High Gear was similar. Back then, players even killed each other."

Shirone shrugged.

"But this time, it feels different. We're being told to repeatedly kill unarmed civilians."

Iruki nodded.

"That’s the rule of the murderers. It’s definitely a system built with malicious intent."

Nade stepped forward.

"Is that so? Personally, I find it interesting. I mean, it’s not like I enjoy killing people in real life, but as kids, we played war games, right? We’d pretend to slash each other with wooden sticks and collapse dramatically. This just feels a little more real. But so what? We survived the Great War."

They had already experienced far too much brutality.

"Let’s just do it. We don’t have time to waste. Or should I just grab someone right now and—"

Just then, Nade pointed to an alley where two young girls were laughing and chatting.

Watching them for a moment, Nade slowly lowered his hand and pressed his legs together.

Iruki gave him a scornful look.

"If killing civilians isn’t an option, how about hunting criminals? This place may be made for murderers, but not all of them are pleasure seekers. There must be cowardly weaklings, mercenaries, and zealots who kill out of blind hatred. If their perspectives exist in this world, then there must be a playstyle that suits us."

"Oh, right!"

Nade suddenly lifted his head.

"Curtis said the same thing. At first, he only hunted criminals to earn points. He said it was inefficient, but we don’t have the luxury of being picky right now."

Shirone looked hesitant.

"If a criminal harms someone, I’d fight to protect the victim with my life. But actively searching for criminals just so we can kill them? That doesn’t sit right with me."

Nade argued back.

"How are we supposed to clear the mission if you overthink everything?"

Eden sided with Shirone.

"I agree with Shirone. We need to distinguish between intent and outcome. What he’s saying is that we shouldn’t normalize this. Right?"

"Yeah. I understand that killing criminals is the best option. But the moment we start rationalizing it, we become part of Melkidu’s system. Eventually, it won’t matter who we kill."

Just like Curtis.

"What about Seina? We need to save her as soon as possible. She’s a real human."

Shirone finally revealed what was on his mind.

"Honestly… I can free everyone right now."

Iruki asked,

"Like in Andre’s Labyrinth?"

"Yeah. By fully unlocking the Immortal Function, we can reset this world’s laws. Anyone who reaches the threshold of enlightenment can return to reality. It wouldn’t even count as a crime. If we do that, Melkidu will be absorbed back into the real world."

Eden asked,

"What’s stopping you?"

"To return with the Immortal Function activated, I’d have to embrace this world. That includes the system designed for murderers, the demons of Melkidu, and…"

Shirone’s eyes hardened.

"… Gyorgi”

It would mean accepting the demonic power that Yahweh had severed from humanity.

"No one understands Gyorgi better than I do. This is probably the key strategy behind Yahweh’s assassination—implanting the demon within me. Seina will be safe. If she weren’t, I wouldn’t have a reason to free this world."

Iruki stood up.

"Alright, now that we’ve decided, let’s begin. Let’s ask the villagers who the worst criminal in town is."

One Hour Later…

"No way! Really?"

"I swear! So I shouted at him—hey! You just crapped your pants!"

"Hahaha! That’s hilarious!"

From a distance, the rest of the group watched as Nade cheerfully chatted with a group of young girls.

"I’m going insane."

They had spoken to over twenty people, yet not a single one of them was a criminal. Everyone was ridiculously kind.

Just then, Shirone shouted.

"Hey! Nade!"

Nade snapped back to reality, waved to the girls, and ran toward his friends.

Gripping his head, he groaned.

"This is bad. I don’t feel like killing anyone. Isn't this village just too peaceful?"

Iruki replied,

"It's a small community. In a place like this, even minor incidents are hard to get away with since the culprit would be immediately identified."

Shirone rested his chin on his hand.

"Even so, Curtis wouldn’t have misled us from the start. He said it was inefficient, not impossible. Maybe he didn’t explain in detail because there are too many ineffective methods."

"That makes sense. Regardless, nearly 200 people are moving about with their own personalities. Something is bound to happen. The problem is, we don’t know when."

Eden spoke up.

"We’ll just have to wait. In the meantime, let’s switch our priorities and get weapons first. We can’t do this barehanded."

Even if they were targeting criminals, strangling them wasn’t exactly appealing.

"Material."

Iruki looked at Shirone.

"What about using your ability to create one? That way, we can save points. A knife should be possible, right?"

"Of course, but…"

Shirone activated Material.

- Insufficient Crime Points to execute a crime. Current Crime Points: 91P.

"Not enough points. Looks like creating a weapon is also considered a crime."

"... Annoying, but fair. So in the end, we have to buy—or rather, steal—one?"

Nade stepped forward.

"I’ll handle it. Wait here."

Glancing left and right, he quickly crossed the street and entered the weapon shop.

"I need to grab something that makes killing easy."

A moment later, the door burst open, and Nade stepped out, holding the exact same knife the murderer had used earlier.

From inside the shop, a furious voice rang out.

"You bastard! How dare you steal from me? Thief! Thief—!"

Without looking back, Nade kicked the door shut with his heel.

BANG!

As his friends watched, he crossed the street again and tossed the knife to them.

"Behold, the skills of a master thief."

Iruki caught the spinning knife by the handle and examined its blade.

"This the best you could get?"

"It was the only short one. There were some glowing ones, but I didn’t have enough points for those."

"How much did it cost? What’s our current points?"

- Your current Crime Points are 76P.

"Fifteen points. Definitely cheap. If we played normally, fifteen stabs would be enough to kill three people. As far as murderers go, that’s a reasonable difficulty level."

Now equipped with their crime tools, the group hid in an alley, waiting for an opportunity.

"We really look like criminals now."

It wasn’t a moment for jokes, and Shirone bit his lip anxiously.

"There’s a chance, but this is highly inefficient. If nothing changes by the end of the day, we’ll need a new strategy."

An hour passed. As the sun began to set, a bell rang near the entrance.

"Raid! We’re under attack!"

Shirone’s group rushed out of the alley and spotted three bandits brandishing knives.

"This is it."

The leader of the bandits shouted,

"For today, this village belongs to us! Bring us three women and your finest liquor, and no one dies!"

A system message followed.

- The bandit gang has joined the village.

"Those bastards…"

While the villagers paled in fear, Shirone’s group felt a surge of excitement.

"Now’s our chance. Let’s go."

Just as they were about to step forward, a voice called from behind them.

"Hold on. We got here first."

Shirone’s group turned around.

Three men stood there, each holding the same type of knife that Nade had stolen.

‘Players. Are they accomplices as well?’

Considering they hadn’t been caught during their investigation, they seemed to be well-trained.

A tall man looked at Shirone.

"You’re players too, aren’t you? But we’ve been waiting here for three days. Even though they’re just NPCs, we couldn’t bring ourselves to kill innocent civilians."

It made sense.

"I’m sure you guys thought the same thing. That’s why I’m asking for a favor. We’re after a wanted criminal—a psychopath who murdered seven women. Would you let us handle this one?"

There were only three bandits, meaning only one team could complete the mission.

The man rolled up his sleeve, revealing a hawk tattoo.

‘Hawk Eye.’

They were private investigators from the Stang Kingdom.

"Looks like you recognize us. We’re unofficial enforcers who handle cases the kingdom can’t touch. The man we’re after is a powerful noble."

He placed a firm hand on Shirone’s shoulder.

"Honestly, I didn’t expect you guys to find this hidden event so quickly. The information about the bandits can only be obtained late at night from the village chief’s house. It took us two whole days just to learn that."

Meeting Curtis had certainly been lucky, but what really moved Shirone was something else.

‘They waited three days.’

Despite knowing the system of this world, they had resisted the easy path of murder.

"Alright. We’ll step aside."

As Shirone backed away, Nade patted his back in consolation.

"Can’t be helped."

The three investigators bowed their heads slightly in gratitude before stepping forward.

"One target per person."

"Sounds good."

Each of them wielded daggers. After a brief standoff, they kicked off the ground and charged.

"Die, bandits!"

As the investigators lunged, the bandits bent low and gripped their longswords tightly.

"Heh heh heh!"

The two sides clashed in an instant.

And then—

All three investigators were slashed and sent flying.

"Aaaah!"

Shirone’s group watched in shock.

"They're ridiculously strong!"

Even Shirone was surprised.

‘They’re definitely powerful. But the investigators aren’t fighting properly… Because of the Crime Points.’

The bandit leader laughed.

"Puhahaha! You weaklings dare challenge us?"

"Damn it!"

One of the investigators, clutching his bleeding side, tried to crawl away. The leader raised his longsword high.

"Now, where was the heart again?"

"Tch! Give me that!"

Before anyone could stop him, Nade snatched Iruki’s dagger and charged into the battlefield.
