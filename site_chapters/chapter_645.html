Chapter 645:

As Seong-eum's position fully shifted to Hwangryongjeon, the crumpled space unfolded, and the scenery returned to its original state.

Although it wasn't a common sight, the guards, who had experienced it a few times, instinctively grabbed the hilts of their swords and shouted.

"Your Highness! To have an audience with His Majesty, you must follow protocol!"

Seong-eum didn't even blink.

"A daughter is just meeting her father."

Her Kar score was 89.2%.

"But if you do this, we…"

As the captain of the guard approached with a troubled expression, Seong-eum raised her hand.

Ether Wave - Nagok.

As the space twisted, the captain's body contorted like a vortex and completely disappeared.

"No matter how high your status, using sorcery in Hwangryongjeon…"

With that, the guards were twisted and vanished by the power of Nagok.

A perfect sealed room.

Seong-eum's ability was powerful because it twisted the very plane on which phenomena existed.

Since there was no space for sound to spread, no one could eavesdrop on their conversation.

"Father, are you afraid of me?"

"Hmm."

Emperor Jin Kang of Jincheon leaned his chin on his hand and smiled.

His physique was not inferior to the men of the guard, with a full head of hair and beard, and his eyes sparkled with a will strong enough to encompass the world.

"Is there a father in the world who fears his daughter?"

"Then why do you keep the troops?"

"Seong-eum."

His deep voice shook Hwangryongjeon.

"Yes, Father."

"The world is vast, and there are many people. To gain the world, everything must be simplified. Whether you are my daughter or anything else, it is natural to bow to the emperor."

Looking down at Seong-eum, who still stood with her back straight, Jin Kang asked.

"Do you think Jincheon cannot contain the world you dream of?"

The Ivory Tower was a transnational institution, and if Seong-eum became a star, Jincheon would lose a valuable talent.

"It's not a matter of the world, but of myself."

Jin Kang nodded.

"Giving you time is good. But never forget. Always, in any situation…"

Jin Kang's eyes flashed.

"Simplicity triumphs over complexity."

With a crack, the space split, and 200 guards surrounded Seong-eum. The captain, standing one step ahead, held a blade to her neck.

"Your Highness, follow the protocol. We cannot tolerate any more disrespect."

As the spatial compulsion broke, Seong-eum glared at Jin Kang with fierce eyes.

"I permit you to take the Ivory Tower's test. Do your best."

"Hmph!"

As Seong-eum turned sharply, the captain's blade twisted like a snake and aimed at his groin.

"Damn it…!"

Angry at the disgrace shown before the emperor, the captain chased after Seong-eum, but Jin Kang spoke in a low voice.

"Let her go."

"But Your Majesty! Her actions just now…"

"She's just immature. It'll be fine, even with the Ivory Tower."

It was a tower that Jincheon would break someday.

"Have fun, daughter of Jin Kang."

The day before the graduation ceremony, Shirone and his friends were ironing their uniforms.

It might look ridiculous like ghosts, but thinking of it as one of the last hurdles to graduation made them feel better.

After ironing, they sat down to write their graduation speeches for the next day.

About an hour later, Nade grumbled.

"I hate writing the most. We fought so hard, and now we have to do this too?"

Iruki said.

"That's life. There's a saying, 'It ain't over till it's over.'"

"Hmm."

Nodding, Shirone turned to Iruki.

"But isn't that saying used in a different context?"

"Who cares? I don't expect much from language."

Curious about what such a person's graduation speech would be like, Shirone craned his neck to look.

"Let's see. If you're crazy and stupid, you're a lunatic. If you're normal and smart, you're a scholar. If you're crazy and smart, you're a genius… Hey, what is this? Are you really going to read this?"

"Hahaha!"

Nade stuck out his tongue and rolled his eyes.

"Why not? We've escaped hell. The teachers won't listen to anything we say."

Nade wiped away a tear.

"But it's a document that will be kept in the records room. Put some effort into it."

"And you?"

Iruki read Nade's speech.

"An angel named Liz. A sun rising like a miracle over the surface of despair…"

"Hey! Why are you reading mine?"

Shirone sighed.

"It's not a love letter. Future students will keep seeing it…"

"That's why I'm writing it. An eternal love letter. It's romantic."

Just then, there was a knock, and Amy entered.

"Mr. Collie wants us to gather in the music room. We're practicing the school song."

"Ugh, I really don't want to do that."

The thought of singing in front of the juniors made their faces hot.

When they entered the music room, all the graduates were gathered.

Even those who chose different paths had to receive their diplomas, so Lycan and Maya were also present.

"Long time no see, Shirone."

Maya approached and waved.

"Yeah. I heard you passed. Congratulations."

Although she had received many scout offers from performance groups, Maya chose to audition and finally signed with the kingdom's most famous performance agency.

With many world-renowned singers produced, her life would be as eventful as Shirone's.

"Thanks. I heard about you too. The Ivory Tower came, right? Ah, I wish I had seen that."

Though Maya joked, her true feelings were different.

She had heard that Amy and Shirone were dating, but they would eventually be separated again.

Even if she had given up, the thought of having a chance with the man she loved made her heart race.

Moreover, as she became famous, she would tour many kingdoms, and just the thought of it made her heart swell.

'Maybe… I might have a chance too?'

It was something she couldn't say out loud.

If she gave even the slightest hint of such feelings, how much would it tear Amy's heart apart, watching from the side?

"Yes, let's do our best. One day, we'll all become famous and meet again."

The school song choir was the final event of the graduation ceremony, and some seniors had created unforgettable, embarrassing moments by suddenly bursting into tears.

It was just a matter of singing for about three minutes, but Collie had a slightly different opinion.

"I plan to include Maya's solo. Since she's a singer and will become famous, it will be a good event."

No one objected.

Maya's singing was excellent, and as long as it wasn't their own solo, the school song didn't matter much.

Following the graduation ceremony schedule, they finished rehearsing for about an hour and returned to their dorms to sleep early.

The morning of the long-awaited graduation ceremony dawned, and with parents, advanced class students, and teachers gathered, the ten graduates stepped onto the stage.

When it was time to read the graduation speeches, Iruki stepped forward confidently.

"If you're crazy and stupid, you're a lunatic. If you're normal and smart, you're a scholar."

Shirone and Nade broke into a cold sweat.

'He's really doing it, that crazy guy.'

The strange logic continued, and at the end of his speech, Iruki spread his arms and shouted.

"When everyone calls me crazy, I will proudly shout back. I am crazy, and this world that calls me crazy is crazy!"

Everyone answered in their hearts.

'You're just crazy.'

As the awkward silence continued, Iruki lowered his arms and turned around.

"That's all."

Nade stood up from his chair, passed Iruki, and took the stage.

"Do you know an angel named Liz? She was a miracle to me in despair…"

The students didn't understand the speech, and the embarrassment was all on Liz, who was sitting in the audience.

'If you don't want to write it, just don't.'

She knew how annoying it was to write a graduation speech.

"And so, I will become the greatest merchant!"

Nade shouted and turned around.

"That's all."

Again, there was no applause, and Siena held her head.

"Oh, those guys."

Etella covered her mouth and laughed.

"Hehe, I like it. At least the juniors are listening intently."

"That's how traditions of troublemakers start."

After the ten graduates finished their speeches, ranging from ordinary to overly emotional to self-aggrandizing, Shirone was the last to step forward.

It was an intentional arrangement, and the effect was excellent.

"It's Senior Shirone."

He had broken the kingdom's records and was now one of the top three candidates for the Ivory Tower.

Even if they didn't care about the others, they had to listen to Shirone's speech.

"I don't think I have any talent."

Shirone calmly read his speech.

"I don't think I worked harder than others."

The audience fell silent.

"Talent and effort are words made up by those who have never truly longed for something."

He wanted to become a mage and loved learning magic.

"Because I loved it so much, even failure was enjoyable, and effort wasn't hard."

Therefore, a mage is someone who loves magic.

"Even if I had less talent, I would have still reached this point. Even without inventing the word 'effort,' I would have honed my magic every day. Even if I had nothing, if you truly long for something, you can't help but go there."

Shirone put down his speech and looked at the juniors.

"Do what you truly love. No talent or effort can beat what you love. So don't be afraid and listen to your heartbeat. If it's still beating fast, you'll eventually get there."

Shirone smiled.

"That's all."

"Thank you for everything, Senior!"

Mark stood up and shouted, and all the students rose and applauded.

When Shirone, who seemed like someone from another world, said anyone could do it, a warm feeling surged in their hearts.

"That was a good speech. It will be an inspiration to the juniors."

Listening to the teachers' conversation, Alpheas smiled gently.

"Shirone…"

Seeing the boy who used to sneak over the wall at twelve now giving advice as a mage who amazed the world, he felt a deep sense of time passing.

"Finally, we will have the graduates' choir singing the school song."

'Oh, I hope it rains.'

The students, lined up with their uniforms fluttering, sang the school song to the accompaniment of the band.

After hearing the song for years since entering, it became hard to distinguish whether it was a song or a spell.

Nevertheless, Maya's skill was top-notch, and when her solo began, even the parents were mesmerized by her voice.

'I thought it was really childish.'

As the school song continued, sadness filled the students' eyes.

'When this song ends…'

They would truly go out into the world.

'I became a mage.'

From the moment he entered until now, all the events flowed through Shirone's mind without missing a single one.

He met good people and matured through many challenges.

"We are the children of intelligence. Alpheas Magic School, shine forever."

In the end, Shirone burst into tears, and as the competition had been fiercer than any other year, tears flowed from the other graduates' eyes as well.

"We are the children of intelligence."

All the students sang in unison.

"Alpheas Magic School, shine forever!"

The graduation ceremony ended.
