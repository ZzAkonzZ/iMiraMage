Chapter 709:

The immense power of the law brought Rian before Paknyeo, disrupting the gears of ethics.

'Cut!'

Thus, the force of the greatsword's vertical strike carried a transcendent feeling beyond speed or impact.

'It ends here!'

Facing a life-or-death strike, Paknyeo experienced a brain-numbing sensation.

'An arch-enemy?'

When something is perfectly split in two, humans can't choose between this side and that.

'Conviction violates the ethics of beasts.'

Paknyeo's monstrous senses were destroyed at that point.

"Screeech!"

Paknyeo retreated with the immense power of the V-12 engine.

"Did she dodge?"

The crushing sense of emptiness was brief as Rian, fueled by his resolve to protect Shirone, charged again.

As the two rapidly distanced themselves, Kido took a deep breath and focused ahead.

"Enter Andre! Kill the wizard!"

Dozens of Maga Bandits, already instructed by Paknyeo, charged at Kido.

'Can I do this?'

If it were just a fight, dying would be the end, but if Kido fell, Shirone would be the next target.

He swallowed hard.

'I'm so thirsty.'

His throat burned with an unbearable thirst.

Shirone arrived at the center of the labyrinth, as confirmed by the model of Andre.

The key was to encompass all 19,000 entrances within the Spirit Zone, and it was a close call.

'If I activate Quantum Superposition in this state…'

The Ultima System, acquired independently rather than through sensory integration, was still imperfect.

As events multiplied, control would become impossible, and unintended experiences would contaminate Shirone's identity.

'I have no choice.'

Staring as if at Goffin waiting at the end of fate, Shirone cast his spell with wide eyes.

Quantum Superposition - Wheel of Infinity.

It started with a double overlay.

Then the two multiplied infinitely, filling the labyrinth of Andre with endless events.

"Ugh!"

From World 1 to World 19000.

Codes that only the Ultima System could decipher unraveled in Shirone's hands, and the iron doors began to glow.

'Who am I?'

Shirone fell to his knees.

As his consciousness faded, he couldn't recall his name, age, or even that he was human.

"……"

Finally, as control vanished, countless Shirones began to unfold independent events.

If this world were someone's dream, the lives of these manifested Shirones were also creations of the original mind.

"I will return."

As all the Shirones looked back at their universes and spoke, 19,000 worlds opened simultaneously.

A great light surged, then silence returned.

At the top of the Ivory Tower, the artificial city.

The seven stars and Araka found Mahagaruta guarding the Grand Sanctuary where Great Star resided.

'The Five Stars.'

There was no hierarchy among the stars, not out of equality but because it was unnecessary.

Those who could gauge the depth of one's state didn't need to mention ranks; they could accept it from the heart.

Especially Mini, Borbor, and Zuo Yi felt the presence of the "sky above the sky."

'The Enlightened One…'

If few understood Luber and Brain, perhaps only the Great Star understood the essence of the Five Stars.

While the two-star residents were mentally agitated, the three-star residents, Arte and Heukgangsi, remained composed.

Luber stepped forward as the representative.

"Buddha, it's been a long time."

When Mahagaruta lifted his eyelids, his eyes were filled with a golden glow.

It was a bizarre sight, but his smile was warm.

"Indeed, Luber. We used to have many conversations. I miss those times."

"Since you don't sleep, we can't meet. Perhaps you could visit sometime."

"I'm having good dreams even now."

Their conversation was like a Zen dialogue, and Mahagaruta, seated in mid-air, slowly turned.

"Please enter. Great Star is waiting."

As Mahagaruta's eyes flashed, the white light disappeared, revealing the majestic Grand Sanctuary.

It felt like waking from a dream, or perhaps this was the dream.

"Great Star, we have come at your call."

A woman in white stood on the glass floor of the Grand Sanctuary, overlooking the planet.

Like gravity, all the stars of the Ivory Tower orbited around her.

"Welcome, my children."

Her clear voice cleansed their minds of any impurities.

"Sit wherever you like."

As Great Star sat on the glass floor overlooking the vast land of Kashan, the stars gathered around her.

"The reason I called you today…"

Everyone had been debating before meeting Great Star, so they focused on her words.

"Today is my birthday."

At first, they thought it was a joke.

'Birthday? Does Great Star have a birthday?'

They knew nothing about her, but they assumed she didn't have human-like concepts.

Borbor spoke first.

"Uh… happy birthday. Hello?"

Great Star replied playfully,

"What's the point of just saying it? You should give me a present."

While the two-star residents were visibly flustered, the three-star residents' expressions grew serious.

"Are you serious? Then how old are you this year…?"

"Well, I'm not at an age to celebrate birthdays. I just remembered the old days. I missed you. That's all."

Her words were heartwarming, but the stars' cold logic quickly identified the source of the unease.

"Great Star, we still need you."

For someone at the pinnacle of wisdom to become emotional meant a significant event was happening in the world.

Arte spoke with determination.

"Tell us. Is it the altar, Anke Ra, Nane, or Shirone? I will handle it."

Great Star shook her head.

"Just because you have the power to destroy doesn't mean you should destroy at will. Everything exists without reason, and that's why it can be noble."

The atmosphere grew solemn.

"Um, Great Star."

Zuo Yi raised her hand and asked,

"Who did you vote for this time?"

It was a question to change the mood, but Arte and Heukgangsi glared at her.

"Who do you think I voted for?"

Mini said,

"Of course, it's Nane. Shirone and Jin Seong-eum are excellent, but Nane is on a different level. His Kar score is the highest."

"Kar is not a tool for competition."

Great Star clasped her hands together and said,

"Think of this universe as a small room. When you first enter, all realizations come at once—smells, shapes, colors, atmospheres. Anyone can say they know this room roughly, and thus, achieving a 50% Kar score is easy."

Great Star’s hands gently opened.

"But to know it more accurately, you need more than the five senses. The position of objects, their uses, the history and meaning embedded in them. Therefore, the closer you get to perfection, the harder it is to increase your Kar score."

Great Star spread her arms wide.

"Now, imagine you think you know everything. You understand the room completely, even down to a speck of dust in the corner. Does that mean you have a 100% Kar score?"

Everyone remained silent.

"You can never be sure. Even if you think you know everything about this room, how do you prove it? What if you missed something? Is what you know really true?"

"No one can say they know their room perfectly."

"Exactly. The 0.01% or even smaller piece missing from 100%—its essence is…"

Great Star’s hands gently rested on her chest.

"The heart."

"The heart…"

"To fully realize that you've reached perfection, the heart must be involved. Nane lacks this, but Shirone has it. If there's a variable, it will likely be a matter of the heart."

As the stars pondered, Zuo Yi pointed at Great Star and asked,

"So, the one who voted for Shirone is…?"

Great Star stuck out her tongue and smiled.

"That's a secret."

The labyrinth of Paitaros.

A lawless zone where the world's murky energy flows, an impregnable fortress that countless monks have challenged to eradicate evil but never succeeded.

"Is this the Pit of Corruption?"

When Kanis and Arin arrived, numerous organizations were camped outside the dark cave of the labyrinth.

They were all top experts from around the world, and Arin, recognizing their faces, said,

"Kanis, let's reconsider. This isn't right. It's too difficult to start with."

Paitaros, seen through the first view, was damp, slimy, and pulsating like the entrance to a glistening intestine.

"That's why it's worth the challenge. If we conquer just the entrance, we'll make a name for ourselves instantly."

Having failed their graduation exams, Kanis and Arin had dropped out of magic school and were wandering the world investigating ruins.

Kanis dreamed of becoming an archaeologist, and Arin supported that dream, but this time it felt like a reckless move.

"If we give up now, we'll never accomplish anything. If you're scared, stay here. I'll go in alone."

Knowing Kanis's stubbornness, Arin sighed.

"Alright, I'll go with you. But promise me you'll listen if it gets really dangerous."

As Kanis nodded vigorously, Arin suddenly turned towards the wilderness.

A boy with a face covered in tattoos, wearing a ragged hood, was walking slowly.

"Ka-Kanis…"

Her voice trembled with severity.

'This isn't the first view. Such a clear incarnation…'

Nane, with his calm gaze fixed on the distant Paitaros, walked steadily.

Nane's time was different from everyone else's.

Kar score: 97.8%.

Nane's thoughts were different from everyone else's.

Kar score: 98.2%.

"…Understood."

Kar score: 99.1%.

For him, the realization gained in the world of the apostles acted as the pinnacle of thesis, antithesis, and synthesis.

"Oh, I was mistaken."

Through combinations close to infinity, he was moving towards perfection.

Kar score: 99.3%.

With each step, Kanis felt like massive barriers were crumbling, leaving him breathless.

"This is impossible…"

Kanis, who had fallen on his backside, looked up at Nane passing by indifferently.

'Shirone, honestly, you're amazing too, but…'

Why did Shirone come to mind at this moment?

'This guy is on a different level. He surpasses you.'

'Seven steps.'

The distance remaining to Paitaros.

'Six steps.'

As the distance shortened, Nane's Kar score continuously broke through insurmountable limits.

'One step.'

Nane, who momentarily paused the universe, took the final step and looked up at the sky.

"I have realized."

Kar score: 99.99%.

As everyone held their breath, Nane pointed to the sky with his right hand and to the ground with his left, saying,

"Throughout heaven and earth, I alone am the honoured one"
