Chapter 1015: Evil Strikes Back (3)

Imperial City of Aganos.

The goblin's attire sharply contrasted with the corridor's decoration of gold and treasures.

Clad in a ragged cloak and carrying a crude spear on his back, the monster's name was Kido.

He was one of the few royal guards who could deliver food directly to Wuorin's room.

"Stop."

A black whirlwind blew in, blocking the path with an owl-like white mask worn by the Wind Guards.

"What? You scared me."

The leader of the squad asked in a sinister voice.

"…Where are you going?"

"Can't you see? That woman asked for some honey water."

The Wind Guards, staring intently at the golden tray Kido was holding, revealed his killing intent.

"Don't get cocky just because the Empress dotes on you. At the end of the day, you're just a pet."

Like most of his close associates, for the Wind Guards , the sole ruler of Kashan was Terra.

These masters of the sword, who embodied fluid dynamics in their bodies, protected Terraje up close.

However, since Kido entered the Imperial Palace, that pride had been shattered.

'Just a goblin.'

From what he observed from afar, Wuorin didn’t truly value Kido either.

She treated him like a servant, sometimes angrily, and occasionally took him for a walk on a leash.

"If I'm a pet, what are you guys? Cattle? Move aside. If I'm late, that woman will throw a fit."

The Wind Guards activated the collective killing intent 'Gaksi,' feeling like thousands of blades were pricking his skin.

"Watch your words. If you insult the Empress again, I'll report you myself. That stinking goblin will be sent to a place he can never return from." The biggest reason the Wind Guards despised Kido was that he harbored affection for Wuorin.

'A dog looking to surpass its master?' Wuorin wouldn’t have kept him around if it weren't for his unique ability, 'The Taste of Memory.'

"Move aside."

Kido had no intention to fight.

"I just had a good idea."

As the leader of the squadron drew his sword, the remaining nine made the same motion, like afterimages.

"Let’s neuter you. Then the Empress can play with you much more safely."

The Wind Guards was serious.

Kido's eyes turned cold, but his hands still clutched the tray.

A subtle vibration that appears before the body moves heightened the tension.

"Stop."

A woman's voice was heard.

"Lady Yula."

The Wind Guards sheathed his sword and bowed his head, while Kido slowly turned around.

'When did she get behind me...'

The leader of the Wind Guards, Yula.

Wearing the same owl mask, but her aura was different from the other The Wind Guards.

"The Wind Guards obeys only the Empress's commands. Remember that your arrogance could destroy the entire organization."

"I apologize."

Yula's chest heaved deeply then settled.

'This is why newcomers are a problem. No matter how much you teach them with words, they don't understand until they experience it.'

As capable as a squadron leader might be, all the Wind Guards must remember this.

'Terraje.'

The cruel madness contained in that name.

'Just because she finds someone displeasing, she could annihilate all of the Wind Guards.'

Yula bowed her head.

"I'm sorry. Overlook my subordinate's rudeness. The Empress is waiting, hurry on." When Kido turned around, the corridor was empty.

Since Yula would not be there anyway, he did not bother to look back and continued walking.

"…It’s not easy."

Kido knocked on Wuorin’s door, but there was no response.

"I'm entering."

Kido lifted his right foot to turn the doorknob while holding the tray with both hands.

"What, you were here?"

Wuorin, who had finished grooming herself, was peering outside from a table next to the window.

"Kido."

She turned her head with a smile, her appearance shining brighter than the backlight from the window.

'She’s really beautiful.'

Although goblin aesthetic standards differ from humans, to his eyes, Wuorin was perfect.

'Maybe because she's eaten many people. Human memories have seeped into her.'

He tried to divert his thoughts, but the truth was clear.

'I love this woman.'

All the desires to laugh together, walk together, talk about the book they read yesterday, and entangle like animals.

If someone finds such natural desires repulsive, there's only one reason.

Kido is a goblin.

"Drink it. You said your stomach was upset?"

Looking at the teacup shaped like a water droplet on the tray, Wuorin's smile deepened.

"Yap."

She hit under the tray with her hand, causing the teacup to topple and spill all the honey water.

Hiding her face to contain her anger, Wuorin held her belly and laughed.

"Hahaha! Look at that face."

"What are you doing?"

"Huh? I hit the tray so the honey water spilled. Oh, right. Could you get some more honey water? My stomach is upset."

Throwing the tray aside, Kido yelled.

"What do you think goblins are! Am I a joke to you? Why is it always a joke when you see me?"

"It's disgusting."

As Kido’s face turned pale with shock, Wuorin laughed sharply.

"It's a joke, a joke! How much I like you."

"Enough."

Kido turned around.

"If you need a pet, find someone else. I'm quitting. I'm leaving Kashan."

"Why are you so sensitive today? What happened?"

Was it because he met the Wind Guards?

'No, this is my problem.'

He couldn't bear the miserable sight of himself the more he stayed by her side.

"Huh? Are you really going? Kido, Kido!"

Before Wuorin could finish speaking, Kido had flung open the door and escaped into the corridor.

As the door slammed shut, the room returned to silence as before.

"Hmph, that’s all he was."

Whether he could really leave Kashan was uncertain, but at least he needed time to think.

As Kido left the Imperial Palace.

Wuorin, sitting at the table looking out the window, had eyes that had grown cold.

"Be careful, Kido."

In the indifferent landscape she viewed, corpses bleeding profusely were emerging.

The face of Harvitz, who had arrived in the capital of Kashan, was bloodied like a demon.

"Thrilling."

He entered the construction site to repair the damage caused by the demonic tree, but no one recognized him.

"Be more terrified."

Every time Wuorin’s inner voice was heard, his eyelids trembled.

"Hey, get the bricks from the storage..."

As Harvitz swung his arm, the neck of a passing worker snapped.

"Come on! Let’s start quickly!" People remained busy, and Harvitz, alone, began his deadly dance.

"Die."

One life per slash.

"Die."

Even as he focused on that tedious task, the city moved rapidly.

The teachers, who had expected a terrible clash, were stunned the moment they opened their eyes.

“Oh my…”

Wizard was crying.

“I’m sorry. I won’t do it again.” Once the floodgates opened, Wizard's sensitivity poured out like a waterfall.

“Yes. Only those with courage can admit their mistakes. It’s something even adults find difficult.”

Wizard, having stopped crying, sniffled.

“Winning and hurting someone are completely different things. You must not do that in the future.”

“Yes.”

As Shirone watched the nodding wizard, he suddenly had this thought.

‘A genius of the era, a magician stronger than adults, intoxicated with all sorts of flowery words, but…’

In reality, the child is anxious about everything.

‘Their own emotions, others’ emotions, even the nameless insects crawling at their feet…’

Everything is scary.

‘No one has taught this child about the world. Thanks to that, their purity is maintained, but…’

That’s why it’s difficult.

‘Am I really going to send this child to Satan? Can I tell them to kill Havitz?’

Shirone, sorting out his complicated feelings, took Wizard’s hand and left the training ground.

While doing so, Wizard, lost in thought, looked up at the teachers and spoke.

“Teacher, I’m sorry. I apologize.”

The dedicated teacher, with a tearful expression, gently stroked her cheek.

“No, Wizard did really well. You were wonderful. I’m so proud of you.”

Lampa, who had been watching warmly, turned his head and brought up the main topic.

“Then shall we… set up the negotiation table?”

Though not outwardly visible, the administrator’s heart was boiling with anger.

‘Damn it! This is the worst situation.’

Who would have thought that the untamable Wizard would become a docile lamb?

“Haha! Yes. Shall we return to the school? Wizard’s parents are waiting.”

Leaving the teachers behind, they entered the school, and the principal approached with Wizard’s parents.

“Welcome. I am Nikolai, a certified 4th-grade magician. It’s an honor to meet the star of the Ivory Tower.”

To magicians, Shirone was a living legend.

His hand trembled as he extended it for a handshake.

“Yes, nice to meet you. I’ve heard of your fame.”

Through Omega.

“You flatter me. Ah, and these are Wizard’s parents. Please introduce yourselves.”

A man and woman in their mid-forties bowed their heads.

“Hello. I’m Wizard’s father. If my daughter has been rude, please forgive her…”

Seeing him speak without even looking, it was easy to guess Wizard’s usual behavior.

“No, we are the ones receiving help. Thank you for raising your daughter well.”

It was a statement representing humanity.

Shirone turned to the mother and asked.

“I’d like to hear about it. How Wizard was born and what phenomena occurred.”

The mother, flustered, replied.

“We’re just farmers, so we don’t know well. What is it, magic or Spirit Zone…”

The father corrected her.

“Spirit Zone.”

“Oh, yes, that. We just want Wizard to be safe. In a world where demons run rampant…”

The mother wiped away her tears.

“I see.”

He couldn’t bring himself to say that her daughter would fight the leader of those demons.

“Mom! Dad!”

At that moment, Wizard, who had just washed up, arrived and ran to the entrance, waving her hand.

“Wizard!”

The child, who threw herself into her parents’ arms, was unmistakably seven years old, making Shirone’s heart even heavier.

‘Even if all conditions are met, it’s useless if Wizard doesn’t do it.’

Wizard pointed to Shirone and said.

“This brother, he’s really strong. Much, much stronger than me.”

“Huh?”

The parents, who had never seen Wizard acknowledge anyone, were dumbfounded.

“I like this brother. I want to keep playing with him. Is that okay? Yes? Yes?”

The parents looked at Shirone again.

“It’s the first time our daughter has acted like this. May I ask your name?”

Thinking that the moment had come, Shirone replied.

“I am Shirone.”

“Oh…”

They had never heard of the Ivory Tower, but the name Shirone was famous even among farmers.

A practitioner of love who killed countless demons and fought for the peace of humanity.

“Thank you. Thanks to you, Shirone, we were able to escape safely.”

“No, I don’t deserve such words. And I won’t in the future either.”

Leaving the confused parents behind, Shirone met Wizard’s eyes.

“Wizard, do you still find magic fun?”

“Yes, it’s fun. It’s more fun playing with you, brother. I want to keep doing magic. Forever and ever!”

Wizard, pouting her lips like a fish, pointed her index finger and drew a long line to the side.

“You could die.”

At Shirone’s words, the parents’ eyes widened.

“You could get hurt or be in a lot of pain. Even so… do you still want to continue with magic?”

“Excuse me! What are you saying right now?”

As the parents tried to intervene, Lampa put his finger to his lips and shook his head.

Wizard repeated Shirone’s words.

“I could die…”

After thinking for a long time, she suddenly looked up as if she had realized something.

Then, looking up at Shirone, she answered with the brightest smile a seven-year-old could give.

“Yes! I want to do it!”
