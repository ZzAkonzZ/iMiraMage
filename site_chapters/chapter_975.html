Chapter 975: Hell of The Demon World (1)

Countless starlights were twinkling at the edge of his vision.

From here, they looked like starlight, but up close, they were massive shockwaves that couldn't be measured by the eye.

'Ikael is fighting.'

The reason angels are strong is that their abilities are not accompanied by a purpose. The Archangel of Destruction simply destroys, and the Archangel of Creation can create anything.

The limitless expansibility of their concepts is indeed the strength of angels, but now, having mastered the universe, Shirone could see the only limit they had.

'The universe.'

Even Uriel cannot destroy what does not exist in the universe, and whatever Kariel creates ultimately becomes something that exists within the universe.

But Ikael is different.

Amplification has the potential to transcend the universe, and this was proven by Goffin.

The starlight on the horizon suddenly spread like the Milky Way and then disappeared from sight.

Moments later, Ikael flew towards Shirone, cutting through the surface of the distant sea.

Her holy light was weakened, and her face showed fatigue, but her eyes held the satisfaction of someone who had made a decision.

"Ah..."

As Ikael spotted Shirone, she increased her speed, causing the sea below to split into a V shape.

Tears of light traced the horizon as Ikael, without slowing down, embraced Shirone.

"Ugh!"

Shirone let out a short scream at the speed akin to a physical attack, but the impact was not transmitted.

'It's Mom.'

Just before colliding with the sea, Ikael absorbed all the inertia with her body and stopped.

The physical presence of Shirone filled the emptiness in her heart, but at that moment, an indescribable guilt tormented her.

"I'm sorry."

Those were Ikael's first words.

"It's not your fault."

The conversation was still awkward, but the emotions in their words were sincere.

"No. I killed you long ago, and even after regaining my memory, I pretended not to know. I am not worthy of being a parent."

Shirone understood that it was something unavoidable, but he also understood the guilt Ikael must be feeling.

"But we can't go on like this forever. It's better to let go of the past."

Ikael shook her head firmly.

"There are feelings that can't be let go. For a parent, that's their child. Don't worry. Seeing your face without guilt would be even more terrible."

"Alright."

Shirone, lost in thought, spoke.

"Come over here and sit down."

Surprised by the unexpected words, Ikael blinked but quietly followed Shirone's instructions, thinking that even blinking could cause harm.

"Is this okay?"

As Shirone looked down at Ikael, who was bowing her head reverently, he raised his hand.

Soft light flowed through his body like smoke, gathering in his hand and shining transparently.

'This is my first step.'

As Shirone placed his palm on Ikael's holy light, many thoughts crossed his mind.

'Nane is right.'

In a cold world, we can't undo anything and are just endlessly blocking pain.

That's why Nane wants all humans to realize the truth and break the chains of suffering.

'But...'

There was a god with a heart.

A god who, after sacrificing himself, sent a ray of light, hope, and a messiah from beyond the world.

Hexa.

'If that were me.'

Denying the cold world to the end, embracing everyone's anger with his heart, he would say this.

"Ikael."

He wants to believe that it can be undone.

"I forgive your sins."

A vast love, without conditions, reasons, or grounds, permeated Ikael's holy light.

"Ah..."

Ikael's shoulders trembled.

It felt as if the entire universe was assuring her that it was okay, that she was safe from it.

'I am truly a happy angel.'

Because she could receive the ultimate, absolute love of benevolence from not just one, but two people.

"Stand up."

There was no more guilt left in Ikael's purified heart.

It was a matter of the heart, but that made Ikael feel even more pity for Shirone.

"How can you do that? How can you forgive everything without any compensation, without any reason?"

"I don't know."

Shirone spoke honestly.

"Maybe it's because I've become stronger. If so, is it hypocrisy? Maybe I'm just following what Goffin taught me. But it doesn't matter. The important thing is that I can do it."

Unlike the law, the heart can be turned back, and the world Shirone sees is such a place.

"As Nane said, reality is painful, and if it didn't exist from the beginning, there would be no suffering...

But also, because it existed, we could meet like this.

"I believe that something still remains here, something that isn't enough just by closing the universe."

"Yes."

Ikael understood.

"What do you want to do? I will help. I will fight for the world Shirone wants."

Because it was her sincere heart after purification, Shirone no longer found it difficult.

"If Nane says how right humans can be, I want to ask him this."

Shirone looked up at the sky and spoke.

"Do you know how vast the human heart can become?"

When Iruki arrived in Bashka, the shock had somewhat subsided, and they were in the process of reorganizing.

"Iruki."

Shirone came out to greet him.

Seeing Shirone, who had been with him in the carriage until an hour ago, outside the door felt strange.

"How's the atmosphere?"

"It's calmed down a bit. Neither good nor bad."

They had sealed the formidable enemy Ymir, but two of the Five Great Stars had died, and they had lost Valkyrie Corps Commander Garcia.

The two walked towards the royal castle of Tormia, Creta, while conversing.

"What about the casualties of the allied forces?"

"They haven't been officially tallied yet. The Army Command will announce it soon."

When they arrived at the castle where Pony was waiting, Amy and Neid ran up and hugged Iruki.

"Iruki."

In the atmosphere of mutual consolation, Shirone asked Minerva,

"How is the Great Star?"

"She hasn't woken up yet. Of course not. She concentrated all of Gaia's power into five planets. Even if that stopped Ymir, we can't expect any more help from the Great Star."

A shadow fell over Shirone's face.

"More than anything, the problem is the army of Hell. We've located most of the legion commanders, but it's not easy."

Minerva immediately understood.

'I see. Shirone isn't just here.'

Currently, Shirone was struggling in various parts of the world where the 12 apostles had first taken positions.

'What kind of experiences is he having?'

He might be fighting a demon legion commander or wiping the tears of a child who lost their family.

'Or maybe both.'

It was impossible without a thought circuit that transcended human capabilities.

If there were a god, wouldn't that god's brain have a logical structure similar to Shirone's?

Seeing Shirone smile, Minerva averted her gaze as if pricked.

"Anyway, the reason the Great Star came to Bashka is because of this. It's no longer safe in the Demon World. That's why she wanted to meet Rian directly."

"Me?"

Rian pointed to himself.

"Yes, you. There are still a significant number of demon remnants left. Moreover, the legion commanders who can open the Demon Realm are still intact. It's probably about countermeasures for that."

"Hmm."

As Rian pondered what those countermeasures might be, Gaold suddenly coughed up blood.

"Cough! Cough!"

The blood was just a handful, but it seemed there was no more blood left to cough up.

Minerva, who had been taking care of him until now, turned to Miro and snapped,

"Hey, you take over now. I'm tired of it."

Miro looked at Gaold, who was crouching on the floor and coughing repeatedly, then turned away.

"Hmph."

Then, as if she didn't want to care, she walked to a corner and leaned against the wall, causing Kangnan's eyes to widen.

"Seriously!"

Sein placed a hand on Kangnan's shoulder.

"It's okay. We'll do it."

As Sein put one of Gaold's arms over his shoulder, Kangnan approached the other side and helped him up.

"Get some rest. We'll take you to the room where Zulu is."

Currently, Zulu was also in a state of fluctuating between life and death after being attacked by Ymir.

Dragging his legs, Gaold headed for the door, not lifting his head until he left the hall.

Watching him with a cold gaze, Miro muttered irritably as the door closed.

"I'm so sick of this."

Since reaching the flawless mental state of All-Seeing Projection, Gaold's pain could no longer shock her.

'It's over now. No matter how much you cling to me, it will only cause you pain.'

She was just angry.

"I'm going to rest too. Let's disperse for today and talk in detail tomorrow."

Although the world was still in a state of urgency, no one objected.

'The Demon War, the assassination of Havitz, the collapse of the flower field, the elemental bomb, the heavenly army, the sealing of Ymir...

It's hard to believe all of this happened in just one day.

'I'm tired.'

Everyone's eyes were hollow.

In an area 21 kilometers north of Bashka, the corpses of the allied forces began to stir.

Following the sound of rocks being crushed, the corpses were overturned, and Paimon emerged from the ground.

"Ugh!"

Mud mixed with mucus spewed endlessly from her throat like a fountain.

"Gasp! Gasp!"

Shaking her neck, she struggled to free her body from the ground. Her face was unrecognizable, her eyes were gone, her nose was disfigured, and the only moving part was her jaw.

"Cough! Cough!"

Despite being the highest-ranking general of the demons, she continued to shake her neck until her body began to emerge.

Soil poured out from beneath her belly, which had swollen more than a hundred times its original size.

"Got it. I'm out."

When she was hit directly by Shirone's Agape, she was transported the farthest away by the extralegal technique Ban.

Using all her momentum, she arrived 120 meters underground here.

At the moment she was melting in the light, she instinctively moved to a space where she could avoid it.

She doesn't remember it well.

"Damn Yahweh!"

If demons also come from human hearts, then Shirone's philanthropy has the power to purify the extreme emotions characteristic of demons into the most stable state.

'It's definitely lethal.'

She was left with only her torso, having eaten so much soil that her belly was about to burst.

"Ugh! Ugh!"

She vomited the soil several times, but it seemed it would be easier to just tear open her belly.

"Hehe."

Feeling a bit better, Paimon laughed madly at the night sky.

She had survived, after all.

"I won! Yahweh!"

Although the plan had gone awry, soon the legion commanders scattered across the continents would open the Demon Realm.

"We won."

As her body began to decay, it started to transform into a massive pathogen.

The disease that started here would ride the wind southwest and eventually infect the entire world.

"Satan."

With her thick eyelids melting away, she muttered as she glimpsed the moonlight.

"Lead the whole world into despair."

Paimon's corpse scattered like smoke and became a formless reaper flying towards Bashka.

The name of the disease was Emotion Sickness (or Emotion Scale).

It was an unprecedented disaster that acted on specific states of the brain to cause horrific tragedies.
