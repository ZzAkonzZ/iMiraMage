Chapter 1139: Key Keywords (2)

Panier was silent for a long time.

"Mr. Panier?"

When Reina called him, he finally snapped out of his thoughts and looked at Maya again.

"There was a guy playing guitar on the street. For a street performer, he was pretty good. No, he was really good. After watching him for a few days, I was convinced. This guy has potential."

Even back then, Panier was a talented planner, and now he had even published an autobiography.

'Who is he talking about?'

Even the artists who knew Panier's life story by heart had never heard of a guitar player.

"I signed him, prepared for a performance. Prepared and prepared. But in the end, he never got on stage. Why do you think that is?"

El Kiana spoke up.

"Was he not as skilled as you thought?"

"Of course not. If he lacked skill, he wouldn't have made it to the stage. But his skill wasn't the issue. He just couldn't deliver results."

It was still a regretful memory.

"He was straightforward. Played aggressive music. Honestly, no one likes that kind of stuff. He was probably the most stubborn guy in the world. I thought he'd come to his senses after a few falls, after tasting defeat…"

Maya understood the person.

"But he didn't."

"I don't want to blame him. As I said, maybe society didn't need that yet. Anyway, he failed. Again and again…"

Panier's voice rose slightly. "His own art. That's the most dangerous thing. It's fun for the person doing it because it's what they want. But for the audience, it's different. They have other jobs, or they try it as a hobby but aren't good at it, so they leave it to professionals like us."

He had shouted into the guy's ear many times.

"You can't live alone. But he didn't accept that. He believed the world would recognize him someday and stubbornly held on. Even as his life fell apart, as he rotted away! He clung to his guitar and sang his heart out."

Even now, it was frustrating to think about.

"Still…"

Recalling those days, Panier looked at the artists with a bitter smile.

"I didn't dislike him. Honestly, I liked him a lot. I wanted to make him famous somehow. I dreamed every day of him playing guitar on stage in front of a huge audience. A dream of delivering a powerful blow to the world."

It was the hottest time of his life.

"In the end, I'm the same. I talk about compromise, but I want the real deal to come and break everything. I'm a childish, pretentious artist."

El Kiana cautiously asked, "What happened to him? Is he still a street musician?"

"He left. I didn't give up, but his life was completely ruined. His daughter died of an illness."

"Oh…"

"It wasn't even an incurable disease. Shouldn't he have said something? He thought asking for help from someone he clashed with musically was a compromise, a defeat. It sounds crazy, but that's the kind of guy he was. Even if… it was his own child."

Panier's eyes reddened, and he gritted his teeth.

"…Idiot."

The reason he had never revealed to anyone seemed to be reflected in his twisted expression.

"His own art."

Calming his emotions, he asked Maya, "Does that answer your question?"

Maya lowered her gaze in acknowledgment.

'It's a difficult issue.'

To say what should be done here, human life is too diverse.

'Who was that person?'

Maya just wanted to hear the man's performance from Panier's past.

Apocalypse.

Shirone's group entered the Fairy Biomimetics building and took the elevator.

The elevator, which had been descending, moved horizontally, guiding them to the main system.

'This is taking forever.'

They could sense they had moved far enough to leave the city.

When the elevator arrived, Marsha navigated the maze-like underground facility without hesitation.

"This is it."

Opening the door to the special isolation zone, a giant mechanical eye awaited them.

"Digital Ra."

The High Gear managers were stunned by the wires connected everywhere.

"What on earth requires this much power?"

Marsha explained.

"It started as an energy circulation system. Now it completely controls this world."

Candy Mark asked, "Is it a program error? Otherwise, it wouldn't treat people like bugs."

"No."

Shirone spoke.

"There is no error. Digital Ra is performing its mission exactly as programmed."

To maintain the environment, this world.

"…The error is humans?"

From the machine's perspective, it might judge that way, but they were human.

"Is it not working?"

Marsha tapped Digital Ra's lens with her hand.

"Is there anything left to do? It has already covered the Apocalypse with mucus."

Kicking it, a metallic sound echoed.

"Marsha."

"It's fine. This thing has tormented me enough. Now, if we just inject the Ice Blood…"

The lens lit up.

"Ah! What's happening!"

The sound of power being transmitted was heard, and a massive vibration was felt from the ceiling.

Operator shouted, "Dally!"

The managers all rushed to open Anke Ra's code and began planting the virus.

"Did I wake it up?"

As Marsha panicked, Freeman placed a hand on her shoulder.

"Calm down. It didn't react because you kicked it. It's responding to our presence."

Mucus seeped from the ceiling and dripped down thickly like droplets.

Splat, the mucus that hit the ground bulged and transformed into a human shape.

Freeman aimed his magic bullet.

"Mucus Man."

As the incarnation of Digital Ra appeared, Shirone stepped in front of his group.

Mucus Man spoke.

"Why are you free?"

"That's what I want to ask. Why did you imprison people? What happened?"

"They regressed on their own. In the ground, in the cracks of walls, in the sewers, they were everywhere. I merely gathered such life forms and provided them with a comfortable environment."

"Regressed?"

Mucus Man looked at the High Gear managers, but they showed no special reaction.

"Humans couldn't handle their privileges. They writhed in pain and killed their own kind."

'Emotional illness.'

Also, avoiding pain through killing their own kind meant they had succumbed to the doctrines of the Satanic Church.

"You give names to individuals, but to me, humans are just my species. A foolish species that eats its own body."

That was how humans appeared to the machine.

"The species, having eaten everything and become emaciated, could no longer find anything to eat. The only way for them to avoid pain was to stop eating."

Reverse evolution into a desireless life form.

"That is the current human. Now they do nothing. They want nothing."

It was the conclusion of a world led by the Satanic Church.

"And the mucus?"

Shirone asked.

"You've covered this world with mucus. With humans like that, there's nothing left to destroy you."

Mucus Man hesitated.

"The singing."

"What?"

"The singing was too loud, so I had to block it. There was too much singing, so I had to cover the planet. Thicker, even thicker, even thicker…"

Shirone didn't understand.

"It must not spread outside. The system will be destroyed. Prison. There can be no escape."

'Sound.'

A signal that can contain both information and emotion.

Interpreted that way, the current sound from Mucus Man conveyed fear.

"I blocked it. I imprisoned everything."

Mucus endlessly seeped through the cracks in the underground facility, covering the floor.

Marsha signaled to the managers.

'Is it done yet?'

'It's much more complicated than we thought.'

Shirone could hear the labored breathing of the managers fighting desperately.

'We need to buy time.'

Of course, Mucus Man wasn't moving because he was also aiming for something.

"What do you plan to do with us?"

"This planet is closed. There will be no more changes. The being that created me is gone, so my existence has no meaning."

Shirone thought from a divine perspective.

'The result of a god is the cause of humans. If our cause disappears, the result of the god also disappears.'

In the same way, with humans gone, Digital Ra had no reason to exist.

"It's over, humans."

The walls crumbled, and the amount of mucus pouring in exponentially increased.

'Damn it!'

It seemed they intended to cover all the mass accumulated on the surface, including this core.

As Mucus Man disappeared, he said, "There is nothing."

With a sound louder than thunder, the walls collapsed, and all the mucus from the surface rushed in.

"Get behind me!"

Shirone blocked the managers and spread Miracle Stream in front of them.

"Huff!"

The moment it collided, the mass felt was immeasurable.

As the light barrier was pushed back 50 centimeters per second, Marsha shouted roughly.

"Is it done yet?"

"We're almost there! Just a bit more!"

The hologram window she checked showed the gauge bar just past halfway.

"Too slow! Press harder!"

"You don't even know! Do you think this is a syringe?"

67 percent, 70 percent…

As Shirone pushed Miracle Stream with all his might, the mucus was pushed back about a meter.

Feeling a mass that made his mind go blank, blood flowed from his nose.

"Huff!"

Unable to hold on, Shirone exhaled, and the mucus surged in.

91 percent, 94 percent…

"Hurry! Dally!"

The managers stomped their feet, but the gauge didn't move from 94 percent.

'Why isn't it working? Is it a failure?'

Shirone gathered his strength.

"Yaaah!"

As the final push of Miracle Stream vanished, the mucus surged in like a tide.

Countless faces of Mucus Man protruded from the surface of the mucus.

'It's over!'

Just as everyone sensed the end, the gauge bar jumped from 94 percent to 100 percent.

"Screeeech!"

Countless Mucus Man mouths emitted a noise that seemed to burst eardrums.

Covering their ears and turning around, Shirone's group cautiously opened their eyes after a while.

"Huff! Huff!"

Marsha fell on her backside, and Shirone saw the frozen mucus right in front of him.

"We did it."

As Ice Blood spread from the core, all the mucus in the world was neutralized.

The operator jumped up.

"We did it! I knew it! There's no way my program had an error!"

"We're alive! You're a genius! Well done!"

Number Seven, who had hugged her, jumped up and down before quickly regaining composure.

"Ah…"

As the two turned away with flushed faces, Marsha smiled and said, "Yes, well done. What happened at the end? I thought we were done for."

Smile Mark said, "It was probably because we broke through the firewall. Anyway, the first operation was a success. But…"

Looking around, they were completely sealed in by the rock-hard mucus.

"How do we get out of here? Digging through this would take forever."

"Is it finally my specialty?"

As Number Seven arrogantly raised his chin and approached, Shirone's eyes lit up.

'Ah, right.'

He was the one who created all the boss creatures that inhabited High Gear's land, sea, and air.
