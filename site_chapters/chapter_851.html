Chapter 851: Acceleration (2)

“Hmmm.”

Parkma, the division commander of the 7th Legion of the army of hell, glared at Ifrit and lowered his axe.

“This is… interesting.”

From behind Amy, her superior officer shouted.

“Commander! Quickly, this way!”

“Go ahead. I’ll buy some time.”

“I can’t do that!”

They couldn’t retreat and leave their commander behind.

“Don’t worry. I won’t overdo it. If we don’t retreat now, the unit will be annihilated.”

Through the Spirit Zone, Amy could sense the confusion among her troops.

“Go!”

As Amy’s sharp command brought her troops to their senses and they began to withdraw, Parkma burst into laughter.

“How admirable! Sacrificing yourself for your subordinates. Well, that’s what makes tormenting humans so fun.”

The longer she could delay, the more survivors there would be among her allies, so Amy remained silent.

“I’ve decided.”

As Parkma swung his axe, a strong wind blew, causing Ifrit’s body to shake violently.

“A human like you doesn’t respond to pain. I’ll whisper sweet words and slowly melt you.”

As a woman on the battlefield, she had already prepared herself for what would happen if she lost.

“Disappointing. Is that all you’ve got?”

Compared to her resolve, the demon’s threats seemed almost laughable.

“I’m a man of action. Once I get my hands on you, you won’t have time to be disappointed.”

That was also true.

Seeing that her allies had gained some distance, Amy’s eyes gleamed as she charged forward.

‘A demon division commander.’

For Amy, who aimed to become a Valkyrie corps commander, this was a mountain she would have to climb eventually.

‘Fire Fist!’

As Ifrit’s fists swung like a storm, explosions of flame erupted all around.

Each punch had the power of advanced magic.

“Is that all?”

But once one reached a certain level, power became a basic skill everyone possessed.

“It’s no fun if it’s too easy…”

Parkma twisted his body and raised his axe as he pushed through Ifrit’s flames.

“I like you!”

As he delivered a powerful blow, a tearing sound erupted from the three holes in his axe.

The Sonic Wave of Corruption.

The moment she heard the irresistible sound, Amy saw a vision of hell.

‘What is this?’

Rotten hands rose from the ground and grabbed her ankles, and soon corpses surrounded her.

‘I can’t move.’

Was it shock?

‘My mind is clear.’

She remembered hearing the horrible sound.

“Huuu…”

The corpses crawling from the ground surrounded Amy and forced her mouth open with their hands.

Parkma walked through the corpses toward her.

“Kekeke, finally caught you.”

As he approached Amy, he held out a centipede with dozens of legs.

“Eat it. It’ll taste good.”

It was utterly repulsive, but a trained mage’s mind could perfectly separate situation from emotion.

‘I can move.’

It was an illusion triggered by the sonic wave hitting her brain.

‘Time hasn’t passed.’

Time is an illusion of the senses; a brain separated from its senses cannot perceive its flow.

As the bug entered her forcibly opened mouth, Amy’s eyes flared red.

If it involved mental manipulation, she could reset it with self-image memory.

As her pupils turned red, cracks began to form in the hellish landscape.

‘Rewind.’

To just before she heard the sound.

Regaining her senses, she saw Parkma reaching out with a grotesque smile.

“Got you—?!”

Amy’s body reflexively turned around as Parkma’s center of gravity shifted sharply forward.

“Fwooo!”

Amplifying her mind to its peak state, flames began to blaze around Amy’s fist.

‘Take this!’

Using rotational force drawn up from her lower body, she struck Parkma’s side with an explosive blow.

“Kraaa!”

As Parkma flew back from the shockwave, black smoke rose from his grasped side.

“You…”

Parkma’s face twisted menacingly, but Amy remained ready to fight.

“I’ll remember this.”

A gleam flashed in Parkma’s eyes.

“I’ll never forget. Even if I have to carve your face into my retina. Remember this: I won’t forget you.”

Immune to mental attacks meant direct confrontation was unfavorable; Parkma leaped away into his allies’ ranks.

“I intended to kill him.”

She realized the power of a division commander firsthand.

“Amy!”

Tess’s voice called out as her cavalry unit cut through demons and approached rapidly.

“Tess!”

Tess accurately pierced demons’ eyes with her sword and pulled Amy onto her horse.

“What are you doing here? Everyone has retreated!”

Sitting back-to-back with Tess, Amy cast fire spells at their pursuers and shouted,

“What’s the situation?”

“We’ve been completely pushed back. Orders are to pull back our defensive line by 2 kilometers.”

Amy bit her lip in frustration.

‘Damn it! I couldn’t even take down one division commander.’

The difference in power was starkly evident.

The rare victories reported from distant parts of the world felt almost like lies in comparison.

Shirone helped Seongeum to her feet.

“What’s going on?” Seongeum's eyes were filled with intensity.

“The army of hell has completely taken over the sea. The 8th Legion Commander, Mitura, is a demon who rules the sea. Jincheon has no way to stop him.”

At the mention of one of the ten legion commanders, Shirone's expression changed.

“Makai?”

“He hasn’t opened it yet. But even so, being pushed back is disgraceful. I heard you saved Kashan. Please help us.”

As Seongeum tried to bow again, Shirone hurriedly grabbed her arm.

“Don’t do this. If it’s about fighting demons, I’ll help. But first, I need to check…”

As Shirone trailed off, Rian glanced sideways.

‘Of course he’s concerned.’

He had been hoping that the leadership of the Ivory Tower would evaluate the demons invading the central continent as the highest threat.

“I know what you’re worried about. I heard from the Grand Star.”

“Huh? The Grand Star?”

Seongeum’s eyes sharpened.

“If we take control of the sea, Jincheon will immediately strike Gustav. That’s where the main force of the army of hell is. If we can gain an advantage there…”

Shirone realized it too.

“The army of hell fighting in the central continent would have to return to Gustav.”

Even without a legion commander, it was Havitz’s unit.

“If you help Jincheon, we will help you. No, I will find a way no matter what…”

“Alright.”

He couldn’t bear to see Seongeum’s resolute expression crumble any further.

As he reached out to Salsu, Material dissolved and the Law Slayer returned to Shirone’s hand.

The reason he could imbue created matter with motion was that Material was Armand’s spirit.

“A naval battle…”

The fact that he had never fought at sea showed how short Shirone’s growth period had been, but with Material, he could find a way.

“Let’s go, Rian.”

Rian nodded vigorously and stood behind Shirone as Seongeum cast an ether wave.

“Thank you.”

As Shirone’s lips curled into a slight smile, the three of them vanished from the mountains.

Shehakim, Third Heaven

Ikael, who had organized Heaven's army, sought out the Immortal Community, Ten Lords, before departing.

Nine executives had crossed over to Earth at Ra Enemy's call, but only one remained here.

‘No, she cannot leave.’

Entering a secret room, she found a young woman who appeared to be in her teens sitting at a long table.

She was seated to the left of where Chief Beron used to sit.

“What are you waiting for in an empty place?” The Ten Lord’s second-in-command, Son Yujong.

Despite her youthful appearance, she was over ten thousand years old and remained silent even in the presence of an archangel.

‘Was this really a good idea?’

It was a contentious issue even among the archangels at White Realm, and Ymir especially opposed it vehemently.

In Heaven's history, there had been powerful beings who reached the realm of transcendence before Ymir or Ogent.

‘She will be uncontrollable. She is different from Ymir.’

Yujong had no purpose in life.

Lacking any standard for judgment made her tendencies close to extreme evil.

‘I wish she would come to her senses.’

The golden ring Anke Ra placed on her head to restrain her became useless when Nane allowed her transcendence.

“Will you fight for Heaven?”

Despite this, they needed to take her because Satan, the arch-enemy of angels, had descended on Earth.

‘We need Son Yujong.’

Fight chaos with chaos.

This was Ikael's conclusion after much deliberation before the final battle with Earth.

“For Heaven?”

Yujong turned her head and stuck out her tongue.

“Why would I do that?”

When Ikael glared and swung her hand, the chair shattered into pieces.

Screech! Screech!

A monkey clung to the wall in fright; it was not human but an animal.

“I wasn’t asking a mere beast.”

She approached like the wind and grabbed the monkey by its neck, pulling it close to her face.

“Where is your master?”

Ikael sought someone who had attained enlightenment in a monkey's body and ascended to godhood—a descendant of Sun Wukong.

Son Yujong was Sun Wukong's great-granddaughter.

Screech! Screech!

The monkey pointed beyond the wall with a dying expression. Ikael looked up at the ceiling.

As her halo spun and pierced through the wall in an instant, a sky 2 kilometers high unfolded above him.

Screech!

Terrified, the monkey wet itself on the ground below.

“Where?”

When it pointed beyond the horizon's mountain range, Ikael's “far sight” scanned the entire range.

Finding Yujong there, she sighed and became a streak of light shooting towards the mountains.

Screech! Screech!

Landing on the ground, she saw the monkey wiping its tears with its arms as it ran into a massive cave's darkness.

Following it closely, Ikael used her holy light to illuminate the dim cave interior.

On a floor covered in dry sand lay a girl using her tangled black hair as a pillow.

“Wake up. Have you been sleeping for hundreds of years?”

“Hmm?”

Yujong frowned and scratched inside her pants before sniffing her fingers.

Knowing how to handle her, Ikael shouted,

“Wake up!”

“Oh, damn! Really!”

Yujong suddenly sat up and turned her head as if nothing had happened.

“This is so annoying! Why are you waking me up?”

“There is a battle. Come with me.”

Yujong chuckled softly,

“A battle? Have angels gone senile? Your eyes look completely out of it.”

“Nane has allowed your transcendence. There is nothing left to bind you now.”

Blinking in surprise, Yujong checked and removed the golden band from her head,

“Oh? It really comes off?”

“There is something I need you for. If we win this war, I will grant you freedom.”

Yujong tossed aside the golden band,

“No thanks. Too much hassle.”

“You’re still arrogant. Do you really think you’ll be safe acting like that in front of me?”

“What if I'm not safe?”

Golden light shone from Yujong's eyes as she grabbed her staff and prepared to leap out,

“What are you going to do about it?”

The cave trembled with explosive energy,

‘Threats won’t work after all.’

Ikael tried another approach,

“If you don’t want to do it, then don’t. But shouldn’t you save your friend who has fallen into hell?”

“A friend? Which friend?”

“Ten Lord’s tenth-in-command Mortasinger. She has been dragged away by an evil ruler and is suffering.”

“What? Morta?”

Yujong jumped up immediately,

If there was one thing she could be counted on for—it was loyalty among monkeys,

“If you don’t want to do it… just keep sleeping here…”

“Wait.”

Yujong bared her fangs,

“Who is this bastard tormenting my friend?”

Ikael smiled and pointed outside the cave,

“Satan.”
