Chapter 788: Dead Calm Zone (1)

When Shirone arrived at the Aganos airfield, Kaidra's handlers had already prepared Ratusa for departure.

Iruki, who had come to see him off, handed Shirone his bag and asked,

“How long do you think it’ll take?”

“It depends on how cooperative the Aracne government is. But it shouldn’t take too long.”

Given the stature of the Ivory Tower, cooperation was almost guaranteed—and that was why Uorin had specifically chosen Shirone for the mission.

“The real issue is the Beta Fish herself, Emery Jaestin. It’s possible… she might not even hate Havitz.”

That possibility weighed on Iruki as well.

“She’s the only survivor while her entire bloodline was wiped out. Fear might have taken deeper root than hatred.”

The fact that she abandoned her name and went into hiding in another country only added weight to that assumption.

“I’ll do my best to persuade her. But if she refuses, that’s the end of it. Just like with Abella—Lawslayer isn’t a weapon that works by force.”

“Right. We’ll try to come up with a contingency on our side.”

After all, if Shirone couldn’t reach her with sincerity, no one else could.

“It’s not a dangerous mission, so try to relax a bit. Aracne is a world-famous resort, after all. You’ve been through a lot lately.”

Shirone’s face had noticeably thinned in recent days.

“It’s not just the combat...”

Clashing with the demons, who held a visceral hatred of Yahweh, must have been mentally exhausting.

“I’ll be back.”

Though he didn’t see it as a vacation, Shirone didn’t show it. He climbed onto Kaidra.

“It’s been a while, Ratusa.”

Ratusa rubbed its beak affectionately, recognizing the comrade who had once crossed Noskarta together.

“Take breaks whenever you need. Don’t push yourself just for our sake.”

Shirone tugged gently on the reins.

“It’s fine. If you get tired, I’ll carry you instead.”

As Ratusa rose, casting a massive shadow, Iruki’s gaze followed it up nearly four meters into the air.

“Let’s go!”

The massive creature dove off the edge of the airfield in a vertical drop.

KIIIIIIAAAAAH!

Its piercing screech echoed out as Ratusa reappeared and soared into the clouds.

“…Carry it?”

Iruki shook his head, imagining Shirone giving a piggyback ride to a giant monster bird.

Kingdom of Aracne.

5-star Hotel Rubystro.

“Maya, you’re all set, right? Did you see the sheet music for this song? Isn’t it incredible?”

Maya had arrived in Aracne three days ago.

“Yes, it’s really good. Upbeat and lively.”

The performance agency Elbor from the Kingdom of Tormia had invested special effort into Maya’s debut.

They had secured the opening stage for the World Beauty Pageant, Aracne’s biggest festival.

“Of course it’s good. It’s a collaboration between the best composer and lyricist.”

Over 20,000 tourists from all over the world came annually to see the World Beauty Pageant.

Palmas, a handsome man in his mid-thirties and Elbor’s CEO, trembled with excitement.

“The timing’s perfect. The scale’s a bit smaller this year because of those damned demons, but the press coverage from all the major networks will be global. Just focus on singing well.”

“Mr. Palmas.”

Maya spoke with a worried look.

“I really appreciate how kind you’ve been to me. I mean it.”

“Haha, what did I even do? I just happened to spot your natural talent before anyone else—”

“I really do like the song. It’s great, everything’s great but…”

She’d been holding it in, knowing how much care he’d put into it, but no matter how much she thought about it—she just couldn’t.

“The lyrics are strange. I don’t think I can sing them like this.”

“Huh? The lyrics?”

Maya threw out her arms and exclaimed,

“They’re too indecent!”

“Hm…”

Palmas blinked, thinking back on the lyrics, then nodded honestly.

“They are a little provocative. But that’s what makes them good, right? As long as it’s not blatant, a little sexiness always works.”

“Still, the meaning is too intense. I know it’s not my place as a rookie to say this, but… I just can’t do it.”

Palmas smacked his lips.

This is the problem.

Flawless looks, an out-of-this-world voice, a background in dance and magic, and great instincts to top it off—

But why!

Why was she so modest and shy?

“Maya, can’t you just close your eyes and sing it? I know your personality. That’s why I cut the undressing sequence, remember?”

Even that just meant showing some shoulder and cleavage, but Maya had flat-out rejected it.

“I—I’d rather do that! Honestly, I think it’s just me, but I really can’t say those things.”

She’d rather show skin than say such words—and faced with that conviction, Palmas couldn’t argue.

Is it really that hard?

Having a crush on a singer is the fastest way to ruin a company—but truthfully, he didn’t want to see Maya strip either.

“Alright, then let’s do this. There’s no time, so I’ll bring the lyricist in right now. Let’s try working on a revised version together.”

“I’m sorry, Mr. Palmas.”

Palmas smiled bitterly.

“This is my job. And if anything ever bothers you in the future, speak up right away. You being comfortable leads to the best results.”

Twenty minutes later, Palmas returned with a strict-looking man in square glasses.

“It’s not that I don’t like the lyrics. They just don’t fit the style I’m going for.”

Unlike at the audition, Maya now voiced her opinions clearly. Lyricist Moster quietly took a seat.

“Alright. Which parts bother you?”

She got right to the point.

“First, the word choices. ‘I’m gonna lick it.’ ‘Listen to the slurping sound.’ What am I even licking? And the double meanings. ‘A man hard like a stick.’ ‘Come inside my open heart’...”

Moster quickly raised a hand.

“Ah, that last one wasn’t mine.”

Maya bit her lip.

“…Anyway, that’s what I mean.”

As Palmas leaned against the wall watching, Moster sighed.

“Writers, by nature… the ones who build stories with words…”

Maya blinked at the sudden tangent.

“They’re all crazy. In this world, there are only two kinds of writers: ordinary people pretending to be writers, and lunatics pretending to be ordinary.”

Moster tapped his temple with two fingers.

“A writer’s brain always runs on two thoughts. Cold, logical, boring thoughts… and the thrilling, disgusting thoughts that destroy that logic.”

He scissored his fingers like blades.

“Those two thoughts clash like electricity and form logical, yet entertaining stories. Dual personalities. That’s how you go mad.”

“And?”

“You could be thinking the most beautiful thoughts… but still have a desire to stomp them to pieces. The important part is balance. Lean too far, and it all falls apart.”

“…And?”

Suddenly, Moster pushed back his chair and dropped to his knees.

“I gave in to that desire!”

“What are you doing?!”

Startled, Maya tried to lift him up, but he crouched even lower.

“When I heard your voice at the audition, I got chills! What an incredible subject! What would it sound like if that heavenly voice sang these lines? I couldn’t resist the impulse!”

“Are you insane?! What kind of thought is that?!”

“Trust me! It’ll work! Please, sing my lyrics with your beautiful voice!”

When Maya turned her head, Palmas was already at the door calling security.

“Take him out.”

Even as he was being dragged away, Moster didn’t give up.

“Please, Maya! I dream of you singing those lyrics!”

The door slammed shut.

“Sigh…”

Drained, Maya slumped into her chair. Palmas approached, smacking his lips.

“Sorry. This was entirely our fault. I’ll get you a new lyricist, no matter what it takes.”

He should’ve known personal obsession would get in the way.

“But it also means the subject matter was so powerful, even a veteran like Moster gave up his paycheck for it.”

If they could even use her modesty as a weapon, then maybe it was worth trusting Maya’s vision.

“Alright, let’s go pure.”

Maya asked nervously,

“There are only three days left—can we finish the lyrics in time?”

“We’ll have to mobilize the entire company. It’ll be tough to find someone with Moster’s instincts, but…”

He wasn’t hung up on that.

“Then… can I try writing it?”

“Huh? You, Maya?”

“Yes. I don’t really understand what Moster meant by those ‘two thoughts,’ but I want to try.”

“Hmm… not a bad idea.”

Now that the old plan was scrapped, leaning into Maya’s true self might be a solid strategy.

“Alright. Give it a go. I’ll make sure it goes to final review. Just keep practicing, alright?”

Maya smiled in satisfaction.

Shirone…

There was a song she desperately wanted to write.

Kiiiiiiii!

Kaidra’s screech jolted Shirone from his morning sleep.

“Yaaawn!”

The air, which had been cold when he fell asleep, was now filled with the warm humidity of the equator.

“Whoa!”

Like the sun had shattered across the sky, tens of thousands of glimmering lights floated above the windless sea.

Islands, where forests and cliffs blended in harmony, launched flurries of vibrant birds into the air like fireworks.

“This place is incredible.”

Shirone shook his head.

“But duty comes first.”

Having traveled eastward through the Ten-Island Kingdoms, Shirone parted ways with Ratusa above the skies of the Kingdom of Aracne.

“Thanks. I’ll buy you something tasty once we’re back in Kashan.”

Kiiii.

He patted Kaidra’s head, and before wasting another moment, leapt from the sky and plummeted toward the ground.

Aiming for Aracne’s capital, Bengol, he descended toward the city where he could already hear the crowd and festival music filling the streets.

“A festival.”

Unlike Minerva, Shirone preferred not to barge through royal walls—that just wasn’t his style. Instead, he landed on the rooftop of a nearby building.

Everyone was too swept up in the festivities to notice a stranger falling from the sky.

“So this is the Kingdom of Aracne.”

The island was the third in the chain of the Ten-Island Kingdoms stretching east from the southern continent’s coastline.

The kingdom’s origin traces back to natives who crossed over long ago by ship from the southern regions…

Perhaps for that reason, even with its development influenced by advanced continental civilizations, southern customs still lingered.

“Guess I’ll head down.”

Leaping into a back alley and emerging onto a main street, Shirone found himself in the middle of a parade.

Open carriages passed by, while Aracnean dancers in bikinis swayed their hips. A host called out through a microphone.

“Everyone! Here comes Miss Yakma!”

The crowd’s attention turned as the representative from the Republic of Yakma passed by in her decorated carriage.

“Miss Yakma! Show us your face!”

The contestant remained hidden behind a curtain, but a silhouetted hand waved in response.

“She waved at me! I love you! I don’t even know who you are, but I love you!”

The crowd erupted with laughter.

“Miss Tormia!”

Shirone’s gaze snapped to attention.

Our kingdom!

Though she remained obscured in silhouette like the others, her profile—sitting with hands on her knees—radiated a calm elegance.

Who is she? I’m really curious.

It was the same curiosity everyone shared. The mystery of each contestant would be unveiled on the day of the World Beauty Pageant.

“Miss South Aymond!”

The host’s voice rang out across the southern sky.
