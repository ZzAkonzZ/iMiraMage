Chapter 1063: High Gear (2)

Shirone was unable to move.

His body felt stiff, like a wooden plank, as if being drawn into some unseen vortex.

A blue light flowed like electricity through wires, and then, suddenly, darkness enveloped him.

"Is the event starting?" When had he closed his eyes?

Boom. Boom. Thunder echoed as Shirone opened his eyes, revealing a sky covered in murky clouds, reminiscent of filthy water.

Cold raindrops fell, half-closing his eyes.

"Hehehe! Found it! Found the stray mutt!"

A voice came from beneath him. Shirone tried to lift his head, but a chain around his neck held him down.

"I'm tied up." His entire body was wrapped in thick chains, pinned to a wooden board. A man walked through the mud, holding the chains slung over his shoulder, muttering angrily.

"Damn bastards. They dare ignore my achievements?"

As the man grumbled, Shirone assessed his own mental state.

"Hmm, as expected. Neither magic nor incarnation works." It wasn’t surprising—incarnation didn’t allow for falsehoods. He had chosen this path, so there was no rejection from his consciousness.

"I could probably break this, though." If he committed himself with the determination of Yahweh’s level, it seemed possible to destroy the chains.

"Still, it’s some serious coding. Whoever made this outclasses Shura."

He idly wondered how the man would react if he broke free. "Not that I'd get to see it." Breaking free would likely stop the event itself, derailing the entire scenario.

"Where are you taking me?" Suddenly, Shirone's mouth moved on its own.

"Let me go, you bastard!"

The man turned his head. "Hehehe! Awake now? You're lucky. You're going to live forever soon."

"No! Send me back to where I came from!"

"Too late for that. Why’d you crawl into the scrapyard? Should’ve stayed inside like the little rat you are."

"Aaah! Aaah!"

Shirone shook his head, thrashing around.

"This feels... dizzying." Though his mind felt calm, the lack of control over his vision was unsettling.

"Haah, haah!" Even the terrified gasps coming from his mouth didn’t feel like his own.

"We're here. We're finally here." From his grounded perspective, Shirone saw a door swing open and soon found himself inside, looking up at a ceiling.

He was on a narrow surgical table, tilted at an incline. For the first time, he saw the man’s face—torn, ragged skin barely clinging to the frame of a mechanical humanoid with glowing blue eyes.

"Do you know what this is?" The man held up a spherical device.

"No idea." Shirone wanted to answer but found his body trembling involuntarily.

"It’s a high gear. A semi-permanent power unit. With this, you can live forever."

"No! Don’t!"

Ignoring Shirone's protests, the man turned to a table.

"Let me introduce myself. I’m Dr. Martin. Developer of the high gear and a tragic genius. Just how much of a genius? I’ve only made one mistake in my entire life. But that one mistake left me like this."

From the table, Martin picked up a half-rusted axe.

"And what was that mistake? Giving immortality to trash like you. Damn it. Immortality can't be revoked. The high gear is a gift from God—it doesn't require food or sleep as long as there’s sunlight."

Screeetch.

The sound of the axe being sharpened echoed.

"What I didn’t anticipate was how worthless you all are. Back when I cured incurable diseases and developed an elixir to stop aging, they worshipped me like a god. Statues of me were erected everywhere. But once I gave them immortality..."

Martin snapped his head around, his voice seething with anger. "They wanted to kill me, to monopolize my technology and become new gods themselves. So they exiled me, destroyed me. But I didn’t give up. Those who defy God will face divine punishment. Hehehe! Yes, punishment."

Caressing the axe handle lovingly, Martin's face twisted into a grin of madness.

"I stole the sun from them."

Shirone had so many questions, but his body wouldn't respond. Martin had finished his preparations.

"The high gear will replace your heart. Billions of nanomachines will circulate through your bloodstream, eventually overtaking your brain. You’ll lose arms? No problem. Attach an excavator if you want. No immune response, nothing."

Martin placed the high gear on the table. Its lens-like grooves emitted a rhythmic blue glow, though it didn’t beat like a heart.

"I’m human! I’m not some monster, you damn bastard!"

The uncharacteristic outburst felt alien to Shirone, likely part of the event script.

"Well then, let’s begin. Hehehe! Hahaha!"

Martin spat on his palm and gripped the axe with both hands, raising it high.

"Your heart is mine!"

Just as Martin charged, Shirone’s scripted scream erupted. "No! Noooo—"

Before his scream ended, the axe plunged toward Shirone's abdomen.

"Huh!" A sensation like his heart dropping overwhelmed him, darkness falling over his vision.

"Beginning customization process," announced a woman’s voice.

When Shirone regained awareness, the damp laboratory was gone. He stood in a mirrored room, staring at his naked reflection as numerous gauges appeared around him.

"This space allows you to modify your physical form. Design your desired appearance and confirm when ready."

Looking through the options, Shirone noticed some were locked.

"Premium content?"

Glancing at categories like Gender Change, Mutations, and Monstrous Forms, Shirone made his decision.

"Confirm. Leave it as is."

Unlike others, Shirone wasn’t here to explore; his purpose was rooted in necessity.

"Customization complete."

Darkness returned, followed by a surreal, dreamlike landscape.

"Hehehe. Fight, fight." Martin’s voice echoed. Shirone realized he was being dragged across the ground, unchained but tethered to something.

"Immortality without the sun? A half-life. Destroy each other. Fight endlessly."

At the edge of a steep cliff, Martin yanked Shirone’s leg and shouted, "This is divine punishment!"

The moment Shirone felt gravity take over, the simulation's haze lifted, and his senses returned.

"Huff... huff..."

Gaining control of his body, Shirone steadied himself and gazed at the dizzying drop below.

"That’s... really high."

If he fell unprepared, even his torso wouldn’t survive intact.

-Activating AI program.

A mechanical voice echoed in his mind.

"Huh?"

A blue overlay appeared on his vision, with augmented reality information flooding in.

-High Gear Level 1. Energy output initiated.

A surge of energy radiated from Shirone’s chest, enveloping his entire body.

"Whoa."

Though his newfound strength countered the fear, the fall’s height still posed a deadly risk.

-Kinetic energy reduction mechanism activating.

The landscape lit up with red circles, highlighting optimal footholds and shifting probabilities in real-time.

"61.7 percent."

Spotting a protruding rock, Shirone grabbed for it.

"Argh!"

The impact felt like his arm would pop out of its socket, and the rock crumbled. Shirone fell again.

Using the highlighted footholds, he gradually slowed his descent.

Boom!

Landing safely on the ground, he steadied himself.

-Location identified: Beginner Zone, Iron Haven.

Cold wind brushed past as he surveyed his surroundings—endless piles of scrap metal obscured his view.

"These are all weapons or armor?"

An augmented reality scan revealed helmets, rifles, grenades—items unnecessary in Shirone’s world.

Ratatatatata!

Gunfire echoed from beyond the scrap piles.

"Looks like I need to gear up."

A notification pinged, displaying an envelope icon in his right eye.

-Would you like to view beginner tips?

"View."

Detailed information filled the display, offering insights he hadn’t encountered during the tutorial.

"Hmm, there are three key user stats: AI Level, Engine Output Level, and Selective Options."

Being Level 1, Shirone had no bonuses in any category yet.

"Well, let’s figure it out."

He scrounged through the scrap piles, equipping a worn helmet, a rifle, and a pistol, filling his belt with ammo.

The gear weighed over 10 kilograms, but it felt light.

"High gear..."

With every cycle of the power unit replacing his heart, explosive energy coursed through him.

"And this is just Level 1? What happens when I reach top rank...?"

A thrill coursed through him, pulling his lips into a grin.

"Alright! Let’s do this!"

Tapping his helmet, Shirone strode confidently into the virtual world.

200 meters away.

"He's here. He’s really here."

A long-haired man perched atop a scrap pile aimed his rifle at Shirone. His Level 7 AI highlighted a code name over Shirone’s figure: Yahweh2.

"Oh, Platinum?"

The man blinked.

"Barebones bio gear, old helmet from the starting zone, and Platinum?"

There was only one conclusion.

"Heh."

To secure such a rare code name, someone must have brought in a nobody from the real world.

"What a catch."

Placing his finger on the trigger, his AI locked onto Shirone’s helmet with pinpoint accuracy.

"The old helmet’s great gear, but Level 7 AI calculates penetration rates, you see."

The reticle displayed a fixed 98% penetration probability.

"Here we go."

He pulled the trigger.

At that moment, Shirone twisted his body, narrowly avoiding the bullet.

The long-haired man’s jaw dropped.

"H-how?"

If Shirone’s AI level were higher, he might’ve sensed the sniper earlier.

"Dodging a bullet with no assistance?"

The man's wide eyes stared at the code name Yahweh2 glowing faintly above Shirone.
