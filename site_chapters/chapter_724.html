Chapter 724:

Labyrinth Andre-World No. 283.

Shirone suddenly realized.

'Immortal Function.'

The answer to the problem he had been pondering for days was seeping into his mind through the circuit of insight.

Expanding his mind infinitely to open this world was the only way to break Andre's seal.

'But… why should I do that?'

How was it different from taking his own life?

"We work hard every day!"

Worker ants emitting cheerful pheromones were lined up to the horizon, carrying food.

Following Queen Garnet's suggestion, Shirone was also moving with a piece of a dead insect.

"Shirone, isn't working enjoyable?"

A worker ant passing by noticed Shirone deep in thought and emitted pheromones.

"Yes, of course."

Shirone smiled, wiping his sweat.

"Focusing only on thoughts isn't good. If you get lost in thoughts within thoughts, you lose what's truly important."

"What's truly important? What is that?"

"Well, maybe it's the reason for thinking?"

"…"

When Shirone stopped walking and blinked, the worker ant shook off the dirt and continued.

"When you actually face something, you realize it's completely different from what you thought. Sometimes, not thinking is the answer."

'Thoughts within thoughts…'

The nature imprinted in the genes of a tiny creature contained its own definition of the eons.

Garnet Colony is not complicated.

The queen's words echoed in his mind.

"Thank you for your hard work today!"

After finishing his work and arriving at Garnet Colony, Shirone washed himself in the water storage.

Communicating through pheromones was rare for ants, but Shirone was human.

'I've worked hard, so it's okay to use a little.'

After washing off the dirt and descending into the colony's underground, he found the noble ants occupying the path.

They were all male ants, filled with hostility towards Shirone, who was resting in the princess ants' area.

"Could you move?"

As Shirone advanced, he felt the web of killing intent they had spread.

Dar-An, who had never liked him from the start, blocked his way.

"Do you think you're getting special treatment? Not at all. You're just a toy for the princesses. The moment they get tired of you, you'll become food."

When faced with the wild killing intent devoid of humanity, Shirone realized where he was.

'They're just acting according to their nature.'

Therefore, he couldn't blame them.

"Move. I understand your feelings, but I'm a different species. I have no reason to fight you."

"Do you know? Garnet Colony is having trouble securing food. The army ants have expanded their territory."

"So?"

Dar-An's jaw pointed at Shirone.

"I want to see how nutritious a human is. That's the role of a noble."

With frequent skirmishes and the nobles needing to continue their lineage, they were extremely sensitive.

'Nothing is insignificant…'

Shirone's life was not insignificant either.

"Move. I'm the queen's special guest. That should have been the order."

"That's only in peacetime. In wartime…"

As Dar-An opened his jaws like scissors, pheromones from the 13th Night burst out from behind the noble ants.

"Stop."

The male ants, already displeased, turned to see the 13th Night exuding a menacing aura.

Dar-An pushed the nobles aside and approached.

"Are you giving orders to a noble? Just because you've been on the battlefield, do you think we're easy?"

The 13th Night, who had won today's skirmish, was covered in countless wounds.

"I'm advising you for your own good, Dar-An. This human is the queen's guest and, more importantly, strong."

It meant the nobles, who had never experienced combat, couldn't handle him.

"Hmph! Because he uses the same abilities as you? Does that mean we have to obey you too?"

"Don't misunderstand. It's a message from Princess Merot. She said to ensure he has no inconvenience."

If it was from the first-ranked Princess Merot, the nobles had no choice but to leave Shirone alone.

"Good for you. Why don't you go and flatter them?"

Following the path Dar-An opened, Shirone descended underground and sighed as he left the nobles' area.

"Thanks. You helped me avoid a fight."

Soldiers obey nobles' orders, but the 13th Night was an ant who had realized the art of transformation.

"The army ants' formation is unusual. The atmosphere is tense. Stay low for a while."

After the 13th Night left, Shirone arrived at his quarters, where he received a warm welcome, unlike in the nobles' area.

"Welcome, Shirone. Was it tough?"

Princesses quickly gathered around Shirone, emitting chatty pheromones.

"How was the outside world? Were there many dead insects?"

"What color was the sun? Color 2837?"

When Merot appeared, the princesses immediately made way for her.

"You made it back safely. I'm glad, Shirone."

The skirmish near the colony was a hot topic among the princesses.

"The army ants are different from us. They minimize their labor force and produce many soldier ants."

On the way to the quarters, Merot explained the situation.

"They expand their territory by occupying colonies. If a full-scale war breaks out, the damage will be significant."

But they couldn't move the colony.

"If war breaks out, I'll fight too. But Shirone, you don't have to stay in the colony."

The situation seemed more serious than he thought.

"You don't need to get caught up in the war. If you want to leave, do it tonight. I'll allow it."

"Merot…"

Ants are tiny creatures.

At least to Shirone, who had lived in the human world, they weren't worth sacrificing his life for.

'Is there a higher value to life?'

Back in his quarters, Shirone lay down on the dirt floor, using his arm as a pillow, and fell into thought.

'I don't want to disappear like this.'

What if someone showed an ant and told him to sacrifice his life for it?

If it were a joke, they'd think he was crazy. If he were serious, he'd get slapped.

'No, it's not because they're ants. There's nothing more precious than one's own life. There must be another way.'

Even the other Shirones exploring different worlds wouldn't open the Immortal Function.

How long had he been sleeping?

Hearing thunder outside, Shirone slowly opened his eyes.

'Is it raining?'

This was the lowest level of the colony.

"It's not rain!"

Shirone jumped up and pulled out the artificial brain, which began transmitting massive amounts of information.

"Stop them! Protect the queen's chamber!"

"Get away, you hideous creatures!"

Shirone sprang out of his quarters, using the momentum to see a horrifying scene that made his heart stop.

'Army ants.'

They were twice the size of Garnet Colony's soldiers, and their heads were three times larger.

Every time their powerful jaws moved, ants' heads fell off.

"I can't die yet! Protect me!"

As the army ants attacked under the cover of night, the male ants on the upper floors began to flee.

Though they usually boasted their large size, they were no match for the massive army ants.

"Where are the soldiers? Protect me now!"

Dar-An emitted pheromones of fear, but only army ants swarmed in.

"Die, you insignificant creatures!"

"Ahhh!"

As Dar-An's body was overturned by the army ants, the 13th Night flew in.

Law of the Earth - Antlion.

As the scenery distorted, the army ants biting Dar-An were torn to pieces.

"You…"

Dar-An, holding onto the last shred of noble pride, refused help but was already unable to move.

"Shirone."

The 13th Night looked at Shirone.

"I will protect the queen."

The queen couldn't move with the colony, making her the most vulnerable in an attack.

"I am the 13th Night."

It was a happy life to have a name.

"Wait! You have to protect me! The queen is already…!"

Ignoring Dar-An's pheromones, the 13th Night left, and Shirone snapped to attention.

"Merot!"

Army ants were already roaming the princess's quarters, and screams echoed everywhere.

"Merot! Mero…!"

When he arrived, three army ants were pinning Merot down, choking her.

Their sharp jaws pressed against her neck as if playing, making Shirone's hair stand on end.

"Shirone…"

Seeing Shirone, Merot opened her jaws.

"Run! Run away!"

At that moment, Shirone's mind recalled,

'When you actually face something…'

You realize it's completely different from what you thought.

It was the pheromones of a nameless worker ant.

"Run! You can't stay here!"

As Merot struggled to hold the army ants, the one biting her neck tensed its body.

"Stay still! I'll cut your neck!"

Just before Merot's neck was cut, a flash from the Photon Cannon struck the army ant's head.

As the fallen army ant was checked by its comrades, they raised their upper bodies and shouted.

"What? There's such a species…!"

Before the pheromones could be emitted, the Photon Cannon struck the army ants, pinning them to the wall.

'When you look closely, all life is the same.'

For beings born in the endless cycle of reincarnation, high and low had no meaning.

'These ants are the same.'

Realizing how to stop the war, Shirone approached Merot and extended his hand.

"Are you okay? Can you move?"

"Shirone… what was that just now?"

She had heard about magic, but she never expected it to be so powerful.

"Let's go. There's something I want to show you."

There was no reason to hesitate any longer.

Labyrinth Andre-World No. 847.

"Kill them! Pay them back for what they did to us!"

Shirone's code was 427,621.

Realizing that no one in Codename had powers anymore, countless citizens stormed the temple.

"Damn it! Let go! How dare you touch me with an 800,000 code!"

Gale, who had been beaten with clubs, couldn't hold back and lashed out, but punches rained down on him.

"Shut up! What's the difference between you and me? Just because your rank was higher, you tormented us!"

Gale, who had harassed countless women using his high rank of 9, was now suffering the most brutal fate.

But the resentment of the lower-ranked residents wasn't limited to those who had directly suffered.

"Kill them all! Let them all die!"

Drine, who had once been the king of Codename, lay unconscious, but the kicking didn't stop.

"You're the worst! You! You!"

With no clear distinction of high ranks anymore, people were destroying everything in sight.

"Shirone, I'm scared…"

Panika trembled, hiding behind Shirone.

"It's okay. I'm here."

In a world where rank discrimination had disappeared, Shirone was the only one who could use powers.

Thus, the residents didn't dare approach Shirone, but it had long lost its meaning.

'There is no such thing as rank among humans.'

There is no standard that defines nobility here and inferiority there.

"Shirone? Where are you going?"

Shirone gave Panika a sad smile.

"There is no life that doesn't deserve to be saved."

Spreading his arms wide as if to embrace the whole world, Shirone slowly closed his eyes.

A single tear rolled down his cheek.

'May everyone be happy.'

There was no reason to hesitate any longer.
