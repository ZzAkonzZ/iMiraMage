Chapter 810: Age of Chaos (2)

The monstrous creature Kaidra rapidly traversed the desert, sending sharp cries into the sky.

'Havitz has become Satan.'

The identity of the creature was Latusa, and the person riding on its back was the Empress of Kashan, Uorin.

The only method to eradicate evil, the project, had failed, and she had suffered from a fever for three days.

Unable to recover, she kicked off her covers to go riding for a change of scenery.

'I can’t believe I failed.'

Her head throbbed again, and her age-old heart beat as if it would burst.

The repercussions of failing to eliminate Havitz had spread worldwide, causing substantial damage to Kashan as well.

'Is the future really this suffocating?'

The real reason for her fever wasn't simply because the problem could be resolved.

'What should be done next?' According to the thoughts of Mitochondrial Eve, Kashan would fall, and Uorin would meet a terrible fate.

'I won't be able to protect Shirone.' While encompassing all is philanthropy, she would not use her powers for the glory of Kashan.

'Gaold is not an option.'

There was only one person for him.

'The Buddha has vanished, and extreme goodness has weakened. Miro won’t withstand extreme evil.'

A terrifying thought crossed her mind.

'Havitz. Extreme evil.'

The path for Kashan to continue its glory, as Gando said, was to join hands with Gustav.

'I can endure if I become the extreme evil.' It might be an easy decision for an individual life, not Mitochondrial Eve…

'Shirone.'

Then, a beautiful ending with a loved one would forever turn into bubbles.

"What do you expect me to do!"

She had to come out because it was impossible under the name of Terra to scream human screams without being caught.

"What am I supposed to do!"

Uorin's cry echoed in the wind when a dark swarm approached from the sky.

'It's demons.'

As Kaidra hurriedly reduced its altitude for low-altitude flight, it seemed already too late.

"It's a human! Catch it!"

The 24th Flying Brigade, part of the Hell's Army's 16th Legion, spotted Uorin and immediately turned direction.

"You insect-like creature!"

Orghante, the commander of the 2nd Division of the 16th Legion, was a demon covered in grayish chitin.

"Die!" His specialty was creating shockwaves in all directions by thrusting his long tongue into the ground.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

As sand soared 10 meters high, the corpses of dead demons tumbled through the air.

"Can't catch me."

Kido, flapping an old cloak, lowered his body's center to the ground.

"Earthbound spirit."

The desert squirmed like a swamp, and Orghante's tongue knotted and exposed its form.

"You son of a—!" Numerous spikes burst from Orghante's chitin, shooting forward like arrows.

Kido muttered as he adjusted his glasses.

"It wouldn’t taste good, though."

For him, who had mastered the laws of the earth, the ground meant something entirely different from other living creatures.

Just as a shark drags a lion into the sea, or a bird pulls a whale into the sky.

'To become one with the earth.'

To Kido, who was the earth itself, everything moving on the ground was like ants on his palm.

"Ugh! What is this?"

Just as the ground seemed to wave like the ocean, suddenly Kido was behind Orghante, swinging his spear.

As he crossed his thick chitinous arms in an X shape, a clang sounded as the blade stuck.

"Kekeke! You're fast, but you're not strong enough. Your weapon can’t cut me."

With the blade still embedded, Kido spoke.

"You didn’t touch me."

"What?"

"What we feel as a collision is actually just the repulsion of electrons."

Nothing can truly touch.

"The positions of electrons are probabilistically distributed, and this probability cloud encases the atom. Simply put, collisions don’t actually happen in the realm of particles."

Transcendental Technique—Mastery of the Origin.

As he concentrated deeply, Kido's eyes sank as if being sucked into the abyss.

"So if I find the probability that my blade's atoms split between your chitinous atoms and pass through...

The blade began to penetrate and split the chitin.

"Hey, wait a minute." Orghante's expression turned incredulous.

"Wait up! This doesn’t make sense! Who fights like this!"

Even as he tried to escape, a tremendous gravitational force bound his legs, making him immobile.

"Damn it! That's cowardly! Let's fight again!"

The blade that had pierced through Orghante's arms finally reached his crown.

"It's not without its difficulties. It's quite hard, plus it's very slow."

As if touched by hot substance, the demon's face split perfectly in two.

"Uaaaah! No! Nooo... Ack!"

Only after the blade passed through his face and reached his neck did Kido release his superhuman focus.

"Huh."

Thud—Orghante's body collapsed forward.

"Where??????"

As Kido peeled flesh from the corpse with his nails and lifted his head to squeeze the blood.

Drops of blood fell on his tongue, and through 'the taste of memory,' the demon's memories seeped in.

"Once, you must have loved." It was before he became a demon.

"What is love?"

Looking up at the sky and lost in thought, Kido suddenly bowed his head and chuckled.

"Kekeke! What happens if I speak like a human?"

He still couldn't fully comprehend Shirone.

"Yes, I am still immature."

Rising briskly, he swung his spear.

"Having acknowledged my deficiencies...

That's the hardest thing to do.

"Now there’s only moving forward." As he shook off his distractions, he felt a thud from the horizon.

"What now?"

"Kekeke! What a stroke of luck. To accidentally encounter the Empress of Kashan among humans."

Over two hundred demons of the 24th Flying Brigade surrounded Uorin and Ratusa.

"Hmph, what can mere demons do if they find me?"

Ratusa threatened with a monstrous cry while Uorin drew the sword from her saddle.

"Well, if it’s the Empress, she must be unlike ordinary humans..."

Brigadier General Dalmang raised a massive serrated greatsword with horns set at right angles on his temples.

"So, isn’t that a delicacy?"

Murderous intent filled Uorin's eyes.

'Can I take them down? It's risky.' Despite mastering numerous techniques as Mitochondrial Eve, her body was inevitably subject to generational changes.

'I haven’t mastered it.'

In the end, no matter how advanced her techniques, the combat power she could exhibit had limits.

"Attack! We need to vent the frustrations we've suffered at Kashan's hands here! Capture her alive!"

Winged demons swooped down on Ratusa, and Dalmang swung his serrated greatsword like a windmill, charging forward.

"Hah! Catch me if you can!"

Uorin countered with magic and avatar techniques as a fierce battle unfolded.

"Ho? Quite the technique?"

With the body of a Yaksha and the avatar techniques of Prajna, powerful magic exploded, but Dalmang remained composed.

"Keke, tired already?" She had never neglected her training, but real combat drained her stamina much faster than practice.

'My body is really weak. Have I focused too much on aesthetic standards?'

She resented her ancestors.

Kyaaaaaaaa!

As Kaidra made a monstrous noise, Uorin turned to see numerous demons impaled with spears on its body.

"Ratusa!"

"Where do you think you're going?"

As Dalmang swung his serrated greatsword at the escaping Uorin, a sharp sound rang out.

Pililililili!

A unique sound wave from the spear's hole was minor, but Uorin remembered everything.

'This sound?'

Something rolled at an unseen speed, and in an instant, Dalmang's head dropped.

"Brigadier!"

As the demons cried out, the shadow of a wheel sharply turned in radius.

Dozens of demons were sliced apart, and finally, Kido appeared.

"Ah, the human female emperor. It's been a while."

Kido grinned sharply and adjusted his glasses as Uorin's eyes narrowed.

"Why are you here?"

"Let's talk later. First, let's deal with this." The variety of demons was vast, but wandering the desert, Kido's combat experience was no less extensive.

"One, two, three, four. Five!"

As he counted, the demons were quickly exterminated, and those with wings took to the sky.

"He's no ordinary creature! Attack from the sky!"

"Arrogant demons."

Kido spun like a top, rolling across the desert on his back.

"Relying on the sky for sustenance...

Avatar technique, Earthbound spirit.

"Why rely on the sky?"

As if invisible hands rose from the ground and grabbed their ankles, the demons were pulled down to the ground.

"Uaaaah! What is this!"

It felt like being weighted down by a ton of iron balls.

"Slice, slice, slice, slice, slice." As the spear spun, the bodies spun, and the world seemed to spin, Uorin watched dazedly.

'He's gotten stronger.' The self-proclaimed Goblin King, the wind guards he had once opposed, now presented a completely different presence.

"Slice, slice, oh?"

In the middle of the desert, surrounded by the dismembered bodies of demons, Kido was the only one still spinning.

As he sprung up from a roll and planted his feet on the desert sand, he exclaimed, "Aha!"

Stopping in front of Uorin, a cloud of dust billowed up, enveloping her entire body.

"Hahaha! It's the traditional Goblin way of applying makeup."

Applying mud during combat was a goblin tradition.

"Whatever..."

As Uorin dusted herself off, she asked, "Why are you here?"

Instead of answering, Kido turned away, picked up a piece of flesh from the brigadier's body, and began to chew on it.

Uorin's brows furrowed slightly.

"I'm in the midst of a quest for enlightenment. Doesn't the human king know that?"

She knew, of course, but didn't particularly care.

"What enlightenment?"

"Love."

Kido approached.

"Goblins are strong but they don't love like humans. I'm searching for a love that transcends feeding and breeding."

When Kido was guarding Shirone in Andre's Labyrinth, he felt human emotions for the first time.

"Hmm, you say that, but you're eating demon meat quite deliciously, aren't you?"

"Hahaha! Of course. I have to eat to live."

It seemed he was far from reaching true enlightenment.

'Barbaric.'

Kido, not wearing pants, let his goblinhood swing freely between his legs.

'It's quite big for his height.'

Feeling Uorin's gaze, Kido, far from embarrassed, proudly thrust his hips forward.

"I'm the King of Goblins."

"… Anyway, thanks. I'll compensate you since you helped. If you want something, just ask."

"I don't want anything. If there is something, it might be true love, but you can't give me that, can you?"

Uorin glanced down at Kido again and then shook her head as if in shock.

"I'm not into that, maybe I could introduce you to a human woman?"

"Haha! No need! I'll find my love myself. And it doesn't necessarily have to be human."

Kido turned around and waved his hand.

"Have a good trip. Watch out for the demons."

"Wait a minute."

Uorin's eyes sparkled.

'He might be useful.'

Kido was strong, a friend of Shirone, and above all, he possessed the ability known as 'the taste of memory.'

"Won't you come with me to the imperial city?"

"The imperial city?"

Kido's lips parted slightly in surprise.
