Chapter 921: The Measure of Desperation (1)

In the year 666 of Omega.

"Save yourself."

Goffin muttered quietly as he watched the black rain falling from the sky.

Erasure Code.

After Anke Ra failed the second reset, this was the last resort chosen.

"Small Purification."

The black rain was dissolving everything it touched, erasing the landscape.

"Are you not going to intervene this time?" Argones, leaning against the cave wall where Goffin was hiding, asked.

"Or is it that you can't intervene?"

Despite being in the same space, Goffin, who had been ignoring Argones until now, finally spoke.

"I thought Great Star would come."

Argones shrugged.

"As you can see..."

With everything on the horizon melting away, it was easy to guess her condition.

"This place isn't safe either. There's nowhere to run. All life on Earth will be annihilated. Do you feel responsible?"

The Gaia people had explosively increased their population to strengthen the Ultima system.

"It was too much. Even Anke Ra was overloaded. But look at the result. This is it."

Small Purification.

"Anke Ra chose to reduce the population at the cost of destruction. Ultima is weakened, and you cannot win."

"Isn't it strange?"

Goffin smiled bitterly.

"When Gaia people were not yet mentally complete, many things happened. They killed their kin or discriminated against the weak. We called it evil."

Even Gaia people, who achieved an integrated mental system, had periods of conflict and discord.

"But Anke Ra defined all Gaia people as evil. Leaving the Photon System is considered a greater evil than murder or any other crime."

Argones spoke.

"Our mission is the perfection of the system. Murder and leaving the Photon System both disrupt the world on a large scale. Of course, leaving the Photon System is much more dangerous."

"Indeed."

God may need to be indifferent.

"If God had a heart, this world would be hell."

"Accept it. The heart is an uncontrollable signal. That's why the hidden world exists."

By purifying powerful emotions that flow into hell, the system stabilizes.

"The same goes for time. Without 0.666 seconds every second, photon signals wouldn't stabilize. Absolute time would be destroyed by individual relativity."

"Yes. Everything is a matter of the heart." Therefore,

"I don't like this world." It's too cold.

"I can guess how much pain Great Star is in, but what about you? How do you feel about all life disappearing?"

All matter was flowing away, creating a vast black river in the world.

"It's sad."

Argones looked up at the sky.

"This is what you caused. Anke Ra tried to grant eternal life through the system of emptiness."

"Eternal life..."

Among Argones's creations were beings without lifespans, but it wasn't true eternal life.

"Eliminating the concept of lifespan is easy. You too are like that. But in the end, death is final. Even if you live for a hundred million years, when you die, all processes are instantaneous. True eternal life transcends the cycle of life and death."

The system of emptiness could make it possible.

"It's not too late now. Give up resistance and obey Anke Ra's will. Enter the next world."

If Gaia people block emptiness, eventually all higher universes will disappear.

Goffin spoke.

"We dream. In that dream, we dream again. It feels like we'll never wake up if we keep dreaming endlessly... But on the other hand,

"The first world continues to flow with time. When the sun rises and fatigue fades, we eventually wake up from sleep."

Argones countered.

"But you'll never wake up. The subject's consciousness keeps delving into dreams."

A massive contradiction created by time and mind.

"Think carefully, Goffin. This world started from a single unique and irreversible illusion. If you block emptiness, everything will disappear. There will be nothing left again. Don't you know how terrible that state is?"

Even an infinite magician shuddered at the thought of infinite nothingness.

"...I have a request."

Argones had no intention of helping but was intrigued by Goffin's request.

"What is it?"

"Save them."

Argones couldn't find words to respond.

"Of course I'll fight. But you don't want this world to end like this either, do you?"

"So? What do you want me to do?"

"Just two..."

Goffin raised his index and middle fingers.

"Let two people escape Small Purification. No matter how much you hate us, they're your creations too, right?"

"There's no way to escape. Anke Ra's Erasure Code won't stop until all life disappears."

"Come on, between us?" "I know how many species you've created. Surely we're not alone in this universe?"

"The universe isn't vast. It's confined by the speed of light barrier based on photon signals. The number of living beings is much smaller than you think."

"But... there are some?"

Argones finally spoke after being cornered.

"Theraphos."

"Oh? Are they similar to us?"

"No. Gaia people developed their minds to extremes while dragons reached the pinnacle of information. If Gaia people are quantum code and dragons are photon code, Theraphos lies in between."

"Hmm, in between."

Something that can contain both mind and information?

"Sound."

That was correct.

"Yes. Theraphos are a species that has elevated waves to extremes. Their technology surpasses Gaia people's because they can transmit information beyond light speed barriers."

It meant intergalactic travel was possible.

"Amazing."

"They're an excellent species—much more stable, devout, and strict than you."

Argones's emotions were conveyed through sound.

"I'm sorry for being reckless then. Anyway, can you convey my intention to Theraphos?"

Back to business again.

"Why should I? It’s not that I dislike you. The problem is the link. There must be a reason why the Gaia people are linked to Idea among many species, but my conclusion is that it’s a failure. You are destabilizing the world."

Goffin suggested, “Okay, let’s do this then. If two Gaia people open a new world, then Theraphos will judge it. If even this new humanity heads towards chaos, then it's okay to destroy it.”

“Hm.”

--- What?

Argones rested his chin on his hand.

“Be precise. Does what you mentioned include deviations in the photon system? No one should access the secrets of the world.”

“Of course. Even if we fail, I hope the survivors continue to live on, even if it's in an eternal dream.”

Tsk tsk tsk.

As Argones pondered rapidly clicking his tongue, his body gradually began to seep into the ground.

“Select two people.”

His voice echoed in the now-empty cave.

“I’ll send the ark.”

God’s Sanctuary, Aravot.

The spire that pierced the sky was the only place on the planet where rain did not fall.

“The war has ended.”

In the chamber of angels, Ikael took in the view of the dark river flowing outside the window.

“They will fight.”

Turning his gaze, Satiel was suddenly beside her.

“Are you still not giving up? The Gaia people will be annihilated with this. The Ultima System will also disappear.”

“If that’s really the case, we also lose the chance to access the world’s secrets.”

Ikael clamped her mouth shut.

Though the tiresome fight with the Gaia people was over, she felt a void in one corner of her heart.

“Why do you side with humans?”

Ikael asked, “The secrets of the world? Even without such things, winning is all that matters. Is that really the only reason?”

“Hardly.”

Satiel smiled as she saw a hexagonal light unfold across the horizon.

“There’s one more reason.”

“Damn it.”

Goffin smirked under the hexagonal light, hiding the Gaia people gasping for breath.

“Is everyone okay?”

The leader of the Gaia people, Patran, squinted his eyes.

“Goffin.”

An entity that transcends the concept of lifespan, following the eternal history of the Gaia people.

“This is not your concern. Regardless of what happens, we must protect the Ultima System.”

“Then everyone dies.”

Faced with a force that could melt the entire planet, even Goffin was helpless.

“Is there a way?”

“One. Well, more like 0.5. But there’s something we need to do first.”

“What needs to be done?”

Goffin brought up the topic of the ark.

“Select two people?”

“Yes. Even if we manage to prevent purification, 90 percent of the Gaia people will disappear. But we’ll fight till the end, won’t we?”

Patran nodded.

“Whoever wins, humanity must be preserved. In that sense, we decided to send two.”

As they were connected to the Ultima, they didn't go through complex procedures.

“It has to be the two people who are beloved the most.”

As if by agreement, everyone looked at a couple.

“There…”

A frail-looking man and a woman with large, innocent eyes approached.

Goffin smiled at them as they prepared for a long journey.

“What are your names?”

“Huh? Oh, yes, I’m Adam.”

Goffin nodded and turned to the woman.

“I’m Lilith.”

Adam and Lilith.

Names destined to become the new humanity on the approaching planet.

“Right. You’ll hear the full story on the ark. From now on, you’ll live endlessly like me. In any situation, you must trust and love each other. You must never cross the forbidden line. Chaos is coming to the world.”

The two nodded as if making a promise.

“Don’t worry. I love Adam, and I’m confident we’ll be together forever.”

Lilith’s eyes sparkled with determination.

“That woman…”

Receiving records from Omega, Shirome felt his heart sink.

Though she looked nothing like her, the light in her eyes was strikingly similar to someone he knew now.

‘Uorin?’

It was the gaze of Mitochondrial Eve.

“Yes. Remember this. You define both good and evil. You must never lose Ultima.”

As Goffin made his final affirmation, a massive disc-shaped flying object cleaved through the clouds.

“The ark.”

The technology of the species that would judge humanity.

“Go. I’ll send you.”

Patran asked, “What do you plan to do? The administrator has powerful enough authority to disrupt the order. We can’t stop it with a Hexa.”

“Not until we know what it is.”

“And then?”

“I’ll swallow it.”

Goffin smiled as he soared towards the black rain that obliterated everything.

“Save yourself?”

As he clenched his teeth in agonizing pain, Goffin retorted.

“Stop talking nonsense.”

Miracle Stream.

As an explosion occurred, a massive hole of light formed, beginning to suck in the surrounding clouds.

“AAAAAAH!”

Anke Ra’s rage, a feeling too intense to be satisfied by merely destroying the world, struck Goffin.
