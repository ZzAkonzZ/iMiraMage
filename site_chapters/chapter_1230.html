Chapter 1230: Missing Link (1)

Year 666 of Omega.

Due to Anke Ra's purification, two Gaians boarded the Ark and departed from Heaven.

With the help of Theraphos, they arrived on a remote planet. Their names were Adam and Lilith.

“This is our land.”

The atmosphere was similar to that of Heaven, and the ecosystem was just as vast.

“Let’s call it Eden.”

While Adam was satisfied, Lilith was uneasy.

“Ultima’s power has weakened. Can we really start a new life here?”

“It’ll be fine. We are a race that fought the gods. You and I—we’ll be happy here forever.”

As time passed, the ancient city of Eden flourished enough to be called humanity.

16.8 billion Gaians.

The explosive population growth was due to their incredibly long lifespans.

After returning from the fields, Adam kissed Lilith, who welcomed him into her arms.

Love that transcended time—perhaps that’s why they were chosen to board the Ark.

“Another peaceful day.”

Looking down at the city of Eden, Lilith smiled warmly.

“There’s nothing more joyful than watching our children live happily.”

She felt like she had everything.

‘They’re all my children.’

If a parent had 16.8 billion children, they might understand how Lilith felt.

“Hm?”

Suddenly, the sky opened, and a dazzling flash of light lit up the land.

As every Gaian in the city fell to their knees, Adam cried with emotion.

“Ah… Aah…”

“Dearest Adam.”

It was Goffin, who would later become a god with a heart.

To win the war of the gods, he came to take the Gaians of Eden with him.

Adam, having once fought in Heaven, willingly accepted. But Lilith did not.

“No! Absolutely not!”

To change Goffin’s mind, she even abandoned Ultima. But in the end, the Gaians left Eden.

Left alone, she cried for three days and nights.

If a parent lost 16.8 billion children, they might understand how Lilith felt.

When even her tears had dried, she heard footsteps approaching.

“Darling.”

Adam was walking toward her.

“Why…?”

“He sent me back to look after you. He said this is where we’re meant to live…”

Lilith shot up and slapped Adam across the face.

“Why did you let them go?! Do you know how I raised them? How I built this city?!”

“You know why. We have to fight! Even if you gave up Ultima, have you forgotten what it means to be a Gaian?”

“Of course I know! I know how dangerous Heaven is, how powerful the gods are! The children… they’ll all die!”

Adam was heartbroken.

He understood what this conflict truly meant.

“So, you really did abandon Ultima,” Lilith said bitterly.

“No. You took it from me—my voice, our children, Ultima, and even my Gaian pride!”

Her once gentle face began to twist into something cold and cruel.

“I’m sick of it.”

Her inorganic hair, evolved for Heaven’s environment, snapped clean in the middle.

“What are you doing?!”

And the new hair growing in was made of organic fibers, like the creatures of Eden.

“Never again…”

With narrowed eyes, Lilith said,

“I won’t let anything be taken from me again.”

Her hatred for Gaians and obsession with preserving her species gave rise to reverse evolution.

She became the first Eve.

“She was beautiful,” Cain said with a smile.

“Though she had a nasty temper.”

Silence fell over the Hall of Evil. Iruki turned to Shirone and asked,

“Why does Mitochondrial Eve define good and evil? What did she even do?”

Shirone answered.

“Lilith gave up Ultima and became Mitochondrial Eve. She later had children with Adam, but the child wasn’t what Adam had hoped for.”

Iruki looked at Cain’s face.

“…He wasn’t a Gaian.”

“No. The ability hadn’t fully awakened. It was just a heart-attuned Gaian transforming her genetic code with deep desire. Of course, the marriage broke down. They clashed constantly, sometimes bitterly.”

Cain’s gaze darkened.

“Lilith resented Adam for treating her like a tool for reproduction. That resentment turned into hatred of men, and she was reportedly cruel even to her son. But the real issue was…”

Shirone paused.

“If a man and a woman are left alone on a planet, and they split apart—then who can they love?”

A crushing loneliness.

Shirone asked again.

“Who are they supposed to love?”

“Ahhhhh!”

Lilith committed bestiality.

She broke Gaia’s code and shared love with many of the planet’s species.

The witch of lust.

That’s what Adam called her before he finally couldn’t bear it anymore and left.

Lilith didn’t care.

‘I want to love. I want to be loved.’

The more she pursued pleasure, the more empty she felt, and the more she sought it—an endless cycle.

But even the witch of lust held to one rule.

“Mother.”

On a rare visit outside, she blankly watched her children hard at work.

“What brings you here today?”

Having not felt human warmth in over a hundred years, she was drying up from lack of love.

And then she saw Abel—tall, strong—and her throat tightened.

Haa…

Her body flushed with heat. She felt dizzy with the urge to leap on him.

No.

Lilith resisted with all her strength.

Perhaps it was the last bit of pride she had left as a fallen Gaian.

‘Beasts don’t matter. But not my child. If I ever take that step…’

There would be no coming back.

The moment that single taboo was broken, desire would be passed down through generations, leading all things to ruin.

Lost in thought, Lilith suddenly noticed her daughter, Lulua, kissing Abel.

‘...Is that so?’

It wasn’t particularly strange.

After all, when they first arrived on this planet, all Gaians were children of Adam and Lilith.

‘Yes… it was inevitable. But Lulua had been close with Cain since childhood…’

She turned her gaze and saw Cain, watching his younger sister with a sorrowful expression.

‘It can’t be helped,’ Lilith thought. Cain would have to let go.

‘Lulua and Abel are meant to be together. Forming a bond with the one you love—that is the right thing.’

No matter how far she had fallen, her rational Gaian logic still remained.

But unlike her—who had once transcended emotion—Cain cried himself to sleep every night.

“Why…”

Cain asked himself.

“Why Abel and not me?!”

He just wanted to love.

He just wanted to build a home and live happily forever with the sister he loved.

“I liked her first! Even before Abel—I loved her first!”

Why couldn't Lulua be two people?

Cain was tormented by the law of this world where, in order for Abel to be happy, he had to be miserable.

“Oh God…”

Cain cried out to the world.

“Why Abel?! What did I do wrong?! Why must I be the one to suffer?!”

And God… was silent.

“Damn it!”

If only Abel didn’t exist…

‘If only he’d never been born. If only that bastard didn’t exist in this world…’

Would Lulua have loved him instead?

And in that moment, Cain had a brilliant thought.

‘What if… Abel didn’t exist?’

His heart pounded with the same intensity as when he’d first fallen for Lulua.

Since their birth until now, not a single person had ever died.

At that same time, in a reeking room, Lilith stood before a mirror, gazing at her naked body.

“Am I… turning into a beast?”

The more she indulged in pleasure, the more her body transformed—dense, reactive, more stimulating.

Reverse evolution was still underway.

“Would… Abel like this body?”

‘You must be happy, Lulua…’

To be loved by both Cain and Abel—two strong, handsome men.

‘If I were my daughter…’

Lilith caught herself imagining something dizzying and spat a curse as she turned away coldly.

‘No.’

It has to end with me.

Instinct is passed down to future generations—it doesn’t run backward.

‘Maybe I should just die.’

That would’ve been better, Lilith—now known as Uorin—still thinks, reflecting on those days.

If only she’d known she would have to endure an eternity.

One day…

The sky was so clear and vast, the crack of thunder seemed to split the world apart.

It was a stormy night, with torrential rain.

While the brothers of Eden rested in their homes, one man walked alone in the downpour.

Cain was anxious.

‘Is this really okay?’

As if possessed, he had left his home—and even now, he knew he could turn back.

‘To kill… Abel?’

His heart trembled at the unknown ahead.

‘No. There's no way I could kill Abel. Why should I? It’s not like he’s food…’

At that time, death didn’t even exist. No one had ever died, so the concept of murder was unheard of.

‘It’s just like hunting.’

Lilith’s children had killed countless plants and animals for food.

If killing Abel was a sin, then being alive itself was a sin.

And that… was the original sin humanity would later carry.

‘I’ll be sad if Abel’s gone. He’s dependable and strong. The others will be upset too.’

Cain hesitated.

But the moment he leaned toward giving up on Lulua, the grip on the dagger in his hand tightened.

‘I can’t do it!’

He wanted to be happy with the one he loved—and he couldn’t bear to see her happy with someone else.

‘Yes, Abel just has to disappear. Life disappears all the time, doesn’t it?’

In the distance, he saw his brother’s house in the shadows—and an intense chill ran down his spine.

“Huuuuu…”

He wanted to scream.

‘Why do I feel so strange? If I want to love Lulua, I have no choice. The world isn’t going to make two of her. If our places were switched… Abel probably…’

Would he have tried to kill me?

Cain quickly shut out the thought and came to a stop in front of his brother’s door.

“A… Abel…”

Thunder rumbled through the heavens.

As his resolve began to waver, Cain clenched his fist and pounded on the door.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

Having pushed himself to the point of no return, all that remained was the judgment of fate.

‘Don’t come out, Abel.’

Maybe he didn’t hear.

‘Don’t come out. It was just thunder. Just go back to sleep like always…’

“What the…? Who’s knocking at this hour?”

And fate…

“Who is it? Oh? What are you doing here, Cain?”

…opened the door.
