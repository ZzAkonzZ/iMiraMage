Chapter 1176:

As Siena's sobs began to quiet, Kuan lost consciousness and collapsed into her arms.

"Kuan."

She checked his condition in alarm, but thankfully, his heart was still beating.

Shirone asked,

"Teacher, what happened?"

Siena, lost in thought, finally spoke.

"The Wind Guards were annihilated. And Kuan… I think he gave something up for me."

She turned to face Shirone.

"I can't go back to the Holy War like this. I want to take care of Kuan for now. Is that alright?"

"…Yes."

Shirone also felt that Kuan should no longer be on the battlefield.

"Don't worry too much. Kuan is strong. He'll come back."

Shirone didn’t know how, but Siena nodded.

"Yes. I will never give up. Kuan will come back to me."

The Sword Demon had left the battlefield.

Outside Delta Headquarters, the protests had been raging since morning.

"Step down, Gith!"

"Honor Shirone’s will! Even Yahweh condemns Gith’s corruption!"

Thanks to his name appearing in a news article, Shirone had quickly gained widespread recognition.

The voices of nearly six hundred people echoed through the gardens, reaching deep into Delta Headquarters.

"Haa…"

A man looking out the window of a neutral office let out a deep sigh.

His name was Raymond.

Gith’s political rival—the very man who had conspired with Arakne to set the trap.

‘Hearing the citizens cry out… it feels like my heart is being torn apart.

How am I supposed to change this rotten country?’

As he recalled the political mission he had set for himself, his eyes grew misty with emotion.

"Lord Raymond."

While his knights remained on guard, Arakne’s Prime Minister, Phedra, entered the room.

"Ah, you've arrived."

Raymond hastily wiped his tears, but it was impossible to fool Phedra’s eyes.

"This must be weighing on you."

"Indeed. So many people are worried about the future of Jive. I feel responsible for them. It’s hard to rest easy."

"Don’t think that way. Isn’t this all for a better world? You’re doing the right thing."

"…Hah."

As Raymond let out a sorrowful sigh, Phedra patted his shoulder and gestured toward the door.

"Come with me. There's nothing better than exercise for stress relief. Working up a sweat is the best remedy."

"Perhaps you're right."

Phedra led Raymond deep into Arakne's sector.

A strikingly beautiful woman greeted them.

"Welcome."

She was the leader of the Sensual Chamber, a key think tank of Arakne, specializing in seduction.

"Prepare a quiet room."

"As you wish."

With Raymond’s knights stationed outside the door, the two men stepped into a secluded chamber.

Inside, two women were bound to racks by their wrists and ankles.

"Today, I’m going to win," Phedra said, picking up a whip.

Raymond picked one up as well, running his fingers over the leather as if savoring the texture.

The game was called the Crying Game. The rules were simple.

Each man would take turns whipping the bound women.

Whoever forced their victim to scream first would be the winner.

Phedra struck first.

A sharp sound cut through the air, followed by the tear of flesh.

"Ughhh…!"

The woman stifled her scream.

The moment she cried out, far worse things would happen to her.

Raymond swung his whip.

"Gith!"

The woman's body arched like a bow.

"And you dare call yourself a king? You're running Jive into the ground! Do you have any idea how much the people are suffering? Do you?! The citizens are shedding tears of blood!"

When Phedra finished his turn, Raymond took his place again, his strike echoing with a sharp crack.

"Ooooh."

From the corner of the room, Havitz, who had been munching on snacks, widened his eyes in amusement.

"That was a good one."

He often dropped by when he was bored, treating these sessions like a spectator sport.

"A world of equality! Huh?! I’m the only one who can create a world where everyone is equal!"

Havitz’s gaze darkened into the abyss.

‘Would the world see this as hypocrisy?’

No.

‘No, this is humanity.’

Monsters of self-justification.

‘They fortify themselves with every conceivable logic. In the end, things like morality and conscience…’

To some, they may be absolute truths.

‘But in reality, they’re just another form of self-rationalization, a way to hide their weakness. The strong have no conscience.’

Humanity was simple.

‘If they have the power to do something, and it brings no harm to themselves, Then nothing is off limits.’

Raymond struck with all his strength.

"For Jive!"

The whip landed again on the already bleeding flesh, and this time, the woman's mouth opened wide.

"Aaaaaahhh!"

Phedra tossed his whip aside and clapped.

"Bravo! That might be a new record!"

"Hah… hah…"

Panting heavily, Raymond braced himself against the rack, a satisfied grin on his lips.

"Feeling better?" Phedra asked.

"Haah! Yes. Working up a sweat really does help. I had a great time, thanks."

"This is my specialty. You only need to say the word whenever you'd like to indulge. And this woman…?"

Phedra cut the ropes binding the screaming woman, letting her crumple to the floor like a corpse.

"She was due for disposal anyway. Take her with you. The Sensual Chamber will handle the cleanup."

"What a pity. Is there no way to spare her?"

"No. If exceptions are made, the game falls apart. There has to be a system, doesn’t there?"

"I suppose."

"Rest well tonight. We’ve set the perfect trap—Gith won’t escape. Jive will finally know peace… under the rule of King Raymond."

Raymond’s chest swelled with emotion.

"All thanks to you, Prime Minister. When I take the throne, I will never forget today."

"Enjoy yourself. Savor it."

At Phedra’s signal, the attendants of the Sensual Chamber dragged the woman away into another room.

With barely a farewell, Raymond quickly followed after them.

Phedra clicked his tongue.

‘Honestly…’

The human brain was like a child’s.

Once exposed to a certain level of pleasure, lesser pleasures ceased to satisfy.

‘They always crave something more extreme. And if they can do it without consequence…’

He didn’t dwell on it long.

"Well, that’s how we make a living, after all."

The women of the Sensual Chamber threw the blood-soaked woman onto a bed before leaving the room.

Raymond touched her wounds.

"Does it hurt?"

"Aaaah!"

"Of course, it does. My heart hurts, too. Just hold on a little longer. I'll make it all better soon—"

In that instant, the woman’s thumb drove deep into the left side of his neck.

"Gah! Gghhhk!"

Even in his shock, he tried to scream, but his vocal cords were completely blocked.

"Yeah. It hurts, doesn’t it, you bastard?"

As she sat up, she twisted her thumb deeper, completely reversing their positions.

"Kh…kkhh…!"

Even as he was pinned beneath her, Raymond noticed how sharp her nails were.

‘S-Someone…! My guards! Where are my guards?!’

"Oh my, you’re still squirming even with your gills shut tight!

I knew it—fresh ones are the best."

Licking her lips, she sank all ten fingers into Raymond’s neck, tearing him apart.

A sickening sound filled the air, and when she had finished ripping through his flesh, she snapped his spine.

"Ahh!"

Grabbing his severed head by the hair, she climbed off the bed just as a member of the Sensual Chamber entered.

"Is it done?"

Lifting Raymond’s head for her to see, the woman replied,

"What about the knights?"

"Roro and I handled them."

She pulled back her lips, revealing serrated, venomous fangs still smeared with flesh.

"It never ends with just the lips."

"Right. Let’s go."

Placing Raymond’s head into a wooden box, she shut the lid and walked out.

Phedra was waiting for them.

"It’s done."

When the lid was lifted, Raymond’s wide, terror-stricken eyes stared blankly at the ceiling.

"My, my, Lord Raymond."

Phedra leaned in close to the decapitated head.

"Why the scary face? Are you upset about dying? Hmm? Is that it? Does it feel unfair?"

He tapped Raymond’s cheek.

"Come on, say something. Do you not understand why you died? Do you want me to spell it out for you?"

"You were sloppy."

Phedra smirked and stepped back.

"You were Jive’s second-in-command, Gith’s right-hand man. Did you think that made you important? Did you think you were anything more than a stepping stone? You weren’t a king. You had nothing to offer me."

If Raymond had been willing to expose his own humiliation to his guards—

"That’s why you could never beat Gith."

Gith could.

The woman from the Sensual Chamber closed the box and asked,

"What about our payment?"

"Seventy percent was paid upfront. We hand over the head, we get the rest. That’ll be the end of our business."

"One percent goes to us."

One percent of the astronomical amount that Gith had to pay Arakne.

"Yeah, yeah. Quit whining."

From the start, Arakne never had any intention of making Raymond the King of Jive.

‘If you’re going to bleed a man dry, you do it properly.’

Even if votes mattered, a powerhouse like Jive would never truly submit to Arakne.

So instead, they had lured Gith into a trap—only to "rescue" him in exchange for a massive payment.

Phedra rubbed his fingers together.

"Big money.

That’s business.

It’s about time we wrapped this up. We’ve done enough work in the Holy War. Now it’s time to enjoy ourselves, don’t you think?"

The leader of the Sensual Chamber hesitated.

"What about Gith? Didn’t we go a little too far? What if this backfires on us?"

"He’ll be fine. We had to push him this hard for the threat to be real.

Raymond’s head is gone—he’ll figure out the rest on his own."

"What about Rangi?"

Phedra’s brow twitched.

"Tch. That sly bastard.

Leave her be. If she’s willing to give up her own position to side with Yahweh, it just means she wants to live that badly. She won’t do anything reckless."

Having heard enough, Havitz turned away.

‘Hmm. Rangi, huh?’

An hour had passed since he killed Gando.

It was time to pick a new target.

‘This is a bit tricky. If I mess with Yahweh directly, it'll be annoying. And if I go near Uorin, that goblin will be there…’

For once, he resented the frequency of divine law.

‘This is boring.’

In the past, he would have delighted in eavesdropping on Arakne’s political schemes.

But now?

It was all dull.

‘Is there even a greater pleasure out there?’

Just as he was wandering the halls, lost in thought,

a familiar voice called out from the corner.

"Havitz."

He stopped in his tracks and slowly turned.

Standing side by side were Zetaro and Smodo.

"You… can see me?"

He was still in Vanishing.

As he observed them staring into empty space, he shifted his gaze—

Wena Wizard was standing there.

"Oh?"

Vanishing unraveled, and Zetaro and Smodo finally registered Havitz in their minds.

"Havitz, it’s been a while."

"Has it? I wouldn’t know. Why are you here? Joining the assassination game?"

"No. I came to propose a game of my own."

"Hmm."

For Havitz, the fact that he wasn’t using divine frequency meant he wasn’t entirely disinterested.

"I suppose a game is the only way to kill me. But I’m already having plenty of fun."

More than that, he wanted to play with Wizard.

"How about you just attack me? I want to wrap this up quickly so I can move on to what I really want to do."

The fact that Havitz’s eyes were fixated on Wizard saddened Zetaro.

‘If I can’t convince him, there’s no chance.’

With that thought, he spoke.

"You’ll like it. I’d bet my life on it."

"Oh?"

"The game is called Truth and Lies."

Upon hearing the name, Havitz, with an excited thrill in his chest, activated divine frequency.

And then—

"Heh… hehehe… hahaha…!"

A grotesque grin split his face.
