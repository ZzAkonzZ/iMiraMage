Chapter 877: Obituary (3)

After the grueling four-hour training session, the students were exhausted.

Among those gasping for breath were Mark and Maria, who were close to Shirone.

"It's been a while."

As Shirone approached with a smile, Mark forgot his fatigue and stood up quickly.

"Senior! I never thought you'd actually come."

Although their time together was short, Mark and Maria had also taken the graduation exam with Pisho.

"Yeah. You heard too, huh?"

"Yes. I didn't show it, but I was watching my friends' reactions," Mark said, blushing as he remembered.

"I'm sorry, senior. I wanted to catch up quickly, but I'm still in the graduation class."

It's not something that can be achieved with just enthusiasm.

Shirone, having already graduated, could only marvel at how he managed to get through it back then.

"It's okay. You're making progress."

Mark took pride in that.

"Of course. I'll never give up."

As the students left for lunch, Shirone's group talked with the teachers in the staff room.

"Huh? Where's Teacher Etella?" She wasn't seen during class, and even her desk was missing from the staff room.

"Teacher Etella..."

Siena chose her words carefully.

"She left the school."

"Left? Where to?"

"Well..."

Siena struggled to find the right words to explain to Shirone.

Alpheas changed the subject.

"So, where are you planning to go now? There are three days until Pisho's funeral, so you should visit home."

"My parents are staying with the Ozente family. I'll be staying there too."

As he spoke, Shirone realized something.

'Rian isn't here.'

There was no one to defend Gai's death, and even his parents were there.

'Maybe it's for the best.'

To regain his composure, he couldn't hide behind someone else.

Shirone's group left Alpheas Magic School after a brief meeting.

They decided to ask about Etella at the funeral.

In the noble district, Amy asked,

"I'm going to my family home. Maya, do you want to come with me? You don't have a place to stay, right?"

"It's okay. I can stay at a hotel." Shirone insisted.

"No. You're a celebrity. And you don't have your agency people with you."

Palmers had requested a separate military transport and was coming down a different route.

"Come with us. Is it uncomfortable being with me?"

"No, absolutely not!" Maya jumped in denial, but it was true that it wasn't the most comfortable situation.

"Then how about going to Ozente?"

"No, that's..."

Maya hesitated more than before.

"Just let me stay at a hotel. I'll be fine in a good place without needing security."

Shirone thought it was best for Maya to stay where she felt most comfortable.

"Alright. See you in three days."

"Yes. Take care, everyone."

Maya, considering Amy's feelings, left first for the lodging area.

"Sigh."

Parting with Shirone was sad, but she felt relieved.

'How long will this go on?'

Both Shirone and Amy were too kind to hurt others, making her position in the middle even more miserable.

'Should I meet someone else?'

She had heard countless times that the wounds of trials should be forgotten with new people.

But if it were that easy, no one would suffer from the pains of love.

'A man better than Shirone...'

Nothing came to mind, and Maya shook her head in frustration.

'I don't know. Thinking like this before a friend's funeral is wrong.'

It was a luxury hotel, but not very tall, only four stories, due to the population size.

Maya's popularity in Tormia was much higher than in other countries, so noble youths recognized her.

"Hey, look! Isn't that her?"

"Yeah, I think so! I've never seen her this close. What's she doing in Creas?"

"Looks like she's alone."

As Maya, who had rented a room on the fourth floor, climbed the central staircase, the youths exchanged glances.

"Should we ask her to hang out?" They had nothing to lose.

In the Cross family's training room, a narrow stone chamber barely big enough for a desk, Kaiden sat cross-legged.

"The fate of the Red Cross Star."

After leaving the magic school and returning home, he spent all his time in the training room.

"Kaiden, it's time to go."

Kaiden's father opened the door and sighed as he looked at the portraits of Maya covering the walls.

'How long will this go on...'

At first, he asked who she was, but Kaiden never answered.

Over time, he learned that the person in the portraits was Maya, a famous singer.

'At his age, obsessed with an entertainer.'

Kaiden spoke.

"I'll leave in an hour."

"At least don't be late for your classmate's funeral. How long will you live like this?"

The world was in turmoil.

Even though it was time for the Cross family, a prominent lineage in Tormia, to step up, the heir was obsessed with a singer.

"Say something. You didn't say a word when you left the magic school. And now this? You don't even train, just stay here drawing every day."

"Training is unnecessary, Father."

Given Kaiden's reputation, his father asked with hopeful eyes.

"...Have you had some kind of enlightenment?"

"Enlightenment is unnecessary."

"Then what is it? What have you been doing all this time, locked up here?"

"I'm accepting it."

As golden energy emanated from Kaiden, his father stepped back.

"This, this is..."

The Red Cross Star of Sacrifice.

From the beginning, what Kaiden needed wasn't intense training or supreme enlightenment.

"I'm accepting my fate."

Giving up on Maya.

If he could do that, the Red Cross Star would elevate Kaiden to the strongest being ever.

Arriving at the Ozente family, Shirone burst into tears upon reuniting with his parents.

Family head Biscoff and Reina, who had just returned home, watched the scene with satisfaction.

But the emotion was brief, and Shirone's face turned cold as he moved away from his parents.

"I have something to say."

"Yes. We have many things we want to hear too." Biscoff offered him a seat, but Shirone knelt on the cold floor.

Reina stood up again.

"Shirone? What are you doing? Why are you like this?"

"Please... listen to me."

Reina sat back down, sensing the seriousness in Shirone's tone, and the story began.

In a calm but honest manner, he recounted everything.

From the first time he met Gai to the incident where Yahweh's rage, which he had no memory of, was unleashed.

"Gai... brother..."

Gai was dead.

Vincent and Olina buried their faces in their hands, and Reina's knees buckled as she staggered.

"Brother... brother..."

Unable to hold back, Reina burst into tears.

Even in the pain that felt like a dagger to the heart, Shirone only looked down at the ground.

"I killed Gai."

As Shirone reiterated the fact, Biscoff spoke for the first time.

His voice was filled with barely concealed anger.

"Does Rian know?"

"Yes."

"What did he say?"

"It has nothing to do with Rian."

Shirone was firm, and Biscoff could easily guess Rian's reaction.

'It's not Shirone's fault.'

Everyone would think so, but from Biscoff's perspective, his son was dead.

"Gai betrayed humanity. He disgraced the family. No one in the world will blame you."

"That's why I came here."

Biscoff stood up.

"Of course. You are my son's enemy. So... what will you do now?"

Reina, who had been sobbing, widened her eyes as she saw Biscoff draw his sword.

"Father, what are you doing?" As the blade approached Shirone, Vincent and Olina turned pale.

"Master..."

Vincent spoke in a trembling voice, but Biscoff's murderous intent didn't waver.

"Why? My child is dead, and Vincent's child should be forgiven?"

"That, that's..."

Vincent fell silent.

If it had been Shirone who died instead of Gai, could he have forgiven the Ozente family?

"Let me ask you one thing." Shirone, with the sword at his neck, raised his head.

"Yes, go ahead."

"Was there really no other way? I know how strong you are. Are you sure there was no other choice but to kill him?"

"No."

Biscoff's face twisted.

"Choose your words carefully."

Shirone thought again.

"I've agonized over it countless times. The answer is the same. If I had judged better, Gai wouldn't have died."

"Sigh."

Biscoff sheathed his sword and returned to his seat.

"Why don't you make excuses? You know I need a reason to forgive you. Do you want to be an enemy? Even if you do, we can't kill you."

"I don't know."

Shirone spoke honestly.

"I don't know what to do. I just thought I might find an answer here."

It wasn't an answer he could find on his own.

"Go."

Biscoff said.

"Even if I forgive you, nothing will change. This is a matter of emotion. And emotions need time."

He could understand.

No, the fact that Biscoff could suppress his emotions this much made him a superhuman.

"There is one condition."

The spirit of the great sword was not cowardly, so Shirone nodded obediently.

"Yes. I'll do anything."

"Forget it. I permit it. At least until this war is over, don't dwell on Gai."

"Master..."

"It's not for you. It's because you need to show that my son's death was not in vain." Tears streamed down Shirone's cheeks.

"Thank you. Thank you."

He finally understood why he thought the Ozente family might have the answer.

There was dignity here.

"Then I'll be going."

As Shirone prepared to leave, Vincent and Olina stood up to follow.

"Master, then we will also..." Reina stepped in.

"Dad, you can't just send him away like that. It would be difficult for Shirone's parents too."

Not accepting Shirone was a form of punishment, but there was no need to complicate matters.

Biscoff, staring at Reina, made up his mind and stood up.

"Alright, I'll go out. I need to inform others about Gai's death too. Shirone, come with me."

Reina asked,

"You're both going out? Together?" Ignoring his daughter's words, Biscoff looked at Shirone and said,

"Do you drink?"
