Chapter 269: Terraje’s Bloodline (2)

Although permission was granted more easily than expected, Shirone had anticipated this situation from the start.

The haves live freely. It was the same when the Ogent Family risked their lives to move the Great Library.

The butlers meticulously categorized the books according to the rules, but the nobles didn’t even care whether the books were lost or not.

Eliza hugged Shirone and kept repeating something, but lost in thought, none of her words remained in him.

Suddenly, Eliza seemed to remember something and abruptly stood up.

“This isn’t the time for this. I can’t have my first meal with my son dressed like this. Dear, I’m going to change my clothes.”

"Do as you wish."

Leaving Shirone behind, Eliza walked to the dressing room, followed by a line of female attendants.

Shirone didn't think clothing should matter that much when it came to having a meal. But just as there’s a dress code at noble parties, it seemed that choosing the right attire was just as important to royalty as finding her son after 18 years.

With Eliza gone, only Orkamph and Shirone were left. An awkward silence hung between them. Whether Orkamph had intended it from the start or simply couldn’t bear the awkwardness any longer, he spoke for the first time.

“Shirone… was it?”

“Yes, it’s the name my parents gave me.”

Shirone emphasized that it was a name given by his parents. Orkamph might have had a name for him too, but Shirone didn’t want to abandon the name he had been given.

“I see. It’s a good name.”

Contrary to his expectations, Orkamph didn't say much.

It occurred to him that perhaps there had never been a name given to him in the first place. If that were true, it meant his parents had decided to abandon him even while he was still in his mother’s womb.

“Do you resent me?”

“I did when I was young. But now…”

Shirone swallowed the words he was about to say. It felt too rude to say that he was actually glad to have been born as Vincent’s son.

Using his unique insight, Orkamph read Shirone’s emotions and gave a bitter smile.

“I suppose you don’t anymore.”

"That’s…."

“I understand. Royalty may seem glamorous, but it's not all that great. There are many responsibilities, and much that must be sacrificed.”

“Much like how you sacrificed me.”

Orkamph was momentarily taken aback by Shirone's sharp remark, but soon returned to his calm expression.

“I’m sorry. It wasn’t that I didn’t want to find you…”

Shirone shook his head, cutting off Orkamph's words.

He didn’t want to hear it.

No, deep down, he wanted to hear it. But he was certain that whatever Orkamph was going to say wouldn’t be the answer he longed for. He was sure of it.

“It’s fine. You’ve found me now, haven’t you?”

Orkamph felt Sirone's extraordinary nature. Despite living as a commoner for 18 years, there was not a trace of unease even in the foreign royal palace. Furthermore, he possessed the mental fortitude to assert his demands even before the king and queen.

‘I see…. Blood is truly thicker than water, after all?’

Having reached this conclusion, Orkamph turned toward his study to proceed to the next step. Then, realizing that Shirone would be left alone, he turned back and asked,

"Dinner is in 30 minutes. Would you prefer to stay here?"

Shirone shook his head. He felt that if he stayed in this room even a minute longer, he’d suffocate.

"No, I’ll step out. Is it alright if I take a look around the palace?"

This request was driven by pure curiosity, without any ulterior motive. For an ordinary commoner—or even a noble—to be invited to the royal palace was an extraordinary honor. Setting aside the matter of prestige, it was a rare opportunity to explore the palace he had been curious about since childhood.

Orkamph readily agreed.

“Of course. You may explore anywhere you wish. After all, this will soon be your home.”

Shirone had every intention of returning to the magic academy. However, he felt it wasn’t the right time to bring that up, so he simply expressed his gratitude for Orkamph's hospitality and left the room.

Walking down the corridor with a sense of excitement, Shirone wondered where to start. The armory, bakery, and chapel were all appealing options, but what intrigued him the most was the gallery, where various artworks were displayed.

As he turned a corner in the corridor, he saw an elderly official standing there. Shirone approached him to ask for directions, but before he could say anything, the official bowed first.

"Sir Shirone, there is someone who wishes to meet you."

Shirone felt his mood sour as soon as he heard the official's words.

Normally, he would have followed without any complaint, but he was already feeling drained by the royal family's arrogant and self-centered attitude, so this situation didn't sit well with him.

“Later. I want to be alone right now. His Majesty said I could go anywhere I want until dinner.”

He didn’t know the rank of the official, but Shirone openly showed his displeasure. He had already spoken his mind in front of the king, so there was no reason to be concerned about the opinion of a mere official.

However, the unknown official flashed a sly smile, as if confident that Shirone would ultimately have no choice but to comply.

"Of course, that's fine as well, but wouldn’t it be better for you to meet this person?"

Sensing an unsettling tone in the official's smile, Shirone suppressed his irritation and regained his composure. This was unmistakably coercion, which made him even more curious. There wasn’t much time left until mealtime—what could be so urgent that this person needed to see him now?

"And who is this person?"

A sharp glint flashed in the official’s eyes.

“It’s His Highness Terraje Zion, the current First Prince of Kazra. At least, for now.”

The official placed deliberate emphasis on "for now." It implied that the position of the First Prince might soon change hands, but for some reason, the tone seemed to suggest the opposite.

Hearing the name Zion made Shirone more cautious. The group he had to be most wary of in the royal palace was undoubtedly Terraje's faction. And now, a key figure from that faction had initiated contact.

Shirone thought it would be best to meet him sooner rather than later to gauge what kind of person he was. Understanding his nature would help Shirone roughly anticipate how they might act in the future. Zion had likely sent the official with the same thought in mind.

“Alright, I’ll meet him.”

“A wise decision, sir. Please, this way.”

The official led Shirone toward the eastern wing of the inner quarters.

The place where the royal family resided was smaller in scale than the part where Orkamph stayed, but it was still sufficiently luxurious.

In the hall that marked the division of the different wings stood a towering lion statue that reached the ceiling, and from there, four paths stretched out in the directions of east, west, south, and north.

After walking about 20 meters to the east, Shirone was met with a door adorned in gold. When the official opened it from the outside, an artificial garden with a floor of white marble awaited.

Before entering, Shirone took a moment to observe the interior.

The garden was beautiful, but it was also the perfect place for someone to hatch a scheme. Despite it being winter, palm trees evoking a tropical feel were planted throughout.

At a table in the center, a younger pair of siblings were enjoying tea, while a sleek black cat and a fluffy white cat played around on the floor.

“Sir Zion, I have brought Sir Shirone.”

Zion was watching the cats play, but upon hearing the official’s words, he simply raised his head slightly without even shifting his gaze.

“Let him in.”

With the command given, the official flashed a sly smile and gestured for Shirone to proceed.

Raising his chin with determination, Shirone walked forward with a straight posture.

Being underestimated from the start would render any strategy useless. The sound of the mysterious official closing the door behind him felt strangely eerie.

As Shirone approached the table, he disregarded manners and scrutinized the faces of the siblings with an intense gaze.

‘So, these are...’

Terraje Zion and Terraje Uorin.

They were the current First Prince and Princess of Kazura.

Of course, they were Orkamph's children. However, their surname was that of their mother, Terraje. The reason was simple: in the power game of political marriage, Orkamph had lost. It was widely known as Orkamph's humiliation.

Zion finally turned his head to look up at Shirone. Then, as if finding Shirone's serious expression amusing, he joked lightheartedly.

“Haha! Don’t be so tense. I’m not going to eat you.”

Shirone didn’t know what the Empress Terraje of Kashan looked like, but by stripping away the features of Orkamph from Zion's face, he could vaguely deduce the Empress's appearance.

Zion was a beautiful young man with silver hair that had a cold tone, deep double eyelids, and the elegant curve of a sharp, cat-like chin. This alone made it easy to imagine that Terraje’s beauty must be beyond extraordinary.

Uorin welcomed Shirone with a smile.

“Hello, I’m Uorin. Broter, you’re that magician, right? I saw you in that magazine.”

The white cat jumped onto Uorin's lap. Shirone couldn’t take his eyes off the fourteen-year-old girl, who held the cat and gently stroked its fur.

If Zion’s appearance gave Shirone a sense of what Terraje might look like, Uorin’s presence made him feel as if Terraje herself were standing before him.

She resembled Terraje far more than Zion did. No, to be more precise, it was as if there was hardly any trace of Orkamph’s blood in her.

She was a beautiful young girl, possessing an appearance that went beyond beauty and exuded an almost spiritual, mysterious aura.

Shirone had experienced this sensation once before—when he first encountered Ikael in Heaven. He never imagined he would feel that same awe toward a human.

Uorin tilted her head slightly and glanced up at Shirone with a timid, anxious look in her eyes.

“W- Why are you looking at me like that? We’ll be family in just a few days, you know. That kind of stare makes me uncomfortable.”

Shirone's face flushed bright red.

“No, I didn’t mean it that way….”

As if nothing had happened, Uorin clutched her belly and burst into laughter.

“Hohoho! Just kidding, just kidding! I heard you’re 18, but you’re so innocent. Come on, sit over here.”

Shirone composed himself and took the seat Uorin offered. He nearly lost the initiative, but this was only the beginning.

As the attendants brought out tea, the conversation started in earnest.

Contrary to the intense psychological battle Shirone had expected, Zion seemed optimistic about the current situation.

It was easy for him to be so, given that he was looking down on Shirone.

“You’re not an idiot, so you must be aware of the state of affairs in the royal palace. You also understand the weight of the name Terraje. I called you here to settle things cleanly. I don’t know what father is thinking, but you’ll never be king anyway. So, it’d be wise not to walk around with your head held too high.”

Pushing the first prince candidate to give up so blatantly was practically an official declaration that he was aiming for the throne himself.

Shirone, having thoroughly studied history, was well-versed in how the bloody battles for the throne within royal families played out.

Yet, this directness from Zion was something Shirone appreciated. After all, he had no interest in royal authority from the beginning.

“Don’t worry. I only came because I was invited. Once this is all over, I’ll go back. I have no intention of living here, and I certainly have no desire to become the king.”

“Hahaha! You speak well. People who say things like that usually end up stabbing you in the back, don’t they? Just like the typical deceitful mage. Oh, wait! You’re not even a real magician yet, are you? Just an aspiring one.”

Uorin scolded Zion.

“Brother, don’t be so harsh. Shirone had a tough childhood. It’s amazing that he managed to enter the magic academy as a commoner. And now, he’s considered one of the most promising talents.”

Uorin, unlike Zion, was favorable towards Shirone.

However, from Shirone’s perspective, her apparent kindness felt even more dangerous than Zion's blatant hostility.

Uorin was also Terraje’s daughter. She had absolutely no reason to be kind to him.

With a beautiful smile, Uorin spoke.

“Brother Shirone seems to still be wary of us. It’s all because of brother Zion. Ah, is it okay if I call you brother?”

Shirone couldn’t quite adjust to the atmosphere here.

The Terraje siblings had completely opposite personalities, like going back and forth between heaven and hell. Trying to keep up with both at once was mentally exhausting.

“Well, nothing’s set in stone yet. But I suppose it's fine?”

“Ei, what does it matter? I’ll just call you brother then. You’re older than me anyway. By the way, brother, can you use magic?”

“Magic? Uh, just the basics.”

Uorin clapped her hands in delight.

“Wow! That’s awesome! You have to show me some time! Magic is always so fascinating. How can fire come out of your hands like that?”

Shirone was increasingly engulfed by an odd feeling.

There wasn’t a hint of any strategic undertone in Uorin’s behavior. He even began to wonder if she was simply a kind and cheerful girl.

But a mountain village girl she was not; as the daughter of the Empress, Terraje, she had to be sharper than most people.

Then, Shirone suddenly realized what Zion and Uorin had in common.

They weren’t afraid of anything.

Zion’s overt hostility and Uorin’s overly friendly demeanor were only possible because they had the immense power of Terraje backing them.
