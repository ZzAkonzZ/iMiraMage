Chapter 405:

"Move aside. You're still hopeless with machines."

Sein pushed Gaold away from the control panel and inspected it himself.

Seeing the former head of the Magic Association being pushed aside was a very unfamiliar sight for Shirone.

But Shirone's eyes soon filled with a sense of nostalgia.

'Ah, I see.'

Nade and Iruki.

If Shirone were to end up in a situation like Miro's, would those two look like this after decades?

Gaold, Miro, and Sein, the founding members of the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Society, who were caught in the center of the most horrific incident in the world.

Even after 20 years, their paths had completely diverged, but their time was still frozen.

Gaold flipped his middle finger at the back of Sein's head.

Even an arch mage recognized by the world couldn't help but act childish when meeting an old classmate.

Unable to let go of his attachment, Gaold kept glancing over Sein's shoulder at the control panel.

But to him, machines were just mysterious objects that worked when you stuck pieces of metal together and chanted spells.

Then, suddenly, he had a mischievous thought and laughed wickedly.

"Hehe, you know that using mental magic on a national intelligence agent is a capital offense, right? If you want to live long, you better hope I get reinstated."

Sein quickly manipulated the control panel and replied.

"I don't care about Red Line laws, you idiot. Once they're under mind control, they won't remember anything that happened here. You seem pretty anxious. Should I just bury you alive?"

Etella frowned.

As a bishop of the Karsis Monastery, it was natural for her to be unable to tolerate a Black Line mage who muddied the world.

But she didn't take any action. She trusted Alpheas.

Even if it was due to a cherished student's earnest request, he wasn't someone who would ask for a mission that broke the Karsis Monastery's beliefs.

'He must have a reason.'

When she heard about Heaven, the first thought that came to her mind was that she shouldn't judge anything.

It wasn't a matter light enough to be evaluated by personal beliefs.

The only thing driving her was the mission to see with her own eyes the full story of the great flow that had likely started 20 years ago, or even much earlier.

Sein entered Istas' master mode.

As green letters flashed on the panel, he quickly input information by combining several symbols.

Siena, who had been waiting patiently, couldn't suppress her curiosity and asked.

"But does the upper floor of Istas really exist? Even the intelligence agents from the National Intelligence Service couldn't find anything and left."

"The upper floor exists. There's just no way to enter it. It was designed that way from the start. Even I, who analyzed all of Istas' patterns, can't access it."

Shirone looked around at the scattered warehouses.

He wondered if a room that couldn't be accessed could exist when all the warehouses were already individually dismantled.

"How is that possible? We can enter all the warehouses now. That's why Mr. Sein created the master key for Istas."

For the first time since arriving, Sein observed Shirone closely.

He knew Shirone was part of the project since Gaold had introduced him with special attention, but he still seemed like a soft rookie.

"How do you know about the master key?"

"I'm a member of the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Society. One of my friends is a savant, and he said that no one could create a more beautiful master equation for Istas."

Sein didn't smile, but his explanation became more detailed.

"That's probably true. It's an equation that equates a complex four-dimensional diagram to three dimensions."

Mathematically eliminating one dimension is comparable to making an ant understand human language. That's why anyone with the master equation can find the research society.

It's a map that takes the form of three dimensions but actually guides you to four-dimensional coordinates.

"The upper floor isn't limited to space, is it?"

Sein nodded.

Shirone's insight was impressive, but if he couldn't grasp this much, he wouldn't qualify to join Gaold's project in the first place.

"Right. In three dimensions, Istas is just a warehouse made up of 89 buildings. But the upper floor exists in a higher dimension. That's why the agents couldn't find it."

"So, in addition to a specific space, a specific time must align?"

"Exactly. Take the 333 cube as an example. When you move the cube at coordinates 1.1.1 to coordinates 3.1.3, what changes is not just space but also time. No matter how fast you turn it, it takes at least 0.1 seconds. This is called spacetime. I devised a way to prevent anyone from accessing Miro by hiding her spacetime at the origin of the cube."

Everyone was now listening intently to Sein.

Even Alpheas, who had once been Sein's teacher, was now in a position to learn after 20 years.

"No matter how you turn the cube, that is, no matter how you change Istas' pattern, the spacetime origin of the cube never moves, and thus the upper floor can be perfectly concealed. The master equation you're using for Istas was created to calculate this."

Shirone swallowed hard.

He hadn't expected such a serious reason behind the master equation, which he had thought was just a playful trick of the underground research society.

Siena spoke up.

"But didn't we gather here today to go to the upper floor? If no one can enter, what are we waiting for here?"

"Another master key."

As if that word was a keyword, there was a crackling sound of electricity in the open space. It was the familiar noise of flicker magic to Shirone.

A pair of man and woman appeared through the trademark magic of a scale mage.

The woman was small and cute with a permed bob, and the man was tall with long blonde hair down to his waist.

The important thing was that both of them were familiar to Shirone. Probably.

If there couldn't be two identical people in the world, the blonde man with bandaged eyes couldn't be anyone else.

The eternal contemplator, Armin of the Radiant Eyes, was walking towards them.

"Oh, brother?"

Siena couldn't believe what she was seeing.

Why was Armin, who should be wandering the world capturing landscapes on canvas, at the center of humanity's worst crisis?

"Is it really you, Armin?"

Siena approached, but Armin coldly ignored her and kept walking.

His wife, Keira, also didn't spare a glance and headed towards Istas.

Siena seemed to be in shock, standing still. Her mind was tangled, and she couldn't think of anything.

"You arrived at the exact time. Well, it would be rude to say that to the world's greatest time mage."

The world's greatest time mage.

A mage who had conquered the speed of light, so the title didn't seem strange to Shirone. But for Siena, who knew nothing, it only deepened her confusion.

"A time mage? What are you talking about, brother?"

Armin remained silent to the end.

Sein, judging that confusion was not good before the big event, introduced Armin. It was ironic for Siena, who thought she knew Armin best.

"To reach the upper floor of Istas, we need the help of a powerful time mage. Armin of the Radiant Eyes is the perfect candidate."

Siena shook her head.

The more she heard, the more confused she became.

"Keira, what is going on? Are you joking?"

Keira, who always laughed cheerfully and sometimes made annoying remarks, didn't laugh this time.

She lowered her eyes coldly and spoke in a high-handed tone.

"Watch your words, Olifer Siena. We are mages of the Ivory Tower's Human Safety Enforcement Division, Armin and Keira, affiliated with the White Line."

"White Line..."

Even Shirone, who knew Armin's identity, was surprised this time.

If the association was in the Red Line and factions in the Black Line, the White Line was a completely independent institution that converged only on the Ivory Tower.

Magic that isn't misused in the world.

Purity that isn't swayed by the ruler's beliefs.

The mages of the Ivory Tower, whose motto was these two principles, pursued the extremes of magic purely.

Their power, known to be on par with an archmage of a nation, was not something a single kingdom could challenge.

When they presented their Ivory Tower credentials, even royalty trembled before the absolute rulers of the magical society.

But Armin was here as a member of the Ivory Tower.

And as a mage of the Human Safety Enforcement Division.

While the Ivory Tower mages didn't differentiate between departments, the Red Line paid the most attention to the Human Safety Enforcement Division, which had some connection to society.

In fact, 32 years ago, when Igor, the dictator of the Kon Kingdom, dabbled in necromancy, a mage from the Human Safety Enforcement Division entered alone, made him kneel, and crawl on the ground, which became a famous story.

Keira approached Gaold and got in his face.

She had come reluctantly because of Armin's stubbornness, but now that they were here, she wanted to make the most of it.

Armin was someone far removed from practicality.

"Since we brought Armin, you must keep your promise. First, the Ivory Tower has the right to record and disseminate information obtained from Miro's spacetime. Second, Olifer Siena is prohibited from going to Heaven. You know Armin came here because of the second condition, right?"

"Of course. But let's make one thing clear. It's only if Siena agrees. I really hate persuading people, especially women."

"Well, it's fine. Armin knows that too. But later, you have to tell him that I definitely emphasized the second condition to you."

"Hehe, you really have a tough life."

Keira frowned as if to say, "What's the point of talking?"

Stubborn Armin.

In fact, the Ivory Tower still hasn't made a definitive judgment about Heaven. They believe it's still a problem that human society can solve on its own.

But Armin fell for Gaold's trick.

To open the upper floor of Istas, a top-level time mage is needed, and Gaold, who wanted to reclaim Miro, couldn't be unaware of his existence.

The result of investigating Armin's whereabouts was the same reason the Ivory Tower could hold onto Armin.

Olifer Siena.

A woman so precious to Armin that he would trade his life for her.

That's why Keira disliked Siena even more.

'Che, does he like that woman so much? A woman with no charm at all.'

When Sein released the mind control, the ten agents fell to the ground like puppets with their strings cut. This was to ensure there were no variables that could interfere with the operation.

"Let's finish this before the agents regain consciousness. I'm going in now."

With those words, Sein pressed a button on the control panel.

The 89 warehouses began to interlock in a specific pattern.

At this moment, even Shirone put aside his thoughts about Armin and focused on Istas.

A time mage from the Ivory Tower's White Line, a first-class grand mage from the Red Line, and the top mental mage from the Black Line.

In the presence of three grand mages who had reached the pinnacle of their respective fields, the upper floor of Istas was about to be opened for the first time in 20 years.
