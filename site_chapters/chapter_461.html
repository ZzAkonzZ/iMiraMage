Chapter 461:

Ymir's body resided deep underground in Helheim.

Far below the icy sea, where even light couldn't reach, Suoi, the bioluminescent creatures that coexisted with Ymir, floated around, emitting faint light.

Suoi, like other creatures in Helheim, had the properties of an incorporeal entity, freely moving between solid molecules and transmitting all the world's information to Ymir through quantum communication.

The only place in Heaven that could confine Ymir's body was the eternal ice of Helheim.

But Ymir didn't think so.

No physical force could confine him.

If it could be destroyed, he could destroy it.

His law, the law of war, was the vanguard, and he knew it wasn't time yet.

Suoi drifted away from Ymir's eyes, displaying various images on their translucent bodies.

Ymir's eyes remained indifferent to the affairs of Heaven, the outside world, and even distant worlds.

Then, dozens of Suoi overlapped, showing a scene of a young man, now a youth, wielding a great sword and fighting many opponents.

Suoi's information was just information.

Ymir trusted his judgment, which was never wrong.

‘Not yet.’

One corner of Ymir's mouth lifted.

Crack! Crack! Crack!

Just that caused a massive 2-kilometer crack in the abyssal ice wall.

‘Not yet.’

Ymir simply waited.

Cariel was busy preparing to annihilate humanity.

Uriel, watching him fly around the many devices of the Great World War, turned his gaze to Miro, who was bound beneath the Milky Way mirror.

Accompanied by Arius, who had received a holy baptism from her, Miro met Uriel's gaze and smiled faintly.

At that moment, Uriel realized.

Despite being the detestable human who had opened Miro's dimension and humiliated the angels, his anger didn't turn to hatred because he held a reverence for her.

It wasn't confidence or arrogance. It was the absence of worry because she had no heart.

Perhaps it was the ultimate state of mind a human could reach, the state of emptiness.

‘This is the first time I've felt curiosity about a human.’

Uriel asked,

“What are you thinking?”

“Thinking? What kind of thinking?”

Miro, resting her chin on her hand, asked back.

“All thoughts. I'm curious about the thoughts flowing through your mind right now.”

“Hmm.”

Miro removed her finger from her chin and pointed at Uriel.

“You're handsome. Do you have a lover?”

Miro's gaze swept over the Great World War.

“For an angel's device, it's too crude. The aesthetics are sloppy. What's that foolish archangel so busy with? I'm hungry in reality, but there's no food?”

Miro listed her immediate thoughts and smiled bashfully.

“Maybe if I ask to be released, you'll be charmed by my beauty and let me go?”

Uriel landed in front of Miro.

A 4-meter-long staff of light formed and spun rapidly.

Heavenly Galaxy Wheel.

Whoooosh!

As the terrifying sound of the spinning staff approached, Cariel turned in surprise before Miro did.

When the spinning stopped, a sharp noise pierced the air, making everyone's ears ring.

Uriel, stopping the staff next to Miro's temple, spoke sternly.

“Know your place. Making me angry won't end well for you.”

“But…”

Still smiling, Miro half-closed her eyes as if sleepy and asked,

“Why did your heart flutter?”

“…”

Angels didn't have hearts, and Uriel didn't even know what a fluttering heart meant.

Yet he couldn't refute her because the more he tried to shake her, the more he felt shaken himself.

Uriel retracted the staff and flew to Cariel. Now, he too wished for Miro's quick demise.

Cariel, watching the central processing unit's screen, said,

“I told you, provoking her wouldn't end well. She's the one who humiliated Heaven's army.”

Uriel had to agree.

How many humans could infiltrate the noble mind of an angel and cause a stir?

Cariel pointed to his forces and asked,

“So, how's your side?”

“First, second, and third divisions are all intact. As are the ordinary angels under my command.”

“I see.”

While Cariel smiled in satisfaction, Uriel glanced back at Miro with unease.

What was she thinking?

“Cariel.”

“Hmm?”

Uriel, still looking at Miro, asked,

“What does 'heart flutter' mean?”

After her audience with Anke Ra, Ikael descended in the elevator of light.

She no longer needed to be confined to her penance room.

Seeing the outside world with her eyes for the first time in a long while, her gaze softened.

The world was beautiful because everything came from it.

She might have forgotten something. But having already forgotten, it wasn't painful.

A sense of relief.

All her sins were erased.

And that was directly connected to the authority of the Archangel, which had once made all creatures tremble.

As she admired the scenery with a beautiful smile, her expression gradually disappeared.

Regaining the dignity of the Archangel, she looked straight ahead and spoke briefly.

“Ashur.”

Clang!

Dozens of panels embedded themselves in front of Ikael, and Ashur appeared.

With Ikael's powers restored, his abilities had grown immensely.

“Your servant responds to your call.”

Ashur's voice was filled with power, and his eyes gleamed sharply.

Being the third Mara of Archangel Ikael filled him with pride.

“Where is Cariel?”

“As feared, he has convened the White Hall and is gathering the forces of the other archangels. In 24 hours, they will likely depart for Purgatory.”

“Relay what you know.”

For Ashur, who controlled signals, transmitting information was faster than speaking.

But it was such an honor that he felt fear.

“Ikael, I…”

“There is no time. I permit mental fusion.”

Ashur bowed with a grateful expression.

Numerous panels unfolded and merged into Ikael's halo.

The information Ashur had gathered quickly flashed through her mind, revealing all the facts.

Ikael lowered her gaze.

Deciding where to start wasn't difficult.

“Phew.”

Ikael's eyelids lowered slightly, and she inhaled deeply.

The next moment, her eyes snapped open, and she roared in anger.

Ca~ri~el~!

“Grrr!”

Ashur, nearby, gritted his teeth and suppressed his fear.

Ikael's voice, amplified immensely, spread from the center of Heaven, Araboth, in concentric circles to other heavens.

Ca~ri~el~!

From the First Heaven to the Seventh Heaven, all beings heard Ikael's voice and prostrated themselves.

Fairies, giants, even ordinary angels, none could resist the anger in her voice.

Ca~ri~el~!

The echo penetrated iron, shaking the buildings, and reached the Great World War at the edge of the universe.

Ca~ri~el~!

“Waaah!”

Cariel, his face a mix of fearful emotions, cried out.

He didn't know the situation, but Ikael was coming. Archangel Ikael was coming.

Moving at the speed of light, Cariel grabbed Miro and Arius and flew away.

Without time to activate his abilities, he pierced the ceiling with his body and disappeared. At the same time, Ikael's afterimage reached the Milky Way mirror where Uriel stood, and with a bang, she appeared.

The air displaced, shaking the Great World War as if an earthquake had struck.

“…”

Uriel looked at Ikael's profile.

Her eyes were filled with supreme confidence.

It was the image of the Archangel he remembered from long ago.

“Uriel.”

“Archangel.”

Uriel immediately bowed his head.

“Where is Cariel?”

Anke Ra had likely given her full authority.

Lying was impossible.

“He went to the Second Heaven.”

The city of fallen angels. The best and only place for Cariel to hide.

“Shall I guide you?”

Ikael shook her head.

Cariel could be caught anytime.

Ra wanted to postpone the war until he received an answer.

So the first person she needed to meet wasn't Cariel.

“I’m going to Purgatory. Uriel, inform the other archangels that no actions are to be taken without my permission. If they disobey, I will use the authority of the Archangel.”

“I will comply.”

Uriel, having received the order, raised his head and asked,

“Are you going to the rebel command?”

“Yes.”

Ikael's eyes sharpened.

“I will meet the Light of Sector 73, Shirone.”

At dawn, Gaold returned to the First Rebel Command, along with the surviving members of Cage B Team.

Shirone's group, who had stayed up waiting for Gaold, ran to meet him.

Gaold looked exhausted, but thanks to Saint Morak's healing, he wasn't seriously injured.

Sein was immediately interested in the cargo Cage B Team had transported.

It was a container holding a large Tagis.

Sein left with the rebel engineers, while the others looked at Gaold.

Having heard about his painful past, they couldn't treat him as casually as before.

Gaold, seeing their reactions, shrugged it off.

“What are you all doing? Aren't you going to assemble the Tagis? The war starts now. We'll attack in 24 hours.”

Kangnan approached.

“Are you okay?”

Gaold raised an eyebrow.

It was unusual for Kangnan to speak with concern, without her usual sharpness.

"Why are you acting like this too? Are you sulking?"

Except for Miro and Sein, she had been by Gaold's side the longest, but this was the first time she had clearly learned about Gaold's past.

"Why would I? If I was curious, I would have asked earlier."

"Heh."

Gaold pointed his thumb back at Cage Team B.

"Try to persuade them. They were half-destroyed by me, but they will be helpful when needed."

As the chief secretary, she knew that Cage Team B was by no means weak. If the high-ranking official they targeted had not been Gaold, the outcome might have been different.

Wiiiiing!

At that moment, a siren sounded from the rebel command control room.

As everyone turned to look, a soldier ran in shouting.

"Air raid! Enemy air raid! It's coming at an incredible speed!"

Everyone's gaze turned to the sky, but nothing was visible.

"What?"

Even the soldier had a blank expression.

Calculating the radar's range, it should have already been within visible distance.

Gaold frowned and said,

"Prepare."

As soon as he spoke, Kangnan assumed a combat stance, and simultaneously, something crashed in front of them.

Despite its speed, it took quite a while for everyone to register its presence.

During that time, the attacker spread its wings of light and looked around at the rebels with its head held high.

"That, that is…"

The intruder in the rebel's first command was a single angel.

With a face imbued with holy beauty, slender arms and legs, and a slim figure.

Shirone felt like his heart had stopped.

The archangel Ikael was looking at him.
