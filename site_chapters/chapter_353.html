Chapter 353: Swordsmans Conviction (5)

Everyone's eyes turned to the sky. The black sword spun, blocking the sun, and then plunged into the ground behind Rai with a thud, embedding itself more than halfway.

Rai didn't look back. His gaze remained fixed on Rian.

Rian, frozen in a stance with his greatsword aimed, couldn't move a finger. The aftermath of his dvine transcendence brought excruciating pain.

In the prolonged silence, Rai clicked his tongue and turned away.

"I lost. Let's stop."

Rian’s eyes widened in shock. It was the first time in 18 years he had heard Rai admit defeat.

But Rai showed no emotion as he pulled out the black sword and sheathed it. He walked over to Clump and bowed.

"I lost. I admit defeat."

"Heh, you got caught up in his pace. But it was nice to see you so fired up for once."

Rai muttered with a smirk.

"This is why you shouldn't get involved with fools."

"But it wasn't so bad, right? Don't you feel a bit relieved?"

After a moment of thought, Rai muttered as he passed Clump.

"...I feel a bit more like a swordsman now."

Reina quickly followed Rai.

"Wait, come here. Is your hand okay? You have a deployment tomorrow, right?"

"It's nothing. I just cramped and dropped my sword."

"Who are you trying to fool? Do swordsmen cramp that easily? Let me see your hand."

Reina grabbed Rai's wrist and examined his hand. The calluses, now as tough as skin, showed that he hadn't reached this point on talent alone.

"Just as I thought."

Reina frowned as she looked at the back of Rai's hand.

The left hand, which cushioned the impact when gripping the sword, had lost all its fingernails on the index, middle, and ring fingers.

"Don't make a fuss. Fingernails fall off easily."

"I've never heard that! Ugh, you won't even be able to cut steak like this. Come here, let me bandage it."

Reina took a bandage from the first aid kit in the training ground and tightly wrapped Rai's fingers.

As the tension eased, even the spectators felt the fatigue. But Kuan still looked at Rian with a serious expression.

‘Divine transcendence...’

Rian’s swordsmanship in real combat was indeed impressive. It was a performance that could never be shown in the step-by-step evaluations of the swordsmanship school.

‘But I would have taken a different approach.’

Divine transcendence was dangerous. It wasn't just about increasing power.

The movement that bit Rai at the end was so peculiar that it would have been difficult to create even with external force. Yet Rai responded to Rian’s strong sword with his own.

Was it pride? Lack of experience? Or were there other variables on the battlefield that he hadn't read?

"A swordsman is a technician who handles the sword."

"General."

Clump had approached unnoticed.

"But a sword is ultimately a tool for killing. That's an undeniable truth. So why do we pursue the sword?"

Kuan couldn't answer.

"A single sword can never change the world. But the sword in one's heart is different. A great swordsman wins battles, but a knight with conviction changes the world. Rai was ignited by Rian’s conviction."

"The sword in one's heart..."

"Perhaps divine transcendence is the embodiment of conviction."

Kuan recalled a time long ago.

Before his Achilles tendon was severed, his spirit had been so strong that he chose death over accepting the Wind Guards offer. Now, that determination felt like a distant memory.

"I once had it too."

Clump looked at Kuan with kind eyes. As a soldier and a swordsman, he felt for his unfortunate junior.

"How long will you wander? Return to the military. My direct subordinate is retiring. If you take the position, it would be a great help."

"I'll think about it. Thank you for your concern."

Kuan was sincere, but he had no intention of returning to the military.

Today's duel had made it clear. He was already a broken sword. Without embracing a new sword, he could never reach divine transcendence.

"Grandfather, can I drop out now?"

Rian approached with determined strides, his face covered in cold sweat from enduring the pain. Having proven himself with results, Clump had no reason to stop him.

"Yes, you won. Do as you wish, whether it's dropping out or going on a knight's quest."

"I told you from the start. There was no need for this fight."

Rai, bandaging his fingers, spoke as he changed clothes.

"Don't end up dead somewhere. If you die, I'll have to go looking for you."

Contrary to everyone's expectations, Rian didn't get angry. They had both given their all, so there was no point in dwelling on the duel.

But one question remained.

"Hey, let me ask you something."

Rai paused while buttoning his shirt and turned around.

"The technique that exploded the ground, that's an application of swordsmanship, right?"

"So what?"

"Why didn't you use swordsmanship on me? You could have broken my sword and ended the duel."

Rai tilted his head in confusion. Was Rian mocking him?

But Rian’s expression showed no hint of provocation.

‘Is he serious?’

Rai looked at Clump.

"Didn't you tell Rian?"

"Heh, what could I do? He took it like a thief. I hadn't even properly chosen a successor. And the Schema opening was a lie too."

Rai snorted at the blatant lie.

"You never intended to give it to me anyway."

"You didn't need it."

"Tch!"

Feeling left out of the conversation, Rian interrupted.

"What are you talking about? Just answer me. Why didn't you use swordsmanship?"

Rai, annoyed, turned towards the exit of the training ground.

"Don't ask me, ask Grandfather. It's a mess. A swordsman who doesn't even know what he's fighting with."

Reina followed Rai.

"Are you really leaving today?"

"No, I'll stay the night. I'm tired. Bring my meal to my room."

As Rai walked away, Rian glared and pointed at him.

"Hey! Where are you going? Why didn't you use swordsmanship? Don't leave me hanging!"

‘There's no cure for stupidity.’

Ignoring Rian’s shouts, Rai pondered.

The Ozent family had both black-haired and blue-haired lineages.

Why hadn't Grandfather given him the greatsword?

As a child, he thought it was because Grandfather favored Rian, who had the same blue hair. But after today's duel, he had a vague understanding.

At least, the rightful owner of that sword was Rian.

Shirone and Tess decided to stay at Reina's mansion for the night.

The three gathered in Rian’s room, catching up on the past six months. The biggest topic was Amy's failure in her graduation exam.

"How's Amy doing? She must be really upset."

They had read about Amy's failure in the Spirit Journal. The article had shockingly covered the news of the Carmis family's prodigy failing early in the graduation exam.

"She was down for a while, but she's okay now. She'll definitely have a different result next year."

"Yeah, Amy will get through it. I miss her. Those were fun times."

Rian also reminisced about their time in Heaven. Realizing divine transcendence while fighting Ymir was a turning point in his life.

"Amy deserves to be a magician. Tell her I'm rooting for her. I'll be there to cheer her on for the next graduation exam."

A year later, Shirone would also take his graduation exam, and Rian wouldn't miss it.

"You're lucky. I can't go. The swordsmanship school ends later than the magic school. Maybe I should drop out too and go on a quest with Rian."

Rian waved his hand dismissively.

"What's this about a top student at the swordsmanship school dropping out? You should graduate at the top of your class."

"But it feels so empty without you. I really want to go with you."

Seeing Tess on the verge of tears, Rian quickly looked for a topic to change the subject. He noticed the sword Shirone had placed next to the desk and asked,

"Shirone, are you carrying a sword now?"

"Oh, that's a gift."

Shirone explained what had happened in Kazra. Since Rian and Tess had also experienced the journey to Heaven, they understood the concept of an object.

Intrigued, they urged Shirone to demonstrate.

First, Shirone performed some tricks with precise control, then activated the Vajra Armor. The sword enveloped Shirone, and an organic robe formed around him.

The eerie yet powerful appearance left even the bold Rian speechless. Tess pointed to the orb floating in the air and asked,

"What is this orb?"

"That's a magic computation device. It replaces a technique called time-sharing, making calculations much faster. And the crystal on the gauntlet is a magic amplification device. Using these two, I can expand the range of my magic."

"That's amazing. I didn't know objects could have such complex functions."

Armand was a weapon that could tempt any swordsman. But his friends showed no interest beyond curiosity. For those who held the sword of conviction, any tool was just a tool.

‘I wish Rian could use Armand.’

A knight's quest was on a different level from the swordsmanship school's curriculum.

The world wouldn't provide Rian with a systematic difficulty. He could easily handle third-rate thugs or robbers, but some criminals were so skilled that even the kingdom struggled with them.

In that sense, Armand, which enhanced the user's physical abilities and had its own defensive capabilities, would be helpful.

However, unlike in Dante's case, the magic sword did not respond to Rian.

Armand provides the optimal combat environment for all users, but to achieve 100% efficiency, a high level of tactical understanding and complex system control is essential.

Rian’s straightforward and forceful nature made it difficult for him to fully utilize Armand's functions, and Armand seemed to recognize this.

‘It's really like a sword, no matter how you look at it.’

But Rian had no intention of relying on anything. His confidence was sky-high after his duel with Rai.

"No need to worry! This is just the beginning! From today, I'll achieve an undefeated record!"

Shirone smiled contentedly as he watched Rian proudly thump his chest.

He was like a fire. One day, he would become a great blaze that would set the world alight and return to him.

That night.

Rian lay curled up under his blanket, groaning.

The aftermath of divine transcendence didn't end with the first wave. The most intense pain came at night when his body was recovering.

“Ugh! Ugh!”

Groans escaped his throat, but he bit his pillow to endure.

He couldn't let Shirone, who was sleeping next to him, hear his pain. More than anything, he feared that if he screamed now, he would never be able to endure it again.

His face was drenched in cold sweat, and his eyes were unfocused. Yet, Rian fiercely stared into the darkness of the night, enduring the pain.

‘Thank goodness. I'm so glad I won.’

If he had lost to Rai in front of Shirone, he would have fallen into despair and been unable to do anything for a while.

Rai was undoubtedly strong. In terms of technical perfection, he was worthy of being called a genius.

But he wasn't an insurmountable wall.

‘The gap has narrowed. It definitely has.’

He couldn't predict that the same result would come from another duel, but in some aspects, he had certainly surpassed Rai. He was confident in that.

‘I can do it. I didn't choose the wrong path.’

Just knowing that the distance wasn't unreachable filled Rian with joy. Even as his body trembled with pain, laughter escaped his throat.

“Heh, heh heh heh.”

The night of pain and pleasure passed.
