Chapter 1225: Forbidden Territory (1)

“You’re saying you want to go to the human world?” asked Luthia, chieftain of the Fireborn.

“Yes, Chieftain. I want to help my husband change the human world. I believe it’s my calling.”

Yorahan bowed his head in silent apology.

To the chieftain, Armand is like a uniquely precious daughter. Letting her go must be painful.

More than anything, Armand was the only warrior among the Fireborn with an active disposition who could protect them.

“I see.”

Luthia smiled warmly.

“So the day has finally come. The Fireborn must help humans. That’s the purpose passed down to us from the first generation.”

Understanding her sincerity, Yorahan had a sudden thought:

Who was the first Fireborn, I wonder?

The Fireborn were mostly passive. Over time, their identity should’ve faded. And yet—

They’ve upheld this mission through the ages. This isn’t about disposition.

It was a kind of enlightenment.

Before truth, neither active nor passive matters. The first Fireborn must have known that…

About humans, and about human life…

Maybe even more deeply than I do.

Yorahan finally spoke.

“We’re not leaving right away. The Mikhas Kingdom’s forces are beginning to encroach on Fireborn territory. At this rate, they’ll make contact within a month. I’ll find a new place for the Fireborn to migrate.”

“Hoho. You really are endlessly kind, Yorahan.”

Yorahan blushed.

“Don’t worry. I know the Fireborn better than anyone. Yes, we are passive, but that’s how we’ve survived until now. We’ll manage.”

Armand stepped forward.

“But, Chieftain, humans…”

Luthia cut her off.

“Yes, they are a dangerous species. But Armand, now you must understand them. Otherwise, how can you truly follow your husband’s path?”

She looked to Yorahan.

“Leave tomorrow if you must. I’ll take care of the Fireborn’s relocation. The world we see is not the same as the world humans see. Even if you find a perfect resting place, it may not be a place where flowers can bloom.”

The Fireborn could survive on sunlight alone.

“I understand.”

With Luthia’s blessing, Yorahan and Armand returned home to pack.

Since this place was so dear to them, every movement felt slow and heavy.

I really don’t want to leave. But if I keep putting it off, I’ll end up living here forever…

As he held back tears for the sake of his dream, Yorahan spotted a box under the bed.

“Huh? This…”

It was a dagger he hadn’t taken out since coming to the Fireborn village.

He turned toward Armand as he examined it.

“Hey, can I use the whetstone?”

“A dagger? You never even look at that thing.”

“Well, we’re heading back to the human world now. I used to carry it all the time. You need something like this to avoid bad people.”

Armand chuckled.

“What are you worried about? I’ll be the one protecting you.”

She wrapped her arms around him from behind and whispered in his ear.

“Besides, I guarantee it—you’ll never even use it. You even flinch when cleaning a sparrow.”

They knew everything about each other.

“Hey, still! I could at least threaten someone with it. Who’s gonna be scared of a rusty blade?”

Yorahan pulled out the whetstone with stubborn pride, and Armand shook her head.

“Honestly…”

Still, she couldn’t deny—it was a little reassuring.

As dawn began to break, Yorahan was stirred awake by screaming outside.

“What the…?!”

When he sat up, Armand was already walking out the door with her longsword in hand.

Pale-faced Fireborn children came running.

“Sister! Something’s wrong!”

“What happened?”

Yorahan followed them outside and saw a group of soldiers occupying the village entrance.

“Cavalry?”

They wore full suits of metal armor, and their banners bore the emblem of a lion’s face.

“The Mikhas family.”

There was no doubt. But the number of soldiers was too small to have made it through such rugged terrain.

The kingdom had been expanding its territory gradually alongside land cultivation. But why now…?

Yorahan looked toward the front of the cavalry unit.

A young man who hadn’t quite grown out of adolescence sat at the front, his chin cleft in two and eyes sharp with intensity.

That must be the eldest son of the Mikhas family… Mikhas Areon.

Last I heard of him was three years ago. That would make him eighteen now. Looks like he’s desperate to prove his imperial blood.

Though not well-known, Yorahan was still a philosopher—he could see through the nature of those in power.

Beside Areon, a pale scholar rode up on horseback.

“Prince, I believe this is the Fireborn village. Please issue your next command.”

“Hmm. The Fireborn, huh?”

Areon stroked his chin.

Strange powers, but according to records, they aren’t dangerous. Rather, they’re a race of slaves who obey any command.

That was what the scholars had written.

In other words, they never betray their masters. Having such loyal servants would be essential for a king. No wonder the old man’s been so adamant about this land reclamation project.

Of course, there were likely dozens of other reasons.

Whatever the case, I arrived before my father. I cut through that forest to get here.

Ambitious, Areon had built ties with many talented individuals—one of whom was the man beside him now: Fisque, who had guided him through the labyrinth-like forest.

“Fisque,” Areon said.

“This will be the first territory I conquer in my history. If I bring back Fireborn, even my father will have to acknowledge me.”

That would solidify his claim to the throne.

“I’m sure he’ll see you as a worthy successor to a great king. None of your brothers have ever accomplished such a feat.”

Areon raised his sword toward the village.

“Conquer them.”

At his command, over a hundred cavalry charged forward with terrifying speed.

The stunned Fireborn could do nothing but watch—until Chieftain Luthia shouted:

“Run!”

Armand charged against the flow of the crowd, sword drawn, sprinting toward the cavalry.

Yorahan followed close behind.

“Dear!”

“Don’t come! Stay as far away as possible!”

“I’m going with you!”

“Are you crazy? You’ll get yourself killed! Protect the children instead. You can lead them—Chieftain Luthia can’t do it alone.”

“Ugh…”

Yorahan turned away, lips trembling. He couldn’t argue—it was the right call.

“Just buy time and fall back!”

Nodding, Armand pushed away her thoughts and focused entirely on the battle.

“Resonance Adjustment.”

Her sword flew through the air, and blood sprayed between the steel gauntlets of the knights.

As knights dropped one after another, Areon’s eyes lit up with interest.

“Who is that woman?”

Fisque opened his notebook.

“She’s one of the extremely rare Fireborn with an active disposition. They’re called carnivores. Combined with their innate abilities, they exhibit formidable combat power.”

“She’s definitely strong,” Areon said. “And strong is beautiful. Fisque, I want that woman captured. Can you take her alive?”

“Yes,” Fisque replied confidently.

“Split the cavalry into three units. One cuts off the retreat, one captures the villagers. Wrap this up in three minutes.”

As the officer signaled with a banner, the cavalry split formation with sharp precision.

Yorahan quickly read the strategy.

They’re faster than us. If we run, they’ll catch everyone. We have to fight back somehow…

A thought sparked. He called out to Luthia.

“Chieftain! Into the forest!”

As the Fireborn disappeared into the woods, the cavalry hesitated briefly—then abandoned their horses and rushed in on foot.

“Pah! What can they do in the forest—huh?”

It was as if the forest itself came alive. Plants and insects swarmed the knights.

“Damn it! They’re using strange magic!”

No matter how many branches they cut down, the attacks didn’t stop. Eventually, they began to tire out.

Scanning the forest, Fisque muttered, “They’re struggling.”

“And yet… They seem more disciplined than expected.”

Burdened with full authority, Fisque dismounted and raised his arms.

“I’ll handle it.”

Suddenly, wind swirled around him, lifting him into the air before he shot forward like an arrow.

Armand twisted just in time to avoid a direct hit, but his hand grazed her shoulder.

“You’re slow,” Fisque said.

In that moment, Armand felt something unfamiliar.

What is this?

In Shirone’s era, she might have recognized it as electricity. But in this age—

“Ugh!”

She couldn’t comprehend the phenomenon of air sharpening like blades.

She clutched her bleeding arm and retreated. Fisque landed smoothly on the ground.

Calling her sword back, Armand asked, “You’re a weapon mage?”

“No,” Fisque replied, extending a hand.

“It’s the power of the mind.”

“Woman, I am one who seeks the truth of the world. My prince has requested your presence. Surrender quietly, and no one will be harmed.”

“Ha.”

Baring her fangs, Armand slashed her sword.

“You underestimate the Fireborn. We love peace, but we are not cowards who fear death.”

Far off in the forest, exhausted knights stumbled out, clutching their heads.

“I’ve read about it in ancient texts. The theory that they’re one with nature appears to be true. But…”

Fisque raised both hands above his head.

“Humans rule the forces of nature. This—this is the fifth element that unites fire, water, earth, and air.”

The air heated, and a fireball the size of a melon formed above his palm.

“It’s called mental concentration.”

The sight of fire being conjured without tools left Armand stunned.

“Fireball.”

With a swing of his arm, the fireball arced toward the forest’s edge.

“No!”

Boom! An explosion erupted, sending black smoke rising from the woods.

“How about that?” Fisque asked. “More powerful than natural fire. This is the potential of humankind.”

As the villagers staggered out, coughing in the smoke, the cavalry quickly surrounded them.

“Make your choice. Betray them, and you might live. Will you fight, or will you surrender?”

Thus, the battle ended.

Though losses on both sides were higher than expected, Areon was in high spirits.

“Is everyone gathered?”

The Fireborn, faces pale with fear, huddled together as Armand looked around.

Where’s Yorahan?

He was nowhere to be seen.

If he’d escaped, she would be relieved. But there was also the possibility of the worst, and her heart grew heavy.

Please… please be alive.

Areon spoke.

“I am a prince of the Mikhas Kingdom. As of today, this land belongs to Mikhas. You are now citizens of the kingdom. If any of you deny this—”

While his grand speech dragged on, Yorahan observed from a crack in the storehouse.

They’ve been captured, after all.

He had successfully led the Fireborn in repelling the cavalry, but the sudden fire in the forest had changed everything.

Magic…

That’s what they called it.

What do I do now?

With the children taken hostage, Armand would never raise her sword.

Outrunning cavalry is impossible. Even if we could, the Fireborn wouldn’t abandon anyone who fell behind. But if we let ourselves be taken like this…

Yorahan’s eyes twitched.

There is a way!

A method to escape with everyone.

I don’t know how the mage will react—but no. This isn’t about luck. They won’t be able to chase us.

Only one question remained.

Can I do it?

Fingering the dagger hidden in his coat, Yorahan’s eyes burned with fierce resolve.
