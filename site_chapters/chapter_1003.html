Chapter 1003: Infinity (4)

The paladin of the Lami Church recalled the ancient ruins he had seen during his pilgrimage.

Traces that couldn't have been made by humans, yet were too artificial to be the work of nature.

'Who are these people?'

The paladin looked at Shirone's group.

"I am Seina, a senior paladin of the Lami Church's Oracle Management Department. Who are you?"

"I'm Iruki."

Currently unemployed.

"I'm Nade."

Same here.

However, some of the paladins lined up behind Seina recognized those names.

"Iruki? Nade? I've definitely heard those names somewhere..."

They were key figures involved in the World War, so they had to have heard of them, even if only through rumors.

"You're the Valkyrie General!"

As Seina squinted one eye, another subordinate spoke up.

"Then Nade is... Nade West?"

Realizing they were more significant than expected, the paladins' previously expanding energy slightly deflated.

Seina turned to Shirone.

"And who are you?"

Nade seized the opportunity.

"Hmph! This person is my friend, one of the Five Stars of the Ivory Tower, and the era's..."

"I'm Shirone."

That alone was enough, and the murmuring quickly ceased.

They were clearly surprised, but their reactions were different from what Nade had expected.

'What's this? Why the hostility...'

Shirone knew the reason.

'Lami Church, the world's largest sect. They wouldn't be pleased with me fighting alongside Zion.'

When Miro founded Zion, great seekers from various religions gathered.

Many from the Lami Church joined, but the Pope wasn't happy about it.

Even setting aside their pride as the world's largest religion, Zion's philosophy didn't align with the Lami Church.

'Miro believed that the ultimate truth transcended any god. But the Lami Church is different. They consider any religion that doesn't worship the one god, Cria, as heresy.'

"So you're Yahweh, who founded the religion of philanthropy?"

As expected, Seina's tone was not kind.

"It's not a religion. I just want to show that this world is worth existing."

"Hmph, all cults say such things to step into sacred places. But no matter how plausible their words, without divine blessing, they're just empty shells."

Seina suddenly smirked.

"Look at Zion. They pursued depth of enlightenment and purity, but didn't they collapse? Those who don't hold a god in their hearts will eventually fall to evil."

Iruki asked, "Are you saying you're different?"

Seina proudly drew the holy sword Astasia.

"Look."

As divine power infused it, a clear blue light, unlike any other sword, shone.

"To fight as God's messenger, you need more than willpower. You need holy weapons, powerful divine power, and a systematically trained organization."

It was very human in a way, but Shirone didn't criticize others' religions.

He still saw them as comrades facing the same enemy.

"Alright. We'll leave now. Please take care of those left here."

Even from a civilian's perspective, it was better to be within a structured system.

"Don't worry about that. But first, I want to hear about what happened here. And..."

If possible, Seina wanted to take Shirone to the Vatican for an inquisition.

'If only it were possible.'

She had heard of Yahweh's power and now confirmed it with her own eyes.

'I can't do it alone. Unless the highest paladins of the church step in...'

She offered an alternative.

"How about joining God's expedition? If your mission is to purify the world's evil."

While Lami Church's help would increase the efficiency of the Ultima Project, Shirone shook his head.

"Sorry. We'll go our own way."

Shirone aimed for a grand unification, not forcing everyone into one concept.

'Even if our interests align now, we'll part ways at the final point. It's better not to get entangled from the start.'

Seina asked, displeased, "Why?"

Knowing he had to answer carefully, Shirone explained as gently as possible.

"We travel to help those in need. That's enough for us."

Giving without expecting anything in return is love.

As Seina looked into the resolute eyes of Shirone's group, she fell into deep thought.

In the center where Hand of God struck the ground, Prejudice's 5 o'clock lay sprawled out.

Of course, the space was the underworld, not reality.

"Kik. Kik."

Countless ghouls gathered around the rocky area where lava flowed.

As they appeared one by one, they soon surrounded her in countless numbers.

"Food... food..."

Even though she was Siok, an apostle of Satan, the starving ghouls would eat anything.

"I'll eat it!"

As the ghouls swarmed like water, an old man with sagging cheeks appeared next to Prejudice's 5 o'clock.

"Back off."

Sloth's 4 o'clock.

Unwanted things don't happen.

"Kieee! Kieee!"

The ghouls that had been rushing at Prejudice's 5 o'clock ignored her and started biting each other.

They knew they were kin, but nothing unwanted happens when Sloth's 4 o'clock is around.

"I'm sorry!"

The latecomer ghouls scattered like cockroaches.

"Tsk tsk."

Clicking his tongue, Sloth's 4 o'clock put his hands behind his back and nudged Prejudice's 5 o'clock with his foot.

As she grimaced and stirred, Sloth's 4 o'clock smiled and asked, "Are you awake?"

Prejudice's 5 o'clock, who had sprung up, realized she had almost died when she saw the old man grinning wickedly.

"I almost died."

"Yahweh got you good."

"That...!"

Grinding her sharp teeth, Prejudice's 5 o'clock screamed so loudly her eyes nearly popped out.

"I'll kill him!"

Even as her scream echoed through hell, Sloth's 4 o'clock showed no special emotion.

"Don't be too upset. You can't do it alone. Yahweh is someone who has mastered the heart."

Prejudice's 5 o'clock stood up.

"I'll kill him. Where is he?"

"He seems to be still around, mixed with those parasitic paladins."

"Heh, a delicacy has arrived."

Ordinary humans were fun, but corrupting a paladin was even more thrilling.

"Let's go. I won't make a mistake this time."

As Prejudice's 5 o'clock moved, Sloth's 4 o'clock followed her with his hands behind his back.

"Are you really going to do it? The opponent is Yahweh."

"What? Are you scared?"

With a slight smile, Sloth's 4 o'clock slowly walked after her.

"...Of course not."

Seina made her decision.

"Denied."

The Lami Church primarily fought evil but had a history of judging many heretics.

"Why? Is helping people wrong?"

"Because your decision lacks God."

Seina's sword glowed even more blue.

"Do you know the difference between sacred doctrine and heresy? Heretics always try to become gods themselves. Your thoughts are extremely heretical."

Feeling the killing intent, Shirone spoke coldly.

"Stop it. We don't need to fight each other."

"I know you're strong. But this is God's command. And God's command..."

As her subordinates all raised their weapons, Seina kicked off the ground and charged.

"Give it your all."

"Tsk!"

As Nade and Iruki took up battle stances, a scream came from behind.

"Aaaahhhhh!"

The fighting stopped and everyone turned to see Ravika writhing in pain.

"What's happening!"

Seina ran over first.

"It hurts! My body, my body... Aaahhh!" Ravika's facial bones began to twist as she let out a violent scream.

"Gurgle! Gurgle!"

Her face twisted left and right, gradually resembling someone else, and then her body's bones began to change.

Seina's eyes widened.

"A physical possession phenomenon?"

Human emotions can affect the body, marking the face with the traces of time.

But for such a sudden change, even to the extent that bones twist, the only being within her teachings capable of embedding such extreme emotions was Satan alone.

"What kind of evil was here?"

Sloth.

One of the twelve powers that sustain Satan's chaos.

"Grrrrrrr!"

Ravika, arching her torso like a bow and roaring, was no longer the woman they knew.

As the noise ceased and she returned to normal, her face had transformed into Prejudice's 5th hour.

"Who are you?"

Seina's sword, aimed forward, trembled.

"Sloth."

Shirone spoke.

"I don't know what enemy the Rami Order fought, but the evil you know is of a different dimension."

"Sloth."

As Seina muttered, Prejudice's 5th hour moved.

"Kill Yahweh! They mock and scorn your god, and they will harm you!"

"Argh!"

As the hidden code was activated, the paladins behind Seina's eyes lit up.

Sanctification, a reflex trained from childhood through brainwashing to automatically respond to demonic temptations.

"Kill them! If you don't kill them now, you will suffer!"

"Aaaaaah!"

A few subordinates whose eyes blazed fiercely drew their swords and held them upside down.

"I'm sorry, Lady Seina!"

Then, raising their heads, they accurately stabbed the swords into the gaps in their necks.

As about twenty people collapsed, Nade opened his mouth in disbelief.

"What? Why so easily..."

Seina's nose bridge contorted. "Damn it!"

Even the power of sanctification couldn't prevent evil when they were possessed; they were trained to choose self-sacrifice.

"They were never weak. The finest paladins."

It was just that the power of the evil shaking their hearts was of unprecedented intensity.

"Lord Almighty!"

Seina's sanctification spread a golden light, starting to burn the evil embedded in her heart.

Ehehehehe! Ehehehehe!

But all that could be heard was mocking laughter, her heart being further consumed by powerful desires.

"Ah... Aahhh..."

Tears streaming, she slowly raised her sword in despair.

"I must die. I cannot be manipulated." She had been baptized from birth, maintaining the faith of Cria through rigorous training.

"Destroy the evil!"

With her eyes tightly shut, she screamed and raised the sword to her neck.

"Ugh!"

Suddenly feeling resistance, the blade stopped abruptly in front of her neck.

"Don't give up."

When she opened her eyes, Shirone was firmly holding the sword handle, resisting.

"What power..."

Confident in her own strength as a knight, her arm couldn't even pull a centimeter.

It was because of the light smoke rising from Shirone's hand, coiling around the blade.

"Let go! I'd rather die than become a minion of evil!"

"You can do it. Don't set limits on yourself!"

'So foolish.'

The activation of the martyrdom code in her subconscious was precisely because she couldn't surpass those limits.

"Uuuuuugh!"

As she pulled the sword with all her might, her arm slowly bent.

Driven by the will of a superior paladin, Shirone fiercely opened his other hand.

"No choice."

Pulling on the handle, Shirone lunged forward and struck her forehead with his palm.

With a hollow sound, a tide of infinite light flooded her mind.

"Hah!"

At the same time, black droplets like ink trickled out from the back of her head.

"Ah, aah."

As the evil cleared from her heart, filled with emotion, her shoulders trembled.

"The human heart..."

Even the tears that flowed seemed made of light.

"Is it truly this infinite?"
