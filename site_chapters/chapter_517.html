Chapter 517: The Nameless Swordsman (1)

The border area between the Kingdoms of Baiden and Ferris is a dangerous region where skirmishes constantly occur even after a ceasefire agreement.

However, it is also an ideal place for a knight-in-training to gain real combat experience.

Rafne Village, slightly closer to the Baiden side of the border.

A lone swordsman crossed the village entrance.

His physique, both agile and strong, proved that his muscles were not just for show, and the large straight sword strapped to his back attested to that fact.

He was Ogent Rian, a knight-in-training who had dropped out of Kaizen Swordsmanship School to wander the world and gain real combat experience.

"Damn…"

A voice like scraping metal came from his completely hoarse throat.

He had wandered the world seeking battlefields, becoming a mercenary for the Kingdom of Baiden and fighting skirmishes against Ferris soldiers.

However, it turned out to be a war of attrition orchestrated by local officials through secret negotiations to receive subsidies from the kingdom, and most of the hired mercenaries were killed to silence them.

Rian, who was suddenly attacked by his own allies, managed to survive and make it to this place.

'If only I had been more cautious…'

Well, that's the life of a mercenary wherever you go.

The Parrot Mercenary Corps he met in Galliant had also fallen into banditry due to noble disputes.

'It looks like I won't be able to beg for food here either.'

One of the reasons war is so exhausting is that there is no proper place to rest even after leaving the battlefield.

The village he had just arrived in was no different; there wasn't even a market, let alone food scraps on the ground.

Many houses had collapsed roofs that hadn't been repaired, and the people's faces were lifeless.

'Then I'll have to cross the mountain tonight.'

No one would give Rian food.

If he threatened them with his sword, they might give in, but he had seen many knight trainees become bandits that way while wandering the world.

"Hoo! Hoo!"

Suddenly, heat rose to his head, and he felt dizzy.

The wound on his side from an arrow during the war began to throb painfully.

He had given himself first aid, but he couldn't stop the infection, and he hadn't eaten for days, so his body couldn't hold out.

Thud!

Rian used his last bit of strength to lean against a wall by the roadside and collapsed.

"Hooo!"

His breath was so hot it felt like it could melt his teeth.

"Am I dying?"

When he had boasted to Clump and stormed out, he hadn't known.

There is no limit to how bad things can get.

Fatigue, pain, fear, despair—everything that ruins a person tormented Rian.

There was no instructor to say, "That's enough," and stop the misery.

The world simply said,

"If you can't endure, die."

'Well, that might be okay too.'

Rian couldn't remember how he escaped the battlefield.

All he had was an omniscient image of himself, covered in blood and screaming, breaking through the encirclement of soldiers from both kingdoms.

'Dying might be a good choice.'

Rian smiled weakly.

Not because he found it funny, but to see if he could still smile.

For the first time in endless despair, he wanted to run away from his life.

'I'm not afraid of death.'

He was only afraid of becoming a skeleton buried in the ground without achieving anything.

"Shirone… Tess…"

Rian blinked slowly. His vision narrowed by half each time his eyelids closed.

"I'm sorry…"

"Mister, what's wrong?"

At the edge of his fading consciousness, he heard a young girl's voice.

"Are you okay? Please wake up."

The sight of the girl's face filling his narrowing vision, like a hole in black paper, was Rian's last memory.

-I don't know yet. But it's possible.

-Smile. Smile.

-It's dangerous, but it's worth a try.

-Smile. Smile.

"Ugh!"

Rian's eyes shot open as he bolted upright.

He couldn't remember the dream, but the low, repetitive sound still echoed in his auditory nerves like a hallucination.

"Damn it!"

The curse that always came out when he had dreams with hallucinations.

Was he gradually going insane?

Rian tried to ignore his negative thoughts.

'What does "smile" mean?'

The only hint was a story his grandfather had told him when he received the straight sword .

His grandfather had been saved by an old man and his granddaughter in a strange village after being mortally wounded while fighting bandits.

He didn't know what treatment he received, but the word "smile" kept repeating in his unconscious state.

'Is it a secret contained in the object?'

Rian stared at leaning against the wall, but unlike Armand, the sword conveyed no information.

"Huh?"

It was only after organizing his thoughts that he realized he didn't even know where he was.

As he hurriedly got out of bed and grabbed the sword's handle, the door burst open, and an eight-year-old girl with brown hair braided on both sides entered.

"Oh? Mister, you're awake! Mom, the mister is awake!"

The girl shouted out the door, and soon a woman in her mid-thirties entered.

She wasn't exactly beautiful, but she had a neat appearance for someone living in a war-torn village.

"You're awake. Thank goodness."

Realizing that the first people he met were a young girl and a woman, and that their eyes and tone held no hostility, Rian finally relaxed his expression.

"Where am I?"

"This is a village in the Kingdom of Baiden."

As someone living in a war zone, she provided information to identify friend or foe first.

"Oh, the village…"

Now he remembered.

There had been a girl who spoke to him just before he lost consciousness.

"But how did you bring me here?"

"You don't remember? You walked here holding my daughter's hand. Though you collapsed as soon as you came in."

Did he?

It seemed he had wanted to live after all, which made him feel bitter.

"Come out. I've prepared a meal."

As the woman led her daughter out, Rian examined his bandaged body.

The arrow wound had healed completely.

It was impossible for someone who couldn't use schema to recover so quickly, but he was used to it by now.

Whenever he had dreams with hallucinations, all his wounds were always healed.

'Maybe Grandpa had dreams too?'

After pondering for a moment, Rian realized he had been rude despite receiving help and hurriedly got up and went outside.

The house wasn't large, so the living room was visible as soon as he stepped out of the room.

The girl was sitting at an old round table, and the woman was cooking soup.

"I apologize for the trouble. You saved my life."

When Rian, sitting next to the girl, expressed his gratitude, the woman shook her head.

"I just disinfected the wound and applied some medicine. It was amazing to see the wound heal before my eyes. It must be schema, right?"

Even without knowledge of swordsmanship, living in a war zone meant hearing various things.

"Ah, yes. Something like that."

Rian didn't feel the need to explain phenomena he didn't understand himself.

"Mister, you're a mercenary, right? What's your name?"

"Me? Rian. And you?"

When doing rough work, it was rare for knight trainees to reveal their full names to avoid tarnishing their family's honor.

"Rian? Hmm, Rian."

The girl kept murmuring his name, then looked a bit disappointed.

"I don't know. Mister, you must not be a famous mercenary. Oh, my name is Liz."

Rian felt a pang of irritation.

He hadn't expected his name to be known in such a small village, but hearing it from an innocent child somehow stung.

"But kid, I'm not a mister. I'm only nineteen."

Liz shrugged.

"I know. All misters say that. They all think they're teenagers at heart."

"……"

As Rian stared in disbelief, the woman brought a bowl of soup to the table.

"Don't tease him. I'm sorry, Liz is just playing around."

"But he called me a kid!"

Ignoring her daughter's words, the woman sat across from Rian.

"Despite your long journey, you don't have much of a beard. Liz is too young to understand."

"But that means my face is…"

The woman covered her mouth and chuckled.

"I'm joking. Now that I see you, you're a handsome young man."

"Haha, I wasn't trying to get compliments…"

Blushing, Rian lowered his head, realizing the mother and daughter were watching him, and awkwardly licked his lips.

It seemed playfulness ran in the family.

"Well, I'll eat now."

Rian wasted no time and began devouring the soup.

Honestly, even if there were a few bugs in the soup, he would have eaten it gratefully.

After waiting for Rian to finish eating, the woman extended her hand for a handshake and introduced herself.

"I'm Sylvia. This is my daughter, Liz."

Liz stuck out her tongue as she made eye contact with Rian.

"And your husband…?"

"He died. He left us three years ago on the battlefield."

Thinking of her father, Liz shouted excitedly.

"I hate war! I hate those bandits who keep coming!"

"Liz, what did I say? When we have guests…"

Ignoring her mother, Liz grabbed Rian's wrist.

"You're really strong, right? Since I saved you, please get rid of the bandits who bother my mom."

"Weren't you saying I'm not famous?"

"Don't worry. Those bandits aren't famous either. And I've heard there are really strong knights who aren't famous. You're one of them, right? You're really strong, aren't you? I've never seen anyone carry a sword as big as yours."

Liz's eyes were sincere.

'Is that so…'

Of course, it was an act of kindness, but the child's desperate hope must have played a part in saving the dying Rian.

However, Rian couldn't help them.

One thing he had learned for sure while wandering the world:

A sword means killing, and killing means responsibility.

It was true that he had received help, but choosing to kill and bearing that responsibility was another matter.

'I can't stay here forever.'

"Don't worry too much. Liz doesn't understand the ways of the world yet. And… it's not all bad."

'Not all bad?'

Rian was more concerned by that statement.

Come to think of it, where did Sylvia get the anti-inflammatory medicine?

Medicine's value on the battlefield was beyond imagination.

Also, having enough food to offer to others didn't match the desolate scene he felt when he first arrived in the village.

'Those scumbags…'

Sensing the situation, Rian tensed and looked up.

"Don't tell me those bandits…"

"Hey! Sylvia!"

At that moment, a drunken shout came from outside the door.

Liz, now pale, hid behind her mother, and Sylvia's face darkened as she slowly stood up.

Bang!

A man kicked the door open and strode in.

He was tall, with a scar running across his nose.

It was Fox, the deputy leader of the Red Spear Bandits.

"Where is he? The guy who stayed here last night! Don't tell me you're hiding him…"

As he scanned the house with a sharp gaze, he stopped when he saw the table.

The guy was much younger and more handsome than he had heard.

"Oh? So that's how it is?"
