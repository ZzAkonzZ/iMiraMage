Chapter 998: Tomb of the Gods (3)

About ten minutes later, Gaold regained consciousness.

His hair had turned white, but his eyes were clearer than ever.

Miro asked, "How are you feeling?"

"I'm fine. Fully recovered."

It seemed impossible for a person, but with Gaold, it somehow felt believable.

'He hasn't lost his memory.' This meant he was fully focused on the battle, and the fundamental reason was likely Miro.

For the team, this was an opportunity.

'If his mind is clear, Gaold is invincible.'

Arius looked down into the massive hole in Ymir's dream and said, "This is the fifth deep layer. This is where the real exploration begins. Shall we go?"

"Of course. Let's start now."

At that moment, Monga spoke up, "Wait a moment. I think it's better if I stay here."

Shirone asked, "Why?"

Monga's Nightmare ability could be effective even in the deep layers.

"If Ymir truly felt discomfort from this nightmare, it's better if I stay here."

Arius agreed, "That's true. Ymir invited us to the deep layers to destroy the nightmare. Therefore, it's unlikely that the Egoist will activate."

"Because he wants it himself."

"Yes. Conversely, if we destroy the nightmare, the Egoist will quickly overwhelm us. Monga is right. Someone needs to maintain this nightmare."

Luber said to Shirone, "Since there's no door, you'll have to return through Drimo as well. It's wise for Monga to stay."

"Then we'll go down. Monga, we're counting on you."

Monga nodded, and following Arius, Shirone's group jumped into the hole.

They entered Ymir's mind, the fifth deep layer. It had been a week since Shirone had taken his teams to various worlds.

During that time, a bloody storm from the demon realm swept across the world.

The number of deaths increased daily, and screams from emotional plague echoed throughout the central continent.

"Kill me! Aaah! Just kill me already!"

Those who had nothing left to lose had to fully surrender themselves to pain.

The lucky ones met Shirone and found brief peace, but it was only a temporary measure.

Shirone bit his lip.

'At this rate, everyone will die.'

Earthquakes, cold waves, scorching sun—none were as terrible as the emotional plague.

'I need to find a way.'

Shirone's gaze turned towards somewhere in the central continent.

The World Health Organization.

Shirone looked through the glass windows along a long corridor at the patients.

All of them were restrained and screaming silently.

"We're at capacity." Shirone looked at Seriel.

It hadn't even been two weeks since the emotional plague outbreak, but her face had aged significantly in that time.

"Honestly, restraint and experimentation are meaningless. No anesthetic works."

Such was the nature of the demon realm.

"Don't be too pessimistic. There must be a way. I'm searching everywhere too."

At the end of the corridor stood Seriel's superior, Thanos.

"You're early."

Thanos nodded and said, "I can't say good morning. There was an emergency transport at dawn. The entire family is dead."

Thanos pointed to a small room for patient consultations rather than an inpatient room.

Looking through the window on the door, they saw a man with his head hanging low.

"Is that Klein?"

Even visible outside his clothes were signs of self-harm; his face was gaunt like a corpse.

But... he somehow looked relieved.

"Yes. A farmer living on the outskirts of Bashka. Epidemiological investigations suggest he might be the first infected with emotional plague."

Shirone remained silent.

"It doesn't mean much now. It's already spread from the central continent to the southern continent. Every day countless nobles come here with bags of money."

In front of a no-smoking sign, Thanos lit a cigarette.

"They must be hoping for something from this medical stronghold. At least hoping to reduce their pain. But we haven't even found a way to alleviate pain yet, let alone a cure."

"They're all high-ranking officials or royalty," Seriel noted.

Thanos sneered, "Of course. He's lucky in a way—he came here with the title of first infected. Or maybe unlucky? He can't even commit suicide now."

Sometimes experts' words can make listeners uncomfortable, so Seriel quickly interrupted.

"Let's go in."

Even as they opened the door, Klein didn't react at all.

His pupils were dilated; his mouth hung open; he somehow looked happy.

'The pain is gone.' He had given up something, and Shirone didn't want to know what it was.

From Shirone's perspective, what made emotional plague more dangerous than other demonic realms wasn't just pain.

'The real danger is in destroying humanity.'

That's why WHO called it Emotion Scale Disease.

"For example," Thanos said, "material things are important for emotions."

"Material things are life itself—food, clothes, shelter—and money sums it all up. Seekers like you might find it trivial," Seriel interjected firmly.

"Shirone doesn't find it trivial."

"In any case... those with more have more to lose. And they're cunning about it. They don't just throw things away recklessly to suppress pain—they use everything bit by bit. But this farmer couldn't do either."

It was said that within three days of infection with emotional plague, he had nothing left.

After that came only excruciating pain.

And this morning he lost his child.

"It's not entirely his fault. At least we can try to reduce his pain."

Shirone used Miracle Stream to suppress emotional plague even on researchers.

If there were a way to eliminate patients' pain entirely, WHO would become a battlefield.

Understanding what Thanos wanted, Shirone shook his head firmly.

"The criteria are clear: using it as quickly as possible to stabilize the world. Letting emotions take over would only increase chaos."

"I suppose so."

No royal or minister would leave Shirone's mystery alone if they knew about it.

'But...'

Thanos looked at Klein again.

'If Yahweh hadn't come here first... that man could have been me.'

Klein spoke up,

"Please kill me."

Seriel walked over and patted his shoulder,

"It was an accident. After three days of pain you lost your sanity."

"I should have died instead. Even if I was insane, I should have died."

"For now try to rest." Klein lifted his head,

"I don't want to live anymore. They say they do experiments here? Do them, whatever you want, cut open my stomach or take out what's in my head, do whatever you want."

A cold silence followed,

Seriel and Thanos held back their words because they honestly hoped for such an outcome,

'There's a limit to experimenting on nobles, even senior members of Holy War are hospitalized here, they've laid eggs hoping they'll be cured first.'

Seriel suddenly thought,

'Even though humanity is heading towards catastrophe, we still have these concerns—maybe humans still have some hope.'

Since it was an unchangeable issue anyway, Seriel cleared her mind and refocused,

'Klein, the presumed first infected, having lost his family, and feeling responsible, he'd stop at nothing.'

'I have some hypotheses I want to test, even just pharmacokinetic experiments on banned substances...'

Seriel shook her head,

'Don't lose your sanity, the more urgent it gets, the more important rules become.'

Having regained her composure, she patted Klein again,

"I appreciate your willingness, but WHO operates under Article 13 of Holy War Convention..."

"Please!"

Klein burst into tears and fell prostrate,

"I'm so angry, I feel like I'll go crazy, I want revenge on this damned disease, and on myself! Please use me!"

Seriel glanced at Shirone,

'Human experimentation.'

It might already be happening underground—but WHO's name carried different weight,

"If you won't use me, I'll die somewhere alone, I don't want to live in this excruciating pain anymore."

Shirone asked Thanos,

"How effective could it be?"

"It might fail, but data collection would be on another level in terms of quality and quantity, after all, we have top biologists gathered here."

"He must not die during experimentation."

"I can't guarantee that, he might die."

"Even so, you can't proceed knowing he might die."

Thanos finally turned his head,

"What are you thinking?"

Shirone approached Klein who was kneeling on the ground, and met his eyes,

"It might be tough, are you really prepared?"

"Prepared? Try me, I'll do anything you ask, whatever it is I'll do it."

Shirone nodded,

"Alright, I will follow your will, humanity will owe you greatly."

Seriel shouted,

"Shirone what are you...!"

At that moment Shirone raised both hands, and drew forth abundant smoke from Miracle Stream,

As a sphere of light formed in heaven and slowly descended, Klein stared blankly with his mouth open,

He had never seen magic before,

"W-what is this..."

"I will absolve your sins." As Agape's light descended upon Klein, it extended his incubation period for emotional plague,

"You won't suffer from emotional plague anymore, this is my heart responding to your resolve, but will you still participate in experimentation? Even if you refuse, I won't change my mind."

Fire burned in Klein's eyes,

"I lost my wife, and killed my child, I want redemption, I won't change my mind."

Seriel's eyes softened, but Thanos still asked sternly,

"You said you wouldn't use that power on patients?"

"The criteria remain unchanged, but Klein is no longer just another patient here."

Rising up Shirone continued,

"He is now someone fighting for humanity alongside Seriel Thanos and myself."

Thanos adjusted his glasses.

"The quickest way to stabilize the world, that's Yahweh's standard huh."

For once he felt his blood boil again.

"Please, Seriel. Make sure to develop a cure. At least find a way to reduce the pain."

Seriel firmly closed her mouth and nodded.

'I will do it. I must succeed. The only way I can breathe easily is by achieving this.'

That day, an undisclosed team was formed within the World Health Organization.
