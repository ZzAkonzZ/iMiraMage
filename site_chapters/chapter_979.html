Chapter 979: The Grand Premise (1)

For those who had an inkling about Shirone and Ikael's relationship, and for those who had no idea, it was a shock to everyone.

The question of an angel having a human child, which shakes the foundations of biology, didn't even arise.

What mattered was that Yahweh, representing humanity, and the leader of Heaven's army were strongly connected.

'Shirone... is Ikael's child?' For the officials close to Adolf XIII, the strengthening of Shirone's cause was not a good thing.

There wasn't much time left until the succession ceremony, but they were people who had experienced many reversals to get to this point.

'There's no giving up until the end. We still have one card left. But if what he says is true...'

Their future was certainly looking bleak.

'Should I switch to Pony's side now? If I don't, I might really get purged.'

Even though the Demon Realm had opened on each continent and humanity was on the brink of annihilation, who really cared about that?

The reason they were here was to protect their own lives, not to save the world.

Shirone, who knew most of human history, also understood their hearts.

'It's understandable.'

As Goffin's last message said, protecting oneself is not a sin.

Shirone waited for everyone in the Grand Hall to gather their thoughts before speaking again.

"Ikael's joining us will be a great help to humanity, but it won't be enough on its own. If we categorize the world's situation in terms of enemies, humanity currently has three powerful enemies: Nane, Ymir, and Havitz."

These were formidable opponents that couldn't be argued against.

"Each of them is deadly to humanity. In terms of depth of power, I believe I am not inferior to them. But the reason I couldn't stop them is..."

Miro spoke up.

"The Ultima System."

"Yes. The level I've reached is based on the heart. After the succession ceremony, I plan to travel the world to gather the hearts of humanity. While kingdoms and cities can be reached through Pony, I intend to personally visit villages and remote areas where the social system hasn't penetrated."

"So?"

Turning towards the voice, Shirone saw Gaold sitting gloomily in a corner.

Kangnan was taking care of him, and Miro immediately looked away from Gaold.

"What are you going to do? Go around asking people to change their hearts for the sake of the world?"

Shirone remained silent.

"That might work. But you know, right? The human heart..."

As Gaold put a cigarette in his mouth, Kangnan lit it for him.

"Cough! Cough!"

Despite being in front of Pony, the future queen, Kangnan seemed to have half given up.

After coughing repeatedly, Gaold took a long drag on the cigarette and continued.

"If a human wants an apple, they won't eat a strawberry. Even though they could just eat it. The heart can't be swayed even by trivial matters like choosing which fruit to eat. If you threaten them with a knife, they'll eat the strawberry, but that's not the heart. Changing the heart means changing oneself. It means becoming a completely different person."

As he threw the still-burning cigarette butt, the officials quickly scattered.

"There's only one way to make a person who wants an apple eat a strawberry. Do you know what that is?"

Gaold held up two fingers.

"Give them both."

Shirone still didn't speak, but his deep gaze answered Gaold.

"If you offer both the apple and the strawberry, a person can willingly eat the strawberry. That's the heart. It's an uncontrollable monster that wants to devour everything. Even the extreme Miro can only cage it. Havitz completely unleashed it. Maybe only Nane can kill that monster."

Completely excising the heart.

"And you want to tame it? I don't want to pour cold water on your idea, but I can assure you, it's impossible. Not every human can become you."

Even though he appeared to be a wreck, Gaold's analysis was still sharp.

Miro acknowledged it too.

'He's not completely gone.'

In a way, Gaold's life might be the very embodiment of the monster he just described.

'It can't be caged, it can't be freed. It can't be tamed, it can't be killed.'

That life might be called Miro, but she didn't want to think that far.

"Mr. Gaold is right."

Now all eyes were back on Shirone, and he readily admitted it.

"As I mentioned to my friends yesterday, this is a technical issue. I don't think we can reach Ultima in time through normal methods."

"Then?"

As Miro spoke, there was a knock on the door.

The large door opened, and Blitz and Eitra, who had left somewhere last night, entered.

"Messiah, we have brought them."

Following the apostles were the familiar dream guardians, Luber and Monga.

Luber greeted them with a kind smile.

"I have come at the call of the Great Star."

As those who had experienced Drimo looked on in surprise, Miro suddenly realized.

"Shirone, you..."

There was such a method.

"Yes. Mr. Luber is a dream guardian who manages the realm of Drimo. In other words, he is connected to the minds of all humanity."

Through Omega, Shirone also understood what kind of being Luber was.

He wasn't as high-ranking as the Great Star or Argones, but he was still a manager responsible for part of the system.

"The plan is this: with Mr. Luber's approval, we will install the Ultima System into the collective subconscious of all humanity. We will directly implant it into their minds."

"Uh..."

Some people tried to speak, but their minds were still not clear.

Finally understanding what it meant, Amy spoke with a look of joy.

"Such a method…” Miro interrupted.

"It's a contradiction."

She was the only one who could spot the flaw in the plan.

"The method itself is good. But it includes a grand premise. The Ultima System must actually exist. At least conceptually, it must be complete. But if that were the case, we would have already solved it through education, training, or mental resonance. The reason we can't is because it's a matter of perception. It's easy to explain what a sense is, but who can understand what it feels like? Even I can't guess."

"That's why the pilgrimage is necessary," Shirone explained.

"This world is made up of the exchange of photon and quantum signals. In other words, half is the background, and beings with hearts fill in the rest to create the world. This means that all events exist as probabilities, and any impossible thing can happen."

Shirone raised his index finger and asked,

"Then why don't quantum phenomena occur?"

Everyone listened intently.

"Why don't we suddenly fall through the floor to the first level, or move to a random place while walking, or transform into bizarre monsters?"

Miro spoke.

"Because those things don't happen. More precisely, they haven't happened even once. It's paradoxical, but you're saying that paradox is the truth, right?"

"Yes. If this world were made up solely of the heart, anything would be possible. It would be chaotic and messy, but logically consistent. But fundamentally, it's photon signals. These signals create preconceptions and reduce the slight probability of such things happening to zero."

Shirone looked at the people.

"In a world made of electricity, the combination of photons and quantum signals seems incredibly efficient. It lays the background and lets the users define it themselves. Photon signals act like a consensus mechanism. The world's definition is created by everyone's consensus, so no special coordination is needed."

The world is built at the speed of light.

"Those who used this world long ago realized the truth and developed the signals to the extreme."

And finally, one of them denied the cold world and ascended against the void.

"Not everyone reached Ultima from the beginning. But a small event, that one event, can change everything."

"The limit of perception. The realm of belief."

Nodding at Sein's words, Shirone created a small ball with material.

"In the world of photons and quantum, all truths are obtained inductively. For example, the fact that this ball can't pass through the wall isn't because it's inherently true, but because it has never happened before. But..."

Shirone tossed the ball in his palm and then threw it towards the window.

"One day."

The ball flew straight through the window and out.

"When that inductive logic is broken."

As everyone stared with their mouths agape, Shirone turned back.

"Human perception can enter a new realm beyond its limits. In other words, the probability that was crushed to zero will suddenly soar."

Because half of the world is made up of the heart.

"People often say they believe in God. But even a clergyman can't be perfect. It's not to disparage them, but no being can believe in something they can't perceive. That's why Eden's barrier can't be perfect. But if a god truly descended into this world, or even a small miracle equivalent to that occurred, the whole world would likely believe in God."

Shirone summarized.

"That's why I want to purify people's hearts through a pilgrimage. Quantum signals are like multiple users sharing the same bandwidth, so if their genuine intentions don't intertwine, the effect diminishes. Just now, I passed the ball through the window, but if someone with strong mental skills had rejected it, the two phenomena would have clashed."

Sein spoke.

"But there isn't anyone like that. No one has expanded their perception as much as you."

"Yes. Because they can't even conceive of it. But now that you've seen it with your own eyes, who knows?"

Miro spoke.

"Even if you say that, I know it won't work. It's not a skill that can be imitated just by seeing it. That's why you're trying to implant the Ultima System directly."

So Shirone's words were ultimately about possibilities, but Miro didn't argue further.

Given the current situation of humanity, they had to try anything that wasn't outright impossible.

"Alright, I understand your idea. But there's a second problem. Even if you implant the Ultima System deeply, what about the aftermath? You realized Ultima in Heaven. You can't achieve integration by skipping from the 6th to the 11th sense without going through the 7th to 10th senses. Even if you understand all the signals, it will only cause social chaos."

Arius, who was lying on the floor with his chin propped up, added,

"The problem is whether humans can accept it. In Shirone's case, Ikael implanted Ataraxia. But even then, he almost reached the point of mental collapse. Can ordinary humans, who aren't Unlockers, withstand such a shock?"

Come to think of it, his nickname was the Grave Robber.
