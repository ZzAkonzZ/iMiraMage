Chapter 432:

Heaven's 5th Heaven, Matei

Giants born from the Elixir of Life return to Matei after understanding the law through a sacred ceremony in Jotunheim.

As "keepers of the law," their main duty is to maintain order in Heaven and fight external forces.

Accompanied by Uriel, Kariel looked up at the massive castle towering over Matei.

At the appearance of the noble archangel, the 15-meter-tall giants who had reached the 4th stage of the Elixir of Life bowed their heads.

They bent as much as they could, but their enormous bodies couldn't even come close to Kariel's 2.3-meter height.

Kariel scoffed and passed through the main gate.

He was already in a foul mood.

The Giant King Ymir had entered hibernation after destroying the "parts," which was understandable.

But at the very least, the legion commander, Girshin, should have come out barefoot to bow his head.

"These insolent…!"

Heaven's current state was a mess.

Since Shirone, the light of Sector 73, broke the Elixir of Life, the citizens began to doubt Anke Ra, and everything changed.

Kariel wanted to fly to purgatory immediately to crush the rebels, but he couldn't protect himself without Uriel.

'Just wait. I'll end this soon.'

If the structure of the Great World War was perfect, Matei's castle was all about grandeur.

It was built purely from natural materials, stacked by the giants' strength, without any influence from Mecha, Nor, or Kergos.

The massive pillars, which only giants could transport, revealed their mindset.

As they passed through the hall and climbed the central staircase, which was as high as a wall, they were greeted by giant guards standing tall up to the ceiling.

A force capable of destroying the world.

But they never moved until Ymir gave the order.

"What brings the great archangel to such a humble place?"

A 3-meter-tall giant, possibly the smallest here, approached, dragging a black cloak.

Though not large, his steps were heavy enough to make the floor tremble.

Girshin, the legion commander who had reached the 7th stage of the Elixir of Life.

If Ymir was like a natural rock, Girshin was like a sword tempered a thousand times.

"Legion Commander Girshin greets the archangel."

Girshin immediately knelt on one knee and bowed his head before Kariel.

Even a warrior capable of splitting mountains with a single blow couldn't surpass the law's established hierarchy.

Kariel found this displeasing.

As one of the eight archangels, he had nothing to boast about in terms of strength, so he felt he could see through the minds of those who worshipped power.

"Does reaching the 7th stage make you sluggish? You only moved 100 meters while I came all this way."

Girshin raised one corner of his mouth.

"Giants don't have the infinite spirit of angels. If you had informed me in advance, I would have come to greet you."

Girshin's response was certainly different from Ymir's.

A giant with some cunning knew that nitpicking over past events was a waste of time.

"I came here personally to give you an order."

"An order?"

"Lead the giant army and crush the rebels immediately."

Girshin's brow furrowed.

He had heard rumors of a conflict among the angels in the Great World War.

But acting independently, ignoring Anke Ra's command to forbid angelic activities, was another form of rebellion.

"But Anke Ra…"

"That's why I came to you. Anke Ra's command only applies to angels. Moreover, the rebels are currently exterminating giants born from the Elixir of Life on sight. The giant army has enough justification to move."

Girshin realized he was in a situation with no way out.

But he had no intention of moving the giant army.

They only followed Ymir.

They obeyed Anke Ra's orders because Ymir did.

"I'm sorry. I cannot comply."

Kariel's face twisted wickedly.

The sound of the guards swallowing echoed like a drum, but Girshin's expression remained calm.

"You're refusing? Are you serious?"

Kariel opened the "Great Code," the sacred relic of the Angel of Creation.

It was a blatant threat, implying that the next words would be a matter of life and death.

"You know this, Kariel. The giant army cannot move without King Ymir's command…"

"Argh! Insolent giant!"

Kariel closed the Great Code and struck Girshin's head with it madly.

Screaming and flailing his arms, Kariel's face showed no angelic dignity.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

Even with his weakened strength, an archangel's attack was not to be ignored.

Blood trickled down Girshin's forehead as he endured silently.

Uriel, who had been watching with folded arms, thought about the sudden change in the angel he had known from the beginning.

'This isn't like you, Kariel.'

Kariel, the Archangel of Creation, was smashing Girshin's head with the Great Code.

'Do you hate humans that much? No, you must know by now. Your current state is like…'

Uriel snapped out of his thoughts.

A terrifying killing intent was rising from the guards lined up on either side.

Though their rank was lower, giants' pride in their species was as strong as any angel's.

Seeing their legion commander subjected to humiliating violence in front of his subordinates naturally filled them with rage.

Thud.

One giant stepped forward, ready to intervene, but Uriel's halo expanded.

Realizing this, Girshin broke his calm expression and glared fiercely at his subordinate.

"Ugh…"

Overwhelmed by the legion commander's gaze, the guard sheepishly returned to his place.

Each strike of the Great Code cracked the floor and caused the ground to sink, but Girshin took no action.

'Now is not the time to act. Even if we all attack, we can't defeat Uriel, the Angel of Destruction.'

Known for his combat power, Uriel was said to rival Ikael at her peak.

'We must endure until our king, Ymir, arrives.'

Kariel, his hand trembling with fatigue, stepped back.

His halo dimmed, and he felt as if his thought circuits were melting.

"Huff, huff."

Girshin said nothing, merely watching his blood drip onto the floor.

To Kariel, who craved creation, it was a repulsive sight.

"Let's go!"

As Kariel turned and walked away, Uriel finally showed some emotion as he looked at the giants.

The presence of an angel, as defined by the law, made the giant's legs tremble like mountains.

"Restrain yourself, Girshin. You are not Ymir."

Uriel's body, with wings of light, rose beautifully and transformed into a flash, flying towards the main gate.

Gaold's face, now white-haired, was twisted into a terrifying grimace.

A powerful air gun spread shrapnel, piercing all forty Grim Reapers.

Suddenly, the ice vibrated, and the Grim Reapers' forms turned to smoke like raging flames.

Woooooo!

As their hoods disappeared, hideous skulls were revealed.

The ghostly wails, maddening to hear, caused the guide to clutch his head and collapse.

"Aaah!"

He should never have come here.

Black Elixir and a luxurious life were mere fantasies to those who heard this sound.

"Please… stop!"

At the guide's desperate cry, Kangnan sprang into action.

Her expression was still grim, her mind paralyzed by memories of an incident ten years ago.

'That fool!'

A Grim Reaper, emitting a vivid purple aura, swung its scythe wildly at Kangnan.

Each slash surpassed the speed of sound.

Though composed of "matter," they operated on principles entirely different from the physical world.

Dodging with agile movements like a bouncing ping-pong ball, Kangnan kicked the black horse's leg.

The thick leg snapped, and black smoke billowed like flames.

Screeeee!

The horse's scream pierced her eardrums, but Kangnan gritted her teeth and endured.

She looked around for Gaold, but the rampaging black horses made the world feel shrouded in darkness.

"I can't take this anymore!"

As Kangnan pushed herself up, her stockings tore at the thighs, creating a pattern of holes.

Just as the Grim Reaper's scythe came down vertically, her body shot up 20 meters into the air, as if escaping gravity.

She saw Gaold buried among the Grim Reapers. Each time their forms turned to smoke, a metallic flash slashed at Gaold's location.

The magic protecting Gaold was a simple air shield, something even beginners could cast, but no attack penetrated the sphere he had created.

"Ugh!"

As Gaold's face twisted like a demon's, Kangnan's heart sank.

"No!"

The air pressure around Gaold suddenly spiked.

Pain Sensation 100,000x - Air Pressing.

Rumble!

The ice shook like an earthquake, and the Grim Reapers' smoky forms were flattened like rubber.

"Huff!"

Gaold puffed his cheeks and exhaled deeply.

During this time, the crushed Grim Reapers slithered away and reformed at a distance.

In Niflheim, even the slightest chance to escape allowed for instant regeneration.

Kangnan, now within the Grim Reapers' encirclement, quickened her pace and shouted angrily.

"Are you crazy? Do you have a death wish…?"

Gaold's eyes, glazed over, faintly smiled.

Seeing his face, Kangnan couldn't bring herself to scold him.

The fact that he could smile was absurd.

"Don't come closer. You almost ruined everything."

"Don't talk nonsense. If you die here, everything ends. Don't you know that?"

"Don't worry. It's different from ten years ago."

Kangnan couldn't hold back and shouted, "It was dangerous just now!"

Self-replicating mutation.

Gaold's condition, diagnosed on the Day of Judgment for the 20.

A creature's functional limits are determined by its environment.

Humans burn because they are born in a place without fire, while Salamanders don't burn because they are born in fire.

If the environment sets the limits, Gaold had severed the link between himself and the environment through extreme suffering.

Without an environment, there are no limits to the mutations occurring at the stem cell level.

Even in daily life, Gaold's nervous system was at least a thousand times more sensitive than a normal person's, making his world entirely different from that of other humans.

It was literally hell.

Simply breathing felt like inhaling glass shards, and a breeze felt like his skin was being torn apart.

But in return, he gained unparalleled endurance and unimaginable mental strength.

"You know it's extremely dangerous beyond 10,000 times pain sensation! Your hair is already turning white, and you're losing your memory. If you push it further, you'll really die!"

"I won't die."

Gaold turned coldly and walked away.

"Pain alone won't kill me."

Kangnan clenched her fists and bit her lip.

But eventually, she bowed her head and slowly stepped back, resigned.

She knew the truth.

It wasn't the pain that would kill him; it was the inability to die that caused the suffering.

She didn't know why Gaold was cursed like this.

One thing was certain: only he could instantly annihilate forty of the strongest spirits.

Gaold's glazed eyes gradually returned to normal.

With a smile back on his face, he taunted the Grim Reapers with a flick of his finger.

"Come on, you pathetic skulls."

Woooooo!

Though they couldn't understand his words, all the Grim Reapers expanded their auras as if on cue.

The screams of countless souls echoed through the snowy landscape, and the purple aura swirled like a vortex.

The vortex transformed into thousands of skulls, all aimed at Gaold.

The sky darkened, the sun obscured, and thousands of skull bombs rained down on Gaold from the darkness.

"Heh. Heh heh heh!"

Gaold gritted his teeth and lowered his stance.

With a wicked grin, his pupils disappeared.

Pain Sensation 100,000x - Air Pressing.

Boom!

Everything crashed down, and the massive darkness was crushed with a deafening roar.
