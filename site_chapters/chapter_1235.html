Chapter 1235:

“Your Majesty.”

The corpse of Areon arrived at the Mikhas Kingdom.

His right-hand man, Fisque, was nowhere to be seen. What returned was only the cold, lifeless body of his son.

“Wha… what is this…?”

King Delkido of Mikhas rose from the throne, struggling to move his powerless legs.

“Huh…?”

When Areon’s pale face was revealed, several ministers collapsed to their knees, and the sound of a maid’s sobbing filled the hall.

The prince’s brothers remained composed.

‘So arrogant all the time… and now this.’

No one could know what they truly felt, but to a father witnessing the death of his firstborn, it was a different matter entirely.

“Ooooh… oooooh…”

Forgetting all kingly decorum, Delkido knelt and clutched Areon’s corpse, then raised his head to the heavens.

“OOOOOH!”

Was it a cry of grief?

“OOOOOH!”

Or the ultimate expression of wrath against whoever had struck Mikhas where it hurt most?

“Seize them.”

Delkido’s face twisted into a demonic snarl.

“Seize them all!”

Everyone in the grand hall bowed their heads in unison and responded as one.

“Yes, Your Majesty!”

The kingdom began to move.

Even in his unconscious dream state, Shirone instinctively sensed it.

That all Yorahan had wanted to convey through the story of his life… was meant for this exact moment.

The First Yora.

One who discovered love within the original sins of consumption and reproduction.

And with that single truth, he became the god of the Yora Faith in human form.

I must witness this.

This is a story.

“Armand!”

A story of tragic love between a man and a woman—one that would later be erased from the records of Omega.

“Yorahan!”

Through a forest set ablaze, armed forces surged forward as the two reached for one another.

“Hyah! Hyah!”

But as the Mikhas cavalry split in two, their line of sight grew more and more distant.

Damn it!

To capture the one who killed his son, King Delkido had mobilized the entire Mikhas military.

No one knew whether he loved his son enough to forsake a conquest war, but…

In his obsession with revenge, one could glimpse a wounded monarch’s pride.

Yorahan, bound and thrown into a mobile prison cell, crawled to the barred window.

Once again, a Fireborn settlement—one they had moved so many times—was being reduced to ashes.

Why…

Why were humans so relentless?

King Delkido subjected Yorahan and the Fireborn to every form of torment imaginable.

Yorahan was imprisoned in solitary confinement.

With all the tendons in his limbs severed, he was left to listen each day to the screams echoing from outside.

“Why…?”

Tears fell.

How can anyone be this cruel? How can a being inflict something so horrific upon another?

Sure, tearing someone else’s nails out didn’t make your own hurt…

But it wasn’t impossible to imagine it.

We can never truly know another person’s truth. And so humanity remains trapped within the prison of ‘self’…

All they can do is act in self-interest.

During his time in prison, Yorahan pondered the selfishness inherent in the concept of “I.”

Love that escapes the self…

He had abandoned his dream of changing the world. Perhaps now it wasn’t even possible. But with his limbs ruined, all he could do was think.

One day, the door to his cell creaked open, and King Delkido finally reappeared.

“You stubborn bastard.”

Delkido’s gaunt eyes gleamed with a murky madness.

“Even after hearing the Fireborn scream every day, you still haven’t gone mad? Well, not that I mind.”

He tightened a whip in his hands.

“Do you know why I spared your eyes, ears, and tongue even after severing your limbs? I wanted you to howl in despair, to feel even a sliver of the agony I’ve endured.”

A scream rang out from the hallway.

Yorahan no longer held any hope that anyone had survived. He just wished for them to die painlessly.

“All this for the revenge of one man? Isn’t it enough? Your son killed Fireborn too.”

“RAAAAGH!”

Delkido lashed his whip.

“You dare compare my son to them? He was a prince! Their lives aren’t worth a speck compared to his!”

Yorahan had no way to block or dodge, but he looked the king straight in the eye.

“All life is equal in value. The pain you feel from losing your family—the Fireborn feel the same. Why can’t you consider their hearts?”

“Why should I?!”

Delkido tossed the whip aside and kicked Yorahan.

“My son is dead! What does any of that matter?! My son died! My son!”

Me, me, me.

Can humanity never escape from that?

When Yorahan didn’t respond, Delkido stood over him, breathing hard, glaring.

“Heh…”

He would do anything—anything—to make this infuriating man feel despair.

“You’ll stay calm to the end, is that it? You and your wife are exactly alike. Fine. I’ll show you something myself.”

At his order, a cart was wheeled into the cell—carrying a large flower pot.

“…No…”

Yorahan’s expression went blank.

Planted in the pot was not a flower… but Armand, the woman he loved most.

“Well? Like it? Bonsai, I think it’s called? I cut off what I didn’t like, stiffened her joints so she couldn’t move. The most beautiful flower in the world.”

To treat a human like a plant…

“Yorahan.”

Upon seeing her husband’s face, a warm smile touched Armand’s lips—before her eyes turned sorrowful.

His body is so damaged…

They didn’t need Small World Resonance. Just a glance, and they knew what was in each other’s hearts.

Yorahan asked, “Are you okay?”

“Yes.”

Armand nodded bravely.

To provoke death was the only way to win against Delkido.

“But I’m getting tired of this.”

Delkido picked up a large pair of pruning shears beside the cart.

“Time for a change.”

Before Yorahan could even speak, Delkido began cutting into Armand’s body.

“How’s that? Beautiful, right?”

Powerless to stop it, Yorahan found a question forming deep within him.

Where did it all go wrong? If he hadn’t killed Areon, would none of this tragedy have happened?

No… I protected my family. I had no choice…

But then again… Was it still just… me?

‘Even I cannot abandon myself—such is fate. But if things continue this way, nothing will ever change. The endless cycle of pain, the revolving door of suffering… to break free from it—’

At that moment, Yorahan’s consciousness expanded at the speed of light, transcending himself and embracing all of humanity.

“Ah…”

Tears streamed down Yorahan’s cheeks.

‘So that’s what it is…’

Love is…

“Hrk! Hhhhuuuh!”

Hearing Yorahan sob, Delkido sneered and turned around.

“Ha! How is it? Now you finally feel at least a little of what I—”

The king froze.

Because though Yorahan cried with his entire face twisted in anguish… the corners of his mouth were lifted.

“…Has he gone mad?”

“Armand,” Yorahan said gently.

“Let’s forgive them.”

Armand’s eyes widened in disbelief, and Delkido, as if he misheard, blinked and asked again.

“…What?”

“Forgive everything. Forgive the world. Let’s end it all here. Let us be the ones to end it.”

Armand held the words in her heart, then smiled sadly.

“I see…”

Finally, they were ready.

“What are you talking about, you lunatic?!”

Furious, Delkido whipped at Yorahan, though his jaw trembled.

‘What am I afraid of… this man?’

Because Yorahan’s expression—his absence of malice—was foretelling something to come.

“Ha…”

Yorahan laughed.

“Ahahaha! Hahahahahaha!”

It wasn’t the laugh of a madman—it was too clear, too free. Delkido instinctively stepped back.

“What are you? What are you trying to do?!”

“Hmm…”

Yorahan stopped laughing and said calmly,

“Is this too fake?”

Delkido shouted, “Kill him! Kill this man now!”

“Then…”

Yorahan slowly closed his eyes.

‘Truly.’

With not a trace of deceit or hesitation, filled with blind sincerity—

“Let’s really forgive them.”

When he opened his eyes again, Yorahan’s expression had transformed into the pinnacle of bliss—a joy no human face could naturally display.

Ahahahahahaha!

His face twisted into a grotesque smile—contorted purely to express joy.

“St-stop…”

It was the embodiment of the concept of love.

Ahahahahahaha!

“AAAAAHHHH!”

Delkido, not knowing what terrified him, dropped to the ground and covered his ears, screaming.

“Please! I’m sorry! I was wrong! Please—!”

Then, as if struck by something, Delkido’s back arched, and light burst from his eyes, nose, and mouth.

“OOOOOOOH!”

It started with him.

The guards in the solitary block, too, emitted light from their faces.

“Aaaaah! No—no, stop!”

They were being purified.

Extreme emotions become demons, but supreme elevation transforms into Dreamo, becoming divinity.

Ahahahahahaha!

Yorahan smiled joyfully… as blood-tears streamed from his eyes.

‘What is love?’

His organs twisted, blood poured from every orifice, his intestines snapped apart.

‘This… is love.’

To embrace it all, to bear it wholly. It was the highest form of self-sacrifice, a transcendent madness.

“Yorahan…”

Unlike the others who screamed and fled in terror, Armand stood still with a sad smile.

“Goodbye.”

And through Dreamo, Yorahan’s emotion flooded into her as well.

The will to forgive everything.

That heart spread to all Fireborns… and even to the people of the Mikhas Kingdom.

“OOOOOOOH!”

When the expanding ring of light centered on the prison swallowed the entire kingdom—

“Lord Luber!”

The Dreemo system crashed.

“This dream is beyond our capacity to handle. If this continues, all humanity will—!”

They would be unified… by love.

“Hmph…”

Luber watched.

The chaotic world of dreams, made up of scattered thoughts and emotions, was being swept away by a massive flash of light.

He had to make a choice.

“Follow me. There’s no time.”

Luber and Monga pushed through the tide of light and entered Yorahan’s consciousness.

Beside the smiling manifestation of Yorahan stood Armand.

Small World Resonance.

Luber arrived where two dreams had fused into one, and approached Yorahan.

“You’ve done something no human can do… You truly forgave.”

“But it’s time to stop. If you go any further, everyone will be trapped in your dream.”

Armand said,

“Yorahan has only one thing left inside him. He won’t listen even to me.”

“But you can. Because you are Yorahan… and Yorahan is you.”

“…Why should I?” she asked. “If he’s become something like a god, then answer me: Yorahan forgave the world. Now, everyone will live in his dream. A world with no pain, no despair. Isn’t that enough?”

“But it’s not everyone’s life. It’s Yorahan’s life. Do you really think… that’s what he would want?”

Armand choked back tears.

“Why does Yorahan always have to sacrifice himself? Can’t we just… let him be, for once?”

“All right.”

Yorahan’s voice turned their heads.

“You…”

He returned with a calm smile, his face glowing with a sacred light.

“You’re right. I can’t impose my dream onto everyone. It’s time for me to step back.”

Armand shook her head.

“No… you finally fulfilled your dream. If you give that up, then I… I…”

She knew in her heart her husband had already died in the real world.

“In that case, I have one condition,” Yorahan said.

“If someone…”

He paused, then smiled.

“If there is someone who shares my dream—then please… pass on my entire life to them.”

Thus began the Legacy Dream.
