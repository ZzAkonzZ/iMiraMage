Chapter 847: At the Edge (2)

“Yaaah!”

Freed climbed vertically up the main body of Argantis with his sword hanging down.

Blue electricity sparked from his left hand, tracing an arc as it scraped the surface of the Makai Tree.

Boom! Boom! Boom!

Explosions erupted, grotesquely crushing the mouths spewing the vile liquid.

‘This isn't enough.’ He had to seal the top of Argantis.

Maximizing the mobility of his schema, he caught up with the speed of the regurgitation, just as a roar was heard.

“Kill him!”

Demons fell from the sky, thrusting red spears.

Freed blocked with his sword, his legs lifting off the ground before he fell back down.

“Grrr!”

He stabbed his sword into the flowing shell of the Makai Tree and clenched his left fist.

Bright flames, like molten lava, flared up.

‘Watch this.’

At first, he swung his sword just to remember his son.

As he gradually learned to endure the sorrow, he became a magic swordsman proficient in both swordsmanship and magic.

“Take this.”

The flames exploded in mid-air.

“Kieee!”

The demons melted, and Freed ascended to the top, moving against the falling fireballs.

‘Is it there?’

The laser from the seeker was pointing at the center.

‘No time!’

And at that exact spot, Argantis began to tremble, preparing to explode.

“Whew!”

As he gathered electricity in his left hand, the surrounding charges collided, creating a thunderous lightning strike.

Boom!

The powerful lightning struck the top of Argantis, eliciting a chilling scream.

Hohohohoho!

As he seared the surface with electricity to seal it, the top of Argantis swelled like a balloon.

Freed increased the power even more.

“Argh!”

Finally, the regurgitating liquid began to flow downward, and the swollen area started to shrink.

‘Success!’

The worst-case scenario of acid rain was averted.

“Ugh?”

But as he stopped Argantis's vomiting, an unexpected situation arose.

“Ugh! Ugh! Ugh! Ugh!”

A repulsive sound came from Argantis's mouth, and it began to shake violently.

“This is…”

It was like a strangled human twisting their body with all their might in their final moments.

Clang! Clang! Clang! Clang!

The rings of Xing's Exorcism of Three Thousand Worlds, which were hooked at the top, broke, causing the seeker’s targeting point to shift.

Xing, who was on the ground, groaned.

“Ugh!”

She was doing her best to hold on, but this time it was beyond her control.

Minerva's voice was heard.

“The targeting point has shifted.”

When Xing turned her head, she saw Shirone standing next to Minerva, his face pale.

“What happened?”

As God's punishment neared the ground, Shirone's consciousness returned.

But the acceleration was so high that laser guidance was no longer possible.

“At this rate, we can't hit it. If it misses even slightly, the shockwave will hit the ground.”

“…We can't stop it?”

“There is still one way.”

As Shirone walked past Xing, Minerva followed him.

“What are you going to do…!”

The moment she saw Yahweh's light flaring up like flames from Shirone's body, she stopped.

“I will twist the law.”

Something only Yahweh could do, not Nane.

But even for Shirone, catching a 10-ton object moving at Mach 40 was a daunting challenge.

“You could die.”

Xing said.

“The law is a seesaw game. If you can't handle it, your law will be destroyed instead.”

As the world's foremost authority on the law, her words were likely true.

Freed flew down from the sky.

“Damn it! It's one thing after another! What now?”

Looking up at him, Shirone smiled.

“It’s okay.”

It wasn't about whether he could do it or not.

“It doesn't matter who is weak or strong anymore. We're fighting together.”

Xing's face turned slightly red.

‘Fighting together?’

The only thing Yahweh desired.

Shirone was willing to sacrifice himself.

‘Transcendence of the mind.’

Yahweh was undoubtedly one of the highest realms one could reach as a Bodhisattva.

‘I can do it. I will change the trajectory.’

The light surrounding Shirone's body flared up, large enough to engulf the entire city.

“What is that?”

Amanta turned his head, and the people were filled with awe at the light filling the sky.

“Ah, ah…”

They didn't know what meaning was contained in that light, but it was the one thing they sought in the midst of hell.

“Grrr!”

The moment Shirone mentally grasped God's punishment falling towards the ground, his face contorted.

‘It's much harder than I thought.’

If it were just about pushing it away with powerful law, it would be possible, but this required precise targeting.

Veins bulged on Shirone's temples, and the blood vessels in his eyes began to burst.

As blood tears streamed down his face, Minerva grabbed his shoulders and shouted.

“No! You'll die!”

Shirone couldn't hear her.

‘Just a little more!’

He had never wanted to succeed so badly.

‘This is the result of everyone fighting together.’

The Five Great Stars of the Ivory Tower were those who had upheld their beliefs without compromise.

If such people were all gathered here, there was still hope for humanity.

‘I want to succeed. I have to…’

Shirone's body trembled in shock as blood flowed from his eyes, nose, and ears.

And at that moment.

‘It’s moving.’

The 10-ton spear, which had stubbornly fallen, began to change its trajectory slightly.

“Grrr!”

As Shirone's pupils rolled back, Minerva's face turned pale.

“Shirone! Stay with us!”

Shirone's eyes snapped open.

‘Here!’

As the trajectory of God's punishment was corrected, it was set to pierce Argantis's body vertically.

Exhausted, Shirone collapsed to his knees, gasping for breath.

“Hah! Hah!”

“Did it work? Did you succeed?”

Mentally exhausted to the point of death, Shirone lifted his head and forced a smile.

“Yes. For now…”

“It’s not over yet.”

Xing said in a sorrowful voice.

“It’s moving again.”

As Shirone turned to look ahead with a grim expression, Argantis was making its final desperate struggle.

Hohohohoho!

Hidden by the clouds, countless branches spread out from the top of the Makai Tree, shaking.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

The air burst, and the world seemed to be ending as the deafening noise pounded their eardrums.

“What is it trying to do?”

Freed asked, and Minerva answered.

“It has no will. It's just struggling with all its might. But at this rate, the trajectory will be off.”

If the angle changed even slightly, God's punishment would pierce through Argantis's body and crash into the ground.

“We have to cut those branches.”

It was the only way, but there was no time to return after calculating the fall time.

Freed rested his sword on his shoulder and said,

“I’ll go.”

“You…”

Seeing Minerva's shocked expression, he gave a bitter smile.

“I have to do it. There's no way I can return in time or cut those branches.”

Someone had to sacrifice themselves.

“Freed…”

“I’m going.”

Just as Freed was about to leap, a familiar voice came from behind Shirone.

“It’s too high to see.”

As everyone turned hurriedly, Freed asked,

“…Who are you?”

A young man with blue hair, carrying a large sword on his back, shaded his eyes with his hand and looked up at the sky.

Shirone couldn't believe his eyes.

“Rian?”

Rian squinted one eye and smiled.

“Looks like you're in trouble, Shirone.”

Countless thoughts flooded Shirone's mind at once, but he quickly grasped the core.

“There’s no time! If we don’t move quickly…”

“Don’t worry.”

As Rian stepped forward, placing a hand on Shirone's shoulder, Freed's brow furrowed.

“Do you know what you're saying?”

“Probably…”

Rian looked up at the sky's edge and then kicked off the ground, charging at a terrifying speed.

“Cut it, I guess.”

Xing shouted.

“Hurry! I can't hold on any longer!”

The rings of the Exorcism of Three Thousand Worlds shook violently and began to crack.

“It’s coming.”

Minerva looked up at the sky and spoke.

“We can't make it in time.”

Rian was climbing Argantis at a terrifying speed, but God's punishment was falling faster than expected.

As he raced vertically towards the sky, Rian spotted a burning point of light in the sky.

“Is it around here?”

With a cracking sound, his muscles twisted, transforming into the body of a Yaksha capable of withstanding the impact.

“What is he trying to do?”

As Xing muttered, Rian kicked off the trunk and leaped out.

“Grrr!”

And at that moment, the Five Great Stars realized.

“That crazy…”

There wasn't enough time to cut the branches of the Makai Tree.

“He’s going to cut the whole thing?”

Rian's waist twisted to its limit as he gripped the large sword with both hands.

“Whew!”

He was falling from about two-thirds of Argantis's height.

Though much thinner than the lower part, it was still over 100 meters thick.

“Yaaah!”

Divine Transcendence, Idea.

If the capital of Kashan were a painting, Rian's sword was a streak of light cutting across the canvas from end to end.

Crack.

The eyes of the Five Great Stars widened as the upper part of Argantis, which had risen to the boundary of the clouds, began to fall.

“He… cut that?”

Shirone clenched his fists.

“Rian.”

He was the strongest Yaksha.

As Rian, having delivered his decisive blow, fell while standing, he looked down at Shirone.

“So don't worry, Shirone. I'm here now.”

Behind Rian, a gray spear traveling at Mach 40 plunged vertically down.

Boom.

The moment the spear tip touched the center of the Makai Tree.

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

A sound louder than thunder roared underground, and the massive body began to explode.

Argantis let out a desperate wail.

Kraaa!

As the spear pierced through its body and buried itself underground, the ground rippled like waves, and the shockwave severed the roots.

Most humans cowered in fear, covering their heads, but the Five Great Stars stared in awe at the magnificent scene.

“We stopped it.”

Minerva murmured.

“We stopped it.”

Rumble!

Dust the size of a city spread widely in concentric circles, riding the waves of the ground.

“Rian.”

Shirone stared intently through the dust.

Although Argantis had been pierced, the shockwave from God's punishment had hit nearby.

“Rian!”

Just as he shouted, thinking it was impossible for Rian to be unharmed, a shadow emerged from the dust.

“Phew, that was no joke. Shirone, was that your magic?”

Rian, with his clothes in tatters and the large sword on his back, appeared.

‘No injuries?’

To be precise, his limbs had been torn off by the shockwave and then regenerated.

“You… are you okay?”

As Shirone and the Five Great Stars watched in astonishment, Rian arrived in front of them.

“Are there no enemies left?”

Confirming that all the demons had disappeared, he finally turned to Shirone and spoke.

“It's been a while, Shirone.”
