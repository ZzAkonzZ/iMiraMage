Chapter 940: Fanaticism (4)

Upon Blitz's suggestion, the eleven apostles listening did not raise any objections.

Whether proving their readiness to die or fighting against all twelve apostles, it seemed rational to them.

"One by one, they will attack me." Meanwhile, Miro felt uneasy. She wouldn’t just take it without retaliating, but once attacked, there was no counter.

"If I refuse... then it's real combat."

Fighting outright with the twelve apostles.

"I'd rather choose the latter."

Regardless of the outcome, what opportunity would a magician have to experience such a battle again?

"But I need to head to the Central Continent right now. It's not the time to assert my pride."

Having made her decision, Miro raised her hands.

"Alright, hit me hard. I’ll take it." Zulu approached.

"Miro."

"Stand back. You know this. He’s not someone to provoke by breaking rules so sloppily."

Since it was true, Zulu gave up her argument and prepared for any eventuality.

"The twelve apostles. Once they stood against the archangels. At the very least, I need to create a way out..."

She had flown long distances using Kaidra but still had enough mental energy to cast one more Postmetry.

It was uncertain if it would work on the twelve apostles.

"What are you doing? After singing Shirone’s praises, have you learned altruism now? Come in." Blitz looked back.

"Who wants to go first?"

"I will."

The woman with red hair, half of it shaved as if clipped off, and three piercings on her left lip approached.

Wearing leather tights that revealed her curvaceous figure and with a long zipper running down the center line of her body, she caught Miro’s attention.

"Probably…

Infercus the Fire Dragon.

She had been the model for the last of the ten levels in Shirone’s graduation exam.

How many spectators had cursed the developers who programmed Infercus for being perverted.

"It's not perverted. It’s almost merciful."

Though she had transformed into a human form, the aura she exuded in real life was beyond what simulations could replicate.

"This monster is going to hit me?"

Infercus jumped down below the rocks, then suddenly stopped and looked back.

"I’m going first, any objections?" Phaethon the Light Dragon taunted.

"What are you going to do if there are objections? Just throw a fit again if you’re not first?" Infercus resumed walking.

"That’s why I asked. Better now than later."

Approaching Miro, she said,

"Let's make this quick. We’re both busy."

Miro’s cheeks trembled as she smirked, and the avatar of the Thousand-Armed Guanyin unfolded.

The apostles looked up at the towering figure of Guanyin.

All being masters of combat, they realized…

"Definitely tricky to tackle."

Poine the Poison Dragon spoke in an old woman’s tender voice,

"When fighting an unknown opponent, you usually start with instincts. Either open with what you do best or target what seems to be the opponent’s weakness. But in front of this Guanyin...

Poine chuckled as if amused.

"She doesn’t even let you think."

There was no angle.

No matter which criterion you threw at her, she was perfect from every direction, making you not even want to attack first.

Zulu muttered,

"Full projection."

It was the sight of the Thousand-Armed Guanyin looking down upon the universe from the top of the cosmos.

"Anyway…"

Infercus raised her fist before her.

"Just do it, right?"

And as she spread her fingers, a gigantic stream of fire twisted through the air, the heat threatening to scorch their skin.

Miro took a deep breath.

"It's okay. It’s not a breath."

There were no set rules, but it seemed Infercus had no intention of undoing her transformation.

"Here I go."

From the top of the sky, she manipulated a fiery orb blazing like the sun, taking a step forward.

"Krrraaaah!"

Miro's nose crinkled intensely. As if the world had turned upside down, a pillar of fire plummeted from the sky.

30 minutes later.

The place where the twelve apostles had been was no longer just rocky terrain.

Soil layers that must have been settling for tens of thousands of years were now exposed as Kangnan and Zulu rushed over.

"Miro!"

The two who arrived were at a loss for words.

Miro, kneeling on one knee and shaking uncontrollably, covered her face with both arms.

The sleeves burned away to reveal bloodied arms, and one foot was deeply buried in the ground.

"She survived."

Kangnan, who had observed all attacks from the twelve apostles, biting her lip until it bled, shuddered at the outcome.

"She held on. She really did."

Though she didn't like the woman much, moments like these evoked a sense of awe in her humanity.

"Miss Miro."

Using formal speech for the first time in a long while as she approached her.

"Aaaaah!"

Miro suddenly stood up, screaming.

"These damn lizard bastards! I’ll kill them all!"

As Miro extracted her buried foot and dashed off, Kangnan wrapped her arms around her waist.

"Hold on! Hold on!"

"Let go! I can't live like this after being beaten up! I’m going to pay them back!"

As Miro fell forward, the weight she carried told Kangnan she was in no condition to fight.

'Well, she just took it without fighting back.'

Without showing it, she checked on Miro, whose eyes were brimming with tears.

"Huuuh, it’s so bitter."

It was a rare sight to see the great Miro in tears, enduring the pain for the sake of humanity.

"It’s extremely noble."

Until now, Kangnan had resented Miro for not understanding Gaold’s heart, but suddenly.

"Because she’s that kind of woman."

Perhaps Gaold endured that horrific pain to help her.

Kangnan spoke softly.

"Let’s go to the Central Continent."

"......So that's how it happened."

Iruki, who had been listening blankly to Miro's story, finally came to his senses and spoke.

"So, you're late because you took hits from the 12 Apostles?"

"Yeah. I tried to come quickly, but every part of my body aches. I guess I'm getting old."

Miro, who had been pretending to be in pain, corrected her expression.

"Anyway, I heard about the elemental bomb. It’s definitely something Shirone's friend would come up with. Truly a surprising strategy."

A shadow fell over Iruki's face.

"If Shirone knew, he would have been angry."

Miro did not deny it.

"So, do you regret your choice now?"

"No. I'll do it. I've already made up my mind."

Miro nodded.

"Good. It's the only way to defeat evil, right? Humanity's power has been severely weakened by the world war. Let's give it a try. I'll help too."

The reason Miro could speak so easily about such a terrible tragedy was because she was also one of the victims.

"So, there's something suspicious."

Iruki pointed to the battlefield map.

"As long as Meirei is there, no information will leak out. The logs of the Earth Dragon are only passed down to Shirone. Until then, they remain in eternal sleep, so no one can access them."

"Hmm. And?"

"The strategy is going as planned. But how should I put it? There's resistance? There are such reactions."

Iruki tightly grasped his index finger with his other hand.

"It's like feeling the pulse of a blood vessel; there's something throbbing inside the enemy."

"You mean they've noticed?"

"Not quite that much, but it's unsettling. Havitz has two paths: bypassing Tormia and going straight ahead or detouring to attack Bashka. Going straight is convenient, and detouring gives them the toy called Bashka. I think even a slight shock would make the army turn towards Bashka. But the feeling of deviation is smaller than expected."

"Hmm. It's subtle, right? If you give a shock, there's resistance, but it doesn't seem like they're refusing to go to Bashka?"

"Yes. If you see the army as an organism, its current emotion is close to conflict. There may be internal disagreements or only a few have sensed something."

"Then now is the timing. The more they doubt, the harder it will be to lure them."

Iruki was glad that Miro didn't need an explanation.

"Yes. Here."

The command stick pointed north of the flower field.

"Create a shockwave here. It needs to deviate at least 11 degrees."

Miro chuckled.

"It's a metaphor, right?"

"Yes."

It's not a strategy that can be explained with just angles.

"I like that it's intuitive. Got it. With me, Zulu, and Kangnan, we can manage more than 11 degrees."

Miro stood up.

"Then, let's work hard."

As Iruki watched Miro's back, he asked,

"Why?"

Miro turned around but didn't speak for a while before finally saying,

"You don't say anything? You know evil must be hated. But good people die too. Why? Because there's no way? Because you'll die too?"

"They'd want to grab even at straws."

Iruki's eyes wavered.

"They'd want to hear something close to an answer. Even if it's from a madman. Or rather, they'd prefer being criticized thoroughly."

Miro walked as if taking a stroll.

"But no one talks to you; you're surrounded only by silent stillness."

Iruki's head slowly lowered.

"And that's my answer too."

When Iruki looked up again, Miro was looking at him with pity.

"I felt the same way when the Judgment of 20 convened; most people were silent."

Only Gaold kept shouting endlessly.

"It was sorrowful and unfair. Humans looked like monsters. But after being in the dimensional wall for 20 years, I suddenly thought maybe humanity was quiet for the first time then."

Silence.

"As much as I hate to admit it, there are problems without answers too. Believing that every problem has an answer is human arrogance. At that time, humanity and intelligence were pale with fear before questions without answers."

Miro smiled as if letting go of past sorrows.

"There was nothing to resent or get angry about from the start. If I had rejected the Judge’s decision back then, things would have gone differently again. Would humanity have perished then? Who knows? It's just a past that's already gone by."

Miro kissed Iruki on the crown of his head.

"Now you stand alone in an unknown world where no one has set foot before. It will be tough but don't be afraid. Wherever you go will become a path, and people will gather along that path to open a new world. Therefore, as one of humanity..."

Stepping back with her fingers on her lips, Miro extended them towards Iruki.

"I remain silent about your choice."

Iruki's fists clenched involuntarily.

"Thank you."

How blessed he was that someone who had experienced the unknown world existed in this world.

"Remember this: right now you are the master of this world."

As the door closed and Iruki calmly closed his eyes,

The silence Miro spoke of did not mean support or opposition or neutrality.

'The choice belongs only to me.'

Whatever he chose would be the first and from there a new future would be born.

"Whew."

As his overheated brain cooled down for a moment,

Iruki could finally get about three minutes of sleep for the first time in a while.
