Chapter 1232:

After Shirone’s story about the origin of good and evil in humanity ended, the group remained silent.

Iruki was lost in thought.

‘So that’s what it was…’

A Gaian enlightened in heart.

For those who could embody wholeness no matter what they did, good and evil, merit and grief—none of it would have been clearly distinguished.

‘Ultima was broken.’ It wasn’t a matter of what act was committed, but how that act was defined.

Shirone continued.

“What happened to Cain after that, I don’t know. Omega’s last record ends with him vanishing into a thick cloud of smoke. In the end, he left reality and fled into the Under Coder.”

Even if it wasn’t recorded, the Melkidu that Shirone’s group experienced had already revealed Cain’s path.

“He must’ve spent countless nights thinking and agonizing over it.”

Cain spoke.

“Where did it go wrong? No—was it even wrong at all? Why did I have to make that decision? Was it truly evil?”

The group listened in silence.

“The conclusion I came to was that everything is just a roll of the dice. I merely threw the dice—not even knowing what number would come out.”

That the cold world had chosen Abel, that Cain had stabbed his brother in the chest—that too was just a number.

“Yes, chaos. We are beings of chaos.”

Cain tapped his own head.

“Before I am myself, there’s a command that drives me. A signal from above—right at the top of this head. A special signal transmitted to us. An electrical spark… maybe even something a priori. What we call ‘impulse.’”

There is a missing link.

“In the end, humans are nothing more than puppets of signals descending from somewhere far beyond. Even the heart. That means we can’t do anything by ourselves—and therefore, we can’t change anything.”

It was the conclusion of evil concerning the Beron problem.

Shirone asked him,

“And does thinking that way make the guilt go away?”

“…That’s always the problem.” Cain turned to look at Lilith’s portrait.

“Melkidu is a world built on the methodology of evil. Countless sinners have gathered here over time. Though I’m trapped here, I’ve heard many stories. And whether their sins were committed knowingly or in a moment of weakness… in the end, all sinners think the same thing. That they wish that day had never happened.”

Because pleasure is fleeting.

“Of course, there are those who stay here because they like it. That kind of person wraps Melkidu in all kinds of grandiose words, treating it like something sacred…”

Cain looked at Curtis.

“Even if you don’t go to prison—even if the event is erased—it doesn’t mean the deed itself disappears.”

There is no sanctuary for demons.

Iruki spoke up.

“There’s one thing I don’t get. According to Shirone, Lilith was killed by Adam—so how did humanity’s lineage continue all the way to now?”

“Because she didn’t die.”

Shirone replied.

“Yes, Lilith died. But the seed of Mitochondrial Eve remained.”

A miracle.

Or perhaps… a curse.

RUMBLE!

On that night of thunder and lightning, Adam had returned to the village to check on his family.

As he made his way first to his wife’s home, a scream cut through the sound of pouring rain.

“What…?”

He changed course and arrived at Abel’s house—where the thick scent of blood hit him.

When he opened the door, he saw Abel’s corpse and Lulua in shock.

“Ah…?”

Lulua’s focus returned and she spoke.

“Dad…”

“What happened here?”

Though Adam was a Gaian who had fought in wars and was no stranger to death—not in Eden.

“B-big brother Cain… he…”

With trembling voice, Lulua explained what had happened.

As the truth settled in, a terrifying murderous intent flared in Adam’s Gaian eyes.

“Foolish boy…”

As he left the house, Adam said:

“Do not leave here tonight. When morning comes, go find your brothers.”

“Yes… I understand.”

Lulua nodded repeatedly, but the moment Adam disappeared, her expression turned cold.

“…What just happened?”

She held up her hands and examined her own body.

“Why me?”

Had she… become Lilith?

The shock of Abel’s death had triggered something—a flood of ancient memories surged into her mind.

‘These are memories from long ago… before Lulua was even born. But Lulua’s life is also engraved in my brain…’

She remembered how deeply she had loved Abel.

That’s how she’d been able to deceive Adam—but truthfully, she now doubted her own identity.

‘A Lulua with Lilith’s memories… or a Lilith with Lulua’s memories… which one am I?’

After a long silence, she stood.

“I am Eve.”

There is no such thing as a single identity.

She was the culmination of a lifeform that transcended generations, living through eternity itself.

‘Then what should I do now?’

Lost in thought with no answers, the sun eventually rose—but Adam never returned.

She went to Lilith’s house.

In the quiet stillness, her own corpse lay on the floor, with bodily fluids staining the ground and a broken neck.

“Haha.”

Perhaps Adam had killed her not simply because of what happened, but because Cain had disappeared.

“Ahahahaha! Hahahahaha!”

Tears streamed down her cheeks.

“It’s over.”

And so, while the era of Gaians in Eden came to a close—Eve’s life had only just begun.

She mingled among the siblings.

Sometimes modest, sometimes vivacious, she played countless roles and spread her descendants far and wide.

‘Forget it all.’

No matter whom Eve loved, it would always be incest—and that was no longer love, only foul desire.

‘Forget everything.’

As the generations passed, her descendants regressed into primitive forms, driven only by animalistic urges.

‘Love me! More!’

With each broken taboo, their ma grew stronger.

Her daughters became new Eves, spreading out across the world.

‘Pleasure! Sensation! Endless desire!’

The reason Mitochondrial Eve is passed only through women…

“Grrr… sniff, sniff!”

…is because they are all Eve.

"Then," Shirone said, "no matter where they were born, every human on this Earth is a child of Eve."

Iruki let out a long breath.

‘At this point, there are so many mutations that epidemiological tracing is meaningless…’ But conceptually, Eve is the mother of all humanity.

"Yeah. What Mother dreamed of was brilliant and beautiful love. But since all of humanity are her children, the only thing she could do was surrender to pleasure. And eventually… she met you. Shirone—you."

Everyone turned to look at Shirone.

"Hexa, you’re an exception. You’re the only human who isn’t a child of Eve. It’s contradictory—like a virgin giving birth—but it’s also a signal and proof sent by the god with a heart. To Mother, you are a special existence."

"So that’s why Uorin…"

The group understood.

Why the empress of Kashan was so obsessed with Shirone—and why Shirone kept avoiding her.

Adam, the Saint Brain, spoke.

"That gaze hasn’t changed."

As Kido kept his guard up, Uorin snapped with a venomous glare.

"Don’t talk to me like you know me."

Though faded through regression to a primal form, her memories of Eden returned as she evolved back into a human.

"You killed me. You saw my neck broken. What face do you have to show up in front of me again? No—"

Uorin smirked.

"Actually, you don’t have a face. What a pathetic excuse for life. Why keep living, looking like that?"

Adam turned to Kido.

"Is this goblin your lover now? You never did change your taste."

Uorin bared her teeth.

She was confident she wouldn’t lose to anyone—but ex-lovers were never easy to deal with.

"Don’t be ridiculous. I’m not who I used to be. Do you know how many generations I’ve lived through?"

"Of course I do. I became what I am now because I’ve also walked backward through human history—alongside you."

Adam had given up his body.

While Lilith had lived anew through Eve’s power, Adam had become a phantom, mimicking humans.

He had lived as men and women, politicians and beggars—experiencing Eve’s history through countless human forms.

"Why?"

Uorin asked.

"Why would you do that? You hated me. Why would you care what I did?"

"To take responsibility."

When he left Eden, Adam had intended to die.

"Lilith, you committed something you couldn’t take responsibility for. That’s the evil you created. But I couldn’t escape responsibility either."

Adam held out his hand.

"Come with me. The end of the world we created is approaching. Shouldn’t we do something for humanity, at least once?"

"No."

"If it’s because of Shirone, give it up. He won’t come to you. He knows what you did."

"Hmph, I don’t care. Do you know what I endured all these long, boring years for? I’d rather give my perfected body and power—honed over countless generations—to this goblin…"

Uorin wrapped her arm around Kido.

"Last warning. Never show yourself in front of me again."

"You have clear eyes."

Adam turned to Kido.

"Don’t fall in love with Lilith. That woman will drain everything from you. You’ll be left endlessly parched."

"Shut up."

Kido thrust his spear forward.

"I don’t care if you’re her ex-husband or whatever. I love her. Yeah, I’m a goblin. So what?"

The fact that he added that last part only proved how conscious he was of the ex-husband.

‘Damn it! I can’t help it!’ He was the man who had once had the heart Kido could never claim.

Adam chuckled.

"You still say that after seeing me? Impressive. Don’t worry. Lilith won’t care whether you’re a goblin or not."

"What?"

Kido didn’t understand.

"You—"

Without avoiding Uorin’s murderous gaze, Adam slowly stepped back.

"Lilith. If you can’t live for humanity, then leave with that goblin. The moment your history is handed over to Havitz, you’ll commit yet another sin."

Even if he hated Adam, Kido understood the gravity of his words.

Adam’s Gaian body disappeared, leaving only a floating gray brain that faded into the darkness.

"Phew."

As the tension lifted, Kido gently touched Uorin’s shoulder.

"Are you okay?"

"Don’t touch me."

Her cold voice made him withdraw slowly, after a moment of hesitation.

"Sorry."

Uorin found that even more irritating.

"Whatever you’re thinking, don’t let Adam’s words get to you. I’m not planning to do anything with a goblin."

Kido nodded.

"I know."

The thirst returned, but he embraced the gaping emptiness with his whole being.

‘I’m the one who loves her. At the very least… she doesn’t reject that.’

Kido didn’t know.

That his savagery and intelligence reminded Uorin of a past she wished she could forget.

But he could guess.

‘She’s been through a lot. Probably did things she’d never want anyone to know. But that’s okay.’

To Kido, Uorin was still the most beautiful and noble empress of Kashan in the world.

"Kido."

And the man that empress loved was…

"Take me to Shirone."

A human who shone so brightly, a monster like him didn’t even dare compete.

"Okay."

Kido answered in a husky voice, then smiled.

"Let’s go. To Shirone."
