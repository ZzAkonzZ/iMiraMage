Chapter 1095: The Final Holy War (1)

North Pole, Ivory Tower.

The tower was enormous, and in the small village on the 387th floor, about 80 people lived.

Inside a small hut, a woman who had been in a deep sleep slowly opened her eyes.

Ivory Tower resident, Bipa.

In High Gear, she was a tutorial manager using the gear mark.

"What's the point of a meeting?"

She had no intention of attending.

Nevertheless, the reason she logged out was because Yahweh's actions had moved her heart.

As she stepped out of the hut, other residents looked at her in surprise.

"Bipa? What's going on? You're coming outside?"

She had been holed up for months, stockpiling food and water, without stepping outside.

"What's the situation?"

The residents squinted their eyes but couldn't avoid revealing the significant event.

"See for yourself."

When they reached the outer wall they pointed to, the holographic curtain disappeared, revealing a mechanical iron wall.

At the 387th floor of the Ivory Tower, even the blizzards raging in the North Pole were beneath them.

She looked through the window.

At the horizon, flames that evaporated the cold were blazing in the Kingdom of Corona.

"Demons."

Click. Click.

Her pupils rotated like a camera aperture, zooming in on the scene of the Kingdom of Corona.

She saw the stars of the Ivory Tower and, among them, a demon swordsman slaughtering numerous soldiers.

The army of hell, led by the first legion commander, Baal.

'They've finally come.'

The fact that the only legion commander who hadn't opened the demon realm was targeting the North Pole was significant.

'Deadly Cross. That was probably the name.'

They intended to bring down the Ivory Tower.

Kashan. Aganos.

As Uorin, who had logged out of High Gear, left the bedroom, Gando bowed his head.

"You've arrived."

It was surprising and welcoming since she had been fully immersed in High Gear, neglecting state affairs.

"Is there anything special you need?"

"No."

Uorin smiled as she passed Gando.

"It's over. I've obtained everything I could from High Gear. How long until the Holy War?"

"Three days."

Gando followed behind, asking.

"If you've obtained everything…"

Walking side by side, Uorin's face turned cold as if nothing had happened.

'It's been a while since I felt this way.'

As Gando swallowed hard, Uorin's thoughts deepened.

'Minus universe.'

Although not everything, Fermi had provided a general overview as agreed.

'This could be the worst Holy War ever. No, perhaps this is our last…'

Uorin stopped walking.

"Gando, I will participate in the Holy War."

Gando immediately responded to the statement that she would reclaim the emperor's throne she had passed to her son.

"Understood."

"Don't be too disappointed. The survivors, forces, and kingdoms after the Great Purge, and the monsters, will all gather. It seems too much for you to handle alone."

Gando trembled with emotion, grateful that she considered his feelings.

"It's the Empress's empire."

A faint smile appeared on Uorin's lips but quickly turned cold.

'I will never give it up,' she said.

"Call Kido."

Southern Continent, Kessia.

Since most of the citizens were intoxicated with Angel, there weren't many shops open for business.

The place Shirone chose for the meeting was a franchise café run by Golden Wheel.

"Welcome."

As he opened the door and entered, a dazed-looking employee greeted Shirone.

He saw tables where nobles were seated, seemingly also under the influence.

'The problem is…'

The group sitting in the corner of the shop didn't look much different.

'This is quite a sight.'

Five High Gear operators had gathered for this meeting, and none made eye contact.

It was the expected scene, given that they were socially inept individuals, but it was still extreme.

"Oh? Could it be…?"

The only one with a bright expression, a little boy, raised his hand when he saw Shirone.

"Yahweh?"

"Lollipop?"

He knew some details about the operators but didn't reveal their real names.

"Wow, it's just like High Gear. Welcome. The atmosphere here is a total disaster."

Even with Shirone's arrival, the operators didn't even glance his way and continued their aimless actions.

'There's the lollipop.'

The middle-aged man with a hunched back and a beard was probably Smile Mark.

The thin woman with long hair seemed to be Black Circle Mark.

'And the other two…'

The ones who caused trouble in High Gear, Operator and Number Seven.

'Number Seven. Is he my age?'

Despite his sturdy build, his nervous leg shaking revealed his personality.

The operator sitting at the head of the table kept brushing her bangs with her hand.

"Ugh. Seriously."

'She must be worried about his acne.'

Shirone guessed the reason the operator's bangs covered her eyes.

"Alright then."

Sitting across from them, Shirone ordered tea from the employee and began to speak.

"Nice to meet you all. I'm Shirone."

No one responded, and Smile Mark turned his head as if he was about to say something.

'They really lack social skills. No opinions at all. How are we supposed to work together like this?'

They had to.

These were the developers of High Gear, who had achieved unprecedented success in Under Coder.

'The world's best coders.'

Lollipop Mark was the first to speak up.

“Let’s start with introductions. This is customary at in-person gatherings. I’m Lollipop. In High Gear, I handle impact and collision processing.”

It was a position requiring sharp reflexes.

“Well, should I go next? Uh… they call me Smile Mark. I’m in charge of…” He launched into a long-winded explanation, followed by Black Circle Mark, who succinctly said, “Black Circle. I manage the meta processor.”

By the time it was Number Seven’s turn, he seemed like he was suffering from a speech impediment, barely able to gather his thoughts.

“Your turn, big brother,” someone nudged him.

“Oh. Uh…” His ears were burning red, and his dilated pupils made it seem like his mind had wandered off to another realm. Shirone brought him back to reality. “Don’t worry, just speak casually. It’s just introductions.”

“Uh, well… um…” Number Seven snapped to attention but kept stealing glances at the Operator seated at the head of the table.

She pushed her bangs aside and snapped, “What’s with all the sneaking looks? Disappointed? Am I too ugly for you?”

“W-what? Who said that? You’re absolutely stunning!” He startled himself with his outburst, then bowed his head in embarrassment while the Operator calmly sipped her drink through a straw.

A long, awkward silence followed.

“Well… introductions are done for now.” Shirone pivoted quickly. “This gathering was my suggestion to the Operator. As some of you may already know, going forward, the Operator will assist me in a project in the real world.”

This statement piqued the administrators’ interest. “What project?”

Shirone explained their strategy for mining the Apocalypse from The Abyss's Domain. Black Circle murmured, “Hmm… a cytoplasmic calcification program. To inject it into the main system, you’d need to know the terminal patterns.”

Number Seven suggested, “We could use AI. Deploy massive excavation creatures to map the area in three dimensions and…”

“But the time it takes to breach the firewall is critical,” someone interjected. “If we embed invasion codes into the program…”

The previously silent administrators now erupted into passionate discussions, almost like a heated debate. Shirone smiled faintly.

‘Their bodies may be in the real world, but their minds are still entrenched in the virtual realm of Under Coder.’

Leaning her chin on her hand, the Operator raised a finger. “Anyway, as I was saying… I agreed to help Shirone. What about you guys?”

The administrators exchanged glances before Number Seven slowly raised his hand. “Ugh, I guess I’m obligated, huh?”

The Operator retorted, “No. Attending this meeting was the only requirement. If you don’t want to, you’re free to opt out.” When he started lowering his hand, she cut him off with a sharp remark, “Just kidding. After all the mess you caused, you think you can leave it like this? You’ve got thousands of tons to weld back together.”

Her unexpected joke brought a smile to Number Seven’s face. “Hah! Like anyone else could handle it. Without me, there’s no way you’re digging up the Apocalypse!”

“That’s true,” she quipped, and though his mouth closed again, the atmosphere had lightened significantly.

Shirone stood up. “Alright, shall we get to work?”

The Middle East, Paras Kingdom

The Paras Kingdom had suffered significant damage during the world war initiated by the Gustav Empire. The most devastating blow was the death of King Kasartak, who had ruled Paras for 40 years.

However, as the cradle of ancient civilizations, the powerful Middle Eastern nation would not fall so easily.

“It’s time to depart.”

In the ornate Grand Hall of the royal palace Delayma, a gentle yet resonant voice echoed.

It was King Kitra, the youngest son of Kasartak. With dark eyeliner accentuating his androgynous features, he was a striking figure.

“Your Majesty, preparations are complete. This Holy War will solidify Paras as the ruling power of the world.”

His ministers had absolute faith in him. Ascending the throne at a young age, Kitra’s intellect made him the perfect ruler for a kingdom that prided itself on its ancient wisdom.

“Hmm…”

The serpent coiled around Kitra’s neck flicked its tongue toward the gathered ministers, silencing them.

“Summon Zulu.”

The ministers hesitated, their heads snapping up. “Your Majesty, with all due respect, the ancient civilization of Paras has never granted access to foreigners—”

Hiss!

The snake let out a sharp, vibrating sound, causing the ministers’ faces to pale. “Shh.”

Kitra softly shushed the snake, which then turned its head to lick him affectionately. As he savored the venom like a kiss, Kitra repeated his order. “Bring Zulu.”

A soldier rushed out of the Grand Hall. Shortly after, the doors opened to reveal Zulu, Shirone, Canis, and Arin entering together.

Zulu, with a pacifier in her mouth, knelt before the king, making soft sucking noises.

“You summoned me, Your Majesty.”

“Zulu…”

Zulu was not only Paras’s pride but also the mage beloved by the late Kasartak.

“It brings back memories. My father adored you, and I once called you aunty. I can’t help but wonder—did you despise him so much that you couldn’t even send a message?”

Zulu’s pacifier made a quiet popping sound as she continued sucking, her expression devoid of any emotion.

‘She’s beautiful.’

Shirone wondered if relentless abuse from infancy had twisted him into something so haunting.

“So, you wish to explore the Pyramid of Truth?”

“Yes, Your Majesty. My companions and I aim to uncover the secrets of the outside world there.”

Kitra’s gaze shifted past Zulu to Shirone and his companions, lingering particularly on Shirone.

“The Five Stars of the Ivory Tower… I apologize for keeping you waiting. As you know, domestic affairs have been pressing.”

Unlike most rulers who feigned courage before stars of renown, Kitra’s sincerity was evident.

‘Such a unique presence. This Holy War will be intense.’

Shirone respectfully replied, “Opening a national treasure requires great determination. Please grant us this for the sake of humanity.”

“Humanity, hmm…”

Kitra crossed his legs casually. “They say the Burning Demon Realm has dried up all the oases. Without you, the damage would have been worse.”

“You’re too kind.”

As if indulging in a formality, Kitra didn’t drag it out. “I grant you permission.”

As murmurs spread among the ministers, Canis and Arin’s tense expressions relaxed.

‘It’s done.’

The ancient civilization’s secrets would finally be unveiled.
