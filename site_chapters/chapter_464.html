Chapter 464:

'Ikael, why…'

Shirone looked at Ikael with sorrowful eyes.

Her face, frowning, was completely different from the kind impression he remembered.

What was she hiding?

No, why had she changed?

The image of her standing on the side of humans after meeting Goffin had disappeared, replaced by the majesty of an archangel controlling humans with powerful authority.

If they had been even a little late, the rebel's first command would have exploded.

Countless lives would have been lost.

More frightening than hitting her was the fact that someone could die by her hand.

"Stop this now. You said you came to make a peace agreement, but now you're changing your words like flipping a hand?"

"Shirone, there is no eternal peace. I only came to propose a truce. But human indulgence has reached the heavens, so I am destroying them by the law."

Anke Ra wants to hear the answer to the question of the one who disappeared. The truce is only for that purpose.

Ra's will, which craves an answer even while prohibiting the activities of angels, was deeply conveyed to Ikael, but she couldn't just leave the arrogant humans alone.

"The negotiations are broken. I will destroy the rebels here and establish Heaven's authority."

"Do you think it will be that easy?"

Gaold's party approached from behind Shirone.

Around them, hundreds of rebels, ready for battle, were mounted on Kuroi. The sound of Titan engines could be heard in the distance.

From Ikael's perspective, it was laughable.

No human could harm an archangel. The very status of the law was different.

If possible, it would be a law beyond the law. Something that transcends humans.

'Like that person…'

Ikael shook her head firmly.

She would think no more.

"Prepare. They're coming."

A thick fighting spirit rose from Ikael's body.

Even without Ataraxia, the powerful aura made the mountains and rivers tremble, and Shirone's party tensed up.

Gaold glared at Ikael with unprecedented sharpness.

The power of the archangel he faced was indeed formidable.

But he couldn't lose.

Even if he couldn't win, he had a reason why he absolutely couldn't lose.

Rumble, rumble, rumble!

The ground where Ikael stood began to crack in a circle as it was pressed down.

Her body was ready to burst out with terrifying explosive power.

Before humans could perceive it, she could reach the entire rebel command.

In other words, the first target would surely die.

Even Cage Team B, who had intended not to get involved in this incident, had to take a defensive stance from a distance.

'The first target to eliminate is…'

Ikael's eyes flashed as she sprang forward.

'Shirone.'

Although Ra had prohibited the activities of angels, archangels born from the original concept were not bound by Ra's law.

Therefore, they were likely already observing the situation here in real-time through 'overlooking.'

Ikael captured the gradually approaching figure of Shirone in her eyes.

The speed was so fast that all the scenery, including Shirone, seemed to freeze in time.

'If I don't show my will, the authority of the archangel will be shaken.'

She had already shown too many ridiculous sides.

If she wavered any more, the archangels would act independently of Ikael's authority, creating a significant obstacle to fulfilling Ra's will.

'Therefore…'

She would eliminate Shirone.

At the point of this thought, her body had already closed half the distance to Shirone.

Then the scenery began to move slowly.

Babel and Gaold leaned in almost simultaneously, followed by Sein, Zulu, and Armin.

Next, Kangnan, then Etella, Siena, and Kuan took counterattack stances.

Finally, even Plu, performing automatic actions through the Dawkins algorithm, did not move.

In the state of Vajra Armor, cognitive abilities increase tremendously.

Therefore, the immobility was entirely his choice.

Thinking quickly can be unfortunate in some ways.

You see things you shouldn't see and think things you shouldn't think.

Ikael gritted her teeth and raised her claws at Shirone.

She would rip out his heart.

Dodging Babel's strike, she extended her five fingers towards Shirone's heart.

The wind, pushed from tens of meters away, hit Shirone, blowing his robe back.

At the same time, Ikael's hand stopped abruptly.

'Why…'

Shirone's face, filled with sorrow, was revealed.

'Are you crying?'

Tears were flowing from his incredibly lovely eyes.

It was a stronger restraint than the authority of the archangel or even Anke Ra.

How much had she given to this boy?

As everyone froze in their counterattack attempts, Shirone spoke in a trembling voice.

"I just wanted to hold you like before… just once…"

At Shirone's voice, Ikael's halo began to shake violently.

The memory was erased. But the memory of the erasure was not erased.

The small, perhaps even thinner than a particle, edge of the erasure was stained with emotions deeper than the universe.

She truly lost her memory, but she realized that it was a memory she should never have lost.

Whatever it was, she had to accept that it was incredibly precious.

Ikael slowly lowered her hand.

She couldn't hug Shirone, but she couldn't hurt him either.

'I just have to accept it.'

Ikael slowly stepped back.

With each step she took back, the tension weighing on everyone's shoulders seemed to lighten by several tons.

When Ikael returned to her original position, a colorful panel shone in front of her, and the three-horned Mara appeared.

Only a few of Gaold's party knew that Ashur was Heaven's greatest swordmaster.

"Lady Ikael."

"Speak."

Ikael still kept her gaze on Shirone.

The reason for her indifferent tone was that she already had an idea.

"It's a rebellion. Kariel has summoned the fire giants to the Second Heaven, and Uriel has joined him. The remaining archangels have scattered across Heaven with different purposes."

They had realized it too.

Although Ikael had regained her original power, she had a fatal flaw that was different from before.

If she couldn't lead everyone with her powerful strength, the archangels would act according to their own judgments.

Of course, only Kariel and Uriel would deviate enough to reject Anke Ra's message after prohibiting angelic activities.

But at least the three Maras under the other archangels were free.

'Heaven is indeed in a period of upheaval. But this is also my burden to bear.'

Ikael asked.

"What about the lower angels?"

"They haven't left the temples. As long as they are bound by the higher law of the archangels, they won't do anything foolish."

It wasn't something he could guarantee.

If another archangel caused another rebellion, it would affect the lower angels as well.

"Let's go back. The negotiations are broken."

Ashur, nodding, looked back at Shirone with a troubled expression.

The sins Ikael committed were erased when she was reinstated, but only Ikael had lost her memory.

Therefore, Ashur had some idea about the relationship between Ikael and Shirone.

But it was extremely abnormal and dangerous.

To perfectly reinstate Ikael, it would have been best to reset her. Erasing the incident itself would prevent any variables.

But Ra didn't do that.

'Or couldn't he?'

Could that be the case?

Even the Ultima system couldn't stop Ra's reset.

Therefore, the fact that he couldn't do it meant that a situational constraint beyond Ra's ability was at play.

'In other words, Ra is currently unable to attempt a reset.'

An electric signal, signifying a shiver, ran through Ashur's body.

'Damn it! That's right. The timeline we're in now…'

They had struck before the underlying event was completed.

Therefore, the war between Heaven and the rebels was only a visible situation, with a high-level psychological war unfolding behind the scenes.

Perhaps someone, a group, an organization, or something larger on the human side.

'That's why Ymir isn't moving. It's completely tangled.'

If they were trying to create a variable first, the event they were experiencing now was the first event outside the reset.

In other words, it was a raw timeline flowing.

Even the supreme spiritual beings, the angels, couldn't predict the variables of time flowing beyond causality.

Anke Ra wasn't suppressing the war but rather fighting alone.

Against someone controlling the timeline from the human side.

'Then I must keep my mouth shut too.'

Not knowing how today's small variable would affect Heaven, he had no choice but to follow Ra's judgment.

Embracing the frustration of not being able to predict even the near future, Ashur signaled Ikael to return.

As Ikael was about to fly up, Gaold asked.

"Where is Miro?"

Today's incident had dampened the rebels' spirits, but Gaold would come. That's why Kariel had imprisoned Miro in Lakia.

"She is in the Second Heaven."

Ikael revealed it without hesitation.

She also vaguely sensed what Ashur was thinking.

To lead the seven archangels, it was best to bind the most dangerous elements, Kariel and Uriel, to some extent.

"I see. We'll go find her."

Ikael didn't respond and flew up like light, disappearing from everyone's sight in the command center.

The atmosphere in the rebel command was solemn.

They couldn't blame anyone else for ruining the unprecedented proposal from Heaven.

The officers couldn't decide on the war and held heated discussions all night, while Gaold's party was deep in thought, each contemplating their own ideas.

Regardless of their decision, the project had to proceed.

This was true not only for Gaold but also for Shirone.

'Ikael…'

Why had she treated him so coldly?

If it was just a misunderstanding, he could accept that.

But in the end, she couldn't attack him.

In that hesitant tremor, there was the message Shirone had truly wanted to hear.

There was a knock, and Sein entered, prompting Shirone to force a smile.

The slight bitterness in his smile was because he guessed the reason for Sein's visit.

"How are you feeling?"

It was strange for a savant like him to care about emotions, but it didn't feel bad.

"I'm not sure yet. It doesn't feel real."

"I won't ask about your relationship with Ikael."

Sein was sincere.

That was Shirone's personal matter.

"I understand. Since I've fulfilled the contract conditions, I won't hesitate. Please plan the operation to activate divine punishment."

Sein nodded.

That was the most important issue for now.

"If there was something about Ikael that differed from your expectations…"

Sein brought up the second issue.

"It might be due to Anke Ra's Akashic Record."

"Are you saying Ikael lost her memory?"

"That's a possibility. When a part changes, the whole changes. Anke Ra is the whole, so it doesn't matter how it changes, but we intend to use the variables that come from it. Ikael is also within that range of variables."

Shirone understood what Sein was implying.

The war wasn't over yet.

"You will carry out an independent mission. It would be best if Zulu supported you, but we have some concerns on our side. Plu will accompany you."

Shirone was pleased.

He had felt it at the Second Command as well, but Plu was the most reliable ally.

"Yes, I'm very happy with that."

"Rest. We'll inform you once the plan is set."

Sein finished his brief business and turned to leave.

As he was about to exit, Shirone spoke.

"Thank you."

Sein closed the door, pondering his final words.

"…It's just part of the strategy."

That might be true, but Shirone felt much lighter than before.

'Ikael.'

He would meet her again.

That was the only reason he had come to Heaven.
