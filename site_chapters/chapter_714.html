Chapter 714:

Imperial City Aganos.

Uorin, accompanied by Gando, visited the secret space known as the 3.8th floor of Aganos.

Upon opening the door, they entered a 20-pyeong room with a bed, where a grand mage, whose name alone would surprise anyone, was sleeping.

The manager of the 3.8th floor, hunched over, waddled over to greet Uorin.

"Welcome, Your Majesty."

"How is it?"

The manager checked the color of the light in the magic crystal ball and bowed again.

The crystal ball, initially black, was gradually clearing.

"It's progressing much faster than expected."

The grand mage was currently repaying Shirone's debt through Fermi's depreciation contract.

A debt spanning 127 years was impossible for a human lifespan, but Uorin did not doubt it.

If such a thing had already happened, it was simply the way of the world.

'The key lies with Hexa.'

If the debt of Valhalla Action was measured in time rather than lifespan, the time Hexa experienced could differ from that of ordinary humans.

'Time-space compression, perhaps Andre, and maybe…'

Uorin avoided premature assumptions.

"When will the debt repayment be completed?"

"It's true that it's faster than expected, but we can't measure exactly how much it's being shortened."

"The relativity of time."

Even if the grand mage paid the debt, its size would be elastic, depending on Shirone's growth rate.

This indicated that the time Shirone experienced in Andre exceeded the normal human range.

"Understood. Let's move to the next stage. Report any special changes immediately."

"Of course. I will monitor without eating, sleeping, or drinking for 24 hours."

"Naturally."

As Uorin turned to leave the room, Gando cautiously asked.

"Aren't you being too generous? When the debt of Valhalla Action is repaid, Shirone will become even stronger. The time-space compression and synergy effects will be enormous."

Uorin gave a bitter smile.

"Gando, do you know what the most powerful weapon in the world is? One that cannot be defended against or avoided, and kills instantly upon striking."

"Is there such a weapon?"

"There is."

Uorin turned and raised her index finger.

"It's trust."

Gando felt as if he had been cut.

"No matter how wary a child is, they entrust everything to their mother and fall asleep. The child doesn't even consider that the mother might strangle them or stab their heart tonight."

"……"

Uorin turned again and walked away.

"Trust never fails, but that's why it's hard to build. If repaying Valhalla Action's debt can build it, it's a bargain."

"Shirone is not an easy opponent either."

Uorin acknowledged this.

"For now, that's fine. I will manipulate the debt contract. If even one second of debt remains, I can plunge Shirone into moratorium at a critical moment."

Gando asked worriedly.

"But can you really strike? At the decisive moment… Shirone's heart."

Isn't the reason trust is an absolute weapon because it cannot be struck?

"I can."

Uorin was resolute.

"Teraje receives the trust of many but trusts no one. Gando, that includes you."

Gando felt a bit sad and hoped that the same applied to Shirone.

"Don't you wish to sleep with Shirone?"

"Ahahaha!"

Uorin threw her head back and laughed.

"Love? Sure, I've experienced love throughout history. But Gando."

When Gando met Uorin's gaze, he felt he understood why she could never be human.

"You will never know the life a Teraje has lived."

Labyrinth Andre-World No. 1.

"Uga uga! Uga uga!"

The primitive planet's humans spoke to Shirone as if boasting trophies, but he didn't hear them.

'Those eyes are definitely Teraje.'

Anyone who had seen a Teraje would think so.

"Shi…ro…"

Realizing something, Mitochondrial Eve instinctively avoided Shirone.

In her early stages, her brain hadn't reached the realm of cold rationality.

However, her genes, which would carve out countless histories, held the foundational events before the reset.

'Perhaps I can live a grand life.'

Mitochondrial Eve first realized her destiny, the wheel of law, in this world.

'I must pass by this event. If the future changes…'

The foundational events she held were only up to just before the third reset.

Thus, the current event was the first, and Shirone had the same thought.

'She's wary of getting involved with me.'

It might be an emotion acting in the realm of instinct, but the intention was clearly conveyed.

'She might not be the first Teraje.'

If there were predecessors, there might be other daughters besides the Mitochondrial Eve before him.

'The problem is that I can't be sure.'

For the early Mitochondrial Eve, who lacked the rationality to analyze foundational events, the primitive planet was the epitome of chaos.

'Will this woman become Uorin in the future?'

Or will she become one of the many daughters of Mitochondrial Eve, buried in the cave of history?

If the woman before him was a direct descendant of Uorin, history would save her even if left alone.

But if Shirone rescued her, the future Teraje might not become Uorin.

'If she's not Uorin…'

Changing the world's history wasn't impossible, and its impact would affect Shirone as well.

"Uga? Uga uga!"

Finally, the words of the primitive planet's humans reached him.

"Hurry! It's a gift for you!"

He didn't want to blame them for just taking their first steps as a species.

But Shirone wasn't from this world, and he couldn't ignore the injustice.

"Uga! Uga uga!"

The humans, stomping their feet in anger, pushed Shirone towards Uorin.

'Teraje…'

In a situation unacceptable in a world with ethics, Shirone's eyes turned cold.

"Stop it. This is…"

"Krraa! Kraaa!"

Mitochondrial Eve shook her head violently.

'Go! Just go! Leave me alone!'

Her rationality wasn't established, but the impulse to send Shirone away surged.

Even so, when Shirone didn't stop, a moment of animal instinct pierced through, and a cry of reason erupted.

"Goaaa!"

At the beastly scream, Shirone gritted his teeth and ran out of the cave.

"Damn! Damn!"

It wasn't just a problem between the two of them; it was a matter of history.

"Your Majesty, the debt of Valhalla Action has fallen below 1%. You must act now."

Awakened by the message from the 3.8th floor, Uorin went down without even thinking of changing clothes.

'Not a moment too late.'

Gando followed immediately, and when they arrived at the room, the crystal ball had become almost perfectly clear.

'The debt repayment will be completed soon. It will take a few more days to set the conditions, but now is the only chance.'

The debt of Valhalla Action would be repaid, but it was about holding the grand mage's time.

"We can't hold it for long. Due to technical issues…"

"One second is enough."

If Uorin could plunge Shirone into moratorium for just one second at the desired moment, subduing him would be instantaneous.

"Then I will take Shirone's genes."

In the coercive manner that countless men had inflicted on Teraje in the name of love.

"It won't be a pleasant sight…"

"What am I doing?"

Under the night sky of World No. 1, with a giant moon, Shirone suddenly realized and changed direction.

"How foolish!"

The impact on the world was too great, but his shaken heart found its place.

"Of course, I have to save her!"

Even if this world moved towards a different future, even if he was forever trapped in moratorium…

'It's the right thing to do!'

Flying at top speed, he arrived at the cave and heard Mitochondrial Eve's screams.

'She'll die at this rate!'

It might be part of history, but Shirone no longer hesitated.

It's the heart.

"Stop! Let her go!"

The humans, sensing the hostility in his voice, bared their sharp teeth and twisted their faces.

"This woman is ours!"

As the words left his mouth, a flash of light brushed past them and struck the cave wall.

Rumble!

The humans, recalling the dragon encounter from earlier, were terrified by the echoing roar in the confined space.

"Ahhh! Spare us! We don't want to die!"

"Get out now!"

Pointing outside the cave, they all quickly passed by Shirone, clutching their heads.

"Are you okay? Get a hold of yourself."

As Shirone untied the vines binding Mitochondrial Eve, she pounced on him like a beast.

"Eek! Eek!"

It was a scream of resentment and anger.

"Why! Why did you save me! Why did you come back!"

Tears flowed from her eyes, though with a different meaning, as she pounded Shirone's chest.

This could end her grand life.

'Death!'

Finding a way to reverse it, she picked up a large stone and tried to smash her head.

'I must die!'

Teraje had developed the mental strength to end their lives without hesitation over countless generations…

'I'm scared…'

But for the early Mitochondrial Eve, death was too terrifying and overwhelming.

"It's okay. Don't worry about anything."

As she slowly turned her head after dropping the stone, Shirone hugged her.

"I will protect you. No one will be able to harm you."

Except for the still vague foundational events, she felt something warm for the first time.

"Ugh! Ugh!"

Mitochondrial Eve buried her face in Shirone's chest and cried sorrowfully like a beast.

"Then let's begin."

In the presence of the greatest wizards, the manager of the 3.8th floor finished preparing to manipulate Shirone's debt.

"Please give the order."

Strangely, there was no response.

Gando, puzzled, turned to look at Uorin, who had been giving orders with cold eyes just moments before.

"Your Majesty?"

The question slipped out, sensing something was wrong.

"What's the matter? Is there a problem?"

Uorin, as if her life had drained away, stared blankly at something without even blinking.

"Ah, ah…"

Seeing the Empress's shoulders trembling, everyone turned their heads with tense expressions.

"Why… why…"

The mind of a Teraje, who had experienced countless lives, could not be shaken even by a god.

"Why… why did you do that to me…"

The great resonance that exploded from the beginning of time pushed something long abandoned back to the present.

'No! I don't love anyone!'

It's the heart.

"I, I…"

Gando and everyone present looked at Uorin with wide eyes, unable to believe what they were seeing.

"The Empress… is crying."

It was the first time they had ever seen a Teraje's tears.
