Chapter 705:

Southern Akkad Desert.

Withstanding the sandstorm of Noskarta was the pyramid of the most famous grand wizard in the Kingdom of Paras.

Zulu's dungeon was a void space where many adventurers came to seek the secrets of death.

But now, it was filled with the scent of humans due to the presence of unwelcome guests.

Kangnan, with her hair tied back, carried a basket of wet laundry into the grand hall.

The vibrations of the pyramid, shaken by Noskarta, were growing stronger over time.

"Is there anything else you need?"

Zulu, sucking on a pacifier, sat in a corner like a soulless doll, blinking her eyes.

"……"

She neither read books nor had hobbies.

Apart from the minimal food needed to sustain life, she consumed nothing, spending most of her time in emptiness.

"I don't remember asking you to do anything."

Kangnan said as she hung the laundry on a drying rack.

"Aren't you bored? Sitting there all day like that."

"Without a heart, there is no time."

It was the words of someone who had reached the peak of detachment.

"Let me know if you need anything."

Wiping her hands with a towel, she walked to the bed and looked sympathetically at Gaold, who showed no signs of waking up.

His once strong body was now skeletal, and his beard, which he had given up trimming, had grown down to his chin.

'It's time to wake up.'

There was always porridge prepared next to Gaold, and Kangnan spooned some into his mouth.

"You're getting thinner. At this rate, you'll really…"

"He won't die."

Kangnan turned her head, showing a hint of relief.

"Really?"

"He should have died already."

"……"

"If he's surpassed the phenomenon of death, Gaold's end won't be death. It will be something more serious."

"He'll come back. Because here…"

Kangnan bit her lip and said,

"There's Miro."

Zulu didn't argue this time.

"Maybe he'll surpass Miro then."

Knowing how persistently Gaold pursued strength, she hoped for it but also felt a contradictory sadness.

'Just wake up. So I can fight too.'

Rumble.

The ceiling shook, and the sound of wind like the wailing of the dead echoed clearly through the walls.

"It's really incredible. How big must the pressure difference be to create a storm in the middle of the continent?"

Whooo!

It sounded like Gaold's scream.

"It's not the wind, Kangnan."

Zulu pointed to the ceiling and said,

"It's the wheel of law. The ethics of this world operate on a much deeper level."

Vanguard was closed.

Momodo, accompanied by her strongest subordinates, approached to meet the Maga Bandits, who entered confidently.

"Welcome. I'm Momodo, the 32nd manager of Vanguard."

Hearing the name of the most famous heroine in the central desert, the deputy leader spoke with determination.

"We're here for a brief matter. Will you cooperate?"

Instead of answering, Momodo looked at the woman next to the deputy leader.

Her hair was disheveled, and her clothes were undone, as if she had been dragged here after an assault.

But the aura she emitted, which felt like being punched just by being near her, was not normal.

'Is she really human?'

Momodo, who prided herself on having met all sorts of bizarre people, had to rethink this time.

This woman was the ultimate monster.

"You know the situation. Until Noskarta passes, no acts of violence in Vanguard…"

Paknyeo approached Momodo.

"I'm looking for Banaya."

Seeing the beast's eyes, Momodo's heart pounded as if it would burst, like prey under a lion.

'I'm going to die.'

Fight or open the way.

Faced with this choice, Momodo made a quick decision.

"Come in. Please don't cause a big commotion."

Paknyeo walked through the armed men without hesitation, and the deputy leader snorted at Momodo as he followed.

Back in their lodging, Shirone's group each took a bed in the corner, except for the one near the door.

"Phew, I'm tired. I think I drank too much."

"Hehe! But it was fun, right? Learned something new after 20 years."

Rian laughed, seeing Shirone's flushed face.

"Will it appear in your dreams? Shirone, how many pairs of underwear did you bring? Keep them next to you."

"Shut up! You were just as dazed, pretending to be an adult!"

Kido, stripped down to ragged shorts, sat cross-legged on the bed.

"Enough. Let the humans handle their own affairs. I'm going to meditate, so don't talk to me."

His scrawny limbs and bulging belly made his goblin body look unimpressive, but as he began to breathe, the alcohol quickly evaporated, filling the room with its scent.

"Kido, you…"

Remembering his request not to be disturbed, Shirone fell silent as Kido's eyes grew even more serene.

'Incarnation technique.'

Despite his frivolous speech, the goblin incarnation within Kido was astonishingly calm.

"Shirone, what do you think?"

Ignoring Kido, Rian sat on the bed and asked,

"About the prophecy we heard today. Do you think that's really my fate?"

"You're still worried about that? The people at the bar said it was a lie."

"But you know it's not a lie."

Shirone slowly took off his robe, delaying his response.

"The law is like a set of rules. Countless gears mesh together to produce fixed results."

Uorin's history search and Ra Enemy's event manipulation were possible because of the gears of the law.

"If Clarice's prophecy was based on the law, it could really be our fate."

"…I see."

"But the future isn't set. Time Explosion and your Deny are acts of breaking the gears' teeth."

"Just a few teeth."

Losing a few teeth wouldn't stop the gears or change the outcome.

Rian leaned back on his arms and said,

"How big are the gears of fate?"

Probably as big as the universe, and certainly not something that could be destroyed by mere actions.

As the atmosphere grew solemn and they fell into thought, a scream echoed from the end of the hallway.

"Ahh! What are you doing, barging in!"

The deputy leader, looking at the pale, blanket-covered couple, turned to the next room.

'Not them.'

He didn't know their appearance, but he wasn't so incompetent as to not recognize Banaya and Yaksha.

"What are you doing! How dare you enter my room…"

Slamming the door shut, he quickened his pace.

'Where are they?'

The building was already surrounded by his men, so there was no escape.

"This is the last room."

Hearing his subordinate, the deputy leader sensed the room's atmosphere for a while before pounding on the door.

"Is anyone there?"

No answer.

"It's urgent. Please open the door."

Still no response, his face twisted like a beast's as he kicked the door open.

"When someone calls… huh?"

A flash of light blinded him.

"Ugh!"

He twisted his body, unable to overcome the momentum, and spun in the air.

As the photon cannon grazed past and struck the wall behind, his men charged into the room.

"Kiii!"

Kido rolled on the floor, unleashing a blade storm, but felt no resistance.

Seeing the scattered bandits, Kido flipped and landed on all fours.

"Be careful. These guys are strong."

Shirone also gauged the man's skill, having dodged the photon cannon at close range.

"Get out! We'll meet outside!"

"Yaaah!"

Rian swung his greatsword, tearing down the wall.

"Catch them! Don't let them escape!"

Kido climbed the wall outside, while Rian and Shirone leaped in opposite directions.

'I need to call Latusa.'

Running down the hallway, Shirone saw the completely sealed Vanguard.

'Where to?'

Sensing a chilling intent, he turned his head to see a blazing blade rushing at him, reflected in the torchlight.

'Dodge…!'

He tried to bend his waist with Armand's reflexes, but his heart felt like it would burst.

'I'm hit!'

A clear, clean wind sound followed as Shirone's head was severed.

This all happened in one second.

"Gasp! Gasp!"

Teleporting 10 meters back, Shirone crouched, his hand on the ground, watching ahead.

His twisted clothes revealed a woman tightly bound in her outfit.

"You… ugh!"

A shockwave burst from his chest.

Looking down, Shirone saw a silver wound on Armand's chest.

'Couldn't dodge?'

Probably wind pressure.

But the metallic wound didn't seem like it was caused by wind.

"Time Explosion."

Recognizing the level of perception from one strike, Paknyeo spoke, and Shirone looked up.

"Who are you? Why are you attacking us?"

"The Council of Ten debated."

'Council of Ten?'

"Whether you or Anke Ra comes first."

"I don't understand. Why the Council of Ten?"

"Shura reported to Amita. You can't achieve the ultimate. So we…"

As Paknyeo lowered her stance, a whirlwind arose.

"Follow Anke Ra."

When Paknyeo disappeared from sight, Shirone activated Time Explosion without looking.

'So fast!'

But as time passed, Paknyeo's movements accelerated, and Shirone couldn't track her blade even with Time Explosion.

"Die."

Axing-Nirvana E-Engine (Thermal Power Inline 8-Cylinder).

When the cylinder of the law powered a single action, the sword struck Shirone's crown like a lightning bolt.

With a thunderous noise, the floor exploded, and Paknyeo remained motionless in the rising smoke.

'How?'

Even with her monstrous senses, she couldn't grasp the result.

'I didn't see it.'

Shirone had moved behind her, panting and ready to fight.

'Magic?'

To confirm, Paknyeo kicked off the ground, and Shirone's body ghosted through the floor of the corridor.

'Unpleasant.'

The unknown was always unpleasant.

"Ugh!"

Paknyeo struck the floor with immense speed, creating a huge hole.

Upon landing, she saw Shirone ignoring all physical barriers and heading outside.

"Could it be?"

Paknyeo's eyes widened in shock.

"Spatial Break?"

The reason for breaking through space was that the topography of the real world and the reverse world differed.

In the real world, a path blocked by a wall might be unobstructed in the reverse world, allowing for the shortest route.

"If it didn't end with Shibulsangpokmae…"

The ground here might be the basement in the reverse world, and the sky could be the rooftop of a tall building.

"He's gotten a bit closer to the ultimate."

As Paknyeo's muttering ended, Shirone, having broken through the ceiling and cast a shining spell, turned to face her.

'Good. With this, I can fight.'

The first ability he developed through Uorin's advice was the seventh sense, utilizing routes in both the real and reverse worlds.

Boundary of Life and Death.

The boundary between this world and the next.
