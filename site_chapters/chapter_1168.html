Chapter 1168:

Jincheon Empire Sector

Jin Kang sat in a lotus position, battling against the vengeful spirit that had invaded his mind after being struck by Mun Kingdom’s assassination technique.

His body was already damaged beyond recovery, yet the emperor’s will remained unshaken.

‘Unryong…’

Jin Kang’s brow furrowed.

‘How dare you try to kill me?’

The ministers prostrated before him, lifting their heads only when they sensed an eerie chill filling the room.

“Oh…! Oohhh!!”

Anchal’s eyes gleamed.

‘The energy of the Azure Dragon…’

A blue aura flared from Jin Kang’s body, and suddenly, a woman’s scream echoed through the chamber.

“Your Majesty! The ghost is terrified! Just a little more…! Please hold on!”

Within the swirling azure energy, the ghost’s apparition emerged, its wails piercing the air.

At last, Jin Kang’s eyes snapped open.

KYAAAAAAGH!!

The phantom shot out like an arrow, sending several ministers sprawling backward in terror.

“Hiiik!!”

Its rage-filled eyes glowed red, yet it ignored everyone else, fleeing from the emperor’s chamber.

“Cough!”

Jin Kang spat out blood, and Anchal hurried to support him.

“Your Majesty, are you alright?”

“… I am.”

Succeeding in reversing the curse didn’t mean his shattered body would suddenly heal.

“Your Majesty.”

“Do not worry. I won’t die just yet.”

Jin Kang’s eyes sharpened.

“I will not miss the sight of that bastard’s kingdom burning to the ground.”

Mun Kingdom Sector

“Uuaaaaaaghhh!!”

With a shrill, undignified scream, Unryong convulsed violently.

“Your Majesty!!”

His limbs seized in spasms, his face turned deathly pale, and dark red veins bulged beneath his skin.

“T-This is…?”

“The Reversal Kill…!”

“Impossible! His Majesty’s chambers are impenetrably warded! There’s no way—Guh!!”

The court sorcerer vomited blood and collapsed.

‘Damn it!’

The leader of Cheongwan Gate instantly understood.

‘This was an assassination that could not be blocked. That’s why we allied with Kashan.

If Reversal Kill was triggered, then it was beyond our power to stop it.’

Yet, there was a contradiction.

‘An unblockable assassination…enhanced with the power of Satan himself to amplify absolute hatred.

And yet, how…? How did it get reversed?

Who holds even greater resentment than us?’

His expression hardened.

“… Jin Kang.”

Was his hatred truly that deep?

The fortune tellers of Cheongwan Gate screamed in panic.

“We need a countermeasure! If we don’t stop it, the curse will spread, and we’ll all die!”

‘How foolish.

They have spent their lives studying the laws of the universe, yet they abandon all reason in the face of death?’

It was already too late.

“Your Majesty.”

The leader of Cheongwan Gate bowed before Unryong.

“… Rest in peace.”

At those final words, Unryong’s eyes slowly shut.

And then—

His stomach ruptured from within, spewing out blood.

“Gaaaahh!!”

The killing curse exploded, growing even stronger.

The leader of Cheongwan Gate crumpled to the ground, and the curse swept through everyone in the room.

A curse of absolute, inescapable death.

Even as he neared his final moments, the leader of Cheongwan Gate struggled to understand.

‘Why…? Why did this happen?’

A woman’s accursed face surfaced in his fading consciousness.

Uorin.

Everything had unfolded according to her plan.

‘I should never have trusted Terraje.’

Total annihilation.

Like a crime scene wiped of all evidence, only corpses remained within Unryong’s chambers.

Kashan Sector

While Havitz stepped out of the room, Uorin and Kido remained awake, drinking through the night.

Kido asked,

“Shouldn’t we set up a Bound Spirit? Havitz might still be lurking around.”

Uorin swirled her ice around in her drink, smiling.

“No need. He won’t be coming back for a while.

Looks like he found something far more entertaining than me.”

Yet deep inside, she felt a tinge of bitterness.

‘But for how long?’

A small part of her worried.

Havitz’s new amusement might soon twist the narrative in ways she hadn’t foreseen.

With a sigh, Uorin lifted her glass toward the window.

“Not much time left until morning.”

Her reflection in the glass met her gaze.

By now, it should all be over.

Mun Kingdom’s sector must be nothing but ashes.

"From the start, your plan was to frame Mun Kingdom, wasn’t it?"

Kido’s half-lidded eyes flicked toward her.

Uorin tilted her head, flashing a serene smile.

“Frame them? Mun Kingdom really is the culprit. This is what we call poetic justice.”

During the Code One assembly, Unryong was given ten minutes to devise a strategy.

But in truth—

“There was never anything to think about. Mun Kingdom was always going to be the guilty party. They actually committed the crime, so there was no need to fabricate anything. Tell me—could there have been a simpler way?”

Kido’s lips twisted.

“To reverse the assassination, Jin Kang was essential. Paras… just wanted to observe the results. By morning, the truth will be laid bare. As for everything else—”

Uorin’s eyes curved into a smile.

“That was just for me.”

Kido muttered.

“Shirone won’t like this.”

King Pony of Thormia was also targeted

"It’s fine," Uorin said.

"When a living creature senses its life is in danger, its sexual desire skyrockets. It's the primal instinct to reproduce.

When death looms over you, you find strength that wasn't there before."

"So you're going to push Shirone to the brink of death because of that?

Do you really believe that's love?"

"Havitz told me—if you don't define something, you'll get exactly what you want.

And he's right. Every failure in this world comes from defining things."

Kido thought to himself.

If she refused to define her feelings for him, couldn't she fall in love with a goblin just as easily?

Uorin gazed out the window.

"That'll never happen."

"Tsk! Who said anything?"

"It's obvious what a goblin like you would think.

To be honest, I find you disgusting.

A goblin who wears glasses and reads books? Just imagining it makes me want to retch."

"You're cruel," Kido muttered.

"Fufu, well, keep trying.

Starting tomorrow, even my life will be on the line.

Who knows? Maybe if death creeps close enough, I'll suddenly find strength I never had."

The kings of the Holy War would all have to guard their necks.

"Why do you have to make everything so complicated?

Just ask for my help.

I'm the Captain of the Royal Guard."

"I refuse."

Kido never got upset by Uorin's words, but at times like this, his heart ached.

She never asked for anything.

It was as if his presence was irrelevant—

as if he could leave at any time, and she wouldn't care.

Uorin watched his dejected reflection in the window with cold eyes.

To a human's aesthetic sense, Kido's face could never be called beautiful.

And yet…

His intelligence.

By devouring human memories, Kido had become more brilliant than any scholar.

The translucent outline of his face overlapped with her own reflection.

It wasn't a trick of the eye.

"Why do I despise him?"

Because his appearance reminded her of a primitive era she once lived through.

An era where consumption and reproduction were all that mattered.

Then again…

"How is now any different?"

The Next Morning

Before dawn even broke, the capital of Jive Kingdom was flooded with newspapers.

"These filthy animals!"

According to Rangi, the representative of Arachne, there had been a secret gathering.

The problem?

One of the women involved in the "entertainment" had died in a horrific S&M party.

Rangi declared,

"The higher-ups of the Holy War are mostly Satanists.

But after seeing what they actually do…

Even Satan himself would be disgusted."

The testimony was detailed, and Rangi's status as the world’s reigning beauty queen added weight to her claims.

And then she dropped the bombshell.

"That night, my partner was Jive's king—Gis."

It was a fatal blow.

Jive Sector

"What the actual fuck is this!?"

Gis roared, hurling the newspaper at his aide, Myron.

The paper smacked Myron in the face, but he quickly straightened up.

"It seems they made their move. Shirone's involvement is unexpected."

The article stated that Shirone, one of the Five Stars of the Ivory Tower, had taken an interest in the scandal.

A silent warning to the world's kings.

"Ugh! My blood pressure’s rising!"

Gis wasn't an idiot.

He knew there was no way to stop this now.

And Myron understood that as well.

"I'm sorry. But this will be the last incident. We'll clean it up before sundown."

"Too slow. The press conference is at three. Get it done before then."

"Understood."

"I mean it. I have a lunch appointment, so I'll deal with it right after."

"... Is it with Plu?”

"And if it is?"

"Will that be alright?

With today’s headlines…

In this situation, you should be careful about your movements—"

"Shut the hell up.

Do you know how many people I have lunch with?

Does that mean all of them are like that?

Hiding right now will just make me look more suspicious."

"But—"

"Rangi is nothing like Plu.

She’s the Secretary of the Magic Association.

If I go down, Thormia falls too.

Nothing’s going to happen. Stop worrying."

Thormia Sector

After two hours of sleep, Plu soaked in hot water to reduce swelling before sitting at her vanity.

A copy of the Jive morning newspaper was laid out before her.

‘So this was Shirone’s move. Gis is cornered. I need to stay sharp.’

Applying toner to her skin, she met her own gaze in the mirror.

She was born a woman— trained for over a decade.

"But before anything else, I am a professional."

As the Secretary of the Thormia Magic Association, her duty was to her nation and its people.

"No overthinking. I am the only one who can complete this mission."

She would drag Gis in, no matter what.

By the time she finished her makeup, an agent from Dante’s department delivered a note.

Gis. Press Conference. 3 PM.

Plu crumpled the note, shoved it in her mouth, and chewed it to destroy the evidence.

Then, she smirked at her reflection.

"And yet, he still wants to meet me?"

He must have thought meeting the Secretary of the Magic Association was a safe move.

"A political calculation. But Gis, you're mistaken. You probably fought tooth and nail to climb to the top. You must have swallowed your pride like a maggot, abandoned your own family. But you let arrogance creep into your mind. That’s why even the greatest fall."

Plu tied her hair back tightly.

"Too bad. We’re not playing politics. We’re waging war."

Delta Headquarters

Since early morning, Seriel had been swamped with work—

until she spotted Fermi walking down the hallway.

"Hey! Hey! Fermi!"

For a second, he seemed to pause.

Then, as if he hadn't heard her, he kept walking.

"You little—!"

Seriel stormed after him, eyes blazing.

"You promised to send me the Angel formula and chemical compositions! Why the hell haven’t I heard a damn thing from you!?"

"Sorry. Next time."

Fermi answered shortly, but his thoughts continued elsewhere.

‘Overnight, six nations attempted assassinations. Each country is suppressing the information under their highest security levels. But once the field agents start handling the mess, the leaks will begin. Probably within the next two hours.’

Which means—

By the time the Holy War begins, every nation will know.

If my intel is correct, Mun Kingdom will get hit with a Reversal Kill.

Damn it.

“I wanted to kill them myself."

Seriel grabbed Fermi’s shoulder and spun him around.

"Hey! Are you ignoring me!? I keep asking—do you know how many patients—"

She froze.

"… What's wrong with you?"

Fermi wasn’t scowling.

He wasn’t giving her a hostile glare.

And yet—

She felt a prickle of fear.

‘That expression… It’s the same as back then.’

There was nothing different about his face, yet she felt like she might burst into tears.

And that face—

was the reason she could never hate him.
