Chapter 1119: Depths of the Abyss (3)

"It's serious."

The think tank of the Kingdom of Mun, Cheongwanmun, watched as the divination board floating on the water began to spin.

The law of nature is present everywhere, and water is a prime example of this.

"This has never happened before."

Even though it appeared still, countless particles were moving within the fluid.

It was truly chaotic.

The divination board responded to this chaotic movement, serving as a device to design the future.

"But to spin like this?"

The inability to predict meant that anything could happen.

"What is going on?"

When Munryong appeared, everyone in Cheongwanmun stood up and stepped aside.

"Your Majesty, you have arrived."

"What is happening? People's positions suddenly changed in the banquet hall."

"Indeed."

The leader of Cheongwanmun nodded.

"Your Majesty, the world's principles are changing. This is something that can never happen naturally. Someone must be artificially manipulating the laws."

"Manipulating the laws? Who could do such a thing?"

"Perhaps…"

The leader had an intuition.

"It is likely Kitra of the Kingdom of Paras. The Zodiac was originally comparable to our Cheongwanmun, but they did not have the ability to do something so extraordinary. They must have obtained something."

"Hmm."

The Kingdom of Mun was the birthplace of divination, and Munryong was not ignorant of the world's principles.

"So what should we do? If Paras's astrology is stronger, the Kingdom of Mun will suffer."

Their divination was being rendered useless.

"There are two options. The first is to adapt to the laws they change. With Cheongwanmun's ability, we can respond quickly."

It was a reasonable approach, but bowing to a rival nation was unpleasant.

"And the second option?"

The leader's eyes sharpened.

"A curse. We send a black curse to Kitra, making his mind unstable."

"Is that possible?"

"If he is a leader of a nation, his energy surpasses that of a tiger. It will require much preparation. However… if everything is in place, it is not impossible."

"Hmm."

Munryong pondered.

'Should we use Paras or kill Paras? Both would be effective, but…'

His eyes gleamed.

The Zodiac of Paras.

The hologram projected above the pentagram was filled with stars observable from the planet.

"Hmm."

Kitra, sitting on the podium, watched the constellations.

'This is quite tricky.'

Even Ankera, the manager of the universe, only had the authority to reset the system.

Changing the system itself, that is, fine-tuning, required the absolute power of the outside world.

'Even a slight deviation can change the essence of the universe. It could become nothing.'

Changing the laws in the banquet hall was a kind of experiment for a massive change.

"Your Majesty."

The door opened, and an astrologer from the Zodiac brought a diagram of the banquet hall's state.

"Fate has changed."

"Bring it here."

As the astrologer handed over the diagram, the snake around Kitra's neck took it in its mouth and brought it to him.

'Let's see.'

The special language used by the astrologers could convey much in short text.

"What is this?"

The astrologer couldn't see where Kitra pointed but could guess.

"It is Kaden, born under the fate of the Southern Cross. But his right hand shows signs of breakout…"

"No, that's not important. It's something that happens anyway and can't be controlled."

When the astrologer looked up, Kitra turned the diagram and pointed to a sentence.

"Shirone. According to the laws I designed, he should have moved 32 meters. But… only 3 centimeters?"

"He is a master of the mind. He is predicted to reach almost the same conclusion from any cause. That is why Your Majesty allowed him to explore the Pyramid of Truth, is it not?"

To find the position of the gods.

'Of course, but… he is much stronger than expected. Soon, he will greet the gods in the Pyramid of Truth. If he persists to this extent, I might be in danger.'

"Nothing changes, Your Majesty."

"Indeed."

The stronger Shirone's resistance to fate, the more precise the coordinates of the gods become.

"I will change the world's laws."

"Make history."

As the astrologers of the Zodiac bowed their heads, Kitra spread his arms.

"I will become a god."

Miro blinked.

"A strange dream?"

"Yes. The sea stretches out, and a giant monster appears. If you are Miro, you should know."

"No, I don't have dreams like that."

"…You don't?"

That could be possible.

'It's just my imagination anyway. This is reality.'

But why did it tear his heart to see Miro not remember him?

"Think carefully. I saw you in a dream. You were trying to tell me something. Right?"

Gaold.

What did the woman in the dream want to say?

"Dad, this man is strange. Let's go."

Miro tugged at her father's hand.

Parents would usually leave immediately, but Sein said something unexpected.

"Wait… let's talk."

In the cozy atmosphere of Sein's house, Gaold and Gangnan sat side by side on the sofa.

"What do you want to talk about?"

Watching his daughter play with Arius on the floor, Sein spoke.

"I have that nightmare too."

Gaold and Gangnan were stunned.

"The sea you mentioned, that monster, it's still vivid in my memory," Gangnan said.

"It could be a coincidence. Dreams are like that. You can interpret them in many ways."

"But the name came up."

Gangnan was frustrated with her husband's obsession.

"Stop saying such foolish things! Do you really wish that nightmare was reality, despite the unbearable pain?"

How could it be good?

Even breathing felt like his throat was being torn apart, and every fight made him wish for death.

Gaold clenched his fist.

'It doesn't hurt.'

Compared to the nightmare, this was heaven, but the problem was that he couldn't shake off Miro's echo.

"I understand," Sein said.

"I don't want to go back to the nightmare either. I love my daughter too much. But…"

Suddenly, a thought occurred to him.

'Why a daughter?'

That small question had kept him from ignoring Gaold and brought him here.

Gaold asked.

"Why doesn't Miro, your daughter, have dreams? Is she lying…?"

"Miro doesn't lie. She's stubborn but a good child."

As he pondered those words, a sound came from the ceiling.

Everyone is gathered.

Everyone looked up in shock.

"What…"

The wooden ceiling bulged like liquid, and a pale-faced boy descended.

"Sorry I'm late. It was hard to find the drifting point. I'm glad you're safe."

"Who are you?"

The boy shook his head.

"It's much fainter now. It can't be helped. I'm Monga, the dream designer. I was waiting at the entrance to the depths and had to come down."

"Dream designer? Entrance to the depths?"

It felt vaguely familiar, but no memories surfaced.

"I need your help. Please persuade Yahweh. He's been staring at the sea all day."

Sein asked.

"The sea? Where is there a sea here?"

"You should know. Don't you all dream? Like a kind of nightmare…"

The atmosphere grew eerie.

"Of course, I know. Even being submerged under the sea. My daughter doesn't seem to have nightmares, though."

"Hmm?"

Monga's eyes, observing Miro, calmed.

'…I see.'

It was a typical Miro-like judgment.

"Let's go. Time is short. Miro did well, but waking Yahweh is urgent. You'll understand everything when we reach the beach."

They would do anything if it meant unraveling the secret of the nightmares that tormented them daily.

"Alright, let's go."

Monga led them out of Sein's house, following the sound of waves.

'How could they not know?'

The group was shocked.

"We've never been on this path. We've lived in this city for so long…"

"That's because of your memories."

Monga explained.

"If you set the memory of living here for ten years as the cause, it becomes the result of actually living here for ten years."

"So this is our first day?"

"Not exactly. It means time is meaningless. That's the kind of world this is."

Sein looked around.

"What happened?"

"Everything was swept away by Ymir's mind. It's proof that Luber was desperate enough to be unable to restore the city."

Between the skeletal buildings, they saw a sandy beach and beyond it, the blue sea.

Hearing the waves directly felt refreshing.

"Hmm?"

A boy sat in front of the foamy waves, looking despondent.

"Shirone."

As they approached, they saw the face from the dream, but his eyes were lifeless.

"Master, I brought your friends. Wake up. We have to fight again."

Monga shook his head.

"He doesn't say anything. He's lost the will to act. I can't say I don't understand, though."

"Why?"

"To withstand the mental pressure of the abyss, he seems to have activated the barrier of dreams. This beach is proof of that. It means he came out of the sea. Anyway, there's no mental attack here. Such things don't happen."

"What things?"

"Aren't you satisfied with this reality? In a unified dream, each person's dream merges into one. In other words, everyone's desires connect to create a single world."

"Does that mean we wanted this situation?"

"It's the closest result. Miro realized it when the barrier of dreams activated. So she erased all her memories. That image manifested as a child."

"Why did she have to do that?"

"To avoid hurting Gaold."

Monga raised his index finger.

"A dream is the fulfillment of desire. If Miro were an adult, Gaold would have endured the pain to love her. Of course, no one knows where it would lead. With everyone's desires mixed, she might have become Sein's wife."

Sein chuckled.

If Monga's words were true, it meant he was rejected by Miro even in the dream.

'An impenetrable wall.'

"So she erased her memories. The only world where Gaold could be happy. It's impossible in reality. Miro wanted that."

"Honestly, there's no way out. The moment we leave, we'll all be annihilated. I had hoped Master realized something, but in this state, it's impossible. Living here isn't a bad choice."

Gaold turned his head.

"Yay! The sea, the sea!"

Woof! Woof!

Arius and Miro ran along the beach, their faces beaming with joy.
