Chapter 1018: Underwater Work (2)

Arachne's sailing ship.

In a high-end restaurant where the sweet music of a musician could be heard, Cairns enjoyed his lamb.

“Where did Langi go?”

At just thirty-four years old, he was the third in line for power in the kingdom and served as the Minister of Foreign Affairs.

“She said she had some business and went to the refugee ship. Should I call her back?”

“The refugee ship, huh.”

Pausing his chewing, he pondered for a moment before nodding and speaking.

“She went to meet that guy Shirone. One of the Five Great Stars of the Ivory Tower. What kind of person is he?”

The magician in a suit standing by Cairns' side bowed politely.

“Yes. He is one of the world's four superhumans and the head of the Ivory Tower's Unified Space Management Department. Currently, he is…”

Cairns waved his knife.

“Ah, no need to recite what's on paper. How strong is he? If you fought him, who would win?”

Even if it seemed like a childish question, it was different when one reached a certain level.

“He is strong. As one of the stars of the Ivory Tower, it's natural, but he has stepped into the realm of infinity, the highest level of magic.”

“Oh? That much?”

It was unheard of for his secretary to concede to any magician's name.

“He is not an opponent I can guarantee victory against. But if I were to fight with everything on the line…”

The secretary's name was Benahar.

“I would predict my slight advantage.”

He was at the pinnacle of the mercenary world and had defeated many certified first-class archmages.

“Interesting.”

Cairns said as he cut his lamb.

“No matter how great the Ivory Tower is, they are still magicians. Set up a meeting. I'll try to recruit him.”

“Bringing him here won't be a problem. If he refuses, we can use force. However, most stars of the Ivory Tower are not driven by material desires. Negotiations might be difficult…”

“Tsk tsk tsk.”

Cairns clicked his tongue and shook his head.

“Do you know what most people misunderstand? They believe there are humans who are beyond money, women, power, and such. But there are no such humans. Because they are human.”

He made a circle with his fingers.

“Some people will fall for just 100 gold. But some will snort even at 1 billion gold. So is negotiation impossible?”

Benahar listened attentively.

“No. If they refuse 1 billion, offer 10 billion. If they refuse 10 billion, offer 100 billion. It's ultimately a matter of quantity and quality. You understand what this means, right?”

“Of course.”

Cairns had invested 28 billion gold to hire Benahar.

“There can't be a man beyond women either. How could that be a living being? Of course, some might act high and mighty. Then what should you do? Push with irresistible charm. Tailor it to their appearance, body, personality, preferences, intelligence, dignity, interests—everything about that person.”

Benahar realized anew.

‘Politicians aren't sane either.’

The reason it couldn't be dismissed as bluffing was that Cairns was currently the Minister of Foreign Affairs.

“Life is all the same for everyone. It's just a matter of degree. Negotiation starts from there.”

Cairns put down his utensils and wiped his mouth.

“Bring that man Shirone before me. After about 30 minutes with him, I'll have an estimate.”

“Yes, sir.”

As Benahar left the restaurant, staff came in with carts to clear the dishes.

“Thank you for the meal.”

Handing over gold coins with a gentle smile made the staff bow even lower.

‘Always the same reaction. Humans are no different from machines—machines driven by desire.’

Cairns was ambitious.

The child who once dreamed of world domination had demonstrated exceptional skill and became a minister in his mid-twenties.

‘The king of Arachne is just a stepping stone. Through this expedition, I will make a great leap.’

The pinnacle of the world was unfolding before him.

Langi suddenly clapped her hands as if she remembered something.

“Oh right! I have something to return to you.”

She took out a book from her diagonally slung bag and handed it to Shirone.

“Huh? What's this?”

“It’s the book you left at the hotel during the World Beauty Pageant. I've kept it all this time.”

“Oh right, this book.”

Turning it over revealed the title 'The Archmage Who Defeats Dragons' on the cover.

“An interesting book.”

Feeling thorns in Poyne's words, Shirone gave an awkward smile.

“I just bought it out of boredom during my travels. Oh! It must have been at the hotel.”

“Yes. At first it was funny but as I read on it became interesting. Especially when the protagonist pulls out the dragon’s heart at the end—it was really exhilarating. Oh, have you read it?”

As murderous intent from two apostles rose sharply, cold sweat ran down Shirone's back.

“…Haha.”

Poyne cleared her throat and spoke.

“Anyway, I have some business to attend to as well. Have a pleasant time.”

“No need for that…”

Langi hugged Shirone's arm.

“Oh my! Then let's tour the deck together. It's my first time on this ship. Thank you so much!”

As they moved towards the end of the deck together, Kaos turned towards them.

“What do you think?”

Poyne's eyes narrowed.

“She’s no ordinary person. She didn't notice our killing intent so she has no combat ability but her emotions are hard to read, she’s a pro.”

“Really? She seemed sincere too though. Anyway if she’s friendly towards Messiah isn’t that enough?”

“You’re naive.”

Poyne turned around with her hands behind her back.

“Human kindness is a fragrant poison, if you get intoxicated by its sweet scent your insides will rot away.”

Kaos followed behind her and spoke.

"I'm deploying to stop the next tsunami. Can you leave your position too? There's unusual activity from the Arachne side."

Poine looked back at the Arachne sailing ship.

"Indeed, the energy is unusually strong. I don't know who managed to get all these strong people on one ship..."

Her eyes narrowed.

"But after all, they're just humans."

Langi's face, looking out at the sea, was sad.

"It was less damaging than in other countries, but many people died or were injured. There was someone I met, and that person was swept away trying to save me."

"That's unfortunate."

"I lived in despair for a while. Then I heard that Shirone was coming. You know how it is, right? Those people in your life you can never forget."

Langi said with tears welling up.

"Even knowing it's a dangerous mission, I felt like meeting Shirone would open up my breathing. Ha! I really feel alive. I haven't told anyone about this."

As Langi moved closer, Shirone spoke firmly.

"What's the real reason you're here?"

Langi's eyes wavered for the first time, and Shirone did not miss that change.

"I'm well aware of Langi's charm. But what can you get from me by lying?"

"I'm not lying..."

Langi could have made any excuse, but realized there was no need.

"My true feelings can't be conveyed."

When he looked up again, Langi's expression had changed.

"Shirone, the truth is..."

Then a voice was heard.

"You were here."

As both turned their heads, a man in a suit was approaching.

"Benehar."

"I've been looking for you. As much as you're Langi, rules must be followed. Outdoor time is 30 minutes."

Langi, deliberately exposing her shoulder, turned around.

"I'll go now. It was nice to meet you,"

Thinking there was still more to their conversation, Shirone called out to her.

"Maybe we could have dinner together later? I'll come to you."

Langi stopped in her tracks, and Benehar's thick eyebrows furrowed.

"Yes."

Langi smiled brightly.

"I'll wait."

Shirone, looking at her genuinely expressive face for the first time, was puzzled.

"Arachne. What are you plotting?"

Benehar said.

"I'm sorry, but the appointment is canceled. You have a prior engagement. I'll pass the message to Langi."

"Who are you?"

Benehar, estimating Shirone's capabilities visually, extended his hand for a handshake.

"Arachne's Foreign Affairs Secretary, Benehar. I'm well aware of your fame, Shirone of the Ivory Tower."

Shirone is not authoritarian.

Thus, his curiosity about someone who does not use honorifics with one of the Great Stars was purely out of interest.

"Ah, yes. Nice to meet you. But what do you mean by canceling the appointment? It's between Langi and me."

"The minister wants to see you. I'll arrange a quiet place, so come over to Arachne."

"No, I mean. I have a dinner appointment with Langi. Why should I follow your directions?"

"Ha."

Power entered Benehar's eyes.

"Even Langi is just an object to the minister. Don't you know what the priorities are?"

"The first appointment is the priority. And people aren't objects. Take that back."

"Not technically incorrect. But we call things we can trade as objects."

That was exactly Cairns' philosophy.

"Trade?"

Feeling there was no more value in continuing, Shirone pushed away Benehar's hand.

"Tell the minister, if he wants to meet me, he should come at my time, at my place."

After saying that and moving towards the captain's cabin, Benehar cracked his knuckles.

"Is there no choice?"

If he were to collide with Shirone, the Grand Sage of the Ivory Tower, at full power, the entire ship might explode...

"That might not be such a bad thing."

As he prepared ice and fire magic in his hands and leaped from the deck.

"Urgh!"

Shirone turned around.

'What is this feeling...'

Before even casting a spell, the sheer force of the spirit zone was nearly heart-stopping.

'No, this can't be. It must be a technique.'

There had to be a countermeasure.

'Is it incarnation arts? Or perhaps the abilities of Yahweh I've heard rumors about? Maybe a type of mental magic...'

As Shirone stepped forward,

"Huh!"

Benehar involuntarily opened his mouth wide, expelling all the air in his lungs.

'Not a technique? Is this really human...'

Pure spirit?

"…Listen carefully."

Suddenly hearing the voice, Benehar realized he had momentarily lost consciousness.

How long it had been was clear from Shirone's position, now much closer.

'Why is he so tall?'

As his senses returned, realizing he was kneeling,

"I'll go meet the minister. I've changed my mind, make sure to tell him clearly."

"Yes, yes..."

"Make sure the minister is ready with clear answers to my questions. If I don't get the answers I want, Arachne will never achieve what it wants."

"Yes, yes."

As Shirone deactivated the spirit zone, Benehar's paralyzed organs quickly started moving again.

"Ah, ah..."

As he convulsed, kneeling on the ground, steam rose from beneath Benehar's feet.
