Chapter 535:

In the standoff between guns and magic, the Undergrounders tried to launch a preemptive strike, true to their nature—but Shirone’s reason moved faster.

‘Photon Cannon!’

A sphere of light surged forward in a flash, and the seven Undergrounders scattered in different directions.

Tat-tat-tat-tat-tat!

The forest was filled with lethal kinetic energy—bullets and bursts of light flew relentlessly, each aiming to strike down the other.

‘This won’t be enough.’

There were too many obstacles to get a clear line of fire.

‘They’re fast… What are these guys?’

Leaping from tree to tree, they fired bullets like rain, leaving no safe moment to lunge in recklessly.

‘I need more firepower.’

Shirone focused more energy into the photon in his right hand, then charged toward the enemy.

Zigzagging through space with rapid teleportation to dodge the barrage, he hurled the Photon Cannon at an Undergrounder hiding behind a tree.

Empowered by magical action and omnipotence, the photon burst clean through the tree, and the Undergrounder, hit by the shockwave, coughed up blood and collapsed.

“Guh!”

‘That’s one down. Six left.’

Shirone dove behind a boulder and fired a Photon Cannon over his head.

An Undergrounder mid-leap took a blast to the gut and crashed down.

‘Five.’

“Boss! The ambush isn’t working! It’s like he has eyes in the back of his head!”

“Shut up! It’s just luck! Everyone, charge! If we don’t, we starve!”

“Yeeaaaaah!”

Riled by the thought of starvation, the Undergrounders lunged in from all sides.

But Shirone, within his Spirit Zone, could clearly see all of their trajectories.

‘My god…’

Woodga watched the unfolding scene with a dazed expression.

It wasn’t just Shirone’s strength—it was that he wielded light.

To the Sun Children, who photosynthesized to live, light was life. That was why they all followed the Arbiter.

“Brother, do you hear that?!”

Snapping back to awareness, Woodga shouted, “It’s bad! It’s Ra’s warning!”

Having taken down three more foes, Shirone suddenly remembered what Woodga was talking about—cutting through the tinnitus with his voice.

‘The magnetic field!’

They had waited at the forest’s edge earlier because of the electromagnetic field generated around the Tree of Life.

“We need to escape! We’re out of time!”

Escaping the battlefield, Shirone grabbed Woodga and Hamei, then activated teleportation.

“After them! Don’t let them get away!”

“They’re food! I’m eating today!”

The terrain was too cluttered to completely shake the Undergrounders, even with teleportation—they simply vaulted over the trees in pursuit.

“They’re still on us! Hurry!”

Gritting his teeth, Shirone focused with all his might.

Breaking down his thoughts to the limit, his body flared like a flash of light bouncing between trees.

The desperate Undergrounders opened fire with everything they had, the bullet marks chasing behind Shirone like a snake across the ground.

“Raaaghhh!”

Spotting the edge of the forest, Shirone pushed his teleportation to its limit.

Then came a sharp beep—and a massive headache slammed into him.

“Grrhk!”

His Spirit Zone shattered, and the group tumbled to the ground, crawling desperately until they broke free of the forest.

“Aaaaaaagh!”

Hearing a gut-wrenching scream, Shirone turned back. The Undergrounders who hadn’t made it out clutched their heads in agony.

“Help me! Aaaaagh!”

Only thirty meters from the edge, but the pain was too overwhelming for them to move.

Blood streamed from burst vessels in their eyes, and then—with one final cry—they convulsed and went still.

“They’re dead,” Woodga said in a trembling voice. “That’s what happens when you ignore Ra’s warning.”

He turned quickly to Shirone.

“You… Who are you? How do you have powers like that? You’re not even a Sun Child!”

Shirone had no intention of conversing yet—not that they could understand him anyway.

‘Magic…’

He had lived in a world where magic existed.

And yet, his memories of entering a cryogenic capsule still remained.

‘Still… if more memories keep coming back, that’s a good thing. I need a safe place for now.’

Only then did he turn to Woodga.

“Take me to where you live.”

Shirone gestured as he spoke, and Woodga gave a slow nod.

“Yeah, fine. You’re our slave, after all. But we’re not getting back today. We can’t cross the contaminated zone before nightfall.”

Shirone realized what Woodga meant as he pointed toward the sun.

‘There is a way… But will it really work?’

Photon Wings.

Golden wings of light burst open from Shirone’s back, and Hamei stared as if entranced.

“Woooow…”

Ra—the name the Sun Children used for their sun, and their sole creator deity.

The scriptures told of angels with wings of light who served Ra.

“Brother… what if he’s…?”

Hamei trailed off, and Woodga said nothing, mirroring her uncertainty.

There was already an Arbiter in their settlement.

There couldn’t be two Avatars of Ra in one community. Bringing Shirone back could be seen as treason.

‘Still… We can’t spend the night out here.’

Newborn Sun Children needed to bask in sunlight to stabilize properly.

Woodga made his choice and took Hamei by the hand.

“Let’s go. It’s not our fault. We’ll just explain everything honestly to the Arbiter.”

As the two clasped hands, Shirone looked to the sky and flapped his wings with force.

Boom! A shockwave erupted as they soared dozens of meters into the air.

The Sun Children screamed at their first experience of flight, but Shirone calmly observed the scenery.

‘So familiar. Then… magic must be the real memory.’

As he rose, the ruined world unfolded beneath him.

Charred skyscrapers like burnt matchsticks dotted the landscape, strange creatures flew through the ruins, and scattered pockets of forest lay across the horizon.

‘This isn’t isolated. Civilization did exist here.’

But what dominated the view most was the mottled, mucus-covered contamination zone.

‘More than seventy percent of the city is overtaken. What is that? I feel like I’m close to remembering…’

Woodga, now more composed, pointed ahead.

“That’s our settlement.”

Through a gap in the buildings, the edge of a massive heap of scrap metal came into view.

“All right—here we go!”

He gave fair warning, but the speed at which the landscape blurred past them made the Sun Children shriek again.

A journey that would take half a day on foot took Shirone less than ten minutes.

Landing on a cracked asphalt road, he found himself surrounded by half-collapsed buildings that once reached over sixty stories.

“Follow me. I’ll take you to the Scribe. But once we’re inside, don’t act out.”

Past the forest of buildings stood a pyramid of interwoven steel girders—an immense structure, far beyond what humans should have built.

Barricades ringed the outside.

“We’ve completed the collection mission. Open the gates.”

At Woodga’s command, two well-built white guards placed fists over their hearts.

“Well done. Go on in.”

The guards didn’t discriminate by race, gender, or age—but there was a shared hollowness in their eyes.

‘Familiar faces… Are they all cryosleep survivors too?’

Inside the pyramid was brighter than expected.

Shirone looked up at the glowing sphere on the ceiling.

‘Shining—an illumination spell. There are mages here too.’

Woodga took Hamei to the cultivation chamber.

The elderly woman who received the newborn Sun Child laid the baby down in a softly glowing space, and only then did the crying stop.

“You’ve done well. That couldn’t have been an easy journey.”

“Hehe, Woodga went with me, so it was fine. I think I could even do it alone next time.”

Knowing how dangerous the mission truly was, the old woman offered only a quiet smile.

“And… who’s this slave?”

“Oh, we found an Ancient’s shelter. Hamei discovered it. But, uh…”

Woodga hesitated, then told her about what had happened at the Tree of Life.

“Hm… I see.”

Falling into thought, the old woman nodded slowly, as if something had occurred to her.

She gently patted Woodga’s head.

“I’ll report this myself. And don’t worry—I’ll make sure they know it was Hamei who made the discovery. Now go and rest.”

Woodga and Hamei’s faces lit up with relief.

To be honest, they hadn’t known how to report what had happened, so this was a weight off their shoulders.

“Yes. We’ll leave it to you, then.”

As the two of them hurried out of the incubation chamber as if fleeing, the old woman’s gaze turned cold. She looked at Shirone and said,

“Follow me. There is a ritual you must undergo.”

Shirone followed her to the very top of the pyramid and waited outside the door while she reported to the Scribe.

Finally, the doors opened, revealing a sanctuary with a pristine, orderly floor unlike anything he had seen before.

At the end of a torch-lit path stood an altar, and atop it sat a man with a strong, commanding presence and short green hair—an aura of authority radiating from him.

“Arbiter, this is the Ancient who supposedly used the power of light,” the Scribe reported, bowing his head.

The Arbiter’s sharp eyes pierced through Shirone.

“Is what the Scribe says true?”

When Shirone showed no reaction, the Arbiter turned to an elderly man in a robe waiting at the side.

“Priest. Examine the boy.”

The old man approached Shirone with unsteady steps, muttering incantations through sunken lips as he spun his palms in slow circles.

“What is this…?”

Shirone’s eyes widened as the priest’s hands began to glow faint blue.

Wherever the priest’s hand passed, golden codes shimmered into view—like frost being wiped from a windowpane.

The chanting continued without end as the priest moved his hands wider, inspecting the codes forming around Shirone’s body.

Then, scanning from Shirone’s face down to his torso, his body turned semi-transparent, revealing more layers of codes.

“Hmm… so that’s what it is…”

The priest extended his right hand into the air, mimicking a grasping motion.

A specific code sequence appeared in the form of a translucent ribbon, held tightly in his hand.

Then, scanning Shirone’s head with his left hand, he summoned another code, which he pulled into alignment with the first and forced into connection.

“Hhrrkk!”

Shirone’s limbs stiffened and trembled as an overwhelming flood of information surged into his mind.

“The language of the gods shall work a miracle upon you,” the priest intoned.

He then turned to the Arbiter.

“It is confirmed, Arbiter. This boy’s body is inscribed with the Language of Light.”

“A lamentable situation. Why would Ra inscribe such a gift upon a mere slave?”

The Arbiter flipped his right hand upward, summoning a massive orb of light that soared to the ceiling.

‘Shining…’

Even as the Sun Children’s language fused with his mental data, Shirone could clearly recognize the spell.

As the Shining spell illuminated the temple, everyone inside raised their hands and shouted in unison,

“O Avatar of Ra! Grant us life!”

Chin lifted arrogantly, the Arbiter gave his command.

“Erase the Language of Light. Only those who submit may continue their pitiful lives of consumption.”

‘Erase what…?’

Shirone barely had time to be surprised that he was beginning to understand their words. What caught his attention more was the hostility now in the priest’s eyes.

“Do not resent me. There can be only one sun in a settlement.”

The priest swept his right hand through the air again and this time seized a ribbon inscribed with red-hued code.

He then shined light from his left hand onto Shirone, revealing more code, and slammed the ribbon into alignment with it.

“GRAAAAGHHH!”

An immense shock wracked Shirone’s brain—an unmanageable flood of information threatening to burst it open.

“Endure it! A slave’s only virtue is obedience!”

‘I am not a slave!’

Gritting his teeth, Shirone resisted the code trying to overwrite his ego.

His Spirit Zone compressed, a Frame beginning to form.

At the junction between his own code and the forced code, an overload triggered, and the terrified priest trembled.

“H-How is this… possible…?”

Shirone’s eyes rolled back briefly, lids twitching, but he clenched harder.

‘Hang on. If I fail here, it’s over.’

Diamond Mind—Unbreakable.

With an indestructible mental state established, Shirone’s eyes snapped open and his Spirit Zone exploded outward.

Boom!

The codes burst with a bang, sending the priest flying across the sanctuary, screaming until he crashed before the altar.

Struggling to raise himself, his face pale, he muttered the words he had tried to say before.

“How can he… reject the language of the gods…?”

Shirone rubbed the spot where the codes had linked and glared up at the Arbiter seated atop the altar.

“What were you trying to do to me?”

“……”

Shirone’s Information Recovery Rate: 57%.
