Chapter 1172:

11 AM

At Delta Headquarters, the only person who knew about Rebecca’s death was still Shirone.

His gaze turned sharp, brimming with killing intent.

‘Havitz.’

The fact that no one else had realized her death meant that Vanishing was still in effect.

‘Should I tell them?’

Most likely. But even if Shirone spoke up, who would believe him?

‘Even I’m not completely certain. I don’t even know exactly who Havitz is… It’s like my senses are being distorted.’

As if identifying an object just by touch, or hearing someone’s words despite covering his ears—

Everything felt warped.

Yet, there was no doubt that Havitz was real.

‘Should I track him?’

Shirone sharpened his 11th sense, attempting to extract any information about Havitz.

‘…Impossible.’

It was divine law at work, ever since Good and Evil had been separated from Ultima.

‘An event where I personally confront Havitz… will never occur. Which means that what just happened…’

Had been Havitz’s intention all along.

‘Why? There’s no benefit in getting close to me. What changed all of a sudden?’

Wizard.

If something significant had happened to Havitz, then the only possible catalyst was his encounter with her.

‘If Havitz is still alive…’

Then Wizard was most likely dead.

‘…I won’t think about it.’

There were too many unknown variables to conclude anything just yet.

‘I have to trust Wizard.’

Upon entering the Thormia Sector, the Royal Guards, who had been waiting, immediately informed Shirone.

“They are all expecting you.”

Inside, Thormia’s high-ranking military officials were gathered.

At the center—Dante, preparing for a briefing.

“Shirone.”

Lupist spoke up.

“Do you know anything? I have no idea why we’re all gathered here.”

“Havitz…”

The moment Shirone said his name—

Vanishing lifted.

“…Huh?”

Dante snapped back to reality, and the others instinctively looked around.

Albino murmured with an uneasy expression.

“…So it’s already begun?”

“Yes. Rebecca is dead. I saw him take her life without hesitation.”

At least, that’s what he assumed.

“When?”

“Around thirty minutes ago.

I don’t know why he didn’t immediately lift Vanishing, but…”

“He’s controlling the time of death.”

Dante interjected.

“He said he would kill one person per hour. If we knew exactly when Rebecca died, we’d be able to predict the next death. That’s not what Havitz wants.”

Lilia asked,

“But can he really deceive everyone like that?

He has to time the lifting of Vanishing perfectly.”

“It’s possible. The fact that we all realized it only when Shirone entered— That wasn’t a coincidence. It was intentional.”

“…Ah.”

The Frequency of God.

Albino nodded.

“Havitz kills someone while hidden in Vanishing, then reads our minds and chooses when to lift it. What he wants is a game. He’s enjoying the way the board is shifting.”

Lupist spoke.

“Let’s assess the situation first.”

Dante moved toward the strategic chart, while Shirone took a seat among the high-ranking officers of the Royal Guard.

Commander Rai

Tactical Operations Chief Tess

Security Chief Diane, Amy’s eldest brother.

“Shirone.”

Tess extended a hand. Shirone shook it, then nodded toward Rai and Diane.

Dante began.

“Let’s start the briefing. With Havitz having declared a killing game, our top priority is identifying allies and enemies.”

Dante pointed at the Kashan Empire’s flag.

“As a leading candidate for Supreme Governance, Kashan’s alliance dynamics are relatively straightforward. They are currently in a strong alliance with the Moon Kingdom.”

Tess raised her hand.

“But I thought Kashan betrayed the Moon Kingdom. Wouldn’t that weaken their alliance?”

“Politics isn’t driven by emotions. At this point, the only card the Moon Kingdom has left is a suicidal pact with Kashan. And Kashan knows it too. For now, their alliance will remain intact.”

Dante continued.

“Next, we have the weaker alliance between Thormia, Jincheon, Corona, and the Tribal Union.”

Rai asked,

“What’s the criteria for strong and weak alliances?”

“If one country collapses and it directly harms another, it’s a strong alliance. If a country falls but its allies suffer no immediate damage, it’s a weak alliance.”

Dante pointed at Jive.

“Jive, Iron, and Arachne form a strong alliance. I don’t need to explain why. Right now, King Gith of Jive is embroiled in a sex scandal. Depending on how things unfold, Iron and Arachne will also suffer repercussions.”

Albino asked,

“This sex scandal… Didn’t Arachne orchestrate this? This isn’t the same as the situation with Kashan and the Moon Kingdom.”

“Of course. But according to intelligence reports, Arachne colluded with an internal faction within Jibe— Specifically, King Gith’s political rival, Raymond.”

Regardless, it remained an internal Jibe issue.

‘…So that’s what was off.’

Shirone finally understood the unease he had felt during his conversation with Langi.

‘That’s why she couldn’t reveal anything. It wasn’t exactly a lie, but that’s why Mikado hasn’t been caught yet.’

Dante continued.

“Paras, Kessia, and Gustav have not engaged in alliance politics. This means they are not interested in becoming the Supreme Governing Nation. To gain their votes, we must approach them cautiously.”

Lupist asked,

“What’s the status of Paras?

Didn’t they also get hit by the curse?”

“It’s difficult to gather information. They’ve completely cut off communication with other nations. The only thing we’ve received is an official statement— They plan to announce the King’s death soon.”

Albino smirked.

“This is going to be quite the show.”

“I share the same perspective,” Dante replied. “However, we cannot make a definitive judgment until we verify the facts.”

Dante turned away from the strategic chart and addressed the room.

“The reason alliance dynamics are so crucial boils down to the vote.

Rom, Garto, and Temica—where their votes land will determine the tide of the war.

And this authority belongs solely to each nation’s representative.”

Lilia spoke up.

“So even if they were cursed and are currently unconscious, they still retain their voting rights?”

Dante nodded.

“That’s exactly why Havitz’s declaration is significant.

Right now, King Pony is in critical condition, but that doesn’t reduce the risk of assassination.

If someone were to strike, it would most likely happen within the power struggles of the nations we just reviewed.”

Albino chuckled.

“Sounds like an opportunity for us, no?

Thormia has its own hitmen after all.”

He was referring to Parka Kuan.

“Yes,” Kuan affirmed.

“I’ve simulated the assassination capabilities of all twelve nations and ranked them based on attack potential. Thormia ranks among the top-tier nations, scoring 94 points in offensive capability.”

This was good news, but Lupist had already grasped what Dante was really getting at.

‘Our offensive power is second to none. But… could our defensive capability become a weakness instead?’

Of course, Rai, the Commander of the Royal Guard, was an exceptional swordsman.

But wars were ultimately a game of relativity.

‘If Rian were still here…’

For a moment, Lupist indulged in wishful thinking.

But unrealistic hypotheticals were always a poison.

“I understand.”

Sensing the weight of the discussion, Rai spoke up.

“Our defense is lacking compared to other nations. We will engrave our weaknesses into our bones and fortify them as best we can.”

Though he spoke calmly, everyone in the room could imagine the shame he was feeling.

‘Damn it…’

Rai clenched his fists.

‘Why… Why am I not as strong as Rian?’

Despite possessing exceptional talent, he had never once let his guard down.

‘Then why can’t I reach it? What did Rian do that I didn’t? If anything, that bastard…’

A sudden realization struck him.

‘…Ah.’

Was it because he had no talent?

No matter how far Rai had run, had Rian needed to run twice as far?

No matter how many times Rai swung his sword—

Had Rian been swinging it even more?

‘Stop thinking about it.’

Rai shook his head.

‘Don’t look for answers in others. I must be the one to reach it. I will become stronger with what I have.’

As the room settled, Albino posed a question.

“Who ranks as the strongest?”

“In total assassination capabilities, the highest-ranked nation is Kashan. The Wind Guards leads their offensive unit, while their defense is handled by the Kashan Royal Guard—Inner, Mid, and Outer Divisions. However, the biggest obstacle is likely Kido, the Commander of the Royal Guard.”

‘Kido.’

Though they now stood on opposing sides, Shirone felt conflicted upon hearing Dante’s assessment.

‘Yeah… Kido is strong.’

Shirone didn’t know much about swords, but he did remember…

Kido was a friend who had risked his life during their journey into Andre’s world.

‘And his convictions are just as strong.’

Albino asked,

“So, statistically speaking— Can Thormia’s offense break through Kashan’s defense? Meaning… can Kuan kill Kido and take Uorin’s head?”

Dante replied,

“Kido’s profile has not been updated since he transferred from Radum to Kashan. However, given his ability of Predatory Consumption—‘The Taste of Memory’, he has likely accumulated a significant level of enlightenment. Even in simulations of a direct duel against Kuan, the outcome remained inconclusive.”

Lupist nodded.

“He seems capable of countering Havitz to some extent as well. There must be a reason Uorin keeps him by her side.”

Dante’s expression grew firm.

“Kido is a formidable wall. However, as a strategist, my personal belief is— No wall can stop Kuan. His swordsmanship has surpassed the realm of response.”

Dante’s true talent wasn’t in his own abilities, but in his ability to assess the talents of others.

Albino took a sip of his tea.

“Ho… So Kashan can be taken down?”

“If the balance of offense and defense remains logical, yes. However, we cannot predict how other nations will distribute their forces. For example— If Kashan places the Wind Guards in defense and Kido in offense, Their overall assassination capability would decrease instead. It would be an inefficient use of resources. But then again, war is full of reckless gambles.”

Lupist nodded.

“I see. From Thormia’s perspective, using Kuan for defense while pushing Rai for offense would be just as inefficient. Ah, Commander of the Royal Guard, don’t take it personally.”

“I understand. It makes sense.”

This time, Rai set aside his emotions.

“We can predict the enemy’s setup, but we must also be prepared for unorthodox strategies. That much is clear. Let’s proceed with the existing plan.”

Albino asked again.

“In other words—we take down the White-Haired One?”

He meant Uorin.

“Looking at the alliance structure, Thormia’s only uncontrollable variable is Uorin. It’s a risky move, but desperate times call for desperate measures.”

“I agree. Relying on others to make the move is just wishful thinking. And in this world, nobody gets what they want for free.”

Dante continued.

“Strategically, Kuan’s offense is unrivaled against any nation. However, based on internal assessments, there is one major variable that must be accounted for—”

“Oliver Siena.”

Albino twisted his beard.

“That swordsman is head over heels for her.

She’s the reason he lost an arm, after all.”

“Yes. In espionage tactics, reducing risk is just as crucial as execution capability. Oliver Siena is an excellent instructor, but she could also become a shackle for Kuan. This isn’t just a matter of intelligence gathering— It requires a decision from the Dragon Fang.”

Albino turned to Shirone.

“So… you’re asking whether it’s more efficient to protect Oliver Siena— Or simply eliminate her?”

Dante nodded.

“Yes.”
