Chapter 555:

Shirone's sequence method finally approached the quintillion mark.

As it did, the vortex of light spread menacingly, and the surrounding scenery distorted grotesquely.

The texture of the vortex was merely the movement of countless particles, but observers could sense the flow through their imagination.

'What on earth is that?'

As Shirone neared the singularity, Miro spoke less.

The light currents filling the training hall blazed as if on fire.

'Just a bit more! Just a bit more!'

As the accumulated experience of each day began to show signs of explosion, Shirone pushed his mind.

'I believe in the power of a day!'

The acceleration he achieved was due to his relentless effort every moment.

What he would gain at the end of burning through the experience of failure would be an immense joy that would blow away all the pain he had endured.

'It's coming! It's coming!'

The realm of quintillions awaited Shirone in the distance.

"Uoooh!"

Screamer's rapid punches hit the sandbag so fast they were invisible.

Once he got the distance, his precise, fast, and powerful strikes were the result of countless repetitions.

'This is the end!'

With all his strength, he delivered a final blow, snapping the wire holding the sandbag with a loud clang.

The sandbag flew three meters and crashed heavily to the ground, leaving Pyroca wide-eyed.

"You…!"

He knew it was childish to show his feelings, but he couldn't suppress the explosion of emotions.

"Aaah!"

Screamer's upper body muscles bulged grotesquely as he clenched his fists and spread his arms.

It was an intense pleasure he had never felt in any victory.

'It's complete!'

As Iruki's eyes, sparking with electricity, snapped open, the long-awaited earthquake began.

"It's done!"

When the bunker that had been underground in Mercodine for so long exploded, the hall was in chaos.

The ground shook, dishes fell to the floor, and maids screamed and ran around, clutching their heads.

"……"

Leaning against the wall, Albino calmly watched the scene and then looked up at the second-floor stairs.

Iruki was coming down.

"Congratulations. You've finally destroyed the family's legacy."

Iruki shrugged nonchalantly but couldn't hide the joy in his eyes.

"I'll apologize to our ancestors when I meet them in the afterlife."

Iruki briefly spoke and left the mansion to prepare to return to school.

"Apologize…"

Watching his son's back, Albino muttered.

"More like the opposite."

"I can do it! Perfectly!"

Fire Strikes spread out from Amy.

The flames, blooming like flowers, shot down all 58 targets without missing a single one.

Accuracy: 100%.

Amy's body trembled as she extracted 58 sound waves from the overlapping explosions.

But the moment of emotion was brief, and she soon stared into the air with fierce eyes.

'I will graduate, no matter what.'

Recalling the humiliation she suffered last year, her red eyes burned.

The water in the pool at the royal annex swirled into two torrents, creating a bizarre vortex.

As Pony, submerged in the water, pushed her hydromancy to the limit, a column of water shot up, and all the water in the pool spilled out.

"Eek!"

The maids, holding her change of clothes, screamed and backed away.

The water, hitting the walls, flowed back to the floor, slowly raising the pool's water level.

'Success.'

Pony calmly closed her eyes, but she was using all her strength to maintain her royal dignity.

"Miss, congratulations! That was amazing!"

The maids' compliments, though they knew little about magic, brought a slight smile to her icy face because it was not authority given by others.

'The future is mine alone.'

Dorothy, covered in a blanket, suddenly sat up, trembling.

After a while, she collapsed, burying her face in the pillow as if her strength had left her.

"Haaa…"

Her glasses were completely askew, and her face, looking at the floor, was filled with satisfaction.

Bzzz! Bzzz!

Her alter ego, Hickory, was dancing, rotating its neck and limb joints.

D-4 Days.

In the candlelit training hall, Shirone was pushing his sequence method beyond even his sleeping hours.

Miro, too, waited persistently, like a midwife delivering a difficult birth.

Both were determined to finish it today.

'9.2 quadrillion! 9.4 quadrillion!'

The realization of unity of mind and body had already been achieved days ago, and all that remained was to strengthen the laws of the incarnation.

And finally, even that came to an end.

Quintillion.

Shock filled Shirone's eyes.

"Gasp!"

Mental transcendence - unity of mind and body.

The mirage rising from Shirone's body ascended and transformed into a giant angel.

Archangel - Time Explosion.

Miro widened her eyes and examined the incarnation.

It resembled Ikael but was male and had distinct features.

The incarnation's body, which had been looking down at Miro for a moment, quickly collapsed and began to be absorbed into Shirone's body.

'What is this?'

The incarnation of Prajna is a phenomenon revealed through mental transcendence.

The stronger the incarnation's power, the larger or clearer the aura becomes.

"Gasp! Gasp!"

Shirone, breathing heavily with a look of disbelief at his achievement, lifted his head.

"I did it! I really did it!"

'It's not gone. It's unified.'

The menacing vortex of light had subsided, but a serene light still surrounded Shirone's body.

As Shirone tried to approach Miro, a light afterimage, following the lines of his body, overlapped around him.

"Wait."

Miro raised her hand to stop him.

"What's wrong?"

"Don't you feel anything different? Like the scenery looks different?"

"No, I don't really feel anything like that."

Shirone said, but Miro clearly saw the light afterimage vibrating every time he turned his head.

'He doesn't feel it? Then, as I thought…'

After completing her initial analysis, Miro pointed to the far end of the training hall and gave an instruction.

“Walk over there.”

As Shirone began to walk, the overlapping recoil intensified, and afterimages separated from his body as if detaching entirely.

‘Time is vibrating. Judging by the gap between the physical body and the afterimages, it’s approximately one second forward and backward.’

When Shirone reached the wall, feeling frustrated, he asked, “What’s wrong? Is something the matter?”

“There is,” Miro replied. “As you know, when you reach the state of oneness, the laws of your avatar begin to affect the world’s laws. And your law…”

Miro found the most precise definition.

“Breaks the wall of time.”

“What? The wall of time?”

“Let’s confirm it first. Walk back to me.”

As Shirone turned and walked toward Miro, the light-based afterimages vibrated in all directions.

Each afterimage represented Shirone’s past and the potential futures he could reach.

“Stop.”

When Shirone stopped, Miro let out a sound of amazement.

It was as if Shirone had stepped back into the past by one second, halting a half-step behind where Miro had just seen him.

“Did you feel that? You just moved back about half a step from where I saw you.”

“No, I didn’t feel anything. I just stopped.”

“That’s exactly it. It’s not teleportation, nor is it time reversal. You’ve simply become far more fluid in perceiving time than other people.”

“I don’t quite understand what you mean.”

Miro raised her hands as if grasping an invisible ball.

“Listen closely. All matter is trapped in the mold of time. Humans, too, can only perceive one second at a time.”

Shirone nodded—this much he could follow.

“But your avatar has broken that mold. It’s as if an explosion has occurred inside you, expanding the mold’s space.”

“So, what does that mean exactly?”

“Put simply, you’re not living one second per second. Both the past second and the future second are included in your present. In other words, you perceive three seconds at once.”

Miro approached Shirone.

“Let’s test it. I’m going to punch you in the chest now, but you’re not allowed to block it in advance. Block it only after you’ve been hit.”

Miro waited until Shirone couldn’t anticipate the timing and threw an unexpected punch.

As Shirone stepped back and caught her wrist, his face turned pale.

“… What just happened?”

“You blocked it after being hit, didn’t you?”

“Yes, I definitely got hit, but… I’m not sure. It feels like I imagined being hit. I remember it, but it doesn’t feel real.”

“Exactly. The conclusion is that I didn’t hit you at all. That’s because your past self erased the event of being hit.”

Shirone released her wrist and asked, “How is that even possible?”

Miro drew an imaginary graph in the air and explained.

“Let’s say the time coordinate when I punched your chest was at 23 seconds. For ordinary people, they can’t escape the event that happens at 23 seconds. But you live simultaneously in 22, 23, and 24 seconds.”

She lightly touched Shirone’s chest with her fist.

“So, I hit you at 23 seconds. But your avatar perceives 22, 23, and 24 seconds simultaneously. This means that what is a fixed outcome for others is still fluid for you.”

“Because 22 seconds is also my present.”

“Exactly. Since 22 to 24 seconds are all part of your present, the result of 23 seconds becomes fluid.”

Miro stepped back and gave another instruction.

“Now, let’s do the opposite. You hit me.”

Shirone took a deep breath, calming his mind. As his will to act dissipated, the vibrations of light disappeared, and the angelic avatar reappeared above him.

‘So this is it. The angel of destruction that breaks time.’

That was Time Explosion.

‘Now!’

The moment Shirone’s eyes flashed, the angel’s form collapsed and reabsorbed into his body. Then, dozens of afterimages burst forth, each preparing to strike Miro.

‘These must represent the probabilities of future events.’

The differing brightness of the light confirmed his theory.

‘The brighter one must be the most likely outcome.’

As Miro bent her upper body to dodge, Shirone’s fist was already touching her cheek.

As expected, Miro felt the trembling sensation of his fist against her skin and looked up.

“Do you get it now?”

“… Yes.”

From Shirone’s perspective, it had been too easy.

Miro ducked, and Shirone simply swung his fist toward where her face would be. That was all.

“For ordinary people, an event that will happen one second in the future is the same as a current event for you.”

Miro straightened her posture and adjusted her clothes.

“Your avatar technique fundamentally manipulates time. Since you perceive three seconds at once, to others, your actions will appear as mere probabilities.”

“That’s what the light afterimages you saw were?”

“Exactly. The light afterimages flowing along the boundaries of your body represent the probabilities of events that will occur over three seconds. But for you, perceiving three seconds at once is so natural that you don’t see them.”

Shirone listened with a blank expression.

While he understood that this ability was a manifestation of his unique characteristics, it still felt like a singularity that defied common sense.

“Now for the final test.”

Miro slowly widened the distance between them.

“This time, I’ll attack you with my avatar. I’ll hold back, but it might still cause mental shock. If you can evade it, you’ll have grasped the basics of avatar techniques.”

The avatar of the Thousand-Handed Avalokitesvara that he had seen before during training.

When the training hall’s candles were extinguished, it moved at a speed too fast to track with the naked eye.

“Alright. I’ll give it a try.”

Recalling that earlier vision, Shirone focused his gaze.

From the state of oneness, the shimmer of light intertwined and reformed into the angelic avatar with wings of light.

The Angel of Time and the Thousand-Handed Avalokitesvara.

The two avatars, summoned by their wielders, glared at each other from the heights of the hall’s ceiling.
