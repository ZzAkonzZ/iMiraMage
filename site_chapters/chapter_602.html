Chapter 602:

When Shirone and Neide arrived at the school, they headed to the Supernatural Psychic Research Laboratory.

Sitting on the sofa, Shirone glared and said, “You said you’d never come back.”

Hearing that Liz was coming to the school must have changed his mind instantly.

“I-I just thought I should apologize to the others. Should I leave again?”

Anyway, getting Neide back was an achievement.

“Never mind. You should rest. You must be exhausted from staying in the mountains.”

Lying on the opposite sofa with his arm as a pillow, Neide recalled the story Shirone had told him on the way.

‘You must have had a hard time too, Shirone.’

As he was lost in thought, someone knocked on the door, and both of them jumped up simultaneously.

“Who is it?”

It was natural to be wary of uninvited guests since the Scramble Royal had just ended.

“It’s Richard.”

Richard, the alchemist from the Golden Wheel.

Neide hesitated at the appearance of someone who didn’t belong there, but Shirone spoke.

“Come in.”

He was curious about the outcome of Fermi and Lycan.

The door opened slowly, and Richard entered, his red eyes trailing behind him.

With a solid build and a strong jaw, he looked human, but beneath his synthetic skin, alchemical machinery operated instead of muscles and bones.

“Why are you here? Is it a message from the Golden Wheel?”

“No, it’s a personal request from Fermi. The Golden Wheel was disbanded yesterday.”

“What?”

It was shocking news.

“Why?”

“I don’t know the exact reason. Fermi and Lycan had a falling out.”

It seemed neither of them had died.

‘Well, Fermi was severely injured at the time.’

Neide asked, “What about the other members?”

“Kaiden is missing. Hershey is furious about being used in the Scramble Royal.”

It was understandable.

“Then why did you leave Fermi?”

They had gathered more for the profits Fermi generated than for loyalty.

But that made this decision surprising.

“Fermi’s value hasn’t diminished, has it?”

“No. But Fermi suggested it first. If the Golden Wheel wasn’t disbanded, he would have to assist two of your team members in the graduation exam.”

“Oh…”

If the Golden Wheel ceased to exist, the obligation to assist two members would be nullified.

“He planned it from the start.”

If he intended to disband the Golden Wheel anyway, he could make empty promises in negotiations.

“Probably. Fermi never makes a losing deal. I don’t know what happened, but he seemed satisfied.”

Shirone recalled the last scene between Fermi and Yolga.

“I came here to inform you of that. The Golden Wheel is disbanded. So, no unnecessary disputes.”

“Fermi plans to graduate this time, right?”

“Yes. It might be better for you. I left with a payout too. A severance package.”

With that, Richard left the research society.

At first, it felt like a blow to the back of the head, but thinking from Fermi’s perspective, everything made sense.

“We were completely fooled. We should have known from the start.”

“That’s impossible, which is why Fermi got in.”

“Sigh. I need to tell the others.”

Two hours later, Amy and Iruki returned to the research society after their evaluations.

“Neide! What happened to you?”

Seeing the scars on Neide’s face, Amy shouted.

“Sorry. I messed up. I have no excuse.”

Iruki spoke.

“It’s fine. We won anyway. Now focus on improving your rank. The graduation exam is really close.”

“Oh, speaking of which…”

Shirone informed them that the Golden Wheel had disbanded, and they were equally shocked.

“Fermi, that coward! He pulled tricks until the end?”

“Let’s think positively. The distribution was unclear, and internal conflicts could have arisen. The other team members will understand.”

Having prepared for over ten years to meet Yolga, they had no choice but to acknowledge it this time.

After a brief welcome back for Neide, Shirone and his friends returned to their dorms to prepare for the next day.

“Iruki.”

Shirone called in front of Iruki’s room.

“Do you know a senior named Liz?”

“Liz?”

Iruki looked surprisingly shocked.

“How do you know that name?”

“Well, I met her this morning. With Neide.”

“I see.”

Iruki glanced at Neide’s room.

“No wonder he seemed down. So, did you talk?”

“Yes. She even bought us a meal. Liz is important to Neide, right?”

“Of course. She’s the one who made that monster of a guy get his act together.”

“What kind of person was she?”

“Hmm, if I had to describe her…”

Recalling Senior Liz, Iruki gave a brief definition.

“She was just like Neide is now.”

Four years ago, when Neide was fifteen.

At that time, he exuded such a menacing aura that his gaze alone could instill fear in others.

He only exchanged a few words with Iruki and spoke to no one else. His solitary behavior was most noticeable during lunchtime.

When Neide received his food and turned around, his friends, who had been watching his back, quickly looked away.

They were afraid he might sit next to them.

“Humph.”

Snorting, he looked around and saw that Iruki was also sitting alone at a table.

They acknowledged each other’s abilities, but eating together with those shunned by others seemed ridiculous, so Neide sat at a table in the corner.

That’s when Liz approached.

“Hello?”

Looking up, Neide saw Liz smiling, and behind her, he heard the murmurs of students.

Liz, a top student in Class Four.

Neide had no connection to this senior, so he couldn’t understand why she was doing this.

“What do you want?”

At that time, Neide didn’t care if she was a senior.

“Isn’t it rude to say ‘what do you want’ to someone who says hello?”

“If you don’t have a reason, get lost. You’re ruining my appetite.”

“Is eating alone enjoyable? Let’s eat together. It’s good for seniors and juniors to get along.”

Liz couldn’t stand seeing someone being left out.

“Annoying. Get lost.”

Liz’s friends behind her were the ones who got angry at Neide’s coldness.

“What a rude kid. We’re letting it slide because he’s young, but he thinks he’s really tough.”

“Liz, just come back. It’s best not to get involved with a crazy guy like that.”

Even though she was a senior, Neide held back once.

“Why don’t you go? Your friends are calling you.”

“Alright. I’ll leave if you answer honestly.”

“What?”

“Aren’t you really lonely?”

Bang!

When he slammed the table, all the students turned to look, and Liz, who had been shrugging her shoulders, froze with wide eyes.

In the cold silence, Neide, taking in all their gazes, stood up and placed a hand on Liz’s shoulder.

“Don’t mess with me again. You might get beaten to death.”

Liz’s face turned slightly pale, but she stuck out her tongue playfully.

After Neide walked away, her friends gathered around.

“Liz, are you okay?”

“What’s with that guy? Should we call him after class?”

“I’ll teach him a lesson. He doesn’t belong in a magic school.”

Still looking flustered, Liz finally seemed to come to her senses and took a deep breath.

“Wow, he’s no joke. He’s even pricklier than I heard.”

“Why did you talk to him? He skips classes and hangs out with bad kids outside school.”

“Hmm.”

Despite her friends’ concerns, Liz looked at Neide’s back with interest.

“Damn!”

After parting with her friends and returning to his room, Neide curled up on his bed, recalling the moment.

If only she hadn’t approached him in the first place, he wouldn’t be suffering like this.

But he couldn’t blame her because the life she had left behind had become too precious to him.

“Engaged?”

Feeling his head heat up, Neide buried his face in his bed.

“Ah! What am I supposed to do?”

A few days later, Liz brought Oscar to Alpheas Magic School.

“Do I really need to come? This isn’t even my school.”

Oscar was clearly displeased.

“But he’s my mentor. I need to greet him and tell him about the engagement.”

This was the reason.

“Liz, let’s talk for a moment.”

Oscar stopped in the hallway leading to the principal’s office.

“Why are you in such a hurry? We can talk about the engagement later.”

“Who’s in a hurry? What’s wrong with mentioning it while we’re here?”

“This is a complicated issue. We need to think about our jobs. If I get assigned here, what about the armory?”

“Yes, I know. But Oscar, I’m not rushing you. It’s just…”

After some thought, Liz confessed.

“Aren’t we in love?”

Liz was a wonderful woman, and Oscar had fallen for her at first sight.

He didn’t want to lose her.

‘Love. If love is everything…’

Seeing Oscar hesitate, Liz felt a sudden fear and, as always, decided to yield.

“Alright. Maybe it’s too soon to talk about the engagement. Let’s just say hello.”

“I’m sorry. I have a lot to think about. As you know, joining the association is a huge opportunity…”

“Haha! I understand. Come on, relax and let’s go!”

When Liz, having calmed Oscar, knocked on the principal's office door and entered, Alpheas greeted her with a smile, having already been informed of their visit.

“It’s been a while, Liz.”

“Hello, Principal! You still look as young as ever!”

“Haha! I used to enjoy those jokes a year ago. Please, have a seat.”

Alpheas offered them seats and served tea.

“And who is this handsome gentleman?”

Oscar, addressing a certified 4th-level mage, bowed politely.

“Hello. I’m Clid Oscar, a certified 8th-level mage.”

“He’s my boyfriend. He’s going to be assigned to the Creas Magic Association.”

“I see. He looks reliable.”

After some small talk, Liz asked, “So, what was the first thing you mentioned? Is something wrong?”

Alpheas shook his head as if in distress.

“Don’t get me started. I’ve aged a lot in the past year. There are so many troublemakers in the graduating class.”

“I met Neide recently. He said he’s ranked last.”

“Rankings don’t matter to those kids anymore, do they? They’re your direct juniors in the research society.”

“Hehe.”

Liz took it as an honorable compliment.

“But that’s not the only reason. This year’s graduating class is exceptionally high-level. There’s never been such fierce competition in the school’s history.”

“Oh, really?”

“Yes. The students’ average abilities are 3.8 times higher than those of other schools.”

Oscar couldn’t understand.

“An average of 3.8 times?”

“Yes. The gap between the top and bottom ranks is unimaginable. It’s not an exaggeration to say that rankings are meaningless.”

Liz, with a dazed expression, said, “That’s incredible. It must be tough for you, Principal.”

He probably wished he could graduate all the seniors.

“Yes. But this is just part of the process. It’s tough now, but the students graduating this year will grow into mages who will shine the brightest in the kingdom.”

And such mages were needed in turbulent times.

“What about Neide…”

Reading Liz’s concern, Alpheas said, “Don’t worry. The kids in the Supernatural Psychic Research Society are not falling behind the top competitors. They’re fighting admirably.”

Alpheas, known for his kind nature but never for empty words, made Liz smile brightly.

“Of course. They’re my juniors, after all.”
