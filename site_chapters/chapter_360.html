Chapter 360: Magic Society (6)

Shirone caught a carriage back to the Magic Association, still feeling the sting from the scolding he had received.

Holding the metal box tightly, he pouted as Plu scoffed at him.

“What? Upset because you got scolded? Who told you to jump into the scene without permission? Do you know what kind of situation this is? Using magic without a magician's license is against the kingdom's laws.”

“I'm sorry about that. But people were about to die.”

Plu couldn't argue with that.

“Hmph, that's that, and this is this. You did it knowing you'd have to take responsibility, so don't complain.”

Shirone fell silent, knowing she was right. Moreover, the terrorist attack had completely changed his perception of Vashka.

“I've heard about Radum.”

Plu, who had been looking out the window, turned her gaze to him.

“A schoolmate of mine used to live there. They said it was a terrible place.”

“Hmm, that's true. The residents of Radum are prohibited from economic activities.”

“Why does the kingdom leave such a place as it is? Terrorism is certainly bad, but isn't there a more peaceful solution?”

Plu was silent for a while before speaking.

“Do you know what happens if you don't have a trash can in your house?”

“What? A trash can?”

“Eventually, the whole house becomes a trash can. But in reality, having a trash can keeps the house clean. Radum is like that. It's the capital's trash can. It doesn't produce anything, but its existence has value. That's why even the nobles can't easily get rid of it.”

“But that's...”

“I know what you're thinking, but don't say it. Convictions can't be forced. If you have a conviction, you have to achieve it yourself.”

Shirone lowered his head, feeling dejected. Discussing ideals that couldn't be realized didn't seem to apply in the professional world.

“But today's incident might make the politicians think differently.”

Shirone looked up again.

“Bat mimicry is a unique ability of vampires. But it's never been reported to manifest in broad daylight. Something is changing in Radum. It could be genetic modification or fusion...”

It was certainly a plausible theory.

Radum was a district where many non-human races who had fled the continent lived together. If there was a place for biological experiments, it would be there.

Shirone turned to look out the window. The capital's scenery was as splendid as when he first arrived. But now, he could see the shadows.

Light and darkness. They defined each other. But Shirone didn't know which came first.

As they crossed the threshold of the Magic Association, Plu turned to Shirone.

“I'll go report, so take the items to the maintenance team in the control room. And stay there! Don't wander off again!”

Shirone hadn't wandered off except for errands. But he turned and walked away, understanding her concern. He walked carefully to the control room, worried the items might be damaged.

Fortunately, the maintenance team inspected the items and found no issues.

Relieved, Shirone left the control room, only to find Plu waiting for him. Her expression was colder than before, indicating she had been reprimanded by her superior.

“Did the report go well...?”

“Ugh, seriously!”

Plu suddenly smacked Shirone on the head.

“Ow! Why do you keep hitting me?”

Having reached his limit, Shirone glared at her angrily.

“I can't stand you! Do you know how much trouble you caused with this incident? Acting all high and mighty without even having a license!”

“It wasn't about showing off. People were dying!”

“Quiet! And now everyone knows you're here at the Association! This is a serious problem. The Chairman wanted to meet you quietly, but now I don't know what to do!”

Shirone felt regretful about that.

“There's nothing we can do about it now.”

“Oh, so you can just leave and be done with it? Are you trying to drive me crazy? Come with me!”

Plu grabbed Shirone by the ear and dragged him to his quarters. She shoved him inside and pointed a finger at him.

“Don't take a single step out of here! Stay put until things calm down.”

Shirone rubbed his sore ear, frowning.

“Isn't this too much? You sent me on an errand.”

“And now you're acting all high and mighty...!”

Just as Plu was about to yell again, Gando's voice came from the corridor.

“Plu, what's going on?”

Plu turned with an exaggeratedly aggrieved expression.

“Mr. Gando, this kid...”

Her words were cut off as she froze. Behind the concerned Gando were two people.

Kangnan, the Chief Secretary of the Magic Association.

And the man with the black fur coat draped over his shoulders was Mikael Gaold.

Seeing Gaold, Plu felt her heart might burst.

She knew he was returning today, but she hadn't expected to meet him here. Since she had joined, the Chairman had rarely come down to the third floor.

‘Why is he here...?’

He could have just sent for Shirone, but he had come to the third floor immediately upon his return.

Moreover, she hadn't yet dealt with the mistake Shirone had made. The news of Shirone's presence at the Magic Association was inexcusable.

Plu instinctively snapped to attention and shouted,

“Squad! Attention!”

Gando rubbed his forehead and shook his head. Gaold, on the other hand, smiled with interest.

“Heh, am I the only one who can't see them? There's no squad here.”

“Ah... I'm sorry! I was nervous!”

Kangnan, who had been partially hidden by Gaold, asked,

“Is everything alright? I heard there was an incident earlier.”

Plu, feeling that the moment of reckoning had come, spoke with wide eyes.

“Ah! I have something to report. Shirone was involved in the terrorist incident earlier...”

“It doesn't matter.”

Gaold interrupted dismissively and walked past Plu. Her face remained frozen in a rigid expression.

Shirone looked up at Gaold and swallowed hard.

He was taller than Shirone had imagined. The strongest magician in the kingdom, whose reputation had only grown since Shirone arrived, stood before him. It might have been an illusion, but Gaold seemed to tower to the ceiling.

Gaold smiled and said,

“You must be Shirone.”

Episode 3: The Strongest Magician

Shirone finally got to ride the Magic Association's elevator, something he had longed for. But the atmosphere was tense, so he couldn't openly show his excitement. Someone needed to press a button, but no one moved.

Gaold stood with his hands behind his back, looking at the floor indicator. Kangnan was meditating in the corner, and Gando couldn't hide his discomfort standing next to Gaold.

‘Mr. Gaold...’

Plu, with her head bowed, felt a mix of disappointment and sadness.

She had done her best to uphold the name of Gaold's elite guard. But all her efforts seemed meaningless in the face of Shirone's name.

Shirone's presence at the Association had likely reached the ears of magicians who opposed Gaold. This project was Gaold's life's work, something he had prepared for over 20 years.

Yet Gaold had said,

It doesn't matter.

She felt utterly insignificant.

She had admired him since her school days and had worked for him at the Association for two years, but she felt less valuable than Shirone, who wasn't even a full-fledged magician.

Kangnan suddenly opened her eyes and walked to the panel.

“Alright, let's go down.”

Shirone looked puzzled.

‘Go down?’

They were on the third floor, so going down meant the first floor. They wouldn't take the elevator to the second floor.

Kangnan took out a key, opened the panel, and placed her fingerprint on a glass plate. A thin light scanned it, and the internal mechanism activated. A panel with 36 buttons, arranged in a six-by-six grid, emerged.

Kangnan took a deep breath and entered a 24-step pattern.

Once approved, a female voice announced from the ceiling,

Moving to the 10th basement floor. Level 1 security area.

Gando and Plu glanced at Gaold. They had taken the elevator together, but only Gaold had the authority to decide how far they could accompany him.

“Hmm.”

Gaold, unconcerned, observed Shirone. Noticing Shirone's gaze fixed on the panel, he chuckled.

“Fascinated?”

“What? Oh, yes. It's my first time in an elevator. I didn't know it had a password.”

“Heh, did you memorize it sneakily?”

“No, I didn't!”

Shirone waved his hands frantically, then blushed and confessed.

“Yes, actually...”

“Haha! Thorough, aren't you? Well, that's how a magician should be. But it won't help. The code changes daily. And the best part is, no one at the Association knows the code.”

Shirone tilted his head in confusion.

“No one knows? But Kangnan just...”

“Yes, she entered the pattern correctly. But she doesn't know what she pressed.”

Was that possible? Shirone wondered.

“How is that possible?”

“The pattern is stored in the subconscious, not the memory. Think of it like playing an instrument. If you practice the same piece every day, your hands move before you think. It's similar. Kangnan spends an hour each day looking at the pattern generated by a random code device. It's important not to think about it. Then she just presses the buttons as her hands move.”

Gaold mimed pressing random buttons in the air.

"Of course, it's different from playing an instrument and requires training, but once mastered, it's more secure than any other encryption system. The more you try to consciously recall it, the harder it becomes."

Shirone was amazed by the extraordinary security system. The fact that such an encryption method, which he hadn't even read about in books, was already in use at the Association spoke volumes about its technological prowess.

‘He seems really excited. Is he oblivious because the Chairman is being nice to him?’

Plu, listening to their conversation, glared at Shirone with annoyance.

She had never exchanged more than a few words with Gaold. Yet, Shirone was not only being taken to a Level 1 security area but also receiving a detailed explanation of the encryption system's secrets.

The elevator descended past the first floor into complete darkness. Shirone had assumed the tenth basement level would be about the same distance as ten floors above ground, but he was wrong. The elevator descended for nearly a minute before the doors finally opened.

Both Plu and Gando were tense, as it was their first time in this place. Waiting for Gaold and Kangnan to exit first, Plu looked around with excited eyes.

“Where is this...?”

It was a massive underground cavern. The ceiling was as high as the sky, and the interior, devoid of pillars, expanded far beyond the footprint of the Magic Association building.

Even Shirone, who had visited many extraordinary places, was astonished by the underground scale. The temples in Miro’s space-time and the buildings in Heaven were impressive, but the emptiness here exerted a different kind of pressure.

‘Why did we come here?’

It seemed unnecessarily spacious for a meeting place.

When Shirone looked at Kangnan, she adjusted her glasses and explained.

“This is the largest space in the Magic Association. It's an open area, but it's used as a bunker, so its durability is beyond imagination. It's also one of the few places free from the magic control devices. It was built for multiple purposes, but in peacetime, it's mainly used for demonstrating classified magic.”

“I see.”

Since the Magic Association analyzed all the magic in the kingdom, it made sense to have at least one such space.

Then Shirone realized something and looked at Gaold in surprise.

“I’m going to demonstrate magic?”

Gaold smiled and turned to look at Shirone, who flinched.

His aura heated the air in the bunker. He was a completely different person from the one Shirone had seen in the elevator.
