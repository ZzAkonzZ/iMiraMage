Chapter 633:

As the unrelenting criticism continued, the participants glanced at Nade, who still showed no will to fight.

‘I knew it would come to this.’

Prings vividly remembered the terror he had experienced at Nade’s hands during the Scramble Royal. Although Prings wasn’t exactly normal himself and had shaken it off not long after, it was an ordeal he never wanted to repeat.

‘He’s clearly a ticking time bomb no one should mess with.’

But now that the final 10 participants had been confirmed, and with nothing left to lose, taking the risk could improve his chances of impressing the scouts.

“Come on, fight me. You’ve got nowhere left to run, anyway,” Prings taunted, gesturing with his hand. However, Nade only stared blankly at the sky, looking utterly defeated.

‘Graduating? Me?’

Would it even matter?

Even if he graduated, no organization would want to hire someone who had accomplished nothing. Just as there was a distinction between official and unofficial mages, the treatment a mage received varied drastically depending on their societal recognition.

If he failed now, he’d succeed in his original plan of disappointing his mother—a success, in its own way.

“I won’t fight,” he said firmly.

The thought of clashing with Shirone was unimaginable.

“This is ridiculous,” Dante muttered. Though he had never witnessed Nade’s full capabilities, he was aware of the apprehension many skilled participants had toward him.

“Do you really expect us to just accept that you don’t want to fight?”

“I don’t care what you think. I’m not fighting.”

His arrogant response only fueled the crowd’s anger.

“A coward like him doesn’t deserve to be a mage!”

“Just crush him! Show him what real power looks like!”

Despite the audience’s outrage, Nade remained unmoved. It wasn’t as simple as it seemed.

Iruki took a step toward Nade.

“How about forfeiting? This fight means nothing to you anyway.”

Iruki recalled the resolution Nade had made the day before the graduation exam. If Nade chose to forfeit now, while it might leave some bitterness, it would at least prevent lasting grudges.

“…”

When Nade didn’t respond, Iruki pointed a finger at him.

“See? Deep down, you do want to fight. You don’t want to fail.”

‘Mother.’

Even with the crowd packed with people, spotting Teria was easy. Her face, though not noble, was strikingly beautiful. Among hundreds of faces, hers stood out, distorted like a demon’s, pressing down on Nade.

‘That horribly hateful face.’

And yet, as her child, he couldn’t help but love it.

“Get out of here! You’re a disgrace to mages! Stop tarnishing the school’s reputation!”

As the crowd’s condemnation grew louder, Teria’s face turned red with shame.

“This is humiliating! What a disgraceful display!” she spat angrily.

“Enough!” her husband, Bolum, snapped. “If anything, you should at least be supporting him.”

“Look at the other students! They’re all fighting their hardest to bring honor to their families. How can he be so selfish?”

Lycan approached Nade from behind.

“You’re annoying. Just die already.”

Grabbing Nade by the neck, he electrocuted him and hurled him to the edge of the colosseum. A massive arc of lightning followed, striking the spot where Nade had fallen.

‘Idiot! Electricity...!’

Prings’ face turned pale, and those who understood the principle of magic assimilation froze, staring at Nade.

“What’s going on?”

But Nade merely lay there, casting a forlorn gaze toward Teria.

When Nade had been tortured by the Chickenhead Bandit Gang, his mental state had shattered. His family rescued him three days later, finding him sobbing among the charred corpses. Despite pouring vast sums into his treatment, his trauma-induced seizures never fully stopped.

“Why are you crying?! Why?! Do you have any idea how much damage you’ve caused me?” Teria had yelled, furious after spending money borrowed from relatives on his treatment.

“M-Mother, I...”

Nade held back his tears desperately. His mother’s suffering was scarier than the monster inside him. Gradually, his convulsions subsided into hiccups and finally calmed.

‘What did I do wrong?’ he had thought.

Once, he had snapped and shouted, “It’s not like I wanted to be like this! What have you ever done for me?!”

Bolum had hurled a bottle at him in anger.

“You insolent brat!”

Perhaps Bolum had been annoyed at his son for upsetting his wife, but Nade felt only helpless resentment.

“Arghhh!”

Desperate to destroy the body his parents had passed down to him, Nade had run headfirst into the wall.

“Aaaaaaagh!”

Teria clawed at her own face with her fingernails.

“Dear!” Bolum cried, rushing to her side. Nade, stunned, watched as her face became bloodied.

And then…

“Just kill me! I’d rather die!” Teria screamed, slashing her wrist with a knife.

“Damn it! Someone, call a doctor!”

As the family scrambled in chaos, only Nade stood frozen, staring at his mother as if time had stopped.

“I’m sorry…”

He sniffled, his face full of regret.

‘Please don’t be angry. This is my fault.’

No child can win against their parents.

“He’s beyond saving. It’s over,” someone muttered.

As Nade wept, the remaining eight participants ignored him, clashing in the center of the colosseum in a dazzling display of magic. While the audience was mesmerized by the spectacle, only Nade and Teria exchanged glances.

“I can’t bear to watch this pathetic display,” Teria said as she stood and turned to leave. Nade clenched his fists tightly.

‘Fine, leave. I’ll never become a mage. I’ll never live the life you want me to!’

It was the outcome he had always wanted.

So why did it hurt so much?

Why did it feel like he was falling endlessly into the abyss?

‘We’ll all go down together. Let’s all die together.’

The moment Teria disappeared beyond the shadow of the exit.

“Ahhhhhhhh!” Nade let out a primal scream as he stood up.

“Why?! What the hell did I do wrong?!”

Electricity surged violently across his body.

“Why am I so wronggggggg?!”

At the same time, Nade leapt behind Shirone in a flash and swung his arm.

“Guh!”

The speed was so overwhelming that Shirone had no time to react and was sent rolling across the floor. The other participants turned their heads in shock.

“Rrrrghhhh!”

Gritting his teeth, Nade trembled violently, exuding a menacing aura as if he could explode at any moment.

“What’s his magic power level?”

Baikal asked, but Elizabeth, blinking in disbelief, was too stunned to answer.

“Elizabeth! The magic power level!”

“S-Sorry! Let me check again!”

As Nade took a single step forward, everyone retreated ten steps.

“Why? Why?!”

Nade’s outcry was fueled by an immense, unfathomable hatred that seemed to rise from the depths of his soul.

“Whyyyyyyyy?!”

The lightning pierced the sky above the colosseum, and the 2000 system emitted a warning sound.

Data capacity exceeded. Emergency system activated. Opening the second core.

The lights of the colosseum flickered.

Data capacity exceeded. Opening the third core. Opening the fourth core.

White lightning spread across the ground like pillars and scattered into hundreds of fragments, coursing chaotically throughout the arena.

“The fourth core?!”

Baikal knew this had never happened in recorded history.

Activating final stabilization system. Opening the fifth core.

Only after all five emergency systems were operational did the 2000 system stabilize, illuminating the analysts’ screens with a shared blue light.

“What’s the reading?”

“...1.48 billion magicles,” Elizabeth stammered, her voice trembling.

Baikal whipped his head around, disbelief etched on his face.

“1.48 billion?! Is that even possible?”

“The problem is…” Elizabeth continued in a shaking voice.

“It’s still climbing.”

Iruki, glaring in displeasure as he checked on the fallen Shirone, spoke up.

“What about your promise? You said you wouldn’t fight Shirone, yet you attack him like this?”

“It’s fine,” Shirone said, slowly standing up.

“This is a test. He wasn’t trying to kill me.”

Though Iruki wanted to believe him, the state of magic assimilation Nade was in left nothing but aimless murderous intent.

“Rrrrrghhhh!”

Shirone looked at Nade’s grotesquely distorted face and gave a sad smile.

“At first, I thought it was a scary face.”

He was so gentle at heart that he couldn’t even express anger unless he directed his hatred outward.

“But it’s really a face full of pain, isn’t it, Nade?”

It was why he could destroy nothing without first destroying himself.

“Rrraaaaahhhh!”

Final magic power: 1.86 billion magicles! It’s magic assimilation! If left alone, he’ll die!

“So this is the perfect chaos they feared.”

The state Nade was in had surpassed the level of chaos Shirone had previously experienced—it was incomparably violent.

Boom!

The pure white lightning burst outward from Nade’s body, expanding across the entire colosseum.

“Gaaahhhh!”

Fjord, who came into contact with the white lightning, fell to the ground screaming in agony, like a bug caught in an electric fence.

“Ughhhhhhh!”

The remaining participants pressed themselves against the walls, waiting for the storm to subside, while the audience was left dumbfounded by Nade’s overwhelming display of power.

“What the hell is that? It’s worse than Infercus!”

Even Teria was at a loss.

Though Nade’s magic assimilation had occasionally destroyed warehouses in the past, she had never imagined he was capable of such devastating power.

“This has nothing to do with me,” she muttered, turning toward the exit.

Bolum grabbed her wrist.

“Where are you going? The exam isn’t over yet.”

“Does that matter right now? If the system malfunctions, we could be sued for damages!”

“Is that all you care about? Can’t you feel Nade’s pain?”

“Let him die! I don’t want to live like this either!”

Grinding his teeth, Bolum forced her back into her seat.

“Sit down! At least today, you’re going to watch your son properly!”

The lightning that had expanded like a sphere around Nade suddenly stopped and surged upward in a single pillar.

“Now’s the time! Attack!”

As if on cue, the remaining participants charged toward Nade, hurling their magic at him.

‘Idiots! This is the 2000 system!’

In a real battle, his body might have been damaged, but attempting to deplete 1.86 billion magicles with anti-magic was an impossible dream.

‘Fools! Just go finish him off!’

Lycan cast Spark magic, pinning Nade from behind as he grabbed his face.

As he tried to twist Nade’s neck, sparks flew, and Nade disappeared, only to reappear from all directions, delivering a flurry of punches.

“Ahhhhhh!”

With the final blow, Lycan’s nose caved in, and Nade’s wrist twisted unnaturally.

“Getting hit means elimination. It’s like God’s lottery draw,” someone murmured as Nade’s chaotic lightning raged wildly.

While others hesitated, Shirone soared into the air.

“Nade!”

The avatar of the Angel of Light emerged, massive and imposing, its raised right arm gathering immense energy into a spear.

‘Angel’s punishment!’

The blinding lance of light descended at incredible speed, but Nade’s body evaporated as if vanishing into thin air.

“He dodged?”

To Nade, whose nervous system functioned like electricity, even the fastest projectiles seemed slow.

‘If that’s the case…!’

Activating Quantum Superposition, Shirone’s body multiplied into hundreds, drawing gasps from the scouts.

Quantum Superposition—300 Overlaps—Avatar Technique—Angel’s Judgment.

“This… this isn’t the level of a graduation exam.”

Three hundred angels simultaneously raised their arms and launched spears of light toward Nade.
