Chapter 225: Waiting for the New Term (3)

Capital Bashka.

Jerome Family was an educator lineage that had produced excellent teachers for generations and was one of the most prestigious families in the kingdom.

When asked who was higher than the king, the joke answer “the king’s teacher” wasn’t far from the truth.

Even if they gathered only the disciples they had raised, it would not be difficult to run an entire nation.

Arriving at the main house of Jerome Family, Alpheas walked through the ancient-style corridors guided by the butler.

Although they had known each other for 30 years, this was his first time visiting her room.

When the butler opened the door, a splendid scene greeted him.

The floor was so clean it reflected his face, and the shelves lining the walls up to the third floor were filled with all kinds of antiques.

To the north, there was a tiger rug big enough to fit ten people, and given her disdain for fakes, it was undoubtedly genuine. However, he doubted that such a large tiger had ever existed in the world.

The woman was sitting on a sofa on a leopard rug. Although in her 50s, she looked young enough to be in her 30s.

She was beautiful even now, but in her youth, she was truly stunning. She was so aloof and tidy that men found it hard to even speak to her.

“If you’re here, close the door and sit down. Why are you just standing there blankly? You’re still as stupid as ever.”

Alpheas couldn't hold back his laughter.

The temperament to look down on all men in the world remained unchanged. Her melodious voice, as clear as a nightingale’s, made him feel as if time had turned back.

“No, I’m just amazed. I wondered what kind of place you lived in, and it seems like you’ve transplanted your exact personality here.”

“Barging into someone’s house without warning and spouting nonsense shamelessly. How could a country bumpkin like you understand the value of my belongings?”

The carpet was undoubtedly top-notch. So Alpheas stomped on it with his shoes still on.

This was an opportunity for his little rebellion, the one who had made her, known as the most arrogant person in the world, cling to him by the leg of his pants.

She didn't scold Alpheas, but contrary to expectations, she looked at him with an expression of utter disbelief.

“What are you doing now? Oh, forget it. Just tell me what you want to say.”

Alpheas grinned mischievously. She was the same now as she was 30 years ago.

Olivia Jerome.

Shee is an officially certified second-class Archmage and currently serves as an inspector for the teachers’ association. Additionally, she had served as the principal of the Royal Magic Academy four years ago. (공인 제2급의 대마법사에 현재 교사회의 감사. 게다가 4년 전에는 왕립 마법학교의 교장까지 지낸 인물이었다.)

“Something I want to say? You didn’t respond, so I came to see you in person.”

“What do you want me to do about it? You got suspended from teaching because of your own misconduct, and now you want me to clean up your mess? Principal of a rural magic academy? And it’s only a six-month temporary position?”

“It’s not set in stone. You can stay longer if you want.”

“But why? Why would I leave this nice house and go to the countryside?”

“It would be nice to spend some leisurely time together, sipping tea as we grow old.”

Olivia revealed her cold anger, making her look even more like her younger self.

“Are you under some illusion? My feelings for you were 30 years ago. Do you think I’ve forgotten how you treated me? More than anything, you’re not charming anymore.”

“Haha! That’s how it is. But you’re still beautiful.”

Olivia gritted her teeth. This was why she hated men. Just because she had given her heart once, they acted as if they owned everything.

It was disgusting to see him shamelessly laughing when he was a freeloader with nothing to show for having been stripped of his position as principal.

“Who on earth are you to be so arrogant? I am an officially certified second-class Archmage, once the principal of the Royal Magic Academy. You are a fourth-rank mage and the principal of a rural academy. I earn more and am respected more. How could you act so shamelessly when you came to ask me a favor?”

“Hmm, well? Perhaps it is because you’re fond of me?”

“You... crazy... fool.”

Olivia's face flushed. To think he could still utter such creepy words at his age. Had he gone senile? He was saying things now that he wouldn’t have dared to say before.

"I'll be honest with you. I didn’t want to look pitiful after seeing you for the first time in ages.” (“솔직히 말하지. 오랜만에 만나서 비굴한 모습을 보이면 슬퍼할까 봐 그랬어.”)

“Oh, not at all? I’ve imagined countless times the sight of you kneeling before me.”

Alpheas smiled bitterly. Well, looking back, her wounds must have been deeper than his.

“Right. If you insist, I’ll make my request plainly. Do I need to kneel here?”

Olivia responded with a scoff. What difference would it make if he kneeled now?

“What have you been up to these days? You lost your job. Are you really going through a late-life crisis?”

“Just reading books, going for walks, and talking about various things with Erina.”

For the first time, shock struck Olivia's eyes.

Alpheas' first and last love. Erina still lives in his heart.

'You still can't forget her. You're the biggest fool in the world.'

After wandering for ten years following the loss of his wife, Alpheas returned with dreams of becoming a teacher and became a student of the young Olivia. As a great genius who established the quantum theory of light, Olivia taught him with affection.

Affection. That was the problem. She shouldn’t have accepted as a student someone older, more talented, and more good-looking than herself.

Olivia, who could not control her growing love, eventually confessed her love to Alpheas.

But Alpheas, unable to forget his wife, rejected her flatly, and from that day on, her life changed 180 degrees. There were more than a few nights when she spent her nights crying over her crushed pride.

What really enraged her was the absurdity of losing the man she loved to a woman who didn’t even exist in this world.

The burning love of the 20s was not visible. And in the end, she ended up damaging Erina's painting in front of Alpheas.

For the first time, Olivia felt Alpheas’s murderous intent. She clung to his trousers, begging, but from that day on, Alpheas didn’t treat her as a human being anymore.

It would have been better if she had stopped there. But Olivia was so desperate and passionate at the time. She lacked the courage to give up her first love that had come to her.

But she shouldn't have done it. Going to Alpheas's room that night was the mistake of a lifetime.

“Don’t get me wrong, Alpheas. The reason I didn’t get married isn’t because of you. It’s just that I find men troublesome."

"Is that why you’re collecting all these strange items? Olivia, there’s no human who doesn’t want to be loved.”

"That’s a base emotion. Back then, I pursued you out of pride. You didn’t give me any attention, being obsessed with a dead woman."

“Suddenly… I remember what happened that night.”

Olivia's face turned pale.

"You came to my room, undressed…"

"Stop!"

Olivia held out her hand and screamed. Though it happened 30 years ago, it was as vivid as if it were yesterday. It was the first and last time she had shown her naked body to a man.

At that time, she wanted to win Alpheas' heart even that way. But he didn’t even grant her the simple request of holding her just once.

'You bastard...'

Even thinking about it now made her so sad she could cry.

To brag about the most humiliating moment of her life like that—why did women always have to be the ones who suffered?

'I can't forgive you. I will make sure you regret this.'

Olivia, having failed to gain the upper hand, decided to get to the point and picked up the envelope lying on the sofa.

"Fine. Let’s take a look."

The document contained information about Alpheas Magic Academy.

“Alpheas Magic Acaddemy. Ranked 4th among the 5 prestigious magic Academy. Ranked 11th among all special purpose academy. That’s pretty high for an idiot. When it first opened, it was truly a mess."

“I was lucky. I had many good students.”

Olivia felt like smacking Alpheas on the head as she used to when they were teacher and student.

"I’ve heard the stories. There’s a boy named Shirone, right?"

“You did a lot of research. Were you planning to agree from the beginning?"

"Think what you want, but what I’m curious about is that the academy only rose in the early. If you look at their performance over the past decade, it is just about on par with the top 5.”

“I did my best to provide students with a good learning environment. Isn’t that enough? The academy’s ranking isn’t important."

"Haha! Still as old-fashioned as ever."

Olivia uncrossed her legs and leaned forward.

"Fine. Six months. I’ll take it on. I’ll make it much more prestigious than it is now."

"Oh? That’s an ominous thing to hear, but I’d be grateful if you did."

“But there is a condition.”

Alpheas raised one eyebrow and smiled. He hadn’t expected her to agree without some kind of compensation.

"I have students I taught when I was the principal of the Royal Magic Academy. Transfer them to your academy. Here are their profiles."

Alpheas postponed the answer and received the profile first.

They were a boy and a girl of the same age, both 17 years old, and their grades were outstanding even at the Royal Magic Academy, the most prestigious academy in the kingdom.

As he flipped through the three profiles one by one, Alpheas' eyes opened wide on the last page.

"Dante? Is this the Dante I know?"

Olivia laughed, covering her mouth.

"Hoho! He’s too good a student for your academy. But now I’m the principal."

Alpheas blinked in disbelief. He didn't know that the world-renowned Olivia would put in this much effort.

Olivia declared, pointing to Alpheas.

"I’ll transfer Eirhain Dante and two others. That’s my condition."

Five mages were racing through the rugged mountains.

It was a young party consisting of people in their early to mid 20s, all of whom were experts in their respective fields and had obtained official certification.

The raid location was a hideout of bandits who had set up a base in the mountains near the capital, and they were a force to be reckoned with, a B-rank bounty.

Destroying a bandit group would give them fame and rewards, but the reason they were climbing the mountain today was different from usual.

"Why is it so quiet? Could they already be dead? They’re just kids, no matter how rude they were."

"Hmph! Does it matter? It’s not like we killed them. You were there too. You saw how much they disrespected us. Just because they go to the Royal Magic Academy?"

“That’s right. No matter how immature you are, how can you ignore a certified mage who is still active? Kids these days are too arrogant. They need to get hit hard before they come to their senses.”

When we finally arrived at the hideout, it was as quiet as a mouse.

As they opened the door with a puzzled mind, a surprising sight unfolded. There were about 40 B-class bandits lying all over the ground.

The students sitting around the round table welcomed them.

"Oh? You’re late? We’ve been waiting for quite a while."

The mercenaries were speechless. They had left only ten minutes late. It was impossible for students to have wiped out a B-rank bandit gang in such a short time.

“Wha- What the hell are you?”

"Why are you taking this so seriously? It’s embarrassing for grown-ups. Haha!"

A boy with a tall and muscular build, unusual for a mage, burst out laughing.

He was Bonipar Closer, a student at the Royal Magic Academy.

His face was sharp with copper-colored skin and his dark brown hair, slicked back, stood out like a hedgehog.

“Hahaha! You were boasting so loudly just a few days ago. Did you come late on purpose because you were scared?"

A girl with cat-like features, Victor Savina, giggled as she mocked the mercenaries. She was small for her age, but her growth was rapid with a waist that looked tightly bound by a rope.

The mercenaries clenched their teeth. Losing to students was a humiliation for active mages. More importantly, if word got out, they could be blacklisted in the mercenary industry.

“I think it’s time to admit it. We won, right?”

Eirhain Dante, the cause of all this, spoke up. With gray hair reminiscent of storm clouds and a handsome yet intellectual and fierce appearance, he commanded attention.

The mercenaries lowered their heads. For active mages, reputation and achievements were paramount. What client would pay to hire a mage who was defeated by a student?
