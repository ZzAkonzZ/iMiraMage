Chapter 285: Terraje’s Proposal (2)

“So you can’t accept me?”

Uorin scratched her cheek with an apologetic expression.

"I'd like to help, but there are many daughters of Teraje in numerous kingdoms who are my rivals. I am particularly young and do not want to stand out yet. And Kazra isn’t exactly a strong foundation either. There’s no need to draw attention and become a target for my older sisters, right?”

Shirone felt a bitter feeling well up inside. Fourteen years old. That was the age of the girl sitting in front of him.

‘You’re really something, Uorin.’

He also realized anew how ridiculous his own behavior had been a few days ago.

Even now that he understood the reality of the royal palace, it was chilling. What kind of guts did he have before to try to deal with a woman like this?

"Fine. If there's no room for negotiation, I'll leave. I need to find Amy."

Shirone stood up as if there was no need to listen further.

If his assumption was wrong, everything would spiral into the worst-case scenario, but there was nothing he could do.

Because there are no fools in the royal palace who miss the time to act no matter how great the danger.

"I wish you luck."

Hearing Uorin’s parting words made his heart drop. But he walked towards the door without showing it.

He must not show hesitation or fear. He had to truly let go of everything and leave this room.

'Ataraxia is not needed. Is that really so?'

Perhaps to Uorin, it didn’t matter much.

But even if it was just a sliver of gain, people always found it hard to give away even the smallest advantage to someone else.

Shirone walked, holding on to a small hope as if it were his lifeline.

Even as his hand reached for the doorknob and began to turn it, Uorin did not respond.

The door opened, and Shirone clenched his eyes shut.

This was the end of everything.

“But if perhaps...”

Shirone's steps came to an abrupt halt.

"If I were to make the maximum concession... that is, if we consider it as borrowing against Oppa’s future..."

Shirone slowly turned to look back at Uorin.

“You could earn at least an opportunity to negotiate with the daughter of Teraje.”

"An opportunity to negotiate?"

Even if he pledged Ataraxia as collateral, all he would gain was the chance to sit at the negotiation table? And even that opportunity requires borrowing against his future.

“Yes. A negotiation. I can’t just help you for nothing. Like I said earlier, I don’t want to put myself in a dangerous position unnecessarily. There's one condition. It shouldn't be a particularly difficult condition, but anyway, if you agree to this condition, I'll be on your side."

Shirone raised his chin as if to say, ‘Go ahead, tell me’.

“Tonight, spend the night with me.”

“Wha- What?”

It was absurd for a condition put forward at the end of such an intense psychological battle. What reason could there be for him to spend the night with her?

As if reading Shirone's thoughts, Uorin added.

"Don't worry. I didn't mean it that way. Just stay with me tonight. Well... if you happen to have other ideas, I don't mind if you try."

Shirone's expression crumpled.

"What on earth are you saying? You're my half-sister."

"Well, that's something we'll only know after the paternity test is done. And honestly, it doesn’t matter. Same-sex marriage is common for royalty who bring a whole kingdom as a dowry."

Shirone couldn’t understand. It felt like solving a series of difficult exam problems only to encounter a nonsensical riddle at the end.

"Why on earth? What does that condition have to do with helping me?"

Uorin sighed reluctantly with a "hmm" and, for the first time, told her own story.

"The daughters of Teraje Family have always been the ones to ascend to the emperor's throne. It might sound strange, but for us, men are merely tools to be consumed according to their use. That's why a daughter of Teraje doesn't keep a man who has another woman in his heart by her side. If Oppa is thinking of Amy, then I cannot accept you either.”

Shirone understood. For a woman of Teraje Family, it wasn't strange to have such an iron rule.

But Uorin was mistaken. Amy was his closest and dearest friend, not someone he held romantic feelings for.

"I understand what you're saying. But I don't have Amy in my heart."

The corners of Uorin's mouth slightly rose.

“I think Zion Oppa is interested in her.”

Something hot welled up in Shirone's chest.

“Of course, if she rejects him, nothing will happen. Despite how he seems, Oppa has too much pride to force himself on a woman who turns him down.”

If Uorin’s words were true, it was a relief. But the fire that had ignited in his heart showed no signs of going out.

Uorin clapped her hands together and said,

"Well then, let's do this. I just want to confirm your feelings. From this moment on, I'll guarantee the safety of Amy and everyone else. I could send people right now and give the order. All you need to do is spend the night with me.”

Shirone didn’t know what kind of threat Amy was under that made her follow Zion, but if he accepted Uorin’s offer, Amy could be saved. On top of that, his family and Reina’s safety would be ensured. There was no reason to refuse.

“Zion… Where did he take Amy?”

I can’t reveal that yet, not until you’ve made your decision. It’s very simple. You just need to trust me. Show me that I take precedence over that woman."

A trembling that started at his fingertips spread throughout his body. Everything was falling in his favor, yet he couldn’t bring himself to make the decision.

Because he couldn’t be sure.

Was human existence so bound by the five senses? What on earth should he believe to follow her words?

There wasn’t a single piece of evidence to suggest that Uorin wasn’t helping Zion. If, by any chance, Uorin was lying, then Amy was in tremendous danger.

"Why are you hesitating? I'm Uorin of Teraje. Not only do I not lie, but isn't this proposal what you wanted? If you can prove that you don’t have feelings for another woman, I’ll support you wholeheartedly. I’ll spare no resources to help you grow your abilities, and when I become empress, you could become the greatest mage in the world."

It was truly a dream-like story for Shirone. And she had the ability to turn that dream into reality.

Even archmages are writing resumes wanting to work under Uorin. Such a huge opportunity was lying right before his eyes.

Lost in thought, Shirone finally made his decision.

"I'm going to Amy. I won't be accepting your proposal."

Uorin sighed, as though frustrated.

"Are you throwing away the incredible opportunity to form a connection with Teraje? No, more than that, you could protect the family you hold so dear. And yet, you’re willing to throw it all away over a woman?”

"That's right. That's what I intend to do."

Uorin closed her mouth as if shocked. But on Shirone's face, neither resignation nor regret could be found.

"But Uorin, for humans, that's everything. The only world that exists is the one we see and feel. Even if you truly save Amy, if I can’t see it for myself, then it’s just an unrealized world. I can’t leave Amy in visible danger while trusting in some invisible safety. That’s why I have to go to Amy."

Sadness briefly flickered in Uorin’s eyes.

"You make life so hard for yourself. It would be easy if you just made a choice."

Shirone smiled bitterly. He often thought the same thing himself. But that's precisely the shackle of intellect. Once you know something, you can’t act as if you don’t.

"I understand how great this opportunity was. I also know how much you, the heir to the emperor, conceded to help me. I'm not doubting you at all. But I have to go."

Uorin walked up to Shirone and hugged him, burying her face against his chest.

"You're such a fool, Oppa. I'm sorry I couldn't do anything for you. Personally, I really liked you. I hope good fortune follows your future."

Then, stepping back a couple of steps, she revealed the information Shirone wanted to know.

“Go to the gallery. It’s the only place where you can avoid people’s eyes. It’s also the place Oppa takes women when he wants to seduce them.”

Once again, a fire ignited in Shirone’s heart. As he readied himself to leave, Uorin pulled something out of her pocket.

"Wait a moment. Take this."

Shirone caught the small object Uorin threw.

He opened his hand to find a glowing red orb. By its color, it seemed to be a ruby, but it lacked the cold texture characteristic of gems.

"This is...?"

"It's the price for El Crouch's porcelain doll. It should be worth at least 100 million."

“No, I really meant for that to be a gift...”

“Of course, I understand your sincerity. But reason and emotion are separate matters. Now that negotiations have broken down, I can't be indebted to you, can I?"

With those words, Shirone had no choice but to accept.

While he felt personal affection, politically they were now strangers. Clearing up any debts was the best way forward for both of them.

"Alright. I'll use it well when I graduate later."

Shirone put the orb in his pocket and walked to the door.

Then he suddenly stopped and looked back at Uorin. Thinking about it, he had received a lot of help from her.

At the dinner table, she had upheld his parents’ dignity and supported his claim to attend magic academy.

In the gallery, she had introduced him to the Objet, and at the Colosseum, she had helped him understand the true nature of the royal palace.

And now, as payment for the porcelain doll, she had provided him with escape funds. In a situation where he was being pursued by the royal palace, 100 million gold would be of great help.

“Thank you, for everything, from the beginning to now. Thinking about it, if it weren’t for you, I wouldn’t have made it this far.”

“Huhu, even in hell, there’s water to drink.”

Uorin answered with Kazra's proverb.

It means there's hope even in difficult situations, but in reverse, it also implies that the small glimmer of hope only strengthens the suffering.

“Haha! That makes sense.”

Realizing the situation he was in, Shirone let out a hollow laugh as he left Zion's room.

“Phew.”

Once the door closed, Uorin let out a deep breath. Then, putting her hands behind her back, which she usually didn't do, she paced around the room.

"Arian Shirone..."

Stopping her steps, she quickly looked back at the door.

Her eyes, now filled with a cold smile, gleamed with madness, like a child standing before the best toy they had ever seen.

“He’s really no ordinary person, is he?”

Uorin burst into laughter and cheerfully returned to her seat, plopping down in the chair. Crossing her legs and resting her chin on her hand, she stared intently at the door through which Shirone had exited.

Of what Uorin had just told Shirone, less than 10 percent was true.

Everything was a lie. She had no intention of putting Shirone to good use, nor of guaranteeing Amy's safety.

It wasn't true that Zion had strong pride either.

Rather, he was a person who would eliminate any woman he set his sights on by any means necessary.

Had Shirone stayed with her this night, his life would have been completely ruined.

"Humans don't have the sense to trust others. Isn't that right, Cleo?"

Meow.

Uorin's cat answered. It was highly unlikely that it would understand human speech, but it didn't matter anyway.

Humans had no way of truly trusting one another.

But people live their lives ignoring that simple truth just because they find the idea unpleasant.

Those who acknowledge the truth and those who turn away from it.

Uorin believed that was the small difference that divided rulers from the ruled in this world.

Uorin tilted her head back over the chair's backrest, looked up at the ceiling, and smiled faintly.

“If you keep refining yourself, one day you might reach that understanding.”

The cat did not answer.
