Chapter 831: Breaking the System (3)

Rian dreamed.

Or perhaps the signals embedded at the genetic level manifested as images through the dream.

Ymir looked down indifferently at Smile, who had perfectly transformed into a human form.

"Has he really gone?"

The only injury he sustained from the blow was a single molar.

As his true form, he could regenerate at any time, but he didn't feel like doing so.

"This doesn't feel satisfying."

The feeling of incompleteness was even more unpleasant than the emptiness he felt after destroying countless weaklings.

He wanted to fight until his whole body was shattered.

A fight so difficult that even if he coughed up blood, screamed, and unleashed all his power, it wouldn't be enough.

While there were a few others who could be considered Ymir's natural enemies, like the three-horned Mara Ashur of Ikael, Ogent was the first person to push him to his limits with sheer strength.

'This world is too light.'

As Ymir reached out to the sun and clenched his fist, the air shook left and right.

"It doesn't even feel real."

A world made of paper that crumbled before he could even savor the sensation of touching it.

To him, a formidable opponent was the only tool that could make all his senses vivid.

"Did he kill him?"

Kariel, covered in dust, approached after being struck by Ogent.

As his halo emitted light, his injured body quickly recovered, but his expression remained grim.

"No, he's asleep."

"What do you mean?"

Ymir pointed to Smile.

"He collapsed the boundary of life and reverse-evolved. He returned to his original form and merged with the woman."

"That's impossible."

At the end of the cells converging through reverse evolution was Argones, known as the father of life.

"It's against Argones' law. No creature can become mucus on its own."

A creature without characteristics is called mucus.

"It's impossible."

Ymir nodded calmly.

"But he transcended that impossibility. Form is merely a shadow of a specific signal. As long as the sun is up..."

As Ymir stomped on his shadow, the ground sank a foot with a thud.

"Shadows can't be destroyed."

They can only be transformed.

"This is why he could reach breaking the System with a mere human body. To destroy him, you need to destroy not the shadow..."

Ymir looked up at the sky.

"You need to destroy the sun."

Kariel, who had been lost in thought, opened his cold eyes and approached Smile.

"Perhaps it's for the best."

In any case, the element of breaking the System had disappeared on its own, and killing Smile would eliminate any further risk.

"I will kill this woman."

There was no need to use angelic law; he could simply cut off her head.

"Wait."

Ymir stopped Kariel.

"You can't kill her."

The reason he promised Ogent was to one day return and settle their score.

"Don't be arrogant, Ymir."

Kariel's eyes hardened.

"You've already broken the System. Even if it's Anke Ra's order, if you stop me, you'll face severe punishment."

"I don't care."

The spot where Ogent had struck still throbbed.

"Everything will feel meaningless for a while."

Seeing Ymir casually handle the archangel's killing intent, Kariel's face twisted in frustration.

"Tch!"

But in the end, he had no choice but to turn away.

Unlike Ogent, a giant who reached breaking the System through pure brute force was unbeatable.

'Only Ra can stop Ymir now.'

As Kariel watched him leave, Smile let out a small groan and opened her eyes.

"Ugh..."

After tossing and turning for a moment, she suddenly opened her eyes and saw Ymir's face three meters above her, causing her to jump up.

"Brother!"

Ogent was nowhere to be seen.

Realizing she was naked, she stepped back and glared at Ymir.

"Who are you?"

"The king of giants."

Thinking she couldn't explain the current situation, Smile finally asked.

"Where is my brother?"

"You've already died once."

Ymir recounted Ogent's actions while Smile was trapped.

"...That's how you were able to survive. Cherish the life he passed on to you."

"My brother..."

Smile touched her belly.

In a way, this was also a form of the Elixir of Life, but she didn't feel scared or repulsed.

'My brother is inside me.'

Together forever.

"What will you do now? If you want to go to Heaven, follow me. I'll protect you until then."

Smile shook her head.

"No. I'll stay here."

"Stay?"

From the sky, it seemed like humanity existed, but it was a completely different society from Heaven.

"Are you serious? You might never be able to return. You're abandoning your family and hometown."

"No. A new life is beginning."

Smile looked beyond the forest.

"My name is Smile."

After a moment of thought, she smiled and looked back at Ymir.

"Ogent Smile."

Seeing the sincerity in her eyes, Ymir nodded and turned away.

"We will meet again someday."

A bat followed Ymir into the forest, chasing him from high in the sky.

'What a strange dream.'

Waking from the dream, Rian felt his body as heavy as a thousand pounds and slowly opened his eyes.

A blade was falling toward his neck.

'What!'

Instinctively raising his hand to block, the sharp sword cut into his forearm.

"Hup!"

As his muscles twisted like a vortex, they caught the blade.

He avoided being cut, but the sight that greeted Rian as he regained his senses was shocking.

The hunters were fighting numerous soldiers, and Xenia was also wielding her dark blade in battle.

"Rian!"

As Xenia shouted, the soldiers of Jaive thrust their spears at Rian.

As the seven spear tips entered Rian's body, the middle of the spear shafts swelled like balloons and split apart.

"How...?"

How could a human body block steel?

Glaring at the terrified soldiers, Rian jumped out of bed.

'Where is my sword?'

was nowhere to be seen.

"Attack! Kill him no matter what!"

At the captain's shout, the soldiers fighting the hunters all rushed at Rian.

"Hmph!"

With a snort, Rian punched, causing the soldiers' armor to cave in and break their bones.

"Ugh!"

As the soldiers scattered like an explosion in the center, Xenia approached.

"What happened? No matter how much I shouted, you wouldn't wake up! You've been asleep for three days."

"Three days?"

No matter how exhausting the battle, his mental strength wasn't weak enough to sleep for three days straight.

'Was it not a dream?'

It must have been a forced state of unconsciousness.

"Where's my sword?"

Xenia spoke with a troubled expression.

"They took it. After you fainted, the security forces moved you here. But for some reason, this morning, a large army attacked, trying to kill you."

"A large army?"

Rian's eyes narrowed as he looked at the cabin door.

He couldn't hear anything, but the killing intent piercing through the door was overwhelming.

As Rian slowly walked and opened the door, thirty of Jaive's elite guards, the 'Shinjang,' were lined up.

Beyond them, a thousand soldiers completely surrounded the cabin.

"Rian?"

Security Chief Benoff was holding Idea .

"Why are you trying to kill me?"

There was only one reason.

He was too strong.

"Rian, we acknowledge your achievement in defeating the vampires, but too much damage was caused in the process."

It was just an excuse.

To King Meyer, the vampires were a threat to the nation's foundation, and if they could be eliminated, he would have turned a blind eye to some casualties.

But the scene Rian created while raiding the vampires' hideout was beyond human capability. The reason it took two days was that there was a suggestion to bring Rian into their country.

Benoff was one of those who supported it, but in the end, Meyer decided, "Kill him while we can."

Rian, who fought in a semi-trance, wasn't unaware of their intentions.

"Return my sword."

As Rian took a step forward, twenty of the Shinjang twisted their waists in unison and gripped their sword handles.

'Strong.'

They were on a different level from the Shinjang he faced in the Colosseum, representing the kingdom's elite guards.

"You're going to kill me?"

The aura from the best swordsmen was suffocating, but Rian walked calmly.

'Collective killing intent, piercing the sun?'

The eyebrows of the First Guard Captain, Salle, twitched.

"Is that Jaive's decision? Even with a war against the demons coming, you're going to kill me?"

"It's because of the war."

Salle spoke.

"The Gustav Empire has absorbed the demons. The future will be a war drenched in blood. The balance of the world is breaking, and the existing order is collapsing. The great powers will fall, and perhaps... a small kingdom might rise."

Jaive was a great power, and Rian, who they couldn't have, would be a threat to their country in any form.

"Even if the world perishes, you'll protect Jaive?"

"That's the king. And we are soldiers who follow the king's orders."

As the killing intent, called the sun, thickened, the hunters in the back hesitated and stepped back.

'It feels like my skin is burning.'

Salle spoke with force in his voice.

"This is your last chance. Abandon Tormia and join Jaive. I promise unprecedented treatment that will break historical precedents."

"Of course, I am a citizen of Tormia. But I won't fight for Tormia."

"...Do you think you can get away with that?"

"Because it's the truth. I don't judge anything. My destination is pure strength. And the only one who can wield it..."

The air shook with the Law of Maha.

"...is my lord, Arian Shirone."

He was going to meet Shirone.

"Give me the sword. It doesn't matter if you're human or demon. Anyone who stands in my way will be cut down."

Realizing his sincerity, Salle extended his hand to Benoff.

"Give me the sword."

After briefly examining Idea, which he received from Benoff, he threw it to Rian.

"Captain! His Majesty..."

"Be quiet."

Salle walked towards Rian.

"We are desperate enough to attack a sleeping swordsman. The king's order is absolute. We must take your head."

It was probably an impossible task.

"So..."

"No way! Captain!"

As the soldiers shouted, Salle assumed a drawing stance, revealing his perfect killing intent.

'End it with me.'

As Jaive's strongest drawing technique unfolded, a vertical flash flew towards Rian.

And another light, splitting that flash vertically, was born into the world.

"Daijaaaaang!"

With Rian's step, the ground split with a thud, and Salle's head fell cleanly.

"Hoo."

Rian's eyes, having created another death, merely sparkled calmly.

'No hesitation. I take responsibility for all deaths.'

"You bastard!"

As the Shinjang soldiers rushed forward, the vice-captain raised his hand to stop them.

"Stop! It's over!"

His eyes were bloodshot, but he couldn't betray Salle's will.

"We're leaving. You have half a day. If you're still in Jaive by then..."

The vice-captain gritted his teeth and turned away.

"I will kill you, even if I have to sell my soul."

As the Shinjang retrieved Salle's body and left, a thousand soldiers withdrew.

"Rian, are you okay?"

Rian, smiling, patted Xenia's shoulder and entered the cabin.

'I'm coming, Shirone.'
