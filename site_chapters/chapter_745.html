Chapter 745:

"Foolish beings."

Nane's eyes, looking up at the sky from the depths of the forest, were filled with rage.

"Do you truly believe there is hope in this world?"

The heart may be larger than the universe, but isn't it humans who betray even that heart as easily as flipping a hand?

Is it natural for living beings to be selfish?

"That, too, is just an illusion created by being trapped in the self."

"Nane, how about attacking the Antarctic?"

Shura spoke up.

"...Eventually, it will have to be done."

If it were a law that could be removed immediately, the psychic realm wouldn't be shrinking so rapidly.

The terrifying obsession with life that living beings have.

'Veron, Shirone, Miro...'

With a karma score exceeding 99%, the truth left in the world is being sucked in like a black hole.

But it can never be perfect, so it was essential to understand what had been overlooked about humans.

"A great chaos is coming to the world. The law will inevitably come to me."

Everyone will come to know how cruel humans can be and why this world must be closed.

"A little more!"

Even as blood vessels burst and tears of blood flowed, Miro's gaze was firmly fixed on the world's law.

The radius of the psychic realm: 4.4 kilometers.

'We need to reduce it by even 100 meters! No, even 10 meters!'

It is estimated that about 300 people can survive per meter of radius.

Ooooooo.

The monks of Zhongchendong supported the law with the lowest bass sound humans could produce.

Miro's eyes widened.

'No! This is the limit!'

If she pushed any further, the law of Zion would be destroyed, and all efforts so far would be in vain.

"Carry it through!"

The totems, engulfed in flames, formed 700 pillars of fire and ascended to the sky.

"It's done."

As long as Zion wasn't destroyed, the 700 pillars of fire would forever prevent the expansion of the psychic realm.

"We did it. We did it."

As the law enforcers looked up at the sky in disbelief, Miro's gaze was sharp.

"Annoying..."

The final radius of the psychic realm: 1.43 kilometers.

This was 430 meters wider than the original goal, meaning approximately 100,000 more deaths.

"Achieving this much against Nane is impressive. You should be proud."

Armin comforted her.

'Honestly, I didn't think we could achieve this.'

It wasn't just the depth of the realm that brought about this result.

It was the experience of blocking the entire heavenly army with the dimensional barrier that showed its effectiveness.

"It's okay. We haven't achieved anything yet."

Miro declared to the world.

"The fight starts now."

Project Zion.

The name of humanity's final legal line of defense against Nane's merit.

As the psychic realm shrank, the armies of various countries, following the Holy War's orders, pushed the demons back from the guideline.

"Fire! Fire!"

Sealing the altars was impossible, but at least they needed to buy time to set up barricades outside the psychic realm.

Even using the ultra-fast wall construction formula developed by the world's best architect, it would take 36 hours.

The Holy War's method to temporarily bind the demons to the passage of the other world was extremely simple.

"Don't hold back! Pour everything you've got!"

The sight of thousands of archers simultaneously firing magic bullets in a 360-degree perimeter around the psychic realm was spectacular.

All supplies were provided by the Holy War, and approximately 9 million tons of magic bullets were to be supplied to each country over 36 hours.

'What a waste of money!'

In monetary terms, it amounted to 1.3 trillion gold, and the soldiers did nothing but continuously draw their bowstrings towards the bombarded area.

The commander looked through his telescope at the altar.

'It's a strategy only possible because it's the Holy War. And it's effective.'

There would be discussions among the demons of the other world, but for now, they didn't dare come out.

The commander slowly lowered his telescope.

'But there are limits to the quantity.'

Grotesque steel frames were rising at a terrifying speed where the sun was setting.

'Everyone is fighting.'

Shirone observed everything happening on the planet from Great Star's perspective.

Great Star's expression was also solemn.

"Miro finally did it."

"Yes, it's a shame. If we had pushed a little more..."

"Zion would have been destroyed."

Shirone knew this.

Even so, the reason for the lingering regret was the insufficient number of people with the will of good compared to the population.

'I have to do it.'

Shirone turned to Great Star and said.

"I can't just watch. I'm going down to seal the altars."

"Of course. But Shirone, you should act after the barricades are completed."

He wanted to leave immediately.

"Shirone, stars should not act for the benefit of a specific country or individual. If you seal the altars of a particular country in advance, human conflicts will intensify."

Shirone clenched his mouth shut.

"Shirone, you must act outside of human life, in a transnational manner. You must not kill someone out of a desire to save someone else. Causing discord only helps Nane's purpose."

The reason his heart was breaking was that there were so many people he wanted to see.

"...Understood."

Family, friends, lovers.

Great Star looked at Shirone, who had taken on a great burden beyond personal ties, with pity.

"Take this."

As Great Star held out her hands, a pentagram pendant symbolizing the stars appeared and floated towards Shirone.

"What is this?"

"It's the key to the planet Hexa. It's only symbolic for you, but you shouldn't get lost."

Using material, he could disassemble the key to Hexa at the atomic level to obtain the blueprint.

"Thank you. I'll cherish it. Although I don't think I'll need to find Hexa right now."

Great Star understood Shirone's feelings.

No matter how much he had reached the realm of infinity, having returned to this world, he couldn't be without a heart.

No, it was because only the heart remained that he missed his loved ones even more.

"Go on down. Luber and Mini are waiting. They will show you where to live."

Shirone held the light of Yahweh in his palm and said.

"Yes. I'll see you when I leave."

Great Star does not forbid the stars from coming and going.

It was just that there was no way to meet, but Shirone could come to her anytime.

'Going down.'

As the law opened the way, Shirone's body disappeared in an instant, just as when he arrived at the Great Temple.

Great Star smiled, knowing Luber had prepared a gift for Shirone.

"For someone who sacrificed for humanity, this is the least I can do..."

It was a sad smile.

"May it be a pleasant dream..."

Below the Great Temple, at the top of the physically highest Ivory Tower, Luber and Mini were waiting.

"Did the conversation go well?"

"She was a good person."

Great Star was as warm as a mother.

"Indeed. Let me show you where you'll be staying."

Taking the elevator to the 400th floor, they saw a small village with fields, mountains, and streams outside the door.

"Stars don't necessarily live in independent spaces. They just move into available houses."

"Yes, I like it."

Civilization is originally built within nature, but the Ivory Tower contained nature within civilization.

'How big is this building?'

It was a pity that he couldn't see the Ivory Tower from the outside since he had entered through a magic circle.

Shirone's house was a two-story wooden building with a red roof, exuding a quaint atmosphere.

Mini asked.

"Will you leave as soon as the barricades are completed?"

Since only Shirone could seal the altars, she planned to support in other ways.

"Then I'll go to the southern hemisphere to investigate Veron's traces. I might find some clues."

Luber nodded.

"I'll instruct the other stars in our department to help suppress the demons."

It was a simple matter to convey through dreams.

"The residents of the other world are strong. Many people will die or suffer."

"If there's someone you want to protect, protect them."

Shirone turned his head at Mini's words.

"I know what Great Star said. That's the fate of a star. But you've already saved everyone. You have the right to save at least the ones you love."

Shirone shook his head.

"The moment I become attached, my karma will rise, and I'll lose the light of Yahweh. The balance of the law will be broken."

"You can't bear it. Even if you have the power to protect, can you stand it if your loved ones die?"

"No, I can't."

Nane had said this.

The one who suffers the most is you.

He had protected humanity through philanthropy but couldn't protect any of his loved ones, a terrible contradiction.

'I must not become human. I must only check Nane as a concept.'

That was the reason for Hexa's existence.

"Let's focus on the altars for now. I'll start with the closest one once the barricades are set up."

Tormia was far from the Ivory Tower, so it wasn't a human decision, and his heart bled.

Luber spoke.

"I agree. The only decisions possible are those to save humanity."

Mini turned her head at the unexpectedly cold words, but Luber smiled kindly.

"But... anyone can dream."

Shirone blinked at the incomprehensible words.

Kingdom of Tormia

At Amy's family home in Creas City, 200 armed soldiers were standing guard.

Amy's father, Shakora, returned from the Creas branch of the Magic Association with a cold look in his eyes.

"Honey! What happened?"

"There was significant damage in the mountainous regions. Nationwide, the death toll is around 7,000. But it seems we've managed to hold them off for now."

"What will happen to Tormia?"

In the face of the worst disaster in human history, even the wife of a top noble was just a human being.

"We've only temporarily halted the demons with a show of force. The barricades won't hold for long."

Isis, who would have been demon food if Amy hadn't been home, shivered.

"They say Shirone has entered the Ivory Tower."

Amy, who had been bowing her head in exhaustion from the battle, looked at Isis for the first time.

"It's not certain. But it seems he played a crucial role in stopping this crisis."

That was the extent of the information Shakora had received.

"They say the place where Shirone is, is safe from the demons..."

The Ivory Tower and Zion were the only places untouched by the terrible disaster.

"If only our family could go there..."

"Mom!"

Amy stood up abruptly.

"What are you saying? How can you think of saving only ourselves!"

"Amy, what are you doing to your mother?"

Shakora's nerves were frayed after seeing the horrific corpses and returning home.

"You may become a soldier, but not everyone is. It's natural for your mother to be afraid."

"But it doesn't make sense."

Isis spoke in frustration.

"Why doesn't it make sense? Even if going to the Ivory Tower is out of the question, shouldn't we at least get some word? You and Shirone aren't just acquaintances..."

Amy couldn't argue.

"That's..."

Between all of humanity stood Shirone and Nane.

It was difficult for mere thinking reeds to understand the two who had carried out their extreme opposites.

But now, with the world on the brink of destruction, they had come up with the closest answers to the truth.

"Sorry. I'm tired, so I'll go to bed early."

"Amy..."

Isis, realizing that her daughter felt the most betrayed, tried to speak, but Amy went into her room.

"I think I made a mistake."

"Leave her be. She won't be hurt by this. She'll feel better after a good night's sleep."

That night, Luber appeared in Amy's dream.
