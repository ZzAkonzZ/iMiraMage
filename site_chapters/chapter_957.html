Chapter 957: What Goffin Left Behind (4)

Signals transmitted in real-time from around the world were drawn into the command headquarters of the Holy War.

"Commander! The signals keep coming..."

"Shh."

The commander put a finger to his lips, indicating there was no need to report this matter, and crossed two fingers.

"Ah..."

When the messenger looked inside the command control room, a giant golden panel was spread out in front of Iruki.

Goldbirds continued to penetrate the panel, updating numerous records.

'Arnold Lampa.'

As expected of the world's greatest information magician, his activity radius was planetary.

'According to this information...'

Demons appearing to be of the highest legion commander level were being discovered all over the world.

In other words, the elite of the demon army was not in the central continent.

'Could they be this cautious? If they had seen through the Holy War's strategy... No, that's impossible.'

Therefore, it's not a normal judgment.

'Something is happening within the demon army. Internal strife or the emergence of a rival.'

Humans only persist stubbornly in such cases.

'Anyway, the elite of the demon army is not in the central continent. They were probably diverted by the top legion commanders and their subordinate units.'

Iruki's expression grew serious.

'Even if we detonate the elemental bomb, the elite demons won't be harmed. Instead, we'll lose Bashka, and millions of people will die.'

The commander, after checking the panel's information, asked,

"Commander, what should we do?"

"Proceed as planned."

The decision was so quick that the commander hesitated, unable to respond immediately.

"Proceed as planned?"

Iruki rested his chin on his hand.

'Diversion of the elite can't be a strategy to seize victory. Therefore, this is psychological warfare.'

Literally, a shake-up.

'In other words, Balkan, whether by choice or not, is not in a position to make decisions. That's why he passed the choice to me. He intends to create new variables to overcome his situation within the army.'

Having organized his thoughts, Iruki spoke again.

"Proceed as planned."

Balkan's strategy only served to strengthen Iruki's resolve.

'This is better. The probability of the demons changing their course voluntarily is now zero.'

It's clear.

'The remaining variable is...'

Iruki's eyes gleamed as he examined the map.

Even after accepting all of Goffin's final records, Shirone kept his eyes closed.

There was no confusion, thanks to the 11 senses, but he still wanted to savor the afterglow a bit longer.

'Goffin. Ikael. Gaia.'

He was not alone.

"So... they were connected like that." A god with a heart.

What Goffin left behind in this world as he departed the photon system...

"Messiah."

Shirone finally understood the reason for his existence.

"Do you feel a sense of mission?"

Karatorsa asked.

"No. I'm still Shirone. Nothing will change, and nothing should change."

"Then, what did you feel?"

"Love."

Shirone looked up at the ceiling.

"The movement of every atom since the beginning. The countless hearts born from that have made me who I am. I want to continue that great love."

'In the end, the choice was love.'

Karatorsa's lips curled into a slight smile.

'Rest easy, Goffin. Your child has grown up well.'

Having laid down all his burdens, Karatorsa felt his life fading and took a comfortable position.

"Go. The 12 apostles will help you from now on."

The log of the Akashic Records.

For Karatorsa, who had literally shouldered the entire universe, extinction was the ultimate rest.

This time, even Shirone paid his respects.

"Thank you for your hard work."

After Goffin reset the world, Anke Ra tried to change history by all means.

The dragons shared the core log of Karatorsa and fought the angels for eons.

'Many dragons have disappeared.'

Except for the 12 apostles, there were almost no dragons left in this world.

'No, the remaining dragons became the 12 apostles.'

What they protected was a pure world, not swayed by the will of any particular being.

"Until now, the dragons shared their thoughts through the core of the Wudeng Dragon. The dragon network. But now, with my extinction, that network will also be severed."

Karatorsa chuckled.

"They're all quirky, but with you, the Ultima, there won't be any problems. Lead them well."

"I have nothing to teach."

The 12 apostles waiting for Shirone now were the strongest dragons who had not bowed to the archangels' power.

"You don't need to teach. They will learn. As they follow in your footsteps..."

As Karatorsa slowly closed his eyes, the era of dragons flashed through his mind.

"Can we continue too?" The Wudeng Dragon's body rapidly lost its life force and vanished in a bright light.

Shirone bowed his head in mourning.

"Thank you."

The time was short.

It was not proper to feel sadness for someone who had completed the hardest life and departed.

"Whew!"

As Shirone took a deep breath, the Miracle Stream began to spread thickly around him.

The scenery of the deep mountains disintegrated into signals, and with one step, he leaped over the vast mountain range. The 12 apostles were waiting at the destination.

"Greetings, Messiah."

As Blitz knelt, the 11 apostles performed the same action.

Although the core of the Wudeng Dragon had disappeared, no one showed any sad emotions.

'The strongest dragons.'

Shirone had already felt everything about the 12 apostles through Omega with his 11 senses.

But seeing them with his five senses, especially his sight, gave him a different feeling.

"Karatorsa has entered eternal rest."

"We know." With the network's database gone, the 12 apostles had to rely on their individual judgments.

"That's why the Messiah must lead us. We will follow you."

It was a matter of faith, not information.

"Alright."

As Shirone looked at the faces of the 12 apostles, they all raised their heads with burning determination.

They all wanted to be called upon.

'Ugh...'

Shirone knew about the competitive spirit among the 12 apostles since the time of the Wudeng Dragon.

In a state of shared thoughts, proving one's value was by being chosen by the core.

'Who should I call first?'

The unnecessary worry that Karatorsa had was now Shirone's to bear.

'First, the Light Dragon Phaeton.'

A boy with a delicate appearance, but known to be the most violent among the 12 apostles.

'The Fire Dragon Infercus.'

She was also the simulation model for the graduation exam, but Shirone subtly avoided her gaze.

'Scary.'

With half of her head shaved and piercings on her lips, it was hard to approach her.

'Honestly, the apostle I'm most curious about is... the White Dragon Asraiker.'

The moment he saw her sitting demurely in a red skirt worn by Eastern priestesses.

'Huh? Huh?'

Shirone was mesmerized.

Asraiker, who wielded the information of the soul among the dragons, had an appearance that overwhelmed all forms in the world.

'They say she's the most beautiful signal in the universe.'

It's not the concept of perfection.

It's an extreme preference that transcends individuality, a charm that fulfills everyone's ideals.

Feeling Shirone's gaze, Asraiker gently lifted her head and smiled.

A feeling of goodwill.

Just her smile sent immense ripples through Shirone's heart.

'This is dangerous. Really dangerous.'

The most pleasurable signal perceivable by the eyes made his heart race and his mind dizzy.

Asraiker, noticing the Messiah's attention, sent an even softer gaze.

'Ugh!'

With a red alert in his mind, Shirone immediately activated the Ultima system.

As the extreme preference information forming Asraiker disintegrated into the smallest units, the emotional turbulence ceased.

"Whew."

Blitz's eyes gleamed.

'To suppress the White Dragon's traits so quickly. As expected of the Messiah, the strongest mind in the universe.'

The 12 apostles showed satisfied expressions.

'Hmph, using beauty as a tactic?'

Asraiker, acknowledging this, gently bowed her head and suppressed her desire to be called.

Shirone looked for another apostle.

'Ah, that man...'

The Water Dragon Kaioh.

The scene of him leading thousands of tons of water and surging from Galliante Island was vivid in his mind.

A dragon who managed the world's time by moving across three oceans over ten years.

Shirone made his decision.

"Come to think of it, we are old acquaintances."

That explanation was enough, and Kaioh sprang up from the line of apostles.

'Tch! It can't be helped.'

Kaioh, with a calm demeanor, had blue hair that reached his ankles and moved with his eyes closed.

'Closed eyes and open eyes.'

In Ultima's history, Kaioh was a dragon with two personalities.

When his eyes were closed, he had a calm personality like a windless zone, but once he opened his eyes...

Kaioh bowed his head.

"Yes. I was very rude back then. Karatorsa had issued a no-approach order."

It was to protect time.

"No. Thanks to you, I could make wonderful memories. It was truly a beautiful sight."

He missed his friends, Gis, Canis, and Arin.

"Ahem."

As the Poison Dragon Poiné cleared her throat and stood up, the other apostles narrowed their eyes.

'The Messiah hasn't even given an order yet. How arrogant...'

Regardless, she walked forward, leaning on her staff, and greeted him politely.

"Greetings, Messiah. I am Poiné, the Poison Dragon."

"Yes, nice to meet you."

As Shirone bowed in return, Poiné's eyes sparkled with interest.

'Oh?'

Someone who has lived through all of Omega's history has no reason to elevate anyone.

Indeed, Shirone did not feel the awe that ordinary humans might towards Poiné.

'But she's still an elder.'

However much he had realized, he reminded himself not to forget what was important to humans.

Perhaps that is what it means to be a god with a heart.

"Hoho, as expected, my predictions were correct. You have many tasks ahead. Command us as your limbs, and if you need anything, just instruct me."

The eyes of the 11 apostles lit up.

'Is she senile?'

Since Karatorsa's disappearance, no representative had been decided.

'Trying to take advantage!'

She played the image of an old woman, but Shirone was not so easily swayed.

'You really can't let your guard down.'

Karatorsa had utilized the competitive structure of the 12 apostles, and now it was Shirone's turn.

"Yes, I will gradually assign tasks. We also need to consider the representative of the 12 apostles."

Poiné seemed rather pleased.

"Of course, we will follow the Messiah's will. That is our reason for existence."

"Speaking of which."

Shirone looked around at everyone.

"We don't have much time, so I'd like to hear the situation first. I haven't obtained the current log. Can you tell me what's happening on the battlefield?"

The apostles who had not yet been called all raised their hands and shouted simultaneously.

"I will explain!"

"...Haha."

Meeting their passionate gazes, Shirone let out an awkward laugh.
