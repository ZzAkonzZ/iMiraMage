Chapter 1173:

Albino stroked his beard.

“Hm. Kuan is a difficult sword to wield. In that sense, Siena Oliver’s presence is significant. For the sake of maintaining his combat capabilities, it would be best to protect her… but…”

Lupist spoke up.

“The other nations are reading our strategy too.”

“Exactly. Whoever captures Siena Oliver will effectively neutralize Kuan’s blade. Especially Uorin—her neck is on the line, so she’ll be desperate to come for her. If we get caught in the middle, we’ll be completely screwed.”

It was a matter of offense-defense deployment.

“The easiest move would be to abandon her. But it’s a risky gamble. At this point, let’s hear Yahweh’s opinion.”

Shirone spoke firmly.

“I will protect my teacher.”

A logical response, but Lupist tilted his head slightly.

“Is that even possible? If you protect Siena Oliver, someone else will die instead. Trying to save everyone will inevitably lead to casualties—it’s a law of balance. But if you play favorites, you stop being Yahweh.”

Yahweh could not favor one life over another.

“Don’t misunderstand me. When I say I’ll protect my teacher, I mean I’ll protect everyone. I refuse to play into Havitz’s game. I’ll break the board instead.”

“You mean attacking the system. That would prevent a contradiction in your ideals, but…”

It was an incredibly difficult feat.

“Do you have a plan? We’ve had this debate before, but an impossible ideal doesn’t help anyone.”

Goffin words.

He had once pleaded—if Shirone had grown strong enough to protect the whole world, then he should listen to everyone’s cries.

‘But I couldn’t do it.’

There had been a time when he had cried in frustration over his own limitations.

Of course, Ultima was still a distant goal, but now…

Now, he had a card in his hand.

“I can do it.”

At the very least—

Seven million people would stand behind him.

“We’ll take a short recess.

Enjoy your lunch, and we’ll reconvene in the afternoon.”

International Tribunal Chief Shirano stood up, her demeanor ice-cold.

‘They had the nerve to reject my resignation without permission?

What the hell do they think I am?’

“Shirano.”

Socrates grinned slyly.

“How about it? Feel like having tea with me?”

“Screw off, pig.

Do you even realize whose fault this whole mess is?

You were the one who rejected my resignation, weren’t you?”

“Technically, it’s procedurally sound.

I simply submitted mine while rejecting yours.”

“One day, I’m going to kill you.”

“Hohoho! Hearing that reminds me of our younger days.

How about it? Shall we reignite that fiery passion from back then—”

Socrates instinctively squeezed his eyes shut, expecting a punch.

But when no impact came, he cautiously peeked through one eye.

Shirano was smirking.

“You idiot.

You still remember something from twenty years ago?

That was pity.

And most importantly—

Back then, I wasn’t even interested in men.”

“…Wait, what?”

Socrates blinked.

“But… but it was good, wasn’t it?”

“Hohoho!”

Covering her mouth, Shirano chuckled gracefully, then pulled a pair of tweezers from her cosmetic pouch and tossed them at him.

“What’s this?”

“A gift.”

“For what?”

“You seem lonely these days.

Might as well enjoy yourself with that.”

Even after Shirano walked away, Socrates kept staring at the tweezers.

His hand trembled slightly.

‘Damn it… She’s so petty it’s annoying…’

With a deep sigh, Shirano exited the conference hall—

And immediately spotted a familiar face.

‘Armin of the Radiant Eyes.’

A resident of the Ivory Tower, currently serving as the brain of Corona Kingdom.

‘Why is he here?

There aren’t even any VIPs in this area.’

Most of the elites were either guarding their kings or targeting other kings in this assassination war.

“Siena.”

Armin approached Siena Oliver, who was leaving with a group of Toromia Magic School instructors.

“…Brother?”

Siena hesitated.

Clearly flustered.

Shirano’s eyes sparkled with amusement.

‘Oh my, my. Is this what I think it is?’

Armin spoke.

“Can we talk?”

“I… um…”

The other teachers stepped away, giving them space.

Siena glanced over Armin’s shoulder, scanning the hallway.

Armin’s wife, Keira—real or fake—was leaning against the wall, arms crossed.

‘What the hell is this? This is way too awkward.’

Armin was still the same caring older brother, but a situation like this, where a third party was involved, was incredibly uncomfortable.

“What’s going on?”

“Seek asylum in Corona. Immediately.”

“…What?”

Siena’s eyes widened in disbelief.

“Why? What happened?”

‘A card game…’

He had just returned from a strategy briefing with Corona’s think tank, who had simulated this assassination war.

“You already know. The killing has begun. Thormia isn’t going to prioritize you.”

Since Havitz was leading the game, predicting exact moves was difficult.

But one thing was clear—

“Your life is in danger.”

Siena clenched her fists.

“And your solution is to run away and abandon my country?”

“All available forces are being dedicated to VIP protection. Right now, you’re just a regular teacher. Come to Corona, at least for now.”

Armin reached for her wrist, but Siena jerked away.

“Don’t treat me like a child. I can protect myself. And you were the one who told me to become a teacher in the first place.”

“How exactly are you going to protect yourself? Do you even realize who’s after you? Without Stop Magic, you—”

“Enough.”

She could barely face Keira.

“Why are you doing this? You have someone precious right there. I also have someone I love now.”

‘That guy is the problem, idiot. He’s too strong.’

“Kuan won’t come. The royal decree has already been issued. He won’t break national law just to protect—”

“What’s going on?”

Shirano, who had been eavesdropping with great interest, suddenly turned her head in surprise.

‘Oh my…?’

A one-armed swordsman approached, limping slightly.

Parca Kuan.

“…Kuan?”

Even Armin hadn’t expected this, but the one most shocked was Siena herself.

“How are you here?”

Noticing the stares, Kuan casually looked around and spoke.

“I figured the meeting would be over by now. I came to have lunch together… Or… were you busy?”

Kuan was a blade difficult to wield.

“Ah… no. I was just… surprised. I thought you’d be too busy.”

“There are some orders I have to follow, but nothing urgent. And… I made a promise.”

A promise to stay by her side.

Siena, momentarily stunned, soon smiled and looped her arm around Kuan’s.

“Let’s go. Let’s have lunch.”

She turned to Armin.

"You should go back too. Don’t worry about things here. The Holy War is already in bad shape, so take care of yourself. You’ve lost weight.”

Seeing Siena Oliver finally relax, Armin couldn't push further.

“Alright, I got it.”

His bandaged eye briefly shifted toward Kuan, but he didn’t say “Take care of her”—

That would be presumptuous.

As Armin turned to leave, Keira pushed off the wall and flicked her hair back.

“So? You feeling better now? Then…”

At that moment—

A sudden gust of wind swept through the hall.

Everyone instinctively turned toward the wind’s direction—

But there was nothing there.

‘The other side.’

Twenty figures, clad in black cloaks and owl masks, had suddenly occupied the corridor.

“…Wind Guards.”

It was expected, yet…

It also felt too soon.

The leader of the formation spoke.

"Siena Oliver. As of this moment, we are taking you into our custody. Come quietly."

The moment Kuan reached for his sword, the leader raised a hand.

"Don’t misunderstand. We are not here to fight. We are here to protect her."

"Protect? From who? Siena Oliver belongs to Thormia."

"For example…"

The leader extended his hand, pointing forward.

"From something like that?"

The moment everyone turned their heads—

Ten bombs flew toward them.

"Yahoo!"

A gang of chicken-headed maniacs cackled wildly, wagging their tongues as they hurled explosives.

From the entrance of the Small Conference Hall, Shirano shouted—

"Take cover!"

A blast shook the hall.

‘Tch! Those bastards…’

Most of the combat-capable people could handle themselves—

But there were also non-combatant staff of the Holy War inside.

‘Are they seriously committing an open terrorist attack?’

As the smoke cleared, a massive silhouette stood firm, shielding the hall.

Socrates' enormous back.

His quivering fat settled into stillness—

And as he turned around, he smiled.

"You good?"

"Move! You're blocking the view!"

Shirano shoved his bulk aside and turned to the collapsed wall where the key figures had escaped.

"…Unbelievable."

A chaotic battlefield had erupted.

"They’re not trained soldiers. They hired mercenaries to conceal their national origin. Now, which country would that be…?"

Even though their styles and skill levels varied, she could tell at a glance—

‘They’re all targeting her.’

With an arm wrapped around Siena Oliver’s waist, Kuan used Outer Gravity to weave through countless swords.

‘He won’t last long.’

Even if Chicken Head wasn’t much of a threat—

The simultaneous assault from the Wind Guards was overwhelming even for Kuan.

The biggest problem was…

‘Siena.’

A one-armed swordsman couldn't fight while protecting someone.

“Kuan.”

As he navigated through the encirclement with an unnatural trajectory, Armin stepped in.

“Leave Siena to me.”

“This isn’t the time for stubbornness. In that condition, you can’t even draw your sword. I swear—I will protect her.”

Armin of Stop Magic.

“…I’m counting on you.”

Knowing the risk Kuan carried, even Siena couldn’t refuse.

“Be careful.”

As soon as Siena was taken away, Kuan’s grip tightened around his sword.

"These bastards…"

The Wind Guards and Chicken Head—

Acting as if they had already secured Siena.

"Do you think I’m a joke?"

Initial Step.

The world spun wildly left and right.

Everyone entangled with Kuan felt their vision whirl uncontrollably.

A Chicken Head mercenary screamed.

“W-What the hell…?! Ugh!”

Before they could even process what was happening—

Dozens of heads rolled.

‘Flies aren’t worth my time.’

Like a storm, Kuan surged forward—

His eyes locked onto the Wind Guards, floating in midair.

“…How terrifying.”

Even as the world trembled, the leader of the Wind Guards didn’t flinch.

‘Hmph. Let’s see how long that arrogance lasts.’

Just as Kuan leapt forward—

A chilling wave of killing intent crashed in from both sides.

‘What?!’

The Imperial Guard of Kashan—Inner, Mid, and Outter—

Were converging at different distances from him.

“Kh…!”

Activating Outer Gravity, Kuan vanished into their blind spot—

But at that moment—

“…Earthbound Spirit.”

A crushing force clamped down on his ankles.

“Damn it—!”

As he slammed into the ground, a silver spear shot toward him, sharp as a hawk’s beak.

“Hhhhhaaaah!”

With Vibration Release, Kuan shattered the gravity spell—

And spun his body to swing his sword.

The battle halted—

All eyes fixed on the clash between Kuan and Kido.

Shirano’s eyes widened.

“Kashan’s Captain of the Imperial Guard?!”

The Wind Guards, the Imperial Guard, and even Kido himself—

“…They sent every available force?

Just to capture one woman?”

“She’s not just a woman.”

Socrates stepped up beside her.

“…They’re here to stop that swordsman.”

Kuan spoke, voice unwavering.

"Leave. Your mission has failed. You will never take Siena."

“…Is that so?"

Kido smirked—

And gestured toward the sky.

Beyond the clouds—

Eighty more Wind Guards descended.

Merging seamlessly with the existing squad.

Shock rippled through the crowd.

“A hundred…?”

The complete formation of the Wind Guards.

The leader of the squad stepped forward—

And removed her owl mask.

Yula.

She locked eyes with Kuan—

Then pointed her sword toward the ground.

“…Kuan.”

And declared—

"This time, too, you will lose."
