Chapter 619:

"How annoying."

While everyone was captivated by Argannes, only one woman voiced her displeasure. It was Nade's mother, Teria.

"What's she smiling about, living in luxury thanks to her husband?"

Her husband, Bolum, tried to calm her down.

"Stop it. The other nobles will hear."

"Why? Are you embarrassed? What did I do in my past life to deserve this? That woman is living in luxury."

Bolum's face turned red.

"Please, not today. It's Nade's exam day."

"If our son was any good, we wouldn't be living like this. The school fees are so expensive. If he doesn't pass today, we'll have to get him a job in a factory…"

"Enough!"

Bolum's patience snapped, and he shouted, drawing the attention of nearby nobles. But Teria, filled with bitterness, didn't flinch.

"What have you done to shout like that? Do you think there's a man here who earns less than you? If you want to shout, earn some money first!"

Bolum fell silent. No matter what he said, Teria would not respond kindly.

"Oh my, it looks like a couple is fighting over there."

Amy's mother, Isis, said, linking arms with her husband, Shakora.

"They might be tense. It's their child's exam day. Let's just ignore it."

Isis sensed the tension in her husband's voice.

"Are you okay? You look tense. Are you worried Amy might fail?"

"If she fails, she fails. It just means she's not good enough. I'm just worried because this is hardest on Amy."

The special talent of Carmis's red eyes was something others envied, but in this situation, it felt like an unnecessary burden on their daughter.

"It's been a while, Shakora."

Iruki's father, Albino, approached with Argannes.

"Yes, how have you been?"

Shakora bowed politely, and Albino introduced Argannes.

"Let me introduce you. This is Shakora of the Carmis family, and his wife, Isis."

Argannes smiled beautifully and curtsied.

"I've heard of your reputation. I'm Argannes."

"Of course, I know you. You're the most famous actress in the world. Please give me your autograph later!"

Shakora cleared his throat, embarrassed.

"Sorry, my wife is very cheerful."

"Oh, it's fine. I'm a fan of Isis too. I was so sad when you retired early."

Albino changed the subject.

"How is Amy? Is she well prepared?"

"From what I saw during the break, she seems to have reached her goal. But we can't be sure of the results."

"Haha, you're too modest. After all, she's a Carmis."

"And how is Iruki? I hear he has great talent."

"He's hopeless. He'll probably do something stupid and fail. I came to the capital just to tease him if he fails."

Argannes puffed her cheeks.

"What's wrong with our son? If he looked like me, he'd be the best man in the world. The Mercodine genes are just too strong."

No one would associate Iruki with Argannes.

"By the way…"

Albino looked around.

"Have Shirone's parents arrived?"

"Oh, you know Shirone."

"Of course. Iruki only talks to me about Shirone. It made me reconsider my son's preferences."

"Oh, I don't think that's it. To be honest, our daughter seems to have taken an interest in Shirone."

"Honey, don't say things you're not sure about…"

"Oh, that makes me even more curious. Who is the boy who captured the heart of the Carmis family's youngest daughter?"

Shakora looked for the Ogent family.

"I heard Shirone is a commoner supported by a patron. That woman over there is Reina, the third daughter of the Ogent family."

The four of them spotted Reina but couldn't find anyone who looked like Shirone's parents.

"Ugh! Why are they so late?"

Reina checked the time and tapped her foot impatiently. Just then, the Ogent family's carriage arrived in the distance.

The assistant butler, Temuran, opened the door, and Vincent and Olina appeared.

Seeing the crowd of nobles, their faces turned pale, but Reina approached them.

"Welcome, Father. I'm glad you're not late."

"Thank you for inviting us. But are you sure we should be here…"

"What are you talking about? Shirone is taking his graduation exam. Of course, you should be here. The participants will enter soon."

A sleepy voice came from inside the carriage.

"Oh? We're already here."

Reina glared and shouted.

"Hey! When did you go to get our parents? We almost missed Shirone!"

"Well, we made it on time. Stop nagging…"

Rian, with a greatsword on his back, got out.

With blue hair, sharp eyes like blades, and a perfectly trained body, he exuded an overwhelming presence.

'Shirone…'

Rian looked up at the top of the colosseum where Shirone's future would be decided.

'You really did it.'

The reality of the situation, unimaginable when he took his knight's oath, was thrilling.

"Oh? Isn't that greatsword from the Ogent family? It looks like the youngest son, Rian, is here."

"They call him the Knight of Mach. He's quite noted among swordsmen."

As the nobles recognized Rian, Reina slowly lowered her pointing finger.

"Looks like you've made a name for yourself. People recognize you."

When he first left for knight training, it felt like they were just sending off a younger brother.

"I don't care about my reputation. If it weren't for Shirone's graduation exam, I wouldn't have come home."

"You brat! We spoiled you, and now you look down on the family!"

Reina kicked Rian's thigh, but it felt like hitting a rock.

"Ouch! Are you wearing armor under your pants?"

Rian looked at her disdainfully.

"When will you grow up? You need to control your temper. Who would want to marry you?"

Tears welled up in Reina's eyes as she rubbed her toes.

'Now he's trying to beat me with strength. It's so sad.'

The days of pulling her little brother's ear and bossing him around were over.

"So, will you leave again after the graduation exam?"

"I have to. My life is just beginning."

"You're so busy. Where are you going this time?"

"Of course…"

Rian looked at the school and smiled.

"Wherever Shirone goes."

A loud bell rang in the dormitory hallway.

"The graduation exam is about to begin. Participants, please proceed to the colosseum."

"Whoo!"

Shirone, who had been sitting cross-legged, opened his eyes.

He had stayed up all night, but his mind was the clearest it had been all year.

Consciously and unconsciously, his mental preparation for today had reached its peak.

'It's starting!'

Shirone jumped up and headed to the hallway, as if he would never return.

His determination was absolute.

At the same time, students poured out from all directions.

Iruki and Nade walked down the hallway and nodded at Shirone.

They were friends who meant more than anything, but for now, there was no need for words.

"Look, the graduating seniors are coming!"

As they arrived at the colosseum, parents eager to see their proud sons and daughters gathered around.

"Mother, there's Shirone."

"Sniff!"

As soon as Olina saw her son, she burst into tears, leaving Reina flustered.

"Mother, why are you crying? The exam hasn't even started yet."

"I don't know… The tears just…"

Her emotions were too complex to explain.

"Try to hold back a little. Shirone needs to stay strong."

Olina quickly wiped her tears and pressed her lips together.

'Mother. Father.'

Shirone looked at his family waiting by the south gate.

'Reina. And…'

Rian stood protectively beside them.

'You've come back.'

In a perfect mental state, free of distractions, Shirone's mind focused solely on the facts.

With a cold expression, he turned and entered the colosseum. Rian smiled.

"He's well-prepared."

Reina thought the same.

When they were humiliated in the Kazra Kingdom, he still seemed like a child, but his gaze just now was no different from that of the official mages she encountered in society.

"Parents, please enter through the east gate! Advanced class students, use the west gate!"

Following the guide's instructions, the crowd in front of the colosseum quickly flowed into the building.

In the waiting room, the participants used their own methods to relax, and no one spoke.

"Whoo."

Shirone also put on the 2000 bracelet given by the staff and took deep breaths, waiting for the time.

'It's been a while, 2000.'

The large colosseum, built to Red Line standards, was equipped with the latest 2000 system, with a 100% synchronization rate.

The magic's power coefficient accurately impacted the mind, making it no different from real combat.

The reason for using the 2000 system was to quantify the participants' states, minimize physical damage during deadly magic exchanges, and ensure the safety of the spectators.

"In 10 minutes, you will enter the colosseum."

Hearing the guide's words, an unprecedented excitement churned in Shirone's stomach.

The fastest 10 minutes of his life passed, and the door to the colosseum opened.

'Let's go.'

As participant number 27, Shirone was at the end of the line, but the cheers for the leaders made it clear they were in a different world.

"Please give a round of applause to support the proud students of Alpheas Magic School!"

"Wow!"

The applause was like a downpour of sound.

Under the golden sunlight, the audience blurred into a single mass.

"Everyone, stand!"

As the students lined up, Principal Alpheas took the stage.

"I commend all of you who have reached this point."

While the opening speech continued, Shirone scanned the audience.

Advanced class students and parents were seated, with teachers on the opposite side, and scouts from around the world in the VIP section.

'So this is the graduation exam.'

The six evaluation categories were: Combat, Special Skills, Hill Occupation, Magic Utilization, Tactical Strategy, and Survival (Enemy Annihilation). Special Skills and Magic Utilization were non-combat, while the other four were combat-related.

The 4:2 ratio showed that the Red Line valued combat ability slightly more in their evaluations.

After Olivia's explanation, the final event, the oath, began.

"One! We are children of intellect! One! We never give up under any circumstances!"

In the height of exhilaration, Shirone couldn't even hear his own voice.

'Finally! Finally!'

His journey from climbing over the Alpheas Magic School wall at twelve to today flashed before his eyes.

"We will now begin the graduation exam!"

The 30 students took positions 15 meters apart along the colosseum's inner wall.

A massive hologram covered the sky, displaying the six categories on a roulette wheel. The central pointer spun rapidly.

As the category was decided, the 2000 system activated, and Shirone prepared to enter the Spirit Zone at unprecedented speed.

'Combat. Special Skills. Hill Occupation…'

Tracking the categories with his eyes, the pointer finally stopped.

"Wow!"

The cheers shook the colosseum, and like a switch had been flipped, Shirone and everyone else had eyes like predators.

'It's starting!'

Alpheas Magic School Graduation Exam, First Evaluation Category:

Survival (Enemy Annihilation).
