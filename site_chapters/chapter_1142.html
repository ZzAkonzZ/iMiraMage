Chapter 1142: Law War (1)

Sanctuary of the Murderer, Melkidu.

Giyorgi stood where the Catacomb leaders and followers of the Demon Sect had gathered.

"Is this everyone?"

Elder Euphrapus spoke. "Elka and Amidal are dead. Now, only four members of Catacomb remain."

Deacon Marit said, "But we confirmed the power of the Emblem. If we hold out a bit longer, we can kill Yahweh."

Giyorgi lowered the Demon Bible and said, "No, it ends here."

Gultan asked, "What do you mean? We should be running away. We need to expand the Satanic Church."

"Now that Yahweh has figured it out, it won't be as easy as before. And more importantly… we can't escape."

That was true.

"He caught up with my quantum movements. Yahweh has detection abilities that transcend time and space."

Giyorgi's words made sense, but Gultan still had a fundamental question.

"Is that really the case?"

"What do you mean?"

"Giyorgi, you were born from Yahweh's demon. But perhaps because of that, I don't feel much hostility towards Yahweh from you. Could it be…"

The black aura emanating from Giyorgi's body made everyone, including Gultan, tremble.

"Gultan, watch your words."

"Yes? Yes."

Although all Catacomb leaders were division-level, at that moment, it felt like the hierarchy was established.

"Your thoughts are unnecessary. Among the demons, only I understand Yahweh."

Marit took a humble stance. "I trust you, Giyorgi. Everything is going as you predicted."

"…Bring Seina."

The Catacomb leaders watched as Giyorgi entered the forest.

'What a strange demon.'

The higher a human's level, the more insignificant the demons of the under world become.

'But Giyorgi is a special case.'

To reach Yahweh's level, Shirone had compressed his murky emotions to the extreme.

'The more you suppress demons, the bigger they grow.'

That massive demon was expelled from its master and fell into hell.

'That is the leader of the Satanic Church, Giyorgi.'

A demon created by Yahweh.

"Whew."

Confirming that Gultan had escaped, Shirone knelt with a pained expression.

Mika spoke.

-Let's go back. The impact from the Emblem's spear was greater than expected. You need to rest.

The wound from the spear had healed, but the demonic energy circulated through his body like poison.

'That was really dangerous.'

The reason the Hand of God was pierced was that it wasn't a photon-based ability.

'The technique of the mind.'

An attack that comes regardless of physics.

If the size of the demonic energy had reached around 100 million, his life would have been in danger.

'A spear that can kill Yahweh.'

Giyorgi's decision to establish the Satanic Church to assassinate Shirone was accurate.

'We must stop it before it grows bigger.'

With a strained expression, Shirone teleported back to the original location.

As the flash arrived, Eden shouted, "Shirone!"

"Yes?…"

Looking around, all the followers of the Demon Sect had disappeared.

Neid and Iruki approached.

"What happened? Where's Seina?"

"We lost her. It seems she even disappeared from the phase space. It must be Giyorgi's hidden code."

Iruki nodded. "Same here. They disappeared in a red light. But if they're not in the phase space…"

"Cough! Cough!"

Maximus coughed up blood.

Eden provided emergency treatment with divine power, but the penetrating wound in his heart was beyond help.

Shirone approached.

"Maximus."

"They went to Melkidu. The sanctuary of murderers. It's Catacomb's hideout. I've heard it only appears in reality during deep nights filled with negative energy."

"Save your strength. I'll heal you now."

Maximus shook his head.

"I know my condition. I also know Seina's skills. And… I'm fine."

Maximus seemed at peace.

"I believed I could turn back time. If I had done this, if I had made a different choice… Wouldn't I have been happier? In the end, I succumbed to the devil's temptation."

Shirone listened with a sad expression.

"What I realized too late is that it would have been the same no matter what decision I made. The way to avoid regret is not to make the right decision but to take responsibility for the choices you make. I was foolish."

Maximus looked at Shirone.

"When everyone can take responsibility for their causes, humanity will be unified. But that's either impossible or a very distant future. If you want to save humanity, you must dig into their causes to the end."

The original cause.

"Maybe even the sin of being born. It will be an incredibly difficult task, but…"

Maximus grabbed Shirone's hand.

"If you can't even dream, amazing things will never happen. But if even one person dreams, it's not the end. So please, until the end…"

An impossible dream.

With a slightly surprised expression, Maximus stared into the void and passed away.

"Rest in peace."

Shirone stood up and looked at the people.

The priests who had escaped the demonic energy bowed their heads, unable to even dress.

"Return to the Vatican."

There was no response.

"Go back and announce all these facts. If we don't stop the Satanic Church, a great disaster will come."

"But!"

One of the priests spoke in a dying voice.

"What right do we have…"

Shirone's eyes hardened.

'That can't be the reason.'

Because they indulged in beastly pleasures that no human should.

Even if they repented, excommunication was inevitable, and they would face the condemnation of believers worldwide.

The priests were miserable.

'I can't do it. How can I admit to such acts with my own mouth?'

Eden approached Shirone.

"They can't help it. To them, the title of priest is more important than faith. If it were the other way around, they wouldn't have fallen into the Satanic Church in the first place."

Iruki said, "Let's focus on rescuing Seina first. Maximus mentioned Melkidu, right? We need to go there."

Neid asked, "It only appears in reality during deep nights filled with negative energy. Where in the world is such a place?"

"It exists," Shirone said.

"In remote areas untouched by humans, the laws of space can develop independently. The civilizations of tribes hidden in jungles are vastly different from ours, right? It's because the observer's perspective is different. When such tendencies persist for a long time, you get places like the Bustark Triangle or the Sky Mountain in the Mino Mountains."

Iruki nodded. "You mean places where ships suddenly disappear or stones float due to strong magnetic fields. But it would take a long time for such a system to settle."

"Yes. In the case of the Bustark Triangle, there was a gap in observation for over 10,000 years. The strong currents kept ships circling the triangle. Such relative isolation created gaps in the laws. Of course, with the advancement of navigation technology, these gaps are closing. Other regions are also disappearing one by one due to the expansion of civilization."

Shirone raised his index finger. "But there are still places untouched by civilization. I saw it in Andre's Labyrinth. Cities like Codename, where every citizen is ranked, or Whiteblack, where you must choose between black and white."

"Aha."

He had heard about it before.

"Melkidu must be a place with its own laws. Maximus called it the sanctuary of murderers…"

Eden frowned. "A place observed through the eyes of murderers."

"…I suppose so."

The thought alone was horrifying.

Delta Headquarters

The national intelligence office in the heart of the Tormia sector was sweltering.

"Whew."

Though strict access control was partly to blame, Dante's heat also contributed.

"Ah, seriously!"

Unable to bear it, Dante slammed the table, causing three employees to look at him.

"Chief, please calm down."

"…Sorry."

He quickly regained his composure and apologized, but his overheated brain showed no signs of cooling down.

With a cigarette in his mouth, Dante closely examined a chart depicting the relationships of the 12 nations involved in the Holy War.

'The whereabouts of Rm, Garto, and Temika.'

In the complex network, Dante calculated all possible scenarios.

'The problem is that things keep going off track.'

Even at this moment, officials from the 12 nations were likely meeting in secret.

Tormia was no exception.

But the intelligence results differed significantly from Dante's predictions.

'It's because of those pyramids.'

The immediate change was the decline of angels, but that alone completely altered each nation's strategy.

Dante was the best in the world at information processing, but changing systems was challenging.

'Changing systems means the meaning of the information changes. An apple becomes a knife. This isn't an information war.'

It's a law war.

'Therefore, we need experts who can extract the meaning contained in the information, experts in the law.'

That's why nations were flocking to the Mun Kingdom.

'In the end, it's about alliances. But we need experts on our side. Letting just anyone in would compromise security.'

'Tormia has talent, but I can't trust them. Once information leaks, it's over.'

Seeing Plu approach Gis was proof that no one could be trusted.

Dante stubbed out his cigarette and put on his coat, heading for the door.

"I'm going out for some fresh air."

"Chief…"

The employees, looking sad, watched the closed door.

"We're hot too."

Stepping out of Delta Headquarters, Dante lit a cigarette, enjoying the cooling breeze.

"Ha, so annoying."

As he watched the guards patrol, he heard a voice from beyond the wall.

"Let me in!"

"Huh?"

The voice was familiar to Dante.

"The laws are changing! If we don't warn humanity, a disaster will come soon!"

A guard shouted, "You need a state-issued pass! Do you know how many capable people are here besides you?"

"I am Liria of the Akeanis Shrine. I worked with Miro in Zion!"

"Miro or not, if you keep this up, we'll follow international law…"

"Liria?"

Hearing the voice from behind, the guard quickly stepped aside.

"Sorry, this woman was causing a commotion."

If someone came from headquarters, there was no need to check their credentials.

"Oh? Dante!"

As Liria raised her hand to greet him, the guards blocked her.

"I said no!"

"Dante! Tell them! Something big is happening in the world!"

"Ah…"

Dante walked over with a dazed expression.

"Soon, a time wave will hit. If we don't suppress the power of the pyramids… Eek!"

Before she could finish, Dante hugged her.

"Um… Dante?"

As Liria awkwardly rolled her eyes, Dante sighed in relief.

"Whew." He was saved.
