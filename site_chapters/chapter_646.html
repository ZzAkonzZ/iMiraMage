Chapter 646:

The Kingdom of Jive

As one of the seven kingdoms of the Holy War, Jive was known for its progressive and liberal society, where citizens wielded significant influence. This made it a hotspot for international students seeking freedom and opportunity.

Among those students was Dalia Nane.

At the age of four, Nane enrolled in a magic academy. By seventeen, he had graduated as the second-highest-ranked mage in the world.

An accomplishment beyond the grasp of ordinary understanding.

Yet, a closer look at Nane's life would reveal an almost monotonously smooth path, enough to bring a wry smile to one's face.

“Nane, have you made your decision?”

The headmaster of Jive Royal Magic Academy personally visited Nane, a student who had set a historical record for the kingdom, to ask him this pressing question.

“No, not yet,” Nane replied.

The headmaster’s eyes widened in surprise at the unexpected answer.

“The graduation ceremony will end in three hours, and the results will be publicly posted by the Red Line Talent Management Bureau. If you plan to take the Ivory Tower test, you need to request the withdrawal of the public posting before then.”

“Then, I’ll think about it until then,” Nane said casually.

The headmaster offered a sympathetic smile.

‘As I thought, he has a kind heart. Even if he doesn’t say it, he must want to contribute to the kingdom that raised him.’

Nane had always been a polite and moral student.

In fact, no one had ever seen him argue or even frown.

‘That’s what happens when someone’s a true genius.’

Having faced no hardship in life only meant his talent had transcended those challenges.

Despite earning the fastest top rank in Jive's history, the headmaster privately wondered if Nane had even used 1% of his potential.

“Just make the choice that feels right for you. Entering the Ivory Tower and making a name for yourself would still bring honor to Jive.”

As the headmaster repeated his encouragement, Nane responded briefly, “Thinking is too much of a bother.”

Hearing such a blunt tone for the first time, the headmaster flinched but dismissed it quickly.

‘Well, it is the Ivory Tower, after all.’

Even geniuses couldn’t help but feel the pressure of such a decision—or so the headmaster thought.

In truth, Nane didn’t care about his future at all.

Whether it was joining Jive’s Magic Association, taking the Ivory Tower test, or dying right there on the spot—it all seemed equally insignificant.

‘Yes, now I….’

Lost in thought, Nane continued walking, and the headmaster found himself frozen as if time itself had stopped.

Birds in the sky were suspended in mid-flight, planets ceased their rotation, and celestial bodies orbiting the sun halted their movements.

The entire universe had hit the brakes.

‘What should I do?’

If existence had no limits, then not even the cosmic scale of Nane’s concentration could be considered unreasonable.

In this universal stillness, Nane threw the dice of the infinite polyhedron toward the end of his thoughts.

Having mastered every conceivable path, he held no preferences, no biases.

Finally, when the dice stopped spinning, Nane realized the single remaining truth within himself.

‘I’ve made my decision.’

As Nane disappeared down the corridor, the headmaster watched his back with kind eyes, silently wishing him well.

‘May your choice bring you happiness.’

In accordance with Red Line regulations, 20 students graduated from Jive Royal Magic Academy. When the time came for the traditional choir performance, they stood in perfect formation and began to sing:

“As one, we bring light to the world.”

The emotional peak of the ceremony moved many to tears.

Then, Nane stepped forward, removed his ceremonial robes, and tossed them aside.

‘What’s he doing? Is this some kind of surprise event?’

The other graduates exchanged puzzled glances, unsure of what was happening.

“Nane! What are you doing?!”

History was made as Jive witnessed an unprecedented spectacle.

Nane stripped off not only his robes but also his shirt, pants, and even his underwear, before leaping onto the stage and bursting into uncontrollable laughter.

“Hahaha!”

“Kyaaah!”

Female students covered their faces, though many couldn’t resist peeking through their fingers. After all, this was Nane—the person they had admired for so long.

‘Now, what happens next?’

Nane’s only curiosity lay in this question.

After exhausting all conventional paths, his journey into the realm of the absurd was driven by a single inquiry: What is “difference”?

Endlessly seeking the unknown, he aimed to redefine his existence as something uniquely singular in the universe.

“Take this!”

To the chaotic tune of the orchestra, Nane spun his hips wildly, releasing a stream of urine in a perfect circle.

“Aaah! He’s insane!”

While most fled the scene in panic, they couldn’t help but feel an unsettling chill as they watched him move with unnatural poise.

‘How can he do that?’

His natural state—completely uninhibited and free of tension or excitement—exuded an unnerving calm.

“Nane, stop! What are you doing?!”

‘What if I don’t stop? What happens then?’

As the torrent continued, teachers raised their arms to shield their faces, and younger students burst into tears.

To truly break free from conventional boundaries, Nane realized he needed to delve deeper into the exploration of “difference”—perhaps infinitely so.

Casting a Flight spell, Nane hovered above the crowd, gazing toward the horizon before flying away.

“What do we do, Headmaster? He’s heading toward the city!”

Should they report him?

The hesitation stemmed from the fact that, even after all this chaos, Nane remained the greatest student in Jive’s history.

“Contact the guards! In that state of mind, who knows what he might do in the city!”

“Understood!”

When Nane arrived in the bustling city square, he landed lightly in its center, drawing everyone's attention.

“Kyaaah!”

Panic spread instantly, and vendors grabbed clubs, mistaking him for a perverted troublemaker.

“What’s your deal? Are you here to mess up our business?”

While Nane didn’t repeat his earlier antics, his mere presence struck fear into their hearts.

His eyes weren’t normal.

‘He’s not just some pervert.’

Though physically slight, Nane exuded pure madness, an aura of complete unpredictability about what he might do next.

‘What is the essence of difference?’

Nane’s actions had no rationale.

This deviation from the norm was what set him apart, and he began his exploration of “difference” from the most primal level.

‘Let’s start with form.’

With that thought, Nane headed toward one of Jive’s most progressive landmarks—a tattoo parlor.

“Welcome…! Aaaah!”

As the female employees of the tattoo parlor screamed at the sight of Nane’s nude body, he stood unaffected before a large mirror.

“Carve graffiti into my body.”

One hour later.

Outside the parlor, 60 armed guards surrounded the building. The captain of the city’s defense force shouted at the top of his lungs:

“Release the hostages! The longer you stall, the worse it’ll be for you!”

Inside, however, the employees could do nothing but continue tattooing Nane’s body. His command was simple: carve every design in their catalog into his skin. This included explicit curses that were now etched down his spine.

‘This must hurt….’

Although advanced magical equipment made the process faster, blood streamed freely from Nane’s countless wounds. Despite this, he calmly admired his reflection in the mirror.

‘Is my form unique now?’

Or does it still conform to some concept?

‘Perhaps I should take it further.’

Gripping a jar of piercings, Nane began piercing his ears one by one.

Crunch. Crunch.

The sound of metal tearing through raw flesh was enough to make the employees burst into tears. By the time he had adorned 13 piercings, they were openly sobbing.

‘An existence that can only be called Nane.’

Suddenly, Nane twisted his body, smearing a half-finished tattoo. Ignoring the mishap, he turned to the dressing table, grabbed bottles of hair dye, and poured their contents over his head.

“Stop! That dye is toxic—it’ll burn your skin!”

With his entire body covered in open wounds, the cocktail of chemicals burned like fire as it seeped into his flesh.

“Hahaha! Kekekeke!”

Laughing theatrically, Nane tilted his head in dissatisfaction, cleared his throat, and erupted into another round of manic laughter.

“Hahahahaha! Hahahahaha!”

At the brink of insanity, the tattoo work was finally completed. Exhausted, the employees fell to their knees and pleaded.

“Please, it’s done! Just leave now!”

“No, there’s one more thing left,” Nane replied.

Extending his tongue, he pointed to it. Two of the five employees fainted on the spot.

“Hiiieee! Hiiieee!”

The horrifying sounds from inside the parlor made the guards outside grit their teeth.

“What’s going on in there? Captain, can’t we just storm the place?”

“He’s a graduate of the magic academy. If we’re reckless, the hostages could die.”

A single fatality would trigger an inquiry by the citizen's council.

Inside, the employee carving a sword tattoo onto Nane’s tongue was pale as a ghost, her eyes swollen from crying.

‘This is hell.’

Though she might quit her profession after this, she would forever be remembered as the person who etched a singularity onto Nane’s magic.

“Sword.”

Memorizing the intricate design of the blade now engraved on his tongue, Nane turned and stepped out of the parlor.

“Arrest him! Now!”

The first squad of guards lunged at him, spears in hand.

‘Combat.’

Nane thought.

Instantly, time froze. The guards, leaping mid-air, were suspended as if mounted on a canvas.

This was focus beyond human comprehension—a realm of total mental transcendence.

‘Sword of Wind.’

The perfect efficiency of his mana surged to unparalleled heights, materializing a blinding white sword in his palm. The blade dissolved into an ethereal shimmer before slicing through the air.

“Guhhh!”

Deep scratches appeared across the steel armor of the first squad. The second and third squads rushed in.

“Surround him! Don’t let him escape!”

Despite their numbers—60 soldiers in total—they underestimated Nane. Their mistake wasn’t failing to block his body but failing to comprehend his mind.

‘Ah, I see now.’

Once again, the universe froze. In the realm of difference, Nane began dismantling countless concepts.

‘A blade of fire. A blade of ice. A blade of lightning. A blade of earth.’

Each held a unique elemental attribute.

‘A savage blade. A gentle blade. A grotesque blade. A cruel blade.’

Even identical attributes diverged in feeling.

‘An unpredictable blade. A predictive blade. A blade that shatters predictions. A blade that counters shattered predictions.’

Even when the feeling remained, the temperament varied.

‘Disassemble everything in every moment.’

The concepts splintered into over a thousand variations, each emitting a spectrum of blades with no repetition.

“What the hell is this…?!”

Before the captain could issue a retreat, the swords erupted outward in all directions.

BOOOOM!

It felt like the Big Bang—a primordial explosion birthing chaos.

When the royal magic academy’s teachers arrived three minutes later, the area surrounding Nane had been reduced to scorched rubble.

“So he finally went through with it,” one of them muttered.

Remarkably, there were no fatalities. Nane had annihilated everything purely for the sake of experiencing a "different" kind of battle.

By the time this fact was confirmed, two hours had passed, and Nane had already disappeared.

He had stolen clothes from an unconscious civilian—not out of necessity but simply because.

If wearing clothes bound him to another conceptual framework, he would strip them off without hesitation.

Watching Nane’s casual thievery, the headmaster clenched his fists in fury and shouted:

“Nane! What the hell are you doing?! You had a bright future ahead of you! Do you intend to disgrace your family’s honor?”

‘Family honor….’

Am I a being defined by lineage?

‘No, of course not.’

Nane dismantled the concept of the surname Dalia and raised a finger to cover one eye.

Turning to the headmaster, he extended his tongue to reveal the sword tattoo etched upon it.

“Hieeeeeee.”

A man who deconstructs all truths of the universe.

Psychedelic Nane.
