Chapter 684: Offering Flowers (4)

Kido, whose thoughts had reached that point, suddenly realized.

‘I see. Heh heh heh, this is getting really interesting, isn’t it? Maybe….’

It could even serve as a counter to Ra Enemi.

“Alright, I’ll take it. Let’s go.”

As Kido urged them on, citing the lack of time, the group moved without complaint.

Rain poured down as if a hole had been torn in the sky.

***

The remaining time until Seonghwa’s attack on the royal capital: 7 minutes and 32 seconds.

Jane’s footsteps gradually quickened as she roughly calculated the time.

To increase her speed, more blood was needed, and the resistance of the hand squeezing her heart grew fiercer with each passing moment.

“Ugh! Ugh!”

Though the sensation in her hand gripping her heart had already vanished, the transcendental magic pushed her body beyond human limits, forcing her to move endlessly.

If the human body were treated as a machine, it was akin to replacing the heart muscle with arm muscles, but durability couldn’t match. Eventually, muscles and tendons began to snap one after another.

Since it was magic, not physical law, the arm, now physically nonfunctional, could no longer move.

“Gasp!”

As her heart stopped again, Jane’s eyes widened.

‘The other hand….’

She had never been more grateful for the fact that humans had two arms.

Switching hands and compressing her heart again, her bodily functions returned, delaying death.

The sound of Aria’s sobbing echoed in her ears like an auditory hallucination, but she had no time to respond.

Carrying her own death on her back, Jane entered the control room where the main system was located.

Unable to remove her hand from her heart, she swung her already ruined arm like a stick to activate the device.

-Aria, the code….

The sobbing stopped abruptly, and an overwhelming amount of code flooded in.

Aria was also giving it her all.

Now, the remaining time was 6 minutes and 22 seconds.

Regaining control was one thing, but to cancel the attack entirely, she needed to reach the cockpit. However, Jane had little time left.

‘The Association President will handle it.’

-Jane! Are you okay?

Though it was just a voice, it was comforting to have someone bid her farewell in her final moments.

-Aria, it’s not your fault.

After a brief silence, a calm voice seeped in.

-The nation will never forget your sacrifice.

With those words, the communication ended.

She could no longer maintain the Spirit Zone.

‘Still….’

Leaning against the cold machinery, she looked up at the ceiling.

‘I kept my promise.’

If asked to recall the happiest day of her life, it was undoubtedly the day Lupist became the head of the Magic Association.

***

“Jane, you’re a genius.”

At Lupist’s words while gazing out the window of the Association President’s office, Jane shrugged.

“I know.”

“Have you ever felt a sense of superiority? The kind where you realize you’re far above others.”

“Hmm, maybe when I was really young? But why do you ask?”

“I’ve never felt such a thing. People wonder why I don’t pursue personal gains. With my powerful magic and position, you’d think I would. But humans aren’t that remarkable. Superiority is only possible when there’s something to compare to. Geniuses, fools—in the end, we’re all just cogs in a system.”

After succeeding Gaold as Association President, there were murmurs in civil society about whether the appointment was too extreme.

“I didn’t know you cared about others’ opinions.”

“I don’t. Or maybe I do. What matters is facing the truth.”

“And what truth is that?”

“Humans are not equal.”

Lupist was firm.

“Ninety percent of humans are responsible for production and reproduction. The remaining ten percent consume their resources and lead the whole.”

“That’s a rather disgusting way of thinking.”

“It is. But what if it’s true?”

Lupist turned to Jane.

“Most people don’t know how the world works. They take everything around them for granted, like air. They never question why bread appears on their table every morning.”

“That’s not something to blame them for.”

“True. What’s a ‘more valuable life’ anyway? What I’m saying is, someone has to face that disgusting truth so they can live.”

Lupist glanced at the nameplate still bearing the name of the former Magic Association President, Michael Gaold.

“I’ll change it now.”

Shaking his head, Lupist placed his hand on the nameplate.

“Gaold was popular. He even had his own guard. He was personable, able to draw people in. But Jane, emotions alone can’t sustain a system. If Gaold empathized with human suffering on an individual level, I protect humanity from the opposite extreme—the system. Even if it’s disgusting, I believe that’s the duty of the Magic Association President, the core cog of this kingdom.”

“…I see.”

It was a deeply personal inauguration speech, meant only for Jane.

“I’m grateful to you. Without you, I wouldn’t have reached this position.”

Unused to praise, Jane scratched her head awkwardly.

“Well, it was my job. And I became the chief of staff too.”

“So….”

Lupist turned around, hands behind his back, and spoke curtly.

“Let’s work well together.”

Jane’s eyes curved into a smile.

***

‘The greatest honor of my life.’

That’s how Jane recalled that day.

Having come this far, she had no regrets about her life, but she wanted to revisit her happiest memory one last time. She continued to squeeze her heart relentlessly.

But as the muscles in her wrist ruptured without mercy, even that became impossible. She pulled her hand from her abdomen.

As oxygen to her brain was cut off, her consciousness was sucked into a deep abyss.

The final moment of her life.

Had she lived without regrets?

‘Respected Association President.’

A beautiful smile formed on her lips as her head drooped weakly.

‘Was I a core cog?’

Jane’s heart stopped.

Arriving at Seonghwa’s entrance, Shirone noticed a unique signal approaching through the Ultima System.

‘What’s this?’

It was the most intuitive signal he’d encountered so far.

With a coherence close to the Ultima System’s immediate predecessor, Shirone shouted.

“Evade!”

***

Dragon Speech—Thunderclap.

As the group scattered, an electric charge erupted where they had been standing.

“Eeek!”

The current spread spherically through the rainwater’s electrolytes.

While the mages could teleport out of range, Rian had no choice but to take the full brunt.

“Ouch! Ouch!”

Kido, bouncing around with his spear stuck in the ground, slid to a stop.

“Hey! That almost killed us!”

If that had been magic, Shirone, with his Spirit Zone active, wouldn’t have missed it.

“Sorry, I didn’t sense it. And it wasn’t magic.”

It wasn’t the Spirit Zone.

Like Elysion, the boundary was faint. Without the Ultima System, they wouldn’t have gotten a warning before the attack.

“Have you arrived, mongrels?”

Kido grimaced and adjusted his glasses at the sight of the massive figure standing in the rain.

“Combat Minister Draker. A dragonkin.”

The fact that half his face was covered in scales suggested his entire body was the same.

The dragon’s amber eyes, sharp teeth perfectly aligned, and cold body steaming despite the winter rain all evoked the cold-blooded nature of dragons.

“This one’s going to be trouble.”

Kido twirled his spear, ready to fight.

“He’s the strongest in Radum.”

“That’s correct.”

Draker’s displeasure was evident.

“But hearing a goblin speak of strength feels like tainting my nobility.”

Even under the gaze of a top predator, Kido remained unfazed.

His mind, which converted the experience of consumption into the experience of the whole, flowed as calmly as a great river.

Rian stepped forward, resting his greatsword on his shoulder.

“I’ll handle this.”

The title of “strongest” drew him in, but the other three had the critical task of finding Ra Enemi.

“Rian, that guy….”

“I know.”

Though not as sensitive as Shirone, Rian could feel Draker’s terrifying fighting spirit, which made such things irrelevant.

“I can’t protect anyone and win at the same time.”

His words implied he could win if he didn’t have to protect anyone, so Draker crossed his arms and growled.

“Do you really think you’re on my level just by standing there? You won’t take a single step forward.”

Rian replied without hesitation.

“Go.”

The three moved simultaneously, but the dragonkin’s eyes accurately caught Shirone, who had taken the lead.

‘You’re courting death.’

Just as he was about to lunge, a chilling sensation ran down Draker’s spine.

Turning his gaze swiftly, he saw Rian, who hadn’t moved a moment ago, now right in front of him.

***

Axing—Deny.

“Insects!”

As Draker raised his arm to block the lightning-fast greatsword, a loud clang resounded.

It wasn’t a sound a living being should make.

‘He’s tough. Half-hearted attacks won’t even scratch him.’

Draker smirked as he blocked the blow capable of splitting an elephant.

“Is this all you’ve got… Huh?”

An overwhelming force pressed down on him from the greatsword still in contact.

“Grrr!”

Finally sensing the danger, Draker poured all his strength into his body, causing the solid ground to cave in and bury his legs.

‘This is impossible! How is he doing this in this state?’

If human strength is called “human power,” dragonkin strength is called “dragon power.”

The terms differ because the units of strength exerted biologically are on entirely different levels.

“You arrogant brat!”

As Draker swung his arm with all his might, Rian floated lightly and landed gracefully.

“What’s going on? I already gave it my all!”

Though all three of Shirone’s group had infiltrated Seonghwa safely, Draker’s pride wouldn’t let him look back.

“I’ll kill you.”

***

Dragon Speech—Dragon Charge.

Propelled by the power of dragon speech, Draker launched a physical attack, lifting Rian’s legs off the ground.

“Ugh!”

Rian was sent flying, piercing through buildings for a full kilometer.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

The continuous impact of wooden, stone, and steel structures against his back nearly knocked Rian unconscious.

‘Stop!’

He invoked Deny, but it only shattered his own brakes.

‘Stop!’

Deny activated repeatedly.

‘I said stop!’

Three hundred walls were pierced, and dozens of steel beams bent and snapped around his body.

“Stop, damn it!”

By the time he finally halted in the wreckage of a collapsed building, he was over two kilometers from Seonghwa.

“You… Are you really human?”

Draker, still pressed against Rian, stared at him in disbelief.

“Dunno. Forgot about that stuff long ago.”

‘Human power rivaling dragon power….’

For a human to kill a dragon is impossible by physical calculations.

“Want to try being a dragonslayer?”

It had to be something beyond mere strength.

The legends of dragonslayers all shared one thing: unwavering conviction.

“Sorry, but I don’t deal with fakes.”

Draker’s eyes filled with a dragon’s wrath.

“You insignificant human!”

Draker kicked the greatsword aside, sending Rian flying through a building’s wall.

“I’ll crush you!”

Following him inside, Draker saw Rian already waiting, swinging his greatsword vertically.

‘I’ll cut you!’

Rian’s conviction, the Will of Macha, began to cleave everything from the edge of the world.

‘What the—?’

An illusion caused by the negation of laws.

The reason time seemed to freeze as the world split was because spacetime itself was being pushed aside.

“I am a dragonkin!”

***

Dragon Speech—Heaven-Earth Dichotomy.

Binary 0 and 1 separated like electrodes, creating a horizon that split the sky and earth.

Rian’s vertical slash struck the horizon head-on.

Kwaaaaang!

Rian’s law cleaved through the center, while Draker’s law pulled apart from both sides.

Neither was absolute.

“Raaaaah!”

Rian and Draker roared simultaneously.

Whoooosh!

The two laws intertwined like a storm, causing a seven-story building to balloon outward before exploding.

Even in the heavy rain, the rising smoke made it seem like magic.

“Is someone still out there? What are they doing?”

Aria spoke.

“My mission is over. You don’t need to protect me anymore.”

Her words were true, but with her body temperature dangerously low from the rain, leaving her would be like abandoning a corpse.

‘Rescue is impossible anyway.’

Kuan’s combat strength was better spent on more constructive tasks than carrying Aria out.

“I’ll be fine. I can hold on.”

Though she said it to reassure him, she knew she was already waiting for death.

“I’ll be back soon.”

Knowing this, Kuan turned away.

“Aria!”

At that moment, the sound of hooves splashing through the rain approached as Brooks arrived with his subordinates.

It was a chance to entrust Aria to someone, but Kuan, a soldier to the bone, was suspicious first.

“Why are you here? Don’t you know this is a restricted area?”

“Damn it! I had no choice! Aria! Snap out of it!”

As if he didn’t want to be there either, Brooks snapped at Kuan before slapping Aria’s cheek.

“Put her in the carriage! We’re leaving!”

“Did you really come all this way to rescue one woman?”

As Brooks hesitated, the leader of Spectrum, Venetia, stepped out of the carriage.

“Take me to Ra Enemi.”

Kuan looked at Brooks for an explanation.

“I don’t know! She was practically dead, then she came back like this! I tried to lock her up, but she said she could see Ra Enemi!”

Though not deeply involved in the project, Kuan wasn’t oblivious to what Lupist wanted.

“Give me Nemesis.”

Staring at Venetia’s outstretched hand, Kuan sheathed his sword and grabbed her waist.

“Later. First, we infiltrate Seonghwa.”

At least this solved everything.

“Take Aria to a doctor. She might be infected.”

Without waiting for a reply, Kuan kicked off the ground, hovering at the critical point of descent before soaring higher.

As everyone stared blankly, Brooks sighed and muttered.

“Where the hell do they even find people like that?”

The Hwazok’s unique technique, Small World Creation.

Plants, though unable to act on their own, combine with countless things to create a world where they can act.

They still lack autonomy (Diamond Armament).

However, when assimilated with objects like Seonghwa, they can at least exercise judgment (Authorized Control).

‘The main system….’

Florino, who felt Seonghwa’s entire mechanism like nerves, realized Jane had succeeded.

‘Are you tormenting us to the end?’

There had been countless times in his wretched life when he wished to wither away like other Hwazok.

‘I don’t want to do anything. Why do I have to decide and act? Why won’t anyone take care of me?’

Venetia had been kind, but she abandoned Spectrum, and his second master, Ra Enemi, hadn’t appeared either.

‘All I want is to be loved.’

He simply wanted to obey someone’s words and entrust his body to warm, affectionate hands.

He wanted to fill their world with the scent of flowers.

‘No one ever loved me.’

When the first Hwazok decided to evolve from plants to animals and primates, could they have imagined how degenerate the sensory organs of the flesh would be?

‘This is your fault.’

The expectation of being cared for as gently as a butterfly might have been the Hwazok’s own naivety.

‘Hitting, tormenting, making me scream.’

The Hwazok are a failed species in terms of embryology.

But the rapid evolution they achieved through their unique techniques was something even Lupist couldn’t scoff at.

Noticing the heavy rain outside through the Spirit Zone, Florino approached with a murderous aura.

“Hand over the code. You must resent the heavens.”

With the clouds blocking the sun, Seonghwa’s photon collection would inevitably slow.

Florino took a deep breath and steadied himself.

‘I have to fight.’

Struggling dynamically went against the nature of flowers, but to prevent his race’s extinction, he had no choice.

“Time is on our side. You can’t defeat me.”

“Is that so?”

Florino, now calm, spread his arms, causing the room to tremble violently.

‘Is Seonghwa moving?’

No, it was growing.

‘If he increases his size to boost photosynthesis, the entire capital will be within his range. I’ll have to kill him.’

Hoping Jane had completed her mission, Lupist conjured dozens of rotating blades—steel storms.

“I’ll tear you to shreds.”

As the blades closed in from all sides, the iron plate beneath Florino’s feet rose like liquid, deflecting the steel.

“It’s useless. I am Seonghwa, and Seonghwa is me.”

Florino, who desired submission, had become the interior minister solely because he was the best caretaker for Seonghwa.

‘It’s not liquefaction. He’s accelerating the growth of minerals to the extreme, giving them fluidity.’

He was likely using the energy collected by Seonghwa, which suited Lupist just fine.

Florino, having opened the iron barrier, spoke with icy eyes.

“It’s a shame. Soon, the capital will be destroyed. Your people will taste the suffering we endured.”

“Do you really think humans are that weak?”

Dozens of sharp steel chunks formed around Lupist, ready to launch.

Florino, glaring at Lupist with resentment, bit his lip and raised the iron barrier again.

“We should have trampled you in your primitive era.”

***

Steel Magic—High-Speed Armor-Piercing Round.

The steel chunks shot out at incredible speed, piercing through the iron barrier with a deafening roar.
