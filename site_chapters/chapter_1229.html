Chapter 1229:

The hall fell silent.

It wasn’t about judging someone by dividing good and evil into sides and going to war.

"Do you really think anything changes because of this?"

The truth was—he didn’t even want to condemn the guilty, encourage the righteous, or punish the wicked.

"I just want to solve the problem."

He simply hated the pinnacle of chaos—when people acted knowing they couldn't take responsibility.

Upon hearing Shirone’s words, Baal understood.

‘This isn’t Yahweh.’

If Miracle Stream was harmful to demons, then Photon Cannon: Infinity was destructive to everything.

‘Atomic destruction.’

A Yahweh who had undergone Minor Purification, purging all internal Maga, shouldn’t even be capable of anger.

‘But… it’s similar.’

To Baal, Photon Cannon: Infinity clearly carried the characteristics of purification.

The difference was quantum versus particle—but the way it destroyed by directly confronting the target was the same.

And that made it all the more unbearable.

‘I lost… to a mage?’

Even having reached the final stage of chaos, he had still been crushed by the pinnacle of intelligence.

‘Don’t make me laugh! It’s not over yet!’

The methodology of evil.

Baal’s pride as 1st Legion Commander and the strongest of the demons no longer mattered.

"Yahweh…"

His twisted body collapsed onto its elbows, pressing his face flat against the floor.

"I will repent."

Intelligence had won.

Yet watching the scene, both Argones and Great Star wore grim expressions.

‘Shirone…’

Good and evil reside at the extremes of the world—but those with purpose are often the most stable.

‘He’s now the strongest human. He can choose anything. If he ever strays…’

The future of humanity could spiral into ruin.

"All right."

Shirone pulled back his fist and stepped away.

"It won’t bring back the dead, but… please repent. Mourn them—truly, from your heart."

Because if no one repents, the day when evil disappears will never come.

Seeing Shirone give Baal another chance, Great Star’s eyes welled with tears.

‘Yes… I remember now.’

He didn’t choose the path of Yahweh because he wanted power—he chose it because he couldn’t bear to harbor malice.

"Thank you."

Baal bowed, his forehead pressed to the ground, but his eyes remained cold.

‘This is the end, Yahweh.’

Deadly Cross — Hell Gate of the 1st Legion Commander.

With Maga at its current strength, the meteor’s arrival would be significantly accelerated.

‘And the destructive power… beyond imagination.’

As Baal’s body began emitting a violet smoke, Xing’s eyes flared with alarm.

"Shirone! He’s—"

Baal lifted his head.

His molecular structure had twisted, his face grotesquely deformed—as if printed on the wrong sheet.

"In the end, I win, Yahweh."

"No!"

As Great Star cried out, Argones launched forward and sliced off Baal’s head in one clean motion.

"…Too late."

The Administrator realized it first. Shirone turned his gaze toward the vastness of space.

‘It’s coming.’

An enormous meteor, heavy with mass.

As Baal’s body completely disintegrated, the Maga that had permeated the atmosphere let out a corrupted voice:

—Kuhahaha! I’ve become pure Maga! You can’t kill me now! So what now? Can you solve this too? Even if you destroy the meteor, humanity will go extinct from the aftermath! You couldn’t protect anything! Did you really think I’d repent?

"No."

He had expected this.

"I only hoped. That’s all. Nothing changes. We’ll keep reaching out. Even if you mock us, even if you sneer, even if we're tricked again by silver tongues like yours…"

The answer was already clear.

"We’re the ones who are right."

Nowhere in this universe exists a sanctuary for demons.

"Once every hour."

Light-like smoke began to rise from Shirone’s body.

"Elikia."

The Ultima System, comprising seven million people, began to reject the Demon Realm System.

—What are you doing?!

It was only 0.3% of all living humans, but if all of human justice lay in that sliver—

‘Then that alone…’

Was 0.003% of the entire universe.

—No! This can’t be! How is this happening?!

"It’s enough."

Compared to the universe, destroying a meteor the size of a speck of dust was nothing.

Shirone opened his arms, and a cross-shaped brilliance launched a beam of light into the heavens.

—Yahweh! You will die in agony! One day, a human you love will be the one to kill you…!

Baal’s voice sped up, sensing his end—but it was a scream that marked his final moment.

—Oooooohhhh! Ooooooohhhh!

A sound like being sucked into the abyss rang out. Then the flash disappeared, and silence fell.

Everyone turned toward Shirone.

"What… was that?"

For Xing, who had governed the Laws, the flash from earlier was the greatest shock of her life.

"Elikia. No…"

Shirone turned to her and answered.

"Ultima System."

Xing’s expression went blank. Umji and Yangji, supporting her, furrowed their brows.

"Xing."

Her willpower was weakening.

‘She’s being persuaded.’

The truth defined by seven million people was enough to shake even Xing’s unshakable philosophy.

Even Argones, who had watched Gaians from the sidelines, thought the same:

‘If this is truly Ultima… rejecting the Demon Realm would be easy.’

But what about Cell Buster?

He couldn’t make a judgment.

‘This level of power wouldn’t be enough to stop it. But… if this is the spark that finishes the real Ultima…’

Then not even the gods could predict the outcome.

‘Should I destroy him now…?’

Great Star stepped in.

"Argones. You promised, didn’t you? Humanity hasn’t trespassed into the divine realm yet."

She turned to Shirone.

"Release me. If the Outer World system collapses, the seals will break, and Hell will overlap with reality."

The demons would descend into the real world.

That was also why Shirone intended to establish Tormia as the new global government.

"Xing… maybe we can never truly understand another person’s truth. But we can walk together—not through logic, but through heart. If even a little of your heart still reaches for me…"

Shirone extended his hand.

"Let’s fight together."

Xing remained silent, but inside her mind, a fierce battle raged.

‘Shirone… just a little more… convince me.’

Uorin was exhausted.

“Haa… Haa…!”

The headaches from foresight, the assassination attempts from agents of multiple nations, the constant brushes with death—it was all too much.

Kido looked back at her.

She’s at her limit.

Physically and mentally.

She’s surviving in the most extreme of states, but even a slight shock now would cause her to collapse.

She was the mitochondrial Eve who had survived through the entirety of human history—but the Uorin of now was different.

If she could believe that every experience was a gift to her new self, she might have endured…

But she no longer has a daughter. Right now, she’s all alone.

She had endured all this time for one reason: to bear Shirone’s child. That was her only purpose.

Her life now is as precious as the millennia she waited. And yet, she’s crossed the threshold of death dozens of times already.

Should she rest?

Just as Kido was thinking this, Uorin suddenly gasped in shock and collapsed to the ground.

“Haa!”

Kido rushed to her side.

“What’s wrong?! What happened?!”

She stared up at the ceiling with a dazed expression, her body trembling violently.

“Elykia… Elykia…”

“Huh?”

She turned her head toward him and said,

“It didn’t activate.”

The light that had always enveloped the entire Holy Ground at the same time each day, revealing Havitz’s location—it hadn’t appeared.

“He used it somewhere else. Shirone… he’s not protecting me anymore. I’m not…”

Through Uorin’s eyes, Kido could see her mind unraveling in real time.

“Uorin! Get a grip! Hey!”

Uorin suddenly clutched her head and screamed.

“Ahhh! AAAHHH!”

I’m going to die. I’m going to die. I’m going to die.

Tens of thousands of years… rendered meaningless.

“Focus! Hey!”

Kido grabbed her shoulders and shook her, but once someone’s will breaks, it’s not easily restored.

Uorin grabbed Kido and screamed.

“What do I do?! Havitz is coming! He’s going to kill me and steal my ability! It’s all over!”

Unable to bear the crushing despair, she screamed again.

“AAAAHHH!”

“Please, just pull yourself together!”

Kido slapped her hard across the face, and her gaze briefly returned to normal.

“Havitz isn’t coming! You’re still remembering him, right?! That means he didn’t activate Vanishing!”

“That’s why Shirone didn’t use Elykia either! Nothing is over yet! We’re still—!”

That moment, Kido’s senses detected movement.

“Who’s there?!”

Something jumped out from around the corner of the hallway—and Kido’s mouth dropped open in shock.

A brain was floating toward them.

“A… brain?”

Hearing that, Uorin turned her head, and despite her fear, she grimaced in disgust.

“You…”

Kido turned to her.

“What? You know what that thing is?”

“Adam.”

As the brain floated forward, a projection unfolded around it, transforming into a man with metallic hair.

“Is that… a human? No, something’s off.”

“He’s a Gaian. And… also my husband.”

“...Huh?!”

While Kido blinked in disbelief, Adam calmly walked down the hall and spoke.

“It’s been a long time, Uorin. Or should I say…”

He paused—then called her by her true name:

“Lilith.”

The name she held before she became Eve.

Nade glanced around nervously.

“This is the Core of Melkidu?”

Cain answered, “That’s right. More precisely, it’s the path to the Hall of Evil. And just so you know—this isn’t some under-coded simulation. It’s a real forest, in reality. Which means time flows here exactly as it does in the real world.”

Shirone realized why Cain had visibly aged.

‘Even if he used Deep Time to slow aging, he’d have to return to reality to bring users into the Core. But even then, it would only be a few hours every five years or so…’

And yet, Cain had become middle-aged—just how long had human history truly been?

They pushed their way through the forest until a colossal altar made of gold rose before them.

Cain gestured toward the Hall of Evil.

“As you might’ve guessed, I wasn’t the one who made this place. The original Melkidu actually referred to the Hall of Evil itself. But after I entered this forest…”

“You created a strange system,” Iruki said flatly.

Cain chuckled, clearly amused.

“Ha! Wasn’t it fun, though? Anyway, come in. Every evil ever committed is recorded here. And once it’s erased, it disappears from reality too.”

Curtis’s face darkened.

At the entrance, they found a staircase leading underground and a corridor glowing with a continuous white light.

Eden asked with a grim look, “How far does it go?”

“Well… as far as human history? Honestly, I’ve never been to the end myself, so I don’t know. Trying to explore the whole thing would probably kill you of old age. Besides…”

Cain turned into the first chamber along the corridor.

“This place here is my favorite.”

The group followed his gaze to an enormous portrait hanging on the wall.

“Whoa…”

It wasn’t just the size—it exuded a strange, eerie aura, as if the artwork itself was alive.

“Not every figure is given this kind of privilege, but this one’s special. You’d know, wouldn’t you, Yahweh?”

Shirone replied quietly, “Mitochondrial Eve.”

At that, the group leaned in for a closer look at the painting.

“Uorin…?”

She was beautiful, but looked nothing like her current self—and her expression radiated something dark, almost malevolent.

“That’s her true form, before reverse evolution. She’s the one who implanted the concept of good and evil into humankind.”

Nade looked bewildered.

“What the hell did she do to go that far? I mean, good and evil?”

“She…”

Shirone turned to the others, his expression bitter.

“She reached for the forbidden fruit.”
