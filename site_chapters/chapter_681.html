Chapter 681: Offering Flowers (1)

The three ministers overseeing Spectrum's offense, defense, and administration gathered around the round table.

Though Ra Enemy had issued orders to gather the Holy Flower's output at maximum capacity to strike the capital, he hadn't appeared in their memories since—but that no longer mattered.

Flarino, the defense minister managing the Holy Flower's main system, spoke.

"Only 13% remains until maximum output. In 24 minutes, the royal capital of Bashka will vanish from Tormia."

Ryka, the domestic affairs minister, fixed his cold vampire eyes on her.

"Impressive. The core code we couldn't hack for decades."

The Holy Flower was fundamentally a defensive ancient weapon, but at maximum output, it could strike even the royal palace.

The reason they hadn't done so until now was because the kingdom had locked it with an encryption during the Holy Flower's early stages.

"Ra Enemy... So he was real."

Though Flarino had hacked the core code, she had succeeded based on Ra Enemy's memories.

At this point, it was proven that he, who existed only in memory without a physical form, had the power to destroy this world.

"That doesn't matter. The point is non-humans ruling over humanity's kingdom."

Drakker, the combat minister, stirred his energy as he spoke.

The draconic aura unique to dragonkin erupted, making even fellow ministers Ryka and Flarino struggle to breathe.

"Shouldn't we move soon? A few humans have already infiltrated the Holy Flower. Let's end this before it gets complicated."

Ryka asked Flarino,

"Who's inside?"

"The Magic Association President and the Secretary. The pact is broken, but if we can't stop those two, everything will be for nothing. Ra Enemy isn't showing up. This must be our problem now."

"Humans... Pathetic."

Ryka's body began dissolving into black smoke.

"I'll finish this quickly."

As he vanished completely, Drakker also rose from the table.

"I'll handle the outside."

"Need any help?"

Drakker curled his lips, reptilian scales shifting.

"Just guard the flower well."

It was a double entendre—guarding the Holy Flower, and her, as she too was a flower.

Though she had reached a level where she suppressed reactions to others' emotions, the sensitivity unique to flowerkin hadn't disappeared.

Flowerkin devoted everything to someone who loved them.

Born that way, she didn't feel bad about it, but humanity's possessiveness was unmatched among living beings.

'Guard the flower.'

Flarino took a deep breath and steeled herself.

'Guard the flower.'

***

'Can I make it in time?'

Jane, having infiltrated the Holy Flower, sprinted toward the main system.

With all power in the stealth facility cut off, she realized Spectrum was preparing to strike the capital.

'I have to do this.'

The problem was Aria's condition, her consciousness linked to the Spirit Zone, deteriorating slowly.

If she lost consciousness, accessing the core code might become impossible.

'Was this the right choice?'

If Lupist had been here, he might have slapped her.

'Association President.'

Over a decade had passed since she began working with Lupist.

She had joined as his aide after an interview when he entered politics as a mage.

Her being a mage likely played a role in her hiring, but their relationship hadn't been smooth from the start.

***

"Criticism is growing in civil society, calling you too extreme. High nobles are particularly opposed to the excessive taxes levied for defense budget increases."

"Proceed as planned. It's necessary."

Lupist wasn't one for elaboration, and Jane was brimming with passion.

"So I drafted a revised proposal. Adjusting the tax increase to around 1.3% and applying progressive rates to the nobles—"

"Damn, you talk too much."

Jane's hand, flipping through documents, froze. Her expression stiffened as she retorted,

"Excuse me? What did you just say?"

Lupist, resting his arms on the table, glared at her and enunciated clearly.

"I said, you talk too damn much."

Too stunned to react, she stood there dumbfounded before her eyes blazed with anger.

"Too much? You're saying I talk too much?"

"Exactly. Got a problem?"

"This is blatant sexism! I'll report this to the National Inspection Office! And unless you apologize properly right now, I'll go public with this! How can a politician with discriminatory tendencies manage state affairs—?!"

"Do it."

Lupist cut her off.

"Do whatever you want. But you know what? You're fired as of now."

The color drained from Jane's face.

"Th-This is unfair—"

"I don't want to work with you. So get out. Scram."

Snorting, Jane slammed the documents on the floor and stormed out, slamming the door hard enough to deafen.

"What kind of lunatic is she?"

That evening, Lupist left the royal palace, coat in hand, after finishing work.

'Need to hire a new aide tomorrow.'

Without someone to handle minor tasks, his fatigue was worse than usual.

"What now?"

Jane was waiting outside the palace gates.

Her eyes burned with determination, and she marched up to him the moment she saw him.

"Hey!"

"Hey?"

"Yeah, hey! You're not my boss anymore, so what? Got a problem with that?"

"Are you drunk?"

"No, but I'm about to be! Let's talk before I quit. I can't sleep worrying about this country with your rotten mindset."

"Then don't sleep. Got nothing better to do anyway."

As Lupist tried to walk past, she sidestepped to block him.

"Where do you think you're going? You owe me a proper apology!"

"I did nothing to apologize for."

"Oh, really?"

Jane looked around deliberately and shouted,

"Everyone, look here! This politician today, he—!"

"Are you insane? What are you doing?"

Lupist grabbed her shoulders.

"Hah! Scared of people knowing, huh?"

"Not scared—just embarrassed."

"Then be embarrassed. Got nothing better to do anyway?"

There was no one more stubborn than her.

"Fine. Let's go."

"Should've done that earlier."

With a huff, she led the way to a bar, and Lupist followed, grinding his teeth.

Two hours later, a thoroughly drunk Jane slurred the same words repeatedly.

"You can't live like this. You'll end up with no friends. Understand?"

Lupist checked the time.

"It's late. Let's go back."

Jane's upper body swayed as if spinning on its own.

"You say go back, so I have to? Huh? You the boss?"

She poked Lupist's temple with her index finger.

"Are you..."

She poked again, harder.

"...the boss?"

Lupist glared at her, baring his teeth as if ready to fight.

"Sober up. You're a mind mage."

"No! I wanna get drunk! What, girls can't drink?"

"I already apologized. Multiple times."

"Say sorry and it's all fine? What about the scars on my heart?"

Jane thumped her chest in frustration.

"You can't live like this. You'll end up—"

It felt like a divine loop in time.

"Come back to work tomorrow."

Jane burst into laughter.

"Just like that—"

"No more 'girl' comments, no sexism, and I'll review your proposals. So come back tomorrow."

Her suggestions had merit.
