Chapter 1024: Electric Civilization (4)

Along the coast of the Kingdom of Dionas, numerous artillery installations could be seen.

Beyond the dense jungle lay the royal castle, and colorful birds flew in the sky.

Shirone, aboard Arachne's ship, initially prohibited the refugees from approaching.

“There might be cannon fire. Let's wait here while I go ashore.”

At that moment, the sound of a cannon echoed.

Shirone's party turned to see that what had been fired into the sky was a firework filled with confetti.

Soon, lively music played, and a fast boat approached quickly.

“Stop!”

The knights of Arachne aboard the fast boat spoke with the pirates and returned.

“King Kalt of Dionas has sent a welcome letter. He invites us to the royal castle.”

Cairns read the letter and crumpled the paper.

“Hmph! How shameless. After sending his brother to attack us, now he invites us in?”

Shirone spoke.

“Perhaps he thought fighting at sea was his only chance. Anyway, we’re going. What about Arachne?”

“…Of course, we must go. But the ship will not dock. We can't bring the treasure into unknown territory.”

Thus, a small team consisting of Cairns, his attendants, Shirone, and the apostles was formed.

“I’ll go too.”

Langi interjected.

“Let me come along. It will help. First impressions are important.”

“You?”

Cairns was displeased, as she was the most important asset in the negotiations.

“This is enemy territory. If you’re in danger, it could put us at a disadvantage diplomatically.”

“I don’t feel safer here. The safest place is…”

Langi hugged Shirone’s arm.

“Right here, isn’t it?”

Cairns was at a loss for words.

“Is that alright?”

Shirone understood why she insisted on coming despite the danger.

‘To help me.’

Also, in Langi’s case, staying on the ship might indeed be more dangerous.

“Let’s go together. We have Jakra in our custody. I’m not sure how valuable he is as a hostage, but…”

Thus, eleven people boarded Dionas’s fast boat and landed.

As they crossed the jungle, they saw a complex path connected by planks between the trees.

There were countless small outposts, and an unmistakable sense of hostility emanated from within.

Poine smiled.

“Their hostility is almost embarrassing. Not that it would have gone unnoticed.”

The rough path required Langi and Cairns to receive magical assistance several times to reach the castle.

“This is the royal castle?”

It was a brick building that might have been fashionable 300 years ago, and its size was comparable to a monastery in the central continent.

“His Majesty is a humble man. He seeks a simple life without greed for wealth or power.”

Poine laughed at the envoy’s explanation.

“Such a man orders the kidnapping of a young maiden? Not that a pirate’s word means much.”

“Do not insult His Majesty. Trying to read his thoughts will only lead to death.”

The truth of this was evident from the terrified Jakra.

When the castle gates opened, pirates lined up along a red carpet.

It was indeed a display befitting the wealth of a great pirate, but Shirone focused on the man before him.

“Kalt.”

A man with disheveled red hair raised his palm to greet them.

“Welcome, travelers, to the sanctuary of peace.”

While most people spoke in whole tones, Kalt’s speech was mostly in half-tones.

“Worry not. As long as I am here, nothing will harm you.”

His strange intonation was unsettling, artistic, and repulsive.

“I am one of the Five Great Stars of the Ivory Tower,” Shirone stepped forward.

“Your brother, Jakra, attacked our ship. You need to explain.”

Kalt glanced indifferently at his brother.

“You wouldn’t claim ignorance, would you? He confessed everything last night.”

“Mm! Mm!”

Jakra, breaking even the mental technique, shook his head, clearly not in his right mind.

“Yes, I sent my brother. To welcome my soul mate, Langi.”

Kalt placed his hand on his chest and bowed.

“Thank you for bringing her here. You are still as beautiful as ever, Langi.”

Langi felt a shiver run through her body.

“Oh, I see you’ve trimmed your bangs. About 2 centimeters, no, 2.3 centimeters.”

While Langi didn’t mind a meticulous man, this was too much.

“Your eyelashes. The thirteenth one is bent by about 0.7 degrees. Where did the fine hair on your wrist, 7.3 centimeters up, go? It’s my favorite.”

It was mentally nauseating.

“There’s no need to fear. I won’t touch you. I don’t need to.”

Kalt’s eyelids began to twitch.

“Because I already feel it.”

He spoke slowly, almost crawling out the words, drooling as he continued.

“I feel everything. Your body, your reactions, your thoughts… Ah, I just became one with you. Integration. It feels amazing.”

Langi’s face was tearful.

‘I hate this. I’d rather die.’

She wanted to grab Shirone and scream that she couldn’t live with this man.

“Integration.”

Shirone asked seriously.

“What are you?”

The ability to perceive everything about Langi could be explained by the effects of an electromagnetic field.

And it reminded Shirone of a special sensory concept he knew.

‘Ultima System.’

The ultimate sense might be electrical.

The Ultima System he had accepted in Babel was also electrical.

‘If the universe was born from electricity, it’s not strange for the ultimate sense to be electrical.’

The unique, first one.

“Who am I?”

Kalt said.

“One who feels the waves of the world. One who is nothing. One who could be a god or mere compost.”

Shirone was convinced.

‘Similar to when I absorbed the Ultima System in Babel. Impossible to explain with just the five senses, but…’

He could feel it.

If that immense feeling had dominated him for over ten years, it was understandable that he wasn’t sane.

“You don’t seem to know, so I’ll tell you. Kalt, you’re sick. You need treatment.”

“Treatment? I’m sick?”

“Yes. It’s because of the OOPArts. I don’t know how you got it, but it made you this way. I can help. Where is it?”

He spoke sincerely.

It was why Kalt, who had ignored everyone else, was silent.

“I’m sick…”

The sensation conveyed by Shirone’s voice, felt as a wave, was true.

The pirates raised their weapons.

“Your Majesty! Don’t be fooled! They want to steal the OOPArts from us!”

In truth, they had never seen the OOPArts.

Since Kalt’s personality changed 13 years ago, everything had gone well.

“OOPArts? Ah, you mean Maika.”

Kalt reached out to the air with a distant gaze.

“It’s a beautiful place. Can you see it? It’s here, floating in the air.”

He snapped back to reality and looked at Shirone.

“Do you want to see it? Shall I show you?”

This time, Poine intervened.

“Messiah, it’s dangerous. We shouldn’t act without a complete analysis.”

It was a reasonable statement, but Shirone’s frequency was already resonating with Kalt.

“You can go to Maika?”

“No, not go. They’re calling. Yes, it feels like they’re calling.”

‘They.’

Words spoken with the five senses could become entirely new at the level of the eleven senses…

“Alright. I need to see for myself.”

If Kalt had returned safely, there was no reason he couldn’t go.

“Come this way.”

Kalt pressed his temples, and blue electricity began to swirl in his eyes.

After a moment, he stuck out his tongue, revealing a thin, rectangular metal plate.

“What’s that?”

The Arachne officials murmured, but Shirone wasn’t particularly surprised.

‘Living beings can’t create machines. Therefore, quantum phenomena. Maybe Kalt…’

Had already become an OOPArts?

“Messiah, this is dangerous. Please don’t implant foreign objects in your body.”

“It’s fine. It’s just a material made with the five senses. I can control it.”

Shirone examined the metal plate Kalt handed him.

“What do I do now?”

“I don’t know. Just… stick it in your head? That’s what I did with my subordinates.”

Shirone pressed the chip behind his ear.

‘It won’t go in.’

It seemed he had to pierce the bone, but could that be called a function?

“It’s okay. Trust me.”

Kalt’s hazy eyes didn’t inspire confidence, but Shirone applied pressure with his fingers.

“Hup!”

Did he really pierce the bone?

That didn’t happen, but electricity flowed through the metal plate, invading Shirone’s mind.

“Gasp!”

Shirone’s eyes flashed blue, and a magnetic field enveloped him.

“Messiah!”

Kaos and Poine, who had leaped forward, felt a familiar shock and grimaced.

“Grrr!” It felt like their brains were burning.

If it progressed further, their memories would be damaged, but the two apostles reached out until the end.

“Me…ssi…ah…”

Just before Kaos’s hand, wrapped in electricity, touched Shirone, Kalt muttered.

“Meet them.”

“Ahhh!”

As the two apostles risked their lives, Shirone’s body vanished in an instant.

“Where? Where did he go?”

Kaos, eyes tightly shut, pointed his sword at Kalt.

"First and last chance. Where did the Messiah go? If you don't want to die, you'd better tell me."

"I don't know."

Kalt had a somewhat sullen expression.

"Perhaps... a terrible truth."

The ultra-ancient civilization of Maika.

"Gasp! Gasp!"

Shirone waited until the blue tint enveloping his retinas disappeared.

As his vision cleared, he looked around.

"Maika."

He was certain because it was unlike any place in Omega.

The city, built in terraces, had numerous buildings like a real civilization.

'It's not a civilization.' But upon entering the buildings, there were no traces of anyone having lived there.

'This is... a kind of device.'

The entire city felt like a machine built for a single purpose.

At the center, two horn-like curved towers stood 20 meters high.

"...I don't understand."

The towers were inscribed with characters, but none could be deciphered.

'The Ultima System doesn't work.' Faced with the definitive evidence that these signals didn't exist in the universe, Shirone shuddered.

'If there is an outside world...'

The closest imagination would be a higher layer implemented based on the mechanism of the void.

'But that doesn't matter.'

No matter how far you descend from reality, the end of imagination is still reality.

'No one knows until they go there themselves.'

Shirone took a deep breath.

'Focus. What's there isn't important. What's truly important is...

Why does it exist?

"Huh?"

Like a distant memory, the city began to shake, and electricity flowed through the ground.

"Ugh!"

As he felt the current and gritted his teeth, an opaque barrier formed between the two towers.

Inside the barrier, a giant eye was staring at Shirone.
