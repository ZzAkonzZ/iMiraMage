Chapter 330: Philosophy of a Combat Mage (4)

"Red Eyes. In other words, your photographic memory corrects errors in the shortest time. It's an incredible ability. In terms of talent alone, you're not just the best at Alpheas Magic School, but on the entire continent. The countless geniuses from your family prove that."

Amy couldn't argue.

Even her brothers from the main Karmis family had already made names for themselves in their fields at a young age.

"So what? I've never once thought of relying on my family! Are you saying that the only reason I've come this far is because of my talent? I've always tried to stand on my own."

"Yes. And that's exactly what I mean by childish."

Amy's face hardened.

"Instead, rely on your family. Use everything you have, everything your family has. You were born with things others can only dream of having, and you throw them away just because you want to do it on your own? That's what I mean by childish."

"I... never thought of it that way..."

"Remember the kids we met at the skating rink? Jokre, was it? Eins Magic School isn't exactly prestigious. Their talents are far below yours. Yet they find ways to survive and become mages. You think you failed because you were unlucky? No, Amy. You're competing at one of the top five prestigious schools in the kingdom. There are countless people more unfortunate than you. If you want to be a mage, especially the best mage, you have to fight and win no matter what."

Amy lowered her head.

Her anger had already dissipated.

No, it felt like everything had returned to square one.

Dante was right.

She was at Alpheas Magic School, where the kingdom's top talents gathered, and she was in the graduating class at the pinnacle.

There was no room for excuses.

In a battlefield where only the best clashed, any complaints would just sound like the whining of the privileged.

"So what should I do..."

She was back at the starting point, unable to overcome the wall.

The second time would be faster.

But that was just a thought in her head.

Now, she had to overcome herself. And that was Amy's first real trial.

'Amy...'

Shirone looked at Amy with pity.

Dante's blunt words must have been a huge shock to her. But he couldn't intervene.

It wasn't out of arrogance, thinking he could help Amy.

It was because Dante's words were true.

Silence fell over the table.

The laughter of tourists chatting around them didn't reach their ears.

Dante drank his cold coffee and watched Amy.

He didn't have any special affection for her. But she had been the most noticeable during the graduation exam.

"Do you want to know why you failed?"

Amy looked up again.

Shirone, lost in thought, also looked at Dante.

Given Dante's expertise in information processing, his analysis would be worth listening to, regardless of the situation.

"Of course, the only time I saw you use magic was during the graduation exam. And that was less than five minutes. But even in that short time, there was something I couldn't understand."

"Are you saying I made a wrong decision?"

"No. It's a more fundamental issue. To get straight to the point, you have a fatal flaw. Especially if you aim to be a combat mage, there's something crucial you're missing."

Amy had never thought she had a weakness. So she swallowed hard, waiting for his next words.

Dante turned his head, holding his coffee cup. After a moment, he pursed his lips and spoke.

"You... lack style."

"Style?"

Amy asked, surprised by the unexpected word. But Shirone nodded slightly.

Lacking style.

It encapsulated the vague sense of incongruity he had felt while being with Amy.

The fact that Dante, with the kingdom's best defensive skills, had pinpointed Amy's flaw after just five minutes of combat was impressive.

Shirone added a comment.

"To put it nicely, you're versatile. I've always felt that you're too amazing for your own good."

Amy's face flushed. The embarrassment was greater because she was still reeling from Dante's blunt words.

"Are you teasing me?"

Dante shook his head.

"No, Shirone has a point. From what I've seen, you have a tendency to try to control every situation perfectly. Because you can."

"Why is that a problem?"

"It's a big problem. Your specialty is sniper mode. Most mages guide situations to environments where their specialties can shine. That's where style comes from. Shirone likes strategies that overpower with brute force because of his Immortal Function. I like counters because I enjoy analyzing opponents. But you're different. If sniper mode doesn't work, you immediately look for a second solution."

Amy felt like she had been hit with a hammer.

"Do you know what's really serious? You actually find that second solution. You're too resourceful. But how long can that approach work?"

Amy's lips trembled.

Stupid. Foolish.

Why had she never thought of this before?

"Most students would hit a wall. But your talent got you through to the graduating class. Then, at the most critical moment, you hit that wall."

Dante clapped his hands together.

"That's when all the participants targeted you. For the first time, you faced a situation where talent alone wasn't enough, in a difficulty spike far beyond the graduating class lessons. Fermi must have known your fatal flaw. That's why you were chosen as the sacrifice."

Dante recalled the graduation exam and gave an example.

"When you felt the tide turning, you abandoned Samuel and targeted Fermi. You thought you could gain the upper hand in close combat with Skima. But that's where it went wrong. The moment you felt the incongruity, you should have created distance. You should have led the situation to specialize in sniper mode. But you didn't."

Shirone chimed in.

"Because you were confident you could handle it somehow."

Dante nodded.

"It's impossible to control every situation in a melee. Even one second is too long for decision-making in combat. If you have multiple options, there's bound to be a delay. That's why style is important. You have to trust your style and bet everything on it."

Amy recalled being trapped in Zenogor's web in Kazra.

She had many thoughts at the time.

But if she had focused solely on what she could do, she could have melted the web with Ignite and escaped.

"You probably analyzed everything in the graduating class. The combat power, judgment, habits, and actions of all the participants. You analyzed everything and decided you had the advantage, right?"

Amy admitted it honestly.

"Yes. Exactly."

"But you didn't consider style. It's as important as combat power. If the compatibility is bad, you need to know when to avoid and wait. If I went back in time and fought Shirone from then, I'd probably win 90% of the time."

Shirone nodded in agreement.

There's no "if" in history, but if his past self fought the current Dante, he would likely lose most of the time.

"Combat isn't just about skill. To put it extremely, Shirone's Ataraxia can be destroyed before it's completed. But why didn't I succeed? Because Shirone constantly guided the situation to his advantage. It's a clash of styles. That's what a mage’s combat is."

Amy remained motionless with her head down.

Her mind was filled with thoughts about magic, and nothing else registered.

Dante spoke.

"Well, I might fail in a similar situation. But I wouldn't be eliminated early without doing anything like you. Anyway, that's my analysis of you from the graduation exam."

Amy finally looked up. Her expression was still sad, but the excitement had faded.

"Thank you for being honest."

No matter the reason, it was kind of Dante to analyze her strengths and weaknesses, even though they would be competitors next year.

Feeling awkward in the solemn atmosphere, Dante wrapped up the conversation and stood up.

"I feel like I brought up an unnecessary topic during your date. Anyway, I'll get going. Have fun. See you at school."

Shirone saw him off to the door.

Outside the café, Dante looked at Amy through the window.

Seeing her dejected expression, he scratched his head and turned to Shirone.

"Sorry. It seemed like you were having a good time, and I ruined it by butting in."

Having had his fair share of relationships at the Royal Magic School, Dante sensed the subtle tension between Shirone and Amy.

The fact that Amy had come on this trip with Shirone meant she had some thoughts about it.

If the atmosphere from the skating rink had continued, Shirone and Amy's relationship might have progressed.

But today's advice had complicated Amy's thoughts.

As a fellow man, it was natural for Dante to feel sorry.

But Shirone didn't see it that way. He was grateful that Dante had given Amy valuable advice.

"It's okay. Without you, we wouldn't have identified Amy's problem. You were a big help. Of course, Amy has to find the solution herself."

Dante smiled wryly.

Shirone seemed untouched by worldly concerns.

In a way, Amy was similar to Shirone, so they were well-matched.

Dante had always had instant relationships. It suited his personality, so he had no complaints.

But he thought it might be fun to slowly build emotions like a caterpillar.

"I didn't give advice just for Amy. Fermi's group will still be around next year. The more rivals, the better. Anyway, see you later. Have fun."

Dante waved and walked away.

Watching him leave as lightly as he had come, Shirone felt a sense of ease and freedom from the traveler.

'May you have a good journey too.'

A Winter Night (1)

In winter, the sun sets early.

Since the old castle ruins closed to tourists at 6 PM, Dante decided to end his sightseeing and head to his lodging.

'It's too early to just go in. Maybe I'll have a beer?'

At eighteen, he could legally buy alcohol.

Dante preferred sipping beer over stronger drinks, especially while traveling.

The shopping district was bustling with people leaving the tourist area.

As he wandered, looking for a comfortable place to drink, he noticed a young woman protesting in front of a bar on the third floor.

Her striking outfit caught Dante's eye.

She wore a loose-fitting garment with sleeves extending to her wrists, cinched tightly at the waist with a white belt. Her outer garment, which reached her ankles, was sharply tailored, and her skirt was red.

Dante guessed she was involved in some kind of ritual.

The large backpack, which didn't match her slender frame, suggested she had been traveling for a long time or had come from far away.

She looked to be in her mid-twenties.

Her skin was as white as if it had never seen sunlight, and she had a delicate appearance. Her black hair, tied high at the crown, cascaded down to her waist.
