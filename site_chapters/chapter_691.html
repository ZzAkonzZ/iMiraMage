Chapter 691: The Pupil of God (3)

"That guy is done for. He has no skills but still acts all high and mighty. If it were me..."

The voices of hearts accumulating simultaneously across the world were classified at an incredible speed according to the standards of the Great Judge Teraphose.

Good: 1

Evil: 278,687

The Republic of North Almond.

"It's mine! I’ll take everything for myself!"

"Crush them mercilessly! If they’ve done wrong, they must pay the price! My fault? …What do you even know about me!"

Good: 12

Evil: 4,562,479

The Kashan Empire.

"I’m a good person. There’s no way I’m not good. So all of you are the bad ones!"

"Why won’t you look at me? I’m the cooler one! I’m the best! You’re all idiots for not realizing that!"

Good: 376

Evil: 67,285,541

The Mediterranean.

"Perish. Perish. Perish. Perish."

"What I think is the only correct answer. Because I’m smart. You’re all stupid."

Good: 1,225

Evil: 128,546,699

The Eastern Zhongtiandong, the Mountain Range of the Capital’s Monks.

"By abandoning myself with all my heart..."

"The courage to resist evil is not about fighting to win but immersing myself in suffering."

Good: 21,318

Evil: 1,589,746,587

The Gustav Empire.

"Make a mistake. So I can mock you, so I can feel at ease. Oh God, let that fool make a mistake."

"I’ll make you suffer. But I don’t care. It’s not me who’s in pain."

The judgment of all humanity concluded, and the final score appeared in Maylay’s mind.

Good: 48,893

Evil: 3,159,963,587

‘It’s enough.’

Those who passed the standards of "good" didn’t even make up 1% of the total population, but the choice was still theirs.

Are humans evil beings?

If so, how would one explain the 48,893 humans still remaining in this world?

As long as even a single good person remains, humanity cannot be defined as purely good or evil.

Rather, as beings who choose between good and evil themselves, they will endlessly strive for the future of humanity.

"Humans are not yet ready for extinction. By the judgment of this Great Judge, I shall erase the incident of La Enemi."

The incarnation of the Great Compassionate One changed gestures and swung its arms wide, causing an immense number of light sources to overlap.

Teraphose Incarnation Art—Compassion’s Demon Extinction.

A clear and pure sound, inaudible to human ears, spread across the land.

The law that weakens the power of evil by borrowing the power of good was delivered to the entire world through the mighty incarnation of Teraphose.

The balance to be restored at a ratio of 50,000 to 3.1 billion was not large, but it was powerful enough to erase the incident of La Enemi.

"Ah..."

Shagal stared blankly at the sky.

The horrific memories that oscillated between reality and illusion gradually vanished, leaving only the original memories behind.

There were no new memories to fill the void left by the erased ones—only the time spent wandering in darkness remained.

"Uwaaaah! Uwaaaah!"

Watching Shagal scream madly in frustration and resentment, Shirone grasped the severity of the situation.

‘This is the aftermath Maylay mentioned.’

Likely, people all over the world were reacting the same way as Shagal.

As the memories of La Enemi disappeared, the "Kahr" value he had acquired through human experiences also began to drop, albeit slightly.

The army of the Reverse World ceased fighting, and Igor and Varshiba, who had been locked in fierce combat, distanced themselves from each other.

"What... just happened?"

"Varshiba, the masters of this world are none other than us. If we lose control, we can protect nothing."

La Enemi had promised Varshiba the independence of the Reverse World, but even that memory was now gone.

Igor turned to Shirone and spoke.

"We shall return. Your affairs are yours to resolve. Today, we fought together, but next time..."

Igor left his words unfinished.

The beings of the Reverse World and the humans of reality were fated to never coexist from the beginning. If such an incident were to repeat, war would be inevitable.

With the retreat of the Reverse World’s army, all that remained were the gruesome corpses and Shirone’s group, covered in blood.

Maylay, having concluded the incarnation art, descended to the ground.

"I have erased the incident of La Enemi. As the representative of humanity, you bear the responsibility for this matter."

"...You’re quick to say that."

He had already prepared himself for it.

"But why do I still remember?"

"Compassion’s Demon Extinction erases all that is controlled by evil. If you were not controlled, your memories remain intact."

"I see."

As the incident of La Enemi vanished, the scenery of the Reverse World slowly faded.

The brain in the vessel was released, and Maylay, having returned to her girlish form, covered one ear.

Through the divine frequency, the voice of La Enemi reached her.

-Find me, Hexa.

La Enemi would not reveal himself to the world until he reached 100% Kahr.

-I will be where you seek.

The voice faded, and Shirone checked the status of the survivors.

Venetia was dead, and Shagal was nowhere to be seen, having likely already left.

As Etela, Kuan, Rian, and Rufist approached Shirone, Maylay spoke.

"I shall return. I do not know what future awaits you, but I hope you make the right choices."

Rufist interjected.

"Why not stay a little longer? It’s not like La Enemi is completely gone yet."

"Teraphose protects the order of the universe through sound. La Enemi manipulated cosmic events, disregarding that order, which is why I intervened directly. Physical interference like this won’t happen again unless another cosmic-scale incident occurs."

"Hmm."

Rufist neither agreed nor disagreed.

Though he longed to exploit Teraphose for the Kingdom of Tormia, they were far too dangerous to handle.

"Hexa, Teraphose has acknowledged you as humanity’s representative. I leave the rest to you. I will contact you if necessary."

As her words ended, Maylay’s pupils returned to those of a normal human.

"Huh? Where is this?"

Her clueless expression left Shirone’s group staring blankly at her.

"You remember nothing?"

"I think I was blown away by the explosion while defending against the bio-energy beam..."

‘She was replaced since then. We were completely fooled.’

Though it would likely remain classified, Maylay, as the only psychic capable of communicating with Teraphose, would be recruited by the kingdom.

Etela spoke up.

"What do we do now? The memories of La Enemi are gone, but he still lingers in this world in a human body."

Kido replied.

"Do we even need to find him? There’s no point in him manipulating events anymore."

The method La Enemi used to collect experiences was on a scale impossible without design from ancient times.

Shirone added.

"But his ability to manipulate sensory memories is still useful. La Enemi might find another way to use it."

"You’re really planning to find him?"

Rufist asked skeptically.

"Didn’t this incident teach you anything? Carelessly, you could turn La Enemi into a colossal disaster. Humanity would be in danger."

"We still have to find him."

"Why?"

"Because of the Ivory Tower test."

It wasn’t about passing the test.

"My thoughts align with the Ivory Tower. Even if humanity is endangered, we must fight to the end and drive La Enemi out."

‘Kahr...’

At any rate, Shirone wasn’t part of the Magic Association, so Rufist couldn’t stop him.

"Do as you please. I’m heading to the Bio Lab. There might still be seeds to recover. I’ll handle the mercenary group matter."

"I’m sorry about Jane."

"...She wasn’t the only one who died. I’ll be in touch."

As Rufist flew away, Maylay also bowed to Shirone.

"I’ll follow the Association Head. Please visit me if you ever become a resident of the Ivory Tower."

As long as Maylay received Teraphose’s oracles, this wouldn’t be their final meeting.

"Sure. Take care, and see you next time."

As Shirone bid farewell, Kuan limped toward Rian.

"Hey."

The moment Rian turned his head, Kuan drew his sword like lightning and swung it vertically.

The blade passed between Rian’s eyebrows with a sharp sound.

"Got it?"

"...Yes."

The afterimage of the flash, containing countless variations in a single strike, lingered in his vision.

Though it was a technique impossible to mimic, Rian understood what Kuan meant.

"Practice."

It was a gift from his former instructor.

"Ah, thank you."

Kuan didn’t even reply and turned away. Etela placed a hand on Shirone’s shoulder to say goodbye.

"Take care, Shirone."

"You’re leaving? What about the payment? I haven’t even paid you yet."

Etela smiled and shook his head.

"I didn’t come for the money. I’ve engraved my teacher’s will in my heart, so it’s fine."

"Are you returning to the school?"

"For now. But... I’m not sure yet."

His hatred for Shagal still lingered, and he doubted he could teach students in this state.

As everyone left, only Rian and Kido remained by Shirone’s side.

After an awkward silence, Rian turned to Kido.

"Aren’t you leaving?"

"Huh? Me? I should..."

For humans, the whole world was their stage, but Kido, as a goblin, would always be a non-human wherever he went.

"If you don’t have a destination, want to come with us?"

Kido’s ears perked up at Shirone’s offer, but his pride wouldn’t let him accept so easily.

"Hmph, what would I do following humans around...?"

"But you can’t hide forever. Come with us. It’ll be fun."

"R-Really?"

Rian also agreed readily.

"Yeah, let’s fight La Enemi together. The more strong allies, the better."

Kido liked the sound of being called a "strong ally."

"Kihihihi! Fine! Then I, in my greatness, shall lend you humans my power!"

Kido struck a pose, brandishing his spear.

"By the way..."

Though their mission in Radum had concluded, a heavy emotion still weighed on their hearts.

"Shirone, what is this world?"

Kido’s playful expression faded as he recalled how Anke Ra came to be.

"I don’t want to be certain. But perhaps we..."

Shirone looked up at the sky and said:

"Are living in someone’s dream."

***

The Kashan Empire.

Wu Orin woke up in a secret chamber of the imperial palace, Agnos, known only to her closest confidants.

Feeling the cool touch of the waterbed, she turned her head to see Gando, biologically male but spiritually her son, reading a book.

"Are you awake?"

Gando handed her a glass of water, and Wu Orin stretched.

"How long was I out?"

"Roughly..."

Gando regretted using the word "roughly."

"One hour and forty-two minutes."

"Hmm, that’s quite long."

After drinking, Wu Orin slipped on her slippers and walked past Gando.

"You’ve been spending a lot of time in the Under Code lately."

Wu Orin had been frequenting the Under Code recently.

"It’s fun. I’m getting stronger quickly."

The program she linked to was "High Gear," a virtual reality world created by professional coders.

One of the most popular paid contents in the Under Code, High Gear required a "Dream Star" pill to enter, each costing over 2 million gold.

There, users could modify their bodies at will, with some even undergoing 100% mechanization.

"Is modifying your body fun?"

Gando, who had never entered the Under Code, found the idea of body modification masochistic.

"The stronger you get, the more competitive it becomes."

Wu Orin pointed to her ear, grumbling.

"I need to upgrade the low-frequency detection function. Stealth mode is so annoying."

She wanted to buy it outright with real gold, but High Gear’s operators strictly prohibited cash transactions.

"Then again, if I bought everything, I’d get bored quickly."

Gando, watching her immersed in the game, asked worriedly:

"...What do you do there?"

"Just chat with people, share item or modification info, fight against opposing factions, stuff like that."

Wu Orin suddenly remembered something.

"Oh right, I leveled up this time. Level 260. That means I can equip a Magnan."

Gando didn’t know what a Magnan was.

"Is that so?"

"Breaking into the top 10 rankings will be tough, though. The top 10 are absolute monsters. Especially ‘White Snake’—their control is so mesmerizing it’s hard to look away."

Wu Orin’s current ranking in High Gear was 17th, with the username "Little Witch."

Pointing at Gando excitedly, she asked:

"Want to join me? I’ll support you fully. Our faction is short on attackers anyway."

It was the first time Wu Orin had invited him to do something together.

Though it should have been touching, Gando only felt bitterness.

‘Because it’s fake.’

In reality, there was nothing he could share with Wu Orin.

"I appreciate the offer, but I’ll decline. I’ll support Your Majesty in the real world."

Wu Orin blinked.

"Huh? The real world?"

Reading her thoughts, Gando added:

"Though even this ‘real world’ might just be someone’s dream—all fake."

Lost in thought, Wu Orin smiled and shook her head.

"No, that’s not it, Gando. You mustn’t think this world might be fake."

"Then...?"

Wu Orin raised a finger.

"The correct answer is to think: ‘This world is fake.’"

"Fake...?"

Wu Orin, amused by Gando’s reaction, sat on the sofa and crossed her legs.

"Linking to High Gear lets me admire technology far beyond our world’s, but not all of it was created by coders. They borrow countless modules drifting in the Dream Sea."

Such technologies couldn’t be applied in Wu Orin’s world.

"So while it might seem crude, I don’t notice the difference. Because of the brain in the vessel."

The brain only perceives what it accepts as real.

"But High Gear has an interesting system. Before modifying your body, you can preview the changes on screen."

Wu Orin tapped her head.

"It’s called an AI simulation. A clone with all my info appears and shows how I’d react in various situations."

Gando just listened.

"It’s so precise, I can’t tell if it’s the screen’s me or the real me. The problem is this: if I hand control to the screen’s me and order it to create a new AI simulation, what happens?"

Wu Orin spread her arms wide.

"Virtual worlds are created endlessly. The beings in those worlds would live without knowing they’re just programs. With billions of worlds already made..."

Wu Orin asked:

"What’s the probability that this place is the real world—the origin of everything?"

Gando couldn’t answer.

"Almost zero. It’s more rational to think we’re just a midpoint in an endlessly nested system. ‘Might be fake’? No. This world is 99% likely to be fake."

Seeing Gando’s stunned face, Wu Orin chuckled.

"Of course, there’s a tiny chance this is real. Believing that is fine too. But I propose a different approach."

"What is it?"

Wu Orin smirked and pointed at the bed with her thumb.

"Stop overthinking and just join me in High Gear."

***

Shirone’s group quickly escaped the concealed facility area.

Occasionally, remnants of the Spectrum blocked their path, but they were no match.

Entering the slums of Radum, starving residents prowled like wild dogs, but the sight relaxed the group, and they continued on foot.

Watching the emaciated people rummage through trash, Kido asked again:

"Hearing you talk like this makes me feel hollow. I thought living in Speed Killer was better than these humans. But if this world is just a dream..."

"It’s not, Kido."

Shirone spoke with certainty.

"No matter what world this is, what matters is that I exist."

Breathing, living—without food, he would die.

"To me, this world is everything. Even if something exists beyond it, that’s not the end either."

"Hmm, I guess? Overthinking seems pointless anyway."

Rian added:

"We survived the battle. We’ll keep surviving. Everything we love is here."

Even their lives belonged to this world.

"Right. That’s why we’ll stop La Enemi. If Anke Ra becomes a god, all the standards we hold dear will collapse."

"Fight to protect those standards."

Kido laughed, resting his spear on his shoulder.

"Sounds grand, whatever it means!"

***

The core members of Shirone’s mercenary group returned to Brooks’ mansion after the mission.

With Jane dead, an Association employee sent by Rufist was waiting to collect the Nemesis rings.

However, the one Nemesis taken by Shagal never returned.

Two days later, Kargin and Joshua, who had fled Radum, visited Brooks’ mercenary agency.

At first, they worried about their future as mercenaries, but upon hearing they were among the few survivors, they were grateful for their cowardice.

After signing the mission failure contract, Kargin felt unexpectedly relieved as he left.

"Haah, it’s like I’ve been reborn."

"What nonsense are you spouting, old man? You’ll be in a coffin soon."

Even Joshua’s sharp joke couldn’t dampen his mood.

"Keh keh! True enough. But this feeling is new to me."

Joshua felt the same sprout of emotion.

"I’ll go this way. Contact me if you need money. Fellow strays should stick together."

They’d need to work hard to pay the penalty.

"I meant what I said."

Kargin looked at him seriously.

"Won’t you come with me? Without you, I wouldn’t have this life."

"Drop it. We made it back alive, didn’t we?"

He hadn’t expected the emotions from Radum to linger.

"Take care."

Kargin grabbed Joshua’s wrist as he turned to leave.

"I love you."

With a look that could melt into Joshua’s trembling eyes, he added:

"Let’s have a child. A beautiful child who looks like you."

Tears streamed down Joshua’s cheeks.

"Really... gone senile? Why would I...?"

Nothing mattered more than living in this world.

"Waaah!"

Joshua threw himself into Kargin’s open arms.

"Disgusting. How much debt do they even have?"

Watching from the mansion window, Brooks grumbled.

‘They deserve to be kicked out...’

At Shirone’s request as the captain, their records showed "Mission Failure" instead of "Desertion."

Grieving Venetia’s death, Brooks wanted to impose a hefty penalty, but Rufist had insisted the mercenary group’s affairs were entirely Shirone’s call.

Especially since Rufist had been in a foul mood—any opposition would’ve cost him his head.

"Boss, Aria’s awake."

At his subordinate’s words, Brooks turned from the window and headed to her room.

Aria, having regained some color after treatment, was preparing to leave.

"Leaving already? Rest a few more days."

"I’ve rested enough. I need to report to the royal palace."

Though Rufist likely handled it, she still had duties.

Despite the doctor’s orders for a week’s rest, her demeanor was flawless as she donned her coat.

‘What a remarkable woman.’

Who’d have thought their reckless fling would ignite real feelings?

"Thank you. I’ll be in touch."

As Aria headed for the door, Brooks blocked her path.
