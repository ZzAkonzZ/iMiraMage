Chapter 339: Wave of Laws (4)

The Ice Queen made a wide turn, but she couldn't widen the angle faster than Shirone, who was rotating at the center. As the first shot hit, a series of shockwaves followed. The photon cannons that had been trailing behind all fired at once, and a thin scream erupted from the Ice Queen's chest.

"Kyyaaaah!"

But she wasn't destroyed. Shirone’s wrist, which was continuously firing, was about to break.

'Ugh! Why isn't she falling?'

Cold air began to spread from the Ice Queen's body, which had been pummeled by the photon cannons. The expansion accelerated over time, eventually forming a massive cloud that obscured the Queen's body.

Remaining oxygen consumption time: 1 minute 2 seconds. 48 seconds. 35 seconds.

The oxygen Armand had stored was rapidly depleting. When it reached zero, he would have to breathe the ultra-cold air. Desperately regulating his breathing, the remaining consumption time stabilized at 4 seconds, and air began to be stored again.

'What's happening? Did I win?'

He had fired photon cannons at a rate of 120 rounds per minute. A normal creature would have been turned into mashed potatoes.

As the mist that had gathered like a cloud dispersed, the Ice Queen finally revealed herself.

'What, what is that...?'

Shirone gaped in astonishment. His pupils shook violently with a look of shock.

Amy's red eyes flashed as she saved the coordinates. Dante and Liria followed her. They had already run over a kilometer. However, due to the nature of the circular maze, they hadn't approached within 100 meters of the center.

As they turned into the shopping district, they saw buildings cut in half by the rising walls. Amy's group entered a two-story house with an exposed structure and hid.

Dante looked out the window. Besides Jocre's group, monsters were roaming all areas.

"We can't keep running. The monsters won't tire. We have to take them down and get out."

Liria shook her head.

"They're victims too. We can't kill them."

"And if we die? The Ice Queen will only get stronger over time. We need to help Shirone quickly. But we're stuck at the monster stage, let alone destroying the maze."

"It's not about efficiency or results. Violence without the will of good is no different from the methods of evil. I'm not forcing you, but if you plan to help me, we must not harm innocent people, even if we die."

Dante smiled bitterly. It wasn't pretense or a lie. Strengthening one's will to control reason was only possible with unwavering conviction.

"Is that the mindset of a mage? Well, we have a naive teacher like that at our school too. Etella."

Liria spoke firmly, as if it were absurd.

"It's not naivety, it's conviction. The world is maintained because there are those who uphold the will of good as much as evil rises. If we follow the methods of evil just because the situation is urgent, we will eventually be ruled by evil! I don't expect you, a mage, to understand the long-standing conflict between good and evil, but...!"

Dante waved his hand to interrupt.

"Alright, enough lecturing. I'm a mage, as you said. So, what's the plan?"

Liria, calming down, explained.

"We'll reduce the range of the wide-area sealing formation set up at the castle ruins to increase its power. It's a technique called Pakyojin. If we get the coordinates right, even a demigod's law can't escape."

Dante's expression turned serious.

"But that means if we fail..."

"Yes, even the last resort, the sealing formation, will be destroyed. The Ice Queen will become even stronger if she escapes into the world. So we only have one chance. If we can't create that opportunity, I won't attempt Pakyojin, even if it means my death."

Dante agreed. If the Ice Queen escaped the sealing formation, the damage would be uncontrollable. It was more efficient to keep her contained, even if everyone here perished.

"Alright. So what do we need to do?"

"The coordinates for Pakyojin can't be set arbitrarily. It must be placed at the point where the power of the sealing formation is concentrated."

"Where is that?"

"It's the apex of the inverted pyramid drawn from the sealing formation. Take me there."

"Another mountain to climb."

Finding the installation point for Pakyojin in a maze filled with monsters was nearly impossible. At the very least, the maze needed to disappear to start the operation.

"Alright, let's go. Liria and I will handle the monsters, and Amy, you take down the maze. You're the only one with the red eyes who can reach the center of the Radius of the Spirit."

Liria asked worriedly.

"Will she be alright alone? Their abilities aren't magic. The other two must have gained abilities comparable to the maze."

"That's why Amy has to go. They'll likely target her first. It's better to split up. Amy, can you do it?"

Amy couldn't answer. Jocre's group had grown stronger. Even before that, they were skilled enough to pass the graduation exam.

'And I... failed.'

Dante spoke.

"You can do it. Believe in yourself."

"I can't. I really have no confidence."

Amy was sincere.

"If you can't believe in yourself, believe in your family."

When Amy looked up, Dante placed a hand on her shoulder.

"If your whole family can do it, there's no way you can't. Use everything you have to fight."

Many family members had proven their talents. That's why she could believe in herself in any situation.

With a determined look in her eyes, Amy followed Dante out of the building.

"Let's go, Liria. While they focus on Amy, we need to get as close to the coordinates as possible."

Liria asked as she followed Dante.

"Will she be alright? She doesn't seem very confident."

"She has her reasons. But she'll be fine. She might be struggling now, but..."

Dante smiled.

"She's not foolish enough to not know who's on top."

"Hohoho! The way you're running is priceless! Truly worthy of a prestigious school student!"

"Tch! Shut up!"

Vivian popped out of the walls at every turn Amy took, mocking her. Despite her best efforts to find a way, she was always in Vivian's grasp.

The wall in Amy's path suddenly opened, and Ludvance appeared, chewing gum.

"Wrath of God."

As he slammed an invisible shock orb into the ground, a shockwave with electrical and elastic force lifted Amy's body.

"Your panties are showing."

As Amy landed with a somersault, Jocre approached rapidly from the side.

"Speed Gear!"

The movement of the Speed Gear, which adjusted inertia from level 1 to 10, completely defied the laws of physics. As common sense was shattered, it felt like space-time itself was twisting.

Amy hastily deployed an air shield. Jocre's fireball exploded, pushing her body far away.

'I can't find Vivian like this. I need to get above the walls somehow.'

In the air, she might be able to use the air tunnel to directly strike Vivian's main body. But it wasn't easy to get such an opportunity against Jocre's group.

'The ground is blocked by walls. Is sniper mode impossible? Maybe if I injure one of them to tie them down...'

Amy shook her head. She was overthinking again. Dante's advice flashed through her mind.

-You have no style.

'Style. My style.'

There's no way to handle every situation. Amy focused solely on sniper mode. At the same time, her eyes widened. A core idea struck her mind like lightning.

'Maybe... No, I can do it. I can do it.'

Examining the structure of the walls, Amy set arbitrary points A and B and drew an imaginary string. Then, using the curvature of the walls, she calculated the radian value and combined it with trigonometry to derive the central coordinates where Vivian's main body was located.

'I have the coordinates. But to strike...'

Sniper mode alone wasn't enough. In a situation where all sides were blocked by walls, the flames couldn't reach.

But Amy didn't give up. Like Shirone and Dante, she deepened her strategy.

'It's possible. As long as it's heavier than air...'

Amy's red eyes flashed like electricity.

'More, more power is needed.'

Power. Power. Power. Power. Power.

Her eidetic memory began to eliminate distractions by backing up hundreds of frames per second.

Extreme self-censorship.

Her mind penetrated with a single purpose, in a state free of even 1% impurity.

Focus on focus.

Her mental power amplified exponentially, as if an explosion had occurred in her mind.

One path.

A temporary state of samadhi, occurring when focusing solely on one thought without any distractions.

'Inferno.'

Two streams of fire twisted like braids from under Amy's legs and rose above her head.

"What, what is that?"

Jocre's group stopped their charge and looked on in shock at the size of the flames Amy had created. The gas density was so thick that the flames dripped like paint.

"That's..."

Jocre, who specialized in fire magic, realized what Amy was attempting.

The thousand-degree fire that even professional mages struggled to achieve.

Judging by the color of the flames, they were burning at a minimum of 1,300 degrees above her head.

"You, you... that..."

Jocre's group didn't dare approach. If the Inferno exploded, everything within the radius would melt.

Amy turned towards the wall and spoke.

"Yeah. You can't even imitate this."

Jocre frowned in confusion. He couldn't understand why she was aiming at something unrelated after casting a spell to break the situation. But Vivian realized immediately. Despite her petrified face, her expression was clearly one of shock.

"No! Jocre, stop her!"

Amy raised her hands, supporting the massive flames.

"Stop her! She's aiming to attack the main body!"

Jocre didn't understand Vivian's words. How could she attack the main body when it was blocked by a 30-meter-high wall?

Amy smiled triumphantly.

"This is my style."

Bending her upper body like a bow, she swung her hands down while snapping her waist. The thousand-degree Inferno flames soared over the 30-meter-high wall and flew into the sky.

Ludvance muttered in a daze.

"Mortar fire..."

The fireball, boasting an immense gas density, arced across the sky.

While mortar fire can ignore cover, it has the drawback of lower accuracy. However, Amy could precisely target the coordinates with machine-like precision thanks to her eidetic memory.

"Kyyaaaah!"

Vivian's clone screamed and disappeared. If the Inferno fell into the center of the maze, she would melt instantly, unable to move in her petrified state.

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

The walls within a 1-kilometer radius simultaneously collapsed into the ground, and Vivian, freed from her petrified state, fled in a hurry, continuing the screams of her clone.

When the Inferno exploded upon reaching the ground, the surface melted like molten metal. The glowing red lava, with dark impurities floating on it, quickly cooled in the cold air.

The terrain before and after the Inferno's fall was completely different. The ground was grotesquely distorted, like the face of a demon.

Vivian, who narrowly escaped death, reached Jocre. The maze had disappeared, but Amy was nowhere to be seen. As Jocre looked around with an annoyed expression, he muttered.

"Damn it! Where did she hide?"

A dull thud sounded from where Ludvance was. A burning stone struck the back of his head, and he fell without even a groan.
