Chapter 622:

"What is that?"

Baikal asked, and Elizabeth answered.

"It's the Ultima System. According to the report…"

"I know what it is. The problem is, how can there be multiple focal points of concentration?"

Because it's the Ultima System.

While it was something that could be accepted without understanding, it was a perplexing situation for a scout who had to analyze everything.

"The system was modified to maximize the impact. Only number 27 could do that."

'He did it. He really did it.'

Shirone, who had annihilated the enemies within the time limit, collapsed to his knees.

'I need to recover my mind.'

Just as he thought that, a shocking announcement was heard.

-Entering the 6th stage of endurance survival.

"What?"

Not only Shirone but all the participants who had been off guard widened their eyes and looked around.

"Heh, it won't be that easy."

Fermi was standing with the werewolf's neck in his hand where the voice came from.

Growl…

If even one creature survived, it was considered a failure to annihilate, and they would immediately proceed to the next stage.

Roar!

The werewolf's neck was cut off by Fermi's air cut.

"Fermi…"

Shirone gritted his teeth and stood up.

Was this how it felt to realize that the oasis in the desert was actually a mirage?

"Good judgment."

Baikal liked Fermi's strategy.

"He understands the endurance survival system well. It's a strategy to weaken Shirone's power while still using his combat strength."

"A cold disposition."

Baikal's gaze returned to Elizabeth.

"That's what a mage is. If Shirone were a mage of an enemy country, who in the Kingdom of Tormia wouldn't applaud Fermi?"

As soon as he finished speaking, a powerful display of magic unfolded in the colosseum, incomparable to anything seen before.

"Finally, the dreaded 5th tier."

The 6th stage was statistically the point where 90 percent of the final 15 were selected.

Roar!

When two hundred golems, each three meters tall, rose from the ground, the view was completely blocked.

The Middle Eastern monster Lamia wriggled its snake-like lower body, and the dragon soldiers equipped their weapons and turned their eerie skulls.

Screech!

As the griffon, with the face of an eagle and the body of a lion, folded its wings and swooped down to the ground, Maria pushed Mark.

"Dodge, Mark!"

The griffon's claw, attached to its single leg, grazed Mark's back.

Although Maria's healing magic followed, Mark's mental state had already plummeted.

'This is… unbelievable.'

Watching the participants fight in a battlefield swarming with monsters, it didn't feel real.

'How can they still hold on?'

If Shirone hadn't cleared the enemies with multiple explosions, they wouldn't have made it to the 6th stage.

"Mark, let's stop now. We're really going to die at this rate."

The synchronization rate of the 2000 system was 100 percent.

Even the physical shocks received from the creatures were converted into mental shocks, but in reality, they would have already died.

"It's not over yet. No one has been eliminated!"

Although he aimed to pass, Mark considered it a half-success if he could just gain confidence in his skills.

'It can't be this different! I worked hard, risking my life to get here!'

Mark focused all his strength, thinking that if he gave up now, he wouldn't be able to do anything in the future.

"Earth Pillar!"

A thick pillar struck the golem, but the massive body, weighing over a ton, broke the pillar and approached.

Roar!

As the golems raised their arms high and prepared to strike, Mark twisted his body to shield Maria.

"Ugh!"

At the same time, a flash of teleportation landed in front of them, and Shirone cast an explosion.

The golem's body trembled and then disintegrated into soil as if it had been ground in a crusher.

"Senior?"

Maria hurriedly greeted him.

"Thank you."

"For what?"

Shirone didn't look back at them.

"Huh? For helping us…"

"I didn't help you. I saved you."

If Mark had stubbornly held out until the last moment, both of them would have lost their lives.

"Get out. Give up now."

Mark shouted with his eyes wide open.

"I can't do that! At least one competitor…"

"Get out!"

Mark's shoulders flinched.

"There are no second chances. If you want to die, do as you please. But don't drag others down with you."

"Senior…"

Mark looked at Shirone disappearing into the battlefield and bowed his head in sorrow.

'I'm sorry.'

It was the heart of a senior who couldn't bear to see his cherished junior die.

Clang! Clang! Clang!

The dragon soldiers, with jaws as strong as iron, raised their antique swords and charged.

"Mark."

At Maria's words, Mark nodded, and the two simultaneously spoke the keyword to leave the battlefield.

"Atra."

The sight of the dragon soldiers striking down with their swords disappeared in an instant.

"Number 29 and 30 have left the battlefield."

"Of course. They lack experience and are too immature to be considered graduates."

Baikal had not been evaluating them from the start.

"Still, the final evaluation must be recorded in the report. Should we grade them as F?"

"Below standard."

Baikal did not budge.

"Write it down like that."

"Yes, yes."

Lara sighed and scribbled with her pen.

With two dropouts, the 10 minutes of the 6th stage of endurance survival were almost over.

Nevertheless, more than half of the creatures survived, and the participants' faces were clearly showing signs of exhaustion.

-Entering the 7th stage of endurance survival.

When the voice of the 2000 system was heard, everyone's focus wavered.

"28 survivors. I've never seen this number enter the 7th stage."

When most participants choose hostility over alliance, the selection of 15 is naturally accelerated.

However, there were occasional cases where everyone focused on completing the mission together, reaching the 7th stage.

"Anyway, it will end here."

The lineup of the 4th tier consisted of creatures known as the kings of their species, the top of the continental food chain.

Roar!

As the desert king Basilisk roared, the spectators' faces turned pale.

With a body length of 12 meters and a weight of 40 tons, its thick tail was powerful enough to annihilate a squad of human soldiers in one strike.

Roar!

The forest king Ogre, with a body reaching 4 meters, roared, and in the sky, the giant monster bird Wyvern covered the sky with its leathery wings, spewing flames into the colosseum.

"This is… hell."

The sight of 87 4th-tier creatures and hundreds of monsters that survived the 6th stage was something that could never be seen in the real world.

"Damn! What is Shirone thinking?"

As the Wyvern's flames scorched the ground and charged, Canis raised a shadow wall to defend.

"Heh, humans have created something interesting."

Muttering that, Harvest pulled out the dragon soldier's skull and transmitted his voice through the mental channel.

-What will you do? If you want to shine, now is the chance.

-I don't know yet. Let's watch Shirone a bit more.

-The strength of dark magic is defense, so holding out isn't a bad idea. Anyway, it seems like 15 will be selected here.

"Canis! Are you okay?"

The golems blocking Canis were crushed with a thud.

As Canis withdrew the shadow wall, three ogres stood tall in front of him.

"Arin?"

Arin ran through the ogres' legs.

"I caught the ones with simpler minds. This should hold for 3 minutes."

Her judgment was also defensive.

"Oh? Mind control?"

Baikal's eyes lit up.

"The mental activity level is beyond the human range. It's amazing that she controlled three 4th-tier ogres."

The sight of the three ogres protecting Arin and wildly smashing enemies was visible.

"Her mental fluidity is tremendous."

"Personally, I hope the kingdom takes her in. She's a talent we don't want to lose to another country."

"A."

If everyone were as clear as Arin, there would be no conflicts of opinion among scouts.

'In contrast…'

Baikal's gaze followed Shirone.

He was still fighting with high stats, but the intense acceleration of his magic was no longer visible.

"Is this the picture you wanted?"

Shirone's face was pale as if drained of blood, and his eyes were gradually losing focus.

'I need rest…'

He couldn't defeat the creatures in a distracted state, and his power was diminishing over time.

'If I could just rest a little…'

The photon cannon still extended fiercely, but the enemies' encirclement was tightening.

"Iruki, Shirone seems very tired."

Nade flew to Iruki and said.

"…So?"

"Let's help him. He needs to rest a bit."

Although they were still a team, Iruki's thoughts differed from Nade's.

"It's Shirone's decision. We don't need to step in unless he asks for help."

"But at this rate, he'll really be eliminated!"

"Even so, there's nothing we can do. If you have the mental space to worry about others, why not take care of yourself first?"

Watching Iruki teleport away, Nade gritted his teeth.

'Damn it! This guy and that guy…!'

Friends who had laughed and chatted until yesterday felt colder than anyone else during the graduation exam.

"Huff, huff."

Shirone's photon cannon, which had been dazzling his eyes, suddenly stopped like a faucet running dry.

As the Basilisk approached, pushing aside the golems, Shirone couldn't respond. At that moment, Nade flew in.

"Shirone! Take a break! I'll protect you!"

Shirone's eyebrows twitched.

"Nade, move…"

"You can't get eliminated like this! You've been pushing too hard from the start. Recover here and then…"

"I said move!"

Nade turned around with a surprised expression.

"Nade, I will become a mage. No one can stop my dream. So move."

'This is bad. He's not in his right mind.'

Shirone's eyes lacked vitality.

He had been casting magic with the highest concentration for nearly an hour, so it was only natural.

"If you don't move… I'll have to fight you. If you want to beat me and become the best, then come at me."

For a moment, Nade's face showed a distressed expression.

"…Hang in there, Shirone."

Only after Nade teleported away did Shirone speak again.

"Let's eat… after this."

Baikal analyzed the situation.

"Number 21 is a mess."

"West Nade. His skills don't seem to have declined, but his behavior patterns are strange. He doesn't step in when he should, and makes good decisions only when it's really dangerous."

"That's what you call a mess. F. Number 27 is the same. He seems to have already reached his limit."

"His magic power level remains constant, but it doesn't increase anymore. The efficiency is extremely low, and the power of his photon magic is less than half of what it was at the beginning."

"Disappointing. I had some expectations when Plu strongly recommended him."

Shirone cast the photon cannon again towards the monsters that loomed like a curtain.

'This is just the beginning!'

As he drew a massive halo through time division, the information of Ataraxia began to accumulate.

"It's here. The Archangel's magic amplification array."

"Hmm, instead of accelerating, he was using his spare mental power elsewhere."

Elizabeth chimed in with rare praise.

"It's an improvisation in a situation without recovery time. That explains the reduced efficiency of the photon cannon."

"But it doesn't change anything. If he passes the 7th stage, there will only be the 8th stage. He's not even considering the second exam."

"It's Ataraxia! Everyone, get out of the way!"

The participants, who noticed Shirone's magic array, predicted the direction of the flash and moved aside.

"Tch, are you really doing it?"

They already knew the power of Ataraxia from the advanced class to the graduating class.

"Is this his last struggle?"

Fermi, who had already distanced himself, watched Shirone's last stand with a smile.

'Good job, Shirone.'

The strategy of using Shirone to advance the stages and then standing out in the higher stages had worked perfectly.

"I will never stop."

With his eyes wide open, Shirone kicked off the ground and charged towards the dazzling light of Ataraxia.

"What?"

As the participants and scouts watched in bewilderment, only a hooded woman clenched her fist.

"Go, Shirone."

The technique was so dangerous that even the Archangel Ikael had never attempted it.

It was the Ataraxia body technique.
