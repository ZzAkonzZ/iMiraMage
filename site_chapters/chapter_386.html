Chapter 386: One Man Team (2)

The senior class schedule had already reached the sixth week.

Competition was becoming increasingly fierce, and rank changes were frequent.

However, no students had changed classes yet.

The teachers were astonished, saying it was the first time in five years. The problem was that the rankings in Class Two, known for their fluidity, had become stagnant.

Class One was dominating Class Two, while Class Three was desperately chasing Class Two.

In other words, the overall level of the senior class was higher than any other year.

An unprecedented situation occurred where 15 students earned 20 points in the survival evaluation, but since most of them were from Class Three, the changes were minimal.

Today was the day when the intense competition would shine.

The strategy and tactics evaluation, which organized groups based on rankings, awaited the senior class students.

"Hmm, then I'll be in Group 5."

Shirone knew without looking at the chart. He had already memorized the rankings of all 30 students.

Group 5 consisted of ranks 21 to 25. Naturally, the group leader would be rank 21, and Shirone, ranked 23, was included in Group 5.

Since Dante (rank 21) and Iruki (rank 22) always scored the same as Shirone, their rankings hadn't changed since the first week.

Ranks 24 and 25 were Canis and Closer, making it a squad capable of scoring high points based on the members alone.

On the other hand, some groups were troubled by their rankings.

A prime example was Group 4.

This group consisted of the lower ranks of Class Two: Screamer, Amy, Suabi, Maya, and Aider, in that order.

Most had maintained their positions, but Screamer had plummeted from rank 11 to 16 in just six weeks.

'Damn it. When will I get out of this mess?'

As a martial artist, Screamer's easiest way to earn points was through interpersonal combat.

However, this year, the matchups weren't favorable, with Class One's skilled students concentrated in the early rounds.

Several students' draw strategies had turned two potential 3-point wins into 1-point results.

Of course, the main reason for his drastic drop was the 20 points lost to the red team in the survival evaluation.

"Hey, everyone, gather around. Let's decide our positions first."

At Screamer's gesture, Group 4 gathered. Watching them, Screamer sighed.

'This balance is a mess.'

Scouts use a metric called 'party contribution.'

It assesses the contributions of members, with a total score of 5 for a 5-person party, distributed based on contribution.

While it evaluates party organization, it can't perfectly assess individual abilities but is excellent for determining responsibility.

A contribution score of 1 means the member did their part. A score of 2 means they did the work of two people.

While this would attract scouts' attention, it also means someone didn't even do their part, indicating poor balance.

The ideal ratio in a party is 1, 1, 1, 1, 1, with everyone fulfilling their roles. But Screamer's group was far from this golden balance.

The problem was Maya.

In Screamer's view, Maya's party contribution was at best 0.5.

This meant the remaining four had to make up the 0.5 to be competitive.

'If Maya is 0.5, Aider is 0.8, Suabi is 1.1, Amy is 1.2, then I have to be... 1.4? That's crazy.'

A 0.4-point contribution in a party was enormous.

It meant he had to increase the party's combat power by 8% alone.

'Hmm, is it possible? We have two utility members.'

Suabi and Maya were both utility types, despite different majors.

Usually, a 5-person party wouldn't include two utility members, but with Screamer, it was different.

A martial artist with both magic and physical abilities could perform exceptionally well with buffs.

'At least we have Amy. I'll lead, and Amy will follow. I'll have to carry the team.'

Having organized his thoughts, Screamer clapped to boost morale. As the group leader, he needed to unite the team.

"Alright, listen up. We're going to decide positions now. If we strategize well, we can score high."

Screamer pointed to himself first.

"I'm the main dealer. And that's it. You get it?"

"Martial artist's killer strategy?"

Amy's words made Screamer nod.

"Exactly. So, Suabi, buff me with everything you've got. Haste, Concentrate, Healing, don't hold back. Even in the sixth week, there won't be anyone that strong. Let's secure victory early. Got it?"

"Y-yes, I'll do my best."

Suabi answered weakly. Though lacking enthusiasm, Screamer didn't mind.

Despite his indecisiveness and poor social skills, Suabi was a utility genius who could chain over twenty buff spells, so he'd do his part.

"Amy, you're the secondary dealer. I don't need to give you instructions. And Maya, what spells can you use? Wide-area buffs would be good."

Sound magic's strength was its wide-range effects.

"Oh? My best is Battlefield Aria. It amplifies magic through resonance."

"Hmm..."

Battlefield Aria was effective in real combat.

But with Maya casting it, it was different. Her buff efficiency was far below average.

Screamer adjusted the martial artist's killer strategy slightly.

"Then let's go with debuffs. Any debuffs you can cast?"

"Rhapsody of Agitation. It amplifies anxiety."

"Good, use that. It's more effective on hundreds than on five."

"Okay, I'll do my best."

Maya nodded deeply, determined.

"What about me?"

Aider asked, supporting his head.

Being young, he seemed less burdened by graduation, unlike Maya.

"Multiplayer. I'll cut through, and you finish them off. Make sure they're dead. It's common to lose because one wasn't killed."

"Got it. No problem."

"Alright! Let's do this! Let's show them what we've got."

Knowing Screamer's personality, Amy found his forced enthusiasm cringeworthy.

'He must really want to get out of here.'

Still, she doubted the martial artist's killer strategy would work.

He might have thought he could handle it alone, given his past reputation as a feared fighter.

'Well, my rank is low now. Let's go with it.'

Even as a team member, suggesting opinions required considering the leader's personality for effectiveness.

Meanwhile, Shirone's Group 5 was also waiting for the evaluation, but unlike Group 4, they had no special strategy.

Dante was with Closer, Shirone with Iruki, and Canis enjoyed solitude.

Of course, he wasn't alone in the mental channel.

-This school is quite boring.

Harvest had finished decoding the "Book of Light and Darkness" after six months of isolation.

But the intense competition in the senior class had kept him from appearing.

-Endure. In a year, we can do what we want.

-Really? Well, we're much stronger now. Graduation should be easy.

The "Book of Light and Darkness" from Heaven's Akashic Records was a theory to amplify darkness using the power of incarnation.

In essence, light strengthened darkness.

Though not yet fully understood to create specific magic, Canis's power had significantly increased.

-Graduation isn't the issue. This time, I must surpass Shirone and uphold our master's legacy.

-Haha, that sounds fun.

The teacher, having finished checking the second training ground, spoke.

"Five minutes until the match starts. The sixth week's mission is 'Extermination.'"

All 30 students entered the second training ground.

Since the ranking was based on which group cleared the monsters first, the evaluation had to proceed simultaneously.

The massive training ground was divided into six sections, and Shirone entered Group 5's area.

As the members gathered, Dante spoke briefly.

"Well, let's do our best."

Shirone asked,

"Any strategy or tactics? You're the leader and an information processing major, so you should decide."

"Tactics? Hmm..."

Dante, resting his chin on his hand, looked at the members. Explosive Iruki, Radiant Shirone, Dark Closer, and Shadow Canis.

"Just kill them as fast as you can."

"Okay."

The five turned simultaneously and moved to the center of the area.

After warming up for about three minutes, the evaluation teacher spoke.

"We will now begin the strategy and tactics evaluation."

No explanation was needed for the sixth week.

The lights on the scoreboard turned green, yellow, and red in sequence, and the same types of monsters appeared in all six sections.

'It's starting.'

Shirone entered his Spirit Zone.

The first task was to identify the species and numbers.

Bats called Jargols flew in the sky. Each was about the size of an eagle.

On the ground were bipedal monsters called Warwolves.

And starting this week, a new type was added: giant worms called Earthworms, which tunneled underground.

'Another 9-tier week.'

The species were manageable, but the problem was the number, over 300.

Roar!

A Warwolf howled and charged.

Jargols screeched with ultrasonic waves, and dozens of holes in the ground spewed acidic liquid from Earthworms.

Group 5 instinctively took their positions.

Shirone and Iruki bombarded the Jargols with magic. Photon Cannons and Atomic Bombs quickly obliterated the bats.

On the ground, Dante and Closer worked together.

Dante set up a teleportation magic circle 10 meters ahead and scattered instant magic circles around.

Warwolves caught in the instant magic circles were automatically transported to the central main magic circle.

When about twenty had gathered, Closer cast his spell.

"Eruption!"

As the ground heated up from friction, the Warwolves looked down. At the same time, a small volcanic explosion erupted, blowing away the entire main magic circle.

"Hahaha! Send more, Dante!"

"Oh, okay?"

As Dante set up more magic circles on the ground, enemies began entering the main magic circle at a rate of three per second.

Closer unleashed all kinds of magic like a fish in water.

Without worrying about targeting, earth magic, known for its physical power, was the strongest, leaving the monsters with no chance.

The ground rolled up like a wave, and the Rock and Roll spell, growing like a snowball, struck the Warwolves.

Huge rocks from the Heavy Stone spell fell from the sky, crushing the enemies.

Then, with three consecutive Eruptions, all the monsters on the ground were annihilated.

Canis stood with his hands in his pockets.

The acidic liquid from the Earthworms flew from all directions but couldn't penetrate his Dark Skin.

Of course, he wasn't doing nothing. His Spirit Zone detected the location of all the monsters underground.

Finally, his right hand emerged from his pocket.

"If the sky is the world of light..."

His index and middle fingers stood upright.

"Then underground is the world of darkness."

Canis's specialty, the saw-blade-shaped power of darkness, swiftly cut through the Earthworms underground.

Screech! Screech!

The Earthworms, standing vertically, all collapsed, spewing green saliva.

-328 monsters exterminated. Completion time: 3 minutes 28 seconds.
