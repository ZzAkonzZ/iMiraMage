Chapter 296: Great Secret (5)

“We came to save you! Where are you now?”

"SHEEEOOOOO!"

Nade bent his four arms and roared toward the sky.

At that, all the Egoists froze in place.

Amy watched the situation with bated breath. Her heart pounded with the faint hope that her voice might have reached Shirone.

However, what unfolded next was horrifying. The Egoists began elongating as if being sucked upward, defying gravity and rising against the falling rain.

Then, they arced through the air and began converging into Nade's Egoist form.

The Egoist's form changed drastically after absorbing its comrades. Its body grew over three meters tall, with insect legs sprouting from its shoulders, sides, and waist.

Its tail swelled like a bee’s stinger, and its face grotesquely stretched both forward and backward, resembling a twisted sausage. Its face split open like a knife cut, revealing saw-like teeth, and a long tongue emerged from its throat, waving like waves.

“Kiiieeeeee!”

Amy's face grew pale. There was no way she could believe that thing was Shirone. The horrifying form, created by merging all the Egoists nearby, was monstrous beyond belief.

The ground shook with a thud as the abomination stepped forward. There was no defeating it. She was certain of that. Yet she took a fighting stance while struggling to maintain her fading consciousness.

If they retreat at just Level Eleven, they won't be able to save Shirone. Even if it meant dying right here, she resolved to fight.

"Good, let's go!"

“Well done! Now run away!”

Armin shouted as he dashed past her. Amy turned to glare at him in disbelief. But she soon canceled her magic and started running. Reina quickly followed.

The three ran with all their might. The Egoist's horrific screams echoed through the rain from afar.

Armin ducked into a dark alley and leaned against a wall, sliding down to sit. Amy and Reina sat opposite him, tilting their heads back and breathing heavily. Raindrops beat against their faces like a shower.

Amy asked before she could catch her breath.

“What on earth is that? How can such a monster exist in Shirone's mind?"

"It's humans' selfish defense mechanism. It's mixed in with the projections. The stronger the projection, the stronger the Egoist becomes."

Amy recalled how Nade had been the strongest projection so far.

"But is it really okay to just run like this?"

“When the ego is attacked, Egoists manifest using the nearby projections. However, when it enters a state of terror as it just did, its inherent nature as an Egoist disappears and can't track our location. It’s incredibly strong, but fighting it offers no benefit, so escaping is the better option.”

Reina rested her elbows on her knees and buried her face in her hands. Steam rose from her body after running so far.

"Phew, is this because we failed to inject the keywords?"

“It’s not that we failed, it’s that Shirone rejected us. His wariness is considerable. Of course, given the real-world situation, it can’t be helped.”

Amy asked.

"Do we have to keep fighting things like that?"

“It’s the only method for now. We must keep injecting keywords until Shirone accepts them. There’s no other way to descend to the First Level without Shirone guiding us.”

“But those things are still part of Shirone’s mind, right? Can’t we reason with them somehow?”

Armin shook his head coldly.

“Egoists are the defensive mechanisms present in everyone’s subconscious. They perceive everything except Shirone as an enemy. The moment the keyword of ‘death’ was injected, even we, who earned 7 and 10 golds of trust, were no exception. It’s not something to take personally. While the world divides into ‘me’ and ‘others,’ to the Egoist, there’s only me, me, me. To them, the concept of ‘others’ simply doesn’t exist.”

Having experienced the Egoists’ relentless hostility firsthand, Amy shuddered.

"How do we fight from now on? They're so strong."

"No, this is actually weak. Egoists become stronger the closer you get to the deep layers. That’s why we must find Shirone’s avatar on the surface layer. If we descend to the instinctual realm at the first stage, the Egoists will dominate most of Shirone’s consciousness to protect him. At that point, they’ll wield power comparable to Shirone himself, the master of this world. This is why divers never descend to the deep seas. If Shirone’s unconscious weren’t already weakened, even Arius might have reconsidered his approach.”

Amy’s eyes gleamed as she asked.

"Then aren't Arius and Zion in the same situation? If the Egoists get stronger the deeper you go, they might die before they even get there.”

Armin shook his head.

“No, they’ll likely avoid Shirone altogether. Grave robbers are experts in the human mind. To us, this world looks ordinary, but each object here carries its own significance. Arius will interpret those meanings and find a direct path down. Of course, the risk increases below the REM realm, but by then, Shirone’s unconscious will likely have deteriorated significantly. That's what Arius is counting on."

Reina said.

“Then how about we use Arius’s method? Amy knows Shirone well, so she might be able to interpret the objects here.”

“Hm, the structure of consciousness differs for each person. Some are stair-like, and some resemble plazas. Others might have corridors or even phased spaces. However, access to lower levels is typically represented as underground—it’s the most natural imagery for the subconscious.”

Amy suggested.

"Then shouldn't we just find a basement here?"

Armin chuckled.

It seemed these women still didn’t understand how terrifying the human mind could be. Well, this was only the Eleventh Layer, after all.

“I was just explaining the general theory. The human mind is extraordinarily complex and delicate. Unless you’re an expert, you would have to search every basement in this world, and even if you found one, there’s no guarantee it would lead to the entrance of the Tenth Level. We can't arrive faster than them anyway. But if we manage to meet Shirone here, the situation could turn in our favor. We must keep injecting keywords."

Reina said with a tired expression.

“And each time we do, the Egoists will find us?”

“There’s no other way. That’s why I told you not to come. It might sound ridiculous now that I’ve accepted your help.”

Amy had no regrets about that. What bothered her was the idea that Shirone’s mind itself was attacking her.

"Haah. It feels strange. Having to fight Shirone's mind."

Armin glared coldly.

“Amy, get a grip.”

"What?"

“This is no time to get sentimental. As you've seen, a person’s mind is extremely dangerous. Even many of the best divers, on par with Arius, have lost their lives here.”

“I know that. That’s why I’m staying alert.”

"Are you really?"

Amy looked at Armin questioningly. She really was alert. No, she was even prepared to risk her life.

“Make no mistake. Shirone isn’t tormenting us. We’re the ones barging into Shirone’s territory and wreaking havoc. This is a deeply personal space, one Shirone has no desire to show anyone. And yet Miss Amy has entered it. Getting angry or disappointed at this world's response is cowardly and base behavior."

"That's..."

Amy was speechless.

She had never considered Shirone’s perspective, as Armin pointed out. If someone were to enter her mind and examine everything in detail... The mere thought was terrifying.

“We’re not going to meet Shirone. We're meeting Shirone's avatar. The avatar is completely different from the Shirone you know outside. Amy, you couldn't withdraw currency. You might feel hurt, but that might be natural for the avatar. When you see Shirone's avatar, whatever he does will probably feel unlike Shirone."

Reina added.

“And yet, that’s probably the truest version of Shirone.”

"Yes. What humans can know about others is only what's shown externally. But avatars are different. They encapsulate every emotion a person harbors, even the shameful, humiliating parts they would never reveal. That’s what we’re going to face.”

Amy paused to think, then took a deep breath. She slapped her cheeks repeatedly with both hands. Her face stung, but it helped her focus. As Armin said, she hadn't been alert, let alone prepared.

"I understand. I won’t judge, no matter what."

Armin finally smiled in satisfaction when he saw the cold light in Amy's eyes. Without this level of resolve, one wouldn't be able to endure what lies in a person’s mind.

What if the heart of a man who confessed his love turned out to be nothing but animalistic desires? What if the inner thoughts of someone you considered your closest friend were filled with jealousy and spite?

How many people in this world could know such truths without feeling betrayed?

Armin emphasized once more.

"Ataraxia will be at least below Level Three. And that place is a repository of things no one ever wishes to reveal to others.”

“Yes. I’ll take no interest in anything I see. After all, I have my own secrets I would rather not reveal. I apologize for being so thoughtless. Not being able to withdraw currency shook me more than I realized.”

Reina chimed in.

"It's okay. I hadn't thought that far either. I would probably have felt upset if I couldn’t withdraw anything too.”

Amy gave a bitter smile.

“Shirone must be upset with me about something too. I made a mistake. No matter how much mental power someone has, and even with Indestructible Diamond, he's still human with the same emotions as everyone else."

Armin, who had been watching outside the alley, slowly turned his head to look at Amy.

“Just now… What did you say?”

"Huh? Oh, that Shirone has the same emotions..."

“No, not that. Did you say Indestructible Diamond?”

Amy blinked in confusion, but seeing Armin's expression was more serious than expected, she quickly said.

“Yes. There is a famous monk at our academy named professor Ethella, and she said that. To wield Ataraxia, one would need to reach the realm of Indestructible Diamond.”

“Ah, what a fool I’ve been...!”

Armin slapped his forehead with his palm.

Why hadn't he thought of it?

Even if inscribed into instinct, Ataraxia was too vast a concept for the human mind to contain.

Normally, it would be enough to destroy one's mind.

And yet Shirone wielded it. Therefore, this teacher called monk Ethella had hit the mark.

“I apologize, Miss Amy. I’ve been acting so arrogant. I’m the fool here. I feel like hitting myself a hundred times over.”

"Huh? What did I..."

Armin gripped Amy’s shoulders firmly as if transferring strength to her.

"You've saved us. Follow me. We're going to Shirone."

Reina adjusted her bow and stood.

"Then are we fighting the Egoist?"

"No. We'll take a detour too. Like Arius."

Armin was certain of success.

This method would allow them to catch up with Arius. Moreover, it was a method that Zion's group, who had to avoid Shirone, couldn't use even if they knew about it.

Leading the two women, Armin emerged onto the street. The Egoists, in their state of terror, seemed to have moved far away, as none were in sight.

Wading through ankle-deep water, they arrived at the outskirts of the main road. There stood a carriage, its coachman sitting in the rain on the driver’s bench.

The coachman wore a wide-brimmed magician’s hat, but for some reason, shadows obscured only his face, making his expression impossible to discern. It was as if he had no face at all.

As Armin approached, the coachman spoke without even giving him a glance.

"We're not operating today."

Armin took out all the remaining gold coins and showed them.

“We urgently need to get somewhere. Please make an exception.”

The sum surpassed the trust Armin had earned, so the coachman couldn’t refuse outright. As expected, his tone softened slightly, though it still carried reluctance.

"It's difficult to operate on a day like this. It's raining too much. Where on earth are you trying to go in this weather?"

Armin glanced once at the two women waiting behind him and requested to the coachman.

"Let's go somewhere where it's not raining."

The driver’s head snapped toward Armin as if struck by lightning. The movement was so unnatural that the watching women gulped nervously. Slowly turning to face forward, the driver gripped the reins and spoke.

"Get in, Customers."
