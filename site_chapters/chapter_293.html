Chapter 293: Great Secret (2)

Armin nodded.

“Yes. The reason Shirone was invited to Kazra Royal Palace is because he possesses an archangel's power."

"So that was it. Those bastards! It wasn't just about finding their biological son."

Reina, unfamiliar with anything related to Heaven, couldn’t fully understand their conversation, but she chose to remain silent for now. She figured it wouldn’t be too late to fill in the gaps after hearing the full story.

Amy suddenly raised her hand.

"Wait a moment. But why did they try to kill Shirone? If Shirone dies, won’t Ataraxia disappear with him?”

Armin pressed his lips tightly together. It was a sharp observation. But since he had decided to accept their help, he thought he shouldn't lie and spoke honestly.

“There’s a reason for that. It was probably Arius’s idea to kill Shirone. At first, I doubt he intended to go that far. He probably planned to infiltrate Shirone’s consciousness and replicate Ataraxia. But he failed.”

“Why? Is it too difficult to replicate?”

"There are several variables, but in my opinion, Ataraxia isn't an ability humans can handle. Forcefully installing it at any point in consciousness could drive someone mad. So the original owner seems to have imprinted it very deep inside."

“How deep are we talking?”

Armin raised his index finger.

“Level One. It's probably in the primal consciousness that governs instinct. If you plant something in the realm of instinct, no matter how bizarre the concept, the mind accepted it naturally as if it had always been there."

Amy nodded. Things were finally starting to make sense.

Armin continued.

“The primal consciousness is a safe haven for oneself but a terrifying place for others. However, someone of Arius's skill should be able to dive into primal consciousness briefly. And it seems he tried, given that there's a door installed."

“And yet, he failed to excavate it?”

"That's the only plausible conclusion. It’s likely protected by an encryption system no human can break or sealed within a capsule so impenetrable that even Shirone cannot access it. That’s why he decided to kill Shirone. That’s why Arius decided to kill Shirone. The deeper one delves into the primal consciousness, the stronger the defensive instincts become. If the unconscious security system activates, even the greatest diver would drown without being able to escape "

“I understand now. That’s why Shirone’s neck….”

“That’s right. Human unconsciousness has roots deep in the body. Therefore, if the neck is severed, the unconscious defense mechanisms collapse. Arius was waiting for that moment.”

Amy bit her lip, lost in thought. There was still one thing she couldn't understand.

“Wait, something doesn’t add up. Even if Arius collapses the unconscious and reaches the first level, what can he do from there? If it’s a power humans can’t wield, then even replicating it would be useless. And there’s no way to imprint it onto instincts like Shirone has.”

Armin felt the inevitable moment had come. From here on was information that absolutely must not be revealed to the outside world.

'I really didn't want to disclose this if possible...'

But without their help, Shirone couldn’t be saved. To increase their mission’s success rate even a little bit, they needed to understand the truth.

“What you are about to hear is confidential information of the highest security clearance, regardless of the kingdom. Of course, no secret stays secret forever, but I'll tell you anyway."

Both women nodded. They didn't promise not to reveal it because they understood Armin's last words.

"Human consciousness... can transform into objects."

Amy blinked. The statement didn’t sink in right away, and it felt underwhelming compared to the heavy prelude.

But as Armin's words sank in, she realized how outrageous it was.

“You’re saying the mind can become an object?”

Armin delayed his answer and threw out an unexpected question.

“Have you ever heard of the word ‘Dreamo’?”

Reina shook her head first, followed by Amy with the same response.

Armin continued as if this was expected.

"Dreamo is a multi-dimensional mental world. The human brain consumes energy to think. So, are thoughts energy? Some people believe energy merely powers the brain, and thoughts are a different concept. They are products created by energy. And that product must be released somewhere. That’s what dreamos are: the space where thoughts are discharged.”

"It's similar to the law of conservation of mass."

"Correct. Dreamo is a space you can enter from dreams if certain conditions are met. You could say it’s a complex of imagination where all your thoughts gather. This leads to an incredible hypothesis: if thoughts can escape to Dreamo, then conversely, thoughts from Dreamo could be brought into reality."

Amy asked with a bewildered expression.

“Has anyone actually been to Dreamo?”

"Why wouldn't they? No, countless people have. They enter through dreams and smuggle things using the rule of equivalent exchange cleverly. However, they say it’s difficult to smuggle ideas that lack strong desires. In any case, Arius uses this method to excavate thoughts.”

"Wait. That means objects from Dreamo already exist. Then what exactly are..."

Amy's eyes widened as she realized something.

"Don't tell me those objects are..."

Arius said with a bitter smile.

“That’s correct. We call them Objet.”

Amy understood why Armin had warned them. It was evidence breaking the existing hypothesis that thoughts were merely energy.

"Literally, Objets are materialized human consciousness. That’s why their abilities are deeply rooted in personal desires. In the realm of thought, this wouldn’t seem strange at all. The problem arises when those thoughts manifest in reality.”

Reina spoke.

“I know about Objet. A mage acquaintance told me they're items that violate the laws of the universe. But hearing this now, it seems they didn't fully understand either."

Armin agreed.

"That's likely. Even among the upper class, even royalty, very few know about Dreamo. Outside of the king and a dedicated department, it’s probably classified as top secret. Objets may seem to ignore causality, but if we accept the hypothesis that thoughts are a type of matter, they're not actually violating universal laws. However, much about the interaction between reality and Dreamo remains unknown.”

Reina said.

"Like 'The Last Wail of Yeager Family’?"

"Oh? You knew about that?"

Reina blushed and shook her head.

"No. I just know what it's called. I was a performer at a royal gathering, and I have good hearing so I pick up a lot. I’m sorry. I just mentioned what I knew..."

Armin smiled reassuringly and shared the tale of 'The Last Wail of Yeager Family'. Amy, who had been listening intently, shuddered at the final incident.

"Did that actually happen?"

“It was. However, there’s debate over whether it was the act of a psychotic patriarch or a phenomenon to restore dimensional balance. hat's certain is they experienced something impossible, and an Objet remained afterward. It’s possible that someone in the family had accidentally accessed Dreamo. While I can’t disclose the conditions for entering Dreamo, the probability of entering by chance isn’t zero.”

Amy was curious about these conditions but kept quiet, thinking it would be impolite to probe further.

“Anyway, the relationship between Dreamo and reality is not something that humans can predict. That's why kingdoms consider accessing Dreamo a serious crime. In truth, Dive Magic itself isn’t inherently bad—it’s used to treat mental illnesses, for example. You need an official diver license. However, Arius is abusing that. That's why he's called a grave robber rather than a diver. Accessing Dreamo is taboo in every nation. That's why Arius is a double-S class criminal wanted worldwide."

Reina's expression turned pale.

"In a way... we’re up against someone truly extraordinary.”

Armin acknowledged it wouldn't be easy.

“Indeed. If he weren’t committing crimes, he would undoubtedly be recognized as a brilliant intellectual. Diving requires tremendous intellectual capability."

Amy pieced together the information she had heard so far.

“Let me summarize. Arius is after Ataraxia. He planned a murder to collapse Shirone’s unconscious mind and is currently heading for Level One. He'll steal Ataraxia there and go to Dreamo. Then bring it to reality through smuggling. If that happens...?"

Amy got goosebumps just imagining it. This was also why Armin came here, even binding Keira with Stop Magic.

“Exactly. Ataraxia will become an Objet. The power of the Archangel will be reduced to a tool anyone can wield. This is a truly catastrophic possibility.”

“….”

Faced with a reality they didn’t want to accept, the two women fell silent, each lost in thought.

She thought she had outgrown fear of thunderstorms, but the destructive roar clawed at her primal instincts, gripping her heart with human fear.

"This is incredible. It's like thunder is striking right in front of us."

Armin said, looking outside.

"The fear of death from the unconscious has risen to the surface level. But this level is remarkably stable. If an ordinary person were in this situation, it would've been complete chaos already. It’s only because it’s Shirone that it’s holding together.”

Amy smiled sadly. Unlike how he had criticized her earlier, Armin openly acknowledged Shirone’s abilities.

What would it feel like to be recognized by such a remarkable mage? The absence of Shirone in this moment felt all the more painful.

‘Well, all of this is because of Shirone, after all….’

Armin cautioned himself against indulging in sentimentality.

"The thread is still constricting Shirone's neck even now. It will worsen, and his mind will start collapsing from the base. The consequences are apparent from the current state of Level Eleventh. Eventually, the minimum conditions for Arius to dive into the depths will be met."

Amy quickly snapped back to the pressing reality.

“Is there any way? A way to save Shirone.”

Stopping Arius and Zion wouldn't end it. If the temporal field releases as is, Shirone will die immediately.

"Currently, there's only one way. We must pass through the REM realm and delve deeper. It’s too late to fix anything from the outside. But if we plant something at the bottom of consciousness, it might be possible."

Everything that happens in Shirone's consciousness immediately becomes Shirone's. The idea was to prepare a method of countering the dire situation by implanting it into his unconscious beforehand.

"Like Ataraxia, you mean planting magic?"

Armin scratched his head.

“That’s impossible. In truth, I don’t know how Shirone came to possess that magic. The archangel must have had some special method. But it’s impossible for us. However, if we delve deep enough, we might be able to engrave simple behavioral instructions."
