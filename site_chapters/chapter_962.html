Chapter 962: Face to Face (1)

Regulation Ban: Triangle Field

Tanote, born from the Triangle of Justice, is a mid-level fairy among the 72 ranks, belonging to the geometric class, and governs the form created by connecting three points in a straight line.

The law of the triangle is the "first confinement," and ancient humans trapped many concepts in this shape, such as arrows (penetration), pottery (storage), and blades (cutting).

On a higher level, there is the horn of Mara's spirit in the triangle, and at the highest point, the Trinity, which signifies "reality."

"Hahaha! It's unfortunate for you! I don't know what you dropped, but the Archangel ordered me to retrieve it."

Of course, what Tanote handles is not a high-level spirit but merely the form of a triangle.

Even so, as the Triangle Field overlapped multiple times, Nade couldn't move his limbs.

"Damn it!"

Tanote moved around Nade, marking three points, and each time, the red lines activated, strengthening the binding force.

'The longer this goes on, the worse it gets. I need to deal with this quickly.'

His muscles and senses were becoming paralyzed.

With his only controllable gaze, he looked down to see the elemental bomb had become a tiny dot.

'Iruki.'

Honestly, he knew.

Even if the elemental bomb detonated safely, he wouldn't be able to live and breathe.

'That's why I can die too.'

As a desperate desire that transcended life took over his body, electricity flowed through Nade's body.

"Argh!"

As the electric signals, accompanied by pain, flowed, his paralyzed senses became clear.

"How... how?"

Tanote's face, who had been continuously generating the Triangle Field, turned pale.

'A manifestation?'

A phenomenon corresponding to the number 4 of the law, not contained in the Trinity.

As Nade gritted his teeth, hundreds of red lines around him began to snap.

'Sorry, Liz.'

In many ways.

'I'm leaving you with lies.'

Magic Assimilation - Thunder God Rebirth.

"Argh!"

As he felt his reason slipping away, Nade screamed, and the Triangle Field was destroyed.

Then, an avatar made of electricity surged from Nade's body, writhing like a dragon.

Tanote screamed in terror.

"Eek!"

The form of the Thunder God, filling his vision, was so fierce and sharp that it seemed to scar his retinas.

As the giant face, entwined with electricity resembling Nade, approached, Tanote's screams stopped.

"Ugh... ugh..."

She hiccuped in shock.

Though the Thunder God had no intent to kill, the certainty of death was overwhelming.

'Nature. It's the avatar of nature.'

It was likely because she felt the pure law of innocence, a trait only the highest fairies could possess.

Nade's thoughts exploded through the Thunder God's body with a thunderous roar.

“Foolish child.”

"Eek!"

Nade's face, retaining a shred of reason, twisted monstrously.

“This is not something you can touch without knowing what it is.”

It was a bomb containing all of Iruki and humanity's hopes.

"Sob, sob."

Tanote's small face was drenched in tears, and her legs trembled.

Yet, her pride in following the orders of the great archangel Ikael remained unbroken.

"Shut up! I follow the Archangel's orders...! Ugh!"

As the Thunder God's hand grasped Tanote, her small body made a popping sound and burned.

"Huuu!"

As Nade, with eyes filled with electricity, looked towards the ground, the Thunder God grew larger and roared.

Rumble!

As the thunderous sound, rivaling the heavens, echoed, the fairies chasing the bomb looked up.

"Ah..."

The last thing their clear eyes saw was the sharp, massive charge of electricity.

Crackle!

As the Thunder God passed, the fairies burned to ashes like a swarm of gnats.

"Haa! Haa!"

Nade, having matched the speed of gravity with electricity, hugged the bomb with his body.

Even with the aftermath of magic assimilation, his eyes clearly checked the timer.

'11 seconds.'

A smile of relief spread across his face.

'It's over.'

With the last 10 seconds of his life remaining, good memories flashed through Nade's mind.

'It's not a hallucination.'

Iruki's eyes, filled with fear but also a stronger desire, looked at the door.

Though it was locked and he had the key, the lock seemed to open as if by magic.

In truth, it was a door anyone could open if they wished.

But the only person with the courage and right to enter without Iruki's permission was one person.

No, he couldn't be sure, but if he had to name one person...

"Shirone."

Outside the wide-open door stood Shirone.

"You, how did you..."

From Iruki's position, the commander was not visible, and Dorothy was looking at Shirone with a pained expression.

"I heard."

As Shirone took his first step into the room, Iruki's shoulders flinched in fear.

"You made an elemental bomb. You succeeded."

"...Go back."

Iruki couldn't meet his eyes.

"I don't want comfort, rebuttal, or encouragement. This was my choice. I'll take full responsibility."

"The elemental bomb operation is a good strategy. It will destroy many demons."

Only then did Iruki look at Shirone.

"Do you agree with me?"

A smile spread across Shirone's lips.

"Iruki, no one can oppose you. Who else could handle this? That's why you're the only one qualified to make this choice. Even if I were Yahweh, the Infinite Mage, or one of the Five Great Stars of the Ivory Tower."

As the commanders looked on in a daze, Shirone knelt on one knee before Iruki.

"I won't judge your choice. The reason I came here is simply..."

Shirone placed his hand on Iruki's shoulder.

"To keep a promise as a friend."

Dorothy murmured.

"A promise?"

Meanwhile, Iruki...

"Ah..."

He recalled a memory, his chin trembling.

"A promise."

Shirone nodded.

"Yes, that promise. When your thoughts lead to a terrible tragedy, when your mind consumes you..."

Tears streamed down Iruki's cheeks.

"To stop you. You asked me to stop you..."

At that moment, from deep within his brain, where the scraps of information were piled up, a small Iruki emerged.

Like someone adrift at sea, he wore a terrified expression, reaching out and shouting.

Save me.

"I'll take responsibility."

Shirone responded to the voice of his heart.

"I'll take responsibility for everything you've had to bear until now. So..."

Shirone's voice trembled as he looked at Iruki, who had become a figure of pain.

"So now, come back."

Iruki let out a pained sob and bowed his head. As Shirone embraced his trembling body, Iruki finally confessed his true feelings.

"I'm scared. I'm so scared I could die."

"It's okay now."

As Shirone patted the back of his friend, who had withered like a dead branch, he strengthened his resolve.

"I'm not afraid."

As Iruki slowly nodded, Dorothy ran to him and embraced him.

"Iruki! Are you okay?"

As Shirone, having entrusted his friend to his lover, slowly stood up, Dorothy looked back at him.

"Thank you, Shirone."

Though he had placed all the burden on Shirone, she honestly couldn't handle it anymore.

Iruki lifted his head abruptly.

"What are you going to do?"

Even just laying down the weight of humanity brought clarity back to his eyes.

"Bashka is too far. What time is it? Right."

A savant doesn't need to check the time.

"It's already too late. By now, Nade would have dropped the elemental bomb."

"Don't worry."

Shirone, with his hands behind his back, looked out the window.

"My heart is already there."

Time until explosion: 10 seconds.

As Paimon ascended to the sky, she taunted Miro's group, who were following her.

"Hahaha! Try to catch me, humans!"

The elemental bomb was still high in the sky, but considering its falling speed, they would encounter it soon.

'Make it the most spectacular!'

Meanwhile, the fairy unit that had been defeated by Nade also reached the main force of the heavenly army.

"We're sorry. We couldn't stop it."

Ikael, instead of showing emotion, observed the rapidly falling bomb.

'It's definitely a human weapon.'

Even Balkan couldn't imagine its power, but the scale of angels surpassed humans.

'Judging by the situation on the ground, it's not just a simple firework.'

With giants already in the human city, they wanted to retrieve the bomb if possible.

'There's enough time.'

If It Were the Archangel of Light

"Raiel."

Just as Ikael was about to give an order, Nane spoke.

"It's fine."

Nane seemed uninterested in the bomb, instead observing the humans screaming in the chaos of Bashka.

"I want to watch. This place will be safe."

It wasn't arrogance; his perfection commanded obedience.

"Understood."

Nade, clinging to the rapidly falling bomb, felt dizzy.

'8 seconds! 7 seconds!'

At that moment, he saw Paimon and Miro's group rapidly ascending from the ground.

'What?'

The first person he noticed was Amy.

'Why are you in Bashka?'

There was nothing he could do now, and Nade let go of the bomb.

"Ahhhhh!"

5 seconds.

'Fine, let's die! Let's all die together!'

4 seconds.

He felt nothing but resentment, and beyond that, a profound emptiness washed over him.

3 seconds.

"Satan."

Paimon spread her arms towards the bomb.

"Bring hell to this world!"

2 seconds.

As the demon realm showed signs of opening, her skin began to bubble and boil.

"Taste it all you want! This is..."

1 second.

"The worst disease I've created...!"

At that moment, brilliant golden smoke enveloped the bomb, and Paimon's eyes widened in shock.

0 seconds.

'Miracle Stream!'

If it had exploded, the process would have been too fast to register.

"Ah..."

Everyone in the sky could see the massive energy vibration trapped in the golden smoke.

The Miracle Stream, transcending time with quantum signals, transformed into Shirone's form.

"Wow."

Suppressing the explosion between his palms, Shirone was purely amazed.

'The particle motion is incredible.'

Of course, it wasn't the first time he had felt such a large-scale energy in the history of the universe.

'Humans made this?'

Iruki had proven that human knowledge could reach the level of destruction.

"Shirone..."

Nade and Miro's group, floating 200 meters in the air, were stunned to see Shirone.

But even more astonishing was...

"This is impossible."

He was containing energy that could devastate kilometers in an instant within a 30-centimeter area.

'Converting energy.'

Just as wind turns a windmill to generate electricity, Shirone converted the particles' kinetic energy into Hexa.

'Any energy can be deadly, but...'

Shirone knew the only energy that couldn't be combined with photon signals.

'Yahweh's ultimate state.'

Agape.

The particles bubbled like foam and exploded in the sky like primordial light.

"Kiiiii!"

As Paimon's arms and legs melted in the light, Miro's group also turned their heads.

'In the end, we couldn't stop it.'

At that moment, they realized that if the bomb had exploded, they wouldn't even be able to think this.

"What?"

When Miro's eyes snapped open, an overwhelming number of screams erupted from the ground.

"Ahhh! It hurts! It hurts!"

The citizens of Bashka, lying on the ground, heard screams erupting from all directions.

"Accursed Yahweh! Damn Yahweh...! Ugh!"

As the tens of thousands of voices filling the area simultaneously evaporated, silence fell.

"Are we... alive?"

People straightened up and looked around, but everything was bathed in light.

"Ah..."

Feeling a warmth in their hearts, they all closed their eyes as if on cue.

'It's warm.'

It was the warmth of Yahweh's heart, Agape.
