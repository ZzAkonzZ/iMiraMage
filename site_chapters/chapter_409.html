Chapter 409:

Magic Association.

Sakiri, an investigator from the Inspection Division 3 who had previously interrogated Kanis and Arin, was heading to the special criminal detention center in the basement of the association.

Accompanying him was Blenner, the Deputy Director of the National Intelligence Service's Third Division.

The Third Division of the National Intelligence Service, known as the Grand Heaven Information Management Division, is a secretive organization composed solely of black agents whose ranks are not disclosed to civilians.

The reason the head of such a department personally visited the association was to obtain something to thwart Gaold's plan.

It was the confession of the third-class grand mage, Isabel.

"Gaold is a problem, a real problem. He's a disgrace to the kingdom. I never liked him from the start. He made everyone tired and then slipped away on his own."

Sakiri wasn't fooled by Blenner's friendly demeanor.

Everyone in the Inspection Division knew that the interim Magic Association president, Lupist, had lobbied Blenner.

"Isabel is really tough too. She looks so pretty, tsk tsk. That's why mages are no good."

It's never pleasant to hear a non-mage badmouth mages, especially when the target is a third-class grand mage you respect.

But Sakiri didn't show his emotions. He could easily suppress his feelings when necessary.

"I've heard you're the best in the field. Do your best. I'll handle the aftermath. Just make sure to get her to talk."

"It's not easy. Even if she's not a combatant, she's still a grand mage. She won't lie out of fear of pain."

"Haha, that's why I called you. They say no criminal can escape the clutches of Sakiri from Inspection Division 3."

"I'll do my best."

Sakiri answered nonchalantly, lost in thought.

Interim president Lupist had long seen Gaold as a thorn in his side. He would love to use the association's full power to eliminate Gaold.

'But things didn't go as planned.'

He had seized the opportunity to fulfill his long-held wish, but the problem was that Gaold's reputation within the association wasn't as bad as expected.

A madman, but pure because of it, and stronger than anyone despite his nasty temper.

This was clearly outside Lupist's calculations.

Isabel's life-risking defense of Gaold at the National Emergency Hearing left many association mages feeling uneasy.

A non-Gaold loyalist had vouched for Gaold's innocence.

With the endorsement of a third-class grand mage like Isabel, the basis for judgment was shaken, regardless of the truth.

Lupist, with his goal in sight, became desperate.

To officially become the association president, he needed a reason to mobilize the association's full power, so he reached out to Blenner, the Deputy Director of the National Intelligence Service.

'This is the conclusion I've reached. But...'

Questions remained.

Forcing a certified mage to perjure and torturing them is dangerous, even for the National Intelligence Service. How did they get approval?

'Who's behind this?'

Sakiri subtly probed Blenner's intentions.

"How far should the confession go? Is it enough to say Isabel was a Gaold loyalist and that's why she defended him?"

"Hmm, it should be a bit stronger. How about saying she was the mastermind behind the assassination attempt on His Majesty? Whether she planned it or funded it, that's up to you."

'They're planning to completely ruin her.'

No one should know what happens in the basement today. They plan to cut off all loose ends.

That's why Sakiri was to handle the interrogation alone, without any observers.

"As long as she doesn't die, do whatever it takes to get the confession. I've set up recording devices. After that, bring me Obscura B. And you'll get a promotion."

'So that's it.'

The final puzzle piece fell into place in Sakiri's mind.

The torture of the beautiful grand mage would be recorded in full. What better snuff film for the old politicians in their decadent social clubs?

That's all it was.

Outside, there were assassination attempts on the king, changes in the Magic Association presidency, and the world turning upside down, but some people were just indulging in their hobbies.

'Perhaps this is also a very human thing.'

Sakiri understood.

If humans have the ability to do something, they will do it.

When they arrived at the special criminal detention center, Sakiri fixed his gaze on the iron door.

Blenner, aware of the situation, showed no interest in what was inside.

"Then I'll leave it to you. Handle it quickly. We need the confession within 48 hours."

48 hours.

"Don't worry. This is what got me this far."

Blenner patted Sakiri's shoulder reassuringly and turned away.

Only when the sound of footsteps faded did Sakiri touch the security device on the iron door.

Entering the spirit zone, the lock read his personal information and clicked open.

Pulling the half-open door, he entered to find Isabel sitting on a chair against the dark prison wall.

Always maintaining a neat appearance and taking pride in it, she had been a model employee of the Magic Association.

But now, with disheveled hair covering her face and her wrists in magic control cuffs on her lap, she was a pitiful criminal.

Three recording devices were set up: two on the sides and one away from the door. Only the one in front blinked red, recording Isabel's state for days.

"Meeting in a place like this feels strange."

Isabel didn't respond.

She just sat there, slumped like a corpse, repeating hollow breaths.

Sakiri ignored it and unbuttoned his sleeves, checking the recording devices.

"Do you know what's the scariest thing I've realized in life? Humans are given infinite freedom. It's true. No matter how absurd, humans can do anything."

Confirming the left recording device was off, Sakiri moved to the right wall.

"Is it impossible to strangle a child you love dearly? If you think so, isn't that a foolish belief? No matter how much you love them, you can just do it. That's human freedom."

After checking the second device, Sakiri looked into the lens of the last active device in front.

"Reset Obscura B for record storage."

Pressing the button, the device shut down, preparing to restart.

Only after all three recording devices were off did Isabel lift her head. Her eyes, devoid of hope, met Sakiri's face.

"Come to think of it, tomorrow is the anniversary of my first son's death."

Sakiri, now a father of two daughters, had once had a son. But he had died at two from Trice disease, which rotted his body.

Sakiri was an irregular eater.

"Well, that's life. Shall we begin?"

Flipping a switch on the wall, the ceiling lights turned on, making the prison as bright as day.

Thud! Thud! Thud! Thud!

The walls opened in succession, expanding the prison to the length of the corridor.

Every conceivable torture device and tool was there.

"Most torture rooms are dark, but I prefer bright ones. Torture is a form of communication. Seeing your body get damaged might feel dirty, but just give up."

Sakiri picked up scissors to cut the prison uniform and checked the blade.

"Where should we start? What's your weakness? Pain? Foreign objects? Fear? Body modification? Shame?"

Isabel spoke calmly.

"You're kind, Sakiri."

"Take it as an insult. There's no point provoking me before we start. Water torture is a good start. It's better to be clean for the show."

"Otherwise... why are you conflicted?"

Sakiri's hand, playing with the scissors, stopped.

"Conflicted?"

"You should know. No matter what you do to me, I won't retract my statement from the hearing. But your irregular eating 'Scales of Truth' is an exception. I don't know the activation conditions, but there's no need to waste time with this charade."

Sakiri, staring at the scissors, sighed and turned around.

"Hah, you and everyone else just talk."

Clang!

The scissors fell back into place with a clatter.

Standing still, Sakiri slowly turned to look down at Isabel.

"Why did you do it?"

Isabel tilted her head slightly, as if she didn't understand.

"Why Gaold? You're not even a loyalist. Gaold and Lupist both use the association for personal gain. Lupist seeks power, Gaold seeks revenge. So why defend Gaold?"

Isabel's answer was simple.

"I don't know."

"You don't know?"

"Sakiri, I don't know. It wasn't a thought-out decision. Yes, as you said, Gaold and Lupist are both self-serving. But the reason I chose Gaold..."

Isabel thought for a moment and then gave a bitter smile.

"I just liked Gaold a bit more than Lupist. That's all."

"......"

Sakiri's jaw twitched.

Turning back to the shelf of torture tools, his face twisted in pain.

'Damn it. There was such an easy way.'

When did the name 'mage' become synonymous with a politician's dog?

Are there no more iron-willed intellects fighting for what they believe is right in the association?

"......48 hours."

Sakiri picked up a 20-centimeter-long tool that looked like an awl.

"In 48 hours, Lupist will move the association, ignoring public opinion. If you perjure yourself, the time will be shorter."

Isabel hadn't considered that situation.

"48 hours. That's quite tight."

"That's how anxious Lupist is. He wants to become the association president with the support of the majority of the members, but as long as Gaold is alive, even his current position is unstable. In other words, the longer you hold out, the more time Gaold gains."

Sakiri pricked the awl with his fingertip and then turned his cold gaze back to Isabel.

"I'll preserve your last bit of dignity as a human and as a woman. It's not because I have any good feelings towards you. I just don't want to cater to the perverted tastes of old men. But the pain will be considerable."

Isabel didn't care. She just stared at the ceiling, lost in thought, forgetting the terrible time ahead.

'Haha, Gaold. How many people are you really inconveniencing?'

Yes, why?

Why did she defend Gaold, who had nothing to do with her?

'Maybe I, too, am captivated by your madness.'

The recording devices restarted, and the lights came on.

Sakiri activated the remaining two devices and stood where the three focuses converged, announcing the official start of the interrogation.

"From now on, we will begin the interrogation of former Magic Association librarian Isabel."

For the first time, Isabel's eyes showed a spark of life.

'Go, Gaold. Transcend good and evil, surpass the future of humanity, and reach the ultimate state that humans can achieve.'

Sakiri grabbed Isabel's hair and yanked her head back violently.

His eyes widened as he looked at her delicate neck.

'From now on, 48 hours. Endure.'

Screams echoed through the detention center corridor.
