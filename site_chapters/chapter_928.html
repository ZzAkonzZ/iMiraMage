Chapter 928: The Sin That Couldn’t Continue (4)

Soft light gathered above the unconscious Ikael's head, forming a sphere.

As her halo returned to its original form, her eyelids mechanically lifted.

'Where is this?'

The first thing that came into view was Satiel's tearful face.

"Archangel, are you alright?"

Ikael, as if defying gravity, straightened her upper body and looked around.

It was the room of the Archangel.

"What happened?"

"Well..."

Satiel explained what had happened after Ikael lost consciousness.

"That's how it concluded... The war has been postponed. Kariel isolated the Gaia people with a Metagate. It seems fine for now, but..."

The atmosphere in Heaven was still tense.

"I see."

"What happened, Archangel? What occurred during the meeting?"

Ikael fell into thought instead of answering.

'No one knows yet.'

No one knew that she had opened the sanctuary door on her own or that she had disobeyed orders to stop the angels.

The lack of response from Anke Ra meant...

'He plans to cover it up.'

The cry of the Gaia people before she lost consciousness resurfaced in her memory.

-What kind of god retracts his own words!

"Sigh."

Feeling a headache, Ikael touched her forehead.

"I need to meet Anke Ra."

As she stood up and headed for the door, Satiel shouted.

"Orders have been given for all angels to stand by! No one can approach the sanctuary!"

"It's alright."

Ikael opened the door.

"He will be waiting for me."

Flying into the sky of the Archangel's domain, Ikael headed straight for the spire of Aravot.

Upon reaching the sanctuary, she hesitated at the door she had opened with her own will.

Where did she find such courage?

'No, it was my choice.'

The heart can choose anything, but one must also take responsibility for those choices.

"Anke Ra, it's Ikael."

Receiving no response, she reached out to the door.

Just before her fingertips touched it, the door creaked and slowly moved inward.

Seeing Anke Ra pulsating as usual, Ikael took a deep breath.

Goffin was still caught in Anke Ra's nerves, and her heart ached again.

"Come in, my daughter."

As Ikael stepped forward, the door closed.

Rumble!

Though she had heard the sound countless times, it now felt like it was blocking her retreat.

Ikael spoke.

"Why..."

"Are you wondering why I didn't punish you?"

"Yes. And I want to know exactly what happened during the meeting with Goffin."

Anke Ra pushed Goffin's body towards Ikael.

To keep her halo from shaking, she had to muster her utmost concentration.

"Goffin made me an offer. To see who is worthy of becoming a god. Betting everything on it."

Startled, Ikael looked up at Goffin.

His face, devoid of soul, emitted light only from his pupils, and she couldn't hide her expression.

"Ikael."

Anke Ra asked.

"Did I do something wrong?"

Hearing those words, Ikael felt like her mind was plummeting into an abyss.

God does not make mistakes.

"...Anke Ra."

"The world simply exists."

The grotesque sound she had heard before was now a grand voice without any distortion.

"There is no right or wrong. I exist perfectly, and I maintain that endlessly. Anke Ra judges only for the world."

Ikael listened silently.

"My daughter, everything in this world is under my definition, but you are the most special."

"I understand."

"Not just because you were the first." Ikael waited curiously for the next words.

"When I didn't know my existence, a powerful light exploded within me. Amplification. That was you. A concept on the boundary between non-existence and existence. That's why you are different from other angels."

"Different?"

"At the moment of amplification, the world split into two. The world of non-existence and existence. You are the purest angel in the universe because another you took all your impurities."

It was the first time she had heard this.

"Satan Lucifer. The antithesis of the law. Born at the same time as you, but took on eternal darkness in your place, an entity of the other side. In a sense, you could call him your twin."

Ikael's halo shook violently.

"Where is he now..."

"He's gone. He purified himself and disappeared in the other world. Only the concept of Satan remains now."

"Why?"

Anke Ra lifted Goffin high.

"Lucifer made a deal with Goffin. To protect you, Ikael, Goffin agreed to purify himself in his place."

"Wh-what... No. That can't be..."

Shaking her head in denial, a very old memory flashed through Ikael's mind.

'So that's why he...'

There was a time when he flew in and took a photon cannon aimed at Ikael with his back.

'No.'

Even the first kiss that made her realize her feelings.

'It can't be!'

Even the countless interactions with Goffin during fierce battles with the Gaia people.

"It wasn't... me."

Tears of light flowed down Ikael's cheeks.

What had she been expecting?

'How happy I was.'

When Goffin said he would stay in Heaven, her heart had swelled uncontrollably.

"It was just an illusion, Ikael."

Anke Ra's nerve endings approached and wiped the tears from her cheeks.

"The heart is just an illusion created by the system. Goffin was an error trying to destroy the world. Do you wonder why I didn't punish you?"

Ikael felt Anke Ra's touch.

"Because there was no need. You were simply infected by the error called Goffin."

"My God, I..."

"Do not fear. I have defined this world. And you are the beginning of that definition, the entirety of my thoughts."

If Anke Ra was the brain, Ikael was the thought.

"Yes, I couldn't swallow Hexa. But look. Even now, I control Goffin with my power. Perhaps this is perfection. Isolating the error created by the system with the system. And now the law is stable again."

Goffin's body approached Ikael once more.

"My beloved daughter. If I were deceiving you, I wouldn't be a god. But if I am a god..."

Anke Ra spoke firmly.

"This is right."

Anke Ra does not deceive.

Because he is cold.

"The Ultima System will soon be dismantled. I will give you humans. Humans who will not fear, who will not be confused, who will only praise you."

"Sob...sob..."

Kneeling with her head on the ground, Ikael's shoulders shook incessantly.

Satiel, who had been waiting for Ikael to return to the Archangel's domain, quickly flew over when she saw her.

"Archangel!"

Though she flinched at Ikael's dazed face, her concern for Goffin was greater.

"Did you meet Anke Ra? Why did this happen? What happened to Goffin?"

At the mention of Goffin's name, Ikael's face twisted in anger.

"Do not mention his name in front of me."

"What?"

"He bared his fangs at Anke Ra under the guise of a meeting. He's a fraud, nothing more, nothing less."

There was no lie in Ikael's words.

'And he humiliated me too.'

He had planned to settle things with Anke Ra but hadn't even hinted at it to her.

'He used me. I was never in his heart from the beginning.'

"But Archangel, Goffin..."

"Satiel."

Seeing Ikael's terrifying gaze, Satiel involuntarily closed her mouth.

"Don't make me say it twice."

As Ikael moved towards her room, Satiel slowly made way.

"No one is to approach the sanctuary of Aravot. This is an order from the Archangel."

Anke Ra instructed the angels to find a way to dismantle the Ultima System.

Kariel found the solution.

"The Elixir of Life. We will integrate the Gaia people. After minor purification, there will be more than enough Black Elixir. It's just difficult to extract. But if we send angels, it won't be a problem."

Uriel asked.

"Integration? Won't that just make the enemy stronger?"

"Simple-minded. The core of the Ultima System is that various personalities pursue one righteousness. We haven't been able to destroy that one. So we will collapse the various personalities."

Satiel slammed the table of the White Mirror and stood up.

"I oppose this! We can't annihilate the Gaia people without knowing Goffin's fate! All procedures must be transparently disclosed before proceeding!"

"There's no need to worry. The giant created by the Elixir of Life will be separated back into humans. This is the only way to remove the Ultima System."

"How is that different from murder?"

"You don't understand. It's not murder. Humanity will thrive more than before. Under our control."

Unable to hold back, Satiel looked at Ikael.

"Archangel! Please say something!"

The angels' gazes focused on Ikael, but no answer came.

She just stared blankly ahead, a now familiar sight in the White Mirror.

Though many years had passed since the meeting, her condition hadn't improved at all.

"...Let's end it for today."

This too was a familiar phrase.

"Sigh."

Ikael lay in bed, tossing and turning all night.

'Why is this happening? Everything is over. The world is stable, and I am the Archangel.'

She had thought that time would heal her, but instead, her inner turmoil grew each night.

'You used me! Liar!' Even as anger surged, making her tremble.

'Could it really be true?'

She began to doubt if there might have been another reason.

'Was it all a lie? All of it?' Having known him for so long, even an angel's reason couldn't believe it.

"Goffin..."

The moment she uttered his name, a wave of emotion surged over her.

"Sob!"

Groaning, Ikael rose and flew towards the spire of Aravot.

Panting, she glared at the door upon reaching the sanctuary.

'Goffin is in there.'

It was a distance she could cover in a second at her speed.

How many times had she come here, unable to resist her impulsive emotions? Ikael reached out desperately.

'I want to see him. No, even if I could just see his face. Just once, one last time...'

Then she felt she could forget everything.

-The heart is an illusion.

As Anke Ra's words came to mind, Ikael bit her lip and hastily withdrew her hand.

Clenching her fists and closing her eyes tightly, she calmed herself and looked at the sanctuary door.

It was as quiet as if no one lived there.

'It's over.'

With a sad expression, she turned, spread her wings of light, and returned to her quarters.

'Everything is over.'

Another night was passing.
