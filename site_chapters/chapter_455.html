Chapter 455:

“Capture him!”

The remaining 18 members of Cage B Team moved even faster.

After killing the carnivore addict Benifis, Gaold targeted the crowd control mage Black and the manipulator Jonah Bang.

There was no way to eliminate Gaold through sheer force.

They aimed to use non-physical magic to create variables, and Gaold was steadily reducing those possibilities.

‘Amplification mage…’

Gaold aimed at the word mage Mami.

As hundreds of air gun shots targeted her per second, a bluish barrier appeared in front of her.

“Grrr!”

Rider, known for his iron defense magic, was shaken to the point of breaking his Spirit Zone.

Rose surveyed the battlefield.

Three members were already dead. If Cage B Team's power dropped below half, the probability of eliminating Gaold would approach zero.

‘We need to take a gamble. At this rate, we'll fail.’

Rose shouted,

“Activate the final plan!”

The remaining members' eyes gleamed.

From now on, the order of death didn't matter.

Metal mage Roche took the lead, and Rider deployed a barrier.

Torrent mage Mongrang transformed water into a dragon and hurled it at Gaold.

“Foolish.”

Gaold snorted.

No matter what attack they tried, one air press would crush everything to the ground.

At that moment, flowers began to bloom on Gaold's body. It was the magic of White, another crowd control mage, along with the already dead Black.

Facing his brother's death, White had already given up on life. He knew revenge was impossible.

‘But you will die.’

A spell solely for that purpose.

White's spirit infiltrated the flowers and directly attacked Gaold's mind.

White collapsed, and his spirit, surpassing the brink of death, made Gaold flinch.

“Grrr!”

As Gaold's extreme endurance activated, the Great Inferno enveloped the space.

No one was surprised by the screams of the hungry ghosts, but White's flowers turned to black ash and disappeared beyond the horizon of hell.

‘Ah, my insignificant life…’

Aroella of the Flame charged at Gaold with unprecedented flames in her hands.

‘Never had a great love.’

She had given money, body, and heart, but in the end, all the men left.

‘But… it was hot, wasn't it?’

Hellfire Infinity!

A whirlwind of scorching flames raged within the Great Inferno. At the same time, Gaold, with a twisted face, crushed the flames with an air press and charged.

Thinking of the smiling face of the last man she dated, Aroella smiled.

A sharp wind passed, and her head fell from her neck.

Gaold smiled back at the smiling Aroella.

‘Nice eyes. A great mage.’

Whether his sincerity was conveyed, Aroella, now headless, smiled brightly as she faded away.

Her body, without a head, rushed at Gaold and exploded.

Even the massive explosion, reaching 6,000 degrees Celsius, was crushed by Gaold's atmospheric pressure.

‘Success.’

Rose's trembling lips curled into a smile.

Gaold felt it too. Scale mage and irregular Monoros had stuck a sticker on the back of Gaold's neck.

Irregular - Battle Arena.

A small arena, one meter in diameter, formed under his feet, and Gaold's body shrank to enter it.

‘A scale type.’

To others, it looked like a miniature in a toy, but to the shrunken Gaold, it was a grand and elaborate arena.

Monoros's irregular ability to scale down his opponent to fit a specific space ratio was powerful because it allowed him to control the environment as he wished.

‘How to escape?’

Gaold looked around the arena, pondering an escape method.

With such a strong scale adjustment, external forces wouldn't affect it.

But if Monoros wasn't an idiot, he wouldn't have designed the arena to allow internal physical forces to affect the outside.

‘Time limit, or steps. Without those, this environment couldn't exist.’

“The way to escape the Battle Arena is through steps.”

As everyone looked down at Gaold in the arena, Monoros spoke.

“Defeat the three monsters that appear in the arena, and it will be released.”

Tarvan said,

“But it's the same scale, right?”

Monoros shook his head.

“There are two ways to enter the arena. One is to shrink the scale with a sticker. The other is…”

Monoros rummaged through the bag at his side.

A giant octopus clung to his hand, wriggling.

“A creature that can fit in the arena can be directly inserted.”

“…I see.”

As Tarvan finished speaking, Monoros dropped the octopus.

Gaold saw a large, wriggling gray object fall from the sky.

‘A Voctopus.’

A species from the Mediterranean coast, known for its ferocity and insatiable appetite, attacking even sharks larger than itself.

The Voctopus, having starved for two days, swung its sharp, spiked legs at Gaold as soon as it saw him.

Gaold sharply expanded the air, cutting off the legs, and charged.

The immense air press crushed the arena, but only Gaold felt that way.

The Voctopus, momentarily crumpled, swung its remaining legs as if unaffected.

“Ha ha, what a joke…”

With the scale reduced, the power of magic had also decreased exponentially.

But Gaold didn't worry about the decline in his condition. He didn't know his limits.

Pain Amplification 1,000,000 times - Air Pressing.

Boom!

The octopus was flattened and eventually exploded, unable to withstand the pressure.

Amidst the swirling innards, Gaold frowned.

Using the power he had wielded against Hel on a mere octopus was humiliating, but he had no time to think about that as extreme pain wracked his body.

Cage B Team, watching Gaold's battle, remained silent.

Monoros explained the next strategy.

“Now you see. Even the great Gaold is reduced to struggling against an octopus when scaled down.”

“So what now?”

“For the second step, we use this.”

Monoros opened the bag on his back. Hundreds of crickets swarmed inside.

Deputy leader Horkin nodded in understanding.

“If it can fit in the arena, numbers don't matter?”

“Correct. Reduce the power with scale, and disrupt with sheer numbers. The final step…”

“I'll handle it.”

Tarvan stepped forward.

The most powerful terminator among the team.

But Monoros shook his head.

“You need a sticker to enter. If you shrink to the same scale, the Kaiser Blast will also weaken.”

“I'll do it.”

Another irregular, Porcol, stepped forward.

To deliver enough power to kill Gaold at the same scale, an irregular ability was the only option.

Monoros agreed. With Porcol's irregular ability, even the great Gaold wouldn't stand a chance.

“Then let's begin.”

Hundreds of crickets were poured into the arena. Monoros placed a sticker on Porcol, shrinking him and sending him into the arena.

Irregular - Fantasista.

His body began to glow brightly.

As a few crickets collided with him, powerful explosions shattered them like shells.

The equivalent exchange of aging and explosions.

He could produce 100 kilobuster explosions at the cost of one hour of aging.

Though Porcol appeared in his early forties, he was actually thirty-two.

As time passed, his lifespan would shorten, but irregulars could only find happiness within their trauma.

“What is this?”

Gaold mercilessly destroyed the swarming crickets.

A foul smell filled the air, and grotesque objects scattered, but his eyes remained fixed ahead.

‘Trying to hide behind this…’

Cage B Team knew what kind of person Mikael Gaold, the former head of the Magic Association, was.

The insects were a distraction. The truth was hidden among the numbers and monstrosities.

Irregular - Life is Short and Intense.

Whoooosh!

Porcol accelerated all the aging his body could handle.

His face rapidly aged, becoming emaciated, and finally, a skeletal figure flew at Gaold.

Facing death, his twisted mind, like a greedy old man envying unattainable youth, was filled with madness.

“Ha ha ha ha! I, killing Gaold!”

Even the terrifying sight of the greedy skeleton didn't faze Gaold.

No, he was tense, facing the twisted thoughts of an irregular who had staked his entire life.

Pain Amplification 1,000,000 times.

As Gaold gritted his teeth and cast his spell, Porcol charged at him.

“Damn it!”

As Cage B Team scattered, the arena exploded, creating a mushroom cloud.

Even with the scale drastically reduced, the power was unimaginable.

“That crazy bastard, how long did he plan to live?”

“Irregulars are always crazy. But still…”

Tarvan stretched his shoulders and approached again.

As the step completed and reality returned, Gaold stood amidst the smoke.

Anyone who knew Gaold wouldn't believe the sight.

His shirt was completely blown off, revealing a muscular, scarred body, with his dislocated right shoulder hanging limp.

His hair had turned white, and blood flowed from various wounds.

Tarvan felt like applauding sincerely.

How many people in the world could withstand the equivalent exchange of life cast by an irregular?

“Impressive. Honestly, I respect you.”

“Now…”

Gaold spoke with difficulty.

“Seven.”

The number of Cage B Team members who had died.

“No, six.”

As Tarvan spoke, a thud was heard. Irregular Monoros fell to the ground, his head severed.

Even if everyone else had avoided the explosion, the irregular caster couldn't move far.

But even he hadn't expected Gaold to counterattack without losing his composure after the explosion.

‘A monster is still a monster.’

“Don't waver.”

Rose approached Gaold.

“He's already critically injured. Even with fewer numbers, we still have the upper hand.”

Rose's eyes were as resolute as Gaold's.

Killing Gaold was her mission as a mage affiliated with the association.

“Just give up. If you surrender, I guarantee your life will be spared.”

“Ha, that's an enticing offer.”

Gaold knew it was a lie.

Rose was the type of woman who would say anything to complete her mission.

But that also meant Cage B Team wasn't entirely confident in their success.

Nothing had changed. He just needed to keep fighting his battle.

“What else do you have? Didn't you mention something about a final plan?”

Rose was at a loss.

They still had utility, amplification, curse, and defense mages, but they had lost many of their dealers.

‘If we lose even one more dealer, our chances will be slim. Even if it means abandoning command, I must die.’

So, there was likely only one more chance for a tactical attempt.

Rose bought time to devise a solid plan.

“Human hearts are truly fickle. When immersed in something, they can't see anything else.”

Gaold, needing time as well, responded with silence.

“But just as quickly, they can change. Why not let go? Your feelings are nothing more than a childish infatuation for something you can't have. Let it go and move on.”

“Maybe you're right.”

Gaold spoke slowly.

“But why should I let it go?”

“Well…”

“Rose.”

Gaold interrupted.

“You must have truly loved someone once.”

Rose thought for a moment and then said,

“Yes.”

“Then tell me. As you've grown older and less childish, have you ever felt anything more beautiful than that feeling?”

Rose couldn't answer.

“There is nothing childish about being sincere. There are only those who endure the pain and those who run away from it. And I…”

Rumble!

The atmosphere vibrated, the forest shook, and the air grew as heavy as steel.

Gaold, his face twisted into a demonic visage, glared at the world.

“I never run away from my pain.”
