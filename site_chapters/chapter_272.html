Chapter 272: Hostile Friendly Relations (1)

The morning dawned on Kazra Royal Palace.

Shirone waited in a designated waiting room provided by the inspection department for his paternity test.

While it wasn’t completely off-limits for visitors, no one came to see him, as it was a significant event being watched by the nobles of Kazra.

Once the paternity test was complete, there would be no return to his old life. What would his future hold from now on?

"Huu, I'm getting nervous for no reason."

“There’s no need to worry. It’s just a simple test.”

Shirone lifted his head with a start.

A tall, slender man stood before him. He didn’t give off a cold impression, but there was a sharpness in his presence that felt unsettling.

A faint chill ran down Shirone’s spine, similar to what he had felt when he first met Armin.

“No way, Unlocker?”

The man responded with a polite bow instead of words.

“My apologies for the late introduction. I am Arius. I serve as an advisor to His Majesty, Orkamph.”

Shirone narrowed his eyes and heightened his guard.

“What brings someone like you here?”

The waiting room was an enclosed space by human standards. Being alone with a master who had infiltrated such a place without anyone knowing was by no means a safe situation.

“I simply wanted to meet you. There’s a certain connection between Unlockers, after all.”

Arius let his presence radiate freely as if to show Shirone that he understood the tension Shirone felt from their resonance.

“I’ve heard plenty about your achievements, Shirone. Especially Photon Cannon—it left quite the impression. I assume it’s an omniscience that manipulates mass?”

Shirone's tension heightened. If Arius was an enemy, the fact that he had already analyzed his abilities was not good.

Arius raised both hands to show that he had no hostile intentions.

“Don’t be so on edge. It’s merely pure interest. You could say I’m a fan. By the way, as for my abilities...”

“Scale Magic, right?”

“Hooh? What makes you think that?”

“This room is sealed on all six sides. You couldn’t have entered with just spatial movement magic. To distort both time and space, it has to be Scale Magic.”

Arius admitted readily. It wasn’t exactly a secret among the higher circles of mages.

“Correct. More than half of Unlockers are Scale Mages. Did you know that?”

“No, I didn’t know that.”

It was the first time Shirone had heard this. More than half of Unlockers were Scale Mages.

Then again, Armin’s magic could also be considered a form of Scale Magic. After all, time and space are inseparable.

“The reason so many Unlockers specialize in Scale Magic isn’t fully understood. It’s believed to be linked to the nature of Immortal Function. In that sense, Shirone, your omniscience is quite unique. The ability to manipulate mass is extremely rare. I must admit, I’m envious.”

“No, it’s not that impressive…”

Armin suddenly leaned in, interrupting Shirone.

“Still, I’m stronger.”

It was a childish remark coming from such a high-level mage. However, Arius didn’t mind and added casually.

“I want to make sure of this.”

“Ah, of course.”

Shirone didn’t bother to deny it. Arius was, after all, someone employed by a king—a senior far beyond a mere magic academy student.

Then Arius tilted his head as if surprised.

“Huh? Aren’t you acknowledging it a bit too easily? Too much humility can be harmful, you know.”

“Yes? It’s not like that. I’m just a student.”

"Of course, your magical power may still be lacking. But who knows what will happen if you cast Ataraxia?"

Shirone was taken aback. Ataraxia was a term from Heaven. Only a few close to him knew about it, and in academic papers, Killain had referred to it merely as a "magical amplification circle."

‘This person has been to Heaven too.’

Arius stared intently into Shirone's eyes. As if trying to find a little fairy living inside his pupils. The corners of his mouth curled up slightly after confirming that the keyword had been properly imprinted.

“How do you know about Ataraxia?”

“Haha! Most mages who’ve been around long enough know about it. It’s the ultimate technique of Archangel Ikael. Of course, I’ll admit, knowing a human could master it was quite shocking.”

“Then have you been to Heaven yourself?”

Arius nodded his head as if it were no big deal.

“Once, a long time ago. But I didn’t find it all that entertaining. Still, I keep collecting information about it—it concerns the survival of humanity, after all. We all owe a debt to Miro, don’t we? Then again, I suppose she’s not too bitter about it, since she became the greatest mage because of it.”

Shirone, who had only thought of Miro's choice as a sacrifice, found Arius's words refreshing.

“Miro’s the greatest Unlocker?”

Arius gave a self-deprecating smile.

“Well, wouldn’t you say so? Honestly, who can really say who’s the best? But she did save humanity, and she now resides in a dimension beyond our reach. No mage born in this era will be able to overcome Miro’s fortress. It's unfortunate."

Though his words seemed harsh, they weren’t without truth.

Everyone deserves a chance to pursue their dreams. Otherwise, this world that Miro protected with her sacrifice would already be as good as dead.

“I see. But Miro must have thought the same...”

“Haha! Of course, I’m aware of that! Don’t take it too seriously. If it weren’t for Oberk, the founder of Turn Undead Magic, humanity would have been overrun by necromancers. It’s the nature of magic that the title of ‘greatest’ changes depending on the circumstances. Besides, there already existed in this world the greatest mage that none could surpass.”

“The greatest mage? Who is that?”

Shirone thought of several people's names.

Was it Kergos, who established the theory of photonization? Ibris, the creator of Ignition Magic, Ignite? Perhaps Gloria, who consolidated the Four-Way Zone system in the Spirit Zone?

"The greatest magician in history... McClain Goffin."

Shirone smacked his knee in disappointment.

"Ah! I knew that person!"

Goffin, the mage who established the gateway to Heaven in the autonomous region of Kerugos. How great a mage he was could also be seen from Ikael's words.

“What kind of person was Goffin?”

Arius scratched his head, seemingly unsure of the answer.

“Hmmm, actually, I don’t know. There are no records about Goffin. There's even debate over whether he really existed.”

“Huh? But Goffin….”

“Yes. His achievements are found all over the world. However, what remains are only ruins. There are no historical records of Goffin himself. Even now, archaeologists roam the world to find traces of Goffin, but not a single written record has been discovered. Not even folktale.”

“How is that possible?”

Shirone was puzzled.

Every person, no matter how insignificant, leaves behind traces of their life. Even a nobody without fame leaves records through acquaintances. So, for someone like Guffin, it would only make sense that the whole world should know of him.

“Whether or not Goffin was a real person is still a matter of academic debate. Some scholars argue that he may never have interacted with any intelligent life during his lifetime. However, there is a hypothesis that is currently in the spotlight."

The so-called greatest mage in history disappeared without leaving any record. Shirone thought that perhaps this was a tremendous magic in itself.

Arius raised a finger and continued.

“The hypothesis is that Goffin did exist, but for some reason, the world forgot about him.”

At those words, a memory came to Shirone’s mind. It was something he had heard from Peope, the fairy of Heaven.

Archangel Ikael was said to have committed an unforgivable sin, but the divine subjects of Heaven do not know what sin it was.

Because Anke Ra has erased said memory.

“Goffin definitely existed, but now he exists as someone who never did. This theory is referred to as the ‘Goffin Erasure’ or the ‘Reset’ theory in academic circles.”

“Reset...?”

“Goffin may have simply erased his name from the Akashic Records. But if that were the case, the entire world should have been altered. So why do Goffin’s ruins still exist? The Reset hypothesis provides an explanation. It suggests that the world we experience has been reset at some point.”

To Shirone, who had not yet reached the depths of intellect, Arius's words sounded like the rambling of a madman. Yet, he couldn’t stop himself from becoming absorbed in the topic—an Unlocker’s nature naturally led them to such contemplations.

“One of the most notable pieces of evidence for Goffin Erasure is something called the ‘Fracture Field Verification Experiment.’ It suggests that the total time and energy of the world we live in are ever so slightly misaligned.”

Arius spread his ten fingers, pretending to hold a sphere.

“The Akashic Records are perfect as a whole. Therefore, this world cannot lose its perfection. However, fractures can occur. A fracture is a form of individuality, not a flaw. Scholars believe that traces of Reset are left within those fractures.”

Shirone found it hard to believe. Yet, if many scholars were discussing it seriously, it wasn’t just a baseless tale.

"If reset is true, does that mean everyone in this world is living the same life for the second time?"

“That would be the case. But don’t think too hard about it. Time doesn’t flow; it’s perceived. For example, let’s say humanity began 10,000 years ago. Did the people of today wait 10,000 years to live this moment? No. They didn’t wait even a second. Because time is perceived from the moment of birth. This is why scholars cite déjà vu as evidence of Reset. The feeling of reliving something you’ve already experienced—that’s a cognitive error caused by the brain confusing time before and after the Reset.”

Arius raised his index finger and posed a question.

“Now, here’s the question. How do you think McClain Goffin was able to reset the world?”

A word automatically came to Shirone's mind.

“Immortal Function.”

Arius nodded, seemingly satisfied.

“Exactly. When consciousness expands infinitely, the ego disintegrates. But any phenomenon has a threshold. If one could fully control the Immortal Function, one would reach a state that is both infinite and finite at the same time—a paradoxical state. In other words, you become the whole while retaining the self."

Arius’ explanation triggered a memory of a similar concept in Shirone’s mind. Becoming the whole while retaining the self means, conversely, that the self is present in everything. And people called such a being God.

"I see. So this Goffin..."

Shirone finally realized why McClain Goffin was the greatest mage in the world, unrivaled by anyone else.

“Yes. McClain Goffin….”

Arius smiled as he concluded.

"He’s the Infinite Mage that is unprecedented in the history of mankind."

Though it was the second time Shirone had heard the term “Infinite Mage,” the impact this time was much stronger.

At the time, he was ignorant of Immortal Function or Akashic Records. But now, he had a vague idea of how a human could take the position of God, and the process of it.

“We don’t know exactly which era Goffin belonged to. Was it 100 years ago? A thousand years? Maybe even before the dawn of humanity. However, the most prominent hypothesis places the reset at 18 years ago. Since then, no traces of Goffin’s achievements have surfaced. Coincidentally, 18 years ago is also when Miro’s Space and Time was born.”

Arius mentioned this as a possibility, but Shirone had already heard directly from Ikael that Miro was Goffin’s successor.

Also, 18 years ago… was the year Shirone was born.

However, Shirone didn’t dwell on that. How could be he was the only person born 18 years ago?

Arius checked his watch. The time for the examination was drawing near, and it was time for him to return to his main task.
