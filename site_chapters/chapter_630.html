Chapter 630:

The impact of the Photon Cannon, cast with Ataraxia, concentrating two-thirds of its power on a single point, was beyond what even the 2000 system could handle.

"Aaaargh!"

As Fermi writhed on the ground, everyone stopped fighting and turned to look.

"Fermi…"

He was screaming in agony.

Students and teachers alike, who knew him, were stunned into silence.

"12 million crash?"

In reality, the entire radius would have been devastated. It was fortunate that it was the 2000 system, which could quantify such power.

"It's not just the power. The mass isn't large, but it struck at a speed far exceeding the speed of sound."

While God's Punishment uses high-altitude potential energy, Angel's Punishment creates kinetic energy through the reversal of time and space.

The power itself couldn't compare to God's Punishment, but the precision strike capability using the incarnation and the ultra-fast projectile that couldn't be dodged even with teleportation were beyond the explanation of overpowered.

"Huff, huff."

Shirone, glaring at the trembling Fermi on the ground, panted heavily.

The incarnation was a realm, but Angel's Punishment, being a magic, consumed mental energy.

"The percentage has dropped to 27%."

'No-mind.'

For a moment, the meaning of the world was cut off, and mental energy began to recover through Immortal Function.

"30%. 32%. It's rising again."

Elizabeth was no longer surprised.

"It's a near-perfect mechanism."

It wasn't just about using highly efficient magic; countless functions were endlessly combined as needed.

"Aaaargh!"

As Fermi stood up with a scream, the audience felt a fear akin to reading a horror novel.

"He withstood that?"

Elizabeth reported.

"Mental gauge at 50%. It's magic."

Without equivalent exchange, it was impossible to fill half of the mental gauge.

Depreciation Deal - Devil's Penalty.

By giving up ownership of all the wealth earned through irregular eater, the efficiency of magic could be doubled, but the magic cast carried the risk of contract termination.

"I've changed my mind."

Fermi glared solely at Shirone.

"I'll eliminate you."

Depreciation Deal - Detonation Time.

Rumble!

Dark clouds swirled in the sky, filled with flames, and a blizzard charged with electricity raged.

"Climate Magic Detonation Time. Damn Black Line."

While Red Line's great mages received royal support, Black Line's mages always needed money.

Of course, the great mage Pentaros, who was among the world's 100 most dangerous individuals, wouldn't sell his magic for mere gold coins, but if it interested him, he might lend it for free.

"He made a deal to destroy the balance of the graduation exam. This is a clear attack on the Red Line. The association must protest to Pentaros."

"He didn't break the agreement. He didn't intervene directly. Anyway, that's not the issue. Detonation Time controls all natural phenomena."

Fermi floated like a god, looking down at Shirone.

"Die."

Flames fell with lightning, and sharp hail poured down like a localized storm.

As winds sharper than blades swept through, Shirone's mental gauge began to drop drastically.

'Incredible power. I'll be overwhelmed at this rate.'

It was time to counterattack, but Fermi was hidden in the dark clouds.

"Ha ha ha! How does it feel to be a frog in a well?"

In the face of the ferocious natural phenomena, other participants didn't dare to intervene.

"Annoying. What kind of graduation exam has such magic?"

As Luman said, it was top-tier Black Line magic, and even the teachers couldn't fathom the magic that defied natural order.

"Tremendous overpowered. Fermi won't last long either."

"The percentage is dropping rapidly. At this rate, he'll be exhausted in 5 minutes."

Ignoring the aftermath, Fermi was solely focused on eliminating Shirone.

'I can't give up here!'

As Shirone plunged into the dark clouds of Detonation Time, massive charges clung to him, and electricity flowed through his body.

"To catch a devil, you have to enter hell."

"Shirone's mental energy is depleting rapidly. He'll be exhausted in 3 minutes."

The thunder from the dark clouds echoed through the colosseum.

'I've won, Shirone.'

Fermi looked at Shirone's increasingly pale face with delight.

'Another year? Let's have fun together.'

Even if he was eliminated after this battle, wouldn't hell be fun with Shirone?

"Huuu!"

As Shirone, opening Immortal Function, squeezed out a Photon Cannon with all his might, Fermi quickly retreated.

'Just dodge.'

Then his head spun, and the scenery twisted.

"What…?"

"It's time."

Boyle, sitting in the audience, turned to Pandora.

"Time?"

"Scent Magic, Pleasure of Paralysis. It's a strategy Master Mael taught me. It's colorless and odorless, so you get addicted without knowing. It takes time, though."

"On Fermi?"

"I accumulated it little by little. It was the only way I could fight. I couldn't even reach that level, but…"

Pandora looked up at Detonation Time.

"At least I can bite his heel."

"Grrr!"

As Fermi's nose wrinkled, a Photon Cannon flew towards him.

"Aaaargh!"

As he twisted his upper body, the flash grazed his ear.

'I won't be bitten by a bug!'

As Fermi spread his arms wide, Detonation Time rumbled, and a powerful electric shock struck Shirone.

"It's over!"

That was one second.

'Time Explosion!'

As Shirone approached, spreading countless golden threads, he compressed a single Photon Cannon.

"Damn…!"

Every time Fermi calculated a space to dodge, Shirone's afterimages scattered in all directions, and finally, a flash struck Fermi's abdomen.

"Gah!"

-Participant Number 1 has exited the 2000 system.

Everyone looking up at the dark clouds was shocked.

"Fermi… was eliminated."

As the dark clouds of Detonation Time disappeared and Fermi fell to the ground, everyone who knew him was stunned.

"Fermi's irregular eater was certainly excellent magic."

Alpheas said bitterly, watching his student's last moments.

"But a mage's pride can't be bought with money."

In his fading consciousness, Fermi caught sight of Shirone standing victoriously.

'Incarnation technique.'

And the numerous spells linked to that incarnation.

"That is…"

Fermi's eyes closed.

'No amount of money could buy that.'

With a thud, his body hit the ground.

No one came to his aid; they merely watched his unconscious form indifferently.

"Is he… dead?"

Olivia answered the audience's question.

"The impact sound is smaller than expected. It's the automatic response algorithm."

Alpheas chuckled.

"He's really something."

Even while being so confident, he had considered the possibility of defeat, which was typical of Fermi.

"Ugh!"

Shirone, who had descended to the ground, clutched his chest and trembled.

"He's quite exhausted. Well, it's only natural."

No matter how excellent the system, recovery efficiency inevitably declined.

'Fifteen left. Fermi is out, and Shirone is tired.'

It was the moment when graduation became a reality in everyone's minds.

'Five more. We need to eliminate five more.'

As everyone watched each other, Luman smirked.

'The team is down, but…'

A crowd control technician never needed allies in the first place.

'I control the battlefield!'

Crowd Controller - Ant Nest.

As Luman focused, his flesh quivered, and a massive amount of earth rose from the outskirts of the colosseum, forming a dome.

"That's quite a scale."

"It's an anti-magic maze. It won't be easy to escape."

A crowd control mage stepping forward meant he had complete confidence.

"Damn it! What is he thinking?"

Trapped in the hemispherical maze where even light couldn't enter, the participants cast Shining Magic.

'Heh, clever guy.'

Only Fjord, positioned at the center of the dome, understood the intention and shrugged.

"Trying to use me?"

But it was a brilliant strategy he had no choice but to follow.

"In that case, gladly…"

Fjord's hands emitted green light, drawing a circle.

Mass Destruction Magic - Friars.

From him, a highly toxic gas exploded and raced through the complex tunnels.

"It's poison magic! Most participants' mental gauges are dropping rapidly."

"Is it a matter of who can endure?"

"Aaaargh!"

Though they couldn't see inside the Ant Nest, the screams piercing through the walls made the audience's faces pale.

'Where's the exit?'

In the three-dimensional maze with anti-magic walls, even the Spirit Zone couldn't pinpoint the location.

"Not a bad situation."

Richard, detoxifying the poison entering his body, expelled the gas through a vent in his side, while Pisho wore a large beetle that detoxified the poison like a gas mask and roamed the dark tunnels.

"I'll end it here."

Insect Magic - Parasite.

Half of his right arm turned mushy and transformed into the upper body of an insect resembling a pill bug.

It was a parasite called Argones, passed down by his master, Kayler.

"Pisho!"

Amy emerged from one of the six split tunnels and stopped abruptly.

'Of all people, Pisho.'

Though she blinked her red eyes rapidly, the shock of the poison was depleting her mental energy even faster.

'This is a disadvantage.'

In a confined space, fighting Pisho, who could detoxify the poison, was a losing battle.

'Is Amy the sacrifice?'

If he had to choose, his feelings for her were always more favorable.

'She's always worked so hard.'

It was like the protective feelings of an older brother for his younger sister.

"Unfortunate. Meeting me was bad luck."

"Don't talk nonsense! There's no luck in the graduation exam!"

Seeing Amy's still spirited demeanor eased his conscience a bit.

"I won't be too harsh."

As Pisho raised his right arm, Argones opened its jaws wide and let out a ghastly sound.

'I need to find Maya!'

Cayden frantically ran through the tunnels of the Ant Nest, searching for Maya.

'She won't be able to endure.'

Unlike other participants, Maya, with her weak mental strength, couldn't withstand the shock.

'Wait for me, Maya!'

Steeling his mind against the shock of the poison, Cayden moved even faster.

'This is it.'

Maya, collapsed in the clearing of the Ant Nest, blinked like a dying animal.

She had done her best, but luck had been everything in this pathetic exam.

'I won't be able to attend school anymore.'

She would have to return to the mysterious tribe and start a business to support her siblings.

Then, footsteps echoed, and a silhouette appeared through the poisonous gas.

'I can't even fight.'

"Maya?"

At the sound of the voice, her eyes snapped open.

"Shirone?"

Seeing Shirone's face approaching without hesitation made her heart race.

'No, I've held on for so long…'

Perhaps more precious than life itself.

"Maya, are you okay?"

His gentle voice broke down her defenses.

"Huh, huh."

In the end, Maya, looking like a fool, burst into tears.

"Shirone…"
