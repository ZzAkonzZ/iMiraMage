Chapter 639:

After the personal greetings, Reina, representing the Ogent family, formally clasped her hands and spoke.

"Congratulations, Shirone. I knew you could do it."

"Thank you."

Shirone bowed his head.

Without the Ogent family's support, achieving his dream of becoming a mage would have been much harder.

"Hohoho! No need to thank us. Thanks to you, the Ogent name is now known worldwide. Don't forget us later."

"Of course. I'll visit the head of the family after graduation."

Having secured the top spot, it was clear that various organizations would reach out to him, and wherever he went, Rian would be with him.

"There you are."

Iruki's father, Albino, approached, and Shirone greeted him nervously.

"Hello. I'm Arian Shirone, Iruki's friend."

"Shirone, no need to be so formal. Just think of me as air."

Iruki felt uneasy about Albino's insistence on meeting Shirone before leaving.

"I've heard a lot about you from Iruki. Please continue to look after my son."

"Look after him? We're friends."

Albino extended his hand, and Shirone quickly shook it.

"Once you're out in society, it's not always that simple. Diplomatic issues can arise."

"Ah, that's true, but…"

"Today is a great day. Work hard for the Kingdom of Tormia."

Albino's words were clearly intended to integrate Shirone into their country.

"Why are you deciding his future? Let Shirone make his own choices."

While the magic department handled contracts, as the head of Yongrye, Albino couldn't let such talent slip away to another country.

"It's not your life either. I'd like to hear Shirone's thoughts."

Though it was an awkward situation, Shirone spoke openly.

"Nothing is decided yet. I'll make the best choice for myself."

Iruki exhaled in relief.

"Did you think it would work? Your mind really is rusty."

'That's why you're still far behind, kid.'

Iruki would follow Albino into Yongrye.

As the brain overseeing the kingdom's policies, having Shirone by his side would make a world of difference.

As a father, Albino wanted to secure Shirone next to his son from the start.

'Anyway, if they're friends, there's no need to make it too awkward.'

Setting aside his ambition for now, Albino approached Vincent to ease the tension.

"Congratulations. You've raised a fine son."

With the highest noble showing respect, Vincent was at a loss.

"No, we haven't done anything."

"Of course."

"What?"

As Vincent looked up, Albino smiled kindly.

"The best parents are those who trust and watch over their children."

"Oh, is that so?"

Having eased the atmosphere, Albino turned to Shirone.

"Visit anytime you have the chance. You'll be more helpful than my foolish son."

"Yes. Thank you."

This time, Shirone accepted gladly.

As Albino walked away, Iruki looked back and asked,

"You're coming later, right?"

"Of course. See you at the party."

The tradition of holding a small party for the graduates on the night of the exam continued this year.

"Shirone, when are you going?"

Amy, having sent her family off, approached.

"Now."

Amy walked past Shirone and bowed to Vincent and Olina.

"Hello. How have you been?"

"Well, you know…"

Vincent laughed awkwardly to cover the social gap, while Olina, overcoming her shyness, took Amy's hand.

"You worked hard."

"Hehe, Shirone was there too."

It was a woman's intuition, and Reina, watching, felt a pang of jealousy.

Rian, unconcerned with his sister's feelings, greeted Amy warmly.

"Amy, you were amazing."

"Riaaan."

Rian hugged Amy, lifting her off the ground with a squeeze.

"Ugh!"

Amy playfully screamed as she was lifted, then set down as Cayden approached.

"Are you going to the party?"

While the joy of passing was undeniable, meeting those who didn't pass was painful.

"Yes. Are you coming too?"

Though it's customary for those who didn't pass to avoid the party to not dampen the mood, Cayden saw it as his last chance to confess to Maya.

"No. I don't have the right. But please tell her congratulations."

"If that's the case, come with us. By now, Maya must know."

Though she might not have realized during the exam, looking back, Maya would know Cayden had protected her.

"That's why I won't go. I don't want Maya to be hurt."

"She won't be. Graduating was her deepest wish. She'll have a lot to say to you."

Cayden shook his head.

"I'd rather she forgets."

"Stubborn as always."

Amy sighed, hands on her hips.

"What will you do now? I know it's not my place, but you can pass next year."

Because Maya wouldn't be there.

When you want something desperately, the Red Cross Star can achieve anything.

"No. I'll return to my family. If not for Maya, I would have left school long ago."

"Hmm, so what will you do now?"

"I'll take my time to think. And…"

Cayden looked at Shirone.

"I don't intend to interfere in your life, but Maya did her best for you. Don't hurt her. If you make her cry, I won't forgive you."

"Won't forgive me?"

His last words irked Rian.

"I can't accept that. I don't know the details, but Shirone will handle it."

"If you don't know, stay out of it. Who are you?"

Rian's temple throbbed with anger.

"Rian Ogent. Shirone's sword."

"Shirone's sword?"

Recalling the knight's oath, Cayden finally recognized Rian.

"Oh, Ogent."

Being from a famous swordsmanship family, Cayden had heard things before entering the magic school.

"I heard there's a blue-haired kid in the Ogent family with no talent at all. That must be you."

Even with just a short conversation, Rian realized something.

In life, there are people you dislike for no reason, and this scrawny guy in front of him was going to be one of those people.

Just like his brother, Rai.

"Yeah, I'm that blue-haired kid. But you must not know this: no one who talks about talent in front of me has ever failed to kneel. Want me to make you kneel right here?"

Cayden chuckled.

"Relying on your size…"

Even if it was the Ogent family, the Cross family, the kingdom's top swordsmanship family, couldn't back down.

As Cayden lightly applied a press technique, Rian felt his body twist and glared.

As the technique was countered, a chilling shiver ran down Cayden's spine.

'What is this?'

He had faced many killing intents at the swordsmanship school, but he had never felt his body split in two so clearly.

'This guy… is a ghost.'

It felt like the lives of all those he had cut down were rushing towards him.

'I must have gotten quite dull.'

Conversely, it meant he had been away from the battlefield for too long.

'A sword.'

The Red Cross Star didn't care about the time it took to reach a certain level, so he didn't feel particularly anxious.

"Remember this, Rian Ogent."

As Cayden left, Shirone turned to Rian and said,

"We're going to the graduation party now. Rian, do you want to come? I'll introduce you to my friends."

"No. Enjoy with your school friends today. Come to the Ogent main house after graduation. Your parents will be staying there for a while."

The Ogent family had already understood that external forces might approach their family due to Shirone's graduation.

"Okay, thanks."

Their consideration was warm and their thorough thinking reassuring.

As Rian took his family away in a carriage, Amy grabbed Shirone's arm.

"Let's go too. We need to have fun."

"Yeah, let's go!"

For his first and last graduation exam, Shirone planned to drink until he was truly drunk tonight.

With a bang, seven fingers pressed piano keys.

A mad melody followed, and Prings, eyes rolled back, shook his body as he sang.

"Sweet lover! Oh! My love!"

Beside him, Fjord played the violin while poisoned.

Even the cracking voice and bizarre dissonance were enjoyable as graduates and friends gathered for a drinking party.

"Cheers!"

Shirone, Iruki, and Nade clinked their beer mugs at the table.

Though exhausted from fighting all day, their spirits soared.

"Ah! So refreshing!"

Nade wiped his mouth and looked around the party hall.

"Come to think of it, Maya isn't coming."

Cayden had asked Amy to pass on his message, but Shirone had already sensed it.

'The farewell is over.'

Iruki said,

"Lycain didn't come either."

"His pride must be hurt. He was completely overwhelmed by Nade in the same field."

"That's not the only reason. I thought he'd clash with Fermi but he stayed surprisingly calm."

"Hmm. Anyway, since he passed, it's a win in the end?"

"Not coming to the graduation party means he's taking the Black Line path. It wouldn't benefit him to get involved with us."

"Well, his father is an assassin."

"Hahaha! Hahahaha!"

At that moment, Amy's laughter echoed from the center of the hall.

"Oh? Amy's dancing."

Holding hands with Seriel and spinning around, Amy's face was flushed.

"I passed! Yay! Seriel! I passed!"

"Congratulations! Congratulations!"

The three men stared blankly before slowly looking away from her.

"She seems drunk. Isn't that dangerous?"

"Aren't we all drunk? Leave her be. She can handle herself with her red eyes."

At least for tonight, all actions would be forgiven.

Meanwhile, Dante met Closer and Sabina outside the party hall.

"You're really not coming in?"

"No. I can't break tradition. And… I want to savor it when I'm truly happy. Next year."

Dante fully understood that sentiment.

"Sorry it turned out this way."

Sabina said,

"The right people passed. Now I get it a bit more."

Everyone had potential but small differences determined who passed or failed.

Closer asked Sabina,

"Aren't you going to see Nade? Or at least tell Dante something for him?"

Knowing her feelings for Nade, Sabina still shook her head again.

"No. I can't go to Nade yet."

Just as Nade once did, those staying behind couldn't hold back those moving forward into society.

"Pardon me. May I come through?"

At that moment, Liz asked Dante's group blocking the entrance for permission to pass.

"Oh, sure."

As midnight approached and they stepped aside for her, Dante stared at Liz intently.

She was beautiful and exuded a mature aura not typical of graduates' acquaintances.

"Wow, she's pretty. Whose girlfriend is she?"

Sabina asked but neither Dante nor Closer knew.

'Haa… What should I do? Should I just leave?'

Descending the stairs again made Liz's heart pound harder.

'No matter how I think about it… they'll see me as crazy.'

How would Nade feel if she not only attended the graduation exam but also barged into the party?

Even after hours of contemplation post-exam, Liz couldn't turn back her steps.

Hearing the strange music from inside, she took a deep breath.

'I'll count to ten before deciding again. One. Two.'

Just then, the door suddenly opened, revealing Nade looking back.

"Hahaha! Hey! That was too much! Huh?"

Heading out for fresh air with friends, he saw a familiar face.

'What? Am I drunk?'

Pale-faced Liz raised a stiff hand in greeting.

"Oh, hi, Nade?"
