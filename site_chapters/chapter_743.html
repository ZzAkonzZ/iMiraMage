Chapter 743:

"Such Terrifying Obsession."

The executives of the Ten Elders had overcome the fear of death, so Veron's obsession also had meaning.

"But the opponent is Nane."

Even though the rulers of each country understood the altars, they couldn't act because they had no means to stop them.

"There is no way to stop the Buddha."

"Poor soul."

Veron's iron pole would be an obstacle to the future Nane sought, but there was no resentment in his voice.

"Rather, sympathy."

If pain arises from obsession, Veron's iron pole had reached the limit of human obsession.

"I will save you from your pain."

The cloud, taking Nane's form, clenched its fists and took a stance to charge at Veron.

"Shura, leave."

"No. I will protect you, Chairman."

Veron smirked and shook his head.

"Sometimes, you're bad at lying."

"..."

"You can't miss this opportunity. Haven't you been waiting for this day since you became human without going through reincarnation?"

Veron's jaw swelled like a balloon, and his eyes bulged out like a toad's.

"Only those who kill the Buddha can become the Buddha. Nane's righteousness is only the righteousness of now."

As a long, red tongue like blood shot out from between Veron's lips, Shura's shoulders trembled.

"The Chairman is serious."

As far as she remembered, Veron hadn't attempted anything with all his might for over five thousand years.

"I will kill Nane and become the new Buddha."

"Anyone can become the Buddha."

As Nane's cloud, over two kilometers tall, rushed towards the ground, the vegetation shook violently.

"Chairman!"

As Shura shouted, Veron raised his staff with eyes full of madness.

"This is my ultimate."

When he struck the ground with his staff, the atmosphere shook like a rubber sheet, and a hole was punched through Nane's cloud.

An evacuation alarm rang in Shura's mind.

"It's starting."

Given the scale of the battle, she should have moved at least 100 kilometers away, but her eyes remained fixed on the battlefield.

Nane was not perfect either, so she had to watch the end of this duel.

"Go!"

When Veron struck the ground with his staff a second time, Nane's cloud gathered into a single point in the sky and exploded.

As a massive band spread to the horizon, Veron muttered.

"Is the real one coming?"

As the cloud disappeared, a brilliant light emerged, revealing Nane.

Sermon - Tathagata.

Shura's lips parted in awe.

"It seems to come from a faraway place."

As a circle of light swords spread around Nane, the surroundings became so bright that even the sun was obscured.

Sermon - Immeasurable.

In an instant, the light exploded, and countless light swords, comparable to sunlight, shot towards the ground.

"Uoooooo!"

As Veron swung his staff at a terrifying speed, the light beams rained down like a shower.

Rumble.

A thunderous sound erupted from the ground, and a radius of one kilometer was scorched in an instant.

"Chairman?"

As the thick smoke cleared, a powerful voice rang out.

"Do you call yourself the Buddha with just this?"

As Veron spun his staff, deflecting the light swords, Sermon - Immeasurable caused a scattering of reflections.

"Ugh!"

Shura squeezed her eyes shut, but the light was so bright that she could see the silhouette of the scene through her eyelids.

"He's holding his own against the Tathagata. This might..."

Since no one had become a god yet, Nane's strength was unknown, but he was not immortal.

"Trapped in the self..."

As Nane smiled and spread his arms, a gray sword vibrated above his head.

"How can you realize non-self?"

Sermon - Penetration.

A gray line was drawn from the sky, and the sword that had split Veron's staff was now pressing against his chest.

"Ugh..."

Just before the sword pierced his chest, Veron clasped his hands together, catching the blade.

"Aaaaaaah!"

As the law concentrated between his palms, it stopped the blade's penetration, but his body was pushed back at a terrifying speed.

Boom, boom, boom, boom!

Even as he was pushed back a kilometer, Veron's law was compressing even more strongly.

"I have to destroy it!"

Just as he was about to crush the blade, Nane spun with a vacant expression and formed a seal towards Veron again.

Sermon - Giant.

The gray sword grew endlessly, widening the gap between Veron's palms and accelerating his retreat.

"Aaaaaaah!"

"That is..."

Shirone, observing the ground from the Great Temple, noticed something unusual.

A gray sword rising from a point in the Kingdom of Iron in the southern hemisphere was vibrating at 7.8 times the size of the planet.

"No, it's advancing."

According to his magical senses, it was advancing at a rate of one kilometer per second, albeit slightly.

"Amita is holding up well."

Great Star asked.

"What do you think? You've faced it directly. Could you stop the current sermon?"

"Yes."

In terms of scale, it was roughly similar, which is why the universe hadn't closed yet.

"It's not the entity but the expansion of the entity. It can be defended legally."

"Hmm."

Great Star listened to Shirone's opinion.

"But it's getting stronger endlessly. Eventually, it will reach a level I can't handle."

That was what Great Star wanted to say, but it wasn't the time to speak it aloud yet.

Not alone, not in numbers, but everyone must fight together.

"Even if it's impossible..."

"Huuuuuu!"

At the end of the 18-kilometer scratch carved into the planet by Nane's Sermon - Penetration, Veron grimaced cruelly.

"Can't I stop it?"

A tunnel had formed along his path to the horizon, and flames flickered around from the heat.

"It's coming."

As the blade, now immeasurable in scale, pierced his palms and touched his chest, a searing pain surged through him.

"Is this my end?"

Suddenly, he remembered.

"Why did I... cling to life so much?"

Gurgle, gurgle.

When Veron had no name, he was just a plump, venomous toad.

Gurgle!

He would hop around the pond, occasionally catching flies with his tongue.

"I want to eat. I want to reproduce."

Repeating the same desires every day wasn't a bad life.

"Predator! Predator! Predator! Predator!"

He realized this when a snake, the natural enemy of venomous toads, swallowed him whole.

...

The snake's venom was more potent than the toad's, and in the suffocating stomach, he blinked in terror.

"I'm going to die!"

He couldn't even scream because it was the fate of a creature born that way.

"It's painful. It's suffocating. I'm melting."

Like many venomous toads before him, Veron was destined to become nutrients in the snake's stomach.

"Die?"

Everything that made him would disappear.

Screech, screech!

As he lost consciousness from the paralyzing venom, Veron opened his mouth wide and howled.

"I don't want to die! Not like this... not like this...!"

As the venom's concentration increased, the snake's stomach squeezed harder.

Save me.

Predators are born with all the abilities to digest their prey, but this time it was quite toxic.

The snake writhed violently, trying to digest him, and it might have ended that way.

Sharp teeth pierced the snake's skin and struck Veron's back.

Gurgle, gurgle.

The snake's stomach began to expel Veron, and the mucus prevented his skin from tearing from the fangs.

When Veron looked back after escaping, the snake was being bitten by a wildcat, its natural enemy.

"I'm alive!"

If Veron hadn't struggled, the snake wouldn't have moved, and the wildcat wouldn't have found them.

"I'm alive! I'm alive!"

Veron, who had fled madly and hidden in the depths of the lake, fell into a faint sleep.

Gurgle.

When he woke up again, countless stars were twinkling in the sky.

Looking up at the sky, Veron was seized by a strange feeling.

"Shameful."

The raw emotion, unfiltered by language, enveloped his entire body.

The way he had struggled violently in the snake's stomach, screaming for his life.

"Why is it so embarrassing?"

The answer to the question Veron first thought of contained a profound truth.

"Because it has no meaning."

The sight of himself struggling without a reason to live was so pathetic.

"Meaning. The meaning of my life."

The toad, who had realized his incarnation, gazed at the stars in the sky until dawn that night.

"Is it called obsession?"

As Nane's sword raced endlessly, Veron's body began to be driven into the ground.

"Perhaps it is."

The flesh of his palms was completely torn away, and the blade had pierced from his collarbone to his abdomen.

"But this is my immortality."

Even if his body disappeared, the meaning he left behind would forever support this world.

"Death is life."

Death also becomes life.

"It's not shameful!"

As Veron struggled with all his might, the law encompassing the world concentrated between his palms.

"Go!"

Screech!

Nane's sword twisted violently and shattered from the tip.

"Huuu..."

Barely regaining his balance, Veron took in the apocalyptic scene before him and moved his lips.

"Shura, are you there?"

Shura appeared, tearing through space.

"Chairman, are you alright?"

"Go to Hexa. Do not let the meaning I left in this world be in vain."

"No! You know how much I dislike that kid! What about the Ten Elders?"

As Shura met Veron's gaze, which had let go of everything, she slowly averted her eyes.

"Why, why me...?"

Veron patted Shura's shoulder.

"...Live on."

As she watched Veron collapse, Shura furrowed her brows.

"I don't know how to live forever..."

Hearing footsteps, she turned around to see Nane approaching calmly.

"So, you chose to remain after all?"

To close this world as soon as possible, even for Veron, who could not go anywhere and wandered the afterlife.

"Being of obsession, do you also intend to follow your predecessor?"

Shura looked down at Veron's corpse.

Go to Hexa.

Recalling Veron's voice, Shura's eyes turned cold as she turned and knelt before Nane.

"To the one who overwhelmed the leader of the Ten Elders, what opinion could I possibly offer?"

Shura's forehead touched the ground.

"I will follow you."

Nane nodded and turned away with his hands behind his back, and Shura approached him from behind.

"What do you intend to do?"

"Open the altars."

Although the altar where Veron's iron pole was planted was scorched, there were still 3,599 altars left in the world.

Sermon - Open.

A sword pierced the sky, flying into space, and then shattered into thousands of beams of light.

Great Star spoke.

"The Great Purification has begun."
