Chapter 244: Magic Clash (4)

The main drawback of explosive magic is that the casting time varies greatly depending on the explosive power, as the omniscience involved is not intuitive.

A 1-Kilobuster has the power to blow away a cart, and an officially certified 10th-rank mage takes an average of 21 seconds to cast a 1-Kilobuster.

However, Iruki, being a Savant, could complete the omniscience in just 3 seconds. And now, the power he was summoning over a span of more than 10 seconds amounted to a staggering 10-Kilobuster.

Closer sensed that something was off when Iruki's magic remained silent, indicating that this explosion would not be ordinary. There was a high chance that trying to defend the entire blast would result in fatal injuries.

Closer, who predicted the point of impact, focused his Earth Skin on the left side. The earth piled up rapidly, forming a solid earthen wall as if time had sped up.

At the same time, Iruki's Atomic Bomb detonated.

Although Closer didn't move, as there was no push condition, the Earth Skin was obliterated without a trace.

“Phew!”

Closer exhaled heavily. He had neutralized the impact, but the force that had blown away his Earth Skin was terrifying.

At this point, Iruki also turned serious. He hadn’t expected Closer to withstand the 10-Kilobuster explosion so perfectly. Closer was much tougher than anticipated.

'I have no choice but to do it.'

Iruki split his focus in two and operated an additional Spirit Zone. The moment two Spirit Zones simultaneously infiltrated his synesthesia, Closer’s eyes widened.

It felt like he was fighting against two mages.

‘Damn it. Isn’t this cheating?’

From this point on, Closer couldn’t afford to enjoy the match. If Atomic Bombs with the power of 10-Kilobusters came at him from both sides, defense would be impossible.

“I’ll end this before that happens!”

Closer cast Heavy Stone, and the earth rapidly compressed in mid-air, forming a boulder weighing nearly a ton that plummeted toward Iruki’s head.

It was simply Yeonsong (an alchemy term for creating new materials/lit. mean ductility), but it was a gravitational weapon that could crush the opponent with only potential energy (energy that is stored in an object or system due to its position or arrangement of parts) if executed at a high place.

Kung! Kung! Kung! Kung! Kung!

Wherever Iruki moved, boulders fell.

Although the process of transmutation took time, and considering the arrival time, it wasn’t particularly effective in direct combat, but in a situation where movement was restricted, the mere thought was horrifying.

‘I see. So that’s what he’s aiming for?’

Iruki figured out Closer's intentions. The fact that the boulders took time to reach the ground meant that it was possible to deploy it in conjunction with other magic to create a time-based attack.

As expected, Closer cast Heavy Stone over a wide area.

While multiple boulders were being formed in the air, Closer skillfully disengaged the omniscience of Heavy Stone and replaced it with a new one.

When Closer cast Slug Magic, the surrounding stones floated up and were shot like bullets.

“How about this!”

From the sky, falling boulders descended, while from the front, stones scattered like shrapnel, flying towards Iruki.

“Tch! I really didn’t want to use this.”

Iruki switched one of his Spirit Zones to a defensive mode and cast a defensive magic.

Hundreds of tiny, 10-gram burst bombs spread out in a spherical shape along the surface of his Spirit Zone. The bomb was then hit by a deluge of stones, creating a small flame.

The students watched in awe as countless chain explosions occurred at the boundary between offense and defense.

This was the Bomblet Barrier, a signature of detonation mages.

It’s a technique where hundreds of micro-bombs are deployed around the mage, intercepting any approaching objects before they can reach the mage. The key was how many micro-bombs one could manifest within the Spirit Zone.

By that standard, Iruki was at a level where he could manifest a total of 387 micro-bombs simultaneously.

In terms of quantity alone, even a professional mage would find it impressive, but despite deploying the Bomblet Barrier, Iruki found himself on the defensive.

The problem was that his Deviate type was too excellent. Most of Iruki's advanced techniques come from Deviate type. However, the Bomblet Barrier achieves its greatest effect when used with a defensive Spirit Zone.

In other words, it was like an attack-oriented swordsman skilled in dual-wielding swords abandoning one sword to carry a shield.

Of course, since Iruki had a Double Spirit Zone, he could at least still hold one sword, but unless he operated two Deviate types simultaneously, he couldn’t break through the earth magic’s defense.

“Kekekeke! I’ll end it with this!”

Closer charged straight into the Bomblet Barrier, covering his face with both arms. It was an action he could take because he trusted in the defensive power of his Earth Skin.

As he pushed through the curtain of micro-bombs, Iruki quickly detonated the Atomic Bomb in a rapid rhythm.

The explosions from the 10-gram busters pounded against Closer’s guard relentlessly, causing him to grimace. But instead of stopping, he pushed in deeper. And just as the Earth Skin shattered, Closer raised his fist and struck Iruki in the face.

Iruki’s eyes spun as he was knocked to the ground.

Silence flowed through the room. No one had expected a mage to deliver a punch in the Heaven's Trials combat.

“Ca- Can he even do that? He punched him.”

“I’m not sure. It’s a match, so I guess it doesn’t matter? There’s no rule against using physical attacks, is there?”

The students' eyes naturally turned to the referee. Ethella also had an awkward expression on her face.

Looking at the state of the contestants with the master bracelet, the blow just dealt had a significant impact.

Up until that moment, Iruki’s gauge had been steadily decreasing at a rate of 1% per minute. The fact that the decrease was as steady as an hourglass indicated how well he had managed his resources.

But the moment Closer’s fist connected, the gauge was halved. It was the downside of mages being vulnerable to physical attacks.

Ethella, having made her decision, stepped back in acceptance.

Given the nature of earth magic, physical collisions could indeed be considered a viable tactic.

“Kukuku, I told you, didn’t I? I gave you a hint, but you didn’t catch on. So much for being called a genius.”

Closer kicked Iruki in the side as he tried to stand, sending him sprawling back onto the ground.

Given that this was the same attack that had taken down even the massive Mark in a single blow, Double Spirit Zone was now out of the question.

Even so, Closer approached with his entire body covered in Earth Skin, just in case.

“Shall we start the remodeling? Let’s get your bodies in shape first.”

Closer lifted his foot and began stomping on Iruki’s body. Iruki could only curl up, covering his face, unable to move.

“Hey, does it hurt? Hurts, doesn’t it? Why aren’t you showing your face? You’re already ugly, so what’s the problem?”

“That bastard…! I’ll kill him.”

Nade clenched his fists and trembled. But he did not rush in for the sake of Iruki's last bit of pride.

“Shirone, you should step back. I’ll take care of this.”

“If we’re going to fight, we should fight together. But I don’t think we need to intervene. Iruki hasn’t given up yet.”

Nade looked closely at Iruki. Iruki's smiling face could be seen between his arms. With the Bomblet Barrier broken, there was nothing else he could try. What was he thinking, smiling even while getting beaten?

“You’re surprisingly tough. Hey, turn around.”

Kicked by Closer, Iruki rolled over onto his back. Contrary to the expected look of fear, his expression was one of ease.

“This guy is really getting on my nerves. This time, I’ll really break your nose.”

Annoyed, Closer lifted his leg and aimed at Iruki’s face. One blow would certainly break his nose.

“Kikik! Kikikik!”

Iruki, who laughed as strangely as his strange voice, bending his back like a bow, then propped himself up on his head and looked at Shirone.

“Shirone, I’m sorry. I was saving this to use on you.”

The students were speechless. Iruki's bizarre behavior was about to begin again.

“You punk!”

Just as Closer, feeling disrespected, brought his foot down like a thunderbolt, Iruki’s lips curled into a wide grin.

“I’ll show you something fun, Shirone.”

Boom! The kick struck the ground.

Iruki rolled to avoid it and slipped inside Closer’s guard. Then, with his slender wrist, he swung a punch.

Closer was incredulous. With Earth Skin and Earthrise active, a punch would only hurt Iruki’s fist.

“Ouch!”

Contrary to expectations, a heavy impact hit Closer’s abdomen, causing him to let out a slight groan.

Closer’s eyes wavered in shock. The power of the punch was just average, but all the defensive magic surrounding him had vanished.

“H- How...?”

Iruki flew up into a martial arts stance imitating a crane, and poked Closer in the eye with two fingers.

“Wacha!”

“Argh! My eyes!”

Closer, who momentarily lost his vision, hurriedly attempted to cast Earthrise again. But the same thing happened—just as the magic seemed to activate, it was as if time reversed, and the magic was canceled.

Tok. Seureuk.

The correspondent who had been watching the match dropped his pen without taking his eyes off Iruki.

"…Cancellation?"

Revoke Magic Cancellation.

It’s an extremely advanced form of magic that nullifies a mage’s omnipotence, regardless of its power.

In magic books, Cancellation is often explained using the metaphor of a glass.

The question is whether the shattered fragments of a glass that has fallen to the ground can restore themselves.

The correct answer is 'yes'.

However, the reason no one has ever witnessed such a phenomenon is that the probability is exceedingly low.

Since the dawn of time, as time began, this world has progressed from a stable state to an unstable one.

Therefore, when a glass falls, it shatters into countless fragments due to an almost infinite number of variables.

However, among those infinite possibilities, there is one where if the direction and speed of the forces acting at the moment the glass shatters are perfectly reversed, the glass can restore itself.

In reality, shattering is merely a state where the bonds between molecules have been broken, so if the forces at the atomic level are reversed, the glass can be restored to a perfect state without even a single crack.

Mages call this the "Returned Glass."

Iruki infiltrated Closer’s Spirit Zone with his Deviate Type Spirit Zone, analyzing the changes occurring in his mind.

Then, by reversing the equations that arose from specific magic patterns, he successfully created the "Returned Glass."

It is a task that requires calculation ability that ordinary humans cannot even attempt to imitate. This is why 72% of mages who can perform Cancellation are Savants.

"Argh! This is so annoying!"

Closer flailed his arms in frustration. Giving up on defense entirely, he cast Heavy Stone, creating about ten boulders in the air.

Iruki pointed his fingers at each one, casting Cancellation on them one by one. As a result, the process of the earth forming into stones was reversed as if time was moving backward, and the stones vanished without a trace.

“Uwacha! Uwacha! Uwacha!”

Iruki stubbornly continued his martial arts moves. To those watching, it might have looked like he was mocking Closer, but he was actually serious.

When he struck Closer's nose, twin streams of blood gushed out.

There was no way to counter the forced cancellation of magic. It was like a fight between a mage and an ordinary person.

The terror of Cancellation Magic was evident on the faces of the teachers. Even if it’s a student, the situation changes if they’ve mastered Cancellation Magic.

"I knew he was exceptional, but when did he acquire such abilities…?"

“His environment will also have a big influence. After all, Iruki's father…"

Mercodyne Albino, the head of the Dragon Thunder.

Although he appears to have a laid-back personality, it’s said that not many in the kingdom truly know what he’s thinking.

Savant have the potential to become the best in anti-magic and Cancellation, so it’s clear that someone like Albino would have trained him from a young age.

“Not all Savants have quick-thinking like Iruki. If managed well, he’ll eventually reach a unique and unparalleled level."

Just as clothes get soaked in a drizzle, the repeated blows from Iruki’s small fists caused Closer’s bones to ache.

Closer’s face was a complete mess. His eyes were swollen, blood dripped continuously from his nose, and his lips were split, causing pain with every movement.
