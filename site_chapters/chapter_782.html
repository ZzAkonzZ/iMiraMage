Chapter 782:

A letter bearing Kashan’s seal arrived in Tormia via the fastest Red Line route.

It was registered mail meant to be delivered directly to the head of Yongrae, and as soon as Albino read it, he got up from his seat.

Knock, knock.

When there was no response to his verbal knock, Albino opened the door and stepped inside.

“If someone comes in, at least look up.”

Peering out from between a towering stack of documents, Iruki raised his hollow eyes.

“If I’m still a person, take a look at what state I’m in.”

Albino approached.

“If a lunatic can still talk crazy, you haven’t gone completely mad yet.”

“…What is it this time?”

Iruki didn’t bother to hide how annoyed he looked.

“Congratulations. You won’t be buried in grunt work anymore.”

Albino tossed the letter, and Iruki glanced down to see the seal of Kashan.

“What is this?”

“Kashan sent a scout offer. You’re to join the Holy War. If you’re willing, the kingdom’s prepared to back it fully.”

Iruki lost interest and looked away.

“No thanks. I haven’t even settled down here properly, and now the Holy War? This is just more nonsense…”

Albino felt a strange pride in his son’s unwillingness to abandon the family legacy so easily—the Merkodine line that had led Yongrae for generations.

“You always said you hated Yongrae.”

“Just because you hate pooping doesn’t mean you don’t go to the bathroom.”

Albino did his best to stay composed as he took in his son’s weary, focused expression.

Yeah… even Merkodine is just a burden to him.

That settled it.

“At least read the letter. The conditions are good, and I think it’ll speak to you.”

Albino tapped the table where the envelope lay, and Iruki paused his work, frowning.

“I said I’m not interested. I’m busy right now. Just go ho—”

“Shirone wrote it.”

Iruki snatched the envelope and pulled out the letter.

“…”

He skimmed it with incredible speed, then went back to the first page and slowly absorbed each line.

“Phew…”

After committing the entire thing to memory, he exhaled deeply and rubbed his face with both hands.

Knowing exactly what his son was thinking, Albino smiled warmly.

“Go. The world needs you. The situation’s a mess, but I can protect Tormia.”

Iruki said nothing.

“You’ve got a talent no one else has. But even that talent is just a miracle of probability gifted by all of humanity rolling the dice. Don’t think it belongs to you just because it landed in your hands. Use it for the world.”

“…I’ll go.”

Iruki’s gaze was firm—his mind made up.

“Using the Red Line International Transit will be the fastest. Once you arrive in the Molton Kingdom, Kashan will open a military channel for you.”

All Red Line nations had magic circle facilities for international travel maintained by the Association.

“When does the Bashka Gate open?”

“Fifty-seven minutes from now. Miss it and you’ll have to wait twelve hours. I’ll handle the formalities—head to the Mage Association right now.”

Due to diplomatic concerns, international teleportation gates could only be opened at regulated times according to international law.

He shouldn’t need to wait once he reaches Molton…

From Molton—Kashan’s neighboring nation—he’d be able to jump directly using the empire’s military channel.

“It’s still going to take a while, huh?”

Considering transfer delays at each terminal, it would average about six hours per country.

“Eat and sleep on the way. Don’t forget to convert your money into international currency. If you meet someone cute, hit on her. Who knows? Maybe in another country—or another planet—your face will actually work.”

Not even worth a reply.

“I’ll stop by to see Neid before I go. Please take care of him while I’m gone. He’s the kind of guy who’ll blow everything up one day—in a good way. You won’t regret keeping him around.”

After returning to Tormia with Liz, Neid was currently researching ancient weapons under Yongrae’s authority.

“…I will.”

Even Albino couldn’t joke as he watched his son walk out into the world.

With a suitcase of documents in hand, Iruki headed straight to the annex where Neid was staying.

“Neid, I’m coming in.”

Classified documents on ancient weapons lay scattered across the floor.

Empty liquor bottles and half-drunk cups of coffee littered the cluttered table, and Neid sat amid the mess, smoking a cigarette.

“You’re a real piece of work.”

Neid looked half-destroyed.

“What’s up? Isn’t it work hours?”

Iruki picked up a rolling bottle and dropped it in the trash.

“Does drinking bring you divine inspiration?”

Neid scrawled something on a piece of paper and took a long drag from his cigarette, then tapped his temple.

“It helps me relax. You can’t be creative if you’re too tense—‘must succeed’ thinking kills ideas.”

“And the coffee?”

“It wakes up my brain when I’m exhausted.”

“The cigarettes?”

“They constrict blood vessels, speed up blood flow. More thinking per minute.”

The holy trinity of creation, apparently.

“You’re gonna die like that.”

“I can still handle it. I’m young. It’s all about focus. Once this is done, I’ll quit everything.”

Right now, Neid needed to be at 120%.

“So, what’s going on?”

Snapped out of his flow, Neid looked up at Iruki.

“Are you going somewhere? Dressed like that?”

Iruki took out the letter.

“Shirone called me. Seems like he needs help. I’m going to Kashan. I won’t be back for a while.”

“Shirone?”

Neid sprang up, grabbing the edge of the table.

“What about me? Did he call me too?”

Iruki pretended to read.

“Says you’re not needed. That a guy like you should just—fill a plate with water and drown your—”

“Don’t mess with me.”

Neid snatched the letter and read it.

“Hm. I see.”

The dazed look in Neid’s eyes vanished, replaced by razor-sharp focus.

“Anyway, sorry about this.”

“Hmph! Don’t be. Go help Shirone. I’ve got my own work to do.”

Back at the table, Neid scribbled furiously, riding the wave of inspiration.

“Just watch. I’ll show you what I can do in this war. I’ll make a fortune and crush the bastards who made Shirone cry.”

No war without money.

Good luck. I believe in you.

Iruki had no doubt in Neid’s potential. He had once been a pure engineer—now he was a creator who chased money for a reason.

“I’ll stay in touch.”

Neid raised his hand in silent farewell. Iruki slowly shut the door behind him.

“We’ve arrived.”

As soon as the attendant spoke, Shirone, who had been wandering restlessly through Aganos’s spacious halls, bolted forward.

He’s here! He’s really here!

When he reached the inner gate of Aganos, Minerva, the card game players, and Uorin were already waiting.

There was no need for the empress of an empire to personally greet a guest, but Uorin was simply in a good mood.

Because he’s Shirone’s friend.

Meeting the friends of someone you love is a joy on par with getting to know the person themselves.

“Open the inner gate.”

As the massive gate parted and Iruki appeared through the opening, Minerva raised a single eyebrow.

“That’s Iruki?”

The Savant Shirone had praised endlessly.

He definitely doesn’t look ordinary.

With his light brown hair, mismatched eyes, and a slim frame that made his oversized coat sleeves flutter, he stood out. Those who had imagined someone resembling Sein watched in confusion as Iruki raised his hand.

“Been a while, Shirone.”

When Shirone didn’t respond right away, Minerva turned to see him on the verge of tears.

“Iruki…”

They were on the same side.

A friend who would never change, no matter what future lay ahead—a friend who would always stand by him.

“Iruki… hiiiii…”

And Shirone broke into sobs.

So that’s what it is.

Minerva looked back at Iruki again.

A person it’s okay to show weakness around, no matter what.

Even Garcia had praised the iron will of the Infinite Mage, someone who seemed unshakable in any situation.

But seeing Shirone collapse in a single second, even Minerva had to acknowledge Iruki.

“Why are you crying?”

Smiling, Iruki set down his briefcase, stepped forward, and hugged him.

“It’s been tough, hasn’t it? You’ve done well.”

That was more than enough. Speechless, Shirone just kept nodding silently.

Uorin approached with a smile.

“Nice to meet you. I’m Teraje Uorin, Empress of Kashan.”

“Thank you for having me. I’m Iruki, Shirone’s friend.”

Even before one of the rulers of the Three Emperors, Iruki met her eyes directly and extended a handshake.

Mitochondrial Eve… Is that the gaze of someone who carries history? And you walked away from her, Shirone?

Uorin understood immediately.

A tremendous nerve. So different, even as a friend.

As the two exchanged glances, sensing each other wouldn’t be easy to handle, Shirone beamed.

“Iruki’s a researcher with Yongrae of the Tormia Kingdom.”

Uorin smiled with a knowing glint.

“Of course I know. It must’ve been a hard journey—thank you for responding to such a difficult request.”

“Whether it’s difficult or not depends on the request itself. Let’s get to it. I want to begin right away.”

Though they hadn’t even caught up yet, the expressions of the card game players brightened for the first time.

“Very well. Gando will escort you.”

They arrived at the panic room.

Though cramped, it was perfect for private discussion. After quick introductions among those meeting for the first time, Uorin began the meeting.

“Shirone likely included it in his letter, but it’s best you hear it all in person to judge for yourself.”

She detailed the current state of the war, the concept of Extreme Evil, and explained Ex Machina and Lawslayer.

“Hm. A weapon that alters law…”

As expected, the Savant Iruki was most interested in the ancient weapon Ex Machina.

Gudio spoke.

“I hate to say this after bringing you here, but we don’t know how well you’ll handle Ex Machina. You’ll have to undergo testing. And we need to assess teamwork too.”

Not out of disrespect—mastering Ex Machina required one of the strongest minds in the world.

“If you fail the test, you may have to return. It doesn’t matter who recommended you.”

“Of course. I’m fine with that.”

Then Ness brought up the key point.

“Also, everything you’ve heard today about Ex Machina must be kept strictly confidential until the war is over.”

If Iruki failed and revealed classified info to his home country, all strategic efforts would collapse.

“…Understood.”

It could’ve been insulting, but Iruki nodded without protest.

“Along the same lines, we can’t provide details about the Alpha-P and Beta-P candidates we’re seeking. That concludes the briefing. Let’s head to Ex Machina.”

“That’s fine. However…”

Just as the card players began to rise, Iruki stopped them.

“Alpha-P and Beta-P. I don’t know who they are, but I think I know what kind of people you’re looking for.”

Agaya asked,

“Why are you so sure they’re people? It’ll take dozens of laws to kill Havitz.”

“No matter how you manipulate the laws… in the end, it’s humans who define good and evil.”

Gudio, having locked eyes with Ness, asked,

“Then… who do you think we’re looking for?”

“Two emotions capable of rippling through the purity of chaos.”

Iruki raised two fingers.

“The one who loves Havitz the most… and the one who hates him the most. For Lawslayer to succeed, those two people are absolutely necessary.”

“…”

The card game players, stunned by how close he’d come to their core strategy, neither confirmed nor denied it.

“Well then, shall we begin?”

As Iruki turned toward the exit of the panic room, Shirone and Uorin exchanged a smile.
