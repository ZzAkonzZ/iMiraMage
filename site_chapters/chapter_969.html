Chapter 969: Simultaneous Events (4)

Ionas' fist, broken down into particles, rapidly took shape right in front of Shirone's nose.

"Ugh!"

As Shirone turned his head, the air was pushed aside, and a sonic boom erupted in the distance.

"You've got some skill."

Shirone fixed his gaze on Ionas.

'Ionas of Extreme Decomposition.'

With a pale face and silver hair down to his shoulders, he resembled a human overall.

However, his eyes without pupils and double rows of teeth would be unsettling to most people.

'Atomic Movement. This is really tricky.'

The ability to decompose his body to the extreme and move at the atomic level.

The attacks derived from this ability reach the pinnacle of transformation, surpassing all techniques.

'It's not just about speed.' The reason he couldn't dodge the arrow even after seeing it was because the reception time of his senses varied.

He could perceive it with his eleven senses, but avoiding the attack was entirely up to Shirone's own abilities.

"Shall we get serious?"

Ionas, decomposed into atoms, launched an attack from an unpredictable trajectory.

'Miracle Stream!'

As the light smoke enveloped Shirone's body, black holes appeared as if he had been hit by buckshot.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

The light scattered into fragments, but Shirone had already retreated 20 kilometers away.

"So, you ran away after all."

Ionas, in particle form, reassembled his face and circled around Shirone.

"I've always wondered."

The movements of approximately 10 quintillion particles were sensed integrally through Ultima.

"Despite being this strong, why don't I feel like the strongest?"

Shirone discovered a technique within the pattern of the 10 quintillion particles' movements.

'Left Punch.'

A punch that could be called a birthright grazed Shirone's side.

'It was a close call.'

Shirone, having widened the distance again, created and fired approximately 4,200 photon cannons.

"Is this all you've got?"

Ionas' arm moved at an invisible speed, deflecting the photon cannons.

From a point 20 kilometers away, Uriel, who had been watching the battle, spoke.

"He was a strong boy."

Whatever the standard of strength, he was a human who made you think that way.

"And now he's actually become strong." While his mental capacity is infinite, humans have physical limits.

Sensing and avoiding Ionas of Extreme Decomposition was close to a miracle.

"But in the end, that's all there is to it."

Rael agreed in silence.

'In fact, Uriel's words are contradictory. Strength doesn't need any other elements besides being strong.'

The reason he couldn't deny it was because of a question that had been lingering in his mind for some time.

'Why can't I, or rather we, feel like the strongest?'

Only Ikael knew the answer.

'McClain Goffin.'

All the top fighters in heaven have a common experience of not being able to defeat Goffin.

When Goffin's erasure occurred, a transcendent concept that couldn't be explained in words emerged.

'Even if it's erased from memory, we've already experienced strength beyond that...

Not being able to reach the feeling of being the best.

'Shirone.'

Ikael's eyes, watching her fiercely fighting child, were filled with sorrow.

'I love you.'

More precious than anything in the universe.

'Though I don't deserve to be called a parent, I can't lose you again.'

She would never gamble with her child's life again.

'Please step back now. Go back and live a happy life. I'll take responsibility for the world's affairs.'

Every time Shirone narrowly avoided Ionas' attacks, Ikael's heart felt like it was being torn apart.

'This won't be enough to defeat Ionas. But it's not because of a lack of skill.'

Something that transcends strength.

'It's just... because he's not Goffin.'

Even if their mental states were equal, Shirone's body was far inferior to that of a Gaia.

'Strength comparable to a giant, a mind that transcends the concept of an angel, the integration of numerous Gaia.'

It was a race that possessed all these qualities, shaking the world of gods to its core.

"Ikael, I'll give you one last chance."

Satiel spoke.

"Reveal the truth about Goffin's erasure and admit your mistake. Then I'll acknowledge your authority."

Ikael clenched her fist tightly.

'I must not act on my emotions.'

To protect Shirone from the entire army of heaven, the position of archangel was absolutely necessary.

"You know it too, don't you? Ionas is my subordinate, but his combat power surpasses mine. If you follow my words, I won't act arbitrarily. In other words, if I order you to leave Shirone alone, you'll comply."

It was a sweet offer.

If she could endure the humiliation of bowing to the one who killed her son.

'I don't care.'

Strength returned to Ikael's eyes.

'I've come back alive. If I can protect Shirone, I'll bow to anyone.'

From the perspective of looking down, Ionas' fist struck Shirone's solar plexus directly.

"Ugh!"

Even though he reduced the kinetic energy by more than 99.99%, the pain was still piercing.

"I think it's impressive."

Ionas walked through the air as if taking a stroll.

"Humans die just from a graze. I'll applaud you for enduring this far with such a body."

Shirone wasn't pleased.

"You're strong. You'd be one of the top ten fighters in heaven. So if there's a single cause of defeat, it's me."

Ionas raised his fist in front of his eyes.

"It means you're fighting one of the top five fighters in heaven."

"Haha."

Despite the pain, laughter burst out.

"...What's so funny?"

Having experienced the entire history of Omega, Shirone knew Ionas' position in heaven.

"Honestly, isn't it close?"

Ionas' eyebrows twitched.

"Ymir and Asher are neck and neck, and below them are Uriel, Ikael, and others."

Huh...?

Shirone pointed at Ionas.

"But I admit that sometimes you get in. Sometimes."

A mere human couldn't know the long-standing debates of heaven, but it wasn't entirely wrong.

"Just kidding. Forget it."

Shirone recovered from the shock.

"It doesn't matter anyway, does it? You'll never be the strongest."

"Now I understand..."

In an instant, Ionas flew above Shirone's head and swung his leg.

With a crack, the air split, and the sea 600 meters below was torn apart.

'When did he...?'

They were already off the land.

As Ionas looked at the rapidly healing surface, he raised his head.

"You're a typical loser. You think that by putting the strongest in front of me, the bottom you're standing on will feel a little shallower, but let me tell you clearly. You and I are on different levels."

"I don't care about levels. Who's stronger has nothing to do with me. Because...

Shirone's eyes widened, and Miracle Stream was powerfully compressed in both hands.

"I have to win no matter what."

A flash pierced the sky, and the Hand of God descended, curling its fingers at Ionas.

An aggressive use of the Ultima system.

'I was hit by that.'

It was a mental ability strong enough to scatter the entire army of heaven across the world.

"How dare you act leisurely against me?"

Ionas' double rows of teeth were revealed menacingly.

"It's not leisure. It's responsibility."

Shirone looked at the sea.

They were in the middle of the ocean, 800 kilometers west of the Zive Kingdom's airspace.

'Here, I can fight.'

Of course, there was life here too, but saving humans was strategically important.

Ionas' triangular prism burned red.

"This is the end."

Extreme Decomposition - Atomic Crash.

Particles compressed into a fist-sized sphere exploded with light, spreading in all directions.

'He increased the activity of the particles.'

Moving with energy on a different dimension, Ionas aimed at Shirone's back and raised his hand.

'After all, a human.'

If he pulled out the heart, he couldn't survive.

'Huh?'

The Hand of God, drawing a circle with thumb and middle finger, had already completely dominated the view.

'Damn it!'

The distance to Shirone's back was 0.03 millimeters.

'Too late.'

Ionas' arms decomposed into particles and crossed in a defensive posture.

At the same time, the Hand of God flicked its fingers.

"Ugh!"

The shockwave struck the entire 10 quintillion particles, and Ionas' body was slammed into the sea.

Boom!

Water splashed, and a rough column of water shot up from the boiling surface.

"Argh!"

He, who had broken the spine of a sperm whale, looked up at the sky with water droplets in his eyes.

"A mere human...!"

Ionas' words were cut off as he saw Shirone raising his hand.

But the reason he was speechless was the Hand of God above Shirone.

'Don't worry, Ikael.'

As if reflecting Shirone's heart, the Hand of God spread its five fingers wide towards the sky.

'We're definitely connected.'

At the same time, a light sphere of unimaginable size was born with a pop on the giant palm.

Not only Ionas but also the archangels observing from afar were at a loss for words.

Shirone looked down at the sea and spoke.

"Feel it too."

Hand of God - Photon Cannon.

"This is my responsibility."

With all his strength, Shirone swung his arm, and the Hand of God drove the Photon Cannon vertically down.

The moment the astronomical mass hit the sea,

"Aaaah..."

Ionas' scream was cut off, and the sea exploded and evaporated in the shape of a crown.

The scale was so immense that every process seemed to unfold in slow motion.

Rumble! Rumble!

With the steam completely obscuring the view, only a terrifying roar could be heard.

As the Hand of God waved its hand, the view cleared, revealing a massive hole in the sea.

'He's gone.'

Ionas was nowhere to be seen.

Soon after, as gravity pulled the seawater back, a spectacular scene of swirling currents unfolded.

At that moment, Satiel cut off her observation.

"...He's been annihilated."

Neither Uriel, Rael, nor the transcendentally sighted Mara spoke for a while.

The power of the Hand of God reminded them of times they did not want to recall.

'Is it not over yet?' It seemed as if Shirone was saying that they were still connected.

Rumble. Rumble.

Like a video made for the amusement of gods, the sea continued to undergo astonishing changes.

'Two minutes have passed. Is this enough?' Once it was certain that Ionas had been annihilated, Shirone turned in the direction he had come from.

And he spoke in a gentle voice to her, who was still watching him.

"Ikael."

Although he couldn't pick up the pieces of her shattered heart after Goffin disappeared,

"You're not alone."

If they could share memories together, he thought that might also be a beautiful life.

"So..."

With a beautiful smile shining in the sunlight, Shirone reached out to her from afar.

"Let's go together, Mom."
