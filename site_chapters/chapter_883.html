Chapter 883: Memory (1)

Alpheas continued speaking.

"A year ago, one of the students at the magic school was murdered by an assailant."

"Murdered?"

"That's when I learned about the ill-fated relationship between Etella and Shagal. You know who Shagal is, right?"

One of the world's 100 most dangerous individuals.

He had even fought against Rian and was a figure associated with the Great Purge.

"I heard there was a distortion of memory." Ra Enemy implanted false memories in Shagal.

Everything he believed he had experienced was an illusion, and as a result, he killed Raphael, Etella's mentor.

"Raphael forgave him. In honor of his will, Etella tried to lead Shagal to goodness, but..."

Alpheas sighed.

"Shagal's mind was in such a deep abyss that she couldn't handle it. He harmed everyone around her. Students and teachers were in danger. Eventually... one student was sacrificed."

"Did they capture Shagal?"

"No. As much as I hate to admit it, he's strong. That's why Etella left the school. Wherever Etella is, Shagal follows."

I understood Etella's feelings.

"Where is she now?" Alpheas shook his head.

"I don't know. They say Shagal's sense of smell has reached a supernatural level. If he can track her down wherever she is, there are two possible places she could be. Either facing Shagal alone in an isolated place...

If she could subdue him, she wouldn't have let him run wild.

"Or hiding among a crowd, waiting for the right moment."

A place where she could hide her scent.

The Kingdom of Pessia.

Bordering Tormia but geographically part of the southern continent.

A developing country plagued by corruption and severe economic disparity leading to slums.

Slum Secte7.

If Radum in the Kingdom of Tormia was a place where the poor schemed in hiding, Secte7 was literally a den of beggars. In this place filled with shacks that couldn't even block rain, about 450 beggars were being kept.

The kingdom provided meals, but the quality of food was so poor it could be considered trash.

Yet they were fed because they served as good guinea pigs for politicians.

Secret biological experiments, assassination plots, unsolved cases—Secte7 was the starting point for many crimes.

"I'm hungry..."

In a 7-pyeong room crowded with people, the stench from their excrement filled the air.

Everywhere felt like a toilet, but in the dirtiest corner sat a woman.

Romi Etella.

Meals were provided once a day, but it was rare for her turn to come around.

Covered in what was once white rags now blackened with filth, beggars glanced at her every time she smeared herself with excrement.

"She's doing it again. What on earth is that woman?"

Since some time ago, her behavior whenever she had time in Secte7 had been bizarre.

But there were many mentally ill people like her here, so the beggars didn't pay much attention.

'I have to leave.'

It had been 30 days since she entered Secte7.

Although she disguised herself with all sorts of foul smells, Shagal's sense of smell could remember even incidents.

'He knows all my scents.'

To erase even the smell of her intestines, she scooped up excrement from the floor and put it in her mouth.

It was nauseating even for a monk's mind, but Etella's eyes burned with anger.

'I can't forgive.'

Until her student at the magic school was murdered by Shagal, she tried to honor Raphael's will.

But what she gained from acting on goodwill was the death of many acquaintances.

Monks from the Karsis Monastery, believers, and magic school students alike.

'The methodology of evil.'

It felt like he was mocking her, asking if she could still forgive him after all this.

'I will kill you.'

Shagal's evil was gnawing away at Etella's goodness.

'I'm sorry, Master. I've lost my heart.'

By the time her revenge on Shagal ended, Etella would no longer be able to call herself good...

'Don't worry, Shagal. You won't die alone.'

It didn't matter anymore.

'I will kill you and go with you.'

Etella's eyes gleamed with determination.

'To eternal hell...'

Mark suggested,

"Seniors, since we're all gathered here for such a memorable occasion, how about having dinner together?"

The sun had already set, and it felt too regretful for everyone to part ways just yet.

Shirone and Amy felt the same way.

They moved to a restaurant and as drinks flowed, candid conversations followed.

As expected, the main topic was the war against demons, and Valkyrie's chief strategist Iruki explained the situation.

"It's not good. We're going to lose."

Despite the grim outlook, they weren't afraid because they were also in the middle of the war.

"The key is Tormia Kingdom. It's the last gateway to the central continent. If demons break through here, no country in the world can stop them."

Dante said,

"Tormia won't fall easily."

Seriel asked,

"Do you have a strategy?"

"It's confidential so I can't share details. Although some might already guess. Anyway, it's almost certain that Tormia will be our final battleground."

Iruki added,

"Valkyrie is preparing as best as we can. Of course, estimating casualties is impossible mathematically but there's still a slight chance we might win."

"...How many will die?"

"Based on kingdoms occupied by demons so far? On average 87% of their citizens died. So if they reach Tormia... most of us here will die."

In somber silence Dorothy spoke,

"So this might be our last reunion then? Shall we toast to commemorate?"

"We won't die."

Everyone turned their gaze to Shirone.

"I won't let anyone die."

"Shirone..."

From Shirone's fierce expression staring ahead they felt what he carried on his shoulders.

"When we started this war they said it was impossible too. But we've come this far haven't we? The Ivory Tower is moving; Seong-eum has closed its spiritual realm; if we all fight with everything we've got..."

Shirone smiled,

"So let's toast to victory."

The alumni of Alpheas Magic School raised their glasses and shouted,

"Let's win!"

The time to leave was approaching fast now.

Stepping outside for some air, Shirone was greeted by the raucous noise from the bar.

"It's coming."

Was it a manifestation of his temporal perception, or just a delusion born from emotion?

"Something huge is on its way."

The sense of acceleration towards the end was more distinct than ever before.

"What kind of future is waiting?"

He could only shudder at the vague image, still beyond his wildest imagination.

"Shirone."

Turning around slowly, he saw Amy approaching with a smiling face, cheeks flushed.

"Are you leaving?"

Shirone had to go.

"I'm sorry. I need to deliver this to the Ivory Tower as soon as possible. Even though we've just met..."

Amy shook her head.

"Let's not just seek our own happiness. I hope for a world where everyone can be happy. I'll fight hard, too. And I'll wait. Because you're the only one in my heart..."

Despite being bolstered by alcohol,

"I need to say..."

Amy beamed.

"I love you."

Shirone saw the full moon in the sky clearly reflected in her eyes.

"Why..."

Tears streamed down his face.

"Why are you crying?"

"Me? Why..."

Shirone hastily wiped away his tears, but couldn't stop them.

"I must remember this moment." He didn't know why, but he felt compelled.

‘I must remember this woman.’

Amy's face, her voice, her scent, and even the moon reflected in her eyes that night.

"I'll never forget!"

Grasping Amy's shoulders, Shirone imprinted her image onto his retinas.

"Amy, you must never forget me. Remember me at all times."

"What do you mean?"

Shirone was scared.

The massive future pressing into this world seemed like it could sweep everything away.

"Promise me."

Although Amy didn't understand, seeing the fear on Shirone's face, she nodded.

"Okay, I'll remember. No matter what happens, even if you're not in this world, I'll remember you."

Shirone kissed Amy, and their passionate breath fused them into one life.

Moments later.

As the sound of spatial displacement roared, the people emerging from the bar saw Amy standing alone.

As Gaold, who had reached the stage of dissolution, unleashed his Air Press, a mushroom cloud erupted from the ground.

Kangnan, alongside Zulu, watched the scene from afar, her eyes shaking with shock.

"How can this be..."

Gaold, having begun to forget even Miro, took drastic measures.

And this was the result.

"How can he grow stronger?"

"Because he's human," Zulu said.

"A human's heart has no limits. But Gaold... chose an easier way."

As the smoke cleared and a huge crater was visible, Zulu descended to the ground.

A wind blew the smoke away, and Gaold emerged with a significant smile.

"It's over."

The demons of the south were completely eradicated.

But for Kangnan, Gaold's smiling face seemed to transcend sorrow and enter the realm of the grotesque.

"Are you really okay?"

"What?"

"You know... "

As the sound of applause echoed through the smoke, Kangnan and Zulu quickly turned around.

"Wow, impressive, isn't it? The mightiest warrior."

The familiar voice and tone made Kangnan's face pale.

Miro was approaching with a smile.

"Long time, no see? I heard our handsome guy was exerting himself, so I thought I'd come and give some encouragement. Have you been well?" Gaold's meeting was heart-thumping, but creating a cheesy scene was the last thing on his mind.

"Let's not go beyond kissing."

Only after setting that boundary was Miro able to come north, but Gaold remained still.

"What? Overwhelmed with emotion?" The moment Miro sensed killing intent, her eyes widened, and immediately the Air Press dropped.

Boom!

Miro quickly created distance as the avatar of Thousand-Hand Avalokitesvara rose behind her.

It was instinctual.

"Run!"

As Kangnan shouted, Gaold charged at Miro, firing his air gun.

"Ugh!"

The technique of Thousand-Hand Avalokitesvara shortened the time, striking the bullets, but she could not bring herself to attack Gaold.

"I'll kill you!"

Gaold, gripping Miro's neck, forced her to the ground.

The force was bone-crushing, but what was more shocking was the look in Gaold's eyes.

"Die. Just die."

It was pure hatred.

"Gaold... why?"

"The feeling of love tends to fade. Gaold was remarkable for keeping it even amidst pain. But now, he's reached his limit."

Even that memory was fading.

"Gaold chose to replace love with hatred. That way, he can hold on a bit longer."

"Ugh..."

As Gaold tightened his grip on Miro's neck, Zulu coldly observed and then spoke in Middle Eastern dialect.

"It's very human."
