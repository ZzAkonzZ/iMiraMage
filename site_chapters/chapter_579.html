Chapter 579:

"Damn it! The impact went through. It feels like my bones are shattering."

Even though he had surrounded himself with the thickest ice wall possible, it had all burst apart.

"Ugh…"

Frings' shoulders shook.

"Hahaha! I see! You're angry, aren't you? So you unleashed your hidden power?"

"I haven't even opened Immortal Function yet."

The laughter abruptly stopped, and a cold murderous intent appeared in Frings' eyes.

"Do you know why I was able to overpower you? Because a day has passed."

"A day?"

The power of a day is great.

"I've already experienced your abilities. That's why I could prepare countermeasures."

Shirone's eyes flashed as he spoke.

"Today's me is different from yesterday's me."

"Ah, I see."

Frings shook off the ice on his arms.

"In short, a wise saying. Then I have no choice but to counter with another wise saying."

He spread his limbs and shouted.

"Tomorrow's me will be different from today's me!"

'What a lunatic.'

As Shirone dashed forward, Frings twisted his mouth into a grotesque grin and cast a spell.

Ice Wave!

A Photon Cannon shattered the incoming wave of ice.

'Immortal Function. The power is definitely different.'

As Shirone flew into the sky, he activated Shining Chain, binding Frings' entire body.

Ice Shackles.

As the ice clung and trapped the light, Frings broke the chains with force.

"Too slow."

Hearing Shirone's voice from behind, Frings' eyes widened.

"Hiyaaa!"

Ice Demon.

With a cracking sound, dozens of icicles shot out from behind, but Shirone was already in front of him.

"I said, too slow."

'Damn it…!'

A curse flashed through Frings' mind.

'You're just fast…'

As the Shining Chain wrapped around him, Frings' body spun dozens of times in an instant.

"Aaaargh!"

The blood rushed to his head, making it feel like it would explode, and Frings' spirit zone disappeared.

The textbook move of a grand mage is the head, something Shirone had learned from Kaiden.

As Shirone swung the Shining Chain with all his might, Frings' body fell vertically.

At the crash site, two overlapping pulses created by time division were pulsating.

'Ice Armor…!'

In the final moment, Frings' strong willpower allowed him to cover his entire body with ice armor.

But even that shattered into pieces in the storm of mass between the pulses.

"Aaaargh!"

He was flung out, rolling on the ground, his fingers grotesquely bent.

"Ugh…"

Shirone walked over to Frings, who was crawling on the ground.

"Hand over the master card."

"…"

Giving up the master card wasn't a big deal, but the thought of surrendering to Shirone's will was worse than death.

"It's in my underwear. Take it if you can."

"I will."

As Shirone approached, Frings suddenly spoke.

"You've been using magic since you were four."

There was no response.

"Do you want to know why you maintained your path as a genius ice mage after Overflow? Shall I tell you?"

A Photon Cannon appeared in Shirone's hand.

"For this very moment!"

Frings shouted as he cast the fire spell Fire Flame towards the maintenance factory.

"Crazy…!"

Shirone quickly distanced himself, carrying the still-recovering Sabina into the sky.

Boom!

The maintenance factory exploded with a loud noise.

"Hahaha! Kill me, Shirone!"

Frings, with all his fingers broken, limped into the flames.

'He's out of his mind.'

Destroying the factory so recklessly meant he no longer cared about being a student.

"What should we do? Won't we all get expelled at this rate?"

Sabina asked, watching the factory explode repeatedly.

"It'll be fine, probably."

Not because it was a manageable problem.

With the world's attention focused on them, the faculty would somehow handle today's events as an accident.

"Let's go back. It's time for the meeting."

The fifth day of Scramble Royal ended.

Shirone's team waited for Nade at their hideout until an hour past midnight.

"He's not coming."

Iruki said.

"And he won't come. He's not the type to miss a meeting without notice."

Shirone asked with a worried expression.

"Shouldn't we look for him? He might be injured."

Sabina glanced at Iruki.

'As expected… Iruki knows.'

How many people in the graduating class could injure Nade?

Even if that were the case, he wasn't someone who couldn't even send a rescue signal.

'The last place Nade was active…'

Iruki recalled the coordinates.

'It's closest to Anchal.'

That was why he suspected Nade's absence was due to his own decision.

'Anchal's mind projection. Definitely Nade's nemesis.'

Having reached that conclusion, Iruki spoke.

"Let's wait until tomorrow morning. For now, let's review without Nade."

As soon as Iruki finished speaking, Amy brought up the main point.

"Eden completed Infinity (○○○○○○) today."

There was a moment of silence.

"I also clashed with Eden in the third round. But…"

Shirone shook his head with a weary expression.

Iruki, who had blown up a mountain peak with Nuclear Fusion, understood his feelings better than anyone.

"We need more power. Either increase the explosive reaction or…"

Iruki looked at Shirone.

"What about Ataraxia? You faced it, so you should have a rough idea."

If the allied team was going all out, Shirone had no choice but to respond in kind.

"I'll give it a try. I'll settle it tomorrow."

"How long does it take to charge?"

"If I do my best, just under 20 seconds."

His friends' jaws dropped.

'That's incredibly fast.'

A year ago, Ataraxia took over a minute.

"We have two days left. We need to find a way to break Eden's Infinity within that time."

"There's one advantage."

Amy said.

"Eden can't call Kang anymore. Because… he's stripped."

You can't call Kang without a master card.

Dorothy said.

"Where could he have hidden it? If I were a scout, I could have found it."

"They calculated and decided on this strategy. But Hickory can store scrambles like Pisho."

Shirone said.

"If Eden can't call Kang, we can bomb without worrying about Pao."

"On the other hand, it means he's that confident."

Confidence in invincibility.

Zero offensive power throughout his life.

He couldn't protect allies or turn the tide, but in this game, it was an absolute ability.

"Even if Eden can't call Kang, Scramble Royal ends in two days. We should prepare our own Infinity in case Shirone can't break the shield."

Dorothy's point was valid.

"From tomorrow, let's save scrambles. Gather six and complete Infinity in one go."

"The enemies won't stay idle."

"It depends on Dorothy."

Iruki looked at Dorothy, who smiled.

'It seems she's overcome her trauma.'

Shirone said.

"I need to hold Infinity to face Eden. In case Eden abandons defense and calls Kang. If we gather scrambles, let's reshuffle my hand first."

Currently, Shirone had collected six cards, so he needed to reshuffle to obtain Infinity.

"That's another problem. No one on our side has a higher hand than Shirone's Oblivion (●○●●○●). We need to create another Infinity…"

Iruki stopped speaking, and everyone turned to him.

"What's wrong? Is there a problem?"

"Wait a minute."

Iruki's eyes moved as fast as his thoughts.

"Shirone, about Shibulsangpokmae. Tell me all the results from opening random cards so far. I can't know the results from reversing time."

"Got it. So… on the first day, I tried to create a trinity with random cards…"

"Just the results."

Shirone recalled and listed them in order.

"Black, white, white, black…"

Since Shirone had opened random cards multiple times to get the best hand, he had more results than others.

At that point, Iruki calculated all the combinations and reshuffles of the hands of the five team members and six allied team members over five days.

"It's different. The probabilities are different."

"What do you mean? Probabilities…"

Iruki muttered, extending his hand.

"Wait. Huh? This doesn't match."

He retraced his steps and finally found and corrected the error.

"I see. It resets every 24 hours. This matches."

"What are you talking about? Explain."

Iruki raised his head.

"This is a real card game."

"…which you must have realized by now."

Hershey said, and Pisho asked.

"How can you be sure? No matter how fast you calculate, paying attention is a different matter."

"Moving a servant isn't intuitive. If a normal person acts on a thought, a servant creates all possible actions and makes the best choice. They live in the moment. It's inefficient, but their thought speed is so fast that you can't tell the difference. By the fifth day, they'll extract meaningful data from the hands so far."

"Will they manipulate probabilities from tomorrow?"

Hershey raised a finger.

"Seventy-two scrambles are summoned daily. The number of black and white cards is exactly half, 36 each. Think of it as laying down hands with seventy-two cards."

Anchal asked.

"Random, but countable?"

"Exactly. It's just like a real card game. There are 36 black cards and 36 white cards. One player takes the black cards, and the other takes the white. The probability shifts based on the number of cards remaining. For example, if all 36 black cards have already been revealed and a random card is opened, what do you think will happen?"

"A white card will appear because there are no black cards left."

"Correct. Knowing just this can help you gain an advantage in the game. Like I did."

"…So that's the strategy you were using during the match."

Iruki continued, "Exactly. There's no other way to explain how Hershy kept securing favorable cards. With Sona's search ability and probability manipulation, they kept control of the match."

Shirone asked, "But even so, there must have been times when they had no choice but to open a random card, right? They could have gotten a full set loss, so it shouldn’t have been that easy to maintain control."

"That’s where another rule comes into play." Iruki turned his palm upward. "A revealed losing hand—meaning a hand lost during 'Kang'—isn’t discarded. Instead, it’s reshuffled back into the random pool."

"So, it’s like a shuffle?"

Hershy gave a thumbs-up with both hands. "Let’s assume there are twenty cards in total. Anchal has six black cards, and Pisho has six white cards. In this state, if someone opens a random card, the probability of drawing either black or white is exactly 50 percent. But what happens if Pisho calls 'Kang' and forces Anchal’s cards to be revealed?"

Anchal responded, "The six black cards I have would be reshuffled back into the pool."

"Exactly. Since there are relatively more black cards than white cards in the reshuffled pool, the probability of drawing black increases."

"So that’s card counting."

Hershy nodded. "Additionally, the cards you held yesterday have no bearing on today’s probabilities. Therefore, by calling 'Kang' to win or intentionally revealing an ally’s hand, you can manipulate the probabilities."

"Here, we need to consider two assumptions," Iruki added. "If Hershy figured out this rule on his own, he wouldn’t know the information I now possess. That’s because the probability was calculated using a technique called 'Simultaneous Probability Explosion.' However, if he received information from someone else, then it’s a different story."

"What information do you know?" Shirone asked.

Iruki and Hershy spoke simultaneously, "The probability of 'R' splits into two types."

Iruki pointed at Shirone. "The reason I was initially confused during my calculations was that Shirone used 'Simultaneous Probability Explosion' about five times in succession, and the resulting probability deviated significantly from the average. Then I realized something—at one point, Shirone intentionally didn’t accept a 'Kang' challenge."

"Yeah, I could’ve won, but I purposely didn’t take it because I wanted to force a reveal and reset my combinations."

"That was the key variable. From this, we can infer another hidden rule."

"A hidden rule?"

"In 'Kang' duels, revealed cards are reshuffled back into the pool. However, if the cards are revealed due to a time-out, they aren’t reshuffled—they’re discarded."
