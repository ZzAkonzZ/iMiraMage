Chapter 251: System Operation (4)

In truth, Overflow isn’t such a big deal.

To the average person, it may seem like the eccentricity of a genius, but if you were to visit the Royal Magic Academy, it would be full of such kids.

Only those who stood out from the crowd and then fought through fierce competition could even begin to be recognized as geniuses in the world of talent.

Still, since she had taken on the task, Olivia couldn’t just handle it carelessly, so Olivia walked towards Dante with a smile that made many students fall in love.

“Hello? You must be Dante?”

Dante didn't even raise his head, let alone respond. Olivia glanced down at the grayish hair that resembled storm clouds. Realizing this wouldn’t be easy, she sat down in front of him to meet his gaze.

The boy continued to stare at the grass beneath him.

‘What is he looking at?’

Olivia also lowered her head to follow his gaze.

It was just an ordinary lawn, not the kind of lawn where you would expect to find buried treasure or a corpse.

With patience and persistence, a minor detail hidden in the scenery came to light.

It was a procession of ants.

‘Is he interested in insects?’

Olivia asked Dante.

“Do you like ants?”

“….”

As expected, there was no answer. But Olivia didn’t press him.

Having raised students ranging from as young as four to over thirty, she knew how to handle situations like this.

“I don’t really like ants. They’re creepy. When they get into the house, they even chew on expensive furniture.”

Olivia babbled on and on about meaningless things.

“When I was little, I once saw ants swarming all over a dead worm. Ugh. Since then, I’ve been scared of ants. You know, that feeling when they start crawling all over you…”

“It’s not about ants.”

Dante spoke for the first time.

Olivia smiled in triumph.

She didn’t know what kind of awakening had triggered the Overflow, but he was still a child. Now that he had started talking, he wouldn’t be able to hold back.

“I’m watching a channel.”

"A channel?"

Dante pointed at one of the ants moving through the grass.

“This ant is called 1011001 (Y). I named it. Its movement range is about four meters. It’s a scout who checks the territory by going around the perimeter.”

Olivia observed closely.

Despite giving it the name 1011001, there was nothing particularly distinctive about the ant compared to the others.

Dante pointed to an ant climbing on a bent blade of grass.

“This ant is 1010111 (W). It moves vertically. It climbs the grass to collect moisture. The same goes for 1010101 (U) and 1101001 (I).”

Olivia was perplexed. She couldn’t tell if he had genuinely memorized the names of the ants or was just making them up to mess with an adult.

“Ants serve a specific function. Together, they activate the whole. That is channel.”

If Dante could read channels, not by seeing shapes but by reading flow, then it's possible that he could even distinguish the names of the ants.

But was such a thing even possible?

Strictly speaking, everything in the world is a channel.

If someone could break down an ant society into units of information, then there would be nothing in the world they couldn’t analyze.

As her eyes adjusted to the surroundings, ants began to swarm Olivia's field of vision.

Now that she looked closer, a 4-meter radius was teeming with ants.

Although it gave her goosebumps, her curiosity about Dante outweighed her discomfort.

“So, you can read the channel?”

Dante lifted his head, allowing Olivia to see his face for the first time.

He was a beautiful child. One of his eyes was bruised purple, probably from being beaten by his friends.

“That must’ve hurt a lot.”

“It’s okay. I don’t have to go to the academy anymore.”

Most teachers would comfort the child and try to convince them to return to the academy. Even without citing empty social conventions, society’s expectation would be to stand firm and face challenges rather than run away.

But Olivia didn't convince Dante. This child was different. Her instincts as an archmage told her so.

“Why don’t you interact with your friends? They said you didn’t say a single word.”

“If nothing can be communicated, it’s the same as not speaking. There’s no reason to speak, so I didn’t.”

Dante recalled the moment and made a disgusted face.

“Friends talk a lot. But it’s just noise. Ants are different. They have a purpose. Even ants that seem purposeless are supporting another purpose.”

Olivia finally confirmed that this was Overflow.

When you delve deep into anything, a world completely different from common sense reveals itself.

Dante had entered that world far too early.

“This ant is 1010110 (V), but it just follows 1010111 (W). But it’s doing something very important. It’s a channel. If no one follows 1010111, nothing gets transmitted. If you look closely, most ants exist just to maintain the channel. Very few ants do truly special work. Can you tell them apart?”

Olivia awkwardly smiled and shook her head.

No matter how exceptional a mage she was, she couldn’t distinguish the roles of every member in the ant kingdom.

“If you watch them all day, you start to realize something interesting. All the channels are connected. For example, this 1111 ant only follows the one in front of it, maintaining the channel. But surprisingly, it’s involved in almost every channel. If this ant didn’t exist...”

Dante lowered his finger and squashed 1111. Olivia frowned, but moments later, she noticed a shift in the ant kingdom and widened her eyes in surprise.

The line of ants broke apart, scattering in different directions. As the line expanded into a chaotic web, it showed that without the channel, the system became meaningless.

The boy watched the ant kingdom collapse with sparkling eyes, a smile that was eerily innocent.

Olivia felt a shiver. The ant Dante crushed was neither a soldier ant nor a special one.

The system cannot collapse by subduing a single ant.

No, that's what she thought until now. But Dante's actions were proving her assumptions wrong.

A blind spot in the system, invisible to the eye.

By suppressing Ant 1111, all the variables resulting from that act were being calculated inside Dante's mind.

It was clear that Dante had a perfect understanding of the ant kingdom's systems, something even the ants themselves weren't aware of.

Dante looked up at Olivia and said with a smile.

“The queen will starve to death.”

Another Sky (1)

Olivia smiled warmly as she watched Dante, who had grown over the past eight years.

"Dante has eyes that can read the channels. And through training, he’s sharpened that ability even further."

Shirone, having shut off Immortal Function, was busy dodging the bombardment. A total of 86 mansions kept changing their structures without pause, leaving no time for any other thoughts.

On the other hand, Dante, who had left the battle to Automaton, seemed relaxed. He could have sat down to read a book at that moment and been fine.

'But I'll have to pay him back for what he's done.'

Dante floated above Pascal Magic Circle. As he slowly advanced, the hundreds of magic circles connected to Pascal moved forward toward Shirone at the same speed.

It was as if a huge space was moving in its entirety.

Whenever Shirone moved, magic poured out from all sides, like gun turrets rotating to fire.

It felt like Shirone was fighting an entire army. A massive force was breaking through his Spirit Zone and advancing on him.

Iruki's face was distorted terribly.

'Damn it! How is such magic even possible…'

He couldn’t think of a way to counter Dante's system. Shirone hadn’t sustained a critical hit yet, but dodging an automated machine indefinitely was impossible.

Shirone also knew that fact. But he had no choice. And it wasn’t as hopeless as Iruki thought.

'I have no choice but to try.'

Shirone conceived a possibility and dashed toward the core of Pascal. The mansions in Dante's system moved faster, amplifying their firepower, but the quicker they attacked, the quicker Shirone linked his teleportation moves.

He had to move.

He had to synchronize with Dante's channel.

As time passed, the students began to stir. Although it was a subtle difference that could not be seen with the eyes, it felt as if Shirone's movements were gradually interlocking with Dante's system.

Nade jumped up and shouted.

"Teleportation! That’s the answer!"

Iruki also raised the corners of his mouth as if he'd just realized something.

"Teleportation is information. He’s blending into Dante’s system to destroy it from within."

Heaven’s Trial bracelets became even hotter. It was a sign that Pascal was overloaded from tracking Shirone’s movements.

Sienna confirmed that as Shirone's movements became more complex, Dante's mental strength gauge decreased rapidly.

‘So the outcome is still undecided?’

Alpheas let out a ragged breath. He felt a slight shiver run down his spine.

'Dante's ability is formidable. But insight—it's also a terrifying talent that can lead someone to the top of the world.'

In a short amount of time, Shirone had perfectly pinpointed Pascal’s weakness. Dante’s Automaton was perfect, but the adjective "perfect" could not be applied to humans.

Pascal's only error.

The only error in the perfect Pascal was Dante himself.

“I would like to applaud Dante for his perfect design. However, he is still young. His mental strength is not keeping up with his technique.”

Olivia had to admit it. It wasn't that she was unaware of Dante's weakness. No, could this really be called a weakness?

Being young is not a sin.

Dante would continue to grow, and one day, he would surely reach a level where he could maintain Pascal for extended periods of time.

But none of these facts could serve as an excuse, because those who dealt with Dante were in the same position.

Above all, Dante's endurance was by no means lacking compared to others his age.

Dante's only mistake in this fight was that his opponent was Shirone, whose endurance was unmatched.

'Perhaps… they just met too soon.'

It was regrettable that one of them, either Shirone or Dante, would have to bear the burden of defeat.

'No, it's the same for everyone. There's no such thing as the right time.'

The battle had already begun, and there was no turning back. And when analyzed coldly, Dante's odds of winning were still over 70%.

Though Dante could only maintain Pascal for another 10 minutes, it seemed unlikely that Shirone could survive more than five minutes within Dante's system.

Olivia said.

"Even so, Shirone won't be able to beat Dante. It's not about underestimating Shirone, it's just that the rarity of their talents is different. Shirone doesn't have the means to destroy Dante's system."

Alpheas couldn't refute. Even in his view, there was still no advantage for Shirone.

But even so, he was a child who made him harbor expectations.

'Is there something I don't know, Shirone?'

Shirone flew all over the entire Heaven’s Trial arena. Throughout the process of dodging Dante's barrage of magic, Shirone had been hit countless times, but with each failure, he immediately adapted.

Iruki was the first to realize and speak.

"Alright. He's perfectly in."

At first, no one understood. But after 10 seconds, the students began to murmur in amazement.

In a battlefield where infinite firepower was raining down, Shirone was freely moving about. It was as if the mansions made way for him as he moved.

Of course, it was just an illusion, since there was no connection between the movements of Shirone and Pascal. Nevertheless, this phenomenon could only happen under one condition.

"Shirone has perfectly deciphered Automaton's patterns."
