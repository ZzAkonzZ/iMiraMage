Chapter 522: A New Contract (1)

Roughly 34 kilometers off the coast of Galliant Island, there lies an island not marked on any map.

Known only to old fishermen who have sailed for over forty years, this “White Island” had long ago been privatized for a hefty sum by an organization from the Galliant autonomous district.

And now, on that very White Island, one of the administrators of the Ivory Tower, Keira, was serving a period of probation while awaiting further orders from her superiors.

The scenery of the shallow seas was filled with mystery.

Countless independently evolved flora and fauna, and mynas that sang in flute-like melodies.

Keira was preparing an early breakfast in a cabin built beside a small waterfall.

It was a modest meal, but for someone who had always been buried in work, it was a long-awaited respite.

“That smells good. Is it rabbit stew?”

Keira froze, the ladle in her hand halting mid-stir.

She hadn’t sensed a presence—but the voice alone was enough.

Armin smiled as he spoke.

“What? You’re going to slam the door in my face?”

But Keira didn’t respond. She acted as if she hadn’t heard him at all and continued stirring the pot.

“…I’ll come back when you’ve cooled off.”

As Armin turned away, Keira suddenly hurled the ladle across the floor in frustration.

It clattered loudly against the ground as she burst into tears and ran into Armin’s arms with all her strength.

“You bastard! I thought you were dead! I really… really thought you were dead!”

Armin gently stroked her back.

“I’m back. Just like I promised.”

Tears still streaming down her face, Keira looked up at him with a bright smile.

“You’re really back for good? Sit down. You must be hungry.”

Her hands busied themselves with the meal, and her mouth didn’t stop moving either.

“I got disciplined for tearing up the Accord Oath. They told me to stay here until you returned. But it was worth the wait. Oh, let’s go to the Ivory Tower right after breakfast. We have to report in.”

“No, Keira. I’m not going back to the Ivory Tower.”

Keira turned, a blank look on her face.

“What are you talking about?”

“I have something to do. I’ll be working with a new team for a while. I know you’ll understand.”

“Understand? You might’ve escaped death, but there’s no way you can avoid the Tower’s surveillance now that you’re back.”

“Of course. But it’ll be fine. The Tower won’t have a choice but to accept my proposal this time.”

“What the hell happened? What new team?”

“Let’s eat first. I’ll explain.”

With a calm smile, Armin sat down at the table.

Something told him it was going to be a long day.

Capital city of the Kingdom of Tormia: Bashka.

Kuan had returned to the Kaizen Swordsmanship Academy. He packed his few belongings into a single bag and changed clothes.

His eyes scanned his wardrobe more seriously than usual, but none of the options satisfied him.

Should I cut off the sleeve? Or just leave it as is?

Just then, the door slammed open, and a sharp voice rang out.

“You crazy bastard!”

It was Kiyora Elise, a surgeon at the swordsmanship academy.

“You’re here?”

Kuan greeted her briefly, then turned back to the mirror.

“What? ‘You’re here’? Damn right I’m here, you bastard! Do you have any idea how much trouble I went through to cancel the resignation letter you submitted today?”

Kuan had missed classes without notice and was promptly dismissed from his position. But Elise had pulled strings to put it on hold.

Given all that, it was impossible to describe the emotions she felt when Kuan finally returned.

He had come back.

Missing not only a leg, but an arm as well.

Especially for Elise—an expert in nerve reattachment—it broke her heart to see her beloved friend in such a state again.

Whether he knew that or not, Kuan looked completely unbothered, like someone who’d just taken out the trash.

“Why’d you cancel it? I spent all night writing that damn thing. Give it back.”

“Ugh, damn it!”

Elise threw the resignation letter to the floor.

She wasn’t training a stray mutt. What was the point of all this if he was going to leave anyway?

“What the hell’s wrong with you? You could’ve stayed here through the break and thought things through! I even got you paid leave!”

“I got a new job. Besides, I can’t be a teacher anymore. My sword can’t be passed down to the students. You should start looking for someone else.”

Elise, a fellow instructor at the academy, had a vague sense of what Kuan had sacrificed in exchange for his missing arm.

Kuan before and after his return felt like completely different people. His aura had changed entirely.

“So you’re not going to tell me anything? You don’t want to be friends anymore, is that it?”

“I’m too old for emotional blackmail. By the way, what do you think? Should I cut the sleeve or leave it?”

“How the hell should I know?! You already cut off your damn arm—what difference does a sleeve make?! You’re driving me nuts!”

“I’ll just leave it, then.”

It would be inconvenient in combat, but visually, it looked better this way.

That’s when Elise finally realized just how much Kuan had changed deep inside.

The way he cared about his appearance, the calmness in his demeanor—he had made up his mind to step back into the world after a long nightmare.

“You really won’t regret this?”

Kuan turned to Elise and said,

“I’ll be fine.”

She had no words.

How could she stop her beloved friend, who was smiling for the first time in ten years?

“Thank you. I’ll keep in touch.”

After patting her on the shoulder, Kuan left the room, taking one last look at the familiar hallways and the training ground where he’d drilled countless students.

Even the scenery he once loathed felt nostalgic today.

I wonder if that guy’s doing well.

Rian, who had dropped out to serve as a knight’s apprentice.

Looking back now, Kuan realized he was actually a step behind Rian.

We’ll meet again someday.

That’s the fate of swordsmen.

Kuan climbed into the carriage he had reserved and told the coachman to head for Kentra City.

A famous livestock region in Tormia, it was where he had shared a meal with Siena before going to Heaven.

And today, he was going to meet her again.

“Hope I’m not too late.”

Arriving twenty minutes late, Kuan flung open the carriage door and entered the restaurant.

“Damn.”

It had the same cozy vibe as before, and most of the customers were dressed modestly.

But to them, Kuan stood out.

Wearing a sword and bearing a rough look, the restaurant went quiet, and eyes turned his way.

That’s when Siena stood up from a table in the corner.

“Over here, sir.”

Kuan stared at her, momentarily dazed.

With her glasses off and hair down, she looked like a completely different person.

As the beautiful woman welcomed him, other customers exchanged puzzled glances between the two.

Even if he didn’t want to care, he couldn’t help but be aware of the attention.

But only Siena was looking at him with genuine warmth.

“Sorry I’m late.”

“It’s alright. Bashka’s quite far. Have you been doing well?”

“Yeah, well…”

Siena had reached out first. The promise she’d made in Heaven must have weighed on her.

Kuan ordered lamb steak.

Life had felt unfamiliar ever since he lost his arm, but there was no need to suddenly change his habits.

But he soon regretted it.

The meat was tougher than expected and hard to cut, and he couldn’t exactly show off his swordsmanship with a steak knife.

Pfft!

A stifled laugh from a nearby table made Kuan pause mid-cut.

When he looked up, a woman was covering her mouth in embarrassment while the man across from her looked awkwardly away.

“Don’t mind them,” Siena said.

“I know better than anyone how strong you are. The stronger a person is, the more composure they have.”

In Heaven, Siena had realized her own weakness—and what the strong truly carried on their shoulders.

“I’ll cut it for you.”

Before Kuan could say anything, Siena picked up his plate and cut the steak for him.

Maybe it worked—people’s gazes softened noticeably.

Of course, it was probably because they mistakenly thought Siena was helping a disabled person out of pity.

Kuan simply watched her, as if entranced.

He noticed how differently she treated him now.

“Miss Siena.”

Still focused on slicing the meat, she replied,

“Yes?”

“You don’t have to go this far. I appreciate the kindness, but if you’re doing this because you feel indebted to me—”

A piece of steak suddenly thrust toward him cut off his words.

If this were swordplay, it was an excellent thrust.

When Kuan looked up, he saw Siena’s face flushed red like a beet, unable to meet his eyes.

“W-Would you like a bite?”

Sometimes, emotions push reason off-balance, and people act irrationally. That’s where Siena was now.

She had developed feelings for Kuan—but never intended to go this far, this fast.

Sensing the mood, the other diners eagerly awaited Kuan’s response.

After a long pause, Kuan slowly smiled, accepted the piece of steak, and placed it in his mouth.

“Ah…”

Then, politely took the fork and gave a slight bow.

“Thank you.”

If Elise had seen this, she’d probably have shown up at his grave just to mock him.

But to Kuan, this was enough.

No one could blame him for carrying at least one happy memory with him before setting off on a long journey.

After the meal, Siena hesitated in front of the carriage that would take her back.

“When the new semester starts, I don’t think we’ll be able to see each other very often.”

“Yes, that’s probably true. Don’t worry about it.”

Siena tilted her head slightly, but she wasn’t exactly skilled in matters of romance, so she fumbled to find the right words.

“If… if it’s okay with you… would you like to exchange letters?”

“Letters?”

Kuan blinked at the sudden suggestion. He fell into thought for a while before nodding.

“Sure. I’ll be waiting.”

Maybe she’d been tense, because her face instantly brightened.

“Okay. Then I’ll send them to the Swordsmanship Academy.”

“The Academy… Ah, yes. That should work.”

“Well then, please take care on your way back, Mr. Kuan.”

Siena gave a polite bow and climbed into the carriage.

Her face, just before the door closed, was visibly flushed.

She would be exchanging letters and getting to know someone she hadn’t even known existed just a short while ago.

It was a fluttering excitement she hadn’t felt since her childhood, since the time she owed Armin a debt she could never repay.

Kuan watched the departing carriage for a long time before turning toward the city gate.

As he entered the forest, his companions, who had arrived ahead and were waiting, revealed themselves.

Armin of the Light asked, “Did you enjoy your meal?”

“I was fulfilling a prior engagement, not there for pleasure,” Kuan replied honestly.

Siena was a good person. But after learning the secrets of the world, Kuan had chosen to protect her over indulging in his own happiness.

“This isn’t enough. I need to get stronger.”

“Jeez, what’s with the heavy mood? You look like someone coming back from their last supper.”

From the other side of the thicket, Miro and Arius emerged.

Still wearing Armand’s robe, Shirone was fast asleep on Arius’s back.

Miro tapped Shirone’s head and said,

“Well, we’re all here. Time to head out. We need to wake this brat up if we’re going to move on to the next thing.”

After returning from Heaven, Miro had asked Armin and Kuan to help with her work.

Though Armin and Kuan were technically rivals in a sense, to her, they were both too valuable to give up.

Especially Kuan—his unique swordsmanship, awakened in Heaven, was powerful enough to reshape the very foundations of swordsmanship as the world knew it.

“I agreed to help, so I’ll go wherever I’m needed—wherever my sword is needed.”

Armin asked,

“So, how exactly are you planning to wake Shirone up?”

“It’s not entirely decided yet. But once I talk to her, I’m sure we’ll find a way.”

“Her?”

Miro smiled.

“Empress Teraje of Kashan.”
