Chapter 1252:

At the top of the Ivory Tower where the sky was visible—

"Gr-... Great Star..."

There was no killing intent. No hostility.

Even if there had been, Shirone probably wouldn’t have been able to respond.

It was that overwhelming.

"Why... why me...?"

In his lonely battle for humanity, the depth of the trust Shirone had placed in Great Star was immeasurable.

Great Star calmly withdrew her hand.

"Gah!"

Shirone collapsed to the ground, and she briefly looked down at her bloodstained hand.

Xing lowered her gaze.

"Shirone..."

A clean, fatal blow.

The faint convulsions running through his body were all Shirone could manage.

"You see?"

Great Star shook the blood from her hand.

"Why do something so pointless?"

Xing trembled as a chill ran down her spine, as if even her soul had frozen over.

"Did you lie to us...?"

"What are you talking about?"

Even Xing, who hadn’t fully trusted her, asked the question—because she had forgotten one simple fact.

She is not human.

"I am an Administrator. I cannot allow someone who has made contact with the Outer World to live."

Great Star nudged Shirone’s body with her foot, flipping him over to reveal his twisted expression.

"Urgh... hhk!"

"I stopped his heart. Revival is impossible. I hope you won’t waste your energy."

Shirone gritted his teeth.

‘I’m dying.’

He tried to reactivate his heart with Miracle Stream, but it was as if it had turned to iron—completely unmoving.

‘She embedded Law-based code... I... can’t regenerate...’

His vision tunneled, narrowing to a pinhole as he drifted on the edge of consciousness.

In his final moments, what came to mind was—

—Save yourself from the terrible truth.

‘Damn it...’

Even the faint sound of breath disappeared. Shirone lay still, his eyes rolled back.

He was dead.

"...No..."

Xing stared in shock.

Though she had watched Shirone die right before her eyes, her mind couldn’t process it.

Only one emotion moved her now—rage.

"I’ll kill you."

As Xing glared with eyes filled with murderous intent, Great Star simply walked forward, unmoved.

"You’re still talking nonsense. I don’t possess life. And now, humanity is finished."

She stopped in front of Argones.

"Begin."

"Hmm."

Administrators may govern independent realms, but in the end, they are still programs operating under divine logic.

Xing didn’t know how much they truly understood about each other...

"Cell Buster."

Argones didn’t look pleased.

His body began to split into countless anti-cells. Xing, snapping back to her senses, reached out.

"No!"

But now that Great Star had regained her freedom, there was no one left who could stop them.

"Guh!"

Great Star blocked Xing’s Law. Argones, transformed into a swarm of cells, ascended into the sky.

‘It’s over.’

Because her convictions were so strong, the moment she thought that, even her will to resist vanished.

"Ugh..."

Xing fell to her knees, tears spilling down her face.

Great Star approached her.

"I didn’t want to go this far. If I could, I wanted to live with the children I created."

"S-So how... how did this..."

But Xing could no longer hear anything. Great Star turned away.

"In the next reincarnation..."

She ascended into the sky as well, leaving only her cold voice behind in the Ivory Tower.

"Try not to make the same mistake."

What remained was Xing, crying—Umji and Yangji, stunned—and the lifeless body of Shirone.

"Aaaah! AAAAAHH!"

Ymir’s Mind.

Shirone’s group exited through the gate installed in Layer 1 of the deep subconscious, then turned back with shocked expressions.

“Luber…”

Luber, holding Ultima in his hands, was still inside Ymir’s Mind.

“I’m sorry.”

Sein, Miro, Gaold, and Kangnan didn’t understand what he meant.

“5 Star,” said Monga, a pained look on his face.

“You made a terrible mistake.”

And at that very moment, in the Ivory Tower, Great Star stopped Shirone’s heart.

“Gah!”

They understood the situation too late—Shirone collapsed before they could do anything.

“You—!”

Miro was the first to charge forward, with Gaold, Sein, and Kangnan right behind.

“The Outer World,” said Luber.

“No one should ever make contact with it.”

As the incarnation of Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara raised his hands and chanted, the gate closed.

“One second.”

Ymir’s superego had already thrown a punch that nearly reached them.

BOOOOOOM!

A massive shockwave swept through Layer 1’s atmosphere, exploding in a blinding flash.

In the aftermath, with everything blown away, Luber stood before the giant’s frozen fist.

“Take it,” he said, offering Ultima.

But the giant’s fist didn’t move.

The superego—Ymir—asked, “Is this a trade?”

“No. Is there even anything left for me to give?”

Shirone’s group had returned to reality. And what remained there was humanity’s ruin.

“Disgusting.”

Of course, if Ultima had been extracted, Ymir’s own future might’ve also been affected.

But if Luber had feared such consequences, he would never have let Goffin strike him.

And so—

“GRAAAAH!”

Ymir erupted in fury.

The superego’s roar was enough to stop the hearts of living beings.

Yet Luber stood his ground calmly.

“I’ll take it.”

The giant’s thumb and forefinger plucked up Ultima, and it dissolved instantly into his form. A black hole bloomed again in the sky.

“I hate being in debt. If there’s something you want, name it.”

“Will you be heading to the human world?”

“What if I am?”

It was the fiercest battlefield in the multiverse.

“Destroy it. Completely. If you're the last one left standing, I’ll acknowledge you as the strongest.”

Luber and Monga vanished.

“Hmph.”

Ymir wasn’t particularly displeased with the proposal—but the idea of a so-called god placing him beneath anyone else irritated him.

“Wait your turn. You’re next.”

In any case, a deal had been made, and Ymir’s role was now clear.

“Heh heh heh… Let’s have some real fun.”

The total extermination of humanity.

Xing’s heart sank endlessly.

“I failed…”

Soon the anti-cells would cover the world, and humanity would regress to nothing, passing once more through its earliest cells.

“If only I’d been a little stronger… if only I had pushed back harder…”

“5 Star.”

She turned to find Umji and Yangji standing nearby.

“Thank you… for everything.”

“You two…”

She realized what they intended.

“We don’t know how long we can hold out, but we can buy at least enough time for humanity to react.”

They would use the power of the Taiji to briefly delay the Law of the Cell Buster.

“No. I still need you both. Yahweh is dead. If you disappear too…”

Yangji shook her head.

“You already know, don’t you? Human thought can never fully reach the perfection of Taiji. We’re ready.”

RUMMMMMBLE—

Thunder echoed from the sky, and the silver anti-cells began to spread.

“There’s no time. Let us do this.”

Xing clenched her teeth.

Even if they delayed the Cell Buster for a moment—what meaning would it truly have?

But still—

“To fight until the very end…”

It meant taking responsibility.

“…Please.”

With Xing’s decision made, Umji and Yangji smiled and nodded.

“Farewell…”

Their bodies glowed brightly as they unlocked their Immortal Function, and their spirits dispersed into the world.

A few moments later—

RUMMMBLE. RUMMMBLE.

The anti-cells, which had covered a tenth of the sky, began to collapse inward into a single point.

And thus was born— a massive, moon-shaped cluster of cells.

Feeling the tremors through her body as if it might explode at any moment, Xing hugged herself tightly.

“I have to think.”

During this short time the two had bought them, humanity needed to find a way—any way to survive.

“Please…”

In a panic so deep it made her blood run dry, her eyes drifted toward Shirone’s cold, lifeless body.

“AAAAAAAH!”

Kangnan screamed and jolted awake.

From dream to dream, and again from dream to dream—after countless nested illusions, she finally arrived…

“Haah… haah…”

In a shrine that felt unfamiliar, yet strangely familiar.

“That was a nightmare…”

But only for a moment. She quickly realized where she was—Galliant Island, not far from Tormia.

“Shirone!”

It hadn’t been just a dream. She looked around—everyone in the group had woken up.

No… not everyone.

Miro quietly approached Arius, who lay peacefully on a bed soaked in blood.

“Goodbye…”

She touched his forehead and glanced around the shrine the Kergoines had prepared.

“No one came in.”

Though the space was sealed, the silence within felt deeper than death itself.

And then—another person still hadn’t awakened.

“Shirone! Wake up! Shirone!”

Kangnan shook his shoulders, but Shirone remained still, his eyes closed like a corpse.

“What’s going on?”

Gaold approached, and Sein replied.

“Must be the aftereffect of a simultaneous event. He must’ve taken fatal damage in another space.”

“But that’s strange. Simultaneous events rely on Incarnation Arts. If the damage was fatal, it shouldn’t hold up.”

After a moment of silence, Miro spoke.

“He’s dead.”

Everyone turned to her.

“Dead?”

“He took a fatal wound—but his mind remains conscious. In other words… his heart is dead, but his brain is still alive.”

Kangnan raised her arms.

“That doesn’t make any sense! If the heart’s stopped, that means he’s dead!”

“How would you know?”

Kangnan went quiet.

“Has your heart ever stopped before?”

“Th-that’s…”

“Of course it’s unusual. But because something unusual happened, we’re in this situation now. Meaning, Shirone is technically dead, but…”

Miro raised her index finger.

“He’s still alive.”

Thump…

‘Please! Please!’

Shirone desperately clenched his heart.

‘Beat!’

Thump. Thump!

At last, his heart began to pound again, and the shock coursed violently through his body.

“Haaaaagh!”

The moment he jolted upright, the most surprised one was Xing.

“Wh-what…?”

Staring in disbelief, she crawled over as she saw him writhing in pain.

“Shirone! Shirone!”

“Grrrragh!”

His body burned as if it were on fire, aching for blood, and the wound on his back remained open.

“Stop the bleeding first—!”

Blood poured out in rivers as Xing concentrated the power of Law.

Combined with the regenerative force of Miracle Stream, the bleeding stopped quickly.

“What happened? Weren’t you dead?”

“I was.”

His heart had stopped, so that much was true. But his brain hadn’t fully shut down.

“Sorry, Xing… because of me…”

“No.”

Xing shook her head.

“I didn’t follow you because of anyone else’s words. I agreed with you. You persuaded me.”

Shirone was grateful for those words.

“The Cell Buster has been activated. We need to find a way to stop it.”

“Where’s Great Star?”

This time, even Xing was angry.

“You still don’t get it? We were tricked. The Administrators are just tools of the gods!”

Shirone felt the same way. But…

“Why didn’t she go for my head?”

“…Huh?”

“Why the heart? No—she could’ve blown it apart. Why just stop it?”

“Are you crazy? She was trying to kill you!”

“I know. But I can’t help wondering—would a so-called perfect computational being make such a mistake?”

“…That…”

Xing had no answer.

“This is important. I need to know for sure.”

“Know what?”

“Maybe Great Star…”

Just like how the Jet he met in the Apocalypse was unlike the others—

“Maybe she had… an error.”

In other words— A heart.
