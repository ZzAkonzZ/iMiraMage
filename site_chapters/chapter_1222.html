Chapter 1222:

At Delta Headquarters, Code One, the summit-level meeting between nations, was progressing rapidly.

Shirone headed to the Jincheon sector.

“This evens the score.”

He had already secured commitments from the Southern Tribal Alliance, the Ivory Tower’s Corona, and the King of Arachne.

“Kesia and Paras will be impossible to persuade. If I can win over Jincheon and the Moon Kingdom, the advantage will tip in our favor.”

The fact that Yahweh was acting on behalf of Tormia was already known to all twelve nations.

Whatever their true feelings, they had nothing to lose by at least hearing him out.

The only nation that had flatly rejected him was Jive.

“Gis.”

Naturally so—when your nation was already poised to become the World Government, why introduce variables?

As Shirone finished that thought, he arrived at the Jincheon sector, where the Five Dragon Generals led him to Jin Kang’s chamber.

“You’ve come.”

Though his flesh was pale and barely clinging to life, Jin Kang still stood to greet him.

“What a resolute man.”

If human vitality could be measured in numbers, Jin Kang had less than 1% remaining.

“How are you feeling?”

“I’m holding up. So, Code One has been initiated. Meeting with the other kings—it must be about the vote?”

“Yes. This is the only way to stop Jive’s dominance. Please lend your strength to Tormia.”

Jin Kang gave a faint laugh.

“You underestimate me.” “Other nations may be kingdoms, but Jincheon is an empire—a world of its own. It cannot easily kneel to a single kingdom like Tormia.”

“Jive will become the World Government.”

“What difference does it make? Whether it’s Jive or Tormia, Jincheon can stand against anything.”

It was the pride of an emperor.

“Shirone, you’ve done your best. Jincheon is grateful to Yahweh. But this is not just a matter between individuals. Even if I die, Jincheon must continue.”

An empire formed by many united kingdoms—Shirone had expected this would be no easy task.

“I fully understand, Your Majesty. But now is the time for unity. Humanity is on the brink of extinction. Gis cannot unite us. Worse, rebellion will break out in regions that, like you, do not support him. Please leave it to me. I believe even Seongeum would want that.”

Jin Kang, who had been listening quietly, narrowed his eyes.

“Are you selling my daughter now?”

“That’s just the truth. Seongeum and I…”

Thud.

He stomped the floor so hard it shook the room.

Shirone worried he might have broken a bone, but more than that, he was amazed at Jin Kang’s remaining strength.

“My daughter is in hell.”

A demonic expression flickered in Jin Kang’s eyes.

This was the pain of a father who had buried his daughter in eternal torment—a wrath that had even twisted her own deadly magic into something more vile.

“Yahweh, do you know why I still live? To save my daughter, and to wipe out every last demon who threw her into that hell. Everything is ready. I’ll fire the Pama Gwangcheon Star at her—but the demons will finally understand what real hell is. I will never forgive them.”

“He’s already a ghost.”

He looked human on the outside, but his soul was steeped in madness.

With a cold wave of his hand, Jin Kang said:

“Go back. Betraying the greater cause may be a man’s shame, but I will no longer compromise with anyone. I reject your proposal.”

If Shirone had been anyone else—if he were not Yahweh—Jin Kang would’ve drawn his sword the moment his daughter’s name was mentioned.

“I can’t walk away like this. I came here to earn your vote, Your Majesty.”

“I do not repeat myself.”

Jin Kang reached for his sword’s hilt and stood from the chair as if he were drawing the blade itself.

“There is a way to bring back Seongeum—Princess Jin Seongeum.”

As Jin Kang was about to swing his sword in rage, he paused and looked up.

“...What?”

“She doesn’t have to die. There may be a way to bring your daughter back into the real world.”

Jin Kang’s beard trembled.

“You said there was no way. If this is a lie just to earn a vote…”

“Professor Etella is a bishop of the Carsis Monastic Order. She is also the teacher I respect most.”

Jin Kang fell silent.

“If Jin Seongeum chose eternal suffering for the sake of humanity, then my teacher is now carrying that same burden toward her own annihilation. It must be infinite pain.”

The candles flickering in all corners of the room swayed violently.

“If she succeeds… I have a plan. Your daughter might be able to return.”

“Guh!”

Jin Kang staggered.

“Your Majesty!”

The Five Dragon General tried to rush to his side, but Jin Kang raised a hand to stop him.

“What is her name again…?”

“Romi Etella.”

As he repeated the name again and again, Jin Kang wiped away tears with his trembling hands.

“Forgive me. Forgive this narrow heart that felt relief, even for a moment. I, Jin Kang…”

He raised his fists to the heavens in a warrior’s bow.

“I will not forget that name, even in death.”

“You must not.”

This time, Shirone’s voice was cold.

Not because Seongeum might live while Etella would die.

But because of the fury that boiled inside him—at a world where a single human being had to bear everything and sacrifice themselves.

“Why must someone…”

…believe they have to shoulder it all alone?

“If everyone gave just a little…”

…then even now, the world could shift in a massive upheaval, like a great earthquake.

“I’ll tell you the method.”

“I will transfer Jincheon’s vote to Tormia.”

An immediate trade.

It was a matter worth careful deliberation—but it was also a matter whose outcome was already decided.

“No need to go in circles. Tell me—how do you plan to bring my daughter back?”

Shirone raised his index finger.

“Pama Gwangcheon Star.”

Tormia Sector.

In a dark room lit by a single small lamp, Albino and Lupist sat drinking tea.

“One more hour until the finale begins. That will mark the official end of the Holy War ceremonies.”

It was said that representatives from every nation would participate in the grand closing event.

“It feels both long and short. I suppose we’ve done all we could.”

“Indeed. I don’t even know what variables might still appear. People think we can see far ahead, but in reality, even trying to see one second ahead is like groping in the dark. We’re all blind, stumbling forward, second by second.”

“Jive has rejected Code One.”

Albino let out a chuckle at the sudden, surprise announcement.

“So, you can throw a tantrum too? I get it. You’ve grown too large, and I’ve grown too old.”

Lupist sipped his tea.

“I’ve never seen you this sentimental, President of the Magic Association. Are you feeling guilty?”

“I do feel guilt. I just don’t let it sway me. Plu is a capable talent. It's a shame to use her like this.”

“She’s someone who could rule the world. Isn’t sacrificing her for the nation a fair enough price?”

“…I suppose so. Shirone is gathering votes from every nation—we can’t just take advantage of his efforts without giving something back.”

At this point, they needed a reason, a justification.

“Don’t worry too much. Plu will do well. She’ll handle the last problem for us.”

Albino sipped his tea, then asked,

“You seem quite fond of her. Could it be that you… feel something more?”

“No.”

Lupist straightened his posture.

“I don’t look at subordinates that way. What I believe is—she’s a key component of this kingdom.”

“Like Jane?”

At the mention of the former secretary of the Magic Association, Lupist fell silent.

Meanwhile, Gis, having finished his duties, was whistling as he walked toward the Jive Sector.

“It’s over. I’ve won.”

By tomorrow, he would rule the world.

Of course, once the letters Fermi had sent reached the nations, things might change.

The documents he had scattered from the window were, for now, just rumors among the maids and janitors of the Holy War site. They had yet to reach national leaders—especially kings.

That might be the true distance between a king and his people.

“Hey, Fedra.”

Gis opened his arms and approached the Prime Minister of Arachne, whom he ran into at the end of the hallway.

“Thanks for your hard work. Money well spent on you, I’d say. So, how does it feel to be the world’s prime minister?”

“Well, it’s…”

Fedra, who had already come to a private agreement with Albino, couldn’t enjoy the moment like Gis did.

“What’s with the face? Be confident. It’s over. Even Jive’s intelligence agency is predicting a landslide victory.”

“Haha, right… So, where are you off to?”

“A date.”

Gis adjusted his collar and winked.

Seeing him so full of energy after what he’d been doing all afternoon, Fedra stuck out his tongue in disbelief.

“You’re something else. I don’t think I could even pee right now. So who’s the lucky girl?”

“You know—the secretary of Tormia’s Magic Association.”

“Ravid… Plu?”

“What’s there to drag out? Tomorrow, not just women—all of humanity will be bowing before me. Might as well get this over with.”

Tormia, huh…

Fedra, who was skilled in seduction and political charm, started to grow suspicious of the situation.

“Wait… what are they doing?”

Gis asked, “What? Is there a problem?”

Lost in thought, Fedra forced a smile and shook his head.

“No. She seemed rather uptight—I’m just curious how you managed it.”

“Haha! It’s all about raw animal magnetism, my friend. Women—they like it rough.”

“Impressive. What confidence.”

“Heh, I’ll introduce you later. Anyway, I’m off. See you at the finale.”

“Yes. Take care.”

As he bowed slightly to Gis’s retreating figure, Fedra’s face went blank.

“Yes… take care, Gis.” You’re not going far.

At the entrance of the Jive Sector, Plu, leaning against the wall, turned her head at the sound of footsteps.

Gis spoke.

“What, already here? Impatient, aren’t you? Or maybe you just couldn’t wait?”

“You’re the impatient one, Your Majesty. There are still people around. Isn’t this a bit early?”

“Is it my fault? The schedule moved up. That Havitz guy… well, never mind that. The finale’s in an hour. We don’t have much time.”

Plu sensed it.

“So you're going to discard me. Lowest of the low.” Not that it mattered—she’d only wanted a single opportunity.

“Fine. I have to attend the official event anyway.”

Without hesitation, Gis wrapped his arm around her shoulder and led her to the prepared bedchamber.

Inside, the Captain of the Royal Guard stood in full armor, waiting.

“Just like the rumors.”

Paranoid Gis always had a guard posted even during intimate moments.

As he undressed, he asked,

“You okay with this? Might seem weird at first, but you’ll get used to it. Just think of him as furniture.”

“Yes.”

Plu didn’t hesitate.

“Haha! I like that. A woman who can plan for the future—that’s my type. Then shall we?”

“I’ll wash up.”

Without waiting for permission, Plu went into the bathroom.

“Heh, so prickly.”

But knowing there was no man who would refuse her here, she washed herself slowly.

“The Captain of the Guard.”

There would be no way to escape against a top-tier swordsman of the 1st Legion.

“I’m going to die.”

As warm water trickled down her hair, she smiled like a girl.

“It’s okay.”

Wiping the steam from the mirror, she looked at herself—her final reflection—and composed her mind.

“I am Ravid Plu.”

Secretary of the Magic Association of Tormia. A loyal servant of the kingdom and its people.
