Chapter 901: Omega (2)

Zion.

The Antarctic continent, struck directly by Uriel's Ragnarok, continued to fracture and tear apart.

As the monastery crumbled, Arius hurriedly escaped to the outside.

"Keng, keng! Urr, urr!"

Though blind, the presence hanging in the air was all too familiar.

"It's the heavenly army!"

The monks of Zion, tasked with its defense, sprang into action, but there was little they could do.

"He is..."

A magnificent vibration spread around Uriel as he flew through the sky.

"An Archangel. Of all times..."

With many of their leaders, including Miro, away, there was no stopping an archangel.

"O humans."

As Uriel's massive body landed with a thud, the monks formed hand seals.

"Hmm."

Uriel looked around.

Steam billowed from all directions as the intense heat melted the ice.

"Where is Miro?"

Dharma from the Community of the Immortals, who had joined Zion, approached Uriel on behalf of everyone.

Behind him followed Hye, a disciple of Dharma, and Sky Hugo, once a candidate for the Ivory Tower.

"Dharma."

The gap in status between archangels and mortals was vast, but Uriel was familiar with Dharma and Hye from the Community of the Immortals.

"Are you betraying us?"

"A seeker proceeds towards righteousness. Those left behind may feel betrayed..."

A fighting spirit rose from Dharma.

"But we cannot cling to a rotten line."

Uriel, listening calmly, felt the laws of the universe tightening around him.

At the same time, Dharma threw a punch.

"Wind Diamond Fist!"

The air compressed, forming a wind that struck Uriel like a physical blow.

"Now!"

With a thud, Uriel was pushed back dozens of meters, and all the monks maximized their laws.

"Bind him!"

Angels born from specific concepts are vulnerable to opposing laws.

Ikael had ordered Zion to be struck first precisely for this reason.

"This is preposterous."

Even laws sealed with psychic powers were insufficient to block Uriel's essence.

"No, it has always been this way."

Adrias Miro.

"A vile race that has barely survived by wringing out one life."

While each angel might think differently, Uriel uniquely recognized Miro as a human.

"Annihilate."

Uriel's heavenly weapon, the Paragon Scepter, spun at a terrifying speed outside his reach.

Screeeeeech!

The sound of the air tearing was enough to pale the monks' faces.

"Heavenly Wheel."

A dark disc flew towards Hye, who extended one arm.

"Diamond Fortress!"

His body shone translucently, and the air around him turned into a 40-meter thick diamond.

"Crack!"

The moment the Heavenly Wheel hit, Hye's focus violently shook.

"I can't hold it."

As he tilted the barrier diagonally, the Heavenly Wheel scraped against the surface and began to climb.

"Wahhhhhh!"

Although he deflected the Heavenly Wheel, soon after, a loud crack sounded as the giant diamond began to fracture.

"This..."

Hye watched the approaching crack with resignation in his eyes.

"Hye, dodge!"

As Dharma shouted, the diamond-transformed body of Hye shattered into pieces.

"Hye!"

With bloodshot eyes, Dharma charged at Uriel, flailing wildly.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

As Uriel countered the Wind Diamond Fist with the Paragon Scepter, Sky Hugo attacked from the side.

"Dump!"

The avatar of the holy knight clad in white armor poured out both cause and effect, and blades flew from all directions.

"Tsk..."

Uriel's disgruntled voice.

A foreboding vibration saw in Hugo's eyes a vast wheel of judgment.

"Ragnarok."

As white electricity wrapped around Uriel's body, thunder cracked.

Boommmmm!

Even Dharma, who had become more determined after Hye's death, halted his steps at that moment.

"How can this be..."

Hugo had vanished without a trace, and the ice continent evaporated following Uriel's path.

"Is this the difference?" Despite knowing about archangels, the power of Uriel was on another dimension entirely.

"This is Uriel."

Rumors that he was as powerful as Archangel Ikael were not exaggerated.

Dharma asked.

"What do you desire?"

"Destruction."

Uriel pointed at the monks.

"Those who betray God shall face calamity."

"We do not follow your god."

"There is only one God."

No reasoning could persuade Uriel, armed with such simplistic logic.

"We will fight."

Behind Dharma, the monks readied themselves.

"Humans are truly strange."

It was a question that had arisen since Miro created a barrier to block the heavenly army.

"You fight a battle you cannot win?"

"You wouldn't understand..."

Dharma bent his legs.

"There is no such thing as a battle humans can surely win."

Watching all the monks rally, Uriel observed.

"Is that so?"

Miro's face, who had blocked the heavenly army, came to mind.

"So they fight."

Her face twisted in resentment, tears streaming.

"If that's the case."

The monks shouted.

"Wahhhhhh!" As the wheel of judgment Ragnarok spun, Uriel's body moved.

"Truly pitiful."

The white electricity that soared from the ground pierced through tens of kilometers of gray clouds.

"There!"

Miro, riding on Zulu's summoned beast Kaidra, pointed to the lightning rising from the center of the Antarctic continent.

"Incredible..."

Kangnan's mouth hung open in disbelief.

The reversing lightning was clear in their eyes, even before they had fully entered the continent.

"Given its size, it's already over."

If the heavenly army had taken Zion, charging in would be suicidal.

"Miro, we need to change the plan..."

"No!"

Miro cut off Kangnan.

"We will survive."

Even if there's only a slight chance that one survivor exists, they couldn't give up.

"I'll increase the speed."

Zulu's eyes sunk into the abyss as Kaidra shrieked.

Screeeeech!

They faced strong winds as they entered the Antarctic continent, but by then, the land was already torn apart.

"Even an archangel couldn't have this kind of power."

Miro sensed it.

"Uriel."

As they approached Zion, Zulu, soaked in sweat, looked upwards.

"Zulu!"

As Kangnan supported the unconscious Zulu, Kaidra vanished from the air.

"Crack!"

Driven by tremendous inertia, Kangnan fell to the ground, inflating her thigh muscles to brake.

She slid across the ice for over two kilometers before hastily twisting her body.

Click! As she stopped moving, she took in the sight of destruction at Zion.

Between the blood-soaked ice, the bodies of monks were tattered and hanging.

"He's here."

"Kack! Kack!"

Arius, the only survivor, writhed in agony under Uriel's foot.

"Uriel."

"The woman who came to heaven."

Given the past interest in the relationships between Gaold, Miro, and Kangnan, it was a remarkable sight.

"Have you reconciled?"

"No. It's a temporary alliance."

Miro walked past Kangnan.

"Humans don't change that easily."

"Grrr! Grrrrr!"

As the owner appeared, Arius showed his fierce teeth and writhed, and Uriel lifted his leg.

"Keng! Keng!"

Arius immediately turned around and rubbed his cheek against Miro's leg, and she crouched down.

"Didn't I tell you to keep watch? What is this?"

Arius spoke.

"Flee."

Despite hearing a human voice for the first time in ages, Miro just smiled.

"You're not a match we can win." As the only one who had explored Miro's inner depths, Arius knew this.

"My heart's fissure."

The feelings for Gaold had grown from a crack to a gaping rift that now dominated her.

"It's okay."

Gaold had said.

"It's just time to make a choice."

As she approached Uriel, just a dozen steps away, Miro prepared for the end of her life.

Most things were easy to let go, but a lingering question remained.

"Why did this fissure form?"

'I thought I detested such men.'

If there had been no fissure from the beginning, her heart wouldn't have grown so large.

"Miro, stop."

Kangnan spoke, and Miro halted as Uriel suddenly stood before her.

"I'm not ready yet, could you give me a moment?"

Kangnan's eyebrow raised.

"Preparation? In this situation..."

"It's unnecessary."

Uriel interrupted, raising the Paragon Scepter indifferently.

"It'll all be destroyed anyway."

Once broken, neither the longing for Miro nor her existence held any meaning.

"Ah, it's a pity."

Looking up at the deadly Paragon Scepter, Miro felt anxiety for the first time in her life.

'I just wanted to settle this.' Why did the fissure form?

"Hurry and dodge, you fool!"

As the Paragon Scepter spun, burning the air, Kangnan shouted fiercely, stamping on the ground.

Miro sighed.

"Ah."

Inevitably, she had to accept it.

"Yeah, you were right."

Even as death approached, Miro turned towards where Gaold was.

"You..."

Before Kangnan could finish.

"Gaold."

Miro said with a bright smile.

"I like you, Zasha."

And now.

"Goodbye."

As the Paragon Scepter fell over Miro's head, Kangnan felt as if time was slowing down.

'It closes.'

The heart shattered by Gaold was being cleanly regenerated.

"Ah..."

Arius could imagine it without seeing.

Such a miraculous birth.

'Erase it.'

The pain was equivalent to the entire world for Miro.

"Sorry, Gaold."

Such pain.

It was a feeling so terrible she couldn't remain sane for even a moment.

"Don't forgive me."

As she suddenly opened her eyes, blood tears streamed down Miro's cheeks.

As the Paragon Scepter passed by, burning the tips of Miro's hair and returning for another round.

'Arsenal.'

Thousands of arms sprouted from behind Miro, clutching the Paragon Scepter.

"Huh?"

Without any preparatory movement, the Paragon Scepter stopped, and the air exploded around it.

'Caught the Paragon Scepter?'

The fissure etched in Miro's heart was erased as if blown away by the wind.

Avalokiteshvara - Full Celestial Projection. From beside Miro, who had turned towards Uriel, tens of thousands of arms sprouted.

With a perfect field of vision, Miro's eyes sparkled, and the palms spread on either side struck Uriel repeatedly.

In a moment that slipped through, endless attacks vibrated Uriel and then struck him with one final blow.

"Ugh!"

Uriel, flung aside, adjusted his posture as he slid across the ground.

'My mind...'

The celestial body above his head struggled to absorb the shock and shook violently.

"Ugh!"

Uriel, on one knee, looked up as Miro approached, brushing off her hands.

"Phew. It's been a while."

The dried blood tears on Miro's cheeks formed a haughty smile.

"I can't quite get the alignment right."

Time would eventually take care of it.
