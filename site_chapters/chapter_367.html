Chapter 367: Special Training (2)

"Alright. I've decided on this one."

Scatter Movement had a much higher difficulty in execution than in theory.

However, for Shirone, who excelled in the Spirit Zone, this was actually fortunate.

He estimated that he could master it in about a month.

"Then I can learn one more. Let's try something more challenging this time."

Shirone headed to the section with new magic books and looked through the advanced difficulty shelf.

Books with titles like "Time Lock Using Quantum Transformation" and "Magic Circle Interference Based on Wave Principles" were lined up, just reading the titles made him feel overwhelmed.

Moreover, most of them were about information magic, which he didn't need immediately.

"Pass. Pass. Pass on that one too."

Shirone read each title carefully as he walked. There wasn't a single thin book in the advanced section.

"Hmm, what's this?"

Shirone stopped in front of a book on the last shelf.

It was similar in thickness to the other books and wasn't about information magic. But what caught his attention was the title.

Laser Guidance Technology

"Laser..."

It required a high level of energy output, but Shirone could easily achieve it using the God Particle. Converting mass into energy was an incredible advantage.

However, for most mages, it was a skill that required long-term training to master. Therefore, the mage who developed Laser Guidance Technology must have been quite skilled.

"Let's take a look."

The white cover indicated it was a photonic magic book, but it had a red band to signify the laser aspect.

Shirone checked the name of the developer under the title and was surprised.

Louis Jacquebain.

"Ah, Jacquebain..."

He was the head of the Particle Research Institute under the Tormia Dragon Tower, a mage anyone studying photonic magic should know.

When he opened the book, it was filled with complex formulas.

Shirone skipped the theory and went straight to the overview of the magic. As he read calmly, his face gradually lit up.

"This is amazing."

It was a fusion magic combining two principles of photonic magic.

Magic is activated when focused, and thus the thought slot is basically one. Therefore, advanced mages combine two or even three attributes to simultaneously utilize the advantages of various series.

In summary, Jacquebain's magic was a photon equipped with a laser guidance system.

To bend light, you usually need optical fibers or special media, but with laser guidance, you could change the trajectory as much as you wanted.

Moreover, it was possible to track moving targets within the range of inertia.

Jacquebain likely developed this magic for the convenience of information transmission, but Shirone was already envisioning the curved trajectory of a Photon Cannon.

"This is what Flu meant. Magic that complements weaknesses and creates synergy."

A Photon Cannon with mass could be given a spin to achieve some degree of curvature. However, as a predictive shot, its efficiency was too low, and the bending angle wasn't significant.

"But if I master Laser Guidance Technology..."

He could hit the target no matter where it moved. The precision would vary with skill level, but for Shirone, who could generate explosive firepower in a short time, it was the perfect magic to reduce mental strain.

"Great! This will be my first fusion magic!"

Shirone took the magic book and looked for Flu. She was reaching for a high shelf in the fire magic section when she turned around.

"Hmm? Did you choose?"

"Yes, here. Two books."

Flu alternated between the two books and nodded. There was an advanced course, but it wasn't a good idea to be intimidated from the start.

"Alright. They seem to complement each other well. Let's tell Ms. Isabel and borrow them."

When they brought the books to Isabel, she issued a loan certificate.

Then, looking at the Laser Guidance Technology book, she asked with a surprised expression,

"This one is quite difficult. Are you sure?"

"Yes. I already know how to use lasers. Is it that hard?"

"It's a specialized field not covered in school. Especially since it uses radio waves, it might be unfamiliar."

The laser Shirone used was different from other mages.

The God Particle allowed him to leap over high barriers all at once, but without a precise understanding of the theory, his range of application was limited.

"I'll give it a try. I want to master lasers this time."

If he was determined, Isabel had no reason to stop him.

"Well, using photons as attack magic is rare, so I understand your ambition. If you have any questions while studying, ask me. The Chairman instructed me to tutor you if necessary."

Gaold's consideration was both appreciated and daunting.

He truly believed Shirone could develop magic to destroy Heaven.

"Thank you. I'll probably be back soon."

"Haha! Sure. Come anytime."

To avoid feeling embarrassed later, Shirone gave a heads-up and got on the elevator.

The iron door of the library closed before the elevator did.

As always, it felt like returning from another dimension.

With Flu's help, Shirone got a personal training room. Strictly speaking, it was Gaold's order.

It wasn't as spacious as the underground bunker, but it was enough for him to unleash the Spirit Zone and train alone.

A sleep capsule was installed in the corner, and the floor material was the same as the 2000 training ground. The simulation device seemed capable of analyzing magic from various angles.

Shirone sat on the cold floor and opened the Scatter Movement magic book.

After quickly reading it from start to finish, he closed the book and organized his thoughts.

"Hmm, it seems both easy and tricky."

The key was the speed of linking passive and active magic. He had to shoot photon output in all directions immediately upon casting teleportation. The principle was simple, but the linking speed had to feel almost simultaneous for the technique to be valuable.

"Let's give it a try."

Entering the training ground, Shirone cast the photonic transformation magic. With a familiar sensation, the scenery ahead warped and rushed towards him.

Arriving 10 meters ahead, Shirone looked at the ceiling, lost in thought. He intended to cast Scatter Movement, but it didn't activate.

"Oh, I see."

When teleportation is activated, the slot is empty. But the movement speed was too fast to cast photon output.

"Does the slot need to switch much faster?"

In that case, Shirone had a specialty to boast about: parallel processing.

Focusing on that, Shirone attempted Scatter Movement based on parallel processing.

By sunset, he was getting the hang of it. After recovering his mental strength for an hour in the sleep capsule, he executed the magic with maximum concentration.

Finally, he shot light in eight directions simultaneously with teleportation.

Moving north, the basic route of Scatter Movement, Shirone looked back, unable to hide his excitement.

"I did it! I did it!"

"What did you do?"

Shirone turned towards the door.

Flu was leaning against the wall with her arms crossed, looking unimpressed.

"Oh? When did you get here, senpai?"

"Just now. What were you doing a moment ago?"

"What else? Scatter Movement, of course."

"Hmm, really?"

Flu walked into the training ground.

Stopping about 10 meters from Shirone, she looked around as if checking coordinates, then turned back to him.

"Do it again. I'll evaluate it."

Flu's firm tone made Shirone a bit nervous.

But no matter how he thought about it, his Scatter Movement was exactly as described in the magic book.

"Okay. I'll start with the basic route..."

"No need. Do the intermediate course. Go to any of the eight directions you want. From what I saw earlier, you should be able to do that."

Though it sounded like praise, it didn't feel like it.

Since he was just learning, Shirone wasn't too worried about failing.

"Let's go southeast."

While Shirone focused, Flu waited with her arms crossed.

'Scatter Movement!'

As Shirone's body glowed golden, light exploded in all directions.

Arriving exactly 10 meters southeast, he clenched his fist.

"Success, it's a success."

His joy was short-lived as he flinched in surprise.

Flu was already in front of him, holding his shoulders with both hands.

Her eyes were half-closed, calm like when she subdued a terrorist.

Usually a nagging sister, at times like this, she was unmistakably a certified eighth-class mage.

"Uh, um..."

"Do you know what your problem is now?"

In form, Scatter Movement was a success. But Flu had pinpointed him among the eight flashes.

Not only that, she moved faster than the caster to block his path.

"Is the linking too slow?"

Flu finally let go of his shoulders.

"Yes. It's not visible, but it can be sensed in the Spirit Zone. Especially for a manipulation-type mage like me, the delay is even more apparent."

"But I did my best."

"It's not about effort. Your linking was excellent. You probably switched at a speed close to parallel processing..."

After a moment of thought, Flu spoke with conviction.

"But that's the problem."

Shirone tilted his head.

Parallel processing is a thought method that rapidly switches two slots.

While thoughts can slow down, there's no faster switching.

"To explain your mechanism: you equip teleportation, cast it, unequip teleportation, then equip photon output and cast it."

Flu demonstrated by putting her fists in and out, representing the linking of the two magics.

Going through at least three steps created a delay perceptible in the Spirit Zone.

But that was the limit of Shirone's ability.

"If there's a delay even with parallel processing, what should I do?"

"Collide them."

"What?"

"Collide them. Don't unequip teleportation, hit it with photon output. And as you hit, equip photon output in the slot."

Flu extended both fists forward.

She struck her left hand with her right, causing the left hand to fly away and the right hand to take its place.

This illustrated her earlier explanation.

"That's how you finish unequipping and equipping simultaneously. It shortens the mechanism. You need to do this in the realm of thought. It's about striking an existing thought with a new one. Mages call this 'Force.'"

Flu demonstrated the process again, but this time, she brought her fists close together and quickly collided them, making a "bang" sound with her mouth.

"Do you understand? Skip the unequipping process and just hit it while equipping. If you ever work in the field, you need to know Force. Passive-active linking is used across all series."

Shirone listened in a daze. Replacing one thought with another?

Ordinary people don't think this way, but for mages, it was an extremely efficient method.

"Force... I didn't know such a thing existed."

"Of course not. It's a practical skill. They don't teach it in school."

"Is it dangerous?"

"No, but it's less stable. You have to precisely equip the slot while colliding thoughts. It's heavily reliant on intuition, so it's not suitable for regular education. Parallel processing is also a professional skill. It's impressive that you mastered it on your own. You'll probably learn Force quickly too."

Flu's words restored Shirone's confidence. Although his first attempt at Scatter Movement ended in failure, learning a new skill not taught in school was an achievement.

The key was mastering Force.
