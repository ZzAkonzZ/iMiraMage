Chapter 649:

"Ra Enemy is…"

Rian had begun to speak out of disbelief but swallowed his words.

Even if the intel seemed questionable, Shirone had paid for it—and the meaningful smile on his face played a role too.

‘But what even is this…?’

Of course, it was entirely possible that Ra had been reincarnated as a woman.

But that wasn’t a particularly important issue to Shirone. What bugged him was the word “presumed.” If Ra is male, then he is. Why the uncertainty?

‘No one’s going to spoon-feed you like at school.’

Shirone was just as disappointed, but now that he’d gotten a taste of how this world operated, the 3 gold didn’t feel wasted.

‘To someone, Ra’s gender might be important. So I can’t say this lowers the quality of the intel. But still…’

That word “presumed” kept bothering him.

“Please explain.”

The sharp glint in Shirone’s eyes caught Estera off guard.

‘He wasn’t like this a second ago…’

Most graduates who came to guilds were those who had given up hope on becoming licensed and were jumping into work for a living.

Especially fresh graduates—they were easy targets for managers to squeeze profits from. You make your money off rookies to make up for losses in veteran deals.

‘So what now? Do I just explain it?’

Usually, charging extra for an explanation would be the right move, but the look in Shirone’s eyes triggered something in her instincts—an intuition honed by dealing with countless mages.

‘Let’s just observe a little longer.’

Trusting her gut, Estera explained.

“In the intel business, information is divided into four levels: Confirmed, Presumed, Suspected, and Rumored. Even at the best of times, it’s hard to verify, but anything listed as ‘Confirmed’ requires proof or documentation.”

“Then what does ‘Presumed’ mean?”

“It’s essentially the same as ‘Confirmed,’ just without the requirement for attached proof. So unless you're a professional information broker, most people deal in the ‘Presumed’ category.”

Because mages traveled widely, they encountered much more than the average person.

When they returned from assignments, they could sell that information to guilds as a side income.

‘It becomes a gathering point for intel. More people use the guild, so the guild profits too.’

Rian asked, “But if there’s no proof, how can anyone trust it? Though in this case, it’s not really about belief, I guess.”

“That’s something the buyer has to accept. Information is only a supplementary tool for completing missions. That’s why even other mages rarely request intel above the ‘Presumed’ grade unless they absolutely need it. But what I can say is—any national-level guild won’t deal in sloppy information. You saw the name under the card, right?”

Shirone recalled the name engraved on the card: Marka Intera.

“Anyone can purchase, but only verified guild members are allowed to sell intel. If you're still unsure, you can track down the provider directly. Just remember, a guild isn’t a government office. Everything runs on trust—keep that in mind.”

“And if the provider lied?”

“They’d be stripped of their guild status. Every guild connected to the network would be notified.”

Knowing what Shirone was worried about, Estera added,

“Of course, betrayal can happen—but if you focus only on the worst-case scenario, you’ll never get anywhere. Most people work based on trust. That’s what keeps the guild system alive.”

If someone wasn’t planning to rob the system and vanish, destroying their livelihood wouldn't be worth it.

Shirone nodded and asked, “How much did the seller get for this card?”

“Fifty silver.”

The guild had earned a 1 gold 50 silver margin, but Intera had likely sold multiple pieces of intel, so it wasn’t unreasonable.

Honestly, if the guild system didn’t exist, who would pay fifty silver for something like this?

“So, there were no ‘Confirmed’ level entries with Ra Enemy's name.”

Estera gave a sheepish laugh.

“Right. None of the keywords brought up confirmed-level intel. Like I said—it’s the realm of professionals. It requires thorough verification and costs way more.”

Rian grumbled, “Shouldn’t you have told us that from the start?”

“Haha, but you learned a lot, didn’t you? Consider it your tuition.”

“So much for that whole speech about trust…”

Shirone cut him off.

“I’ll take them all.”

Estera blinked. “Huh? What?”

“I’m buying the rest of the cards. There are 26 left, so that’s 52 gold, right?”

As Shirone placed five mid-grade gold coins and two low-grade ones on the table, Estera finally realized what her instincts had been telling her.

‘This kid…’

He was fast.

Just from his behavior so far, it was clear this was his first time dealing with a guild.

Yet deciding to purchase every information card meant he had skipped over all the trial-and-error most beginners go through.

‘Yeah… a veteran would’ve made the same call.’

With all pretense gone, Estera handed over the cards.

“…Take them.”

Shirone slid all 26 cards into his cloak and turned to Estera once more.

“I’d like to join the guild now.”

Another solid decision.

‘He really switches gears fast for his age.’

Just ten minutes ago, he’d been jeered by the mages here. But when it came to missions, he could completely shut out emotion.

His skill was still unknown—but he clearly had potential as a working mage.

“Alright. To be honest, joining the guild really is worth it. You get way lower fees, and most importantly, only guild members can access B-rank and higher requests.”

That was exactly what Shirone had been thinking.

“Yes. I’d also like to take on some work.”

If you could buy information, you could sell it too.

And since Silver Ring allowed free entry and exit, Shirone figured he might as well earn back some of the cost on the road to the capital.

“Hm, is there anything specific you’re looking for? We have jobs in combat, negotiation, education, scouting, and delivery.”

“Delivery sounds good. I’m heading to the capital anyway.”

“Oh, perfect. There’s one job that fits you exactly. And the pay’s solid. First, can I see your mage license?”

Shirone pulled out his unofficial license.

“Here.”

Estera picked it up from the table and examined it.

“Wait… Alpheas Magic School? That’s one of the Five Great Academies.”

It was rare for a graduate of such a prestigious school to show up at a guild, which piqued her interest even more.

“What’s your name…”

When she saw it, Estera’s face froze.

At first, she thought she had misread it, but no matter how many times she checked—yes, it was the name she knew.

“Arian Shirone! You're Shirone?”

As her thoughts aligned with her memory, Estera’s eyes widened like they might pop out of her head.

People working in the field had little reason to remember some student’s name—unless that student was Arian Shirone.

The boy who set the greatest record in the kingdom’s history. A candidate for the Ivory Tower itself.

Rian grinned broadly.

“Ha, guess the secret’s out? That’s my lord, Arian Shirone. And I’m the—”

“What! Arian Shirone?!”

The mages in the hall jumped to their feet.

Even as a candidate, someone with the title of “Star” had abilities on par with a national archmage—they all knew that.

With that realization, the kid who had looked like a fledgling moments ago now felt towering, as if he could break through the ceiling.

Estera gulped and checked the license again.

‘It’s real. No forgery.’

If word got out that Arian Shirone had joined Silver Ring, they’d easily surpass the other top three national guilds.

‘What do I do? I have to keep him. Ugh, and of all times, the Guildmaster had to be out…’

Shirone, aware of what she was thinking, asked once more,

“Guild membership and withdrawal really are free, right?”

“Of course, absolutely.”

She smiled sweetly—while thinking something very different:

‘…But not for you.’

Of course, he wasn’t someone they could just hold onto because they wanted to.

And if Shirone was currently taking the Ivory Tower test, there would be no real reason for him to join a guild within the kingdom.

‘But then again, there’s no real reason to leave either, is there?’

Having organized her thoughts, Estera handed him a key.

“Well, since you’ve joined, we should offer you lodging. Take this and go to Room 3 on the 4th floor.”

“Okay. But shouldn’t I fill out some paperwork or something?”

“Hahaha! No need to wait around for that, is there? I’ll come by your room later and take care of it. Oh, right—your license.”

Though she had given him a hard time earlier, her service had now improved significantly. Not that Shirone held grudges anyway.

“Sure, I’ll leave it to you.”

As Shirone climbed the stairs, the moment he disappeared from sight, the mages in the hall surged toward the counter.

“Estera! Can I get a copy of his guild certificate? No, sell it to me! How much?!”

If they submitted proof along with it, just a single piece of confirmed intel could sell for over 100 gold.

Estera sighed.

“Wake up, people. From this moment on, this information is the exclusive property of the Silver Ring Guild, Kentra Branch. Go drink something.”

The mages clicked their tongues and turned away, but instead of heading back to the hall, they exited the building.

With an Ivory Tower candidate in their midst, even presumed-grade intel was bound to carry high value.

That was the power of guild credibility—and thanks to that trust, word of Shirone’s presence spread quickly throughout the entire kingdom.

Room 3 on the 4th floor, which Shirone entered, was massive—equipped with a full seven beds.

“This is… a bit much.”

Even a kid would know this was excessive, but as a display of the Silver Ring Guild’s intentions, it was effective.

“Well, whatever. It’s free,” Rian said as he flopped down. Meanwhile, Shirone sat on the bed opposite and began reading through the intel he’d purchased from the guild.

“Oh, right. The cards. So? Any useful information?”

After Shirone finished reading and set the cards down, Rian got up and started flipping through them one by one.

“Let’s see… Ra Enemy is presumed to be a mage… What kind of obvious nonsense is this?”

It might have been valuable to someone else, but to Rian, it felt like a waste of 2 gold.

Still, there were a few pieces that stood out.

“Presumed to have graduated from the Kazra Kingdom’s magic academy. Suspected height between 175 and 180 centimeters. Rumored to have met someone from the Ivory Tower. Huh?”

Rian frowned as he read the next card.

“Ra Enemy is rumored to be deaf?”

He tossed the card onto the blanket in frustration.

“They’re allowed to sell garbage like this?”

“But look at these.” Shirone showed him a few other cards.

“Rumored to be blind. Suspected to have a rare disease. Rumored to be mute. And each one comes from a different source than the one you read.”

“Huh. That’s definitely weird. But some of this has to be false. Even the mage part is just presumed, right?”

“I don’t believe it all either, but it’s not something you can outright ignore. If anyone lied, they’d be kicked from the guild, and in severe cases, they could even face retaliation.”

In that sense, selling bad intel was like risking your life.

“But then what if it’s like this? Maybe all the ‘rumor’-grade info is just trash from the start. No one would retaliate over that. I mean, rumors are just stuff people say without thinking, right?”

Rian had a point.

“True. ‘Confirmed’ grade requires proof, and ‘Presumed’ probably means someone saw evidence firsthand.”

“Then what about ‘Suspected’?”

“Probably based on secondhand comments from someone close to the subject. That would make ‘Rumor’ something like… hearing what someone else heard.”

“...So it’s completely worthless.”

“But if you put all this together, there’s one important thing we’ve learned.”

Shirone pointed at the cards containing Ra Enemy’s name.

“There’s just too much information. And more than that—Kazra’s borders are currently closed.”

That was due to the paternity scandal involving Shirone.

“Wait. Then how did information from Kazra make its way to Tormia?”

“That’s the key. It means that guild members from other countries are still able to gather presumed-grade intel. Which means... the Ivory Tower doesn’t not know where Ra is.”

Shirone’s thoughts were almost a certainty now.

“They know—but they can’t find him.”

A being hiding within that strange contradiction.

“They know,” Rian repeated, “but can’t find him?”

That was Ra Enemy.
