Chapter 436:

"Diamond Armor."

When Shirone muttered and held Armand vertically, the blade quickly covered his body, transforming into an organic robe.

The Nor soldiers cheered.

Currently, Shirone was touring the subordinate units of the Second Command, integrating the rebels.

Today was the last day of the schedule, and someone had expressed curiosity about Shirone's sword, leading to a demonstration.

In Heaven, swords were exclusive to the Kergoin, so it was natural for the Nor mages to be puzzled.

'Phew, this feels like a performance.'

The name "Light of Zone 73" was indeed impressive.

At least, the reactions from those on the field were much better than those from the command center.

Focus point shape change. Unified signal system detected.

'Huh?'

Shirone narrowed his eyes at the unfamiliar information.

Focusing on the voice, Armand conveyed new information.

Scanning user's brain. Synchronization. Suggesting integration into a single mental system.

'Ah, the Ultima System.'

When a special change occurred in Shirone's mind, Armand's functions also adapted accordingly.

Armand's computational ability relied on Shirone's brain, so it was natural.

Name: Ultima System. If integrated into a single mental system, replication, transfer, and contract termination are impossible.

Awaiting user's decision.

'Impossible to terminate? Is that even possible?'

It meant that the contract-based object would change to a bound object, making it a perfect personalized weapon.

In a way, it wasn't bad since it meant completely owning Armand, but it required careful consideration.

'Once it becomes mine, I can't sell it.'

Worth 4 billion gold.

Although Shirone wouldn't sell Armand for any price, removing the possibility of transfer was unappealing.

'What if I integrate it?'

Unpredictable.

Armand couldn't find an answer to that.

Armand couldn't analyze what Shirone didn't know.

'Well, I have no choice.'

Shirone decided to share the Ultima System with Armand.

If they exchanged information through a single signal, he could feel Armand almost like his own skin.

Future variables couldn't compare to the current urgency.

In Heaven, there was no tomorrow, and it was rational to cling desperately to any chance of enhancement.

Constructing integrated mental system.

"Ugh!"

Shirone threw his head back and opened his chest wide.

Armand's organic material tightened rapidly, pressing against his body.

Dozens of tendrils protruded like spikes, moving erratically before wrapping around him like a whirlwind, changing shape.

"Wow!"

The rebels watched Shirone with wide eyes.

The ragged robe had transformed into a simple and neat appearance.

Ultima Version (Function Installed: 0)

The ideal mage’s robe in Shirone's mind was realized, controlling the organic material down to the last detail.

'Incredible. This is just like my body.'

Previously, they shared information through the brain, but now signals were exchanged in the realm of instinct, bypassing reason.

It was true integration.

Shirone's complex mind was simplified into signals and embedded into the object.

Thus, Shirone and Armand achieved perfect compatibility, connecting down to the nerve endings.

'Two Ultima Systems…'

Another entity capable of unifying and controlling all signals, whether biological or non-biological, had been created.

Shirone didn't know how this would work in the future, but one thing was clear.

"Nephilim, lead us to victory!"

"Until the Light of Zone 73 becomes the Light of Heaven!"

It was a perfect propaganda performance.

Jinma Species Galtomic.

A powerful S-rank species in Purgatory Hunt, it was a gel-type collapse form with no clear shape but could mimic a powerful entity by absorbing the law of evil when attacked.

Currently, Galtomic, which was driving Armin's group into a corner, had a form mixed with a human upper body and a horse's lower body.

Every time it swung its fists, black flashes surged, and rocks the size of cliffs shattered.

An S-rank creature meant it was powerful enough to have no natural enemies within a 100-kilometer radius.

Galtomic's true value lay in its immense magical power, which absorbed the law and converted it into its strength.

"Krau! Krau!"

With a unique shout, Galtomic flew up and swung its fists like a storm, devastating the ground as if the distance had disappeared.

Armin's group, scattered in different directions, began their counterattack.

As a blizzard set in and the temperature dropped rapidly, Kuan and Etella surrounded Galtomic.

Their sword and fist movements blended rhythmically, and in the tempo of their seamless attacks, Galtomic was struck and fell to the ground.

When Armin cast a slow spell, Galtomic, trapped in slowed time, was hit by Shina's Glacier Bombing.

As the glacier, weighing nearly 60 tons, fell, the ground shook.

In the rising mist of cold air, Galtomic, using its last strength, charged at Shina like a centaur's spear.

At the same time, Kuan, flying in an S-shaped trajectory, appeared in front of Shina and spun.

As the air vibrations from Kuan's high-speed rotation at 10 revolutions per second pierced the eardrums, his sword split Galtomic in two.

Whooosh.

Kuan's rotational force, combined with Shina's ice magic, made her nose feel cold.

Shina had over ten defensive methods for surprise attacks learned through experience, study, and training, but in this case, she should naturally express her gratitude.

"Thank you. You saved me."

"I was just waiting for a chance to counter."

Shina watched Kuan's back as he walked away.

She had come to understand more about his cold personality since arriving in Heaven.

It was fundamentally different from her own coldness.

'I wish I could have a deep conversation with him at least once…'

Shina was gradually becoming interested in Kuan, but Kuan was sinking deeper into self-loathing.

'Damn it! I've completely lost it.'

There was no technical mistake in cutting Galtomic. The problem was his pounding heart.

The thought that flashed through his mind in that moment was that he wanted Shina to recognize his worth.

It was an unfamiliar feeling for Kuan.

He had never wanted to understand someone's thoughts before.

But now it was different.

He wanted to know how his actions affected her.

'Get a grip. This is a battlefield, and my grave.'

"Shina, are you okay?"

Shina, who had been watching Kuan, quickly changed her expression and turned coldly when Armin approached.

"I'm not a child. I don't need that kind of concern anymore."

Etella mentally organized the emotional arrows pointing in different directions among the three.

Their teamwork wasn't great, but perhaps this situation was creating a strange synergy.

'Anyway, Armin is amazing.'

Galtomic was a powerful creature that could rival any monster on the continent.

Of course, they hadn't faced weak enemies in their hunts so far.

Despite that, their success was largely due to Armin's contributions, in addition to the effective cooperation of the four.

Crowd control provided critical opportunities in battle. And Armin, currently in the party, was probably the world's most powerful crowd control mage.

'If we keep this up, we can start the factory soon.'

As Etella predicted, when they arrived at Yameng, the guards murmured.

Initially skeptical, they were now impressed by the daily capture of A-rank or higher creatures, making the hatred from the first day when Armin beheaded a comrade seem insignificant.

Frankwine, who confirmed Galtomic's corpse, smiled.

"Impressive. I didn't expect you to find a strategy so quickly."

They hadn't thought of a strategy. Armin's crowd control was just that effective.

"Just curious, what Elixirs did you get?"

Etella answered honestly.

"Twelve White Elixirs, thirty-two intermediate Green Elixirs, and fifteen advanced Red Elixirs."

"That's amazing. You've worked hard. How about taking a break? With that, you could live lavishly on the mainland."

Armin replied.

"We're not here for leisure. Once we capture the three monsters—Kuzan the Devourer, Olkir the Photosynthetic Beast, and Ringer the Armored Mineral Species—the contract will be fulfilled."

"Of course. After that, I'll give you an entire factory building. You can make whatever you want."

"We'll be going now."

As Armin's group left the room, Meatgun, who was standing next to Frankwine, spoke.

"Isn't this too fast? Even the top hunters on the mainland take days to capture Galtomic."

"It's probably because of that blind man. The others are good, but that blind man is different. In terms of hunting rank, he's at least Triple S."

Frankwine's instincts were animalistic in both business and hunting.

"That's why I'm concerned. They could become a hindrance to Yameng in the future."

"Don't worry. A merchant doesn't choose customers. Just send the corpse to Raycis. And don't forget our conditions."

Meatgun seemed to have more to say but eventually left the room.

Frankwine, now alone, grinned wickedly.

'Heh heh heh, it turns out to be a huge game. Right, Raycis?'

The Fuzix Machine flashed.

As the glass tube disassembled, Shirone, who had undergone biological fusion, emerged.

The Type A clone without the Ultima System was noticeably different from Type B.

While it could still accept certain traits without issue, there was a significant level of collapse, and the form was uncontrollable.

Shirone's body was covered in red scales, and smoke rose from one of his eyes.

It was the day of the comprehensive fusion of the corpses received from Yameng. It was also the first day the traits of the Jinma species Galtomic were added.

"Commander, look at these numbers! They're incredible!"

The readings on the self-developed magic power measurement device were rising exponentially.

The magic amplification of Galtomic was combining with Shirone's incarnation, emitting immense magical power.

As Shirone's magic exploded, Akamai trembled.

Even the enhanced Akamai, stronger than the prototype, was struggling to contain the power, with the Antithesis nearly breaking.

Boom!

Photon Cannons fired in all directions of the laboratory, and Raycis watched Shirone with awe.

"Ah, Shirone. You are so beautiful."

It was a demonic love.

A love with no consideration for the other, driven solely by possessiveness.

"Commander! We need to take action! At this rate, the laboratory will be destroyed…!"

Even as the researcher's cries were drowned out by the roar of the Photon Cannons, Raycis didn't move.

As the fusion progressed, Shirone was approaching the ultimate creature.

And all the information obtained here would be used to further enhance Akamai.

"Hehehe. Shall we start with the first kiss?"

Raycis's skin began to redden, her waist bending and stretching, with vertebrae protruding along her back.

Her thighs hardened like rocks, and her feet elongated into reptilian claws.

"Raaaargh!"

Smoke mixed with fire and lightning spewed from her mouth, engulfing Shirone.

"Ughhh!"

Shirone's body melted but regenerated instantly.

It was the trait of the infinite cell proliferator, Kenser.

"Kekekeke! Magnificent, Shirone! Don't you think so too?"

Shirone used the ability of the invisible ghost, Hororos, to disappear.

Without the Ultima System, the various monster instincts were acting directly within his mind.

"He's gone! Find him quickly!"

As the researcher reached the scanning device, a sudden burst of mucus melted him.

"Aaaargh!"

It was the ability of the acidic poison king, Muusa.

A direct hit could even dissolve ultra-strong metals formulated with Elixir.

As the mucus hit the floor, toxic gas rose.

The researchers clutched their throats in agony, and Raycis blocked the mucus with her broad hand.

Her hand melted away, revealing bone, which then crumbled like spent matchsticks.

"Ugh!"

Raycis had reached her limit.

If the fight continued, the laboratory would indeed be devastated, as the researcher had warned.

"Release Akamai!"

The researcher, on the verge of fainting, crawled to the scanning device and pressed the button.

As Shirone's coordinates were confirmed, three additional Akamai entered from the side walls, activating Antithesis.

The eyes of the Akamai, desperately holding Shirone, were bloodshot.

Yet Shirone continued to move slowly.

Raycis's monstrous face twisted in disbelief.

'Even four Akamai can barely hold him.'

It wasn't just because new traits had been added.

Shirone's potential was exploding through biological feedback.

Armin's group faced their first major challenge since receiving the list from Yameng.

The creature that troubled them was the double S-rank armored mineral species, Ringer.

Standing 2 meters tall and weighing 14 tons.

It had four legs but could roll into a ball and move quickly when needed.

Its speed made it difficult to hunt, but the most serious issue was its carapace covered in grayish-white metal.

Boom! Boom! Boom!

Wherever Ringer charged, a path was created, and everything, trees and rocks alike, was shattered.

Armin cast two types of slow spells on either side of Ringer. As the time zones overlapped, Ringer bounced at incredible speed.

Bang! Bang! Bang!

From the perspective of time relativity, even rebar should have been torn apart, but Ringer just rolled around like a ball hit by a wild spin.

"Phew, this is really…"

Armin had never encountered such a creature before.

If there were different types of strength, Ringer was undoubtedly the most durable creature.

Etella, seeing that Kuan's sword couldn't even scratch it, made a decision.

"At this rate, we won't catch it even if the sun sets. Let's cooperate."

Shina spoke up.

"Brother, slow Ringer down. I'll cast Absolute Zero."

Etella agreed with the plan.

"Then I'll finish it off."

After a brief discussion, they moved into action.

As Ringer slowed under the slow spell, Shina hit it with Absolute Zero, the strongest ice magic.

"Kiaaa!"

At the same time, Etella charged.

Thousand-Handed Avalokiteshvara Thunder Strike.

Etella's body created afterimages as she delivered rapid uppercuts.

As the final double palm strike landed, 68,000 interference waves were generated.

Boom!

Ringer's curled body shook heavily.

Five seconds later, a fine crack appeared on the carapace, and Ringer's body burst open.

"Phew."

Etella sat down, exhausted.

The most astonishing thing was that even the ultimate technique of Yin-Yang Wave Fist could only create a crack in its durability.

"That was insane. Definitely double S-rank."

Armin agreed.

"If it weren't for the wave-type attacks, it would have been much harder to deal with. I'm curious, though. Why is Yameng collecting the corpses of such creatures?"

Clap. Clap. Clap.

At that moment, applause came from the forest.

Feeling a sense of foreboding, Armin's group turned their heads, and silence followed.

"Fancy meeting you here."

"Indeed. We must be fated."

"Huh?"

"Sorry for not recognizing you earlier. Bishop Romi Etella of the Karsis Monastery."

As if on cue, a murderous aura emanated from everyone.
