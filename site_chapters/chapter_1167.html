Chapter 1167: Satan’s Proposal (1)

Air Press was far more powerful than the Air Gun Gaold had used before.

“Grrrhhhhh!”

A terrifying pain—one even Gaold himself had not expected—crashed down on him.

“Gaold! Get a hold of yourself!”

Seeing no response, Sein paled and looked up at the sky.

Ultima’s eye was there.

‘Damn it! Disappear already!’

As long as they defined Gaold inside this dream, his memories of reality would persist.

Kangnan muttered,

“It’s gotten bigger.”

No, to be precise, it had come deeper inside.

Sein spoke carefully.

“Gaold, stay calm. Take it in slowly. Return to the 1.5th layer.”

“Hrrrghhh…”

It was uncertain whether Sein’s words were helping, but Gaold’s expression gradually relaxed.

And along with it, Ultima’s eye retreated beyond the veil of the world.

Even after the pain had faded, Gaold remained motionless for a while, likely in deep shock.

‘He didn’t know.’

No—he had known in his mind, but one can only truly understand when pain actually strikes.

Just how powerless humans really are.

The leader of the Merchant Alliance surveyed the site of destruction, where even blood had evaporated, and asked,

“What happened? Was it… some kind of bomb?”

In a world without magic, their imagination had its limits.

“… Something like that,” Sein answered vaguely.

As he brushed the question aside, Gaold pressed his hands to the ground and slowly rose to his feet.

“Let’s go.”

Only an hour ago, his face had been fine—but now, he looked like he had aged years.

Kangnan said,

“Gaold, you can’t do this anymore.”

There was no reply.

“If you keep awakening, our world will collapse. No, before that—you will go insane.”

Even Gaold himself wasn’t sure if he could step into that pain again.

“Will he be able to forget this time?” Luber murmured.

“Humans repeat their mistakes, but some trauma is carved into the unconscious. No matter how strong one's will is, there are hesitations they can’t overcome.”

Monga spoke.

“They call that phobia.”

“What Gaold just did—the moment he broke his limits—Ultima’s eye had nearly invaded halfway. If it goes beyond that, the control will shift to Ymir.”

“… And if Gaold keeps getting stronger, so will Tamo. If the compromises keep breaking, eventually, the 1.5th layer will collapse.

And then—total annihilation.”

“There’s nothing we can do,” Luber sighed.

“If I break the rules first, Ymir’s unconscious will invade the 1.5th layer without restraint.”

“… Then we have to trust Shirone”

Luber didn’t answer.

When Zhuangzi’s Butterfly activated, Shirone had clearly received signals from the outside world.

‘The real Shirone must have suspected the outside world existed.

And also about the perspective of a god.’

But knowing something in theory and experiencing it firsthand were entirely different matters.

‘Even if he couldn’t analyze the signal, the shock of a closed world shattering must have left an impact.’

Perhaps, an overwhelming sense of emptiness.

‘That emptiness reflected into the 1.5th layer, making him lose his will. That’s the most reasonable explanation.

But deep inside—there must still be a flickering ember.’

Drifting in the boundless ocean of an open world, Shirone was waiting—desperately.

‘And if something triggers that ember, it will roar back to life.

The power to overcome that emptiness…

Might exist in Armand.’

Luber, as the administrator, couldn’t decide what outcome would be best.

Kangnan pointed toward the inner section of the Tamo Syndicate’s base and shouted,

“It’s over there!”

Arius was collapsed on the ground, bleeding. Shirone and Miro were desperately trying to escape.

“What… What the hell is that?”

The Merchant Alliance felt a cold shiver seeing Tamo’s abilities break their understanding of reality.

“Oi! Is this all you’ve got?”

Bullets twisted against all logic, curving mid-air as they flew.

Sein realized,

‘He awakened—just like Miro.

A normal attack won’t work against him.’

But at the same time, he didn’t want to force Gaold to awaken.

Instead, he rushed toward the iron door.

“Let’s go! If we fight together, we can win!”

Seeing someone boldly charge forward, the Merchant Alliance also stormed through the gates.

“That’s right! It’s just one guy!”

For three seconds, they actually believed they stood a chance.

“Khahahaha! What’s wrong? Is that all?”

A few bullets hit Tamo directly, but he didn’t even react.

Then, with a wild grin, he unleashed his machine gun on the alliance.

Four people dropped instantly.

“Aaahh! He’s a monster!”

The survivors fled, hiding behind the walls, too terrified to show themselves again.

Kangnan shouted,

“Fight back! We can hurt him!”

“No. Absolutely not.”

She turned her head—Tamo was standing there, lazily spinning his machine gun.

“You can have the best weapons in the world, but in the end—war is fought by people. If you let fear get to you—

You’re already dead.”

Kangnan clenched her lips.

Meanwhile, Miro and Shirone ran toward Sein.

“Dad!”

Sein hugged Miro, exhaling in relief before turning his gaze forward.

“Stay back. We’ll handle this.”

“No! That bastard is insanely strong! Shirone and I have to help!”

She was right.

But if they fought recklessly, the 1.5th layer would collapse.

“… Listen to me, Miro.”

Before Sein could explain, Tamo slammed his foot down, reloading his machine gun.

“Let’s wrap this up.”

Their group scattered, forming a defensive perimeter, but Tamo didn’t hesitate.

The battle continued.

A bullet pierced Sein’s knee.

Blood splattered from a wound on Kangnan’s right arm.

“Ghhk…!”

And thirty minutes later, none of them could even stand properly.

“Hehehe… Hihihihihi!”

Tamo tilted his head back, laughing, drinking in the pleasure of victory.

“What? Is this it? I’m starting to get pissed. I haven’t even had fun ye—huh?”

Above them, the moon split open, revealing something like a human eye.

“What the…?”

A massive pressure crushed his body.

“GRRRRAAAAAHHH!”

He felt no pain.

But the terror of his body breaking apart ravaged his mind.

For the first time, Tamo screamed.

Miro stared in shock.

“… Mister…?”

Meanwhile, Gaold—who had also been shot—staggered forward.

“… Finally, huh? What a pain in the ass.”

His expression remained blank, but the way his nerves twitched under his skin—

He looked like a demon.

“Dad… What’s happening to him?”

“Miro. Gaold is—”

Before Sein could explain, Gaold slammed his fist into Tamo’s face.

Tamo’s jaw snapped to the side, sending him rolling across the ground.

As he flipped back to his feet, his eyes were filled with fear.

‘What the hell is this?!’

The pressure from earlier was bad enough—

But even this one punch sent a shock through his brain.

“Pain.”

The proof of being alive.

“Khahahaha! This is thrilling! Hey! You—”

Before he could finish speaking, an enormous mass of compressed air slammed his body into the ground.

Gaold let out a guttural scream.

“Uaaaahhh!!”

As Air Press detonated in rapid succession, the ground rumbled violently.

Miro stood up.

“... Uncle is... in pain?”

Sein’s explanation had been brief, but the scene before her—Gaold transforming into a monster—was a story in itself.

"Graaaahh!!"

Shock-bleached hair, whites of his eyes rolling up, blood spraying from his torn throat.

Tears welled up in her eyes.

“Stop it.”

Even though she couldn’t remember anything, a thought naturally surfaced in her young mind.

Why is that man doing this?

“Just stop already. Even if you do this, I won’t change.

Honestly, I... don’t dislike you.”

She bit her lip.

“But every time you’re like this, you scare me.”

Gaold had poured his entire being into casting Air Press—now he was suffering a backlash of equal magnitude.

"Graaaahhh!!"

His limbs twisted involuntarily, and his mind became blank, overwhelmed by pain.

Meanwhile, Tamo shuddered in ecstasy.

“Kahahaha! More! Give me more!!”

Pushing through the crushing air pressure, his trembling body stood tall.

Sein looked up at the sky.

“No... This isn’t good.”

Ultima’s eyes had finally crossed the halfway threshold—revealing their full form.

"Khrrrrr!!"

As the compromise shattered, reality's memories rushed in for everyone.

‘If this continues, we’re all dead.’

Sein clenched his teeth and activated the Iron Wheel Eye, whirling it toward the frontlines.

With the power of Sun and Moon Light Wheel, Tamo’s movements slowed, and in that instant, Kangnan leaped forward like a beast.

"Yaaaaahhh!!"

Her knee smashed into Tamo’s ribs—but the enormous recoil sent her flying backward.

"Khk! He’s even stronger now!"

Heat distortions shimmered around Tamo’s body.

“Oh? So we’re doing this all the way, huh?”

The moment he kicked off the ground, his figure vanished.

Bullets rained from above, like a torrential storm.

Gaold, Sein, and Kangnan launched a counterattack, but Miro and Shirone still couldn’t move.

“What… is this?”

Reality’s memories had flooded in, merging with the dream memories in a 50-50 split.

It was disorienting.

Miro realized something.

‘So that’s how it is... Uncle, you really did...’

“Uaaaaahhh!!”

Gaold, barely conscious, unleashed a devastating Air Press at Tamo.

"Weak! Weak!!"

With the rules of the 1.5th Layer completely shattered, Tamo’s abilities now overwhelmed everyone.

“Ueeeek!!”

Gaold, unable to endure any longer, coughed up blood and collapsed.

Miro stepped forward, blocking Tamo’s path.

“Come at me!”

She still didn’t know what was real.

But within both conflicting realities, one truth remained unchanged—

She had to protect them.

“Gaold.”

At the sound of a voice fading from memory, Gaold’s head snapped up.

A barrage of bullets filled his vision.

"Khhh!!"

Meanwhile, Shirone was caught between the overlapping memories of the dream and reality, unable to regain clarity.

The ideals of protecting humanity clashed with the utter meaninglessness of it all.

‘I have to fight… to the very end.’

But even as he thought that, the emptiness pulled him into its abyss.

‘I… I can’t fight.’

His heart felt completely hollow.

—There was a man.

Then, suddenly—someone else’s voice resounded in his mind.

—He existed.

A fire ignited in his body.

His chest felt like it was drowning, swelling to the point of bursting.

—A story he never wanted anyone to know.

“Uaaaaaahhh!!”

Shirone activated his Mind’s Art, freezing the bullets in mid-air before they could reach Miro.

—But in the end… everyone would know.

A sorrowful, immense dream unfolded inside his mind.

"Agh…?"

Shirone’s eyes lost focus, and he collapsed forward.

“Miro!!”

At that moment, Gaold, his face twisted into a demon’s snarl, hurled his strongest Air Press at Tamo.

“Khahahaha!!”

Tamo’s body erupted into flames.

‘Pain…’

He could feel the process of his body being destroyed.

‘I’m alive.’

Bones shattered, his body compressed to half its size, yet he stared straight at Gaold.

“I… am…”

Then—his body was compressed again.

Until nothing remained.

Not even blood.

—KUUUUUUNG!!

The earth caved in, and an enormous pillar of fire shot into the sky.

“Get back!!”

Sein’s Sun and Moon Light Wheel blocked the scorching heat and shockwave, while Gaold grabbed Miro and rolled to safety.

Kangnan cheered.

“We did it! We actually won!!”

“No… We’re already too late.”

Sein’s eyes widened in despair as he looked up.

Countless eyes filled the sky.

“The world… is collapsing.”

If the 1.5th Layer disappeared, everyone here would be devoured by Ymir’s subconscious.

‘It’s over.’

Just as Sein shut his eyes in resignation,

Miro looked down at Gaold’s face and whispered.

“… Uncle.”

Her lips brushed against his forehead.

“… You can stop fighting now. I understand everything you’ve been trying to say.”

Gaold’s expression froze in shock.

And then—

Ultima’s eyes… withdrew.

"Come out of here… and live with me."

"When I grow up… I’ll become your bride."

A perfect compromise.
