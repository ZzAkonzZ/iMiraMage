Chapter 332: Midwinter Night (3)

Lyria answered without hesitation.

"Think of it simply. Evil is anything that rejects good."

"Really? Then what is good?"

"Anything that rejects evil."

"..."

Understanding Dante's silence, Lyria explained further.

"I get that question a lot. But it's because people try to analyze it in only one way. Logic is just one way humans understand the world. If you look more broadly, you'll accept things beyond logic."

Lyria crossed her index fingers in an X shape.

"Good and evil define each other and prove their existence. Without good, how would you know what evil is? Conversely, the concept of good becomes clear because evil exists."

"Hmm, so the two opposing concepts support each other's existence."

"Exactly. Mages call this the 'Law of Numbers.'"

"The Law of Numbers?"

"Every number has meaning. At first, there was nothing, or maybe infinity. The number that represents this is 0. Then, as unique names like good, evil, Dante, and Lyria were given, things began to exist. That's 1. That's why a sealing formation must include the caster's name."

"Hmm, a unique name. Then what about 2?"

"The reason you couldn't define good and evil is that you saw them as separate 1s. But if you combine them into the concept of 2, that becomes the law."

Lyria held up three fingers and continued.

"Lyria is 1, and the law is 2. Therefore, 3 is the number that finds a perfect self within the law. In the shrine, we call this the Trinity. Of course, reaching a perfect Trinity is difficult."

Lyria interlocked her fingers to form a triangle.

"Everything in this world starts from the number 3. This is the Law of Numbers. It's the essence of the Akeanis Shrine and the first lesson given to exorcists."

Using the Law of Numbers, one can represent all the principles of the universe.

It's similar to how Dante structures the world with 0s and 1s.

And this is the insight of the mages.

Lyria continued.

"A sealing formation is different from a magic circle. Only those who have refined their minds through endless training can trap evil. That's why it's a spiritual power. Of course, this is also why it's not recognized academically."

Dante, having organized his thoughts, nodded.

"I understand. So what do I need to do?"

"I might need your help if the disaster materializes through necromantic summoning. Once it materializes, it will affect reality. Set up a magic circle and protect me if I get in danger. You don't have to save me, but if the spell is interrupted before the sealing, it will be fatal. How long do you need to prepare?"

"Hmm, not long. I can start now."

Seeing Lyria's puzzled look, Dante chuckled.

"I don't like large magic circles. I usually use instant magic circles."

Lyria nodded in understanding.

Instant magic circles might be less powerful, but they offer flexibility in unexpected situations.

In fact, exorcists also use makeshift sealing formations when necessary.

"Alright. Let's begin."

As Lyria knelt and prepared to pray, Dante stepped back to brace for what was to come.

"O spirit lingering in the air, I bind your hands with the chains of holy power. Stay in place. Turn around. As the substance dedicates itself, a thousand masks will be removed."

Lyria's prayer continued endlessly.

She strengthened the will of the substance to pressure the non-substance. Speaking the will to increase its intensity was similar to word magic.

With the mage's signature, the necromantic summoning began.

"In the name of Lyria, I command you to reveal yourself."

Dante expanded his Spirit Zone and prepared for battle.

For a minute, nothing happened.

But since Lyria remained in her praying position, he didn't relax.

Soon, he felt the ground tremble.

The vibrations weren't strong, but they covered a vast area. Snow on the Ice Queen's castle in the distance fell like dust.

'Damn! Come out if you're coming out.'

Dante surrounded the area with a defensive magic circle.

As time passed, the vibrations grew stronger. But still, there was no sign of the substance.

"Ugh..."

A groan escaped Lyria's lips.

She was exerting all her strength, squeezing out every drop of her spirit to push something into reality.

Suddenly, the scenery flashed blue. Finally, a massive electric grid spread across the night sky.

Dante looked up, stunned.

It was clearly not a human-made network but a form created by the random connections between charges.

"Gasp!"

Lyria's upper body jerked as she gasped for breath.

Dante was at a loss. Was that the substance of the disaster? If so, should he fight it?

But how could he fight something like that?

"What is it? You need to tell me so I can act."

Lyria shook her head with a troubled expression.

"I was mistaken. That's not an evil spirit. It's a pure mental entity."

"A mental entity? Explain it so I can understand."

"It has a neutral will, not biased towards either side. I can't seal it in this state. But it seems to be quickly absorbing the law of this place."

Dante observed the signals exchanging through the electromagnetic network.

The patterns were increasing exponentially, causing the sky to flash with blue light.

'Damn, what is this?'

There wasn't a single repeated pattern.

It was evidence that the entropy of the information the law was learning was extremely low.

How could that be?

Even a newborn's brain isn't this active. Genetic learning about nature is already embedded at that stage.

"...It's not of this world. It came from somewhere else."

There was no other explanation.

To the mental entity, the information it was gathering here was as valuable as state secrets to humans.

Lyria didn't know about entropy. But she had a similar thought to Dante.

If the mental entity were of this world, it wouldn't have been born in such a pure, neutral state.

"Then we must seal it now! If we leave it, it will quickly absorb the world's evil! I'll attempt the sealing formation!"

As Lyria knelt again, a sound like nails on a chalkboard came from the sky.

The sharp noise found its frequency, creating a bizarre sound wave that no human could produce.

"Who... am I?"

Dante's face grew serious.

The question "Who am I?" meant it had developed self-awareness.

So what would happen next?

Before he could organize his thoughts, a simple yet blind shockwave, devoid of any individuality, struck the ground.

"Damn it!"

Dante set up a defensive magic circle around Lyria.

The mental entity's shockwave hit the ground with immense density, spreading out in a ring and generating a strong wind.

Swept away by the powerful wind, Dante was thrown back.

"Dante!"

A panicked Lyria ran to him.

The mental entity disappeared into the night sky, but she couldn't even think of chasing it.

"Dante! Dante! Wake up!"

Tears welled up in Lyria's eyes.

She had brought Dante into this. He had also kept his promise to protect her until the sealing was complete.

It was her mistake in misidentifying the enemy's substance that put Dante in danger.

If anything happened to him, it would all be her fault.

"Ouch, my head. That scared me."

At that moment, Dante sat up, rubbing his head.

Lyria stared at him in disbelief.

"Dante, are you okay?"

"Whew, that's why instant magic circles are great."

Dante showed his palm. A coin-sized defensive magic circle glowed blue.

In the moment of crisis, he had structured a defensive magic circle in the shortest time, splitting the shockwave.

Lyria finally understood the situation.

But she wondered if it was possible to do that in a split second without any margin for error.

Exhausted, she collapsed into a sitting position.

"Thank goodness. I'm sorry. Do you know how scared I was, thinking you were dead?"

"If I could die from this, I'd have died a hundred times already. But what was that? How can a mental entity produce a shockwave?"

Lyria regained her composure and stood up.

"It was probably the law. It seemed like a defensive instinct that emerged with its self-awareness."

"The law? How can the law produce such monstrous destructive power? It was almost at the level of magic."

Mages who use the law strengthen the opposing concept to pressure the concept they want to attack.

So if magic artificially twists phenomena, the law twists phenomena according to the natural order.

That's why it isn't bound by equivalent exchange, but its physical power is generally weaker than magic.

"It was probably a law that transcends human limits. If humans accept the law as 2, it accepts it as 1. It has always been the law. It must have existed as a mental entity for eons. Now that it has materialized, the strength of the law is different."

"So that's why the information entropy was so low. If it were of this world, it would have appeared by now. Anyway, the lower the entropy, the faster the changes will occur."

Even without understanding information entropy, Lyria agreed that time was of the essence.

"That's the problem. We need to capture it as soon as possible. Now that it has materialized, it will surely transform into something that can destroy the world."

"But... it's still in the early stages, right? There's no guarantee it will turn into evil."

Lyria shook her head firmly.

"High Priestess Poriter can't see all the world's disasters. But once she sees the future, it's never wrong. She said it would bring about the end. I'm certain that mental entity will become evil."

"Hmm, I see. Well... we wouldn't have known something was here if it weren't for Poriter. So, what do we do now?"

Lyria hesitated for a moment, then looked at Dante.

There was something she needed to address before they proceeded with the exorcism.

"You're really strong. Honestly, I didn't expect much help from a student."

"What? Suddenly? You don't need to say that; just tell me the plan."

"No, I'm serious. I've been involved in many exorcisms with the Order. I know how powerful that shockwave was. Without you, we wouldn't have made it this far. On behalf of the Akeanis Shrine, which follows the path of good, I thank you."

Lyria bowed respectfully with her hands clasped.

But Dante didn't care.

He had heard words like "amazing" and "thank you" so often since childhood that they were almost boring.

"Alright, enough of that. What do we do now? It's already materialized, so necromantic summoning won't work, right?"

"To the newly born mental entity, this place is like a cradle. It won't leave easily. We don't have much time, though."

"Hmm, what about going out and getting help? No one believed you earlier, but now they can see the disaster for themselves."

Lyria shook her head with a regretful expression.

"It's too late. By the time we get back from the city, everything will be over. If the mental entity reaches the stage of intelligent reasoning, even I won't be able to hold it."
