Chapter 984: The Unchoosables (2)

It might have been hasty to identify someone just by their missing arm, but Siena knew in her heart.

"Kuan?"

The man, who had sheathed his sword, slowly turned around.

Except for his closed right eye, he looked exactly like the Kuan Siena remembered.

"Are you alright?"

Siena ran to Kuan.

She couldn't decide whether she wanted to hug him or slap him hard.

'You bad man.'

A slap would do.

'I won't just stand by.'

As she accelerated her steps, she saw Kuan's face.

A scar ran diagonally across his right eye.

The short, slanted scar hadn't just cut the skin; it had pierced the eye.

'You really are a bad man.'

With all her strength, she swung her arm, but her face buried itself in Kuan's chest first.

"Why..."

Hot tears soaked Kuan's chest.

"Why are you so foolish..."

The power of the demons she had faced was on a different level from any enemies she had fought before.

But Kuan's swordsmanship had reached a level far beyond theirs.

It was that kind of wound.

Kuan wrapped his one arm around her back and spoke, trying to keep his emotions in check.

"It's alright."

Hadn't he protected her?

If losing one eye meant he could protect her, it was a small price to pay...

'This isn't enough.'

Knowing the strength of the enemies, Kuan was prepared to pay even more.

"Let's go. Where are the others?"

Siena's eyes flashed angrily as she pushed against Kuan's chest.

"Is that all you have to say? You left without a word and now you come back and say this?"

"Yes."

His calmness was infuriating.

"Then why did you come? Why did you come all the way to this remote place? There must be plenty of places for you to fight."

He didn't know.

When Shirone told him to follow his heart, the first person he thought of was Siena.

'What was I expecting?'

He still had the form of a human, but how long could he maintain it?

In Kuan's mind, he saw a future where even his limbs were severed, leaving him a mere chunk of meat.

"It's part of the strategy. I have nothing left. You should give up too, Siena."

"Uh..."

She opened her mouth to speak but then closed it, her expression turning cold.

At that moment, the demons who had heard the explosion filled the front.

"Keke. Who are you?"

They were about three meters tall, with muscular bodies covered in mouths, despite having no skin.

Even with a talisman covering their faces vertically, the feeling of disgust couldn't be suppressed.

Brigade Commander Kererer.

One way to classify demons was by the number of mouths, and Kererer had a staggering 237.

"Step back."

Kuan blocked the way with a serious expression.

'A glutton.'

Ordinary gluttons had the energy efficiency to digest a loaf of bread in two seconds.

'That's why they're always hungry...'

But when a glutton was full, they were one of the most troublesome demons to deal with.

Kuan drew his sword.

"I'll lure them away as far as possible. You should escape."

"No. I'll fight too."

"These are stronger than the Turtle Demons. You can't handle them with your current skills."

"It doesn't matter to you, does it?"

Her cold tone, filled with lingering resentment, made Kuan frown as he looked at her.

"I only have one arm."

The sorrow in his voice was palpable.

"I can't fight while holding you."

Siena's eyebrows drew a sad arc as Kererer burst into laughter.

"Puhehehe!"

The sight of tongues shooting out of all his mouths like rainbows made her feel nauseous.

"So that's it. Even if you die, you want to protect that woman... But what should I do? I'll eat her first. Puhehehe!"

One of his subordinates frowned.

"Stop laughing. You're making us hungry."

Kererer turned serious.

"Alright, let's do this quickly."

As the aura of the carnivorous demons rose, Siena shuddered.

The primal demon.

The desire for consumption that had existed since the birth of life made her mind hazy.

'I have to run.'

Just before she could combine her teleportation spell, Kererer was already in front of her.

Her retina captured the demon's face, but the signal hadn't yet reached her brain.

'Huh?'

When she came to her senses, she was no longer there, and all the events that had happened in an instant flooded in.

'Kuan.'

She felt his one arm wrapped around her waist.

Biting her lip, Siena spoke.

"Because of me..."

"Stay focused. This is just the beginning."

At his words, Siena looked up and saw the demons moving at incredible speed.

'They're following us?'

Even an ordinary glutton's energy metabolism was 30 times that of a human, and the carnivorous demons were 240 times.

"There! Over there!"

Though the voices sounded distant, they were already close by, creating a strange feeling. At that moment, Kuan spoke.

"You might lose consciousness."

"What..."

As Siena opened her mouth,

The Clown Pierrot - Super Tilt.

The landscape tilted more than 70 degrees, turning the flat ground into a massive cliff.

"Huh!"

Gravity churned her stomach, and the acceleration made her blood rush backward.

"Ugh!"

The world spun.

In that spinning landscape, the afterimages of the demons flashed across her retina at incredible speed.

'Where am I?'

Having lost her sense of direction, Siena was forced to accept the scenery.

The sky and ground aligned vertically and then began to tilt sharply to one side.

'I'm falling.'

The gravity made her think so.

"Ah..."

But the scenery beneath her feet was a sky covered in white clouds.

She was falling into the sky.

'And this is...'

The world Kuan lived in.

'So twisted.'

A world where confessing love or holding hands for a peaceful walk was a distant dream.

Siena's eyes filled with tears.

'But still...'

The sea of the sky they looked at together was so beautiful that she couldn't give up.

"Kuan, I..."

Just as she was about to voice her resolve, her stomach convulsed.

"Ugh!"

Her cheeks puffed up with the urge to vomit, but she couldn't bring herself to do it in front of Kuan.

"Siena."

The scenery spun half a turn, and the weight of standing on the ground spread through her body.

They hadn't gone very high, but enduring it was important.

"Ugh! Ugh!"

Kuan patted her back.

"If you need to vomit, do it. The constant change in gravitational acceleration makes it natural."

She held it in and whispered fiercely.

"Is that the problem right now? The demons..."

As she stopped speaking, the shouts of the demons searching for Kuan and Siena echoed from all directions.

"Come out! Where are you?"

"Coward! Come out quickly! Do you think we're that hungry?"

They were in the middle of the scattered demons, and none of the enemies could find Kuan's location.

'This is definitely a circus.'

Kuan spoke.

"They've expended a lot of energy, so they won't fight as they did before. Now we just need to finish them off."

Though they were shouting loudly, their shoulders were slumped, and their voices were hoarse.

"I'll do it."

Not one to steal the spotlight, Kuan frowned even more.

"You need to get used to this."

Smiling, she focused her eyes and unleashed ice magic in all directions.

Thick icicles pierced the open mouths of the carnivorous demons, eliciting screams.

As her vision cleared, Kererer, who had been looking around, finally spotted Kuan.

"How dare you mock me!"

Though his stance was good, he was much slower than before.

"Ugh!"

The thunderous rumble of hunger echoed from Kererer's stomach.

"Wait! I'll chew you up!"

Grabbing a surviving subordinate, he hugged him with both arms, and all his mouths moved.

"Argh! Argh!"

"Hold on. I'll avenge you."

As hundreds of mouths sucked the flesh, Siena felt nauseous again.

"Now I feel better."

Throwing the dried-up subordinate to the ground, Kererer glared at Kuan.

"I won't just kill you..."

Kuan disappeared.

Simultaneously, the ground in Kererer's vision tilted and then completely flipped 180 degrees.

"What...?!"

As he fell into the sky, waving his arms, he didn't realize.

His severed head was rising into the sky, spinning and making him dizzy.

"Argh!"

With a thud, his head hit the ground and rolled.

Siena stared blankly at Kererer's body, still flailing its arms.

"Let's go."

As Kuan, having sheathed his sword, returned, she snapped out of it and quickly turned her head.

"Right!"

Though Saad seemed to have escaped safely, demons still gathered at the central building.

"The principal is still there. He's surrounded by demons..."

At that moment, an explosion erupted from the central building.

"Oh my..."

Siena turned to see a massive mushroom cloud rising into the sky.

'Keke. Keke.'

Buried under the rubble, Alpheas laughed.

"Alpheas! Are you alright? Hey!"

Forgetting the blood flowing from her head, Olivia crawled over and shook him.

"Don't worry. This won't kill me."

Using a light spell, she saw a massive pillar crushing his knees.

"...Why did you do it?"

When the demons entered the building, Olivia tried to cast a self-destruct spell.

But Alpheas was faster.

"A bomb?" He pressed the detonator switch for the bombs stored in the basement, protecting Olivia with magic.

"Why? Are you going to sue me?"

Storing explosive materials in a school is clearly illegal, but Olivia chuckled.

"Is this what you were confident about? Even as a wizard."

"It doesn't matter, whether it's magic or bombs."

"...That's so like you."

Just like the young Alpheas who thought it didn't matter if it was particles of light or waves, as long as it was beautiful.

"Wait. I'll get your leg out first."

When Olivia tried to lift the pillar with magic, Alpheas grimaced.

"Arthur. It's probably buried 10 meters underground. If you touch it wrong here, you'll be crushed to death."

"It's the same. A second collapse will happen soon. We have no choice but to try, whether it works or not."

"Don't do it."

At Alpheas' voice, which was different from usual, Olivia gently turned to look at him.

"Can you stay with me a little longer?"

They already knew.

There was no way for the two of them, exhausted, to escape from the underground.

"Okay."

Olivia squeezed into the narrow space and lay down next to Alpheas.

It was quiet.

"What are you thinking about? Your wife?"

"I'm not that shameless. I already said goodbye before the demons came."

"Then?"

"I was wondering if this is also foolishness..."

Alpheas turned his head.

"It wasn't that I didn't have feelings. When you confessed, I thought I could live a new life. I'm sorry. I didn't have the courage."

Olivia's eyes filled with tears.

"You're saying this now? Idiot."

"Hehe, but you heard it while alive, right?" She nodded with a smile and gently stroked Alpheas' cheek.

As she turned off the light magic and slowly brought her lips closer, a stream of light flowed in from outside.

"Huh?"

The next moment, the entire building debris began to slowly rise, drawn by some force.

"Principal! Are you okay?"

Above the pit, Shirone's face appeared, and above that, a giant hand of light was grasping the debris.
