Chapter 1097: The Final Holy War (3)

‘Come to think of it…’

Shirone recalled that Ares's profession was an explorer and soon understood.

‘He said he was making maps.’

Ares spoke.

"Wherever I go, I hear about you. How's Amy? Is she doing well?"

"Don't tell me you haven't been home?"

"Haha! Life turned out that way. I send letters occasionally, but I can't receive replies."

Ares seemed happy, which was typical of the free-spirited Carmis family.

"Anyway…"

After their pleasant reunion, he became aware of the surrounding gazes and whispered.

"Let's go somewhere quiet first. You're trying to enter the Pyramid of Truth, right? Let's talk."

Although the mission was still dangerous, it was good to hear about Ares's recent activities.

"Shall we have a meal then?"

"Sure. My team is waiting. I'll introduce you to them, so take a good look. They're all capable people."

Despite his subtle attempt to turn it into an interview, Shirone remained unfazed.

As they headed to where the team was, Ares asked.

"How have you been?"

There was no need to ask who he was referring to.

Stang National Magic School.

The last training session ended at sunset.

"Huff. Huff."

Wizard was breathing heavily, but her expression was full of accomplishment.

‘I did it.’

She had learned everything Shirone could teach, the essence of Shirone himself.

"Congratulations, Wizard."

The teachers clapped, and Shirone and Amy, who had been watching the training, walked over.

The reason they couldn't just smile was that her first real battle would be too harsh.

‘Can she do it?’

Kill.

‘Even if it's the worst human in the universe, killing someone is not easy. It's something separate from skill. If Wizard hesitates even for a moment…’

Havitz would not miss that gap.

"Teacher."

Wizard looked up at Shirone with clear eyes.

‘Good eyes.’

Clear and bright, as if she couldn't even kill a bug.

‘But that's why it's possible.’

Although he didn't intend to glorify it, Wizard's thinking transcended the realm of killing.

‘She has no resentment towards Havitz, no special purpose or will. Pure beyond humanity.’

That's why she could see through Vanishing.

‘It's like a natural phenomenon. No, her mind is already nature. To describe Wizard…’

A benevolent Havitz, perhaps?

The deep meaning in those words weighed heavily on his heart.

‘The only natural enemy. This is the only way.’

Now, she could understand his feelings just by looking at his eyes, and she smiled as she spoke.

"Everything is ready."

"Alright. Let's go."

Zybro, where the Holy War Assembly is held.

"Rest well today. We'll leave the Kingdom of Stang tomorrow. I'll go with you."

"Yes. Thank you for everything."

As Wizard walked away, Shirone sighed, and Amy patted his shoulder.

"You did your best. Trust her."

"I should."

Shirone's eyes softened for a moment, and he suddenly turned his head as if he remembered something.

"Oh, by the way, have you heard from Ares?"

"Huh? My brother? No, I haven't. Why?"

"Well… he's actually with me now. In the Kingdom of Paras. It seems he's investigating the pyramid."

Amy, who had been blinking in a daze, suddenly frowned.

"That idiot…"

Then she grabbed Shirone's ear and shouted.

"Hey! If you're alive and well, you should come home at least once! Do you know how worried Mom and Dad are?"

"Ouch!"

Shirone winced, but Amy was half out of her mind.

"No news if you're dead or alive! The only letters we get are bloody drawings. I really…"

"Stop, stop!"

Shirone grumbled, rubbing his ear.

"That's not how you do it. This is…"

But when he saw the tears in Amy's eyes, he couldn't say anything.

"I really thought you were dead…"

Shirone hugged Amy and patted her back.

"It's okay. He's doing well."

Wizard, who had turned around at the entrance of the training ground, had been watching them for a long time.

Nagnak Oasis.

"When this is over, you should contact them. They're very worried."

Ares smiled sadly.

"…Really?"

When you love your work too much, you can end up hurting the people you love.

"Ares!"

At the rough voice, Shirone looked ahead.

Three people who appeared to be Ares's team members were looking puzzled.

Shirone recognized one of them.

'Great Swordmaster Euphrates.'

Except for the gray beard, he looked almost identical to his younger self recorded in Omega.

'A master of dual swords. Belligerent personality.'

He must have been well over sixty, but he still seemed to be active.

The other two looked like a couple, in their late twenties, wearing priestly robes.

The woman asked, "Ares, who are these people?"

Ares laughed heartily as if nothing had happened and introduced Shirone.

"Arian Shirone. Amy's boyfriend."

That was all the introduction needed since there were few in the world who didn't know Shirone.

"Yahweh?"

"Nice to meet you. I am…"

As Shirone extended his hand for a handshake, he noticed the priests frowning slightly.

'They're from the Lami Diocese.'

If they were wary upon hearing the name Yahweh, they could only be followers of the monotheistic Lami faith.

The man looked at Ares.

"What's going on?" He was asking why Ares had brought them here despite knowing the relationship between Lami and Yahweh.

Ares scratched his head.

"Well, it's just that…"

In other situations, there would be no need for introductions, but this time was different.

"We were rejected at the government office. Even after showing the minister's certification, it was no use."

The woman raised an eyebrow.

"What do you mean? The Vatican lobbied… I mean, persuaded them so much to get that."

She continued, glancing at Shirone.

"Give it to me. I'll go."

"No need. They won't listen no matter what we say. Besides, there's a rumor that the current king isn't normal."

The man asked, "So what does that have to do with bringing Yahweh? Can he get us in?"

"Exactly."

"Huh?"

"He has a pass directly approved by the king. With this, we can get a free pass too."

Shirone interjected.

"I never said I'd accompany you."

As the priests' expressions grew serious, Ares pushed Shirone forward and said,

"Come on! Let's eat first."

As Ares led Shirone's group away, he winked at the priests.

The woman asked,

"…What should we do?"

"Let's go for now. The Pope has high hopes for this expedition."

If there was something in the outer world, it had to be the monotheistic god believed by the Lami faith.

Thus, ten people gathered in a restaurant and sat side by side at a long table.

When Ares introduced himself, Kanis and Arin looked surprised.

'So that's Ares. I didn't recognize him because of the beard, but now I see his eyes resemble Amy's.'

Although archaeology and map-making were different fields, they fell under the same broad category.

"These two priests are from the Lami faith, as you might have guessed."

The man's name was Glen, and the woman was Lucia.

"You know the Vatican is divided into two departments: the Diocese Management Department and the Holy Knights Department."

"Yes."

The Holy Knights Department had paladins.

"Glen and Lucia work in the Diocese Management Department. Both are top-notch clergy."

For priests, faith was like mental strength for wizards; Shirone understood its significance.

'Indeed, if it's a mission to investigate ancient civilizations, the Vatican wouldn't send just anyone.'

Next, he introduced Great Swordmaster Euphrates, and then it was Shirone's group's turn. Glen and Lucia listened calmly until they heard about the Twelve Apostles.

'Dragons.'

They had seen Ice Dragon Frigi and Earth Dragon Argan fill an oasis with their breath a few days ago.

Although they were in polymorph form now, the chill from that time still lingered in their bodies.

Ares cleared his throat.

"Now that we've introduced ourselves and while we wait for food, let's hear it. How did you get the king's approval? I heard he's eccentric and reckless."

"We got help from Zulu." When Shirone explained what had happened so far, Ares's group nodded in understanding.

"Hmm, you stayed quite a while. But it's good you got the permit. So…"

Ares pointed his thumb at himself.

"Let us join too."

"No." Ares looked stunned by the immediate rejection while Glen and Lucia frowned.

"I'm sorry. I'd like to help you too, but this is really dangerous."

"How do you know?" Glen challenged.

"The Vatican has eyes and ears too. Ancient civilizations are related to the outer world, but no one knows exactly what kind of place it is." Shirone knew this well.

"Shall I say it? Yahweh, you just don't want Lami to investigate the Pyramid of Truth. If God's existence is proven there, Yahweh will be considered heretical."

"I don't care about that."

"Then why not let us join? Do you think we'll give up because it's dangerous? Ares and Euphrates here are stronger than your friends sitting next to you!"

Kanis flared up.

"What did you say?"

"Kanis, calm down," Arin tried to stop him but Kanis's shadow had already extended beyond his back.

'Magical creature.'

As long arms emerged from both sides of Kanis's shadow, Euphrates also revealed his killing intent.

Ares raised both hands.

"What is this atmosphere? He's my sister's boyfriend. Everyone calm down."

Shirone didn't want to fight either.

"This is pointless. The pass is mine. Forming a team is my right."

Glen didn't back down either.

"Exploring God's existence is our right. If we can't go in, neither can you."

Kanis spoke up,

"Shirone, just accept it. Glen or whatever your name is—does your god tell you to steal other people's passes?"

Glen's face turned red,

"Don't invoke God's name carelessly! This is my personal fight!"

"Haha! It's personal now? Well then…"

"Stop it!"

Lucia stood up abruptly and shouted,

"Don't blaspheme God anymore! Glen is doing this because of me. Glen, let's give up."

"No way. I'll find a way to get into the Pyramid of Truth no matter what."

Kanis said,

"Then go get your own pass."

Ignoring Kanis's sarcastic remark, Glen stared straight at Shirone,

"Let's decide here. You or me—who is more suitable to explore God?"

"I'll make it clear: I refuse. I don't want to fight anyone. I'm sorry, Ares."

Since Glen had ruined the atmosphere this time even Ares had nothing to say,

"We should leave now."

As Shirone tried to leave his seat Glen glared fiercely and lunged at him,

"Wait!"

"Glen! Please… Ugh!"

Lucia who had stood up to stop her lover suddenly clutched her chest and collapsed on the floor,

"Lucia!"

"Aah! Aah!"

Her entire body trembled with immense pain as veins rose along her neck reaching her cheeks,

"Lucia! Hang in there! Just a little longer! Lucia!"

Shirone immediately recognized it,

"The emotional disease."

It was a disease created by demons that had spread across continents reaching even the Vatican.
