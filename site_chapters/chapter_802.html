Chapter 802: Law Killer (3)

From Havitz’s reaction alone, it was clear that what Abella had said was true.

The head housemaid skipped all other formalities and summoned her to the grand hall.

It was a story about a flower garden that Havitz often reminisced about, and even the Gustaf Empire’s generals looked on with curiosity.

"Uncle Havitz."

Abella approached with a smile.

"Abella!"

As if transported back to that day 14 years ago, Havitz grabbed Abella's hand.

"You've come. Do you know how anxious I've been? I wonder how the flower garden turned out? The shop is..."

"I'm sorry, Uncle."

Abella interrupted Havitz, causing all the maids in the grand hall to flinch.

"She’s crazy. To interrupt His Majesty..."

Among the maids serving directly in the grand hall, there was a so-called "Havitz Manual."

"She’s going to die."

There's no standard for responding to chaos.

It's merely coincidental, like the belief that "if you perform a rain ritual, it will rain."

"Rule 283: Do not react when Havitz is excited and speaking."

For the maids, such superstitions were their lifeline.

"Huh? What are you sorry for?"

So, Havitz’s unexpected response shocked them further.

"Has the curse been broken?"

Abella smiled bitterly.

"I did open the flower shop, but the flowers you gave me died because I didn’t take care of them well."

"So that’s why you couldn’t come."

"No. I didn’t want to meet you. I was too young back then. I just lived thinking that’s how our relationship was meant to be."

"Really?"

Havitz seemed a bit disappointed.

"But you opened the flower shop. Don’t be too upset, I can buy you as many flowers as you want."

The head housemaid was stunned.

"The Emperor is making considerate remarks?"

Even she, who had spent her life in the imperial palace, had never seen such behavior.

"Maybe... we were wrong about Havitz."

Interrupting him had never been the issue.

"Could it be that the many maids he killed were for entirely different reasons than we assumed?"

The head housemaid was at a loss, and the other maids were even more confused.

"Why isn’t he angry? Why doesn’t he kill her?"

Don’t make eye contact, don’t use negations, among many other broken superstitions.

"How pathetic."

Balkan, reading the maids’ demeanor, squinted one eye.

"Fearing death without understanding why it comes is terrifying. The Emperor is also a person. He enjoys playing with friends and knows what love is."

Havitz is human.

"He just wants to have fun. After all, he's only ever thinking about using others for his own ends."

Chaos is not something to be understood with the mind.

"Empathy. Just be honest and show your emotions without calculation. That’s all there is to how the Emperor interacts with people." Of course, adults can't do this.

"But Abella can."

Because she is a friend who empathized with Havitz, the personification of chaos and emotion.

"Anyway, since you’re here, you’ve kept your promise."

Havitz, discarding trivial reasons, sat down where the map was spread and tapped the floor.

"Come here. We’re about to conquer the world, and you should join us. My friends are fun too."

The Emperor's unreserved demeanor was touching, but Abella's eyes were filled with sadness.

"Nothing about you has changed."

"You’ve aged a lot? You've grown taller and become more beautiful. If you don’t like conquering the world, shall we play something else?"

Tears welled up in Abella's eyes.

"You’re a bad person."

Everyone's hearts sank, and even Balkan couldn’t guarantee Abella's future this time.

"Empathy and hostility are clearly different. Will the Emperor remain calm this time too?"

Havitz blinked.

"People say that, that I’m bad. But honestly, I don’t really understand. To me, this is also..."

Abella spoke tearfully.

"It’s so nice because you’re like the old uncle with the mustache."

"Ha-ha-ha! Well, if you're happy, that's what matters."

Relief washed over everyone as Havitz laughed, and Balkan's eyebrows twitched.

"Ho? This is..."

Abella felt hollow.

"If you were scary like everyone else, I could hate you freely and harbor resentment..."

The ultimate evil, plunging the world into hell.

"But for me..."

He was the kindest and warmest uncle who would grant any wish.

"It’s not like I’m going to die."

In Havitz’s embrace, Abella could be happy for life, but for humanity’s sake, she had to kill him.

"That's what Alpha Fish is."

Collecting herself, she wiped her tears and said,

"I can’t play house with you like before. I'm an adult now."

"Really? What shall we do then?"

"Adult games."

Abella walked toward Havitz, who was sitting on the floor.

"Uncle, will you marry me?"

The generals’ gazes shifted to Havitz, and a chorus of gasps escaped from the maids.

"She's crazy. She’s going to marry that psycho?"

Havitz immediately nodded.

"Sure."

Balkan foresaw the emotions that had sprouted in Havitz’s chaos after 14 years.

"We met as friends... but my connection with Abella was a bit different."

"The fate of a witch. Perhaps it's only natural to be united with the one who freed her from that curse."

The one who reaches a certain truth must be strong, and thus Havitz is strong.

"That’s why he was so lonely." It's not wrong to say he was bored.

After all, those who could empathize with Havitz were few in the world.

"In a way, they are soulmates."

Abella puffed her cheeks.

"What? Even so, to agree so easily. Do you even know what marriage is?"

"Of course, I like you too."

Havitz stood up, took Abella's shoulder, and spoke seriously.

"I want to marry you."

Thus, the childhood friends who met at the beach 14 years ago promised to become husband and wife.

The oppressive summer heat gradually subsided, and autumn, tinged with fall colors, arrived.

The war was still fierce, but inside the Gustav Empire, preparations for the Emperor’s wedding were in full swing.

"Long live the Emperor! Hooray! Hooray!"

Streets were filled with the flower gardens Abella loved, and a national celebration event was held.

Twenty days before the wedding, as Marsack Palace was being decorated, Crouch made his move.

Navigating the complex ceiling paths as freely as his own home, he held the Law-Execution dagger.

"Target change."

Minerva said.

"The reason Alpha Fish is critical is that she can get closest to Havitz. But she can't enter carrying the Law-Execution."

While other items might be hidden inside the body, carrying a knife for months was not feasible.

"That's why we need a transporter." El Crouch.

"Before sending Alpha Fish, a specialist will already be positioned inside the palace. And on the night of Havitz and Abella’s wedding, the Law-Execution will be transferred."

Shirone asked.

"The transporter can’t approach Havitz. If meeting is impossible, how will it be transferred?"

"Use a target change." Minerva pulled out the dagger.

"What happens if, for example, while the Law-Execution is activated to kill Shirone, it stabs Woorin instead?"

"What happens then?"

"The intent within the dagger doesn't change. However, the holder’s intent is reset. Of course, this in itself isn’t significant. But if the dagger is destroyed in this state, it’s a different story."

"Isn't destruction impossible?"

"Yes. Even if destroyed, the Law-Execution will reappear at a perfect, arbitrary location."

Iruki said.

"But if reset, the appearance location of the Law-Execution can be artificially manipulated."

"That’s right. If destroyed in a reset state, the Law-Execution appears to the person most aligned with the intent within the dagger. Thus, through the law, Alpha Fish can transfer the Law-Execution."

This is the 'target change.'

"The execution time is 20 days later, at the wedding night. Until then, the Law-Execution must be reset."

Currently, the intent within the Law-Execution is directed at Havitz, but no significant legal change has occurred.

"That’s how difficult an opponent he is." Meaning it’s impossible to kill by any method, but for Crouch, it felt like the Law-Execution was just waiting for the right moment.

"My target is Catenia."

Having already killed the maid Orphea and taken her life, Crouch was planning another transformation.

"Becoming Catenia is best. If so, I can get even closer to Havitz."

With Abella engaged to Havitz, Catenia, as her maid, was living a prosperous life.

"Here it is."

Arriving at Abella’s room, Crouch landed in the hallway through the ventilation shaft.

The torch on the wall was the only light.

"Alpha Fish probably doesn’t know. Indeed, it might be better not to inform her."

The law is too sensitive and delicate; even a slight change could be irreversible.

"A cat thief has crept in here?"

Though Crouch had no heart, he feigned a shrug with superhuman speed.

"Damn, already?"

Turning around, one of the Gustaf Empire’s generals, the battle doll Natasha, tilting her head.

"Oh, Lady Natasha. Hello."

Regardless, as Orphea the maid, Crouch bowed politely in greeting.

"You're truly amazing."

Orphea responded with a puzzled expression.

"What do you mean…?"

"Wow, amazing! I can't feel any amateurish vibe at all! Such incredible acting."

It was none other than the shadow of Kashan's Emperor, Uorin, but he didn’t entertain such thoughts.

"How did you know?" Natasha seemed to read Crouch's mind and spoke with a sympathetic expression.

"You got caught by Balkan’s military spirit. He hires an assassin every month to try and kill His Majesty."

To compensate for the inability of the crowd spirit to detect single individuals, the risk of assassination was taken monthly.

'If an assassin joins, my own killing intent also becomes part of the crowd spirit.'

Anticipating this, Crouch did not panic and showed his true colors.

"So, you've come to kill me?"

Though his appearance, attitude, and voice remained the same, to Natasha, he seemed like a completely different person.

"Really fascinating. I just got goosebumps. Anyway, that’s correct. But they also told me to find out who sent the assassin. Whether the target is His Majesty or Abella."

'It’s refreshing dealing with someone so straightforward.'

Despite the unnecessary details, there was also a feeling of being ignored.

"Just tell me."

Crouch pulled out the Law-Execution dagger from within his maid uniform.

"I’ll tell you, after I cut your throat."

"Kakaka! That sounds good too."

As expected, Natasha wasn’t tense, but for an assassin who exploited situations, this was an opportunity.

'Interesting. Natasha...'

Ultimately, changing the target to anyone would do, but if it could be divided into Natasha, the assassination success rate would skyrocket.

'If that works, breaking one doll isn’t too regrettable.'

Since everyone has only one life, Crouch, who has two, could perfectly strike at his opponent's blind spot.

"Shall we start then?"

As Natasha crouched down, lowering her center of gravity, her arms slowly rose above her shoulders.

"The Dance of the Reaper."
