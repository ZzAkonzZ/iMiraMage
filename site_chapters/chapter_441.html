Chapter 441:

Shirone and Plu peeked into the warehouse where the captured rebels were held.

Raycis's subordinates, accompanied by Akamai, were guarding the iron door.

"What should we do? If we approach, we'll be caught for sure."

"Akamai seems to contract with its user through blood adhesion. We need to eliminate the user before they can react."

There were three Akamai, floating among seven men.

It was impossible to determine which one was contracted with which user just by looking at the spacing.

"We need to take them out all at once."

"It's all about timing. If we give them time to react, we'll be the ones caught."

"What if we approach using teleportation?"

"It's the easiest method, but not guaranteed. They're Nor mages too. If they're Raycis's subordinates, they must be skilled."

Shirone thought of Armand.

After being captured by True Akamai, he had lost the magic sword, but there was something Raycis didn't know.

Armand, integrated with the Ultima System, was connected through a single signal that transcended space and time, allowing information to be shared regardless of distance.

"I'll call Armand."

In Diamond Armor form, he could eliminate the subordinates before Akamai activated.

Given his physical abilities, which allowed him to fight Ikasa on equal terms, it was certainly possible.

"But they'll know our location. What if they don't know we've escaped yet? The more time we have, the better."

"Why not assume they already know? If we free the rebels, they'll find out our location anyway."

Shirone had a point.

The later Raycis realized, the higher the success rate of their plan, but basing a strategy on uncertain assumptions was risky.

"Hmm, alright. Let's do that. But no mistakes."

Shirone reached back and attempted precise control.

He couldn't accurately determine Armand's state, but he could sense its location through a vague feeling.

'This way.'

Feeling a magnetic pull, he amplified the sensation, and Armand flew into his grasp, turning at a right angle down the corridor.

Diamond Armor.

Shirone took a deep breath, watching the subordinates' reactions, then launched himself without warning.

"What the…?"

The blurry afterimage flickering before their eyes was the subordinates' last thought.

As Shirone's body spun, tendrils from Armand sliced through their necks faster than they could react.

Thud. Thud-thud.

Shirone frowned slightly at the unpleasant sensation flooding in through Armand.

He had no hesitation in eliminating those conducting cruel biological experiments, but it felt different from using magic.

'Stay calm. This isn't just my mission.'

Plu observed Akamai's reaction.

The users were dead, but if the blood adhesion remained, there was a chance they could still react.

'Should I deal with it? Or would that cause more trouble?'

Shirone didn't hesitate and attacked Akamai.

His robe fluttered as black afterimages slashed through the air.

The three Akamai were split in half like soft tropical fruits, falling to the ground.

Plu was amazed at the speed, unable to react.

'The Ultima System. It's incredible. With that, it will be a great help in fighting Heaven.'

Shirone and Plu exchanged glances in front of the iron door.

Breaking the lock and opening the door, a sharp gust of wind blew from inside the warehouse.

"Ugh!"

Armand's robe, strong against impact but weak against cutting, was nearly shredded.

Feeling the pain of being cut, Shirone quickly crouched and looked inside.

Dozens of glowing eyes appeared.

"Grrr."

The warehouse didn't hold the surface rebels.

Or maybe it did. Shirone recognized some faces.

But their appearances had changed, retaining only a few human traits.

Plu, who had dodged the surprise attack using the Dawkins algorithm, clutched her aching shoulder and grimaced.

"That wicked woman…"

Raycis had turned the rebels with no use into soldiers.

Though not made with the Fuzix Machine, their physical abilities, enhanced to telomere level 5, were formidable.

"Raaaargh!"

The berserk Nor soldiers charged at Shirone.

Over a hundred emerged from the warehouse, none retaining their former intelligence.

Light Curtain!

The light barrier struck the monsters, tearing their flesh and breaking their bones.

But the monsters didn't stop charging.

'What's going on?'

From the destroyed parts, flesh wriggled out, regenerating organs in an instant.

'Infinite Cell Proliferator Kenser.'

Plu, realizing the monsters' state, spoke.

"They have regenerative abilities. Ordinary attacks won't work."

The monsters split into two groups, targeting Shirone and Plu, but the weight of the battle didn't lessen.

Telomeres, the ends of chromosomes that determine a creature's limits, had been enhanced by Raycis to boost physical functions.

Combined with Kenser's trait of unlimited telomere usage, she had created soldiers that wouldn't rot or die.

Originally intended to match the Mecha's Guroi in the war against Heaven, they were now a major biological weapon against the Light of Zone 73.

"Raaaargh!"

Some had snake-like eyes, others had arms like worms, and some had become beast-like wolves. Their forms and abilities varied, but their ferocity was unmatched.

Armand's tendrils moved at incredible speed, slicing through their flesh, but Shirone felt like he was hitting a wall.

'At this rate, I'll tire out first.'

The monsters, closing the distance, pressed Shirone down with their bodies.

"Grrr!"

As the monsters piled on Shirone like a grave, one that hadn't tasted flesh let out a frustrated growl.

Crack!

The grave trembled.

Crack! Crack-crack!

The shaking intensified, and from within the pile of bodies, a monstrous scream rose as if from the abyss.

Crack-crack-crack!

The sound of bones breaking, muscles tearing, and organs and blood mixing filled the air, and everything began to be sucked towards the center.

"Raaaargh…!"

The last remaining monster crumpled like paper and disappeared into a black sphere.

Shirone struggled to his feet, supporting himself on the ground.

Having cast a shadow three times larger than usual, his body convulsed.

"Huuu!"

The power he had unleashed by pushing his mental transcendence to the limit with Immortal Function had significant aftereffects.

"Shirone, are you okay?"

Plu ran over.

The area where she had been fighting was littered with charred corpses.

If Shirone was a hard puncher who fought with brute force, Plu was an irregular fighter who used precise algorithms.

No matter how strong Kenser's regenerative abilities were, once ignited with Phoenix and delayed with the Dawkins algorithm, they couldn't withstand it.

"I'm fine. Just a bit overloaded."

Plu felt a slight chill as she looked at the corridor where 5 tons of weight had disappeared without a trace.

Gathering the monsters in one place and casting a shadow.

It was a straightforward, brute-force tactic with no irregularity.

"In the end, the rebels who could help us ended up like this. It's just the two of us now. We have to stop the Elixir of Legends."

Shirone had no intention of retreating.

Not only did he need to eliminate Raycis, but he also had a promise to return to Ikasa.

"There they are! Get them!"

Raycis's subordinates appeared from around the corridor corner.

They were elite combat soldiers who had started searching when Armand left its original position.

But the most despairing thing was that each of them had an Akamai.

They instinctively took combat stances, but the same thought crossed both their minds.

'Damn! We're too late!'

As Akamai's eyes opened and Antithesis activated, Shirone and Plu's movements slowed drastically.

It felt as if they were born unable to move, with no strength in their muscles.

'No, it's still weaker than the one guarding the cell. I can still land a strike.'

The problem was timing.

He had to either decapitate three people at once or eliminate three Akamai in a single attack.

'Wait until they're all within the horizontal slash trajectory.'

As Shirone and Plu couldn't move, Raycis's subordinates relaxed and approached.

"Phew, we finally caught them, those rat-like creatures. We almost got killed too."

Raycis had become extremely violent when the plan went awry, and seven people had died just by being near her.

If they had lost Shirone and Plu, they would have met the same fate.

A bald man approached with a sinister smile.

Having had his pride wounded by Plu during the affinity test, he now reveled in the reversal of fortune.

"Well, we meet again like this."

"You piece of trash. Aren't you afraid of the heavens after doing such things?"

"Heavens? You must not know, but this is the heavens. This isn't a place for the foolish humans of the earth to roam freely."

The bald man stroked Plu's chin.

"Hmm, the real thing is different."

"Get your hands off me."

"Kekeke, don't be like that. Your clones are probably all dead by now. You should cherish your one and only life."

Plu kept a close eye on the bald man's movements while meticulously calculating the positions of those in the corridor.

'Just a little more.'

She couldn't strike until all three Akamai were within range.

But the man leaning against the corner, smirking, showed no sign of approaching further.

"Hehe, we only need Shirone. So why not just…"

"Kiaaa!"

A black creature suddenly emerged from the corner, grabbing the man and throwing him to the ground.

Then, like a swarm of insects, dark figures swarmed the corridor, quickly turning their attention towards them.

"Aaaargh!"

The creature that had knocked the man down pressed its body close to the ground, shaking its torso, causing the man's legs to twitch as they stuck out from the corner.

"No, don't! Aaaargh!"

It was Garas.

Although its form was different from what Shirone had seen in the isolation room, none of the Garas looked the same anyway.

Garas attacked all creatures indiscriminately.

"Kiiik! Kiiik!"

The Akamai caught by Garas let out a choking sound, its pupils dilating as it trembled and fell.

The Garas, momentarily restrained by Antithesis, screamed in frustration at losing their breeding targets and charged down the corridor.

"This is…!"

The bald man's face turned pale as Garas, with its mixed traits of various creatures, charged.

Using magic was not even a threat.

Even if his body was torn apart, the creatures would undoubtedly implant their offspring in his belly.

Shirone used the strength he had reserved for a counterattack to pull Plu towards him.

As he embedded his tendrils into the wall and created distance, the horrific scene unfolded before them.

Black creatures swarmed the corridor like a seething mass.

From the depths of the darkness, the bald man crawled out, reaching towards Shirone.

"P-please…"

It was a look of utter despair.

"Kill me."

"Kiiik! Kiiik!"

"Aaaargh!"

The bald man's upper body was lifted, his face contorted with shock, fear, and pain all at once.

Shirone and Plu felt their hair stand on end.

It was fortunate they couldn't see Garas's actions hidden in the darkness.

"Let's go, Shirone."

As Plu tugged at his collar, Shirone took one last look at the man, whose eyes had rolled back and was drooling, then turned away.

The jaws of desire were devouring the command center.
