Chapter 769:

Wigo’s main estate was a massive temple built on the grasslands, 14 kilometers outside the capital city of Gardan.

Aside from the Eastern Sky Temple, the sanctuary of the monks, this was the place that housed the most seekers in the world.

However, most of them were now involved in the Zion Project, so by the time Shirone arrived, the temple was quiet.

“Wigo?”

A crowd of Wigo’s cousins surged forward upon hearing that the president of the Magic Association had arrived.

“What’s going on at the estate? And who are these people?”

When the High Judge of Theraphos issued judgment upon all of humanity, less than one percent were deemed aligned with “good.”

The fact that these cousins hadn’t entered Zion meant their cultivation had not yet reached its apex.

Wigo introduced them personally.

“These are Stars of the Ivory Tower. This is Star Arian Shirone, and this is Star Mirach Minerva.”

“Arian Shirone?!”

To the relatives who supported Wigo, Shirone was a deeply painful name.

“What an honor to receive someone so great. For a Five Star at the summit of the world to visit such a humble place…”

Before the mood overheated, Garcia stepped in to explain.

“Wigo has asked for guidance. If possible, we’d like to borrow a space suitable for Avatar Combat.”

The cousins’ faces lit up.

‘He’s finally seized the opportunity. Well done, Wigo.’

Even if Miro didn’t consider Shirone his disciple, it was undeniable that he had once studied under her.

If Wigo could defeat Shirone using Avatar Arts here, the Sky family could reclaim its title as the world’s greatest seeker lineage.

‘This wasn’t just Wigo’s burden.’

Reading the hostility in their eyes, Shirone realized where Wigo’s twisted resentment began.

‘This place has no teacher.’

Surely Cassia, the head of the family, understood the state of his son’s heart—but even so, he had taken everyone and left for Zion.

To protect humanity from the Demons.

‘Thank you.’

Shirone’s gaze shifted.

‘I’ll set Wigo right myself.’

The cousins backed away, their faces pale.

“Ugh! Wh-what’s that?!”

Even if their training was lacking, they were still Sky family—there was no way they wouldn’t sense the chill that froze the very soul.

With the warmth of Yahweh’s light withdrawn, Shirone now appeared to even Archmage Garcia as the perfect magician.

‘Is this really a magician barely in his twenties?’

Like absolute zero—no flicker of movement—his mind was cold to the extreme.

“Whew, how’s that? Impressive, right?” Minerva stepped up beside Garcia.

“Terrifyingly pure and terrifyingly calm—this is the mental state every mage dreams of.”

“...I can understand Wigo’s heart now.”

Just once, he wanted to feel it.

“To not be swayed by any stimulation... what must that feel like?”

“Give up—it’s easier that way. This is what a mage should be. Doesn’t he look way better like this than when he’s in Yahweh-mode?”

Garcia said nothing to her pointless remark.

“Let’s go. I want to end this quickly.”

When Shirone’s voice rippled through the Spirit Zone, Wigo felt his heart freeze with a sharp snap.

Tch. Show-off.

The Infinite Mage who had mastered all magic.

You couldn’t stand against that kind of purity of mind.

“Just to repeat, this is Avatar Combat. Using magic against one another is—”

“I know. Just take me to the field.”

Wigo turned toward the training ground.

“Follow me.”

Casting a Fly spell, Wigo led the way, followed by Shirone, Minerva, Garcia, and the cousins.

‘Hmph! Let’s see how far your arrogance gets you!’

The place Wigo brought them to was a vast clearing—big enough to engage in Avatar Combat without restraint.

The horizon was uninterrupted, and the site had been specially provided by the Republic of South Aymond.

“Alright! We’re here, so let’s get started!” Minerva clapped excitedly and turned to Garcia.

“Still not going to bet? Don’t tell me you’re too patriotic to wager against your nation’s golden boy.”

“I don’t gamble on others’ pain,” Garcia said firmly.

“But if it’s just a matter of opinion—I would place my hopes on Wigo.”

“Hahaha! So you’ve still got your pride.”

“…Do you really think so?”

Garcia wasn’t belittling Shirone’s ability, but Wigo had spent his life fighting an imaginary labyrinth.

‘The Avatar born from that effort…’

There are no eternal champions. Later generations will always analyze and absorb the best of the previous ones.

Today was the day Wigo’s efforts would bear fruit.

“No physical attacks allowed. The match will be decided solely by combat between Avatars.”

At their level, there was no need to worry—but the mental strain would be considerable.

“Understood.”

As Shirone deployed his Sequence Formula, the Avatar of the Radiant Angel surged skyward, towering above them.

Wigo’s cousins widened their eyes.

“Whoaaa…”

The distortion caused by the temporal field made the landscape waver to a nauseating degree.

“Magnificent.”

With a sharp smile, Wigo responded by deploying his own Avatar.

“Then allow me to show you my weapon.”

A thick, paint-like white smoke billowed from Wigo’s shoulder.

“Heavenly Knight.”

The smoke climbed to match the angel’s height and transformed into a white-armored knight.

Its form was sleek and agile, clearly designed for mobility. The armor gleamed as though coated in glass.

Its frame was slender enough to be mistaken for female, and it wielded twin nine-meter sabers in both hands.

‘That’s not just big, it’s crisp. Breaking that will take serious force.’

For the Sky family—raised studying realms from birth—Sequence Formulas were their specialty.

“How is it? This Avatar is called the Heavenly Knight. I believe it’s worthy of standing against a Five Star.”

To make the duel easier, Shirone put more than 300 meters between himself and Wigo.

“…Let’s begin.”

Still ignoring him, Shirone prompted Wigo to wrinkle his nose as he completed the Avatar deployment.

“You won’t even be able to track it!”

Just the Heavenly Knight closing the distance with its 20-meter frame was enough to make the mind reel.

‘This is it!’

Crossing its twin blades into an X, the Knight went to slash the angel’s neck—but Shirone let out a long breath.

“Phew!”

That was one second.

The space around the angel warped—and suddenly it was above the Heavenly Knight.

‘Heavenly Judgment!’

A beam of light shot down toward the Knight’s wide back. Wigo’s eyes snapped open.

‘I already knew!’

The white-armored Avatar flipped front to back and launched upward toward the angel.

‘Dump!’

With an impossible trajectory, the Knight twisted its torso and narrowly avoided the strike.

As the shockwave tore across the ground, the twin blades multiplied into countless copies, stabbing at the angel from all sides.

“Kh!”

Shirone furrowed his brow.

“It’s not over yet!”

The Knight’s white armor rippled like liquid, morphing into impossible stances as it lunged forward.

‘This is…!’

Shirone finally understood.

“Can you dodge my Dump?”

Sabers rained down from every possible angle—no blind spots—and pierced the Avatar of the Radiant Angel.

Each strike was a killing blow. The mental feedback crashing into Shirone’s mind was immense.

“Huuuuh…”

Yet his eyes remained steady.

‘You’re still holding on? Well, of course you are—you don’t break.’

Wigo’s lips curled in a twisted grin.

“Then suffer forever.”

The Knight’s form completely dissolved, transforming into a form beyond comprehension as it attacked again.

“…Impressive Avatar Art,” murmured Minerva with a serpent-like chill in her voice, exhaling a long stream of smoke.

“The collapse of form is the collapse of logic. So… does that mean it’s possible to print out thought itself?”

“It’s a law called Dump,” Garcia said.

“Wigo can output everything he thinks directly through the Heavenly Knight.”

That’s the law he wields.

“Even if Shifok expands the present, reactions are still nothing more than consequences following causes. But Dump…”

“Outputs cause and effect simultaneously.”

“Exactly.”

While this wasn’t possible through physics, it could be achieved through the mind—and that expression of unique individuality was what gave rise to an Avatar.

“To surpass Miro?”

Minerva didn’t care about Wigo’s psyche, but the moment she saw the Heavenly Knight, that was the first thing she thought of.

“The Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara of Miro—calmly perceiving and embracing all angles of the world.”

In contrast, the Heavenly Knight was a manifestation of Wigo’s own self being projected outward toward the world—an embodiment of explosion rather than observation.

“It’s an Avatar that reflects the essence of the Sky family. In terms of depth of Realm, I’d say he’s not far behind a Star.”

“Hmph.”

Minerva scoffed, but it was the only response she had to Garcia’s point.

‘I’m winning! I’m going to win!’

The Heavenly Knight was showing exactly what the collapse of logic looked like—what it meant to output raw thought.

It was not beautiful in any way.

But because it was so divorced from human aesthetics, it had become the most functional form in the universe.

‘Do you want to know what’s truly terrifying?’

If it were just Dump, one might scoff depending on the user’s level.

“Sky’s thoughts fill the entire sky.”

The Heavenly Knight struck the Avatar of the Angel with a form that blocked all trajectories—nullifying even temporal variables.

There was nowhere left to stab; the Angel’s body was impaled by countless sabers.

“Ghhrrrr…!”

A groan escaped Shirone’s lips, and sweat beaded on his forehead.

“Still holding on out of pride?”

The Heavenly Knight retracted its blades and stepped back.

“That’s disappointing. If you knew you couldn’t win, gracefully admitting defeat would’ve been a more beautiful scene.”

“Yeah. You really are strong.”

Shirone slowly raised his gaze.

“If I admitted defeat here, would you be satisfied?”

“Don’t talk nonsense.”

The Heavenly Knight extended countless spikes, mirroring Wigo’s fury.

“Trying to wriggle your way out with an excuse?”

Watching the full force of the Unlocked mind flood into him, Shirone grit his teeth.

‘Far more twisted than I thought.’

He hadn’t planned to go easy from the start, but now…

‘I’m sorry, Cassia…’

From here on, even in an Avatar duel, Wigo’s life could no longer be guaranteed.

‘This is the only way.’

When he first realized this technique, he’d assumed he would only ever use it against Nane.

‘Heavenly Judgment!’

The Lightborn Angel drove its spear downward.

“I told you—it’s useless!”

Wigo’s thoughts were projected again, and the Heavenly Knight contorted into an unimaginable shape to dodge the beam of light.

‘That’s one second.’

The Lightborn Angel—formed of time—rapidly shifted forms through Bachji, Ipdo, and Resonance.

‘And this timeline…’

Shirone clenched his teeth and unleashed an Avatar technique.

‘Bounce it!’

Lightborn Angel Avatar Art – Time Vibration.

“What…?!”

Even in a fleeting moment as brief as the smallest unit of time, Wigo’s thoughts managed to wedge their way through.

‘Why is it coming again?’

That’s what he couldn’t understand: how was Heavenly Judgment striking again within such a tiny sliver of time?

‘Dump is being—!’

When he finally dodged the three thousandth spear, Wigo understood what was happening.

‘The same moment is vibrating.’

Time was still stuck at the beginning of that one second.

‘How much longer…’

At the point when that single second had repeated 6,281 trillion times, Wigo’s mind finally reached its limit.

‘I… I don’t even know anymore…’

The moment he let go of the taut cord of cognition, time began to flow once more based on the standards of the five senses.

“AAAAAAGGGGHHH!”

It felt like his mind was being destroyed.

And in that fading, distant consciousness, the last thought Wigo grasped was this:

One second, infinitely repeating, containing 100,000 distinct events—a shockwave of overwhelming vibration.
