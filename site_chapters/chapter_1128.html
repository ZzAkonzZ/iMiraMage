Chapter 1128: Triggers (4)

"What is going on?"

Lupist and Plu, who had just left the conference room of Yongrae, froze in front of the hallway window.

A massive pyramid loomed faintly beyond the horizon.

Even with modern technology, it would take years to build such a structure.

'Impossible.'

The fact that it was erected in an instant made no sense.

Shirone came running.

"Mr. Lupist."

Ikael's group had left for the sun, and he had activated a simultaneous event at Delta headquarters.

Some unknown agreement had taken place, but they did not show it.

Plu asked.

"What is that?"

"It seems to be something from the Kingdom of Paras. Probably… a structure from an ancient civilization."

Lupist's expression became serious.

"I thought it was built with direct intervention from the outer world. Why now?"

"Ancient civilizations existed before human history. I suspect it's a passage connecting to the outer world. Soon, the world's system will change."

Shirone spoke about the state of the angels.

"Angels with hearts have not changed. That means it's not immediately harmful to humans."

"That may be true, but the impact will be significant. Moreover, the intensity will increase over time."

A Delta official approached.

"An emergency holy war meeting is starting. Representatives from each country are to gather at headquarters quickly."

Other countries also seemed to be in turmoil over the current situation.

'Kitra will come too.'

Lupist said.

"Let's go. Something will come up. It's best to listen first and then respond."

"Go ahead."

Lupist turned his head at the unexpected remark, but Shirone remained silent.

'Wizard.'

By now, it should have been settled in some way, but the silence was strange.

"I have somewhere to stop by."

As Shirone ran down the hallway, Lupist and Plu looked at each other's faces.

"He didn't notice, did he?"

"No."

It was only a matter of time anyway.

At the place where she had fought Havitz, Wizard had been lost in thought for a long time.

When even her tears had dried up, she slowly raised her hands and grasped her neck.

"As expected."

Dying was the right thing to do.

With her already broken spirit, she couldn't face Havitz again.

As her grip tightened and oxygen was cut off from her brain, the scenery sparkled.

Her face turned red, but her expression did not change.

'I'm not afraid of dying.'

What she truly feared was Shirone's face looking down at her corpse with pity.

'I'm sorry, brother.'

As consciousness faded and death approached, her heart gradually felt lighter.

It must have been because she had carried so much.

"Sob. Sob."

Tears flowed down.

'I will die.'

Rather than living and disappointing Shirone, it was better to meet him as a corpse.

'Just a little more.'

As her mind convulsed from the shock and she no longer felt pain…

"Aaaah."

Wizard eventually let go of her hands.

-Just promise me one thing.

Shirone had said.

-Do whatever you want. If you think that way, I will support you. But…

Never think it's impossible.

"I'm sorry. I'm sorry…"

She had to fight.

Her destiny and genius existed solely for that purpose.

Grabbing her trembling legs, Wizard stood up.

'Kill Havitz.'

She could no longer look at him indifferently, but she understood Havitz that much more.

'It's about not killing him.'

That was also the way Gustav IV targeted Havitz.

"You can't kill Havitz."

Balkan said.

"The event of killing Havitz doesn't happen. Therefore, to kill Havitz…"

Zetaro said.

"You must not kill him."

"Right. It's a contradiction, but as long as we like Havitz, we can still approach him."

Natasha asked.

"What about Vanishing?"

"It's hard to expect purity from us. But that's because they don't know Havitz well."

Smoto said.

"There's no way to stop Vanishing, but you can make it so it doesn't activate. That's what you mean, right?"

That was also a contradiction.

"I first realized it when I married Abella. On the first night, she tied Havitz tightly to the bed."

Zetaro also recalled that time.

"I noticed the assassination plan late and went to Havitz's room. It was close."

"No, it was already too late. If Abella had stabbed with the 'Law Kill,' Havitz would have died. But then Havitz…"

Told Balkan to leave.

"Everything is just a joke. Regarding the death that comes with goodwill, Havitz seems to expect it. Even his own death must be interesting."

Smoto said.

"It's easy to say, but it's difficult in reality. Maintaining such a contradictory mental state is not human."

"Or it's genuine."

Balkan turned his head.

"You."

The three of them looked at Zetaro.

Havitz muttered as he walked through the forest.

"How thrilling."

There was only one thing he had not experienced despite committing all the world's evils.

'Dying.'

Ten shadows appeared at his feet, and Siok reappeared.

"Satan, you called."

They still thought Wizard should die, but a fanatic could not hold a grudge against their god.

"Two are missing. Teeth are gone."

"I'm sorry."

Currently, the positions of 4 o’clock and 12 o’clock were vacant due to their deaths.

However, that was only from a human perspective. From the perspective of a god, concepts never disappear.

“Hurry up and elect replacements.”

It wasn’t something that could be rushed, but Havitz wanted to have a proper "playtime."

“Understood.”

The Siok.

These were the 12 positions uniquely capable of traveling between reality and hell, even when the spiritual realm was sealed.

In the deep caves of the reverse world, Etella lay turned toward the darkness, as if lifeless, deep in sleep.

She had asked countless times about the duration of purification.

87 aeons and 2,875 epochs…

When she heard this was the seventh-longest time since the reverse world had existed, she crumbled.

‘I wish I were dead.’

Even as she slept, she desperately wished for it.

If only she could close her eyes and forget herself, she hoped never to awaken again.

“Haa…”

Even sleep became unbearable, and when she awoke to reality, clear tears streamed down her face.

Connected by the chain of yin and yang, Shagal leaned against the wall of the cave, lost in thought.

There was more than enough time—an overabundance, in fact.

‘Director…’

Raiden, the director of the circus troupe.

The man who had killed the only person Shagal had ever loved, Tia. In turn, Shagal had driven a dagger into Raiden's body.

‘Was it all a lie?’

The Green Circus Troupe had never existed in this world.

‘Or… did it?’

Having fallen to hell and reached his current state, a thought had crossed his mind.

‘Then what is real?’

Do the people living within their memories hold the truth of the world, except for me?

His gaze shifted to Etella.

‘Tia…’

Though her face was turned away and unseen, Shagal was convinced she was Tia.

‘Because I believe so.’

In reality, he didn’t know Tia’s face, nor who she truly was.

“Tia.”

At the sound of Shagal’s voice, Etella’s shoulder flinched, but she didn’t move further.

The chains scraped coldly against the floor as Shagal crawled toward her like a beast and climbed on top of her.

The same act. The same days. Over and over.

The chain of yin and yang made each other's emotions disturbingly indistinguishable, as if they were their own.

Shame, resentment, pleasure, and anger mixed together until, in the end, they felt nothing at all.

A vortex of yin and yang.

Rian asked, “Eight Extremes Imperial Constitution?”

“Yes. I’m planning to attack the demonic realm using the sound of sanctity. Theoretically, it’s possible, but it’s hard to say how effective it will be.”

“You’re ruthless.”

Rian reflected on the resolve of Jin-Gang.

“Even if it succeeds, sanctity won’t be free. It’ll linger, even if the physical body disappears. Jin-Gang knows this and is still determined to seek vengeance.”

Lost in thought with a bitter expression, Rian turned to Shirone.

“You’re not just going to let this happen, are you? I’m here now. If I use the fire of Gehenna to purify the karma of sanctity…”

“Even so, we can’t bring it into reality. The only way is to use the Siok.”

The 12 fanatics who create gaps in time.

“The number 12 in law represents perception. Humans divide the whole into 12 parts: 12 hours, 12 constellations, 12 apostles, and the Siok. It’s no coincidence. Even if the Siok die, their concept will simply be replaced by someone else.”

“So, the plan is to place true sanctity in the Siok’s position. But… where can we find it?”

The assassin Lichela, hanging from Son Yoo-jung’s waist, showed interest.

“It’s probably at the Fire Management Control Branch.”

Everyone’s gaze shifted in that direction.

“Control Branch?”

“Yes, I heard about it once. The living who fall into hell are usually taken by the Black Riders. But it’s said that, in rare cases, Satan selects elites to send there.”

“Elites.”

Shirone rested her chin in her hand.

“So those who succumbed to Satan’s temptation are there. Then Mortasinger must be among them.”

Mortasinger—ranked 10th in the Council of Ten and the reason Son Yoo-jung followed Ikael to the human world.

“Mortasinger?”

Son Yoo-jung grabbed Lichela’s hair and lifted her head.

“Where is she?”

“I don’t know exactly! I’ve heard he’s somewhere west of hell, but I’m not sure!”

Shirone interrupted.

“Stop it.”

“What do you even know, you fool?”

When Son Yoo-jung didn’t listen, Shirone raised his voice firmly.

“I said, stop it.”

The chain of Gingoa, wrapped around Yoo-jung’s neck, tightened sharply. Her eyes widened in shock.

“Hrrgh!”

On her knees, she turned to Shirone with a pitiful expression.

“Huh?”

Finally regaining her senses, Son Yoo-jung also noticed the strange energy radiating from a distant mountain.

Shirone’s expression grew serious.

“Rian.”

“Yes, what is that?”

From the cave deep in the mountain, an indescribable, eerie presence wavered like a mirage.

“Woah…”

As if nothing had happened, Son Yoo-jung straightened her posture and Shirone gave a command.

“Let’s go.”

They teleported to the entrance of the cave. From the darkness, groans echoed.

A man and a woman.

‘Shining.’

Projecting a sphere of light from his palm, Shirone advanced about 30 meters and saw faint silhouettes.

Familiar faces.

“Etella, Teacher?”

If it had been a pleasant situation, Etella wouldn’t have been crying. Rian understood immediately.

“This is disgusting…”

Drawing his broadsword, he launched himself forward, and Shagal turned his head.

Moving like a beast, Shagal instinctively raised both arms, but a flurry of swords rained down on him.

Son Yoo-jung gaped, her mouth hanging open.

‘What a murderous aura…’

Dozens of daggers flew simultaneously, but Rian didn’t retreat.

“Hmph!”

With a single swing, he deflected the daggers and used the broad side of his broadsword as a shield to close the distance.

“What…?”

It was then that Shirone noticed the chain connecting Etella and Shagal.

“Rian! Wait—”

Before he could finish, Rian, with godlike strength, pinned Shagal against the wall.

“Guhhh!”

Blood gushed from Shagal’s mouth under the pressure, and at the same time, Etella, who had been covering herself, coughed violently and vomited blood.

Realizing what was happening too late, Rian stepped back from Shagal, glaring at him like a predator.

“What’s going on? Why is she…”

Shirone murmured gravely.

“The Chain of Yin and Yang.”

It was the worst possible entanglement.
