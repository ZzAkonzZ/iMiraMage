Chapter 430:

Shirone and Plu waited in a room at the 2nd Headquarters. Although the team member called it "waiting," they knew it was essentially isolation.

Plu, having observed the situation outside, sat next to Shirone.

"Now, tell me what happened."

Shirone explained everything that had occurred in Babel's control room, including the Ultima System that unified all signals and the historical records of Babel obtained through it.

Plu took a deep breath and pondered.

She had heard bits about Heaven's history, but this was entirely different.

"The person whose record was deleted in the Babel Project, could it be related to the reset?"

McClain Goffin.

Shirone was almost certain.

What shocked him most was that Goffin was connected to the grave crime committed by Ikael.

"Looking back, Goffin must have had a reason to leave this world, unrelated to its affairs. That's why he chose the complex method of resetting and reconfiguring."

"That makes sense, but I think it might also be related to the Ultima System. How does it work?"

"It's more of a feeling than a language. Just as you feel heat when you touch something hot or a sting when pricked by something sharp, these signals come in almost infinitely differentiated."

"Hmm, I think I get it…"

While she understood intellectually, it was impossible to grasp the sensation without experiencing it.

"In that sense, the Ultima System seems to be the opposite concept of the Akashic Record. If the Akashic Record is perfect even when parts change, the Ultima System clearly distinguishes all parts. If all Gaia were integrated with the Ultima System, it wouldn't be an exaggeration to say they could have opposed Anke Ra."

"But conversely, the fewer the numbers, the weaker the power, right?"

"Yes. I can only distinguish the meanings of the signals."

Plu had a different opinion on that.

"That's a really useful ability. It will help here, where we don't know the language, and its applications are limitless."

"Yes. If we survive this place."

Plu gave a bitter smile at Shirone's grim joke.

"True. By the way, where did Babel go? Since it's a weapon against Heaven, did it go there?"

"Possibly. We should report this to Sein. It could be a significant variable."

"Right, let's ask Gardrak when we get a chance. He seemed really eager to leave here."

"Clove too."

They chuckled quietly.

Heaven's 6th Heaven, Great World War

Since Arius entered Drimo, Kariel hadn't left the Great World War.

Miro still showed no signs of waking up.

It wouldn't be easy.

Even someone well-versed in the human mind would find it as difficult as releasing a fish into the sea and catching it again ten years later to extract only Miro's essence from Drimo.

'Brahma will handle it well.'

Brahma, the Mara with the greatest insight, was trustworthy.

Yet, the beautiful face watching the central processing unit remained shadowed.

Then, a category of data stored long ago flashed on the screen.

The year indicated it was information deeply buried in the angels' memories.

Kariel's eyes lit up with curiosity.

"What is this?"

After staring at the screen for a long time, Kariel tilted his head in confusion.

Babel had descended.

Tracking its location confirmed it. A red dot rapidly flying over purgatory appeared on the screen.

"But how?"

Kariel had been the one to dismantle Babel's production process during the war with Gaia.

As the Archangel of Creation, Kariel disliked Uriel's method of destruction.

It was unproductive, irreversible, and foolish.

Instead, he had modified the algorithm in the Babel program to reset Babel's recognized enemies from Heaven to the citizens.

This was the main reason Gaia's second resistance failed.

"Who activated it?"

Since Goffin's erasure, pure Nephilim no longer existed in purgatory. And no angel would defy Anke Ra's command to awaken Babel.

Kariel transmitted Babel's data to the screen.

From the moment the program activated, Babel's exploration unfolded in a first-person view.

Kariel recognized a familiar face.

As Babel approached, the boy's face zoomed in.

Babel ultimately deemed the target non-hostile.

If the boy on the screen was who Kariel thought, it made perfect sense.

"Heh heh."

Kariel felt a thrill in his soul. A deep, satisfying laugh erupted from within.

"Ha ha ha ha! Ha ha ha ha!"

Finally, his opportunity had come.

All humans deserved to die, but Shirone was especially detestable.

The human Ikael loved most.

"Can he still hold his head high in front of me after seeing this?"

For the first time in a while, Kariel left the Great World War.

The first thing that came to mind was Ikael's pale, stricken face.

Heaven's 7th Heaven, Aravot

In Aravot, where Anke Ra resided, there was a room where Archangel Ikael, once the head of the angels, was confined.

Kariel walked briskly down the corridor, a victorious smile on his face.

Of course, he had no intention of handing over Shirone's information easily.

Even with his power sealed, Ikael's name remained a symbol of glory among the angels.

It was too soon.

But the thought of this amusing incident was too tantalizing to keep to himself.

'Let's see that proud face. I hold the reins anyway.'

Just before reaching Ikael's room, a spatial rift appeared before Kariel, and hundreds of glass panels shimmered at different brightness levels.

As the outlines connected, they solidified into the form of a man.

A signal that could store unique information in specific panels.

Ikael's three-horned Mara, Ashur, blocked Kariel with eyes as deep as his dark hair.

"What brings you to Aravot, Angel of Creation?"

"I'm here to see Ikael. She's inside, right?"

Kariel moved forward as if he didn't need an answer, but Ashur quickly blocked his path again.

Kariel's face twisted in anger.

"Is a mere Mara going to stop me?"

He vividly remembered Ashur appearing and drawing his sword just as he was about to capture Shirone.

At that time, he had let it go because Ashur was following Ikael's orders, but this time he wouldn't forgive it.

"Ikael doesn't want to see anyone."

"Anyone? A confined angel has such bold dreams. Move aside, you insignificant Mara."

Kariel roughly shoved Ashur aside and burst into the room before he could be stopped.

The foremost of all angels, the radiant Ikael, sat gracefully on the floor, waiting for Kariel.

"It's been a while, Kariel."

Kariel forced a smile.

Even now, standing before Ikael made his halo shrink, but today he didn't want to show weakness.

"Hmph, sitting on the floor, how ridiculous. Where's the authority you once had as the head of the angels?"

Ikael smiled kindly.

"Your presence has weakened. Well, you've always been so focused on one thing that you couldn't see anything else."

Indeed, Kariel's complexion was noticeably gaunt. Even the spark in his eyes, once full of creativity, had dulled.

"What are you up to that I don't know about? Anke Ra must have forbidden angelic activities."

"Heh heh, what will you do with that knowledge? You have no power left. You're dying to know what I'm planning, aren't you? But what can you do? You're just a puppet angel with nothing but your old reputation."

"You look like a child begging for milk. What are you so afraid of?"

Afraid? Me?

Kariel's eyes widened.

"Ha ha ha! I'm not afraid of anything. I'm even greater than you! Who would I…"

Kariel's words abruptly stopped.

Ikael began to untie her pure white robe.

As she pulled it off her shoulders, the garment slid down.

Her shoulders, supporting her proud neck, her slender yet strong arms, and her flawless back revealed Kariel's frozen face.

"Come here, Kariel. My clever child. Come into my arms like before and tell me everything. Then sleep well, and everything will be fine."

"Uh…"

Kariel, with a dazed expression, almost ran to her but quickly regained his senses.

The Ikael he so hated was still beautiful.

The most perfect body in a world of pure white.

The pinnacle of purity, with no particles or impurities.

He wanted to nestle in her arms and rub his cheek against hers, telling her everything.

Yes, that was everything.

That alone had been the ultimate Ataraxia.

'But she… she!'

A mere human had defiled her.

"Shut… up!"

Kariel's halo expanded into a spinning ring.

Everything in the room rattled, and Ikael's golden hair fell over her face.

Kariel approached, shouting madly.

"Shut up! Do you have the right to say that? You… you…! Ugh!"

Kariel couldn't withstand the mental turmoil and quickly lowered his head.

His already weakened presence was on the verge of collapsing.

'Damn it! Why…!'

Not once, never, had he ever desired to possess or monopolize her.

A pure reverence, entirely different from human desire.

He had once felt absolute happiness just knowing that the archangel Ikael existed.

'Foolish humans! Vile creatures!'

Why couldn't humans leave beauty as it was?

Did they want to possess it so badly? Couldn't they share this beauty with others?

Everyone wanted to be loved by Ikael. Was it out of jealousy that they took her away?

To Kariel, Ikael was no longer an object of reverence. She was a withered flower, broken by the hands of a mere human.

'I can't forgive this. No, I won't forgive this!'

Kariel leaned in close to Ikael, who was still bowing her head, and whispered.

"Listen carefully. I will never forget. So I will do what you hate the most. Because you did what I hate the most!"

As Kariel stormed out of the room, Ikael, with a sorrowful expression, adjusted her clothes and spoke softly.

"Ashur."

At her call, Ashur prostrated himself before her, his expression equally sorrowful after witnessing his master's humiliation.

"Your eternal servant responds to your call."

"Please investigate what Kariel is planning."

Due to the law of familiars, Ashur's power was weakened as much as Ikael's was sealed, but he had no reason to refuse her request. However…

"The atmosphere in Heaven is becoming increasingly tense. If something like today happens again…"

Ikael's bright smile rendered Ashur's concerns moot.

"It won't happen again. Please."

No matter what sins she had committed, Ikael was still Ikael.

With just one smile, countless angels and Maras would willingly lay down their lives for her.

Ashur bowed silently.

His body shattered into hundreds of glass panels and vanished in an instant.
