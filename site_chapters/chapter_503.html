Chapter 503: Wholehearted Resolve (2)

“Humans…”

Uorin spoke.

“…are capable of throwing their hearts into anything.”

This was the conclusion she had reached after experiencing countless lifetimes.

“But they cannot receive in return. We exist only to cast ourselves toward something.”

Because of this, desires and ambitions are never fulfilled, and the world remains in perpetual chaos.

“Being so self-centered, they might even choose to end themselves.”

When humans actively seek death, they become something beyond mere mortals.

“They even devote everything to things devoid of life.”

“In some sense, it’s selfish,” Gando remarked.

Uorin smiled at his words.

“Yes, humans are selfish creatures. But Gando, that’s exactly what makes them terrifying. They are so selfish that they can perform acts of altruism. We are such paradoxical beings.”

Uorin did not know the precise events occurring in the Heavens, yet she understood without needing to see—because she understood the essence of humanity.

Gaold and his companions would inevitably collapse the future.

With profoundly selfish hearts and an overwhelming sense of altruism, they were likely throwing their very souls toward something.

“This place…”

Kuan moved forward.

“…is my grave.”

As he pushed off with his right foot and launched himself forward, for Nameless, the world itself seemed to flip upside down.

In truth, it was Kuan’s body that had flipped, and now he floated inverted, twisting erratically in midair.

‘There’s no way to predict his sword’s path.’

Even with his superhuman insight, which far surpassed human limits, Nameless couldn’t discern Kuan’s attack trajectory.

“Hup!”

By a hair’s breadth, Kuan’s blade grazed past the back of Nameless ’s neck.

Only then did Nameless faintly realize why he had been paralyzed in judgment.

There was no way to predict the sword’s path because it didn’t exist in the first place.

Amid endless uncertain variables, Kuan constructed decisive movements in real-time.

It was the complete opposite of human thought—acting with a set purpose—and thus, even if one understood, they couldn’t defend against it.

‘Should I cut off my arm too?’

The thought briefly crossed Nameless s mind, but he quickly shook his head.

Kuan had severed his arm because balance was no longer necessary.

It wasn’t that he gained enlightenment by cutting off his arm; rather, he no longer needed his arm because he had already attained it.

‘Terrifying… This is the sword.’

For the first time in his life, Nameless truly understood the terrifying depth of the sword, the very first skill he had ever learned.

Perhaps it didn’t matter what skill he had chosen, but now he knew—there was infinite mystery in the sword he wielded.

‘I want to conquer it!’

A powerful desire that could not be replaced by anything else struck Nameless ’s mind.

Countless small epiphanies, gained through years of monotonous, repetitive practice, crystallized in him at lightning speed, pushing his swordsmanship toward its ultimate peak.

“Hyeeeeeaaaah!”

Nameless ’s pupils contracted sharply, and his tongue lolled from his open mouth.

Wielding his twin swords erratically in front of him, he looked like a madman.

Watching him flail like a child throwing a tantrum, both Siina and Armin shuddered in horror.

The pinnacle of swordsmanship, reached by a genius driven mad by the blade, was neither beautiful nor radiant as humanity had imagined.

But in terms of sheer efficiency in killing, no swordsman could dispute its perfection.

Like a wave of countless blades approaching from behind, Nameless charged, carrying with him innumerable potential sword paths.

“No!”

Siina sprang to her feet, trying to rush out of the barrier, but Armin grabbed her by the waist and pulled her back.

There was no question of Nameless stealing their techniques. The mad genius was clearly uninterested in magic.

And that was precisely why he had been able to reach the pinnacle of killing, where even Armin would be decapitated the moment he revealed himself.

“Siina! This isn’t our fight!”

MNameless ’s reaction speed, perfected by choosing the path of the sword, had reached a level that even the most gifted magician couldn’t match.

Tears welled up in Siina’s eyes.

Nameless’s brutal swordsmanship all but guaranteed Kuan’s death.

‘Because of me… It’s because of me…’

For the first time, such a thought occurred to her.

Everyone knew that anyone could die during this mission, and Kuan’s death would simply be one of many.

That was why she could previously speak with such confidence.

But at this moment, Siina felt guilty.

It also meant that Kuan’s death could no longer be lumped together with the deaths of everyone else.

‘He must not die! If you die…!’

Pushing Armin away, Siina took a step beyond the barrier.

“Don’t come!”

At that moment, Kuan’s shout froze her in place.

Though her recognition lagged behind, she saw it simultaneously—the moment Nameless ’s sword sliced through the air before her.

Nameless had missed Siina, yet he said nothing.

His gaze remained fixed solely on Kuan, the enigmatic man before him.

“...please.”

At long last, Kuan added a polite honorific, his body drenched in blood from numerous wounds.

He was breathing as rapidly as his heartbeat, yet his eyes still shone with unyielding resolve.

Ensuring that Armin had pulled Siina back behind the barrier, Kuan turned his fierce killing intent fully toward Nameless.

‘Damn it. This guy is really strong.’

If this being crossed into the human world, how many soldiers of the Windguard would have to be sacrificed to stop him?

Even on the brink of death, a swordsman’s curiosity was irrepressible.

And because of that, another question naturally followed.

‘If I were to fight Windguard now… could I defeat him?’

For the first time, Kuan consciously confronted the thought he had long avoided due to trauma.

He still wasn’t entirely confident he would win, but neither did he believe he would lose as he once had.

At that moment, Kuan felt a surge of heat and, for the first time since his birth, was glad to be alive.

‘I’ll end this here.’

Though his skima was suppressing the bleeding as much as possible, it couldn’t fully stop the blood flowing from his severed arm.

No matter what the outcome, this was the end.

‘I have no regrets.’

Kuan let his sword hang low and slowly advanced toward Nameless.

Step by step, he limped forward, his resolve unwavering.

Nameless’s lips twitched uncontrollably as he watched Kuan approach.

With each step Kuan took, the world seemed to sway more violently until, finally, his figure began to flicker.

It was as though he was slipping out of reality itself.

‘I could never do that, no matter how long I trained.’

This wasn’t a matter of skill but of individual nature.

The moment Nameless realized that his sword was fundamentally different from Kuan’s, he felt, for the first time, a genuine fondness for the blade.

‘Then this… is my sword.’

As Nameless forward, Kuan spun his body into a swift rotation.

“Hyeeeeeaaaah!”

If Nameless’s maddened technique embodied precise killing intent, Kuan’s swordsmanship was dazzling and elegant.

‘Yes.’

Within the chaotic whirlwind of the world around him, Kuan focused solely on Siina’s face.

‘I am a clown, a mere jester.’

Neither able to approach nor pull away, he was merely a jester dancing before her.

“Hyeeeeeaaaah!”

As Nameless’s countless sword paths seemed to split the very fabric of space into infinity, Kuan hurled himself toward that infinite death.

‘I’ve won.’

Even if he were to die, Armin would remain by Siina’s side.

‘I’ve won.’

He had surpassed Armin.

He had saved Siena.

At the bitter end of a wretched life, all that remained was a small consolation no one would ever acknowledge.

Kuan’s eyes turned red, but he grinned grotesquely, gripping his sword tightly.

What did it matter?

‘I am… a sword demon!’

Kuan’s body spun faster than ever before, shaking wildly as he hurled himself forward with all his might.

“Raaaagh!”

Amidst Nameless’s countless sword paths, a single streak of light sliced through, cleaving through the infinite chaos.

“Ergaaaaaa!”

Zulu let out a choked scream.

Red blood streamed from her wide-open eyes, and her soulless expression was grotesque enough to send chills through anyone watching.

Oooh!

With every passing moment, the Lich grew stronger.

Kali’s four great weapons activated their abilities one after another, but the Lich absorbed death as though it welcomed it.

A sphere of electricity hovered above the Lich’s head, discharging lightning in all directions.

Even after the blue electricity vanished, the white afterimage remained imprinted on their retinas, and Zion’s equipment lay in ruins amidst the flickering light.

Zulu began to sob uncontrollably, as though her mind had regressed to that of a frightened child.

She felt utterly alone.

The loneliest being in the universe.

The heart that she could not offer to anyone had become a gnawing maw, consuming her from within, dragging her deeper into the abyss of solitude.

Oooh!

“Oh my god…” whispered Kali, her skin trembling.

The Lich was a stark embodiment of how far the human mind could sink.

Zulu’s spirit grew increasingly dark and murky, and the Lich’s lightning storm continued to spread unchecked.

Hundreds of arcs of electricity raced along Zion’s walls until they merged into a single bolt and struck Kali head-on.

“Aaaaah!”

Even the agonized scream of the Goddess of Death paled in comparison to the grief emanating from the Lich.

The Lich wrapped its electric body around Kali, merging with her in a final, devastating act.

Sssss…

Fractures resembling spiderweb cracks began to spread across Kali’s blackened form until her body hardened into brittle ash.

Even after both the Lich and Kali had disintegrated, the shattered mind that had fallen into the abyss did not calm.

Zulu dropped to her knees, hugging herself tightly, repeatedly calling out Erga’s name.

“Erga… Erga…”

“Krrrrrr…”

As Sangsa, the familiar, activated, a small black bird appeared in front of Zulu.

Erga, with a cold expression, gazed down at Zulu with an unmistakable look of disdain.

“Ahhhh! Aaaaah!”

Kanya clutched the mangled remains of the barbell and wept bitterly.

The destroyed object in her hands looked worse than death itself.

Though life had never existed within it, that detail did not matter to Kanya.

Humans are so unbearably lonely.

“Why… why is life so difficult?” Uorin asked, gazing up at the ceiling like a lost child.

“Why does it hurt more to give your heart away?”

As Gando looked at Uorin’s faint expression, a thought crossed his mind.

Did she know? Did she realize that she was hurting while thinking of someone?

That he, watching her, was hurting as well?

‘Probably not.’

Because that’s what it means to be human.

In the Halls of Corruption, after even Kariel had departed, it was only in the final moments of war that Uriel began to vaguely understand Anke Ra.

Gaold was still fighting, but he no longer posed any threat.

His mind had already sunk into hell, and what now drove him was nothing more than the primal instinct of a final, desperate struggle.

“Ahhhh! Aaaaah!”

Kangnan pulled at her bound arms with all her might.

A tingling sensation spread from her wrists, where the Binding Curse had taken hold.

That tingling must have been pain—but for Kangnan, it was her only hope.

‘It’s working!’

The fact that she could feel pain meant that the Binding Curse was failing to function properly.

A blind, unwavering will to reach Gaold drove Kangnan beyond her physical limits.

“Ahhhh! Argh! Argh!”

The handcuffs clanged loudly against her wrists, skin tearing and blood seeping from where her flesh had peeled.

‘This is what it means to be human.’

Uriel pondered.

The one who throws their heart into everything.

A being capable of both good and evil, one who disrupts the absoluteness of the world through their contradictions.

A being born from the Akashic Records, yet destined to move beyond them.

“Ahhhh! Argh! Argh!”

Kangnan thrashed her arms violently.

As the overwhelming power of divine transcendence surpassed the force of the Binding Curse, an ominous sound echoed through the air.

Crunch. Crack.

Her bones groaned under the strain, and the recoil from her frantic movements grew increasingly fierce.
