Chapter 605:

Liz asked again.

“What?”

“Do you know who Oscar was meeting?”

“Well, the branch manager…”

“His daughter joined them. They linked arms and went for a walk before going into the house together. Doesn’t that raise any suspicions?”

It would be a lie to say it didn’t.

Even with his certification, Oscar’s skills weren’t enough to get a special appointment at the association.

“You must be mistaken. The branch manager’s daughter might have just joined them and escorted him.”

Given the circumstances, that interpretation was possible, but Neide wasn’t so naive as to miss the subtle emotional cues between a man and a woman.

“I’m not lying. If you had seen it yourself, you’d believe me.”

Liz felt humiliated.

“Stop it. Why are you doing this? Nothing will change because of this. You know that, right?”

“Why won’t it change? Oscar doesn’t truly love you!”

“Even if that’s true, it’s my problem to solve! I don’t want to hear this from you! Don’t ruin even the good memories!”

“Senpai…”

The details of Liz and Neide’s school days were their secret, but now they were just good memories.

“Just leave. Meeting you was a mistake. Even if Oscar has another woman…”

Biting her lip, Liz forced out the words.

“I will never come to you.”

Feeling like the sky was falling, Neide lowered his head and left Liz’s room dejectedly.

“Mom! Mom!”

Neide was eight years old.

On their way to the capital to secure a loan, the West family’s carriage was attacked by the Chickenhead Bandits.

The carriage was overturned, and the servants were brutally killed by the bandits.

Neide’s mother, Teria, ran towards the only standing carriage but was soon caught by the bandits.

“Hehehe! Where do you think you’re going?”

The Chickenhead Bandits were half-mad, and the one who grabbed Teria stuck out his pierced tongue.

“What a beauty! You’re mine now!”

“Ahhh!”

Hearing his mother’s terrified scream, Neide charged at the bandits without hesitation.

“Let my mom go!”

“Poor kid.”

They thought a single blow would be enough to deal with an eight-year-old.

But the bandit raising his sword saw blue lightning flying towards his chest and widened his eyes.

“Gah!”

As he was struck and thrown back, everyone’s attention turned to the child.

“Magic? From a kid?”

The coachman shouted from the carriage.

“Get in quickly!”

The carriage had already started moving, and Teria and Neide ran with all their might to catch up.

Teria got in first, but Neide, just a step behind, became the bandits’ prey.

“Mom! Mom!”

From the rapidly moving carriage, Teria’s face turned pale as she watched Neide being captured by the bandits.

“Stop…!”

Her voice was filled with all the anguish of a human soul.

“Stop! Stop!”

“We can’t! There’s no way to rescue him! At least one of us has to survive to call for help!”

“Mommy!”

Neide’s screams echoed through the mountains.

“Sigh.”

In the cold early morning, Neide sat crouched by the roadside, looking up at the Dolphin Inn, his breath visible in the air.

There was no one around, and only the diligent birds in the distant forest were chirping.

“I knew this would happen.”

Oscar still hadn’t returned.

He might tell Liz that the conversation took longer than expected, but Neide, who had seen the scene himself, knew the truth.

It must have been an offer he couldn’t refuse.

Having attended a magic school, he knew how fiercely mages competed and how desperately they sought success.

If the branch manager’s daughter had shown interest, even someone from a non-prestigious school like Oscar would be tempted.

‘That doesn’t matter.’

Everyone has their own priorities.

‘Doesn’t he care about the pain Liz will feel?’

What angered Neide was Oscar’s response.

If success was more important than love, why get engaged and bring Liz here?

‘There’s only one reason.’

As Neide’s eyes grew cold, he saw Oscar approaching in a coat, looking exhausted.

He might complain to Liz, but his carefree demeanor showed no sign of guilt.

“Busy, aren’t you? Running around everywhere.”

Oscar turned at the sudden provocation and widened his eyes when he saw Neide.

“What? Have you been waiting here all night?”

“You look pleased. Did things go well with the branch manager?”

“What nonsense?”

Oscar was initially flustered, but quickly assessed the situation like a mage and his face turned angry.

“Did you follow me? You really are a scumbag. Pathetic.”

“Let’s talk.”

Oscar snorted and turned away.

“Why should I? And whatever misunderstanding you have, I just went to the branch manager’s house for business.”

“If you’re so confident, you won’t mind if I tell the truth?”

“Haha! To Liz? Go ahead. Do you think she’ll believe a jealous fool like you?”

“No. To the press.”

Oscar’s expression hardened.

“If what I saw is accurate, you’ve committed a serious crime as a certified mage, disgracing the kingdom. Having an affair with the branch manager’s daughter to get into the association? What will happen if this gets out?”

Mages are known for their independence, but certified mages serving the state have a code of conduct.

“…What do you want?”

“I told you, let’s talk.”

Oscar checked that no one was around and approached Neide.

“Fine. Lead the way.”

Neide placed a hand on Oscar’s shoulder and cast a teleportation spell, causing a loud noise and a flash as the scenery changed.

They were in a clearing in the forest on the outskirts of Creas, and Oscar sneered as he took in the surroundings.

“Typical scumbag. Did you think bringing me here would scare me? Even a certified mage… Gah!”

Neide’s punch turned Oscar’s head.

“You bastard!”

Oscar’s eyes blazed, but Neide was already gone.

Moving too fast for human reflexes, Neide struggled to maintain control.

‘Grr!’

But as the emotions he had suppressed for this moment were released, a terrifying killing intent began to consume his mind.

“Gah!”

Another unexpected punch knocked Oscar down.

‘Damn! What is this?’

Movement faster than teleportation.

It could be the Flicker magic, a trademark of Unlockers, but if that were the case, Liz would have mentioned it.

“Stop hiding and come out, you coward!”

“Who are you talking to?”

Oscar turned towards the voice, but the punch came from the opposite direction.

As the punch landed on his cheek, Oscar spun and fell to the ground.

All he felt was humiliation.

“Ahhh!”

Finally awakened to his killing intent, Oscar teleported to create distance and armed himself with his specialty, fire magic.

“I’ll kill you!”

He shouted, but with no target to vent his anger on, he was frustrated to the point of madness.

‘Damn! Where is he?’

Even with his Spirit Zone covering a radius of several dozen meters, he couldn’t detect Neide’s presence.

‘Is he farther away?’

But the thought was proven wrong as a straight punch from an unexpected direction sent Oscar rolling on the ground.

“Grr!”

Spitting out blood, he heard the quiet footsteps of a cat approaching.

“Get up.”

Despite the words, what followed was a brutal beating, with Neide stomping on Oscar as if to kill him.

“Grr!”

As Oscar groaned, curled up on the ground, Neide stepped back and then kicked him in the chest.

“Gah!”

Oscar flipped over and twitched, and Neide finally caught his breath, pushing his hair back.

“Get up.”

Being able to control the situation with just this much venting was a magical achievement, but his mood was at an all-time low.

“I said get up. Don’t make me end it here.”

“You… you bastard…”

As Oscar pushed himself up, Neide’s foot struck his jaw.

“Gah!”

“Get up.”

Faced with an impossible command, Oscar’s vision darkened as he crawled to a tree trunk and leaned against it.

“Just hit me. If you want to hit me, go ahead.”

With no way to fight an invisible opponent, there was no point in gauging the difference in their levels.

“Is this your last bit of pride? At least try to fight back.”

“Hmph. You’re just annoyed with me, right? Think of it as stepping in shit and vent your anger. Then get out of my life.”

“I can’t do that. You know I’m a scumbag, right?”

Oscar’s already pained face twisted further.

“Why are you doing this? What do you gain from ruining me? If I break up with Liz, isn’t that good for you?”

“Beg. Go to her and beg. Say you lost your mind, that you won’t do it again, and beg until your hands and feet are worn out.”

Oscar was momentarily stunned.

“You naive fool. What do you gain from this? Do you think your pure-heartedness will work? Women want sweet lies, not insignificant truths.”

“Maybe.”

Neide crouched in front of Oscar.

“If I had played it cool, if I hadn’t cried, if I had told those sweet lies, maybe she would have come to me.”

Neide’s hand tightened around Oscar’s throat.

“Grr!”

“But sometimes, someone is so precious that you can only treat them with sincerity.”

Oscar struggled as his airway was blocked.

"You must have felt that at first. Her breath was sweet, and just meeting her eyes made you feel like you were losing your mind. And yet, you weighed her against your success?"

Neide's face twisted like a monster.

"You should have given up one. In those sweet lies you told to win her heart, was there really no sense of responsibility?"

'This guy is insane.'

Neide's eyes held a supernatural killing intent that no human could contain.

The realization that he might die here made Oscar's mind scramble for an escape.

'Right, I'm a certified mage. This is self-defense.'

His opponent was an uncertified rogue, and he was the one being attacked.

"Alright. Alright, I'll do as you say."

As Neide slowly stepped back, Oscar rubbed his bruised neck and asked one last question.

"Do you really have to go this far? I bet my life on this. Wouldn't it be better for both of us to end it here?"

"That's for Liz to decide. Tell her the truth and beg. You will never get a job at the Magic Association."

"Sigh. This is really frustrating."

Oscar, resigned, lowered his head and touched the ring on his finger behind his back.

'Once he's in my Spirit Zone, it's over.'

A certified mage with a substantial income would carry emergency magical equipment.

"Sure, right now Liz might seem like your whole world. But as you get older, you'll understand. You might not know this as a student, but…"

Oscar extended his fist.

"The adult world is a harsh place."

As the Lightning Thunder spell in his magic ring activated, a bolt of lightning struck Neide's head.
