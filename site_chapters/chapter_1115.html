Chapter 1115: A Grand Dream (3)

Havitz's applause sent chills down the spines of some, making one thing clear:

'He must be killed.'

The ability to read the waves of the heart was, in some ways, more annoying than Vanishing.

With the proposals of the Holy War concluded, it was time for the representatives of each country to speak.

'Tonight is the key.' Assassinations, lobbying, solicitations, and secret deals would all take place unofficially.

"Jive has angels. This power can sustain the world. Every country can prosper." The speech was straightforward.

'Damn, I can't push too hard because of him.'

If they provoked Havitz with high-intensity strategies, they would end up looking foolish.

"Give Gartto to Jive. Of course, if you give us Rom, it will be a strong alliance."

Gis smiled as he spoke.

"Oh, and we politely decline Temica."

No one laughed.

Representatives, mindful of the divine frequency, tried to hide their true intentions as much as possible.

The 12 countries presented their reasons for why they should lead the world.

With the main event of the Holy War, the free speech session, remaining, there was a 20-minute recess.

Gustav IV, Smoto, spoke.

"Everyone is too concerned about Havitz. It doesn't matter what he's thinking. If you're that kind of person, just push through. What are you so afraid of?" Balkan said.

"Humans find beauty in virtue. But some countries strategically avoid standing out."

Zetaro said.

"It's just a probing battle. Once some secret alliances are formed tonight, they'll start throwing punches tomorrow."

"So, how's the balance of power?"

"Hmm."

Balkan read the crowd's energy.

Energy is relative; even if you lock two beasts together, the strong and the weak will be distinguished.

Among the psychological warfare of the 12 countries and hundreds of brains, a special flow was felt.

"In terms of winning rates, roughly, Jive 86%, Kashan 92%, Tormia 78%, Jincheon 66%…"

Zetaro nodded.

"As expected, Havitz is strong. It's unusual for Tormia to be in the top three, though."

"Is it because of Yahweh? Even though he's the strongest mage, he's not a particularly tricky opponent. Havitz doesn't seem to care much either."

Balkan said.

"From an evil standpoint, it's easy. It's like a sucker in a gambling den. The problem is that the sucker's wealth is infinite, so you can't easily backstab them…"

His gaze shifted sideways.

"The important thing is that Tormia isn't third. The place where the crowd's energy gathers the most is…"

He saw Kitra with a snake around his neck.

"The Kingdom of Paras."

Kitra, who had been staring blankly ahead, kissed the snake's flicking tongue.

"Smooch. Smooch."

Slowly turning his head, he met Balkan's eyes and gave a chilling smile.

Smoto asked quietly.

"What's the winning rate?"

"I don't know. It's abnormal. I've never seen a flow like this. If I calculate it numerically, probably…"

Balkan squinted one eye.

"128%?"

Zetaro blinked.

"That's over 100%. Is that even possible?"

"That's why it's strange. Even if you rigged a card game, you wouldn't get such a winning rate. So, the only scenario where you can exceed 100% is…"

Smoto said.

"If the system is rigged."

"Right. Winning rates are calculated under the same rules. But if the system is rigged so that a low hand wins, how would you express it numerically? You'd have to exceed 100%."

Zetaro murmured.

"Hmm. 100% represents the whole, and exceeding the whole is conceptually impossible. But if a new concept that transcends the whole opens up…"

Something clicked in his mind.

"The outside world. Paras must have accessed that secret somehow."

Shirone was thinking the same thing.

'I can't figure out Kitra's intentions. I need to finish exploring the pyramid as soon as possible.'

Currently, contact with everyone in the pyramid, including Canis, Arin, and Zulu, was cut off.

"The meeting will resume. From now on, the chairman's intervention will be minimized for free speech."

The representatives' eyes changed, and Gis of Jive stood up to open the floor.

"Let's end the war. Cooperating with Archangel Satiel here to quickly achieve peace would be a great gain for humanity."

Jingang of the Jincheon Empire said.

"Stopping the war won't end it. The people of Jincheon are suffering from radiation mutations. I don't think angels can solve that problem."

Satiel scoffed.

"Hmph, what do you expect me to do about human weakness? Their bodies change from mere radiation."

Sweat dripped from Gis's forehead, but controlling Satiel was difficult.

Rampa said.

"It seems the Archangel has no intention of coexisting with humanity. If Jive becomes the leadership council, won't it lead to another catastrophe?"

"No, that's not…"

Satiel interrupted Gis.

"Don't worry. I have no intention of tormenting humans like some others. Once I achieve what I want, I'll lead my army and leave."

Munryong of the Kingdom of Mun asked.

"What do you want?"

"It's personal. But if you interfere with my work, disaster will befall you."

Satiel, having said that, looked at Shirone.

'Ikael. Where are you?'

Despite searching the entire planet from above, she couldn't find Ikael.

'She's avoiding me.'

Ikael’s stealth ability was so powerful that even Anke Ra couldn’t detect her.

However, the reason Ikael stayed hidden wasn’t for herself but to conceal Wena Wizard.

After all, they couldn’t openly bring humanity’s hope—someone capable of killing Havitz—into plain sight.

Shirone checked on his other self’s location.

“Two days from now, she’ll be in Jive.”

The problem was that Lampa knew about it.

Training Wizard was part of the Ivory Tower Project, after all.

“Lampa wouldn’t help Satan. But the situation has become delicate.”

The Ivory Tower was stirring with internal discord.

“This was unexpected. Depending on the results of the Ivory Tower, there’s a risk of information leaking.”

This was why Shirone had no choice but to go to the Ivory Tower.

“Hell?”

Havitz’s voice echoed.

“Should I get rid of it?”

All eyes in the council chamber turned to him, and Uorin frowned.

“There he goes again, doing whatever he wants.”

Despite her unease, representatives from the nations couldn’t help but feel a glimmer of hope.

“Is that possible?”

“It’s possible. I’m the Lord of Hell. I can speak to Lethe and purify Hell.”

Pony interjected, “Purifying Hell would destroy your world, wouldn’t it? Are you mocking us?”

“No, I’m serious. Here’s what I realized today—Hell won’t disappear as long as you humans are alive. But I do have one condition. Give me 10% of humanity.”

“Ten percent?”

“It’s better than everyone dying, isn’t it? I’m still Satan, after all—I’ll need materials to rebuild Hell. Once you decide, Hell will vanish entirely.”

This meant liberation from disaster.

However, 10% of humanity, handed over to Satan, would endure such torment that Hell could be rebuilt.

“Are you even listening to yourself?” King Gis shot up from his seat.

“Insulting human dignity has its limits. Do you even realize how large 10% of humanity is? Humanity would never agree to such a decision!”

“Hm.”

God’s frequency resonated.

“Shut up. I’m going to be the king of the world.”

“If you refuse, I can’t help you. I’m just making an offer. How about this, then? If 10% is too much, just give me one person.”

“What? One person?”

Havitz pointed at Shirone.

“Yahweh.”

Even as Uorin felt an overwhelming surge of murderous intent rise within her, Havitz didn’t stop.

“He’ll be enough. If you hand him over, the world will find peace. Don’t you agree?”

“You…”

As Pony, Seriel, and others who shared a bond with Shirone rose from their seats, Satiel spoke up.

“Who do you think you are?”

“Foul, filthy beast. The Nephilim is not someone you can lay your hands on.”

Though Satiel referred to him as a Nephilim, Shirone knew he was being addressed.

“Why?”

Satiel had once driven the biological child of Ikael and Goffin to death.

From a human perspective, it was as if an aunt had sent her nephew to his doom.

“Do you feel guilty?”

Even as Shirone stared at her, Satiel deliberately avoided her gaze.

“Don’t misunderstand. I’m doing what must be done for heaven. Killing you was my pride.”

So why did it feel so unpleasant?

“Maybe…”

In Shirone, Satiel saw both the angel she hated most and the human she loved most.

“No, it’s not guilt. I am Archangel Satiel. If anyone kills you, it will be me.”

She declared, “Satan, you may be able to deceive humans, but you cannot fool angels. If you think you can keep a Nephilim under your thumb, I will crush you with force.”

“Kukuk.”

God’s frequency activated.

“She’s quite something too.”

The day had come when even the stagnant mind of an archangel could sense human resonance.

“There is a way to destroy Hell,” Jingang stated.

“The Jincheon Space Agency has been researching Hell and has developed the technology to achieve visible results. Soon, Hell will disappear—not by angels, but by Jincheon.”

“What’s the method?”

“Photon Fortress.”

It was the spatiotemporal interception weapon used by Princess Jin Seong-eum.

Anchal elaborated, “Photon Fortress warps space. The same applies to the Under World, as proven by Princess Jin Seong-eum. Furthermore, we have the data she left behind.”

Raising her index finger, Anchal projected a hologram of the Photon Fortress within a large sphere.

“With this, we’ve developed a weapon capable of directly striking Hell: the Anti-Demon Photon Fortress. We’ll obliterate Hell by detonating the fortress.”

Havitz blinked.

“Hm.”

The fact that Satan didn’t refute it validated the potential of the Anti-Demon Photon Fortress.

“Listen closely, Havitz,” Jingang said, his eyes bloodshot.

“You’ll pay dearly for what you did to Jincheon. I’ll become an evil beyond you and punish you.”

The weight of those words—spoken by a father who had sent his beloved daughter to Hell—resonated clearly with Havitz.

Shirone understood his feelings.

“He must have endured everything for this moment.”

He felt the weight of countless words Jingang had swallowed just to deliver this one declaration.

“Let’s adjourn for a moment,” the chairperson announced, prompting the representatives and officials to flood out of the chamber.

“Today’s meeting ends here.”

Everyone could sense it.
