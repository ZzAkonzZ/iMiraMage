Chapter 981: The Grand Premise (3)

As Shirone rode the carriage to Alrog Street, he kept his gaze fixed on the scenery outside the window.

Lupist stared intently at Shirone, while Plu, sitting next to him, swallowed nervously.

'This isn't easy.'

Though they were comrades in battle, from now on, they had to speak as representatives of Tormia.

Lupist spoke.

"The Holy War will be reorganized soon."

"...I see."

Shirone didn't turn his head.

"It means the power structure of the Three Sovereign Realms will change. Tormia will monopolize the technology of elemental bombs. This has already been agreed upon with the Supreme Commander of the Holy War."

'Iruki...'

Originally, it was a contract to provide the flower field in exchange for the secrets of the elemental bomb.

"But by protecting Bashka, you've changed the situation slightly. Anyway, if you don't agree, the Supreme Commander won't fulfill the contract."

That was the issue.

Plu clasped her hands together and waited for Shirone's response.

'I can't beg him to do it.'

Though they were close like siblings, emotions couldn't sway matters concerning humanity's safety and the future of their country.

Shirone finally spoke.

"The technology of elemental bombs is possible. Kashan, Jincheon, and Gustav won't be as powerful as before. Are you thinking of pushing one of them out?"

"No."

Lupist leaned forward.

"That was when the balance of power was tight. I intend to elevate Tormia to the position of a leading nation."

It might be ambition, but from Tormia's perspective, they didn't want to miss this opportunity.

"Of course, if it's recognized by the Ivory Tower."

The approval of one of the Five Great Stars of the Integrated Universe Management Department would carry weight with other stars.

"I'll leave the elemental bombs to you. If Iruki decided that way, I won't oppose it."

Though it seemed like an easy acceptance, Plu realized he had quickly drawn a line.

'It means he won't give more power to the kingdom. This is already an achievement...'

It couldn't be helped that it felt lacking.

More importantly, the fairies and dragons under Shirone's command were once powerful forces in the Holy War.

"Sister."

Plu snapped out of her thoughts and looked up to see Shirone smiling.

"I'm sorry."

Plu also smiled and shook her head.

"No, I'm sorry."

Sometimes, focusing on one thing makes you forget what's truly important.

Shirone was Shirone.

Since their first meeting at the Magic Association, he had never disappointed her—a good younger brother.

"Come to think of it, you've grown a lot. It feels like just yesterday you were struggling with Force."

"Haha! And you were so strict back then. Now you're Chief Secretary."

They reminisced endlessly.

"Hmm."

Though not entirely satisfied with the outcome, Lupist closed his eyes without further demands.

"Oh, right! The Five Great Families gathering?"

"Yes, I remember. And when we passed by, you secretly gave us a high five..."

What truly mattered was already in their possession.

The intersections extending from Alrog Square in all four directions were filled with crowds.

"Long live Tormia! Long live Tormia!"

Though it was unusual for a king to change in a day, surprisingly, the citizens weren't too shocked.

The rapid changes amidst complex global affairs seemed to provide a sense of stability.

Most procedures were completed, leaving only Adolf XIII to hand over the crown.

As Pony knelt and bowed her head, Adolf XIII approached with his crown removed.

'I... I...'

Though he thought he had resigned himself, something hot surged from deep within his heart.

'Why me?'

The decades he had waited to become king flashed through his mind like a lantern slide show.

He had finally become king and now had to hand everything over to Pony in just one day—a direct descendant ranked lowest in succession order.

"Your Majesty?"

The crown hovered above Pony's head for a long time and trembled slightly.

"Your Majesty."

The second call from an official carried a hint of menace.

The longer he delayed, the more suspicion would grow among the people—something Lupist didn't want.

Adolf XIII relaxed his grip on the crown, which brushed past Pony's face and fell to the floor. The clear sound echoed through Alrog Street's end in silence.

"Nonsense..."

Adolf XIII clenched his teeth tightly.

"Don't talk nonsense!"

As citizens in the square looked on in shock, he shouted in a fit of rage,

"This is fraud! I am truly noble royalty! Why should I be swayed by these people?"

Shirone's team watched from below with a tense atmosphere.

Nade spoke up,

"He'll end up dead at this rate."

Iruki added,

"This is what they call royal rage. He held out as best as he could but reached his limit here."

Adolf XIII pointed accusingly at Shirone's team,

"Don't be deceived, citizens! They are the ones who dropped bombs over Bashka! You're being fooled!"

From behind the platform where Lupist was observing events closely, he nodded at Plu,

"Take care of it."

It was part of their agreed plan. Plu gripped her phoenix staff tightly and steeled herself for action.

"This crown is mine! The man born to be king, that is me, Adolf... Argh!"

As Adolf XIII tried to place the fallen crown on his head, he suddenly screamed.

Plu, who was about to intervene, stopped in her tracks, and Shirone's eyes filled with confusion.

'What is this?'

At first, he thought it was an ambush, but there was no magic that could deceive his eyes.

"It hurts! Argh! My head, my head...!" Forgetting his royal dignity, Adolf XIII clutched his head and rolled on the ground, then grabbed his chest.

"A blade, a blade! It feels like a blade is piercing me!"

It felt like watching a play rather than reality, leaving the people speechless.

And then, the next moment.

"Aaaah!"

With someone's piercing scream, everyone began to collapse and roll on the ground.

"It hurts! My whole body hurts!"

The moment they instinctively felt something was wrong, the same pain struck Shirone and his companions.

"Ugh!"

Shirone finally realized.

As he inhaled, his throat felt like it was tearing, and his lungs felt like they were on fire.

'This isn't magic. It's something more fundamental...'

A kind of system.

Looking around, he saw that his friends, as well as Miro, Sein, and Kangnan, were all suffering.

While everyone in the square was writhing on the ground, one person stood out.

'Gaold.'

Only Gaold seemed unaffected by the pain, wearing a serious expression.

As the sound of a jet's rupture was heard, Minerva landed in front of Shirone.

Even her face, supporting the Great Star, was slightly contorted with pain.

"You've awakened."

As Shirone approached, the Great Star freed herself from Minerva's support and walked forward.

"Shirone, it's the Demon Realm. Paimon's Demon Realm has opened."

"Paimon?"

Hadn't she been dissolved by the light of Agape?

"She revived by a narrow margin. It's my fault. If I hadn't lost consciousness..."

Shirone shook his head.

"It's not your fault, Great Star. Even if you were conscious, it would have been difficult to detect."

It was a dilemma similar to the angels' "clairvoyance."

"Gather Rian and the others. I'll tell you how to eliminate the Demon Realm."

It was the right thing to do, but currently, all of Shirone's companions were writhing in pain.

"What kind of Demon Realm is this?"

"Paimon's Demon Realm is a hospital. It's a disease unknown to this planet. The name is Emotion Sickness. It's a terrible disease where pain is regulated according to the scale of emotions."

"The scale of emotions?"

The Great Star looked around at the people and explained.

"The human body reacts in special ways according to the mind. Heart rate, brain waves, hormones, everything changes. Emotion Sickness is a disease that lies dormant in the pituitary gland and activates when the body reaches a certain state. Simply put, when you love something, the pain skyrockets, and when that object is removed, it stabilizes."

As she finished speaking, Shirone's contorted face returned to a calm state.

"...I see."

"How did you do it, Shirone?"

"I killed it."

Shirone looked at his friends and said,

"A bit of air." "But this is something only I can do. Because I can love even trivial air with the same weight."

It was the realm of absolute philanthropy.

Even the act of breathing and eliminating air carried the same weight as killing a person for Shirone.

"Someone who lives on an oxygen tank might be able to do the same. But without philanthropy..."

"You can't keep breathing. You have to cut out the air itself from your heart."

"Yes. It's difficult, but for now, kill something. Something you hold dear."

As he spoke, Shirone shuddered.

'I see.'

It was truly a terrible disease.

Meanwhile, his friends, taking a hint from Shirone's words, began to devise their own methods.

"Got it."

The first to resolve it was Iruki.

"What did you kill?"

"The Supreme Commander of the Holy War."

It was a kind of mental murder.

"Oh."

There were many things humans hold dear in life.

Choosing the Supreme Commander of the Holy War was typical of Iruki, but...

'Even if it wasn't Iruki.'

What else could one kill?

Nade and Amy seemed to have the same thought, their eyes filled with determination.

"Whew, I'm done too."

What they abandoned were the positions of head of the Nade Group and commander of the 1st Valkyrie Legion, respectively.

As one by one they escaped the pain, Adolf XIII cried out in tears.

"Argh! Save me! It hurts! It hurts!"

Though not struggling, Pony's pain was no different.

'I can't give up being king.'

Even though she had heard how to alleviate Emotion Sickness, Pony's situation was different from Shirone's friends.

'If I give up being king, Shirone loses a means to lead the country.'

So what should she kill?

"Your Majesty! Relinquish your royal authority! Then the pain will disappear!"

Most officials used Iruki's method.

Honestly, it was easy.

If you had to keep swallowing glass shards instead of air, anyone would give up their career.

But the weight of the crown was different.

"Fine, I give up! I'm not the king anymore! I give up! So please..."

Adolf XIII's eyes widened, and he clutched his throat.

"Argh! It hurts! It hurts even more!"

"Just saying it won't work! You have to truly, truly give it up!"

"Argh!"

Watching the horrific scene, Shirone's group realized another terrifying aspect of Emotion Sickness.

'You have to cut it out completely.'

This meant that as the symptoms of Emotion Sickness progressed, one's personality would inevitably change.

What would be left at the end?

"Ugh!"

Kneeling on one knee, Miro gritted her teeth and endured.

'I won't give up anything.'

She had already abandoned everything except the line, so there was nothing left to give up.

'The pain keeps increasing.'

Every heartbeat felt like her chest was being torn apart, and her lungs were so hot that even taking the next breath was terrifying.

"Miro, give up something!"

Kangnan's voice grew faint, and cold sweat dripped from her forehead.

'This isn't just pain. Being alive itself is hell...'

Miro finally realized.

'This is like...'

A man's face flashed through her mind.

"Is it very painful?"

Looking at the shadow cast on the ground, Miro struggled to lift her head.

The only human unaffected by Emotion Sickness.

No, someone who lived in such terrible pain that he didn't even notice it.

"You..."

Gaold looked at Miro with pity.
