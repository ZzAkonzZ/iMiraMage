Chapter 1089: Reload (3)

"Attack!"

Nearly 4,000 users gathered at the Zeta Plateau fired their weapons simultaneously.

The combined firepower was far stronger than the initial clash of the Solar War.

"Damn it."

Yet, compared to the 72 million durability, it was just a scratch.

"Graaaah!" Dr. Martin's roar sent powerful sound waves that battered the users' machines.

Feeling resistance like facing a strong wind, the rankers charged forward.

"Go! Go! Let's take him down quickly!"

As users approached, Dr. Martin raised his fists and slammed them into the ground.

The noise mixed with the roar exceeded the limit of what sound could contain.

'He's strong.'

Even rankers with powerful armor could be killed in one hit.

'But his pattern is simple.'

Aegis communicated widely.

Based on our analysis, Dr. Martin has three attack patterns: physical, beam, and sound wave attacks.

Though threatening to lower-level users, rankers could avoid them.

The problem was his durability.

"This is insane."

Durability: 71,138,473/72,000,000 Even at full charge, the highest firepower, Dr. Martin didn't even catch fire.

Everyone, fall back!

Rankers, checking the satellite, turned with shocked expressions at Yahweh2's communication.

The light sphere, seen directly, vibrated far more violently than on screen.

'He's condensing photons. About a minute?'

Yahweh2, passing through the rankers, fired Michelangelo at Dr. Martin.

Boom!

Dr. Martin's face, unmoved by grenades, twisted as if it would break.

"What…"

Rankers couldn't believe their eyes.

Durability: 70,605,985/72,000,000 Aegis reported to Yolga's son.

Impact: 532,488. Unbelievable. 133 more hits to complete destruction.

It's like a duel between users. Fermi was astonished.

'The resonance amplitude matches the system's limit. To increase power, the condensation time must exponentially increase. So, one shot per minute.'

As Shirone stepped back, the Operator moved in.

'Collapse.'

As the black sword struck the ground, a black magnetic field expanded explosively, pulling Dr. Martin.

"Insignificant creatures!"

Dr. Martin's upper body, resisting with his hands on the ground, toppled forward.

"Graaaah! Graaaah!" Amidst the noise-laden screams, users fired another volley.

"Surprisingly doable?"

Durability decreased faster than expected, and Yahweh2's minute-interval Michelangelo accelerated the damage.

"We can win! We can do it!"

Finally, as durability fell below 36 million, Dr. Martin's weak spots caught fire.

At that moment, users near the Zeta Plateau began arriving.

"Yay! Finally, our turn!"

Their targets were not Dr. Martin but the fiercely fighting rankers.

Aegis communicated.

Anarchists. Low level but disruptive during the battle.

No need for fancy words. Just troublemakers. Drive them out. They can't reconnect anyway.

Fermi grabbed one troublemaker's head and electrocuted him.

"Aaagh!"

As the struggling machine exploded, Yolga's son brushed off the smoke.

Little Witch approached.

"Working hard."

"Overtime. What's up? Holding a grudge?"

He had shot Little Witch, who had surrendered.

"Of course, I hold grudges. Whether I act on them is my decision. So, what do you think?"

"We should protect it. Think of it as playing hero to save the world."

Little Witch looked at the Operator.

"She's essential for Yahweh2. She can create a program to excavate the apocalypse."

Thus, she was an obstacle.

'Should I kill her?'

If the Operator died, Shirone would inevitably come to Kashan.

Yolga's son exuded killing intent.

"If…"

Uorin interrupted.

"I know. It's a foolish thought. But maybe now is the time to uncover the world's secrets."

From High Gear's perspective, reality was the outside world.

Fermi agreed.

"If you go to the outside world from reality, Argones activates. Confirmed in the apocalypse. But here, and with Yahweh2, we might gain information. Assuming we reach Ultima."

"Will Nane return?"

"Argones didn't activate because Nane had admin authority. But analyzing that, the outside world didn't see Nane as an enemy. If Nane returns to our world, it might all end."

"We must find it."

What Buddha knows, Yahweh must know.

"Yahweh2's foolish actions help, but humanity reaching Ultima is impossible."

"Humans inherently dislike others. When the majority is right, a minority always rebels."

As her back opened, eight guided missiles flew out, trailing tails.

Nearby troublemakers exploded.

"These guys. If only it were this easy in reality…"

"I have a plan. Yahweh2 might not understand, but I'll prevent annihilation."

"Yolga…"

This time, Fermi interrupted.

"No. It's business. If everyone dies, who will we make money from?"

Seeing Fermi smile, it seemed easier to understand him without expressions.

"I'll help."

Little Witch raised a finger.

"If I help, it'll be easier to set the stage. But we share the information gained here."

'Expected.'

Like candy coated in poison, Uorin's system understanding was unmatched.

"I propose too. Yahweh2 is a puppet. I'm the one moving him."

Giving Uorin control over Shirone risked her going berserk.

"Fine. You handle it here."

With the decision made, Fermi asked.

"So, what now? Number Seven seems to be behind this. Can we use him to set the stage?"

"I know his location, but it's useless. Killing him won't make the creature disappear. Yahweh2 will just hate us."

'Do we have to solve it here?'

Aegis communicated.

Anarchists are increasing. About 1,000 now.

A significant number, but expected given the location.

Release the hounds.

Golden Wheel moved, and Uorin's subordinates began hunting the troublemakers.

Meanwhile, the forces fighting in the Solar War desperately pushed Dr. Martin.

'Durability 790,000! Almost there!'

Though 80 users were lost, they fought well against the strongest machine.

Shirone was at the center of that fight.

'One last shot!'

Seeing the rapidly falling durability, Shirone fired Michelangelo.

"Graaaah!"

As flames engulfed Dr. Martin's massive body, he began to fall forward.

"Aaah! Dodge!"

As the enormous mass crashed into the ground, causing an earthquake…

There was a moment of silence.

"We did it. We really did it."

They had completely worn down the 72 million durability.

"We won…!" At that moment, Dr. Martin's head exploded, causing new casualties.

"What? A final pattern?"

Realizing how naive that thought was, everyone understood the gravity of the situation.

Dr. Martin's Revenge. He has revealed his true form. The AI consumed by madness will eliminate users.

From the spot where the head exploded, a machine with 14 legs, resembling a spider, emerged.

Augmented reality displayed the information.

Dr. Martin (Core).

Seeing the level 4,500, users checked the durability in a daze.

Durability: 750,000,000/750,000,000

"750 million…"

A mechanical voice devoid of emotion emanated from the massive machine.

"Exterminate. All. Humanity."

Sirens blared, and a black curtain spread like a net from behind the core.

All of it was grenades, flying at users at incredible speed.

Boom! Boom! Boom! Explosions mushroomed across the vast land as if scattered from the sky.

Half of the users who participated in the Solar War died, and communications flooded in.

Regroup! We fight again!

As the smoke cleared, the sky was filled with countless mechanical creatures.

The sheer number was enough to sap the will to fight from the users.

'It's over.'

As everyone thought that, one user charged at the core.

"Operator!"

She sprinted at supersonic speed, drawing her black sword horizontally and gritting her teeth.

"Number Seven…"

Seeing the 750 million durability, she knew it wasn't just a simple rampage.

"You want to destroy my world?"

She would rather die than see that, and this time, she was serious.

"You bastard!"

Dodging the barrage, she reached the core and increased High Gear's output, swinging her sword.

"Think I'll let someone like you take it?"

No matter how many times she swung the black sword, the core didn't care and continued scraping the ground with beams.

The satellite screen showed users being overwhelmed by the creatures. Even rankers were helpless.

"Die! Die!"

As she swung her sword in rage, a shadow loomed over her.

The core's leg aimed at her and began to descend with all its might.

The Operator raised her black sword.

"Yaaah!"

As metal met metal, the sheer mass made her mind reel.

The blade shattered like glass.

'Damn it.'

As the massive leg was about to crush her…

The core's leg suddenly stopped, and the Operator couldn't believe her eyes.

"H-how…?"

Yahweh2 had arrived beside her, holding up the core's weight with his bare hands.

"The system is broken."

As Dr. Martin's core fully emerged into this world, it damaged the system.

Thus, it was shattered.

"Then…"

Shirone could also reach the unique signal at the end of this world.

'Void.'

As Yahweh2's eyes widened, the core roared and slowly lifted its leg.

"Get out quickly!"

The Operator, regaining her senses, leaped away, and Shirone pushed off the ground, escaping the radius.

As the ground shook with a thud, Shirone soared high into the sky.

'There is no fixed form.'

As the light sphere condensed in Michelangelo grew exponentially, two suns appeared in the sky.

As the Operator looked up, Shirone vertically slammed the flash down.

"Kiaaaah!"

The signal from the core's system sounded like a machine screaming.

Durability: 660,989,764/750,000,000

'Power: 89 million?'

All 14 legs bent, and the body was crushed to the ground.

Durability: 420,818,812/750,000,000

'Additional damage. Crushed by force.'

As the noise subsided, Fermi, still calm, looked at the core.

"Void."

No doubt, since he could do it in reality.

"Now then…"

In every city of High Gear, countless creatures were ravaging users.

"From now on, it's uncharted territory."

Ultima System.
