Chapter 818: The World They Live In (2)

The border area between Kashan and Jive.

In the middle of the sun-drenched desert, a man in priestly robes was reading a book.

Under a black fedora, curly black hair fell like a curtain over his face.

Sitting on a rock with his long legs bent at right angles like a stork, he looked up at the sun and exhaled.

"Haah."

Dry smoke billowed out.

"Coming again?"

Lowering his gaze to the horizon, he saw an old man with a black umbrella approaching through the heat haze.

"Still holding onto the Bible?"

As if placed there by a god, the old man was suddenly in front of him.

"A life without mercy is like an endless desert."

"You abandoned God, didn't you?"

The old man added,

"To become a god." With a snap, the Bible closed.

"What brings you here?"

"The Lord is looking for you." The priest's name was Nike, known as the strongest Almas in history.

"The weather is nice."

With grayish skin unlike typical vampires, he looked up at the sun.

"...Are you alright?"

Vampires are weak to ultraviolet light, but their inherent rapid regeneration allows them to endure it for a while.

An Almas can typically endure for about four hours, but their activity level decreases.

"Haah."

As Nike's brown skin burned and he exhaled hot steam, his skin quickly regenerated.

'He's a monstrous man.'

His regeneration ability was ten times stronger than other Almas, almost reaching the level of a Lord.

"Just because you can endure it doesn't mean it's not painful."

Nike's lips stretched into a long slit as he entered a biting state, with his gums receding to reveal his fangs.

"There is no immortality without pain."

Biting, the act of sinking fangs into a target, allows for suction and injection.

Suction is the act of drawing blood, while injection involves injecting prions to make the target obedient.

"What mission is it this time? Hunting hybrids?"

One-tenth of the half-demons worldwide had been captured and subjected to cruel experiments by Nike.

"Jinma has arrived."

For the first time, Nike's gaze turned to the old man.

"He said he would eliminate all vampire weaknesses and take over this world. We are currently collecting blood to awaken the two remaining Lords."

"I see. What do I need to do?"

"Come to Jive. We will find you."

As the old man's face transformed into a grotesque bat's face, he quickly burrowed into the ground.

Nike looked towards Jive.

"The promised land."

When Rian arrived at the security headquarters, Benoff was waiting impatiently.

"What happened last night? You don't look well."

"You don't look too good either, Captain. You've heard about the blood bank incident, right?"

"Don't get me started. The Shinjang completely fooled us. Even the chief didn't know."

"I was at the scene."

"You were?"

Rian recounted the events of the previous night.

"What? You met a hunter?"

"Yes. Someone who can distinguish vampires. That's why they were always ahead of us."

"Where are they now?"

Rian shook his head.

"Well, they operate without authorization. So, what are you planning to do? Are you joining them?"

"I'm an outsider. I don't want to get involved in the royal palace's affairs. And personally..."

It was an opportunity to uncover Smiling's secret.

"I can't stop you if that's your decision. But let's exchange information occasionally."

Surprisingly, Benoff was very agreeable.

"What are you thinking?"

"Actually... I understand how you feel. It seems there's an internal spy in the royal palace."

Benoff made a discreet proposal.

"Soon, the royal palace will hire hunters from around the world. To catch hidden rats, you need to release the cats."

"The ones I know won't participate. They..."

"No, that's not what I mean. The hunters you know can locate vampires, right?"

Rian caught on.

"Under the right conditions."

"The royal palace is holding interviews to hire hunters. High-ranking officials will attend. It's the perfect chance to identify the spy. If you help with this, I'll overlook your hunters' activities."

It was beneficial for both sides, and Rian knew Xenia would likely agree.

"I'll talk to them. But you must ensure their safety. They won't act directly."

"I understand. I just need to know who the spy is. Only you and I will know, so it won't leak."

After parting with Benoff, Rian carefully made his way to the Genocide hideout.

As people who had slept in the morning began to wake up, Rian gathered everyone.

"...This proposal came in." Grain stroked his chin.

"An internal spy. If there's a vampire in the royal palace, it complicates things."

Facet said,

"It feels like they're watching us, but it's related to our work too, right?"

Most seemed to agree, but the final decision was up to the Silver Born, Xenia.

"I don't trust anyone."

Her colleagues, knowing her personality, remained silent.

"Shall I be honest? The fact that Rian revealed our work bothers me."

Katain defended,

"They already know about hunters. And Silver Born. He only mentioned meeting one."

"But he said the Silver Born is here!"

Fowler said,

"He didn't say where. Xenia, that's unreasonable. We're also struggling to find the vampire hideout. The royal palace publicly announced they're hiring hunters. This might be our breakthrough."

Xenia remained firm.

"I know it's a good proposal. But this is my issue. It's emotional."

Rian said,

"I apologize if it upset you. I'm not forcing you. If you want, I'll cut ties with them."

"Don't treat me like a child! I said it's a good proposal! It's just, I..."

"Yes, it's scary."

Xenia was neither human nor vampire.

"What?"

"You're afraid of joining forces with people you don't trust. But you don't have to."

"Hmph, easy for you to say. What if they use me? What if I end up fighting as their pawn?"

"Then I'll cut them down."

Everyone looked at Rian.

"Whether it's the King of Jive, the Shinjang, or the security team, if they break their promise, I'll fight for you."

Xenia couldn't understand Rian.

"How can you say that so easily? You're human too. You'd kill humans for me?"

"If you think while holding a sword, it's too late. The thinking must be done beforehand."

It was Rian’s philosophy.

"With a sharp blade, you can't achieve love or sacrifice. You take responsibility for every death you cause. I've already made that decision."

"You are..."

As a vampire hunter, Xenia had caused many deaths.

Perhaps she had ignored those deaths.

But Rian said he would take responsibility.

"You said you have someone you really want to protect. What about them? What if you become a fugitive for life? Will you still fight for me?"

"Don't make me say it twice."

He had already made that decision.

"That's my conviction."

It felt so distant that Xenia couldn't find a reason to argue further.

"I don't trust humans."

"Xenia."

As Facet was about to intervene, Xenia raised her head.

"But since you said you'd take responsibility, I'll help this time. You must stay by my side."

"Alright."

As Rian nodded, Xenia turned with a pout.

"I'm hungry. Let's eat."

Grain's lips curled into a slight smile.

'You've met a good person, Xenia.'

Everyone has painful memories, but her suffering as a half-demon was longer than most.

'Maybe Rian can take responsibility for her cursed life.'

No, only Rian could.

The Genocide team roamed Rodenin's streets every night, searching for vampire hideouts.

They captured a few Vesica vampires, but even they had no information about their superiors.

"Damn! They're dodging us like rats!"

In the medical room, Grain conducted cruel experiments to learn everything about vampires.

"Argh! I'll kill you!"

Xenia's expression remained indifferent as she inhaled Blood Q, but her companions watched her closely.

'Did Xenia...'

Did she scream like that too?

"It won't work."

Apparently not thinking about it, Xenia stood up.

"We have to join forces with the humans."

"You're still debating? We already agreed. The hunter interview is tomorrow."

"Being ordered and doing it voluntarily are different. Rian, are you ready?"

Rian handed her a hood.

"You'll wear this. There won't be an identity check, and we'll watch from the highest point."

It was the limit of what Benoff's authority could do, but with Rian by her side, she had to trust him.

Facet saw them off.

"Be careful. There's no mission today, so you can have a nice meal afterward."

"Do you think this is a vacation?"

"But it's just the two of you. Enjoy your time together."

Xenia didn't seem to mind Facet's whisper and turned without answering.

"Haha, she's cute."

On the way to the royal palace for the hunter interview, Rian asked the hooded Xenia,

"Are you okay?"

"I can manage. The sun isn't too strong today."

Even so, she regularly inhaled Blood Q when out of sight.

"They're here."

She sensed it as soon as they arrived at the royal palace.

"How many?"

Xenia rolled her eyes.

"Not many. Three or four. But they're all Almas. Can you handle it? You won't last against this many."

"Who says I can't?"

Rian had given his all in every fight in Rodenin, but it was by human standards.

His essence was Yacha.

If they intended to devastate the area, even Almas couldn't stop Xenia.

After a formal inspection by Benoff, the two entered the colosseum with their weapons.

Their position was where the guards were stationed, while the royal palace officials sat across from them.

"Do you see them?"

"Yes, two."

Xenia inhaled from the Blood Q again.

"Over there, the young secretary. He's a vampire. And the old man in the VIP seat. He's also a vampire."

They naturally kept to the shadows.

"His Majesty is coming out!"

King Meyer of Jive stepped onto the platform.

The royal guard 'Shinjang' stood behind him in their unique stance, legs together and torsos twisted 45 degrees.

'They're all strong.'

Meyer proclaimed,

"As the capital of the kingdom is overrun with demonic forces, we have summoned capable hunters from all over. We hope you will fully demonstrate your abilities and contribute to the peace of Jive."

The iron gate rose, and about 100 hunters entered the center of the colosseum.

As Rian observed them, he heard Xenia's heavy breathing and turned to her.

"What's wrong?"

Her face, hidden under the hood, was filled with shock.

'That guy.'

The person who caught her eye was a priest with a hawk-like appearance and black hair down to his shoulders.

He had two iron rods coiled around his shoulders and a cross tattooed under his chin.

"Nike..."

Xenia's teeth ground together.

He was the enemy who killed her parents and the one who had thrown her into the laboratory.
