Chapter 1001: Infinity (2)

Borshua Region, Kingdom of Akros.

As Shirone walked along the rugged mountain path, he suddenly stopped.

"Huff! Huff!"

Nade, who was climbing the slope with his tongue out like a dog, turned around, leaning on the ground.

"What's wrong? Are you tired?"

"It's gone."

"Huh?"

Iruki, who was leading, turned around and sat down on the ground.

"Let's take a break. What's going on?"

"One of the simultaneous events has disappeared. Or maybe I just can't sense it anymore."

"Where?"

"In Ymir's mind. To be precise, the moment I was engulfed by Goffin's Hand of God, my presence vanished."

"Goffin?"

It wasn't the real Goffin, but explaining it all would take too long.

"Anyway, the signal is cut off. The quantum signal isn't entangled with me here."

Nade tilted his head.

"Is that possible? Quantum signals transcend time and space. How can it be blocked?"

"It can't. At least not in this universe. The only possibility is..."

Shirone rested his chin on his hand.

"The me in Ymir's mind and the me here have become completely different people."

Nade's face turned pale.

"Could it be... that you died?"

"No, I don't think so. I'm still alive here."

His friends indirectly understood that Shirone's life was connected.

Iruki said, "If you didn't die, is it something like an ascension in rank? But you're the Infinite Mage."

"Even if that's the case, the quantum signals should overlap. So the remaining possibility is..."

Shirone stopped speaking.

'The outer world.'

If information from outside the universe had combined, even the Ultima System could be blocked.

'What happened?'

He wanted to check immediately, but the Shirone in Ymir's mind didn't seem to think so.

"Nothing will change. Let's proceed as planned. We'll find out once the mission there is completed."

As Iruki stood up, the three of them resumed climbing the steep mountain path.

"This is exhausting. I don't even know where we are anymore. Isn't this a waste of time?"

Iruki tapped his head.

"According to the map in my head, the demon army didn't pass through here. Naturally, the path is rough."

As they reached the summit at sunset, Shirone's group wrinkled their noses in disgust.

"Ugh!"

A terrible stench filled the air.

"The smell must be trapped in this valley. The air quality is different. What on earth..."

Iruki looked down the mountain but couldn't see anything due to the clouds.

"Let's go."

The descent was quicker, and they soon reached the middle of the mountain.

"What is this..."

They saw the fence of a small village with about 100 households.

The sign at the village entrance was broken and unreadable due to dried blood.

"Let's go in."

Amidst the sound of creaking windows, they saw a corpse lying on the ground.

"Hoo!"

The corpse, covered in maggots, was too decomposed to interest even scavengers.

Iruki, picking up a stick to examine the corpse, said, "The head was smashed with a blunt object."

"Demons?"

"Judging by the distance, it's too far. And the buildings are too intact for it to be the work of demons."

"Could it be the aftermath of the emotional plague?"

Shirone shook his head.

"It's not impossible, but the emotional plague spreads southwest from Tormia. This is the northeast, the opposite direction."

Iruki threw the stick aside.

"Let's take a look around. There might be survivors."

As they moved towards the village center, they saw more corpses, all with their heads smashed by blunt objects.

"Shirone, over here."

Nade pointed to a wall with the words "I am the king" written in blood.

Below the wall sat the corpse of a man with both wrists severed, looking dejected.

"Shirone, do you recognize him?"

"No. It's been 22 years, so I can't tell just by looking at the face. If I at least knew his name..."

Nade searched the man's clothes.

"There's something." He pulled out a letter.

"Can you read it?"

"Yes. Shirone, give me some light."

As Nade approached, Shirone and Iruki leaned in to read the letter together.

-My dear, something is wrong. I can't bear this anxiety. That woman, yes, the one who never reveals her name—she claims she has no name—since she came, everything has gone mad.

"That woman?" Listening to Nade's voice, Shirone continued reading.

-Remember, my dear. Have we ever met anyone from outside the village since we were born and raised here? That wicked woman deceived the village chief with her cunning words and ruined everything. And eventually... she sacrificed our child.

Shirone felt uneasy thinking about the man's severed hands.

-I have a confession. When I was young, I met someone from outside the village. I was gathering herbs and got lost after injuring my leg. They saved me with the miraculous power of Yor. They taught me about the heart of love.

Iruki thought, 'A member of the Yor sect. Did they meet during missionary work?'

-The reason I kept it a secret... you know how much the villagers fear the outside. But I know this world is filled with the love of God.

'Fear of the outside.'

Shirone's eyes deepened as he read the letter.

-That woman! That wicked, vicious woman! She's definitely a demon! It's terrifying that the villagers believe her words. It's like they're dreaming with their eyes open.

'A form of hypnosis?'

That would be the easiest explanation, but Shirone felt he was missing something.

'No, it's not that.'

-I'm going to follow the village chief to the holy land of God. Maybe I can wake the people from their dream. Only I can do this.

The woman continued writing.

-I'm sick of fighting with you. It's the villagers who are crazy, not me. Look at our newborn son. Take Leas and run away. If you don't believe me, we'll lose our son.

Nade read the name at the end.

"Your wife, Rabica."

Shirone searched the records in Omega's library within his 5D cube.

'There are so many.'

There were many Rabicas in the universe, including humans from other civilizations.

But the only species that could surpass the speed of light barrier was the Teraphos.

'It's different when considering individuals.'

Shirone set the region and year within the physically possible range.

'Just one. It's her.'

The information only went up to when she was seven years old, so she would be 29 now.

"This is Kashiram Village. It was built 230 years ago by war refugees. They've been self-sufficient and isolated from the outside for a long time."

Iruki said, "There are too few corpses for the number of buildings. They must have gone to the holy land mentioned in the letter."

Nade, flying into the sky, pointed ahead.

"Shirone, Iruki. Let's go that way."

Following Nade's flight, they saw a completely collapsed building.

It was the largest building, and it seemed to have burned down, with all the materials turned to charcoal.

"The village hall. Why did they burn it?"

Shirone, casting Hand of God, grabbed the building with a giant hand of light.

Lifting a portion, the horrific scene inside the village hall was revealed.

Charred corpses lay with their jaws wide open and bodies twisted.

"There was a fight here."

Shirone, placing the debris down, examined the stone wall near the auditorium.

"The building collapsed as it burned. There might be something that didn't burn."

As Hand of God lifted the wall with its thumb and index finger, a wooden notice board was revealed.

On the left, the village chief had written:

-Worship. As the messenger of God, I declare that by burning the place of return, you can enter the holy land of God.

On the right, there was an announcement:

-We will burn the village hall at 5 PM. Everyone must attend. Those who have reasons or cannot attend will face God's punishment. Do not bring food. There will be a ceremony to atone for everyone's sins with the blood of a pure child.

Nade said, "Something... seems normal but isn't."

"That's called madness. Anyway, let's find this holy land of God."

Shirone's group searched the village. The scene, decorated with corpses of murder and suicide, was a test of courage.

Reaching the area with warehouse buildings, Shirone pointed across the watermill.

"There are signs of an explosion."

A massive pile of rocks was visible below the cliff.

"They must have sealed the entrance."

It was a mystery how they had the power to blow up the entrance after living in isolation for 200 years.

Iruki said, "Judging by the state of the corpses, it's been at least three weeks. Whatever happened, it's already over."

"We'll find out when we get there."

As Hand of God moved a large rock, a gap big enough for a person to enter appeared.

Entering, they saw a winding path lined with sharp stalactites.

"Hello! Is anyone there?"

Only Nade's voice echoed back.

After some time, crawling through the narrow entrance, Iruki asked, "Why did they seal the entrance? It's not something people could escape from."

"Maybe to prevent escape?"

"But the village chief went in too. And they were told not to bring food."

Many thoughts crossed their minds, but they couldn't know without checking.

From the narrow passage ahead, they heard a sound.

"Clack. Clack."

"Shh."

As Nade signaled, Shirone dimmed the Shining Magic.

'Infrared vision.'

Activating Miracle Stream, Shirone's group saw the darkness peel away into a faint green hue.

'What the...'

Three people, crouched like mice, had their faces buried in the belly of a dead body, eating something.

"Give me the fingers."

Their tone was so casual that it sounded like they were asking for the salt.

"Chomp. Chomp."

Even trying to ignore it, the sound reconstructed the scene in their minds, and Nade couldn't hold back any longer.

"What are you doing here?"

As the three people around the corpse looked up, their eyes glowed in the infrared vision.

"Who are you? Who?"

They couldn't see Shirone's group.

"Answer quickly! What are you doing, locking the villagers in the cave?"

"Aaah! Demons, demons!"

Despite not being able to see, they ran away with astonishing speed.

"They must know the terrain. Should we chase them?" Nade asked, raising his lightning magic, but Shirone shook his head.

"No. Let's examine this first."

The corpse was severely mutilated, with most of the flesh torn off.

"I feel like throwing up."

Unlike Nade, Iruki remained calm.

"At least they had food. This way, they could survive for over three weeks. The question is why."

As they simultaneously stared into the depths of the cave, cold anger filled their eyes.

"Let's chase the chief. We need to save the survivors first."

Shirone, having cast the Shining magic, raced through the cave, which was as bright as broad daylight.

Descending almost vertically into a pit, they saw an iron cage trapping survivors.

About 20 people were confined there, and among them, a young woman clung to the bars and asked,

"Who are you?"

Seeing her normal eyes, Shirone was certain.

"Ravika."

The woman, momentarily shocked, quickly composed herself and shouted,

"Capture the chief! We need to kill him before that woman comes! He is a demon!"

While Nade and Iruki guarded the rear, Shirone walked down a side path.

"Hehehe, hehehe."

From around the corner, even before turning it, came disturbing laughter and a foul smell.

Swearing burst from Nade's mouth,

"Son of a bitch."

The chief, buried among more than twenty corpses, lifted his arms with a rapturous expression.

Long things dangled all around him.

"Welcome to the sanctuary of God."
