Chapter 809: Age of Chaos (1)

Abella gritted her teeth but couldn't help but groan from the pain radiating from her heart.

"Ugh..."

Everyone watching her, even the Gustav Four Knights who prided themselves on understanding Havitz...

"Why? Why?"

They couldn't comprehend the current situation.

"Your Majesty."

They hadn't experienced Satan's 0.666 seconds, but even if they had realized it, nothing would have changed.

"Huff, huff."

Havitz, hiding behind Abella's body as a shield, had bloodshot eyes.

"Huff, huff."

His breathing was so rough that his nostrils flared, and his tear-filled eyes were full of sorrow.

"Dar...ling..."

With a clinking sound, Law-Killer shattered, and Abella's eyes rolled upward.

"It's broken."

Balkan, who had been staring blankly at Abella's fallen form, slowly lifted his gaze.

'Havitz, you broke Law-Killer.'

Havitz, still breathing heavily, was only mourning the death of the woman he loved.

As the door to Ex Machina opened, the smell of blood wafted out with the steam.

"Quick! Move quickly!"

The medical team waiting outside carried the unconscious Iruki on a stretcher.

Agaya looked around Ex Machina.

"Phew, it's useless now."

Iruki had forcibly twisted the law, causing the computational devices to melt from the heat.

'But we gained a more powerful brain.'

Watching Iruki being carried away, Agaya prayed silently.

'If only he can wake up.'

As the commotion settled, Guido, who had been smoking heavily, stomped the ground.

"Damn it!"

"What happened? Did we fail?"

The special forces commander of Kashan asked, but the card players remained silent.

After a long pause, Ness spoke.

"Law-Killer did something unpredictable."

This was the only fact they could infer from the current outcome.

"He killed the woman he loved."

If Havitz had used Abella as a shield simply because he didn't want to die, Law-Killer would have twisted even that law and pierced Havitz's heart.

"He didn't kill to survive." Mice spoke in a trembling voice, still in disbelief.

"He killed for fun."

He did it simply because it seemed more entertaining.

"Is there anything closer to chaos than betraying the woman you love most at the critical moment?"

Silence fell again.

"So, what happens now?"

No one answered the special forces commander's question, but the same thought crossed everyone's mind.

'Gaold defeated the Buddha. But the era of humanity is coming to an end in a short history.'

It was truly the age of chaos.

"Huff, huff."

Havitz was crying.

"Abella, Abella." Because she was the woman he truly loved, for the first time in his life, he questioned his final choice.

'Who am I?'

Dying in place of Abella wouldn't have been difficult.

'But why did I have to do this?'

Patricide is common in the world, but the basis of that act is always hatred.

'Because I truly loved her.'

Abandoning someone you loved with all your heart without a moment's hesitation is something no one can do.

'Something no one can do.'

When you do such a thing, what kind of emotions will you feel? What exactly is that feeling?

"Heh, heh heh."

He was so curious it drove him mad.

"Hahaha! Yes, that's it! This feeling! This pleasure that no drug can give!"

The reason he didn't accept Satan's offer was only one.

"This is really fun!"

Because he was already Satan.

'Awakening.'

Balkan realized, seeing the purple haze rising from Havitz's body.

"Uoooooh!"

Havitz's eyes widened as the giant figure of Satan shot up into the sky.

If there is a concept that allows you to decisively kill the person you love, it is chaos.

And humans call it extreme evil.

"What is that?"

Guy, who was almost sitting with his lower body bent, hurriedly looked towards the inner castle.

Shirone and Minerva were also watching the same thing.

"My God..."

The form of extreme evil, made entirely of demonic energy, seemed to engulf Bashken.

Even the madman Guy realized the current state was unusual and sheathed his sword.

"If we meet again on the battlefield, I will kill you without mercy."

Shirone didn't respond, and Guy, as if he didn't expect an answer, passed through the castle gate and ran towards Havitz.

"What will you do?"

Minerva asked.

"We failed."

The appearance of Satan, the king outside the law, indicated that Law-Killer had been broken.

"It's my responsibility."

The world would now be filled with a great evil that neither philanthropy nor extreme good could handle.

"No, it's our responsibility."

"They said half-hearted good incites evil."

The counterattack of good that could be executed because the Buddha was absent.

'Should we not have provoked it?'

The situation Mahoro Amanta worried about was the emergence of the strongest extreme evil, breaking through good.

'It's really difficult. How can everyone in this world...'

How can everyone be happy?

"Shirone, let's go back. We need to come up with a plan."

The area of good that they had managed to expand a little over a year would rapidly shrink.

"What about attacking Havitz now?"

The thought that they hadn't succeeded kept coming back.

"Don't be hasty. We barely managed to stop the holy war. If you try something, they'll respond with something."

If only I were human and everyone else were just puppets, the world would turn as I wished...

"This is not a world you can change alone. Unless the power of good is concentrated into one, extreme evil cannot be destroyed."

Thus, the world falls into suffering.

"From today, the Cotria Republic will disappear. But there are still dozens of countries we need to protect."

Shirone, biting his lip until it bled, followed Minerva and flew into the sky.

"Human desire. It truly knows no end."

Havitz's incarnation, having become extreme evil while still in the flesh, proclaimed from the end of the sky.

"From now on, you are the Satan of this era. Lead my army of hell. The 72 legions will follow you."

To the voice that shook the entire Cotria Kingdom, some felt ecstasy, others despair.

As the purple haze seeped into Havitz, his eyes scanned with a gleam like shimmering oil.

"What happened?" Balkan approached.

"I don't know. He's mumbling something."

Havitz didn't care, but leading the 72 legions of demons seemed like fun.

"Abella has returned."

Death was like a mother's voice calling you to come in for dinner.

"Night is coming."

So, the playground pranks would end someday.

"After all, we are abandoned children."

Children not sought by their parents continue to run around even after dusk falls.

"One last thing..."

Havitz looked at his friends.

"Let's conquer the world once before we go."

Balkan's eyes filled with mischief, and Natasha grinned excitedly.

"Great! No one goes home first!"

The Gustav Empire, which had destroyed the Cotria Republic, began a full-scale world war.

The neighboring country of Kashan lost a tenth of its territory, and the Middle Eastern kingdom of Paras suffered a fatal blow.

"We need to break through the Akkad Desert. That way, we can take over dozens of kingdoms in the central continent."

Across the sea, a fierce naval battle was raging with the Jincheon Empire.

"The sea is not easy. First, occupy the Hui and Zhong kingdoms. Cut off the support routes of the Seven Kingdoms."

The kingdoms of Hui and Zhong occupied the waist of the eastern continent.

"What should we do with the occupied countries?"

At the official's question, Balkan looked at Havitz.

"What should we do?"

"Play with them for a while and then kill them all. No human other than the Gustav Empire should set foot in this world."

It was our playground and also the ultimate goal of the demons.

"By the way, there's something that bothers me."

Balkan looked down at the world map.

A red line starting from the northwest region of Kashan ran vertically down to the central continent.

"Along this line, all the demon armies were annihilated. As if erased by an eraser."

Natasha's eyes gleamed.

"The Knight of Maha."

The number of demons he killed while heading south exceeded ten thousand, including two legion commanders.

"I thought it was a childish goal of a knight in training, but he really drew a straight line through Kashan."

Smoto was indifferent.

"There's no need to worry. He's not coming our way, and how much can one person erase anyway?"

The red line drawn on the map was indeed thin.

"The problem is the legion commanders. We lost the 69th and 57th legion commanders. Ordinary soldiers can be replenished quickly, but losing a legion commander is a big loss."

Natasha pointed to herself.

"Should I go?"

Balkan didn't want that.

'I can't imagine Natasha being killed by someone. But this line is really unpleasant.'

If it's hard, you detour, and if it's dangerous, you run away. That's the trajectory an ordinary person would draw.

'There are no signs of hesitation.'

He just broke through.

"For now, let's keep an eye on it. Have the kids monitor the movements of the Knight of Maha."

It was spring, and blood-red flowers were blooming.

"You... a mere human dares...!"

The demons of the 55th Legion, who ruled the southwestern desert of Kashan, were dead, covering the sand.

"This is not the end. A billion demons will surely find you and tear you to pieces..."

"How tiresome, always the same words."

A young man with blue hair grown to his shoulders and a beard like fur strapped a greatsword to his back.

"Do you guys memorize these lines or something?"

The massive body of the 55th Legion Commander, Powell, split from the crown of his head with a chilling sound.

"Is this the end of the desert?"

The blue-haired man closed one eye and looked at the horizon where a city stood.

The Knight of Maha, Ogent Rian (22 years old).

"So, I've finally arrived."

He had never seen a map.

He just walked south following the stars, eating whatever he could find when hungry and digging into the ground to sleep when cold.

'Walking eventually leads to arrival.'

He had defeated many strong enemies but never felt like he had truly gained strength.

"Can splitting the sky be considered strength?"

As he swung his greatsword vertically, an illusion of the world splitting appeared before his eyes.

He didn't know.

"Kido, how about you? Have you made any progress?"

He wondered where his first companion in the quest for enlightenment was and what he was doing now.

"Do your best. I'll go ahead."

With the certainty that Kido hadn't given up, Rian walked towards the visible city.

"I should wash up. People might mistake me for a monster."

It was his first encounter with civilization in a year, but the smell of demon blood was too strong.

'If I've come to the right place, this should be the Kingdom of Jaive.'

The Seven Star Kingdom of the Holy War.

All he knew was that it had long rainy seasons and a school that produced the Buddha of the era, Nane.

"I hope I can meet Shirone."

Rian didn't know.

That a race ruling the night of the world was roaming the streets of Jaive, thirsting for blood.
