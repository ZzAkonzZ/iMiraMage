Chapter 345: Divine Transcendance (2)

"Form a defensive square!"

Forty soldiers quickly formed a circle, understanding that in a situation where the enemy couldn't be identified, preventing isolation and defeat was paramount.

Kuan finally spotted the enemy.

About 20 meters away, a dark curtain shimmered before white masks suddenly flipped open, revealing their wearers.

"What is this?"

Figures wearing owl-like masks swirled around a point before forming a triangular formation.

Shing!

Thirty soldiers drew their swords in unison, but the sound was singular. The sight of them standing in the same pose, aiming diagonally, brought back information about a particular group Kuan had learned about during commander training.

"Could it be..."

The Wind Guards, the most formidable swordsmanship group known.

'Why are the Wind Guards here?'

The Wind Guards were the imperial guards of Empress Teraje of the Kashan Empire. Their presence meant only one thing: the cult leader Kointira was under Teraje’s protection.

"What are you plotting, Kashan?"

"You'll find out in the afterlife..."

The Wind Guards' signature simultaneous strike was launched.

"You will know then."

"Argh!"

A soldier from the second company screamed. Even Kuan felt the tremendous murderous intent. No ordinary soldier, even a veteran, could stand against such a force.

"Damn!"

Kuan dashed forward, and the Wind Guards dispersed like the wind, only to regroup rapidly. Dozens of swords created thousands of afterimages as Kuan's movements seemed to defy gravity.

'Damn. How many centers of gravity are there?'

The skill of the Wind Guards was astonishing. They were humans who seemed to implement fluid dynamics with their bodies. Losing all sense of direction, it felt like fighting in zero gravity.

But what truly surprised was the Wind Guards.

'It’s taking too long. How many swings have there been?'

'Around 23,000.'

It was rare for anyone to withstand over 1,000 strikes from the Wind Guards. Essentially, they could grind down any being in under a second.

'How playful can God be?'

If the world were perfect, all creatures would be happy as lesser beings. However, nature always creates a 0.1% mutation. That 0.1% makes the rest of the 99.9% seem trivial.

'A being born from chance... specially chosen.'

That was the Wind Guards' impression of Kuan.

Technique, artistry, physical balance, the ability to manipulate gravity, the thought of creating new paths.

'This man is a genius.'

As soon as the thought crossed their minds, the Wind Guards expanded like wings, then regrouped as a fierce wind swirling around Kuan.

'To kill a genius.'

The Wind Guards' blades occupied all space. They were so fast it almost felt slow.

"Charge! Charge!"

At that moment, the company members rushed in. Upon entering the Wind Guards' radius, they were shredded like meat caught in machinery. Kuan's escape from the sword net was almost instinctual.

'This is madness...!'

The oldest member of the company, an old man named Kovira, passed by Kuan without a chance for a farewell, but his smiling eyes conveyed a thousand words.

'Survive, Captain.'

Watching his company being slaughtered in an instant, Kuan understood why they had to die.

'I see. At least one must escape.'

It was necessary to inform the higher-ups about Kashan's involvement. Otherwise, more search teams would fall into the Wind Guards' trap.

'This is the best course. If anyone should survive, I have the highest chance.'

As Kuan propelled himself away with external gravity, he coldly watched the swirling black storm in the darkness. He hadn’t even shared a day or a look with the soldiers, yet they had all departed from this world.

'Why...'

He felt something hot inside him. A massive flame had ignited from a place he thought had been extinguished to ashes.

"Why the hell! Damn it all!"

As Kuan exploded with external gravity, the Wind Guards, like feathers, regrouped in a triangular formation 20 meters away.

"None of you think about returning alive."

The Wind Guards did not respond. Their minds were racing with the unexpected situation.

'The mission has failed.'

Or, it almost did.

If Kuan had decided to flee, the Wind Guards would not have been able to catch him. The trivial lives of forty had created an unprecedented precedent against the mighty Wind Guards.

The leader of the Wind Guards slowly raised a hand and removed her mask, revealing a woman's face with narrow, sharp eyes beneath thin, sparse eyebrows.

"This is the first time a living being has seen the face of the Wind Guards."

"So what? Expecting me to be grateful?"

"Join the Wind Guards. We will lead you."

For Kuan, recognized by the world as a genius, this was an extraordinary proposition. Especially since they were directly under Teraje of the Tri-Emperor. Accepting would mean a meteoric rise to the highest echelons available to a swordsman.

Kuan finally sheathed his sword and slung it over his shoulder.

"I refuse."

The woman's head tilted slightly. Which swordsman would reject an offer from the Wind Guards? Clearly, he had lost touch with reality from his subordinates' deaths.

"It's unfortunate about your subordinates. But a new future awaits you. Don't bury your talent in the past."

Kuan scoffed. No matter how great the Wind Guards were, there can't be two under heaven.

"Don't be mistaken. My men did their duty as soldiers. The foolish ones are those commanders who can't control their emotions."

"Then why refuse?"

Kuan's sword once again targeted the woman's brow.

"Ever wondered how embarrassing it is? Claiming to be the strongest while you mob up to slash?"

"Then it's a breakdown."

At the same time as the woman put on her owl mask, the thirty Wind Guards dispersed like wind and swooped towards Kuan.

'Wait. I'll take at least one with me.'

How could he face the underworld without even a companion to accompany him if he deserted the expectations of his subordinates?

Kuan spread external gravity all around as he dove into the storm created by the Wind Guards. The movements created by the masters of external gravity were almost like an art form beyond combat.

'I see. The answer was surprisingly simple.'

Kuan leaped over the wall. He was able to do it because he hadn’t intended to survive. This time, even the Wind Guards had to tense up. The forest shook with the eerie sound of dozens of swords.

'Is a sword this beautiful?'

At the height of ecstasy, Kuan's sword split the path. A Wind Guard's head detached from its body, and simultaneously another's heart ceased as it was pierced.

'Two. That should be enough to pay for the meal.'

Whoosh!

As the Wind Guards realized the death of their comrades, the turbulence exploded like a storm. The simultaneous death of two members was a very rare event in the history of the Wind Guards.

Kuan calmly observed as their fury whirled like a hot wind. However, they didn't kill him. Instead, they sliced off his Achilles tendon like a sliver of fish flesh.

"Argh!"

Kuan screamed, not from pain but because everything that made him who he was, including his recent enlightenment, had vanished with the 30 grams of flesh that had been stripped away.

The nickname "Sorcerer of the Battlefield" had come from his miraculous skill with the sword, which could take an opponent's neck in any situation, but it was also backed by phenomenal agility and mobility.

Without the Achilles tendon, there was no genius. He was no longer the Sorcerer of the Battlefield.

"Ugh..."

Kuan couldn't stand. He couldn’t even try. Yet, he reached out and crawled up the slope. The eerie sound of the Wind Guards swirling around him echoed. The voice of 28 men uttering a single syllable each would be unforgettable for a lifetime.

You’ve become a mere bug.

Kuan kept crawling. Death was not frightening because he had lost everything. But he couldn’t give up on life after abandoning the 40 members of his unit.

Struggle like a bug.

"Argh! Shut up!"

Bark like a dog.

"Kill! Just kill!"

Confess to your master. Tell them the Wind Guards have spared you. Wag your tail and snitch.

As the Wind Guards' wind grew fiercer, the sound of flames crackling could be heard. To Kuan, it was as if the world was burning.

Burying his face in the ground, he clenched his fists. He could no longer endure. He couldn’t prevent his life's beliefs from plummeting into the abyss.

"Argh!"

Kuan’s scream echoed in the now silent Ghost Forest.

"..."

Kuan opened his eyes. His face was still expressionless, but sweat dotted his forehead.

"Damn."

He felt as if he was pinned down, unable to move a finger. This always happened after dreaming of the Wind Guards. He had to lie still like a corpse for 10 minutes, just as on the day 10 years ago when his Achilles tendon was severed.

After barely escaping the Ghost Forest, Kuan lost consciousness and was urgently transported back to Thormia.

He reported that Teraje was protecting the Dark Order, but as expected, it had no impact on the political situation.

The search battalion surrounded the mountain range for about a month in a stagnant local war before disbanding.

There seemed to be some sort of agreement with the Kashan Empire, but that was a matter for the politicians, not something Kuan was involved in.

Kuan could not become a hero. The event of that day vanished into the annals of history without even a mention of the Wind Guards.

The military had offered him a position as a cavalry battalion commander—a two-rank special promotion—but it was clearly intended to silence him.

Kuan chose to discharge. His mind was already too shattered to wield a sword.

For a while, he lived like a hermit. He couldn't even dream of suicide as he thought of his subordinates, nor did he have any proper supporters.

Then one day, at the suggestion of his only friend, Kiyo Elis, he took a position at the Kaizen School of Swordsmanship.

'I can move now.'

Kuan climbed out of bed, limping to the window. Pulling back the curtain, the morning sun shone on his old desk.

Numerous papers were scattered about.

"Psychological Views on the Phenomenon of Divine Transcendence," "Incarnation Techniques," "The Correlation between Will and Phenomena"—though the titles varied, the themes were consistent.

In the corner of the desk were notes Kuan had scribbled with his own thoughts, which he now swept away with his hand.

Despite gathering all material on divine transcendence, he still hadn't reached it. Let’s assume for a moment that it’s possible to move the body with the power of will. But what exactly is will? He was still limping. No matter how hard he tried to walk properly, it just didn’t work.

'Ogent Rian.'

Who could have predicted?

That a boy with no apparent talent would achieve what Kuan, recognized as a genius, could not.

'I hope it’s not just becoming a fool.'

Kuan shook his head. He knew the truth. His sword was already broken. The moment his Achilles tendon snapped, the god of swords had also turned his gaze away from him.

'Another year has ended.'

Today was the last day of the second half at the school of swordsmanship. The subject Kuan was in charge of was wrestling, among the combat evaluations.

Even if Rian could perform divine transcendence, he couldn’t withstand the enhanced strength of Schema users.

It was probably going to be another miserable day for him, but he was curious about the half-year's achievements nonetheless.

Tess entered the locker room with the female cadets in her group.

Wrestling required close physical contact, but since there was no distinction between men and women in the swordsmanship school, everyone had to remove their tops during evaluations.

Women were allowed to wear tank tops, however.

Tess struggled to put on the tank top. Not only was her frame large, but her ample bust meant she almost had to cram her breasts in by force.
