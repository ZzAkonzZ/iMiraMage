Chapter 237: Battle Simulation (7)

To establish the Halo, Shirone needed to infinitely expand the Immortal Function.

However, since he had already grasped the concept of a perfect circle, he could simplify the mechanism from the second attempt onward.

A Lifting target is highly sensitive to changes in mental state. Therefore, if the mind remains in absolute stability, the target should not move.

This meant that it would soon be possible to draw a perfect circle without entering the realm of infinity.

From that point on, instead of regular Lifting training, Shirone focused on keeping the target completely still.

After a month, he started seeing some results.

It was always fascinating to see a target, with maximum repulsive force, floating calmly within the Spirit Zone.

This was something only Shirone could achieve, having grasped the sense of a perfect circle through the Immortal Function.

The students kept expressing their admiration, but they couldn't understand why Shirone was practicing this way.

Closer said.

"He’s been doing that since last month, and yet he can't even hold it for 10 seconds."

Savina added a word of disapproval.

"He's not a monk, so why go that far? How does this even help with magic?"

The target floating above Shirone showed no signs of movement. This meant that his mental state was completely undisturbed.

It was akin to a brain-dead state, a mental condition that a normal human couldn't maintain. Only Shirone, who had reached the state of Indestructible Diamond, could manage it.

However, even Shirone couldn't hold out for more than 10 seconds. Eventually, the target began to shake violently and flew out of the Spirit Zone.

Dante was the first to speak.

“It seems to have gotten a little bigger….”

"Huh? What’s gotten bigger?"

“His Spirit Zone. It seems bigger than last time.”

Closer and Savina looked at Shirone again. Even with their eyes, it was clear that it had grown larger than before.

“No way. If it were that easy to grow Spirit Zone, there wouldn’t be anyone in the world who isn’t archmage. Usually, you have to go through ascetism or gain some enlightenment... Wait, enlightenment?"

Savina realized from her own words that while mage do undergo awakenings, the Spirit Zone, being a pure mental entity, is more closely related to a state of spiritual pursuit.

This was why Ethella, a bishop of the Karsys Holy Order, was also renowned as a Zoner throughout the kingdom.

Savina's prediction was correct.

Having reached the state of Indestructible Diamond, Shirone had broken through the limits of expanding his mind. In the meantime, as he continued to spin in perfect circles, his Spirit Zone was strengthening at an incredible speed.

Even as Closer and Savina continued to spew out belittling remarks as if they could not accept it, Dante silently looked at Shirone.

@

The time for the Heaven’s Trial class had come again.

The three teachers, Ethella, Sienna, and Thad, were in charge of the class, and now the students accepted it as a given.

Sienna took the lead and led the class.

"Starting today, we will be learning about interpersonal combat. It differs significantly from tactical training, so please follow the instructions carefully."

Interpersonal combat had many differences from tactical training.

First of all, because powerful magic exchanges occurred, the gauge depleted much faster. In other words, the intensity of anti-magic would increase.

Moreover, the problem was that a "push" judgment could also apply to people, but only in the case of passive skills.

A typical example of an accident was when two mages who had cast teleportation collided with each other midway.

If that happened, no matter how virtual reality it was, it would inevitably lead to serious injuries.

"The reason why three instructors are supervising this lesson is due to the risks associated with interpersonal combat. First of all, since today is the first day, we will start with the practical exercises. If there are any volunteers..."

"I'll do it."

Boyle, who had been waiting for this day, raised his hand.

"Very well. As for Boyle's opponent..."

"Teacher, may I choose my opponent as well?"

Sienna was about to refuse, thinking it wasn't ideal to let personal feelings dictate the choice, but Boyle was already eyeing his target.

Dante pointed at himself in disbelief.

“Me? You want to go against meright now?”

"That's right. You've been acting all high and mighty, so you won't back down now, will you? If you're really that great, prove it with your skills."

If he came out like this, Dante can do nothing about it, but being number one in the kingdom isn't a position that anyone, whether dog or cow, would challenge as they like it.

"Are you so desperate to beat me? No matter what?"

“Don’t get me wrong. I'm the rightful top student in the advanced class, so the challenge is yours. So why don’t you come up? I'll straighten out that rotten attitude of yours."

“Hahaha! Great words! How about this then? The loser has to kneel and admit defeat to the winner."

Ethella interjected.

“Dante, this is strictly part of the class….”

"It's fine. Please allow it, Teacher Ethella. In fact, this is exactly what I wanted."

Boyle asked with an earnest look in his eyes. The memory of getting beaten by Closer was a lifelong humiliation. Rather, he was grateful for the opportunity to redeem himself.

As the duel was set, Shirone became curious about how Dante would handle Boyle. There was no attack magic in the photon system. Of course, Dante would have learned other types of magic, but he had clearly said that he was Shining Boy, the mage of light.

While Shirone was lost in thought, Boyle and Dante waited for the showdown at Heaven’s Trial, about 50 meters apart.

In interpersonal combat, early distance plays a significant role.

Once the battle began, countless variables would come into play, but the start was always with both opponents facing each other, so deploying a sequence magic for a preemptive strike was key.

Of course, summoning mages has a different disposition from other mages, so they has more freedom in initial tactical maneuvers.

The standard strategy for a summoning mage was to forgo a preemptive strike and summon their familiar as quickly as possible to defend against the opponent's attack.

Aware of this, Dante spoke as if making a proclamation.

"Go ahead and summon your familiar first; let's start this leisurely. I'm not the type to make a fuss right from the beginning either."

Boyle found it hard to believe Dante’s words.

However, it didn't seem like Dante was lying just to leave room for complaints before the fight even began.

In any case, it was an advantage for summoning mages who had no choice but to be defensive in the beginning.

"If that's what you want, go ahead. I'll stick to my own style."

It was a response that allowed Boyle to avoid being swayed by mind games while also shackling Dante to his own words.

Dante didn't lose his composure, but he scratched his eyebrows awkwardly. He had thought Boyle was a sheltered flower, but it turned out he could think for himself.

'Summoning magic. That's definitely tricky.'

Summoning mages can summon creatures through a unique and difficult magic called Yearning (상사/相思/Sangsa).

The first entity that a summoning mage learns to summon is themselves.

This is referred to as a doppelgänger, and once this is successful, the doppelgänger has the potential to transform into a third entity.

Summoning something means you have a complete understanding of that entity.

This is the essence of Yearning, which involves three stages.

The first is the Engraving Stage, where the mage uses all five senses to observe the entity they intend to summon, imprinting the image in their mind.

Since the summoner must perfectly remember the appearance of the summoned creature, summoning mages possess observational skills that far exceed those of ordinary people.

A representative exercise for this is called the Arrangement Method.

This involves mixing 30 sticks that differ in length by only one millimeter each and arranging them in order of length within one minute.

The second stage is called the Empathy Stage, where the mage studies the behavior of the entity to gain complete confidence in their understanding.

Although some mages can do this with their vast knowledge, most mages tend to develop this understanding as they live together.

If any living creature spends more than a year in an isolated place, its characteristics are bound to be imprinted in your mind.

The simpler the creature's intelligence, the easier it is to grasp and clear this stage.

The third stage is the Extinction Stage, the most crucial part.

In this stage, the mage kills the imprinted entity, erasing its physical existence.

However, if the entity appears in reality afterward, the summoning mage can manifest it through the doppelgänger.

Killing the entity is not always necessary. The key is to create a state where they can 'never be seen again'.

The more this state is reinforced, the more vivid the summoned familiar becomes.

It was a gruesome feeling to kill an entity with which you had built a deep connection over many years, but keeping it alive was also risky.

For instance, there was a case where a female mage hesitated during the Extinction Stage when trying to summon Opoi, a monster that could cast healing magic.

In the end, she managed to clear the Extinction Stage by selling Opoi to a monster collector. However, five years later, she heard through some channels that Opoi was still alive, and as a result, she lost her ability to summon Opoi.

Summoned familiars are categorized from 10th tier to 1st tier based on the level of Yearning achieved. According to the Magic Association's research, there are 13,872 types of familiars in the lowest 10th tier, but only three are listed in the 1st tier.

It is a well-known fact that one of the 1st tier familiars is the Lich, the archmage of the undead monster world.

The fact that this summoned beast was controlled by a woman named Zulu, an archmage from the Middle East, spread and turned the academic world upside down.

The reason this caused such a stir is that to clear the Extinction Stage, the existence of the summoned entity must be erased, but a Lich is a monster that has lost the ability to die.

Some speculated that Zulu herself became the Lich and merely summoned a doppelgänger, but unless she spoke out, no one could know the truth.

Boyle said, clearing his throat haughtily.

"Last semester, I was the second-best in the advanced class, just barely. Do you know why I deliberately didn't move up to the graduation class and stayed here? Because I thought I was still lacking."

"Yeah, that seems about right."

“But not anymore. I’ve put in four years of hard work, and I finally succeeded over this break. My familiar will tear you apart."

"I look forward to it. Honestly, Merces was a bit of a letdown."

Boyle's lips split open. At this moment, he was neither a child in his mother's arms nor a model student who valued principles, but a summoning mage.

"Don't worry. This time will be different."

As Sienna shouted to start the battle, Boyle and Dante entered their Spirit Zone at the same time.

As the sequence method unfolds, their minds were strengthened, and the Spirit Zones took on their respective formations.

Boyle’s formation was defensive, while Dante’s was a targeting type.

"Come forth, Gaos."

Where Boyle reached out, another Boyle was born. Following the profound mystery of a mage's doppelgänger transforming into the doppelgänger of a summoned familiar, it then transformed into the 6th-tier familiar, Gaos.

Gwaaaaaaaaa!

A monstrous creature, standing 3 meters tall, assumed a quadrupedal stance. Its smooth, hairless skin was a bluish color, and its muscles were split like rocks.

The body was similar to a human's, but it had no eyes, as if it was wearing an eyepatch, and its entire face from the bridge of its nose to its jaw was just one large mouth.

Inside, sparsely spaced fangs were entwined with straw-like tongues, and along its spine, from the neck down, protruded tentacles that emitted electricity.

Its hands had four fingers, with 10-centimeter-long nails as hard as diamonds.

“Ga- Gaos. He really summoned Gaos.”

The students were stunned. They had heard that Boyle had been training hard over the break, but they never imagined it would be to summon Gaos.

Gaos is among the top-tier of the 6th-tier familiars, and in terms of physical abilities alone, it could even compete with some 5th-tier monsters.

The teachers were just as shocked.

Although there were no summoning mages in the current graduating class, very few students in the academy's history had ever managed to summon a 6th-tier familiar before graduation.

"My goodness. When did he learn summoning magic like that? The time constraints over the break should have made that impossible.”

Thad smiled bitterly at Ethella's words.

"He must have been planning this for a long time. That kid has been preparing for this all along."

The cunning of the younger generation was enough to make one shake their head in disbelief.

Whether in the advanced or graduation classes, mages are generally weak in physical strength. By summoning Gaos, a creature with physical abilities around the 5th tier, Boil had a much easier path to passing the graduation exam.
