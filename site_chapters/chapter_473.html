Chapter 473:

Plu, with a fractured left upper arm, couldn't move easily.

If the Dawkins Algorithm activated in this situation, her broken arm would flail uncontrollably, and the pain would directly translate into a loss of combat effectiveness.

Not everyone could have the extreme endurance of Gaold.

"Ugh!"

Every time Raun approached, Plu had to widen the distance, which severely hurt her pride.

Especially the murky desire in his eyes made her even more uncomfortable.

"Heh, just as I thought."

Raun clenched his fists.

Clang! Clang!

Electricity once again sparked from the area near the Piper's spine, gathering in his fists.

No matter what tricks she tried, she wouldn't be able to dodge two Electric Shockwaves.

"You've never bowed to anyone, have you? You'd rather break than lose, training and honing your mind countless times with that resolve."

Plu glared at Raun without answering.

With the Dawkins Algorithm sealed, her only option was to watch his every move closely.

"I know that competitive spirit made you who you are. But do you know there are beings on a different level? I've gained immortality and will continue to live this life forever."

The mechanical sound from the Piper's translation device grated on Plu's nerves.

She felt she could do anything if it meant crushing his jaw.

"What are you trying to say?"

"The point is, I don't dislike women like you."

Raun spread his clenched fists apart.

"I'll give you a choice. Become my woman. You don't have to worry about being discarded. I'll cherish you until you grow old and die. Of course, in my own way."

As Raun approached confidently, Plu clutched her injured arm and stepped back.

But her eyes had grown cold and calm.

For those who had gained immortality, regret and remorse didn't exist.

They could always start over and had eternal time to make up for any mistakes.

"Now I understand."

Plu bared her fangs, lifting her upper lip.

"You're not a human living an immortal life."

"Hmph, the jealousy of mortals…"

"You're already dead."

Raun stopped talking and lowered his eyelids.

"You're already a dead human. You're missing the most essential thing for a human."

"Missing? Me? What am I lacking?"

"A heart."

Plu swung Phoenix with her right arm, taking a combat stance.

"You have no heart. With nothing to give, nothing matters to you. Your body is just a wooden log, and the slight stimuli running through it are all you have."

Crackle!

Electricity surged from Raun's fists.

He finally realized that he shouldn't have entertained the madness of mortals.

"I'll kill you. I'll show you what true death is."

As soon as he finished speaking, Raun lunged.

'Dawkins Algorithm.'

Ignoring her injury, Plu activated the Spirit Zone again.

Raun's attacks were more violent and persistent than before, but Plu's movements were now unrestrained.

Even if her broken arm flailed uncontrollably or her spine broke, she didn't care.

What mattered to her was not the future but this moment.

That's why she could fight with everything she had.

Raun's attacks, synchronized with the machine, were still precise, but Plu charged with her life on the line.

As time passed, Raun's face hardened.

No matter how much he attacked, Plu's momentum only grew, reaching a point where her life was in danger.

The height of her resolve. When she surpassed that threshold, Raun finally realized.

He could die.

"This… this…!"

Feelings he never wanted to acknowledge pierced Raun's heart.

Fear. And a sense of defeat.

Neither was something he could handle.

"Aaaargh!"

He discharged all the electricity from his fists into the ground, spreading powerful currents in all directions.

Plu didn't care and lunged, her citizens' clothes burning away to reveal her bare skin.

'Oh, come on… again?'

Plu gritted her teeth.

Her skin was red and blistered from first-degree burns, but the electric currents shaking her muscles were worse.

'Ugh!'

Even as her body refused to obey, she cast a spell with sheer willpower and swung Phoenix at Raun.

'Phoenix Strike!'

Screeeech!

As the fiery phoenix, glowing like molten lava, shot out, Raun raised his arms in terror.

His mind went blank, unable to react.

As the immense flames surged, Raun's eyes widened in horror.

More terrifying than pain was death.

Having lived for a thousand years and expecting countless more, the fear of his life ending at this moment was more terrifying than anything.

"No, nooooo!"

Raun screamed.

His scream was the most pitiful cry a human could make.

Plu looked at Raun with calm, indifferent eyes, showing no hint of sympathy.

He was burning.

His flesh charred, leaving only the metallic frame of the Piper.

In the end, he would transform into another substance and be absorbed by another life.

That was life.

Thud. Thud.

Plu turned away from Raun's charred corpse.

But the wounds inflicted by Raun were severe, and she collapsed as soon as she stepped out of the building, her body exposed.

"Haa…"

Immortals were strong, but they had corresponding weaknesses. And Shirone would surely understand those weaknesses.

'There's nothing I can do. I'll have to wait until he comes to help.'

Plu looked down at her burned thigh and pouted.

It couldn't be helped during a battle, but as a senior, her pride was severely wounded.

"Eek!"

Deina, with a terrified expression, kept retreating.

The sight of the tendrils' blades aiming for her neck made her heart shrink in fear.

She was going to die.

Her life, which had promised an eternal future, was about to end here.

Shirone was no longer intimidated.

No matter how skilled they were in incarnation techniques, they had infinitely more to lose in death than mortals.

That's why he could charge forward.

As Photon Cannons spread in all directions, Murka and Deina could only retreat.

The fear of death overwhelmed them, and finally, Murka, the greatest warrior of the Kergos, screamed.

"Aaaargh!"

Shirone seized the momentum and activated Akamai.

As the Antithesis took effect, Murka couldn't move a finger.

The strong figure who had overcome the power that restrained even fallen angels with divine transcendence was gone.

Incarnation was the entirety of a human's being, and with a broken spirit, it was impossible to break free from the Antithesis's restraint.

"S-Save me! Please!"

Murka shouted into the empty air, unable to even turn his head.

His terrified eyes lost focus, the black and white blending like a liquid.

Without hesitation, Shirone leaped in and swung the tendrils, the sharp metal slicing through Murka's neck.

"Save…"

Thud. Thud, thud, thud.

His head rolled on the ground, stopping at Deina's feet.

"Eek!"

She saw death.

Deina lost the will to fight and collapsed.

Meanwhile, Arman absorbed Murka's body, restoring Shirone's strength.

Completely absorbed.

What was a natural fact for living beings was an unbearable reality for Deina, striking her mind.

Long ago, she had also been part of the cycle of eating and being eaten.

But this time, she was the prey, not the predator.

"Please, spare me…"

As Shirone approached, Deina pleaded in a trembling voice.

Seeing Shirone's cold eyes, she quickly knelt and begged, rubbing her hands together.

"Please, just spare my life. I'll do anything you say. I'll crawl on the ground, I'll pass between your legs."

Shirone's expression remained unchanged.

Only the four tendrils rose high into the sky, following his emotions.

How many mortals had she killed for fun, just because she had gained immortality?

It was anger, and he had no intention of stopping.

"I don't want to die. Please, spare me. I'm begging you."

Deina, who had lived for a thousand years, cried like a newborn, rubbing her hands together.

Death would be a shock to any living being, but for someone who had escaped the chain of life, the desire to live was incomparably greater.

Shirone's tendrils shot into the air and then hooked down on Deina.

"Aaaargh!"

Her piercing scream echoed as the tendrils' blades pinned her to the ground.

"Knowing how precious your life is…"

Shirone bit his lip and spoke.

"Why do you treat others' lives so carelessly?"

"Ugh, ugh."

Deina was no longer in her right mind.

Her completely broken will was reflected in her incarnation.

Unlike Shirone, who had reached his state by overcoming death, her incarnation technique, gained through gradual self-realization in immortality, was fragile like glass.

"I was wrong. Please, spare me, master. I'll follow you forever."

With her incarnation shattered, her self-esteem collapsed.

She had fallen to the most pitiful state a human could reach.

Only then did Shirone retract the tendrils and speak.

"Get up."

He didn't set subjective standards for saving or killing people.

But he also didn't believe in objective standards.

Human life couldn't be measured by any standard.

So the reason Shirone spared Deina was simply a rational decision that she was still needed.

"Get up. We need to find Senpai."

"Yes, I'll find her, master. Leave it to me."

Shirone led Deina out of the building.

The immortals they had faced this time were qualitatively different from other hunting units, and judging by the scale of the destruction, it was easy to locate where Plu had fought.

"Senpai!"

Shirone ran towards Plu, who was leaning against the building wall.

Her exposed skin was covered in red burns.

But more serious was her broken arm.

"Are you okay? How did this happen?"

Shirone glanced at Raun's corpse inside the building.

He must have been an immortal, just like the ones Shirone had faced.

Even in the Vajra Armor state with Arman, Shirone had struggled initially, so he could only attribute Plu's victory to her extreme endurance.

Plu, not wanting to show weakness, stood up.

"I won't die from this. But who is she?"

Shirone looked at Deina and asked.

"Can you heal Senpai's wounds?"

Deina, on the verge of tears, shook her head.

"I don't have healing magic. I'm sorry, master."

"Master?"

Plu raised an eyebrow.

It was a long story, so Shirone cut to the chase.

"First, we should find some clothes."

Plu let out a long sigh.

"Master!"

At that moment, Deina turned around and shouted.

Simultaneously, an unknown force completely crushed the three of them.

"Ugh! This is…"

It wasn't the power of the Nor.

As they looked ahead with puzzled expressions, a group of fairies appeared beyond the wall.

Mirka, the Fairy of Awe, ranked second among the 72 classes of fairies, looked down at them with an arrogant expression.

Her elaborate makeup and large wings indicated her high status, but that wasn't what mattered to Shirone at the moment.

"Feope?"

Feope, standing beside Mirka with a sad expression, had her head bowed.
