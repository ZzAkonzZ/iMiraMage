Chapter 599:

Shirone’s consciousness was enveloped in light.

It was so bright that he could keep his eyes open.

‘Where is this…?’

It felt as if his body had disappeared, leaving only his mind, and he didn’t feel like he was standing.

He tried to raise his hand but saw nothing. He could look anywhere but saw nothing.

In the minus one second of 12:00 AM in the upper echelons of Istas, where even time was lost, Shirone finally faced Goffin.

“Hexa.”

When he turned his mind towards the voice, a shimmering shadow appeared in the distance.

As it approached, its form became clearer, but waves of light distorted its clarity, preventing further approach.

‘Is he really human?’

Shirone couldn’t be sure.

The black silhouette had a face and limbs, but its body was elongated like a stick due to the distortion of light, and some parts seemed thin and broken.

“Are you Goffin?”

Goffin didn’t answer, and Shirone grew anxious, fearing he might disappear at any moment.

“Answer me! You owe me an explanation! Who am I? Am I Ikael’s child?”

“Hexa. You are the result.”

Shirone didn’t want to be called Hexa.

“Speak clearly. I’m very angry right now.”

After a pause, Goffin’s voice continued.

“You have no cause.”

“What…”

How could there be an existence without a cause?

“You are the result. An absolute coincidence. Therefore, you are free, and therefore, you can fight.”

Even if Shirone couldn’t accept Goffin’s words as truth, he felt devastated.

“Are you saying I have no parents and no reason to be born?”

Even a speck of dust in the universe would have more meaning than this.

“Answer me! Are you saying Ikael is not my mother?”

“She was once your mother.”

An inexplicable rage surged within him.

“Stop dodging and speak clearly! Come out here! Don’t hide there, show yourself!”

“I am in front of you. I am not in front of you.”

Was Goffin insane?

They were speaking the same language, but Shirone couldn’t understand a word.

“Don’t analyze, just accept. Accept it as a whole and become one. That is Ultima.”

In a moment of realization, Shirone understood.

“Erasure. You don’t exist right now.”

Because he didn’t exist, he couldn’t be called anything, and Shirone couldn’t place him in front of him.

“I don’t ask for much. I just want to understand. Who am I? How can I know?”

“When you know everything, you will know everything.”

It wasn’t wordplay or evasion.

To Goffin, who transcended human thought, this was the most certain answer.

“When I know everything…”

In human terms, it meant reaching the end of truth.

“How can I meet you?”

“Beyond infinity.”

It was the last phrase left in the Gaia records at the ruins of Heaven.

“Beyond… infinity?”

Shirone struggled to continue the conversation with Goffin, but this was beyond his understanding.

“How is that possible?”

Anyone who knew what infinity was would think the same.

“It means there’s no end, right? How can you go beyond that?”

“Why do you think there’s no end?”

“Isn’t it obvious? Numbers go on endlessly…”

“Have you ever reached the end of numbers?”

Shirone fell silent. Of course, he hadn’t, but that’s why it’s called infinity.

“Infinity is just the largest concept humans can conceive. Look at the ultimate. Go beyond infinity at the end of infinity.”

Shirone asked again.

“Are you saying there’s an end to numbers?”

“There is.”

Goffin’s answer was firm.

“And beyond that, I exist.”

Goffin’s silhouette began to shrink.

“Wait! I haven’t heard anything yet.”

“I have told you everything. This is the last of my existence.”

Once the upper echelons closed, Goffin would be unfindable anywhere in this world.

“I will wait at the ultimate.”

As Goffin shrank to a point and disappeared, a massive storm of light surged in.

Though it carried no external force, Shirone’s mind was swept away like a wave and began to disintegrate into the smallest units.

“Wait. Wait…”

As the light faded, he saw the back of his hand reaching out to Goffin, and then his eyes closed.

“Shirone! Are you okay? Shirone!”

Anchal’s slaps brought Shirone back to consciousness.

“Gasp!”

It felt like his senses were being reassembled after being disintegrated.

“Where am I?”

“Outside Istas. You ran out the door. I followed you and found you collapsed.”

As his senses stabilized, Shirone’s eyes half-closed.

“Did you meet Goffin?”

Anchal asked just before he fell asleep.

This was also crucial information about the upper echelons, but Shirone wasn’t sure.

‘Did I meet Goffin?’

He remembered the conversation in the light, but it felt so unreal that he was convinced it was a dream.

“I don’t know. But this much is certain.”

A tear fell from his half-closed eyelid.

“I have… no roots.”

Shirone fell into a deep sleep again.

“So, you’re leaving?”

Vice Principal Olivia sipped her tea as she faced Anchal, who had come with packed bags.

“I’ve obtained all the information I wanted. I need to report to His Majesty the Emperor. And to Valkyrie. Then all the nations of the Tri-Imperial and Seven Kings will know.”

Olivia wondered if Anchal would really hand over such secrets to another country but had no interest in politics.

“Anyway, it’s good for you too. With the upper echelons closed, there’s no need to shut down Istas.”

“Yes, it’s good news for the graduating class. One strong competitor is gone.”

“Of course, I’m a pro, but…”

Anchal reflected on her time at the school.

“The current graduating class is quite strong. Dante, Canis, and Fermi too.”

Names etched in Olivia’s mind.

“Why did you tell me? Weren’t the events in the upper echelons top secret?”

Anchal chuckled.

“You don’t trust me. I will share the information with Valkyrie. It’s too big for one empire to hold. And…”

Anchal thought of Shirone, who had woken up that morning.

“Even if I keep quiet, the person involved in the events attends this school.”

“True…”

The secrets of the upper echelons Anchal revealed were astonishing.

“Yolga cast a ripple in Miro’s heart, and that ripple gave Shirone the choice to leave Miro’s space-time. Are we just living in predetermined time?”

“You can’t be certain. I think it’s not that time is fixed, but that we have the conviction to make the same choices.”

Like Yolga, who moved forward knowing she would die.

“Conviction.”

Anchal slung her backpack over her shoulder and stood up.

“I have to go. Time is short. Give my regards to Shirone.”

“Will this affect Shirone negatively?”

“I can’t guarantee it. I’m just a part of the empire. But…”

After a moment’s thought, Anchal spoke.

“I’ll do my best as the director of the Jincheon Astronomical Bureau. Tell him to contact me if he needs help.”

“Will do.”

Having a direct line to the director of a secretive bureau in the Jincheon Empire was an unimaginable network for an ordinary person.

“Well then…”

“Wait. One more thing.”

Olivia called Anchal as she headed for the door.

“Giving Shirone a choice means Miro remembers the events in the upper echelons, right? How is that possible?”

After Goffin’s erasure, Miro should have forgotten about Yolga’s death.

“I’m not sure. Miro is strong enough to be called transcendent. But I also think Yolga’s death didn’t just leave a memory; it changed Miro as a person.”

“Hmm, that could be.”

Anchal shrugged.

“It’s just a theory. Only Miro knows the truth.”

“Right.”

Only Miro would know.

“Paré!”

In the basement of the Marika Hotel in the Kingdom of Roblang, a drinking party was in full swing as usual.

Everyone knew Miro, who had been staying at the hotel for days, and she had tied the tavern’s record by drinking twenty-one glasses of Polka.

“Amazing, this girl! Twenty-one glasses of Polka! I saw the legend when I was sixteen, and now a new legend is born?”

“One more glass and she’ll break the record! It’s been 34 years!”

Hearing this, Miro held out her empty glass.

“The title of the best can’t be without my name!”

As people cheered, Sein dragged Miro away with a weary expression.

“Stop drinking. You’re really drunk.”

“Hey, what’s the big deal? Today’s a good day.”

It was the day the politician Miro supported had pushed through an agenda in a secret meeting at the hotel.

“We’re leaving soon, so I have to enjoy Roblang’s specialty to the fullest.”

“No matter how strong you are, drinking this much will leave you in no shape to handle a crisis.”

With her cheeks flushed, Miro pointed unsteadily.

“So you’re waiting for me to pass out drunk? Sneaky. Fine, let’s do it.”

Miro put her glass on the table and shouted.

“I challenge you!”

“Alright! Hey, everyone, gather around! A new legend is about to be born!”

Sein sighed.

‘Miro, why are you doing this?’

She was always up for fun, but he had never seen her drink so heavily.

“Sein, this drink is called Polka.”

Miro, resting her chin on the table, looked at the two streams of liquid filling her glass.

“I don’t want to know.”

“Long ago, the fishermen of Roblang enjoyed two types of alcohol. Polt, a distilled liquor from the north, and Enka, a fermented drink from the south. They often fought over which was better when they went out to sea. Finally, a captain suggested mixing them and drinking together instead of fighting. That’s how Polka was born.”

As he watched the swirling Polt and Enka in the glass, Sein finally understood Miro’s feelings.

“What does ‘Paré’ mean?”

“You and me, all of us.”

“…”

The Polka was ready.

“Alright! If you drink this, you’ll be the best!”

“Drink! Drink! Drink!”

Miro raised her arms and smiled broadly.

“Great! Here we go!”

As Miro grabbed the glass, everyone fell silent, and the strong drink went down her throat in big gulps.

“Oh…”

When the last drop was gone, all the drinkers in the tavern stood up.

“Wow! She did it! It’s a new record!”

As Miro took a deep breath, dozens of people shouted in unison.

“Paré!”

“Ah, that feels great!”

Miro, sticking out her tongue, collapsed into her chair, but Sein didn’t scold her anymore.

“Are you satisfied now?”

“Yes. I drank more than enough. Let’s leave in the morning.”

“When you wake up, will you even remember?”

“Hehe. It doesn’t matter if I don’t remember.”

Miro looked up at the ceiling.

“The feelings will remain.”

“…I see.”

The light from the lamp blurred, perhaps because of the alcohol.

‘Sister Yolga, you’re watching, right?’

She was small but immense, the lowest yet looking higher than anyone.

Raising her empty glass towards the beautiful face shimmering in the light, Miro toasted.

“To the future we will create together…”

Paré.
