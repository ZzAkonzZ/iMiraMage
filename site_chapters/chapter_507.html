Chapter 507: The Blowback of Evil (1)

Satiel flew away, and Ikael turned to check on Shirone.

Shirone was hunched over, arms hanging limply.

Though standing, it was as if his mind was already lying down, and his eyes were vacant.

"Shirone."

Ikael flew towards Shirone, who was slowly collapsing forward.

But before she could reach him, someone else ran up and caught Shirone.

It was Plu, who had been unable to intervene in the battle that surpassed human limits.

"Shirone! Snap out of it, Shirone!"

Plu cradled Shirone's face in her lap, slapping his cheeks, but he remained motionless like a corpse.

The massive flash of the photon cannon had been intense even for Plu, who had been away from the battlefield.

She didn't know how Shirone had managed to amplify the power to such an extent, but she knew that in the world of magic, there were no rewards without a price.

Seeing no sign of Shirone regaining consciousness, Plu turned to glare at Ikael.

"What happened? Why isn't Shirone waking up?"

Even in front of an archangel, Plu showed no fear.

Her mind had reached its breaking point, leaving only anger, but she also sensed no malice from Ikael.

In fact, Ikael looked at Shirone with more concern than anyone.

'Shirone…'

From the moment he cast Ataraxia in its physical form, his mind had been shattered.

But reaching the state of Prajna had delayed the mental explosion.

If it had stopped there, there might have been some hope.

"Shirone defeated Faiel using the judicial halo Valhalla Action. He is now paying the price."

"Valhalla Action?"

Plu looked at Shirone in shock.

Considering the scale of the final photon cannon, the price Shirone had to pay was unimaginable.

"How long… will he be like this?"

Ikael shook her head.

No one could know except the computational trigger of Valhalla Action.

"I don't know, but it will be much longer than you think. Fifty years, a hundred years, maybe even two hundred years."

Plu looked at Ikael with irritation.

An archangel, and that's all she could say?

"Two hundred years? Shirone is human. Humans don't live that long. The magic shouldn't have worked in the first place!"

That's true.

If the result couldn't be reached, the cause shouldn't exist.

But conversely, if the result was reached, it means the cause was valid.

"Valhalla Action is just a trigger that mediates the Akashic Records. According to human logic, the cause should precede the result, but Valhalla Action reverses that logic. In this case, since the result exists, the cause must be possible."

Though still infuriating, Plu wasn't a foolish mage.

"Then what if Shirone dies of old age? Or gets killed? The result is already there, so how does the cause change?"

"I don't know. But that's the Akashic Records. Changes in parts don't matter to the whole. No matter how parts change, the whole is always perfect. If Shirone dies, some change will occur. But it won't matter to the whole."

"God is indifferent, is that it?"

Though the concept of god differed between angels and Plu, Ikael nodded.

Only living beings give meaning to things like a lover's death, incurable diseases, or heinous crimes.

To the whole, Shirone's death is as significant as a pebble rolling down a mountain.

"Exactly. And perhaps… that might be one way to bring Shirone back."

Plu's eyes widened.

"What do you mean?"

Ikael shook her head again.

Even an archangel couldn't know what computations Valhalla Action had performed.

She just wanted to convey the gravity of the situation.

"Sadly, it's beyond my capability. But Shirone is human, so humans must find the answer. Isn't it human to endlessly seek answers even in despair?"

Plu started to shout something but then closed her mouth and looked down at Shirone's face with a sullen expression.

Ikael was just as sorrowful.

'It's truly unfortunate.'

Perhaps… if Shirone had progressed in normal time, he might have found a way to defeat Faiel within ten years.

Ikael saw that much potential in Shirone.

But there are no hypotheticals in the Akashic Records.

Thus, Valhalla Action must have calculated the exact time Shirone needed to reach that level and demanded it as the price.

It's difficult for a human to live beyond a hundred years, but not impossible.

In Shehakim, there are countless people over a thousand years old.

"For now, there's no need to worry. He reached the state of Prajna and prevented the explosion. Valhalla Action can't put a debtor of desire in a state of moratorium."

Hearing that Shirone wouldn't die, Plu felt a bit relieved.

It wasn't easy, but there were other pressing concerns.

"Gasp!"

Suddenly, a thought struck her, and she turned to Ikael.

Time had passed significantly, even by her senses.

She shouted, filled with the fear that it might already be too late.

"Something terrible has happened! Divine Punishment…!"

Ikael, who had been focused on Shirone, quickly looked up at the sky.

Her eyes widened in shock as she scanned the heavens.

"Oh no…"

Gaold was surrounded and attacked by fallen angels like prey.

Unconscious, he swung his arms aimlessly, while the angels, with fierce eyes, attacked and retreated, slowly suffocating him.

They weren't doing this just to torment Gaold.

It was fear.

The memory of Gaold slaughtering countless fallen angels and Maras in Rakia made them hesitate to approach.

But from the wall, Kangnan's heart felt like it was being torn into a thousand pieces.

"Aaaah!"

The more she struggled, the more she focused on breaking her wrists.

Crack! Crack!

With screams that sounded like battle cries, her palm bones gradually shattered as she swung her arms.

Divine Transcendence - Yaksha.

Uriel defined Kangnan's state as such.

Those who burn their will with the power of anger, changing the law of their bodies.

Gaold's pain fully seeped into Kangnan's anger, strengthening her will.

Crack! Crack!

Finally, her palm bones completely shattered, and her broken fist slipped out of .

With a body tormented by pain all night, even taking a step was difficult, but Kangnan sobbed as she fell from the wall.

In a state between landing and falling, she crawled for a while before standing up and walking towards Gaold.

"Sir… Sir…"

"Argh! Argh!"

Gaold flailed his arms, unaware of Kangnan's approach.

His consciousness, memory, and thoughts were already gone.

What moved him was the flash of pain in the darkness.

Watching him twist and grab at the light made her feel like her insides were turning.

"Sir…"

Kangnan approached with her broken fists hanging limply.

'It's okay if you love Miro. It's okay if you don't come to me.'

She knew why he was fighting like this.

'It's okay if you don't know how hard it was for me. So…'

Kangnan hugged Gaold's waist.

Surprisingly, Gaold's frantic movements stopped.

"So…"

Kangnan looked up, tears streaming down her face.

"Don't be in pain. I'll fight for you, so please don't be in pain."

"Now!"

As Gaold's excitement subsided, the fallen angels gathered their courage and charged.

If they didn't eliminate him now, they didn't know how long this standoff would last.

As Kangnan shuddered and glared at the approaching enemies, she heard Gaold's weak but clear voice above her.

"Mutt…"

"Sir!"

Kangnan looked at Gaold in surprise.

Compared to before coming to heaven, his face was so weak he looked like a corpse, but he still wore a handsome smile.

"Are you okay? Are you conscious?"

"…Keep the house safe."

Kangnan burst into tears.

Even in a state where his mind had fallen into hell, he had climbed up to deliver this message.

From the deep, deep abyss, he had pulled himself up to see her one last time.

"No, sir! Don't go! Don't leave me!"

"Eliminate heaven's enemy! End it in one strike!"

Turning his head, Gaold saw the fallen angels flying towards him and twisted his mouth into a grotesque smile.

"Keke."

Pain amplified a hundred million times? A billion times? Ten billion, a hundred billion times?

'Who cares?'

"No, sir! Don't do it!"

"Pain is just…"

Gaold looked down at Kangnan and stroked her head.

As if trying to engrave his voice directly into her mind.

"Just a sensation."

Air Pressing.

Boom!

The moment the formless, heavy sound exploded, the ground shook as if an earthquake had struck.

And then there was nothing.

"Aaaah! Aaaah!"

Kangnan cast a spell and collapsed, sobbing in Gaold's arms.

Gaold, smiling with his eyes closed, looked peaceful, but the price he paid for that one smile was the deep, deep fires of hell.

"Aaaah! Sir!"

Uriel, standing on the wall, watched with his arms crossed.

What he had to confirm, following Kariel like a duty, was the final understanding of humans.

'Is that… human?'

In the end, Gaold had erased Rakia.

One human had wiped out the fallen angels and their Maras.

'The maddening obsession of humans with no end.'

Gaold is neither Prajna nor Yaksha.

Therefore, that is not all there is to being human.

But it is certainly an extreme aspect of humanity.

'Should I eliminate him now?'

Uriel summoned the Paradise Staff.

"……"

He looked at the pitiful figure who had lost everything after fighting countless formidable foes.

Angels are forbidden from acting.

Ra's command, which he initially couldn't understand.

But now, Uriel fully grasped the situation.

'It would be best… to eliminate him.'

Yet, part of him wanted to leave things as they were.

The opportunity gained by mobilizing all the forces in Rakia.

But that method didn't suit Uriel, the Archangel of Destruction, at all.

Rumble!

As Uriel spread his wings of light, the wall shook.

"Satan."

The strongest enemy in heaven at this moment.

Slowly rising above the wall, Uriel transformed into a flash of light and flew towards the center of Zebul.

"Aaaah! Aaaah!"

Kangnan's screams echoed through the silence of Rakia.
