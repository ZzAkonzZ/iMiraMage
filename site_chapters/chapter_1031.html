Chapter 1031: Black Chains (3)

Case of Emotional Syndrome, Number 4-312

In the underground mountain range surrounding the Tormia Royal Castle, a terrifying monster lurks.

“Haah! Haah!”

A monster known by the name of "human."

“It hurts. The pain is driving me insane.”

Richela, a notorious murderer who kidnapped and killed 23 women and girls over four years, was a heinous criminal. Yet, even he, who had inflicted unbearable suffering on countless others, could not endure his own torment.

“Argh!”

The moment his foot struck a rock, the impact made it feel as if his toe bones had shattered.

“Damn it! Damn this pain!”

At the mid-stage of emotional syndrome, physical pain becomes 200 times more intense than normal.

He could temporarily defer the pain by giving up what he loved, but...

“Hnnngh! Hnnngh!”

The only thing left to him, as tears streamed down his face, was his own wretched body.

‘I’m starving.’

Even his hunger felt 200 times worse, yet he couldn’t descend to the city under martial law.

Crawling up the mountain, he finally reached the cave that served as his hideout.

“Help me, please…”

The final victim he had designated as emergency food trembled, tied up with rope.

“Shut up.”

Grasping the knife in his stubby fingers, where joints had already been severed, he ended the hostage’s life.

The thrill of murder had long since been forgotten.

“I need to eat… I have to eat something…”

As he sliced a piece of flesh from the still-warm body and brought it to his starving mouth...

“Arghhh!”

It felt as if his throat were ablaze.

He recalled how he had survived the early stages of emotional syndrome by abstaining from human flesh.

‘No, no!’

The fact that he could still feel pain was proof that he continued to crave human flesh. And in such a case, the syndrome’s threshold would rise rapidly.

“Arghhh! Stop! Please, make it stop!”

His desperate screams echoed through the cave, stabbing at his eardrums like sharp nails.

“Hahh! Hahh!”

Gripped by pain that exceeded his tolerance, Richela clutched the knife and fell to his knees.

‘Might as well…’

The blade trembled as it approached his neck, and hot tears streamed down his face.

“No. I don’t want to die.”

Just because he was indifferent to the lives of others didn’t mean his own life was insignificant to him.

“Huff! Huff! Damn it all! I’ll kill them all!”

Richela lowered the knife and placed it against his own finger joint, severing it like a guillotine.

...

The pain was so intense he couldn’t even scream. His head tilted back before a feral cry escaped him.

“Arghhh!”

Even so, the emotional syndrome didn’t disappear.

At this point, losing a finger or two wouldn’t even bring him back to the latent stage—it had progressed far too severely.

“I—I don’t want to die!”

One joint, then another—Richela continued his brutal self-mutilation.

“Arghhh!”

Three days later, the World Health Organization, in cooperation with the Tormia Patrol, recovered his corpse.

When Seriel examined it, she reported that Richela’s body was in such a gruesome state that it was unbearable to look at.

As Son Yoo-jung was flung from the moon’s surface and hurtled toward the planet, a wild scream escaped her lips.

“Kraaaah!”

Her furious face looked so primal that she resembled an ape more than a human.

Watching from the moon, Nane smiled, his eyes gleaming mischievously.

“Come back once you’ve gained enlightenment.”

As Son Yoo-jung entered the atmosphere, her body caught fire like a struck match.

Her clothes began to burn away.

“I’ll kill you! Once I save my friend, I’ll come back and kill you for sure!”

Though her original goal had been to enter hell, her mind was now consumed by rage after her fight with the Buddha.

Nane wiped the blood from the corner of his mouth.

“She still doesn’t get it.”

Shaking his head in exasperation, he turned away just as flames ignited in Son Yoo-jung’s eyes.

“You…”

Rumble…

In that moment, the scenery of the world shifted, and the blue sky overlapped with clouds of fire.

“Hell.”

As the golden blade of the Buddha’s sermon shattered like glass from her chest, a golden collar locked itself around her neck.

“Grhhhhh!”

From afar, it looked as though a meteor was crashing down as Son Yoo-jung plummeted several kilometers.

Boom!

Hearing the deafening roar, the residents watched in shock as flames briefly flared on the horizon.

“Ow...”

Lying spread-eagle at the center of the crater, Son Yoo-jung couldn’t even move a finger.

‘Buddha, you damn bastard.’

Even the incarnation of the stone ape, renowned for its unbreakable endurance, couldn’t withstand the immense shockwave.

‘I want to fight again.’

As that thought crossed her mind, a human head cautiously peeked over the edge of the crater.

“A person. It’s a person.”

It was Richela, the demon formed from the murderer who had died in reality.

When Son Yoo-jung raised her head, a group of people appeared behind Richela.

Someone whistled.

“What’s this? Some kind of fashion statement?”

She was stark naked except for a thin golden collar around her delicate neck.

‘A cursed collar.’

She could have used a single strand of hair to create clothes, but shame didn’t matter when she was already reduced to primal instincts.

The only thing that annoyed her was the familiar metallic pressure faintly constricting her neck.

‘Damn that Buddha bastard! What did I do to deserve this?!’

—Come back once you’ve gained enlightenment.

The largest of the group kicked Richela’s rear.

“What are you doing? Get down there already.”

“Eek!”

As Richela tumbled down the slope, those watching burst into laughter.

“Hahaha! What a fool.”

Face buried in the ground, Richela clenched his fists and swallowed his anger.

‘Endure it.’

Though he had been a monster in the real world, among these even greater monsters, he was nothing more than a lamb.

‘If I mess up here, something truly horrible will happen. I won’t even be able to die.’

Richela had seen it with his own eyes.

One poor soul who had defied a senior was left crawling across the ground for 300 years, his limbs severed.

“Hey, pretty lady. Need some help?”

Before they could finish speaking, Son Yoo-jung flipped herself upright with a powerful leap and landed gracefully.

“What’s this? You guys into me or something?” Sitting with one leg crossed over the other, there was nothing human about her posture.

“You’re all so ugly. I’m not in a good mood right now, so let’s just fight. If you win, I’ll do whatever you want.”

A brief silence followed.

“Heh, interesting woman. So, where should we—”

Before he could finish his sentence, the muscles in Son Yoo-jung’s thighs split along their fibers, swelling rapidly.

“W-what the…?”

Before anyone could react, her body shot forward like an arrow, shattering the man’s skull.

“One down.”

Shirone pointed ahead.

“That’s Laviette.”

A city far larger than any other structure on the horizon came into view.

“The city of magical engineering. It’s enormous.”

“Yes. Hell’s society is divided into two main parts: the military and internal affairs. Most demons invading the human world are soldiers, with Satan as the supreme commander.”

“So, is internal affairs different?”

“No, Satan’s power is absolute. But there’s not much for him to do in practical terms. The territories of the underworld are governed by the nobles of the internal administration.” Shirone opened his palm.

“Barons, viscounts, counts, marquises, and dukes govern vast territories, and those overseeing capital-level cities are called grand dukes.”

“So, what about Laviette?”

“It’s a grand duchy ruled by a demon named Marguerite. From what I know, he’s the best in the underworld when it comes to magical engineering. It’s said there’s no secret he doesn’t know.”

Rian felt uneasy.

“It’s unusual to hear you praise a demon.”

Shirone was just stating facts, not giving praise, but he understood Rian’s point.

“It’s different from the demon army. They invaded us, but the residents here are just living their lives in this hellish society with a different system.”

Shirone continued.

“Of course, that doesn’t mean they’re good. Demons are demons. But Grand Duke Marguerite understands hell the best, so we might get information about the Chains of Gehenna.”

“If it’s because of me, it’s fine. It’s my burden to bear. But…”

Rian looked up at the sky.

“It’ll be dark soon.”

Just as water evaporates and forms clouds in reality, the fires of hell rise and gather in the sky here.

But at night, the flames disperse, followed by a terrifying cold.

“Resting in the city for a day might be good. Overexertion can affect our mental strength.”

Shirone was concerned.

As they quickened their pace towards Laviette, Rian asked again.

“What exactly is magical engineering? I haven’t seen any machinery on the way here.”

“You saw the Wailing Wall. The logic of the underworld is different from reality. It’s like the difference between card games and chess; the systems are different. To put it simply, there is engineering here. But…”

Shirone hesitated.

“You probably won’t like it.”

When they reached Laviette’s gate, Rian understood Shirone’s words.

“What is this?”

A wall similar to the Wailing Wall surrounded the city.

“Gatekeeper, humans have arrived.”

But it was much more standardized, and the pattern of human faces seemed machine-printed.

Seeing the arch-shaped gate resembling human body parts, Rian felt dizzy.

‘Horrifying.’

Dried, twisted legs decorated the sides of the gate.

‘They’re over 10 meters tall. Are they really human?’

Arms wrapped around the arch, and an old woman’s face with closed eyes was at the center.

‘Overall, it looks like an old person crouching with their head in their hands. But…’

The positioning, as if the limbs were severed and placed far apart, was deeply unsettling.

“Yahweh.”

The gatekeepers, recognizing him with their demonic intuition, lowered their voices and pointed their spears.

“How dare you come here? Get lost before we cut you down.”

“We’re on our way to the Fire Corporation. We also have business with Marguerite, the ruler here.”

“Heh.”

They laughed in disbelief.

“So what? Did anyone ask? You can’t come in, no matter the reason.”

“I can.”

The gatekeepers flinched.

“There are no enemies in hell, that’s Satan’s doctrine, right? I’d prefer you let us in quietly before I force my way in.”

“Snort!”

No matter what, Laviette would suffer if they clashed with Yahweh.

‘My role is to minimize the city’s damage. Let them in and follow the higher-ups’ orders.’

The fact that there was life here, that the residents of the underworld were different from the demon army.

“Open the gate.”

The old woman’s small face at the center of the arch opened her eyes and revealed her teeth.

“Creak!”

Then, the two arms descended like clock hands and began prying open the gate.

The sight of what seemed like self-mutilation made Rian frown.

“Creak! Creak!”

The sound of tearing flesh was heard, and dark red blood gushed from the gate.

As the fingers pulled left and right, the gate opened with a loud crack.

The gatekeeper spoke.

“There are no enemies in hell.”

Or everyone is an enemy.

“But Yahweh, you’re an exception. You won’t be welcomed inside.”

“…Let’s go, Rian.”

As Shirone walked calmly, Rian followed with a bitter smile.

“Now it feels real.”

He finally realized where he had fallen.
