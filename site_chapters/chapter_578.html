Chapter 578:

Alpheas Magic School Maintenance Factory

This place, where simple magical teaching aids are maintained, is off-limits to students and even staff at night due to the risk of theft, loss, and fire.

And now, damage was beginning to occur there due to Sabina's air cut.

With a spark and a crack, the barbed wire split, and Frings, who had thrown himself forward, shook his head.

"No, no."

Frings is strong. Even the title of third in the graduating class hierarchy is just a summary of his many magical abilities. In actual combat, a battle mage is a ferocious position, and with the attribute of freezing, even Sabina, a fellow battle mage, couldn't help but feel powerless in front of his ability to instinctively evade most spirit zones.

"Hmph!"

Frings grabbed Sabina by the neck and slammed her against the iron bars.

"This…!"

"Release the spirit zone."

His voice was cold enough to pierce the heart.

"Or your neck will fly off."

Feeling her esophagus freeze, Sabina released the spirit zone.

"Even so, the master card will never…"

"Shut up. I'll ask the questions."

The moment she saw Frings' eyes, her heart sank.

'Cold.'

Every time the cold air from his hand seeped into her nose, it felt like her insides were freezing.

"You won't last long. At most, ten minutes? If you keep breathing like this, your insides will turn into ice blocks."

Sabina didn't understand why he was causing pain, ignoring the victory conditions of the Scramble Royal.

"What do you want?"

Frings leaned in close to Sabina and took a deep breath.

"Haaa!"

Watching his trembling eyelids, she felt a murderous intent rise but couldn't bring herself to show it.

"You smell disgusting, Sabina. Like you've fallen into a sewer."

As Sabina choked back tears, the cold intensified, causing her veins to feel like they were tearing apart.

"Hngh!"

"What are you thinking? Waiting for Nade? Do you think he'll come after seeing the rescue signal?"

Sabina kept her mouth shut.

"Answer me. Or you'll die."

Frings would certainly do it. His expression, with the mask off, showed no law, morality, or dignity as a student of the magic school.

"Fine…"

"Good, you're a good girl. Now I'll ask you questions. If I think you're lying, you'll die. If you stall, you'll freeze to death. Now, stage one."

Sabina's lips trembled.

"Let's start with something easy. What did you eat for breakfast today?"

"Bread."

"Be specific! What kind of bread? What did it taste like? Did you have it with milk? Or juice?"

As the cold intensified, Sabina grimaced and shouted.

"I had cream bread with milk! It was so delicious I could die! Why!"

"Oh, really? Cream bread with milk…"

Frings' eyes rolled back.

"…you ate it. Very deliciously… Sweet, wasn't it? It must have melted in your mouth."

'I want to kill him!'

Frings' pupils returned to normal.

"Good. Second question. When was your first kiss? With whom? What were you thinking, and how did it feel? Where was it? What position were you in?"

Sabina gritted her teeth.

"Do you think I'd tell someone like you?"

"Oh, so you're going to hold out? Was it such a secretive kiss? A precious memory?"

Even colder air seeped into her nose.

"It's a good choice. How does it feel to weigh a precious memory against your life? Are you scared? Do you wish this situation was a lie?"

'Save me! Save me, Nade!'

Blocking out Frings' words, Sabina cried out internally.

'I'm not scared. He'll come to save me.'

The reason she could endure Frings' abnormal madness was because of Nade.

'It's nothing. It was much scarier back then.'

Sabina's pupils began to dilate.

"The effect is starting to show. Why not give up? Do you want to die over something like this?"

Frost began to form on the barbed wire of the maintenance factory.

"Hurry! Hurry!"

Nade cast teleportation with all his might.

As far as he knew, none of his teammates were the type to ask for help over trivial matters.

'It seems to be Sabina's area.'

As he sped up the tempo of his teleportation, monsters with horns on their foreheads attacked from all sides.

Jincheon Sorcery - Red Goblin.

Reflexively laying down plasma and striking with electricity, the goblin's body tangled with blue electricity and exploded.

'It's Anchal! I need to call Kang…'

Currently, Nade's hand was ○●○● (democracy), and Anchal's hand was ○●ⓡ●.

'He chose black for the last card. So the random card is likely to be white.'

If the hands were the same, it was advantageous to call Kang.

"Anchal, Kang."

Anchal responded.

"Kang."

As expected, it was the same democracy, making Anchal's surprise attack puzzling.

'My hand is already revealed. Why did he ambush me knowing we had the same hand?'

It wasn't a place with a scramble.

"Did he come to block the rescue? He proposed a secret agreement yesterday. Such a bat-like personality."

Anchal bowed politely.

"Thank you."

'What is this woman…'

Anchal continued.

"But I am fighting my own battle. I don't care about Scramble Royal."

"Then move. I'm busy."

"Nade, who are you?"

Nade kept his mouth shut.

"I was trained to analyze humans from birth. A cold, mechanical analysis."

"So what? Are you curious about how to be the last in the graduating class?"

"The strong and the weak are clear. Most are weak, of course. But you…"

Before making a definitive statement, Anchal raised his single eye and examined Nade.

"I really don't know. So I felt the need to confirm it before the great cause."

Recalling Sabina's words, Nade spoke.

"I am me. I don't know what you want to know, but nothing will change."

"Really?"

Anchal waved his hand.

Jincheon Sorcery - Mind Projection (Self-Portrait).

"Now, your true form…!"

Anchal's single eye widened.

'What is that?'

"Rrrr!"

Electricity surged from Nade's body and exploded outward.

"Ahhh!"

The scenery flickered as if day and night were alternating, and when the true form was finally revealed, Anchal's body shot up in a flash.

'I can't read it.'

Even Anchal, a master of illusions, couldn't analyze the ability to project the opponent's mind as an image.

"Could it be…?"

Boom!

Real electricity, not an illusion, spread out, scorching the entire area where Nade stood.

Anchal, who landed precisely on a branch 200 meters away, watched Nade flee into the forest with the self-portrait attached.

"How did it get to that state…"

What was revealed through mind projection was an incomprehensible mystery.

"Can you stay sane?"

"Haaa…"

Cold steam escaped from Sabina's lips.

Her mind was becoming hazy, and her whole body felt numb as if it were freezing.

"The end is near. How does it feel to die?"

'I have to speak.'

It's better than dying.

'I can't speak.'

It's better to die.

The ambiguous boundary where Sabina struggled was exactly where Frings found pleasure.

"Now, your last chance. Will you answer, or will you die?"

"I…"

As Sabina's mouth opened, Frings' expression hardened.

'Spirit zone?'

As he threw himself, a flash passed through the spot where he had been.

"Huff!"

Sabina, who had collapsed to her knees, turned her head with a pained expression.

"Nade?"

Contrary to her expectations, Shirone was standing there, but she limped towards him without feeling disappointed.

"Sorry. I couldn't handle it alone."

Shirone, who had directly faced Frings' skills, knew them best.

"It's okay. Are you hurt?"

"I'm not in good shape. I don't know exactly, but I won't die."

Shirone grimaced and glared at Frings.

"What are you doing?"

"I don't understand. What are you doing?"

"No matter how emotional you get, it's a game by rules and agreement. I can't tolerate tormenting the opponent like this."

Frings mimicked Shirone's words in a baby voice.

"I can't tolerate tormenting the opponent."

Then, approaching the blushing Shirone, he said.

"What will you do if you can't tolerate it? Hit me? Then I'll run away like crazy."

Frings' finger hurriedly pointed at Shirone.

"Did you think about why I said I'd run away? Do you think I'm afraid of you? Not at all, not at all."

His finger shook side to side.

"That's the true victory. You getting so mad you go crazy and collapse from anger. Honestly, don't you feel frustrated too? Just let it all out."

Frings shouted, stretching his arms towards the sky.

"Strength is true freedom! Breaking free from morals, laws, and school rules to gain infinite freedom!"

"So… are you infinite now?"

"Huh?"

"The way I see you, you're an empty shell endlessly suffering because you can't quench your thirst."

"Oh, such a dignified expression. For a commoner."

Frings seemed desperate to make Shirone angry.

"No matter what you say, I'll stick to the line I've set."

"Really?"

Frings disappeared.

"Then die of frustration."

Just as Frings, who had cast teleportation, was about to leap over the wall, Shirone's flash collided head-on with him.

"What the…!"

Frings, who had undone his photonization, couldn't get over the wall and retreated as Shirone, revealing the incarnation of the Light Angel, charged forward.

'Is that it? The ability to manipulate time.'

The reason he chose a head-on collision, risking fatal injury, was because he could reverse it.

'It's rather convenient.'

If he didn't intend to crash into it anyway, Frings could handle it as he pleased.

Seolbinghwa.

As the ice blades fluttered like petals, Shirone made a wide detour and cast Photon Cannon.

"Hehehe, are you angry?"

Sensing the spirit zone, Frings dodged the flash, shaking his upper body as if dancing.

"It'll never hit. A defensive four-way formation… huh?"

Frings squinted as if he couldn't believe it.

'There's no spirit zone?'

No, that's not it.

'There's a spirit zone everywhere. What on earth…'

As the Photon Cannon emerged right next to him, Frings' upper body bent almost vertically.

'An escape type? No, it's different. Why isn't the magic activating?'

As the focal point disappeared through Elysion, Photon Cannons were born from all directions, covering him like a net.

"Oh my…"

Sabina, who was watching, gaped.

The reason a mage's position is important is that it is the focal point of their mind.

Therefore, she couldn't understand the principle of magic activating without a focal point.

"Now I get it."

Frings, like a true battle mage, drew a conclusion closely related to victory.

'There is a radius. Only the boundary has disappeared. In that case…'

Frings' eyes curved like seagulls, and his lips split deeply enough to form deep wrinkles.

"I'll run even farther."

Shibulsangpokmae!

As Shirone widened his eyes and shattered the mold of time, dozens of Photon Cannons surged past Frings' back.

"Damn…!"

Boom boom boom boom!

Frings, exposed to the hail-like flashes, disappeared into the dust.

"Ugh!"

A groan was heard first, followed by the wind blowing away the dust.

Frings, with ice chunks at the ends of his limbs, was trembling, covering his face with both arms.
