Chapter 799:

“Kill me.”

Mortrasinger meant it.

“I’m tired of living with this hideous face. Do whatever you want with me—just make sure I die at the end.”

Her ability had manifested because her complex was more unbearable than death or pain.

“I’m not going to kill you.”

Mortrasinger looked puzzled.

“I know mental scars are the hardest. If it were easy to change, no one would be suffering. But…”

She was beautiful. Without a doubt.

“That doesn’t mean there’s no way. I can help you—so that you can love yourself.”

Mortrasinger’s response was cold.

“You don’t get it. You’re beautiful. You’ve never had to live with a face like mine.”

“You’re not ugly.”

“Liar.”

Shirone conjured a mirror with Material magic and held it up to her.

“Look. Look at your face.”

“Kyaaa!”

Mortrasinger recoiled in horror, jerking her head away, but Shirone only pressed harder.

“Look! This is you! What part of this is so hideous?!”

“Get that away from me! Why are you tormenting me?!”

If it were that easy to bring about catharsis, she wouldn’t have committed such a horrific act of terror.

“If you can’t accept it from yourself, then believe someone else. I think you’re beautiful.”

“You’re lying! You’re just pitying me!”

“It’s not a lie. I’d stake my life on it.”

Mortrasinger’s eyes sharpened suddenly.

“Then prove it. Die in front of me. If you do that, I’ll believe you.”

“Okay.”

Shirone released the Shining Chain and took her hand, guiding it to his throat.

“I can’t kill myself. So do it for me.”

Shirone couldn’t include himself in the act of killing—not even out of self-loathing. For him, suicide was equivalent to extinguishing all life in the universe.

“Heh! You think I won’t? My ability might be gone, but snapping your neck is—!”

As a high-ranking member of the Council of Ten, Mortrasinger’s grip was terrifyingly strong. She began to crush his throat.

Shirone closed his eyes and waited for death in silence.

He wasn’t expecting her to change her mind, or suddenly have a revelation from the act.

He simply believed.

That humans were beings capable of choice.

Even the murkiest heart…

Even a demon’s.

There’s no such thing as a heart that can’t turn back.

That, in the world of emptiness that the Buddha had realized, amidst the clash between good and evil, was the only hope love could dream of.

Mortrasinger’s arms began to tremble.

Why… why is he going this far…?

It felt like the tiniest thread of light was seeping into her completely twisted heart.

“…Am I really…”

She slowly withdrew her hand.

“…beautiful?”

Every time Hyega moved, diamond dust crystalized in the air, forming a trail like the Milky Way.

I can’t even breathe!

Though matter reverts to energy under the laws of the universe, if inhaled before that happens, it could destroy the lungs.

“Your way of running away is rather impressive.”

Marsha stepped back, having lost her dagger.

“It’s already been over 100 seconds.”

Snorting through his nose, Hyega charged forward, his whole body encased in diamond.

“Then kill me.”

Her Regulation Exception—Confiscation—had been undone by Shirone’s catharsis, but her resolve remained unchanged.

As his piercing strike came at her, Marsha twisted her body and hugged Hyega from behind.

Let’s see who dies first.

Pulling a new dagger from inside her thigh, she began hacking into Hyega’s body without hesitation.

As twelve new slashes appeared, gas formed from desire started seeping from his body at a rate of 12% per minute.

“You’re prepared to die?”

Despite dodging Hyega’s attacks at point-blank range, she never stopped cutting.

No point in backing off—either way, it’s death.

Even Hyega realized it now.

I have to end this first!

With flawless martial technique, he drove Marsha back against the wall and stabbed her abdomen.

“Guh!”

At that moment, Hyega’s desire was fully depleted—and with it, his free will.

“W-Why…?”

He was sure his strike had landed.

“Too bad for you~”

Marsha sucked in her stomach, proudly showing off her flat belly and belly button like it was a trophy.

“You cheap trick…”

“Cheap?”

She shoved him with her shoulder, and the exhausted Hyega collapsed backwards.

“Do you think it’s easy to keep this figure at thirty?!”

“……”

As she looked at the speechless Hyega, Marsha turned toward the entrance.

“You still not done? What’re you even doing?!”

Freeman, reloading his mana rounds, aimed at Bodhidharma, who had shot dozens of meters into the air, creating massive palm-shaped shockwaves on the ground.

“Damn it! That damned monk!”

The air pressure alone was enough to lift bodies—and without a direct hit, there seemed no way to bring him down.

“I’ll handle this.”

Seike of the Moon Clan bent his knees as the tiger tattoo on his right thigh lit up.

“Right of the Fierce Tiger.”

He launched a kick toward the sky, and a beastly roar exploded through the air.

GRAAAAAAA!

The shockwave curved through the air, hitting just beside Bodhidharma, exploding into chaotic turbulence.

Gghhh!

The Tiger’s Side Attack overlapped dozens of rotational vectors into a single shockwave.

“Diamond Sutra.”

Bodhidharma thrust both hands downward, launching himself even higher with a boom.

“Wind Style—Titanic Rakshasa.”

Joining his hands in prayer, he thrust them like blades. The compressed air, as hard as steel, shot upward like a spear.

GRAAAAAANG!

The two techniques collided midair with a deafening boom.

“HEY! What the hell is all this noise?! What are you idiots doing?!”

Dragging Hyega by the scruff of his neck, Marsha pointed at Freeman and shouted.

“Hold on! I almost got him!”

Just as Freeman pulled the trigger on Bodhidharma—

“Hmm?”

A brief violet glow, like the setting sun over the ridgeline, flashed from the mountain.

“…Mortrasinger.”

"Of course you're beautiful."

Shirone nodded with a smile.

"I'm serious. I’d never lie about that."

"You really think… I’m beautiful?"

She still couldn't quite believe it. No one had ever risked their life to say that to her before.

"Come with me. I know someone who can help you believe in yourself."

If it was Miro or Arius, they could dive into Mortrasinger’s personal hell and bring her salvation.

"Can I… be forgiven?"

Yahweh does not discriminate against those who repent.

"Nothing’s happened yet. From now on, you’ll fight with us."

Whirrrrr.

Is this real?

A single fly entered the forest.

Could I really escape this hell…?

Just as Mortrasinger was about to take Shirone’s hand, she recoiled and shrank back.

"No! There’s no way I’d be forgiven! It’s a trap!"

The moment Shirone’s lips parted, the world froze over in shades of gray—everything stopped.

"Aaaaaaah!"

From above, a solemn requiem echoed, accompanied by a grand choral chant.

"Wh… who are you people?"

Twelve figures in pitch-black robes stood in a perfect circle around Mortrasinger.

"Hell’s Prison." Time’s prison—0.666 seconds outside of natural law.

"Prepare to receive Him."

As the twelve turned in unison, the monochrome world peeled away to reveal a massive lake of lava.

"Your doubt is justified, my child."

The center of the molten lake swelled, and a grotesque, blood-smeared humanoid form emerged.

The Ultimate Evil—Satan.

Its voice burned with molten rage.

"Yahweh has deceived you. Only I can give you what you truly desire."

Activating a chronoprison from outside the spiritual domain was a violation of order—but in this world, God was absent.

"What I… want?"

"Behold, my child."

A red, oil-slicked mirror rose beside Satan, reflecting Mortrasinger.

"Ah… Aaaaah…"

A mirror of the soul.

The reflection was horrifying—but to Mortrasinger, it was the most beautiful form she had ever seen.

"This is the face I’ve always dreamed of!"

"I can give it to you. Worship me. Become the most beautiful woman in the world."

It was a far sweeter offer than anything Shirone had said.

"I accept. Please… give me that beauty!"

The requiem chorus swelled louder.

"…The contract is sealed."

As the twelve beings sank into the earth, Mortrasinger too was pulled down, as if swallowed by a swamp.

"No…"

Suddenly regaining clarity, she screamed.

No! That’s not me!

Realizing the horror of what she saw in the mirror, she fought back—but there was no undoing a pact with Satan.

"Save me, Shirone! Please, save me!"

She reached out in desperation, but the distance between her and Shirone’s outstretched hand only grew.

"Khahahaha! I’m taking her, Yahweh! I’ll claim everyone you love!"

Within time outside of law, Satan whispered mockingly into Shirone’s ear.

"As long as no Buddha exists, the world is mine. Suffer forever, wretched Yahweh."

Mortrasinger’s face sank into the ground.

"Shirone…"

As the chronoprison faded completely, Satan, still in human form, also submerged into the fires of hell.

The gate to hell closed, and a massive burst of violet light flashed for the briefest moment—

0.666 seconds.

"Huh?"

Mortrasinger vanished without a trace, and Shirone narrowed his eyes as he stood.

"What happened?"

A kidnapping?

No—it’s more than that. Even if it was at an invisible speed, there should’ve been a psychic trace.

Not unless time itself had stopped.

But how? Even stop magic can’t affect matter directly…

Shirone’s eyes widened.

"Satan."

Only those beyond the law could deceive Yahweh.

So even the Ultimate Evil can break the rules…

He had preyed on the split-second doubt in Mortrasinger’s heart and dragged her into hell.

"I need to report this to Zion."

Shirone launched into the sky with a fly spell and quickly returned to the venue.

The battle had calmed. Hyega, now being supported, and Marsha ran toward him with urgency.

"Shirone! What happened?"

"Where’s Lady Mortrasinger?"

Shirone turned to Bodhidharma and explained the situation.

"She was taken to hell?"

"Ever since Theraphos allowed rule breaking, it looks like the demons have changed tactics. She won’t be able to escape on her own."

"Hmm…"

Bodhidharma exchanged glances with Hyega.

"She was still our leader. The Immortals’ Community will go to Zion. If there’s a way to save her, it’s there."

"I agree. But I can’t promise they’ll welcome you."

Though he said that, Shirone was sure Miro would accept them.

She’s a Supreme Virtue, after all.

Glancing around, Shirone confirmed that there were no casualties, and finally released the Time Vibration.

"So this is it? Too bad. I was having fun."

Marsha looked disappointed—but Shirone, who had lived through hundreds of iterations, was exhausted.

What happened in Satan’s time is beyond my reach.

It was a time that didn’t exist within law. Even if he went back again, Mortrasinger wouldn’t be in this reality anymore.

"Shirone! Where did you go? I was scared to death!"

The beauty pageant contestants came rushing over.

"The terrorist’s been stopped. You can relax now and continue the competition."

Langi beamed as she asked again.

"Really? You really caught them?"

Before Shirone could answer, she wrapped her arms around his neck and jumped up and down.

"Yay! We’re finally free!"

One after another, the other contestants hugged him too.

"Thank you. But it’s a shame, you know? If you’d been in the dressing room, you would’ve gotten quite the show."

"No thanks."

He meant it from the bottom of his heart.
