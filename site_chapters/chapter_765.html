Chapter 765:

“I only heard about it.”

Since Uorin had met far more people than any ordinary being could imagine, Gando didn’t bother asking who she meant.

“Humans have five senses.”

Uorin drew on the table with her two index fingers.

“It’s like a card game. You combine five cards to define various rules. Of course, depending on the creature, the number of cards differs, and which cards they hold varies as well.”

“And the rules they define will be different too.”

“Exactly. And that’s where the problem begins. Defining rules means one thing—it means the game has begun.”

Uorin tapped her temple.

“From the moment the game starts, combinations accumulate endlessly. Eventually, one realizes—there’s one more card beyond the five you hold. A card you can’t possess.”

Time.

“The experiences taken in by the five senses are stored as a single unified sense—synesthesia. The rate at which this synesthesia accumulates is what we call time.”

Even time is something perceived through the senses.

“But sometimes, we feel time differently. Like when you knew something would happen, and then it actually does.”

“The rate of sensory accumulation changed.”

Uorin nodded.

“We already exist in every point in time. But because we’re bound by the five senses, we only perceive reality at the moment synesthesia accumulates. There are beings, however, that accumulate experience far faster than humans.”

Angels were a prime example.

“Compared to a human’s present, an angel’s present is far larger. From our perspective, it’s as if reality itself expanded in a burst.”

The sixth sense—one that living beings can attain.

“It’s called Shifok. Shirone, who holds the incarnation of the Radiant Angel, perceives a present far larger than we do.”

“…He’s not human.”

“In gaining that card, Shirone changed the rules of the game.”

Because senses are precise, like objects, Shirone—who perceives time as a sense—can grasp all space existing in the same moment. He awakened to a new world.

Gando looked out the window.

“The Spiritual Plane.”

“Even humans, if they’re sensitive enough, can feel it sometimes. Like someone being beside them—or strange eyes peeking through a crack in the door… though that’s nothing compared to the realm of Bachji.”

“If it isn’t precise, it isn’t truly sensory.”

Uorin was satisfied.

“From Shifok to Bachji, and eventually to Choehni Bardo—Shirone fully unified even the seventh sense. At that point, he began to perceive the mind as if it were an object.”

Uorin spread her arms.

“What’s left is expansion. After unlocking the Immortal Function, Shirone entered the threshold of the eighth sense—Hypercognition. He could sense all the information in the world.”

“He nearly didn’t come back.”

“If it hadn’t been for Yahweh’s mind supporting him. But in the end, he became the Infinite Mage.”

“Is there more beyond that?”

“String.”

Uorin raised one finger.

“Shifok perceives photons. Bachji perceives quantum particles. And Hypercognition perceives photon-quanta.”

In sequence, he conquered space-time, non-physicality, and information.

“Information is signal imbued with identity. So then—how is that signal formed?”

She curled her finger.

“Form. Every piece of information in this world is made up of infinite combinations of vibrational forms.”

Form is everything.

“To define it simply—every event that occurs in this vast universe happens just once.”

Uorin hooked her curled finger on her thumb and snapped it.

“It happens as the result of a single exertion of force.”

“Just once…”

“Exactly. To put it in the simplest terms: someone was walking along and saw a taut string. So they just… flicked it. That’s it.”

Gando pressed his lips together.

“I don’t know how hard they flicked it. Depending on the strength, the results of the universe might fracture infinitely. But either way, the string vibrated. And from that amplitude came probabilities—space, time, matter, non-matter, all the information—combined into a specific form, and created the world we live in.”

Uorin looked up at the ceiling.

“Within the amplitude of that one vibrating thread, we survive—relying on its force. Someday it’ll stop. But even I, after an eternity of life, cannot imagine when that far-off day will come. We don’t even know when the amplitude might begin to fade. And by then, the universe itself will likely have ended.”

It was a scale Gando could scarcely fathom.

“Vibration is everything. In the endless back-and-forth of extremes, all information continues to tremble.”

“Then Resonance…”

“Yes.”

Uorin turned toward Gando.

“Those who awaken to Hypercognition no longer perceive the world as fixed. It’s a world constantly quaking from a single force. And when the eight senses merge with the string…”

She opened her fan with a solemn expression.

“It means they can resonate with the vibration of the world itself.”

“…”

Gando didn’t understand everything—but a deep awe made him bow his head.

“Grrrrrr!”

Maraduke snarled, tightening his grip on Shirone’s neck, his expression twisted by the violent tremor.

“What… are you?!”

What was truly terrifying wasn’t the vibration itself, but the countless other vibrations riding atop it.

It was as if the entire world trembled in response to Shirone’s resonance—and Maraduke’s vision shook violently, up and down.

“Detestable Yahweh!”

He twisted Shirone’s neck with force, but each residual image of Shirone wore a different expression of anguish.

“Ghhhrrraaaagh!”

Through those expressions, Maraduke realized something—the number of afterimages exceeded tens of thousands.

“What is this?!”

He wanted to snap his neck immediately, but Yahweh’s light canceled out the aura of destruction he emitted.

“Die! Die! DIE!”

His muscular arms bulged to their limit—and Shirone’s vibration grew more intense.

It felt as if there were 100,000 Shirones. Amid their layered expressions, tormented screams spilled out.

Cries that wouldn’t exist even in hell.

“Hhhhrrrgh…”

Before this cosmic phenomenon, Maraduke felt—for the first time in his life—fear of the unknown.

Shirone, weeping blood. Shirone, screaming in agony. Shirone, pleading for his life.

“…Are you mocking me?”

Among the afterimages, some smiled. Some stuck out their tongues. Some burst into laughter.

“You dare mock a Legion Commander?!”

Just as Maraduke prepared to unleash everything in a final surge—

The afterimages began to disappear.

No—they were resonating.

The tens of thousands became thousands, then hundreds, then mere dozens—

“Ghhhrrrrrhhh!”

Now only a single Shirone remained, trembling rapidly forward and backward, bearing a uniform expression of agony.

No—it’s not shaking.

If a single person had truly shaken at that speed, their brain would have liquefied inside their skull.

It’s… countless events.

It was the process of 100,000 Shirones from Quantum Superposition merging into a single unified presence.

“Grhhh…”

The moment that final afterimage converged—

“Still at it?”

The burden of Valhalla Action disappeared.

“We die together, Yahweh!”

At the very instant Maraduke detonated his power for a suicidal blast, Shirone’s eyes snapped open.

Resonance!

Now fully unified, Shirone released Yahweh’s light into a 40-kilometer Spirit Zone.

“GRAAAAAAAAAHH!”

Only the infected demons remained—and they crumbled to ash with a chorus of shrieks.

“…So warm.”

With her eyes gently closed, Minerva smiled faintly around her pipe.

“Y-Yahweh! YAAAHWEEEH!”

As Maraduke’s screams pierced the air, the crushing sensation on Shirone’s neck vanished completely.

When Shirone opened his eyes, Maraduke knelt before him—his limbs melting, teeth clenched in fury.

“How… how wicked are you?”

To withstand Yahweh’s light meant the Legion Commander’s demonic force was nearly equal to Satan’s.

“Don’t think it’s over.”

Maraduke raised his head.

“There are still ways to torment you. Repent, Yahweh. Abandon your foolishness and follow the path of the demons!”

His body darkened—revealing his true demonic form.

That beautiful appearance had merely been a vessel of string-bound flesh. Now, a hideous shadow loomed over Shirone.

“I will annihilate you.”

A sound like crackling insects replaced his voice.

Ataraxia.

As Ataraxia’s sphere enveloped Shirone, an explosive light erupted.

Dragon-Slaying Magic: Quasar.

Countless beams of light curved through the air, imprisoning Maraduke in a radiant sphere.

“GRAAAAAAAH!”

It looked like ink dropped in water—but far more grotesque, as the demon’s silhouette writhed within.

“I won’t forgive you! Satan—please… strike down Yahweh…!”

As the strings of force disintegrated, sound vanished. And when Quasar’s light faded, not a speck of dust remained.

“It’s over, huh.”

When Shirone turned around, Minerva was walking toward him.

“No. It’s just beginning.”

As Shirone looked out toward the sunset beyond the horizon, Minerva smacked him on the head.

“Ow! Why’d you hit me?”

“That wasn’t cool at all! Did you think I’d gaze at you with teary admiration just because you turned your back like that?”

“…No?”

Minerva pointed her finger in his face.

“Anyway, I hope you’ve realized it by now. Let go of that obsession with saving everyone. It’s better to act based on efficiency.”

Shirone rubbed his aching head as he replied.

“Still, it’s something I have to do.”

“Even after all that happened to you…!”

“But if I can’t protect them, then… it can’t be helped, right?”

The fierceness in Minerva’s eyes softened.

‘He’s finally begun placing himself within the whole.’

What cannot be done, simply cannot be done.

‘And accepting even that—that’s true fulfillment.’

It was also the highest efficiency in pursuit of perfection.

“I’m not afraid anymore. I’ll just do my best, and focus on what I can actually do.”

Having shattered his delusion, Minerva’s conviction to follow Shirone’s version of perfection remained valid.

“Good. Then let’s seal the Altar. We’ll report to the Great Star and head straight for Kashan.”

The Stars always had countless tasks to handle. With that, Minerva leapt into the sky aboard Jet.

Now that he could catch up to her, Shirone remained behind for a moment to gather his thoughts.

A new sense. This must be the ninth.

He didn’t know that the ninth sense was called Resonance, but he had heard of the tenth.

Formless.

And now, he had a vague idea of what world that sense was hidden within.

It manipulates the information of all worlds. And if that’s possible, then theoretically…

Reset—the ability to initialize the world itself.

The sense of Anke Ra.

If this world had a true master, then it was surely the authority of the Administrator. Even Shirone wasn’t sure whether he could reach it.

The reason a human like Shirone was able to attain the sixth sense was because he had inherited the senses of an Archangel.

Ikael.

And the reason he could rise all the way to the ninth was because the final sense—the eleventh, Ultima—had drawn him upward.

Goffin.

Ikael pushed him forward, and Goffin pulled him upward.

They made me human… and still—

With only one final sense remaining, Shirone thought of them and felt a deep, aching longing.
