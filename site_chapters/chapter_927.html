Chapter 927: The Sin That Couldn’t Continue (3)

Boom! Boom! Boom!

Every time the massive muscle mass pulsed, Goffin's skin tingled in response.

The rhythm of the pulsations quickened.

The sanctuary filled with sound waves until there was no empty space left, and finally,

Boom!

With the last reverberation, silence fell.

"Why should I?"

Anke Ra's eyelids lifted from above and below, forming thin slits.

"I am everything in this world. I am the one who defines all. I have no reason to negotiate with you."

"Then define me."

Anke Ra's upper eyelid formed a V shape.

"You can't define me. Because you don't know me. I am the only thing in this world that you don't know, right?"

Hexa.

"So stop bothering others and let's settle this between us. If you can swallow me, this world will become clear again."

Creak! Creak! Creak!

The nerve endings spread all around clashed, creating a chilling noise.

'He's conflicted.'

Watching Anke Ra's pupils contract and dilate repeatedly, Goffin fell into thought.

'I'm the one at a disadvantage as the challenger, but Anke Ra has the pride of being a god.'

The Akashic Records and the Ultima System.

'Let's see who is truly a god.'

The two laws that permeate the world were fiercely pressing against each other.

Ikael, with her ear close to the door of the sanctuary where Anke Ra was, blinked.

She knew she couldn't hear, but Goffin's last words lingered in her mind.

'Hearts connect.'

That much she could understand.

'He wants me to continue? With whom? If there is someone I can connect my heart with...'

Ikael sighed.

'I hope this resolves well.'

Even now, in the vast expanse of Heaven, angels and Gaia people were in a tense standoff.

'We can't repeat the same war.'

Suddenly, this thought crossed her mind.

'Or am I just hoping that Goffin won't leave this place?'

There is no answer.

The longer the silence in the sanctuary, the more anxious Ikael's heart became.

"Fine."

Anke Ra made a decision.

"I will allow access to the Akashic Records. But you will also lose your will as you merge with the whole."

Goffin nodded obediently.

"I don't mind, as long as you keep your promise. Release all Gaia people from the photon realm except me."

It took three minutes for the answer to come.

"Granted."

Given Anke Ra's thought speed, it was enough time to count all the atoms in the universe and more.

"Confident, aren't you?"

"I will disassemble you and return you to the world. Everything will be under my control."

Anke Ra's nerve strands approached Goffin, wrapping around his legs and climbing up.

As they passed his knees, climbed his waist, and reached his neck, Goffin slowly closed his eyes.

'This is my only chance. And...'

Goffin's eyes snapped open.

'My last gamble!'

The moment Anke Ra's nerve terminal pierced his ear, light exploded from Goffin's pupils.

"Aaaah!"

As the Akashic Records invaded the Ultima System, it began to absorb all sources.

"Creak! Creak!"

Anke Ra's eyes literally shook at the speed of light as he tried to disassemble Hexa.

"Screeeeech!"

The electromagnetic roar pierced through the sanctuary and spread throughout Heaven, with Ikael receiving the greatest shock.

"Ugh!"

With her ear against the door, she barely managed to steady her trembling halo and bent over.

'Anke Ra's scream.'

Even for Ikael, it was a first.

'Why? What on earth is happening in there?'

She wanted to break down the door and enter, but such an option didn't exist in Ikael's mind.

"Creak! Creak! Screeech!"

In the sanctuary filled with light, Goffin was suspended in the air, connected to the nerve terminal.

Anke Ra mobilized all his abilities.

'All authority belongs to me.'

He dominated Goffin's thoughts, will, and spirit, yet Hexa remained undismantled.

'Why?'

For the first time since his creation, Ra's nerves twisted in agony from the confusion.

'What is this? Infinity.'

'No, it's impossible. Infinity is a trait not granted to any being. If it can't be absorbed...'

Destroy it.

Anke Ra sent high-frequency signals repeatedly, pushing the signal of destruction into Hexa.

Goffin's body trembled from the shock, but the concept of Hexa didn't even crack.

'This can't be.'

Even using all the concepts that exist in the world, he couldn't approach Hexa. 'Only delusions are endlessly repeating.'

Ra's thoughts, having circled the entire universe and returned to the starting point, reached Goffin's words.

'Why can't I become a god?'

At this moment, at this point, Anke Ra's Akashic Records faced an error for the first time.

'No.'

An unsolvable error.

'I am God. It must start from there.'

The greatest problem in the universe occurred.

Anke Ra, who governs the whole, denied the problem itself instead of solving the error.

"I am everything!"

Anke Ra's command, encompassing all concepts he created, was issued to all angels.

"Eliminate the Gaia people! Eradicate all errors in this world!"

Hearing the voice through her halo, Ikael jerked her head up.

"What..."

War begins.

Naturally, Archangel Ikael should also head to the battlefield, but her eyes were fixed on the door.

'I need to know what's happening.'

Her fingertips trembled as they reached for the door.

'No. I must not open it without Anke Ra's permission. It's not within my authority.'

Goffin.

As electricity sparked through her halo, Goffin's voice from long ago came to mind.

-You have to choose for yourself.

Ikael's face contorted in distress.

"Sigh!"

A groan escaped between her teeth, and her expression twisted, but her hands finally touched the door.

"Oh God..."

The sacrilege committed by the Archangel.

"I can do it."

Pushing her body's weight with her palms, she opened her eyes wide and shouted.

"I can choose!"

With a bang, the heavy door was pushed inward, and her view opened up.

"This, this is?"

Goffin, caught by Anke Ra's nerves, was floating in the air, his limbs flailing.

Anke Ra irritably spread sound waves.

"Who allowed entry to the sanctuary?"

Ikael was confused.

"Great Anke Ra, what is happening? Why is Goffin like that..."

"Go. Eliminate the Gaia people."

The thought that this was not the result Goffin wanted rooted her to the spot.

"Please tell me! Did Goffin agree to this? Or did he do something disrespectful during the meeting..."

When Anke Ra's eyes widened, veins bulged, and all nerves scratched the air at Mach speed.

"Graaaah! Cough! Cough! Cough! Cough!"

As the waves created a terrible noise, her halo exploded into dust.

Momentarily blank, Ikael turned around and spread her wings as soon as her halo recovered.

'Something is wrong!'

Accepting her initial thought, she flew with all her might to the confrontation site.

"Kill them! Annihilate the Gaia people!"

Countless angels flew through the air, bombing the Gaia people.

Satiel shouted.

"Wait a minute! Something's wrong! They don't even intend to fight!"

Rejecting Anke Ra's command was a significant act, but the last words stuck in her mind.

'They don't intend to fight?'

Looking at the ground, she saw Gaia people who hadn't even activated the Ultima System, raising their heads.

"What are they thinking?"

Seeing their unwavering eyes even as their kin died, she suddenly realized.

'This isn't Goffin's will.' Ataraxia unfolded.

"Stop! Cease the attack! There's been a mistake! All angels, return to me!"

No one returned.

"Eliminate them! Humans are errors! Eliminate them!"

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

Watching the angels' relentless bombing of the ground, Ikael was in despair.

"Goffin doesn't want war."

The Gaia people spoke in unison.

"Therefore, we will not fight. We will uphold Goffin's will to the very end."

Ikael couldn't understand what it meant.

"Do you understand now?"

The Gaia people's voices pierced through the noise of the bombing and struck her halo directly.

"Anke Ra is not a god! What kind of god retracts his own words! Goffin sacrificed himself to teach you directly!"

Ikael's eyebrows furrowed, and tears streamed between her clenched teeth.

"Stop."

The angels pretended not to hear.

"Insignificant humans! You have no right to exist in this world!"

Ikael shed tears of light.

'I must stop this at all costs.' But how?

No one can defy the command of Anke Ra, the incarnation of the Akashic Records, the law itself.

'Satiel.'

Finally, she realized that only Satiel hadn't attacked the humans.

Goffin's words came to mind.

-I've learned to put my heart into the sound a little.

Heart.

'My heart. If I shout with all my heart...'

Will it reach someone?

Throwing herself towards the largest Ataraxia ever expanded.

Ataraxia - Physical Realm.

As her halo passed through the halo, an overwhelming storm of emotions surged in.

"Haaaaa!"

A euphoria that felt like her mind was about to explode.

Ikael, shedding tears of light from pure emotion, shouted with all her might.

"Stooooop!"

The world shook.

The halos of the angels were pushed in the direction of the waves and then scattered into smoke.

"...r"

The angels, whose minds had been completely cleansed, regained their senses and looked back at Ikael.

"Arch, Archangel."

Their hearts had connected.

"Stop the war. All of you, listen to me..."

Ikael's halo shook violently, and she began to lose her power and fall.

"Archangel!"

As the angels flew to catch her, Ikael, with a faint smile, continued to speak.

"You must trust me."

With those words, she lost consciousness, and the angels looked up at the remaining seven archangels.

Raiel spoke.

"Open the White Mirror."

Metiel frowned.

"What White Mirror? Anke Ra's command is absolute. We have no choice."

"But it's strange that the Archangel tried to stop it. She always follows Anke Ra's commands." While Metiel fell silent, Satiel stepped forward.

"How about we postpone judgment until the Archangel wakes up?"

Uriel asked.

"What about the Gaia people? Leaving them like this is dangerous."

Kariel suggested.

"Take them. I'll manage them. I can ensure they won't cause any trouble."

Kariel's willingness to personally intervene indicated a special purpose.

'Human.'

Kariel had finally obtained the material missing from his life experiments.

Satiel added.

"Let's suspend the war for now. Anke Ra wouldn't have reversed his words, and Ikael wouldn't have disobeyed his command. We can make a judgment later."

Indeed, there were many parts they couldn't understand with the information they had.

Faiel looked at Kariel and spoke.

"Can you take responsibility for your words? If chaos breaks out in Heaven, I don't know how I'll react."

"Don't worry. I'll use spatial transfer technology. I'll isolate them with a Metagate."

The Gaia people complied with the angels' demands, and a long procession headed towards Aravot.

The angels blew victory trumpets and celebrated, but the archangels' expressions remained serious.

'What's really going on?'

Only Ikael knew what had happened in the Aravot meeting.
