Chapter 703:

"Did you say Yaksha?"

The Maga Bandits were composed of S-class criminals from the Empire, so they were well aware of Yaksha.

'Not that they could ever measure up to the leader.'

Yaksha was a title based on one's level of mastery, not just brute strength, and even among Yaksha, the levels varied greatly.

As Paknyeo untied her faded, multicolored turban, her ominously black hair flowed down like a horse's mane.

She wiped the sweat from her chest with the turban and carefully examined the King Scorpion corpses.

The area looked like a meteor had struck, with scorpion bodies torn to shreds. Some were cleanly cut in half, while others were ground into small pieces.

"One Banaya. Two Yaksha."

Judging by the combination, it was clear that the group she was looking for had been here.

"Where did they go?"

The deputy leader, puzzled by the unusually talkative leader, reported what he knew.

"They went south."

"What's to the south?"

"Vanguard… the largest oasis in the area. Merchants use it as a midpoint before crossing into the Kingdom of Paras. It's heavily guarded."

Paknyeo handed the turban to the deputy leader and mounted her Omek.

"Ah…"

The deputy leader struggled to resist the urge to bury his face in the turban, overwhelmed by its intoxicating scent.

'Get a grip. She's not a woman. She's a beast.'

She never washed until it rained and had never been seen wearing underwear. Her only garment was a tattered robe, and it was shocking to see her relieve herself while walking.

He never found her beautiful.

But why did his chest burn like fire whenever he was near her?

Paknyeo gave her order.

"Vanguard. We're heading south."

The bandits' eyes widened.

"Are you serious? The desert god is approaching."

As the beast king, Paknyeo was most attuned to the changing winds.

"They left hours ago. It's too late. We won't make it in time."

"We'll run fast."

The deputy leader wanted to fulfill Paknyeo's wishes.

"But what about our current mission? The altar business?"

"We'll do it later."

Only Paknyeo could offer such a solution.

"…Understood."

The deputy leader, now on his Omek, shouted to the bandits.

"Run at full speed! Our destination is Vanguard!"

'She's crazy. We're all going to die.'

Paknyeo and the 40 bandits set off south.

As night fell, the temperature dropped rapidly, and flying through the sky felt like their flesh was freezing.

"Brr, it's cold. This is a problem."

Uorin's insistence on reaching Vanguard before sunset now made sense.

"Hang in there. I see the oasis."

A massive oasis reflected the moon in the sky, with a shopping complex beside it.

'That's Vanguard.'

Three apartment buildings formed the outer wall, and numerous stalls filled the inner courtyard.

Rian commented,

"That's a fortress. The buildings are steel-framed, and the windows are double-paned. Why build it like that?"

"It is strange. And have you noticed? People have been appearing frequently since sunset."

Merchants who had been hiding somewhere were now quickly gathering at Vanguard.

"Indeed. Like they're being chased."

Kido laughed.

"Hehe! What's the worry? Uorin told us to come here. She's crazy, but she knows what she's doing."

It was contradictory but oddly convincing.

"We fought like mad today, so let's rest properly. We have plenty of money."

As Kaidra approached, the merchants at Vanguard looked up and shouted.

"Hey! Look at that!"

The terrifying creature scared the Baltan, and sirens blared from Vanguard's watchtower.

"A monster! A monster has appeared!"

Then someone pointed at Shirone.

"Wait! There's a person!"

Kaidra, accustomed to such situations with Uorin as its master, landed at the nearest spot.

"What about Latusa? If we take it to a stable, it'll be a meal in one go."

"Uorin said it doesn't need special care. If we let it roam freely, it'll find its own food."

As Shirone dismounted from Kaidra, he stopped and looked ahead with a serious expression.

Many merchants were staring at them.

"Uh… hello?"

He greeted them in the common continental language, but the merchants, who only spoke Kashan, didn't understand.

"Not a Middle Easterner? What's with that hideous green face?"

"Looks like he's poisoned by the desert. Tsk, tsk, poor guy. No chance of getting married like that."

Kido had never eaten a Middle Easterner, but he understood their looks and clicks of the tongue.

"You idiots! I'm a goblin! And I'm the best-looking one!"

The clicking grew louder.

"Where are you from?"

An elderly merchant asked, and Shirone replied,

"Rian, tell them we're not merchants, and that creature is my summoned beast. Introduce me as a wizard."

That should be convincing.

As Rian roughly translated, murmurs spread among the merchants.

"A mage? If he can control that beast, he must be powerful."

"Then…"

Their eyes changed.

'Wizards have money.'

As soon as they thought this, the merchants swarmed around Shirone.

"What's going on! Why are they doing this!"

Hawking was strictly forbidden inside Vanguard to prevent fights, but outside, it was fair game.

Especially if a wizard who could summon a beast might buy all their goods.

"Here! Buy my stuff! It's top quality! Guaranteed!"

"Tell me what it is!"

Through the Ultima System, Shirone understood many voices.

"Buy mine! Hurry up before I kill you!"

'If you're going to kill me, why sell?'

A skeletal man offered a black lump that smelled foul.

"Looking for this? It's an elixir of immortality."

"Immortality?"

"Just a spoonful in water, and you won't die until morning. You'll have an amazing night."

"I'm not buying! I'm not buying anything!"

"You can do it alone! Haven't you heard the saying, 'Don't let your left hand know what your right hand is doing'?"

Shirone cried out,

"Rian! I don't understand a word!"

At the same time, Rian grabbed Shirone and leaped over the crowd.

Kido, with crooked glasses, waited in a daze, and they all turned together.

"Run! Get inside!"

As they passed through Vanguard's gate, Kaidra's cry echoed faintly in the sky.

"Wow! That was intense! Total hard sell."

As Shirone caught his breath, a woman's laughter rang out.

"Hohoho! Of course. This is the heart of the desert ruled by emptiness. You can't stay sane here."

A plump woman in her 40s, flanked by two armed guards, approached.

Her dark skin was powdered white, and her lips were blood-red.

She held a Middle Eastern fan, and her see-through dress revealed a tightly laced corset.

"And you are…?"

The woman flipped her fan and introduced herself.

"I'm Momodo, the 32nd manager of Vanguard. I heard you're a wizard. Is that true?"

"Yes."

Rian didn't need to translate.

"Forget the commotion outside and spend lavishly in Vanguard. Tonight will be quite long."

"What do you mean?"

Rian translated this time.

"The desert god will soon cover this place."

"The desert god?"

It was a term from the dead man's diary.

"The desert god is Noskarta, an equatorial wind created by the unique environment of the Akkad Desert. A massive sandstorm that sweeps everything away."

"If it's such a great sandstorm, how has this place survived?"

Momodo pointed to a large machine installed between the U-shaped apartments.

"It's a sand removal device. It clears the sandstorm immediately. The never-drying oasis Luna and this device are Vanguard's pride."

The only sanctuary in the central desert.

'Hmm, so the King Scorpions' migration…'

How powerful must the sandstorm be to make 2-meter-long monsters flee in droves?

"Vanguard is safe. So spend money! Enjoy! Not even the desert god can stop your indulgence."

After Momodo's promotional speech, Shirone's group explored the colorful market.

"She seems confident. Let's relax. At least we won't be buried in the desert."

If a sandstorm came, they'd be stuck for a while.

"Shall we? Let's eat first. We haven't had anything since fighting the King Scorpions."

They chose grilled salamander skewers.

It looked unappetizing, but Kido believed in trying local specialties when traveling.

They bought some meaningless souvenirs and special cream to protect their skin from the sun.

"Traveling is fun. Where to next?"

"How about there?"

Shirone pointed to a row of tents along the road.

"Astrology shops. The tribes of the Akkad Desert are known for their astrology."

"Oh, predicting the future?"

Kido snorted.

"I don't believe in that. Just statistically, there are so many. Did all the world's prophets gather here?"

"Haha! True."

Shirone felt the same, but as a Banaya who understood the law, he was intrigued.

"True astrologers are rare worldwide. Let's give it a try. It's part of the experience."

People lined up at every tent.

'Well, it is the desert.'

Survival in a land ruled by death naturally brought anxiety about the future.

"It's popular. We might have to wait long."

"That tent has no line."

A tent with a crystal ball flag had no one waiting.

Kido eyed it suspiciously.

"Places like that usually have no skill."

"So what? We don't believe in it anyway. Hearing someone else predict our future could be fun."

Since they had decided to take a break, they wanted to try different things.

"Hehe! I'll become the ruler of the world."

With that, Shirone pushed aside the tent flaps and entered the astrology shop.

"Excuse me. Huh?"

A girl around their age was sleeping with her head tilted back, her face cover lying on the table.

As Shirone watched her drooling and showing no signs of waking up, Kido followed him in and said,

"There's always a reason why a place goes out of business."
