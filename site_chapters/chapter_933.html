Chapter 933: In The Flower Field (1)

Plu approached Lupist, who was waiting for the army of Hell, and reported.

"As of now, the asylum processing for 286 survivors has been completed."

While some, like Neid, remained in the Kingdom of Tormia, most fled to other countries.

Another pair of survivors, Lupist and Plu, chose to stay on the battlefield.

Though there was a high chance they would be caught in the explosion of the Elemental Bomb, they detested the idea of fleeing without a fight.

"Shall we go then?"

As the Tormia Allied Forces, joined by the Holy War, formed a crane wing formation, Lupist moved to the front.

"Let me join you."

Turning around, he saw Dante approaching.

If Lupist's memory wasn't failing him, Dante wasn't part of the assigned forces.

"Surprising. I thought you'd be in the bunker."

There was no intention of mockery.

To lure the demons to Bashka, many sacrifices were needed, not elite troops.

"I'm tired of being cooped up. When else would I get to enjoy the breeze? Actually, I'm curious. Why are the chairman and the chief secretary here?"

The Lupist Dante knew wasn't someone who let emotions sway him, no matter the situation.

"Of course, it's not just willpower that brought me here. Let's call it efficiency."

"Efficiency?"

"Anyway, I won't die."

Was it excessive confidence? Perhaps, but his words changed the eyes of the surrounding commanders.

'Being able to speak positively even in the most desperate situations—that's what makes a leader.'

A messenger's shout came from the side.

"Enemy sighted!"

Looking ahead, they saw the blood-red army of Hell surging from the horizon.

"First, we need to break their spirit."

As Lupist moved, forming steel in his palm, Dante stepped forward.

"Leave that role to me."

"You?"

It wasn't that Lupist doubted Dante's skills, but the enemies they faced now weren't ordinary demons.

The reduced number of demons compared to the start of the war meant that the survivors were elite and veterans.

"If you fail, it won't just be your death. The army's morale will be crushed."

As Dante passed by Lupist, he spoke.

"Do you know why I'm the final manager of the Flower Field? Despite my low Red Line rank?"

While he had excellent skills in handling information...

"It's because I'm the kingdom's best defender."

As the demons approached within 2 kilometers, the ground began to bounce like a rubber sheet.

Plu looked back at Lupist.

"Chairman, are you really going to let him go?"

"Let's watch for now."

If he were someone who didn't know his place, he wouldn't have made it this far.

Like a lone sailboat in the middle of the vast ocean, Dante stood alone on the plain.

'Central Calculation Magic Circle.'

As he activated Pascal, a magic circle over 200 meters in diameter appeared.

"Roar!"

Countless magic circles rotated and assembled into a three-dimensional mansion.

"Commander! Look there!"

Though Balkhan was far from the vanguard, he could clearly see it.

"Damn it! What's that?"

The sight of lasers spreading like a net and starlight boiling was astonishing, but the size was the problem.

'Something enormous.'

Dante's eyes widened.

"Information Castle!"

The starlight exploded into a blinding white light, transforming into an unbelievably large structure.

"Huh?"

The demons, realizing the shadow cast on the ground, simultaneously looked up.

"It's a castle?"

Seeing a life-sized castle appear in reality was bewildering.

"It's just magic! Push through it!"

Like a school of anchovies charging at a giant ship, the demons increased their speed.

-Welcome to Information Castle. Entering user setup mode.

Dante sat in the control room, observing the demons through the multi-screen display.

Not only was there no blind spot, but it also provided close-ups and panoramic views.

"Automatic control and targeting. Prioritize targets by proximity. Maximum firepower level."

-Settings confirmed. Enter the approval password.

Dante cracked his knuckles.

"Playground."

-Entering defense mode. Secured 287 targets. Firing in 4 seconds, 3 seconds, 2 seconds...

"Let's have some fun."

As the countdown ended, 3,000 cannons on the outer walls opened fire.

After Ikael left with Goffin, Heaven experienced a period of great confusion.

Though Anke Ra remained silent about Ikael's departure, the angels had their suspicions.

'It's all because of humans.'

As hatred for humans took root deep in their minds, expansion policies became popular.

Giants were sent to various parts of the universe, and one even arrived in the world where Shirone lived.

A giant who reached the 6th stage of the Elixir of Life disassembled its body, reaching the clouds, to proliferate humans.

A new humanity.

But Adam and Lilith, who had settled here long ago, no longer existed.

Time flowed on.

Omega 987.

Heaven, having overcome the absence of the Archangel, boasted its greatest prosperity in history.

Cities of giants, fairies, and Shinmin emerged, and fallen angels were managed separately.

"Sigh."

One day, as Satiel was lost in thought, something happened behind her.

"What?"

Turning around, she saw dozens of glass panels revealing a translucent boundary, sparkling.

Ashur's body assembled like a puzzle, and as the glass panels disappeared, the real figure stepped forward.

"Satiel."

"A-Ashur?"

It wasn't surprising that a 3rd-rank Mara had infiltrated the Archangel's room with her abilities.

'Ikael.'

Realizing the situation, she rushed to Ashur.

"What happened? Is the Archangel safe? And Goffin? What happened to him?"

"They are all safe, fortunately."

Satiel sighed in relief.

"Do you know how much time has passed? If they couldn't return for some reason..."

"Ikael is looking for you. If you wish to accompany me, I will guide you."

Satiel's eyes darted left and right.

"Without reporting to Anke Ra?"

"Yes. That's why I'm here." Though not as permanent as Goffin's Hexa, Ashur could also block signals.

"Sigh!"

Puffing her cheeks and exhaling deeply, Satiel paced the room in conflict.

'I hope she refuses.'

Even if Satiel sided with humans, taking someone from the highest place in Heaven was dangerous.

But Ikael had a reason to meet Satiel, and he understood it logically, so he had no choice.

"If you're uneasy..."

"I'll go."

Having made her decision, Satiel approached.

"I need to meet her now. Take me to the Archangel."

The fact that Ashur still called Ikael the Archangel was his only solace.

"Yes. Then, excuse me."

As Ashur activated the signal, hundreds of glass panels surrounded them, appearing flat from any angle.

'Move.'

With a clear sound, the glass panels shattered, and they were gone.

"Where is this?"

Arriving instantly at their destination, Satiel took in the scenery of the wilderness, where forest and sea met.

"Follow me."

Without explanation, Ashur hurried, and Satiel followed without complaint.

Meeting Ikael was the priority now.

'Will Goffin be there too?'

Feeling an unfounded excitement, she crossed the forest, and Ashur stopped in front of a cave in a cliff.

"Ikael, I've brought Satiel."

"Good. Let her in."

As Ashur stepped aside, Satiel walked in with a tense expression.

Even without her halo, the interior was bright, and at the end of the cave, she saw the person she had longed to see.

"Goffin?"

He turned his weary face, busy soothing a crying baby.

"Hey, Satiel. Long time no see."

Until that moment, she hadn't realized.

'A human baby? Whose?'

It was an event beyond the reach of an angel's mind.

"Satiel."

Regaining her composure, she knelt before Ikael.

"Archangel! Why are you hiding in such a humble place? Please return to Heaven with me."

"Thank you, but there's something I need to tell you first."

Ikael extended her hands to Goffin.

"Give him to me."

"Wait a moment. I think he wet himself. I need to change his diaper again."

"It's fine. Just give him to me."

Satiel watched in a daze as the baby was transferred from Goffin's hands to Ikael's.

"Archangel, that child is?"

"My child."

Satiel felt like she was hearing something incomprehensible.

"What?"

But when she looked up, Ikael was holding the baby with an incredibly happy smile.

"Can you believe it, Satiel? This is my child. Proof that Goffin and I are connected."

Satiel's mouth fell open.

"H-How can an angel and a human...?"

Goffin brushed his hair back and smirked.

"Haha! I'm a genius, after all. It's Hexa's technology. I call it Agape."

Satiel finally grasped the reality.

"The Archangel and Goffin..."

Ikael, showing the baby's face, spoke excitedly.

"Yes. Goffin and I succeeded. Now we can all be connected. No one has to be left out."

Of course, but...

"Satiel, help us. Our child is the proof, so Anke Ra will have to accept it. We can go further. This isn't the end."

Naturally, but...

"The reason I called you is because you're the only one who can do this. No one else who has fought for humans for so long could accomplish it. Please, Satiel."

Their achievement was too miraculous to end with just the blessing of two people.

"And one more thing."

Ikael added with a gentle look.

"Would you give this child a name?"

"A name."

A human born from an angel.

A being that could be linked to the Idea, more than deserving of a name.

But as soon as Ikael spoke, the first word that came to Satiel's mind was,

'Why?'

At this point, Shirone stopped accepting the records of Omega.

Records received beyond the five senses, up to the eleventh sense, were not clear by human standards, but...

'I can feel it.'

Because of that, it approached Shirone with a true clarity that transcended human thought.

'I can feel how much Goffin and Ikael cherish and love this child, with everything.'

Because of that, he couldn't go any further.

'I know.'

That Ikael's child eventually met a terrible death without even having a name.

'And how much Satiel came to despise humans after a very long time.'

Even without directly confirming it, he could guess what had happened to them.

'But...'

The child who would soon face death was not Shirone.

'Then why?'

When Igor revived Shirone's greatest fear within him, he clearly experienced the death of that child.

'It wasn't implanted information. It was my memory.'

From the child's perspective.

And the image of Ikael clutching her chest and sobbing sorrowfully was still vivid in his mind.

Shirone accepted Omega again.

'What happened?'

To understand what the name Hexa meant in the tragic events that befell them.
