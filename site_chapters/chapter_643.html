Chapter 643:

A star orbiting intelligence, and a satellite orbiting and assisting that star.

Lyman was speechless, holding out a badge too dangerous to carry around as a joke.

'Ivory Tower? Why is the Ivory Tower here?'

While he didn't dare to ask, Olivia stepped forward.

"I am Olivia, the vice-principal of this school. What business do you have with Shirone?"

"It's nothing major. This happens all over the world at this time."

Arte, slightly bowing, brought his face closer to Shirone.

"Congratulations. You have been selected as a candidate to receive the title of Star from the Ivory Tower."

"What?"

Not only the advanced class but also the graduating students were wide-eyed.

The vast three lines dividing the magical world.

Anyone aspiring to be a mage knows about the Black, Red, and White lines.

But since the Ivory Tower was just a concept to them, only students who liked to daydream expected it to have any connection to their lives.

Olivia, aware of this, asked with a blank expression.

"Shirone is… the best among graduates worldwide?"

She didn't know the Ivory Tower's selection criteria, but those who received the title of Star were always the best in some field.

So, if they were selecting a candidate from among the students, it had to be the best graduate.

"This time is a bit different. The first place is a bit ambiguous. That's why we call them candidates. Shirone ranked fourth by the Ivory Tower's standards and will undergo a simple test to see if he, along with the second and third places, is suitable to receive the title of Star."

"Fourth…"

Ranking by the Ivory Tower meant Shirone was the fourth best among graduates worldwide.

'Shirone, you're a real monster.'

Having seen the graduation exam, there was no room for disagreement, and the next thought was that there were three more people ranked higher than Shirone.

"Let's discuss the details separately. Originally, a 2-star resident would handle the selection, but since they are currently in orbit, I had to come."

Though his tone was gentle, it was also a request not to take up too much of a third-class resident's time.

"Shall we go?"

When Arte pointed his fan at the school building, Shirone looked back at his friends.

They all nodded without anyone prompting, and Nade mouthed silently, 'What are you doing? Go, you idiot!'

That desperate expression made Shirone nod automatically.

"Yes, I understand."

Arte smiled contentedly and was about to turn away when Lyman, who had been watching the situation, stepped forward.

"Please wait a moment."

The voice came from behind, but Arte didn't turn around and covered his face with the fan.

Even that small gesture hinted at his slight annoyance.

"What is it?"

"No matter how prestigious the Ivory Tower is, isn't this out of order? Currently, Shirone is a criminal who committed a serious crime against the kingdom…"

"Sigh."

Arte let out a small breath and slowly raised the fan covering his face.

"Wind Chime."

Ding-ding.

A clear bell sound rang in Shirone's ears.

The sound from the small bell at the end of the fan was too grand, and everyone's expressions hardened as if they had the same thought.

'Time…'

In the feeling of slowing down, only Arte's right arm holding the fan was moving.

"Officer! It's dangerous!"

As the guards, sensing the killing intent, passed by Lyman and drew their swords, the fan made a popping sound and pushed the wind backward.

"Ahhh!"

As the huge gust of wind blew, the swordsmen planted their swords in the ground.

It felt more like a wall pushing in than wind, and it felt like their flesh was being torn from their bones.

"Ahhh!"

They tried to withstand it with the pride of the royal guard, but the wind force was beyond human endurance, and finally, they all lifted off the ground and flew away into the sky.

Tokey, hanging on Arte's shoulder, put his hands over his eyes and watched as grass, trees, humans, and carriages all disappeared beyond the horizon.

Nothing remained in the place where the gust of wind had swept through.

Even the teachers couldn't close their gaping mouths at the power that had blown away even a single blade of grass, and a shiver ran down Shirone's spine as he watched.

'A magic of the Blow series.'

But could this even be called Blow series?

It was not wind but a typhoon, and it was not strange that Gaold came to mind with the power of a different dimension of magic.

"No matter how prestigious the Ivory Tower is, there are no repeated words."

When Arte flicked the fan with a displeased expression, the small bell rang.

Though it was separate from the magic, just hearing the tinkling sound made the students flinch and shrink their shoulders.

"It seems the message wasn't conveyed properly. Let me repeat. Shirone is a candidate to receive the title of Star from the Ivory Tower."

It was the same statement as before, but this time it was properly conveyed.

"Shall we go? I still have one hundred and eighty-seven problems in this world to solve. I hope Shirone can reduce it to one hundred and eighty-six as soon as possible."

Feeling an irresistible force, Shirone slowly nodded, and Arte turned precisely at a right angle and walked towards Alpheas.

"May we use the meeting room?"

"Of course."

From the school's perspective, having the Ivory Tower intervene would alleviate the burden of resolving Shirone's issue.

The only concern was the term "candidate pool" rather than a clear candidate.

'This might be Shirone's only chance.'

Watching Shirone and his friends follow Arte inside, Alpheas prayed earnestly.

'Please, let him seize this opportunity.'

Entering the meeting room, Shirone looked back at his friends at the door and glanced at Arte.

"Um, excuse me, but can my friends come with me?"

They had promised to help during the negotiation.

"Oh, no. We can wait outside."

Amy, quick-witted, waved her hands, but Arte didn't mind as long as it ended quickly.

"If it helps with the decision, it's fine. Please, have a seat."

Sure, here's the translation:

Getting a chance to sit with a star from the Ivory Tower was more difficult than an audience with the king, so Amy, Iruki, and Nade entered the meeting room with excited expressions.

As Shirone sat across from Arte, Tokey jumped onto the table and crawled over.

Tokey, with a tail like a spear, smiled expectantly as he swung it over his face.

"Nice to meet you. You seem quite different from your profile."

It was unclear if this was meant positively or negatively.

"Oh, yes. Nice to meet you."

When Shirone cautiously grabbed the tail, Tokey nodded as if that was the correct response.

In that position, Shirone asked Arte.

"You mentioned an evaluation. Is there a test?"

"Don't worry. It's not a particularly difficult test. The real challenge is that the Kar scores of the two competitors are higher than yours."

"Kar score?"

"Let me explain from the beginning. The most accurate way is the fastest."

As Tokey swung his tail, blue glowing text appeared in the air.

It was the record of the top four candidates from the documents received by the Ivory Tower.

Arte looked at it and said.

"These are the records collected by the Ivory Tower. As you can see, the first place is Ra Enemy, with a Kar score of 98.7%. Normally, this person would receive the title of Star, but…"

Arte paused, giving Shirone and his friends time to examine the records.

Indeed, the score was significantly higher than the second place, Dalia Nane, who had 89.4%.

"Ra Enemy… Wait, Ra Enemy?"

Realizing something, Shirone turned his gaze, and Arte nodded.

"Yes. He is the reincarnation of Anke Ra, whom you erased. Although it could be considered a subsequent life, the Ivory Tower views his incarnation as a continuation of his previous life. That's why there hasn't been any special movement in heaven."

Shirone spoke with a serious expression.

"But if Ra has been born, an incident will soon occur. Do you have any countermeasures?"

"Well, that's for humans to handle."

Arte added, waving his fan.

"The final war is certainly an important event for humanity, but it's not something that concerns all the residents of the Ivory Tower. At least, not for someone like me. Although some stars seem to be paying attention."

It was a difficult statement to understand.

"If a war breaks out, humanity could be destroyed, right?"

"It's not about life and death. What the Ivory Tower values is the balance of the world. I see war as a process of restoring balance."

Seeing Shirone frown in displeasure, Arte tapped the table with his fan.

"I'm not opposing your thoughts, Shirone. If you're interested in this, you could become a star and work in the Human Safety Enforcement Department. After all, we are also humans and would like to support humanity as much as possible."

It was a strangely chilling statement.

Since this was an interview for the best job in the world, Amy changed the subject to prevent further conflict.

"So, what is the Kar score?"

"It's the Ivory Tower's standard for finding those suitable to become stars. It's a metaphysical concept that's hard to explain in words, but if I had to define it, I'd call it perfection."

Iruki tilted his head.

"Isn't everything relative? What's the standard for perfection?"

"There is no standard. Perfection is simply perfection."

It was a concept that seemed understandable yet difficult to grasp deeply.

In any case, if it signified perfection, Shirone's score of 88.9% was remarkable.

'It means he's almost perfect.'

That's why he could be a candidate for the Ivory Tower.

'But…'

Amy's gaze shifted to the names Dalia Nane and Jin Seong-eum.

If the woman with the surname Jin was from the Jincheon Empire, she would be royalty, and Nane was 0.5% closer to perfection than Shirone.

"What kind of competition will Shirone face with them?"

"The Ivory Tower made an exceptional decision due to Ra's reincarnation. The top three people within a 0.5% range of Kar scores are given a chance. It's simple. Just…"

Everyone's eyes focused on Arte's lips.

"Find Ra Enemy."

"Find Ra…?"

"Yes. There's no deadline, and the method doesn't matter. Find Ra and return to the Ivory Tower to become a star."

Though it sounded as easy as breathing, it wasn't a simple task.

Even if they set out to find him, they had no idea where to start.

Nade asked bluntly.

"Isn't this just passing the job off as a test? You're asking Shirone to find someone the Ivory Tower can't."

"Hahaha! That's also true. Currently, there are 27 residents with the title of Star in the Ivory Tower. Due to their nature of traveling around the world, we are quite short on manpower."

"Short on manpower…"

"But Shirone."

Arte interrupted Nade and leaned forward.

"Becoming a star means gaining absolute authority that transcends nations. Even kings must follow the standards you set."

That's the Ivory Tower.

"If you can't even find Ra, you can't receive the title of Star. What do you think? If you agree to the test, I'll inform the higher-ups."

Shirone, who had been lost in thought with half-closed eyes, raised his head.

"No. I won't go to the Ivory Tower."

"What?"

His friends looked at him in astonishment, but Shirone remained firm.

"Hmm."

Tokey scratched his chin with a meaningful smile, and Arte's eyes gleamed coldly as he covered his mouth with the fan.

'As expected, the candidates are…'

Close to perfection.
