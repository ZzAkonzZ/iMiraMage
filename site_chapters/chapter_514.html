Chapter 514: Questions Left Behind (4)

"Find Anke Ra."

Gando understood the meaning of those words.

Judging by Uorin's reaction, the time when Ra attempted the reset was now.

In other words, it meant that Satan had been annihilated in some way.

It also meant that Ra had not found the answer to the remaining question.

"Even if a part changes, the whole remains perfect. But that whole is not the same as the whole before the part changed. However, through the ability of erasure, Anke Ra can perfectly erase only one concept."

"How is that possible?"

"The balance of the whole."

Uorin placed her hands on her knees in a cross-legged position and formed a circle with her fingers.

"When one concept is erased, a new concept replaces it. Since it was determined from the beginning, the whole does not change. Therefore, even if Ra is erased, the new concept to replace him is already in this world. I will now find it."

That is erasure.

Gando thought for a moment and then had a new question.

"Then the Goffin Erasure…"

"Yes. A new concept would have been inserted in the place where Goffin was erased."

Several possibilities flashed through Gando's mind.

"But that's not the important thing. I have the clearest base event as Mitochondrial Eve, but those with some foresight would also feel a strong déjà vu. We must find Ra before they do."

Gando understood.

"What do I need to do?"

"Protect me."

"Me…?"

Why him, of all people?

Even outside the door, the world's strongest swordsmanship group, the Wind Guards, were waiting, ready to kill.

"From now on, I will search for Anke Ra's new avatar through a history search. It's a complex and vast task that involves searching all of human history."

Gando couldn't even imagine the scale of that task.

"The history search is when Terraje is most vulnerable. If anything happens, take me to this place."

Uorin handed Gando a note.

After looking at the location for a while, he put the paper in his mouth, swallowed it, and nodded.

'So that's it…'

The Empress of Kashan is one of the most important figures in the world.

She also has the ability to protect herself, but when the history search begins, she becomes completely defenseless.

Strength is not important in the task of beheading a girl.

The most important thing is trust.

The reason Gando, Mistra's biological son, was given this task.

'And if I try anything foolish…'

Crouch would behead him.

In the end, the Wind Guards, Crouch, and Gando had to check each other and protect the Empress.

"Understood. You can trust me."

Uorin wasn't particularly worried.

Terraje's insight into humans, with all the records since the dawn of humanity, was as precise as a machine.

Gando, who had already given his heart to Terraje, would never betray her.

"Then let's begin."

As the door closed behind Gando, Uorin took a deep breath and closed her eyes.

History search.

A vast sea of information, as if containing the entire history of humanity, surged into her mind.

As all kinds of languages, cultures, and records were transmitted, Uorin's eyes trembled.

It felt like listening to the murmurs of tens of thousands of people in the center of a cave.

Gando took a position in the corner, within the bounds of the carpet, and watched Uorin.

After a while, her eyes began to glow as if they were on fire.

The light penetrated her closed eyelids, making it look like two gold coins were embedded in them.

'This will take longer than expected.'

All the events of the Goffin Erasure became base events, and the events after the third reset were overlaid on top.

Among the nearly infinite events, only one would be different.

'I must find it.'

Uorin's brow furrowed sharply as she increased her information processing speed.

'I must find it first.'

Zion, underground of the temple.

Inside the black sphere floating in the center of heaven's munitions factory, kinetic energy with a power of 2.19 million kilobusters struck in the form of a flash.

When the mass collided with the object, extreme heat was generated, burning the munitions factory itself without a trace, and the immense gas explosion expanded the internal cavity.

Rumble!

The ground of the temple swelled into a hemispherical shape, cracked like a turtle's shell, and red lava formed in the gaps.

The next moment, the ground, which had risen tens of meters high, collapsed, and shockwaves spread from the impact point.

The ground rippled like waves, and the structures on it crumbled.

The seismic waves spreading throughout heaven swept through Aravot.

Until then, many were still fighting for their desires, but at a certain point, everyone stopped and looked in one direction.

"Ah…"

The spire of Aravot slowly tilted, then snapped at the waist and began to collapse.

Both angels and humans knew what it meant.

The death of a god.

The end of mythology.

The eternal, singular being that had ruled over and controlled human lives had disappeared.

"You did it, Shirone."

Shina looked at the crumbling Aravot.

The light from Zone 73 had brought down Aravot.

Eventually, Shirone would be recorded in history as the one who killed a god.

"Anke Ra…"

Uriel withdrew the Ultimate Staff and looked towards where Aravot had been.

The fact that Ra had been erased was conveyed through the hollow sense of loss left in his spirit.

But conversely, like the intaglio of a print, the remaining concepts proved Ra's existence.

Goffin was erased, but those who had strong memories of him still knew he existed for this reason.

In any case, Aravot had collapsed, and Ra was gone.

Conquering humanity and subjugating them through the final war was ultimately Anke Ra's will.

For the members of heaven, it was like losing a compass in the vast ocean.

'What should we do now?'

Uriel looked at Ikael.

With Ra gone and the archangels lost, unable to operate the White Mirror, she was the only one they could rely on.

"Uriel, cease hostilities. Further fighting is meaningless."

"But Archangel…"

"My word is Anke Ra's will."

Only then did Uriel withdraw the Ultimate Staff.

Ikael used the term "Anke Ra's will."

This meant that Ra's will had not ended, so it was best to follow Ikael's orders for now.

"Listen well, humans."

Ikael, spreading her wings of light, flew into the sky and proclaimed to everyone, vibrating their spirits.

Those nearby and those far away all lowered their weapons and looked up at the sky.

"The war is over. You have won."

There wasn't even a murmur, and the silence was chillingly clean.

"Ra has been erased, and there is nothing left to control you."

As the reaction was lukewarm, Ikael drove the point home.

"From this moment, humans are free."

"Ah…"

"Uwaaaa!"

Cheers erupted from the surviving rebels all over heaven.

Humans had brought down a god.

It was a war fought with desperate resolve, but the vague fear of divinity that humans couldn't help but hold.

Overcoming that and finally regaining freedom made them forget the sorrow of losing comrades, even if only for a moment.

"We won! We really won!"

"Uwaaa! Freedom! Humans will never surrender!"

Ikael calmly accepted the cheers of the people flooding in from all directions and looked over at Shirone's group scattered throughout heaven.

Unlike the rebels immersed in the joy of victory, none of them were smiling.

At that moment, Miro approached and asked,

"What are you up to?"

Ikael lowered her altitude and looked down at Miro.

"Up to?"

"Why did you surrender? You could have wiped out the humans now."

If Anke Ra had chosen erasure from the moment the world opened, the angels he named shouldn't exist.

But he chose erasure 23 seconds before the destruction of heaven, right after Satan was annihilated, allowing the angels to survive the Anke Ra Erasure.

Ikael turned her gaze to the horizon of ruins.

"There is no special reason. With the will that moved us gone, the war is meaningless."

"…What do you plan to do now?"

"Wait. Judgment comes next."

Ikael turned her gaze back to Miro.

"Nothing is over yet. Humans have gained freedom, but that freedom can quickly turn into indulgence."

"Are you talking about Satan? But that's a choice for humans. Whether it's a god or a demon, humans judge only for humans."

Being selfish allows one to be altruistic.

That is humanity.

"Take care of Shirone."

With those words, Ikael spread her wings and flew into the sky.

All the remaining angels in the temple turned into flashes and followed her.

Miro, licking her lips as she looked up at the spectacle in broad daylight, scratched her head and muttered,

"Shirone?"

She saw Plu carrying Shirone from the collapsed spire of Aravot.

"Uncle."

Kangnan stopped crying.

The war was over, and the person she loved had gone to hell.

Gaold's peaceful face only tore her heart apart.

How many screams must Gaold be letting out in the deep hell where he couldn't even say he was in pain?

"I will never give up. Just like you."

As Kangnan turned around, Gaold's body fully leaned on her back.

As Kangnan turned around, Gaold's body fully leaned on her back.

However, with her broken, dangling wrist, she couldn't carry a man taller than herself.

"Need some help?"

At the voice from beside her, Kangnan turned her head, her eyes flashing with hostility.

The survivors of Cage B Team were approaching.

"Stop. If you take one more step…"

Kangnan's fighting spirit bristled like thorns.

The mission given to Cage B Team by the Magic Association was to eliminate Gaold, and now was the best time to achieve that goal.

As everyone halted, Tarvan, the interim leader of the team, stepped forward.

"Don't worry. We're really here to help."

"Do you think I'll believe you?"

In reality, Cage B Team was filled with anger, having lost their leader, Rose, and deputy leader, Horkin.

Moreover, Tarvan had to say goodbye to his childhood friend, Roche.

But for professional magicians, there was something more important than personal feelings.

The information they had gathered, scattered across heaven, would become global secrets.

If they could return to their original world, the Kingdom of Tormia would gain a significant advantage in espionage, which was far more important than eliminating Gaold.

"Of course, Gaold and you won't be able to set foot in the association again. But given the situation, we need some help."

With the Metagate's coordinates disrupted, the only way they could return was by using Miro's abilities.

Kangnan, who knew this better than anyone as the former secretary of the Magic Association, finally handed over Gaold.

However, her gaze was not kind as she watched Tarvan carry Gaold away.

"Not set foot in the association? No way. Gaold will definitely return."

Grinding her teeth, Kangnan waited for that time.

"Is it over now?"

Armin, who had found Kuan in the ruins, approached Miro and asked.

Shina ran over to check Kuan's condition.

He was unconscious but still faintly breathing.

After confirming Shina's sigh of relief, Miro turned to the other side.

Cage B Team was bringing Gaold and Kangnan.

With them, all the project members were finally gathered.

Fortunately, there were no fatalities, but all they had gained was the glory of their wounds.

Miro looked at the setting sun beyond the horizon and said,

"No. It's just the beginning."

Thus, Anke Ra was erased.

Leaving only one unanswered question in the world.
