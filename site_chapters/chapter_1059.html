Chapter 1059: Graveyard of Information (2)

From the start, it was a nightmare.

'Is this the effect of Dream Star?'

It wasn't easy for Yahweh's mind to be filled with anxiety, likely due to the brain's chemical reactions.

"No matter what, this is a bit much…"

As he stood up, countless corpses formed a wall approaching from the horizon.

"Yahweh, do you know those who died because of you? Do you know those you couldn't save?"

Shirone's guilt.

Behind the ideal of saving humanity, there were people dying right now.

All life ends in death.

The words Fermi had said before taking Dream Star seemed to linger in his subconscious.

"Feel our pain, our resentment!"

Even as the corpses approached with hostility, Shirone didn't move.

'This kind of dream.'

He wanted to alleviate his guilt by being torn apart by them.

"What are you doing?"

Fermi appeared among the rotting corpses, looking around Shirone's dream with interest.

"Hmm, I thought you were just a stern old man, but you have a surprisingly soft side?"

"Don't talk nonsense. This is your doing. What kind of drug did you give me?"

"Even if you say that, it's the next-generation Dream Star improved by the best drug designer. Fear and Eros have clear purposes, making address matching easy."

As soon as Shirone recognized Fermi's words, the forms of the corpses became more concrete.

Although they didn't seem blurry before, everything was a mental effect.

"Uuuu… Uuuuu!"

Between their tattered clothes, glimpses of chests and genitals could be seen.

As Shirone made a disgusted face, Fermi shrugged as if he understood.

"When two things mix, this is what happens."

Shirone looked away.

"So… what do I do now? Under Coder is outside the boundary of Dreamo."

"Nothing. Just wait until Dream Star is fully absorbed in your stomach. When your brain is half-awake, the dream will collapse, and then you go to Under Coder."

It was a simple method, but the waiting place being a nightmare was the problem.

The corpses, now close enough to show their teeth, were waiting for an opportunity.

The reason they couldn't attack immediately was that Shirone's mind was blocking the progression of events.

Blocking the progression of a dream was something even a pro diver couldn't do.

Fermi smiled with his eyes.

"You've changed a lot, that freshman brat."

During magic school.

"We fought a lot. Back then, you were so rustic, but now you're the Messiah who will save humanity."

"I don't want to talk about that with you."

"There's nothing else to do, right? In a dream, the concept of time is all mixed up. If you immerse yourself, it goes faster than reality, but like this, who knows how long you'll wait."

Fermi looked to the side and said,

"It reminds me of the senior survival test. Trapped in ice water, cut by blades… Did we reach the 7th stage? Oh, I withdrew at the 6th stage."

"Rrrr!"

The corpses took a step closer.

"Honestly, I thought you'd die at the 7th stage. If I knew you'd pass, I would've followed through."

"What are you trying to say?"

"Just… I feel a bit resentful? Anyway, I'm a loser, but this is a dream."

Fermi extended his hand.

"Maybe we can turn things around."

Feeling a chilling killing intent, the corpses lunged at Shirone.

"Raaaah!"

The escape route was in the air, but he would be intercepted by Fermi.

'Rage.'

The corpses caught in the explosion of light were torn apart, and pieces of flesh flew tens of meters.

In the open view, Fermi grabbed a chip made from a depreciation deal and put it in his mouth.

"Let's enjoy this."

Regulation-style - Unmanned Sniper. In the next moment, hundreds of bullets fired from 374 places in the air aimed at Shirone.

'It's called a railgun.'

The speed of the bullets accelerated through electromagnetic fields was something even an archmage couldn't react to.

"Ugh!"

As the Hand of God swept Shirone up, the bullets struck the ground.

The shockwave shook the earth.

"Raaaah!"

In the place where the corpses' flesh fell like hail, Shirone stood unscathed.

"Hmm, something I haven't seen before? A kind of… mental technique?"

Looking up at the giant hand of light floating in the air, Fermi smiled.

His eyes were friendly.

"You deceive your mind like a law. It's a good ability. But it won't work twice."

"Really?"

Feeling the genuine killing intent, Shirone responded sincerely.

"Shall we try?"

As the Hand of God spread its palm towards the sky, a giant photon cannon formed.

High-speed bullets and a powerful cannon.

Even in the imminent situation, Fermi calmly observed the Hand of God.

'He used scale to transcend the limits of power. It's typical of Shirone, but…'

He turned to face forward again.

"If it were me, I would've developed the trick further. No matter how strong the power, there's always an escape route. On the other hand, that technique can subdue the enemy 100% if the situation fits. It weakens on the second use, but that's the charm of a trick, right?"

Shirone frowned.

"What does it matter what magic I research? Mind your own magic."

"Haha, true."

As Fermi turned away, the ground shook, and the scenery began to crack.

The dream was collapsing.

"Time flies when you're immersed. It seems the drug is taking effect now. Let's get going."

Shirone deactivated the Hand of God but couldn't shake off his unpleasant feelings.

"So you picked a fight on purpose?"

"Well, sort of." He was a man whose true intentions were impossible to read.

The two, now light in the Under Coder, followed a blue line to the middle layer.

In a room filled with numerous mirrors, Shirone asked,

"Where are we going?"

"The Abyss. The graveyard of information and the world's final destination. It's also called the Apocalypse. Sounds familiar?"

Shirone had heard that discarded information was restored to forge his signature.

After passing through the middle layer, they touched a mirror leading to the Abyss.

As the boundary reversed, the world of the Apocalypse rushed into Shirone's eyes.

"What is this…?"

From a high vantage point, Shirone looked down with a dazed expression, and Fermi asked,

"Why? Surprised because it's different from what you expected?" It certainly was.

'Miro said the Apocalypse was a place that embodied a future world destroyed. But this…'

The entire visible landscape was covered in an unknown mucous substance.

"Is that mucus?"

"Yes. Since Nane left the photon system, the Apocalypse has been completely covered in mucus. According to investigations, over 98% of the surface is mucous."

Fermi pointed around.

"As you know, the Abyss is the graveyard of information. It's an end-of-the-world scene realized based on the real world. I think this predicts a cell buster in reality."

Shirone was quite surprised.

"How do you know all this? Even Omega's logs are very old records."

"As time passes, even a king's secret diary ends up in bookstores. You're revealing things to me now, right? So in the future, many more people will know."

"The future?"

At that moment, he realized.

"No way… you?"

What an audacious thought.

"Yes. The Abyss endlessly accumulates the future. Therefore, the longer something exists here, the closer it is to our reality's near future. So wouldn't it be possible to predict events that haven't happened yet? I call this time mining."

Shirone blinked.

"Since then, I've thoroughly investigated the Apocalypse. I've even picked up scraps of paper abandoned deep in the mountains. But those are still future information that hasn't happened in reality."

The irony of trash here becoming gold in reality.

"I collected such information as much as possible to predict and prepare for future events. The emotional disease is one of them. Of course, your decision to make a deal with me came after hearing this explanation."

"I understand."

Shirone felt confident in his past judgment.

"I won't ask about the deal. But how did you collect so much information? Even if it's trash, finding near-future information must be difficult."

Fermi blew his bangs away.

"It was really tough. Trying to do it alone didn't work out. So I hired them—the Parrot Mercenaries."

"Sister Marsha is here?"

Smiling, Fermi pointed his thumb at the lowlands covered in mucus.

"Want to go see?"

Flying through the sky, they arrived at a city center where a giant excavator was set up.

Marsha stood next to it while mercenary leaders sat on metal boxes drinking.

Fermi walked over with his hands behind his back.

"How's it going?"

"Huh? Fermi, what brings you here…"

Marsha stopped mid-sentence upon seeing Shirone and then shouted with delight,

"Hey, Shirone! Guys! Come here! We have a celebrity!"

The mercenary group now dealt with global matters, but Shirone's appearance still caused quite a stir.

"If it's Shirone, that's Yahweh! Deputy leader, didn't you say you fought him before?"

Freeman, still without eyebrows, leaned on a box with a bottle in his mouth and said,

"…I didn't fight him directly."

Marsha expressed her joy by ruffling Shirone's hair.

"You! How did you get even cooler? A man needs power after all, right?"

Marsha had a knack for making any conversation comfortable.

"You haven't changed. How have you been?"

"Haha! I'm dying doing things not meant for me—detective work, tomb raiding, and now construction. Who knew singing about mining would lead to actual mining?"

Marsha's face looked more haggard than before but her eyes sparkled like a child's.

"Are you drilling through mucus with this machine?"

"Yes. We're taking a break now but it's pointless anyway. Even if we drill through it regenerates."

Fermi asked,

"If even the tallest buildings disappear, it must be quite thick. Have you reached the bottom?"

"We hit solid ground early this morning at about 480 meters."

Shirone's eyes widened.

"480 meters?"

Marsha tapped the excavator.

"It's piled up immensely. Even if we drill down exploration is impossible. More importantly…"

The mucus underfoot wriggled and extended like tentacles wrapping around Marsha's leg.

The tip of the tentacle hardened like a needle and shot up just as Marsha roughly yanked it away.

"This sticky thing is driving me crazy. Ugh, typical—it knows how to be pretty, huh? This thing’s definitely male."

Freeman approached.

"It’s because you’re near the excavation machine. It seems to instinctively sense anything that threatens it."

"Freeman."

Before Shirone could even properly greet him, Marsha tossed the tentacle onto the ground and snapped angrily.

"Shut up. Then you should’ve been nearby. All you do is drink booze all day. You—curse."

"Curse?"

Freeman explained, "Her mouth’s gotten so foul that the crew held a vote. Marsha’s banned from swearing for now."

"Hmph! You think that’ll stop me? Curse, you little curse! I swear I’ll just curse you into pieces!"

"…Haha."

Shirone stared in bewilderment.

‘They sure live in an amusing way.’
