Chapter 1147: Trigger (2)

"Over there! Full attack!"

As demons gathered from all directions, Minerva extended her hand.

"Can you stand?"

As Shirone grasped her hand, a loud whooshing sound came from behind her.

Her jet, their means of transportation, was speeding through the demons, piercing through them.

"Damn it! Don't let them escape!"

As Minerva grabbed the jet, the two of them soared into the sky.

"Hehehe! Try to catch us!"

Though flying demons soon filled the sky, they couldn't match the jet's speed.

Once they were out of enemy range, the two circled the sky, wary of any pursuit.

With one hand around Minerva's waist, Shirone examined the wound from the sword.

Minerva asked, "Are you okay? How did this happen?"

"I was hit by Baal."

"To stop Deadly Cross, I had to take a hit. But this… it's not healing well."

If Miracle Stream couldn't heal it, other recovery magic would likely be ineffective.

Minerva said, "It's the power of Gangui, right? The intent to kill is embedded in the wound. Normally, it would be fatal."

"Baal will return."

"First, you need to recover. Let's go to the hideout. There's a place my subordinates use."

"…Alright."

After shaking off any pursuit, Minerva directed the jet towards the deep mountains of Huig's territory.

As Shirone walked through the corridors of Delta Headquarters, he grimaced and touched his abdomen.

The wound from the Ivory Tower throbbed.

"Ouch."

Though there was no actual sword wound, the quantum aftereffects were clearly felt.

'A finger injury doesn't affect other fingers. But pain is felt by the whole body.'

In that analogy, the severity of the injury could lead to death.

"Whew."

Feeling the need to rest, Shirone leaned against the wall and gazed at the sunset outside.

'The day is ending.'

Though no one in the Holy War would likely sleep.

"Shirone."

In the mostly empty corridor, as most had gone for dinner, a beautiful voice called out.

Miss Arachne, Varho Langi.

'Come to think of it…'

Though he had a connection with her, meeting her in the Holy War felt entirely different.

'Arachne's strength is seduction. And she is at the heart of that strategy.'

Why had she approached him?

Sensing Shirone's wariness, Langi made a desperate request.

"Let's go somewhere quiet. I have something to tell you."

"…Alright."

Miracle Stream enveloped them, and they flew through the window into the sky.

Upon arrival, Langi shivered.

"Gasp!"

Beyond the clouds, like a cotton blanket, the sun hung like a gold coin.

"Here, no one can hear us."

The dizziness that made lying impossible was a bonus.

Langi spoke.

"I attended Arachne's banquet. And… the person I entertained was Gis."

Shirone's expression grew serious.

"Yes. The tracking article in the newspaper, I was the informant for that incident."

The journalist's name was Meikle.

"The response was more explosive than expected, but we couldn't bring down Gis. It lacked credibility."

Shirone just listened.

"But if I step forward, we can definitely bring him down. Tonight, I'll meet Meikle. An article with my name will spread through tomorrow's newspaper."

"Why?"

As far as he knew, Jive and Arachne were allies.

"Arachne would suffer losses from that incident too. Why take the risk…"

Then he realized.

"That's why you could do it."

"Yes. From the start, Arachne intended to bring down Jive. In seduction, strategy seems important, but it's not. The side with nothing to lose ultimately wins."

Langi gave a bitter smile.

"If we bring down Jive, our reward is certain. I can't say which country, but… the problem is, I'll die regardless."

It was a predictable outcome.

"They'll talk about asylum, identity change, but they won't leave someone who knows the truth. I'll disappear without a trace or be killed and made to look like a suicide. Call it paranoia if you want, but…"

Langi looked at Shirone.

"The more insurance, the better. Just knowing you are aware will lessen the pressure on me from my country."

"You want me to make it public?"

"Yes. Lend me your name, Shirone. If I can include it in the article, I'll take responsibility for bringing down Gis."

For Shirone, troubled by Plu's issue, it might be a good outcome.

'But can I trust her? If she has ulterior motives like Gis…'

Shirone muttered to himself.

"Mika."

A voice echoed in his mind.

-Yes.

'Is Langi lying?'

-I cannot make value judgments. Her brainwaves and heartbeat are at a consistent level.

Clairvoyance provides all mathematical information in real-time, but interpretation is up to the observer.

For example, Mika can track all keywords related to 'death' at this moment. However, she cannot determine whether the emotion behind the word 'kill' is sincere or a joke.

'Anyway, her current brainwaves and heartbeat are consistent. I can detect changes based on this.'

More importantly, bringing down Gis would be beneficial for Tormia as well.

"I'll allow it."

As the sun set, Shirone returned to Delta and moved to the Tormia sector. Opening the door to the think tank, he found Lupist receiving a report from Plu, who had just arrived.

"After that conversation, we had dinner and parted ways. The next meeting is tomorrow afternoon."

Albino nodded.

"It seems we've made some progress. Though she still seems cautious."

Shirone, roughly guessing the context of the conversation, sat down without a word.

'The bomb will go off tomorrow morning. Plu won't be able to meet Gis anyway.'

Unaware of Shirone's intentions, Lupist looked at him curiously.

'I thought he'd react like before. Could he really have decided to follow my words?'

No, that couldn't be.

'He's not one to give up. There must be a new variable. Of course, he won't tell me…'

Albino's expression also seemed unusual, prompting Lupist to devise a plan.

"It's intelligence from the National Intelligence Service."

He intended to probe Shirone's thoughts.

"We have information on Gis's preferences. It's a bit ridiculous, but it's top-secret. He's a pervert. Read it. It might help you formulate a plan."

Plu quickly skimmed through the document.

Her expression didn't change, which was expected for Lupist.

"Gis is always guarded by 20 bodyguards, even when he's with a hostess."

Plu handed over the document.

"Shinjang. They're the fastest-reacting bodyguards among the 12 nations of the Holy War. Assassination will be difficult."

Seeing no reaction from Shirone, Lupist couldn't hold back any longer and asked,

"Are you okay? I thought you'd be furious. You hated this strategy."

"I still do. But there's no choice. You wouldn't listen if I tried to stop you. And isn't the cooperation with Tormia on this matter postponed?"

Lupist realized.

'Something's up.'

Drawing a line with Tormia meant he preferred to act independently.

'We didn't finalize it this way in the afternoon meeting. Something must have happened since then.'

He wanted to ask directly but knew he'd have to abandon the seduction strategy to get an answer.

'Hmm.'

Lupist pondered deeply.

Dante, who had introduced the mage Liria to his subordinates, immediately began the investigation.

"Look at this document."

Liria skimmed through it and put it down.

"I don't know. I'm not an information person."

"I've explained everything. Just classify which pattern it fits among the ones I suggested."

She examined the document closely and finally nodded.

"I see. You know the information has changed but not why, right?"

"Exactly."

"But there's an expert for this. Shirone. He's well-versed in both information and law."

Dante lit a cigarette.

"That's true, but I need to trust him. I do trust him, but I can't."

"I get it. But I'm the same. I can't fight for the kingdom. I'm here for humanity's peace."

"It's fine. Just classify it."

Since Liria couldn't decode the cipher, Dante felt safe entrusting her with the task.

It would be a lie to say he had no conflicts…

'Yes, this is right. If I left it to Shirone, he'd decode everything with Ultima.'

Though not an enemy, their goals differed.

"Alright. I'll do it." Liria took out a pen and flipped through the document.

"Hmm. This is good fortune, and this is a case where fortune turned into misfortune. In this section, great fortune continues…"

If Dante's analysis was perfect, Liria's words wouldn't be wrong.

'It's like weather forecasting.'

Pretending to smoke, Dante decoded the cipher as she analyzed the document.

'So that's it.'

He finally understood the meaning behind the incident.

"It went wrong at this point. They say it's cursed, but this red line…"

Liria's hand stopped abruptly on a page.

After a long silence, Dante put out his cigarette and asked,

"What's wrong?"

"This… it's a deadly curse. The great fortune we've had so far will all dry up."

"Let me see."

Sensing the gravity of the situation, Dante grabbed the document and read the cipher.

'It's the alliance code. The alliance structure of 11 nations against the Mun Kingdom. Why is this bad?'

According to Dante's analysis, the most dangerous country was the Middle Eastern Paras Kingdom.

Liria added,

"It's not just the deadly curse. It's the first law that underwent the most significant change due to the pyramid construction. The information at the bottom. I don't know what it is, but it's turning the curse into a great disaster."

Dante silently checked the spot.

'Alliance code…'

Kashan.

This is what they call the meeting of kings.

"Code One." Unlike Code Two, the method used by officials to meet, Code Four had a different weight.

"Prepare for all contingencies."

Munryong entered the temporary barracks 20 kilometers from Delta.

"Welcome, King of Mun."

Uorin, sitting at the end of the round table, smiled and offered him a seat.

Munryong clasped his hands and said,

"Thank you for coming out personally, Your Majesty. I appreciate your decisive action."

"Honestly, I was surprised that the Mun Kingdom proposed Code One. But…"

As the attendants brought out drinks,

"I believed you wouldn't disappoint. You are a person of great courage."

Munryong's lips curled up.

"Let's have a drink."

Kashan was one of the top contenders among the 12 nations of the Holy War, but he was confident.

'They won't be able to refuse.'

The great deadly curse.
