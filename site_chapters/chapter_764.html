Chapter 764:

"They're really relentless."

The nanites commanded by Maraduke were called Solok, a sweeping force even within the Underworld, where microorganisms could not exist.

A being beyond Law.

Demons (Ma) were not bound by the Law because they belonged to the psychic domain created by humans.

Maraduke's demonic energy, which defied physics, was also the power of Solok acting in the Underworld.

If I hadn’t blocked it with Dark Sphere…

He would’ve died.

“Fuuuuh…”

As Choeoni Bardo was released, the scenery of the Underworld burned away like paper on fire.

In the ruins—something that would’ve taken humans at least a month of battle to create—Maraduke stood solemnly.

“…That was an excellent decision.”

Shirone glared at him, but the aftershock of the 100,000-fold Quantum Superposition scrambled his focus.

Getting past this point… is really hard. I should consider this my current upper limit.

Dramatically reducing Kar values was the only way to retain identity within the mental infinity he was accessing.

Depth of mastery…

If Miro or Veron had reached the level of Yahweh, it would’ve been beyond this.

Why is my own mastery still so shallow?

Planning 100,000 simultaneous events wasn’t something within human capacity, but Shirone only felt frustration.

"Only extinction can stop ruin."

Maraduke let his greatsword hang and slowly advanced.

"Hahahaha! Hahahahaha!"

His laughter was deeply unpleasant, but in Shirone’s still-unsettled mind, it bought him some precious time.

"This is Yahweh?"

As his laughter ceased, an ominous black flame erupted from Maraduke’s body.

"This light… won’t stop me."

He vaporized his body and suddenly appeared in front of Shirone, swinging his sword.

“No demon ever repents.”

Shirone couldn’t react.

My mind’s still scrambled…

He had stacked 100,000 Dark Spheres just to generate an 8-meter gravity field.

Valhalla Action!

As events increased, control became harder and intensity weaker.

Computationally impossible!

And yet, this was also Shirone’s signature efficiency.

Valhalla Action!

A pursuit of perfect possibility.

If all variables could be removed—then total perfection was the fastest and strongest path.

There it is!

Perhaps that’s how he had reached the realm of infinity.

“Haa! Haa!”

Constantly perfect—then surpassing that perfection again and again. For someone who had reached this height…

“You wield a fascinating Law.”

…what remained?

“It certainly isn’t something human.”

Maraduke gazed with interest at the Valhalla Action magic circle floating above Shirone's head.

I can’t compute it.

Because demons stood outside Law, Valhalla Action could not establish an equivalent exchange with them.

Dodging was the best I could manage.

The second attempt at computation had only allowed Shirone to evade.

No fancy ability. Just raw power.

Evading Maraduke’s blade had cost Shirone a shocking 1.87 seconds in his own time—an eternity at their level.

–Angels that cannot be broken…

Finally, he understood what Minerva meant.

Ataraxia, Valhalla Action—these are the pinnacles of Laws shaped by angelic senses…

But demons resided in opposition to Law.

That’s why—chaos.

Maraduke lunged.

“What do you desire?”

Not even temporal displacement could handle this. Shirone reactivated Valhalla Action.

“Guh!”

To move just 16.7 meters while ignoring time, he had to buy another 4.3 seconds.

“Human happiness?”

Maraduke seemed amused as he leisurely flew after him.

I can’t keep running forever!

Like compound interest, each use of Valhalla Action multiplied Shirone’s time debt.

But if he didn’t dodge now, he’d be beheaded—so he had no choice but to keep stalling.

“You’ve succeeded, Yahweh.”

Maraduke gathered every Solok particle in the Underworld, black smoke erupting from his body.

“To make a demon feel sympathy…”

His apocalyptic demonic energy swept out, engulfing a four-kilometer radius around Shirone.

There was no sound, but the entire space—enough to hold a small town—vanished from the world.

“Guhhh!”

Four kilometers away, Shirone collapsed, unable to move.

“Pain and pleasure are sensations…”

Maraduke appeared in an instant.

“But what delusion gave birth to happiness?”

Even knowing it was futile, Shirone triggered Quantum Superposition through Valhalla Action.

Thousands of Shirones moved—but Maraduke wasn’t someone who could be outnumbered.

“No matter how you struggle…”

Each swing of Maraduke’s sword erased countless events from existence.

“You can’t find what doesn’t exist.”

He moved faster than temporal displacement, seized one of the clones, and drove his sword through its forehead.

It’s fine. That’s not the real one.

The cold sensation pierced the clone’s skull—and that iteration of Shirone died.

“Graaagh!”

Jet’s razor-sharp spearhead plunged into Commander Beshum’s mouth, burst through the back of his skull, and pinned him to the ground.

“Guhh…”

Having already taken down the other three commanders, Minerva leaned against a collapsed wall, gripping Jet’s shaft.

“Phew.”

Blood streamed from her lips, her clothes in tatters, exposing much of her body.

With Jet still lodged in his mouth, Beshum muttered,

“…You're strong.”

Even from a demon’s perspective, she didn’t seem human.

“I’m not strong.”

Minerva, ripping a strip of cloth to press against the wound on her shoulder, replied,

“I just fight well.”

“…”

As he absorbed her words, Beshum looked toward the battle between Shirone and Maraduke.

“…Why haven’t you killed me?”

“I will. When I feel like it.”

“Not just an excuse?”

“Excuse?”

His torn jaw twisted into a smile.

"You can't defeat our Legion Commander either. Are you just planning to survive by claiming you did your part?"

Minerva's gaze pierced through to Maraduke.

"...I won’t deny it."

There was no need to throw away her life.

"But if I’m being honest, it leaves a bad taste. Just… something about that kid."

"Yahweh’s insufferable."

Minerva laughed.

"Haha! Exactly. People live, people die. Isn’t that life? In another sense, he’s just obsessed with purity."

"You like him."

Minerva leaned her forehead against Jet and looked down.

"Why do you think that?"

"There are only two kinds of people who deny Yahweh. Those who hate him terribly… and those who love him terribly."

"You don’t think I’m in the first group?"

"It’s the same either way."

Why do demons hate Yahweh?

"Hmph, how dignified—just what you'd expect from a division commander."

As Minerva spun Jet in circles, Beshum’s limbs convulsed violently in agony.

"Grrrh…"

Letting go of Jet, Minerva brought her long pipe to her lips.

"At first, everyone wants to be perfect."

As the pain faded, Beshum tilted his pupils toward the sky again.

"Anyone can line up pencils neatly. And then they think: This is beautiful."

Minerva’s pipe traced invisible pictures in the air.

"That’s how the journey toward perfection begins. Build a house, add more floors, believe you have to be happy, that your life must be flawless."

The drawing dissolved into smoke and crumbled.

"Then one day, you realize it—can I really become perfect?"

"Humans are foolish."

"Yes. They disappear, leaving only the craving for perfection behind. When they could’ve just let go a little and lived."

Minerva turned toward Shirone.

"If you believe a world without a single pain can exist, then that’s surely obsession."

Shirone, the 2,458th one, pierced by Maraduke’s blade, died in agony.

It’s fine. I’m not the real one.

But they were all Shirone.

"That’s why it makes me angry. Yahweh’s love for the whole is noble, but…"

Minerva’s smoke drifted long and slow into the air.

"Shirone, where are you in that whole?"

Maraduke, having cleaved through all the Shirones, finally seized the neck of the original.

"Heheheh… pathetic Yahweh."

Minerva shook her head.

"That’s why he can’t win."

Love without self-love—such benevolence is nothing but arrogance in sacrifice.

"His obsession with perfection causes everything to fall into the realm of impossibility."

Shirone was powerful, but he didn’t fight well.

"He wants to take <Lawslayer>, slay Extreme Evil, save the world, close the Altars, and not lose a single soul…"

And Minerva liked that about him.

"But let it go."

Even the filth she couldn’t hide before any saint seemed to become equal in front of Shirone.

"So what if the world dies today?"

That’s why she liked Shirone… just a little more than she liked Nane.

"It’s not your fault, Shirone."

But if he couldn’t realize it—then maybe it was time to hand the world back to Nane, she thought as she looked down at Beshum.

"May you be reborn in paradise…"

He was already dead.

"Khahahaha! Khahahahaha!"

Clutching Shirone’s lifeline in both hands, Maraduke burst into raucous laughter.

His beautiful appearance now fully betrayed by the cruel nature of a true demon.

"To see Yahweh in tears!"

On the brink of suffocation, bound by Valhalla Action’s accumulating debt, hot tears streamed down Shirone’s face.

I’m sorry… everyone…

He couldn’t protect the world.

Because of me… because I was too weak…

Just like how Jaycee lost her husband, countless others would now suffer and weep blood.

"Any last words? I’ll make sure it’s immortalized in demon history."

Amy.

At the mention of final words, the first thing that came to Shirone’s mind was Amy’s face.

I want to see you.

The only person Shirone ever truly loved.

If only I’d listened to you back then…

If only he hadn’t ignored the voice in his dreams, screaming for him not to go.

"Then I’ll record it like this."

Maraduke, having run out of patience, said,

"Yahweh didn’t even make a peep."

Shirone trembled and slowly raised his head.

"The reason I understood benevolence…"

The reason he loved all things that existed—

"Was because I didn’t want to lose even one person."

From Maraduke’s arms, an untouchable vibration surged upward.

"Die! You wretched thing!"

Maraduke poured his full strength into snapping Shirone’s neck—but an even greater force was pushing his fingers away.

"W-what is this…?!"

Shirone’s entire body began to emit radiant light—an overwhelming presence beyond even Quantum Superposition.

"Yes, Shirone. If you truly want to save the world with Yahweh’s light…"

From afar, Minerva murmured,

"You must surpass fullness."

The ninth and final sense a living being could reach—Resonance—was awakening within Shirone’s body.
