Chapter 423:

"Th-this can't be…"

The gatekeeper was at a loss for words.

As far as he knew, Shirone was a Nephilim who wielded light magic, but he had destroyed Oprica with dark magic.

The problem was that it was broad daylight, with the sun shining brightly.

Using the power of darkness in a world filled with the essence of light was impossible unless one was free from the laws.

"Are you going to continue?"

Plu pulled out the magic staff 'Phoenix' from her cubrick and swung it.

Shirone had taken the initiative, but if she had been a bit slower, the Phoenix staff would have incinerated the prison.

The gatekeeper's face twisted in anger.

"Don't spout nonsense. We are the Nor. We are not swayed by insubstantial myths!"

As the gatekeeper cast the sound magic Peri, an ominous sound like a crow's caw filled the sky.

Three Nor warriors landed behind the gatekeeper, riding the wind magic Aeos.

They were Nor guards who had been on standby nearby.

"Hey, what's going on? You called all of us? Huh?"

A bald man among the guards frowned as he spotted Shirone's group.

"Who are these kids?"

"They're from the 1st Headquarters. That blond one is Shirone, the light of Sector 73."

"Shirone?"

The bald man stared at Shirone.

Like the gatekeeper, his expression twisted in displeasure.

"Ah, that Shirone? The one who sided with the foolish Mecha tribe."

Plu stepped forward and said, "Do you need to call more people? We're busy, so if you're going to do something, do it quickly."

The gatekeeper instructed the guards, "Don't underestimate them. Their magic system is different from ours."

"We know. It's much weaker than ours."

As Plu snorted and raised the Phoenix staff, Shirone placed a hand on her shoulder and stepped forward.

"I'll handle this, senior."

No matter how the Nor perceived Shirone, his crucial role in the war against Heaven wouldn't change.

To rally the tens of thousands of Nor under the headquarters, he needed to demonstrate the Nephilim's abilities.

"Alright. But make it quick."

As Plu stepped aside, Shirone slowly walked towards the gatekeeper.

"Take us to the headquarters. There's no need for a pointless fight, is there?"

"Hmph, you live up to your reputation. But do you know? The true strength of the Nor starts now."

The gatekeeper glared at his comrades and said, "Prepare the Mahagart."

The bald man looked puzzled.

"Mahagart? Do we really need to go that far?"

"Didn't you hear? He uses strange magic. It's best to end this in one go."

"Tch!"

The bald man reluctantly complied.

Regardless of rank, the gatekeeper's judgment was paramount at the entrance to the headquarters.

The remaining three followed the bald man's lead.

Ancient magic wasn't as individualistic as modern magic, but that trait could be an advantage.

It allowed for the casting of group magic.

As the surrounding air grew heavy and condensed, Shirone compressed photons in front of him.

The four Nor cast their magic simultaneously.

"Mahagart!"

A powerful wind, surpassing the density of gas, surged forward.

Once caught, it was a spell so powerful that it would slam the victim into a wall, tearing their limbs apart.

Gritting his teeth, Shirone fired the Photon Cannon.

As the heavy beam pierced the center of the air, the air rippled like waves.

Finally, with an ear-splitting crack, the Mahagart shattered into pieces.

"Ugh!"

The backlash from the Mahagart made the Nor's clothes flutter.

The sound waves alone were enough to make them feel faint.

"Th-the Mahagart…"

The Nor's group magic was as intricate and powerful as the Mecha tribe's third-stage combat system.

It was a spell that required perfect teamwork.

The fact that such a spell was destroyed by one person was a blow to the Nor warriors' pride.

Plu had already anticipated this outcome.

'Fools. They should have considered the difference in density.'

No matter how strong the wind, it couldn't push back a Photon Cannon compressed to a single point.

That's why modern magic had diversified air magic into press and blow types, allowing for various applications.

'He's trained a lot in a short time.'

The Photon Cannon's power was much stronger than when Shirone was at the Magic Association. His final year must have helped.

Having trained together in scatter movement and laser guidance, Plu was as pleased with Shirone's growth as if it were her own.

Shirone's group followed the gatekeeper deeper into the forest.

Though Shirone's display of power was shocking, it hadn't broken the Nor warriors' spirits.

They only obeyed their commander, believing she would lead the Nor to victory.

Even so, they reconsidered because the situation had surpassed their judgment.

They couldn't understand the principles behind it, but Shirone's ability to destroy a four-person spell in one blow was worth reconsidering.

"I'll guide you, but don't think we'll accept you. The commander will never agree to an alliance with the Mecha tribe."

Plu asked, "What caused the split? There must have been a reason."

It wasn't strange for conflicts to arise due to the differing mindsets of the Mecha and Nor, but in wartime, with Heaven as a common enemy, uniting forces was the logical course.

She didn't think they had split just because they were different.

"The Mecha tribe is arrogant. They think what they know is the best. The Nor have their own ways. But they didn't acknowledge that. War is fought to win. We don't want to join hands with weaklings again."

The gatekeeper pushed aside some shrubbery.

"We're here. This is our headquarters."

Shirone tilted his head. All he saw was a sheer cliff.

"This is the headquarters?"

As the gatekeeper cast the Peri magic, a distinctive bird call pierced the sky.

Soon, holes appeared in dozens of places on the cliff, and Nor sentries revealed themselves.

The ground shook as the cliff transformed into a fortress.

A door opened on the ground, and stone steps descended from above.

'Ancient magic.'

The Nor, using their affinity with nature, had magically shaped the entire mountain into a natural fortress.

Upon reaching the headquarters, formidable-looking individuals surrounded Shirone's group.

A tall, striking blonde woman approached with a dignified stride from the main gate.

She was Raesis, the Nor's grand magician and the 2nd Commander of the rebels.

"What's going on? The sound of Purin's bird call?"

The gatekeeper left Shirone's group and whispered something to Raesis.

Plu switched the drone's translation to an internal channel and amplified the sound.

A warning appeared in her retinal vision, indicating that sound collection was impossible. It seemed they were using magic to block the sound from spreading.

"Hmm, understood."

After hearing the report, Raesis nodded and walked towards Shirone.

A master of ancient magic with a Spirit Force of 32%.

Plu remained on guard, ready to act at any moment.

But Raesis's previously expressionless face suddenly brightened in front of Shirone.

"Welcome, light of Sector 73."

While Shirone was bewildered by the unexpected welcome, Raesis spread her arms and hugged him tightly.

She smelled strongly of perfume.

A scent as thick as blood.

Raesis led Shirone's group into the base inside the cliff.

At that point, Clove and Gardrak left, and the remaining two were guided to the commander's office.

The floor was carpeted, and two three-seater sofas faced each other.

To Shirone, it was a more familiar setting than the Mecha tribe's.

Raesis, seated at the head of the sofas, gestured for them to sit. Shirone and Plu each took a different sofa, as if prearranged.

A position that allowed for easy cooperation in case of an emergency.

Raesis noticed their unusual seating arrangement and smiled slyly.

"So, what brings you here?"

Shirone spoke.

"As you might have guessed, the 1st Headquarters has proposed an alliance."

Raesis rested her chin on her hand and crossed her legs.

"An alliance. What's the point now? What will we achieve by joining forces?"

Shirone relayed the words Sein had used to persuade Crude.

After hearing the explanation, Raesis also fell into thoughtful silence.

"Hmm, paralyzing the Aegis system. If that happens, it would be beneficial for us too."

"Once we enter Heaven, we can create variables. So why not join forces with the 1st Headquarters?"

"But before that, I have one question."

Raesis winked and asked.

"If we conquer Heaven, who will be the king?"

"King? What do you mean?"

"I mean, who is this alliance for? We were on good terms when we first started the rebellion. But now, aren't we done with the Mecha tribe? Even if I order an alliance, do you think the troops will follow? We've already split once. There's no guarantee they won't betray us again."

From the Nor's perspective, it was a valid concern.

As Shirone struggled to answer, Raesis continued.

"So, I have a proposal."

"A proposal?"

Raesis pointed her finger at Shirone.

"If the light of Sector 73, the symbol of the rebel war, joins the 2nd Headquarters, I'll actively persuade the troops."

Plu asked, "It's not a bad proposal, but does it matter? If we form an alliance, we'll be unified again."

"True, it doesn't matter. That's why I'm proposing it. The Nor aren't foolish enough to make an offer that will be rejected. But shouldn't we have some insurance if we win the war? If the Nor hold the symbol of freedom, it will be enough to persuade the troops."

Seeing Plu nod, Shirone agreed.

Just as the Mecha and Nor had their own circumstances, Shirone's team only needed to get the rebels to move.

"Alright. After the war, the Nor can use my name."

Raesis clapped her hands with a bright smile.

"Great! You've made a wise decision. Now, let's get you registered."

"Registered?"

"Since you've joined the 2nd Headquarters, we need to measure your Spirit Force."

Shirone and Plu stood in front of two adjacent changing rooms.

To measure Spirit Force, several tests were required, one of which was a physical examination.

Before entering the changing room, Plu spoke.

"If anything happens, focus on returning here rather than fighting. The most important thing is to regroup."

"Yes. You be careful too, senior."

As they entered their respective changing rooms, two drones followed their owners, splitting up.

Hearing the door close behind her, Plu looked around.

It was an empty chamber with another door leading further inside.

A Nor woman approached.

"Please take off your clothes and give them to me. I'll return them after the examination."

"How much do I need to take off?"

"Everything. We need to sterilize in the disinfection room and collect blood and hair samples."

Plu unbuttoned her blouse one button at a time.

Even though they had joined the 2nd Headquarters, Raesis was still not entirely trustworthy.

She could be ambushed in the worst-case scenario, but being naked wouldn't diminish her combat abilities.

'Well, it's not like my clothes have any defensive power.'

What intrigued her more was the Nor's examination process.

'Collecting blood? What are they planning to do with it?'

As she handed over her last piece of underwear, the woman placed it in a basket and said, "Um, you'll need to remove that ring too… Oh!"

Plu's eyes, which had been calm even while undressing, suddenly turned cold.

Staring intently at the woman, she raised her ring finger and said, "This stays. If the purpose is sterilization, the ring shouldn't matter, right?"

The Phoenix was stored in her cubrick.

As a tool that amplified her magic, she couldn't remove the ring even if she took off her underwear.

"Oh, yes. It doesn't really matter. This way, please."

The woman, pale with fear, hurried out of the room, and Plu finally relaxed her expression.

She had intentionally shown hostility to gauge the woman's reaction.

If the woman had tried to take the ring by any means, it would have indicated that the examination was a trap.

But the woman showed no such intentions.

'Maybe I'm overthinking this?'

Plu followed the woman to the next room.
