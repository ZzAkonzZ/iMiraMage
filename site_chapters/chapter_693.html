Chapter 693: Reunion Scene (2)

The king and high-ranking officials had anticipated the Ivory Tower’s arrival, but the pressure of facing the emblem directly was overwhelming.

King Adolf broke the heavy silence.

“For what reason has the Ivory Tower visited the Tormia Kingdom? Our kingdom has never acted against humanity.”

Tszoi’s lips curled coldly.

“You know things get complicated if I have to say it myself, right? It’d be better if you confessed willingly.”

The System Inspection Department was the Ivory Tower’s division most closely involved with global affairs.

Dealing with diplomatic crises, famine, refugees, and wars, they frequently met kings and knew exactly how to handle them.

“There was indeed a recent incident, but it was merely the elimination of a terrorist organization threatening the kingdom.”

Tszoi lifted her skirt.

“How rude. Peeking at a lady’s underwear.”

King Adolf didn’t even lower his gaze.

“Last chance. Mock me one more time, and I’ll drag you by the collar, king or not.”

The king’s pupils trembled as he clenched his jaw.

‘What does she even want?’

The royal mages and guards were equally furious.

‘Damn it! Does she think we’re scared?’

Even if Ivory Tower mages were powerful, they’d rather die than endure such humiliation.

But this wasn’t about personal pride—it was about the kingdom’s survival.

One wrong word could cost billions in gold, and if the Ivory Tower’s residents denied Tormia’s legitimacy, it would mean the end.

Adolf softened his tone.

“We have nothing to hide.”

Tszoi’s gaze turned icy.

“Still clinging to pretenses? Let’s see if you keep that up when I make you kneel.”

Just then, the grand hall doors burst open, and a group rushed in.

“Apologies for the delay, Your Majesty.”

Rufist, head of the Magic Association, gave a quick bow, followed by Shirone’s group, who scanned the room nervously.

‘That girl…’

Tszoi immediately stood out to Shirone.

Dark skin, curly hair tied into horn-like spikes with hundreds of rubber bands.

Even outside the palace, her overwhelming aura would have been suffocating.

“Who are you?”

“Rufist, head of the Magic Association. Also the person in charge of the Radum incident. If you have something to say, say it to me.”

Tszoi scoffed.

“This place is full of fools. The Ivory Tower doesn’t negotiate. Confess, and I’ll spare you the humiliation.”

“What do you want us to confess?”

“Typical of kingdoms…”

Tszoi lifted her skirt, making Iruki, Kido, and Rian stare blankly.

“What’s with those polka-dot panties? What’s your point?”

Kido pointed openly, but Tszoi ignored him, scrutinizing Rufist’s face.

“…….”

Then she suddenly turned and waved at the officials.

“See? It’s that simple.”

The officials, seeing no difference from the king’s reaction, frowned in displeasure.

‘What’s the difference? Is she just mocking us?’

“By the way…”

Tszoi turned back and pointed at Shirone.

“Who are you?”

Of all the people in the grand hall, only Rufist and this boy hadn’t looked at her panties.

“Arian Shirone.”

“Shirone? Ah, you’re Shirone?”

If he was an Ivory Tower candidate, it made sense.

‘His combat skills are decent. He’s gotten quite far.’

Not looking was harder than it seemed.

Avoiding something was still a form of observation.

True ignorance required complete erasure, like not knowing there was gold under a tree until someone mentioned it.

“Arian Shirone?”

The officials whispered, and even King Adolf studied Shirone closely.

‘That boy…’

The top graduate in the kingdom’s history, a talent he had let slip away.

The fact that Tszoi, who had no qualms about pointing at the king, moderated her tone for Shirone only deepened his regret.

‘Ivory Tower residents. I thought they were just bullies, but they do acknowledge some people.’

Perhaps there was a level he couldn’t comprehend, but as a ruler, pride was irrelevant.

“Fine. I’ll treat you like a human, just this once.”

Tszoi fully turned to Rufist.

“Bring me the Seed of Life now. I’ll destroy it myself.”

Rufist shook his head.

“Is that all? The Tormia Kingdom doesn’t have it. We never collected it in the first place.”

Shirone’s mind raced.

‘They didn’t collect it?’

After Ra Enemi’s disappearance, hadn’t Rufist gone to retrieve the seed from the fallen Anke Ra?

“Then where is the seed now?”

“I don’t know. It was never there, or if it was, whoever took it has it now.”

Tszoi gnashed her teeth, deep in thought.

‘Are these fools serious?’

Though Rufist was telling the truth, such psychological traps were how they deceived the Ivory Tower.

“You won’t regret this? You know this isn’t over just because you avoid the storm, right?”

“Of course.”

Rufist bowed.

‘This is something even the king can’t be told.’

After Terraforce the Grand Judge erased Ra Enemi, people began losing memories of specific events.

This global phenomenon, called “Amnesia Sickness,” had drawn the Ivory Tower’s attention to Tormia.

Thus, Rufist’s plan was to renounce ownership of the seed and weather the storm.

“You’ve thought this through. Pray today’s decision doesn’t bring disaster.”

“It won’t.”

Tszoi suspected there was a way to reclaim the seed, but from now on, it was a political matter.

“Let’s go, Musso.”

As Tszoi and Musso left the grand hall, Iruki addressed the king.

“Apologies for the disturbance, Your Majesty. We’ll take our leave now.”

The officials, now fearless, shouted.

“What? You think the palace is your playground? How dare you come and go as you please!”

“My apologies. But the association head here…”

“We only avoided disaster because the Ivory Tower let it slide. One misstep, and we could’ve suffered huge losses! And what’s with that goblin?”

The stress from Tszoi seemed to have gotten to them.

“Enough.”

Adolf raised his hand.

“Didn’t they rush here to assist me?”

Iruki played along.

“Exactly, Your Majesty. As they say, many hands make light work.”

Shirone paled.

‘Please stop, you lunatic.’

Though the joke bordered on disrespect, Adolf laughed heartily.

The future head of Yongrae, successor to Albino.

One day, the fate of the nation would rest on this young man’s shoulders.

“Indeed. You and Shirone are classmates, correct?”

“Yes, the best of friends. He came to me today for a favor. With that in mind, may we take our leave?”

Adolf glanced between Iruki and Shirone.

‘Friends, huh…’

He hoped Shirone would become a star of the Ivory Tower.

“You may go.”

“Thank you.”

As Iruki turned to leave, Adolf added,

“Ah, before you go, I have something to say to Shirone.”

Shirone bowed.

“Yes, Your Majesty.”

“It’s regrettable that I failed to recognize your worth.”

Though Shirone had faced setbacks due to social barriers, it had been his own choice.

Still, hearing it from the king himself stung.

“Your Majesty, I…”

Adolf continued.

“On behalf of all Tormia’s citizens, I sincerely apologize for the hurt I caused you.”

The grand hall buzzed with murmurs.

Though it was political rhetoric, Shirone felt something warm rise in his chest.

“Thank you, Your Majesty.”

Iruki and Rian were even happier than Shirone.

‘Finally, he’s being acknowledged.’

Of course, Rian knew better than anyone that Shirone wouldn’t stop here.

After leaving the grand hall, Shirone’s group sprinted down the corridor, worried Tszoi might have already left the palace.

In the distance, they spotted the two figures exiting the palace grounds.

“Wait! Please wait a moment!”

“What now?”

Tszoi turned with an annoyed expression, and Shirone ran faster.

“I have a question. About the Ivory Tower test’s passing criteria.”

“The Ivory Tower test? Ah, Ra Enemi? Why ask me? You should contact the Humanity Safety Enforcement or Balance Department.”

Even as the world teetered on the brink of catastrophe, the Ivory Tower’s departments remained strictly separate.
