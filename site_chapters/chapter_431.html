Chapter 431:

Secret Underground Laboratory of the 2nd Rebel Headquarters.

Raesis did not reprimand Shirone and Plu for the loss of two Special Mobile Unit members. While her subordinates wanted Shirone's rank revoked to restore the commander's honor, Raesis was more intrigued by the completion of Babel's exploration.

After isolating Shirone and Plu in their quarters, Raesis collected their blood samples again. The official reason was the potential infection by an unidentified pathogen, but the real motive was to secure a clone of Shirone, who had changed after entering Babel.

"The Tomb of Angels… So it was Babel? It really existed? I thought it was just a myth."

With Shirone confirming Babel's existence, Raesis had no choice but to believe it. If the myth was true, she could also guess what spirit Shirone had discovered there.

"Probably the Ultima System. If Shirone learned the ancient language, it would be an excellent opportunity for me."

Raesis approached an iron cage in the laboratory.

A grotesque creature was banging its head against the bars.

"Kiik! Kiik!"

Every time the cage rattled, the biologists recording the creature's state shuddered.

The King of Reproduction, Garas.

An extraterrestrial species with a reproductive instinct 100,000 times stronger than humans, capable of mating with 1,027 known species, including humans. Despite being biologically male, it perceives even men as reproductive targets.

According to academic reports, Garas had a 2% difference in success rates when attempting to reproduce with human females versus males.

Garas has no definitive form, as it can mate with anything from small insects to giant monsters. However, it shares common traits like the "protein palace" organ that manipulates amino acid sequences and rapid cell division to create reproductive organs.

Despite being ten times more intelligent than humans, Garas has not developed an independent intellectual civilization due to its sole interest in reproduction.

Whether it's the ultimate amusement of advanced intelligence or a natural occurrence due to their unique environment is unknown. What is clear is that Garas is a reproductive entity with the powerful ability to break down species barriers, making it one of the highest-risk isolated species in purgatory.

"Commander, why did you bring such a creature here? If it escapes, the headquarters will be doomed. The entire area will become a Garas colony."

"It's fine. As long as we manage it well. Without it, we can't produce Shirone's clone."

Raesis had successfully combined Shirone's genetic information with Nor eggs, but the slight differences in their evolutionary processes prevented implantation. Using Garas's genetic affinity as a bridge could solve the problem.

The biologist responded with a troubled expression.

"Then… we'll start today."

"No. Clone Plu first."

"What? Why… Could it be?"

The biologist's face turned pale.

Combining Shirone, Plu, and Garas could indeed produce clones explosively. But even a scientist who had crossed many forbidden lines felt a pang of conscience this time.

He was human, after all.

Made of flesh and blood, and deeply averse to pain and death.

'Is this woman really sane?'

That was the biologist's only question.

"Commander, is this really necessary?"

"We're at war. We must accept small sacrifices for victory. You knew that when you agreed to help me."

"But this is…"

Raesis interrupted.

"We don't have much time. If the Babel Shirone explored is the one I know."

"Why? I thought Babel was a weapon created by ancient Gaia to attack Heaven."

"Of course, but…"

Raesis fell silent.

There was no need to cause a commotion in the headquarters prematurely.

The one who sealed Babel back then was Archangel Kariel.

Niflheim

The world of the dead, where everything is frozen.

Before Gaold's group lay a vast ice field, surrounded by frozen mountains in the distance.

A world so harsh that ordinary creatures would freeze solid before encountering anything.

If Zulu hadn't summoned a Tier 2 monster, Salamander, to raise the surrounding temperature, they would have been in the same predicament.

After all, this was the place where the body of Ymir, the king of giants, was trapped within the "Wall of Eternal Ice."

The 3-meter-long Salamander lay flat on the ground, emitting heat from its entire body.

Yet, the ice didn't melt, and the Salamander shivered from the cold.

As the flames weakened, the Salamander's true form, a lizard, became visible.

Gaold exhaled frost and asked, "How many do we have?"

Kangnan checked her pouch.

"Twenty-four."

"I see."

Collecting Black Elixir was taking longer than expected.

Rebel 1st Commander Crude had said there was a 50% chance, but the actual probability of obtaining Elixir from capturing spirits seemed less than 20%.

Even so, it was a high probability, and with 24 Elixirs, they could buy anything on the mainland.

But the results were meager for the effort, and they had lost a significant amount of stamina.

'It's cold.'

Kangnan exhaled a long cloud of frost.

Wandering Niflheim for six days was madness for humans.

Zulu hadn't been able to dismiss the Salamander for even a moment, preventing her from sleeping.

Kangnan looked at Gaold's broad back.

How cold must he be?

If Kangnan was cold, Gaold was a thousand times colder.

If Kangnan's fingers were numb with frostbite, Gaold was in a thousand times more pain.

'How can he not show it? How is he enduring this?'

"We'll tire out at this rate."

Gaold looked at their guide.

A Nor with a rat-like face and scars.

He could navigate Niflheim because he had spent his entire fortune to contract with an ice spirit.

But in purgatory, no matter how valuable a spirit was, if one's magical skills were lacking, it was worthless.

The only job he could get was as a guide in Niflheim, a job with a high risk of death.

The terms were strict.

He wouldn't participate in combat but would take 50% of the items collected.

Gaold wouldn't normally agree to such an unfair contract, but after realizing how low the chances of obtaining Black Elixir were after their first hunt, he reconsidered.

Niflheim was vast, and encounters with spirits were surprisingly infrequent.

They would exhaust themselves or freeze to death before collecting enough.

It was better to head to "Hel," where the ruler of Niflheim resided, and hit the jackpot.

"How much further to Hel?"

"Ah, just a bit further this way…"

The guide, pointing ahead, finally gave up and shouted, "No! I can't go any further! Let's turn back!"

He was terrified of Gaold's group.

No one approached this land of death, but occasionally, madmen seeking fortune would enter.

The guide had explored Niflheim six times with such people.

Even finding one Black Elixir was profitable, and two meant he could rest for two more years.

Before meeting Gaold's group, he had barely managed to kill spirits on the outskirts, praying for Black Elixir to drop.

But these people were different.

They ignored the outskirts and headed straight for Hel, capturing nearly two hundred Grim Reapers in six days.

The Grim Reaper, the strongest spirit on the mainland, ranked Triple S.

When he saw the 5-meter-tall reaper on a black horse wielding a giant scythe, the guide nearly wet himself.

The thrill of finding Black Elixir kept him going, but he honestly wanted to go home now.

"Really? You can quit anytime."

Gaold agreed readily.

But he added one condition.

"If you break the contract and go alone."

'You bastard!'

The guide gritted his teeth.

He couldn't match their strength, so he had to endure, but the resentment burned deep.

With twelve Black Elixirs, he could live in luxury forever.

No, he would marry a hundred mainland beauties and have a thousand children.

It was a desperate fantasy, but the value of twelve Black Elixirs made it possible.

"Fine, let's go to the end! But my role ends at Hel. I don't know the way from there."

"Do as you please."

Gaold turned and continued walking on the ice.

Though a towering figure to everyone, Gaold's face was gaunt.

Zulu stopped sucking on her pacifier and pointed ahead.

"There's something there."

The guide's face lit up with professional pride as he scanned the area.

'I've lived to see this.'

The reason he could guide in the flawless Niflheim was the bodies of intruders over the ages serving as markers.

As Gaold's group approached, they saw over a thousand frozen creatures.

The times and species varied, but all had expressions of terror.

"Is this the entrance to Hel?"

"Wait. I've never been here. According to my research…"

The guide pulled out a notebook and checked his notes, turning away as if to keep them secret.

As the only Niflheim expert on the mainland, his notes were extensive.

"Hmm, from the body of a Katesis species frozen with one eye closed…"

The guide found a bipedal creature with a lizard face and a long tail and approached.

"The direction indicated by the left index finger."

The guide frowned at the left hand.

'Damn, it only has three fingers. Which one is the index?'

Guessing it was the middle finger, the guide pointed about 15 degrees from their path.

"Hel should be that way. So I'll… Eek!"

The guide fell back in shock as he looked at Gaold.

A terrifying aura emanated from the group.

"Th-that is…!"

A thick mist rose from the ice.

It was a familiar scene for everyone, but this time the situation was different.

A dark horse's silhouette emerged on the ice, letting out a high-pitched scream louder than a woman's.

On its saddle sat a Grim Reaper, shrouded in black smoke, holding a 4-meter-long scythe over its shoulder.

There were forty of them.

This was the maximum number for a single battle.

"Damn it! Dying for twelve Black Elixirs!"

The guide, having given up hope, cried in despair.

No one comforted him.

Zulu also realized this was on a different level of difficulty.

"I'll dismiss the Salamander."

With their stamina already significantly depleted, it would be tough for Kangnan and Gaold to handle this alone.

But Gaold pushed Kangnan back and advanced towards the Grim Reapers alone.

"Stay here. You'll get caught up in it."

"Are you crazy? What are you planning to do? And I'm not weak enough to get caught up in the middle of a fight!"

"I know. But…"

Gaold, leaning as if about to collapse, looked back at Kangnan.

"This time, it's different."

Kangnan's face turned to stone as she sensed something.

Gaold's hair, starting from his forehead, was rapidly turning white.
