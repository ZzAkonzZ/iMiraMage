Chapter 845: Participation (5)

Amon, buried under the rubble of a building, lifted a boulder the size of a house and revealed himself.

“Hehehe.”

The area hit by the photon cannon was deeply dented, with all the surrounding bones protruding.

“Detestable Yahweh.”

He lightly tossed the massive debris aside and spread his tattered arms.

“Do you know who I am?” Amon's voice echoed grandly in the silence.

“I am Hell.”

From the 1st to the 10th.

The reason Satan's direct descendants are special is that they are closer to a system than an individual entity.

They are known as Satan's Ten Great Disasters.

The demons call this disaster the Demon System, or Makai, and once Makai is opened, the individual's consciousness completely disappears.

‘Does it matter?’

They become a disaster.

“Blame your foolishness.” As Makai opened, worm-like tentacles burst through Amon's skin.

The sight of them multiplying at a terrifying speed and growing his body made the demons flee in terror.

“Makai has opened!”

On the other hand, the humans, unaware of the impending disaster, watched in a daze.

“W-what is that?”

Rumble! Rumble!

Amon's legs took root and expanded in all directions, penetrating the ground.

Buildings toppled like dominoes, and the rate at which the roots expanded was matched by the rapid growth of his main body.

Uorin's head tilted towards the sky.

“My God…”

A massive tree, large enough to dominate the capital of Kashan, was growing, twisting its black trunk.

Holes opened along the trunk, and tens of thousands of mouths of various shapes and sizes opened simultaneously.

“Hohohoho!”

The sound, unlike anything in the world, made Uorin's shoulders tremble.

‘That must be…’

Makai Tree Argantis.

In Teraje's memory, the descent of the Makai Tree into the world was an event from a distant past.

An era when dragons and angels clashed.

The Makai Tree destroyed seven ancient nations and sought endless expansion.

No one cared about humanity, which was still in its infancy.

Eventually, humans organized a world coalition and managed to repel the disaster at the cost of one-third of humanity.

‘It's different now. But…’

Watching Argantis pierce through the clouds made it seem like there was no future left.

“Oh God.”

Humans, having given up on fleeing, knelt one by one and began to pray.

It was the only action they could take.

“Hohohoho!”

The sound emitted by the Makai Tree was truly horrifying, but Shirone imagined something even worse.

‘No creature can be this large.’

Because the energy of a planet is limited.

‘Therefore…’

The Makai Tree, rooted in the capital of Kashan, sucked up the energy of the earth.

“Oh God, please save me… Ugh!” At the same time, humans dried up and fell to the ground like mummies.

The foundations of buildings turned to sand, and the sound of collapsing structures echoed from all directions.

The death toll was approximately 20,000.

Even more horrifying was the fact that the Makai Tree, having absorbed nutrients, was growing even larger.

“Damn it!”

Shirone spread his wings of light and charged. He couldn't be sure of the situation at the Ivory Tower, but it was clear that the Grand Star was not in normal condition.

‘Angel's Punishment!’

He maximized the incarnation of the Archangel, but it was still less than one-hundredth the size of the Makai Tree.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

Spears of light rained down incessantly.

‘It doesn't even budge!’

The base of the Makai Tree, with a diameter of 20 kilometers, appeared as nothing more than a wall to Shirone.

“Hohohoho!”

The second absorption.

Tens of thousands of humans turned into mummies again, and the entire ground of the capital of Kashan sank.

Whoosh!

A city-sized dust cloud rose, blocking Shirone's view.

‘If this continues, the world is doomed.’ Makai Tree Argantis.

It was the system corresponding to drought among Satan's Ten Great Disasters.

‘I have to defeat it before it's too late!’

If it were a matter of technical difficulty, it could be overcome with learning, but in this case, it was meaningless.

“Kill Yahweh!”

Voices of demons came from within the dust.

As Shirone flapped his wings and retreated, the cloud cleared, revealing swarms of flying demons.

‘Ataraxia!’

The information of amplification, accumulated in an instant, unfolded before Shirone's eyes in a massive circle.

Just before the demons filling his vision threw their spears, a photon cannon passed through Ataraxia.

“Kiaaa…”

The beam, fired with a roar, swept away the demons and struck the trunk of Argantis.

“Argh!”

The beam, amplified to its maximum output, moved horizontally, scraping the trunk of the Makai Tree.

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

‘This isn't enough! Stronger, stronger!’

As the explosion from Argantis shook the heavens and the earth, people cowered and covered their heads.

“Ugh! Ugh!”

It already felt like they were in hell.

When the beam of the photon cannon had traveled approximately 10 kilometers, the energy of amplification dissipated.

“Hah! Hah!”

The tree, with grooves carved by the shockwave of the beam, began to tilt sideways under its weight.

“It’s falling!”

As the top tilted 15 degrees, black branches spewed from the mouths on the trunk.

“Grrraaah!”

Hundreds of thousands of black branches extended from the falling direction, supporting the weight.

Though it was fast, to Shirone's eyes, it appeared slow, making it even more repulsive.

“Hohohoho!”

In a posture as if it were propping itself up with one hand, Argantis rooted itself again and absorbed more nutrients.

The ground sank further, causing the imperial city of Aganos to shake precariously.

“Your Majesty! We must go now!”

As Uorin, led by the Wind Guard, flew into the sky, she shuddered at the sight below.

The entire city was drying up.

“Ugh!”

In the empty Great Holy War, the Grand Star knelt and hugged herself with both arms.

Every time the planet's nutrients were sucked up, it felt like dozens of syringes were simultaneously drawing her blood.

‘I can't let this continue. I have to stop it.’

As the incarnation of Gaia, she had the ability to block the energy circulation of the planet.

But stopping the engine of a massive planet was a last resort.

It would take time to restart the circulation, and in the meantime, many places would rot.

‘Destroying is easy, but restoring is hard. I can still hold on. I can still…’

The Grand Star's shoulders twitched.

“Argh!”

The planet's energy was sucked out with twice the force as before.

“Hurry, hurry…”

With a haggard face, the Grand Star checked the ground and saw the four Great Stars rapidly approaching.

As Freed's strike cut down the demons, the capital of Kashan came into view.

No, could it even be called a human city?

“Damn it! Is that the commander?”

Civilization had disappeared, leaving only a massive, black tree standing crookedly.

Flying beside the running Freed, Minerva spoke.

“No, that's a phenomenon. A disaster. It's the demon system you said didn't matter, Freed.”

Freed gritted his teeth.

“…I get it, so stop nagging.”

Amanta, sitting cross-legged on the rotating World Wheel, spoke.

“With no Buddha, such things run rampant. Anyway, what will we do? It's a bit much to handle with force.”

Indeed, without Nane's Sermon, it seemed there was no easy way to resolve this.

“What else can we do? Let's try. First, we'll join Shirone and then figure out a plan.”

“There is a way.” Xing, trailing dozens of afterimages, approached while standing still.

“With my law, I can temporarily suppress the growth of the Makai Tree. Then we seal the capital and blow it up.”

“Blow up the city? Where will we get the firepower?”

Amanta agreed.

“The World Wheel. Sacrificing the city's population should provide enough firepower.”

“Shirone won't allow it,” Freed said.

“If the Makai Tree grows any larger, it will be a bigger problem. We have to accept some sacrifices. They're all dying anyway.”

“It's not a matter of numbers.”

As the Great Stars fell silent, Xing spoke.

“I'll try to persuade Shirone. If he doesn't agree, we'll have to fight individually.”

Finally, as they passed through the city, a hell unlike anything seen from afar unfolded.

“Graaah!”

People were drying up by the second, and the buildings and sky were filled with demons.

“Shirone!”

Minerva increased the thrust of her jet and flew into the sky.

“Minerva!”

Shirone, who had been fighting alone, turned to her with a weary face.

“How is it?”

“No matter how much I attack, it's useless. It absorbs nutrients and recovers from the impact.”

Minerva glanced at Xing and spoke.

“There is a way. Go down for a moment with Xing…”

“I know what you're thinking.”

Shirone, without taking his eyes off Argantis, spoke.

“But that won't stop it. No, we can't win against the demons that way.”

“This isn't the time to argue.”

“Listen.”

At Shirone's words, Minerva quietly listened.

From the countless mouths on Argantis, whispering voices leaked out.

“I want to eat. I want to eat more.”

“I will eat it. I will grow bigger. I won't give it to anyone. Only me, only me, only me!”

Minerva swallowed hard at the countless voices hidden within the screams.

“They are demons created by humans. If we use the World Wheel to destroy the city, that monster will surely disappear, but…”

The resentment of the humans sacrificed here will become demons again, bringing an even greater disaster.

“I agree with that.”

Xing had approached unnoticed.

“But if we don't stop it now, we'll face even greater sacrifices. This is the best choice.”

Shirone bit his lip.

“There is a way to destroy the city without sacrificing humans.”

Shirone turned to Xing.

“How long can you hold it?”

“At its current size… about five minutes?”

Freed shouted as he cut down the massive roots protruding from the ground.

“Whatever it is, do it quickly! It's still growing!”

There was no time for further thought.

“Leave it to me. I'll handle it.”

As Shirone and Minerva descended to the ground, Xing looked at Argantis.

“…An illusion.”

She raised her hand slowly, taking in the Makai Tree, which had turned the size of imagination into reality.

“I forbid the demon. I forbid devouring, I forbid digestion. I forbid activity, I forbid freedom.”

Xing's law began to suppress the law of Argantis.

“Hohohoho!”

Crack. Crack.

As the nutrients were suddenly cut off, the massive branches twisted grotesquely as if their bones were breaking.

“Therefore…”

Xing extended the bracelet on her wrist.

“I forbid everything about you.”

Law, enforced.

“Graaah!” The simultaneous roar from the mouths of the Makai Tree caused many to lose consciousness.

“Grrr.”

The law was a kind of seesaw game.

As Argantis writhed in agony, wrinkles formed on Xing's face.

While fighting the demons, Minerva assessed the situation.

‘Anyway, we've bought some time. But how on earth will we deal with that monster…’

Her eyes widened as she looked at Shirone.

‘Ataraxia!’

Shirone, with a massive magic circle above his head, was frowning as he calculated something.

“Could it be…?”

Shirone's eyes flashed open.

‘Here!’

A massive red beam shot up at the speed of light, piercing the sky and extending into space.

As mass condensed at high altitude, a gray spear with a mass of 10 tons began to form.

“Shining Spear!”
