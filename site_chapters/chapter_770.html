Chapter 770:

The spear of light pierced through the Heavenly Knight, and the ground rippled like waves.

The destructive power of Ruin had been suppressed by adjusting the mass, but the speed was far greater.

“Ugh… ahhhh…”

Wigo slammed both knees to the ground from the intense shock, nearly collapsing.

“Hrk!”

He caught himself with an elbow, barely preventing his face from hitting the dirt.

I…

His brain convulsed, and the signal transmitted through his nerves—only after a long moment did he begin to analyze what had happened.

How long was I trapped in there?

Just one second.

But the memory of repeating that second 100,000 times was fully imprinted in Wigo’s mind.

His cousins murmured among themselves.

“What the hell just happened?”

Heavenly Judgment was a globally renowned Avatar technique, but no one could comprehend how Wigo had been defeated in a single strike.

Garcia felt the same way.

I don’t understand. What on earth happened in there?

Wigo wasn’t just a top talent in South Aymond—he was among the best in the world.

And since the duel was between Avatars, not the actual bodies, there should’ve been no way to generate a shock of this magnitude.

“Any theories?” Garcia asked, which said everything about how surreal the situation was.

Minerva replied coolly.

“Vibration.”

Gone was her amused expression; her gaze had shifted to the icy clarity of a true witch.

“…”

Garcia chewed on the word.

With just a single term, his mind cut through countless possibilities and landed on one conclusion.

“You’re saying one second vibrated?”

“Exactly. He bounced the timeline. The line oscillates endlessly between point A and B, but the amplitude of the vibration remains one second. And within that single second of vibration, countless other one-seconds are probabilistically distributed.”

Garcia listened intently.

“The real question is—how did Wigo perceive this vibration?”

The cousins leaned in.

“The answer: cognitive dissonance. He most likely experienced both an instant and eternity at the same time.”

Just hearing the explanation was enough to induce cognitive whiplash.

One cousin asked, “How is that even possible?”

Minerva raised a finger and waved it up and down.

“This is the vibration of the timeline. Even if you try to perceive a single second at a fixed point in that vibration…”

Her finger continued to move.

“There is no second there. The moment has already passed, and only probability remains.”

Thus: the instant.

“But if you do perceive a second at a fixed point…”

Her finger stopped.

“The probability collapses. It ceases to be a vibration.”

Garcia spoke.

“100% probability. It becomes an actual, absolute second.”

Thus: eternity.

“The outcome depends on the observer’s intent. But Wigo vibrated in resonance with Shirone’s one second.”

Minerva curled her thumb and forefinger in succession.

“That means Wigo experienced two things at once: First, 100,000 seconds crashing down in the span of one second. Second…”

Now Garcia understood.

“100,000 repetitions of a single second. That’s why he experienced both an instant and eternity.”

Minerva lowered her hand.

“Strictly speaking, it’s not an instant or a vibration anymore. There’s no existing term for what Wigo just experienced.”

Shirone had called it Time Vibration.

“But judging by Wigo’s expression, I can guess what kind of experience it was.”

Still in a daze, Wigo stared blankly, terrified at his inability to form words.

Am I going to end up brain-dead?

To repeat the most intense moment of battle 100,000 times was a nightmare no one would want to relive.

“Are you okay?”

Hearing Shirone’s voice, Wigo slowly looked up at him.

“…”

The horrific memories surged in again, and his eyes shook with fear—then he collapsed backward.

“Uhhh…”

Shirone looked at him with a pained expression.

This is why I didn’t want to use it on anyone else.

It had been his trump card against Nane’s Void.

The fusion of Shifok and Bachji.

Shirone’s super-cognition could sense the Idea and integrate all information of the world.

That’s Ipdo.

And now, through Resonance, he had reached the stage where he could vibrate all that information—shake the entire world.

“If I can shake the world…”

If the timeline of this world could oscillate endlessly between points A and B—

“Then the past and future would overlap.”

The present would vanish, and the world would unravel into a field of pure probability with no fixed constants.

“There would be no inevitable future, and no irretrievable past.”

A kind of time slip.

“An act of reversing time.”

As Wigo slowly raised his head, he saw Shirone’s face—wearing a sad, gentle expression.

“This is how I fought Nane’s Emptiness…”

Even if it was still incomplete—

“It was the only way I could think of to save everything.”

Wigo’s face twisted, and clear tears rolled down his cheeks.

What have I done…?

Yahweh of Compassion.

A man who bore the burden of not allowing even a single misfortune to exist in the world.

Father…

The depth of Realm isn’t everything.

I’ve been such a fool.

Realizing just how vast Shirone’s heart was made Wigo feel ashamed of his own pettiness.

“Go to Sion,” Shirone said, extending a hand.

“That was a remarkable Avatar technique. Let’s fight together. This world needs people like you.”

“Ah…”

At last, he found his voice.

“Thank you.”

As tears streamed down Wigo’s face, even his cousins fell silent, sensing the weight of it all.

Minerva added, “You think it was just one second?”

Garcia hadn’t thought so, but hearing it now, he had no intention of denying it either.

“Repeating a moment won’t save the world. And you can’t erase all the Demons either. But…”

Minerva thought of Veron.

“One man left a question behind before he died. Can humanity change the future by its own will?”

“The Veron Problem. If the outcome is already determined by Law, then Nane’s Void may also be correct.”

“That’s why it wasn’t just one second.”

Shirone’s answer to the Veron Problem had been: Yes.

“He shattered a predestined future. He threw a direct challenge at Nane’s realization.”

If the future can be destroyed—

“Then whether the world is truly suffering has entered a realm where we can no longer know—until we reach the very end.”

Shirone helped Wigo to his feet.

“You’ll be okay once you rest.”

The cousins took Wigo and walked him away, and Shirone turned to Minerva.

“Let’s go. We’ve stayed too long.”

Garcia approached.

“Thank you on Wigo’s behalf. If there's ever anything I can help with, please don’t hesitate to ask.”

Minerva scowled.

“Hey! You’ve never said that to me. And you dare size up an Archmage like me, just a little old Association President?”

Respect is for those who act like people.

Seeing how polite he was to Shirone only made Minerva dislike him more.

“I’m just glad Wigo’s safe,” Shirone said. “The more who can help in Sion, the better.”

For the first time, Garcia shared something personal.

“Yes. Gustav’s recent movements are unsettling, and the Holy War is recruiting talent worldwide. I’m planning to apply for the Valkyries as well.”

“What? You, President?”

“It’s not final. But I intend to take the test. It’s time for all of us to unite. Of course… I won’t pretend my kingdom’s honor and personal career have nothing to do with it.”

“Oh, that’s only natural.”

Not everyone had to be Yahweh.

If he’s the greatest fire mage in the world, he’ll pass the test easily. Or maybe even get a free pass.

Shirone didn’t show it, but he respected that deeply.

“I wish you success. If we ever meet again—please look after me.”

That was what Garcia had truly wanted to say.

“I wish you good fortune.”

The jet carrying Shirone blazed with terrifying fire, cutting across the sky at Mach speed.

As the temple rapidly shrank behind them, Shirone turned to Minerva.

“We’re going to the Ivory Tower, right?”

“Obviously. Time Vibration is, in itself, a form of destruction. There’s going to be talk. Once that starts, we probably won’t be heading to Kashan for a few more days.”

“What kind of talk takes that long?”

“You’ll understand when you hear it.”

She clearly had no desire to get involved with Theraphos.

“In the meantime, I’ll handle the smaller stuff. There’s the South Aymond issue, and we need to register Lawslayer.”

“You’re registering Lawslayer?”

A Five Star who couldn’t use satellites was allowed to register one additional item with the Ivory Tower.

“No matter how necessary it is for eliminating Extremes, it’s still an ultra-lethal weapon. You need it registered to use it freely. Otherwise, there’ll be massive global backlash.”

“Ah. Makes sense.”

“I’m entrusting Lawslayer to you. Feels like it’s finally found its rightful owner…”

It was an oddly sentimental tone.

Well, considering what Lawslayer means to Minerva…

Handing over a weapon soaked in trauma and a terrible past was no simple thing.

“But what’s your wish?”

“Hm?”

“I lost the bet. What’s your wish?”

Minerva grinned.

“You look like you’re kind of expecting something.”

Shirone shook his head firmly.

“Not at all. I’d just rather get it over with. I’ve got a feeling it’s not going to be something normal.”

What kind of wish could a Five Star possibly have?

“Hmm, okay.”

Minerva turned in her seat, then looked back with a sugary gaze fixed on Shirone’s lips.

“What should I do~?”

Shirone shivered.

Minerva held up two fingers.

“I’ll give you a choice. Something we can do right now, or something that can only be done later. Pick one.”

Something we can do now…?

As Shirone mentally ticked off possibilities, he swallowed and said,

“Something that can only be done later. So if we can do it now but just delay it, that doesn’t count, right?”

“Smart boy. Most of the things I like can be done here. But what if it’s something like marriage? You can’t do that on a jet, right?”

“…Are you out of your mind?”

Minerva laughed brightly.

“Just teasing. Anyway, good—‘only later’ it is. You promised.”

“I should at least know what it is.”

As the jet pierced through clouds, Minerva’s eyes took on a distant glow.

“Time Vibration…”

“Huh?”

“If… someday, you really do get the chance to save everyone…”

Her voice was deeply serious. Shirone leaned in, but then she quickly shook her head.

“Never mind. Better to save it. Makes it more dramatic, right? I’ll tell you when the time comes.”

“You can’t just drop something like that! What is it?”

Minerva stayed silent—not for herself, but for Shirone’s sake.

You wouldn’t be able to accept it.

Life is suffering.

Though she traveled with Shirone, in truth, her philosophy was far closer to Nane’s.

Don’t try to save me, Shirone.

That was the one thing she truly wanted to ask of him.

“Ugh, seriously! If you keep acting like this, I’m canceling the whole wish! You’re just messing with me!”

Shirone’s frustrated voice made her smile, though there were tears glistening in her eyes.

I need to wipe them away.

A witch’s heart that could never be cleansed.

Let the moonlight wipe it away…

As they passed over the Mediterranean, the great Arkad Desert of the northern continent came into view ahead.
