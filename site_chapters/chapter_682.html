Chapter 682: Offering Flowers (2)

She banged her head on the table and turned to look at Lupist.

The mocking emotion finally disappeared from the corners of her lips, which had been curled into a smirk.

“It’s awkward for me to say this, but you really know how to pick subordinates. How far will you go? Minister of Defense? Head of the Magic Association? Yeah, the Head of the Magic Association sounds good. I’ll make you the Head of the Magic Association. No matter what it takes, I’ll make sure you become the Head of the Magic Association.”

“Let’s get up now.”

Lupist helped Jane up and requested a room from the staff.

Even as he carried her to the third floor, Jane remained unconscious.

“Mmm, I’ll make it happen.”

When Lupist laid Jane down on the bed as if she were collapsing, she wrapped her arms around his neck.

“Where are you going? You’re not going anywhere.”

“You’re drunk. Go to sleep. Or sober up.”

If she were a mental-type mage, a single switch would have cleared her up instantly.

“Hehe, what if I don’t want to? You can’t just live for work, right?”

“You’ll regret it tomorrow.”

Jane gave a bitter, wry smile.

“What wouldn’t I regret?”

“…….”

The next morning, Jane, now completely sober, bolted upright in bed.

‘Oh no!’

She turned her head and saw Lupist, who had already washed up, putting on his coat.

Her memory was exceptionally sharp, and the events of the previous night flashed vividly through her mind.

‘I must have been crazy, crazy, crazy!’

Jane slapped her cheeks with both hands.

Spending the night with her superior?

The thought of how awkward work would be from now on made regret flood over her like a tidal wave.

Lupist, who had pocketed his pocket watch, walked toward the door and said,

“I’ll go ahead. Come to work in 30 minutes.”

“Hey…!”

Lupist stopped at the door.

“You know, right? This was a mistake—no, an accident. Something like this will never happen again.”

“I know. It’s the end for both of us.”

Lupist closed the door behind him as he left.

“Be careful not to let a scandal break out.”

Jane, still disheveled, stared blankly before mimicking Lupist’s tone with a resentful expression.

“Be careful not to let a scandal break out.”

Then she flipped the bird at the door.

“So cold.”

Throwing off the blanket, she stood naked by the window and stretched lazily.

“Let’s see, today’s schedule is…”

It felt like her first day at work, under the newly risen sun.

***

‘There was a time like that.’

Jane smiled faintly as she recalled a memory from long ago.

They were both so meticulous that in over ten years of working together, they never crossed the line again.

As a politician and an aide, they devoted themselves to their respective roles and raced through the years like arrows, but whenever Lupist’s mood soured, Jane would sometimes think back to that day.

‘I thought no one was more perfect than us. Looking back, we were so young, Association Head.’

Her mind calmed, and her thoughts grew clearer.

‘That’s right. I just need to do what I can.’

What wouldn’t she regret?

As she continued downstairs, a strange energy began to rise at the end of the hallway.

“What’s this?”

A pitch-black darkness writhed ominously before gradually taking shape and regaining its color.

‘A vampire.’

“I’ll give you credit for making it this far.”

Lyca, a pureblood vampire, approached with her hands behind her back.

“I underestimated humans too much. It seems my instincts have dulled considerably.”

With every step Lyca took, a chilling energy pressed forward like a wall, but retreat was not an option for Jane in her mission.

“By order of His Majesty the King, as of today, the control of the Living Flower belongs to the Tormia Kingdom. Surrender peacefully and obey the King’s command.”

Jane, now fully composed as a cold mage, moved her tentacle-like Spirit Zone threateningly toward Lyca.

“Hmph, after raising it all this time, now you want to take it?”

“You knew this would happen, didn’t you? You should be grateful we let you run wild until now.”

“That’s what I dislike about humans.”

Lyca’s lower body darkened and transformed into hundreds of bats that flew at Jane.

“Thinking they’re such great beings.”

***

The interior of the Living Flower, which absorbs photons to grow, is partitioned by thick iron membranes, much like the cells of actual plants.

This is because the substance known as photominerals mimics the genetic mechanisms of living organisms, causing the cells to grow larger or differentiate as development progresses.

Moreover, just as human cells repeatedly divide to form complex and precise structures like the heart, liver, nerves, or bones, the Living Flower creates such intricate mechanisms on its own, surpassing human engineering knowledge by leaps and bounds. Thus, it could be called a machine far beyond human comprehension.

Therefore, it was no surprise that the door opened automatically when Lupist arrived in front of the Living Flower’s control room.

Inside the hexagonal space spanning 40 pyeong, countless wires, display devices, and highly precise interception systems were busily operating.

Lupist ignored the ten Black Shadows guarding Florino and stepped forward.

“Long time no see, Florino.”

They had met once before, ten years ago, at a negotiation table arranged through a secret channel in the Tormia royal palace.

“Yes, Lupist.”

Florino’s expression was icy.

“Things don’t always go as planned. I never wanted you to become the Head of the Magic Association.”

For the non-humans of Radum, Lupist, a prominent far-right figure in the Kingdom, seizing the center of the Kingdom’s power was nothing short of a disaster.

“Things don’t go as planned? That’s a flawed way of putting it. Isn’t it more accurate to say that when someone fails, someone else succeeds?”

It was no coincidence that Micae Gaold, classified as a core leftist figure, ascended to the position of Association Head despite the concerns of politicians.

At the time, he was pouring his heart and soul into the Heaven Project, and the issue of domestic terrorism rooted in Radum was merely a secondary matter.

“So, you’re finally going to pluck the flower?”

The thought of handing over the Living Flower, nurtured for decades, to the Kingdom in its entirety made Florino’s stomach churn.

“No. Do you know how much this is worth? I can’t just pluck it.”

Lupist unfolded his Spirit Zone and summoned steel lightning.

“I’ll take it, roots and all.”

“Protect the Minister!”

The all-female Black Shadow unit dispersed into the shadows.

The gray skin of the Black Shadows becomes transparent in darkness, making them exceptional for protecting key figures.

Additionally, their female physiques, which are no less capable than males’, made them ideal for safeguarding the Flower Tribe’s psychology.

‘Dark Arts.’

The shadows expanded like water, filling the room.

With the power to swallow even Shining Magic whole, the light vanished, and in the perfect darkness, several Black Shadows approached Lupist.

‘The best protection is eliminating the threat.’

Literally, assassination.

Their movements, boasting perfect assimilation into the darkness, were difficult to detect even with the synesthesia of the Spirit Zone.

‘Got him!’

Four blades silently sliced through Lupist’s body.

‘Nothing’s there?’

The sensation at their fingertips disappeared, and their nervous systems detached like blocks breaking apart.

“Are you stupid?”

The dismembered corpses of the Black Shadows littered the floor.

“What were you thinking, attacking steel?”

Steel is something to avoid, not attack.

‘That bastard…!’

The remaining Black Shadows shed tears of blood at their comrades’ deaths but refused to reveal their presence.

Technically, they were exceptional, but their patience wasn’t superhuman.

“I’ll kill him!”

As the remaining Black Shadows prepared to charge, Florino raised her hand and shouted.

“Stop!”

“Minister! But…!”

“Lupist isn’t an easy opponent. Leave him to me.”

Though she appeared strong, there was no guarantee how long she could hold out against the iron-willed mage who showed no emotion.

“Hand over control of the Living Flower.”

“Let me make a proposal. Provide a place for the non-humans of Radum to live. If you do, I’ll give up on intercepting.”

“No.”

Establishing separate facilities for non-humans in the Tormia Kingdom would incur losses beyond measure.

‘Jane will handle it.’

Lupist trusted Jane’s abilities.

“You’d rather shed blood when there’s a peaceful solution?”

“Peace?”

Lupist spread his palm, and sharp metallic blades spun like a storm.

“Do you think you’re human?”

He swung his arm, and the gray metal spread like mercury, aiming for Florino’s neck.

“Minister!”

A Black Shadow pulled her down, and the fragments exploded in midair, penetrating the darkness.

Screams followed as the remaining Black Shadows’ corpses were revealed outside the shadows.

“What’s the difference between us and you?!”

Florino, lying on the floor, cried out in a pitiful voice.

“We have emotions, culture, history! We can love, rejoice, and share sorrow! So why do you only reject us?!”

“First, you don’t pay taxes.”

Lupist took another step forward.

“Second, you don’t bear human children. Legally, you’re not eligible for national registration. Enslaving you or integrating you into brothels might have short-term benefits, but in the long run, supporting you is a burden on the Kingdom. It’s a waste of resources on a completely different level from keeping pets or livestock.”

Florino sobbed.

“And third.”

Lupist looked down at her with cold eyes.

“Our proud Tormia citizens, who pay taxes, produce children, and provide labor, dislike you. So just disappear. The world is better off without you.”

Florino’s shoulders trembled.

“How can you be so cruel? Just for those reasons… you think it’s okay to kill lives?”

“Just those reasons?”

Lupist kicked Florino’s chin.

“Minister!”

The last remaining Black Shadow hurriedly supported her.

Seeing the shock in Florino’s eyes, the Black Shadow gave up on attacking Lupist.

‘This is bad. Her submissive instincts are surfacing.’

The Flower Tribe is strong, but only a select few, like carnivorous plants, are born with aggression.

Even Florino, who had trained tirelessly to resist others’ desires, was nothing more than a flower before the authority of the Head of the Magic Association.

Lupist approached the display of the energy output device and pressed the cancel button, prompting a window requesting a code.

He clicked his tongue and approached Florino again, creating a storm of blades.

“Let’s start by cutting off your limbs.”

“You bastard!”

The last remaining Black Shadow leaped forward, but her face was sliced off by the storm of blades and fell to the side.

“If you want a quick death, hand over the code. Torture isn’t my specialty, but I guarantee you won’t last a minute.”

She was terrified to the point of losing her mind.

“You know how many of the Flower Tribe have been sacrificed.”

The scent of the Flower Tribe filled the room.

“Being owned by humans, sold off when they’re bored, exploited by new humans, and withering away from stress—that’s us. Humans even pity stray dogs! So why won’t you extend a helping hand to us?!”

“Why should I care about your circumstances?”

Florino closed her mouth with a bitter expression.

“Do you think humans don’t have a history of parents watching boiling water poured on their children’s necks? We didn’t get anything for free. People are always full of complaints. Equality is distant, and not everyone can be happy. But even building this crude system required countless human sacrifices. It’s a system created by humans, for the happiness of humans alone. And now, you creatures, who contribute nothing, suddenly appear and demand to be included?”

Lupist’s emotionless attitude only made Florino’s heart grow colder.

“You’ll regret this. You’ll see what consequences your selfishness brings.”

A floral avatar bloomed from Florino.

As the Violet Tiara, renowned as the most beautiful among the Flower Tribe, revealed her form, the room seemed to brighten.

‘I should capture her alive if possible.’

Even if Jane would handle it, it was better to keep Florino alive just in case.

‘It’d be a waste to let the Living Flower be destroyed.’

Repairing the control device would cost a fortune.

“Watch closely.”

Florino’s avatar dissolved into glowing powder, emitting a brilliant light like neurons.

“This is the wrath of flowers trampled by humans!”

***

Avatar Arts—Creation of a Microcosm.

***

“Ms. Jane!”

Aria, suffering from severe infection symptoms, jerked upright.

Through their mental resonance, the current state of Jane’s mind was transmitted to her as if she were experiencing it herself.

“What’s wrong?”

“It’s a vampire! A powerful one!”

Kuan’s expression darkened.

If someone could push Jane, a mage of her caliber, to the brink, it could only be Lyca, a pureblood vampire from Spectrum’s leadership.

“Kuan, you should go! Ms. Jane can’t fight at her full strength.”

Defeating Lyca while mentally resonating with a sick person was nearly impossible.

“No. My duty is to protect you.”

If Aria were attacked after entering the Living Flower, the code would be lost.

Given the obvious blow to the capital, even if Jane died, Aria had to be protected at all costs.

“But! Ms. Jane is…”

Guilt weighed heavily on her, as Jane had chosen the harder path to save Aria.

“If Ms. Jane dies, I’ll take you to the Living Flower then.”

Kuan coldly spat out the words and gritted his teeth as he looked at the Living Flower.

‘Please stay safe….’

***

Black smoke unfurled and raced down the hallway, transforming into a human figure before Jane’s eyes.

Before the form fully materialized, Lyca’s palm struck Jane’s solar plexus, and a massive shockwave pierced her body.

“Guh!”

Her body didn’t move an inch, but it felt like her life had been flung into space.

Her knees buckled, and she staggered forward before collapsing backward, hitting the back of her head.

“How annoyingly tenacious.”

Lyca clicked her tongue as she looked down at Jane, whose eyes were open in death.

Around them, the corpses of their vampire subordinates dissolved into black smoke.

Though not purebloods, they were powerful souls in their own right.

For Jane to wield seven such vampires through mental domination was no small feat, which only made Lyca more irritated.

“Well, it’s over now.”

Lyca vanished in an instant, her cloak fluttering.

***

-……!

Aria’s voice seeped into Jane’s dead brain.

-Ms. Jane! Ms. Jane!

‘My mission.’

A faint glimmer of life returned to her lifeless pupils.

Her heart wasn’t beating, but she still had about 20 seconds before brain death.

‘Association Head.’

A belated flashback passed through her mind as Jane slowly raised her hand.

‘For the nation and its people….’

It was astonishing that she could focus with a dying brain, but it was nothing compared to the most difficult feat for a mage: self-harm.

Gathering wind magic at her fingertips, she stabbed her own stomach and grabbed her heart.

‘I can’t die yet!’

Casting Mental Transcendence, a mental-type spell, strength beyond her physical limits contracted her heart.

“Ugh!”

Blood circulated, and her bodily functions gradually returned, but the heart’s contractions couldn’t be sustained by a woman’s grip strength.

‘I have to reclaim control of the Living Flower.’

Feeling her wrist muscles tear, Jane leaned against the hallway wall and forced herself forward.
