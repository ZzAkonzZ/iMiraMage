Chapter 718:

"Merot."

As Garnet's pheromones were emitted, the artificial brain experienced its greatest tremor ever.

"Ugh!"

To control the countless ants in the colony, even the voice had to be different.

"You longed for the outside world, just like I did when I was young. Today, you have brought something fascinating."

Everything that happened in the Garnet Colony had already been reported through the maid ants.

"I thought you would like it."

Merot, folding her wings and showing respect, looked at Shirone and spoke.

"Tell Mother what you told me. I hope you find the answers you seek."

As Merot stepped aside, Garnet's incredibly long antennae approached Shirone.

"I have heard of humans."

The definition of humans from an ant's perspective was entirely different from what Shirone knew, but it didn't seem absurd.

"Humans are said to be very large, but you are small."

"That's because…"

As Shirone explained about Andre, Garnet, who was listening carefully, nodded.

"So, the world is divided into many independent worlds… It is like looking at us."

Andre was similar to the ant colony system.

"Exactly. Worlds distinguished by special criteria. And the criterion for this world is you, the queen."

Because Garnet had built the colony.

"I understand what you mean. So, what do you want to ask?"

Shirone quickly poured out his questions.

"The system. What is the criterion for dividing workers and soldiers? What is the vision for Garnet Colony? How are the layout of the rooms and the size of the territory determined?"

If the ants' construction and prosperity of the colony were similar to Andre's 19,000 worlds, he could understand Goffin's thoughts through the queen's intentions.

"When the food supply is insufficient, how much does the workers' labor increase? How do the princesses and nobles waste resources…"

Garnet shook her head and interrupted.

"It's not like that."

"What?"

"I understand the intent of your questions, but I don't think I can give you the answers you want. I never thought about such complicated things while building the colony."

"You never thought about it…?"

Did Goffin separate the 19,000 worlds without any criteria?

"Then how did you build such a precise system?"

"There was just this."

Recalling the time when she first arrived here, Garnet looked up at the ceiling and spoke.

"I wanted everyone here to be happy."

"…"

Shirone felt as if he had been struck by a hammer.

"I wanted my beloved children to be safe. To build a new colony happily, without anyone being sacrificed…"

Garnet looked down at Shirone again.

"So, I can't understand your questions. Why think about such things? Don't we already have a system of love? My colony is not complicated at all. I think the complicated thing is your thoughts."

"Uh…"

Shirone stepped back, holding his forehead.

'I wanted everyone here to be happy.'

In this world, in this system.

'There are no 19,000 criteria.'

Only one criterion divided the chaotic space-time into 19,000 worlds.

Realizing an unimaginable purpose, Shirone shed tears of overwhelming emotion.

"Love for everything."

It is called philanthropy.

Labyrinth Andre-World No. 1583.

The survival test, which took a year for the 2,000 residents of Whiteblack, began.

At the entrance to the White zone, many residents gathered, burning with determination, in addition to the ten participants.

Of course, their determination was nothing compared to the participants who would soon commit brutal murders on Anarchy Mountain.

'Can we really win?'

Despite the 80% of the produce at stake, the participants didn't seem particularly trained.

'Well, I don't like this method either…'

The standout would be the first candidate, and Shirone was the most noticeable person in White recently.

On the other hand, Black was a zone where war was a daily occurrence.

'We can only hope for a weak opponent.'

Since the system allowed for sufficient monopoly with 20% of the production, the strong had no reason to risk their lives in the game.

The village chief shouted to the residents.

"Everyone! Today, we must fight against those who worship violence. Please wish the participants heading to Anarchy Mountain good luck!"

Applause erupted, but the participants, including Shirone, remained expressionless.

"Now, let the battle of the law begin!"

As the village chief shouted at the top of his lungs, the participants boarded the carriage to Anarchy Mountain.

'It's a prison under the guise of transportation.'

The interior was clean, but there were dozens of guards, and it was unclear how they would react if anyone tried to escape.

"You're Shirone, right?"

A middle-aged man sitting across from him spoke.

"I'm Marco. Father of two daughters, son of an elderly mother, and husband to Janie."

Shirone's voice became slightly prickly at the strange introduction, as he guessed the intention.

"Is that so? I have no family to take care of."

At least in this world.

"Don't misunderstand. This is White's rule. If you have any complaints, we can decide by vote here."

Without needing to hear the rules, it was predictable that a unanimous vote would come out.

"It's fine. Just tell me what you want."

"The reason our village is called Whiteblack is because a unique moon rises every year during the battle of the law."

Marco opened the carriage window and pointed outside.

"Do you see the law changing?"

Shirone, sticking his head out the window, saw the moon half-covered in darkness, like an eclipse.

"When the battle begins, the light and dark areas, which were divided in half, invade each other's territory and change into the shape of a droplet."

Shirone thought of the principle of yin and yang.

"We call that moon Whiteblack. We don't know when it first appeared. The important thing is that if you don't receive the moonlight at the top of Anarchy Mountain, you can't get the weapon."

"Weapon?"

Marco leaned forward and spoke.

"When the representatives of White and Black receive the moonlight, 120 weapon cards are scattered throughout Anarchy Mountain. They are in the form of cards, and possessing them allows you to use special abilities."

It was the effect of the law.

"There are two types of cards: job cards and weapon cards. There are a total of 20 job cards, and they are automatically distributed to all participants. Your physical abilities change completely depending on the job you get."

"For example, what kind of jobs are there?"

"There are all kinds of jobs. Twenty jobs are randomly distributed each year. If you get a doctor, you gain healing abilities; if you get a swordsman, you gain powerful physical abilities."

There was an element of luck involved.

"The remaining 100 cards are various weapons. They can be transferred and taken, but not destroyed. Ultimately, the 20 participants must obtain one job card and then combine various weapon cards to fight."

Shirone rested his chin and fell into thought.

'This is on a different level from individual actions. This is a grand law.'

He felt Goffin's presence in the wheel of law, which was interlocking in the realm of gods.

"The problem is the timing of the Whiteblack moon's harmony."

Marco continued.

"To obtain the weapon, the representative of each zone must swear under the moonlight. If we fail to swear, our side's weapons won't activate."

"You want me to make that oath?"

It was a brazen request, but the nine participants would point to Shirone anyway.

"Black also needs the weapon. The weakest person will come out, so it won't be too dangerous."

'It will be really dangerous.'

White's participants wouldn't act until they obtained the weapon, but for Black, it was the perfect opportunity to reduce their numbers.

'To fight Black on equal terms, White must have the weapon. Am I the scapegoat?'

As everyone held their breath, waiting for the decision, Shirone leaned back in his chair and spoke.

"Alright. I'll do it."

A sigh of relief was heard, and Shirone frowned, feeling a bit resentful, and opened one eye.

'Huh?'

The woman sitting next to Marco looked at Shirone with an apologetic expression.

'Her name is Breeze, right?'

All he knew was that she worked as a hairdresser.

She mouthed the words, 'Thank you.'

When they arrived at Anarchy Mountain, Marco spoke.

"We need to get to the top as quickly as possible. If we're even a little late, the Black guys will have set up camp."

'When did they say it wouldn't be dangerous?'

Since he had to enter the most dangerous place to understand this world, he nodded without complaint.

"How do I make the oath?"

"Under the Whiteblack moonlight, just think that you belong to White. It will be over in an instant."

"Then I'll go."

As Shirone quickly ran to the top, the remaining nine participants finally began to formulate their strategy.

"Once we get the weapon, we'll head to the area we mentioned before…"

No one thought Shirone would return.

By the time Shirone reached the top, the harmony of the Whiteblack moon was almost complete.

'Is this also the law?'

The moonlight shining directly from the sky created a circle with a diameter of several meters on the ground.

In the area where light and darkness intertwined in the shape of yin and yang, Shirone stepped into the moonlit area.

'What is Black's strategy?'

There was no movement yet, but he could feel the killing intent, indicating they had already arrived.

The circle drawn by the moonlight on the ground became clearer, finally achieving perfect harmony of black and white.

"It's starting!"

As the residents of Black shouted from all directions, someone began running towards Shirone.

'This is my chance! I have to stop them here!'

If Black couldn't obtain the weapon, the balance of the law would quickly tilt in White's favor.

"Ahhh!"

The person entering under the black moonlight was a boy who looked too young to have even started school.

"What…!"

Shirone hastily stopped his attack as the boy fell to the ground, crying.

"Please save me! Please save me!"

As the light and darkness of Whiteblack rapidly rotated, a massive pillar of light shot up into the sky.

A hundred beams of starlight spread out, and twenty job cards curved like guided missiles, striking each participant's chest.

'My position is exposed.'

Quickly twisting his body, Shirone saw seven cards losing their light in the bushes.

'Eight people, including the boy. So, all but two are gathered here?'

They were here to kill Shirone.

'My card is…'

Setting aside his anger at using the boy as a decoy, Shirone checked his job card.

'Seeker?'

Before he could analyze its specific traits, a Black participant flew out of the bushes.

"Yahoo! Jackpot! Let's take care of the scapegoat first!"

Whatever job they had, they were incredibly fast and already had a weapon card in hand.

"Go! Power Bomb!"

The card flashed, and dozens of bombs rained down on Shirone and the boy.

"Ahhh! Save me…!"

Boom! Boom! Boom!

The massive explosions shook the mountain's peak, and the Black participant laughed maniacally.

"Hahaha! Now it's 9 vs. 9! Fair, right?"

"You're talking nonsense."

As the participant turned his head at the voice from the side, a photon cannon struck his abdomen.

"Ugh!"

The man screamed so loudly his eyes bulged and he flew dozens of meters, falling off the cliff.

"Ahhh…!"

As the scream faded, Shirone, holding the boy, scanned the surroundings with sharp eyes.

'The killing intent is gone. They fled.'

The Black participants were quick to assess the situation.

The battle of the law, Whiteblack.

Survivors: 19.
