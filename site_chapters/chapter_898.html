Chapter 898: Strike of Destruction (3)

Iruki pointed at the map.

"The Abyssal Cliff."

It was a 160-kilometer-long gorge along the border between Tormia and Kazra.

"It’s easy to think of it as a place where ambushes lurk, but Balkan will come through here."

Geographically, it was just a gorge, but the Abyssal Cliff was a massive terrain over 17 kilometers wide.

"Most importantly, passing through here is the shortest route to Tormia. Also, we’ve lost half our forces at the Kanian Plateau."

It was damage sustained in just one day of battle.

‘And Amy...’

Iruki pushed the thought away and continued speaking.

"But the greater the damage, the more we can strike at the vital point. With our limited Valkyrie forces, they wouldn’t expect us to set traps in such a vast terrain."

"And there aren’t any, actually."

Iruki nodded.

"Yes. There are no traps. But there is an elemental bomb that can blow up the entire gorge."

"Phew."

As this was a key strategy that could end the war, the tension among the commanders was at its peak.

"We’ll stop them here."

The pointer indicated the center of the gorge, 70 kilometers from Tormia.

"The power of the elemental bomb is beyond imagination. The terrain will absorb some of the impact, but our forces and nearby cities won’t be safe."

A commander spoke.

"This is an opportunity gained at the cost of many sacrifices. Not doing it is not an option. What we can do is detonate it at a point where the expected damage is minimized."

Iruki remained silent.

"Commander."

"Yes. I don’t intend to keep my conscience to myself. The operation will proceed as planned. The day of the decisive battle is approaching. There must be no mistakes."

As he finished speaking firmly, the commanders left the meeting room with a look of relief.

Aromi approached Iruki.

"Is it hard to make a decision?"

Knowing the weight of the commander’s role, she was particularly worried about his shaken appearance.

"No. It’s not that."

"Then is it because of Major Amy?"

He couldn’t deny it.

‘We couldn’t recover her body.’

If there had been a definite conclusion, he wouldn’t be suffering so much.

‘If she survived, she would have returned to the unit. Did the demons capture her?’

With the entire army of hell descending, it was like dropping a bomb on her head.

"Commander, it’s presumptuous of me to say this, but..."

Aromi hesitated before speaking.

"If Major Amy is alive and captured by the demons, killing her might be a mercy." Knowing how cruel the demons were, Iruki partly agreed.

"The reason Balkan didn’t realize the variable of Gitaruman is..."

Iruki finally spoke his inner thoughts.

"Because their scope of thinking is different from mine. They can read the flow of the crowd but not the flow outside the board. They can’t think about what they can’t imagine."

"Right. But why are you saying this now..."

"That’s how I feel now."

He couldn’t shake the feeling that he was missing something.

"If Amy was captured, she would suffer terribly, and Balkan would use that. There’s no better way to break my sanity."

"Hmm."

"But there’s no response. Maybe Amy wasn’t captured by the demons."

Aromi wanted to give hope, but now was a time for cold judgment.

"Commander, it’s just a hypothesis..."

"I know."

Iruki knew too.

"Even if Amy was captured, there are hundreds of reasons not to announce it. It could be a ploy for Shirone or Yahweh. So it might be a delusion..."

The psychology of hoping for a friend’s safety might have altered his thinking.

"There’s constant noise. Whether I use experience or logic, nothing seems clear."

Aromi realized that Iruki was in a calm state.

"You mean something might be happening outside your thoughts."

"Yes."

Iruki looked back at the Abyssal Cliff.

"Like with Gitaruman, it might determine the success or failure of this strategy."

Where on earth was Amy?

"Phew. Phew."

Amy’s rough breathing echoed in the cabin built in the mountains of the central continent.

Her hair was short, and her body, covered in burns, was wrapped in bandages.

Fortunately, the heat hadn’t penetrated deep into her skin, and the scabs would soon fall off.

"She shows no signs of waking up." Shura said, looking at Amy lying on the bed.

"Her body has recovered, but the real issue is her mind. It’s fundamentally different from Samadhi."

Samadhi is based on the cosmic principle of emptiness, but Amy’s multiple consciousness was highly artificial.

"It’s like she dug herself into the ground and closed the exit. She won’t be able to get out on her own."

Nane bit his finger and spoke.

"Remove the bandages."

"What are you going to do?"

"She’s currently in a state where she’s closed her eyes, blocked her ears, and shut off all her senses. Human communication won’t be able to pull her out of the abyss." When Shura removed the bandages from her abdomen, new skin had grown, revealing reddish skin.

Nane, who had written a circle of blood letters around her navel, sat cross-legged.

"Stay as far away as possible. It won’t be bearable."

To bring Amy back, it required a sermon as intense as enlightening a rock.

"Don’t overdo it."

As Shura left the cabin, Nane smiled kindly and slowly closed his eyes.

His lips parted gently.

"Mahaprajna..."

As the unique rhythmic sermon continued endlessly, the mountains and forests began to tremble.

"Ugh!"

Shura, rushing into the forest, covered her ears.

‘It’s not a sound.’

Nane’s voice, piercing the mind, resonated directly in her head without passing through her eardrums.

"Form is emptiness, emptiness is form..."

Inside the cabin where the sermon echoed, Amy’s body heated up as if it were burning.

"Haa. Haa."

Even in an unconscious state, her breathing became rough, and a thin voice escaped from her throat.

"Shirone."

The Great Temple of the Ivory Tower.

With all the Five Stars gathered at the call of the Great Star, Shirone finished his story.

"Argones."

It was shocking.

"So, whether Nane wins or Havitz wins, the world ends?"

Freed spoke with a displeased face.

"It’s ridiculous to underestimate the Ivory Tower. Do they think we’ll just sit back and do nothing?"

Silence followed.

"Hey, why is everyone like this? You’re not scared, are you?"

Minerva spoke.

"It’s not about fighting or not. It’s a situation where the fundamental system is activated. The best thing is to end it before it reaches that point. For that..." She looked at Shirone.

"Shirone must achieve the integrated mental system before Nane realizes it."

There were no objections.

For the same reason, none of the stars of the Ivory Tower had told Shirone.

‘Amy has disappeared.’

If even the Great Star couldn’t find her, it meant someone or some force equivalent to him had taken her.

‘It could be Nane. But it could also be Havitz.’

Both were possible.

‘I can’t reveal it now.’

Xing spoke.

"Shirone, before you prepare for the final battle, there’s something you need to know. There’s a fact you don’t know."

Minerva turned pale and looked away.

‘Tsk! That stubborn person.’

Xing, who believed everyone but herself was an illusion, felt no need to lie.

"A fact I don’t know?"

Xing turned her head and spoke.

"In the central continent..."

At that moment, the Great Temple of the Ivory Tower shook.

"What?"

There was no reason for the Great Temple, floating in space, to shake, unlike the spire on the ground.

The Great Star spoke with a bitter expression.

"They’re finally coming."

Through the glass window reflecting the planet, something burning hot from the friction of the atmosphere was visible.

"A dragon?"

The sight of it rising vertically, emitting electricity from its blue scales, was truly awe-inspiring.

Shirone remembered the identity of the dragon.

"Blitz, the Thunderwing Dragon."

One of the twelve apostles of the dragon race that guards time, it was a creature he had met in Andre’s first world.

It was also one of the few first-class dragons that could leave the planet and enter space.

Boom!

Blitz attached its massive body to the ceiling of the Great Temple and bent its long neck to look inside.

"Great Star."

A cold voice that pierced directly into everyone’s mind without passing through the air was heard.

The Great Star looked at the ceiling.

"Apostle of Time, what brings you to this place?"

The large eyes scanned the Great Temple before fixing on Shirone.

"Our Core seeks you."

"The Core...

The Immortal Dragon Karatorusa.

"You mean it has awakened from eternal sleep?" The story of leaving with the words "I will handle it when no one else can" is famous.

"Something immense is coming. The awakening of the Immortal Dragon is only a matter of time. Shirone, the one who has opened the sense of non-form, follow me. The Core has something to convey to you." Shirone approached the Great Star.

"What will it convey?"

"How to fight."

"Fight whom?"

Blitz turned his head significantly, looking down at the northern hemisphere of the planet. His gaze encompassed the Eastern Sky.

"The heavens."

A peak piercing through the clouds.

At the highest point of the Eastern Sky stands a shrine built by the greatest human mage.

The Gate of Goffin.

A massive iron sphere inscribed with angelic runes began to vibrate high in the sky.

The sphere rippled like liquid and then exploded violently, blowing away the entire peak.

Boom! What remained in the place of the explosion was a massive tunnel large enough to swallow the entire Eastern Sky.

"Is this the Earth?"

A foot suddenly emerged from the black sphere and stomped down on the ground.

Boom!

A small hill burst apart.

"Haha, it's so tiny."

Following that, numerous giants appeared, and fairies flew into the sky.

Angels and their followers, the Mara, arrogantly passed through the tunnel, and finally, four archangels appeared.

Though they had lost the concepts they each represented, the mere presence of their aura made the world tremble.

The Archangel of Destruction, Uriel, spoke.

"Finally, we have arrived. It took longer than expected."

The Archangel of Light, Raiel, said.

"It could have taken even longer. That's why it's strange. It feels like the dimensional barrier was too easily pierced."

Uriel muttered.

"Miro...?"

What could have happened to the greatest human monk who single-handedly held back the army of heaven?

The giants and Mara, who had been waiting for this day, couldn't suppress their fighting spirit and roared.

"Let's fight! Let's punish the arrogant humans who deny the gods!"

Ikael was watching them.

'Shirone.'

The reason she couldn't share their sentiment was because Hexa was here.

"Archangel! Give the order to launch! Give us the chance to teach these creatures the dignity of the gods!"

Ikael slowly opened her mouth.

"...Ataraxia."

Her halo expanded rapidly, spreading a brilliant multicolored ring.

"Huh!"

Amidst the sudden silence.

"To all troops."

Only her amplified voice echoed loudly.
