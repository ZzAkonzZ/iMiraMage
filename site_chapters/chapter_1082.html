Chapter 1082: Fully Armed (4)

Operator Database

Operator Number Seven continued to search Yahweh2's logs until reaching Moon Island.

The battles with the mafia group were broken down into signals and streamed endlessly.

'He handles Donatello skillfully.' That was fine.

But then the Operator appeared, and Number Seven frowned.

'What is this?'

Even when she destroyed the West Country users without hesitation, he just thought she had a nasty temper.

Watching the subsequent logs, Number Seven was gripped by an unknown emotion.

It was the first time the Operator had shared her story with someone.

Most of those in Under Coder had fled here because they hated reality, hadn't they?

'The real world.'

What did the Operator want to hear from someone who held the fate of reality in their hands?

When he checked the final log between them, Number Seven ground his teeth.

'Respect?'

For him, this place was everything.

There was no reason to show respect to humans fighting fiercely in reality.

Exiting the database, he saw the scenery of a factory deep underground.

'Then what am I?'

Number Seven fell into silent contemplation.

Shirone, equipped with two legendary parts, took the train back to the East Country.

Aegis greeted him, and they took the train to Nasca City, Golden Wheel's mid-point base.

Though most buildings were just steel frames, there were surprisingly many users.

"Nearby, there are hunting grounds frequented by users around level 200. But more famous is the bounty hunter's sanctuary. There's an unwritten rule that what happens here doesn't lead to guild disputes. So rankers often come here for duels."

Looking around, Shirone saw many strong-looking individuals, even for level 200.

At Golden Wheel's hideout, Yolga's son emerged from Mid Gear.

"Your gear looks different. Good parts, I see."

Shirone retorted.

"You knew they would betray me, didn't you?"

"No need to go help the West Country. Items don't drop even if you die. The Operator's appearance surprised us too. What did she say?"

It seemed the mafia had uploaded the recorded video.

"Just… various things."

As Shirone hesitated, Fermi looked back in surprise but didn't press further.

"Follow me. We don't have much time, so let's start right away."

Entering an underground room, Fermi turned around holding a part in each hand.

"Take them. They're yours." They were the Strong Armor Raphael and Metal Wings Da Vinci, obtainable from the East Country's hunting grounds.

Raphael was a rough mineral, and Da Vinci looked like feathers plucked from a goose.

"Equip them?"

As Fermi gestured, Shirone first equipped Raphael.

Light spread, and seven metal spheres embedded themselves in Shirone's machine like joints.

Both elbows, both sides, both knees, and finally, the solar plexus where High Gear was located.

"Strong Armor Raphael is the most durable armor in High Gear. It's almost indestructible by user weapons. But it's tricky to use."

As expected.

"The metal spheres embedded in your machine will protect you in any situation. But only one of the seven parts at a time. The information should be in augmented reality, so try it out."

"Hmm."

Activating Raphael on his elbow, his entire right arm was covered in a mirror-like clear metal.

Fermi explained.

"Now your right arm is invincible. It can withstand physical attacks and block enemy bullets. But when one part is activated, the others are exposed."

"But the part's durability remains."

"Of course, but if you only reach level 300 by the Solar War, ranker attacks will be fatal. You'll have to block them with Raphael."

"It's like a flag-raising game. Deciding which part to activate in which situation."

"It seems simple, but in a battlefield full of bullets, it's extremely difficult. It would be easier for a schema user, but since it's not your real body, you'll adapt."

Shirone alternated activating each Raphael, gauging the defense range.

"Upper body defense is the trickiest."

Fermi agreed.

"There are three choices. Activating the left side covers half the upper body, including the back. The right side is the opposite. Lastly, the heart, where High Gear is located."

"The head is impossible."

Fermi nodded.

"Even with Raphael, headshots are still possible. You can block with your arms or legs, but sometimes you'll need to dodge. For that, you'll need…"

Shirone picked up the feather.

"Metal Wings Da Vinci."

Equipping it, a new part with fluttering feather effects attached to Shirone's back.

Visually, nothing changed.

"Da Vinci is called Metal Wings because it's a mechanical device, not actual wings. It's a full booster, using explosive power for propulsion. Open it."

Activating Da Vinci, energy nozzles popped out as the shoulder plates opened.

"Power at 100%."

Reading the text, Fermi explained.

"Even at 100%, it will be fully depleted in less than 10 seconds if used at full power. Use it sparingly. It's self-charging, but recharging takes a long time."

"How does it recharge?"

"The sun. According to High Gear's lore, Da Vinci is made with Low Gear technology. So it can convert solar energy directly without passing through Mid Gear."

"Hmm, so it's an incredibly powerful booster? I heard legendary parts depend on the user's skill. Is Da Vinci also difficult to handle?"

"It is. Try it out."

"Here?"

Fermi shrugged.

"All buildings in Nasca City are abandoned. No one will claim damages if something breaks."

"Got it. Then…"

Shirone activated Da Vinci.

As the lens of the propulsion device lit up blue, the machine shot out at supersonic speed.

"Wh-what?!"

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom! As the sound of endless walls being pierced echoed, Fermi muttered.

"Too fast, way too fast."

Ten minutes later.

From the freshly drilled underground tunnel, the sound of Shirone's footsteps echoed as he walked back.

His machine was completely scratched up.

"You… want to die?"

If he had crashed into the wall at full propulsion speed, Shirone would have returned much faster.

'Because he would have died.'

But coming back the same way instead of the starting point meant he had adapted.

Fermi noticed Shirone's right arm was covered in bright silver metal.

'Impressive reaction speed.'

Activating Raphael just before the collision, he had protected his machine while continuing to break through.

"There's nothing like real combat to learn. Now you see? Each function is excellent, but the four legendary weapons synergize with each other. Depending on how the user utilizes them, they can offset level differences."

Shirone disliked Fermi because he was a liar who only spoke the truth.

"Tch, you could have just explained that."

Fermi pointed at Shirone.

"Plus, with time bombs and bat senses, the efficiency of legendary grade will increase. But even so, the chance of defeating the Operator is less than 10%."

Shirone had clearly seen how powerful the Operator's machine was on Moon Island.

Fermi looked around and asked.

"So, how far can you go? In reality, you reached the end."

If enlightenment in High Gear was like enlightenment in reality, resonance, void, and ultima remained.

"Understanding the world ends with enlightenment. After that, it's about penetration. Resonance is about merging with the world. But to me, this world is…"

Not real.

"You can decide it's real, but the heart can't. I'm here for reality. My goal is to defeat the Operator, uncover the apocalypse, and eliminate the demonic realm from the world."

Fermi understood.

"Hmm, so you have to win with what you have now. Fine. Golden Wheel just needs to transport you to the Operator."

The rest was up to Shirone.

"Let's go. You need to be at least level 300 to match the Operator's output. I'll assign you a teacher."

"A teacher?"

Following Fermi outside, Golden Wheel members were already waiting.

The first team exuded an aura, and behind them, the Destroyer team stood awkwardly.

"Oh? Yahweh2."

Seeing Shirone, they ran over.

They had significantly leveled up while he was in the West Country, and their parts were much more advanced.

"Wow! Michelangelo!"

But their appearance paled in comparison to Shirone, equipped with the four legendary grades.

Aegis approached.

"The reason for finding legendary grades first is not just the difficulty but also the faster leveling. You'll be hunting with Wild West from now on."

Shirone looked at the code name Wild West.

He was a skeleton wearing a cowboy hat and equipped with a .44 Magnum.

As he stared at the High Gear shining inside his ribcage, Wild West approached.

"Nice to meet you. I'm Wild West."

Though it was eerie to see a skeleton talk, nothing was impossible in High Gear.

"Ah, nice to meet you."

Death Princess spoke.

"We'll be leveling up too. The goal is Magnan. We need to be in the second team to participate in the Solar War."

West, standing awkwardly, said.

"Leave it to me."

Though his stance, like drawing a gun, looked silly, the code name was familiar.

'West of Endurance.'

Equipped with Skull Company's armor, he was definitely specialized in hunting rather than dueling.

"Let's go. At least level 300 by the Solar War. After that, it's good to level up even more. Since moving takes time, let's start conquering from here."

Following West, Shirone entered the hunting ground near Nasca City.

The creature levels were 100 levels higher than Shirone, but not unmanageable.

"Wow…"

It was like hunting with armor-piercing rounds at level 20, and the experience gauge soared.

As time passed, users gathered.

"Hey, look. It's Yahweh2."

Already famous on the forum, and equipped with all four legendary parts.

"…Shall we?"

In Nasca City, even if they fought, there would be no conflict with Golden Wheel.

"Hey, West."

Turning to the call, seven members from different guilds had gathered.

'Level 307, Level 311, Level 287…'

Though from mid-sized guilds, they were notable within their ranks.

"We don't want to interfere with your Solar War preparations, but can we duel Yahweh2?"

"Hmm."

After a moment of thought, Wild West stepped aside.

"Ten minutes."

"That's enough."

As the users approached, Shirone, already aware of their presence, turned.

Wild West communicated.

It's okay if you die. It's faster to return to the starting point. The level difference between you and them is about 2000 levels. Though it's a meaningless sum, think of it as practice to reduce the gap with the Operator.

The more duel experience, the better.

Yes. I'll try.

Crouching, Shirone activated Metal Wings Da Vinci, and his machine shot out at supersonic speed.

Boom!

It took three minutes to annihilate the enemies.
