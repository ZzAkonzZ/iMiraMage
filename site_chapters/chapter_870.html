Chapter 870: Groggy (5)

"I'll speak," said Canis as he walked up beside Dorothy.

"If the only need for Valkyrie is the mineral, does it really matter if it's not at this specific location?"

Ilruki simply replied, "Right."

"So why are they driving us out? This place is historically significant. Moreover, our team has drained our personal funds to support this project."

"There’s supposed to be compensation involved, right?"

"That's not the point."

It was not a matter of pragmatism, but of emotions.

"Shouldn't at least some convincing be done? If the entire mountain range can be used, why are they being so restrictive?"

"The Valkyrie is busy. They won't set a limit on mining quantities, and they don’t have the capacity to care about your circumstances."

Canis cracked Ilruki's resolve, realizing that Ilruki wanted zero percent uncertainty.

"So, in other words, we're just a nuisance to them?"

"That's one way to see it."

Fury ignited in Canis's eyes.

"Oh, really? You've grown up, Ilruki."

At that moment, the guards behind them drew their swords, and a menacing aura spread.

"Damn it!"

Closer and Sabina entered the spirit zone, but their minds had already recognized the truth.

'They’re really powerful.'

The mere intensity of their collective killing intent was almost suffocating.

"Canis, it's useless," Arin said.

"Ilruki won't change his mind."

The Ilruki they could see through the spirit zone was like a giant flame burning life as fuel.

'It’s different from risking life.'

The determination to succeed was a feeling that transcended life itself.

"Damn! You think you're lucky."

When Canis withdrew his hostility, Closer and Sabina also deactivated the spirit zone.

The atmosphere was decidedly grim.

"Do you have to speak like that?" Dorothy asked.

"You could have explained it nicely. Asked for help, sought understanding. He’s not a stranger to us…" Ilruki looked at her.

The once 4D girl who loved fantasy was now a competent adult leading the team.

"You've changed a lot."

Her outfit revealing her chest boldly and her direct manner were different from before.

"No, I’m wrong."

Many things change as one ages.

But because they shared the same time, there were things that were bound not to change.

"Outside school is a tough place. I'm not as stubborn as I used to be. But I'm still pursuing my dream. Canis, Arin, Closer, and Sabina, none have changed the dreams we had during our school days."

"Then let’s say I've changed."

"That's what you said to me!" Dorothy exclaimed.

"There are no adults in the world, just children and those pretending to be adults. You still hold that sentiment, don't you? Then why speak like you're some real adult?"

It was hurtful.

While calculating, the Ilruki Dorothy remembered really knew what was important.

"I’m sorry, but we don’t have time."

Only Arin knew the true meaning behind those words.

'Ilruki's right, Dorothy.'

Whether it was a problem with his body or not, he had lost his attachment to life prematurely.

'He’s not revealing it because... he wants to stand proud before his friends.'

'I would have done the same.'

Therefore, even in a situation of discord among classmates, she could only keep silent.

"Really? No time?"

Dorothy turned briskly and headed towards Hickeri-19, sprawling out on it.

"Go ahead! We can't step aside! Either step over us or kill us, do as you like!"

Dorothy's face, tightly closed, looked sad.

'You rotten kid.'

For anyone else maybe, but Ilruki shouldn't have done this to her.

'How much Dorothy had looked forward to this meeting…'

Understanding her feelings, Sabina lay next to Dorothy and shouted.

"Yeah! Kill us, kill! Kill us!"

Aromi adjusted her glasses and said, "I will proceed with the arrest."

As the knights began to step forward, Ilruki turned and said,

"Let's leave it for today."

"But…"

"This team has been studying the Arka Mountains for a long time. They are competent, and it's not good to start conflicts already."

While it went against Aromi's professional ethics, deference to Ilruki was a priority.

She told Canis, "Following the general's orders, I'll give you a day. I hope you make a good decision."

As the Valkyrie’s carriage moved away, Arin approached the two and said,

"They’ve left. Let’s get up."

Dorothy sat down with a gloomy expression.

"I can't work today. Let's go back and plan. We need to review Ilruki’s proposal."

Even when angry, a wizard does what needs to be done.

"I’m sorry our accommodations are humble. I didn’t expect you’d be staying…"

Ilruki’s stay was in a temporary barracks 300 meters from the mine.

"It's fine. How's the excavation team?"

Aromi made a gesture as if looking outside.

"They’re holed up in the temporary buildings. It seems like they're reviewing the documents we've handed over."

Ilruki stood up from his chair.

"They will probably accept it. They're upset because of me, but they're sensible and intelligent. Don’t worry."

Aromi forced a smile.

"I’m not worried. I'm concerned about the general's health. Now that it's come to this, take a break for a day."

He wanted to rest, but thoughts of hell’s army preoccupied his mind.

Except for those on night watch, everyone else was asleep.

As Ilruki, sitting in a chair, heard footsteps, he lifted his eyelids.

"Let go. Put that down." At the entrance of the barracks, Dorothy was being held by the knights.

When she took off her work clothes, her youthful appearance was revealed, though her dress still seemed uncomfortable.

"General, this woman is..."

"It’s fine."

After the knights saluted and left, Dorothy, rubbing her arm where she had been held, approached.

"Impressive. Those people hang on your every word."

"What’s going on?"

Dorothy didn’t answer.

"If it’s about the mining rights, my stance won’t change. Starting tomorrow, the military contractor we’ve contracted with..."

"Forget about that."

Dorothy interrupted him.

"It's not just about work. Don’t we have personal issues to resolve?"

Dorothy approached more quickly than usual.

"Have you been drinking?"

"That's not important. What I want to know is... why did you do that?"

"Do what?"

Dorothy had never forgotten.

"We kissed."

"You did that."

"Anyway! And we dated."

For 13 minutes, to be exact.

"Why? Did you feel even a little for me? Or was it just curiosity? Which is it?"

"Why does that matter?"

"It’s important to me."

Her first man, her first kiss.

She could have left it as a pleasant memory, but seeing his face, she couldn’t help but ask.

"I...

As Ilruki recalled his school days, he sighed, stopped talking, and turned away.

"Let's stop. It's meaningless."

"Meaningless? Why? Because you’re in a high position now? Is it too childish, or are you afraid I'll hold you back..."

"I’m about to die."

Dorothy’s words stopped abruptly.

"What?"

"My brain's too good. It's eating away at my lifespan. And recently, it’s gotten worse."

If it was a joke, it was cruel.

"What, what are you talking about? Who dies from using their brain?"

"It’s not immediately dangerous. Maybe I can last quite a while. But I’m essential to the Valkyrie, and I have no plans to stop. If I keep overdriving like this, I might..."

Ilruki mimed an explosion with a 'boom'.

"No."

Dorothy shook her head.

"That can’t be."

Angry to hide her precious emotions, but in truth, she dearly missed her friend.

"Ha ha! Don't be scared. I’m still okay, right?"

For now, at least.

"Being honest does feel better. I can’t afford to weaken. I’m responsible for too many lives. Oh, your Hickeri 19 was impressive. You’ve upgraded it a lot?"

Dorothy's face fell.

"Why are you so foolish? You haven’t changed at all, not then, not now!"

Ilruki gave a bitter smile.

"Anyway, help me out. You’re the team leader, so persuade your team..."

Before he could finish, Dorothy grabbed Ilruki by the collar and threw him onto the bed.

"I didn’t come here to talk about work. I absolutely need an answer."

Dorothy climbed onto the bed, placing Ilruki’s head between her legs, dressed in short jeans that revealed her thighs.

"What are you doing?"

"If you don’t know that, you’re not much of a man." Of course, he knew.

"You’re crazy."

"Why suddenly act like this? I’m Dorothy."

Of course, he knew that too.

"I told you, I have no intention of stopping. Anything between us might be too short because my life might be too short."

Dorothy smirked.

"Would you skip dinner knowing you’ll die tomorrow?" After a moment of thought, Iruki slowly turned his head left and right.

'Left thigh. Right thigh.'

There was no escape.

'Well, it’s straight ahead then.'

Ilruki sat up sharply.

The next morning.

Dorothy, like a cat burglar, slipped out of the barracks and entered the temporary building as if nothing had happened.

In the living room, the team was reviewing the lengthy legal documents passed by the Valkyrie.

Sabina looked up.

"Where have you been? You weren’t in your room this morning?"

"Oh? Ah, I just went to check on the mine."

As she deflected and entered the living room, Canis grumbled discontentedly.

"Damn, even so, being driven out like this is absurd."

Sure enough, that was the agenda.

"Hey, listen."

Dorothy cautiously began.

"What if we just step back? Honestly, Ilruki must be overwhelmed because of the war. Artifacts are important, but we need to ensure humanity survives first, right?"

Overnight, her attitude had changed, and as the team members blinked in surprise, Arin said,

"Actually, we were leaning that way too. After all, there’s no chance of winning a legal battle against the Valkyrie."

Sabina leafed through a folder.

"I’ve reviewed it, and the conditions aren’t bad. We can secure rights to the next excavation site, and the penalties we've set as conditions are sufficient."

Dorothy's face brightened.

"Then, should I meet the director? Maybe I can negotiate even better terms."

The folder snapped shut.

"No objections there. But there’s definitely something we need to address first."

"Huh? What do we need to address?"

Sabina pointed at Dorothy with sleepy eyes.

"You slept with him, didn’t you?"

"Really?"

Closer turned around with keen interest as Dorothy rolled her eyes.

"Um, about that... even with ten mouths, I couldn’t explain."
