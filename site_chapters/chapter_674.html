Chapter 674: The Secret of Radum (2)

The fall lasted roughly four seconds.

Mages could cast Fly spells, but the warriors had to act fast.

“Aaaaah! Save me!”

Bicon flailed, losing his senses.

Even as a schema user capable of agile combat in heavy armor, freefall was a different matter.

As Shirone spread his radiant wings, Rufist shouted.

“No! Advance toward Sanghwa!”

Ignoring him, Shirone surged toward Bicon, but the electrical net was faster.

“Damn it……!”

Then Jane teleported, snatching Bicon like a hawk and landing safely.

‘Tch, what a mess.’

The others followed, and Rufist reluctantly changed course, landing as well.

On the ground, towering buildings blocked their view, suffocating them with frustration.

“Status check first.”

“All 17 accounted for. No stragglers.”

Jane eyed Sanghwa.

“It’s collecting photons again. Being on the ground will be a different story.”

Rufist’s eyes blazed.

“That’s why I said advance! Are you defying the chairman’s orders?”

“……My apologies.”

As Jane bowed, Shirone frowned.

“We’re in this together, aren’t we?”

It was a fair point, but to Rufist, it sounded like civilian whining.

“Listen close, rookie. To you, your mercenaries might be everything. But I’m obligated to balance the scales for every citizen of the capital—no, the entire kingdom. The scales of equally unfortunate deaths. The kingdom won’t tolerate endangering others for one person’s stubbornness.”

Bicon approached and bowed.

“My apologies. Give me one more chance. It won’t happen again.”

Rufist didn’t even respond, turning away.

Rebuilding broken resolve was hard; those who could do it wouldn’t have wavered in the first place.

“Jane, expect disciplinary action after this. You’re only spared now because you’re useful.”

The words were cold, but disappointing the chairman cast a deeper shadow over Jane’s face.

‘Accessories…….’

To Rufist, every human in the kingdom was just a cog in the machine.

“Sorry. Because of me……”

As Bicon flushed and apologized, Jane forced a smile.

“So, what’s the plan now?”

At Yordik’s question, Shirone looked up at the iron tower looming beyond the buildings.

“What are the odds they’ll attack us even if it destroys Radum?”

“100%. They know this is the final battle. They’ll stop us at all costs.”

“Why not strike first?”

A single Angel’s Punishment could destroy Sanghwa’s emplacements.

“Who gave you permission? That’s kingdom property. We preserve the system until we retrieve the seed.”

Mayray, eavesdropping on Ra’s thoughts via divine frequency, pointed down the street.

“Enemies are coming.”

Ra’s voice was always past tense.

Meaning the enemies were already here, countless non-humans shimmering beyond the concealment veil.

“Our position is exposed. The interception will begin.”

“Annoying.”

Rufist turned to Shirone.

“We advance to Sanghwa as fast as possible. Can you do it?”

Understanding the chairman’s intent, Shirone took a deep breath and nodded.

“Yes. I can.”

“Full speed ahead. Kuan, guard the VIP. The rest, cover the flanks and rear.”

As Kuan stuck close to Aria, the eerie bass of the Gader race echoed from within the concealment.

“Open fire!”

Enemies revealed themselves one by one.

Members of Spectrum’s lower factions: the Black Revolution, Photons, Speed Killers.

“Kill the humans! Complete the revolutionary cause!”

Their numbers swelled, filling the streets, and Rufist scowled, opening his Spirit Zone.

‘Iron Wave!’

Steel magic activated, and a 10-meter-high wave of iron surged forward.

“Wh-what the hell?”

The team gaped as the expanding wall outpaced the wind, and Rufist leaped.

“Follow. Stragglers get left behind.”

Hundreds of non-humans screamed as the iron wave crushed them, and Shirone unleashed the Radiant Angel’s avatar skyward.

‘Angel’s Punishment!’

A spear of light slammed down beyond the wall, obliterating the area and sending bodies flying.

Boom! Boom!

Confirming no allies were hit, Shirone unleashed consecutive Punishments.

The hellish screams beyond the wall made the team shudder.

‘Monsters.’

It was hard to tell who wasn’t human.

“Stop them! Stop them, damn it!”

“You crazy goblin, how the hell do we stop that?!”

If the clash of the strongest spear and shield was a paradox, their combination was calamity.

‘That’s an Ivory Tower candidate?’

The chairman was one thing, but Shirone’s prowess, rivaling a first-class archmage, was terrifying.

‘Just a little further.’

With Sanghwa now within a kilometer, Kuan tensed.

‘Finally, the upper class?’

To Kuan’s knowledge, only middle-class and below non-humans resided in the concealment zone.

They were formidable, but the upper class waiting at Sanghwa’s spire was on another level.

‘Rumors say there’s a pureblood vampire…….’

As he advanced, sword humming, Mayray, eavesdropping on Ra Enemi, shouted.

“Interception orders are in!”

Rufist halted, lowering his stance and clasping his hands.

‘Golden Jade!’

An impossibly thick iron wall formed, encasing the team in a hemisphere.

Mayray began counting.

‘One. Two. Three.’

At four, Sanghwa’s energy beam struck the Golden Jade head-on.

“Aaaaaah!”

The noise inside was hellish.

Screams were drowned in the din, but Mayray’s gaze remained sharp.

‘Fired after four seconds. No doubt.’

Ra Enemi was currently at Sanghwa.

“Damn it! Do something!”

The beam superheated the Golden Jade, temperatures skyrocketing.

They understood how a steamed chicken felt.

“We’ll cook alive!”

Yordik blasted frost from his Ice Globe.

It cooled momentarily, but the effect was negligible, evaporating into steam.

‘Breaking the Jade means death. Then we’ll gather heat to the limit…….’

Calculating calmly, Rufist cracked the Golden Jade at the breaking point.

“Rally to Sanghwa!”

Compressed heat exploded, scattering the team with iron shrapnel.

“Aaaaaah!”

The blast separated those who could endure from those who couldn’t.

Most lost control, but Kuan grabbed Aria and dashed clear.

As the beam closed in, Shirone unleashed a prepared spell.

‘Ataraxia!’

Instantaneously, the photon cannon passed through the magic circle, releasing a colossal flash.

The mass of light repelled the beam momentarily, but even Ataraxia’s amplification couldn’t hold it.

‘This is insane!’

Still, Shirone’s effort bought everyone a chance to escape.

Only Rian remained, charging through the gale to shield Shirone and Ataraxia.

“Uaaaaah!”

As he slammed his greatsword down, the beam engulfed them.

‘Hold! Hold!’

Rian’s divine transcendence was always formidable, but when protecting Shirone, it was peerless.

“Grrrrrr!”

The energy was beyond mortal endurance; vibrations alone shattered his fingers.

‘Protect! The kingdom of faith……!’

Fractures spread from his hands, muscles rupturing, his left eye crushed, cheekbone torn away.

Finally, the beam vanished.

“Ughhhhh…….”

Rian’s ghostly groan escaped his lips.

The beam had split around him, carving two vast paths through the city—a testament to the power he’d withstood.

“R-Rian…….”

Shirone’s voice trembled.

Miro had mentioned Rian’s limits, but surviving an ancient weapon bare-handed seemed delusional.

“Rian! Don’t die!”

Rian spoke.

“Right. I won’t die.”

Gripping his greatsword, he rose and turned.

“As long as I live, you won’t die.”

With one eye closed, he smiled as his body rapidly regenerated.

Ssmile. Ssmile.

Ignoring the whispers, Rian checked the state of his greatsword, Idea.

‘Impressive.’

Countless foes had fallen to it, but this was its first exposure to such power.

Yet, true to its indestructible nature, the blade bore no scratches.

‘No idea how long the whispers will last…….’

Not that it mattered.

He’d have charged in even if this were his last stand to protect Shirone.

‘Thank you, grandfather.’
