Chapter 1216:

The ability was called Image Copy.

A form of irregular magic that allows the caster to output and replicate what they have memorized in their mind.

“This thing’s pretty expensive. And the reason is…”

When Fermi activated the magic, the document he had just been holding was perfectly duplicated.

“Because it can revolutionize labor. As long as you memorize it once, you can keep printing copies.”

That was why time had been so tight.

“So? What are you planning to do with it?”

“Information entropy. The value of information changes depending on how many people know it. For example…”

Fermi leaned in.

“Like our little personal tastes. Those should never leak out, right?”

Seriel’s cheeks flushed slightly.

“Wanna die?”

“Anyway, that’s the idea. As of now, the people who know this future information are me and Shura. Probably the King of Iron will know too…”

Fermi handed her a document.

“Here, take one.”

Knowing the value of the file, Seriel had no choice but to take it, even though she knew it was a joke.

And then she realized.

“You’re not seriously—?”

“That’s right. Information known by everyone becomes worthless. From now on, I’m going to officially distribute this document to all 12 nations in the Holy War. In short, this information becomes trash.”

“But… are you sure that’s okay? Once everyone knows, you won’t be able to control it anymore. Unless you have a way to update it…”

“That’s not possible.” Now that they’d reached the end of Omega 999, going back to Apocalypse was out of the question.

‘Even if I did go back, mining would be impossible.’

God’s interference was practically a given at this point.

“There’s no need to update it. The file only contains information on the potential World Leader. I didn’t include anything truly important.”

Though no one would probably notice anyway.

“Important information?”

When the thought of Amy’s death crossed Fermi’s mind, he fell silent. Seriel didn’t press further.

‘I’m sorry, Shirone.’

He felt the need to apologize to his eternal rival—because the only one who had accepted his idea was Shirone.

‘No one else…’

No one else would try to erase their memory after learning of the death of someone they love.

Leaving Seriel behind, he opened the window and looked out at the countless events unfolding outside Delta.

‘The hour of the end.’

As he felt the compass of the world spinning wildly, Fermi raised his hand.

Image Copy.

Holding dozens of identical documents in his hand, Fermi spoke quietly.

“How far can a human really see?”

He would not run away.

‘I’m Yolga’s son.’

The one who sees far.

No, the one who will witness the very end.

Fermi turned back to the window and scattered the stack of documents into the air all at once.

As the white papers fluttered like butterflies, everyone outside Delta looked up.

“What is that?”

Fermi turned back to Seriel and smiled.

“Entropy: Zero.”

And with that… the future reset to zero once again.

Meanwhile, Shura, having received the information from Fermi, walked down the hallway with a cold gaze.

She’d skimmed through the documents quickly, but she didn’t care about some world leadership nonsense.

What filled her mind instead was the question Fermi had posed.

‘The Veron Problem,’ as it was called.

The issue surrounding the Iron Spike that Veron, 1st seat of the Council of Ten, had implanted in the Kingdom of Iron.

‘His will transcends the law and remains etched in that time, that space, even now.’

A spike driven into the world.

‘Can humanity truly change the future?’

Even if you know the future and take a different action—if that action was also already predetermined?

‘Veron tried to push that question to the absolute limit. It’s not just a philosophical pillar. That spike is still embedded, holding out against the laws of God.’

If God pulls out that pillar, it would mean His victory. But if He can’t pull it out…

‘Then humanity can change it.’

A future entirely different from God’s will.

‘I will wait.’

Shura held the document tightly.

As long as the Iron Spike remained in the Kingdom of Iron, she would grant the King what he wished.

‘Until the true king returns.’

As Shirone ascended the Ivory Tower, his face was pale.

‘It’s endless.’

The demons were divided into the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd Legions, occupying key strongholds on each floor.

"Kill Yahweh…!"

The demons, engulfed in the flash of the Miracle Stream, screamed in agony as they tumbled across the ground.

"Graaaagh!"

Ordinarily, they should have been completely purified—but only half their bodies had melted away.

‘They’ve gotten even stronger.’

The biggest concern was just how much stronger Baal, the 1st Legion Commander, had become.

"Kill! Do not fear annihilation! The more we resist, the more Yahweh will suffer!"

The demons had lost all sense of fear.

‘This isn’t bravery.’

The belief that making others suffer is more important than one’s own happiness…

‘Ataraxia.’

Was this simply the nature of Maga—the inevitable core of demonic existence?

"Graaaaah!"

Faced with another surge of amplified power, this time even the demons couldn’t hold their forms.

When the flash faded, only a pair of ankles remained where the demons had stood.

"Haah… Haah…"

Shirone caught his breath, recovering his mental focus.

‘They’re trying to buy time.’

It was a strategy to keep him tied down while Baal targeted Great Star.

Of course, the Ivory Tower’s Stars were guarding the upper floors, so reaching her wouldn’t be easy—

But even that no longer felt certain.

As he passed through the scattered remains—mere ankles left behind—emotion surged in Shirone’s chest.

"No human rejoices in another’s misery."

It’s just that they are not happy themselves. And when there’s no other way to be happy…

"They follow the methodology of evil."

He wanted to believe that.

Shirone also hated evil and believed in being responsible for its consequences.

But—

‘I want to believe people can change.’

If good and evil are merely determined by circumstance, then what is it that humans truly have the power to choose?

Shagall screamed.

“I won’t change!”

He was half out of his mind, overwhelmed by the pain transmitted through the Taeguk chain from Etella.

“What do any of you know?!”

As he slaughtered the demons blocking his path, blood-red tears streamed from Shagall’s eyes.

If all his past—everything—had been memories manipulated by the gods…

“You’ve never lived like I have!”

Would anyone else have turned into Shagall under those same circumstances?

“I am evil! Pure evil! I’ll kill them all! I’ll kill everyone, and once I’m the only one left—”

Would that finally make the rage inside him disappear?

“Aaaaaagh!”

Crushed by unbearable pain, Shagall dropped to his knees.

Etella’s grief fed Shagall’s fury, and his fury fed back into her again.

And so, the Taeguk spiraled into limitless growth.

—Remaining purification time: 82 aeons, 873 kyung, 7,601 trillion... haaah…

A sigh was heard from the InfoMater.

Clearly a broadcast error, but Liona, the InfoMater of the Fire Management Company, didn’t care.

“Four aeons of time just evaporated in an instant…”

That was how massive his karma was.

Of course, she had known. But when they had first entered the underworld, Liona had never imagined it would come to this.

How could such karma possibly be purified?

“This kind of ending…”

She fell deep into thought, facing the conclusion of two souls who once seemed like they’d remain in hell until the end of the universe.

Vivian, who was assigned to Rian, asked,

“Liona, what are you doing? You’re supposed to be giving the guidance message properly. What if the boss finds out?”

“I don’t know. I just suddenly thought… what’s the point of all this? Maybe even we…”

As administrators, they understood.

“This must be the end.”

That the gods were about to close the world, and there wasn’t much time left.

“It was fun though, wasn’t it?”

Liona gently brushed Vivian’s hand from her shoulder and turned her gaze back to the vision.

“The final evil of humanity.”

As Shagall’s assigned InfoMater, she felt a sense of duty to witness his end.

Shirone was certain.

“If there’s no ability to choose, then there is no integration.”

That’s why Gaian beings who had reached an integrated mental system were those who had transcended the heart.

“I’ve seen their world.”

Where everyone understood one another so deeply that, no matter what happened to the universe, it didn’t matter.

“And toward that realm…”

When Shirone arrived at the 23rd floor, a gruesome sight awaited him.

The 3-Star resident Arte and his satellite Tokei lay on the ground with their heads severed.

‘The Balance Department… annihilated.’

On the other side lay the bodies of Hokgangshi of the Law Department, High Judge Tanjura, and his satellite Daeho.

“Guh!”

Just as Shirone confirmed that Hokgangshi had stopped breathing, Tanjura let out a strained groan.

“Tanjura!”

Shirone rushed over and activated Miracle Stream, but it was no use.

‘He’s already dead.’

Only the upper half of Tanjura’s body remained, and even so, he managed to force a smile.

“A high judge’s ruling… suspended sentence. I’ve held onto my pathetic consciousness until 5 Star arrived.”

His heart had stopped; only his brain remained faintly active.

“What happened?”

“As you can see… Baal wiped us out. That bastard’s become a monster. A monster made by humans.”

Shirone clenched his teeth.

“We did our best to hold him off. If you hurry, you might make it in time.”

Shirone wanted nothing more than to rush to the top floor, but he couldn’t just abandon Tanjura.

Tanjura shook his head.

“It’s fine… I’m already dead. If there’s one regret, it’s that I’ll die without hearing the answer.”

Was Xing right?

“I believe that no one can truly understand another person’s truth. That’s the limit of my intelligence. But…”

Tanjura placed a weak hand on Shirone’s shoulder.

“I must have been wrong… right?”

In his final moments, the answer Tanjura longed for seemed different from the truth he had always believed in.

“I…”

Shirone didn’t know either.

Swallowing back the tears rising in his eyes, he could only offer a single, desperate hope.

“I believe you were right. I’ll prove it—no matter what.”

Tanjura smiled faintly.

And as if not wanting to burden him any longer, the light quickly faded from his eyes.

“Whew…”

Shirone’s jaw trembled, but his eyes, fixed upward, were filled with killing intent.

“Demons…”

The truths known to all were like sandcastles—crumbling in the face of twisted desires and rage.

‘These people… they were trying to protect something until the very end.’

The light of intelligence was fading.

Ivory Tower – Floor 201.

Following behind Baal, the 1st Legion demons were slowly slaughtering the tower’s residents.

“Guess raw power wins in the end. No matter how ‘intelligent’ they think they are, they can’t beat us.”

While indulging in their cruelty, the demons suddenly turned toward the stairway.

“Huh?”

Shirone walked in, his body drenched in blood.

“What the—? Why are you so late? Turns out Yahweh’s pretty lazy, huh? And yet you act all—”

A swirl of radiant smoke spiraled around the demon’s body—then exploded, tearing flesh apart on the spot.

The laughter died instantly as the demons grasped the shift in atmosphere. Shirone spoke.

“I believe nothing is beyond change.”

“Hey, cut the lecturing—”

“But you… don’t change.”

The demons fell silent.

“Just don’t. Stay evil, right in front of me. Just for a few more seconds.”

“Isn’t that a given?”

Shirone fought to hold his emotions in.

“Even so, I have to believe that you can repent. I want to believe it. But…”

His voice trembled.

“…a part of me doesn’t want you to.”

“Oof…!”

The demons burst out laughing.

“Puhahaha! What’s this guy saying? Is Yahweh drunk? Why are you babbling nonsense?”

“You wanna know why?”

Shirone’s eyes widened.

“Because I’m really pissed off.”

The moment Yahweh’s fury exploded, blinding light filled the entire 201st floor.
