Chapter 923: The Measure of Desperation (3)

Ikael stared at the hexagon spinning in front of her for a long time.

"What are you trying to do?"

"Exactly what it looks like. I'm implanting hexa into your mind. Of course, you have to accept it."

The mind of an angel was a holy light.

"And after I accept it?"

"You'll come to know about me. And through hexa, I'll come to know about you."

Ikael twisted her lips to the side.

The idea of a human infiltrating the noble mind of an angel was somehow unpleasant.

'But.'

If that human mind was hexa.

"If I accept the hexa, will I reach the same level as you?"

Goffin shook his head.

"Understanding is the process of extracting and reconstructing based on known information. Hexa exists outside the law."

"That's what I'm saying. If I can't handle hexa anyway, there's no point in doing this, right?"

"Some realizations..."

Goffin chose his words carefully.

"...can only be understood after reaching the result. If you can't achieve it yourself, blindly believing is one way. There's nothing to lose, right? It might seem crazy now, but once the process is over, you'll see that I'm right."

Ikael, having reached a high level of mental discipline, understood Goffin's words.

'But believing is another matter.' To be honest, after fighting for eons, there was no doubt about Goffin's character.

'It's just... it feels strange.'

Was this fear or excitement?

"Once again, there's nothing to lose, right? I'm already in a groggy state. If you don't like it, you can kill me then."

"Fine."

Ikael nodded.

"But if you try anything funny, don't expect mercy."

"Leave it to me."

Goffin smiled and extended his hand, and after hesitating, Ikael slowly bowed her head.

"Ah..."

The moment hexa penetrated the holy light.

"Gasp!"

Ikael's eyes widened.

As the information of hexa melted into the furnace of her mind and integrated...

'Unbelievable!'

It was an exhilarating joy incomparable to any other pleasure in Ikael's life.

'We become one.'

It felt like conquering all of Goffin, and at the same time, being conquered by him.

'No, we shouldn't be like this.'

There was no resistance to the enemy's philosophy, and even if he was wrong, she wanted to accept everything.

"Ah..."

Goffin's life flooded in.

'How much pain must he have endured.'

Everything he was angry about and sad about, even things that couldn't be conveyed in words.

'You're trying to consume me.'

Clinging to a shred of reason, Ikael desperately tried to push Goffin away.

'I don't want this. If this continues, I won't be able to hate you.'

She would come to understand Goffin.

"Stop..."

As Ikael struggled to pull away, Goffin embraced her waist.

"It's okay."

He slowly lifted her upper body and brought his face to her lips.

"Leave it to me."

As if that was all there was to it, Ikael, in a daze, couldn't move.

When Goffin's lips touched hers and parted, a hot object entered.

"...!"

Ikael's shoulders trembled as if struck by lightning.

'What is this! What is this! What is this!'

The act, never experienced in an angel's body, threw her into a panic.

'Wet, disgusting.'

It was so unpleasant, yet part of her wanted to continue this bizarre act...

'You bastard.'

Just a little.

How much time had passed? She couldn't tell, but she felt they had already crossed an implicit boundary.

When Goffin's hand reached Ikael's chest, the integration was complete.

"Get away!"

Startled, Ikael pushed him with both hands, and Goffin's upper body hit the ground.

"Ouch!"

Goffin, making a pained expression, winked at the panting Ikael.

"I thought you wanted it too."

"What are you doing!"

As her mind returned after the process ended, she realized what she had done.

"How dare you defile an archangel! I'll kill you right now."

Ikael raised her hand.

"It's disgusting."

Goffin said.

"Bodies rubbing together, saliva mixing, organs losing their original meaning...

That's what being a living creature is.

"But that's our life. Without it, nothing continues."

"Why should I care about your circumstances? Angels are inherently noble, pure, and absolute beings."

"How was it?"

Goffin asked.

"Did you really hate it?"

Honestly.

'I don't know.'

It remained in her mind, but the memory felt like it wasn't hers.

"That's because you controlled my mind..."

"If you didn't completely hate it."

Goffin interrupted.

"It means you have a heart too."

Ikael's holy light trembled.

"...Heart?"

"Yes. Even the most disgusting acts can feel beautiful, and any flaw can be seen as perfection. Hexa is the heart. That's why it's omnipotent, and that's why..."

Goffin spoke with conviction.

"You don't need the world to be perfect. An indifferent god is not a god."

It doesn't have to be cold.

As Ikael bowed her head in thought, Goffin spoke.

"Now you understand me. Even you, born from photon signals, have a heart. That means we're not different. We can go further."

Ikael shook her head.

"I'm confused."

The possibility that all concepts could be unified through the heart was undeniable.

But ultimately, because it was the heart...

"I don't know what you want me to do."

Nothing was clear.

‘What do I think of this human? Do I hate him? Or...’

This feeling of drifting in the middle of an endless ocean was something Ikael had never experienced.

Goffin understood her feelings.

"That's enough." He knew what it meant for an angel, especially the first archangel, to lose her bearings.

"I'm not asking you to do anything right now. You can't do it anyway. If you're confused, that's your heart. Just accept it."

Ikael, who had just taken her first step, couldn't understand everything yet.

But one thing was clear.

‘Humans...’

Were they this free?

‘Gaia people.’

And the only species that integrated all that freedom.

"I will fight."

Ikael slowly stood up.

"If I just have to accept it as you say, my heart is still in the world of law. Is this okay?"

"Of course."

It might seem like nothing had changed, but in reality, everything had.

"Hmph."

When you realize you have a choice, that's when the struggle begins.

Just before flying away, Ikael glanced down at Goffin and said,

"Take care of yourself."

As she flapped her wings of light and flew beyond the horizon, Goffin finally relaxed.

"Whew."

He brought his right hand to his face, recalling the sensation from a moment ago, and wiggled his fingers.

"This is driving me crazy."

Then he covered his eyes with that hand, cooling the rising heat.

‘Lucifer...’

His mind was just as troubled.

Omega transmitted all the events contained in the beginning and the end to Shirone as a single signal.

The events humanity was experiencing now were just the tip of the iceberg, not even 1% of Omega.

‘There was a great myth.’

Goffin, who had mentally integrated with Ikael, analyzed the infinite algorithm of the angel.

Gaia people and Goffin, who couldn't give up the Ultima system, implanted the halo into a machine.

Thus, Anke Ra, the strategic killing weapon to kill gods, Babel, was created.

In the year 738 of Omega, humanity built factories to mass-produce Babel and called them the Tower of Babel.

"Arrogant humans."

In the year 777 of Omega, Anke Ra ordered all angels to destroy Babel.

The fundamental concepts that made up the world and the countless Maras derived from those concepts attacked the Tower of Babel.

Watching the black angels fly swiftly, Satiel bit her lip.

‘It looks like Ikael. No, that's Ikael.’

It was already known that Goffin and Ikael had made some kind of deal long ago.

So it made sense that Babel's form resembled Ikael, but she still wondered if it was really true.

‘If something happened between the archangel and Goffin...’

Ikael's voice was heard.

"What's wrong, Satiel? Your holy body is trembling."

Turning her head, Ikael had approached and was looking at the battlefield with indifferent eyes.

"Why...?"

Satiel couldn't hold back and asked.

"Does Babel's form look exactly like you? Is it the enemy's trick, or...?"

"What are you trying to say?"

Ikael asked coldly.

"Are you doubting me, who erased countless Gaia people from this world?"

"N-no."

Babel was a threat to gods, but Ikael also understood the essence of Hexa and neutralized the "Hand of God."

‘Yes, it's just a simple deal.’

The Ultima system of the Gaia people had weakened to its worst state.

It was undoubtedly Ikael's achievement.

"Fight!"

Following Goffin's voice, the light of Miracle Stream quickly spread across the battlefield.

"If we stop this, we win!"

As the concepts held by the angels were destroyed by Miracle Stream, the number of Babel deployments noticeably increased.

The Tower of Babel, as tall as Araboth.

Among the Maras, some were larger, but Babel's function was equivalent to an angel.

"We might be pushed back."

Whether Uriel heard it or not, Ikael's indifferent face was filled with anger.

"I'll go."

As she opened Ataraxia and headed to the ground, hundreds of Babel flew in.

"Ridiculous tricks!"

Every time she struck with her hand or kicked, the debris of Babel fell with the sound of breaking metal.

She didn't show it, but her mind was complicated.

‘There's a limit to mockery.’

After destroying all the replicas that looked exactly like her, she landed on the ground and shouted.

"Goffin!"

Goffin, who was fighting fiercely, turned his head at Ikael's voice.

As the light in Goffin's eyes ignited, his body evaporated and disappeared.

Boom!

The shockwave from their colliding bodies pushed the air into a massive sphere.

In what could be called a vacuum, the only visible thing was the intermittent bursts of white light.

To the two fighting at super speed, it seemed as if time in the outside world had stopped.

As their fists crossed and struck each other's faces, Ikael and Goffin were pushed back the same distance.

"Ugh!"

Goffin's eyes glowed along his trajectory.

‘Answer me, Goffin.’

Watching him, Ikael, with her slender body over two meters tall, clenched her fists.

‘Is it mockery? Or...’

What did Goffin define her as?

"Ikael!"

As Goffin charged with a shout, Ikael gritted her teeth and launched herself.

"Answer me!"

As they collided, the rapidly expanding air rolled tens of tons of rocks like leaves.
