Chapter 1141: Key Keywords (4)

Meanwhile, there were still those who refused to repent, even after being struck by Eden’s divine power.

"Ugh, no! I don’t want to go back!"

Eden spoke sternly.

"Foolish souls, why can’t you understand that the end of corruption is nothing but emptiness?"

"Ahhh! Just leave me alone!"

Was it pain?

To them, living a disciplined life again seemed worse than death itself.

‘The Satanic Cult… It’s truly insidious. To think they could corrupt someone’s mind to this extent.’

The priests shouted in despair.

"I just want to indulge more!"

Even brains soaked in pleasure could regain sharp clarity when pushed into a corner.

‘We need to kill that woman.’

Their expressions suddenly shifted, and they wept pitifully, holding out their hands in fake repentance.

"Oh, we repent, Lord."

Nade shouted a warning.

"Eden! Don’t trust them! Turn your barrier into a physical defense!"

Although Eden hesitated, she couldn’t bring herself to repel those who appeared to repent.

"I was foolish… so please… die, you damned woman!"

An eerie, chilling feeling brushed Eden’s neck, blurring the line between good and evil.

Nade stepped in.

"I’ll handle this!"

Before Eden could stop him, thunder roared, and a massive bolt of lightning surged into the sky.

"You fools!"

The incarnation of the Thunder God’s Wrath roared furiously, causing the pale-faced priests to retreat.

But their pleasure-driven minds, whipped into frenzy, overcame even the fear of death.

"Ahhh!"

The priests charged forward recklessly, while Nade’s emotions turned cold and calculated.

"Lightning of Truth and Lies."

The Thunder God’s incarnation disintegrated into electrons and spread at the speed of light through the air.

"Urgh!"

The priests froze mid-step, unnerved by the tingling sensation that crawled over their skin.

Nade extended his hand.

"Those who do not repent, do not come any closer. If you defy my command, you will face judgment."

The priests exchanged glances.

When no immediate harm occurred, one of them cautiously stepped forward, his eyes pleading.

"I repent… this foolishness…"

CRACK!

As soon as he took a single step, a blue lightning bolt struck down from above, crashing into his head.

The priest collapsed, his limbs convulsing, while Eden stared wide-eyed.

"What… was that?"

The power remained fierce, but the Thunder God’s wrath was perfectly controlled.

"Nade, what is this?"

"Judgment of the Thunder God. It’s similar to a lie detector. It reads the brainwaves of the target and reacts automatically. If they deceive themselves…"

CRACK!

Another lightning bolt struck someone else’s head.

"This is what happens," Nade explained, his words carrying an air of finality that paralyzed the priests in place.

"Don’t try any tricks. I’m not like Shirone; I lack both the ability and the grace to embrace everyone."

That was the decisive blow.

While the evil priests were immobilized, those who had truly repented fought desperately.

"Lord! Please don’t forgive me!"

For those who didn’t want to escape the pleasures they were addicted to, the hearts of those who had escaped felt utterly shattered.

Iruki didn’t care.

"All this talk about gods everywhere."

He simply subdued the enemies in front of him, determined to help Shirone achieve his dream.

Cancellation.

The ability of Amidal, the Dreamer, was once again undone, and the priests’ divine power intensified.

"Arghhh! You brat!"

The fury of the demons, once treated like slaves, now erupted toward the sky.

"I’ll kill you all!"

Amidal assumed a stance, drawing his dagger to his side and lowering his upper body.

"Take this, my ultimate technique!"

Gripping the dagger tightly, he roared as he imagined a storm of sword energy.

"Endless Sky Slash!"

A technique meant to slice through a radius of hundreds of meters.

"Hyahhh! Hyahhh!"

As he rapidly swung the sheathed dagger back and forth, the priests abruptly halted.

"Arghhh! Faster! Faster!"

The onlookers watched him blankly, their faces filled with disbelief.

There was no sword energy, only a boy wildly swinging a dagger.

Iruki spoke with pity.

"What are you even doing?"

Realizing his ability had been canceled again, Amidal angrily flung the dagger to the ground.

"Damn it!"

"Play pretend with your friends at home. I’ll send you back there soon enough."

"You bastard!"

As the priests tightened their encirclement, Amidal ground his teeth, glaring at Iruki.

"It’s real! Damn it…!"

The priests’ divine power surged, and soon the sound of bones cracking filled the air.

Iruki turned his head.

"Where’s Shirone?"

Shirone was locked in fierce combat with three high-ranking Satanic Church elders who had their emblems glowing in the sky.

The evangelist Elka observed Yahweh’s heart through a mirror.

"This will be a huge win if we can convert him."

If Yahweh joined the Satanic Church, the ideological war would be over.

"What does he want? Should I ask him to play along?"

In her mirror, Shirone appeared with a sorrowful face, tears of blood streaming down his cheeks.

"How pathetic."

It made her feel like the ultimate villain.

"Why does he always…"

Angry, she smashed the mirror reflecting Yahweh with her fist.

"Make me out to be the bad one!"

As the mirror shattered, a powerful shockwave rippled through Shirone’s heart in reality.

"Ughhhh!"

The elder of the Satanic Church, Euphrathus, shaped the collected demonic energy into a spear.

"The Spear of Emblems."

A crimson, ominous spear charged toward Shirone with terrifying speed.

The Hand of God intervened but was pierced through the center of its glowing palm with a loud pop.

Elka smirked.

"We are the Catacombs."

The assassin branch of Yahweh.

"Demonic energy is different from magical energy. It’s a weapon made from the emotions of the humans you love. Therefore…"

Unlike the demons Yahweh had cut from his heart, this energy could clash with Miracle Stream.

"Roughly 30 million members of the Satanic Church."

Even with Shirone’s immense power, it wasn’t enough to withstand it alone.

"This is all for this one moment."

The leader of the Catacombs, Gyorgi, had forsaken a direct confrontation with Yahweh to establish the Satanic Church.

"Damn it!"

Shirone experienced the fleeting moment when the Spear of Emblem flew toward him at the speed of light.

‘Why?’

Why was the unification of evil so easy?

Even when burning his life to its limits, he couldn’t elevate a single human with Ultima.

‘Why?’

Why did evil gather so effortlessly into a massive tide?

Before he could find an answer, the Spear of Emblem pierced Shirone’s side.

Euphrathus let out a monstrous roar.

"Uoooooh!"

The electrifying thrill nearly drove him mad with ecstasy, but at that moment, Shirone grabbed the spear.

"It’s not over yet."

Gyorgi’s strategy was brilliant, but with just 30 million people, Hexa wouldn’t be broken.

"Yaaaaah!"

As Shirone gripped the spear, the light of Agape flowed from his body, traveling up the shaft.

Elka inspected her shattered mirror.

"What?"

The light swelled like liquid, exploding in a blinding flash that engulfed her face.

"Arghhh!"

Her eyes, nose, and mouth melted away, and she flailed her arms desperately. Shirone shouted.

"I will never give up!"

As the Spear of Emblem embedded in his side shattered, a new Hand of God emerged.

Elka crawled on the ground, screaming.

"Maximus!"

Hearing her cry, Maximus flinched and recalled her instructions:

— Convert Seina…

She had promised him the ultimate pleasure.

"L-Lord Archbishop..."

As Maximus's eyes darkened with hesitation, Seina stepped back hurriedly in alarm.

"Seina, I…"

Even if someone had passed through Eden’s barrier, there was no guarantee they had truly repented.

"Don’t come near me!"

Her hand reached for the holy sword Astasia, but the sacred blue light no longer flowed.

‘I’ve lost my divine power. I can’t fight like this.’

Elka screamed again.

"Seize her!"

At the same time, Maximus widened his eyes and lunged forward, grabbing her shoulders.

"Seina!"

Seeing the look of resignation in his eyes, Seina hesitated and couldn’t draw her sword.

"I’m sorry," Maximus said.

Kneeling at her feet, tears streamed down his face as he spoke.

"I have succumbed to the devil. Strike down the sinner who has defiled God’s name."

Elka pounded the ground in frustration.

"Arghhh! Damn it!"

The Hand of God soared in an arc and slammed into Elka, crushing her body with a thud.

Eden shouted.

"Seina! Get out of the way!"

Nearby, the King of Darkness, Gultan, activated quantumization and flew toward her.

"Nade!"

As Iruki screamed, Martial Artist Marit issued orders to the priests.

"Charge!"

The moment the priests, who had been frozen in fear of the Thunder God’s Judgment, were forced to move—

"No! Stop!"

Dozens of bolts of lightning struck simultaneously, slashing through the air and creating a cacophony of explosions as bodies were torn apart.

Amidst the bloody and visceral chaos, Maximus unleashed his divine power.

"Begone, you demon! Guh!"

The holy sword Astasia pierced through Maximus's back, emerging from his abdomen.

"Ah… Ahhh…"

Seina, who had stabbed the archbishop, let go of the sword’s hilt and stepped back slowly.

"W-why did I…"

Gultan had tampered with her mind.

Mental Craftsmanship.

He manipulated her into stabbing Maximus, lowering the walls of her heart. Then he gripped her soul roughly.

"Ughhh!"

As her mind was reshaped, red energy flickered in Seina’s eyes.

"… Lord Satan."

‘It worked.’

Gultan had successfully converted her.

As he disappeared into the ground with Seina, Nade’s lightning struck too late.

"Damn it! We lost them!"

Because it was a movement that ignored the bounds of space-time, their trajectory couldn’t be tracked.

Shirone surged forward.

"Mika!"

— Yes, coordinates 39398749…

Mika’s hyper-perception transmitted Gultan’s coordinates to Shirone as he moved through space in jumps.

‘He’s trying to evade pursuit.’

As Gultan darted around in every direction with a victorious smile, he glanced back.

"Ha, there’s no way they’ll ever catch…"

Shirone was closing in.

"Ahhh! Ahhh!"

A startled Gultan jumped through space again, but Mika never lost his trail.

‘He was targeting Seina from the start. To prevent the truth about the Papacy from being exposed.’

"Stop! Please! Just this once!"

Shirone roared.

"Stop right there!"

Gyorgi read aloud from the Devil’s Bible.

"Thus said the Lord, ‘Agrippa, why do you weep so bitterly?’ And Agrippa replied, ‘Because the woman I love has married my brother.’"

Ecclesiastics of Agrippa, Chapter 3.

"O wandering lamb, cast aside jealousy and resentment. Slay your brother and take his wife."

Agrippa hesitated.

"If I kill my brother, people will condemn me. If I take his wife, I will lose my position."

The Lord replied:

"Now all that was your brother’s is yours. None shall condemn you. Bury your brother’s wife in the dead of night if you must."

Agrippa knelt down.

"I am afraid. I fear that hypocrites will imprison me. I fear they will behead me."

"I shall be with you, and you shall gain all you desire without payment. Take what you will, and come to the sanctuary of murderers…"

The Devil’s Bible snapped shut.

"Melkidu."

"Got you…"

Just as Shirone’s hand was about to reach them, Gultan and Seina were engulfed in a crimson light and vanished.

"What?"

This wasn’t quantumization.

"Mika."

— They are gone.

"Gone? What do you mean?"

— They are no longer in this world. It appears they’ve exited a spatial dimension that can be tracked by coordinates.

‘Hidden Code. But to create a power this strong…’

Shirone furrowed his brows.

"Gyorgi."

It was Yahweh’s demon.
