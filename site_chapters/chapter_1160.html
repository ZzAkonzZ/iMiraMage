Chapter 1160:

Rian's great sword roared as it struck the ground, reducing the Satanic congregation hall to rubble.

Hell Prison couldn't believe it.

"Why isn't the Hidden Code working?"

All ten members activated their abilities at once, yet Rian's movements remained unaffected.

"No, it worked."

The proof was in his face—twisted in a demonic grimace, tears of blood streaming from his eyes. To him, at this moment, Shirone was the enemy who had massacred his family, the villain who had tortured everyone he loved.

"It worked, but it doesn't matter."

Rian's eyes were locked solely onto Hell Prison.

"I am Shirone's sword."

No matter how much Shirone changed, he was a knight who upheld the oath he had sworn to himself.

The Arrogance of One O’clock sneered.

"... Is

When Rian slammed his great sword into the ground again, a shockwave rushed forward at an overwhelming speed, too fast to react.

Pride of One O’clock rewound their decision, shifting Hell Prison's position, but the unease remained.

‘If being an idiot goes this far, it's a talent. This ruins our plan. At this rate, maybe we should just—’

She glanced to the side.

Even while Rian fought desperately, Shirone remained standing firm.

Company executives, led by Lette, had encircled him, their killing intent sharp as blades.

Shirone asked coldly.

"Are you planning to fight me?"

"As I said, our first principle is negotiation. But we cannot afford to lose Hell Prison."

Of course, her true motives ran deeper.

'We just need to stall for time until the ultra-ancient civilization is unlocked. It's almost there.'

The moment the connection to the Outer World was complete, the authority of the Administrators would strengthen exponentially.

‘Satan will handle it. If Yahweh is defeated in the real world, nothing will be able to stop the OOPArts.’

The OOPArts—Pharos' King Kitra.

Venistoff spoke.

"Make your decision now. If you accept our terms, there will be no need for conflict."

Shirone glanced at the Company executives.

'These are Administrators.'

Unlike demons, they wouldn't be affected by the Agape Mind.

'A battle of power against power.'

The smog of Hexa, devoid of Agape's emotions, burned around Shirone like wildfire.

Lette's gaze turned ice-cold.

"So, you refuse to compromise? Foolish. Is fighting demons all there is to Yahweh?"

"The only fool here is you."

Shirone's eyes sharpened.

"Release Jin Seong Eum."

"I already told you, it's impossible. She can't become like Hell Prison. Even you accepted that."

"Then we just need to purify the Organic Brain. Isn't that right?"

For the first time, Lette's eyes wavered.

"Of course, the Limbo have no karma. But that also means they can be purified at any time."

The agents' faces twisted in horror.

"The only thing preventing it is their attachment to life. If the essence of the Organic Brain is the resentment of those who fell into Hell without ever experiencing the joy of birth, then all we need to do is release that resentment."

"... How?"

Lette asked.

"You already know, don't you? Logic doesn’t work on Limbo. No method of persuasion will ever succeed. They don't even understand what resentment is. All they have is an overwhelming, indescribable obsession. I wasn’t lying to you."

"That's true. But you also didn’t tell the whole truth."

Shirone had finally identified the lingering discomfort that had been nagging at him.

"You know how to purify the Organic Brain, don’t you?"

"I don't."

Lette's lips trembled.

"Say it."

She couldn't reveal it.

If the Organic Brain—the Central Processing Unit—was purified, all karma within the Under World would reset.

'In such a world, demons cannot survive.'

It would be the equivalent of the sun suddenly vanishing—the very foundation of all life collapsing.

"I’ll say it clearly—such a method does not exist. But if you insist on hearing it..."

Hellfire ignited in Lette’s hands.

"Then you can hear it from your corpse."

As the agents' eyes burned with violet flames, a thunderous explosion erupted from the 12th floor.

It was the sound of Rian hunting down Hell Prison.

Meanwhile, the fragrance of Shagal's memories thickened the sealed chamber of the Karma Management Department.

Bound to the Chains of Taiji, Etella retraced the memories as if she were Shagal himself.

'Why is Archbishop Raphael here?'

Etella had met him when she was seven, drowning in the agony of Overflow.

She had no clear memory of that time.

Only the sensation of sinking into a deep abyss—and a slightly cynical child—came to mind.

In her recollection, Raphael asked:

"Name?"

"Shagal. Orphans are usually named after the place they were found, unless there's something special about them."

"I see."

Raphael accepted the child, studying him intently with his indifferent gaze.

"This child... his eyes?"

"Yes, he has almost no response to light. His vision was likely damaged by the sun. There are some slight reactions, but the doctors say he will eventually go blind."

Etella turned to Shagal.

'What?'

Blind?

Even Shagal seemed unaware of it, his face blank as he sank into thought.

'I'm blind? That can’t be. Then what have I been seeing all this time?'

He knew Tia, but he couldn't clearly picture her face.

Maybe even Etella's face…

'Was I… seeing through scent?'

The answer to his doubts came from Raphael within the scent of memory.

When Shagal was four, he could walk without any obstacles.

In fact, he could perform acrobatics far beyond a normal child's capabilities, thanks to his enhanced schema.

"Incredible."

At the time, Raphael, who was assigned as his caretaker priest, couldn't hide his astonishment upon discovering Shagal’s secret.

'Instead of seeing the world, he has learned to observe his own body in greater depth. Of course, he also has natural talent. His parents must have been incredibly physically gifted.'

The only peculiar habit was that he sniffed frequently as he walked, unlike normal people.

'Should I tell him?'

That he was blind.

'I don’t know. He thinks he sees the same as everyone else. Even his eyes seem to focus properly.'

It was the power of the Sensory Schema.

‘An astonishing sense of smell. His ability to extract information from the particles in matter is beyond normal comprehension.’

Raphael made his decision.

'Not yet.'

Someday, the truth would have to be revealed.

But Raphael didn’t want to burden a young child with that pain too soon.

After all, no one at the Karsis Monastery knew that Shagal was blind.

Raphael smiled.

“Shagal, let’s go inside. It’s time to eat.”

“Yes!”

Shagal's face brightened as he ran to his mentor, as cheerful as any other child.

It was a happy time.

Then, as the perfume of condensed time continued, it guided Shagal into the memories of his seven-year-old self.

"Hello. I'm Romi Etella."

A young girl, wearing oversized glasses and expressionless, bowed politely to the priests.

The present-day Etella inhaled the scent even deeper.

‘That child… was me.’

At the age of seven, she had fallen into Overflow and was taken in by the Karsis Monastery.

The priests welcomed her with warm smiles.

"Ah, so you're Etella. We've heard a lot about you. Don't worry too much, you have an extraordinary talent. You can become a great magician."

Hiding behind one of the pillars in the hall, Shagal pursed his lips into a small ‘o’ shape.

‘Oh, a magician.’

Even young Shagal found that fascinating. But what interested him even more was Etella herself.

‘She's… pretty.’

That must have been the scent he perceived.

A few days later, Raphael summoned the two of them.

"The Yin-Yang Wave Fist is a powerful martial art used to fight against the evils of the world. The appropriate training age is around ten, but Shagal, you could start now. And Etella..."

Raphael turned to her.

"Would you like to learn as well? You're the same age as Shagal, and physical training will help you overcome Overflow."

‘Learning martial arts?’

Her goal was to become a magician, but if it could help her overcome Overflow, she wasn’t in a position to be picky.

“Yes. I will do my best.”

The training began.

"First, hold each other’s hand and practice pulling and pushing against each other. You can treat it as a game at first. The important thing is to learn how to use force."

While Shagal’s heart pounded with excitement, Etella showed little interest.

‘He's the same age as me, not an adult. There's no difference in strength. I can win easily.’

Her usual competitors were adults.

“… Huh?"

The moment Shagal moved, she felt the strength drain from her body.

It was as if she had lost control of her own body.

‘It feels like he’s pulling my strength out. How is this possible? I shouldn’t lose when it comes to sensory perception.’

That was her mistake.

‘His senses are far beyond mine.’

Determined, she pushed herself to her limits, and their match continued for over ten minutes.

'They’re both exceptional talents.'

Raphael was pleased.

'Shagal's physical ability is beyond human. But Etella also maintains an excellent balance of mind, energy, and body.'

As she gritted her teeth and leaned forward, Shagal anticipated the final moment.

‘Now, if I reverse her force—’

Then, as Etella’s face drew close, Shagal froze.

‘This is… Etella.’

Sniff, sniff.

As he placed his nose against the nape of her neck, inhaling her scent, Etella jolted back in shock and shoved him away.

“What the hell are you doing?!”

Shagal tumbled onto the ground with a loud thud, and Etella shouted, unable to contain her anger.

"You bastard! How dare you pull something like that in a sacred training hall?! And you call yourself a monk?"

"I-I just thought you were really pretty… so I looked at you, that's all."

"Who in their right mind does that just because someone is pretty?! Just because you don’t have parents doesn't mean—"

"Etella!"

Raphael’s voice cut through sharply.

Etella flinched but was still seething with frustration.

"I'm leaving."

As she turned and left, Raphael approached Shagal, who was on the verge of tears.

"Are you alright?"

"I hate Etella. She has parents and a rich home. She just doesn’t want to be friends with me."

"Try to understand her. Etella is going through a confusing time. She has the mind of an adult trapped in a child’s body."

"No. Etella doesn’t understand. She doesn’t know what it's like to be an orphan. I would never treat someone like that."

"Shagal, all humans make mistakes. Countless mistakes, even without realizing it. But the fact that you can have these thoughts means..."

Raphael spoke gently.

"It means that someone has already forgiven you before."

Shagal trembled.

‘Why…?’

Why was Archbishop Raphael saying the same thing as Raiden, the leader of the Fallen Leaf Circus?

Etella turned her gaze toward Shagal.

‘Did I really say that?’

That couldn’t be.

She didn’t even remember Shagal.

Or maybe…

‘Maybe I really did forget.’

After all, people live their lives wrapped in countless acts of forgiveness they aren’t even aware of.

“Etella, I’m sorry.”

Shagal stood in front of her door, apologizing over and over.

“Please don’t be mad anymore. Let’s train again, like before. Okay? I won’t look at you anymore, I promise.”

There was no response.

Shagal lowered his head in disappointment and turned away.

And that was the last time.

The Great Purification.

A new universe began, and the next day, after their fight, Shagal was gone.

Of course, no one remembered Shagal.

As Etella continued training in the Yin-Yang Wave Fist, she looked up at Raphael, who was staring at the sky.

"Master, is something wrong? You look troubled."

“You don’t understand, do you?”

His chest ached for no apparent reason.

"It feels like something precious has left our side. And yet… I don’t even know what it is."

The lingering scent of the past—a powerful déjà vu.

‘What am I missing? Someone should be here, right next to me…’

Had the world truly changed beyond recognition?
