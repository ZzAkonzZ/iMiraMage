Chapter 866: Groggy (1)

"You don't remember?"

On the beach, around a campfire, Gaold, Kangnan, and Zulu sat in a circle.

"It happened before. It got better for a while, then worsened. But the biggest problem is..."

Gaold lifted his face, illuminated by the torchlight.

"There's no pattern to the memory loss."

He spoke indirectly, but Kangnan understood.

"Miro."

Even when his memory occasionally failed him since his days as the guild leader, he never forgot about Miro.

Gaold asked why he was fighting.

'The pain is so intense, it's causing him to lose his essence.'

Kangnan asked,

"What if you forget Miro?"

"It doesn't matter."

Gaold was firm.

"I'm not particularly anxious about that. Even if I forget Miro, it doesn't matter. But I'm telling you honestly because I'm worried about weakening our strength. If I forget Miro, I won't be able to unleash the power of destruction."

"That's the problem right now!"

Kangnan stood up abruptly.

"You don't even know why you're fighting! You're suffering so much pain! Let's go back to Zion right now! Tell Miro honestly! This is your limit!"

"And then?"

Kangnan shrank under Gaold's gaze.

"Leave. Take Miro and run away. Live happily somewhere. Miro said she would do that."

"...She did."

Kangnan realized.

Because Miro promised to do that, Gaold couldn't go to her.

"Foolish human."

Gaold gave a pained smile.

"Haha, don't make it sound so strange. I'm a magician too. I can't rest until I see the end. Anyway, it's not a good situation. Let's focus on solving the problem."

Zulu spoke.

"It's just that you don't remember. Your reason hasn't disappeared. If you stay calm and understand yourself, you won't make mistakes in battle. The problem is that your power has weakened."

Gaold rested his chin in his hand and fell into thought.

"Power..."

As a magician, being weakened by leaving Miro didn't feel good.

'Is there a pain greater than love?'

Above all, it was humiliating that his mind was measured by the level of pain.

"A shock therapy might be necessary."

"Miro."

Three people entered Miro's room, where she was meditating.

Behind him were the executives Armin and Kuan, and Lilia, who had now become a full-fledged magician.

"You're here?"

When Miro opened her eyes, the serene atmosphere disappeared, revealing her human nature.

She had become much thinner since Gaold left, but her beauty had not faded.

Sein asked,

"Are you really okay? You're eating too little. Your sleep time is extremely short. You can't fight like this."

"It's actually helpful."

The more her body withered, the sharper her mind became.

Although her body would eventually break down if this continued, she was preparing for the final battle.

"With the spiritual realm gone, there's no need for many people in Zion. We'll start our activities now."

That's why she called the three.

"Kuan, Armin, and Lilia, go to Tormia. Stop the army of hell there."

Kuan asked,

"Why Tormia? The demons haven't even reached Kazra yet."

"Half of the demons were wiped out by the minor purification, but the legion commanders survived. If they gather in the central continent, it's only a matter of time before they break through. Tormia is the only barrier. And..."

Miro raised the corner of her mouth.

"You want to go, don't you? To Tormia."

Olifer Sienna was there.

While Kuan fell silent in thought, Lilia nodded energetically.

"Yes. We'll leave as soon as we're ready."

The three who were to leave for Tormia exited the room, but Sein remained.

"Miro."

Knowing what Sein was about to say, Miro shook her head.

"Please leave. You're disturbing my concentration."

The conversation always ended there.

But now, with the spiritual realm sealed and activities unrestricted, Sein pressed harder.

"Are you really not going to meet him?"

Miro remained silent.

"They say he single-handedly devastated the south. He doesn't have much time."

"He'll hold on."

"No. No matter how strong the mind is, the brain is a physical organ. It has its limits."

She knew that too.

"Gaold won't come. So you have to go. Check his condition. This is an order as a soldier."

Miro bit her lower lip.

"What if he asks me to stay?"

Sein turned away.

"...It would be fortunate if he has that much strength."

The door closed.

The Gustav Empire was now as empty as when the planet first formed.

The demons were almost annihilated, but not entirely gone.

When the legion commanders left the empire, the quick-witted demons also fled in a hurry.

The surviving demons scattered worldwide, spreading what they had seen.

‘It rained. A bombardment of light’.

‘Everything was destroyed! Demons, the world, everything was swept away!’

The rumors spread rapidly.

‘It was the first time I saw such a terrifying Yahweh. Never, never make him angry.’

‘We'll all be annihilated! He won't forgive us!’

They were half out of their minds.

‘I saw it! I saw something I should never have seen! Ahhh!’

The demons who heard the rumors shuddered in fear of Yahweh.

Three weeks passed like that.

Shirone and Rian crossed the Akad Desert and reached the border of the central continent.

Rian, riding a horse they found two days ago, spoke to Shirone, who was sitting behind him.

"It's probably the Kingdom of Bornai."

It once was, but now it was devastated by the war with the demons.

Shirone was silent as if dead.

It was natural that he had no strength to speak after days of starvation, but the more serious issue was the wall in his heart.

During their journey, Rian told him what had happened in the Gustav Empire.

A bombardment that erased an entire empire in just a few hours.

If Shirone hadn't met Guy in some world, the planet would have been destroyed.

Since hearing that, Shirone hadn't spoken a word except for basic bodily functions.

'The shock must be great.'

Great power is a magician's dream, but only when it can be controlled.

The disaster of the minor purification was the first magic Shirone couldn't control in his life.

"Let's find some food first."

Unlike the desert, the central continent was a place where civilization flourished, so they should be able to find food.

After riding for two hours, they arrived at a city that had been severely destroyed.

Judging by its location, it was probably called 'Arka,' but there was no way to confirm.

As they stepped over the fallen gate, they saw the ruins of buildings dismantled into bricks.

Rian felt relieved to see rats scurrying among the decaying corpses.

"Hmm?"

Rian gently pulled the reins.

'Killing intent.'

It was a low-level killing intent that anyone with some war experience could sense, but there were quite a few of them.

"The horse, the horse."

People with haggard appearances holding spears appeared from all directions.

Giving up on an ambush and relying on numbers meant they were untrained civilians.

"Get down. That horse is ours."

They didn't even seem to have experience in looting.

"Who's in charge here?"

Based on the situation, Rian realized they were survivors living self-sufficiently in the ruined city.

An old man with missing teeth shouted.

"I said get down! That's ours! Do you want to die?"

It wasn't a threat to Rian, and Shirone didn't even look up, staring at the ground.

"Stop. He's a soldier. You can't handle him."

Looking up at the collapsed building, a woman with short hair like a man's was holding a longsword.

She nimbly jumped down from a height of four meters and approached Rian.

"Are you a straggler? How did you get here?"

"We're on our way home. If you let us go, we'll leave quietly."

"Where's home?"

"Tormia."

The woman's mouth curled into a cold smile.

"Must be nice. Still having a place to go back to. Leave the horse and go. We'll use it for food."

The horse was Rian and Shirone's food.

"We can't do that. I repeat, we don't want to fight. You're not bandits, are you?"

"Bandits?"

The woman was displeased.

"Seeing that you still have some conscience, I can see how you survived. Have you ever really fought in a war?"

To her, Rian and Shirone looked no older than their early twenties.

"You must be high-ranking nobles who don't know. The justice you shouted about is already dead. If you don't give up the horse..."

Rian didn't even reveal his killing intent, but if she crossed a certain line, her neck would be cut.

"Give it."

Shirone spoke.

"Just give it to them, Rian."

"Shirone."

"I don't want to fight anyone."

Shirone needed time to think.

"Haha, the young master is quick-witted. Yes, you can't throw away the life you barely saved here."

Rian made a suggestion.

"We'll give you the horse, but give us some meat in return. You can do that, right?"

"Some meat? Are you crazy? Even a handful of burnt seeds is a matter of life here. And you want meat?"

The woman turned away.

"We'll slaughter the horse and dry it. Follow me. We'll give you a bowl of porridge."

Needing to eat something, Rian got off the horse and carried Shirone.

They arrived at a temporary shelter made from the remnants of buildings, with a campfire burning.

Around 100 survivors were busy maintaining their camp or cooking porridge.

Though the area around the fire was warm, Shirone insisted on staying in a cold corner.

"Are these all the survivors?" the woman responded with a bitter smile.

"There were more at first. Most died from disease because there were so many corpses. Those still alive must have strong vitality."

The woman threw two blankets to them.

"Use these. One per person. Meals will be distributed in an hour. Follow the rules while you're here."

Rian quietly returned to Shirone.

"Shirone, cover yourself with this and try to get some rest."

He wrapped one blanket around Shirone's shoulders and placed the other over Shirone's knees.

A man watching from a distance scoffed.

"Are you lovers or something? Such devotion."

Caring for others in this shelter was a luxury and against the rules.

"Newcomers?"

A man's voice came from behind Rian.

Turning around, Rian saw a cold-looking man approaching with the woman.

"We got a horse. We'll leave soon."

The woman spoke, but the man ignored her and looked back and forth between Rian and Shirone.

"What are you doing?"

The man frowned and pointed at the two blankets covering Shirone.

"Didn't I say one per person?"

"I gave him mine. What's the problem?"

"No. There's no concept of 'mine' in this shelter. Only 'ours.' Take it back and cover yourself."

Rian could give up the horse, but he wouldn't compromise on Shirone's comfort.

"It's up to us how we use what we receive. What right do you have to interfere?"

"I'm the manager of this shelter."

The man opened his palm, and flames flickered.

"I'm a magician. Is that qualification enough?"

While Rian remained silent, Shirone continued to stare ahead with indifferent eyes.
