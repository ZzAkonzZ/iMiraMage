Chapter 1259:

Swordsmen from every nation focused their minds to break free from Ymir’s signature hallucinations.

“Urgh! Ugh!”

Countless soldiers vomited from nausea, but as expected of the carefully selected elites, the average time it took them to recover from the disorientation was less than a minute.

“Huu…”

And everyone soon realized—some of the scenes they saw during the hallucination were not illusions.

“Ymir is…”

The groaning Giant King twisted his face in pain.

“Grrrrgh…”

Since they had experienced firsthand the extent of Ymir’s power, the shock hit even harder.

Naturally, all eyes turned to the blue-haired swordsman with the incredible back.

“Everyone.”

Rian twisted his waist and smiled warmly.

“Long time no see.”

Clump fought back tears at the sight of his grandson returned from hell.

‘I must be getting old.’

If he weren’t the commander of a national army, he might’ve slipped up emotionally.

“Well. You’re finally here? Looks like you’ve grown quite strong…”

Before Clump could finish his sentence, Tess dismounted and dashed toward Rian.

“Hey.”

As Rian raised a hand, she dove into his arms.

“Whistle~”

Even in this situation, people whistling meant their nerves were thoroughly dulled.

Smiling, Rian asked:

“You been well?”

“Idiot.”

Tess looked up, her eyes glistening.

“If you were coming, you should’ve at least told me!”

“...Sorry.”

He knew it was a strange thing to say, but truthfully, there were many things he felt sorry about with Tess.

Ymir spoke up.

“Sorry to interrupt, but can I say something too? I’m actually the one who’s happiest to see him.”

It was Rian’s blow just moments ago that was the sole reason Ymir had crossed the universe.

Tess snapped.

“Don’t want to hear that from someone who tramples on lives like ants.”

“Oi.”

Ymir’s eyes sharpened, and his fist technique etched into Tess’s heart like a carved sigil.

“Guh!”

The attack would strike a moment later—it was fist technique, but even if it weren’t, it would’ve been fatal.

“The strong one is that guy, not you. If you have no right, don’t butt in.”

“Stop.”

As Asura’s aura spread from Rian, the pressure tormenting Tess vanished.

No attack came.

“Hah… hah…”

Tess gasped for breath, but Ymir no longer paid her any mind.

‘He dispelled my fist technique so easily…’

He was only interested in the monstrous Asura aura rippling from Rian’s shoulder.

‘Has he reached the level of a martial god?’

Asura Balbalta (The Buddha of the Battlefield).

‘That sensation just now…’

The perfect strike unleashed after mastering every battle in the world.

‘It’s not talent or tricks.’

Just the ultimate accumulation of experience through sheer effort.

‘How many people has he cut down?’

He was so excited he was itching to fight.

“You’ve grown. So this time it’s a mental awakening? Good. Then finally, you and I—”

“Why fight?”

Rian’s question made Ymir fall silent.

“Ymir, you are strong. The title of ‘strongest’ has always followed you. But you’ve done nothing. Your battles have no cause, no purpose.”

“…So?”

“If you’re not trying to gain anything by defeating me, if all you want is victory, I’ll give it to you. So, can’t we stop fighting now?”

In the silence, Clump thought:

‘That reckless kid…’

Now spouting words of such maturity.

‘No, honestly, he’s better than me. He must’ve endured incredible trials in hell.’

“What kind of nonsense is that?”

Ymir’s face twisted terrifyingly.

“Seen so much blood you think you’ve become a Buddha? No—you’re a demon. A demon who follows the scent of blood. Your realm is perfect for cutting people down.”

“Probably.”

Rian didn’t deny it.

“But I don’t want to win anymore. That’s all. If you want victory, you can have it.”

“Idiot.”

Victory meant nothing.

“No. Forget it.”

Ymir curled his lips coldly.

“I’ll give you a reason.”

As his killing intent grew endlessly, everyone in the field turned pale and looked away.

“Fall back!”

The speed of retreat varied by skill level, but to Ymir, they were all insects.

“Too late.”

His full-power strike slammed into the air, and compressed air burst out like steel.

“Die, all of you.”

He intended to open Rian’s lid—to make him snap—but space warped again.

“Hm?”

An aether wave.

More than distortion, space bent into what looked like a line, aimed at Ymir.

Boom!

Ymir’s head twisted 90 degrees as he was struck by the rebound of the steel-pressured air he had launched.

“…What the hell are you?”

From the rift in space, Jin Sung-eum revealed herself.

“Empress of Jincheon.”

As those who survived scattered, she said to Rian:

“From now on, I’ll travel the world gathering forces. I can’t help in battle.”

Her mission was strictly transport.

She learned aether waves were ineffective when she fought Ymir’s molars.

“Right. I’ll leave it to you.”

The giant’s army was on its way, so humanity needed to unite its strength.

As Jin Sung-eum vanished, Ymir grumbled.

“This place is full of gnats. Maybe I should just blow the whole area away?”

“No need.”

As Rian drew his greatsword, a killing aura like thousands of blades rushed in.

“I’ll face you.”

“Keheheh…”

See? It’s inevitable.

‘Guys like us…’

Ymir tilted his body like he was about to collapse, then vanished, kicking off the ground.

“I can’t live without this!”

Rian, charging in with the same speed, scattered afterimages by the hundreds, thousands, tens of thousands.

“Grrrrrgh!”

Each image, a memory of a different battlefield, a different fight, filled the background like a hellish tapestry.

‘Asura…’

An enlightenment surpassing all those experiences.

‘Balbalta!’

The most perfect, flawless strike pierced Ymir’s senses and dug into his side.

‘Shit!’

Ymir belatedly blocked the greatsword’s blade with his elbow—

‘Ugh, damn…!’

KWAHHHHHHH!

The flash had already carved a massive gash across the world.

In the underground temple of the Kergo ruins, Shirone’s group faced off against the newly reborn Nameless one.

“Where is he?”

It was a question with no context, but for a revenant, there could only be one strongest memory.

Kuan.

The swordsman who struck down Nameless.

As Shirone remained silent, Nameless stepped forward.

“No matter. If he’s number one in this world, I’ll run into him eventually after I kill a few.”

“A few?”

Nameless tilted his head.

“Why not? There’s no need to kill everyone. Once I kill one person, everyone at that level falls beneath me. Kill the next one, and all the ones around that level drop, too…”

A mechanism for determining the hierarchy of a battlefield.

“In the end, only the ones at the very top remain. I’ve already killed about twenty here, so that probably knocks down at least three hundred million under me.”

Shirone looked over the corpses of the Kergo tribesmen.

Mahatu…

Had they not held the chamber with their lives, the group might have been wiped out just a moment earlier.

Nameless is probably a vaccine program. In other words, an administrator.

I don’t know if he lost his memory because of the Missing Link or if he’s pretending, but…

There was nothing to gain from fighting him.

“Heh, they all think that at first.”

Gaold stepped forward.

“What’s this talk about number one? A rookie who still has baby fuzz mouthing off, huh? Come on. I’m ranked 5.5 trillion—I’ll take you on.”

“No.”

Shirone extended a hand to stop him.

“Nameless instantly masters any ability. There’s no need to show him our power.”

Even Gaold understood that much.

“So what? We can just take him out now, right? He’s someone we’ll have to fight eventually.”

“Heaven was different. We can’t just think about ourselves anymore. If Nameless copies our full power, it’ll compromise every plan we have moving forward.”

“Hmph.”

“The reason God chose him as a hitman is because he was designed with a law that lets him reach the top in a closed world faster than anyone else.”

A fusion of the Angel of Achievement and Garas.

“For now, let’s eliminate the wild card. I want to delay Nameless from reaching me as long as possible.”

Gaold asked, “You make it sound like you’re number one.”

Honestly, isn’t he?

“This isn’t the time for that. We need to regroup at the Holy Temple. Something’s not right over there.”

“Still—”

Just as Kangnan began to speak, the space by the wall warped, revealing a corridor from Delta Headquarters.

“Shirone.”

Seongeum had come to escort them.

“Oh ho.”

The group quickly picked up on Shirone’s plan and entered the Holy Temple corridor.

Miro glanced at Seongeum.

Perfect timing. So space is time, too? I understand now why Shirone tried so hard to save her.

Of course, her greatest admiration for Seongeum was probably her indomitable will—enough to face even hell.

As the group transferred to the new location, Shirone stood at the entrance, keeping an eye on Nameless, and spoke.

“I’ll deactivate the simultaneous event. Seongeum, please bring Zulu from the Pyramid of Truth.”

“Got it. Be careful.”

Once Gaold’s group vanished and the temple’s scenery turned back into a wall, Shirone’s expression hardened.

“Nameless.”

The rage of the Kergo people, who had to be sacrificed for humanity, was in his voice.

“You’ll never surpass Kuan.”

A world where being superior automatically made one the best—that was merely God’s logic.

As Shirone vanished after ending the simultaneous event, Nameless remained behind, deep in thought.

How to cut down Kuan…

The only way to overpower a movement that defied common sense was to dominate space.

As Nameless closed his eyes, the air began to tremble, and the scenery warped.

Ether Wave.

As Nameless’s body disappeared, a loud crash rang from the opposite wall.

He had crashed headlong into it and collapsed to the floor.

What had gone wrong?

“Ah.”

With insight rivaling that of God’s intelligence, he quickly began correcting the error.

It’s a lot harder than I thought.

Determined to do it right this time, he sat cross-legged and entered a deep meditation.

Time until mastery: 38 minutes, 27 seconds.

The Pyramid of Truth.

Since Shirone had left for the outer world, Zulu’s group remained at the summit.

There was nothing they could do but wait—but soon, a change arrived here too.

“Glenn!”

As Shirone activated Ouroboros, even Glenn, who had lost consciousness, began to stir.

“I want to live.”

Ares saw hope, but Arin, who had entered her first trance, saw things differently.

“Nothing has changed yet.”

As Shirone’s tachyon and God’s influence collided, Glenn’s state hovered between human and non-human.

“G-Glenn…”

Lucia reached out and held his hand.

She had collapsed from the shock of having both the hand of God and the hand of the devil within her, but—

Yahweh…

That experience had redefined her understanding of divinity.

Give Glenn the courage he needs.

For when we save ourselves, that is when God dwells within each of our hearts.
