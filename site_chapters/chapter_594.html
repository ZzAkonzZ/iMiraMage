Chapter 594:

Event Coordinates

Time: 1:57 AM

Location: Istas Warehouse No. 9

‘No one has come yet.’

Anchal, who was inspecting the interior, sat on Istas' control device, anxiously tapping her foot.

If time was infinitely looping, the person who activated the device would also return here.

“Damn it!”

Realizing this, Anchal understood one overlooked fact.

If the event in this room was already in the time zone where Istas had been moved, no one would come here.

‘The event has already been twisted.’

Thinking she needed to confirm this, Anchal moved towards the exit when Shirone burst through the door.

“Shirone?”

“Anchal!”

Shirone ran to her as soon as he saw her.

“Have you been waiting here all this time?”

“No, I just arrived. What happened?”

Shirone bit his lip and walked to the control device.

“I failed. The events keep changing, and I can’t stop them.”

“What are you talking about?”

Anchal, who had come straight here after parting with Shirone, didn’t understand anything.

“Damn it! What should I do? What should I…”

The way Shirone operated the device showed it wasn’t her first time.

As Istas vibrated, Anchal hurriedly stopped her.

“What are you doing? You said it’s dangerous to twist the events.”

“We’re all going to die anyway!”

Shirone shouted.

“We have to reverse it. I…”

“Tell me what happened. I haven’t encountered any events yet.”

Shirone turned her head with a devastated expression.

“It’s my mistake. I shouldn’t have let Yolga meet Miro from the beginning.”

“What?”

Anchal still couldn’t understand.

“There it is.”

Shirone arrived at the Supernatural Psychic Research Society, referencing Istas' equations.

When he opened the door, Goffin’s door, activated by Fermi, stood tall.

‘Miro hasn’t arrived yet?’

“Shirone.”

At that moment, Miro’s voice came from outside the door.

Event Coordinates

Time: 1:48 AM

Location: Supernatural Psychic Research Society

Before Shirone could speak, Yolga stepped forward.

“Miro, do we really have to go this far? Why do we have to fight?”

“I thought we finished talking earlier? If you block my way again, even as my sister, I’ll have to act.”

Yolga’s comrades scoffed.

“Do we look like pushovers? If you insist, you’ll be the one to die.”

“Stay out of this. I’m talking to Yolga.”

Edgar’s face turned red at Miro’s provocation, and his mechanical arm roared as the cylinder reciprocated.

“So you want to do this now?”

Edgar charged.

“I’ve never liked you! I’ll kill you right now!”

As Miro unleashed the Avatar of Kannon, Shirone intervened.

“Move, kid!”

Armand’s robe fluttered as tentacles swung rapidly.

“What the…!”

Clang, clang, clang!

Edgar’s mechanical arm created afterimages, shielding his body like a shield.

Although he blocked it, the two-meter giant was pushed back to where he stood.

“Grrr!”

The tremor traveled up his metallic arm.

“Edgar, are you okay?”

Mustang asked, not taking his eyes off Shirone.

“That guy isn’t ordinary.”

Shirone retracted the tentacles and spoke.

“Yolga, you promised.”

“Yes, I will keep my promise.”

Miro scoffed.

“Shirone, don’t listen to Yolga. You’ll lose yourself.”

Miro’s words made sense, but if he didn’t do this, he would disappear.

“You know the outcome is already twisted. If Miro can persuade Yolga, she’ll take her own life.”

Miro’s expression turned serious.

She knew Yolga’s character better than anyone, so it wasn’t a lie.

“In that case, can I join in?”

At the familiar voice, Shirone turned to see Fermi entering beside Miro.

“You…”

As Shirone began to speak, Yolga asked.

“Who are you?”

It was an instinctive attraction, and Fermi’s eyes wavered for a moment.

“I’m a merchant. The smell of money is strong here.”

But he didn’t reveal his identity.

He wasn’t here to indulge in nostalgia or reunite with his mother.

What mattered was the present and the future, not the past.

He was the most clear-headed person to confirm what had happened in this space and time.

‘Be cold. Be calm.’

“Nice to meet you. I’m Yolga.”

“I’ve heard of your reputation.”

Fermi shook hands and nodded to Edgar, Nine, and Mustang, whom he had called uncle and aunt as a child.

“You resemble Fermi.”

Mustang’s muttering had weight, but Yolga just smiled.

“No need for idle talk.”

Miro distanced herself firmly.

“No deals. If you interfere with the mission, I’ll kill you.”

“Miro, this isn’t like you.”

As Yolga approached, the Avatar of Kannon extended dozens of arms to block her.

“Sister Yolga, you have to understand. I regret that you followed me here, but I can’t stop.”

“Your actions will lead to everyone’s destruction. The Adrias family, the Ardino family.”

Yolga had already foreseen the future.

“I told you there’s a reason. I’ll guard the barrier after Goffin. That’s enough for now.”

“No reason can precede the outcome. I’ll take responsibility. Whatever the problem is, I’ll solve it.”

“If it could be solved, I wouldn’t be doing this.”

“Then tell me!”

Yolga shouted.

“Who is that child? Why is Goffin leaving? Why won’t you tell me?”

“Sorry.”

Miro couldn’t tell Yolga.

“Just die.”

The Avatar of Kannon swelled and struck Yolga.

With a bang, the floor shook, and Yolga’s comrades drew their weapons.

“Attack Miro!”

“No, it’s okay.”

Yolga’s voice made Miro’s eyebrows twitch.

Prajna-Saint Maria.

Yolga stood still, enduring the Avatar of Kannon’s space-time-defying bombardment.

‘This is mother’s…’

Fermi looked up at the avatar above Yolga.

A beautiful woman with only a face and torso raised her head to the ceiling.

Her eyes were closed, tears of blood flowed, and a chain gag was in her mouth, suggesting how much she could bear the world’s weight.

‘Annoying.’

To Miro, Yolga was also a good person.

But as an enemy, Saint Maria’s avatar, which absorbed any shock, was the most troublesome.

“Miro.”

Yolga spoke sincerely.

“Please. Just this once, trust my judgment.”

As Yolga placed her hands on her knees and bowed deeply, Miro’s expression twisted further.

“This is why…”

The Avatar of Kannon grew larger.

‘Sister can’t be allowed!’

Thousand-Armed Kannon Strike.

Yolga’s body shook under the violent attacks from all directions, and thicker tears of blood flowed from Saint Maria’s eyes.

“Enough is enough!”

Nine gripped his sword and charged at Miro.

“Hmph! Pathetic…”

As Miro prepared to counter, Shirone flew in and struck Nine.

Four tentacles struck the sword in succession, slamming Nine into the wall.

“Grr!”

“I won’t tolerate breaking promises.”

Like passing a baton, Edgar leaped at Shirone.

“Fool! Miro attacked first!”

The martial artist’s Skima was excellent, and Edgar’s figure vanished before Shirone’s eyes.

‘Armand can find him!’

As Armand relayed the image captured in the retinal afterimage and Shirone twisted his body, he saw dozens of fists flying simultaneously.

Clang, clang, clang!

When the robe was struck, the metallic sound made Edgar’s eyes widen.

“What the hell is this!”

Shirone, protected by Ringer’s armor, stepped back and compressed the Photon Cannon.

Without time to be surprised by the swelling light sphere, the flashes poured out in succession.

“Ugh!”

Edgar swung his arms to deflect the Photon Cannon, but realized the impact.

‘This brute…!’

The light had physical force.

Enough to shatter his arms.

Boom!

Edgar’s body, pushed to the wall, disappeared in the smoke from the flash bombardment.

“Grr!”

Reappearing, Edgar was completely hunched, hiding his body behind his forearms.

Just seeing his trembling figure indicated the force he had endured.

‘Edgar was overpowered?’

That was Mustang’s impression, but Yolga’s reaction was different.

“…Goffin?”

Miro flinched and met Yolga’s eyes.

Her keen insight quickly connected the dots, alternating between the child Miro held and Shirone, and for the first time, anger filled Yolga’s eyes.

“Miro, you…!”

“It was an unavoidable choice.”

“Miro!”

As Yolga shouted, Saint Maria’s eyes snapped open, and the chain gag broke.

Then, two arms sprouted from her shoulderless torso and charged at the Avatar of Kannon.

Prajna-Break Maria.

As Maria and the Avatar of Kannon clashed, Istas shook.

“Abandon it! That child is a time bomb!”

“Sister, give up!”

Yolga was too good a person to get angry without abandoning herself.

As Nine spoke, the surface of Break Maria began to boil like lava.

“It’s going to explode! Everything will be blown away!”

The power of all the lies Yolga had sincerely accepted in her life bursting out at once was as strong as the world's evil.

“Sister! No!”

That was why Yolga had come here.

‘This must end. That child must not exist!’

“Shirone!”

Miro threw Hexa and strengthened the Avatar of Kannon to its limit.

“Go through Goffin’s door!”

“Ugh!”

As Shirone extended his tentacles to catch Hexa, the child’s body glowed and disappeared.

Depreciation Transaction - Reverse Teleport.

A top-tier photon magic that teleports the desired target to oneself.

The caster was none other than Fermi, and Hexa was in his hands.

“Give me the child!”

“Hm.”

Fermi, looking at the child, gave a meaningful smile.

“Shirone, so this is you?”

Seeing his gaze, Shirone’s heart pounded.

“You, could it be…?”

But the madness was brief, and he approached Yolga, handing over Hexa.

“Here, it seems you need this.”

“Ah…”

Prajna-Saint Maria.

Yolga’s avatar closed its eyes, the chain gag returned to its mouth, and its arms disappeared from the shoulders.

Holding Hexa, Yolga knelt.

‘This child…’

Breaks the laws of the universe.

What that means is beyond even extreme insight, but the moment humans rely on something beyond human, the future disappears.

“Sister, you can’t do this.”

As everyone watched, forgetting time, Hexa frowned and began to cry.

In the silence, the child’s cries mixed with Yolga’s sobs.

“How could you… to a child…”

Yet Yolga was still a good person.

“Sister.”

As Miro approached, a sharp chain sickle flew and struck Hexa’s head.
