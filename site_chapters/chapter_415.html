Chapter 415:

Shirone and his group headed towards the mainland.

Although traveling on Zulu's summoned creature would be faster, it wasn't a good choice given the current conflict in Purgatory. The skies of Heaven were filled with angels, Maras, and drones, so they wanted to avoid being detected from a distance beyond Etella's spirit zone radius.

Contrary to their expectations, the Forest of the Profane was eerily quiet.

They had imagined angels patrolling the skies, dealing with heretics and rebels, but this was not the case.

Kangnam asked, "How should we interpret this? An unexpected change? Or evidence that the rebels have been wiped out?"

"Either is possible," Sein replied.

Plu tilted her head. "Is it possible for the forest to be quiet if the rebels haven't been wiped out?"

"Logically, no. But if you think about it, Miro's disappearance is the same. Anything can happen. We have to consider that for now."

Sein was feeling the same unease he had felt in Miro's spacetime.

In fact, the unexpected situation in Purgatory only heightened his anxiety.

'This is something I've been planning for 20 years. So why is it going wrong?'

Should he trust his current instincts?

Perhaps it was just a coincidence. Anyone could match the cause to the result after the fact.

Sein concluded simply.

'Don't overthink it. Just calculate both scenarios. I can do it.'

"Someone's there," Etella said, stopping and turning to the right.

It wasn't something she sensed nearby. Information was flowing in from her spirit zone, which covered a 2-kilometer radius.

About 20 individuals were moving with agility beyond the norm.

The sounds of weapons clashing and explosions were transmitted through her synesthesia.

"It seems they're in combat. About 800 meters away."

Plu glanced at Gaold and asked, "Shall we check it out?"

She needed approval to increase their mobility.

Given the unexpected situation in the forest, staying hidden was one option, but Gaold chose a direct approach.

"Check it out."

As soon as the order was given, the entire team vanished in a flash.

Zulu took charge of moving Kuan and Kangnam, and except for Shirone, everyone was a professional, so navigating the complex forest was no problem.

Shirone had also mastered movement control in his final year, but the difference in skill level was clear.

Especially Gaold, Sein, Zulu, and Armin, who moved through the complex terrain like ghosts.

'How much training does it take to move like that?'

When they arrived, the previous silence was replaced by a cacophony of noise filling the forest.

"Chase them! Rip out their hearts!"

"Kyaaaah!"

The Kergo tribe, clad in black armor, were emitting their characteristic shrieks as they pursued the Mecha tribe.

Their movements, in harmony with their mounts, were like a storm, but the Mecha tribe was not easily caught.

'What is that?'

The Mecha tribe's mobility came from mechanical devices connected to a skeletal frame outside their bodies.

The frame extended from their shoulders to their arms, down their spines, and to their legs.

With each movement, the joints clicked and whirred, propelling them 5 to 6 meters with each leap.

"Strength augmentation device, Piper. They're Mecha tribe combat soldiers."

The Mecha tribe moved through the forest as if they had springs on their feet.

But the Kergo tribe were also excellent hunters.

One of them, driving the Mecha tribe into a trap, suddenly leaped from the forest and swung a sword.

"Hup!"

A Mecha soldier raised an exd.

A holographic shield spread out, creating a shockwave, but the Kergo warrior's strength was slightly superior, and the soldier staggered and fell.

The Mecha soldiers gathered around their fallen comrade, looking around.

The Kergo tribe had blocked their retreat and were emerging from all sides.

The short-haired female squad leader of the Mecha tribe whispered into a drone attached to her wrist.

"This is Recon Team 2. We're surrounded by the Kergo tribe. Requesting support."

There was no response.

But given the nature of radio communication, she couldn't be sure the request hadn't gone through.

'Please, please...'

From 20 meters away, Shirone's group watched the situation.

The Kergo tribe prided themselves on their pursuit skills, but they were powerless against Sein's Equilibrium.

Even their breathing was reduced to a silent level by the Iron Wheel Eyes, rendering their senses useless.

Kangnam asked Gaold, "Which side do we choose? Kergo or Mecha?"

This was the first crossroads they faced in Heaven.

The citizens were divided into three tribes: Kergo, Mecha, and Nor, each with different combat methods, cultures, and mindsets.

Choosing the starting tribe would significantly impact their journey to Miro, so they needed to be cautious.

Rustle.

A Kergo warrior patrolling the perimeter of the formation appeared.

The warrior's eyes widened in shock.

It was natural to be surprised by the sudden appearance of 10 people in a place where even sensory schemas had detected nothing.

His head snapped towards his comrades, and he took a deep breath to shout.

"Ye..."

Gaold's hand moved swiftly.

"Gii..."

With a thud, the Kergo warrior's head flew off.

Shirone and Plu stared blankly at the headless, trembling muscular body before snapping back to reality.

Suppressing emotions and acting rationally was a normal human response, but Gaold's reaction had been purely mechanical.

"In any situation, if you think you need to kill someone, do it. I'll handle the consequences. Even if it's the wrong choice, there's no worse situation than a team member dying."

It was essentially Gaold issuing a license to kill.

It was a thoroughly correct approach for team survival, but saying he wouldn't question the right or wrong of decisions was something only Gaold, who could crush any situation with force, could say.

This was almost the only reason he was chosen as the team's commander.

Kangnam glanced at Gaold and asked, "So, we're choosing the Mecha tribe?"

There were many values more important than another's death, but the Kergo tribe was an organization bound by divine law.

Having already killed one, the barrier to compromise was now higher with the Kergo than with the Mecha.

"For Anke La!"

Gaold's eyes twitched in displeasure.

"We're going with the Mecha."

As soon as he spoke, Kuan sprang into action. This was a job for a hitman.

"Squad leader! We'll be wiped out at this rate!"

The Mecha tribe was barely holding on, taking a beating.

The Piper augmented their strength, but it was limited to large muscles, making it nearly impossible to win a close-quarters battle against the Kergo warriors, whose entire bodies were weapons.

"Retreat! I'll hold them off!"

The squad leader charged into the fray.

She knew what happened to those captured in heretic hunts, but she had to buy time for reinforcements.

"Cut off that woman's limbs and bring her torso!"

The mere words made her teeth chatter.

She wanted to run, but the Kergo warriors were already leaping to cut off her arms.

Whoosh!

A powerful gust of wind swept past the Kergo warrior.

"What...!"

The woman's eyes widened in shock.

The Kergo warriors' heads popped off their bodies like corks from bottles.

'Seven left.'

Spinning in midair, Kuan identified the remaining enemies.

He had killed six with one strike, and no one knew there had been twelve subtle changes in that strike.

"An enemy! Kill him!"

Only after losing half their strength did the Kergo tribe realize the attacker's presence and moved.

Kuan's body continued its strange movements, bound by threads of external force extending in three directions.

He swung like a pendulum, then suddenly shot forward, spraying blood from the bodies of four warriors he passed.

'Three left.'

Another warrior's head was severed.

'Two.'

The enraged Kergo squad leader's eyes widened.

They also used external force, but only as a supplementary means to cut enemies. Kuan's bizarre movements, which felt like mockery, were a first.

"Don't mock Kergo's techniques!"

As the squad leader swung his crescent blade, Kuan's path curved like magnets repelling each other, and he flew past the leader.

"Haha! Got you!"

A warrior with a ponytail, who had been lying in wait, swung his sword at the incoming Kuan.

"Huh?"

But there was no resistance.

Looking up, he saw Kuan floating in midair, unmoving.

Kuan glared at the ponytailed warrior in annoyance, then slowly turned his gaze aside.

Simultaneously, his body spun like a top, drawing him towards the squad leader as if winding a thread.

"Captain! Behind you!"

Before the squad leader could react, Kuan's sword took his head.

As the external force dissipated, Kuan landed and spun, throwing his sword.

The sword flew faster than an arrow, piercing the falling ponytailed warrior's chest and pinning him to a tree.

With the Kergo tribe annihilated, the forest fell silent.

The dazzling battle had lasted barely five seconds.

Shirone's group finally entered the scene.

They were inwardly impressed by Kuan's skill, which they had only heard about in rumors, but Kuan's face was twisted in frustration.

'Damn it! How much of a mess have I become?'

In the past, he would have cut them all down before a single breath of external force ended.

But now, one remained, and the last one was dealt with using an improvised throw.

"Mr. Kuan, are you okay? Are you injured...?"

As Siena approached, Kuan quickly wiped the expression from his face. He had no desire for her sympathy.

"I'm sorry. It seems my balance isn't perfect yet."

Siena tilted her head in confusion.

As a mage, she couldn't possibly understand what Kuan had done wrong.

After a moment of thought, she simply said what she had prepared.

"You were amazing. I haven't seen much swordsmanship, but I feel like my perspective has broadened."

To Kuan, her words were embarrassing.

He just gave a bitter smile and walked past her, leaving Siena to wonder if she had made a mistake.

Meanwhile, the Mecha tribe gripped their weapons nervously as Gaold approached.

Although these newcomers had saved their lives, it was difficult to clearly identify friend from foe in Purgatory.

Moreover, if a single swordsman had wiped out the Kergo squad, they were clearly outmatched in terms of skill.

"Who are you? Heretics?"

Gaold looked at Sein.

Some team members knew the language of Heaven, but more than half needed translation.

As Sein's mental magic translated the woman's words, Gaold took another step forward.

At the same time, the Mecha tribe flinched and stepped back.

The Pipers attached to their bodies clicked and whirred, adjusting the angles of their joint connections with each movement.
