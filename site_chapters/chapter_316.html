Chapter 316: One-Third (2)

Shirone shared his experiences in Kazra.

He left out the details about Armin, as Amy had requested, but there was still so much to tell. He had debated with a king, befriended Uorin, the daughter of the queen, clashed with high-ranking officials in the magic department, and demonstrated Ataraxia in front of the highest nobles.

His friends listened with their mouths agape. The scale of events was unbelievable for an eighteen-year-old. Shirone had been to the royal castle.

"So you got stabbed? You really almost died."

Shirone lifted his shirt to show the scar. The stitches were still visible.

Iruki asked worriedly, "Is your head okay? That must have been quite a shock."

"Luckily, after a few days of rest, I'm much better. My stomach still aches, though. Oh, and I brought an object back. Come to my room later, and I'll show you."

"What? Really? Weapons are banned on campus. You got really brave after getting stabbed, huh?"

Shirone waved his hand tiredly. "I didn't want to, but I had no choice."

Armand, too proud to be left behind, tormented Shirone with mental waves every night. He had to promise to bring it along just to get some rest. Like most demon swords, Armand had a very demanding personality.

"Let's go see it now. I can't wait! Diamond Armor! Direct Control!"

"Later. I have somewhere to be right now."

"Where are you going?"

Iruki nudged Neide. "It's the graduation exam. He has to spend the last night with his girlfriend."

Shirone shook his head firmly. "No, it's not like that! Amy followed me to Kazra to help. I want to go support her."

"Sure, sure. Anyway, you should go. The iron gate closes at sunset. Tell her good luck."

Shirone checked the time and was startled. "Oh? It's already this late? I'll be back. See you later."

Leaving the training ground, Shirone headed to the graduation building.

He hadn't sought out Amy immediately because the experiences of the incarnation were floating just beyond his consciousness. The memory of confessing his jealousy to Amy was so vivid that he wanted to hide in a hole.

If he had talked to her right after the incident, it wouldn't be so awkward, but circumstances didn't allow it. Shirone had collapsed in the carriage and was taken home, while Amy returned to school without a break to prepare for the graduation exam.

'Oh no, how am I going to face Amy?'

Shirone shook his head to clear his thoughts.

Amy had followed him to Kazra despite having an important exam. Without her, he wouldn't be attending magic school with a clear mind today.

'Right, for now, I won't think about anything else. Amy's success is the priority.'

She had entered the school at twelve, and now, six years later, her graduation was just a day away. She was ranked fourth in her class, an incredible achievement in just half a year.

'So I won't be able to keep my promise? Well, it doesn't matter.'

Shirone had promised to catch up to Amy, but she had worked tirelessly to reach her high rank. If she graduated, he would gladly acknowledge his defeat.

Passing through the iron gate, the symbol of the graduation class, he saw some seniors walking in the flower beds.

No one looked at Shirone. Today, they were absent. Their minds were entirely focused on tomorrow.

Some seniors were concentrating on mental unification, while others thought it better to relax. A group was gathered around a bonfire, chatting.

Many familiar seniors were there, including Amy and Seriel.

"Oh, there's Shirone!"

"Really?"

Shirone approached with a stiff face. He hadn't expected Amy to be with other seniors.

"Hello..."

Seriel ran up and hugged Shirone's face.

"Shirone! What are you doing here? Did you come to cheer me on?"

Buried in Seriel's chest, Shirone's cheeks burned.

But he felt her body trembling and realized she wasn't joking. He could hear her heart racing.

'This is the graduation class.'

Everyone here had dedicated their lives to becoming mages. And if they passed tomorrow's exam, they would achieve their dream.

The odds were one in three.

It seemed like a high probability when it was someone else's problem. But when he thought about it, the remaining 20 would have to endure another year of fierce competition.

With that thought, Seriel's embrace didn't feel embarrassing.

Shirone hugged her tightly. Everyone had worked hard. He couldn't imagine anyone failing.

But if possible... he hoped she would pass.

"Do your best, senior. I'll be cheering for you."

"Thanks, Shirone."

Only then could Shirone see Seriel's face. It was a mix of fear and excitement.

Even the expression that seemed on the verge of tears was noble. He knew how much effort she had put in to get here.

Shirone turned to Amy.

Unlike Seriel, her face showed no tension or excitement. She was fully prepared. Her six years of growth seemed perfectly aligned for tomorrow.

He felt relieved and grateful. If she had shown any sign of wavering, he would have been tormented by guilt for taking her to Kazra.

"Amy, you too. I'll be cheering for you tomorrow."

"Thanks. I'll do my best."

Amy's mind was like a great river, flowing as one stream of thought. There was no room for awkward memories of Kazra.

Shirone chatted with the seniors. Even the student council president, Amira, was there. She had led a sit-in protest when Shirone was trapped in Miro’s Time and Space, but now it was just a memory.

"Finally, tomorrow, this tedious school life ends."

Most nodded at Amira's words. No one thought they would fail the day before the exam.

But Samuel didn't look good.

Shirone remembered when he had come as Etella's demonstration assistant. He had amazed the advanced class students with his incredible ultrasonic magic.

But he had failed the graduation exam last year, and this was his second attempt.

According to the school's statistics, the chances of passing the second time were quite low, so it was natural for him to be nervous.

Amira comforted Samuel.

"Don't worry too much, Samuel. You've worked hard. You almost passed last time. This time, you'll get good results."

"Thanks. You too."

Since no one passed alone, it was humanizing to see competitors encouraging each other.

"Hey, you guys are all here?"

Famous seniors from the school approached.

Richard of Alchemy. Lycan the Electric Monster. Hershey of Sona.

And leading them was Ardino Fermi, the top-ranked student in Class One.

Fermi, always in a suit, looked more like an accountant than a mage. The Ardino family was known as a merchant family.

"Why do you all look so serious? Don't be so tense. I've been taking the graduation exam for six years. Haha!"

Fermi joked, but no one laughed.

It was a mystery why he, always the top of the graduation class, hadn't graduated yet. Some rumors said he was a broker who took money to influence the exam, but nothing was confirmed.

"Fermi, aren't you too complacent? If you keep this up, you'll be taking the exam with juniors next year too."

"Haha! Just doing my best. This is something you leave to the heavens."

Fermi felt a gaze and turned. Amy was looking at him boldly.

"Wow, our little lady is as spirited as ever. How's your condition?"

"Perfect. How about you?"

"Me? So-so."

"I hope you take the exam in top condition. Tomorrow, I'll be graduating as the top student."

"Haha! I can't beat you. Alright, let's compete properly. Tomorrow, we're not classmates but rivals."

As Fermi and his group walked away, those around the bonfire watched them warily. Their skills were one thing, but they were also veterans who had taken the exam at least four times, so their experience couldn't be ignored.

To change the mood, Amira spoke up.

"I visited the World Meteorological Organization last vacation. It was amazing. They have over a thousand professional mages."

"Wow, that's impressive. They say the head of the organization has more power than a king."

Weather controls human needs, food production, and ecosystems. That's why climate mages in the Red Line must pass a national exam, or using magic is a crime. Since there are no borders, international agreements are needed, and the World Meteorological Organization oversees them.

"Even if it's not the World Meteorological Organization, it's powerful. One of my relatives works at the observatory, and even high-ranking nobles can't ignore him."

"But you have to pass the national exam. Someone I know couldn't pass for ten years and had to change majors."

Amira spoke confidently.

"That's why it's a challenge. High risk, high reward is my motto. If I pass, I can aim for the highest position as a mage."

"Yeah, you can do it. If you get a position, make sure to contact me. Haha!"

Having been the student council president, Amira naturally envisioned herself as the head of the World Meteorological Organization.

In the future, when she realized the gap between the two positions, today's words might embarrass her, but what did it matter? Someone would achieve it, making it a dream.

Starting with Amira, everyone shared their aspirations.

Seriel initially dreamed of working at a health center and living a normal life, but now she wanted to develop vaccine magic at the World Health Organization. She believed vaccine magic would lower the cost of medicine.

Amy still aimed to be a soldier, but after visiting Kazra, she had a new sense of mission.

Amy still aimed to be a soldier, but after visiting Kazra, she found a new sense of purpose.

Listening to her, Shirone thought of Zion. When he heard how Zion had threatened Amy with war against Tormia, his blood had boiled, making him want to join the military himself.

Samuel planned to double major. While the Word Magic was important, he intended to study mental magic abroad.

"Wow! Everyone's got tough lives ahead. Let's give it our all! Tomorrow, we'll all become mages!"

"Yeah!"

Shirone felt his heart swell as he looked at the seniors taking their first steps toward their dreams.

He sincerely hoped everyone here would pass.

Morning arrived.

The Colosseum, where the graduation exam would take place, was packed with people.

Though not mandatory, the entire student body had signed up to watch, and most of the graduating class's parents had come to the school. Scouts from various national magic associations and reporters were also present.

Shirone couldn't believe the prominent figures he had only seen in newspapers were casually walking around. It felt more like a political event than a school exam.

"The graduation exam is really something."

Iruki commented nonchalantly, "It's like this every year. It's crazy. I just hope my dad didn't come."

"Shirone, isn't that Amy's father over there?"

Karmis Shakora and his wife Isis were standing arm in arm in front of the Colosseum.
