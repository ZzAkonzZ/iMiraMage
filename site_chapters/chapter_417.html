Chapter 417:

"Shirone?"

Kanya's voice trembled.

It was definitely Shirone. But how could he be here?

No, that didn't matter anymore.

If the person on the screen was Shirone, it meant new hope for the despairing rebels.

"Unit 5 is down! Provide cover!"

Hearing the radio, Kanya snapped back to reality and turned her head to the multivision.

Most of the units were immobilized, but they still had functioning weapons.

And they were all aiming at Shirone.

"Wait, no!"

Kanya hurriedly pressed the radio button.

"Don't shoot! He's not an enemy!"

When there was no response, she wasn't reassured even after sending the message. She checked on Shirone.

He was standing there confidently, fully exposed.

"Damn it...!"

She pressed the emergency escape button, and the reinforced glass of the cockpit shot forward with a pop.

Detaching herself from the Piper in the Guroi's synchro machine, Kanya crawled out of the bushes, raising her hands.

"Stop! Cease fire!"

The voice of the squad leader came from the speaker of Unit 5.

-Commence firing.

Click! Click! Click!

Kanya closed her eyes tightly, but all she heard from the remaining three Gurois was the sound of metal clinking.

"What... what is this?"

Looking around in confusion, Kanya realized the scenery was distorting.

At the center of the distortion, a pair of giant gears were interlocked and spinning.

Sein's Equilibrium had neutralized the chemical reactions in the gas.

"Duds! They're duds, sir!"

"Same here. Check the ignition plugs."

"No issues! Damn it...! Ugh!"

Boom!

Kuan and Etella took down one of the Gurois.

Though it took longer than expected given their skills, it was significant that the two, specialized in fighting large creatures, had destroyed the machine with their physical abilities.

The reinforced glass of the Guroi popped out.

The pilot, hanging from the synchro machine, was unconscious, and Armin stood beside him.

With precise coordinates, a spacetime mage could infiltrate even a sealed space.

The other team members also easily destroyed the Gurois, leaving only the squad leader's unit intact.

Gaold approached the Guroi with interest, stroking his chin as if encountering something unknown.

Instead of offering a handshake, he looked up at the purple-glowing machine and spoke.

"Get down. If you don't want to die."

Pop!

The reinforced glass shot up to the ceiling as the squad leader, still attached to the Piper, lunged at Gaold.

"Die!"

"No, sir!"

Kanya shouted, but the squad leader's sigma was already descending towards Gaold's head.

"Kanya?"

Gaold looked at Shirone. Then, as if it were a separate matter, his fist swung swiftly, striking the squad leader's jaw.

"Ugh!"

He fell pathetically, rolling on the ground with unfocused eyes.

If Gaold hadn't deactivated his press magic, the squad leader's face would have been obliterated.

"Do you know her, Shirone?"

Shirone squinted, looking more closely.

"Kanya? Is that you, Kanya?"

Kanya, finally relaxing, walked weakly towards him.

Thunk! Thunk! She detached herself from the Piper and ran into Shirone's arms.

"Shirone, Shirone! Waaah!"

The sudden shift from a deadly confrontation to a sweet reunion left everyone bewildered.

"Is it really... Shirone?"

There wasn't a single rebel who didn't know Shirone.

The first Nephilim to stop the Elixir of Myths.

The rebellion had been organized after the riots in Shamain District 73, and they called Shirone the "Light of District 73," honoring his legacy.

Kanya wiped her tears and gave a shy smile.

"Sorry. I didn't recognize you with that strange outfit."

Shirone didn't mind. Anyone would have reacted the same if they met an enemy in disguise.

"It's okay. How's Lena?"

"She's with the main force. She'll be thrilled to see you."

"Haha! And your parents?"

"Dad's still going strong."

"...I see."

Shirone didn't ask about her mother.

Even back then, she didn't have much time left, so he could guess what had happened since Kanya didn't mention it.

There is no good death in this world.

But Shirone hoped she had left happily, surrounded by her family.

"How long has it been since I left?"

"Exactly 432 days."

Kanya didn't need to think long. The day Shirone stopped the Elixir of Myths was monumental for the rebels, so there was no need to calculate.

'432 days.'

It wasn't much different from the time where Shirone had lived.

Goffin's Gate distorted space, so there was no time discrepancy due to distance.

The slightly faster time here was likely due to the gravitational field around the planet.

Shirone asked the question that had been on his mind.

"I heard you're at war with Heaven, but I haven't seen any angels or Maras on the way here. Why is that?"

Kanya shook her head.

"We don't know either. We fought fiercely, or rather, we mostly ran, but one day the angels just stopped appearing. Even on the full moon when they always patrolled."

Sein, listening through the mental magic link, interjected with a gleam in his eye.

"When exactly did this change start?"

"About 130 days ago?"

130 days.

Comparing it to the original world's timeline, it was roughly three months ago.

That was also when they lost contact with Gaold's loyalists who had infiltrated Heaven.

'Something definitely happened in Heaven. What is it? Is it related to Miro? Or...'

The squad leader looked at the damaged Guroi with a bitter expression.

If Shirone, the "Light of District 73," was here, there was no need to fight, but they had lost too much.

"This is bad. The damage to the unit is severe."

Shirone approached with an apologetic expression. The Guroi was too damaged to be repaired without extensive maintenance.

"I'm sorry."

"It's not your fault. We struck first."

The female squad leader, feeling somewhat responsible, showed her palms in frustration.

"But that man created a bizarre illusion first. If you experienced it, you'd see why anyone would think he was an enemy."

Plu interjected.

"But you aimed the arc first."

"He provoked us first."

"You misunderstood first."

The woman couldn't hold back and shouted.

"How could we not misunderstand with a face like that! He approached like he was going to hit us!"

A moment of silence followed.

When the consensus seemed to be that Gaold's face was to blame, the squad leader decided to drop the argument.

"Enough. Let's recover the units and rejoin the main force. They're not in a good situation either."

Sein asked, "Where is the main force?"

"The Plains of the Dead. They're currently conducting a giant extermination operation."

The squad leader walked towards his Guroi.

"This is the only unit ready for immediate deployment. I'll lead the way... huh?"

Gaold was standing behind the squad leader.

The squad leader, one foot on the unit, looked at him in confusion as Gaold gestured to the side.

"Move. I'm taking it."

"What? You're going to pilot this?"

Kangnam sighed and approached.

"Why do you want to pilot that? It's synced with the Piper, so you can't move it even if you get in."

Gaold's solution was simple.

"Then give me the Piper too."

Kangnam looked at Sein.

It was an official question to the team's order, asking if they should let this man do as he pleased.

Sein nodded.

Shirone would destroy Heaven, but Gaold had to handle the angels. He was the only one who could buy time against multiple angels, so it was best to leave combat matters to him.

"Let him do as he wants."

Five minutes later.

Clang! Clang!

Gaold, equipped with the Piper, boarded the Guroi.

The squad leader, having lost everything, could only stare into the distance.

Shirone, watching with concern, asked Plu.

"Is this really okay? We've just calmed things down, but taking their equipment seems..."

"He must have a reason. He wouldn't mess things up just out of curiosity, right?"

Shirone fell silent.

Plu, feeling a pang of conscience, eventually changed her tune.

"Actually, I don't know either. But everyone here followed him because he's that kind of leader. I just trust him too."

Gaold connected the Piper to the synchro machine.

As he moved his arms inward, the Guroi mimicked the motion.

"Not bad."

Without the reinforced glass, Gaold observed the surroundings as he moved the vehicle.

"Hmm, how about this?"

He rotated the waist joint to its limit, making the Guroi spin like a top.

"Hehe. Kangnam, look at this."

When Gaold playfully attacked with the Guroi's arm, Kangnam dodged with a slight lean and couldn't help but shout.

"Ugh, seriously! Should I just destroy it?"

Having already destroyed four Gurois, Kanya pleaded sincerely.

"Please don't. The Gurois are precious equipment."

"Fine. This guy is stressing me out."

Gaold finally examined various aspects of the Guroi.

While not comparable to the synesthesia of the spirit zone, the multivision provided excellent visibility, and the firepower was comparable to an air gun.

"How many of these do the rebels have?"

"That's classified. I can't tell you."

With a clanking sound, Guroi's gun barrel pointed at the platoon leader.

It was impossible to tell where the joke ended and the seriousness began.

"Even if it's confidential, I don't know. At best, I know the battalion's equipment status. How could a mere platoon leader know the total production of the rebel army?"

Gaold smacked his lips and stopped the machine.

Jumping down from Guroi, he threw off his piper and quietly joined Shiro's group.

Kangnan crossed his arms and glared.

"Why? You acted like you were going to ride all day."

"I'm getting motion sickness. It's worse than talking."

Kangnan snorted.

Meanwhile, Gaold's mind was getting complicated.

'That won't be enough. At least 2,000 units? No, more than that.'

This was the power of the Guroi unit that Gaold calculated was needed to capture a single Heavenly Angel.

At dusk, Shirone's group emerged from the forest.

When they reached a hill overlooking the Plain of the Dead, they saw the scene of the giants' procession and the 23rd Battalion of the 1st Command of the rebel army engaging in localized battles stretching to the horizon.

The battalion had a total of thirty Gurois.

However, since one team, including Kanya, was sent as support troops, only twenty-five Gurois were currently in operation.

The Guroi unit, divided into five teams, cooperated with the infantry to lure a few giants at least one kilometer away from the procession and then concentrated their fire.

Every time the giants fell, torn to shreds, Shirone made a horrified expression.

Giants were also human.

The Mecha tribe members, who were once citizens, knew this better than anyone, so Kanya's face was also shadowed.

"The rule is to eliminate giants immediately upon discovery. If they reach Jotunheim and achieve mental unity, they will wield the power of giants."

The platoon leader spoke as if making an excuse, but he wasn't wrong.

If eight egos overlapped, the schema's diagram would become as powerful as an excellent swordsman. At that point, a mere battalion's force wouldn't be able to handle it.

The group quickly returned to the main unit after entering the plain.

Seeing the group return with only one Guroi, the company commander initially looked incredulous, then eventually exploded in anger.

"What happened! You said it was a Kergoin! I sent five units to deal with it quickly, not to handle it like this!"

"I'm sorry."

The platoon leader repeated the same words.

After all, he was responsible for the platoon, so he had no excuses.

But Kanya was different.

Shirone had returned. And with much stronger allies than before.

This was a boon incomparable to the loss of tanks.

"Company commander! I have something to report!"

"Who are you! Can't you see I'm scolding the platoon leader! Are you ignoring the command structure!"

Although the Guroi pilot held the rank of a squad leader in the infantry, it was a great discourtesy to report directly in front of the platoon leader.

But Kanya couldn't wait.

With the sound of the giants' invasion line closing in, it was something the highest authority had to know.

"The light of Sector 73!"

"What? What nonsense are you spouting now?"

Kanya shouted, bursting with anger.

"Shirone has returned!"
