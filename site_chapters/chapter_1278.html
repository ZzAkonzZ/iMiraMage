Chapter 1278: Final Chapter (6)

Year 6, September 27.

It had already been six years since humanity freed itself from the laws of God.

The fact that there had been no official wars since the beginning of this new era was a miracle that would remain in human history.

Experts called it an obsession with purity.

"Who will commit the first crime? Which country will produce the first criminal? That’s the issue. No one wants to be remembered as the first villain."

A reporter asked:

"So you're saying humanity hasn't matured—people just don't want to break the illusion of perfection?"

"Humans are creatures of desire. It's possible crimes are already happening in places we can't see. Some countries may even be suppressing information. Still, I don't think this will last much longer. Once the first official act of evil appears, the second and third will come quickly."

Three years ago, the first official murder occurred.

The perpetrator was a 32-year-old lawyer named Reaper. What he said to the judge in court was simple:

"I wanted to be the first."

Humans are strange.

Just as the experts predicted, crimes began appearing worldwide—but arrest rates remained notably high.

And so the world continued.

They had come down from 100 percent unified consciousness, but not by all that much...

Back to ordinary life.

"I'm heading out!"

Amy, having taken off her uniform and wearing a dress for the first time in a while, tripped and fell at the front door.

"Puhaha!"

Ares burst into laughter.

"You’ve really perfected the art of falling. Don’t go around calling yourself a beauty."

"Shut up. This dress is so uncomfortable."

"Just wear it however. It's not like you're pretty anyway."

"Want to die? How long are you gonna keep lazing around at home? Go get a job or something!"

"Nope. Your allowance is more than enough."

"You seriously said that to your little sister? Ugh, whatever. I’m leaving. Make sure you eat."

Even while rushing out the door, Amy didn’t forget to check her reflection in the mirror.

Ares let out a bitter laugh.

"Geez…"

He understood his sister’s feelings.

Returning to his newspaper, he read a front-page headline.

"Yahweh! Flying from the South toward Tormia! Attending his alma mater principal’s wedding?!"

Shirone...

Folding the newspaper, Ares looked up at the ceiling.

"Amy’s going to cry at this rate."

Outside the gates of the magic academy, reporters from all over the world were already gathered.

"This is so embarrassing!"

Today’s bride, Vice Principal Olivia, grumbled as she tugged at her white wedding dress.

"Why? Isn’t it nice to be celebrated?"

Wearing a tuxedo, Alpheas was calm as ever.

"Are you kidding me? It’s bad enough getting married at my age, and people aren’t even here for me!"

"It’s an honor. One of our school’s greatest students is coming back."

Olivia couldn’t deny it.

"It’s not even certain. Even if he left the South yesterday, there are dozens of countries between there and here."

It was believed that Shirone had returned from the realm of infinite void three years ago.

Believed—because there had been no official announcements, and only a few acquaintances had seen him since.

Alpheas said,

"We really don’t know what it means to return from the infinite void. Maybe it’s something deeper than waking from a dream—something like facing pure emptiness."

"He hasn’t lost his heart. He’s still helping rebuild the world with the Twelve Apostles. Even Vincent says he seems the same."

"And that’s what’s strange."

If there had been rumors that he’d changed, that would be easier to understand...

What did you see, Shirone? In that true nothingness?

Olina asked,

"Do you think he’ll stop by home?"

Right now, Shirone’s birth mother, Ikael, was staying with the Vincent family.

"If he’s coming to Tormia, he will. He’s never once missed a visit."

Vincent muttered,

"Geez... People talk like my son has to take responsibility for the whole world."

Olina sighed.

"I’m more worried about Amy. Those two acted like they couldn’t live without each other."

"Maybe..."

Ikael thought to herself,

"Everything must have returned to nothing. Dreams, illusions—even emotions. It takes time to rebuild all that. But in the end, he’ll remember. He’ll remember the most important reason he belongs in this world."

Just like how Goffin once sent Hexa for her.

The South.

"Move those supplies to the open lot. This storage building’s full!"

Kangnan, having returned to her hometown, was helping rebuild southern civilization.

"Mister! Don’t climb over that—!"

Amid the chaos of the construction site, a familiar face appeared.

"Huh?"

Sein approached with a smile.

"Wow! It’s been so long! Has it been a year? What brings you to the South all of a sudden?"

"Something came up. Just thought I’d check in."

They moved to a quiet clearing and sat on some lumber, sipping drinks.

Sein asked,

"How’s Shirone?"

"He left last night. Blitz said he’d fly him there, so he should make it in time."

"Has he made a decision?"

"I don’t know. He’s hard to read. But if he’s going to the wedding, that means he intends to see her."

"Yeah..."

Sein asked again,

"What about you? Are you okay?"

Kangnan understood what he meant.

"I’m okay. Gaold isn’t in pain anymore. That’s enough for me. What about you? Are you in touch with Miro?"

"No. I’m not avoiding her or anything, though. Miro is finally living her own life—not as some divine guardian, just as a woman. At least for now, I don’t want her to feel responsible for anything."

"Yeah..."

In the end, their feelings were the same.

"You're living a beautiful love, aren’t you?" Sein said with a smile.

"Maybe," Miro replied.

"Go," Gaold said.

"I'm not going," Miro answered.

"Go!" Gaold repeated.

"I said I’m not going, damn it! Why are there so many events? Now I finally understand what Shirone went through."

As key figures in world peace, the two traveled from country to country attending ceremonies and events.

"Still, this time the event fee’s pretty generous. We don’t have a single coin saved, you know!"

"Don’t get too caught up in the world."

"Look who's talking. It’s because of all the trouble you caused. What the hell did you do for the Magic Association to blacklist you?"

Gaold grumbled in a teasing tone.

"I was busy saving you."

"Agh, seriously!"

Frustrated, Miro started swinging her arms, jabbing Gaold in the ribs.

"Ah! That was a bone—definitely a bone!"

Grimacing in pain, Gaold collapsed onto the bed, prompting a sigh from Miro.

"Why are you such a crybaby? What a waste of a body. Why do you even work out?"

"Heh, you know why."

Gaold, now lying on his back, spread his arms and winked.

"Still got some energy left, right?"

Miro glanced at the clock for a moment.

"Only 30 minutes."

"Amy!"

When they arrived at Alpheas Magic School where the wedding was being held, she spotted familiar faces.

"Tess! Seriel!"

Rian and Fermi were following close behind.

Tess said, "It’s been so long! Sorry, I’ve been caught up with promotion exams. How’ve you been?"

"Haha! Same here. You’re here for the wedding?"

Though Seriel and Fermi had ties to the school, Rian and Tess didn’t.

"Of course! Shirone’s coming, right? Just let him show up—I'm gonna deck him."

"Come on, it’s just a rumor," Tess said, though she clearly looked hopeful.

As they entered the venue, they ran into more familiar faces among the large crowd.

Canis and Arin, Maya and Kayden, Dante and Plu were also waiting in the guest seats.

And then…

"Amy!"

Always a source of laughter, but also someone who brought too many memories with him—

"Iruki! Nade!"

Two of the Three Idiots walked over.

When Amy saw the baby in Nade’s arms, her eyes widened.

"No way?"

"Haha! Yep. She finally made it into the world. Daisy, say hi. This is your Aunt Amy."

Amy burst into laughter.

"Daisy? A girl?"

"Yeah. Cute, right? Though to be honest, she’s still so new we don’t really know yet."

Iruki added, "It’s a fifty-fifty chance. If she wants to take after Liz, she’ll have to fight a tough battle with herself."

Nade retorted, "If she’s in a tough battle, then what about you?"

Amy smiled warmly.

‘They haven’t changed at all.’

When the rehearsal began, a crowd of reporters gathered.

"There are so many."

Knowing who they were waiting for, Amy’s heart began to pound.

A shadow passed over the outdoor garden.

"Hey, is that…?"

A massive dragon glowing with blue light glided through the sky and landed in the distance.

Then a voice rang out.

"Thanks, Blitz."

Blitz bowed politely and flew away again. A figure in a hood was left standing.

Shirone turned around with a smile on his face.

"Hey."

Camera flashes exploded like fireworks, and tears welled up in Nade’s eyes.

"Shi…"

All their friends rushed toward him at once.

"Shirone!"

"Haha! You guys been well?"

"You jerk! How could you be so busy you can’t even write a single letter?"

"Sorry. Things have been… huh?"

Shirone’s face lit up.

"Nade! You?"

"Yeah! What do you think? I created life before you. Daisy, meet Uncle Shirone."

"Hi?"

Shirone lifted her up, and Daisy gave him a bright smile.

"Haha! Seems like she likes you. Just as I thought—she’s my daughter, she’s got good instincts."

While everyone chatted excitedly, Amy stood watching from a distance.

‘What the heck? I thought something serious had happened…’

He’s exactly the same.

Feeling a bit of disappointment, Amy turned away just as Tess walked up to Shirone.

"What are you doing? Go see Amy. Don’t tell me you forgot everything in that infinite void or whatever?"

Shirone gave a bittersweet smile but didn’t answer.

The wedding started a little late as the school staff cleared the reporters.

During her speech, Olivia mentioned Alpheas’s late wife, Erina.

She spoke of her gratitude, her regrets, and the responsibilities she would carry until the end of her life.

Alpheas added briefly:

"That’s love in old age."

The guests burst into laughter, and the ceremony ended in a wave of joyful blessings.

And during the reception, for the first time in six years—Shirone and Amy met again.

"Amy."

As if by some unspoken cue, the hall fell silent and everyone turned to look at them.

"It's been a while."

"Hmph, now you show up? Do you know this wedding felt longer than the six years I waited for you?"

"I'm sorry. I needed time—time to be sure. To know if what I was thinking, what I was feeling… was really right."

Amy asked gently, concern in her eyes.

"What happened?"

"In the world of Infinite Nothingness…"

Shirone fell silent for a long time, but no one dared to even breathe too loudly. They waited.

"I didn't exist. Because I wasn’t there, there was nothing. No way to explain it either. I simply… didn’t exist. No time, no space, not a single thing."

A chill swept through the crowd.

"It must have been terrifying. But maybe it’s because there was no fear at all—that it was truly, perfectly nothing. And then… one moment, I existed again. In this world."

"I see."

What kind of heart must he have had, realizing only after existing again that he had been in absolute nothingness?

"Even now, I don’t know. I don’t know what happened there that allowed me to return here. I believe it was to meet you… but I had to be certain. Otherwise, the vast fear of that nothingness would swallow me up again, and I’d lose my sense of self. So…"

"You traveled the world?" Amy asked.

Shirone nodded.

"I worked for humanity, I took care of my family, I carried out every piece of my vision. One by one, I resolved everything… so I could meet you."

That’s how he spent those years.

"And now, I’m sure. The reason I returned from the Infinite Nothingness is..."

With all the courage in his heart, Shirone said:

"I love you."

All the illusions he had wandered through in a world without time, space, or even himself—

"You…"

—became complete certainty in this moment, and brought a smile to his face.

"You are my infinity."

In a world that felt frozen in time, Amy’s quiet sobs began to break through.

"Hhkk... huhuhh..."

Tess watched her kindly as Amy nodded through her tears, wiping her eyes with both hands.

‘I’m happy for you, Amy.’

As everyone looked on with warmth in their eyes, Nade clapped and called out:

"Alright, alright! Let’s get this moment on record! We won’t get another chance like this until Iruki’s wedding—and let’s face it, that’s never happening."

"Puahaha!"

Their friends gathered one by one as the same optical camera used for the wedding photos was set up again.

Amy, now smiling again, turned to Shirone before running over to join them.

"I want to be in it too!"

"Good. You stand in the middle. Fermi, you go over there. Shirone, what are you doing? Hurry up!"

"Got it."

Looking at the baby giggling between her mom and dad, Shirone thought:

We...

Someday, we’ll travel the stars.

"When I count to three, say ‘Daisy’ together, okay? You know how this works?"

We’ll learn what truly matters from the secrets of the stars.

"Alright, alright. It’s cramped! Hurry up."

On the day we overcome division and conflict and reach an integrated mental system...

"Okay, gather in. Taking the shot now!"

...we’ll be dreaming of another universe, from the edge of this one.

"Here we go! One, two, three!"

And so, we will endlessly, eternally—

"Daisy!"

—journey into infinity.
