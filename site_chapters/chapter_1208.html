Chapter 1208: The World We Live In (1)

Zet continued speaking.

“This world is made of particles.”

It was an obvious statement.

“And the Outer World is made of antiparticles. At least, that’s how it is by the standards of this world. Matter and antimatter define each other through the concepts of existence and non-existence.”

Zet continued calmly.

“Particles are categorized into photon signals and quantum signals. Photon signals govern laws, while quantum signals process emotion. Of course, for me, that would be an error.”

Shirone understood.

“If that’s how we exist, then what determines which being holds a higher status? It would depend on how much of the total system they occupy. That metric is energy.”

Existence is energy.

“And energy is mass times the speed of light squared. Therefore, you could say energy is mass.”

Mass is…

“It’s about how intensely one exists within the flow of time. Expressions like ‘heavy’ or ‘light’ are just definitions created by human senses. In reality…”

Zet clenched his fist.

“It’s simply existence.”

The space around his fist distorted, and shards and dust were pulled toward it.

“This is what we call gravity. And when gravity becomes too strong—so strong it exceeds the universe’s limit…”

Zet opened his palm, and a small black sphere formed, sucking in nearby dust.

“It pierces the veil of the world and leads to the Outer World.”

A black hole.

“It can be created, you see. A singularity with zero volume and infinite density.”

“…Yes.”

Both the Archangel of Existence, Metatron, and Shirone could create singularities.

“It’s not really a special trait. It just means that a substance has reached the limit of the universe. Nothing can grow stronger indefinitely. When existence exceeds the boundary, the universe cannot contain that signal and spits it out beyond the veil.”

Zet then erased the black hole.

“So we can create it, but we cannot enter it. Or rather, we cannot come back out.”

A space that even trapped light.

“Time ends at the singularity. Beyond that lies the world of antiparticles. Thus, the Outer World operates on imaginary time.”

“A divine perspective,” Shirone said.

Zet nodded.

“If particles are the concept of existence, then antiparticles are the concept of non-existence. So for particles, existing is natural, and the time of their destruction is only a brief moment. In contrast, for antiparticles, non-existence is the default, and their moment of creation is extremely short.”

It explained why antiparticles are so rarely observed in the universe.

“And so, through pair creation and destruction, particles and antiparticles generate matter and energy. That’s the mechanism through which our universe was born.”

Shirone mulled over Zet’s words.

“The means through which God influences this world is also antiparticles. It’s meaningless to say ‘still’ or ‘even now’ because we’re talking about imaginary time. When time is imaginary, it means space is less than zero—in other words, it acts faster than the speed of light. Put differently, it changes the laws by moving backward through time.”

Shirone recalled the Pyramid of Truth.

“That’s OOPArt, isn’t it?”

It explained why ancient artifacts seemed to appear without any cause.

“Yes. God’s will applied across time and space. That signal is called a tachyon. An imaginary signal born from the world of non-being.”

Shirone murmured.

“The world of non-being…”

If antiparticles formed the Outer World under the concept of non-existence, then from that perspective, their own world was…

Just one grand illusion.

“Do you want to meet God?”

When Shirone looked up again, Zet was sitting cross-legged in a posture of reverence, like a monk.

“To meet God, you must cross the veil of this world. In other words, you can never return.”

During his school days, he had once returned to reality through Miro’s manipulation of space-time.

And in High Gear, he had even mimicked divine thought to return using the Under Coder.

But all of that had been possible because reality held a higher status than the Outer World.

This time is different. To return to reality, I must understand the signal of the antiparticle.

Zet finally got to the point.

“I will tell you.”

“What? But how…?”

“I am an error outside the main system. I can’t override permissions, but I can still access them. I can connect to the Core and transmit the invocation for tachyon.”

The power of God, capable of turning all existence into illusion.

“It’s unlikely that the invocation alone will bring you enlightenment, but you’ll need it if you want to meet God. Otherwise, going to the Outer World in this state would be like crossing a river from which there’s no return.”

Like Nane.

“Of course, the same goes for me. Accessing the Outer World is an unforgivable error. I will be discarded soon.”

Shirone asked again.

“Why go this far? You’re still living in this world.”

“Who knows? Maybe because I am an error.”

Zet looked out the window.

“Ever since I realized that the countless ‘me’s outside and the ‘me’ here are not the same… I started to think this way. This miraculous mistake, this feeling that will never come again…”

He turned his head again.

“I just thought… maybe I should pass it on to someone.”

Shirone’s chest tightened.

“But if you do that, then you’ll…”

It was something he didn’t want to say aloud, but it had to be confirmed.

“You’ll die.”

This Zet was not the same as the others outside.

The death of this one—who had awakened to the heart and stood as the only one of his kind—would be no different than a human’s death.

“How will I die?”

With one hand resting on his knee, Zet gently placed the other over it, lowered his head, and answered with a calm voice.

“I think I’m ready.”

Shirone felt his heart warm, and after taking a moment to steady himself, he answered.

“Yeah.”

Then he knelt down before Zet.

“Please teach me.”

“There will only be one chance.”

Silence fell, and on Zet’s face screen, red Sanskrit-like script began to appear.

“Data output.”

Ikael felt a jolt of electricity run down her spine.

“It’s moving. We’re overcoming the Law.”

The seven archangels gathered there now represented nearly all the foundational concepts that had formed the early universe.

“Just one more step…”

As Ataraxia radiated with dazzling brilliance—

Transcend!

In that moment when their spirit forms ignited, the twisted laws that had bound them were rapidly restored.

That was how it felt.

Zet began outputting the data with immense speed, almost shouting.

‘I can’t miss this. Not a single syllable.’

Kneeling in front of him, Shirone desperately held on to his fading consciousness.

The sheer entropy—its informational density—rivaled the very blueprint of the universe.

A groan slipped from his lips.

“Hnnngh…”

Though Zet’s voice wasn’t grating, the syllables were a bizarre, incomprehensible sound—like nothing any civilization had ever conceived.

I don’t understand it.

Having reached the realm of Ultima, Shirone could decode any signal. So what he couldn’t grasp wasn’t the form, but the meaning.

It’s an entirely different concept. Not even the kind of thing a human mind can remember.

Had he not understood how gods thought, he’d have been annihilated within a second from the flood of incoming information.

“Ahhh—!”

As his pupils rolled upward and consciousness began to fade, Zet’s voice suddenly stopped.

It’s over!

Snapping to, Shirone immediately began mentally repeating the invocations at the speed of thought.

Number 1, number 2, number 23, number 328…

Committing the structure of each syllable fully to memory, Shirone looked up at Zet.

Maybe it was just his imagination, but Zet looked peaceful.

“Did it transmit properly?”

Shirone straightened and answered.

“Yeah. All of it.”

“I’m glad. It was… a pleasure.”

Hearing those parting words, Shirone shook his head.

“You can’t go yet. You have to help me analyze the signal. Thankfully, the main system hasn’t—”

Just then, a thunderous crash rang out as the ceiling came down.

“Watch out!”

Shirone grabbed Zet and rolled across the floor, eyeing the massive chunk of debris that nearly crushed them.

Huge…

The shaking of the world of Law itself—

It meant Ikael had succeeded.

Shirone looked at Zet again.

With the main system down, the lights on his body had gone dark.

“Come on, hang in there…!”

All the other Zets were probably in the same condition, but Shirone couldn’t leave.

“I didn’t even get a chance to say thank you…”

“What the hell’s going on?”

Marsha glanced around in a panic.

The Zets that had been fighting the Operator all collapsed at once, and the glowing central pillar had lost its light.

“We did it! Shirone pulled it off!”

The details were complicated, but none of that mattered.

“Can you do it now? You can do it, right?”

Too focused to respond, Lollipop Mark quickly attached the Under Coder interface.

All we need is the codebook.

Through a self-powered hologram, the initialization signal from the main system appeared.

“Got it! I have no idea what it means, but at least now I can make something.”

To compare it to human language, it was like a meaningless jumble of letters. But with the correct communication protocol, the data itself could attack the system.

With the Zets now unusable, the entire excavation team gathered to watch Lollipop Mark’s process.

“Hack it! Hack it!”

“Hold on!”

After tunneling into the database through several routes, Lollipop Mark looked at the storage size and swallowed hard.

“Um… how long do you think this miracle will last?”

“I dunno. Ten minutes? An hour? Why?”

“There’s some data I really want to grab, but it’ll take 2 hours and 14 minutes to download all of it. If the system reboots before that, it could counterattack us.”

“No, no, just get what we need.”

“That’s the thing—I don’t know where the necessary stuff is. The language is different, so I can’t pinpoint what’s important. But…”

Lollipop rotated the hologram.

“I can at least read the title of this file. It says Omega 999.”

“What? That’s in our language!”

“Yeah. I think records that existed before the future got weird were preserved as-is. Either way, Omega 999 is the last file that’s readable.”

After that point, all records were no longer written in human language.

“Hmmm…”

Operator rested her chin in her hand.

“So it’s a choice. Risk it and download everything, or just grab what’s urgent.”

Knowing how valuable information from the future could be, Marsha winced.

‘I want all of it… Damn, what a waste.’

Lollipop Mark said,

“Team leader decides. We don’t know how long until the system comes back online.”

“Ugh, this is insane…”

With a deep sigh, she made the call.

“Get the urgent stuff first.”

“Roger.”

As Lollipop pressed the hologram button, the gauge began to rapidly fill.

Everything happening in the real world—every piece of history—was now being transmitted in the form of the file Omega 999.
