Chapter 537: Such a Problem (1)

Miro's group, who had fallen into the Abyss, wandered through a ruined world bathed in twilight.

It was an apocalypse completely different from what they had imagined—an indication of just how far into the future the information had projected.

They had landed in a contaminated zone, and wherever they looked, the area was coated in grotesque mucus.

“Looks like most of the city is like this. What the hell is this stuff?”

As Miro stepped on the mucus with a wet slap, it rose up from all sides and tried to engulf her.

Rian swung his sword, slicing it clean through. The splattered mucus quickly reabsorbed into the rest.

Marsha removed the pipe from her mouth and shouted.

“Ah, seriously! Stop stepping on that stuff! How many times is it now?”

“But it’s kinda fun. Splat, splat.”

Fermi peered past the city’s edge and spoke.

“The sun’s going down. First, we should find somewhere to rest.”

Marsha exhaled smoke as she approached.

“Staying aboveground seems dangerous. Those weird flying things are worrying too.”

“How about that tunnel?”

Rian pointed at a subway entrance on the sidewalk.

“Good idea. We can’t enter the buildings anyway.”

Grrrr...

Just then, from around the corner of a block, a creature appeared on foot.

Although they hadn’t been here long, they’d assumed the mucus had driven off all surface creatures. This was unexpected.

“A monster?”

Its enormous lion-like face had a mouth torn all the way to its ears, with jagged teeth like nails driven in at random.

It was an Electric Liger, one of the few walking beasts in the Apocalypse.

Despite the mucus coating the ground, it walked straight toward Miro’s group. The mucus splashed up, but a bluish current surged across its body, instantly burning it away.

“It’s adapted to the environment. Electric-type monster, it seems.”

“I’ll handle it.”

As Rian stepped forward, gripping the hilt of his greatsword, Fermi raised a hand to stop him.

“No need. Every magic has its counter. And since I can use all kinds of magic, I’m the best fit for this.”

As Fermi swallowed a chip, the Electric Liger surged forward, electricity crackling from its body.

‘Lightning Thunder.’

Three powerful bolts of lightning crashed down, and the creature let out a distorted scream before collapsing.

“Of course, a true mage breaks through any affinity.”

Marsha pointed a finger accusingly.

“Oh please. You just didn’t want to waste money.”

“Well, there is that too.”

Miro smirked slightly.

Crafty guy.

The Apocalypse was a world made of code.

So Fermi had likely been testing whether the monster’s electricity was part of its own information or part of the world’s structure.

If it were the monster’s own, it would’ve been immune. But Fermi’s lightning worked—meaning, even though this is a virtual world, it’s structured just like reality, with avatars and laws separated.

Fermi tapped his chin thoughtfully.

At the very least, death here likely means death in the real world for us. Finding Shirone as fast as possible is the key.

Marsha asked,

“By the way, those casino chips—how many can you even swallow?”

“As many as my stomach can hold. Though overdoing it can cause headaches or nausea. Why? Thinking of making a deal?”

“If it's for the mission, maybe. I’ll think about it.”

Fermi studied her carefully.

She's a Regulation Exception user, right?

She used to extract magic the traditional way, but if she had undergone catharsis, her current abilities could be something else entirely.

“Anyway, what about your ability? I feel like I’m the only one being scrutinized, and I don’t like it.”

“Well, you’re the broker. Product demonstration is part of the job. I’m not ready to reveal mine yet—you know why.”

Fermi changed the subject and pointed with a finger.

“Nice tattoo. Mean something?”

On the back of Marsha’s left hand, which held her pipe, was a small tattoo— a cross-shaped dagger, the hilt swirling like smoke.

“Got it after I left Galliant Island. I’ve got a few others too. For example… here.”

Marsha slipped her hand under her skirt, pushing deep below her navel.

“Wanna see? Curious?”

Fermi shook his head at her suggestive look.

“I’ll pass. Not into that.”

Any further interest might lead directly to death.

“Let’s head inside. The sun’s setting.”

Led by Rian, they descended the stairs into the subway entrance. A derailed train lay at the end of the seemingly endless tunnel.

Miro lit up the space with a Shining spell.

“Good. No mucus down here. Still, let’s not stray too far from the entrance.”

Screeeeeech!

As soon as she finished speaking, a horrible scream echoed through the tunnel.

“Help! Please!”

“Eat! Eat! We eat!”

Unintelligible voices echoed down the tunnel. Miro’s group immediately prepared for combat.

“Hey, over here!”

A ventilation cover next to the tracks clattered open, and a man poked out his head.

“Don’t go that way. The Undergrounders will eat you alive. If you want to live, come this way.”

Yeah, like we can trust that…

No one was trustworthy in a place like this.

Screeeeech!

Another scream followed, and Miro made a decision.

“Let’s go. Whoever we end up fighting, at least this guy speaks our language.”

Come to think of it…

The man had been using their language.

After they dropped into the shaft beneath the vent, a narrow tunnel stretched before them.

Several dozen meters in, the man opened a metal door in the wall.

“Come in. This is my home.”

The makeshift house, built from an old engine room, was filled with strange objects—digital audio players, monitors, and unidentifiable devices.

“Honey, what’s going on?”

“We’ve got guests. Could you make some tea?”

Turning to Miro, the man said,

“Been a while since I’ve seen humans. You’re Abyss Walkers, right?”

“Abyss Walkers?”

The man shook his head like he’d phrased it wrong.

“I mean people who came through Under Coder. Intact humans without information loss.”

Miro replied,

“That’s right. Then you’re one too?”

“Happened a long time ago. I don’t know how much time’s passed in the real world.”

When his wife brought the tea, the man gestured for them to sit.

“Have a seat. My name’s Malta. I’m a mage like you. This is my wife, Curia.”

“Nice to meet you. I’m Malta’s wife.”

Miro sat on the sofa and asked,

“You’re a mage? Then why are you here?”

“This world is called Apocalypse. It’s part of the Abyss. Maybe I’m here for the same reason you are—I came looking for my wife.”

Fermi looked at Curia, who remained expressionless even through the conversation, and understood.

Her information’s damaged.

“My wife in the real world is in a vegetative state. I was a pretty successful mage. I tried everything, but nothing worked. So I came here—to this hell where she exists.”

“But she’s not real...”

“I know she’s fake. But what does it matter? You can't even imagine how much time I’ve spent in this world. Eventually, I came to a realization—my real self is probably already dead. And then, everything became clear. This is where I belong now.”

“There’s no way out?”

Malta shook his head.

“No. No matter what you try, there’s no way out of the Abyss. Some lunatic said otherwise, but…”

“A lunatic?”

“Name was Gaold. Total madman. Hair a mess, sunken eyes, skin and bones—Undergrounders wouldn’t even bother eating him.”

Miro’s eyes lit up.

“You met him? Where is he now?”

“He’s already gone. Said he was going back to reality. I asked how he planned to do that. Know what that crazy bastard said?”

Malta spread his arms in disbelief.

“Said if you want to escape hell, just go to the end of hell. Ha! Who can’t think of that?”

A faint smile formed on Miro’s lips.

You’re still pushing forward, huh, Gaold.

Even though the avatar had fallen asleep, the information that composed him continued to reach toward extremes, even in a world at the end.

“Honestly, it’s not like I didn’t try,” Malta said, pulling out a full-sized map he had drawn himself from beneath the coffee table.

“I tried to do what he said—go where he told me. But I couldn’t last even a week. Once you leave the city, it’s all desert. Desert after desert. That guy’s probably dead by now.”

“No, my friend isn’t dead. Not until I die.”

Fermi pointed to a marked dot on the map.

“Is this our current location?”

“Yeah. I decided to live here, so I mapped out everything I could. There are tons of underground facilities in this world. Some buildings have hundreds of rooms. A few of them are so filled with that mucus stuff you can’t even go inside. They call it Mucus here.”

“What about the tree and sun symbols?”

“Those represent Life Trees and settlements.”

Malta began explaining the species known as the Children of the Sun.

“There are three Life Trees in this city, so there are three settlements. Each one is ruled by a different Arbiter. They’re not exactly aggressive, but the problem is the Undergrounders.”

“The shrieks we heard from the cave?”

“Yeah. They look kind of human, but they move like they’re using Schema. Since they can’t photosynthesize, they hunt. They use weird weapons, too. Best to avoid them if you can.”

Malta ran out of things to say and held his hands out.

“If you want to take the map, go ahead. It’s no use to me anymore.”

“Oh, thank you—” Rian started to reach out for the map, but Fermi interrupted.

“No need. I’ve memorized it. For now, let’s rest here tonight. Tomorrow we can begin surveying the area around the settlements.”

Rian slowly pulled his hand back.

“Resting isn’t the issue. You can even use this place as a forward base if you want. I’m just glad to see people again. Well, excluding that crazy guy from before.”

“Thanks. We’ll take you up on that.”

“Feel free to use the facilities. Since I’m a mage, we have plenty of water. I even figured out how to recharge electricity. This world has a lot of conveniences. If you want to wash up, the bathroom’s open.”

As Malta smiled kindly, Marsha took a deep drag from her pipe and stood up.

“Then I’ll go first. I think I got splashed with some of that gunk. Feels nasty.”

“Just press the button. Water’ll come out.”

With a small wave, Marsha entered the bathroom, turned on the hot water, and began to undress.

“Whew… so tired.”

Letting the water pour over her body, she glanced at her reflection in the full-length mirror.

Seven tattoos marked her body: the back of her left hand, the palm of her right, between her breasts, below her navel, inside her left thigh, above her hipbone, and one in a place that couldn’t be seen. They weren’t real tattoos, but magical markings used to activate the Regulation Exception known as the Dagger of Depravity.

Shirone…

The wounds from her childhood at the hands of her adoptive father had been soothed through the catharsis given to her by one boy.

"You can kill me if you want."

The trauma hadn’t disappeared, but she had found the strength to confront her past.

It was something that couldn’t be converted into any material value.

If this could repay that debt…

Deactivating the Regulation Exception, the seven tattoos vanished from her body.

After finishing her shower and drying off, she noticed the unfamiliar toilet with its futuristic design and sat down, resting her chin on her hand.

“By the way… how much can I get out of this job when it’s all over? I really should think about expanding the business… Huh?”

After finishing her business, Marsha looked around.

“Toilet paper…”

Where it should have been, there were two buttons—one green, one red.

“What the hell…?”

She pressed the green button. A strong jet of water shot up from beneath the seat.

Startled, Marsha jerked up and stared at where the water had come from.

“What the— Is it a monster?”

It might’ve been nothing to someone familiar with such technology, but for Marsha, who had lived in a different era, it was a humiliating experience.

“I should just break this thing…”

She wanted to smash it, but as a guest, she couldn’t very well go around destroying someone else’s stuff.

Five minutes later—

“Ah, that was refreshing.”

Marsha walked out with a towel over her head, acting like nothing had happened. Miro, who had been planning with Fermi, turned to her.

“How was the water?”

“Clean. Oh, right. Hey, Miro?”

“Hm?”

A sly, catlike smile spread across Marsha’s lips.

“Don’t you need to use the bathroom?”

That night, at midnight, a massive sphere of light rose above the settlement in the city’s northwest.
