Chapter 872: All That Conflict (2)

Havitz regained consciousness three days later. Although dying was not an event that occurred for him, Balkan wondered if it could.

"What if he takes his own life?" Or an equivalent event.

"I'm dying of boredom. When are we going to fight?"

Smordo twisted his waist as if he was itching for action.

"We've already missed the timing. The next advance will be after the legion commander has gathered everyone."

"That's maddening. Isn't there something fun to do?" Zetaro asked.

"There’s nothing we can do right now. Just find something to do inside the barracks."

"Damn! Alright, Natasha, come with me." Natasha pointed at herself.

"Why me? I’m tired right now."

"What are friends for?"

Smordo abruptly grabbed Natasha's wrist and dragged her into the room.

"Eek."

Natasha made a face as she followed, and Zetaro shrugged his shoulders and went for a walk.

Balkan alone remained, sitting outside the tent, watching Havitz’s back.

"I can’t figure out what he's thinking." A woman's voice was heard.

"Is it already okay for him to move?"

The woman approaching with a child at her breast was named Deris. Unlike when they first met, she had lost weight.

"Why? Are you worried?"

"Not really."

As Deris soothed her nursing child, she asked.

"Why do you guys fight? It's as if you're desperate to kill people."

"If you don’t like it, you could leave."

"Where would I go? Where else would treat me as well as here? I feel like a queen here."

"Then you're on our side too."

Deris looked back at Havitz.

"You don't seem like a bad person."

"Good and evil are just criteria humans use to interact with each other. If there was only one person in the world, neither good nor evil actions could be performed."

Because there would be no others.

"Of course, killing people is bad. But from a cosmic perspective, there's no essential difference between killing a person and kicking a rock by the roadside."

"Why should we look at it from a cosmic perspective?"

"Because that’s where the answers are." Where do humans come from?

"There are guys like me. It's easier to maintain one evil than for everyone to perform good."

Balkan pointed at Havitz.

"You said he doesn’t seem like a bad person, right? That's because Havitz embodies an evil that transcends humanity. Whether he kills a hundred million people or violates women, such acts of evil presuppose the existence of others."

"Is he different?"

"Havitz’s thinking assumes a state where all humans have vanished. His desire is so vast it appears as if he desires nothing. Of course, if humanity were to vanish, the standards of good and evil defining him would completely disappear too..."

Deris kept silent, not understanding, as Balkan fell into thought.

"It’s connected."

Link.

If humans were to achieve a unified mental system, then the standards of good and evil would also disappear.

"No, hasn’t it already been achieved?" Gaia reached the ultimate.

"The missing link. Something specific evaporated between Gaia and humanity."

"What happened?"

Why do humans, divided into numerous personalities, fight over standards of good and evil?

The first human must have known.

Maya, who made a name in the Miss World pageant, had reservations for numerous events abroad.

Most of those nations no longer existed.

"Damn! How can we be so unlucky?"

At the gathering of Maya’s team, the head of the planning company, Palmers, burst out.

"It would have been a huge success! We could have made a fortune if it weren’t for the demons!"

The coordinator spoke.

"There were so many victims, and you're talking about money? It's lucky that Thormia still exists."

"I donated to the memorial fund in the name of the agency. Anyway, people who are alive need to live. Every event has been canceled, we might go bankrupt."

The manager said.

"Maybe we should rethink this? Events have been continuously canceled, but benefit concerts are thriving. If we connect with the Valkyrie to perform for the soldiers..."

"Hmm?"

Palmers brightened up.

"That's a good idea. Not just good, it's great. Maya will definitely be a hit."

She had the skills and the looks.

"The problem is the fierce competition. Not only do we need an offer from Valkyrie, but to electrify the soldiers, it takes more than just singing well."

That was precisely the issue.

"We need to expose more," the coordinator said.

"I've seen a morale-boosting performance before. Exposure isn’t the issue. It’s how provocatively they dance, I was blushing just watching."

"Really? How was the response?"

"Insane. The cheers were so loud I thought my eardrums would burst."

Maya bit her lip in thought, and the manager cautiously spoke.

"At least we need to match that level of intensity. Valkyrie is an army. If we can't boost the soldiers' morale, they definitely won't book Maya."

"Hmm."

Palmers, who had a soft spot for Maya, wasn't pleased.

But he was a showman.

"We can't ignore the audience's needs. There must be some compromise."

It would be best for Maya.

"Let’s think of a costume that matches the song. Not too sexy, but lively and healthy." After brainstorming, Maya’s team had a rough concept.

"Expose the collarbones and upper chest, and instead of wearing shorts, go for a skirt as short as possible."

The focus was the chest.

"Maya, what do you think?"

When no answer came, Palmers clasped his hands and pleaded.

"I know you want to compete with your singing. I promised you that. But you need to seize the opportunity now. Just the upper half of the chest, okay? Just that much."

"That won’t work."

Palmers sighed.

"Ah. Honestly, I also..."

"Listen to me to the end. My point is, it's too ambiguous like this. There's no distinction."

Maya, staking her life on her singing, thought they had to try to the fullest.

"If we lower the line further..."

It would explode in popularity, but the agency couldn't exploit their diva like that.

"How about this instead? Cover the top part of the chest but expose the lower part." Maya's hands moved across her chest area.

"Huh? The lower part?"

It didn’t make sense at first.

But as the image formed in his mind, he felt a thrill.

"That's incredibly provocative."

The coordinator and manager nodded.

It was just the difference between the top and bottom, but no one had tried such an outfit before.

Palmers, stunned, asked.

"Maya, are you really confident?"

"Honestly, it's unfamiliar, but with practice..."

"No, I mean about the chest. I mean, the line, right? It needs some firmness too."

"Yes, well..."

She had never thought about it.

"It's just a woman's chest."

That confidence was enough.

"That’s it, it’s settled! This will definitely be a hit! Manager, get the choreography ready, and coordinator, start on the costume! I’ll meet with Valkyrie officials."

Palmers rushed out excitedly.

Three days later, a concert was held in the capital of Thormia, Bashka, which had been scheduled in advance.

Despite the reduction in entertainers' activities due to the war, Maya was still popular in Thormia.

"Introducing the newcomer singer, Maya, who stirred up a storm at the Miss World pageant!"

Cheers flew in from all directions.

"Sister, I love you!"

"Maya, my love Maya! Wave your hand, please!"

Approximately a thousand audience members gathered, with Valkyrie's band officers also present.

"Director, is this okay? There are so many exiled entertainers that we're already at capacity."

There's no such thing as enough in war, but too many performers could dilute the soldiers' focus.

"We can’t just sit on our hands. I’m hoping for a proper idol to come forward."

Meanwhile, Palmers, waiting backstage for Maya's performance, was pale.

"What do I do? Can she do it?"

The coordinator reassured him.

"Why are you so nervously out of character? We know Maya’s talent."

"That’s true. But it's not about that this time. Will it really work? It will, won't it?"

The manager moistened his dry lips and said.

"Leave it to heaven. We’ve done all we can."

As the prelude played and the spotlight shone, Maya appeared wearing a coat.

A downpour from the sky... her self-written song "Light Rain."

Initially arranged as a ballad to utilize the outfit concept, the intense and longing emotions in her voice silenced the audience.

"Shirone."

Was he still wandering the battlefield?

"I'm coming to meet you."

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

As the song returned to its original tempo with a powerful drumbeat, cheers exploded.

"Now!"

Maya threw off her coat and raised her voice.

"Light, come down!"

The audience was momentarily stunned by the provocative outfit unseen anywhere else....

"Wow!"

Like a downpour, a thousand voices erupted in unison.

The outfit, with a collar up to the neck, revealed just the lower part of the chest at a carefully cut point.

While the waistline and belly button were exposed, the actual area of chest revealed was not very extensive.

About three centimeters high.

That three centimeters of exposure drove the audience wild.

"Maya! Maya!"

As everyone called her name, Maya also nearly lost herself in the high notes.

"Maya! Maya!"

At this moment, neither demons nor war mattered, only surrendering to the rhythm.

In the deafening sound that struck directly at the heart, the only expressionless people were Valkyrie's band members.

"Not bad at all. The singing is top-notch too.

What do you think, Director?" The director, watching Maya sing passionately for a long time, spoke up.

"Sign her up. Deploy her to the front lines immediately starting tomorrow."

"Understood."

The aide opened a file and added Maya’s name.

As much as the audience was excited, chaos erupted backstage.

"Yay! Yay! It's a hit! A hit!" The manager and coordinator held hands and jumped around.

"Light, come down!"

As Palmers, mimicking Maya’s dance moves thoughtlessly, saw the two, he scolded them.

"Hey! What are you two doing? Go get a scoop or a hook!"

"Why suddenly that?"

As they blinked and stared, Palmers frantically rubbed his fingers together and shouted.

"We need to rake in the money! Hahaha!"

The manager and coordinator realized.

"Ah, right! We need to rake in the money! Hohohoho!"

Maya's song reached its climax, but the audience's voices were so loud they couldn’t even hear her.

"Maya, I love you!"

Another magician who had graduated from Alpheus Magic School joined the crowd.
