Chapter 243: Magic Clash (3)

"I won't let this slide!"

Savina charged forward without even the energy to cast Haste. After all, her opponent likely wasn't in a condition to cast Plasma either.

At that moment, shocking words came from Nade's mouth.

“I lost.”

Savina, who was about to attack Nade's neck at the sudden declaration of surrender, hurriedly stopped her charge.

In her heart, she wanted to end it with her own hands, but attacking after a surrender was declared would result in disqualification.

Now that it had come to this, it didn’t even feel like she had won, leaving her with a strangely unsatisfied feeling. But she reasoned that Nade must have exhausted all his strength in the final clash.

"Hmph, you're lucky. Kneel and formally admit your defeat."

Nade kept his promise right away. Since he was already kneeling, all he had to do was move his lips.

“Yeah. I lost.”

Savina's expression twisted in annoyance. The reason for establishing this rule was to enjoy the sight of the loser wallowing in defeat. However, she didn’t sense any bitter emotion from Nade.

"Are you even a man? How can you just kneel so easily after losing to a woman?"

"I lost, so I said I lost. And honestly, it's really embarrassing, you know? Can I stop now?"

Nade hung his head low as if he had truly been defeated and returned to his friends. It might have felt less irritating if he had at least laughed it off, as if denying the defeat.

Sade licked his lips as he watched the retreating Nade.

'Let's try a little more. Savina was almost at the bottom too. Still, good job, Nade.'

The match ended sooner than expected. Every student in Class Four is bound to have at least one magic that has strong destructive power.

In actual combat, there's not often an opportunity to cast such magics, but in this match, the elements aligned well, and their skill levels were similar, allowing for a clean conclusion to the battle of strength.

Nade, who arrived at his friends, closed his eyes tightly and bowed his head in an uncontrollable feeling of regret.

“Shirone, Iruki! I’m sorry! I lost! Really, I’m sorry!”

“How can you say that? We fight as a team. I can’t guarantee I’ll win either. It's okay, you did your best."

Iruki added a word.

"You did well for yourself. You've improved since before."

Honestly, he didn't expect Nade to go this far. When he fought Lucas of the Parrot Mercenary, he had given up completely, but recently, he seemed to have gained some control.

"I’ll go back to the dorm and rest first. Is that okay?"

“Yeah. You must be tired, so go ahead."

Even Shirone couldn’t stop him from doing that.

And so Nade left the practice grounds, blending in with the crowd of students. But the place he headed to was not the dormitory, but a secluded park. Confirming that there was no one around, he finally let out his true feelings.

“Argh! Damn it! You idiot!”

He wanted to be Shirone's strength. But Savina was really strong, and if the fight had continued there, the price he would have had to pay would have been beyond imagination.

He still needed the safety of the academy’s boundaries.

“Hohoho! You were here? You were acting so calm, but now you look so embarrassed you want to die?”

Nade let out a sigh involuntarily. When he turned around, Savina, who had followed him, was standing there.

"Do you still have business with me? The match is over."

"No, I think there's still something left. Honestly, I feel unpleasant. I don't know why, but I get the strong feeling that you haven't fully accepted your defeat."

"I even knelt. What more do you want me to do?"

“I don’t know. How about kneeling again here?"

Nade couldn't afford to accept Savina's stubbornness. He just felt sorry for Shirone and was ashamed of himself for not being able to help his friend.

“Just figure it out yourself. If you want to spread rumors that you’re stronger than me, I won’t say anything, so just leave me alone. I’m leaving.”

Savina said as Nade turned to the dorm.

“I looked into it a bit, and it turns out the West Family is quite infamous, huh?”

Nade's steps suddenly halted.

“Worse beyond comparison. I heard the head of the family is a lazy gambler, and the lady of the house is a social outcast? They’re known for living in luxury despite having nothing…!”

A thunderous rumble echoed.

Savina, whose windpipe were suddenly blocked, saw the world spinning in a chaotic manner. When she came to her senses, she found herself slammed against a tree trunk deep in the forest.

Nade grabbed Savina's neck and leaned his face close.

“Say that again.”

“Gasp! Gak!”

Savina’s gaze met Nade’s, and she was paralyzed with fear. It wasn’t a human expression. He wasn’t just an evil person or a psychopathic murderer—he was simply a monster.

‘I’m dying….’

It was a conviction, not an assumption. It would have been better if she had been caught by a murderer, because there is no fool in the world who would expect mercy when bitten by a beast.

“You want to live?”

Savina nodded desperately. As her consciousness gradually faded, she lost control and wet herself without realizing it.

“Should I snap your neck? Or should I drain all your blood?”

Savina had no idea what he was saying. It was as if her head and body were disconnected, with her legs moving on their own.

“Hngggg! Hngg!”

Nade distorted his face and collected an electric current in his hand. He was going to evaporate her blood.

Just as the electric energy concentrated in his grip, a face flashed in his mind. Her voice gently calmed the beast within Nade.

'Nade, one mistake is enough, don’t you think so?’

Nade's eyes were bloodshot as if blood tears were pouring out at any moment. However, human nature gradually returned to his pupils, and he released Savina's neck and slowly stepped back.

“Keok! Gah!”

Savina collapsed to the ground, gasping for air. She still couldn’t fully grasp what had happened, but her lungs frantically sucked in oxygen.

“You won, I lost. That’s all that happened today. Got it?”

Savina nodded. She was ready to nod no matter what word came out of Nade's mouth.

But Nade seemed satisfied with that and left the forest. Just before exiting, he stopped. He slowly turned his head to look back at the massive tree.

‘Eek! What should I do? Did he notice me?’

Pandora, hiding behind the tree, felt her heart pounding. She had planned to get revenge on Savina but accidentally witnessed something she shouldn’t have.

Nade's voice was heard.

“Pandora, I’ve already taken care of the revenge, so you should go back.”

Mentioning her name was a warning not to escalate the situation. Though she couldn’t see him behind the tree, Pandora covered her mouth and nodded.

Nade left the park in a miserable mood. In the end, this was how it always turned out. He wished it would just rain and wash everything away, but the sky, like his mood, remained gloomy and dull.

“Iruki, I’ll never become a mage.”

The weekend of the second match had arrived.

With the match between Nade and Savina became popular, more people showed up than last week. In particular, many expected this week’s battle to be even more intense, since if Iruki lost, the third match would be canceled.

Perhaps that's why even the teachers who said they weren't interested in the kids' fight were sitting around, smiling awkwardly.

While Ethella, who was in charge of the refereeing, checked the items, Shirone and Dante and their crew waited on the side.

Savina's expression was not good. The trauma of the beating she suffered in the forest was still not forgotten. No, on the contrary, it was getting worse, and she was having nightmares every night.

As the time for the game approached, Closer began to warm up. He relaxed his muscles with a difficult stretch that was not typical of mages, then turned to Dante while running in place.

“I’ll finish this. There’s no need for you to step in, you know?”

The rule was that the first team to win two matches would win the showdown. However, Dante knew that would never happen as long as Olivia was in charge.

“Still, the showdown won’t be cancelled.”

“What? Why?”

“Because Master wants to trample on Shirone. She’s already submitted a special request to the Teachers’ Association. The academy won’t want this event canceled either.”

“I see. So even if I win, you and Shirone will still fight?”

“But the stages will be different. If we secure two wins, it’ll make Shirone’s defeat even more humiliating. That would probably be the best outcome.”

“Kukuk. Fine, I’ll set the stage perfectly for you.”

As Ethella gave the command to come to the center, Iruki and Closer moved forward. After the explanation of the rules was over, Closer looked down at Iruki as if showing off his height and said.

“When anti-magic comes in, you get a tingling sensation in your nose. But when you fight me, it won’t be just anti-magic. Have you ever had your nose broken? By a punch, I mean.”

Iruki did not answer.

“It hurts a lot. It hurts so much that tears stream down your face. From now on, I’m going to crush your nose just like an orc would. Even if you scream for me to stop, I’ll keep beating you. That’s okay, right?”

The corners of Closer's mouth went up wickedly.

“Why aren’t you saying anything? Scared already? But there’s nothing you can do. The match has started and you’re going to get beat up like crazy. In 10 minutes, you’ll be bleeding from your nose and screaming in pain. You’ll probably lose a few teeth too. That ugly face of yours will get even uglier.”

Ethella intervened.

“Refrain from unnecessary talk before the match. Both of you, proceed to your designated positions.”

After shaking his big fist at Iruki, Closer returned to his position and devised a strategy based on the information he had learned before the fight.

Iruki’s specialty was the Deviant Type with fast targeting and instant Heat Point Explosion. Above all, he had a strong Savant ability in Sequencing, so a preemptive strike was out of the question.

Closer wiggled his fingers as he prepared for the initial response, standing 60 meters away from Iruki. Since his strength lies in defense rather than offense, there should be no problem if he follow the standard.

“Begin the match!”

As soon as Ethella's signal dropped, multiple explosions erupted around Closer. His eyes widened in surprise as he covered his face with both arms. An explosion right at the start. Deviate Type magic typically shrinks as the distance increases. But the fact that it had traveled 60 meters without diminishing, even though Closer hadn’t taken a single step, was a testament to the highest level of Sequencing.

Shirone gave a bitter smile. He had already sensed it during the Speed Gun test, but the power of Sequencing using logarithms was incredible.

In fact, Iruki's Speed Gun score was the highest in the history of the magic academy, except for Shirone, who had unlocked Immortal Function.

The students were stunned by the sight of Closer’s massive frame being engulfed in flames. Even with a 50% sync rate, repeated hits could still be dangerous.

Finally, as the explosions cleared, Closer’s figure emerged. The earthen wall protecting his entire body crumbled, revealing his face, which was still smiling confidently.

“Kuku, is this it? It tickles like a mosquito bite.”

He strengthened his body with the passive skill Earthrise and even cast Earth Skin, which protected his body with a barrier of earth. Such explosions didn’t even register as an impact.

Closer then closed the distance using teleportation, forcing the fight into close combat, which was the opposite of Iruki’s preferred style.

“Rock and roll!”

As Closer swung his hand as if scooping up earth, the ground rippled, and the surface began to coil.

A boulder, growing to a diameter of 3 meters, rolled over the spot where Iruki had been standing.

Iruki escaped with teleportation. However, the moment his feet touched the ground, the surface crumbled as if a trap had been set.

“Kukukuk! This is the end!”

Using the Earthquake, a signature of Earth Magic, Closer immobilized Iruki and then linked it with the Eruption magic. An explosion occurred due to the frictional heat generated by compressing the earth. Nearly 5 tons of soil gushed out from the hole Iruki had fallen into.

Iruki shielded his face with both arms as he was propelled into the air. Meanwhile, in his mind, the explosive equation was rapidly unfolding.
