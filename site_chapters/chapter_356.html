Chapter 356: Magic Society (2)

After quickly getting ready and stepping out, Shirone saw the guards lined up in front of Plu. Hesitating for a moment, he quietly joined the end of the line.

Plu glanced at Shirone and then began giving instructions for the day.

“This is the Magic Association, the heart of the kingdom's magic. While many excellent magicians work here, there are also many criminals and a high risk of terrorism. Today, let's guard the third floor with impenetrable security. Remember, the Magic Association is the strength of the nation.”

Plu carefully observed the guards' faces before shouting,

“Now, disperse to your positions!”

As the guards scattered to their assigned areas, Shirone was left alone in the corridor. Plu beckoned him with a finger and turned around.

“Follow me. Our job is to patrol and manage the facilities. If you see anyone suspicious, question them. If they don't have an approved ID from the Association, you can arrest them immediately.”

It sounded like a significant responsibility, but Shirone doubted there would be many suspicious people at the prestigious Magic Association.

As expected, Plu's words were mostly bluster. After observing for about an hour, Shirone noticed that she had little to do. She received greetings from the guards, watched minor incidents, and then resumed her patrol.

‘This job seems pretty easy.’

He realized that the reason the job had become so comfortable was that many excellent magicians had held this position over the years.

Just then, the ceiling lamp turned red, and a siren blared for about three seconds before abruptly stopping, as if someone had cut off the noise.

Shirone looked around nervously, but Plu stood calmly, waiting for someone.

A guard came running from around the corner, but his expression wasn't particularly urgent.

“Excuse me, Chief of Security.”

“What happened? The siren went off.”

“Well... the control board of the magic control device overheated, and the internal circuit burned out. We've contacted the maintenance team.”

Almost all areas of the Magic Association are regulated by magic control devices. Although it's known that they don't work on second-grade Grand Magicians due to technical limitations, they can prevent most terrorist attacks and reduce damage from occasional staff conflicts.

“Hmm, that's been causing trouble lately. Hey, follow me. Let's check it out.”

Following Plu to the control room, they found alchemists and magicians working on the wall.

Shirone examined the control board of the magic control device with interest. Although the technology was also used in the 2000 system at the magic school, it was his first time seeing the internal circuit.

As expected, it was a masterpiece of precise magical engineering. Even though Shirone was now at a senior level, he couldn't fully analyze the circuit.

Plu approached the maintenance team and asked,

“How's it looking? Can it be fixed?”

“Ah, Ms. Plu. This will take some time. The board is completely burned out. This is the third time this year. It seems there's an error in the sub-control device, and we'll need to replace it entirely.”

Plu was deeply concerned about the malfunctioning magic control on the third floor, which she was responsible for.

“I've already reported it to the higher-ups. They might replace it with a new one in the next quarter's maintenance, but can we do something temporary in the meantime?”

No matter how strong the Magic Association was, it couldn't afford any gaps. A malfunctioning magic control device meant a serious security breach on the third floor, which couldn't be taken lightly.

“If we repair it, it might last another month. But the mutol has melted, and the Janes device needs replacing. These aren't standard supplies, so the Association doesn't have them in stock. We'll need to order them.”

“Hmm, but ordering will take too long...”

Plu bit her nails, deep in thought.

The magic control device was also a security matter, so it was under her jurisdiction. Usually, even non-standard supplies were kept in stock, but with 18 floors, the first to use them got them.

Plu looked at Shirone. Watching his blank expression as he examined the board, she finally made up her mind and spoke.

“Hey, you.”

“Yes? Me?”

Plu beckoned him closer. When Shirone approached, she led him to a corner of the control room and spoke quietly so the maintenance team wouldn't hear.

“I need you to run an errand.”

“An errand...?”

“Take a carriage to Gold Street. Turn right at the central fountain, and you'll find Akim Alchemy Shop. It's the Association's designated supplier. Go there and pick up a few items.”

Plu took a notebook from her inner pocket and wrote down a list of items. She tore off the paper and handed it to Shirone.

Holding the note, Shirone read the unfamiliar item names one by one. Although it was a simple errand, he was a bit worried since he was unfamiliar with such things.

“Okay, I'll go. But how do I pay?”

“You don't need to. The Association settles the bill at the end of the month. Just show them this.”

Plu handed Shirone her staff ID. It had a serial number and her name, Ravid Plu, with her certified 8th-grade rank engraved in bronze.

The photo was from two years ago when she had just joined, wearing large, round glasses, making her look quite different from now.

Blushing, Plu glared and shouted,

“Hey! What are you staring at? Put it away quickly!”

“Ah, sorry.”

Shirone quickly put the ID in his inner pocket.

For a woman, certain photos could be terrifying, and Plu regretted it immediately. But it was already done.

‘Ugh, I should have changed it earlier.’

She had thought about replacing the photo before, but work always got in the way, and she kept forgetting.

Feeling embarrassed, Plu cleared her throat and adjusted Shirone's clothes.

“Anyway, be careful. Don't flaunt it; keep it in your pocket. Not just anyone can carry a Magic Association staff ID.”

Shirone knew the importance of the Magic Association staff ID.

Even the bronze ID allowed access to over 90% of the kingdom's certified institutions and free use of more than 60% of the Red Line's facilities. It also granted arrest and prosecution authority over all classes except royalty within certain areas and allowed unrestricted execution of surveillance, infiltration, special operations, and special dispatch missions with national cooperation.

It looked like an ordinary plastic card, but it contained quantum encryption codes, making it impossible to duplicate.

Holding such a powerful card made Shirone feel a sense of responsibility. He checked the ID in his pocket once more and nodded.

“Yes. I'll be careful.”

Shirone turned and left the Magic Association. Although he had the chance to ride the elevator he had dreamed of, he was too nervous about the unfamiliar errand and chose the familiar stairs.

Taking a carriage to Gold Street, he found numerous shops arranged in concentric circles. The streets branched out in eight directions, each area specializing in different goods.

A central sign indicated "Alchemy Street." Following the arrow to the right, he found a building that looked five times larger than a regular one.

‘This must be Akim Alchemy Shop.’

Entering the shop, he saw eight rows of 20-meter-long shelves. None of the items on the shelves were the same, and even as a magic school student, Shirone couldn't identify most of their uses.

‘Nade would go crazy here. I'll have to come back with him after graduation.’

The counter was at the far end of the shop. There were about 20 customers, and in the corner, alchemists were sitting with dozens of items spread out, examining them.

The person at the counter was a sharp-looking young man. He was reading a book titled "Intermediate Alchemy," indicating he was either an alchemist or an aspiring one.

“Excuse me, I have a question.”

As Shirone approached, the man closed his book and stood up.

“Yes, what are you looking for?”

“I'm from the Magic Association. Do you have these items?”

Shirone placed the note Plu had given him on the counter and handed it over. Judging by the variety and quantity of items, he thought it would be impossible to explain.

The man glanced at the list and quickly understood.

“It looks like the magic control device overheated. I'll find the items for you. Can I see your staff ID? We settle transactions with the Association at the end of the month.”

“Ah, yes. Here.”

Shirone handed over Plu's staff ID.

The man went behind the counter and scanned the card. A beep sounded, indicating approval. He returned the ID to Shirone and said,

“Thank you. Please wait a moment.”

The man took a thin metal box and went around the shelves, collecting the items. Some alchemy materials were delicate and couldn't be placed just anywhere.

While waiting, Shirone hummed and looked around, tapping his fingers on the counter. When he turned towards the alchemy books, he saw two people in black hoods quickly turn their heads away.

‘What's that? Why are they wearing hoods? Are they magicians too?’

Shirone watched them a bit longer, but they didn't do anything suspicious and disappeared behind the opposite shelves, pretending to look for items.

Avoiding Shirone's gaze, they moved to a secluded corner. The man on the right whispered,

“Damn, they're from the Magic Association. What do we do? We can't carry out the plan here.”

“Revolution requires risk. Let's do it here.”

“But if we fail, we can't save our brothers. Let's move to Gold Tower. We prepared an infiltration route for situations like this.”

“Tch, fine. I wanted to save that place for last.”

The hooded figures quietly left the alchemy shop.

Shirone waited at the counter for about five minutes. The man who had collected all the items returned and placed a metal box on the counter. Despite the variety of items, they were surprisingly small and didn't seem too heavy.

“Here are the items. We can deliver them, but they won't arrive until after 5 PM. If you're in a hurry, you can take them now.”

“I'll take them now. They don't look too heavy.”

“Please handle them carefully. Some items are sensitive to shock.”

“Okay, I'll be careful.”

Shirone carefully carried the box out of the shop, holding it as if it were a newborn baby. Not knowing how fragile the items were, he walked as cautiously as possible.

As he reached the main street and looked around for a carriage, a loud explosion erupted from a block away. The ground shook, and terrified screams filled the air like a tidal wave.

“What was that?”

People poured out of the alleys onto the main street. The faces of those at the front were filled with fear, and their half-dilated pupils still reflected the shock of the explosion.

“It's a terrorist attack! Gold City has exploded!”

“A terrorist attack?”

Shirone looked up at the sky, where smoke was rising.

A terrorist attack in Vashka, the capital of the kingdom. It was unimaginable.

The citizens' reactions varied. Most frowned as they looked towards Gold Tower, but those busy with work cursed and hurried on their way.
