Chapter 400: Rapidly Changing Political Situation (4)

Kashan Empire.

The emperor's coronation of the Kashan Empire, the ruler of the northwestern continent and holder of one-third of the human realm's territory, was an hour away.

As always, the succession process was unofficial and secretive.

In the 23rd-floor spire of the grand palace Aganos, only the two Terajes, who shared the same memories, and a few who knew personal secrets were present.

The transfer of power from Teraje Mistra to Teraje Uorin.

To outsiders, it appeared to be a typical succession where a parent passed the throne to their child, but some knew the truth.

The only thing that changed was the name.

Teraje was the proper noun for 'something' that had lived through the ages, continuously renewing itself.

"With this, the handover related to the Holy War is complete. Well, if the Holy War is over, everything is done."

Fourteen hours ago, Uorin had visited the World Chamber in the central continent.

There, she was first introduced to the Holy War, and in an hour, she would ascend to the Triumvirate.

Even within the Holy War, the world's most powerful superpower, Teraje's influence was immense.

Below the Triumvirate were the Seven Kings, the rulers of the seven most powerful nations on the continent, and the two military leaders of the fairy and dragon races.

These twelve lords of the Holy War were the pinnacle of the world.

Uorin, who had become the ruler of the world from a princess of Kazra in an instant, showed no joy on her face. After all, everything was just a subset of the name Teraje.

"No need for idle talk. We've already verified that there are no discrepancies between your memories and mine. But I have a question."

Uorin stopped and looked back at Mistra.

"Why is this happening so quickly? I thought it would take another four years for me to ascend the throne."

Since the memories of the mother and daughter were shared, they were two yet one. Therefore, any inflection point in their thoughts had to be addressed.

"Hmm, well..."

Mistra looked back at Uorin.

While she had black hair and intense eyes, Uorin had white hair and a transparent impression.

Neither her husband, Orcamp, nor she had white hair. Hence, a mutation.

She was 'herself,' possessing the rare 'future vision' among the abilities of 'history search' that made the current Teraje possible.

Perhaps it was inevitable that Uorin would become the emperor among the many daughters.

However, today's coronation was an irregular move that even Mistra hadn't calculated.

"Don't worry. I'll ensure my disappearance. But there are memories that must be passed on since your birth, so I followed you to Aganos."

Once Uorin ascended to the Triumvirate, there was no reason for Mistra to exist in this world. However, there was something she had to finish in her time.

Advancing the succession by several years and quickly pushing things forward by accusing Micaiah Gaold of treason were for that reason.

Entering the room, Mistra took a locked box from the desk drawer. She handed it to Uorin, who was waiting on the sofa, and sat across from her, crossing her legs.

"This box contains all the answers to your questions. It's a secret of the current humanity that no one knows. If used well, it will be a powerful weapon, but given the situation, I had to act first."

"How do I open it? I can't break it."

"I have the key. I can't show it to you yet. Before that, there's someone I want to introduce to you."

Right on time, there was a knock on the door.

Only a handful of people in the world had the authority to enter the 23rd floor of the grand palace Aganos, so Mistra immediately told them to come in.

A tall man entered. He had a kind impression with narrow eyes, his purple hair slicked back except for one strand.

It was Gando, the former head of security for the Tormia Magic Association.

As the first son of Teraje Mistra, he was biologically Uorin's brother, but psychologically, he was her son. Bloodline meant nothing to Teraje.

Gando bowed to the ambiguous position of his two mothers.

"You called, Mother."

"Yes, come here. You handled this well."

For Gando, who had been adopted into Tormia and raised by the Ganza, the Tormia Magic Association was his whole life.

But it was all a lie.

Even joining Gaold's inner circle was a false life designed by his mother.

In that life filled with lies, the only genuine feeling Gando found was his reverence for Gaold.

But in the end, he had framed Gaold for treason and fled to his mother's arms.

"Oh, why the face? Is it because of Gaold? You didn't have personal feelings, did you?"

"The only person important to me is you, Mother. But... I respect his achievements as a magician."

"Sigh. Gando, you are Teraje's son. There's no need to respect a mere head of the Magic Association."

Gando remained silent.

The Triumvirate had the right to say such things. But having spent most of his life in Tormia, he still couldn't tell which side was true.

Uorin examined Gando and nodded.

"He's definitely different from Zion. This time, you chose well."

"Hehe, right? I didn't expect much from a son. Since he has talent, should we train him as a magician? You can use him if you want."

Gando lowered his head.

Chosen well? Train well?

What was he to Teraje?

A human? A creature? Or just a cog in the clockwork, meant to turn forever?

"Keep him? Hmm, I'm satisfied with Crouch. Well. Gando, come here."

Gando approached Uorin like a slave sold at the market. Every time her gaze pierced him, he felt stripped naked.

He couldn't show his discomfort.

Today, his biological mother, Mistra, would disappear. If Uorin abandoned him, only death awaited.

"Well, let's keep him for now. I'll need to focus on Kashan for a while anyway."

"Thank you, Mother."

Gando bowed deeply. His eyes, looking at the floor, trembled.

He would die.

If Uorin was Mistra's incarnation, he couldn't trust any word from her mouth.

"But I have two conditions."

Uorin chuckled. Setting conditions for Teraje of the Triumvirate meant he wanted to live that much.

"Speak. If it's something I can grant..."

"Ensure I don't conflict with anyone in Kashan."

He wanted to be recognized as an independent entity. If he survived and had to assist Uorin, it was a necessary measure.

"Alright. The second?"

"Don't make me criticize you. I'm just a warrior fighting blindly for you."

It was a plea to be treated like a dog. Desperate but unwilling to descend into abject humiliation.

It might have been a cold, rational strategy as a magician, but Uorin didn't care.

Raising a dog wasn't a bad idea.

"You've saved your life, Gando."

"I am grateful for your mercy, Mother."

Mistra waved her hand.

"Leave now. There's still work to do."

This might be Gando's last meeting with Mistra, but there was no maternal warmth in her tone.

As Gando left, Mistra took out a key from her bosom, as if it was time.

"The reason for the accelerated succession is to create variables. Gaold will go to heaven and do something. Ultimately, it will benefit us."

"Do you think he can succeed? A 20-year plan fell apart in an instant."

"Success doesn't matter. What matters is that something changes. Humans gamble on low odds because the reward of victory outweighs the despair of defeat. I just gave Gaold that chance."

"I still don't understand. I don't know what you're thinking. How can this be?"

"Of course. That's why you need to open the box. It contains a deja vu of 'underlying events' that only I know. Reading it will make my thoughts clear."

Teraje held all memories from ancient times to the present and could quickly search through them with the unique ability 'history search.'

Initially, it was just simple data retrieval.

But with the Goffin Reset, this ability evolved explosively. The history after the reset created 'underlying events' beneath the existing history.

Two different timelines sharing the same period created a form of future prediction through deja vu.

With 'future vision' added, Uorin could potentially conquer heaven in her time.

"The reason I hid it until the end was to prevent subjective judgment from interfering with history search. You need to see and judge for yourself. After all, I am you, and you are me."

Mistra walked to the desk. Her life's mission was complete once she handed over the key.

Uorin asked.

"How will you do it?"

"Anything will do, but let's keep it clean."

Mistra picked up a dagger from the desk. The blade, drawn with a swish, scattered the light in the room.

Uorin nodded and inserted the key into the box. Meanwhile, Mistra gripped the handle with both hands, lifted her head, and placed the blade on her neck.

Blinking at the ceiling, she suddenly remembered and looked at Uorin.

"Right, you said you'd take Shirone. Is that still your plan?"

Uorin shrugged as she opened the box.

"Ataraxia, and he's handsome, right? Nothing else seems important. Why, is there a problem?"

"No. Alright then."

Mistra lifted her head again.

She had a slight bad feeling, but it was also a common occurrence. The long history of Teraje was not weak enough to be affected by a mere boy.

As Uorin opened the box, Mistra's sword moved sharply, and a fountain of blood spurted out. A moment later, there was a thud as Mistra's body fell.

Uorin didn't even glance at her. Her interest was solely in the information inside the box.

The box contained documents on two matters.

The title on the cover of the first document was "Goffin Erasure."

Since the reset was estimated to have occurred 19 years ago, Uorin, born after that, had a hazier understanding of the underlying events than Mistra. However, this was not particularly important information, as it had already been understood by the Holy War and major institutions of various countries.

Uorin set aside the first document, a relic of the old era, and took out the new document underneath.

'This is it...'

Her hand, which had been reaching for the coffee cup, paused and then returned to grasp the document with both hands. She spent a long time savoring the title in the center of the document.

Anke La Erasure

Uorin quickly flipped through the pages. Storage first, analysis later.

In five minutes, she had memorized all 200 pages word for word. She then brought a candlestick and burned the document.

Uorin's eyes, staring at the flames, were sharper than ever. She finally understood what Mistra had intended to convey.

'You were right, Mistra.'

Gando knocked and entered the room. He hesitated for a moment at the completely changed scene but then returned to his calm expression and bowed.

"Mother, it is time for the coronation."

"I understand. I will go now."

Gando turned to look at Mistra, who lay on the floor.

She had been beheaded with a sword. Even without looking, he could tell it was a flawless cut.

"What should we do with the body?"

"Dispose of it."

Uorin's short answer indicated not to disturb her thoughts.

Gando carefully removed the body, making no noise with his footsteps.

She was his birth mother. But now, Mistra was Uorin. An existence beyond human common sense. That was Teraje.

Uorin strode out into the hallway.

At the end of the 23rd-floor tower was a balcony overlooking a square filled with hundreds of thousands of imperial citizens.

Drawing back the curtains and stepping onto the balcony, she saw the massive crowd stretching to the horizon.

A tremendous roar erupted from the ground upon seeing Teraje. The earth shook, and the heat rose to the top.

"Long live the Empress! Long live the Empress! Long live the Empress!"

Uorin raised her hand. The volume, which seemed unable to rise any higher, doubled, deafening her.

The whole world lay at her feet, but Uorin only looked down coldly.

Humans are unique creatures with as many personalities as there are individuals, but when gathered, they become a species bound by the single name of humanity.

Not someone, but the giant monster called humanity was roaring at her.

'Shirone, there is no time. Meet Ikael. You must change things. If not...'

Uorin looked up at the sky. Only the sun was higher than her.

'Humanity will perish.'
