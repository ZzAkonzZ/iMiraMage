Chapter 620:

Entering the first stage of the Survival Evaluation. Tier 10 monsters will be summoned randomly.

As the 2000 system activated, the students entered the Spirit Zone.

'Survival evaluation…'

This category was known to be polarizing among students. It involved eliminating creatures from Tier 10 to Tier 1, as listed in the Red Line Bestiary, over ten stages.

"Gather in the center!"

Without needing to be told twice, all 30 students converged at the center of the colosseum. The 2000 system randomly summoned creatures like King Tarantulas, Sulfur Wolves, and Underground Lizards from remote natural environments.

"What the…!"

While veteran graduates had experienced all six categories, more than half were facing the Survival Evaluation for the first time.

Summoning complete: 1,429 Tier 10 creatures.

The primary mission was enemy annihilation, but it was ultimately a survival test. The colosseum quickly filled with insects, beasts, reptiles, and all sorts of vicious creatures, making the students shiver.

"They're coming!"

As if the ground was shaking, all the creatures charged at the students.

"Heh, as a service…"

Fermi's Blizzard created a snowstorm across the area, and the remaining students cast their specialized magic.

The dazzling array of colors lasted only a moment before the 1,429 creatures vanished within three seconds of appearing.

Stage 1 complete. Stage 2 will begin in 30 seconds.

Each stage allowed 10 minutes for annihilation. If the time exceeded 10 minutes, the next wave of creatures would be summoned regardless of the remaining creatures. If they succeeded within the time limit, they received a 30-second rest.

Scouts from around the world were busy, and the Tormia Magic Association scout team, including Baikal, Elizabeth, and Lara, began their work.

Elizabeth, wearing a bracelet connected to the 2000 main system, saw her retina light up.

"Annihilation time: 3.24 seconds. Total magic power: 23,409 magicles. Highest value: Fermi's 11,000 magicles. Lowest value: Eden's 0 magicles."

Lara's floating notebook opened, and a pen moved rapidly through writing magic.

"Hmm, 11,000 magicles."

It was hard to differentiate students in the first stage of Survival Evaluation, but precise control of magic was noteworthy.

"Participant number 1, Ardino Fermi. Controlled magic in the tens of thousands without error."

"He did it on purpose, right?"

Baikal nodded at Lara's question.

"He knows only the highest value is checked in the first stage. He got the scouts' attention in three seconds."

"He's a veteran. He always scores well. There's even a rumor he intentionally failed before."

Evaluators combine the opinions of analysts and recorders to assign overall grades. While updated in real-time, making a good first impression was crucial.

"Judgment pending."

But Baikal remained calm.

"We didn't come here just to see this."

Entering Stage 2, Tier 9 creatures appeared. There were over a thousand of them, and they were twice the size of Tier 10 creatures, being higher in the food chain.

"Get ready!"

From sharp-toothed leopards to monkeys with razor claws, beasts that ordinary people wouldn't dare hunt charged, and the magic power surged.

Flames, ice, lightning, and light created a dazzling spectacle for several seconds.

"Annihilation time: 46.22 seconds."

"That's fast."

Baikal knew the average Stage 2 annihilation time was 1 minute and 32 seconds.

"Total magic power: 76,530 magicles. Highest value: Fermi's 22,000 magicles."

"Interesting guy."

That was all Baikal had to say.

"Entering Stage 3. Things will change now."

From Tier 8, the creatures included monsters from the monster kingdom, not just strong animals. While Stages 1 and 2 involved fighting strong animals, Stage 3 introduced rarely encountered monsters.

Roar!

'Ulka!'

Shirone remembered the monster he first applied divine particles to photon output, but now over 200 of them were charging from all directions.

In the sky, the monstrous bird Taicon screeched, while underground, the giant scorpion Scorpion swung its 2-meter tail and scuttled like a cockroach.

The Blizzard was laid down again, but it only slightly reduced the creatures' activity.

'No one will fail at Stage 3, but…'

From this point, managing mental strength became crucial, and the participants' minds raced.

'Saint Barrier!'

As the encirclement tightened, Eden's barrier expanded into a spherical shield.

"Participant number 25, Candler Eden. Magic power: 30,842 magicles. Standard hardness: 307.5."

"Not bad. Durability?"

"Measured at standard strength: 27 press per millimeter."

"Oh?"

Elizabeth's eyebrows twitched.

"Increasing magic power. 38,000 magicles. 45,000 magicles. 69,000 magicles…"

As the Saint Barrier expanded to a 20-meter diameter, it pushed back the encirclement like grains of sand.

"Standard hardness: 1,209. Standard strength: 87 press per millimeter. Magic efficiency: 187%."

Generating nearly double the power with single magic was an incredible omnipotence.

"Faith-based omnipotence?"

Baikal summarized.

"Grade D. Overprotection. No matter how efficient, a wasteful mindset is useless."

Lara recorded Eden's real-time grade on her page.

"Hurry up! Are you just going to watch?"

As Eden shouted, the participants sprang into action.

'Tch! Holding back too much won't look good in the evaluation.'

Some took the opportunity to show off their skills, with the most spectacular being the Fist Mage, Screamer.

"I'll smash them all!"

Teleporting through the monsters and attacking, Ulkas fell, spewing blood.

"Participant number 10, Logan Screamer!"

Elizabeth's analysis sped up.

"Teleportation cycle: 124 RPM! Obsession error: 0.9%! Average distance: 10 meters 3.4 centimeters!"

"Grade C. Self-absorbed."

"Participant number 4, Carmis Amy! Firepower coefficient: 2.4! Heat: 875 degrees Celsius! Accuracy: 97%!"

"Participant number 14, Victor Sabina! Firing rate: 7.9 shots per second! Cutting strength…"

Scouts from around the world analyzed the participants' every move in various languages, overwhelming the audience.

"…Intense."

Watching from the VIP section, Rian muttered.

Reina, attending her first magic school graduation exam, was also overwhelmed by their intensity.

'So these are the people evaluating us.'

It was a harsh reality that everything about the participants, not just their magic skills, was being valued.

As Olina watched her son's battle, she covered her face with her hands, and Vincent placed a hand on her shoulder, hearing her sobs.

'Dear, my heart feels like it's going to burst.'

He watched his son fiercely deploying magic.

What grade would these capable people give Shirone?

At first, he was overwhelmed by the unimaginable scene, but eventually, all a parent's senses focused solely on their child.

'Shirone, is this the world you dreamed of?'

His son had never shown his true feelings, even when they tried to raise him as a hunter's child.

How desperate he must have been.

Thinking of the struggles Shirone faced alone to reach the top of this fierce competition brought tears to Vincent's eyes.

'God, please let Shirone pass.'

Among the countless prayers rising to the heavens, whose voice would God listen to?

"Annihilation time: 4 minutes 55 seconds. Total magic power: 323,000 magicles."

At this point, magic power in the hundreds was meaningless.

"That's high for Stage 3. Who's overdoing it?"

Elizabeth's gaze caught a boy.

"Highest value: 110,000 magicles. Participant number 27, Arian Shirone."

Baikal's brow furrowed.

"I didn't think he was foolish."

Having been introduced by Plu, Baikal had high expectations, but this was unexpected.

'Now I have to push forward.'

Having passed Stage 3, Shirone fully released his tactical limits.

The 30-second rest passed quickly, and Tier 7 creatures appeared.

There were 870 of them, the first time the number dropped to the hundreds, but their abilities were on a different level from Tier 8.

Monsters with magical properties, like the sound-magic-casting Harpy and the fire-breathing Flare Raptor, were included, and they had the intelligence to solve situations.

Screech!

As the Flare Raptor led the charge, the Survival Evaluation quickly turned chaotic.

"The formation is collapsing."

The essence of the Survival Evaluation was that participants were both allies and competitors.

The first test ended when half the participants, 15, remained, making strategic maneuvering essential.

'Photon Burst!'

As the light barrier pushed back the massive weight, the force pushed out the energy, and the Scatter Movement was cast.

Photon Cannons spread in all directions, and the scouts' eyes focused on Shirone.

"The force is fast."

Elizabeth noted something different.

"Magic power exceeds 220,000 magicles."

"What? 220,000?"

Lara stopped writing and asked.

"It's still rising. 260,000 magicles! 320,000 magicles! 440,000 magicles!"

As if to prove Elizabeth's analysis, the Photon Cannons raining from the sky scorched the ground.

"440,000 magicles."

It was already beyond professional standards.

'Still not enough!'

Shirone single-handedly took down 32% of the monsters, and the number kept rising.

"Who is that student? He's doing everything by himself!"

Even the parents, who had given up trying to spot their own children, were captivated by Shirone's prowess.

"Hmph! There's always one like that, sprinting at the start but unable to keep up."

While most parents were annoyed, Iruki's father, Albino, was smiling.

"Impressive. I expected this from what Iruki said, but I didn't think it would be to this extent."

"Indeed. It's still early, but he's clearly standing out among the participants."

The scouts had mixed opinions.

"Something's off. What's going on? His profile says he's cautious…"

Some focused on the visible numbers, while others were puzzled by his excessive response, ignoring the difficulty.

Baikal was among the latter.

"Why did he make that choice? Wastefulness doesn't score well."

"It seems personal."

Baikal had a vague idea of Shirone's situation.

"That's unfortunate."

Showing off power isn't everything.

"If he wanted to impress the scouts, he should have chosen a different method."

"Evaluator… it's still rising."

Elizabeth's voice trembled.

"Magic power: 520,000 magicles. 610,000 magicles. 690,000… Immortal Function signal detected! 870,000! 940,000!"

'No one…'

Shirone gritted his teeth.

'No one can decide my future!'

As the Photon Cannons, firing at 300 rounds per second, rained down, the monsters fell like bugs hit by a hammer.

The last Flare Raptor collapsed with a squawk, and Amy stared at Shirone in disbelief.

'What is he thinking?'

Baikal's expression grew serious.

"Magic power."

Elizabeth snapped back to attention.

"Oh, yes. 1,630,000 magicles."

"…"

Baikal covered his face with his hand, frowning.

"That's it, Shirone."

At the same time, a woman in a hood, sitting high in the audience, smiled slightly.
