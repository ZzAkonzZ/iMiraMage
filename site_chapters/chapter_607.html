Chapter 607:

As the blade of Armand wrapped around Shirone's body formed its structure, a sense of speed disparity occurred in that fleeting moment.

When activating Diamond Armor, unlike when the robe unconsciously covered his body, Shirone could clearly feel the flow of fibers emanating from the blade, while Nade was approaching at an incredibly fast speed.

'I can't match the timing.'

Before he could even transform into the state of Diamond Armor, Nade's claw scratched from the top of Shirone's head to his face.

'I have to reverse it!'

As he manifested the Temporal Reversal, the reality of his face burning scattered into a nightmare that hadn't happened, and the claws passed right in front of Shirone's eyes as he leaned back.

It was like a lightning strike.

"Damn…!"

He avoided the attack by reverting one second of the past into reality, but the event also reversed Armand to its state before activating Diamond Armor.

'Let's run!'

He tried to teleport with all his might, but Spark Magic transcended space like the movement of electrons, pinning Nade at the destination.

"Graaaah!"

Nade, as if unable to withstand the saturation of electricity, unleashed lightning in all directions again.

The power of the Spirit Zone, perfectly synchronized with electricity, surpassed the concept of artificiality, and as the atmospheric charge discharged, a massive lightning bolt struck the ground.

Boom!

There was no reason.

It was just a massive lightning bolt.

"Damn it! What the hell did you do to end up like this?"

Iruki shouted as he rolled down the hillside, holding Oscar.

If his mind had already assimilated with electricity, even escaping was impossible.

"I… didn't know."

Oscar, who had struck Nade with lightning, made a feeble excuse, but there was no time to be angry at him.

"Stay here! We have to save Shirone!"

Iruki, who climbed the hillside, saw Shirone about to be burned by Nade's hand.

'That can't be canceled.'

Only a god could do it.

"Nade! Snap out of it!"

Shirone shouted without giving up, but his voice no longer reached.

Thunder and lightning struck, and Nade, clad in plasma, suddenly appeared in front of him.

'Diamond Armor!'

As he perceived the future one second ahead with Temporal Reversal, the golden thread of the blade grotesquely unfolded, encompassing numerous changes of Armand.

"Huuup!"

Armand was equipped just in time, and the anti-thesis installed in the artificial brain activated.

'It's not effective.'

Taking away the will from an entity without a motive for action changed nothing, and Nade's claw struck his side.

"Ugh!"

The silver metal of the ring's gauntlet stood out where the claw hit, but the shock of the electric current traveling through the conductor was significant.

'Scatter Movement!'

Seven Photon Cannons, excluding Shirone, scattered in all directions, but none could touch even a hair of Nade.

'Then…!'

Shirone gritted his teeth and swung his tentacles.

Two streams of tentacles created black afterimages as they swung, then quickly covered Nade at a speed invisible to the eye.

Thud! The tentacles were caught by Nade's crossed arms.

"What…!"

As he clenched his fists, the burnt tentacles snapped with a crackling sound.

'That's the problem.'

Iruki bit his lip.

The power of magical assimilation was close to a disaster, but more serious was the assimilation itself.

'It's not something you catch by seeing.'

It was a reaction after sensory reception.

Like dodging a stone flying from behind the moment it touched his hair.

'That's the fear of 100% affinity.'

If the Spirit Zone was electricity, the reaction speed of the senses had already surpassed the realm of living beings.

This was the basis for Iruki's judgment that Shirone couldn't catch Nade in his current state.

"Graaaah!"

Even so, there was a blind spot.

The more Nade's control was eroded by magical assimilation, the further he moved from human thought.

As expected, he didn't move even in a crisis, absorbing the plasma charge according to the laws of nature.

"Nade! Please…!"

Thousands of wire-like electric shocks sprang from Nade's body with a flash.

In the brief moment when Shirone and Iruki turned their heads from the blinding light, Nade had already reached Oscar.

"Help! Somebody help!"

Shirone and Iruki threw themselves simultaneously, but Nade's hand, charged with electricity, was already aiming at Oscar.

"Nade! No!"

"Graaaah!"

Just as Nade was about to burn Oscar's skull, Iruki shouted.

"It's the life that Senior Liz gave you!"

The electricity surrounding Nade flickered, and his claw stopped right in front of Oscar's eyes.

"Huuuh!"

Oscar, who had already lost his mind, burst into tears like a child, trembling all over, while Iruki shouted again.

"If you cross the line, you can never come back!"

The charge surrounding Nade began to stabilize.

"Senior Liz was… my everything."

"Please save me! Please save me!"

It was only rationality holding him together; even for a mage, death was terrifying.

And for Oscar, who had already lost his mind, the only thing left was the instinct to protect his pitiful life.

"That guy, he really controlled it."

The ferocity of magical assimilation awakened by the lightning strike was on a different level from what Iruki had seen, but Nade suppressed it again this time.

"Shirone."

An alien memory, as if it belonged to someone else, surfaced in Nade's mind as he looked back at Shirone.

'I tried to kill him.'

Nade, who was wearing Armand's robe and on high alert, spoke tearfully.

"I'm sorry. I'm sorry."

As soon as he finished speaking, Nade collapsed from his knees.

"Nade! Snap out of it! Nade!"

Shirone ran over and shook him, but only a single tear fell from his closed eyes.

"Ugh."

The first thing Nade saw when he woke up was the dormitory ceiling flickering between his eyelids.

"……"

He couldn't even think of getting up as he tried to reconnect the scattered thoughts in his head.

"Oh? Iruki! Nade is awake!"

Shirone, who entered the room, called out to Iruki in the hallway.

"Oh? Really."

Iruki's smiling face came into Nade's still unmoving view.

"Did you sleep well?"

"……How long has it been?"

Shirone replied.

"A day and a half. It's almost evening now."

"I see…"

Nade, deep in thought again, asked after a while.

"What about Oscar?"

"He's alive."

Nade thought this would be his most pressing concern.

"No, I remember. What happened?"

Shirone exchanged glances with Iruki.

"I checked the plaza this morning, and it was posted on the bulletin board. Oscar passed the special recruitment."

"……"

Whether he clung to it until the end or the matter was naturally resolved due to his mental state, it was unclear, but probably the former.

"Oscar is a mage too, so he must have made the best decision."

"Yeah. Adults… live such hard lives."

Iruki said.

"There's nothing more important than making a living."

"After you fainted, I talked to Oscar briefly. He said he didn't intend to do it from the beginning. He received an offer from the head's daughter at a social gathering in the capital and came alone, but Liz insisted on coming and even took a vacation…"

"He wanted to meet me."

Nade understood her feelings.

"What will you do now? Will you come to the press room with us? If you're still angry…"

Nade turned his head to the side.

"It doesn't matter. Whether I get a job at the association or not. I'm just worried about Senior Liz. She must have left by now, right?"

"Probably. She must know about Oscar's situation by now."

Nade closed his eyes.

"Let me rest a little more. I'm tired."

Shirone pulled the blanket up to his neck.

"Okay, sleep a little more. We'll bring your dinner too."

There was no response, and Shirone and Iruki carefully left the room.

An hour later.

Shirone opened the door with a tray of dinner.

"Nade, eat something. Huh?"

Iruki, who followed in, also looked around blankly.

The blanket was neatly folded, and the curtains fluttered in the breeze from the open window.

'He must have already left…'

Even so, Nade headed to the Dolphin Inn.

He had already emptied his mind, but his heart sank when he peeked inside through the inn's window.

"Senior Liz…"

He saw Liz sitting at the bar with her head down, empty bottles beside her.

Thinking that she still had something left here, Nade's eyes filled with tears.

"Isn't that woman drinking too much?"

"Leave her alone. She must have been dumped."

Hearing the murmurs from behind, Liz emptied another glass of strong liquor.

Her body naturally slumped, and her slender wrist precariously supported her heavy head.

"Why…"

Why did she come here following Oscar?

At the peak of happiness, just before her engagement to the person she loved, why did she think of him?

"Graaaah!"

The warehouse was blown away without a trace, and neither Wiz nor the mage was visible.

Nade, struck by Ultrix's lightning, couldn't suppress the murderous intent boiling from his mind.

"Damn it! This is the first time!"

Sometimes magical assimilation happened instinctively, but the lightning from outside couldn't be controlled.

"Aaaaah!"

As the electricity discharged, the area around Liz turned black.

"Run away!"

Nade squeezed out the words, desperately suppressing his strength.

"Nade! What's happening?"

"Go quickly! If you stay here, you'll die too!"

"But what about you?"

She couldn't run away, leaving the person who saved her life.

"It doesn't matter. Anyway, I…"

Nade's thoughts reached somewhere, and he twisted his lips into a grimace.

"I'm someone who doesn't deserve to live."

Having been abandoned by his family and turned into a monster, life held no meaning for him.

"No. I'm going with you."

Having made up her mind, Liz approached.

"Are you crazy? Don't come near me."

The moment she touched him, she would be electrocuted, and without magical assimilation, she couldn't withstand it.

"You can hold on. You're holding on even now."

Liz slowly reached out and touched Nade's shoulder.

"Ugh!"

Desperately suppressing his strength to avoid killing her, hideous veins bulged on his face.

"Stop it… Please. I can't hold on any longer…"

"Then we'll die together."

Confirming that it was effective, Liz tightly closed her eyes and hugged Nade.

"Aaaah!"

At the same time, Nade, who had blocked the discharge of electricity, writhed in pain.

"Let go! Just go!"

"You can do it! Just a little more!"

"I have no reason to live anyway! It's better for someone like me to die!"

"Why do you have no reason to live? You saved me!"

"Idiot, that's…"

Just as his limbs were about to explode from the mental strain, Liz kissed him.

"Mmm!"

As the soft lips of a woman transmitted through his entire nervous system, his rampaging mind began to calm down.

Nade hugged Liz's waist, and the two trembled with infinite freedom, entrusting everything to each other's actions.

Blue electricity.

That's how Liz defined it.

No matter how much time passed, it would forever remain as an afterimage of her first kiss.

"Sob! Sob!"

Liz's shoulders shook sadly as she buried her face in her arms, and Nade, watching from outside the window, covered his face with his hands and cried.

"Aaaah. Aaaah."

Sweet lies were unnecessary.

"Sob. Sob."

There was a time when just liking someone was enough.

"Aaaah. Aaaah."

So many things that were lost because of their purity.

Leaving all those things behind, Nade's most brilliant school days were coming to an end.
