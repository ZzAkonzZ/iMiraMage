Chapter 504: Wholehearted Resolve (3)

McClain Goffin.

The fact that the being who came closest to annihilating the Akashic Records throughout the history of Heaven was a human left a deep scar on the angels.

There was no concept of forgetfulness in the angels' Holy Light Body, but it had been so long ago that the weight of memory had fossilized that moment. Now, that forgotten existence surfaced vividly in Faiel’s Holy Light Body.

‘It was the same back then.’

The cold memory thawed, bringing confusion once more.

Are humans strong or weak?

Why is it that despite being slaves to base animal desires, they sometimes transcend them and reach the pinnacle of spiritual existence?

Humans have been defined by countless beings thousands of times, yet no word has ever been crafted to perfectly describe them.

Is it because they’re such complex creatures?

‘No.’

Faiel thought.

‘It’s because they’re not fixed.’

Humans were constantly moving toward something, even in this very moment.

‘That’s why they must be annihilated.’

Faiel’s Holy Light Body expanded explosively.

The rapidly expanding halo that could swallow Heaven itself tightened faster than light, collapsing into a single dot and vanishing.

“Stop!”

Ikael, speeding through the sky, realized what was happening and shouted in desperation.

Apatheia—The Eye of Annihilation.

Faiel’s signature ability, The Eye of Annihilation, was an ultimate technique that embodied the very concept of annihilation. Once activated, even other archangels feared its overwhelming power.

“I enact the will of the Archangel.”

The glowing eyes beneath Faiel’s hood transformed into perfect halos—two circles with a deep, clear dot at their centers.

Though his face appeared comical at first glance, a closer look revealed something so inhuman and emotionless that it inspired both fear and awe.

‘Something is off.’

Shirone couldn’t bring himself to counterattack.

Through Direct Insight, he saw that Faiel was merely an empty shell. The true source of annihilation had dispersed evenly throughout the world.

That explained why Faiel’s Holy Light Body had vanished.

—I close the world.

As Faiel’s eyes slowly closed, the colors of the world surrounding Shirone began to fade.

“Ghh!”

Shirone’s body trembled violently as he felt the sensation of his incarnation being erased.

I.

If I disappear, so does the universe.

That was the essence of The Eye of Annihilation, a technique that ended the world by closing the self.

“Aaaaaah!”

Though the Eye of Annihilation had only closed halfway, Shirone writhed in despair.

Had he not armed himself with Armand’s incarnation through the Principle of One Mind, he would have already dissolved into nothingness.

‘I must endure! If I don’t endure…’

He would eventually be annihilated.

But all Shirone could do was cling desperately to his fading incarnation.

The Eye of Annihilation was ending his world with precise timing and speed.

A blood vessel burst in Shirone’s eye, and blood trickled down his nose.

He poured every ounce of his strength into maintaining his incarnation, but whether Faiel closed his eyes or not was entirely up to him.

This was the overwhelming power of an archangel’s fundamental concept.

‘This is infuriating…!’

As his presence faded, his Spirit Zone could no longer exert its influence.

Finally, as he entered the sub-material phase, Shirone’s body became translucent.

“Impressive for a Nephilim.”

Faiel’s voice rang directly in his ears.

“But your world is about to be closed.”

Faiel’s eyes drooped as if drowsy.

Shirone was on the verge of becoming completely transparent, as if he had never existed.

“Huff!”

At that moment, Shirone’s incarnation flared with intense light.

The Eye of Annihilation flickered open, and Shirone’s form reappeared clearly in the world.

Only Ikael, the archangel, could amplify an extinguishing incarnation so rapidly.

“Shirone!”

“You won’t escape!”

Satiel, who had been chasing Ikael, grabbed her by the neck and slammed her into the ground.

“Ghh!”

She pressed her down with her entire body, pinning Ikael between her legs while exerting crushing force on her neck.

As Satiel’s Holy Light Body expanded into a halo and unleashed her disintegration ability, Ikael’s wings of light began to lose their strength.

Having used her power to recover her memory and revive Shirone’s incarnation, Ikael had no strength left to resist.

“Ikael! Ghh!”

Shirone tried to run toward them, but the Eye of Annihilation closed again, restraining him.

“Faiel! Kill him! I’ll finish off Ikael!”

While the Eye of Annihilation was extremely efficient at eliminating a single target, its drawback was that Faiel’s mind dispersed into the world, leaving him defenseless.

Satiel’s restraint of Ikael eliminated that weakness.

“Disappear! You disgrace of the angels!”

Satiel’s eyes burned with resentment built over countless ages—resentment beyond what any human could comprehend.

‘Because of you… If it weren’t for you…!’

“Ghhhh!”

Watching Ikael being disassembled, Shirone gathered all his strength to cast a spell.

“Ikaeeeel!”

Chains of light wrapped around Satiel’s body.

Having witnessed Faiel’s torment firsthand, she instinctively flinched.

However, the radiant chains passed harmlessly through her, offering no restraint.

“Ghh!”

Shirone clenched his teeth in frustration.

With the Eye of Annihilation more than halfway closed, his magic was failing to manifest properly.

Satiel, momentarily relieved, resumed pressing down on Ikael with a vicious glare.

A face she couldn’t stand to look at.

A face so beautiful that she hated it with every fiber of her being!

“I’ll ruin you before I annihilate you!”

Judicial Halo—Nostalgia.

Boom! Boom! Boom!

Fragments of light burst from Ikael’s face.

Destroy everything.

Her authority, her beauty, her detestable memories—everything would be disassembled!

“Aaaaaaah!”

Thud!

A dull impact struck Satiel’s temple.

‘Huh?’

Realizing too late that she had been struck, Satiel skidded across the ground, struggling to regain her balance.

As she turned, confusion flickered in her eyes.

There weren’t many beings in Heaven capable of producing enough force to push back an archangel’s body.

“Kariel? Why are you…?”

Kariel stood there, his expression tense.

According to Satiel, his hatred wasn’t much different from hers.

‘Then why?’

Even Ikael, who had just been saved, stared at Kariel in stunned silence.

Kariel understood their confusion.

To be honest, he didn’t know why he had attacked Satiel either.

‘What have I done?’

He had come to annihilate Ikael himself, determined to end the foolish angel who had thrown her heart away for a mere human.

Perhaps his weakened mental state had played a role.

The moment he saw Satiel’s Nostalgia, his Holy Light Body had shaken violently, and from that point on, everything went blank.

Kariel slowly turned his gaze and locked eyes with Ikael.

Although it hadn’t been his full intent, the result was that he had saved her.

Ikael—the one he resented.

Yet, what Kariel was feeling now… was unmistakable anticipation.

“Is it that… the heart is contradictory?”

Uorin nodded.

“Yes. In truth, altruism is a serious error when viewed through the lens of survival of the fittest, which is the grand principle of the universe. Then what is altruism? It’s nothing more than another form of selfishness—one that loves the altruistic self. Just like how someone can mistakenly believe they have earned the right to hate because they love someone too much.”

Gando reflected.

Do I… resent Uorin?

“Ahhhhh!”

Shirone’s scream drew the attention of every angel.

It wasn’t just the cry of a mere human that caught their attention.

Shirone’s mind, which had been recklessly expanding, was now collapsing, as if swept away by a massive wave, losing its form entirely.

“Shirone!”

Ikael rushed past Kariel and ran toward Shirone.

At the end of an extreme form of amplification, an explosion awaited.

If she couldn’t stop it here, Shirone’s mind would be torn apart, never to return to its original state.

“Ghhhh!”

Shirone clutched his head, writhing in pain.

His thoughts were being shredded, as if his brain were being torn apart.

It was nothing like the euphoria he had experienced when unlocking the Immortal Function—this was pure violence.

‘No! Not yet!’

He struggled desperately to maintain focus, but his mind had already swelled beyond the point of control.

Ikael, squeezing out the last of her strength, activated Ataraxia.

Once again, she amplified Shirone’s incarnation, delaying the explosion by sheer force.

It was like pouring water into a broken jar, but she didn’t hesitate.

Even if it was only for five minutes, even if she could only prolong Shirone’s life a little, she was willing to give up everything.

‘Why…?’

Kariel stood dazed, watching Ikael pass by without sparing him a glance.

Did she not see him?

“Faiel! Kill Shirone!”

Satiel charged forward, her voice venomous.

The more Ikael tried to protect Shirone, the more Satiel’s rage grew.

‘It should have been mine! All of it should have been mine!’

Kariel snapped out of his stupor when he saw Satiel aiming for Ikael’s back.

Judicial Halo—Simularch.

Countless mechanical components burst out of a compressed leather pouch, forcing themselves into the space around them and assembling in midair.

Clatter, clatter, clatter!

Within moments, a weapon resembling a bronze shield with eight bladed edges appeared, spinning rapidly.

Simularch—a weapon that never ceased spinning. The more it spun, the stronger it grew. The stronger it grew, the harder it became. The harder it became, the faster it spun.

Though crude in design for something created by the Archangel of Creation, it was proof of how urgent the situation was.

Nostalgia—Physics Collapse.

Satiel, having understood the mechanism that surpassed human intelligence, widened her eyes and activated her ability.

The weapon trembled before rapidly disassembling—first into mechanisms, then into components, then into individual parts.

It happened so quickly that it looked like an explosion, but without fire or sound.

The weapon passed Satiel as it broke down into alloys, then minerals, then molecules, and finally atoms, disappearing entirely.

“Ha! Did you think you could defeat me?”

Though she had never fought Kariel before, she had no doubt that she wouldn’t lose to someone considered the weakest in combat among the archangels.

“…”

As Ikael shielded Shirone, Faiel finally gave up maintaining The Eye of Annihilation.

“What a disgraceful sight, Archangel.”

He didn’t like the current state of affairs, with the archangels entangled in such chaos.

Once again, things were falling apart.

Shirone. And Goffin.

Whenever the variable called ‘human’ entered the equation, everything turned into a mess.

“I’ll put an end to this.”

Thud!

Faiel kicked off the ground and shot into the sky.

Boom!

He descended, crushing Kariel beneath him as he landed beside Satiel.

“Ka… Kariel?”

Seeing Kariel’s shattered form buried in the ground, Satiel, who had been fighting fiercely moments ago, relaxed her expression and stepped back.

In truth, she held no personal grudge against him.

In fact, at times, she had even felt a sense of camaraderie with him, as they both opposed Ikael.

“A fallen angel who has lost their authority has no reason to exist.”

Leaving those words behind, Faiel departed, and only then did Satiel truly take in Kariel’s state, with his chest crushed.

“Kariel… Are you okay?”

She approached him cautiously, checking his condition with a mix of curiosity and concern.

Perhaps it had been too long since she last saw something like this.

Kariel, lying on the ground, gazing at the sky, wore a peaceful smile—one she had never seen before.

As if everything had returned to that happy moment long ago...

Chapter 505: Wholehearted Resolve (4)

“Kariel…”

Satiel couldn’t believe her eyes.

At least by her standards, Kariel had always been the most refined and intelligent of the archangels, possessing an unmatched elegance.

But now, the Kariel before her bore no resemblance to the dignified figure of the past.

Faiel had brutally stomped on him, bending his waist completely backward so that his upper body was crushed beneath him.

His face was wedged between his legs, and his chest had been utterly ravaged by the aura of annihilation.

Half-buried in the ground with only his upper body visible, gazing up at the sky, Kariel looked like the most pitiful, miserable figure in the world.

Yet, his eyes held a depth and tranquility she had never seen before.

‘Ah, I see now.’

Even though his back was broken, Faiel’s aura of annihilation was relentlessly eroding Kariel’s already weakened existence.

In any case, it was clear he wouldn’t last much longer in his current state.

Yet, the peace he had found at the end of his existence felt sweet, as though it were a reward for the long years he had spent resenting Ikael.

‘If only… I had given everything from the start.’

Had he known he would hate her this much, had he known he would waste countless years on futile hatred…

He might as well have given her everything she wanted from the beginning.

‘Well, it doesn’t matter anymore.’

He had thought he would never be able to forgive her. He had believed that no logic could ever change his mind.

But the moment he saved Ikael, his hatred vanished like a lie.

All that remained in the void where hatred once lingered was a self-satisfaction at having saved her and a deep sense of regret.

“Kariel, are you okay?”

Satiel’s voice broke through, and Kariel turned his head with great effort.

For the first time since the war began, he considered his role as an archangel.

“We must not annihilate Ikael.”

Satiel’s face twisted in frustration.

“What are you saying…?”

“I won’t last much longer. You know that.”

Satiel ground her teeth.

Metatron and Metiel had already been annihilated, and Kariel wouldn’t hold out much longer either.

If Ikael were to be annihilated as well, the disappearance of four archangels would tip the scales of law in favor of Satan.

If that happened, no means within the angels’ power would be enough to stop Satan.

“I understand how you feel. You and I aren’t so different. But unlike you, I have something—or rather, someone—I can throw my heart to.”

That fact alone filled Satiel with even more resentment.

“A heart? Do you really think a noble spiritual being like an archangel would be swayed by something as trivial as emotion?”

Kariel let out a dry chuckle, gazing up at the sky.

“You’re already being swayed.”

Sensing the end of his life, Kariel recalled a distant memory that had long been buried in the depths of his mind.

‘Isn’t that right, Ikael?’

“There was amplification at the beginning.”

Ikael had gathered Kariel and Uriel and explained.

“Within that amplification, the concepts of existence and non-existence were born. In other words, being and non-being came into existence.”

Meeting Kariel’s curious gaze, Ikael reached out with a gentle hand and stroked his head.

Kariel purred like a cat, enjoying the attention.

“Being and non-being. You two are the ones who govern those concepts—creation and destruction.”

Kariel asked, “Then what existed before amplification? Was there truly nothing?”

Ikael adored Kariel’s insatiable curiosity.

“Nothingness… is…”

“It is nothingness,” said Uorin.

Gando listened intently, watching every expression Uorin made as she spoke about the beginning.

“That means no time, no space. It’s not the same as imagining a blank page. Even if you erase a blank page, that’s still not true nothingness.”

Humans can understand the concept of nothingness, but they cannot experience it—because it existed before experience itself.

“Then what existed before this universe was created? Was it simply nothingness?”

“Perhaps,” Gando replied.

“But consider this, Gando. If the state of ‘being’ did not exist, then neither could the state of ‘non-being.’”

Therefore, even if there was nothing at the beginning, humans should not define it as ‘non-being.’

Defining it as nothing creates a paradoxical contradiction that violates the very concept of nothingness.

“Then how do we define the state of the beginning?”

Uorin raised a finger.

“A shutdown state.”

“Shutdown?” Kariel echoed.

He already understood, but he wanted to hear it again.

“Yes. Whether something exists or doesn’t, it’s in a shutdown state—no matter, no concepts.”

Ikael had summarized, “Something happened.”

Even for archangels born from the original conceptual source, they could only describe the shutdown state in such terms.

“That event transformed the shutdown state into an active state. At that point of amplification…”

Uorin crossed her raised fingers into an X.

“Two states—being and non-being—came into existence by referencing each other. Information defined by 0s and 1s combined in infinite patterns, and finally, this universe began to form.”

Uriel asked, “Then what is amplification?”

Since Uriel rarely showed interest, Ikael turned to him with an intrigued expression.

“What caused this world to be amplified?”

Ikael smiled awkwardly.

“That… even I cannot say.”

Uorin added, “No one knows. All we know is that something acted, and the world awakened. What was that something? A faint electrical spark? A cosmic explosion powerful enough to push the universe outward? It doesn’t matter.”

Uorin snapped her fingers.

“At this moment, that snap could mark the beginning and end of a universe. Or it could be the absolute decree of a transcendent being.”

“I see.”

The possibilities were endless, and the truth unknowable.

What mattered was that something amplified and set everything in motion.

“But what’s fascinating—no, what’s truly strange—is…”

Uorin leaned back in her chair, crossing her arms.

“Humans have a word that perfectly mirrors that process. Can you guess what it is?”

Gando pondered for a long while before finally admitting defeat.

“I don’t know. What is it?”

“It is…”

Uorin paused for dramatic effect, then smiled meaningfully as she answered.

“The heart.”

“Ghhhhh!”

Amidst the collapsing chaos of his mind, Shirone found a faint glimmer of stability.

At last, his vision cleared, revealing Ikael’s face—so pale it was almost translucent.

‘Why…?’

Shirone knew there was no hope of survival.

He had accepted it, and thus had no regrets or lingering attachments.

Yet, here was Ikael, desperately trying to save him.

Why?

What meaning could extending his life by a few more minutes possibly have for her?

“You fool.”

Faiel activated Apateia and crushed Ataraxia.

“Ahhhhh!”

Ikael’s scream rang out, and Shirone’s barely stabilized mind began to collapse once more.

“No! I won’t let it end like this!”

As everything rushed toward its conclusion, one thing surfaced in Shirone’s mind—Zulu’s teaching.

The mind governs the spirit.

But since the mind has no physical form, it cannot be trained directly.

‘The secret of a tranquil mind.’

Encapsulating oneself in the cycle of breath, halting the activity of the mind.

‘I will control this explosion!’

Shirone inhaled deeply and exhaled slowly.

With each exhalation, he let go of something important.

Ikael, Heaven, life, death, and even the hatred that had filled his mind were all released.

Only the rhythm of inhaling and exhaling remained.

Returning to the fundamental essence of being alive.

“Inhale. Exhale.”

Plop.

Shirone’s Spirit Zone, which had lost its tension and hung loosely, suddenly regained its elasticity and sprang upward.

“The heart.”

Gando echoed Uorin’s words softly.

“Yes. No matter what the primal event was, what was necessary for that event to occur was the heart. It is from the action of the heart that this universe began.”

Uorin rolled up her sleeves and extended her right fist.

“Thus, the heart changes the laws of existence.”

Boom!

With a speed too fast for the eye to catch, her elbow straightened, and her fist pierced the air, creating a powerful shockwave.

“When you desire to throw a punch at transcendent speed with all your heart, that wholehearted desire changes the law and moves your body.”

“So, that’s divine transcendence.”

“Divine transcendence, Yaksha.”

Uorin corrected the terminology precisely.

“It momentarily amplifies rage and alters the laws affecting the body. The workings of the heart are truly mysterious.”

“An impressive skill.”

“Ha ha, then let me show you one more thing.”

Uorin raised her fist again and pointed it toward Gando.

“I’ll pierce your face.”

Gando gulped as he felt a chill run down his spine.

He knew she was serious—there was no room for doubt in her intent.

“Well? Do you think this is possible?”

Gando calmed his racing heart and shook his head.

“It’s impossible.”

“Why?”

“Because it surpasses the heart’s authority.”

Uorin nodded and lowered her arm.

“Correct. Will is mine alone. Even if I unleash my strongest strike, piercing your face is a completely separate matter.”

“Divine transcendence governs only the body.”

“But…”

Uorin suddenly raised her fist again, catching Gando off guard, and before he could say anything, she extended her elbow with terrifying speed.

“What about this?”

Boom!

Her fist slammed into the air, creating an explosive sound.

Though there was no way her fist could reach him at that distance, Gando vividly felt as though her blow had smashed his face.

“…!”

Gando barely managed to stifle a scream, his arms trembling uncontrollably.

It took him a long moment to realize that his face was, in fact, unharmed.

“…How is this possible?”

Uorin looked pleased with Gando’s composure.

An ordinary human would have lost their mind under such pressure.

“What I attacked wasn’t your physical body, but your manifestation. What you experienced was my will to shatter your head.”

Only then did Gando understand.

The young girl sitting before him was a true monster.

“It’s mental transcendence.”

Uorin corrected him once again.

“Mental transcendence, Prajna. It is the control of the manifestation in a state of perfect emptiness. It’s the opposite of divine transcendence, which focuses the heart. No human living a mere hundred years could reach this level. Even those who have grasped the concept of the manifestation cannot easily imitate it.”

Gando acknowledged it without hesitation.

As a magician, mental transcendence was a distant dream for him—something no amount of effort could achieve.

It wasn’t a realm that could be reached through sheer effort in the first place.

“I didn’t mean to intimidate you. There are both Yaksha and Prajna in the world, but they are simply that—different paths. And yet, you withstood my strike, didn’t you?”

“Thank you for your high praise.”

Gando’s gratitude was genuine.

Normally, Uorin treated him like something less than an insect, but today, she was making an effort to acknowledge him. It was enough to move him.

Yet, it also left a bitter taste.

In the end, he was merely a recipient of sympathy.

If it weren’t for the memories of his biological mother, Terraze Mystra, Uorin would have had no reason to show him such kindness.

Uorin observed Gando’s somber expression for a moment before letting out a quiet chuckle.

Perhaps she had been too kind, but for today, it didn’t seem to matter.

After all, her son would soon bear a great responsibility.

“Breathe in, then exhale.”

“Pardon?”

Gando looked up, puzzled, but Uorin simply repeated the same words.

“Breathe in, then exhale.”
