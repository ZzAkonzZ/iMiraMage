Chapter 900: Omega (1)

"Is it beginning?"

Electricity flowed through Blitz's eyes as he surveyed the Alphones Mountains from the sky.

Through a bidirectional network, the thoughts of the Immortal Dragon Karatorusa were being transmitted.

'Dragon language.'

The language of dragons is in binary.

'The smallest unit of information that forms a shape.' Therefore, it is precise.

'In human language, the term "red" might have an almost infinite spectrum...

Binary can accurately express all types of red that exist in the universe.

'There is no distortion in our conversation.' The end point of infinite regression.

This is why dragons can build a massive database to share each other's thoughts.

'But it's just accessing information.'

The Ultima System recorded in the dragons' database was not just at this level.

'Unary.'

The Ultima System can accept all signals in this world as they are.

'In a perfectly disassembled world, what you face might be emptiness...

No information actually exists.

'You have the only thing that can dwell in that desolate emptiness.'

The mind [心].

Only the mind flows in a place where there is nothing.

'That is humanity.'

Shirone would soon realize this too.

'Only humans can transcend the administrator's authority and strike the outside world.'

This is possible when all minds, all quantum signals, are unified into one.

'Gaia did that.'

The collision of the Ultima System and the Akashic Records.

"It is something you must see as well." Blitz's gaze penetrated deep underground through the illusion of the Alphones Mountains.

Surrounded by Karatorusa's immense light, Shirone stopped thinking and opened his mouth.

"Are you talking about all the information in the world, omniscience?"

Shirone's Ultima System accepted Karatorusa's binary without difficulty.

"It's not academic knowledge defined by humans. Knowledge is ultimately a realm of interpretation. What I am trying to convey is something that exists as it is. It is knowing."

The light pressed on Shirone as if it had mass.

"I am the guardian of time, Karatorusa. Without time, there are no phenomena. Nothing can be hot, melt, or freeze."

That is why it was called the Immortal Dragon.

"Only I can lay out the signals that make up this world in units of time. And now I will pass that record to you. Everything in the world, the beginning and end of time."

A powerful voice seeped in.

"Omniscience, Omega."

Knowledge is contained in the flow of time.

'The history of everything.'

Shirone recalled the day he completed the spine of knowledge in the Ozente family's grand library.

'The path of the mage.'

Everything started from that point.

And now, the seeker of truth was trying to encompass everything in this world.

"I will accept it."

An endless amount of data began to penetrate Shirone's light.

"It will be a long journey."

Literally, the beginning of the universe.

As Shirone's self gradually faded, he completely disappeared.

"Ah."

Everything was rushing in.

In the beginning, there was light.

'Gasp!'

With an indescribable shock, Shirone's eyes opened wide, and a flash exploded in his vision.

'I am...

Karatorusa's record was structured with an infinite array of binary information.

'Anke Ra?'

With ten senses transcending time and space, Shirone absorbed the entirety of this world.

'What is this?'

When Anke Ra first realized its existence, it questioned causality.

'Was it a dream?'

It couldn't have suddenly come into being.

'Or is this a dream?'

In a world without time, Anke Ra finally concluded.

"I don't know."

There might have been an actual world, or it could have been a mere illusion without a subject.

"Only what I perceive is everything."

The mind program god.

Anke Ra's mind, which first perceived the world, spread through quantum signals.

Eight concepts separated from the unified field, and the universe formed with the rush of time.

Numerous planets, including the sun, began to operate with their own rules under the initial force.

'The beginning of the law.'

Although unpredictable by human thought, causality was already determined from this moment.

According to that causality, something called life was born in the Goldilocks zone.

'Cell.'

The simplest organic matter repeated evolution and extinction, experimenting with numerous species.

The environment was still harsh.

"Great Star."

From the boiling sea, Argones, the father of life, slowly emerged onto the land.

"The strongest life form has been born."

When Argones, who had not moved an inch, spoke again, a vast amount of time had passed.

"A mutation."

The Great Star turned her head, and Shirone saw the strongest life form he knew flying in the sky.

'Karatorusa.'

With numerous dragons, including the Immortal Dragon, dominating the land, sea, and air, the Great Star spoke.

"They are pitiful children."

Argones agreed.

"As time passes, the environment will stabilize. If that species is preserved, the balance of the biosphere will be destroyed."

They were a kind of prototype.

"If possible, now..."

As a murderous intent emanated from Argones' body, the Great Star turned back with a sad expression.

"I think all life is precious."

"Is that a personal opinion? That's unusual."

It was strange that beings born from Anke Ra had such different thoughts.

"They came from me. It's all my fault. If I had created a better environment..."

"The law has no meaning."

Argones interrupted.

"We are at the beginning of the world. The ripple effect of being biased will grow uncontrollably over time. I exist for the biosphere, not for specific creatures."

Shirone realized that this was the beginning of the conflict between Argones and Gaia.

'But...

Even in Shirone's time, dragons existed as apostles of time.

'What happened?' Omega flowed again.

As all events integrating time and space flowed in, omniscience was becoming perfect.

"Roar!"

The dragon's roar was transmitted in binary language and materialized in Shirone's senses.

As dozens of shattered moons scattered fragments across the sky, meteors were falling.

"Why..."

Karatorusa, embedded in the ground, twisted its body and slowly raised its head.

"Why do you seek to destroy us?"

"Because you are strong."

Argones' voice was cold.

"There is no predictable range for creatures, but you have become too strong. You can think of it as glory."

At first, they were just strong creatures.

But the incredible cognitive abilities created by extreme environments repeatedly regressed...

'The source of the world.'

They reached the stage of dominating phenomena.

"How does that become a reason for annihilation?"

"I don't know."

Argones didn't know either.

"We exist without reason, and all history is driven by a mission that transcends this world."

"Are you talking about the outside world?" Anke Ra defined everything in this world, but there was one fact it couldn't know.

Where did I come from?

"Life inherits genes from its predecessors. But Anke Ra is different. Can you imagine the thoughts of a being born from nothing? It's just a mission. A mission to fill this world with life."

"What if that mission becomes corrupted someday?"

Argones tilted his head and, after another vast amount of time, answered.

"Whatever the whole becomes, that is the whole."

"Then what is the meaning of life? Only Anke Ra can dream new dreams. If all life is reset, what are we doing now?"

"...What are you trying to say?"

"I'm talking about your authority. If such a situation arises, as the one responsible for all life, you cannot be without fault."

Anke Ra, Gaia, and Argones were born with the mission to manage their respective realms.

'My mission...'

Karatorusa spoke.

"I will protect it."

Argones raised his head.

"What?"

"Even if Anke Ra dreams new dreams, I will protect the dreams of all life. As an apostle of time..."

Karatorusa, raising its massive body, looked down at Argones with numerous dragons on its back.

"History will not change."

Argones fell into thought.

'A truly unique creature. A mutation? But that too is just causality of the law.'

He wanted to believe there was a reason for existence.

"I will be watching."

As Argones, who had allowed the preservation of the dragons, turned and walked away, the Great Star appeared.

"What is it?"

"It's fortunate that we can protect the children."

The Great Star smiled and added.

"Dear."

"...Hmph."

Leaving a cold response, as he passed by, the Great Star turned and said.

"See you often."

It was just such a world.

An era when they didn't even know the true meaning of their mission and thought they were everything.

Shirone endlessly accepted Omega.

And at some point, he felt with his ten senses an event that would shake the foundation of this world.

"It feels good."

Now, in a stable environment, Argones and the Great Star looked down at the creatures on the ground.

"Yes. They resemble you."

As humans born from the earth, the men's skin was rough, while the women's skin was smooth like moist clay.

They all had fierce expressions, and their metallic hair extended down to their waists, giving them a strong appearance.

"Shall we call them Gaia?"

At Argones' words, the Great Star turned her head.

"Is that really okay?"

"Their form resembles me, but everything about them comes from you. It's better to call them Gaia."

The Great Star was pleased.

"Our children..."

At that moment, Argones' eyes widened, and a serious light began to shine.

"What is it?"

Feeling the same thing, the Great Star looked up at the sky, and Shirone matched her gaze.

'It's not the sky.'

Countless flashes, likely originating from outside the world, were being fired at the Gaia people.

User code input-link.

The Gaia people, receiving the signal, raised their heads, and golden electricity flickered in their eyes.

"Grrr!" A man who had been frowning soon calmed down and looked around.

"Is this it? It's terribly desolate. Doesn't seem like much fun."

A woman resembling a wolf checked her hands alternately and approached him.

"We're the first. Even though we'll forget everything once the missing link code is activated, it still feels a bit unsettling. Usually, it's completed in the fetal state."

As other users began to lose their memories, the woman had a dazed expression.

"Ah... it's coming for me too." The light in her pupils was fading.

"Take care. See you in the next life." As the woman's memory was blocked, the man also looked up at the sky, waiting for the moment.

"...The beginning of endless reincarnation." Argones approached.

"Who are you?"

As the man turned his head and smiled, Argones was purely shocked.

No being in the world could display such perfect arrogance.

'That light in his eyes.' The light that looked down on all things with disdain, where did it come from?

'As if...'

It seemed to say:

I am your god.
