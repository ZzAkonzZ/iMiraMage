Chapter 1026: The Circulating World (2)

Destura let out a screech.

It was likely a vocal organ meant to threaten living beings, but it was useless in space.

‘What are these things?’

Combined with Nane’s thoughts, Destura looked like an inverted human when viewed from above.

Its joints seemed bent backward, making it a chilling sight.

‘Surprisingly strong.’

But from Son Yujeong’s perspective below, it was just a terrifying monster resembling a bug.

‘In that case…’

Heating her body intensely, Son Yujeong flew through space like a jet, spewing flames.

Nane’s eyes sparkled.

‘The Nimbus Cloud.’

Seeing the stone monkey’s technique, which once disrupted the heavens, in action brought back memories.

Each swing of her staff tore Destura’s flesh, but the lack of sound made it feel unreal.

Finally, as Destura disintegrated into mental energy, Son Yujeong lowered her two staffs.

“Phew!”

Smoke billowed endlessly from her mouth as if burning logs.

Son Yujeong walked slowly through the cloud obstructing Nane’s view.

As she penetrated the barrier of the Sermon, her body, heated like molten metal, quickly cooled.

Nane spoke.

“Thank you for dealing with that nuisance. But what brings a restless monkey to the desolate moon?”

Son Yujeong pointed her staff at him.

“You know everything, don’t you? I’m not like my grandfather. I’ll live as I please.”

Shura formed a hand seal.

“Son Yujeong, put away your hostility. No matter how reckless you are, such behavior before Buddha is unacceptable.”

“Shura.”

Son Yujeong frowned.

“You too. Mortasinger was captured. Are you just going to watch as a fellow member of the Council of Ten?”

“The Council of Ten…”

A hint of longing crossed Shura’s eyes.

“It disbanded. Since Veron left, there’s been no leader to guide us.”

“And Buddha killed Amita. Yet you stay by his side?”

Shura remained silent.

‘Veron…’

He had told her to find Shirone.

‘I can’t do that yet.’

She couldn’t follow his last wish because she still didn’t know how to ‘live.’

‘In a world filled with lies and emptiness,

what does it mean to live?’

Until she understood the secrets of this world, she wouldn’t leave Buddha’s side.

“Hmph! What does a thunder monkey know? You’re just mad because your friend got captured!”

It was true.

“Fine, it has nothing to do with me. But I have business with Buddha. So Shura.”

Son Yujeong’s eyes glowed gold.

“If you interfere, I’ll beat you up.”

The Golden Eyes of Truth.

If they could see the truth of all things, Shura’s Gestalt would be useless.

‘Lucky you met your great-grandfather.’

Associating with Mortasinger was far from enlightenment.

Yet having everything good in the world made Shura envious.

Nane asked,

“So what do you want from me?”

“The way to Hell. You must know it. Send me there. I’ll get my friend back.”

“Hell…”

Of course he knew, but teaching a monkey the enlightenment of a bat would take time.

“Going isn’t the problem. It depends on which Hell you want to go to.”

“What do you mean?”

Son Yujeong hated having to think things through.

“When you bear karma too heavy for reality, Hell will call you. So decide.”

A sword emitting frost and another burning with flames appeared on either side of Nane.

“The Eight Cold Hells or the Eight Hot Hells? The Poisonous Snake Hell or the Mountain of Knives Hell? If not…”

Behind Nane, countless swords spread out like a fan, emitting multicolored light.

“You can name any place you want.”

The moon trembled.

It would affect the human planet, but such thoughts were overshadowed by the immense avatar before them.

“Buddha.”

The image of Buddha rising beyond the moon’s horizon seemed to be at the edge of the universe.

Son Yujeong watched with pursed lips and a blank expression before mischief appeared on her face.

“Haha! So you’re saying you’ll send me to Hell. Fine, I accept your challenge!”

Shura’s eyes narrowed sleepily.

‘That crazy monkey.’

Challenging even after seeing such cosmic grandeur was likely due to an innate mental disorder.

‘But Buddha…’

Son Yujeong’s power had drawn out Nane’s sincerity and couldn’t be underestimated.

“Grrr!”

Lowering her stance and twisting her waist, Son Yujeong heated her inorganic body intensely.

“Come.”

As Nane’s lips curled into a smile, her jet exploded with sparks in her eyes.

“Kiaaa!”

Her extended staff traced a fiery arc as Sermon surged from the edge of space.

In the Underworld.

After escaping from the Valley of Wails, Ethella and Shagal wandered through hell in search of a village.

"It's too vast."

It would have been normal to find something after walking over 700 kilometers in reality.

"Ugh!"

While lost in thought, Ethella’s arm was scraped by a branch. Everything in the forest was made of iron, so it was as if she had been slashed by a blade.

"This is getting annoying."

Feeling the same pain through the chain, Shagal strode up to Ethella.

"Aren't you paying attention? If you're going to die, do it alone. Don't drag me into it." Shagal was also extremely irritable.

"I'm sorry. But we've been walking for days and still haven't seen anything like a village. We need to think."

"Why should I? The only one shivering here is you. I'm just watching you suffer because you can't achieve what you want."

"Enough! We’ve fought enough! How long are you going to keep harping on me?"

"Forever."

Shagal smirked.

"Do you think I hate you? Far from it. This place is paradise compared to reality. At least you are real. So keep crying. Make it more enjoyable for me."

"You're trash."

Ethella despaired.

"Worthless trash. I should have ignored you from the start. There were plenty of others who could deal with trash like you."

In a fit of rage, Shagal pulled Ethella close and forced a kiss.

"Don't!"

Losing control, Ethella pushed Shagal away and delivered a powerful blow to his jaw.

The chain rattled, and both of them fell to the ground from the impact.

"Owww..."

Hit directly by Ethella’s counterattack, Shagal struggled to flip over and shrugged his shoulders.

"Hehe! Hehehehe!"

All he could do was laugh.

"Ha-haha! I'm going crazy! Ah, really crazy!"

Meanwhile, Ethella trembled miserably, holding back her tears.

'Why me...'

It was karma.

Knowing this, she still hated the reality of being with this man.

"Let's endure. We have to overcome this."

Thinking of the pain of Jin Seong Eum, her own troubles seemed slightly comforting.

Pulling herself up, Ethella said,

"We're both too on edge. Let's rest a bit. You don’t want to fall prey to demons either, do you?"

Trying to manage her emotions superhumanly, Shagal was not in his right mind.

"Hehehe! Aaah, this is driving me mad! Hahahaha!"

Angry, Ethella lay down on the ground and turned her back coldly.

'I should ignore him.'

Time passed.

'Huh?'

Realizing she had fallen asleep, Ethella quickly got up.

'What?'

She noticed she was no longer in the place where she had fallen asleep, but in a completely different environment with soft soil and a small spring about 10 meters ahead.

'Water, water.'

She wouldn't die from not drinking, but dehydration was as torturous here as in reality.

As she crawled toward the water, Shagal sat nearby, holding a clump of dirt to his nose.

"Did you bring me here?"

Shagal remained silent for a long time, only after shaking the dirt off his hands did he speak.

"I moved because I smelled water. I was so thirsty I thought I'd go mad."

His dehydration had blurred his memory.

'How could there be water in hell?' The thought was brief, and Ethella, as if bewitched, plunged her lips into the water.

Gulping.

The sound of the water flowing down her throat was refreshingly satisfying to hear.

"Ha! Ha!"

As her mind cleared, she finally had the spare capacity to worry about the next thing.

"Where are we?"

"I don't know. You know, you were just wandering around in the iron forest, right? It must be because of a magnetic field that disrupts the sense of direction."

"Why didn’t you say that before..."

Realizing that arguing was pointless, Ethella swallowed her words and sighed.

"Keep talking."

Shagal stood up.

"There must be something to find here. There's water and a smell of corpses. This must be an artificial place created using corpses. Otherwise, there's no way such a cozy spot could exist in hell."

His logic made sense.

'But who could it be? If there's a being in hell that needs water, besides a growth god...' At that moment, the bushes rustled, and Ethella quickly turned her head.

Someone's voice was heard.

"What’s this, someone’s here?" From the bushes, which resembled animal remains and plants that didn’t exist in reality, a group of menacing figures emerged.

Each looked ferocious, fitting for a place called hell.

A one-eyed man widened his eye.

"A woman? A woman."

She had a purity that didn't fit the dark hell known for harboring corrupt souls.

"What are you?"

Shagal asked while sitting, but the seven ruffians didn’t even pretend to listen.

"These ones have chains. They're burdened with karma. Let’s see, what kind of misdeeds..."

A man with a forked tongue picked up the chain and looked between Shagal and Ethella.

"Oh, chains of fate?"

Laughter erupted.

"Interesting. Hey, what are you? What did you do? Adultery? Or incest?"

Shagal stood up.

"I don't care who you think you are, but leave. This place is mine now."

"Oh, is that so? You vulgar perverts. You, do you know who we are? Once we were serial killers who commanded the world, buddy. The real scary ones." A man approached from behind without a sound and held an axe to Shagal’s neck.

"Don’t move. If your head falls off, the rest of your hell life will be miserably hard."

Two surrounded Shagal while the others approached Ethella.

"A beauty, huh? Must have mesmerized men in your lifetime. Too precious to be just food."

Ethella stepped back, but couldn’t distance herself due to the chain connected to Shagal.

"Stop."

Shagal spoke.

"The moment you touch that woman, you’re dead."

Despite feeling chills while speaking, Ethella's pain was his pain.

"Hahaha! So touching? Fallen into hell but still keeping the love?"

The man holding Shagal’s back tapped his neck with the axe, threatening him.

"Don't be mistaken, mate. You're the worst one here. That’s why you fell here."

'Tap, tap.'

Remembering the rhythm of the taps, Shagal materialized a dagger in his hand.

"Quick-blade."

With incredible speed, he stabbed the one-eyed man’s neck, and a sound of slicing the wind followed.

"...what?"

As the blade stuck and blood spurted, the murderers realized the situation.

"This bastard!"

As the man swung the axe with the same speed, Shagal ducked and stretched out his left hand.

"Ugh!"

Grabbing the man’s neck while his right hand’s quick-blade moved back and forth rapidly.

Pfff-pfff-pfff-pfff!

It seemed to vibrate, and blood and entrails poured from the seventeen wounds in his abdomen.

"Ugh..."

"What, a serial killer?"

As the man flipped his eyes and collapsed, Shagal held a new dagger and turned around.

"Who’s next?" The forest was silent.
