Chapter 955: What Goffin Left Behind (2)

As Ikael led the army of heaven, following Shura, she recalled events from long ago.

'Perhaps at that time...'

Goffin was erased.

It was likely the reason Anke Ra decided to become human, even at the cost of destroying his own dignity.

'I can't remember, but that event... it must have shocked every being in the universe.'

When Goffin was erased from the Akashic Records, Ikael felt as if a hole had been punched through her chest.

"Gasp!"

A massive sense of loss overwhelmed her, so immense that it felt like an eternity had passed.

"Ah..."

And then, moments later, tears of light rolled down her cheeks.

Her memories hadn't returned.

But now she realized what she couldn't remember.

"Goffin."

It was just that her thoughts couldn't reach him, but her heart was filled entirely with Goffin.

"He left me."

It felt like her heart was being torn out, and the remaining emptiness was vast enough to fill the universe.

"He came to me."

Ikael curled up and sobbed.

"We were connected!"

There were no memories to trace back, but her painfully aching heart was speaking.

"I loved him..."

Even after her tears soaked the ground, her sobbing showed no signs of stopping. How much time had passed?

'I want to know.'

Countless scenes her heart had sent up were tangled and jumbled, connecting to nothing.

"Right now!"

Determined to confront Anke Ra, she sprang up and approached the door.

"...!"

But her hand couldn't open the door.

"No."

She didn't know why, but she felt that going to Anke Ra now would lead to another tragedy.

Even though it seemed like there was nothing left to lose, that feeling persisted.

'I have to wait.'

She knelt weakly.

'Why do I feel like this isn't the end?'

Determined not to fail a second time, Ikael steeled herself and lifted her head.

"Goffin, don't worry. I will continue."

Goffin's erasure left a void in Ikael's heart that nothing could fill.

And 19 years later, Ikael amplified that engraved shape and finally regained her perfect memory.

Meanwhile, Ikael wasn't the only one shocked by Goffin's erasure.

To varying degrees, everyone who knew Goffin felt a heart-wrenching shockwave.

The purer the mental state, the stronger the shock, and even archangels felt a sense of dissonance.

'What is this? Something has cracked in this world.'

Of course, the one who felt the crack most clearly was Anke Ra, the avatar of the Akashic Records.

"Kiiiii!"

In the sanctuary of Araboth, Anke Ra's tendrils moved at an unprecedented speed.

'A specific code has left the photon system.'

This was a clear error.

Even if he didn't know the code's substance, no other being could do what Anke Ra couldn't.

'Find it.'

Identifying the erasure code was like searching for a needle in the universe.

"Kiiiiii!"

Anke Ra went into overdrive.

'Even if the system is destroyed, this must be found.'

The universe shook, and the system's overload pushed the world to the brink of explosion.

'Found it.'

It was a human.

'McClain Goffin.'

How could this error be fixed?

Even after searching the entire Akashic Records, there was no solution.

'Restore! Restore! Restore!'

Yet Anke Ra endlessly delved into the problem of Goffin's erasure.

'Error found! Restore! Error found! Restore! Error found!'

By now, it was unclear whether Goffin or Anke Ra was the error.

The Akashic Records heated endlessly, and just as the universe was about to shatter.

"Roar!"

Red light burst from Anke Ra's eyes, against his will.

'Initialization? Why?'

The universe was heading towards a shutdown state.

"Kiek! Kiek! Kiek!"

He mobilized all means to prevent the reset, but every command was denied.

'This is like...'

Anke Ra felt a force stronger than the universe.

'Idea.'

Even the highest-performing machine can't withstand a single flick of a switch.

'A force acting from outside the world.'

That light and simple force was destroying all of Anke Ra's commands as it entered.

'How can this be?'

Anke Ra lost the meaning of his existence.

"No! I am the universe! I am the whole! No one but me can initialize this place!"

Shame.

It wasn't an emotion understood through information, but something he had actually become.

It was an emotion, the first feeling he had ever experienced.

"Kiiiii!"

Dropping black liquid like oil from beneath his eyes, Anke Ra roared in fury.

"Goffiiiiiiin..."

The world went dark.

Feeling the flash narrowing from infinity's end to end, he thought,

'Why can't I...'

Become a god?

"The movements of the demon army haven't changed."

Based on the information arriving at the command control room, Iruki ran a simulation.

When Rian defeated Natasha, he cheered, and when Amy returned, he cried.

"But."

Looking at the overall situation, it was only a matter of time before the flower field collapsed.

'According to my analysis, the demons should have already turned towards Bashka. Someone must be stubbornly holding their ground...'

Is it really possible?

'It can't be. Meirei's divine frequency is a perfect ability. If it's possible...'

The incident where Son Yu-jung destroyed the Abyss Cliff.

'The elemental bomb should have fallen there. Did they infer the intention of the holy war from that event?'

Iruki shook his head.

'But is that possible? The demon army doesn't even know the concept of an elemental bomb.'

No existing weapon has the power to annihilate the demon army all at once.

'Would they really risk breaking through the flower field just on the vague expectation that something big might happen in Bashka?'

If true, the person named Balkan was one of two things.

'Either someone suffering from extreme paranoia or a literal monster of imagination.'

Probably both.

If true, the probability of the demon army voluntarily heading to Bashka was close to 0%.

There wouldn't even be an opportunity to detonate the elemental bomb.

'We're going to lose.'

For the first time, the word "defeat" clearly emerged in Iruki's mind.

"The flower has been broken!"

Even without a report, it was clear that the giant structure was collapsing.

'We won't hold out much longer.'

Amy's arrival allowed them to hold out 15 minutes longer than Lupist had expected.

A short time.

But in that time, the number of demons the flower had annihilated was beyond imagination.

'But why don't I feel good about it...'

No matter how many demons died in the flower field, it was ultimately infinite for humans.

'We have to lure them to Bashka. Without the elemental bomb, this is a fight we can't win from the start.'

"The flower has been broken!"

A report came in, and wrinkles formed on the forehead of Protea, the chief of the flower tribe.

"Ugh!"

The pain of the flower tribe members being captured by the demons flowed through the small world window.

'Why?'

Tears of blood flowed from Protea's eyes.

'Why do we have to be treated like this?'

If they were human, they would have bitten their tongues and died.

"We can't even do that!" Even if they withered from stress, plants weren't given the function to commit suicide.

"Protea!"

Enox came running from the front, looking back.

"Release the small world window! Push the defense line back 400 meters and counterattack again!"

"Oh, lord of the forest!"

In a shout mixed with all kinds of emotions, Enox confirmed the tears of blood flowing down her cheeks.

"Let's go, Protea."

Enox grabbed her shoulder hard enough to hurt.

"I know how you feel. But trust the humans."

"Why should we?"

Protea shook her head.

"I don't care about gods or ideas. We just want to live happily. We want to live quietly in the forest, basking in the sunlight, without bothering anyone."

"That's why we're a failed race." As Protea looked confused, Enox met her eyes directly.

"Protea, there's no way to be happy without fighting. That's where we and humans diverged."

History tells us.

"The reason humans dominate this world isn't just because they're strong. It's because they have a much longer history of persecution and a history of struggle where they never stopped fighting for a moment."

Enox pointed to the battlefield.

"Look. Do you think humans are brave? Not at all. They're all terrified."

Protea, half out of her mind, watched the allied forces charge with screams.

"But humans still fight. The blood of ancestors who went through a history of struggle tells them. Happiness isn't given by others; it's something you fight for and seize yourself. They instinctively know that fighting is better than being terrified."

"That's what humans..."

Enox grabbed Protea's shoulders and turned her around.

"Trust the humans. Not because they're gods. They know how to fight the world. If humans can't do it, no race in this world can."

Looking straight into Enox's eyes, Protea took a deep breath and nodded.

"Yes. I'll fight for the flower tribe."

As a gap opened in the allied forces, a large number of demons rushed in.

"There they are! Catch the elves! That one's mine!"

"Go! Build the defense line!"

As Enox pushed Protea's back and drew her sword to charge at the demons.

"Huh?"

The demons stepping forward were suddenly sucked into a magic circle engraved on the ground.

Splatter! Splatter!

And then, from a completely different magic circle, they were squeezed out as juice.

As the input and output of the magic circuits kept changing, demon flesh splattered everywhere.

"What is this?"

A gate magic circle quickly formed under Lupist's feet as he looked back.

"Whew, we've arrived now."

Led by a tired Dante, the 3rd division's troops floated up from the ground after setting traps.

Assessing the situation, Lupist looked at the magic circle and then turned back.

"An infinite loop track?"

Dante gave a strained smile.

"It's deadly to large armies."

The principle is this:

Connect two spatial movement magic circles and place blades between them.

That's enough for the trap.

"Unlike physical traps, the load capacity is infinite. With this, we can kill demons endlessly."

Lupist nodded.

"It will also be useful for protecting specific areas. Smart thinking. But durability is another issue, right?"

"Yes. With my skills, 30 minutes is the limit."

"30 minutes?"

Lupist laughed helplessly.

"Do you really think we can survive that long?"

The allied forces were fighting the greatest battle in history, but humans weren't that strong.

'We're out of stamina.'

Meanwhile, the demons who had just joined the battlefield were full of energy.

'The only ones who can hold out are... Garcia, Amy, and Rian.'

'The number of demons those three have taken down accounts for 25% of the demon casualties. Truly, they are...'

A dangerous thought crossed his mind.

"Wait."

Dante asked.

"What's wrong?"

"Isn't it strange?"

"What?"

"You just joined, so you wouldn't know. Our commanders are undoubtedly the strongest. But why?"

Lupist's gaze turned to the demon army.

"Why haven't any of the enemy's strongest commanders, the legion commanders, appeared?"

Dante's eyelids fluttered.
