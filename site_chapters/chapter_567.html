Chapter 567:

Shirone asked, "What exactly do you mean by 'cannot harm'?"

"It includes both physical and mental attacks. However, it does not apply to tactical applications, meaning secondary actions."

Hershey said, "Secondary actions, huh. So, setting traps and waiting for participants is allowed?"

"Yes. Since the attack's source is not the loser of the Kang but objects, strategies, or terrain."

"I understand. What if someone chooses Pao?"

"Refusing the challenge. If you choose Pao, you also cannot harm the challenger until the next Scramble is summoned. However, in this case, your cards are not destroyed."

"But you can keep your cards."

"Exactly."

Iruki rested his chin in thought.

Whether you lose a Kang or choose Pao, not being able to harm the challenger is a significant risk.

'You could be chased by the opponent for nearly two hours. If they manage to steal your Master Card during that time…'

The game's dynamics could shift rapidly.

'It's not just about competing with card hands. The rules encourage destroying the opponent's Master Card. Tactics and combat skills are crucial.'

Amy asked, "What if the priority is the same? I mean, if the card combinations are identical."

"If the same hand appears, there is no winner or loser. Also, the cards are not destroyed."

Though not explicitly stated, it implied that they should fight it out.

'Hmm, I thought there would be a priority based on acquisition time, but it seems matching the hand is enough.'

So, there's no need to open Random Cards immediately.

"That concludes the game rules. Does everyone understand?"

There was no response.

While they understood the rules, grasping the full intent would require actual gameplay.

'The rules are simple, but the use of Random Cards and the two-hour invincibility variable could complicate things.'

Ruru wrapped up.

"Basically, the game period is seven days. The player with the highest hand at midnight on the last day wins, and all rewards and penalties are distributed through the Judge."

In summary, to win in Black and White, you must meet one of two conditions:

Destroy all of the opponent's Master Cards or maintain the strongest hand until the end.

'Neither is easy. To destroy the Master Cards, you must defeat the entire opposing team. On the other hand, maintaining the strongest hand for seven days is difficult. Sacrifice strategies will be necessary.'

"The Scramble locations are displayed as XYZ coordinates, and once collected, they disappear from the Master Card. These were the rules for 'Black and White.' Use the rules creatively and enjoy the game with your friends."

As Ruru disappeared, the Judge's light turned off.

"Enjoy the game, my foot," someone muttered.

The 12 participants, having heard the Black and White rules, were silent, each contemplating the variables and their abilities.

'We've won. This game is ours.'

Though he didn't show it, Neid was thrilled inside.

One second in photon time.

If time is fluid, Shirone's time manipulation could be highly effective.

Iruki delved deeper.

'Casting Slow can extend one second. But Shirone's time manipulation is beyond that.'

The key was the use of Random Cards.

While the exact time for a Random Card to become Black or White was unknown, it wouldn't exceed one second.

Thus, Shirone could repeatedly open Random Cards until he got the desired color.

This negates the randomness disadvantage of hidden cards.

Shirone must have thought of this, but he maintained a poker face, watching the opposing team.

'Only my friends know my ability.'

Even if they didn't know, it was clear this wouldn't be an easy game, yet the alliance team looked confident.

'Baseless confidence? Or do they have a killer strategy?'

As thoughts settled, Hershey resumed the negotiation.

"Now, let's move on to rewards and penalties."

Neid spoke up.

"First, let's confirm what's already decided. If we win, the six members of the alliance team must rank lower than us until the graduation exam."

Hershey noted it down.

"Alright. If our team wins, the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Group must leave Istas."

This part was easy, but the next was tricky.

"Now, let's move on to individual stakes. If anyone has a reward or penalty they want to propose for this Scramble Royal, raise your hand."

No one raised their hand immediately because it wasn't just about stating what they wanted.

It was a negotiation.

To secure a reward, you had to accept a corresponding penalty.

"I have a proposal."

When Kaiden raised his hand, Shirone's team tensed, especially Shirone, who nervously bit his lip.

A jealous person could demand anything.

'I don't mind if it's about separating me and Amy. But if he plans something against Amy…'

As anxious thoughts swirled, Shirone took a deep breath.

'Stay calm. Even unreasonable conditions can be negotiated.'

The only concern was that once they accepted the Scramble Royal, the game had to be played.

Refusing would be considered a forfeit, and if they refused that, it would mean war.

"If our side wins…"

Shirone swallowed hard.

"Shirone, you must sincerely confess your love to Maya."

"…What?"

Shirone's eyes widened at the unexpected demand, but he was relieved that Amy wasn't involved.

Amy, however, reacted differently.

"You jerk! Do you think you can control people's feelings with this game?"

"I'm not forcing you. If you can't do it, you'll pay with your life. We'll discuss the penalty for breaking the contract later."

"You…!"

"Amy, wait."

As Amy raised her fist, Shirone spoke.

"Nothing is decided yet. If it's something we can't do, we can negotiate."

Though Shirone said this, he sensed they would have to accept Kaiden's proposal.

"Yes. The negotiation is with Shirone. You know this is the most realistic condition I can propose."

Amy said, "Shirone, ignore him. We have about 11 hours left. Let's see this through."

Kaiden's eyes widened.

"Do you really want to see it through? If this is all the negotiation, I should raise the stakes. How about if we win, Amy becomes my doll? Or should I sell her to the Golden Wheel?"

Amy slammed the table with both hands.

"Who do you think you are? I'd rather die!"

"Then die!"

Kaiden shouted back.

"Do you think this is school? No one will care if you die. I've already made the maximum concessions for smooth negotiations. The underground research group has its own rules, and everyone here agreed to them. If you don't want to participate, just leave."

Shirone asked, "What does it mean to you if I confess my love to Maya? You like her, don't you?"

"She is my muse."

Kaiden's eyes gleamed with madness.

"I can sacrifice anything for her happiness. I can also kill anyone."

"Everyone, calm down."

Iruki tried to control the atmosphere.

"It's just a proposal. And Kaiden made it clear he won't back down. So…"

Electricity sparked in Iruki's eyes as he glared at Kaiden.

"You're prepared to pay a corresponding penalty?"

Iruki might have been the angriest for involving Amy.

"That's something to decide after hearing the conditions."

Hershey stepped in to deflect Iruki's hostility.

"But before that, can we propose a penalty from our side?"

Neid asked aggressively, "Why are you proposing our reward?"

"Just listen. It's not a bad condition."

Sabina, calming Neid, asked, "Alright, let's hear it."

Hershey smiled and raised a finger.

"If you accept Kaiden's proposal, we'll ensure one of your members passes the graduation exam."

"…What?"

"We won't just stamp the pass, but we'll use all the Golden Wheel's connections and abilities to ensure it."

'Fermi.'

It was certain that the Golden Wheel acted as brokers for the graduation exam, though there was no evidence.

'Hershey couldn't propose this alone. They set the negotiation limits beforehand.'

Hershey raised two fingers.

"If you don't like that, how about this? Until the graduation exam, all Golden Wheel members will rank lower than your team."

It was a bold proposal for a magic school where graduation was paramount.

'Why are they so obsessed with the Scramble Royal? What's the secret of Istas?'

"Let's accept it."

Shirone's team turned to Dorothy.

"What?"

"Let's accept it. The research members aside, Sabina and I are outsiders. We need that kind of reward to give our best."

Dorothy looked at Iruki.

"Isn't that why you brought me in?"

Iruki nodded.

"That's true."

Neid said, "But Dorothy, even if you graduate that way…"

"I know. I don't want to pass with someone's help. I don't need special treatment. But if I'm on the edge of passing, I can't guarantee anything. Does anyone want to stay another year?"

Dorothy held out her hand.

"Sorry, I misspoke. My point is, it's more efficient to accept it for now, regardless of using the pass card. Kaiden isn't asking for Amy's body or for Shirone to date Maya. It's just a confession. And we get a pass card. You know what that means?"

It meant they could control Fermi's group in the graduation exam.

'The Golden Wheel will do their best. Since it's a contract through the Judge, they must pay the penalty for breaking it. Hershey is telling the truth.'

As Iruki pondered, Dorothy added,

"Don't get me wrong. If there were another way, I'd reject this proposal. But there's no better compromise for Shirone, Kaiden, Amy, and Maya. Honestly, isn't it stupid not to accept?"

"Just a confession? Regardless of Maya's response?"

"Of course. Whether Maya accepts or not doesn't matter. But the confession must be sincere. If you can't do it, you'll have to pay the penalty."

"Phew."

It's quite ambiguous.

Logically, the scales had already tipped, but emotionally, it felt like they were balanced again.

Neid clutched his head.

'Hershey won't back down. It's a condition for participating.'

Knowing this, Hershey proposed the most powerful negotiation card, even at a loss.

"Let's step outside and discuss our opinions."

He wanted to hear Shirone's thoughts first.

"Go ahead. Today is for that."

As Hershey suggested, Neid walked around the table and called his friends.

"Everyone, come out."

Glaring at the alliance team, the five of them walked around the table and out of the research group.
