Chapter 411:

As the giant bird Kaidera flew towards Galliante Island, it spotted a cargo ship crossing the open sea and lowered its altitude.

When Zulu dismissed Kaidera, the four people, including Siena and Kuan, began to fall rapidly towards the sea.

Zulu and Siena used fly magic, while Shirone spread his wings to control his descent.

For Kuan, a swordsman, altitude was not a familiar environment, but he didn't even blink as he confirmed the ship's position.

His ability to generate external force through vibration to counteract gravitational acceleration was no less than a mage's skill.

Siena was impressed by the skill of the death magician she had only heard about in rumors.

Ten years ago, he was considered unparalleled in swordsmanship movement.

Such a person had lost his Achilles tendon, much like the one who had lost his eyes for her.

'Come to think of it, they are similar.'

The four landed on the deserted aft deck of the ship.

At the same time, Zulu summoned the auto-prism, making them all invisible.

'As expected, things are easier with skilled people.'

How many could infiltrate a ship at sea and reach Galliante without being detected?

If Sein were here, he could have used Equilibrium to block out sound as well.

No matter how dangerous Heaven was, with a team like this, they wouldn't be easily defeated.

The warehouse was filled with goods from the mainland.

The four of them went to a corner where sacks of rice were piled up and sat back-to-back in pairs.

"When we reach the port, I can't summon the auto-prism. There's a risk of being detected by a Jonah. Flue will be waiting at the rendezvous point, so we need to get out without being noticed."

Siena handed a robe to Kuan.

Zulu could cover her face with her current clothes, and Shirone could use his diamond armor to blend in.

An hour later, the ship docked at the port. There was a commotion outside the warehouse, and the door opened hurriedly.

As soon as the sailors entered, the four slipped out.

The deck was busy with workers loading cargo from the island, so no one paid attention to them.

The dock was bustling with ship mechanics, cargo handlers, warehouse managers, and traders.

Gis, who had a deep connection with Shirone, was also running around the docks, soliciting travelers.

"Hey, hey! I'll carry your luggage! I'll guide you to Galliante's famous Kergo ruins! Translation services available!"

Shirone pulled his robe's hood down and glanced at Gis.

'You're still the same, Gis. Is Yuna doing well too?'

Even though the Parrot Thieves had left, it seemed he was living a decent life.

"Huh? Who's that?"

When Gis turned around, Shirone lowered his head and followed his companions out of the dock.

He wanted to say hello, but there was nothing to say as he was heading towards his death, bidding farewell to the world.

'Be happy, Gis.'

Gis, staring blankly at Shirone, was approached by his friends with worried expressions.

"Ugh, why aren't we getting any customers today? Huh? Gis, what's wrong? Did you see a ghost? Who was that?"

"No, I just felt strange. Those people..."

His friend looked at the hooded figures and frowned. It seemed Gis's bad habit had resurfaced.

"Hey, don't bother with people like that. They look dangerous. If you get involved, you might not survive."

Another friend agreed.

"My instincts as a ten-year hawker tell me the same. Those people reek of danger."

Despite his friends' warnings, Gis couldn't take his eyes off them until they disappeared.

He didn't know why, but a nostalgic feeling washed over him.

'Come to think of it... I wonder how they're doing.'

"Hey! Over here! Need a porter?"

A large man who had disembarked from the passenger ship approached, carrying luggage in both hands.

Gis quickly snapped out of it. He raised his hand and ran over faster than anyone.

"Yes, yes! I'm coming!"

Kergo Ruins.

Gaold, Kangnan, Sein, Zulu, Flue, Etella, Siena, Kuan, and Shirone.

The nine participants in this project gathered together for the first time.

There was no need for a separate briefing as they had talked along the way.

There were a few agents in the ruins, but Sein, who had been waiting for a day, took care of them all.

Of course, they couldn't let their guard down, so it was best to cover their faces.

However, the main target, Gaold, threw back his hood and even took off his robe, tossing it to the ground.

Kangnan gritted his teeth and spoke.

"Put it on while I'm being nice."

"It's suffocating. Once we go in, it's over. From now on, we're heading to the Kergo autonomous district."

Sein added.

"If there's someone waiting at Goffin's Gate, we'll assess their skills and recruit them. We might add one more person to this team."

The group headed to the stepped altar on the east side of the central temple.

It was the place where Shirone had opened the Immortal Function and passed Miro's test.

A Kergo native was guarding the entrance, but it wasn't the same person Shirone had seen before.

With Chief Kadum's death, the political landscape of the Kergo tribe had likely changed significantly.

When Shirone and his friends visited a year ago, the native had exuded a terrifying aura, but now he seemed nervous, swallowing hard at the sight of Gaold's group.

As a Kergo warrior, the gatekeeper couldn't fail to sense the refined, blade-like aura emanating from the nine of them.

Gaold approached and spoke.

"Kertia, ro Hoima. Akrasia, Widmia Benzen."

Knock on my door, and it will open.

It was a nostalgic password that remained in Shirone's memory.

But the native's reaction was different this time. Shaking his head with a serious expression, he spoke in Kergo.

"Not now. There are too many people waiting to go to Heaven. Come back in three days."

Gaold frowned slightly and swung his hand, sending the native flying with a thud.

Shirone, staring in shock at the motionless native on the ground, asked Sein.

"What did the native say to make him so angry?"

"Gaold doesn't understand Kergo."

"......"

When Gaold activated the device, the wall of the altar collapsed, revealing a door.

Kangnan added a comment.

"But he doesn't like Kergo."

Entering the altar, they descended an endless square spiral staircase.

After about 20 minutes, they reached a corridor. As before, two natives guarded the iron door.

From here, it was the world of Kergo, with no intersection with outside civilization.

The natives had no qualms about displaying their tattoos and were much more aggressive than those guarding the entrance above.

"Wait. How did you get in? From today, entry to Heaven is..."

Gaold struck the native's jaw with the back of his hand, sending him to the ground with a thud.

The other native, about to shout in anger, was silenced by a blow to his jaw from Gaold's other hand.

Shirone, staring blankly at the two unconscious, nose-bleeding natives, thought this wasn't right.

If they opened the Immortal Function in the Hall of Achievement and Sacrifice, they could go to Goffin's Gate with everyone's welcome. There was no need to cause trouble with Kergo.

"Um, can't we just follow the proper procedure? I know some of the natives here, and if we light all eight eyes of the angel..."

"Angel?"

Gaold stopped abruptly and turned his head.

"Shirone, this is just a toy my classmate made."

Shirone fell silent.

He could sense Gaold's terrifying anger, but that wasn't the only reason. He thought he understood why Gaold disliked Kergo.

Inside the iron door was an octagonal room.

Three men guarded the opposite iron door, and in the center stood a man with white tattoos symbolizing a Kergo priest.

Even Shirone could see that their level of power was different from those who had blocked their way before.

But Gaold ignored him and entered the altar where the eight spheres created by Miro floated.

The priest frowned and moved towards them.

"Who are you? How dare outsiders enter the sacred angel's room..."

Before he could finish speaking, Gaold grabbed a sphere and cast a spell.

A simple air press shook the altar with a thud.

The priest, looking around in shock, quickly turned his gaze to the sphere.

The white light that Shirone couldn't see a year ago was now shining brightly.

Shirone stared intently at the sphere.

It was amazing that the white light was on, but more surprising was the small number displayed.

1,008,000.

"One million eight thousand points..."

Gaold approached the stunned priest, looked him straight in the eye, and said briefly.

"Move."

The priest's legs moved on their own, making way for Gaold.

It was a score far exceeding the passing mark in the language of the gods.

When they reached the Kergo autonomous district, warriors who had heard the news were guarding the entrance.

Their eyes, holding their weapons high, gleamed with vigilance towards the outsiders.

"This is the sacred land of Kergo! If you trespass, we will kill you without question!"

As Gaold walked towards the wall of spears, he compressed an air sphere in his palm.

Compared to the citizens of Heaven, the Kergo here were cute.

Warriors willing to die for Anke La. Moreover, their individual combat abilities were on a different level.

But to Gaold, they were all just Kergos.

"Everyone, lower your weapons!"

Just as Gaold's press magic was about to explode, a sturdy man walked through the warriors.

His black tattoos had turned gold, but Shirone recognized his face immediately.

It was Mahatu, who had opposed Chief Kadum as a member of the Anti-Angel faction.

The golden tattoos indicated that he had become the chief.

As expected, the warriors lowered their weapons and knelt.

"Chief! They are heretics who assaulted the Kergo priest! Why do you dampen our resolve?"

Mahatu briefly met Gaold's eyes before walking towards the eight companions standing behind him.

"It's been a while, Sir Shirone."

Mahatu had learned the continental language.

He believed in improving the lives of the tribespeople by separating religion from society, a necessary skill for negotiating with the Galliante government.

"Mr. Mahatu, you've become the chief."

"I don't know if I'm qualified, but it happened."

The warriors couldn't contain their anger.

To them, assaulting a priest was the worst crime imaginable.

"Chief! Explain yourself! Who are these people?"

Mahatu asked Shirone with a troubled expression.

"Why didn't you prove the angel's power?"

"Ah, well... there were some circumstances."

Mahatu seemed to understand.

Recently, many people had been flocking to the Kergo ruins.

What on earth was happening in Heaven?

The severity of the situation was evident from the level of the people Shirone had brought.

'What are these monsters?'

As Kergo's greatest warrior, Mahatu could sense it.

Each of them was a master who had honed their skills to the extreme. Especially Gaold, who didn't seem human.

Mahatu, who thought he would never lose in terms of fighting spirit, felt his resolve waver just by briefly meeting Gaold's eyes. Shirone couldn't help it either.

Although extreme conflict was avoided due to ongoing negotiations, the tension between the warriors and Gaold remained intense.

"Punish the heretics who assaulted the priest! We are ready to fight! We're not afraid of you!"

"Yeah, come on! Come at us!"

Kangnan looked at Gaold arguing with the warriors who didn't understand him and rubbed his temples in frustration.

'This guy shouldn't be the commander.'

Yet, he let it go because sometimes Gaold's methods were necessary.

After all, wasn't this the day Gaold had waited for 20 years?

No matter how annoying he was, Kangnan couldn't scold him today.

"Everyone, quiet! How dare you defy my orders!"

"Chief! We are ready to die for your command. But compromising with heretics denies the very existence of Kergo!"

"Fools. Do you know who is here?"

The warriors' gazes turned to Shirone.

But having been imprisoned under Kadum's iron rule a year ago, they had no way of recognizing the boy's face.

Mahatu raised his hand and introduced Shirone.

"This is Sir Shirone, the messenger of the gods who punished Kadum's faction and saved us, the Anti-Angel faction."
