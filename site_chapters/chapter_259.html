Chapter 259: The Revealed Truth (4)

However, Shirone shook his head.

There's less than a month left until the semester ends.

The practical exams were over, and only the theoretical exams were left.

Since he will be entering his senior year next semester, he had to think that if not during this period, he would have no time to reinforce his basics.

Logically, he knew that, but his emotions weren’t as easy to control.

Shirone was usually unwavering no matter what happens, but he couldn’t focus on his studies when it involved his parents.

That’s why, as soon as class ended, he entered the research group and became lost in thought.

Nade opened the door to the research group and came in.

“Hey, Shirone, we’re here.”

Iruki placed a bag of bread on the table and sat on the sofa opposite Shirone.

"I brought some food. You must be hungry, so eat if you want."

“Yeah, thanks.”

"We ran into Dante at the cafeteria."

“Really? How’s he doing?"

"Well, he’s gearing up for something. Looks like he’s staying at the academy. He said he’ll definitely beat you in the graduation exam and graduate first as the number one. I told him to tell you directly. He’ll probably declare it soon, so you might want to prepare a good comeback line."

Despite feeling troubled, Shirone couldn’t help but laugh.

Iruki was as thorough as ever. With Dante’s group now positioned as rivals, Iruki seemed more than ready for battle.

"Alright, I’ll think of something. Thanks for making me laugh."

Iruki gave a bittersweet smile at Shirone’s words. It was clear that, despite his outward composure, Shirone’s heart wasn’t at ease.

Nade, who was wondering if there was a way to ease Shirone's mind even a little, suddenly remembered a rumor and spoke up.

“I heard this afternoon that Principal Olivia has called Killain. She’ll probably come up with a solution, so don’t worry too much."

"Yes…."

Shirone had also heard rumors that Olivia might stay at the academy.

But with the situation already unfolding, it was unclear how much she could help.

The most frustrating thing was not being able to do anything.

Rumors have no substance, so it couldn’t be fought, arrested, or locked away.

All Shirone could do was hope that this storm would pass without causing further trouble.

@

Killain, who had received the summons, took a carriage that night and arrived in Creas.

Given Olivia’s high-ranking position as a high-ranking inspector in Teacher’s Association, a poor response could result in serious consequences. (올리비아는 교사회에서도 최고 간부인 감사직을 맡고 있기에 서툴게 대응하다간 사달이 날 것이다.)

Killain packed only her essentials, found an inn to stay the night, and headed to Alpheas Magic Academy the next morning.

The students who recognized her started gossiping.

The article revealing Shirone’s parents had clearly stirred up controversy. Regardless of how they felt about Shirone, no student would look favorably upon Killain.

She realized today’s meeting wouldn’t be easy.

'It doesn't matter. I was prepared for this anyway.'

By exposing Shirone’s background, she had preserved Dante’s star power.

Not only had she outsmarted the other reporters scrambling for a new star, but she had also secured her livelihood for a while longer.

Olivia would undoubtedly want Dante to succeed as well.

While Olivia had the social standing not to tolerate provocative articles, she would likely settle the matter with a few words of reprimand.

Killain straightened her clothes and stood in front of the principal’s office. Clearing her throat, she reported in her usual cheerful voice.

“It’s Killain.”

“Come in.”

Olivia's voice was the thing she, as a reporter, envied most.

If the gods allowed her to choose, she’d rather have Olivia’s voice than the rank of a second-rank archmage.

But today, something felt off.

As an authority in word magic, Olivia’s voice had a unique power to pierce through human emotions.

And today, her voice did not carry a pleasant feeling.

As she entered the principal's office, Olivia was sitting there with a cold expression. Unusually, former principal Alpheas was also present, and opposite them sat Shirone.

“Hello, Olivia, Alpheus. Oh, and hello, Shirone?”

Killain greeted all three of them warmly, making eye contact.

No one answered. Olivia, in particular, didn’t even offer her a seat.

“This article… Can you explain this…?”

Olivia threw the magazine at Killain's feet.

It was never a pleasant feeling to see a magazine with her article thrown on the floor like that.

But Killain didn't show it. The atmosphere was much worse than she had anticipated.

“It is the job and calling of a reporter to convey the facts. Is there a problem?”

Shirone's brow furrowed.

While a reporters may have a duty to report the facts, that didn’t give them the exclusive right to pursue their own values at the expense of others.

Even if the boundaries aren’t always clear, with the duty comes the responsibility to act carefully.

Killain held her head up straight as if she had nothing to be afraid of and met the gazes of the three.

This wasn’t the first time she had faced this kind of pressure while covering aspiring mages.

She had dealt with high-ranking nobles lobbying for favorable articles and even mysterious groups that raided her home and wrecked everything.

She overcame all the adversities and reached to this point.

Olivia also knows this situation better than anyone else.

There was no way she could do anything to Killain, as she had overcome the threats of so many competitors in an attempt to make Dante a star.

"Of course, I anticipated that Shirone’s interview would cause a social stir. But Olivia, it couldn’t be helped. I just transcribed the interview as it was. Shirone’s background being different from others isn’t my fault."

Olivia smiled brightly.

Is this it? Killain thought, genuinely believing it was over.

But Olivia’s smile vanished in an instant.

"You’ve grown bold, Killain.”

Killain's face stiffened.

"When you first joined Teacher's Association, you were just a rookie who couldn't even speak to me. And now, not only are you standing toe-to-toe with me, but you're even trying to play word games with me..."

Killain sensed that things were going strangely, but she was fighting for her livelihood.

She would rather die here than see her ten-year career go up in smoke.

“Huhu, is that so? It's been so long, I barely remember. Then again, I suppose you've aged quite a bit, Olivia. I never imagined you speaking this way before. I guess the trials of life have really taken their toll."

Olivia and Killain locked eyes in a direct, tense stare.

Alpheas opened his mouth because he couldn't stand to watch.

"Miss Killain, regardless of how important your article may be, when documenting someone's life, you still have a responsibility..."

“You stay still.”

Olivia interrupted.

What a frustrating old fool. Does he really think he can persuade someone as shrewd as Killain with some speech about morality?

Even The informal tone Olivia used in an official setting was clearly a tactic to pressure Killain.

Alpheas, embarrassed, stayed silent, but the effect was powerful.

Killain maintained her smile, but her face had turned as pale as a sheet of paper.

“Have you went mad, Killain?”

Killian wasn't breathing. Instead, her heart was pumping oxygen frantically throughout her body.

Even a child could see that if Olivia was serious about crushing her, Killain wouldn't stand a chance.

What Killain couldn’t understand was why Olivia was treating her this badly.

After all, everything she had done had been for Dante, and Olivia had tacitly gone along with it in the past.

"If I was rude, I apologize. But Olivia, this couldn't be helped. No matter how high up you are in the Teacher's Association, you can't interfere with a journalist's work. If this gets out, I won't be the one in trouble."

Killain's words had some truth to them.

No one in Teachers' Association had the authority to correct what the editorial department had written as factually correct.

The only exceptions were when the royal family issued a decree or when illegal actions was revealed during the writing of the article.

“No, Killain. You’re the one who’s ruining your career as a reporter. How did you get the interview with Shirone’s parents? According to Shirone, his parents are the type of people who would never carelessly reveal such things.”

Killain flinched.

She hadn’t expected Olivia to be this relentless. She had been somewhat at ease, assuming this wouldn’t be such a big deal.

Of course, she hadn’t used threats or overt pressure. She had simply exploited the natural worries of concerned parents.

“I just… kept asking, and they eventually told me. You know how people get nervous when it's called an interview. It happens all the time."

Olivia let out a disbelieving smile.

"Killain, do you think I don’t know you?"

Killain pressed her lips together tightly, glaring at Olivia with resentment.

Olivia was well aware of Killain's tactics—after all, they had worked together for ten years to turn Dante into a star.

That's why she felt even more wronged. It was not enough to give her an award for her service, but when Olivia called her and pressured her, she felt like crying.

“Since they’re a commoner who know next to nothing about the world of magic, you must’ve emphasized just one thing. Teachers’ Association. You used the fact that Shirone is still a student and exploited the authority of the Association. From the parents' perspective, they had no choice but to agree, thinking that being marked by the Association would mean the end for their child."

Shirone's eyes sharpened with intensity.

If Killain had really pressured his parents like that, it was something he could never forgive.

Killain felt like a cornered rat. It was clear that Olivia was determined to take her down today.

But there was still one more weapon left.

It was self-destruction.

She dropped her mask of pretense, adopting a sorrowful expression as her eyes filled with tears.

"Yes, that's what I did. So what? Olivia, you know how this works. Everyone does it. If you stop us from going that far, we wouldn't be able to write anything at all."

There was an unspoken threat in Killain's words. She was implying that even Dante had benefited from similar actions.

But Olivia didn’t flinch, not even a twitch of her eyebrow.

Of course, if one were to dig deeper, Olivia might have her own faults.

However, it was a mistake to think that Teachers' Association's inspector and the Red Line's second-rank archmage were in the same position as Killain.

"Killain, I'm not blaming you for being a reporter. I’m punishing you for betraying the trust I placed in you for so long."

Killain felt as if her world was collapsing.

It was a threat to see who would survive if they bit and tore at each other.

If it were someone like Alpheas, there would be nothing to fear.

But Olivia was streetwise and had a certain materialistic streak.

If such a person had been born with a genius mind, there would have been no room for Killain from the beginning.

"What betrayal? To be honest, I thought you’d be happy. Dante lost. I was just rooting for your disciple to succeed."

Only then did Olivia's expression soften.

Having stripped Killain of all her pretenses, now the real conversation could begin.

“I appreciate your consideration, but it is presumptuous. And this would have been for your own good, not mine. Without Dante, your position in the Association would have been shaken. But that’s not the case for me. Dante may have lost, but Shirone won. And Shirone is also my student, so I’ve lost nothing."

“Huh? What did you say?”

Killain made a bewildered expression.

Since when was Shirone her student?

She looked back at Alpheas, but he had a blank expression on his face as if he didn't know anything.

“Shirone is your student? But you're just temporary staff. I thought you were stepping down as principal soon."

"I was going to, but I changed my mind. I’m staying at Alpheas Magic Academy and will take up the position of vice principal starting next semester. Which makes you a traitor, trying to ruin the future of one of my students."

Killain still couldn’t fully grasp what Olivia was saying, her eyes darting around in confusion.
