Chapter 610:

"Yes, hello."

Nade greeted back.

Unlike before, he didn't seem to be avoiding her, but this made Sabina feel even more pushed away.

"What happened?"

She asked directly, true to her nature, but Nade only gave a bitter smile.

He wanted to be honest, knowing her feelings, but he knew that such honesty could hurt her and affect her performance in the graduation exam.

"I hope you graduate."

Nade was sincere.

She was one of the few who accepted him as he was.

Realizing Nade's consideration, Sabina turned away, looking dejected. Pandora, who had been watching, spoke up.

"Must be nice. Even in this situation, you have the luxury to worry about boys."

Before the second half of the graduating class started, Pandora had gone to the headquarters of Alones, a perfume manufacturing company, to learn the secret techniques of the fragrance mage Mael.

Like Pisho's insect magic, it wasn't a field advantageous for evaluations, but once she graduated, her expertise would guarantee her success.

"Don't get cocky just because you're higher in rank. Air mages are a dime a dozen in society."

Azellio Pandora (Final Rank: 19th in the graduating class).

Major: Fragrance Magic, Alones Series.

Special Notes: Mael's disciple.

Landing in Class Two thanks to the Scramble Royal was a significant psychological boost.

Sabina, recalling how she used to bully Pandora in the advanced class, approached her.

"W-what?"

Despite Pandora's disgust, Sabina gently straightened her crooked collar.

"Yeah, you too. Let's go shopping together once we're out in society."

When she first transferred to Alpheas Magic School, she felt humiliated competing with students from the provinces.

But after a year of the intense process in the graduating class, her life at the Royal Magic School was long forgotten.

'This is my alma mater now.'

Watching Sabina walk away, Pandora muttered softly.

"You too, make sure you pass. You've worked hard."

No one in the graduating class was lazy.

'She's not a bad person.'

Sabina smiled and walked away as the bell rang, signaling 5 AM.

Head teacher Collie arrived at the assembly point, leading three large carriages.

"We're taking the carriages to the Creas branch of the Magic Association. Mind your manners inside the carriages, and hurry up, we don't have much time."

The 30 students boarded the carriages, ten in each.

Shirone, Iruki, and Nade got into the first carriage, where Dorothy was already seated at the far end.

"Ugh."

Shirone and Nade glanced at Iruki and then quietly took their seats.

'Of all people, Dorothy…'

They hadn't heard the details, but it was clear that Iruki and Dorothy had dated and broken up in exactly 13 minutes.

The reason was that there was no strong attraction; the extremely realistic Iruki and the otherworldly Dorothy had quickly reached a mutual understanding.

"Are you okay?"

Surprisingly, Iruki spoke first.

"Yes. It was tough, but I'm better now."

"I'm sorry if I hurt you. But what's important to us is the graduation exam."

"Yes. We'll get through it. You too, don't get too hurt and focus on the exam."

Nade was bewildered.

"…Are you two doing this on purpose?"

The 13-minute relationship and breakup seemed absurd to him, but the two seemed sincere, looking in different directions.

Both Iruki and Dorothy were unusual.

Taylor Dorothy (Final Rank: 13th in the graduating class).

Major: Zoner’s Manipulation Series.

Special Notes: Uses the mental sharing system Mimesis. Unique manipulation pattern input method for maximizing omnipotence.

While the atmosphere in the first carriage was awkward, the third carriage was filled with murderous tension, ready to explode.

This was due to the standoff between Fermi, who was reading a book at one end, and the electric monster Lycan at the other.

After being betrayed in the upper part of Istas, Lycan, who killed the child Yolga tried to protect, was pursued by Fermi until the upper part disappeared.

Fermi's injuries were severe at the time, so there was no major confrontation, but even if they had clashed, Fermi would have died.

"Hey, Fermi."

Fermi turned his head.

"Do you know why I didn't kill you then?"

Fermi smirked.

"Because I can kill you in the graduation exam. You know, right? No one is held accountable for deaths that occur during the graduation exam."

They were on their way to the Magic Association to sign off on this.

"In other words, you're already a dead man. So why not close that book and start praying? You don't have much time left."

Gunjo Lycan (Final Rank: 3rd in the graduating class).

Major: Electric Magic, Lightning Series.

Special Notes: Son of Gunjo Bedium, the strongest hitman of the Black Line. Master of top-level movement techniques and Spark Magic.

Fermi closed his book.

"Lycan, do you love me?"

"W-what?"

Lycan's face twisted in anger.

"Hate and love have similarities. You throw all your emotions into them. So, I've grown a bit disgusted with hating you."

"……"

"So I thought about it. What's the best revenge on someone I hate? It's erasing them. Not pretending to be indifferent, but truly erasing them. Just as hate and love are similar, so are revenge and forgiveness. So I…"

Fermi reopened his book and looked away.

"Don't care what you do."

"You bastard…!"

Lycan stood up, but Hershey reached out to stop him.

"Hold on. That's psychological warfare too."

Is it really?

It was hard to believe, but it was Fermi, so there was confusion.

'Don't get swayed. If you get caught up in his words, it's over.'

Hershey thought.

'The Golden Wheel is dissolved, and there's no more collusion. Now we need to focus on Fermi.'

Hershey intended to become the next Fermi.

'From now on, I'll control the graduation exam.'

Meyer Hershey (Final Rank: 23rd in the graduating class).

Major: Sound Magic, Sonar Series.

Special Notes: Superhuman hearing, capable of detecting even the heartbeat of living beings.

Leaving their personal issues behind, the three carriages arrived at the Creas branch of the Magic Association.

As they opened the doors and got out, the sky was turning a bluish color.

"Welcome. This way, please."

An association staff member, who had been waiting, guided Collie.

They moved in two rows to the fifth basement level of the association, where Oscar was waiting in front of the iron door with the spatial transfer magic circle.

"Before using the spatial transfer magic circle, I need to go over some precautions."

Oscar, soon to be the son-in-law of the head of the association, couldn't be exempted from the early morning duties assigned to the lower ranks.

"First, the coordinates we'll be transferring to are a secure area 4 kilometers from the capital…"

Nade didn't hear Oscar's words.

They had briefly made eye contact when they first arrived, but it was best for both of them not to show any reaction.

'He must be troubled.'

Shirone looked at Nade with concern, but his expression didn't reveal much.

"Have a good trip."

When Oscar opened the iron door, a massive magic circle with a diameter of 100 meters was revealed.

It was clear that astronomical funds had been invested in this military facility.

"Impressive. It could transport an entire battalion at once."

If Dante, an expert in information magic, said so, it must be true.

The light of the magic circle grew brighter, and after about five minutes, it was so bright that they couldn't see the faces of the people next to them.

"Don't move."

With Collie's final warning, the light flashed explosively, and the entire graduating class disappeared.

Tormia Royal Castle, Krata.

The royal castle, home to hundreds of thousands of people, was a city in itself, and the armory located at the western outer gate was bustling with both official and unofficial weapon manufacturing.

Liz, the armory's magical parts manager, was checking the parts inventory as she walked around the warehouse.

It was a typical day.

But the partner who had promised her a future was no longer there.

"Manager. Manager."

A craftsman who had worked at the armory for 30 years called out to Liz.

"Yes? Oh, yes."

"What are you thinking about?"

"Oh, I'm sorry. I didn't realize…"

"No, I'm worried about you. Did something happen during your vacation?"

Liz decided to be honest.

"My fiancé stood me up."

Her wounds were starting to heal.

"That scoundrel…"

"He wasn't that bad of a person…"

"No, I mean his eyes must be bad. To let go of a beauty like you, Liz. He'll regret it later."

The craftsman's wit lifted Liz's spirits a bit.

"Go inside and rest. No deliveries are coming today. I'll take care of the warehouse."

"Oh, why?"

"The plaza is closed off, so no transport. The Magic Association seems to be holding an event. Some kind of inspection for the five prestigious schools."

The five prestigious schools.

That meant Alpheas Magic School would be included.

'Nade will be coming too.'

She hadn't felt good since she had said harsh words and left that day.

No, she actually wanted to see him.

'What am I thinking now… I must look like a strange woman.'

The craftsman, watching Liz fidget like a nervous puppy, knew what to say at that moment.

"Do what you want. I don't know the story, but…"

"Pardon?"

"At my age, I've learned a few things. It's much better to regret something you did than to regret something you didn't do."

Somehow, those words resonated with Liz.

Alrog Central Plaza.

The plaza was enormous, given that it was surrounded by the core institutions of Bashka, and the closure of all eight-lane roads leading to it caused significant complaints from citizens and merchants.

However, their grumbling didn't escalate because of their pride in the magical prowess of the Tormia Kingdom.

The six-meter-high platform in the center of the plaza was intricately carved from artificial ivory, so beautiful that it seemed a waste to use it for just a few hours.

Soldiers from the Royal Guard, a cooperating institution, controlled the citizens along the plaza's perimeter as carriages from all over the kingdom began to arrive.

As the drumline of the military band preparing for the inspection heightened the tension with a fast beat, the herald shouted.

"Oris Magic School!"

Three carriages stopped on Road 6 of the central plaza, and 30 graduating students in black uniforms stepped out.

"Kai Knight Magic School!"

"Bermin Magic School!"

As students gathered in front of the barricades, the carriages from Alpheas Magic School stopped on Road 3.

"Alpheas Magic School!"

"They're here. Let's get out."

The students, who had been watching the crowds on their way, looked a bit nervous.

"Wow!"

As soon as they set foot on the ground, the cheers of the people made their hearts swell.

'This is…'

This was the heart of Tormia Kingdom's magic.

The aspirations and reverent gazes of the people, which they couldn't feel at school, seemed to pierce their skin.

"Are they really all mages? They're so young."

"They're about to become mages. Only the best students in the kingdom were invited."

The students gathered here today would eventually become mages and lead Tormia Kingdom in all fields, including economics, society, military, politics, arts, and science.

'My heart… feels like it's going to burst.'

Mark shivered under the gaze of people looking at the future leaders.

For someone who had only been around those who dreamed of becoming mages since childhood, this was the first time he felt the gaze of society, and it was enough to draw out a passion he didn't know he had.

'Smart move, this association head.'

Collie nodded, feeling the atmosphere of the central plaza.

Internally, it was about imprinting the association's image on the elites who would become future employees, and externally, it was about emphasizing what the most important institution in Tormia Kingdom was. Lupist's plan was working perfectly.
