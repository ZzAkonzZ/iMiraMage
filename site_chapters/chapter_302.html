Chapter 302: Secrets of the Abyss (3)

Armin, who had been keeping watch outside, urged her.

"We don't have time. If you're going to do something, do it now."

Amy clenched her teeth and closed her eyes tightly.

There was no way. But she couldn't just go down to the 1st stage without doing anything.

'If it were Shirone... Shirone could do it.'

Amy opened the last page and moved the pen.

Shirone.

This was the best she could do.

If she couldn't find a way to save him, she could at least try to change the flow of consciousness.

At that moment, Reina shouted from the living room.

"Amy!"

Amy put down the pen and ran to the living room with Armin.

A figure clad in armor that mixed the properties of living beings and metal was charging at Shirone.

"Zion!"

Amy's eyes blazed with fury.

Arius, the only way to escape this place, was nowhere to be seen, but she was glad. Zion was the person she wanted to beat the most right now.

Reina pulled Shirone away just in time, and Zion's blade sliced through the sofa.

Amy, fueled by her rising anger, strengthened her fireball and threw it. The flame, twice as large as usual, flew at Zion and exploded right in front of him.

Zion was thrown against the wall by the explosion. But just before impact, his tendrils grabbed the wall, minimizing the damage.

As Reina moved Shirone away, Armin and Amy simultaneously blocked the path.

"It's a diversion tactic."

Armin's Spirit Zone, when fully expanded, covered the entire cabin.

He might not be able to detect an assassin's equalizing, but he wasn't clumsy enough to miss Zion's presence.

'He was hiding in the 1st stage. So Arius has already gone down?'

Just as Armin's group had to stay in the 2nd stage to save Shirone in reality, Arius needed time to extract Ataraxia. That's why he sent Zion to buy time.

"You're determined to take it, aren't you?"

As Armin stepped back towards Shirone, Reina understood and stepped forward.

Flicker magic and weaker spells couldn't impact the golden armor.

"Amy, you know what to do?"

"Yes."

They recalled the battle in the food storage and moved apart.

They planned to exploit Zion's lack of skill in handling blind spots.

"Hmph! I won't fall for it twice."

Zion sent tendrils to both sides, preemptively blocking the targets' dispersal. Then, using the tendrils anchored in the wall, he launched himself at Reina.

Reina shot an arrow in the split second she rolled on the floor.

As the arrow struck his armor, Zion's body lifted into the air.

Amy, using a targeted Spirit Zone, fired multiple Fire Strikes.

Each time the flame spear grazed him, the organic material of Armand's armor crisped and burned.

"Arrogant wench!"

Zion spun in midair like an acrobat and launched himself off the wall.

With eight tendrils embedded in the wall, he gained tremendous speed.

Amy twisted her body, only catching a glimpse of his afterimage.

Zion crashed to the floor with a thud, spinning on his knee as he swung his blade.

A silver flash passed, slicing Amy's collar and exposing her collarbone and upper chest.

Red blood slowly welled up on her milky white skin. A slightly deeper cut would have severed her aorta.

"You bastard..."

"Haha! You look just like a tavern wench!"

Blood from Amy's wound trickled down her cleavage.

The contrast of white and red fueled Zion's madness.

As he licked his lips and prepared to charge, Shirone glared at him.

"Raaaargh!"

Darkness gathered between Zion and Amy, and a sleek, agile shadow emerged.

Egoist - Killing Form.

Its jaw was sharp like a fang, and its hair, as sharp as blades, swept back in a large arc down to its waist.

Its long arms were like complete swords, and its reverse-jointed knees stored immense kinetic energy.

Even its feet were shaped like crescent moon blades.

"Shreeeeek!"

The piercing shriek was so sharp it made Zion recoil in fear.

The Killing Form compressed its reverse joints and sprang, disappearing without a trace.

It didn't charge head-on.

But Zion couldn't predict its path, so his eyes remained fixed.

Warning. Unable to recognize moving object. Nervous system paralyzed. Switching to automatic defense mode.

As the warning transmitted at the speed of thought, Zion's cloak wrapped around him. Simultaneously, tendrils extended in all directions, striking at the unseen presence.

But the Egoist's Killing Form evaded even that, crossing its arms and striking Zion.

As the shadow of the blade passed at supersonic speed, the cloak's surface rapidly hardened, leaving a clear X-shaped scar.

Boom! The sonic boom followed.

"Raaaargh!"

Zion, thrown by the shockwave, rolled on the floor as the cloak unfurled.

He stopped rolling by stabbing the floor with his gauntlet blade and looked up at the standing Egoist.

Moving object detected. Nervous system relaxed. Automatic defense mode deactivated.

Armand immediately assumed a combat stance, but Zion had already lost the will to fight.

There was no need to risk his life after spending 4 billion gold.

He had bought the time Arius needed.

Reading its user's emotions, Armand activated the mobility system, and Zion disappeared from Shirone's group's sight like the wind.

Amy and Reina gave up the chase and checked on Shirone.

In Armin's arms, Shirone was so transparent that objects could be seen through him.

"Shirone! What happened?"

Armin explained.

"He used the last of his strength to manifest the Egoist. He can't move anymore."

"Armin, run away."

"No. If we don't stop Arius, we can't leave either. Show us the way down. If Ataraxia is stolen, a great disaster will occur."

Armin laid Shirone on the floor. The avatar's outline glowed brightly, and the floor melted like chocolate, flowing downward.

"Is Shirone dying?"

"No. He's going down. The entrance to the 1st stage is Shirone himself."

Amy crawled to the floor, which was sinking in the shape of a person. She shouted at Shirone, who had already descended more than 5 meters.

"Shirone! I'll save you! I will definitely save you!"

The darkness of the underground engulfed Shirone's surface like liquid.

Maybe it was an illusion, but Amy clearly saw a faint smile on Shirone's lips.

And finally, the entire 2nd stage was sucked into the hole.

Shirone's Mental Stage 1. Primal Consciousness.

It was the end of the abyss.

Amy, who had fallen along with the scenery, looked around.

It was an empty, yet not dark, horizontal plane.

Somewhere, she heard the sound of a heartbeat, like being submerged in water.

"This sound...?"

Armin appeared from the darkness.

"It's the sound Shirone heard when he was in the womb. This place is called Primal Consciousness, containing records from conception to birth. It's where instincts are formed."

"So Ataraxia was in the final stage."

Armin smiled wryly at Reina's words.

They had embarked on this journey with only a vague possibility in mind. But to think the power of the archangel was imprinted in the realm of instincts.

'How did he do it? Could it be possession?'

Even so, it was too dangerous.

Possession grants stronger mental exploration abilities than diving, but the Egoist's resistance would be immense. Attempting possession on a normal Shirone would risk even an archangel's destruction.

'Well, I can't be certain. If it's an archangel...'

Armin thought the odds were 50-50.

Honestly, he couldn't definitively say that any being could enter another's mental world and overpower the avatar.

But since it was an archangel, there was room for variables.

A being that far surpassed human abilities.

Thinking about his experiences in heaven still made him shudder.

"Let's go. Arius's work must be quite advanced by now."

Reina looked around and asked.

"But... where do we go?"

"Just walk. This place isn't a space. Mages call it a spot. Since there's no direction, it doesn't matter where you go. To add, a mage’s Spirit Zone is also a phenomenon that occurs by concentrating the mind in the 1st stage."

Amy nodded at the new information.

The 1st stage was a storage that compressed higher concepts.

Since it wasn't a space, its volume was infinite.

Infinity might not be a vast something but just a single point.

Following Armin's words, they walked aimlessly. Since there was no change, it felt like walking in place.

When they saw an object 50 meters ahead, the sense of distance created a change in the 1st stage's scenery.

It was a blue garden with the sound of birds chirping and random white statues.

Amy, who had only seen strange machinery in the great world war in heaven, didn't notice, but Armin recognized the angelic sculptures at a glance.

'Is this Ikael's trace?'

Armin's group entered the temple beyond the fountain. The coolness of the marble floor felt refreshing.

They saw an 8-meter-tall angel statue, and in front of it, Arius and Zion were waiting.

Arius realized who the masked man was.

He had never seen the face, but among the scale magicians who dealt with time and were blind, there was only one person.

"Hmm, this is unexpected. I apologize for not recognizing someone of your skill. But it feels a bit strange. Honestly, I never imagined you would be the one to break the 'agreement' first."

Armin pretended not to hear and examined the statue.

The archangel had her hands clasped at her chest, smiling. Her wings wrapped around her body, and a halo floated above her head without any support.

'Is this the materialization of Ataraxia...?'

Turning to Arius, Armin spoke sternly.

"The agreement is still valid. I am here as a magician to deal with a grave robber, unrelated to any organization."

Arius nonchalantly cleaned his ear.

"Well, as far as I know, your world isn't run that freely. After all, don't you play a matriarchal role in the magical society?"

"So? Are you saying you won't give up the grave robbing? You won't be able to escape anyway."

Arius pretended to blow nonexistent earwax off his finger.

"Is there a need to escape? Once again, you have no authority to arrest me. No, never mind. This isn't the time to argue about that."

Pointing to the statue, Arius introduced it as if it were his own.

"What do you think? The Archangel of Amplification, Ikael. This is Ataraxia, known to be her most powerful ability. I imagined it, but who knew it would be this beautiful?"

"Do you think we'll let you take it so easily?"

Zion lifted his chin and spoke.

"Of course, I'll take it. Do you know how valuable this is? From now on, I am the owner of Ataraxia."

"You bastard! Someone like you doesn't deserve to live!"

Amy's eyes blazed as she charged.

At that moment, her vision distorted, and the distance seemed to stretch.

When she regained her senses, Armin was standing beside her.

It was Flicker magic.

"What's wrong? We need to catch Arius quickly."

Armin cast Wind Cutter.

The wind, shaped like a shuriken, shattered with a clang at the midpoint. Thin electricity spread out in a right-angle pattern from the point of impact.

"It's a mental wave barrier. Divers call it 'cat's cradle.' It's woven from strands of Shirone's consciousness to form a barrier."

Arius clapped his hands with a delighted expression.

"As expected, you're knowledgeable. The Eternal Seeker's reputation is well-deserved. Correct. While you were slowly following like turtles, I played around with Shirone's mind."
