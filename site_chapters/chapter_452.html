Chapter 452:

“They stole the Tagis?”

Mitgun, who was always confident in front of the leader, couldn't help but bow his head this time.

Yameng's elite troops had been defeated.

There were no signs of a struggle at the battle site he inspected. It was a one-sided beating.

More than half of the bodies showed signs of being killed instantly.

“Yes, that's what happened. We're currently searching for them.”

Bang!

Frankwine's punch crumpled the iron table inward.

“You don't know who did it? There must be an organization familiar with the secret routes among those who attacked Yameng!”

“But there's no organization in the homeland capable of taking down my subordinates so easily. You know that.”

Frankwine's punch struck Mitgun.

With a loud crack, his tall body slammed into the wall.

“But they were taken down! Who was it? If you don't know, you'd better make something up.”

Mitgun got up, wiping the blood from his lips.

If he didn't pull himself together quickly, he might die.

“If I had to guess, it would be them. The ones who have been catching Rank S prey every day.”

“Armin, or whatever his name is?”

If they had to name suspects, it was definitely them.

Each of them was highly skilled, and the ability to kill his subordinates was rare even in the homeland.

But why? Wasn't the Tagis being transported to the rebel command?

‘Hmm, I guess I need to check it out.’

Frankwine put on his coat and said,

“We're going to the First Command.”

Mitgun responded immediately.

“How many subordinates should we gather?”

“Subordinates? Do you think they can handle this? Just you and me.”

Mitgun's eyes changed.

It was a signal between the two who had crossed countless deadly situations to settle in Purgatory.

“Understood.”

Mitgun gritted his teeth and stretched his neck.

He didn't mind being hit by Frankwine for no reason, but he was itching to deal with the scum who had backstabbed them.

The atmosphere at the First Rebel Command was the tensest it had been since Babel's invasion.

Commander Crude, accompanied by Gaold's group, greeted Frankwine.

The rebel soldiers stood fully armed, but Frankwine walked in as if he owned the place.

“Hmm, do you live in a place like this? It's more rundown than I thought.”

It was understandable, given that half of the facilities had been destroyed by Babel's attack.

Crude skipped the details and got straight to the point.

“What happened? The Tagis hasn't arrived even though the scheduled time has passed.”

“Why are you asking me? We sent it.”

“Are you serious right now…?”

As Crude stepped forward with a glare, Sein placed a hand on his shoulder.

“Wait. I'll handle this.”

Sein looked Frankwine over with a cold gaze.

The leader of Yameng.

He had thought of him as just a merchant from the homeland, but the aura he exuded was extraordinary.

“You said you sent the Tagis a while ago?”

“Yes, we did.”

“But it hasn't arrived here?”

“Well, that's your problem, not mine.”

Sein couldn't understand why he was so confident.

If he intended to deceive them, he wouldn't risk his life to come here.

‘The goods were sent but didn't arrive? Hijacked?’

Sein realized Frankwine's implication.

“Are you suspecting us?”

“Suspecting?”

A violent glint flashed in Frankwine's eyes.

“Are you joking? Do you all want to die?”

“Ugh!”

As if the air had turned toxic, Crude's lungs ached, and he stepped back, holding his breath.

The rebels aimed their arcs but couldn't bring themselves to shoot. The vague thought that they would die the moment they pulled the trigger filled their minds.

“You'd better confess while I'm being nice. This is clearly your doing. Who was it?”

A thought crossed Sein's mind.

‘They've finally moved.’

Yameng's strength was said to be the best even in the homeland.

If they had lost subordinates and come all the way here, the hijackers must be highly skilled.

“Let's go somewhere quiet.”

In the command and control room, Sein sat Frankwine down and said,

“First, I'd like to hear what happened.”

Mitgun tilted his head.

If a massive amount of minerals and elixirs used to make the Tagis had disappeared, it would be natural to rage and try to pin the blame on someone.

But the man in front of him was more interested in the hijacking process than the goods.

When Frankwine crossed his arms and signaled, Mitgun began to explain.

Of course, he could only describe the state of the bodies after everything had ended.

But it was enough information for Sein.

About ten bodies were cut by something sharp. One body was completely exploded into mush.

And that one body was the leader.

‘Roche of Metal, Tarvan of Vibration. Cage B Team.’

Though not as synchronized as Cage A Team, Roche and Tarvan had been comrades since childhood and were the most powerful dealers in Cage B Team.

Frankwine noticed and quickly spoke.

“You seem to know something. If so, we have a grievance too. As for the goods…”

“The Tagis doesn't matter.”

Frankwine frowned at Gaold's words.

“Doesn't matter? What do you mean?”

“I mean we won't hold you responsible for it.”

Gaold took Sein's place and sat down, and Frankwine's gaze swept over him from head to toe.

‘This guy is on a different level of power.’

Everyone here was skilled, but Gaold had a different nature.

Battle-hardened? No, he had fought battles beyond that.

Just like himself.

“Not holding us responsible means…”

Gaold interrupted.

“Instead, we have a request. There's something you need to find for us. If you're the largest community in the homeland, you can do it.”

“Find what?”

Gaold spoke.

It was a short word, but it was enough to make Frankwine frown deeply.

“Ha ha ha.”

Frankwine's shoulders shook.

“Does this guy think I'm a pushover?”

Bang!

Frankwine kicked the table and swung his fist at Gaold.

Of course, Gaold didn't just take the hit.

Shirone's group was startled by the sudden fight, but Mitgun charged in with his fists raised as if he had been waiting for this.

Etella blocked him, and their rapid punches were exchanged.

As Etella's fist aimed for his side, Mitgun blocked it with his elbow.

Boom!

The vibration of the Yin-Yang Wave Fist spread through Mitgun's body like ripples.

‘A wave?’

Mitgun was pushed sideways and collided with the wall.

But Etella couldn't follow up. The same kind of shock was spreading through his own body.

‘What is this?’

“Annoying…”

As Gaold prepared to cast a spell with an irritated expression, Etella shouted.

“No! That person is…”

A powerful air press crushed the command and control room.

All the equipment on the ground was flattened, and Frankwine and Mitgun's backs bent under the pressure.

‘Mirror Cycle.’

The next moment, all the pressure in the space was directed at Gaold.

Boom!

The spot where he stood caved in, and Gaold felt his own power for the first time.

“Ha ha, I still have a long way to go.”

Gaold grinned, and silence fell over the command and control room.

Frankwine and Mitgun were shocked by Gaold's unscathed state, and the others were surprised to see Gaold affected even slightly.

Sein turned his gaze to Mitgun.

‘What a strange ability. It doesn't reflect the shock but seems to repel the event itself.’

The first thing that came to mind was an irregular type, but even his Iron Wheel Eye couldn't detect any special activation conditions.

The only guess was that they were from another world.

“Phew, this is…”

As Mitgun rubbed his fists and walked out, a crack appeared on his face.

Frankwine, watching Mitgun touch his cheek in disbelief, turned his gaze back to Gaold.

‘The Mirror Cycle broke? This guy is incredibly dangerous.’

In the original world, there weren't many who could match Mitgun.

As he realized this, his cumbersome anger subsided, and his merchant's mind began to work again.

“Fine, I'll find it. But we have conditions too.”

Gaold nodded readily.

He didn't have time to waste fighting Yameng.

“Speak.”

“Give us the facilities of the Second Rebel Command, Raysis's facilities. They're useless to you now anyway.”

“Fine. As long as you find it properly.”

Frankwine snorted and turned away.

Crack. Crack.

As Mitgun moved his facial muscles, more cracks appeared on his face, but they soon healed.

After the two left, Sein asked,

“Gaold, what are you thinking? This wasn't part of the original plan.”

“I heard the wind in Niflheim.”

Gaold said only that and turned away.

If Miro was captured in the Great World War, it might already be too late to return.

Gaold's clock was ticking faster than anyone else's.

That night.

As the group gathered, Gaold spoke.

“I'll be away for a while. Prepare to attack as soon as the Tagis support arrives.”

Plu asked,

“Where are you going? If it's a mission, take the team…”

Gaold shook his head. It was something only he could do.

“I'll be back by tomorrow. Keep the house safe, you mutts.”

As Gaold disappeared into the darkness, Shirone asked Sein,

“Is he going to find the Tagis?”

“Probably. If what Hel said is true, Miro's life is hanging by a thread. He knows there's no time to delay.”

“But how does he know where to find it?”

“Gaold doesn't need to find it. They'll come to him.”

Sein looked at Kangnan with a troubled expression.

“You said his pain threshold increased to a million times?”

“Yes, that's the level of power I witnessed.”

“That's dangerous.”

“I agree. If even a little…”

“No, that's not what I mean. When I say it's dangerous, I mean his mental state. Since coming to Heaven, Gaold's pain threshold has risen, and his power has increased significantly. And now he knows Miro's location. Even if he doesn't show it, he's probably not in his right mind.”

Kangnan suggested,

“Should we track him now?”

“Impossible. As annoying as he is, there's no way to follow him. We have to leave it to him for now.”

Shirone looked into the darkness where Gaold had disappeared, feeling a sense of foreboding.

“I've been curious for a while, what happened between the Chairman and Miro?”

It was an innocent question, but everyone perked up their ears.

Only Sein, Gaold, and Miro knew the story.

Sein looked up at the sky.

If Gaold was to return, it seemed the night would be long.

“This isn't the place to talk about it. Let's move somewhere else.”
