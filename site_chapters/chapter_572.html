Chapter 572:

While Shirone's team was gathering their members, the alliance team also convened at their hideout to discuss their next steps.

Like Shirone, the last to arrive was Kaiden.

"…What a mess."

Hershey noted that Kaiden's condition was seriously bad.

His once-clean face was covered in blood, and he was caked in dirt from head to toe.

"What's Shirone's current location?"

Hershey shook his head.

"The strategy has changed. You need to actively participate in the game now."

"I refuse. We agreed that I'd have a free role on the first day. My match with Shirone isn't settled yet."

"I know, but Eden's strategy was discovered too early. You know the stakes—if we lose, you'll have to pay the price too."

Kaiden gritted his teeth.

"We'll give Eden a free role sooner than expected. I'll also focus on locating Iruki. The enemy will try to destroy our cards. We need someone to collect Scrambles in the meantime."

Pisho raised his hand.

"I'll do it."

Insect magic was perfect for collecting scattered Scrambles.

"Good. The rest of you, secure Scrambles and support Eden. If we create the strongest hand by the third day, the game is over."

"Damn it. I could have killed him."

As Kaiden muttered, Hershey finally showed interest.

'Hmm, even using a sword, he couldn't overpower Shirone?'

While some at the magic school used Schema, Kaiden was on another level.

A genius who graduated top of his class from Kaizen Swordsmanship School at fourteen.

He was skilled enough to lead a battalion in war.

'Foolish. What is art compared to this…'

It seemed like fate that he gave up a promising life under the protection of the Cross family to join the magic school.

"So, how was it? Facing Shirone?"

Kaiden's grumbling stopped, and his expression turned serious.

The hatred remained, but he was honestly impressed.

"He's quite good. I understand why Fermi is wary of him."

"Oh?"

It was the first time he acknowledged someone in battle besides Fermi.

"But it felt a bit strange."

"Strange? How so?"

Everyone was equally tired from the late-night fight, but Hershey didn't overlook any detail.

"How should I put it? It felt like he avoided my attacks."

Hershey relaxed his eyelids.

"That's Shirone's movement…"

"I know, he's the best in school. But that's not it."

"I don't understand. Explain clearly."

"It's a feeling. There was no incongruity in the battle, but every time Shirone dodged my attacks, I kept thinking, 'How?'"

Kaiden, struggling to find the words, lifted his head.

"Yes, at some point, the tide turns. The battle progresses with a feeling of 'I can win this,' but at the decisive moment, it reverses. If Shirone were truly strong, I shouldn't have felt 'I can win this' from the start."

"Hmm…"

Hershey tilted his head and stroked his chin.

"That's vague. Was there anything unusual?"

Kaiden recalled the battle.

"There weren't many decisive moments, so I'm not sure, but it seemed like his body glowed."

Eden interjected, "Shirone's specialty is light. Any magic he casts will emit light."

"That's true, but…"

"Is it intuition?"

Hershey believed Kaiden's words.

If his precise intuition hadn't activated, he wouldn't have had these thoughts.

"We need to investigate further. Kaiden, keep covering Shirone. Can you do that?"

"I was planning to. Just tell me his location."

Hershey spoke sternly.

"I asked if you can do it. You seemed to struggle today. If you're outmatched…"

"It was just a reconnaissance."

Kaiden said indifferently.

"Shirone will die by my hand soon."

A smile spread across Hershey's face.

"Let's hope so."

Scramble Royal, Day 2.

Thanks to their efforts to block Eden's card collection the previous night, Shirone's team managed to keep her hand at ○○ (Saint).

The rule that Scrambles couldn't be transferred unless collected directly worked in their favor.

However, they had to bear the cost, as Pisho quickly collected the Scrambles Shirone's team gave up, gathering six cards.

Despite this, Iruki didn't abandon the original strategy.

"The core rule in the Black and White game is 'The winner of a Kang doesn't waste cards.' What does this mean?"

Amy replied, "The team that forms the strongest hand first has a significant advantage."

"Exactly. That's why we blocked Eden's collection despite the cost. The enemy has figured out our strategy, so things will get more complicated from today."

"That's why we're gathered here."

Shirone's team's starting point on the second day was in front of the central administrative building.

"There are 12 Scrambles. Let's collect the closest ones first. If we set the direction, the enemy can't calculate their routes."

"A blindfolded sword is harder to dodge."

Sabina said, "Leaving it to chance makes it harder to predict reactions. But what if Eden takes a long detour?"

"Then we'll give up one card and rethink. Blocking her with Saint yesterday was crucial."

Ruru's message about the Scramble summoning came through.

"Alright, let's go!"

Six flashes flew in different directions.

'Found it, Scramble.'

With Hickory on her shoulder, Dorothy entered Training Ground No. 4, not far from the graduation class.

As she spotted the Scramble wedged in a rock crevice and leaped towards it, she felt a chilling sensation.

'Danger.'

The moment Dorothy stopped, sharp ice flowers bloomed around the Scramble.

"Too bad. It's a miss."

Frings approached with a sly smile.

"It's not a miss. I found you, you loser."

Frings, who had been defeated by Amy's Kang the previous night, currently held two cards.

His Master Card showed ○●, a dead hand, openly aiming for a third-stage hand.

"Of course, I'm a loser."

Dorothy, who had defeated Anchal with ○●○● (Democracy) the previous night, added a Random Card, making her status ○●○●ⓡ.

Although she had a favorable condition, calling a Kang on Frings, who had a dead hand, was something to consider.

'Is it more important to destroy a dead hand than to reveal a fifth-stage Random Card?'

Frings interrupted her thoughts.

"Kang. Aren't you going to call it?"

"I will, if it gets dangerous."

Frings was the third-ranked senior and a combat mage.

'Could it be a dead hand?'

From ○●○● (Democracy), the only possible next-stage hand was ●○●○● (Revolution).

If Dorothy's Random Card was White, it would become a dead hand, giving Frings no reason to hesitate.

'I'm curious. I could call a Kang myself, but…'

Frings slyly stroked his mustache.

'If it's Revolution, I'll be defeated again.'

He didn't want to be treated as a fringe character anymore.

"If you don't call a Kang, can I start attacking?"

'Kang is the last resort.'

As Dorothy made a beeping sound with her mouth, Hickory descended from her shoulder and landed on the ground.

Thud!

The heavy thud was unexpected from a doll that came from her delicate body.

"Whoever calls a Kang, it works in my favor. And I can't give up the Scramble."

Hickory's toothy mouth emitted a dazzling light.

'Four-dimensional Hickory.'

Beep beep.

Even as Hickory performed a comical dance like moving a protractor, Frings' expression remained unchanged.

Having clashed with her for over a year in the graduation class, he was well aware of her manipulation abilities.

'Dorothy. An interesting subject.'

As icy mist rose around Frings, the world sparkled as if frozen.

'Deploy!'

As Hickory lunged with a steel fist, Frings soared gracefully.

Frozen Flower.

Spinning ice petals struck Hickory's body.

His specialty was freezing, and his field was ice formation.

Instead of extreme cold, he used ice to shape attacks, a typical combat mage technique.

'Iron versus ice. Iron has the advantage, but…'

Frings cast his magic with graceful movements.

'You know you picked the wrong opponent, right?'

With a series of cracking sounds, thick ice stalactites bit into Hickory from all directions.

'This ends here.'

Thin Ice Ridge.

As Frings swung his hand, a thin layer of ice spread horizontally, occupying the coordinates.

'Thin Ice Ridge can cut through steel.'

Frings targeted Hickory because it shared a mental link with Dorothy, a mimesis.

When Dorothy manipulated Hickory, Hickory manipulated Dorothy's brain in return, creating a bidirectional control system. Such constructs were called mimesis in the magical society, and the school considered Hickory as part of Dorothy's body.

'Anyway, I just need to deal with the tin can…'

As the Thin Ice Ridge approached, freezing the horizontal plane, Hickory accelerated Dorothy's brain.

The increased computational power was fed back to Hickory, allowing it to input commands with the extra time.

'Iron Screwdriver (←↙↓↘→ + Attack).'

Commands were the only way to control Hickory.

As Hickory spun, shattering the ice, it flew to the ceiling and manipulated Dorothy to throw her body against the wall.

"Ugh!"

The impact was severe since it wasn't voluntary, but it was better than being cut in half.

'Hickory. Attack tendency 85%.'

Even as Hickory switched to an offensive mode, swinging its spherical fists wildly, Frings remained calm and composed.

'She's updated its martial arts.'

Though the stance was comical, few could withstand punches powered by an 85-horsepower engine.

'Now!'

Dorothy's eyes gleamed as she input a command.

'Super Rocket Punch (→→ + Attack, Defense).'

As soon as she input the specific pattern, Hickory extended its right arm.

"Hmph!"

Frings leaned back to avoid the roaring punch, shattering the ice wall that enclosed them.

'That's a bit dangerous…'

Watching the direction of the Rocket Punch, Frings suddenly stepped back with a glint in his eye.

'Mammoth Stomp (↓↓↓ + Attack).'

As Hickory stomped down, the ground split around it.

'Time for the finishing move!'

Dorothy rotated her thoughts in a complex pattern.

'Fire Blast (↓↘→ ↓↘→ + Attack, Defense, Movement).'

Hickory's chest plate opened, revealing a flamethrower.

Faced with a 10-meter-long jet of flame that scorched the air, Frings retreated without hesitation.

'Control Mode (Attack + Movement)!'

The returning Rocket Punch began to move with precision.

'Do it like this (→→↑↘)… and like this (↑↑←)…'

Frings, realizing too late, protected himself with Ice Skin, but couldn't fully block the impact.

With a loud crash, Frings was sent flying, and Dorothy clenched her fist as she reattached the Rocket Punch.

'Victory Pose Macro 3 (↓↑↓↑ + Defense).'

Hickory placed its hands on its hips, danced, and then blew a kiss with its spherical fist towards Frings.
