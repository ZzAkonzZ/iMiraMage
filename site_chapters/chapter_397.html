Chapter 397: Rapidly Changing Political Situation (1)

The shouts of the medics were drowned out by the students' silence. The sight of Aider's battered body on the stretcher spoke louder than any words.

The fate of the first person to challenge Shirone was the epitome of misery. Fear was already taking root in the hearts of some students who had been weighing Shirone against Fermi.

With Aider out of the fight, Screamer's team was disqualified from the hill capture, but Shirone's expression was grim.

No matter how sweet the efficiency of evil, humans have a desire to like and do good.

At least, that's what we want to believe.

But Aider activated the AT algorithm at the last moment.

It wasn't as if a lightning bolt struck from the sky or a giant hand came down to snatch him away.

Are humans truly infinitely free beings? Or are they just part of a vast system beyond their perception?

"Shirone's team wins. 20 points awarded. This concludes the hill capture evaluation."

Walking over to his friends, Shirone placed a hand on Maya's trembling shoulder and apologized.

"I'm sorry. I'm sorry we dragged you into such a tough fight."

"I'm fine. I'm more worried about you."

Shirone was a decisive and cold person, enough to completely remove Aider from the graduating class.

But that was just the manifestation of his insight, taking the shortest path to his goal, not strength born from emotion.

'It must be hard for you, Shirone...'

In truth, he didn't want to fight anyone. All his troubles began from there.

What might be an easy choice for someone else was the most complex problem in the world for Shirone.

Aider received emergency treatment outside the battlefield.

When a medic confirmed his heartbeat and shouted, someone nearby ran off urgently.

Electric Monster Lycan, having seen enough, turned to Fermi.

"Aider succeeded. He really got under Shirone's skin."

Fermi shook his head.

If he were someone who acted purely on emotion, he would be easy to manipulate. Though their natures were extremely different, Shirone was similar to him.

"Actually, his emotions have only made him more composed. If he didn't crush Aider, a second or third Aider would appear. He calculated that it was best to nip it in the bud."

"Anyway, the strategy worked. We saw all of Shirone's techniques. Now we can gather spells that can counter him and strike back. Wasn't that the plan from the start?"

"Of course, but..."

Fermi rested his chin in thought.

In an evaluation worth 20 points, anyone would give their best. But it's natural to want to save something like the laser-guided algorithm.

In that sense, Aider met the terms of the deal precisely. Yet, what was this nagging feeling in his mind?

"I understand."

Lycan turned back to Fermi.

"He was hiding it? No, he never hid anything. Light Wings, Scatter Movement, Laser-Guided Algorithm. Even if it wasn't Aider, he would have used those cards if necessary. So why was I mistaken? Because Shirone's growth rate is far faster than anyone else's."

Before joining the graduating class, he couldn't use any of these techniques. Now he had three new ones.

Even if we knew everything about Shirone now, predicting how much he would evolve by the graduation exam was impossible.

"In just three months, he mastered two intermediate and one advanced spell. In terms of speed, it's almost like the power enhancement I achieve through my financial management."

Without looking back, Fermi spoke to his companions.

"Special bargain sale."

"Huh?"

"Tell all customers there's a 20% discount. And from now on, we only buy advanced spells. Buy as many as possible before the graduation exam."

Sona's Hersy approached with a worried expression.

"But Fermi, that will affect our profits this year..."

"What did I say?"

Hersy's face turned pale under Fermi's gaze. As perfect a manager as he was a deviant, Fermi was terrifying.

"If you don't eliminate minor variables, they become unmanageable when they hit."

As Hersy repeated the words, Fermi turned coldly.

"Do it."

"Got it."

As Fermi left, the remaining four followed, glancing at each other.

The area around the battlefield was still chaotic. Some students were desperately trying to calm Screamer.

They hated meddling in others' affairs, but leaving things as they were might result in Aider's death.

"Let go! I'll kill that bastard! Let go of me!"

"Screamer! Calm down! Aider is seriously injured! If you hit him, he'll die for real!"

There might not be any place left to hit on someone with multiple fractures, but with Screamer's temper, nothing was certain.

"I'm hitting him to kill him! That arrogant bastard dared to ruin my plan?"

Using an unagreed strategy had disqualified the team. Plus, Aider's collusion with Fermi had driven Screamer, sensitive to back-alley loyalty, over the edge.

"Ugh! This is so frustrating!"

Screamer kicked the ground, throwing dirt at Aider. A medic, unable to bear it, glared and pointed a finger at him.

"You! Stay at least 10 meters away from the patient! Or I'll report you to the teacher!"

"Damn it!"

Regaining his senses, Screamer shook off the students' hands and turned away. For now, there was nothing he could do, but once Aider recovered, he would have to be prepared to be half-dead.

Screamer walked to the water fountain, opened a bottle, and drank it all in one go. When Shirone's group approached, he pointed at them with the bottle.

"Don't get cocky just because you won. If it weren't for Aider, the outcome would have been different."

Nade scoffed.

"That's funny! We had the game in the bag anyway. And you promised, didn't you? You said you'd apologize to Maya."

Screamer ground his teeth. He had to apologize because he had said he would, but he couldn't bring himself to do it.

There was some injustice. How could they win with a notorious troublemaker like Aider?

"Tch! Apologize, my foot. Here, have some water."

Screamer handed over the bottle as if to end the matter. But the reaction was unexpected.

Maya, still traumatized, flinched and trembled, making Screamer sigh.

'Ugh, scared after a few hits. How can someone like that become a magician...'

Maya had no talent for magic. No, beyond talent, her mindset wasn't that of a magician.

People's personalities vary, and in reality, what matters is who gets their way.

Her swollen face, rabbit-like frightened eyes, and the way she held her hands like someone being punished were all irritating.

"Maya, you..."

Just don't become a magician.

Screamer swallowed the words. It was her life and her choice.

Seeing her as a person rather than a magician, he felt a bit of sympathy for her, looking dazed after being bitten by a mad dog.

"Alright, I'm sorry."

Screamer frowned and handed Maya the bottle.

"I'm sorry for calling it the sound of a pig being slaughtered. You sing well, so keep it up. Don't worry about Aider. I'll send him back to the hospital as soon as he returns."

"Screamer..."

To Maya, Screamer was a difficult person. Always angry, often in the same group, making her heart race just by meeting his eyes. Hearing his apology, all her pent-up sorrow burst out in tears.

"Ugh, I can't stand this. I apologized, okay? I'm leaving."

Screamer shook his head and left the training ground with another bottle.

Two weeks after the hill capture evaluation, on a holiday.

Shirone, Nade, and Iruki headed to the advanced class with flowers. They had heard that Siena had been promoted to a certified level 5 magician after receiving an award for her thesis from the royal city.

Having a certified level 5 magician as a regular teacher at a magic school under the Kingdom of Tormia, except for the Royal Magic School, was a great honor for Alpheas Magic School.

"But is it okay for us to go? Graduating students rarely leave the Iron Gate. They say it creates a sense of disparity for the juniors."

"We should congratulate her. She took care of us a lot during the presentation."

As they entered the advanced class building, the eyes of the students in the hallway widened.

It was rare to see graduating seniors, let alone three at once.

"Wow! It's Senior Shirone!"

"Senior Iruki and Senior Nade are here too!"

Students poured out of the classrooms, quickly filling the hallway.

Seeing the bright-eyed juniors, Shirone recalled his astonishment at Samuel's ultrasound demonstration.

'Well, I was like that too.'

Now, competing daily with people of Samuel's caliber, it was unimaginable.

So, the juniors he had taken classes with just a few months ago looked much younger.

"Hello, seniors! I'm Oern, the top prospect of Class Four!"

A young boy stepped out of the crowd and bowed deeply.

Shirone tilted his head, not recognizing the face from last year's integrated classes.

"Oern?"

"Yes. I enrolled this semester. You wouldn't know me, senior."

"Oh, a transfer student."

Oern shook his head with a smirk.

"No. I started magic late too. Similar to you, Senior Shirone."

Shirone then took a closer look at Oern. Being in Class Four right after entering the magic school meant he had made a significant impression in the entrance test.

'Hmm?'

Sensing a sudden dangerous aura, Shirone instinctively entered the Spirit Zone. Oern's Spirit Zone pierced his chest like a sharp spear.

'Hmm, I see.'

A promising student in the advanced class would surely have heard stories about Shirone.

Moreover, his followers, Mark and Maria, were well-established here.

Aider's departure from the graduating class must have also influenced this. Depending on the graduation schedule, there was a possibility that one student from Class Four could move up, so it was natural for Oern to be highly motivated.

'How about this? You have to acknowledge me now, right?'

Oern knew how impressive the skills of the graduating class were. However, he didn't think his talent was inferior to Shirone's, who was currently the most famous at the magic school.

Shirone had started in Class Seven despite entering at eighteen. In contrast, Oern, two years younger, had entered directly into Class Four.

Shirone could only smile.

This was why graduating students rarely left the Iron Gate. If they had to deal with juniors who didn't understand the world, they wouldn't be able to get anything done.

'He certainly has talent. But...'

Suddenly, Shirone widened his eyes and glared at Oern.

His Spirit Zone transformed into an offensive mode, with about 200 spikes extending towards Oern.

"Ah!"

Startled, Oern fell to the ground. He looked up at Shirone with a frightened expression.

He had thought he wouldn't be outmatched in the Spirit Zone, but the offensive mode just now was on a different level.

'This can't be. Mark definitely said he was defensive...'

Shirone crouched down to meet his eyes.

"You. You're specialized in offense, aren't you?"

"Huh? Oh, yes."

He had thought so. Even from their brief encounter, Oern's energetic personality was evident, similar to Amy's.

"Sharpening your mind isn't everything. In offense, speed and agility are crucial. If you maintain the same mental state like you do, you'll quickly become predictable."

"I-I see."

Oern looked up at Shirone in a daze. The gap was much wider than he had vaguely imagined.

'So this is the graduating class?'

As Shirone took a step forward, the students blocking the hallway parted to the sides like a river.

"He's really bold. Reminds me of when I first met Iruki."

At Nade's words, Shirone grimaced, recalling his first encounter with Iruki.

"No, compared to Iruki, he's really humble."

"Confidence is proportional to skill. In that sense, it's natural for that kid to be more humble than me."

"That endless confidence of yours..."

As they continued their conversation, dozens of juniors watched the three of them walk away down the hallway.
