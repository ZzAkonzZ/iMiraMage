Chapter 953: The Age of History (4)

'Park Ji-kyung.'

After concluding a deal with Karatorsa, Goffin entered the underworld through the Choeni Bardo.

The world of demons is as vast as the universe of reality, but its geography is completely different.

"It's going to be a rough journey."

To return to the planet where Miro is, the route Goffin chose was to cross 72 hells.

It was the only way to meet him.

"Behemoth the Demon God."

Unlike human evil, the evil of the fallen angels flowed into the underworld and was born.

It's a mixture of all sorts of emotions, distinct from Mara, which is born from clear concepts.

"He is not subservient."

Even without the horns that are a symbol of Mara, he wandered for a long time and eventually settled on Miro's planet.

Of course, by the standards of the underworld.

"It's been a while. King of Beasts, Behemoth."

Before Goffin stood Behemoth, massive as a mountain, with seven eyes symmetrically embedded.

"Goffin, you've come to see me?"

"I crossed 72 hells. You wouldn't invite me no matter how much I called. Were you avoiding me on purpose?"

"… Hmm."

With a cheeky turn of his head, Behemoth pointed with his claw, longer than a spear, to the side.

"You look terrible. Sit. How about we drink some good liquor I've got?"

"I came to ask a favor." Goffin sat down on a chair made of bones and crossed his legs while Behemoth opened a bottle of liquor from reality.

"I know. I've already retired, but my power still counts here in the underworld."

Behemoth had lived too long and had retired by blocking the flow of evil entering him.

Now, he just killed time doing menial tasks like a lower demon in the human world.

Nevertheless, the demons still recognized him as an elder second only to Lucifer.

"Try this. It's pretty good." Behemoth tipped the bottle with the tip of his claw, and Goffin did not hesitate to lift his glass.

"I had a child."

Behemoth paused for a moment, then filled his glass and poured himself a drink.

"When? As far as I know…"

"Yes, he died. But he didn't die. Ikael and I are still connected."

Behemoth downed the drink, which was hardly a drop compared to his size, in one gulp.

"That thing, Agape or something? The ability to create life with a signal of the heart?"

It was a word detested by demons, but the retired Behemoth didn't hesitate.

"Yes. The child is already dead, but the body isn't important in the power of Agape. However, there's one problem."

"What problem?"

"The child isn't human." Behemoth thought as he poured more liquor. According to his understanding, combining Hexa and Agape could manifest a human body.

"A problem of the mind?"

Goffin nodded.

"Yes. Considering Ikael and me, we've reached a high state of being based on this world."

"Ha, pretending to be noble."

Behemoth snorted, but didn't delve any deeper.

"You've observed many human lives in the underworld, so you know. Humans embody traits of both good and evil. They laugh, they suffer, they're happy. All these experiences blend to form their own perspective, their own Idea. But my child to come…"

Behemoth muttered in a serious tone.

"There's no evil."

"Exactly. I'm a Gaian apart of an integrated mental system, and the child's mother is Ikael, the first concept of this world. There's no room for evil. So, I'm asking…"

Goffin got to the point.

"Will you become the evil for my child?"

Behemoth turned his gaze from the glass to the landscape of the underworld, lost in thought.

"But you know."

He pointed back at Goffin.

"Isn't that too perfectionist? It's okay to have one human without evil. That's fitting for a child of you and Ikael. There's no need to stick to a standard model."

"No, it's the opposite. Perfect isn't Agape; it's not me. Gaian's integrated mental system is complete because of the many failures humanity has committed. Without it, Ultima can't be realized."

"Standards would be lost."

"Exactly. True goodness isn't just being kind; it's the will to suppress inner evil. True love isn't just giving foolishly, but the commitment to give everything while knowing the cost. If either good or evil is lacking, humans can't reach Idea."

"He who doesn't understand evil, can't comprehend good?"

"That's right. Only within the cycle of good and evil can we approach humanity. And at the center of that humanity is Idea. Philosophy is just a means; ultimately, there's neither good, evil, sacrifice, nor love."

Goffin shrugged and smiled.

"Look, even now. I've realized everything in this world, and I'm drinking with you, the greatest villain."

Behemoth licked his lips, turned away.

"I do not repent."

"That's why I'm asking you. And… it's pretty childish to think like that now, isn't it?"

"Hmph."

Behemoth swept up the remaining liquor bottles next to him, crunching them in his mouth.

"So you're telling me to die. After sending away Lucifer, now you try to bury me too?"

"It's time. You know?"

Behemoth closed his mouth.

Since he had blocked his own evil, he had no attachment to living in this world.

"Are you sure? About your child. If for some reason I'm dragged out, I might end up killing that brat."

"If possible…"

Goffin grinned.

"Make him as strong as possible."

"Ha ha ha ha."

Behemoth chuckled as he poured liquor into both his and Goffin's glasses.

"Call me when it's time. The first and last time, I'll come out to meet you." The glasses clinked.

Returning to heaven, Goffin went to the place where he and Ikael had dreamed their honeymoon.

It was a devastating scene.

The memories of that time were painfully sweet, perhaps because they were so happy.

"So I chose this place."

Holding his newborn child, it was where Ikael had smiled the happiest smile in the world.

On Goffin's palm, a hexagon of Hexa rose and then dissolved into a light smoke.

"Chances are fifty-fifty."

It wasn't a technical problem.

Agape, a kind of Hexa, was a miraculous ability that combined hearts to create life.

If Ikael didn't love Goffin, the two signals would never merge.

"It's okay if she doesn't remember me."

They were connected by the heart.

"So, Ikael."

As Goffin spread his arms, Ikael's tears contained within the Miracle Stream soared into the heavens.

"I love you."

The greatest love in the universe, Agape.

The Miracle Stream exploded in the form of a cross, connecting the ends of the universe.

Shirone savored the miracle that occurred in an instant through Goffin's perspective endlessly.

"Ah??????"

The light literally filled the universe, condensing at a speed imperceptible to living beings.

Looking up at the white light in the sky, Goffin's feelings were transmitted intact.

"Ikael."

That Agape was successful was clear evidence that she still loved him.

"Thank you."

He had given his all, and she had received his all.

"Our child."

Tears welled up in Goffin's eyes at the sight of the special shadow forming in the white light.

"This is Hexa."

Omega's transmission ended.

While Shirone savored the process of his own birth, Karatorsa's voice was heard.

"All the information you've received so far has been about every event that occurred in the first universe. And now…"

A new Omega surged in.

"What's this?"

It was a log starting over as many times as the universe had been reset.

"Amazing."

It's vast information by human standards, but compared to the whole universe, the changed events were less than 0.1 percent.

"Even in a deleted state, Goffin almost perfectly preserves the original history."

After the reset, Anke Ra spared no means to eliminate his own errors.

The battles of the angels following his will and the apostles guarding time were conveyed through the 11 senses.

"Hoo."

With a sigh from the Immortal Dragon, all records of Omega finally passed to Shirone.

Karatorsa, seemingly lacking the strength to raise his head, lowered his posture and rested his chin on the ground.

Shirone waited.

After a long while, Karatorsa finally raised his head again and spoke.

"During the transfer of the Akashic Records log, I couldn't do anything. Not just because I literally couldn't, but because if it fell into the hands of another being, the balance of the universe would have been drastically skewed."

Shirone understood.

How heavy the responsibility was.

And the sense of liberation Karatorsa must feel at this moment.

"Whatever being enforces this world next, the age of gods is coming to an end. It will either close or continue. That's what Goffin defined as Omega 999 years."

It was the end of an era.

"And that's all I can pass on. History is past events." Shirone, or Hexa, did not appear in Omega.

"If you come to know the logs of your life after your birth, it will no longer be your life. You won't be able to exist as you are now."

Shirone achieved Ultima amidst countless doubts.

"But knowing the log would collapse the basis for judgment. That is, it would break the realm."

If Shirone knew every event and feeling of the countless people and friends he had met.

"I wouldn't be able to see the world from my current perspective."

That was enough.

The library of knowledge set up in the depths had transformed into a perfect 5-dimensional cube.

"Everything in the universe is contained in it."

Having absorbed the action of particles in its entirety, the mathematical formulas and definitions spoken of in academia were unnecessary.

Shirone spread his palm in front of him.

"When I think of something."

The 5-dimensional cube rotated, finding what was needed in its infinite volume.

"The time it takes…"

None.

Within the 5-dimensional cube, time is a manipulable physical quantity.

"Combining that omniscience with Hexa."

A hexagonal light rose from Shirone's palm and shattered with a clear sound.

"If I throw my whole heart into it."

The light smoke swirled and turned into a flower not found on the planet, starting to grow on his palm.

As Karatorsa stared intently, Shirone slowly raised his hand.

The flower thrown into the air dispersed into thousands of lights, pouring down like a fountain.

Bright, colorful flowers bloomed on the solid floor, and the cave quickly filled with the scent of flowers.

Shirone smiled and said,

"Miracles happen."

The brain of God.

The principle behind the activation of Miracle Stream.
