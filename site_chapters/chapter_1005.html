Chapter 1005: The Age of Struggle (1)

"Kieee!"

The hallucination that only Seina could hear was now audible to everyone.

The black droplets forcibly expelled from Seina's mind coalesced into a sinister form, swirling in the air.

"W-what is that?"

It was enormous, almost covering the sky, and Seina's subordinates had never seen anything like it.

"Evil, is it?"

The reason they felt such intense fear was that the object in the sky was the emotions of humans, living souls.

Something everyone harbors deep within.

'I'm not afraid.'

Only Seina looked at the form of evil with extreme calm.

'It seems unrelated to me. I don't even doubt that it could harm me.'

Without it in her heart, there was no fear.

Of course, doubt would sprout again as she lived, but for now, it was a miraculous phenomenon for her.

The emotions of prejudice swirled like a vortex and eventually scattered into the concept of chaos.

'Yahweh of the era.'

Seina looked at Shirone.

Seeing eyes devoid of hatred shook her heart, but she quickly shook her head.

'I can't acknowledge it!'

Acknowledging Shirone was akin to denying the existence of the one god, Cria.

'I was just weak! All humans are beneath God. Humans cannot become gods.'

She snapped.

"Don't think you've won with this. Our doctrine is more devout and sacred than you think."

Shirone, not wanting to get entangled with the Lami Church more than necessary, thought this was enough.

"Order your subordinates to stop their self-sacrifice. We must prevent meaningless deaths."

"It's not self-sacrifice; it's martyrdom. And it's inevitable. It's not coercive, but we've learned from birth not to succumb to evil."

"Then..."

As Shirone moved towards the paladins, Prejudice's 5 o'clock raised her voice.

"Kill Yahweh! He will kill your families and take your loved ones. Yahweh is the absolute evil that must not exist in this world."

Doubt rose again in Seina's heart, but the aura of baptism still lingered.

However, the other paladins couldn't contain their anger and almost simultaneously raised their heads.

"God! I offer my body to you!"

"There's no need to die."

Shirone, who had reached the center of the formation, spoke.

"What she says is true. So you can attack me if you want."

"Shirone! What are you doing?"

Nade shouted, but there was no response.

'This is the only way.'

When Shirone expelled the evil from Seina's heart, he sensed it with his eleventh sense.

Evil comparable to Satan.

'Even Eden felt fear from this emotion. At least Seina's level of faith is needed.'

"Yaaah!"

As prejudice was confirmed as fact, the paladins simultaneously drew their swords and charged.

"Fools! Stop immediately..."

Seina, running towards her subordinates, widened her eyes in a daze.

Every time the divine-powered blades swung, Shirone's upper body created multiple afterimages.

"Evil demon! Die!"

The paladins, trained in battles against demons, were not inferior even by world standards.

But no matter how fast they swung their swords, Shirone's neck was never there.

Iruki muttered, "Quantum superposition. That's dangerous."

Because it was a matter of probability.

While it had the advantage of dodging any attack, it could also be hit by a child's swing if unlucky.

Nade said, "So... that's why he's not moving?"

"No, he doesn't need to move. If rolling the dice determines everything, actions are meaningless."

He could dodge attacks with very high probability or be hit with very low probability.

"Anyway, let's deal with Prejudice's 5 o'clock while Shirone buys us time."

At that moment, Seina approached.

"Wait! If we act rashly and fall for the evil's temptation again..."

"We won't."

Nade, glaring ahead, said.

"No matter what she says to slander us, I won't attack Shirone."

"How can you be sure?"

Having experienced the hidden code firsthand, Seina knew how terrifying the mind's influence could be.

"I've already tried to kill Shirone once."

"Shirone said if I didn't struggle, I'd sink. For the first time, I thought I might live my life for myself."

It was the first time he had spoken of it.

"And it really happened. I achieved my dream and met someone I love. So..."

Blue electricity surged from Nade's body.

"No matter what Shirone does to me, I won't resent him."

Kraaaang!

The electricity gathered in the sky became Thunder God's Rebirth and rushed towards Prejudice's 5 o'clock.

She spread her arms.

"Heh, as much as you want."

Thunder God's Rebirth attacked fiercely, but the current's movement bypassed her.

'Hidden code.'

He sensed it but couldn't think of a way to counter it.

"I'll have to step in."

As Iruki walked towards Prejudice's 5 o'clock, Seina followed and asked.

"What are you going to do? That's the evil's deception."

She might not know Siok, but she seemed to fully understand the nature of evil.

"That's why I'm interested. Logic that defies the logic of reality. If so..."

As Iruki's eyes lit up, atomic bombs trailed across the sky.

"I'll learn."

Boom! Boom! Boom!

The chain explosions passed by everywhere except where Prejudice's 5 o'clock stood.

'This is ridiculous.'

Iruki's smiling lips trembled.

'Something impossible by my reasoning is happening. Like a dream...'

As he focused, electricity sparked from his eyes.

'Overdrive.'

Its performance was on par with Ex Machina.

The nerves in his brain sparked as he analyzed the logic of the hidden code.

'It's not logic trapped in one dimension.'

Multidimensional equations.

This was the transcendent logical system that constituted human dreams.

"Ugh!"

It was as difficult as collapsing all existing mathematics and creating new mathematics.

'No, it's different from that.'

There is no immutable truth in multidimensional space.

'Just a bit more...'

Iruki explored the vast maze with infinite doors at the speed of light.

'Open this door here...'

As the red door opened, sparks flew from a specific part of his cerebral network.

'Turn here and open this door.'

As his brain felt like it was burning, Iruki returned to the beginning for the first time.

'Ah.'

Resentment was just a luxury.

'Let's try again.'

After thousands of rotations, a vague image of chaos emerged.

'Having a door means you can return. That's logic. But multidimensional equations...'

Once a door is opened, the returning door disappears.

'It's chaos.'

By now, even Seina had joined the fight, but Iruki couldn't see her.

"Heh heh. Heh heh."

Blood streamed from Iruki's eyes, nose, and mouth, but he laughed.

"This is fun."

Meanwhile, Shirone grew increasingly anxious as time passed.

'This is getting dangerous.' He had dodged attacks using quantum superposition over 40,000 times.

'Is Iruki not done yet?'

Even without exchanging words, they knew their roles.

'Breaking the hidden code is impossible.'

But if anyone could turn a moment of chaos into logic, it was Iruki.

'Just one moment.'

"Huff!"

At that moment, the paladin's eyes activated their divine sight, and soon all their eyes lit up as if infected.

"Get out! Get out of me!"

Sloth's 4 o'clock had begun to act in earnest.

"No! I don't want this! Oh, God. I'm sorry. I..."

Their arms slowly rose, and sharp blades aimed at their throats.

One paladin stood out, glaring at Iruki with a sinister look, his sword lowered.

'He succumbed to temptation.'

His divine sight extinguished, he bared his fangs and charged past Shirone.

"Iruki!"

"Grarrr! Grarrr!"

As the paladin growled, Sloth's 4 o'clock's hallucination whispered in his ear.

-No one can harm you. Help me, and I'll make Seina choose you.

Even a paladin knows love.

"Se-Seina."

But demons amplify small human desires into immense cravings.

The paladin raised his sword towards Iruki.

"I am the strongest!"

Shirone cast Miracle Stream, but it faltered against the hidden code.

"Iruki! It's dangerous!"

Iruki, lifting his head, revealed blood-soaked eyes as he stood.

'It's done.'

Chaos has no logical pattern.

But if a specific moment is fixed like a photograph, its form is mathematical.

'One-time password.'

And if it's mathematical, Iruki could say this:

"Cancellation."

As all information reversed, Sloth's 4 o'clock's hidden code was destroyed.

"Aaah!"

Caught off guard, Prejudice's 5 o'clock—Rabica's left arm—was severed by Seina's sword.

'What? Why did it get cut?'

Sloth's 4 o'clock's hallucination whispered.

-The hidden code was hacked. It's temporary, but there's a superhuman hacker.

'What nonsense...'

Nade's Thunder God's Rebirth surged forward.

"Damn it!"

As Rabica's facial bones twisted again, Shirone cast Stop, feeling like he had caught a big fish.

Time of the law disappeared, and in 0.666 seconds, two Sioks appeared.

As their faces came into view,

"You... you bastards..."

Furious, Shirone, Iruki, and Nade simultaneously attacked.

"Dieee!"

Thunder God's Rebirth turned into lightning and struck, followed by an explosion from the strongest detonation equation.

Boom!

As the earth-shattering roar cleared Seina and the paladins' minds, they looked to the sky.

"Damn it! We missed them!"

Prejudice's 5 o'clock was flying away, carrying Sloth's 4 o'clock.

Nade said, "No, there's one more shot."

The paladins turned their heads at the sound of air bursting behind them.

A giant fist of light was coming over the horizon.

"Yaaah!"

As Shirone swung his arm, Hand of God rushed forward at the speed of light.

At that moment, Prejudice's 5 o'clock threw Sloth's 4 o'clock into the light and dodged.

"You..."

The old man's face turned red.

"You dare... Ugh!"

Struck by Hand of God, the old man's eyes popped out like fireworks.

"Hmph, useless things should die."

Laughing at the falling Sloth's 4 o'clock, Prejudice's 5 o'clock disappeared into the air.

"Huff, huff."

Shirone, watching the scene, stomped his foot in frustration.

"We missed one."

Iruki approached.

"It can't be helped. At least we got some satisfaction. It's fortunate it ended like this."

Seeing Iruki's blood-stained face, Shirone steadied himself.

"Sorry. I knew it was dangerous, but there was no other choice."

"What are you talking about?"

Iruki wiped the blood and smiled.

"Now I feel alive. I'm glad I followed you."

At that moment, a commotion arose among the paladins.

"Let go! I'll die! Let go of me!"

The man who had succumbed to evil and tried to attack Iruki was being restrained by his comrades.

"Calm down! It could happen to anyone!"

"No! I, I... Aaah!"

Shirone's group watched as the man broke down in tears.

"He was really strong. He must have trained his whole life."

"But his heart couldn't keep up. Not that we can blame him."

How many people in the world have no gaps for evil to enter?

'If there are, they must be the ultimate good.'

Shirone understood why Miro emphasized the depth of the mind over physical strength.
