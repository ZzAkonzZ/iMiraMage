Chapter 292: All Sorts of Variables (6)

"Since it's come to this, I won't stop you. But I'd still advise you to wait. I can't guarantee your lives if you go in. Leave Shirone to me."

The masked figure quickly threw these words over his shoulder and dove into the Door.

Amy examined Shirone's condition.

A thin web-like wire, tightening around his neck, was clearly visible. Moreover, his gaze hadn't moved an inch from where he had been bound.

But it hadn't completely stopped, so if left like this, he would inevitably die.

Amy and Reina exchanged glances, locking eyes. Without hesitation, they jumped through the Door.

Five seconds later, the Door disappeared.

Zenogre and Shirone were slowly thawing, frozen at the crossroads between life and death. A very slow "Ig-" sound leaked from between Shirone's lips.

As though he tried to shout ‘Ignite’.

Great Secret (1)

<Separate> Shirone's consciousness. 11th level surface consciousness.

Kwareureureung!

Thunder crashed. Rain was pouring down in torrents. The dark clouds blanketed the entire sky, making it impossible to tell if it was day or night.

Amy looked around the streets in the pouring rain. The city was definitely new to her, but somehow it felt familiar.

Buildings lined the cobbled streets, and whale oil torches flickered outside the shops, weakened by the harsh winds.

The usually visible beggars had all taken shelter, and only men in ready-made clothes were hurrying along, clutching their collars.

In the distance, a carriage covered with a tarp was heading for the city gate, trying to leave the city.

It was probably daytime.

Amy and Reina shivered from the cold as they looked at each other. Their clothes clung to their bodies from the rain, and their hair looked like drowned rats.

"Where on earth is this…?"

"So you did come in."

Instinctively, the two women covered themselves and turned around. Although they were clothed, the wet clothes clinging to their bodies made them feel exposed so they couldn't help but feel wary.

From the darkness of the alley, the masked figure approached.

"I was waiting, thinking you might come."

Amy asked.

"Who are you? And what are you to Shirone?"

Instead of answering, the masked figure removed his mask. They had wondered how he could see with his face covered, but thin bandages were covering his eyes. He had been blind from the start.

"I am Generade Armin. I have a bit of a connection with Shirone."

Amy tilted her head, puzzled. A connection with Shirone?

Of course, Shirone would have connections unknown to others. But if he had known someone of this caliber, there's no way he wouldn't have mentioned it.

"How can we believe that? Shirone never said anything about..."

Armin ignored her, glanced around, and gestured at the two women.

"Let's get out of the rain first. Follow me."

Having stood in the downpour for just a few minutes, their body temperatures had dropped rapidly, leaving them little choice but to follow.

And besides, even between women, they didn't want to be seen in such an embarrassing state.

The place Armin took them to was an establishment that served as both restaurant and inn.

Before stepping inside, Amy glanced at the sign. It looked somewhat similar to Thormian writing, yet subtly different, making it impossible to decipher its meaning.

“Welcome.”

As they entered, a woman in her forties knitting behind the counter greeted them with a formal tone.

From the outside, the building had seemed too narrow, but once inside, it stretched deep into the back.

The main hall spanned roughly seven pyeong (about 23 square meters), with three tables set up for drinking. In one corner, a group of hunters was warming themselves with hot drinks.

As they walked past the hall and turned a corner, a long hallway extended further inside with a hearth set into the floor. There must have been drainage installed, as rainwater hadn't reached this far.

Square tables were arranged around, where people in travel attire were eating.

Armin first gestured for the women to sit in the empty seats, then took a seat across from them.

As they settled, a musician wearing a pointed hat near the hearth began tuning a four-string instrument.

Inns frequently visited by travelers typically contract musicians to play when a certain number of people gather.

Even when that number wasn’t reached, patrons could pay for a private performance.

The musician played his instrument and sang in a sorrowful tone.

It was a song about a knight who, having lost his beloved, sets out in search of a place to die. Though the melody was gentle, the lyrics were hauntingly sad.

Reina was taken aback to realize the musician was highly skilled. However, the variations deviated from basic harmonics. It felt like several songs mixed into one.

Yet, the other travelers seemed unfazed. In fact, they appeared to pay no attention at all—to anything. Not even to the two women whose wet clothes clung to their figures.

Amy found this odd. Of course, it’s not that she expected to attract attention from everyone, but the situation was undeniably strange.

Upon closer observation, she realized the patrons weren’t actually eating. Their forks were meaninglessly going back and forth between food and mouth.

The reason they seemed to be eating was that they muttered continuously in a melancholy tone.

Unreadable sign. Strange song with odd melodies. People not eating. And gloomy expressions.

Amy suddenly felt goosebumps. She finally realized that this place was unlike any world she knew.

"Just where is this?"

Armin, as if he had been waiting for her to reach this question, nodded.

"This is Shirone's consciousness. Before you arrived, I took a look around. Here seems to be the 11th of the 12 stages of the human consciousness, the surface consciousness.”

She had heard Arin mention in Galliant Island that the human consciousness was divided into twelve stages.

But that wasn’t the concern right now. The problem was that they were now inside that consciousness.

Reina must have felt the same way, as she asked back.

“So, this place right now is Shirone’s consciousness?”

Armin nodded as if that alone could convey all the explanation needed.

To explain such a strange world, it was more effective to make them believe rather than try to persuade them.

Amy saw through Armin's intention and suppressed her curiosity.

For now, it was best to believe him. Wherever they were, finding a way to save Shirone came first.

"We need to chase Arius. Besides, Shirone's neck will be cut in the real world soon."

Armin smiled and shook his head.

"There's quite a difference between real-time and consciousness time. You must have followed me right away, correct? Yet, I’ve been here for over thirty minutes already."

Amy did some intuitive calculations.

Though there was a slight time difference, they had entered almost simultaneously with Armin. If that thirty-minute gap had happened so quickly, time here was clearly moving much faster than in the real world.

'No, it's more accurate to say that consciousness operates at an accelerated speed.'

Armin continued.

"Shirone should be safe for the time being. Ideally, I would slow time down even more, but even Temporal Field has its limits…."

Currently, Armin was maintaining Time Stop Field over Keira, who was 14 kilometers away at the Kazra Royal Palace, which left him with little extra time in reserve.

If it were Slow Magic that ordinary mages use, he could have slowed time much more extremely, but that wouldn't have been a wise choice in the situation.

Because Slow was active magic after all.

If he filled the only omniscience slot that most mages possess with Slow, he would surrender the initiative to Arius, another mage of his caliber. Moreover, his opponent wasn't easy enough to compete within a divided temporal resource state.

Thus, he had opted for the passive technique of Temporal Field.

The Temporal Field, which simultaneously occupies time and space, continues to exert its influence even after activation, thus freeing up an omniscience slot to flexibly respond to the opponent’s movements.

The reason Arius could not act rashly in the standoff was because he had perceived this fact.

Thanks to this, Shirone narrowly avoided instant death, but Armin’s own temporal resources were down to its tail. Creating another field was always possible, but if its effect weakened, it would be pointless.

‘The only move left is the Flicker. I guess I have to try with this.'

Armin had waited for the two women because the state of consciousness they had entered—the 11th level of conscious awareness—was dire.

He had no intention of putting Shirone’s friends in danger, but if they had come here of their own accord, he would have to accept their help.

"Of course, we have no time to spare. We need to find Shirone as quickly as possible. But coincidentally, I'm in a state where I've almost lost my combat ability. That’s why I need your assistance. First, I'll tell you the information you absolutely need to know."

Reina took a listening posture and said,

“Yes, I want to help. So please tell us. Why is Zion going so far as to hire assassins and bribe even Arius to kill Shirone? Especially since Arius is affiliated with Orkamph?”

"To explain that, we should first talk about Arius. His alias is the Grave Robber, quite infamous among the Seven Sins of magic in the Black Line."

Amy frowned at the mention of Black Line.

Though there were criminals in the Red Line too, they were people who raised their ranks through infamy and achievements.

Arcane, for instance, was one of them.

The Black Line, on the other hand, was far from honorable. Those on the Black Line pursued only their own pleasures, indifferent to the destruction they caused to society in the process.

"You're saying someone like that has been by Orkamph's side all this time?"

“His skills are undeniable. He’s an Unlocker and a Scale Mage, mainly known for infiltrating minds and stealing thoughts. He installed a Door in Shirone's surface consciousness. He probably dove at least once without Shirone knowing."

Amy tapped the table. A dull, clicking sound echoed.

“So, this entire inn is a product of Shirone’s consciousness?”

“Yes. It appears as a place, but in reality, it’s a landscape created by Shirone’s mental activity.”

Reina asked.

"What's the principle behind this? How can someone’s thoughts materialize into such a concrete world?”

"All mental processes acquired as humans grow up become objectified. Love, anger, indifference—they all become an objects. You could think of it as language, but since this is a realm tied to the unconscious, ‘objectification’ is a more accurate term.”

Armin looked around the inn's scenery and continued.

"Then why does the arrangement of objects resemble reality despite being a mental world? It’s because all objects in this 11th level pass through the 6th level, or the realm of REM, before emerging here. If the unconscious were projected here directly, it would be a completely chaotic world."

Amy stroked her chin and said,

“Hmm. So, you’re saying that a person’s mind is filtered through this REM level, which converts it into objects that rise up here?”

"Exactly. Since REM is a mind state during sleep, it's easier to understand if you think of it as dreams. Dreams are the boundary between consciousness and unconsciousness. That's why two characteristics appear simultaneously in dreams. Going up from the 6th level, consciousness strengthens, going down, unconsciousness strengthens."

Arius pointed to the ceiling.

"This is the Eleventh Level surface consciousness, the highest level excluding the shell level. As such, it’s a highly objectified world—realistic, intricate, and expansive.”

The musician's sorrowful melody played softly.

The tune’s jumbled notes no longer felt eerie now that Amy thought of it as passing through a dream filter.

Reina asked.

“You mentioned Arius is a ‘Grave Robber.’ So what exactly is he trying to steal from Shirone’s mind?”

Amy answered.

"Ataraxia."
