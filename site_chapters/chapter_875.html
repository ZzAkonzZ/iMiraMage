Chapter 875: Obituary (1)

Rian thought he could never forget Tess's voice, but it was a bit hazy.

"Huh?"

Looking up at the high ground, he saw Tess sitting on a black horse, looking teary-eyed.

"You jerk..."

Here you are, alive and well.

"Haha, Tess? It's been a while." He hadn't expected much from the start, but treating him like a chance encounter was too much.

"You crazy fool! Is that what you say when you see me? You haven't contacted me at all..."

Amy's voice silenced Tess.

"Shirone."

Her trembling voice was filled with a joy close to fear.

"How are you here...?"

It didn't seem real, but even if it was a dream, she hoped it would never end.

"Amy."

As Shirone slowly walked towards her, Rian stepped aside.

The soldiers whispered among themselves, sensing the subtle tension between the two.

"I heard the demons call him Yahweh. Could it be that the Major knows him?"

Among those seasoned in war, there was no one who didn't know the Infinite Mage.

"This seems serious. Could it be that the two of them..."

"Shh."

Shirone stopped in front of Amy.

'Is it really Amy?'

Even seeing her eyes, which turned red when she was excited, it didn't feel real.

It didn't matter.

"Shirone..."

Before Amy could finish speaking, Shirone hugged her tightly and buried his face in her shoulder.

"I missed you."

Amy's scent.

He didn't call it a fragrance because it felt like Amy might disappear at any moment.

'Amy. It's really Amy.'

This primal, unique scent was the only proof that she was real.

What should he say?

The sacrifice of Seongeum, the death of Guy, the rampage of Yahweh—so many things had happened.

But now, hugging Amy, Shirone could only say one thing.

"Don't disappear."

Tears welled up in Amy's eyes.

"Okay."

Words weren't important if they felt the same, and Amy hugged Shirone tightly.

"Is that man her lover?"

The soldiers' eyes widened, and some approached Tess.

"Is it true, Sergeant? Is the Major's lover really one of the Five Great Stars of the Ivory Tower?"

"The Five Great Stars of the Ivory Tower?"

Reading her subordinates' minds, Tess chuckled.

"No, just a schoolmate." That's what connections are.

And another schoolmate, Maya, was watching Shirone and Amy from a distance.

'There's no place for me here.'

It wasn't just a matter of distance; she could tell from their eyes.

"Maya, let's go back."

Palmers, who was behind her, grabbed her shoulder and turned her around.

"...Okay."

Tess also felt bad watching Maya walk back to the quarters.

'It's unfortunate. The situation was bad. Of all places, the three of them had to meet here.'

It wasn't the time to worry about others.

Seeing Shirone and Amy together made Tess angry, and she hit Rian's arm.

"You idiot! What have you been doing?"

"What do you think? Fighting a war."

It was a statement that left no room for argument.

"I've killed so many demons. I kept cutting and cutting until I got here."

Rian touched Tess's head and asked.

"How was it? Did it help?" Tess, pulling Rian's arm over her shoulder, smiled as she looked ahead.

"Good job."

It was a joyful reunion for almost everyone.

But it was wartime, and they couldn't stay emotional forever.

After hearing how Shirone had come here, Amy gave orders to her lieutenant.

"Send a messenger immediately and prepare to withdraw. We'll move as soon as we get orders from the top."

"Yes, Major."

With the annihilation of the 30,000-strong demon detachment, there was no reason for Amy's army to stay here.

'What a perfect victory.'

It was an achievement worthy of a medal, but that wouldn't happen.

'Shirone and Rian.'

In front of the Infinite Mage and the Knight of Maha, today's victory felt insignificant.

'They're not forces that should be in a mere battalion.'

Even Amy, who had experienced many wars, realized how valuable Shirone's power was.

"Shirone, you said you're going to Tormia?"

The death of Rian's eldest brother, Guy, was also a sad event for Amy and Tess.

"Yes. I'm going to the Ogent family to ask for forgiveness. I think that's the right order."

It was typical of Shirone's conscience.

"Alright. Our unit will be moving too. Is it okay if we accompany you on the way?"

"Of course. I'm not leaving immediately either."

Reporting about Shirone to the Valkyrie meant that the Chief Commander would also know.

'If Iruki needs me, there's no reason to create a variable by leaving the unit early and not being able to respond to an urgent situation.'

The duty officer approached.

"Major, the barracks are almost ready. What about the performance? They asked..."

They had to ask because of the contract.

"Oh, right. The performance."

Amy, realizing it late, said.

"Shirone, Maya is here too. She's here to perform for our unit."

Shirone's face lit up.

"Really? Maya?"

Amy asked the officer.

"What did the company representative say?"

"They said the singer seems to be in shock from the attack. If it violates the contract, they'll have to do it, but..."

'It's not because of the attack.'

Shirone had heard from Tess that Maya was there when they reunited.

"Forget it. We also have some fault, so let's end it here. Give the soldiers some beer."

Alcohol was prohibited during the performance, but the nearby demons had been annihilated.

"Shirone, are you going to see Maya?"

"Well... is it okay?"

Shirone wasn't a fool.

"What are you talking about? She's a schoolmate, not a stranger. Go ahead. I'll check the worksite and follow."

"Alright, then."

As Shirone left the barracks, Amy leaned on the desk and held her head.

"Ugh, this is driving me crazy." She was so stressed she could die.

'Why today of all days?'

Why did the heavens send Shirone and then put him in a situation with Maya?

Anyway, she had work to do.

When she arrived at the entrance of the unit, they were busy clearing demon corpses and repairing the fence.

"Amy."

Tess, who was managing the site, ran over.

"How did it go? Did Shirone say anything? Did you kiss? No, you're already in a hot relationship..."

Amy looked at her in disbelief.

"What nonsense are you talking about? This is the army. It's not a place where you can mess around just because your lover is here."

Tess also noticed the subordinates' eyes.

"Who said to do it openly? Everyone's probably thinking the same thing. Not doing it is foolish."

"Hmm."

Amy was human too.

The stress of the battlefield was unimaginable, and her frustration was at its peak.

The only reason she had been able to endure was that she had no experience with men.

"Major, we're changing the fence to a C-shape. It will save time."

Amy looked at the soldier with a blank expression.

He was habitually putting the pen cap on and off while looking at the blueprint.

Amy swallowed hard.

"Push! Push!"

At the entrance, soldiers with hammers were driving thick stakes into the ground.

"Tighten it! More, more!"

On the other side, soldiers were sweating as they inserted long bolts into nuts.

"...!"

Suddenly, she felt hot.

'Ugh, I need to get a grip.'

Before she could delve deeper, Amy shook her head to clear her thoughts.

"Are you sick? Your cheeks are really red."

"It's nothing. Anyway, I'll go see Maya. You finish up and rest."

As Amy turned to leave, Tess asked.

"You're worried, aren't you?" Amy stopped walking.

"Shirone loves you. So you don't need to think too much about Maya."

"I don't know."

She was a rival, but also a friend and comrade.

"It's not a simple matter. It feels like I'm committing a sin. In a way, it feels like violence."

Is it really the best to show love to Shirone in front of Maya?

"You missed him too. Besides, we're soldiers. We never know what will happen or when."

For soldiers, there is no tomorrow.

"We won't die. We'll definitely win."

Amy smiled as she looked back.

"Don't worry about me. Focus on Rian. You two are really hard to understand."

Tess, with nothing to say, puffed her cheeks and looked up.

"You have to look at the sky to reach the stars."

Past midnight.

In an empty field, Amy and Maya looked up at the night sky, lost in thought.

"I'm sorry, Amy."

Maya knew her position well.

"For what?"

"For making you worry. It must have bothered you to see me laughing and talking with Shirone."

Amy chuckled.

"You're really the same. So what? It's good for friends to have fun together."

No matter what she felt inside.

"But you know how I feel about Shirone. Even now."

"I heard from Tess."

If she were in Maya's position.

"I don't feel good either. Honestly, I don't know what to do in times like this."

"Don't think like that. I have no intention of being a hindrance. If you care about me, just do what you want."

"Do what I want..."

Amy turned her head.

"I won't do anything that will bother you."

"Uh?"

Maya blinked.

"It's not out of pity or guilt. We're friends. I just want everyone here to get along."

She didn't want to make it about being a man or a woman.

"Amy..."

How could she be just like she was in school?

"What? Why are you making that face? Don't misunderstand. I'm not giving you a chance..."

Maya wiped her tears.

"Thank you. Thank you, Amy."

She was happy to have such a wonderful friend, but it also hurt her heart.

'This way, I can't even try to take him away.'

The next morning.

A messenger from the Holy War landed in Amy's unit with the speed of a storm.

"Urgent orders!"

Amy greeted the messenger with a tense expression, surprised by the quick response.

"What's the matter?"

Tess and Rian arrived, and Shirone, who was helping pack, approached with Maya.

The messenger took out a letter.

"Orders from Chief Commander Iruki of the Valkyrie. Shirone, Amy, and Maya are to return to the Kingdom of Tormia immediately and proceed to the city of Creas."

Maya pointed to herself.

"Me too?"

If it was related to the war, Tess might not be involved, but Rian couldn't be left out.

Shirone fell into thought.

'Me, Amy, and Maya?'

The only commonality among the three was that they were all from Alpheas Magic School.

"Here is the Chief Commander's letter."

As Amy opened the letter, Shirone and Maya stood behind her, reading over her shoulder.

"What...?"

It contained the obituary of Pisho.
