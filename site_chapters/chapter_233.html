Chapter 233: Battle Simulation (3)

Dante could no longer bear the insults.

"Is this for real? You think I'm a joke? You expect me to believe that?"

“What Shirone said is true.”

Iruki came over and wrapped his arm around Shirone's shoulder.

"I understand why you're upset, but Shirone really doesn't know who you are. That's because he's been in this magic academy for less than a year. This is only his second semester."

Dante stuck out his lips with a dumbfounded expression. Given that the semester had just begun, it meant that Shirone had been learning magic for only about half a year. And yet, it was difficult to understand that his Spirit Zone was as big as his own.

'No, no. These guys must be conspiring to corner me.'

Feigning nonchalance, Dante scoffed.

"Hmph, so you're telling me that everyone here has been bowing down to a guy who's only been learning magic for half a year? Are all the kids at this academy trash?”

The students were furious, but they could not bring themselves to question Dante because what he said was true.

The only person qualified to respond was Shirone himself.

"Why do you have to put it that way? We don't learn magic to defeat others."

“Haha! You speak well. Well, I haven't seen your skills for myself yet, so I can't say for sure."

Regardless of why magic was originally created, modern society operates on a system of endless competition. In such a situation, to focus on the essence of things was either the idealism of the weak or the hypocrisy of the strong.

Dante, who was convinced that it was the latter, stood on tiptoe to look down at Shirone.

"I'll expose all your lies. Just you wait."

@

After the day's classes ended, the teachers of the advanced class gathered in the meeting room.

Olivia was carefully writing down the problems she had found while inspecting the academy throughout the day. Then, she placed a fresh sheet of paper on the table and began the meeting.

“Since we have already toured together, I don’t think there is any need for a lengthy explanation. If things continue like this, the academy’s ranking will drop even further. I think special measures are needed.”

The teachers had already anticipated that Olivia would drop a bombshell. But the question was, what kind of bombshell?

"I've considered various methods, but it's clear there aren't any suitable alternatives within the advanced class. However, there is one approach we can take: adopting the graduation class's system."

The teachers' eyes widened. Before they could fully process what she was suggesting, Olivia made her declaration.

"I propose that we open the Combat Simulation Training Grounds, Heaven's Trials (이천번異天煩), to the advanced class."

Thad stood up as he slammed the table.

“Principal! Heaven's Trials is too dangerous. Even the graduating class is using the training grounds under the teacher’s control.”

"What exactly is so dangerous? There's no need to coddle the students. Even if Heaven's Trials is close to actual combat, we can lower the synchronization rate."

Seeing that Olivia was serious, Thad tried to reason with her calmly.

“Principal’s approach is also right in some ways. It is true that we have overprotected the children in the advanced classes. However, I dare say that our graduation class is by no means lacking in quality. They are competing at a sufficiently high level of intensity, and I believe it's sufficient to limit such training to them."

"How can the upper tier be intense if the middle tier isn't? The advanced class is the stepping stone to the graduation class, but where do you get the graduating class of this academy? With that mindset, you won't achieve any innovation. You need to rethink your approach."

Ethella spoke up.

"But then what about the graduation class? Heaven's Trials is open to them every day."

"I'm aware there are two Heaven's Trials training grounds. Including the Colosseum, which is used for graduation exams, there are three locations. If we manage the schedule well, we should be able to utilize one of them in the advanced class.”

The teachers fell silent. The graduation class conducts real combat training through Heaven's Trials. While it's not impossible to apply this to the advanced class, the intangible value of the pride held by the graduation class must be considered.

Above all, it would go against the educational philosophy that Alpheas has upheld for decades, working tirelessly for the school.

As the silence dragged on, the atmosphere became unclear—was this meant to be a meeting or a confrontation? Olivia, who had been patiently waiting for opinions, finally nodded.

"Alright, I see what's going on. Is it that you can't listen to me because I'm only here temporarily? Is Alpheas still the headmaster in your hearts?"

"N- No, that's not it at all...!"

"It's okay to be honest. It's something any human might feel. But don't be mistaken. As the current chairwoman of Teacher Association and former principal of Royal Magic Academy, I didn’t come to the academy just to patch Alpheas’s gap. Alpheas himself is well aware of this, which is why he came to me personally to ask for my help."

Thad was sweating profusely. As Olivia had said, she was the current principal. And the teachers had to acknowledge that fact.

But what cannot be done, cannot be done. She would be gone in six months. Alpheas' philosophy was the academy's history and tradition, and it needed to be maintained.

"No one here denies your reputation, Miss Olivia. I, too, respect you deeply. But such a sudden request is something we..."

"Do as Olivia says."

The teachers turned around in unison. Alpheas, who had entered through the back door, was smiling. With the mental support of the teachers now present, Olivia showed signs of irritation.

"What is this? Are you here to supervise me? I don't believe I need Alpheas' permission to exercise the authority granted to me."

"Not at all. I was just out for a walk and thought I heard some high-spirited discussion. Hohoho!"

"Then kindly stay out of it, Former Principal."

"Before I go, I would like to say a few words to my former student. Thad, remember that Olivia is the current headmaster. You'll have to be well aware of that."

"Understood."

When Alpheas himself stepped forward and drove the wedge in, the other teachers found themselves without further arguments. There had been doubts about whether they had truly handed over full authority to a temporary headmaster for six months, but now there was no room for doubt.

“Haha! Well then, this old man will take his leave."

Olivia scoffed but refrained from further showing her displeasure. It was something that needed to be firmly established at least once.

When Olivia turned to look at Thad, he bowed his head, mentally prepared, and said.

“I will announce this to the advanced class.”

@

A notice was posted on the advanced class bulletin board, informing them that future integrated education would be conducted at Training Ground 27.

The lower class students were confused, but those in the upper classes, who knew what that place was, couldn't help but feel tense.

Nade and Iruki also sensed that the situation was not normal.

"Isn't that where Heaven’s Trial is? Is this really okay?"

"You didn't expect it to end smoothly anyway, did you? What's the difference? It's not like we'll die."

As Iruki said, they wouldn't die, but it was indeed dangerous.

This battle simulation wasn't like the childish war games they used to play, making sound effects with their mouths.

"Still, it's nerve-wracking. Are they really going through with it?"

"Who knows? Given the new principal's tendencies, it's possible. She did say she was a proponent of real-world experience. Anyway, let's go tell Shirone."

Nade looked around.

"By the way, where's Shirone? He hasn't even eaten dinner?"

"He said he was skipping it. We’re supposed to meet at the research group tonight. He mentioned he had something to do at the training ground."

"At this hour?"

"He said it wasn't something he could do when it's quiet. He figured now was the best time to practice."

“No way… Ataraxia?”

Iruki smiled knowingly.

"It's the perfect time to sneak a peek. Let's go."

@

Shirone was standing alone in the empty training grounds. The reason he had come here, even skipping dinner, was to train with Ataraxia.

The one-minute preparation time felt unbearably long, even to him. The magic circle, not limited by space, could be used as a siege weapon, but he wasn't planning on jumping into a war anytime soon. Triggering mass destruction required the utmost caution.

'I'll get used to it after practicing hundreds or thousands of times. But realistically, I might only be able to do it once a day most.'

The lack of repetitive training was another hurdle in training with Ataraxia. However, Shirone did not give up. Hadn't he succeeded when he set his mind to reading history books in Ogent Family's library? Every endeavor gains momentum, and as long as he kept moving forward, he'd eventually find himself running.

"Alright! Let's do this."

Shirone cast Halo. A one-meter-wide circle appeared before him, drawing in countless concepts.

The multi-colored colors rushing in from the front were a surreal spectacle to behold at any time. After passing a certain stage, a multi-dimensional structure beyond the realm of understanding emerged, and Ataraxia was born.

Shirone decided on a magic while aiming at the target in front. Since it was Photon Output rather than Photon Cannon, there was no physical force, so he could use magic to his heart's content.

"Hey, Shirone...!"

The moment Nade and Iruki entered the training grounds, a massive beam of light shot out from the Ataraxia magic circle.

The light was so intense that if viewed from above, the entire training ground would have been illuminated, and even students far away noticed the mountain beyond glowing and turned their heads in amazement.

Nade and Iruki’s eyes widened in shock. The sheer amount of light output was enough to send shivers down their spines.

It was beyond the scale of what humans could handle. It felt like witnessing a phenomenon that should only occur in nature, right before their eyes. What could possibly remain in the wake of that light?

Surprisingly, not even a speck of dust was disturbed. Iruki realized that the magic Shirone had cast was Photon Output. Cold sweat ran down his back. If it had been Photon Cannon, the cliff that blocked the training ground would have been obliterated.

‘As expected, Shirone is incredible. It has to be at this level for my finishing move to shine.’

Nade awkwardly moved his legs, as if he had forgotten how to walk.

“Hey, Shirone. This….”

Before Nade could finish speaking, Shirone collapsed with a thud.

Normally, he would have retained some level of consciousness, but since this was a once-a-day training session, he had exhausted all his strength.

“Shirone, Shirone! Come to your senses.”

Nade lifted Shirone and gently slapped his face. He couldn’t help but imagine the worst, as there have been cases where mages died from overexerting their magic.

Contrary to his worries, Shirone quickly opened his eyes.

“Huh? When did you get here?”

“Are you okay? We’ve been here since you started using Ataraxia or whatever it’s called.”

“Hahaha! You saw that? I was trying to keep it under wraps.”

Shirone pushed himself up from the ground with his hands. He wobbled a bit but wasn’t so unsteady that he couldn’t stand.

Iruki approached without taking his eyes off the target.

“The magic you used in Heaven was Ataraxia combined with Photon Cannon, right? I wonder what level of destructive power that would have. After seeing this, I can’t exactly ask you to try it out.”

“That’s right. I doubt I’d ever use such a powerful force in reality. But I still want to keep refining it. The problem is that it takes too long to prepare.”

“That might not be entirely true. There could be a chance to use it in real life soon.”

“Huh? What are you talking about? A chance?”

Iruki glanced at Nade. Initially, they had come to complain to Shirone, but now that things had turned out this way, their interest was piqued.

“Starting tomorrow, we’ll be training with Heaven’s Trial. Principal said so.”

“Heaven’s Trial? What is that?”

Iruki said, pointing to the sky.

“It’s an artificial trial.”

Nade added.

“It’s a battle simulation system exclusively for archmages.”

@

Ten minutes before the start of the integrated class for the advanced students, the tension was higher than usual.

The 27th training ground was an open area where the mountain was cut flat, and the floor was densely finished with small metal blocks.

There were four steel towers erected along the outer perimeter, which appeared to be seven meters high.

The teachers overseeing the training were Sienna, Thad, and Ethella. The students were pleased, knowing they would be receiving guidance from all the popular teachers.
