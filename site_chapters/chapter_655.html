Chapter 655:

‘What’s with this guy now?’

Rian, already overwhelmed from facing two powerful figures in the stench-filled underground sewer, was baffled.

Meanwhile, Lupist fixed his gaze on the man standing still like a corpse, gripping a cheap kitchen knife in one hand.

‘Shagal the Rapid Blade.’

The killing intent that would have suffocated a normal person only tasted like an addictive snack to him—spicy and thrilling.

Sniff sniff. Sniff sniff.

Confirming Shagal’s habit of tracking by scent, Lupist was certain of his identity. Only then did he turn to Rian.

“What’s your name?”

“Rian. Ogent Rian.”

The Knight of Maha.

If his name had reached the ears of the Association’s director, then his ability was a given. But to still be standing after fighting Shagal—that was unexpected.

“And you? Who are you supposed to be?”

“Me? I’m the head of the Magic Association.”

Lupist said it casually, then studied the dozens of wounds carved into Rian’s body and nodded.

“You’ve got some impressive regeneration. A healing build?”

“Not exactly...”

Rian would rather die than admit he couldn’t use Skima.

“Well, lucky you. Surviving against one of the world’s top 100 most dangerous people.”

“The top 100…?”

He’d heard the term a few times during his knight training, but this was his first time seeing one in person.

Still, what echoed in his ears more than “top 100” was that first phrase—lucky.

“You’re saying I survived by luck?”

Lupist didn’t answer, walking instead toward Shagal.

“So you’ve changed your weapon? A clever way to erase your identity, I’ll give you that…”

For those who had refined their skills to a certain level, even murder became a form of art. Just from looking at the body, one could identify the killer.

Some even called it a murderer’s fingerprint—and Shagal had just wiped his clean.

“You changed your weapon?”

“Shagal the Rapid Blade. Known for wielding dozens of daggers simultaneously with uncanny speed. I think the Skima technique was called… 'Folding Ground'?”

If “Folding” was a Skima technique that increased efficiency by doubling movement through mental diagrams, “Folding Ground” enhanced specific body parts by focusing energy into one area.

“Hmph. So what if he changed weapons…”

“And Shagal was a master of stabbing, not slashing.”

Rian now remembered: the wounds carved across his body weren’t slashes—they were punctures.

“The demi-human corpses in the sewer… that was your doing, wasn’t it? Crude work. No wonder no one noticed.”

Shirone had even assumed they were torture victims.

“Are you… here to arrest me?”

It was the first time Shagal spoke.

Lupist did have the authority to arrest him, and as a magician affiliated with the Association, it was technically his duty.

But Lupist hadn’t entered through the front door just to apprehend someone. He had a more pressing mission.

“So you’re the 'Gatekeeper of the Sewers' hired recently. That was unexpected. But I’m not here for you. Take me to Brooks.”

At that moment, Shirone came running, splashing through the water.

“Rian! Are you okay?”

“As you can see.”

Seeing Rian unharmed, Shirone marched toward Shagal, his eyes blazing.

“Hey! Just what do you think you’re—”

But then he froze, noticing the man standing beside Rian.

“…Director Lupist?”

“Well, well. If it isn’t Tormia’s little traitor.”

Of course he knew Shirone had left Red Line after being selected as an Ivory Tower candidate.

Still, it wasn’t mockery in his tone—more like a backhanded joke. After all, it was the kingdom that had thrown mud first.

From behind Shirone, a sharp voice rang out.

“To think the Director of the Magic Association would come down to a place this filthy… Heh. That sends chills.”

Next to Aria, who held a torch, Brooks’ smiling face emerged like a ghost from the darkness.

Sitting alone at the sewer gate, water dripping all around, Shagal stroked a cheap kitchen knife and sank deep into thought.

‘Ra Enemy…’

He had never once killed without enjoying it.

Unlike most murders driven by necessity, he killed purely for pleasure.

Eventually, that pleasure became so intense, he couldn’t go even a day without tasting it.

Just as it isn’t a sin for a beast to hunt for food, so too—Shagal believed—it wasn’t a sin for a murderer to kill.

Though all nations outlawed murder, the only judgment Shagal feared wasn’t man’s laws—it was divine punishment.

And Ra Enemy… was the only one who had ever made him fear murder.

It was like suddenly going mute. Like losing the ability to perform.

Ever since encountering him, killing hadn’t been fun anymore.

“I’ll kill him. I swear it.”

Shagal’s eyes glinted with eerie resolve.

Meanwhile, Shirone and the group followed Brooks to a meeting room.

On the way, Lupist explained who Shagal was—but Brooks only shrugged.

“Is that so? In this line of work, you get all kinds wandering in.”

That was all he had to say. When they reached the meeting room door, Aria hung her torch on the wall and turned to leave.

“I’ll head off first. I’m tired.”

“Want me to walk you?”

“You look busy. Get to work. Shirone—see you next time.”

She called him out by name.

“Oh, okay. Take care.”

Though Shirone doubted they’d meet again, Aria turned with the certainty of someone who knew otherwise.

After she left, Lupist asked, “Is that woman safe?”

“She’s from the Tesia family. Keeps secrets better than most of my men. Don’t worry.”

With a smug grin, Brooks opened the door—but Shirone halted.

“Actually, we’d like to leave now. If you could just sign the completion slip for the request…”

“Ah, right. Of course.”

Shirone had only followed along for that one thing.

“You stay too,” Lupist said curtly.

Rian’s expression twisted.

He didn’t care if the man was Director of the Association or not—he had no right to order Shirone around anymore.

Especially after implying his survival had been due to luck. Just as Rian opened his mouth to protest, Shirone stepped forward.

“Sorry, but I’m busy. And I’m not part of the Red Line anymore.”

Lupist smirked.

“Funny. I thought it was settled, but you’re still sore.”

“I’m still human. Of course I’m upset.”

“Fine. I’ll apologize for the kingdom’s judgment. Still, it’s better to have friends in high places, isn’t it? You’ve got connections here.”

“Well, that’s true, but…”

Shirone wanted to wrap up guild business before news of the bandit extermination spread.

“And I suspect what I’m about to say is closely related to your Ivory Tower test.”

“What do you mean?”

Lupist’s smile faded.

“They’re not looking for Ra Enemy by chance. Every major organization in the world is hunting him down. Including the Tormia Magic Association.”

Shirone stared at him in silence.

Lupist jerked a thumb at the meeting room.

“So, interested now?”

There was no room for argument. The four entered a soundproof room.

Brooks brought out a bottle of liquor and four glasses, then sat at the table.

“So, Director—what’s the real reason you asked to meet me in secret?”

“It’s not that secretive. When someone wants to hire mercenaries, they call a broker.”

“A mercenary, huh. I doubt there’s a single one good enough for someone who commands the kingdom’s greatest mages. Well, maybe Shagal.”

“We’ll include him. It’s not about skill. It’s about deniability. This is something the Association can’t officially get involved in.”

Brooks popped the bottle open with a click.

“Heh, the mighty Association playing it safe? Now you’ve got me curious. What’s going on?”

“We’re shutting down Radum—the underbelly of Bashka.”

Brooks froze mid-pour, quickly withdrawing the bottle so his trembling hands wouldn’t show.

But Lupist had already caught everything, his grin razor-sharp.

“My intel says the leader of ‘Spectrum’—the largest demi-human union in Radum—is imprisoned here, isn’t he?”

“What are you talking about…”

“I saw fourteen demi-human corpses on the way here. I also saw the engraved bone tattoos on their right upper arms. You don’t find Radum’s people up here for no reason, do you?”

Brooks realized he had no choice but to make a deal and took a seat.

“What do you want?”

“Hand over the leader of Spectrum—Venezia.”

“I thought there was a non-aggression pact within the kingdom? You must understand what would happen if Spectrum found out the Association was involved.”

Every terrorist group in Radum would descend on Bashka and tear it apart.

“That’s why we need mercenaries. Officially, the Association won’t be involved. We’ll form an off-the-record unit and wipe them out.”

“Why? Everything’s been peaceful until now.”

“It was. But this time, the situation isn’t just about the kingdom.”

Brooks gave him a confused look, and Lupist pointed his index finger sharply.

“Venezia—she wasn’t captured. She came willingly. I’d bet my life on it.”

And it was true.

“Let me be clear—you’re not someone who can keep her around for long. If you try, she’ll destroy you as well.”

“Who will? Radum’s forces?”

“No. Ra Enemy.”

Shirone whipped his head around.

“Ra Enemy? Are you saying this Venezia woman is connected to Ra?”

Lupist took a breath and poured a drink for himself.

“You know this, don’t you—that Anke Ra erased himself and triggered the Great Purification.”

“Of course I know.”

“The Empire was probably the fastest to act, but Tormia also began its own Ra search based on intel from Cage B Team. The idea was to eliminate him before he fully emerged into the world. But we failed.”

“You couldn’t locate Ra?”

“No—just the opposite. There were too many Ras.”

Shirone frowned, and Lupist continued.

“To be exact, Ra existed, but it was like he didn’t. Countless people have seen Ra, spoken to him, some even say he graduated from a magic academy. And yet no one could track him down. Every lead spirals into another, looping endlessly. Do you know what that means?”

Lupist downed the bitter liquor and said,

“Ra Enemy seems to exist only as an ‘event.’”

“Only as an event?”

“When you think about it, it makes sense. Humanity has no shortage of trackers and clairvoyants who could find Ra. But knowing that, he wouldn’t have simply reincarnated into a traceable vessel. We should have realized that sooner.”

“What is Ra even thinking?”

“The answer lies with Venezia. She’s the only member of the Tri-Brain species—someone who perceives the past, present, and future through three separate brains. She knew Ra’s event would alter her and voluntarily imprisoned herself. Maybe she even foresaw us coming here.”

“When you say ‘us,’ do you mean… including me?”

Lupist’s expression turned cautious.

“Shirone. You must know by now that nations all over the world are watching you. But even we don’t fully understand you. So I ask—do you sense anything?”

“If you mean sensing something…”

Shirone’s eyes widened.

“Shagal said I had already met Ra Enemy.”

“Hmm. The 'scent of an event,' then. It’s an incredibly troublesome ability, but if he said that, it’s likely true.”

Rian had heard it too, but still couldn’t understand.

“But you don’t remember it. If Ra only exists through events, shouldn’t some kind of memory remain?”

“No. It’s exactly because of that. Ra can’t impress any event upon me.”

Shirone recalled Goffin’s words and spoke with certainty.

“Because I have no cause.”
