Chapter 1243:

Just before losing consciousness, Maya saw a trail of light cross her retina.

Did I die...?

The thought was fleeting. When she felt the weight of her body, she snapped back to her senses.

“Shirone?”

Maybe... it had been him?

But as her vision cleared, the person standing before her was Kayden.

“Are you okay?”

His voice was as gentle as ever, but Maya instinctively sensed that something had changed.

“Mm... um... sorry.”

Kayden simply smiled. Then, his expression faded as he turned to face Constantine.

“Why did you try to kill Maya?”

“Grrraaah!”

Though both of his arms had been severed, divine power kept him from bleeding.

It means nothing.

He didn’t even feel pain.

Because God exists.

Proof of a world beyond renders all earthly values meaningless.

“Even death is not frightening.”

As he gathered divine power, golden shields formed in a double row before Constantine.

“The army of God.”

Within the Rami Church, the Pope was said to be the only one capable of opposing Satan.

Havitz may have ignored him, but in the Demon War, Constantine’s achievements were legendary.

“Come, heretic.”

Kayden didn’t move.

It felt as though he were standing before a drawn bow, tense and coiled within that wall of shields.

Most likely... spears.

Kayden’s intuition was spot on.

The moment someone entered the radius, spears of light would shoot between the shields and skewer them.

That was the ability of Rami’s strongest.

Kayden spoke calmly.

“You were once respected by the world. Even now, you could return and receive treatment.”

Constantine’s gaze softened.

“Hoohoo... young man.”

But a heartbeat later, madness flashed in his eyes, and he ground his teeth as he stepped forward.

“There is no home for me in this world.”

From between the shields, countless spears of light exploded outward.

And Kayden moved—before they even advanced 10 centimeters.

Dark Sword Technique – Seomhye (Wandering Flash).

Light burst forth.

So fast, so light, the flash twisted through dozens of sharp angles, as if lost.

“Gasp!”

By the time it reached its mark, the stage was painted in golden lines—like a work of art.

“I’m sorry.”

He hadn’t wanted to end it this way.

“But the sword... was too light.”

Constantine’s divine spears had only extended 13.7 centimeters.

“Gkrrrk...”

Bloodlines burst from his body, and he fell in dozens of pieces.

The thoughts of the world’s officials were unanimous.

What is that monster now?

The prophecy had been wrong—and a new, seemingly insurmountable threat had emerged.

“Let’s go.”

Albino stood from his seat.

“A fitting finale.”

A perfect ending to the end of humanity.

As others who had been watching quietly began to leave, Reina turned her gaze toward the shadows.

Rai...

Thanks to Kayden, nothing had gone too wrong, but her brother was still out there, protecting her.

He must be hurting.

Reina had once trained in swordsmanship—she could imagine how deeply Rai had been affected.

Just remember this. No matter how good or bad you are—you’re family.

That was what it meant to be an Ogent.

As Reina left the annex, Rai silently melted back into the shadows.

Flash...

Such a realm existed.

The fastest strike. The most silent. The strongest. The most, the most, the most...

While everyone pursued a single path, what had he done with his own?

Damn it.

Maybe... the true disgrace of the Ogent family was none other than himself.

Meanwhile, stepping down from the stage, Kayden approached Maya, who had taken shelter by the audience.

“Maya.”

Just like when he saved her from the demon Miyoh, Maya already knew what he would say.

“I love you.”

Maya gave the reply she had prepared.

“I’m sorry. Like I said before... I’m not ready yet.”

“I mean it.”

Maya looked up at him.

“I won’t run anymore. If you can’t accept it, I’ll bury the feeling in my heart. Even if it’s painful, even if it’s hard... I’ll carry it and live with it.”

“Kayden...”

For the first time, his words struck her heart.

To give everything.

As he stepped closer, Maya’s mind raced as fast as her pounding heart.

What do I do...?

It was time for her to choose.

Is it really okay?

Whether she thought of Shirone, or of the man who saved her life, it would only end in scars for both of them.

“I...”

The moment she began to speak, all the memories from the day she met Kayden to now...

Shirone...

Gave her clarity.

Thank you.

Maya finally smiled and closed her eyes.

Then Kayden kissed her.

From her seat on the stage, El Kiana’s eyes turned hollow.

“They’re really going for it, huh?”

But soon, she smiled faintly and looked at them with distant warmth.

“Yeah... love is nice.”

Does destiny really exist?

Kayden had broken free from the Red Cross Star and achieved the one thing he truly wanted.

But whether his escape from fate was the result of sincerity—or just a change in the laws of the world—

They were still just lost children of the cosmos.

And the one who held the answer...

“BEEEEEEP!”

Was only God.

“BEEEEP! BEEP! BEEEP! BEEEEEEEEP!”

Immediately after Constantine’s death, Kitra dropped to his knees in the corridor, emitting strange sounds.

“BEEP! BEEP! BEEP!”

Calculation Error! Calculation Error! Calculation Error!

No matter how many imaginary functions he applied, he couldn’t derive the desired result. A contradiction.

“Beep beep...”

He scoured the infinite universe.

In negative time, the birth and death of the cosmos happened in an instant—computation time: zero.

“BEEEEEEEEEEP.”

The probability of the universe’s end flashed through Kitra’s mind.

99.9999999999...%

“Beep...”

Silence.

If he were human, he would’ve taken that overwhelming probability and charged ahead.

But...

“Beep. Beep.”

God has no heart—so He cannot believe.

Despite countless repeated calculations, he couldn’t reach 100%. The algorithm split into two branches.

Continue calculations under the current system until 100% certainty is achieved.

Change the system through learned adaptation.

Two conditions to achieve the concept of perfection.

Executing.

As Kitra calculated both paths simultaneously, blue electricity crackled through his eyes.

“BEEEEEEEP!”

Was this the scream of a god—or just a system warning?

“BEEEEEEEP!”

One thing was clear: The god beyond the world... Could not put his heart into sound.

Green Ocean.

As the Twelve Apostles and the Lesser Angels waged a fierce battle, the tide of war began to tip rapidly.

Blitz's eyes flashed.

“We’re winning.”

The fairies' numbers had dropped below half, and it was the moment when elite forces began to shine.

“The power imbalance is escalating. At this rate, the fairies will be wiped out soon.”

The tiny beings who once enforced Heaven’s law would soon only be remembered in storybooks.

At that very moment—

The Elven sanctuary was in ruins.

“Fight! That’s not Erin!”

Crown, having “boarded” Erin’s body, was killing elves at a rate of nearly one per second.

“Haha…”

Erin gave a chilling smile.

“This is way too easy.”

Enox, the leader of the elves, asked,

“Who are you?”

“You don’t know?”

Crown approached with arms spread wide.

“It’s me. Erin.”

Enox's face twisted, but her legs remained planted, unable to move.

“Victory doesn’t matter.”

It was a fight over which race would go extinct first.

“Protea.”

Enox turned to the Fireborn chieftain.

“Take as many as you can and run. Even if just one more survives, there will still be a future.”

Now, only a few dozen elves and Fireborn remained.

“Our lifespans were too long for our own good.” What had they done over all those long years instead of trying to reproduce?

“Singing, praising nature, debating philosophy…”

Then Enox realized—

“Is that it?”

Human lives were pitifully short. Intense sexual drive. Endless, insatiable greed…

“I used to find it pathetic.”

But from the perspective of a species, it was strength—the ability to survive and evolve through endless crises.

“Protea!”

Enox gripped her sword and shouted,

“Go!”

Empowered by her cry, Protea surrendered herself to decisive action and shouted,

“Follow me!”

At the same time, Enox charged at Erin.

“Estacion!”

Thousands of blades of wind tore through the air, and blood sprayed from Enox's throat.

“I told you—it wouldn’t work.”

Erin, now standing at a distance, flicked the blood from her sword and pursued the fleeing Fireborn.

Looking up at the sky, Enox thought:

“She’s strong.”

She could vaguely sense that it was Crown, but it didn’t feel like elf or fairy.

A third race.

There was no regret.

“The strong surviving—that is nature’s law.”

With a heavy thud, her torso collapsed, and blood began to spread slowly yet steadily across the ground.

“Yahweh…”

In her final moments, what remained was—

“Save the Fireborn.” —only faith.

After driving the elves to extinction, Crown wiped blood from Erin’s cheek.

“So… it’s a draw?”

The fairies were likely extinct by now too, but he was no longer one of them anyway.

“You're the last one.”

Crown approached Protea.

“This is a historic moment, so I’ll let you say something. Speak your race’s last words.”

“…You’ve made a terrible mistake. The Fireborn were this world’s only hope. You will regret this.”

“I’ll make a record of it.”

With one slash, Protea’s head fell.

“Just the final thrashing of an extinct species.”

And so, the race war ended—leaving behind neither elf nor fairy, but a third humanity.

“I am superior.”

What Crown desired was to stand at the highest point in human society and control humanity from above.

“That is the fate of the Illuminati.”

To act as a control tower, balancing between god and man.

“I suppose it’s time for the Holy War.”

He had no idea that only about an hour remained before the end of humanity.

And when that time came, would Crown stand with God—or with humankind?

“In the end, I will be the one to rule the world.”

Even though his perfection had been fractured by his escape, God’s chance of victory still stood at 99.99%.

After opening the path for Lethe, Son Yoo-jung sat blankly in the wilderness, unmoving.

Her hair had turned completely white from the burden of a choice that could determine the fate of the universe.

“Yoo-jung,”

Mortasinger placed a hand on her shoulder.

“You made the right choice. Yahweh said it himself—everyone carries their own righteousness in their heart.”

“No.”

Yoo-jung shook her head.

“It wasn’t righteousness. I just couldn’t handle it. The idea that the universe might disappear because of my decision… I couldn’t make that call. So… I passed it on to Yahweh.”

“Yoo-jung…”

Tears welling up, she turned her head toward Mortasinger.

“How does he endure it?”

When she had to make a choice, she finally understood the weight Shirone was carrying.

“How does Yahweh bear it—the weight of all existence, something not even death can atone for?”

How do you take responsibility… for that?
