Chapter 1203:

The Underworld.

As Shirone and Rian raced forward to rescue Jin Seong Eum, a scorching wind blew from behind, making them turn around.

“That’s insane.”

A massive heat haze wavered over the horizon where the Fire Management Company of the System Control Branch stood.

“Now that Son Yoo-jung has attained enlightenment, Lethe won’t have an easy time. We need to put as much distance between us as possible.”

Rian agreed, but seeing the hordes of demons ahead made it clear that wouldn’t be easy.

‘They’re still getting stronger. Is this really okay?’

Even if they managed to save Jin Seong Eum, once the Soul Realm opened, every demon in Hell would spill into reality.

“Let’s just do what we can.”

Shirone, reading Rian’s thoughts, spoke firmly.

“I’m sick of people sacrificing themselves, taking everything on alone.”

Rian fell into thought.

‘Shirone, I’m not the smartest. I can’t put it into words perfectly, but what you’re saying contradicts itself.’

Because Shirone himself was trying to take everything on—trying to carry Jin Seong Eum’s burden alone.

‘If you really plan to end everything by yourself…’

Rian charged forward.

‘I won’t let that happen!’

Grinding his teeth, he swung his greatsword, unleashing a thunderous roar that sent demon flesh flying.

“Block them! Don’t let them advance!”

The demons threw themselves forward, becoming a wall of flesh.

Shirone found it strange. When he looked up, he understood why—the sky was darkening with a growing black cloud.

‘The Black Chains. They’re not going to let us go that easily.’

Just by the sheer size of their formation, he could tell this was an overwhelming force. Counting them was meaningless.

“Rian!”

At the same time as his warning, Miracle Stream activated.

- Apprehend the sinner.

The moment radiant light exploded from Shirone’s body, the black cloud surged downward, swallowing the ground below.

“Mom. Mom.”

When Etella came to her senses, she was in a place she had never seen before.

A tunnel stretched endlessly like coiling intestines, with pools of foul-smelling liquid gathered in the corners.

A searing pain throbbed in her ears.

“Mom. Mom.”

A fetus, its face half-melted, was gnawing at her ear.

Lowering her head, Etella saw the countless unborn clinging to her body.

“Why did you abandon us?”

The voices echoed through the tunnel, untraceable to any single child, but that didn’t matter.

“I’m sorry.”

Every life in this tunnel was her child, and she had already made her decision.

“Come. Follow me.”

This place was likely a part of the karma-processing system, and at its end awaited eternal erasure.

‘It’s okay.’

Purifying all of Limbo was her righteousness.

But there was one lingering thought she couldn’t shake…

‘Shagall.’

The Taeguk chain had not disappeared yet.

‘I believe… you could have been a good person.’

But it was far too late for that now. Too many had already died by his hands.

‘This step is for you as well. I don’t know how long this journey will take…’

But may he, too, find rest.

As Etella walked through the tunnel, more and more Limbo souls clung to her.

Through the Taeguk chain, their excruciating pain transmitted directly.

“Aaaaaaagh!”

Shagall clutched his entire body, rolling on the ground.

‘It hurts.’

It was the pain Etella was experiencing.

“Damn it!”

Forcing his convulsing body upright, Shagall sprinted down the corridor again.

‘Wait! Just let me see you!’

As he burst through the engine room doors, a vast brain floated between massive cliffs.

“Hah! Hah!”

He gasped for air as he saw the chain extending from his chest, linking him directly to the brain’s core.

With a wild cry, he unleashed a storm of rapid-fire blades and leaped toward the cliff.

“Come out!”

When he stabbed his dagger forward, a swollen-eyed fetus parted its lips, unable to even open its eyes.

“I said come out!”

With a disgusted sneer, he flung the fetus off the cliff as if throwing out trash and swung both arms wildly.

Even as the Six Brains were torn apart piece by piece, his heart pounded anxiously.

‘Where are you?! Where?!’

His dagger, embedded in the wall, slid through effortlessly, piercing the opposite side with no resistance.

With a half-crazed look, Shagall turned back the way he had come.

‘She’s gone.’

The brains before him were nothing more than hardware. Etella had already disappeared into the depths of the underworld’s system.

“Why isn’t she here?!”

Shagall frantically tore through the Six Brains, but suddenly, his body froze. He collapsed to his knees.

“Aaaaagh!”

Through the Taeguk chain, he felt the overwhelming despair that consumed Etella.

- Remaining purification time: 87 aeons, 2,875 kyung, 3,241 trillion, 120.3 billion, 10,019 hours.

The message, which he didn’t want to hear, forced itself into his mind.

‘Why?! Why are you doing this?! You could have just told me! You could have threatened me, warned me! Why go this far?!’

But deep down, he already knew.

The time they had spent together, the countless things she had sacrificed…

“Ah! Ah! Ah! Ah!”

Overcome with self-loathing, Shagall gripped a dagger and stabbed it into his thigh.

‘I want to destroy myself.’

No, I want to be crushed, ground down by the machine.

“Die! Die!”

But he couldn’t. Until his karma was purified, he was cursed to live.

Even as blood gushed from the wounds he inflicted, his consciousness only sharpened.

Then, a thought struck him.

Maybe it wasn’t that he couldn’t die—maybe it was that he didn’t want to.

“Haaaaah…”

Maybe, if he tore himself apart, if he made himself suffer enough, Etella would try to stop him like before.

“Why are you doing this to me…?”

Bound by karma, Shagall’s pain was transmitted directly to Etella.

“Mom. Mom.”

The fetuses, unable to drink milk, gnawed at her body like starving beasts. Their numbers had now filled the entire tunnel.

“…Let’s go.”

Tears streaming down her face, Etella continued walking.

“I’ll save you all.”

Shagall screamed.

“Enough!”

Ripping the blades from his body, he staggered out of the Six Brains.

“So this is your revenge?! You think I’ll just take this lying down?! Fine, I’ll chase you! I’ll find you no matter what!”

I’ll drag you back to Hell.

‘I’ll make sure you stay with me forever!’

Taking a deep breath, Shagall activated the Resonance of the Incident.

"There’s No Smell."

If Etella had become part of the process, it meant she had either disappeared from the underworld or was now present everywhere.

"I can find you."

Shagall’s sense of smell sharpened beyond its limits, and finally, he pinpointed a specific location within the process.

At the same time, the Taeguk chain stiffened and stretched out toward an unknown destination.

"I'm coming."

Even as he bled profusely, Shagall hurled himself forward, smashing through a wall, following the chain.

"I'll kill you. The moment I see you, I’ll slap you across the face. Then I’ll take my time making you suffer—until you scream!"

His mind was consumed by the most horrific thoughts of what he would do to Etella.

- Remaining purification time…

Yet, his pain continued to be converted into karma.

The Twelve Apostles who had joined the battle of the races surrounded Uriel from all sides.

As the Golden Dragon Metira blocked Uriel’s Nirvana Staff, the Poison Dragon Poine spun her body and dove into his chest.

Her backward kick sent the massive archangel flying, crashing through trees with a deafening snap.

Uriel, who had been pushed back and crushed a boulder, regained his footing with sheer tenacity. Poine smirked at him.

“Looks like times have changed. Archangels are out of fashion now.”

Of course, this wasn’t true—it was merely a provocation.

The Thunder Dragon Blitz scanned the battlefield.

‘Even with us here, the battle between the Elves and Fairies is still dead even. That speaks volumes about Uriel’s influence.’

Though the Twelve Apostles had each reached the pinnacle of different elemental forces, the true strength of dragons only emerged when they fought in their original forms.

‘The biggest problem is that we can’t use our breath attacks.’

If all Twelve Apostles transformed into their true forms and fought, no matter how vast the Green Ocean was, it would be reduced to scorched earth.

Blitz recalled the orders given by Messiah.

‘More important than winning this war is ensuring the survival of the Elves and the Fire Tribe, who are on the brink of extinction.’

If they were to perish, any chance of altering the future of the Apocalypse would be completely erased.

‘We've already lost a considerable number of Elves. We can't afford to lose any more. We must protect them here.’

Determination burned in the eyes of the Twelve Apostles.

A temporary Fairy outpost was set up three kilometers away from the Green Ocean.

“Crown, sir.”

A Fairy officer dragged two bound Elves into the laboratory.

“Let go of us! What are you planning to do?”

A pair of Elves, one male and one female, were shoved into the room. Crown turned to face them.

“Oh?”

“Just kill us! No matter how much you torture me, I won’t break—!”

The defiant Elf’s voice turned into a shriek the moment he saw the scene inside the room.

Crown pressed a finger to his lips.

“Shh. Don’t scare the kids.”

“What… what the hell have you done?”

The creatures that once inhabited the Green Ocean were pinned to the walls, their skulls split open to expose their raw brains.

“Hm? What have I done? Oh, just… having a look.”

Crown casually sat in a chair at the center of the table.

“Don’t just stand there. Come, sit. We have a deep conversation ahead of us—about the future of life.”

The two Elves, compelled by the Fairy’s abilities, found themselves kneeling beneath the table.

“The future of life? This is slaughter! You’re killing living beings!”

Crown remained composed.

“Elves were originally Fairies.”

He smirked.

“You were once like us—mere extensions of my will, my obedient limbs. But then you mingled with humans, and now you've become an entirely new race.”

“What’s your point?”

“I just find it strange. Reproduction—it's an impossibility without a mechanical process involved.”

“We are not the same as other animals. Elves are born from the pure fruit of love.”

“I know. You call it ‘Agape,’ don’t you? The Immaculate Conception—creating life without mechanical processes was originally a privilege reserved only for the Archangel Kariel.”

Crown’s voice softened into a thoughtful murmur.

“I’ve thought about it. What is it, exactly? How could I become capable of such a thing?”

A long silence followed before he spoke again.

“I couldn’t figure it out.”

Seeing Crown’s eerie smile, the Elves’ unease deepened.

“I have a theory. The concept of life—before it manifests as a biological entity—must have some invisible, special momentum to it. Otherwise, how do you explain the tragic mechanism where life creates offspring, only to perish itself?”

To him, the core of this mysterious momentum was Agape.

“Let us go.”

The Elf spoke with resolve in his voice, though, in truth, he wanted to beg for death instead.

Crown suddenly floated up onto the table.

“I reject that mechanism.”

He stepped toward a massive bear hanging on the wall, plunging his small hand into its exposed brain.

The bear’s eyes rolled back as its body convulsed.

“If I transplant my brain into this beast, I will become this creature. I will reproduce as countless bears. And when I grow old, I will simply transfer into another body—whether it be a tiger, an eagle, a human…”

Crown’s grin twisted into a mad sneer.

“An Elf.”

“Aaaaaah! Aaaaaaaaah!”

Bound and helpless, the two Elves wept in despair.

“Enox.”

Crown muttered.

“If I take over the body of the Elf leader, Agape won’t be necessary anymore. But before that, I need a significant amount of data.”

He turned back to the captured Elves.

“You two will be the first step in my experiment.”

And thus, the first step of Fairy Biomimetics—the force that would one day rule the future of the Apocalypse—began.
