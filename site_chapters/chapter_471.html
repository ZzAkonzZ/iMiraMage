Chapter 471:

Shirone and Plu, having met Feope, infiltrated Paradise and changed into the citizens' clothes.

While a fairy's abilities could distinguish between the real and the unreal, trusting Feope was an entirely different matter.

Plu hesitated for a moment about whether to follow her, but since Shirone had no objections, she decided not to intervene hastily.

After all, she needed clothes too.

Once outside Paradise, Shirone received a rough explanation from Feope.

Currently, the Aegis system was neutralized, and the interception system was paralyzed.

This meant they could freely climb over the walls from the First Heaven to the Seventh Heaven.

Therefore, the entrance to the wall was guarded by giants, and the airspace was temporarily patrolled by fairies from the Citizen Management Department.

"Does this mean we have to engage in aerial combat to cross over to Araboth? This is tricky."

Plu rested her chin on her hand, deep in thought.

While teleporting over was quick, breaking through the fairy units with different abilities was a highly challenging task.

"There is one way."

Feope said.

"To pass through the fairy units in the airspace, you need a permit from the officials. If you get that, you can safely cross over to Araboth."

Shirone hesitated to answer.

Feope didn't know why Shirone was trying to go to Araboth.

If she knew he intended to activate divine punishment to destroy Heaven, what expression would she make?

'Why… why aren't you asking anything, Feope?'

Come to think of it, it didn't make sense for her, a member of the fairy department, to help him while a war with Heaven was ongoing.

Could she be doing all this just out of personal affection?

Plu, who had the same thought, asked without revealing her intentions.

"Can we get the permit?"

"I don't know. Even if we request it from Lord Igirin, it won't be easily granted. But if you still want to try, I can help."

Shirone asked.

"Where do we need to go?"

Feope pointed to the northwest of Shehakim.

Beyond Paradise stood a conical spire made of white bricks.

"That's the Citizen Management Department of Shehakim."

Shirone and Plu exchanged glances.

They would be walking right into enemy territory.

No matter how much they trusted Feope, they couldn't gamble with such an important mission ahead.

"We should just climb over the wall. Can you guide us to where Araboth is?"

Feope looked at Shirone with sad eyes.

In truth, she didn't want him to go to Araboth.

If he fought against Heaven to the very end, Feope would have to make a decision, and it would only lead to tragedy.

"Alright. Follow me."

Feope turned and flew towards the pointed end of Shehakim's pie-shaped area.

Shirone and Plu also cast fly spells and followed her, advancing about 10 kilometers.

"There they are! After them!"

A group began to chase them, leaping across the rooftops of Paradise.

They were the Kergos who had gained immortality and entered Shehakim.

"Eliminate the heretics!"

"Screeeech!"

With screeching cries, the Kergos swarmed from all directions.

Gaining immortality meant they had hunted countless heretics for Anke Ra since their time as citizens of the First Heaven.

Their combat power was on a different level from other Kergos wandering purgatory.

"What should we do?"

"Once they've spotted us, we can't shake them off. Their tracking abilities are incredible."

As soon as Shirone finished speaking, the Kergos warriors shot into the sky as if propelled.

Wielding sharp blades, they skillfully manipulated external force and had strength comparable to giants who had completed the elixir of myths.

"Scatter!"

Shirone, Plu, and Feope split in different directions to disperse the enemy's forces.

As they descended to the ground, more citizens occupied the alleys, following them.

There's a bounty on us!

Feope's telepathic message penetrated Shirone's mind.

Once they gained immortality, they no longer had animalistic needs.

What they desired were high-level ambitions like honor, pleasure, and power.

"Heh heh heh, if we catch them, we can become officials of the Immortal Council?"

Hearing the noisy voices from the alley, one of the Kergos drew a sword that glowed like a fireball in the sunlight.

It was Murka, one of the Kergos warriors.

His body, now in the prime of youth, was muscular, and his tanned bronze skin gleamed as if oiled, but he was actually 1,023 years old.

"If I become an official of the Immortal Council, I'll have access to Utopia. I'll buy all the fancy clothes I want there."

A Nor woman walked up beside Murka.

Her blonde hair was finely braided, and she wore a golden silk robe, the most valuable of the citizens' attire.

She was Deina, an immortal of the Nor race.

Her well-developed chest gave the robe beautiful curves, and her legs, revealed through side slits, were smooth.

But she was not much younger than Murka.

Clank! Clank!

A young man equipped with a Piper, a Mecca device, performed acrobatics and jumped down from the rooftop of Paradise.

Thud!

Landing on one knee in front of the two, he ran his hand through his hair with a smirk.

He was Raun, an immortal of the Mecca race.

While all Mecca pilots could handle a Piper, his skills, honed over nearly a thousand years, were so precise and refined that he was almost one with the machine.

"It's been a while since the three of us teamed up."

Raun drew his Signa and Exd and turned towards the commotion.

Though human, they had transcended mortality.

Having lived for over a thousand years, they had already achieved enlightenment, making them the strongest among humans in terms of single combat power.

For these immortals with exceptional combat abilities, this war was not a tragedy but an entertaining diversion.

As Deina spun the air with her hands, her body floated up.

"Let's see who catches them first. The winner becomes an official of the Immortal Council."

"Fine by me. Though it'll be me."

Murka gripped his sword in a reverse grip and leaped forward.

Raun manipulated his Piper, zigzagging up the wall and disappearing, while Deina increased the wind pressure and shot straight up.

Boom!

As the wall shattered and the Kergos warriors burst out, Shirone cast teleportation to widen the distance.

No matter where he fled, enemies swarmed from all directions.

This hadn't happened until recently, so his curiosity grew.

What was driving them?

"Screeeech!"

Hearing the screech from the sky, Shirone looked up sharply.

A warrior, silhouetted against the backlight, was falling headfirst.

The dark sword light grazed his nose, and before he could even retract his sword, Murka charged.

"Hahaha! Jackpot!"

'Jackpot?'

Through the Ultima system, the warrior's voice conveyed its meaning.

It made Shirone realize they weren't fighting a war.

They were chasing him as part of a hunting game, a form of amusement.

'But…'

Murka's movements were more agile and powerful than any Kergos Shirone had seen.

As he spun and unleashed a sword wind, the solid outer wall of Paradise shattered.

Following the mage's principle of creating distance, Shirone fired the Photon Cannon.

At the same time, Murka's eyes flashed, and various Skima techniques enhanced his vision.

Tiny nerves spread throughout his body, glowing momentarily and projecting outside his skin, making his body appear ghostly.

'He's fast! I can't perceive him!'

Shirone immediately spread his palm and activated Vajra Armor.

As the robe enveloped him at top speed, Murka's sword narrowly struck his side.

Boom!

"Ugh!"

Shirone crashed through the wall and into the building of Paradise.

The metallic body of the armored mineral species, Ringer, revealed grotesque scars and gleamed silver.

If he had been a moment slower, his body would have been torn apart.

Shirone tumbled like a flying squirrel, diving deeper into the building.

He had evaded the Photon Cannon with just his physical abilities.

'Is this what an immortal is?'

Even with eternal time, living beings had limits, but Murka's mastery of Skima had surpassed human limits.

"Haha! Are you scared already? Nephilim is nothing special. To think the citizens worship someone like you as the Light of Sector 73. They're really stupid."

Shirone's eyes sharpened.

Sensing his master's irritation, Arman spread its tendrils like a peacock's feathers.

"Oh? You have such tricks too?"

Murka showed no tension.

His thousand-year mastery of Skima allowed him to respond to any variable.

If the opponent was fast, he would slow down. If they were strong, he would evade. If they were durable, he would create the power to destroy them.

Perfect control over the human body.

That was Murka's specialty, earning him the title of the greatest warrior of the Kergos.

Hearing a sound like an insect's wings, Murka's eyes widened, and he twisted his waist.

Whoosh!

The tendrils, swinging with a strong wind, passed Murka and smashed the wall.

A speed far beyond his expectations.

'What is this?'

As Murka twisted his waist in a bizarre posture to observe Shirone, Shirone planted the tendrils in the ground and leaped.

An unknown resentment had built up in his heart.

As a human, Shirone couldn't help but feel a sense of jealousy towards immortality, a feeling only living beings could understand.

'Here I go!'

Photon Cannons fired in all directions, and four tendrils swept wide, clearing any space for evasion.

"Huff! Huff!"

Murka, as if in a training exercise rather than a real battle, controlled his breathing and dodged the attacks.

'He's truly impressive.'

The longer the fight went on, the brighter Murka's eyes shone.

Had he ever faced such a strong human in his thousand years of life?

Upon gaining immortality, humans transcended their mortal status and reached a higher realm.

His current combat power was on par with fairies and giants.

'But I can see it. I'm stronger.'

He would become an official of the Immortal Council and gain even more power. What he needed was right in front of him.

"Let's get serious!"

Murka, unleashing the Kergos' inherent combativeness, shot forward like an arrow and attacked Shirone.

Clang, clang, clang, clang!

Simultaneously, silver scars appeared on Arman's robe, and the shockwaves made Shirone's bones ache.

'That's a very sturdy robe. I like it.'

Murka slipped his sword under his armpit and spun.

As external force spread in all directions, his body rotated, and the sword created a disc-like afterimage as it flew towards Shirone.

'Can he block this too?'

Just as the blade was about to bite into Shirone's neck, an immense restraining force bound Murka's feet.

"Ugh!"

As Shirone retreated, the sword cut through empty air, and Murka struggled to stay upright.

'What is this?'

A single eye in a floating orb to Shirone's left was watching him.

The Antithesis that had even restrained the fallen angel Ikasa.

But Murka was different from Ikasa.

This was why he resided in the higher Third Heaven, Shehakim, rather than with the fallen angels.

"You, what is this…?"

Despite using all his Skima abilities to move, the restraining force grew sharper, piercing his muscle fibers.

It was impossible without a universal law that nullified the target's actions.

Shirone focused the Photon Cannon in front of him.

With the added magic amplification of Galatomic, the massive flash surged forward to obliterate Murka's face.

Boom!

At that moment, a wall of air formed in front of Murka, and the Photon Cannon exploded.

"Huff!"

Though not as powerful as Gaold's, the strong atmospheric pressure made Shirone glance towards the door.

"Hahaha! Struggling this much? You've aged, Murka."

'Who is this woman?'

A woman with a cold expression and a seductive smile floated a meter above the ground, entering Paradise.
