Chapter 699:

Shirone and Uorin didn't move until Rian and Kido passed through the Metagate.

Although they weren't particularly close, time seemed to flow slowly because they instinctively knew they could no longer be excluded from each other's lives.

'You've matured.'

Living as the Empress of Kashan meant that.

They had met when she was fourteen, and although she was still a fresh sixteen, she had grown into a beautiful woman who had shed much of her childishness.

Thanks to Shirone being the center of attention, Rian and Kido could freely observe Uorin.

'Is that woman Teraje?'

Thinking of her as the person with the most powerful authority in the world made even the space around her seem to ripple.

'She's definitely incomparable to Orcamp.'

Although she was said to be his daughter, it was clear that she hadn't inherited any of his genes.

"Uh, so…"

Before Shirone could say anything, Uorin quickly ran and hugged him.

"Brother Shirone!"

It was an unexpected situation, and even Gando who entered the room late blinked in disbelief.

"Do you know how worried I was, afraid something might happen to you?"

Looking down at Uorin's earnest eyes, Shirone hugged her tightly and said,

"Thank you, Uorin."

Although it wasn't visible as she was buried in his chest, there was a hint of shock in Uorin's eyes.

"I heard you saved me. I don't know how to repay this kindness."

Kido turned his head as if to ask if this was okay, but Rian just watched the situation cautiously.

Gando spoke carefully.

"Your Majesty."

The imperial castle Aganos was a perfect fortress equipped with all defense facilities for magic, law, and physics.

Moreover, Uorin was the highest-ranking human, protected by the guards divided into close, middle, and distant units, and the wind field.

But now, she was in someone's arms, ignoring all those defenses.

'That's why I tried to stop her.'

Leaving a Metagate in Kazura was like handing over the most valuable life in the world to someone else.

'To bring Shirone here.'

Jealousy.

The word briefly crossed Gando's mind, but he quickly shook his head.

'I must protect Her Majesty.'

As he decided to approach, Uorin first shrank her shoulders and slowly pulled away.

She gently pushed Shirone's chest with her slender fingers and smiled with a different expression than before.

"You've become stronger, Shirone."

Not calculating meant there was no fear.

Gando stopped in his tracks, and Shirone gazed intently at Uorin's unmasked face.

'Was it a lie after all?'

Why was it?

Even though he had expected it, he felt a sense of disappointment.

"It's thanks to your help. Armand too, and saving me from the moratorium."

Shirone asked belatedly,

"But… can I speak comfortably?"

If the playtime was over, the girl in front of him was not a sixteen-year-old but Teraje, who held thousands of years of history.

"Of course. It's awkward for players to act stiffly. Sit over here. Gando, bring some tea."

Since he was a being born for Teraje’s purpose from the beginning, he didn't feel betrayed even as he watched Gando head to the tea room.

"Let's see, the blue hair must be Rian…"

Sitting with her legs crossed, Uorin looked at Kido and raised an eyebrow as if asking for an explanation.

Kido bared his teeth and pointed to himself.

"Kido of Consumption. King of the Goblins."

Kido thought such a title was only natural when facing the ruler of Kashan.

"Hmm, King of the Goblins."

Uorin looked Kido up and down and then pouted her lower lip.

"You look quite funny."

"…"

Seeing Kido's expression, she chuckled and turned to Shirone.

"I thought you would visit Kazura someday. So, have you shaken off your guilt?"

"I just told the truth. It caused quite a stir."

While Shirone talked about what happened in Kazura, Gando brought tea.

"I know I did something cruel to them. But I didn't want to leave any room for doubt. Otherwise, I wouldn't be able to live my life."

Uorin looked into her teacup and said,

"You're foolish. The prince wasn't killed by you."

"But he died because of me."

"That's why you're the last among the Ivory Tower candidates."

When Shirone squinted his eyes, Uorin looked up at the ceiling and said,

"If you don't care, that's it. Guilt? No matter the reason for your birth, you are you."

"But…"

"Yes, it's not easy. Emotions are like that. They complicate everything. Good and evil are just matters of emotion. That's why those who control emotions control humans."

Uorin lowered her head again.

"There are plenty of psychopaths without emotions in this world. But very few can make cold decisions while embracing emotions. Whether humanity or truth comes first, that's where you and Nane differ."

Teraje seemed to know everything humanity knew.

"What kind of person is Nane?"

"First of all, strong. Even if you fought, I couldn't guarantee the outcome."

It was a more generous evaluation than Zuoibo's.

"But Nane's true talent lies elsewhere. Like you, he observes everything and moves forward, but he doesn't judge right and wrong. So he's very fast. How fast, you ask…"

Uorin smiled.

"Now Nane surpasses you."

It had only been five seconds since the first evaluation.

"I see."

Shirone felt he understood what kind of person Nane was.

"Orcamp and Eliza's feelings, your feelings, countless emotions. You always ponder in your head and follow the optimal conclusion. But Nane acts first. Whether it's something humans can do or not, no matter how extraordinary, he tries it and draws conclusions. Therefore, his realizations are precise and astonishingly fast. He's just a machine that absorbs truth."

It was an expression so intense it was chilling.

"For example, you erased Ra Enemy's random numbers with the bubble of events, but Nane would have absorbed Ra Enemy with the same random numbers."

As the silence of realizing the strength of an unexpected competitor filled the room, Kido pointed at Uorin.

"If it were you? Among the three Ivory Tower candidates, who would you vote for?"

Uorin's eyes narrowed like a seagull's.

"Haha, that's a difficult question. In front of the person involved."

"You don't care about such things, do you?"

Even with a brief conversation, Kido had grasped that Uorin was different from ordinary humans.

"The vote of the stars? Nane seventeen votes, Jin Seong-eum nine votes. And Shirone got just one vote. If it were me…"

As Uorin put her finger to her lips and thought, everyone tensed up.

"I would probably vote for Nane or Jin Seong-eum."

A shadow fell over Rian's face.

'Still not enough, huh.'

It was an evaluation from none other than Mitochondria Eve, so it couldn't be ignored.

"But if I had to bet my entire fortune…"

Kido's eyes filled with anticipation again.

"Of course, Nane. Do you know how much my fortune is? It's only natural to bet on the most likely side."

'This woman is a pervert too.'

Uorin's appearance, scratching her head and sticking out her tongue, was so annoying.

Although he didn't show it, Shirone also had to lower his head in a gloomy mood.

'So that's how strong he is.'

Uorin, who was looking at him kindly, said,

"But… if I had to bet my life?"

She pointed at Shirone.

"Then I would vote for you, Shirone."

Realizing something, Shirone quickly raised his head.

"That's how it is. Someone else's opinion, without any stakes, doesn't matter at all. Reputation, money, life, thoughts change countless times depending on those. Only you can evaluate your life."

Something hot surged in Shirone's chest.

"Nane has destructive experiences. Jin Seong-eum has unwavering integrity. Of course, both are fast. But you have caution that doesn't judge right and wrong recklessly. So it's okay to be a little slow. That will guide you to 100%, not 99%."

Shirone clenched his fists.

'For perfection.'

"You just chose a slightly difficult path. I don't know who cast that one vote for you, but they must have thought the same as me. If they had to bet their life… That's the only truth. You are fully qualified to become a star."

Rian looked at Shirone, whose eyes were welling up.

'The Empress of Kashan is immense.'

There would be very few people in the world who could comfort Shirone with their heart.

Shirone took a deep breath and calmed himself.

"Thank you, Uorin. I really feel confident now. I'll definitely pass the Ivory Tower test."

"Of course, you should. Who do you think voted for you?"

"Huh?"

Uorin's confidence was evident as she shook her head slyly after speaking openly.

"Never mind. So, you want to know where Ra Enemy is, right?"

"Oh, right."

That was the purpose.

"Gando, bring paper and a pen."

While Gando went on an errand, Shirone asked,

"Do you know where Ra Enemy is?"

"No, unfortunately, I don't know the exact location. I can only make an approximate guess."

"Guess?"

Uorin began to explain as she placed the paper and pen on the table.

"Ra Enemy's location is important even in Kashan. So, as soon as the third reset began, I tracked history. But I got hit hard. What I found was just an incident related to Ra Enemy, not the real thing."

It was a miracle that Shirone managed to capture Ra Enemy, considering even the great Uorin was deceived.

"So, I used a different method."

"A different method?"

Uorin twirled her pen and said,

"I investigated the entire history of all humans except Ra Enemy."

Shirone's mouth dropped open.

"You investigated… everything?"

She drew a circle the size of a fist on the blank paper.

"It's not as difficult as it sounds. Humans have free will, but there are biological patterns. This is called probability."

She dotted the inside of the circle with dozens of points and connected them with lines.

"Some people spend their entire lives in their hometown, while others travel the world. Some get married and start families, while others move because their homes are ruined."

Shirone watched the lines being drawn at an incredible speed.

It seemed like she was just doodling with the pen on the paper, and the circle was filled with countless lines.

"If we assume this circle is the world we live in, the paths of humans based on probability are this complex. However…"

As the lines continued to fill the circle, a particularly white, empty area stood out.

"When you analyze billions of paths, you inevitably find areas where probability breaks down."

Uorin crossed her arms after putting down the pen.

"Simply put, these are areas where people have entered but never come out. I call these causal holes."

Places where once you enter, you can never escape.

"Where are these places?"

Uorin held up three fingers and said,

"Jessica. Andre. Paitaros."

Hearing names that everyone encounters at least once in their lives, Shirone looked up from the paper.

"Could it be?"

"Yes. The world's three great labyrinths. This is where the real Ra Enemy is."
