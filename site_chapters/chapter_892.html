Chapter 892: Coming Disaster (1)

The Valkyrie 2nd Corps, which had begun marching from various parts of the central continent, gathered at the Kanian Highlands.

Tess, looking at the rocky expanse stretching to the horizon, turned to Rian.

"What happened to Shirone?" Logically, he should have reported back to the Ivory Tower by now.

"I don't know. But he'll be fine." Shirone is strong.

However, it was unsettling that even the Holy War hadn't yet located Yahweh.

'Why didn't he go to the Ivory Tower? Or couldn't he?'

For Rian, Shirone was the top priority to protect.

Knowing this, Tess asked,

"Are you sure you should stay here?" Rian remained silent.

He desperately wanted to find Shirone, but knowing Havitz's abilities, he couldn't leave.

'And Natasha.'

Her unmatched speed made her impossible for anyone but Rian to handle.

'She'll come for me.'

From the demons' perspective, the Mach Knight was a key target that needed to be dealt with first.

"We just need to hold out for one day."

When the revised orders from the Holy War came down, it felt like a breath of fresh air for the entire 2nd Corps.

"I'll stop Natasha. But I can't handle Havitz too. Be careful, Tess."

Thinking of Vanishing, Tess swallowed hard.

"Don't worry. I'll keep my wits about me."

Even though keeping her wits about her wouldn't necessarily help, there was no other option.

"Will Amy be okay?"

Finding Amy in the camps spread over several kilometers was difficult.

"She's in the Magic Division, so she'll move frequently according to strategy. We might meet during battle."

Rian spoke calmly.

Once the battle started, everyone had to give their all to their duties.

'No one can do it for you.'

Saving oneself was the only way to survive this hell.

"Amy..."

Tess sent her thoughts to her friend somewhere out there gathering her resolve.

'Let's survive together.'

With the return of those who had been excluded and the revised orders from the Holy War, morale soared.

As the appointed time approached, discipline became a cold blade aimed at the enemy camp.

"Phew."

Amy's 6th Battalion was positioned behind the vanguard to provide fire support.

'I can do this.'

Iruki had said they would die.

There were no disagreements, but Amy believed in what she had done up until now more than anything else.

'Fight!'

She stood up and left the tent where her troops were waiting.

"Lieutenant Colonel, it's time."

Seeing the determination in her troops' eyes, Amy realized there was no need for many words.

"Let's go."

Amy's unit joined in between the 20,000-strong vanguard that had split into two wings.

They would support whichever side faltered first and could not rely on further orders from above once engaged.

"All troops listen up!"

The vanguard commander was Brigadier General Taigang, a famous swordsman from the central continent.

"The enemy we will face is...!"

A massive dust cloud rose from the horizon as the ground began to shake.

"General."

There was no time for farewells.

'This is war.'

Taigang turned back to his troops with a smile as he watched the dust cloud indifferently.

"It has been an honor serving with you."

His sentiment pierced their hearts faster than words could reach them, and their eyes reddened with emotion.

"Wooaaah!"

As if pushed by their cheers, Taigang spurred his horse forward and drew his sword with a shout.

"Kill them!"

It had begun.

In the highlands where strategy and tactics were useless, humans and demons slowly closed in on each other.

'They're coming!'

Amy's unit saw shadows hidden in the dust as they charged from mid-position.

"Kraaaah!"

The 200 million-strong army roared as they charged forward with an overwhelming noise that couldn't be fully comprehended by sight alone.

"Huuu..."

A groan escaped through Amy's clenched teeth despite herself.

Fear.

But it was okay.

'It's fine. I've made it this far.'

From now on, what moved the army wasn't mere will or determination but an unstoppable momentum.

"Commence bombardment!"

Following Amy's command, hundreds of magicians fired flames toward the front.

And then, everything was trampled underfoot.

Valkyrie Headquarters.

"Reporting! The 3rd Division of the 2nd Corps, gathered at the Kanian Highlands, has been annihilated!"

The commander asked.

"What is the estimated scale of the damage?"

"It's impossible to determine precisely. But when calculated per unit area, it's estimated to be over 30,000."

"Hmm."

A breath like air escaping a punctured lung filled the room as Iruki bit his nails.

"30,000," he muttered.

It had only been 2 hours since the battle started.

"We need to hold out for another 22 hours."

The most miserable fact was that there was no way to assist the 2nd Corps.

"Don't think about what can't be done."

Iruki stood up and walked to the other side of the table to examine the map.

"Forget the highland battle. What we need to think about now is the counterattack. How are the deployments?"

"They're completed. The optimal location is here," the commander pointed out as Iruki's eyes, colder than ever, scrutinized the spot.

6 hours into the operation.

"What's going on?"

Covered in dust, Amy stood up, watching the world spin around her.

"Where am I?"

Kanian Highlands.

"What am I doing right now?" War.

An immediate response surfaced in her mind, but it felt as if it were blocked by a barrier, lacking any sense of reality.

"Am I alive?"

She knew that activating her self-healing memory could help recover a shocked mind.

But her instincts resisted.

"Why am I here?"

She didn't want to regain her senses.

Her instinct desired to go mad, remaining unaware of anything.

"Aaaah!"

One-meter-tall demons with uneven teeth rushed towards Amy.

Her eyes ignited with fire, and Amy twisted her body to dodge the attack.

The shocking reality and stress surged, threatening to rip her mind apart.

"Uuuugh!"

But she didn't go mad.

"Hehehe! A survivor! A survivor!"

With flames springing from her hands, Amy turned the approaching demons into roast.

"Commander!"

At a familiar voice, she turned her head to see Beric, along with 14 others, limping towards her.

"You guys!"

It felt like meeting God in hell.

"Are you okay? You're injured!"

Beric's right wrist was missing, and in his haste, he hadn't even applied a tourniquet.

"I'm fine. Please, get to safety. We're quite far from the main force."

"If you keep bleeding here, you'll die." Amy ripped her uniform into thin strips and tightly wrapped them around Beric's stump.

"Aaaah!"

As the band tightened, excruciating pain pressed on his nerves, but this was not the time to worry about such things.

"Is this all the survivors?"

The 1st Battalion commander said.

"I'm not sure. These are all the comrades we encountered on the way here."

Amy nodded and brightened her fiery eyes.

"Where exactly?"

Even without knowing the starting point, her self-healing memory accurately determined her current coordinates.

Amy pointed west.

"The main force is over there. From now on, follow me. We'll avoid combat as much as possible, so take care of the wounded."

The escape operation began.

Her fiery eyes led the way through any situation, but it was impossible to perfectly avoid the hordes of demons filling the sky and the earth.

After several minor skirmishes, three comrades were lost, and another died two hours later.

"Ha! Ha!"

The distance would have been impossible if not in a straight line.

"I won't give up."

The thought of circling the same spot out of frustration made her feel like she would go insane.

As the sun set, Amy finally heard the distant roar of battle.

"We've made it! Just a little longer, everyone."

Since the demons were advancing in disarray, it seemed feasible to detour to the main force without issue.

"Hehehe! Found them."

As a voice echoed from the sky, Amy's face turned pale and despair filled her comrades' eyes.

"I've missed you."

Dangling from the legs of a monster, a demon with the head of a bull sneered evilly.

"Of all things..."

She had previously encountered the division commander, Parkma.

"No, it's not a coincidence."

Parkma, clutching a giant axe in both hands, fell from the sky with a thud.

"Thought you could escape?"

The side where Amy had hit him still throbbed painfully.

"Didn't I tell you, I'd carve your face out of my retina if I had to. I haven't killed anyone since then because I've been looking for you." Amy bit her lip as she glanced behind her.

"We were almost there."

Facing a fresh Parkma in their exhausted state was akin to suicide.

"Is it me you want?"

Realizing what Amy was about to say, Parkma grinned vilely.

"No. What I want is for you to do what you hate most. For example..."

Suddenly, Parkma swung his large axe, and the heads of the injured comrades were severed.

"Like this?"

Amy's face twisted in rage, and the remaining survivors launched a joint attack.

"Dieeee!"

Flames and frost raged, but Parkma, seemingly unconcerned about dodging, continued to advance, taking the hits.

"Hehe! Seems like you've taken quite a beating? You're much worse off than before."

The large axe showed no mercy, and eventually, everyone except Amy and Beric had fallen.

"Now, with no more distractions, shall we get to our little project?"

As Parkma stomped closer, Beric stepped in front of Amy.

"Go, commander. I'll buy us some time."

That was what Amy wanted to say.

"You go. I'll handle this."

"That doesn't seem likely. You know I can't make it back to the main force with my skills."

"I'm the commander. This is my responsibility to bear."

"… Ultimately, humans die."

"What?"

As Parkma paused, axe slung over his shoulder, watching, Beric turned back.

"My father said, when you have to make a tough choice, remember that all life ultimately ends in death. That way, you can really make the right choice."

Beric turned forward again.

"Of course I want to live. But if I can't, I want to make the right choice."

Amy clenched her teeth.

"The right choice? Dying in my place is the right choice?"

"No."

Beric slowly stood up.

"Since I was born a man, isn't it better to look good in front of the woman I love?"

If he was going to die anyway.

"I was so happy when you returned to the unit. Because you chose us over your boyfriend. Honestly..."

Beric turned back, a sad smile on his face.

"I felt like I had beaten that blessed guy. Go. You deserve to live. And I'll become an incredibly cool man."

Beric charged forward.

"Forever."
