Chapter 632:

The pilot insect with its thin legs fell to the ground, and Amy crushed it under her foot.

"I won't forgive you."

Whether Shirone helped Maya or not, Amy was responsible for her own actions. The shame of having her emotions exposed and the humiliation of being controlled by someone else's will fueled her anger.

'Fire In!'

Her body ignited like dry wood, and the fire spirit Ifrit appeared.

But instead of attacking everyone as expected, she charged at Shirone.

'Shirone… you're too kind!'

Ifrit's fist created a heatwave as it swung, and the afterimages of Time Explosion weaved through the flames.

In this unexpected turn of events, everyone watched as Amy grabbed Shirone by the collar.

"Let's talk after the exam."

Shirone smirked slightly, and Amy spun around, throwing him towards Pisho.

"Damn!"

Pisho raised Argones, but a second later, a Photon Cannon exploded in his abdomen.

"Gah!"

In reality, his body would have been crushed, but the anti-magic of the 2000 system wasn't physical force.

'Good, one down!'

Atomic Bomb's chain explosions hit the groggy Pisho.

-Participant Number 24 has exited the 2000 system.

Now there were 13 survivors.

Though Shirone and his friends had eliminated Pisho with a surprise attack, Shirone was still the most suitable next target for elimination.

"Huff. Huff."

If not for the tension of the graduation exam, Shirone's mental strength would have already been exhausted.

"A mage…"

He couldn't speak.

'I will be.'

As the participants' magic focused on Shirone, his percentage finally dropped below 1%.

'I'm sorry, Shirone.'

Maya desperately stood up.

"Take this!"

Cayden was still protecting her, but she could only see Shirone.

'It's my fault. I'm too weak…'

If he hadn't saved her from the Ant Nest, he wouldn't be facing elimination now.

'But that's who Shirone is.'

The only person who showed her genuine care among the cold-hearted mages.

'He'll never change.'

Tears welled up in Maya's eyes.

"I love you so much."

Irregular Eater - Only This Moment.

As she began to sing, the participants were forcibly stopped, and murmurs spread through the VIP seats.

"Magic power level: 120 million magicals."

"What?"

"Only This Moment" was the song Maya sang during her first and last stage performance.

'It's an irregular eater.'

Realizing they were being forcibly distracted, the participants sensed the gravity of the situation and looked around nervously.

'Mental strength is recovering.'

Meanwhile, Shirone's mind was experiencing a tremendous surge.

'Accept my song, Shirone.'

Though it carried an extreme risk of being sung only once in a lifetime, displaying such concentration with 120 million magicals was solely due to her genius.

'Amazing talent.'

Reina had heard many voices as a court musician, but this voice had a resonance that couldn't be analyzed by technique alone.

"Maya…"

Tears welled up in the chief's eyes.

At twelve years old, she had a chance to perform as an opening act for singer Canary. Her singing left the audience spellbound but was met with boos instead of applause.

'She was too exceptional.'

Canary's popularity was so immense that fans couldn't accept a twelve-year-old's song as an opening act. Maya never got a second chance after that disastrous performance and gave up singing when she received sponsorship offers from perverted nobles instead.

"Only- This Moment."

At first struggling against it, even the participants had to acknowledge they had to listen to her song now.

'It feels like all my hair is standing on end.'

Collie trembled as he clutched his hair with both hands.

'Who says this girl is weak?'

He was the one who had been captivated by twelve-year-old Maya on stage and brought her to magic school.

'She's not singing to look impressive. She doesn't practice every day just for evaluations.'

It wasn't about doing her best; it was about sincerity. Looking up at the world from the lowest position and sublimating that pain into genuine emotion.

'If you think about it this way, everything is beautiful…'

Criticizing, analyzing, comparing to rank things…

'Do we rank our emotions?'

If not, then Maya's song has no rank either.

"Only- This Moment!"

As her song reached its climax, intense emotion surged through the participants like waves.

"Shirone's percentage is rising while everyone else's is falling."

"It's not taking from others; it's balancing."

Theoretically speaking, it was emotional equalizing created through vocal resonance.

"Huuuh!"

As Maya's pure high note pierced the sky, Sabina fainted from overwhelming emotion.

'Two more!'

With only two more eliminations needed to decide the final ten, Pony bit her lip until it bled.

'This can't be! Why isn't anyone else falling?'

The desperate pleasure derived from participants' despair reached its peak through royal downfall when…

"Gasp!"

Pony's knees buckled and hit the ground hard.

'I did my best…'

What had she lacked?

Without finding an answer to that question, she collapsed like other eliminated participants.

'Just one more.'

As the participants' determination burned even brighter, only Cayden wore a sad smile.

'I love you, Maya.'

Watching Maya, who only had eyes for Shirone, Cayden bid his farewell.

'But I don't think I can go with you.'

When this song ended, Maya would be exhausted, but a completely different future awaited her compared to Cayden's exhaustion.

'I wish you happiness. Truly.'

With a clatter, his cross sword fell, and Cayden closed his eyes, accepting the song with all his heart.

It wasn't a matter of technique.

In that moment of acceptance, all prejudice vanished, and her song became an emotional experience rather than magic.

'My muse…'

As Cayden silently collapsed, a pure, 100% untainted voice echoed through the sky.

"Leav-ing."

A shiver ran down the spines of everyone gathered in the colosseum.

'She's a genius. There's no need to verify that.'

Reina looked at Maya with astonished eyes.

The fact that she moved everyone in a gathering of scouts from around the world foretold that her tomorrow would be completely different from today.

'It's over.'

Taking a deep breath, Maya looked up at Shirone.

"Thank you for everything, Shirone."

Shirone couldn't say a word and simply bowed his head with the utmost reverence.

It was a song sung by someone who was about to become the most famous person in the world, and it was sung for just one person.

"Goodbye…"

As Maya closed her eyes and collapsed, the irregular eater ended, and the participants regained their freedom.

Their minds quickly processed the reality.

'Wait, so what happens now?'

-Participant Number 26 has exited the 2000 system. The final 10 have been decided.

The 2000 system continued the announcement.

-Maya Shinbi. Final graduation rank: 10th. Remaining participants: 9.

"Wow!"

The audience stood up and gave a standing ovation.

The applause was for the final 10 who had successfully graduated, but it was also a greater tribute to Maya, who had overcome adversity to finally earn the title of mage.

"Chief! Sister, she…!"

Maya's siblings were speechless, and the chief discreetly wiped away his tears.

'Well done! Truly well done, Maya!'

As praise poured in for Maya, who had beautifully decorated the final hurdle of graduation, Elizabeth asked,

"Maya Shinbi. Please give the final evaluation."

From the 10th rank onward, graduates were officially recognized and addressed by name as a sign of respect.

"F. She's too lacking in every aspect to work as a mage."

Lara, who seemed to have expected this, clicked her tongue as she recorded it, and Baikal added an unusual comment.

"But if I ever get the chance to attend her performance, I'd definitely want to reserve an S-seat."

The applause filling the colosseum showed no signs of stopping, and the remaining participants felt their legs weaken.

'Passed, I passed.'

No matter what, the feeling of successfully graduating was exhilarating beyond compare.

'Really… I graduated?'

Amy felt a surreal euphoria and a crushing sense of relief at the same time.

But only for a moment, she quickly steeled herself and shook her head.

'Get a grip. It's not over yet.'

The moment she felt satisfied, that would be her limit.

Looking around, she saw the other participants gathering in the center of the colosseum with expressions that said it wasn't over yet.

"First of all, congratulations…"

Dante raised his chin and said,

"But we still need to determine the best, right?"

As if on cue, everyone's gaze focused on one person, and Shirone smiled as he stretched his neck.

"Anytime."

Maya's irregular eater had equalized the participants' mental strength.

So this was a new evaluation, a true battle without the worry of elimination.

"Boooo!"

At that moment, the audience began to boo.

The voices were directed at Neid, who still stood in the corner, unlike the participants ready to fight in the center.

"Coward! How can someone like you be a mage?"

"There's no such thing as a mage who passes by luck! Give your spot to another participant!"

While Shirone had stood out the most, the other participants had also fought desperately to earn their spots.

In contrast, Neid hadn't shown any mage-like abilities in the first evaluation and hadn't clashed with anyone in the second evaluation, reaching this point through an unbelievable stroke of luck.

It was only natural that the audience, moved by Maya's success, resented his inclusion in the final stage.

"Neid isn't a coward."

Liz, observing as a graduate of Alpheas Magic School, looked at Neid with pity.

"He just… lost his way."

She felt even more sorrowful because she had erased the only path Neid had left.

"If only he could get one more chance."

Clasping her hands, Liz fervently hoped Neid wouldn't let go of his last thread of hope.

"West Neid. He hasn't shown anything special worth evaluating. No battles. No one wanted to face him."

Elizabeth's words were met with agreement from Lara.

"Luck is a skill, but this is extreme. Has this ever happened before?"

"It's impossible."

"What?"

Baikal spoke with a serious expression.

"There's no way to reach the final ranking without any clashes. So it's not that Neid ran away; everyone ran away from Neid."

"But… he didn't seem particularly skilled."

"That's why he's being booed. I don't understand. What could make those who defy death avoid him?"

For a mage, something more terrifying than death.

"An uncontrollable situation."

Realizing something, Baikal raised his head.

"A variable that could destroy the system. What they feared was perfect chaos."
