Chapter 500: Despair (3)

Kuan used Schema to tighten his muscles and stop the bleeding. Then, as if nothing had happened, he calmly pointed his sword.

"Oh, so there's a technique like that."

A thin smile appeared on Nameless's lips.

His insight, which allowed him to understand and apply anatomical diagrams just by looking, was beyond human comprehension.

"But I don't need it."

As Nameless charged, 24 external forces erupted from Kuan's body.

Each external force expanded and contracted like a ball attached to a rubber band, vibrating Kuan's body.

It was an extremely strange movement, but to Nameless, it was just a predetermined path.

Proving this, Nameless displayed far superior movement, pressing Kuan.

A trajectory beyond all human prediction, expectation, even imagination and delusion.

"Ugh!"

Another fatal blow landed, and Kuan grimaced as he landed on the ground.

Seeing Nameless cross his twin swords and push forward, Kuan raised his sword but was pushed back to the wall by the force of the charge.

Clang, clang, clang!

The blades clashed and locked in place, and the two swordsmen's gazes collided between the sword lights.

"Ah, now I understand."

Nameless's interest shifted from swordsmanship to Kuan.

Armin's use of Stop magic to create a barrier made sense.

The moment the barrier disappeared, Nameless was confident he could use Flicker magic to instantly strike both of them.

But he wondered why Kuan, realizing the difference in skill, was still fighting desperately.

The answer he arrived at was the highly inefficient and irrational human heart.

"Are you dying to save them?"

Kuan briefly met Siena's eyes.

It was a moment of genuine emotion that even a swordsman's superhuman nerves couldn't suppress.

And Nameless didn't miss the woman's face reflected in Kuan's eyes.

According to his memory, it was love.

Though he hated to call him father, it was the highest form of the species' reproductive desire in Garas's animalistic nature.

'No, that makes even less sense.'

Garas would use any means to fulfill its desires, even modifying its body.

But Kuan's actions seemed to go against such basic animal instincts.

"They plan to use you as bait to escape. And the woman doesn't love you. She wants to bear the child of the man beside her. So why are you doing this?"

Though his desire for achievement had replaced his reproductive urge, Nameless, with Garas's genetic material, saw no reason to doubt what he observed.

"Huuu."

Kuan took a deep breath from deep within.

He didn't need Garas's intuition to know that Siena had feelings for Armin.

So why was he doing this?

The only thing he would gain from saving Siena and Armin was death. What was he fighting for?

'Lucky mages.'

Mages always made the best choices.

So Siena's decision to stay with Armin was likely the right one this time too.

Just like when he had rushed to the restaurant after receiving urgent funds and had unhesitatingly talked about death.

He wanted to be by Siena's side.

Not as a mere swordsman, but as the one and only man.

And at this moment, Kuan let go of all hope.

"What do I know, idiot."

Kuan exhaled all the breath he had been holding and swung his sword.

Nameless took a few steps back, frowning as if looking at something insignificant.

"Foolish. Choosing death without even knowing the justification for your actions. Is that what it means to be human?"

As Kuan charged, Nameless aimed his twin swords and narrowed his eyes. His arms seemed to dissolve like smoke, and a high-speed slash enveloped the front.

Whoosh!

The speed was enough to be called a sword barrier.

The sound of air burning reached Kuan's ears as dozens of blades rushed toward him.

'I've found my grave.'

Even as absolute death approached, Kuan felt a strange sense of elation.

The grave he chose was in Siena's heart.

If he could die in front of her, for her, that would be enough.

'I know, it's childish.'

Kuan sharply changed his trajectory, bypassing the sword barrier.

'Even though I'm angry…'

He couldn't bear the jealousy of seeing Armin holding Siena, yet he couldn't say a word.

Kuan circled Nameless even faster.

Reacting, Nameless's sword twisted like a snake, leaving dozens of wounds on Kuan's body.

Even if they weren't fatal, the blood loss was lethal.

"Let go! Let go!"

Siena screamed for Kuan, but her voice couldn't penetrate the barrier of time.

"Siena! Calm down! It's not time yet!"

Armin's heart was just as heavy.

If he could close the distance with Flicker magic and use Stop on Nameless, there was a chance to overcome this crisis.

But if Nameless evaded the magic the same way, Siena would be 100% certain to die.

"Wait! Just a little longer!"

"Kuan! Kuan!"

In the rapidly spinning world, Kuan briefly caught a glimpse of Siena's face.

'Yes, this is me.'

A clown dancing with a sword in front of her, hoping she would understand his feelings.

"Heh heh heh! Heh heh heh heh!"

Kuan laughed, his lungs heaving, but his twisted face looked like he was crying.

"Fine, you can have the title of sword genius."

Finally, Kuan penetrated the sword barrier.

"I am the sword demon."

Whoosh!

External forces extended in all directions, and Kuan's body spun like a top.

"Huh?"

The movements of the two swordsmen, who had already surpassed human capabilities, were imperceptible to onlookers.

But Nameless, deeply understanding the essence of external force, felt a chill for the first time in battle.

'What is this?'

Kuan's movements were ambiguous.

If his previous trajectory had been a single path, it now felt like a forked road requiring a decision.

'No, it doesn't matter. It won't change anything.'

If there were only two paths, he would suppress both.

As Nameless passed Kuan, he slashed three times in an instant, but then he was shocked.

'I've been cut?'

Turning his head in disbelief, blood spurted from a gash on his right shoulder.

The first wound he had ever experienced was intensely unpleasant and repulsive.

But his current state of mind was too confused to care about the pain.

'When did this happen?'

It was a forked road.

No, denying the result was foolish.

So the only thing to consider was…

'Another path I couldn't see?'

Was it possible?

As he pondered, Nameless saw Kuan spinning with a devilish grin.

"Heh, it was shallow. But this time, it'll be deeper."

Their swords clashed again.

In the brief moment that seemed like a single exchange, dozens of strikes were traded, and another chunk of flesh was sliced from Nameless's left thigh.

He couldn't see it.

Kuan's sword moved unpredictably.

"That can't happen…!"

The anger from suppressed ambition was as intense as the ambition itself.

As his pride was trampled, the explosive emotions from Garas's nature reached their peak.

"Nooooooo!"

As dozens of external forces pulled Nameless in different directions, his trajectory twisted wildly.

Even seeing the tangled path like a ball of yarn, Kuan didn't blink and muttered.

"…Annoying."

Clang!

The swords clashed dozens of times, but the speed was so fast that it burst into a single dissonance.

As Kuan staggered to a stop, a line of blood appeared on his back, and blood spurted out.

"Hah! Hah!"

Panting, Nameless stopped moving, and a thin red line appeared on his neck as his skin was cut.

"Damn it…!"

He had lost track of Kuan's sword path again.

But this only made Nameless doubt his certainty.

His imagination, far superior to human insight, should have overwhelmed Kuan.

"It's strangely annoying…"

Kuan had been muttering the same thing for some time.

It felt like his mind had opened again, as it had when he had risked his life against Pungjang and gained enlightenment.

"Ah, I see. Now I understand."

Kuan staggered back like a drunk.

His eyes, having emerged from deep contemplation, were half-dazed and unfocused.

"This is how it should be."

Thud!

The sound of flesh and bone being severed was heard.

Siena, who had been calling Kuan's name repeatedly, and Armin, who had sensed an opportunity and decided to flee, both went pale.

Nameless blinked as he looked at Kuan's right arm lying on the ground.

It was severed just below the shoulder.

He had cut off his own arm.

'Why?'

He vaguely understood, but the first thought that came to mind was that this was not an achievement.

'He limited his own potential. Is he insane?'

"So, how about this?"

Nameless looked up again at Kuan's voice.

Holding his sword with his remaining arm, Kuan appeared weaker than before, but the situation quickly changed.

The landscape seemed to tilt as if the ground had collapsed around Kuan.

Asymmetrical Extreme - Clown Pierrot.

"You…"

For the first time, fear appeared in Nameless's eyes.

It was an achievement toward death that only a biased human could reach.

"Why? Why?"

Plu, who had caught Crude just before he fell, carefully laid him on the ground.

His entire body was broken and twisted, making it hard to say she had saved him.

Crude's dazed eyes wandered aimlessly in the sky.

Thanks to Piper's frame, his body wasn't crushed, but the pain was even more intense.

"Why did you come for me? Why? If you were going to die like this, why did you come for me?"

Plu shouted at the dying Crude.

Even though she knew it was cruel, she couldn't contain the anger exploding from her chest.

"I… hate you so much…"

Crude spoke in a dying voice.

"I have no family."

With that short sentence, Plu's face twisted in realization.

"At least you should have a reason to die…"

"Stop! I don't want to hear that! What am I supposed to do with you saying that in this state?"

Crude gave a faint smile.

Giving everything to the woman he loved.

Even sacrificing his most precious life to remain in her heart forever.

Perhaps that was the greatest revenge.

"Plu… I love… you…"

Crude's tongue stiffened, and his eyes closed.

Plu hugged Crude's lifeless body with trembling arms.

The more she tried to hold back, the more overwhelming her emotions became, and she finally couldn't stop herself from sobbing.

"Aaaah!"

Loving someone so much that you end up hating them, hoping they would at least understand that hatred, and then dying in the end.

That was the intangible, endless nature of the human heart.

That heart weighed down on Plu's chest like a heavy stone, making it hard to breathe.
