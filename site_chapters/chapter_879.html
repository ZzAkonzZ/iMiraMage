Chapter 879: Biological Program (1)

The Kingdom of Thormia.

A nameless village in the Sanferos region.

"Quickly! Please go faster!"

A carriage was racing down a perilously steep mountain road at a terrifying speed.

Designed solely for mobility, the sleek vehicle was followed by a trailer loaded with medical equipment.

As the carriage wheels hit a rock, jolting, the medical staff inside braced against the walls.

"The road is too rough! At this speed, we're going to..."

Before the coachman could finish, Seriel pushed aside the curtain and stepped out from the cabin.

"It's okay! Please, go as fast as you can!"

Gritting his teeth, the coachman whipped the reins, and soon, a ramshackle village came into view beyond the mountain path.

Though officially part of the Kingdom of Thormia and not unnamed, the village was completely quarantined at present.

Three days ago, an unknown disease was reported that caused mutations among the villagers, and it was exactly what Seriel was looking for.

"We're here!"

The door banged open, and a man with stark white hair and Seriel leapt out as if jumping into water.

Following them, medical staff from the World Health Organization hurriedly began to gather their equipment.

Tenos, the head of the molecular biology department, was a man with stark white hair and a gruff demeanor.

"Where is the mutant?"

Soldiers in anti-contamination suits guarded the entrance.

"They're all dead, only one remains, but his condition is very poor."

"Move!"

Pushing past the soldiers, shouts came from the medical officers.

"It's not safe! Entering without a proper epidemiological investigation is..."

"Seriel! Come with me!"

"Yes!"

Taking a medical bag from another staff member, Seriel followed Tenos into the village.

"What should we do? If this spreads..."

The commander gave an order.

"Damn it! Seal the entrance. Shoot any villagers trying to escape!"

As the army loaded their bows, a line of medical staff from the World Health Organization followed inside.

In the village of 300 households, bodies were scattered haphazardly, and the eyes of the survivors were filled with terror.

"Please save us! Please let us go!" People reached out to Seriel as if escaping from the pits of hell.

But she, without even blinking, continued deeper into the village.

There were bodies there too.

'The mutation is more extensive than I thought.'

Could these still be called human?

Some had limbs transformed into insect-like appendages, while others had entire bodies that had morphed into animal forms.

"Manager! Seriel!"

Startled back to reality, she turned towards the voice calling from a cabin.

"Yes!"

Inside, a man, pale to the point of being translucent, was tied to a bed.

"Krak! Krakaa!"

He seemed to have lost his mind, his eyes unfocused, but terror was evident.

"He's still pre-transformation! Extract it!" The medical team tore off the man's clothes, and Seriel climbed on top of him.

"Krakaa! Krakaa!"

Suppressing his struggles with both hands on his sternum, she quickly examined his entire body.

'Where is it? Where?'

The point where the parasite begins to mutate the host's body is typically no more than 20 centimeters from the initial injection site.

'Found it!'

Seriel's eyes widened upon discovering an injection wound on the man's chest.

"Damn! It's the heart!"

It was likely a full-body mutation.

"Scalpel!"

As medical staff handed her a scalpel, the man's upper body thrashed even more violently.

"Krak! Save...!"

The hauntingly human scream.

Seriel bit her lip and relentlessly sliced open his chest with the scalpel.

"Krakaa!"

Blood spurted out, splashing Seriel's face with red as if brushed on.

Fortunately, the blood didn't reach her eyes, but it was still unclear how the parasite might infect others.

"OOO OOI" Inserting her hand into the man's heart, Seriel felt something unusual and pulled it out.

A mass of cells, squishy like a tumor, writhed as if alive.

"Extract!"

As the medical team placed the parasite into a metal box, it spread out like whiskers.

'It's almost instinct.'

The moment that was confirmed, the medical staff snapped the box shut, and Seriel hastily administered emergency treatment.

"I'll apply a recovery spell."

Tenos nodded and locked the box.

"Please! Please!"

While Seriel's recovery spells were well-known within the World Health Organization, it was only skin deep.

The man, drained of his strength by the parasite, looked up at Seriel, his lips moving.

"Sir, I..."

"Don't speak. Recovery is the priority now."

"I... I dreamed."

His expression accepted death, a case unique in Seriel's experience.

"It felt like my body was burning, but then suddenly the pain stopped. Was it you?"

"It's because we extracted the parasite. You can live. Just stay conscious a bit longer."

"Light was pulling me, and then I opened my eyes."

He reminisced about his dream.

"The scent was nice, the touch comforting. Things someone as poor as me could never experience."

Seriel didn't stop him.

'This man too...'

There was a commonality in the mutation reports being collected from around the world.

"That's when it occurred to me in my head. Oh, I'm rich. It was all a dream."

Tears streamed down his cheeks.

"But it was just a dream, really. Yes, I'm a poor man."

"What did you see in that dream?"

She was still casting recovery spells, but his life force was not reviving.

"A hazy sky. Maybe it was a window. Beyond the sky, something was looking down at me."

"What was it?"

"I don't know. Can't see it well. Something small, greyish... Suddenly the sky opened, and...

He came to his senses and realized he was here.

"So it was a dream. I was rich. No, this must be the dream. Sir, I want to go back."

As the man cried out in tears, Seriel grasped his face.

"Stay with me! Your wounds have healed! You can live!"

"No! It's all fake! Everything...!"

His lungs swelled rapidly, his pupils rolled up, and he stopped breathing.

Seriel attempted resuscitation, but his body temperature rapidly cooled.

"Enough, manager. Stop. Write the report."

Seriel's head dropped.

"Mutant case B-38. Argothes parasite extracted, biotic recovery completed but deceased. Cause of death...

Her eyes flashed as she looked up.

"Presumed suicide."

As the medical team quickly documented, Seriel instructed as she stepped off the bed.

"Under international convention, the corpse of the mutant is handled by the World Health Organization. Proceed discreetly. This matter is not yet to be disclosed."

The public disclosure of this fact by the Valkyrie would occur after Pisho's funeral.

As Seriel stepped out, Tenos was lighting a cigarette outside the cabin.

"Hard work."

Passing her the lit cigarette, Seriel took a deep drag.

"Why do they die?"

Tenos was silent.

"Recovery was perfect. No wounds, all bodily functions and metabolism were in order. How can a person just die?"

"Maybe it's emptiness?"

"People die just from feeling empty?"

"They shouldn't say that."

Tenos lit his own cigarette.

"You know those times? When you feel like life leaves nothing behind. Whether it's good memories or bad, once they're past, they're just neural signals in the brain."

He pointed straight ahead with the cigarette between his fingers.

"People live for the future, but in the end, only the past remains. Living fiercely just to keep those memories is inefficient."

"I didn't know you thought that way."

Tenos shrugged.

"I'm talking theoretically. Living is about experiencing sensations. But sensations are just impulses. Can that be called life? And the moment it's perceived, it's already the past."

Smoke trailed long from the cigarette.

"Past, present, future, there are no humans anywhere. The only thing that truly exists in this world is emptiness."

"That's Nane’s saying."

Tenos smiled.

"Tried to mimic Buddha, but honestly, I'm not sure. If humans are just signals from the outside, dreaming is no different. Was it really suicide? Maybe he realized there's no substance here."

"You want to call it enlightenment? Near-death experiences are personal. They can't be scientifically proven."

"So you need to find out." Tenos discarded his cigarette.

"Pisho's choice was right. Lucky to have an ivory tower star among classmates. Even the Valkyrie can't access the secrets without betraying the ivory tower. Go. Find out what Pisho discovered." Of course, that was the plan.

"Understood."

"Too slow by carriage. Use spatial displacement. It's fine to go straight after quarantine at the branch."

"Everyone involved in the Argones Project must follow the upper management's designated routes."

"I'll take responsibility. The guidelines will be lifted by tomorrow anyway. Don't worry about here, focus on Creas." Seriel had hoped for this, bowed, and cast a spatial displacement spell.

"I'll be back."

A roar split the sky.

On the day of Pisho’s funeral.

Creas City Cemetery buzzed with celebrities from all walks of life from the morning.

"There are so many people."

Having been long affiliated with the magic school, a significant crowd had gathered around the Alpheas Magic School sign. However, there were also figures seemingly unrelated to Pisho elsewhere.

Dorothy said,

"This is definitely not just a funeral. Let's not let our guard down and keep an eye on the situation."

Arin asked,

"Could there be a fight?"

"Not necessarily..."

Sabina poked her in the ribs.

"Come on, spill it. You must know something from your boyfriend, right? We're all friends here."

At the mention of a boyfriend, Dorothy's face turned red.

"I don’t know. Iruki hasn’t told me anything. He just said it could be dangerous."

Closer raised his finger.

"Over there."

The principal of the magic school, Alpheas, and Vice-Principal Olivia were talking with students.

"Principal!"

As Dorothy and the exploration team greeted him, Alpheias smiled warmly.

"You came. Thank you."

"Of course we came. But something seems off. What's going on?" "It's because of Pisho's will. There seems to be a problem we don't know about. There was even a protest a little while ago."

"A protest?"

As Dorothy turned her head, officials from foreign countries were arguing with the Valkyrie’s representatives.

"This is unreasonable. Do you know how much the Miel Kingdom has invested in the Valkyrie?"

"Please understand. As I mentioned before, neither the Bio-monstrosity Research Institute nor the Valkyrie has the authority to disclose the will."

"No, what are you talking about...!" As the official was about to cause a fuss, a knight pointed towards the entrance.

"Ambassador, over there."

As if the remark was a signal, hundreds of people simultaneously fell silent.

"The Five Stars of the Ivory Tower."

Shirone, the only person Pisho had granted reading rights to, was entering with Amy.

"Have you finally arrived?"

The one with the greatest power in the world.

Even the protesting officials had to fall silent when Shirone arrived.

As all eyes focused on one spot, Shirone walked towards Alpheas Magic School.

"Hello?"

Because Pisho had stayed in the graduating class for a long time, there were faces Shirone didn't recognize.

Seriel ran to Amy.

"Amy!"

Seeing her friend's face after a long time, Amy hugged Seriel tightly.

There was a smell of cigarettes.

"Amy! You sly girl! How could you live without a single contact? I'm disappointed!"

"Sorry. I had my reasons."

She had narrowly escaped death several times in the training camp before being dispatched to the battlefield.

But it seemed Seriel hadn't had an easy time either, as her face was much thinner than before.

"Was it hard?"

"Hehe, what's hard for me? You're the one fighting on the battlefield. By the way..."

Seriel alternated her gaze between Shirone and Amy, smiling mischievously.

"Coming in with Shirone means you were together last night too? You sly thing."

"No! Shirone came to pick me up. I tried to bring Maya too, but she had already left..."

Maya waved from behind the alumni.

"Shirone."

Their eyes met, but there seemed to be no change in emotions compared to before.

"Well, that's right."

Maya didn't know that Shirone had followed her to the hotel.

Although nothing happened, if Shirone had any feelings, it would have shown in her eyes.

‘It's okay. Because I love you. It doesn't hurt more than the pain of forgetting.’

Maya forced a smile.

Unlike the foreign officials who were cautious, the alumni treated Shirone without reserve.

"Shirone, you've become really famous, haven't you?" Screamer, the current champion of Creas Colosseum, extended his hand to Shirone.

"Oh? Screamer! It's been a long time!" During school days, his belligerent nature was overwhelming, but now he exuded the aura of a dignified fighter.

Not only the alumni but even the seniors flocked to greet Shirone, making it a hectic time.

"Shirone! Do you remember me? When you were in the advanced class, we bumped into each other at the lake garden. You even carried my books then."

"Oh, yes...

Did I?

I can't remember such trivial things, but it wasn't a situation where I could pretend not to know.

Everyone was glad, and it was reassuring to know they shared the same school roots.

"What's that? Some kind of reunion?"

The foreign officials looked bewildered.

The position of the Five Great Stars of the Ivory Tower was one that even a king had to kneel to.

They couldn't understand the sight of people teasing Shirone by tapping his head or pulling his clothes.

"Everyone likes him. I can guess what kind of school life he had."

Some officials showed respect, but most were there with the fate of their countries at stake.

"Hmph! Nonsense! This is no time to be idling. Isn't that right?"

The officials dispatched from the southern continent nodded sharply.

"There's definitely something. We must find out."

Although demons were invading the world, this wasn't the first crisis in history.

The human system was still intact,

and the war situation wasn't entirely bleak.

"After the world war, the power structure will be reorganized. This time, it's our turn."

While everyone was thinking the same, an official from the Miel Kingdom approached Shirone.

"Star of the Ivory Tower."

The noisy sound disappeared.

"As you know, you have the right to view Pisho's will."

"Yes, I heard. Where is the will?"

An employee of the Supernatural Biology Research Institute said,

"It's not a will, but a video. Pisho recorded it for you. No one except Pisho has seen this video."

"A video..."

The official from the Miel Kingdom interrupted again.

"All kingdoms must unite to fight the demons. If Pisho's will is closely related, sharing the information is the right thing to do."

An official from the Benivar Kingdom stepped forward.

"If it can't be disclosed to everyone, at least choose a representative. To add, the Benivar Kingdom is sending the most military supplies to the Holy War."

Realizing the intention to monopolize the information, officials rushed forward, shouting.

"We won't yield to Benivar. Our kingdom has sent the most troops to Valkyrie!"

"Nonsense! If you put it that way..."

As the officials argued, Shirone looked at them calmly.

When the voices grew louder and the atmosphere became chaotic, Shirone's voice was heard.

"Stop it."

Though the voice was small, the officials felt something chilling and fell silent.

The bright smile was gone, and Shirone, exuding the aura of a magician, stood there.

"Let me speak."

The officials listened.

"There are people who haven't arrived yet. People who miss Pisho. I'm the same."

Shirone looked around.

"I want to mourn my departed friend. If you don't have such thoughts..."

His cold gaze pierced the officials.

"Just stay still."

"Ugh!"

There was an irresistible force in Shirone's voice.

"Let's wait for now."

As the officials quietly backed off, Shirone smiled again and turned around.

"Let's get ready too."

Under the name of the world's strongest magician, it was clear what attitude the officials would take.

But most of those looking at Shirone were thinking something entirely different.

"You haven't changed at all."

Now they knew.

How much courage and sacrifice it took to get angry purely for others.

"Why didn't we know back then?"

Perhaps the one who had the hardest time during school days was Shirone.

"Oh? It's Iruki."

The sacred chariot arrived, and Iruki got off.

Powerful knights, whom no foreign official could command, guarded him.

Dorothy walked past them without hesitation and linked arms with Iruki, causing the students to murmur.

"What? Are they dating?"

It was the first time Shirone heard this.

"Iruki, you..."

Before she could speak, a luxurious carriage quickly arrived and stopped at the entrance.

Nade, with a coat over his shoulders, and Liz, neatly dressed in a suit, got off together.

"Shirone!"

Tears welled up in Shirone's eyes.

"Nade!"

The reunion of the trio, once called the troublemakers of the magic school, brought back nostalgic memories for the alumni.

As countless words were exchanged, Dante crossed the cemetery entrance on time.

"It's Dante."

Closer and Sabina, who were also part of the trio, brightened their eyes.

"Working at the Royal Archives? Nonsense."

Every time they visited the capital, they looked for Dante but never saw him.

Given his skills in information magic, he was probably an agent handling top-secret matters.

"Can't be upset."

So many people had gathered that there was no time to sort out emotions.

It was just nice to see everyone.

"Is everyone here now? Then let's start the funeral."

Just as the employee of the Supernatural Biology Research Institute was about to start the ceremony, a huge carriage arrived.

"What's that?"

Seeing the items loaded in the carriage, people were stunned.

It was a huge wreath made of gold.

"Is this Pisho's funeral?"

The delivery company employee asked, holding the paperwork, and the venue manager signed and asked,

"It is, but who sent it?"

"I don't know. If anonymity is requested, we have no way of knowing. Anyway, it's definitely delivered."

Since it was too heavy for a person to carry, two magicians used air magic to move the wreath.

As the surprised gazes followed, Shirone looked at the black ribbon on the wreath.

There was a short phrase.

"In memory."

"It's Fermi."

Everyone guessed.

"Who else would do such a thing? What's he up to? He used to be annoying, but now I miss him."

"He's probably raking in money somewhere. If that's pure gold, it must be worth over 2 billion gold."

Nade had heard about Fermi but it wasn't the time to bring it up with Shirone yet.

"Then let's start Pisho's funeral."

Shirone's alumni took the closest seats to the grave as the coffin without a body was brought in.

They hadn't heard why there was no body, but it seemed better not to know.

"He was an excellent magician and biologist. His sacrifice will forever remain in our hearts..."

The alumni's faces were full of sorrow, and some shed tears as the coffin was buried.

Even after the funeral ended, the lingering emotions continued, but the officials couldn't wait any longer.

"Now, please make the announcement."

The director of the Supernatural Biology Research Institute appeared, dragging a heavy iron box on a cart.

"This video recording device contains about 27 minutes of footage left by Pisho."

A heavy tension filled the air.

"We haven't checked the video either. Of course, you won't believe it, but..."

The officials nodded.

"Pisho said there would be a way to prove it in the video. We believe the Star of the Ivory Tower will verify our innocence."

Now it was up to Shirone to decide who would watch the video with him.

Seriel approached.

"Shirone, this is important for humanity. I have information too. I'll cooperate as much as possible."

Iruki and Dante also chimed in.

"Let us join too. You know it's not just curiosity."

Shirone, deep in thought, spoke.

"I'll check it first. There must be a reason why the authority was given to me. Then I'll decide."

That was the most reasonable way.

"Is that okay?" The director nodded.

"Full authority is with Shirone.”

"Where would be a good place to check? A sealed room with magic control or..."

"No. I'll just do it nearby. Anyway, I need to decide again after I see it."

No one here could access the information through Shirone's ninth sense.

"That place looks good."

It was a building 200 meters away from the cemetery where the caretakers stayed overnight.

As the director floated the iron box with magic, he pointed to the building.

"Let's go. We'll open it inside the building."

When Shirone entered the building with the director, the remaining hundreds of people waited anxiously.

Time passed endlessly.

"Damn it! What are they doing? It's supposed to be a 27-minute video. It's already been over 50 minutes."

Someone pointed to the building.

"There he comes!"

As Shirone slowly walked towards the people, there was a sound of swallowing.

Seriel asked,

"How was it, Shirone? Did you confirm Pisho's will?"

"Yes. I saw it all."

The director proceeded with the final step.

"The late Pisho said the video should only be shown to those Shirone agrees to. Now, please make a decision on the transfer of authority."

Shirone fell into careful thought.

"I will not..."

There was not even a sound of breathing.

And finally, Shirone, who had made a decision, raised his head and declared to the audience.

"I will not disclose the video to anyone."

Pisho was right.
