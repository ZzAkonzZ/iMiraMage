Chapter 881: Biological Program (3)

"What on earth are you talking about!"

The foreign officials shouted in unison.

"Can't disclose it? Do you even understand what this matter is about? If the Ivory Tower intends to monopolize the information..."

"I know what this matter is about."

Shirone interrupted.

"That's why I can't disclose it even more."

The video Pisho left was neither confidential nor mysterious.

The reason it couldn't be shared was...

"Because it's the truth."

The truth Pisho conveyed by sacrificing his own body.

Some people approach the truth through enlightenment, but sometimes that truth can be harsh.

"There will be no reversal. And the video recording device has already been destroyed. The Ivory Tower will review it and then make an announcement."

Dante raised his hand.

"Wait, the Ivory Tower will review it? Does that mean you're ignoring the state?"

"It's not ignoring. If it's just about talking, I can reveal it here. But considering its impact, it's not something that can be handled at the kingdom level."

Seriel had a rough idea but couldn't clear her doubts without hearing it directly.

"Can't you at least tell us what it's about? We have some information too."

"Sorry. Give me some time to think."

While Seriel looked dissatisfied, Iruki nodded calmly.

"I'll ask later." The statement that it couldn't be disclosed was just political rhetoric for the public.

"If it can be reported to the Ivory Tower, it can be told to me too."

He believed there was that level of trust.

"I can't accept this!"

An official, furious, shouted.

"We didn't come all this way to hear such nonsense! The whole world will condemn you!"

"I never asked you to come."

Shirone glared sharply at the official.

"Also, I don't care what actions your kingdoms take, but I advise against it."

"Hmph!"

The Ivory Tower's judgment is always right.

Not because it's actually right, but because they can enforce their will.

"Let's go back! This was a waste of time!"

The official from the Mael Kingdom was the first to turn, followed by the other officials.

Even as they left, they kept talking.

"Are we really going to back down like this? We have stakes in the Holy War too. We should file a lawsuit at the national level."

"We won't win. We have to engage in espionage. After all, there's no such thing as a perfect secret."

As the officials left, Shirone spoke.

"It's over. Let's go eat."

The teachers and students of Alpheas Magic School took their seats at a pre-booked restaurant.

It was a lively gathering, but some people were deep in thought.

As some left and others stayed, Iruki approached Shirone.

"Let's talk for a moment." Seeing Shirone follow Iruki, Seriel fell into thought.

"I need to follow too."

The thought was strong, but conditions had to be met.

"Someone who needs the information. And someone who can be trusted with it."

Amy stayed for the former reason, but Seriel couldn't meet the latter condition.

"He wouldn't want me to come."

Dante, seemingly of the same mind, stayed put, while Nade started walking.

"I need to go too."

Liz stopped him.

"Stay here. To follow them, you need to meet two qualifications."

"I know what they are."

Any magician would know.

"Then why go? You don't need to know."

Nade looked back at Liz.

"Because I'm curious." "Don't worry. The important thing is trust. I can just listen and keep quiet."

"Even to me?"

Nade blinked.

"Won't you tell me? Are you sure you can do that?"

"Uh, well... you won't ask, right?"

Liz gritted her teeth.

"Just stay put."

Nade licked his lips and returned to his seat.

Shirone and Iruki entered the forest near the cemetery and sat on a rock.

"What happened?"

Shirone understood Iruki's question perfectly but just smiled.

"What was recorded in Pisho's video? Are you saying you can't even tell me?"

"Yes. Sorry."

Watching Iruki's reaction, Shirone changed his expression and laughed.

"Just kidding."

"I know. It wasn't funny at all."

The Spirit Zone unfolded.

Since Shirone's mind was the strongest barrier, Iruki felt comfortable asking.

"What is it?"

Shirone moved to sit next to Iruki.

"The truth of this world." Three hours earlier.

The director who installed the video recording device checked one last time before leaving the building.

"It's all set. Is this place really okay?"

"Yes, it's fine."

After the director left, Shirone used his material ability to create a new wall.

It was a sealed room where even air couldn't pass.

"Pisho."

After creating a small chair, Shirone activated the video recording device, and the recorded video started.

"Shirone."

Pisho was there.

Of course, it wasn't the appearance Shirone remembered from school days.

"Sorry to surprise you. There wasn't much time. I had to solve one more problem. You understand, right?"

Shirone nodded.

"I'll keep it short. The reason I gave you access to the video isn't because you're one of the Five Great Stars or my classmate. It's because you're Hexa."

Pisho's left side twitched.

"Humanity has uncovered a lot. We roughly know the principles by which the world operates. But there are still unanswered questions. Where exactly is this place?"

Why do we exist?

"The fact that something exists is very strange. If something exists, it means there's a cause."

Who created the universe?

"Alien life form Argones. The parasite occupying my body. It's telling me this."

Pisho pointed to the twitching part.

"There was never a universe to begin with. Therefore, we don't exist either. There was never an event where something existed somewhere. Shirone, it's just nothing."

"From the beginning, there was nothing, nothing happened, and it's the same now. 'Existence' doesn't exist."

"Then why...?"

The Pisho in the video answered.

"Then why do we exist? Why does it feel like there's a universe? It's an error because we don't fully understand the concept of nothing."

Shirone remained silent.

"There's nothing. Really nothing. We just mistakenly think there's something. Who exactly? The subject of that mistake doesn't exist either. That's how it is, Shirone."

Pisho raised his hand with difficulty.

"To explain nothing, you have to borrow the concept of something. That's where it goes wrong. Time, space, illusions. If you move a pencil on a desk to another place, does the spot on the desk become nothing? Absolutely not. Even the thought of moving something is something. Humans can't truly imagine nothing. The limit we can approach is knowing that we can't imagine it. That's the end."

Shirone imagined nothing.

Through logical and conceptual combination, he could envision a perfect state of nothing, but it wasn't truly nothing.

"To truly imagine it..."

Since even the self imagining it must not exist, it was impossible to imagine from the start.

"I call this infinite nothing."

Pisho gave an embarrassed smile.

"It's a concept we can't even approach, but we're human, so we gave it a name. Anyway, Shirone, if you believe me, you'll understand."

Pisho's eyes changed.

"There's nothing, only illusions. It's not a contradiction. Even the fact that you're mistaken at this moment is an illusion. There's just nothing. You need to know that."

Pisho's shoulder twitched more violently.

"Everything exists within that illusion."

Just as humans can't imagine nothing, they can't imagine everything existing either.

"Everything is a matter of perspective. The difference in perspective on nothing separates good and evil, merit and love, countless things. In reality, there's nothing. Yet, the reason I'm giving this video to you and not someone else is..."

Pisho's shoulder exploded with a pop.

"Illusions are free."

Shirone captured his bitter smile.

"You can refer to the report from the Supernatural Biology Research Institute for information on Argones. The problem is... this thing knows too much. By human standards. And for some reason, it seems to have a mission to erase any creature that approaches this secret."

If the video were made public, Argones would erase all humanity.

"There's no time. Although it's suppressed with drugs, it will wake up soon. Shirone, you have to choose. Will all humanity fight, or will you fight alone?"

As Pisho's mutation accelerated, his head bent sideways as if it would break.

"Damn it..."

The fact that he couldn't help was heartbreaking.

"Argones is the source of all life. But you're different. You're free from its control."

Because he's Hexa.

"Whether it's infinite nothing or infinite something, it doesn't matter. Even if it's an illusion, it's the world I was in. So..."

"Pisho!"

As Pisho's eyes rolled up, Shirone ran to the video.

"I've definitely... conveyed it to you. Shirone..."

Finally, Pisho's neck completely broke, and the mutated cells drooped and stretched.

"Ugh! Ugh!"

Shirone clung to the video, crying.

"No. No..."

The reason there was no body in the coffin.

When the host died, the cells quickly melted and spread like porridge.

"Ha."

Returning to his seat weakly, Shirone bowed his head in deep thought.

"Why is that important?"

Pisho's message revealed the truth of the world, but it seemed nothing would change anyway.

"Why does Argones try to erase humans who approach this secret?" The key to the outside world.

"Infinite. The infinite magician."

Can't imagine a state of nothing?

For Shirone, who had no further progress after reaching the ninth sense, a chilling feeling was conveyed.

"Wait a minute... could this be..."

The screen reflecting in Shirone's retinas flickered.

Lost in thought, Shirone suddenly lifted his head.

"What?"

In the still unfinished video, Pisho's cells, which had turned into a gelatinous substance, began to wriggle.

They became more active, as if alive, and the surface bulged.

"Is there more to the video?"

Finally, the gelatinous substance, taking on a human form, dripped liquid as it moved towards the screen. The place where a mouth should be opened into a round shape, then stretched and a voice emerged.

"Shi...ro...ne..."

It was Argones, the father of all life.
