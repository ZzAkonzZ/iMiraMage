Chapter 1047: New Humanity (2)

The snowy trail that ran through Laviette’s city now had a long, deep groove, as if a giant snowball had rolled across the ground.

Rumble!

With a deafening sound reminiscent of thunder, Son Yu-jung was sent flying from one end of the city to the other.

“Ugh… damn it…”

Embedded between the ground and a wall, she glared forward with her head bent awkwardly.

A red-skinned demon was approaching with his hands behind his back.

“This is driving me insane.”

Through her blazing Fiery Golden Eyes, Son Yu-jung saw Shirone’s figure, as if she were facing an overwhelming, unstoppable truth.

“Kiieeek!”

Distorting her face in anger, she unleashed a threatening growl, but Shirone only clicked his tongue.

“Still haven’t learned your lesson?”

“What did I even do wrong?! I’m just living my life the way I want! Why the hell are you all meddling?! I came to save my friend! I don’t care about this damned Hell!”

“That’s exactly the problem. You create chaos without thinking, worsening every situation.”

“What nonsense are you spouting?! You act however you want too! Nane, Ymir, they all do whatever they please with their power, so why can’t I?!”

“‘Living the way you want,’ huh…”

Shirone stopped walking and looked down at Son Yu-jung.

“Or is it that you don’t know what you should be doing?”

“You lack conviction. You’re a bomb—neither good nor evil wants to keep you around.”

Her power was enough to destroy Laviette’s entire city.

“Because you lack conviction, you’re easily swayed by others’ emotions. People will either try to manipulate or control you. You’ll always be surrounded by those kinds of people.”

Richelra, who had remained silent during the overwhelming battle, briefly showed a nervous expression.

“So what? And you’re any different? What’s so special about you or Buddha? Even now, I’m still wearing this damned Geingoa chain around my neck, so what’s with the preaching?”

“You might think that way for now. Help me purify Hell. If you can find enlightenment in the process, you’ll gain true freedom.”

Son Yu-jung snapped back defiantly, “I’m already free! I can do whatever I want! If it weren’t for this damned chain!”

Her fighting spirit reignited, and her body turned red-hot. Shirone sighed deeply.

He could finally understand Nane’s reasoning.

‘If someone with ill intent tries to exploit her, she’ll become a deadly variable. I have to restrain her now.’

Son Yu-jung bared her teeth.

“Grrrr!”

Although she was ready to fight, she wasn’t sure what more she could do against Yahweh.

‘What do I do? She’s using some weird techniques. Honestly, this is even more annoying than Nane’s sermons.’

Through her Fiery Golden Eyes, she noticed something lurking in the shadows, watching them.

‘Huh?’

It was two of the Seven Generals—Ilgak, an ogre with long horns, and Baeksa, a woman with white snake-like skin.

‘So that weird feeling was coming from them. If they want to fight, they should just fight. Why keep watching?’

Realizing that they had been monitoring even Shirone’s thoughtful expression, Son Yu-jung saw a glimmer of hope for escape.

‘Right. I was wondering why Yahweh disguised himself as a demon—he’s wary of those guys.’

According to Richelra, Yahweh was the most hated being among the demons.

‘I don’t remember the incantation for the Geingoa chain.’

Having made up her mind, she rose shakily to her feet and shot into the sky.

“Hehehe! Just try and catch me!”

Even as her voice quickly faded into the distance, Shirone’s expression didn’t change.

“Come back.”

As soon as he uttered those words, smoke shot up like a meteor and crashed back to the ground.

A scream followed.

“Aaaah! Damn it! You damn Yahweh!”

Son Yu-jung, clutching the golden ring around her neck, writhed on the ground in agony.

“What the hell?! Why is this happening?!”

Richelra, who was tumbling alongside her, froze in fear at the sight of Son Yu-jung’s face.

Her veins were bulging, and a storm of emotions swirled in her fiery eyes.

Shirone muttered again.

“Son Yu-jung.”

“Argh! Stop it! Please stop!”

His voice was small, but it felt as if the entire world was vibrating around him.

“I’ll give you three seconds. One… two…”

“Eeeeeek!”

As soon as the pain vanished, Son Yu-jung leaped up and charged at Shirone.

She landed heavily, kneeling on one knee, panting heavily.

“Hah… hah…”

For the first time, she saw Shirone’s true form, fully manifested as Yahweh.

“You… weren’t even trying to hide it?”

Shirone gave a bitter smile.

“Yeah. You’ve ruined everything, as always. So now you have to help me.”

“I… I don’t want to… I said no!”

As Shirone narrowed his eyes, Son Yu-jung flinched.

“Eek!”

Geingoa, a weapon that responds to the true will of those who have attained enlightenment, tightened its grip on her.

Even without using forbidden magic, every word Shirone spoke carried the weight of divine law.

Son Yu-jung stretched out her hand.

“Stop! I told you not to do this!”

“Then listen to me. From now on, you’ll refrain from reckless behavior and help me with my task. Got it?”

Shirone glanced at the shadowy corner.

‘They’re gone.’

When he had revealed herself as Yahweh, the unmistakable killing intent had filled the city, but it had vanished in an instant.

‘Not stopping me immediately must have been a strategic decision. Still, keeping Son Yu-jung by my side is better than letting her go.’

Just as her thoughts ended, Son Yu-jung, eyes wild, charged again at terrifying speed.

“Fine! Let’s die together, you bastard!”

“Tsk.”

With a simple click of his tongue, the Geingoa chain tightened, and Son Yu-jung’s eyes widened.

“Guh!”

Even her staff was flung aside as she clutched her neck and collapsed.

“No! No way! Get this thing off me! Just kill me instead!”

As Shirone watched her coldly, Son Yu-jung eventually broke down in tears.

“Okay! I was wrong! I’ll do whatever you say, just please stop!”

Part of Shirone felt pity for her.

“You’re not like Havitz. You’re still a blank slate, undecided on your path. That’s probably why Nane found it regrettable.”

Had it been deemed that she was beyond redemption, Nane would have eliminated her long ago.

“Get up.”

With a tear-streaked face, Son Yu-jung stood and wiped her eyes. Shirone gave her instructions.

“For now, just follow my orders. You’ll understand why soon enough.”

“Understood. So, what do I do now? Should I start by taking off my clothes?”

As Son Yu-jung made a teasing gesture to undress, Shirone raised an eyebrow.

“Cut it out.”

“Eek!”

Feeling the Geingoa tightening slightly, she quickly knelt on one knee in fear.

“Stop picking up bad habits. You have a mission to change the flow of the era. Don’t turn away from your responsibility.”

“Tch.”

Son Yu-jung clicked her tongue in annoyance.

‘I thought I could talk my way out of this. Turns out Yahweh’s no different from those stuck-up monks.’

Shirone turned toward Magritte’s castle.

She turned away.

‘Lette must have heard by now. That makes the fortress the most dangerous place.’

Behind her, Son Yu-jung could be felt sneaking away quietly, like a cat.

“Son Yu-jung.”

She flinched, shoulders jerking.

“Yes? Yes!”

“Go to that fortress. Don’t fight the demons, just follow me directly.”

“Understood.”

Son Yu-jung replied sullenly, while Shirone spread her wings of light and soared into the sky.

‘Wait for me, Rian.’ As the roar of Shirone’s departure echoed, Richelra, who was strapped to Son Yu-jung’s waist, shouted urgently.

“This is our chance! Let’s escape quickly! While we’re at it, let’s head straight for the flames of hell and purify—Ow! Owwww!”

In a burst of frustration over all the indignities she had suffered, Son Yu-jung grabbed his nose and twisted it hard.

Blood dripped from Richelra’s nose.

“If we could escape, I’d have done it already. Even from a hundred thousand miles away, she’d hunt me down.”

In the end… they were caught.

“Arghhhh! This is driving me nuts! I’m going to lose my mind!”

As she leapt into the sky, a trail of smoke stretched behind her like the tail of a comet.

Meanwhile, back in the command tent, the atmosphere was so oppressive from the killing intent radiating off the Seven Generals that it was hard to breathe.

Execution Commander Itaka asked, “Are you saying Yupi was Yahweh all along?”

Her pupils absorbed all light, and her hair writhed like a living thing.

“One-horned” Ik said, “I saw it with my own eyes. We lost contact after she entered the city, and now it turns out she was pulling this kind of trick.”

Moo-nom, who had been sitting in the corner, dazed, glanced sideways at Oris.

“But what’s the reason? Why would Yahweh bother disguising himself as a demon and fighting intruders?”

“Most likely… it was the Grand Duke’s orders.”

Kimara spoke up, “Currently, the head of the Fire Management Company is staying in the city. And it seems the Grand Duke is inclined to protect Yahweh.”

“Lette is?” Itaka’s hair stretched rapidly toward Magritte’s fortress.

Her eyes widened, and for the first time, she rose from the executioner’s seat.

“Mother of Hell, welcome.”

As everyone turned their heads, the tent flaps opened, and Lette appeared.

All seven generals adopted the same posture as Lette took in the mood of the room.

“Such an oppressive atmosphere. What business could require all seven generals to exude this level of killing intent?”

Everyone waited for Itaka’s response, but she remained silent, unwilling to risk disrespect.

‘The Grand Duke… seems to be protecting Yahweh.’

Expecting the Seven Generals’ reaction, Lette spoke coldly.

“Explain. I’ve already informed the Grand Duke, and there will be no more mercy.”

‘She means it.’

Kimara trembled, sensing a completely different aura from the Lette they had encountered at the fortress.

“Fine. Let’s start by eliminating the Execution Commander.” As Lette raised her hand, the purifying flames of Hell blazed intensely.

‘The Mother of Hell.’

While all demons inherit Satan’s traits, Lette has the ability to reduce any demon to their primal essence.

“Yahweh…”

Before her elbow could fully extend, Itaka fell to the ground and raised her head.

“Yahweh deceived us.” The purifying flames vanished.

Meanwhile, Rian let out a guttural scream.

“Arghhhhh!”

The Chains of Gehenna erupted from his body, as though trying to devour him whole.

“Endure it. You made the choice. This is the fastest method we have.”

Normally, binding the Chains of Gehenna to 〈Idea〉 would take three full days.

Thus, they used a random-sequence method—continuously drawing out the chains and spinning the karma code until a connection was established.

“Ughhhhh…”

Hearing Rian’s groans subside, Magritte felt a shiver of pure awe.

‘What an extraordinary body.’

The pain Rian was enduring was greater than anything even the denizens of Hell had experienced.

‘Humans truly are strange creatures.’

How could anyone not resent Shirone, who had subjected Rian to such unreasonable suffering?

‘Hang on, Shirone! I’m coming!’

With chains wrapped tightly around his entire body, Rian’s eyes burned with fiery determination.

This time, even he couldn’t suppress his scream.

“Arghhhhh!”

With a metallic grinding sound, the chain unraveled from his hand and coiled around the handle of the great sword, merging with it.

As 〈Idea〉 glowed a fierce red, Magritte exclaimed in awe.

“It worked!”

Had it been any ordinary weapon, it would have melted in seconds, but 〈Idea〉 remained unbroken.

“It doesn’t shatter.”

That was Ozent’s conviction.

Clutching the handle of the great sword tightly, Rian felt the explosive heat coursing through it.

Magritte raised both hands in alarm.

“Wait! Not here!”

Before he could finish, 〈Idea〉 shot forward, embedding itself in the wall of the lab.

“Huff!” Rian’s eyes widened as an immense energy surged through the chain connection.

“Yaaaahhhhh!”

The molten, glowing wall evaporated completely, exposing the underground landscape.

‘Incredible purifying power.’ Magritte trembled.

‘It’s possible. With this, we can cleanse the true nature of negative karma.’

In other words…

‘The spiritual realm will be opened.’
