Chapter 664:

"That's not allowed."

Before Rian could respond, Shirone firmly cut in.

"The judges are us, and the recruits are chosen by us. It's you, Mr. Wigg, who needs to demonstrate your skill—not Rian. If you don't like those terms, you're free to withdraw from the evaluation."

Every word was right, and Wigg found himself at a loss for words.

But having already drawn his swords, his pride wouldn’t let things end like this. He was certain Rian would respond differently than Shirone.

"Let's say the mage thinks so—what about the Knight of Mach? Does he think the same?"

Not all knights are muscleheads, but they are people who will throw away any sense of efficiency in the name of conviction.

Now that Wigg had made a name for himself as a rising star among swordsmen, there was no way he could back down after being challenged.

"Of course not," Rian said.

Wigg's lips curled into a grin.

"But if my liege refuses, I won't do it."

Even Wigg understood the weight of a knight's oath.

"Then let me put it differently. If you back down here, you’ll be disgracing your liege’s honor."

Rian smirked.

"You… haven't sworn the knight's oath yet, have you?"

"That’s true."

"A sword does not judge. If Shirone tells me to cut, I’ll cut. If he says to be cut, I’ll be cut. No matter what words you use to sway my emotions, my will is not my own."

Wigg slowly lowered his swords.

"Mr. Wigg, I understand how you feel, but you need to assess the situation properly. This is my mercenary corps, and I decide who passes or fails. If you really want to face Rian..."

"If I really want to?"

"Then prove to me why you're a swordsman I absolutely need on my team. If you make me think I must take you with me no matter what, then I might grant you a real duel with Rian."

"...Understood."

Wigg bowed deeply, accepting the reasoning.

When he raised his head again, the carefree smile was gone, and a chill had settled over his expression.

"Then allow me to demonstrate my swordsmanship. I am Wigg of the Whirlwind."

If his earlier provocations were meant to draw attention, they worked. The three judges now watched him coldly, intent on gauging just how good he really was.

Dual blade techniques began.

After showing some basics, Wigg swiftly transitioned into a flurry of slashes, slicing in all directions with dazzling movement.

He ran, jumped, spun, and landed—yet the arc of his blades never faltered. If anything, they grew faster.

‘Impressive agility.’

Wigg was capable of operating four schemas, which ranked him among the top even among swordsmen.

Those without schema awareness can't even tell what their bodies are doing during a simple downward slash.

That’s why Rian had practiced countless times in front of a mirror, correcting errors by sheer repetition.

But if someone can detect changes in four distinct areas of the body simultaneously, they could likely tell which of their ten toes had more weight on them and in what order.

‘This should be enough.’

That was the extent of raw talent.

Wigg now fully activated his schema.

'Mitochondria Build.'

This schema enhanced general physical function and had strengths in balance.

It was divided into regeneration and acceleration. Judging from his speed, Wigg had clearly chosen acceleration.

‘Schema fold.’

Schema folding focuses control by compressing the schema. Reaching seven folds—the theoretical human limit—yields an astounding 128x efficiency.

From that point on, it enters the realm of gods. Since the legendary swordmaster Kadel reached it 300 years ago, no one else has succeeded.

Wigg’s limit was three folds, and as he folded the Mitochondria schema, every movement whipped up a strong wind.

"Whoa..."

Even Shirone couldn’t help but be impressed this time.

His fundamentals far exceeded those of the bandits, and with his efficiency now eight times higher, his movements left afterimages despite Shirone’s enhanced senses via Armand.

‘This ends it.’

As his centrifugal force built up, Wigg began to spin like a vortex.

With all muscular, neural, and sensory systems under simultaneous control, a terrifying whirlwind surged across the clearing.

When he came to a stop before the judges, a piercing shockwave sliced through their ears like awls.

"That’s it."

Wigg calmly sheathed his swords and stepped back to his original position.

'Rian...'

Shirone waited for a careful judgment.

Even someone like him, not well-versed in swordsmanship, could see Wigg was extraordinary. Which made Rian’s opinion all the more important.

"Brilliant technique. The name Wigg of the Whirlwind is well-earned."

"Thank you."

Wigg sheathed his swords behind his back and asked,

"I’d also like to hear your thoughts, Shirone. Did I pass?"

"...Yes."

Having emphasized fairness all along, Shirone couldn’t lie now.

"Then let me offer my condition. If you really want me in the mercenary corps, there's something we must address. You're the commander, sure—but I can’t risk my life under a deputy who’s weaker than me."

Shirone scratched his cheek awkwardly.

He hadn’t seen all the candidates yet, but aside from Kuan, Wigg might be the strongest of them.

"Shirone, no need to hesitate," Rian said.

"If he’s someone you need, then use him. That’s all that matters to me."

Rian stood up, as if he already knew the answer.

"And no swordsman follows a weak commander anyway."

Sensing Rian’s resolve, Shirone nodded.

"Alright. I permit a real duel. It might be presumptuous of me, but please don’t let this interfere with the mission. I trust you can handle it professionally."

Wigg agreed readily, but inside, his thoughts were different.

‘It’s because I’m a pro that I won’t hold back.’

He had joined Shirone’s mercenary corps to raise his fame—half-hearted effort was never an option.

"We don’t have much time."

Rian slowly raised his greatsword and stepped into the clearing.

"I’ll entertain you for five minutes."

‘He’s seriously underestimating me. Living off your friend’s fame, huh?’

Wigg of the Whirlwind and the Knight of Mach had both risen to prominence around the same time.

The only reason Rian could evaluate him now was because he served Shirone, the Ivory Tower candidate.

‘But do you know? From now on, even your precious master won’t be able to protect you.’

With a soft sound, Wigg drew his twin blades, and Rian pointed his greatsword with one hand.

"Come at me."

His brute strength was clear, but there were no immediately noticeable advantages.

‘Plenty of openings.’

Wigg had reached this level through natural talent. Rather than caution, aggression took over, and he activated his schemas and charged.

"Haah!"

It was an entirely different level of precision compared to his earlier demo. Even Rian looked surprised as he twisted his body to dodge.

‘What the—?’

He had prepared for a counter, but even his initial reaction was slightly off.

‘Don’t tell me… he’s actually just all talk?’

Wigg launched a barrage, his whirlwind swordsmanship relentlessly hammering Rian.

Rian barely blocked or dodged, but his movements were technically unrefined—it became obvious almost instantly.

‘This is ridiculous.’

That clumsiness actually surprised Wigg.

‘He can’t use schema?’

Schema is essential for controlling the body with precision.

Even a simple sword swing requires managing balance, posture, and power distribution across the muscles—but watching Rian, it seemed like he was just reacting on instinct.

‘This guy is supposed to be the Knight of Mach? Can he even see me?’

Beyond four schema layers, efficiency starts to drop by half—but even so, to someone like Wigg who could operate four schemas, Rian’s swordplay looked like that of a novice.

‘His blade path changes every swing. He doesn’t even know how he’s cutting.’

A sloppy fight always leaves a bitter aftertaste.

Frustration rising, Wigg closed in to finish it.

"That’s five minutes," Rian said.

Wigg scoffed.

‘What a joke.’

That was plenty of time to expose the Knight of Mach as a fraud.

"Haaaah!"

Wigg charged with his twin blades in a horizontal stance—only for Rian’s body to spin at a completely different speed from before.

It was perfect.

It was a word that flashed through Wigg’s mind like lightning—too fleeting a moment to define in language. A sudden sense of dread welled up inside him.

‘Only this technique, right now, is perfect.’

Embodiment.

A state in which, through endless repetition, the body absorbs a movement entirely.

Just as most adults have “embodied” the act of walking, Wigg had practiced this motion as many times as walking—making it possible now.

‘Block it—!’

That was his instinct.

But it was also Wigg’s only mistake—and he would have no second chance.

The flat of the greatsword struck his twin blades, and the next thing he knew, consciousness was gone.

In the fading moments before blackout, he realized one thing: the speed of the strike couldn’t be explained by schema—and if he flew any farther, he would die.

‘So this is Mach…’

Even as his body shot through the air like an arrow and slammed into the wall of a building, no scream escaped him. Cracks spread across the stone like spiderwebs, and Wigg dropped beneath them.

“Mr. Wigg!”

Shirone rushed out and checked his condition—thankfully, he was still breathing.

Rian sheathed his greatsword and spoke.

“I pulled his neck before he passed out. If the impact had been just a moment later, he would’ve died.”

His skull would’ve shattered.

“Sorry. I couldn’t hold back.”

“No, honestly, this was for the best. I wanted a clear pecking order before we deployed anyway.”

Though there was no injury and Wigg had been knocked out in a single blow, Rian didn’t feel good as he returned to his seat.

‘I only won.’

He tried to tell himself that winning was everything—but fighting someone like Wigg, who relied on technical skill, always left a bitter taste.

“Shirone, let’s keep going.”

As Wigg was carried off by guild members, the evaluations continued.

From the Silver Ring side, Kon was accepted. One candidate from War Wagon and one from Blood Rose also passed.

An archer, a survival and traps expert, a spearman, and a healing mage were added, and at last the mercenary corps began to take solid shape.

Candidate #199, Kuan, didn’t even need to draw his sword—his presence alone knocked Aria off her chair. Finally, the last applicant for the second round, Meirei, stepped in.

Shirone, who had been curious about her for a while, paused the evaluation to ask a question.

“You said you can hear Ra’s voice, right?”

“Yes.”

“Can you hear it right now?”

Without answering, Meirei covered her ears with both hands and tilted her head toward the sky.

“…Yes, I can hear it.”

No one knew why Ra’s voice drifted as electrical signals, but right now Shirone was simply curious what he was saying.

“Ra’s voice is never clear. It’s always mixed with countless noises.”

“What kind of noises?”

“Screams.”

Meirei furrowed her brow, analyzing the sound.

“Screams of pain, roars of rage… He is always…”

Her eyebrows twitched, and then she stopped speaking.

“What’s wrong?”

“Just a moment. He’s speaking.”

Slowly raising her eyelids, Meirei relayed what she had heard.

“Carte Mu Cielle, Cartision Be Rabecca Furvel.”

Aria tilted her head.

“I don’t understand. I’ve never heard those words before.”

“It’s an ancient language. Very old. It means…”

“If you want to find me, seek the hell within you.”
