Chapter 1062: High Gear (1)

As soon as the Operator ended the voice call, Shirone turned and said,

"Let's go. We need to meet them right now."

Fermi realized something.

"You've never done this before, have you? The Operator's level is 487. They're ranked 4th overall. Meanwhile, you'll be starting from level 1. Do you think you can use magic in High Gear?"

"I have a rough idea. But we won't know until I actually fight."

Magneti's mouth hung open in surprise.

"Alright, listen."

Fermi sighed and explained.

"In High Gear, you get bonus stats as your level increases. You can level up by hunting creatures, which usually correlates with playtime. Because of the death penalty, it's beneficial to hunt creatures lower than your level. But the Operator is both a user and an operator. Do you know what that means?"

"They have less playtime?"

"Yes. Much less compared to other users. Yet they maintain a rank of 4th overall. This means they hunt extremely efficiently, and it also means they're not just relying on level and equipment. Of course, their equipment is top-tier, and they have 487 bonus stats."

Shirone nodded.

"I know it's a disadvantage. But we need to defeat the Operator to mine the Abyss."

"No, it's not just a disadvantage…"

Fermi stopped speaking.

He could say this because he had turned many impossibilities into possibilities before.

'Gritting your teeth in High Gear is useless. It's all about the system's numbers.'

No matter how desperate you are, the stats don't change.

"Fine, just play first. Once you're inside, your thoughts will change."

Shirone turned to Magneti.

"How do I get in?"

"I'll send you to the tutorial, but first, you need to create a user code. Please tell me your desired code name. However, if another user has already taken it, you can't use it unless it's a special case."

Since he wouldn't be in this world for long, the code name didn't seem to matter much.

"Well, anything will do…"

"It's best to choose your code name carefully. In High Gear, 80% of customization features are free content, so it's often hard to identify someone just by appearance. Your code name becomes your face."

Fermi added,

"Pick something you like. It's not just about feeling good; it's an important element in High Gear too."

"Then I'll go with Shirone."

Fermi wanted to advise against using his real name but knew it wasn't entirely uncommon.

"Alright. Let me check."

Magneti manipulated the screen and shook her head.

"It's a duplicate code. There's already a user with the code name Shirone."

"What?"

Shirone was baffled.

"Someone's using my name?"

"It's common for people to borrow famous names. For them, it becomes an alias. A name like Shirone would trade at a high value."

"Even trading…"

"If you really want your real name, how about adding numbers? Like Shirone1234."

That didn't seem right.

"I'm Shirone; I don't want to use a fake name. How about Arian Shirone?"

"Let me check."

Again, she shook her head.

"It's a duplicate code."

Just as he was about to lose his temper, Fermi suggested,

"What about Yahweh? It will surely be taken too, but at least you can add numbers since it's unique."

Shirone seriously considered it even though Fermi just wanted him not to use his real name.

Before he could decide, Magneti checked.

"It's a duplicate code too. Incredible! Yahweh1 through Yahweh12 are all taken. How about Yahweh13?"

Who are these people?

"If there are this many with the name Yahweh, how many users does High Gear have?"

Fermi explained,

"There are quite a few but not as many as you think. Those are all ghost users. They bring real people here and preemptively take names—vagrants or heavily indebted people. When a user dies in reality, the preemption is lifted."

Shirone's eyes narrowed.

"They kill people just to sell a code name?"

"Who knows if it's just for that…"

Magneti said,

"If you don't like numbers, I can suggest codes that include 'Yahweh' based on unused patterns we've analyzed from users."

Magneti read from the list of suggestions.

"I'm the real Yahweh. YahwehStrongMan. YahwehJustRules. FemaleYahweh. DisgustingYah…"

"Stop."

Magneti nodded understandingly.

"It's incredibly popular. If these are the suggested codes, all usable names must be taken already. It shows how famous it is too."

Fermi pointed out,

"The top one isn't bad: I'm the real Yahweh. The fourth one, FemaleYahweh, is also good conceptually. You can change gender with paid content."

"No way."

Fermi chuckled.

"Well, it seems you're getting immersed in High Gear, so that's not bad. How about this method? You can buy a Platinum code name."

Magneti quickly added,

"Platinum code names are paid content. They appear in gold, so it doesn't matter if they're duplicates. Of course, there's still fierce competition, but the high price means you have a good chance of getting a decent code name."

"How much is it?"

"100 million galaxies."

It was the amount Fermi received as a referral reward.

"Plus, you can only buy it once. As I said, your code name is your face in High Gear."

"Hmm. How much is 100 million galaxies worth? I can't really grasp it."

"It's not low. The 100 million galaxies Son of Yolga received is the highest referral reward. The amount varies based on the referrer's rank."

Magneti raised her index finger.

"But the reason 100 million isn't necessarily high is that value varies greatly depending on the standard. For example, if you're starving, even 1-point food would be fine. But there are 100 million-point foods in the world. When rarity is the standard, there's no limit to luxury. It's the same in High Gear; even slight performance differences can make something worth 100 million times more."

Fermi said,

"Go with Platinum. It's the money I got as a referral reward, so it's right to use it for you. But to make a 'normal currency exchange,' you need to clear certain sections. More importantly, if your goal is to defeat the Operator, 100 million galaxies won't even be enough for snacks."

"Haha! That's true."

Magneti smiled with her eyes and then quickly hid her expression.

"Alright, I'll search for a Platinum code."

She narrowed her eyes.

"Oh? Yahweh is already taken, but Yahweh2 is available. Yahweh3 and Yahweh4 are taken, but Yahweh2 is open…"

Fermi said,

"That means the user must have died."

"Yes. Many codes have been deleted since the spiritual realm opened. Of course, preemptions have also increased. This one was probably deleted recently."

Fermi suggested to Shirone,

"How about this one? Even if it's not the original, it's worth investing 100 million galaxies."

Shirone's eyes widened.

"Yahweh2…"

He began to understand why people were so obsessed with code names.

Magneti consoled him.

"It might not be ideal, but there's hope. You can become a ranker in the duel category. If you make it into the top 100, you get the privilege of changing your code name. Even if it's someone else's code name, rankers get exclusive rights."

"Alright, I'll go with Yahweh2. I don't care about the code name; just let me in."

Fermi checked his wristwatch.

"Then I'll log in now. After the tutorial, come straight to the city of Atogram. Let's meet at the Freedom Square tomorrow evening."

Since it was a world without magic, it would take at least half a day to travel there.

Fermi requested Magneti,

"I'll go first. Send me to the memory of my avatar. Code name Son of Yolga."

"Transmitting. The last remembered location is the Thunder Mountains, the Graveyard of Giant Machines."

A circular blue light formed under Fermi's feet, and his body disintegrated into the air.

As Shirone watched in amazement, Magneti spoke, and he turned to her.

"Registering Yahweh2 as your user code. You'll be automatically transferred once the avatar scan is complete."

A circular ring also formed under Shirone's feet, but it didn't disintegrate as quickly as Fermi's.

He felt a strange sense of restraint.

"This feels uncomfortable."

"You're sensitive. Users connecting to High Gear have special coding in their avatars. Without it, you can't link simulation stats. Avatars also have restrictions. It's a kind of lock; without it, events can't occur. Also, if you destroy the system intentionally, you'll be considered an illegal user and lose all rights, so be careful."

"Does that happen often?"

"No. In High Gear's history, no user has destroyed the system with avatar techniques. But this time is different."

He was the real Yahweh.

As the explanation ended, Shirone's avatar began to dissolve like bubbles.

Just before leaving, he remembered something.

"Um, Magneti. Thanks for earlier. Because of you, I could contact the Operator…"

"You're doing it again."

Magneti leaned on the table and smiled with her eyes.

"I told you, this is a fake world. Fake names, fake faces, all sorts of lies. If you overlook that, you'll struggle no matter how strong you are."

"But you understood my sincerity. You must be a good person in reality too."

"…That's an honor."

Knowing she shouldn't, she spoke.

"Shirone, I think this way. Even though the world is fake, the people here are real. In reality, we're bound by many constraints. We hide and decorate a lot to avoid tarnishing our names and faces."

"But here, there's none of that. High Gear reveals the true nature of living beings. Don't expect things like today. Live thoroughly as Yahweh2. So please… no matter what happens."

"I hope you don't get hurt."

"Ah…"

Shirone's voice faded into the air as particles of light ascended to the ceiling.

In the empty castle, Magneti's voice echoed.

"New user registration complete."
