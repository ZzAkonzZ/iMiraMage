Chapter 1145: Law War (4)

Fire Management Company – System Control Division.

The System Control Division was as vast as the scale of the Under World itself.

Beyond countless buildings such as warehouses and engine rooms, a modern structure loomed in the distance.

Richella spoke.

“That should be the headquarters of the System Control Division. Be careful. There are many administrators.”

Son Yujeong swung her twin Yeoui rods.

“Hmph. Administrators or whatever, I’ll crush them all. They dared to kidnap my friend?”

“No, if they got a job there, it wouldn’t be considered kidnapping, would it? Maybe your friend—”

“Shut up! Do you want to die?”

Son Yujeong lifted the chains wrapped around her waist and shouted directly into Richella’s ear.

For someone who was nothing but a face, it was a form of torture, yet no one sympathized.

“Damn it! This is unbearable. Is there really a hell? No chains or anything.”

“Hahaha! Of course! I’m invincible. Even hell can’t stop me.”

Shirone furrowed his brow.

“Son Yujeong.”

As the Gingoa constricted, her face turned pale.

“I-I’m sorry!”

“Don’t get arrogant. Never forget the reason Nane sent you to the Under World, not even for a second.”

The image of Nane plunging the Bat Sword into her flashed through Son Yujeong’s mind, and her eyes burned with rage.

‘That damn Buddha.’

The only difference between Shirone’s Choeni Bardo and hers was that she couldn’t escape by her own will.

‘For her to return to reality, she must reach the Bat Ground. And that means…’

It meant she had come to understand emotions.

“Let’s go.”

Upon arriving at the entrance of the System Control Division, they saw men in suits standing guard.

Richella spoke.

“They’re administrators. We call them Agents. I’ve heard they’re ruthless.”

Inside the premises, Agents were patrolling, and among them, Black Reapers could be seen.

‘Agents…’

Even though records of the Under World were scarce in Omega’s archives, their notoriety was well known.

They were system specialists of the Under World, responsible for purification processes.

‘The Black Reapers capture them, and the Agents pass judgment. Their authority in the Under World is absolute.’

Etella spoke.

“Storming in headfirst doesn’t seem like a good idea. Even if we defeat them…”

Tampering with the purification system was another matter entirely.

“Nonsense.”

Shagal stepped forward.

“These damn chains can be broken. What’s the hesitation? Just kill them all.”

Rian blocked his path with his sword.

“Shirone makes the decisions. If you disrupt the operation, you’ll taste my blade.”

“Do whatever you want. That woman will die anyway.”

“You’re pathetic. Hiding behind a hostage because you don’t have the guts to stand by your own convictions?”

Shagal’s eyes narrowed.

“What do you know?”

“I know enough. A hyena that preys only on the weak. If not for the Taegeuk Chains, you’d be dead.”

Illusions of daggers flickered around Shagal.

“Oh yeah? Try it, then.”

Etella spoke.

“Stop this. Now isn’t the time for us to be fighting each other. Gathering intel comes first.”

Though they understood, neither backed down.

“You’re just garbage.”

Rian despised Shagal, and anyone would.

“Heh, do you hate me that much? Why? Because I tormented this woman? Just for that?”

“Just for that?”

As Rian stepped forward, Shagal yanked on the Taegeuk Chains.

Hiding behind Etella, he laughed.

“Hahaha! That’s right! I’m trash! If it makes you uncomfortable, I’ll do even worse!”

Shirone stepped forward.

“Let her go, Shagal.”

“Then work harder. Hey, woman. Tell them to follow my orders from now on. Otherwise…”

Shagal stabbed a knife into his own thigh.

“Gah!”

Through the Taegeuk Chains, the pain was transferred directly to Etella, twisting her face in agony.

The blade plunged in again and again.

“Say it! Hurry up!”

Unable to hold back, Rian charged.

“You bastard!”

“Haha! You want to cut me down? Go ahead! I’m curious to see what happens too!”

Shagal shoved Etella aside and spread his arms, waiting for the Dajikdo.

Rian gritted his teeth.

‘I want to cut him down! This absolute villain!’

But knowing Etella would suffer, he had no choice but to hold back.

‘Even so…’

His left fist clenched.

‘I’m sorry, Etella. But I need to land at least one punch!’

If he didn’t, Shagal would keep trying to control the group.

Besides…

‘I need to vent my anger too!’

Just as his fist was about to smash forward at incredible speed, Etella hugged Shagal.

“Urgh!”

As his Divine Transcendence stopped him in his tracks, a shockwave burst outward.

Etella spoke.

“Stop this.”

Rian had no words for her.

“Hah! You were about to kill me, and this is all you’ve got? In the end, you’re just another talker.”

Etella turned Shagal’s face toward her.

“You too. Stop this.”

“Stop what? I don’t listen to anyone. I’ll torment you, over and over, until you go mad.”

Just as Shirone was about to step in, Etella spoke.

“You never tormented me.”

‘Teacher…’

She turned to the group.

“The Taegeuk Chains are an endless cycle. None of us can claim to be the victim. This isn’t just his fault.”

Shagal was the most taken aback.

“What the hell are you saying?”

Etella recalled the last moment she had spent with Shagal in reality before her life ended.

“I was the one who accepted him. The only person who has the right to resent him is me. But I don’t resent him anymore. Because I now understand what I must do.”

The fate of Taegeuk.

“This man’s life is my life. So please, treat him kindly.”

Silence fell over them all.

‘Sacrifice.’

Even though it wasn’t his burden, Rian felt the searing pain of sacrifice gnawing at his soul, as if it would rot him from the inside out.

‘That too is conviction. A blade of its own.’

Rian spoke.

"I understand. Now that I’ve heard Etella’s resolve, I will no longer judge Shagal."

Etella turned back to Shagal.

"You should help us too. You want to break the chain, don’t you? If we work together, we can do it."

Was that so?

‘Do I… really want to break the chain?’

Shirone spoke.

"Then let’s form a plan. First, we need to understand the system of the underworld."

As the group began strategizing, Shagal remained distant, separated from them.

Etella’s emotions traveled along the chain, reaching him.

‘Hypocrites.’

A warm, kind sensation.

‘They must actually find me disgusting. If I die by accident, they’ll probably be overjoyed as if they’ve won the world. So why…’

Shagal’s eyes turned red.

‘Disgusting.’

Through that unknown, overwhelming emotion, sharp rage pierced through like a blade.

‘Don’t pity me!’

The wickedest of the wicked.

"KRRAAAAAH!"

At Shagal’s furious roar, everyone turned their heads—he was already sprinting toward the System Control Division.

"Shagal! Aah!"

The chain stretched with a snap, extending 30 meters, dragging Etella along the ground.

"That idiot!"

With Rian at the lead, the group chased after him, but Shagal had already reached the entrance.

"What’s with that guy?"

The agents standing guard spotted Shagal and put whistles to their lips, their expressions calm despite the situation.

As they puffed into the whistles, a high-pitched, mocking voice rang across the sky.

—Intruder! Kyaaha! Intruder alert!

From every sector, agents dressed identically swarmed toward him like a colony of wasps.

"GRRRH!"

Shagal, already blinded by rage, saw nothing but a single enemy—no matter how many stood before him.

‘Rapid Blades.’

The air around him rippled like a mirage, and an enormous barrage of blades rained down like a storm.

An agent grabbed Shagal’s shoulder.

"You bastard—! UGH!"

The moment the blade pierced the agent’s right eye, his uniform was punctured by dozens of holes.

"AGH! Guh! Ack! AAH!"

Within seconds, the agent was nothing more than a human pincushion, but Shagal showed no signs of stopping.

"RAAAAAH!"

His fury refused to fade.

‘Die! Die! DIE!’

If he had the stamina, he would repeat this act endlessly, like a machine.

‘You’re looking down on me? Am I a joke to you?’

The urge to destroy was clear, but he didn’t even know what "broken" meant anymore.

"RAAAAAAH!"

So he just kept stabbing.

Until the form changed, until every last cell was obliterated, until even atoms were crushed into nothingness.

‘I’ll destroy it! I’ll destroy it no matter what!’

That kind of madness.

When Shirone and the others arrived at the entrance, they were left speechless at the gruesome overkill unfolding before them.

"My god…"

Even in hell, the sheer malice emanating from Shagal was unparalleled—so intense that even the agents hesitated to approach.

Etella screamed.

"STOP!"

Her emotions traveled through the chain, but Shagal only fed them back with greater darkness.

"RAAAAAH!"

Why was it shameful?

‘The one who was humiliated was her. So why am I the one feeling this way? Why is it ME?!’

‘Why me?’

‘Yeah, I’m a murderer. I killed. I’m the worst kind of scum. That’s why I’m being punished, right? I died. I fell into hell. But if that’s not enough…’

Then more suffering wouldn’t matter.

‘Just answer me one thing.’

‘Why me?’

‘Why do I have no past? Why did I have to fall in love with Tia? When it was all fake anyway…’

If a god existed, at the very least, shouldn’t it answer that much?

"SHAGAL!"

Etella desperately poured her feelings into him, but the more she tried, the stronger his rage grew.

"RAAAAAAH!"

Thousands of daggers shredded the agents in front of him, slicing their bodies into unrecognizable pieces.

A voice echoed from above.

"Thank you for your generous purification. Remaining purification time: 87 aeons, 2,875 eons, 3,241 trillion, 6,401 billion, 721 million, and 38 hours."

Shirone lifted his head.

"What?"

From what he remembered, the purification time had been roughly 4 billion hours.

‘So this is how it works.’

The karma of the Taeguk cycle—yet the two involved were too far gone to recognize it.

"Stop! Please, just stop!"

At Etella’s desperate cry, Shagal’s assault came to a sudden halt.

"Haa… Haa…"

Where the Rapid Blades had once raged, only a grotesque massacre of corpses remained.

"Shagal… Shagal…"

Etella’s hands trembled as she shared his emotions, and blood-red tears welled in Shagal’s eyes.

"Teacher, are you okay?"

Shirone finally understood how the Taeguk cycle amplified itself.

‘This is hell.’

If Etella forgave Shagal, he would only crush her with even greater rage.

‘An endless cycle.’

The threshold of their suffering would only grow, and only an overwhelming emotional force could erase their karma.

A voice called out.

"Quite the spectacle you’ve put on."

The approaching agent was different from the others, escorted by black-clad enforcers.

"I’m Makula, head of the Purification Team at the System Control Division. If you had informed us in advance, my subordinates wouldn’t have made a mistake."

Rian asked,

"You’re letting us in?"

"Of course. You’re VIPs of our company. Follow me, I’ll show you the way."

Makula turned formally and walked away, his tone polite but unreadable.

Rian glanced at Shirone.

"What do we do?"

"Whatever the scheme, it’s better than fighting. Let’s go inside the main building."

Following Shirone’s lead, the group moved. Etella and Shagal slowly rose to their feet.

The two of them did not meet each other’s eyes.
