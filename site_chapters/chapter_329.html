Chapter 329: Philosophy of a Combat Mage (3)

Seeing the look of a craftsman in Shirone's eyes as he sewed, Dante spoke with a bewildered expression.

"Hey, do you really have to sew here?"

"This is the only coat I brought. And if I do it outside, my hands will freeze."

Shirone blew on his still-cold hands and focused on sewing again.

After ordering tea at the counter, Dante returned with Armand, which Shirone had leaned against the wall.

Shirone glanced at Armand. The usually temperamental cursed sword was quiet, as if acknowledging that Dante was worthy of examining it.

With people watching, Dante didn't draw the sword but inspected its exterior while holding it vertically.

"So, this is an S-class object."

He had heard a rough explanation on the way.

A cursed sword equipped with the concepts of precise control and diamond armor.

It was a perfect weapon that not only amplified magical power but also maximized physical offensive and defensive capabilities.

Dante threw Armand against the wall. Shirone used precise control to return it accurately to its original position.

Seeing this firsthand, Dante smiled.

"It's a good weapon. Considering its rarity, I understand why it's worth over 4 billion gold."

Amy, who had been warming herself by the fireplace, turned her head and spoke.

"You talk about 4 billion gold so casually? Well, the Airhain family is ranked 20th in the kingdom's business world. But why does a young master from such a family need to get coffee from others?"

"Haha! Coffee made by others always tastes better. And coffee bought by others tastes even better. Besides, if a single sword is worth 4 billion gold, my parents would be shocked too."

Shirone, who had finished sewing the first button, connected the needle to the second button and said,

"Too bad. I was thinking of selling it for around 3 billion if you were interested."

Armand's gem vibrated with a hum.

"I'm not really into collecting things. I don't even buy souvenirs when I travel."

Dante calling an S-class object a mere collectible showed he wasn't a typical person.

But Shirone liked that about him.

After all, mages only cared about what they liked and ignored everything else.

While Shirone finished sewing and put his coat back on, the server brought their drinks.

Shirone and Amy drank their juice and cocoa in silence for a while.

Just a moment ago, they had been laughing and having a great time, but with Dante around, they suddenly became reserved.

Dante sat with his legs crossed, sipping his coffee and watching them.

Then he pointed at Amy with his coffee cup.

"By the way, how are you doing?"

"Huh? What do you mean?"

"Neither of you seem like the type to go on a casual date. Is this a consolation trip or something?"

Shirone put down his cup and looked at Amy. It was the first time she had talked about the graduation exam.

"Hmm."

Amy rested her chin on her hand and looked out the window.

"Honestly, I don't know. It's not that I've lost motivation, but everything feels so vague."

Having had enough time to organize her thoughts, Amy spoke calmly.

"Anyway, I did my best for six years and took the exam. But I failed. I don't know what to do next. I feel like just improving my skills won't be enough. So, I guess..."

Amy sighed and shrugged.

"I'm just trying to pull myself together. It's probably a psychological issue with the second attempt. I need to find or create a special trigger."

Dante nodded.

Of course, he hadn't even considered the possibility of failing the graduation exam. But if he had, he would have had the same concerns as Amy.

"I see. But what exactly happened during the exam? I watched, but it's different from being there in person."

Shirone looked at Amy with a tense expression. It must be a memory she never wanted to recall.

But he also wanted to hear it.

Especially since he would have to face Fermi's group after the vacation, the more information, the better.

Fortunately, Amy didn't seem particularly distressed. It seemed she had already overcome the initial pain.

"I was ganged up on... starting 1 minute and 32 seconds into the battle."

Amy's red eyes glowed as she recalled the information from that time.

"As soon as the one-on-one combat category was announced, the 2000th duel activated, and all participants moved quickly. At that moment, I thought the first target should be Samuel. It seemed other participants thought the same."

Shirone spoke.

"Because Samuel is a word magic user."

"Right. Word magic takes time to activate. During training, Samuel's level of ultrasonic magic was 148 syllables per second. It takes at least 4 seconds to activate intermediate magic. But once the word magic is complete, its amplification power is immense, making him the top priority target in one-on-one combat. But Fermi disrupted the flow."

Amy's expression turned serious.

"It was really strange. That feeling. The sense of incongruity. Of course, during the battle, I didn't have the mental space to think about collusion, but I instinctively decided to change my target to Fermi. In hindsight, that was a mistake. With Lycan's plasma covering the area, I couldn't see the target clearly. So I tried close combat, but it seemed they had a prearranged play."

Amy was silent for a while, probably recalling the critical mistake she had made.

She took a deep breath as if to shake off her regret and returned to the present, dropping her shoulders.

"After that, the tide turned, and as you know, I was pushed back and eliminated early."

Dante, deep in thought, asked,

"But I heard Fermi's group has been using that tactic for a long time. The prearranged play, I mean."

"Right. I wasn't unprepared. In the graduating class, we train in six exam subjects on a rotation. You can understand the factions and each other's skills. But something was different on the exam day. Even if you consider the prearranged play as part of their tactics, there was no need for them to shield Samuel from concentrated attacks. But the atmosphere completely shifted. I still don't understand why that happened."

"The atmosphere shifted..."

If they could change the battlefield's atmosphere at will in one-on-one combat, it meant their skills were far superior to expected.

But there was no reason someone with Amy's keen eye wouldn't notice if they were hiding their skills. That's why Amy was confused.

'It's not just a simple disguise tactic. There's something undiscovered. Probably the key player is...'

Fermi, the top-ranked student in the graduating class.

He could either perfectly conceal his allies' skills or drastically enhance them.

Whatever method he used, it was clear he could do one of those two things.

"Hmm, so you were ultimately overpowered. Then there's no room for excuses. No wonder Master Olivia didn't intervene."

Amy shook her head.

Of course, she had no intention of making excuses for her defeat. But it wasn't because she lacked skill.

In a fair fight, or at least if the neutral factions hadn't been swayed by Fermi, she would have made it to the final ten.

"If those are the rules, I don't want to argue. But compared to the other participants, my skills were never inferior. I'm not saying Samuel should have been eliminated instead of me. If Fermi can control the battlefield's flow, anyone targeted would inevitably be eliminated."

Dante blinked in surprise. Amy's expression showed she was sincere.

"I admit it can be frustrating. But the opponent ultimately played that way, right? So isn't it accurate to say you were overpowered?"

Amy put down her juice glass and sighed.

"Ugh, you keep misunderstanding..."

"No, I understand perfectly. Fermi's group controlled the battlefield's flow, and everyone targeted you."

"So you're saying it's my fault? Why? Because I'm usually annoying? Or because I seemed like an easy target?"

"Calm down..."

"Do I look calm to you?"

Amy slammed the table and stood up. The café patrons turned their attention to her.

Realizing this, Amy quietly sat back down, but her face was still flushed.

"Fine, everyone is entitled to their opinion. But I fought in the graduating class for a year and took the exam. If you were in my situation, Dante, could you say the same thing?"

Dante didn't answer.

Of course, his opinion hadn't changed.

But he felt he wouldn't get a rational response from her in her current state.

'...That's what I think, but...'

Dante was a mage. And so was Amy. Hiding the truth because of emotions didn't suit either of them.

"Don't be childish, Amy."

Amy's eyes filled with anger.

"Childish? Me?"

"Alright, then let's ask Shirone. What do you think about the results of this graduation exam?"

When Amy turned to him, Shirone swallowed hard. No matter what he said, it wouldn't please her.

"Shirone, do you think the same? Am I being childish, as Dante says?"

"Uh, well... I don't know. I..."

"Be honest. I respect Dante's skills, and I respect yours too. If both of you see me that way, then it must be true. I want to know the exact truth."

Amy was sincere.

As a student majoring in magic, she wasn't weak enough to be comforted by empty lies.

Above all, she believed in herself.

She hadn't failed the graduation exam because she was weak or lacked skill.

Shirone hesitated, then finally spoke, unable to bear the weight of silence.

"Morally, the exam was unfair. But no matter the situation... a mage should be able to win."

Amy felt all her strength drain away.

If even Shirone said that, she was likely mistaken.

Amy felt all her strength drain away.

If even Shirone said that, it was likely she was mistaken.

But she couldn't accept it in her heart.

"Childish? Me?"

She had never been lazy while studying magic. She prided herself on fighting harder than anyone else.

But childish?

What broke her heart the most was the possibility that their judgment, which she couldn't understand, might be true.

Dante spoke.

"Of course, you're strong. It's undeniable that you rose to the 5th rank in the graduating class within a year."

Amy slowly turned her gaze to Dante.

"But strictly speaking, it's because your talent is exceptionally outstanding."

"Talent...?"

"Currently, Alpheas Magic School has students as talented as those at the Royal Magic School. There's Iruki of Savant, Nade of Alchemy, Arin of Superhardness, Kanis who handles magical creatures, Shirone the Unlocker, and me, who uses the Central Calculation Magic Circle. Of course, Sabina and Closer are also formidable. Boyle of Summoning Magic and Pandora of Scent Magic each have their specialized talents."

Amy listened quietly to Dante.

"But if I were to rank based on what we were born with..."

Dante took a sip of his coffee and pointed at Amy.

"It's you. Amy, you're the best at Alpheas Magic School."

Amy didn't feel happy.

She still couldn't grasp what Dante was trying to say.
