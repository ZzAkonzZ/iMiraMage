Chapter 544:

Armand’s overwhelming pressure was familiar to Shirone.

It felt like something sharp pierced his spinal cord, and his entire body surged with power as if he’d been fitted with a power pack. His bodily functions reached peak efficiency, and Armand’s data synced seamlessly with his own.

In his memory, the six options embedded in the demon sword flowed clearly.

Only one, Valhalla Action, appeared as unavailable through the intuitive brainwave-transmitted data.

“Shirone, did something come back to you?”

Shirone looked down at Marsha.

Her intense emotional imprint had raised his memory recovery rate to 78%, allowing him to reach the state of oneness through mind. Because the real Shirone was fused with Armand in reality, the information had been faithfully reconstructed even in the Apocalypse.

I don’t know anymore. I can’t tell what’s real and what’s not.

He thought regaining his memories would bring clarity. Instead, it only deepened the confusion. Now, everything was jumbled—memories, illusions, dreams—and he couldn’t tell what was real or imagined.

First, I have to save Yoa.

Shirone gently set Marsha down and stood.

“Let her go.”

“You foolish human.”

From all around, beasts conjured from the data of Digital Ra surged out of the mucus.

In response, dozens of tendrils burst from Armand’s robe, lashing out with terrifying speed, smashing the beasts aside.

Watching Armand use Kujen’s Consumption Apostle ability to absorb the mucus, Fermi’s face twisted in disbelief.

“What the hell is that?”

When he first saw Shirone back at the arboretum, he’d thought he could subdue him. But now? Shirone was operating on an entirely different level.

“Armand. Shirone’s weapon,” Rian said.

Even he looked rattled. The Armand before them wasn’t like the one he remembered. Its form, its abilities—everything had changed.

Shirone absorbed the last of the mucus and once more faced Ra, who still held Yoa.

“Fighting’s pointless. Give it up already.”

“I merely follow the course of nature. Even if you plead with a falling rock, it won’t stop.”

“Nature’s course, huh?”

Ra’s body began to dissolve into mucus, starting to consume Yoa.

“Yes, nature.”

“Housemaster! Run!”

At the same time, countless tendrils erupted from Shirone’s robe, surging toward Ra.

“It’s too late. Once energy is converted—kuh!”

The moment the tendrils stabbed into Ra’s body, Shirone’s eyes snapped wide open.

Soshikaisekiyu — Microcosmic Realm Unleashed.

“Aaaaagh!”

Ra’s form turned translucent like a lightbulb, radiating a blinding white light.

With Ra as the medium, the entire room’s mucus lit up. Yoa shook herself free and escaped.

Simultaneously, Armand invaded Digital Ra’s database, pulling Shirone’s consciousness into a trance-like state.

“Housemaster!”

Yoa’s voice grew faint as the scream of a thousand souls echoed into Shirone’s mind, filling the void of sound.

The entire history of the Apocalypse, recorded by Digital Ra, rushed into Shirone’s consciousness.

War. Famine. Disaster.

Children who lost their parents. Parents who lost their children.

Why? Why must humanity…?

Tears streamed down Shirone’s face, burdened with all of humanity’s sins.

So cruel…

“AAAAAAGH!”

Suspended by Armand’s tendrils, Shirone arched his back and screamed. A blinding flash of light burst from his body.

As his data recovery rate soared, an angel-winged avatar formed from Shirone’s body.

“Transcendent Mind—Prajna.”

Miro let out a breathless whisper.

To have reached this point using only the data of the Apocalypse… it was safe to assume that there was no more “real” Shirone left to bring back.

Please… just stop already!

The agony of history pierced straight through Shirone’s spirit.

Finally, he arrived in a white room—where a lone Mucus Man awaited.

“Do you see now? This is why I must exist. Humanity doesn’t need more humans.”

Shirone said nothing. He only cried.

The one truth he understood after hearing humanity’s collective scream was simple:

Humanity…

…should do nothing at all.

“Wretched human.”

Shirone slowly turned his head.

“Do you know why I haven’t exterminated humans, even though you are the most useless creatures in the ecosystem?”

He couldn’t answer.

“It’s because you humans made me think that way.”

Ra looked up at the ceiling.

“You imagine yourselves superior, but in reality, you are the most unstable organisms alive. I spared humanity in search of an answer to that contradiction. And now…”

Ra’s gaze turned back to Shirone.

“I have found the answer in you. If humanity truly is contradiction incarnate, then you are the one who can change this world.”

“Change it?”

Ra placed a hand on its chest.

“To become one. I can read the system of the world, and you are a singularity who can alter it. By combining, we can create a new world.”

Shirone swallowed hard.

The despair and suffering he’d seen through Digital Ra flashed through his mind.

“Human, I offer you one more chance. All of humanity’s sins, its suffering—I can erase it. Through the sacrifice of one person, eternal peace will come to this world.”

It was an ideal far beyond the concerns of any individual.

But the cost to Shirone was something no individual should ever be asked to give up.

“I…”

After a long silence, Shirone finally spoke.

“What was that?”

The others looked around.

The glowing lights dimmed, and the violently spinning lens of Digital Ra gradually returned to its original speed.

“Phew…”

Ra exhaled as Armand’s tendrils withdrew.

Now that their thoughts had been shared through Soshikaisekiyu, no more words were needed.

“Housemaster! Are you alright?”

With a bittersweet smile, Shirone took Yoa’s hand and looked back at Ra.

“Send us outside.”

Marsha frowned.

“What are you talking about? You’re coming with us.”

“Marsha… Rian…”

But when she saw the transcendent sadness in Shirone’s eyes, she couldn’t take a step closer.

“I’m sorry.”

Faced with the hope of changing the world, Shirone let everything go.

Before Rian could say anything, the mucus enveloped Shirone and Yoa and carried them up the wall—outward.

Miro turned to Ra.

“What did you do to Shirone?”

“Nothing. He made the best decision a living creature could make. I merely offered hope.”

“Hope? What kind of hope?”

“There’s no reason for you to know.”

A glow bloomed from Miro’s body—her Avatar of Compassion manifesting.

“I’m in a pretty delicate mood right now, so you’d better answer clearly. Just how much do you really know?”

Her two giant palms stretched out on either side of Digital Ra.

“Information is endlessly replicable and restorable. Destroying me won’t destroy me.”

“We’ll deal with that when we get there.”

Ra, prioritizing its mission, concluded it was best to preserve the machine.

“I will fuse my system with Shirone’s mind and change the structure of this world.”

“You mean to absorb him?”

“‘Integration’ is the proper term. I will synchronize with his neural network and purify the world. It will be like—”

Ra suddenly stopped, eyes wide. It turned its head, then quickly turned again in the opposite direction.

A mechanical, disjointed movement—as if even quantum computation couldn’t analyze what it had just detected.

“…Who are you? Where are you accessing from?”

The group exchanged confused looks—there was nothing there.

But something flashed through Miro’s mind.

No way…?

As she suspected, Ra gave up the search for the unknown entity and stared into the void.

“Why are you helping me?”

A long silence passed.

“I see. Understood.”

Ra turned back to Miro.

“With this, you can no longer stop me. You’d best give up and leave.”

Miro’s eyes flashed sharply.

But if that person had begun to interfere, there was nothing more they could do here. She clicked her tongue and turned around.

“Let’s go. We don’t have time to waste here.”

Fermi tilted his head. “We’re leaving? Wouldn’t it be better to get more information out of Ra? It’s a rare opportunity.”

“Oh, right.”

Miro turned back to face Ra.

“Remember this—I always finish what I start.”

The Avatar of Compassion erupted from Miro’s body and, with cruel precision, crushed Digital Ra between its massive palms.

With a sickening crack, the lens of Digital Ra was shattered like scrap metal, and the Mucus Man was absorbed into the sludge as though he’d fallen into water.

“Let’s go.”

Rian, Marsha, and Fermi stared in disbelief at the destroyed Digital Ra before snapping back to their senses and following after her.

Not long after, bluish electricity began to crackle through Ra’s remaining shell.

—System backup complete. Estimated recovery time: 0.000023 seconds. Done.

Though the sound wasn’t as silent and serene as before, the engine began to whir once more. A new Mucus Man rose from the sludge.

Watching the direction Miro had exited, it muttered softly:

“…Humans, honestly.”

After exiting the Fairy Biomimetics Research Lab, Miro immediately began looking for any trace of Shirone.

But if he’d used flight magic, there would be no visible trail on the surface.

Eventually, she gave up and turned to the others.

“Listen carefully. We’re seriously running out of time. We have to move fast now—this is the biggest variable.”

Fermi asked, “What exactly happened? Was it because Ra spoke to someone?”

“I got a report from Arius. Most likely, the one who accessed Ra was Shura.”

“Shura?”

“She’s a senior member of the Council of Ten, the Heaven Immortals Community. If it’s her, she can probably infiltrate anywhere in this world.”

“A Coder, then. But is she really that serious of a threat?”

Miro whipped her head to the side, flipping her hair.

“Hmph, as if I’d worry about someone like that.”

But a moment later, her expression turned deadly serious.

“To be honest, she’s not someone to take lightly. The entire Council of Ten is made up of Prajna and Yaksha. And their first-ranked member is Amitābha.”

Prajna and Yaksha were just classifications of one’s spiritual tier, but how that power was wielded varied dramatically in combat capability.

Miro hadn’t explained this earlier because the Council of Ten members were all masters of the highest arts within their respective levels.

Rian asked, “So is this Shura the top-ranked Amitābha?”

“No. I don’t know her exact rank, but judging by her history, she’s probably a mid or lower-ranking member. Still, that’s not what makes her dangerous. It’s because this is the Under Coder’s world.”

Marsha asked, “Why does that matter?”

“She’s a Lawbringer. The most effective class when it comes to defending against avatars. If she’s a Council of Ten officer, then she’s probably the strongest Lawbringer among all humans.”

“So we’ve drawn the short straw,” Marsha muttered.

Someone who was the strongest Law-user in reality would exhibit near-godlike powers in the Under Coder’s realm.

“They probably picked Shura to counter me,” Miro said. “But that’s not the only reason. Like the Teraje, the Council of Ten has its eye on Shirone. The Apocalypse is like a lab for someone like Shura.”

“Since everything happening here can be analyzed through code,” Fermi added.

Marsha frowned. “If she can infiltrate anywhere in this world, then how do we even find her?”

Miro shook her head. “We don’t have to. She’s probably in the colony—rallying the Children of the Sun to bring them to Shirone.”

“How can you be so sure?”

“Because Shura is a Prajna with the Avatar of the Serpent. People who know her…”

Miro pictured Shura’s face and said,

“…call her the God of False Doctrine.”
