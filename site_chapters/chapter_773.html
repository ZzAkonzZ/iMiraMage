Chapter 773:

Beneath Kaidra, the monstrous bird soaring across the Pacific Ocean, countless starlights reflected by the sun rippled like waves.

Zulu sat in the cockpit guiding the path, while Kangnan and Gaold sat face-to-face in the hollow of the beast's spine.

Breaking the long silence, Kangnan spoke up.

“Don’t be too disappointed.”

Zulu subtly turned her head, but Gaold remained with eyes closed, lost in meditation.

Giving up on empty consolation, Kangnan revealed her true feelings.

“No, seriously—how could they reject us like that, just because they’re monks?”

They had visited Zion, but the response had been a flat refusal of entry. In the end, they had to turn back without even seeing Miro.

“If it’s for the balance of the Law, then it can’t be helped,” said Zulu.

“Hmph, then what about Armin and Kuan? Even in Zion, they’d still need a combat unit.”

With Gaold’s group's capabilities, they should have been welcomed with open arms.

‘That’s exactly why it doesn’t make sense.’

Armin had claimed that Miro was away, but this couldn’t have happened without Miro’s own instructions.

“It’s not because of the balance,” said Gaold, finally opening his eyes.

“Then why?” Kangnan asked.

“She’s uncomfortable seeing me.”

Kangnan narrowed her eyes.

“Are you joking? Even if you were dismissed, you're still the former president of the Tormia Magic Association—and they turned you away at the door?”

Gaold gazed out over the vast ocean.

“She finds it awkward to face me. After everything that happened in Heaven… and besides, she still owes me a debt.”

“What debt?” Kangnan asked.

Zulu answered instead.

“Miro asked Gaold to sleep with her.”

Kangnan whipped her head around so fast it looked like her neck might snap.

“What? When?”

“When you were captured after crossing the drawbridge. The two of them had a huge fight. Even the mighty Miro got emotional back then.”

Kangnan had been the life Gaold saved.

“S-So…?”

“He didn’t do it. Gaold went to save you, and Miro said she’d sleep with him after he came back safely. And now…”

Gaold had returned.

“Wait, are you seriously telling me humanity’s greatest seeker rejected us because of that?”

Zulu shrugged.

“Coincidence or not, it’s a fact that Miro’s avoiding Gaold. Otherwise…”

Kangnan looked at Gaold.

‘He still… feels that way about Miro, doesn’t he?’

Whether in Hell or in reality, to Gaold, the world remained the same—a place of endless suffering.

And yet, the only reason he returned… was because Miro was here.

Nane and Shura climbed the highest peak of Eastern Jungcheon-dong, obscured by clouds.

‘There…’

…lay the Gate of Goffin.

‘Raise the army of Heaven and end the war.’

Theraphos guarded the order of the universe, but even it was merely a part of the system created by Anke Ra.

If Nane himself were to lead the angels to attack humanity, even Theraphos would have no grounds to interfere.

‘Ra is everything.’

After Veron’s death, the community of immortals began acting independently, but Heaven’s army remained intact.

‘This might be the end.’

Sensing the end of the world drawing near, Shura recalled Veron’s last words before his death:

“Live.”

‘But… is anything eternal in this world?’

Eternity is something inherently unreachable—and thus belongs not to fact, but to the realm of belief.

Shura couldn’t understand belief.

‘Anyway… I’ll think it over a little more.’

Cursing the man who’d left her with such a difficult task, she arrived with Nane at a shrine where twelve gates had been erected.

At the end of the gates, a mysterious cave shimmered with strange energy. Nane lit the way and entered.

“The Gate of Goffin.”

A massive iron sphere, etched with henna markings, floated near the ceiling—high as the mountain’s summit.

“Open—Gate of Heaven.”

As Nane’s sermon activated, a steel-colored blade shot upward and pierced deep into the iron sphere.

The sphere broke apart into fragments, forming a black orb that began absorbing the metal shards.

“Let’s go. To Heaven.”

The cave shook violently.

“It’s dangerous!” Shura warned.

But Nane looked up at the rapidly distorting dimensional passage with interest.

“They’ve blocked it.”

“Huh?” Shura asked.

Just then, the passage began spitting out the fragments, and the orb swiftly reassembled itself.

“A dimension sealing another dimension…”

Turning back toward the exit, Nane asked,

“Was that your doing?”

Shura frowned as she saw a group blocking the exit.

“Miro.”

Miro sat on Arius’s back, crouched like a beast, while Sein, a strategist from Zion, stood guard by her side.

“How did you get here?” Shura asked.

Miro stood.

“What a silly question. You opened the Inner World, so naturally the next step is Heaven. But it won’t be easy—I’ve done a lot of studying on this subject.”

As Shura snorted, about to retort, Nane stepped forward first and asked,

“Seeker—do you defy my will?”

Miro studied Nane’s face.

‘So this is the current Buddha.’

One who had mastered all Right Paths, and endured all heretical ways to finally reach the truth.

‘So this is the gaze that pierces the universe.’

Thin and draped in a hooded robe of rags, his face and arms covered in countless tattoos—surely his whole body was inked.

‘And yet…’

Would it be strange to say he resembled Shirone?

“‘Defy’ is such an unpleasant word. As long as neither side yields, this battle won’t end.”

Nane’s gaze filled with sorrow.

“That’s why I intend to end it.”

“No.”

The incarnation of Avalokiteśvara burst into radiant light.

“You back down, Nane.”

Just as the highest mountain in the north might be the lowest in the south, the highest place for Miro was a vantage point with no blind spots.

Having reached the omniscient state of Avalokiteśvara, Miro’s overwhelming presence was bolstered by Arius, who willingly became her dog.

“Woof woof! Grrrr!”

Shura wrapped her arms around Nane from behind.

“Mere mortal, know your place.”

Revealing her serpent nature, her tongue stretched out long and red like blood, mocking Miro’s party.

“You dare threaten the Buddha?”

Sein’s Iron Wheel Eyes spun swiftly.

‘Shura, the false god.’

Even one who denies all of existence—even she could not deny Nane’s righteousness?

‘She’s on par with Miro. Nane should be left to her. I’ll coordinate with Arius.’

As if thinking the same, Arius rose to his feet.

“Leave it to me.”

Miro couldn’t answer.

‘No magic even leaves a scratch. Maybe the right move is to quickly subdue him with a powerful axing.’

Even if the Buddha is righteous, as long as it's Incarnation Magic that twists the Law, she could still land a hit.

“I have something to discuss with you, alone.”

At Nane’s instruction, Shura separated from him and headed toward the exit. Arius and Sein also turned to leave.

“Miro, be careful.”

Even if she couldn’t win, if Miro were to die here, the balance of the world would tip drastically.

“I know.”

Of course, Miro was aware—but this wasn’t someone she could kill while keeping her own survival in mind.

‘I have no choice but to stake my life.’

As Sein, Arius, and Shura exited the cave, Nane stepped closer.

“You are truly a benevolent incarnation.”

Even Avalokiteśvara must be one of the many faces of the Buddha.

“But the era that needed you is over. Leave everything to me now—and awaken from your dream.”

A thunderous roar erupted from outside the cave.

‘It’s begun over there.’

At that moment, Miro cleared her mind of all thoughts. In her head, an immense sequence of formulas unfolded at blinding speed.

“Oh Buddha, if you are truly merciful…”

The incarnation of Avalokiteśvara rose all the way to the cave’s ceiling, spreading thousands of arms like a fan.

“…could you not have embraced even their dreams?”

The paradox of emptiness.

As Nane’s head tilted ever so slightly, Avalokiteśvara’s axing activated.

‘I cannot cry.’

Giant palms carved sweeping arcs through the air, rushing in from either side of Nane.

‘I cannot weep for their dreams.’

As time compressed, the number of afterimages multiplied.

‘Because it was a dream I created.’

Conversely, the more afterimages there are, the faster the blow approaches a singular point in time.

‘It is something I must end.’

Incredible speed.

In the end, what made all those afterimages feel real was that the total impact was measured by the sum of them all.

‘I struck!’

Such was the axing.

‘He absolutely… can’t… dodg—…’

Within fractured time, yet another fracture occurred, and Miro’s thoughts slowed to a crawl.

‘Do it…’

The universe came to a stop.

As he looked up at Avalokiteśvara’s palm—now devoid of afterimages—Nane contemplated emptiness.

‘Mercy for dreams…’

Drrrrrrrrr!

Suddenly, Avalokiteśvara began to vibrate, pulling Nane abruptly out of his thoughts with a flash of bewilderment in his eyes.

‘It got faster.’

BOOOOOOM!

The palms collided, and the sheer pressure blew off the entire ceiling of the cave.

Amid the rising haze, Miro clicked her tongue.

‘Tch! He dodged it!’

Just in time, Nane had launched himself upward through the collapsed roof and now glared down at her from above.

“Is that the spirit you guard the world with?”

Nane’s tongue moved.

“Sermon: Mind Net.”

Thousands of blades formed a mesh, soaring from the cave to entangle Avalokiteśvara.

‘A sealing technique?’

As the Law was neutralized and Avalokiteśvara struggled within the net, Nane pointed a finger at Miro.

“O bearer of the Buddha’s heart.”

A blade born at his fingertip began to spin rapidly.

“I am the Buddha.”

The sword rotated with unimaginable speed, plunged into Miro’s position—and one second later, the entire peak twisted and warped like a corkscrew.

RUMMMMMBLE!

As the mountain collapsed and the center exploded, Miro emerged between the rising boulders.

“Fine! You and I—let’s finish this!”

Avalokiteśvara grew endlessly, tearing apart the Mind Net. Nane’s eyes gleamed.

“Prajñāpāramitā.”

The Pure Land.

The colossal incarnation, larger than a planet, manifested over 200 million afterimages of its arms, pressing in on Nane.

‘Can he dodge this too?’

Maybe if he jumped out of the star system—but Nane, bound to Anke Ra’s system, would never permit such a breach.

“Sermon: Open.”

Nane crossed his arms in an X, and two swords emerged from his fingertips, spinning in a wide arc.

Centered around that circle, the world expanded. Avalokiteśvara’s twin palms pressed in from both sides.

“Ghhhhaaaa!”

It was like trying to crush a massive sphere with both hands—the air trembled, the ground shook.

“I won’t run.”

Miro had resolved to face death.

“I’ll stake everything and fight.”

Because she was someone who gives, not one who merely sustains the game.

“Samādhi…”

The Realm.

Miro’s consciousness was sucked into the void, and Avalokiteśvara’s speed spiked—compressing the air around it.

With a metallic, screeching gust of wind, the three fighting on the opposite peak turned their heads.

‘Samādhi? That fool…’

She had finally done it.

“No!”

Shura shouted—just as Avalokiteśvara, now covering the sky, brought both palms crashing together.

“……!”

Her ears couldn’t register the sound, and her nerves went numb as darkness swallowed everything.

With no sensation left, her consciousness began to fade.

‘Damn it! Shock!’

Being mentally specialized, the three each deployed their own powers to protect their minds.

‘Miro…!’

Whether she was standing or had fallen, whether her bones were broken or not—Sein had no way of knowing.

He simply prayed.

‘Please, just be alive.’

The thunderous echo shook all of Jungcheon-dong.
