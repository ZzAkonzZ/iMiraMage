Chapter 1132: Chain Reaction (4)

Gis observed Plu’s face.

‘Hmm.’

According to information from Dante’s department, Gis’s preferences were considered quite hardcore.

"He’s not particularly interested in conventional beauty. For him, sexual pleasure is intertwined with domination. If she’s a princess, even if she’s unattractive, he’d welcome her."

Albino added,

"Of course, I’m not saying you’re unattractive."

Gis nodded.

‘Not bad.’

He liked Plu’s attitude—remaining composed and showing no signs of displeasure despite his gaze.

‘The secretary-general of the Magic Association, huh…’

Naturally, he wasn’t someone inexperienced enough to show his emotions from the start.

"Come to think of it, there hasn’t been much interaction with the Magic Association in Tormia. How are things going over there?"

"We’re doing our best."

"Well, it’s not an easy task. Politics, after all, isn’t something you can manage alone."

"Of course, you’re right."

While it sounded like a mundane conversation, analysts breaking it down at a micro level understood the implications.

‘Tormia can’t even make a sound.’

That reaffirmation of power stirred something deep within Gis.

‘Ah, I suddenly feel drawn.’

Placing a hand on Plu’s shoulder, Gis said,

"In any case, isn’t this a gathering for world peace? I have a little time; let’s have some tea."

"It would be an honor."

Plu’s eyes grew cold as she followed Gis.

‘Not today anyway.’

Gis wasn’t careless enough to act in front of foreign officials.

As Fermi walked past, he glanced at Gis and Plu.

‘He’s already making his move. Quicker than expected.’

From the moment rumors spread in the daily papers, he expected someone to make a move.

‘But Tormia? There’s no way Shirone, that naive guy, would have suggested this.’

Just then, Shirone, who had exited the conference room late, looked at Plu with a serious expression.

Seeing this, Fermi realized:

‘They didn’t coordinate this?’

This is quite bold.

‘Lupist. Definitely a formidable opponent.’

While other nations were calculating their moves, they had bitten down on instinct.

Fermi briefly made eye contact with Shirone as he passed him indifferently.

‘Good luck. You’re going to have a headache for a while.’

While it could be a good bargaining chip, Fermi was more concerned about the pyramids.

‘If Uorin stormed out, it likely means the law or the system has changed.’

Fermi looked out the window.

"Not bad."

Apocalypse.

At the place where mucus covered the entire world, five managers of High Gear had gathered.

"It’s finally complete."

A white sphere, resembling a snowflake, slowly rotated above the operator’s palm.

"Ice Blood."

It was a special code that, when injected into Digital Ra’s core, could harden the mucus.

A voice came from behind shortly after.

"You’re already here?"

It was Shirone and the leaders of the Parrot Mercenaries, having arrived after being summoned to Apocalypse.

Marsha pointed.

"Is that the virus or whatever it is?"

"Yes. It’s extremely powerful. Once we implant it into the core, we won’t be able to control it either."

"Doesn’t matter. This world is abandoned anyway."

Shirone surveyed the scenery.

"It seems to have grown. The height of the mucus is different. Even the excavators are gone."

"Huh? You’re right."

Marsha confirmed that the mucus was blocking much more of the sky than before.

Shirone could guess why.

‘It’s because of the pyramids.’

The distortion of the law had weakened the angels' status, and Apocalypse had also changed.

‘More mucus. What does that mean?’

Deputy Commander Freeman said,

"Let’s head down for now. We haven’t mined in days. You seem to be in a hurry too."

The Parrot Mercenaries were also aware of the pyramids being erected worldwide.

"Yes, that’s right."

As they took their first step, the mucus reacted, surging violently into the air.

"Ahhh! What is that?!"

While the High Gear managers panicked, Marsha sliced off the tentacles with a dagger.

The severed tentacles twitched momentarily before dissolving back into mucus and being reabsorbed into the ground.

"Ugh, this is annoying. Why is it so aggressive? It wasn’t like this before."

‘The nature of the mucus has changed.’

This meant the algorithm of Digital Ra, buried underground, had altered.

‘Rejecting life with greater force hints at what’s to come in the future.’

Shirone activated Miracle Stream.

"I’ll handle this."

As the radiant mist spread, the mucus’s surface began to ripple like waves being pushed back.

"I’ve wrapped it in a barrier and compressed it with pressure. It won’t be easy to break through."

Of course, if the mass covering the entire world concentrated in one place, that would be a different story.

‘Then it’ll be all-out war.’

Fortunately, the mucus didn’t react excessively, and the group safely reached their destination.

"Digital Ra is down here. To get there, you’ll need to ride a strange device for quite a while."

Though Shirone didn’t remember, Fermi knew the geography of Apocalypse.

"Then..."

Retracting the radiant mist, Shirone summoned Hand of God above his head.

"Be cautious."

The leaders of the Parrot Mercenaries escorted the managers.

‘The mucus regenerates continuously. The best approach is to dig through as quickly as possible.’

Hand of God raised its index finger and drove vertically into the mucus.

Feeling a massive vibration, the mucus raised giant tentacle-like pillars dozens of meters away.

The lollipop-mark man shouted,

"Ahhh! It’s coming!"

At the same time, the Hand of God embedded in the ground spun like a drill and sank deep below with a thud.

"Jump down!"

The excavation team dodged the tentacles flying at them from all directions and quickly jumped into the pit.

The narrowing hole completely closed, and the tentacles embedded in the surface exploded like masses of water.

"Ahhh! Aaaah!"

The stout, middle-aged man with the smiley face mark screamed like a little girl as they plummeted into the seemingly endless depths.

‘This is incredibly deep.’

The Hand of God was drilling through faster than gravitational acceleration, which was precisely the problem.

‘The pressure is building too much.’

Looking up, Shirone could see the tunnel he had created closing rapidly.

‘What’s this?’

At that moment, the immense resistance pressing against the Hand of God disappeared as if it had been a lie.

"Shirone!"

Marsha shouted.

"Urgh!"

Shirone, who had turned the Hand of God back into Miracle Stream, caught everyone with it.

“Hah… Hah…”

Suspended in midair, the operator looked down at the city 200 meters below them.

"What… what is this?"

Instead of being filled with mucus, the interior revealed a massive space about the size of a city.

Marsha flew over to Shirone.

"Thanks. There’s no way Fly magic could have handled that kind of acceleration."

"You're welcome. But… what is this place?"

It appeared to be a massive dome with a curtain of mucus spread along the city's outskirts.

"Maybe it’s something like this," Marsha said, raising a finger.

"The Apocalypse world reacts to changes in reality. If some future event caused the expansion of the mucus to accelerate unnaturally…"

"Then pockets of trapped air would form."

"Exactly. The mucus predicts bursts like cell ruptures in reality. But the reason the ground level rose isn’t because there’s more of it; it’s because it swelled. There might be other spaces like this scattered across the Apocalypse world, like holes in cheese."

Shirone nodded in agreement.

"Let’s go down and take a look."

The excavation team descended to the ground using magic and began surveying the city.

"It’s changed a lot. There are buildings I haven’t seen before. This complicates things."

There were traces of mucus residue on the ground, but not enough to hinder exploration.

The managers entering the Apocalypse for the first time felt melancholic in the eerie silence.

"Doesn’t seem like anyone lives here."

Marsha was thinking the same thing.

"They weren’t entirely gone before. The Children of the Sun, dynamic humans. But now..."

Now, there was nothing left.

"They were likely swept away by the flood of mucus. It’s not surprising. At this point…"

Uuuuugh... Uuuuugh...

A groaning sound from somewhere stopped the group in their tracks.

"What’s that?"

Shirone quickly scanned their surroundings and sprinted toward the source of the sound.

It was a square, metal structure.

‘I’ve seen this before.’

A memory of the Maze of Andre came to mind.

‘World No. 19,000.’

A world among countless others that humanity had reached, only to face psychological ruin.

"Let’s just leave it," someone suggested.

"What are you talking about? There are people trapped inside."

Marsha leaped onto the structure and grabbed the handle of its metal door, pulling on it.

"Ugh."

When it didn’t budge, Freeman climbed up.

"Step aside. I’ll break it."

Freeman loaded a magical explosive round and aimed at the mechanical panel beside the door.

"Wait!"

The lollipop-marked man raised his hand to stop him.

"Leave it to me."

The boy climbed up to the warehouse and carefully examined the panel’s structure.

"Wow, this is incredible."

The operator asked, "Can you open it?"

"Ten seconds."

He held a custom-made card to the panel, and it turned transparent, revealing its code.

"Hmm. Impressive tech, but the pattern is simple. Did they only intend to lock it?"

Click. The mechanism disengaged.

"Convenient. How did you do that?"

"Hehe, isn’t that why you brought me along? Anyway, it’s ready to open now."

Marsha grabbed the handle.

"Let’s see…"

The door opened, and, as Shirone had feared, a chorus of horrified screams erupted.

"Ahhh! What is this?!"

Inside, men and women, from children to the elderly, were tangled together in a grotesque, naked mass.

"A human storage facility."

Everyone turned to Shirone.

"This is humanity’s worst-case scenario. What you’re seeing here is what happens."

"The worst-case scenario?"

Marsha peered into the warehouse.

The sight of people licking fluids off each other’s bodies in a desperate bid to survive was far from anything resembling human life.

"Wait… isn’t this the Apocalypse? Isn’t it built based on information from reality?"

"Yes. Which means, if nothing changes in reality, this is what we will eventually become."

"But why?"

Marsha’s eyes shook with unease.

"Why would this happen? Why to us?"

"We don’t know yet. To find out, we need to go deeper. And to excavate, we’ll need to implant the virus first."

Shirone turned away.

"Let’s go. Every moment we waste here, the real world moves closer to this future."

As Marsha stood there in a daze, Freeman gently closed the steel door.

"Let’s go, Marsha."

With heavy hearts, the excavation team moved forward.

Their only solace was the fact that the route to Digital Ra was still intact.

"This is it."

Shirone paused to look at a monument beside a building.

"‘Fairy Biomimetics Laboratory.’"

While his memory had gaps due to the data loss, there was one thing he realized.

"Wait. Fairy?"

Long ago, fairies had been split in two by Guffin.

"What is it, Shirone?"

Marsha asked, but Shirone didn’t respond.

‘Elves fused with humans using the power of Agape. Fairies controlling humans from the heavens.’

And now, the fairies that once ruled the heavens were on Shirone’s planet.

"They weren’t elves."

As Marsha blinked in confusion, Shirone’s face twisted with anger.

"It was the fairies who were controlling the human world all along."
