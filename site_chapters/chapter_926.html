Chapter 926: The Sin That Couldn’t Continue (2)

Armin knew that it wasn't about underestimating Rian's skills.

'It's a well-known fact that no one can match the power of the Knight of Maha's sword.'

However, when it came to technique, Kuan's movements were considered the best among all of humanity.

'Bypassing Havitz's perception. If Kuan can't do it, no one can.'

With Sein's Iron Wheel Eye and Meirei's ability 'God's Frequency' added, it wasn't an impossible task.

'Even if there's only a 1% chance, it's worth trying. Havitz is that kind of person.'

Eliminating the extreme evil would end the war.

"Before we go, I have a confession to make," Meirei said.

"Theraphos is fundamentally a race that pursues good. But if a full-scale war between good and evil breaks out, they will no longer interfere in human affairs."

Sein spoke.

"I know. Theraphos judges humanity, and if evil wins, they destroy everything. Although it seems meaningless if good loses..."

Armin added.

"So if a full-scale war between good and evil starts, you won't be able to use 'God's Frequency' either."

"Yes. This might be our last chance. That's why I want to do this now."

"What is it?"

Instead of answering, Meirei extended her hand to Dante.

"Do you have an awl or something sharp?"

Dante walked to the desk and threw an awl used for making document files from a drawer.

"Thank you."

Meirei caught the handle and aimed the awl at her ear as she continued speaking.

"The unregulated use of God's Frequency is activated by blocking the ears. It cuts off the world's waves and only accepts Theraphos's frequency. But there's a limit to blocking with hands. Noise gets mixed in."

Meirei stopped speaking and inserted the awl into her ear, piercing her eardrum.

As she slowly stirred it around, her nerves jumped, causing her eyelids to tremble.

But that was all; Meirei calmly removed the awl with an indifferent expression.

"It's done now."

Blood gushed from her ear canal, dripping down her earlobe.

"If it's just about hearing sounds, there's no problem. But the waves of the mind are very sensitive. By losing my right hearing, I should be able to hear more clearly."

Dante swung his arm and splattered blood on the floor after catching the awl thrown by Meirei.

Sein, who was in charge of order, checked the time.

"Alright, let's go. The army of Hell is advancing on Tormia. We need to isolate Havitz before that."

As the assassination squad followed Sein out the door, Dante called out to someone.

"Lilia."

"Hmm?"

"Be careful."

Lilia chuckled.

"Oh come on, don't worry..."

Realizing something suddenly, Lilia turned back to Dante.

"You're really strange today. This isn't like you."

"What?"

"Why are you so serious? The coolest person in the world? You're at your best when you do things your way."

Her cheeks slightly reddened, but Lilia returned to a serious expression and spoke again.

"I don't know what you're thinking, but don't let your emotions sway you. Be yourself. I'm scared."

The joke about dying if one's personality suddenly changes had some truth in law.

"Okay."

Only after seeing Dante's relaxed smile did Lilia nod reassuringly.

"Then I'll be back." As the door closed and Dante returned to his seat in the shadows of the wall, he lit another cigarette.

'Iruki.'

They weren't particularly close, but that made him even more inclined to suggest having a drink together.

"There should be some snacks."

Having made up his mind, Dante stood up abruptly and headed for the door when Ena from the security team bowed her head.

"Team leader, I have something to report. The activation code for Biochemistry..."

"Ena can handle it."

Ena looked up from flipping through documents.

"What? What does that mean...?"

"I'll delegate all authority over Rope to Ena. You won't need any handover since it's you."

Since Biochemistry would be controlled by noble families anyway, it didn't matter who opened the code.

"Oh, um..."

Ena accepted it after realizing it meant a promotion while looking around nervously.

"What are you planning to do?"

Despite his cold smile, Dante's eyes were burning with determination.

"To the battlefield."

He wouldn't stay hidden deep underground fiddling with scraps of code.

"I'm so angry I could go crazy."

Omega 892 years.

After many long years and increasing their numbers, Goffin led Eden's residents back into Heaven once again.

But even angels hadn't just been enjoying peace.

"Why?"

Through Babel, Kariel analyzed Gaia's technology and created numerous ancient weapons.

"Why did you come back?"

Knowing this war would be different from before made Ikael both glad and horrified at Goffin's return.

"You want to fight again? Aren't you tired of it? Admit your limits. Humanity has already lost."

In war, winners take everything.

"Heaven has mastered all human technology and advanced further. The result will be the same if we fight again."

Ashur finally arrived then.

"Ikael."

Before he could catch his breath after confirming Goffin's face, he also gritted his teeth in anger.

'Is the war between gods and humans starting again?'

Whether he knew Ashur's thoughts or not, Goffin spread his arms nonchalantly and asked,

"Did you miss me? You look like you're about to cry."

Ikael couldn't understand it.

"You say that now? Do you think time erases our past too?"

"You misunderstand something. We're not here to fight."

"Huh?"

After blinking for a moment in thought, Ikael subtly brightened up.

"...Are you surrendering?"

If Gaia raised a white flag, they could at least elevate their status as much as possible.

"No. We don't recognize Anke Ra."

Feeling like he was being toyed with, Ikael raised her voice angrily,

"You say you're not here to fight but want to become gods? Are you playing word games with me?"

"Maybe." Goffin extended his hand,

"As Gaia's representative, I request a meeting with Anke Ra. Take me to Ra."

Not only Ikael but also many angels who had already arrived were left speechless with their mouths open wide,

"You want to meet directly...?"

"Yes. No one knows better than me how long our war has been going on through every battle fought. I don't want any more senseless attrition wars. The reason I've returned is to settle things with Ra once and for all."

Metiel shouted,

"A mere human wants an audience with God? Even angels can't see Him without being summoned!"

"If you refuse..."

Goffin's eyes lit up as Gaia's Idea expanded massively into the sky,

"We'll just do it again then."

Most angels showed hostility but didn't express it out loud,

"Even if there is a chance of winning in heaven...

They are Gaian.

Memories of battles etched in the minds of the angels for eons passed by.

'Just when you think it's over, they tenaciously turn the situation around. That's the most annoying part.'

To fight or to compromise.

Among the divided opinions of the angels, Ikael stepped forward as the representative.

"You, who deny God, want to meet Him? No words can persuade Him."

"I can."

Ikael's eyebrows twitched.

"I will stay."

Amidst the murmuring angels, Ikael focused all her nerves on Goffin.

"I will give up becoming God. In return, let the remaining Gaians go. Would that count as a negotiation?"

"Uh..."

Ikael halted her words, realizing she was trying to gauge Anke Ra's intentions first.

"Agreed."

Her voice amplified through Ataraxia, the symbol of the archangel's power.

"I will arrange a meeting. However, you will be the only one who can participate."

"That’s sufficient."

As Goffin looked back, the Gaians filling the land simultaneously disconnected their links.

The angels held their ground as the meeting's outcome could lead to war.

Goffin, rising into the sky, approached Ikael.

"Shall we go?"

"Hmph."

She turned away coldly, but her face was contorted as she moved away quickly.

"Ha."

Why is this happening?

'How much have I regretted since that person disappeared?'

If only I had held on a little stronger at the last moment, if only I had thrown my heart out more honestly.

Goffin, who had quietly approached by her side, said,

"You look like you have something to say."

"That..."

Ikael's lips moved, but no words came out, as if something was blocking her throat.

"Do you plan to stay in heaven with us?"

She realized anew how difficult it was to put her heart into her voice.

"Hmm."

Goffin nodded.

"Perhaps that will be the case?"

"Yeah???"

Her response was lacking, but a faint smile formed on Ikael's lips as she turned her head back.

"Well thought. I will help you a lot."

Unaware that Goffin's eyes were filled with sorrow, Ikael arrived in Aravot.

"Anke Ra, Gaian representative Goffin McLain has requested a meeting."
