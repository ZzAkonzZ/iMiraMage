Chapter 618:

In Richard's room, filled with various mechanical parts, the sound of machinery echoed. Surrounded by mirrors, he meticulously adjusted his body with tools, a necessary task the day before the graduation exam.

The cover from his right chest to his side was open, revealing the core power unit attached to his heart, glowing red. His detached right arm lay between his legs, and after finishing the soldering, he attached the arm to his shoulder.

As the mechanical sounds, like scratching an itch, followed, his five fingers wriggled like octopus tentacles, and the ball bearings in his elbow rotated, performing movements impossible for a human.

"Hmm."

Richard was satisfied.

'Next, system synchronization.'

Winking one eye, he adjusted his brainwaves, causing the mechanical iris in his eye to emit a blue glow.

In the blue-tinted vision, like being underwater, numerous measurement data changed in real-time.

Richard's father, Ainca Marto, was the owner of Ainca Alchemy, the world's second-largest alchemy foundation, and the head of the Bio-Robotics Research Institute under Valkyrie.

The fact that a direct family member of such a prestigious family willingly participated in an experiment with less than a 30% success rate indicated the family's nature.

Even implanting a mechanical device in the brain had less than a 4% success rate, but Marto had no hesitation in opening his eldest son's skull.

'Heh, we have many siblings.'

What he gained from modifying 35% of his body with alchemy technology was the trust of the foundation's board and biologically impossible physical strength.

'Click correction.'

Extending his right arm and inputting a command, a crosshair appeared at the final point his fist aimed at.

'Activate combat simulation.'

The room transformed into a forest, and numerous monsters emerged from cover, charging at Richard.

'Equip fire magic stone.'

His elbow joint shook violently.

The magic stones Richard used were not the disposable ones sold on the market but special products of Ainca Alchemy, advanced materials mass-produced from the "Essence of Spirits," of which only 17 existed worldwide.

'Fire!'

As his right arm moved back and forth with a clanking sound, virtual fireballs shot out in rapid succession, knocking down the monsters.

'Switch magic stone. Ice.'

With a hissing sound, his elbow rotated, and sharp ice spikes were fired.

The automatic tracking function calculated the monsters' current coordinates and acceleration, selecting the primary target, and Richard only needed to shoot accordingly.

After annihilating 200 monsters, a mission complete signal appeared, followed by an analysis chart.

Three monsters had approached within a 20-meter radius, and the click error rate was 0%.

'Deactivate combat simulation.'

As the dense forest disappeared and the room's scenery returned, Richard smiled.

"Perfect."

Ainca Richard (Final Rank: 7th in the graduating class).

Major: Alchemy Engineering.

Special Notes: Modified 35% of his body with alchemy technology. Heir to the Ainca Foundation.

Amy and Shirone sat side by side on the bed, silent for a while.

"……"

The reason their hearts raced just by being next to each other was because it was a special day.

'Whatever the outcome…'

Today, the possibilities they dreamed of would become harsh realities.

"Do you think we'll pass?"

Amy, who had never relied on anyone else's judgment, genuinely wanted to hear it this time.

"Of course."

Even if it was a lie, Shirone needed the reassurance too.

"We've done our best. Think about all the days we've worked hard."

"But that's true for everyone. If we fail here…"

They would have to fight for another year.

No, could they really keep fighting?

"I'm not confident. I want to leave it to fate, but… it's hard."

"Amy."

Shirone turned her to face him, holding her shoulders.

"You're not inferior to anyone in the graduating class. I can vouch for that."

"Really?"

"Yes. You're one of the best students. Look at yourself objectively. No matter how fierce the competition, if you don't make mistakes, you can pass."

Thinking about Shirone's words, Amy realized he was right.

She had analyzed the graduating class for a year, and if she took the exam normally, she could achieve good results.

"Shirone."

As the tension faded, she finally noticed Shirone's face.

'What will happen to us?'

Reading Amy's expression, Shirone said,

"We are who we are. No matter the outcome, my feelings won't change."

Even if they both passed and moved on to a bigger world, their hearts would remain connected.

"Yes."

Her small voice felt like the world was closing its eyes, and Shirone slowly leaned in.

"Amy…"

As the sound faded, Amy gently lifted her chin.

They were close enough to feel each other's breath, and just before a monumental moment in their history…

'Wait, just a moment ago…'

Amy's eyes widened.

"Shirone, wait. Time out."

"Huh? What?"

Shirone, who had been leaning in completely, quickly pulled back, blushing.

"Why, what's wrong?"

"Well… it's just that right now might not be the best time. Actually…"

Amy glanced at the bathroom, and Shirone realized.

"Oh, right."

From a woman's perspective, a first kiss after vomiting would be a terrible memory.

In the awkward atmosphere, Shirone tapped his knees.

"Ahem, it can't be helped."

He couldn't hide his disappointment, and feeling guilty, Amy quickly added,

"It's not that I don't want to. Really. So…"

"I know. It's okay."

As Shirone smiled and reassured her, Amy shyly lowered her head and then asked hesitantly,

"Should I… go brush my teeth?"

Shirone couldn't help but laugh and fell back onto the bed.

None of the 30 students believed they would fail the next day.

No matter how the rankings were set, surprises could happen, and everyone had a secret strategy prepared for success.

"A strategic alliance?"

In the garden behind the graduating class building, Binder, Suabi, and Luman were having a secret conversation.

"Yes. You guys are in the utility series. My proposal isn't bad."

Binder, who organized the meeting, adjusted his horn-rimmed glasses.

He had barely made it into Class Two, but he was an excellent theorist, scoring 100 in advanced class theory and perfect marks in the graduating class's implementation evaluation.

"As you know, I scored full marks in all basic magic series. But it's true I don't have any special strengths."

Suabi and Luman listened intently to his cold analysis.

"But if you help me, my balance could become the best weapon. If Suabi's buffs focus solely on me and Luman's crowd control creates the right situations, we can face anyone in the graduating class."

Elcras Binder (Final Rank: 20th in the graduating class).

Major: Magic Analysis, Implementation Series.

Special Notes: Master of 36 types of regulated magic designated by the association.

"Hmm, it makes sense."

While Suabi accepted it positively, Luman scoffed.

"Do you really think that makes sense? In the end, only you benefit. High competition will break the team."

Luman, who had gained more weight from binge eating over the year, still had a quick mind.

"Maybe. So I have a proposal. If one of us has to drop out, I'll be the first to give up the exam."

This time, even Luman had to think carefully.

"Hmm, you're willing to take the risk in exchange for monopolizing the utility. But how can we trust you?"

"There's no way to prove it. We could write a secret contract, but that wouldn't guarantee 100% trust."

"Exactly. What if you betray us?"

"It won't happen, but if it does, I'll be branded a coward for life. It's not a bad proposal for you either. This contract perfectly complements our weaknesses."

Binder was the only one in the graduating class who would offer utility in exchange for risking elimination.

"I'm in."

Suabi raised her hand first.

"In the six graduation exam categories, four involve combat skills. As a utility, teaming up with Binder isn't a loss. We're strong in the remaining two categories."

"Adding me makes it perfect."

If Binder and Suabi had already decided, Luman knew joining them would maximize synergy.

"Alright. Let's team up. But there's a condition. If someone has to drop out after Binder, it will be Suabi. If not, I'm out."

It was a selfish demand, but Luman's crowd control could quickly turn the tide, making him essential.

"Fine. I'll be the second. But remember, this strategy assumes all three of us will pass."

"Don't worry. I'm the only crowd controller in the graduating class. Whether it's 15 against 15 or 1 against 29, I'll control the situation."

Podran Luman (Final Rank: 16th in the graduating class).

Major: Trap Magic, Crowd Control Series.

Special Notes: Binge eating. Excellent situational judgment.

"Good. Let's go with this. Let's make sure all three of us are among the successful candidates tomorrow."

By sunset, all 30 graduating students had finished their preparations.

The morning of the graduation exam dawned.

The students waited in their rooms to be called, while in front of the large colosseum where the exam would be held, scouts from various regions, advanced class students, and the parents of the exam participants had already gathered, creating a bustling scene.

"Scouts, please enter first and take your designated seats!"

As the official opened the west gate, 287 scouts from 65 countries under the Red Line moved forward.

Most of the parents gathered there were notable figures in the kingdom, but their eyes were filled with earnest hope as they watched the scouts who would determine their children's futures.

Knowing this, the scouts either looked straight ahead or reviewed the student profiles they had received in advance, avoiding eye contact.

"Time flies. That troublemaker is already taking the graduation exam."

A man who looked just like Iruki stepped out of a splendid carriage.

The arrival of Mercodine Albino, the head of Yongroe, drew the attention of all the parents.

"It's Albino."

It was quite a shock that the kingdom's top intellect was personally attending.

"If Albino is here… could it be?"

"Don't be ridiculous. How much trouble could Iruki have caused?"

When Iruki's mother stepped out, she drew even more attention than Albino.

A stunning woman in her mid-thirties, she was the kingdom's top actress, Magellan Argannes.

It was said that humans were made in the image of gods, but Argannes was considered more beautiful than any deity. She was the epitome of beauty in the Tormia Kingdom and was already famous worldwide.

"A goddess."

Someone muttered, forgetting their noble demeanor, and everyone agreed.

"To think we'd see Argannes in person…"

For the parents, today was a historic day.
