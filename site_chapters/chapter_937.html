Chapter 937: Fanaticism (1)

"Damn it!"

The faces of the demons charging towards Dante's Information Castle twisted in anger.

'A siege defense out of nowhere.'

And not just any makeshift fortification, but a fully completed structure specialized in defense.

"Attack!"

As the army of Hell reached their conclusion, 3,000 cannons fired all sorts of magic.

Though it was basic pure attribute magic, the firepower of 9,000 rounds per second was overwhelming.

"Argh!"

The leading demons fell, and the second line, stepping over their bodies, didn't last much longer.

The piling bodies began to reveal a specific boundary in the struggle between firepower and the army's strength.

'Is this even possible?'

The pinnacle of magic.

Too complex for one human to handle, but for Dante, it was his specialty and profession.

"Charge! Overwhelm them with numbers!"

To breach an emotionless castle, the demons had to accept sacrifices.

"Go! Go!"

As they charged, ready to die, the boundary of power began to push towards the Information Castle.

"Almost there! Just a bit more!"

As the leading demons approached within 40 meters, Dante propped his feet on the table and lit a cigarette.

"Activate Automaton."

Tilting his head and lighting the cigarette, the castle rose over a meter.

Rumble!

The blood-soaked leading demons couldn't believe their eyes.

"What, what is this?"

No, they didn't want to believe it.

"The castle is moving."

Though slow, the sight of the massive castle moving was spectacular.

But for the demons watching, it was infuriating enough to make them want to kill anyone nearby.

Exhaling a long puff of smoke, Dante leaned back in his chair and watched the multivision.

"If only it were faster."

Automaton moved all the magic circle coordinates within the Information Castle simultaneously.

Precise like clockwork, but the speed depended entirely on Pascal's calculation speed.

'If only I had 10 more years, or even 5.'

He regretted not being able to create an impregnable system.

'No excuses.'

Dante smiled wryly and stubbed out his cigarette.

"Some people still manage to do it."

Thinking of Shirone, who had widened the gap, Dante began to operate the castle in earnest.

The demons following the retreating Information Castle were filled with murderous intent.

"These demons are going crazy!"

Even at a slow pace, the number of demons needed to advance a meter was a clear advantage.

The demon formation, which had dominated the horizon, now focused on the Information Castle.

Lupist, watching from the Tormia camp, muttered in disbelief.

"He's moving the castle?"

Though the demon army numbered in the hundreds of millions, it wasn't enough to completely change the flow.

"Impressive."

But if one person could create such a disruption, the efficiency was roughly 10,000 times greater.

"Tormia has many talents."

Garcia, the second legion commander of the Holy War and Amy's mentor, had approached unnoticed.

Lupist, offering a brief courtesy, spoke.

"He was once considered a talent representing the kingdom. There's no disagreement even now..."

"You're talking about Shirone. It's enviable."

There wasn't a mage in the world who didn't know Yahweh was from the Kingdom of Tormia.

"I'm not particularly proud. In a way, that child doesn't belong to the kingdom."

He was already beyond the system.

"There's no point in clinging to what can't be controlled. What I'm concerned about is that."

Lupist pointed to the Information Castle.

"The 3rd Division will support the defense. No matter what happens, bring Dante back."

"Yes!"

Knowing Dante's strategic value, the division commander moved the troops accordingly.

Seeing them on the multivision, Dante lowered the stairs from the castle wall.

"Let's start here. Deactivate Automaton."

Stopping the calculations needed for movement allowed various systems to activate.

Firepower naturally surged, and the demons were shattered in front of the castle walls.

"Argh! Argh!"

As the demons' screams echoed through the speakers, the screen flashed red.

An enemy had entered a blind spot.

At that moment, Dante operated the device.

"Got it! Break it down!"

As the demons reached the castle gate and raised a massive hammer, a voice came from the door.

-Enter the password.

"Huh? Password?"

Looking closely, they saw a panel with numbers and symbols near the doorknob.

As the demon, half-open-mouthed, reached to press the keys,

Bang! Bang! Bang!

Cannons fired from the inner castle, striking the demons in front of the wall.

"You idiots! Just break it down!"

As the vanguard was annihilated, the second line of demons swung massive scythes and headed for the gate.

"I'll cut it down!"

At that moment, Dante grabbed the microphone and spoke.

-The password is 1111.

The demon, who had raised both arms, hesitated with an internal conflict, then lowered the scythe and extended a finger.

"Damn it!"

Pressing the number 1 four times, the demon found the door unchanged and shouted.

"What? It won't open!"

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

As the second round of cannons fired, annihilating the second line, Dante's voice came through.

-...You need to press enter.

The demons' eyes filled with rage.

"Is he toying with us?"

From then on, it was pure destructive madness, with all sorts of weapons striking the gate.

"It's damn sturdy!"

Though more durable than expected, the immense mass pushing from behind couldn't be stopped.

"We broke through!"

As the gate opened and the warning voice echoed from the control room ceiling, Dante stood up.

"Activate resistance system."

The demons pouring into the gate like vomit were stunned by the interior.

"Is this a real castle?"

Vertical stone paths, fountains, and statues were visible on the grassy grounds.

The inner castle, surrounded by a moat, was manned by Tormia's 3rd Division.

"At least there's something to kill! Let's go!"

As the demons headed for the inner castle, mines exploded on the grass, and cannons emerged from the statues.

The fountains circulating flammable liquid ignited, sending fireballs in all directions.

"Run! Run!"

It was one obstacle after another.

As they entered the drawbridge to cross the moat, a hologram of a beautiful woman appeared.

-Welcome to Dante's Castle!

"Shut up!"

Even knowing it was fake, they swung their claws, but the ground beneath their feet vanished.

As the drawbridge disappeared, they fell into the moat, where powerful toxins corroded their skin.

"Argh!"

The green liquid bubbled, and snakes emerged, spraying acid everywhere.

'What is this place?'

Wondering how many more traps they had to pass to reach the lord, the demons...

"Damn it."

Felt a chilling dread just before death. The greatness of the Information Castle lay in its ability to twist the demons' path by at least 0.1 degrees.

'0.1 degrees.'

Though barely noticeable to the eye, Lupist was satisfied with that.

'The important thing is that it twisted.'

Even if the starting point seemed the same, the difference would become stark over distance.

'Iron Wave!'

Waves of iron pushed the ground, and Garcia's inferno flames burned the enemies.

In the air, Plu's phoenix formation spewed fire according to the algorithm.

"Hahaha! Humans! Humans!"

The battlefield resembled hell, but for the demons, it was the most comfortable place.

'That's the problem. It's hard to break their morale.'

Beyond physical abilities, the demons' belligerence was unmatched by humans.

Seeing the allied forces retreat, Enox, the king of the elves, raised her arm.

"The enemy of humans is the enemy of elves."

Lupist had requested the demon army be diverted towards Bashka. Even a simple analogy required altering their path by over 30 degrees.

"Open the Flower Field!"

The ground shook, and over 300 massive structures spread their steel petals wide.

A shadow covering several kilometers spread, and immense energy seemed ready to explode from the petals.

"What is that..."

Enox lowered her arm.

"Annihilate!"

The flash weapon's power scorched the earth, instantly wiping out 3,000 demons.

"Scatter! Break through the flash!"

Orderly?

No, the formation scattered into near chaos, making it harder for the attackers.

If the pilot of the bioweapon had been human.

"This is no problem."

Connected to the bioweapon through the small world window, Flarino quickly adjusted the targeting points.

Controlling it at the speed of nerves, it was no different from stepping on ants for her.

The roar buried all sounds, and the demons burned just from being grazed by the flash.

Even while fighting, Lupist meticulously observed the battlefield after retreating to the Flower Field.

'We're doing better than expected.'

The rate at which the demons were decreasing due to the Hua tribe's strikes far exceeded the standard.

'Should I be happy?'

In 30 minutes, the number of dead demons was roughly over 150,000.

In other words, the firepower had wiped out an entire army of a small kingdom in 30 minutes.

The problem was that this enormous number was less than 0.1 percent of the entire demon army.

'Meanwhile, the allied forces' casualties are about 4 percent.'

As time passed, the asymmetry of power would accelerate, but that wasn't the main issue.

'They will keep coming.'

No matter how many they killed, the soldiers would feel the enemy was infinite.

When standing under a waterfall, one doesn't think about when the stream will stop.

'That's what it means to be in the hundreds of millions.'

This was precisely why Iruki risked everything on the Elemental Bomb Project.

Balkhan, positioned at the center of the army, rested his chin on his hand.

"Hmm."

Through the crowd control device, he observed the entire situation unfolding in the Flower Field.

'They're definitely being lured in. And the bioweapons...'

Watching the continuous firing of the flashes, he nodded.

"Judging by the reaction speed relative to its size, it's not a machine. Humans would be even slower."

He reached a conclusion.

"It's the Hua tribe."

It couldn't be explained without the small world window.

Balkhan called out, but Smoto was still fixated on the Information Castle at the horizon.

"What are you looking at? Want to go?"

Snapping out of it, Smoto turned to Balkhan.

"No, I just like to hit all the hot spots when I travel."

Balkhan smirked.

"I'd like to send you, but not now. Focus on the ancient weapon. Where is it weakest?"

Smoto, with his obsessive-compulsive tendencies, identified the most disturbed movements among the 300 bioweapons.

"Northwest. The twenty-third, forty-seventh, and eighty-eighth. Those three are different. They seem scared."

Balkhan nodded.

"Seize one. If you capture even one, you can destroy all the nearby bioweapons. Go with Zetaro."

"Okay."

As Smoto and Zetaro took the lead, elite demons quickly followed. Natasha pointed to herself and asked,

"Balkhan, what about me?"

Though Balkhan pondered carefully, there was only one task Natasha could do.

"Kill him."

The Knight of Maha.

Hearing the welcome news, Natasha turned her gaze to the Flower Field, her eyes gleaming.

"Perfect."

Rian's fighting spirit, rising from the center of the Flower Field, was drawing her in.
