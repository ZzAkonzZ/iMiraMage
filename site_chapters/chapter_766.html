Chapter 766: Destruction (1)

"Execute Yahweh!"

The 8 million demons in the capital of Fasia were only the tip of the iceberg.

It was nothing short of Hell.

In the blazing inferno stretched a line of demons that seemed to have no end.

"What if it’s the Walking Dead?"

Minerva understood what Shirone was thinking.

"You’re wondering if it could wipe out all demons?"

The Spiritual Realm was merely a passageway between the real and Under Worlds—if an infection were to begin here, it could spread through all of Hell.

"You’re the one who said not to use forbidden spells."

"That was because I didn’t know what it was. If the enemy is demons..."

"If it were possible, I would’ve done it already."

When Shirone didn’t respond, Minerva confessed honestly.

"The truth is, I already tried."

"What happened?"

Minerva shook her head.

"The Legion Commanders aren’t fools. Within 2 minutes and 32 seconds of the infection spreading, they quarantined the entire area."

"Was it an ability?"

"Yeah. Likely the 9th Legion Commander, Paimon. She handles military command within Hell’s army. Unless we go into full-scale war, she’s not someone we’ll run into immediately..."

Minerva shifted direction toward a region where animal bones were piled like mountains.

"The millions infected in Fasia didn’t even meet the distance needed to trigger Paimon’s quarantine. The capital itself ended up being a perfect quarantine zone."

There were 2 billion troops in Hell’s army, and they were being endlessly produced.

"With your power now, you could probably take on two Legion Commanders—but the ones ranked in the top ten, be careful. Their Ma density is on another level."

Shirone took it to heart.

"We’re almost there."

Below the blazing clouds of flame, dozens of structures spiked upward like obsidian spears.

It was the fortress of Maraduke, who had lost his master.

"This your first time sealing a Legion Commander’s domain?"

The only way to seal an altar was to eradicate all Mal in the region.

"Yes."

Since the Inner World itself was made of Ma, the key was to sever the tributaries of Ma known as "fireplates."

"We still don’t know where the original source lies."

Currently, armies from around the world and Valkyries from the Holy War were risking their lives to explore the Under World.

"In any case, the Hellfire that originates from the source flows through the Inner World like a circulatory system, supplying Ma everywhere."

After passing through the mountain range of animal bones, Maraduke’s fortress came fully into view.

"Dear God..."

Even Shirone, who had sealed several altars, was rendered speechless at the sight.

A river of lava, over ten kilometers wide, burned with a blinding crimson light as it wound its way through the land.

"This is the upper stream of Hellfire."

Just as civilization in the real world is based on water, the Inner World flowed with rivers of fire.

"Help me! Please, someone save me!"

Thousands of humans thrashed with half-melted arms in the lava.

"The dead..."

"To be exact, the minds of the dead, condensed into Ma. The fear of death is overwhelmingly powerful for ordinary humans."

"Hell is a reflection of the human heart."

Zulu's insight had been correct.

Ultimately, the dead who could not withstand the pain of Hellfire would be reborn as demons under Satan’s will.

Gaold...

He had not succumbed.

Which is why, even now...

Unaware that Gaold had regained consciousness, Shirone prayed he would return soon.

"Let’s descend."

As Jet dove rapidly toward the ground, Shirone followed with a swift contraction of his Light Wings.

The roar of the burning lava was enough to deafen them, and in the distance, the residents of the Inner World were gathered in a ring formation.

Minerva ignored them and pointed toward the lava.

"What do you think? Can you destroy it?"

From the ground, the river of Hellfire looked as vast as an ocean.

"I can’t promise. Hellfire is far more persistent than demons. Even removing one stream took everything I had."

Minerva nodded.

"It’s the original substance of Ma. At this scale, it’s equivalent to the emotional weight of about 500 million humans in the real world. The problem is, as long as Hellfire flows, demons can resist the light of Yahweh."

That’s why sealing a Legion Commander’s domain was far more difficult than an ordinary altar’s Spiritual Domain.

"There’s only one way."

Minerva pointed toward Maraduke’s fortress.

"Within every Legion Commander’s fortress is a fireplate. It regulates the density of Hellfire to create powerful demons. We need to seize the device that controls its flow."

With the conclusion clear, the two launched straight toward the fortress.

Though the residents couldn’t approach Shirone, the army stationed at the fortress was burning with determination.

"It’s Yahweh! Stop him no matter what!"

They were the ones whose lifeline he had come to sever.

Countless troops, led by Division Commanders, gathered at the entrance.

"Open the way."

In the very heart of concentrated Ma, Yahweh’s light couldn’t serve as healing—but Shirone stepped forward anyway.

"Angel’s Punishment."

The Incarnation of the Archangel rose to massive proportions, gripping a spear of light and gazing down upon the earth.

"This is my whole."

Fullness.

Through Resonance, quantum information was unified—Shirone no longer needed 100,000 versions of himself.

"Repent."

And finally, as the spear of light stretched into a radiant line and plunged down—

"...!"

The light struck first, followed by a shockwave that turned the army of Hell into ash.

"Wh-what?!"

Demons watching from afar were stunned as a vast dust cloud rushed in on the wind.

"RAAAAH!"

The sound ripped through the air, shaking heaven and earth, and the terrified demons turned and fled.

"R-Run!"

Just as they thought they would never again set foot here, the dust cloud engulfed them.

Minerva shielded her face from the flesh-shearing wind and frowned.

"Pffff—!"

Moments later, the wind reversed.

The shockwave had created a vacuum that now sucked in the surrounding atmosphere.

"Let’s see here..."

In front of Minerva was a crater that could have only been formed by a meteor strike.

"No doubt about it... this is the realm of Destruction."

With a human body, he had finally reached it.

What comes next?

Beyond the thrill of the awe-inspiring moment, a grave expression surfaced on Minerva’s face.

"Huuuurgh!"

Aset Meirei, the one who corresponds to Hearing among the Great Purifiers, rolled her eyes back in a sudden gasp inside a private room of the Tormia Magic Association.

“What happened?”

A mage, assigned to guard her room under Lupist’s orders, burst through the door.

“Yes?”

Meirei blinked as she turned to him.

“No, just now… I heard something…”

“A sound? Nothing happened, ma'am.”

The mage glanced around the room. It was as tidy and quiet as could be—no sign of disturbance.

‘A bodily function, maybe?’

If it were a woman, it could be something embarrassing.

“Understood. Please call if you need anything.”

“Yes, thank you for your concern.”

The mage failed to notice that the human emotion had vanished from Meirei’s calm gaze.

“Well then…”

As the door shut, the faint trace of humanity vanished completely from her face, leaving only the mask of a flawless doll.

“High Judge.”

Covering one ear, Meirei responded. A message came through from the High Judge of the Theraphos.

—A force exceeding biological limitations has just been detected.

“I see. Who is it?”

—Humanity’s representative, Hexa.

“….”

She realized why the High Judge hesitated.

“What is the ruling from the Assembly?”

The reason Theraphos is referred to as an evil deity in human scriptures is because they have indeed destroyed countless civilizations.

But they are not invaders.

In fact, they are guardians of cosmic order. For that reason, the Star of Heaven once proposed this hypothesis:

User Protection System.

It’s something that can be inferred from the Absolute Three Principles recorded in the Great Codex of the Theraphos species:

One. We shall promote the advancement of humanity and intervene in its history if necessary. One. If evil triumphs completely in the conflict of good and evil, we shall destroy that civilization. One. If any sentient being attempts to breach the higher system, we shall actively prevent it.

The purpose of the Theraphos seems less about racial interests and more about maintaining the balance of the universe.

And that balance is judged not by human notions of happiness or suffering, but by the existence of being itself.

‘So that no single variable disrupts the balance… so that no cheats are committed in the fierce game of life.’

Judges of the Law.

Having come to understand the Theraphos through countless revelations, Meirei waited calmly for the next words.

—Hexa’s “Ruin” violates the third clause of the Absolute Three Principles, but some Judges interpret it by focusing on the second clause instead.

There is no hierarchy among the principles. That’s why they are all labeled “One.”

—In the end, if evil wins, we enact destruction. And now that good and evil have reached their final standoff, there are Judges who believe that Hexa’s actions might still allow for a grace period.

“That’s reasonable. If evil wins, everything ends. But Hexa’s Ruin only constitutes active intervention.”

—You’re sharper than the Assembly.

It was clearly a joke—but from it, Meirei could gauge where the High Judge’s opinion leaned.

‘Which also means the opposition is considerable.’

The Theraphos can hear every sound in the universe—Meirei’s thoughts were no exception.

—Yes. Judgment Day may come sooner than expected. Though the final decision will be mine.

“What is my role?”

—Go to Sion. Your ears will be needed to oppose evil. If there's any chance good might prevail, the Assembly’s decision may change.

“Understood.”

Realizing the call hadn’t ended yet, Meirei quickly asked,

“I do have one question. If Theraphos guided humanity… then who established the Absolute Three Principles that guide the Theraphos?”

A natural curiosity—she was human, after all.

—Just as you suspect.

The connection ended.

“Anke Ra…”

And now, it was Nane.

Fiercely staring ahead, Meirei suddenly rose and threw open the door.

“Let me speak to the Association President!”

Shirone and Minerva, having seized Maraduke’s fortress, found the “Furnace” on the eighth basement floor.

Inside the giant smelting chamber, several hundred meters across, lava seethed as the souls of the dead flailed helplessly.

“Horrifying.”

If not for Yahweh’s light shielding them from the heat, they would have combusted on the spot.

“Which means that equally horrifying demons must be born from it. They probably draw Hellfire from there.”

Dozens of drainage channels embedded in the walls constantly fed lava into the system.

“If we cut off the upstream fireflow, the demons in Maraduke’s territory will lose their strength. They’ll probably get reassigned to other Legion Commanders’ zones.”

“What if they reopen the furnace later?”

“We make sure they don’t. North Aymond is already in anarchy, so we’ll notify the Chancellor of South Aymond. The Holy War can dispatch a garrison if needed.”

“Nothing ever comes easy.”

“That’s life. Now you see why the Stars are always busy? You could circle the whole planet and still not be done.”

Shirone approached the control mechanism of the Furnace.

“This is pretty old-fashioned.”

“You know how it is—Ma reuse all the stuff humans discarded.”

“…”

Just as he reached for the switch meshed with hundreds of interlocking cogs, a sound echoed from the furnace.

“S-Spare me…”

A single demon clawed their way out of the blazing lava, dragging their mangled body onto the floor.

“Please… the pain is unbearable…”

The wretched creature crawled forward, but Shirone didn’t let go of the switch.

“Aaaah… Yahweh…”

The demon suddenly lunged, baring its fangs.

“What makes you so special?!”

A blast from Shirone’s Photon Cannon sent the demon hurtling back into the molten core.

“Don’t act pure! You’re just like us! I hate you! Arrogant Yahweh…!”

The demon dissolved into its base essence.

“….”

With sorrowful eyes, Shirone watched the scene unfold, then slowly pulled the switch down.

Clunk. Clunk. Clunk.

The sound of turning gears echoed, achingly mournful.
