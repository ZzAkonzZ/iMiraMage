Chapter 942: Hidden Code (1)

Shiok.

As the fanatic who follows Satan appeared, Havitz stepped back as if he had nothing to do.

"Shiok is human?"

Even while looking at the woman's face with her hood off, Sein wondered.

"Why is she human?"

Of course, the concept of Satan is also performed by the human Havitz, so it's not unusual.

Miro for the extreme good, Nane for emptiness, Shirone for philanthropy.

Any concept needs hardware to manifest as a phenomenon.

Therefore, Sein's question was not a logical error but purely an emotional issue.

In other words, why must Shiok also be human?

"Because they can choose."

Being created to follow Satan and choosing to follow Satan are different.

Fanaticism.

The act of throwing one's mind in the most dangerous way.

"Even if Shiok's will is separate from Havitz's, it's ultimately the same as Havitz's will."

For example, wanting to kill someone.

If you act directly, it will be troublesome, and if you try to hire someone, it will leave a trail.

"But what if someone acts on their own?"

Could there be a more efficient existence as Satan's hands and feet?

"No need to hide anymore."

The remaining 11 Shioks, who had been covering their faces, simultaneously pulled back their hoods.

Women, old people, children.

Common appearances that could be seen anywhere in society.

A woman with a cut on her forehead glared at Kuan.

"An attack coming from outside perception. Interesting. I never thought I'd meet someone else who handles hidden codes."

Kuan asked back.

"Hidden code?"

"You wouldn't know. It's the unknown knowledge of the hidden world. For you to perceive the world logically, illogical codes must be tested. It's a code used in a kind of test mode. We call it a cheat."

Sein didn't understand.

"You think this world is logical. But it's not. You just adapted to the code of reality."

She raised her index finger.

"In dreams, no matter how absurd the events, they make sense, right? Of course. It's called Drimo, where many codes of the hidden world go in and get filtered. Only codes with ensured stability are applied to reality. In the end, what you believe to be logic is just rules set for convenience by those who created this world."

Sein's group listened blankly.

"Codes deemed useless for the real world in Drimo, those without ensured stability, escape through a buffer called 'Under Coder.' There, they combine with discarded information from the real world."

Sein asked.

"And after they combine?"

"They are endlessly stored in a system called 'The Abyss.'"

Miro had mentioned it before.

'The final destination of all information.'

A place where the last future the world reaches based on the current state is implemented.

'She said it was like hell.'

"It's a very interesting place."

Shiok thought differently.

"All processes until the end of the world are accumulated there. If you carefully examine the ruins or records lying there, you can know what happens at specific future points. Go there once. It's really fun."

Sein shook his head.

"If the present changes, the future changes too. If we win, The Abyss won't be hell."

"It's all predetermined."

The woman sneered.

"The hidden world, reality, Drimo, Under Coder, The Abyss. Five systems operate simultaneously in the same timeframe. Can you imagine? What can you change?"

Sein remained silent.

"Listen carefully. Humans are just tenants using the space called reality. You can't change the future."

"But all systems are operated for the space called reality. If humans are the users as you say, we have the right to reject the current world."

"Hahaha!"

The woman, who had been laughing madly, suddenly stopped and put on a cold expression.

"There was a time when that was possible."

There was an era when humans defined everything.

"But not you. You're not connected. How can humans who don't realize the missing link change the law? Sure, some humans handle the administrator code. But do you think that will make this world budge?"

As Sein closed his mouth, the woman looked at Kuan.

"In that sense, I want to praise you. It's quite a good cheat. But we're on a different level."

The blood flowing from her forehead disappeared, and even the wound from the sword began to fade.

"Healing power? No, the wound itself is disappearing."

Meirei muttered.

"Pride's 1 o'clock."

"Huh?"

As he looked at her, the woman's wave was transmitted as an auditory hallucination through "Nemesis."

"......Unbelievable."

It was an incomprehensible phenomenon.

"Yes, I am Pride's 1 o'clock. I handle the hidden code that can change past choices."

"Nonsense."

Kuan spat out.

"I still clearly remember the moment I cut you. My memory isn't a lie."

"That's your thought. What do I care?"

Pride's 1 o'clock shrugged.

"Still don't understand? The logic you know isn't absolute. This is a cheat you don't know. Look, the wound is gone. I regretted it. If I had bent my upper body a little more, I wouldn't have been cut. So I changed my choice."

The wave of her mind was telling the truth.

'Is it real?'

Pride's 1 o'clock can change past choices and output results through the butterfly effect.

'Endlessly changing.'

This means that all regrettable events in someone's life are erased, supporting the phenomenon where Havitz's decisions are always right.

'This is dangerous.'

Kuan, who played the role of a hitman in the Holy War, instinctively knew how difficult it was to kill Pride's 1 o'clock.

'I have to create a death without regret.'

Two main methods came to mind.

'Make her seek death herself, or drive her into a situation where death is unavoidable.'

In both cases, the emotion of 'regret' wouldn't be triggered, so she couldn't change her past choices...

'Is it even possible?'

He didn't think he could do it.

-This is Meirei. From now on, I'll eavesdrop on all 12 Shiok's waves. Remember them clearly.

Shiok charged.

"For Satan's pleasure!"

Sein, Armin, and Meirei couldn't move as they were blocking Havitz's law with their respective magic.

"Absolute Barrier!"

As Eden deployed a barrier, Shiok scattered, and Lilia amplified Kuan's law.

Kuan's figure disappeared from Shiok's perception.

'I can't rush in recklessly.'

Meanwhile, through "Nemesis," the names and hidden codes of Shiok echoed in his mind.

Sein stored Pride's 1 o'clock in his memory.

'A woman with a fierce impression and sharp teeth. She can change past regrettable events, and it's a cheat because the process is ignored.'

The next wave came.

'A man with a bald head. Defiance's 2 o'clock. The hidden code wins in all kinds of probabilities?'

Havitz came to mind first.

Shiok's abilities were derived from Satan's chaos, so it was natural.

"Kuan! Kill 1 o'clock!"

Shiok couldn't find Kuan anyway, so he shouted with all his might.

'Pride's 1 o'clock, who reverses past choices, is the top priority for elimination. Even killing one will create a hole in Shiok.'

At least for now.

"Foolish!"

A woman in her mid-30s with owl-like big eyes and prominent cheekbones shouted.

"Why kill Pride's 1 o'clock? Do you hate being first? How can you command a battle with such paranoid thoughts? Therefore, you're trash!"

Sein couldn't think of a reply.

'Why?'

Realizing he couldn't refute her, he heard the wave Meirei eavesdropped on.

'Stubbornness's 3 o'clock. The hidden code... wins in all kinds of rebuttals? What is this?'

Detailed information followed.

'...Because no matter what argument you make, you can't break her logic. Damn, where does this nonsense come from?'

As a logician, Sein tried to counter her code with more intensity.

"Among the three analyzed so far, 1 o'clock has the most significant impact on the battle. Got it? I'm not strategizing based on paranoia."

"Oh, I don't know! Annoying! Your face looks weird, and why is your voice like that? The conclusion is, you're wrong!"

The cheat was applied.

The logical connections forming Sein's thoughts were destroyed, causing dizziness and anger.

"Ugh!"

Fire boiled inside, but with reality's logic gone, he couldn't refute.

'Is this the hidden code?'

Not being able to change the past, syllogism, probability being mathematical.

'These propositions are just human thoughts. Rules set for the administrator's convenience.'

From outside the universe, it doesn't matter how the world operates.

"Suddenly silent? Well, you're full of contradictions. On the other hand, I only made claims based on facts. Therefore, you're a fool!"

"......Shut up."

"Shut up? What to shut? Slap your cheek!"

Feeling like he really should, Sein gritted his teeth in defeat and lowered his head.

'Damn! Why can't I win?'

As everyone kept silent, Stubbornness's 3 o'clock laughed heartily.

"Hahaha! Not worshipping Satan, your life is a failure! Long live Satan! Oh, Satan!"

As Sein ignored her and turned his head, another wave of the mind came.

'Laziness's 4 o'clock.'

An old man with sagging cheeks, and soon the hidden code was revealed.

Unwanted things don't happen.

'Damn it.'

Cold sweat ran down Sein's back.

'Havitz and Shiok were avoiding the law like this. So should I kill 4 o'clock first and then 1 o'clock? Or, depending on the situation, maybe even 2 o'clock... Wait a minute.'

'But Kuan injured 1 o'clock.'

If the pinnacle of asymmetry is also a cheat, it means Shiok couldn't respond perfectly.

"Lilia..."

Sein, who was about to give a verbal order, realized Stubbornness's 3 o'clock and used "Nemesis."

-Lilia, when you see an opening, suddenly amplify Kuan's law. The wider, the better.

Lilia, who nodded, planted her oak staff into the ground and watched the battlefield.

At that moment, a plump woman with a dissatisfied expression shouted,

"That woman is a traitor!"

"What?"

Everyone turned to look at Lilia.

"What nonsense..."

Lilia, who was shouting with a frown, was startled by the looks of her comrades focusing on her.

"Why, why are you looking at me like that?"

Meirei eavesdropped on the wave of the woman who had shouted a moment ago.

'Prejudice's 5 o'clock. The hidden code... everyone trusts it.'

'This is ridiculous.' Sein felt it was a powerful code that could make people believe even a good person was a murderer.

"Wait a minute! Why would I betray you?" She knew in her head.

'Yes, there's no reason for her to betray us. She's from Zion and a nun who hates evil.'

But cheats don't follow the logic of reality.

As the hidden code was input into everyone's minds, endless doubts arose in their brains.

'Was her life guaranteed? Or did she collude with evil to gain higher fame...'

Anything could be doubted.

'Come to think of it, Lilia went out of the room alone a month ago. Yes, her tone was strange too.'

Even lifelong couples can be torn apart by a single trivial doubt in our lives.

"No! I'm not a traitor!"

It is the tragedy of the human race.
