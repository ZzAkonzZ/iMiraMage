Chapter 548:

"Shirone!"

Rian, with the sun at his back, raised his greatsword high.

There was no way to recover Shirone’s memories without inflicting a shock akin to death—or even greater—on her mind.

‘Shirone, this is...’

Gritting his teeth, Rian swung the greatsword with all his might.

‘This is my greatest strike!’

As the greatsword slashed through the air at an unbelievable speed, a black fissure traced the path of the blazing trajectory.

As Rian's Deny disrupted the law, the barrier surrounding the pyramid peeled away like the skin of a grape.

‘Deny... How infuriating.’

Shura scowled and muttered under her breath.

Although there was no time to turn and look back, the terrifying force imbued in the greatsword was unmistakably transmitted through the air.

“...”

Shirone simply observed without any reaction.

Even Armand’s robe, which was supposed to respond to the faintest nerve stimulus, showed no signs of defensive movement.

As expected, the greatsword came to an abrupt halt before his, and Shirone spoke to Rian, whose frustration was clearly written across his face.

“Rian, you can’t make me awaken.”

“Ugh!”

As Shirone watched Rian’s trembling hands gripping the greatsword, his gaze shifted to the path he had taken to get here.

“You’ve cut down hundreds to reach this place...”

His eyes turned back to Rian.

“But I feel no hostility in your blade.”

As if acknowledging her words, the trembling in Rian’s arms subsided.

“You’re right. You’re absolutely right.”

He couldn’t kill her.

The Shirone Rian knew was not someone so weak that his mind would awaken merely at the brink of death.

“Rian, how much of this is true? What are you... to me?”

“I am your sword.”

Rian swallowed a bitter groan.

“My flesh, my blood, my bones—they are all yours!”

Rian’s voice, as if embodying the hand of truth, gripped Shirone’s heart, and a flood of memories from the distant past began to surge into his mind.

‘But… if what Rian says is true, if all of this is a lie, then what am I?’

Shura shouted.

“What are you doing? Kill him already!”

A blade aimed at his neck came flying toward him, forcing Rian to twist his greatsword to block it. His eyes widened.

“Huh!”

The incredible force, strong enough to destroy Deny, sent Rian flying as Shura leapt off the staircase in pursuit.

‘What a mystery.’

To her, Rian was an anomaly even within the vast span of 10,000 years.

‘What law is this?’

The Yacha’s Axing distorts laws, but whether the body can withstand it is another matter.

Typically, the more the distortion intensifies, the more grotesquely twisted the laws affecting the body become.

This was why no senior member of the Shilo Council was a normal human.

‘It’s not Schema, though.’

In contrast, Rian’s Axing didn’t consider his body at all.

Perhaps his transcendent regeneration was his Axing, but even a Death Knight could not climb out of the realm of death back into the realm of life.

‘Shall I confirm it?’

The terrifying spinning force of Shura’s body, propelled by the explosion of Nirvana Energy, began to unleash.

With a series of laws sequentially implemented, the devastating eight consecutive strikes seemed ready to rip Rian’s body apart.

“Rian! Ugh!”

As Shirone tried to rush to Rian, an immense amount of information suddenly invaded her consciousness.

Through the Mucus, the Digital Ra attempted to unify their consciousnesses with Shirone’s.

Human, become one with me.

“Not yet, not yet!”

Rian couldn’t leave this world as long as the scar of truth engraved on his heart throbbed painfully.

You and I will change the world.

“Aaaaaah!”

As the Digital Ra connected to Shirone’s brain, their memories began merging into one.

‘Kwanum Thunderstrike Field.’

As Kwanum’s hand flew toward the pyramid, Shura stood in its path, activating the law of defense.

Gestalt of Condensation—Ten Commandments of Denial.

A massive inverted cross materialized in front of Shura, blocking Kwanum’s hand.

“This is driving me insane. Let’s settle this outside.”

Miro gritted her teeth hard.

For a human in an open state to wield the laws of a trapped world was like freely building with toy blocks.

If the pyramid in reality was a singular 1 in the law’s count, the pyramid here—referred to by Shura—would correspond to a 2.

The boundaries of the laws were fragmented like blocks, and the limits of the world were clearly defined.

This meant Shura’s laws were executed as clearly and unambiguously as magic in reality.

“So you want to see this through to the end, huh?”

Miro, who had been glaring at Shura’s purely defensive stance, amplified the scale of her actions.

“Eeeek! Eeeek!”

Meanwhile, Marsha, struggling against the mucus, shouted at Fermi as escape seemed increasingly unlikely.

“Do something! We’re going to die here!”

Fermi, also restrained, clicked his tongue as he assessed Shirone’s condition.

‘Tch, no choice.’

Although it wasn’t enough, they couldn’t afford to delay any longer.

‘Ice Magic: Hyper Intercooler.’

When the grand magic of an archmage, purchased for a staggering one hundred million gold, activated, a frosty mist rose around Fermi.

The mucus froze with a loud crack, and the ground gleamed like the arctic tundra in an instant.

As Marsha checked the freezing mucus creeping up from below, she prepared to escape.

At the same time, the colossal avatar of Kwanum descended from the sky and crushed a quarter of the pyramid.

Rumble!

As the pyramid, its defensive barrier nullified by the laws, crumbled, Shura hissed venomously, her snake-like tongue flicking.

“Detestable humans...!”

Even in her domain, all she could do was slightly deflect Kwanum’s attack.

But Miro hadn’t lived for 10,000 years.

For a human, fleeting as a mayfly, to manipulate such a colossal concept was something the immortal Shura could not comprehend.

“Grrrrk!”

Shirone, severed from the Digital Ra, collapsed forward, unable to withstand the tremors of the pyramid.

“Lord Consul!”

As Yuga, observing from the ground, attempted to climb the pyramid, an uncanny sensation struck repeatedly from behind.

“Hrrk!”

Turning around, he found Marsha standing there, plunging a dagger of treachery into him again and again.

“What... what have you done to my body?”

As he saw the smoke of desire escaping from his form, Yuga asked, but Marsha wasn’t one to answer.

“Die.”

As Yuga reached out to shatter the silver shard, the surroundings were suddenly plunged into darkness.

“What’s this?”

Fermi grabbed Marsha’s hand and activated teleportation.

‘Dark magic: Veil.’

Once out of the darkness, Marsha turned her head to see shadows drawn from the direction of the pyramid taking the shape of a sphere, trapping Yuga within.

“Let’s coordinate our attack now.”

With Shura’s barrier destroyed, there was no longer a need to divide their focus.

While Fermi disrupted the guardians of the colony with an array of spells, Marsha stabbed recklessly with the dagger of betrayal.

The smoke of desire clearly revealed their positions, but Fermi’s skillful use of Veil magic and crowd control prevented the guardians from closing in. His prowess was undeniable.

‘They said Shirone is his rival. This guy isn’t ordinary either.’

Depreciation Deal didn’t manifest unique powers like other specialized techniques, but its advantage lay in completely disregarding elemental affinities.

Simultaneously wielding opposites like light and darkness, water and fire, meant being able to counter all possible variables.

In terms of mission execution alone, finding a professional mage of this caliber was nearly impossible.

“We need to eliminate it as quickly as possible. We don’t have time.”

As Fermi stated, the mucus was discarding frozen sections and generating new slime to climb toward Shirone.

“Shirone!”

Rian leapt toward the pyramid again, but this time, the formidable barrier of Paknyeo stood in his way, a challenge even for him.

‘Nirvana Energy!’

The endlessly accelerating cycles of the law granted Paknyeo’s body unnatural agility.

Though her attack patterns were simple, the higher the RPM rose, the faster and stronger she became.

BOOM!

When Paknyeo’s blade struck Rian’s greatsword, the impact twisted the bones in his entire body.

“Grrrraaaah!”

The two Yachas, snarling like beasts, glared at each other.

Then, as Rian’s regenerated left arm grabbed the hilt of the greatsword, Paknyeo’s face went pale.

In that grotesque moment, sensing the vast wave of power about to unfold, Rian infused his blade with Deny and swung it with all his might.

“Hyaaaaaaah!”

The tide of destruction surged forward.

Before the unstoppable wave of law, Paknyeo broke the cycle and twisted her body to evade.

BAM!

The moment the greatsword slammed into the ground, Paknyeo, watching Rian’s arm muscles rupture, gasped for breath.

“Huff... huff.”

The weight of Rian’s swing felt as heavy as the weight of the world.

Though she didn’t think she’d lose, she had no idea how she could win.

“Step aside. I must... get to Shirone.”

As Rian raised his head and advanced, his steps abruptly halted.

Paknyeo, her face contorted like a wolf, rolled her eyes back and grinned.

“You’ll go... all the way with me?”

For the first time, Rian took a step back.

“You’re really going to...”

Though her pupils returned to their place, they no longer resembled those of a human.

“... go all the way with me?”

Axing-Nirvana E-Engine.

With a loud bang, the ground shattered, and Paknyeo’s form vanished.

Her eight-cylinder engine was built solely for acceleration, leaving her body trailing as if it were torn apart, a series of afterimages spreading outward.

Instinctively, Rian raised his greatsword.

Simultaneously, dozens of cuts appeared across his undefended body, spilling blood everywhere.

“Grrrrrgh!”

The speed was so overwhelming that it should have torn his body apart.

But the Schema tempered over ten thousand years allowed Paknyeo’s body to endure.

“Not yet. Not yet.”

Paknyeo’s muttering voice was accompanied by a bizarre laugh, as though she had two vocal cords.

She had never reached her speed limit before, simply because no enemy had survived long enough.

‘But you’re different.’

Rian was different.

This man neither dodged nor fled; he would respond to her with everything he had.

“Damn it!”

All Rian could do was defend his neck with his sword.

Without a moment’s respite, his flesh was ripped away, and it felt as if his body was on fire.

‘Am I failing to protect my master once again?’

Rian’s gaze collided with Shirone’s atop the pyramid.

“Rian...”

Shirone stared at the blood-soaked Rian with a dazed expression.

As information from distant memories surged into his mind, the emotions overwhelmed everything he had experienced here.

I am Rian Ogent.

His name was Rian Ogent.

Let’s be friends. What do you think?

The moment he took that hand, his entire life changed.

I am Shirone’s sword!

He had always protected him from behind.

“Rian... how could I ever forget you?”

As Shirone’s memory restoration reached 99%, hot tears streamed down his face.

“Riaaaan!”

Even amidst the gales created by Paknyeo’s movements, Rian heard Shirone’s voice clearly.

‘It’s fine, no matter how much I break.’

Rian gripped the hilt of his greatsword as if to crush it, while Paknyeo, at the peak of acceleration, let out a cry of pure exhilaration.

At the moment of certainty, when she believed she could kill, her pale blade, like a wicked drill, tore into Rian with ferocious afterimages.

Clenching his jaw so tightly that his teeth threatened to shatter, Rian muttered to himself.

‘My flesh, my blood, my bones!’

And with every ounce of strength, he swung both arms.

‘They’re all yours!’

FWOOOOOOOM!

An iron-colored sphere enveloped Rian’s body.

As Paknyeo’s consecutive strikes collided with the impenetrable wall, the sphere glowed fiercely, repelling her afterimages.

“What is this...?”

With his Deny-infused arms, Rian had swung his greatsword, momentarily defending all directions.

“Grrraaaah!”

Rian’s arms, reduced to bare bones, clung to the hilt of the greatsword.

“I’m coming, Shirone!”

Gripping the hilt with the power of law, Rian launched himself into the air.

BOOM! BOOM! BOOM!

Rian’s form vanished, leaving a trail of explosions across the ground on either side of Paknyeo.

‘He’s moving so fast I can’t see him.’

Left or right?

As her eyes darted back and forth, Paknyeo froze in realization and shivered.

‘I can’t tell.’

Even her grotesque senses fell silent, sending chills across Paknyeo’s entire body.
