Chapter 797:

Pony narrowed her eyes and asked again.

“Someone acting strange?”

“There’s a chance the terrorist is mixed in with them.”

Pony already knew what kind of power the terrorist’s Regulation Exception was based on.

“Is that even possible? There weren’t any openings for something like that. Security was really tight.”

Pony wasn’t weak by any means, but the terrorist’s skill far exceeded her expectations.

“They might’ve swapped places after arriving at the venue. Do you have any hunches?”

Pony looked back at the door.

“If we’re talking hunches, there are too many—that’s the problem. Everyone’s shaken up after what happened yesterday. Some are trying to act cheerful, and others… the opposite.”

It was to be expected.

“If they replaced someone’s face, they wouldn’t be able to speak normally. Their eyes would be slightly unfocused too.”

If a hostage had been swapped, and they were still in their right mind, they would’ve screamed the moment their body changed.

“There is someone… one person who hasn’t said a word since this morning.”

“Who?”

Pony searched her memory.

“As of 9 a.m., there are three people who haven’t said a single word. Miss Baiden, Miss North Aymond, and Miss Yakma.”

“Hmm, three suspects.”

“Why don’t we just compare their height? Even if their face changed, the body’s still the terrorist’s, right? How precise is your visual memory?”

“About ±0.3 centimeters. But the terrorist’s face was covered, so I don’t know their exact height.”

“So we’ll have to figure it out directly. Should I call them out one by one?”

Shirone shook his head.

“That’s too dangerous. If the real contestant is still being held hostage and something goes wrong… if the terrorist panics and lashes out…”

“Then what will you do?”

“Swapping a face requires precision. The Hidden Piece must be stashed nearby. While I search for it, keep an eye on the contestants.”

“Got it. I’ll head back in now. If I take too long, it’ll look suspicious.”

As Shirone ran down the hall, Pony composed herself and opened the dressing room door.

The contestants tilted their heads.

“Huh? Where’s Shirone?”

“I told him to wait outside and sent him off.”

“Why? It’s really okay if he’s here.”

“Hmph, not for me. I’m not changing clothes in front of a classmate, of all people.”

She said that while pulling out her swimsuit and changing. The others didn’t suspect a thing.

‘Can I finish in time?’

There were only five minutes left before the competition began.

A power that divides a radius into specific spatial blocks. It has to be inside the building.

According to Shirone’s calculations, it was within 100 meters.

‘If only I could use quantum superposition…’

Just as it’s impossible not to see what you can see, it was equally impossible to dull his resonant senses to a passive level.

‘Then…’

This was a completely different world. In Shirone’s world, human will could change the future.

Time Vibration!

Shirone shook one second and, within the repeating interval, searched every room nearby.

Nothing! Still nothing!

An endless one second.

By the time he finished sweeping the entire building and reached the dressing room again, the contestants had already left for the stage.

No. There’s no way it’s not here.

It had to be one of two things.

Either it’s truly not here, or the target is already moving.

But if the target was moving quickly, they should’ve shown up in his Spirit Zone.

Damn it!

At the sound of cheers from outside the hall, Shirone finally ran out of the building.

…

Twenty seconds later, the air where Shirone had stood hardened into diamond, forming a humanoid shape.

Diamond Immoveable Heart.

With a faint thud, the crystallized air shattered into powder, revealing Hyega, holding a hostage with one arm.

As long as I think absolutely nothing, the Spirit Zone won’t detect me.

A power that manifested true emptiness in the physical world.

Though Hyega himself couldn’t move in that state, it was impossible to locate him while he maintained it.

Master… is this really the right thing to do?

As a member of the Immortals’ Community, he had to follow orders, but taking a hostage was not the way of a monk.

What meaning is left in a path that’s lost its purpose?

The hostage, whose face had been replaced with Mortrasinger’s and covered with a cloth, began to twitch as if emerging from hypnosis.

“Sleep a little longer.”

Hyega placed his hand on her solar plexus and whispered, calming her tremors in an instant.

“Wow, more people showed up than I expected.”

Though the crowd had thinned since yesterday, more than 100 people still filled the front rows of the audience.

Langi said, “I heard the Arakne government deployed special forces just for this event. And the swimsuit portion is the highlight of the pageant.”

It wasn’t often in life that one got to see the most beautiful women in the world in swimsuits.

“Miss Arakne! Barho Langi!”

The crowd roared as Langi took the stage, a one-woman army of applause and cheers.

“Whoa! She’s amazing! Langi!”

Her body, which she had prepared all year for this day, looked almost unreal.

“Now, let’s move on to the Q&A session. What inspired you to join the World Beauty Pageant?”

“Since I was a child, I’ve been passionate about world peace. I care deeply for society’s marginalized, and…”

Of course, the audience was paying more attention with their eyes than their ears, so Langi gave the textbook answer.

‘Pony! Any progress?’

Shirone, now among the audience, glanced at Pony, but she shook her head.

‘I’m not sure. Are they really here?’

The killing intent earlier had been real.

“Miss Viden! Maroni Adelka!”

Pony gave a subtle nod.

Watch her. She’s a strong suspect.

She had been cheerful and talkative—even to Pony—but hadn’t said a word since the attack.

“You look amazing today! Miss Adelka, what’s your favorite food? And why?”

Still wearing a dark expression, Adelka began to cry as she opened her mouth.

“I… I want to go home.”

“…Excuse me?”

“I’m scared. I don’t want to die. The terrorist attack… Please, just let me go home.”

A stage official signaled to cut it short, and the host quickly changed topics.

“Looks like Miss Viden is feeling quite nervous today. Let’s move on to our next contestant!”

As Adelka sobbed and walked back to her seat, Shirone’s mind turned over with concern.

It’s not Viden. Then…?

With the suspects narrowed down to two, Miss Yakma stepped up onto the stage.

“Alright then, let’s begin with a question—”

Before the host could finish, she raised her hands to her face—and clawed at it.

It’s her!

As a surge of killing intent erupted, her nails raked deep across her face.

“Kyahhhh! It hurts! It hurts!”

Regaining her senses, Miss Yakma screamed as she realized another person’s face was attached to her body.

The audience jumped to their feet. The Hidden Piece ability activated again, twisting space into chaos.

Now!

Shirone, already in position, vibrated one second using Time Vibration.

I have no choice but to kill her!

Just before she clawed her face, Shirone lunged—

What…?!

But Mortrasinger’s hands moved even faster, crushing Miss Yakma’s skull with both hands.

Only one second?

Mortrasinger’s face, hidden within Hyega’s Diamond Immoveable Heart, formed a faint smile.

Damn it! Again!

Shirone rewound one second and tried again, but once again, Miss Yakma’s head was destroyed instantly.

She did it on purpose.

She had created a major incident that would consume the entire one-second loop, knowing Shirone’s ability.

By pushing the perception window forward, no matter how many times Shirone vibrated that second, he couldn’t prevent her death.

So what now? Even if I kill her, the hostage won’t survive. But if I don’t kill her…

Shirone would kill everyone in the venue.

The life of one versus the lives of many—Yahweh doesn’t weigh such things. You won’t be able to do anything.

Realizing the strategy Mortrasinger had devised the night before, Shirone stopped the Time Vibration.

“Damn it.”

Standing upright with her face torn apart, Mortrasinger looked utterly grotesque.

“Ahhhh! Save us! It’s a terrorist attack!”

Pony immediately cast a spell, but Hidden Piece activated, flipping the ground and sky.

“Someone, help—!”

KUUUUUUNG!

As the ability deactivated mid-use, people were buried in the earth, and their screams cut off all at once.

“Mortrasinger.”

Hyega appeared, and the face of Mortrasinger, covered with cloth and without expression, returned to the faceless body.

“Kyak yak yak! Kyahahahaha!”

As the Anmu Mercenaries, outside the radius of the Regulation Exception, rapidly approached, Bodhidharma stepped in to block them.

“Diamond Palm!”

As Bodhidharma extended his hand, the air compressed into the form of a massive palm, halting their advance.

Mortrasinger pointed at Shirone.

“See that? It’s clear, isn’t it? Whether you’re Yahweh or whatever—this is what happens when you cross the Council of Ten.”

“…”

Shirone’s gaze was ice-cold.

“I’ll never forgive you.”

“Oh yeah? Go ahead. But no matter how godlike you are, even Yahweh can’t bring back the dead. I win!”

“Ataraxia.”

A massive halo burst like thunder, coalescing into a dazzling array of information.

“Hahaha! Kill me! I still win! All the world’s beautiful things have already died!”

“…Ritual of Flesh.”

It was an unpredictable spell, but the enraged Shirone pierced through the amplification circle without hesitation.

“Grrraaagh!”

The Time Perception Field expanded exponentially, and the only unique time in the universe dulled the nine senses.

“Aaaaaaagh!”

And then—he vibrated it with Time Vibration.

I can endure this!

As the timeline shook with an amplitude of plus-minus 30 minutes, events began to rewind.

30 minutes ago.

“Huff! Huff!”

At the entrance of the venue, Shirone knelt on the ground, panting heavily.

So this is what it feels like.

Whether it’s one second or one hour, Time Vibration resets a localized timeframe.

If I could bounce time all the way back to the beginning…

Reset.

A privilege granted only to administrators—those with ten stages of perception.

With Ritual of Flesh, the limit is one hour at best.

He still couldn’t grasp how to truly reset time perfectly.

First things first—save the people.

Time is space.

He couldn’t cover all space, but within the resonance of an hour, he should be able to save those in the venue.

The culprit is Miss Yakma.

Killing the terrorist would stop the massacre, but he wouldn’t be able to save the hostage.

The one-armed monk was holding her. Judging by his power, it wasn’t about moving the target—it was about erasing their existence.

Still, his Spirit Zone detected nothing.

Let’s test it and see what happens.

This was only the first vibration, so Shirone kept his mind calm and approached the dressing room door.

“I’m coming in.”

Knowing it would already be open, Shirone threw the door open roughly—and paper poppers exploded as he stepped inside.

“Surprise!”

Pop pop! Confetti fluttered down, but Shirone didn’t even blink.

“Surprised? We prepared a little welcome party! Come on in!”

As they pulled him by the wrist, Shirone’s gaze landed on Miss Yakma—her expression utterly vacant.
