Chapter 974: The Absent Presence (4)

Ymir's face twisted.

The pressure kept increasing to a terrifying degree, far beyond the weight he was currently feeling.

'What is this?'

As the pressure, which seemed to know no limits, continued to rise, Ymir's knees began to buckle.

Boom!

The ground sank about three meters deep first.

"Hmm."

Gaold's expression, as he glared at Ymir buried in the ground, was unexpectedly serious.

'He withstood it?'

Only Gaold knew what a mere three-meter-deep pit signified.

"What, what is this?"

Shirone's group looked around.

If the atmospheric pressure was enough to buckle Ymir's knees, the city should have been blown away already.

"What happened?"

As Tess muttered, a fierce voice tore through the sky from afar.

"You idiot!"

Looking up, Minerva was diving rapidly towards the ground on a jet.

Upon arrival, she grabbed Gaold by the collar and pulled him close.

"Are you crazy? The Great Star didn't sacrifice herself for you to act recklessly! If the city gets blown away..."

As Amanta, Freed, and Xing arrived with the Great Star in tow, she turned her head.

"Hah. Hah."

As expected, the Great Star's eyes were half-rolled back as she panted heavily.

'Gaia's ability.' She had absorbed all the shock that Gaold had inflicted on the planet moments ago as the incarnation of a star.

Realizing the situation, Miro looked at where Ymir was with a newly surprised expression.

'Even so, for the ground to be crushed...'

It meant that the Great Star was not in normal condition.

Shirone approached her.

"Are you okay?"

The Great Star smiled as she recovered from the shock.

"Don't worry. A star won't be destroyed by this level of impact. It's fine."

Shirone knew this too, but it was also true that her suffering was separate from the shock itself.

The Great Star looked around and asked,

"Where is Rian?"

Shirone couldn't answer, and sensing the atmosphere, she changed the subject.

"Listen carefully. The reason I came here is to seal Ymir."

It was welcome news to hear but didn't feel realistic at all.

'Seal Ymir?'

He was a being whose strength couldn't be completely suppressed even by Shirone's Hand of God.

The Great Star extended her hand.

"Shirone, give me the key to Hexa Planet."

It was a planet given to Shirone as a gift when he became one of the Five Great Stars of the Integrated Universe Management Department.

"Ah..."

As Shirone took out a pendant from his bosom, she received it and said,

"From now on, I will invoke the law of Pentagram."

In law, 5 symbolizes 'external force,' encompassing all types of summoning and sealing.

What she was attempting now was literally using stars and planets as a pentagram.

"These insignificant things keep gathering."

Ymir, who had been listening from the pit, clenched his fist and floated into the air with sheer muscle strength alone. Shirone's group looked at him with renewed dread as he defied gravity with just his strength.

Landing on the ground, Ymir asked the Great Star,

"You intend to seal me?"

"Yes. Unfortunately, your existence is meaningless in this universe. If you didn't affect anything, it would be different, but since you've taken action, we have no choice but to use this method."

Ymir slowly raised his knee.

"Is that so?"

The next moment he stomped down hard on the ground, sending an immense shock into the Great Star's incarnation.

"Ugh!"

"Great Star!"

As her companions hurriedly supported her, Ymir looked down at the ground he had stomped on.

"Hmm, quite solid."

Despite intending to collapse everything around him with that stomp, only a footprint remained in place.

"I'll give you some credit. But do you think that's enough to stop me?"

Even ‘Law Killer’ couldn't find a way to annihilate his existence completely.

"It can be done."

The Great Star hooked five pendants onto her fingers and brought her hands together as if in prayer.

"Stop Ymir for a moment."

Though those gathered here were unprecedentedly strong individuals in this world, none could answer her request. So far, Ymir had only stopped moving when he chose not to move himself.

"This is an interesting game. I'll gladly play along. But I need some sense of purpose too."

Ymir lowered his stance and said,

"The game ends when I kill you."

"Stop him!"

Shirone's group moved simultaneously. The Hand of God combined with Sun and Moon Halo and now Xing's law 'Penetration' acted on Ymir. Freed charged forward,

'The Great Star is preventing planetary destruction.'

Seeing her suffer was truly painful but...

"...Is that so?"

Facing their strongest enemy yet made Freed's fighting spirit push his lips into a smile involuntarily.

"Hiyah!"

Steel magic engraved on his sword,

"I'll chop off an arm at least."

As Skima reached his limit, a massive iron ring left afterimages.

Not quite the realm of breaking, but Ymir had already absorbed enough of the laws of restraint.

The thrilling feel of his hand struck Freed's mind sharply.

'It caught him right.'

He thought so until the magic-infused steel sword shattered into pieces with a loud crash.

"That tickles."

What Freed saw was...

"Ah..."

Not Ymir's fist, but the image of his son smiling from within a tunnel of light.

"My son, I..."

Before his smile could form, Ymir's fist exploded Freed's face.

"Freed;" Amidst the headless corpse still standing, Amanta furiously hurled the Wheel of Worlds.

Screeeech!

Even caught in Gaold's Vacum Press, Ymir twisted his waist to grab the Wheel of Worlds.

"Huh?"

With a cracking sound, all of Ymir's fingers caught in the wheel broke.

'It’s done!'

The balance of the world.

If Ymir was an entity soaring infinitely above the cosmic average, he might at least blow away his torso.

"Ugh!"

With three fingers broken, Ymir still clenched the Wheel of Worlds tightly.

The moment the Wheel shattered, Amanta suddenly thought.

'Maybe the world is already...'

Too tilted to allow any balance.

"Taha!"

Ymir's power tore Amanta's body to shreds, and all could only watch.

"Ha ha."

Arius laughed emptily.

'If he is so strong that he cannot sense the universe... he should not have existed in the first place.'

Shirone’s eyes grew fierce.

'Anke Ra.'

How contradictory.

That a being meant to stabilize the world had created such a monstrous entity.

'The integration of 10 billion Gaia people.'

That the majority of user permissions were concentrated in one entity meant a cosmic monopoly of power.

'We’re all going to die.'

The reason the damage had been minimal so far was that all those fighting knew their place.

'I have to take the risk.'

Ataraxia - Physical Realm.

As the mind transcended, the power levels of the Hand of God drew a massive upward curve.

"Ugh!"

Ymir's movements slowed.

But as always, Shirone’s solo Ultima System had its limits.

"Quick! The Flesh Realm can't last long!"

Miro shouted.

"Gaold! Press down!" Air Press was cast immediately, and once again the giant's body was buried deep in the earth.

"Now's the time! Attack!"

The moment all firepower focused on Ymir, the force was comparable to a G3 level transporter.

"This bastard!"

Ymir, breaking through a Thousand Hands Avalokitesvara’s palm like glass, charged at the Great Star.

"I’ll kill you!"

There was no longer any means to block him, and a massive fist flew towards the Great Star’s face.

"I won!"

Thud!

Just when Shirone's group thought it was all over, they were shocked.

"How?"

Ymir's fist, reaching towards the Great Star, trembled in mid-air.

Tears rolled down Tess’s cheeks.

"Rian!"

A blurry human figure appeared in front of the Great Star, and biological tissues started to attach.

"Is this your last struggle?"

Ymir, having already defeated his opponent, amplified his power with a cold demeanor.

"Huh?"

His arm didn't extend.

"Ugh!"

Though the rising power was endless, Rian’s spirit was also reaching the same level.

'Divine Transcendence.'

If you wish to do it, just do it.

'Don’t imagine.'

The moment you think of what can and cannot be done, the world of thought closes.

'In a state where I have completely erased my form...'

The ultimate realm of the body.

'It ends when I reach it!'

As the bandwidth of Idea expanded, signals were fired at an incomparable speed.

Rian's body was instantly restored, and he twisted while holding onto Ymir's fist.

"Ugh!"

The time it took for his arm to twist was fleeting, but it was the first time Ymir had succumbed to strength.

'Now.'

The Great Star's eyes shone as she spread her arms.

"The Law of the Pentagram."

Five pendants transformed into light and cuffed Ymir's hands, ankles, and neck like shackles.

"Transmission."

As distant planets in the universe pulled Ymir's body, his limbs were spread wide.

"Argh!"

Veins bulged in Ymir's neck as he exerted force, causing his limbs to move slightly towards the center of his body.

"Damn it!"

But ultimately, he couldn't withstand the weight of the planets shackling his hands, feet, and neck, and his body was spread out again.

'This won't work.'

If he stubbornly resisted like this, his face and limbs would be torn off.

"It won't take long."

Exerting his last bit of strength, Ymir trembled as he looked down at the Great Star.

"Wait."

As soon as he finished speaking, Ymir's body turned into light and disappeared along with the five stars.

Minerva asked,

"What happened? Is he dead?"

"No, unfortunately."

After sensing something for a moment, the Great Star unfolded a vision before her eyes with a serious expression.

"See for yourself. This is Ymir's current state."

Miro's face turned pale.

"My God..."

In the vast middle of the universe, Ymir floated fixed in position at the points of the pentagram.

Tess asked,

"Are those twinkling things stars?"

"No," Shirone bit his lip. "They're galaxies."

Dozens of galaxies spanned the distance from Ymir's face to his feet.

"Yes. By using the key of stars, space is distorted and transmitted to planets. I thought this could dislocate Ymir's limbs or maybe even his neck..."

Shirone said,

"He covered the spatial distortion with size."

"Yes. He poured all his power into enlarging himself. Naturally, his physical strength converges to zero. He'll be fine for now."

Miro agreed,

"Indeed, even sending a signal to move a finger would have to cross the universe."

In the vision, Ymir shrugged his shoulders.

"Keke."

Although no sound could be heard in space, just seeing him turn his head sent chills down their spines.

'Are those eyes bigger than galaxies?' Ymir moved his lips,

"Don't be too happy. My strength has weakened, but even the planets of the pentagram have become smaller than my cells."

They could understand him just by reading his lips.

"I'll be somewhere. The moment my power concentrates there, the seal will break and you will face destruction."

Ymir's eyes widened,

"Aaaaaah! Aaaaaah!"

The sound of him channeling power through his entire body spread between galaxies.
