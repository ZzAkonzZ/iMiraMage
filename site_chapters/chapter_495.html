Chapter 495:

"What's that?"

Kuan was the first to show aggression as he approached.

The man had a peculiar appearance, as if he didn't quite meet the standard of a human.

"What was I born to achieve?"

His features were misshapen, and the skin below his neck was covered in Garas's chitin. His fingers were sharp, like he was wearing steel gloves, and thick, sharp hairs stood out like a hedgehog's.

He was the Nephilim, born from the angel of fulfillment, Adio, and Garas, known as Nameless.

From the moment of his birth, he had followed the question in his heart like a calling, and names were of no importance to him.

As Nameless looked at the sword Kuan was holding, he spread his hands wide, and with a burst of light, two swords appeared in his hands.

Kuan raised an eyebrow in displeasure as Nameless lifted the swords and asked,

"What is this thing called?"

It was strange that he had manifested swords without knowing what they were, but Kuan answered honestly.

"A sword."

"A sword. How do you use it?"

"If you're asking how to use it…"

Kuan launched himself, drawing out his external force.

"This is how you use it."

Kuan's body spun like a top in front of Nameless, swinging his sword.

At that moment, Nameless realized that the sword would soon cut his neck and take his life.

Bang!

Nameless's twin swords blocked the attack, and Kuan's sword struck them hard.

It wasn't a technique or tactic, just a defense to stay alive.

The only reason Nameless could block the sword of an instructor from the Kaizen Swordsmanship School was due to the physical traits inherited from the angel and Garas.

As the blades scraped against each other, sparks flew, and Nameless was pushed back, slamming into the wall.

Barely regaining his balance, he kept his guard up and muttered something with his head bowed.

Kuan found the response surprisingly weak and felt something was off.

"Does he not know swordsmanship? Then what was that intense sense of unease I felt at first?"

Nameless finally lifted his head.

"I understand."

"Understand what?"

Kuan gripped his sword and walked forward.

"What a sword is."

Nameless disappeared from Kuan's sight.

Kuan, whose entire focus was on movement, couldn't lose sight of him just because he was fast.

Kuan's pupils fixed on a deep spot to the left as he searched for the vanished enemy.

Clang! The swords clashed again, but this time the situation was completely reversed.

"Can't react?"

Is human thought truly infinitely free?

This had been the question in Kuan's mind since he first picked up a sword.

And the conclusion he had reached through years of swinging his sword was clear: "No."

Human thought seemed free, but from the perspective of a truly free being, it was highly mechanical and bound by patterns.

To overcome this limitation, Kuan had trained in movement all his life and reached his current level.

But now, Nameless's movement transcended even Kuan's thoughts.

"Did he know swordsmanship?"

The thought crossed Kuan's mind, but he quickly corrected himself.

He wasn't already knowledgeable; he was growing through the clash of swords.

"No, this is evolution."

The explosive growth in swordsmanship, or realization, was beyond explanation.

Nameless's twin swords began to swing in trajectories beyond Kuan's understanding, and within just five minutes, he inflicted a fatal wound on Kuan.

"Ugh!"

Kuan, slashed across the chest, spun to keep his distance and retreated to the end of the corridor.

The humiliation as a swordsman hurt more than the wound on his chest.

If Nameless had picked up a sword for the first time today, he had caught up to everything Kuan had achieved in 30 years in just five minutes.

"Is that possible?"

Nameless was a Nephilim, a hybrid of the angel of fulfillment and Garas.

Through this unprecedented hybridization in the history of heaven, Nameless had developed new traits.

The angel's noble spirit combined with Garas's transcendent reproductive desire, converting it entirely into a desire for achievement.

While Garas pursued only reproduction, Nameless's mind was now focused solely on understanding techniques and transcending them to deeper levels.

Nameless was breaking through barriers that a human would need years of relentless training to overcome at an incredible speed.

I want to do better. I want to do better. I want to do better.

Nameless absorbed Kuan's 30 years of swordsmanship like a hole that sucked in everything and sublimated it to a higher level.

In the face of attacks imbued with that level of swordsmanship, Kuan could only desperately defend.

"This can't be happening! This can't be happening!"

Gritting his teeth and enduring desperately, Kuan found Nameless's swordsmanship increasingly surpassing the limits of his imagination.

"We need to help!"

As Armin and Siena prepared to cast spells on Nameless, Kuan, using a moment of clarity he had squeezed out, shouted,

"No! Run!"

Only those who had fought Nameless directly could understand his terror.

But Armin and Siena had already begun to act.

Armin cast a flicker spell to distract Nameless, and Siena fired seven ice spears simultaneously.

Thanks to this, Kuan escaped from the brink of death, but he knew the cost would be much greater.

Nameless, having dodged the ice spears and widened the distance, immediately showed interest in the mages' techniques.

"What is that?"

The intense desire for improvement, far beyond human imagination, struck Nameless's mind.

I want to do it! I want to do it! I want to do it! I want to do it!

That immense desire pushed all of Nameless's functions beyond their limits, and his spirit zone opened.

Armin, sensing Nameless's spirit zone with his synesthesia, was horrified.

It wasn't just finding the focal point of his mind.

"He unlocked the Immortal Function."

Nameless, having cast the flicker spell, moved behind Armin and swung his sword.

Faced with Nameless's ability to use both the spirit zone and schema simultaneously, Armin felt his hair stand on end as he bent backward.

As the first strike missed, Nameless immediately cast a spell, firing ice spears at Siena.

Only then did Armin realize why Kuan had been unable to do anything but be pushed back.

"Damn it! How can such a creature exist?"

It's unfair.

No genius could instantly copy the ice magic of a certified 5th-level mage.

But that was just emotional frustration; Armin knew the truth.

There are no such things as unfairness in this world.

If he was born that way, no matter how bizarre, that was just reality.

"Now that he's mastered the flicker spell, we can't run."

Facing a creature he had never encountered before, Armin's cold rational mind couldn't find a basis for judgment.

The only possibility was to use all their strength to eliminate Nameless immediately.

But if Nameless's growth speed exceeded his expectations, they would be the ones to be devoured.

Even so, choosing the former was the right thing to do, but if Siena's safety was at high risk, the judgment changed.

"I must save Siena. No matter the cost, I must get her home safely."

That was the only gift he could give his beloved sister by risking his life.

Armin hugged Siena, who was unable to do anything against Nameless's chaotic movements, and rolled on the ground.

"Isn't there anything more fun?"

As Nameless, with eyes full of madness, charged, Armin realized what he had to do.

"I'm sorry, Kuan."

As Nameless's swords slashed in an X, Armin surrounded himself and Siena with a barrier of stop magic.

"Hup!"

Nameless stopped his swords just before being caught in the stop.

An action impossible with normal inertia, achievable only through Nameless's unknown realization.

"What is this?"

Nameless's spirit zone immediately analyzed the anomaly of the stop magic and transmitted it to his brain.

If he had advanced a bit more, he would have been trapped in the stopped time.

Armin, holding Siena, watched Nameless beyond the barrier with a tense expression.

With the inside of the barrier emptied, the two were free from the timeline, ready to escape with Siena at the slightest opening.

"If he masters the stop as well…"

The thought alone was terrifying.

Perhaps it would have been better to die here.

But for Armin, who only cared about Siena, it was never an option.

"Hmm, I see."

Nameless savored the stop's barrier for a moment, then widened his eyes and cast a spell.

As Armin and Siena watched tensely, Nameless tilted his head slightly.

"Huh? It doesn't work?"

Armin's heart, which had been racing madly, found a moment of calm in a sigh of relief.

"Don't think this is over. Ra will never accept your existence… Ugh!"

With a pop, another angel exploded in Satan's grasp.

Frankwine, embodying Satan's concept, hunted down the hiding angels one by one, and as their numbers dwindled, the seesaw of law tipped rapidly.

"How dull. Ordinary angels don't even make a dent."

A law beyond the law.

No matter how well-armed the angels were with concepts, they had no effect on Satan.

Thud! Thud!

Two impacts pierced the ceiling of Zebul and struck the corridor where Frankwine stood.

"Oh? This time, it's substantial?"

Frankwine's lips curled at the arrival of the big shots.

The archangels of combination and decomposition, Metiel and Satiel, each wielding the fundamental concepts of strong and weak forces, arrived.

"A pathetic rat hiding on the opposite side of the law, running wild without knowing fear," Metiel said coldly as she walked forward.

Realizing through their vision that Metatron had been annihilated, the two archangels immediately flew to Zebul.

To balance the seesaw of law, it would have been best for them to stay hidden, but with all the ordinary angels in hiding by Ra's command, they had no choice but to act quickly to resolve the situation.

"Do you truly think so, archangel? The one who fears is not me, but Ra of the law," Satan taunted.

"Don't pretend to be noble, you hybrid with an unclear concept. It's extremely unpleasant," Metiel retorted, raising her hand slowly. Satiel, standing beside her, also spread her halo.

Metatron's gravity field was a pure manifestation of a fundamental concept. While powerful, it likely worked to Satan's advantage, who was invincible to the law. But Metiel and Satiel were different.

When the two archangels combined their powers, they could manipulate all particles in the world, allowing for extensive secondary applications.

"Kekeke, it's fun to see the pretty ones' tricks," Satan provoked as Metiel and Satiel simultaneously flew at him.

As the forces of strong and weak combined, matter melted like paint, transforming Zebul's landscape into something entirely otherworldly.

Bizarre substances with extraordinary properties manifested, and unimaginable phenomena began to occur around Satan.

In the surreal landscape resembling an abstract painting by a madman, the two archangels launched a tremendous attack on Satan.

Radiation tormented Satan's cells, and the superheated air scorched his entire body.

"Rrrrgh!"

Particles that distorted time bound Satan's hands and feet, and metals that warped space crushed his body like clay.

"Let's end this," Metiel said, and Satiel nodded, charging forward.

Though often rivals, in battle, there were no better partners in all of heaven than these two archangels.
