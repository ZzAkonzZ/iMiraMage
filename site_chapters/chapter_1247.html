Chapter 1247:

Sein and Kangnan stood frozen in disbelief.

“Oh my god…”

Ymir’s body was in ruins, with not a single unscathed spot left under Gaold’s relentless, maddened fists.

As Gaold lunged forward and threw a punch, Ymir’s head twisted violently, as if it might snap.

“Khrrgh!”

More than the split lips or shattered teeth, it was the mind-numbing shock that struck the hardest.

What kind of monster is this guy?

He had fought countless powerful foes before, but no one had ever pushed him this far.

“This won’t…”

Unfazed by the broken arm dangling at his side, Ymir launched his other fist.

Vacuum Press.

Gaold’s eyes widened as he caught the giant’s punch with both hands. A sharp cracking sound rang out.

“Guh!”

And for the first time, Ymir pulled away of his own will.

His fist had been crushed.

Even Sein—normally level-headed—had a surreal thought flash through his mind.

Could it be…

It was Kangnan who said it aloud.

“Is he… winning?”

He was beating down the King of Giants—hailed as the strongest being in the history of Heaven.

Sein looked to Miro, seeking confirmation.

“What do you think?”

She, too, hadn’t imagined Gaold would make it this far.

“…How much must it hurt?”

Her chest tightened.

“How much pain is he in right now?”

Because it was Ymir… and for a moment, they had forgotten that.

The atmosphere quickly sobered again.

“Huff… Huff…”

Gaold’s gaze was unfocused.

Where am I?

Only fragmented images floated to mind—his brain had gone blank.

“Ah…”

I was fighting that guy… what was his name again?

“Ymir.”

“Gaold.”

Ymir smiled with his eyes.

“That’s a great name. I imagine everything you’ve achieved is packed into that one word.”

Gaold didn’t respond.

“It really is a great thing. Ymir. That’s a good name. Miro. That’s a good name. Goffin, Ogent. Ashur. Uriel. Right, Rian.”

…What about us?

Sein and Kangnan felt a lump rise in their throats, but Ymir only gazed off into the void.

“To hone a single trait to its utmost extreme and reach a level beyond reach—that’s what it means to transcend. So then, who among them is the strongest? It’s a fascinating question. In the end, someone loses—but no one ever thinks it will be them.”

The fact that he was talking so much meant Ymir was in high spirits.

“They all believe they can win. Not because they’re perfect—but because they have absolute trust in their weapon. And they want to test it. I’ve defeated countless opponents, but honestly, winning or losing doesn’t matter. What matters is—”

Ymir’s eyes darkened once more, black as ink.

“—the fight itself. A battlefield where you can fully unleash that transcendent state you reached with everything on the line.”

“Entertainment.”

As the suffocating killing intent surged, Gaold’s pace quickened.

“Haven’t had enough of a beating yet, huh?”

“Heh, maybe not.”

Ymir threw his crushed fist straight at Gaold’s face.

The moment it struck the barrier of Air Press, another explosion of heat and a tremor burst forth.

“Guh!”

Gaold’s expression twisted into a devilish grimace, while Ymir grinned from ear to ear.

“You were the strongest.”

It would’ve been better if they’d met in the real world.

“…It was a good dream.”

As Gaold’s body went flying, the earth began to crack outward in a radial pattern.

Rumble—!

Kangnan, who had been staring blankly at the shockwave spreading across the land, suddenly darted forward.

“Gaold!”

Ymir wasn’t in good shape either.

He looked at his fingers—bent in all the wrong directions—then lifted his gaze.

“Is this the end?”

At that moment, a flash of light struck Ymir in the head from the side.

His neck bent about twenty degrees as he absorbed the impact, then slowly turned his head.

“…Who are you?”

Shirone appeared, walking with Luber and Monga at his side.

“Ymir… the Ultima—”

“Yeah, yeah.”

Ymir waved his hand dismissively.

“No need for complicated talk. I’m not interested in listening. If you want something—fight me for it.”

It was a statement worthy of a true martial artist, but Shirone, being a mage, had a different way of thinking.

There’s no point in fighting here. Recovering Ultima comes first. No—at the very least…

He had to escape Ymir’s dream.

I must be detached from the simultaneous event. That’s why the Transcendent Dream was able to start in the first place.

In other words, a different version of Shirone hadn’t yet accepted Yorahan’s revelation.

Shirone turned toward the black hole.

Extracting Ultima is top priority.

According to Luber, Arius had already entered, but the odds of his return were slim.

“Hey.”

Ymir didn’t like Shirone.

“You seem more interested in something else. Decide fast—fight or die. You’re annoying.”

Fighting someone who didn’t view battle as the goal, but merely as a means, was a pain.

Goffin… your son took after his mother more.

It was the same reason Ymir didn’t list Ikael among his worthy rivals.

Miro stepped forward.

“Shirone, head into the black hole. Arius must’ve set something up. I’ll take care of things here.”

Ymir smiled.

“You’re interesting too. But don’t you get it yet? The reason I named Gaold the strongest isn’t because of his power.”

Ymir’s wounds began to heal at a terrifying pace.

“It’s his mind.”

As all the broken parts rapidly regenerated, Sein clenched his fists and bit his lip.

Of course… this isn’t the real world. This is just an avatar. If the mind recovers, so does the avatar.

In short, it was a battle of minds.

If Gaold had shattered Ymir’s avatar through sheer willpower…

Then Miro, whose mind is specialized for defense, would be at a disadvantage here.

It was abnormal that Gaold had reshaped his avatar within someone else’s mental world.

Then a surprising voice spoke.

“Go, Shirone.”

Supported by Kangnan, Gaold was walking forward.

“…Hoo?”

Even Ymir was surprised.

“I felt your body burst, y’know. Heh. Well, I guess you’re an avatar too. What an endless obsession.”

Gaold spoke to Shirone.

“Go.”

“But—”

“I won’t last much longer.”

No matter how obsessive one was, there were limits to regenerating an avatar inside someone else’s mind.

“I’m counting on you.”

As Gaold gave a small nod, Shirone launched himself toward the black hole.

Ymir didn’t chase.

Good!

As the landscape below faded away, Shirone felt a wave of relief—but the black hole was farther than he thought.

Ultima is there.

And finally, his body was pulled into the extreme gravity field.

The speed of light.

Shirone was in darkness.

Where… am I?

Countless stars were being drawn into a singularity, and Shirone’s body followed their gravitational path.

Imagining all those stars colliding at once was dizzying—but he realized something.

Time is flowing backward.

It was like the nightmares he used to have every night when he first accessed the Immortal Function during his school days.

They called it hyperesthesia.

So that’s it…

He felt it.

Information can’t exceed the speed of light—but the mind can transcend both time and space.

Planets broke down, and Shirone’s body began to disintegrate into particles.

And yet… I remain.

He finally understood what part of a person lingers the longest.

The concept of ‘me’—before I even exist as ‘me.’

Following the path guided by imaginary time, Shirone dove deeper than particles.

Threads of light that unified time itself vibrated—countless events repeated their births and deaths.

A boundary beyond the outside world—where only possibility remains.

In the state of oneness with all things, Shirone felt his concept of self expanding.

It’s merging.

It was like… a colossal explosion.

Canis asked, “Alright, now do something. How do we summon God? You said you could do it.”

His agitation stemmed from how deep his resentment and anger toward God ran.

“Show yourself.” For God to appear meant to take form—and if that was the case, at the very least, they could fight back.

“It’s eating me alive just holding it in.”

Lucia, who had been quietly gazing at the unconscious Glenn, refocused her eyes.

“It’s a matter of conceptual integration.”

“Integration?”

“It doesn’t matter what the concepts are. In this world, the moment something exists, it’s divided in two. Because nothing can be defined without referencing something else.”

Shirone—and everyone else—had come to understand that much through their journey.

“Only God can define such a thing as one. Good and evil, light and dark, presence and absence—it doesn’t matter. When those dualities are merged, they become an Idea that needs no further definition, and God will only appear through that concept.”

It was a theory with a strong ring of truth.

“I see,” Ares said. “God’s right hand and the devil’s left hand. It wasn’t a coincidence that one more power emerged.”

“Yes,” Lucia replied, raising both hands. “God’s right hand relaxes the heart, while the devil’s left hand twists it. So then—what happens if I activate both powers at the same time, while holding my hands together?”

Shirone answered, “A Tai Chi effect. Neither side gives way, and it will circulate endlessly.”

Lucia nodded. “God should be able to manifest through that conceptual balance.”

“But Miss Lucia,” Shirone said. “You do realize, if it becomes infinite, even the concept of existence vanishes. If the Law of Two collapses, you can no longer be defined.”

It was because he could hold infinity within himself that Shirone was known as the Infinite Mage.

“I…” Lucia looked at Glenn. “I’ve decided to forgive him. Glenn… he probably had no other choice. Back when we were poor, maybe he approached me out of admiration, maybe it wasn’t even love…”

She smiled softly. “But I don’t want to define it. If I can embrace Glenn with my heart, that’s enough for me.”

To not define.

“To give everything, wholeheartedly, in sacrifice.”

Realizing there was no stopping her, Shirone stepped back. Lucia turned toward the device.

“Glenn…”

To save the entire universe—

“I love you.”

Loving even just one person was enough.

“Dear God…”

Lucia clasped her hands in prayer, gently closed her eyes, and activated her powers.

“Have mercy on us.”

As God’s right hand and the devil’s left hand activated simultaneously, Lucia’s heart began to rotate.

The pyramid trembled, and every character etched into its inner walls began to glow blue.

Electricity…

As the energy generated by the yin-yang rotation focused into the device, everyone realized it at once.

“The ancient civilization’s technology… it’s a generator.”

And the electricity being drawn here was powerful enough to pierce the veil of the outer world.

“God…”

A moment in human history unlike any before.

“...is descending.”

"Shirone! What are you doing?" Shirone, who had been crying in front of Seina, heard Nade’s voice.

'I don’t know. Why does my heart ache like this?'

It wasn’t the first time he had felt this way.

When he parted ways with Amy, when she went to the sun, and now, in this very moment.

'Maybe I’m...'

Destined to carry immense sorrow from now on?

Shirone was completely unguarded, but Gyorgy also remained motionless.

'Choose, Yahweh.'

Will you carry through, or will you embrace?

To achieve what no one among good, evil, or neutrality had ever done—only one thing was needed.

Shirone slowly lifted his head.

"Sacrifice."

The Immortal Function activated, and Shirone’s body began to radiate with a brilliant light.
