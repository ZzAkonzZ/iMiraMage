Chapter 644:

"Wait a minute! Please, wait a minute!"

Before Arte could respond, Amy hurriedly reached out her hand.

"Shirone, what are you doing? Do you not understand your situation?"

"I understand. But just because my situation isn't good doesn't mean I want to do something I don't want to."

"Don't want to? This is the Ivory Tower, the place every mage dreams of."

"Even so, I can't follow something that isn't right."

"Not right…"

Arte lowered his fan and revealed his face.

"That's an interesting opinion. What are you thinking?"

"Kar score means perfection, right? But what you did, claiming perfection, was ultimately subduing by force."

Shirone couldn't understand Arte's decision to blow Lyman away just because there wasn't enough time.

"Hmm, it seemed like you didn't like it."

"I don't. But it's not about feelings. If strength is the truth, how is the White Line different from the other lines?"

Tokey shrugged his shoulders.

"Heh, I told you. Different from the profile."

Arte, convinced, snapped his fan shut and drew an imaginary vertical line on the table.

"Alright. Let me explain it this way. Humans belong to the number 3 of the law because they choose between good and evil. The pinnacle of this 3 is the seeker. But Shirone, we are not seekers. We are beings of intelligence, corresponding to the number 4 of the law. In other words, we do not choose between good and evil."

"Then what is the Ivory Tower?"

"We are beings that distinguish between good and evil. Is good truly good? Is evil truly evil? The most crucial aspect of the number 4 of the law is endless criticism. And what this means is…"

Arte stood up and extended his hand for a handshake.

"Congratulations once again. Shirone, you are fully qualified to take the Ivory Tower's test."

His friends asked in bewilderment.

"What?"

Meanwhile, Shirone stared at Arte's outstretched hand and then looked up, not understanding.

"If I had agreed to take the test from the beginning, would I not have been given the opportunity?"

"In my case, yes. As I said, there are no special criteria for Kar. Candidates notified by residents other than me might not have had such a test."

Arte smiled.

"That's the Ivory Tower. Becoming a star means you can criticize anything, and I was merely criticizing you in my own way. Just as you criticized me and the Ivory Tower a moment ago."

'Ah.'

Amy began to understand what Kar was.

"Therefore, the residents of the Ivory Tower support the judgment of the stars. Even if opinions differ, they believe it is a conclusion reached after harsh self-criticism. Is strength the truth? If a resident concluded that, it is also close to perfection."

Arte shook Shirone's hand himself.

"Receive the title of Star. The Ivory Tower does not impose any ideology on you. It simply shares the things you criticize with your intelligence with the world."

Shirone's heart began to race.

He only wanted to move towards the critical conclusions drawn through intelligence, aiming for 100% Kar.

"Wait. There's something we need to address here…"

Iruki stepped forward.

"Since Shirone has become a candidate for the Ivory Tower, can we assume he has met some qualifications?"

"Of course. Even if Shirone refuses, the door to the Ivory Tower is always open."

"Then what about the contract terms? We can't just accept the offer without any conditions, right?"

Tokey spoke.

"If you're talking about salary, the Ivory Tower doesn't offer any. However, we can provide as much funding as needed for mission execution."

This time, even Amy was surprised.

"Really, not a single penny?"

"Because it's unnecessary. Being trusted by the residents of the Ivory Tower means you can criticize anything, and you already have full access to public facilities worldwide by agreement. If you're hungry, go to a public institution and eat. If you need something, talk to a king or official. They will supply it through the fastest route."

"Wow, that's really crazy."

Hearing about the practical benefits, Nade also felt what the Ivory Tower was like.

If he had received such an offer, he might have revived his dream of becoming a mage.

'Well, that's why it can't happen.'

Nade's Kar was far inferior to Shirone's.

"Of course, this is only after receiving the title of Star. No benefits are provided to candidates. Building trust in Kar before becoming a star is the priority."

Naturally, but Iruki wanted to gain some advantage through this opportunity.

"Do you know Shirone's current situation?"

"No, I don't."

A third-class resident didn't need to know the candidate's situation.

"The Kingdom of Tormia has falsely accused Shirone, preventing him from graduating. What do you think about this?"

"Then he just shouldn't graduate. The Ivory Tower doesn't care about anything other than Kar. Shirone just needs to find Ra Enemy and come to the Ivory Tower."

Amy spoke.

"But that's too much. The third place is royalty with immense connections, and the second place, whoever they are, probably has at least a mage certification. As it stands, Shirone would have to travel as a civilian. I'm not saying it's unfair, but you could help him obtain the qualifications he could have had."

"Hmm."

Arte tapped his head with the fan, lost in thought.

"Alright. Is it just about graduating?"

Shirone nodded obediently.

"Yes. Even an unofficial mage certification would make traveling much easier."

It wasn't about being greedy now, but he wanted to have the diploma from the magic school filled with so many memories.

"Understood. I'll talk to them."

Arte stood up, and Tokey climbed onto his shoulder, looking back at Shirone.

"Make sure to become the twenty-eighth star. It would feel good if the person we notified became one, wouldn't it?"

"Yes, I'll do my best."

As Arte and Tokey left, the students gossiping outside the building fell silent.

The teachers also looked tense, but Arte calmly surveyed the surroundings as if used to such situations.

Then he signaled Lyman, who was sitting like a refugee receiving treatment, to come over with a wave of his fan.

"Yes, yes!"

Despite his injured body, he moved as quickly as possible and prostrated himself at Arte's feet.

Having briefly left due to concerns about his daughter's study abroad expenses, he had returned after being struck by Arte's magic.

"You called for me, Observer of Intelligence."

"From now on, Shirone will take the Ivory Tower's test. Ensure there are no issues with his graduation."

"I will convey your will to the king. The star's will shall be smoothly fulfilled."

When mingling with foreign officials, one often hears stories of kings being grabbed by the collar by stars. The common advice is always the same: if a star wants something, just do it.

'It's already been thoroughly criticized.'

This was the Ivory Tower's operational manual.

"Let's go, Tokey."

Tokey waved to the crowd.

"Everyone, live happily until we meet again."

A band of light swirled around Arte's feet, forming a massive vortex that shot up into the sky.

'So this is the Ivory Tower.'

As the students stared in awe, Lyman, who had belatedly exited the building, glared at Shirone, grinding his teeth.

He had intended to make Shirone crawl between his legs, but now Shirone had become untouchable, even by the king.

'Hold it in. No one has ever ended well after crossing the Ivory Tower.'

With three candidates, Shirone had a 33.3% chance of becoming a star.

If he meddled now, the future consequences would be too great to bear.

"Arian Shirone."

Lyman forced a smile.

"By royal decree, all your crimes are pardoned. I hope you become a great mage who elevates the kingdom's name."

Although the royal decree hadn't officially been issued, it was a foregone conclusion, and the students cheered at Lyman's declaration of defeat.

"Wow! Congratulations, Senior Shirone!"

"This is incredible! The Ivory Tower!"

Being a candidate for the Ivory Tower was a great honor for their alma mater, so the students' joy was natural.

'Find Ra.'

Meanwhile, Shirone felt the weight of the 33.3% chance.

This was a problem of a different dimension than anything he had solved before.

Moreover, the Kar scores of his competitors, though slightly, were higher than his.

'Who are they?'

Shirone was genuinely curious, beyond the competition.

The Imperial City of Jincheon, Yeomra.

With a population exceeding 200 million, the scale of Yeomra, where the emperor resided, was vast even among the Three Sovereign Realms.

The number of the imperial guard was comparable to a nation's army, and there were over 2,000 sorcerers.

They had an overwhelming numerical advantage, but they lagged in quality. Currently, they were recruiting skilled individuals from around the world with astronomical funds.

"Princess, His Majesty the Emperor is calling for you."

As soon as the maid finished speaking, the woman meditating in the octagonal pavilion opened her eyes.

She was Jin Seong-eum, the thirteenth daughter of Emperor Jin Kang of Jincheon.

Her gaze was as firm as steel, and her tightly closed lips were as red as flames.

"I will go now."

As Seong-eum prepared to leave, the maids distanced themselves according to the numbers engraved on their jade necklaces.

The distance, ranging from 10 to 1,000 steps, indicated their authority to approach Seong-eum. If they took even one step closer, they would face severe punishment.

Upon arriving at the palace with eaves soaring towards the sky, the guards raised their spears in unison.

The captain of the guard, Oh, approached with the sound of clanking armor and bowed.

"Princess."

Seong-eum's brow twitched.

"23 steps. Step back."

"……"

Seong-eum measured the distance with everyone she met, but as the captain of the guard, it was impossible for Oh to leave his post.

"Hmph, really?"

As Seong-eum scoffed, Oh realized he was already 23 steps away from her.

'……There's nothing I can do about this.'

Her obsession with space stemmed from a special realization that made her the greatest sorcerer in Jincheon.

The reason the emperor called her today was that the Ivory Tower, which only the world's best could enter, had designated her as a candidate.

"110 steps. 230 steps. 87 steps. 433 steps."

Seong-eum assigned distances to the guards as well.

Though the basis was unknown, it felt oddly convincing, suggesting there was some standard.

When the guards, like Oh, didn't leave their posts, Seong-eum's eyes flashed with anger.

"You…!"

The maid, the only one allowed within 10 steps, hurriedly spoke.

"Princess, today His Majesty has specially…"

'Ether Wave.'

As Seong-eum focused her mind, all sounds disappeared, and even the shapes of the scenery were pushed out of perception.

In a world of darkness, countless vibrations were transmitted like raindrops falling on the surface of water.

If gravity was caused by matter pressing down on space, even empty space was filled with something.

What she realized as an unlocker was the base that constituted space itself, namely ether.

'Space Compression.'

Sensing the emperor's wave, she cast a spell, compressing the 4-kilometer distance, and the scenery of Hwangryongjeon rushed in.

With different landscapes touching each other, Seong-eum's leg crossed the boundary of space.

"Father."

With just one step, she had an audience with the emperor of the Jincheon Empire.
