Chapter 858: Singularity (1)

How much time had passed?

The woman, who had been breastfeeding, gently pushed Harvitz's shoulder away and said, "It hurts. Stop eating now."

Harvitz slowly detached his mouth, his face covered in cold sweat.

"Haah."

The taste of breast milk was sweet yet rancid, but Harvitz's mind was completely empty.

What filled the 10-minute gap, not even present in his memory, was the thrill of rest.

'This is the beginning.'

The woman’s breast milk seemed to contain all sorts of pleasures concocted by humanity.

"Do you want to be a baby?"

Lifting his head to the woman's question, Harvitz wondered, 'What do I want to become?'

The world had given him many names, but in reality, there was no defined self in his own mind.

Tangled thoughts, and the occasional pleasures shot through these variations were all that existed.

"Wah, wah."

Harvitz mimicked the sound of a baby crying.

The act of feigning innocence with a dry face was chilling in its sheer pretense.

'This is chaos.'

Like an aborted baby.

Although it was never born, it lives on in the memories of its parents in a tragically unreal form.

'A point where all contradictions are unresolved and compressed.'

Natasha understood Harvitz.

'Would Harvitz emerge if the torment felt at the moment of discovering two children drowning was infinitely amplified?' And now, he was awakened.

"Wah, wah."

Realizing he could not become anything, Harvitz defined himself through that realization.

'I am Satan.'

A village 17 kilometers away from Habitz.

In the village that had been ravaged first by demons and then looted by bandits, the breaths of those awaiting death could be heard.

"Ugh."

A woman with her left leg severed and bleeding profusely was struggling to crawl on the ground.

"Baby."

She reached out to a child who had been pierced by an arrow and had fallen.

"Mom, it hurts."

Tears welled up in the woman's eyes, and Nane and Shura were watching the tragic scene.

"It's okay, baby."

She stroked her child's head and said,

"When you die..."

She gritted her teeth, trying to hold back her sorrow, but she couldn't suppress the overwhelming grief.

"When you die, it will be okay."

As Nane tightly pressed his lips together, the woman's heart-wrenching scream echoed.

"Ahhh! Ahhh!"

The woman, who had been screaming beside her dying child, eventually died from excessive bleeding.

Nane closed his eyes and clasped his hands together.

"May you be reborn in paradise."

Shura spoke.

"Let's go back. We're not even fighting the demons."

Nane, who had been meditating in the mountains, suddenly suggested going out into the world, which puzzled Shura.

"No. There's nothing more we can do by just sitting. Let's look around the world a bit more."

"What are you?"

A giant shadow approached from the pitch-black darkness.

They were demons who had strayed from their group during the war, each injured.

Shura took a fighting stance.

"Step back if you don't want to die."

"Heh, how ridiculous. Do you think we'll be scared just because the spiritual realm is closed?"

Nane asked.

"The spiritual realm... is closed?"

It had only been a day, so Nane hadn't heard the rumors about Seongeum.

"You don't need to know. Just be our punching bags quietly."

Nane had no interest in the demons.

"The spiritual realm is closed."

When he opened the altar, he thought humans would be easily swept away in the Great Purification.

"Humans are strong."

Getting beaten by Gaold was one thing, but he had indeed underestimated the world.

"Go back. From now on, do not commit evil deeds and live in repentance."

The demons looked at each other.

"Hahaha! What are you saying? Are you crazy?"

"Don't commit evil deeds to demons? A lowly human like you lecturing us..."

The demons' faces froze simultaneously.

"A... Buddha..."

Shura, who turned back with a pale face, saw the shimmering light emanating from Nane's body.

"A... Buddha..."

It wasn't that they didn't realize.

Nane could become a Buddha again and destroy the world anytime he wanted.

"I told you to go back."

As Nane's eyes widened, the demons scattered in all directions without even having time to scream.

When the enemies disappeared, the energy that seemed like it would tear the world apart vanished as if it were a lie.

"Master, why do you hold back?"

"It's not time yet."

Just because one is enlightened doesn't mean the world changes.

"Shirone, who loves and pities even demons. I want to understand his thoughts a bit more."

Even when seeing the world from the perspective of compassion, if there is no wavering in the Buddha's viewpoint...

"It becomes truly right."

Nane looked back at Shura and said,

"One extreme cannot support the whole. Shirone must be thinking the same as me."

All those who asserted themselves from their extremes were passing through a singularity.

"When we complete the circle... whatever it is, it will end."

The morning sun rose.

The Jincheon army, which had entered Gustav through the spatial distortion of Gwangcheonseong, was stunned by the scene before them.

"What on earth is this..."

Everywhere they looked, demon corpses were piled up.

"Is this a disaster?"

All the buildings were in a state of collapse, as if a natural disaster had swept through.

"It's Yahweh's power. Maybe we didn't even need to intervene."

Oryongjang realized a new fact.

"Princess."

Although Seongeum's sacrifice had raised Jincheon's morale, what she truly wanted to rely on was Shirone.

"That's why she spent a day with him..."

His eyes reddened.

"No, we can't just stay like this. This is the place created by the most noble person in Jincheon. We must win."

The Jincheon army advanced towards the capital again.

From far beyond the horizon, the screams of demons could be heard like a downpour.

Shirone no longer ran.

"If Nane had closed the world... Seongeum wouldn't have had to suffer eternally in hell."

"I thought if I could endure it..."

Others could endure it too.

"I don't know pain."

Saving the world with the spirit of compassion would make everyone happy, but Seongeum could not return.

"Destroy."

As Shirone moved, waves of blinding light spread endlessly.

The divine particles, reaching near-light speed levels, swept away everything around them.

"Ahhh! Run away!"

The demons occupying the city near the capital, Pamelion, scattered in all directions.

Although they held Yahweh in high regard, they screamed in terror before the current Shirone.

"Run! Ru..."

Time seemed to slow down almost to a stop.

"Run... away..."

Infinite particles of light pierced the backs of the demons filling the landscape.

Before the explosion occurred, their massive bodies disintegrated like dust, followed by a sweeping shockwave.

Boom!

Like a flood bursting, light filled every street corner, sweeping everything away.

"Wow, that's scary."

In a situation where no one could look back, there was one person watching Shirone.

With jet-black hair down to her waist and an angelic, pure face.

It was Melania, the 2nd Legion commander of the army of hell.

"Yahweh is very angry."

Even the particles of light rushing at an unreactable speed couldn't approach her.

"What should we do, commander? At this rate, we'll all die."

A low-ranking demon with no position clung closely to her side.

"Leave it. When a gentle person gets angry, you have to soothe them until their anger subsides."

That's why she saw meaning even in the death of tens of millions of demons.

"What if the anger doesn't subside?"

Even Melania thought Shirone's anger was terrifying enough to end the world.

"Then... I'll have to go and beg."

"Run away quickly!"

Rian, who had entered the capital Marsak, cut down demons while evacuating people.

"Shirone won't care about humans."

Without that level of resolve, he couldn't give up Yahweh.

"It's not that he belittles life."

Compared to Seongeum, who was trapped in eternal hell, death was rather a rest.

"Go! Quickly!"

As Rian cut down demons, civilians living in the capital screamed and fled.

"There are too many people."

He had been fighting for over 12 hours, staying up all night, but many still hadn't left.

Boom!

In the battlefield filled with all kinds of screams, a particularly jarring explosion was heard.

The sound was clearly meant to attract attention, and when Rian turned his head, a giant with black hair stood there.

The man's bizarre smile and madness took Rian's breath away.

"Brother?"

The once upright appearance was gone, but the characteristic impression of Ogent remained.

"Long time no see, Rian."

The eldest son, Ogent Guy.

Rian, who hadn't heard from his eldest brother since he left home long ago, lowered his greatsword.

"How are you here?"

Shirone hadn't mentioned Guy.

"As you can see, I've made a name for myself."

As Rian struggled to understand the situation, demons charged from behind Guy.

"There he is! Kill..."

Without even looking back, Guy's single strike split dozens of demons in two.

"An incredible quick sword."

Ogent's sword wind was honest, leaving no dullness even when cutting down the genius of technique, Rai.

But Guy's strike was imbued with a violent nature, as if it were cut with a saw rather than a sword.

"How did this happen? Have you been living as a spy here all this time?"

"A spy?"

Guy shrugged his shoulders.

"As you can see, I'm the commander-in-chief of Gustav. The army of hell is beyond my authority, though."

Guy walked briskly towards the confused Rian.

"How's the family?"

Rian couldn't answer.

"Is Grandpa healthy? Rai is brilliant as always, and I wonder if tough Reina will ever get married."

"Brother, I..."

Before he could finish, Guy approached and raised his sword high, then struck it into the ground.

"My dear little brother."

He then roughly hugged Rian, rubbing his hairy cheek against him.

"I missed you."

They were family.

"Brother."

Rian dropped his greatsword and hugged Guy back.

"I missed you too. How did this happen? Come back with me. The family is waiting."

Guy, holding Rian's shoulders, stepped back.

"You've grown sturdy. You couldn't even reach my waist before, but now you're almost my height."

Rian spoke again with a desperate expression.

"Brother, come back."

Guy shook his head with a smile.

"I can't."

"Why? Why not? Whatever you've done, the family can handle it together."

"It's not guilt. If I had any left, I wouldn't have come this far."

"Then what is it?"

"The ultimate in swordsmanship. To reach it, I abandoned everything. And Rian..."

Killing intent rose from Guy's body.

"You too."

Guy grabbed the sword stuck in the ground and swung it at Rian's neck.

"He's serious."

Rian, who had bent his upper body, picked up the greatsword that had fallen to the ground and swung it upward.

"Good move."

Guy, who had widened the distance, smiled and said,

"Good reaction. Of course. Even if it's your brother, if you sense killing intent, you should respond like that."

"I'm a knight too. If it's like this, we have no choice but to fight."

"I know. The Knight of Maha. They say you're incredibly strong."

As Rian watched silently, the scenery around Guy began to distort.

"What is this?..."

"Yes, my naive little brother."

Guy said, his eyes filled with madness.

"The bloodline of Ogent Smille is not yours alone."
