Chapter 976: Hell of The Demon World (2)

The moon had risen, but the place where the four archangels had fought still retained its heat.

The most noticeable traces were the massive grooves extending over 10 kilometers in different directions.

From those three directions, Satiel, Uriel, and Rael walked almost simultaneously.

The three archangels met in the center and maintained a long silence.

Uriel was the first to speak.

"...She's gone."

Satiel's fist flew, but Rael quickly grabbed her wrist.

"Calm down."

"Let go."

Satiel kept her gaze fixed on Uriel.

"I'm serious. You'd better let go. Otherwise, you'll be the first to be annihilated by me."

Uriel spoke into the air.

"There's nothing more pathetic than blaming others for a defeat."

"Blaming others?"

Satiel shook off Rael's hand and approached Uriel.

"You're the one who didn't do your best! Do you think I don't know? Are you planning to betray Heaven too?"

Uriel remained silent.

As Satiel extended her hand, the judicial halo Nostalgia began to spin at a terrifying speed.

"I'll disassemble you."

Just as she was about to attack, Uriel spoke.

"Ikael..."

Her fingers twitched, and Uriel looked Satiel straight in the eyes.

"Why did she leave us?"

Rael spoke.

"She chose blood over Heaven."

Only after saying it did he realize how bizarre the situation was.

'I see. This is really strange. An angel, especially an archangel, having a human child.'

Uriel spoke.

"I'm not being sentimental. Ikael choosing Hexa means she abandoned Heaven of her own will."

Satiel felt her patience reach its limit.

"So what? Isn't that why you're so angry right now? What do you want to say?"

"It's not so much what I want to say, but I never thought it was possible. That an angel could betray Heaven."

Rael asked.

"So you just let her go? Simply because... you couldn't understand?"

"I will fight Ikael."

Uriel turned away.

"But from now on, it will be my fight, not Heaven's. I will define this war by my own will."

Satiel didn't understand, but Rael thought it was perhaps a natural outcome.

'Uriel...'

His actions were heavy, and he was clumsy at expressing his opinions, but he always seemed to do what Ikael wanted.

'The one who felt the greatest sense of loss might not be Satiel, but Uriel.'

Or maybe even himself.

'Uriel has left. Now the remaining archangels are Satiel and me. So I...'

Honestly, Rael didn't want to choose anything since the moment Ikael left.

'That's what Ikael was.'

The only direction in a directionless world, the only meaning to an angel who didn't know the meaning of existence.

"You get lost too."

Satiel spat out.

"I don't need a conflicted archangel. And you don't want to stay with me anyway, do you?"

Rael, who had been gazing at Satiel, turned away without a word.

As she watched Rael disappear into the darkness, Satiel ground her teeth.

"Are those things really angels?"

Her holy light began to turn red again.

"What kind of archangels are they? What kind of noble beings in the universe? In the end, they just envy humans!"

Her two-horned Mara, Galio, reached out a hand.

"Lady Satiel! You must preserve your holy light..."

"Ikael! Ikael! Ikael!"

Red tears welled up in Satiel's eyes.

"Why is it always Ikael? What's different between her and me? No matter what Ikael does, she's forgiven, but why can't I be?"

Looking back, she wondered if there was anyone in the world who had received as much universal love as Ikael.

"From now on, I am the archangel."

Wiping her tears with her arm, she declared.

"Listen, all forces! I will punish Ikael, who betrayed Heaven! I am the most noble angel and the only one to uphold the will of Anke Ra!"

Her voice echoed loudly, but all that returned was a hollow echo.

"I will annihilate all humans Ikael loves! I will correct the law!"

Watching their master scream in near despair, Galio felt miserable.

'No matter how much she shouts...'

Her voice couldn't shake the world like Ikael's.

After washing up at the lodging, Shirone walked down the corridor to Amy's room.

It was past midnight, but the sounds from inside the rooms indicated that others were also having trouble sleeping.

"Amy."

Amy, who came out at the sound of the knock, smiled and invited him in.

"Come in."

Shirone could sense the deep sadness behind her smile.

"I came because I was worried."

"I'm okay."

Garcia had been her mentor and the person who had shaped a significant part of her life.

"I'm sad, but I won't cry. I've decided that. I don't want anyone to cry when I die."

She seemed to be aware that the war wasn't over yet.

"I met my mom."

Although it was daytime on the other side of the planet and incidents were happening all over the world, this was the only good news Shirone could share.

"Really? You mean your biological mother? Then, could it be..."

"Yes, it's Ikael. It's a bit awkward now, but it will get better with time. I'll introduce you to her."

Amy was genuinely happy for him.

"Of course. How did it happen?"

Sitting side by side on the bed, they shared everything that had happened so far.

When talking about Nane, Amy was cautious, but Shirone understood.

"I guessed it was probably like that. I also reached this point through Nane’s philosophy."

"But it doesn't feel right. Honestly, I was shaken. Not by Nane, but by what he proposed. The reason I'm bringing this up is that I feel like I'm betraying you..."

"Yes. I felt the same. I left you and our friends for humanity. That's how it is, Amy."

Shirone placed a hand on Amy's shoulder.

"What is hard for me is also hard for you. That's the heart, and now we know it."

Amy suddenly thought that Ultima might not be so far away.

"Shirone."

As Amy's voice became emotional, Shirone turned to face her.

Her hair, burned by the fire, was short, but it didn't change who Amy was.

The silence grew longer, pushing their faces closer together.

Just before their lips touched, the door suddenly opened.

"Amy, what are you... Oh?"

At Tess's voice, the two jumped apart, their faces turning red.

Tess chuckled.

"Shirone is here too. Did I interrupt?"

"Haha, no."

While Shirone waved his hand dismissively, Amy glared at Tess.

"Why ask when you already know?"

Tess, who had been watching Amy's reaction with amusement, smiled broadly and pointed outside.

"Come on. Let's have a drink."

"At this hour? Aren't you tired?"

"Nade says all non-injured personnel are to gather in the dining hall. It's been a while since we all got together, so we should at least have a welcome drink. Rian doesn't know the magic school friends well. Neither do I."

"Oh, right."

Although Shirone was friendly with everyone, there were some who hadn't interacted much with his friends.

"Alright, let's go."

As they headed to the dining hall, the noise echoed through the corridor even before they arrived.

"Oh? It's Shirone, Shirone!"

Nade, who had already downed two beers, stood up, chewing on meat, and grinned.

"Oh, they came together? See, I told you they'd be together."

Amy's eyes narrowed as she spotted the instigator, and Nade laughed, clutching his belly.

Dorothy brought drinks and food.

"Thank you."

If Shirone hadn't come, Iruki might not be here now.

"Thank you? We fought together."

In the cheerful atmosphere, the drinks kept flowing, and everyone's faces turned red.

Even as they shared their tales of valor, Rian sat alone, sipping his drink with a smile.

"Nice to meet you."

Iruki spoke to Rian, who was sitting next to him.

"I've heard a lot about you from Shirone. Of course, I've also read hundreds of reports. Let's get along."

Seeing Rian's empty beer glass, Iruki picked up a bottle and offered it.

As everyone watched with satisfaction, Rian politely took the glass with both hands and extended it.

An awkward silence followed, and even Iruki seemed taken aback, staring at Rian.

Tess shook her head, squinting.

'Oh, this frustrating guy.'

Shirone's friend.

Rian thought he couldn't be casual with his lord's friends.

"Nice to meet you. I'm Rian, Shirone's knight. I look forward to working with you."

Amy spoke up.

"Rian, you don't have to be so formal. We're all friends and about the same age."

"Sorry if I ruined the mood. But I can't be the first to be disrespectful."

Shirone gave a gentle smile.

'He's still tense.'

The reason they were sharing their tales with the help of alcohol was to relieve the stress of battle.

But Rian was different.

As long as he was protecting Shirone, he would never let his guard down.

Such a friend was reliable and trustworthy, but Shirone also believed in having fun when it was time to relax.

"Yes, Rian. I hope you can relax. After all, we're fighting as a team."

Only then did Rian take the glass with one hand.

"Alright, alright! Let's drink until we drop tonight. Cheers, everyone! Cheers!"

After a few more rounds, the conversation shifted to the Ultima system.

Iruki asked,

"What are we going to do? Honestly, the only breakthrough is the Ultima system, but is it really possible?"

Integrating the minds of humanity.

"I have a few alternative ideas. I'll discuss them with Miro when she arrives tomorrow."

Amy was surprised.

"There are alternatives?"

The fact that there were alternatives, regardless of the solution, was shocking.

"Yes. They're not perfect, though."

"Like what?"

Tess, who had been drinking from a bottle, asked, and Shirone thought for a moment before standing up.

"Then let me show you here."

Everyone stopped chatting and watched as Shirone clasped his hands and closed his eyes.

Light spread between his palms, eventually becoming a transparent glow.

"Miracle Stream? What's he doing all of a sudden...?"

Shirone spread his arms and looked around with a playful glint in his eyes.

"Your sins..."

Light poured from his palms, filling the dining hall.

"Are forgiven."

"Ah..."

A moment of awe followed, and about ten minutes later, in the dining hall,

"Uwaaaah!"

Nade was crying his eyes out.

"I'm sorry. I didn't know such love existed in the world... I'll live a good life. Uwaaaah!"

Iruki, looking exasperated, shouted,

"Stop crying already! How many sins have you committed to be crying like this?"

It was a rare sight for the Thunder God.

"You cried too! And who was it that bawled their eyes out looking at me in the Holy War? You're the crybaby!"

"...Saying you'll live a good life was a lie."

Shirone agreed.

"Haha, it's not that easy. But the light of Agape purifies the human heart. I'll explain why this is important in tomorrow's formal meeting, but it will help establish an integrated mental system."

Dorothy asked,

"But isn't your explanation too technical? I was feeling moved and satisfied."

Shirone smiled with his eyes.

"Given the situation, it's not about wishing things would turn out well. We must succeed. After tomorrow's meeting, I plan to travel the world and spread this light."

"Travel?"

Nade and Iruki's eyes lit up simultaneously.
