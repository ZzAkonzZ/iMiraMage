Chapter 327: Second Outing (5)

But it was too early to celebrate. The target, which seemed about to fall, bounced back due to its elasticity.

"Too bad! The first attempt is a failure!"

Vivian stared at the oscillating target in disbelief and then glared at the announcer.

"Why is it a failure? It hit the target perfectly."

"No, no. I clearly said the target must be knocked down."

"Isn't this a scam?"

"Haha! A scam? Is this your first time at the winter festival? Everyone knows this. Besides, do you know what kind of collection this is? If you resell it later, the doll is worth more than one gold."

Jokre turned to Rudvans and asked.

"It seems there's a spring attached below. Can you do it?"

"Well... there are still nine throws left."

Rudvans placed the snowballs in a convenient position and focused on the target.

'They've made the angle tricky. I guess I have to rely on a straight throw.'

Over the next ten minutes, he threw all nine remaining snowballs.

Despite his determined efforts, Rudvans remained expressionless, but sweat formed on his forehead, and steam rose from his head.

"Too bad. I think I could do it with one more try."

The attempt was a failure.

He hit the target with the second and third throws but missed the fourth, fifth, and sixth, which was his downfall.

Hitting the target with maximum force from a distance greater than a sports field was not easy, even for a semi-professional.

He managed to hit the target with all the remaining snowballs from the seventh throw onward, but the target still didn't fall.

Shirone got a hint from this.

'You have to use the elasticity to knock it down. No wonder they installed a spring on the target.'

If you hit the target consecutively while the pendulum motion is still active, the elasticity gradually increases. Eventually, it reaches a tipping point where it can't withstand the weight and falls.

Rudvans' accuracy in hitting seven out of ten was impressive, but he failed because the force wasn't applied continuously.

"Amy, how about giving it a try?"

"Me?"

Amy looked at Shirone in surprise.

In fact, her playful nature made her eager to try such a fun game. But usually, boys would take on the challenge to show off in front of girls, so she just watched.

'Ah, I see what's going on.'

She understood Shirone's intention. The person who needed to show off here was herself.

'In that case, there's no need to hold back.'

Amy walked over to the announcer and paid ten silver.

If Rudvans was the representative player for Tuku, Amy's pride was on the line as a future sniper.

"I'll give it a try too. Here's ten silver."

"Oh, it's been a while since we've had a female challenger. But miss, the soldiers in the watchtower won't be swayed by beauty. Still, I'll give you an extra snowball as a bonus. Do your best."

"No, ten will be enough."

"Haha! What a spirited young lady! Everyone, give a round of applause for this brave girl!"

The spectators applauded as the slender girl, who seemed unlikely to even throw a snowball to the watchtower, stepped up to the challenge.

Amy licked her lips and picked up a snowball. Her eyes turned red as her photographic memory recorded the current state.

"Here we go."

With a cute motion, she swung her arm, but the snowball fell short, not even reaching halfway.

The spectators laughed good-naturedly.

But Shirone held his breath, knowing Amy's true skills were about to show.

Unlike the first throw, Amy took a proper stance and stared intently at the target.

Her photographic memory corrected the errors and made her brain sense the precise trajectory.

Using the basic build of Skima for strength enhancement, she firmly grasped the snowball, widened her stance, and swung her arm powerfully.

The snowball flew at a speed similar to Rudvans' and hit the target, making the spectators' eyes widen.

Amy stored the state of the second throw in her photographic memory. Then she mechanically threw the third, fourth, and fifth snowballs.

With each hit, the target's oscillation increased until it finally couldn't withstand the eighth throw and fell with a thud.

"Wow! She knocked it down! A girl knocked down the target!"

"Miss, are you an athlete? That's amazing!"

Amy looked up at the ceiling and exhaled with relief.

Having tried it herself, she realized it was indeed a challenging game. Even a Skima user would struggle without trained sensory skills.

Of course, a Skima master could knock down the target in one shot with cannon-like power, but such a person wouldn't bother making a scene at a festival just to win a doll.

The announcer gladly handed the prize to the successful challenger.

Holding the Ice Queen doll, Amy thanked the cheering spectators and lifted it like a trophy.

Vivian looked at the smiling Ice Queen doll with a wistful expression.

"Oh, I'm so jealous. I wanted that."

Shirone and Amy, excited, walked away without even remembering Jokre's group.

Rudvans spoke.

"...This is a bit annoying."

Jokre felt the same.

"Hmph, she's always been self-centered since she was a kid. That's why she failed the graduation exam. They say she was beaten up. No wonder she can only hang out with commoners."

Shirone and Amy remembered Jokre's group only after reaching the first floor.

They had no desire to go back. Those people hadn't approached with good intentions from the start.

Amy hugged the Ice Queen doll to her chest and rubbed her cheek against it, enjoying the soft texture.

"Hehe! It's so cute."

Shirone felt content just watching her.

Despite her outward personality, she liked cute dolls. Her room was filled with teddy bears, after all.

'I'm glad she has such a normal taste.'

He genuinely thought so.

Unlike Woorin's collection of El Crouch's porcelain dolls, which were too eccentric to collect unless one had a peculiar taste.

"Since we won it for twenty silver, even if we sell it for just one gold, we made eighty silver."

"Haha! That's so like you. But isn't it time to stop thinking like that? You're the richest person in the magic school."

"The richest? Me?"

Amy pointed to Shirone's coat.

"Armand. They say its stolen value alone is over twenty billion gold. At auction, it would fetch at least twice that. In other words, you're carrying our entire school with you."

Only then did Shirone realize and blinked.

The amount was so astronomical that he hadn't even considered the sword's value.

Thinking about walking among so many people with an S-class object worth over forty billion gold suddenly made his side, where the sword was strapped, feel heavy.

'What if someone steals it? No, that's not possible. It's mentally linked to me.'

Amy carefully placed the Ice Queen doll in her bag. Shirone, who had been watching, pointed outside.

"It's hot from the game. Let's go out and cool off."

"Yeah. I need some fresh air too. How about skating? I heard the ice quality here is really good."

Shirone nodded eagerly.

He remembered the fun times skating on a frozen lake with skates his father had made for him as a child.

"Great! Let's head to the skating rink!"

The skating rink was filled with couples.

Shirone and Amy joined the flow of skaters circling the rink.

The cold winter wind felt refreshing.

While gliding on the ice, following the person in front, Amy could momentarily forget her worries.

But her good mood vanished the moment she saw Jokre's group entering the rink.

Not joining the flow, they showcased their skating skills in the center of the rink.

It wasn't against the rules, but Amy, already annoyed with them, found it irritating.

"What? Did they follow us here?"

"They probably came to skate. Don't mind them."

Amy didn't want to get involved with Jokre's group.

Seeing them enjoying their time after passing the graduation exam only deepened the wound of her failure.

"Forget it. Let's just avoid them. We've cooled down, and I want to look around other places."

"Alright. Let's go."

Shirone and Amy slowly skated towards the exit.

"Hey! Leaving already?"

When Shirone turned around, Jokre was skating towards them at full speed, waving his arm.

He quickly pulled Amy aside as Jokre skidded to a stop, spraying ice shavings into their faces. The cold particles wet their faces, making Shirone frown.

"Why are you doing this? Right in our faces."

"Haha! Why are you getting mad over that? You're more uptight than I heard. It was just a joke."

"Why are you joking around like this?"

Amy pushed Shirone aside and stepped forward.

"Why do you keep following us? If you have something to say, say it straight. Stop acting like kids."

"It's been a while since we met. Let's have some fun. We still have some unfinished business."

"Unfinished business? What business?"

The only thing Amy remembered about Jokre was that he had transferred to another school, seemingly running away from competition at the prestigious school.

'Huh? Could it be...?'

A vague memory surfaced in Amy's mind.

Before transferring, Jokre had confessed his love to her.

At the time, it was just a routine event. It was such a trivial incident that it was surprising she even remembered it.

But today, she was mindful of Shirone's feelings.

No woman would take the word "date" as a joke, even if said jokingly.

Amy was a woman and not naive.

She had followed Shirone on this 2-night, 3-day trip fully aware that anything could happen.

She didn't want to make Shirone uncomfortable with a meaningless past.

Simply being confessed to wasn't a bad memory, but having the person in front of her was a different matter.

"Enough. I don't want to talk to you. Shirone, let's go somewhere else. We don't need to hang out with people like them."

As Amy tried to leave, Jokre ignored Shirone and approached her.

"Wait a minute. Let's finish our conversation..."

Jokre stopped talking and turned his head. Shirone was holding his shoulder, frowning.

"She said she doesn't want to."

"Let go of me. We might be from different schools, but I'm still your senior. I'm an official mage."

"That's that, and this is this."

Jokre was taken aback by Shirone's audacity.

"You're getting cocky because people around you are treating you well. Do you know that Amy and I have known each other since we were thirteen? It's none of your business."

"What does it matter when you met? In that case, we met when we were twelve. Stop bothering someone who doesn't want to talk to you and leave."

"What? You met at twelve?"

Jokre was stunned by Shirone's lie.

Having memorized the journal, he knew about Shirone's past.

How could someone who had only been at the magic school for a year have met Amy at twelve?

Moreover, he was the son of a mountain man, not a noble.

"You're surprisingly shameless. Do you think that lie will work? Do you know what happens if you get on a senior's bad side in this field? Especially someone like you with no connections. You'll be kicked out."

"If there are seniors who make petty threats, I don't want to be there."

"You little...!"

Jokre lost his temper and raised his fist at Shirone, who didn't back down.

But just as he was about to swing, a snowball flew from somewhere and hit the back of his head with a thud.
