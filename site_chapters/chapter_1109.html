Chapter 1109: The Unknown Depths of Humanity (1)

Past midnight, Shirone and his group made their way through the Vatican’s secret passageways, descending into a dimly lit corridor where the lights had been extinguished.

‘At the end of this hallway lies the Inquisition.’

Shirone’s goal was to uncover the true nature of the Inquisition, making a discreet approach essential.

According to Seina, the guards rotated every eight hours.

‘Regardless of their rank, they’re clergy. It’s better not to use mental magic.’

After a tense wait, the clock struck 2 AM. Footsteps echoed in the darkness.

“Ugh, I’m so sleepy,” murmured one guard as he walked past Shirone’s group, blinking groggily.

“Standing guard in the dark is brutal.”

“Yeah, especially the night shift.”

At the end of the corridor, the relief team was already waiting.

“Anything to report?”

“Nothing. No one ever comes here.”

Most of the guards didn’t even know the Inquisition’s exact location.

‘Now’s the time.’

As if on cue, Shirone’s group moved in unison. Guided by faint, firefly-like light, they advanced until a large iron door blocked their path. The metallic scent in the air was overwhelming.

The time window was brief—at most, one minute before the new guards arrived.

‘Can we do this?’

Despite Seina’s concern, Shirone skillfully manipulated her Miracle Stream, thinning it into a delicate thread and threading it into the lock mechanism.

‘Sound!’

Realizing the potential noise, Seina looked up in alarm, but the lock clicked open soundlessly.

‘Vacuum magic.’

As the iron door creaked open, a cacophony of voices echoed out.

“Ah!” Shirone quickly shut the door, controlling the sound waves with precision.

“God! Punish me!”

A scream pierced the air, and Seina’s face turned pale.

“This… this is the Inquisition?”

Iron doors lined the corridor like a prison, and from behind them came horrifying sounds.

“No… this can’t be.”

Even for a basement, this was too far removed from the sanctity and dignity expected of the Vatican.

“I wouldn’t have thought this way before receiving your blessing.”

Seina couldn’t argue.

‘I already knew.’

Though she’d never seen it with her own eyes, she’d always had an inkling of how the Inquisition dealt with heretics.

It wasn’t fear of what she now realized.

The true horror was…

‘How could I live so blindly, without questioning any of this?’

Even with such inhumane acts happening beneath the Vatican, she had never stopped to think.

“Let’s go. This is just the beginning,” Shirone said.

“The beginning?”

Shirone’s gaze pierced the darkness.

“Paladins are trained from childhood. Do you know where?”

Seina searched her memories.

“Of course not. We were too young, and by the time I could remember, all I knew was it was somewhere deep in the mountains.”

Shirone pointed into the darkness.

“Beyond this place lies the paladin training grounds. The Vatican isn’t just the church itself—north of here, the mountains stretch out, dotted with numerous villages. Those who are sentenced by the Inquisition are exiled there.”

“And there… is where paladins are trained?”

“It’s a question we need to confirm for ourselves. Let’s move.”

As they advanced, Seina became lost in thought.

‘The grueling training of my childhood.’

It had been a grueling ordeal, both physically and mentally.

‘But I survived. I lived with pride, defeating countless evils.’

How had things come to this?

‘I need to reclaim my divine power. As Yahweh said, I must confirm the truth and decide for myself.’

As they passed the iron bars, Seina glimpsed the horrors inside.

“Kill me! Just kill me!” shouted one prisoner, his voice filled with fury.

Others wept.

“I was wrong! I was so foolish! Please forgive me, O Lord!”

Still others laughed maniacally.

“Hehehe! Yes, more! Let me taste heaven!”

Seina’s hair stood on end.

“This… this is just like the paladins of the Inquisition.”

Shirone nodded.

“When thinking itself becomes a torment, the human mind simplifies to its most primal state. That’s why they’re so fanatical—and strong.”

Seina had once been strong, too.

“But that’s not true strength. Real strength lies not in destruction, but in the power to protect.”

At that moment, an iron door creaked open, and an Inquisitor emerged, his body drenched in blood and tears of blood streaking his face.

A Crying One.

“I… I am so sad. This world… this reality…”

Despite Shirone’s group standing nearby, he walked past them like a machine, heading toward another room.

Iruqi’s expression turned grim.

‘He’s completely gone. He doesn’t even see us. His mind is paralyzed, and he’s operating on autopilot.’

Inside one of the opened rooms, a brutally beaten man lay collapsed.

Seina approached.

“He’s lost too much blood. Hold on, I’ll stop the bleeding—”

“Kill me,” the man rasped.

“Please… just kill me and free me from this prison.”

Seina, her face crumpling with emotion, closed her eyes and shouted, “I’ve seen enough!”

“Yahweh was right,” she admitted.

“I was wrong. There’s no need to investigate further. Let’s leave and rescue these people first.”

That would have been the logical decision, but Shirone remained deep in thought.

“Kill me! I said kill me!”

The man weakly flailed his arms, his strength drained. Seina took his hand.

“It’s okay now. We’ll get you out of here.”

“Kehehe.”

A scraping sound came from the man’s throat.

“Kehehehe! Get me out? Of what?”

“From this prison, of course—”

Suddenly, the man’s eyes focused sharply for the first time.

“Is there a prison this vast?”

As everyone grappled with the meaning of his words, the man lunged forward, screaming.

“Kill me! KILL ME!”

Blood splattered across Seina’s face as she recoiled in terror, just as she stumbled backward.

"It's pointless."

An eerie voice came from beyond the iron gate.

‘When did he get here?’

A man with a robe that trailed to the floor stood holding chains in both hands.

Iruki asked, "Who are you?"

"Vanisa."

He was second in rank in the Holy See, known as the Forgiver.

Even Seina, a paladin of the Oracle Management Division, had never encountered him before.

"What is going on here?"

Vanisa remained unresponsive.

"A tragedy like this in the sacred Holy See? Who authorized this?"

"…God."

When Vanisa removed his hood, the robe slipped off his body like a snake shedding its skin.

Seina’s eyes widened as nausea surged from the pit of her stomach.

‘What has he done to his body?’

Certain parts of his flesh were missing, exposing bones, and his abdomen bore twisted marks, as if his organs had been contorted.

Shirone stepped forward. "I’ll handle this. He’s strong."

Yaksha or Prajna. No, perhaps something that combined both.

"Kill… Kill him."

The tortured man groaned in agony as Vanisa pointed with his scythe.

"You're lucky. Why not grant him mercy?"

Shirone retorted sharply. "Taking him out of that prison is mercy."

"Prison?"

Vanisa’s lips curled into a smile. "We are all imprisoned.

Right here."

He thrust his scythe into his own chest. "This prison of flesh."

"An absolutely perfect constraint that permits not even a millimeter. That is the body. Look at mine."

As he spread his upper body, broken ribs tore through the flesh and jutted out.

Seina turned her head away, but he continued.

"Where in this body… is there room for me to rest?"

Vanisa approached slowly. "There's nowhere to hide. Pain is entirely mine to bear. And that is why God created us."

Neid unleashed lightning. "He's out of his mind."

"Out of my mind? From the moment we are born, we are imprisoned and subjected to endless suffering until we escape this prison. When we can no longer endure the pain, God opens the gates of the prison for us."

Vanisa stopped within scythe's reach. "He releases the mind that has grasped absolute truth from the prison of the body. You’re the ones who are out of your minds."

Some choose suicide at the end of despair.

"That man cannot kill himself. He hasn’t attained the truth. Yet, he seeks to escape the prison of flesh. I am helping him."

Twisted logic.

It surely was, but it was irrefutable for one reason:

‘The outside world.’

Now, Shirone had an inkling of God’s identity.

‘Vanisa doesn’t know about the outside world. His beliefs are self-realized. But they were also born from extreme suffering...

A single human’s truth.’

"Foolish creations, God has imprisoned us in flesh. We can never escape the karma of existence. Repent. Realize the truth through suffering. I will forgive you."

"Why?"

Shirone sought truth from the madman.

"What’s the reason? Why does God punish us?"

"For one reason only."

As Vanisa raised his head, his body tilted forward.

"For the glory of God."

"Move!"

Neid and Iruki sprang into action, while Shirone activated Miracle Stream to shield Seina.

As light smoke wrapped around Vanisa’s arms, a tremendous pressure bore down.

"Ugh!"

Even with severed muscles, his body moved—divine transcendence.

"Yahweh."

The reason he resisted the power of Miracle Stream was mental transcendence.

"You are the heretic."

Clenching her teeth, Shirone whipped the light, slamming Vanisa into the hallway wall.

Boom!

Before the echo of the explosion subsided, Shirone shouted. "Go! Find the exit!"

Understanding Vanisa's strength, Neid and Iruki grabbed the weakened Seina by the wrist and ran down the corridor.

"Conviction, flesh, philosophy, movement."

Vanisa’s broken body rose like a zombie. "Do you think those can accomplish anything? Absolutely not, Yahweh. It’s an illusion."

Snapping a protruding rib, he seized his scythe and charged again.

"There is no way to escape God’s prison."

Kaiden couldn’t sleep. ‘Maya.’

He was stationed in the Delta Headquarters in the Tormia district, guarding the artists' residence, where he had been assigned the room next to Maya's.

‘Is she asleep?’

Though he stared at the wall, he saw nothing but the vivid play of his imagination.

‘Stop it. Guarding Maya is enough. I don’t need anything more.’

That’s when he felt something strange in his lower half.

"What?"

Looking down, he found his pants undone, and his right hand moving on its own.

"Ahhh! What the hell! Damn it!"

God's Hand Syndrome.

It seemed to react to desires, but its criteria for action were incomprehensible.

"You stupid hand! Stop it! I’m not some monkey!"

As his left hand wrestled his right, Maya burst through the door.

"Kaiden! What’s going on?"

"Ahhh! Ahhhhhh!"

Kaiden was suddenly grateful for his body’s superhuman reflexes.

Frantically pulling up the blanket, he saw Maya blush and wave her hands. "S-Sorry! I didn’t see anything! I swear!"

‘Damn it! Damn it!’

Kaiden buried his head in the blanket in frustration.

‘I’m hopeless. I’ll never be able to protect Maya.’

As if to contradict his thoughts, his right hand raised its index finger and slowly moved it side to side.
