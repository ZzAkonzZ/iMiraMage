Chapter 467:

The Seventh Heaven, Araboth.

While the war between Heaven's army and the rebels raged on the outskirts of Heaven, Araboth, located at the center of the concentric circles, remained tranquil.

Under the spire, where neither the sound of cannons nor screams could reach, Ikael stood, gazing at the distant rising smoke.

Her eyes held no emotion. Only the dry, unwavering will to carry out Anke Ra's will remained.

As a piercing presence approached Ikael, her halo expanded reflexively.

Chiiiiing!

The halo spun at an unprecedented speed, indicating that her thoughts were also at their peak.

Few in Heaven could make Archangel Ikael this tense.

"What are you guarding?"

A handsome, androgynous figure approached from Ikael's left.

It was the Archangel of Light, Raiel.

"Or are you waiting for someone?"

From her right appeared Metatron, a being with shoulders twice as broad as other angels, despite his short stature.

Then, in front of her, appeared Metiel and Satiel, the Archangels of Fusion and Disintegration.

"So, the war has begun. And with a humiliating invasion at that. Disappointing, Ikael."

Surrounded by four archangels with the spire of Araboth at her back, Ikael's expression remained cold.

As she briefly glanced at each of them, only one thought crossed her mind.

'Faiel isn't moving.'

The Archangel of Annihilation, Faiel.

Though the four archangels present were powerful enemies dealing with fundamental concepts, Faiel's presence was special to Ikael.

In terms of sheer power, if Uriel, the Archangel of Destruction, was her equal, Faiel was practically her nemesis in the concept of amplification.

Of course, this was true for Faiel as well.

In this uneasy situation, it seemed they had chosen to observe for the time being.

'Is that fortunate?'

"Hey, Ikael."

Metatron approached with a creaking neck.

"I know what you're thinking. But aren't you underestimating us too much? Even you can't take on all four of us."

"Why did you rebel? Is it a rejection of Anke Ra?"

As Raiel took a step forward, blue electricity crackled around him.

A being whose essence was light, not magic.

Even Ikael wouldn't be safe if hit directly.

'They haven't summoned their Maras. They've already deployed them to the battlefield.'

Raiel spoke.

"Our stance on Anke Ra is firm. But you are not."

Satiel approached.

"Is Archangel Ikael a thing of the past? Being swayed by a mere human is a greater sin than being a fallen angel. This is your doing."

For the first time, a murderous intent flickered in Ikael's eyes.

"Arrogant fools."

Angered by the provocation, Metatron activated his halo.

An immense gravitational field spread out, making the very landscape seem to bend towards him.

"Perhaps you've been Archangel for too long. Even if you were the first angel, there's no hierarchy among archangels. Let's settle this here."

As Metatron finished speaking, the four archangels simultaneously activated their powers.

Electromagnetic force, gravity, weak force, strong force.

The four fundamental forces of the universe simultaneously pressed down on Ikael.

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

Four shockwaves exploded simultaneously.

As the archangels disappeared, Ikael's nose wrinkled in anger.

Her halo expanded to a diameter of several dozen meters, and a massive Ataraxia shot up into the sky like a pillar of light.

The immense power swept through Araboth.

Flash!

Everyone in Heaven could see the massive surge of energy rising above the spire.

The Fifth Heaven, Matei.

In the city of giants, where everything was larger than human standards, the main force of the rebel headquarters was fiercely fighting.

Mecca's technology was specialized for the giants, and 2,000 units of their main equipment, Kuroi, had been deployed, accounting for a quarter of their total.

"Advance! Advance!"

Commander Crude, aboard a Titan, fired heat flash cannons in all directions, encouraging his troops.

In response, the Kuroi pilots unleashed massive firepower on the charging giants.

In just two hours since the war began, they had advanced a mere two kilometers.

But the deeper they went, the fiercer the enemy's counterattacks became, and the giants' abilities increased exponentially.

"Damn it!"

The two kilometers they had advanced now felt like a mere two kilometers.

The Kuroi units were helpless against the powerful giant army, whose elixir of myths had reached the third stage.

Bullets couldn't penetrate their muscles, and they didn't have a significant advantage in strength either.

"Yaaah!"

Kanya fixed Kuroi's barrel forward and fired madly.

As the war began, her reason evaporated, and she was filled with the rage of losing her father and the memory of her mother, whose life was shortened by the elixir of myths.

"Sister! Stay calm!"

Lena, equipped with infantry gear, shouted.

Even as she shouted, her hand was pulling the handle of the arc.

As the explosive rounds fired, a giant clad in armor appeared before her.

It was Orsimo, the commander of the first battalion of the giant army.

"Uooooh!"

Bang!

Blocking the bomb with his shoulder armor, he swung a massive greatsword.

The power of Skima in his strike surpassed Mecca's machinery, and Lena couldn't react to the speed.

"Lena!"

Kanya turned her body at the sight of her sister's danger on the multivision.

But Kuroi's response speed couldn't keep up with her urgency.

Why had she forgotten? She still had someone to protect.

'Idiot!'

Tears welled up as regret flooded in.

At that moment, with a clang, someone blocked Orsimo's sword.

"Ugh!"

Orsimo's strong features twisted like a beast.

As the commander of Heaven's guard, he was well-versed in Mecca's technology.

But the equipment blocking his path was something he had never seen before.

"Ba-Babel?"

Lena, weakened and sitting on the ground, looked up.

Babel's back, reflecting the sunlight, shone with a dull light.

According to Shirone's algorithm, Babel was programmed to prioritize saving Kanya and Lena.

In other words, it meant that Babel was always monitoring their status.

Sensing the sisters' danger, Babel had immediately left the battlefield and rushed to their aid.

"Does a mere machine dare to challenge a giant!"

Orsimo gripped his greatsword with both hands and swung with immense force.

But even the strength of a battalion commander couldn't scratch Babel.

With a clear metallic sound, the greatsword was blocked by her arm, and Orsimo's eyes widened in shock.

The vibration transmitted through his hands made it clear how solidly Babel was constructed.

Target eliminated.

Babel deflected the sword and, with a swift chop, severed Orsimo's head.

As she landed gracefully, Lena, who had been watching in a daze, muttered unconsciously.

"Th-Thank you."

Though a machine couldn't have emotions, she was human, and her gratitude to her savior was genuine.

Watching this, Kanya bit her lip.

The complex tangle of emotions, like tangled blood vessels in her heart, transmitted pain through her nerves.

Babel was the enemy who killed her father.

But now, she had saved her beloved sister.

This left her confused.

How should she perceive the metal angel before her?

Ching!

Kanya aimed Kuroi's barrel at Babel.

The image of her father, pierced through, was still vivid. The more she thought about it, the more sinister Babel seemed.

'Sister…'

Babel turned and looked at Kanya expressionlessly.

Aiming the barrel was a clear sign of attack.

But her algorithm didn't include retaliation against Kanya.

Click!

In the end, Kuroi's barrel lowered again.

Kanya shouted at Lena, as if to vent her confused emotions.

"Don't leave my side from now on!"

Leaving those words, Kanya advanced again.

The thought of protecting Lena cooled her head, but the emotional knot remained.

Gradually, a path opened up beyond the bodies of the giants.

The Second Heaven, Lakia.

Gaold's party, except for Shirone and Plu, watched the pillar of light rising from Araboth.

It meant an unknown variable had arisen, as the rebels couldn't have reached Araboth yet.

Armin spoke.

"That's a powerful energy no human could produce. It's likely an archangel."

Zulu said.

"If there's discord among the archangels, it's our chance. We must go now."

Gaold had no objections.

Even if all the archangels gathered to block them, he intended to sweep them aside and advance.

Sein spoke.

"Let's move. Once the operation starts, there's no need for improvisation or assistance. Focus solely on completing your individual missions."

Shirone had infiltrated first through a covert operation, and the remaining members were all tasked with rescuing Miro.

Since Miro was likely in the deepest part of Lakia, once they rescued her, they could enter the temple through Araboth.

Kangnan spoke.

"Even if we change the coordinates of the Akashic Record in the temple, we have no way to return. Miro's space-time is already broken."

"We have to trust them."

Gaold thought of Frankwine, the leader of the Night Watch.

There was no way out of Heaven except to bring what he had promised on time.

But Gaold didn't care.

For now, rescuing Miro was the top priority.

"Move at full speed. If anyone falls behind, we'll leave them."

As soon as Gaold finished speaking, he took off.

Everyone followed, each using their unique abilities to keep up with his incredible speed.

At the entrance to Lakia, numerous fallen angels and fire giants awaited them with hostile eyes.

'Miro…'

Gaold increased his speed even more.

Finally, they had made it this far.

The gears of time, which had stopped just 20 meters short, began to tick again.

The distance to Miro was 42.7 kilometers.

"Stop them! Don't let them get close!"

The fire giants conjured massive fireballs.

A wave of fire surged over the heads of the giants, who were lined up for hundreds of meters.

"Fire!"

It was a tsunami of heat.

Gaold's eyes sparkled as the fiery wave approached, ready to burn everything in its path.

There was no need to hold back anymore.

Miro was just beyond this.

"Raaaah!"

Gaold's face twisted like a demon's, veins and nerves bulging.

Pain sensitivity increased by 5 million times.

Boom!

An unprecedented shockwave.

The fiery tsunami was instantly obliterated by the overwhelming atmospheric pressure.

Simultaneously, fallen angels with wings of light flew in from both sides.

As Armin cast a stop spell, dozens of angels froze in place.

Iron Wheel Eye - Equalizing.

Sein's eyes spun rapidly, controlling the environment.

Increasing the atmospheric pressure wasn't tactically beneficial, but it was to enhance Gaold's combat power.

"Charge!"

The fire giants rushed towards Gaold.

The once orderly formation began to distort with emotion, all of them charging at Gaold.

The distance to Miro was now 40.3 kilometers.
