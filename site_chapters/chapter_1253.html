Chapter 1253:

Xing asked, "An error…?"

"That's the only explanation that makes sense. Why else would she throw away the perfect chance to kill me?"

Even Xing couldn't argue with that.

"So what now? We're holding off the Cell Buster with the power of the Taiji, but it won't last much longer."

Shirone looked up at the mass of anti-cells compressed in the sky.

"What makes the Cell Buster so dangerous is that anti-cells endlessly divide. Destroy one, and it becomes two. Destroy two, and it becomes four. Keep dividing like that, and they'll eventually outnumber all of humanity. That's how the world ends."

"They're still multiplying?"

The fact that the mass was vibrating was proof enough.

"If this keeps up, it’ll explode. And when it does, an unimaginable number of anti-cells will flood into the world."

"I’ll find a way."

"How?"

Shirone shook his head.

"I don’t know yet. But… if anyone might have the answer, it’s him."

Ardino Fermi.

'He’s smarter than me in this kind of stuff.'

Once his wounds had healed enough, Shirone stepped away from Xing, who had been supporting him.

"That ends the Ivory Tower’s simultaneous incident. I’ll meet Fermi at the Holy War. Xing, gather the remaining Stars here and join me there."

"Before that… I want to ask something."

Though Shirone's survival was a miracle for humanity, there was still a lingering concern.

"What do you think of Great Star? Umji and Yangji sacrificed themselves. I need you to give me a clear judgment."

"I believe."

Shirone’s voice was firm.

"Of course, I don’t mean I trust Administrators. That was clearly my mistake. But there must’ve been a reason Great Star didn’t kill me. Something that made it impossible for her not to."

"And if there wasn’t?"

Xing asked, "What if there was no reason? What if she simply thought stopping your heart was enough?"

"Then…"

Shirone’s eyes turned sharp with killing intent.

"I’ll never forgive her. Not just Great Star—but even the gods. They’ll all pay for mocking humanity."

Xing smiled. "That’s all I needed."

Umji and Yangji probably would’ve said the same.

"See you at the Holy War."

Shirone’s body faded into light, leaving Xing alone at the peak of the Ivory Tower.

"Alone again?"

As the thought that no one can ever truly know another’s truth began to resurface—

"5 Star."

The Stars of the Unified Space Administration Department entered.

‘Mini. Ariana. Kira.’

Though most of the Ivory Tower’s residents had been injured or killed, there were still allies left to fight.

"Let’s go."

Xing decided— She would trust Shirone.

Electromagnetic Phase Space.

In this invisible domain where waves of light-speed signals intersected, the Administrators communicated.

Though their exchange was cold, if it were interpreted in human terms…

"Why did you do it?"

Anyone would think Argones was angry.

"What do you mean?"

"You could’ve made sure he was dead. Cut his head off, destroyed his brain—that would’ve been easy."

Great Star’s response came slowly.

"I don’t know what you’re talking about. Shirone surviving was not my intention. I did the best my program allowed me to."

Argones didn’t respond.

Administrators govern individual sectors to prevent system disturbance.

‘What’s going on with him?’

If translated into human thinking, the analysis from Argones’s sector might sound like this:

‘It’s hard to say the Gaia Program changed its method for humanity’s sake. That would be a contradiction as extreme as me not activating the Cell Buster.’

‘An error?’

Should I report this to the higher sector?

If I were just a program, I’d be obligated to—but Argones couldn’t bring himself to do it.

"Stop it. If you're doubting me, know that I won't overlook you either."

Though they were equals in authority, in system hierarchy, Gaia was above Argones.

‘She’s blocking me completely. Why?’

Argones asked, "I don’t want to fight you. If there’s really no issue, then we can let the core system decide."

"There is no issue."

As Argones searched for a route to bypass Great Star, a new signal arrived.

"Humanity has been denied Ultima."

It was Luber.

"Good work. What about Shirone?"

"He escaped Drimo. Was that your doing, Great Star? If so, it was a brilliant decision."

"Escaped…?"

Argones’s signal surged violently.

"Yes. I sent him. It was the only way to place a restriction on Ymir. Ultima is already out of reach—so the only thing left is humanity’s destruction."

Is that so…?

‘What is wrong with them?’

Argones suddenly wondered, ‘Don’t tell me… I’m the one with the error?’

Great Star said, "There’s no need to overthink this. Your Cell Buster has also been sealed, hasn’t it? If you’re going to blame system errors for miscalculations, then you’re not free from them either."

Monga asked, "But where is the Hidden Code Administrator?"

Lethe of Forgetfulness.

All the Administrators simultaneously sent signals—but no response came.

"This must be the real error."

To not respond at a time when the Outer World’s system was under attack—

Luber said, "We just need to do what we’re meant to. In our respective sectors—destroy humanity and close the universe."

"Fine."

Argones approved.

"Let’s conclude that all sectors except Lethe’s are functioning properly. No further judgment errors will be tolerated. I’ll monitor all programs from now on."

Great Star replied coldly, "If you can."

"...Disappointing."

If they were human, maybe they would’ve said it that way.

The Administrators’ conference ended, and Great Star returned to her system.

In a dim space laced with faint blue light, she knelt quietly.

‘I can’t report this.’

From within her internal system, one directive kept looping endlessly.

Why?

Why?

Why? Why? Why? Why? Why?

‘What happened to me? What went wrong?’

Why? Why? Why? Why? Why?

That one unknown question—its cause, its origin—kept endlessly computing within Great Star’s mind.

Rian had finally arrived.

It was the deepest part of the Valley of Wailing, where once a massive river of fire branched off into countless streams.

“Hah… hah…”

Carrying an unconscious Shirone, his body torn and battered from cutting through waves of Black Chains and demons, Rian had no time to tend to himself—because the enemy was still pursuing them.

Aaaaghhh!

A frail, feminine scream.

No—it wasn't a scream of the body. Jin Seong Eum, chained to thousands of links erupting from the valley, didn’t move an inch.

Which meant…

A scream of the soul.

Aaaaghhh!

Even Rian, hardened by a life of war, felt his bones ache at the sound.

“…Go.”

Jin Seong Eum, limbs spread apart, slowly raised her head.

“Go back…”

They’d met before. Yet her eyes held no recognition, no familiarity.

It’s horrifying…

Was she really trying to endure this pain forever?

“I’m Rian.”

“I said… go back.”

“From here on, I’m going to save you. It’ll hurt—a lot—but I need you to endure it.”

“No… If you purify me… the underworld will open… the world will become Hell…”

“There’s a way. I don’t know it—but Shirone said so. You can go back.”

As she fell silent, Rian gently laid Shirone down and lowered his greatsword.

The chain connecting his hand to the sword heated up, and his Idea blazed red-hot.

“Fuuuuh…”

This was Gehenna’s flame—the fire that purifies all.

Rian had endured horrible pain to get here, but this… this was another level entirely.

“Here I go.”

Jin Seong Eum nodded faintly. Rian’s expression shifted—his face now that of a yaksha—as he charged forward.

“Yaaaah!”

The moment his sword pierced the ground, the chains tethered to the valley quivered violently.

“Hnghhh…!”

Jin Seong Eum, who had never shown weakness beyond the scream of her soul, threw her head back.

“Kyaaaaaaah!”

The chains turned red, then burst into flames, and her accumulated karma began to be purified.

Rian’s face twisted further in agony.

Endure it. Just hold on.

The pain was one thing—but what truly devastated him when he first struck with the greatsword was the realization that…

It never ends.

The karma was so vast, it could have belonged to all humanity.

—Sinner!

Black Chains flew down from the sky. The demons pursuing them had finally spotted Shirone.

“It’s Yahweh! Kill Yahweh!”

Damn it…!

Rian hesitated.

Pain, he could endure. But no amount of effort would speed up the purification process.

Shirone told me to save Jin Seong Eum. I am a knight. I must follow my lord’s orders.

With senses sharper than any insect’s, Rian detected the chains of the Black Chains and the tremors of incoming steps nearby.

“Khhh!”

He twisted his body.

I have to protect Shirone!

Before thought could even catch up, his body moved on instinct. As he reached to yank the greatsword from the ground—

“Stop.”

A woman descended from the sky, holding her hand out.

“Eek!”

The demons flinched in horror. Even the Black Chains dared not disobey.

“Lady Lethe…”

She slowly scanned the crowd of demons, her gaze finally meeting Rian’s—his body still burning as he stood.

He really did it. Ridiculous human…

Even if Gehenna’s flame could purify karma, no human should have been able to endure it.

“You…”

“Don’t move.”

Lethe took one step forward, and Rian reflexively tensed, preparing to leap.

“Stay right where you are. If you so much as move, the sword buried in the ground will sever her neck.”

She meant that Jin Seong Eum could not be used as a hostage—but Lethe’s thoughts were different.

Even this human gets anxious.

She turned to look at Shirone, lying unconscious on the ground.

Yahweh…

In the Choeni Bardo, the boundary between life and death, emotions become tangible, visible.

That Yahweh was completely defenseless now—was proof that he had truly forgiven everything.

Lethe turned around and spoke.

“Withdraw. I will protect this place.”

“Lady Lethe?”

The demons were stunned.

“Why are you stopping us?! This is our chance to kill Yahweh once and for all!”

“I…”

Lethe wasn’t sure if she could convince them. But she forced herself to speak.

“I will fight… on Yahweh’s side.”

Not even a murmur followed.

What filled the air instead was betrayal—and a level of hostility toward Lethe never seen before.

“We must’ve misheard her.”

“No. You heard me clearly. Yahweh has forgiven darkness. Now that the gods seek to close the world, I will follow Yahweh’s will.”

—Lethe.

A Black Chain spoke.

“Isn’t this a system error? An administrator has no authority to defy the decisions made by the gods.”

“I do.”

Lethe lifted her chin and curled her lips into a faint smile.

“Because I am the Heart Program.”

As the only administrator who could compute emotion, she was the sole variable capable of defying God.

Tilting her head slightly, Lethe said,

“Continue.”

Rian hesitated—then accepted. There was no other option but to trust her.

“…I’m counting on you.”

Once it became clear that a human and a demon had joined hands, the leader of the horde spoke.

“Understood.”

And in the next instant, every demon erupted in rage—charging straight at Lethe.

“Die, Lethe, you traitorous wretch!”

As the children she had raised turned to kill her, Lethe’s brow arched in silent sorrow.

Traitorous, huh?

You must’ve been so sad too… Yahweh.

“…Heh.”

Flames of purification ignited in her eyes, and soon her entire body burst into fire like a volcano.

“Come at me as much as you like.”

No matter what sins you’ve committed—she would forget them all.

“I am your mother.”

Lethe, of Forgetfulness.
