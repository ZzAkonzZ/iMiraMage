Chapter 568:

The six gathered in the hallway remained silent, caught between lingering anger and a momentary release of tension.

Sabina, who was the most objective about the situation, asked, "What do you think about the alliance team's proposal?"

"The graduation exam pass card was proposed knowing our team includes outsiders. They joined the Scramble Royal just to lower the ranks. They must have thought the condition of passing the graduation exam would definitely work," Iruki said.

Dorothy asked, "Should I apologize? I was the one who first suggested accepting the proposal."

"No. You made a reasonable request. A team where one person has to sacrifice isn't worth having. Teamwork is crucial in this game."

'Cold,' Dorothy thought, as Shirone spoke up.

"I don't have any complaints about Dorothy's judgment. Personally, it's an unpleasant proposal, but everyone has their own goals. If we criticize Dorothy, we're worse for bringing in outsiders in the first place."

Neid drew everyone's attention.

"Alright, let's be ruthless from now on. Acting on emotions won't get us anywhere. Let's focus on efficiency."

Everyone agreed, but it was still difficult to speak up. As the leader, Neid took the initiative.

"Honestly, I think it's a good proposal."

Shirone nodded.

"I think so too. I don't like the idea of putting human emotions on the line, though."

In truth, he wanted to cancel the Scramble Royal and declare war on Kaiden immediately.

But logically, it was already a partial success that Amy wasn't involved in Kaiden's demands.

'I'm sorry, Amy. But if Kaiden tries to do something to you, I won't be able to fight.'

Even if it meant accepting a forfeit.

Iruki glanced at Amy and said, "As Shirone said, if we set aside emotions, it might not be a serious issue. Even if Shirone confesses, Maya won't accept it under these circumstances. I can talk to her beforehand."

"Ugh!"

Amy couldn't hold back her emotions.

Seeing her biting her lip with tears in her eyes, everyone fell silent.

'Yes, Iruki is right.'

But it was too humiliating and hurt her pride too much to change her mind easily.

Understanding her feelings, Sabina spoke.

"Amy, if you really don't want to…"

"Two."

Amy interrupted.

"Two people must pass. Otherwise, I won't do it."

The difference between one and two people passing the graduation exam was significant, but no one objected.

"Alright, let's go with two. Don't worry too much. You know we'll win."

Instead of answering, Amy glared at Kaiden, who was in the research group.

'I will never forgive you.'

With their opinions aligned, Neid led the women back inside, while Iruki, left in the hallway, asked Shirone,

"Are you really okay with this?"

"Yes. Honestly, I'm relieved. If Amy had been included in the rewards and penalties, I…"

"No, not that."

Shirone adjusted his expression and looked at Iruki.

"Graduating two people. Amy hasn't lost her reason. She probably thought giving Dorothy and Sabina a fair chance at the cards would motivate them to fight hard. But you know, in the end, you and I will be competitors in the graduation exam. If those two get Fermi's support, the difficulty will increase."

Shirone smiled, realizing this.

"Yes. It's fine."

Then, in an instant, his face turned icy cold.

"I'm just waiting for that day to come as soon as possible."

Iruki felt a chill as he watched Shirone walk back to the research group.

'Fermi, everything might have gone your way until now…'

Iruki closed the door behind him.

'But Shirone won't be easy.'

Ten hours had passed since the negotiations began.

The 12 participants looked exhausted, with dark circles forming under their eyes.

Hershey, who had meticulously recorded the decisions, moved on to the final item.

"Now, only the detailed adjustments remain. Any opinions?"

Neid spoke up as if he had been waiting.

"No one will be held responsible, even if there are fatalities."

Though his voice wasn't loud, the hostility towards the opposing team was at its peak.

"Alright. Same for us, so let's agree on that."

As Hershey recorded it, he recalled Fermi's instructions before coming to Istas.

"Armand? What's that?"

Fermi frowned.

"It's Shirone's object. If possible, it should be banned in this Scramble Royal. No, it's essential."

"Essential? Is it that powerful?"

"If Fermi says it's dangerous, it really is."

"If it's such a great weapon, the other side won't give it up easily. Can we negotiate that?"

Fermi smiled and pointed at Hershey.

"In that case, propose this."

Having finished his thoughts, Hershey spoke.

"This time, we propose that Shirone's Armand cannot be used in the Scramble Royal."

'They know about Armand.'

It confirmed Fermi's involvement.

"That's unacceptable."

Shirone shook his head firmly.

He didn't want to appear reliant on a weapon, but his desire to crush the enemies was stronger.

"This is an activity unrelated to school. I'll use Armand, and you can use weapons if you want."

"If you're stubborn, we have no choice but to use our trump card."

Neid snorted.

"What card do you have? Did Fermi tell you something? Nothing is worth exchanging for Armand."

"Really? If I heard from Fermi, you must have heard from Shirone."

"What do you mean?"

Hershey raised a finger.

"If Shirone doesn't use Armand, we won't use Fermi's chips."

Shirone bit his lip.

'Damn, I didn't think of that.'

"You must understand quickly. Fermi currently holds magic worth about 10 billion gold. For reference, the most expensive magic Fermi has used so far is a few million gold. If you use Armand, Fermi is prepared to give all his chips to the alliance team, even at his own expense."

Shirone responded strongly.

"Hmph! Even so…"

"Yes, I've heard it's a great weapon. But you can't lead the game alone. Meanwhile, all six of us will be strengthened."

'Hershey has a point. Strength is necessary, but teamwork is more important in this game.'

The rules inherently blocked the use of force if you lost a Kang.

Also, if he used Armand, he would have to fight an alliance team wielding magic worth 10 billion gold.

"Let's start coordinating our opinions. Everyone, come out."

Shirone led his team to their 11th secret meeting.

"As you heard, we need to revise our initial strategy. I didn't expect Fermi to be this interested in Istas…"

Neid stroked his chin.

"We initially thought the alliance team would ban Iruki's Cancellation, as it's a counter to Eden's defensive magic. But Armand…"

"If it's worth 10 billion gold, we'll be at a disadvantage. What's more efficient?"

Only Shirone, who knew Armand's functions perfectly, could judge.

"Let's accept it. Magic worth 10 billion gold is definitely not appealing. But not under these conditions."

Iruki clarified.

"So you think Armand is more efficient? If it's for our safety, don't worry."

"No. The balance seems right, as Hershey said. I mean we should push further. If Fermi saw Armand's abilities in the Under Coder…"

Shirone perceived an unknown probability.

"Even if they take a bit more loss, they'll choose to ban Armand. That's Fermi's nature."

Neid said, "So the maximum value is higher. Got it. How about banning Eden's full-body state?"

Sabina said, "But that's just a way to implement magic, like banning Dorothy's Hickory. If we argue the validity, we could be attacked too."

"There's something more important."

Amy spoke.

"As long as Iruki's Cancellation isn't banned, we can counter Eden's defensive magic. What's more helpful now is sealing Anchal's Magic Eye."

Sabina asked, "But Anchal didn't remove the blindfold during the graduation exam. Will she unseal it in the Scramble Royal?"

"It's not about probability. Even a 1% chance can cause confusion at a critical moment. It's better to seal it now. Especially since Anchal has many secrets."

Anchal came from the Jincheon Empire, a culture entirely different from where Shirone's group lived.

The Western and Central continents were dominated by Kashan, the Eastern continent by Gustav, and the Eastern lands by Jincheon. These three emperors ruled the world.

"Anchal's specialty is mental magic, specifically illusions. But unlike Arin, it's combined with Eastern techniques, making it unfamiliar."

While Arin penetrates the opponent's mind, Anchal creates near-real illusions to attack externally.

According to the scout during the graduation exam, her illusion blades could inflict mental damage equivalent to the fourth stage of the survival test, which Shirone passed.

"Anchal's illusion magic is closer to omnipotence than omniscience. If the Magic Eye is unsealed, the intensity won't be just the fourth stage."

Facing such illusions for seven days would be tough.

Considering how many students passed the fourth stage of the survival test, it was better to block it.

"Alright. We'll seal Anchal's Magic Eye."

With their opinions aligned, Shirone returned to the research group and proposed to Hershey.

"We'll accept. But we'll also ban Anchal's unsealing. If you accept that, I'll ban Armand."

For the first time, Anchal's expression changed.

'They planned to unseal it.'

As Iruki felt confident, Hershey turned to Anchal for her thoughts.

"What do you think?"

Anchal quickly adjusted her expression and nodded.

"Fine. I wasn't planning to unseal it anyway."

Anchal actually felt relieved.

'Scramble Royal isn't everything. Even if we win here, we'll have to fight the Golden Wheel again.'

At that moment, the bell signaling midnight rang.

"That concludes the negotiations. If there's anything else to add, speak now. I'll input it into the Judge."

Hershey asked, pulling out paper and pen.

"Nothing. Now, we just have to fight."

After 12 hours of intense negotiations, there was nothing left to say.

Despite some regrets, most were satisfied with the outcome of the negotiations.

As Hershey recorded the issues on the Judge's paper, a stack of 48 memos piled up.

"Let's begin."

Hershey inserted the papers into the Judge one by one, then pressed the lens that looked like a buzzer.

With a click, the lens flashed blue, and Ruru's voice was converted into an electric signal that instantly resonated in everyone's minds.

Scramble Royal commencement.

Waiting time: 10 hours, 58 minutes, 32 seconds.
