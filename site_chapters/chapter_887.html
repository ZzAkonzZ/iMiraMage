Chapter 887: Desperate Measures (1)

“Dance of the Reaper”

The moment Natasha's voice reached him, a blunt impact struck Rian's face.

The last confirmed location of her was 40 meters away from this point.

'At the same time as the sound...?'

She had reached him.

'No, maybe even faster.'

The landscape blurred left and right, and he couldn't even comprehend where or how he had been hit.

'I need to create distance.'

Rian kicked off the ground and retreated.

If Natasha was pursuing, she would be somewhere ahead regardless of her path.

"Yaaaa!"

With a massive swing of his great sword, a boulder as big as a house lifted into the air.

'Can she break through this?'

Rian pushed against the rock with his foot, exerting all his strength.

"Hah!"

As Natasha charged towards him, she noticed the giant rock flying at her like a cannonball.

'Hmm.'

Just as it approached, the vector reversed, and she stepped back, throwing a right jab.

Thud.

Before fragments from the rock could scatter from her fist-sized punch, a left jab followed.

Thud, thud.

Her upper body moved at a speed creating afterimages, rapidly swaying left and right.

Thud, thud, thud, thud, thud, thud.

Her arms and fists, moving from her shoulders, hit the rock at speeds invisible to the eye.

Thud, thud, thud, thud, thud, thud, thud, thud!

The expression 'devouring' was fitting, but the speed was so fast that the rock shattered instantly.

"Damn!"

By the time what could be called a thought occurred to Rian, Natasha was already in front of him.

The speed that shattered the rock reciprocated, and Rian's whole body felt a heavy impact.

Taking the delay of the consecutive hits, Natasha swung a massive punch, turning Rian's jaw.

"Ugh!"

Feeling his bones rattle in pain, Rian slid back, regaining his balance.

"Ouch..."

Natasha, pausing on one leg, turned to look at Rian and shook her fists.

"Pretty tough. And to think these fists break rocks."

Rian remained silent.

Without the regenerative capability of Idea, his muscles would have torn and his bones crushed.

'If it's just about moving fast, I can do that too.'

But thought also follows the speed of nerves, hence the scope of thought narrows.

'What kind of woman is this?'

Natasha had no such weaknesses.

The reason for removing her eyelids was because even blinking was an obstruction at her level of speed.

'So-called genius, then.'

Rian raised his chin and spoke.

"Bad luck. I haven't met anyone who fights with talent that hasn't fallen to me."

"I'm a woman, though?" Natasha pointed at Rian.

"Know what your ability is. It's just like Guy's, right? Even your swordsmanship is quite similar."

Ogent Guy.

Recalling his brother, Rian's chest tightened with sorrow, but he buried it and spoke.

"Don't be mistaken."

Rian pointed to himself.

"I am the origin."

Right, brother?

"Really?"

As if she genuinely didn't know, Natasha looked intrigued and cocked her head.

"Anyway, are we continuing? You're strong, but honestly, your fighting isn't that great."

By Natasha's standards, that was true.

"Don't worry. I've heard that plenty. You won't be disappointed this time."

Rian gripped his great sword tightly.

'The cavalry hasn't arrived yet.'

If something had happened at Tess's location, he needed to end the fight quickly.

'The ultimate strike.'

No matter how good her combat sense was, if she couldn't even react to the speed at which he would strike, that would be the end.

"Hoo."

As the aura of Maha emanated, the scenery around Rian vibrated chaotically.

"Ho."

Above the crouching Natasha, the incarnation of Death shone with ten rays of light.

"Here I go."

As he spoke, a streak of light burst forth centered around Rian.

The force of Disruption struck the ground, creating a massive quake that split the earth for hundreds of meters.

The powerful seismic wave startled the distant cavalry horses.

"Ho?"

Havitz turned around.

"Interesting fellow."

Idea's realm transcends physics, altering information itself, putting it outside the laws...

"But that doesn't mean you can ignore the laws."

Havitz was unconcerned.

"Just because you can tear a bit of the universe's veil doesn't mean you're as fast or strong as the universe."

As his muscles, torn, rapidly regenerated information, Rian surveyed the area.

Natasha, poised to turn, had a line of fissure extending endlessly beneath her feet.

Out of the ten rays connected to the Reaper’s skull, three were severed.

"Huh."

Regaining her composure, Natasha exhaled.

"Really fast."

Her compliment meant she was alive, and thus, Rian wasn't pleased.

'She dodged the Disruptive sword.'

Even the True Demon Faust couldn't evade such a strike.

'Maybe...'

Competitors on Rian's level were few in this world.

'This woman might be the strongest.' At least on this planet.

"You're Rian, right? The Knight of Maha." There was no hostility in Natasha's eyes, but then again, there hadn't been when she had killed countless humans either.

"Why kill humans?"

Rian asked.

"What's the reason for this war? Havitz might do it for fun, but I can't sense any belief in you."

"Hmm."

Natasha put her hand to her lips, pondering.

"No special reason. Honestly, it's not even fun. It's just something I'm good at? Havitz said I was good at it too."

She smiled broadly.

"What I really love is dancing. But I can't do that anymore."

"Why not? Your physical ability is top-notch."

"Not the best."

Natasha's eyes moved sideways.

"I'm just a puppet."

Silence followed.

"Can't you fight for humans?"

"Be on your side?"

"Sort of. You're human too, so it would make sense to fight the demons on behalf of humanity."

She seemed to have never really considered it before, and after a long pause, Natasha shook her head.

"I'm sorry. I don't want to betray Havitz. I knew him before you did. Honestly, I don't really understand the meaning either."

It was clear that something was off, but that made Rian even more frustrated.

'There seems to be a clue somewhere.'

He regretted not being more eloquent.

"Rian!"

From a distance, Tess's voice could be heard as a group of cavalry approached.

Havitz was riding behind Tess, but no one noticed.

Seeing that Natasha was still standing, Tess made a wide detour and reached Rian.

"Are you okay?"

She tried to dismount, but Havitz was holding her waist tightly, making it impossible.

Rian turned his head as he watched Tess's futile attempts to dismount.

Most of the cavalry had one arm severed, and some were missing ankles.

Even though Rian's brain recognized the sight of blood flowing as normal...

'What is this?'

He felt a bit nauseous.

'Am I missing something?'

The mere act of doubting was significant.

'I can't stand this anxiety.' The realm of Idea?

Or was it the fresh sensation of encountering an event from another space for the first time?

'It's probably just my imagination.'

But ultimately, humans cannot abandon their brains.

"Rian, what's wrong? Your face is pale."

When Havitz released his grip, Tess finally dismounted and approached Rian.

"It's nothing. But..."

Havitz beheaded the man behind Tess, cutting off Rian's words.

'What the hell!'

Havitz turned around with a smile.

"You'd better decide quickly. If I kill everyone, your girlfriend is next."

As he spoke, he stabbed another soldier in the back, the blade protruding from the chest.

Rian’s retinas clearly saw the blade, but his brain processed it as if it wasn't there.

"Let's go back. You're not in good shape right now."

Even as Tess confirmed Natasha's position and pulled Rian's arm, soldiers were dying.

"Tess..."

Rian hesitated.

'Should I say something? No, there's no way to explain it.'

They would think he was crazy.

It was like clapping and saying the sound was strange.

"Three left."

Havitz, killing the cavalry, gradually approached Tess, spinning his sword.

"Two."

Rian's shoulders trembled as he watched a soldier's head fall off.

'I'm missing something important.'

Sometimes in life, you get a bad feeling, but this was magnified a thousand times.

"The last one."

The cavalry was annihilated, and Havitz approached Tess with his sword extended.

"Tess."

"Yes?"

It didn't matter if they thought he was crazy.

"Get down!"

As Havitz raised his sword horizontally, Rian pushed Tess's shoulder down.

"Yaaah!"

And with all his might, he swung a blow that swept everything around him.

Boom!

A massive shockwave, imbued with the law of Maha, flew towards Havitz's neck.

In the 0.666 seconds of that event,

"Ooooooooh!"

A formation of twelve demons rose from the ground, surrounding Havitz.

All time except for 0.666 seconds was filled with attacks, and Natasha quickly moved.

The sound of the world splitting echoed in their eardrums, and Tess tightly closed her eyes.

"Ugh!"

As she waited for the pain to subside, she jumped up and shouted.

"What the hell was that?"

Rian kept his mouth shut.

His gaze was fixed on the ground two meters from where Tess stood.

'What is this?'

It looked like twelve black holes forming a circle, as if a fire had been lit there.

'They weren't there a moment ago.'

Maybe he hadn't seen them. This alone couldn't prove he wasn't crazy, but...

'No, something was definitely there.'

An abnormal phenomenon had existed here.

"Kiieeee!"

At that moment, a strange bird flew from the demon camp, making an eerie cry.

'Retreat signal.'

Natasha checked the position of the moon and said,

"Let's play again next time."

As she kicked off the ground, her body evaporated, and Havitz approached Rian.

"Interesting. They call it Idea, right? You're worth watching."

He's everywhere, but no one can feel him.

"Someday, I'll control all the links. Until then, keep digging into the secrets."

It was Satan's whisper.

As Havitz left the area and released his transformation technique, Tess's eyes widened.

"This... this can't be...!"

Seeing the bodies of the fallen soldiers, everything finally made sense.

"Havitz was here."

He had blocked her path, killed her subordinates, and even rode with her.

"How could I not know? No, I did know. I knew it! Rian!"

Rian swallowed hard.

"Havitz..."

The twelve holes where the demons had been looked like gateways to hell.
