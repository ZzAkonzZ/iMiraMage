Chapter 867: Groggy (2)

The man managing the nameless shelter was a mage named Betherus. He had revealed nothing but his name, but there was a military air about him as he directed the shelter.

‘Betherus.'

If he had been a famous mage, even Rian would have heard of him, but he had not. Even Shirone, who was more knowledgeable about the magic society, showed no reaction, but even if he had known, it would have been the same.

"Come on, hurry up with the tent work. It's going to rain tonight." The actual leader of the survivors was a woman named Marin, the second-in-command of the shelter.

She too was a soldier, but from her interactions with Betherus, it seemed they hadn’t known each other before.

'Deserter, maybe.'

The army of hell was truly formidable, and not just anyone could escape from it.

It was logical to think she had deserted her troop before the battle began.

"Hey, rookie! Come and help quickly! If you don’t want to sleep outside!"

Due to the aftermath of a typhoon that had hit the Gustav Empire, it often rained in the Central Continent.

Having already surpassed the military age, Rian decided to stay in the shelter until the rain stopped.

"Move aside, I'll do it."

There were only about 100 survivors, just enough to clear the bodies and gather food from the vicinity.

The shelter was still a mess, and above all, heavy building debris obstructed their efforts.

As Rian lifted a rock as big as a human torso, people's gazes shifted.

"Wow."

Rian, with the rock on his shoulder, casually threw it, and it flew several meters before crashing down.

Marin, sensing the power of Schema, asked him.

"You're strong. Why do you carry such a big sword? Who taught you?"

Rian had been influenced by many swordsmen, but he couldn't really call anyone his master.

"Well, if I had to say, from everything?"

Even after saying it, Rian chuckled, thinking it didn’t quite fit as a philosophy.

Marin seemed to think it was presumptuous as she slightly furrowed her brow and asked.

"What's your name?"

Rian looked back at Shirone.

Despite the disapproving looks from others, he appeared lost in thought.

'He'll need time.'

Leaving quietly was the best way to help Shirone.

"Just…"

As Rian cut himself off, Marin became more irritated.

"Forget it. Acting all high and mighty."

Since he was going to leave after the rain stopped anyway, she didn't press further.

"Hey! Kid!"

A man in his mid-40s, unable to hold back, scolded Shirone as he cleared debris.

"Don’t you see everyone working? Do you still think you’re a noble? Come and help!"

Shirone didn’t respond.

'Noble.'

Memories of rising from a woodsman’s son to one of the Great Stars of the Ivory Tower flashed through his mind.

'What am I even?'

A self-deprecating smile formed, and to the man, it felt like an insult.

"This son of a…"

Thud.

The ground shook as a giant rock fell.

The man turned in surprise to see Rian, who had just thrown a rock twice as big.

"Leave him be. If I do double the work of my friend, isn’t that enough?"

"Geez!"

The man clenched his fists, but seeing the size of the rock Rian threw, he swallowed his anger.

"Damn, annoying brat!"

Even after being cursed at, Rian continued working without complaints, which brought Marin over.

"What's wrong with your friend? Has the war driven him mad?"

It wasn’t far from the truth.

"Something like that."

The woman clicked her tongue.

"Lucky to have survived. How’s he going to live on being so frail?"

Rian was worried too.

'He's very tired.'

He had been beaten by the world.

Just because he had managed to stand until the end didn’t mean he wasn’t hurt.

Could Shirone ever love the world again?

"Pitiful."

From a distance, Betherus, who had been overseeing the work, quickly approached Shirone.

Rian spoke in a low tone.

"Hey."

But Betherus ignored him and conjured a flame in front of Shirone.

Simultaneously, the killing intent from Rian made Marin freeze beside him.

"Gasp."

A scream rose in her belly and choked in her throat, feeling like it was going to burst.

As Betherus cast a fireball not at Shirone but outside the shelter, Rian's killing intent disappeared as if it were a lie.

"Haah."

The sensation of the tension fading felt almost orgasmic, and a moan escaped naturally.

'What was that sensation just now?' It was a stimulus and sensation beyond what could be imagined based on experience.

Rian’s gaze was locked on Betherus.

"See? That's what magic is." Shirone didn’t even turn his head.

"What do you think I went through to gain this power? Every day I trained my mind, relentlessly."

Disgust was evident in Betheru’s eyes.

"You can't even imagine the pain. Still, I gritted my teeth and struggled. In this world, I tried to survive somehow."

For the first time, Shirone looked at Betherus.

"Do you know why I dislike you? Because you’re selfish. If I were you, no matter how hard it was, I would have stood up! I would have done something!"

"Perhaps. But…"

Shirone spoke weakly.

"You're not me."

Betherus’s temper flared as he grabbed Shirone by the collar and yanked him up.

"Get up! There’s nothing impossible! You’re doing nothing because you’re soaked in a defeatist mentality! Stop waiting for someone to help and clench your teeth and move!"

"I don’t want to do anything."

Shirone’s body seemed glued to the floor, and Betherus roughly threw down his collar.

"Damn! Do you think the world is easy? Is there anyone here who isn’t having a hard time? You should at least pretend to try."

Shirone pulled up his legs and sank into thought.

'I see.'

No matter how hard he tried, no matter the pain he endured, no one really recognized Shirone’s life.

If Betherus heard that he was one of the five stars of the ivory tower, what reaction would he show?

'Words! Words! Words! Sick of them.'

Shirone, too, being human, couldn’t help but resent the demons that slaughtered people.

But on the other hand, it seemed rather grandiose to define a whole based on one aspect of a person.

'If you’re not going to live it for them…'

Humans should perhaps remain a bit more silent about others.

"What a pathetic creature. I’m letting this go only for today. At dawn, get lost." Betherus was still not appeased, but Marin finally sensed the seriousness of the situation.

Rian’s momentary murderous intent was brief but enough to change her perspective, hardened from surviving in the military.

"Betherus, look at me for a sec."

"What is it?"

As he realized Marin’s face was troubled, sounds of flying objects approached from outside the shelter.

"Ha ha ha! Found them! Humans, humans!"

As the survivors looked up with pale faces, they saw red figures flying outside the unfinished tents.

"It's, it's demons."

It was unexpected for demons to come to a place barely inhabited by just over 100 humans.

"Damn it! Everyone, get to defensive positions…"

Before Betherus could issue an order, dozens of fireballs fell, devastating the surroundings.

"Aaaah! Save the people!"

In the ensuing chaos, only Shirone and Rian remained motionless.

"Don’t burn them! We need to eat!" As the demons with folded wings landed, the number they saw made Betherus tremble.

'Too many…'

There were at least thirty demons.

But to Shirone and Rian, who had faced enemies by the tens of thousands, this was virtually nothing.

'It's not an army. They’re deserters too.'

Without any emotional disturbance, the two faced the situation.

Demons over two meters tall with spear-like tails patted their stomachs and spoke in human language.

"Thought this place was empty but here are humans. Thought I was going to starve but this is great."

"Oh? There’s a woman there too! Ki ki ki!"

Betherus realized the timing of the fireball's explosion was terribly off.

'Damn it! Because of that guy…'

As he turned his fierce gaze, Shirone’s indifferent face seemed even more detestable.

"Lots of people here. Give them a choice."

As if enjoying a rare entertainment, the demons leisurely pointed at people.

"First, only half get eaten by us. Second, each person cuts off one arm and one leg to offer us."

Either choice would provide enough for the thirty demons.

"Give us time. Let us consult and…"

"What's there to consult about something so grand? You seem like the leader, just decide."

Betherus swallowed hard.

If he chose the first option, only half would be sacrificed, and he would definitely be among the survivors.

'Just endure a moment of shame.'

Marin's eyes conveyed the same message.

"We'll save half."

"Puha haha! Fine, that's fun too. Then quickly choose who will be eaten. We’re very hungry."

It was probably a difficult decision, but choosing the first victim was all too easy.

Betherus pointed at Shirone.

"That one."

"Wow, this guy is totally cold? Not even hesitating! Anyway, good. Hey, you won!"

Shirone didn’t move.

"Hey! Come over here! The leader sold you out!"

"Hehehe! Completely terrified. Alright, if you're trying to escape reality, I'll bring you back to it."

A demon with reptilian claws stomped towards Shirone.

"Hey, kid! Snap out of it."

The demon, with a mocking grin, shook Shirone's shoulder, but Shirone didn't even turn his head.

"Haha, completely out of it."

The demon bent over to look into Shirone's face and suddenly stopped speaking.

A long silence followed.

"What are you doing? Bring him over. We're hungry."

The demon's lips parted slightly.

"Uh..."

He seemed to be choosing his words, but nothing came to mind.

"Uh, uh..."

The demon, with a terrified expression, slowly backed away and fell on his backside.

"Ah... Ahhh..."

The survivors, including Betherus and Marin, who had served in the military, had never seen a demon so scared.

'What's going on?'

The demon's legs continued to mimic a retreating motion, even as he lay on the ground.

"Run, run away."

"Run? Why should we run..."

Before he could finish speaking, the demon in front of Shirone crawled on the ground and screamed.

"It's Yahweh!"

Shirone turned his head.

The moment they saw his face, the demons recalled the rain of light that had fallen on the Gustav Empire.

"Ahhh!"

With expressions drained of life, they all spread their wings simultaneously.

"Stop."

"Ahhh?!"

Their screams abruptly ceased, and the demons froze.

"You, come here for a moment."

In front of Yahweh, who had wiped out an entire empire, the thought of running didn't even occur to them.

As the thirty demons cautiously approached, Betherus's face went blank.

'Yahweh?'

The mythical figure he had heard about countless times in the military.

"The Infinite Mage?"
