Chapter 798:

As Shirone stepped into the waiting room, events he'd already experienced began unfolding in the exact same sequence.

“Sorry, it’s my fault. I was talking with Langi yesterday and your name came up…”

Veron had been right.

She’s repeating everything word for word.

Humans cannot change the future.

The cogs of law that operated this world meshed together with frightening precision.

A world where even the nuances of speech are dictated by law. I understand now why Uorin considered Havitz so dangerous.

If it were chaos, responses would vary each time.

So what now?

Langi approached.

“I figured I’d feel safer if you were here. So I told them everything.”

Nodding, Shirone walked toward Miss Yakma—Elfsur Laura.

“Hello?”

He wanted to gauge her reaction first.

Laura turned her head with an expressionless face.

Of course. She’s under hypnosis.

Just recalling the gruesome murder committed by the true terrorist made his skin crawl.

“Twenty minutes until the event begins,” announced an official.

And without any chance to react, a chilling killing intent radiated from Laura.

Damn!

Just a greeting was enough to trigger her Regulation Exception. The terrorist’s state of mind was volatile, hypersensitive.

I have to kill her!

Drawing on Yahweh’s realm, Shirone cast Wind Cutter—Laura’s head flew cleanly off in a fountain of blood.

“Eeeeeek!”

The contestants screamed, and Pony looked on in stunned disbelief.

“What the hell are you doing?!”

Shirone stared at Laura’s face.

“Gck!”

As the hypnosis broke, her expression turned to shock—and then she died, tongue lolled.

That was the real body. The one-armed monk’s doing.

Mortrasinger, already exposed to Time Vibration, had set a failsafe.

A warning: “Kill me, and the hostage dies.” And now my hands are tied.

He had to reverse time again.

Reading reactions won’t work. I need a different approach…

Just then, the door burst open and the security force stormed in.

“What the hell is this?!”

Jaestin’s eyes widened, and Marsha stared at Shirone in disbelief.

“…You did this?”

Shirone didn’t make excuses.

“Yes.”

“Why?”

Should he ask for Marsha’s help?

Not yet.

As with the 1-second loops that struck the terrorists, Time Vibration could shake another person’s time as well.

That’s resonance.

But this time, he had to go it alone.

If I resonate Marsha’s timeline…

They’d both be trapped in endless time loops until the desired result occurred.

She could withstand ten, maybe even a hundred loops.

But a thousand? Ten thousand?

Eventually, she’d go mad.

Only a magician of infinite capacity could endure a life that repeated by the hour, tens of thousands of times.

“I can’t explain right now.”

As the other women hurried to cover themselves, the guards restrained Shirone and dragged him away.

With twenty minutes still on the clock, the event was canceled.

“Talk! What the hell is going on?!”

Sitting in the empty arena, Shirone didn’t respond—he was counting the seconds in his head.

Five minutes, thirty-nine seconds… thirty-eight… thirty-seven…

They couldn’t actually arrest the Star of the Ivory Tower, but the guards continued to surround him.

“Let’s do this properly now.”

Realizing the time had come, Shirone lifted his head.

“What do you mean, properly?!”

That marked the end of plus-thirty minutes.

“See you again, sister.”

The moment he spoke, the timeline snapped—reversing toward minus-thirty minutes.

“Ugh! Damn it!”

His head reeled from the jolt, but by now, Shirone didn’t collapse anymore.

Entrance to the venue.

Thirty minutes before the attack.

The one-armed monk must be near the waiting room. The moment the terrorist dies, he’ll kill the hostage.

If even Yahweh’s realm couldn’t detect him, the monk’s power truly bordered on nothingness.

So if I don’t kill the terrorist, there’s a chance the monk won’t even receive the external information.

Shirone rushed for the waiting room.

If I can precisely slice the boundary between the terrorist and Miss Yakma… I can save the hostage.

With that resolution, Shirone burst through the door. The contestants once again popped paper fireworks.

“Surprise!”

Ignoring everything, Shirone ran in and cast Wind Cutter right beneath Laura’s neck.

“Gck!”

The terrorist died instantly, and Laura followed moments later.

Took 1.43 seconds to process.

Enough time.

“Eeeek!”

As screams rang out again, guards stormed in, and Shirone sat silently in the empty audience seats.

“Talk! What the hell is going on?!”

Looking up at Marsha, Shirone calmly replied:

“See you again, sister.”

Loop 3.

“Surprise!”

The terrorist died again.

No one should die.

Shirone had to slice the boundary within a 0.3 cm margin of error. That sliver was his target.

Loop 17.

“Surprise!”

Blood gushed from the terrorist’s neck like a fountain.

Loop 68.

“Surpr—”

The now-familiar surprise greeted Shirone as he activated Wind Cutter once again.

Missed.

This time, Miss Yakma’s neck was severed.

The boundary lies between them.

Invisible to the naked eye, but on a microscopic level, there had to be a gap wider than atoms.

Marsha always said the same thing.

“Talk! What the hell is going on?!”

“…”

Time reversed again, silently.

Loop 287.

After slicing through human necks for nearly 287 hours, Shirone’s mind was starting to fog.

I’ve found the 0.6 cm boundary, but cutting it precisely is too difficult.

Just like 0.1 cm contains a thousand 0.0001s, unless he could measure perfectly, the attempts would continue.

“Twenty minutes until the event.”

As the terrorist’s intent leaked again and the women began changing and laughing, Shirone felt nothing.

“Hohoho! Not much to see, huh? Still, now that you’ve had a good look, you’ll stay and guard us, right?”

He’d seen this scene dozens of times—it no longer stirred anything in him.

“Miss Laura.”

Shirone reached out gently—and the killing intent bloomed again.

Wind Cutter.

The body and face separated with a dull snap, but visually, nothing seemed amiss.

“Found it.”

Exactly 0.3736 cm.

The hostage isn’t dead.

Hyega didn’t react, and Mortrasinger’s body flailed in panic.

This time, you can’t stop me.

As the terrorist moved to destroy Laura’s face, Shirone’s Wind Cutter slashed through the torso.

From the empty stands, Marsha cried out.

“Talk! What the hell is going on?!”

“Sister.”

For the first time, Shirone synchronized her timeline with his own.

“Come with me.”

Loop 288.

The moment the door opened, Shirone dashed in and cleanly separated Laura and the terrorist at the neck.

Now that I know the boundary…

He could shift the Hidden Piece.

“Now!”

Mortrasinger’s face returned beneath her cloth veil, and Hyega appeared in the corridor.

How?!

Shock overwhelmed Hyega’s expression.

The hostage!

Realizing it too late, he raised his hand for a killing strike—but Marsha was faster. She snatched Laura away.

“Not sure what’s going on here…”

Though Shirone’s explanation made little sense, the fact that he’d repeated an hour was clear enough.

“Hide-and-seek is over!”

Sliding past Hyega, Marsha twisted her body and drove the Blade of Blasphemy into his back.

“Ghhhk!”

He felt no pain.

“Regulation Exception?”

From Hyega’s back, a mist of desire leaked out—at a rate of 1% per minute.

“Don’t worry. We’ve got 100 minutes to fight.”

The clarity of positions—normally a weakness of the Blade of Blasphemy—was now a strength.

“Marsha! What’s going on?!”

Freeman, who had followed them belatedly, rushed to Marsha’s side.

He really doesn’t know.

Marsha had experienced it once. But Freeman, surprised by the sudden events, was clearly lost.

You’ve grown closer to godhood, Shirone.

If he could go back in time, Shirone wished he’d just explained everything during the first loop.

“All I ever wanted was to punch this guy square in the face.”

Watching Hyega’s movements, Marsha spoke.

“There’s one more outside. Take the hostage and stop him. No one is allowed to die.”

Getting trapped in an endless time loop was something she’d rather avoid.

“You said 100 minutes, correct?”

As Hyega raised his hand in a martial stance, the air around his palm crystallized into a diamond-like sheen, sparkling like a legendary blade.

“Go.”

The Gunner moved like the wind.

“You won’t last even 100 seconds.”

As Hyega charged, Marsha touched the tattoo engraved on her body.

“Blade of Blasphemy.”

A dagger materialized in her hand.

“How dare you interfere with my mission!”

Blasted by Shirone’s Photon Cannon, Mortrasinger crashed through a wall and was flung into the sky.

How the hell did he figure it out?

Even if leaking killing intent was unavoidable, it should’ve been impossible to precisely locate the boundary of the neck.

“Hidden Piece!”

She activated her Regulation Exception to suppress him—but Shirone, having fought her dozens of times, was no longer fazed.

“Would you just cut it out already?!”

Shirone bound her in Shining Chains, then sent them both plummeting vertically toward a distant mountain peak.

“Kyaaaaa!”

But this wasn’t enough to kill an immortal.

“What the hell is your problem?!”

He continued smashing her left and right, slamming her into the ground until she was completely limp, only then did he stop.

“Ghhhhh…”

She couldn’t move a single finger, but Shirone didn’t release the spell.

“It’s over. Once your face is exposed, your Regulation Exception will vanish, right?”

Mortrasinger trembled all over.

“Please… just kill me! You don’t want to see this ugly face either, do you?!”

“Shut up! Do you even realize what you’ve done?!”

If he hadn’t reversed time, she would’ve slaughtered everyone at the pageant.

“What do you know about me?! I couldn’t care less if all the pretty ones die! No, I’ll kill them all!”

Shirone’s heart ached.

“No matter how much you hate yourself, if you don’t love who you are…”

He tore off the cloth covering Mortrasinger’s face, and she let out a scream.

“Nooooo!”

“What…?”

The woman’s face, eyes clenched tightly shut, was unblemished and smooth as a pearl.

“Please… don’t look. Let me cover it again.”

Shirone didn’t believe beauty could be measured by numbers.

But one thing was certain—if you asked a hundred people, all of them would call the tearful Mortrasinger beautiful.

“You’re… beautiful.”

She could’ve competed in the pageant herself and no one would question it.

“Liar! What’s beautiful about this?! Where is there someone uglier than me?!”

He started to get annoyed.

“I’m telling you, you’re really beautiful!”

But Mortrasinger wouldn’t listen.

“Why are my eyes shaped like this? Why isn’t my nose sharper? Look at these lips—so faint and colorless…”

“Ah…”

Shirone finally understood.

Her Regulation Exception.

Mortrasinger was beautiful—but her mind was so twisted, even she couldn’t see it.

“Dirty, ugly, disgusting face!”

She was the embodiment of a complex.
