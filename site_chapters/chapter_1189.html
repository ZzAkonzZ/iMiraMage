Chapter 1189:

Son Yoo-jung and Mortasinger sprinted across the warehouse grounds of the System Control Branch.

“Move!”

Countless Black Chains pursued them from above, hurling their dark chains toward the ground.

“Yoo-jung! Above you!”

Even before Mortasinger could finish speaking, Son Yoo-jung swiftly swung her two Yeoui staffs.

Sparks scattered as her clubs struck, but several chains coiled tightly around her arms and legs.

“Kh…!”

She felt her strength being drained, dark smoke seeping from her mouth.

Nimbus Cloud.

Twisting her entire body, she yanked on the chains with both arms, dragging the Black Chains down in a single powerful pull.

“Urgh! H-how…?”

Her Fiery Eyes of Golden Perception flared open, and her crimson club hammered against the Black Chains’ weak points.

“You think a stone monkey is easy prey?”

The Black Chains scattered into smoke, and Son Yoo-jung exhaled heavily, letting her shoulders drop.

“Hah… Hah…”

Mortasinger approached.

“Are you alright?”

“Yeah… No. These things are seriously tough. Honestly, they’re the type I hate the most. It doesn’t even feel like I’m hitting them.”

There was no satisfying impact.

“By the way, did you lose your abilities too? That Hidden Piece thing—whatever it was.”

Before joining the Satanic Order, Mortasinger had been a Rule Eater, someone who could shatter space like a puzzle.

“I’m not sure yet. I broke free from Satan’s brainwashing, but I don’t know what’s real anymore.”

“Well, take your time figuring it out. First, let’s go. We need to find a way out of Hell.”

But when they reached the main gate, Son Yoo-jung hesitated, unable to take the final step forward.

“What’s wrong? Aren’t you leaving?”

Son Yoo-jung ran her fingers over Gingoa.

He’s not reciting the Gingoju. Is he really not going to summon it? Is he underestimating my abilities? Or…

Richella’s voice rang out urgently.

“What are you thinking about? Just go! If we run far enough, they’ll never find us!”

“Yoo-jung.”

Mortasinger stepped closer.

“After falling for Satan’s temptation, I went through countless things in Hell. And in the end, I became like this.”

Yet, now that she had broken free from her brainwashing, she realized something.

“Even so, I don’t deny it. Maybe because, at one point, I truly loved this form. Of course, saying I have no regrets would be a lie, but I’ve learned the power of the heart. Don’t lie to yourself. You don’t have to run away because of me.”

“Mortaa…”

It was the affectionate nickname she used for Mortasinger.

“I’m not saying we should go back. And I’m not saying we should escape, either. Just do what you want to.”

Rian leapt down.

“Ugh.”

Resting his sword on his shoulder, he strode forward, causing the demons to instinctively retreat.

The Chains of Gehenna.

Anything that so much as brushed against them would be incinerated.

“Are you alright, Shirone? I heard something weird on my way here. What exactly is going on?”

Shirone spoke.

“We’ve got a chance.”

“I see. Then what do we do now?”

“Jin Seong Eum.”

The explanation was short, but for Rian, the only thing that mattered was where Shirone’s sights were set.

“Alright. Let’s go, Shirone.”

Lethe fired a blast of purification flames.

“Don’t let them escape!”

Between the dozens of fireballs, demons rushed in, targeting Shirone.

Rian twisted his body and swung his sword, snuffing out the purification flames in an instant.

“Urgh…”

The demons split in two, their bodies bursting into pure magic, which was absorbed into the blade.

“Grrr…!”

After purging the magic in an instant, Rian widened his eyes, scanning the area ahead.

Lethe shouted.

“No!”

As Rian plunged his sword into the ground, bubbles began to rise from the floor of the assembly hall.

“Grrr…!” Rian’s face twisted, and an explosion erupted from the 11th to the 13th floor.

Hearing the deafening roar, the Black Chains guarding the warehouse grounds turned toward the sanctuary.

Arrest the sinners.

Leaping down from the sanctuary, Shirone and Rian saw the sky shrouded in black clouds.

They were all Black Chains.

“Shirone, this is bad.”

With agents and demons pursuing them from the sanctuary, they had no time to waste.

“Run!”

As they sprinted, Rian asked,

“You said we need to retrieve Jin Seong Eum. If we purify her with Gehenna’s flames, can she really return to reality?”

“There’s a good chance.”

Shirone adjusted his direction, keeping an eye on where the Black Chains were moving.

He continued speaking.

“If we destroy the central processing unit, it will be impossible to calculate purification time.”

“But that doesn’t mean karma disappears, does it?”

“If the system collapses, anything can happen. Just like how the numbers in a bank account aren’t real money. If the banking system is destroyed, the money disappears.”

“… I see.”

As they spoke, the Black Chains had already closed in, now only a few hundred meters away.

There was no escaping the storm clouds.

“Tch!”

The Black Chains halted, keeping their distance, while agents formed a tight wall behind them.

Do we have no choice but to fight?

Seeing Shirone and Rian stop, Lethe smirked.

“The Black Chains are a program. They can’t be purified by Gehenna.”

She extended her hand.

“Capture them.”

Rian gritted his teeth. He had already experienced the power of the Black Chains at the Wailing Wall.

Foolish sinner.

As the Black Chains gathered like storm clouds and lunged toward Rian, a hole suddenly burst through their formation.

“Kraaaah!”

The outer edge of the opening burned bright red.

Engulfed in Nimbus Cloud, Son Yoo-jung stood at its center.

“The weak ones always stick together. Makes me want to smash them.”

Through the opening, Mortasinger arrived, and Son Yoo-jung strode forward.

“Yahweh, you.”

Her Yeoui pointed at Shirone.

“Why didn’t you recite the Gingoju? Are you ignoring me? Do you not need my help?”

“I don’t.”

Son Yoo-jung’s eyebrow twitched.

“There’s someone else… someone who needs your help more than I do. Fight for that person.”

The monkey’s long fangs bared.

“Shut up. You and Buddha are all the same, spouting pretty words. But when the time comes, you’ll call me, won’t you? No matter where I am, no matter what I’m doing, you’ll chant the Gingoju and—”

“Why did you come?”

Shirone wasn’t one to be deceived by meaningless words meant to hide true feelings.

“If you’ve already made up your mind, why did you come to me asking such things?”

“You…!”

At that moment, the Black Chains launched their chains.

Rule Eater!

Mortasinger activated her ability, splitting the entire landscape into 98 sections.

“It worked!”

The fact that she had regained her magic was proof that she had completely freed herself from Satan’s brainwashing.

Like rearranging a cube, she shifted the positions of the Black Chains, causing their own attacks to scatter in different directions.

Mortasinger turned back to Shirone and shouted.

“We’ll handle this! Hurry!”

A realization.

It was a conviction born from having experienced both extremes.

As Rian fought off the agents, Shirone turned his gaze to Son Yoo-jung.

“Well done. You saved Mortasinger.”

“… It wasn’t me.”

“Yes. But intelligence always finds its way back to the truth. And you played the biggest role in that.”

“What do you expect me to do?”

Son Yoo-jung lowered her Yeoui and shouted.

“I’m not Mortasinger! I don’t know math, or magic, or science, or anything!”

Shirone smiled bitterly.

“Neither do I.”

“They say knowledge is understanding the world, but no matter how much I learned, where I arrived in the end… was ignorance. Because this world began from nothing.”

Shirone took a step forward.

“Wisdom is knowing that we cannot know everything. It’s not being trapped in stubbornness, listening to many voices, and being able to change oneself.”

Just as Mortasinger had.

“You are an unrefined gem. That’s why Nane sent you here. Open your heart. Surrender yourself to all possibilities. And if, after that, you are still able to choose—then that is true righteousness.”

“True righteousness? Opening my heart? Is it really that easy? If I were to consider all possibilities, does that mean even killing people and throwing the world into chaos could be righteous?”

“Yes.”

Shirone’s voice carried certainty.

“If you truly opened your heart, if you truly entrusted yourself to every possibility…”

He pressed his index finger against Gingoa.

“Then you would never make that choice.”

With a sharp crack, Gingoa shattered, but Son Yoo-jung was already lost in thought.

Never… choose?

What is righteousness?

Throughout human history, countless people had spoken endlessly about righteousness.

Righteousness is…

If one sat quietly in an empty room, closed their eyes, and looked within—

“It is simply what is right.”

We already know it.

But worn down by the cruelty of reality, exhausted by human brutality, we deliberately turn away.

“Son Yoo-jung.”

Shirone spoke.

“You saved a person from Hell. Now, there is no reason for you to be bound anymore.”

Son Yoo-jung ran her fingers over the place where Gingoa had been, then lifted her gaze to Shirone.

Yahweh and Buddha.

At what point did these two, so alike, diverge?

A fire lit in Son Yoo-jung’s eyes.

“Kiiiii!”

She shot past Shirone like a bolt of lightning, striking an agent with her Yeoui.

“Gah!”

As if sound had been erased, the noise of battle suddenly surged back in all at once.

“Go.”

Son Yoo-jung spoke with her back turned.

“Whatever you’re planning, you won’t get far with these guys on your tail. I’ll handle them. You get out of here.”

“Thanks. Rian, let’s go!”

As Shirone leaped away, Lethe screamed.

“Catch Yahweh!”

Son Yoo-jung shouted.

“Mortasinger!”

As if waiting for the cue, Mortasinger used Rule Eater to shift both of them far away.

“Damn it! What now?!”

By the time the puzzle-like space returned to normal, Yahweh had already left the facility.

“After them!”

An agent barely got ten centimeters off the ground before his face was caved in.

The lingering afterimage of Yeoui filled the space, followed by the sound of flesh being pounded.

In an instant, dozens of strikes landed, and the agent’s body twisted unnaturally in a jagged shape.

“Ghhk…”

As he collapsed with a heavy thud, Son Yoo-jung exhaled steam and turned to Lethe.

“You think you’re going somewhere? I was just getting warmed up.”

Lethe smirked coldly.

“Hmph. There’s only one place you’ll end up anyway. I’ll tear you apart limb by limb before going after them.”

“You think you can? You know, don’t you? A stone monkey doesn’t break, even if a mountain crushes it.”

Son Yoo-jung connected her two Yeoui before her, and her body flared with golden light.

Shatter Evil and Reveal Righteousness.

Shattering evil and revealing righteousness—the true justice she sought.

I’m sorry. I will never waver again.

As the severed ends of the Yeoui disappeared, fusing into a single club, Lethe furrowed her brow.

Great Sage, Equal to Heaven.

A long time ago, someone had wielded this same energy…

… unleashing it upon Hell like a storm.

Son Yoo-jung’s face twisted like that of a demon.

“Kiiiii!”

As fragments of light scattered, holes burst open across Lethe’s torso.

That insane monkey bastard!

Lethe retreated at an incredible speed, but a streak of light pierced through her left eye.
