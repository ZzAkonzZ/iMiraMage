Chapter 640:

Nade couldn't believe his eyes.

There was no reason for Liz, who should be at the capital's armory, to be here.

Of course, that was just Nade's emotional reaction. Shirone, on the other hand, assessed the situation calmly.

"Uh, should we have another drink?"

"Wait a minute. We need to talk about how much we struggled back then…!"

As Iruki, with bleary eyes, pointed accusingly, Shirone pushed him inside, shouting,

"Then go talk about it!"

Bang! The door closed, and Nade finally accepted the reality.

"Why are you here, Liz?"

There was only one reason she could be here, but she couldn't bring herself to say it.

"Well… you see…"

As expected, Nade didn't welcome her with open arms. Flustered, she blurted out,

"I left so abruptly back then. I came to congratulate you. Congratulations on passing the graduation exam."

She forced a smile, but her expression and gestures were awkward.

"Thank you."

Though the thought of a new future crossed his mind, his wounded heart wasn't ready to open.

Standing before Nade's closed door, Liz realized she couldn't do anything more.

How long had it been since she parted ways with Oscar? How could she knock on Nade's door now?

"Well… have a good time."

As Liz climbed the stairs, Nade grabbed her wrist.

"How can you just leave like that?"

Feeling tears welling up, Liz shook her wrist.

"Sorry. I shouldn't have come. I'll go."

"Oh, come on!"

As Nade refused to let go, Liz's heel caught on the stairs, causing her to fall backward.

"Ah!"

Nade, pushed back, hit the basement wall, and Liz quickly turned to check on him.

"Are you okay? Are you hurt?"

Nade didn't answer, staring into Liz's tear-filled eyes for a long time.

"Can't you stay?"

Liz, unable to hold back her tears, buried her face in Nade's chest.

"I'm such a bad person, aren't I?"

"No, you're not. Oscar betrayed you first."

"But… no matter what, this is strange."

"It is."

Nade gently pushed Liz away, holding her shoulders.

"So let's be strange just this once. I won't make you feel strange again."

As a wave of relief washed over her, Liz burst into tears.

"Sorry! I'm sorry, Nade."

Sabina, watching from the first floor, turned away sadly.

"Let's go. We need to start training again tomorrow."

"Yeah. Let's fight like crazy."

Following Sabina, Closer placed a firm hand on her shoulder.

The graduation party continued past midnight, finally winding down around 4 a.m.

Most had passed out at the tables, while Shirone and his friends left the party, pleasantly drunk.

Holding Liz's hand, Nade looked back at his friends.

"I'll take Liz back. You guys go ahead."

Everyone knew he wouldn't return tonight, but no one said it out loud.

With the graduation exam over, they could do whatever they wanted.

"Alright. See you tomorrow."

As Nade left with Liz, Seriel, realizing something, patted Amy's back.

"I'll head back to my lodging."

Seriel had booked a room in the city.

"At this hour? Stay in my room."

'Always so loyal…'

Though it was endearing, as a friend, she should give them space tonight.

"It's fine. I need to leave early tomorrow. We'll see each other at the graduation ceremony anyway."

"But it's dangerous to go alone…"

Though it didn't make sense for a certified mage to be in danger at night, emotionally, it was different.

"I'll take her."

Sobered up, Iruki offered, and Seriel agreed.

"That works. I feel safe with Iruki."

"…What do you mean?"

"Alright, alright! It's late, let's go."

As Seriel pushed Iruki away, she suddenly turned back and winked.

"Amy, congratulations."

Knowing the meaning of the wink, Amy blushed, but it was too late to argue.

With their friends gone, leaving them alone, the atmosphere grew awkward.

"Shall we walk a bit? To sober up."

As expected, Shirone didn't suggest going in right away, and Amy nodded nervously.

"Sure."

Lost in their thoughts, they walked through the garden and sat on a bench under a crystal lamp.

"Sigh, this is the last time we'll walk here."

"Yeah. We worked hard for today, but honestly, I never thought this day would come."

Amy turned to Shirone and asked,

"What will you do?"

"Huh? About what?"

"I think you've gone too far. Scouts from all over the world will make offers. Will you leave the Kingdom of Tormia?"

Though the basis for his choice would be future prospects, what Amy really wanted to know was their future together.

Knowing it wasn't an easy question to answer, Amy started the conversation.

"I think I'll stay in Tormia. I'll prepare for the certification for a year and apply to the magic unit."

The Karmis family was the top noble family in Tormia, making it difficult to accept offers from other countries.

"But Shirone, I want you to do what you want. Even if you go to another country, we…"

"I won't go."

"Huh?"

Scouts might not know the inner thoughts of the participants, but Shirone had to be honest with Amy.

"If you're staying in the kingdom, I'll stay too."

"You, you fool. It's not something you can decide so easily…"

"I like you, Amy."

Amy's heart began to race wildly.

"I'm going to join the Tormia Kingdom Magic Association. And with you…"

Thinking it was too soon to talk about a distant future, Shirone smiled and asked,

"What do you think? If I stay in the Tormia Kingdom to be with you."

"Idiot! Of course, that's…"

With so many possibilities ahead, could he really limit his future for love?

But tonight, her heart overruled her mind.

"That sounds good. I like you too, Shirone."

As she accepted, a thrilling joy surged through him.

"Amy…"

Unable to bear looking at the slowly approaching Shirone, Amy closed her eyes tightly.

Their emotions were as delicate as walking on thin ice, but the feeling of their lips touching was so sudden.

Silence. Again, silence.

The night that seemed like it would never end was passing.

The data from the graduation exams held simultaneously at Red Line-affiliated magic schools was rapidly spreading to magic associations worldwide.

The Red Line's infrastructure, based on national networks, was so extensive that it practically encircled the planet. On graduation exam days, even the Black Line and White Line were on high alert.

Some students would choose the Black Line, but entering the White Line wasn't a matter of choice.

It was the sanctuary of the world's greatest mages, and mere genius wasn't enough to catch the attention of the rulers of the magical world.

North of the border between the Kashan Empire and the Gustav Empire, there was an icy region accessible only by crossing the frozen sea.

People called it the Arctic, and in this cold, intellectual place stood the Ivory Tower, known as the pinnacle of magic.

No matter where you started from, it felt like the farthest place on the planet, but it took only half a day for the Red Line's graduation exam data to arrive.

"Hmm, interesting."

An old man walked down the vast hallway, so wide that you could only tell it was enclosed because you could see the distant walls.

Araka, the chief administrator of the Ivory Tower.

At 117 years old, he had become a resident of the Ivory Tower at 16 and had never left since.

The Ivory Tower was like a city, with many people working there, but only a few could leave.

Within the tower, these people were called stars, and they were given the title "Star" before their full name. Exceptionally, some worked on a contractual basis with special agreements.

A notable example was Armin of the Light, but considering his unique magic, such cases were almost nonexistent in history.

Thus, the title "Star" was incomparable to any worldly power, signifying the trust of the entire Ivory Tower's residents and serving as a shackle to keep them within the orbit of intellect.

Currently, there were 27 people with the title of Star in the Ivory Tower, and with their assistants, called satellites, they traveled the world solving superhuman challenges.

The kingdom and even the empire were wary of the Ivory Tower's influence because all its residents were cutting-edge mages.

Today, Araka held a list of candidates for the Ivory Tower's new stars.

"Great Star, I have something to report."

As Araka spoke upon reaching the elevator, light enveloped him, transporting him to the sky.

The Great Star resided at the top of the tower, an artificial satellite rotating with the planet outside the atmosphere.

Through the window, space stretched out, and looking down, the planet's continents spread like a map.

A first-time visitor would grasp the scale's significance just by being there, but Araka didn't even glance around as he walked straight ahead.

At the entrance of the Great Hall of Wisdom, a man who looked like he was all bones sat cross-legged, floating in the air.

"Long time no see, Araka. It's been 1,273 days. Is it time for a new star to rise?"

One of the five brightest stars in the Ivory Tower, the Enlightened One, Mahagaruta.

"Great Star, it's been a while since we had star candidates. Is the Great Star inside?"

"I wouldn't know. But if the sky has allowed it, why not go in?"

Light burst from Mahagaruta's wide-open eyes, enveloping the world and instantly changing the scenery.

The Great Hall of Wisdom.

Surrounded by a hemispherical glass dome, looking at space from here made one forget that the Ivory Tower was rooted in humanity.

"What brings you here, Araka?"

Though the Great Star was unseen, Araka prostrated towards her voice.

"I have received the data of graduates from magic schools worldwide. I have selected ten candidates worthy of the title of Star. Please review them."

"These are still unripe. What is there to review? Just bestow the title on the most outstanding one. Did you come here for such a trivial matter?"

The Ivory Tower only selected the world's top prospects, and true to its stringent standards, it considered nothing but potential.

"Great Star, this time is different."

"Oh?"

Araka raised his head and spoke.

"For the first time in the human world, a graduate has surpassed 90%."

As if a breeze blew, air gathered, and a woman in snow-white clothes appeared.

"90%."

Her voice, even in speech, was pure.

Araka walked on his knees and presented the list.

"Please review and evaluate it yourself."

It was a list of the ten most outstanding prospects in the current world.

Dalia Nane (17 years old): 89.4% Special Note: None

Wena Wizard (4 years old): 79.1% Special Note: Innate Awakening

Galmun Claude (25 years old): 78.8% Special Note: Irregular Eater
