Chapter 804: Law Execution (5)

"Where is it?"

As the steel wire tied to the skeleton's fingers swayed, Natasha's speed increased even further.

"There, the right corridor."

Smodo, who was on Natasha's arm, used his meticulous perception to track Crouch's movements.

"He's gone far. What's there?"

"Nothing. He's trying to get as far away as possible. Maybe it's dangerous."

The fact that they were moving away from Havitz’s apartment made it clear that they were unaware of certain intentions.

"Turn left here."

Natasha sharply turned her body and sprinted down the long corridor.

"It's probably a trap."

"Maybe. But it's not a trap meant for us. We have no choice but to check."

The place they arrived at was the dungeon of Marzak Palace, specifically at a dead end on the lowest floor.

"You're here."

Crouch greeted them, holding his severed head in one hand.

"How are you alive with your head cut off?"

Natasha remarked with amazement, but Smodo realized the gravity of the situation.

"What are you plotting?"

The severed head suddenly opened its eyes.

"Nothing. Just looking for a place to die."

"Really?"

Smodo said.

"Catch him. Do not kill him."

Natasha's body vanished as if evaporating and suddenly reappeared, snatching Crouch's face.

Whoosh!

The mere action caused a strong wind to sweep through the dungeon.

"There's no chance in a direct confrontation."

Knowing Natasha's capabilities, Crouch willingly handed over his face.

"Fascinating. But how do you kill this?"

As Natasha lifted the face to eye level, the corners of Crouch's mouth curled up.

"I didn't mention this before."

Simultaneously, the headless body charged and stabbed Natasha in the abdomen with a dagger.

"I needed a companion for the journey to the underworld." The uniqueness of the dagger that Crouch held strongly pressured Smodo’s meticulous perception.

'Is that an object?' He suddenly realized.

"Damn! It's 'Law-Killer'!"

In a room filled with perverse tools, the rough breaths of a man and a woman heated the air.

Even as they shared intense kisses, Alpha-Fish, Abella, could not hate Havitz.

'It won't work. If this continues, the mission...' For her, assassinating Havitz was not a desire but a chore.

She undertook the mission because an innocent person had died in her place, driven by the will to return what was received.

"Uncle, let's start."

She pushed Havitz’s chest, and he fell back onto the blood-red bed.

Slowly climbing over Havitz, Abella pulled the chains from the iron bed's head and locked them around his left wrist.

With a smirk, Havitz chuckled, and Abella kissed him tenderly, tormenting him.

'Now the right hand.'

The chains rattled.

"Argh!"

Crouch thrilled at the solid sensation felt through the handle of the 'Law-Killer'.

'It's like stabbing a rock.'

Despite aiming for the heart, her muscles gripped the blade as if they were made of stone.

"Doesn't die because it's a doll?" Natasha, apparently unconcerned about the blade in her abdomen, drew Crouch's face even closer.

"No, if that's all, I could just smash the doll. You have multiple lives, right?"

Crouch was stabbing with all his might, but the dagger was still pushed out more than half.

"In the end, you're weak. If you were strong, you would have used the same method as me."

Although Natasha was also a doll, she was confident she wouldn't be destroyed.

"How many lives do you have? Infinite? Will you come back to life even if you die this time? Shall we test it?"

While posing the question, Natasha peered into Crouch's eyes and smiled as if she realized something.

"Is this your last?"

Smodo said.

"Natasha, don't act rashly."

'Law-Killer' is considered the worst weapon in humanity.

'Legal murder. This isn't something to be resolved here.'

Seeing the dagger with his own eyes for the first time, he was aware of its dreadful history and knew he had to approach it with caution.

"Answer me honestly. What are you plotting? What's the intention behind 'Law-Killer'?"

As Crouch remained silent, Natasha's arms swelled.

"Argh!"

The head held between her hands crunched like a balloon being squeezed.

"The facial bones are steel-like, but such monstrous strength."

Under the dominion of the god of death, Natasha's physical limits meant nothing.

"Smodo, what do we do? It seems he can't feel pain. Then we can't torture him."

"No. This is enough."

Smodo made a decision.

"Just leave it as it is. 'Law-Killer' changes the law. As long as it's embedded in your chest, nothing will happen."

"Ah, is that so?"

Despair filled Crouch's eyes.

"Just hold on here. I'll speak to Balkan. If something happens to Abella..."

Ding. Ding. Ding.

As Smodo turned around, the sound of the bell signaling midnight rang from beyond the wall.

"Kukukuku."

Crouch's mouth eerily split into a grin.

"I've won."

Smodo shouted.

"Natasha! Kill him!"

As soon as the words fell, Natasha's fingers stood up, and like squeezing juice, Crouch's head burst.

'Good muscles.'

At the end of his life, Crouch twisted the handle of the dagger with all his might.

'Perfectly good muscles.'

The muscles were so solid they could repel an arrow, so the dagger snapped with a clang.

'It's not a person who kills.'

Everything followed Crouch's script.

'The situation kills.'

As Crouch's body collapsed, Natasha finally glanced at her heart.

"Smodo, the dagger..."

The blade that was embedded faded like an illusion and vanished into the air.

"What? What kind of law is this?"

Although 'Law-Killer's effects were well-known, only Minerva knew its detailed functions.

Having shouted at a dead man changed nothing, so Smodo turned to leave the dungeon.

"We need to tell Balkan!"

Natasha, swift as a swallow, caught him and they raced off.

"Is everything ready?"

Havitz’s voice, playful and excited, pained Abella.

Ding. Ding. Ding.

As the bell tolled midnight.

'It starts.'

As if she had been under a weak hypnosis, the moment she heard the bell, a murderous intent boiled within her.

"Yes. Everything is ready."

Abella, having locked Havitz’s limbs, straddled his waist and raised her torso.

"Hehe, a woman rides the emperor. So... what now?"

"Just wait and you'll see."

Abella's voice was cold, but Havitz took even that as a game and nodded.

'The bell has rung.'

That meant, in any case, Crouch had disappeared.

'Law-Killer'...

By stabbing Natasha, the target change had reset the user's murderous intent.

'It must have broken.'

The 'Law-Killer' that Crouch risked his life to destroy would find the closest user who harbored hatred...

"Uncle."

...and fall into Abella's grasp.

"I'm sorry."

Holding 'Law-Killer' inversely, Abella looked down at Havitz with cold eyes, the dagger poised vertically as if to stab him at any moment.

"This isn't the play I thought of." The clear intent to kill was etched in Abella's eyes.

"Uncle, you've done a lot for me. You freed me from the fate of a witch, and I could open a beautiful store..."

The tip of the dagger trembled.

"But you've killed too many people, Uncle. And in the future..."

"Shh."

As Abella flinched her shoulder, Havitz slowly nodded.

"If that's what you think, then just do it."

Abella felt a vast fear.

'What am I doing?'

If walking a tightrope is for adults, then breaking that rope and plunging into boundless freedom felt like this.

"I'm going to kill you!"

"I know. That's what you want to do."

No.

'It's not what I want! It's because Uncle is a bad person, that's why he has to die...'

What she really wanted was.

'I don't know. I don't know what I should do!'

Who was Havitz, and did someone like her even have the right to kill him?

'Who gives that right?'

If Havitz were killed, what would become of the world?

'I don't know anything!'

Perfect chaos.

For the first time since her birth, Abella, having deviated from the law, trembled in vast freedom.

"Your Majesty!"

Bang! The door opened, and all members of Gustav's Fourth Knight Order stormed into the room.

Although she had imagined it, the sight was unbelievable to everyone present.

"Balkan."

Havitz turned his head.

"Didn't I say no one should come in? Today is for Abella and me."

"What are you talking about? You seem about to die soon."

"Get out."

Havitz looked up at the dagger again.

"If I die, Abella becomes the emperor. Let her do as she wishes. Even if she turns the whole world into a flower garden."

Zetaro shrugged his shoulders.

"Puhit! That's hilarious! A flower garden."

Balkan, too, had no right to interfere with his close friend's desire to spend the wedding night alone with his wife.

"Understood."

With that disappointingly brief answer, Balkan turned around, and the remaining three followed.

The door closed.

"What? They really left?"

"Abella."

Startled, Abella turned her gaze.

"Do whatever you want. It's something I've always wanted to do for you."

Abella's eyes welled up with tears.

"Why, why are you so nice to me? You've killed so many people! Why are you only like this with me?"

"Because we're friends."

The reason was simple.

"Sometimes, when playing with friends, we end up killing or being killed."

In Abella's mind, something that had been holding her together snapped.

"Sob, sob!"

As tears fell unchecked, she arched her upper body and lifted the dagger.

"Aaaaah!"

With a scream that seemed to tear her apart, she thrust down with all her strength, and the 'Law-Killer' plunged deep.

Havitz didn't even blink.

As the dagger embedded in the bed began to vibrate violently next to his ear.

"Argh!"

As soon as Abella released her hand, the 'Law-Killer' was destroyed.

"Why? How?"

Aganos, Kashan.

"Woorin."

As Minerva entered the room abruptly with Gando, Uorin was visible, holding the fragments of a porcelain doll.

"He didn't make it back."

This time, Gando did not clean up for her.

"I thought to retire him if there was one left."

"To him, retirement would have been death. Let's consider it a grand retirement."

Woorin carefully gathered the fragments.

"How is it on the Marzak side?"

Minerva gave a thumbs up.

"The target deprivation was successful. It seems Alpha-Fish has completed its mission perfectly."

"Really."

As Woorin got up, her face was now filled with solemnity.

"Gando, gather the 'Law-Killer' team and the Ex Machina team. The real battle starts now."

"Understood."

It was the beginning of the plan to assassinate Havitz.
