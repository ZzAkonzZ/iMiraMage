Chapter 748:

Gustav Empire

The Gustav Empire, which, along with Kashan and Jinchun, divided the world into three powers, was steeped in sorrow over the emperor’s passing.

Black flags fluttered in every corner of the streets, and no one dared to show their teeth in a smile. Anyone caught laughing in public risked not only their own neck but that of their entire family. Weeping was considered proper decorum for imperial citizens, and the wailing that echoed from every household haunted the dreams of many.

Thus, Havitz XVI quietly left the world on a cold, starry night at the age of 122. Officially, at least.

Amid the overwhelming national mourning, there was only one place where laughter and the ecstasy of indulgence remained uninterrupted: The easternmost region of the empire, Arkaba, ruled by the emperor’s youngest son, Havitz XVII.

The official walking through the Grand Hall of Arkaba Palace toward Havitz XVII’s private wing wore a grim expression. ‘This must never be known… The truth about the emperor is…’

He died in a frenzy of pleasure.

Havitz XVI’s corpse had been discovered with eyes wide open and frothing from every orifice. The 17 women who had been with him in bed, along with the head maid who reported the scene, were executed on the spot.

‘Havitz XVII, the sole surviving bloodline of Gustav.’

During his 122 years of life, Havitz XVI had fathered 782 children. But now, none remained alive. This was due to the unique succession law of the Gustav royal family: Any royal over the age of 20 was granted a license to commit fratricide.

Once a royal turned 20, they were allowed to kill any of their kin, and in extreme cases, a special trial would be held to determine the successor. Four years ago, Havitz XVII had secured his succession by brutally eliminating 17 other royals.

‘In other words…’

He had killed 781 of his siblings.

Despite the emperor’s death, which should have sent him rushing to the capital for the succession ceremony, Havitz XVII remained in Arkaba. This was due to a confidence so absolute it defied all reason.

Anyone familiar with Havitz XVII’s ways would say the same thing in unison: He is an enigma, impossible to analyze.

Even the most prominent nobles of the empire wouldn’t dare to attempt a coup, paralyzed by the uncertainty of facing such a man.

As the envoy from the capital reached the towering golden doors, he swallowed nervously and took a deep breath. “….”

Pleasure-filled moans and cries of ecstasy seeped through the doors.

“Your Highness, Darmov, the Minister of Internal Affairs, humbly requests an audience.”

Instead of a response, more cries rang out, and as if that were a signal, the guards opened the door.

‘This is beyond imagination.’

The first thing to hit him was the stench of debauchery. Inside the grand hall, over 200 men and women were engaging in beastly acts. Though they were all young and beautiful, seen as a whole, they looked like grotesque creatures fused into a writhing mass.

“What do you want?”

A middle-aged man, completely naked, sat on a golden throne, puffing on an ornate pipe.

‘That must be…’

The King of Desire, Gustav Havitz XVII.

His face was absurdly long, his nose resembled a horse’s, yet his eyes were strikingly beautiful, almost feminine—creating a bizarre contrast.

‘He’s exactly as the rumors described.’

In this space saturated with indulgence, he alone refrained from partaking, merely observing others with detached amusement. According to rumors, the pipe he smoked contained Asker, an extremely potent narcotic banned throughout the empire. A single day without it could drive users to suicide.

‘Yet Havitz XVII enjoys Asker.’

Precisely 3 grams every 24 hours.

‘The greatest restraint creates the greatest desire.’

This famous quote encapsulated Havitz’s nature and explained how he had survived among over 700 siblings.

“The empire mourns the emperor’s death. You must hold the succession ceremony without delay.”

“I can’t be bothered. There’s no woman or man there I want to strip, no object I desire, and no ambition to pursue. I’ll come when I feel like becoming emperor, so, for now, give it to whoever wants it.”

Havitz smirked and pointed at Darmov.

“Why don’t you take it? Before you die, wouldn’t it be fun to fondle the empire like a beautiful woman?”

‘What kind of confidence is this?’

Chilled to the bone, Darmov hurriedly replied.

“Your Majesty, I am too foolish to comprehend your jest.”

“Hahaha!”

As Havitz tilted his head back in laughter, a plume of Asker smoke escaped his throat. Suddenly, the laughter ceased.

“Fear of pain equals fear of pleasure.”

Havitz placed the pipe back between his lips.

“Does your heart race at the mere thought of immense pleasure? Are you afraid you won’t be able to restrain yourself, that you’ll lose who you are?”

It felt as if his airway had closed, making it difficult to breathe.

‘Please, let this end soon.’

For the first time in his life, Darmov regretted becoming the Minister of Internal Affairs.

“My father…”

Havitz XVI had tasted every sweetness in the world, yet there was one thing he could never have.

“Adrias Miro, wasn’t it?”

The successor of Goffin, humanity’s greatest defender who had blocked the heavenly army at the dimensional barrier. The moment he lost her, Havitz XVI became an insatiable seeker of pleasure, like a man dying of thirst.

“He lost his restraint. I have no interest in being emperor, but from that perspective, he was a pitiful man.”

Having served the emperor his entire life, Darmov couldn’t hold back any longer.

“Your Majesty, it is inappropriate for the future emperor to speak ill of the late emperor.”

“It doesn’t matter.”

Havitz XVII rose from his throne.

“If this world were a place where some god handed out a few problems and rewarded those who solved them…”

With a metallic ring, he drew a sword from beside the throne.

“Everything would be simple. No one would feel wronged, and no one would agonize over how to live. But you see,”

Descending the steps, Havitz kicked aside the writhing bodies of two entangled individuals.

“There are no problems to begin with. This world is more like… a plaza. A place where people gather and do whatever they want.”

Without problems, there are no correct answers.

“Whatever you do in that space…”

The sword pierced through the two bodies and slammed into the ground with a thud, making Darmov flinch.

“Nothing happens.”

The impaled individuals groaned, but the Asker addicts were too far gone to care. Havitz pulled out the sword, briefly inspecting the bloodied blade before shrugging.

“Well, I’ve killed someone. So, what do you think? Do you believe I’ve done something wrong?”

The sword’s blade touched Darmov’s neck.

“Th-the citizens of the empire are all Your Majesty’s possessions, so…”

“No, no! That’s not what I’m asking! What I mean is, there’s nothing in this world you mustn’t do! And there’s nothing you absolutely must do either. It’s just…”

Havitz, struggling to find the right words, suddenly shouted at Darmov.

“It’s all nothing!”

The bloodied sword pointed at the corpses.

“They’re dead! They must be in pain. So what? What happens to me? Nothing! They’re just dead because I killed them!”

“Hngh… hngh…”

Urine trickled down Darmov’s pants.

“That’s why this world is so amusing.”

If human emotions were to manifest as reality, what form would Havitz’s take?

“I don’t care about being emperor. If you’re here to talk about something boring, I’ll make it entertaining.”

“The Holy War is beginning.”

Darmov squeezed out his words with all his strength.

“It’s the 420th Council. With no ruler in the empire… Your Majesty must attend the Holy War… hngh!”

Suddenly, the sword was withdrawn.

“The Holy War, huh?”

Havitz closed his eyes in thought, his eyelids twitching as his whites showed.

“Yes, that’s far more interesting than the empire.”

Uorin.

“Ah… this is driving me crazy. It’s really hard to hold back…”

Darmov cautiously stepped back as he noticed Havitz’s excitement mounting.

“Yes, that’s it. One by one, snap… snap…”

He didn’t even want to think about what Havitz was imagining.

“But that’s just a fleeting amusement. True desire isn’t like that. Do you know what I really want to conquer, Minister?”

“I-I wouldn’t dare…”

“The world. Everything that exists.”

Spreading his arms wide while gripping the sword, Havitz’s body stood fully erect, his navel raised high.

‘This isn’t a human. Even if he is, we can’t categorize him as one of us.’

The new emperor, Gustav Havitz XVII.

“I will conquer the entire world.”

A lone fly, origin unknown, buzzed around the ceiling.

"Now, we go to strike the last remaining one."

In the end, demons are a species created from the remnants of information left behind by the extinction of humans, essentially using souls as their material.

The furnace that melts these demons is called the "Hellfire," where the souls of humans are constantly being melted down.

When the souls that fall into this Hellfire are purified, they merge into one to become demons, and their abilities are determined by the nature of their combination.

"Let's go, my loyal subordinates."

In Satan's army, the rank of division commander is equivalent to that of a national commander in human terms.

Although the Zion Project has reduced the psychic realm, humanity has effectively left the worst of the demons in the desert.

"Kill the humans! With every scream they make, we will grow stronger. Give them the most horrific deaths!"

Thus, humans define demons as:

Evil spirits.

"This world is ours!"

A force of 3,000 troops crossed the desert.

Their destination was the dungeon of the great mage Zulu in the Kingdom of Paras, where Gaold and Kangnan were hiding in a pyramid.
