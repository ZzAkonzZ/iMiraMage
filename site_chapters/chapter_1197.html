Chapter 1197:

The underground facility of the Holy War.

As Uorin walked, the uneasy mechanical noises from the engine room made her feel like she was on the verge of a nervous breakdown.

"I can’t see it."

The vibrations of the timeline were too massive and fast, making it impossible to find a stable reference point.

"Where am I supposed to go?"

Shirone was suppressing Havitz’s assassination game through Elikia, but variables still remained.

"If he could use it continuously, he would have. That means there's a delay in his ability to break the Vanishing."

Probably once every hour.

More than anything, Shirone, who was managing simultaneous events, couldn’t possibly focus solely on Havitz.

"If someone else is in danger elsewhere, Shirone will use that ability."

Because he is Yahweh.

If another nation were to target someone in this situation, the most obvious target was the Empress of Kashan.

"Every kingdom is after me."

Why wouldn’t they be?

The Wind Guards were annihilated, and even Gando was dead.

In the entire history of Kashan, had there ever been a time when the Empress was this defenseless?

"Is this where it ends for me?"

Dizzy from the thought, Uorin pressed her hand against the wall.

"Huff… Huff…"

Her chest felt tight as if she were choking, her blood circulation failing, leaving her dizzy to the extreme.

Kido supported her.

"Are you okay? I’ll carry you. You need to rest."

"Haha."

She let out a hollow laugh.

"Isn’t it ridiculous? The only thing left for the Empress of Kashan is a pet goblin."

Kido didn’t get angry.

"We need to escape the Holy War. The royal guards will hold them off while you run. If they find out the Empress above ground is a decoy, we won’t even have the chance to escape."

"I can’t."

"Why are you being so stubborn? You’re smart. Kashan or not, you need to survive first. That’s the only way there will be a future."

"Even so, I can’t."

Uorin clenched her teeth.

"I could give up the empire a hundred times over. But I must remain the Empress of Kashan to the end."

"Why?"

"Because if I don’t, I’ll be nothing to Shirone."

Kido fell silent.

"If I don’t have a voice in the Holy War, if I don’t have overwhelming military power, if I don’t have the authority of an Empress, then what am I? Just a woman. A plain woman who can’t even fight like Amy."

Uorin grabbed Kido’s shoulders.

"Do you have any idea how long I’ve waited for Shirone? You wouldn’t know. Even before humanity began… I have waited for Shirone! So why! Why do I have to give up?!"

"Alright, calm down."

"I will never give up! There has to be a way. If I just hold out a little longer…"

Longing only for a passionate love, she had gone from a Gaian to nothing more than a primitive human.

But…

"I can’t fight."

No matter how superior her genes were across history, becoming someone like Amy was impossible.

Neither Shirone, Rian, Kuan, Gaold, nor Miro… No one, no matter how strong.

"It was never possible."

Because it required something beyond mere biological traits—something greater than life itself.

"I will protect it."

The feeling of defeat she had toward Amy was precisely that.

"I can’t lose Kashan as well."

"Alright, I got it."

Kido gripped his twin-bladed spear with one hand and supported Uorin as they moved forward.

"Let’s do this. Where do we go?"

Just as Uorin was about to activate her foresight again, a thick fog began to roll in.

"What’s this?"

Before they knew it, an old, oriental-style door stood before them, creaking ominously as it swung open.

"The Ghost Gate."

Jincheon’s Bureau Chief, Anchal, emerged.

"Foresight doesn’t work on me, for I control illusions. This is the end, Empress of Kashan."

She removed her eyepatch, revealing the demon eye embedded in her eye, glowing with a pure blue light.

Uorin clenched her fists.

"Damn it."

Breaking through the royal guards and uncovering the decoy’s deception alone made Anchal a first-rate elite.

"I found you, Empress."

Natasha hung from a pipe overhead, while from the shadows below, a masked swordsman approached.

It was Rai, the captain of Thormia’s royal guards.

Three of them.

Anchal spoke.

"You used your head, but you’re not the only one with a brain. Give up. Kashan is finished."

Kido didn’t wait for Uorin to answer.

"Hah, you bastards think I’d even blink just because you’re coming after us? Get lost if you don’t want to die."

Of course, he knew how strong they were.

"But there’s hope. There are 13 minutes left until Havitz’s assassination game. If I just hold out until then…"

Anchal asked, "Who’s going first?"

Neither Natasha nor Rai answered. Instead, she nodded and kicked off the ground.

"The one who grabs her wins."

The moment the battle began, shockwaves erupted everywhere except for where Uorin stood.

"Wind Tempest."

As the demon eye glowed, hundreds of blades materialized in the air, all flying toward Kido.

"Ughhh…!"

Its penetrative force was weaker than Incarnation Arts, but its adaptability was near limitless.

"She’s tricky."

The mind’s insight of Anchal, the speed of Natasha, the technique of Rai.

"Ten minutes left."

As Kido took on attacks from all three simultaneously, his body accumulated small but numerous wounds.

"This is… a goblin’s level?"

Anchal was astounded.

"No, he’s already surpassed the limits of his species. He’s not human, nor goblin—he’s something else entirely."

Kido’s eyes burned.

"Immovable Mind."

With a Buddhist revelation, he shattered the illusion and bound Natasha’s speed with earthbound spirits.

Rai infiltrated from behind.

"I’ll take him."

As Kido deflected an attack to the side, his eyes flashed as he spun and swung his spear.

"Dominion of the Origin."

A slash that severed atoms—the ultimate strike that could cut through all matter.

"Tch!"

The assassins distanced themselves, watching as snake-like currents of electricity crackled through the air.

"If I hadn’t blocked that, I would’ve died."

Panting heavily, Kido stood in front of Uorin.

"Two minutes left."

Anchal approached.

"How grotesque. Or should I say repulsive?"

"The taste of the Great Purification… You are no truth. You are nothing but a monster of mixed realizations."

Her gaze locked onto Uorin.

"Does the Empress of Kashan have no sense of morality? What have you been feeding this beast?"

"Everything except my own flesh."

Everything she could procure with her power had gone into shaping Kido's body.

"So what? What does it matter what I feed my own dog? You want some too?"

Anchal’s demon eye gleamed.

"You don’t even feel shame."

As smoke rose from all directions and Natasha and Rai finished preparing to move—

Elikia’s light enveloped the Holy War.

'Havitz?'

Those who had already experienced it were unsurprised and immediately checked Satan's location.

'... I

As Anchal backed off, the Ghost Gate appeared.

"Leave the Holy War immediately, Empress. This may be the last chance given to you."

Like a dream, the Ghost Gate vanished, and Natasha and Rai melted into the shadows.

Kido dropped to one knee.

"Phew, we should be fine for now."

With Havitz’s location revealed, killing Uorin would now violate international law.

"Don’t let your guard down. They’ll be back soon."

"Of course, you’re the number one target. But whoever eliminates you will be disqualified from the Intelligence Bureau. They can’t move recklessly."

As long as Elikia was active.

'The worst-case scenario is if Elikia isn't triggered and multiple people die.'

If that happened, there would be no way to determine who Havitz had killed, making it impossible to identify the culprit.

'Thank you, Shirone.'

Though he hadn’t done it for Uorin’s sake.

"Let’s go. No matter how tired you are, keep using foresight. If Anchal tracks us down again, we’re finished."

"Got it."

As they walked through the engine room, Kido glanced up at the ceiling.

'Another casualty.'

Rest in peace.

Ten minutes before Elikia activated.

Zetaro trudged toward the Gustav Sector, shoulders slumped.

'Smodo was the first to go home.'

Why Smodo?

'Because he wanted to stay with me longer? No… Was I never even on his mind from the start? Why…?'

The truth was unknowable.

'Soon, it’ll start again. Thinking about Havitz means Vanishing is about to activate. Who will be the next target? Maybe this time, it’ll finally be me…'

Zetaro stopped walking.

From the far end of the corridor, Havitz approached, mumbling to himself, his lips split open.

"Tch, took a hit. So that’s the strategy? Fine, in that case—"

At that moment, their gazes met.

While Zetaro’s heart pounded like it would explode, Havitz didn’t even acknowledge him.

"Hmm… Right. This is going to be fun. The thrill of this game is that I might lose."

As he passed by like a stranger, Zetaro realized—

'I’m not fun for him anymore.'

A silent declaration of severance.

"Has it already been an hour?"

Abandoning thoughts of Wizard, Havitz sprinted through the corridor in his Vanishing state.

'Let’s end this quickly.'

The royal office of the Corona Sector.

"The demons have grown too powerful. At this rate, the Ivory Tower will be annihilated. We must act quickly—"

Despite the abrupt entrance, the officials inside remained focused on their meeting.

Some played card games, while Armin and the Stars of the Ivory Tower gathered together.

Havitz snorted.

"Who should I kill?"

Having been thwarted twice by Elikia, he opted to wait in advance this time.

"You."

He held a blade to the neck of King Udlai, watching and waiting for the right moment.

'Four seconds. Three seconds. Two seconds…?'

Just then, Udlai’s eyes suddenly widened. He kicked back from his chair, throwing himself away.

"Your Majesty!"

A red orb rolled out of his pocket, splitting into two as it hit the floor.

〈The Last Cry of the Jaeger Clan〉.

A relic so rare that not even vast wealth could procure another.

"These bastards really…"

Havitz’s eyes flared as he lunged.

At that moment, Elikia’s light erupted in dazzling radiance.

'Havitz!'

The Stars of the Ivory Tower, moving faster than thought, instantly turned their bodies.

"Kill him!"

Before Elikia disappeared.

As Armin cast a Stop spell, Hell Prison spiraled upward, tearing through time itself.

'A complete form.'

As a high-speed clash unfolded, Havitz glanced at King Udlai.

The royal guards had already formed a protective wall around him.

He had lost.

A first in Satan’s history—an unprecedented event.

Havitz, overwhelmed by rage, charged recklessly.

"Block him!"

The Stars moved to defend Udlai, but Havitz’s target was the maid cowering on the floor.

"Hurgh…!"

As the blade plunged into the maid’s throat, everything—Stars, Hell Prison, the battle—came to a halt.

'This… isn’t play anymore.'

Killing solely to avoid losing the game—this was proof that Satan was now the hunted.

The Stars seethed with fury at the meaningless sacrifice.

"Even third-rate scum wouldn’t do this…"

"So what?"

Pulling the blade from the maid’s throat, Havitz spoke.

"It’s not a bad outcome, is it? If I lose the game, I’ll just start a massacre."

That was true.

'Havitz proposed the game. There was no set penalty for losing. But then… why is he so desperate to win?'

Vanishing activated again.

"Shall I prove it now?"

Even with Havitz out of sight, the Stars expanded their Spirit Zone with unshakable focus.

'Damn it! I missed something. Focus. Focus.'

"Tch."

Havitz scoffed. But instead of making another move, he turned toward the door.

'Can’t end the game yet.'

At least until the "Truth or Lies" game concluded at midnight, he had to keep the rules in place.

"Keep sweating."

As Havitz opened the door, he was struck in the side.

"Keuh!"

Knocked back several meters, he clutched his stomach, his face twisted in pain.

"Urghhh…!"

The Stars, regaining their senses, widened their eyes.

"You…?"

"Sorry, excuse me for a moment."

Wizard stepped through the doorway, speaking indifferently.

"I'm in the middle of hide-and-seek."
