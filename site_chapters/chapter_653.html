Chapter 653:

"What do you mean, it’s over?"

When Shirone blinked and asked, Austin held the note out again.

"What’s this?"

"A map of the sewer passage beneath Mr. Brooks’ estate. Estera assigned me as your escort, but… it looks like I won’t be able to go any farther."

Most of the horses were already dead, and after a bombardment that erased an entire cliff, the surviving ones had bolted.

Shirone examined the map.

It wasn’t as detailed as a blueprint, but from the layout, it was clear that wandering in without a guide would be disorienting.

"You’ll probably figure it out soon, but our guild has been the exclusive handler of letters to Mr. Brooks. I don’t have any specific advice for someone like you, but just remember one thing. An assignment always follows the conditions set by the client. Don’t concern yourself with anything beyond delivering that letter. This map should help you. Once you reach the end of the passage, one of Brooks’ people will guide you."

Shirone looked up and asked,

"What about you, Mr. Austin?"

"I’ll handle cleanup here. I’ll collect your loot on your behalf. Of course, I’ll take a ten percent cut as commission."

They hadn’t planned to collect loot, but refusing compensation offered would be foolish.

"Sure. Please do."

Now that they knew many organizations were targeting Armand, Shirone had no intention of taking the main road. He accepted without hesitation.

"If I plan to follow after tidying things up, it’ll take time. So I have a favor—after finishing the assignment, please stop by the Silver Ring’s main branch."

Guilds are extremely sensitive to information, and especially after an incident like this, word would spread like wildfire across the continent.

By the time Shirone reached the capital, multiple guilds would surely be trying to recruit him.

Austin had chosen to step back not because of inability, but because from a veteran’s perspective, it was better to secure the loot than chase what couldn’t be caught.

"Don’t worry. I understand the value of trust, too."

Austin gave a bitter smile.

That’s exactly why I’m worried…

Shirone may have been aware of his strength, but to Austin, that was merely a measure of raw power—not a guarantee of how far one could go. He was still a rookie.

Trust…

In this world, trust was often nothing more than insurance for the weak.

In the guild’s world, where strength was everything, Shirone had already passed the point of needing to invoke trust.

"You’ll need to move quickly if you want to reach the capital before sunset. Better get going."

"Yes. Be safe too, Mr. Austin."

"Oh, and one more thing."

Just before Shirone and Rian entered the mountains, Austin called out.

"Once you reach the capital, it wouldn’t hurt to look into Mr. Brooks a bit before you complete the delivery."

It was just a letter delivery, but Austin’s veteran instincts couldn’t help sounding a warning.

Sometimes even a minor variable—like who happened to take the job—could throw everything into chaos.

Shirone, who had paused in thought, smiled as he nodded.

"Alright. Don’t worry."

Right… who am I to be worrying about him?

Once Shirone and Rian disappeared into the mountains, Austin picked up a longsword and began beheading the corpses of the bandit leaders.

Third Reset. The Great Purification.

End of the Shirone Five-Bandit-Crew Annihilation Incident.

Using Armand’s power, Shirone raced through the mountain path like a wild animal, crossing a peak in just 20 minutes.

As the capital’s outer wall and the palace closer to its center came into view, Rian made a suggestion.

"Shirone, let’s wash up before we go in."

Bringing the stench of blood into the heart of civilization would benefit no one.

"Yeah, good idea."

Flying above the terrain using a spell, Shirone found a small lake where he and Rian landed.

Was it fate, or consequence?

There sat Ra Enemy.

Perched on a rock by the lake with one leg lowered, his olive-toned skin and sharp features gave him a striking presence. His eyes—holding either everything or nothing—were unreadable, and his firmly closed lips gave away neither joy nor sorrow.

"It’s cold. Are you alright?"

As Shirone passed by the rock Ra sat on, he asked.

"This kind of cold is nothing. Though come to think of it, you don’t look like you need a wash—no blood on you or anything."

"Still, I want to. My head’s a mess after the battle."

Nothing cleared the mind like cold water.

As Shirone transformed Armand into sword form and tossed it to the ground, Ra glanced at it and tilted his head.

In the meantime, Shirone and Rian stripped down and jumped into the lake.

"Ugh, cold. So cold."

Shirone shivered, hugging himself, while Rian dove underwater and shot up.

"Puh! Feels great."

Shirone watched him quietly.

That calmness—probably earned after countless battles—but the Rian he knew had never been an indifferent killer.

"Rian… how do you deal with it?"

Rian froze, hands mid-splash.

"I don’t deal with it. I just… don’t think about it."

"Don’t think?"

"There’s no right answer. Whether they were good or evil—once I draw my sword, I don’t hesitate. That’s the decision I made."

Rian submerged himself.

"Shirone, there’s no such thing as a 'right time' to kill. Nor a wrong one. It’s like deciding whether or not to drink a glass of water. What matters is responsibility. I’ve chosen to carry that. So I don’t think about it."

Ra nodded as he listened.

"Responsibility for life."

Rian looked down at his cupped hands in the water.

"A single life takes away many others. It’s incredibly unfair. No matter who they were, we can’t deny that our actions disrupted the balance."

He turned toward Shirone.

"So let’s mourn, Shirone. Mourn for life itself."

And keep carrying that weight forever.

"Yeah."

As they closed their eyes in silent prayer, the winter wind howled around them.

And just as Ra looked up at the sky and smiled in contemplation—

What was that?

Shirone’s eyes snapped open.

I thought someone was there...

Even activating Elysion revealed nothing.

This world—what people called “reality”—was only ever the sum of five senses.

Yet just moments ago, Shirone had felt like an entirely new sense had opened, revealing the world in a way he'd never experienced—then vanished again.

"What’s wrong, Shirone? You don’t look so good."

"No, it’s nothing. Let’s go."

Shirone brushed away the thought of what wasn’t there, climbed out of the water, and changed clothes.

As he approached, Ra, who had been silently watching from just a meter away, slowly turned and stepped aside to make way.

"Let’s hope we get there before the rumors start spreading."

By the time they arrived in Bashka, the sun was setting.

Before joining the modest line at the gate, Shirone pulled out the letter Estera had given him.

"I think I need to find out what this letter actually says."

He had been suspicious from the start, and Austin’s cryptic advice lingered.

"But didn’t they say it was encrypted?"

"I’ll try to decode it."

Homemade ciphers weren’t highly professional, which ironically made them harder to crack—but Shirone could extract a general understanding using the Ultima System.

"So you’re saying it can interpret information on an integrated level?"

"Yeah. It’s like a language that comes before language. Super convenient."

It was thanks to the Ultima System that he’d also decoded the true meaning behind the flute-birdsong signal Mari used.

After reading the letter, Shirone tucked it back into his coat. Rian asked,

"What did it say? Is it something dangerous?"

"No, not really. Just something personal."

Rian, not the type to pry into someone’s private matters, asked no further questions.

Even though it was just the two of them, being a mage and a noble meant they passed through the city gates with ease. Once inside, Shirone followed Austin’s advice and began gathering information on Brooks.

After visiting several shops, they finally heard something at a weapon store.

“Mr. Brooks? Why are you looking for him? Mercenaries?”

“No, we’re mages.”

“Ah, so you’re with the Silver Ring Guild.”

The shop owner nodded, as if something had clicked.

“Mr. Brooks is a mercenary broker.”

According to the owner, he was a savvy businessman, with contracts at seven major organizations under his belt.

“In Tormia, there’s hardly a mercenary who hasn’t passed through Mr. Brooks’ hands. But whether he’s at his estate today… I’m not sure. He’s always a busy man.”

A mercenary broker...

This was exactly what Austin had been concerned about.

If the man worked on the front lines of war, it was only natural that the security around his estate would be tight.

Based on the letter decoded using the Ultima System, stealth and the ability to sneak in undetected were absolutely necessary.

It should be fine if we go through the sewers.

After leaving the shop, the two arrived at Brooks’ estate.

Like the homes of other nobles, it occupied a large plot of land—but unlike them, it was fortified.

Plainclothes guards stood at the gate, and within the archway, men carrying torches patrolled the garden.

“They’re all skilled.”

Just sensing the aura in the air, they were leagues above ordinary bandits.

“…We really should go in through the sewers, right?”

Following the map, they reached a manhole about 200 meters from the estate.

Waiting for a moment when no one was around, they opened the cover and climbed down. As they descended into the foul-smelling space, Rian picked up a familiar scent among the stench.

“Shirone, I smell rotting flesh.”

The thought of a corpse left to decay in the city sewers was unsettling, but the fact had already happened—there was no need to dwell on it.

“Yeah. We can’t let our guard down.”

As they drew closer to Brooks’ estate, the smell thickened until they finally discovered a corpse—one that couldn’t have been abandoned more than a few days.

“What is this? It’s not human.”

They couldn’t identify the exact species, but the corpse had pale pink skin—it was a demi-human.

“There are signs of torture.”

“No, it wasn’t torture.”

The body was too mutilated for it to have been done just to cause pain.

“…Let’s just do our job.”

Austin’s words came back to him: don’t worry about anything outside the assignment.

After navigating the maze-like sewers, they arrived at a staircase that was clearly man-made.

At the top of the stairs, a heavy iron gate blocked the way. Sitting in a chair near the drainage side was a man, arms crossed.

Rian halted instinctively, without a word from anyone.

Who is this guy…?

The man had long hair draped over his face, like a woman’s, making it impossible to read any traits or intent.

He felt like a corpse just sitting upright.

Sniff sniff. Sniff sniff.

The man, previously silent as death, made a sniffing sound and slowly raised his head.

Rian gripped the handle of his greatsword and stepped in front to shield Shirone.

This guy… is incredibly strong.

And that only made it stranger.

If someone of this caliber were guarding the estate, wouldn’t Austin have at least mentioned his name?

The man’s dull, animal-like eyes shifted between the two of them before fixing on Shirone.

“You… met Ra, didn’t you?”

“…What?”

The man slowly stood from his chair. In both hands now were daggers, shaped like kitchen knives.

“Where is he?”

His name was Shagal.

What even Brooks, his employer, didn’t know… was that he was a serial killer listed among the world's 100 most dangerous individuals.
