Chapter 637:

Electricity could also illuminate the darkness, but even that was just darkness in the face of Shirone's light.

Nade couldn't bear the holiness that embraced the entire world.

The overwhelming presence of Shirone, superimposed countless times, was a new kind of fear for Nade in his magic-transformed state.

This fear, combined with his anger, pushed his magic power to new heights.

"Aaaargh!"

As the 2000 system overloaded, the colosseum's lights flickered.

The growing darkness amplified the audience's anxiety, and those with sensitive nerves trembled in fear of a major disaster.

"What an absurd graduation exam."

If it were just Nade or Shirone alone, the 2000 system, built with the kingdom's best technology, wouldn't have come close to crashing.

But now, both were recording numbers beyond comprehension, and no one could predict if the system could handle their clash.

'You don't understand, Shirone.'

From the start, Shirone had no roots, so he didn't know what it was like for those roots to rot.

The contradictory pain of not being able to cut them off or leave them intact.

"You don't understand!"

Electricity twisted and flew, striking Shirone with concentrated force.

"Grrr!"

Dozens of Shirones were electrocuted and fell every second.

But the events in the quantum realm bubbled like foam, sustaining the fractal limits indefinitely.

'Live! Live, Nade!'

His 2000 bracelet was on the verge of breaking, but he didn't hesitate.

Quantum Oscillation - Photon Cannon.

The mass wave's impact converted the quantum shock into anti-magic, striking Nade's mind.

'This won't extinguish my anger!'

If it were anger that could be extinguished, his life, clinging to it, wouldn't be so miserable.

Magic Transformation - Thunder God Rebirth.

"Aaaargh!"

Without needing to control his endlessly rising magic, Nade unleashed all his power, and the thunder god's form twisted into thousands of strands, reaching for Shirone.

-Alert! Alert! System overload. The 2000 system is shutting down. Time remaining: 10 seconds. 9 seconds…

"Damn it!"

As different energies crossed, half the audience stood up, and even the analysts shut down the main system, preparing for the worst.

'Watch closely! My anger! The monster I've become!'

It was as if Nade's inner cry could be heard.

'Nade, I know how hard it's been.'

Anger grows by consuming human hope, and once it takes hold, it blinds you to what's truly important.

'But… we're almost there.'

At least Nade hadn't given up.

Clinging to the vague light that he might one day fly free, he had waited for parents who would never return, biting the rope.

'Now you have us! So…!'

Quantum Oscillation restored the events erased by Thunder God Rebirth, expanding the flash to its original size.

"Live, Nade!"

As the flash descended, Nade's eyes widened in awe.

"Ah…"

The holy light began to push back the anger of his magic transformation.

'Ah, Shirone.'

His bloody tears turned into clear liquid.

'You are beautiful.'

So… radiant.

'That's why I… want to be your friend…'

As the massive light expanded and erased Nade's form, a long silence followed, as if the world had just been born.

The prolonged journey of light made the entire process feel like a dream, and the 2000 system had indeed shut down.

There was no announcement, and people finally came to their senses, looking around the battlefield.

"Over there!"

Nade lay sprawled in the center of the colosseum, with Shirone watching from a distance, panting.

Realizing the graduation exam was over, a wave of emotion surged through the crowd.

"Wow!"

Everyone stood up, cheering, and a standing ovation rippled through the colosseum.

"Shirone! Shirone! Shirone!"

In the chorus echoing throughout Creas City, Nade's name was never mentioned.

'So this is how it is…'

Even if he cried tears of blood and roared like a monster about his painful life, no one cared.

-Fight to the end! Struggle to the end!

Did Shirone know?

Is that why he had shouted so desperately, knowing the frustrating situation?

"Nade."

Kneeling, Shirone lifted his head.

He didn't know exactly when the 2000 system had shut down, and the anti-magic impact must have been immense.

"Nade!"

Too weak to stand, Shirone crawled towards Nade.

"I'm sorry… I'm sorry, Nade."

As his focus on the graduation exam dissolved, emotions flooded in.

As he sobbed, tears streaming down his face, Nade's chest heaved.

"Heh, heh heh."

As Shirone stared in disbelief, Nade sat up with a self-deprecating smile.

"What, I lost even after all that?"

Having rejected friends, family, and his own life, he had poured everything into his magic transformation.

He had thought he could destroy anything, but now, blocked by the wall of Shirone, he felt nothing but absurdity and embarrassment.

"Nade, are you okay?"

Nade looked at Shirone's tear-streaked face and turned away.

'What right do I have…'

He hadn't shown his true face, not even daring to call himself a friend.

As the 2000 system rebooted, the announcement finally came.

-West Nade. Final graduation rank: 2nd.

The audience fell silent.

-Top graduate of Alpheas Magic School's graduation exam, Arian Shirone. Data for all 30 participants will be sent to the Red Line.

"Dear, our son…"

Vincent and Olina were too exhausted to even be surprised anymore, their mouths agape.

The audience took a long time to process their emotions after witnessing the most intense graduation exam in the kingdom's history.

"Rian, did you hear that? Did you just hear that?"

As Reina patted her brother's shoulder, she turned to see Rian trembling, his fists clenched.

He was a young boy.

Annoying enough to tattle when hiding in the library from the sword instructor Kite, skilled enough to succeed in his first sword kill with a wooden sword, and strong enough to defeat her in a real duel.

Take responsibility for what you must.

What better phrase to describe his character?

But don't take on responsibilities that aren't yours.

No regrets for what the heart chooses.

Even if I die, I won't regret becoming your friend.

That boy was Shirone.

"Shironeee!"

Rian shouted with all his might.

"Let's go! To the world!"

Rian's voice echoed through the colosseum, prompting the audience to rise and applaud.

"You're the best! The pride of the Kingdom of Tormia!"

"You'll definitely become a great mage!"

It was so loud that even nearby voices couldn't be heard, and Miro and Poltar, hugging each other despite just meeting, jumped up and down, forgetting their embarrassment.

"Yay! He's the top graduate! Shirone is the top graduate!"

"I told you! I knew he could do it! My judgment was right! Oh, wait!"

Poltar, who had been celebrating as if it were his own achievement, rummaged through his bag and hastily recorded his thoughts in a notebook.

Meanwhile, Miro, watching Shirone shyly greet the applause, felt a bit sad.

'You're ready.'

The 2000 system didn't make it easier than real combat, but there was a gap between virtual and reality.

If all the magic from the graduation exam had clashed in reality, the outcome might have been different today.

But since everyone fought with the same opportunities, there was no more verified method, and this was why Miro felt sad.

'Shirone, from today, you are the kingdom's top student. But with that comes greater responsibility.'

The world Shirone had to fight in was a real battlefield far more horrific than the graduation exam.

'Congratulations. And I'm sorry.'

As one of the people who helped Shirone grow, Miro could already see the challenges he would face.

'It will be hell. You won't want to endure it. You'll shed tears of blood, Shirone.'

Even without invoking the duty of the strong, humanity would eventually stand at the brink of destruction, and Shirone, as part of the whole, would have to lead everyone.

'So enjoy today to the fullest. It's your day.'

As Miro finally smiled and turned away, a middle-aged bald man approached.

"There you are, Miro. I didn't recognize you with the hood."

It was Fermi's father, Enrique.

"Oh, so you came for your son?"

"The atmosphere was different this time. But he failed."

"That's unfortunate. Did you see Fermi?"

"He left without saying anything. He might try again next year, or he might quit school."

Fermi was also an invaluable asset to Miro.

"Don't worry. He never makes decisions that don't benefit him. Though that's what makes him annoying."

If he was Yolga's son, there would be no problem trusting him.

"Hmm, Miro. I've heard that name… Wait, Miro!"

Poltar's eyes widened in shock.

He didn't know about the 20 Judges, but he couldn't forget the name of the world's most renowned talent.

Feeling cognitive dissonance from Miro's puffed cheeks and pointing finger, Poltar trembled as she put her arm around his shoulder.

"I'm in a great mood, so let's go for a drink. Of course, you're buying."

"…Are you really Miro? You haven't aged a bit. What have you been doing all this time?"

"Hohoho! If you hear my story, you'll be shocked. Enrique, what are you doing? Let's go."

Shaking his head, Enrique followed and asked,

"Aren't you going to meet Shirone?"

"It's fine."

As Miro walked towards the colosseum exit, she looked back.

"We'll meet at a higher place."

Amidst the continuous applause, Shirone extended his hand to Nade.

"Nade, can you stand?"

Nade turned his head away.

"Forget it. Don't think of me as a friend. I don't deserve it."

"Don't say that…"

As Shirone frowned and tried to speak,

"You! Speak clearly!"

Iruki ran over and smacked Nade on the head.

"Ow! Why did you hit me?"

As Nade turned, Iruki's finger poked his face.

"Why did you attack me twice? You did it on purpose, didn't you?"

Nade blinked and frowned.

"I don't know! Maybe your face invited my fist! I'm already in a bad mood, and you're butting in."

Iruki narrowed his eyes.

"What's there to be in a bad mood about? You said you didn't care, but you still got second place."

Deciding not to argue, Nade turned back to Shirone.

"Anyway, don't forgive me. I'm leaving the trio from today."

This time, Shirone smacked Nade on the head.

"Ow! Why are you doing this?"

"Because it's frustrating! Not fighting doesn't make you friends! Being able to reconcile after fighting does! Don't you have that much trust?"

Gritting his teeth, Nade looked down sadly.

"I just can't forgive myself. I don't know why I did it."

"Living seriously is important, but…"

Iruki placed a hand on Nade's shoulder.

"You don't have to live so seriously. It's all over. Just shake it off and move on."

Could he really deserve that?

As he questioned himself, Shirone extended his hand again with a smile.

"Let's go eat, Nade."
