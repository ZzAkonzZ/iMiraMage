Chapter 596:

A hunched old woman ran along the rough mountain path.

At 82 years old, she couldn’t stop, carrying the fate of the kingdom in her arms.

‘Your Highness, you must survive.’

The first prince of the Kazra Kingdom.

Though abandoned to escape the invasion of the Yakma Republic, one day he would return to Kazra and sit on the throne.

‘Anywhere there are people…’

She needed to hide the prince in a place even she couldn’t explain, and finally, she found a hunter’s cabin.

“What is that…?”

As the old woman opened the stable door and entered, Shirone held his breath.

Anchal’s illusion magic was activated, so she wouldn’t notice, but his heart pounded as she approached.

“Your Highness, please preserve your health.”

The old woman placed the child down, clasped her hands in a short prayer, and quickly left the stable.

“Who is this child?”

Miro asked.

“The first prince of the Kazra Kingdom, the child of Orkamp IV and Eliza.”

Shirone, who had been invited to the royal palace and experienced many hardships, knew the situation in Kazra better than anyone.

“But this is our home.”

“If you’re Hexa, then yes. And now, I’m going to switch you with the prince of Kazra.”

“What?”

Leaving Hexa here meant the prince of Kazra, who should have been here, would go to the upper echelons.

“Why do that? Can’t we raise both? And how did you know the prince would come here?”

“There’s no way to know, not until you experience it.”

As Shirone confirmed the old woman’s footprints disappearing, he realized.

“A reset?”

“Yes. The initialization is already underway. But because Goffin separated the upper echelon’s time from the original time, we can observe it.”

Anchal said.

“The inflection point before and after the reset. You inserted and connected the event of switching the child at that moment’s coordinates.”

“Exactly. That’s why no one can approach, except for the upper echelons of Istas.”

As Miro placed Hexa down and tried to pick up the prince left by the old woman, Shirone grabbed her shoulder.

“You haven’t answered yet. Why take him?”

“Because he shouldn’t exist. According to Goffin, Hexa is a being that has been safeguarded against Anke La’s reset and any other factors.”

Thinking of Behemoth, now merged with her avatar, made her shudder.

“But this child isn’t. If left alone, he’ll be used.”

“I can’t accept that.”

Shirone’s breath grew heavy.

“If I survived because of someone’s sacrifice… what does my life mean?”

Yolga would think the same.

‘Sister could never do such a thing.’

That’s why he had to do it.

“Shirone.”

Miro held the prince.

“I’m not killing him. He’ll stay in the upper echelons of Istas.”

“That’s the same thing! He’ll live the same short time over and over! This child, who doesn’t even know reason, language, or the existence of the world!”

“True.”

Miro didn’t ignore reality.

“If there was a better way, I would have taken it. This is Goffin’s final conclusion, and humanity must choose. I’ve also made my choice, and I’ll be trapped in the wall of time forever.”

Shirone thought it wasn’t worth arguing.

“Anchal, release the illusion magic.”

Anchal didn’t move, and Miro got angry.

“Shirone! Think rationally!”

“How can I be more rational here?”

“Do you know what happens if Anke La freely triggers the reset? Our future disappears! All humans will lose their free will and move according to Anke La’s design!”

This time, even Shirone was horrified.

A dictator could control humans, but under such a dictator, there would at least be the freedom to resist.

But in a world ruled by Anke La, they would live forever as his puppets, with even time stolen from them.

“If Anke La loses his weakness, humans won’t even be able to die! He’ll reset and erase the cause!”

Perhaps that was truly the power of a god.

“Do you want that future? A life where you can’t decide to progress, regress, or even stagnate? The moment he becomes a god, humans lose their value as living beings!”

Shirone’s gaze turned cold again.

“That might be true. But that choice is ours to make. Not Goffin’s, but all of ours.”

Miro reacted sensitively.

“Don’t talk like Yolga.”

“What’s wrong with that? Now I understand. Mars might be different, but at least Yolga came here to stop you because you have dangerous thoughts.”

“Dangerous thoughts?”

“There’s no choice. How is this different from Anke La?”

“That’s sophistry. That’s why I said I’d sacrifice myself.”

“Why should you sacrifice yourself?”

“Because I’m human! Humans can’t move forward without someone’s sacrifice! You were born to sacrifice, so why don’t you understand?”

“Sacrifice?”

When Shirone asked, Miro bit her lip.

“If you truly pity this child, humbly accept the sacrifice.”

“I don’t understand.”

Miro sighed.

“Do you know why you must be free from Anke La’s reset? To perfect the dimensional door.”

“What do you mean?”

“…Honestly, I was confused when I found out you were Hexa. Because Hexa is the being that must become the material for the dimensional wall I’ll create.”

As Anchal crossed her arms and listened, Shirone muttered.

“Material…”

“Goffin will leave. And I’ll create the dimensional wall in his place. But with an angel’s power, it will eventually be breached. That’s why you, Hexa, are the solution.”

Hearing this, Shirone recalled the first time he met Miro in her space-time.

“No way…”

“I’ll create another dimension within the dimensional world I made. One that you, unaffected by the reset, will inhabit. Then neither angels nor Anke La can breach the dimensional wall. And humanity will survive forever.”

Creating another dimension within a dimension, endlessly delving deeper.

It was a concept entirely different from the meditation Miro attempted alone, creating a perfect gap.

“It’s infinite because there are two of us. I can’t do it alone.”

“But I’m here.”

Even Miro was puzzled by that.

“Honestly, I don’t know why you still exist in this world.”

“Because you sent me.”

“No. That can’t be. I assure you, I’ll put you in my wall. I have to.”

“But it’s true.”

With the evidence of 19 years later in front of her, there was no way to refute it.

‘I can’t understand. If Hexa didn’t create the wall, how is the world still intact?’

It was before Gaold abandoned the gods.

“Anyway, the threat hasn’t disappeared. That’s why you came to the upper echelons. If you’re willing to sacrifice for this child, become the sacrifice for my wall.”

It was a cold tone, and that was Miro, but…

Let’s keep in touch.

Recalling her words the night before they parted after training, Shirone spoke.

“I can’t accept it. The Miro I know wouldn’t make this choice.”

“No, I know myself. The possibility of changing my mind…”

“Waaah. Waaah.”

Suddenly, the sound of a crying child shook Miro’s gaze.

‘Hexa…’

Shirone felt the same.

A sorrowful story that could only be told through tears.

That story brought to the surface a fact everyone had forgotten until now.

‘This child is also a living being.’

“Who’s making noise at night!”

At Vincent’s voice, Shirone flinched and looked up.

“You wretch! I’ll chop you up!”

Vincent’s appearance as he entered the door was unimaginably young, and tears welled up in Shirone’s eyes.

Seeing the newborn, Vincent hurriedly hid his axe and approached cautiously.

“Waaah. Waaah.”

“Father! Abandon that child!”

Shirone shouted, pounding on the barrier of the illusion, but Anchal’s magic, borrowed from the power of the Magic Eye, was strong.

“Father! That’s not your child! Your son is…!”

It should have been the prince of Kazra.

‘Not me. The prince should have lived.’

He should have grown up loved by good foster parents, Vincent and Olina, and returned to the royal palace.

“Who’s playing tricks! Abandoning a child, you wretch! Come out now!”

“Father…”

They were such parents.

“You really abandoned him! There’s no more chance! Show yourself! I’ll smash your face in!”

Shirone couldn’t tell them to abandon him anymore.

He couldn’t deny the lives of those who gave everything to raise their son.

“Honey, what’s going on?”

As the startled Olina entered the stable, Vincent’s trembling hands held the child.

“What is it, that child?”

At Vincent’s next words, Shirone finally cried.

“It seems… he’s our child.”

Time: 1:13 AM

Location: Warehouse No. 44

“Phew.”

As Lycan opened the door to Warehouse No. 44, the vault designated by Fermi awaited him.

“How much will be gathered here?”

Although he had to take a long detour due to the changing structure of Istas, he ultimately achieved his goal.

‘In this place, cause and effect can’t be distinguished.’

Though an assassin, Lycan was also a wizard.

He had experienced numerous events and knew how the upper echelons currently operated.

‘Three degrees to the left. Sixteen degrees to the right.’

His heart raced like a child opening a gift as he unlocked the vault.

“How long do I have to wait?”

Before casting the stop spell, Fermi had answered Lycan’s question.

“Time isn’t important. The event is. Once you complete the event of placing the ‘Last Scream of the Yeager Family’ in the vault, the past and future will endlessly repeat, accumulating instantly.”

“Until the event of the vault being filled occurs.”

Fermi winked.

“Exactly.”

‘I’m going to make a fortune.’

Expecting the 5 billion gold object to pour out, Lycan positioned himself to receive it as he opened the vault.

“…What?”

In front of his blinking eyes was an empty vault.

“Why?”

Thinking it might be a mistake, he placed the object he had and left Warehouse No. 44, then re-entered.

‘Does the event need to conclude?’

When he reopened the vault and saw that even the object he had placed was gone, his face twisted in rage.

“Event.”

There was only one conclusion.

An event where someone took the items from the vault had already occurred.

“Even tricking me?”

And there was only one person who knew the vault’s secret.

“Fermiiii!”

Blue electricity surged around Lycan’s body.
