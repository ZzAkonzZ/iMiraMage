Chapter 1182:

The coffin carrying Kittra was moved to the secret chamber of the Zodiac Twelve and creaked open.

"Your Majesty."

Kittra sat up.

The reason his heart did not beat was simple—he was already an OOPArt, a being governed by the laws of objects rather than life.

"The time has come."

The endless waves of time distorted the laws inscribed within the pyramids standing across the world.

The Great Adjustment.

The reason it took longer than expected to alter the fundamental concepts within the pyramids was—

"Satiel and Raiel."

Two Archangels maintaining the existing laws at the sun’s core.

"All concepts stem from the Eight Archangels. That means their power is two-eighths of the whole… in other words, one-fourth."

Not insignificant, but far from enough to stop the cosmic shift of the Great Adjustment.

"The time has come."

A fire ignited in Kittra’s eyes as the Great Adjustment mechanism began to spin, fractures forming on its outer shell.

From within, light erupted, only to be swallowed by a pitch-black sphere that emerged from the cracks.

“U-ugh! Aaahhh!”

Everything in the chamber was sucked toward it—furniture, relics, even the members of the Zodiac Twelve.

Rumble… Rumble…

Kittra listened to the gravitational waves, sounds no ordinary human could perceive, as he fixed his gaze on the dark sphere.

'Inside that… lies nothingness.'

Paradoxically, it contained all possibilities.

"O God, descend upon us."

Kittra rose from the altar, spreading his arms.

"Final Adjustment."

The inscriptions within the pyramids across the world began to glow with a brilliant blue light.

As the waves of light accelerated, an eerie phenomenon took place.

A massive electromagnetic field over 80 kilometers in diameter formed around the pyramids.

"Huh? What’s this?"

A man drinking tea in his home suddenly felt a faint current run through him.

"Got goosebumps all of a sudden…"

In that moment, he realized the scenery inside his house was overlapping with entirely different landscapes.

A grand mansion.

A shabby single-room apartment.

A bustling tavern.

Even the interior of a coffin buried deep underground.

'What kind of sorcery is this…?'

The man stood frozen, but only for a moment.

Somehow, he instinctively understood what was happening.

'Depending on my thoughts…'

Everything in the world was changing.

"Honey? What are you doing?"

A woman emerged from the scenery of the grand mansion.

There was nothing different about her compared to reality.

Or rather, his current reality now seemed more like an illusion.

"You… you’re…"

The man remembered her.

His first crush from middle school—the first girl he had ever loved.

"Why that face? Were you thinking something dirty? Haha!"

She laughed, clutching her stomach, exactly as he remembered from childhood.

But he couldn’t understand what was happening.

'What’s going on…?'

Time and space twisted as the laws rewrote themselves, reconstructing the logic of causality.

'Oh, right.'

Ten years after middle school, he had confessed to her at a reunion. That’s how they ended up getting married.

'That’s right… that’s exactly what happened.'

As if he had actually traveled back in time and lived through it again, the memories seeped into his mind.

'Which one is real…?'

And yet, his real wife still remained, like a fading afterimage.

'Like probabilities…'

He wasn’t a mathematician, but he instinctively grasped the concept.

'My real wife is about 60% real… and this wife is about 40%…'

Superimposed upon one another.

"Honey, let’s go shopping. You remember that outfit you wanted? I’ll buy it for you."

As his real wife’s presence faded, the man instinctively shook his head.

'No… I like this wife better.'

Or rather…

A new thought surfaced, and once again, the scenery began to shift.

'The woman I truly loved… was my coworker when I was twenty-six.'

He imagined her beautiful face.

'Why isn’t she here?'

Countless realities overlapped, yet there wasn’t a single trace of a world where he was with her.

'Did she really hate me that much…?'

Even if he wished for it, if she didn’t, then that future could never exist.

'I really loved her, though.'

She had married a far more successful and capable coworker.

'If only I had power… if only I had money…'

Suddenly, he felt himself shift into a version of him that had fought through grueling competition and risen as a successful businessman.

'I can meet her now.'

In the probability-driven sequence of events, she appeared before him, but her response was cold.

"Sir, your meeting is about to start."

The man felt a wave of disappointment.

'That’s it? Just a secretary? Even after all this effort?'

If he couldn’t win her love no matter what, then there was only one method left.

'It’s fine. I can just change it again, right?'

The businessman reached out and kissed her, using his authority.

A moment later, he felt a heavy blow to his head.

She had struck him with a metal trophy from the desk in disgust.

"Ahhh!"

Blood gushed as he collapsed, his consciousness fading.

As his vision dimmed, the scenery disappeared into darkness.

'I'm… dying.'

What he had failed to consider was that this phenomenon wasn’t happening only to him.

"Please, honey, save me."

He called out to the last remaining 1% of his wife’s presence.

She turned around with a smile.

A different man walked up to her and wrapped his arms around her, kissing her tenderly.

"Hic… hhic…"

As his probability of death exceeded 99%, the memories of his children vanished from his mind.

'No… why is this happening…?'

Desperately, he tried to recall his original family, but they had long since moved on to pursue their own desires.

'I have to live. I can’t die.'

At that moment, the darkness lifted, and new choices entered his perception.

But the beautiful landscapes he had seen before would never return.

"Help! Somebody help me!"

Screaming, he jolted upright in a filthy slum dwelling.

"Hahh… Hahh…!"

Everyone had chosen lives aligned with their own desires. There was no place left for him to return to.

'But I’m alive.'

As he looked around, the world was still a shimmering haze of countless overlapping realities.

"Heh… Heheheh…"

Then I won’t lose either.

Even after just a few instances of shifting realities, his humanity had already been fundamentally altered.

"It’s fine. I can change it again. If I set my mind to it, even this wretched life can become paradise."

The laws of the world trembled.

Realizing that he could not read Wizard's mind, Havitz spoke.

"A draw."

It was inevitable—they had no choice but to play the game.

"The opening move is decent. Is it your turn to propose now? Let's hear it."

Wizard remained focused on her objective.

'It worked.'

The key to Zetaro's proposed game was how much Havitz enjoyed it.

'If the kiss was effective…'

This situation, where the truth could never be known, would ultimately drive Satan toward death.

"I…"

Wizard spoke.

"For one day, I will love you."

"And I, for one day, will hate you. The day ends at midnight. That is my proposition."

One of them had to be true.

"The game's rules state that truth must be proven through actions or evidence, correct?" Havitz asked.

"Yes. If it is true that I love you, I will kiss you again."

"And if you hate me?"

"I will break one of my fingers."

A finger wasn’t a life-or-death matter, but Havitz understood immediately.

‘I could die.’

Within the premise of the game, Wizard could perceive his Vanishing.

Conversely, he could not detect Wizard’s super-speed movement—the missing frame.

‘I need at least two frames. But that means I won’t even be able to approach her.’

Facing an awakened Wizard would not be easy.

‘In the end, one of the two will be proven—either she kisses me, or she breaks my finger.’

The warmth of Wizard’s lips still lingered on his.

"I accept."

Wizard's eyes turned cold.

‘As I thought…’

He still doesn't understand just how cruel this game is.

"Then it's my turn. I will kill Shirone. And I will kill Uorin. By midnight, as per your condition."

Because he also had to play the assassination game simultaneously, he had chosen a longer timeframe—but that also made it an extreme gamble.

Wizard thought carefully.

‘The only time he caught onto my thoughts was during yesterday’s battle. I didn’t expect that leaked information to come back like this.’

If Havitz targeted Shirone, Wizard would have no choice but to hate him.

‘It’s a proposition meant to force my emotions into a predictable reaction. He’s desperate to uncover my truth.’

Havitz. Satan.

‘It's almost laughably pure.’

The new variable of Shirone had appeared, but this was all within the predictions of Balkan.

"Alright, I accept."

"Then it's decided. By midnight, we must both prove our claims. If you lose…"

Havitz grinned.

"You can look forward to what I do next."

"Havitz."

Wizard strode forward with purpose.

"I love you."

As Havitz frowned in confusion, she twisted her body with all her might.

'Now it begins.'

Clenching her teeth, she delivered a devastating blow to his side. Havitz’s eyes widened.

“Gahh…?!”

With magic-enhanced strength, even Satan’s body couldn’t absorb the brutal impact.

‘The kiss… the fingers…’

Were just bait to lure Satan into this very moment.

"Havitz."

As the kneeling Havitz slowly lifted his head, Wizard smiled down at him.

"I love you."

Her eyes flashed murderously as her small fist smashed into his jaw.

"Argh!"

His head snapped to the side, and he skidded across the floor, catching himself on his shoulder. He turned to look at Wizard beyond the pain.

She approached.

‘Yes, you’ll never be able to read me.’

As long as the kiss and the broken finger were the only limitations, any action within the game was fair game.

‘But you’re already caught in the system.’

Balkan had said this:

"An entity beyond the laws—Hell’s Prison is annoying, but Satan’s greatest weapon is Vanishing. If we neutralize that, he will never be truly invincible. Of course, this only works because Wizard exists."

"And if it doesn’t work?"

"It will."

Balkan smirked.

"Satan. That pure chaos. Humanity never knew what to do with a concept that walked into reality. But now, it’s different. Too many have died to understand Havitz."

Even if they failed before.

"Abella, whom Havitz loved. Jaestin, who despised Havitz. The assassination attempts during the Great War. All that information now defines him."

It wasn’t meaningless sacrifice.

"Humans fail more than any other species. But through failure, they eventually find the solution. And this time…"

Balkan had said:

"How about we try this?"

Havitz coughed up blood.

"Ugh…!"

Even as Wizard closed in, his Vanishing failed to activate.

Because this was still a game.

"Why…?"

Leaning against the wall, Havitz looked at Wizard with genuine confusion.

"Who knows?"

Wizard smiled sweetly, her eyes glinting with malice.

"You’ll have to figure that out yourself. That’s what makes it a game."

Panting, Havitz staggered away down the corridor.

"Why are you running? You like me, don’t you?"

"Hell’s Prison."

Twelve shadows emerged on the floor, and in an instant, Havitz vanished.

‘Did I push too hard?’

No. He would definitely come back for her.

"Where did you go?"

In this absurdly playful killing game, Wizard hypnotized herself.

"Come play with me, Havitz."
