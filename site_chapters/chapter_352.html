Chapter 352: Swordsmans Conviction (4)

‘Is that the skill of a swordsman who just turned twenty?’

Kuan was deeply impressed by Rai's swordsmanship.

It was not only precise but also exquisite. If the Ozent family had two lineages, this was undoubtedly the era of the black-haired lineage.

“Huff! Huff!”

Rian gasped for air, supporting the greatsword with his trembling arms. It was the first time the greatsword, which he had swung every day for a year except during meals, felt so heavy.

‘Lift it. Lift it.’

Rai's eyes twitched. The greatsword, which he was pressing down with his black sword, was slowly rising.

‘He still has strength left?’

The shockwave from the sword vibration should have left him powerless.

Rai tried to press down harder, but the immense force pushing him back made it seem impossible to hold down.

Sensing something ominous, Rai withdrew his sword and created distance.

At the same time, Rian swung his sword and stood up. He tore off his tattered shirt, revealing his bulging muscles.

Rai immediately recognized Rian’s characteristic.

‘His recovery is incredible.’

To elevate divine transcendence, which surpasses physical limits, to the level of a technique required unimaginable hardship. It was clear how much grueling training Rian had undergone to increase his feedback.

“I acknowledge that you did your best. But I am the one who will lead the family.”

Rai used external force to move to Rian’s side and swung his black sword at Rian’s unprotected right arm.

In a normal situation, Rian should have taken a defensive stance.

However, Rian instead opened his ribs and raised his greatsword vertically.

‘What?’

In that split second, Rai's thoughts raced.

Was he sacrificing flesh to take the bone? Or was the best defense a good offense?

Neither made sense. The greatsword's movements were inherently slower than a light sword.

Then, Rian’s greatsword accelerated inexplicably, creating afterimages as it dropped towards Rai's forehead.

Rai twisted his body, creating a shockwave. He slid in a semicircle, following the external force, and escaped the radius.

But Rian’s greatsword followed, roaring through the air.

‘Damn it! What is this?’

Rai narrowly dodged, feeling a chill down his spine. He had almost lost his head.

Reina, watching the battle, anxiously stomped her feet.

As the emotions of the two clashed, the intensity of their killing intent increased. At this rate, one of the brothers might end up dead.

“This can't go on! We need to stop the duel.”

“We can't. Neither Rian nor Rai would want that.”

Reina looked at Tess with a mix of disappointment and concern.

“Tess, no matter how much you want to protect Rian, this isn't right. Those two are fighting to kill each other. You should have stopped Rian from the start.”

“Rai isn't fighting out of hatred.”

Tess vividly remembered the night she brought a late-night snack to Rian’s dorm at the swordsmanship school and heard the terrible groans through the door.

“Rian trained to surpass his limits every day. And at night, he suffered in pain. He would bite on a cloth to endure the agony that felt like his body was breaking apart.”

Reina couldn't say anything to Tess, who looked like she was about to cry.

Even though they were family, she couldn't understand the pain Rian had endured. Only those who had chosen the path of the sword like Rian could understand him.

Rai's kick struck Rian’s solar plexus. Rian, who had been lifted into the air, rolled on the ground and knelt to regain his balance. He had been hit in a vital spot and was visibly struggling.

The cold aura had disappeared from Rai's face.

As time passed, he felt Rian’s defense solidifying. It was because Rai was getting emotionally involved. He found it frustrating.

“You idiot! Why don't you realize you're no match for me? Stop being so stubborn!”

Rian slowly stood up. He aimed at Rai in the same stance as before he fell.

“Come at me.”

“You little...!”

Rai's eyes flared with killing intent. The ominous aura he emitted reached even those standing on the outskirts of the training ground.

Rai's full-power movements were ghostly. His fast and precise swordsmanship left more cuts on Rian’s body, gradually slowing his movements.

The difference in skill was clear. No matter how hard he tried, there was a gap in talent that couldn't be bridged. But the clearer that gap became, the angrier Rai got.

It was foolish for the family to place their hopes on someone like Rian. Rian had no future. Swordsmanship without technique was just a display of brute strength.

“You fool!”

With a precise technique, Rai deflected the greatsword and delivered a spinning kick, causing Rian’s head to spin like a top.

For a moment, it felt like his neck had broken. His body spun and fell with a thud.

Rai, breathing heavily, glared at Rian. Even his collapsed form looked pathetic. With a direct hit to the chin, Rian would likely be unconscious until the next day.

“Heh.”

At that moment, Rian’s chest heaved. Slowly lifting his head, Rian spoke with a torn smile.

“That tickles. Do you think you can kill me with that?”

Rai was stunned by Rian’sresilience, getting up like a zombie no matter how much he was beaten. But he quickly looked at his brother with disdain and spoke coldly.

“Kill you? Do you think you're worth killing?”

Rian’s eyes widened.

“Why kill Grandfather's precious youngest grandson? There's no need to kill someone like you. I'll just beat you until you lose consciousness. Got it? You're just the youngest of a swordsmanship family with no skill or talent. You're a useless idiot, you fool!”

“Yeah!”

Rian shouted, curling his body.

“I am a useless idiot!”

Rian charged, swinging his sword. It was an attack that would make anyone with a weak heart hesitate just from the sound of the wind.

Rai hurriedly raised his black sword. Bang! The greatsword struck, sending shockwaves down to his knees.

Rian pressed his face close, their swords locked.

Seeing Rian’s torn smile reaching his ears, Rai wondered if his brother was sane.

Should he continue like this? Should he call a doctor?

“Mock me all you want. I've heard it all my life. Call me pathetic, call me a fool. Scorn me, despise me, curse me. But I will...!”

Rian pulled back his greatsword and took a full swing stance.

“Never stop!”

The greatsword seemed to grow tenfold in Rai's mind. The overwhelming strike shocked his brain, scrambling his perception of the sword's path.

“Yaaah!”

As Rian shouted, Rai's gaze dropped.

‘Ankle!’

Realizing the sword's trajectory too late, Rai jumped.

The greatsword dug into the ground, lifting a wide sheet of earth.

A black line raced through the center of the sheet. With a tearing sound, it split in half, and Rai burst through the dirt.

But waiting for him was another upward swing from Rian’s greatsword.

The unexpected follow-up attack made Rai's heart race. As he arched his body, he felt the wind graze his chin. His hair stood on end.

“I hear nothing! Even if everyone mocks me, I will only move forward!”

Rian pushed Rai relentlessly, venting all his pent-up frustration.

For the first time since the duel began, Rai was being pushed back. Even Kuan and the family showed signs of surprise.

‘Rian...’

Reina's eyes filled with pity.

He was a child who had grown up constantly compared to his older brother, who was two years older. Honestly, she didn't think she could beat Rai even if she wielded a sword. Perhaps she had used music as an excuse to pass the burden of the blue-haired lineage onto Rian.

“The blue-haired lineage has always had many troublemakers.”

Reina pouted and looked at Clump.

“The black-haired lineage always does its part. They're rational and cold. But the blue-haired lineage often has unpredictable personalities. They're stubborn and single-minded. That's why when a blue-haired person becomes the head of the family, it's considered a gamble.”

“What are you talking about all of a sudden? You're blue-haired too, Grandfather.”

“Heh, indeed, the existence of troublemaking blue-haired individuals is one reason the Ozent family is limited to the second rank. But looking back at the family's history, it was the blue-haired lineage that made the Ozent name known as a family of swordsmen.”

Clump gazed intently at Rian, who was swinging the heavy greatsword like a storm.

“They don't take detours. They don't look back. Even if they break upon impact, they push through. That's the blue-haired conviction. I don't know how far Rian will go. But when he finally looks back from where he arrives, his path will be a straight line without deviation.”

“Here I come!”

As Rian charged with an all-out offensive stance, Rai didn't dare clash swords.

It didn't feel like he was facing a person. He couldn't shake the feeling that something absolute was lurking behind Rian.

Divine transcendence? The power of the mind?’

Rai's eyes blazed.

“Don't make me laugh!”

Rai launched himself, meeting Rian’s sword head-on. Their swords clashed, sparking in the center of their gazes.

The immense shock transmitted through the hilt brought Rai back to his senses. He realized he had been too excited.

But by then, his senses had already left his grasp.

‘What is this? There's a limit to being reckless.’

As Rai quickly retreated, Rian pounced like a wolf, closing the distance unnaturally, as if defying normal inertia.

When the greatsword came close, Rai twisted his ankle completely and spun, simultaneously striking Rian’s Achilles tendon with the opposite foot.

“Ugh!”

Rai, twisting like a dragon, rose and slashed at Rian’s back with his black sword. It was a move executed in a state of no-mind, where life and death were no longer considered.

“Rian!”

At Tess's shout, Rian twisted his upper body.

In an abnormal posture, he swung the greatsword with one hand, deflecting the black sword and sending Rai's arms flying behind him.

“Wow...”

An involuntary exclamation escaped Rai's lips.

It was a completely unbalanced stance. Yet, Rian had deflected a vertical slash with all his weight behind it using one hand.

‘No, this isn't just that level.’

The law of action and reaction didn't apply to Rian’s sword. An absolute force that defied physical laws was embedded in each of Rian’s strikes.

“Hoo.”

Shirone exhaled the breath he had been holding. Watching Rian overpower Rai made his heart burn with excitement, even though he wasn't a swordsman.

“Tess.”

Tess, who turned to Shirone, had a face pale with tension.

“Rian has really become strong.”

Tess finally shed tears at Shirone's acknowledgment. She knew who Rian had endured all his hardships for.

Unable to speak, she nodded repeatedly.

“Yaaah!”

Despite the pain that felt like his body was breaking, Rian swung his sword. Now, Rai no longer retreated and parried all of Rian’s attacks.

If it was about surpassing physical limits, he wouldn't last long. He just had to block this one time. No, just one more time...

Rai was getting frustrated.

‘Damn it! When will this end?’

Suddenly, a thought flashed through his mind.

‘Ah...’

Rai finally realized what kind of trap he had fallen into.

‘This guy is an idiot.’

At the same time, Rian’s greatsword struck the black sword.

With a clanging sound, pain shot through Rai's finger joints. His muscles cramped, his hand opened, and the black sword spun into the air.
