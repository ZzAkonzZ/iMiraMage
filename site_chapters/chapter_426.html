Chapter 426:

The Plateau of Corruption

A barren plain where the wind carried a strong sulfur smell.

The desolate land had no vegetation, and dark clouds stretched to the horizon, spitting lightning.

It was a dry, eerie, and empty place.

The 3rd Special Mobile Unit descended on beams of light, landing with a force unlike any Elizer they had experienced before.

Feeling as if he had been thrown by an uncontrollable force, Shirone rolled on the ground.

"Ouch, my head. Are you okay, senior?"

Shirone's eyes widened as he took in the surroundings.

Eight rectangular panels, each 4 meters high, were erected in all directions. Beyond the panels, 2-meter-long spikes were densely packed as far as the eye could see.

When lightning struck, powerful electricity flowed through the panels, causing an explosion of light inside the area they enclosed.

"This is…"

A team member spoke.

"This isn't a natural area where essence gathers. It's a device designed to collect light artificially."

"Was it made by the Mecha tribe?"

"No one knows who made it. It existed before the citizens lived in Heaven. Only the angels know what happened in this forsaken land."

Thunder struck again.

As the lightning split into thousands of branches and was absorbed by the ground, the earth glowed a bluish hue, like swarming insects.

When a powerful white light burst from the panels, Shirone squinted and gave an order.

"Let's move away. It could be dangerous to stay here."

The 3rd Special Mobile Unit avoided the lightning zone, dodging thousands of spikes.

Although they all had the ability to fly, the air was dangerous in such an environment.

They arrived at a crater 4 kilometers from the light essence.

It looked as if something had crashed at a terrifying speed, leaving a deep depression in the ground.

Sweeping the ground at the center revealed a metal plate, indicating something was buried deep underground.

"So this is why it's called the Tomb of Angels."

"Yes. For centuries, many heretics have tried to excavate this site, but all efforts were in vain."

A team member pointed around.

"Some even tried to excavate the entire area, but anyone who dug too deep was electrocuted. Records say hundreds were killed."

"So we need to break this seal."

Shirone examined the metal plate closely.

Though the form was different, it was clearly written in the angels' language, Hena.

As a Nephilim, Shirone might be able to open it. In fact, he probably could.

Shirone looked at Plu.

Entering the Immortal Function wasn't an issue, but from here on, it was uncharted territory.

"Let's open it. If it gets dangerous, we can leave immediately."

Plu wanted to go in.

The fact that heretics had risked their lives for centuries to excavate this place meant there was something of great value buried here.

Moreover, Shirone had Mass Teleport, allowing them to leave quickly if needed.

"Alright, let's start."

As Shirone placed his hand on the metal plate and activated the Immortal Function, the Hena script glowed, and a massive beam of light shot skyward.

As the team members stepped back, a deep rumbling from underground vibrated through their feet.

"Damn! Let's move back."

At a team member's stern suggestion, Shirone quickly exited the crater.

Rumble!

As the metal plate vibrated, a vast amount of soil poured down, and something began to rise.

"What is that?"

The 3rd Special Mobile Unit watched as a massive bell-shaped structure emerged from the ground, covered in Hena script on its sturdy metal surface.

The 3-meter-high structure split open at the bottom, revealing an entrance.

After waiting for a while and seeing no immediate danger, Shirone leaped to the entrance.

Above the dark entrance, letters were engraved on a metal plate.

Since it wasn't Hena, Shirone sent a drone to decode it. The translation was transmitted to his mind.

Babel.

"Ba…bel?"

The team members murmured simultaneously.

Only that word was in the citizens' language, not the angels'.

Plu turned to the team members and asked, "Babel. What does it mean?"

"We don't know. It's in the citizens' language, but we've never heard it before."

It wasn't particularly helpful information.

"Shirone, what now?"

"Let's go in. Our goal is to explore, after all."

Leading the way, Shirone cast Shining Magic and entered the structure.

Stairs led downward, and as they descended, a corridor stretched out before them.

Lights installed at 1-meter intervals lit up as they stepped forward, extending into the darkness.

Shirone stared in awe.

It stretched endlessly. Judging by the distance, the structure above ground was just the tip of the iceberg.

They arrived at a circular space, with a metal plate above the entrance inscribed with the citizens' language: Control Room.

"Hmm, Control Room. At least we can understand that."

Despite the team member's joke, no one laughed as they entered.

Lights flickered on, revealing devices connected by wires, reminiscent of those seen in the Great World War.

Beyond the devices was a massive conical pillar. A similar pillar descended from the ceiling, with an electric mass seemingly trapped between their tips.

"That…!"

The team members were speechless with shock.

The flickering light, unable to maintain a consistent form, was undoubtedly an electric spirit, known to be impossible to collect in its pure natural state.

Plu, observing from a distance, stroked her chin.

"Hmm, what is that? The power source for this place?"

'Foolish. It's not something so trivial.'

The team members all silently mocked Plu's ignorance.

What they were seeing was the result of the unique characteristics of the Plateau of Corruption, a device that collected lightning on a massive scale, and the passage of time, creating the rarest spirit in purgatory.

The team members exchanged cautious glances.

Due to the nature of their unit, often dispatched to remote areas, they had their own regulations.

If they acquired a special item during a mission, the highest-ranking member would decide its disposition, and rewards would be distributed based on mission performance.

While such rules were rarely followed in the field, there was no better way to maintain order.

'That's mine.'

All the team members had the same thought.

They hadn't even properly started the mission, so according to the rules, they would have to hand over the precious electric spirit to Shirone.

But the electric spirit was a once-in-a-lifetime, or even a once-in-a-rebellion, opportunity.

"Let's check it out first!"

Muttering a flimsy excuse, a team member dashed forward.

"Wait! Don't approach recklessly…!"

Before Plu could finish, the team member reached out to contract with the electric spirit.

'Got it. With this, I…!'

Had his heart ever raced this fast?

The overwhelming anticipation was electrifying.

"I'll contract with it!"

As he shouted, spheres shot out from the walls.

The man instinctively covered his face as the spheres struck with a series of loud pops, shaking his body.

"Ugh…!"

Shirone looked around in shock.

The steel walls were pitted with as many indentations as there were spheres.

Yet, none of the spheres had fallen around the man.

They had shot out from the walls and embedded themselves in the opposite wall.

The lack of deviation in their trajectories indicated the immense force behind the spheres.

'Couldn't even react. They all pierced through.'

Thud! The man's knees hit the floor.

As his arms fell limply, his face revealed holes matching the wall's indentations.

Everyone heightened their vigilance.

Mourning their comrade's death would have to wait until they survived and left this place. For now, they focused on the immediate situation.

"It said Control Room. Why are there traps?"

"They're not traps."

Thunk! Thunk! Thunk! Thunk!

As Shirone finished speaking, the control room's walls began to detach sequentially.

They weren't traps. The earlier attack was just a procedure to remove the fixed steel doors.

Inside the compartmentalized walls were 2-meter-tall steel golems, standing like corpses in coffins.

Their recessed eyes lit up with red light as they grabbed the walls and stepped out.

"Those who approach Babel will face divine wrath."

Two team members prepared a group spell.

"We'll annihilate them with Gardin."

Though only effective in special conditions like lightning storms, Gardin was known as one of the most destructive group spells.

Even steel golems could be fought in a place where an electric spirit was present.

"Stand back! We're going to incinerate them!"

Despite their words, the team members extended their hands forward without giving Shirone and Plu time to retreat.

"Here we go! Gardin!"

Shirone flinched and stopped, while Plu looked at the team members in confusion.

Despite their grand declaration, no magic was cast from their hands.

"What? Why isn't the essence…?"

Group magic was impossible without precise Spirit Force connection. Yet, not a single spark of electricity gathered.

Shirone muttered.

"It's not an electric spirit."

"Eliminate those who approach Babel."

The golems, speaking in mechanical voices, scattered to target different individuals.

"Fight! Everyone, prepare!"

Plu drew Phoenix and struck the ground, casting Phoenix Fire.

Fiery blasts spread in all directions, striking the golems, but their metal bodies remained unscathed.

In the confined space, the Nor warriors could only dodge the golems' attacks with basic wind magic.

"Shirone! Destroy them with lasers!"

Plu called for support, but Shirone remained frozen in place.

"Shirone! What are you doing? It's dangerous!"

Five golems marched forward, shaking the ground.

As a massive fist flew towards Shirone, a shadow crossed his face.

Boom!

The ground shook.

It wasn't the vibration of the control room but the tremor caused by forty golems halting their movements.

Shirone looked up at the golem's fist, which had stopped right in front of him.

The golem's red eyes flickered rapidly, as if calculating something.

Target category: Human.

Attribute: Nephilim.

Code search: Iris pattern recognition.

Data search: Phase 1. Complete. Phase 2. Complete.

Result…

Result…

Result…

The golem's vast amount of stored data was thoroughly searched, leading to a single conclusion.

Thud! Thud! Thud! Thud!

All the golems simultaneously extinguished their red lights and collapsed forward.

The weight was so immense that the room shook as if the structure might collapse.

"What happened? Why did they suddenly…?"

The team members, still tense, couldn't move, while Plu, having retracted her Phoenix Fire, approached Shirone.

"What happened? Why did the golems fall?"

"I don't know."

"You don't know? Then why didn't you attack?"

Shirone was pondering the same thing. Why hadn't he felt threatened by the golems?

'Just like that time…'

When Behemoth merged with his avatar, the physical memories disappeared, but the integrated feeling remained. It was the same now. An emotion, not language, conveyed the situation.

"Senior, I don't know why, but…"

Shirone turned to Plu with a dazed expression.

"I feel like I've been here before."
