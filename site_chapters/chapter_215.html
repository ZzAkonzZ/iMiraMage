Chapter 215: Heaven Commotion (2)

Kwaang!

The moment Ymir's straight punch struck, only the greatsword remained, and Rian's figure disappeared.

All sorts of thoughts were rushing through Rian's head as he flew rapidly. To block any additional attacks, he needed to regain his balance and land properly. He barely able to keep his balance. It felt like he could land on both legs.

But what he realized the next moment was that his body was rolling on the floor crumpled.

As the shock in his body subsided, he felt the cold floor against his cheek.

An unintelligible sound leaked out of his mouth. He wasn't even in pain, but he couldn't muster any strength.

Ymir observed the sprawled Rian and picked up the greatsword that had fallen to the ground. Though it was a greatsword for a human, in Ymir's hands, it looked like a common longsword.

Ymir's eyes gleamed as he inspected the blade of the greatsword.

“Hooh? This could be quite useful.”

"G- Give it back. That's my... grandfather's..."

Rian, trembling, got to his feet.

Thanks to training his body to the limit every day, he recovered from the shock much faster than an average person. This was what one might call endurance.

Ymir grinned and threw the greatsword. It slid across the floor and reached Rian.

"Want to try again? I like your determination."

Rian picked up the greatsword with difficulty and charged again. The gap in strength was clear, ut if Ymir was heading to the Great Hall of World, he had no choice but to fight.

Ymir and Rian clashed in the middle of the hallway. Ymir didn't even bother to block the greatsword.

Clang! The greatsword struck Ymir's forehead. No, it would be more accurate to say that he blocked it with his forehead.

Ymir's fist flew in a wide arc.

Rian realized he couldn't dodge it. Realizing that fact, Rian opened his eyes wide. If he had to die, he wanted to fight until the very end.

Ymir felt a thrill from Rian's gaze.

'It's a shame I don't know your name.'

Ymir's fist created a gust of wind as it cut through the spot where Rian had been.

There was silence for a while.

Ymir's expression turned displeased. Is there anything more unpleasant than an intruder interfering in the middle of a battle?

It was a strange yet familiar phenomenon.

Just before his fist connected, he clearly saw Rian split into dozens of squares.

“Really… They say that high-ranking beings don’t know how to ride the wave.” (“하여튼…… 높은 것들은 분위기를 탈 줄 모른다니까.”)

About three meters away from where Rian was, square glass panel flickered and the images of two people were projected.

The handsome man with black hair was Ashur. And next to him, Rian was kneeling.

Ymir smiled coldly. The mere fact that Mara, known for her heavy buttocks, had come from Heaven was enough to suggest that something unusual was happening in the Great Hall of World.

"Yoo, a busy man is here. Where's your beloved that you're wandering the battlefield alone?"

"Ymir, not following the law will bring bad consequences. Leave now."

“Haha, what if I don’t abide?”

“There is no choice but to annihilate your existence."

As Ashur spread his palm, about 30 glass panels formed three rows in the air. A long, thin longsword emitting a purple glow was projected there.

“Is it a signal?”

Ashur's ability, Signal, collected all kinds of signals.

Each glass plate contains one signal, and by combining the patterns on the panels, a nearly infinite number of phenomena could manifest.

Ashur's longsword, Shockwave, was also created by receiving 30 different signals.

Due to the electromagnetic pulse, it vibrated on its own, making it a highly lethal weapon that could devastate one’s inside once penetrate.

Rian looked up at Ashur blankly. It was as if he had died and been reborn, with the middle process omitted.

“Who are you?”

"Go. I'm an ally."

Though the words were brief, Rian understood immediately. It was clear that Shirone had created some kind of variable.

Rian glared at Ymir, but he hadn't even looked at Rian ever since Ashur had appeared.

Biting his lip, Rian ran towards the Great Hall of World.

His insides boiled with anger. He hadn't been raised so noble that he feared defeat. But the frustation that he had not succeeded in even one attack was painful.

He could sense Tess's drone following him. Although the machine remained silent, he could hear her comforting voice as if it were an auditory hallucination.

"Damn it! Damn it!"

Rian sprinted with all his might towards the Great Hall of World.

@

Ashur was still blocking the way. He wanted to follow Rian to the Great Hall of World, but for now, stopping Ymir was the priority.

Using Signal's ability 'Transmission,' he could send a signal to his desired destination and instantly move there, but this was impossible with the Great Hall of World.

Since the place was coated with electromagnetic waves, it was faster to run than to wait for the signal to be transmitted.

That was also the reason why he sent Rian first.

"Stand down, Ymir. I will not tolerate any more indulgence.”

Ymir said, stroking his lower jaw.

“It seems like something important has happened since you even left your beautiful angel behind. Why don't you let me in on it?"

“It’s none of your business.”

"Why do I feel like it does?"

Ashur did not deny it.

The King of Giants, Ymir, sought only battle. Having been stripped of his body simply for being strong, he was waiting for a fierce battle that could only be won by fighting with the power of his true body.

On the other hand, Ikael was of the opinion that war must be prevented.

If Ikael wishes, Ashur will follow.

As long as he stepped in, nothing would happen to Heaven. Thus, there was no reason for Ymir to be interested.

Ashur aimed his Shockwave at Ymir's solar plexus.

“By the command of Archangel Ikael-nim, I hereby deprive you of all rights given."

“Kekeke, did a lowly angel have that kind of authority?”

Ashur lunged like a whirlwind.

Ymir's forearms, crossed in defense, split open, spraying blood. The vibrations of the electromagnetic waves burned his bones fiercely.

Ashur glared at Ymir with a look of disdain.

"A mere giant dares to insult an archangel? You are nothing but flesh. Do not defile a noble spirit with your disgusting tongue."

“A mere giant?”

One of Ymir's eyebrows twitched.

“I am Ymir.”

Sensing the murderous intent, Ashur increased the distance between them. However, Ymir closed the 10-meter gap in a single leap.

Ymir's powerful straight pierced Ashur's abdomen.

“Ugh!”

Shock filled Ashur's eyes.

His form, frozen in time, began to blur, and the 180 or so glass panels containing signals flickered and disappeared.

Ymir turned around with an annoyed expression.

Ashur, having completed his transmission to a new space, stood there with an elegant demeanor, his Shockwave sword extended.

“Kuku, aren't you overexerting yourself? I heard your power is severely restricted."

“I am not in a position to be pitied. You should worry about keeping your own body intact."

“Is that so?”

"Ice Hell."

The space surrounding Ashur and Ymir was surrounded by glass panels.

About 2,400 glass panels flickered as they began collecting signals of extreme heat and cold. The sight of fire and ice alternating every 10 centimeters was dizzying.

What will happen here when all the signals are transmitted? Would it burn to ashes? Or freeze solid?

"This is what you brought upon yourself. Don't blame me."

"I blame none. For I am the strongest."

Ymir charged forward, baring his teeth like a wild beast. In response, Ashur raised his cold eyes and swung his Shockwave.

As the transmission completed, a storm of heat and cold raged simultaneously.

@

"Tess! Just wait a bit longer! I'm almost there!"

Rian no longer conserved his oxygen. After running for 10 minutes since parting with Ashur, he finally reached the bridge leading to the Great Hall of World.

The bridge was 1 kilometer long and 60 meters wide. There was no guard fence, so it felt like he was floating in the sky. When the strong wind blew, his lower abdomen churn for no reason.

At the end of the bridge stood a dome-shaped structure. It was the Great Hall of World where his friends were being held.

It was good news that the building was visible, because it meant that Canis had disabled the Meca System.

“What, you’re still here?”

When he looked back, Canis was approaching.

"How come you're already here?"

“I came through the portal. But what's with your face? You look like you got beaten up."

Rian touched his face. He hadn't even noticed the swelling from rolling on the ground, feeling no pain. He looked up at the drone with a sense of guilt.

'You must have been worried, Tess.'

Canis also looked at it. He could tell without even hearing the explanation that it was Tess's.

It was definitely a good thing that she was able to send the drone flying even when she was being captured by an angel.

"What's the situation inside? You've checked, right?"

“Yeah. It seems okay so far. But….”

Suddenly, the drone became strange. It flew erratically, then dropped as if it had lost power.

Rian hurriedly caught it and inspected it.

The operating light was off. Since it was connected to Tess's brainwaves, it was evidence that something had happened to her.

"What the? What's going on all of a sudden?"

“I don’t know! There's no time!"

Rian leaped first. If it was Dark Port, they could get there in one go, but the bridge in the sky was entirely within the realm of light.

Canis, who grabbed Rian, swallowed his pride and cast Photon Magic.

A streak of light carrying the two of them raced across the bridge.

@

“This punk!”

Amy shouted, glaring at Kariel. Tess and Arin's faces were also twisted with anger.

As information from the panel implanted in their heads infiltrated their minds, it felt as if they were drunk, making their heads spin.

"Don't worry. Your hormones are being adjusted to a state suitable for the Elixir of Life."

Kariel, who had been indifferently observing the women's condition, turned his gaze to the screen.

“Nothing really changes yet. It will be different after the Elixir of Life is completed. But it doesn't matter, does it? Any living being must experience this once."

“Shut up! Who are you deciding that! Do you think we'll just do whatever you want? I'd rather bite my tongue and die than go through this!"

Kariel frowned as if he had seen something disgusting.

"I can't understand. You're not being punished. You've been chosen for the noble task of bearing a Nephilim. There haven't been many women who refused the Elixir of Life. Do you not wish to attain eternal life?”

“I don’t want eternal life! Even if I die tomorrow, I'll live my life the way I want!"

"There's no reasoning with you. In any case, nothing will change. Once all the information contained in the panel is transmitted, it can’t be undone.”

- Elixir of Life progress: 20 percent.

A female's voice was heard from the ceiling. It seemed to provide updates every 10 percent. Hearing the emotionless voice from their perspective made it even more eerie.

Amy struggled to escape from the handcuffs.

If only she could free her hands, she intended to grab and rip out the panel implanted in her forehead.

Even if it cost her life, she would live on her own terms.

“Tess! Amy! Arin!”

The door to the Great Hall of World burst open. Rian and Canis entered simultaneously as if racing each other.

Tess, who had been watching them cross the bridge, shouted.

"Rian! Over here! Quickly, help us out of this!"

Rian flinched as he took a step forward. He was overwhelmed by the scale of the Great Hall of World that was beyond his imagination.

It wasn't just the scale but also the unfamiliar objects and equipment.

On one side, a planet was rotating, while on the opposite side, a tower was dazzling with all kinds of lights.

"Damn it! What is this place?"

Kariel flew up to a height where he could see the humans all at once.

"Did you follow the women here? This too must be the foolishness of an ignorant human being.”

"Are you the one responsible? Don't think you'll get away with what you've done to my friends."

Rian raised his greatsword, remaining highly cautious.

In contrast to his strong word, his actions were careful. Having witnessed the power of the Fallen Angel Ikasa, Rian could infer the might of an archangel.
