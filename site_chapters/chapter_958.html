Chapter 958: The Right Choice (1)

The commanders of the allied forces were masters in their fields, but that alone couldn't secure victory.

The demons were strong not only because of their physical power but also due to their different bodily organs, constitution, and mental abilities compared to humans.

They could reflect physical force, manipulate elements, or destroy minds like Igor, the Lord of Terror.

The reason why the former First Legion Commander, the Great Swordmaster Fidero, died was likely due to extreme mismatches in attributes.

In the end, anyone could die in the battle of the flower field.

"Advance! Advance!"

Despite the shouts, the allied forces were continuously pushed back.

And finally,

"Ugh!"

The back of a soldier fighting at the front collided with the shield of a soldier at the rear.

With their lives on the line, the anger in their eyes quickly turned to despair.

"Already..."

It was the end of the flower field.

When they finally widened their view, there was nothing left standing among the three hundred giant structures.

'We were pushed back this far?'

The flower field, planted with 300 giant structures, was vast, spanning 23 kilometers in a straight line.

Lupist gritted his teeth.

'Damn it...'

Changing the course of the demon army was nothing like turning the head of a running horse.

The allied forces were always surrounded by demons and had to generate rotational force within that.

In terms of power difference, it was like moving water currents below the surface to rotate an entire lake.

'Even so...'

The allied forces had managed to turn the demon army's course by 14 degrees compared to when they first entered.

'According to the Holy War's predictions, if this much inertia is applied, they should naturally change direction.'

This strategy was possible because the demons' behavior was different from that of human armies.

'Humans use war as a means to achieve a purpose...'

For demons, war itself was the purpose.

They simply charged towards places where they could torment humans.

'Therefore, the fact that inertia isn't working means human-like will is at play.'

This was also related to why the legion commanders were not included in the demon army.

Lupist unfolded a map in his mind.

'If they continue straight... it's still not enough.'

No matter how large the army, they would pass by Bashka.

'We have to do something here.'

Lupist shouted.

"Everyone!"

But seeing the soldiers' faces, Lupist couldn't bring himself to say "charge."

Despair was evident in everyone's eyes.

"The flower field has been occupied."

The power of the flower field was undeniable, and the soldiers had relied on it psychologically.

"It's over. We can't win now."

Seeing the soldiers who had lost their will to fight, Lupist couldn't push them any further.

It wasn't because he was soft-hearted.

'They fought really well. They couldn't have done better.'

Once he understood this, even the cold-hearted Lupist couldn't do anything.

"Everyone, retreat."

The unexpected order shocked the soldiers.

"Commander."

"Retreat. I'll handle this. Go back to Bashka and plan for the future. You'll be needed."

A silence fell.

"We're sorry, Commander."

And as if on cue, everyone lit up their eyes and pointed their weapons forward.

As the demons surrounding them chuckled, Lupist turned his head.

"What are you doing?"

"Well, we do want to live, but..."

The centurion laughed.

"We don't feel like running away. Our hearts aren't moving."

Having fought together, no more words were needed.

"This can't be right, can it?"

That one sentence was enough, and Lupist glared at the enemy with the most terrifying look.

"...That's right."

The allied forces, focusing their last remaining will, prepared to charge.

"Everyone!"

Now he could say it.

"Charge!"

As if their souls were pulling them, the allied forces advanced, forgetting how to run.

"Hahaha! The final feast!"

As the demons swarmed in, imagining the humans they would soon devour, their attention was suddenly drawn to the sky behind the allied forces.

"Huh?"

Without anyone saying a word, their gazes turned to the sky behind the allied forces.

"What, what is that?"

A towering avatar of Avalokiteshvara, reaching endlessly into the sky, looked down at them benevolently.

"Prajnaparamita."

The hands of the avatar, clasped together, suddenly opened.

"Paradise Palm."

In an instant, countless afterimages filled the sky, spreading like a fan.

The two palms, circling the ends of the world, collided where the demons were.

Countless afterimages converged into a single point, and all the impact exploded at once.

"Argh!"

The seemingly impenetrable wall of demons was torn apart without limit, and Lupist shuddered.

'Cognitive dissonance.'

Every process was vivid in his mind, but looking back, it was astonishingly instantaneous.

A path opened from the rear of the allied forces, and the formation split in two.

"Why are you so surprised? Never seen a pretty woman before?"

Lupist realized.

'Yes, we still have that.'

An ambiguous figure to call human, the strongest defender of humanity and the ultimate good of the era.

"Adrias Miro."

Enjoying the attention of the allied forces, Miro shrugged as she approached Lupist.

"If you're interested, contact me. I'm currently single."

As Lupist watched, Dante approached and pointed.

"You're too late. The other leaders of Zion are already carrying out operations elsewhere."

"You brat!"

Before he finished speaking, Miro placed her fist on Dante's cheek and turned it sharply.

"Ouch!"

It didn't hurt, but the sheer absurdity of it left him stunned.

"How dare you compare me to those idiots? Don't you know Adrias Miro? I'm a busy woman."

Dante's eyes lit up.

"It was your mission. And they risked their lives on difficult tasks."

"Difficult tasks? No, you should call them impossible tasks."

"...What do you mean?"

"Hehe. Kid, you act smart, but listen carefully. This sister will tell you."

Miro placed her hand on Dante's head and smiled.

"No matter what you think, the world is much deeper and more complex. Everything I do has a profound meaning."

Lupist interrupted.

"I'd like to hear about that profound meaning you know."

"It means Satan isn't here."

Dante asked.

"Because Zion's elite diverted him?"

"No. He wouldn't have come anyway. The world's operation is beyond human reasoning. You can't calculate it with thought. You need to understand the essence."

Lupist asked.

"What is Satan's essence?"

"The most cowardly being in the universe. He never gets involved in large-scale wars from the start. He always reaps the sweet rewards of victory from behind. Remember this. Satan always gets what he wants. If you don't act with that in mind, you'll be swept away by the laws of this world."

"So you sent your comrades to their deaths? They could all be dead."

Miro was unfazed.

"Don't focus on humans, look at the whole. I didn't send just anyone. To catch Satan, you have to meticulously handle the laws first. Zion's elite went to do that."

Dante agreed.

Only with Miro's perspective, observing the world from the highest point, could they trap Satan.

Seeing Dante's changed expression, Miro was satisfied.

"Of course, it's you who will change the world. If you hadn't fought so fiercely, could I have arrived? In that sense, death is also life. All lives come together to create this situation."

That is the law.

"...So what do we do now?"

"What do you mean?"

Miro stepped forward.

"We open a new future."

Kiaaaa!

From beyond the demon army, Kaidra flew in, carrying Zulu, Gangnan, and Arius.

Shirone chose the Golden Dragon Metira.

'He's taciturn, so the explanation should be brief.'

His gray hair was parted to the sides and flowed down in waves, and he wore a coat with sharp angles like blades.

He was quite handsome, but his steel eyes gave him a more eerie feeling.

"What?"

Shirone asked again after hearing the explanation.

"Iruki is dropping an elemental bomb? On the capital of Tormia?"

"Yes. The human army is currently carrying out the operation, and the success rate is fifty-fifty. However, if we participate, we can completely change the situation."

Shirone blinked.

'So... they're planning to annihilate the demon army in exchange for the entire population of Bashka.'

The Ice Dragon Frigia spoke.

"It's a good strategy."

She had short silver hair with bangs cut horizontally and a dreamy expression.

True to her reputation as a dragon who never showed emotions, her evaluation was straightforward.

"It's the best humans can do. If it succeeds, there will be hope for humanity."

Shirone nodded.

"Yes, that's right."

The Iruki Shirone knew was someone who could drop an elemental bomb to annihilate the demons.

"That's why it's not like Iruki."

And the Iruki Shirone knew as his closest friend would never let it come to that.

'Even if it meant sacrificing his own life...'

He was someone who would find a solution no matter what, and he had the ability to do so.

'...It must have been hard, Iruki.'

Poiné asked.

"Where should we go? Bashka, where the elemental bomb will explode, the Holy War where Commander Iruki is, or anywhere in the world. Everyone needs you, Messiah."

The 12 apostles would fight according to Shirone's will.

"There must have been a reason."

Shirone said with a sad expression.

"There must have been the biggest reason in the world, something that couldn't be solved just by dying."

Somewhere, the sound of someone sobbing could be heard.

"That's right, Messiah. Please don't be too sad."

A girl with black hair braided into two pigtails.

Her large eyes, filled with tears, were set in a face framed by a dress made of grass and straw.

This was Eitra, the Grass Dragon.

Despite her slender legs that looked like chopsticks under her dress, she was an indispensable apostle to Shirone.

'She's a crucial apostle for me.'

Her healing abilities, dealing with life-related information, made significant contributions to all dragon battles.

"Yes, thank you."

It was the first time an apostle had received praise, but this time, no one felt displeased.

Blitz asked,

"Messiah, where should we go?"

After carefully reviewing the world situation as explained by Metira, Shirone slowly opened his mouth.

"We will..."

'It's shifting.'

Even in the midst of the enemy's camp, Lupist could sense the massive inertia.

Slightly but surely, the demons' movements were starting to twist.

'Four degrees. Just four more degrees.'

The force acting below the surface.

'Now three degrees.'

The shockwaves from Miro, Rian, Amy, and everyone else combined...

'Two degrees!'

Rumble, rumble, rumble!

'One degree!'

Finally, the massive lake that was the demon army began to rotate entirely.

"It's done!"

As Lupist shouted, the demons broke through the allied forces' formation.

The direction was southwest.

'If they continue this way...'

They would directly hit the capital of Tormia, and the elemental bomb would explode over Bashka.

'Is it a disaster or a miracle?'

No one dared to make a judgment.
