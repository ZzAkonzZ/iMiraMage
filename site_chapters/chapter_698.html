Chapter 698: Reunion's Stage (7)

The weakening military discipline and the rise of cult leaders couldn't be entirely separated from Shirone's influence.

"I apologize for coming to you like this. But there's something I must tell you, Your Highness."

"Yes. I heard it's about the lost prince."

Shirone remained silent, as he wanted to deliver the news himself.

"...May I ask one thing?"

"Go ahead."

"When your conversation with His Highness ends, might there come a situation where I need to draw my sword?"

Shirone intended to tell Orkamp the truth.

That his son had died in Hexa's place.

"Perhaps... that might happen."

"I see."

Walker furrowed his brows, which had already turned white with age.

"Then I will strike with all my might when the time comes. Please understand."

Rian's eyes darkened like an abyss.

'That will never happen.'

Kido had already descended the mountain and was waiting.

After arriving in the city by carriage, the group used the Magic Association's spatial teleportation circle to jump to the Kazura royal castle.

Perhaps the bureaucrats thought it best to avoid involvement in such a sensitive matter, as only bodyguards were present.

Following a discreet route to the inner palace, Walker inspected Shirone's group's weapons.

'I'd like to disarm them...'

But they would likely refuse, and forcing the issue would be impossible.

Since the matter had already been approved, Walker reported to Orkamp beyond the door.

"Your Highness, Lord Shirone has arrived."

"Let him in."

The door opened, revealing Orkamp and Eliza seated side by side on the dais.

'You've grown so gaunt.'

The so-called royal aura was nothing more than an illusion manifested through titles. The king and queen of Kazura no longer shone.

"Greetings, Your Highness. I am Arian Shirone."

Orkamp's eyes narrowed at the unfamiliar demeanor.

'He's become a completely different person.'

Facing the king of a nation, Shirone showed no signs of nervousness—only unwavering determination to achieve his goal.

"A soldier said he heard your words in a dream. I was skeptical at first, but it seems to be true."

The oracle of Teraphos had been properly delivered.

"I apologize for startling you. I came because there was something I had to tell you."

Before hearing it, Orkamp raised his hand.

"Everyone, leave."

As the attendants exited the inner palace and the door closed, Eliza leaned forward eagerly.

"Don't tell me... you found our son?"

"Yes, I found him."

Eliza's face froze in shock, as she hadn't dared to hope for this much.

"R-really... our son...?"

From this point on, there was no turning back. Shirone's gaze turned icy.

"I found him, but I couldn't bring him back. Your son is no longer of this world."

"W-what?"

Shirone laid out all the facts.

Though he spoke only the truth, the words twisted and distorted in their minds, sounding like a tale from another world.

Orkamp's voice was strained as he asked, "So... our son is dead?"

"No. It's not like that."

Shirone stepped forward and knelt.

"Shirone! What are you doing?"

Rian and Kido looked at him in shock, but Shirone had already steeled himself.

"The prince didn't just die. He sacrificed himself in my place."

Silence filled the inner palace.

'Shirone... you...'

Orkamp gripped the armrest of his chair.

'How can you be so cold? How can you be so cruel!'

This was something he didn't need to say.

If he had any consideration for a parent's heart, he could have simply said their son was dead and left it at that.

'You don't even want to leave a shred of guilt behind, do you? You want to wash your hands clean and walk away!'

Shirone raised his head.

"You may take revenge. You may punish me. If it's within my power to endure, I will accept it gladly."

"WHYYYYYYYY!"

Eliza screamed like a banshee, her face twisted with rage.

"Why you! Why! Why! Who do you think you areeeee!"

Her eyes bloodshot, she shrieked as if vomiting her very soul.

"I'll kill you! I'll kill you horribly!"

As Eliza lunged forward with claws bared, Rian and Kido gripped their weapons.

"Walker!"

At Orkamp's command, the door burst open and elite guards stormed into the inner palace.

"Protect His Highness!"

Walker, having assessed the situation instantly, charged at Shirone.

'He must die.'

Nothing was more important than the king's safety.

"Uwaaaaah!"

Rian twisted his body and drew his greatsword.

Their blades clashed, the tremendous force sending both sliding back across the floor.

"Grrrr!"

As Rian lowered his stance and aimed his greatsword, Walker halted his charge and extended his sword in a cross guard.

"Huuuuh."

A single exchange was enough to gauge each other's strength.

'Incredible power.'

Kido glared at the surrounding guards one by one, twirling his spear dazzlingly.

"Hmph! Come at me, come at me if you dare."

Shirone spoke in a calm, subdued voice.

"Kido, Rian. Stop. Lower your weapons."

"No. I can't do that."

When Rian refused, Kido, who had half-lowered his spear, hastily resumed his combat stance.

"It's fine. I don't want to fight. Lower your weapons."

Rian was as resolute as Shirone.

"No. I will protect you."

Shirone scowled and shouted.

"Lower them!"

It was the only way to extinguish Rian's fury, but Rian kept his lips tightly sealed.

Caught between the two, Kido looked back and forth in confusion.

"W-what? What do we do? Decide quickly."

Shirone spoke calmly again.

"Rian, you promised to listen to me. This is an order. Lower your weapon."

"Then stand up. If you do, I'll lower it."

"......"

Shirone also refused to yield.

"Shirone, you have no intention of defending yourself right now. And these people harbor hostility toward you. I won't sheathe my sword. Punish me later if you must after we leave this place."

Seeing the logic in Rian's words, Kido snorted and tightened his grip on his spear.

"Rian, I have no roots."

Rian's pupils trembled.

"My origin lies not with me, but with the prince of Kazura. That prince died in my place. That's the starting point of my life."

'Damn it!'

Rian bit his lip until it bled.

"If I can't lay everything to rest here... I don't think I can move forward."

Reason and emotion clashed, and the sound of the greatsword's grip creaking could be heard.

'What are you asking of me?!'

With Shirone behind him and a swordsman exuding clear killing intent before him, lowering his weapon was an impossible order.

'Fine, I guess I can't sheathe it.'

Walker was the same—his duty was to protect the king.

As the masters, far beyond ordinary humans, stared each other down without blinking, Orkamp issued a command.

"Walker, that's enough."

Walker, who had been glaring at Rian with bloodshot eyes, nodded faintly as if signaling.

'We lower them at the same time.'

The reaction speed of those in the inner palace was akin to human arrows. A single misstep would lead to disaster.

The two men observed each other with the utmost concentration as they lowered their arms.

The moment the two swords clattered to the floor, the explosive tension vanished as if it had never existed.

"Whew..."

As the guards followed their captain's lead and sheathed their weapons, Kido exhaled deeply and stretched.

'Traveling with humans is exhausting.'

Though the standoff had been brief, it felt like they'd fought a full-scale war.

***

In the face of the unyielding situation, Eliza trembled violently.

"You... how dare you...?"

Even she couldn't discern her own emotions.

The world offered no guidance on how much rage one should feel at the death of a child whose face they'd never known.

But the injustice of living as a sinner for twenty years was reason enough to kill Shirone.

"I'm sorry. I'll do anything within my power to make amends."

When the word "amends" came from the mouth of someone with a one-in-three chance of becoming a star, Orkamp's eyebrow twitched.

But Eliza had already lost her rationality.

"Die. That's the only way you can make amends."

"That won't do. It's true the prince died in my place, but it wasn't by my will."

"Walker! What are you doing?! He's the prince's killer! Kill him!"

When Walker didn't move, Orkamp intervened.

"How about this instead? There are things worse than death. Imprisonment in the underground dungeons."

It was where political prisoners were held, subjected to unimaginable tortures.

"If you refuse even this, I'll have no choice but to resort to brute force."

Meeting Orkamp's gaze, Shirone nodded.

"Understood."

As Shirone's group followed Walker toward the door, Eliza hurled curses.

"I'll torture you horribly! I'll never forgive you!"

Walker closed the door, as if to block out the queen's voice, and walked down the corridor in gloomy silence.

'Has Kazura become this powerless?'

No—Shirone had simply grown stronger.

"Isn't this too cruel?"

"What is?"

At Shirone's cold retort, Walker realized he could demand nothing.

"His Highness is an outstanding politician, but he is still human. Perhaps... it might take some time."

It was a timid act of revenge, but Shirone's expression didn't change.

"It doesn't matter."

Descending to the third underground level, the stench hit them before the door even opened.

Along the dimly lit dungeon corridor were cells, from which moans could be heard.

"A year here is enough—no torture needed. You'll go mad. All you'll have left is a broken body."

Walker led them to a relatively clean wooden cell and opened the door. Shirone, Rian, and Kido filed in.

"Then... I hope you can leave again someday."

Only after Walker left did Kido speak.

"Shirone, what are you thinking?"

"Sorry. It won't take long."

"I know. To jump to Kashan, we need the king of Kazura's approval. But Orkamp must have made a deal. There was no need to suffer in the underground dungeon."

"It's not just about going to Kashan. Like I told Rian, if I don't lay everything to rest now, I never will."

Just then, a hoarse laugh came from the opposite cell.

"Kukuku... you finally came. I knew you would."

A madman clung to the bars, his mouth gaping.

His face was skeletal, his hair patchy, and his front teeth entirely missing.

In that horrifying, barely human visage, Shirone sensed something familiar.

"Zion?"

Zion, the son of Teraze and older brother of Uorin, was imprisoned here.

"You were here?"

"Yeah, I was here. That monster—my sister—put me here. Did Uorin send you too?"

Through the Heaven Project's Gaold and Miro's help during the moratorium, Shirone had learned of Uorin's true nature, but his only memory of her was from the paternity scandal.

"She really imprisoned her own brother here?"

"Puhahaha! Own brother? Nonsense! It's all lies! To that witch, humans are just livestock! She uses them when needed and discards them without mercy! You'd better brace yourself. Since the witch abandoned you too, you'll end up as miserable as me. Ehehe! Ehehehe!"

Zion's crazed eyes made it clear he was already insane.

"I'll watch you suffer! Right from here!"

"Who's making all that noise?!"

A voice from the depths of the darkness made Zion scramble back and fall on his rear.

"Uwaaaaah...!"

His reaction alone confirmed whose voice it was.

The man was covered in flabby fat, and his pupils were positioned far beyond normal focus.

His scalp was visible where hair had fallen out, and a coin-sized hole revealed gray matter.

"His brain is literally polluted."

Kido remarked casually, but Zion drooled and trembled in terror.

"Kekeke... our little one came looking for me? I'll shut that noisy throat for you."

Unbuckling his belt, the man entered Zion's cell, grabbed his hair, and dragged him into the shadows.

"No! Stop! No!"

The screams cut off abruptly. Later, the torturer reemerged.

"Heheh, that should keep him quiet for a while... Hm?"

Noticing the newcomers in the opposite cell, he licked his lips and approached.

"Hoh? What do we have here?"

Meeting Shirone's disgusted gaze sent a thrill through him.

"Heheh, you know... once you're here, you can never leave."

Unlocking the cell, the torturer stepped inside.

"How much you can please me determines the intensity of your torture each day. So..."

Pulling iron pliers from his pants, he reached for Shirone.

"Let's pull out some teeth."

The moment his hand touched Shirone's shoulder, Rian lunged and punched the torturer in the face.

With a bone-cracking sound, the lump of flesh flew and slammed into Zion's cell bars.

Without even looking at the torturer, Shirone said, "Thanks, Rian."

"That's my job."

Returning to his spot, Rian added, "I'll protect you."

Faced with Shirone's refusal to act and Rian's unwavering protection, Kido's eyes grew hollow.

'Both of you are insufferable.'

By nightfall, Rian's snoring echoed thunderously through the dungeon.

"Truly fearless."

Kido clicked his tongue at the sight of him sleeping soundly in a place even goblins would despise.

"Shirone, why don't you just make up with him?"

Shirone smiled. "We never fought. Rian wouldn't think so either."

"Still, clashing isn't good. Not that I'm taking sides, but Rian has lines he won't cross."

"I know. That's why I'll do as I please."

"Huh?"

"If Rian were the type to yield to me, I wouldn't be able to judge coldly. I'd be too concerned."

"Hmm, is that so?"

"Even if Rian weren't here, I'd have acted the same way earlier. But..."

Shirone stuck out his tongue. "Because Rian was here, I could stay unharmed."

Shirone and Rian weren't using each other.

"Humans really live such complicated lives."

Kido rested his arms on the open cell bars. "It's kind of fun, in its own way."

Rian rolled over, scratching his belly. "Ugh... I'm hungry..."

"Hahaha."

As hunger overpowered sleep, Rian's eyes snapped open. "Huh? You're back already?"

Shirone had already sensed it too.

The sound of the door opening echoed, and Walker approached with a torch.

He ignored the unconscious torturer and the wide-open cell door.

"Come out. His Highness summons you."

"The time has come. Let's go."

Rian stood, equipping his greatsword.

"After sleeping all this time, you act like you were waiting..."

After waiting ten minutes in the panic room, Orkamp entered carrying a box and sat across from Shirone.

"Was it bearable?"

"Thanks to your consideration. I'm surprised it was this quick."

After all, wasn't he the one who caused their child's death?

"If I must concede, I do so thoroughly. That's my principle. And since I can't give you what you want..."

"What do I want?"

"To go to Kashan, no?"

Logically, there was no other reason to visit Kazura.

"But a jump is impossible. Nominally, we're under Kashan's rule, but it seems we've fallen out of favor."

"I see."

That possibility wasn't entirely unexpected.

"Instead, take this."

Orkamp handed over the box.

"Uorin—no, Teraze left this behind before departing Kazura. She said to give it to you."

Shirone examined the box with his eyes only.

'Mitochondrial Eve. What's the intent here?'

Accepting the box, Shirone looked up and asked, "What about the queen?"

"She's not well. But she'll forget eventually. It's not a memory that brings her happiness. That's the kind of woman she is."

Orkamp spoke frankly.

"To be honest, I'm not even sure if we ever really had a son. But a mother's heart is different, no? You'd understand."

With matters concluded, Orkamp rose from his chair.

"Leave Kazura as you please. And if you meet Teraze..."

"I'll ask her to lift the sanctions."

Orkamp nodded and exited the panic room. Kido and Rian leaned toward the box.

"What is it? What did she leave? Not another Objet, right?"

Armand had also been given by Uorin.

"Who knows? I'd rather not owe more debts..."

Opening the lid, they found a small iron box with a familiar emblem engraved on its surface.

"Hena."

The language of angels, capable of containing unique laws.

"It's sealed. Can you open it with Immortal Function?"

Rian recalled Shirone unlocking Tess's drone's seal in Heaven.

"Yeah. But it feels a bit flimsy."

Activating Immortal Function, the Hena emitted a red light.

"As I thought..."

The Hena's law contained a security circuit.

"It's a one-to-one correspondence system. Absolutely unbreakable."

If someone wrote the number 7653, only they would know its meaning.

"But the Ultima System can do it."

As Shirone was about to input the code, he suddenly looked up.

"Huh? Ultima?"

"What's wrong? It's clearly meant for you."

"Uorin said she left this before leaving Kazura. At that time, I hadn't yet awakened to Jikji."

"Hmm, now that you mention it..."

"Which means this was entrusted to the Great Purifier. In other words, it didn't exist during the second reset."

"It's a bit unsettling. Can we trust it? They called her a witch in the dungeon."

"Let's unseal it first."

Inputting the code, the Hena's iron shell peeled away, revealing its contents.

A familiar mechanical device.

"A Meta Gate. Why this?"

Rian spoke. "Is it about Ra Enemi? To find something in Heaven?"

"Or maybe it's not new. It might already have specific coordinates memorized."

Kido slapped his palm. "Kashan!"

"Possibly. But..."

They called her a witch, a monster.

"It might lead somewhere horrifying."

As the three stared at the Meta Gate like it was an abomination, Rian made the decision.

"Let's go. We have no other choice."

Activating the device in a spacious area, the box opened, and a massive black sphere distorted spacetime.

"Heaven or hell. Hell or heaven."

Listening to Kido's ominous chant, Shirone pushed himself into the black void.

His vision darkened, and soon the gloomy torchlight of the panic room was replaced by blinding white daylight.

'Where is this?'

His half-lidded eyes took in the endless marble floor and dozens of windows flooding the space with sunlight.

And beneath the windows, a beautiful white-haired girl stood with her hands behind her back, her silhouette alone radiating grace.

'I don't know, Kido. Whether this is heaven or hell.'

Shirone's honest impression upon arrival.

"Hm? Who's there?"

Sensing presence, the girl turned and, upon seeing Shirone, widened her eyes and smiled.

"Oh my?"

It was Teraze Uorin, the empress of Kashan.
