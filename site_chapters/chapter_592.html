Chapter 592:

“Do you know me by any chance?” Shirone asked.

“What are you talking about? How would I know you?”

It was clear that the maze Shirone discovered upon first entering Istas was where he met Miro.

“I don’t know how to explain it, but…”

It was true.

“I know you, Miro. I fought alongside you and learned a lot from you.”

Miro’s face, which had been blinking for a moment, soon became serious.

“You succeeded.”

He was referring to Goffin’s directive.

“So, how long has it been since the time closed?”

“Nineteen years.”

It was an encounter with a boy from 19 years in the future.

Shirone prepared answers in advance, thinking of the hypothetical questions Miro might ask about his fate, whether he was living happily, and how the final war ended.

But what he heard was a cold voice.

“Why did you come here?”

“What?”

“If the mission succeeded, you should have destroyed Istas or buried the incident. Why did you come back?”

“Well, that’s…”

Frustrated, Miro added.

“Did you really come in without knowing where you were? The infinite loop structure of time. Your entry must have twisted the incident! How will you take responsibility?”

As Shirone stood speechless, Miro, who was racking his brain, asked again.

“Are you Fermi by any chance?”

That misunderstanding was unwelcome.

“No, I’m Shirone.”

Shirone?

Miro frowned.

“Hmm. Shirone.”

No matter how much she searched his memory, it was a name she had never heard before.

“Anyway, don’t do anything from now on. Stay out of the incident as much as possible.”

“I can’t do that. The reason I came here is…”

“There’s no need for a reason. Just prepare for the end of your life. Anyway, if Hexa dies, this world will end too.”

“What is Hexa?”

Miro pointed to the child in her arms.

“This child. Goffin intends to remove Hexa from the cosmic initialization. Got it? If Hexa disappears, Anke Ra will destroy humanity.”

Remove from cosmic initialization.

“Does that mean it won’t disappear even if reset?”

As Shirone immediately got to the point, Miro was momentarily puzzled but soon agreed.

“Well, there’s no such thing as coincidence.”

Someone without that level of information had zero chance of infiltrating the upper echelons 19 years later.

“Yes. Only Hexa can withstand Ra’s reset. So you sacrifice yourself. Let me complete my mission.”

Shirone swallowed hard.

“Maybe, that child might be me.”

“What?”

Miro, who was staring at Hexa’s face, was taken aback.

“Now that you mention it, you do look alike.”

Although appearance changes as one grows, the fundamental impression doesn’t easily disappear.

“But I’m not sure. If you’re Hexa, why did you come here?”

Shirone was frustrated by the ongoing assumptions.

“I might be Goffin’s son.”

“What?”

Miro’s eyes widened.

“So tell me. What exactly is happening here?”

“Haha…”

The laughter grew louder and reached a peak.

“Hahaha!”

It wasn’t just a pleasant feeling, so Shirone was both bewildered and angry.

“Why are you laughing?”

“You’re Goffin’s son? No, that’s absolutely impossible.”

“How do you know that, Miro? Did you ask Goffin directly?”

“No, it’s just…”

Miro laughed again as if it was absurd and continued.

“There’s no need to ask. There’s no way you’re Goffin’s son.”

“How can you be so sure?”

Shirone’s voice rose, but Miro looked even more baffled.

“Do you really not know? Goffin is the most famous mage in this world. Everyone knows that.”

“How is that a reason?”

Finally, as Shirone raised his voice, Miro also shouted in frustration.

“Fool! Goffin doesn’t look like us!”

Silence fell.

“What?”

Shirone’s face went blank.

“Doesn’t look like us?”

Miro suddenly realized.

“Oh, were you erased?”

In Shirone’s timeline, no one could accurately recall Goffin’s appearance.

“Sorry. I didn’t consider that. I’ve never experienced erasure myself. Anyway, you’re not Goffin’s child. You look completely different.”

“Are you saying he’s not human?”

Miro pondered seriously.

When a previously unseen intelligent species appears, deciding which species to classify them as is a matter of compromise between the individual and society.

“He’s human… He has all the characteristics of a human, and Goffin also claimed to be human.”

That’s why Goffin is protecting humanity against Heaven.

“But he’s an ancient Gaia. It’s hard to say you’re his child.”

Shirone knew this, but had never heard about Gaia’s appearance.

“Then what does Goffin look like?”

Miro rested his chin on his hand.

“Hmm, you might be surprised at first, but it’s strange, I guess? So his face is…”

As Miro carefully recalled Goffin’s appearance, his eyes shook with shock.

“I can’t remember.”

“What?”

Instead of answering, Miro looked at Hexa.

Still asleep in her arms, Hexa’s presence was gradually fading.

“This is bad! The incident has twisted! Hexa must have died in the future!”

Since the beginning and end are connected, Hexa’s death could affect the present Hexa.

“What? What?”

Shirone raised his hands in confusion.

As Hexa’s presence faded, Shirone’s body also became increasingly blurry.

“Why is this happening?”

This proved it for sure.

“Hexa. You’re really Hexa?”

If Hexa dies, Shirone cannot exist either.

Furthermore, the fact that Goffin’s memory was disappearing meant that the closed loop of time had opened.

In other words, she would face Goffin’s erasure without completing his directive.

“No!”

Miro jumped up and grabbed Shirone by the collar.

“Get out of these coordinates immediately!”

He didn’t know what future awaited, but creating variables was the only way to survive.

“But… where should I go?”

“Goffin’s door! Go there! I’ll stabilize Hexa there! That incident must be protected at all costs!”

Shirone nodded repeatedly.

“Hurry! There’s no time!”

As Miro left the room, Shirone also cast a spell.

“Ugh!”

Once again, it felt like his head was going to explode, and he perceived the entire incident.

“Gasp! Gasp!”

The space remained the same, but Shirone fell forward, gasping for breath in a completely different time.

He was alive.

His physical presence was reviving.

‘Why did Miro’s mission fail?’

It was the first thought that came to mind.

‘I just met Miro.’

He had never harmed Miro or intended to.

The only thing he could guess was…

‘Because I asked about Goffin.’

His curiosity about Goffin had ruined things.

‘That means, in the future, my obsession with Goffin caused Miro to fail his mission?’

Mission failure meant Hexa’s death.

‘I have to go to the Supernatural Psychic Research Society!’

It was the place where Goffin’s door was located, as Miro had said.

‘Please, don’t be too late.’

Confirming the warehouse’s mark, Shirone ran in the opposite direction of where Miro had gone.

Event Coordinates

Time: 12:53 AM

Location: Istas Warehouse No. 65

“Ugh.”

Only corpses remained where Miro had swept through.

“Damn girl.”

The sole survivor was Lucan, an assassin from Mars Unit 1.

Despite being only twenty-three, Lucan had been recognized for his swordsmanship and excellent judgment, earning him a place in the secret unit of the Tormia Kingdom, Mars.

“I thought life would be easy.”

It was a dangerous job, but he was confident in his abilities and had a bright future ahead.

‘This is just a death sentence.’

All 42 members of Mars had written their wills before coming here. Not because they might die, but because they knew they would never return—a kind of death certificate.

‘What’s patriotism worth?’

Honestly, he didn’t want to do it. He wanted to rip off his Mars badge, quit, and shout that he was going back home.

‘What’s the point of honor if you’re dead?’

But no one did, and in the end, they were all dying.

Even if he had refused the mission, he would have died, but if he was going to die a meaningless death, he should have at least struggled.

“Ha! Damn world.”

As Lucan pushed himself back with his hands on the ground, his shattered lower body remained eerily in place.

‘How do you beat a monster?’

If Miro hadn’t been distracted by the child, his body would have been turned into jerky like his comrades.

‘Even holding on with Skima has its limits.’

As his strength waned, blood gushed from beneath his intestines.

“Is this the end…?”

Just as his vision began to blur, the door opened and a group of people entered.

Event Coordinates

Time: 1:01 AM

Location: Istas Warehouse No. 65

“Oh? A survivor…”

Behind a petite woman stood two men with powerful auras and a slender woman.

“Yolga.”

Lucan struggled to lift his head and see her.

Her dark brown hair curved gently over her forehead, and her face was clear and transparent.

She was small but exceptionally beautiful.

“Damn it!”

Lucan’s face twisted like a demon.

The only ones who could stand against the inhuman monster Miro were Yolga and her companions.

When Mars was being slaughtered, they hadn’t shown up, but now they appeared?

“Are you okay? You’re badly injured.”

Yolga approached, pulling back her hood.

“Badly injured? Does this look like just an injury? It’s all your fault. You wretched thing.”

“Calm down. It’s dangerous.”

“We’re all going to die anyway. Because of you, everyone’s dying.”

Yolga remained silent.

“I know. If you hadn’t coaxed the king, Mars wouldn’t have been deployed.”

“I’m sorry. But we still need Goffin. We’re not yet capable of facing Heaven’s army.”

“Shut up! What does Heaven’s army matter? I’m about to die, and you’re talking about that?”

“I’m sorry.”

As Yolga bowed her head, Lucan gathered blood in his mouth.

“Ptooey!”

He spat in her face.

“You bastard!”

Edgar, a two-meter giant with a mechanical arm, approached, swinging his fist.

“Don’t you have any convictions? Are you so afraid of dying?”

“I’m just resentful of dying a meaningless death. From the start, we were no match for Miro. We were just meat shields.”

“You don’t have much time left, but I’ll end it now.”

As Edgar approached, clenching his steel fist, Yolga raised her hand to stop him.

“If you have any last words, say them. I’ll deliver them.”

“Hmph, you won’t make it back either. It’s all over.”

Lucan, who had been sneering, thought for a moment and then laughed mockingly.

“If you want to repent so badly, how about a kiss? I’ve wanted to kill you since I first saw you.”

“…”

“Oh, you’re married, aren’t you? Well, it doesn’t matter, does it? We’re all going to die anyway. Oh, right, I don’t have a lower body, do I?”

The more murderous the looks from Yolga’s companions, the better Lucan felt.

“Haha, you’re all…”

At that moment, Yolga slowly leaned in.

“I’m really sorry.”

She grabbed Lucan’s face and gave him a deep kiss.
