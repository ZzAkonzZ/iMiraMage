Chapter 661:

‘So this was your plan all along.’

Lupist had orchestrated everything so Shirone would form a mercenary unit, only for him to join as a member. It was another of his carefully laid schemes.

With the Association off-limits for this mission, a small elite team was the best option—and if so, it made sense that the most powerful figure, the Association head himself, would step in.

‘You said I was the captain…’

It felt like a betrayal, but having a strong ally was ultimately a good thing, so Shirone chose to let it go.

“Uh, then…”

Looking at the paperwork, their names were undisclosed, and their specialties were listed as espionage and assassination.

‘We were going to disqualify applicants with unknown identities…’

But with Aria overseeing the paperwork, there was no issue. She was clearly brought on for this exact situation.

“For now, let's move on to testing. Since you're both mages, let’s start by seeing your Spirit Zones.”

“I’ll go first.”

Applicant 28, presumed to be Lupist, stepped forward.

Just as Shirone tensed up at the idea of evaluating a certified First-Class Archmage’s Spirit Zone, a wave of overwhelming killing intent stabbed through his synesthesia.

‘Whoa!’

Offensive Spirit Zones usually radiated in all directions, but right now, every thorn was directed straight at Shirone.

He knew he had to pass the guy no matter what, but the seriousness of his display still irritated him a bit.

‘Should I just fail him out of spite?’

But at that moment, Lupist’s Spirit Zone subtly trembled and transmitted a message to Shirone’s synesthetic senses.

Like reading Braille with his fingertips, Shirone pieced together the piercing hostility into a coherent sentence.

—Pass 213 no matter what.

‘I’ll decide that myself, thank you very much.’

Grumbling inwardly, Shirone passed the paperwork to Aria.

“Thank you. Next, number 29.”

As Secretary Jane entered her Spirit Zone, Shirone sensed a liquid-like flow—flexible and soothing, in stark contrast to Lupist’s.

‘She’s a mental-type mage.’

He knew Arin, a mage blessed by the gods in this field, but even in comparison, this woman’s mental fluidity was overwhelming.

‘It feels like I’m submerged in the ocean.’

Shirone marked “Pass” on her sheet and spoke.

“Thank you. The successful candidates will be announced after the first round. I wish you both the best.”

“Thank you very much.”

As 28 and 29 walked off toward the building, Shirone watched them intently.

‘213? Must be someone strong.’

He’d find out soon enough.

From applicant 30 onward, the stronger ones began trickling in. By the time they hit the triple digits, more than 20 had passed. Then came number 121.

“Next, please. Huh?”

Seeing the name, Shirone tilted his head in disbelief.

“What the…? Why is she here…?”

He looked up slowly—and saw a familiar face.

“It’s been a while, Shirone.”

“Ms. Etella?”

Her ragged cloak made her look very different from how she appeared at school, but it was undoubtedly Romi Etella of the Alpheas Magic School.

“What are you doing here?”

“I heard about your mercenary recruitment on the way. I’d appreciate a fair evaluation.”

She bowed politely like any other applicant.

‘She must have her reasons.’

Even though it was proper to follow protocol after graduation, it still felt surreal to see a former teacher like this.

Shirone didn’t want to be the one to evaluate her, and he braced himself. But then she took a martial arts stance.

‘Phew, looks like she’s opting for Skima assessment. That’s a relief.’

This time, it was Rian’s eyes that sharpened.

‘So this is Etella…’

Shirone had praised her countless times, and even now, her aura was unmistakably formidable.

Etella rotated her arms slowly and then struck with a basic move of Yin-Yang Wave Fist—a straight punch.

“Ha!”

That was it for the demonstration. Shirone turned toward Rian, a bit underwhelmed.

Rian sat, chin in hand, lost in thought, his expression unreadable.

‘Is that really enough? She’s definitely skilled, but…’

To the untrained eye, her move didn’t look that different from the other applicants’ demonstrations.

“Thank you.”

Some might interpret it as disrespect toward the evaluators, but Rian’s tone was courteous as he marked her as “Pass.”

“I hope we see you again in the second round.”

That she left without exchanging words with Shirone made it clear—she was treating this mission seriously.

“Rian, was that some kind of amazing technique?”

Shirone, genuinely curious, asked why she passed.

Rian scratched his head with a wry look.

“Honestly? I don’t know.”

“What? You don’t know?”

“Yeah. I’m doing the evaluations, but I’m not great with technique or theory. I mean, my own swordsmanship was learned entirely through actual combat.”

Shirone suddenly remembered—Rian had even dropped out of a swordsmanship academy after being deemed talentless.

“Then what’s your criteria?”

“There’s only one. I imagine taking the attack myself. And when Etella threw that punch…”

Rian turned to him and said,

“I got chills all over my body. I don’t know why, or what exactly was so great about it. I just knew I didn’t want to fight her—and so I decided I’d rather have her on our side.”

For Rian, who lacked any technical talent, his honed instincts were everything.

If those instincts gave such a clear warning, Shirone had no reason to argue.

“Got it. Makes sense.”

As more competent people passed the test, Shirone felt a weight lift off his shoulders.

‘With the Association Head and now Ms. Etella... maybe I don’t have to carry all the burden myself.’

As the sun set, most applicants had finished testing. It was time for number 199, the last in the 100s.

Aria smiled as she rested her chin on the table.

“He’s here. My secret weapon.”

Curious, Shirone and Rian checked the paperwork—and Rian’s expression froze when he saw the name.

“No way…”

The sound of footsteps—oddly off-rhythm—gave them a strange, unsettling feeling.

A man walked toward them with a long sword hanging at his side, limping as he went.

The sleeve covering his missing right arm flapped with every step.

‘Parka Kuan.’

He’d heard rumors that Kuan had discarded his own arm. His aura was different now, so he checked the profile to confirm.

It said he’d once been an instructor at the Kaizen Swordsmanship School.

“It’s been a while, Rian.”

“Guh…”

As a swordsman, Rian respected him. But looking back, he didn’t have a single fond memory involving the man.

Shirone was equally stunned and turned to Aria for an explanation.

“What’s going on?”

“He’ll be handling my personal protection. Feeling better now?”

Shirone knew better than anyone how skilled Kuan was.

“But how do you even know Mr. Kuan?”

“What do you mean? I’m not going to overlook a Kaizen sword instructor. Besides, he’s currently working as a civilian investigator in our mission area. He’s a perfect bodyguard for me.”

‘So he’s in Radum now.’

He’d heard that Kuan was involved in something with Miro after returning from Heaven.

“Then… is Armin or Sein around too?”

“No. I’m the only one in Tormia right now.”

“I see.”

Shirone looked disappointed—he’d hoped to see Miro again—but Kuan furrowed his brow.

“What? You don’t trust me?”

“No! It’s not that, I swear…”

Rian cut in.

“Enough. Let’s just proceed with the test. If he doesn’t pass, he won’t be joining us anyway.”

It was a chance to repay the humiliation Rian had suffered at the Kaizen Swordsmanship School.

"You may have been an instructor once, but there will be no special treatment. Please give it your best."

Kuan merely snorted.

"Still an idiot, I see."

Rian's eyes flared.

"What did you say?! Did you forget who’s the judge here? I’m not the same person I used to be!"

‘Hmm, true enough...’

He really had become a different person since those days at the academy. Having heard about Rian’s progress from working with Miro, Kuan was curious to see it for himself—but first came the public test.

"Well, let’s see if you can still swing a sword."

Kuan’s attitude lacked even the slightest tension, and Rian watched him lazily with his chin resting on his hand.

Just as expected, Kuan drew his sword half-heartedly, limping on his right leg as he adjusted his stance and began slashing through the air.

"..."

After a moment, Rian slowly removed his hand from his chin and unconsciously sat up straight.

His gaze locked onto the smooth arc of the blade slicing through the air, his face filled with awe—and a touch of sorrow.

‘Beautiful.’

He’d always known the ideal form was out there, but could never clearly picture it. Yet here it was.

Day after day, tirelessly swinging his sword—this was the path, the form Rian had always longed for.

‘So this is the beauty of a genius’s blade…?’

Having grown himself, he could now analyze that realm of swordsmanship.

After about a minute of flowing strikes, Kuan skillfully returned his sword to its sheath and grumbled.

"That good enough?"

"Yes. Thank you for your demonstration."

Even if he were an enemy, Rian couldn’t deny his acceptance.

Aria added, "We can talk more after the test."

Kuan nodded and exited the testing area as the 200s began to enter.

Fatigue was starting to pile up from the long evaluation, but when the turn for applicant 213 came, Shirone snapped back to attention.

‘What the...?’

A girl, mid-teens at most, radiated a completely different aura than any veteran before her.

Her hair was jet-black and bobbed short, and even her thick coat couldn’t fully hide her frail figure.

Hands clasped politely in front of her, she waited quietly as Shirone checked her profile.

Her name was Meirei. A mage. Not from Tormia, but from the Kingdom of Gallon.

Gallon, which shares a northern border with the Kazra Kingdom, is called a Holy Kingdom, though most countries regard its god as a dark deity.

Beyond that, there wasn’t much on the profile—except one peculiar detail that caught Shirone’s eye.

‘Regulation Exception.’

Her exact ability wasn’t specified, but there was no doubt it was crucial to this operation.

"You may begin."

Meirei calmly unfolded her Spirit Zone.

At fifteen, there was nothing especially impressive in terms of raw power, but there was something unique—something that could only come from a Regulation Exception.

‘So that’s why he told me about her.’

If not for Lupist’s heads-up, Shirone would’ve likely failed her for lacking fundamentals. Without a solid foundation, no one could survive in Radum.

He checked “Pass” and handed the paperwork along.

"Thank you. I hope the results are favorable."

But Meirei didn’t leave. She kept staring at Shirone.

"Is there something you’d like to say?"

"Are you Shirone?"

Her voice, heard for the first time, was as clear and flowing as a mountain stream—almost addictive in tone.

"Yes, I am. Why?"

"He’s afraid. Because you’re a ‘Touch.’"

Her cryptic words made Shirone furrow his brow.

"Who’s afraid of me?"

"Ra Enemy."

Shirone’s eyes widened.

"How do you know about him…?"

"I can hear him."

Meirei covered her ears as she spoke.

"I hear Ra’s voice."

She was Aset Meirei, the “Hearing” of the Great Purification—an individual capable of eavesdropping on the divine frequency of Ra. A Regulation Exception.
