Chapter 734:

“Wow!”

Kido exclaimed in awe. Even among those present, including Ymir, whose upper torso had been obliterated, no one could deny that Rian’s strike had reached the level of artistry.

“Th-that’s…”

Unlike Seongeum, a magician, Munkyung, who pursued the ultimate physical form, understood the profound meaning behind Rian’s punch.

‘He captured everything in that blow.’

The shortest distance between two points is a straight line.

‘Then what’s the shortest path between two moving points?’

That, too, is a straight line.

‘Therefore, an already completed straight line contains every possible curve.’

Rian’s punch had followed that principle, and it was a feat no one but him could replicate.

‘It wasn’t that Ymir didn’t dodge. He couldn’t dodge.’

Among the sword techniques Munkyung had studied, there was a concept known as first form. But witnessing Rian’s attack just now made him question whether any number of curved forms would ever matter.

“Phew…”

Leaning against the wall, Ymir slumped down, the right side of his body from his shoulder to his waist having been obliterated in a circular pattern.

“Does it hurt?”

Despite his condition, Ymir asked about Rian’s wellbeing.

“Urgh…”

As Rian writhed in pain, his muscles, contorted by the Yaksha transformation, began returning to their original state, albeit slowly.

“Pain,” Ymir said, lifting his head with a grin, “is the extremity of sensation. If we define death as the state where nothing is felt, then pain is life itself.”

“Grrgh…”

As he clutched his shoulder, writhing in agony, Ymir continued with a smirk, “Cherish it. It’s proof that you’re alive.”

The moment pain vanishes, so does life.

“Wait…” Rian gritted his teeth and raised his head, “I still haven’t heard… Why did Ogent…?”

What did Ymir know?

“You’ll find out soon enough—at the peak of pain.”

They would meet again. Ymir’s eyes closed as his body began shrinking, cells consuming themselves in the process of self-destruction.

When the process of obliteration ended, only one lone molar remained where Ymir had been.

“Wait! Gah!”

As Rian’s body returned to its human state, he curled up in immense pain, prompting Kido to panic. “Wh-what do we do? We have to get out of here now!”

“I’m fine. But Shirone…”

Ymir had vanished, but Shirone’s condition had worsened even further.

‘It’s not an explosion.’

A cold dread seized Seongeum. ‘Could he be dying?’

The thought struck her—Shirone might burn away completely and disappear.

“It’s blocked…”

In his fading consciousness, Shirone muttered, “Something… something’s blocking me…”

If he couldn’t break through, he would die. But that was a task only one of the 19,000 Shirones could accomplish.

“Shirone! We’ll go treat Rian first!”

With the demise of the Mara bandits, the death of the female warrior, and Ymir’s disappearance, it was the right decision.

Kido hoisted Rian onto his back, though the height difference made the arrangement awkward.

“I’ll take him,” Seongeum offered, stepping forward.

But before she could act, Kido froze mid-step, eyes fixed on the ominous glow ahead. “W-what is that?”

A sinister flame, forming a spherical shape, flickered eerily. Emerging from within was a young man, covered head to toe in tattoos visible even through his tattered clothes.

“You’re too late, Shirone.”

The man’s eyes locked with Shirone’s. In that instant, both Rian and Kido disappeared from view.

Though their speed made it impossible for an ordinary person to track them, the tattooed man remained indifferent, his gaze detached.

‘We can’t win. No one in this world can defeat him.’

Kido, breathless and trembling, clutched his chest, unable to calm his pounding heart.

“It’s not because we’re weak. It’s because he’s beyond anyone’s ability to handle.”

His eyes conveyed absolute certainty.

“Your Highness, those tattoos…”

“Yes, I know.”

Blocking Shirone’s path, Seongeum, known for her vast knowledge among the Ivory Tower candidates, spoke up. “They call him Dalia, or Psychedelic Nane.”

“Does it matter what I’m called?”

Ever since consuming Anke Ra’s dream, everything in this world had begun converging upon Nane.

‘Something’s different.’

Now, with three Ivory Tower candidates gathered in one place, it was clear that none of them were the same as before.

Nane stepped closer to Shirone, despite Seongeum standing between them. “Though I wish I could take immediate action to relieve all suffering beings, I’ve come to fulfill a promise, Shirone.”

Shirone, clinging desperately to his fading consciousness, forced himself to stand. “A promise? What promise?”

“You’ll find me where you seek.”

Shirone’s brows furrowed at the mention of what Ra Enemy had once said. “How do you know that…?”

“Don’t you see it yet?”

Nane formed a circle with his thumb and index finger. “I am the whole.”

Though the statement did not lead to a clash of blades, it struck like a profound truth lodging itself in Shirone’s mind.

“Anke Ra…”

Though the form and feeling were different, if they were truly the whole, there was no need to distinguish between Nane and Anke Ra.

Seongeum stepped forward. “I don’t know what you’re talking about, but if you’re our competitor, shouldn’t we test our strength against each other?”

Though she understood the depth of Nane’s power, she felt more at ease facing him than Shirone did.

“Stop. You won’t stand a chance.”

It wasn’t a dismissal of Seongeum’s abilities.

“You’ll die.”

“…It doesn’t matter.”

Though her time with Shirone had been brief, if she had to choose an opponent, it would be Nane.

“You’re a good person. Please, enter the Ivory Tower in my stead and fulfill your dreams.”

Munkyung’s face turned pale. “Your Highness!”

“Munkyung.”

For the first time, Seongeum smiled warmly at him. “You may bury my body.”

“Argh!”

Letting out a feral cry, Munkyung drew his sword and charged at Nane.

‘I must… I must at least try!’

Even as he swung with all his might, Nane merely watched the descending blade.

‘I will kill him! I will definitely kill him!’

The gap closed rapidly: 0.5 centimeters, 0.1 centimeters, down to 0.0000001 centimeters.

‘I’ll cut him.’

And at that exact moment, Nane made his declaration.

Sermon – Cutting Blade.

As though birthed from the very fabric of time, a gray blade materialized and sliced vertically through space, halting Munkyung’s strike.

‘I… lost…?’

Before he could process what had happened, his vision was filled with radiant light as he crashed to the ground 20 meters away from Nane.

“How…?”

By the time Munkyung regained his senses, Seongeum was already lunging at Nane.

“Do not insult my subordinate.”

Though Nane, who had achieved enlightenment, no longer felt emotions like insult, he found Seongeum’s ability intriguing.

“A remarkable insight.”

“Hmph! Don’t delude yourself into thinking you’re above me.”

Ether Wave – Danagog.

The space surrounding both Seongeum and Nane distorted, twisting its directional flow completely.

‘Come, do whatever you wish.’

Any attack would be funneled toward Nane.

“Space, is it?”

Extending both arms, Nane formed complex seals, summoning a pitch-black sword that devoured everything around it.

“Gravity.”

The distorted space created by Danagog was pulled entirely into the blade.

“And waves.”

An intangible sword rose high above Nane’s head, vibrating violently as it disrupted the ether waves.

“And now…”

With a serene expression, Nane clasped his hands together. A radiant aura burst forth, revealing thousands of multicolored swords forming a brilliant halo around him.

“All things.”

Sermon – Supreme Nirvana.

Countless swords representing various elements encircled Seongeum before launching toward her in unison.

At the brink of death, a single question arose in her mind:

‘Is he… a god?’

The first to pierce her was a radiant sword of light, followed by countless others bearing the essence of countless concepts, tearing through her body.

‘Huh?’

Even in a state where life had left her body, the last thing Seongeum saw was Shirone’s face.

“I… I died, didn’t I?”

“No. You didn’t.”

In that fleeting moment, the two found themselves standing on the boundary between life and death—Choeni Bardo.

“An intermediate state?”

Even though Supreme Nirvana had failed, Nane remained unaffected, showing no signs of agitation.

“Annihilate death.”

His sermon triggered another transformation, manifesting as a black blade that pierced through the veil of the inner world, aiming straight for Shirone and Seongeum.

‘So this is it.’

As the sword closed in, Shirone realized what had been obstructing him.

‘It was me all along.’

Immortal Function.

As his mind expanded into the realm of infinity, Shirone once again reversed the cause and effect using Valhalla Action.

“Spatial collapse, is it?”

Nane changed the seal he was forming.

“Neither beginning nor end.”

A pale, radiant blade swam serpent-like through time, transcending cause to chase after Shirone’s result.

‘Is he… a god?’

Shirone couldn’t help but harbor the same thought.

Goblin Transcendence – Binding Spirit.

“Raaagh!”

Kido rolled in front of Shirone, activating Binding Spirit, slightly distorting the trajectory of the time-transcending blade. It veered off course, embedding itself into the wall of the cavern.

Boom!

A massive section of the thick dirt wall was obliterated. Everyone stood in stunned silence until Nane ceased his attack.

“I do not understand.”

His voice alone was enough to send chills down everyone’s spines. At that moment, Rian, supported by Munkyung, joined them.

“What is it you cling to?”

Despite their formidable prowess, the five gathered warriors collectively took a step back in the face of Nane’s mere presence.

‘This isn’t just strength… It’s something beyond that.’

No fitting words came to mind.

“This world ends the moment you close your eyes, does it not? Why do you struggle so desperately to live?”

Gathering his courage, Shirone stepped forward.

“There’s no need for a reason. We’re alive. And that alone is reason enough to live.”

To Nane, who had reached the pinnacle of understanding, Shirone’s words signaled one of two things:

Either Shirone was wrong, or there was something Nane had yet to comprehend.

“I came to you because I promised I would, and because I want you to keep your promise.”

“What promise?”

Though Nane’s KAR level continued to approach perfection, at 99.99999…%, the endless journey toward absolute completion stretched on.

“You have a duty to answer the question that remains.”

Pointing at Shirone, Nane asked,

“Why is it… that I cannot become a god?”

A heavy silence filled the cavern.

“A god…”

Though Kido detested the word more than anything, even he couldn’t deny that Nane might deserve such a title.

“Shirone, I am almost right.”

A single crimson sword, radiating ominous light, floated vertically above Nane’s head.

“Because I am almost right, I bear the weight of irreversible karma in order to save this world. But because I am only almost right, this will be your final chance. If you have no means to deny me, then this dream ends here.”

Surely, that sword embodied such a concept.

“Shirone, stand back. I’ll smash that cult-leader wannabe to pieces.”

Despite his pain, Rian raised his sword. But Shirone shook his head and took a step forward instead.

“I don’t know if my answer is correct.”

Counting even what he couldn’t remember, this was the third time Shirone had heard that question.

And for the first time, he felt he might have an answer.

“But you will never drive that sword into this world.”

Nane nodded.

“I understand that much. But you’re still missing something critical. Tell me—why can’t I drive it in?”

After a brief moment of contemplation, Shirone slowly turned, gazing at his companions—Rian, Kido, Seongeum, and Munkyung.

“And the countless other lives that exist in this world…”

“No, Shirone!”

Realizing something, Rian shouted in alarm, but it was too late. Shirone spread his arms wide, stepping toward Nane.

“This is my answer.”

Immortal Function.

It wasn’t for casting magic.

With a heart embracing all life, Shirone opened his mind completely. His body radiated light in the shape of a cross.

“What… what is that?”

Simultaneously, the sealed gateways within Andre trembled, bursting forth with brilliant, blooming lights.

“Those are…”

19,000 crosses began to rise.
