Chapter 1257:

"Eat."

Siena once again held out the fruit.

"I protected—"

"Eat it, damn it! You have to eat so you can protect me!"

Why was she so angry?

After throwing the fruit, she collapsed into Kuan’s chest.

‘Why does he have to be the one hurting?’

Movements that defied human limits—proof that he had given up being human.

"All because of me!"

What even was love?

If she'd known Kuan's end from the beginning, she never would’ve started.

Before he lost his hearing.

Before he lost his sight. Before he cut off his arm…

This is my fault.

If she hadn’t been there, Kuan wouldn’t have ruined his life like this.

“Siena.”

Kuan smiled like a fool.

“Don’t…… cry……”

It was like he was saying, It’s nothing.

A clown.

One who limped on purpose, who tripped and stumbled, who pretended not to see—just to make others laugh.

"I'm sorry."

Siena strengthened her resolve.

“Don’t worry about anything. I don’t care what kind of person you are. I’ll be by your side.”

Only then did Kuan finally grab a handful of the fruit.

Seeing him devour it like a starving beast, all dignity forgotten, broke her heart again—but Siena forced a smile.

"That's right."

Once she got Kuan to eat something, Siena turned her attention to the noises outside the cave.

“Stay here for a moment.”

Though they had distanced themselves from the battlefield, an ominous feeling crept in—one too strong to ignore.

She soared into the sky and scanned the city.

“Oh my god…”

Torches flickered in every street, and a massive crowd was flooding in from beyond the horizon.

‘What is happening?’

It was as if all of humanity was converging on a single point.

Strange.

From high in the sky, and only from such a distant vantage point, she could see it clearly—

‘This isn’t how humans move.’

Even ants—at the peak of social evolution—didn’t form lines like this.

And then their voices rang out in unison.

—Praise the God.

Goosebumps prickled Siena’s skin. One word echoed in her mind.

“Apocalypse.”

Demons made flesh. Humans turned to objects. And next would come…

Kwooooo!

A blazing meteor tore through the night sky.

“……A giant.”

After Maya's performance ended in a bloodbath, world leaders immediately convened for emergency meetings.

Fermi, King of Keshia, sat in his office, face cold and still.

‘There’s nowhere left to run.’

Processions of In converged from all over the world, closing in on Jaive’s borders.

‘The disappearance of the emotion disease means the system of the under world has reset. But…’

The boundary between Hell and reality had also vanished.

‘This is exactly what Satan wanted. That’s inevitable. The real problem is Cellbuster.’

Even Fermi couldn’t recover the corrupted data from Marcia’s mined future.

‘Aside from the delay of Yin and Yang by a few days, I have no usable intel.’

Just then, a knock.

“Your Majesty.”

His aide entered with dazed eyes, still under the lingering effects of Angel.

Fermi exhaled softly.

‘The disease may be gone, but quitting it is still a personal choice.’

“About that thing you mentioned before. The, uh… what was his name again…”

Fermi bristled at the vague response but fought the urge to lash out.

“Whom are you referring to?”

He had issued too many directives to remember each one, and most hadn’t even been reported back to him.

“Ah, Ranstin. We've found his location.”

Fermi sprang to his feet.

“What?”

“Uh, it’s... Rove Lanstin—”

Before he could finish, Fermi pulled the aide into a tight hug.

“Ugh! Y-Your Majesty?!”

“You did it. Truly well done.”

The only one who could counter Cellbuster: Guitar Man. Though whether he could make it in time was another matter.

‘There’s hope.’

Fermi remembered Yolga’s words:

—Always remember, you still have a choice.

Anger changes nothing. Only the calm can influence outcomes.

If Fermi had snapped at his inept aide, this result never would’ve come.

‘And even now…’

The choice still remained. So he calmed his heart.

“I believed in you. What you’ve done today is equivalent to saving all of humanity.”

It was a lie, of course.

“Ha-ha! I, uh… I’ll keep doing my best! Please trust me!”

Though still under the effects of Angel, his eyes were visibly clearer than before.

“Good. Head to the Corona Sector and find a man named Chong. He’s a star of the Ivory Tower.”

The world’s second-best communicator.

No—since Fermi had already purchased Lampa’s magic, he was now the best.

‘If Chong finds Lanstin, Seong-eum will bring him in.’

With the specialists of transmission and transportation working together, they might just make it in time.

“This is a matter of utmost urgency.”

Now full of motivation, the aide nodded vigorously and left the office.

“Whew…”

Fermi fiddled with a cigarette as he approached the window.

‘Now it’s the wave of In?’

The tasks piled up endlessly.

‘The Holy War has gathered the world’s leaders. If this place is overrun, humanity will lose the will to fight.’

Humanity…

‘That’s his specialty.’

Having left the burden to Shirone, Fermi lit the cigarette and exhaled a long breath.

“Fuu…”

A meteor streaked across the night sky.

“…How irritating.”

A future that fit too perfectly.

“Kukukuku…”

It was so massive, Ymir had no trouble traversing the universe.

This wasn’t about speed. It was about scale. And when he recondensed, a human planet lay before him.

‘What should I do with it?’

Even without his contract with Luber, he had no intention of forgiving the humans who humiliated him.

‘But crushing them one by one is too petty… and honestly, annoying.’

Ymir wanted only one thing: battle.

‘Ah, that’s right.’

He clenched his massive fist.

‘Let’s start with one punch.’

The target of the King of Giants was no mere individual or nation—it was the planet itself.

In front of Delta's main gate.

—Follow the will of God. The southern warriors and the procession of In continued their fierce clash of strength.

“Hold onto your heart!”

The southern warriors, wild and ferocious, swung their blades fearlessly and cut down the In... but—

‘There’s no end.’

The ever-replenishing crowd now numbered in the millions.

“Guh!”

When a warrior was dragged down, the In trampled over them with an uncountable number of feet.

“Aaaargh! Aaagh...!”

Even the toughest bodies were crushed into pulp.

“We’ve reached our limit,” said Euntara.

“Even with the power of Elikia, we can’t drive them all away. Just holding the front gate until now has been a miracle.”

Shirone’s expression had grown grim as well.

“To be honest, I wonder if there’s any point to this. War is fought to be won. But if the only way to win is to kill all of humanity, can we even call this war?”

All over the world, humanity was losing its heart.

—Follow the will of God.

Shirone considered his final option.

“I’ll bring them back.”

“You will?”

Tachyon was a superluminal signal.

Just as God had manipulated human causality, Shirone could alter their past as well.

“Multiverse,” he said.

“The reason they’ve lost their hearts is because they kept reliving their regrets in endlessly branching timelines. But if I could rewrite their pasts and give them satisfying futures... maybe, just maybe, they could regain their hearts.”

Euntara frowned.

“If you’re not mad, then you’ll explain how that’s even possible. Changing the past? Let’s say there’s magic for that. But this is the past of all humanity. Even if it were possible, could you bear the weight of all their desires?”

That was the reason Shirone couldn’t be certain.

‘A god wouldn’t care.’ Lacking a heart, a god could simply grant whatever life humans wanted.

‘But me...?’

Could he do it?

Could he fulfill a request from someone who wanted to pass the test—by making someone else fail?

‘I just need to find a future where everyone is satisfied.’

Could he accept a request to destroy someone’s life—just because they didn’t like seeing them happy?

‘No one is born evil. I just need to give them a life where that thought would never occur.’

Could he really... do it?

“Huuuh...”

Tears were already welling in his eyes, but Shirone steadied himself and walked toward the In procession.

“I believe I can.”

Love.

The kind Yorahan showed—an overwhelming level of self-sacrifice, accepting all, enduring all. A transcendent madness.

“Fuuuuuu!”

As the southern warriors stepped back, the In began to swarm toward Shirone.

—Follow the will of God.

Cold-hearted deity.

“Tachyon.”

From the body of a god with a heart, a swirling energy of mingled light and darkness began to shimmer.

“Wh-What is that…?”

As Euntara’s eyes widened, the energy burst out in a great wave, visible even to everyone inside the Holy Hall.

Causal Manipulation – Ouroboros.

Time flowed and stopped all at once in a paradoxical state. The world turned grey.

—Follow... God’s... will… kkkkchchchchchch!

All the In in the world began to emit distorted machine noises, golden light exploding from their eyes.

‘This is where it begins.’

Now inside the state of Ouroboros, Shirone was seeing a world different from before.

The background shattered like mosaic tiles, human bodies unraveling like strands of spaghetti.

He was racing through imaginary time. It didn’t matter how long it took.

In that moment of zero seconds, Shirone understood the past of every In.

‘Of course they lost their hearts.’

Their desires collided endlessly, erasing even their will to live.

‘How heartless…’

God hadn’t even tried to understand them.

‘First, avoid the collisions.’

Now that he’d become god, he realized—crafting outcomes that satisfied everyone wasn’t actually that hard.

‘We really are infinite beings.’

The truly horrific truth was…

—As long as I’m happy. As long as I’m superior. I’m right. Everyone else is wrong!

Me! Me! Me!

Humans were no different from God in their cold indifference to others.

‘Why do we only think that way?’

The concept of “I” might just be the most detestable word in the universe.

‘Forget it. This is stupid.’

Accepting all their desires—it was impossible unless you were a machine.

Shirone collapsed to his knees.

‘Yeah, screw this! Why should I suffer for you people…!’

Then—

“Ah.”

Sudden self-loathing swelled up within him.

‘Am I really any different?’

He was just another human, judging others like the rest of them.

‘Even if I’m the most detestable thing in existence…’

We still reach out our hands to one another with a pitiful smile.

‘Let’s live together.’

To overcome the barrier of humanity and reach infinity… the only way was—

Love.

Hot tears streamed down his face.

‘Of course I want to be happy. Of course I want to feel superior.’

We all want that. Every one of us.

As his chin began to lift—at that moment, the weight of humanity’s desires surged up from his stomach.

“Uweeeeeeegh!”

As if vomiting up the toxins of the under world, Shirone hurled it all—and the In procession stopped in its tracks.

—Kiiiii! Live... Kiiiii!

“Uweeegh! Ughhh!”

Emotional purging.

As Shirone vomited the last remnants of evil, the voice of an In whispered beside his ear.

—Live...

I want to live.
