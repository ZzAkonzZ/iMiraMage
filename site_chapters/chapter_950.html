Chapter 950: The Age of History (1)

Ikael and Ashur arrived at the seventh planet through the Stargate installed in Zebul.

The sky was blue, not unlike heaven, but everything else was smaller.

From the sky of Galliant Island, looking down at Mount Toa, they saw the natives—dark-skinned with solid muscles, shorter than heaven's Kergo—fiercely fighting.

"Stand back, depraved heretics! We are priests who obey the command of God!"

Most of the angel faction were emaciated and unfocused, whereas the anti-anegl faction was as fierce as wild beasts, clear-eyed and strong.

"Foolish ones! Even as our tribe starves, you chew hallucinogens! We cannot entrust the future to you! Now, we govern!"

Ikael rested her chin on her hand.

"Hmm."

Anke Ra does not gain material benefits from the people.

However, sacrificing to verify the faith of humans on distant planets was important.

The angel faction conducted expensive annual rituals and sought to feel God through their bodies with a hallucinogen called "Looph."

'Aren't these admirable humans? Believing in God is enough.'

Then Anke Ra would lead them to heaven and grant them eternal life.

However, the anti-angel faction prioritized life.

They warned against the addiction to Looph and advocated using the money spent on rituals to help starving tribesmen.

"Such arrogant fools."

Ikael's cold demeanor was that of a true messenger of God.

Ataraxia unfolded, and a halo covering the sky amplified her voice.

"Humans, listen!"

The thunderous sound, as if the sky itself were shouting, made everyone look up.

"Is that..."

Even the angel faction, who carried God in their hearts, couldn't tell if the sight before them was hallucination or reality.

But it didn't matter.

"A messenger of God, a messenger of God has come!" The angel faction prostrated themselves.

"These depraved heretics oppress us! Please, look upon us with mercy!"

The almost victorious anti-angle faction grimaced.

"Damn! An angel?"

Kergo had legends about Anke Ra since they came from heaven.

But only Nephilim could pass through heaven's gates, and it was the first time an angel appeared.

Ikael continued.

"Stop fighting immediately and repent. Otherwise, God will punish you."

The angel faction jumped up and shouted at the anti-angel faction.

"Ooooh! Ooooh!" How joyful could they be?

Their believed God had responded while they even gave up their lives.

"Punishment?"

The leader of the anti-angel faction grimaced fiercely.

"What have we done so wrong! Is living a sin! Is it a sin to want to protect our families!"

"Sin."

Ikael was resolute.

"Put away your nonsense!"

The leader of the anti-angel faction threw a spear, but Ikael was too high up.

"When our tribesmen starve, you're nowhere to be seen, but now you say we've sinned? My wife committed suicide due to excessive Looph use! My comrade's child died! Is that the will of God?"

"Yes."

Anke Ra merely maintains the system that allows humans to use the world.

"How could you understand the profound will of God?"

They didn't know how much sacrifice was made to restore stability to heaven now, or how dangerous humans who denied God could be.

"Those who deny God cannot remain in this world. But those who follow Him will gain eternal life."

"Give eternal life to the dogs!" A muscular middle-aged man with a thick beard wept.

"Who wants to live forever! Save my son, my son! I can die right now, my son... huuh!"

Ashur, looking back at Ikael, saw her fists trembling.

The muscular man pleaded sorrowfully.

"Please just save our son. Why should he die? Whatever punishment, give it to me. Why my son..."

Ikael's eyelids fluttered, and the giant Ataraxia seemed to flicker and vanish.

'Why?'

She couldn't bear it.

'Why does it hurt so much?'

Inside the archangel, whose wounds had never been inflicted by anyone, a pain seemed to transcend the universe.

"Lady Ikael."

As Ashur spoke worriedly.

"Shut up."

Ikael bit her lip.

Even her whispered voice echoed through heaven and earth, her eyes filled with an unfathomable rage.

"It is all God's will." She hated that she could say nothing more than this, as if programmed.

"God's will?"

The leader of the anti-angel faction shouted.

"What is God's will then? He's great! He's omnipotent! He can give us eternal life!"

Tears streamed down the leader's face.

"People I love have died! My heart is in so much pain! I'm going mad! And how indifferent must this God you speak of be!"

All the anti-angel faction stood up, armed.

"Can't you even shed a tear for us, not a bit of sympathy?"

The moment her heart was transmitted, it was as if Ikael had been hit by a cannonball, and her upper body was thrown back.

"Haak!"

Ataraxia broke, and the returned celestial body vibrated fiercely.

'What is this?'

A scratch in the deepest part of her spirit itched.

She wanted to scratch it until blood flowed, flesh was dug out, and bones were scraped.

'No!'

Ikael clenched her teeth.

'I am a noble archangel.' As Ataraxia expanded again, Galliant Island shook.

Ataraxia amplified the volcanic activity 20,000 times, causing Mount Toa's crater to explode with tremendous lava.

"Oh! God!"

The angel faction, delirious with hallucination, adored the sky, while the anti-angel faction was thrown into chaos.

Ashur, unable to grasp Ikael's intentions, evacuated the angel faction using the power of Signal.

'It's still a mission.'

Everything was covered in hot lava, and volcanic ash fell like snow from the sky.

It was an easy task.

However, tears did not stop in Ikael's eyes as she punished humans and returned.

'Stop... Huuuuuh! Huuuuuh!' It was always the case, but this time it was severe.

'Pain. It hurts too much to bear.'

Only Anke Ra's cold eyes could possibly alleviate her pain.

'Ah, God. What terrible sin have I committed? How dreadful a sin it must be to inflict such pain on me.'

Anke Ra's voice did not return.

250 years later, a human arrived on the ash-covered Galliant Island.

With hair made of steel and quite tall, he introduced himself as a human.

"McClaine Goffin?"

Kergo was wary, but their attitude completely changed after seeing the abilities of the Nephilim.

Since God's punishment, the angel faction's status had skyrocketed.

Goffin surveyed the still-unrecovered civilization.

"It's a disaster."

It was Ikael's ability.

"When our wife gets angry, it's scary."

Not understanding Goffin's words, the young chief Kang knelt down.

"Messenger of God, we receive your command."

At that moment, Shirone felt strange.

'That person will be Kadum in the future.'

Seeing a real person from Omega meant that the present was close.

"The volcanic explosion was a long time ago, but it's still backward. Poor."

Hearing Goffin's words, Kang bowed his head.

"What could be the problem? We only praise the greatness of God. Everything is God's will."

"?????? Yeah."

After a long silence, he surveyed the island once around and then turned back.

"Gather the people. It's time to work?"

"Huh?"

Kang blinked.

"We'll restore this place. We have to live, right?"

Goffin dug into the ground and uncovered ancient ruins buried under the volcanic ash.

For every 10 meters advanced, human bones appeared, and the natives quietly prayed to God.

'Found it.'

Deep in the ruins, a self-luminous red orb was shining.

"What's that?"

"A coordinate recorder."

It was probably the material of the Stargate used when the natives first arrived here.

"A signal body that gives the coordinates of this place from space?"

As long as the signal body was here, heaven's forces could invade at any time.

'This is the last.' So far, Goffin had found 73 signal bodies on this planet.

As light emanated from Goffin's body, the Kergo knelt and bowed their heads.

"Oh, Anke Ra! Look upon us!"

A Miracle Stream activated, and the dirt disappeared, creating a massive space instantly.

"This, how can this be!"

"We will make a door here."

Kang asked, moved.

"By door, do you mean the door of heaven that was lost in the volcanic explosion?"

"Yes." Goffin was rebuilding the door not for interaction with heaven but to create a shield.

Collecting heaven's coordinates in reverse, he was building a barrier that could not be penetrated by any means.

The Miracle Stream, swallowing the signal body, caused a material transformation, turning into a massive stone door.

Later, it was Goffin's door.

'I've installed 73 doors on the planet. Enough to create a dimensional wall.'

Kang asked.

"Messenger of God, can we go to heaven now?"

"To open this door, an infinite key is needed. Remember, do not damage the door marked with Goffin's name."

"I'll engrave it in steel."

Since the human Ogent appeared, Anke Ra's caution towards humans had reached its peak.

'The heaven expansion project must be considered a failure.'

According to Goffin's research, numerous human races had disappeared from space, and it was only a matter of time before it was this place's turn.

'It starts here.'

As Goffin left Galliant, he looked down at the planet from a height that captured its entire surface.

Small, but comforting.

It was a perfect starting point to continue his and Ikael's journey.

"Human, the end of Omega is not far."

As Goffin raised both hands, all the doors named Goffin across the planet activated.

"Grow stronger."

Pillars of light sprouted across the continents.

"Seek righteousness. Do not repeat. Remember, everyone has a heart."

The pillars of light converged in Goffin's hand, and he clenched his fist.

With a bang, the space rippled, and an invisible energy spread across the entire planet.

It was a dimensional wall.

'I can't rely on this forever.'

Before leaving, Goffin had one last task to complete, so he returned to the human world.

In the flow of Omega, Shirone found a place familiar to his senses.

'The Kingdom of Tormia.'

It was the city of Creas, where his alma mater, the Alpheus School of Magic, was located.

"Hey, how come without any notice!"

The director of the Creas Magic Association hurried over.

The world's greatest magician, McClaine Goffin, had visited, and even bringing the entire staff wouldn't have been enough.

"Don't make a fuss. I have some quiet work to do."

"Of course!"

Even a king would bow his head.

Goffin sat on the sofa and sipped coffee.

"I stopped by the holy war a few days ago and heard an interesting story. There's a student at the magic school with amazing talent."

The director, recalling the pinnacle of power—the Triple Emperor, the Seven Kings, the Two Kings—quickly found the desired answer.

"Ah, you mean Alpheus School of Magic. Yes, for the first time in school history, someone has passed the seventh stage of the survival test."

Goffin set down his coffee cup.

"Can I see that child? Preferably without anyone knowing."

Two days later, Goffin was staying at a shabby inn where no one knew he was there when there was a knock on the door.

Goffin, looking out the window, closed the curtain and turned around.

"Come in."

A quiet muttering came from outside the door.

"Even though we've met before, you're using informal language. It's annoying."

Although not intended for him to hear, Goffin's ears couldn't be fooled.

As he chuckled, the door opened.

"Excuse me. I heard that a very high-ranking person is looking for me... Oh?"

A young woman, still with a girl's air about her, blinked at Goffin's non-human appearance.

"Adrias family's Miro?"

"Yes, that's right..."

Goffin's first impression of Miro, transmitted through Shirone's intuition, had come full circle.

She was still a novice.
