Chapter 631:

Fire Mist heated the air, and Fire Strikes rained down on Pisho.

'He can't use insect magic here!'

As someone who controlled living creatures, Pisho's abilities were also limited in the toxic gas.

'One thing that bothers me…'

It was the grotesque insect attached to Pisho's right arm.

'A parasite?'

If he wore a gas-detoxifying insect on his face, the insect connected to his body could also withstand it.

"It's useless, Amy."

Despite being hit by several Fire Strikes, Pisho remained unfazed.

'I've never seen this ability before.'

If the creature was parasitic, it wouldn't be a problem for it to be considered part of his body.

'Then I'll cut it off!'

Screech!

Pisho's arm extended, and Argones lunged with its jaws wide open.

"This is nothing!"

For a schema user, it was easy to dodge, and Amy approached Pisho, chopping down on his arm.

The force could break bones, but surprisingly, there were no bones.

'What is this?'

As she felt the squishy sensation, Pisho grabbed her with his other hand.

'Trying to grapple?'

Twisting her legs to generate rotational force, Amy struck Pisho's abdomen, her fist sinking into his belly with a squelch.

The disgusting sensation made her shiver, but she quickly made a decision.

'I have to destroy his heart!'

Her hand, digging into his belly, grasped his heart, but again, it felt squishy.

"I am no longer human."

As Pisho's indifferent voice reached her, Argones bit her neck.

"Gasp!"

"Don't be afraid. You've passed the exam."

Her fist, lodged in his belly, slid out, and Amy's face went blank.

"As my loyal servant."

The alien creature Argones.

First reported to academia 230 years ago, this creature was believed to have traveled on a meteor from outside the solar system, maintaining its vitality even through atmospheric entry.

It kneads the host's cells, transforming the body into a completely different biological substance while retaining its appearance. Academia predicts that many humans are infected with Argones.

"You… you…"

Amy struggled to move her stiff tongue.

"Impressive. Maintaining consciousness even while infected."

Argones, parasitic on the host's body, infiltrates other creatures with pilot insects, taking over their central nervous system.

'No, I've lost control.'

Despite repeatedly backing up her self-image memory, her body wouldn't move as she willed.

"Don't struggle. It'll only make it worse for you."

Like a child blindly trusting their mother, Amy's body automatically responded to Pisho's commands.

"What have you… done to my body?"

As Pisho raised his right arm, the mushy form gradually returned to a human shape.

"Even lifespan is just a function created in the evolutionary process of cells. Argones, with no average lifespan, merges with the host's body, maintaining biological activity forever."

It was a form of eternal life.

"But it's only theoretical."

Pisho lowered his arm weakly.

"The human body, with cell division halted, will continue to accumulate errors, and I too will eventually become a vegetative state covered in cancer cells."

That was the fate of a human who accepted Argones.

"Don't worry. You won't become a monster like me. I'll extract it after the exam."

"Just… to pass… the exam…"

"I donated my body for the advancement of biology. After the graduation exam, I'll enter a quarantine facility."

Eventually, cancer cells would spread to his brain and burn him out, but Pisho's intellectual curiosity was stronger than that.

"Let's go, Amy."

Driven by the genetic impulse to obey, Amy slowly turned and followed Pisho.

"Shirone…"

Kneeling in front of the tearful Maya, Shirone touched her forehead.

"It's too hard… I want to stop…"

The world revered mages, but Maya, who had lived among them, was sick of the very word "magic."

"They're all crazy. If I stay any longer, I'll go mad."

Shirone knew it was a world that couldn't be endured with an ordinary mind.

"There's not much left. Just a little more…"

"I can't. The tribe, my siblings, I can't think of anything anymore."

Maya closed her eyes tightly and sobbed.

"I just… wanted to love you a little more…"

Maya wouldn't be able to endure.

"For the last time… I tried so hard… to sing…"

Watching Maya convulse, Shirone made a decision.

"Let's get out of here, Maya."

The combination of Luman's Ant Nest and Fjord's toxic gas would weaken the competitors' mental strength, so it wasn't a bad situation for Shirone.

'I can't love you, but…'

Focusing his partially recovered mental strength through No-Mind, the incarnation of the Light Angel appeared, aiming its spear outward.

'Elysion!'

The sense of direct perception pierced through the anti-magic and located Luman outside the Ant Nest.

"I can give you one more chance."

Incarnation Technique - Angel's Punishment.

As the spear of light pierced through the inner walls and exited the Ant Nest, Luman's eyes widened.

"What!"

By the time he shouted, the flash had already pierced his torso.

"Gah!"

-Participant Number 16 has exited the 2000 system.

As Luman lost consciousness, the Ant Nest crumbled, and the trapped participants quickly surveyed their surroundings.

'Damn, he hit Luman. Just a little longer…'

If it had lasted just 10 more minutes, someone would have been eliminated, making graduation easier.

'Maya! Where's Maya?'

Cayden thought only of Maya, and when he saw her in Shirone's arms, he looked confused.

"Why…?"

Meanwhile, Iruki quickly grasped the situation.

"So that's what happened."

Shirone had eliminated Luman to save Maya.

'An emotional decision. It's also a privilege of the strong, but…'

He wanted to graduate together, so it was disappointing.

'Are you really okay with this? This time, it was clearly a bad move.'

Shirone gently laid Maya down.

"This is all I can do for you."

"Shirone…"

Though Iruki couldn't analyze it precisely, he could sense that Shirone had sacrificed something.

"Just hold on a little longer. So you won't regret it later."

That alone made Maya feel like she had gained everything.

"Phew, I'm tired."

Cracking his neck, Shirone walked towards the participants, slowly raising his hand in a gesture to come at him.

At the same time, Cayden raised his cross sword and charged.

'I couldn't protect Maya!'

As Shirone braced himself for the attack, Cayden rushed past him and ran to Maya.

"Take this!"

Blocking Maya's path, he twisted his body and swung his sword, deflecting Richard's metal arm with a clang.

"Damn!"

With only four more eliminations needed to guarantee graduation, targeting Maya was logical. Richard didn't appreciate Cayden's decision to break that logic.

"Don't you care about the exam? You're crazy."

"You're one to talk."

He didn't want to hear about madness from someone who had modified 35% of their body.

'As long as Maya can be happy…!'

He had long since transcended jealousy.

'I can be as unhappy as needed!'

Demonic Sword Technique - Mega Tornado.

Richard, blown into the air by the strong wind, quickly regained his balance and locked onto Maya with his mechanical eyes.

'Eliminate Maya first!'

As the fire magic stone activated and flames spewed out, Cayden swung his cross sword at the incoming fire.

"Hyaaaah!"

The cold air extinguished the flames, and Richard's right arm rotated 45 degrees, equipping another attribute.

'So that's how it is!'

Meanwhile, other participants focused their attacks on Shirone.

'His reaction speed has definitely slowed! We're winning!'

The scouts analyzed that Shirone was too exhausted to even deploy the Force Dimension.

"After taking down Luman, his mental gauge dropped to 4%. This might be the end."

Baikal also felt regretful.

"The price of an emotional decision. That's why mages must remain cold."

Amy, having abandoned her specialty in sniping, relentlessly attacked with her fists while blinking her red eyes rapidly.

'Is she being controlled?'

The abnormal speed of her self-image memory backup added to his suspicion. Then Amy spoke.

"Foolish Shirone."

"What?"

Unable to control her body, she couldn't stop her thoughts from spilling out as words.

"Is Maya more important than the graduation exam?"

"That's…"

Shirone recoiled at her blunt words, and Iruki clicked his tongue as he flew in.

"You reap what you sow. Why show sympathy?"

Atomic Bomb's chain explosions attacked Shirone, and Sabina fired Wind Cutters into the blasts.

"Grrr!"

Maintaining Time Explosion was burdensome, and his mental gauge continued to drop.

"Wooooo!"

As Shirone, who had overwhelmed his competitors so far, faced elimination, the audience's cheers grew louder than ever.

"Quite sadistic…"

Baikal remarked.

"It's like a spectacle."

Elizabeth couldn't deny it.

"Geniuses who dedicated their lives to one path face their limits and despair. Is there any more entertaining spectacle?"

As a scout, he had witnessed countless competitions.

"Talent, effort, or wealth. If anyone conceded just a little, humanity could be happy right now. But such things have never happened in history. It makes me think it's not that we don't do it but that we can't."

"On the other hand, such fighting spirit has driven human progress."

"Of course. What I mean is…"

What he truly wanted to know was…

"If everyone is happy, why is progress necessary?"

What are we fighting for?

"I also rose to this position through competition. That's why Number 27's decision is the worst. Sometimes you just lose sight of what's truly important."

'This isn't right!'

Breaking free from the parasite's mental control, Amy fired Fire Strikes in all directions with her burning red eyes.

'Was there a place I couldn't reach?'

She sensed a deeper part of her mind untouched by the controlling hand.

'Dare give me shame?'

Entering a state of unity with her path, Amy heated her cells with fire energy as Elizabeth shouted,

"Her body temperature is over 42 degrees! This is dangerous!"

'She's trying to extract the pilot insect.'

Having settled in a human environment, rapid heating would force it out but could also be fatal.

'But she might die. Does she really want that?'

'Get out! Get out of my body!'

Even in her dazed state, Amy's self-image memory found the pilot insect controlling her central nervous system and focused heat there.

Seriel stood up and shouted,

"Amy! Don't do it!"

Human heat could kill bacteria but expelling a parasite required risking death.

'Even if I defeat Shirone…'

Her red eyes flared up like an explosion of light.

"I'll do it with my own will!"

A bulge formed on Amy's neck and burst open as a bug shot out.
