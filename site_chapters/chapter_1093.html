Chapter 1093: What is God? (3)

Shirone had a blank expression.

'It's not an illusion.'

Even his current thoughts seemed to flow backward, as if time were reversed.

"Huh? Huh?"

After a moment of confusion, Shirone quickly assessed his state with a clear mind.

'Come to think of it…'

His thoughts were the opposite when experiencing the multiverse and being in the outside world.

'Thoughts reverse based on inside and outside. In other words…'

Time flows backward.

"Hmm."

Deep in thought, Shirone moved straight through the undefined space.

'I am walking forward.' But is that true?

'What if I'm actually walking backward, and time is flowing in reverse?'

Let's conduct a thought experiment.

'If you record someone walking forward and play the video backward, what happens?' The movement will look awkward.

'But the reason it looks awkward is because we're used to our time. If time itself flowed backward…'

Shirone walking forward would look awkward instead.

'I get it.'

Shirone took a deep breath.

'The reason time reverses.'

This world is made of two signals, photons and quantum particles combined.

'That's the eye. An eye looking from inside out and an eye looking from outside in. Depending on that perspective… time's direction reverses.'

'For example.'

Shirone thought of a puppet.

'A puppet is connected by strings, with a control stick above its head. The puppet believes it can move on its own.'

At least it believes so.

'The control stick moves. The puppet raises its right arm. Now, here's the question.'

'The puppet thinks it raised its right arm, causing the control stick to move.'

This is how humans perceive the world.

'To put it more intuitively… I dug the ground, so the ground should be dug.'

In other words, the puppet raising its arm is the cause, and the control stick moving is the effect.

'From the outside world's perspective…'

The control stick moving is the cause, and the puppet raising its right arm is the effect.

'It changes based on perspective. So cause and effect completely reverse…'

And so does the thought system.

'The sense of receiving time also reverses.'

Shirone shivered.

'For God…'

Time flows backward.

'Ikael's words to me. Bedium's last will. That's what they meant.'

It's narrowing down.

'God is time. So how does that God's thought process work?'

What is a result for us is a cause for them.

'I marry her because I love her. That's human thinking. But…'

God would say:

'I marry her. Because I will love her.' Such reversal.

'I can use magic. Because I will learn magic. I became the world's greatest swordsman. Because I will work hard enough. I went to prison. Because I will become a murderer.'

A reversal where results humans must achieve through countless wills are achieved from the start.

What is God?

'The end of all time.'

A result.

'God is a result for us.'

Expanding this thought can infer what the outside world in reality is…

'Knowing all history, Omega. If you reverse the world from God's perspective…'

Civilizations decline, life disappears, the universe shrinks, and finally reaches that point.

'There will be a moment when the beginning starts. And applying God's perspective there…'

Shirone bit his lip.

"There is another universe with negative time."

The outside world.

'A universe was born through some amplification. But that universe wasn't one,

it was two.'

If there are two directions of time, there must be two universes as well.

'And that universe has reversed causality. Based on this inference…'

He was devastated by a terrible truth.

"The final point of our world, this place's result, becomes the beginning in the outside world."

Why is that dangerous?

'Humankind will eventually conquer space.'

When humans reach the same position as their creator, having mastered everything that exists,

they become gods.

'And paradoxically, that god exists at the moment of negative universe's birth.'

What kind of god?

What do we ultimately become?

"Machines."

Like High Gear, at the pinnacle of mechanical civilization without lifespan, aging, or disease…

'A program.'

Sent by administrators from the outside world, moving by their mission…

'Why are administrators needed?' Though just a hypothesis now, their judgment made sense strangely enough.

Because they see from God's perspective.

'There are two universes. The problem is, the outside world's universe knew everything from the start.'

Because they started as gods.

'But in that world, God is heading towards extinction.'

Becoming human, becoming life, becoming matter, and eventually meeting an end.

'Even God can't stop time. God is time. So what choice does God make?'

"Control the outcome of the world we live in."

Multiverse.

"Never reach the final outcome, spreading this world like bubbles."

A diagram of the entire universe formed in his mind.

'In the beginning, there was light.'

The universe spread out from that light, with time running both positively and negatively.

'One side follows human causality. The other follows divine causality.'

In the coordinates where human causality applies, infinite universes bubble like foam.

'In other words, God manages this place. And as long as those bubbles don't disappear and are maintained…'

The God on the other side is eternal.

'Without being downgraded to human, it extends endlessly. Ultimately controlling our causality.'

What grudge would God hold?

'Cold calculation. Logical judgment. After all, God is… our final form.'

Shirone took a deep breath.

"I found it, Gaold. Where God is."

But he couldn't meet Him.

'To meet God, one must leave reality for the outside world. Now I understand why we shouldn't approach the outside world. Why Argones activates.'

Because it's an act of killing God.

'Has Nane already met Him? Probably. But if he hasn't come… - Am I Nane who swallowed Anke Ra's dream? Or Anke Ra who swallowed Nane's dream?'

Shirone shook his head.

'Enough idle thoughts. I'll think about it outside. First, let's return to the original world.'

Now he had a rough idea of how.

'I can reverse it.'

If time has a negative direction, the mind can also become a signal that reverses.

'Using God's perspective, God's thought.'

Reverse causality.

"Huuu!"

It was a task of twisting the standards of the world and the essence of humanity, but Shirone was Yahweh.

'I can do it. If it were impossible, I wouldn't have realized the reversal of thought here.'

He started with the smallest things.

'I exist.'

From there, countless thoughts began to reverse as if time were being rewound.

"Ugh!"

It was a grand task that shattered the 5-dimensional multi-cube, the foundation of Shirone's knowledge.

'I will return.'

To the place where his loved ones are.

'I will go.'

As his mind gradually reversed, light began to seep into the undefined space.

His head felt like it was splitting.

"Aaah!"

Darkness fell like shards, and light suddenly burst in through the gaps with rough force.

'Reversal of causality.'

Shirone recalled when he entered the Taiji while fighting Giyorgi.

'Goffin's message.' - They are watching you. Trust no one. Save yourself from the terrible truth.

'So that's what it was.'

In the perfectly reversed 5-dimensional multi-cube, Shirone defined God's thought as this:

Ouroboros.

"We won! We did it!"

As Dr. Martin's machine crashed to the ground, users cheered.

"This means no end of service, right? I almost lost all my parts."

"Are parts important? This is our base. What fun is life without High Gear?"

As East and West Country users chatted amicably, some remained silent.

'Yahweh.'

They were users who had exchanged hearts with Shirone in the world of High Gear.

The Operator looked up at the sky.

'Where did he go?'

Abnormally exiting the world of High Gear wasn't unrelated to reality.

Fermi approached Uorin.

"The situation is quite serious. Even if he exited, I thought it was reality, but he hasn't woken up."

Golden Wheel's subordinates confirmed that Shirone was still asleep.

"It's okay."

Uorin's voice trembled.

"He'll come back. That's the kind of person he is. He can't abandon this world no matter what."

"What if he doesn't come back?" She had never thought about what she would do in a world without Shirone.

"I'd probably be angry." Her voice was calm, but Little Witch's face was twisted.

"After that, I don't know."

Making expressions with a mechanical face meant the signal itself was distorted.

'Eve was originally Gaia.'

For some reason, Ultima seemed to have disappeared, but her current appearance was purely eerie.

"Hey, look!"

Destroyer707 pointed to the sky.

Everyone looked up, and the Operator's shoulders began to tremble.

"Ah…"

The sky seemed to swirl and then revealed a familiar machine.

The result came first.

"Yahweh!"

As if gravity had collapsed, a hole opened in the sky, and light surged from the ground.

"Huh? He's back?"

As Fermi spoke, Uorin dashed forward.

"Aaah!"

What was I trying to do?

'Yes, I only need you. It's okay if you don't love me. Just stay in this world.'

Compared to the despair she felt when Yahweh disappeared, the current situation was a blessing.

As Shirone landed, he was certain.

'I'm back.'

Even if it was no different from countless other illusions, his heart clearly shouted that this was the place.

Uorin ran towards him.

"Here! Here!"

And just as she was about to throw herself, the Operator ran from the side and hugged Shirone.

"Hey…"

Uorin stopped, and the users looked at her in surprise.

"You idiot!"

The Operator shouted.

"Why are you so reckless? What's Dr. Martin compared to that? High Gear can be rebuilt. But if something happened to you…"

There would be nowhere left to go.

Perhaps it was a fear like losing her parents, so Shirone understood her.

"Sorry. But I came back like this."

Destroyer707's team and users rushed over.

"It was amazing. This Solar War was the best experience of my life! Thanks to you!"

"Shall we contact each other outside? Oh, you're human, right? I'll give you my address…"

Fermi placed a hand on Uorin's shoulder.

"Don't be too disheartened. It's not love. Though who knows what the future holds."

"Hmph, what do you take me for? Ten thousand years is nothing. Do you think I'd be shaken by one kid?"

"True."

Fermi was also satisfied with the outcome, and Shirone, leading the users, said.

"Let's go back."

To the world we live in.
