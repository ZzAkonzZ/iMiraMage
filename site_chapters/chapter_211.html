Chapter 211: The Truth of Heaven (5)

“It’s a double lock!”

“That’s right. Quite meticulous, isn't he? I'll show you Arcane's memories now, so check it out for yourself."

Harvest's memories flowed in. It seemed Arcane had come to Heaven with his comrades.

In the midst of this, he entered Ingris alone.

As expected, Eureka proposed a contract.

Arcane accepted the proposal, staking his unrestricted access to Ingris. At the time, she was already mad, so as soon as the contract was concluded, she began to attack Arcane.

'Master….'

Unlike Canis, who couldn't even lift a hand in the battle, Arcane engaged in close combat with Eureka without being pushed back at all.

'Unbelievable. How can he cast magic while his words are being edited?'

Canis delved a little deeper into the memories.

The solution was as ingenious as one would expect from Arcane. Using magic from the Abyss system, he erased all his memories.

If thoughts disappear, there's no concept to sell.

It didn't end there. Arcane began restoring the morphemes of the language one by one. (Note: A morpheme is the smallest unit of language that contains meaning. The word "incoming" has three morphemes "-in," "come," and "-ing.")

The aim was to find Mara's name by comparing all possible combinations of morphemes with the editor's reactions.

At one point, as the nearly infinite number of words were being assembled and disassembled, Eureka suddenly screamed.

Pale-faced, she flew away to a place without Arcane, not even looking back.

The position where Arcane's language combination roulette stopped was Eureka.

“…Is this possible He combined tens of thousands of languages in that brief moment."

"There are no limits to what can happen in mind. Well, even so, you can't write novels with it. It's mathematical. He laid out the morphemes, created an algorithm, and spun it like a roulette. His brain must have been quite sharp in his younger days. After all, he was a genius, just look at the fact that he created me. If he hadn't been so fixated on dark magic, he wouldn't have remained just a third-rate mage."

Canis felt proud to be Arcane's disciple.

Besides, wasn't it because of the note left behind by his master that he was able to defeat Eureka?

Perhaps Eureka had been hiding until Arcane left. During that time, Arcane must have found out Eureka's information and written down the serial number.

The last memory of Arcane entered Canis' mind. A shiver ran through him as he learned the true reason for the double lock.

Arcane was passing the location to his disciple through memories, not records.

Canis walked to where Arcane had last stood, and, following his master's movements, extended his hand toward the panel.

'Master, thank you.'

Of course, Arcane at the time would not have known that he would be taking Canis as his disciple. But the message he left for his future successor was clear.

Mage, remain an eternal youth.

Canis took out the panel designated by Arcane and converted the language. He confirmed the sentence that appeared on the glass plate.

The Book of Light and Darkness.

Canis quickly skimmed through the pages. One phrase caught his eye and he stared at it intently.

Harvest placed a hand on his shoulder and said,

"You've achieved your purpose in coming to Heaven."

"No. I can't say that until we get back safely. I have to rescue Arin."

“I guess so. Do you think they're okay over there? We've been delayed quite a bit."

Canis handed the panel to Harvest and went to the central control device.

"It's the right time for Rian to arrive. Copy the records. I'll try to disable the Meca system."

Harvest placed his palm on the glass plate and copied the text in its entirety. He would turn the pages and memorize the vibration patterns.

When Canis arrived at the central control device, he couldn't touch anything. Cold sweat ran down his forehead.

He had watched Eureka touch the screen and understood the principle, but it was impossible to handle equipment he had never seen before like an expert.

"Damn it, why is it so complicated?"

Canis kept pressing random buttons. Even if he made the worst mistake, what could happen other than a malfunction?

At some point, the entire map of Zebul appeared.

When he touched the Great Hall of World, the screen zoomed in, revealing numerous entries in succession.

As he entered the security system, he noticed the setting to disable the Meca system.

Without hesitation, Canis chose to deactivate it.

Several areas that were shown as dotted lines on the map became clear.

"Canis, I've saved all the records. Since I remembered them as patterns, I'll have to convert them to language when I have time."

"Good. I found something too. Looking at the map, every area of Zebul has a portal to Ingris. In other words, this place is the heart of Zebul. So, if we use the portal in reverse, we can exit through the portal closest to the Great Hall of World."

Canis and Harvest ran towards the end of Ingris.

It's fortunate that things worked out well, but the situation hadn't improved much.

Arin was still there.

They could only hope that Rian wouldn't be late.

A mass of Ikael's memories flooded into Shirone's subconscious. The order of time was jumbled, but it was faster than the speed of thought, so there was no sense of contradiction.

Shirone was shocked when he saw the form of Ra that Ikael had conveyed. It was the pinnacle of grotesqueness. However, the level of intelligence it contained surpassed that of the current human world.

"Even if parts change, the whole remains perfect."

He was the first intelligence to realize the existence of the universe.

"The universe is something God abandoned. No one knows it exists! But I gave it meaning. Therefore, this universe is mine.”

The first beings to whom Ra gave meaning were angels.

The first entity of causality became the second entity, and from there, it was further subdivided into giants and fairies. Each acted according to the law, spreading Ra's will.

Ikael took Shirone to a world where countless creatures lived, but there was no one who could give them meaning.

A giant, standing two kilometers tall, arrived through the dimensional gate. As the giant stood up, its face pierced the clouds, and the mountain range lay prostrate below its waist.

Ikael had also informed him about the giant.

It is said that Elixir of Life has a total of 10 stages, and only giants who surpassed the sixth stage could leave for a new world.

The giant roared. Its body began to gradually disintegrate, with blood and flesh falling like rain.

Another long time passed… and humans began to appear one by one in the place where the giant had fallen.

When humans formed societies and developed civilization, an angel came and granted a child to a girl's body. One day, the child would become a Nephilim and lead people to Heaven.

As time passed, changes began to occur in human society.

Humans, with their ability to give meaning, grew rapidly and eventually became so intoxicated with their own power that they began to turn away from God.

The army of Heaven descended and punished them.

The battle was one-sided. The power of Heaven was overwhelming, and angels and Maras destroyed numerous civilizations.

Yet humans persisted and continued to repeat the same mistakes.

One day, a group that denied God appeared on an island called Galliant in the south.

Archangel Ikael herself stepped forward. She, who could activate the power of all things, amplified the volcanic activity of Galliant by more than 20,000 times, causing a volcanic eruption.

Time quickly passed, and a world similar to the era Shirone lived in unfolded.

Humans were still prospering.

Judging that blasphemy had reached its peak, Anke Ra finally decided to annihilate humanity.

The final war. The end of the world.

Shirone watched the situation in Heaven at that time.

A portal, tens of meters high, was open, and the army of Heaven, composed of giants, fairies, and divine subjects, was preparing to march.

In the sky, the Vibration of Angels resonated, and on the ground, the roars of armored Maras echoed.

'No! Absolutely not!'

Shirone shouted, forgetting that the scene unfolding before his eyes was all in the past. There was no way to win. If Heaven's army entered, the world would end.

As the order to advance was given, the massive army, led by angels, surged into the portal.

Shirone's consciousness also followed them into the portal. After passing through a dazzling tunnel, an exit appeared.

His stomach churned with tension. This was a past memory.

But then, how had humanity managed to stop Heaven's army?

'There is no such war recorded in history.'

As soon as their destination came into view, the Maras began to roar with fighting spirit. Shirone flew ahead of them. And at the moment she reached the end of the portal, he froze.

What unfolded before his eyes was not the original world.

A pale, empty space. And there, a woman stood.

It was Adrias Miro.

"Goffin, I will never forgive you."

Miro was crying. As she glared at the massive army alone, she raised her fist and cast a spell.

Then, something astonishing happened. The leading Maras began to stop as if blocked by an invisible barrier.

The angels looked at Miro in bewilderment. In contrast, the Maras pounded on the barrier, shrieking.

"You unholy thing! You will pay the price for betraying Ra!"

“Remember this well, Miro! The end has only been postponed! When the time comes, you will be the first to die!"

Shirone learned the whole truth.

Another space-time was created between the gaping hole between heaven and the human world, severing the connection.

That was… Miro's space-time.

A burly man flew to the end of the portal from the group of angels.

It was Archangel Uriel.

"How long do you think you can hold out by doing this? There is no way to avoid the final war. We will return. Use the little time you have left wisely."

Miro bit her lip until it bled. As the new space-time filled in, the distance between them grew.

Uriel glared at Miro fiercely and then turned around. Then, the angels started to return to Heaven one by one.

However, the Maras remained until the end, hurling curses. They screamed desperately until Miro's figure could no longer be seen.

Shirone also moved away with Heaven's army. But she couldn't take her eyes off Miro.

He realized why the great magician Arcane had tried so hard not to let the world know, even erasing his own memories.

One day, there was a moment when humanity was on the brink of destruction.

And Miro, even now, was staying in her own space, delaying the end of the world.

The Angel's Magic Formula (1)

Shirone opened his eyes.

The first thing he felt was a sense of refreshment. It was as if he had just woken up from a long sleep.

How long had he been asleep?

Shirone, whose thoughts had reached that point, raised his body and shouted.

“No! My friends…!”

Ikael reassured Sirone.

"Don't worry. You couldn't have been asleep for more than five minutes."

“But why is my body…?”

“Your mind became clearer as my light penetrated. I could activate everything.”

As he said, his mental strength, which had been exhausted from fighting battles one after another from Ikrin to Tabu, had completely recovered.

"Oh, thank you. I didn't even realize..."

“It is fine. By the way, how do you feel now that you know Heaven's secret?"

Shirone tried to recall the memories Ikael had given him. It was hazy, as if a drean, but it was definitely not a dream.

He had learned an astonishing truth. Especially shocking was the fact that Miro had done such things.

"The ability to connect dimensions is unique to angels. But we have been passing down the angels' abilities to humans for a long time."

“That’s what Nephilim are.”

“Yes. Nephilim are the ones who lead humans to God. However, some Nephilim do the opposite. Most of those civilizations break away from the will of God and build their own civilizations. Just like the world that Shirone lived in.”

Shirone understood why the ancient magic apparatus was different. At some point, humans deviated from God's will, and the magic they had developed independently was the Spirit Zone.
