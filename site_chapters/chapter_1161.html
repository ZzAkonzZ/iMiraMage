Chapter 1161:

As a result, in the Great Purification, Shagal had never met Raphael or Etella.

But the Underlying Incident.

Ra Enemy reconstructed false memories for Shagal based on information up until he was seven years old.

“Waaah! Waaah!”

Ra Enemy, who wished to become human, sought out Shagal to awaken his sense of smell.

From that moment, every piece of information implanted into his memories had been an outright lie.

“Tia…”

And Raiden had remained in Shagal’s memories as a vivid scent.

‘Then… what was I doing before I met Ra Enemy?’

The Karma Management Department exposed the raw truth through its scent—an answer to that unknown.

A lonely figure muttering to himself in the depths of an unnamed mountain, clutching a rock, waving his hands aimlessly.

“… Ah.”

There was no one else.

He had lived over twenty years in those fabricated memories, utterly alone.

“… Ahhh.”

As if a screen had split apart, Shagal’s false memories bled into the scent.

The day the violent gang attacked the Fallen Leaf Circus.

“Master! Tia!”

Amidst countless corpses, he had found Tia and then saw Raiden, who had killed them all.

Raiden had spoken.

"Ra Enemy."

Back then, Shagal had been incapable of grasping the meaning.

But now, in the present, he suddenly understood.

‘He knew all along.’

Even while trapped in false memories as if under hypnosis, his sense of smell had detected the deception.

‘I knew… that none of this was real.’

Raiden lunged forward.

"Ra Enemy!"

A duel of quick-draw swords unfolded, and Shagal killed his own master, Raiden.

For the first time, Shagal, now in reality, could see the true, lifeless face of his former mentor.

‘Raphael.’

Master.

The single lesson Raphael had imparted to him, a truth that transcended time and memory—

"Forgive."

Forgive everything that ruined your life.

"Ahhhhh!"

Shagal clutched his head and screamed in agony.

‘Forgive?’

His entire life had been stolen from him. And he was supposed to forgive that?

‘Why?!’

He at least wanted a reason.

"Why should I forgive?! It wasn’t my fault! Why do I have to bear the responsibility?!"

The chain between them carried Shagal’s rage directly into Etella, who struggled to calm herself.

“Stay calm! If you lose control here—”

“Ahhhhh!!”

Shagal didn’t want to hear a single word. The moment he screamed, the scent of his memories shifted.

"Ah…"

After killing Raiden in his memories, Shagal had spent his time chasing Ra Enemy, wandering between reality and dreams.

If the Underlying Incident’s perfume still lingered, then perhaps meeting Raphael had been inevitable.

‘Strong.’

The previous successor of the Yin-Yang Wave Fist had driven Shagal, already a world-renowned killer, into a corner.

Raphael clicked his tongue.

‘So this is Shagal. A horribly twisted man. If left unchecked, he’ll become a poison to the world.’

That’s what he thought.

Yet he still couldn't kill him.

From the moment he saw Shagal, an inexplicable pain had surged in his chest.

‘I don’t understand.’

What had happened? What words had they exchanged?

‘I forgot.’

Like a memory from a past life, like when he once buried his face in his mother’s treasured keepsake.

‘So that’s how it was.’

He could never truly know what had happened.

But Raphael had embraced the existence of Shagal nonetheless.

‘That’s how it was, Shagal?’

Raphael had spoken.

“Whichever path you take, the way back will always be behind you.”

The quick-draw sword pierced Raphael’s heart, followed by several more slashes.

“Hah… hah…”

In the pitch-black darkness, devoid of even moonlight, Shagal stared at Raphael’s lifeless body.

Of course, Shagal’s skill was far beyond the point of needing an opponent to hold back for him.

But there was something… strange.

"Did he stop at the last moment?"

It wasn’t an accident. A single misstep in that moment should have cost him his life.

No answer came.

“… What a joke."

The words that had haunted his mind endlessly now came back to him like a cruel boomerang.

‘What was it you were trying to tell me?’

Even Raphael probably didn’t know.

But if he had managed to suppress his rage, if he had spoken with him just a little longer…

“You bastards!"

Eyes bloodshot, Shagal inhaled the perfume, sucking in every trace of memory.

The moment he met Etella, the moment he mistook her for Tia, and every step leading up to the present.

"Why is this happening to me?!"

It was all a manipulation.

The rage of having his entire life played with consumed him completely.

"I’ll kill every last one of them!"

Shagal had been made into a murderer after the fact.

“I’ll wipe out every single one of them!”

And now… he had become the worst of the worst.

The doors of the Karma Management Department swung open, and agents stormed in, alerted by the commotion.

As they realized their comrades had been wiped out, their faces twisted with fury.

“How dare you… Ugh!”

A quick-draw sword pierced through, puncturing the vital points of all the agents standing by the door.

“Kiiii! Kiiii!”

The indiscriminate hatred toward the world focused entirely on the agents, its sheer intensity terrifying.

“Shagal! Stop!”

Etella clung to the chain, desperately trying to hold him back, but it was beyond her ability to handle.

“Hah!”

More of the chain unraveled from her chest as Shagal burst through the door and dashed into the corridor.

‘It keeps extending.’

As the Taeguk’s effect expanded, the chain connecting them grew endlessly longer.

‘If this continues, what will happen?’

Etella was about to run into the hallway to stop Shagal, but she suddenly halted.

‘Master.’

A realization struck her—an answer to the question that had lingered in her mind ever since she had heard of his death.

‘You sacrificed yourself… for Shagal.’

That was virtue.

Watching the chain continue to unravel from her chest, Etella slowly closed her eyes.

‘I will honor your will, Master.’

For a moment, she focused on the warmth in her lower abdomen. Then, her eyes snapped open, and she ran outside.

The Six-Brained Organ was in the opposite direction from Shagal.

Thormia, the Code Source of the South.

With Captain Rai leading the guard, Shirone and Lupist arrived at the meeting point.

They were deep in the mountains, where the warriors of the Southern tribes watched them with the keen eyes of beasts.

“Chieftain, Shirone has arrived.”

By directly mentioning his name, Lupist had effectively relinquished his own authority to speak.

“It’s an honor to meet you. I am Shirone.”

The chieftain, Untara, was a gaunt-looking old man, yet the tribespeople treated him with great reverence.

That alone revealed how the Southern tribes perceived life itself.

“Welcome, Yahweh.”

Untara remained seated, yet his voice carried an undeniable air of courtesy.

“I heard you wished to meet me. Why?”

Of course, Shirone was curious about something else.

‘How did he stop the Killing Intent?’

As if reading his thoughts, Untara smiled faintly.

“I wanted to meet you once. I have heard that Yahweh pursues an integrated mental system.”

It felt like he had answered two questions at once.

“The reason the South, despite being underdeveloped compared to the rest of the world, was able to participate in the Holy War is that we suffered little damage from the demons. And the greatest contributor to that was a man named Gaold.”

Gaold’s rampage had once annihilated the demons of the Southern continent, leaving them utterly defenseless.

“He was the epitome of human destruction. But no single man can defend the vast South alone. We also had to find a way to protect ourselves.”

Untara’s gaze sharpened.

“Elychia.”

Shirone listened intently.

“All the tribes of the South worship different gods. To honor their gods, they dance and sing through the night. In civilized terms, they enter a trance state, ultimately reaching ecstasy.”

“I see.”

Shirone understood what Untara was getting at.

“Elychia is a collective dance and song, created by the unified hearts of the Southern tribes—a technique that can alter the world's laws.”

Of course, it was different from Ultima.

Using ecstasy was merely emotional integration, disregarding individuality.

‘But perhaps…’

In practical terms, this might be the closest thing humanity could achieve to Ultima at this stage.

“I will show you myself.”

At Untara’s signal, the tent opened, revealing a group of warriors standing amid the torchlight.

Seventy, precisely.

“Elychia.”

At the murmur of the leading warrior, seventy voices resonated in unison.

“Ooooo… Ooooo…”

They bounced their upper bodies rhythmically, bent their waists, and stepped in unison.

As they traversed cross-shaped patterns, the ground trembled with every synchronized stomp.

While the Thormian officials observed, Rai gripped the hilt of his sword.

“Ooooo… Ooooo…”

Time passed, and one by one, the seventy tribespeople entered a state of trance.

‘The laws are shifting.’

He couldn’t see it with his eyes, but through the Spirit Zone, Shirone could feel it clearly.

‘Unless minds are perfectly unified, quantum phenomena won’t occur. In the end… this is a trance state.’

As the rhythm accelerated, bright ripples of light flickered in the eyes of every warrior.

‘A signal.’

But a signal for what?

“Hoo! Ha! Hoo!”

The warriors stomped the ground violently and, as one, lifted their heads toward the tent—glaring straight at Shirone.

“Kraaaah!”

A colossal phantom beast, formed from the amassed light, lunged directly at Shirone.

‘Just as I expected.’

Despite the hostility of the attack, Shirone observed it indifferently.

As if hitting an invisible barrier, the phantom beast flattened like a coin right before reaching him.

Clap. Clap. Clap.

Slow, deliberate applause echoed as Untara sat with a composed smile—his throat now caught in the blade of Rai’s great sword.

“This was an act of aggression. He must be killed.”

The only reason Rai had hesitated to strike was that Shirone’s power was holding his sword back.

“The Captain of the Guard is correct. However… I think we should listen a little longer.”

At Shirone’s words, Rai smoothly withdrew his sword and returned to his position.

Untara’s eyes curved like a seagull’s wings.

“Impressive. To nullify Elicia, the South’s deadliest technique… You truly understand the depths of the mind.”

“It wasn’t that strong.”

Even Shirone’s provocation didn’t faze Untara.

“Indeed. The strength was merely this much. I apologize for displaying such crude skill.”

Lupist, unable to hold back, interrupted.

“What are you trying to say?”

“What I mean is… there are over seven million people in the South who can perform this.”

For the first time, a chill ran down Shirone’s spine.

“You want to know how I stopped the Killing Intent? The power of unity—the force that arises from the integration of seven million minds. And I wish to offer this power to you, Yahweh.”

Untara pointed at Shirone.

“For someone who has mastered the art of the mind, using Elicia would be like giving Yahweh wings. Ultima… may still be distant, but with the South’s abilities, I can offer you at least seven million people as a gift. However, before that, I have one question for you.”

“You want to know what I can give the South in return?”

Untara laughed.

“Ha-ha! You’re more blunt than I expected. Well, it is a reasonable question. But you’re wrong. Things that have clear answers do not need to be asked.”

“Then… what is it you wish to know?”

Sensing his willingness to negotiate, Untara leaned forward, resting both arms on the table.

“What… will you do with this power I offer you?”
