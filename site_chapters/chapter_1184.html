Chapter 1184:

As Shirone’s group arrived at the solar convective zone, flames coalesced, giving birth to a phoenix.

Even the phoenix of a planet was powerful, but one born in the Sun was on an entirely different level.

"So, you have finally come."

The star energy exuded by the phoenix reduced the Miracle Stream’s diameter by a full meter.

"I have been waiting for you, Ikael."

The group received the phoenix’s words through Ultima, Idea, and their spiritual bodies.

Ikael spoke.

"I have come to meet Satiel."

"I expected as much. But it is a foolish endeavor. Nothing can defy the order of the whole."

"You cannot know without trying. Even if this universe perishes, the heart will remain."

She wanted to believe that.

"… Do as you wish. This is your sanctuary. But humans cannot enter the core. In fact, I do not even understand how you are standing here."

The phoenix looked over the man enveloped in light and the naked woman, then ignited its flames.

"Leave!"

Even with the Miracle Stream forming a protective barrier, Shirone felt his consciousness waver.

‘This energy is overwhelming.’

Amy, knowing time was of the essence, stepped outside the barrier.

Blinding light poured down, and her body ignited once more.

The phoenix narrowed its eyes.

"The Mother of Light… How could a human reach the source of the signal? It is impossible."

"Go, Ikael," Amy said as she slowly floated.

"I will handle this."

Ikael hesitated briefly before steeling her resolve and stepping out of the Miracle Stream’s protection.

“Hmph!”

Like a candle, her body was extinguished, and her spirit emerged within the Sun’s flames.

"I am sorry, Saint Mother of the Sun, but I cannot allow Satiel to be erased like this. That is what the heart means."

The phoenix glared at Amy.

"It is futile. You cannot persuade a god. No being can."

Ikael answered with silence, bowed, and then slipped into the Sun’s core.

"So, you are the human who wields the Idea of Fire."

The phoenix straightened its upper body.

"I acknowledge that you have the right to be here. But in the end, you cannot surpass me."

"Are you sure about that?"

Amy’s flames burned with the brightest white light, as if on the verge of an explosion.

Shirone bit his lip.

‘A six-meter diameter.’

Within the Sun’s brutal conditions, the range of Hexa’s power was barely enough for close combat.

‘I have to wait for the right moment.’

At that instant, the phoenix expanded its form massively and charged at Amy like an arrow.

"Be annihilated!"

The impact was so powerful that Shirone felt as if space itself was collapsing.

Meanwhile, deep within the Sun’s core, Satiel and Raiel fought desperately.

"Ughhh!"

Every time they tried to reverse the flow of the universe, it felt as though their spiritual bodies were burning away.

"Why…?"

Satiel cried out.

"Why do you deny us?! Why, why?!"

The entity representing the god Anke Ra had never once revealed itself to them.

"What is our purpose?!"

For the authority of God.

The moment humans reached divinity, causality would be reversed, and the roles of creator and creation would be exchanged.

‘Even so…’

What authority does a single error named Yahweh have to overturn the entire universe?

‘What are we, the ones you created? What happens to the noble concept of the archangels? The world you built, the rules you established—do you have no sense of responsibility for creation?’

God had no heart.

From the perspective of the imaginary number realm, it was nothing more than a cold existence removing errors according to a program.

Ikael pierced through the radiative layer.

"Satiel."

As she had fully entered in her spiritual form, there was no way to conceal her emotions.

"Stop this now. Become human. You, and I, both need to become human."

"What about the other angels?!"

Satiel’s eyes burned with resentment.

"You always think only of yourself! What about the angels without emotions? They will be nothing but tools for humans!"

"Satiel, that’s not—"

"Resist!"

Raiel’s spirit rammed into Ikael, sending a flood of chaotic signals between them.

"I am sorry, Ikael. But I cannot allow this. I cannot become human."

"If we continue this way, we will be crushed beneath the wheels of the Law. Without Yahweh, we cannot withstand it."

"Haha! Is that so?!"

Satiel’s eyes darkened with malice.

"Goffin, you disgraceful human-lover! Don’t lump us in with you!"

"Satiel, even your actions are not truly for the angels. You just want power to take revenge on me—"

"And what of it?!"

Satiel’s shriek sent tremors through the Sun’s core.

"Yes! I have gained a heart, too! You received hope, passion, peace, and love from Goffin, didn’t you?!"

Satiel howled.

"But what I received was hatred, resentment, rage, betrayal, despair, and humiliation! That is my heart!"

Raiel’s spirit trembled like a ripple.

"Satiel…"

In this state, where the spirit could not conceal truths, Ikael finally understood her.

‘So that’s it.’

She had no desire to see Ikael suffer.

‘How beautiful.’

Ikael was grateful that she could recall her precious time with Goffin.

"Satiel is right."

Ikael spoke.

"My heart is filled with hope, peace, and love. But Satiel’s heart contains only hatred, resentment, and betrayal."

They were completely opposite.

"But in the end, it is still the heart."

Raiel’s spirit wavered in conflict.

"The Law divided Satiel and me into the chosen and the forsaken. But the heart does not. I, too, know pain. I, too, know resentment."

The pain of losing the one she loved most.

"We can undo this. It is possible in this place. Let Satiel go."

"No! Raiel, don’t do it!"

What was she afraid of? Satiel, half-mad, violently shook her head.

"Satiel will not yield."

"I will prevent the Law from changing. I will help. I cannot lose Satiel like this."

There was no way to refute it. Raiel, resigned, released the restraints.

"I refuse! Don’t come near me! I would rather be erased than share a mind with you!"

Satiel fought desperately to resist, but Ikael ultimately merged her spirit with hers.

As the three archangels shared one mind, Ataraxia activated.

"Gah!"

The concepts of disintegration and light intensified, shaking the Sun’s core violently.

"Ah, aah…"

Satiel, unable to reject the rising serenity, had no choice but to acknowledge it.

‘Yes, I already knew.’

Only Ikael was worthy of being the Archangel.

‘I already know. Goffin was never taken from me. From the beginning, Goffin always loved Ikael. But… what about me? Now, I don’t even exist. There’s no one left for me to resent.’

“I’m sorry,” Ikael said.

“I monopolized it all. Everything you wanted—I took it all for myself.”

"You…"

The moment Satiel tried to push Ikael’s mind away, a shocking signal surged through her.

"Goffin…"

Lies were impossible here.

"He was always grateful to you. And he always felt sorry. Because he could never love you. Because he hurt you."

Tears poured from Satiel’s eyes.

"Hnghh…"

Nothing had disappeared.

"Goffin never resented you. If not for you, our love could never have been realized. In fact, he was always worried about you."

Uncontrollable sobs erupted from her.

‘I… I…’

Even without a body, even after leaving the photon system, Goffin was still here.

"Now, focus, Satiel."

There was no form to a spirit, yet Satiel felt Ikael embracing her from behind.

"We will manifest this."

"Hngh!"

Her hatred hadn’t disappeared yet, but she no longer wanted to push Ikael away.

‘I’m going.’

Amplification, light, disintergration—three immense concepts shook the Sun’s interior.

Zodiac Twelve of Delta Headquarters.

In the chamber, where only a single black hole remained, Kitra immediately sensed the reversal of order.

"You persist in interfering."

With only moments left before divine law was completed, the displeasure grew.

Of course, it was merely an emotional reaction—no archangel could overturn the will of God.

"You call it physics."

Countless equations had been devised by the creations of this world, which merely existed as it was.

"Creations, answer me. Is there ever a case where one plus one does not equal two? No. Because God decreed it so. Therefore, no matter what you do…"

Kitra spread his arms wide.

"I am omnipotent, omniscient, and absolute."

As the black hole doubled in size, pyramids across the world began emitting light.

The radius of overlapping events expanded.

As Shirone was hurled by the shockwave of Amy’s phoenix, an eerie sensation gripped him.

‘The laws of the Sun are shifting drastically.’

That was the feeling.

The simultaneous events cascaded through time and space, revealing the full scope of the situation.

‘Kitra.’

With the magnetic fields around the pyramids expanding, human desires intensified.

‘And the stronger they grow, the more powerful demons becomes. If this continues, humanity will truly be doomed.’

Even Yahweh, who had reached the peak of the heart, would find the laws of the Sun an equivalent force.

‘How do I stop this?’

Shirone considered everything he could do and weighed the possibilities.

‘Fifty-fifty.’

Truthfully… he wasn’t confident.

‘I’ve never tried this before. No, I might even end up affecting the simultaneous events themselves.’

It was an incredibly dangerous and vast attempt, but humanity had no place left to retreat.

‘Just a little longer, Mother.’

Encouraging Ikael in his heart, Shirone accelerated away from the Sun at maximum speed.

Meanwhile, Amy and the phoenix had paused their battle due to the massive solar winds erupting from the Sun.

"You have incurred the wrath of God," the phoenix said.

"Give up, human. Changing the laws set by God is impossible. No matter what—"

The phoenix’s neck snapped back violently, thrown into space.

"Huh?"

The immense mass and powerful divine law were too distant to see with the eye.

Amy muttered.

"Shirone…?"

"This… this is impossible. How can a human alter such an enormous law…? This is impossible."

At this moment, what the source of the signal detected was not a quantum fluctuation but the unmistakable signal of photons.

Shirone’s eyes widened.

"Fuuuuu!"

The spiritual power he had regained by distancing himself from the Sun now concentrated between his clasped palms.

‘Materialization.’

He would block divine law with law itself.

Between his open hands, countless materials burst forth, rapidly assembling at a mind-boggling speed.

A mechanical structure, the size of an entire nation, continued to build endlessly.

"Urrrrgh! Urrrrrgh!"

As it connected at near-light speed, it began to wrap around the Sun’s equator like a massive ring.

The phoenix roared.

"You intend to enclose the entire Sun?! How dare a mere human!"

Of course, even with the intelligence of the fifth-dimensional archive, he couldn’t match the outer world's technology.

But—

"Uaaaaaaaah!"

If he could implement structures on a stellar scale, even existing technology could manipulate the laws.

"This is…"

Even Amy was at a loss for words as an unprecedented construction began.

"Mika."

■ Construction progress: 0.00002%.

Within the tesseract, the design blueprints detailed an ultra-massive artificial structure capable of fully controlling stellar energy.

‘Alright, let’s see who wins this in the end.’

It was called Shirone Sphere.
