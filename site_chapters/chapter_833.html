Chapter 833: Ivory Tower Grand Assembly (1)

400th floor of the Ivory Tower.

Even amidst the ongoing war with the demons, the natural scenery on the 400th floor was tranquil.

In the artificially created natural landscape, people planted seeds and prepared for a hopeful tomorrow.

'I have been defeated.'

In a red-roofed house on the outskirts of the village, Shirone was meditating.

'I lost.'

The plan to assassinate Habitz through had failed, and the world was heading towards catastrophe.

The death toll from the demons was already approaching one million, and Shirone felt responsible for it.

'Why did I lose?'

After much contemplation, he concluded that it was because he was weak.

The holy light of Yahweh enveloped his face, but it wasn't in a beautiful spherical form.

'I must endure.'

The spirit of philanthropy that loves all things equally.

'I must accept everything.'

He understood this intellectually, but suppressing his inner anger was beyond human capability.

When one's mind leans too far in one direction, the spirit twists, and one's level breaks.

'The heart must move.'

If he could become philanthropy itself beyond calculation, Shirone would finally achieve perfect freedom.

"Huh!"

It seemed impossible.

'I must forgive. I must embrace it.'

Like the tears flowing from Shirone's eyes, drops of Yahweh's light fell like burning embers.

"Exterminate humanity!"

By absorbing the army of hell, the Gustav Empire had become the world's strongest military power in name and reality.

King Smodo reorganized an army of one billion according to the characteristics of the demons.

Currently, under Balkan's plan, an army of fifty million was heading towards Kashan's capital.

"Isn't this too easy? It's boring."

It was as if an entire nation was moving, and no human army could stop them.

In a massive chariot pulled by Maranka monsters from the hidden world, Habitz and Gustav's four knights were gathered.

"Uorin."

There was no need for the emperor to be on the battlefield, but if the occupied country was Kashan, it was different.

"I'm looking forward to seeing their expressions when we capture the capital."

From an open carriage, Habitz looked at the army of hell filling the sky and earth.

It was a procession possible only because they were not human.

"Satan!"

A demon with wings on its red tail approached with a trident leading a flying unit in reverse flight.

It was a messenger who had learned human language.

"We have occupied Benaford, a commercial city in Kashan."

Benaford was a wealthy city located 40 kilometers from here.

Balkan gave orders on behalf of Habitz.

"We will stay there tonight. You continue to advance without rest."

As soon as the messenger received his orders and left, Balkan turned to Habitz and spoke.

"This means we've taken one-third of Kashan's territory. It's time to make a decision."

If they occupied half of Kashan's territory, it would open a path to invade the central continent.

Smodo spoke up.

"Jaive, Molton, Borna. These three countries border Kashan. If we break through here, we can take over not only the 21 kingdoms of the central continent but also the southern continent without any obstacles."

"I'm going to Uorin."

Balkan sighed at Habitz's disinterest in strategy, tactics, or even war itself.

"Then we'll split our forces. Ten million will attack Kashan from Benaford while the remaining forces move south."

Natasha looked puzzled.

"Forty million in the central continent?"

"There hasn't been any movement from the Holy War yet. They're probably planning their final stand at the central continent's border. Ten million is enough to take Kashan."

No one objected to Balkan's words, and that night Habitz's forces arrived in Benaford.

Once one of Kashan's three wealthiest cities, it had been utterly destroyed.

Habitz set up camp in a collapsed area and summoned the city's rulers.

Twenty high-ranking nobles, including the mayor, were dragged out bound with ropes.

Knowing Habitz's nature, their faces were pale with fear in their eyes.

'We're going to die anyway. That's not what's scary.'

They couldn't even imagine how this madman would torment them.

"I'll spare half of you."

At Habitz's indifferent words, everyone looked up in disbelief.

'Half? Half?'

One out of two people would die. But there was hope that they wouldn't be chosen.

"But I'll torture the other half."

Since it wasn't torture to extract information, the nobles' shoulders trembled with fear.

"Raise your hand if you want to live."

At first, no one raised their hand.

Shame at begging for their lives and buying time to analyze the situation held them back.

'We're going to die anyway. It's better to take a gamble.'

Three quick-thinking individuals raised their hands simultaneously while others turned their heads almost breaking their necks.

Their eyes were filled with confusion and anger mixed with vague hope that they might survive.

"Release them."

Upon Habitz's order, a soldier untied three prisoners and helped them stand.

"Leave. You are free."

The faces of the remaining 17 turned ashen.

"He said he would save half."

Now, only 7 could survive; seeing a glimmer of hope, they all raised their hands.

"Please save me! I have children!"

"I need to live! I would do anything for you, Lord Habitz!"

After listening to their pleas for a moment, Habitz handed a pair of dice to Zetaro.

Then he threw them to the man sitting farthest to the left.

"From now on, pair up and throw the dice. The person with the higher number is free."

"Uh..."

The nobleman gazed down at the dice that fell in front of him and then lifted his head with a vacant look.

"Roll them."

Startled by Habitz's curt command, he shakily picked up the dice and rolled them.

"Huuuugh!"

The two six-sided dice added up to 7, a 3 and a 4.

"Next. The person next to you."

The man kneeling next to him grabbed the dice without making eye contact.

The dice rolled out of his trembling hands, seeming to take an eternity to stop.

"Ah, ahhh..."

The dice showed a 6 and a 2, totaling 8. "Aaaahhh!"

The man with an 8 shouted in relief, while the man with a 7 went into a daze.

"This is absurd."

It was just a game of dice, and yet a mere difference of 1 decided their fates.

"Please, just one more chance!"

"Take him away. Torture him until he dies." The man couldn't accept his fate even as he was dragged away from the tent.

"Please! Just one more chance!" Balkan smirked bitterly.

'This is the cruel reality of the world. All you can do is roll the dice.'

Human free will was merely this trivial.

'But some humans escape the yoke of the law to enforce their will.'

Certainly not those present here.

"Next. Roll the dice."

The dice continued to be passed to the next person, and cries of joy and despair erupted in turn.

"One person is left, right?"

Since three had been allowed to leave, one person remained without a partner for the dice duel.

"Ah, please... mercy..."

Habitz stood up as the nobleman feared missing even a chance at half a chance.

"I'll play with you."

"Your Majesty, how can I possibly play against the Emperor..."

Habitz sat down in front of the noble, passing him the dice.

"What does that matter? I've always wanted to try this. Beat me, and you become the emperor. And I will..."

Looking around, Habitz then spoke to Zetaro.

"Right. Zetaro, kill me if he wins." Balkan interjected.

"Your Majesty, such a proposal is..."

Before he could finish, Zetaro burst into laughter.

"Hahaha! Alright! This will be an exciting game. I'll officiate then."

As Zetaro approached Habitz, sword raised as if to strike, the noble's eyes rolled back in fear.

'Madmen. They're not sane.'

"Roll it. This is a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity."

The tense atmosphere in the tent made it the most thrilling game.

"Please. Just once..."

The noble, his body shaking, squeezed his eyes shut and threw the two dice.

For a moment, the air froze, and the noble, eyes wide in disbelief, gasped.

"Ah! Ahhh!"

The dice showed 6 and 6, adding up to 12.

'It's a miracle! Thank God!'

While Habitz's officers looked on gravely, Zetaro burst out laughing.

"Hahaha! Now what will you do? Surely the emperor won’t go back on his word?"

Any other emperor might have struck down Zetaro, but Habitz merely smiled.

'Indeed, you’re the best.'

Most entertaining.

"Now it's my turn."

Despite the stakes, Habitz showed no hesitation as he threw his dice.

"Here goes."

All eyes followed the dice.

They showed 6 and 6, adding up to 12 again. The nobleman drooled in shock.

"Tie. One more time."

"Huuuugh."

Tears streamed down the face of the nobleman, who looked like a child in distress.

The crying didn’t stop.

"Ah, ahhhh."

As his nerves spasmed uncontrollably, the two dice slipped from his palm.

Again, they showed 6 and 6, making 12. "Ahhhh! Ahhhhh! Ahhhhh!" The nobleman, half-mad, continued to scream through his tears and snot.

Balkan’s gaze finally turned stern.

'There’s no tampering with the dice. But three consecutive 12s? That’s statistically improbable...'

Habitz snatched up the dice.

"Pretty good. Now, this time?" As he tossed the two dice into the air,

"Aaaahhhh!"

The soldiers, tense enough to choke, yelled as they stared at the dice.

"It's 12! Another 12!"

While not impossible, such odds are not commonly encountered.

"Please... save me."

The noble begged, but Habitz was resolute.

"Roll."

Physically exhausted but still hoping for a miracle, the noble threw the dice.

They showed 1 and 2, adding up to 3.

"Then, is it my turn?" As Habitz scooped up the dice, the noble shivered violently and opened his mouth wide.

"Krrraaaah!"

A bucketful of blood poured out as his eyes rolled back, and he collapsed forward.

Zetaro checked his condition.

"He’s dead."

"Pity. I was going to save him." Returning to his seat, Habitz grimaced and fidgeted with his legs.

"Boring. Isn't there anything fun?"

By the next morning, 20,000 citizens of Benaford had been horribly murdered.

'Forgive. Love.'

30 hours into his meditation, Shirone's eyes streamed with bloody tears.

He could no longer suppress his rage.

'No, I must not waver.' The battle between philanthropy and anger was a tightrope walk that could destroy the mind in an instant.

"Huuuuugh..."

Blood dripped from his eyes, nose, and lips, soaking his clothes.

'Endure. Even swallow the rage.' Drops of Yahweh's light began to fall like candle wax.
