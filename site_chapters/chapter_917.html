Chapter 917: A Cold War (1)

A photon signal not found in the law.

The archangels assessed the situation at Goffin's level, showcasing superluminal time perception.

Metiel frowned.

"Tch, a mere human."

If Goffin had deflected Uriel's destruction and dodged Raiel's light speed, it would be difficult to overpower him in combat.

‘It's not over yet.’

Sensing the immense energy stirring beyond Goffin, she raised her eyebrows.

‘We've only broken each concept.’

The world would only be complete when the concepts of the eight archangels were unified.

"Yaaaah!"

As Uriel charged, spinning the wheel of paradise, Goffin twisted his body and extended his hand.

‘It bounces.’

At the same time, Metiel activated Union.

‘Fusion!’

As explosive materials combined with the spinning wheel of paradise, it transformed into a powerful killing weapon.

Goffin smiled grimly.

‘Combining concepts...’

The moment the wheel of paradise struck Hexa, a massive explosion blew away the surrounding area.

"Over there."

The archangels' gazes moved simultaneously, and Goffin was seen crashing into the ground several kilometers away.

Boom!

Satiel, who had been watching from a distance, quickly flew over to check Goffin's condition.

‘Metiel's strong fusion.’

She knew best the extent of its destructive power, being the opposite concept.

‘Is he dead?’

As she thought this, the ground shook, and Goffin's upper body slowly rose.

"Ugh..."

Covered in dust, blood trickled from his mouth, but he didn't seem to have any fatal injuries.

"Raising the difficulty, huh?"

If they came out like this, the story would change.

"Hmm, a combination of eight concepts."

As Goffin, deep in thought, raised his head, Satiel hastily took a fighting stance.

"Come, if you're going to..."

Goffin extended his hand.

"Hey, pretty angel, can you help me up?" As Satiel stared in a daze, Goffin struggled to push himself up with his hand on the ground.

"My legs won't support me. It seems the shock is still lingering."

Then it's an opportunity.

"Why?"

But Satiel, instead of harboring hostility, asked.

"Why ask for help? We are your enemies, blocking the Gaia people's goal."

The strongest enemies, at that.

"Because I can't do it alone. But if everyone works together, is there anything impossible?"

"Why do you want to work together?"

Satiel shouted.

"You look down on us! You see us as mere tools to use this world, so why talk about going together?"

"Tools."

Goffin, shaking off the shock, stood up and brushed the dirt from his pants.

"Do you have any good memories? Like precious memories you want to keep forever."

Satiel raised an eyebrow and replied.

"Of course I do."

"Then nothing is insignificant."

Her holy body shook greatly.

"We were born. Therefore, we live. There may be no reason for our existence, but..."

Goffin smiled with his eyes.

"But it's real. Like me, like other Gaia people, you have lived too."

"Lived...?"

Goffin extended his hand again.

"Life is just one way of continuing. You are alive too. So come with us. Whatever the outer world is like, I will never let go."

Satiel's gaze turned to Goffin's hand.

‘God is indifferent.’

Because he must be perfect.

Thus, the world is cold, and the law is mechanical, but if God had a heart...

‘Nothing is insignificant.’

She thought he might say the same thing as Goffin.

"Yes."

Feeling the warm light in her holy body, Satiel clasped her hands and smiled brightly.

"You are right."

At that moment, the archangels flew in from the distant sky.

"There!"

As Kariel flew in the lead and expanded the judicial halo, the other archangels added their concepts.

‘Simulacrum!’

As the concepts of amplification, destruction, light, existence, union, and disintegration were integrated, a complex machine with intricate mechanisms was born.

‘A universal machine that cannot be avoided, cannot be destroyed, and will inevitably annihilate!’

The sphere, containing almost all the technology that makes up the universe, was fired at Goffin.

‘This is the end!’

As Kariel was convinced, Satiel took a step forward, her eyes shining as she spun the judicial halo.

‘Nostalgia!’

Physics collapse.

As 30,000 cracks appeared on the surface of the sphere, it disintegrated, and 2.4 billion nano mechanisms broke down.

"Ugh!"

As Satiel gritted her teeth, the mechanisms broke down into components, components into molecules, and molecules into atoms.

"What?"

As the machine disintegrated before his eyes, a cool breeze brushed Goffin's face.

"Satiel!"

Metiel shouted in anger.

"How dare you betray us! Can you still call yourself a servant of God while siding with humans?"

The only weakness of the universal machine was that while it couldn't be destroyed, it could be disassembled.

‘Damn it! If only Satiel's concept had been added!’

But instead, she was siding with Goffin, making it infuriating.

"It's not too late! Admit your mistake and help us fight, and we will forgive you."

Satiel smiled.

"Who forgives whom?"

Union and disintegration were twin concepts, but she had indeed been intimidated by the grand and magnificent Metiel.

"Nothing is insignificant."

It was only after hearing Goffin's words that she was finally convinced.

‘I am not wrong.’

She confidently extended her hand and declared.

"Satiel, the archangel of disintegration, has decided to consider the human's proposal based on her own judgment."

Metiel ground her teeth.

"That..."

"Stop."

Ikael, who had stopped the internal conflict, looked at the Gaia people filling the ground.

‘No gaps.’

To break the Ultima system, they had to subdue Goffin, but Satiel's cooperation was essential.

"The eight archangels hold equal status. We can't change Satiel's mind right now."

Metatron asked.

"Then what do you plan to do?"

"We will hold a meeting in the white hall. All angels under my command, return to Araboth immediately."

Ikael's voice spread over a wide area.

"Today is a truce. Goffin, you must also persuade the Gaia people to halt their advance."

Goffin spun Hexa on his fingertip and asked.

"What about the reset?"

Anke Ra would likely abandon the reset, but Ikael didn't want to say it out loud.

"It will be as God wills."

The seven archangels retreated towards Araboth, and Satiel flew up belatedly.

Goffin asked.

"Will you be okay? It looks like you'll get scolded a lot."

"Hehe, probably."

No regrets.

"See you next time, wonderful human."

As Satiel flew away in a beautiful arc, Goffin scratched his head and headed towards the Gaia people.

"Whew, we stopped it for now... but it looks like this will be a much longer war than expected."

Valkyrie Unit under the Holy War.

Following the orders of the Chief of Military, Lupist and Plu arrived at the locked iron gate within two hours and waited.

The Secretary-General of the Magic Association, Plu, checked the time.

"The handover process seems to be taking a while."

Just as he finished speaking, footsteps echoed from the deep tunnel outside, and Dante appeared.

Recognizing the faces of Lupist and Plu, he approached calmly and bowed.

"I apologize for the delay. I am Dante, Deputy Director of the National Intelligence Service."

Code Black.

In the Kingdom of Torumia, Dante was the only person besides the royal family who could access Black.

"Yeah, we know each other, right?"

They had met at a royal-hosted party, but this was their first actual conversation.

"Yes, I'm sorry I didn't greet you sooner. As you know, security is critical in my job."

Even a brief conversation with the head of the Magic Association would inevitably spawn countless rumors.

"No, before that," Lupist mentioned. There was a time when students from the top five prestigious schools gathered at the Magic Association for an examination hosted by Red Line.

"Ah, yes."

Dante responded, puzzled by the sudden reference to his school days.

"Alpheus Magic School especially stands out. Those students are now professionals, fighting hard."

'Was he always like this?'

Dante figured out the real intention behind the conversation.

'Iruki.'

While Shirome was the most powerful magician known to humanity, Iruki's reputation was the highest externally.

"With the head of the Magic Association and Code Black, I can guess why the Chief of Military has summoned us. It won’t be good news for the kingdom."

It was an unspoken pressure to reveal any information he knew beforehand.

Dante remained silent.

From experience, he knew there was nothing more perfect than silence in maintaining security.

As the iron door opened, a guide spoke.

"Please, come in."

During their walk to the underground bunker, Plu, with Lupist between them, extended a handshake.

"Pleasure to meet you. I'm Plu, Secretary-General of the Magic Association. You're friends with Shirome, right? We're quite close too."

"Yes, I've heard a lot about you."

"Ho ho, really? By the way, the intelligence service must be overwhelmed, right? I heard that the monks of Zion are in Torumia."

It was Code Black.

'A well-matched duo.'

Plu, who knew how to speak indirectly, was just the person the cold-natured Lupist needed.

Once again, Dante’s silence led the three without conversation to the end of the bunker.

"Enter." Iruki, covered with a blanket and sitting on a sofa, greeted them with a faint smile.

"I've been waiting for you."

Dante took in Iruki's face.

'He looks even more pale.'

Lupist bowed politely.

"I am Raphael Lupist, Chairman of the Thormia Magic Association. It's an honor to meet you."

After Plu and Dante also introduced themselves, Iruki gestured to Aromi.

"No need for tea."

It was a signal to not let anyone pour.

"We're short on time, let's proceed. This is a matter of great importance for humanity. We need the cooperation of the Kingdom of Torumia."

Iruki began to explain a strategy that was being secretly developed.

"Bashuka...?"

The plan was to obliterate it with an elemental bomb.

"How many people are currently in the capital?"

Plu, swallowing hard with a pale face, quickly gathered his thoughts.

"About 6.5 million. The population fluctuates due to the war, but that’s a close average."

"6.5 million."

That meant all those lives would be reduced to ashes.

"You must realize, this operation hinges on security. We'll lure the enemies to the capital and annihilate them all at once."

There was no objection to the strategy itself, but the idea of the kingdom's capital being obliterated was shocking.

"Those of you here can survive. It would be a serious loss of life to know yet die."

The three remained silent.

"So, I want to leave the decision to you. After calculations throughout the night, the maximum number of people we can exclude from this plan while maintaining security is..." Iruki held out both palms. "100 people."

100 people out of 6.5 million.

"Please identify 100 individuals from Bashuka who must survive. I won’t question your choices. Whether it’s family or someone vital to society. If you can list them now, I will handle it."

Lupist looked down at the blank paper Iruki offered.

'So that’s what it was.'

He realized why the royals hadn’t been called to a strategy meeting that planned to obliterate the capital.
