Chapter 1007: The Age of Struggle (3)

Kaiden's sword was vibrating at a terrifying speed right in front of Shirone's neck.

It felt like something was being endlessly compressed, but it never reached Shirone.

'Just a hair's breadth.'

The moment Kaiden's switch truly flipped, the law would break his will.

"Even with Hexa's ability, Agape, we can't completely cure the emotional disease. It might provide temporary relief, but if we don't solve the underlying problem, it will only lead to a more terrible disaster."

Relying on someone's ability alone won't allow us to fight against the demons and the heavenly army.

"Is that the reason? Is that all Maya means to you? If you had seen her with your own eyes..."

"My conclusion remains the same."

Amy swallowed hard at Shirone's cold response.

"Because she's a friend, you save her. But how do you set that standard? Someone who has spent a certain amount of time with me? Someone who has helped me? Or should I line them up in the order I like them?"

Kaiden understood Shirone's words, but his heart still resisted.

'Maya.'

He couldn't forget the terrified look on her face when she heard Shirone's name.

"So you can't help Maya?"

"My standard for the emotional disease is singular. I help only as part of a strategy to stabilize this world as quickly as possible."

"Maya is a strong person."

Kaiden genuinely believed that.

"Fighting isn't the only strategy. Her singing can move people. She has enough recognition. You can't say she's not part of your plan, can you?"

"Last night, Iruki almost died."

Amy looked at Shirone in shock.

"But thanks to that, we eliminated a powerful evil. It was an amazing achievement. Neid also fought risking his life. My friend Rian is wandering in hell, suffering from burning pain."

"...What are you trying to say? Do you think Maya can't do that?"

"No."

Shirone lowered Kaiden's sword.

"If I asked for help, Maya would do it no matter what. Is that really what you want?"

"When Iruki cast Overdrive, I knew it and didn't stop him. I even considered it in my strategy. That's exactly it. I need comrades who can face death with me, not people I have to take responsibility for every time there's danger. And for such comrades, suppressing the emotional disease is essential to achieve the highest level of performance."

"Maya is..."

Shirone interrupted.

"Kaiden, you don't have the right either. Do you even know what war is? Do you know what happens to women captured by demons? Maya is a precious friend. That's why I didn't send her to the battlefield."

Kaiden couldn't argue.

"Soon, a holy war will break out. Not only in Tormia, but the remaining kingdoms will also vie for supremacy. There are already rumors of countries allying with demons or the heavenly army. Listen carefully, Kaiden. The world is moving too fast, and it's spinning out of control."

Only Shirone could see the whole picture.

"I'm not forcing you. If you don't want to care, you can stay locked in your room. Fight the emotional disease and wait quietly until others achieve results."

"What should I do?"

The Shirone Kaiden knew wasn't one to nag without a solution.

"You said there was a reason you brought me. If Maya can be happy, I'll do anything."

"Protect Maya."

It was the one thing Kaiden couldn't do.

"By any means necessary, even if it means cutting her out of your heart."

"And after that?"

"I'll suppress Maya's emotional disease. She might be able to sing again. Soon, the holy war will become a battlefield that condenses all the world's affairs. Maya will be needed. But I can't protect her."

Kaiden had to protect her.

"So I have to give up on Maya. Truly, so I can never love her again."

"I don't think your feelings are trivial. In fact, they're really important. But the end of the era is coming. If you want to take responsibility for someone, you have to be ready to take responsibility for the world."

"Alright, I understand."

If Maya could sing again, what did his feelings matter?

"Bring Maya."

Kaiden's eyes were less lively as he sheathed his sword and descended into the basement.

Amy gave him a sympathetic look, but she wasn't much different.

Shirone had suppressed her emotional disease.

'Comrades.'

For the victory of humanity, both Amy and Shirone would willingly choose death.

'It's okay. We can still fight together. Shirone is by my side now.'

The words about taking responsibility for the world if you want to take responsibility for someone had that meaning.

Shirone said, "Kaiden will do well."

If his feelings for Maya weren't an obsession, love had the power to burn oneself.

"Let's wait for Maya."

Shirone's smile was sad, so Amy nodded more vigorously than usual.

Returning to the tavern, Kaiden was told by the owner that Maya had left work.

"If you're lying to me..."

"I swear I'm not! What would I gain from lying about this? Please believe me!"

The owner, whose collar Kaiden had grabbed, was squirming.

"Damn! I knew I was jinxed ever since that big shot Maya came to audition."

Despite knowing this day might come, he had hired her partly because of his personal feelings in addition to business strategy.

Because watching a world-famous singer fall from grace was an entertaining diversion.

Kaiden threw the owner to the ground and climbed onto the stage, then slipped out through the back door of the shop.

"She should be at home by now."

Having gotten the address, he planned to visit the inn where she was staying.

The owner spat as he got up.

"Damn, scum always shows up."

Although he had made a tidy profit exploiting Maya, it wasn't about the money anymore.

Like an addiction, the time he had controlled Maya as an employee nagged at his mind.

"I knew I should have interfered when I had the chance."

Crunch, crunch.

The sound of someone chewing hard peanuts came from a corner of the tavern.

"Who's there?"

The owner squinted and saw a man still sitting at a table.

"The shop's ruined. We're closed. Get out now."

"It’s not Yahweh."

"What?"

The owner, startled by the harsh, unhuman-like voice, scrunched up his face and approached.

"If you're drunk... Cough!" A fork thrown from the darkness stuck in the owner's mouth as his body was flung to the stage.

In the silence, the figure in the darkness spoke.

"This complicates my plans." The figure rose from the chair, hand in pocket, and walked towards the wall.

As he touched the wall, black smoke rose from his body and he passed through the material.

Wearing a deep hood, he blended into the crowd and headed for the city square.

The fountain was broken and muddy water flowed, around which dozens of people gathered.

"Repent, everyone."

In the center, a man with a short beard, dressed as a priest, preached.

"God loves you. The pain you’re experiencing is a trial sent by God. So..."

"Stop spouting nonsense!"

A shabbily dressed man holding a bottle shouted.

"How many gods are there? Do you know what happened to others who came here spouting that nonsense? They were all stoned and fled! A god? I'm dying in pain every day. What about God? I've lost my children, my wife, and my home, and I'm left all alone! What can a god possibly do for me now?"

The people lacked the energy or will to agree, but their eyes held a chill.

The priest, watching the man, smiled thinly and nodded.

"Anything. If you believe in God, anything is possible. A nice house, a more beautiful wife, even the pain will be removed."

At last, the crowd stirred.

"Really? This damned disease can be cured?"

Many religious figures had preached here, but none had been so blatantly doctrinal.

"Of course," the priest nodded happily.

A gaunt woman asked.

"Which god are you talking about? What god is it?"

"The name isn’t important. You might have heard many gods' names, but in truth, there is no name for God. All the gods you know of are actually the identity of the God I'm talking about."

It sounded ridiculous but plausible.

"Then prove it! If it’s a god, then I can regain everything I've lost!"

"Come to me."

The man, barely believing, moved forward, and the priest touched his shoulder.

"Are you ready to follow God’s doctrine?"

"Sure, if it cures my disease. Well, not just for that, but..."

"Ha ha, that’s fine. God is merciful. Naturally, God must grant what you desire. It’s a fake god that only brings pain and demands obedience."

"Then I believe. What should I do?"

As the man's tone shifted to respect, the priest met his gaze and said.

"Do what you want."

"What?"

"Listen to the voice of your heart. What do you desire? Do you want a house? To hold a beautiful woman, to possess countless treasures?"

The man, as if entranced, answered.

"Yes."

"Do it."

The priest gestured around.

"If there's a house you want, it's yours. If there's a woman you like, take her. If someone disrespects you, hit them. You don’t need to worry. Under the presence of God, you are perfectly safe."

"But still..."

It wasn’t entirely unpleasant to hear.

After losing everything to emotional disease, who else had offered him such comfort?

"Watch. This is the true God." The priest pulled out a pendant with an inverted cross and held it in front of him.

"Have you suffered in the pain given by false gods? That is the sin. The true God will not hurt you. Repent. Ask for forgiveness."

The man wept.

"I was foolish. O God, please hear my prayer."

The priest turned the man around.

"Now, take what belongs to the believers of the false god. Don’t worry. God has remembered your face; now you can enjoy everything. You have become a king."

"I, I am a king."

As the man moved, the frightened crowd began to back away slowly.

"Ah, ah..."

Seeing the terrified faces, a fierce impulse surged in his chest.

"It's all mine!"

Among the fleeing people, a woman was caught by the man.

"Give it here! This too, this too!"

He snatched a shabby cloak that kept her warm at dawn and a piece of leftover bread.

To those who had followed the action guidelines and given up much, these were their most precious possessions.

"No! I have nothing else to eat but this!"

"Shut up!"

The man raised his fist, and the woman shrieked, covering her face with both arms.

"Ha, ha."

Breathing heavily, the man finally realized what he had gained.

"It’s bread. Plus, the cloak, now it’s my cloak."

He couldn’t believe how easily he had acquired something.

"O God."

The man stuffed the bread in his mouth, drooling as he chewed.

"It tastes good, O God. It is delicious."

Realizing something, the man gasped and looked back at the priest in shock.

"It doesn’t hurt. The emotional disease..."

According to the action guidelines, food types should be segmented and given up one by one.

But for survival, the last piece of food must be kept.

For the woman, it was bread, and for the man, it was alcohol.

"I ate the bread and I'm not in pain. How is this possible! My disease is really cured!"

At that moment, the fleeing people turned around in surprise.
