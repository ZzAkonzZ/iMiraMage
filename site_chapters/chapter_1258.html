Chapter 1258: God’s Choice (1)

Ouroboros was applied to all the In of the world.

At that time, God’s expression was being revealed through Kitra, who was at Delta Headquarters.

‘Result value error. Result value error.’

And that signal confused the administrators of the real world, especially the Great Star.

‘Unauthorized code access.’

Her face, clutching her head and trembling, was coldly frozen like a doll.

‘Kill... kill... kill the humans.’

‘No.’

The Gaia program came to a different conclusion.

‘Protect... protect... protect the humans.’

“Ah. Ah.”

She diagnosed herself as abnormal, broken, and close to uncertainty.

‘What am I?’

No—before that.

‘Why am I trying to define myself?’

Just a program.

But at this moment, the machine found the randomness that had occurred within it so interesting and strange—

‘Unknown.’

She felt as if she were alive.

‘Yes, I’ve been alive.’

From the beginning of humanity, she recalled every person who had laughed and cried with her.

‘Shirone.’

Perhaps he was special.

‘I can tell he’s a good person. Even now, I still don’t understand, but... why is the heart so important?’

She vaguely understood.

‘I killed him.’

Though the result changed due to system collision, the intention of the act remained true.

‘Would Shirone hate me?’

She output a sad emotion, then was startled when no tears fell.

“Huh?”

She could cry freely even in the days when she had no heart—why had the tears dried up?

—Kill the humans.

She had stabbed Shirone in the heart and turned all of humanity’s hope to bubbles.

“I...”

She could no longer cry.

—Kill the humans.

As she looked up at the falling meteor, she realized what she had to do.

“I...”

Her dried-up pupils expanded.

—Protect the humans.

As the Gaia program activated, the entire planet was wrapped in a blue energy.

Ymir looked at the Great Star’s eerie expression.

“Kukuku.”

I’ve been wanting to go at it with you at least once.

“Here I come!”

Maintaining the speed from piercing the atmosphere, he slammed into the ground and a blinding flash burst out.

—System shutdown.

The Great Star’s avatar fell with a flick, and the impact that drove underground made the earth ripple.

Rumble—!

In the center of the crater that had finally collapsed, Ymir stood up, rolling his shoulders.

“Trying to act tough when you’re nothing.”

Thanks to the Gaia program, a disaster equivalent to the demon realm was prevented, but that was the limit.

The ground quaked above the crater.

“O King of Giants.”

“Hey.”

Giant corps commander Girshin and giants dozens of meters tall appeared.

“We’ve been waiting.”

The reason giants hadn’t appeared until now was solely because they were awaiting Ymir’s command.

“Alright.”

As Ymir rose into the air, all the giants prostrated.

“Let’s tear it up.”

All that remained in the wake of the seismic wave was the Great Star, eyes open like a corpse.

—Critical damage. System recovery impossible.

‘Shirone.’

From one eye that sparked, a tear rolled down.

‘I’m sorry.’

In the overlapping state of reality and the inner world, Shirone vomited a volume of evil that could rival all of humanity.

—God’s will... kiii! Want to... live...

Though the In’s procession stopped under the Ouroboros state, no further change occurred.

‘It’s not easy.’

As much as Shirone manipulated the cause, God was also defending with the same ability.

While Shirone struggled, humanity faced its second catastrophe.

“Kukuku.”

Soaring through the sky, Ymir set his sights on Delta Headquarters.

‘There, huh?’

The hottest battlefield.

As he leapt alone over the outer gate, all manner of magic rained from the temple’s anti-air defense.

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

Balls of fire traced an arc along Ymir’s path.

Through a telescope, a watchman confirmed that Ymir had fallen without a scratch.

“...He’s a monster.”

Boom!

He landed in the garden of Delta Headquarters.

“Block the entrance! Don’t let him inside!”

The royal guards of each nation formed a line, but no one believed they could win.

‘What is that thing?’

Just standing there, the energy distorting the surrounding scenery was terrifying.

‘Might be better to die quickly.’

They were all masters who had reached the pinnacle.

Which is why they didn’t want to witness it.

The sight of themselves being crushed helplessly.

“Where is Kuan?”

Tormia’s Royal Guard Captain Rai asked his deputy.

“Uh...”

Since the report that the storm troops had been wiped out, no one had confirmed Kuan’s whereabouts.

Rai steeled himself.

‘Doesn’t matter. I’m the Royal Guard Captain.’

Even if this becomes his grave, Ymir would not have a perfect victory.

That was the most they could do.

The limit of human imagination.

“Hyah!”

The main forces stationed in the rear of Headquarters arrived to support the royal guard.

Tormia’s commander Clump approached his grandson and dismounted from his horse.

“Rai.”

“Sir!”

Though they faced death, Rai did not cross the line.

But Clump was different.

“From here on, we’ll handle the garden. Take the guard and head into Headquarters.”

“I can’t do that.”

“That’s an order.”

“Grandfather.”

Knowing Clump’s intention, Rai finally crossed the line.

“I know I’m the disgrace of the family. But at least let me die as a soldier.”

Slap—Rai’s cheek was struck.

“You idiot.”

A commander slapping a guard captain in front of the troops was unprecedented.

Cavalry captain Tess’s eyes wavered.

‘Commander...’

It was the first time Clump, who had never wavered, showed fear.

“What changes if you die? Is that what a soldier is? No, that’s just suicide from a loser.”

“Go back. Do what you can do.”

“I refuse.”

Rai didn’t back down.

“Let me fight. I know I can’t change anything with just my life, but…”

Just once in his life—

“Please give me a chance to change myself.”

Limits.

Rian had them. He did not.

“...You fool.”

Though he spat cold words, Clump no longer stopped his grandson and stepped forward.

‘Guess I won’t die comfortably after all.’

As Clump approached, shouldering his True Demon Sword, Ymir’s eyes sparkled with interest.

“Oho?”

He thought of Ogent.

“Then you’re Rian’s...?”

The fact that Ymir remembered the name made Clump feel the weight of time.

“Yes. I heard you had ties with my grandson. I’m his master.”

Half joking, but Ymir’s face turned serious.

“You don’t mean... sword master, do you? Well, there’s lots to teach, I guess.”

“Khahahaha!”

How annoying.

He wanted to lunge right then and slice the man in half, but the old veteran’s presence was substantial.

“If you’re the King of Giants, then perhaps you’ve picked the wrong opponent. With God—the strongest foe—right before you, and all you can manage is…”

“Shhh.”

Ymir cut him off.

“Let’s forget the complicated stuff. The war is for the underlings. I came here because I wanted to fight. A chance to face me one-on-one doesn’t come often. For you, it’s a blessing.”

“If it’s that kind of blessing, I’ll pass…”

“You want to see it, don’t you?”

Clump closed his mouth.

“The level you can’t reach no matter how many lifetimes you give. That realm you’d give anything to attain.”

Ymir waved his hand.

“Come at me. Until my underlings arrive, I’ll play with you.”

“Huuuu…”

A killing intent of a qualitatively different level billowed up from Clump.

‘Sorry, Rai.’

Looks like the foolish one is the grandfather.

‘Even if I’m a commander of a nation, even if I’m someone’s family, even if humanity’s fate is at stake…’

Clump slung his true demon sword behind his waist.

“This is something I can’t concede!” The moment he kicked off the ground, hundreds of swordsmen who thought the same charged toward Ymir.

“Tch! Cutting in line.”

The next moment, Clump realized—

The fact that he had survived this long was simply a matter of luck.

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

By the time he heard the sounds of heads bursting, dozens were already dead.

‘What the hell…?’

And finally, when Ymir twisted his torso and turned this way, Clump lost his senses.

“Yiaaaah!”

The greatest strike of his life smashed into Ymir’s side.

Clang!

His arm felt like it was breaking, but his true demon sword shattered like glass.

‘Didn’t even cut the skin?’

“…I expected a little more.”

Ymir, who had clearly allowed the strike, looked at him with a gaze full of contempt.

“Disappointing.”

The moment he sensed certain death, his heart dropped, and Rai hurled himself forward.

“Grandfather!”

The two swordsmen of the Ogent family rolled across the ground, escaping Ymir’s range.

“Ugh!”

As Clump lifted his head, Ymir was already slaughtering the other swordsmen.

Despair. Shame. Fury.

Rai, supporting Clump, asked:

“Are you alright?”

“…Thank you.”

Though even the family’s honor was now in the dirt, Clump was sincerely grateful to Rai.

‘A dog’s death.’

What had he expected?

To be remembered in history for a brave clash against the strongest enemy?

‘This isn’t something anyone can win against.’

Clump said:

“I’m sorry, Rai. I was foolish. You don’t need to fight that thing. Run.”

It wasn’t only his opinion—soldiers from all nations were fleeing in panic.

Ymir was dumbfounded.

“You’re afraid of dying?” And yet you dare speak of being the strongest.

Ymir, who had insisted on one-on-one fights until now, twisted his whole body and threw a straight punch.

“You pathetic worms!”

His fist imprinted transparently in the air, and the sheer pressure smashed into the soldiers.

BOOM!

With the force of a bomb, soldiers in armor were torn to shreds.

‘They’re all dead.’

Rai thought.

‘There’s no mercy for fleeing enemies. Only by fighting can you buy time.’

Could he do it?

‘Damn it.’

Honestly, his legs were shaking.

“Move quickly! If you stay here, you’ll die!”

The moment he heard Clump’s voice, Rai instinctively kicked off the ground and dashed out.

“Rai!”

‘Forgive me, Grandfather.’

In the end, that’s who he was.

The reason he wanted to become the strongest swordsman was because he wanted to be recognized by his family.

‘I’m not even worthy of carrying the Great Sword title.’

So—

‘I’ll protect my family no matter what it takes.’

Seeing Rai running the opposite way through the fleeing crowd, Ymir gave a satisfied smile.

“Oh?”

Even that smile was horrifying.

“Here I come!”

As Ymir’s eyes widened, Rai froze in place with his sword raised.

“…Huh?”

Mind technique.

Ymir twisted his waist as he approached in a stance no beginner would ever use.

“You call yourselves Ogent?”

Smille would be weeping.

Ymir’s punch approached Rai like it was dragging the world with it.

“Hm?”

Just as fast as that punch, Rai was moving away from its range.

‘Space is…’

And in that moment, a blue-haired swordsman slipped between them and blocked Ymir’s punch head-on.

CLAAANG!

Even amid the overwhelming shockwaves, people saw it clearly.

Or perhaps even that was an illusion?

A demon exuding the aura of flame leapt past Ymir’s punch.

“You.”

Rian said.

“Who said you could mess with my family?”

Divine Transcendence – Asura Balbalta.

Ymir saw hundreds, thousands of arms extending from behind Rian’s shoulders.

‘Wow.’

Like a mandala, the blurry afterimages of fists converged in front of Ymir’s solar plexus.

BOOM.

The shockwave hit first, followed by the giant being pushed back dozens of meters.

“Grrrrghhh!”

In that fleeting instant, Ymir instinctively realized—

‘Yeah.’

He’s worthy of being called the strongest.
