Chapter 1006: The Age of Struggle (2)

Seina felt her face flush.

The Paladin of the Oracle Management Department holds the most noble mission in the Lami Church.

Such a person succumbed to evil, swayed by mere human emotions, and could not bear to face Shirone.

'I have no right to blame.'

If it weren't for Shirone's baptism, she would have also ended her life by stabbing herself.

"Prepare to leave," Seina ordered the paladin, turning to look at Shirone with a resentful expression.

"I owe you. But this is not all of the Lami Church. The highest-ranking Paladin of the Papal Office is..."

"That doesn't matter," Shirone said.

"Shiyok is deceiving people. It's a problem that even the Lami Church cannot overlook."

It was the most human evil.

"We will go to the Papal Office. We need to report the full story of the incident and receive a new oracle."

Seina examined the twisted corpse of Ravika.

'It's a pity. If she were alive, she could have testified. We need to take another survivor.'

With that thought, she looked around, but no one approached the paladin.

'Foolish ones.'

She was angry at those who judged only by the immediate outcome, but today was her defeat.

"We will leave the villagers behind," Seina said, mounting her horse.

"That doesn't mean I acknowledge you. Soon, an inquisitor from the Heresy Management Department will come for you. Be cautious."

The most secretive department within the Lami Church.

'And the most dangerous place as well.'

If the Oracle Management Department moves with faith, the Heresy Management Department moves with hatred.

In a way, just mentioning the existence of such a department was an act of goodwill.

"Let's go."

Shirone did not avert his gaze until Seina and the paladins left the village.

'We fight evil, but our beliefs differ.' As Shirone's philanthropy spread to the world, the number of conflicts would increase.

Nade asked, "Shirone, what about the survivors? We can't take them all on a journey."

Shirone checked Iruki's condition.

Although he was no longer bleeding, the aftermath of using Overdrive had not yet subsided.

"Let's camp at the foot of the mountain today. I've told Mr. Lampa to find a suitable city for them."

Wiping the blood from his face, Iruki thought.

'Arnold Lampa. If he's the best information mage in the Ivory Tower, he should be able to find it well. More importantly...'

It was crucial that the survivors who experienced Shirone's miracle spread the word in the city.

'If we plant seeds like this, they will eventually bear fruit. Maybe...'

He thought that the point of direct confrontation with the Lami Church might come much sooner than expected.

In the southern city of Tormia, Gaspa, from the capital Baschka, the demons spread out radially and continued to move south.

The kingdom's swift response prevented annihilation, but the aftermath of emotional disease was more terrifying.

The streets overflowed with vagrants who had abandoned their homes, and with law enforcement failing, crimes were incessant.

"It's serious."

In the eyes of those who had destroyed something precious, one could see not just malice but even the intent to kill.

Shirone said, "The World Health Organization has established action guidelines based on the cases collected for dealing with emotional disease. They are divided into 17 categories, with life-critical items placed later to control the threshold of emotional disease."

"The Cross family received it too. I haven’t seen it myself. How long can one last if they follow the guidelines exactly?"

"A maximum of two months. The goal is to find a cure within that time, but so far, there are no results."

Shirone's gaze turned towards the vagrants.

"The item about abandoning residences corresponds to category 8. This is a guideline for at least four weeks later, but already there are many who have abandoned their homes."

"Well, there is no rule that satisfies everyone."

Cayden, who hadn't left his house for a long time, felt a fresh sentiment upon seeing the real world.

"Everyone is bearing their own pain. Maybe my worries are trivial compared to theirs..."

Shirone asked, "Cayden, how did you manage to resolve your emotional disease?"

"No different from others. My father endured by giving up one precious thing after another. Heirlooms, property, distant relatives... The Cross family has declined to the point of no return."

Cayden turned his head.

"But I guess I'm a bit different. The destiny of the Red Cross extracts 100% of all talents except for what I truly desire. As long as I don't give up on Maya, emotional disease won't be a problem."

Amy asked incredulously, "You don't get more sensitive?"

"That's right. I don't know the exact reason. After all, if emotional disease is a kind of illness, then I've hardly ever been sick."

Shirone asked, "Hardly ever means... you have been sick?"

"When I was very young. I was scared after hearing about exhaustion disease from my grandmother. I prayed not to get sick, but the next day I caught a cold."

What you truly want does not come true.

"I was sick for about a year after that. I wanted to get better. My grandmother apologized with tears, but it was in vain."

Cayden shrugged.

"Of course, I grew up hearing about the destiny of the Red Cross. If I thought it was okay to be sick, maybe I would have gotten better."

Shirone understood.

"The mind is not something you can change easily."

"Right. Especially when you're young. Anyway, it wasn’t serious. I practiced swordsmanship and got healthier. And gradually, my mind also started to strengthen."

Shirone realized just how cruel the law of the Red Cross destiny was.

"If one has experienced such bizarre events all their life, the fear of deeply desiring something is inevitable."

Amy asked, "How about now? Because you love Maya, you can overcome emotional disease, right? Isn't that fortunate?"

"Let me put it this way."

Determination filled Cayden's eyes.

"If I could escape this cursed fate, I wouldn't care about emotional disease or anything. If I could achieve what I want even for a moment, I'd be fine with dying."

No one could define Cayden’s life.

"If you desperately want to sleep, you can't fall asleep. If you're desperately hungry, you're not hungry. I had to be trained not to get obsessed. Do you know what that means? It means I've never once done what I wanted since I was born."

Shirone's eyes narrowed.

"I see. Emotion Scale."

Cayden's emotional scale was twisted beyond any clear priorities.

"It might not just be a problem with his immune system."

Cayden asked, "But why is Maya here? She’s a major singer, right? I heard she signed with Valkyrie."

"Emotional disease."

Shirone's face darkened.

"Everyone ends up giving up many things they cherish. But truly precious things are not easily forsaken."

Amy asked, "For Maya, that's singing, right?"

"Yes. I heard she terminated her contract with the event organizers. The president wanted to keep her, but that also would have become an emotional scale. It was an unavoidable choice."

"But still..."

As they left the main road and entered a gloomy alley, Cayden’s brows furrowed.

"Why such a place?"

"I’ve only heard the information; I’ve never met Maya personally. Perhaps she needs to be this emotionally fragmented to be unable to sing otherwise."

They finally arrived at a bar marked with a red sign depicting women's underwear.

As they descended the narrow staircase to the basement, cheap instrumental music played along with a singing voice.

"Maya?"

Cayden recognized her the moment he heard her.

"Guests?"

A man squatting by the entrance smoking a cigarette looked at them.

Shirone remained silent, and the man threw his cigarette away and stood up.

"Table fee is 5 gold. Drinks are charged separately. You can’t go on stage or throw things."

The fact that he specifically mentioned such obvious rules irritated Amy.

"So, other things are allowed? And 5 gold, do you know how much that is?"

The man rubbed his ears with an annoyed expression.

"You knew what you were getting into, right? Maya's here. The top singer in Tormia."

Shirone handed over a gold coin.

"Here you go."

After checking the amount, the man squatted back down by the entrance and lit a new cigarette.

"Go inside. Just a heads-up, the women there aren't much fun."

Taking the money and giving a warning in advance.

"Cayden, go ahead."

Shirone intended to stay outside.

"By myself?"

"Yes. It might hurt Maya's feelings more if I go in."

Cayden took a deep breath and nodded as he looked up at the sign.

"Alright."

With each step down the stairs, the drum sound grew louder and his heartbeat quickened.

As he turned the corner at the basement entrance, the smell of stale tobacco hit him, and aside from the stage, it was completely dark.

Maya was humming to a blues performance.

Despite the cheap lighting and her movements, Maya's appearance was still beautiful.

'Why, why?' Her attire felt too cold even amidst the heat from the men's enthusiasm.

Cayden surveyed the patrons.

Men with dead eyes spouting vulgarities and engaging in perverted acts.

"Maya."

The singing continued.

Maya's face, staring into the void above the table, showed no emotion.

'Enough...'

Clutching his fist and grinding his teeth, Cayden couldn't hold back anymore.

"Stop it!" The musicians stopped playing, and finally, some emotion seemed to stir in Maya's eyes.

'Who is this? Too drunk?' It was too dark to see his face. Meanwhile, the surrounding drunkards stood up.

"What's wrong with you? Just when things were heating up..."

Cayden pushed a drunkard's chest and quickly walked toward the stage.

"Uh..."

Maya's eyes widened.

"Cayden?"

She hastily put on a coat she had thrown on the floor and averted her gaze.

"You, how did you know to come here?"

"Maya."

Words Cayden wanted to tell her flashed through his mind.

'What should I say?'

His sincere feelings for her would inevitably return as tragedy before the fate of the Red Cross.

"Outside..."

Cayden choked up.

"Shirone is outside. So let's go together. We'll protect you."

"Shirone?"

Her face, which had momentarily regained composure, suddenly turned pale.

"Shirone."

Her gaze wavered uneasily, then she hurriedly turned and ran backstage.

"Maya! Wait, Maya!"

As Cayden was about to follow, a drunkard grabbed his shoulder.

"You jerk! Making a scene and now running off? Cough!"

With incredible speed, Cayden spun around and swung his fist, sending the drunkard spinning through the air.

The drunkard fell backwards, and the sobered patrons hesitantly stepped back.

"This..."

Being able to hit the drunkard was because there was someone else he desperately wanted to hit even more.

"Shirone!"

Cayden sprinted up the stairs, extending his sword toward Shirone.

His thrust was as forceful as a greatsword's, but it still couldn't pierce Shirone.

"Ugh!"

His strength drained, he felt as if he had forgotten how to hold a sword.

"Don't get worked up."

Struggling, Cayden twisted his neck and glared at Shirone diagonally.

"Answer my question. You said... you have the ability to cure emotional disease?"

"Only temporarily."

"Answer me!"

"Yes."

"Then why didn't you cure Maya? Why did you let her get to this state?"

"That's why I brought you."

As Cayden failed to understand, Shirone's gaze turned icy.
