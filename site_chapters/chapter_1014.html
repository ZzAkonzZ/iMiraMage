Chapter 1014: Evil Strikes Back (2)

Aganos of the Imperial City.

Emperor Gando of Kashan never failed to pay his morning respects to Wuorin.

"Mother, I'm entering."

As he opened the door, Wuorin was lying on a bed large enough to roll over ten times.

She was not asleep.

'Still at it?' It seemed she had spent the night in "Under Coder" again.

Even though she had been absorbed in virtual reality before, it had become more severe since she stepped down from the throne.

"Mother."

"O" ......hmm.

Slowly opening her eyelids, she vaguely stared at the ceiling's design before sitting up.

"Ah, my head. What time is it? Could you ask Kido to bring some honey water?"

"It's morning. Are you really alright? Where do you connect every dawn?"

It shouldn't cause a headache just from experiencing Under Coder.

"Yeah, it's fun. Just a bit... 'deep.' Call Kido, will you? My head is spinning."

Gando could no longer endure.

"You need to take care of yourself. The Holy War is going to be reorganized soon. Kashan isn't as strong as it used to be."

The demonic tree Argantis was lethal.

"After all, other countries are in a similar situation. The kingdom I established isn't so vulnerable."

"I'm not doubting your abilities, Mother. The problem is that the heavenly army is quiet. There are rumors that some states have allied with them. If we keep this up..."

"Ah, noisy. My head hurts and you babble on. Becoming an emperor has made you insolent."

Gando was furious.

"The emperor is you, Mother! Terraje is Kashan! There's only so much I can do."

'At least he understands his place.' That's why Gando's head was still attached.

"Alright. I'll look for a solution. Go do your work. But don't you meet any women?"

"What about the Holy War?"

"Ah, come on!"

Wuorin, shouting abruptly, sighed in front of Gando's stern gaze.

"I'm not just playing around. I go into Under Coder every day to gather information."

Gando snorted in disbelief.

"That place is swarming with social misfits. What kind of information are you gathering there?"

Wuorin chuckled.

"You wouldn't understand, being as dull as you are. But Gando, the world you know isn't everything."

"That may be, but can you please solve the real problems first? Yahweh has appeared all over the world, performing miracles. Most countries have benefited, but Kashan is an exception."

"Shirone..."

Wuorin's gaze grew somber.

"Excluding Tormia, Kashan Empire has been the biggest supporter of Shirone. Yet, it's being completely ignored. We need to respond somehow."

"Shirone won't come."

"Why not?"

"He wouldn't want to meet me."

If Shirone had grasped the incident with Adam and Eve, it was natural for him to keep his distance from Wuorin.

"It's an old story. But we'll meet eventually. Right now, there's a more pressing issue."

She said, brushing her white hair at the vanity.

"Open the inner gates. Tell the guards to limit their patrols. Preserving our strength is the priority."

Gando frowned.

"I don't understand your orders. Why open the inner gates if we're preserving our strength?"

"Frustrating."

Wuorin, brushing her hair, looked at Gando in the mirror.

"That Yahweh does not exist in Kashan means..."

Through a chain reaction of laws.

"Satan is coming."

The teacher, hearing Wizard's words, checked on Shirone's condition and realized belatedly.

‘Indeed... it feels different.’

Blades, fire, beasts, lakes covered in thin ice—things that have threatened human life since ancient times.

Though the exact reason was unknown, his heart raced as if faced with such things.

“Alright.”

As Wizard walked to the training ground, the uneven ground instantly became flat.

“Hmm.”

Rampa let out a low sound.

‘Grown again. In just three minutes of battle, making leaps that encompass a lifetime of an ordinary person.’

Walking along the path Wizard had cleared, Shirone felt relieved.

‘Thank goodness.’

Because she was a strong child.

The enemy Wizard had to face next was Havitz, an evil so extreme that no one in the world could touch him.

‘Her skills are more than sufficient. But if she can't control his mind, she'll be devoured by Satan.’

It was a time for firm and overwhelming education.

“It begins.”

The golden haze surrounding Shirone's body ascended to a height of 20 meters.

“Wow.”

Unlike everyone who was amazed, Wizard flinched and stepped back.

“What? Wizard...”

In this unprecedented situation, the students and teachers who knew her had blank expressions.

“She hates losing more than dying...”

Only Amy and Lampa knew that Wizard's judgment was excellent.

Bartok asked.

“The technique of the Five Stars he just displayed, what ability is it?”

Wizard, who had subdued him leisurely, was now on guard, so it was natural to be curious.

“Quantum superposition.”

Lampa said.

“If the mind changes the law, the current mind of Yahweh exists only as a probability. It seems like it's just standing there, but all possible actions are superimposed.”

“...Is that possible for a human?”

“Isn't it right in front of you? Anyway, the Five Stars is serious. They intend to crush his spirit from the start.”

Bartok turned his gaze to Wizard.

“Does she know what that ability is?”

“She knows. Even if she can't understand it, she'll feel it intuitively. That's what super logic is.”

“She isn't moving. Does that mean she hasn't found the answer?”

“Such an interpretation is the limit of common sense. Wizard's creativity transcends the logic of reality. If her thoughts were to take shape, wouldn't it be similar to Drimo?”

Bartok swallowed hard.

“That's why I'm really curious. What is emerging in the mind of that genius who has encountered Yahweh's ultimate?”

Lampa's eyes narrowed.

“What on earth is coming to mind?”

All the mages who heard the explanation felt a creepy chill and focused on Wizard.

At that moment, Wizard opened her mouth.

“Wow.”

An image that she had never thought of in her seven years of life filled her mind.

“What is that, brother?”

It was also unusual for Wizard to ask a question before understanding it.

“Hexa.”

When Shirone extended his hand, a Hand of God the size of an adult emerged and aimed at her.

“It’s a technique that deals with the mind. It’s kind to good people but punishes bad people. So maybe... it might hurt you.”

“Why should I be punished?”

“You know why.”

The life disappeared from Wizard's eyes again.

“It doesn't matter. I'll win.”

As soon as she finished speaking, Wizard stepped back a great distance and cast a spell.

‘Excellent judgment. But... even an extraordinary idea can't destroy all of Shirone's potential.’

“Aha! Now I understand!” Despite that, had she realized something? Wizard's magic became even more bizarre.

Shirone's smile trembled.

“It's dangerous.”

As the Hand of God rushed at tremendous speed, Wizard's eyes sparkled.

“Huh? It's nothing special?”

Seeing her dodge and counterattack simultaneously, Shirone grasped the core of the thought.

‘So that's it.’

If human logic is a complex maze, Wizard's logic is an infinite open space without walls.

“La la la. La la la.” In that space of thought, the seven-year-old wizard wandered around freely, humming.

‘Block from infinity.’

When the Hand of God was dispatched, a huge wall fell into Wizard's open space.

“Surprised!”

She stared at the wall for a moment.

Even the pinnacle of savants, Iruki, could only have a maze-like form of thought...

“It's okay. I can go back.”

She could wander in all directions.

“I should plant it here.”

Stopping where she felt like it, she planted a seed in the ground, and it sprouted instantly.

“Grow. Grow.”

The specific form of the sprout suggested new possibilities and grew into a giant world tree.

Rumble!

“Yay! I destroyed it.”

At the moment the world tree destroyed the wall, Shirone faced a crisis for the first time in reality.

‘What an incredible talent.’

Even while avoiding the pursuit of the Hand of God, her magic was evolving in real-time.

“Shirone, isn't the Five Stars being pushed back?”

At Bartok's words, not only the teachers but also the members of the Ten Wizards murmured.

In an actual confrontation, there are no walls of thought. But the momentum alone could feel the advantage or disadvantage.

“The Five Stars is a defensive magicians.”

Lampa said.

“It's not that one is more advantageous than the other, but the nature of the battle is different. Attackers focus on the target, but defenders have to read the entire situation.”

Amy raised the corners of her mouth slightly.

“It's too big, that's the problem.”

Wizard, spreading her arms towards the endlessly growing world tree, jumped up and down.

“I destroyed them all!”

The trees she planted destroyed all the walls Shirone had built.

“I won! Stupid brother!”

Thud! Thud! Thud! Thud!

With attacks so simple they were almost clumsy, Wizard kept retreating endlessly.

“No matter how many walls you build, here is... huh?”

How far had she moved from where she started? Something touched her back. When she turned around, a black wall stood there.

“Why is this here? It's not even the place for the confrontation...”

Casually rummaging through the seeds, she suddenly realized and turned her head.

The black walls Shirone had built stretched endlessly beyond the horizon.

For the first time, Wizard's expression hardened.

“Why here...”

She had enclosed the vast space within walls that her sight could not reach.

It was still a vast space.

“It's not infinite.”

Lampa couldn't hold back his bursting laughter.

“While Wizard was focusing on the target, the Five Stars was looking at the place her thoughts couldn't reach. And from there, they laid the groundwork, gradually narrowing the radius.”

Bartok muttered.

“If the creativity that could unfold endlessly is trapped within walls, from now on, it's the realm of logic.”

“Yes, that's it.”

Thud! Thud! Thud! Thud!

Wizard's face turned pale as she watched the walls closing in from all sides.

“Hmph! Ah, there are still many places to escape!”

But Shirone remained cold and only drove her into a perfect situation.

Thud! Thud! Thud! Thud!

“Why? Why?”

As paths formed and she had to change direction inevitably, anxiety overwhelmed her.

“I don't know where this is!”

No matter how many crossroads she wandered, she only saw black walls.

At the end of a deep passage, Wizard instinctively turned right.

“...It's blocked.”

Touching the wall with both hands and looking up, she ran in the opposite direction again.

How naive was her heart to think the remaining path would be a way out.

“Ugh...”

But what awaited her was a cold wall emitting a chilling aura.

Rumble!

The ground shook, and when she looked back at the narrow path, the Hand of God was approaching with tremendous force.

Lampa muttered coldly.

“Checkmate.”

As Shirone's eyes gleamed with murderous intent, the Hand of God expanded to a huge size in the eyes of Wizard, who had zero movement.

“I hate it. I hate losing.”

In the eyes of Wizard, who was about to cry, the Hand of God expanded greatly.

“I'm...”

She closed her eyes tightly.

“I was wrong.”

Boom!

The walls of thought collapsed, and the people watching the confrontation gaped in astonishment.

“Yes.”

A gentle voice.

When Wizard, feeling a hand patting her head, opened her eyes, she saw Shirone's face.

“You're a good child.”

Wena Wizard, age 7.

For the first time in her life, she met an adult.
