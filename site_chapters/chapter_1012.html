Chapter 1012: Shadow of God (4)

The Kingdom of Stang.

Located at the southernmost part of the central continent, it was relatively less affected by the demons.

"Due to the Elemental Bomb Project, most of the army of hell entered the flower fields of Vashka."

Amy responded to Lampa's words.

"Then they were all scattered to the south after being baptized by Shirone's light."

"Yes. They struck the southern continent like shrapnel. However, the southern continent, unlike the central continent, is not densely populated with nations, and the number of demons had already decreased, so catastrophic damage was avoided."

Shirone spoke.

"And the southern continent is... the only region where the legion commander's demon realm hasn't opened."

It was also the place ruled by Fermi.

"Whether it's luck or not. But the Kingdom of Stang in the central continent is truly fortunate. Thanks to the demons spreading from Tormia, the Kingdom of Stang in the west suffered almost no damage. Due to these various factors, it is considered one of the candidates to seize the hegemony of the next world."

In other words, it had the potential to stand out in the upcoming holy war.

"From here on, it's the Kingdom of Stang."

As Shirone observed, the wall built along the border stretched to the horizon.

'The fortunate one of the battlefield.'

Having an intact wall alone showed how blessed they were.

They had departed before dawn, and when they crossed the border, the morning sun was shining down.

Upon arriving at the border guard, the highest minister of the Kingdom of Stang was already waiting.

"Star of the Ivory Tower, it is an honor to have you visit our Kingdom of Stang."

The official, bowing deeply, handed over the king's letter with both hands.

As Shirone skimmed the letter, it contained an apology for not being able to come out to greet them.

'Indeed... not an ordinary person.'

No matter how it was dressed up, the fact remained that a mere king had ignored the Five Stars.

"His Majesty wished to come out personally. However, due to the influx of immigrants from other countries..."

"It's fine."

The words of a politician held no value.

The only reason they came here was for Wizard, and it was better to handle that quickly.

"I'd like to start with a tour."

The official, already informed by Lampa, bowed as if he had been waiting.

"Yes. A carriage has been prepared."

The reason they didn't use magic to travel was because it was a 'tour.'

The destination was the newly established 'Stang National Magic School' in the Kingdom of Stang.

While the official handled the procedures, Shirone was able to hear more details from Lampa.

"The Kingdom of Stang, which suffered minimal damage from the war, accepted immigrants and aggressively incorporated talented individuals from around the world. Wena Wizard is one of them."

"The fact that it was discovered this late means there was a scheme by the Kingdom of Stang."

"Yes. It was kept strictly confidential, and it seems they scouted with a large sum of money. According to the investigation, the position of the head of the Magic Association of the Kingdom of Stang was also conditionally guaranteed."

"The head of the Magic Association...

It wasn't an offer that could be made to a mere seven-year-old wizard.

'What kind of child could it be?'

Half curious, half expectant, they entered the school where many students were engrossed in training.

As expected of carefully selected talents, the intensity of the training was considerable, and the ages varied.

While looking for the youngest child, the official, who had finished the tour procedures, came with documents.

"As requested by the Five Stars, their identity has been kept confidential. The students and teachers believe a royal inspector is visiting."

"Yes, thank you."

The comprehensive analysis would have already been completed by the Ten Mages of the Ivory Tower.

'Let's observe from a distance.'

If it was a seven-year-old child responsible for dealing with the extreme Habitz, it was better to approach cautiously.

Lampa pointed to the 2000th training ground.

"That child is Wizard."

Where Shirone and Amy's gaze turned, a girl was fighting adults.

With a bright face and blonde hair braided into two pigtails, her body was smaller than expected.

'She looks like just an ordinary child.'

But it was clear from her movements that she was beyond the ordinary.

"Fun! Fun!"

Among the young adults who were deploying magic with all their might, she was the only one having fun.

The most bizarre thing was that all 14 students were targeting Wizard.

Amy couldn't say anything. If it was just about dodging attacks well, she could be called an excellent wizard, but...

"What is that...?"

She was using the concept of 'movement,' which was so familiar to humans, in a form never seen before.

"Super logic."

Shirone spoke.

"It completely deviates from the causality we know. It's not something that can be obtained through training. It's not even talent. Just..."

To define it accurately.

"She was born that way."

A presence was felt behind Shirone's party.

"Our analysis is the same."

The Ten Mages of the Ivory Tower, five 1-star residents, and their satellites stood in two rows.

At their sudden appearance, the official flinched and instinctively bowed.

'Thirteen stars gathered in one place? This is unprecedented in our kingdom.'

It was likely due to the value of Wizard.

The Ten Mages of the Ivory Tower, who didn't even glance at the official, approached Shirone and greeted him.

"Greetings to the Five Stars."

After observing Wizard for a long time, Shirone finally turned around.

"Yes, nice to meet you. We saw each other during the Ivory Tower's grand assembly. I'm sorry about Mr. Freed."

Leader Bartok shook his head.

"The master enjoyed fighting strong opponents. Since the opponent was Ymir, he wouldn't have had any regrets."

Thinking the introduction was sufficient, he explained.

"As evaluated by the Five Stars, Wizard is a super logic-based magician. It seems that synesthesia obtained at the fetal stage had a significant impact."

Lampa continued.

"She sees colors in sounds and hears sounds in shapes. Of course, other magicians can do this too. But the unique point of Wizard is that even her thinking process is synesthetic."

The leader spoke.

"To use the game of Go from the East as an example, humans predict the next move after making one. Even if they draw the entire sequence in their mind, the order of thinking is strictly logical. But Wizard..."

"Disregards causality."

"Yes. It's not reverse thinking, nor is it thinking from the middle. It's like taking a snapshot of the whole thing. Simply put, if humans develop one move at a time..."

The leader took a deep breath.

"Wizard develops the beginning and end of the game, each match, all at once."

Lampa pointed to Wizard.

"No matter what movements the students attacking Wizard make, or what magic they cast, it doesn't matter. The moment a change occurs, the entire picture is completed in her mind at once."

Shirone watched Wizard's battle.

"Bang! Bang! Bang!"

She was firing air guns, a basic form of magic, at the older students.

"There's no power."

"Yes. She's dealing with the students with the minimum damage that the 2000th can judge. It takes about 2000 hits to knock down one person. Yet the result is..."

With the scattering air guns, 14 students simultaneously groaned and fell.

"This is how it happens."

"...I see. Simultaneous suppression is impossible unless the opponent's mental strength is all set to one."

"Yes. The fact that this level of coordination is possible means that thousands of victory patterns have emerged in her mind. If the only goal is to achieve victory, she can win even if 200 people attack. If she accepts some level of damage..."

Lampa stopped speaking.

He felt uneasy about mentioning that theoretically, she could suppress over 400 people.

"Hahaha! I won! I won again!"

Wizard, running with her arms raised among the students who were in shock, approached a girl who was gritting her teeth and blew air while biting her tongue.

"Fwooo! Fwooo! I won. I won."

The girl's eyes widened.

"Damn it!"

But seeing the face of a seven-year-old child, she didn't even have the energy to get angry and lowered her head.

"Fwooo! Fwooo!"

Wizard went around to each of the losers in the 2000th, sticking out her tongue and teasing them.

"I won. I'm stronger."

Shirone spoke.

"She seems to be particularly mischievous towards the girls."

"Yes. She follows the older boys better. There's no malice. She just likes winning and showing off her strength. Of course, from an adult's perspective, that's behavior that should be curbed."

Amy frowned.

"That's what I'm saying. It's a magic school where skills are tested regardless of age. Why are the teachers just standing by? If this continues, how miserable must the other students feel..."

"Ahem, ahem."

The moment Amy heard the official of the Kingdom of Stang clear his throat, she realized.

'Ah, I see.'

Even if Amy were the king, she wouldn't impose any sanctions on Wizard.

Even if all the students here dropped out, it would be much more beneficial to protect Wizard.

'At just four years old, she was ranked 6th among the Ivory Tower candidates. At that time, Shirone was 4th. And three years have passed.'

Lampa spoke.

"Being classified as the nation's top asset, even the principal is cautious around her, as she can handle opponents from level 6 to level 4, transcending eras."

"A genius. There's no officially recognized teaching staff. Above all..."

Shirone nodded.

"It makes sense. Without this level of ability, she wouldn't be able to face Satan."

Conversely, it meant that the price the Ivory Tower would have to pay to take her would be enormous.

'I understand why the king is being so stiff.'

As Shirone turned his head, the official, who had been smiling softly, bowed.

'Once the tour is over, the real negotiations will begin. Money alone won't solve this.'

Even if they tried to use authority, they wouldn't be able to persuade a seven-year-old child.

Shirone continued.

"Allowing her to indulge in her vanity isn't good. For now, it's just for fun, but once she realizes that victory brings more, her mind will become clouded."

Magical skill was a secondary factor. The necessity of Wizard lay in her purity.

Lampa spoke.

"The Five Stars have a point. But who can control that little rascal? Ordinary children at that age are mindful of their parents, but Wizard is different. She has no superiors. Her skill is already the best in the kingdom, and her potential is the greatest in humanity. If we handle her clumsily, we might end up hurting her."

"So, we mustn't handle her clumsily..."

Wizard's voice reached them.

"Teacher, are those older brother and sister magicians too? Can I go say hi? Yes? Yes? Yes?"

Shirone's gaze, watching her, calmed down.

"We need to provide overwhelming education."
