Chapter 720:

"Argo?"

No matter how much he squinted, the hologram in front of him was definitely Amy.

"What do you see?"

"Uh, well…"

Argo nodded as if Shirone's answer wasn't important and continued speaking.

"Emotional focus is crucial for reincarnation. Although rare, if focus weakens, errors can occur in the reincarnation program. The form you see now appears when your brainwaves are most focused."

"Amy…"

He missed her even more today.

"Remember it well. If errors occur in the reincarnation program, you could lose your memories in reality too. If you lose your code, it's hard to recover."

"I'm not suffering from memory loss."

"I know. Your brainwaves don't indicate that. You seem to have come from a different time. That happens occasionally."

Argo seemed to know a lot, perhaps even everything.

"How can I leave this place?"

"You speak like a technician. Interesting. Would you like to follow me? I'll show you around."

Although he hadn't met the humans he wanted, Shirone felt he could have a conversation with Argo, so he followed her into the elevator.

Just like the drones that perfectly controlled gravity, the elevator operated without noise or even the slightest vibration.

"This world's name is Utopia. It originally had many names, but after the last reincarnation refuser died, representatives from various countries held a meeting and created it. Currently, about 16 billion people live in Utopia."

"Live?"

He thought they might be dead.

"Yes, they are all alive. And they neither increase nor decrease. It seems humans have chosen to live new lives here forever."

When the elevator arrived and the doors opened, a massive space as large as the tower's circumference appeared.

The ceiling was at the top of the tower, and along the cylindrical walls, countless people were inside the glass tubes he had seen from outside.

There seemed to be more than 10,000.

Shirone, lost in the grandeur, asked Argo.

"What kind of world is this?"

"It's the same world. I don't know what kind of world you lived in, but it won't be different. It just converges endlessly."

When Argo raised her hand, a large hologram three meters high lit up, showing numerous images.

"Prototype Argo. The first fully synchronized simulation program. It connects to human brainwaves, allowing them to experience entirely different worlds."

As the images on the large hologram changed, the life of a young man passed by quickly.

"So, the people sleeping here are living entirely different lives inside those glass tubes?"

"Yes. When Argo was first serviced, it was just a commercial entertainment product. Virtual reality has its limits. The thought that you can return at any time reduces the sense of reality."

Being trapped is the only reality.

"So humanity made a significant decision. They closed the world so that people couldn't know Argo was a simulation."

"Closed the world?"

"It wasn't easy. Providing virtual reality and making people truly believe it are technically very different. Plus, there were issues of stability and ethics…"

Argo skipped unnecessary information.

"Anyway, Utopia solved humanity's problem of eternal life. They completed the final program, Argo."

When Shirone looked up, he saw a massive iron sphere with mechanical lenses floating above.

He hadn't noticed it in its smaller form, but the giant eye resembled something.

"Anke Ra?"

Amy's hologram placed one hand on her stomach and bowed politely.

"Let me formally introduce myself. I am Argo, the reincarnation manager of Utopia, and the Akashic Record."

Shirone had met the digital Ra in the Apocalypse, but he didn't remember it as it was a place where information remnants gathered.

"I don't understand what you're saying."

"Let me introduce Utopia. There are spare spots available. If you wish to reincarnate, I can assist you."

When Argo raised her head, two drones flew in and faced each other in front of Shirone.

Electricity flowed from Amy's hologram eyes, and the two drones communicated in their language.

"Zzzzt! Zzzzzzt! Zzt! Zzzzzzt!"

As the eerie electromagnetic sound ended, screens displaying each person's life appeared in front of over 10,000 glass tubes.

Feeling his fingertips tremble, Shirone looked around at the screens with a pale face.

"Argo is an eternal life program. For humanity to live forever, I too must be eternal. I am an AI capable of self-system recovery. Therefore, even if reset, I do not disappear."

"Reset?"

"No system is perfect. Sometimes a reset is necessary. Although it's a virtual world, there is a specific intention. When the world doesn't flow as intended, we reset and try again. In other words, we develop the eternal life program again in Argo's world. We live forever through the eons."

For the first time, Shirone felt fear.

"Another problem is if someone opens the closed world. It's classified as the most serious error. In such cases, we reset or erase the entity."

"Erase?"

He thought of Goffin's erasure.

"Removing specific information. But if that happens, it causes system confusion, so unless conditions are met, I don't have the authority to erase."

Even if it was virtual, it was troubling if Argo could erase information at will.

"In Utopia, Argo manages reincarnation, but it is also linked to the world humans dream of. However, even I don't realize it's virtual there. No entity should know. It must be a closed world."

World No. 9999 was entirely different from the world Shirone lived in, but he wondered if that was really the case.

"Do they live like that? Without ever thinking it's fake, believing they're living in reality?"

Argo brought a screen showing someone's life in front of Shirone.

"Photons mediate the signals between Argo and humans. Biological signals use electricity, and precision systems use quantum transmission, but the basic medium is always light. It's so sophisticated that they can never think it's fake."

Argo looked at the screen and spoke.

"Since the time in the virtual world is based on the speed of light, no material can be faster than light. Also, as the basic medium, the speed of light is always constant."

It was the principle of the invariance of the speed of light.

"When perceived at the speed of light, time stops, but that's allowed within normal limits. However, if it becomes faster than light, time is disrupted in the system. Time paradoxes cause overload. It's a serious problem."

Shirone watched the life of a soldier fighting on a battlefield through the screen.

The soldier was crying out in severe pain from a serious injury.

"Did they want this kind of life?"

"No. While it's not impossible to artificially set a life, it's prohibited by law. The person dreaming is a woman, though the screen shows a soldier. The system determines what entity they will be born as in the next reincarnation, based on Karr."

"Karr?"

"It refers to the degree of violation of causality. The lower the Karr, the easier it is for the system to predict, and the higher the Karr, the harder it is to predict. Usually, Karr increases with each reincarnation, but it is not set to exceed 90%. It threatens the system."

The soldier on the screen was dying from an arrow wound.

"What happens when they die?"

"They wake up from the dream. When they come out of the capsule, drones conduct a detailed examination. They can rest in Utopia for a few days or reincarnate again."

"But they don't know until the moment they die? The stress must be immense. Are there no side effects?"

"There are some."

Argo admitted frankly.

"It was the biggest challenge of the Utopia project. A closed world is good, but no one wants to experience the fear of death. So, representatives from various countries developed a highly efficient program."

"What kind of program?"

"God."

Amy's hologram raised her index finger.

"When the God program was introduced, the system's stability improved remarkably. The concept of no end reduced stress levels by over 90%. Therefore, entities with a certain Karr level in Argo are set to establish a god."

The screen showed the soldier praying to a god, then suddenly there was noise.

"What's this?"

The screen endlessly showed a first-person view of rushing through a tunnel of colorful lights.

"It's a near-death experience program. It's an electromagnetic tunnel that forms during the process of updating reality after a life cycle ends. Here, they recover their memories of reality. Sometimes variables occur during loading, causing them to return, but the God program absorbs the errors."

Argo's lens tightened and then relaxed.

"Fortunately, the transmission was successful. Taking care of those who return to reality is also the administrator's role."

The screen changed to a view of Utopia, showing the glass tubes installed inside the building.

"No!"

As the lid opened, a blonde woman suddenly sat up.

It had been a terrible death, but she soon recalled her memories of reality and sighed.

"Ugh, so annoying. Why do they always shoot arrows at me?"

It had been a lifetime, but her reaction was as if she had just woken from a bad dream.

A drone flew to her and opened its lens.

"Did you have a pleasant journey? This is Utopia."

"I know."

The woman answered dismissively and stepped out of the capsule, with the drone following her.

"Starting a detailed examination. Do you need anything?"

"Water. A sedative."

The drone extended a hose from its body to her mouth, instantly generating water.

Then it moved to her neck and injected the sedative with a soft click. The woman's shoulder twitched, but that was the extent of her reaction.

As the drone examined her, the woman asked,

"What about my next reincarnation?"

"Karr value 67%. There are 2,598 options available in the system."

"Can I be born as a human?"

"Yes, you can."

The woman stretched and murmured,

"67% is still a long way to go. What's the record for the person who has gone the furthest?"

"Currently, it is Omega 876 years, based on light power."

"Wow, that's really fast."

Shirone, watching the screen, asked Argo,

"Omega 876 years?"

It was a unit of time recorded in the records left by Goffin in Babel.

"Omega is a unit that divides the beginning and end of time into percentages. The core of the Utopia project is to keep delving into simulations. When you reach Omega 1,000 years, you enter the next simulation, so the frequency of returning to reality decreases."

It meant living forever without wasting even a few days in reality.

"As simulations continue, the period to reach Omega 1,000 years will shorten based on the time here."

Because it is the eons.

"Ten years, five years, two years, continuously decreasing until finally one second, 0.5 seconds… Eventually, you will live eternity in a moment."

Shirone couldn't believe it.

"But time is still passing here, right? In 100 years, they will eventually die."

"Of course. But for the residents of Utopia, that future will never come. In the world of the eons, time will be infinitely divided."

It was the secret of true eternal life.
