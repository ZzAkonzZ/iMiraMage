Chapter 973: The Absent Presence (3)

After Shirone annihilated Ionas, Ikael's holy light no longer wavered.

It simply illuminated the sky with a brilliance greater than anything in the world.

'Goffin.'

It was a mix of pain and joy, like pouring cold and hot water into the heart simultaneously.

'Truly, truly...'

Ikael saw in Shirone.

'Our child.'

The image of the man she loved most in the world.

Shirone, who was observing through clairvoyance, seemed to be right in front of her, reaching out his hand.

"Ah."

As Ikael raised her arm to grasp his hand,

"Restrain yourself."

The archangels surrounded her.

Even Uriel, who had secretly supported Ikael, now showed only calm hostility.

"You know, don't you? If you go to Shirone now, there will be no turning back."

Uriel was on her left, Rael on her right, and Satiel guarded her rear.

As Ikael turned her gaze left and right to feel them, she saw that Maras blocked the path ahead.

Satiel spoke.

"Give up. No matter how strong you are, you can't stop all of us. And we won't show any mercy."

For a long time, Ikael felt the archangels' killing intent piercing her like thorns.

"Heh."

One corner of Ikael's mouth lifted.

Uriel, Rael, and Satiel couldn't react to the unfamiliar sense of incongruity.

They could only wonder if there had ever been a time in history when an archangel had sneered at someone.

Ikael asked,

"You think I can't stop all of you?" She had already said so.

Ikael turned to look at the archangels.

"Answer me. Do you truly believe that I can't stop all of you?"

Though they had already said so, the archangels were compelled to reconsider as if under a spell.

Is it really so?

Can we...

Can we subdue Ikael?

"Shirone is my son."

Having kept it in her heart for so long, just saying it made her tremble with emotion.

'I'm not afraid.'

Goffin was gone, but wasn't his son, who resembled him so much, reaching out his hand?

"I've returned after many years, but now I won't back down. As an archangel, I command you: if you block my path..."

Ikael spoke coldly,

"Don't expect any respect from me anymore."

Satiel unleashed the judicial halo Nostalgia and retorted,

"Respect? That's what we should say. You're not an archangel. You're a cancerous existence fighting for humans and betraying the heavenly army."

In the face of clear heavenly rules, Uriel couldn't show personal preferences.

'...Do we have no choice but to fight?'

The judicial halo Ragnarok unfolded, and Rael activated Accelerator.

"You've relinquished your authority yourself, so don't blame us."

"My authority is given only because it's me."

Ikael let out a hollow laugh.

"It's something you don't need to mention, worry about, or break."

As soon as she finished speaking, three archangels and Mara in front charged simultaneously.

It was an almost cosmic momentum, but Ikael's eyes sank into a deep abyss instead.

She spoke,

"增 (Increase)."

At that moment, countless beings on earth saw another sun floating in the sky.

Ymir twisted his body painfully as he was trapped by Law Killer's law.

Shirone's group could only watch while calming their rapidly beating hearts.

They didn't know how they could kill Ymir, but the law was trying to create an incident by drawing in everything that existed in the universe.

It was beyond human imagination; all they could see with their eyes was the surface of the black space surrounding Ymir occasionally glowing with rainbow colors.

"Ugh!"

The killing intent that could kill all living beings in the universe began to squeeze Ymir's heart.

'Die, please die.'

Even those who didn't believe in gods clasped their hands together instinctively because they honestly didn't want to fight again.

'You just need to disappear.'

Should the universe have to sacrifice itself?

As the black space rose to his neck and covered his entire body, Ymir glared at Shirone and spoke,

"In this way..."

His voice was cut off as his mouth was blocked by space, and eventually even his face was covered.

What Shirone's group saw was probably just a black sphere containing the entire universe.

"Is it over?"

Soon after, the black sphere vibrated violently, foretelling destruction.

Rumble!

The ground shook up and down like an earthquake as buildings swayed left and right.

'It doesn't matter. As long as we can catch him here.'

At that moment, white cracks began to form in the black space.

The cracks accelerated, and golden light burst out fiercely from the grotesquely split gaps.

"Argh!"

Ymir spread his limbs wide and roared as the black space clinging to him burned away without a trace.

Even after witnessing that scene with his own eyes, it took Shirone a long time to utter the word "failure."

'It can't be.'

〈Law Kill〉 never disappears until it kills its target.

If it can't kill through a specific incident, it will manipulate circumstances artificially to take its opponent's life—it's a law weapon designed for that purpose inherently.

'And yet it couldn't kill Ymir...'

Those who knew 〈Law Kill〉 and even those who didn't instinctively realized one thing:

"There is no way to kill him." At least not in this universe.

A chilling truth sent shivers down their spines.

"The attempt was good."

Ymir walked forward with a slightly tired expression.

"I don't know what it was exactly, but it was impressive. However..."

Ymir unleashed a shockwave and lunged at everyone causing mental confusion.

"It's not my preferred method."

"Dodge!"

The incarnation of Avalokiteshvara created an iron wall with countless palms.

"Hiyaaa!"

As Ymir charged straight through it, Miro's pupils shook momentarily.

"Whew!"

Quickly regaining her composure, she took a deep breath and swung both arms wide.

Avalokiteshvara - Twin Thunder Palms

As Ymir got caught between her palms in prayer position, billions of strikes surged through extreme time towards him.

"Hehehehe!"

Even in this situation, the sight of Ymir laughing was terribly unsettling.

"Weak."

As Ymir stomped his foot, the earth trembled.

"So weak!"

At the same time, a chillingly sharp sound cut through the air, carried by the rumble of the ground.

"... I"

It must have been instinct.

Ymir, who had been delivering a straight punch to Miro's face, turned his head.

'What?'

It was an air bullet the size of a fist tearing through the atmosphere.

The moment he moved his other hand to block it, a loud bang pushed Ymir back.

"Ugh...!"

The bullet he caught exploded, tearing its shell apart.

'It hurts.'

The shock pierced his bones and exited.

The group, who had been dumbly watching Ymir being pushed back dozens of meters, slowly turned their heads.

"Ah...?"

The sound of dying, and from the shadows of the alley, someone stepped forward.

At first, they thought it was a corpse.

With disheveled hair and a gaunt face that resembled a skull, it was a man.

"Ah, ah...?"

Even speaking seemed difficult, and his legs were stiff, but Miro recognized him at a glance.

"Gaold."

More than joy, she couldn’t understand how he had managed to come this far in such a terrible state.

Like a lost person, he looked around, then his eyes landed on Miro, who had narrowly escaped death.

A flicker of life returned to his pitch-black pupils.

"Don’t cry."

Gaold said with a dazed expression.

"Smile."

Sein approached.

"Gaold you..."

As he got closer, a foul smell wafted from him, but even that was nothing compared to his dire condition.

Miro asked,

"Why did you come?"

When the group turned their heads, her face was expressionless.

"Just die. Why show up in front of us in such a state?"

Sein's eyes widened.

"Is that what you say now? After whose life saved you?"

Miro put out her hand as if to stop him.

Even if it wasn’t to maintain the absolute purity of the Quantum Projection, she would have said the same thing.

"Go back. Your role is over."

"I can’t go back."

"Why?"

Miro gritted her teeth.

"Why are you being so pathetic? I told you I liked you. I said I’d be your woman if you wanted. You were the one who rejected that. And now I despise someone like you."

"... Yeah."

"Then get lost! Disappear from my sight!"

"Alright, Miro. So..."

Gaold lifted his chin and faintly smiled.

"Smile."

Though still an incomprehensible remark, Miro clenched her teeth with force in her eyes.

As Gaold moved, Ymir, who had been observing his torn palms, lifted his head.

The emaciated human limping towards him did not feel weak at all.

'It’s the peak of something.'

Ymir intuited.

'If I am an existence infinitely far from sensation...'

Ymir stood up again, showing his intent to kill.

"The being closest to sensation."

"What are you babbling about?"

As Gaold, his one eye half-closed, spoke, the corners of Ymir’s mouth rose.

"... You did well to come."

Everything breaks, and he becomes the dignity of the universe.

"I am the strongest!"

As Ymir's heavy body launched forward, Gaold's face twisted like a demon.

'Vacum Press.'

Ymir’s rush halted abruptly as his waist bent sharply.

"Hehe! It’s a proper technique, but..."

As Ymir slowly lifted his head, trembling, Gaold's nerves started to jitter all at once.

"Hehe, hehehehe!"

But his expression, like Ymir's, was also wickedly smiling.

"You call yourself the king of giants?"

Triggered by Gaold’s characteristic taunt, Sein realized the unusual danger and reached out.

"No! If you break here..."

Everyone in the city would die.

"Ugh...!"

Gaold's eyes rolled back, ignoring even the semblance of listening.

'Pain?'

In a mental space devoid of light, an endless corridor unfolded.

'What is pain?'

Standing at the starting point, Gaold walked towards the blades stretching from the destination.

The blade pierced his abdomen.

'It’s pain.'

Despite feeling the blade slicing continuously inside him, he didn’t stop.

As he moved forward, another blade awaited at the shoulder.

'I move forward.'

The number of blades increased, piercing his forehead, heart, thighs...

Finally, in front of Gaold, countless blades waited, enough to pierce his entire body.

'It is moving forward.'

Even amid pain like burning fire, his legs did not stop.

If he stopped here, the pain might lessen, but if that meant death...

'Ugh...!'

Gaold charged with all his might.

'Aaaah!'

Even as thousands of blades sliced through him, not knowing where the end was, or even if there was an end.

'I won’t stop.'

Throwing himself towards the visible pain, that was exactly...

"My life!"

As Gaold's consciousness compressed tremendously, the eyes of Shirone’s group trembled with shock.

"Crazy..."

At the same time, an immeasurable Air Press crushed Ymir’s body.
