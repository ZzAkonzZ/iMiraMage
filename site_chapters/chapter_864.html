Chapter 864: Rain (3)

"Theraphos existed before humans and achieved a far superior civilization."

As the Chief Justice walked around the Grand Temple, Grand Star followed him with her eyes.

"They endlessly explored the essence of the world. Where does it end, where does it begin? Does it even exist? The conclusion we reached was..."

The Chief Justice stopped and looked back at Grand Star.

"This world is electricity." It was a fact, but Grand Star could say nothing.

"And we are already using electricity. The hypothesis we can derive from this reflection is..."

The Chief Justice raised his finger.

"Electricity was supplied from somewhere, and the universe was born. Just as we supply power to machines."

The Chief Justice clasped his hands behind his back and turned around.

"Before that? There was nothing. Only void. No existence, no reason to exist."

It was a state of being turned off.

"We pondered. Why was this world born? What does it exist for?"

They delved endlessly.

"And what we discovered was the Akashic Records, the cosmic database, and the existence of Anke Ra."

According to that definition,

"Anke Ra, who dreams of the universe, cannot criticize himself. The moment he realizes the dream, it is no longer a dream. Therefore, he believes he is unique and cannot suspect that he was created by someone else."

"But he began to doubt."

"Yes. The result of the Gaians breaking the Akashic Records and leaving the photon system threw him into contradiction."

It was an event that cracked Ankera's uniqueness.

"Why was Anke Ra absorbed by Nane?"

The Chief Justice answered his own question.

"Because the blood of Gaia flows in humans. Gaia's departure means that what is the end for us is not the end for humans."

"A world created for humans."

"Or a world created by humans. In fact, humans are gods, and they borrowed that concept for this world."

It was a painful assumption for everyone except humans.

"Theraphos accepted it. Surprisingly, it was easy. Rather, it gave us a new role after everything had become meaningless beyond the technological singularity."

This too might have been designed.

"Theraphos delved into the reason for existence. And concluded that as the judges of the universe, we should promote the stability of the world. It's like a malfunctioning software system."

Grand Star turned her gaze to the screen showing the ground.

"Sending the Ark to save humans is in the same context."

Now, all life forms except humans were extinct in the Gustav Empire.

"Humans have a unique code. Of course, it changes in real-time, but Theraphos's information-gathering ability is the best in the universe. As long as they are trapped in the magnetic shield, they will be safe."

Grand Star asked a question she was curious about.

"What about Guy? Why wasn't he protected by the shield? Was it because he reached the Idea?"

The Chief Justice shook his head.

"I said humans have a unique code. It's a temporary code. But the Idea is different. It's a perfect user code that never changes. Among the current survivors, Rian is the only one with that code."

Rian was protected by the magnetic shield.

"Then what about Guy?"

"The activation condition of the magnetic shield is the inner voice generated by a specific code. Guy..."

The Chief Justice looked up and said,

"He didn't want it."

"Didn't want it..."

"Well, it's hard to express emotions in words. But unlike other codes, he no longer had a desire for life. That's all."

"Calmed down...?"

Shirone clenched his fist as he chewed over Guy's words.

"How could I be? Think about what you did to humans! So many innocent people died! Seongeum has to wander in hell forever! Why! Why did you lie?"

Even though Guy was dead, the Shiyok had not appeared.

"Before I fought you, I met Rian."

Shirone's shoulders twitched.

If this situation wasn't a dream, then Shirone had killed Rian's brother.

"I thought he was hopeless when he was being immature, but today I saw that he had grown into a truly impressive man. Stronger than me."

"Rian is different from someone like you."

Shirone tried to separate Rian from Guy.

"Haha."

Guy acknowledged with a laugh.

For a moment, a pleased emotion passed through his eyes as he rested his chin in thought.

He remembered Rian's back.

"...I thought I could do anything to become stronger. Right and wrong didn't matter."

Guy spread his arms as if seeking agreement.

"Honestly, isn't that true? In the end, the winner is right. If you become truly strong, things like moral conscience and justice don't matter."

It sounded like a final statement, so Shirone couldn't interrupt.

"Live responsibly... I finally understood Rian's words."

"I can't live like that. The moment I saw what that youngest boy was carrying on his shoulders, I was disgusted. How can he live like that?"

He had run away, but there was something he had to tell Shirone.

"Rian is the strong one."

The person who can take on the most responsibility is the strongest.

"You said he's your friend. Please take care of my brother."

"...What are you doing?"

Shirone gritted his teeth.

"What difference does it make to say that now? Don't pretend to be family all of a sudden! Do you think I'll forgive you? You're a murderer!"

It had to be a dream.

"What about the lives of those who died for no reason? If you die..."

Guy must not die.

"How do I explain this to Rian?"

Guy smiled brightly.

"No need to explain. Just dump it all on that fool. Tell him to take responsibility."

"Is that something a brother should say?"

"He's strong."

Guy closed his eyes.

"That's what a knight is."

He wanted to be like Rian.

As the light connecting Guy faded, Shirone's face turned pale.

"Wait! If you go like this..."

Finally, the light disappeared, and Guy's body became blurry and began to be swallowed by darkness.

Eternal annihilation.

"No!"

Shirone reached out in a hurry, but his senses couldn't catch any signal.

Rumble.

The black cloud filling the sky began to turn gray and eventually became transparent.

"Rain, it's raining..."

As the people sheltered by the shield looked up, they saw a massive spaceship floating in the clear sky.

"Shirone."

Rian immediately swung his greatsword at the dome.

A tremendous roar erupted, but the dome didn't break, and the shockwave swirled in the narrow space.

"Ugh!"

The reason it couldn't be easily destroyed was that the state of the Idea was also a kind of electrical signal.

"Yahhh!"

Rian repeatedly swung his sword, ignoring the destruction of his body.

'Shirone! Shirone!'

Rian didn't understand why the world-destroying rain was falling.

But whenever such an apocalyptic event occurred, Shirone was always there.

'Wait!'

Buzz!

As the greatsword vibrated and struck the dome, it finally shattered the magnetic shield with a tremendous explosion.

"The Hexa Program has been terminated." The faces of the Theraphos species were not suited to show emotions, but the Chief Justice was clearly confused.

"What happened?"

Grand Star’s eyes widened as she watched the ground.

"Shirone..."

The Hexa particles on the screen gathered densely and restored Shirone's body.

"Impossible. He can't regain his body after turning into a signal."

According to Theraphos's theory, there was no creation code.

"No."

The Chief Justice raised his head sharply.

"I see. The Idea is..."

"Shirone has returned!"

Grand Star exclaimed with joy, but the Chief Justice's expression was unexpectedly serious.

"Is it not over yet?"

"What about the Ark?"

"We'll withdraw it. The selection is canceled. But I don't know if this situation is beneficial or harmful."

The Chief Justice's words were quite meaningful, but Grand Star turned to the screen with a smile.

"Thank you."

It was all she could say to Shirone, who was facing terrible pain.

"Huh?"

As Shirone regained consciousness, the recent events passed through his mind like a dream.

"Wounds...?"

He checked his chest and found that the place where Guy's sword had pierced was completely healed.

But what was even more strange was that the anger that had been growing without limit had disappeared.

"Where am I?" It was as if he had come to an entirely different planet. All he could see was an endlessly stretching horizon.

"Everything is gone."

Guy had said it.

Do you know what you've done?

"No."

Shirone didn't know what disaster Yahweh's anger had brought to the world, but one thing was clear.

Guy's words were true.

"I, I..."

Shirone knelt weakly, searching for Guy's body, which had left no trace.

"This can't be. This can't be..."

Was it not a dream?

"I killed them all. People, Rian's brother, all living things... Animals, plants, not even a single blade of grass was left."

"Shirone!"

At Rian's voice, Shirone's head snapped up.

"Rian?"

Rian, who had rushed over, grabbed Shirone's shoulders and checked his body.

"Are you okay? What happened?"

"Rian, you're... safe."

"The people are safe. Some kind of shield was deployed. It must have been that spaceship."

As Shirone looked at the receding spaceship, Rian briefly explained what had happened.

"Rain of light fell..."

He hadn't seen it with his own eyes, but imagining the phenomenon he had created was horrifying.

'I tried to destroy the world.'

"It's okay. Anyway, we're safe. The demons are wiped out. Let's go back. I need to find my brother."

Shirone's heart sank.

"Maybe he was protected by the shield too. Damn, he should have listened when I asked him to come with us."

"Rian..."

Rian, not hearing Shirone's voice, continued to look around for Guy.

"Did he already escape? Or was he injured..."

"Rian!"

Unable to watch Rian worry about his brother, Shirone raised his head and shouted.

"What's wrong? Your face is pale." Shirone's mouth opened and closed.

In his mind, now blank, self-destructive words surfaced.

"I killed him."

"What?"

"I killed Guy."

Rian's eyes calmed.

"I don't know. It feels like a dream, but it's not. Guy saved me. So I..."

"Shirone."

As Rian spoke in a calm voice and approached, Shirone cried out.

"Rian! I killed your family..."

Rian pulled Shirone into his broad shoulders.

"It's okay."

Knowing he was sincere made it even scarier.

"As long as you're alive, it doesn't matter who you killed. I'll take care of everything."

Tears streamed down Shirone's cheeks.

"You've been through a lot. You've been fighting non-stop. Rest a bit. After a nap, you'll feel better."

Rian was a kind-hearted person, so he couldn't be okay.

'He'll carry even this burden.'

As Shirone leaned his soul on Rian, his consciousness began to fade.

At the end, Guy's words suddenly came to mind.

'The demon realm, Yahweh's anger...'

The strongest one is Rian.
