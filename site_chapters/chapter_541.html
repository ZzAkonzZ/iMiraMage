Chapter 541: Forbidden Sanctuary (1)

As she washed herself under the shower, Miro closed her eyes and sank into thought.

This just keeps getting worse.

Malta was dead, and his wife had been erased in the course of Fermi’s experiment.

Rian had seen that too as murder, but the reason he ultimately kept his mouth shut wasn’t solely because of Shirone.

The “open state.”

Everything except the Abyss Walkers was a lie—life itself had never existed here, only informational constructs.

That includes Shirone.

If they were to meet, Shirone might recover some of his memories, like with the Malta couple. But in the end, they would be distorted memories.

“Miro.”

A voice came from the bathroom. Miro opened her eyes to see Arius shimmering like a mirage, kneeling in a bow.

“You’ve finally entered?”

“I’m only transmitting information through the code. But I won’t be able to maintain this for long.”

Meaning time was short.

“Alright, speak.”

“Shirone is currently located in the northwestern sector of the city. However, I couldn’t retrieve any detailed data due to a firewall. It seems someone with Coder abilities is there.”

“One of the mutants Malta mentioned?”

“Possibly.”

“What’s Shirone’s condition?”

“His information flow is stable. But… by mission standards, it’s not looking good.”

Exactly as Fermi had predicted.

“I see.”

“Search around the settlement. You’ll find Shirone.”

“A settlement, huh. But I need to communicate with them. Can you code their language?”

“It’s possible. I’ll inject the necessary data. But once I do, I won’t be able to return.”

“I don’t care. Code the others too.”

Arius bowed his head.

“Please be careful. Two new data entries were added to the Apocalypse recently. They aren’t perfect Incarnates—not Minority Conceptions.”

“Abyss Walkers. What should I watch out for?”

“I couldn’t identify them exactly, but when I first encountered their code…”

Arius looked up.

“…it felt like there were two more of you, Miro.”

Miro froze, her hand paused in mid-wash.

“That’s how powerful their Incarnation is. They've split into two now, but if you meet one, even for you… it could be trouble.”

Miro didn’t look offended.

So, the Council of Ten sent people… But why are they here?

There was only one possible answer.

They’ve come to the same conclusion as Uorin.

Miro turned off the shower and stepped out.

“Got it. From here on, I’ll handle things. Inject the code.”

“May your form be preserved.”

Arius’s projection dissolved into a stream of symbols and numbers, fading away.

After a night of steady rain, a clear sun finally rose over the skies of Apocalypse.

Having left the settlement early in the morning, Shirone and Yoa decided to explore deeper into the city while the weather held.

“The rain’s cleared.”

Yoa smiled brightly, and Shirone replied while looking up at the sunlight.

“But without water, survival would be impossible.”

“It’s alright. With what we’ve stored, we’ll be fine for a month. I just really love the sun.”

A blush spread across Yoa’s face.

“And… I like your light too, Arbiter.”

Shirone summoned a Shining spell and let it float above his head.

Yesterday, it had been part of a strategy. This time, it was just teasing.

“Aaah! I love it!”

“You’ve got quite the appetite, Yoa.”

Yoa understood the ancient joke.

“Haha! That’s true. Actually, I—”

She stopped as soon as she saw the sharp glint in Shirone’s eyes and heightened her awareness.

Ambush.

Yoa activated Silver Mirror, stepping in front of Shirone just as bullets rained from all sides.

Between the shimmer of her metallic arms, Undergrounders could be seen.

“Meat! We eat meat!”

There were over forty of them, at a glance.

“It’s a combat squad! Get behind me!”

Yoa was duty-bound to protect the Arbiter at all costs, but Shirone didn’t hesitate for even a second.

Scatter Movement!

In a flash, Shirone split into eight directions in photonized form and unleashed Photon Cannons everywhere.

Then, chaining teleportation spells, he wove a net of light that turned the surroundings into a storm of destruction.

“Wow…”

Yoa stared in awe, momentarily speechless—until she heard engines rumbling down the alley.

“Ahaha! It’s hunting time!”

A jeep mounted with a machine gun appeared, flanked by seven motorcycles. The soldiers whooped with excitement.

“Commander!”

Shirone’s eyes widened at the sight of a massive Undergrounder, twice the size of the driver.

“What the hell…?”

There was no way to gauge an average size for their species.

“That’s the Commander. The combat squad’s leader.”

“We’ll have to retreat for now.”

Spreading his Photon Wings, Shirone grabbed Yoa and took to the sky. The machine gun on the jeep rotated quickly, leaving a black trail of bullet marks behind them.

“Uuuuugh!”

As they soared toward a rooftop, Yoa shouted.

“Arbiter! Below!”

The massive Commander was scaling the wall with shocking agility.

What is this thing?

As bullets started flying again, Shirone dove off the ledge with a Rainbow Drop.

“Yaaaaaah!”

The overexcited Commander twisted midair and hurled himself toward Shirone.

Are you insane?! That’s twenty stories!

“Ahahahahaha!”

Apparently unfazed by the idea of falling to his death, he aimed his rifle downward and opened fire in a crazed burst.

Shirone canceled teleportation and activated Overcharge, bending the bullets slightly off course with a shimmering light barrier.

I can see the bullets… this is terrifying.

Focus! If I miss the timing on this fall, I’m dead!

Praying no ricochet would catch him, Shirone teleported just before impact and skidded along the ground.

“Goddamn it!”

The Commander landed hard right after, tumbling and crashing into a junked car.

“Uwaaargh!”

“Commander! Are you okay?!”

The jeep and motorcycles rallied to his side.

With his face twisted like a gorilla’s and shoulder dislocated, the Commander popped it back in and climbed onto the jeep.

“Go! It’s feast time!”

“Kyayayaya!”

The howling cries of the monkey-like mob sent chills down Shirone’s spine.

These guys are seriously dangerous. Physically sure, but their weapons rival magic in power…

Or was it that his magic was only on par with their weapons?

Pushing the confusion aside, Shirone said:

“We’re not going to shake them. We’ll have to fight.”

“There is one place,” Yoa said. “They absolutely won’t follow us in.”

“Absolutely?”

“Yes, probably. Because even I don’t want to go there. We call it the Forbidden Sanctuary.”

“That dangerous?”

“I don’t know. It’s part of the contamination zone, but no one’s been there in ages. And honestly, I’m starting to feel weird just thinking about it.”

Hmm. I don’t feel anything.

If it was just a meme encoded into the minds of Apocalypse’s inhabitants, Shirone had no reason to hesitate.

“Take me there. Lead the way.”

Uncomfortable but unable to defy the Arbiter’s order, Yoa pointed ahead.

“That way.”

They arrived at the tallest building in the city.

It was entirely covered in mucus, with no visible signs of damage. That alone was unsettling.

“This place definitely gives off a weird vibe.”

Anyone native to Apocalypse would find it downright ominous.

“But the mucus is blocking the entrance.”

Yoa shattered her Silver Mirror.

“It’s best not to touch it, but when we’re in a hurry, this is how we make a path.”

The mirror’s shards scratched the mucous coating along the floor. It twitched like a living thing, then slowly receded.

“There they are!”

Undergrounders came charging from across the block. Shirone grabbed Yoa and activated Overcharge.

“Let’s go!”

With the barrier pulsing, the entrance opened wide before them.

“Don’t let them escape! Fire! Fire!”

Without looking back, Shirone and Yoa disappeared through the door just as the jeep screeched to a stop in front of the building.

“They ran into the Forbidden Sanctuary? Cowards.”

“What should we do, Commander?”

Two full-grown adults could feed the entire squad easily.

But even the battle-hardened Commander, who had leapt from twenty stories, didn’t want to set foot in that place.

“No choice. We’re leaving. Let’s find a different hunt.”

The engines roared as they drove away.

A drop of mucous splashed against the stone marker standing beside the building’s entrance.

FAIRY BIOMEMETICS RESEARCH LAB.

KWAANG!

A thunderous boom echoed from the other side of the city, causing Miro's group to stop and look back.

“What was that? A battle?”

The sound of gunfire followed shortly after.

“Let’s go check it out. Sounds like it’s getting intense.”

Now that they knew Shirone was in the settlement, they couldn’t just ignore it.

Using spatial teleportation, Miro skipped them across the distance in a single leap, and the group began running in the direction of the gunfire.

“How annoying.”

Fermi cast his Air Skate spell, lifting slightly off the ground and gliding swiftly across the pavement.

“You’ve got all kinds of weird spells,” Marsha quipped.

Striking a wide-legged pose as he turned, Fermi replied, “Can’t fly like with a Fly spell, but for movement magic, this one has the best cost-performance. And great mobility, too.”

He ran a quick set of run steps, then glanced back at the group.

“In any case, I’ve sunk all my money into this operation.”

It was a mission worth 190 billion gold if successful.

Rian said, “They’re getting farther ahead. Looks like a pursuit.”

Annoyed, Marsha asked Fermi, “Hey, how much is that? You can transfer it, right?”

“Two million gold. Interested?”

“Shut up.”

Marsha resigned herself to running on foot.

“This is weird. Really weird.”

Shirone looked around the building lobby.

It was a place he’d seen many times in the past. Other than the mucus clinging to the walls, nothing seemed particularly unusual.

“Of course it’s weird. No one ever comes here,” Yoa said.

“No, not like that… I’m sure I’ve been here before.”

A memory surged through Shirone’s mind.

A pure-white office with an oak table. A man in glasses handed him a document and said—

—“Congratulations, Mr. Shirone. You’ve been selected for artificial stasis. Just sign here.”

—“No. I can’t leave behind the people I care about and survive alone.”

—“You don’t understand. This isn’t about personal survival. You’re being selected as a representative of humanity.”

—“But how could I…”

—“Don’t act arrogant. Even I, who am handing you this contract, will be staying behind. This is the end of the line for us.”

“…Did I really end up alone?”

Yoa didn’t fully understand his feelings, but the fact that Shirone looked sad was enough for her.

“Don’t worry. You’ve got us now.”

Shirone walked away from the walls and looked up the stairwell.

I’m sure that office was at the top of this building…

But now the upper floors were completely sealed off by reddish, glistening mucus—just a wall of slime.

“Think they’ve gone back by now? They won’t wait too long just to hunt.”

Ignoring Yoa’s nervous comment, Shirone tried to search deeper into his memories.

—“Let’s head underground. There’s no time.”

—“Sorry. I was only thinking of myself.”

—“…Please create a better world.”

Underground. There’s something underground.

Shirone ran toward the elevator and called out to Yoa.

“Can you open this door?”

“Huh? Oh—yes.”

Yoa turned her arms into metal and wedged them into the gap between the doors, then pulled hard.

“Hrrraaaa!”

The doors creaked open to reveal a vertical elevator shaft.

“There. Now we can get underground.”

“You’re really going down there?”

“If you’re scared, you don’t have to come.”

Yoa’s expression changed instantly.

“No. I have a duty to protect the Arbiter.”

“I’ll be fine.”

Shirone tossed a Shining spell down the shaft and grabbed the elevator cable to begin his descent. Yoa followed right behind him.

…

One minute later, a bulge formed in the mucus spread across the ceiling of the lobby—shaped like a human face.
