Chapter 963: Face to Face (2)

'I can see it.'

The world, filled with the signal of Agape, began to regain its colors.

Miro, who had descended to the ground, looked around.

The city of Bashka, once teeming with demons, was now eerily empty.

"Ugh, ugh."

Even the strongest demons, those with the densest concentration of dark energy, were half-melted, crawling on the ground.

'Half of them are gone.'

Judging by the population density, at least 60 million demons had been annihilated.

Of course, the city of Bashka was almost entirely wiped out.

The surviving army was outside the range of Agape, beyond the walls of Bashka.

'But the important thing is...'

The humans in the city had not suffered any harm.

"We survived."

The citizens, regaining their senses, stood up.

"We're alive! We survived!"

As their cheers rose to the sky, the group finally felt the reality.

'Shirone...'

Always a welcome face, but now it felt like seeing someone return from the dead.

"Whew."

Shirone, the last to descend to the ground, took a deep breath and smiled at everyone.

"Hello?"

As always, his simple greeting brought tears to their eyes.

"Shi..."

As Amy opened her mouth and moved towards him.

"Shirone!"

Nade, using spark magic, closed the distance in an instant and hugged Shirone.

"You idiot! What happened? You should have told us!"

"Sorry, there were circumstances."

Knowing each other so well, Nade didn't need an explanation.

"Haa, really..."

He never doubted Shirone would come.

Iruki would think, Nade would prepare, and Shirone would always solve the problem.

"I really thought I was going to die."

When the elemental bomb timer dropped below 10 seconds, his heart had pounded.

Suddenly, Nade remembered.

"I need to tell Iruki. He's in a dangerous situation every second. If we're not careful..."

"It's okay. Iruki knows too."

"Huh?"

Shirone couldn't have gone to the Holy War first, knowing about the elemental bomb.

'That's not like him.'

The mind tends to lean towards urgency.

But that too was just human logic, unable to solve two events simultaneously.

"Shirone."

At Rian's voice, Shirone turned.

"Rian."

He didn't make a fuss.

In this horrific battlefield, Rian was the only one who could momentarily forget Shirone and fight.

Perhaps out of pride, Rian smiled and pushed Amy forward.

"Ah."

Though Nade had beaten her to it, she felt a flutter of relief.

'Am I ready to meet Shirone?'

Having done everything she could, only cold reality remained.

At this point, facing everything.

'Shirone...'

He didn't seem human.

"You've become stronger, Amy."

It sounded like consolation, and Amy's first feeling was unease.

"I worked hard. Joined the Valkyrie, trained with Garcia, and..."

She listed as many things as she could.

"I fought on the battlefield, risking my life to fight alongside you. And then I reached the fire system..."

She didn't feel like she was getting closer to Shirone.

"I almost died, but..."

As Amy struggled to hold back her sorrow, Shirone spoke.

"I'm sorry."

He meant it.

"I hurt you. I shouldn't have said those things. I'm the worst boyfriend."

"What?"

Amy steadied her trembling lips.

"What's the worst? You did your best. I was weak, couldn't fight alongside you..."

"No."

Shirone shook his head.

"I was the weak one."

Those around them waited for Shirone's first true words after reaching a new level.

"I was weak, so I said those terrible things. You must have been struggling as much as I was, but I made you bear my pain too."

The Ultima system is possible because everyone has a heart.

"So..."

Imagining the wounds Amy had to bear, Shirone said,

"Let's fight together. No matter the outcome, I'll never leave your side."

Seeing Amy's shoulders tremble with emotion, their friends smiled.

"...Hoo."

Amy clenched her fists and nodded.

'I won't cry.'

Not until they ended this war together, she wouldn't shed a single tear.

Meanwhile, the heavenly army watching from outside Bashka's walls was momentarily stunned.

Especially Satiel, who felt a near-terror after the light of Agape burst forth.

'What is this?'

It was definitely a type of Hexa, but it was an unfamiliar feeling.

'Is it part of Goffin's erasure?'

It felt different because it lacked the usual discomfort.

'In the end, it's something I don't know. But also... something I do know.'

Having attempted to restore memories countless times, she could only infer one possibility.

'If it's Goffin himself.'

She hadn't experienced Agape, but if it was related to Goffin, the contradiction was resolved.

'If that light feels like Goffin...'

Satiel's gaze turned to Ikael, who still wore a cold expression.

'Why?'

If Ikael had regained her memories, she should have reacted to the light.

'Could it be she hasn't regained her memories?'

Observing Ikael intently, Satiel shook her head fiercely.

'No, it's abnormal. Even I, without restored memories, felt a mental shock.'

The lack of disturbance in her aura meant she was deliberately controlling her mind.

'She's alive.'

Ikael looked down at the scene where millions of humans had been saved and the demons had vanished.

"Are you relieved?"

At Nane's question, Ikael turned her head.

"I have no special feelings about humans..."

"Someone once said, a sound without heart is just an empty shell."

As Ikael remained silent, Shura spoke.

"How do you feel, Buddha? Many demons died, and humans survived."

Nane...

"I opened the spiritual realm."

He looked down at the rejoicing citizens who had regained their lives.

"I summoned the demons, and as a result, many humans suffered. Do you think it's cruel?"

"Am I happy they survived? Of course, I'm happy. But demons are the spirit of the era. They're not foreign objects."

Nane rubbed his nose with a sad expression.

"Since when did humans... start suffering and fearing mere demons?"

Shura couldn't answer this time either.

"Did I open hell? No, I opened an era. Even before the spiritual realm opened, someone had to bear the terrible pains that became real and engulfed everyone. How many of those rejoicing now are free from responsibility for this era's hell?"

"But humans are inherently..."

"There was a time."

Nane, who had swallowed Anke Ra's dream, knew of humanity that existed long ago.

"Humans who didn't fear the spirit of the era, truly strong humans."

The archangels nodded.

'That's right.'

In the history of heaven, demons had never held significant power.

'Gaia fought against the gods. Demons had no place before their beliefs.'

They had no fear of what was within them.

"That's why I can't yet decide on the necessity of my existence."

Nane spoke.

"I'll go and hear it myself."

The heavenly army crossed Bashka's walls and landed on the street where Shirone stood.

Separated by a single block, Yahweh of the era and Buddha of the era faced each other.

One by one, sounds disappeared, and finally, a silence without even breathing arrived.

Nade swallowed dryly.

'My heart feels like it's going to burst.'

Just seeing those lined up behind Nane was overwhelming.

'That's an archangel. Is that Ikael, the one Shirone mentioned? That one must be...'

"Don't be nervous."

Miro placed a hand on Nade's shoulder.

"You never know until you try. We're not pushovers either. Including you."

The smile of ultimate good calmed his heart.

"Ah..."

Finally, he noticed those guarding Shirone, and a shiver ran through him.

'And me.'

The strongest (self-proclaimed) electric mage, Nade.

'I'll end this fight.'

As he looked ahead with determination, Shirone stepped forward alone.

"Hey, you...!"

Shirone turned and smiled.

"I'll be back."

Only then did Nade see Nane approaching alone from the heavenly army.

Each step Yahweh and Buddha took to meet felt like an eternity.

Dante, watching seriously, chuckled.

'Brings back memories.'

In magic school, during the 2000’s battle, Shirone and Dante had walked like that.

'Neither of us ever lost.'

In their short lives, both had only experienced victory, and the result was Dante's defeat.

'That defeat...'

He was grateful for it because it freed him from the obsession with victory and allowed him to savor life.

'But you still carry it.'

As sword remnants rose around Nane, Shirone also summoned the hexagon of Hexa.

'Go.'

Dante pushed Shirone forward with his gaze.

'That's the end of the world.'

Shirone shattered Hexa into golden smoke, and Nane's sword aura clashed with it.

As they stared at each other in that state, the onlookers had the same thought.

'They look alike.'

Shirone's pure impression and Nane's tattooed appearance were nothing alike, but...

'Why does it feel like this? Are my eyes playing tricks?'

It was as if one person stood before a mirror, the left and right reversed.

"The other side of the mirror."

Miro spoke.

"Then who is the real one?"

Nane's sword aura rose like thorns, and the Miracle Stream split and clashed.

Rumble!

The countless sword remnants wrapped in light made their eyes dizzy.

"The fight began the moment they took their first step."

They were merely blocking each other's path, but neither had ever stopped moving.

'I'll break through.'

When Shirone gritted his teeth and frowned, Nane mirrored his expression exactly.

'What is necessary?'

The countless concepts, all in harmony, attacked Shirone in the form of swords.

'There is no absolute necessity.'

As the Miracle Stream wrapped around Nane's sword, all those concepts scattered like illusions.

'It's not that cold. That's what a heart is.'

As Hexa and the sword aura clashed with a thunderous noise, neither side dared to intervene.

'The same thing is colliding.'

Even though they were just facing each other, it felt as if the laws of the world were shattering.

"The world is one anyway."

Miro said.

"For the two people who have perfectly defined that world, the only difference left is perspective. Like a mirror. But the problem is, no one knows who is inside the mirror. And because both are perfect, they will never break."

Sein asked,

"Then what?"

"So, what breaks is the perspective. When the mirror shatters, they will realize it themselves."

Who will be the one to fall apart into pieces.
