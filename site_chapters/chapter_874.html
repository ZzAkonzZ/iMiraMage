Chapter 874: All That Conflict (4)

At the eastern border of the Kingdom of Gallon lies the Kingdom of Akros, stretching along the coastline.

"Our hometown isn't far."

From South Akros to the Kingdom of Kazra, it was a short distance to Tormia.

The 3-meter-tall metallic creature, Salsoo, stopped at the edge of a steep cliff.

Shirone and Rian dismounted from Salsoo and watched the numerous plumes of smoke rising beyond the horizon.

"It seems the situation in the central continent isn't good either."

During their journey from Gustav through the desert to this place, they had rescued far more survivors than expected.

But from now on, they would be entering the world's largest battlefield, actually occupied by demons.

"Amy is probably fighting somewhere over there."

Rian said.

"And Tess too."

After dismantling Salsoo and storing the "Law Killer," Shirone and Rian descended the cliff together.

To reach Tormia, they had to break through the encirclement of two hundred million demons.

No matter which path they chose, they would inevitably face battles, and that time came sooner than expected.

"Ughhh!"

The ground they thought was solid suddenly rose, revealing a massive creature made of mud.

Its face was rectangular, and its two arms were still fused with the ground.

"Roar!"

From the gaping hole of its huge mouth, numerous demons poured out like vomit.

"What the...?"

The demons, with skin cracked like a wasteland, scratched their large jaws and looked around.

Then they spotted Shirone and Rian and widened their eyes.

"Yahweh..."

Rian drew his greatsword and moved forward.

"A rear guard."

It was a moment that revealed Balkan's meticulousness, but even he hadn't set this trap specifically for the two of them.

"Let's go, Shirone."

Rian kicked off the ground, and Shirone cast a teleportation spell, flying towards the enemy faster than him.

"An ambush...!"

Before the words were finished, a loud explosion erupted, tearing apart all the demons within the radius.

"They're coming! They're coming!"

The 1st Battalion of the 2nd Legion's Mage Division was busy cleaning around the barracks since morning.

Cleaning on the battlefield usually meant maintaining minimal hygiene, but today was different.

"Hey, Corporal! Get the guys to pull out the weeds quickly. Fix the fence too! I won't tolerate any mistakes!"

"Yes, Sergeant! Leave it to us!"

Watching the soldiers' morale from a distance, Amy clicked her tongue.

"Geez, they're only this enthusiastic at times like this."

Tess, sitting next to her, laughed.

"Well, it's these morale-boosting performances that make fighting worthwhile. Honestly, this war doesn't offer much gain, does it?"

War, a manifestation of human greed turned madness, was now fought solely for survival.

"By the way, do you know the singer Maya? I heard she's from Tormia Magic School."

Amy's eyes narrowed.

"Yeah, I know her."

Welcoming a successful alumna's performance was a good thing, but personally, she had a complicated history with her.

"Hmm, your reaction is lukewarm. Was there a love triangle at school or something?"

Amy remained silent, and Tess, who had guessed correctly, was startled.

"Really? Then with Shirone... If it's a love triangle involving Amy, it must be Shirone."

"Forget it, it's all in the past. And it wasn't a love triangle! Maya had a one-sided crush on Shirone."

"Hmm."

Tess propped her chin and fell into thought.

It was straightforward, but there seemed to be a reason why Amy emphasized it.

'It must have been serious.'

Beric, who had finished cleaning the barracks, ran over.

He was a corporal who had been promoted to squad leader after being severely reprimanded for writing a teasing note about Amy in the resolution.

"Major! Loyalty! The 4th Squad has finished cleaning!"

Amy saluted back.

"Good job. Rest for about an hour, then help set up the stage for the performance."

"Yes, ma'am!"

Tess shrugged her shoulders.

"Hey, try cleaning like this when the commander visits. Maybe you'll get promoted out of admiration."

Beric scratched his head.

"Hehe, you know, Sergeant. It's a dream come true to have a morale-boosting performance come to the front lines."

Given their close proximity to the demons, Maya's decision was indeed commendable.

"But don't worry! I will never betray you. My heart belongs only to you, Major."

Amy chuckled.

"Got it. Dismissed."

She took it as a silly joke from her subordinate, but Beric was thrilled just because Amy didn't deny it.

"Yes, ma'am! Loyalty!"

Watching Beric run off excitedly, Tess turned her head playfully.

"Amy, 1 point."

They couldn't help but laugh, but Amy's face soon turned serious.

"Jokes aside, I'm worried about the demons. It's suspiciously quiet these days."

Currently, Amy's 1st Battalion was facing a detachment of 30,000 demons.

It was a large force for a detachment, but the higher-ups had ordered them to hold them off.

Tess looked over the enemy lines.

"The early warning system is solid. Even if they attack during the performance, we can respond."

"That's true."

The problem was Maya.

'I hope nothing happens.'

By noon, the temporary stage was completed, and three hours later, Maya's performance team arrived.

"They're here! They're here!"

In a civilian setting, security would be necessary, but in the strict military, all they could do was clap and cheer.

When Maya got off, the applause momentarily ceased.

"Wow..."

Her delicate appearance, filled with the nostalgia of civilian life, captivated the men's eyes.

"Hello."

As Maya waved cheerfully, genuine applause soon followed.

"She's pretty."

Tess's honest impression.

Meanwhile, Amy showed no particular emotion as she greeted Palmers.

"I'm Palmers, the representative of the performance planning company Elbor. It's an honor to be invited."

As the Valkyrie of the Holy War and the battalion commander of the magic division, Amy held a position of considerable influence even in civilian society.

"This is a battlefield. We'll make all necessary preparations, but please follow military protocols."

"Of course. All lives are precious, but Maya is the company's star. We trust you, Major."

Only then did Amy turn to Maya.

Seeing her friend's face on the battlefield made her heart swell, but she couldn't show her emotions.

"Nice to meet you, Maya. First, let's get you settled..."

"Amy!"

Before she could finish, Maya threw herself into Amy's arms, tears streaming down her face.

"I missed you! It must have been so hard. Do you know how much I've been waiting for this day?"

'You haven't changed at all.'

It was like turning back time, memories of their magic school days flashing by.

Amy smiled warmly and hugged Maya.

"Yes, you came well."

Seeing Amy's genuine self, the soldiers watching widened their eyes.

'Is that really the Major?'

"Maya! Maya!"

Under the dozens of magical lights in the sky, Maya's performance continued with fervor.

When she sang "Light Rain" and took off her coat, even the mages nearly lost their minds.

"Great song."

In an open space far from the stage, Amy and Tess stood side by side, watching the performance.

"Yeah, she sings really well."

Even if no one else did, Amy could feel Maya's sincerity in the lyrics of "Light Rain."

Suddenly, her eyes reddened.

"Amy, are you crying?"

"Huh? No, I..."

As she wiped her tears and tried to explain, a warning sound echoed through the sky.

"Emergency! Emergency! All troops, defensive positions!"

Amy's mind went cold in an instant, and she turned with a burning resolve.

"What's happening!"

"It's a demon detachment! They're currently breaking through Waterdrop Valley at top speed!"

"What? The traps?"

Numerous traps had been set up to block the shortest route.

"They're pushing through with human wave tactics."

"That's impossible. A force of only 30,000 can't pass through Waterdrop Valley."

"It might not be an attack." Tess said.

"It's a typical pattern of those being chased. Another unit might have secretly hit their rear."

It wasn't an unlikely scenario.

"Alright! All troops, fortify positions! Hold the defensive line!"

As the soldiers moved in perfect order, Maya watched blankly from the stage.

"Maya! It's an attack! Let's get to the bunker!"

Palmers, who had put a coat on her bare body, tried to pull her away, but Maya didn't move.

"Light Rain..."

From the horizon, a shower of light approached, tearing through the night's veil.

"Maya! The performance is over! Don't be stubborn, let's go..."

As Palmers urged her, a loud explosion was heard from the defensive line.

"No way..."

Maya jumped off the stage and ran towards the battlefield, leaving Palmers' cries behind.

"They're coming! Respond with Common Magic A-3!"

The cavalry blocked the entrance, and mages on the high ground equipped magic in both hands.

The massive magical lights, incomparable to those used during the performance, lit up the night like day.

"Roar!"

Seeing the number of demons flooding in from the end of the path, Amy's brow furrowed.

"What's going on?"

It was hard to believe they had passed through Waterdrop Valley, given the sheer number of demons approaching.

"Amy! Let's strike first!"

Just as Tess was about to deploy the cavalry to secure the mages' range.

"Wait! Hold your fire!"

Amy gave the order.

'Tess is right. This isn't an attack.'

The faces of the demons, magnified by compressed air, clearly showed fear.

"Run! Run away!"

As the demons who had crossed the third line of defense surged like a torrent, a chilling sound of a sword cutting through the air was heard.

"What?"

As if the scenery had been cut, the bodies of the demons were split, and through the curtain of their flesh, the silhouette of a swordsman briefly appeared.

"Is that...?"

Tess's heart pounded.

"Major! What should we do?"

As the leading demons were annihilated and the soldiers fell into confusion, Amy raised her hand.

"Hold your fire."

A barrage of light poured from the rear.

As far as she knew, there was only one mage in the world who could wield light with physical force.

"Detestable Yahweh! You... you...!"

Before the demons' curses were finished, a curtain of light exploded from all directions, collapsing the cliff.

Rumble!

The ground shook briefly and then fell silent. Through the rising smoke, Rian appeared.

"Cough! Cough! Ah, the dust."

All the soldiers stared blankly at the swordsman, who was bent over and coughing.

Suddenly, a strong wind blew, clearing the dust, and Shirone approached Rian.

"See, this is what happens when you go too fast."

"Who was it that rushed me because we didn't have time? If it weren't for my strategy, we wouldn't even be halfway... huh?"

Feeling the gazes pouring in from all directions, Rian stopped talking and looked around.

"Shirone, it looks like we've broken through."

It was clearly a human unit.

"Oh? Right. Hello. Sorry, but where exactly are we? We're trying to get to Tormia."

"Where are you...?"

Tess, whose shoulders were trembling, shouted.

"You came here without even knowing that? You idiots!"
