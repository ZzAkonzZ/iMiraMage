Chapter 1217: The Final 5 Hours (1)

Green Ocean.

The race war grew fiercer with the angels’ intervention.

“Damn it!”

While the Twelve Apostles were scattered, the one facing Uriel was the Fire Dragon, Infercus.

His Paradise Mace roared as it spun, and Uriel flowed in like the wind.

“The war is over.”

Having taken a direct hit from the archangel's charge, Infercus clutched her thick chest.

“What nonsense.”

As the battle dynamics shifted, half of Infercus's face began to distort into something monstrous.

Eventually, her upper body turned into a dragon, and she unleashed a small breath attack right into Uriel's face.

“Kuoooooo!”

Uriel, his face steaming, was kicked and sent flying dozens of meters before crashing into the ground.

“You’re really starting to piss me off.”

From the sky, the Draconic Knight Argan and the Black Dragon Abyris landed beside her.

Argan wore a troubled expression.

“You used your breath? If you do that, the Elven Forest will burn down! Have you forgotten what the Messiah said? Unless it's to protect someone, don’t break the taboo…”

“Are you kidding me? You see all those angels attacking us? This isn’t the time to worry about that. We need to win if we want to protect the elves.”

Abyris slowly stood up.

“What now?”

The female warrior with black skin—her strong, resolute eyes began to glow with an amber fire.

“So that’s how it is?”

She released her polymorph, revealing her true form as a 20-meter-long black dragon, then soared into the sky.

“Rrrrrrrrr…”

As she glared menacingly at the ground, both Apostles and angels looked up simultaneously.

“Th-that…”

It was the Apostles who were more shocked than the angels.

“That crazy…!”

The moment the Apostles hastily moved to dodge, Abyris opened her jaws and released her breath attack.

‘Shadow of the Abyss.’ A dot no larger than a claw mark appeared on the ground—and rapidly spread, consuming the forest.

The darkness had no substance.

Because of that, there was truly nothing—and every living creature on the surface experienced a perfect realm of nothingness.

They couldn’t even feel where their limbs were, rendering their ability to move: zero.

‘We’re completely immobilized.’

Abyris’s breath attack, Shadow of the Abyss, manifested the bottomless pit of the under world wherever she chose.

Its charge time: 12 hours—making it the fifth longest delay among the Twelve Apostles.

Abyris laughed.

“Kuhahaha! Taste the horror of oblivion! I am the Black King Berserk Dragon, Abyris!”

Phaeton shouted.

“Don't be ridiculous! You're just a crazy dragon! What if we had gotten caught in that too?!”

As more Apostles began to arrive from the sky, Abyris grinned, her mouth stretching wide.

“Kuku, now this is more like it. Finally feels like a real fight. What are you all waiting for? Join in.”

“But the Messiah will scold us, you know? He’s pretty strict about uncontrolled power…”

The Draconic Knight Argan raised a finger and added,

“Oh, it’s fine. Infercus said she’d take responsibility. We’re not at fault.”

“Hey! How can you just—”

Just as Infercus was about to object, the Thunder Dragon Blitz spoke.

“One’s not here.”

The Apostles turned their gazes to the abyssal darkness now covering the entirety of Green Ocean.

“Who is it, that slowpoke Apostle?” After quickly counting the remaining eleven, they couldn’t bring themselves to blame the one left behind.

“The Soul-White Dragon.”

From within that vast abyss where even one’s self could be lost, the White Dragon Asriker knelt.

She, who possessed the highest ego, was adored by all living beings.

“The Abyss, huh.”

But even that meant nothing in the realm of nothingness. She still shone with a beauty that transcended meaning.

“What a foolish thing to do…”

As an Apostle who feared falling out of favor with the Messiah, it took great courage to make such a decision.

Her gaze into the darkness grew hazy, and her body began to radiate a pure white light.

Releasing her polymorph, Asriker revealed her true form—a dragon made entirely of spiritual energy.

“Fweeeeeeee!”

Though her features were sharp like a fox’s, aside from her massive eyes, the rest was a chilling blankness.

Breath Attack — Wrath of the White Bones.

Even within the abyss, ghostly skulls emitting light screeched as they scattered in all directions.

Its charge time: only 6 hours.

Any soul pierced by Wrath of the White Bones perceives itself as being completely dead.

‘It doesn’t work on immortals, transcendents, or the inanimate, but…’

When combined with Shadow of the Abyss, it became a mass destruction ability.

As the forest—feeling as if it had already died—began to wither pitch black, angels collapsed to the ground.

“Hhhhhh…!”

They may be eternal, but the fear of death shook their very minds.

“That place is your grave.”

Asriker returned to her polymorphed form and sat demurely, savoring the abyss.

Poiné gave a smiling glance.

“Spectacular. Just… spectacular.”

The forest now looked like a massive pit.

And within that darkness, uncountable skull spirits floated aimlessly.

“So she finally did it. Asriker’s an accomplice now.”

Ignoring Infercus, who still clung to his bitterness, Poiné turned to Blitz.

“How much time do we have left?” The Thunder Dragon Blitz said nothing.

“Don’t pretend you don’t know. With Karatorsa’s disappearance, we’ve lost the core. If there’s anything that needs to be passed on, it would’ve been told to you. Omega 999. How long until the end?”

A dragon—the Apostle of Time.

If one had endured from Omega Year 1 to 999, even a rough estimate should be possible.

“About…”

Blitz finally spoke.

“Five hours.”

Silence fell.

“According to Karatorsa, today is the final day of history as decreed by God. But it’s just an estimate.”

The Light Dragon Phaeton said,

“The Messiah probably has a sense of it too—he received Omega, after all. Either way, it just means we need to protect the elves for five more hours, right? Shadow of the Abyss should hold for a while. Easy enough?”

If all Twelve Apostles broke the taboo, five hours should be manageable.

“There will be variables.”

Poiné remained wary.

“Did we expect the angels to join in? This isn’t just a battle of strength. If the elves are wiped out, we lose the war.”

“So how do we kill them? We’re blocking everything.”

“Well…”

Poiné’s eyes narrowed.

‘What sort of scheme are those little things plotting?’

Melkidu’s Middle Layer.

While Penna, not an accomplice, remained hidden, Shirone’s group wandered through the warehouses at the port.

Of course, security was tight. To open even one warehouse, they had to bribe the guards.

"Halt. Who goes there?"

When they were first caught by the patrol, Shirone quickly assessed the conditions for bypassing the guards.

"Ah, well, we got lost."

"Lost? At this hour? And you’re… users—"

"Please give me a chance to persuade you."

The guard immediately changed his tone.

"Ah, alright. My personality is timid. It costs 1 million points per persuasion attempt."

"Cheaper than expected," Shirone thought.

But once the dice duel began, the difficulty was on a completely different level from store clerks.

Of course, for Shirone, difficulty didn’t matter.

"Quantum Collapse!"

He succeeded in one shot, and the patrolman gave a wry smile.

"Not bad, kid. But watch yourself. If we meet again, I won’t let you off so easily."

It was a one-time persuasion.

After about three hours of exploration by Melkidu time, the group reached a realization.

"Most of the warehouse goods are food supplies for the royal castle or items meant for users. At this rate, we’ll be at it forever," Eden said.

"Even if the bribes are cheap, we can’t keep paying. A slow leak still soaks your clothes."

They had searched roughly twenty warehouses.

"Sure, we could sell items to earn points, but we’d have to leave the middle layer to do that. Here, they’re useless."

Iruki chimed in, "We only have enough for maybe three more attempts. I’d like to keep at least 7,000 points. We might need a middleman to persuade the security chief, even if it costs 50 million."

"Then let’s try just three more times."

Nade turned toward a small warehouse in view. "How about that one?"

"There are two guards. Not very efficient."

"At this point, it’s a gamble. Might as well try something we haven’t. Come on. I’ll do the talking."

As they walked over, two guards raised their spears and shouted fiercely.

"Stop! Owl! Give the passphrase!"

"Hey, we’d like to make a deal."

Their brazen attitude caught the guards off guard, and they exchanged confused glances before lowering their spears.

"Fine. My personality is…"

After the bribe, the guards gave them ten minutes and left the warehouse entrance.

"Got it," Nade said the moment they opened the door.

"Royal castle supplies."

They were top-tier goods meant for system administrators.

"If users had stronger items than the system could handle, it’d break everything. But more than that..."

Iruki pointed to a mid-sized, still-sealed crate that had probably just arrived tonight.

"This one looks way more interesting."

"Agreed."

Everyone gathered around the box, noticing the sticker that read “Under Censorship.”

"Not officially registered."

They pried it open and peeked inside. Various items were neatly wrapped in plastic bags.

And what they found was…

"Check it out. A 'Rapid Recovery Pass' necklace. The description’s written in weird code, though. Can’t read it."

"Let me see," said Shirone.

"It’s a hidden code. This item can bypass Melkidu’s physical barriers. Looks like it’s used when a building collapses or if there’s trouble in an underground facility. According to the description, it corrects bugs where normal groundwater can’t penetrate and boosts stats to pass through steel walls over three meters thick."

Eden added, "If it’s marked for censorship, it must be undergoing testing before being implemented into the system. In other words… even administrators don’t have access to this item yet."

Nade was already scooping them up. "How many should we take? Five?"

"Six."

"Oh right, Curtis is with us too."

Iruki gently pushed Nade aside, who was cradling a handful of gem-studded necklaces.

"Move over. Let me take a look."

What Iruki pulled out was a security registration card.

"Looks like being a guard is basically a system role. If we have this, we’re guards too. At least, as far as the system is concerned."

Everyone’s eyes sparkled as they understood.

"Aha."

And ten minutes later…

"What the hell?! I told you ten minutes—huh?"

The two guards who came storming in blinked in confusion when they saw Shirone’s group.

"Sir! No abnormalities in the area!"

Wearing a commander’s badge, Nade cleared his throat and walked toward them.

"Hrm! Good work. All quiet, I assume?"

"Yes, sir! Nothing to report!"

"Working late must be rough."

"No, sir! It is my duty and honor to serve the kingdom!"

"Haha! Hearing that makes me proud. Well then, I’ll leave it to you. Let’s go."

As Shirone’s group marched off in unison, the guards saluted with raised weapons and shouted,

"Take care, sir! Loyalty!"

Outside the warehouse, the group stifled laughter, and Nade turned back, waving the registration badge.

"Sorry."

The crate was empty.
