Chapter 717:

Labyrinth Andre-World No. 283.

Following the 13th Night, Shirone entered the Garnet Colony, a kingdom more prosperous than any human city.

The paths leading underground were tangled like a maze, and without sunlight, nothing was visible.

"Is it okay if I create some light?"

When Shirone cast a Shining spell, the 13th Night tilted its head but soon lost interest.

There were indeed creatures that could create light.

'Everything is well-organized.'

The room was vast, and most of the ants were diligently performing their assigned roles without slacking off.

"This way."

After passing the spore farm where worker ants gathered, they entered the area below the hatching chamber, and the 13th Night visibly tensed.

"From here on, it's the nobles' territory. If you don't behave carefully, you could die."

In ant society, the nobles were male ants who never worked and only waited for nuptial flights.

'There are so many.'

Numerous noble ants were spending their time in their respective chambers.

Some ants were tormenting captured slaves, while others were having lavish parties with aphids they had gathered.

The 13th Night respectfully emitted pheromones to all of them as they advanced through the cave.

Most of the nobles paid no attention, but suddenly, a noble with magnificent wings like a cloak appeared through a hole in the ceiling.

"What brings a soldier here?"

"Lord Dar-An."

Only nobles in the Garnet Colony were given names, and the names of male ants ended with "An."

"We have captured a new species. We are taking it to Queen Garnet."

The 13th Night could not lie.

"A new species, you say?"

Indeed, Shirone's appearance was unlike any species Dar-An had seen before.

"Hmm, quite an inconvenient structure."

Dar-An evaluated Shirone's appearance purely from a functional perspective.

"I will conduct an experiment. Leave this species here."

The 13th Night strongly opposed.

"This is not that kind of species. I apologize, but it uses the same abilities as I do."

As killing intent emanated from Dar-An, Shirone felt his blood freeze.

"Do you think you are special just because you survived by luck?"

Most nobles resented the 13th Night.

Firstly, unlike other soldiers distinguished by serial numbers, he had a unique name given by the queen.

He was also the only soldier ant who had survived since the Garnet Colony was first established.

"I apologize. However, the queen is very curious about what I use…"

There were no ants in the Garnet Colony that could overpower the 13th Night, but a coup was out of the question.

The nobles would marry the princesses and produce many offspring in place of the 13th Night, who could not reproduce.

However, before the nuptial flight, the queen's orders were absolute, so Dar-An swallowed his anger and pointed his antennae at Shirone.

"You must please the queen. If not, I will tear your body apart soon."

Shirone's reproductive body conveyed a sense of death to the male ants.

Dar-An roughly turned and dug back into the ceiling, disappearing. The 13th Night looked at Shirone and spoke.

"I told you, you could die. If you want to go back, this is your last chance."

In the worst-case scenario, they might have to fight tens of thousands of ants in the deepest part of the ant nest.

"It's okay. I came in prepared."

Surviving and returning would be extremely difficult, but Shirone's destination was not the surface.

'To return to my original world, I must meet the queen.'

When he first fell here, he couldn't grasp anything, but as time passed, his thoughts became clearer.

'I think I know how to break the seal of this world.'

It felt as if another version of himself from another world was giving him hints.

"This is the royal chamber."

In the deepest part of the colony, a massive cavity with thirty-two branching paths was located.

Seeing the significantly larger princess ants moving around, the 13th Night spoke.

"Soldiers cannot enter Queen Garnet's chamber. To have an audience, you need the permission of the first-ranked Princess Merot."

The 13th Night, looking around, approached a familiar princess ant and bowed.

"Princess Panot, a soldier greets you."

"The 13th Night, it's been a while since you came here. Is something happening on the surface?"

"I have brought a new species that the queen will like. Do you know where Princess Merot is currently?"

Princess Panot extended her antennae towards Shirone.

"Is this the new species?"

While the nobles saw Shirone as a competitor, the princess's genes were filled with curiosity about the new species.

Regardless of reproductive capability, obtaining better genes was the ultimate goal for insects.

"Interesting. What are you good at? How long do you live? What do you eat? How do you reproduce? Are you strong?"

As a barrage of questions came, the 13th Night provided a clear answer.

"It can use the same abilities as I do."

"What? Really?"

Princess Panot's antennae quickly examined Shirone.

"Then you must be very strong? The 13th Night is strong too. Even the Paratra army ants can't match him."

"Thank you for the compliment."

As the 13th Night emitted polite pheromones, Princess Panot turned around.

"Alright, I'll take you to my sister's chamber. She's probably drinking aphid juice with Sister Romit."

Although Panot was interested in Shirone, it was proper to wait for Merot due to the hierarchy.

Since the princesses were not violent, the 13th Night gestured for Shirone to follow.

"Princess Merot will take you to the queen. My role ends here."

In ant society, where everyone performed their assigned roles, the 13th Night left without wanting anything more.

"Just wait a bit, my sister will come."

Shirone looked around Princess Merot's chamber, fortified with a solid outer wall, and sat on a pile of soil.

After waiting for about 30 minutes, the outside became noisy, and a giant ant appeared in the cave.

'That's Princess Merot.'

Although Shirone didn't know much about ants, she smelled different from the other princesses and surprisingly fragrant.

"They say it's a new species. It seems to have come to meet Mother."

The voices of numerous princesses gathered outside the cave were transmitted through the artificial brain.

'It's quite a commotion.'

"Wait outside. I'll talk to it."

Merot, emitting strong pheromones, drove her sisters away and brought her face close to Shirone.

"So you're the new species. Nice to meet you. I'm Merot."

"Hello? I'm Shirone."

"I heard you came from the outside world. What's it like there? There must be many interesting things compared to the colony."

Unlike Panot, who was interested in Shirone's biological abilities, Merot was more curious about his world.

'She has a curious personality.'

Talking to an ant was also interesting, so Shirone told her about the human world.

"Hmm, you use tools."

Merot, touching Shirone's fingers, nodded.

"I see. Your functional segments are very detailed."

Merot, who confirmed Shirone's fingers, wrists, arms, and body with her antennae, looked up at the Shining spell.

"So this is magic."

Merot, putting her antennae into the sphere of light, emitted ecstatic pheromones.

"…It feels good. It's my first time touching light."

Her wariness rapidly disappeared, as conveyed through the Ultima system.

"You're a really unique species. I want to visit your world too."

From a human perspective, ants were part of the world, but from the opposite perspective, it was an enormous scale.

'Maybe humans are the same.'

Seeing Merot forming a bond with a new species, Shirone felt his heart warm.

'Nothing is insignificant.'

It's just that we don't try to look.

"Huh?"

Shirone quickly caught the drop of aphid juice falling from under Merot's chin.

"There's something on you. Let me clean it."

"Huh?"

As Shirone brushed the tangled hair under her chin, Merot shivered as if electrocuted.

"It was aphid juice. It's all clean now."

"…"

Merot, who hadn't moved for a while, slowly turned and left the cave.

"Where are you going? What about the queen?"

Muttering in confusion as Merot disappeared without a word, Shirone wondered.

"What's wrong with her?"

As Merot left the chamber, numerous princess ants gathered around her.

"Sister, what happened?"

The sisters, realizing the massive amount of pheromones emitted from Merot's antennae, were startled.

"Did the new species say something bad?"

"The human, no, Shirone…"

Merot, embarrassed to reveal it, whispered with a small amount of pheromones.

"He touched under my chin."

"What!"

The horrified princesses stomped their feet.

"Pervert! Worse than a dead maggot! How could he do something so barbaric!"

As the artificial brain reacted violently to the signals outside, Shirone came out of the chamber and asked.

"What's wrong? What did I do?"

The numerous princess ants flinched and quickly scattered in a radial pattern.

Pheromones were continuously emitted among them.

"Did you hear? He touched under Merot's chin."

"Under her chin? Oh my! How can such a shameless species exist? More barbaric than army ants."

Feeling the hostile atmosphere, Shirone approached a nearby princess ant and asked.

"Did I make a mistake? You have to tell me."

No ant answered, and they just whispered among themselves, criticizing Shirone.

"The worst pervert. Like a moth's shell. How could he touch… under the princess's chin? It's unbearable."

Shirone felt wronged by the barrage of criticism.

"So what exactly is under the chin?"

"Ahhh!"

The princesses emitted pheromones of shock and scattered in confusion.

"Did you hear? That human openly did it!"

In an instant, the area in front of Merot's chamber was empty, and Shirone stood there with a bewildered expression.

"…What just happened?"

Merot, who remained, sighed and approached with her head lowered.

"The place you touched is very important to us. It's connected to our lives and also serves as a sign of consent during nuptial flights. Princesses don't let anyone touch it unless they allow it, not even other princesses."

"Uh, so…"

Shirone's face turned pale as he rolled his eyes.

"I'm sorry! I really didn't know! I just saw aphid juice on your hair and wanted to clean it…"

"Enough!"

Merot hastily interrupted Shirone and patted his shoulder before turning around.

"It's okay since you didn't know. I was just surprised because it was my first time. Follow me. I'll take you to meet Mother."

"Okay. I'm sorry."

Feeling considerate of Merot, Shirone followed her to the core facility of the colony.

"Mother, there's a species I want to introduce to you. It's a human named Shirone."

Shirone looked up in awe at the massive queen ant that reached the ceiling of the ant nest.

'That ant…'

It was Garnet, the pinnacle of the colony system that controlled hundreds of thousands of ants.
