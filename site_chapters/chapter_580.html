Chapter 580:

"Ah…"

Shirone and his companions finally realized.

"Do you understand? Another hidden probability in R. If we use this, we can manipulate probabilities even more powerfully than Hershey."

"…that's what you think."

Hershey smiled.

"In the 30-year history of Scramble Royal, it's an unwritten rule never to reveal hidden rules after the match ends."

"But how did you know?"

Hershey rubbed his thumb and forefinger together.

"Money. While you're in school, you follow the unwritten rules, but do you think people who graduated decades ago care? Especially those who led underground research groups. Many of them are scum—drunks, gamblers, womanizers, drug addicts. Give them some money, and they'll spill everything."

"So… why are you telling us this now?"

Hershey glanced at Frings, who was kneeling in a corner of the forest.

"I won't forgive. I'll get revenge. I'll drive them mad with anger. I'll uncover every secret."

With his ten fingers bandaged, Frings muttered, lacking his former sharpness.

'He's completely lost it.'

Nevertheless, his madness would be needed at some point.

"We need someone to tactically manipulate probabilities. Frings is in that state, and Eden won't show up until the end. At least Kaiden joining us tomorrow is a relief."

Pisho asked, "What do I need to do?"

"Collect as many scrambles as possible and give them to me. If they create an Infinity, the next ranking will decide the winner. I'll win the card battle. The match ends in two days. From now on, it's about focus, not choice."

"…that's likely their strategy."

Iruki slammed his fist into his palm.

"In other words, they'll concentrate their cards on Hershey to end the match. The total number of cards we can collect is 144. We have to decide the outcome within that."

Dorothy said, "I won't lose to Pisho. If Sabina keeps him in check, we can collect at a similar pace."

Amy asked, "So what's our strategy against Hershey's?"

Iruki smiled and said, "We spread our cards as much as possible."

No one disagreed.

The next morning.

A visitor arrived at the Aramones Orphanage on the outskirts of the capital, Vashka.

The children, who were planting sweet potatoes in a small garden, stopped what they were doing when they saw a young man in a neat suit.

"Hello, kids."

"Who are you?"

The young man looked around without answering.

"This place hasn't changed."

A woman in her early forties, who was working in the garden with the children, slowly stood up.

"Fermi."

"How have you been, Rachel? You look as beautiful as ever."

The compliment was appreciated, but not something she expected from a friend's son.

"Why are you here?"

"Is it a problem to come home? Has it been 14 years?"

After losing his mother, Fermi had to stay at the orphanage due to family circumstances.

"Come inside."

Fermi smiled at the children and entered the orphanage.

"That man is really handsome."

"Yeah, but he's kind of… scary."

Inside, Rachel served tea.

"I thought you'd never come back."

"Was there any reason not to? It's been 14 years."

Rachel hurriedly spoke.

"Fermi, about that day…"

"It's fine. I've already forgotten. You don't need to feel guilty. I was the one who tempted you."

Rachel's face turned red.

"Here… take this."

Fermi pulled a check from his pocket.

"It's a check from the Central Bank of Tormia. Use it with confidence."

Rachel's eyes fixed on the amount.

'One billion gold.'

She sighed and shook her head.

"Fermi, this is…"

"Take it. If the Yolga Foundation hadn't gone bankrupt, you wouldn't be struggling like this."

The Yolga Foundation was a children's welfare foundation established by Fermi's mother, Yolga.

"I can't accept it."

"Why? Because it's dirty money? Don't worry. It's a legitimate transaction."

Director, this is a legitimate transaction.

The red lips of a nine-year-old boy were still vivid in her memory 14 years later.

'Don't think about it. Don't remember.'

"I'm sorry."

When Rachel looked up, Fermi was gazing at her with clear eyes.

"I didn't mean to hurt you. I'm sincere."

It was just a light kiss with a nine-year-old child.

But the emotions she felt at that time had become a lifelong trauma, tormenting Rachel.

"You… haven't changed at all."

When Yolga, who was officially declared dead after going missing, was no longer supporting the orphanage, it began to falter.

At that time, the Ardino family was in such a precarious situation that they couldn't even step into the world, and Fermi had come to the orphanage seeking refuge.

Massive debts, debt collectors who came every day to turn the orphanage upside down, and the temptations reaching out to a beautiful woman.

Rachel, who couldn't abandon the dozens of children, held on but eventually hit her limit.

Director, I'll get the money.

At first, she could only laugh at the audacity of the nine-year-old boy.

She thought he was just craving love.

He had lost his great mother, Yolga, overnight, and his new mother, Rachel, had to share her attention with dozens of children.

'Fermi was a special child.'

His unparalleled intelligence and insight, his ability to understand the world, even made the adult Rachel feel small.

Maybe that's why she was even harsher.

Because she felt she might rely on him.

I earned one gold.

When she saw the gold the nine-year-old brought, Rachel's eyes widened.

Where did you get this?

I earned it.

Tell the truth! Did you steal it? No matter how poor we are, stealing is…

I really earned it. Won't you take it?

With money that could feed all the orphans right in front of her, Rachel gritted her teeth.

Should I repay the debt, Director?

What?

The debt. Should I repay it all?

Rachel, coming out of her reverie, looked at Fermi.

His confident expression, the mysterious ease that made it seem like nothing was impossible, reminded her of an old friend.

'How can he be so much like Yolga?'

If I repay the debt, will you be my Rachel?

What are you talking about, Fermi? I'm always…

I don't need dozens of siblings.

Rachel fell silent.

I just need someone who will love only me.

It was a deal made on a whim.

She wasn't in her right mind.

Six months later, Fermi had repaid the orphanage's debt of 12 million gold.

No one knew how he had earned such a fortune.

"Fermi, I'm sorry."

Rachel said, choking up.

"I… can't be your mother."

Fermi's expression barely changed, but his eyebrows seemed to rise slightly towards his forehead.

"I know."

Fermi stood up.

"Use this money to establish a foundation. Please fulfill the dream my mother couldn't."

He left the check and headed for the door. Rachel stood up abruptly.

"Fermi."

"Don't worry."

He opened the door and turned his head slightly.

"…I won't come back."

As he walked down the hallway, unchanged from 14 years ago, he headed for the shabby exit where the children were waiting.

"Who are you, mister? Are you here to bother our director?"

"Definitely. He looks just like the debt collector who came last time!"

Fermi asked, "Do you like the director?"

"Of course! She's the mother of all of us!"

Fermi! The director is the mother of all of us!

Hearing the voice from 14 years ago, Fermi slowly extended his hand.

Even as a shadow fell over his face, the child couldn't move.

With a click, Fermi knelt down to meet the child's eyes.

"Here, buy something tasty with this."

It was a small silver coin.

Unable to hide his conflicted feelings, the child spoke in a dying voice.

"Uh, we shouldn't take this. The director will scold us."

"It's okay. It's a legitimate transaction. So take this and promise me one thing."

Placing the silver coin in the child's hand, Fermi said.

"When things get tough, you must protect Director Rachel."

The children shouted in unison, as if confident in their ability.

"Of course! When I grow up, I'll punish all the bad guys who bother the director!"

"Good."

Fermi smiled and left the orphanage.

As he looked up, a clear sky without a single cloud spread out, and Yolga's face appeared.

'Mother, is this enough?'

Fermi's face twisted grotesquely, and hatred filled his eyes.

'Now begins the revenge.'

At noon, the sixth day of Scramble Royal began.

With only 144 combinations left to decide the outcome, both teams moved quickly and cautiously.

Pisho's insect magic was the fastest at collecting scrambles, but while Sabina kept him in check, Dorothy struggled to keep the ratio at 50-50.

'Nade didn't come.'

Shirone, searching everywhere for Eden, was troubled.

'He didn't leave the battlefield. What happened?'

According to the master card status, Nade had secured a scramble.

Acting independently without telling his friends meant a mental change.

Iruki had said to leave him alone for now, but Shirone, unaware of the situation, was frustrated.

'Found it! A scramble!'

Spotting a shining scramble in the distance, Nade's thoughts were momentarily pushed aside.

At the same time, Eden emerged from the bushes.

"You don't know when to give up."

She was still in her bare state, and the master card was nowhere to be seen.

'She can't call Kang.'

Without a moment to relax, Shirone launched a Photon Cannon, but Eden shook her head.

"I told you, didn't I? As long as I am blessed by the divine, no one can harm my body."

"Is that so?"

As soon as he fired the Photon Cannon, Shirone teleported and quickly grabbed the scramble.

'Damn, she doesn't even care.'

Even as the Photon Cannon hit her, she didn't blink, which made Shirone even more frustrated.

'But if she's going to act like this, it actually makes things easier.'

Casting Elysion, Shirone began to charge Ataraxia in the sky.

If this was what invincibility looked like, she didn't care where Shirone was and just stared straight ahead.

'I can't hurt her, but…'

Shirone used Shining Chain to bind Eden's entire body and threw her into the sky.

Canceling the time division and focusing all his energy, the Ataraxia in the sky began to charge at an incredible speed.

Even while bound by chains, Eden seemed drowsy, just waiting for the next moment.

"God, I believe in your presence."

As her prayer began, Ataraxia started to spin, emitting a brilliant, multicolored light.

'Immortal Function!'

Pushing his mental strength to the limit, Shirone compressed the Photon Cannon above his head.

Eden, Ataraxia, and the Photon Cannon aligned in a straight line, perfectly aimed, and Shirone clenched his teeth.

'As long as it's in the sky, I don't have to worry about power!'

With that thought, a thick flash pierced through Ataraxia.

For the first time, Eden's eyebrows twitched at the immense amplified light, but even that was swallowed by the flash and disappeared.

'It's started!'

Everyone participating in Scramble Royal stopped what they were doing and stared with open mouths at the massive pillar of light reaching into the sky.
