Chapter 396: Hill Capture (5)

"How about my strategy? It worked perfectly, didn't it?"

Screamer licked his lips.

He never thought Aider was a good guy. Even if he was, would there be anyone in the graduating class who trusted others more than themselves?

However, strong self-assertion is often difficult for others to digest.

"Anyway, enough. Reverse position? Were you trying to ruin the game? You should have at least told us in advance."

"Hehe, sorry. It was my trump card, so I kept it hidden. Anyway, now that Maya is down, we have the advantage. I was actually planning to make them lose by disqualification."

Nade's interference had thwarted his plan.

Aider prided himself on being crazy, but Nade's face at that moment was something far beyond normal.

"Aider."

At Shirone's voice, Aider turned around.

"Huh? Shirone, this is our camp. Oh, does it not matter by the rules? Still, eavesdropping on our strategy is cowardly."

"...Why did you do it?"

Aider tilted his head. Then, as if realizing something, he snapped his fingers.

"Oh, you mean Maya? Why? You didn't come all the way here to say something boring, did you?"

"I'll ask again. Why did you do it?"

Aider's expression twisted.

Now that his true nature was revealed, he no longer pretended to be intimidated by his seniors.

"What's the problem? I was just trying to put the guard in a groggy state. Screamer hit, Nade hit. Maya is just too weak. If it were Amy..."

"Don't misunderstand. I'm not here to get angry. That's not what I want to ask."

"Really?"

Aider clasped his hands behind his head.

"I told you clearly after the survival test."

Shirone's eyes turned cold.

"Don't mess with me."

"..."

He had said he would crush anyone who tried to mess with him. In other words, Aider was now on Shirone's hit list.

"Hahaha! So, should I say, 'Oh, I understand. I won't mess with you again'?"

In the past, he might have pretended to be scared, but now, with the power of Fermi's organization behind him, he had nothing to fear.

"As long as you understand. I just wanted to confirm that."

Shirone turned and walked back to his camp.

His friends approached. With only a minute left until the start of the sixth round, they needed to quickly strategize.

"Shirone, let's defend this round. Maya needs time to recover her mental strength."

"No, we'll end it here. I'll handle it, just block Screamer's team. If I'm alone as the forward, they won't come to back him up."

Maya wouldn't be able to recover during the match. Aider's violence had inflicted too deep a wound on her, who had strengthened her omnipotence by excluding her core.

Iruki spoke.

"Shirone, don't get excited. I'm annoyed too, but winning is important. Losing will make you even angrier. You know that, right?"

"It's fine. Really, I'm okay."

Shirone smiled with his eyes, then suddenly turned with a cold expression. The terrifying chill made his friends step aside.

"This is bad. He's really angry."

At this point, no one could stop Shirone.

As expected, as soon as the sixth round started, Shirone flew straight at Aider without looking at anything else.

"Whew, this is chilling."

Aider's smiling lips trembled. It was a strange expression, a mix of fear and excitement.

The one with the strongest mental strength in the graduating class was flying at him to crush him.

"The more, the merrier."

The thought of not being able to touch the enemy he wanted to kill made his body tingle.

Aider dodged through the barrage of Photon Cannons, increasing Shirone's frustration.

"Hahaha! What now? I haven't been hit once. It'll take a lifetime to pay back for hitting Maya."

Shirone didn't respond and landed in front of the flag.

Wind Cutters formed in Aider's hands. He planned to counterattack the moment Shirone grabbed the flag.

"Ataraxia."

As a pale light formed 10 meters above the peak, drawing a huge circle, exclamations erupted from outside the battlefield. It was twice the size of the halo he had used against Dante.

Aider stared blankly at the halo. Then, realizing something, he hurriedly retreated outside its range.

'Damn. This guy is really annoying.'

There was indeed such a method. By deploying Ataraxia above the peak, even if someone grabbed the flag, they couldn't easily approach.

'In that case...'

Aider crouched low, calculating the timing to jump out. Once the magic circle was complete, there would be no opportunity.

"30 seconds."

Shirone spoke.

"30 seconds left. If you can't stop me within that time, you'll never be able to beat me."

'I know that, damn it.'

Knowing it but being unable to do anything was because he had seen the power of Shirone's magic circle with his own eyes.

If he got caught up in it, he might end up in the infirmary for the next round, regardless of winning or losing.

'He wouldn't kill me, right?'

After all, this was the 2000 system.

In a real battle, he would never make such a choice, but with the filter of simulation, it seemed like a winnable gamble.

'I'm going in!'

The fact that a magician's power drops to less than half in a time-split state also contributed to Aider's decision.

It should have been that way.

But as the Photon Cannon brushed past, his hair stood on end.

'Damn! Is it still impossible?'

In terms of single combat power, Shirone was expected to be among the top in the graduating class.

Even in a time-split state, the power of one shot was beyond Aider's capacity.

'No, it won't hit me. It can't hit me.'

The deal with Fermi had certainly paid off.

With Ghost Movement, he could graduate even without Fermi's gang's help.

"What? Even Shirone is nothing special? That's why you were just sucking your thumb while Maya got hit."

Shirone fired the Photon Cannon at maximum speed.

But Ghost Movement effortlessly pushed Aider's body aside, dodging the flash.

"Hahaha! I told you, it won't work! So from the beginning..."

At that moment, a powerful impact hit the back of his head.

'Huh...?'

Aider's leg wobbled.

The AT algorithm was broken. The fear of that fact was greater than the pain of the anti-magic.

Looking up in disbelief, Shirone's face blurred. The impact was much stronger than expected.

'It was a direct hit.'

But how?

The AT algorithm responds to all objects causing air resistance. Whether it came from behind or rose from the ground, it should have reacted.

"What... is that?"

As his focus returned, he saw the Photon Cannons floating around Shirone.

They weren't particularly large or clear. Just a sinister red dot in the center of the light.

"Laser-guided algorithm."

Dorothy muttered.

It was a high-level core comparable to the AT algorithm. The omnipotence of lasers itself was top-tier.

'That's why I...'

Dorothy's constructs also had functions similar to the AT algorithm. But the laser-guided algorithm was practically a nemesis to the automatic reflection function.

"Damn it! Damn it! Damn it!"

Aider flew around the peak, dodging the homing Photon Cannons. But no matter where he fled, he couldn't shake them off.

He wanted to peel off the skin marked by the red dot.

It was a spell with a power approaching 8,000 press per shot.

'Damn it! What kind of spell is this!'

The flight trajectory of the homing Photon Cannon, which intercepted a level 7 drone, was enough to catch up with the movements of a frontline professional magician.

Aider couldn't escape the clutches of the laser-guided algorithm and was eventually caught.

"Ugh!"

Just before colliding with the homing Photon Cannon, Aider's body wobbled, and the flash grazed past.

The relief was short-lived; true fear set in.

Eight beams of light slid endlessly over the AT algorithm like hyenas circling their prey.

"Huh? Huh?"

As his body shook involuntarily, nausea surged. But the fear of the impending impact was even greater.

"Aaaah!"

The homing Photon Cannon, coiling around his waist like a snake, exploded on the back of his head.

"Ugh!"

If not for his endurance from passing survival level 4, he would have lost consciousness.

Aider, who had crashed to the ground, lifted his head. His emotions were laid bare, showing a demonic expression.

"This damn...!"

Bang bang bang bang bang bang!

The successive impacts from the air drowned out Aider's voice.

The homing Photon Cannon showed no mercy. The unfeeling light bombarded wherever the laser guided.

"Aaaah!"

Aider clutched his head in agony. It felt like hundreds of spikes were drilling into his brain.

Realizing he couldn't win like this, he saw Shirone approaching and hurriedly knelt.

"I-I'm sorry! Brother!"

Shirone's steps halted.

"I was wrong! I won't do it again. Take the flag and end it. Please!"

"What did you do wrong?"

"Hitting Maya. Hiding my true nature and pretending otherwise. I'm originally like this. I can't help it. But I won't do it again. I'll apologize to her."

Magicians know exactly how their actions affect the outside world. Therefore, they do not show anger unless it leads to the best outcome.

"...Make sure to apologize to Maya."

Shirone walked past Aider.

'Ah, Shirone... you really are...'

Aider's eyes flickered as he watched Shirone.

If he could land a proper blow on the back of Shirone's head, if he could see Shirone's eyes filled with blood and beat him...

How thrilling would that be?

The mere thought sent shivers down his spine, almost making him lose consciousness.

The anticipated pleasure led him into the Spirit Zone. With the AT algorithm activated, the Ghost Movement spell began to envelop him.

'I can do it. At this distance, he can't counterattack!'

Shirone looked up at the sky. His burning anger quickly froze over.

The sky was cold.

With 100% concentration, he sharpened his Spirit Zone, and the core and omnipotence created a powerful synergy.

Suppressing even the slightest anger, he added it to his magical power.

That was the anger of a magician.

'Now!'

Just as Aider, eyes wide open, was about to lunge at Shirone, Screamer shouted.

"Idiot! Above! Above!"

Aider's face went blank as he looked up.

Ataraxia was complete. And above it, the laser of the Homing Photon Cannon was aimed at his forehead.

It was different in power and scale from what he had used on Dante. The synchronization rate was 50% back then, but now it was 80%.

"Damn it!"

Aider clutched his head and crouched.

The Homing Photon Cannon pierced through Ataraxia, and a massive flash shot straight down from 10 meters above.

"Aaaah!"

The massive waterfall of energy turned into anti-magic, destroying Aider's mind. His nose bled like a cannon, and tears of blood flowed.

Rumble!

Aider's body began to vibrate at a terrifying speed.

While humans are not subject to the jungle rule, the interaction between spells was clearly implemented.

The AT algorithm, caught in the shockwave, began to function, moving Aider.

The problem was... there was nowhere to escape.

"Ugh! Ugh!"

Trapped in the massive flash, Aider bounced at an incredible speed. It was as if someone was shaking his spine at will.

Crack! Crack! The sound of bones breaking echoed, shocking everyone.

The flash lasted only 1.6 seconds, but it was enough time to break every bone.

Aider, flung out of the flash, rolled down the hill. His eyes, staring at the sky, were devoid of pupils.

Shirone walked back to Aider after Ataraxia disappeared.

Even seeing Aider's limbs twisted in impossible directions, his eyes remained calm.

"Keep your promise to apologize to Maya."

If it had been Shirone who was broken, would Aider's actions have been justified?

Was it Maya's weakness, unfit for a magician? Or Aider's extreme cruelty, fitting for a magician?

Was this just part of the harsh competitive system of the graduating class?

There was no need to judge. Shirone would continue forward, and Aider's journey in the graduating class ended here.

For a magician, that was all that mattered.

The doors to the battlefield opened, and medics rushed in.
