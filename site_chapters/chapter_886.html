Chapter 886: Memory (4)

"Emergency! It's an emergency!"

Just after midnight, a special unit of demons ambushed the right flank of the Valkyrie camp.

"Move quickly! Move!"

Unlike before, when Valkyrie had to cover the entire central continent, their defensive perimeter had significantly narrowed.

Despite a greater concentration of troops than before, the situation was still unfavorable.

"Damn it! I knew this would happen!"

Tess, fully prepared, mounted her horse and led her subordinates.

"Tess."

Rian caught up to her side.

"Don't die."

In a situation where he couldn't just fight for Tess, that was all Rian could say.

"Don't worry and go. Sweep them away."

As soon as Tess finished speaking, Rian changed direction and broke away from the cavalry.

"Hoo!"

As his muscles twisted, his body transformed into a demon form, and every time he hit the ground, shards of rock burst forth.

"That's incredible."

A cavalry soldier, staring blankly at the rising dust columns, remarked.

"His stride must be over 20 meters?"

It wasn't just the length of the stride; he was moving his legs as swiftly as a horse.

"It was originally the power of a giant."

Tess, looking at the horizon filled with the army of demons, drew her sword.

"Rian will strike first, and we'll follow. Get ready."

"Yes!"

As the cavalry picked up speed, Rian had already approached right in front of the enemy.

'It feels different.'

This detachment was only a small part of the enemy, but the aura was completely different from the forces they had fought before.

'They are elites.'

What was Balkan thinking?

'It doesn't matter.'

Thinking was for the strategists, and Valkyrie certainly had brilliant minds watching over this situation.

"Grrr!"

Rian's nose bridge contorted, and terrifying strength flowed into both hands gripping the great sword.

If it had been a normal sword, the force might have crushed it.

But <Idea> withstood all that power, delivering an explosive sensation to its wielder's hands.

"Yaaah!"

With a massive arc, the great sword's shockwave tore the demons apart like cloth.

The moon hung in a fan-shaped view that had opened up.

"Hnngh!"

With a step forward after striking, Rian pulled the sword back and swung it horizontally.

Once again, the world seemed to split, and where the flash of light passed, the bodies of demons were cleaved apart.

At that moment, Tess's cavalry arrived.

"Charge! Wipe them out from the inside!"

Emboldened by Rian's initial attack, the cavalry charged with a fearsome aura, thrusting their spears forward.

"Die, filthy demons!"

Boom!

Dozens of horses soared into the air at that moment, and the cavalry finally realized.

"This...!"

It was simply that Rian was too strong; the demons they had fought until now were nothing compared to this.

'We'll be isolated like this.'

Most of the demons had already pushed the Valkyrie forces back by tens of meters.

"Tess!"

As Tess cut through the demons one by one to clear a path, she saw the cavalry being surrounded by the enemy.

"Let's retreat! If we stay like this...!"

Sensing the killing intent, Rian stopped and turned his head towards where Tess was looking.

"What are you dreaming of, you insignificant creature?"

A gray demon with the upper body of a praying mantis and insect-like legs stood there. On its left were three slender, sharp arms, and on its right, one abnormally thick arm.

"He's an officer. Everyone, be careful."

At Tess's command, the cavalry reformed their ranks.

"I am Yun-goe."

He was a brigade commander of the first legion, regarded as the strongest in the army of hell.

"And I am Dae-seok-sang."

The ground thumped, but the voice's source remained hidden in the darkness.

Shortly after, a demon whose face, large and elongated, occupied half of its body appeared.

With bulging eyes, it split its mouth to its limits and laughed.

"I'll enjoy eating you."

Simultaneously, Yun-goe moved like the wind, infiltrating the cavalry's formation.

"You first."

A giant hand flew towards Tess's face.

"Where is it?"

Natasha's eyes shifted to the left.

"Aaaaah!" Dozens of soldiers where she passed fell with broken necks.

"Where is it?"

The Valkyrie company commander gave an order.

"Capture them! Surround and attack!"

But honestly, even he didn't know what entity was felling his troops.

"It's impossible! We can't pinpoint the location... Ack!"

Before the report could be completed, a hole appeared in the soldier's chest as if something had pierced through.

"Damn...!"

What's the use of fighting if you can't see what you're fighting against?

"Commander! Aaagh!"

As if multiple enemies were present, soldiers were collapsing from completely different locations.

"Retreat! Retreat!"

As the wind whispered in the commander's ear,

"Gah!"

The scene flipped, and when he came to, he was floating upside down.

'How...?'

At least, the fact that the enemy hadn't killed him made such thoughts possible.

"Where are you?"

Holding the back of the upside-down commander's neck in her extended hand was Natasha.

As she loosened her grip, he fell headfirst but quickly flipped to raise his head.

"Gah!"

A woman without eyelids was staring straight ahead.

'It's Natasha.'

As if the situation of the battlefield didn't concern her, she didn't divert her gaze and asked.

"Where is the strongest person here right now?"

The commander realized.

'It's an assassination mission.'

The location of the Knight of Maha, the unit's strongest asset, was confidential regardless of rank.

"How would I know that?"

Natasha turned around.

"I'll let you live if you tell me."

Life was precious to everyone.

"Are you promising?"

"Yeah. It's too vast to search without help."

"Fine. I'll tell you where the strongest person in this unit is. Right here."

The commander pointed at himself.

"I am the strongest soldier! So fight me, you idiotic woman! Hahahaha!"

"Do you not want to live?"

"Shut up! My subordinates who died at your hands are watching from hell! Bring it on! Try to kill me!"

Natasha looked around.

"Were they close to you?"

"Dieeee!"

As the commander charged with his sword raised, Natasha raised her eyebrows and said,

"Sorry."

As the sword sliced through the air, Natasha's form blurred and then vanished without a trace.

"What?"

It seemed faster than thought, and only belatedly did the commander's cognitive gears start to turn.

'Combat Doll Natasha.'

The rumor that she was the strongest combat machine, devoid of blood or tears, was well known.

'How much do we really know about our enemy?'

Perhaps that was the key.

Right before Yun-goe could grasp her, Tess twisted her body, stepped forward, and stabbed with her sword.

"Heart!"

The strike hit its mark, but Yun-goe's face remained wickedly smiling.

"Heh, the thing about my heart is..."

Before he could finish his sentence, his body shook and split in half at the waist.

Rian stood between the split halves.

"What about your heart?"

"Aaaargh!"

As Rian twisted his body, Dae-seok-sang was seen swallowing a soldier whole, crunching him bit by bit with his front teeth.

"Tasty."

The still-living lower half of the soldier flailed uncontrollably.

As Tess and Rian felt a surge of anger and rushed forward, Dae-seok-sang's body suddenly swelled.

"Huh?"

Boom!

Dae-seok-sang's torso burst open, a strong gust whirled around leaving an afterimage.

"Oops."

Natasha, who had slowed down abruptly, stuck out her tongue while maintaining her fist-out pose.

"It was too dark to see." Dispatching the brigade commander with one strike was no surprise considering her speed just moments before.

"Rian, that woman..."

As Tess tried to convey something, Natasha, locking eyes with Rian, flashed a smile and waved.

"Hello?"

From her body, the incarnation of a dark-robed reaper rose and vanished along with her.

"Damn it!"

In the face of such extreme speed, Rian gave up thinking and blocked the front with his great sword.

In a split second, Natasha, having spun around seven and a half times, struck the broad side of the sword with her foot.

Before his brain could even process it, Rian's arm bent, and his chest was struck...

"Gah!"

He felt the breath being knocked out of him and was flung tens of meters away.

"Huh?"

Tess, who had been looking forward unknowingly, belatedly turned around.

Instead of Rian, Natasha stood there.

"What just happened?"

It was clear Tess was no match, but Natasha seemed uninterested.

"I blocked it."

Whether she knew or it was instinct, she had reacted to her own speed.

"Ho, really?"

Over Natasha's shoulder, Havitz's face suddenly appeared.

"Sounds fun, doesn't it, Natasha?"

Havitz's voice was audible, and his body was indeed present in that space, though no one else could perceive it.

"Shall we check again?"

Unaware of Havitz's presence, Natasha kicked off the ground and charged at Rian.

"Rian???"

As Natasha vanished right before her eyes, Tess bit her lip and prepared to chase after her.

"You shouldn't go. That human is supposed to play with Natasha."

Havitz blocked Tess's path and pressed on her solar plexus.

"Just wait. I'll go."

Tess couldn't move forward, but she felt no dissonance.

"Pretty, isn't she? Your girlfriend?"

Havitz's face came close to Tess's nose, but her mind registered nothing.

"Sir! We need to retreat!" The cavalry soldiers saw Tess pacing in place as if blocked by something.

But no one thought it strange.

"We're being pushed back! We need to leave now."

As the lieutenant grasped her shoulder, Tess, giving up on chasing Rian, turned around with a sigh.

"Alright! Follow me!"

As Havitz watched her for a moment, he drew his sword and approached the lieutenant.

"That's not going to work."

He grabbed the lieutenant's head, pulling him down, and his sword touched the back of the lieutenant's neck.

"Shhk-shhk."

As he slowly scraped the blade up and down, cutting the neck, the lieutenant continued to converse calmly.

"We need to break through at least 300 meters. First, we need to gather our forces—Shhk-shhk."

Finally, the blade came out, and the lieutenant's head fell.

"Steady! Steady!"

The killing intent flashed in Tess's eyes at the death of her subordinate, but there was no one to direct her rage at.

"These damned bastards!"

Cause and effect were mere illusions of the brain.

After all, doesn't the human brain in dreams resolve contradictions in causality as if they were normal?

"Having a pleasant dream, are you?"

Havitz casually strolled around Tess, killing soldiers as he saw fit.

Even as limbs were severed, their brains accepted the current situation as normal...

"Hmm."

Growing bored of the slaughter, Havitz wiped his sword on a corpse and turned to look at Rian.

"Should wrap this up quickly, shouldn't we?"

Tess bumped into his back.
