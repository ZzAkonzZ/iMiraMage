Chapter 1271:

Richard spoke.

"Here's how it works. As the final recorder, I transmit the information from the Apocalypse to the Mindless Dragon, Karatorsa. Karatorsa will then share that information with the Twelve Apostles, and Uorin will provide the history while Adam performs the calculations to actually enact it."

"Yes, it’ll be a very precise operation. On the other hand, you’re free. Do whatever it takes—save Amy. And if you fail, you’ll use the coordinates of the Iron Pole to try again. Endlessly."

Fermi added,

"You’ll be meeting our past selves, but you yourself must reverse time directly."

Shirone cannot remain in the present.

"The time-space you’ll be traversing is the very one we’re trying to preserve. The Iron Pole will interact with you to prevent you from slipping into another universe."

Nade spoke.

"The Iron Pole is on the surface, where we are now. It’ll be protected under Shura’s lead. And from the center of that pole, Uorin will provide you with a new history every time you return. Using future sight and History Search."

Shirone's expression turned grave.

"That means... there’s a limit."

Richard nodded.

"The Iron Pole is a concept, but Uorin is a living being. Her endurance isn’t infinite. The more you regress, the more she’ll be exposed to time waves. She’s a Gaia, so she’ll last much longer than a human, but the chances aren’t unlimited."

"Uorin..."

"Remember, Shirone. This may be your fight, but the outcome will determine the fate of all humanity. If you save Amy, we all live. If you fail, we all vanish."

Adam spoke.

"She’s the one most responsible for this situation. She has her own burden to bear. Though she’s not fully persuaded yet, she said she wants to talk to you first."

The one who split Ultima.

"I’ll meet her."

Eventually, he had to.

Maybe Uorin had sensed this moment would come, even if vaguely.

Fermi said,

"Then let’s go back up. We need to finish all preparations today. We’re running out of time."

As Jin Seoung Eum began casting the ether wave to set the coordinates, Minerva approached.

"Shirone, do you remember the promise we made?"

"A promise…?"

"When we raced to North Aymond—I won. The loser had to grant the winner one wish."

"Oh…"

"My wish. I’ll tell you now."

He didn’t know why she chose this moment, but Shirone nodded.

"Don’t save me."

"What?"

"In the history enforced by the Twelve Apostles, you’ll do everything you can to save Amy. But I know you—you won’t want anything to go wrong for anyone else in this time-space either."

It was true.

"But my history… you can use it however you want. Salvation doesn’t suit me."

The infamous arch-witch who wreaked disaster across the world with “Lawslayer” and Walking Dead.

Did she feel her service as one of the Five Stars of the Ivory Tower wasn’t enough to atone?

Or… perhaps no matter how she lived, the trauma of her childhood could never be erased.

"Okay," Shirone said.

"When the time comes, I’ll make a decision."

Minerva knew well he was not the type to give up on anyone… but still.

"Thank you."

That was enough for her.

Just before leaving the perfect sealed room of the Recorder, Shirone turned back and said,

"Richard, I’m going to defeat God."

Richard, who might one day become a god himself, smiled.

"Hey, I’m pretty cool, aren’t I?"

Shirone laughed.

Humanity lived.

It was humanity who had truly lived in this world—he firmly believed it.

As Jin Seoung Eum pulled the space around them, from beyond the threshold, Seriel asked with concern,

"Will you be alright?"

Everyone crossed through, and Jin Seoung Eum sat on the bed, supported by her.

"A bit of rest, and I’ll be fine."

Shirone nodded.

"While Seoung Eum rests, I’ll go meet Uorin. Where is she now?"

Adam said,

"I’ll take you."

"I’ll go alone. I think that’s better."

Realizing the meaning behind Shirone’s words, Adam explained where Uorin was staying.

Following the directions, Shirone arrived at the door of a secluded room.

"Uorin."

Receiving no response, he opened the door himself.

Uorin, with bandages around her calf, smiled brightly.

"You’re here, Shirone."

Leaning on Kido for support, she beamed so radiantly it was painful to look at.

"I was waiting. I have something to—"

Smack!

Her cheek turned with the slap.

Kido's eyes widened, but Uorin didn’t move from where she stood, cheek still stinging.

Shirone didn’t even know himself.

He’d tried to stay composed, but the moment he saw her smiling face, he lost control.

This was why he had wanted to avoid her.

"Haha."

She laughed heartily.

"Yeah, sure, I did some wrong. So this is how you end it? Then now—"

A tear slid down Shirone’s cheek.

"Why?"

Did I ruin everything?

Almost as if she heard the thought, Uorin's heart ached, but she forced a light tone.

"Hehe, you really hate me, huh? But what can you do? Everything depends on me now."

She didn’t want this carefully prepared moment to be ruined by heavy emotions.

"You need me, right? And of course I’m willing to help. My only condition is you say you lo—"

"I will."

Her mouth shut.

"I’ll do anything. Anything you say, I’ll do it."

Shirone pleaded.

"Just save Amy. You don’t even have to stay by my side. I’ll give you whatever you want..."

This time, even Uorin couldn’t hold it in—her eyebrows curved like a broken arc.

She barely held back her sobs.

"Say you love me."

"I love you."

It was a love that could never be fulfilled.

"Say you’ll stay with me forever! Not Amy—me, Uorin! Tell me you love me!"

"I’ll stay by your side forever."

Uorin bit her lip hard.

‘I won.’

Not wanting to show her tears, she turned her back to him, forcing her lips into a smile.

‘Shirone is mine. He said he loves me.’

She said,

"Alright."

Things could have been far more cruel, but she wouldn’t ask for anything more.

"I’ll do it. Go save Amy."

And at that moment, Kido understood.

Uorin—Lilith—the mitochondrial Eve—truly loved Shirone.

That… was love.

From the beginning of humanity until now, it had always been about this one moment, this one woman’s…

True love.

Uorin said,

"Go. It’s all settled now. Save humanity. I don’t have the right to say this, but still."

As Shirone stepped closer, she quickly turned away.

"Go."

As if it had never begun… so it wouldn’t feel like it had ended.

"Go! Just go!"

Even comforting her might have been too cruel—so Shirone left the room in silence.

He didn’t think of it as Eve’s atonement.

He simply took to heart the pure devotion of a woman who had loved him.

When the door closed, Uorin let the tears she had held back pour freely.

She sobbed uncontrollably.

Kido stepped forward.

"Hey, you okay?"

"Hug me," Uorin whispered through tears.

"I’m so lonely I could go insane. I feel like there’s a hole in my chest. Kido, please."

Kido… couldn’t do anything.

As Shirone returned to the plaza after meeting Uorin, Xing rushed up to him.

"There you are. This way."

When they entered the resistance command room, Shirano and the other executives were already gathered.

Lethe, the overseer of the Underworld, was reviewing the world map—and standing beside her was...

"Great Star."

The overseer of the planet.

"Shirone."

Unlike the time she had coldly pierced his heart with words, now she looked frightened.

"Back then... I just..."

It wasn't that she lacked courage—she simply hadn't known what to say.

"Thank you."

But Shirone understood.

"For standing with humanity. Whether it was an error or a feeling, it doesn't matter. You're fighting with us now."

If not for her decision, humanity wouldn't have even had a final chance.

Shirone turned to Xing.

"Are you okay?"

If anyone had a right to feel betrayed, it was her.

"I don’t think all my doubts have been resolved. But since you persuaded me, I’ll trust you."

Lethe spoke.

"Save the small talk for later. Let's get to the point. Yahweh, did you meet the recorder of Omega?"

Shirone nodded.

"Then this will be easier to explain. We’re moving up the launch schedule. The bats are disappearing faster than expected."

It was Satan’s will.

"Great Star and I held out as long as we could, but it's not enough. In six hours, the demons will break through. And that... will be humanity’s final war."

"When do I leave?"

"The sooner, the better. Humanity is about to hold the vote for the World Governance Council. You must depart before then. Please be ready within the next hour."

“One hour.”

It would be a journey just for him. One he would make alone.

Kido stood dazed.

"What are you doing?"

Even when Uorin asked from where she sat on the ground, he couldn't accept what was happening.

"Uorin, this is just giving up."

"It’s not."

Everything was for Shirone.

"Each round trip through the Iron Pole means enduring the time waves. Two is better than one, right?"

The taste of memory.

If Kido consumed Uorin’s blood, the durability of the timeline would double.

"Then just give me your blood."

Uorin shook her head.

"Kido, you're dying with me. And I’ve already achieved everything I wanted. So... it’s okay."

"It’s not okay!"

Kido was furious.

Even if he couldn’t love her, the Empress of Kashan should always remain a noble woman.

"I’m a monster."

She smiled faintly, a bit surprised.

"So am I."

No...

"I’m a monster too."

Kido shut his eyes tightly.

He knew, deep down. But it was the one thing he never wanted to hear.

"Ugh..."

With tears in his eyes, Kido approached and gently laid Uorin down, supporting her back.

"Vanishing."

The only kindness a goblin could offer.

"I love you, Uorin."

She would never consciously perceive it, but somehow, Uorin felt it and closed her eyes.

"...Haa..."

A faint, trembling moan.

And then Kido, with the gentlest brutality, bit into the nape of Uorin’s neck.

The taste of memory.

The universe.

The planet was scorched black.

The oceans were gone, and rivers of lava flowed like nets across the blackened land.

"Hrrmph..."

Havitz inhaled deeply.

Flames flickered back to life in his corpse-like body, and the planetary magma flared with renewed heat.

"Haah..."

With his exhale, even light dimmed.

Within those deadly breaths, even the demons trembled in fear.

"Hhrmph..."

Molten metal erupted from the rapidly superheated earth, and the demons cowered.

"Eek!"

Touched by Satan’s fire, they too would be turned to charcoal.

"Wizard."

Havitz's face twisted.

"Where are you?"

He had turned the entire planet into a Death Field, yet her location remained undetected.

'She’s either hiding in a place I can’t find...'

Or she had already burned to death.

Both possibilities infuriated him. As he inhaled again, the planet began to boil.

"Wizarddddd!"

And at that moment, the Great Court of Theraphos officially declared the victory of evil.
