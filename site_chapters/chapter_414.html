Chapter 414:

The Arun Mountains, located in the northeast of the Kingdom of Tormia.

This place, filled with a sinister aura since ancient times, is home to the sealing magic circle of Belkir, which was sealed long ago.

Just as magic existed even before it was understood, the ones who sealed the demon were mages who dealt with the law.

However, after thousands of years, the will of good has faded, and Belkir's evil has slowly crept into the gaps, beginning to erode the law.

This is the story of the resurrection of the demon Belkir.

Breaking through 20 meters of underground rock, Belkir's imposing figure emerged in a cavern within the mountains.

Standing 15 meters tall and weighing 32 tons, his massive body resembled an octopus, with decayed, dark skin.

His two arms looked human but were actually a fusion of colorful snakes.

His reptilian face was grotesque, with eyeless sockets filled with fiery red flames.

As the flames burned, Belkir's 24 tentacles writhed like waves.

"I will destroy the world! All living beings will bow beneath me!"

This was the single law that defined Belkir.

But the great star of thousands of years ago had to face the reality of time.

Human civilization was no longer so fragile as to be destroyed by the appearance of a single demon.

A sect of mages, who had long identified the locations of demons through ancient documents, sent a cooperation request to the kingdom's intelligence department.

With the king's approval, a Cage-level party was formed, and 21 mages entered the cave in the Arun Mountains.

Now, 8 hours and 37 minutes later.

Cage B Team, composed of certified 4th to 6th-level mages, had severed all of Belkir's tentacles and was launching a final offensive to ensure his complete annihilation.

"Graaaah!"

Belkir writhed through the cave, flailing his snake-like arms.

Despite possessing all kinds of regenerative abilities, the power of 21 humans was beyond what he could handle, even with his prowess from thousands of years ago.

"Insignificant humans! How dare you! Gaaah!"

Belkir's body caved in an X shape, splitting open and spewing a viscous liquid.

A fat, bald man jumped into the wound, swinging two cleavers with increasing speed.

"Is the X just decoration? Huh? Decoration? Huh? Decoration? Huh? Decoration?"

The man was currently under the effect of 28 powerful buffs.

His arms showed no signs of stopping, endlessly splitting the flesh in an X shape, digging towards the heart.

The man's face lit up as he discovered the pale purple muscle mass.

Then he bit into the demon's heart.

"Delicious! So delicious!"

He pulled the heart with his teeth, cutting the connected parts with his cleavers, causing chunks of flesh to fall off.

As he swallowed, the intense flavor of raw meat exploded in his mind like fireworks.

"Uooooh! Uoooooh!"

Carnivore Addict Benifis

Certified 5th-Level Combat Mage

Amplifies omnipotence through the ecstasy of eating prey's meat

Special Note: Runs a butcher shop

"Ugh..."

Belkir held on until the end.

Most creatures would have died from such an assault out of sheer decency.

It showed how demons were born from concepts of persistence and obsession.

"I can't... disappear like this..."

"I'm sorry. I'm really sorry."

A pale-faced woman with long white hair down to her waist hugged Belkir, tears streaming down her face.

Every time her hand stroked the demon's body, the writhing flesh that seemed to regenerate stopped moving.

"How painful it must be. How agonizing. Heehee! I'm sorry. I can't help it... Heeheehee!"

Uncontrollable laughter seeped through her sobbing voice.

The contrast between her large, tear-filled eyes and her grotesquely twisted mouth was chilling.

Saint Moriak

Certified 6th-Level Healing Mage

Specializes in large-scale healing magic with a diameter of 100 meters

Special Note: Severe mania

A demon called a demon lord in the human world would be equivalent to a 2nd-tier Mara in Heaven, but now he was just a demon on the verge of annihilation by 21 humans.

Belkir finally realized he was trapped.

The mages' abilities had sealed him off and battered him for 8 hours, leaving him no chance to endure.

As the demon struggled, Cage B Team's leader, Rose, looked for the main dealer.

As expected, he was in the corner, smoking a cigarette and slacking off.

"Hey! Aren't you coming? Do you know how much this party costs per hour? Does the association dig money out of the ground?"

"Ugh, that witch."

Tarvan stubbed out his cigarette and assessed Belkir.

He was definitely softened up, perfect for annihilation.

'Just die already, you persistent bastard.'

Rushing at Belkir, Tarvan spread his hands, emitting a high-frequency sound that pierced the eardrums.

Kaiser Blast.

All the mages who heard the sound scattered.

As Tarvan's hands extended forward, Belkir's massive body began to tremble.

"Remember this, humans! I am...!"

Boom!

With an explosion, Belkir's form vanished without a trace.

Flesh splattered everywhere but melted away in the heat.

"Mission complete."

It took exactly 8 hours and 49 minutes from Cage B Team's arrival to achieve this result.

At that moment, the communication mage, who had been dozing against the cave wall, suddenly opened his eyes.

Information was being displayed on the crystal ball before him.

It took about 400 lines of code to convey a single line of text.

"Team leader. Team leader."

When Rose turned around, the communication mage floated the crystal ball above his palm and spoke.

"The association is calling. All members of Cage B Team are to assemble."

Magic Association.

The 18th-floor corridor, where the president's office was located, was crowded with mages.

The faces of Cage B Team, scattered and waiting for their leader, were filled with discontent.

Cage B Team was composed of the best in their respective fields.

As the busiest mages in the kingdom, they naturally weren't pleased with being called in after a mission.

"What's going on? I need to get home to my kids. They better not have more work for us."

"Why is the team leader taking so long? Do you know anything, old man?"

Tarvan asked Horkin.

As the oldest member and the one in charge of orders, he might know something, but he just wore a mysterious smile.

When the president's office door opened and Rose came out, the mages swarmed her.

Her expression wasn't good, indicating it wasn't pleasant news.

"Team leader, what did the president say?"

Currently, Lupist was acting as the interim president, but the staff didn't care who held the position as long as they were paid properly.

"It's an urgent mission. It seems you all need to go out again."

No one spoke, but the cold, murderous glares made the corridor feel uneasy.

Saint Moriak asked, "Why us? There's Cage A Team too."

There was little difference in skill between Cage A and Cage B.

Even though they were the best in their fields, Tormia's defense wasn't so weak that they couldn't field 40 such talents.

Cage A Team had long worked together, while Cage B Team was assembled as needed.

This small difference could lead to feelings of inferiority.

If the association had a mission, it should be for Cage A Team, not them.

"I suggested that too. But currently, Cage A Team is temporarily out of commission."

Horkin said, "Because of Gaold."

"Yes. There's someone in Cage A Team suspected of being a Gaold loyalist. Until the investigation is complete, we're the kingdom's only Cage-level party."

Tarvan said, "But we can't do it. Didn't you negotiate the contract, team leader? And Roche here has a daughter who can't even walk yet. His wife will kill him."

"That's right, team leader. Please exclude me. I'd rather pay the penalty..."

Rose's eyes narrowed.

"Don't be mistaken. This isn't a guild. The payment is just a reward from the association. The kingdom has issued a first-class mobilization order. If you refuse, you'll lose your Red Line certification and face legal consequences. You know the current atmosphere in the kingdom. It's best to comply."

The mages fell silent.

They couldn't imagine their positions outside the Red Line, so they had no choice but to follow orders.

Rose's expression softened slightly.

"As your team leader, I apologize. But the association has promised ample rewards for this mission. Triple the usual payment and reputation points equivalent to a promotion."

The mention of reputation points changed the mages' expressions.

From the 6th level, the points required for promotion were immense, so succeeding in this mission would be a significant boost.

Horkin asked, "So, what's our mission?"

Rose hesitated, indicating the difficulty.

Even for a Cage-level team, it seemed too challenging.

"The target is... a human."

"A human? Which human?"

Rose's eyes gleamed sharply.

"Former Tormia Magic Association President, Mikael Gaold."

Heaven's outskirts, Purgatory. The Forest of the Profane.

As Shirone emerged from the tunnel of flowing light, a vast sky unfolded before him.

Beyond the sea of trees, the concentric circles of Heaven were visible, with drones swarming like gnats in certain areas.

Having experienced this before, Shirone took a deep breath and prepared to land.

As expected, his direction shifted sharply, and he fell vertically.

As expected, the direction shifted sharply, and he fell vertically.

The sea disappeared, and the forest rushed past.

He kept his eyes open until the ground was right in front of him, then twisted his body.

"Phew."

His landing was much more stable than before, but no one congratulated Shirone.

The nine who had passed through Goffin's Gate ahead of him were scattered, each looking in different directions, lost in thought.

Sein spoke.

"This is the Forest of the Profane. It's the habitat of the Dokgakgui."

"Dokgakgui, huh? A quiet place. Shall we go?"

A quiet place.

Shirone recalled the time he was surrounded by the Dokgakgui.

Thanks to that, he met Kanya and Lena and entered Heaven, but it was far from a safe place.

'Well, for these people, it might be considered quiet...'

As they walked, Sein explained.

Purgatory is divided into three main areas: the habitat of heretics, the mainland, and the outskirts of the community.

The outskirts of the community, in particular, refer to areas inhabited by non-citizens, who are specifically called border dwellers.

Sein's current destination was the mainland, the only neutral zone where heretics and border dwellers gathered, making it essential to visit for information.

As they passed through the middle of the forest, the ground suddenly began to shake as if an earthquake had struck.

The group, on high alert, waited for any new developments. When the shaking subsided, they exchanged glances and moved forward through the forest.

"My goodness..."

In the plains below the mountain, hundreds of giants were marching in formation.

Giants created through the elixir of myths.

At the time, the mere existence of giants was shocking, but according to Sein, they were currently heading to Jotunheim, the land of giants.

There, they would regain their identity and return to Heaven to uphold the law. If they fell into corruption, they would be banished to Muspel, the land of fire.

"Giants are a force to be reckoned with. But they're just monsters. It's best to ignore them and move on."

The faces of the ten people hidden in the bushes disappeared silently.
