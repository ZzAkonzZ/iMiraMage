Chapter 776:

Upon arriving in Zion, Meirei shot up from her bed, covered one ear, and dropped to her knees.

“Chief Justice.”

A transmission had come from Theraphos, who had been stalling with a filibuster to delay the punishment of Shirone for breaking the system.

—The situation has been reversed.

“I heard.”

Nane’s sermon, carried through the law of the mind via quantum transmission, had imprinted itself onto every lifeform in the universe.

Because it functioned through the mind, most lifeforms couldn’t even comprehend that they’d received it. But those who understood what it meant to break the system—realized it immediately.

Now it’s possible to break the system.

—Anke Ra—more precisely, Nane, who had consumed Ra’s dream—has voluntarily broken the third clause of the Absolute Three Principles.

“What will happen to Shirone?”

—He is permitted to break the system. So is Gaold. Of course, the judgment of Theraphos will continue. However...

The Chief Justice chose his words carefully.

—Next time, the ruling won’t be on a single planet, but on the survival or collapse of the entire system.

The end of the world was approaching.

“What should I do?”

—Aid Zion and stop the evil. Miro will guide you. We must prevent the day Theraphos destroys the universe.

The transmission ended.

“Nane survived.”

Gaold’s group had searched Jungcheon-dong for two days but found no trace of Nane or Shura.

Only while flying toward the South Pole on Kaidra and hearing Nane’s sermon did they realize he was still alive.

“But there’s no longer a Buddha.”

Miro recognized it as an opportunity.

“If we assume Shura is alive, it won’t be easy to find Nane. But now we can end the game. Once we return to Zion, we’ll launch a full-scale assault against evil.”

By sealing the altars and eradicating the Extremes, the radiant flower of good would once again bloom in the world.

From the pilot seat of Kaidra, Zulu spoke.

“Yahweh will handle the sealing of the altars, but even beyond that, the demons are strong. It won’t be an easy fight.”

Everyone except Miro turned to look at Gaold.

Yes, the demons are strong. But if it’s Gaold…

A mage who had transcended "ten-thousand", the one who defeated the Buddha—he could very well take on the 2 billion-strong army of Hell.

The only problem…

Sein glanced back and forth between Gaold and Miro, who hadn’t exchanged a single word—or even a glance—since the battle ended.

Of course she’s confused.

When Miro had carried the burden of everything, things had actually been simpler.

But now the dynamic has changed.

Miro needed Gaold, and Gaold still loved Miro.

You could say it didn’t matter.

But you still have feelings for him, don’t you?

She didn’t want to admit it, but the only person who remained in Miro’s mind… was Gaold.

Miro could feel Sein’s gaze, but she ignored it, flipping her hair back like nothing was wrong.

This is driving me crazy.

People always think it’s someone else’s story when they push you into it.

Sure, if anyone could be called “a man” to her, it was Gaold—but what she felt was no more than a speck of affection.

So tiny, in fact, that she couldn’t tell if it was love, friendship, or pity.

Maybe I should’ve just dated him back then and ended it.

Back at Alpheas Magic School…

During Stage 6 of the survival test, “Extreme Heat,” maybe she could’ve accepted his confession.

Would that have changed the outcome?

Maybe.

And now it’s all too complicated.

If Gaold had already won her love and still reached this point, Miro could’ve accepted it.

But if she accepts him now…

What happens to Gaold?

The man who endured all this clinging only to his love for Miro—if he finally gets what he wants, his entire system of self may collapse.

He could die. Or at the very least…

The mental strength that once allowed him to overcome even the Buddha would dull, and in the worst-case scenario, he’d become a hollow shell.

Does he know?

He probably does.

That’s why he’s avoiding me too.

After crawling through Hell twice and finally surpassing her, Gaold still had nothing.

And yet he still chooses to fight.

For a moment, Miro looked up at the sky, thinking from Gaold’s perspective.

What does the end of the world matter to someone like him?

He was a man who could die at any moment and feel no regret, as long as he spent the rest of his life with the woman he loved.

That’s fine. If that’s what you want, I’ll do it.

Hadn’t he suffered enough?

You’ve already given so much for the world. If you fall apart, I’ll take care of you. And if you die, I’ll bury myself beside you.

But Gaold’s choice was still to stay by her side—and fight for her.

And now you say you can’t love me anymore?

Maybe this was why she had avoided him for so long. But the time had come to decide.

“Sigh…”

Miro’s sigh made Arius twitch his shoulder.

Poor man…

“Shirone! Time to move out! Get ready!”

When Minerva stormed into Shirone’s home, he was already fully prepared.

“Huh? That was fast.”

He didn’t need to wait for Nane’s sermon to understand—he had already heard the situation from Great Star.

Gaold is back.

He’d surpassed ten-thousand, defeated the Buddha, and with Nane’s permission to break the system, there were no longer any masters over the world.

The shock Gaold sent through the Law had triggered a chain reaction—one that ultimately released Shirone from the judgment of Theraphos.

“This isn’t the end, is it?”

Minerva nodded.

“The Buddha disappearing is just the beginning. In a world with no righteous one, anything becomes permissible. Chaos is coming.”

“The Extremes will grow stronger.”

Minerva handed him a document titled Lawslayer.

“We have to stop them. Let’s head to Kashan. Even with Lawslayer, we’ll need the Holy War’s support.”

Shirone was already planning to visit Uorin, so he followed without complaint.

Just as they tried to leave for the Corona Kingdom via the magic circle on the first floor of the Ivory Tower, four familiar figures approached.

The card players?

Minerva smirked.

“Well, look who it is. Fossils who hadn’t left the White Inn in 14 years show up here?”

The bald old man, Agaya, said:

“The game’s over. We came to tell you the result.”

“Oh? So who’s the winner?”

Gudio, with his red beard, answered:

“The one who wanted to keep the game going.”

Compassion.

It may have been just a card game, but heading into such a brutal war, it wasn’t the worst outcome to hear.

“So what now?”

The skeletal man, Ness, asked.

“Reality’s changed. I figured we’d rerun the simulation. This time, the one who wants to end the game—”

“There’s a more interesting game waiting. Want to join?”

To target the Extremes accurately with the laws of Lawslayer, they needed the card players.

Maice, the man with slit-like eyes, asked:

“There’s a gamble more fun than this?”

“No matter how fun simulations are… it’s time we showed what we can do in a real game.”

“What kind of game?”

“One where we bankrupt the one trying to take everything.”

“……”

Maice glanced at his companions to gauge their reactions, but Minerva already knew what their answer would be.

Because these people…

Were truly mad gamblers.

“You’ve got enough stakes to play, right?”

All four of them answered at once.

“Yes.”

The Imperial City of Aganos, Capital of Kashan.

Having received a summons around midnight, Uorin had been pacing non-stop through the vast imperial palace since dawn.

“Shirone is coming!”

With Minerva, no less.

“Why her, of all people?”

Uorin’s eyes, already heightened with excitement, narrowed.

“It doesn’t matter. I’ve waited too long for this…”

It wasn’t the kind of waiting a human lifespan could withstand.

“I can finally meet him. Shirone!”

Even though it was only a shift in memory, to her—the one who held those memories—it felt no different from actual time passing.

“Gando!”

The door opened immediately.

“You called for me.”

“Preparations are proceeding without issue, right?”

“If you hadn’t summoned me thirty seconds ago, I would be checking right now.”

Gando knew that as long as nothing went wrong in welcoming Shirone, today, at least, Uorin would not be angry.

“Thirty seconds? Isn’t that a little stingy? I’m pretty sure it’s been at least five minutes.”

“My steps aren’t that slow.”

True to his nickname “Thunderclap,” Gando had sprinted across a 200-meter corridor in the blink of an eye.

“Anyway, good.”

Everything was good.

“I need to do my makeup. Call the coordinator. Keep it light. But make the lips a little redder…”

Gando looked worried.

“Your Majesty, you are already the strongest and most beautiful person in the world. I don’t think you need anything more.”

“What does my beauty matter? Shirone needs to find me appealing. Just get the stylist.”

“Your Majesty…”

Gando steeled himself.

“Are you truly okay with this? I’ve never seen you so shaken.”

As Uorin changed her earrings, she spoke softly.

“…I know.”

“You do know, don’t you? Shirone is a tool necessary for your daughter. If you fall in love with him…”

So be it. Let me die here.

“…Will you become a daughter again? And love your father?”

The words escaped his lips—and now Gando closed his eyes, ready for her to command his execution.

“…Gando.”

She called him by name.

“Yes, you’re right. Teraje isn’t an individual. It’s history. But you know as well as I do—Shirone has returned with that history.”

Gando didn’t want to hear it.

“Kill me. Please, Your Majesty, you must kill me!”

If the colossal history called Teraje was defined solely by one human—Uorin—then the future of Kashan could not be guaranteed.

“At first, I just wanted to survive. But ever since Shirone occupied the beginning of my memory, I’ve upheld history while waiting only for this day.”

Gando shouted.

“Kill me! Just kill me already—!”

“My son.”

At that moment, Gando’s mind went blank as if struck by a hammer.

Son.

A word he believed had no place in this world—he couldn’t even register how it made him feel.

“There is no Uorin without Kashan. It’s true I’m shaken. And I know I’ll be condemned by the world. But still…”

Uorin turned to him with a sorrowful smile.

“Can’t you just cheer for your mother?”

His strength gave out. Hot tears silently streamed down his cheeks.

“……”

Bowing his head and wiping his eyes, Gando steadied his trembling voice and said:

“They’ll arrive in the evening. Please try to rest, even just a little. I’ll make sure everything is prepared without issue.”

“…Thank you.”

The door closed with a soft, dignified sound.

Left alone in the room, Uorin placed a hand on her vanity and gazed into the mirror.

“…I know.”

She, who had risen to the throne of empress by endlessly analyzing the past based on the base scenario—Teraje.

“That’s how I made it this far.”

Among a playthrough longer than history itself, the fleeting moments when she could enjoy an unexpected event were brief.

A single time should be enough for a life.

The same events, the same lines—she had repeated that dull playthrough as many times as she had reset, all for one reason…

The only mystery I have left.

The most radiant event in Teraje’s life.
