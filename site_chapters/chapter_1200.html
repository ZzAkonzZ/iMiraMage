Chapter 1200: Living (1)

Apocalypse.

Dashing through the towering buildings of a future city where the Law had triumphed, Shirone bit his lip.

‘There’s nowhere to hide.’

The city, devoid of even a single speck of dust, was inhabited only by androids known as Zets.

A mechanical voice echoed.

"Form is emptiness. Emptiness is form."

Soaring into the sky to evade a barrage of projectiles, Shirone widened his eyes as he looked down.

‘Damn it.’

The vast city below was crawling with Zets, as if someone had painted his surroundings silver.

‘Where are they all coming from?’

A Zet hovering through electromagnetic force formed a mudra, and instantly, all the others synchronized, chanting the same Sanskrit phrase.

"Destroy the law. Do not dwell in illusion."

From the ground, an uncountable number of projectiles formed a net of fire, closing in on Shirone.

"Tch!"

A deafening explosion erupted as he defended himself with Miracle Stream, landing on the rooftop of a building.

‘Why?’

Every time he explored the city, the same question gnawed at him.

‘What am I missing?’

A future where Law triumphed meant that the Fairies had won the Great Race War.

‘That’s why the Twelve Apostles were sent to the Elves. But even so, the outcome hasn’t changed…’

There had to be an unknown variable, something outside of Shirone’s perception—something from the Outer World.

"Eliminate delusions. Eliminate delusions."

The Zets clung to the walls like spiders, rapidly scaling the buildings. Shirone’s hair stood on end.

‘Even if I win here, nothing will change. How long will this…’

Then, a mechanical voice spoke.

"O lost soul."

One of the Zets was seated in the lotus position.

‘When did it get there?’

As Sanskrit characters flashed across its facial screen, the Zets in the sky suddenly dispersed, as if they had lost their sight.

Shirone, staring in disbelief, finally lowered his gaze and saw that an entrance had opened in the ground.

"If you seek the world beyond, follow me."

Feeling the vibrations of the Zets moving through the walls, Shirone leapt into the dark opening.

Meanwhile, at Gaold and Miro’s wedding, countless eyes began invading through the ceiling.

Even as the collapse of the 1.5th floor echoed around her, Miro couldn’t take her eyes off Gaold.

‘Gaold.’

The boundary between dream and reality was crumbling, overlapping in a way that made nothing feel real anymore.

One thing was certain—Gaold’s choice was something that had transcended desire itself.

Gaold groaned.

"It hurts."

The pain was back.

With no limit to his pain receptors, he could perceive this world more vividly than anyone else. The strength of his magic, derived from those senses, was undeniably the greatest among humanity. But the clearer his perception, the greater his suffering.

Was this a curse or a blessing?

Gaold simply called it life.

"Life is suffering."

As Ymir’s all-seeing eyes infiltrated more than half the room, reality overtook the dream.

"It’s neither a curse nor a blessing."

For someone who couldn’t die from shock, the only way to escape pain was to end his own life.

‘Why wouldn’t I want to die?’

He could hang himself right now, slit his wrists, or throw himself from a great height.

If only he could be struck by lightning, if only a boulder could roll in and crush him, if only someone could cut his throat while he slept.

‘Why can’t I die?’

What else was left in his remaining life? Gaold chuckled to himself.

‘Because I’m human.’

Because tomorrow, maybe—just maybe—something good could happen.

Even though he knew it never would.

Even though decades of waiting had changed nothing.

But still, tomorrow—the tomorrow that had yet to come…

Molten tears streamed down his face.

‘Who knows?’

For the first time in his life—or even if it was the last time—perhaps he could have a day where he could truly laugh without holding back.

"Just once."

He needed nothing else. He would give up his flesh, his blood—everything.

"Just once."

He wished to know what it felt like to be happy.

"Miro."

My Idea.

"There is no tomorrow here."

Suffering makes philosophers of men, and philosophy leads to the path of Extreme Good.

"You are not here."

Perfect Order.

If one simply closed their eyes, the correct path was something everyone already knew—an endless practice of perfect righteousness.

"If you can smile in that world..."

Gaold clenched his teeth, and Infernal Frostflame erupted.

"Then will I finally be able to smile too?"

Would there be rest even for a wandering soul in hell?

"Haaahhh!"

Would tomorrow come for me as well, Miro?

As Gaold’s face regressed in age, water began to flood in from all directions.

Luber let out a low murmur.

"So this is how it ends?"

The human heart was like a sunflower—if it glimpsed even a faint light of hope, it would inevitably turn toward it.

"Humans cannot leave even the smallest doubt unchecked. That is why the existence of the Outer World is dangerous. The world must remain closed."

Monga shouted.

"Luber! What should we do? At this rate, the 1.5th floor will be completely submerged!"

It was too late to stop it now.

"Protect the Five Star."

"But…!"

Hadn't they retreated to the 1.5th floor because they couldn’t withstand the pressure of Deep Layer 2?

"It’s the only way now. Hold out as long as you can. At the very least, the ones who must survive until the end are the Five Star."

Even if it was just 0.1% of a chance—hope had to be preserved.

"Understood."

Realizing Luber’s resolve, Monga swiftly fled the wedding hall.

"My god…"

From the sky, a towering tidal wave loomed over the city, its height immeasurable as it surged forward.

"Five Star!"

Monga, having reached home in the shortest possible time, embraced the still-sleeping Shirone.

"The water pressure isn’t the problem… The impact alone will—"

At that moment, an astronomical volume of water crashed into the city, and along with the remnants of the 1.5th floor, Gaold’s group was dragged into the abyss.

Kangnan’s eyes widened.

"Ah."

Everything became clear.

"We suppressed Leviathan in Deep Layer 2. Then, all of a sudden, the water level rose, and…"

This was the result.

"It was a dream… all of it."

The nearly twenty years spent in the 1.5th floor had become nothing more than a fleeting memory upon waking.

"Gaold refused to compromise."

Now that Kangnan was also back in reality, she had no reason to blame him, but the situation had not changed from before.

"What is he planning to do? Is he really trying to die?"

The pressure they were experiencing was part of Ymir’s consciousness. If they could not exert an even stronger force, they would be completely wiped out.

Gaold glared into the darkness of the deep sea.

"I can do it."

He had already overpowered the Tamo organization on the 1.5th floor.

"Ymir cannot feel anything."

He was simply too powerful.

"His frustration over the lack of sensation pushes him into battle. His threshold must be as unfathomable as the depth of this sea."

If they failed to strike the seabed hard enough, Ymir wouldn’t even notice Gaold and the others were there.

"Air Press."

The moment he activated the spell, an excruciating pain coursed through his body, but there was no room for hesitation.

He had already abandoned the comfort of a happy illusion.

"There’s no turning back now."

Gaold’s eyes rolled back as the power of Air Press surged beyond limits.

It was a contest between a human who had reached the pinnacle of sensation and a giant who existed in absolute numbness.

The size of Air Press was enormous, yet to Ymir, it was no more than a pinprick.

The high-speed bombardment of compressed air began to pierce through the water pressure, drilling downward.

"Damn it! It’s still not enough?"

The depth was almost unbearable, but a will that had transcended desire refused to break.

"Khrrrrr!"

How long had he been holding on? At this point, it wouldn’t have been surprising if the others had already suffocated.

"It’s too far. I can’t reach it."

Gaold.

A voice he had never once forgotten, not even in dreams, echoed in his mind.

"Miro."

As if something had shoved him forward, his consciousness pierced through the limits of pain, bringing a fresh wave of agony crashing down on him.

Blood from his eyes mixed into the ocean, his limbs convulsing.

Then—

A faint resistance.

He had touched it.

The impact struck Ymir’s nerves.

An overwhelming heat followed, and the entire sea evaporated. Gaold’s scream finally became audible.

"Aaaargh!"

Kneeling on the seafloor, clutching his body, he could no longer even recall what pain was supposed to feel like.

"Khrrrrr!"

The reason he could endure even in hell—because there was still a tomorrow.

While Luber and Monga checked on Shirone’s condition, Sein, Kangnan, and Arius rushed over.

"Are you alright? Stay with us!"

The moment Kangnan saw Gaold’s face, she hesitated.

An overwhelming sense of dread washed over her, as though she were reaching out to touch a person whose skin had been flayed.

"Step aside."

Miro approached, and before anyone could stop her, she wrapped her arms around Gaold’s neck, pulling him up.

"Aaaargh!"

Kangnan’s eyes blazed with fury.

"Are you insane? Do you even realize what state he’s in?"

Ignoring her, Miro tightened her hold on Gaold’s neck, using her other hand to ruffle his hair.

"You did well."

Still trembling from the pain, Gaold slowly lifted his head.

Miro grinned.

"No matter what anyone says, there’s nothing better than being held by someone in the flesh, right?"

"Hrrrrr!"

His bloodstained eyes burned with fury.

"Yeah… she’s always been like this."

How could he have forgotten?

"A complete lunatic. A total maniac. An absolute—"

As his consciousness began to fade, Gaold closed his eyes and smiled.

"Yeah."

Ever since their school days, she had always made his heart race.

Gaold’s head slumped forward, and Miro, watching him for a moment, gently laid him down.

"Either way, we made it. This must be Ymir’s primal psyche—the first layer of the deep consciousness."

Miro stomped the ground beneath them.

"Hmm?"

Unsatisfied, she stomped it again, harder.

"Tch! Just break already."

"It’s pointless."

Arius, who had lived as a dog in the dream world, approached on two feet.

"Gaold forced his way through the deep sea’s numbness to transmit a stimulus. But this ground is just as solid. Kicking it won’t do anything."

"Really? Then should I just smash it?"

As Miro activated the Thousand-Hand Avalokitesvara, Kangnan realized that Miro was more than a little angry.

"The suffering Gaold endured… she wants to return at least a fraction of it."

If that was the case, then Kangnan had no choice but to accept reality.

Arius continued.

"That’s possible. The numbness has already been broken, so if we keep digging, we’ll reach the core. But I don’t think it’ll be necessary."

He pointed to the horizon, where a submarine volcano loomed.

"When Gaold’s Air Press reached the ground, it triggered an eruption. The heat must have evaporated the ocean."

"That’s possible?"

"The mental world is built on symbols and metaphors. Put another way, this means Ymir’s desire burns incredibly hot and strong."

The group fell silent.

Kangnan chuckled dryly, unsettled by the sheer magnitude of Ymir’s buried emotions.

"How much has he been holding back? This is terrifying in its own way."

"Haha! Don’t worry. Giants aren’t capable of reproduction. Though I’m sure Gaold will be thrilled. And as for Shirone…"

Luber approached, carrying Shirone on his back.

"He’s still asleep. That must mean his Transcendent Dream is still feeding him information."

Miro nodded.

"We can’t wait forever. Time flows faster here than in reality."

She lifted Gaold onto her back.

"Let’s go. To the final gate, where Ymir’s avatar awaits."

Sein stepped forward.

"I’ll carry Gaold."

"It’s fine."

Walking toward the volcano, Miro glanced back.

"I need something to say to him later."

Sein took a moment to reflect on her words before nodding.

"… Alright."

And so, they moved forward toward tomorrow.
