Chapter 418:

Shirone's group headed towards the command headquarters barracks where the battalion commander was, passing through the battlefield where bullets were flying.

Even though it was called the command headquarters, it was right in front of the battlefield, so they had to shout to be heard.

Only after entering the barracks did the noise subside somewhat.

The company commander, who had already finished his report, was standing at ease in a corner, and Kanya accompanied Shirone's group to facilitate smooth communication.

The battalion commander did not take his eyes off the strategic map even after Shirone's group entered.

"I've heard the story."

He was a cold-looking man with white hair slicked back, and his thick mustache made him look stubborn.

"Are you the Nephilim?"

Shirone did not deny it.

Regardless of the truth, it seemed more effective to use the rebels this way.

The battalion commander moved on to the next question as if he wasn't interested.

"I'll ask directly. Why did you come back?"

Shirone didn't understand the question.

"You are like a myth to the rebels. But myths are most beautiful when they remain myths. Some of the soldiers think of you as an irresponsible deserter. Why did you, who had already left the battlefield, come back here?"

Shirone instinctively knew that the battalion commander was one of those who thought of him as a deserter.

"I have no grand reason. As before, I am just fighting with conviction as a single magician."

"Conviction..."

The battalion commander pulled back the tent flap and looked at the battlefield.

"The light you brought to Sector 73 is now the sun for 70,000 rebels. I don't want to extinguish that light. If you came here just for your conviction, I'd rather not have met you."

Shirone realized that the battalion commander would not move.

What was in his cold eyes was not determination, but a sense of mission driven by the belief that he had chosen the wrong path from the beginning.

"Even a small conviction can have the same purpose. I want to speak directly with the commander. Please let me go to the rebel headquarters."

"I refuse."

The battalion commander's eyes were filled with undisguised hostility.

"Fine, I'll be honest. The war is already lost. No, it was on the verge of defeat. If it weren't for the sudden halt in Heaven's offensive, the war would already be over. The gap was that overwhelming. Many soldiers lost their lives. The only card we have left is negotiation with Heaven. If you plan to recklessly ignite the fire again and run away, the light of Sector 73 will only be a hindrance."

"But the war is still ongoing. And Heaven is currently not moving. This could be an opportunity. If we can accurately grasp the situation, we have a good chance."

"Hey, light of Sector 73."

The battalion commander leaned in.

"Don't evaluate the Mecha tribe with the mindset of the Nor. It was the Nor who started the rebellion in the first place. But now? Only our Mecha tribe is dying. The reason Heaven stopped the battle? Who can find that out?"

"I can find out."

The battalion commander fell silent. His teeth ground together inside his closed mouth.

"Listen well, kid. Do you think I don't know what you're trying to do? No, actually, I don't know. But I can already see that whatever you do, you plan to use us. People call you a myth, but to me, you're just a seed of disaster. A myth with no ability, who barely defeated a fairy chief. Do you know how fiercely we fought while you ran away? What do you plan to change now?"

"I will meet Ikael."

"Pfft..."

The battalion commander let out a breath.

"Puhahaha! Puhahaha!"

It was truly laughable.

The Archangel Ikael. Meeting the highest authority of Heaven's law?

If it were that easy, tens of thousands of citizens wouldn't have died.

"Now I know for sure. You're not a myth. You're a butcher who led countless citizens to their deaths with your silver tongue. Company commander! Arrest this guy! Don't let the soldiers know."

Just as the company commander was reluctantly about to follow the order, the tent flap opened and the adjutant rushed in.

"Commander! Emergency! Giants have broken through the rearmost defense line and are attacking."

"What? Why? Which idiot lured the giants here? What's the reason?"

"It's not confirmed. Since Guroi Team 3 lost contact, the defense line has been continuously breached..."

The battalion commander slammed the tactical table.

"Damn it. Why?"

It's rare for variables to arise in giant elimination operations. The reactions of giants, who have no identity, are always the same.

But now the rearmost defense line was breached.

He didn't know where the law had gone wrong, but living the same day as yesterday, he could only think of one thing.

The battalion commander grabbed Shirone by the collar.

"See? You're the root of all this. Because of you, Guroi was destroyed, and now there's a tactical hole. Are you planning to annihilate our battalion too?"

Kanya shouted with all her might.

"Commander! That's an unfounded accusation! Shirone is..."

"Shut up! Does a pilot who couldn't even protect his own equipment have anything to say in this situation?"

The adjutant urged.

"Commander! Give us orders! If we leave it like this, we won't last 30 minutes and we'll be annihilated!"

The battalion commander put on the piper hanging on the wall. After pocketing the arc, he grabbed the signa and exd and glared at Shirone as he left the barracks.

"Leave here quickly. I don't want to see you."

Kanya shouted.

"Commander! They can also be of help!"

"Didn't you hear me? Shirone is the main culprit who changed the giants' law! Our job now is to fight the enemy, not hide behind a deserter like him!"

As the battalion commander left, Kanya had no choice but to follow.

She couldn't just watch her comrades die as a soldier.

"Shirone, I'm sorry."

She gave a sad smile as she left the barracks.

"But I'm so glad to have met you."

Shirone knew it was her way of saying goodbye.

But Shirone still hadn't given up hope.

'The change in the law. They said the law could change with the manifestation of will. Maybe really...'

Kangnam spoke.

"The situation is worse than expected. Can we really move the rebels like this?"

Sein spoke.

"There are still many cards to play. Tagis too. The problem is the headquarters. From what I heard about the military community, it seems they are hiding their location, so it's likely we won't get information from the mainland either. We have to do it now."

Zulu spoke.

"I think the change in the giants' law is important. It doesn't make sense that giants, whose only law is to go to Jotunheim, are acting collectively. Can anyone provide an answer to this?"

No one could.

In Heaven, where the Akashic Records materialize, the law is an artificial system, but in the world where Shirone's group lives, it's just the principle of the universe.

Just as a butterfly's wings can cause a storm, it's impossible to pinpoint where the law changed because all minor variables are involved.

Gaold proposed his own method.

"Let's wipe out the giants first. If we save lives, they'll let us meet the commander or whoever."

Shirone raised his hand.

"Leave that to me."

"No. It's too dangerous. You have a special mission. Don't think your life is entirely yours."

"No. I think it's a matter of the law."

Sein asked.

"Do you have any guesses?"

"Yes. But think of it as an experiment rather than a guess. There's something I must confirm in Heaven."

"Attack everyone! Never retreat!"

Seven giants grabbed a Guroi and slammed it to the ground, then began stomping on it.

The cockpit was destroyed, and the terrified pilot screamed. But even that was cut off a second later.

"Damn it! Aaaah!"

The battalion commander, who arrived late, fired the arc repeatedly. It was indiscriminate shooting without any aim.

Even so, the giant was hit.

Even so, the fact that it was unharmed made the battalion commander feel miserable.

"Damn it! Damn it!"

From the beginning, they were beings that humans couldn't handle.

What did the elixir of life matter? What did it matter that their lifespan was limited?

Humans lost the path to immortality for defying the gods. And now they were facing extinction.

"Aaaah!"

When the battalion commander struck the giant's leg with the signa, the giant, feeling the pain slowly, turned its head.

Seeing the dilated pupils sent chills down his spine.

"Exterminate... the heretic."

His legs trembled, and he couldn't suppress the groan escaping between his teeth.

"Hic. Hic."

As the giant approached, the battalion commander's face turned into a crying expression.

He had witnessed many deaths, but his own death was incomparable to any of them.

"Exterminate... the heretic."

"No, I'm not a heretic..."

When the giant's fist came down, the battalion commander screamed silently and closed his eyes tightly.

Grrrr...

Hearing a tremendous low growl, he slowly opened his eyes to see a beast he had never seen before standing like a black wall.

Hearing a tremendous low growl, he slowly opened his eyes to see a beast he had never seen before standing like a black wall.

Its body was sleek like a leopard with soft, black fur, six legs, a flat face, and fangs over a meter long. The ground was cracked where it had crouched to receive the giant's punch.

"What, what is this?"

"What do you think? It's a Tier 3 monster, a Cougar."

Gaold's group had arrived unnoticed.

Zulu stepped forward and snapped her fingers, and the Cougar turned around with a cute sound that didn't match its size.

"Go play."

Roar!

As the Cougar leaped off the ground, the view opened up as if a black curtain had been pulled back.

Every time the beast's claws swung, the giant's body was sliced apart.

In terms of physical ability alone, the Cougar was considered the strongest of Tier 3 monsters.

The battalion commander, who had been staring at the battlefield in a daze, slowly turned his head.

"Who are you people?"

Gaold grinned.

"Humans. Just like you."

"Lena! Lena!"

"Sister!"

Kanya hugged Lena, who was fighting among the giants, and leaped away.

When the giant's foot stomped down where they had been, their bodies bounced as if they had fallen on a rubber mat.

Lena, terrified by the unfamiliar law, groped Kanya, who was unarmed.

"Sister! Are you hurt? Where's Guroi?"

Before Kanya could speak, tears welled up in her eyes.

Her poor little sister. It was sad to send her off without experiencing anything good in life.

Sensing something, Lena smiled.

"Sister, I'm okay."

Kanya rubbed her cheek against Lena's.

Her smart sister must have known. The bodies of their comrades were all around them.

She had already decided on her last words.

She wanted to say this to her sister, whom she had waited for as much as she had.

"Lena, Shirone..."

"Brother Shirone?"

When Lena looked up at the sky and spoke, Kanya followed her gaze, her eyes shaking with shock.

Shirone was flying in the sky with golden wings.

'Is this Heaven's war?'

The view of the battlefield from above was horrific.

Giants massacring humans looked no different from humans massacring ants.

'But I can end this. If this works...'

Shirone cast Shining Impact. The brightness, comparable to sunlight, blinded everyone.

The effect was even greater in the dim time of day.

"Argh!"

"Damn it! What the hell!"

After a moment, people regained their sight.

The giants, terrified of the sudden blindness, became even more ferocious and started roaring.

Then something flashed in the sky, and a brilliant rainbow-colored light spread across the night sky.

As the rainbow-colored spots shimmered on the plain where the darkness was approaching, both giants and humans looked up.

"That, that is..."

Shirone, who had completed a giant Ataraxia above his head, flapped his radiant wings and looked down at everyone.

The Mecha tribe was stunned.

Shirone's current appearance was exactly like the being that the citizens had feared.

"Ugh. Ugh."

Hundreds of giants knelt, shaking the ground, and began to cry with their mouths open like fools.

Giants were also beings of the law.

Even in a state of lost identity, Ikael's magic circle exerted a powerful presence from within their instincts.

"Law... Adjuster..."

Thinking that kneeling wasn't enough, the giants pressed their foreheads to the ground and lowered their bodies as much as possible.

Finally, the humans' figures were revealed.

But they, too, only glanced at each other briefly before looking back up at the sky.

"Nephilim..."

A word that had been forgotten in their minds slipped out of the heretics' mouths.

"Sister, Brother Shirone is here."

Lena, in Kanya's arms, burst into tears.

Even if someone called him a deserter, even if they said they regretted being deceived by him and defying the gods, for her, who had never doubted Shirone, this was a miracle.

"Yes, Lena. Shirone... really is the descendant of an angel."

With wings more splendid than an angel's, and a brilliant halo, the symbol of an archangel, above his head, Shirone had returned.

From the light of Sector 73, to become the light of Heaven.

"Nephilim. Nephilim."

"Law Adjuster..."

Everyone knelt at Shirone's feet.
