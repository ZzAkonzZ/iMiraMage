Chapter 817: The World They Live In (1)

"The usage is simple. Press the button, and the metal rod generates heat. This heat vaporizes the blood."

As Xenia held the Blood Q, she pressed the button with her thumb, causing a tube to rise from the end.

"You inhale through this."

She put the tube in her mouth and inhaled, causing a few strands of her silver hair to stand up and her eyes to sharpen.

"So, you're consuming blood."

No matter how it's done, the fact remains the same.

"Yes. I am also half-vampire."

A cool menthol scent mixed with the smoke from her mouth.

Grain explained,

"Drinking blood is somewhat repulsive, so it's designed to be inhaled as vapor. It also has the advantage of quick absorption, as it goes directly through the capillaries."

"Where do you get the blood?"

"From humans. Through non-legal means."

He didn't use the word "illegal."

"I won't make excuses like 'we had no choice.' But when Xenia inhales Blood Q, her sensitivity increases more than a hundredfold. She can track all vampires within a 2-kilometer radius."

As Xenia inhaled the Blood Q again, her hair stood even more on end, and a few strands trembled.

"Seven vampires 700 meters northwest. Three vampires 900 meters south. There are also some to the east."

Fowler gritted his teeth.

"It's a real bat haven."

"The number has been increasing recently. We've already identified four Almas. Do you know what this means?"

Rian answered,

"A Lord."

"Yes. One of the three vampire Lords is in Rodenin. In other words, we have a chance to end this dreadful war."

To exterminate the vampires multiplying through injections, they must eliminate the root.

Grain said,

"Fight with us. In return, we'll help uncover your secrets."

"Don't worry. I've already sworn to take responsibility for the deaths I've caused."

The Knight of Maha was a name that appeared on the candidate list for the Great Swordmaster among enthusiasts.

The Genocide team brightened at gaining a powerful ally, but Xenia was an exception.

"Let's talk."

As Xenia went upstairs, Grain said,

"As the team leader, she has something to say. Just remember this: she's not a vampire."

He added,

"And she's not human either."

Xenia is a Silver Born.

"I understand."

The Blood Q, which vaporizes and inhales repulsive blood, was a tool that showed her true nature.

Leaving the group in the medical room, Rian followed Xenia up to the cabin.

The door was open, and she was looking at the moon with her sword, mixed with Obscura B, drawn.

"What do you want to say?"

Xenia turned around.

"Why do you fight demons? Is it hatred? Revenge? Or is there another reason?"

It was a fitting question for a half-demon.

"To protect the one I love."

Xenia fell into thought.

"And you? Why do you fight vampires? If you must hate someone, isn't it humans?"

Humans are the most exclusive species.

"You're not wrong. Both my parents were half-demons. I know human hostility..."

Xenia sneered.

"Are humans even worth hating? You know, even half-demons can't match us. They're just prey. It wasn't humans who killed my parents; it was vampires."

The moonlight was cold.

"I lived a normal life. Drinking human blood with my parents, sometimes looking at the moon..."

Xenia asked,

"Does calling it normal sound strange?"

"No. Humans are the same. They live normally while preying on countless species."

"But they brought us tragedy."

"Purebloods?"

"They call us hybrids. They don't know love. They can't even reproduce. They're viruses."

Her sword, the Dark Blade, trembled.

"To purebloods, humans are food, and hybrids are bugs they can't even eat. My parents were captured..."

Xenia tore her collar, revealing a lightning-shaped scar on her chest.

"I was experimented on too. Even inhaling Blood Q doesn't remove this scar. It's not my skin tissue. They messed with my insides. Do you know that feeling?"

Rian thought he might understand.

"All Genocide members lost someone precious to vampires. Fowler lost his wife, Grain his daughter..."

Xenia looked at Rian.

"What did you lose? You fight to protect someone precious? Then you're underestimating this world. You're also unique. If they get their hands on you, you'll end up like me. You won't endure it."

Xenia didn't distrust Rian but all selfish humans.

"No matter how hard it is, if you can comfort yourself, you're not ready to fight. To me, any saying or verse in the world is just disgusting."

It was the world of vampire hunters.

"I wanted to become stronger."

Rian spoke for the first time.

"I have someone I really want to protect. But that person is much stronger than me."

Is Shirone still fighting the world?

"I think about how to become stronger. But when that question arises, I wonder what strength is. There are many answers, but none resonate."

Xenia listened silently.

"So I just decided to walk. It felt like I was getting closer with each step, so I just walked."

He had cut down ten thousand demons that way.

"From the northern end of the land, heading home... and I arrived here. Am I stronger now? Can I protect the one I love?"

It was a simple, almost foolish method for someone considered a candidate for the Great Swordmaster.

"I don't know yet. But I'm still walking straight. Maybe I'll know when I get home."

"What if you still don't know what strength is then?"

"I'll walk again." He spoke of an endless journey too easily.

"Strength, as you know, isn't about swordsmanship, increasing muscle, reading books, or quoting famous swordsmen..."

It was the only truth he realized while crossing the desert.

"It's not that complicated. It feels complicated because you don't understand it. If I can walk the whole world, if I can break through anything in front of me..."

Rian drew his greatsword.

"It means I can go anywhere."

Xenia, floating backward, lowered her Dark Blade diagonally.

"At that point, there's only one place I need to go."

Shirone.

"Return to my lord."

With inertia-defying movements, Rian charged at Xenia.

"Taha!"

As Xenia spun to avoid the attack, she swung her rapier at Rian's nape.

'What?'

As if his body had reversed, Rian was already turning, raising his greatsword.

With a clear metallic sound, the Dark Blade soared high into the sky.

Xenia, watching the dark sword's path reflected in the moonlight, raised her head in a daze.

"Your situation, my situation, it's not really complicated. If you break through, everything becomes simple."

It becomes complicated when you lack the courage to break through.

"I don't intend to stop. So you shouldn't stop either."

Catching the falling Dark Blade, Rian reversed the blade and extended the handle.

"If I ever try to stop, you can cut my neck with this sword."

Xenia, who had been staring at the extended handle for a long time, snatched back the Dark Blade.

"Hmph, not bad. But remember, I don't trust humans or vampires. I only trust the people I believe in."

Rian also felt lighter, knowing he couldn't soothe her wounds every night.

"Let's go. We need to wipe out the vampires."

Returning to the underground facility of the cabin, the Genocide team was having an early breakfast.

"Welcome back. Did the talk go well?"

When Xenia threw the Dark Blade, it stuck to a wide magnet on the wall with a clang.

"We agreed to fight together."

Though she left room for doubt, the fact that Rian had convinced Xenia made him look different.

"Come and sit. Eating is important if we're going to fight."

As they laid out a meal of boiled meat and fresh vegetables, the meeting continued.

"Even with Blood Q, we can't pinpoint the Lord's location."

Facet said,

"To find out, we need to capture an Almas. But the blood bank is closed, right?"

Rian said,

"Maybe a vampire has infiltrated the royal palace. Almas can withstand sunlight. I know someone in the security team, so I'll ask."

He needed to meet Benoff anyway.

"Will you be okay? It's already morning."

"A day will be fine."

Xenia stretched and yawned.

"It won't be just a day from now on. You should quickly adapt to the time difference."

For vampire hunters, morning was sleeping time.

"Alright."

As he stepped outside the cabin, sunlight poured through the branches.

'Is Irene safe?'

The memories of the previous night were dark, like a dream.

"Lord, I have brought the blood."

At the vampire hideout deep underground in Rodenin, Almas Masker arrived.

With Tempest, who had been in charge of the blood bank, gone, he had to roam all night to meet the quota.

An old man, who seemed incapable of aging further, turned his gaze from a chair engraved with grotesque bat carvings.

It was Vampire Lord Benedict.

"Ugh!"

Just meeting his eyes sent a chill down Masker's spine because Benedict had created him.

"You're too late. He is very angry."

"I'm sorry! The hunters interfered..."

"I don't want to hear excuses."

As his voice echoed in the brick dome, all the Almas kneeling nearby bowed their heads.

"An immortal being swayed by mere humans. It's a disgrace to vampires."

Masker had something to say.

"But the opponent was a Silver Born. And among the humans I met was the Knight of Maha."

"The Knight of Maha?"

"You may not know, my Lord, but he is classified as Class One among humans."

"Class One."

Vampires dominate the night of humans, but there are exceptionally strong individuals among humans.

Class One is the highest classification among vampires, and even one Almas would struggle against them.

"Form a unit for me. I'll take other Almas and wipe them out."

Benedict shook his head.

"There is something more important right now. Awakening the remaining two Lords from their slumber."

It had been two thousand years since all three Lords appeared in the world simultaneously.

"For now, don't worry about the hunters and focus on gathering more blood."

As Masker was about to bow his head, a cold voice came from behind.

"If it's the Knight of Maha, that's different. He defeated my comrade, Paknyeo."

Benedict stood up abruptly.

"How could you come to such a humble place..."

The origin of vampires and the third-ranked member of the Ten Lords, Faust, appeared with a beautiful smile.

"Greetings, Jinma!"

As dozens of vampires knelt, he looked up at the ceiling and murmured,

"He will be our final piece of the puzzle."
