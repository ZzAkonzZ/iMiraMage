Chapter 827: Anger (3)

"O-Ogent..."

Not just Damian—everyone in the River Clan stood pale and speechless.

Ogent had killed someone.

"So what?"

Did they really think something like this could never happen?

"It’s not like I didn’t do it because I couldn’t."

He had held back, believing that someday they would understand. "Heh..."

But life doesn’t work that way.

"He killed someone."

Blood-red tears streamed from Ogent’s eyes.

"Huuu... huuuhhh..."

He hadn’t wanted to cross the line into the irreversible. But now, there was no reason to hold back.

Whether it was good or evil.

"Aaaaaghhh!"

It didn’t matter anymore.

"My son’s killer!"

The moment Leard’s father charged at him, Ogent’s body vanished like smoke.

Before the River Clan could even swing their weapons, heads flew in all directions, blood erupting into the air.

"Wh-what...!"

Terrified, no one dared to approach. Ogent stood there, breathing heavily.

"Haa... haa..."

And then the thought came. If someone asked him if he really couldn’t endure it—if they asked if he truly had to go this far—

"I can endure it."

He didn’t want to hide behind excuses.

"I can endure it. I am enduring it!"

But what Ogent realized was—

"Why? Why should I be the one to endure it?"

No one had fought for Smile.

"I could’ve just lived. As long as Smile was happy, it didn’t matter how miserable my life was."

As Ogent’s rage poured out of his body, the trees of the forest began to wither and die.

"O-Ogent..."

One by one, people dropped to their knees under the crushing force of his killing intent. Damian looked up, drenched in cold sweat.

"Do you plan to kill everyone?"

"No."

Even if he slaughtered the entire world, it wouldn’t make this fury go away.

"I'm going to take Smile back."

It wasn’t anyone’s fault.

Something had just gone wrong. But he no longer cared for tired, philosophical reflection.

As Ogent turned toward the direction where Heaven lay, Damian cried out.

"No! If you go to Heaven, you’ll die!"

'Yeah, dying is scary.'

It really was.

'No one wants to die because they want to. Everyone wants to live. They're desperate to live.'

But Ogent had no more reason to ask why.

"I’m done. I’m done with this filthy world."

Truthfully, he had never wanted to be strong. He had never wanted to be the greatest swordsman.

It just felt like maybe—just maybe—there might be happiness somewhere along that path.

"Wait for me, Smile."

But when he finally arrived, no matter how hard he looked, there was no happiness to be found.

Just a mirage. It’s not dreams of happiness that drive people forward.

"I’ll save you. No matter what."

What drives living beings—is rage.

A furnace embedded deep in the heart from the moment of birth.

"Grrrraaahhh!"

As the Law of Mach activated, the ground beneath Ogent’s feet split open with a sharp crack.

"RAAAAGH!"

Screaming, Ogent sprinted toward Heaven.

He no longer knew how his limbs were moving, or what thoughts were forming in his mind.

Every part of him was melting into rage.

'Smile. Smile.'

What ran through the world now was neither beast nor man—just something grotesque and monstrous.

'Smile. Smile.'

And yet, it was closer to purity than anything else.

"Huff! Huff!"

By the sixth hour of running, Ogent’s body had deteriorated beyond what could be called human.

"Hkk! Hkk!"

The pain made him cry.

'Why do I even need to live?'

He couldn’t run anymore, but he had to. He wanted to give up, but he couldn’t.

'I’m so angry.'

Who ever said life was beautiful?

'What lunatic said that?'

Two kilometers ahead stood the great wall of Heaven. Ogent dropped to his knees.

He didn’t know how he’d made it this far with every bone in his body broken.

Only that he felt… farther away from something.

'I’m slipping away... from being human.'

Every finger gripping his sword was bent backward, but it didn’t feel strange.

As if his body had always been this way.

"What does form even matter?"

Why did he have to be human?

"Heh... heheheh..."

Even the last traces of reason melted in the furnace of his rage. Ogent was going mad.

"It’s fine."

As long as it let him save Smile—nothing else mattered.

"Here I go."

Again, Ogent’s body surged forward, and the Law of Mach came sweeping in from the distant horizon.

His fuel was rage.

'Cut them down.'

Following the violent current of that fury, Ogent’s muscles began twisting into unnatural shapes.

'I don’t know what I’ll become...'

His mind was now so pure that not a single stray thought could enter.

"RAAAAAHHH!"

Idea.

"Smile!"

A sword came down in a vertical slash, and at that moment, Heaven’s iron wall exploded with a thunderous roar.

With the memories of Nike and Ogent fused within him, Rian arrived at the mansion of a vampire named Miguel.

‘It’s underground.’

The moment he accepted information that wasn’t his own, his body moved on its own.

Rian smashed through the mansion door and twisted his waist sharply, slamming his greatsword into the ground.

The shockwave pushed surrounding buildings away, and a massive crater formed beneath his feet.

"Rrghhh!"

Unable to control the force, Rian flipped the greatsword and stabbed it into the earth.

It was a technique of sword energy—Seismic Slash.

As the blade sank to the hilt, the ground began to tremble.

Rumble rumble rumble!

Seismic waves rippled outward, splitting the land. The supports of the underground space collapsed, and Rian’s body plunged endlessly into the depths.

"What was that?"

The Umbrella Man, who had been speaking with Faust, turned around.

Screams from the Besica and Almas ranks who had been guarding the hideout echoed through the corridor.

"I’ll go check it out."

"No."

Faust stopped him.

"Everyone go. Bring him to me."

Just as Faust had predicted—if Rian was truly of Ogent’s blood—the Umbrella Man had no chance of victory.

"Everyone, you say?"

Two Lords, Agnes and Benedict, moved in unison toward the exit.

It was humiliating, but due to the principles of Injection, they couldn’t defy the True Demon’s order.

"Tch. Against a mere human?"

Benedict dissolved into black smoke first, with Agnes following close behind.

Not even a minute after teleporting, they saw Rian rushing forward, sweeping through vampires as he came.

‘Incredible speed.’

His greatsword was easily the size of a person, yet he swung it with such speed that it was invisible.

‘So this is why the True Demon sent us.’

Faust, ranked third among the Ten Lords, had reached the peak of what any creature could achieve in terms of activity and force.

And now, Rian was clearly approaching that same peak.

"Flank him!"

The Umbrella Man couldn’t even react before the two Lords split into twin streams of black shadow.

"Urgh!"

By the time he realized what was happening, Rian’s greatsword had already cleaved him clean in half.

There wasn’t even time to activate his half-spirit, half-demon body.

At the moment the Umbrella Man died, Benedict and Agnes appeared behind Rian.

"Die!"

The blood spilled across the ground rose into thousands of crimson spikes and shot toward him.

Dozens of them embedded into his torso, transforming into saw blades that tore his body apart.

"Khrrghhh..."

They thought it ended too easily—until Rian, body wrecked, stood back up.

‘How far can one fall?’

If the body is destroyed, and even the mind that accepts that body is broken, then what remains at the end?

"Stubborn, for a human!"

With every strike from the Lords, Rian’s flesh flew, and his bones broke in unnatural ways.

‘Why won’t I die?’

Tracing back the lineage of countless creatures that existed before humans—

‘Form is just an illusion.’

What remains in the end isn’t matter.

‘Concept. Schema. Information.’

Hearing Smile’s voice echo in his mind, Rian’s eyes snapped open.

‘I see now, Ogent.’

To cut something down, there’s no need to remain human.

‘If you truly desire to cut—’

Cut.

With a deep hum, Rian’s arms ignited and disintegrated into the air.

Only the greatsword remained—defying impossible friction and slicing the world in half.

"Graaaaah!"

The instant the Lords were struck by the blade, the air temperature soared and began to burn their bodies.

The walls of the underground passage boiled like oil, bubbling wildly.

"Aaaaagh!"

Gas, unable to compress any further, erupted outward in a massive explosion.

BOOOOOM!

The explosion from underground blasted through the surface, leveling the surrounding terrain.

"Haaah... haaah..."

Rian had lost both arms from the shoulders down, but from a distance, no one could tell.

The greatsword still stood upright in front of him, as if gripped in both hands.

‘This is the destination.’

The ultimate peak a living creature could reach.

For the intent to cut to perfectly align with the result—of something being cut—

‘The law must be twisted entirely.’

To reach the Law of Mach, what’s required is—

‘What Ogent realized—’

The first concept that birthed the living world.

‘We were infinitely free.’

We were once one.

As his arms began to regenerate, Rian looked up at the sky beyond the blasted ceiling.

"Now I understand."

He realized why, in their final battle, Ymir had hurled the greatsword out of Andre’s labyrinth.

—"It’s not a blade you can wield just by copying it half-heartedly."

When you swing something from the level of pure concept, physical matter has no meaning.

If it had been an ordinary sword, it would have evaporated from the heat before it even struck the enemy.

But this sword—

‘What kind of man was he?’

If you held in your hands a blade that could never be destroyed, the destructive power you could unleash would exceed human comprehension.

"That which cannot be destroyed... is the strongest."

Ogent’s fury—the massive furnace he carried within him—had taken root inside Rian’s heart.

"So you’ve finally reached it."

From within the blasted underground chamber, where a 400-meter radius ceiling had been completely obliterated, Faust emerged.

"......"

Rian, having absorbed all of Ogent’s memories, turned to face him with a calm expression.

"R-Rian..."

Even though everything else had been wiped away by a single slash, the barrier containing Xenia and the Hunters remained untouched.

‘He blocked it?’

A slash executed at the level of concept was something no living being should be able to withstand.

Which meant that Faust must have reached the same level.

"We think a lot about ourselves," Faust said as he approached.

"How long can we live? How fast can we become? How strong can we grow? We live a year longer, push forward one second faster, lift one gram more—just to find out."

He pointed his index finger at Rian.

"You are the end. The exact point where mind and body converge. That is the limit of a living creature."

Rian gripped the greatsword with his regenerated arms.

"Limit."

Faust nodded.

"Your lack of confusion means you’ve seen it too. Yeah, I’ve seen it. With my own eyes. That’s why we seek the same thing."

Killing intent began to rise from Faust’s body.

"The one who wins here—transcends the System."

Breaking the System.

A human who surpasses the hierarchy of life itself.

"Ogent."

Their time now flowed beyond eons, all the way to the ancient Heaven.
