Chapter 497:

"R…run…"

Kanya muttered unconsciously.

"Run away."

Babel did not respond.

Due to the priority of the Shirone algorithm, it was an order she could not follow.

But to Kanya, the algorithm no longer mattered.

In Babel's form, protecting her and Lena, she saw the face of the most precious person she had lost a year ago.

"Mom…"

Babel was not her mother.

Being a machine, she could not feel pain, and having no life, she had no fear of death.

It was entirely different from her mother, who had willingly accepted death for her children without showing her pain.

"Run away! I don't want your help!"

But why were tears flowing?

Even now, the memory of her father's death was vividly etched in her mind, so why did her heart ache so much?

"Babel protects Kanya and Lena."

The vibrations from her breaking body mixed noise into her voice device.

"You're our enemy! You killed our dad! You killed him! You're not my mom!"

"Babel will pay the price when the war is over."

"No! No, you're not!"

Kanya hugged Babel and cried.

The thought that someone was still protecting her was so unfair and frustrating, and she regretted not realizing it sooner.

As Girshin's attacks grew stronger, Babel's body shook more violently.

The red panel in Babel's eyes flickered, and machine fragments, like human flesh, sprayed from her back.

She was dying.

Kanya could feel Babel's condition.

"What will I do if you leave like this? I'll kill you! I'll kill you in the most painful way!"

Machines don't die.

They just get dismantled.

Babel decided to convey that information but erased the sentence just before output.

"I do not feel pain. But…"

Kanya, covered in tears and snot, looked up.

For the first time, a clear voice, free of noise, came from Babel's cold face.

"Perhaps this is what pain feels like."

"Ugh, ugh."

The overwhelming sadness crushed Kanya's heart.

"I'm sorry I couldn't protect you until the end."

As Babel's red panel burned brightly, Kanya shook her head like a child and screamed.

"No! Don't do it! That's an order! Follow my command! Don't do it!"

Babel, receiving an impossible order, searched for a response but found no choice that would stop her tears.

Perhaps it was a delusion, but Kanya thought she saw a slightly troubled smile on Babel's face.

Self-destruct mode activated. Map weapon Babel.

Having endured destruction with less than a 0.1% chance of success, Babel pushed her output to the maximum.

"Uoooooo!"

As Girshin aimed his final strike at Babel's shattered back, a flash shot from the glass sphere of the map weapon.

"Damn it…!"

The glass sphere shattered, and the electron cannon scattered wildly.

The flash scorched the entire rear radius, leaving nothing behind.

Target elimination failed.

Receiving the final signal on the central device, Babel tried every method to reactivate but could do nothing in her shattered state.

'Kanya…'

As the short name was input into the computation unit, all devices powered down, and eternal darkness fell.

"No! Get up! You can't be dead! Get up again!"

Kanya hugged Babel's remaining torso and wept. She missed the machine she had hated the most in the world.

Babel's electron cannon scorched a 2-kilometer radius.

Even Girshin, the greatest, couldn't guarantee survival against such speed, power, and range.

But it was only a hypothesis, and his expression was grim as he stood on a plateau 3 kilometers from the battlefield.

When Babel's cannon glowed, a hundred glass panels around him flashed, transporting him through space.

Signal.

Every giant knew the ability of Ikael's three-sided Mara, Ashur.

'Ashur. Ymir's rival.'

Girshin glared at Ashur with disdain.

Though their rivalry for the top warrior in heaven was in the past, Ashur remained a thorn in his side, as Ymir was the greatest in his eyes.

"I won't thank you. I could have dodged that ambush easily."

Even as the second-ranked giant, he had no pride to uphold against the archangel's three-sided Mara, but Ashur didn't care.

If such pride mattered, he wouldn't have evacuated Girshin in the first place.

"Don't underestimate Babel's computation. It wasn't guaranteed. You know that."

"Hmph, whatever. I'd rather die than run. It's not a giant's way."

"No. Further combat is meaningless. All we can do is minimize losses for the next war."

Girshin frowned.

"What nonsense? Victory is within reach."

"Don't you see what lies at the end of this war?"

"We only fight. The outcome doesn't matter."

"Then let me tell you. Why hasn't Ymir moved yet?"

Girshin fell silent.

Ymir's animalistic intuition had reached a transcendent level beyond even the realm of animals.

"This isn't… the end?"

Ashur nodded.

"Satan is descending in Zebul, annihilating angels. Humans have cast powerful magic directly at Aravot. Either way, heaven will fall."

Girshin's expression grew serious.

"But Ymir hasn't moved. Therefore…"

"Yes."

Realizing no further explanation was needed, Ashur concluded.

"Ra's will is about to manifest."

Second Heaven, Rakia.

Kangnan stood with her wrists bound in the Hall of Corruption, .

's ability was limited to absorbing power, but it was fixed in space using the angelic power of Hena.

After a night of torture, Kangnan was in a pitiful state, barely able to stand.

The biggest problem was the lifeless look in her eyes.

Gaold wouldn't come.

It was acceptance rather than resentment.

She had seen how fiercely Gaold had lived to save Miro, so she could understand.

"It's past noon. It seems he has abandoned you."

Uriel, floating with her arms crossed outside the wall, looked beyond the broken bridge.

Kangnan didn't respond.

She knew it, but admitting it was another matter.

Uriel slowly turned to look at Kariel standing beside Kangnan.

'Weaker than before.'

An angel's greatness comes from the mind, so mental shock directly affects their presence.

Though Raiel had lifted the stop magic, the humiliation from humans had further weakened Kariel.

Dark shadows lay under her eyes, and her once handsome face was gaunt, with no trace of archangel authority.

Whoooosh!

A strong wind blew from Aravot.

The sand mixed in the wind hitting her back felt like burning pain to Kangnan.

'Life is… pain.'

That's what Gaold had said.

"Kariel, let's begin the execution."

The fallen angel Mauriel approached for permission, but Kariel remained silent.

Since being defeated by human magic, her weakened mind had deepened her hatred for humans.

The question was, what was the source of that hatred?

'I…'

When did she start hating humans?

The doubt Kariel had never considered met precisely with the question Uriel had always pondered.

'Indeed, Kariel. Isn't it strange? Angels are great, but humans are confusing. Are we really…'

Are we truly superior to humans?

Without an answer to that question, there is no future for heaven.

That's why Uriel had watched Kariel's long struggle to the end, and finally, it bore fruit.

"He's coming."

All the fallen angels and Maras in Rakia turned to the plain before the wall.

Mikael Gaold, who had made them realize human desire, was slowly walking forward.

"Why…?"

Kangnan looked at Gaold in shock. Even seeing it with her own eyes, she couldn't believe it.

When Uriel landed, Gaold stopped walking.

"Where is Miro?"

"……"

Gaold looked up at Kangnan without answering.

Hanging by her handcuffs, her clothes were torn, and she was covered in wounds.

Gaold stared at her expressionlessly for a long time, then suddenly smiled.

"Kekeke. You really got it, mutt."

"You…!"

Kangnan shouted, squeezing out her strength.

"Idiot! Why did you come here?"

It was her honest feeling.

After all the time spent to save Miro, why come here and give it all up?

"Your rough mouth means you're still holding on."

"Go back! Go to Miro! Have you forgotten what it took to get here?"

"Yes, I forgot."

"…What?"

Kangnan was speechless at his easy admission.

"I forgot what you sacrificed to save me. I've lived forgetting it."

Kangnan's eyes filled with tears, and she fought to hold back her sobs.

He knew?

She had never mentioned it, never wanted him to know.

So she could be satisfied with this.

"Fine. Then go back. I'm fine… Please… find happiness."

Gaold started walking again.

As he neared Uriel, all the fallen angels flew in to surround him.

Forced to stop, Gaold looked up at Kangnan and smirked.

"Don't be too angry. I'll leave soon enough. But first, I must keep my promise."

Kangnan's lips trembled.

'No. Don't say it.'

"I promised never to leave you again."

Her heart began to race.

Gaold's rare gentle smile conveyed extreme unease.

He hadn't abandoned Miro or come to save her.

He was ready to let go of all the hatred he had built up over 20 years of suffering.

Mauriel approached, sneering.

"You were supposed to bring Miro. Do you think you can leave alive after breaking the deal?"

"Haa, this guy and that guy…"

Gaold lowered his head and let out a quiet sigh.

When he looked up again, the kind face was gone, replaced by the mad mage once more.

"This is driving me crazy."

As the nightmare from ten years ago resurfaced in his mind, the color drained from Kangnan's face.

"Don't…"

Gaold's eyelids trembled as he looked up at the sky, his pupils slowly rolling back.

"Don't do it…"

Unable to hold back her tears, Kangnan sobbed and pleaded.

"Please don't… sir."

At the same time, Gaold's hair stood on end.

'Goodbye, cruel world.'

Poof!

All the fallen angels surrounding Gaold vanished, leaving only a few drops of liquid behind, as if they had evaporated.

Pain.

A hundred million times.
