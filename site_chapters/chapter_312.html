Chapter 312: The Wheel of Causality (4)

Crouch listened intently with a serious expression.

"But you know, how should I put it... Yes, if you live long enough, you start to see a bit of what's inside the box. To explain it from a human perspective, you begin to understand who will be unlucky and who will be fortunate."

"I see."

Teraje had witnessed countless events from the origin of humanity to the present.

Feeling that an excessive gift would come out even before spinning the roulette was not due to supernatural powers or magic. It was an understanding of chaotic patterns gained from spinning the gacha machine countless times.

"It was just a thought."

Perhaps thinking it sounded boastful, Uorin cut herself off with a cute gesture, sticking out her tongue.

Crouch found her endearing. It was an absolute love for an unattainable being.

"Your foresight always amazes me, Your Majesty. So, what do you plan to do now?"

"Hmm, even someone as skilled as Arius couldn't extract Ataraxia. There must be something I don't know. I plan to ask Zion. I have some guesses."

"Then what about Shirone?"

It was clear that Uorin had summoned the Wind Guards, but he hadn't heard anything about Shirone's fate. If she had taken him for another purpose, he would need to act.

"I feel that Shirone alternates between red and blue balls. I'm not sure yet. I want to observe a bit longer. Let him live his life. Just block any external interference, whether from factions, organizations, institutions, or nations."

"Understood."

Uorin gave orders befitting the Triumvirate.

Maintaining Shirone's life as if today's events hadn't happened was possible because Teraje's world was far broader than Shirone's.

"And I will inherit Teraje's castle. Inform the sisters of each country to dissolve within two years."

"I will obey."

There was no need to seek permission from the ruler of Kashan. Uorin's judgment was Teraje's judgment.

In the early days of the lineage, disputes arose over who would succeed the predecessor. But over the ages, Teraje evolved to a stage where she could accept death.

The sisters would accept it and end their lives when the time was right.

But Uorin could also be the one to end her life. So why did Uorin decide she should inherit Teraje's name?

The only difference between her and her sisters was that she had met Shirone.

"Do you think Shirone can prevent the final war?"

Uorin looked out the window, satisfied with her subordinate's insight. Morning had come, and bright sunlight was illuminating the world.

"Hehe, prevent the final war? That's not the reason to inherit Teraje's name."

"Then...?"

"Heaven is just another conquerable territory. It doesn't matter if it's not now. I am an eternal being. One day, I will conquer beyond heaven to the ends of the universe."

Crouch gaped in astonishment.

Conquering the universe. It was an ambition to dethrone Anke Ra, the incarnation of the Akashic Records, and become a god herself.

"Ataraxia is a shortcut to that future. If Arius failed, it means no one can excavate it. But wouldn't it be different if it were me?"

"Then..."

"Yes."

Uorin looked at Crouch and placed her hands on her chest. Her figure, smiling in the sunlight, sparkled with a golden hue.

"I will become Shirone's daughter."

The next morning, the medical team from the Kashan Empire transported Shirone to his quarters.

They had done everything they could, but he had not regained consciousness. Even if he did, they said there was only a fifty-fifty chance his mind would fully recover.

Vincent wept at the possibility that Shirone might never become a mage. Amy and Reina comforted the sobbing Olina.

"Don't worry. They were just giving a general prognosis. Shirone's mental strength is different from others. He will surely recover completely and wake up."

Olina nodded repeatedly, her voice hoarse.

Sensing her resolve, Vincent wiped his face with his sleeve and comforted his wife.

"Yes, let's wait. The medical team said it's fifty-fifty. It's not a hopeless situation yet. We need to be Shirone's strength now more than ever."

Hearing this, Olina stood up abruptly. She couldn't just sit and cry as a mother.

She placed a wet cloth on Shirone's feverish forehead and clasped her hands in prayer.

Amy, knowing the extent of the shock Shirone had suffered, understood that such temporary measures were ineffective. But she kept silent, respecting Olina's motherly concern.

Leaving Shirone in Olina's care, the three quietly left the room.

Having survived, they now had to think about practical matters. They wanted to pack up and leave immediately, but Kazra was currently under Kashan's administrative control.

Interference in internal affairs was considered a national disgrace. Despite the nobles' strong opposition, they couldn't openly rebel because Teraje was still technically Orkamp's wife. They had no choice but to wait until the paternity test results came out the next day.

Spending a night in enemy territory felt like walking on thin ice.

The Kashan medical team assured them that the Kazra nobles wouldn't act, but they couldn't be sure. The biggest concern was whether Eliza would remain passive.

Olina had slapped the queen. It was a capital offense for a commoner to assault royalty. Olina would have done the same even if she could turn back time, but getting out unscathed was another matter.

The long night finally ended, and morning came.

The attendants of the Ogent family, who had stayed up all night watching over Shirone, had dark circles under their eyes. Contrary to their fears, nothing had happened. It seemed the Kashan medical team hadn't spoken lightly.

On the day of Shirone's paternity test, the nobles of Kazra focused on the court.

The safe displayed in the inner garden was retrieved. Since a public surveillance system was in place, no one had accessed the safe during the exhibition period.

The alchemists opened the neatly sealed caspi. If anyone had planned to tamper with it, now was the only chance, but with Kashan in control, no one dared approach.

The alchemists confirmed the results, handed them to Kashan's minister, and prepared a report. Before the public announcement, they sent a letter to the king's apartment.

Eliza had been bedridden with a fever for two days.

The fact that Shirone had tried to kill her was shocking. Plus, her cheek still hurt from Olina's slap. She hadn't slept a wink the previous night, with her fever reaching 40 degrees.

Orkamp's mental state was also far from normal.

He was furious about the internal interference, and their only hope, Shirone, was under Teraje's protection. The test results were their last hope. If Shirone was confirmed as their child, they would have some leverage.

'I need Shirone. I need Shirone.'

Just then, the interior minister arrived with the documents from the alchemy department.

"Your Majesty, the results are in."

Eliza snatched the documents before Orkamp could react. Her hands trembled with tension.

She sat on the bed and opened the file. Inside was an official document stamped by the alchemy department.

The paper in her hands shook.

She read every word on the document. When she finally saw the results, tears streamed down her cheeks.

She hugged the document tightly and looked up at the ceiling.

"Oh, my son..."

Olina had stayed up all night nursing Shirone. Her dedication seemed to pay off as his fever subsided a bit by morning. Amy looked at Shirone with pity.

The Kashan medical team said there was a high chance he would wake up within 24 hours. But if he didn't, they couldn't predict when he would regain consciousness.

It might take a day or two, but in the worst case, he could remain in a vegetative state for over a year.

'Well, he did cast an unbelievable spell.'

A monster from the first level of the deep had clawed, torn, and ripped apart Shirone's mind.

It was like tearing a child's blanket to cover an adult's body. Even considering Shirone's rapid growth, it would take at least five years of training to reach that level.

The mental shock he had suffered was unimaginable.

Could he recover?

If his mental resilience had lost its elasticity, he would be a wreck for life.

'Sigh, what am I thinking? Shirone wouldn't... Shirone...'

There was a knock at the door. When Reina opened it, Eliza's maid bowed.

Amy thought the inevitable had come.

"The queen requests your presence, Olina."

Olina nodded calmly.

She changed the wet cloth on Shirone's forehead, kissed his cheek, and prepared to leave.

"Let's go. Lead the way."

Vincent followed with a worried expression.

"Dear, let me go with you."

"No. The queen called for me. Don't worry. I'll be back soon."

Olina had her reasons for needing to meet Eliza alone.

If the charge of assaulting royalty was upheld, she wouldn't be the only one facing execution. She had to negotiate to protect her family.

The Grand Hall still bore the marks of the battle. The debris had been cleared, but the collapsed floor was temporarily repaired with planks, and the broken pillars were left untouched.

The king and queen's attire was more elaborate than ever. Olina could sense their desperate attempt to hide the humiliation of losing control to Kashan.

Eliza looked down at Olina arrogantly, but when Olina didn't avert her gaze, she felt a pang of confusion.

When they first met, she had been too scared to even make eye contact. But now, why was she glaring at the queen of a nation? Was it because she was a mother? Perhaps.

But she couldn't accept it in her heart.

'If our positions were reversed... could I do the same as her?'

It seemed like something any mother could do, yet it wasn't something just anyone could do, a contradictory thought.

"I clearly remember what you did to me."

Olina let out a very small scoff.

Orkamp thought she had dust in her nose, but Eliza, being a woman, knew it was a scoff.

Her eyes seemed to say, "If there's one thing I regret most in my life, it's that I only slapped you once."

"Do you think I'm a pathetic mother?"

"I'm not someone who can judge others. I just couldn't allow my child to be harmed."

Olina replied, bowing her head respectfully.

Orkamp couldn't sense the high-density psychological warfare going on between the women, so he kept his mouth shut.

Eliza recalled the situation when Shirone had gone berserk.

"Yes, it was scary. But Shirone attacked me. If I had gotten closer, I might have died. You act like you did something greater than me, but what would you have done in my situation?"

"I would still have gone to Shirone."

"Why? Because you're a mother?"

"Because my child was stabbed with a knife."
