Chapter 1048: New Humanity (3)

Secret Room

In a private space known only to himself, Fermi opened the box excavated by the Parrot Mercenary Corps.

Inside were memo papers still covered in dirt and pieces of metal with unknown uses.

It was the future from the past.

'It will take time to restore the memory chip.'

It had been buried for a long time, and the hardware itself was severely damaged.

Fermi set the chip aside.

'Carefully, carefully.'

Complete restoration was difficult, but even a single word could be invaluable.

Using tweezers, Fermi picked up a memo and gently held it under the desk lamp.

Someone's diary.

"Let's see."

Or perhaps the last desperate record written before death.

"New Humanity?"

Fermi noted the term in his mind and unfolded the twice-folded paper with the tweezers.

It was an official document, half torn as if it had been destroyed, with Eastern characters written on it.

"Politicians, always the same…"

Even in the face of the world's end, their dedication to not sharing information with other countries was unwavering.

He deciphered the text with flexible thinking.

'The second beast. The number of the beast 666. The three-stage filter reaching the end. The potential rampage of Argones.'

Setting the document down, Fermi rested his chin on his hand.

'As expected, the end is unavoidable. Humanity, having reached the singularity, cannot ignore the outside world.'

This singularity is divided into three-stage filters.

'The first stage is awareness of the outside world. The second stage is contact with evidence of the outside world. And the third stage is… escaping the photon system.'

According to Fermi's analysis, humanity was just passing through the second stage filter.

'The beginning of this is the New Humanity.'

More excavation would be needed for detailed information, but it was clear it was related to the outside world.

'When the third stage is reached, the world will end somehow. But "somehow" is a bit…'

In his thoughts, the end was as difficult as integrating humanity into Ultima.

'It's very hard for any event to reach an extreme. Moreover…'

Hadn't Goffin already escaped the photon system?

"Hmm."

Fermi tapped the tweezers on the desk.

'Just because one escapes the photon system doesn't mean they enter the outside world.'

He wanted to fill the gaps in his reasoning.

'Or… the concept of the outside world might be entirely different from what Goffin or we think.'

One cannot guard against the unknown.

'A perfect end? Transcending causality? Does this world allow such a system?'

Even when Nane tried to close the world, wasn't Shirone there to defend it?

'That's the rule. In a world where extremes exist, there must be corresponding extremes.'

Therefore, the end is something that transcends the world.

'It's unimaginable. Beyond the brain's function, it would be something our brains can't process.'

What form would it take?

"The end of numbers."

Whatever unimaginable form it takes, it would ultimately come down to numbers.

With a click, the tweezers were fixed on the desk, and Fermi's gaze fell on one of the memos.

'The second beast.'

A being born to welcome the first beast and destroy the world.

'Where is it?'

Shirone would be there.

Holding the official document with the tweezers, Fermi carefully examined the seal on the torn part.

"Bukjeon."

Borbor and Tanju Ra, having safely passed through Bukjeon's city gate, entered a brewery.

A scholar with swords at his waist bowed his head.

"You've arrived."

It was Tanju Ra's satellite, Daehou.

"Where is Mika?"

As the inner tent was drawn back, an old man with a hunched back came out holding a jar.

After confirming the stars, he spoke.

"Many of the residents have been replaced by the New Humanity. The owner of this brewery is also one of them."

Borbor's satellite, Mika, was an electromagnetic organism that could parasitize others' bodies.

Having no emotions to begin with, Borbor was indifferent to Mika.

"The mechanism?"

"They use mental radiation to mutate surrounding organisms. It's similar to the technique used by the Great Star. A tainted mind turns nearby creatures into monsters."

"Quite dangerous. It was naive to think we only needed to close the areas where the demonic realm opened."

"Mutations are unpredictable. Moreover, this one can control minds. It means he can induce mutations at will."

Tanju Ra's face twisted.

"The ability to induce biological mutations. That's serious. If we don't eliminate it in Bukjeon, the continent will be overturned."

Borbor agreed.

"Where is the leader of the New Humanity?"

Daehou replied.

"Mika's ability has searched the city's strongholds, but no one knows the location. It's likely near Governor Hwanggak. Or it could be Hwanggak himself."

"That makes sense. Let's meet the governor and confirm."

Mika asked.

"What about the body?"

"Kill it."

As soon as the order was given, Mika took a knife and stabbed his own heart.

Moments later, the electromagnetic organism emerged from the corpse and merged with Borbor through the current.

-I have taken control of the brainwaves. Shall I think?

"No, handle incoming signals. Report any unusual mutations."

-Understood.

The two stars and their two satellites left the brewery and headed straight for the inner gate.

Mika, who had been silent until now, sent a strong signal to Borbor's brain.

-They are not human.

Though they looked human, the soldiers' eyes held no emotion.

"What's going on?" Tanju Ra asked, looking at the pointed spears.

"We've come to see Governor Hwanggak. If you let us in, we are willing to talk."

As soon as he finished speaking, a powerful aura emanated from the soldiers.

-Stand back. They cannot be analyzed.

Mika sensed a qualitatively different pressure from any organism he had parasitized before.

Borbor looked up.

'If the mutation intensifies at close range… the leader of the New Humanity is in the castle.'

He didn't know the extent of the mental radiation's power, but even stars of the Ivory Tower wouldn't be exceptions.

"Hoho! Non-humans with such authority. What shall we do now?"

Borbor made a decision.

"Force our way in."

Simultaneously, the soldiers' skin tore, revealing forms that were a mix of human and locust.

"Kieeek!"

At the monstrous scream from their gaping jaws, Tanju Ra's eyes gleamed with excitement.

"Interesting."

"Above!"

As Daehou shouted, a swarm of locusts filled the sky from the castle wall.

The sound of chewing was heard.

In front of Governor Hwanggak's corpse, a 17-year-old girl was eating his arm.

She had an innocent look as if she knew no darkness, but her eyes were not human.

"Tasty."

As she tore off a piece of arm muscle, the bone was exposed.

"Lady Piri."

It wasn't a name given by her parents.

Having evolved into the New Humanity, she gave herself a name meaning "out of reason."

"Barbelve, is there more of this? Strong human meat. I want to keep eating it."

At the end of Piri's gaze stood a giant locust-human over two meters tall.

Wings hidden under its carapace fluttered, and a scorpion's tail rose above its head.

"This is not the time to enjoy eating. There are powerful beings outside the gate."

Piri's face lit up with joy.

"Really? More tasty meat? What are you waiting for? Go catch them!"

"…Aren't you staying too long? We should destroy this city and leave."

"Haha! It's fine. We're strong. You know that, right? No one in this world can beat us."

That was true.

Though they hadn't fought all of humanity, they had a precise understanding of themselves.

Such abilities, such intelligence.

'Have we become gods?'

Piri extended her hand.

"More. Meat."

The locust's eyes watched her, then it spread its wings and turned to the window.

"I'll bring it right away."

The locust-humans surrounding Tanju Ra at the inner gate trembled.

"Kieek! Kieek!"

They couldn't move because Tanju Ra had sentenced them.

"For inciting chaos, 600 years in prison! For causing severe mental harm to innocent residents, 987 years in prison!"

As soon as the sentence was pronounced, the legal binding force restrained the locust-humans.

"Therefore, combining all charges…"

Tanju Ra grinned.

"Death."

Simultaneously, Daehou flew in like the wind, swinging his twin swords left and right.

'Executioner's Blade.'

Like scissors cutting through a landscape, the locusts' heads were sliced off in chunks.

"Hoho! Another exemplary punishment today."

Using the leverage law, the Executioner's Blade had theoretically unlimited cutting power. Tanju Ra stroked his beard contentedly.

'With an infinitely long pole and fulcrum, one could move a planet.'

That's why he could inflict physical damage even in a 'confined' state.

Borbor approached.

"Don't let your guard down. This alone wouldn't have destroyed the northern gate of the Cheongryong Mountains."

Daehou sheathed his swords.

"These are Bukjeon's mutants. Are they qualitatively different from the Cheongryong Mountains' mutants?"

Before he could answer, the castle gate opened.

When they saw a being that didn't look human walking gracefully like a human…

-We must flee.

Mika sent a warning, but Borbor couldn't move a step.

'If I move, I'll die.'

The feeling surrounding the locust-human was so eerie that it gave Borbor that certainty.

'Strong? So I can't win? No, it's not that kind of temperament.'

He didn't know.

Even without having seen the entire universe, he felt this being shouldn't exist.

'What's the problem? The aura of its form? The grotesqueness of its evolution? No, these are all things I can imagine.'

The only thing he couldn't imagine was…

'Numbers.'

When mathematically expressing the information that made up this being, there was a significant discrepancy.

'It has different numbers from us.' Something that doesn't exist anywhere in the universe.

Her thoughts were transmitted to Mika, and the decision was made as quickly as electricity.

"Run!"

Tanju Ra and Daehou fled, while Borbor charged forward.

'I don't mind dying…'

But he had to avoid a situation where everyone here was annihilated and couldn't report back to the Ivory Tower.

-I'll handle it.

As Borbor handed over control of his brain to Mika, his body twisted like rubber.

His limbs flew like whips to wrap around the locust-human's body.

-Unable to perceive.

The enemy's form vanished, and a powerful impact hit the back of Borbor's neck.

"Ugh!"

Mika reported.

-89% of brain function is paralyzed. Entering a coma state.

'No. Buy as much time as possible.'

-Refusing.

The locust-human, carrying the unconscious Borbor at its waist, looked towards the castle gate.

"Should I chase them?"

He hesitated for a moment but then remembered Piri, who would be waiting impatiently.

"Meat tastes best when eaten alive."

The inner gate closed.

Tanju Ra's eyes burned red.

"Aaah! Damn creatures!"

Though fleeing frantically, his mind was tearing the locust-human apart a thousand times.

"Stay focused. Reporting comes first."

As Daehou's ability, Executioner's Blade, split the outer gate, a vast field came into view.

"Huh?"

Tanju Ra's eyebrows twitched, and both men hurriedly stopped.

"Great Star?"

Shirone was approaching with two apostles.
