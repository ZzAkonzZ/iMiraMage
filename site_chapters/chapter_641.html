Chapter 641:

Nade woke up in Liz's bed, feeling the hangover from the previous night and grimacing.

'Liz…'

Turning his head, he saw Liz sleeping with just her face peeking out from under the blanket.

'I should have drunk less.'

He needed to remember everything clearly!

"Hmm."

Seeing the clothes scattered on the floor, Nade squinted and slowly lifted the blanket.

"Stop. Put it back."

As the blanket returned to its place, Liz smiled and stretched.

"Cheeky. Did you sleep well?"

"Hehe, I was just trying to cover you up because you looked cold. Let's get up and have breakfast."

"Ugh, I can't eat. I think I drank too much last night."

Liz, wriggling under the blanket, crawled over to Nade and blew a raspberry on his stomach.

"Haha! That tickles!"

"Let's stay a bit longer, okay?"

As Nade lovingly looked at Liz, his smile gradually faded.

"Liz."

"Yes?"

"Won't you regret this?"

At Nade's serious question, Liz adjusted her expression and sat up, wrapping herself in the blanket.

"Why would you say that? Do you regret it?"

"I'm not going to become a mage. I don't have any savings. There will be many hardships ahead. Can I really make you happy…?"

Realizing what he meant, Liz smiled reassuringly.

"It's not about you making me happy. It's about us creating happiness together."

Having lived his whole life with responsibility, Nade didn't even know what happiness was.

"Let's swim together. I'll try my best too."

"Liz…"

As Nade looked at her with teary eyes, Liz shyly smiled and asked,

"But are you really okay with this? With your graduation rank, a future as a mage wouldn't be bad."

Nade shook his head.

"This is my limit. Iruki knows that too, which is why he's going to Yongrye. If I'm satisfied, I can't go any higher."

"Is that so? As long as you don't regret it."

"Of course, I want to be the best. But I can never be the best with magic. You know that from seeing Shirone."

"True, he does have a different aura."

"Exactly. It's a subtle difference, but very few can make that impression. It's that kind of realm. Competing at the highest level is unimaginable without someone like Shirone."

It was a battle to see how much difference one could make among those who reached the same level.

"A pyramid of competition built with human blood and flesh. I've stepped down from it to walk a new path with you."

"Thank you, Nade."

As Nade held Liz in his arms, he looked out the window at the bright sunlight, cheering for his friend's future.

"Good luck, Shirone."

Tormia Kingdom Magic Association.

"This is infuriating."

Returning from a special trial, Lupist slammed his desk, splitting it as if struck by an axe.

"They pushed it through, didn't they?"

Chief Secretary Jane's expression was also grim.

"Unanimously. Shirone can't graduate. Criminals are barred from using public institutions."

"A criminal? On what grounds?"

"Royal defamation."

"Shirone… defamed the royal family?"

The file Lupist threw fell under the broken desk, but there was no need to read it.

"He committed twenty-three offenses. Disrespect, defamation, sexual harassment, assault—anything they could pin on him."

"But those were issues Pony agreed to handle when she enrolled in the magic school. By that logic, other students also committed offenses against her."

"The standards are vague. If she felt humiliated only by Shirone, that's enough. Being a commoner is a big deal."

"He didn't seem like that kind of person."

"Of course not. This is a scheme from the politicians."

"…They're trying to tame Shirone."

"Exactly. They don't really intend to prevent his graduation. The average trade value scouts worldwide have set for Shirone is about 2.25 billion gold. There's even a rumor that the Jincheon Empire has prepared 30 billion gold."

Given Jincheon's recent aggressive moves in the magical society, it wasn't entirely impossible.

"If Shirone becomes a free agent, the kingdom can't handle it. So they're blocking his graduation. Those without graduation qualifications aren't listed on the Red Line."

"But it's a risky strategy. Shirone has other options besides the Red Line."

"If he chooses the Black Line, his family, friends, and everyone connected to him will suffer. The White Line hasn't added new members in nearly four years. Even if they did, would he reject the kingdom's offer, expecting to be the world's number one?"

Shirone had set incredible records, but Tormia was just a small country from a global perspective.

"He's cornered."

"The higher-ups are confident, so they're pushing it. They'll demand a lousy contract in exchange for dropping the royal defamation charges. Probably around 100 million, with the rest going into politicians' pockets."

"It's a bit bitter."

"As a mage, I don't feel good about it either. But this way, there's little chance of losing him to another country. No matter how he's treated, strengthening the association's power benefits us. The negotiation team has probably already left."

"Do you think Shirone will accept it?"

"He just needs to close his eyes and bend once. Then he'll have his graduation and future as a mage secured."

"Under the Tormia Kingdom's system."

Lupist understood Jane's hesitation.

"He's smart. Let's hope he makes the wise choice…"

Nade returned to school and found the advanced class students gathered in front of the central bulletin board.

"What’s going on? Did something happen?"

As he pushed through the crowd and read the notice, his eyes widened in shock, and he turned around instinctively.

"Shirone! Where is Shirone?"

When he opened the dormitory door, Shirone was sitting alone on the bed, with Iruki and Amy by his side.

"What happened? Treason against the royal family?"

Shirone raised his hand with a tired expression.

"Welcome back. Did you have fun with Senior Liz?"

"That's not the issue right now! What’s this about canceling your graduation?"

Iruki spoke up.

"Calm down. We already handled everything while you were away."

Nade, who had brought a chair to sit on, asked sharply.

"Why did this suddenly happen?"

"It must be the kingdom's scheme. Pony accused Shirone. Although the Ogent side is preparing an appeal, it won't work. It's a special trial by royal decree."

Nade's eyes flashed with anger.

"Where is Pony? I'll go see her."

Amy said.

"She's in the girls' dormitory. But it's already under control. The mages from the Creas branch of the Magic Association are guarding it tightly. We almost got arrested trying to force our way in."

"Arrested? With Shirone's skills, he could sneak in without being caught!"

Shirone smiled bitterly.

"Pony didn't lead this. It's not her nature. It must be an order from her family. Iruki thinks it's a strategy to lower the contract terms. So I'm waiting for now."

"Begging for help wouldn't be enough! We have two friends from the highest noble families."

"It's a royal decree. Noble titles won't help."

"Terraje! Doesn't she know you? Even if it's the royal family, the empire would intervene!"

Iruki shook his head again.

"This is an internal matter of the kingdom. If Kashan interferes, Gustav and Jincheon will move. Do you understand? We're not students anymore. This is a real-world fight."

"Real world or not, it's unfair!"

"Calm down and think rationally. If you get marked by the kingdom, you won't be able to do business here. If you get more involved, Senior Liz could also be affected. Is that what you want?"

As he thought about it, his heart sank, and the harsh reality hit him.

"I predict they'll demand a secret contract in exchange for graduation. Holding your family as hostages. The best we can do is raise the contract terms as much as possible. Shirone doesn't mind staying in Tormia."

"Stay here? After how they've treated you?"

Shirone held Amy's hand and said.

"I don't want to leave Amy. Besides, I'll become a mage. I could get a job at the Magic Association."

Even now, the memory of their first kiss was vivid, and he couldn't ruin all that happiness.

"Ah, this is driving me crazy!"

Unlike the furious Nade, Shirone and Amy just looked at each other apologetically.

Rebelling against the royal decision meant accepting they couldn't be together, so they were cautious not to hurt each other by speaking first.

'It's okay if it's a bit unfair. As long as I'm with Amy…'

There would be far more happy days than sad ones.

At that moment, someone knocked on the door loudly.

"Arian Shirone! By royal decree! Welcome the administrative officer from the royal magic department!"

"I'm coming."

Shirone, having prepared himself, walked to the door and looked back at his friends.

"See you later. Please give me some advice during the negotiation."

In the central square, all the students were already gathered in a circle, and a golden carriage was standing with guards.

Next to the administrative officer Lyman was Pony, who looked pale and had her head bowed.

'She looks emaciated.'

The once bright eyes of the mage were now empty, as if her soul had left.

Despite Pony's resistance to the royal decision, she was just one of many royals.

'I am a puppet. A puppet of bloodline.'

Ignoring Pony's trembling lips, Lyman read the decree loudly.

"Royal Decree No. 2467! The graduation exam results of Arian Shirone, who committed the serious crime of treason against the royal family, are hereby nullified!"

Even as numerous charges were listed, Shirone listened calmly.

"But!"

As Iruki predicted, there was a twist.

"The gracious royal Pony has decided to consider it a foolish mistake of a lowly person, so kneel and apologize here!"

Lyman folded the decree and pointed.

"Kneel and beg, you lowly thing! Bow your head to the ground!"

It was the state's intention to tame her from the start, but Pony, now a puppet, would rather die.

'What am I…'

Being naked would be less shameful, and Shirone felt dizzy as he knelt.

'Hmm, surprisingly compliant. It means the decision is made. Then starting at 80 million instead of 100 million might work.'

Saving 20 million would mean about 500,000 less, which he planned to add to his daughter's study abroad fund.

'I hate this. I really hate this.'

Watching Shirone's hand lower to the ground, Amy bit her lip.

'Because of me, because of me…'

She wanted to stop him immediately, but knowing Shirone wanted this as much as she did, she couldn't speak.

"Noble royal."

Contrary to his opening, Shirone, who had been thinking for a long time, slowly straightened his knees and turned to the students.

"I'm sorry, Amy. I…"

Amy burst into tears and nodded repeatedly.

"Yes. Do it, Shirone."

Only Amy's consent mattered to Shirone, who then looked straight at Lyman and spoke.

"I'm sorry, but I cannot obey that order. I did nothing wrong to Pony."

Lyman looked astonished.

"What, what?"

Unable to bear the shame, Pony burst into tears, and Iruki muttered with a bitter smile.

"He finally did it."
