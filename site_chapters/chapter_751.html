Chapter 751:

Holy War.

The sight of the twelve rulers who governed the world gathered in one place was nothing short of surreal.

With a single word, they could shake the entire world—and any agreement reached in this assembly would trigger a massive geopolitical upheaval.

Though Basari, the President of the Cotria Republic—this Holy War’s host—sat at the chairman’s seat, the actual dynamics placed the Dual Kings in the position of moderators, while the Seven Kings watched for signs from the Three Emperors.

Uorin, the head of the Valkyries, closed her folder.

“Then we’ll set the magic bullet supply ratio as discussed and…”

She paused briefly, pretending to collect herself before continuing smoothly.

“Shall we take a ten-minute break?”

No one showed it openly, but the talks had already been going on for seven straight hours.

‘That’s exactly the problem.’

Uorin had paused for one reason only: because of Gustav Havitz XVII, sitting directly across from her at the massive round table.

‘How long are you going to keep staring?’

Since the start of the Holy War, Havitz hadn’t spoken a single word. He hadn’t even looked anywhere else—only at Uorin.

Even now, during the break, nothing changed. He lounged sideways in his chair, rocking it idly just as he had at the beginning.

‘A provocation… no, it’s not that.’

He was analyzing.

The way he stared, like he wanted to capture every last detail of her down to the tip of a hair, made Uorin not even want to imagine what was going through his head.

“Hoho, come to think of it… I’ve yet to offer my congratulations. On becoming the new ruler of the Empire.”

Mayer, King of the Jive Kingdom and representative of the Central Continent, reached out to shake Havitz’s hand.

“……”

But Havitz didn’t even turn his head, continuing instead to rock his chair.

“Ahem. Ahem.”

Mayer’s awkwardness was palpable. Behind him, the Shinjang, Jive’s royal guards, simultaneously placed their hands on the hilts of their swords on their left hips.

“Now, now, let’s not do that. Not in a place like this…”

No one would dare disgrace the King of Jive under normal circumstances—but this wasn’t a normal place. The Holy War was full of such people.

‘Arrogant bastard. He was my equal not long ago.’

Before the death of Havitz XVI, Havitz XVII had only ruled over a fraction of the empire.

“By the way, what kind of person was this Nane?”

Moonryong, ruler of the Moon Kingdom, asked, prompting interest from Jin Kang, Emperor of Jincheon.

“He graduated from Jive,” Mayer replied, feigning an awkward smile.

“There’s not much to say. Just a foreign exchange student who became an enemy of mankind.”

His evasive tone, seemingly cutting off further information, darkened the expressions of the Seven Kings.

Even that small gesture chilled the room, prompting Uorin to finally lock eyes with Havitz.

“I’d like to ask you something.”

Havitz, who had seemed like he wouldn’t respond at all, suddenly spoke.

“How long’s it been?”

Four individuals sat behind Havitz on the floor in various positions.

“Seven hours and forty-eight minutes,” replied a woman kneeling on the floor, her tone almost questioning.

She looked like a clown, wearing a flamboyant, impractically frilly dress, her face painted stark white, and her eyelids cut away so that her eyes always appeared wide open.

Her lashes were drawn on in thick black ink—three lines above and three below.

She was Natasha, one of the Four Great Arts of Gustav—his battle doll.

One of the unique spectacles of the Holy War was the diverse royal guards each ruler brought.

But Havitz brought no such unit. He came with only four generals.

‘So those are the ones I’d only heard about…’

Though no one showed it, many had been secretly curious. At last, attention shifted from Havitz to those behind him.

‘The Four Great Arts of the King of Desire.’

Havitz himself might not be sane, but those drawn to his world-class charisma were powerful in their own right.

The Four Great Arts were elite—each one a master at the pinnacle of their field.

“Seven hours and forty-eight minutes…”

Havitz murmured quietly, then finally sat upright and propped his chin on the table.

“So she’s ‘Mitochondrial Eve’… and yet she’s surprisingly impatient, that so-called Empress of Kashan.”

Just from the subtle tremor of one of the 100 Wind Guards, a gust of wind swept through the chamber.

“Stop.”

The turbulent air stilled instantly.

“It wasn’t anything worth being patient for. But being stared at like that would make anyone uncomfortable.”

Uorin responded by the book.

“They say you survive by receiving a man’s genes, right?”

A well-known truth among representatives of the Holy War, but an unspoken rule that no one dared mention.

“How about receiving mine?”

Lightning flashed in Gando’s eyes, but Uorin wasn’t shaken by such provocation.

“Your predecessor made the same offer once.”

A top-secret file no one else knew about.

“But he didn’t meet the standard, so I turned him down. Let alone you, who’s inferior to even him…”

Havitz yawned.

“Sounds fun, though. I wonder what kind of child would be born. What happens if my daughter gives birth to my daughter again?”

‘He’s not even listening. Does he have no trauma at all?’

She had tried provoking him by invoking his father to trigger an inferiority complex—but there was no reaction.

‘That’s confidence bordering on madness. Peak narcissism.’

Havitz XVI, his predecessor, had indulged in desire but knew how to exercise restraint.

‘A monster created by the times.’

Uorin, who had lived through the course of human history, knew how evil and cruel people could be.

But Havitz, sitting before her now, felt like the embodiment of all that wickedness combined.

“Let’s see… three daughters, then each of them has three more… no, maybe I’ll use the sons to make daughters…”

As Havitz muttered without pause, Uorin ignored him and resumed the meeting.

“Then let’s move on to the next item: Valkyrie deployment and each nation’s budget allocation for altar defense.”

Even at the Holy War, every nation pursued its own interests.

So it was customary to bring up sensitive issues like this only when fatigue was starting to set in.

‘Now the real battle begins.’

Just as all the representatives tensed up to seize even the smallest advantage, Havitz suddenly shouted:

“That’s it!”

The grave atmosphere shattered like glass.

“Let’s farm them! Hang them up and make them give birth once a day!”

“You bastard!”

As Gando leapt to his feet, Jin Kang, Emperor of Jincheon, slammed the table.

BOOM!

The heavy marble table trembled ominously. The five Great Generals representing Jincheon—the Five Dragon Generals—hardened their expressions.

‘Father…’

Seongeum, who had fought against Nane and earned the rank of Chief General of the Azure Dragon Unit, was also present at the Holy War.

“I don’t mean to lecture the ruler of the world…”

The fact that Havitz turned to look at someone other than Uorin was enough to gauge the weight of the moment.

“But wouldn’t it be better if you shut that filthy mouth of yours? Or at least do something about the shit smell coming out of it.”

A catastrophe seemed imminent.

“Are you saying… you want me to shit? In your mouth?”

As Yeodal, the chief general of the Five Dragon Generals, leaned forward, the Yellow Dragon Unit behind him clinked forward in full armor.

If you tried to stop him, you’d lose the upper hand—so Jin Kang didn’t.

“Foolish young king, this will be the last time we tolerate your whining over your dead father. From now on, choose your words carefully.”

“And if I don’t?”

“Then one of the Three Emperor seats will go to a dog raised by our people.”

Havitz sniffed and looked away.

“A dog? A dog, you say?”

Then he pointed straight at Jin Kang.

“You?”

Natasha tilted her head back and laughed.

“A-hahahaha!”

Yeodal channeled force into her body, but Seongeum was quicker—she unleashed an etheric wave to stop her movements.

‘Miss… why?’

Yeodal could see Seongeum biting her lip.

‘This is a fight between Havitz and Uorin. If Jincheon gets dragged into it, we’ll end up taking all the blame.’

Among the Three Emperors, whoever acted first would undoubtedly be targeted by the others—it was an obvious truth.

“To the representative of Jincheon, thank you for saying what I, as a woman, could not bring myself to say.”

Uorin gave a respectful nod to Jin Kang, subtly signaling an alliance—and then began a full-on provocation of Havitz.

“I feel the same way. This is a place where the future of the world is being decided. If you’re not fit to participate, maybe go wipe yourself up in the restroom or something.”

“You insolent wench!”

An old man with wild hair, seated behind Havitz, suddenly leapt onto the table and ripped open his shirt.

“Gustav~!”

His scrawny ribs and dark nipples were fully exposed.

“Havitz!”

Contorting his body like he was having a seizure, he glared up at Uorin with his eyes rolled high.

He was Smōdo, Internal Affairs King—one of Gustav’s Four Great Arts.

“I kept quiet because you were pathetic, but did you just talk to the Emperor of the Gustav Empire like that? Wanna go? Huh? Want me to wipe the floor with you right now?!”

‘What kind of clown show is this?’

Government officials from various nations clicked their tongues, but the expressions of the other leaders at the round table were dead serious.

‘Smōdo of Internal Affairs… the only one capable of supporting Havitz. Which means what Havitz truly desires is…’

War.

‘Is that really it? But if he planned to go to war against the entire world, then from the beginning—’

Uorin, unable to read anything from Havitz’s face, quickly corrected her thinking.

‘No… this isn’t that kind of calculation.’

The reason Havitz was referred to as an unknown entity—

The reason no one could analyze him—

Was because he had no thoughts.

‘I finally get it.’

Having gone through three world resets, Uorin had observed Havitz XVII’s life three times.

As with many others, he became king by killing 781 of his siblings—every single time.

‘But the problem is…’

He had never killed them in the same way. Not once, in any of the timelines.

‘Chaos.’

A mind devoid of patterns or structure, ruled only by a swirl of tangled emotions.

In this age of chaos, it was inevitable that someone like Havitz—chaos incarnate—would rise to prominence.

“What? You pissed off? Then come at me! I’ll slaughter every last Kashan bastard! I’ll kill and kill and kill until not one of them is left on this earth!”

“……”

Silence fell over the Holy War chamber—because one wrong response now would ignite a true global war.

Only Jin Kang stood firm.

“You really think you can win? Against Kashan, Jincheon, the Seven Kings here, the Dragonkin, the Fae?”

Havitz had no thoughts.

“Yeah. I think I can win.”

He was just full of baseless confidence.

‘Then again… in a way, maybe that’s everything.’

When someone truly believes they can achieve something, the very laws of the world seem to tilt in that direction.

“I’ll win. So let’s do it—war.”

Even the representatives of the Seven Kings swallowed nervously from the tension, but Uorin’s mind remained icy calm.

‘Is this an unstoppable flow now? Then maybe we flip the board at the next flag? No… that’s too far ahead.’

From the Great Purification Period onward, there were no more foundational events, meaning even Uorin couldn’t predict the outcomes clearly.

That’s what made it chaos.

‘Where the hell are you and what are you doing…?’

She could only hope that Shirone had received the message properly.
