Chapter 564:

After the alliance team left, Neid's face relaxed.

"Phew, it ended up like this. Sorry, I just accepted it."

Iruki didn't mind.

"It was something we had to face eventually. We also have reasons why we can't give up Istas. So, who are we going to recruit? Do you have anyone in mind?"

Shirone and Neid spoke simultaneously.

"Amy."

Iruki nodded.

"Right. I thought the same, which is why I set those conditions. But the problem is that Amy is already ranked fifth. We'll need to appeal to her emotions."

"I'll ask her."

If it was Shirone, there was a chance.

"Okay, Shirone will handle Amy. What about the other two?"

There was a moment of silence.

"Do you really have no one in mind?"

Neid propped his chin on his hand.

"Hmm, there are many candidates. There's the Canis team, the Dante team, and Boyle could be good too. Summoning magic could counter Pisho."

Iruki shook his head.

"We have to recruit from outside, but this is an underground matter that shouldn't be widely known. We should only approach those we think have a real chance. In that sense, Canis is risky. He's targeting Shirone."

Shirone spoke up.

"Boyle doesn't have any particular merit compared to other candidates. Dante is likely to move as a team."

Neid made a distressed face.

"Then who do we recruit?"

"In that sense, how about Dorothy?"

Shirone looked at Iruki.

"Dorothy?"

Neid, sensing something, widened his eyes and shouted.

"You! I knew it! Be honest! How far have you gone?"

"Nowhere. Do you know why I got close to Dorothy even though I'm not interested in her?"

Neid blinked.

"Huh? You weren't interested?"

"I told you, I don't like things I can't analyze."

"Then why did you get close to her?"

"On the first day of the graduation schedule, Dorothy was set to duel Shirone. Do you remember what she said? She said she'd do whatever Shirone wanted if it ended in a draw."

"Ah."

Iruki raised a finger.

"She's obsessed with rank and uses conditions and rewards. Besides, a Zoner has many advantages in Scramble Royal."

Neid finally understood.

"Okay, then Iruki will try to persuade Dorothy. What about the last person?"

Iruki asked.

"How about Sabina?"

Skill is important, but emotional compatibility is also crucial for forming a team.

In that sense, Sabina was an excellent choice.

"Absolutely not."

Neid shook his head firmly.

Victor Sabina, the only girl in Dante's group.

She was a strong air magic user who could fire dozens of wind cutters per second and had defeated Neid in a duel, so her skills were undeniable.

"Don't just say no, think about it. She seems to care about you. I'm trying to persuade Dorothy, and Shirone has a difficult request too."

"No! No! Absolutely not!"

As Neid flailed his limbs, Shirone sighed and spoke.

"Then what are you going to do? It's not about whether you can or can't. Why are you acting so unlike yourself?"

Neid, looking distressed, rubbed his hands together.

"Still no! Just give me one chance. I'll find an ally within the time. Sabina is really not an option!"

There was no stopping someone who was so adamant.

"Alright. Then Neid will find an ally in time, and Iruki and I will persuade Amy and Dorothy."

And so it was settled.

That night, Shirone went to Amy's room.

Considering Amy's skills, there was no Plan B; she had to be recruited.

'No one is following me.'

Since the enemies had already declared war, it didn't matter if they were being followed.

When he knocked, Amy's voice came from inside.

"Who is it?"

"It's me, Shirone."

Amy, who opened the door, tilted her head.

She was in her pajamas, her hair still damp, as if she had just washed.

"Shirone? What brings you here suddenly?"

Although she wasn't the type to dress up, her relaxed attire made her look different from how she did during the day.

As Shirone looked at her with shining eyes, Amy blushed and asked shyly,

"Why are you staring at me like that? You saw me earlier too."

Her tone was curt, but her face didn't show displeasure.

"I just thought you look good in pajamas too."

"Pfft."

Amy chuckled as if she had heard something ridiculous.

"You wouldn't flatter me for nothing. What do you want?"

"Actually… I have a favor to ask."

"Haha, I knew it. What is it?"

Encouraged by the sweet tone in Amy's voice, Shirone smiled brightly and said,

"This time, the Paranormal Psychic Science…"

Bang!

The door slammed shut.

The final negotiation for the Scramble Royal was scheduled for this weekend.

Until then, Shirone and his friends had to find allies, so they decided to use their only break time, lunch.

Among the potential allies, Amy was the most crucial, so Shirone persistently followed her.

But turning the heart of someone as stubborn as Amy was no easy task.

"Amy, this is really important. You know what the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Group is."

"……"

"It's the research group founded by Miro. If the history of that group ends with us…"

"Ugh, really!"

Unable to bear it any longer, Amy shouted.

"Why should I care if the research group falls apart or not! By the way, are you really not going to graduate? Did you refuse the evaluation for this?"

"No, I have to graduate…"

Looking at Shirone, who hung his head with a gloomy face, Amy twisted her lips.

'Oh dear, how desperate must this innocent kid be…'

Shirone, who hated causing trouble for others, was this desperate, which meant he was really earnest.

And the fact that she was the one he was asking didn't feel bad.

'But still, how can I commit for a whole week…'

The risk was too great to allow it based on emotions alone.

Even if she was offered a graduation rank, it wasn't much of a merit for Amy, who was already ranked fifth.

Moreover, she was worried that she wouldn't be able to fully concentrate on her evaluations during the Scramble Royal period.

Of course, she was asked to participate only when she had time, but she didn't want to be a half-hearted burden.

'If I'm going to do it, I should do it properly.'

To the extent that she could even give up graduation.

But when she thought about whether this incident was that serious, she still had doubts.

"Alright. I'll think about it. So please, cheer up. You're making me feel sick."

Shirone's face finally brightened.

"Thank you, Amy."

Listening to their conversation, Neid thought.

'It's not going to be easy for Shirone either.'

Then he turned to Iruki, who was eating in front of him, and asked,

"How about you? Is it going well with Dorothy?"

"Yeah. We're going to talk in detail later."

"Oh, she's showing interest?"

"Are you an idiot? Do you think I'd bring up the main topic first? I told her it was about graduation rank, so she has no choice but to come."

"You're pretty cautious. How do you come up with these things? You said you weren't interested in women's psychology."

"Because I'm not interested, I can see it clearly. Do you know the common trait of men who can't date? They want to date too much. Women are full of emotions and are naturally skilled at handling men's emotions. The moment you lose your cool, you get eaten alive."

"Tch, you sure talk big."

Neid felt even more anxious.

Anyway, Amy was showing some interest, and Iruki seemed likely to succeed for some reason.

'This is bad. I need to find a candidate by today.'

Although being friendly with everyone was his weapon, the fact that he rarely had deep connections was becoming a disadvantage.

'Screamer? He's loyal despite being fierce. No, he's not suitable for Scramble Royal. I can't trust Luman either.'

While he was lost in thought, he saw Suabi standing in front of Dante, fidgeting.

'Huh? What's going on?'

Suabi, a utility major.

Despite her indecisive and hesitant personality, she was a talented utility mage who could cast dozens of buff spells without rest.

Although she was countered by Iruki's cancellation, she was a suitable ally for Scramble Royal.

"Guys, I'll be right back."

As he approached where Suabi was, he saw Sabina and Closer, who always hung out with Dante, looking dumbfounded.

"So… you think you like me?"

At Dante's question, Suabi's face turned red as if it would explode.

"No, I mean… I'm not sure. I think I like you, so I wanted to ask you."

Neid, eavesdropping from behind, clicked his tongue.

'This is serious. She's indecisive even about liking someone?'

Also, one more thing to add to Iruki's theory was that if you're good-looking, you don't need to think too much.

Dante, who seemed used to this, responded naturally.

"So, in summary, you want me to tell you if you like me or not?"

Suabi nodded.

"Hmm, well. Even if you ask me that…"

"I'll tell you."

As everyone turned their surprised gazes, Neid raised his hand like a referee and joined the conversation.

"Suabi, you don't like Dante."

"I don't?"

"Of course. You haven't really talked much, have you?"

"But… my heart races."

"That's because you're momentarily infatuated with his looks. Wouldn't someone who approaches you first and talks with you be a much better person for someone as shy as you?"

"Is that so?"

Neid seized the moment to get to the point.

"Yes. Now you understand? Actually, you like me…"

"Thank goodness. Unrequited love is really painful."

"…"

As Neid stood there like a scarecrow, watching Suabi walk away, Dante spoke.

"What are you doing?"

"Shut up! You ruined everything! What are we going to do now?"

"What do you mean? What did I do?"

Sabina slammed her utensils down with a bang.

"Hey, Neid. Come here for a moment."

"No. Why should I?"

Sabina's eyes widened as she gritted her teeth.

"When I say come, you come."

She usually avoided eye contact, but today she was full of authority.

When Neid stepped outside the cafeteria, Sabina, leaning against the wall, interrogated him.

"What have you been up to lately?"

Neid averted his gaze.

"What do you mean? Stop meddling…"

"If you need help, just say so. I'll help you."

"I don't need anything."

Sabina's eyes flashed with irritation, but she sighed and walked past Neid.

"Accept help when it's offered. No wonder you can't get a girlfriend."

"That…!"

Sabina didn't look back as she re-entered the cafeteria, and it took a while for Neid to calm down and return to his seat.

"What did you talk about with Sabina?"

"She offered to help. But I refused."

Iruki looked over at Sabina.

"Why don't you like her? Because she seems like a troublemaker? You're from the central region, but in the capital, there are many girls who dress like that."

"Who said anything? Sabina is fine. I have good taste too."

"Then what's the problem?"

"Sigh, Sabina…"

Neid sighed deeply and swallowed his words.

"She saw my true self."
