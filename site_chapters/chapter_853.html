Chapter 853: Acceleration (4)

Valkyrie Base 73

In a bunker located in the mountains of the southern continent, the Valkyrie high command, all holding VIP status, had gathered.

Humanity's win rate in the wars being fought around the world was less than 7%.

But without these leaders, even that 7% wouldn't have been assured.

“Urgent report! The 7th Brigade of Bornai has been annihilated! Four million demons are crossing the Max Mountain Range.”

On the 3D map on the military table, the flag of the 7th Brigade disappeared.

Then, four red markers, each representing one million units, moved towards the Max Mountain Range.

“Commander-in-Chief, your strategy?”

All the strategists who had reorganized the markers on the military map based on intelligence turned to one person.

Valkyrie Commander-in-Chief Merkodain Iruki.

He had narrowly escaped death after recklessly executing an overdrive in Ex Machina.

He was thinner and his hair had turned gray, but his eyes, fixed on the military map, were unwavering.

‘This is really tough.’

From the start, this was not a war they could win.

The best the commander-in-chief could do was minimize damage and buy time.

The reason he could become the commander-in-chief, overcoming many skilled individuals, was because it was a poisoned chalice.

‘Counterattack is impossible. We need to retreat a bit more.’

But Iruki had won.

The 7% win rate included the efforts of the Five Great Stars of the Ivory Tower, but he had achieved the miraculous feat of securing a victory against Havitz's army.

Electricity sparked in Iruki's eyes.

‘Overdrive.’

The residents of the Ivory Tower, ‘the card players,’ watched Iruki in awe.

‘It's a miracle he's alive.’

Iruki had opened his brain, but strictly speaking, he had been consumed by Ex Machina.

‘That moment of synchronization.’

Iruki thought like Ex Machina.

‘The world of quantum probability.’

In truth, humans shouldn't need to understand that much to win a war.

‘If you cut, it gets cut. If you push, it gets pushed. War is ultimately just the interaction of particles.’

This is called the law.

‘But Satan exists outside the law.’

This is how Iruki defines it.

The state of existing as probability before the outcome is observed is the essence of Havitz.

‘Chaos cannot be predicted.’

But Iruki could establish new strategies from that unpredictable point.

‘It's not that we lose because we deserve to. It's not that we must lose. The moment we respond to Havitz, the event of defeat becomes fixed.’

Only Iruki understood Havitz.

‘Then we just don't observe.’

If they could conduct events outside the maximum radius where a specific outcome could occur for Havitz.

‘We can't win, but we won't lose either.’

That's how they achieved a 7% win rate.

‘As long as we don't touch directly, Havitz's chaos is actually a poison to Balkan's military power.’

Millions had already died, but their sacrifices had gained something significant.

‘The opportunity will surely come.’

Iruki was waiting for the day when Havitz's choice would become a blunder that changed the war.

“Should I call it strange…”

Balkan continued.

“We can lose. Winning and losing in war isn't the main issue. The strange thing is the cause of the defeat. It's like…”

Balkan looked at Havitz.

“It feels like they're thinking like Havitz.”

“Hmmm.”

Smoto stroked his chin.

“They're using the same strategy? But that's only possible because it's Havitz. Anyone else would lose every time.”

“But they won.”

A silence fell.

“It's too deliberate to be a fluke. First, splitting the forces before Kashan. If we had just invaded the central continent, we wouldn't have lost.”

Balkan pinpointed the core issue.

“They anticipated we would split our forces? That doesn't make sense. If they had anticipated it and positioned their army, Havitz wouldn't have split his forces.”

Zetaro nodded.

“He always makes the winning choice. But Havitz split his forces and ended up losing.”

“Exactly.”

Balkan was almost certain.

“Among them, there's someone who can bypass Havitz's events.”

It's not just about distance.

“I don't know how. I don't know how it's possible. But there's no other explanation.”

Natasha asked.

“I don't understand what you're saying, but how dangerous is this for us?”

“In my opinion…”

Just as Balkan was about to speak.

“Should we stop?”

Havitz, who had been squatting, raised his head to the sky and spoke softly.

“What? Havitz?”

Balkan asked in disbelief.

“Should we stop here? I suddenly feel like it.”

It was a statement that completely negated their actions so far, but that was precisely why it was so Havitz-like.

Balkan asked.

“Why?”

“Where else in the world is there a game more fun than this? But…”

Havitz yawned.

“I'm starting to feel bored.”

Zetaro spoke with a regretful expression.

“I'll come up with a more fun way to kill.”

“Come to think of it… humans are really pitiful.”

His companions' eyes widened.

‘Havitz feeling pity?’

It was impossible.

“For humans, life is everything. As long as they can live, fun doesn't matter.”

Smoto realized.

“Is it Uorin?”

“Uorin said she loved Shirone. Did that change something in you?”

“Not really…”

It was fun and thrilling, so he had no regrets about sacrificing his wife, Abella.

“It's just boring.”

Havitz, who had everything in the world, couldn't escape loneliness.

‘I want to play.’

He just… thought of Abella.

‘I want to play.’

Havitz pursed his lips towards the sky.

“Moooo.”

As he made a sound like a cow, the four knights of Gustav looked at each other.

“Moooo.”

A dry cry without a single tear.

The sorrow of extreme evil.

‘A day’s time.’

Shirone pondered Seongeum's words.

Wars were raging in various countries, and countless people were dying even now.

But Jincheon was no different, so there must be a reason for Seongeum's proposal.

“Why?”

Shirone asked.

“There’s plenty of time after the war. Why is spending a day with me so important?”

Seongeum kept her lips tightly shut.

‘I’m sorry, Shirone.’

The reason she couldn’t reveal was closely tied to the safety of the Jincheon Empire.

“I know how important the war in the central continent is to you.”

Amy.

“Your beloved, and your homeland, the Kingdom of Tormia, are also close to the front lines. I know it’s an unreasonable request. But you promised me ten days.”

Seongeum looked straight at Shirone.

“That’s because my ability bought you those ten days. Trust me. If you give me one day, I’ll find a way to buy you even more time.”

Knowing Seongeum’s upright character, there was no need to doubt her words.

It bothered him that he didn’t know the reason, but time was passing even as they spoke.

“Alright.”

Seongeum’s face brightened dramatically.

“Thank you. Really, thank you.”

“But what do you plan to do in that one day? Do you have something in mind?”

“As you know, ether waves can disturb the spatial field within spacetime. So I can go anywhere I want, but…”

Seongeum looked up with a wistful expression.

“Looking back, I realized I’ve never really been anywhere.”

Shirone stared at her.

“I don’t have any grand plans. Just for one day… help me see the world.”

‘She’s planning to die.’

Otherwise, there was no reason for Seongeum, who was a candidate for the Ivory Tower, to suddenly act like this.

“Alright.”

Knowing how high her karma was, Shirone decided to follow her wishes for now.

“Go wherever you want. I’ll follow you anywhere.”

“Then…”

Seongeum glanced at Rian standing behind Shirone.

“Go ahead. I’ll wait here.”

Sensing some resolve in her, Rian got up from the table and sat on the bed.

Seongeum, showing her gratitude with a small nod, cast an ether wave.

Their bodies were sucked into the space, and soon a completely different landscape appeared.

“Where is this?”

It was a valley with a small waterfall.

Sitting on a rock, Seongeum took a deep breath and smiled contentedly.

“I’ve never been to a place like this with someone.”

Because she hadn’t allowed anyone close.

“Looking back… I wonder why I kept such distance.”

Shirone sat next to Seongeum.

“You can change from now on. When the war ends, there will be days when we can all be happy together.”

“Maybe…”

Persuasion was impossible after all.

“Tell me. What are you thinking…”

Before Shirone could finish, Seongeum leaned in and kissed him softly.

Shirone’s eyes widened in surprise, and after a while, Seongeum’s lips parted.

Only the sound of the waterfall could be heard.

“I’m sorry.” Seongeum lowered her head.

“It’s okay. I was just a bit surprised.”

“I heard you have someone you love. I’ve hurt you. Please forget it.”

That might be true, but Seongeum seemed to be overthinking things.

It was because she was pure.

“Why did you do that?”

“I always thought… if I had someone to walk beside me, in a quiet place like this…”

Seongeum changed the subject.

“We competed together for the star of the Ivory Tower. You, me, and Nane.” It was an unforgettable memory for Shirone as well.

“Yeah. Back then, I never imagined the world would change like this.”

“You pursued philanthropy, Nane pursued emptiness. Each of you upheld your beliefs to the end. It might sound strange, but sometimes I feel proud of you all.”

“You’re strong too. And you’re still fighting for the world.”

“…Am I?”

Shirone’s compliment made her feel better.

“Time and space are essentially one field. If you’re fighting for the world, that’s enough for me.”

She wasn’t afraid.

“Seongeum, if you’re having bad thoughts…”

“The day has just begun.” Seongeum stood up.

“I don’t want to think about it right now. Let’s enjoy this moment a bit more. Let’s go somewhere else.”

“What? Already?”

“I have a lifetime’s worth of wishes to fulfill. I don’t want to miss a single place.”

“I don’t mind if it takes longer.”

He didn’t want to pressure her.

“No. One day is enough. But I have a favor to ask.”

Seongeum extended her hand to Shirone. “Will you hold my hand?”

Shirone looked at her slender fingers and slowly took her hand.

‘No matter what you’re thinking… I won’t let go of this hand in the end.’
