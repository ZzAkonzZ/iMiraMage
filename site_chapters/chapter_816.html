Chapter 816: Dark Clan (4)

Genocide's hideout was a small cabin in the forest beyond the city walls.

Though it was a cozy place with a fireplace burning logs, descending underground revealed a fortress equipped with sophisticated machinery.

There was a passage leading to an armory and, on the opposite side, a machine room.

"Welcome back. Did everything go well?" A woman with grease on her face emerged from the machine room.

She had short, boyish red hair and a dirty towel around her neck.

"Oh, who's this?"

Xenia didn't trust anything uncertain.

"Not sure yet. We met on the way. Where's Dr. Grain?"

"In the medical room. He seems to be developing a new drug."

Entering the end of the hallway, they found a space larger than the cabin, filled with medical facilities.

A white-bearded doctor, who had been looking through a microscope, turned his chair around.

"Ah, our vanguard is back."

Dr. Grain.

A doctor and clinical trial expert with Genocide, he knew everything about vampires.

"I see a new face. Surely... not a vampire?"

It was a common joke, but this time the group's reaction wasn't so friendly.

"That's why we brought him. This man is called the Knight of Maha. At least, that's what he claims."

"Oh? That makes him even more suspicious. Cutting down ten thousand demons isn't within the realm of living beings."

"Enough with the nonsense. Can a human whose heart has stopped come back to life?"

Grain looked at Rian for an explanation.

"What do you mean?"

"Actually..."

Having heard about Grain on the way to the hideout, Rian also expressed his curiosity.

"Hmm, you say you hear Smiling's hallucination and your wounds heal, even from a state of death."

"Yes. It seems I've forgotten how to die."

Grain nodded.

"That's unusual. But it's not entirely unique. Immortality is a fundamental characteristic of life."

"You mean, not dying?"

"What is death? We don't say a stone has died when it breaks. Life also came from inanimate matter. If you trace back to the primordial life, there's no concept of death."

Rian listened silently.

"Why didn't life evolve into immortality? Because of viruses. Tiny entities between life and non-life infect specific information."

Grain clapped his hands.

"Do you understand? Without changing information, life would go extinct due to viruses. Mutation alone can't defend against them. So life adopted a unique method of combining information. By combining male and female information, new information is created, and old information is discarded."

"That's death."

"As long as viruses exist, old information must be discarded, and new information must be endlessly produced. This is why animals avoid inbreeding. Combining old information doesn't help fight viruses."

Grain pointed at Rian.

"Smiling's hallucination, your grandfather heard it too. But you don't know who Smiling is."

"No."

"You might know subconsciously. Even if it's not in your memory, it could be imprinted in your genes."

"Genes?"

"The deeper you go into the world of particles, the more you realize there's no analog in this world. Life is just a collection of minimal information units. I think your phenomenon is a very old genetic information responding to specific stimuli."

Xenia asked,

"If that information is activated, does it mean not dying?"

"It's possible. As I said, death wasn't originally a function of life."

Rian asked,

"Is it possible to bring out the information recorded in those genes into my mind?"

He wanted to know.

Who was the owner of this voice, and why did Ymir know about Ogent?

"I don't think it's impossible." Grain stood up.

"The hallucination you hear is also genetic-level memory infiltrating your mind. But to delve deeper, a complex process is needed."

"What kind of complex process?"

"Genetic information isn't human language. But the brain is a perfect interpreter. If specific stimuli trigger memories, you can vary the direction and intensity of those stimuli."

Grain was an expert.

"It might be beyond what a living being can handle. Even if you regenerate, the pain is real. So, how far have you experienced?"

"Well, I've experienced my heart stopping many times." Saying this, Rian looked at Xenia, who shrugged as if she now believed him.

"Have you ever been decapitated?"

"I've never been completely separated."

Grain pursed his lips.

"If I were to experiment on you, I'd start by cutting off your head. I want to know the limits of your recovery."

"My brain has been damaged before."

Grain shook his head.

"That's different. The body is a combination of essential functions, none more important than the others. Non-essential parts have already been eliminated. Without experience, it's a dangerous attempt. You wouldn't want that either."

Rian wanted to know about Smile's hallucination but couldn't risk dying meaninglessly.

"I still have things to do."

"Right, it's a last resort. Stay here and try various things."

Rian asked,

"Then what about vampires? Did they activate immortality at the genetic level?"

"No. Quite the opposite." Grain looked at Rian.

"Strictly speaking, they're not living beings. They're viruses. They inject specific information into humans to control them or absorb their information to regenerate. Everything follows the virus mechanism. They're semi-spiritual beings, neither living nor non-living."

"Did the virus evolve?"

"No. They were originally living beings." Grain's thoughts traveled back in time.

"A long time ago... there was a bat that realized incarnation and gained a human body."

It was an old story.

"Like any human, he obsessed over immortality and eventually achieved it. But he wasn't satisfied. No matter how infinite the lifespan, he couldn't prevent individual extinction. That's why I can't cut off your head."

A living being can be destroyed by a powerful force.

"He wanted to exist until the end of the world. Then he found the most efficient way by tracing back to the origin of life."

"A virus."

"Yes. Semi-spiritual. By giving up half of his physical form, he became an undying virus."

Grain's eyes gleamed with malice.

"That's the origin of vampires. Currently ranked third in the Ten Lords, Jinma Faust."

"...You know about Heaven."

"I don't know much. What I know is from tracing the vampire lineage. Faust, who achieved true immortality, naturally lost his reproductive ability. Instead, he injects a special prion from his fangs to enforce obedience."

"You mean purebloods?"

"When Jinma stayed in our world, he injected three humans. They became the Lords who rule vampire society."

Grain drew a lineage in the air with his finger.

"The Lords created dozens of Almas ranks, and Almas created thousands of Vesicas. The further down the lineage, the more they diverge from Jinma. But one day, someone in the Vesica rank mutated."

"What kind of mutation?"

"They gained reproductive ability."

"A Vesica who fell in love with a human woman had a child with her. Surprisingly, the child also had vampire traits. We call them half-demons. But purebloods call them this."

Grain raised his index finger.

"Hybrids."

Xenia's face darkened.

"Many hybrids spread across the world. Various crossbreeds like werewolves emerged. Of course, breeding among half-demons was much more common." Grain glanced at Xenia.

"In that process, a mutant with abilities even pureblood vampires couldn't achieve was born."

"What kind of...?"

"Just as viruses have unique wavelengths, vampires have special wavelengths. This mutant could accurately sense and track those wavelengths."

The biggest reason vampires couldn't be eradicated was their indistinguishability from humans.

"That's an incredible ability."

"Yes. That's how vampire hunters were born. Their characteristic is silver hair from birth. So we call them Silver Born."

Only then did Rian look at Xenia.

"Hmph, what?"

Grain said,

"Yes. Xenia is a Silver Born. Her skin burns in ultraviolet light, and she has a low ability to detoxify silver, but her body regenerates through blood, and she has physical abilities comparable to vampires." At that moment, the woman from the machine room, engineer Facet, entered the medical room.

"But she eats human food instead of blood and has reproductive ability. She doesn't have the bat incarnation either."

The bat mimicry, one of the vampire abilities, was passed down from Jinma Faust's incarnation.

"Facet, are you done with your work?"

"Counting bullets isn't much work. Anyway, with the money from this job, I increased the silver ratio in the alloy. The paralysis effect will be 10% stronger."

Fowler bumped his fists together.

"Haha! I can't wait to shoot! I want to see the look on the vampire's face when they get hit!"

Facet extended her hand to Rian.

"Nice to meet you. I don't know what's going on, but it seems the misunderstanding is cleared. I'm engineer Facet. I modify firearms."

"Firearms?"

Drug expert Katain said,

"There are three main ways to deal with vampires: firearms, drugs, and metals. They all target weaknesses."

Facet continued,

"Firearms use silver. It slows down vampire recovery. Drugs use a substance called Anticell, made by Dr. Grain. And metals—" Xenia drew a black metallic rapier.

"Dark metal. Made with an alloy containing Obscura B."

"Obscura..."

It was a material used in video recorders, rare and handled by the state.

"Vampires can't penetrate Obscura B."

Facet said,

"It's incredibly expensive. It's a mineral that forms only in ultra-low temperature dark rooms and can't be made with alchemy. So it's used in metals, not firearms. A skilled swordsman is much more efficient with metals than with firearms."

Xenia smiled, a rare sight.

"What effects does Anticell have?"

"It basically coagulates blood and destroys cells. Vampires are defenseless against cell-level attacks."

Grain held up a syringe.

"But you need various preparations to deal with them. If you get a shot of the power pack I developed, it has pain-relieving effects and enhances muscle strength. It also increases agility by strengthening the nervous system."

There would be side effects.

"Does everyone fight using that?"

"No, Xenia is an exception. For half-demons, there's something much more effective than the power pack."

Xenia took out a slender device.

"Blood Q (Menthol)."

It was a tool that heated blood to produce vapor for inhalation.
