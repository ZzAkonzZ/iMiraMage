Chapter 1187: The Terminus of Humanity (1)

The Apocalypse excavation team stood before the androids, unable to determine whether they were friend or foe.

“What are you? Humans or machines?”

“We are Zet,” the lead android responded.

“We are those who have attained enlightenment of the universe’s principles. Beings free from all suffering—perfect and flawless.”

“Run,” Shirone said.

At his command, the beings who called themselves Zet assumed a half-palm stance.

“Salvation of all beings.”

As Sanskrit characters appeared on their visors, metallic spheres engraved with Sanskrit formed around them.

Tanhwa—Conversion.

A method by which machines imposed themselves upon living beings.

“Form is emptiness. Emptiness is form.”

The spheres shot forward at blinding speed, bombarding the area with explosions that rippled through the city.

“Ugh…!”

Hiding behind Miracle Stream, the excavation team gasped at the unscathed ground beneath them.

“Damn it! What the hell is this city made of?”

“Law,” Shirone answered.

“This city is reinforced by Law itself. Our will cannot influence it.”

Perhaps it was truly flawless.

“Go.”

Shirone activated Hand of God.

“I’ll hold them off. You must retrieve the data. We need to understand the cause of this.”

Marsha hesitated.

“But how? This world… there’s nothing left. It’s been completely erased.”

Even the artifact she had been holding had long since crumbled to dust.

“There must be a database—a repository for the Law. Find the main system and hack into it.”

Marsha looked incredulous.

“That’s Buddha you’re talking about! You’re telling us to hack Buddha?”

The Zet, displaying Sanskrit characters across their visors, began analyzing Shirone’s Hand of God.

“The pinnacle of delusion,” the leading Zet spoke.

“Suffering beings, follow us. Delusion stems from ignorance—once you grasp the truth of the universe, your doubts will vanish, and suffering will cease.”

“… You want to turn me into a machine?”

“The distinction between life and death is merely an attachment. You need only accept the one true truth.”

One truth.

As I suspected. It’s not the mind that has been unified, but the Law itself. And if this world is the result of that…

Then the gods have won.

No, nothing has been decided yet.

If a variable arose anywhere within the Five Great Systems, then even Apocalypse would be altered.

If this is a world that has reached the pinnacle of Law, hacking the database might be impossible for now…

But if Shirone’s Sphere was completed, If the Twelve Apostles won, If the Under World was purified—

Or anything happens!

Shirone clenched his fists.

“The battle isn’t over. Humanity—Mankind—will stand against the Law.”

“Desire only brings suffering. Liberate yourself from attachment and open the gates to paradise.”

Though their tone was gentle, the Zet summoned spheres brimming with a sinister Law around them.

“The cessation of desire leads to enlightenment.”

As the excavation team fled, Shirone intercepted the spheres with Hand of God.

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

Each explosion of Law made his mind reel.

This is… overwhelming.

Perhaps not as extreme as Nane’s sermon, but the enlightenment of machines was a force in itself.

As Shirone defended against their attacks with the power of the mind, the Zet expressed their emotions through a silent pause.

“… King of all sentient beings.”

A single Sanskrit symbol filled their visors.

“Om.”

From between the buildings, countless Zet emerged, chanting the same phrase in unison.

“Ooooooom.”

Their sheer numbers sent cold sweat down Shirone’s spine.

I won’t last long against this…

As the press conference for Gis approached, the protests outside Delta Headquarters grew even louder.

“Abdicate! The King of Jive must step down!”

“Remove the corrupt ruler who squanders the people’s taxes! Down with the oppressors!”

Amid the deafening shouts, Gis stood at the window of his office, glaring at the crowd.

“Filthy scum!”

Jaw clenched and teeth grinding, his right hand was busy pleasuring himself.

“Scream louder, yeah? Get me more excited.”

Across the room, Myron, reviewing the press release, adjusted his glasses and approached.

“The draft is ready. Please review it.”

“Hahh… Hahh…!”

Panting and shaking his upper body, Gis snatched the documents from Myron’s hands.

“Shit! Give me a shake.”

“… Yes, sir.”

As Myron reluctantly assisted, his sharp snake-like eyes skimmed over the contents of the report.

“What the hell is this? Who wrote this draft?”

“The secretary’s office…”

“No, never mind. Does this make sense? It says I should cry while talking about my daughter’s death?”

“We thought it would invoke empathy—”

“I know that, dumbass! But this is too obvious! Do you have any idea how sharp citizens have become these days?”

Gis slammed the papers on the desk.

"Whoever it is, a reporter is going to ask about it, right? That’s when I suddenly get choked up… Huh? Why can’t you read the flow of the times? This is why you’ll never get ahead."

"I apologize."

"The pain of losing a child is unlike anything in the world—erase that line. It’s too aggressive. What, are parents whose kids didn’t die supposed to feel left out? It sounds like I’m flaunting my kid’s death."

"Understood."

"And don’t use inversion in the sentences. The parts where I stutter need to be more spread out, not all crammed into the end…"

As Gis reached for a pen on the desk, Myron scurried over in a crouch.

"Forget it. Just revise it like this."

"Yes, sir."

Freed from his… other role, Myron took the documents. Gis walked back to the window.

The voices of the citizens rang out.

"Abdicate, Your Majesty! Step down!"

"Heh… It’s driving you nuts, isn’t it? But the more you protest, the more I’ll take for myself. You lot can chew on this and—ah…!"

Gis twitched and trembled.

"Ughhh…!"

Myron, still revising the speech, glanced at Gis’s convulsing back.

Is he even human?

How little must he think of others to do something like this without feeling a shred of shame?

It’s the same reason people don’t feel embarrassed undressing in front of animals.

To Gis, humans are…

Livestock. Or perhaps even less.

"Phew, that felt good. Is the revision done?"

"Ah, yes."

Gis pulled up his pants and walked over as Myron politely handed him the revised document.

"Good. We’ll go with this."

"The press conference is in ten minutes. We should head to the venue. The Royal Guards will handle security."

The conference hall at Delta Headquarters was packed not only with journalists but also with dignitaries from various nations.

Albino murmured, "If Gis is here, it means the cleanup is done. He’ll want to wash his hands of everything here, but…"

Thormia’s trap was still intact.

"His Majesty, the King, is entering!"

As the journalists all rose, Gis stepped onto the podium with a solemn expression.

"Good day, citizens of the capital, people of the noble territories. I stand before you today…"

When his speech ended, the flood of questions began.

"Is it true that you attended a secret banquet? There are claims of perverse acts."

"That is not true."

"So, are you saying the reports are false? There was a death involved. Please be honest."

"Of course, there was a banquet. However, it was a routine gathering, as commonly held in the Holy War. There were no sexual entertainments, nor was there any confirmed death."

Unless someone deliberately leaked information, everything that happened in the Holy War remained top secret.

"Are you truly denying it ever happened?"

"Ladies and gentlemen, this Holy War determines the future of humanity. In such a setting, does it make sense for the world’s rulers to engage in the kind of debauchery you’re suggesting?"

No one knows.

Because no one here has ever been king.

"There are rumors that Princess Rebecca has died. Can you confirm this?"

For a brief moment, the blood drained from Gis’s face.

As his daughter’s face flashed in his mind, his lips trembled, and his chest ached as if it were being torn apart.

"My daughter… huff…!"

Gis did not suppress his emotions. He sobbed openly.

And that was all it took.

Albino’s gaze turned icy.

He isn’t emotionless.

No, to be precise, he understands emotions.

It’s true that he mourns his daughter’s death.

But… so what?

Gis thought to himself.

So what if my kid died? So what if I’m sad? What do you want me to do about it? Skip meals?

Albino muttered, "He feels emotions, but that’s as far as it goes. Because he can see emotions objectively, he uses them. Shame, conscience—even the grief over his daughter’s death."

"That’s what makes him strong."

This was the pinnacle of psychological murder.

"In natural selection, predators evolved fangs. If human society is also a survival-of-the-fittest arena, then the lack of emotions is a form of evolution."

"When you look at history, all rulers were ruthless."

"Exactly. If food and reproduction drove animal evolution, then for humans, it’s wealth and power. Those are forces beyond mere fangs. Emotions get stripped away faster, and the 1.5-generation humans are born."

"So in the end… only those kinds of people will remain?"

"Who knows? I understand that it contradicts humanity’s ideals. But if you put 100 first-generation humans and 100 of the 1.5 generation in the same space, what do you think will happen? The first-generation would be wiped out in an instant."

There was no denying it.

"Emotions. That fragile word weakens humans more than anything else. And every human knows it. Yet they still can’t let it go. Just as herbivores love grass, emotions are why humans live. But predators? They have a different palate."

"They don’t eat grass—they eat the ones that eat the grass."

"Exactly. Just like now."

Hiding his abyssal thoughts, Gis continued speaking.

"I take full responsibility for any distress I have caused. Therefore, here and now…"

He bowed deeply.

"I sincerely ask for your forgiveness. If you grant me another chance, I will prove myself in the Holy War."

A king apologizing in public—an unprecedented event. The journalists’ pens practically flew across their pages.

Albino muttered, "That was the finishing blow."

Meanwhile, Gis, still bowing, felt euphoric, as if he were soaring.

Here, take it. The food you all love so much.

Feels amazing, right? This is a first, isn’t it?

A king apologizing to his citizens.

You people are happy as long as your pride is acknowledged.

But not me.

I need to have the most expensive things in the world. I need to own every woman I like.

I need to indulge in luxuries that commoners could never dream of affording in their lifetime—every single day.

And yet, pride?

What a joke.

You idiots, do you know what a spine and a neck are for? They exist to bend. To pick things up from the ground.

Even a three-year-old can do it.

And you idiots attach meaning to something so easy?

Ah, this is good.

The thought of standing above them all again made his lust boil over.

I am the king. I will take everything.

Why?

Because I have the power to do so.

A reporter shouted, "Do not mock us! The citizens are not foolish enough to fall for a mere political gesture!"

Another journalist retorted, "Watch your words! He is still the King of Jive! Show proper respect!"

Hurry up and wrap this up.

I’ve got better things to do.

Soon, the citizens’ reactions to the articles would explode into chaos.

But Gis didn’t care.

Whatever is said…

There will never be one unified opinion.
