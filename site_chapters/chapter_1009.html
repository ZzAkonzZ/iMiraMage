Chapter 1009: Shadow of God (1)

Upon reaching the mountain, Amy discovered a special place with signs of battle.

“She’s gone.”

Maya was nowhere to be seen, and a loud explosion was heard from over the next mountain.

“She’s quite fast.”

As Amy prepared to cast a spatial teleportation spell, a flash of tension crossed her eyes.

Twisting her body, a hooded man approached and threw a punch at her.

“Ugh!”

As she defended with both arms, it felt as though fire had ignited in her solar plexus before it exited through her back.

“Gasp!”

“Mr. Giyorgi really…”

The person who had passed through Amy flipped his hood back and muttered.

“He’s obsessed with Yahweh, but he shouldn’t have sent a comrade.”

Meanwhile, Amy tore at her collar, examining the pained area.

“What is this?”

There was a darkly burnt trace between her ribs that gradually faded over time.

Amy turned around.

“What are you?”

With red skin, perky ears, and more bristles on his forehead, he almost resembled a fire ant.

“Gultan. An assassin specialized for combat against Yahweh. Truthfully, I prefer not to fight anyone other than Yahweh…”

Catacomb’s selection criteria were based on individuals who could perform at 6.8 times their normal abilities in combat against Yahweh.

“But I’m still a division commander.”

As Gultan circled widely to approach, Amy followed him with her eyes. Realizing why her previous defense had failed, she noticed him moving through thick trees.

“He can phase through matter.”

Amy quickly leaned back as Gultan’s fist whizzed past her face.

“Ho?”

Interest flickered in Gultan’s eyes.

“You also have remarkable physical abilities.”

Through wars with demons, Amy’s schema had reached a high level.

“Fine.”

A giant of fire erupted from her body, roaring flames towards Gultan.

The area within dozens of meters was engulfed in fire, yet laughter came from behind Amy.

“Ha!”

Simultaneously, a hot force struck her back.

“Gasp!”

Since it hit from behind, all she could see was black smoke emerging from her chest.

“I had no time to dodge. How did this happen?”

The ability to phase through matter.

“It’s the ground.”

“Yes. Physical barriers are irrelevant. By the way, this makes it the second time.”

A bad premonition washed over her.

“What do you mean?”

“It means I’ve touched your heart twice. Oh, don't worry. You won't die.”

Gultan’s skill was fine manipulation.

He transformed his body into quantum states to phase through targets, then shaped their minds.

“My heart… what do you mean?”

“Craftsmanship is a delicate operation. To create the desired form, I need to touch it multiple times.”

Amy just listened.

“But a skilled craftsman can get the rough shape with a few touches. Meaning, I’ve roughly got the framework after two touches.”

“Framework?”

Gultan spread his arms wide, smiling.

“I assure you, by the end of this fight, you’ll be madly in love with me.”

That was also a key tactic of Catacomb to weaken Yahweh’s powers.

“Damn!”

The ominous part was, since the second strike, her hostility towards Gultan had decreased.

“These guys are no joke.”

They had meticulously analyzed Yahweh.

“I know you have the ability called Scarlet Eyes. Investigating a target's close associates is basic for an assassin. But it's useless. The heart I touch lies much deeper than the mind.”

Amy tried to deny it.

“No, that can’t be. How could I ever love a demon that looks like a fire ant?”

“Such a repulsive reaction. I don’t particularly like you either. Human women are fun to torment but not really my taste.”

“Can it be reversed?”

“There’s no fixed form to a heart. It might change over time, but can you really escape it? The third touch will be quite detailed.”

“No.”

Amy’s eyes blazed red.

“Try all you want. You won’t touch my body again.”

“I’ll have to fix that attitude first.”

Gultan submerged into the ground, reappearing behind Amy to raise his fist.

“You can’t dodge this.”

The reason Amy had allowed the strike before wasn’t due to slow reflexes.

“Quantum.”

During his phasing, time and distance were irrelevant.

There were conditions and constraints, but they weren't important given Amy’s current situation.

“I’ll touch your heart.”

Just as his fist was about to contact Amy’s back.

“What?”

Her body dispersed into fire, causing Gultan to stagger forward.

“Spontaneous combustion.”

Standing with flames swirling around one hand, Amy’s appearance caused Gultan’s expression to turn grave.

“Fire movement technique? This is new.” To confirm, Gultan attacked again, but only caught remnants of heat in his hand.

“Got it.”

As fire rose where he turned, Amy flickered like a mirage.

“Her movement speed isn’t fast. The real problem is her reaction speed.”

Gultan’s attempt to extinguish her fire had snuffed it out like a candle.

“And to ignite a new flame, to rekindle immediately. Her movement speed might be similar to convection, but…”

During movement, she was nearly invincible.

“What are you waiting for? Not going to attack?”

As Amy fluttered her hand, Gultan clenched his teeth.

“There must be a specific interval where she reacts to air movement. Either move very slowly…”

Or fast enough to pass through the wind.

“Punching faster than the speed of sound is too much for me. At least before I make a move, she can’t ignite either, which is comforting.”

He thought so.

“If you’re not going to attack, can I?”

Amy recalled her identity as a magician.

“Crimson Flame.”

Flames climbed her body and gathered into a massive sphere high in the sky, large enough to lose sense of distance, making Gultan gape.

“Wait! Let’s negotiate!”

Before he could get a response, a fireball of 6,000 degrees Celsius smashed into the peak.

The mountain burst into flames instantly.

“Aaah!”

The power of the magic was enough to burn even the caster, but Amy was smiling.

“To extinguish the fire…”

Flames whirled down the ridges like a river, forming a giant vortex that pierced the sky.

"What’s that?"

Shirone, locked in a battle of yin and yang with Giyorgi, spotted the towering flame pillar on the mountain.

"Amy’s Complete Combustion."

If she was using the pinnacle of fire magic, her opponent must be formidable.

"Don’t overthink it. Just trust her."

That’s what comrades do.

"More importantly..."

Among the myriad sounds in the Tai Chi domain, one phrase stuck in Shirone's mind.

They are watching you. Trust nothing. Save yourself from the terrible truth.

The voice didn’t belong to the citizens.

"Not Giyorgi either."

Then who could have said such a thing?

Shirone concluded, "No one in this city could have said that."

"An existence outside this world?"

As if assembling a message by cutting and pasting newspaper letters.

"A message for me."

The fact that it appeared during the breakdown of the laws lent weight to this hypothesis.

"Alright, let’s think. First, they are watching me. Who are they? Why not reveal themselves?"

Is this some kind of censorship?

"And trust nothing. If this world is fake, it sounds like the philosophy of Xing."

She believes she is the only human.

"No, I’m right. No matter what the world is, wherever the heart is cast is real. That’s undeniable..."

Suddenly, a chill ran down her spine.

"Save yourself from the terrible truth."

What truth could be so horrifying that the messenger used such words?

She didn’t know.

But one thing was clear—the message pierced through to the deepest part of this world.

"Such insight."

The Tai Chi domain expanded beyond the city, encroaching upon the vast lands.

Cayden lay in the underbrush, gravely wounded, screaming in despair.

"Aaaaaah! Stop it!"

Watching Miyo torment Maya was hell.

"Hohoho! Why did you resist?"

"Cayden..."

Maya’s eyes had lost focus, yet she maintained her sanity and her will.

"Run. I’m... it’s okay."

"Aaaaaah!"

Cayden’s anguish deepened, and he slammed his face into the ground.

"Move! Move, damn it!"

Unless he could let go of Maya in his heart, he would remain powerless.

"I don’t care if I die! No, I’ll give up on Maya! Let her go! Let her go!"

Even if he never saw her again.

"Let go, let go..."

But the heart doesn’t move as commanded, and all he could do was flail helplessly.

"Damn it! Why can’t I... why..."

No one could blame him.

"Kekeke! You’re pathetic. What can you do, so weak and helpless?"

Just then, flames engulfed the mountain, and Miyo’s laughter ceased instantly.

"What?"

Faced with the unexpected, Miyo finally decided to execute Catacomb's strategy.

"Fine. I’ll release you from your misery."

Miyo grabbed Maya’s face and began forcing her hand into her mouth.

"Gah! Guh!"

Watching Maya struggle, Miyo said,

"But I’m taking your voice."

That would drive Yahweh mad.

"No, no!"

Cayden snapped out of his daze and screamed, but there was no way to stop her.

"I’ll rip out her voice and present it as a gift to Yahweh."

"Guh! Guh!"

Maya wept as Cayden slammed his fists into the ground.

"Move! Move, damn it!"

With nowhere left to retreat, he couldn’t figure out how to regain control.

Bloodshot eyes like spiderwebs spread across Cayden’s vision as he pushed the ground with all his might.

"Let her go! Let her go!"

But he couldn’t. If he didn’t, Maya would—dying wouldn’t matter—saving Maya was all that mattered.

Miyo looked back at Cayden as she shoved her fist into Maya’s mouth and grinned.

"Now, for the grand finale!"

"Aaaaagh!"

Driven mad by fear, Cayden’s vision blurred, and Miyo’s voice grew distant.

"Hoho! This beautiful voice will be mine..."

Rip.

A tearing sound echoed through Cayden’s mind.

"What?"

The next moment, Miyo glanced down at her state with an incredulous expression.

"Why?"

Her arm had been cleanly severed.

"Hah... hah..."

Panting heavily, Cayden recalled the moment.

"What happened?"

Turning his stiffened face, he saw the sword that had cut Miyo’s arm.

"I... I cut her?"

Though it was clearly his arm and his senses were intact, it felt as if it had escaped the control of his mind.
