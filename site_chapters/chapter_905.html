Chapter 905: Face to Face (2)

Shura's eyes were bleary.

"Just kill me instead."

As Nane continued his irreverent ranting throughout the night, even the mountains and forests trembled.

Although Shura was stuck on the outskirts of a shelter created by Gestalt’s power, the noise seemed not to diminish at all.

Kururururu.

Feeling a vibration rolling in from afar, Shura regained her senses after a long time.

"Huh?"

As the seismic waves rushed in rapidly, they burrowed toward where Shura was.

As Gestalt collapsed and the veil of deception lifted, the mountain range stretching to Vashuka became visible.

"What's happening?"

Having followed Buddha and learned much about the world, she guessed:

"This isn't a natural occurrence."

If such a strong earthquake had started at the coast, a tsunami should have swept over the continent by now.

"It’s the power of breaking the system." Several figures crossed Shura's mind.

"Shirone, Rian, Gaold…"

But recalling anyone who could cause a continent-shaking earthquake seemed insufficient.

"The heavens have opened."

It was natural that figures from Heaven, almost like her homeland, came to mind first.

"I need to tell him."

Even though Nane was still preoccupied with his chanting, Shura kicked off the ground and ran toward him.

Inside the cabin where Amy lay unconscious, Nane's voice filled the air.

"Mahaprajna…"

The words leaping from Nane’s mouth seemed to accumulate like mass-bearing matter.

"Avalokiteshvara…"

Just as Nane’s voice abruptly stopped, a slight vibration shook the building.

"Um."

It wasn’t just the earthquake that interrupted him.

Despite being overwhelmed by numerous enlightenments, Amy’s eyes slowly opened as if receiving a signal.

"… are you awake?"

Confused, she didn’t realize that Nane’s voice was speaking in human language.

"Where am I?"

Feeling like a limbless creature, her eyes moved side to side.

"I’m sure..."

Terrible memories flooded in.

She had encountered Commander Parkma during an escape attempt with her comrades and they had all been annihilated.

"Beric."

She wasn’t supposed to die like this.

"I’m the only survivor." Grateful for a man’s kindness that saved her life, Amy bit her lip.

"What kind of soldier am I?"

Suddenly, Nane’s face appeared in her vision.

"Are you okay?" Amy stared at him for a moment.

"Who is he?"

His face, marked with numerous tattoos, was unrecognizable.

But his eyes resembled someone dear she knew.

"Shirone."

As this thought reached her, all the information flowed into a conclusion.

"Nane!"

Looking down at the startled Amy, Nane smiled kindly.

"Yes, it’s me, that Nane."

Amy quickly sat up.

"Ah!"

Immediately, she felt as if her muscles were on fire, and she collapsed back onto the bed.

"You are like a newborn child. Not only will you feel pain, but moving forcibly could harm your body."

"Why are you here?"

The era’s Buddha.

She couldn’t look kindly at someone who judged the world as empty and sought to close the universe.

Especially since he stood diametrically opposed to Shirone, he was clearly an enemy.

"Your condition isn’t good yet."

Nane covered her with a blanket and stood up to walk to the door.

"I’ll prepare some food. Until then…"

Pausing, he slowly turned around.

"Hah. Hah."

Amy, who had gotten off the bed, stood quivering with pain.

Gritting her teeth, she spoke.

"Answer me. Why are you here? What have you done to me?"

Nane, grinning, replied.

"Well, this and that…"

But as Amy’s expression turned icy, he quickly corrected himself.

"You were consumed by the blaze. It was powerful enough to even burn consciousness. So, I woke you."

"Woke me?"

Recollections of that time came to Amy.

In her grief over Beric’s death, she had given up on life and raised her firepower.

"How did you wake me?"

Explaining would take a long time, so Nane turned again and said,

"Rest first. I’ll bring you something to eat."

Though it seemed true that Nane had helped her, Amy remained silent for a moment but then glared fiercely at him.

"To eliminate Buddha." Hadn’t Nane’s existence resulted in all this? Of course, he had also played a crucial role in her life’s separation from Shirone.

Her eyes blazed red, and a great flame of energy rose in her hand.

It seemed as though she had wiped all the dust from her spirit, increasing her power significantly.

"I can’t help being ruthless!"

Aiming solely to subdue Nane, Amy reached out her hand.

"Die…"

With a thud, a sword pierced right through Amy’s solar plexus.

"Sermon. Thorough."

The sword, emitting a blood-red light, dispersed like an illusion, and sparks flew before Amy’s eyes.

"Aaaaah!"

Unimaginable pain struck, and her knees automatically hit the floor.

Hearing a scream from outside, Shura burst through the door shouting.

"Nane??!"

As she stood speechless at the scene unfolding before her, Nane turned to Amy.

"Yahweh creates from nothing, but Buddha realizes what is. If it's painful..."

"Uuuuuugh!"

Cradling her arms and raising her head, Am’s gaze remained unyielding.

"If you think that’s my pain."

Having experienced everything in this world, Nane's voice acted as the truth.

Shura approached.

"When did you wake up?"

"I regained consciousness just a moment ago. But it seems a great earthquake has occurred."

"It must be Heaven’s army. They probably came here to meet you."

Nane smiled meaningfully.

Even if he had swallowed the dream of Anke Ra, his philosophy differed from what the angels pursued.

"They’ll find us eventually. Even Gestalt’s power can’t hide us from the angels’ senses."

Despite the pain, Amy overheard their conversation.

'Heaven’s army has come?' With Nane alone being a formidable presence, adding them to the mix left no hope for humanity.

"Let’s wait and see."

Just as Nane was about to leave the room, Amy asked,

"Wait. What are you planning to do with me?"

It was unlikely that Nane had suddenly saved her life without a reason.

'She knows.'

She was Shirone’s lover.

"Even if you keep me captive, you won’t get what you want. I’d rather die than be used."

She had no desire to be a hostage.

"Hah. You really are stubborn."

Indeed, that was why she had sparked Nane’s interest among many beings.

"If you ask what I intend to do with you…"

Looking up at the ceiling, lost in thought, Nane met Amy’s eyes and smiled.

"First, we need to feed you."

Since there was no table in the cabin, the three of them sat in a circle on the floor to eat.

The meal was simple, made of grass, but it was the nutrients Amy needed.

"Ugh."

She hadn’t felt the pains when she was furious at Nane.

"You should avoid straining yourself for a while. Once your condition returns to normal, you can do as you please."

Amy slammed down her utensils.

"I don’t understand. You know who I am, yet you do this? What’s your real intention?"

Shura pointed accusingly.

"How dare you speak so informally to Buddha! Have you forgotten that we saved your life?"

"Then tell me why you saved me! If it was just to save me, what about all those who died at the hands of demons?"

"That is..."

Honestly, Shura also had her doubts.

"The reason we saved you is..." Nane began.

"Yes, because you are Shirone’s lover."

"What?"

Hearing it with her ears made it seem absurd.

"Shirone and I are companions in enlightenment. Though our paths differ, the distance we must travel is the same. I personally respect him. I was curious. The Yahweh of benevolence, to him, what kind of woman would be irreplaceable?"

"You’re mistaken. Shirone was just a school friend. He has nothing to do with benevolence."

"Is that so?"

Leaving only a brief response, Nane continued to eat.

Meanwhile, Amy, without touching her food, continued to glare persistently.

Eventually, Nane gave up eating and continued,

"If I threatened to kill you, would Shirone cede the path of enlightenment to me?"

The answer came immediately.

"No, never."

"You’re wrong."

Nane shook his head.

"Shirone would yield. If it meant saving you, he might even give up the entire world."

Amy’s eyes narrowed fiercely.

"So that’s why you saved me? Because you couldn’t use me to threaten Shirone if I died?"

"No, it’s actually the opposite. I want to understand Shirone. I want to accept all of him."

Whatever happened afterward didn’t matter.

"So, may I love you?"

Food spat out from Shura’s mouth.

"Poof!"

It was as absurd for Amy as it was for Shura, and an awkward silence stretched long in the cabin.

"No."

Amy regained her senses and spoke.

"Do you think that makes sense now? Why do you love me? What do you know about me?"

"We can start getting to know each other from now. And loving someone is a personal freedom, isn’t it?"

Amy thought of Beric.

"Of course, but you don't love me. You love the person Shirone loves."

"Yes, but everyone is the same. There's nothing in this world that can be loved without a reason. At first, it might be something trivial. Then, as the feeling grows and grows, it becomes something irreplaceable."

"I'm not forcing you. Just recover comfortably. I was just being honest about why I saved you."

Nane left the room, giving Amy some space.

'What is this situation?'

In a slightly dazed state, Amy chewed her food slowly, feeling a sharp gaze on her forehead.

When she looked up, Shura was staring at her with a displeased expression.

"You must feel like you have the whole world. Yahweh, Buddha, and now you're going to unify the universe?"

"What? What did I do?"

Although Amy knew she was the one who was wronged, Shura felt strangely irritated.

"Anyway, you should recover quickly. If heaven’s army starts moving, the world will end. Even if you die, you should see your lover first, right?"

Amy couldn't nod, but she knew that recovery was crucial, just as Shura said.

Annoyed by Amy's silence, Shura threw down the dishes and walked to the door.

"You do the dishes."

The door closed with a bang.
