Chapter 1266: End of History (2)

Kido spoke.

"Stand back."

He understood, since Havitz had lost a leg.

‘This guy thinks everything is a game.’ But—

"From now on, it’s no game."

He truly intended to destroy something. In a way, he had cast his heart into this world.

"Uorin."

The man, black as charcoal, opened his eyes wide. The chaos that had always swirled in his gaze was now compressed and compressed again, hardening into the purest, most extreme malice.

‘Satan doesn’t need Vanishing. But maybe even that isn’t necessary anymore.’

Whether others realized it or not, Havitz would get what he wanted.

"Hoo..."

As Havitz exhaled, flames ignited on his body, and the scenery around him began to burn.

The grotesque landscape of the underworld, made of guts and remains, unfolded.

Kido stepped in front of Uorin.

‘He scorched the bats.’

Though Havitz’s flames had gone out, embers were still burning from the cracks in his charred body.

"I’ll give you a chance."

Though, in truth, there was no such thing left.

"Hand over Uorin. Then I’ll kill you without pain." He wasn’t lying this time.

"It’ll really hurt."

Those who heard him imagined the worst pain imaginable.

Kido said,

"I can’t do that."

With Havitz’s chaos shattered, humanity now had some way of opposing Satan.

‘He must need History Search. Probably to find that kid from earlier.’

To find Wena Wizard, future sight was necessary.

‘The one empty place in all the threads of human possibility. That’s where she is.’

Wizard.

"Choose."

With every step Havitz took, the ground beneath his feet turned into entrails.

It was grotesque—though only for a moment. Kido made his decision and dashed forward.

‘Vanishing.’

Though it was difficult to maintain the state of chaos, Havitz had already lost that ability.

‘I’ll kill you in one strike.’

Kido’s spear struck Havitz’s neck—only for the steel weapon to burst into flames.

‘What?’

The blade, turned to charcoal, burned like a wick and caught his hands.

"Aaaaargh!"

Vanishing deactivated. Overwhelmed by immense pain, Kido staggered back, hands trembling.

Uorin’s face went pale.

"K-Kido..."

Kido looked down at his blackened hands. Tears welled in his eyes.

‘This is horrific.’

It wasn’t a level of pain one could endure.

But what made pain so terrifying was that it never considered the victim’s perspective.

"Ughhh!"

His hands were stiff and agonizingly unmovable, but Kido forced strength into them.

"Aaaaaaagh!" The charred skin peeled away, exposing raw flesh.

"Haa... haa..."

Even that pain was beyond bearable.

"S-Satan..."

As everyone sensed the gravity of the situation, Havitz spread his arms.

When flames erupted once more across his body, even the landscape of the underworld began to burn black.

"This world..."

Screams of the dead echoed.

"Will turn to ash."

The extreme law—Death Field.

Wherever his hands touched, wherever his feet stepped, only death remained.

"Only pain."

This was the world defined by Havitz at the end of Omega.

Tess was on the verge of tears.

"E-Amy..."

Where the avatar of the Great Sun Tathāgata had disappeared, not even a single strand of hair remained.

Complete erasure—emptiness.

"...There would have been no pain."

Nane lifted his head. Dried trails of blood marked both his cheeks.

"This world is suffering. It must be closed. I will save all beings and lead them to paradise."

Evil heads toward pain. Pain toward emptiness.

"I am..."

From behind Nane, radiant light, as bright as the sun, spread outward.

"Buddha."

The true Buddha.

By casting away the last lingering attachment—Amy—he had become perfect.

And Shirone—

"You..."

Rising to his feet with unfocused eyes, he screamed toward the sun.

"You bastard!"

In the silence where no one could speak, the man’s voice echoed wide.

"Aaaaaagh!"

Soaring alone, Shirone dashed toward Nane at his fastest speed.

"I’ll kill you!"

There was no trace of the former Yahweh in his glare, filled with murderous intent, or in his furious tone.

"Sermon."

Nane shouted.

"Ban."

The moment a red sword shot in like a flash and pierced Shirone’s solar plexus, his eyes widened.

“Gah!”

His strength drained from his body as he plummeted, and even then, he still hadn’t realized.

‘...Why?’

Why isn’t Miracle Stream activating?

“Shirone.”

Nane said with a calm expression.

“You are not Yahweh.”

How could he love something else when there is no one he loves more than her in this world?

Only now did Shirone realize—but he had no desire to regain his composure.

“Don’t make me laugh.”

His expression twisted again with fury, and the Immortal Function shattered Buddha’s Sermon.

‘Yahweh or whatever.’

Nane, you alone—I will kill no matter what.

“Shirone! No!”

Miro shouted, but already consumed by hatred, Shirone charged again.

“Photon Cannon.”

Thousands of spheres of light formed and all fired in one direction.

“Foolish mortal.”

As the beams of light converged like vanishing points in a perspective drawing, Nane took a half-seated stance.

“Let go of your attachments.”

Boom!

A massive explosion shook the atmosphere.

“What’s that noise?”

Hiding in the cave, Siena peeked outside again. The sky was lit like broad daylight, and two types of magic were clashing.

Orbs and swords.

She was a mage too, and she realized there was no magic in the world beyond what she was seeing.

“...Siena.”

A sudden voice made her turn around to find Kuan approaching with a disheveled expression.

“I’ll... protect you.”

Hearing that, Siena stopped hesitating and smiled.

“It’s okay. This doesn’t concern us. Sleep a little more. You must be tired.”

Siena pushed Kuan back down.

Of course, the back of her neck tingled as she turned her back on the battlefield, but it would be insane to drag an injured Kuan into that fight.

“Siena, protect.”

Kuan insisted stubbornly.

No matter how much she pushed him, he kept returning to the cave entrance, and Siena started to sense something was wrong.

“What’s the matter?”

The next moment, the space around them warped, and a completely different scenery appeared.

A dungeon underground somewhere.

‘Spatial magic.’

And high-level magic at that—something only the top-tier mages could cast.

“Success.”

Spoken lightly, the one who appeared from the rift was something in human form.

‘A person... No, is it even a living being?’

Its body was white like plaster, muscular, and lacked reproductive organs.

“Nameless.”

Siena’s voice trembled.

It felt different than when she’d seen it in Heaven. It was as if even the faint traces of biological instinct had been stripped away.

“Haha, found you.”

With that bright, innocent smile, Nameless approached Kuan.

“Don’t come any closer!”

Siena activated Spirit Zone, but an unpleasant memory surfaced.

‘This one steals others’ abilities.’

Or more precisely, learns them—but she didn’t even want to call it that.

“It’s okay. I’m not interested in you.”

Nameless’s gaze was fixed solely on Kuan.

“I don’t need to beat everyone.”

Defeating a high-ranked opponent meant defeating everyone below them.

“This time will be different.”

Nameless’s arms stretched like watercolor paint and transformed into two swords.

Seeing that Kuan didn’t react at all, even in the face of aggression, Nameless tilted his head.

“That’s strange.”

According to his past-life memories, Kuan should be at a level so high it was untouchable.

“There’s nothing to learn.”

He was slow, clumsy—even less than the lowest rank in the battle order.

“You’ve become useless.”

Siena’s pride was hurt more than Kuan’s.

“Don’t speak like that! He sacrificed himself to protect me!”

“Protect?”

Nameless recalled the moment his mother gave birth to him.

“...I don’t get it. That guy has a higher rank, so why would he sacrifice himself for you?”

‘He doesn’t understand.’

But she couldn’t fight either.

Considering Armin barely held him off with Stop magic, running away was also out of the question.

“Well, since I’m here, I’ll take you down at least.”

As Nameless’s swords turned toward Siena, a terrifying bloodlust filled the air.

‘I can’t win.’

Just as that assumption was becoming reality, Kuan finally moved.

“I will... protect.”

Nameless said,

“Move. I don’t need to kill you. You’re even weaker than the ones I just killed—”

“Siena... protect... I...”

Frowning at the stubborn fool, Nameless changed his mind.

“Fine.”

A revenge match against the one who had pushed him out of the ranks of life—he could allow that much.

“Your intelligence seems to have dropped too, but watch carefully. This is a level beyond your former self.”

Nameless spread an etheric wave and disappeared, and white swords surged from every direction.

“How about it?”

The voice came from the left.

“I’ve completely taken control of the space. Where will you dodge now?”

Another voice came from the right.

Kuan, who had been blankly watching, limped forward.

“No! Don’t step in so carelessly...!”

Siena grabbed his shoulder, and Kuan turned to her.

“Siena.”

The corners of his lips curled up in a smile.

“Smile.”

Nameless’s twin swords spiraled, compressing the space where the two stood.

The clown pierrot—simpleton.

The next moment, Kuan was suddenly in another space and, by habit, swung his sword.

There was no blood, but behind him, Nameless’s head had fallen.

Just as Siena trembled in awe, the severed head on the ground opened its mouth.

“H-How? There was nowhere to dodge.”

“That’s...”

Kuan chuckled briefly and said,

“That’s what you think.”

‘Is that so?’

Nameless blinked.

‘I don’t get it.’

Just what did that guy see?

As the final moment came again, the only memory Nameless could recall was this:

A being born from the union of Garas, obsessed with breeding, and the angel of accomplishment, Adio.

‘Mom, I’m sorry. I couldn’t...’

I couldn’t become number one again.

No one had ever told him it was okay.

And maybe, Nameless thought, that was more painful than not becoming the best.

Siena, dazed by the short battle, hurriedly snapped out of it and ran over.

“Kuan! Are you okay?”

“Hehe.”

Kuan seemed pleased.

“Protected you. Siena.”

In that moment, she realized—

‘He’s not a fool.’

That level.

She’d thought she was protecting a broken man, but in reality, it was she who had been protected.

“Thank you.”

In a world crumbling moment by moment, Siena was the safest person alive.

‘Until the very end, together.’

With that thought, she took Kuan’s arm and began to lead him—when applause echoed from the forest.

Clap! Clap! Clap!

Siena quickly turned around and saw a woman with elven traits smiling.

“That was a touching battle. Really great potential.”

“Who are you?”

An elf would normally be an ally of humanity, but madness flickered in her eyes.

“Well, how should I introduce myself?”

A man’s tone?

“Illuminati.”

The body’s name was Erin, but the one piloting her brain was Crown.

“By the way...”

Crown pointed at Kuan.

“That one doesn’t seem to have a pilot, does he?”
