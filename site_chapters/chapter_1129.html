Chapter 1129: Chain Reaction (1)

Shagal gritted his teeth.

"Ugh!"

Dozens of quick swords materialized, and he swung them all as he charged at Rian.

Etella shouted.

"Stop!"

Her sincerity took control of Shagal's body, and the chains quickly shortened.

"Ugh!"

Shagal fell backward with a thud, and Shirone's group looked at him in a daze.

"Teacher, what happened?"

Etella bit her lip. The most embarrassing thing was showing that her spirit had already been broken.

"I'm sorry. I couldn't help it."

Etella, who was wearing tattered clothes like rags, slowly stood up.

She had to hold onto the wall as her legs gave way, but gradually her strength returned.

"It's exactly as you see. I embraced Shagal and fell into hell. As a result, I'm being punished like this."

"The purification time?"

When Shirone asked, the voice of the informant penetrated from outside the cave.

"87 years, 2,875 eons, 3,241 trillion…"

Etella's resignation was understandable.

It seemed to be a significant number for Shagal as well, as he erased his murderous intent and leaned against the cave wall.

"Tch! Annoying interference."

Etella said.

"Shirone, Jin Seoug-eum in the Valley of Lamentation. She is also bound and accepting all karma. Please save her."

"I came to hell for that reason too. And I will save you as well."

Shirone explained the method.

Saving Etella would also save Shagal, but it was not something to worry about.

"No, don't do that."

Rian, who purified hell by burning himself, and Jin Seoug-eum, who closed the spiritual realm and saved humanity.

'Compared to them…'

She was just rolling around with a devilish man.

"Please leave. I have given up on saving Jin Seoug-eum and finding Shirone. I am not qualified to fight."

"Don't blame yourself."

Shirone knew Etella.

'A righteous person who could stand against Satan in heaven. Only someone like that could embrace Shagal.'

Living in a world where she couldn't die, entangled with the man she hated most…

'It must be terrible.'

In the inner world, karma is not something to be resolved but a weight to be borne.

'An amount of karma that cannot be resolved is given from the start. Rian is just an unusual case.'

The purification time wasn't a rank badge, but 87 years meant something like that.

"We need your ability. The power of Taiji will surely help us in what we are trying to do. And maybe we can find a way to purify it."

"Can you break this damn chain?"

Rian glared at Shagal's words.

"You stay quiet."

He couldn't touch him because of the chains, but his anger towards him remained unchanged.

"Heh heh heh, this is great. Hey, let's go. Aren't you sick of me too? Don't you want to be free?"

Etella met Shagal's eyes.

Some emotion only they knew passed between them, and Shagal's gaze turned aside.

"Hmph."

He vowed not to feel guilty.

"Fine, if my power can help. But can you give me a moment? I want to…"

She wanted to cleanse her body to wash away her mind.

"Yes."

Miracle Stream rose from Shirone's body, and Hand of God pierced through the cave.

Son Yujong raised her head and opened her mouth wide.

"Wow."

Ymir might be the best in combat, but seeing Yahweh's ability was awe-inspiring.

A giant hand of light dug into the mountain and filled the hollow with clear water.

Shirone finished the work in an instant and said,

"Son Yujong, give her new clothes."

Although he could create clothes with Material's ability,

"Yes."

Son Yujong plucked her hair.

When all the clothes were made, Etella held them in her arms and looked up.

"One more… can I ask for one more thing?"

As Rian frowned in confusion, Shirone calmly said,

"Shagal's clothes too."

"Really? That guy is really bad. Earlier…"

Yahweh's gaze shot straight at her, making her shoulders twitch.

"Oh, okay."

Etella gathered what she needed and looked back.

"Let's go."

Shagal glared at Rian as if something displeased him before clicking his tongue and standing up.

"When these chains are off, I'll kill you first."

Rian replied,

"I hope you do."

The two arrived at the lake Shirone had created.

Kneeling down first to quench her thirst, Etella scooped water into her hands and turned her head.

Shagal raised his middle finger.

"You drink plenty."

As Etella washed herself after taking off her ragged clothes, Shagal became even more displeased.

"You've been out of it for a while; now you're in the mood to fight again? Well, if you can break these chains, you'll do anything."

"You come too."

Etella called after finishing her grooming.

"To fight evil, your heart must be pure. This will help."

"Evil? Are you crazy?"

Shagal shouted,

"I'm the worst of all evils! No evil can scare me! And what? A pure heart?"

"Shagal."

Etella's sincerity was conveyed through the chains.

"Ugh."

"As you can treat me however you want, I can teach you goodness. So come here."

"So annoying."

Thinking it was better than showing more ridiculous behavior, Shagal walked over.

"Here..."

As Shagal lowered his head, Etella scooped up some water and began washing his hair.

"Archbishop Raphael once said, 'If the power of evil is selfishness, the power of good is sacrifice.'"

Even though Shagal knew these were the words of someone he had personally killed, he remained silent.

"For good to destroy evil, that too is no different from evil. Sacrifice, Etella. Especially as evil grows stronger."

She lathered his hair with soap, creating bubbles.

"That is the balance of yin and yang. Evil isn’t meant to be destroyed—it is meant to be transformed into good."

"A good person, huh? I killed him."

Shagal’s words still had a way of cutting, but Etella no longer reacted with anger.

"Yes. Archbishop Raphael forgave you in his final moments because he foresaw the great battle between good and evil. Destroying evil won’t make it disappear. But if you become good..."

"Couldn’t you also become evil?"

"Certainly."

The greater the sacrifice, the easier it is to fall into malice. Etella wasn’t immune to this danger either.

"I resented you. I cursed my situation. But it’s over now. Our journey, yours and mine—this will be the end."

Splash. The water poured over him.

‘The end.’

Shagal’s long hair, like a curtain, hid his face, but droplets of water trickled down his cheeks.

The Holy War was in chaos.

"What do you think you’re doing?! To act like this in the midst of a Holy War—it’s clearly an act of aggression!"

Gis pointed an accusatory finger at Kitra, the King of Paras, visibly furious.

"What did I do?"

"You, you...!"

You made the angels useless.

That was the crux of the issue.

Although countless lives had been lost as pyramids rose across the world...

‘Isn’t this actually a good thing?’

From the perspective of other nations, the fact that the power of angels had been neutralized was cause for celebration.

‘It’s not like we’re the ones suffering.’

Kitra spoke calmly.

"Once again, I’ll say it—I didn’t will the creation of these ancient structures. Nor could I have stopped it."

The Kingdom of Corona responded sharply.

"We’ve already uncovered evidence that the Zodiac manipulated the laws. We have proof!"

"Even so, nothing will change. Those of us seated here know well enough that humans cannot block the intervention of gods."

"That isn’t a god."

Everyone in the assembly turned toward the observer’s seat.

Pope Constantine, who had refrained from speaking except on religious matters, stepped onto the platform.

"A true god would not destroy human lives with such crude structures.

This is the work of a demon."

"A demon?"

Kitra chuckled faintly.

"The Holy Knights of the Anti-Demonic Order have been dispatched to investigate the pyramids. They will thoroughly uncover the truth and gather evidence. And if you, Kitra, are proven to be a heretic..."

Constantine’s eyes burned with intensity.

"I will punish you myself."

A golden radiance, said to be the pinnacle of divine power in the Lami Church, filled the chamber.

‘As expected, it’s incredible.’

Everyone’s gaze wandered in search of Havitz, but, predictably, he was nowhere to be seen.

The Pope gritted his teeth.

‘Is he evading even this? That cunning bastard.’

Quietly hoping for a contest of power, Constantine turned his gaze to Shirone.

Since their first encounter, he had received reports that Shirone had not left the papal territory.

‘What is he planning?’

Shirone, who had been thinking about Havitz and Wizard, noticed the Pope’s stare and met his gaze.

‘He wouldn’t even dream of it.’

Of the horrors unfolding in what he believed to be sacred ground.

Thirteen kilometers from the Holy See, scattered across a forest, lay a series of small villages.

As Shirone and his group moved between the villages under the cover of night, silence eventually fell over them.

Seina bit her lip.

‘Is it all a lie?’

In the villages she had observed, countless children were undergoing harsh training.

Children, emaciated from starvation, or praying until their throats bled.

Through such grueling discipline, the Holy Knights supposedly gained the ability to stand against evil.

‘So this is the truth behind the Martyr Code.’

The moment she realized that the powers they believed were bestowed by God were merely the result of brainwashing.

‘It’s all over.’

Seina had lost her divine power.

"Let’s just go back. I’ll take responsibility and report this. Someone will listen to me."

"Do you really think so?"

Shirone stopped in front of the entrance to the village located in the deepest part of the forest.

A sign read, "Village of Heaven."

Even before stepping inside, the malevolent aura was so thick it stank like rot.

“Haa…”

Though no lights were on within the village, Seina could feel it too.

“What is… in here?”

Without a word, Shirone led his companions into the village.

Even without entering the houses, they could hear screams and wails that resembled desperate prayers.

"There’s no one walking around. Are they trapped inside?"

As Neid scanned their surroundings, they saw a figure heading toward the village hall.

"Hide."

Seina pressed herself against the wall and peered out. Her eyes widened.

"The High Priest?"

It was Maximus, the third-ranking figure in the papal hierarchy. But the dignified man she knew was unrecognizable.

‘How could someone change so much in a matter of days?’

His eyes were vacant as if intoxicated, and his face was pale and unwashed.

"Let’s follow him."

Trailing Maximus to the village hall, they stopped at the door and frowned at what they saw through the windows.

"Ugh…"

Inside, naked men and women, covered in animal blood, were tangled together.

Though it was difficult to discern specifics, Seina recognized some familiar faces.

"I know those priests."

Her voice trembled.

Inside, Maximus washed his face in a basin filled with blood. Then, removing his clothes, he slowly joined the crowd.

As the blood-soaked figures writhed against each other, Seina felt her stomach turn.

"Ugh!"

Iruki propped his chin on his hand.

"Doing this in broad daylight? And here, in the papal territory no less. This isn’t brainwashing—this is more like a demonic ritual."

A woman’s voice called out.

"Exactly. A demonic ritual."

When Shirone turned, a hooded girl stood at a window across from them.

"This is the headquarters of the Satanic Church. And it can only be accessed through the underground passages beneath the Holy See. That’s why you couldn’t find it."

Seina asked, "The Satanic Church? Wait, who are you?"

"Me? Yora."

The girl pulled back her hood.

"It’s been a while, Shirone, Iruki, Neid."

It was Candler Eden.
