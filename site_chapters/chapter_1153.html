Chapter 1153: 12 o’clock, 12 Nations (4)

"Starting point, huh..."

As Iruki and Nade remained on alert, scanning the forest, Shirone muttered to himself.

"Mika."

As expected, there was no response.

"So only the entrance materializes in reality? That would mean we've already detached from the phase space..."

Eden spoke.

"The people we saw in the smoke aren't here. It looks like everyone has a different starting point."

A woman's voice asked.

"Are you accomplices?"

"Accomplices?"

"If you do not choose between ‘solo’ and ‘accomplice,’ Crime Dice will not activate."

Nade clasped his hands behind his head and asked.

"How about you make a recommendation? I mean... there must be pros and cons to each, right?"

"Solo has many advantages. Above all, no one wants to entrust their life to someone else."

Silence followed.

"So, either way, we're doing something dangerous."

Iruki stepped forward as he spoke.

"But if solo is advantageous, wouldn't everyone choose it? What’s the benefit of being an accomplice?"

"There isn't much. However, if you choose ‘accomplice,’ the mission to kill each other will not be activated."

There seemed to be no real choice.

"We'll go with ‘accomplice.’"

As soon as Shirone made the decision, two dice emerged before the group, glowing brightly.

They were six-sided dice marked from 1 to 6.

"Crime Dice determines your fate. The moment Crime Dice touches the land, the judgment begins. Accomplices cannot roll the dice consecutively. However, if you roll doubles, you get one additional chance."

Nade grabbed the dice.

"Let the left hand commit murder without the right hand knowing. May you find rest in Melkidu."

As the ominous statement concluded, Nade shouted.

"Hey! At least explain what this means! Oi!"

Iruki spoke.

"It's no use. It's just a system message. It has enough intelligence to hold a conversation, but it doesn’t seem capable of predicting reactions or understanding psychological states."

Eden asked.

"What should we do? Crime Dice is supposed to determine our fate."

Nade shook the dice in his hand.

"We have no choice. No one's chasing us right now, but we need to save Seina."

Iruki spoke.

"One person can't roll consecutively, right? So, who wants to go first?"

Nade held out the dice.

"Here, roll it, Shirone."

"…Why me?"

"Didn’t it say no one wants to entrust their life to someone else? But I want to entrust mine to you."

Eden raised her hand.

"I agree."

Shirone, who had received the Crimson Dice, turned his head.

"It said the judgment starts when the dice touch the land. But what exactly counts as ‘land’?"

Iruki spoke.

"It probably refers to natural terrain. Meaning, artificially created surfaces—like special boards where dice numbers can be manipulated or high-friction areas like mud—wouldn't count."

That made sense.

"Hmm, I see. Alright, here goes."

Shirone threw the dice, and the six-sided cubes bounced off various objects before finally coming to a stop.

"It stopped."

As the group watched tensely, the dice began to glow, revealing their numbers.

Between the numbers 2 and 4, a "+" symbol appeared, merging them into 6.

Nade spoke.

"Six, huh… Now what—wh-wh-wh—"

His voice distorted like static as the scenery around them changed at an incredible speed.

"—What just happened?"

By the time his voice returned to normal, they had already moved inside the entrance of a village.

The settlement consisted of around 100 households, with people moving about between the buildings.

"This is Dorian Village."

The woman’s voice returned.

"C-rank mission: Kill three villagers. Completing the mission will grant you Crime Points."

"What?"

"Gather Crime Points to strengthen your dice. Points can also be used to steal items from the shop."

"Huh. I see."

Nade muttered as he shrugged his shoulders.

"I don't get any of this. What exactly are Crime Points? Some kind of currency?"

"Crime Points support your desires. Your current points: 100P."

Shirone spoke.

"This really feels like the system from the Underworld. They also have something called ‘purification time’ there."

Iruki nodded.

"Well, this place is related to Satan, after all. But what does it mean that Crime Points ‘support our desires’?"

There was no answer.

"Here's what I think."

Eden raised a finger.

"In reality, criminals are always on the run. If their desperation not to be caught grows strong enough, Melkidu appears before them at a certain time."

"And on top of that..."

Iruki walked toward the entrance and stretched out his hand. A transparent barrier appeared, preventing him from leaving.

"The interior is a sealed space. It’s possible that all of Melkidu is structured in blocks."

"The dice!"

Nade snapped his fingers.

"The dice determine how many spaces you move forward. Each space contains a village."

Iruki nodded.

"A sanctuary for murderers. If criminals fled here to escape pursuit, then naturally, enforcers would have followed. But unless they rolled the exact same number on the dice, they could never catch their target."

Eden spoke.

"That just leaves the points. The idea of ‘strengthening the dice’ is unclear, but it’s probably tied to survival."

Nade spoke.

"The mission told us to kill three villagers. So, are the villagers actual humans? Or are they just system-generated illusions?"

As he thought of Xing, Shirone spoke.

"Either way, we've only moved six spaces forward. We don’t know how vast Melkidu is, but we’re still at the beginning. Let's gather information from the people here before we decide anything."

Upon entering the village, they saw six shops lined up on both sides, filling the block.

"There's a tavern. Should we check it out?"

At that moment, a man emerged from a weapon shop, holding a 20-centimeter-long knife.

The group instinctively stopped in their tracks as he flipped the blade over, examining its edge.

"Hmmm...?"

Then, after letting out a long yawn, he approached a passerby before even closing his mouth.

“Huk!”

The passerby’s eyes widened as the knife struck him unexpectedly, and the man repeatedly stabbed his abdomen.

“Aaaah! Help! Somebody, help!”

The victim collapsed to the ground, spewing blood, but no one paid any attention.

“P-please... help...”

In just ten seconds, the man had died. The murderer wiped his knife clean on the corpse and stood up.

“Early missions aren’t worth it.”

As Shirone’s group watched in shock, Iruki spoke.

“This is strange. Why isn’t anyone reacting? They could be the next victims.”

Even after seeing the corpse on the ground, passersby continued walking as if nothing had happened.

The man, after cleaning his knife, strode toward a woman selling candles from a basket.

“Hello. Would you like to buy a can—”

The 20-centimeter-long blade pierced straight through the woman's throat and was pulled out again.

Even as a fountain of blood sprayed into the air, the man struck several more times to ensure she was dead.

“One more left.”

As he turned, his eyes met Shirone’s group, who had been watching.

The mere act of being interested in a murder was enough to reveal them as players.

“Tch.”

Although he showed no fear, his expression seemed slightly uneasy as he walked past Shirone.

“Hold on.”

Nade called out to the man’s back.

“What?”

“You were doing a mission, weren’t you?”

“And what if I was?”

Glancing at his friends and confirming their agreement, Nade asked,

“Why doesn’t anyone care about what you’re doing? The ones being killed were clearly terrified.”

The man blinked for a moment before shrugging as if something had just clicked.

“Heh… Hehehe.”

Shirone’s group waited for him to continue.

“You guys are rookies. Haven’t even been through a single cycle yet, huh?”

“Cycle...”

Was it some kind of repeating system?

“Yeah, whatever. Anyway, since you’re curious, why don’t you tell me—why do people ignore murder here?”

Despite the risk of exposing themselves, asking outright was the most efficient way to find answers.

“Asking people is the fastest way. Besides, what’s the worst that could happen?”

Wasn’t Shirone here with them?

“I don’t feel like telling you.”

The man smirked.

“Figure it out yourselves. You guys have no sense of self-preservation. Consider yourselves lucky—if your points weren’t so pathetically low, I would’ve taken them for myself.”

“What do you mean by that?”

Nade pressed further, but the man turned away, clearly uninterested.

“Get lost. Pray that we don’t meet again—because next time, it’ll be miserable for you.”

As they watched him walk away, Nade grinned.

“Well, if that’s how it is... we’ll just have to force the answer out of him.”

“Lightning of Truth and Lies.”

It was the same technique from the Temple of Thunder that had paralyzed the believers in the Heavenly Village.

- Insufficient Crime Points for this action. Current Crime Points: 100P.

“Huh?”

When the technique failed to activate, the group finally realized the seriousness of the situation and checked their own magic.

The same female voice rang out.

"Magic is unavailable. Or rather, according to the system message, it seems to require points.”

The man laughed.

“Idiots.”

Without looking back, he raised his hand and flipped them off.

That was enough to make Nade snap.

“That bastard...!”

Even if magic was unusable, they had fought on the front lines of the Great War.

"Just some petty thug who stabs random passersby?"

With that thought, Nade moved to strike the man from behind—

But then, someone shouted from the side.

"Stop!"

Nade turned to see an older man, his face lined with deep wrinkles as if worn down by life.

“Who are you?”

Even under the piercing gaze of the Thunder God’s disciple, the man did not avert his eyes.

“Don’t do anything you’ll regret. If you attack that guy, you’ll be the ones who suffer.”

The word "you" snapped Nade back to reality.

The murderer clicked his tongue.

“Tch.”

He glanced at the older man before quickly leaving at a faster pace than before.

Shirone remained on guard as he asked,

“What would have happened if my friend attacked him?”

"Your points would have been deducted. You’re accomplices, right? If this is your first time in this village, you should have 100P.”

The older man pointed at the murderer.

“How many points do you think he has? You don’t know, do you? But even if he only had 200P, you’d be done for. Once a ‘Crime Attack’ is triggered, points keep getting drained until one side reaches zero.”

"So we’d be at zero, and he’d still have 100P left.”

“Exactly. And 100P is enough to stab you twenty times.”

Nade’s face paled.

"Don’t worry too much. Even at zero points, you won’t be restricted in normal actions. You’re mages, right? In the real world, your friend could have taken him down easily. But this is the Underworld. Here, points are everything."

The man gestured toward the tavern behind him.

“Well? I think I can help you. But I’ll need at least a beer in return.”

Shirone spoke up.

"That’s fine. We need information too. But… if you’re here in Melkidu, doesn’t that mean you’re a criminal as well?"

Could they really trust this man?

“It’s natural to be suspicious. But I’m a detective. I’m investigating the Edrena case.”

Shirone’s eyes widened.

“The Edrena case?”

It was the infamous unsolved case that once shook the Iron Kingdom—the disappearance of four women, all the same age.
