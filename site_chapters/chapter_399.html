Chapter 399: Rapidly Changing Political Situation (3)

Bang!

Shirone burst into his dorm room, slamming the door shut with the same force, and rushed to his desk.

The neatly arranged notebooks scattered once again.

Shirone grabbed one and flipped through it rapidly until he reached a blank page, then picked up a pen and sat down.

'The core. I need to write down the core first. So, what does core mean? Oh, right, the core...'

His mind was so fully awakened that he couldn't process all his thoughts at once.

His hand, holding the pen, trembled. It felt as if someone might come into his mind and steal his thoughts.

First, he wrote down a large guideline. Feeling relieved that he had avoided the risk of forgetting, he continued.

His hand was still busy, and he began to integrate numerous ideas from his previous notes into the core concept.

The pen moved across the paper as if it were cutting through it.

His handwriting was as messy as his thoughts were fast, and he didn't blink as he followed the journey of his writing.

After the theory, all that remained was the calculation. Shirone used simple physical theories to verify its feasibility.

Accurate calculations would need expert verification, but even simple formulas could determine usability. It was that kind of problem.

Finally, when the calculations were done, Shirone set the pen down with a loud clack.

On the last page, the final result exceeded the numbers he had hoped for.

Shirone stared at the notebook, dazed.

It was as if his soul had been sucked into it, and he swallowed hard.

Bang!

His fists slammed onto the desk like hammers. Shirone buried his face in his chest and trembled, unable to move for a long time.

"Haaa."

When he finally lifted his head, the initial euphoria of creating a new spell was gone from his face.

"It really... works."

It wasn't a certainty yet. But theoretically, it was possible.

Despite the complex constraints, if the conditions were right, Shirone could erase heaven from the map.

A sudden fear washed over him.

The possibility was so slim, or rather, he had thought it impossible, that he hadn't even considered venturing into this territory.

Surely, countering heaven's final war could be seen as a form of self-defense.

Intellect argues that there's no need to go through something unnecessary.

But.

'If I had known Aider's nature in advance, would I have still used the same strategy?'

This was a more difficult question.

If Aider hadn't attacked Maya, and if he had still been removed through a rational decision, would the result be the same as now?

Magicians have two criteria for efficiency: justification and practicality. If Fermi leaned towards practicality, Shirone prioritized justification.

Practicality yields immediate results, but justification leads to a flow that ultimately brings greater benefits.

Neither can be said to be better. However, the matter of destroying heaven was clearly a strategy leaning towards practicality.

"I need to meet Ikael."

Why did she pass on Ataraxia? Did she know that her choice would boomerang back to destroy heaven?

"I need to meet Ikael."

He had one more reason to meet her.

The graduating class schedule had now passed the 12-week mark.

The students had been running non-stop without a break, but recently, the atmosphere at school was chaotic. No, the entire kingdom was in turmoil.

Graduating students always had newspapers in their hands. Whether in the dining hall or the park, they talked about the shocking incident at the Magic Association.

Former Magic Association President Micaiah Gaold, wanted for treason

Shirone read the headline at the top of the newspaper several times before turning to the article.

It mentioned an assassination attempt on King Adolf XII of Tormia 10 days ago.

Adolf wasn't a tyrant and was popular even in Bashka, making it a shocking event.

But even more shocking was that the person accused of being behind the assassination was Micaiah Gaold.

Newspapers were competitively publishing related evidence.

It was cited as evidence that he had stopped visiting the association and gone into hiding two days before the assassination attempt.

Students entering the park were heard complaining.

"Seriously, treason of all things. What was he thinking?"

"Who knows? He used to be the pride of the school, but now he's its disgrace. Treason will hit the school hard too. It's one thing to block the path for future students."

They were speaking freely in Gaold's absence, but their feelings were understandable.

Shirone read the article again.

Gaold had disappeared along with the Magic Association's head of security, Gando, and the third-floor manager, Plu.

The lower section of the newspaper recorded the questions and answers from yesterday's National Security Emergency Hearing.

Most of the Magic Association staff listed circumstances that made them suspect Gaold of treason.

Only Isabel, a certified level 3 librarian at the Magic Association, claimed Gaold's innocence, but she was eventually arrested on the spot by the kingdom's intelligence agents and was currently imprisoned.

"Whew, things are getting complicated."

Shirone crumpled the newspaper and looked up at the sky.

Gaold had plotted treason.

Was this even possible?

No, for someone of Gaold's caliber, treason might be possible.

But the question was, why?

Nade spoke.

"Even a top-level arch magician has to run when the state moves against them. So what happens now? I saw some strange people at our school earlier. Iruki, do you know anything? You get letters from your father often."

If Albino, the head of Yongrae, had accurate information, Iruki would know, but he shook his head.

"He said it's too early to tell. Besides, it's not something to write in a letter. Those people were probably from the intelligence agency. They'll search everywhere Gaold might hide."

Iruki looked at Shirone.

He knew Shirone had visited the Magic Association during the break. He wasn't sure, but he felt Shirone was hiding something.

"Shirone, do you know anything?"

"..."

Shirone said nothing. Especially in this situation, he had to be even more careful with his words.

If a certified class 3 like Isabel could be arrested on the spot, there was no guarantee that harm wouldn't come to his friends or himself.

'What's going on? What's happening?'

Did Gaold really try to assassinate the king?

The likelihood was very low.

A project that had been prepared for 20 years was now just a few months away. The king's assassination could be part of the project, but even so, it seemed too futile a failure.

'The article says Gaold often showed arrogance. It also mentions he insulted the king to foreign diplomats. But he remained president of the association. There must have been some emotional equilibrium between the two powers. So why did it suddenly break now?'

It was too reckless and monumental an event to happen just before the journey to heaven, which required utmost preparation.

'Or maybe that's why it was inevitable?'

Shirone thought back in time.

'Gaold prepared the project for 20 years. It's impossible to control information perfectly. As the time for ignition approached, actions to extinguish the fire began. That's the most plausible explanation.'

If the assumption was true, it was the worst-case scenario.

Shirone was part of Gaold's project. If the intelligence agency dug deeper, his name would inevitably come up.

'Who's moving? The Kingdom of Tormia? Or something beyond that? How much do they know? How long can I keep this hidden? What should I do to prepare?'

"...rone. Shirone."

Shirone snapped out of his thoughts and looked at Iruki.

"Huh? Yeah?"

"What are you thinking about? Are you okay? You look pale."

Nade looked at him with concern.

"Huh? Really? Shirone, can you tell us what's going on? I didn't want to ask because I thought it might trouble you, but if it's too much to handle alone, we'll fight with you."

Revealing anything was impossible. It would be pushing his friends into danger beyond personal feelings.

Of course, Iruki and Nade would understand that. But they would also understand his current silence.

"Sorry. I'll tell you everything when I get the chance."

Iruki didn't feel disappointed.

Shirone wasn't stupid. If talking could solve the problem, he would have done so already. It just made him angry that he couldn't help his friend.

"Alright. We'll pretend we don't know. But don't be stubborn. The most important thing is you. If you get in danger, I'll start taking action too."

Hearing that from Iruki of the Merco Dine family was reassuring.

Albino might not have written anything in the letter, but Iruki might know more than he let on. It was possible with his family.

"Thanks. But really, I'm fine. I'll head back to the dorm. I have some things to think about."

Shirone stood up coldly, but his heart felt different. He was grateful to have Iruki and Nade. In the worst-case scenario, Amy and Rian wouldn't ignore him either.

Even if something happened to him, they could protect his beloved family.

That was enough for Shirone.

As summer arrived, rainy days became more frequent.

The morning was sunny, but the weather had turned, and it was now raining.

The early monsoon had arrived.

Past midnight, Shirone stood by the window, watching the pouring rain.

Three days had passed since Isabel's arrest, but there was still no news of Gaold.

The newspapers, exaggerating the facts, had turned him into a public enemy.

The people presumed to be from the intelligence agency were no longer visible. But they might have just gone into hiding.

The evaluation schedule continued as normal, but the students' motivation had noticeably declined.

They were disheartened by the fall of the kingdom's top magician, the president of the Magic Association, to such a miserable state.

'Is Isabel okay?'

As far as Shirone knew, Isabel was not part of Gaold's inner circle.

So, had she defended Gaold at the hearing out of simple loyalty?

Or was there something else he didn't know about?

He recalled the beautiful face of Isabel, who had kindly explained the laser-guided algorithm to him.

And the gentle way she had shown him how to use the Cubrick on his finger on the last day.

Knock, knock.

The sound of knocking made Shirone's heart drop.

It was already past midnight.

There was no reason for anyone to visit his room at this hour, not even the teachers.

Knock, knock.

Shirone entered the Spirit Zone. To avoid detection by the person outside, he kept the range extremely narrow.

As he approached the door, silencing his footsteps, he heard no voice from the unknown visitor.

"...Who is it?"

The lack of response meant it wasn't his friends. They would have signaled in some way.

If the door was broken down, he would teleport away. He kept this one guideline in mind as he closed the distance.

Finally, reaching the door, Shirone brought his eye to the peephole.

His tense expression turned blank for a moment before he hurriedly grabbed the doorknob and turned it.

"Senior?"

Plu stood there, clutching her injured shoulder.

She looked exhausted, her face pale and her lips blue and trembling. It was clear she had been through a lot.

Her clothes were tattered, and she wasn't wearing any underwear, so her soaked skin was visible through the wet fabric.

The only thing that remained unchanged was the determined look in her eyes behind her glasses.

"Shirone..."

A brief look of relief crossed Plu's face before she collapsed into Shirone's arms.

"Senior. Senior."

Shirone carried Plu to the bed and, after carefully checking the hallway, closed the door silently.

When he returned to the bed, Plu was already sitting up. She covered her chest with her arms, but it was only out of consideration for Shirone; her expression remained resolute.

The thought of preparing warm tea crossed his mind briefly. But it remained just a thought.

She had fled from Bashka to the magic school in Creas.

The fact that she had come to him, someone with no power, meant their situation was far more serious than he had anticipated.

"Senior! What happened? I was so worried!"

There was so much he needed to hear, but he didn't know where to start.

Plu seemed to be choosing her words carefully. After a moment, her face twisted with frustration.

"Gando... no, Gando..."

Plu slowly turned to Shirone. A single tear rolled down her sharp, determined face.

"Gando betrayed us."
