Chapter 627:

"Boyle!"

While everyone was wary of Shirone, only Pandora ran to Boyle.

Seeing him face down and convulsing, she turned and shouted, "Medical team! He seems to be unconscious!"

As she turned Boyle over to secure his airway, a low sob escaped.

"Ugh. Huuuh."

Boyle was crying.

Unable to move a finger, his pitiful state under the gaze of countless spectators evoked even more sorrow.

"Why me…?"

When he was competing with Pandora for the top spot in the advanced class, he never imagined such a despairing future.

"I did my best…"

The world doesn't want everyone to be happy.

"You did well, Boyle."

She wasn't just talking about the past year in the graduating class.

"Make sure you pass, Pandora."

Feeling the genuine sympathy in Boyle's last words as he was carried away on a stretcher, Pandora glared at Shirone.

"Ugh!"

The moment her eyes met Shirone's, who was vigilantly watching the surroundings without a hint of distraction, she involuntarily stepped back.

'I can't win in a direct confrontation.'

Though her insides boiled with anger in the face of harsh reality, the weak have their own ways of fighting.

"You understand, don't you?"

Mark, who had finished first aid and taken a seat in the audience, spoke.

"Honestly, I didn't expect the gap to be this wide."

When Shirone told him to leave, his heart was torn with resentment, but now, having witnessed the outcome of the survival test, all that remained was a feeling of utter defeat.

"Don't blame yourself too much, Mark. Let's consider it a valuable experience."

As Mark, comforted by Maria, bit his lip and lowered his head, Saad approached.

"Maria is right."

"Mr. Saad."

Mark reflexively turned but couldn't bear to look at him, so he dropped his head again.

"I have to admit it. I did my best. But Shirone and I are on different levels of talent."

"Talent, huh."

Many people talk about talent, but no one ever claims to possess it themselves.

"Maybe such a thing exists. But even if it does, humans aren't that extraordinary, Mark."

Saad turned to look at Shirone and continued.

"No human can create something from nothing. That's not talent. Shirone's experience, the number of failures, the problems he had to solve, were overwhelmingly more than yours."

"Experience…?"

"Yes. And now, through today's test, you too have gained invaluable experience."

Mark finally looked at Saad.

"Only those who rise from failure become stronger. Skill and power aren't everything."

At that moment, a familiar voice interrupted.

"A mage who can respond to all variables, right?"

Saad turned in surprise, and Mark and Maria shouted with joy.

"Senior Seriel!"

A year ago, she graduated from Alpheas Magic School and was Amy's close friend.

"Hello, Mr. Saad."

"Yes. It's been a year. I heard you passed the certification. How have you been?"

"I recently got an internship at the World Health Organization."

Mark's eyes widened.

'The World Health Organization. She really did it.'

It was the dream job for a healing mage and also the goal of his crush, Maria.

"Oh, that's great! Congratulations!"

"Hehe, I was lucky. Many familiar questions came up."

No one gets a job at the World Health Organization by luck, and Seriel's composure made her seem like she lived in a different world.

'Is this the aura of a professional?'

It wasn't just the makeup and suit; her confidence showed in every word and action.

"Come visit after the test. Everyone will be happy to see you."

"Yes, I'll come by later."

As Saad left, Seriel leaned on the back of Mark's chair and looked down at the colosseum.

'Amy. You've finally come back.'

She felt immense guilt watching Amy, who was eliminated early due to the collusion of the Golden Wheel, during the graduation exam a year ago.

"Senior, please sit here."

Mark moved aside to offer his seat, and Seriel placed her bag on her lap and sat down.

"Thank you. The situation has become quite interesting."

"Yes. It's amazing. Senior Shirone turned the tide in an instant. On the other hand, Senior Amy… still doesn't seem to be showing her true abilities."

Seriel smiled bitterly at Mark's honest assessment.

"Her heart is too soft."

"Senior Amy?"

"She still hasn't made up her mind."

Watching Shirone, who was observing enemies in all directions within the Force Dimension, Amy felt a mix of emotions.

'It would have been nice if you had told me.'

She knew there was no room for personal feelings in the graduation exam, but to Shirone, everyone was an enemy now.

'Even on the day you came to see me…'

Was Shirone thinking about the current situation?

"On the surface, Amy seems fiery, but she's a gentle soul. She can't ignore someone in distress and forgives even when she knows better."

Listening to Seriel, it made sense.

"Senior Shirone must be even more troubled."

"But it's okay."

Seriel proudly watched her close friend fiercely competing in the colosseum.

"Karmis doesn't allow two mistakes."

Amy's face flushed red.

'It's just beginning!'

Unlike a year ago when she was eliminated early, the current situation was balanced, so there was no need to be nervous anymore.

'I just need to take my own graduation exam!'

With Amy's movement as the signal, the other participants simultaneously cast teleportation.

As Shirone spun around, Amy's Fire Strike hit Prings, who was targeting Shirone's rear.

'Targeting me?'

Prings, who defended with an ice curtain, tilted his head, while Iruki, Dorothy, and Hershey attacked the other participants.

"A new system pushes out the old one."

Baikal's analysis was accurate.

If they couldn't take down Shirone immediately, there was no need to stubbornly insist on it at a loss.

It was more advantageous to use Shirone's system to eliminate other participants for a better chance of passing.

Only selfish judgment remained, with no room for emotions, and the battle began to descend into chaos.

"Damn it! What the hell?"

Screamer, who hadn't made a decision yet, watched Shirone amidst the simultaneous battles.

'One hit should take him down.'

Everyone knew Shirone was exhausted, but it was also true that they couldn't find a way to break Quantum Superposition.

'Should I do it? Just take the hit?'

While Screamer hesitated, Shirone quickly identified friend and foe in his mind.

'There are no allies anyway. If they want to use me, it's better for me to strike from behind.'

It wasn't a bad choice, but there were still more opponents against his system.

'Let's eliminate the neutrals first.'

As Shirone targeted and charged at Screamer, who had been hesitating moments ago, he quickly widened the distance.

'Damn it! I can't be the sacrificial lamb.'

Rushing into the Force Dimension controlled by Shirone without a plan was akin to suicide.

'Haha, a monster is a monster.'

Fermi, who still had some leeway, was also circling the outskirts, avoiding Shirone's domain.

'A 4D space…'

The Force Dimension Shirone had created was a miniature version of the upper layers of Istas, where all events existed simultaneously.

The difference was that Goffin's Force Dimension had no limits on superposition, using the time energy of stop magic.

'This could be fun. Should I try to break through?'

Judging by the spells Shirone had cast so far, his mental gauge was clearly below 10%.

'No, not yet.'

Even so, the power of Quantum Superposition was strong enough that Fermi couldn't guarantee victory, and he wasn't one to throw himself into uncertain battles.

"That's Shirone."

Iruki's father, Albino, smiled as he spoke.

"The fact that 26 people are reacting to a system proposed by one person in different ways means there's no room to exploit."

Amy's father, Shakora, agreed.

"He's a kid with an overwhelming presence. The situation perfectly fits the description of controlling the battlefield."

Arganes nudged Albino's side.

"Honey, don't just talk about Shirone. Analyze Iruki too. You're still his dad."

"He's quick-witted. But there's too much to say."

That was the end of his analysis of his son.

"By the way…"

Shakora watched Shirone, the only one freely moving around the colosseum.

"The abilities he's showing now must be why he could go all out in the first evaluation. Is it improvisation, or a calculated strategy from the start?"

"Probably both."

Albino's gaze followed Shirone.

"A calculating person would never choose such a strategy. But it's not like he pushed through without a plan either."

"Then…?"

"He just knows."

Albino could guess Shirone's thoughts.

"He must have experienced countless failures and achieved corresponding successes. When that process repeats, you naturally come to know. No matter what obstacle comes, you'll find a solution. You end up believing in yourself."

"Insight into oneself, is it?"

"Yes. In Yongrye, we say the top of your head opens up. It's thinking that flows freely like water."

Shakora watched Shirone, who was dominating the battlefield and guiding the enemies' reactions.

"Impressive. Certainly, Amy…"

"Yes, Iruki…"

They spoke simultaneously.

"Could fall for him."

Shakora turned to Albino.

"What?"

Albino smiled silently, watching Iruki fight alongside Shirone.

'It's a good problem, kid. Do your best.'

Calculations quickly ran through Iruki's mind.

'Seven people want to use Shirone. The best move is to eliminate my nemesis. The most troublesome opponent is…'

Iruki's gaze fixed on Dante.

'You.'

Dante was also using Shirone's system, but in this situation, allies didn't mean much.

'Atomic Bomb!'

As the chain-exploding flames rushed, Dante deftly avoided the explosion's path.

'Fine, let's do this.'

He had thought of Dante as an opponent he had to compete with since the Royal Magic School.

Central Calculation Magic Circle - Pascal.

As 200 instant magic circles of different colors floated around, the audience erupted in cheers.

"The real start."

As various spells poured out through the automaton's function, Iruki cast zigzag teleportation.

'Pascal is a good spell.'

Iruki's Spirit Zone crashed into Pascal.

'But you can't beat me with logic.'

Overdrive!

As electricity surged through the circuits of his brain, his eyes flashed, and Pascal's battery was instantly analyzed.

'Cancellation!'

He thought it was over.

"What?"

But Pascal remained intact, with 200 eyes glaring at Iruki.

'Encrypted?'

A security device using knot theory was triple-layered on Pascal's circuit.

"Did you think I wouldn't prepare?"

As Dante spread his arms and flew over Pascal, 200 amplification magic circles overlapped in the form of a tunnel.

Ultra-precision Serial Magic Circle Baekdo.

"If you're the pinnacle of logic, I'm the designer who handles it."

Screeeech!

The Fire Strike, amplified 189 times after passing through 200 magic circles in series, was fired.
