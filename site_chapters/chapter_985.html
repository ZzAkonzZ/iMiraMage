Chapter 985: The Unchoosables (3)

"Shirone?"

Shirone, confirming that the two were safe, smiled and said, "Just wait a moment."

The sound of the Hand of God dumping the building debris on the clearing at the top of the mountain could be heard from here.

"Huh? But..."

The position in which Alpheas and Olivia were lying seemed quite suggestive.

"What were you two doing?" Olivia, realizing she was still in his arms, hurriedly pulled away.

"Ugh!"

Alpheas groaned in pain.

"S-Sorry. Are you okay?"

Still grimacing in pain, he winked at Shirone.

"Thanks for saving us, but could you cover us up again? We were at a crucial moment."

Olivia's face turned bright red.

"What nonsense are you spouting, you crazy old man!"

Shirone laughed.

Seeing that both of them were full of energy, it seemed they had no internal injuries.

"I'll get you out now."

The Hand of God penetrated the ground and lifted the two as if pulling them out of water.

"Hmm, light."

Alpheas smiled.

Who would have thought that the child who once asked what magic was would become the light that saves the world?

Upon reaching the ground, Olivia could see the power of the bomb that had just exploded.

"My goodness..."

The entire radius was blown away, and a huge crater had formed where they were.

Siena came running.

"Vice Principal! Are you okay? What happened? The school exploded!"

Olivia gestured with her chin.

Alpheas, who was receiving first aid from Shirone, gave a thumbs up.

Reina said, "His kneecap is shattered. Shirone performed magical treatment, but surgery will be needed as well."

"We'll have to go to the military. It's the only place where we can meet a surgeon in this situation." Saad's voice was heard from outside the crater.

"Master! Are you safe?"

Arriving in front of the group through teleportation, he was momentarily speechless upon seeing Shirone.

"What happened? The explosion?"

After hearing the whole story from Siena, Saad sighed in relief.

"Thank goodness. The others are hiding in the mountains. Shirone, your parents too."

When the light magic was shot into the sky, the students came down from the mountain ten minutes later.

"Huh? Senior Shirone!"

Mark and Maria were the first to run over, but Shirone couldn't properly greet them.

He saw Vincent and Olina standing among the students, looking haggard.

"Mom."

Not expecting to see their son, Vincent and Olina were frozen in place.

But nothing could overcome the burning warmth in their hearts.

"Shirone!"

"Mom!"

As Shirone roughly hugged Olina, Vincent cried and embraced them both.

Countless questions that didn't wait for answers poured out between them, and people watched with satisfaction.

Shirone impulsively blurted out, "I found my biological mother."

Why now?

The thought that he had made a mistake flashed through his mind, but it seemed there would never be a better time.

"Uh, I thought I should tell you first. Of course, Dad and Mom..."

"Yes."

Olina, who was looking into her husband's eyes, gently stroked Shirone's cheek.

"That's really great."

"...Yes."

In the end, Shirone shed tears.

The night streets of Vashka, under martial law, had turned eerily quiet like a ghost town.

Occasionally, the screams of people suffering from emotional sickness floated through the air like ghosts.

A loud bang echoed, and somewhere else, a woman's terrified cries could be heard.

Pony, looking down at the city from the royal castle, was already exhausted.

'We can get through one more day.'

After breaking two more fingers, she realized that self-harm wouldn't solve the problem.

'The increase in the threshold is much greater than expected. At this rate, I won't last a few days. I have to endure.'

The key was to eliminate the demon realm, and Shirone and Rian would leave for the underworld at dawn.

'Will it be okay?'

According to Shirone, the process of going to hell in a living body is truly horrific.

One could easily become a demon.

Even if one maintained their sanity, they wouldn't be normal, Shirone predicted.

Looking down at the garden, she saw Tess standing alone, looking up at the moon.

'Is she Rian's lover? She must be troubled.'

No one was in a position to pity anyone else, so Pony turned her gaze away and went inside.

"Sigh."

Tess sighed.

'Are they really going?'

Knowing how to cross the point of no return made her insides burn.

'I can't endure it. It's madness.'

If it weren't for the situation where all of humanity was at risk, Shirone wouldn't have taken Rian.

'That bastard.'

Even when she was angry at Rian, thinking about the fear he must be feeling made her shiver.

'Yes, I have to pull myself together.'

Returning to the castle, Tess grabbed some snacks and headed to Rian's quarters.

"Rian, it's me."

"...Come in."

As expected, his voice was trembling.

Taking a deep breath, she forced a bright expression and opened the door.

"Hi? Want a beer..."

Tess froze with the bottle in her hand.

"Huff! Huff!"

The room was hot, and Rian was doing push-ups with his shirt off.

He turned his head slightly and said, "What are you doing, not coming in?"

Though his expression was strained, there was no trace of fear about tomorrow.

Tess closed the door helplessly and sat on the bed.

"Are you okay?"

"Emotional sickness? It's not a problem for now. I can recover even if I break or tear something."

"No, not that. Do you know what you have to do tomorrow? You'll be in the fire constantly..."

Tess hurriedly stopped talking.

Even if it wasn't something she had experienced, it was horrifying. How must the person hearing it feel?

"Yes, I have to set myself on fire. That's how I can enter the underworld."

"Aren't you scared?" Tess finally couldn't hold back and blurted out.

"You don't know what effect the fire on your body will have in the underworld! Are you crazy? Or are you really an idiot? You might not die, but that's even more terrifying, you fool!"

Rian stopped exercising and stood up.

"Tess."

He took the beer bottle from her and opened it.

"Don't worry. I'll do well." Watching Rian quench his thirst with beer, Tess suddenly got goosebumps.

'He's not a fool.'

It's courage.

The courage not to succumb to anything was Rian's talent.

After finishing his workout, Rian drank beer with Tess.

Time passed endlessly without any mention of tomorrow, and before they knew it, it was midnight.

In the brief silence that followed, Tess, fiddling with the bottle, burst into laughter.

"Hahaha!"

Even though a man and a woman were in the same room, the atmosphere was like this.

"What's up, suddenly?"

When Rian asked, Tess's laughter turned into a self-deprecating smile.

"We're cool."

She looked at Rian and asked, "Or am I the only one who's cool? Are you really not thinking about anything?"

Rian remained silent.

"Do you want to...?"

Even after mustering the greatest courage, there was no answer, so Tess hurriedly changed the atmosphere.

"Hahaha! Just kidding! As if I would..."

Maybe that's why it felt even more sorrowful.

Something hot gripped her heart, and tears fell.

"Can't you just say it? I won't bother you at all, just say you love me once."

"You're a good person."

"Why! Why!"

Tess stood up abruptly in front of Rian.

"You're leaving tomorrow! I don't want anything! Just say it once, what's so hard about that?"

"Why did Shirone choose me?" The sudden question made her angry, but she knew she wouldn't get any answers without going through Shirone.

"Only you can enter the underworld, you're strong, and Shirone trusts you the most."

"No."

Rian shook his head.

"If those were the only reasons, Shirone would have gone alone."

It made sense.

"I don't know the principle, but Shirone seems to be everywhere at once. When I heard that, the first thought I had was, how do I protect him?"

Tess fell silent.

"If Shirone dies in one space, what happens to the Shirone in other spaces? Do they all die, or only the Shirone in that space?"

"So... did you ask Shirone?"

"No, I couldn't ask. If it was tactically necessary, he would have told me without asking. But Shirone didn't reveal it. That means..."

Rian's eyes turned cold.

"If one Shirone dies in one space, all the Shirones in all spaces die."

Tess swallowed hard.

"That's why he didn't say it. Knowing this would lower morale."

"But it's just a guess. If you ask Shirone tomorrow..."

"Do you really think so?"

Rian interrupted.

"Do you think Shirone would ask a friend to endure terrible pain if he could do it alone?"

No.

"I will go to hell by any means necessary. And I will protect Shirone there. Tess, I understand your feelings. But I don't want to take responsibility for anything other than Shirone. I won't be able to make the right choice when it really matters."

If lingering attachments to reality delayed his judgment, he had to cut them all off and go.

"So, you can't take any responsibility for me? Not even a word? Not even 1%?"

Rian spoke firmly.

"Even 0.1 percent." "I'm sorry. You can resent me as much as you want. I'm already prepared. I will definitely..."

"Hahaha! Hahahaha!"

Tess's laughter was mixed with tears, but somehow her expression looked relieved.

"That's so like you. Do you know that? You're the worst of the worst among the men I've met."

"Sorry."

"No, it's okay."

Tess grabbed Rian's head with both hands.

"I'll never regret it. Now I know why I like you."

Because he is a true knight.

'And so am I.'

She tightened her grip on Rian's head and spoke with a sharp expression.

"Go. Go and fight."

"Tess."

"Listen to me carefully. You must maintain your sanity. And if Shirone is in danger..."

Tess held back her tears.

'Don't cry, Tess. Smile. You have to smile.'

Somehow, she managed not to show her tears, but she couldn't make a smiling face in the end.

"If Shirone is in danger..."

The words she bit her lip to hold back finally passed through her throat and came out of her mouth.

"You must die. Promise me. Don't hesitate for a moment. Protect your lord."

Rian bowed his head quietly and closed his eyes.

"Okay."

Suddenly, he had this thought.

If peace ever comes to this land, it would be nice to live with Tess.
