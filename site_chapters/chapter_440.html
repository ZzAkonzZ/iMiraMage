Chapter 440:

The sleek body of the metal angel, reflecting the moonlight, was modeled after the beauty of living creatures, but Sein and his group could see the coldness hidden within its beauty.

Of course, Babel was beautiful.

But it was also chillingly cold. Just looking at it made their bones feel like they were freezing.

Sein’s iron wheel eyes spun with a fierce sound. The calculations were faster than ever, but he still felt it wasn't enough.

'What is this?'

There was no unique aura that could only be felt from the strong.

But conversely, there was a sense of unshakable, extreme coldness that seemed like it wouldn't waver against any strong opponent.

Target setting: No priority.

Random target algorithm activated.

Babel's visual lens caught sight of Lena.

No one could see the focal point hidden behind the red panel, but one person, Lena's father Witcher, threw himself forward.

"Watch out!"

Was it extreme paternal love that made him choose Lena over Kanya?

The choice was accurate, and Witcher's body stopped abruptly as he pushed Lena away.

Babel had already approached and pierced Witcher's chest with its cold hand.

"Dad?"

Lena's face turned pale.

She was teetering on the edge of sanity, about to lose her mind.

"Dad!"

Blinded by rage, Kanya charged at Babel.

Enemy approach detected.

Response type: Instant kill.

Babel disappeared without a trace, and Witcher's lifeless body fell.

Due to the extreme relativity created by the high speed, Kanya's brain couldn't process the incoming scenery in order.

In the illusion that Witcher fell first and Babel appeared before her, only one thing was clear.

'I'm going to die.'

Screeech!

Babel's knife-hand stopped just in front of Kanya's neck.

Over her shoulder, Sein’s iron wheel eyes spun sharply as he took a combat stance.

One equilibrium calculated from Witcher's death.

But Babel expanded its halo and dismantled the complex cogwheel calculations.

'How is that possible?'

Equilibrium was the ultimate crowd control technique, calculating both rational and emotional coefficients.

The fact that it dismantled such a personalized code in less than a second meant it wasn't a preset program but learned in that moment.

'It's not intuition. Is it a probabilistic algorithm?'

A method of reaching conclusions by repeatedly generating random numbers to understand the target.

No matter how much of a savant Sein was, Babel's computational speed was far beyond his reach.

"Step back."

Sein spoke briefly.

"Dad…!"

"Hurry!"

There wouldn't be a second chance.

If she didn't understand now, she would die.

Fortunately, Kanya still had something to protect, and she desperately held onto her sanity and ran to Lena.

"Lena! Come this way!"

Even as she carried the half-dazed Lena on her back and fled, Babel didn't move.

Its visual lens was focused solely on Sein, analyzing his every move.

Target: Human. Accuracy 99.8%.

Type: Programmer.

Result: Highest level threat. Priority algorithm applied. Primary target for elimination.

Babel's body slowly turned towards Sein.

Two entities unparalleled in computation, recognizing each other in different ways, glared at each other.

"Emergency! Everyone, prepare for battle!"

Crude's voice echoed through the night sky as he ran to the hangar.

Clone incubation room.

The two aides sent by Raycis prepared themselves, holding a container filled with water.

With the materials needed for the Elixir of Legends gathered, those inside were now just meat.

Raycis's proposed method was to immerse the body of the Acidic Poison King Muusa in water.

Inhaling the powerful acidic gas produced would dissolve any creature's lungs, killing them within 10 seconds.

There was enough time to complete the task, but starting it made them feel breathless.

"One sip and it's over. Let's get this done quickly."

"Ugh, but it feels a bit off. Even if they're clones."

"Stop talking nonsense. Have you grown attached? They're just livestock. Let's get it over with."

When they opened the iron door, a wave of heat escaped.

The incubation room was just a large room, and the clones, unaware of their impending death, were focused on their tasks.

The man placed the container in the center and took out Muusa's body. As the chunk of meat fell in, the water began to bubble.

"Let's go, quickly."

Covering their noses and mouths with their collars, the two ran to the exit. White smoke began to rise from the container.

With Ikasa's help, Shirone and Plu escaped from the cell and ran through the underground corridors of the command center.

Everyone was focused on the Elixir of Legends, so there were no soldiers wandering around.

Of course, those who hadn't accessed the core information were all restrained.

"We can deal with the soldiers, but the problem is Akamai. It's like a demon caught in a sealing formation."

"It'll be fine as long as Raycis doesn't notice. Let's hurry. Where's Garas?"

"I saw a map in the genetics lab. Let's head there first."

Plu retraced her steps to the genetics lab.

While Plu deciphered the map on the wall, Shirone picked up the documents on the lab desk.

Thanks to the Ultima System, just looking at the sentences converted the signals into a single code.

It was an experimental report containing information about the clones.

Since his clone was also somewhere here, Shirone examined it carefully but couldn't get past the first page without frowning.

"How could they do this… What do they think humans are?"

The report detailed the grotesque process of mass-producing clones through the triangular mechanism of Shirone, Plu, and Garas.

As he quickly flipped through a few pages, his hand stopped again.

His eyes, slowly reading with the Ultima System, shook with shock.

"Hey, Senior. Have you read this file? The clone incubation research data."

Plu, squinting as if she had a headache, was focused on the large map.

"Senior."

"…No. I only checked what was necessary. This drone's decryption speed is too slow."

Shirone let out a long sigh.

He wanted to convey what he had read but couldn't bring himself to say it.

'What should I do? I have to tell her.'

"I found it. The Mormot isolation room. Fortunately, it's not far."

Plu hurried out of the lab.

They didn't know when Raycis would realize they had escaped, so time was of the essence.

Shirone felt somewhat relieved. Sometimes, ignorance was bliss.

Following Plu, they arrived at a tall iron door.

Plu took out Phoenix from Cubrick and cast a fire spell, melting the lock into molten metal.

As they pulled the handles from both sides, a bizarre sight greeted them with a loud noise.

"My God…"

Like an ark from a great flood myth, all kinds of creatures were packed into a 14-story apartment-like structure.

At the end of the isolation room, they found a glass chamber instead of iron bars.

Inside the 2-meter-tall glass chamber, a black creature the size of a human torso squirmed.

The King of Reproduction, Garas.

The fact that it was specially isolated in a place filled with numerous creatures indicated its extreme danger.

Eight spider-like legs clung to the glass wall, and an organ resembling lips on its underside continuously opened and closed as if trying to expel something.

It had a single eye on its gelatinous head, which was fully open, giving it a startled appearance.

"Is this… Garas?"

"Garas has no specific form. This appearance is just one of the countless generations that have passed down."

Garas's gelatinous head folded in half, and its eye focused forward.

As it wiped its eyeball with the eyelids above and below, Shirone felt a chill run down his spine.

With only one desire, it had only one purpose.

There might be creatures stronger than Garas, but no species was stronger than Garas.

Garas turned its gaze to Plu, analyzing her species with its superior intellect.

As its eyelids narrowed halfway, as if smiling, Plu felt a surge of anger.

The scene from the warehouse flashed in her mind, causing a terrible headache on her left temple.

"Kiaaaa!"

At that moment, Garas stretched all its legs stiffly like spears and let out a scream.

"Damn…!"

The force was so intense that Shirone and Plu hastily leaned back.

Feeling their backs damp with sweat, they realized how tense they had been.

"What should we do, Senior? Should we really release it?"

To neutralize Akamai and stop the Elixir of Legends, they needed Garas's power.

But they couldn't bring themselves to open Pandora's box.

No, perhaps this was a door to hell.

If Garas regained its freedom, the command center would be devastated.

Anything alive would become a target for reproduction, causing the number of creatures to increase exponentially.

"We have to do it. Release it and run. We'll rescue the captured rebels and then take down Raycis."

It was the only option.

They didn't have the luxury to plan a strategy that included a way to survive.

"Then… I'll open it."

Shirone's hand trembled as he reached for the lock.

No one could predict the outcome as he pressed the button, and the lock clicked open.

Confirming this, the two turned simultaneously. Without looking back, they cast teleportation.

Crash! Garas broke out of the glass chamber and flowed out.

As it staggered on its eight legs, its body began to grow to match the size of the space.

"Kiaaaa!"

Naturally, its scream grew louder, and Shirone and Plu, feeling as if the sound was right next to them, screamed as well.

"Aaah!"

As if on cue, they both leaped to the sides, rolled, and slammed the iron door shut.

Moments later, the sound of countless creatures' screams and the thudding of Garas's movements echoed through the door.

Shirone was now certain.

He had indeed opened the door to hell.

"You fools!"

Raycis stormed into the room.

As the researcher had reported, Shirone and Plu were nowhere to be seen.

The True Akamai, now shriveled like a deflated balloon, lay pathetically on the ground, and only Ikasa remained in the cell.

"I told you to check, didn't I?"

"W-well, since Akamai was here, we thought it was safe to proceed with the experiment preparations…"

With a loud thud, the researcher's face was struck.

The attack was so fast it was invisible, but Ikasa could see it.

The grotesque whip hidden under Raycis's cloak.

"Hmph, you carry that monstrosity around as your body? Truly hideous. Even the ugliest Gorgol among the Mara isn't as repulsive as you."

Grrrr!

Raycis gritted her teeth, and a rough growl escaped between her fangs.

"A weak angel dares to insult me? Soon, you'll be inside my belly."

"Hahaha! Yes, you must be desperate to have my beautiful body. But what will you do? No matter what, it seems your hideous appearance can't be fixed."

Ikasa's sharp tongue was relentless.

Though she couldn't move until she repaid the cause of Valhalla Action, her angelic pride was more noble than death.

"I'll tear you apart!"

With each step Raycis took, the ground shook.

Her skin reddened, revealing her monstrous face, and she charged at the cell with her massive jaws wide open.

"Raaaargh!"
