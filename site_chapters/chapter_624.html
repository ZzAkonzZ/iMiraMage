Chapter 624:

Shirone slowly stood up.

He couldn't fully recover, but his eyes were clear, and he showed no signs of weakness.

-Entering Stage 9 of Extreme Survival.

The voice of the 2000 system made the participants' hearts sink.

'Stage 9.'

The number alone was overwhelming.

"From now on, it's a different dimension. The average stats are 8.7 times higher than Tier 3."

The increase seemed unreasonable for an exam, but in reality, there was no intermediate stage between Tier 3 and Tier 2.

The Colosseum had been downsized, but only creatures of demon lord level or higher were listed as Tier 2.

Powerful beings representing elements, such as the fire incarnation Phoenix, the ice-breathing Feroton, the death reaper Grim Reaper, and the thunderstorm-bringing Thunderla, swept through the Colosseum.

"One person led us here. This makes Extreme Survival meaningless. There could be mass casualties."

Unlike Shirone, who had progressed step by step, the other participants had encountered a disaster while standing still.

"We have to exploit the virtual system's weaknesses. Unlike reality, the higher the tier, the simpler the patterns."

High-tier creatures mostly lived outside human habitats and had exceptional intelligence, so simulation data was lacking.

"Of course, if you know the patterns, you can dodge dozens of blades."

That was the fear of intelligence overpowering brute force.

"But you can't dodge thousands of blades. They'll overwhelm you instantly."

"……"

Thousands of blades—that was the current battlefield situation.

Fwoooosh!

Every time three Phoenixes spewed fireballs, more than half the Colosseum was engulfed in flames.

Feroton, covered in iron scales, flew through the air with massive fin-like wings, creating straight ice paths, while Thunderla unleashed lightning strikes at random coordinates.

On the ground, the giant Grim Reaper, standing 5 meters tall, swung its scythe from atop a horse.

"What are we going to do now? Can we even take the second evaluation?"

Extreme Survival selected 15 participants based on skill, but now it was just bad luck if you got eliminated.

'No, you'll die. Even in the 2000 system.'

The attacks from Tier 2 creatures were powerful enough to destroy even a mage's mind.

'Absolute Barrier!'

As Eden's shield blocked the front, 17 creatures unleashed a concentrated barrage.

"Ugh!"

The name Absolute Barrier became meaningless as it turned into a wide-area defense spell, but the fact that it could protect over 20 participants only increased her value.

"My son will die at this rate!"

The Colosseum was a death trap under the demon lords' bombardment, and Eden's shield was the only safe zone.

"What are you doing? We sent them to school to become mages, not to die!"

Parents knew the competition was fierce, but the current situation was beyond acceptable.

"End this! Move to the second evaluation!"

It felt like a test of luck, so it made sense to suggest moving to the next evaluation.

"The situation is critical."

Olivia, hearing the parents' complaints, spoke.

"But there's no cancellation. This is the graduation exam."

Even Alpheas, the principal, could do nothing now.

The exam was being conducted simultaneously in all countries under the Red Line.

"Find a solution! We can't hold on any longer!"

Eden's mental strength was at its limit.

Phoenix's fireballs spread like oil on the shield's surface, and Feroton, fixed in the sky, continuously breathed freezing air.

'If the shield breaks…'

Some students tried to come up with countermeasures, raising their mental strength.

"Shirone! It's dangerous!"

Before they could act, Shirone charged towards the center of the demon lords' concentrated firepower.

"Reckless! You'll die!"

Baikal didn't want Shirone to die like this.

"What…!"

But Shirone passed through the demon lords unscathed.

"What is that?"

Scouts stood up to get a better look.

"Whooo!"

As Shirone crossed the Colosseum and reached the end, he activated the sequence method, and golden light burst from his body, rising into the giant incarnation of the Archangel.

Screeeech!

As the 2000 system identified the most active target, the demon lords turned and charged.

"Th-that…!"

Just as everyone sensed a disaster, Shirone's time technique created thousands of whirlwinds, distorting the scenery.

'Time Explosion!'

A single flash of light gracefully escaped the chaos of fire, scythes, freezing breaths, and lightning.

"Ugh!"

Simultaneously, analysts from various countries clutched their heads and groaned.

"What's happening?"

Elizabeth shouted, frowning.

"The 2000 system's time is malfunctioning!"

"Time?"

"It's not recognizing it properly. The error rate is about plus or minus 1.14 seconds!"

"What does that mean?"

"It's reversing time!"

Baikal finally looked at Shirone.

There was no apparent anomaly, but as the system accumulated errors, the creatures' movements began to stutter.

"Incarnation technique…?"

A skill only the highest-level Prajna could use was being displayed in the graduation exam's Colosseum.

"Lady Reina, this might be a strange question…"

Vincent, staring blankly at Shirone weaving through the demon lords, asked.

"Is that student really my son?"

Reina, too, was witnessing an unimaginable scene.

"Well… I don't know."

"It's Shirone, sir."

Rian, placing a hand on Vincent's shoulder, looked at Shirone with a flushed face.

"I swear on my sword."

"Now! Everyone, attack!"

The participants, once again outshone by Shirone, belatedly joined the battle.

'Nuclear Fusion!'

After long calculations, Iruki unleashed his ultimate magic, causing an explosion in the Colosseum's center.

"52,437 kilobusters! The explosion exceeds 50 megabusters!"

It was the first time such a figure appeared in the graduation exam.

As if to restore their trampled pride, each participant's specialty erupted, and the scouts were busy evaluating, analyzing, and recording.

'Just a little longer!'

The total data generated in the Colosseum threatened the performance of the specially designed 2000 system, but it still couldn't annihilate the demon lords.

-Entering Stage 10 of Extreme Survival.

"Are we finally going to see it?"

As the scouts marveled at witnessing one of the rarest events in Red Line history, warning lights flashed.

-Access to the 2000 main system granted. Activating first-class security program. Summoning Program Dragon, Fire Dragon Infercus.

The emergency announcement made the audience's eyes widen.

"A dragon?"

Even among Tier 1 creatures, it was a top-tier creature, far beyond a mid-tier lich.

Screeeech!

A piercing scream erupted, and the Fire Dragon Infercus appeared in the Colosseum's center, its head nearly piercing the ceiling.

"What is that?"

Even the participants, who were determined to give their all, were intimidated by the dragon's ferocious presence.

Grrrr…

Infercus, spewing flames between its clenched teeth, roared at the sky, and a cloud of fire covered the ceiling!

"It's dragon speech."

Dragons perceive and even control the world in binary code, not language.

Even the advanced 2000 system was nothing compared to a dragon's brain, and the magic generated by its powerful calculations was on a completely different level from anything human.

Roar!

The flames it spewed towards the ground seemed to pierce through, and the heat alone caused mental damage.

"Ugh!"

In the physical world, their bodies would have already burned.

"Dodge!"

"Where to?"

The only safe zone was outside the Colosseum, which meant failing the exam.

'We can't win this! What are we supposed to do?'

There was no time limit since it was the final stage.

'This is insane!'

Dodging the breath that turned the ground into lava, the participants cursed the unknown exam creator.

It was as if they were saying, 'How dare you try to solve the problem I created?'

"Karinter is indeed a pervert."

Baikal knew the person responsible for designing the Extreme Survival evaluation.

"But it's a program approved by the Red Line. There must be a blind spot."

There was a blind spot, but it was very narrow.

Roar! Roar!

Every time Infercus breathed, the participants scrambled to dodge.

"Damn it! Fly up! Scatter!"

Infercus, raising its head to chase the participants, unleashed a deafening white noise towards the audience.

"Aaah! What's that?"

The audience, facing the dragon's breath head-on, forgot their dignity and retreated, but the flames vanished as if hitting a barrier in front of the stands.

"Take it down! If we don't, we'll all fail!"

Thinking they were at a disadvantage the longer it dragged on, all the participants cast attack magic, but Infercus continued to breathe fire, following its set pattern as if nothing had happened.

"Analyze the parameters. Infercus's magic power was 360 million magicals, right?"

Elizabeth opened the percentage parameters.

"It's currently at 98.7 percent."

Despite being hit by nearly 30 mages' attacks, it had only lost 1.3 percent.

"Oh, it's back up to 98.8 percent."

Like the participants, the creature also recovered magic power.

"Based on the current rate, the estimated clear time is 197 hours, 44 minutes, and 15 seconds."

"…It'll be next year before it's over."

"Infercus's magic recovery rate is 14 percent per minute."

Faster than Shirone's meditation.

"Unless they can overwhelm its magic power instantly, the designer will win."

"What do the students gain by winning?"

Annoyed, Baikal frowned.

"Evaluator! Look!"

Lara pointed to the Colosseum.

Shirone was firing lasers, accumulating damage on Infercus's body.

The breath still swept the space menacingly, but it couldn't burn Shirone, who had activated Time Explosion.

"Now! Everyone, attack!"

Knowing the laser's function, the participants began casting their ultimate spells.

"Parameters."

Elizabeth responded immediately.

"It's decreasing rapidly. 88.7 percent. 86.4 percent."

The participants were focused solely on ending it here.

"67.6 percent. 64.2 percent. The estimated clear time is down to 24 minutes, 43 seconds."

"Incredible. But can they maintain this firepower until then?"

"It'll be tough. The laser accounts for 40 percent of the total damage."

Roar!

Infercus roared in pain for the first time.

"Yes, Shirone."

As the hooded woman crossed her legs and rested her arm on the adjacent seat, a middle-aged man glanced at her in displeasure.

"Push it, push the world away."

When Shirone's laser disappeared, everyone turned their heads.

"Shirone! What are you doing?"

Even a slight drop in firepower would allow Infercus to recover its mental strength quickly.

'This won't work!'

Desperately, Shirone combined Time Explosion with 4D Ataraxia.

"52 percent! 53 percent! It's still recovering!"

The audience, who had been cheering in unison, let out disappointed sighs, and the middle-aged man fidgeted in frustration.

"Hey! What the…! Attack quickly! How can you give up now?"

As the hooded woman looked down at the magic circle emitting a rainbow of light, she muttered.

"Ataraxia."

The middle-aged man frowned and asked, "Are you talking to me?"

Ignoring him, she continued.

"Long ago, angels were like natural disasters to humans. So great and powerful that all they could do was submit."

'What is she, crazy?'

Seeing her muttering to herself, the middle-aged man turned his head away.

"In fact, the beings angels had to fight were dragons, who ruled the planet before humans."

"……"

But strangely, he was drawn to the story.

"Since then, humans have had two gods. The god of the distant universe became religion, and the god here became the name of the king who ruled humans."

"What on earth are you talking about…?"

Unable to hold back, the middle-aged man asked, and the hooded woman turned sharply and asked.

"Do you know what humans called angel magic after witnessing the war of the gods?"

"Well, I don't… really…"

Stammering under her inexplicable intensity, the woman smiled slightly and turned her head to the battlefield.

"Dragon-slaying magic."

Photon Cannon.

The flash that passed through 4D Ataraxia dispersed into an enormous number of beams, concentrating on Infercus.

Roar!

"It's dropping again! 44 percent! 38 percent! 27 percent!"

Watching the rapidly converging stars, the audience felt a primordial sense of divinity etched into their genes.

'This is the end!'

Shirone, raising his fist, glared at the writhing Infercus.

Annihilation magic, Quasar.
