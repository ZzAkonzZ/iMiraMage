Chapter 439:

With the activation of the external artificial brain, the time for Ataraxia to reach its target more than doubled.

Shirone cast his spell, glaring at the colorful halo aimed forward.

'Photon Cannon!'

As the photons compressed and trembled as if about to explode, Ikasa appeared before him.

"Ugh!"

When Ikasa's fist struck his solar plexus, Armand intercepted it, but couldn't completely nullify the shockwave.

The equivalent exchange of cause and effect.

Valhalla Action merely reversed the order of potential and kinetic energy, but for beings living within time, it was an insurmountable challenge.

"How dare you!"

Ikasa unleashed a rapid series of strikes with Valhalla Action, and Shirone deflected the punches with Armand's tendrils.

When the Photon Cannon fired at close range, Ikasa evaded the attack with dazzling weaving.

In the battle where martial arts and magic unfolded simultaneously, the surviving rebels couldn't even think of intervening.

"Is that a Nephilim…?"

A human was fighting on equal terms with an angel.

Even a fallen angel was an untouchable existence to the subjects.

For the subjects, it was as shocking as having their ancient beliefs shattered.

'This can't be happening!'

Ikasa couldn't accept the reality either.

A human who couldn't even move a finger a year ago was now fighting on par with him, empowered by the full moon.

"A mere insect!"

Ikasa desired to unleash the most powerful series of strikes on Shirone's coordinates.

As the afterimages of her fists formed a massive wall and surged like a tidal wave, Shirone took an extreme defensive stance.

The Ultima System activated immediately, twisting Armand's robe along his body and thickening it.

Fist-shaped patterns appeared all over Shirone's body.

"Ugh!"

The attack was so fast that he couldn't think of anything but defense.

But once this storm passed, the opportunity would come to Shirone.

'There's a limit to what can be done against a sentient target. If I can endure this, I can win.'

As Shirone endured the bone-crushing pain from Ikasa's final blow, he concentrated Ataraxia.

His prediction was accurate; Ikasa, having accelerated the outcome with Valhalla Action, couldn't move at all.

'But it's delicate. Can I time it right?'

He had no choice.

Considering the angel's durability, this was the only way to end it in one strike.

Ikasa, watching the rapidly accumulating Ataraxia, felt like a condemned prisoner on the gallows.

'That's dangerous.'

The signature move of Archangel Ikael.

If completed before Valhalla Action returned, its mere existence could induce panic.

'Six seconds. Five seconds. Four seconds.'

Ataraxia versus Valhalla Action.

It was a battle to see who could complete their angelic power first.

"Now. Deploy it."

As Raycis, monitoring the situation from the underground command center, gave the order, all creatures on the surface simultaneously froze.

'What?'

Sensing the abnormal change, Plu struggled to lift her gaze.

A giant eyeball moved its pupil rapidly, observing everyone on the ground.

Biological Weapon - True Akamai.

The power of Antithesis reversed the law.

As if bound by ropes, Ikasa screamed, narrowing her shoulders.

"Ugh! This…!"

Even for a fallen angel, the restraint was absurd.

It was a law so strong that even a regular angel would struggle to withstand it, let alone one empowered by the full moon.

If Ikasa was restrained to this extent, the others were no exception.

The only one showing any movement was Shirone, enduring with the power of mental transcendence.

'What immense power. What is this?'

Even Armand's robe couldn't withstand it, disassembling back into the magic sword.

A researcher monitoring the situation from the underground spoke.

"All targets restrained. What should we do?"

Raycis discarded the mask of the commander.

With the materials gathered, the rebels on the surface were nothing more than useless meat.

"Imprison the soldiers and bring Shirone, Plu, and the angel. Prepare the Elixir of Life immediately."

Raycis's aides moved at once.

While the Elixir of Life was being prepared, Shirone, Plu, and Ikasa were locked in a cell.

Of course, the real prison was the True Akamai outside the cell, watching them.

Plu gritted her teeth.

She hadn't expected Raycis to act while an angel was invading.

'No, it's that powerful a weapon. How did she create it?'

The three who had been fiercely fighting moments ago were now cellmates.

Plu glared fiercely at Ikasa, who had ruined everything.

But emotions weren't important in this situation, so she first conveyed the necessary information to Shirone.

Raycis was enhancing herself through biological experiments, using Shirone as a material, and utilizing Garas and Plu for mass-producing clones.

Even for the angel Ikasa, it was an absurd story.

"A lowly species trying to become a god? Foolish."

It was hypocritical coming from the angel of desire, but Shirone didn't think much differently.

'They were cloning me? What on earth…'

At that moment, the door opened, and Raycis entered.

"Hehe, an angel and a Nephilim. What a delicious pantry. Or should I say luxurious?"

Ikasa bared her teeth and shouted.

"Do you know what happens to those who defy an angel, you insignificant human?"

"Kekeke! Who's the one caught by a creature made by this insignificant human?"

Raycis fearlessly entered the cell and sat in front of Ikasa, stroking her chin.

A ferocious fallen angel, but her body was undoubtedly that of an angel.

"You're truly beautiful. Perfect to become my body."

Shirone asked.

"Why are you doing this? Even if you become stronger through these inhumane experiments, you'll just be a monster."

"A monster…"

Raycis recalled Satan's words from long ago.

"Form is merely function. Only inferior humans assign aesthetic value to it. Therefore, strength is beauty."

Raycis's facial bones twisted, transforming into a monstrous appearance.

Her eyes slanted, her nose protruded like a beast's, and her lips split to her ears.

Flames flickered in hr snake-like eyes, making the surroundings shimmer like a mirage.

As the memory vision showing scenes from the past activated, Shirone felt as if he were in a laboratory.

In the central Fuzix Machine, the bodies of Antithesis Akamai, Devourer Kuzan, Infinite Cell Proliferator Kenser, Jinma Species Galtomic, and Armored Mineral Species Ringer were separately placed.

"How is it? These are the creatures that will lead me to the body of a god."

Shirone smirked.

"Do you think you can become a god with those?"

"Mortality is not inevitable for living beings. Death is merely a function chosen by organisms. If it's for a perfect single entity, death can be eliminated. You and the angel will fill the remaining two slots. By combining all of this into one, I will become the god of the world."

"You're insane."

Plu's eyes filled with hatred.

She had witnessed how Raycis mass-produced clones from start to finish.

"Hehe, you're nothing but excrement that can't even merge with my body. Think whatever you want while you wait to die."

Raycis left the room with those words.

Soon, researchers would drag Shirone and Ikasa to the Fuzix Machine.

There was no flaw in the Elixir of Legends created by Anke Ra.

Even Ikasa's angelic body would dissolve into black liquid when mixed with the plasm.

Plu strained with all her might, but the restraint of True Akamai was so immense that she couldn't move a finger.

"This is bad. If we stay like this, we'll all die."

Ikasa sneered.

"A mayfly talking about death. How amusing."

"Haha, really? Angels have long lifespans, right? It must be frustrating to disappear like this."

Ikasa fell silent.

It was true. While eternal death was impossible as long as the Akashic Records existed, annihilation was one of the most unpleasant events for an angel.

"There is one way."

"A way?"

Shirone asked, struggling to turn his head.

The fact that Ikasa could move even within True Akamai's restraint indicated how strong Shirone's incarnation was.

"Of course. Do you think a noble angel would be defeated by a mere human-made creature?"

Plu asked.

"You're being defeated right now. What's the way?"

Ikasa hesitated for a moment before speaking.

But the feeling that it wasn't worth it lingered.

"Using Valhalla Action to destroy this creature. In other words, bringing the result of destruction forward."

"That doesn't make sense. If that's possible, why are you captured?"

When Plu questioned, Shirone answered.

"Because the cost is too great."

Ikasa no longer hid it.

"That's right. Given the restraint, the cost is at least equivalent to 8 hours of energy. If I destroy this, I'll be incapacitated for 8 hours."

"Eight hours…"

Ikasa, who could bring down a mountain in less than 30 minutes, had to pay 8 hours of labor for this restraint.

It was possible because it was the Antithesis of the law, not physical force, but it still indicated how powerful the creature was.

Shirone understood why Ikasa hesitated.

"I promise to save you. So, free us."

"Hmph, do you think I'll believe a human's words?"

"You have to. If we stay like this, we'll all die."

Ikasa's face twisted in irritation.

"Even if I get out, what difference will it make? This creature isn't the only one. We'll just get caught again and end up back here."

"I have a plan," Plu said. "Garas is imprisoned in the underground lab. If we release it here, this place will become a battlefield for them."

"Hmm."

Ikasa pondered carefully.

The King of Reproduction, Garas, was a notorious creature even she knew well.

"Using Garas to eliminate this biological weapon? Well, if it gets loose, it's not impossible."

"Ikasa."

Shirone, using his mental transcendence, slowly approached Ikasa and met her eyes.

"I promise I'll come back to save you. Please, free us."

The human desire for life was indeed persistent.

It was the reason Anke Ra had to control humans, and it was also why Ikasa had become a fallen angel.

"Hmph, don't compare me to a mayfly human. I'm not afraid of death. It's just a bit annoying."

"But you also…"

Ikasa cut Shirone off.

"Fine. I'll let you go."

Shirone looked surprised, and Ikasa snorted dismissively.

"It's not for you. I just want to see that woman who challenges an angel's authority fail."

"I'll come back to save you. Wait for me."

Ikasa closed her eyes instead of responding.

As her halo expanded, the energy of Valhalla Action began to accumulate in the halo.

'The restraint is much stronger than expected.'

The calculation of Valhalla concluded it would take 9 hours and 43 seconds.

That was the time it would take Ikasa to destroy Akamai with the power of his incarnation.

'But it seems capturing a regular angel would be difficult.'

If she hadn't become a fallen angel, she wouldn't have been caught so easily, a small consolation for her.

'To think I'd rely on a human.'

Ikasa took a deep breath and opened her eyes wide.

Valhalla Action!

"Kiiiiii!"

True Akamai let out a monstrous scream.

Its massive focal point moved rapidly like light, and its gelatinous body shriveled up.
