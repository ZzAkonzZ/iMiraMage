Chapter 227: Where is Shirone? (2)

Cheers and applause erupted. The students’ anticipation was greater than ever, as they were greeted by someone who had served as the principal of the kingdom’s top educational institution.

“I have heard of the fame of Alpheas Magic Academy, so I have no intention of interfering with the existing policy. However, the modern magic society pursues high efficiency and practicality. Pragmatism. This is the seed I wish to plant during my term as principal.”

The implication was that Alpheas Magic School's curriculum had been detached from real-world applications until now. It would be understandable for the students to feel indignant, they could only accept it since an archmage had stated it.

“This is serious. I knew this would happen.”

Iruki, who had attended the Royal Magic Academy, had a gut feeling about Olivia the moment he saw her.

He heard that he retired from his position as principal after many years, but he doesn't know what brought him to this point.

Similar comments circulated among the Class Four students.

“I had a feeling it would be like this. The predictions of <Spirit> were correct. We're in for a tough time starting tomorrow.”

“I heard her disciples are coming here too.”

“I know, I read it too. One of them is Dante, right?”

Shirone listened to their conversation. It seemed many students had already known about Olivia's appointment during the break.

“Iruki, what is <Spirit>?”

“An academic journal published by the Teachers’ Association. Most magic academy students are subscribers to <Spirit>. It covers things like graduation exam trends, the latest magic trends, and issues occurring at academies.”

“I see. Iruki, do you read it too?”

“Are you crazy? Why should I care about what other academies are doing? I do things my way.”

Shirone also asked Nade.

“What about you?”

“I used to borrow it from friends, but I don’t read it anymore. Since I’m going to be an engineer, I don’t find it that interesting.”

Iruki chimed in.

“But if it’s Dante, the academy will be turned upside down. He’s a guy who can be considered number one in the kingdom’s student rankings.”

Ranked #1 in the Kingdom. Shirone couldn't even guess the weight contained in those words.

What kind of student would he be? No, how would he feel?

Being number one in the entire kingdom.

On the way back to the dorm after the entrance ceremony, Iruki asked.

“Shirone, what are you going to do? I don’t think we’ll be able to see Principal Alpheas today.”

“It’s the first day of academy, so everyone will be busy. I’ll just go and see him during lunch time after class tomorrow morning.”

There was a mountain of questions he wanted to ask Alpheas. How much was known about the existence of Heaven, and what was the story behind Miro of space-time. But today wasn’t the right time. With that in mind, Shirone returned to his dorm, postponing his inquiries until tomorrow.

@

Shirone, who joined his friends early in the morning, entered the classroom to take the first class of the historic class.

The room was already about half full. Having always been the first to arrive in Class Five, this felt different.

As Shirone was getting nervous, the students in Class Four were also watching Shirone and his group's movements without knowing it.

In particular, Shirone was always the number one topic of interest for the students, as he was an unprecedented case of rising from Class Seven to Class Four in just half a year.

In the 50-year history of the academy, there have been students as outstanding as Shirone. However, those exceptional students were typically placed in higher classes from the start, making Shirone’s case unique.

Shirone felt strange seeing those who had been his seniors until yesterday now as his classmates. On the other hand, Nade and Iruki waved their hands as if it was the most natural thing and sat down in empty seats.

“You finally made it. Nice to meet you.”

A hand suddenly appeared in front of Shirone's eyes. When he looked up, a smiling boy with a round chin and a pleasant demeanor stood there.

It was Chris Boyle, ranked first in Class Four, specializing in summoning magic.

As expected of a boy who was always described as a model student, his clothes were impeccable. His collar was buttoned up tight around his neck, and his hair was neatly parted in a 2:8 ratio.

Shirone said, holding Boyle's hand.

“I’m glad to see you too. Though I just barely made it here.”

“Haha! You don’t have to be so humble. This is the final gate to the advanced class. Now is the time to appeal to yourself rather than be humble.”

Although it was advice given with good intentions, it felt like he was looking down on Shirone. However, as Boyle said, this too was a way of asserting oneself.

“Thanks. Let’s get along well from now on.”

“Yes. Although, who knows how long that will be.”

“Huh? What do you mean?”

“What are you talking about? Of course, I’m talking about applying for the graduating class.”

Students in Class Four could apply for the graduation class at any time. This year, Amy had been the first to apply, followed shortly by Seriel. So far, eight students have entered the graduating class, so there are only two spots left.

“Usually, there’s a surge of registrants towards the end of the semester, but oddly enough, Amy took on the challenge so quickly that there aren’t many spots left. It’s a first-come-first-served basis.”

The students perked up their ears at Boyle's words. From Class Four onwards, results weren’t as important. It was a strategic decision whether to push for the graduation class or to further solidify one’s basics.

Of course, no one filled out their application for graduation in a rush.

Once you move on to the graduating class, you have no time to refine your basics. In addition, it is known that the shock of failing the graduation exam, which is held every year, is greater than one might think.

In fact, while 60 percent of those who passed the graduation exam did so on their first attempt, only 20 percent succeeded on their second attempt.

Considering such data, students carefully reviewed their decisions about moving to the graduation class.

Boyle asked.

“What about you? Of course, you plan on proving your worth in Class Four and taking one of the two remaining spots, right?”

Last night, Shirone also discussed the matter with his friends. And they concluded that it was premature.

Although he had scored over 80 in all subjects in Class Five, it had been mere improvisation to pass the exams.

There was a way to directly pass the graduation exam upon entering the graduation class, so even if he spent half a semester strengthening his basics, it wouldn’t be a loss.

“No. I’ll stay in Class Four for this semester.”

It was an unexpected statement for Boyle. He had assumed that Shirone, who had ascended to Class Four in record time, would challenge himself to break records.

Was it a deceptive strategy? That didn’t seem to be his personality. In any case, it wasn’t a bad thing if a strong competitor decided to take a step back.

“Then there’s nothing I can do. Anyway, let’s try our best. Iruki, Nade. Let’s do our best.”

“Yeah, well… not sure how hard we’ll work, though.”

Iruki said in a tone of annoyance. The students sensed that the three of them wouldn’t be advancing to the graduation class anytime soon.

It became an opportunity for them to reconsider their decision, as Shirone’s group would not have made such a decision without a reason.

It was no coincidence that the first person to greet Shirone and his group was Boyle, the leader of Class Four.

This was part of the power dynamics in Class Four.

That's why Azelio Pandora, ranked second, was in a bad mood.

When there was number one student named Amy, there was no need to worry because all the students were second students. However, the score difference between Boyle and Pandora, who were currently first and second, was only one point.

As Boyle's conversation neared an end, Pandora quietly approached Shirone and raised her hand.

“Hello?”

Nade let out a groan. He was a friend to everyone, and the fact that he was groaning was enough to tell her personality.

She was a well-known eccentric in the advanced class. A girl who thought she was the most extraordinary person in the world.

Shirone also knew that fact. However, the way she lived self-centeredly without any friends seemed lonely.

“Hello, Pandora. We’re in the same class now. Feels a bit strange.”

“Hoho, how does it feel to take classes with a beauty like me?”

Pandora tilted her upper body and sent a glance. In such a situation, even Shirone had to keep a straight face.

Though her appearance wasn’t unpleasant, her heavy makeup was overwhelming. His heart started beating strangely just from smelling her perfume. This was likely related to her specialty in Scent Magic.

“Oh my, look at your blushing face. Don’t be shy. Let’s be friends from now on.”

Pandora's blouse had two buttons fewer than usual, as if she had approached Shirone intentionally. When she leaned forward, Shirone wasn’t sure whether to close his eyes or keep looking. Either option felt like it might be impolite.

“Hey, what’s going on here? Shirone already has someone special.”

Nade covered Shirone's eyes. Then, for the sake of his blind friend, he peeked inside Pandora's blouse.

Pandora was too annoyed to care about the boys' stares.

Shirone’s someone special was none other than Carmis Amy.

When she was in the advanced class, no student dared to boast about their abilities.

Whether it was grades or looks, she was superior in everything. The reason she flirted with Shirone was to prove that she could make him her man if given the chance.

“Hmph, I know you're dating Senior Amy, but you can't even see her, right? Besides, she’ll pass the graduation exam and leave school soon. Shirone needs a new woman. A sophisticated and intelligent woman like me.”

Pandora subtly gathered her cherished ‘heart’ like a treasure.

“Anyway, you’d better ride the line well. I’ll catch up with Boyle sooner or later. If you guys stay in my line, I’ll look after you too.”

Iruki, who had been pulling Shirone towards him, shook his finger.

"Aren't you ignoring us too much? We’re the Shirone Line.”

“Hohoho! Since it’s your first time, enjoy yourself to the fullest. You’ll soon realize Class Four isn’t that easy. See you later, Shirone.”

Pandora blew a kiss with her hand and returned to her seat. Even before the first class started, Shirone felt overwhelmed. But he got a glimpse of how Class Four operated.

Boyle and Pandora, the first and second in the rankings, were aiming for the graduating class, while the other students below them were keeping an eye on them.

Shirone thought that it was a good thing that she gave up on graduating this time. He had no intention of avoiding competition, but he didn't want to have an emotional fight with his friends over the narrow gap that was decided on a first-come, first-served basis.

He decided that if he could achieve significant growth this year, he would apply for the graduation class early next year, just like Amy did.

The first three periods passed smoothly, and as they waited for the fourth period on mental theory, the teacher entered with three transfer students.

Students who regularly subscribed to <Spirit> magazine recognized them and started whispering.

Victor Savina.

Bonipar Closer.

Airhein Dante.

Student of the Royal Magic Academy in the capital Bashka, they were currently the most talked-about students in the kingdom.

In particular, Airhein Dante was a genius among geniuses with such outstanding talent that he was called the next-generation archmage even among current active mages.

“Now, now! Everyone, be quiet. These are the transfer students who will be attending Class Four from today. They will introduce themselves, so please greet them warmly.”

Dante went up to the podium and looked around at the students. His first impression was 'old fashion'. In his eyes, which had been enjoying the latest fashions in the capital, everything looked old-fashioned.

Dante fixed his gaze on Boyle. He was clearly dressed like a mama's boy, but his eyes were unnervingly confident.

‘Is that Shirone? It can’t be, right?’

It was different from the feeling he had heard from Olivia about Shirone. He didn’t know about his appearance, but at least the vibe didn’t match.

On one hand, a girl with a lot of makeup was flirting with him. Although her face was pretty, her makeup was so thick that he couldn't bear to look at her. If she went around the capital like that, she would have been branded a barmaid.

“Dante, what are you doing? Introduce yourself.”

Dante snapped out of his thoughts and spoke.

“Nice to meet you. My name is Airhein Dante. Let’s get along well.”

That was the end of the self-introduction.
