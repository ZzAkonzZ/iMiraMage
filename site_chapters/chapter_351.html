Chapter 351: Swordsmans Conviction (3)

"Do you think you're something just because you have a rank? Regardless of my grades, I haven't just been playing around. When it comes to the sword, I won't lose to anyone."

"Do you think divine transcendence is such a big deal?"

Reina looked at Rai in surprise.

She had mentioned Rian’s divine transcendence in passing before. But for Rai to remember it, given his self-centered nature, was unusual.

"Transcending the body? That's impressive. Should I applaud?"

Rian’s eyes flared up.

"What? Are you...!"

"If you rely on that, your body will break first."

Rian’s mouth shut.

"Yes, divine transcendence is a difficult state to achieve. But that doesn't mean it's great. The world is full of difficult yet useless things. Moving a mountain with a shovel might be harder. But no one does that. What do you plan to do by transcending the body? You're a human made of flesh and blood. Divine transcendence? You might get a bit stronger than others, but that's it."

It was a harsh truth.

Even Shirone, who had reached a similar state, couldn't refute it this time. Divine transcendence. Surpassing the limits of magic through the power of an avatar. But if you exceed the feedback, your mind will eventually collapse.

Rian gritted his teeth and barely managed to speak.

"Shut up. What do you know? You've never experienced it."

"Yes, I can't. Impressive. I support you."

Rai turned away again. Before leaving the room, he said,

"Congratulations. You've finally done something I can't."

Rian felt his sanity snap. He couldn't control his racing heart, and his body trembled involuntarily.

How effective divine transcendence would be in real combat was uncertain. But it was the only hope that had miraculously come after years of training.

"...Wait."

Rai ignored Rian and kept walking.

"I'm busy. Take care of yourself."

"I said wait!"

Rian’s shout echoed through the room. No one was surprised because they had expected this outburst.

Rai turned back, clearly annoyed, and Rian walked up to him.

"Ozent Rai, I challenge you to a duel. Let's settle this with real swords."

Reina rubbed her temples as if she had expected this.

"Rian, you keep..."

"No, I'm serious. If I lose to Rai, I'll go back to school. How about that? Is that a fair condition?"

Rai scoffed.

"I don't care if you go to school or not. Quit if you want. Just don't bother me."

"Haha! Really? You're not running away because you're scared?"

Rai's face twisted slightly.

"Scared of you? That's ridiculous."

"Oh, so you're busy? You always brag about ending me with one strike. Or are you saying a swordsman is just someone who plays with ranks and talks big?"

"Sigh."

Rai sighed tiredly and nodded sympathetically.

"Fine, I'll duel you. Wait for me in the training ground. Get ready."

As he turned to leave, he looked back at Rian.

"And don't provoke me again. You're not good at it."

"That...!"

Rian was about to rush out when Shirone grabbed his arm.

"Calm down, Rian. You're the one who got provoked."

"Oh."

Rian realized this and calmed down.

But his anger didn't disappear. Rai had turned the tables with just one word, making Rian furious. Shirone thought Rian was too naive.

Tess, who had been listening outside, entered cautiously.

"Rian, are you really going to do this? If you lose, you have to go back to the swordsmanship school."

"Hmph, have you ever seen me go back on my word?"

"That's not what I mean..."

Tess glanced at Kuan. Since Kuan had already stated he wouldn't rescind the withdrawal, even if Rian changed his mind, there would be additional problems.

But Rian didn't care.

If he lost to Rai, he would kneel and beg to return to school. If they didn't accept him, he would wait at the school gate.

"I'll do it. It's a promise. But I'm not going back. I challenged him with the determination to win."

Rian looked at Clump.

"Grandfather, you permit this, right?"

Clump, arms crossed, thought seriously. Finally, his eyes opened wide.

"Let's go to the training ground."

The training ground was behind Reina's mansion. Besides Reina, Clump and Rai lived there, but Rai was always busy with deployments, and Clump mostly stayed at the general's barracks, so it wasn't well-maintained.

In the small clearing, Rai, who had unpacked his gear, stood with a sword at his waist, waiting for everyone. While Rian prepared, Reina approached Rai with a worried expression.

"Hey, are you really going to do this? I don't like it. There's no need for brothers to fight with real swords."

"Let him do what he wants. Otherwise, that fool won't agree."

"What if something happens?"

"We'll deal with it then. Anyway, isn't this good for you? That fool will go back to school."

If the duel ended safely and Rian accepted defeat, Reina would be relieved. But the two were like oil and water, so her concern didn't disappear.

"Alright, just go easy and finish quickly."

"I'm busy too. I'll do that."

Reina shrugged. Rai, despite his self-centered nature, was a sensible brother who didn't need nagging, unlike Rian.

‘Well, there's someone else to worry about.’

Rian, glaring at Rai from the corner of the training ground, took deep breaths.

Tess approached and vigorously massaged Rian’s shoulders and arms. His muscles were tense, indicating how significant this duel was for him.

"Come on! Relax!"

Tess did her best to boost Rian’s spirits. She didn't want him to quit school, but she hated seeing him humiliated even more.

Rian remained silent, focusing on calming his mind. With his family, Kuan, and Shirone watching, he couldn't afford to lose.

Reina approached and placed a hand on Rian’s shoulder.

"I'm neutral in this duel. So don't overdo it."

"Hmph, you should worry about Rai. I'm not the same as before."

Reina said nothing.

Rai wasn't considering defeat, and neither was Reina. Having wielded a sword herself, she knew the gap in their skills better than anyone.

‘Rian has no chance of winning. Rai won't go easy on him for the sake of his brother's pride.’

When Clump and Kuan entered the training ground, Rian took the greatsword his grandfather had given him.

As Rian walked to the battlefield, Shirone spoke from behind.

"Rian, make sure you win."

Rian turned and smiled. The tension had vanished.

"No problem. Leave it to me."

When Rian reached the center of the training ground, Rai drew his black sword. It was longer and thinner than a regular longsword, designed for Ozent swordsmanship, and made of black steel, making it very hard.

Rai swung the black sword in an X shape and then pointed it skyward.

"Let's start quickly. I want to rest."

"You won't be resting; you'll be bedridden. I don't know about your deployment, but you should cancel it."

"Whatever. Just come at me."

Rai didn't even want to respond to Rian’s words.

Rian found it frustrating. Rai, who mastered any technique instantly, couldn't see Rian, who had no natural talent, as family.

The sneer Rian had seen during their childhood duel flashed through his mind.

"Anyway, thanks for accepting the duel. Our bad blood ends today."

Rian held the greatsword with one hand and aimed it. The precarious balance, pushing human limits, was intimidating just to look at.

Rai lowered the black sword diagonally. It was so long it almost touched the ground.

The greatsword and black sword were evenly matched in length. But the black sword, being lighter, would be faster.

‘I can't match him in technique.’

Rian planned his strategy. It was as straightforward as his personality.

‘Overwhelm him with strength!’

As he clenched his teeth, his eyes lit up. Powerful footwork propelled his body forward.

"Here I come!"

Rian raised the greatsword and charged. The force of the heavy greatsword falling from a height of three meters was unimaginable.

At the same time, black flashes shot from Rai's body, targeting Rian’s openings. Rian couldn't even swing the sword halfway before switching to defense.

Rai's fast and sharp attacks hammered the greatsword's surface like a blacksmith's hammer.

"Ugh!"

Rian’s body flinched at the unexpected impact.

It felt different from Tess's light sword. Swift and precise, but with the unique strength of Ozent swordsmanship.

Once he lost the initiative, Rian was endlessly pushed back. The process was so one-sided that regaining control seemed impossible.

Rai moved as if dancing alone in the clearing.

When Rian reached the edge of the training ground, the spectators couldn't help but gasp. Even Tess, who supported Rian, was mesmerized by Rai's swordsmanship.

"Yaaah!"

Rian’s sword slashed horizontally. The air sizzled as it burned.

Barely creating space, Rian took a step forward. But Rai's lightning-fast attacks forced him back again.

Rai didn't stop attacking. It was as if he had Rian cornered and was beating him.

Rian’s counterattacks decreased, and soon he struggled even to defend.

‘Tsk, no progress at all.’

Rai didn't want to waste any more time on a boring match.

He relentlessly hammered Rian’s sword, then reversed his black sword and drove it into the ground. As the blade sank in, a vibration traveled up through Rian’s feet.

"What...!"

Rian looked down in surprise as the ground swelled like air was filling it, then exploded with a bang.

"Ugh!"

The shockwave from the exploding ground struck Rian’s feet, causing his calf muscles to spasm.

The technique was called Earth Explosion.

It was a sword technique Rai had mastered at just twelve years old, now driven into the ground.

Rian’s knees buckled, and he couldn't maintain his balance. He ended up sitting down, lifting the greatsword with only his upper body strength.

When Rai's black sword struck with a loud clang, vibrations traveled up the greatsword.

Rian gritted his teeth and held back a scream.

It felt like his arm muscles were tearing apart. The shockwave that traveled through his body burst out, causing his shirt to rip as if stabbed by dozens of knives.

Rian glared heavily at Rai. Rai's expression remained indifferent as he held the black sword.

The spectators sensed the duel was over. The shockwave had torn through Rian’s body, and even if he looked fine on the outside, his muscles were likely too damaged to move properly.

Ozent Rai, the second son of the Ozent family.

His skill was the real deal.
