Weren't the highest-risk targets Code C?!"

It won't succeed."

"The forces currently attacking Code A consist of 100 Wind Guards, the Imperial Guard Units Inner, Mid, and Outter, and Captain of the Imperial Guard Kido."

It showed just how much they feared Kuan.

I’ll mobilize the troops!

We need to move out—!”

Then what does that mean for the Empress’s defenses?"

They were almost nonexistent.

"…Ah."

As realization dawned on everyone, Albino slammed the table and snapped his head up.

"There’s no time!"

Blood shot through his eyes, signaling the urgent crisis.

"Dalli—go kill that bitch!"

"Yahoo! It's hunting time!"

A ruffian in a chicken-head mask sprinted after Empress Uorin, who was wrapped in a hooded cloak.

“Kahaha! Gotcha!”

“Aaaah!”

Only to reveal a silver-wigged decoy.

“Damn it! Another fake!

We’re not getting paid for this shit, right?”

He raised his sword, ready to behead her in one stroke—

But the leader of the Chicken Heads shook his head.

“Don’t kill her. There are too many big shots here. If things get out of hand, we’ll be the ones paying the price.”

“Haha! Boss, we all heard it, right?

Satan’s taking the blame for everything anyway.”

"Yeah, but still… That guy… Hmm?"

The leader blinked.

"Wait. What was his name again?"

The name Havitz did not come to mind.

"…Tch!"

Meanwhile, as Shirone sprinted through Delta Headquarters, a sudden grimace twisted his face.

‘Something just happened.’

Havitz had vanished.

The feeling of being misaligned with reality was sickening—

But Shirone still grasped the truth.

‘Vanishing has been activated.’

The killing game had resumed.

"Who’s the next target?"

Whoever it was—

Shirone had only one course of action.

“Mika.”

Yes.

Unified neural waves detected: 7,033,019.

99.8% of the total required.

‘Too slow.’

Thirteen seconds was enough time to kill someone.

"There! Over there!"

The forces of various nations—all with her head in their sights—were closing in.

She activated Future Sight.

'Not this path.'

The time field, distorted by Parath’s influence, caused a massive headache.

Hearing the echo of her pursuers’ voices closing in.

"Find her! She couldn’t have gotten far!"

Her fingernails scraped against the marble floor.

‘I won’t die!’

Pulling her hood deeper, she gritted her teeth and crawled forward.

‘You think I’ll die? I am Uorin. I’ll crawl through hell itself if I have to—’

The moment her migraine lifted—

She ran again.

‘I will survive!’

As she raced through the maze-like corridors—

A figure suddenly drew close beside her.

“Your Majesty!”

Her heart stopped—

Until she realized it was Gando.

She let out a shaky breath.

"Support me. I’m too dizzy."

"You’ve done well to hold out. From here on—I’ll take over. We can escape Delta."

Even her Future Sight agreed.

"Alright. Let’s go."

But there was one thing Future Sight could never predict.

“I was waiting for you.”

Havitz spoke.

But in Vanishing mode, no one could perceive him.

"Your mind is an open book to me."

The first kill was only a setup.

With Kido gone, Uorin was completely defenseless against Satan.

“I’ll be taking you now.”

His sword flashed toward her neck—

As he absorbed her History Search.

Mika spoke.

Unified neural waves detected: 7,047,113.

Elykia is complete.

‘Now!’

And activated Miracle Stream.

His eyes radiated light—

His entire body trembling with raw power.

"Hnnngh…!"

The strength of seven million minds was enough to rewrite the laws of the world.

‘Where are you hiding, Havitz?!’

It was a power capable of nullifying the corrupted laws split from Ultima.

“HAVITZ!”

And in that moment—

“Die, Uorin.”

Havitz's Vanishing was undone.

"Huh?"

Havitz's sword was already within striking distance.

'Why is he here?!'

Gando shoved her aside.

His neck was severed in an instant.

‘Mother.’

He seemed to smile—

But with his head flying through the air, she could never be sure.

Blood gushed before her eyes—

And before she knew it, she had collapsed onto the floor.

"What…?"

Havitz turned to the corridor.

“How did you know?”

Reading Shirone’s divine frequency, he glanced back at Uorin.

“You got lucky.”

Before she could even process what had happened, a paralyzing shock took hold of Uorin’s mind.

‘This… is Satan.’

Even though his perfect strike had failed, Havitz showed no emotion at all.

"Hmm."

A fly buzzed within Havitz’s left pupil before it shot out like a tentacle, grabbing onto Gando’s face.

Uorin watched, forcing herself not to gasp, as his face was absorbed.

That could have been her.

"That makes two."

Havitz flicked the blood off his sword and turned.

"One hour remains."

Outside Delta Headquarters, everyone engaged in battle was suddenly bathed in Shirone’s light.

"What… the hell?"

The quantum transmission made them all feel like one massive, interconnected being.

Of course, as soon as the light faded, everything returned to normal.

But something was seared into their memory—

The state of Satan, as Shirone saw him.

"Havitz…"

"He killed Gando."

From now on, each nation would be responsible for any murders that occurred within the next hour.

‘A ceasefire.’

The bloodbath that had swept through the Holy War suddenly stopped, leaving Kashan’s forces deep in thought.

‘This is dangerous.’

Even if Havitz had withdrawn, Uorin remained completely vulnerable.

‘We must protect Her Majesty.’

With Gando dead, the Kashan Imperial Guard felt a primal instinct—a deep-seated urge to return to their Empress’s side.

"Khh!"

But the one suffering the most was Kido, who was still battling Kuan.

‘Uorin, do you really need to keep pushing this? You almost died.’

"Capture Siena."

For a ruthless swordmaster, only one duty remained—to carry out the Empress’s orders.

As one hundred Wind Guards descended upon Armin, Kido was left with no choice but to engage as well.

"Tch!"

His spear spun like a wheel, creating a swirling barrier, but Kuan, using his external gravity, matched his movements.

‘Strong.’

"Armin! It’s dangerous!" Keira screamed.

Even with Flicker magic, it was impossible to dodge one hundred Wind Guards.

‘Damn it!’

‘Stop!’

A desperate spell was cast,

And thirty Wind Guards were instantly frozen in time.

Hell’s Prison—0.666 seconds of hell.

"I can’t be the only one pissed off, right?"

shattering the time field.

The blades only grazed his fingertips.

And with lightning speed, returned to the main formation.

"Siena!"

Seizing that opening, Kido lunged with his spear.
