Chapter 491:

"Hmm."

Meatgun tightened his grip on the dagger, but Etella's strength was so formidable it felt almost mechanical.

Even in the tense situation where the blade was trembling in front of her neck, Meatgun was puzzled.

'What's going on? She was definitely dead.'

Life gradually returned to Etella's wide-open eyes.

Yin-Yang Wave Fist Revitalization Technique.

A technique that momentarily stops breathing and increases abdominal pressure to explosively boost strength.

However, Frankwine's attack was so powerful that it caused a momentary cardiac arrest.

'If I had been a little later, it would have been dangerous.'

Fully regaining consciousness, Etella slowly pushed Meatgun's wrist away.

Sensing the futility, Meatgun abandoned the stab and leaped back just as Etella's kick shot up.

Meatgun, who almost suffered the same critical hit as Frankwine, shuddered and touched his groin.

Unless he had sold his soul to the devil, he would never want to be hit there.

Etella quickly got up and took a fighting stance.

Still aching from the blows, she straightened her back and snapped,

"Are you crazy? Selling your soul to the devil."

Etella hadn't seen Satan with her own eyes, but her monk's intuition told her.

No human could harbor such evil within them otherwise.

"Following the boss in business, I realized something."

Meatgun tilted his head to one side.

"If it can be sold, nothing is precious."

As Etella sprang forward, Meatgun raised his fists and prepared to fight.

As the essence of the Yin-Yang Wave Fist stormed in, Meatgun continuously ducked and dodged the attacks.

Mirror Cycle - Event Reflection.

The moment Etella's fist struck his side, the same impact exploded in her side.

"Ugh!"

Taking advantage of the opportunity, Meatgun struck Etella's face, sending her spinning back several meters.

"You can't touch my body."

Meatgun, with an air of invincibility, rubbed his fist and slowly approached Etella.

His ability, Mirror Cycle, reflected events by perceiving his body as a mirror.

This ability manifested through the distortion of time, space, and mind in his world.

He perceived a mirror that reflected events with his mind, distorted space to implement that mirror, and finally distorted time to project the mind into reality until the mirror was implemented in a specific space.

"It's an embarrassing technique since it's been broken before."

To prevent event reflection, one must break the mirror in Meatgun's perception.

Gaold had done it at the headquarters, but Etella's fists were far from capable of such a feat.

"More than enough to handle one woman."

This time, Meatgun initiated the attack.

Realizing the ability to reflect events, the psychological pressure was immense, regardless of its effectiveness.

However, through long training, Etella overcame her fear and counterattacked Meatgun's assault.

In terms of martial arts, Etella was superior, but Meatgun had no reason to avoid her.

"Huuuup!"

As all events entering Meatgun were reflected, waves began to spread through Etella's body.

It felt as if she was standing in front of a mirror, hitting herself instead of Meatgun.

This conveyed that if she delivered the final wave here, it would be herself, not Meatgun, who would break.

"I told you, it won't work."

Meatgun's voice grew faint, and his entire body trembled as his consciousness began to fade.

'I won't give up! I must…!'

She had to stop Frankwine.

After losing to Arcane, her resolve for good had only strengthened.

And now, facing her lifelong enemy, that resolve filled her entire being, erasing any doubt about her commitment to good.

Her head felt like it was splitting open, but it was a sensation of transcendental liberation rather than pain.

'What is this?'

Thump! Thump! Thump! Thump! Thump!

Each of Etella's punches penetrated the Mirror Cycle, delivering a dull impact to Meatgun's body.

Yin-Yang Wave Fist Ultimate Technique - Divine Transcendence Strike.

'Damn it!'

A technique that directly transmits the power of the avatar to the opponent's avatar.

As the shockwave shook Meatgun's mind beyond his body, cracks began to form in the Mirror Cycle.

Realizing the danger, Meatgun retreated, but his legs had already given out, causing him to stagger.

Simultaneously, Etella rapidly approached and struck his abdomen with both hands.

The Mirror Cycle's effect was still active, but there was no hesitation in her movements to shatter the mirror.

Thunder Strike.

Boom!

The numerous waves swirling inside were swept away by a single wave, causing a massive shockwave to explode.

Meatgun, hit in the abdomen, slowly lowered his fists and stood still for a long time.

As he staggered back, a mouthful of blood gushed from his mouth.

"Huuuup!"

Etella bent over, clenched her fists, and gathered the waves spreading through her body.

Thanks to the shock to Meatgun's mind, the mirror's clarity had diminished, but she would have delivered the same attack regardless.

Without such resolve, she couldn't have delivered the avatar's strike to her opponent.

Paradoxically, that conviction shattered her enemy, and Etella approached Meatgun, who lay sprawled on the ground.

"Don't move. If I treat you now, you might survive."

Meatgun didn't respond.

He had never begged for his life from anyone before.

"…You're a strange woman."

Meatgun struggled to lift his hand and took a disk from his inner pocket, throwing it at Etella's feet.

"Go to the boss."

Meatgun's eyes slowly closed.

"He'll probably like it."

Etella put the disk in her pocket and bowed her head in mourning for Meatgun's death.

"Whew!"

When she opened her eyes again, Etella's gaze was much clearer than before the fight with Meatgun.

'I can feel it, where he is.'

After awakening her avatar technique, everything became clear.

Frankwine's avatar was not human.

Absolute evil.

A massive aura of evil was blowing in from afar, from Zebul.

Rumble!

The walls of heaven were collapsing from all sides.

The noise was so deafening that even shouting right next to someone couldn't be heard, and the rising dust made it impossible to see an inch ahead.

Despite losing their sight and hearing, the rebels showed no signs of stopping their advance.

"Sister! We're only 10 kilometers from Aravot!"

Hearing Lena's transmission, hope began to light up in Kanya's eyes for the first time.

While most of the rebels had escaped through the collapsed walls into Zebul, the 1st Command remained in Matei, continuing their advance.

Currently, Matei was the only strategic point that could block the path to Zebul, and someone had to stay behind to hold off the enemy's pursuit.

'We can win! We can really win!'

If they could just reach Aravot, if they could just reach Anke Ra, the war would be over.

Ra wouldn't move.

Being an absolute existence like the sun, Ra wouldn't engage in battle or flee.

Kanya surveyed the surroundings through the multivision.

Everyone was fighting fiercely without regard for their lives, but the ones achieving the most outstanding results were not humans but machines.

Ancient weapon Babel for archangels.

The power output from its powerful engine surpassed the strength of giants, and its blade-like fingertips easily tore through tough muscles.

'Amazing. Who on earth created that…'

Kanya hurriedly stopped her thoughts.

As a member of the Mecha tribe, she marveled at Babel's design, but admiring an enemy was unthinkable.

-Enemy sighted! A large force!

Hearing the transmission, Kanya snapped back to attention and checked the vision.

A massive force of giants was charging, filling her field of view.

"We'll take care of it! Cover us!"

As 120 members of the Norse unit stopped fighting and prepared collective magic, the Guroi unit swiftly moved to protect them.

Ancient magic Estacion, possible only when over 100 mages connected their minds.

The atmosphere within a 100-meter radius was torn apart, starting to shred the flesh of the giant unit.

"Whew!"

Even watching through the vision, the ferocious power of the magic was palpable.

"Sister! It's not working!"

Lena shouted in a terrified voice.

Despite being hit by Estacion, none of the giants fell.

Their basic durability was on a different level from the giants they had fought so far.

They were officers who had reached the 5th stage of the Art of One.

"Crush the enemies! They're just insects!"

The giants swung their weapons, destroying the rebel lines.

With each flash of the blade, Guroi was cut down, and people were sent flying by the mere wind of the sword.

-Map weapon activated. Codename Babel.

Babel, which had descended to the ground, transformed into map weapon mode.

Its upper body opened wide, its limbs anchored to the ground, and it aimed its cannon, embedded with a giant glass sphere, at the giants.

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

As the electron cannons fired in bursts, holes were punched through the bodies of the giants filling the field of view.

Kanya, along with all the rebels, watched the scene in a daze.

The only emotion they felt was a small but intensely realistic sense of hope that they might actually win.

"The struggle of insects is so pathetic I can't bear to watch."

On the wall of Matei, one giant watched the standoff between giants and humans.

Giant Corps Commander Girshin.

In his hand, which fluttered a black cloak, was a long, broad double-edged sword.

Having reached the 7th stage of the Art of One, he was undoubtedly the second most powerful giant, though he could never surpass Ymir, who was at the 10th stage.

"Ymir, I will end this war on your behalf."

Girshin kicked off the wall and leaped to the ground.

As he landed and swung his sword, rebels within a 10-meter radius were cut in half before they even knew what hit them.

"Enemy! Surround him!"

The rebels who spotted Girshin turned around in unison.

They still didn't realize what kind of monster stood before them, and by the time they did, their brains and bodies were already separated.

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

Girshin didn't stop and charged into the heart of the rebel forces.

Like a wind playing with feathers, humans were swept away with his every move, and blood sprayed into the air with each swing of his sword.

"Lena! It's dangerous!"

Kanya, seeing this through the multivision, quickly twisted Guroi's direction.

Just by seeing Girshin's single strike, she could tell he was on a different level from any enemy they had fought before.

As Girshin, who had cut through the rebels in a straight line, raised his double-edged sword towards Lena, Kanya blocked his path and aimed her cannon.

"Foolish."

Before she could pull the trigger, the cannon was sliced, and the body was split in two.

The cockpit hit the ground with a tremendous impact, but Kanya desperately held onto her consciousness.

Through every screen of the multivision, she saw Girshin swinging his sword.

"Ugh!"

As she pressed the emergency escape button, Guroi was dismembered like a toy.

Lena, thrown out of her seat and rolling on the ground, looked around without a moment to check her own safety.

"Lena!"

Girshin, having cut through Guroi, was charging towards Lena.

"Nooooo!"

Just as Kanya's heart was about to stop along with Lena's life, a gray flash flew in and blocked Girshin's path.

Clang!

Even amidst the chaotic noise, the clash of metal against metal pierced Kanya's ears clearly.

"Oh?"

Girshin raised an eyebrow at the appearance of a machine that blocked the strength of a giant at the 7th stage of the Art of One.

"I remember. It was fun back then."

"Babel will protect Kanya and Lena."

Kanya hurriedly crawled to Lena and hugged her sister, who had fainted from shock.

"Why?"

There was no reason for Babel to deactivate its map weapon mode and come to save her and her sister in the middle of a battle.

It was an entirely human decision.

Even in Shirone's algorithm, protecting Kanya and Lena was the third priority.

But that was why Babel moved.

Kanya could never know, but from the moment Girshin appeared, the first and second priorities in her mind were erased.

And that meant the probability of victory, calculated through Babel's transcendent computation, converged to zero percent.
