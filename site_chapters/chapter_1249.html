Chapter 1249:

Anchal spoke.

“Your Majesty, there are ten minutes remaining until the power transmission of the Pama-Gwangcheon Core is complete.”

Her tone was even more reverent than usual—because Jin Kang was standing at the edge of death.

“Khak!”

Jin Kang coughed up blood.

“Heh…”

Half-mad already.

He acknowledged it himself, but in truth, he was savoring the feeling.

Seongeum…

He probably wouldn’t get the chance to see his daughter’s face.

Could I dare wish for such a luxury?

“Your Majesty, please issue the command. Once the transmission begins, it cannot be undone. This is your last chance to turn back.”

Despite the immense weight of the decision, Jin Kang looked at Anchal with gentle eyes.

“Thank you.”

He wished that there would be no regrets.

“Your Majesty…”

Anchal bit back the tears welling in her one eye and returned his gaze with unwavering resolve.

“Please give the order.”

Jin Kang’s expression twisted, turning almost demonic.

“Kill them.”

All of them.

Rian ran. And kept running.

“Out of the way!”

Shirone’s body was light as a feather, but the weight of his life was far greater than Rian’s own.

“Yaaah!”

The blade of a yaksha protecting his lord cleaved through the Black Chains.

There’s no end to them.

Even beyond the field of vision, more and more Black Chains swarmed in.

Distance remaining… roughly 40 kilometers.

It was a distance he could cover in no time if he pushed himself—but breaking through the Black Chains was the problem.

No, even arriving is a problem. While Jin Seong Eum’s chains are being purified, Shirone will be completely defenseless.

Rian made his decision.

“I’ll protect him.”

He didn’t need to calculate it. In any situation, Shirone’s life was the top priority.

The Black Chains flew in again.

—Foolish sinner. Why do you reject purification and bring chaos to the laws of Hell?

A swarm beyond counting spiraled through the air, converging their power into a single mass.

“Fuuuuh!”

Gripping his greatsword in reverse, Rian charged head-on.

Don’t worry, Shirone.

As he gritted his teeth, the world trembled, and an immense surge of power flooded into his right arm.

“Yaaaaah!”

As his shoulder rotated, the muscle in his forearm vaporized—and the mass of Black Chains exploded in a flash.

—Ooooooooh…

As their echoing cries faded, Rian looked down at his right arm—reduced to bone.

Smi-le… smi-le…

Ogent’s bloodline began restoring his body, but the healing was clearly slower than before.

There’s not much time left.

Even if his Idea was from divine transcendence, there was still a limit to how fast a biological body could recover.

“It’s fine.”

Pouring strength into his barely healed arm, Rian grabbed his greatsword and kept walking.

Even if I’m destroyed… my Idea will not be broken.

Shirone stood before the massive hole torn in the sky beyond the Pyramid of Truth.

The All-Seeing Eye stared directly at him.

“What are you thinking?”

Shirone asked, but as before, the eye gave no response.

“Do you think you're defining me?”

He wasn’t afraid. He simply wanted to do everything he could before stepping into the unknown.

“Is Goffin in there?”

He recalled the reaction he saw at the Micah Ruins. Was it thought, consciousness? The eye remained unmoving.

“Answer me...”

A Photon Cannon materialized in Shirone’s hand.

“I said answer me!”

A beam of light shot toward the hole in the sky—and from the other side, a beam just like it shot back.

As if he had thrown a ball into a mirror.

“Alright.”

It seemed that God had no intention of meeting halfway in either of their worlds.

“If that’s what you want… then I’ll come to you.”

Finishing a deep breath, Shirone cloaked himself in Miracle Stream and launched himself into the rift.

As his head crossed the boundary where the beams had clashed, the All-Seeing Eye vanished and a pitch-black space unfolded.

“Yania Idumor.”

If the absence of light was everything, then there was no way he should be able to see his own hand.

Nothingness—or something like that.

A reversal of thought.

He had experienced it before in High Gear, so Shirone quickly regained his mental balance.

“Is it really that different?”

High Gear was a lower level of reality, but this Outer World didn’t seem all that special.

Just...

The space changed.

“It’s a unique focusing method called sequential counting.”

Etella was speaking.

“If any of you have ever counted from one to one hundred—whether in your mind or aloud—please raise your hand.”

Among the students of Alpheas Magic School, hands went up, but Shirone’s gaze was distant and indifferent.

“Everything’s just a grand illusion.”

Just by recalling it, he returned to Class Five’s integrated lecture session at his alma mater.

Etella continued.

“Alright, then how about from one to one thousand?”

While students glanced around at each other, Shirone quietly studied the familiar faces.

There was Nade, who had once been awkward around him, and Iruki, with whom he had clashed the day they met.

“Ha! You were all so young.”

Though, of course, so was he.

A faint smile formed, but it was soon replaced by sadness as he turned his eyes away.

“This isn’t my world.”

It was just one among countless infinitely nested bubbles of universes.

“Sequential counting, as the name implies, is the skill of listing numbers. Unlike language, numbers…”

Etella suddenly paused.

“Shirone?”

As she locked eyes with him, Etella's expression turned grave.

“You…”

She asked,

“Who are you?”

All the students in the advanced class turned to look at Shirone, but he had nothing to say.

“No… Who are you? You’re not Shirone.”

Etella was certain.

Just a moment ago, his eyes had been sparkling with curiosity—but now, they were completely different.

“He’s not a student. No… this is beyond comparison, the gaze of someone who’s reached the edge of the world.”

Her instincts said it aloud.

“...God?”

The students began to murmur.

“What’s going on? Why is the teacher acting like this?”

Sensing the growing unrest—and feeling he was disturbing this world—Shirone rose to his feet.

“Professor Etella.”

The person he respected the most.

“Thank you.”

Feeling a strange sense of farewell, Etella reached out her hand.

“Wait, just a moment—!”

Darkness again.

“Phew…”

He wondered what was happening now in that universe he’d just visited—the one he’d mistaken for his own.

“A meaningless question.”

There were, after all, infinite versions of that universe.

“I can’t go back now.”

Thanks to his experience in High Gear, Shirone could find a place his heart belonged—but that wasn’t this.

“Outer World God…”

Now it was time to confront Him.

Nane must’ve come here too.

What had he realized? Where was he now?

No way…?

Shirone stopped walking.

If this was a space where only illusion existed, then there must be only one way to encounter God.

“The only illusion.”

He turned his head—and there, like a mirror, was his own reflection.

“But there’s no mirror.”

As Shirone slowly approached, the other version of himself matched his movements, closing the distance.

The other raised his hand—slowly—toward him.

Ymir’s Black Hole.

Shirone regained his clarity amidst an overwhelming sense of unity and found himself in a dark space.

As he tested the world of illusions and thoughts of Nane surfaced, he suddenly stopped walking.

“The only illusion.”

A god is the result—unique and singular—so no matter the illusion, there is only one place it can reach.

Shirone turned his head.

“But there’s no mirror.”

The two Shirones from simultaneous events approached each other with perfectly identical steps and placed their hands together.

After savoring the moment for a while, Shirone lowered his hand, and the mirror phenomenon returned to nothingness.

Which Shirone was he?

He was both the Shirone who had experienced Yorahan’s Transcendent Dream and the Shirone who had not.

“There’s no contradiction.”

Shirone realized why the version of himself inside Ymir’s dream had been disconnected from reality.

“I was blocked by a wall of logic.”

Here, there are no contradictions.

Infinite freedom where anything one does becomes correct—that is the true nature of the Ultima System.

“You must have been so lonely, Nane.”

Perhaps in the vast emptiness that couldn’t withstand even the extremes of justice, he had screamed in despair.

“I should meet him too.”

But first, extracting Ultima was the priority.

However, being the one and only god within infinite illusions meant that causality could not be reversed.

“A reversal of thought won’t take me back. Even if I obtain Ultima, if I can’t bring it into reality…”

Resolved, Shirone recalled:

“God.”

Countless humans imagine their own version of god, but whatever they envision, there is only one god.

That single illusion finally guided Shirone to the place where god resides.

The material was too smooth to be stone, too soft to be metal.

The entire space was white.

“Is this the highest level of the universe humanity can reach?”

No, it wasn’t.

“It’s just clinging to results through imaginary time. This isn’t the end of human potential.”

If one were to break the chain of causality crafted by god, humanity could reach even further into the future.

Perhaps that was Goffin’s final message…

“Beyond infinity.”

Calmly taking his first step, Shirone walked down a hallway devoid of any distinguishing features.

Then, for the first time, he saw something else.

“Hehehe! Hehehehe!”

Arius, crawling on all fours like a dog, was spinning in circles.

“Woof! Woof! Hehehehe!”

Shirone reversed his thinking once more and called out:

“Arius.”

Arius suddenly stopped and turned his head, crawling over.

“Uhh… Haha! You came! You finally came, Shirone!”

“What happened to you?”

Arius kept bowing.

“Ohh! Our god! Save humanity! Take this wretched body! Puhaha!”

He was half-mad.

“How many universes have you wandered through…?”

Even if it’s just an instant—after drifting through countless illusions, anyone would end up like this.

“Huuuuhuuuu!”

Arius began to cry.

“It’s all meaningless. You can go anywhere. Be anything. A dog, an insect, even air. God is infinitely vast, infinitely expansive… No one can endure it. We couldn’t even become insignificant beings!”

That is what infinity is.

“Uwaaah! I’m nothing! Just kill me already! In this damned place, even death becomes an illusion…!”

“Arius!”

Shirone’s stern voice silenced him.

“Pull yourself together. That’s just god’s trait. You know it. With Ultima, humanity gains the power to fight. Can you extract it? You said Miro must’ve left a way.”

“Miro…”

My master.

“Extraction’s easy.”

Arius spread his arms.

“Here, this is Ultima. Take it. Carve it from the wall, breathe it from the air. Everything here—the very concept of this space—is Ultima. It’s the opposite of reality. Here, integration is the default. No contradictions. Just declare the result, and the cause will assemble itself.”

He buried his face in his hands.

“Haah… The problem is, we can’t go back. We’re trapped here. I never should’ve come.”

Shirone stood, having fully grasped the situation.

“Where is god?”

Arius trembled.

“You met him, didn’t you?”

“I don’t want to go… The silence, the vibrations… I’ll go mad.”

“Then we need to find a way back. I have an idea. Tell me—where is god?”

“…Where else?”

Arius smirked.

“Obviously—where god is.”

In that instant, a surge like electricity passed through their bodies, and a boundless white space unfolded.

Floating three meters above the ground, suspended in the air, was a glass sphere the size of a human head.

Though it wasn’t quite accurate to call it that.

Various electric spectrums flickered within its membrane in a spherical form—one could only guess.

“Is that… you?”

The glass sphere did not reply in words, but Shirone instinctively felt the shift in the electric patterns.

“That is…”

The ultra-intelligent computational entity—

God.
