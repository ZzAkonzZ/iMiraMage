Chapter 1180:

Jive Capital Hospital.

Gangster Travis had no trouble slipping past what should have been a heavily guarded facility.

‘Even during lunch hour, it shouldn’t be this empty.’

He had met with Gith’s advisor, Myron, exactly two hours ago.

Myron had handed him a large sum of money with a simple instruction—silence the journalist, Michael.

Myron had said, “You know what this means, don’t you? Just make sure he doesn’t write the article. Use any means necessary.”

Feeling the weight of the briefcase in his hands, Travis felt another shiver run down his spine.

“That’s all?”

It seemed too easy.

“That’s all. I’ve handled the preparations and cleanup, so all you need to do is go in and do your job. But preferably, no deaths. You’re a professional, aren’t you? If you pull this off, you won’t need to play thug anymore.”

A dream of rising above his station—one he had never thought possible—was now within his grasp.

“Leave it to me. I’ll put my life on the line.”

Travis entered through the hospital’s main entrance without hesitation.

“Alright. Let’s do this.”

Once inside, none of the guards stopped him.

‘This is where the nobles get treated, huh?’

He recalled the time his son had broken a bone while playing. He had run through the city, carrying his crying child on his back, searching for a hospital.

‘Room 607.’

Even though this was the VIP section, there were no guards in sight.

“Heh.”

Marvelling at Myron’s influence, Travis opened the door.

“Wh-Who are you?”

Inside the spacious hospital room were three beds, a single patient, Michael, and his wife.

“You’re Michael?”

His wife pressed the emergency button, but the alarm didn’t ring—likely because the wires had been cut.

Maykel stepped forward to block Travis.

“I asked who you are! What the hell do you think you’re doing here?!”

“You don’t need to know that. You have an article you’re planning to publish today, right? Hand it over, and I’ll leave quietly.”

As Travis moved toward the typewriter, Michael threw a punch.

“You damned thief!”

Travis’ head snapped to the side, but the pain only fueled his rage.

“Get the hell out! Do you have any idea who I am?!”

Before Michael could finish speaking, Travis drove a fist deep into his stomach.

“Ugh!”

As Michael collapsed to the floor, his wife rushed to his side.

“Honey!”

“No, don’t come near!”

Holding his wife back, Michael pleaded,

“Why are you doing this? You’re making a mistake! If you go through with this, you’ll— Gah!”

Travis silenced him with a brutal kick to the face, then glanced at the child sleeping in the hospital bed.

‘So that’s what this is.’

They were making him do the dirty work.

“Listen, Mr. Journalist. I’ve got nothing to lose. You don’t want to get involved with someone like me.”

Lying on the floor, Michael thought, ‘Why isn’t anyone coming?

Could Raymond have been compromised?

No… that can’t be.’

The Raymond he knew was a true politician, meticulous about his security.

“So that’s how it is, huh?” Travis sneered. “You’re really gonna make me go through with this?”

Michael turned onto his stomach.

“Don’t hurt my son. He has a weak heart. He’s spent his whole life in hospitals.”

Travis clenched his jaw.

‘Goddamn it.’

He had a son too.

“So just don’t write the article. Then your son lives, and you walk away unharmed. Simple, right?”

“Listen carefully. This isn’t just about me. This is about the future of the kingdom.”

The future of the kingdom?

“Is that so?”

Travis didn’t know what that meant.

If he did, he wouldn’t be living like this.

“This is really pissing me off.”

Does that so-called future include thugs like me?

“You think I’m an idiot?”

Travis knocked over all the medical equipment in the room and grabbed the child by the collar.

The boy was much lighter than expected.

His heart twisted in his chest.

Michael’s wife screamed and rushed forward.

“No! Please don’t!”

“Stay back!”

As Travis shoved her aside, the child slipped from his grip and tumbled to the floor.

Chaos erupted.

His mind raced wildly.

“Listen to me! Just listen to me!”

His hands trembled.

Every time he stomped on the unconscious child, a sickening shudder ran through his body.

“Hrrghh!”

Just do it.

‘I can endure it. Just ten minutes… no, five minutes. If I just shut my eyes and get through this, my family will be safe.’

Feeling his resolve waver, he let go of the last thread of his conscience.

“Die! Just die already!”

Money.

“You think I’ll back down? I’m a thug! A piece of human trash!”

That goddamn money.

‘Give it to me!’

His face distorted beyond recognition, he let out an inhuman scream and kicked the child in the stomach.

“Give me the moneyyyy!”

Michael’s wife screamed in agony as Michael crawled forward, clutching Travis’ leg.

“I’ll do it! I’ll destroy everything! Just please, stop! I’ll do anything, just stop!”

“Haa…! Haa…!”

As Travis stepped back, Michael threw the documents from the typewriter into the trash and set them on fire.

“There! It’s done! Just leave! Please, call a doctor! Please!”

Travis couldn’t bring himself to speak.

“I… If you ever try this again…”

His voice trembled too much. He knew he shouldn’t say any more.

Spewing curses, he stormed out of the hospital, eyes bloodshot.

His entire body convulsed.

‘It’ll be fine. I didn’t kill the kid. I was told not to let anyone die.’

But in truth—he was terrified.

“Hrrk!”

As he entered the city, all the emotions he had suppressed erupted at once.

“Hrrrghhh! Hrrrghhh!”

He didn’t even try to hide his tears.

‘What the hell is this?! What the hell am I doing?!

Don’t I get to live too?!

Is this even a life?!’

People stared at him, but then—

BOOM!

An explosion rocked the block ahead.

Screams filled the air as smoke rose into the sky.

Travis froze.

“Overthrow Jive!”

A gang of thugs in chicken-head masks emerged from the smoke, attacking citizens at random.

“Huh? Huh?”

Maybe this had nothing to do with him.

But an eerie sense of dread gripped his heart.

As he turned to flee—

“Gah—!”

A longsword pierced his back, running straight through his heart.

“Got him! We got him!”

Travis coughed up blood and collapsed.

The masked attackers surrounded him.

“N… No…”

He stretched his hand toward home, but countless blades ran him through.

“Kahahaha! Die! Die!”

As the faceless terrorists lynched him, the last emotion Travis felt was—

‘I'm sorry, honey… Our son…’

An overwhelming sense of helplessness toward the world.

The bombing incident in the capital was quickly relayed to Delta Headquarters via breaking news reports.

Fermi walked down the corridor, reading a document.

Investigation into the route of the smuggled explosives is ongoing. Based on their description, the suspects are presumed to be affiliated with the terrorists at Delta Headquarters. They appear to be heavily drugged. Identification is impossible.

‘Drug soldiers. If their brain memory units have been damaged…’

One kingdom came to mind.

“Iron.”

The only nation in the southern continent that Fermi, despite his control over the drug trade, had never been able to infiltrate.

‘They manufacture drugs under direct orders from the royal family and use them to develop soldiers. Indiscriminate terrorism is just another tool for them to consolidate their monarchy…’

“Fermi.”

Hearing a familiar voice, he looked up. Seriel was standing there.

Meanwhile, Gith’s advisor, Myron, discreetly entered the Iron Sector.

‘Made it past the worst of it.’

Inside a secret chamber, the King of Iron, Basak, and the Prime Minister of Arachne, Phedra, were waiting.

“Ah, Myron. Congratulations. That was clean, wasn’t it?”

“It’s all thanks to Your Majesty.”

Using Iron’s terrorists to eliminate Travis had been the most efficient method.

Phedra chuckled.

“A violent radical opposing the democratic monarchy gets blown up in a terrorist attack, and some thug dies along the way? No one cares. If anything, the public loves it—poetic justice, right?”

But does that mean evil was defeated?

“By tomorrow, the Jive scandal will be buried. Hey, Myron. What are you standing around for? Sit down.”

“Ah, yes.”

Phedra smirked.

“What, still holding a grudge? Just because I squeezed a little out of Jive? You feeling betrayed?”

“Of course not.”

Myron’s gaze sharpened.

‘Betrayal? What a joke.’

He had never trusted humans in the first place—how could he feel betrayed by them?

Basak asked,

“Where’s Gith? I heard his daughter died too. I was going to treat him to a drink, y’know, as a condolence.”

“Oh, he had a lunch appointment.”

“Kekeke, still chasing women even after all this? What an animal. That’s what I love about Gith—he’s got guts.”

Myron said,

“What’s more important is the press conference later. For Jive, this isn’t over yet.”

Phedra chuckled.

“You’re a loyal one, aren’t you? What’s there to worry about? Gith will handle it just fine.”

“But…”

“It’s fine. The people hate thugs, murderers, and rapists, but they don’t hate us.

In fact, they love us.

Do you know why?”

Myron shook his head.

“No matter how much they deny it, deep inside, they all want to be like us.”

“Humans can never completely condemn someone they secretly aspire to be.

Their subconscious won’t allow it.

Because maybe—just maybe—one day, they could be in our position.

Even if they never get there, they still imagine it.”

Phedra spread his arms wide.

“In the end, we are the physical embodiment of human desire. We exist inside everyone.”

“You mean to say we’re all the same?”

“No.”

Phedra grinned.

“I mean, we are their justice.”

Myron fell silent.

“Gith will be fine. He might be a little annoyed, but nothing will happen to him.”

‘Does he really believe that?’

Phedra winked and pointed at Myron.

“Trust me. There is no scenario where you lose your power.”

At that moment, Myron felt a sense of relief and realized something.

‘I am human too.’

A monster of self-justification.

Gith and Plu met for lunch.

Since Gith had only recently learned of his daughter’s death, Plu carefully chose her words and demeanor.

“First of all… I’m truly sorry for your loss.”

Gith looked up in slight confusion before realization dawned on him.

“Oh, that.”

Tears rolled down his face.

The grief of losing a child was undoubtedly the greatest pain, and for once, Plu’s heart softened.

“Stay strong. Maybe you should take some time off—”

“I’m fine.”

His voice was calm, but something about his demeanor sent a chill down Plu’s spine.

“Heh, why? Does it seem strange?”

“Ah, no, it’s just…”

“Of course, losing my daughter is painful. Even now, it feels like my heart is being ripped apart. But what’s the point of acting on emotion? With a weak mindset like that, I never would’ve made it this far.”

He cut into his steak and took a bite.

“Let’s be honest. You want something from me, don’t you? I can give it to you. Ah, I know. How about carrying my child? I just lost my daughter, after all.”

‘A monster.’

Plu finally understood why Gaold, the former head of the Magic Association, refused to associate with politicians.

“Then… how about the position of Jive’s Magic Association Head?”

“The Magic Association Head, huh? Sounds good. I like you, Plu. You’re rational and clear-headed. How about tonight? Are you free?”

“Of course.”

Plu smiled.

‘Fine, Gith. You can have all of me—body and mind. But this time, you’d better be prepared.’

Since the assassination game, Thormia’s strategy had shifted.

‘Tonight, you die in my arms.’
