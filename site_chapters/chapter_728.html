Chapter 728:

Upon reaching the summit of WhiteBlack's moon, Shirone emphasized to the man who had hidden Kai's body,

"If you try anything foolish, you won't get another chance."

"I know. I don't want to die either."

The man, named Jurak, was a skilled operator from the Black zone who had voluntarily participated in the survival test.

"I've had my fun. Now I just want to end this."

His words, reflecting the philosophy of the Black zone, piqued Shirone's curiosity.

"You could fight as much as you want over there. Why did you join the survival test?"

"I was at the bottom when I was born. Let's see… I was about eight when I killed someone for the first time. I didn't feel any guilt. They tried to kill me first. Anyway, as time passed, I got stronger…"

Jurak smiled broadly.

"I wanted to see how strong I had become. Isn't that true for everyone? No one just looks up. They run like mad because something is pushing them from below."

"So… you joined just for that reason? To see how strong you are?"

"Fighting is everyday life in Black. It's so common that if you're hungry, you just break into any house and kill. Then you eat their food. It's not fun, it's just life. It's not a dangerous game. Think about it. Could White handle such a demon if they entered?"

Jurak waved his hand dismissively.

"They could never win. We are gamblers who bet our lives to take others' lives. Whether a gambler enters or not determines the outcome of the law's fight."

"So the similar win rates…"

"Haha! Yes. White needs hope to fight their best, right? We send weak ones on purpose, lose a few rounds, and then take it all at once when they're heated up. This time, it was my turn to enjoy… but…"

A shadow fell over Jurak's face.

"I got hit from behind. Because of you. How could they send a magician? There aren't many even in Black."

Shirone didn't want to hear more.

"Take me to Kai."

"Don't worry. I brought a weak one to raise the difficulty. They should be guarding him like a lifeline."

Jurak led the way east from the summit of Anarchy Mountain, pushing through thick willow branches.

"Here we are."

The scene he proudly introduced had neither Kai's body nor any living person.

"What… what is this?"

Jurak hurriedly turned to Shirone, waving his hands.

"No, no! I told Russell to guard him! This wasn't my intention…"

"Activate. Suicide Squad."

A card flashed from where the voice came.

Suicide Squad (Summon).

Description: The ancient Gillitians chose mutual destruction at the end of their lives.

Effect: Summons the soul of an ancient Gillitian by destroying an S-rank card.

Special Note: Activates a critical strike.

"For Gillitian!"

Unlike typical spirits, the Gillitian, covered in tattoos, appeared vividly real.

"Ugh!"

A blade pierced Jurak's back, and soon his clothes were soaked with blood.

"How… how did you get the Suicide Squad…?"

It was Russell's only card, but it required an S-rank card to activate.

"You bastard! Because of you…"

Russell, pale as if sick, limped into view, with a familiar woman beside him.

"Breeze?"

The one who activated the Suicide Squad was Breeze from the White zone.

'How did she know to come here?'

Breeze looked at Shirone with resigned eyes and said,

"Sorry. I had no choice."

"Breeze! You traitor!"

Three participants from the White zone, bleeding, pushed through the bushes after her.

Atria gritted her teeth and shouted,

"Shirone! Be careful of that woman! She stole all our cards and ran away! Black… huh?"

Atria stopped mid-sentence upon recognizing the man next to Breeze, and Ballad looked dumbfounded.

"Russell? You participated too?"

Russell had promised a future with Breeze.

But Breeze's parents couldn't accept him due to his weak heart, and their marriage was annulled by a vote.

Unable to control his anger, Russell resorted to violence and was eventually exiled to Black. That was three years ago.

"So you were meeting secretly? Without us knowing?"

Even Ballad, who was in charge of security in the White zone, didn't know they were still seeing each other.

'Come to think of it…'

Breeze had detailed knowledge of weapon cards.

"Russell! How dare you deceive me!"

As Ballad charged with his sword raised, Breeze summoned the Magic Return Soldiers Shirone had given her.

"Kyaaah!"

Ten skeletons in old armor rose from the ground and staggered towards Ballad.

"This… this is…"

Having handed over all his weapon cards, Ballad had to fight with pure physical strength.

"Breeze! I'll kill you!"

Ignoring the curses as he smashed the skeletons' heads, Breeze grabbed Russell's hand and turned.

"Hurry! This way!"

As the sound of flesh tearing echoed, Breeze's shoulders shook, and her eyes widened.

"Damn woman."

Jurak, already soaked in blood, stood with a dagger precisely embedded in Breeze's heart.

"Detective."

By the time Russell realized the presence of the stealth ability, Breeze's heart had already stopped beating.

"Breeeeze!"

Shirone checked the state of Jurak and Breeze, but they had already turned into cold corpses.

"Serves you right, traitor!"

As Ballad, who had wiped out the Magic Return Soldiers, approached, Russell collected Breeze's cards.

"Don't try anything. Even with weapon cards, I can kill a weakling like you in an instant."

Russell, too, had lost his reason in the face of his lover's death.

"Shut up! You're no different from those who exiled me! What did Breeze do wrong to die in a place like this?"

"It was decided by vote! We expelled violence to maintain peace in White! And Breeze betrayed us! She conspired with you!"

"Don't talk nonsense."

Russell pointed at Shirone.

"Breeze participated in the survival test to save me. To get the CPR card!"

Marco shouted,

"Do you think that makes sense? CPR doesn't cure heart disease."

"Of course not. But if you revive someone after they die, their body becomes as good as new. Everyone in Black knows that."

Atria frowned.

"So she tried to save you? But you were exiled to Black. One of you had to die."

"In Black, I'm worse than trash. It's not a place for a sick person to live."

Russell was dying.

"The village chief told me to participate in this survival test and end my life grandly. I had already given up on life. But Breeze…"

Russell's gaze turned to the dead Breeze.

"Breeze never gave up. She said if I got better, we could run away together. If only this damn heart would beat properly! I was confident I could make her happy for life!"

Regardless of the outcome between White and Black, they planned to cure the disease and cross Anarchy Mountain.

To a world that wasn't divided into black and white, a place that was entirely gray.

"But now it's all over."

Russell looked at Shirone with desperate eyes.

"Kai is already dead. Please use the CPR on Breeze. I'll die instead! Just save her!"

Shirone looked down at the card with a bitter expression.

'Whose card is this?'

At that moment, Marco tackled Russell from the side.

"Give me my card! Hurry!"

With two Black participants still remaining, weapon cards were essential.

Ballad and Atria, who had rushed over, also pinned Russell down, fighting over the card.

"That's my card! Give it to me!"

"Shut up! I have a family! I'll keep it, and you protect me!"

"I'm the one who fought the hardest here! It's all mine!"

Pinned down by the White participants, Russell closed his eyes tightly and shouted,

"Breeeeze!"

As his voice of despair filled the night sky, Shirone threw the CPR card into the wind.

'This is human life.'

Even if White and Black achieved harmony, the fierce nature of living beings could never be beautiful.

'So, law, do not mock us.'

Isn't that the only way to live?

"Mom! I'm coming back! I… I!"

"Breeeeze! Breeeeze!"

Looking up at WhiteBlack's moon, Shirone slowly spread his arms and said,

"Do not mock human life."

His body, having unlocked the Immortal Function, radiated light.

Jin Sung Eum spoke.

"It's different from what I heard in the rumors."

Indeed, it was.

'I thought it would feel a bit warmer.'

The boy's cold eyes were comparable to her own.

'More than anything…'

Watching him made Sung Eum uneasy.

Like a condensed flash about to explode, he seemed ready to release something at any moment.

'He has almost reached something.'

If touched, he would explode, but even if left alone, the chances were high.

"Shirone, candidate for the Ivory Tower, I am Jin Sung Eum, princess of the Jincheon Empire and sorceress."

"I know. I've heard about you."

As Sung Eum moved, Mun Kyung's eyebrows twitched, and he measured the distance with a serious expression.

'Twenty steps.'

Sung Eum measured the distance to everyone.

"I've heard about you too. I'm jealous, annoyed, and maybe a little expectant…"

'Fifteen steps.'

The pace at which she approached Shirone was still steady.

"You seem very precarious, but there must be a reason. I'm not disappointed by that. Rather, it makes me realize anew that you are my competitor."

'Ten steps.'

From this point on, it was the realm of the superhuman.

"But I didn't get here through anyone's understanding. So, when I look at you now…"

'Five steps.'

Here, Sung Eum's steps halted.

"Honestly, it makes me angry."

"…"

Frowning, Sung Eum took another step towards Shirone.

'Four steps.'

"The Ivory Tower means nothing to an Ivory Tower candidate. What I truly hoped for was…"

'Three steps.'

Mun Kyung's eyes widened.

"For the first time in my life, I thought I might meet someone who could walk alongside me."

'Two steps!'

Mun Kyung was disheartened.

"Shirone, your card is certainly impressive. But unfortunately, it seems you can't walk alongside me."

As Sung Eum's steps finally stopped, the warriors of Sambo gaped in disbelief.

"I'll give you one step."

At a distance close enough to feel each other's breath, Sung Eum's finger pointed at Shirone's forehead.

"I am one step ahead of you."

In the silence, Mun Kyung shouted in his heart.

'Take pride! Worship Her Highness! Being granted one step is an incredible honor!'

Shirone could hardly imagine the view from right in front of Jin Sung Eum's face.

'So this is Jin Sung Eum.'

It was just as the Ivory Tower residents had said.

'What does it matter?'

Before coming to Andre, Shirone might have been provoked by Sung Eum's words, but now he felt nothing.

"Something about the card…"

"Hmm?"

As Sung Eum looked puzzled, Shirone smiled and stepped aside.

"If you insist on keeping that one step…"

Then, looking back at the path, he said,

"Please, go ahead."

"…"

Sung Eum's eyelids fluttered rapidly.
