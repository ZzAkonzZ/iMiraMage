Chapter 931: Even If It’s An Illusion (3)

The cave, activated with Hexa's powers to evade Anke Ra's pursuit, echoed with sobbing.

"Sniff, sniff."

It was Goffin mimicking Ikael.

"Sob, sob."

Ikael, blushing, turned away.

"Stop it! How long are you going to tease me?"

Goffin leaned against the cave wall, laughing.

"It's fascinating. Archangel Ikael, crying like a little kid. It’s really too precious to see alone."

"Who said I was crying…"

Recalling the recent events, Ikael stopped mid-sentence and looked away, overwhelmed by emotion.

"Hmph."

She turned around sulkily, then after a moment of thought, approached Goffin with a serious expression.

"Goffin, I've made up my mind."

"About what?"

Kneeling in front of Goffin, she slightly raised the corners of her mouth and said,

"I want to share love with you."

Goffin, tense from her serious tone, burst out laughing and leaned in.

"Yeah."

"No, not just a kiss."

As Ikael gently pushed him away with her hand, Goffin too leaned back against the wall, his expression becoming serious.

"So…"

"Yes, love. The kind of love you humans share. Now that our hearts are connected, I’m not afraid anymore."

Goffin's mouth hung open, and Ikael quickly asked with an anxious expression.

"What’s wrong? Don’t you want to share love with me?"

"No, no. Not at all."

Goffin rapidly shook his head, a serious tension finally setting on his face.

Perhaps a solemnity.

"I was just surprised by the unexpected question. But… are you really okay with it?"

Ikael spoke with determined eyes.

"Don’t worry. I'm the leader of the angels, who have never faced the impossible. I’ll manage this time too."

"No, it’s not that grand. The problem is…"

"You said it’s okay. Let’s clean up and get started right away. I'll show you that I can do it perfectly."

"Right away?"

After a moment of thought, Goffin nodded.

"Yeah, that’s also fine. A good idea."

Watching Goffin adjust his position, Ikael wore a confident smile.

Time passed.

"Are you going to keep doing that?"

"Huh?"

As Ikael raised her eyebrows and asked back with an innocent look, Goffin flailed his hands.

"No, I mean, shouldn’t we take some kind of preparatory stance if we’re going to share love?"

Realizing what he meant, Ikael slowly rose from her seat.

"Oh, right. Sorry."

She then looked around and sat down on a flat rock on the floor, crossing her legs.

Then she neatly placed her hands on her knees and gently turned her head, revealing her profile.

"Does this work?" It was truly a heavenly figure, but Goffin watching her was simply bewildered.

"...What are you doing now?"

"Huh? Why? Don’t I look beautiful? Should I do this instead?"

Watching Ikael take another elegant pose, he finally realized.

"Be honest. You don’t know, do you?"

"Yeah, I don’t know! How should I know? You think the archangel would have spied on humans making love?"

Goffin sighed and said,

"That’s not it. Come here. I’ll explain."

As Ikael moved closer and leaned in, Goffin covered his mouth and whispered in a small voice.

"The position is like..."

Hearing Goffin's words, Ikael's eyes widened and her expression turned to disgust.

"How, how can I do such a thing!"

"Or like this."

Goffin leaned in and whispered again.

Softly.

Ikael, who hesitated but listened, paled, and her body trembled.

"Cancel. I need to rethink this."

As Ikael turned away swiftly, Goffin grabbed her wrist.

"Why? What’s wrong?"

Shaking off Goffin's wrist, Ikael burst out in shock.

"I am a noble archangel! That’s not human love! It's beastly! Animalistic!"

"Humans are animals too."

Ikael's mouth snapped shut.

"Of course, we are rational animals. But when it comes to sharing love, that doesn’t matter."

"Because our hearts are connected." Goffin smiled contentedly.

"Yes. When sharing love, humans become animals. But I won’t force you. If you don’t want to..."

"No. I’ll do it."

Ikael finally understood.

"It’s about becoming one with you."

A different, fervent energy filled both of their eyes, and Ikael sat down gently beside Goffin.

Their lips softly met, and Goffin embraced Ikael and laid her down on the floor.

"Ah..."

A breath that coexisted fear and excitement leaked from Ikael's trembling lips.

"To become animals."

The feeling of plunging to the origins of life was embarrassingly indescribable...

"Now what becomes of me?" Part of her wanted to see how far this abject emotion could amplify.

"Ikael."

Goffin said,

"I love you."

As Ikael felt electricity coursing through her body, her celestial body soared.

"Ah..."

That hearts are connected.

For the first time, Ikael learned that the mere body heat of a human could feel so intensely hot.

"So that's it."

In the cold world.

When she realized how far removed this world was from what is called a heart.

"So that was it, Goffin."

She felt a chilling shiver.

"20 minutes until enemy entry!" The messenger's report sent a wave of tension through the Tormia Allied Forces as they held their positions.

Two kilometers away, in the "Flower Field," the Hua and Elf tribes awaited instructions.

The sight of 300 Hua and 600 Elves together looked like a natural landscape.

Rian and Tess, having arrived at the Flower Field, moved to find Enox, the commander of the defense forces.

Tess grumbled, "Why are we in the rear? I can fight well too."

"It's a strategic decision. Winning isn't the only thing that matters. To lure the demon army, we must hold the Flower Field."

"Who doesn't know that? But why us? The battle will be fought by the Biochemists anyway!"

It was frustrating to have to watch their comrades die from just 2 kilometers away.

"Do you really think that?"

Tess turned to Rian's pointed question.

"Natasha?"

Gustav's 4 Generals, the combat doll Natasha.

She had previously fought Rian to a draw in a duel.

'Honestly, Rian was slightly at a disadvantage.'

"I won't be caught off guard like last time, but she's still a tough opponent. If we can't stop Natasha, this place could become hell."

"Are you saying Natasha's combat power surpasses the Flower Field?"

This time, Rian chose his words carefully.

"It's just about compatibility. The Flower Field is firepower, while Natasha is speed."

There was no need to incite fear before the battle, and their conversation ended as they reached their destination.

"Hello."

As Rian greeted and approached, Protea and Enox, who were standing at the front, turned simultaneously.

"Oh? A famous person has arrived."

While Enox's eyes were filled with welcome, Protea shrank back.

'Who is this human?'

The Lotus lineage of the Hua tribe, except for the carnivorous Armand, was known for its weak passivity.

But the moment she met Rian's gaze, that slight passivity seemed to magnify to its extreme.

"Ozent Rian."

Enox poked Rian's chest with her finger.

"The strongest human, the world's greatest swordsman, the Knight of Maha. Right?"

Rian briefly wondered how Kuan would react if he heard this, but then dismissed the thought.

"Thank you for recognizing me. Of course, I respect you as the commander of the defense forces, Enox. However, I..."

"I know. Special mission. There's a very strong human on the other side too, right?"

Enox glanced forward briefly before turning back and shrugging.

"What's the big deal? They're just humans. They can't match Rian, who possesses the Idea. Plus, you have me, the best swordsman of the Elf tribe."

Enox's waist flicked, and the sword at her side made a clear sound.

Though similar to Tess, his level of power was enough to hold a position among the two kings.

'She's certainly strong. But I haven't heard much about the Elf tribe's combat.'

In any case, they would find out soon enough, so Rian nodded and turned back.

"Well then. Good luck."

"Yes, you too, Rian."

As the two returned to their positions, Protea, who had been holding her breath, finally spoke.

"Enox, Fairy of the Wind."

Like the fairies of Heaven, Elves are born with inherent natural attributes.

However, these are not products of nature but genes passed down from the ancient Norin.

"Why are you kind to humans? They trample flowers and destroy forests."

While the Hua tribe couldn't surpass humans due to their passive desires, Elves were different.

Their bodies were stronger than humans, and their pure, clear minds produced unparalleled concentration.

"Hehe, I like humans. And the human just now, strictly speaking, isn't human."

"What?"

"One who possesses the perspective of a god."

Seeing Protea's confused expression, Enox began recounting the Elves' lore.

"Long ago, in an era when gods and humans coexisted, there were the Fairy and Norin races. They seemed to be enemies, but not always. The Norin developed a spiritual civilization like the Fairies. They also knew many things that fascinated the Elves. But the most important thing is that they were of divine lineage."

"Divine lineage? So their ancestors created nature? As far as I know, Anke Ra..."

"Nature doesn't exist."

Protea fell silent.

"There are no forests, seas, or rivers. This world is made up solely of the perspective of gods. That's called the Idea."

The Idea also appears in the Hua tribe's lore.

"Rian possesses the Idea. He is a human who twists the world's laws with a god's perspective. Isn't that amazing?"

Enox's eyes gleamed with excitement.

"Perhaps my current feelings are like those of our ancestors' fairies. They sought the Idea from the Norin. The Norin, in turn, wanted to become stronger beings through the fairies. According to lore, it was a mutual exchange. That's how we, the Elves, were created."

"Then why didn't they obtain the Idea?"

"There was a serious obstacle. The fairies were too small."

As Protea tilted her head, Enox smiled brightly.

"If a fairy gave birth to a Norin's child, her belly would burst."

"You know, as a creature of nature, interspecies unions always cause harm. One side must sacrifice for it to succeed. Even if it does succeed... only pain remains."

Shuddering at the horrific thought, Protea suddenly realized something.

"Then how were Elves born?"

"One day, someone with the perspective of a god appeared and solved everything. The records don't remain, but given the possession of the Idea, it was undoubtedly a human. Thus, the fairies split into those who served Anke Ra and those who followed humans, becoming Elves."

The Elves' lore was tumultuous compared to the Hua tribe's, but it seemed they might share the same roots.

Protea's voice trembled.

"What did they do to create life between completely different races?"

"Heart."

Enox placed her slender white hands over her heart.

"Conveying light with all one's heart. A signal of life that no law can invade. According to lore, the one who created us called that light..."

Enox's eyes sparkled like stars.

"Agape."
