Chapter 523:

Kashan.

A powerful empire that occupies one-third of the human world’s territory.

The grand palace of Aganos, home to Kashan’s ruler, Empress Teraje, was a military and cultural fortress, fully equipped with defenses against magic, law-based attacks, and physical force—an autonomous sanctuary all on its own.

“Wow, it’s been a while.”

Having arrived in the capital of Kashan, Miro’s group stood by the city square fountain, looking up at the towering spires of Aganos.

Hundreds of towers jutted up into the sky as if they were trying to pierce and kill it—a truly majestic sight.

Teraje…

In the year since being named the Shield of Humanity, Miro had traveled the world, meeting with all kinds of influential figures.

Among them, the one who left the strongest impression was none other than Empress Teraje Mistra of Kashan.

It was the first time in her life she had ever looked at someone and thought, this person is strange.

Well, officially, anyway. Unofficially, there was Goffin—but he had already been erased from her mind.

“Auntie, how much is this?”

Bored while waiting for their scheduled meeting time, Miro wandered the shops around the plaza, doing a bit of shopping.

Armin and Kuan remained by the fountain, while Arius trailed behind Miro, carrying Shirone—hidden beneath a black cloth—on his back.

“That? Two gold.”

“What? Two gold for this?”

What Miro had picked out was a braided bracelet made of three different leathers in three colors.

“Cowhide, horsehide, camelhide. At that price, you're getting a steal.”

“Ah… cow, horse, camel.”

Miro set a single gold coin down on the vendor’s stall.

“Let me have it for one gold. It’s barely worth anything, really.”

The shopkeeper stared at her, dumbfounded.

“What? Do you know how rare leather is in Kashan? You want it for half-price? That’s daylight robbery!”

Miro forced a charming smile.

“Auntie, do you know who I am?”

“Who are you supposed to be?”

“I’m someone here to meet Teraje. You know—the Empress. I’m an official guest of the Empire. Can’t you give me just this one little discount?”

The shopkeeper’s eyes widened in anger.

“You cheeky brat! How dare you toss the Empress’s name around like that! Outsider or not, this is the capital of Kashan! Get lost before I call the patrol!”

With Teraje’s name failing to help, Miro resorted to her last card.

“Listen, lady. Empress or not, I’m the one who’s been protecting this world for twenty years! If it weren’t for me, you wouldn’t even be here selling this crap!”

The shopkeeper’s face turned pale with shock and horror.

“This one’s nuts! Someone, call the patrol! A crazy woman’s loose!”

“Crazy woman? Why, you—!”

The commotion quickly drew the attention of everyone in the plaza.

Meanwhile, Kuan and Armin by the fountain could only wish they didn’t know her.

“She’s completely shameless,” Kuan muttered.

Armin gave a weary smile. “I heard she was famous for being eccentric back in school. Personality doesn’t change that easily.”

“…But is this okay? I thought anything involving Miro was classified on a global scale.”

“Well… if it means getting local goods for half price.”

When Kuan glanced at him over the terrible joke, Armin just shrugged sheepishly.

“She’s the one saying it. What can we do?”

Kuan sighed and turned his head toward Miro—just as the sound of clanking armor came from the entrance to the plaza.

A unit of soldiers marched in, fully clad in heavy armor. Embedded in their waists were amber gemstones that gleamed with golden light.

They were the Outer Guard of Aganos—the "Far Guard."

The Empress’s royal guards were divided into three units—Close, Middle, and Far—based on the proximity of their protection.

Though the Far Guard protected Teraje from a distance, even the sight of royal guards within the city was enough to make the locals tense up.

“Hey, lady! If you’re drunk in broad daylight, go sleep it off! What’re you doing harassing small business owners?”

“Why won’t you believe me? I am Adrias Miro! I told you, the Empress summoned me!”

The captain of the guard spotted the woman arguing with the merchant and sighed.

“So we didn’t even need to look…”

Teraje’s instructions, passed down through command channels, were simple: “Bring me the loudest woman in the marketplace.”

As the guards approached the stall, the merchant, finally realizing the situation, froze completely, color draining from her face.

“Th-the royal guard…”

Not just the city patrol—but the Empress’s own royal guard.

Even more shocking was that the guard captain stepped forward and addressed Miro with utmost courtesy.

“We’ve been expecting you. Her Majesty instructed us to escort you with the highest respect. Please, follow me.”

“Hmph.”

Miro, who had been lazily eyeing the captain, suddenly grinned and turned toward the shopkeeper.

The terrified woman’s lips trembled as she watched Miro’s slender wrist, now adorned with the leather bracelet braided in three colors.

Following the captain, Miro and her group entered the inner sanctum of Aganos without facing a single obstruction.

Servants and attendants scattered to make way, and the great gates opened on their own, no knocks required.

The sight of impregnable fortress doors swinging wide open like madness itself left a deep impression on those who had spent their lives inside the royal palace.

The guards bearing the amber emblem were not permitted beyond the twentieth floor, so the captain stepped back at that point.

Ascending the narrow stairs to the twentieth floor, they were met by the Middle Guard of Aganos, marked by the diamond emblem.

Clad in white plate armor, the Middle Guard captain approached Kuan and held out both hands respectfully.

“No weapons are permitted beyond this point. Your sword, please…”

Kuan didn’t even pretend to listen.

As Miro’s group continued down the corridor, the captain made no move to stop them and simply followed behind.

At the twenty-third tower floor, ten members of the Wind Guards stood at the end of the hall, all wearing owl masks and guarding the door.

The remaining ninety had already been dispatched around the world in search of Anke Ra’s incarnations.

But the ten who remained were the Wind Guard’s top officers, and the suffocating killing intent that radiated from them was enough to overwhelm even seasoned warriors.

“Damn, this is intense. Did they release poison gas or something?”

Joking as she walked up, Miro addressed the one at the front of the group.

“Would you mind stepping aside? What’s the point of calling me here if you’re just going to block the door?”

“…No one but you is allowed to enter.”

Hearing a voice he could never forget even after ten years, Kuan furrowed his brow.

And for once, Miro didn’t respond with a quip.

Even within a fortress like Aganos, this room was special—and she had a reason to enter it alone.

“Wait here for a bit. I’ll go in first.”

As the door opened and the space within expanded, Miro stepped forward, her eyes gradually turning cold.

The click of the door closing echoed behind her.

Sitting in a chair with her legs crossed was Uorin.

“It’s been a while, Miro. You haven’t changed a bit.”

“I’ve never met you before.”

Miro already knew that Teraje was Mitochondrial Eve.

But the woman before her wasn’t wearing Mistra’s face—and that alone was a disappointment.

“Sit down. Tea? Or maybe wine?”

“Before that, I want to speak in private. That’s why you called me here, isn’t it?”

Though no one else was visible, Miro knew someone was watching.

“Crouch, wait outside.”

A brief silence followed.

“It’s fine. The fact you were brought here at all means this is beyond anything you could handle alone.”

As soon as those words were spoken, the window to Miro’s right opened and slammed shut in a flash.

“Happy now?”

When Miro slowly took her seat, Uorin, watching her face closely, chuckled.

“You’ve got that look like you want to slap me.”

“From the very beginning… everything was your plan, wasn’t it?”

Miro spoke not to Uorin, but to Teraje Mistra, still alive in Uorin’s memory.

“Of course. Thanks to me, you finally escaped that wretched dimensional wall too, didn’t you? What’s it been—twenty years? How does it feel to be back in the world? Oh, that bracelet’s cute.”

“It’s not so bad. The world’s more interesting than I remembered. But…”

Fwoosh!

The Avatar of Avalokiteśvara erupted from Miro’s body like a blaze, lashing out toward Uorin.

Though the face of Avalokiteśvara was serene, the dozens of arms forming different mudras surrounded Uorin with crushing force.

“You shouldn’t have touched my friends.”

Miro had tried to carry the sins of humanity alone, but in the end, she’d dragged Sein and Gaold into it too.

Especially Gaold—a devout believer—had become a demon and was now wandering through Hell.

No one could remain unafraid in the face of the killing intent radiating from humanity’s most powerful prajñā—and yet, Uorin knew Miro far too well.

“I get what you’re saying. So there’s no need to pretend to be angry. I understand how you feel, but…”

To a prajñā of detached mind, even anger was merely a worldly emotion.

As the manifestation of Avalokiteśvara vanished in an instant, Uorin examined the cubic crystal she’d stuck to her nail that morning.

“I didn’t sacrifice Gaold. He was simply the only one who could do it. Just like you were the only one worthy of sacrificing yourself for humanity.”

Miro pursed her lips.

As expected, affectation meant nothing to Mitochondrial Eve.

After adjusting the cubic’s position, Uorin looked up.

“So, I assume you didn’t come all this way just to scold me. What’s the reason you asked to see me?”

“You already know.”

“Hmm?”

Uorin tilted her head like she truly didn’t.

‘She’s so infuriating.’

It was an unspoken demand: If you’re going to ask for help, at least act like it.

“I remember now,” said the woman standing at the front of the Wind Guard.

“That swordsman. It was him back then.”

She removed her owl mask, revealing the face of the woman who had appeared in Kuan’s nightmares.

Her face was pale as snow, her eyebrows faint, and the sharp glint in her eyes—hidden beneath heavy lids—was exactly as he remembered from ten years ago.

The price of refusing her offer had been the severing of his Achilles tendon. His life had changed entirely after that.

Now, seeing that very woman just a few steps away, it was no wonder the desire to kill boiled up inside him.

“You’re still holding a sword, I see. I’ll give you credit for that mutt-like tenacity.”

As the woman mimicked the slicing sound from the day she had cut through his tendon, Kuan’s eyes flared.

At the same time, her gaze sharpened with intrigue.

‘What… is this?’

The world was tilting slightly to the right.

Ten degrees, twenty, thirty—until it felt as though the floor were sliding out from under him. Instinctively, he shifted weight to his right foot.

‘I feel like I’m about to fall.’

Kuan spoke quietly.

“Don’t provoke me. I’m holding back with everything I’ve got.”

He had clearly reached some new realization.

But what kind of realization could twist a person’s very nature into something so bizarre?

‘Even back then, I acknowledged his talent…’

But this was on a different level entirely.

The Kuan whose Achilles tendon had been severed ten years ago no longer existed in this world.

‘I’ll need to restrain him for now.’

The woman slowly shifted her center of gravity to the left.

And as she did, the tilted world began to return to balance—until everything was once again perfectly level.

‘Definitely not an easy opponent.’

The reason Kuan hadn’t immediately drawn his sword, even with his life-ruining enemy right in front of him, wasn’t just because of his sense of duty—it was because she was part of the Wind Guard.

‘Wait. Someday, I’ll cut off all your legs.’

While Kuan bided his time with that thought, the woman was still consumed by the strange sensation she had felt from him just moments ago.

‘What the hell was that?’

Unable to suppress her curiosity, she opened her mouth.

“You…”

But before she could finish, Miro’s voice cut in.

“Arius. Come in.”

The woman frowned, disappointed, and pulled her owl mask back on, stepping aside.

As Arius entered the room, Uorin raised a hand.

“It’s been a while, Arius.”

They had once crossed paths at the royal palace in Kazra, but their positions now were vastly different.

Uorin, once a princess, had become one of the Three Emperors, and the grave-robbing member of the Seven Magical Sins had become the strongest prajñā’s devoted servant.

“Lay him on the table.”

When Miro yanked back the edge of the cloth, Shirone was revealed, wearing Armand’s robe and seemingly fast asleep.

‘Shirone…’

For the briefest moment, Uorin’s eyes softened with unexpected warmth.
