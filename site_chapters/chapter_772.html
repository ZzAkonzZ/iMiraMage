Chapter 772:

While Shirone was speaking with the Great Star, Minerva registered the Lawslayer with the Ivory Tower.

Araka of the Internal Affairs Department handed her the documents.

"From now on, regardless of the outcome it causes, the Ivory Tower will acknowledge Lawslayer as 'righteous.'"

"Good work."

"I only hope it’s used for the right cause. Though, if it’s Saint Shirone wielding it, there’s no need to worry."

"Oh, so I’m the one you need to worry about, huh?"

Araka simply smiled with her eyes in lieu of an answer.

“You seem much lighter now.”

Before Shirone arrived, Minerva’s face, having returned from her mission, had been colder than the pile of corpses she’d left behind.

“Do I?”

Lawslayer cannot be destroyed.

Of course, unlike Rian’s weapon Idea, it doesn’t possess the durability of an absolute concept.

‘A dagger that anyone could shatter with a hammer.’

But in the end, Lawslayer would be reborn by the laws of this world and fall into someone else’s hands once more.

‘The crystallization of killing intent.’

An endless chain reaction of death.

“It’s only because it’s Shirone, isn’t it?”

Minerva turned away, shaking the folder in her hand as if she had read Araka’s thoughts.

“Thanks. I’ll pay you back later.”

Araka watched her walk away.

‘Yes… the world will never forgive you.’

For she was a grand witch who had stood at the pinnacle of wickedness.

‘So at the very least, may you forgive yourself. Otherwise, how could you bear it?’

No one in the world wished happiness for someone who had once driven humanity to the brink of destruction.

She only hoped it wouldn’t be too painful.

Araka thought to herself.

"Lawslayer is dealt with, and the ruling from Terraforce will take time…”

As Minerva organized her thoughts and walked down the hall, a brain of pale gray matter swam through the air and approached her.

“You’re still at the Ivory Tower, brain?”

A brain of unknown origin.

Even Minerva, leader of the Department of Human Safety, did not know whose brain it was.

She could only make a few guesses.

“What’s the matter? You were waiting for me?”

“…"

Sensing it sought somewhere private, Minerva led the brain into her room.

It floated over to a chair and cast illusion magic. Suddenly, a handsome man was seated, one leg crossed.

“What’s this, some kind of special service?”

“Not to your liking?”

His voice was smooth as honey, yet Minerva’s eyes narrowed even more.

‘Here comes the first assumption.’

Even if it could use magic, a brain is still just a brain.

‘It can’t hear or see.’

Then how does it respond to external stimuli with such precision?

‘Only one possibility.’

The brain was using the tenth sensory level—Formless. If that were true, then what it contained could also be inferred.

‘Akashic Records.’

In Minerva’s view, there were only two ways to withstand the resets that occur at the Formless level.

‘One is Hexa.’

A new signal from outside the system.

‘And the other…’

To copy the Akashic Records and store them in a new vessel.

‘It’s not Anke Ra.’

The brain’s Akashic Records likely contained primitive data distinct from the current version.

‘It’s the Age of Great Purification now.’

Three resets had occurred, wiping vast amounts of information, with no one able to recall them.

But someone must have felt the need to preserve the original data to resist the resets.

‘What were they trying to preserve?’

In reverse, what was the event most erased by those three resets?

‘The history of Heaven.’

The first humanity, Gaia, who opposed Anke Ra.

‘Which means the owner of this brain, with high probability…’

Was a Gaian.

Since Goffin’s final departure from the Photon System, Gaia no longer existed in the universe.

‘But their resistance against the whole remains…’

Through this formless brain.

“Not really…”

Minerva smiled faintly.

“Not my type. Do you even understand women?”

“Do I not?”

The brain rapidly rotated through illusionary images—men, women, young, old—like a roulette.

The one it finally stopped on was Shirone.

“You’ve gotten cheeky.”

The brain had entered her division for one reason: to enforce human safety.

“I heard you’ve decided to erase Nane.”

“Heh, do you hate him that much? Or is it Anke Ra you truly despise?”

But the brain didn’t take the bait.

“We don’t need to entrust everything to Hexa.”

Minerva couldn’t ignore those words—not when they came from what might be the only remaining backup of Goffin’s data.

“I know. I’ve got something like you in my own head, remember? This isn’t a simple problem.”

“I heard you met the ‘Card Game Players.’”

“I saw the possibility of ending the game. But betting on a gamble and changing reality are two different things. Whether it’s good, evil, emptiness, or love—only a massive shock can break the balance.”

“So, you’ll help Love and strike at Evil?”

“Should I help Emptiness and attack Good instead? Out of all possible choices for humanity, it’s the most rational.”

“Do you believe Lawslayer will be enough?”

Minerva fell silent.

“Evil can’t be overcome by rational judgment. Even if we rewrite the laws…”

“I know. I’m searching for another way. For now, we wait to see how Shirone’s Breaking the System is judged.”

Minerva leaned her cheek against her hand and muttered.

“Would be nice if something triggered it all…”

Eastern Jungcheon-dong.

Though it had been incorporated into the Jincheon Empire, it remained a sacred legal site untouched even by the Empire’s authority.

Tens of thousands of peaks piercing through the clouds had once been places where those who followed the Way of the Law refined their hearts.

It was known as The Monks’ Mountain Range.

But now that the monks of Jungcheon-dong had left for Zion, only common folk who tilled the fields remained...

“Keuhahaha! Humans! Humans!”

…and the demons who slaughtered them.

“Evacuate the civilians!”

The Jincheon Empire’s military responded swiftly, but the number of demons who had occupied Jungcheon-dong reached 400,000.

‘Disgusting bastards.’

Even though Jincheon boasted one of the world’s largest populations, they could spare only 20,000 troops to defend Jungcheon-dong.

“Hold the line! Fight with your lives!”

The monks had gone, but if this sacred site of the Law were to fall into demon hands, humanity’s morale would plummet.

“Graaaah!”

The overwhelming surge of the demon horde was like a great river, and the Imperial forces couldn’t even take advantage of their defensive position.

Gartas, commander of the 7th Division of the Hell Army’s 4th Legion, was a grotesque being whose body was covered with needle-like spikes protruding from his skin.

“You idiot! Is that all you can do to torment them?!”

A demon named Agu, who had been dismembering a human, flinched at Gartas’s roar.

“S-sorry, sir!”

Just as Agu was about to strike again with a dead soldier’s helmet, Gartas thrust his spike-covered foot into Agu’s torso and sent him flying.

“Uaaaagh!”

“No imagination! At the very least…”

Grabbing a soldier’s head, Gartas slammed it into the ground, rubbing it back and forth with incredible speed, like he was erasing it.

“Do it like this!”

Watching the horrific scene from afar, General Park Gi of the Imperial Army snapped.

“You bastard! What do you think humans are?!”

Riding down the hill on horseback, Park Gi caught Gartas’s attention. The demon’s eyes gleamed coldly.

“A fine new toy.”

“Even if I die, I’ll take your head with me!”

Park Gi, a master of fourth-level martial arts, fought fiercely. Dozens of strikes were exchanged in the blink of an eye.

“This is the end!”

His spear pierced Gartas’s throat.

“Kuhuhu, that feels good.”

But Gartas simply walked forward, wrapped his massive body around both horse and rider, and crushed them together.

Kiheeeeeing!

The horse died instantly from shock, and countless spikes pierced through Park Gi’s armor.

“Ghhk!”

“Feels good, doesn’t it?”

Driving slender spikes through a body without killing—just enough to keep someone alive—was a universally recognized form of torture.

“Like this. Like this. Like this.”

Each twitch of Gartas’s body brought new waves of agony, and in the end, Park Gi could only scream in surrender.

“Are you enjoying yourself?”

Turning toward the voice, Park Gi saw a man with tattoos on his face standing there with a sorrowful expression.

“K-Kugh!”

Gartas dropped Park Gi’s body and turned with a contorted, furious face.

“The Buddha…”

If Yahweh was the object of demons’ hatred, the Buddha was their object of fear.

“What brings you such joy?”

Behind Nane, Shura was slaughtering demons en masse with her Gestalt ability.

“Why are you here? Wasn’t it you who freed the altar? You wanted humans to understand fear, didn’t you?”

Nane had come in search of Goffin’s Gate.

“Then there’s no reason for us to fight…”

“Sermon. Pain.”

The moment the words fell, a violet sword flew out and pierced Gartas’s solar plexus.

“GRAAAAGH!”

Gartas’s eyes bulged so wide they looked like they might pop out. He dropped to his knees, clutching his stomach.

“IT HURTS! IT HUUUURTS!”

“Yes. That is pain.”

Even as consciousness slipped away, Park Gi couldn’t believe what he was seeing—Nane had taken down a division commander in a single blow.

‘So this is… the Buddha…’

As Nane stepped forward, Gartas recoiled in horror.

“W-Why are you doing this to me? I did everything you asked! Didn’t I?!”

“What I asked?”

“GAAAAAH!”

The sword vibrated in his gut, spreading pain so intense that it scattered his demonic energy.

“Please! Stop, I’m begging you…!”

“Listen well, lackey of Hell.”

Nane touched the crown of Gartas’s head with the tips of five fingers, freezing him in place.

“If your kind had never existed—or at the very least, if you were even capable of repentance…”

His face twisted in sorrow.

“…I would have no reason to close the world.”

“No! Then just kill m—!”

“Perish.”

As the sermon activated, Gartas’s body burst with a soft pop and scattered into the eternal void.

Realizing the Buddha had appeared, the remaining demons fled in all directions, and the battle now over, Shura approached.

“He’s still breathing.”

Park Gi weakly lifted his head.

“Lord Buddha…”

Nane leaned in to hear him.

“What happens… if I die? Is there really nothing after death?”

“It is merely awakening from a dream.”

Nane placed a hand on Park Gi’s chest.

“This is but a fleeting dream. So don’t fear. When you open your eyes, you’ll recall this moment and chuckle quietly.”

“…Is that so?”

Through the pain, Park Gi managed a faint smile.

“I hope I wake up soon.”

Closing his eyes with the most peaceful expression in the world, he likely would not fall into Hell.

Nane pressed his palms together in prayer and wished him peace, then raised his eyes to look around.

The scene, filled with corpses, resembled the Hell he had seen in the inner worlds.

“There are no more survivors.”

Shura thought these words might bring some comfort.

“Is that a good thing?”

But Nane questioned him.

“W-Well, war is always horrible. So many lives suffer, and precious people disappear…”

Nane clutched at his chest.

“Ah.”

His mouth opened involuntarily, and from deep within, a scream erupted.

“AAAAAH! AAAAAH! AAAAAAH!”

The vibration of the sermon shook all of Jungcheon-dong, and Shura, unable to grasp its meaning, simply trembled.

“My heart aches.”

The thought of the suffering of all those souls cast into the demon world tore at his heart a thousand, ten thousand ways.

“Even with this pain… I still…”

Nane turned to Shura with a sorrowful expression.

“…cannot shed a single tear.”

The irony of Emptiness.

And so, Nane questioned everything.
