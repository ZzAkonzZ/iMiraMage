Chapter 865: Rain (4)

When the magnetic shield sent by the Ark was lifted, the Jincheon army finally advanced into the capital of Gustav.

The army was silent, unable to find words.

"Is this it?"

The world, flattened by the particles of light, showed no trace of demons.

An overwhelming victory.

"What are we, really?" What was this victory for?

The phenomenon that transcended human physics and arithmetic left them feeling powerless.

"What about all the soldiers who fought until now? The innocent citizens of Jincheon who died! What about us?"

There was no one to blame.

"What do we exist for?"

If one man's anger was all it took to end the world, what did it mean for those who remained?

Yahweh's anger left the world in desolation, erasing the Gustav Empire from the map.

"Commander! The storm is getting stronger!"

A powerful storm, equivalent to category six, swept across the continent, but there was nothing left to blow away.

Thud! Thud! Thud!

Deep underground, Shirone, who had fallen asleep, heard the sound of rain in his dreams.

"Gasp!"

Though he had no memory of it, his body knew that the droplets of light from the minor purification had destroyed the world.

"Are you okay?"

Rian's voice came from the darkness.

"Where are we?"

Without thinking about where he was, he asked, and a torch was lit on his right.

In a space over 30 pyeong that Rian had undoubtedly dug out, dozens of people were sitting.

Their expressions were a mix of fear and resentment as they looked at Shirone.

Gustav had been protected by demons, and now they had lost even their place to live.

Rian looked up at the ceiling and spoke.

"A typhoon is blowing. Let's stay here for now."

It was the strongest wind Rian had ever seen, but with nothing standing above ground, it would soon pass.

"I want to go."

Shirone spoke weakly.

"Let's go." Rian looked at Shirone, who was hanging his head in a depressed manner, and thought.

Although he was the main hero of the victory, he understood Shirone's desire to leave the people of Gustav.

"Alright, let's go."

As Rian stood up, an old man muttered.

"You're crazy. You'll be blown away by the wind."

"If the rain seeps through the ground, collect the water. The typhoon will pass in about two days."

Since Rian had created the underground shelter with a few sword strikes, the people didn't argue further.

"Get on my back."

When Rian offered his back, Shirone shook his head.

"I can go on my own. And to cross the typhoon, I'll need to use magic."

"No, rest a bit more. You're exhausted. I'll break through with Axing. Get on my back."

As the world's strongest magician, Shirone's mental strength would recover with just a few hours of sleep.

But his heart was wounded.

After a moment of thought, Shirone decided and climbed onto Rian's back.

'He's so light.'

Rian, who could lift boulders, was shocked at how weightless his friend's body felt.

It seemed withered.

"Let's go."

Rian placed his greatsword against the ceiling and struck upward with all his might, creating a hole with a pop.

As people stared blankly, Rian leaped out of the shelter in one bound.

Whoosh!

The tremendous wind struck them, but Rian's body, reinforced with Deny, didn't budge.

Rain poured down, and the angle of the falling raindrops was almost horizontal.

"Hoo."

Rian, holding the greatsword in front to cut through the wind, slowly walked and asked,

"Where should we go?"

Shirone clung to Rian's neck like a lost child.

"...Rian."

After walking aimlessly for ten minutes, Shirone spoke.

"Don't you resent me?"

Rian thought.

'If I say I don't resent him... will Shirone believe me?'

Even if it was an unavoidable situation, could he truly have no hatred for the one who killed his family?

"I'm sad that my brother died too."

Though his voice was buried in the wind, Shirone heard it through Rian's back.

"I wanted to talk to him more. But Shirone, I don't resent you."

"Because of the knight's oath?"

"Maybe..."

Rian spoke honestly.

"But the knight's oath means nothing. There are many knights in the world, and they betray their lords. I don't resent you because of the knight's oath, but because you are my lord."

Shirone was too exhausted to immediately grasp the difference between the two.

"It's not your fault. If you decided to do something, I believe there was a very big and deep reason I couldn't understand."

"The greater good."

Shirone hugged Rian's neck tightly.

"Do you think my family will understand too?" Rian couldn't answer.

"Will Reyna, Grandpa Klump, and Uncle Bishoff believe me?"

"Let's go to the Ivory Tower."

The magicians of the Ivory Tower might be able to comfort Shirone.

"I'm going to Tormia."

"Shirone."

"I'm going to the Ogent family to ask for forgiveness. I can't do anything until then."

Rian silently turned in that direction.

Whoosh!

The typhoon roared as it struck them.

There was nothing left on the ground.

The sea of Jincheon burned fiercely.

"Ahhh!" In the flames, Mitura, the commander of the 8th legion, fought with all his might, but he couldn't hold back the four Five Stars.

"The demon realm..."

Shouldn't he open it now?

"Idiot."

Minerva, flying 100 meters in the air, inhaled from her pipe and exhaled smoke.

As the flammable gas reacted with the fire, an explosion blew away the surrounding sea.

"Ugh!"

Burning in the steam, Mitura spread his limbs wide and roared.

"I'll kill you all!"

Amanta, the balance master, led the charge.

"He's bluffing. Keep going."

As he predicted, the demon realm didn't open, and the remaining three launched a full-scale attack.

"Ugh! Ugh!"

Mitura was miserable.

"Why..."

Why couldn't he open the demon realm?

Even with the resolve for self-destruction, he couldn't reach the destination.

"The state of mind seems separate from strength."

Freed raised his sword and charged.

"But sometimes, it's everything." Though they couldn't open the demon realm, the Five Stars each had convictions that transcended death.

"Do you want to know why you can't open the demon realm?"

Mitura, bound by Xing's law, looked up at Freed, who was flying into the sky.

"You can't open it because you can't."

The mind is truly mysterious.

"The demon realm!"

Mitura shouted, but the demon realm didn't open, and Freed's sword beheaded him.

With a splash, Mitura's face, still showing surprise, sank into the depths of the sea.

"It's over."

As Freed sheathed his sword, the three remaining Five Stars quickly flew around.

"The demons fled. What's going on?"

Mitura was an idiot, but it was unusual for the demons to scatter against the commander's orders.

"It must be related to Gustav. Shirone went there, so it must have been resolved somehow."

It would be a while before they were shocked by the news of the minor purification.

Xing spoke.

"Let's leave this to Jincheon and return to the Ivory Tower. We need to plan our next steps."

There were no objections, and the four Five Stars immediately crossed the sea towards the Arctic.

Jincheon survived.

The 11th legion, which had taken over the south, was fiercely battling a party of just three.

The legion commander had already been annihilated by Gaold, but they still had over ten million troops.

"There they are! Kill them!"

Gaold, wearing a tattered coat, was accompanied by Gangnan and Zulu, who had summoned Cougar, ready for battle.

Gangnan spoke.

"Leave this to us. You need to rest."

Gaold was in a dire state, his hair white and his body emaciated.

"Rest?"

From the moment he refused eternal rest, there was no rest in Gaold's life.

"What is that?"

As always, Gaold marched straight towards the approaching army.

The power of destruction would annihilate the demons, but Gaold's magic came with pain.

'I can endure.'

Giving up now would make all the years he endured too regretful.

"Miro."

Gaold's eyes rolled back.

'Air Press.'

The sensation...

"Huh?"

When he opened his eyes, the air smelled completely different from the demons surrounding him.

'What is this?'

Grotesque demons with blades instead of arms charged at Gaold.

"Die!"

Reflexively firing the air gun, the twisted bodies were torn to shreds.

'Where am I?'

As he struggled to clear his mind, a brigade commander raised a hammer behind him.

"Watch out!"

Gangnan leaped and struck the brigade commander's face with her knee.

With divine power, the monster's face shattered, and Gangnan twisted her head as she landed.

"What are you doing! Open the exit quickly!"

"Time..."

Gaold, mumbling, asked.

"What day is it today?"

Gangnan frowned at the sudden question but had no time to argue.

"The 16th! Why?"

"The 16th?"

Four days of memory were missing from the moment he cast Air Press on the army.

"Damn it."

Since developing self-replicating mutations, he had experienced short-term memory loss, but never for this long.

"Attack!"

Demons with bat wings fired fireballs at Gaold and Gangnan.

"Gaold...!"

As Kangnan shouted, Gaold's eyes lit up, and the atmospheric pressure soared.

"Ahhh!"

Clutching his head and screaming, Kangnan and Zulu, who were walking on the beach, turned to look.

"Are you in pain? Isn't this happening too often lately?"

Gaold looked around.

'The sea. Is this the edge of the island?'

He noticed Kangnan's clothes were singed.

"When did you get hurt?"

"It happened this morning when we broke through the encirclement. You were with me. What's going on?"

This time, it had been about six hours.

Zulu, realizing something was wrong with Gaold, spoke in her usually quiet manner.

"Tell us why. If we're going to keep fighting, we need to know."

"I..."

His memory was cut off.

"Ahhh!"

When he came to his senses, he felt immense pain, and demons surrounded him.

"I will avenge the commander!"

The cylindrical pillars of Air Press struck the demons, but Gaold felt completely out of place.

'What am I?'

Why was he fighting?

"Let's retreat! There are too many of them! The power of destruction is also decreasing, remember?"

Was it?

'The power is weakening?'

Because he couldn't bear the pain.

"Wait a moment."

Overcoming Kangnan's grip on his coat, Gaold placed his hand on her shoulder.

"What now?"

"I..."

Gaold asked with a confused expression.

"Why are we fighting?"

"To get Miro. She wants you to fight. Is that enough?"

Gaold's pupils shook.

"Miro."

...Who was that?

Kangnan gritted her teeth and spoke.

"Be honest. Is there something you're hiding from us?"

"Oh, Miro."

He remembered.

"It's nothing. I've just been thinking a lot lately. Anyway, let's get out of here."

As Gaold started to move, Kangnan grabbed him.

"Really?"

"Yes."

Kangnan's eyes widened.

"Do you know? You asked me the same question two hours ago."

Gaold couldn't answer.
