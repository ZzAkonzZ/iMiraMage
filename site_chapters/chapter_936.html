Chapter 936: In The Flower Field (4)

Though Ymir failed to annihilate Goffin, merely delaying his actions for a considerable time was enough to fulfill his initial purpose.

On that tragic day, Ikael had fled with her child in her arms, using all her strength.

Anke Ra used the Akashic Records to strip Ikael of her powers, and numerous angels and maras attacked her.

Ashur, Lethe, Ramus.

Ikael's three guardian spirits fought fiercely, but without Ikael's powers, they couldn't hold out.

In the fierce battle, two maras perished, and only Ashur survived, albeit with fatal injuries.

Ikael and her child were imprisoned in heaven's jail, and soon the day of execution was scheduled.

The multitude of citizens gathered in the square pointed at Ikael and shouted.

"Punishment for the fallen angel! Bring pain! Annihilation!"

Even as she was insulted by those she once ruled, Ikael only worried about her child.

"Please! Kill me instead! Just save my child, our child!"

The maras shouted.

"Shut up! What kind of angel are you! Pure spirit? You seem utterly depraved!"

Shirone shed tears.

'Ikael.'

Even on this second encounter, the sight of Ikael's wailing overwhelmed him emotionally.

Everyone looked at her with disdain.

"Kill her! Kill the corrupted creature!"

Especially Kariel, who looked at Ikael with a gaze not just disdainful but almost maniacal.

'She bore a human child?'

He didn't even want to know how that was possible.

'The most noble archangel...

Harboring the desires of a beast.

"Please, not the child... I beg of you."

Ikael looked around at the archangels with a desperate expression, but they all averted their gaze.

Only Kariel met her eyes, and his celestial body trembled intensely.

'So why did you do this?'

Why?

The most powerful, pure, and beloved angel by God, needing such a piece of flesh?

'Then what am I? What are we angels to you, Ikael?'

Though there were differences in degree, surely all angels were thinking the same.

"Waaah, waaah."

The child cried sorrowfully.

As if sensing death through animalistic instinct, but Shirone knew it was merely responding to Ikael's voice.

"Begin the execution!"

Odriel's three-sided mara approached with a huge axe.

As flames erupted from his nostrils and touched the child, Ikael lost her senses.

"No! Stop it! Aaaaah! Aaaah!"

Though her amplification powers were sealed, her screams echoed far and wide through the heavens.

"Huuuuh..." In a corner of the abode of Zebul, Satiel clutched her head.

"Aaaaah! Aaaaah!"

With each scream of Ikael, Satiel felt her celestial body being shattered.

"How long will you drag this out?"

Metiel, the Archangel of Union, entered.

Seeing Satiel crouching half-mad, her expression turned fierce.

"You got what you wanted, didn't you? But now you're acting weak? Don't tell me you feel guilty?"

"Get out."

Satiel's voice was icy cold, but Metiel was enraged.

"You really don't fit as an archangel. Too weak. You followed Ikael around and then betrayed her, and now you act like a victim. What kind of angel are you..."

Metiel stopped talking.

Suddenly standing up, Satiel's eyes burned with an unreachable murderous intent.

"What did you say? Say it again. I'll kill you." "Did I do something wrong? No, the sin is Ikael's. She had a human child. Yes, I did what an angel should do. Even Anke Ra praised me. But what's wrong?"

Satiel found a way out of the pain.

"Yes, humans are the problem. Those despicable pieces of flesh have caused chaos in the world."

In Satiel's eyes, which she thought most resembled humans, there was only a cold pride like law.

"Yes."

Metiel could only respond that way.

If she pushed her further, she might even break the angelic taboo.

"Decapitate!"

As someone cried out, the mara's axe beheaded the child.

"Aaaaah! Aaaah!"

The half-mad screams of Ikael were torture even for the archangels.

"My baby! Our baby!"

As Ikael cried tears of blood and distorted her face in agony, she screamed.

"You wretched beings!"

In a different sense of madness, not just the archangels but even the citizens turned to look at Ikael.

"What crime have I committed? Open the White Hall! I will clarify everything myself!"

"White Hall? Haha, do you still think you're an archangel?"

Provoked further by the mara's mockery, Ikael's eyes flashed with anger as she continued.

"I am not the one who sinned! It's not me but Anke Ra! I will expose everything he has done!"

'She's completely lost her mind.'

All the angels thought so.

"Listen carefully! Anke Ra is not a god! Anke Ra, in his meeting with...

Time stopped.

And the memories began to erase from the minds of all beings, including angels.

At this point, Shirone realized.

'When I first met Ikael in heaven.'

She had been imprisoned in Aravot for a great sin, but all beings had forgotten what that sin was.

'It wasn't Ikael's sin.'

What Anke Ra truly wanted to erase was his own sin for not keeping his promise to Goffin.

'God broke his promise. He lied.'

Could there be any clearer evidence that this world is unstable?

Unable to reset after the worst scenarios caused by Goffin, Anke Ra chose to erase only specific events from the Akashic Records.

This type of erasure caused fractures throughout, but leaving it would have destroyed everything.

Shirone stroked Ikael's face tenderly as she slept peacefully, having lost her memories.

'Forget.'

Perhaps it was the only peace she could have.

Humans, having lost their memories, lived as busily as ever.

The angels also performed their duties diligently, despite the unease of empty memories.

'I have sinned.'

Only Ikael remained unable to step out of her prison in Aravot.

'I have inflicted an indelible wound on Lord Anke Ra.'

Only guilt remained.

'What was the sin?'

Currently, other archangels were pursuing the last Gaian, McClain Goffin.

If even one individual existed, the complete dismantling of the Ultima System was impossible.

'Goffin. I surely know, yet I can't remember.'

Filled with information, the celestial body now only retained a feeling.

Fighting, antagonizing, screaming.

'That feeling.'

But on the other hand.

'Was it happiness?'

If so, it makes no sense.

The only thing she could guess was that she had known him for a very long time.

'Anyway, I am not an archangel. Until I repent of my sin, I need not worry.'

On a night with a crescent moon, Ikael rose from her bed.

"Who is it?"

Beyond the curtain of darkness untouched by moonlight stood a human as tall as an angel.

"It's me."

The moment she saw Goffin's face, Ikael was stunned.

"A Gaia? How did you get here?"

As Ikael tried to get up, Goffin smiled and extended his hand.

"It's okay. Just stay seated." As if by magic, she found herself wanting to stay still with an intense passion.

"I'm sorry. It's all my fault. I couldn't protect you."

When Goffin, kneeling on one knee, grabbed her wrist, Ikael quickly pulled away.

If the person before her was the only Gaia, there was no need to ask his name.

"Goffin, how dare you come here? I'll capture you and present you to Anke Ra right now."

Goffin's heart sank at her cold voice.

'You are my everything.' He wanted to shout that they had loved each other, that they were connected with all their hearts.

Goffin wiped away his tears and said, "I've come to say goodbye."

Only Goffin knew how much Ikael had suffered after losing their child.

'You don't need to remember. Forget it forever.'

If this could prevent Ikael from hurting, Goffin was willing to leave.

"Are you saying you're going to end your life?"

Not understanding Goffin's words, Ikael suddenly asked.

"No, but why are you telling me this?"

Goffin couldn't hold back.

"Because I love you."

"Rubbish! Is this another trick to humiliate me? Even now, you... huh?"

As she raged, Ikael felt something on her cheek and suddenly stopped speaking.

"Why?"

Tears of light were streaming down like a waterfall.

"Why am I crying?"

Watching her, Goffin smiled sadly with tear-filled eyes.

"Even if memories fade, emotions remain."

That's what the heart is.

"So even if you don't remember, it doesn't make me sad. I'll take your heart with me."

Ikael's tears floated in the air and settled on Goffin's palm.

"Emotions remain?"

Ikael was still confused.

'Why won't the tears stop? Why does it hurt so much? What did I do with this human...'

"You don't need to try. As long as the heart remains, you'll know everything someday. And then..." Goffin gently stroked Ikael's cheek.

"I hope you won't be sad."

As he stood up and kissed her halo lightly, Ikael's body trembled.

'This feeling. This touch.'

It was familiar.

No, it wasn't just familiar; it felt like something she had experienced repeatedly throughout her life, making her mind feel warm and cozy.

"Goodbye, Ikael."

As Goffin's body turned to light and disappeared, Ikael could only watch.

The room fell silent again.

"Oh. Ohhh."

As if something in her mind had broken, Ikael's tears didn't stop all night.

The place where Goffin fled from the archangels' pursuit was where the Ark of Minor Purification had arrived.

It was Shirone's planet.

'It has changed a lot.'

The humans born from the giant's body looked different from Goffin but had similar life directions.

They were also descendants of Gaia.

'Is it still the primitive era?'

Though early civilizations were emerging, reaching the truth seemed far off.

Turning his back on the war-torn civilizations fighting for more wealth, Goffin thought,

'It's not the right time.'

Even if he revealed the secrets of the world to them, nothing would change.

"What should I do now?" The civilized areas were extremely narrow, and Goffin wandered the planet's vast nature, thinking.

Though he carried Ikael's heart in Hexa, the loss of their child weighed heavily on him too.

"It's all so meaningless."

Perhaps this was the way of the world.

Feeling the need to reflect on everything, Goffin stopped for the first time.

Walking on the surface of a vast lake, he sat cross-legged on a lotus flower.

"Well, I'll be relying on you for a while. Please take care of me."

Though it was just a plant that couldn't hear or speak, it was Goffin's only companion.

"My heart is deeply wounded. There's no one to listen to my complaints."

Rewinding time, Goffin recalled when it all began.

"A long time ago, there was a race called Gaia."

It was a story spanning 40,000 years.
