Chapter 546:

Mucus had completely engulfed the city.

The sight of viscous slime sloshing in every direction, flowing toward some unknown destination, was dizzying to behold.

“We’re blocked! Turn back!”

Faced with a wave of slime filling an entire city block, Miro's group turned the corner.

Tentacles protruded from the mucus, trailing behind them as if tickling their backs, before being reabsorbed into the main body.

“Damn it! It’s going to get in our way until the end!”

“Hold on a bit more. We’re almost out.”

By the time they exited the city, dawn was breaking.

The mucus flowed endlessly toward somewhere in the desert, and at that moment, Miro reviewed the plan.

“Shura will probably try to stop me. But according to reports, there’s one more Council of Ten officer. If that’s the case, I won’t be able to help you.”

If she could handle two of the Council’s officers, it wouldn’t be a bad trade-off.

“So let’s split up. Fermi and Marsha, circle around and attack from the sides. That’ll split their focus.”

Rian spoke up.

“So I’ll use that opening to infiltrate the pyramid.”

“Exactly. Destroy the Law using your Axing. That should let you reach Shirone.”

Rian’s Axing nullified the Law.

“Can I really do this?”

“A Yaksha’s Axing acts directly on the body. That’s what makes them so troublesome to a Banya. It’s all about how strong your will is.”

‘Will, huh...’

Rian turned the word over in his mind several times.

The mucus blanketed the horizon like a creeping fog.

Abandoning energy circulation entirely, it surged toward Shirone with a speed far beyond their calculations.

“The farthest point from the mucus is to the north.”

“I’ll go.”

Breaking from formation, Rian increased his speed and sprinted across the desert.

With Axing activated, every step he took caused bursts of sand to erupt like landmines.

“Alright, let’s begin too.”

At Miro’s signal, Marsha and Fermi split to the east and west flanks.

Assaulting all sides of the pyramid simultaneously was no easy feat for a group of just four, but if they could hold out, it would give Rian a real chance.

“They’re splitting up, just as expected.”

Atop the pyramid, Shura opened her eyes.

Standing beside her were Paknyeo and King Kong, the leader of the Undergrounders.

The 3-meter-tall brute with a thick coat of fur gazed down at the 300 or so subordinates circling the pyramid.

“We’re ready to move at any time. Just give the word.”

Paknyeo asked, “Where’s Miro?”

“South. She’s coming straight from the city toward us.”

Shura issued her orders to the Guardians.

“Guard the east and west. I’ll take Miro myself.”

King Kong asked, “And our unit?”

Shura glanced toward the northern horizon where Rian was circling wide.

“Take care of the swordsman. Looks like he’s a decoy. Three hundred should be more than enough.”

“UOOOOOO!”

With her command, King Kong pounded his chest and leapt down from the pyramid.

“IT’S WAR! GET READY!”

“KYAHAHAHAHA!”

As monkey-like screeches echoed across the desert, Paknyeo turned to Shura.

“What about me?”

“Protect Shirone. I think I’m about to go all out with Miro.”

From the northwest settlement, Arbiter Bebeto used his coder abilities to ascend to the top of the pyramid.

“Lady Shura, I have arrived.”

As Miro sprinted across the desert toward them, a dark gleam lit up in Shura’s eyes.

“Hehehe… Long time no see.”

“Shura.”

Aiming her gaze at the top of the distant pyramid, Miro took a deep breath and prepared her opening strike.

“Fwooooo…”

From her body, the Avatar of Avalokiteshvara burst forth like a flame.

At its peak scale, the avatar’s massive form seized the sky itself, forcing all who saw it to gape in awe.

“W-What the hell is that?!”

Prajñāpāramitā.

Miro’s eyes flared as she pressed her palms together.

‘Pure Land Burial!’

The space-time around her fractured into extremity, and Avalokiteshvara’s massive arms—each imbued with immense power—came crashing down on the pyramid from both sides.

“Kyahahahahahaha!”

At the same time, a colossal serpent avatar burst from Shura’s body, coiling tightly around the pyramid.

Apostate Banya – Collapse Gestalt.

KWA-AAAAANG!

A shockwave like the heavens falling shook the pyramid to its foundations.

Each time Avalokiteshvara’s dual arms pounded the barrier, streams of code flickered across the dome.

“As expected… this won’t be easy.”

The God of False Doctrine, who deceives the Law, was clearly the perfect counter to Miro, who had reached the pinnacle of truth.

“Kihihihi… Still such a fiery brat, aren’t you, Miro?”

As Shura grinned down from the pyramid, her lips stretched upward like a snake, all the way to her eyes.

“They’re coming!”

From the east and west flanks, Guardians rained down over Fermi and Marsha’s heads.

Mutant abilities varied widely, and each of them now had to take on six or more opponents alone.

‘To pay back a debt.’

Marsha drew her Dagger of Blasphemy.

‘For 190 billion gold.’

Fermi swallowed a depreciation-linked trade chip.

Moments later, thunderous magical explosions rang out from the east and west faces of the pyramid.

“Damn it! They’re having all the fun up there! Ooh-ooh! When do WE get to fight?!”

As Taejang complained, King Kong, arms crossed, responded in a stern voice.

“Wait. Our king has promised us eternal food. After this, we’ll never starve again.”

“Food spotted!”

From the northern perimeter, an Undergrounder with a telescope shouted.

A lone swordsman shimmered in the desert’s heat haze, walking toward them.

“Shirone…”

Rian watched Shirone ascend the steps of the pyramid and slowly began walking forward.

Mucus had fully claimed the surrounding land, and its ooze now crawled toward Rian with unexpected speed, but there was no need to rush.

There was only one chance, and even if it cost him his life, this mission had to be completed.

‘While I was gone… you fought harder than I ever did. Stronger than me…’

When Miro described the life Shirone had lived, the first emotion that flooded Rian’s mind was regret.

‘I… didn’t have the strength to protect you.’

Only those who’ve truly considered death can understand another’s resolve.

‘Whether you were fighting or not… death must have always been beside you. And even in that fear, you grit your teeth and took another step forward.’

Three hundred Undergrounders formed layered defenses, and the mucus trailed Rian’s very shadow.

‘Now, I’ll protect you.’

Rian’s steps came to a halt.

He drove his greatsword into the desert sand and knelt on one knee, offering a knight’s prayer.

‘Raise a kingdom of conviction within my heart.’

The mucus curved like a snake, encircling from behind.

‘Infuse my blade with an unbending will and the pride stronger than steel.’

The slime split, flowing around him.

‘Grant me the courage to choose what is right…’

SPLASH!

A wave of mucus rose and crashed down over Rian.

‘…and carve into me the resolve never to falter before it.’

By the time his Axing activated, Rian was already 5 meters beyond the mucus.

‘Let me always stand on the side of the weak…’

“KYAAAYAYAYA! KILL HIM! KILL HIM!”

An elite squad of Undergrounders rushed in, tracing curves across the desert.

‘…and never surrender to the logic of the strong!’

Rian split the first Undergrounder in two. (1 kill)

‘Guide my sword by the spirit of chivalry alone!’

He twisted his torso, cleaving another’s waist clean through. (2 kills)

“Open fire!”

Bullets slammed into the desert, kicking up clouds of sand.

As the mucus approached within reach, Rian’s steps quickened—until he was running.

“Don’t let him get through! Stop him here!”

After the elite squad was annihilated, the main force charged in, riding jeeps and motorcycles.

Rian blocked their path with his greatsword. The impact of bullets rattled his bones—but just as always, <Idea> never breaks.

“YAAAH!”

His will to strike down his enemies nullified their Laws. With a single swing, he cut through Undergrounders and their motorcycles. (3 kills)

“Flank him from the right!”

Rian didn’t care about the detours. He simply cut down anything that blocked his way. (5 kills)

“Don’t let him through!” (6 kills)

Another wave charged, spraying bullets—yet Rian sliced through the landscape itself, opening a path. (8 kills)

“What… What the hell is that guy?” (10 kills)
