Chapter 1045: The Limits of Discipline (4)

Wizard gained courage.

“Thank you.”

No longer needing to worry about her parents’ opinions, the standards for her training soared dramatically.

“It’s still not enough. More.”

Lingering too long at 6,000 beats per second, the point Shirone had previously demonstrated, was likely a psychological barrier.

“Don’t try to control your mind. Become your mind. Don’t analyze it—throw yourself into the unknown.”

“Hnnngh!”

Wizard’s gaze shifted once more, and her heartbeat rhythm began to skyrocket rapidly.

The speaker that announced the buzzer exploded with a pop, and error messages appeared on the recording device.

The teachers gaped in stunned silence.

“She’s exceeded the capacity of the image zone.”

Very few people could comprehend how far her ability had risen at that moment.

Bartok muttered under his breath.

“7,200 beats per second.”

He wasn’t counting.

Like a synesthete counting leaves by feel, he was experiencing Wizard’s mental pulses as a whole.

Shirone had the highest degree of synesthesia among them.

‘To be precise, 7,287 beats per second.’

As Wizard’s eyes rolled back, teachers rushed over to check the image zone.

“Master Bartok, perhaps we should stop for now? Without knowing the precise numbers, this could be dangerous…”

“She’s broken through.”

Shirone’s eyes gleamed, and the faces of the Ten Council of Mages flushed with excitement.

“Broken through? What do you mean?”

Amy answered, “She’s caught the flow. Breaking through limits isn’t easy. If she can’t push to her peak after breaking the wall, it’ll hurt her progress in the future.”

Bartok admitted honestly, “I can’t tell anymore. What level is Wizard currently at?”

Shirone took a moment to assess.

“9,273 beats per second.”

The students and teachers were so awestruck they couldn’t speak, though there was still some unease about stopping at that point.

‘The goal is twice what I demonstrated—12,000 beats per second.’

In essence, the level at which Shirone’s berserk mode could obliterate an entire small city.

‘Unless she reaches that level, she won’t be able to advance to the next stage. There’s still so much to teach, and time is running out.’

“Hahh… hahh…”

At that moment, Wizard’s body trembled, and harsh groans escaped from her mouth.

Amy frowned. “Shirone, she’s in shock.”

“She can’t help but be afraid. It’s a world completely unknown to her, something she’s never experienced before.”

Wizard’s groans turned into screams.

“Aaah! Aaaah!”

Seeing her parents’ faces turn pale, Shirone approached the image zone.

“It’s okay, Wizard.”

The screaming stopped abruptly.

“I’ve been there too. Everything you thought you knew crumbles, and you feel like you don’t know anything anymore.”

‘Is this the right path? Am I doing something wrong? If I fail now, will I lose my only chance?’

Wizard heard his voice as if from a trance.

“But that uncertainty is proof that you’re still fighting. Don’t seek comfort by finding the right answer. Embrace the uncertainty that’s tormenting you and move forward.”

There is no compass to guide you.

“Fight.”

That was all Shirone could say.

“Push through.”

“Aaaaaah!”

Focus returned to Wizard’s eyes, and her heartbeat surged at an incredible speed.

Shirone clenched his fist.

‘She did it.’

Maintaining a steady 12,000 beats per second, Wizard looked around.

“Whoa… what is this?”

She still seemed scared, but her flushed face showed more exhilaration than fear.

Clap. Clap. Clap.

Bartok started clapping, followed by the Ten Council of Mages, Amy, the teachers, and the students.

“Incredible…”

Even Wizard’s parents, who knew little about magic, could sense that something extraordinary had just happened.

As Wizard stepped down from the image zone and halted her heartbeat, everyone rushed over.

“You were amazing, Wizard! That was incredible!”

Though her face was pale from blood loss, Wizard’s expression was one of pure happiness.

“Hehe! If I hadn’t listened to you at the end, I would’ve failed. I was so scared…”

Amidst the waves of congratulatory voices, only Shirone’s eyes carried a hint of sadness.

‘It’s been proven.’

That she was the only one who could eliminate Havitz.

‘Enjoy yourself. Do whatever you want. The world will grant you endless freedom. No one will dare criticize you, no matter what you do.’

And that’s what made him sadder.

‘But there’s a price you must bear. One thing you can never put down…’

Hope.

‘No matter the situation, you can never speak of impossibility. Even when everyone else gives up, when everyone else waits for the end in despair, you must always say this:’

It can be done.

‘That’s your destiny. The future entrusted to you.’

Shirone couldn’t bring himself to smile in the face of the immense hope that rested on a seven-year-old child.

‘At least now, we can fight. We have created the natural enemy capable of killing Satan.’

Steeling his heart, Shirone turned to Bartok and asked, “Has there been any word from Lampa? What’s happening in Kashan?”

“There’s no word yet. Havitz also disappeared after entering Aganos.”

‘What is he planning?’

Shirone could have gone directly to Kashan to check, but a formidable opponent named Uorin awaited there.

‘Until the Holy War Council convenes, no special actions should be taken. Her foresight will change the political landscape.’

Everything would be decided at the Holy War.

‘There’s no time. I must complete Wizard’s training and succeed in purifying the Demon Realm.’

The outcome of Shirone’s simultaneous efforts would determine the fate of humanity that day.

‘Only 23 days remain.’

That was the time left until the Holy War Council.

"You ungrateful brats. How could you do this to me? After everything I've done for you!"

Lethe stormed out of the Duke's castle, unable to calm her anger.

"This is the last time. Never again… Huh?"

As she looked up at the loud explosion ahead, she saw an entire district of the city being obliterated.

"What is that?"

Even Lethe knew that the intensity of the shock wasn't something commonly seen in hell.

"Huh?"

In the distance, she saw two figures fiercely battling.

'Who are they? Demons?'

One was clearly a demon, but the other was a species that didn't exist in hell.

Boom!

A flash of light from the demon's hand struck the other figure, causing a thunderous explosion.

It wasn't the kind of impact and noise that could come from hitting a mere living body.

"What is this?"

Lethe immediately took off.

"Ugh!"

Son Yu-jung's face twisted as she slid across the ground.

'What kind of person is this?'

No matter how vigorous a young body was, it couldn't defeat a martial arts master.

'She neutralizes all my attacks.'

The way the demon fought was exactly like that.

"Hmph!"

As she put strength into her feet, the sliding soles dug into the ground, creating a mound of dirt.

"Who are you?"

Son Yu-jung glared at the red-skinned demon, who was emitting a golden haze.

"Why does it matter?"

Shirone was angry.

What set her apart from other strong opponents was her lack of interest in anything but combat.

'A thunderbolt.'

Whether the world was destroyed or humanity perished, her mind was filled only with a fighting spirit.

'You're really annoying, Nane.'

As Shirone's expression grew colder, the light emanating from his body became even more vivid.

'That's the problem.'

Son Yu-jung tensed up.

She couldn't understand the logic, but it felt as deep as Nane's teachings.

"You're not a demon, are you? Why are you interfering with me? Just do your job."

"What?"

Shirone sneered.

"Don't be mistaken. You're the one interfering with my work. Whatever your goal is…"

As he clasped his hands, the radiant light compressed with terrifying pressure.

"You are insignificant compared to me."

When Shirone spread his hands, a blinding flash illuminated the front.

'This guy…'

As Shirone charged faster than the light, Son Yu-jung gritted her teeth.

'Golden Eyes.'

She usually felt disgusted by those who pretended to be great, but not this time.

'What?'

What she saw through the eyes that pierced the truth was a heart filled with pure love.

'Yahweh?'

A chill ran down her spine.

'Just my luck!'

Who would have thought that after being struck by Buddha, she would encounter another truth here?

"Go."

As Shirone struck upward with his palm, a sphere of light hit her abdomen.

"Damn it…!"

There were no more questions.

But just because the truth was revealed didn't mean the impact on her body lessened.

"Aaah!"

Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!

As Son Yu-jung disappeared, crashing through buildings, Shirone watched indifferently.

The most annoying part was that it wasn't over yet.

"Hoo."

That's why Nane had fitted her with the chains and sent her to hell.

"Just wait, Nane."

His smiling face lingered in her mind.

Rian asked,

"Two methods?"

"Yes. You know why the chains of Gehenna break. To fix this error immediately, there are two methods. One, fix it systemically. Two, fix you."

"Hmm."

Rian couldn't decide which was better.

"Each has its pros and cons. If we fix the system, you won't be bound by karma anymore. Since you've transcended the system, it's better to set you outside its parameters."

It meant he wouldn't have to endure the burning pain in his heart anymore.

"What does fixing me mean?"

"It means bypassing the karma set on you. In other words, making the system mistake you. The reason this is possible is because of your weapon, 'Idea.'"

Rian drew his greatsword.

"This?"

"That sword has an unbreakable code applied to it. It requires a master code, so it can't be made in hell. Using it, we can bypass the karma."

"I don't understand the technical terms."

Marguerite explained at Rian's level.

"We'll connect the chains of Gehenna to 'Idea.' The pain of fire will continue, but this world won't demand your karma anymore."

"I see."

Marguerite spread his index and middle fingers.

"The first method takes a long time but is advantageous for you. The second is quick but requires you to endure the pain. Honestly, I don't like either. Ideally, I'd analyze everything about you and restore the system, but…"

He doubted Lethe would give them that much time.

"So you decide. The first or the second."

Rian didn't hesitate.

"The second."

"…Are you sure? The pain will be incomparable to what you've endured so far. When the chains of Gehenna connect to 'Idea,' the pain will circulate. Of course, your sword's capacity to purify magic will be unlimited."

"I don't mind."

Rian sheathed his greatsword on his back.

"I'm worried about Shirone. He tries to listen, even to demons."

"Are you different?"

To Marguerite, Rian also seemed human.

"Yes."

Rian said,

"I know demons have feelings too. But… nothing can take precedence over Shirone."

Marguerite, who had been glaring at Rian with sharp eyes, sighed and picked up a knife.

"Come this way."

The surgery began.
