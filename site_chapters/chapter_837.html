Chapter 837: Depression (1)

The army of hell split into two in the central city of Kashan.

Natasha and Zetaro led the 40 million troops heading towards the central continent, where the Holy War was stationed.

The remaining 10 million, under Balkan's orders, charged towards Kashan's capital.

In Aganos, the capital of Kashan, a grand party was held on the eve of a war that would determine the empire's fate.

In the grand hall filled with lively music, all the prominent nobles of Kashan gathered.

'Why are they holding a party in such times?' Their faces were uniformly grim, and the bad news coming through their secretaries weighed heavily on their hearts.

"Is this what a human party is like?"

Kido, looking at the long table laden with countless delicacies, swallowed his saliva.

"Where should I start?"

He wasn't picky about food; he would eat anything edible.

As he walked from one end of the table to the other, stuffing his mouth, the nobles frowned.

"What's with that goblin?"

"I heard he's the Empress's pet."

"Pet? But he's carrying a weapon?"

Weapons were strictly prohibited except for the guards, so Kido's spear was particularly conspicuous.

"Burp!"

As he let out a loud belch, the noblewomen covered their noses and scattered.

"Even if I die, I should eat before I go."

Even for Kido, today's banquet was unusual.

Though he had no interest in politics, rumors suggested that Kashan would fall within seven days.

'Is this like a last supper?'

Since following Uorin into the capital, he had seen how much the Empress possessed.

It was no wonder she seemed to have lost her mind, knowing all of it could turn to dust.

"Her Majesty the Empress is entering!" At the guard's shout, all the nobles stopped what they were doing and turned towards the door.

Slowly, the door opened, and Uorin entered with a bright expression, wearing a black dress.

Adorned with diamonds and pearls, her beauty left the nobles in a daze.

"Wow..."

Even Kido, whose aesthetic sense was inferior to humans, was momentarily speechless.

'Beautiful.'

It was the first time he was captivated by a living form.

"Good evening, everyone."

Everyone focused on Uorin's words.

"You must be wondering why we're holding a grand party in the midst of a war."

Kido glanced at Gando beside Uorin but couldn't read his expression.

"To be blunt, the war situation is not good. And we might... lose the war."

It felt like a lump in his throat.

"But don't worry."

Uorin smiled beautifully.

"Kashan will never fall. I will use every means to protect the empire and continue its powerful history forever."

The name Teraje, which had never known failure since Kashan's founding, inspired confidence.

"Enjoy yourselves. We are showing the world that we are not afraid."

The nobles shouted in unison.

"Long live Her Majesty the Empress! Long live Her Majesty the Empress!"

The musicians quickly resumed playing, and Uorin approached the nobles, making eye contact with each one.

"How is it? Are you enjoying the party?"

"...What are you thinking?"

While boosting morale was important, Kido thought this war was hopeless.

"Well, what do you think?"

Kido frowned, thinking of the only way to prevent Kashan's destruction.

"Surely you don't mean..."

Uorin extended her hand.

"Shall we dance?"

Kido turned his head and snorted at her small, delicate hand.

"Hmph, a goblin dancing..."

"Come here."

As Uorin pulled Kido by the wrist into the ballroom, the nobles looked shocked.

"Is she really going to dance with a pet?"

Only Gando and Wind Guards knew Kido's true identity.

They thought the Empress had gone mad, but no one could diminish Uorin's beauty.

"I don't know how."

At Kido's grumbling, Uorin smiled and led him.

"It's easy. Just follow me and turn like this."

As Kido focused on the dance, he glanced at Uorin.

"Are you afraid of dying?" Uorin gave a sad smile.

"If the army of hell captures Kashan, Habitz will come for me first."

Even Uorin, with Teraje's blood, couldn't imagine the disaster he would bring.

"Even if my long life ends in death, I can accept it. But I still have a reason to live."

"Shirone?"

Uorin didn't answer this time either.

"I'm not saying I'll give up Kashan for free. There will be negotiations. Even if it means the fall of Teraje, Kashan will not disappear."

"Why not fight?"

Kido found himself feeling pity.

"I'll protect you. No one will touch you, so don't be afraid and fight."

Uorin shook her head.

"We can't win. Only Shirone can do it. But Shirone won't fight for Kashan."

'Only Shirone can do it.'

Uorin's words left an ache in his heart.

Tormia Kingdom Magic Association

Association President Lupist placed the urgent document on his desk and massaged his temples.

'The Holy War won't be able to hold them off.' The news was that the army of hell had suddenly bypassed and was heading towards the central continent.

'They can't break through to Tormia.' As he was thinking this, there was a knock on the door.

"President, the candidate for the next chief secretary is here."

Since Jane's death, the chief secretary position had been vacant, but with the war breaking out, he needed someone to assist him.

"Let them in."

The door opened, and a woman with a stern expression and blue hair down to her waist entered.

She was Lavide Plu, a certified 6th-level mage.

"Hello. I'm Plu, the head of security on the third floor."

Lupist had already reviewed all the details about Plu through her profile.

Her magical skills and judgment were excellent, making her a suitable candidate for chief secretary.

However, there was one thing he needed to address.

"You've heard that Gaold is active, right?"

"Yes, I have."

She had once fought alongside Gaold in the Gaold Guard against Heaven.

"Between the Association and Zion, what is your priority?"

Plu paused to think.

Such an attitude might hinder her rapid promotion, but she was unbothered.

"I still respect Mr. Gaold."

Lupist's expression remained unchanged.

"But as always, my priority is the well-being of the Tormia Kingdom. Personal feelings have no place in this."

'She resembles her.'

Lupist thought of Jane, who had passed away.

If possible, he wanted to keep the chief secretary position vacant while he was president.

'There's no one as capable as you.' In a world heading towards destruction, her name was even more missed.

"How's your sleep?"

At the unexpected question, Plu blinked and adjusted her glasses.

"Well, I haven't heard any complaints."

Lupist chuckled.

'Yes, after all, we are all...'

Parts of the kingdom.

"Follow me. There's somewhere we need to go."

As it was already dark, Plu asked with a puzzled expression.

"Where are we going?"

Lupist replied briefly.

"The flower garden."

Grand Hall

"Are you alright?"

The Grand Star had been disoriented for a while due to the shockwave from Gaold in the south.

The Grand Star, the incarnation of the stars.

Receiving a force exceeding the planet's durability was like being directly hit by magic.

"I'm fine."

The Grand Star raised a hand to calm the stars.

"Currently, the south relies on a team of just three led by Gaold. The power of breaking the System is immense, but they can't cover all areas alone." The screen at the front changed, showing Habitz's army again.

"Gaia's ability can observe most events on the planet. This will certainly help in the war. But first, we need to clarify something."

The Grand Star finally got to the point.

"Do you all agree to erase the existence of demons from this world?" There was no immediate answer.

"Demons are beings created by human emotions."

Amanta, the Great Star of the Balance Department, spoke.

"There's no reason to separate humans and demons. To exaggerate a bit, erasing demons is like erasing humans."

Minerva spoke.

"But if we leave them be, demons will take over the world. Our top priority should be the war against demons. We must mobilize all the Ivory Tower's capabilities..."

"Heh, nonsense."

At the loud voice, Minerva turned her head.

A man in golden armor sat with his arms crossed.

It was Sigmund Freed, the Great Star of the System Inspection Department.

Known as the King of Greed, his armor was the unique set of the Five Divine Generals.

It was the most advanced magical equipment, boasting an incredible 65% anti-magic capability.

"Nonsense?"

Minerva's eyes filled with killing intent.

"That's right. No matter how evil Habitz is, can he compare to you in your prime?"

It was Minerva's sore spot.

"What do you know about me, kid? Do you want to die?"

"I know one thing for sure. Why you cling to Yahweh."

Freed pointed at Shirone and sneered.

"Only that kid can save you. You want forgiveness, right? So what did you give in return? Come on, kid. Was it as hot as the rumors say?"

As Minerva grabbed her staff, Shirone sighed softly.

"Haa."

He slowly raised his head and spoke.

"Annoying."

Just that one word sent a chill through the other stars.

'He's the Great Star of the Integrated Universe Management Department.'

They had heard he reached the level of Yahweh, but it was different from what they imagined.

'What is this uneasy feeling?'

Like a ticking time bomb, his lower abdomen churned.

But as a fellow Great Star, Freed wasn't shaken.

"Heh, the symbol of philanthropy has a foul mouth. What will you do if you're annoyed? Fight me here..."

Before he could finish, Shirone stood up and threw a photon cannon.

"Hup!"

Freed leaped back, but Shirone instantly teleported to catch up.

'Is he serious?'

Freed was the only star in the Ivory Tower who wielded both sword and magic, but he couldn't react to Shirone's speed.

'Even so...!'

The anti-magic effect of the Five Divine Generals' armor deflected the photon cannon at a considerable distance.

'That's how it is!'

As Shirone closed in again, he cast another photon cannon.

As the light sphere vibrated through the 65% anti-magic, Freed's eyes widened.

'How?'

At this distance, it was unavoidable.

As soon as the thought ended, the photon cannon struck Freed's abdomen directly.

"Ugh!"

As the pain of his abdominal muscles tearing surged, the shock disappeared like a lie.

"Huh?"

As Freed looked around, all the stars were in the same position.

'He bounced time.' As if nothing had happened, Shirone stood in his original spot, glaring at Freed.
