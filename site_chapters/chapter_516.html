Chapter 516: A New Journey (2)

Heaven, Third Heaven, Shehakim.

The aftermath of God's punishment striking Zion caused the residences of the immortals in Paradise to collapse in the direction of the seismic waves.

However, amidst the ruins, one building remained intact: the Grand Hall of the Judicial Institution of the Leaders, located north of the city square.

Currently, in the underground part of the building, known as the "Chamber," the top ten leaders of the immortals were gathered.

These leaders, known as the "Council of Ten" among the immortals, had all reached the art of incarnation after living for countless years.

Murca, Deina, and Raun, whom Shirone faced during the war, were masters of incarnation who had lived for over a thousand years but fell into ruin due to their attachment to life.

In contrast, the leaders of the Council of Ten had enjoyed immortality for at least 10,000 years and had transcended even their attachment to life and death.

"The Garas were annihilated by the giants."

The bald old man, the only one sitting at the head of the rectangular table, spoke.

His eyes were slick and black, and his throat puffed up like a toad's when he spoke.

Council of Ten Chairman Beron (age unknown) - Amita Prajna.

The man sitting to Beron's left spoke.

"A failure of selective evolution. The angels have all left."

His face, with its sculpted beauty and blond hair swept back, was pale as a corpse, and sharp fangs were visible inside his mouth as he spoke.

Council of Ten Rank 3 Faust (estimated age 13,000 years) - White Ghost Yaksha.

The man sitting opposite Faust spoke.

"The Garas don't matter. The important thing is that Anke Ra has been erased. From now on, we must rule humanity."

He had a solid build with reddish, round bumps on his head, and his triangular teeth were tightly clenched in a combative manner.

Council of Ten Rank 4 Huama (estimated age 17,000 years) - Diamond Yaksha.

The woman sitting opposite Huama spoke.

"The light of Zone 73, Shirone, right? It seems he fought against immortals during the war."

Her hair was as black as pitch, blending into the darkness, and her smooth, snake-like body seemed to dissolve the boundaries of her form, consisting only of curves.

Her silhouette was beautiful, but thinking of it as a human body gave it a grotesque feeling.

Council of Ten Rank 7 Shura (estimated age 12,000 years) - Apostle Prajna.

"Hmph! Even if they were immortals, they were still babies who hadn't even weaned. There's no need to worry about anyone except Miro. Surely, Shura, you're not scared too?"

Shura retorted to Huama's taunt.

"Who's scared? I'm just curious about this human named Shirone."

Then she imagined Shirone and licked her entire body with her snake-like long tongue.

"Looks delicious, slurp."

Faust, who had watched her tongue quickly retract into her mouth, added.

"It's not something to overlook. He annihilated Archangel Faiel, and the flash seen just before Anke Ra's erasure was his doing. And most importantly…"

Huama slammed the table in frustration.

"That's because!"

"He's the Ultima System."

"Ugh!"

Huama turned his head in frustration.

Even the immortals couldn't refute Shirone, who possessed the spirit of the Gaia people, their predecessors.

The woman sitting at the end of the table muttered.

"A human who killed a god…"

Her face had a fierce, wolf-like impression but also a strangely delicate feeling.

Her black hair was as thick as a wild horse's mane, and she wore a thick, tattered turban that stuck out like flames.

She wore ragged clothes as worn as her turban, with loose ties revealing her bare chest.

She leaned her head on the hilt of a great sword, one leg up on the chair, revealing the deep red of her inner thigh.

Council of Ten Rank 9 Baknyeo (estimated age 11,200 years) - Asura Yaksha.

After a long silence, Beron spoke.

"We can't say he killed him. After all, Ra erased himself by his own will. But it's true that the boy was a major variable in this war."

Faust asked.

"Do you intend to recruit him into the Council of Ten?"

Shura said.

"That's a bit hasty. Shirone's avatar is currently inactive, so we can't even hear his opinion. I wouldn't stop you if you went to the Under Coder."

"Anyway, you just want to eat the kid."

Huama, who had snapped at Shura, gave his opinion.

"With Ra erased, there's no one to guard against. If we have to pick, it's only Miro among the angels and humans. Ultima or not, strength is everything."

Though they had the power to rule the world with their strong incarnation skills, they had to live under strict control until now.

They couldn't oppose Anke Ra, who could erase information from the Akashic Records.

But now, humans had gained freedom, and the shackles on the immortals were completely removed.

Faust spoke.

"It's time to decide. Is the only existing Ultima System, the light of Zone 73, Shirone, an enemy or an ally of the Council of Ten?"

As the silence grew longer, Beron suggested.

"Let's decide by majority vote. After all, we are human. Now, those who consider Shirone an enemy, raise your hands."

After a moment, the ten people in the chamber expressed their thoughts by raising their hands.

As Beron said, the vote was very human.

A black sphere appeared on a snowy mountain at an altitude of 7,000 meters.

Soon, Miro and her group began to emerge one by one.

"It's cold."

Plu shivered and looked around.

They were at the summit of a mountain range with thin oxygen, and all they could see was a horizontal snowstorm.

"We could summon a salamander to endure it, but that would cause an avalanche."

Zulu summoned a 3-tier monster, Kaidra.

The group quickly left the mountain range on the back of the giant bird, which could fly 7,000 kilometers a day.

They arrived at a village located at an altitude of 600 meters.

The gloomy landscape was covered with snow clouds, the stone-paved roads were wet, and the people's clothing was unfamiliar.

When the giant bird suddenly landed in the small village, all the residents stopped what they were doing and turned to look.

Miro approached an old man with deep wrinkles.

"Excuse me, where are we?"

With Sain's mental magic translating, the old man blinked and pointed to the mountain.

"Escatu."

Considering it was his first encounter with magic, his reaction was calm, but Miro understood where they were.

"Ah, Escatu."

It meant the center of the world, the name of the mountain range in the northeast of the continent.

"At least we've entered the continent. So, what should we do? Since we've met like this, how about a small gathering?"

When no one responded to her joke, Miro licked her lips and got to the point.

"Let's part ways here. Be careful, as there might be pursuers. Does everyone have a place to go?"

Kangnan said she would go to Zulu's hideout with Gaold, and Shina and Etella planned to return to the magic school.

Kuan kept his residence a secret, and Armin, having left the Ivory Tower, was free to go anywhere.

The problem was Plu.

The Magic Association she had to return to was already under the control of the new chairman, Lupist.

"Will you be okay? Why not come with me?"

Plu shook her head at Kangnan's suggestion.

"No. I'll return to the association first. I'm worried about Lady Isabel."

"What if they question you about what happened in heaven?"

"If they want something, I'll have to tell them."

Everyone's eyes focused on Plu.

"Since Cage B Team has already gone, I don't know how much they'll get from me, but I'll provide information if necessary. I'm also a certified magician of the Kingdom of Tormia."

Hearing this, Kangnan nodded.

"That's like you."

She wasn't included in the project just because she followed Gaold.

Knowing she was a professional who prioritized her mission in any situation, they could trust her to assist Shirone.

"Still, be careful. Lupist won't easily reinstate you."

"It's something I have to endure. But can they afford not to use a talent like me?"

Kangnan laughed at Plu's jest.

Believing in what you think is right.

If a professional magician thought that way, no one could stop her.

"Take good care of Mr. Gaold. I'll be working for the association from now on, but…"

Plu winked with one eye.

"Once a comrade, always a comrade."

"Yes, once a comrade, always a comrade."

Understanding the deeper meaning in Plu's words, Kangnan signaled Zulu that it was time to leave.

"Anyone going to the city can ride. I'll take you."

"Oh, in that case, we'll…"

Only Shina and Etella decided to accompany them to the city.

The remaining people weren't particularly busy and weren't weak enough to struggle in rough terrain.

Armin looked up at Shina and spoke.

"Shina, you'll be a great teacher."

"Brother."

Shina understood Armin's words.

She had felt humiliated when used as bait to draw the Eternal Contemplator into the project, but now she knew.

Why Gaold wanted Etella more than her.

"Not everyone needs to fight. Not everyone needs to be strong. Go back to school and do what you want. Become a great teacher."

For the first time, Shina showed a gentle expression.

"Yes. You should be happy too, brother."

Those words made Armin feel a bit sad, but he also smiled.

His beloved sister had finally overcome her past trauma and was starting a new life.

"Then, see you next time."

The giant bird Kaidra soared into the sky, and Armin also disappeared with a flicker spell after bowing to Kuan.

One by one, they each went their separate ways, and finally, Arius, carrying Shirone, approached Miro.

"Miss Miro, what should we do?"

Miro looked around the vast world spread out before her.

"Well, where should we start?"

The world she returned to after 20 years was full of unknowns, but to her, all those unknowns were just a playground.

Uorin, having finished the history search, opened her eyes wide.

"I found it!"

After a long journey through the entire history, she had found a crack different from the base event.

Gando asked urgently.

"Did you find it?"

"He was born in the Middle East. Age between 15 and 30. Male. His name is Akaze, but he could have changed it if he left his hometown."

When Uorin called for the Wind Guards, the door burst open, and 30 people wearing owl masks were sucked in like smoke.

"You called for us."

"Use the best intelligence to find him. He was born in a tribe in the Akkad Desert. His family died in the Oasis War, and he is one of the survivors."

That was all the information Uorin provided.

It was a task that required searching the entire world with just an approximate age, gender, and simple information, but it was worth it.

"Understood."

The Wind Guards left silently, and Uorin paced the room, biting her nails.

Ra had descended to earth.

His purpose was singular: to destroy humanity, which was building chaos.

"Do you really mean Ra was born on this earth?"

"Yes. We must find him before he begins to act. If we fail to do so…"

Gando could imagine the words Uorin couldn't bring herself to say.

It was the reincarnation of the Akashic Records.

When he finished his judgment of this world, the event would approach humanity with an unprecedented shockwave.

"A great disaster has come."

"It doesn't matter. Even if he's a god, he can't challenge Terraje's stronghold. We must be fully prepared."

Finally, Uorin stopped and looked at Gando.

"A new era has begun, Gando."

Following the Goffin Erasure and the Anke Ra Erasure, this was the third and final reset.

It was called the Great Purification.
