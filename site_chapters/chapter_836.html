Chapter 836: Ivory Tower Grand Assembly (4)

When they reached the top floor of the Ivory Tower, Araka from the Internal Affairs Department was preparing for spatial transfer.

"Shirone."

Minerva from the Human Safety Enforcement Department greeted Shirone and his group.

"Minerva."

Though she was usually detached from worldly affairs, she was fully dressed in her witch's attire and holding a staff, looking quite serious.

Luber approached with the stars.

"We meet again." She gave a slight bow and took in the stars of the Integrated Universe Management Department one by one.

While she recognized most of them, Kira was unfamiliar to Minerva.

'So that's Kira.'

Kira the Corpse Collector.

An undead mage once called the second Minerva, a catastrophic figure.

"Hello! Nice to meet you! I'm Kira!"

Despite her considerable experience in the Ivory Tower, she still maintained a strict demeanor.

It was rather unsettling.

"Yes. Let's get along well." It was just a greeting, but given the upcoming Grand Assembly, it was a statement with a clear purpose.

Mini rested her chin on her hand.

'Well, Minerva was also involved in the project, so she can't back out. We're in this together.'

Shirone observed the members of the Human Safety Enforcement Department.

Floating in the air was Saint Brain, with several men of fierce appearance behind it.

"Who are they?"

Minerva turned her head.

"Oh, let me introduce them to you. These are the stars of the Human Safety Enforcement Department. This is..." Before she could finish, a man with a face full of scars approached.

It was Laska, a 3-star resident of the Ivory Tower, known as the Butcher, a metal mage.

"Hehe, sis. Is this the kid you wanted to personally deflower?"

Minerva shrugged at Shirone's glare.

"Of course, I'm joking."

Laska approached Shirone.

"Listen, kid. I don't care if you're a Great Star or whatever. If you lay a finger on my sister..."

As soon as he met Shirone's gaze, Laska felt a chilling sensation and abruptly stopped.

'What is this?'

It wasn't killing intent.

In fact, Shirone's innocent, blinking eyes showed no hostility.

'I'm afraid?'

He could withstand Minerva's deadly aura to some extent.

'What is it?'

The unknown only heightened his fear.

"Tch!"

Laska stepped back, forgetting the prestige of the stars, and Luber nodded slightly.

'He has transcendent senses.'

Even Monga had confirmed the madness of Shirone's benevolence in the REM realm, and Laska sensed it just from his gaze.

'As expected of a 3-star.'

The Human Safety Enforcement Department dealt with disasters and crimes in the human world.

Ironically, except for Saint Brain, they were former villains who had once terrorized the world.

Minerva smacked the back of Laska's head.

"Ouch! Why did you hit me, sis?"

"Why did you pick a fight you couldn't win and embarrass me?"

"I just wanted to see what he was like since you found him interesting."

Minerva sighed but also sensed Shirone's change.

'Yes. You're going mad too, Shirone.'

It's true that this world can't be endured with a sane mind.

"Sorry about that. Our department is full of troublemakers."

"It's fine."

Shirone spoke briefly.

He didn't specify what was fine, but it didn't matter.

Minerva introduced the stars and satellites of the Human Safety Enforcement Department one by one.

There were no 2-stars, but besides Laska, there was another 3-star and several 1-star residents.

"Five in total. We have 16 stars. The second most among the five departments. Combined with the Integrated Universe Management Department, we have 28. We can put up a good fight."

"What do you mean by fight?"

"As you know, the roles of the five departments are different. We handle disasters and crimes, the Balance Department ensures world stability, the System Inspection Department oversees major nations, and the Law Department governs the laws."

Minerva raised a finger.

"For example, if a powerful demon descends, the Human Safety Enforcement Department and the Law Department get involved. If the descent happens in a country, the System Inspection Department also gets involved. The problem is that each department has a different perspective on solving issues. That's where your department, the Integrated Universe Management Department, comes in."

It was no coincidence that the Five Great Stars of the Integrated Universe Management Department had always supported the Grand Star.

"But now the situation has changed. Honestly... we were defeated miserably. We don't know how the other departments will react. In such times, the number of stars can be a criterion for the Grand Star's judgment."

"Hmm, judgment."

"Like the last Grand Assembly, the scope of this one is the entire world. We'll discuss Habitz, Zion's situation, and countermeasures against Nane."

Shirone's head shot up.

"Have you found Nane?"

"No. Even with the Grand Star's Gaia ability, we can't locate Nane. The false god, Shura, is with him."

Shura, ranked 7th in the Ten Lords, could easily avoid the world's attention with her gestalt.

Deep in the mountains of the central continent.

In a pristine natural setting devoid of human presence, a man was tilling the fields.

It was unusual to see someone with tattoos covering their entire body, including their face, working with a pickaxe.

"Master."

At the distant call, Nane straightened his back.

"Eat this before you continue."

Shura, wearing tattered rags and a towel on her head, carried a tray on her head as she walked over.

"You work hard."

Though they had been hiding in the mountains, away from human society, for quite some time, Nane's level had not improved.

"If you feel sorry for making me work, achieve enlightenment quickly. The outside world is in chaos."

Nane gulped down the water and turned his gaze.

"The outside?"

"A man named Habitz has become extreme evil. The army of hell is devastating the kingdom."

"I see."

Nane stared at the horizon, lost in thought.

"When do you think you'll become a Buddha?"

"If I want to, I can do it right now."

Shura's face lit up.

"Have you finally realized the secrets of the world? Then teach me! Quickly!"

Shura was impatient, but Nane just smiled enigmatically.

"There's something I need to check first."

Nane took out a seed from his pocket.

"What's that?"

"I don't know. I collected them one by one during my walks. If I plant this, it will sprout."

Nane planted the seed and covered it with soil.

"I don't know what will sprout, but let's start here. This is the beginning."

Shura pondered Nane's words and asked.

"Planting seeds?"

"No."

Nane looked at the ground with warm eyes.

"It's the intention to plant."

The intention to plant seeds.

'This is good for now. So let's watch, Shirone. What this world is...'

It wasn't time to define it yet.

"It's spring."

Shura followed Nane's gaze to the sky.

In the Grand Hall, 27 stars and their satellites, along with Araka from the Internal Affairs Department and the record keeper, were present.

Sitting in a fan shape around the Grand Star, their eyes burned with determination as if they were in enemy territory.

"Everyone is here."

As the Grand Star spoke, all the stars bowed their heads in unison.

"Greetings, Grand Star."

Araka swallowed hard at the sight of these accomplished individuals speaking in one voice.

'It's truly incredible.'

The potential of what they could achieve if they moved with a single will was unimaginable.

'The problem is that it's impossible.'

That's why the Grand Assembly was convened.

"As the only official gathering in the Ivory Tower, I will also show respect. Before hearing your views on the current situation, let's briefly review the state of the world."

Activating Gaia's ability, a high-altitude view of the earth appeared at the front of the Grand Hall.

Araka held his head.

"My goodness..."

From a height where even the largest objects were invisible, the path of the marching army of hell was clearly visible.

"An army of about 50 million is advancing towards Kashan and the central continent. What do you think?"

The stars remained silent, their faces calm.

Amanta from the Balance Department spoke.

"Exterminating the demons is..."

"Wait. Sorry, Amanta." The Grand Star interrupted.

"Before discussing extreme evil, there's something you need to know. Currently, there are three humans who have reached the level of breaking the System. Of course, we have one as well..."

No one turned their gaze, but Shirone felt as if his body was being pierced.

"The first person to examine is this one."

As the scene shifted rapidly, the southern continent came into view.

Zooming in, Shirone saw familiar faces battling the army of hell.

'Gaold.'

"Kill them! They're just humans!"

The number of demons surrounding the altar seemed to be in the hundreds of thousands.

"Disgusting, really!"

Following Gaold, Kangnan gritted her teeth and soared into the sky.

Despite her destructive combat skills, the sheer number of demons was overwhelming.

"Kangnan, I'll handle this."

Black waves swirled in Zulu's eyes as the lich soared high into the sky.

Lightning struck within a radius of hundreds of meters, and the demons' screams pierced the sky.

"Kreee!"

After passing through the exploding demon corpses, they reached the altar at the center of the spiritual realm.

"You've come."

The 11th Legion Commander, Pisarochi, known as the ruler of hellfire, was a powerful mage.

"No need for the commander to step in!" The ones protecting Pisarochi were all at least brigade-level, incomparable to any previous foes.

"Ugh!"

As Gaold glared at Pisarochi, his hair began to turn white.

"Die...!"

With a thud, the ground shook as the brigade commander's body was flattened like liquid.

Thud! Thud! Thud!

Like invisible iron stakes falling, presses struck from all directions, creating holes in the enemy formations.

"Aaah!"

As Gaold's ultimate technique, the Great Inferno Hell, unfolded, the surroundings transformed into a fiery hellscape.

"Kahaha! This place belongs to us anyway..."

Another brigade commander was crushed.

'What are you trying to do?'

Watching Gaold's nerves twitching in pain, Kangnan bit her lip.

'You can't hold out forever.'

Gaold didn't care.

'Miro.'

He didn't know how long the war would last, but to him, it was just a fleeting moment.

'I'll end it.'

Boom! Boom! Boom!

Hundreds of presses pounded from all sides, clearing the view like a curtain being drawn.

"Pathetic fools!"

Finally, Pisarochi gathered hellfire in both hands and flew into the sky.

"Ah..."

At the same time, Gaold's body flinched.

'Again...'

Zulu, who had been charging, grabbed Kangnan and teleported in the opposite direction of Gaold.

"We must avoid it."

Kangnan was worried about Gaold but had no choice but to let Zulu take her away.

"Huuu!" Pisarochi hurled a massive fireball.

"This ends now!"

Gaold, with tears of blood streaming down his face, looked up.

'It's coming.'

An unstoppable force.

'Damn it!'

At the edge of his consciousness, a smiling Miro's face appeared.

'Miro.'

Air Press.

Pisarochi's body, the fireball he threw, and the countless demons surrounding them all vanished in an instant.

'Get lost.'

The power of breaking the System crushed the ground, creating a deep crater and sending up a surge of heat.

Boom!

As the explosion that blew away several kilometers occurred, the Grand Star in the Grand Hall staggered.

The 26 stars and satellites watched the scene in silence.
