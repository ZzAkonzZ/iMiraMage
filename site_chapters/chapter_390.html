Chapter 390: Combat Readiness (3)

"That's incredible, Fermi! How much did you compress the air to reach 1,000 press?"

"Yeah, even if it hits armor, the impact would be significant."

Shirone was unimpressed. In the past, he would have at least clapped, but it didn't compare to Gaold's air gun.

Fermi steadily conquered the master difficulty. When he finished the 10th level of the movement category, performing acrobatic flying, applause erupted.

The level of skill required to spiral up like a screw, draw a large circle, and then freely glide through the air like a pinwheel was impossible without mastering the basics of both press and blow categories.

"Fermi, all master. 100 points."

It was the moment Fermi reclaimed the top spot in the graduating class.

"He's certainly impressive."

It was rare for Iruki to acknowledge someone.

This time, even Shirone had no grounds to argue. It was an all-master achievement.

"Indeed. His fundamentals seem very solid."

"No, that's not what I mean. Fermi was an all-master last year too. Just in a different major."

Shirone quickly turned his head.

"A different major?"

"Yes. He registered for earth magic. The year before, he was an all-master in fire magic."

Could it really be possible? Changing majors every year and mastering all of them?

For a high-level magician, student-level difficulties might be manageable, but it was an unfair comparison. Fermi was a student.

Shirone stared blankly at Fermi. When Fermi made a throat-slitting gesture with his thumb, the emotions from the survival evaluation resurfaced.

Fermi had achieved all-master and became the undisputed number one.

It was an event significant enough to tip the scales for students who had been weighing Shirone and Fermi.

'It's too late to regret, rookie. I won't let you graduate until you lick my shoes.'

Reading the anger in Fermi's eyes, Shirone stood up and spoke to his friends as he headed to the training ground.

"Iruki, Nade. I'll go up first."

Nade and Iruki looked at each other. They knew Shirone wasn't yet at the level to challenge for all-master.

"What is he thinking? A head-on confrontation won't benefit him. It'll just expose his abilities. If he fails, he'll be completely crushed."

"He must have a plan. Let's trust Shirone."

The students' attention focused on Shirone.

Amy and Arin turned their heads from their conversation, and Canis's eyes sharpened.

Dante, sitting on the ground, propped his chin and watched.

'Fermi achieved all-master. How will you respond?'

The evaluator held the evaluation sheet and asked.

"Shirone, your major is Divine Particle. What are your challenge tasks?"

"Power level 7, projectile speed level 8, firing speed level 7, defense level 7..."

Shirone listed the tasks one after another, just like Fermi.

Up to week 7, Shirone's average special skill evaluation level was 3.6. The levels he was now reciting were more than double that.

"Lastly, movement control, level 10."

The evaluator's eyes sparkled.

Level 10 was master difficulty. Moreover, the movement control category for Divine Particle had much higher standards than the photon category.

It was clear that Fermi's all-master had influenced Shirone's decision.

However, it was also true that this was insufficient to compete with Fermi.

In fact, Shirone had chosen only seven tasks, and except for movement, the difficulty levels were between 7 and 8.

"What is this? If you're going to do it, do it properly. This is half-hearted."

"He's being stubborn. He needs to follow to some extent so that other students will be conflicted between Fermi and Shirone."

"If that's the case, it's a huge mistake. This won't even scratch Fermi. It's a typical first-year mistake. Freshmen can't help it."

This was the opinion of most of Class Two.

Dante withheld judgment and fell into thought. His lips curled as he quickly blinked and calculated.

"Hmm, that method..."

Meanwhile, Fermi's group watching from the hill already had faces of assured victory.

"Heh, is this his last struggle? It's kind of cute, isn't it, Fermi?"

Electric monster Lycan looked at Fermi.

Fermi was smiling. But Lycan, who had spent years with him, knew it was the smile he showed when he was most furious.

'That damn rookie...'

Shirone's first evaluation was the single attribute power category.

This category didn't exist in the photon series but was newly established based on the mass of the Divine Particle.

As he closed his eyes and concentrated, a sphere of light was born, compressing at a terrifying speed as if pushing space away.

As the suppressed vibrations turned the students' eyes golden, the photon cannon fired with a roar.

The flash embedded in the glass orb spread into fragments of light, and the gauge spun to calculate the power.

2,242 crash.

It far exceeded the 1,800 crash standard for level 7 single attribute power.

When Shirone checked the score and looked at Fermi, the students finally realized something and began to murmur.

When converted to energy units, 2,242 crash was roughly equivalent to 8,200 press.

In other words, the destructive power of compressing the atmosphere with 8,200 press was 2,240 crash.

This was 7.3 times higher than Fermi's 1,123 press, showcasing the power of the Divine Particle.

Shirone's evaluation continued.

In the projectile speed category, the photon cannon's speed was an impressive 100 meters per second, far surpassing Fermi's projectile speed.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

Shirone fired the photon cannon at the target ahead at a tremendous speed.

Even in broad daylight, the surroundings flashed, and the students' vision was filled with golden streaks.

"165 rounds per minute. Level 7 passed."

Fermi's master level for firing speed was 160 rounds per minute.

Having already surpassed Fermi in three categories, the students now grasped Shirone's intention.

"I see. That guy Shirone..."

The seven tasks Shirone presented as challenge tasks were also common to the air category.

"He's surpassing all of Fermi's evaluation tasks."

When the students turned to Fermi, he was watching Shirone with his face covered by his palm.

Only his close companions knew that his eyes were burning with murderous intent.

Shirone didn't achieve all-master.

But even so, he scored higher in the same categories than Fermi, who did achieve all-master.

The students felt the same. Air magic could be overwhelmed by the Divine Particle at level 7.

"That damn bastard..."

Fermi's companions were startled. It was the first time his emotions were so blatantly exposed.

Feeling the gazes of many students, Fermi finally lowered his hand and smiled.

'Oh, right, the business smile. The business smile.'

A businessman must remain composed. Even if debt is choking them and a knife is about to stab their back, confidence can open a way out.

Of course, the crisis was real. In reality, students were halving their expectations of Fermi's success and shifting them to Shirone.

The air category was at the level of the Divine Particle at level 7. The strategy of raising his stock through comparison with Fermi's scores worked perfectly.

"Shirone is truly amazing."

Aider approached from behind Iruki. His major was also in the air category, so it was natural to feel bitter.

"His talent is different. How can we beat someone like that?"

"Talent?"

"Yes. The advantage of the photon category is that it has no mass. But Shirone can even add mass to it. No one can beat that."

Iruki acknowledged Shirone's magical talent. But he didn't think Shirone had come this far just because of that.

Talent is just a tool. The speed of digging the ground with bare hands versus with a shovel is different.

If the ground is magic, the shovel is talent.

But no matter how much you have a shovel, the ground won't be dug unless you use your arms, waist, and legs.

Therefore, even a moment of realization is just the accumulation of daily training exploding.

Shirone dedicated himself every day and broke through his limits daily. Even if progress was slow, it eventually accumulated to achieve results.

All success in the world starts from knowing that the future will surely come.

"I don't think anyone is born empty-handed."

"What?"

Aider looked down at Iruki.

"Some are born with shovels, some with hammers, some with pens. Those with shovels can dig the ground faster, but they can't write well with them. Talent isn't given; it's found and developed by oneself. The air category has something only it can do. Just because you haven't found it doesn't mean you should take lightly what others have found. Talent is also effort."

Aider pondered those words. Although he had spoken out of displeasure at Shirone's efforts being belittled, it was a piece of advice more valuable than gold to him.

"Yes. Thank you, Iruki."

Iruki raised and lowered his eyebrows. His lack of offense and rather grateful attitude was indeed fitting for the youngest. It was why Aider had no enemies.

"Lastly, movement control. Master difficulty. Reaction speed must be below 0.8 seconds."

Shirone took a deep breath and composed himself. Although he felt dizzy after exerting himself in six categories, it was nothing compared to his past practical experiences.

The evaluator activated the Red Line certified evaluation magic, Navigation.

A small sphere was born, and inside it, an arrow indicating direction spun like a compass that had lost its polarity.

Movement control is a category where the magician must accurately execute the movements suggested by the navigation.

The shape, length, and color of the arrow each represent direction, distance, and speed, and the magician must perform them within the reaction time.

The master difficulty for Divine Particle is a reaction time of 0.8 seconds. This is 0.2 seconds shorter than the master difficulty for the photon series, which is 1 second.

At the neural level, 0.2 seconds is a significant gap.

Nevertheless, the association set this standard because they believed rapid acceleration and deceleration using mass would be possible.

"Reaction time of 0.8 seconds?"

The students looked bewildered.

Photon series magicians, of course, differentiate time to pre-construct a series of processes. Applications like Patrol and Rainbow Drop come from this.

However, movement control is not a category where you move as you think.

Navigation will suggest directions, speeds, and distances that are completely unexpected, and the magician must react immediately.

In such a situation, demanding a reaction time of 0.8 seconds seemed too harsh.

'No, the association's judgment is correct.'

Shirone recalled Collie's teachings. The standard exists because someone is achieving it.

He had heard that Etella's reaction time for movement control, called superhuman, was 0.1 seconds.

At that level, one could traverse densely wooded areas with just teleportation. It was also said that one could dodge attacks with just upper body weaving by repeatedly teleporting in place.

Of course, as a student, Shirone couldn't take comfort in what a renowned monk on the continent could do.

But he understood the association's thinking correctly.

Do not judge Divine Particle by the standards of the photon series.

It was a unique ability that only Shirone possessed.

'I will conquer it with my skills.'

Light rose from Shirone's body like flames. The flames, growing in size, became a pair of giant wings that burned brightly.

"What, what is that?"

The students' eyes widened. It was magic they had never seen or even heard of before.
