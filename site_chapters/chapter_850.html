Chapter 850: Acceleration (1)

Boom! Boom!

A massive creature made of black rock was walking through the mountains, shaking the ground.

Salsu, equipped with the Law Slayer as its engine.

Salsu, which could change its form with Material, had a sleek, glass-like surface and moved on four legs.

Its face was round, and Shirone and Rian were riding on its back, which curved down from its neck.

“Eek!” The city guards of a nearby town quickly halted their horses and moved aside as the 5-meter-tall Salsu passed by.

“What, what is that?”

As guards, they should have stopped it, but the sight of moving metal made them hesitate to approach.

“Stop! Stop!”

When Salsu stopped, the guard captain approached.

Even on horseback, Shirone's face suddenly appeared from a higher vantage point.

“Hello.”

“A human?”

Relieved that it wasn't a demon, the guard captain asked,

“Who are you?”

Shirone handed over a pass stamped with Kashan's seal.

“I’m a mage from the Ivory Tower. We’re trying to use the teleportation magic circle in Tashian City.”

It was the fastest route.

After verifying the document handwritten by Uorin, the guard captain immediately saluted.

“Oh, I’m sorry. We’re in a state of emergency.”

“It’s okay. What’s the damage like?”

The guard captain's face darkened.

“All the cities have already been destroyed. You probably won’t be able to use the teleportation magic circle.”

Shirone thought that might be the case.

“I see. Thank you for letting me know.”

“I’m sorry I couldn’t be of more help.”

Shirone shook his head as he observed the tired faces of the guards.

“You saved us a lot of time. Well then…”

As the Law Slayer activated, Salsu began to move along the mountain path at a terrifying speed.

“That’s a star of the Ivory Tower.”

The guards, who had heard reports that the Ivory Tower had prevented the fall of the capital, expressed their admiration.

“Yeah. They’re different from us.”

Even in a brief encounter, it wasn’t hard to read the determination in their eyes, which seemed to shine with resolve.

“Why do they fight?”

Having already seen their hometowns destroyed, the guards sensed the end of the world.

“If it were me, I would have taken my family and fled. They have enough power to do so.”

“I don’t know.”

The guard captain felt the same.

“How could we understand?”

Lying on Salsu’s back, lost in thought, Rian raised his head and asked Shirone,

“We’re going to the Ivory Tower, right?”

“Yeah. We need to check the world situation. If another disaster like Argantis appears somewhere…”

“The army of hell is heading to the central continent.”

The meaning was clear.

“Amy is there too.”

“We defeated Amon, but an army of 40 million is still a significant threat. If possible, why don’t we go?”

“I want to.”

It was his honest feeling.

“I want to see Amy right now.”

“Then go see her. No matter what happens, I’ll protect her. And your family too.”

“If I were a weak soldier, I wouldn’t even think about it. They fight just to survive. To protect their families.”

“You can do that too.”

“If we do that, we can’t win the war.”

That was the problem.

“If a commander leads an army based on personal feelings, it’s annihilation. Especially now, when humanity’s survival is at stake. If we act on our emotions, everyone will die.”

He understood it in his head, but…

“I don’t want you to have regrets.”

Shirone smiled.

“Is it Uorin’s words that are bothering you?”

Rian clicked his tongue.

“That you can’t beat Nane? Forget that nonsense. If he shows up again, I’ll split him in two myself.”

“It won’t be easy.”

Rian’s face turned serious at the unexpected answer.

“What do you think?”

“My thoughts…”

Shirone recalled his conversation with Uorin.

“You think I can’t beat Nane?”

“Yeah. You know it, Shirone.” Uorin had said.

“Nane is almost always right, and evil is almost always the majority. In terms of quality and quantity, good and love can’t compete.”

It was an impossible fight.

“What good and love have achieved is barely preventing the world from ending. But if Nane returns, it will be the birth of a true Buddha.”

“Then it will be perfect truth, the only right.”

“Even if that’s true, it doesn’t make me love you.”

Uorin had covered her eyes.

“Even among the Teraje bloodline, I’m unique. The reason I knelt to Havitz was that I felt death from him.”

It wasn’t magic.

“When you mix blue and red marbles in a jar, you can roughly guess what will come out. When I looked at Havitz, it was a life where only blue marbles came out endlessly.”

The pinnacle of probability.

“But for a moment, I felt something different. The emotion I felt was definitely a red marble. It was probably because of you. If I had died to Havitz, Havitz would have died to you.”

The war could have ended.

“Even so, I chose to live. In the same vein, my future vision tells me that you will come to me.”

“What does that mean…”

“Do you want me to tell you the only way to avoid it?”

Shirone had remained silent.

“Meet Amy. Forget about humanity and the world, and find happiness with the one you love. Until Nane closes the world.”

“Give up?”

Surprisingly, Uorin had nodded.

“Because you can’t win. If Nane returns, nothing can be undone.”

Shirone, coming out of his recollection, said,

“We can’t win. No one can.”

Rian’s face twisted.

“Why are you being like this? You stopped Nane before. You can do it again.”

“No. The reason I rejected Uorin’s words isn’t because I can stop Nane.”

“Then why?”

“Because I believe we can’t come back.”

“Hmm.”

“If Nane becomes Buddha, we’ll lose for sure. But he can’t become Buddha. He sympathizes with the world of emptiness but never plants his heart.”

“What if he does?”

Shirone thought for a moment.

“Then it can’t be destroyed.”

“…The law is profound.”

“Right. If we think Nane will return, we can’t do anything. Our job is to eliminate evil.”

They had to prevent the situation where the ten commanders of hell opened Makai again.

‘The army of hell advancing to the central continent has lost its commander. It’s right to go to a more intense battlefield.’

Knowing that Shirone was the one suffering the most, Rian didn’t push further.

“Huh?”

Suddenly, Shirone turned his head, and Rian, sensing something strange, stood up.

In an instant, Rian drew his large sword and climbed onto Salsu’s head, seeing the space distort.

“What is that?”

From the space that twisted like a vortex around a point, a woman appeared.

“Shirone.”

“Huh?”

Shirone’s eyes widened.

“Seongeum?”

Princess Jin Seongeum of the Jincheon Empire, who had competed with him as a candidate for the Ivory Tower, stood there with a stern expression.

Her face was more haggard than before due to the war with the demons, but her strong spirit remained unchanged.

After bowing her head to Rian, she approached Shirone and spoke.

“Sorry for coming suddenly. I was on my way back from the Ivory Tower. I heard you were here.”

“Why the Ivory Tower?”

Tears welled up in her eyes as she bit her lip.

But she quickly regained her composure and bowed deeply before Shirone.

“Please help us! Save Jincheon!”

Shirone and Rian looked at each other.

“All troops, ready!”

At the commander’s order, the shouts of the standard-bearers echoed.

The army of hell, which had reached the tri-nation border faster than expected, was advancing from 2 kilometers away.

The ground shook, and black dots rose from beyond the horizon, covering the sky like they were in zero gravity.

“Hoo! Hoo! Hoo!”

The sound of the leading infantrymen taking deep breaths could be heard even from hundreds of meters away by Amy.

‘They’re coming.’

Even with 40 million, they couldn’t cover the entire border, so she thought they could handle it strategically.

But the sight before her was so overwhelming that it blew away even that thought.

“Kahahaha! Humans! Humans!”

Demons of various heights, shapes, and features dominated the land, letting out monstrous cries.

“All troops!”

The commander’s order was given.

“Advance!”

As tens of thousands surged forward like a tide, Amy’s battalion also moved.

“Support the infantry!”

As 200 soldiers from three battalions cast military-style teleportation, a sharp beam extended forward.

‘We need to suppress them with firepower.’

The key to the magic attacks of a regular magic unit, not a special forces unit, was not power.

The unit’s capability was determined by how long they could accurately pour a certain amount of firepower into a specific area.

“Sector 32! Firepower level 10!”

At the brigade commander’s order, flames flared up in the hands of the mage.

As Amy raised her right hand to give the magic casting order to the battalion,

“Argh!”

The front-line infantry unit was instantly crushed as massive demons pushed through.

‘Already breached?’

Estimating the number of deaths from the collision, she gritted her teeth and swung her hand.

“Fire!”

Hundreds of fire spells hit the ground, causing massive flames to erupt instantly.

“Kraaa!”

The demons melted into grotesque forms, but the enemies kept pushing forward endlessly.

“Retreat! Retreat!”

Just minutes into the battle, the news of the breach made Amy dizzy.

“Damn it! It’s over! We’re all going to die!” Screams erupted from all sides.

“Stay calm! The enemy forces just concentrated on our side! It was just bad luck!”

Some units would be in a better situation than theirs.

“Reinforcements are coming soon! The balance of power will be restored! Just hold on a little longer!”

The advantage of the magic unit was their higher mental resilience compared to regular infantry.

Amy’s battalion remained composed, casting spells while retreating without being swayed by the chaos.

After retreating 300 meters, they finally saw their allies' flags fluttering around them.

“Here! Hold the line here! Sector 4! Form a net formation…”

“Commander!”

Turning at the voice, she saw a demon with bull horns charging at her with a massive axe.

“Kuhahaha! War is fun!”

Seeing the terrifying purple aura emanating from the demon, she instinctively knew it was of division commander level.

‘Damn it! That’s why!’

She realized why they had been breached so easily.

“Commander! Get back!”

If they had drawn the worst lot, retreating now would still mean annihilation.

‘That’s war.’

Dismounting, Amy lowered her stance and focused her mind.

‘Ignite!’

Flames erupted from her body, shooting up into the sky and transforming into the fire giant Ifrit.

“What, what is that?”

Even her subordinates were seeing this spell for the first time.

Ifrit, crouched low, brought its thick arms down beside Amy and glared forward.

“Come at me.”

A red light flickered in its large eyes.
