Chapter 1016: Evil Strikes Back (4)

Wizard's parents were dumbfounded.

“What is she saying? You shouldn't talk about dying so carelessly.”

The father apologized.

“I'm sorry. She's still a child and doesn't understand the ways of the world. She probably doesn't even know what dying means.”

“I do know. It's really scary.”

As a magician of super logic, she would have already felt all the possible outcomes that could happen to her.

“Yes, it's very scary,” Shirone said.

“Dying might not be the end. You could break an arm or suffer something terrible. Someone could cut your body with a knife…”

“Stop! Please stop!”

The mother was on the verge of tears.

“Why are you saying such horrible things? She's only seven years old! Save the world yourself!”

“I will.”

Shirone replied coldly.

“I don't want to send Wizard. That's why I'm trying to scare her even more. So, are you still not scared?”

“No, I'm fine.”

Wizard didn't hesitate.

“I might get hurt, and it might be painful. But I'll still do it. I want to keep going.”

‘This child really understands.’

She had never experienced it, but she understood it through insight.

“You're a magician.”

Wizard, smiling as if she had been praised, turned to her parents and drove the point home.

“I'm sorry, Mom. I want to follow my brother. Please let me go, okay?”

The sudden change in their daughter shocked the mother.

“Wi-Wizard.”

Just that morning, she had been acting immaturely, causing headaches for the teachers.

Lampa's eyes narrowed.

‘She was waiting. No, she might not have thought of it consciously, but deep down…’

Was she afraid?

‘No one could give her the right answer. Loneliness, anxiety, fear. Those negative emotions must have manifested as a sense of superiority.’

But Wizard now was different.

‘All her senses are fiercely absorbing Shirone. What is this, and what must be done to become like this?’

It was one of the correct answers.

As the parents, who realized Wizard's determination, were at a loss for words, the administrator intervened.

“Ahem, please come in. Now that we've heard Wizard's determination, let's proceed with the detailed procedures…”

“There's no need for that.”

Shirone spoke without even turning his gaze.

“We'll give you the amount Stang wants. If there are other conditions, tell Mr. Lampa.”

The father shouted.

“My daughter is not an object! Even if Wizard wants it, we still oppose it!”

“Yes. I also think it's unreasonable. I just confirmed her determination, but my thoughts haven't changed.”

Shirone knelt on one knee in front of Wizard.

“Listen carefully, Wizard. The enemy you will face is one of the strongest humans in the world. No, he might not even be called human.”

Wizard's eyes sparkled.

“So I can't send you right away. You will stay here for a while, and I will test you. It will be very harsh and painful. If you pass, you will become a magician like me. But if you don't pass…”

Shirone met the parents' eyes and continued.

“You are not ready to face the world yet. You will wait until I call you again. Can you promise that?”

Although he knew Wizard's determination, the opponent was Havitz, known as the extreme villain of the era.

‘As long as Satan remains, humanity has no future. If everything is going to be ruined anyway, I'd rather do it myself…’

It was the only consideration he could give Wizard.

“Okay. I'll do that.”

Although it took longer than usual, when the answer finally came, Shirone nodded and turned to the administrator.

“Nothing is decided yet. We will stay at the magic school for a while and teach Wizard. Of course, if you want, other students can join the classes.”

It was a decision to gain the consent of the Stang Kingdom, and as expected, the administrator swallowed hard.

‘The stars of the Ivory Tower at the school…’

Although it was disappointing not to secure the contract immediately, this much would satisfy the king.

“I will report to His Majesty first. Whether he approves or not will be communicated later.”

Ignoring the administrator, Shirone bowed to Wizard's parents.

“Please entrust Wizard to us. You are welcome to observe the classes anytime. If you still refuse afterward, I won't insist any further.”

The couple looked at each other.

They still couldn't understand Shirone's intentions, but they had only one choice.

Kido was strolling through the bustling area where reconstruction was underway.

"What am I doing?"

He had inadvertently entered human society, but there was no place for him here.

"She's truly evil."

Remembering the humiliation he suffered from Wuorin made him seethe, but the thought of leaving made him regretful.

"There were fun times, too. Maybe I should just give in and go back. I might regret it if I don't."

—Because it's disgusting.

Those words Wuorin spat out stuck in him like a dagger, igniting a fiery impulse.

"Ah, forget it! It's so annoying!"

He slumped down at the edge of the cobblestone path, glaring at the construction site ahead.

Harvitz was slashing a worker's throat.

"Hmm…??"

Kido rested his chin in his hand.

"Maybe my dreams were too big."

With each dance of Harvitz's blade, headless corpses twitched, spurting fountains of blood.

"It's not because I'm a goblin, but because Wuorin is an Empress. Even normal humans can't look down on her."

With that thought, he felt lighter.

"There's no need to feel inferior. From Wuorin's perspective, humans and I are equally insignificant. At least I'm on the stronger side..."

Emerging from the construction site, Harvitz indiscriminately slaughtered the passing civilians.

"Of course, that doesn’t change anything. After all, she thinks of me as an animal."

Kido sighed deeply as a severed arm of a citizen dropped in front of him.

"Yeah, I'll just get some air before going back. I'm smart, I should endure it. Why isn't Shirone coming?"

With a clear head, Kido stared blankly at the bloody severed arm.

That was when it hit him.

"…This is strange."

He didn’t even realize he had spoken those words.

"Huh? Strange? No, wait, it is really strange. But what's strange?"

He looked up to see Harvitz approaching.

"It's been a while, goblin."

His voice was clear to the ear but didn't quite reach his brain.

"Let's make this fun. Enjoyably fun."

As Harvitz raised his sword, Kido bit into the flesh of his right hand.

Clang! The blade struck the ground.

"Ugh! Ugh!"

Kido leaped up, gasping for breath as he racked his brain.

"What is this…?"

Harvitz still wasn't registering in his perception.

But within the memories infused with the taste of events, a reality different from the present was unfolding.

"Ho?"

With a force that could pound the ground, Harvitz slowly lifted his upper body.

"You're an interesting one, indeed."

His ability to eat his memories again provided him with objective information.

"Chomp! Chomp!"

Kido greedily sucked up the blood.

"It's getting clearer."

And just as he caught a blurry silhouette, he pushed off the ground and retreated.

"It's Harvitz."

It wasn’t a perfect form, but there was definitely a monstrous silhouette wielding a sword.

"What to do? Should I run? But I don't even know where I am. If he's right beside me…"

He acted as he thought.

"…then!"

Kido pulled out a spear from his back and wildly slashed around before rolling on the ground.

It might have been a delusion.

But unlike humans, a goblin's strong survival instinct chose life over shame.

"Didn't he come closer? Or did he avoid it?"

Kido stuck out his tongue, tasting the air.

"…No good. The chances of Harvitz's cells floating around are low, and it's too faint to even be called a flavor."

He had no choice but to run.

"Wait."

Kido flipped backward to land and sucked blood from the wound on his right hand again.

"There. It's still there. Something terrible is watching me from a blurry shadow."

If he could kill Harvitz here.

"If I could decapitate the man who gave Wuorin, the Empress of Kashan, the greatest humiliation of her life..."

"Poo!"

Kido gripped the spear with both hands.

"Relying on the taste of memories for an attack is too late. By a rough calculation, it takes about twelve steps for him to reach me, between three to four seconds."

The imaginary second hand moved.

"Two seconds. One second."

Ambushing while the opponent was off-guard was undoubtedly a brilliant strategy, but...

"I can hear everything," Harvitz had something called 'God's frequency.'

Kido, who had activated the earth law 'Geomancer,' rotated on his back and turned in all directions.

"Now!"

The localized gravity twisted, and Harvitz's feet stuck to the ground as if mired in a swamp.

"Hehe, what an amusing skill."

For the first time in a long while, a dry orgasm of boredom surged in his heart as he repeated mundane murders.

"Get caught! Get caught!"

Kido endlessly whirled his spear towards an enemy that might or might not be there.

"As long as the Geomancer is active, he can't run far. The moment of killing is when you realize it!"

The image of a spinning top made of blade afterimages approached Harvitz at a terrifying speed.

As he raised his long sword piercingly, calculating the timing, he muttered softly.

"Hell."

A time not in the laws, 0.666 seconds.

"Huh?"

A black shadow that sprung from the ground enveloped Harvitz, but time did not stop.

"What?!"

As Kido shouted while rotating, a powerful impact hit his side.

"Argh!"

Kido, who had braced himself with the spear, slid on the ground and checked the impact point where a black robe stood.

"…Good judgment."

The wandering six o'clock.

Assuming all actions necessary to achieve something had been taken, he obtained the result.

"What are you all?"

Without answering Kido's question, the wandering six o'clock returned to the position of hell.

Harvitz circled around those standing in their own time.

"What?"

The place at four o'clock was empty.

"Where did one go?"

Hell was human, and without needing an answer, God's frequency told him.

"Hmm, Sirone."

As the phenomenon of vanishing disappeared, Kido was finally able to confirm the fallen bodies around him.

It was a bizarre and horrific scene, but for Kido, there was a task he needed to resolve first.

"I will protect Wuorin."

His heart's wounds hadn't healed, but he still couldn't give up on her.

"Harvitz! Listen carefully. From here, one step…"

"Okay, lead the way."

"Huh?"

Harvitz tapped his head.

"You’re saying you’ll come out to meet me? With the voice of your heart. Yeah, you were decent for a red."

With a blank expression, Kido suddenly bared his teeth fiercely.

"This woman is really...!"
