Chapter 366: Special Training (1)

Shirone was officially registered as an intern and began staying at the association. Kangnan thought it was safer to announce this before any strange rumors started circulating.

The first thing Shirone did was write a letter to send home. Kangnan reviewed it, and it was approved.

After sending the telegram in the city and returning, he found Flu waiting on the third floor.

"Did you send the letter?"

"Yes, they said it would take about five days. Were you waiting for me?"

"Yes, the Chief Secretary asked me to."

Shirone was still reeling from the shock of his meeting with Gaold. Hearing that Kangnan had requested something made him nervous.

"From today, I'll be helping with your training."

Shirone had expected some form of assistance from Gaold while he was here, but he hadn't anticipated being taught directly by a professional mage.

The reason he couldn't be happy about it was that it was Flu, the nagger, who would be helping him.

"Why the face? Are you sick?"

"No, it's just so sudden."

"What's sudden? The special training or me?"

"Both, actually."

Flu laughed heartily.

"Well, you're stuck with me. But don't worry too much. I won't be teaching you directly."

"What do you mean?"

Flu rummaged through her inner pocket, sticking out her tongue and rolling her eyes, indicating it was buried deep.

"Ta-da! I got this from the Chief Secretary. Do you know what this is?"

In Flu's hand was a large golden key.

"Well, it looks like a key, but..."

"This is the key to the Magic Association's library! Without the Chairman's approval, no one can enter this area!"

Shirone took a moment to react. He needed to understand what the library was to know whether to be excited or not.

"What are you waiting for? Let's go."

Although it was Gaold's consideration for Shirone, it was Flu who was jumping with excitement.

She led Shirone to the 16th floor of the association.

When the elevator doors opened, a solid iron door blocked their way.

Out of habit, Shirone almost walked into it.

Flu laughed, as if she had expected it, and inserted the key.

The door opened automatically, revealing a grand sight.

The entire 16th floor was a library, with bookshelves stretching out in perfect rows like reflections between two mirrors.

"Welcome. You must be the intern."

A graceful woman in her mid-thirties appeared from beyond the iron door.

She wore a formal black suit, her black hair neatly tied up. She had light makeup, pink lipstick, thinly groomed eyebrows, and large, deep eyes with double eyelids.

"Hello, Ms. Isabel."

Flu bowed politely and gestured for Shirone to come forward.

"Introduce yourself. This is Isabel, the head librarian and a certified third-class mage."

Shirone was startled and bowed. The disparity between her rank and youthful appearance was jarring.

"Hello, I'm Arian Shirone."

"Yes, I've heard about you from the Chairman. You're a promising talent, so work hard. Come in, I'll show you around the library."

Isabel turned, and Flu and Shirone followed her inside.

"This is the floor where the magic books are stored. There are about 28,000 volumes. We have most of the regulated magic registered in the kingdom."

"28,000 volumes?"

Shirone couldn't hide his amazement as he looked around.

Every book here represented a magic developed by someone. He trembled with excitement.

Flu, as if expecting this reaction, raised her finger.

"See, I told you. It's a huge privilege to be here."

Shirone barely heard Flu's words.

The library had thin books of ten pages and thick ones of up to a thousand pages. The covers and designs varied by attribute and series, and the heights were all different.

It looked like an artistic representation of the myriad magics in the world.

'Ah, that's why an archmage has to manage this.'

Magic books contained detailed information about specific magics.

If one could enter the Spirit Zone and had an exceptional mind, they could learn new magic through self-study.

High-level magic books would be incredibly expensive, and the value of 28,000 volumes was beyond imagination.

"I'm sorry. I couldn't believe it at first. Ms. Isabel looks too young to be an archmage."

Isabel covered her mouth and chuckled. Most mages were proud, but she seemed pleased to be told she looked young.

"I understand. But being an archmage doesn't necessarily mean being strong or destructive. I'm in an administrative role. The Red Line rank is determined by reputation and achievement points. When I first came to the library, I was only a certified seventh-class mage. After organizing 1,500 magic books over ten years and my mentor retired, I became a third-class mage. My achievement points skyrocketed, but my reputation is still low."

Achievements and reputation varied greatly by profession. Some high-ranking mages were powerful in combat, while others specialized in different areas.

Having worked in the library for ten years, Isabel likely had little practical experience.

But Shirone was impressed by her in a different way.

"1,500 volumes..."

The process of registering regulated magic involved interviews or correspondence with the magic developer.

Once the principles and capabilities were documented in a thesis format, Isabel reviewed, summarized, and bound them into magic books.

This required an exceptional understanding of magic and extensive knowledge.

'Indeed, anyone who organized 1,500 magic books over ten years would become an archmage.'

He knew enduring those ten years was extremely difficult.

In that sense, Isabel deserved to be a certified third-class archmage.

"Anyway, just think of me as a friendly sister. Honestly, the title of archmage is a bit burdensome."

Having only met formidable and extraordinary archmages, Shirone found this refreshing.

Thanks to this, he could explore the library more comfortably.

Magic books were broadly categorized by system and then further subdivided chronologically.

There were very old ones and those leading the latest trends.

Seeing a copy of a photonic magic book gave him a slight thrill.

After the introduction, Isabel returned to her desk, leaving them to explore freely.

As she leaned over her desk, a pen moved on its own beside her.

Having worked in the grand library of Ozend, Shirone could imagine how much Isabel's work speed had accelerated over ten years.

'With that level of skill, organizing 1,500 volumes in ten years isn't impossible.'

Flu patted Shirone's back.

"Hey, what are you staring at? Ms. Isabel is married. Is she your type?"

Shirone shook his head, looking serious.

"Absolutely not."

Flu, ignoring him, trembled with excitement.

"The Chief Secretary said you could look at anything. Let's start by choosing the magic you'll practice."

Take anything and learn it. Shirone guessed Gaold's intention.

In addition to training, he wanted Shirone to find hints for magic that could destroy Heaven.

But giving him free access to the library was unexpected.

Gaold's promise to spare no support seemed genuine.

Flu, being smart, likely knew Shirone's special training was related to Gaold's project. But as she had vowed before entering the Chairman's office, she showed no sign of it.

'Or maybe she's genuinely enjoying this...'

They went to the section with photonic magic books, Shirone's specialty.

Flu scanned the books on the shelves, offering advice.

"Don't bother with overly simple magic. It's inefficient. For example, what's the point of learning 'Have Fun Making Ping Pong Balls with Photons!'? It might be useful for a performance magician, but not for you."

Shirone took the three-page book Flu mentioned. It described a game of bouncing light balls off mirrors, fun for the whole family and great for dieting.

"Magic developed too long ago is also not worth it. There are many countermeasures for outdated magic. And you don't have time to learn high-difficulty magic. It would take at least six months, and you have to return to school."

"Ah, I see."

Flu's practical advice snapped him to attention.

He had to consider the time needed to learn the magic. The key was to find magic he could master while at the association.

"Of course, the choice is yours. Preferences affect capabilities. But you need guidelines. First, choose magic that complements your weaknesses. Second, choose magic that synergizes with what you can already do. Third, balance quality and quantity. That should do it."

So Shirone visited the library daily, browsing through the books.

The sooner he chose a magic, the more time he'd have to practice, but he wanted to choose carefully since this opportunity was rare.

'There are so many. It would take years just to look at the photonic series.'

There were trivial magics, ingenious ones, and some with extreme difficulty that seemed impossible.

It was a different world. Like a child in a candy store, he couldn't focus.

'No, stay calm. If I run out of time and can't learn anything, I'll regret it.'

Having adapted to the classification system, Shirone focused on finding magic that fit the guidelines.

He then found an intriguing title in the ten-year-old magic section.

Advanced Teleportation Techniques: Scatter Movement

"Hmm, teleportation."

He was interested because he considered teleportation one of his specialties. The fact that the book wasn't too thick also influenced his choice.

The clean white cover indicated it was a photonic magic book, and below the title was the name of the magic developer, Kindra Buki.

"Kindra Buki. Never heard of them."

Though he didn't know the person, Shirone respected them.

Registering independently developed magic as a regulated magic was up to the mage’s discretion until their death.

Therefore, while there are countless unregistered spells in the world, mages who willingly share the essence of their magic for the benefit of future generations are particularly admirable.

Without them, there would be nothing to learn at magic schools.

"Well then, I'll read it carefully."

With a reverent heart, Shirone opened the book and read for about ten minutes.

"Oh... I see."

It was quite an ingenious spell.

'This will definitely work. It will be effective in real combat.'

Scatter Movement is a spell that disperses photon output in all directions immediately upon casting teleportation.

To the opponent, it appears as if the mage is spreading out in all directions, creating an illusion.

'Hmm, so it uses passive magic and then transitions to active magic. Since passive magic has a lasting effect, it can be implemented simultaneously with active magic.'

From the middle of the book, detailed techniques and principles were laid out.

As Shirone continued reading, a brilliant idea suddenly struck him.

'What if I combine this with the God Particle?'

In other words, instead of dispersing photon output, he could fire Photon Cannons in all directions while teleporting.

If Buki intended Scatter Movement as a distraction tactic, Shirone could evolve it into a movement technique with offensive capabilities.
