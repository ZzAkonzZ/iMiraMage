Chapter 924: The Measure of Desperation (4)

As the wind pressure vibrated the angel's wings, Uriel covered his face with his arm and asked, "Who won?"

There was no winner.

In the middle of the plain, where the wind had pushed everything aside, Ikael and Goffin were still glaring at each other.

"Huff. Huff."

Goffin's steel-like hair stood on end, and the Idea of struggle flickered in his eyes.

Uriel spoke.

"How immense is the human heart?"

The rage of Anke Ra that Goffin had swallowed was an emotion vast enough to destroy the world.

"He almost digested it."

Though the aftereffects hadn't completely disappeared, Goffin's recent prowess reminded Uriel of his prime.

‘No, even more than that.’

Endlessly opposing the gods over eons had endlessly tempered his mind.

"He traded Hexa for the Halo. As a result, the Ultima system's functionality dropped below 10%. But... McClain Goffin."

"Maybe we should have killed him then. The real threat isn't the Ultima system, but Goffin."

A human with enough influence to turn heaven's gains into slight losses.

"It's a matter of hindsight. And the real result isn't out yet. If we topple the Tower of Babel, Goffin will be isolated. Gaia people will disappear from the world."

Uriel remained silent, but he couldn't shake the ominous thoughts.

‘Will that really happen?’ Even if Gaia people are exterminated, if Goffin isn't subdued...

‘It will continue someday.’

Thinking of a distant future beyond the reach of an angel's time perception, Ikael's role was crucial.

"Let's go. We need to end this war." The archangels flew towards the battlefield where angels and Babel were evenly matched.

Ikael had no time to check the situation in the sky.

"Answer me."

Goffin responded coldly.

"Answer what?"

The thrill of the kiss they shared long ago remained a timeless memory for both of them...

"Babel."

They were also arch-enemies who fiercely destroyed each other's camps as representatives of angels and Gaia people.

"Why do you care about that? Just break it, no matter how it looks."

"Answer me!"

The old Ikael would have said that.

"Why me? What am I to you? How do you define me in your heart?"

Before understanding the heart.

"And you?"

Ikael fell silent.

"This time, let me ask. Why are you asking? What answer do you really want to hear?"

‘The answer I want to hear.’

What words did she hope would come from Goffin's mouth?

"I don't know."

Ikael lowered her arms weakly.

"So you tell me."

She understood the essence of the heart through Hexa, but she didn't have the courage to express it.

"Don't look for it in me. There's no right answer in the heart. You have to choose for yourself."

It's paradoxical.

Because you can choose anything, you end up unable to choose anything.

Boom!

The sound of the Tower of Babel collapsing was heard, but Ikael and Goffin didn't look away.

"Humanity has lost."

The archangels began massacring the Gaia people, breaking through the significantly weakened Ultima system.

"You're the only one left. You're the only Gaia person we can't annihilate."

The collapse of the Tower of Babel.

In the year 777 of Omega, humanity lost to the gods.

"Is it over?"

Maybe.

But as long as Goffin, the Gaia person, remained, they couldn't surrender.

As Goffin silently turned away, Ikael reached out her hand.

"Come with me."

It felt like he would never return.

"You have nowhere to hide. Wherever you go, the angels will find and kill you.

I'll protect you."

Perhaps she had fought fiercely to gain the courage to say these words.

"This time, it's quite good. You can put some heart into your voice."

Goffin tilted his head slightly and smiled.

"But it's still not enough."

Before Ikael could speak, Goffin activated Miracle Stream and disappeared.

"Archangel."

The archangels arrived.

"Are you alright?" Just looking at her tattered clothes made of light showed the intensity of the battle.

"Yes..."

Goffin's last words echoed endlessly in Ikael's holy body.

‘Not enough?’

The reason she couldn't express her heart.

‘How do I define Goffin?’

Until she was sure of that, her words wouldn't reach Goffin.

"Archangel who led us to victory."

Kariel knelt on one knee and pleaded.

"Please give me the spoils of Babel. I will analyze their technology and create life. They will follow the angels and praise Anke Ra."

The scene where Goffin made flowers bloom with Hexa inspired Kariel, the archangel of creation.

"Follow the angels and praise Anke Ra?"

They would later be called Nephilim.

‘Can that really happen?’

Can a being with a heart blindly follow something?

"...Do as you wish." Ikael knew how meaningless it was to judge something she hadn't experienced.

‘Aren't I realizing it now?’

She thought she would be fine, but when Goffin disappeared, she felt a gaping hole in her chest.

‘He's not here. He's nowhere.’

She searched the entire planet with her vision, but there was no trace, as if he had evaporated.

‘Where did he go?’

A cold wind seeped into Ikael's hollow chest.

Ancient city, Eden.

Adam and Lilith, who had boarded the ark to escape Small Purification, started a new life on a small planet.

Their infinite lifespans were inherited genetically, allowing the Gaia people to quickly prosper.

"Another peaceful day."

Adam's eyes were gentle as he looked at the landscape from a high place in the city.

Lilith smiled and said,

"There's nothing more joyful than watching the children live happily."

The only words Goffin left before leaving heaven were to love each other.

There were temptations, but Adam loved Lilith, and Lilith loved only Adam.

Thus, the citizens of Eden could fully accept them as their parents.

"Huh?"

As Adam sensed something, the Gaia people looked up in surprise.

The sky opened up massively, and a blinding light poured out.

"Oh, oh."

Adam trembled with emotion and teared up.

"Dear Adam."

At the unforgettable voice, Adam finally knelt and wept.

"You've come. You've finally come."

Lilith also knelt beside her husband, but her awe was mixed with fear.

‘Why?’

After waiting for a long time without seeing Goffin, Adam couldn't hold back and spoke.

"Please come down. We have built this beautiful city according to your will. What happened to the others? Have you become a god?"

After a long pause, Goffin spoke.

"I must take your children."

"What?"

Adam, not understanding, asked again, and Lilith looked up with wide eyes.

"W-what do you mean? Where are you taking my children, the citizens of Eden..."

"No!"

Lilith stood up abruptly.

"That is absolutely not allowed. Everything Adam and I have built over our lifetimes. We started alone and, over eons, achieved this prosperity..."

"I understand."

Lilith turned to Adam's voice.

"Honey."

Adam's eyes were filled with sadness, but a resolute light shone stronger.

"If that is your will, take them. There must be a reason."

As he finished speaking, a giant stone gate was erected in the center of Eden, concentrating light.

As the gate disintegrated into a sphere of light, Lilith shouted at the sky.

"No! Absolutely not!"

"Honey! Don't you know what he means to the Gaia people? If we hadn't escaped Small Purification, we wouldn't be here!"

"Then let it be so! You can't take my children! I won't send them back to that horrible place!"

Goffin remained silent.

"We will go, Mother."

As Lilith turned, all the Gaia people on the ground were emitting light from their eyes.

"You all..."

"We've heard the stories since we were young. You always said we would all become gods someday."

"No."

Lilith shook her head.

"They are my children."

As tears streamed down her cheeks, her mind began to detach from the Ultima system.

"Honey..."

Was a mother's heart greater than the whole?

Adam could only watch.

Everyone except Lilith shared the same thought, and the Gaia people looked up at the sky and said,

"We will fight too."

Thus, Goffin revived the Ultima system and regained the opportunity to leave the Photon System.

It was an event in the year 799 of Omega.

Heaven, Omega 892.

In heaven, where Gaia people had disappeared, many creations of Argones and Great Star emerged.

Beings called extraterrestrials crossed over, some with unique intelligence.

Only humans did not exist.

"Damn it! Damn it! Damn it!"

In the temple built after the Gaia people disappeared, Kariel's frustration shook the heavens.

"Why isn't it working!"

Watching Kariel's outbursts had become Uriel's greatest amusement lately.

"Maybe you're dumber than humans?"

"Shut up! Look at the Great World War I created! It's a far superior work to the Tower of Babel!"

Kariel, snapping back fiercely, looked at the corpse of an extraterrestrial on the experiment table.

‘Nephilim aren't being created.’

The dream of creating a new humanity with the abilities of angels was hitting a wall.

"...We need humans."

Uriel crossed his arms.

"Then it's impossible. There are none here. Not even species similar to humans are being born."

The expression "fear of humans" is taboo, but the shock Gaia inflicted on Heaven was just that great.

"Sigh."

Uriel didn't miss the noticeable weakening of Kariel's halo.

'Even the Archangel of Creation couldn't achieve this, no matter how much mental strength he poured in. What on earth is Goffin...'

At that moment, the Great World War shook.

"What the...!"

Kariel quickly flew to the screen, checked the data, and widened his eyes.

"This, this is...!"

"Sigh."

Ikael, who was staying in the Archangel's office, rested her chin on her hand and let out a long sigh.

'It's been happening more often lately.'

Her triangular Mara Ashur was guarding her back with a worried expression.

'The war is over.'

Peace had come to Heaven, but her pierced heart still seemed unhealed.

"I'm sorry. I must look so pathetic, right?"

Ashur bowed his head.

"Please don't say that. If you are pathetic, the entire universe would fall into despair."

At that moment, Ikael chuckled at the bone-dry joke, and then the sirens blared throughout the Archangel's domain.

‘Warning! Abnormal law detected. Total Kar: 16.84 billion. Kar level: 100 percent.’

Ashur raised his head, and Ikael sprang to her feet.

"100 percent?"

It was the same level of righteousness as the law.

‘Analyzing singularity. Suspected Ultima system. Assumption. Certainty. Fact. Highest defense level Aegis mode activated.’

The eight archangels scattered across the Archangel's domain,

All the angels under their command widened their eyes and shouted.

"What?!"

With a bang, the walls of the Archangel's domain shattered, and Ikael flew out like a flash of light.

"Archangel!"

Ashur chased after her, but it seemed impossible to catch up with the current Ikael, except for the light.

'Where is it? Where?'

Her amplified senses scanned the entire planet, and finally, a pillar of light came into her view.

'Idea.'

As she watched the countless pillars of light pierce the sky,

"Wow, it's been a while."

She lowered her gaze to the voice from the ground, and a man was smiling with his fangs exposed.

"How have you been? Bored without me, right?" Goffin greeted with his index finger on his eyebrow, and Ikael felt a surge of emotion.

But soon, her lips curled up, and then she furrowed her brows fiercely.

"Yeah."

She probably smiled.

The place that had never been filled, even after waiting for an eternity, no longer felt cold.
