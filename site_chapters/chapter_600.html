Chapter 600:

Shirone woke up but couldn’t get out of bed until after noon.

“Goffin…”

The words Goffin had left behind swirled in his mind like a puzzle.

“I need to go.”

Rising from bed, Shirone washed up and headed to the Supernatural Psychic Research Laboratory.

Taking a deep breath, he cast Time Leap, causing the time barrier to shatter with a swirl of time energy.

The noisy sounds of the upper echelons were no longer audible.

‘It’s closed.’

With Miro out in the world, Hexa couldn’t become the material for the wall, and thus the upper echelons had no reason to exist.

‘He’s gone.’

Thinking that Goffin had completely left, Shirone sank into the sofa, feeling empty.

‘I have no cause.’

It felt like being alone in the universe.

“Shirone, what happened?”

Amy and Iruki entered.

“What? The evaluation is already over?”

“The evaluation schedule is a mess because of the Scramble Royal.”

Without Neide’s return, the allied team had no choice but to forfeit.

“You weren’t in the dorm, so we came here. What’s going on? Anchal told us to ask you directly.”

“By the way, where’s Anchal?”

“She left. She dropped out.”

“I see.”

He had expected it.

“Now tell us. You promised.”

“Well…”

Shirone explained everything that had happened in the upper echelons.

Since there had been no secrets so far, they understood quickly, and Amy looked sad in the somber atmosphere.

‘Poor Shirone.’

He had given up his dream of becoming a mage to find his roots, only for it to end like this.

“I have no roots. I don’t know what that means, but I don’t even want to know. It feels too awful.”

“There’s no result without a cause. Even if it’s a perfect coincidence, it doesn’t mean your existence is meaningless.”

Amy supported Iruki’s words.

“Yeah, Shirone. There must be a reason. Goffin even said ‘once was.’ Don’t give up so soon.”

Feeling that the conversation had become too dark, Shirone changed the subject.

“By the way, what about Neide? Hasn’t he come back yet?”

“He probably feels responsible for leaving the Scramble Royal. He might need more time.”

“But we won. Neide should know that. I’ll go look for him. I’ll visit the West family.”

“He probably didn’t go home. He’s likely camping out somewhere.”

It didn’t make sense for someone with a home and money to camp out, but Iruki was certain.

“Tell me, Iruki. Why is Neide like this? At least tell me what happened so I can persuade him.”

If Neide wasn’t going to leave forever, it would come out eventually.

“Shirone, you have no roots, but…”

Iruki began cautiously.

“Neide’s roots are diseased.”

The early autumn wind in the mountains was even colder at night.

“Brr, it’s cold.”

Neide curled up by the campfire, trying to sleep.

“Please let tonight pass safely…”

Nineteen years ago, a genius was born into the West family, but no one in the family knew it.

“Ugh, so annoying!”

Neide’s mother, Teria, had been fixing her makeup at the vanity for over an hour.

‘Using these cheap cosmetics…’

In a normal wealthy family, her extravagance and wastefulness wouldn’t have been an issue.

Who could have known?

The West family, a real estate dynasty for generations, was burdened with massive debt.

‘One wrong marriage ruined my life.’

She had met Neide’s father, West Bolum, at a social party.

Seeing his expensive watch, designer clothes, and a seven-square-meter carriage made by a master craftsman, she thought a rosy future awaited her.

Drunk, she became pregnant with Neide and joined the West family, only to board a sinking ship.

“Pathetic man.”

The family was falling apart, but the head of the house, Bolum, was still lost in drink and gambling, rarely coming home.

No matter how much she yelled at him to save the family business, Teria, whom he had taken in after a one-night stand, was just a nuisance to him.

“The young master is crying again.”

At the nanny’s words, she threw her comb to the floor.

“Then make him stop! That’s what you’re here for!”

“But once he starts crying…”

“Annoying!”

Teria pushed the nanny aside and went to Neide’s room.

“Mom! Mom!”

Seeing her son crying with his mouth wide open made her think of her pathetic husband, and her hair stood on end.

“Why…!”

Teria screamed at the top of her lungs.

“Why are you crying! Why are you crying! We’re giving you everything we have! What’s your problem!”

“There’s a strange sound… a sound…”

“Are you stupid? Why are you making things harder for me!”

Contrary to Teria’s misunderstanding, Neide was suffering from an overflow of understanding the world’s principles.

Neide was only three years old.

“Mommy. Mommy.”

Teria’s face twisted in rage, and she slapped Neide hard.

The physical shock made the child stop crying for a moment, and until Neide’s mind was completely destroyed, this was Teria’s only method.

“Gasp!”

Neide sat up abruptly, looking around in a panic before sighing in relief.

“Phew, thank goodness.”

The fact that no electricity had been discharged meant he was regaining control, but he still felt miserable.

‘I should go back soon.’

Having left the Scramble Royal, he felt too ashamed to face his friends.

“I need to wash up first.”

By the time he descended the mountain and reached the city, dawn had broken, and Neide went to a public bath to wash away the fatigue of camping.

There weren’t many people, but everyone glanced at Neide’s body.

The burn marks all over him were evidence of torture by the notoriously cruel Chickenhead Bandits.

He should have died, but Neide saved his body by killing his mind.

“The scars are still there…”

The mirror showed the nail marks he had made on his face.

The scabs had fallen off, but it would take more time for them to heal completely.

‘At least I can control it now. I’ll stay at an inn from today.’

Neide had no intention of returning to school yet.

“He’s not here. Where did he go?”

After searching the city of Creas all day, Shirone sat on someone’s roof and sighed.

The streets were full of people, but those weary of life didn’t seem to have the luxury of looking up at the sky.

He couldn’t search the entire city in one day, but expanding his Spirit Zone in his Golden Armor state made it seem not entirely impossible.

“Did he really go into the mountains?”

If so, there was no way to find him, so it was better to keep searching the city.

Spreading his wings, Shirone flew up and observed the ground with hawk-like vision through Armand’s enhanced senses.

“There!”

Finally, he spotted Neide coming out of a bathhouse three blocks away.

“Not even an old man…”

Licking his lips, Shirone flew over the blocks and landed on a rooftop.

As his tentacles lightly touched the ground, his body spun, and by the time he descended, Armand was deactivated.

Landing nimbly, people around him stepped back in surprise.

“What, what is this? A circus?”

Hearing the murmurs, Neide turned and his face hardened.

“Ha! Found you!”

“Shirone?”

Frowning, Neide lowered his head and started to run before Shirone could speak.

“Hey! Why are you running?”

Shirone, bewildered, took off after him.

“Shirone! Don’t follow me!”

“Why are you running?”

“I don’t know! Just leave me alone!”

Despite bumping into people and tripping over market stalls, they didn’t stop.

‘He’s persistent.’

Knowing Shirone’s stubbornness, Neide cast a teleportation spell and flew into the sky.

“Ahh!”

Pedestrians screamed, and some merchants pointed to the sky, shouting.

“A mage! That’s definitely magic!”

For students of the magic school, it was routine, but most people never had the chance to see magic in their lives.

“That’s…!”

Gritting his teeth, Shirone looked up.

Neide had calculated that Shirone wouldn’t use magic in front of people, but having heard from Iruki, Shirone wasn’t giving up this time.

“Stop right there! Just wait until I catch you!”

As flashes of light shot up, economic activity on the street halted, and everyone looked up at the sky as if time had stopped.

Neide shouted at Shirone, who was closely pursuing him.

“Ahh! Are you crazy? What if someone reports us to the school?”

“Then why are you running? Just talk to me for a moment!”

‘This is bad. He’s serious.’

Knowing Shirone’s movement skills, Neide imagined the inevitable capture and swallowed hard.

“Don’t be ridiculous! I’ll show you my true power!”

One minute later.

“Aaah!”

Caught by Shirone, Neide fell to the ground in the middle of the market and rolled.

“Come here!”

“Help!”

His freshly washed body was now covered in dust, but Neide crawled on the ground.

But as soon as he stood up, Shirone grabbed him and pushed him against a wall.

“It’s over! Give up!”

As Shirone’s face suddenly came closer, Neide quickly turned his head away.

He couldn’t bear to show his face.

“Look at me! Don’t turn away!”

“No.”

“Seriously!”

When Shirone changed his position to face him, Neide’s head whipped to the other side.

As they repeated this dance of avoiding and confronting, murmurs arose from the surrounding crowd.

“What are they doing so early in the morning? How embarrassing.”

“Wait a minute, aren’t they both guys?”

Blushing, Neide pushed away Shirone’s arm from his shoulder and shouted.

“Ugh! Let go! You’re making people misunderstand!”

“Then why are you running? Did you do something wrong?”

After panting for a while, Neide suddenly looked sad and hung his head.

“I’m sorry, Shirone. I messed everything up. I was the one who dragged you all into this.”

“What are you talking about? You fought your best. Iruki told me everything. Besides, we won the Scramble Royal.”

It was a relief for Neide, but it also increased his guilt.

“Ha, I should have been there.”

“Everyone’s worried about you. Let’s go back to school.”

Meanwhile, a man and woman who had just arrived in the city were bewildered by the commotion.

“What’s all this ruckus since earlier?”

The blond man was tall and handsome, and beside him was a sophisticated woman from the capital, Bashka, holding his arm.

“They’re saying something about a mage in that block. Apparently, it’s a young kid.”

“Then a student? From Alpheas Magic School.”

The woman smiled at the man’s words.

“Maybe so.”

“Geez. The student management must be terrible in the provinces. This would never happen in the capital.”

The woman glared at him.

“Are you insulting my alma mater?”

“Haha! Is that how it sounds?”

“Anyway, let’s go check it out. Who knows? We might meet someone we know.”

“We’re busy, can’t we just…!”

As the woman dragged the man along, he reluctantly followed, licking his lips.

“Haha, I wonder what kind of kids…”

Pushing through the crowd, the woman’s eyes widened when she saw the two boys arguing.

“What?”

“Why? Do you know them?”

The man yawned and asked, but she ignored him and approached the boys.

“Neide! Is that you, Neide?”

Hearing the familiar voice, Neide turned his head and froze, looking like his soul had left his body.

“Liz… senpai?”

“Wow! It’s really you, Neide? How long has it been?”

“What are you doing here, senpai?”

Aple Liz.

The former president of the Supernatural Psychic Research Society and Neide’s first love, who had pulled him out of hell.
