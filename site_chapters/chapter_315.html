Chapter 315: The Wheel of Causality (7)

Shirone, still reeling from the mental shock, lay down on the bed and fell asleep. Vincent and Olina sat on the opposite bed, while Amy sat on the floor, gazing out the window.

Lost in thought, Reina finally spoke with a relieved expression.

"Phew, those were a really long four days."

Amy, enjoying the breeze, turned around.

"Indeed. And I was really surprised. Orkamp wasn't his real father."

Vincent shook his head, recalling the events.

"I was just as shocked. When my wife went into the Grand Hall, I thought we'd be buried here."

Reina looked at Olina.

"So, what will you do? Will you tell Shirone the truth?"

"I've thought about it, and I think it's better not to. He'll find out eventually anyway."

Amy asked, "Why not? Queen Eliza even gave you an official document stating he wasn't her child. You should make it clear to Shirone that she's not his real mother."

"I saw Shirone's eyes when he entered the Grand Hall. Maybe he already knew they weren't his real parents."

Reina agreed. "He's smart and perceptive. Shirone said he had no parents other than you two. Maybe he said that assuming other real parents might show up."

Thinking about it, it made sense. At first, they thought his words were out of betrayal, but Shirone wasn't one to throw tantrums.

"He's a good kid. He spoke boldly in front of the queen, but he's really an exceptional son. I feel ashamed for dragging him into this mess."

Reina offered words of comfort. "Don't think that way. It was unavoidable. The place and time they abandoned their child matched, and it was the only case of an abandoned child at that time."

"Wait a minute!"

Amy seemed to realize something and raised her hand.

The maid who abandoned her son said she left him in the stable at the Valley of Dawn. That's why Shirone was invited to the castle as a likely candidate. But the results showed he wasn't their child.

"Then who is Shirone?"

Everyone fell silent, understanding the weight of the question.

If he wasn't Orkamp's child, who had abandoned Shirone?

Vincent vividly recalled that night. He heard a baby's cry while sleeping and went to the stable. But there were no signs of anyone having been there.

What happened that night? Why couldn't they find Orkamp's child? What did Shirone realize that made him say those words to Orkamp?

With countless answers buried deep within, Shirone remained in a deep sleep.

Armin arrived in Toshka Village. He had heard on the way back that Shirone was safe.

But now he had his own problems. If rumors spread that he was involved with a Black Line mage, the higher-ups wouldn't stay quiet.

'It should be fine. I explained everything clearly.'

He had explained to Amy why he wore a mask, so she would keep her promise.

Even if she didn't, there was nothing he could do. Trust was fundamental.

Armin looked up at the house surrounded by a time barrier. It was deathly quiet.

Sighing, he cast a Flicker spell and found himself inside the room.

As soon as he arrived at the spot where he had cast the Stop spell, time resumed, and a vase shattered.

Keira glared at Armin. She had been distracted by the falling vase and couldn't tell how much his position had changed.

"How many days... has it been?"

"About four days..."

Keira bit her lip in frustration.

"So you went after all? Even though I told you not to?"

"What are you talking about?"

"Don't lie to me! Otherwise, why would you cast a time barrier?"

Armin lied calmly. "I was just angry and needed time to think."

Keira scoffed. Who would believe that?

But what infuriated her was that she had no way to confirm it. His confident lie meant he had left no trace. So he still held the upper hand.

"Fine. I'll report to the higher-ups. But first, report to me."

"I'll say it again, I didn't go anywhere."

Keira ground her teeth.

Armin was known for his stubbornness since joining the organization. Once he clammed up, no method could make him talk.

Just then, a small red bird flew through the wall. It was a Telebird sent by the higher-ups.

The level of photomancy required was extreme, and flying 78,000 kilometers to deliver information was only possible for a mage who had reached the pinnacle of information magic.

A red Telebird signified a Level 1 crisis.

The bird glowed red and inscribed letters in the air. Keira's face turned pale as she deciphered the code in real-time.

"This can't be. It can't be this fast..."

Armin watched the red bird vanish like a flame and walked to the window. Before going to Kazra, he would have thought the same as Keira, but not anymore.

"I've changed my mind. Let's go back, Keira. There's something we need to report to the higher-ups."

He wasn't one to change his mind on a whim. Keira guessed that the events in Kazra and the Telebird's message were not entirely unrelated.

"You know something, don't you?"

Armin remained silent.

He hoped Shirone could enjoy his school days a little longer. Once everyone knew, the world would never leave him alone.

'We might meet sooner than expected, Shirone.'

The Kingdom of Kazra cut off all diplomatic channels.

There was no official statement. They only communicated through a hotline that the Kashan Empire had taken over governance.

Allied nations were displeased, but with Teraje standing firm, they couldn't openly exert pressure.

It was the power of the Triumvirate, and thus the Kingdom of Kazra faded from the world's attention.

Shirone rested for a few days and returned to school. The advanced class curriculum was over, but the graduation exam, the school's biggest event, was the next day, so the atmosphere was intense.

Returning to school meant he wasn't royalty, so his classmates' attitudes remained cold. But since it wasn't certain, they couldn't hurl insults like before.

Shirone had felt a bit hurt before leaving, but compared to the humiliation in Kazra, it was child's play. Besides, he had more pressing concerns lately.

A monster was sleeping inside him.

The feeling of a vast memory that once existed, the emptiness in his brain, was proof.

At first, it was just a vague feeling. But as he rested, the outline emerged like a fossil being unearthed.

Some memories were completely lost, but scenes that left a strong impression gradually became clearer.

When the demon forcibly expanded his mind, the pain was unbearable.

Or was it really?

In truth, he felt a bursting sense of liberation at the end of the pain. The acceleration through the rampage was exhilarating, like being high on drugs, and every time magic was activated, a thrilling pleasure flowed through him.

'No, that wasn't me.'

It wasn't him. It was a new being, a fusion of Shirone and the demon. But following that line of thought led to the conclusion that it was still himself.

The demon still slept deep within Sjirone. And the key to unlocking the seal was still in his hand.

'I mustn't open it. If I can't control myself, nothing can be mine.'

The demon had taken control because he was weak. To reverse the master-servant relationship, he needed unwavering strength in any situation.

Shirone went to the training ground. The regular course was over, so no students were around.

After performing a water ceremony for about an hour, he calmly opened his eyes and focused on photons. Taking a magical action of holding a ball with both hands, photons formed between his palms.

The demon had expanded his mind to the brink of the Diamond Body's elasticity collapsing. It couldn't have been a calculated attempt. Without the Diamond Body, he would have been a wreck, confined to a corner.

Shirone was surprised by the different compression of photons.

When preparing for the Supernatural Psychic Science Research Society's presentation, he had strengthened his Spirit Zone by rebounding his mind daily. But this time, the rebound was far more extreme.

As he concentrated on the divine particles, the light source of the Photon Cannon gradually shrank, transforming into a white sphere with a clear outline. It was like looking at platinum burning in sunlight.

Further compression would exponentially increase the density, creating a spell called Dark Sphere, but it would inevitably lead to a loss of control.

Shirone pressed the photons up and down. The flattened photons flew like a disc, slicing through space and returning.

He caught it, split it in two, and shaped it into shurikens. As he extended his hands, the shurikens pierced through space and returned with a reverse spin.

Even after two shapings, the form didn't collapse, so Shirone split it further.

He shaped eight throwing knives with crescent-shaped wings on streamlined bodies, resembling birds, and held them between his fingers.

Crossing his arms in an X, he swung them, and the knives responded sensitively to the wind pressure, slashing through the air with a sound like a swarm of locusts. Anything entering the radius would be shredded instantly.

Shirone gathered the slowing knives, four in each hand. The density was significantly weakened. As he combined the knives like clay, the photons exploded, scattering blade-like flashes. The sparkling fragments of light floated before his eyes like petals.

"Hmm..."

Thanks to controlling the photon's luminescence, he could perform detailed shaping without using fractal batteries. Since function comes from form, the utility of photon-based magic was incomparable to before.

But Shirone wasn't satisfied.

As he pondered, he sensed someone behind him. Turning around, he saw Iruki and Neide with their jaws dropped.

"Oh? When did you guys get here?"

Neide was too stunned to respond.

They had thought it was typical of Shirone to head straight to the training ground upon returning, but this was different. The magic he was using now was on a completely different level from a few days ago.

"What was that just now? A new spell you developed? What's it called?"

"It doesn't have a name. I was just experimenting with different things. I've managed to control the luminescence of photons. But using it as a weapon seems inefficient. I need to think about it more."

Photons are inherently difficult to shape, so spells involving shaping consume a lot of mental energy. Unless it was significantly more effective than the Photon Cannon, there was no reason to switch to an attack spell.

Iruki concluded, "So, the spell that left us dumbfounded was just a simple experiment. A piece of trash magic that you'll never look at again."

"...No, it's not that bad."

Iruki chuckled and asked, "What happened? I heard the governance was transferred to Kashan, but what else?"
