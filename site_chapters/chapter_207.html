Chapter 207: Immortal and Eternal Ra (4)

Ymir knew that his allotted time was over.

As he slowly turned around, indescribable frustration and anger were etched in his eyes.

If Ra said so, then it must be so.

But his intuition was telling him something different from Ra. No, he was realizing the truth that Ra was hiding.

'I need to see it with my own eyes.'

Ymir headed toward the city of angels, Zebul.

Shirone looked around after entering Ikael's room.

It was different from what he imagined. The scene of the room, with tables, chairs, beds, and even carpets, was no different from a place where humans live.

Yet, it didn't feel like it belonged to humans.

All the objects were bathed in sunlight, as if they had been isolated from the flow of time, without the slightest flaw.

It was truly peculiar to see objects that were absolutely flawless.

It was a detail that could not be seen with the human eye.

However, anyone present here will be able to sense a purity that is close to perfection.

Shirone picked up a teaspoon from the table and saw his reflection.

As expected, it was eerily clean, not something a human could craft. He thought that this might be what they meant by a holy relic in the human world.

'Huh? Come to think of it...'

Ikael, the most important person, was nowhere to be seen. Peope had clearly said that she couldn't leave the room.

Could it be that he had been deceived?

Shirone, feeling scared, walked around the room frantically. As expected, there was no one. Everything was clean. It was a perfection that bordered on horror.

Suddenly, a beautiful melody echoed in her ears. Shirone stopped and looked at a window five meters high.

A clear resonance like a harp was emanating from there.

The angel's vibration.

As if possessed, he went under the window.

Sunlight streaming in through the window began to take shape. When he came to his senses, a woman was sitting on the windowsill, looking outside.

It was a face so beautiful that no painter could capture it.

Shirone confirmed that a Holy Light Body was floating above her head. It was much brighter and warmer than the fallen angel Ikasa's.

As her figure became clearer, wings of light emerged. The large, spread-out wings of light brought joy to Shirone's eyes.

“Welcome, Shirone.”

Shirone was convinced.

She was indeed Archangel Ikael.

The first entity of causality. Among them, it is a symbol of nobility that God cherishes the most. Such a being was before his eyes.

Ikael landed softly on the floor.

Her over two-meter height was imposing, but that physical feature didn't overshadow her warmth.

It was a strange feeling that he couldn't explain in words. Even saying she was beautiful seemed like criticizing her.

Is this what goodness looks like when it takes form? If he realized the truth when meeting Miro of Space and Time, then Ikael was the embodiment of goodness.

Ikael looked around for a seat with a playful expression that didn't match her mature appearance.

However, as if there was no way around it, she plopped down right where she stood.

Shirone watched in astonishment as Ikael's skirt slowly settled. Then, Ikael, sitting with her legs modestly tucked, tapped the floor and called Shirone over.

“Come, come this way.”

Feeling an irresistible urge, Shirone moved close to her.

They were so close that their bodies almost touched.

Perhaps she had suggested sitting on the floor to get a closer look.

As expected, Ikael leaned in. As she gazed steadily at Shirone, her lips gradually curved into a smile.

When Shirone lowered his gaze, unable to hide his embarrassment, she finally spoke.

“I wanted to meet you, Shirone.”

"Yes? You know me?"

Ikael scratched her eyebrows as if she were in trouble.

Her behavior resembled that of a human. Considering that even the fallen angel Ikasa had reached the peak of arrogance, it was quite shocking.

"Knowing or not knowing isn't important. What's important is how much you know. But as you know, that’s not something you can explain with words, right?"

Shirone mustered the courage to look at her face. As both absorbed in each other's eyes, a sense of unity sent a shiver through his body.

Then he raised his head and looked at the Holy Light Body. It was his first time seeing it up close.

The sight of the light floating above her head was truly marvelous.

Shirone hurriedly came to his senses, realizing that he had been staring at it for too long.

“Oh, I’m sorry.”

Ikael shook her head as if to say it was okay.

“The Holy Light Body is the mind of an angel. Humans enter a spirit zone through concentration, but an angel's zone is outside. This is called the Over Spirit."

After hearing the explanation, Shirone saw that the Holy Light Body in a new light. It was like constantly maintaining an out-of-body four-way deviant type.

It was literally the angel's mind.

It was everything about her.

Feeling a bit embarrassed, Ikael added,

“It used to be brighter than it is now. But I committed a sin, so Ra restrained the power of the light."

"What sin did you commit?"

As soon as Shirone asked the question, he quickly apologized.

"Oh, I'm sorry. I shouldn't have... "

"It's okay. Even though I committed a sin, I don't regret it even to this day."

Shirone somehow found himself relieved. He didn't know what the sin was, but if Ikael didn't regret it, then perhaps it wasn't a shameful act.

"Um..."

Shirone hesitated.

He knew it's a ridiculous request, but he just can't give up when his thoughts start to wander.

"Go ahead. You can say anything."

"Just once..."

"Yes?"

“Can I touch it just once?”

Ikael blinked and then burst into laughter as if finally understood.

It was an unexpected request.

There is not a single being in all of Heaven who would dare to touch the Holy Light Body of Archangel Ikael.

"Sure. Just touch it.”

“Um, is this rude? By any chance….”

Ikael shook her head as if it didn't matter.

Only then did Shirone feel reassured and reached out his hand.

The Holy Light Body that he directly touched was light and energy. A pleasant sensation flowed down the back of his hand.

“It’s warm.”

"That's because Shirone is a warm person."

"Hehe."

It was the first comfort he felt after coming to Heaven. Being with her made it seem like there was no need to worry about anything in the world.

He wanted to stay with her forever.

At that moment, Ikael's hand reached out. Startled, Shirone stepped back, and Ikael couldn't hide her embarrassed expression as she explained.

"Ah... your hair was a bit tangled."

“Haha, I ran hard. Actually, a lot happened.”

Shirone joked as she shook his hair. He was just surprised that her body would touch him when he wasn't mentally prepared.

At that moment, Shirone's eyes widened. It felt like he had been hit on the head with a hammer.

How can this happen without going crazy?

He had completely forgotten that the most important thing remained.

“Oh, no! Amy!”

Shirone jumped up.

Only now did he think of his friends. This showed how impressive his meeting with Ikael had been.

"Please help! My friends have been captured! They said something about the Elixir of Life and making them bear Nephilim."

"Oh dear. That’s a big problem.”

“They treat them like heretics and all that, but they are by no means bad people. No, even if that were the case, I can't accept them being taken like this."

Ikael pondered for a moment.

“Shirone, do you like your friends? Enough to risk your life for them?”

“Yes! Of course!”

Ikael nodded as if she was satisfied with the answer.

"Alright. But Shirone, if you want to save your friends, there's something you must hear."

He didn't think there was anything he needed to hear in the current situation.

But since it was Archangel Ikael's words, it was difficult to refuse. Above all, the words, 'If you want to rescue your friends' were decisive.

"Something I must hear? What is it?"

“About Heaven. And about a woman named Miro.”

Shirone was startled. He hadn't expected Ikael to mention her name.

Adrias Miro.

When he first came to Heaven, he thought Miro was just one of many unlockers.

But the more he learned about her, the more he realized that she had a deep connection to Heaven.

He wanted to know who Miro really was.

“Do you know Miro?”

"Yes. I've met her before. I like her, though I’m sure she doesn't like me."

Shirone was shocked. The fact that Ikael and Miro had met was surprising, especially since Miro disliked Ikael. From Shirone's experience, Ikael seemed to be a good angel.

“It won’t take long. So, could you give me a moment to comb Shirone’s hair?”

Ikael's Holy Light Body expanded into a ring.

As the intense light rotated along the ring, a comb made of light materialized in her hand.

Photon Sculpting.

She had created a delicate tool from light in an instant.

No matter how powerful an angel's psychic powers are, the nature of light does not change.

It was a tool that could only be created through a complex omniscience that was incomparable to Shining Chain.

Shirone realized that all the answers were in the Holy Light Body.

The moment he felt an untouchable energy from Ikasa, it was when her Holy Light Body spread out into a ring.

"How did you sculpt photons? Is it related to the change in your Holy Light Body?"

"The Holy Light Body can process a vast amount of information quickly. It's called a Halo. It's similar to the magic circles humans use. The halo spins at the speed of light, assembling numerous concepts."

Ikael used a magic circle as an example, but hearing it, Shirone felt comparing them was almost disrespectful.

It was a way of assembling concepts literally at the speed of light.

Even if dozen mages worked together, they couldn't match the speed of one halo.

"Then let's start. Lie down here, Shirone."

Ikael pointed to her lap. She seemed eager to brush Shirone's hair.

Shirone thought that this time there must be some kind of meaning to it, so he lay down as told.

After all, where else could he get such luxury?

Lying on the lap of an archangel, looking up at Heaven, Ikael began to comb his hair.

'What?'

Shirone felt his consciousness fading.

The more Ikael brushed his hair, the more it felt like his consciousness was being pulled out in chunks. He didn't want to faint. His friends were captured. If he lost track of time, it would be the end.

'No. No...'

Shirone's eyes slowly closed.

Ikael looked at Shirone's sleeping face with sad eyes and then looked up.

Many thoughts passed through her mind. And she penetrated Shirone's psyche through the comb.

Like reading a storybook to a child, Ikael began speaking in a beautiful voice.

"Long, long ago..."

The Truth of Heaven (1)

The Great Hall of World in Zebul was Kariel's laboratory and an observatory that governed the operation of the world.

Because the space had been erased with a special function of the Meca System, as long as the security device was active, not only was entry impossible, but even traces of it could not be found.

True to the name "Great Hall," the space was vast enough to contain the entire Alpheas Magic School.

The overall color scheme was a mechanical navy blue, and tens of thousands of electrical signals were flashing across the domed ceiling.

In the northwest space, a hologram of a globular cluster of stars was slowly expanding.

At the center floated a massive steel sphere over 30 meters in diameter, with two bands rotating along its orbit and axis.

Amy and her group sat near the globular cluster, their wrists bound with steel cuffs.

Ikasa, who had kidnapped the women, had hoped for reinstatement as an angel, but after Kariel's silence, he returned to the Second Heaven.

That was the only happy moment Amy and her group had had since they arrived here.

Conversely, other than that, everything else was bad.

Who would have thought that someone as high-ranking as an archangel would even use handcuffs on them?
