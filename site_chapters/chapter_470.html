Chapter 470:

Gaold's eyes took in the Hall of Corruption.

The moment he saw Miro standing on the wall, his heart turned into an even larger black smoke, enveloping the world.

"He's coming! Stop him at the entrance!"

Elite fire giants blocked the entrance to the drawbridge.

Their average height was a staggering 7 meters, and they all wore heavy armor, numbering in the hundreds.

As the giants charged in formation and struck with their massive swords, the bridge trembled.

Gaold leaped into the air and unleashed a powerful air press.

Boom!

The power of an air press from a mage with pain sensitivity increased by 2 million times was something even the strongest giants had never experienced before.

"Ugh!"

But the giants endured.

Their transcendent physical abilities, enhanced by the fifth stage of the elixir of myths, combined with five layers of Skima and exceptional techniques, allowed them to withstand the attack.

'Is the real challenge starting now?'

Sein, who had arrived late after defeating Tiara, felt a sense of crisis as Gaold's power was nullified for the first time.

Miro, watching from the distant wall, also knew it was a desperate situation.

This was, after all, the strongest unit in purgatory.

'We can't win like this.'

It wasn't just the warriors attacking Gaold.

As fire giants who wielded unique magic, various fire techniques raged across the bridge.

"Here I come!"

Gaold charged into the midst of the giants, tearing through the battlefield.

As air guns fired in all directions, holes were punched through the giants' armor.

The air, solidified by extreme atmospheric pressure, was no longer just air but sharp projectiles.

The sharp air, like spears several meters long, pierced through the giants' bodies repeatedly.

"Get him! One hit is all it takes to win!"

No matter how powerful Gaold was, he was fundamentally a mage.

If a giant's attack landed even once, he would be incapacitated in an instant.

But Gaold's true strength was just beginning to show.

He wasn't called the strongest battle mage of the Kingdom of Tormia just for his power.

The skills he had honed over 20 years of enduring immense pain were deeply ingrained in his body, allowing him to find the most suitable responses even in a state of lost reason.

As he dodged enemy attacks and swung his hands, sharp air cutters sliced through the space.

The bodies of giants with muscles stronger than steel were cut into chunks, and their Skima-less corpses were crushed by the air press, spraying blood particles.

It was a fireworks display with living bodies.

Boom! Boom! Boom!

Giants exploded in all directions, and the density on the drawbridge thinned.

"Ugh!"

Gaold gritted his teeth so hard they almost broke as he continued to eliminate the enemies.

Sein's party, who had just entered the drawbridge, could only watch in a daze, unable to come up with any strategy.

'Is that Gaold…?'

Armin erased all the vague images he had of Gaold before coming to Heaven.

Pain, the most extreme form of control for living beings.

Any desire would naturally fade before such pain.

But Gaold chose pain.

For desire.

It was a decision that defied the basics of living beings, a madness unique to humans.

'Human…'

Armin realized why everyone here was following Gaold.

They hadn't come to Heaven just to save Miro or stop the final war.

They had followed Gaold to see how far one human could transcend humanity.

"Raaaah!"

Gaold pushed the giants back with immense atmospheric pressure.

While the air press alone couldn't explode a fully intact giant, the momentum of his charge, fully converted into mental strength, pushed the giants' mass backward.

"Ugh!"

The giants gathered at the final barrier were indeed strong.

Despite his desperate push, he had only advanced about a kilometer.

Given that a dense formation was advantageous for blocking a charge, the enemies knew this was their last line of defense.

'Bastards…'

Gaold glared ahead with terrifying eyes.

It wasn't directed at anyone in particular, but at the entire world.

'From the beginning… I shouldn't have relied on someone like you.'

If he had refined his strength without depending on a transcendent being.

Maybe then, he wouldn't have had to let Miro go.

It was a thought he could entertain after enduring 20 years of superhuman discipline.

"Close ranks! We can't let him through here!"

As the giants holding back Gaold's atmospheric pressure shouted, more giants crowded in from the rear.

The weight ratio between Gaold and the giant unit was already over 1 to 1,000, but still, the giants were being pushed back.

"This… this is…!"

Gaold drew even more strength.

Slowly, slowly. He just needed to take one step at a time towards Miro.

'I hate you.'

Gaold's heart, now a massive entity larger than the giants, pushed the world.

'You said you could give everything…'

A god that gave nothing.

Tuk! Tuk tuk!

As the heat of extreme discipline rose from Gaold, his shirt began to burn.

His skin, covered in muscles, was adorned with countless scars that could only be etched through blind abuse.

"Hold the line! Don't retreat!"

The giants at the front shouted.

And with their final cries, their bodies burst under the pressure.

"Heh. Heh heh."

A hollow laugh escaped Gaold's torn lips. His black heart was laughing too.

He heard a voice mocking the world and sneering at God.

'Well, there's nothing to hate. From the beginning…'

Gaold's expression twisted as the atmospheric pressure pushing the giants turned into sharp blades and exploded all at once.

'You never existed!'

Boom!

Dozens of giants were blown to pieces, and a path opened.

Once he gained momentum, nothing could stop Gaold.

He tore through the mountain of bodies, finally crossing half the drawbridge.

As the giant unit's mages gathered their energy for a final strike at the end of the bridge, the heat distorted the scenery like a vortex.

'Gods…!'

Gaold, like a creature that could only charge, lowered his head and drew more power.

His heart, now a massive flame, collided with the flames conjured by the giant mages.

Boom!

The unimaginable explosion was clearly heard by Miro, 6 kilometers away.

Miro closed one eye and turned her head.

The hot wind Gaold was sending reached her.

Thump, thump.

Her heart beat faster.

Maybe… just maybe, he could really make it here.

"Raaaah!"

As the density of giants decreased beyond the drawbridge, Gaold charged madly.

He was getting closer, little by little.

"Go! Gaold! Goooo!"

Sein's voice reached him even in his frenzied state.

The distance to Miro was now 6 kilometers.

"All units, charge!"

At Surt's command, all the giants rushed towards Gaold.

Leading them was Uriel's three-horned Mara, Shiva.

'Foolish human…'

Shiva, wielding crescent swords in both hands, charged at Gaold.

The immense atmospheric pressure clashed first, but as the incarnation of destruction, his strength tore through the air, closing the distance.

"Raaaah!"

As Gaold and Shiva's power clashed, the other giants spread out, looking for an opportunity.

Approaching Gaold, who had single-handedly crossed the drawbridge, would mean missing a decisive chance and turning into a bloody mist.

No one used explosive techniques, but everything around them burst apart as they moved.

Gaold's abnormality, when faced head-on, was beyond Shiva's imagination.

Even as a three-horned Mara, he was Uriel's subordinate.

Being pushed back by a mere human was a first for him.

'No, it's not a matter of pride.'

Shiva acknowledged Gaold.

He was not human.

He couldn't be precisely defined, but he was transforming from a human into something else.

"You are a worthy opponent!"

Shiva pushed Gaold with all his might, and Gaold's combat power increased in response.

Miro was there.

His pain sensitivity reached its highest level in history, and every time the crescent swords cut his skin, it felt like his brain was being torn apart.

"Raaaah!"

Gaold advanced, crushing the vast space with atmospheric pressure.

The thought of defeating Shiva had no place in his mind.

He only moved forward. Towards Miro.

Everyone watching from the wall began to feel a growing sense of urgency.

The distance to Miro was now just 1 kilometer.

'I'm coming!'

Gaold increased his pain sensitivity even more.

Boom!

Even the solid ground was crushed under his pressure, forming a massive crater.

The distance to Miro was now 500 meters.

Shiva gritted his teeth and attacked with all his might.

Every swing of his crescent swords, moving at invisible speeds, created gusts of wind that bounced off Gaold's atmospheric pressure and spread in all directions.

Boom! Boom!

Everywhere Shiva's sword wind struck, dust rose, and massive scars over 40 meters in diameter were etched into the ground.

The distance to Miro was now 100 meters.

'I'm coming. I'm coming to Miro!'

Gaold, with his tongue out like a demon, screamed.

He had worshipped God, hated God, and finally erased God from his existence.

"Raaaah!"

He had endured all the world's scorn and ridicule to reach this point.

"Raaaah!"

He had finally arrived.

The cursed 20 meters that had never closed were now right in front of him.

'Now.'

Shiva's eyes flashed.

The only gap in the otherwise perfect Gaold.

At the critical distance of 20 meters, Shiva's crescent sword slashed Gaold's abdomen.

"Aaaargh!"

As his pain sensitivity skyrocketed, the physical impact became an excruciating agony that pierced Gaold's brain.

"Push him back!"

"Raaaah!"

The giants roared and all charged at Gaold.

Gaold quickly regained his senses, but he couldn't fend off the giants' overwhelming physical force.

No matter how much he trained, the giants' strength crushed him, intensifying the pain.

'I hate you… I hate you!'

Gaold's mind flashed back to 20 years ago.

Just like then, Miro was slipping away.

'I'll kill you! I'll mock your name, curse you, and erase you from the world!'

All he felt was hatred for God.

The madness of a human desiring the unattainable, blaming, hating, and ultimately killing a nonexistent God…

'Miro…'

Gaold looked at the wall through the giants' bodies.

He wanted her so desperately.

He wanted her to be his, more than anything.

'Miro…'

Gaold's right hand, trembling, reached out towards Miro.

'God…'

Once again, a human sought God.

'Why, why can't I have it? Even after all this pain, why can't I have it?'

The distance to Miro was 150 meters.

'I would live for eternity in the fires of hell. Just please, let me have her… just her…'

The distance to Miro was 200 meters.

"Push harder! More, more!"

Gaold coughed up blood.

He was filled with resentment, anger, and the frustration of seeing all his efforts turn to nothing. The knot of dark anger boiled up from his gut.

"Sob! Sob!"

Shiva, squeezing through the giants, glared at the sobbing Gaold.

'He must be killed here.'

Gaold, unaware of the sharp blade aimed at his heart, stared into the void.

On the day before the judgment of the 20, Miro's voice thundered in his mind.

Then save me.

"Ugh!"

Gaold's entire body shuddered as an immense discipline spread through him.

As the sea of fire spread to the horizon, his black heart roared, growing large enough to engulf the world.

"Raaaah!"

Pain.

10 million times.

Boom!

Everything surrounding Gaold fell like rain.

The ground shook and rippled, and the giants at the front were swallowed by the earth's gaping maw.

"Ugh! Ugh!"

Gaold's body trembled with pain. He stomped his feet and shook his limbs.

"Don't let him get close!"

Finally, Surt, who had been watching from the wall, leaped out.

Gaold flailed his arms and charged towards the wall.

The distance to Miro was 100 meters. 80 meters. 50 meters.

"Uooooh!"

As Surt, the greatest swordsman of purgatory, swung his flaming sword, Gaold extended his right hand.

Boom!

The air shield around his body blew away the flames, and Surt's eyes widened in shock.

"This… this is…"

As he stared at his sword, now reduced to a bare hilt, Gaold swung his hand.

With a thud, Surt's body exploded and vanished without a trace.

The distance to Miro was 20 meters.

"I'll kill you!"

The giants, now leaderless, charged in a frenzy.

But their rage was nothing compared to Gaold's hatred.

Boom! Boom, boom, boom!

Every giant on the ground died.

The only one left was the giant mage, Deputy Commander Urotas, but he was too terrified to descend from the wall.

"Huff! Huff!"

Gaold walked to the base of the wall, where no one else stood in his way.

6 meters. 4 meters. 2 meters.

Miro looked down at Gaold with an expressionless face.

His blood-soaked body looked far stronger than it had 20 years ago, covered in countless scars.

Finally, Gaold stopped in front of the wall and looked up at Miro.

The distance to Miro.

"Huff! Huff!"

None.
