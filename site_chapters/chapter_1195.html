Chapter 1195:

"Wash up first. If we go like this, no restaurant will even let us in."

Nade finally managed to escape Penna’s cloak but realized they had no proper place to wash.

"I have an idea."

Shirone led them to a secluded area and used Materialization to create a makeshift bathhouse.

Penna’s eyes widened.

"How did you do that?"

"It’s just an ability I have. Since Corkolas doesn’t use points, things like this are possible."

His explanation was brief, but Penna didn’t seem to care.

"I’ll be quick!"

As soon as she entered the bath, they could hear the sound of her gulping down water.

Shirone’s face turned pale.

Iruki noticed and asked,

"What’s wrong? This kind of thing shouldn’t be hard for you."

"Yeah, it’s not… but…"

The aftermath of constructing Shirone Sphere near the sun was affecting him.

Simultaneous events didn’t mean splitting his presence—every version of Shirone existed at 100 percent everywhere.

‘But Armand is not me. I cover it with mental power, but there’s still a limit.’

As the silence stretched, Iruki understood.

‘Something serious is happening.’

He didn’t pry further. Knowing wouldn’t help—there was nothing they could do.

‘We’re experiencing Melkidu, but for Shirone, this is just one of many incidents.’

Even now, he was controlling everything elsewhere.

‘Stay strong, Shirone.’

Iruki patted his back wordlessly, and Shirone felt a tightness in his chest.

‘Thank you.’

They wouldn’t know.

Of all the countless simultaneous events, this was the only place where he could actually smile.

Not because his friends were strong.

Not because Melkidu was easier than the other places.

‘Even if I fail, it’s okay.’

To his friends, he wasn’t the Infinite Mage, one of the Five Stars of the Ivory Tower, or Yahweh, the guardian of humanity’s fate.

‘To them… I’m just Shirone.’

Just a fellow graduate from Alpheas Magic Academy.

‘Even if I act sensitive sometimes, even if I stay silent… they don’t see me any differently.’

Because they understood why.

Feeling at ease for the first time in a while, Shirone looked up as Penna stepped out of the bath, shaking water from her hair.

"Finally, I feel alive again. Let’s go, I’m starving."

Shirone smiled.

"Yeah."

They headed to a nearby restaurant.

As Penna stuffed food into her mouth, she suddenly choked and gulped down water before speaking.

"What happened to Curtis?"

"Ah, that guy. It worked out. Apparently, he was kind of a big deal before. His crimes were so heavy that they didn’t send him anywhere else—they locked him up in the capital, Parme. He’s in the highest-security prison."

That didn’t sound like a good outcome.

"At least I avoided extra trouble. But when I realized I had no way to contact you guys, I almost lost it. I could’ve just stayed in the capital, but I had no way to reach you. So, I had no choice but to use "Loss of Will" to come here."

Shirone reassured her.

"You didn’t waste anything. We had to come here anyway. I think this place has clues about the bribery program."

"Really? Hmm. Yeah, that makes sense. If an underworld organization exists, there’s no better place than here. I was watching from the garbage heaps, and it was insane."

"What did you see?"

"Dismemberment. They were cutting up bodies like they were pork. No hesitation at all. But thanks to that, I picked up some intel about the Three Great Gangs."

Penna’s information was similar to what they had heard before but was more up to date.

"The ones who took control of the supply shipments this time are Johan Cartel. That’s why Desperado is furious. There’s going to be a huge war."

Shirone stood up.

"Alright, then we’ll head to Johan Cartel. The ones controlling the supplies must be in contact with the underworld organization."

"Ugh, Johan Cartel…"

Penna seemed like she wanted to say something but shook her head.

"Fine! Let’s go! There’s no such thing as a sane gang among the three anyway. The problem is finding their base. They won’t just let us waltz in."

Nade returned from paying the bill, looking annoyed.

"Dammit. Just for this meal, we had to spend a Bonanza item. Let’s just go outside and beat the info out of someone."

As they stepped outside, they saw that the entrance was blocked by gang members from the Three Great Gangs.

A man aimed a machine gun at them.

"It’s about time you made a choice. We’re not patient people."

Nade scanned their faces, then turned to his friends with a grin.

"This just got a lot easier."

As the others smirked, only Penna was trembling.

Johan Cartel.

Their headquarters was a monastery, filled with a solemn atmosphere—a stark contrast to the sound of gunfire.

"Is he a new recruit?"

In a dimly lit room, a woman in a nun’s habit was sipping from a liquor glass.

She was Aria, the boss of Johan Cartel.

"Yes, ma’am. He’s fresh—hasn’t been through any other gangs."

In front of several high-ranking gang members, a man in his mid-twenties bowed his head.

"Greetings! My name is Boto! I will serve you with all my devotion!"

Aria tilted her glass and asked,

"How exactly will you serve me?"

"… Excuse me?"

"How will you serve me?"

"Ah, well…"

Unable to handle Aria’s sultry gaze, Boto shifted his eyes toward a shelf.

It was filled with men’s undergarments with openings, whips, and dog collars connected by chains.

Aria snapped her fingers, bringing Boto’s gaze back to her.

"Listen carefully. Johan Cartel is the most historically significant organization in Melkidu. We are the only ones who directly inherit the legacy of Cain, the founder of Melkidu. Since ancient times, we have controlled this place, ensuring that our brothers in the real world remain strong."

"Yes."

"But then, at some point, the likes of Red Union and Desperado, these nobodies with no lineage, started crawling in and acting like they owned the place. I don’t like it. Do you get what I mean?"

"Don’t worry. I was a big shot outside. My hands are itching for some action."

At that moment, there was a knock at the door.

"Boss, we brought them."

"Let them in."

As Aria looked over Shirone’s group, her expression twisted in disgust.

"… What the hell is this?"

They weren’t just some gangsters. They were something entirely different.

"So, I hear you've been snooping around? What are you? Cops? Or are you looking for someone?"

Shirone spoke.

"Tell us about the underworld organization. We want to enter the Core through the back door."

"Ah…"

Aria’s gaze turned hazy.

"The back door. I like that."

"Heh."

Understanding the implication, Boto couldn’t hold back his laughter, making some of the gangsters turn toward him.

"Ahem, sorry."

Aria, who had been staring at him expressionlessly, placed her drink down and stood up.

"Come here."

Boto hesitated before stepping closer. Aria leaned in, grabbing his wrist.

"B-boss?"

"What’s wrong? You like this kind of thing, don’t you?"

She lifted the hem of her nun’s robe and guided his hand between her thighs.

"Feel this. Yeah. Hold it tight."

Looking down, Boto suddenly realized that he was gripping a dagger in reverse.

"Wait… why am I—"

Before he could finish his sentence, Aria forced his hand and stabbed the dagger into his stomach.

"Gah!"

He instinctively tried to push her away, but the blade had already sunk two-thirds of the way in.

"Aaagh! A knife! I—I've been stabbed!"

As he stumbled back, Aria grabbed a bottle of liquor and swung it full force.

With a loud crack, the bottle shattered against his head, sending him sprawling onto the ground.

"Aaaahhh! Aaaaagh!"

Blood gushed from his skull, and the knife in his stomach burned like fire.

"M-my stomach! There's a knife in my—"

"Heh. Then pull it out, dumbass."

As the gangsters kicked him like a toy, Boto writhed and screamed.

"Stop! No! I’m dying! Ahhh! Help me!"

Aria waved her hand dismissively.

"Shut up. Get him out of here."

Boto was dragged away by his legs, leaving a bloody smear across the floor. The wooden door slammed shut like the lid of a coffin.

"Hah… Now then."

Settling back into her chair, Aria spoke.

"The back door is my specialty. Among the Three Great Gangs, no one is better at breaking through than me."

Melkidu's system was believed to have been created by Cain, humanity’s first murderer.

Of course, the truth was in the Core, but Johan Cartel had honored his legacy for generations.

Both in the real world and in Melkidu.

"So you came to the right place. But what can you offer me in return?"

"Nothing."

"… What?"

Shirone spoke.

"I won’t do anything. That should be more than enough as a deal."

Aria’s expression went blank again.

"Do you even know what a gang is?"

"I don’t want to know."

Watching them torture their own people for fun had irritated him enough already.

"In this place, no one fears guns. Why? Because the moment you start fearing them, you’re already dead. You just pull the trigger. The one who shoots first lives."

Now he understood the mentality of the beach drifters.

"It’s like a game of Hold’em. Even if you have pocket aces, if someone goes all-in, you hesitate. The weak get eliminated. Only the ones with guts call the bet."

"What are you trying to say?"

Sensing killing intent, Shirone’s tone sharpened.

"I’m saying I’m going all-in, dumbass."

In an instant, Aria grabbed a machine gun, and every gangster in the room opened fire on Shirone.

Gunfire roared, and the dimly lit room flashed with bursts of flame.

Penna covered her ears and curled up.

"A-aah!"

She thought she was dead.

But no matter how long the shooting went on, she didn’t feel any pain.

She hesitantly peeked with one eye—then sat up in shock.

"W-what the hell!?"

One by one, the gangsters stopped firing.

The only thing left was the ringing in their ears.

"Who the hell are you?"

A haze of light particles surrounded Shirone, holding all 700 bullets in midair.

"Call."

The next moment, every bullet reversed direction, flying straight back at the gangsters at the same speed they were fired.

"Ugh!"

The bullets stopped just short of their foreheads, spinning at high velocity.

The gangsters froze in terror.

Shirone spoke.

"You said no one fears guns? No—you just can’t see what’s really happening."

"Now? Do you feel even a little scared?"

It was true.

"This is your last chance. The last chance for me to do nothing. Make your decision."

Aria bit her lip.

"… We lost."

The bullets ceased spinning and fell to the ground with heavy thuds.

"Hah… hah…"

As her subordinates collapsed in shock, Aria was relieved she was already sitting.

‘Cain…’

So it was true.

— Through his first murder, Cain discovered the efficiency of evil and created Melkidu's system. — He gave humanity hope—that any sin could be forgiven.

As a pureblood of Johan Cartel, Aria had learned many things from her predecessor.

— We kill. We plunder. The world calls it crime, but we call it sacred. — Evil is far stronger than good. It is purer, and it resides in the deepest part of humanity.

The nun of Johan Cartel had a duty to enter Melkidu and control Corkolas.

— But Aria, evil is powerful, yet it has limits. — It must always remain in the shadows. It must never long for the light.

— Why?

Back then, she couldn’t understand.

— Why can’t we shine? Cain gave all sinners hope.

Her predecessor left one final message.

— This is the ultimate secret that Johan Cartel must protect until the end. Never forget it.

When Cain left Melkidu, he left behind a single truth.

— There is no sanctuary for demons.
