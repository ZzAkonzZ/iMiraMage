Chapter 588:

The news of Eden's retirement spread among the Scramble Royal participants.

It was shortly after Quasar had exploded.

"Retired?"

Hershey looked stunned.

'Did they really break her barrier?'

In the Golden Wheel's simulations, Eden's defensive ability was unmatched among the graduating class.

"There's no more Infinity (○○○○○○)."

Iruki said with a smirk.

"Only Amy and Kaiden are left."

Hershey quickly calculated the hands.

Shirone's team's strongest hand was Sabina's ●●●● (Demon).

'Kaiden's hand is…'

○●○● (Democracy).

'Got it!'

Hershey shivered with excitement.

Only Iruki could calculate the strongest hand in the complex combination battle, but Hershey had another strategy for the worst-case scenario.

"Hahaha!"

Hershey burst into laughter, making Dorothy raise an eyebrow.

"What's so funny? Has he lost it?"

"Well fought."

Hershey said, calming down.

"Really impressive. Honestly, I thought it would be an easy win. I commend you for holding on this long."

"Do you think Kaiden can beat Amy?"

Hershey shrugged.

Unlike Eden, the Golden Wheel's simulations showed their combat abilities were evenly matched.

"It doesn't matter who wins."

Hershey was confident Scramble Royal was over.

"Again, the winner is Kaiden."

"Hand over the master card."

Amy extended her hand to Kaiden.

"Master card? Oh."

Kaiden blinked in realization.

"Hurry. You're not going to be stubborn now, are you? That's not like you."

"I don't have it."

"What?"

Amy frowned in confusion.

"This morning, Hershey took my master card without the team's knowledge. I can't call Kang, but your hand was lower anyway. That was Hershey's condition for letting me chase you."

"Wait. Hershey took your master card? The master card?"

Amy, suddenly realizing the implications, checked the participants' card statuses.

Kaiden's Democracy was the strongest.

'If Scramble Royal ends like this, the allied team wins.'

Amy's heart sank as she turned to Kaiden and shouted.

"You idiot! Why didn't you tell me sooner? We're going to lose!"

Kaiden was just as baffled.

"Why would I tell you? We're on different teams."

"Shut up, you fool!"

Amy kicked Kaiden's side, sending pain through his broken ribs.

"Ugh! What are you doing?"

"Oh, I can't stand this!"

As Amy turned to run, Kaiden reached out.

"Wait…!"

"Shut up! I'll deal with you after the match!"

His trembling hand reached for his broken ribs, and saliva dripped from his teeth.

"Ugh!"

Indeed, there was no sympathy for a mage.

"Kaiden doesn't have the master card."

Hershey declared triumphantly.

"I took it this morning. Even if Amy beats Kaiden, she can't reshuffle the cards. If Kaiden wins, the result is obvious."

A moment of silence followed.

"Now you understand? The allied team wins Scramble Royal."

"Iruki."

Dorothy turned to Iruki, who asked.

"Where's the master card?"

"Who knows?"

Hershey's body glowed with a flash.

Sona had been analyzed and couldn't be used, but teleportation couldn't be canceled.

"See you after the match."

At the same time, Sabina approached from behind and grabbed his neck.

"Don't move."

The sharp energy of Air Cut swirled at her fingertips.

"Is this your last desperate act? Pathetic."

Dorothy approached with Hickory.

"Hand over the master card. Or we'll use force."

Then the ground beneath Sabina wriggled, and a long centipede wrapped around her ankle.

"Damn!"

Pisho descended upside down from a tree on a spider.

"Let Hershey go. It's a venomous centipede."

"Don't be ridiculous. Do you think I'll die alone?"

Sabina tightened her grip on Hershey's neck, and Hickory's fist aimed at Pisho.

In this standoff, Sabina spoke.

"Search Hershey. We don't have much time. We need to find the master card."

Hershey spread his arms, amused.

"Haha! You never disappoint! But what if I don't have Kaiden's master card?"

"Shut up. We'll check."

Sabina's voice was filled with menace.

"Go ahead. Just because I received it doesn't mean I have it. Search all you want. If you find it in time, you win."

"Iruki, hurry!"

Dorothy shouted, and Iruki slowly approached.

"I don't usually let men touch me. You won't find what you want…"

"Hershey."

Iruki interrupted, looking up.

"Are you an idiot?"

"What?"

Hershey's face twisted in anger.

"Yeah! I'm an idiot! I'm an idiot!"

Amy turned back the way she came.

Exiting the forest, she found Kaiden still clutching his side and groaning.

"Hey! I just need to retire you, right?"

"That's why I told you to wait…"

"Shut up! We have less than two minutes!"

Grabbing Kaiden by the collar, Amy activated Schema and hoisted him onto her back.

"Ugh! Too rough."

"Who cares!"

Casting teleportation repeatedly, Amy landed near the magic school's main gate.

10 seconds remaining until the end of Scramble Royal.

"This is crazy!"

Calculating the distance, Amy ran with Kaiden on her back.

5 seconds, 4 seconds, 3 seconds…

Hearing Lulu's electric signal, she used all her strength to throw Kaiden.

"Go, Kaiden!"

2 seconds, 1 second.

As Kaiden's body crossed the gate, another signal came through.

Kaiden, out-of-bounds loss. Scramble Royal match time has ended.

The strongest hand is Demon (●●●●). The winning team is Shirone, Iruki, Nade, Amy, Dorothy, and Sabina.

Rewards and penalties will be applied through the judges.

"Huff! Huff!"

Only after hearing all of Lulu's signals did Amy collapse, supporting herself with her hands on the ground.

"Such a brute…"

Kaiden, lying outside the gate, writhed in pain.

"We won!"

Sabina and Dorothy dropped their combat stances and jumped in joy.

Pisho remained expressionless, while Hershey was in shock.

"Ah…"

It took a while for Hershey to realize how foolish his thoughts had been, meaning it hadn't even crossed his mind.

'Why did I do that?'

"When cornered, even absurd hopes seem positive."

Hershey's eyes turned to Iruki approaching.

"It's like insurance in a card game. Assuming a specific situation and betting on it is a good strategy, but it can force all assumptions into that situation."

"Hmph, stop acting smart…"

"Thinking of tricks…"

Hershey's face twisted, but Iruki continued, recalling Dorothy's advice.

"You lose sight of what's truly important."

"……"

Realizing his complete defeat, Hershey gritted her teeth.

"I'll see you at the graduation exam. I'll crush you."

"Just focus on helping us."

The condition was to support two members of Shirone's team in the graduation exam.

As Hershey teleported away, Sabina and Dorothy approached.

"Good job, Iruki."

"Yeah. Thanks to you two following the strategy."

Dorothy said.

"We'll discuss it when everyone's here, but the condition is tricky. Choosing two people."

Due to Kaiden's emotional response before Scramble Royal, Shirone's team had proposed the strongest condition, but now the reward distribution was unclear.

Iruki suggested.

"How about this? The two lowest-ranked among us before the graduation exam get the reward."

Sabina agreed.

"That's a good idea. Honestly, it's emotional, but I want all six of us to pass."

No one would deliberately lower their rank to get help from the Golden Wheel.

'Because we know what's truly important.'

Feeling grateful for being at the magic school, Dorothy smiled at the sky.

"Are you really leaving now? Your injuries seem severe."

Amy had caused them, but Kaiden knew her personality by now.

"I can use simple healing magic. I'll get professional treatment on the way."

Since Scramble Royal was illegal, he didn't plan to visit the infirmary.

"Okay. You'll come back, right?"

"I just want some fresh air. Visit home. Besides, my rank is quite high."

In hindsight, it was absurd.

"To drop below Nade's rank, I won't be back for at least a month."

Nade's graduation rank was last, and his return was uncertain.

"Haha! Right? Take care. I'll raise my rank in the meantime."

Kaiden smiled wryly and turned away.

"Say hi to Maya for me."

"No. Come back and tell her yourself. I'm uncomfortable around her."

"Hahaha!"

Suddenly, Kaiden threw his head back and laughed.

"You're a perfect match. You and Shirone."

Amy blushed.

"What nonsense? We're not even…"

"You broke my ribs, and Shirone did the same a few days ago."

"……"

"Beating me without giving my bones time to heal. I'm really tired of you two."

Amy asked nervously.

"Are you…"

"Don't worry. I won't interfere anymore. Enjoy your life."

As Kaiden walked away from the school, he looked back at Amy and smirked.

"Karmis."

Amy smiled back.

"Have a good trip, Cross."

The participants, still feeling the aftermath of the match, gathered around Iruki.

Shirone arrived with Eden, and Amy joined last.

"Phew, that was close, right? Everyone did a great job," Shirone said.

They all looked exhausted.

"How about we grab a bite before heading back?" Amy suggested, rubbing her growling stomach.

"I'm hungry too. But at this hour, no shops will be open, and the campus store is closed," Sabina agreed.

"Let's go to the campus cafeteria. I can make something with the ingredients there. I can cook simple dishes, and the followers said they were tasty," Eden offered.

"Eden, you?"

Despite losing the match, Eden looked more at peace than before.

"Feeding the hungry is also an act of love."

Iruki nodded. "Alright, let's go. Pisho, want to join us?"

Even after the allied team disbanded, Pisho remained.

"…I'm not lonely."

Pisho was the oldest in the graduating class.

"Who cares? Let's review the match. There might have been unexpected win rates. You handled the scrambles too."

"If you put it that way."

Though the pain of defeat was great, Pisho knew the importance of analyzing the reasons for failure.

As they headed to the cafeteria, Shirone called out.

"Wait. There's a problem."

"A problem?"

"Going into the cafeteria at this hour is against the rules."

"……"

Everyone stared blankly, and in the awkward silence, Iruki asked, "Are you serious?"

"Yes. What if we get caught and the teachers scold us?"

Everyone avoided answering and walked away.

"Wait! I'm really worried…!"

Hearing Shirone's hurried footsteps behind them, Pisho remarked, "That's Shirone for you."

"Of course. It's a magic school," Amy said, stifling a laugh.

"He's not normal either."

Scramble Royal ended.
