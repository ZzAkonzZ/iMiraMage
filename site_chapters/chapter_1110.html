Chapter 1110: The Unknown Depths of Humanity (2)

Maya stared blankly at Kaiden’s strange behavior.

Kaiden, lifting his head cautiously, noticed his right hand acting out again and quickly pulled his arm back.

“Damn it! This is driving me crazy.”

“Kaiden, are you okay?”

“Oh, um…”

There was nothing he could say.

“What’s going on?”

Other residents sharing the same corridor emerged into the hallway, and Reina, the leader of the troupe, entered the room.

“What’s the matter? What happened?”

Everyone’s gaze fell on Kaiden, who was sitting under a blanket, his face burning with embarrassment.

“Hm.”

Having grown up with brothers, Reina could guess what had happened.

“I thought something serious had happened. Maya, don’t worry about it. He’s just healthy, that’s all. But Kaiden, please be mindful of the noise at night.”

‘No, that’s not it.’

Kaiden wanted to salvage the situation, but his tongue failed him under the scrutiny of so many people.

Reina gently pulled Maya by the shoulder and headed for the door.

“Let’s go, Maya.”

As they walked out, she whispered, “I know you’re old classmates, but be careful. I’ve been watching him, and honestly, he seems a bit shady… maybe even perverted.”

“Ah, okay.”

Maya wanted to deny it, but she couldn’t be sure herself.

“All right, everyone, let’s head back to our rooms. We’ve got roll call in the morning, so get some rest.”

As Reina dispersed the group, Maya glanced back at Kaiden’s door in the now-empty hallway.

Meanwhile, Kaiden hurriedly pulled up his pants, nearly in tears.

“Damn it. You idiot.”

He thought it might have been better to run through the streets of Jive naked than to endure this.

As long as Maya wasn’t there to see it.

‘I should go back to Tormia. What kind of knight am I? Staying here will only cause trouble for everyone.’

He felt like someone destined to sit alone in a gloomy room, endlessly sketching the face of the woman he loved.

Then came a knock, followed by Maya’s voice. “Can I come in for a moment?”

Kaiden’s heart sank.

‘Not now. How could I face her?’

Just as he was about to refuse, Maya continued. “I really need to tell you something. It’s important.”

When Kaiden opened the door, Maya entered with a calm smile.

She looked around the room before turning to him. “How’s your hand?”

“Huh? Oh…”

Kaiden sighed as he looked at his troublesome right hand, then spoke honestly. “I’m sorry. It’s what you think it is. I made a fool of myself. I don’t even know how to apologize…”

“It’s fine. It’s not the first time.”

Maya knew about Kaiden’s uncontrollable right hand.

What she didn’t know was...

“What’s your true intention?”

“Huh?”

“You’re always kind to me, but your right hand always seems to have its own… inappropriate ideas. Honestly, I don’t know what you want.”

“No way! Inappropriate thoughts? I’d never dream of such a thing!”

Kaiden waved both hands, but only his left moved. His right hand formed an “OK” sign with his thumb and forefinger.

Maya’s expression turned blank as Kaiden’s right hand gave her a thumbs-up.

“Would you stop already!”

Kaiden yelled, hitting his right arm.

As another battle between his hands broke out, Maya spoke.

“Kaiden, I’ve been thinking a lot since your confession. But honestly, I… I still can’t forget Shirone.”

Kaiden’s right arm fell limp.

Whenever Shirone’s name came up, his right arm would always go still.

“I-I see. I wasn’t expecting anything anyway…”

“But,” Maya interrupted.

“I’m trying to move on. Shirone has Amy, after all. I can’t cling to him forever, can I?”

Kaiden’s heart raced.

“I can’t make any promises. But… can you give me a little time? Would that be too much to ask?”

“No, not at all. I can wait. I’ll wait forever—no, for eternity if I have to.”

It was a foolishly grand answer, but Maya smiled.

“Thank you for always caring about me. Good night.”

“Oh, yeah. You too…”

After Maya left, Kaiden sat frozen for a long time before collapsing onto his bed.

“She’s just pitying me.”

How pathetic and pitiful he must have seemed.

“Ugh.”

His eyes welled up.

“But she didn’t say no.”

For someone crushed by the weight of the Red Cross Star’s destiny, it was the first glimmer of hope Kaiden had ever known.

“She said it’s okay to wait.”

He felt himself breaking free from the confines set by God.

‘Maybe… maybe I can do it.’

Through his tear-blurred vision, his right hand moved closer and gently covered his eyes.

The incident in the artist's residence was a minor mishap, but Captain Rai of the guard was on edge.

‘No sleep for me tonight.’

Not that he was planning to sleep anyway.

As he passed through the artist’s district into the teaching area, he saw someone approaching in the distance.

Even from the limp in their gait, Rai recognized them.

‘Parka Kuan.’

Rai stood straight and saluted as Kuan approached. Kuan gave a brief nod in return.

“Where have you been?”

Without stopping, Kuan replied, “I just went for a walk to clear my head.”

Kuan’s formal tone made Rai uneasy.

“You don’t have to be so formal. It makes me uncomfortable.”

Though Rai outranked him, Kuan was a world-renowned swordmaster.

As Kuan passed without acknowledging his comment, Rai asked, “Why did you decline the position of Captain of the Guard?”

Kuan stopped.

“I hope I’m not being presumptuous, but I heard you had a reason. Even without that, I think you’d be better suited to the role than I am.”

‘Siena.’

Since arriving at the sanctuary, Kuan hadn’t met her even once.

A one-eyed, one-armed, limping man.

‘Heh.’

What dreams could those with intact bodies expect him to have?

“Do your best.”

As Kuan walked away, he muttered, “A one-armed man cannot fight to protect others.”

By the time Rai turned around, Kuan had disappeared from sight.

"Heretic!"

Vanisa, restrained by the Miracle Stream, shouted, his jaw dislocated and hanging loosely.

Shirone swung the light’s energy in a full arc, slamming Vanisa into the wall with a loud thud.

‘Oops!’

If he had been an ordinary human, every bone in his body would have shattered.

“Ughhhhh…”

Despite his broken form, Vanisa rose to his feet.

‘He’s a Denier.’

Trapped in the prison of his flesh, he had transcended death.

Unlike Rian, however, his body did not regenerate.

“Stop,” Shirone said sincerely.

“Not being able to die doesn’t mean you’re alive. If you keep destroying your body like this, you’ll just become a lump of meat.”

Like the living corpses of the Under World.

“Foolish wretch,” Vanisa growled, madness flashing in his eyes.

“Nothing will change.”

Charging again, now more like a monster than a man, Vanisa’s distorted figure was once again engulfed by the light’s energy.

“Gaaahhh!”

Wherever the light touched, black ash scattered.

‘As expected, it doesn’t purify.’

It only burned away, leaving nothing behind.

If the light of Agape continued to pour into him… would there be anything left of him at all?

“Give up. You’ve turned into a demon in human flesh. For demons, purification means annihilation.”

“Graaagh!”

Vanisa’s ribs all erupted from his body, extending like spider legs, with one broken rib hanging uselessly.

“Flesh, mind, spirit, emotions—”

The sharp, spear-like bones shot toward Shirone’s neck.

“We exist only for the glory of God!”

“Damn it!”

The light of Agape exploded, and Vanisa was hurled into the darkness of the corridor.

“Phew.”

Wiping the sweat from under his chin, Shirone approached to find Vanisa’s body charred like coal.

Through the gap in his dislocated jaw, a faint groan escaped.

“Ahhh…”

It seemed something remained.

“Where… am I?”

“In prison,” Shirone replied.

As Vanisa pondered his words, his glazed eyes fixed on the ceiling.

“Does this mean… I can leave now?”

Shirone couldn’t answer.

“I guess not. God has not forgiven me.”

This was the third immortal Shirone had encountered.

‘Why can’t they die?’ Rian, Gaold, and now Vanisa.

“I see it, Yahweh,” Vanisa croaked with his hoarse voice.

“God has determined your end. You will never win. You’ll shed tears of blood… hahaha.”

With a crumble, the muscles of his face, now coal-black, broke apart.

“Grk… grrk…”

Shirone stared at the lifeless body, which could no longer convey anything, and slowly turned away.

A wailing sound echoed through the air.

By 4:30 AM, lights flickered on one by one in the Delta headquarters.

By 5:00 AM, the corridors buzzed with activity as delegations from various departments of the sanctuary arrived in Delta.

“Ugh, so uncomfortable.”

Seriel from the World Health Organization stretched and spotted a familiar face in the distance.

“Huh?”

It was Amira, her classmate from magic school, now working for the World Climate Organization.

“Wow! Amira!”

“Oh, Seriel! It’s been ages. I’ve heard about you.”

As they exchanged updates, Dorothy and Liz, who had arrived early, approached.

“Hello, nice to meet you.”

Dorothy was Iruki’s representative, while Liz attended the sanctuary as a director of Neid’s group.

“So, is Iruki really not coming? It’s bound to come up during the public speech,” Dorothy said, shaking her head.

“I don’t know. There’s no word from him. Shirone hasn’t said anything, so I suppose it’s fine, but…”

Liz patted her shoulder. “Don’t worry. He’s probably just goofing off somewhere strange. That’s what guys like him do.”

As small talk continued, Seriel scanned the room.

‘Amy isn’t here?’

Then her expression stiffened.

“Well, well, look who it is.”

It was Fermi, sipping coffee as he approached.

Considering the number of people he had wronged in school, the women’s expressions naturally darkened.

But what Seriel felt went beyond that.

‘I didn’t want to see him.’

Liz spoke first. “Long time no see. Still as shifty as ever? You’ve finally wormed your way into politics after all that scheming at school, huh?”

Fermi shrugged. “Haha! If it helps the world, I’ll gladly throw myself into the fire.”

He winked at Seriel. “I read your paper. Your analysis on emotional disorders was excellent.”

“…What are you doing right now?”

“What?”

“Why are you talking to me? Do you think I’ve forgotten what you did? You turned countless people into drug addicts.”

“Which is why I’m talking to you. I’ve got a great idea…”

As Fermi reached to place a hand on her shoulder, Seriel swatted his arm away.

“Don’t touch me.”

As Fermi smirked, a staff member from Jive approached.

“The sanctuary’s official event is about to begin. Attendees, please make your way to the main hall.”

The women dispersed to their respective departments, and as Seriel passed Fermi, she ground her teeth and said, “You. Meet me later.”

Fermi watched her retreating figure with an amused grin.

“Still as cold as ever.”

Just like back in school.
