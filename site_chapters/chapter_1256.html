Chapter 1256:

In an unknown forest—

“Grrrraaagh!”

A burning skeletal knight pierced through the veil of the Under World and crossed into reality.

In his right hand, now stripped down to bare bone, he gripped a massive greatsword that remained unscathed by the flames.

“Kiieeee!”

Like a monarch of the damned returning from Hell, he slammed the sword into the ground, his hollow sockets staring ahead.

With a divine transcendental force, the earth trembled beneath him. His body gradually began to regenerate, and the flames slowly died down.

“Ughhh…”

As his skin regenerated, Rian shouted,

“Hot! Damn, that’s hot!”

He panted, brushing off the remaining embers with both hands, steam still rising from his scorched body.

“Huff… huff…”

The flames of Gehenna had finally cooled. But the fire he had set upon himself before entering the Under World had incinerated every last piece of his clothing.

“I made it back?”

Rian steadied his breath and glanced around. But he was standing in the middle of a forest in some unknown nation, completely lost.

“Well… Seongeum’ll probably come get me.”

He didn’t know the exact principles, but her mastery of spatial magic was second to none.

Lethe appeared out of the air.

“You’re a monster, too, you know.”

Landing lightly on the ground, she alternated her gaze between Rian’s body and the greatsword embedded in the earth.

“Escaping while purifying the flames of Gehenna… No wonder Yahweh trusts you.”

Rian scratched his head awkwardly at the sudden compliment.

“Well… I’m alright, I guess.”

“You’re more than that. Enduring Gehenna’s fire means you’ve overcome all the emotions of Hell. There were monks who self-immolated and achieved enlightenment that way.”

It was called zatal-ipmang, death by seated meditation.

“But you went beyond that and returned to the physical world. Your divine transcendence… may actually rival that of a god.”

Lethe truly believed it.

The Martial God, Yaksha.

At this point, there was likely no human alive who could defeat the Rian that had returned from Hell.

Rian picked up his greatsword and inspected the blade.

“Maybe… I guess I’ve gotten stronger. But I can’t say for sure until I actually fight someone.”

“It’s a mental domain. Until you meet someone who truly pushes you to the brink, you won’t realize it. But be cautious about your recovery rate. Restoring the signal of your Idea and denying death is almost an exploit—so there are limits. If it keeps stacking, your Idea itself might vanish.”

Rian was already aware.

“How long do I have?”

“I can’t say exactly. But don’t you have a sense of it?”

His feeling was— Not long.

“It’s fine.”

As long as he could hold out until Shirone fulfilled his purpose, the rest didn’t matter.

“And also…”

Lethe hesitated, then finally couldn’t hold back and yelled,

“Could you please stop talking so confidently in that state?! I may not be human, but I have a heart program! At least pretend to cover yourself! This is so annoying!”

“Hm.”

“‘Hm’? No! Do something already! Grab a leaf or something and cover up!”

‘Sheesh, so talkative.’

But now wasn’t the time to leisurely pick leaves—Ymir was coming.

Can I win?

He had grown stronger through his journey in Hell, but Ymir still loomed like an enormous wall.

Lethe stared at the deeply serious Rian, dumbfounded.

‘What kind of guy is this?’

Jincheon Sector of the Holy War.

As Jin Kang’s body was moved according to imperial law, Anchal approached Seongeum.

“Your Majesty.”

Though she was now Empress by Jin Kang’s decree, Anchal’s respectful tone still felt strange.

“Speak comfortably. I haven’t gotten used to it yet.”

“You must get used to it. The fate of Jincheon’s people—and of all humankind—is in Your Majesty’s hands.”

Seongeum nodded.

“I understand. What should I do first?”

“First—”

Anchal quickly explained everything that had happened in reality while she was in Hell.

“A Simultaneous Event, huh.”

Once again, Shirone proved himself. The more you learned about him, the more boundless he seemed.

My zero-step.

Knowing what that smile meant, Anchal felt a pang of sorrow.

He’s the perfect match for her…

Seongeum regained her composure.

“I understand. Since the Simultaneous Event is over, we’ll need to gather everyone around the world and bring them here.”

“Indeed. Every second counts. Your role is the most important.”

In terms of war logistics, Seongeum’s spatial magic was unparalleled in transport and supply.

“Understood. I’ll go to Rian first.”

Closing her eyes, Seongeum sent out an Ether Wave to scan for spatial coordinates.

‘There.’

He was quite far away, but she bent space and collapsed the distance to zero between them.

“Here I go.”

The forest materialized inside the building as Seongeum gracefully crossed into its boundary.

One minute later…

“Your Majesty?”

Seongeum returned alone, her face as red as a beet. Anchal frowned.

“What happened?”

“Oh…”

“‘Oh’?”

With a trembling voice, Seongeum said,

“Bring… bring some clothes. The biggest size you can find. Hurry.”

Galliant Island.

Deep within Mount Toa, home to the Kergo Tribe, Shirone was speaking with Gaold and the others.

“So that’s how it is.”

The betrayal of the Dreamwalker Luber, and even the administrator who had once sided with humanity, had been acting under the will of God.

Miro didn’t seem fazed.

“If we’re going to fight God anyway, it doesn’t matter how many more enemies we have. The problem is we didn’t extract the Ultima.”

It must have weighed on her heart too—that Arius had risked his life to succeed.

Shirone spoke.

“There’s still… one method left.”

“A method?”

“We failed to unify humanity using an Object, but that doesn’t mean Ultima is out of reach. If all of humanity joins minds…”

Sein cut in.

“That’s just idealism. Nothing’s changed from before we entered Ymir’s mental world.”

“There is one difference. Tachyon,” Miro interjected, catching on quickly.

“You…”

“Yes. I can’t guarantee success. But this is our only hope.”

Gaold asked, “Didn’t you say Ymir was coming?”

Shirone nodded.

“Planet Hexa has been annihilated. Considering his size, he’s probably already crossing the galaxy.”

“How long will that take?”

“Depending on his will, it could be any moment now.”

Shirone continued, “First, we have to solve the immediate problems before we can reach Ultima. Ymir. Cellbuster. And the OOPArts. If humanity becomes an object under God’s command, this world will lose all will.”

Kangnan said, “I don’t get everything, but I get the gist. So we just go to the Holy Temple, right?”

“Seongeum will come to get us. And I’ll wrap up the simultaneous event. But before that…”

Shirone turned toward the temple door.

“We should check.”

The faint smell of blood couldn’t be ignored.

Kangnan stepped forward to push the stone door, but it didn’t budge—it had clearly been locked from the outside.

“No good. Should we break it down?”

As soon as she spoke, Gaold’s air gun fired past Kangnan’s shoulder and struck the stone door.

Boom!

But the door didn’t move an inch.

When Kangnan turned back with a fierce glare, Gaold just grinned and shrugged.

“Heh, this is interesting.”

Sein said, “Something’s off. It’s like it’s immune to physical force. But is that even possible?”

“Maybe it’s just because that guy’s a weakling.”

Kangnan shot back in petty revenge, but she too knew the truth.

‘If Gaold couldn’t break it…’

Then it really couldn’t be broken.

“I’ll try.”

Shirone stepped up to the door.

The Miracle Stream transformed into the Hand of God, and a massive hand grasped the door.

“Ugh…”

The moment he sensed it through his mind, Shirone realized the nature of the door.

‘Obsession. Desire.’

A vast emotion was insisting this door must be protected.

‘I’ll tear it off.’

Creak...!

With intense focus, the stone door groaned ominously as it began to crumple.

It sounded like someone screaming in agony. Kangnan shivered for no reason.

Crack!

Shirone laid the square slab of stone on the ground and looked down the passage.

Everyone was dead.

Sein examined the ancient runes inscribed around the torn edge of the door.

“It was completely sealed. What happened here?”

Miro, checking the corpses, spoke.

“Clean. The work of a professional. And they haven’t been dead long—six hours at most.”

“Over there!”

At the end of the corridor, Kangnan pointed as a white figure disappeared around a corner.

Shirone dashed forward.

“Wait!”

As he rounded the corner, another white figure turned down the next hall.

‘That thing…?!’

Determined now, Shirone teleported, instantly closing the gap.

“You! Who are you—”

But when he finally got a clear look, Shirone couldn’t finish his sentence.

The figure, with a body like a plastered human, stood with its back turned, its flesh pure white.

It was a strange sight, but that wasn’t what made Shirone hesitate.

‘It’s dangerous.’

Ultima’s senses—or perhaps Tachyon’s signal—were giving a clear warning: Do not approach.

“Identify yourself.”

Shirone asked again, but it seemed like a difficult question for the white figure.

“Hmm. Who am I?”

From both hands extended twin blades, smooth as ivory, fused directly with his arms.

Even that seemed fascinating to him.

‘What is he…?’

As Shirone was still processing, Gaold and the others finally caught up and stepped forward.

“Did you kill the villagers?”

“Don’t come closer.” Shirone raised a hand to stop them.

None of them would lose in a fight, but the sense of wrongness here was beyond anything they’d encountered.

“I don’t know.”

After a long pause, the white figure finally spoke.

“I don’t have a name.”

Nameless.

‘No way…’

A memory flashed through Shirone’s mind—an anecdote Armin once told him about Heaven.

A hybrid of an angel and Garas, an unimaginable creature had once been born.

‘Thankfully, Kuan struck it down before it could grow stronger.’

Armin had added:

—If we’d been even a little later, no one could’ve stopped that thing.

So then why had this revenant returned?

‘Damn it.’

Shirone understood.

‘When I connected to the outer world…’

This was a special administrator sent by God to correct an error—a kind of vaccine.

‘If Armin was right, the Nameless one can absorb the abilities of powerful beings and eliminate them.’

A natural genius in the truest sense.

One that could make all previous geniuses look like frauds.

‘To copy a spontaneous mutation from reality—does God seriously have no imagination?’

It was just a bitter complaint about the worst possible enemy.

Of course, for a god who had mastered all the laws of the universe, imagining something beyond those laws was impossible.

The Nameless one said,

“I don’t know who I am. I don’t know how I was born or why I’m here. What I do remember…”

He spoke carefully, as if dredging something up from the depths.

“The terror of a terrible death…”

And something so exhilarating it made him forget even that death—

“Blade dancing.”

His eyes lit up.

‘It’s him.’

“That guy…”

Kuan.

Ten kilometers from the Holy War, in a forest, Siena had gathered some fruit.

“Try this. I spent all day collecting it.”

“Siena…”

Kuan spoke.

“I’ll protect you. I will… protect you.”

Siena’s eyes welled up with tears.

“Me… protect me, Siena…”

Once hailed as a genius of the sword beyond human comprehension, he was now a fool who had lost everything.
