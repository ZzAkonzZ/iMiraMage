Chapter 763:

“Commander.”

Beshum, commander of the 7th Division under the 24th Legion, approached in clanking black steel armor.

“Our soldiers are attacking one another.”

Lord of Ruin, Maraduke—an impossibly beautiful man—sat upon the throne with a faint smile on his face.

“Can it be contained?”

Beshum immediately prostrated himself.

“Leave it to me!”

“Hmph.”

As Maraduke rose from his seat, the other three division commanders standing by offered their salutes.

“An intriguing ability. Infection, was it...?”

What he saw through the window was not an army but a massive wave of indistinguishable chaos.

“Aaagh! Die! Just die already!”

Infected: 165,843.

Maraduke bared his fangs in a grin as the infected demons slaughtered their own.

“A truly demonic sight.”

As they savored the irony, all four division commanders silently observed the devastation outside the palace.

Infected: 325,574.

On a scale of millions, the wave of infection spread in rippling arcs, pushing toward the leader’s palace.

“Clean it up.”

Maraduke gave his command decisively.

“They’re worthless now.”

The situation had passed the point of no return. Once infections exceeded a million, even division commanders would find it exhausting to suppress.

“We obey.”

Meanwhile, Shirone and Minerva walked through the battlefield, eyes fixed on the distant leader’s palace.

“Kill yourself! As soon as you're bitten, take your own life!”

Demon brigade commanders shouted threats, but the chain of command had already collapsed.

Some demons took their own lives, but statistics always regress to the mean. Those who missed their chance began attacking others, and the infection snowballed.

Infected: 653,329.

Walking through the path the infected demons cleared, Shirone swallowed hard.

It’s over. This spell is terrifying.

While the high population density helped, even if they were spread out, total annihilation would’ve just been a matter of time.

Which is why it hurt.

A spell of fratricide.

When Minerva had first revealed this spell to the world, humanity was pushed to the brink of extinction.

It was originally created to wipe out humans.

What depth of hatred must a person hold to conceive of a spell like this?

“Don’t get all sentimental.”

Minerva exhaled a puff of smoke and gave a crooked smile.

“You’re flattering me too much.”

“…”

As Shirone was lost in thought, an explosion boomed from within the leader’s palace.

“Looks like they finally realized their house is on fire.”

At the tip of Minerva’s pipe, four figures glowing with a visible violet aura appeared.

“Division commanders.”

As she finished speaking, a demon in black steel armor stepped forward and swung his sword.

“Pitiful wretches.”

The sword's path, which should’ve ended in an afterimage, remained writhing in space and sent out a massive shockwave.

“Aaaargh!”

Over 300 demons were slain in a single strike, and the other commanders also began exterminating their own troops.

“A countermeasure, huh.”

By eliminating the infected before it spread further, they could at least keep the horde from breaching the palace.

“A fittingly demonic judgment. But it won’t be enough.”

The infected had already surpassed the number of uninfected.

“They might buy themselves time. But look.”

She pointed to the top of the palace, where the 24th Legion Commander Maraduke was glaring down at them with an irritated expression.

“He’s handsome.”

Minerva’s voice turned ice cold.

“He’ll be strong.”

There was no denying the infernal energy blazing off him like fire.

“I’ll take the commander. You handle the division leaders.”

Shirone shook his head.

“No. This is my job. I’ll take care of the legion commander—you handle the division commanders.”

Minerva scoffed.

“You ever fought a legion commander before?”

He hadn’t.

“You know how strong a division commander is. But a legion commander is on a whole other level. The finest product born from the concentrated essence of Ma.”

Shirone recalled the memory of the Lord of Terror, Igor, tormenting the mercenary corps in Radum with his “brain in a vat” projection.

“Demons born from Ma, infused with aspects of Satan—that’s the army of Hell. From division commander upward, their individuality becomes so grotesque it defies logic. That’s a different conversation from Nane’s righteousness.”

Satan was a being who existed outside the laws of the universe.

“Nane’s sermons are powerful because they’re right. His tongue becomes a sword of truth that pierces the world. Meanwhile, your Yahweh is powerful because he can make what’s wrong no longer seem wrong.”

All the laws of the world loved Shirone.

“That’s what makes you the Infinite Magician. Anything imaginable can be made real through the light of Yahweh. So, does that make you a god?”

He was not.

“You may wield divine power, but you’re still human. A god doesn’t imagine—He simply is. He created everything already.”

If Shirone were truly a god, then even someone like Jayce wouldn’t be forced to accept a world filled with suffering.

“You talk big, but the truth is, you’re strong. You’re probably the only one whose infinity can stop the massive righteousness of Nane. But even that’s a battle of scalability. In terms of depth…”

Her voice was firm.

“I don’t think your level has surpassed Miro or Veron. At least not yet.”

That, too, was true.

“You’re only twenty. I followed your lead because I saw potential—not because I believed you could save the whole world right now. Same goes for Jayce’s situation, doesn’t it?”

With all demons except the top few commanders infected, Minerva began to move forward.

“Leave it to me. I’ll cover the rear.”

Shirone placed a hand on her shoulder.

“Please let me do it.”

“…This isn’t a game. If you die, humanity ends. Not that I care personally.”

“I won’t die. Death doesn’t save anyone.”

Minerva clicked her tongue and turned away.

“Fine, do what you want. It’s your world—you can boil it or poison it for all I care…”

“Don’t worry. I survived against an archangel in Heaven, didn’t I?”

Minerva gave a bitter smile.

“Angels that can't be destroyed by System Destruction…”

“System Destruction?”

“Anke Ra is the master of that system. No owner would willingly set fire to their own home.”

“I don’t get it.”

“Ask the Great Star. She’ll explain why Anke Ra had no choice but to seal Ymir.”

Spinning her jet broom, Minerva crouched low, ready to launch.

“If you insist, I won’t stop you—but be careful. A legion commander can withstand Yahweh.”

With that, Minerva rocketed forward on her jet, and Shirone, shelving his questions for now, launched himself as well.

Defeat the legion commander, and the war ends.

With a beat of his Light Wings, he shot straight toward the spire of the palace where Maraduke waited.

A sonic boom erupted behind Minerva, but without looking back, Shirone faced his foe.

“Yahweh, is it?”

The legion commander’s blade stood vertical, and black energy crept up the hilt and engulfed the edge.

"Dangerous."

As Maraduke swung his sword, the landscape split in two, and a line ten meters thick extended all the way to the horizon.

“What is that...?”

Shirone, using temporal displacement to avoid it, landed atop the opposite spire and scanned the ground below.

There was no sound, no shockwave.

The perfectly straight line, as if drawn with a ruler, was a trench of unfathomable depth.

How deep is it?

One kilometer? Ten? Perhaps more.

“You can’t be impressed this early.”

When Shirone slowly turned his head, Maraduke was already there, holding a black orb aloft in his right hand.

“The Ma of Ruin.”

The orb, oily and viscous, plummeted downward, piercing through the ceiling of the leader’s palace.

“…”

With Spirit Zone, Shirone confirmed that the orb continued piercing through layer after layer, tunneling through the ground.

It’s still falling…

A phenomenon that defied the laws of physics.

“Don’t worry. It can’t pierce a star.”

Shirone turned back toward Maraduke.

“It’ll just… hurt a little.”

Because he existed outside the Law, he was unlike Nane. Even the Infinite Mage could only push his imagination to the limit.

How do I stop this…?

Far away, the Great Star clutched her body in pain.

“Ugh!”

The tremendous shock dealt to the star was transferring directly to her body.

“Are you alright?”

Approaching her inside the Grand Sanctuary of Earth was Star Mahoro Amanta, another member of the Five Stars.

The highest-ranking star of the Department of Balance, he was a slender, bald man with brown skin and a face lined with deep crow’s feet.

A wheel spinning rapidly between his thumb and forefinger gave off the sound of winding gears, drawing the Great Star’s gaze.

The World Wheel.

Mahoro’s name meant “He who turns the World Wheel.” The sound meant he was ready to act, if necessary.

The Great Star forced a smile.

“Don’t worry. This isn’t enough to shatter me.”

After all, she had endured even the collision of an asteroid in the distant past.

“Indeed. Demons can’t shatter you, and reaching Overclock is far beyond their means. I simply meant there’s no need for you to suffer needlessly.”

Amanta always pursued moderation, and he felt the current path of Ma had gone too far.

“You sound like Theraphos.”

“…We share the same philosophy. If you don’t abide by the rules, you cease to exist. I’m neither on the side of Ma nor humanity.”

As the gears spun rapidly, the Great Star’s sorrowful face reflected between the spokes.

“I just hope I’m not the one making you sad.”

When Amanta clasped the wheel with his hand, the sanctuary grew preternaturally still.

“Take care.”

Leaving those words behind, he vanished. The Great Star turned her eyes to the projection of North Aymond flickering across the ceiling.

“Shirone…”

Judgment Day was drawing near.

Angel’s Judgement!

Hundreds of radiant archangels hurled their lances of light, scorching the earth below.

The leader’s palace had already lost its shape, and the capital—once a testament to a dictator’s greed—had become a wasteland.

I can’t get him…

Each time Maraduke’s sword blurred through the air, the heads of countless Shirones were lopped off.

All of them were him—but sacrificed for the sake of protecting the original body.

The real issue was the ruinous energy the sword left behind like ink, erasing anything it touched.

Homing Photon Cannons!

Two hundred photon blasts arced toward Maraduke, but he had no reason to move.

“Veil of Ruin.”

A black liquid enveloped him, and the photon blasts vanished into silent ripples of light.

“I misjudged you.”

Maraduke let his sword hang at his side, preparing for a wide horizontal swing.

“You’re not even worth stringing up as a trophy.”

As his sword art, Apocalypse, unfurled, black liquid slashed outward in massive arcs.

Spatial-temporal displacement!

Shirone passed through the trajectory of the strike and entered the Underworld via Choeoni Bardo.

So this is it!

If it ignored physics, then it had to be an ability triggered from the realm of the bat.

As expected, the black liquid was spreading even into the Underworld—but its form there was different.

“What… is this?”

Thanks to buying time with a Materialized shield, Shirone could now sense the presence through Spirit Zone.

Tiny organisms were eating through metal.

Nanotech…

So numerous they dissolved steel in an instant. Shirone lunged away in alarm.

“You think knowing changes anything?”

Maraduke tracked Shirone’s position in the Underworld and swung his blade in the real world.

“Of course it does.”

Dark Sphere.

Tenfold Superposition.

The divine particles condensed into a terrifyingly dense point, creating a black hole with an 8-meter gravitational radius.

KIEEEEEE!

The black liquid, still spiraling mid-arc, was sucked in with a shrieking wail.
