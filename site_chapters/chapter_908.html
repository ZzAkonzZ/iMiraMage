Chapter 908: Ultimately Human (1)

Amy and Nane sat by a campfire.

Fish caught from a nearby river were roasting on wooden skewers.

'Looks delicious.'

Amy's stomach growled in response to the signals from her brain.

"O"

Nane laughed and said,

"It seems our meal is quite modest."

For Amy, who was used to high-calorie military food, Nane's diet was indeed lacking.

"Just talk comfortably. We're about the same age."

"I'm two years younger."

Given his talent as the Buddha of the era, there was no need to ask, but hearing his age still stung.

"You're just an adult? You're a real genius."

"It's not a matter of time."

"No, it is a matter of time. The faster your thinking speed, the slower time flows for you."

If Nane fully utilized his brain functions, time would almost stand still for him.

"Don't praise me too much. Shirone's insight is just as impressive. His reasoning surpasses logic."

Amy's expression softened.

Hearing Nane acknowledge Shirone brought an inexplicable sense of relief.

"Can't you avoid fighting?"

The world was heading towards catastrophe, but she felt the answer might be close.

"Can't you resolve things with Shirone through dialogue? He's a reasonable person. I'm sure you could find another solution."

"I know."

Nane felt the same way.

"But it's not necessary. Even now, countless Shirones and countless Nanes are debating in this world."

It was a world overflowing with opinions.

"Many people are suffering in the process. Dialogue isn't needed. It's time to enforce our beliefs."

"I don't understand."

She understood intellectually, but...

"The reason I fight for humanity is because I want to be happy. I don't understand why Shirone and you carry so much burden."

"Yes. That's exactly how humans think."

"You're human too."

Nane fell silent.

"You said you'd love me to understand Shirone. I can't understand that, but I think I get one thing."

Amy spoke with a sympathetic expression.

"You're really lonely too." Nane's eyes wavered.

"No one can understand your enlightenment. That's why you carry everything alone..."

Just like Shirone.

"At least I can acknowledge that you're fighting a lonely battle. So when it's hard, you can give up, and when you're sad, you can cry. You're human before you're Buddha."

At that moment,

A single tear rolled down Nane's cheek.

'Poor person.'

She wasn't surprised to see Nane's tears because she had already seen Shirone's tears.

These were not the tears of Buddha but of the human Nane.

"I'm sorry. This is..."

As Nane tried to wipe his tears, Amy approached and gently reached out her hand.

"I really don't understand."

Her thumb wiped away the tear under Nane's eye.

"You're so different..."

Two people who were truly different,

"But you cry the same way."

Seeing Amy look at him with the same eyes she used for Shirone,

Nane's heart began to race.

Gaold regained consciousness.

"Where am I?"

He lay sprawled in the middle of a dense forest with no sign of human presence.

"How much time has passed?"

He asked himself, but his brain, which had already discarded memories,

gave no answer.

"Grrr!"

As pain surged again, nerves along Gaold's neck tingled unpleasantly.

'It's coming again.'

Life was overwhelming him once more.

"Aaaah!"

Because he was alive,

The sheer pain he had to endure for that reason alone paralyzed his mind.

"Hehehe! Hehehe!"

Even as he shed bloody tears, laughter escaped from Gaold's lungs.

"Want to hear something funny?"

No matter how much it hurt,

"I won't die."

What made Gaold who he was now wasn't the hallucinatory pain of mutation but his endurance of it.

As the pain showed signs of subsiding, Gaold crawled on all fours and leaned against a tree.

In the pleasure that followed the pain, his vision blurred and he saw unknown illusions.

To reduce shock, his brain had been releasing far more endorphins than normal for some time now.

Immersed in pleasure, Gaold let out foolish laughter while his limbs trembled.

The reason he had sent Kangnan and Zulu away was that he didn't want them to see him like this.

In the flickering scenery like electricity, he saw an illusion of Miro crying with a sad face.

Don't cry,

That's just how life is,

Don't cry,

As Gaold's dilated pupils began to slip back into oblivion,

Minerva pointed out a devastating scene on the ground as she flew Great Star in a jet,

My goodness...

Gaold had left marks as if meteors had descended from the sky, creating a series of craters along 100 kilometers.

Riding on Amanta's world ring, Freed muttered with a sickened expression, "This is the power of a planetary scale. How can anyone withstand this?"

Minerva, pale as a sheet, turned to Great Star and said, "Just hold on a little longer. We'll be able to catch up soon."

Xing remarked, "Gaold's rampage has cost us time. If he won't cooperate, we might have to deal with him."

The Ivory Tower's Stars, initially heading straight for Rian, had to abruptly change their course due to the unexpected breach.

'I would normally ignore it but…'

If they hadn't blocked Gaold's frenzied magic, the planet itself might have been destroyed.

"It's over there."

As they passed an area that had become a wasteland from forests and rivers, lush forests appeared again.

"Found him."

As Xing detected Gaold with the law and changed direction, the rest followed.

"Hah, hah."

When they reached the forest, Gaold was shaking violently, having expelled all sorts of filth.

"Damn it."

The mage of the breach, battering Great Star, was, against expectations, merely a lump of flesh in human form.

"He's completely broken."

"Kekeke."

Gaold, briefly regaining focus, laughed upon seeing Great Star and the Ivory Tower's Stars.

Though he recognized their authority, his mind was already saturated with endorphins.

"Hey, where's the Central Continent?"

No one answered Gaold's question.

"I need to go to the battlefield, but as you can see, my brain’s fried. Where exactly is this place?"

In reality, Gaold was heading towards the Central Continent, but Minerva did not want to disclose this.

“Michea Gaold, former president of the Torumia Magic Association."

Even hearing his formal title, Gaold just blinked vaguely and coughed aimlessly.

"We are the Stars of the Ivory Tower. And this is Great Star, the pinnacle of the Ivory Tower and the avatar of the planet."

Gaold’s gaze shifted to the pale Great Star.

"Nice to meet you."

Despite the struggle, Great Star managed a smile, but Gaold’s gaze indifferently shifted away.

"So what?"

Freed grasped the handle of his sword and stepped forward, but Minerva stopped him.

"I respect you as a mage who has reached the realm of destruction, but in this uncontrollable state, you pose a threat to Great Star."

Gaold listened silently.

"You're heading to the Central Continent, indicating you still have the will to fight. So I ask, for what are you fighting: good, evil, or love?"

"Keke."

Unable to hold back, Gaold couldn't suppress the laughter welling up inside.

"You're talking nonsense."

Maintaining utmost respect, Minerva inevitably frowned this time.

Gaold lifted his chin and said, "You really cherish your beliefs, huh? Decide on something, follow it, and feel like you've become something grand, huh?"

Amanta turned the world ring.

"Choose your words carefully."

It was a clear threat, but Gaold compressed the air before him with a smirk.

"How should I live? Just breathing each day is already killing me."

"Good, evil, love?"

As he pressurized the fist-sized air bullet, the atmosphere violently shook.

"What do I care about all that!"

Boom!

The air gun fired with a deafening sound, and the Ivory Tower's Stars sprung into action.

"Phew!"

Suddenly armed, Freed grounded himself tens of meters away.

'If that had hit, I'd be gone.'

As if a giant awl had pierced through nature, a tunnel extended along its path.

"Ha, ha."

Gaold, gasping for breath, had his eyes roll back.

"Ugh!"

Once again, as pain assaulted him, causing his nerves to flail wildly, his limbs shook violently.

"Poor human."

Minerva, with Great Star on her shoulder, returned to her original spot and looked at Gaold.

"Don't bother. You've done enough, haven't you? Thanks to you, the world has been saved. It means you can rest now. Besides, the woman you love…"

Minerva hesitated for a moment.

"... has already forgotten you and become a true paragon of virtue."

"Paragon of virtue... Really?"

He had left thinking it would be so, not particularly shocking.

But.

"You don't know Miro."

Gaold had no intention of stopping.

"Yeah, you'll never understand…"

As another wave of pleasure substances surged in, the vision of Miro crying flickered.

"Ah."

As filth leaked from Gaold's trousers, Xing grimaced and covered her nose.

"Shouldn't we handle this here? He seems out of his mind. He's hardly human anymore."

"Hardly human?"

Gaold grinned, looking at Xing.

"Eat."

His gaze shifted to Freed.

"Sleep."

And then back to Minerva.

"And crap."

As pleasure overwhelmed his senses, Gaold trembled and asked with a sneer,

"Do you think you're any different? Tell me, Stars of the Ivory Tower. Do you feel like grand beings just because you sit atop the world, sitting on the toilet, crapping, babbling about the universe and good and evil... Hehehe!"

Minerva's expression soured.

'He's completely mad.'

Gaold's laughter abruptly stopped.

"That's enlightenment. What you think is farthest from you is actually inside your belly. Get it, you most expensive crap machines in the world?"

"I've decided."

Freed gripped his sword and advanced.

"We'll deal with it here." As if expected, Gaold flicked his wrist limply on the ground.

"Come on, then."

Slumped against a tree, he seemed incapable of any combat.

"What are you waiting for? Come at me."

Behind the maliciously grinning Gaold, an endless inferno unfolded.
