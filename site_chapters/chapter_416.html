Chapter 416:

Gaold barged into the Mecha tribe's ranks like a bandit, looking down at them with a commanding presence.

"You're rebels, aren't you? Where's your headquarters?"

Finding the rebels' main base was one of the reasons they were heading to the mainland, so getting information from these people would save them some trouble.

However, contrary to his expectations, the woman's face, which had been filled with fear just moments ago, changed upon hearing Gaold's voice.

She stood up abruptly, raising her arc and shouting.

"It's the munitions community! Everyone, arm yourselves! We die here today!"

Following the squad leader's orders, the soldiers took up fighting stances.

With their sigmas and exds at the ready, their expressions showed a resolve to face death.

"Leave this place immediately! Or you'll become food for the arc."

The arc's laser was aimed between Gaold's eyes.

A long-range weapon of the Mecha tribe that fired explosive orbs.

But the woman knew it wouldn't work against him.

'I've staked my life on this, so I might as well try to bluff.'

"Heh. Heh heh heh."

Gaold's shoulders shook with laughter, his face wrinkling as if air had been pumped into his lungs.

When he looked up again, his eyes blazed with an unearthly fire.

"You're annoying me with your babbling."

The Great Inferno engulfed the Mecha tribe.

Whether it was a misunderstanding or not didn't matter. Gaold wasn't kind enough to spare those who dared to challenge him.

Caught in the inferno, the Mecha tribe soldiers turned pale, drooling and trembling.

The screams of the damned were so chilling they felt like they could tear their eardrums apart.

It was a fear beyond what humans could endure.

Even the squad leader, who had faced life-and-death situations many times, could only sit down, her jaw trembling.

"Please... stop..."

Only after the Mecha tribe lost their will to fight did Gaold release his restraint.

The tear-streaked woman looked up at him with resentment.

"Why are you tormenting us? We're fighting against Heaven. You're humans too, aren't you?"

"I don't know what nonsense you're talking about, but I'm not from the munitions community. And weren't you the ones who used to call border dwellers 'mockers' and look down on them?"

"That... that was..."

As the woman tried to retort, trees began to fall in the forest behind the Mecha tribe.

Everyone's eyes turned to the source of the noise.

Something massive seemed to be approaching rapidly, but it was still out of sight.

-Magnetic field deactivated. Argon engine activated.

The forest scenery shimmered, revealing the outline of an enormous bipedal armored vehicle.

Standing nearly 4 meters tall, it had a balanced ratio of upper to lower body, with short, reverse-jointed legs and relatively long, thick arms.

The hydraulic cylinders pumped in different cycles, matching the engine's sound.

-Enemy detected. Preemptive strike authorized.

The vehicle extended its four-fingered hand and charged.

As Gaold stepped back, it slammed its body into the ground, raising a wall of dirt.

The vehicle, covered in dirt, seemed unable to rise on its own due to its weight, but it kept moving its legs, shoveling away the dirt and regaining its balance.

Meanwhile, Shirone's group maintained their combat intervals, spreading out.

Shirone also activated his diamond armor, pulling up his hood and observing the situation.

'What is that?'

The bipedal vehicle's torso rotated smoothly like oil on water, its upper cockpit scanning the front.

The cockpit was an oval glass sphere with a purple sheen, as if electricity was flowing through it, making it impossible to see inside.

"Guroi. It's a second-stage combat system of the Mecha tribe," Kangnam explained.

Gaold narrowed his eyes.

"Impressive. It looks much better in person."

When he had only seen the specifications on paper, he had thought it was just a walking pile of scrap metal.

But in actual combat, it was highly mobile and could be tactically operated depending on the pilot's skill.

The method of erasing its presence by distorting the magnetic field was the latest technology not yet documented in recent papers.

The upper part of the Guroi rotated and faced the Mecha tribe.

"Is the entire second squad safe? What about the Kergo?"

The voice from the speaker was neutral, but the tone suggested it was likely a man.

"The Kergo tribe has been annihilated. But it wasn't us."

The Guroi's cockpit turned back to Gaold.

"Who are they?"

The woman hesitated.

The bearded man had clearly said he wasn't from the munitions community.

But the illusion he had shown was a form of intangible violence that could only be inflicted with blind hostility.

She would rather go mad than enter that hell again.

"They attacked us."

The Guroi issued orders through an internal channel.

-Eliminate them. Switch to attack mode. Increase the argon engine to gear 5.

Clang! Clang! Clang!

The five advancing Gurois simultaneously changed the shape of their hands.

The four fingers folded outward, and a massive barrel with a black hole emerged from inside.

-Commence firing!

Bang bang bang bang bang bang bang!

The iron cylinders, weighing hundreds of kilograms, shook violently, producing a deafening roar.

Heavy bullets shot out at a rate of 300 rounds per minute, shattering rocks and splintering trees.

One of the Gurois turned its body towards Gaold.

The black bullets flew rapidly towards Gaold, but their speed suddenly decreased as if they were buried in something.

Thud! Thud thud thud!

The bullets, unable to penetrate the air shield, gathered, forming a wall of black iron.

"Hmm, iron balls."

Gaold waited until the bullets lost their spin, then caught one in his hand.

The Guroi unit's dismay was evident in the shaking of their machines.

"Sir! He caught the bullet with his hand!"

"Keep firing! They seem to be border dwellers! Don't judge the enemy based on past experiences!"

Gaold weighed the bullet in his hand, then flicked it upwards with his thumb.

"It's like an air gun. It would hurt if it hit."

Kangnam glared and said, "Are you going to do it or not? Or should I?"

Gaold caught the falling bullet.

"Get ready. I want to try piloting it myself."

Kangnam gritted her teeth at his flippant remark but didn't intend to overturn the commander's decision.

"Can you capture it without damage?"

When Kangnam asked Sein, his Iron Wheel Eyes began to spin.

"Calculating. It will take about a minute."

Kangnam worriedly surveyed the battlefield. A minute seemed more than enough time for all five Gurois to be destroyed.

"Damn it! Who are these people?"

The pilot of Guroi Unit 3 was in a dire situation.

The AMOLED vision installed in the front of the cockpit provided a 360-degree view with seven split screens, but the man with the sword seemed to jump between the screens, making it hard to track him.

'This world is full of strange things.'

Kuan slashed at various parts of the Guroi.

Each cut split the metal like flesh, sending sparks flying.

'It's thicker than armor. Where's the pilot?'

By chance, he severed an engine artery, causing Unit 3 to lose power and stagger.

"Damn it! This guy!"

The pilot raised both arms to regain balance.

The Guroi, a second-stage Mecha combat system, required the pilot to meet the first-stage criteria.

The first stage was the Piper, with the Guroi's movement mechanisms connected to the pilot's body parts, so those without enhanced strength couldn't easily move an arm.

Struggling to stabilize, the pilot searched for Kuan.

But instead of Kuan, he saw a cute girl with long brown hair and dorky glasses.

"Cute, my foot!"

Despite her appearance, she was a martial artist, and each strike shook the Guroi, causing internal explosions.

"Fine! I'll smash you to pieces!"

The pilot gritted his teeth and swung his fist.

The Guroi's torso accelerated, and the thick barrel struck Etella.

Boom!

The pilot paled as he realized his torso wouldn't turn any further.

Checking the multivision, he saw the woman blocking the Guroi's barrel with her elbow raised to her face.

"How... How is this possible? One arm has 17 horsepower."

"Hoo..."

Taking a deep breath to calm her arm muscles, Etella moved to the Guroi's side.

Yin-Yang Wave Fist - Wall Thunder Palm.

As she struck the Guroi's leg with her palm, the metal resonated with the wave, sending a deafening sound through the pilot's eardrums.

"Aaagh!"

Grabbing his ears involuntarily, the Guroi's arms mimicked the motion, causing it to fall forward.

"Damn it! Someone deal with that big-breasted woman! She's not human! She's a monster!"

"This side first! He's cutting the engine arteries! Take out the swordsman!"

"Who am I supposed to deal with first?"

Kanya, spinning the Guroi's torso and scanning left and right, shouted in frustration.

It was chaos on both sides.

Bang! Bang! Bang!

The impacts on the machine made Kanya close one eye.

With her remaining eye, she saw a tentacle monster flying through the forest, anchoring its tentacles to trees.

"That damn monster!"

Bang bang bang bang bang bang!

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

The Guroi's bullets shattered all the trees in the area where the tentacle monster was moving.

Despite this, the monster continued to shoot beams of light, engaging in combat.

Thunk! Thunk!

Each time the light hit, the Guroi, weighing several tons, shook, and the impact was fully transmitted to Kanya through the Piper.

"This is really annoying!"

Wasting bullets on a single monster was frustrating.

Due to the munitions community's interference, the rebels' weapons were dwindling daily, so only those capable of delivering the most efficient strikes were selected as pilots. Kanya, an ace shooter with the arc, was one of them.

"Why is that monster...!"

What annoyed her most was that the monster's abilities reminded her of the magic of someone she missed dearly.

The incident where Shirone stopped the Elixir of Myths didn't just save Kanya's mother.

The hope he left behind had grown into a massive storm, becoming the first principle of the rebels' beliefs.

Recalling memories of such a precious person because of that monster was deeply unsettling.

"No matter how many bullets it takes, I'll take you down!"

With all the surrounding trees shattered, Shirone's range of movement was severely limited.

Realizing he couldn't win a prolonged battle, Shirone threw off his robe and began casting his spell in earnest.

Light gathered at the center of his spirit zone like a retina, forming a single red beam that shot at the Guroi's leg.

While destroying the torso would be most effective, targeting the leg joints was more practical during combat.

Warning! Internal heat detected! Activating cooling system! Output exceeds allowable range!

"What now?" Kanya checked the dashboard with wide eyes.

The interface showing the Guroi's front view had the left leg flashing red.

Energy overload! Energy overload!

The output gauge kept rising.

At 240%, 370%, and finally reaching 600%, the knee joint broke with a loud crack.

"Ahhh!"

The Guroi's body tilted, and the cockpit slammed into the ground.

The impact felt like falling from a building, rattling her bones, but Kanya held onto the controls.

"Die, monster... huh?"

As the AMOLED vision cleared, the target in the crosshairs automatically came into focus.

"What... what is this?"

Kanya couldn't believe her eyes.

The face of a boy who had appeared even in her dreams was magnified on the screen.
