Chapter 1262: The Sun Risen at Night (1)

Mika spoke. —Detected change in the Sun. Shirone Sphere’s completion is currently ahead by 0.3 seconds.

Strength returned to Shirone’s eyes. ‘It worked!’

He didn’t know what Ikael had done, but the end point had been pushed back ever so slightly. ‘It’s enough.’

—Progress: 99.99%.

Just before the birth of the greatest artificial structure in the universe, dozens of times larger than the Sun. “Hah.”

It was a magnificent sight.

“Satiel!” Ikael shouted.

‘I should’ve said sorry…’

It was really something she should have said first. ‘I knew.’

She had always known how Satiel felt about Goffin.

It wasn’t that it had especially bothered her, or that she’d acted out of jealousy and tried to interfere. ‘It just…’

…it had just ended up that way.

They had fought desperately, loved, fled from Anke Ra, and had a child… ‘No.’

Maybe, deep down, Satiel had always remained in her heart. Maybe that’s why she had run all the way to this point.

Tears streamed down Ikael’s face. “Satiel.”

She should’ve said it long ago. “I’m sorry.”

I loved you so much too.

—Hehe.

Satiel smiled beautifully. “Ahh…”

It was the face of the kindest, most righteous angel among the Hundred Wings, as Ikael remembered her.

And as her final moments approached, her voice seemed to echo faintly. —Take care.

“Ahhhhh…”

Ikael, overwhelmed by pure, tearful emotion, watched as Satiel began her long journey.

From existence into nonexistence.

And in the process of becoming one with the universe, Satiel met Shirone. —A name.

It would’ve been nice if someone had given her one.

Perhaps it was the outstretched hands of apology from Goffin and Ikael.

—Shirone. That’s a beautiful name.

Though her form had vanished, she embraced the back of the boy who fought against God with her heart. —You can do it.

Shirone felt it.

A special force was rushing in from a realm too small for human observation. ‘Here we go!’

As the Shirone Sphere was completed, all the information contained in the photons began to reverse. ‘God.’

As Shirone stared down the being beyond the universe, someone reached out a hand behind him.

Kitra’s face trembled wildly. “Beep! Beep-beep!”

In the 50-50 balance that split the universe in half, the domain of God began to crash. —75%! 52%! 32%!

As Shirone’s tachyon repelled divine causality and imposed itself on reality, the In regained their hearts. ‘Invalid! Invalid! Invalid!’

And then, Kitra… ‘Critical damage detected.’

With a popping sound like an explosion from within, he was hurled to the end of the hallway.

“Kh, kh, kh, kh…”

Smoke rose from his completely burned-out eyes, and a strange sound came from his throat.

He would be discarded, but Kitra’s damage was not God’s damage.

—Modifying algorithm.

God began a second attempt—a system change through learning.

—Learning complete.

Why had it failed?

Humans.

From the beginning of time to the present, God reviewed every log where the heart had influenced outcomes.

—Analyzing cause.

If quantum signals had been mishandled, then the system must be rebuilt on a quantum foundation.

—Restarting End Program.

Kitra’s lips moved briefly.

“Hu…man…”

The light in his eyes went out silently.

‘Goodbye,’

Having said her farewell to Shirone with her heart, Satiel continued her journey toward the end of the universe.

There was no direction.

Just an endless process of scattering until existence itself disappeared.

‘Does it feel like sleeping forever?’

No—even the subject sleeping, or any stray thought in a dream, would no longer exist in perfect nothingness.

Satiel was not afraid. ‘It’s okay.’

She drifted further from the universe. ‘Ahh.’

The farther she moved away, the clearer things became—and those realizations adorned her final moments.

‘In that far, faraway universe… were there stars and moons?’

‘Can love…’

…fit inside a heart so immense?

Facing the perfect annihilation where even the concept of “none” doesn’t exist, Satiel could still smile. ‘It’s okay.’

In that place of absolute nothingness… ‘Goffin is there.’

Uorin clutched her head.

"Aaaah!"

Even with her eyes shut, the flashes of precognitive visions were blinking in and out at an incredible speed.

Survival. Death. Survival. Death. Survival. Death.

'What is this? What's happening?'

She had no idea.

She didn’t know Havitz and Kido were slashing at each other right beside her, or that blood was spraying everywhere.

‘Kill Uorin!’

Havitz couldn’t perceive Kido.

‘Protect Uorin.’

And Kido couldn’t perceive Havitz.

That bizarre triangle created by the double Vanishing was turning every second of their battle into a catastrophe.

“Grrrk!”

Kido was losing focus.

He couldn’t even feel the bleeding anymore. Just maintaining Vanishing was its own form of hell.

‘I feel sick. Why am I doing this? Why do I keep swinging at nothing?’

He didn’t know who it was, but it had to be someone with the same ability.

‘All I can do is endure.’

At that moment, Havitz charged forward.

‘Kill Uorin! Kill her!’

His sword bounced off the blade barrier that Kido had projected.

‘Why can’t I kill her?’

If he didn’t kill Uorin before midnight, the chance of winning the Truth and Lie game would drop to zero.

‘But Wizard still has a chance. Her condition is either that she loves me… or hates me.’

The ways to prove it: kiss Havitz, or break one of his fingers.

‘She’ll probably break it. This whole fight will be decided in an instant. She must be waiting for the chance somewhere.’

Considering Wizard’s ability to eliminate a frame of perception, he couldn’t let his guard down.

"So annoying."

He didn’t know what ghost stood before him.

‘Stealing my ability?’

There are things no one can steal.

‘Fate.’

Havitz deactivated Vanishing and began to move freely.

"Heeheeheehee!"

‘Havitz!’

Kido’s eyes sharpened, and his spear thrust forward at terrifying speed—

‘Huh?’

With a movement no master could replicate, Havitz evaded the attack and swung his sword.

Kido realized something.

‘He didn’t dodge.’

Havitz simply wasn’t there where the spear was aimed.

His sword slashed deeply across Kido’s chest, sending a geyser of blood erupting.

Vanishing broke, and Uorin cried out.

“Kido!”

Like a soul ascending, Havitz raised his longsword high in front of Uorin.

“Kehkeh! Got you!”

Kido ran in, but Havitz’s blade was already touching the crown of Uorin’s head.

‘Too late.’

At that very moment—

Dance of the Reaper — Asynchronous Concerto.

Faster than Havitz’s sword could pierce flesh, a silhouette shot in.

Whack!

With a dull thud, Havitz was slammed into the wall, his neck twisted completely around.

Kido was speechless.

“…What?”

Natasha, one of the 4 Gustavs, was standing in front of Uorin.

“Get up.”

As Uorin turned her head quickly, she saw Havitz looking back at them over his twisted shoulder.

"Hee… heeheehee."

That laugh from a 180-degree turned neck sent chills down even Uorin’s spine.

“S-Satan…”

His neck had bulked up grotesquely, the skin torn and muscles fully exposed.

Eyes brimming with madness, Havitz rose again.

“How’d you do that?”

A question to Natasha—the one who had killed him despite his "undying fate."

“I saw it.”

She had seen Balkan’s severed head.

And in the dead gaze within, she saw—perhaps imagined—a special memory.

“You know it too, Havitz.”

She didn’t know the name.

Probably erased through some process. But like a scent, it rose to the surface.

—Havitz? Havitz? Let’s play.

There had been four friends who always called him out in front of the house.

“So what?”

Natasha began walking forward.

“You just wanted to play. Balkan, Smodo, Zetaro, and me.”

“I am playing.”

What the hell am I even saying?

It was like another personality inside was giving the answers on its own.

Same with Natasha.

“But still… I don’t want to kill my friends. You broke the rules. Look now—you’re not even dying.”

Havitz turned around slowly.

Then grabbed his own face and—with a loud crack—rotated it back into place.

‘My laws don’t work.’

Another sign that Natasha was linked to him too.

“It’s my choice. Live or die. I’ll do what I want. I’m sick of playing with you guys.”

And that was the part that made her the angriest.

Natasha—

Dance of the Reaper.

With an explosive burst of air, Natasha charged and pummeled Havitz.

Crack! Crack! Crack!

Every time a bone broke or a muscle tore, Havitz’s body grew larger.

“Kehahaha! More! Hit me more, idiot!”

Shedding his human disguise, he was becoming Satan.

He reached into the air and snared Natasha’s wrist like a fish in a net.

“Told you—it won’t work.”

With a blood-red face and bared teeth, Havitz hurled Natasha away.

“You can die too!”

Just then—from a blind spot Havitz hadn’t accounted for—Wizard lunged forward at terrifying speed.

‘That was her plan all along?’

The manifestation of the Void God rose from Wizard’s body, swaying with its long tongue.

‘Shit!’

Havitz knew it was too late but still clenched his fingers.

Ultra-Skill — Null Creation.

A single frame of this world vanished.

“Kh…”

What he expected in the next frame was the sensation of his finger being broken.

But instead—

Smack.

What he actually felt was Wizard’s soft lips pressing against his.

Even after Wizard landed lightly, Havitz stood there, dazed.

‘Why?’

Wizard said calmly,

“I win.”

DONG. DONG. DONG.

The bells tolled midnight.

In the Sacred Arena, the time was 11:52.

With 8 minutes until the end, Rian and Ymir’s fighting spirit was burning at full blaze.

“Good! Good!”

Every punch collapsed buildings, every step shook the earth.

RUMBLE! RUMBLE!

Even though the soldiers had evacuated hundreds of meters away, it still didn’t feel safe.

Clump said,

“We should back off a little more.”

They were already absurdly far, but no one objected.

‘This isn’t a battle of this world.’

The landscape was being sliced, the air expanding like a gong, and the terrain was shifting by the second…

‘A battle of gods.’

Soundless explosions pierced their ears as the perimeter slowly widened.

‘There’s no shame in retreating from this.’

Meanwhile, in the unrelenting slugfest, Ymir trembled in delight.

‘Yes… this is it.’

An enemy who wouldn’t fall no matter how much you hit them.

Divine Transcendence — Asura Balbalta.

“Grah!”

And an enemy who hit back harder than the damage they received.

“More.”

Harder.

Rian’s blade scraped his cheek, then slammed into his ribs.

“Grrrk!”

The pain was so intense it brought tears to Ymir’s eyes.

“…Heh.”

It felt like every cell in his body was waking up.

‘I’m alive.’

I’m alive.

Overwhelmed with emotion, he clenched his fists and opened his jaw wide to the limit.

“GAAAAAAHHHHH!”

The King of Giants shed tears.
