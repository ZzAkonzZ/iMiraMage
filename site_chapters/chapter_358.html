Chapter 358: Magic Society (4)

Shirone was too out of breath to respond. He had been observing the situation from afar but rushed over at full speed when things became critical.

"How can you just kill someone without warning? I don’t know what’s going on, but can’t you resolve this through dialogue?"

"What? Kill someone? How dare you reduce the great cause of revolution to mere murder!"

Aria recognized Shirone’s face. He was the boy she had felt a liking for during their encounter in the carriage a few days ago. But now was no time for such thoughts.

Tears and snot streaming down her face, Aria pleaded with Shirone, her only hope.

"Please save me! Please, I beg you!"

"Shut up! The revolution has already begun!"

As a scythe was placed against Aria's neck, Shirone acted quickly and resourcefully. Clutching a metal box to his side, he raised his hand and shouted.

"Wait! In that case, take me as your hostage instead!"

The scythe stopped just before it could pierce Aria’s throat.

The revolutionary had trained for three years to complete their grand cause, but they had never encountered such a situation before. Who would willingly risk their life for a stranger?

"You want us to take you as a hostage?"

Shirone pulled out his Magic Association ID from his inner pocket, covering Plu’s picture with his thumb, and held it out.

"As you can see, I’m an employee of the Magic Association. Taking me hostage would be much more effective than a civilian."

The revolutionary finally caught on to Shirone’s ploy.

It was a stalling tactic. They had never seen a Magic Association ID before, and verifying it here was impossible.

"Hmph! You’re a Magic Association employee? You, a kid? Do you really think I’d believe that?"

At that moment, a terrorist standing by the window, who had been observing Shirone carefully, spoke.

"Bring that kid over."

All the revolutionaries in the Gold Tower turned their attention to him. Though his face was hidden beneath a hood, the atmosphere made it clear that they were puzzled.

"Captain, do you really believe him?"

"It’s not about believing him. He is a Magic Association employee. I confirmed it at Akim Trading Company. If we capture a Magic Association employee as a hostage, our demands will be much easier to meet."

One of the revolutionaries relayed the captain’s message through the window. Since it was the captain’s order, there was no need to doubt it.

"Hey, the captain said to bring him. Get him in here."

The revolutionary holding the scythe clicked his tongue and roughly shoved Aria aside. Then he pointed his scythe at Shirone and gestured with a flick of his wrist.

"Fine. Walk over here slowly. If you try anything, we’ll kill all the hostages."

"I know. That’s why I’m offering myself as a hostage."

Shirone set the metal box on the ground, trying to avoid any suspicion. The revolutionary holding the scythe nervously asked,

"Wait. What’s that?"

"This is something I purchased from an alchemy shop."

The revolutionary glanced back for confirmation, and the captain pondered for a moment. While he hadn’t seen it personally, he knew the boy had indeed visited the shop to buy something.

"Fine. Bring that too."

For the Black Revolutionaries, currency was meaningless. Even a bundle of cash couldn’t buy more than an apple on the black market.

But goods were different, especially items ordered by the Magic Association. Those could likely be traded for a substantial amount of weapons.

"Hey, carry that over your head and come here."

Shirone lifted the metal box with both hands and walked towards the Gold Tower.

As soon as he crossed the entrance, three revolutionaries surrounded him. One snatched the box, another tied his hands behind his back, and the third forced him to his knees, placing a large sword against his neck.

"Stay still. Resist, and you die."

Shirone didn’t respond, instead surveying his surroundings.

The hostages were gathered in one spot, guarded by three terrorists. Aria knelt nearby, separated from the group.

Aria, realizing she was alive, looked at Shirone with a mix of emotions. She felt guilty and astonished to learn he was a Magic Association employee.

"You’re a magician?"

Shirone didn’t look back, only giving a bitter smile. Even as a magician, he couldn’t access the Spirit Zone inside the Gold Tower due to its magic suppression system.

‘I didn’t expect the magic control system to be active. Well, it’s the Foreign Exchange Bank, so it makes sense. But how did these people infiltrate?’

He glanced at the large hole in the floor of the Gold Tower, suspecting they had dug a tunnel to gain entry. It must have been a months-long effort, meaning this was no impulsive crime.

‘This could get dangerous.’

As Shirone pondered, a hulking terrorist holding a flail approached. Even under his robe, his massive frame was unmistakable.

"Haha, a Magic Association brat shows up. What should I do with you?"

His voice was unnaturally deep, almost incomprehensible despite its clarity.

The terrorist crouched, grabbing Shirone’s chin with a hand as rough as bark.

After tilting Shirone’s face left and right, the terrorist seemed to have an idea and pulled out a dagger from his coat.

"Yeah, this should be fun. Hey, girl."

Aria looked at the terrorist with anxious eyes. Whatever he wanted, it wasn’t to let her go home and study.

The terrorist spun the dagger nimbly, then gripped the blade and held out the handle.

"You’re grateful to the person who saved you, aren’t you?"

"Y-yes."

"Then as a thank-you, stab him once. His thigh should do."

Aria’s face turned pale, her jaw trembling with fear.

"W-what? How could I…?"

Even stabbing a stranger would terrify her, but hurting the person who had become a hostage to save her? That was unthinkable.

A terrorist wielding twin axes took an interest and approached.

"Haha, that’s a good idea. Hey, do it! Or we’ll kill you first!"

The high-pitched and low-pitched voices overlapped eerily.

Terrified by the threat, Aria reluctantly took the dagger. But she couldn’t bring herself to stab Shirone in the thigh.

"Hah? Even a Magic Association kid stays calm in this situation?"

Shirone continued to look ahead calmly. Reacting now would only amuse the terrorists. Of course, getting stabbed would hurt, but that was still a future event.

Seeing Shirone’s stoic demeanor, Aria looked up at him. His expression unchanged, Shirone spoke words that alleviated her guilt.

"I’ll be fine. Protect yourself."

The flail-wielding terrorist lost patience and bellowed.

"Hurry up! Stab him! Before I smash your skull!"

As the flail was raised menacingly, Aria burst into tears and brought the dagger toward Shirone’s thigh.

"I-I’m sorry… I’m so sorry."

The reversed blade trembled violently. She couldn’t imagine what it would feel like to pierce flesh with cold steel.

Sobbing, Aria pressed the dagger’s tip against Shirone’s thigh.

Even then, Shirone’s expression didn’t change. They wouldn’t kill him. He was their most valuable hostage.

"You’re surrounded! You’re surrounded! Release the hostages and surrender immediately! Failure to comply will result in a full-scale assault!"

All the terrorists turned their heads toward the windows. The Thormia Counter-Terrorism Unit had set up barricades and surrounded the Gold Tower.

The elite forces—emergency response units, special suppression squads of magicians, bomb disposal teams of alchemists, and sniper squads of gunners—were all deployed.

The flail-wielding terrorist spewed unintelligible curses, frustrated at losing his chance to humiliate the Magic Association brat. Or perhaps it wasn’t even human language.

For the Black Revolutionaries, nothing was more important than the revolution. Regaining his composure, the terrorist grabbed Shirone by the back of his collar, lifting him off the ground, and dragged him to the window.

"We are the Black Revolutionaries! If you do not release Deputy Commander Kanimar and our comrades immediately, the hostages will die! We will execute one person every ten minutes!"

The counter-terrorism unit didn’t flinch. Meeting their demands would only lead to greater ones in the future.

But they couldn’t sacrifice the hostages either, especially if any high-ranking nobles were among them. That would create significant political fallout.

"Damn bastards! If you want to die, die alone!"

The unit leader ground his teeth as he glared at the terrorist. A raid was inevitable, but the timing to minimize casualties was critical.

It could happen now, or in five minutes. The burden of how much time to give them rested entirely on his shoulders.

Just then, a deafening roar tore through the air, and a woman wielding an unusually long staff landed with a thud.

Plu, who had flown directly from the Magic Association, quickly analyzed the situation.

‘The counter-terrorism unit has arrived, and all civilians have been evacuated. But where is that guy…?’

The alchemy shop wasn’t far, so he should have been nearby, observing the situation. Yet, no matter how hard she looked, Shirone was nowhere to be seen.

‘What? He didn’t come? Did he actually run away? Well, that’s better for me…’

Her gaze fell on the entrance of the Gold Tower. When she saw Shirone, held hostage by a terrorist with a resigned expression, her eyes widened, and her jaw dropped.

"What the hell are you doing there, you idiot?!"

Her outburst drew the counter-terrorism unit’s attention.

"Ah…"

The captain frowned and asked,

"Who are you? You must be a magician if you teleported here."

Plu, regaining her composure, walked confidently towards him. Although she wasn't part of the cooperation team, this area was under the jurisdiction of the Magic Association, so she had authority.

"I'm Plu, Chief of Security at the Magic Association. What's the current situation... huh?"

As she patted her chest, Plu realized with a start that her ID badge was missing.

‘Oh no, I gave it to that kid.’

The captain watched Plu's changing expressions with a steady gaze. If she hadn't teleported, he might have thought she was just a crazy woman from the neighborhood.

"Chief of Security at the Magic Association? Why are you here? We've already assembled the response team."

"Ah, well, I thought I might be able to help..."

Without her ID, Plu's voice was less confident. The captain, looking annoyed, turned back to face the situation.

"Go back. We have enough forces to handle them. The problem is the hostages."

Plu knew from experience that the difficulty in dealing with terrorists was that they used innocent people as shields.

But she couldn't just leave Shirone behind.

‘This is so frustrating. What was he thinking, getting caught by terrorists? Magic doesn't even work there.’

Suddenly, Plu's eyes lit up.

‘Wait, magic?’

Maybe Shirone being captured could be an opportunity.

With a hopeful strategy in mind, Plu pushed through the response team and reached the captain.

"Captain! Captain!"

The captain sighed and turned around.

"What now? We're busy."

"I have a way to save the hostages!"
