Chapter 1237:

Etella kept walking, endlessly.

“Mom… Mom…”

The children of Limbo—those who had never been born and died before they could live—had now become the very space around her.

“I’m sorry…”

Because the fear that Limbo felt… was the same as the fear of the child in her womb.

“Mom is sorry.”

Etella’s heart felt like it was being torn apart.

Her physical body must have already been shredded, but the Schema—the spiritual framework—continued transmitting endless pain.

Like the Buddha offering his body to the hungry ghosts, she too was walking toward the end of the system.

‘Once I reach that place…’

She, her unborn child, and all of Limbo would disappear into eternal nothingness.

‘Shagall…’

He was the one she was worried about.

‘You have to understand me.’

Because perhaps that was the only way Shagall could ever truly atone for his existence in this world.

“M… Mom…”

Every step was heavy.

Not like moving forward, but like dragging an entire space—the weight of life—with her.

‘I can’t fall.’

Though her resolve to purify Limbo never faltered, the truth was… her strength was running out.

“Haaahhh…”

No matter how hard she tried, she couldn’t move forward against the sheer weight of life. Her knees gave out.

‘Is this… all I can manage?’

The reason this world is so cruel… is because there are things you can’t accomplish even if you give your life for them.

‘I have to go. I have to save these children…’

And that’s why responsibility is both the heaviest burden and the most beautiful conviction a human can carry.

“Mom… Mom…”

They were crying out—they wanted to live.

“I have to stand…”

Just then, a voice echoed in her mind.

…Ah, testing connection.

‘What…?’

Etella blinked in confusion and asked aloud,

“Who… are you?”

—Ether resonance successful. Then now… Ah, Your Majesty. Step back, all of you.

‘What is this…?’

The sound of voices from reality, heard in the depths of hell, sent chills down her spine.

—Romi Etella.

A cold, solemn voice was followed by a female interpreter.

Jin Kang and Anchal’s voices came through almost simultaneously.

—I am Jin Kang, Emperor of the Jincheon Empire. That place must be Hell, where my daughter now resides.

‘Jin Kang…’

Jin Seong Eum’s father.

—Perhaps saying nothing would be the kinder thing to do for someone trapped in Hell. But once again, I speak cruel words, driven by my selfishness.

“Your Majesty! Can you hear me?”

But there was no reaction. It seemed Etella’s voice couldn’t reach them.

‘Ether Resonance…’

It was likely a method of embedding information in spatial waves.

—Please save my daughter.

Once she understood, Etella went quiet and listened.

—We’ve developed a Pama-Gwangcheon Core capable of sending information into the Underworld. You, as a master monk and magician, must already understand the theory. The problem is the coordinates. Originally, I planned to concentrate energy around my daughter to destroy Hell itself…

‘He’s… incredibly determined too.’

That was responsibility.

—We’ve decided to change the coordinates and send the energy to you instead.

Etella nodded.

‘It’s possible. I’m inside the process itself. If the explosion happens from here, not only can I save Limbo, but the Underworld’s purification system will crash. And if that happens…’

Jin Seong Eum’s karma would also disappear.

—I don’t know.

Jin Kang continued after all his technical explanations.

—I don’t know what kind of situation you’re in. It must be painful. But when the energy from the Pama-Gwangcheon Core is condensed, you’ll experience even greater suffering. I wonder if this is really the right thing to do. Perhaps it would be better to pray for your peace, even if it means letting my daughter die…

“No! No!”

Etella shook her head fiercely.

‘I can save her! I can still save Jin Seong Eum!’

Yes, it was terrifying.

But she had witnessed what Jin Seong Eum had sacrificed for humanity in the Valley of Wailing.

‘She must live. She… deserves to live.’

—To inflict pain upon someone you cannot even see or feel… that is truly horrifying. Especially when that someone has given everything for the sake of humanity. To push you further into suffering… it makes me feel as though I’ve become a demon.

“Your Majesty!”

As Etella cried out, Jin Kang’s voice trembled.

—Please save her.

Etella clenched her jaw.

—Please save my daughter. If it means I have to die… even if I fall into Hell myself…

Jin Kang would do it without hesitation.

Because the heart of a parent who wishes to save their child… is a desire so strong it cannot be fulfilled even by offering one’s life.

‘Responsibility.’

Sometimes, you’re not even allowed to die as you wish.

—Romi Etella… I don’t know how you’re reacting to these words right now. Maybe you’re cursing me. Maybe you’re screaming that this is wrong. Just imagining that drags me into a deeper hell…

Jin Kang took a deep breath.

—But I will believe. I will believe that Romi Etella is the greatest seeker of truth in the world—and that she’s ready to sacrifice herself for the sake of humanity. I will believe in you.

Etella smiled and nodded silently.

—…I wish you good fortune.

The voice cut off.

And then, searing heat surged through her entire body.

‘It’s begun.’

Etella gritted her teeth and rose to her feet.

To maximize the effectiveness of the Pama-Gwangcheon Core, the detonation had to occur in the deepest part of the system.

“I can do this.”

She could take responsibility with her life.

‘Jin Seong Eum, humanity still needs you.’

The pain came crashing in.

“No!”

Shagall screamed.

Nearly driven mad, he tore through the underworld like a rabid beast, flailing his limbs wildly as he ran.

“Aaaaaaah!”

Etella’s pain came crashing through the Taeguk chain, and Shagall collapsed to his knees, his eyes rolling back.

—Time remaining until purification…

It had been cut by more than half since he first arrived here, but it was still an eternity.

“Why did you do something so stupid?! If you disappear like that, if you vanish like this…”

I have to see you again.

An overwhelming wave of emotional anguish surged through him, and with it, another massive chunk of karma was purified.

Suddenly, Shagall realized something.

“Damn it! Goddamn it!”

The pain he was feeling now—the despair—was exactly what he had inflicted on Etella.

“I hurt her. I… destroyed her like this.”

“Karma…?”

Shagall wiped his tears and clawed at his head.

“…What do I do?”

Did I really ruin everything?

“What do I do? What do I do? What do I do? What do I—”

He couldn’t take responsibility.

“No!”

Shouting in denial, Shagall screamed,

“Why are you doing this to me?! What do you expect me to do?! You could’ve just come back!”

There are some things that can never be undone.

“I’ll kill you!”

If he let go of this hatred too, it would feel like there was nothing left—nothing between him and Etella.

“I’ll kill you! I’ll kill every last one of you!”

Shagall hurled his demon-ravaged body into Hell.

DONG. DONG. DONG.

The bells of the Holy War rang out, announcing 10 o’clock.

The national representatives and officials who had gathered at the annex theater half an hour earlier wore solemn expressions.

With the finale performance, the official ceremonies of the Holy War would come to an end…

‘What’s even going on anymore?’

The situation was so tense and urgent, it didn’t feel like anything was ending.

‘The country that kills Maya becomes the World Leadership Nation? What kind of prophecy is that? Can we even believe it?’

It was a conclusion drawn from analysis based on the information Fermi had distributed to each country.

Albino muttered.

“Another bloodbath is coming.”

Lupist asked, “Will it really? The source is Apocalypse, but it could’ve been manipulated.”

“Fermi doesn’t tell clumsy lies. Lies breed more lies, and eventually lead to self-destruction. His strength comes from being armed with logic.”

Albino recited from the records of Apocalypse.

“At the end of the Holy War, two women will sing. Applause will rain down on Maya, and the army of Iron will kill her. Iron will become the World Leadership Nation.”

“Not Jive, but Iron.”

Lupist glanced around the seats.

“Gis isn’t here.”

“That means Plu succeeded. The other nations aren’t visibly shaken either. So far, things are going just as Fermi’s intel predicted.”

“But what does Maya’s death have to do with becoming the World Leadership Nation?”

“That’s the mystery. From what I can tell, the information from Apocalypse records only the outcomes of key events in chronological order. Almost like… a machine wrote it, not a human.”

Only humans require causality.

“Still, I’m not convinced. Will Maya really die? The performers don’t know, right?”

“No. It’s classified.”

“And our side?”

“Preparations have been made.”

If the information was true, then the nation that kills Maya must be Tormia.

“Ogent Rai?”

“No. One of the performers, a pianist named Reina, is her family. We can’t entrust it to her.”

“Naturally.”

“Besides, I don’t think it’ll be carried out. If Fermi knows, then Shirone must know too.”

“Come to think of it… he’s not here.”

Not seeing Shirone, Albino twirled his beard.

‘What are they up to?’ Fermi’s reason for revealing the information likely came from a security breach.

‘Probably Iron. Otherwise, there’d be no reason to devalue the intel so much.’

Now came the question of truth.

‘The timeline matches. A one-hour gap in Havitz’s killing game. Gis’s death…’

So then, will Maya die?

‘Shirone isn’t showing up. Is it false intel? No, it’s real. I’m just missing something.’

Albino’s face hardened.

“Maybe we’re thinking about it the wrong way.”

“What do you mean?”

“It’s not that we kill Maya, but that we’re forced to kill her.”

Lupist blinked.

“This is the trap of updated information. Human reasoning goes from cause to effect: if Maya dies, the killer becomes the World Leadership Nation. But to the gods, it’s the reverse. They don’t care who wins—the key is that Maya dies.”

“But how would that even happen? What could make us kill her?”

“That’s the point we’ll soon reach.”

Albino fixed his gaze on the doors of the annex.

“Where Shirone will appear.”

—

At the entrance of Delta Main Hall, the clash between protesters, the Anti-Demon Division, and the warriors of the southern tribes had turned into a warzone.

“Hmm.”

Untara sensed that the balance of power was suddenly shifting.

‘This feeling… it’s familiar.’

An unseen presence was moving through the ranks, slicing down warriors as if from nowhere.

‘And it’s getting closer.’

Havitz’s eyes gleamed with killing intent as he cut down warriors and made his way toward Untara.

“Disqualified. Disqualified. Disqualified.”

The Wave of In affected him too, but it caused no visible change.

“Interesting.”

His mind was already chaos to begin with.

“You’re done too.”

Havitz stood before Untara, sword raised—but did not move.

If the southern forces collapsed, the protesters would flood into Delta Hall and seize control of the world.

Yet regardless of the political stakes, Havitz dispelled Vanishing and slowly backed away.

“At last, I get to see you.”

“Hrk.”

Untara gasped in shock, but Havitz’s eyes were already fixed elsewhere.

“Yahweh.”

“You’ve survived, Satan.”

Bathed in the light of Elikia, Shirone stepped into the battlefield—pushing the chaos behind him.
