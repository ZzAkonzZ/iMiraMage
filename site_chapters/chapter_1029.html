Chapter 1029: Black Chains (1)

The gap dividing the Wailing Wall was so narrow it was barely visible from a distance, but up close, it was a substantial 20 meters wide.

“Hah. Hah.”

People just breathing.

“Hey, you there. Water. Can you give me some water?”

People asking for help.

Rian walked silently, not reacting to anything.

When they could no longer see the way they came or the way forward, he spoke for the first time.

“Shirone, what are these?”

Or should he call them ‘this’?

“Those who haven’t been purified.”

Shirone saw the countless hands and feet protruding from the Wailing Wall, writhing.

“The Wailing Wall isn’t just here. It’s spread throughout the underworld. In reality, it’s like a storage facility for construction materials.”

“Storage? Are you saying humans are the materials?”

“There’s something called the Black Chains. Actually, it’s more like a system. It’s an active attack form of the purification system, capturing living beings that fall here.”

“So they piled humans here? Why? Shouldn’t they be purified, as you said?”

“Hell is the reverse side of reality. The properties are opposite, but many things are needed to live, just like in reality. In reality, there are organic and inorganic materials. Organic materials are used as food, and inorganic materials become the components of many structures.”

Rian understood.

“Hell is the opposite.”

“Yes. The food for demons is the purified demons born from the fires of hell. It might sound like they’re eating their own kind, but not all demons are the same. Humans eat chickens and pigs, which are also living beings. The same goes for rice and wheat.”

Shirone pointed to the wall.

“Conversely, what serves as inorganic material in hell is the unpurified demons, the raw emotions of humans. But when inorganic materials are needed in large quantities, a special storage facility is required…”

“That’s what the Wailing Wall is.”

“Yes. A structure of this scale would be used for dam construction on the River of Fire or city expansion projects. There are subcontractors that process and transport the Wailing Wall…”

Shirone shook his head.

‘That should be enough explanation about the structure. It’s too harsh for Rian.’

For Shirone, who encompassed both reality and the underworld, the Wailing Wall was just a system, but for Rian, it was different.

“What we need to be cautious of are the Black Chains. They’ll bring in escapees captured from all over hell, and they’re likely here too. Though the scale is so vast that the chances of encountering them are low.”

In reality, it was like the odds of meeting a manager in a large logistics warehouse.

Rian spoke with determination.

“No need to worry. Whether it’s the Black Chains or whatever, I’ll just cut them down if they appear.”

It wouldn’t be easy.

‘The chains of the Black Chains are the most binding hidden code in the underworld. Even if we broke the Chains of Gehenna…’

Lost in thought, Shirone heard an old woman’s voice from the left wall where Rian was walking.

“Save me.”

Rian, who had tried to avoid looking, couldn’t ignore the desperate voice.

Turning his head, he saw an old woman with her face and arms protruding like horns, crying.

“It’s too painful. Please get me out of here. I beg you…”

“Shirone.”

Before Rian could finish speaking, Shirone replied.

“It’s impossible.”

“There’s absolutely no way? For example, if we purify the wall with the Chains of Gehenna…”

Shirone shook his head.

“Rian, listen carefully. These aren’t real humans. They’re their emotions.”

“I know. But they’re asking for help. What could be more important?”

That was the kind of person Rian was.

“Of course.”

That’s why Shirone respected his friend, but he knew human emotions well.

“It’s different from purifying the fires of hell. That’s pure demonic essence. The Chains of Gehenna burn you, and you can overcome it. But these are still unpurified, chaotic demons. If you stick the Chains of Gehenna here…”

Shirone spoke with a troubled expression.

“All their emotions will be transmitted to you. You might lose yourself.”

Even so, Rian was the type to persist, but he would likely relent at this point.

“...Alright.”

Because he wouldn’t want to do anything that would endanger Shirone.

‘I’m sorry, Lian.’

Knowing this, Shirone still felt burdened by his decision.

“Please… save me. Have mercy.”

Shirone took one last look at the old woman’s desperate face and turned away.

“Let’s go. If we want to get out of the Wailing Wall by today, we need to keep walking.”

As Rian turned away from the wall to follow Shirone, a violent wind blew.

A harsh voice mixed with the wind.

-Foolish sinners.

Looking up, they saw something like a black cloud passing quickly.

“It’s the Black Chains.”

As soon as Shirone spoke, he and Rian pressed their backs against the wall, hiding.

‘There are quite a few of them.’

Even looking through the 20-meter-wide gap, the sky was covered with black clouds.

Rian asked.

“I agree with avoiding unnecessary danger, but are they that powerful?”

In case of an emergency, it was better to understand the enemy’s strength.

“It’s a system issue. Their chains have immense binding power on unpurified demons.”

“Hmm.”

As Rian frowned, a bony arm reached over and wrapped around his neck.

“Here…”

It was the old woman who had asked Rian for help earlier.

“They’re here! The escapees are here!”

The voice was so loud it hurt their ears, but the first emotion was confusion.

‘Why?’

As if mocking Rian’s feelings, the countless faces embedded in the wall began to shout.

“Two of them are hiding! Hurry! Hurry!”

The voices continued to spread, climbing the wall and finally reaching the sky.

Then the black cloud entered the gap and descended rapidly towards Shirone and Rian.

“Let’s run, Rian!”

Rian tore off the old woman’s arm and dashed forward.

“Here! Here!”

Countless fingers in the wall pointed at them, and screams filled the air.

“They’re going this way! Catch them!”

Rian, catching up to Shirone, asked.

“Why are they reporting us? Do they get released from the Wailing Wall if they earn credit with the Black Chains?”

“No, they’re treated as inorganic material in hell. Release is impossible.”

“Then why?”

The reason they reported them to the Black Chains.

Shirone could think of only one reason.

“Because they want us to be unhappy.”

“What?”

“There’s nothing in it for them. They just hate that we’re in a better state. So they’re trying to drag us down. To make us suffer the same misery.”

Those who had struggled to even open their eyes were now screaming at the top of their lungs.

“Here! Over here!”

Listening to their voices, Rian calmed the flames in his chest.

‘Don’t think.’

They are just them.

‘Protect Shirone. Focus only on that and move.’

The Black Chains were coming.

Ragged cloth fluttered, and white faces flashed between the darkness.

‘Skeletons? No, the problem is their cloaks. I can’t read their movements.’

A substance not of this world.

It was like a physical change where gas, liquid, and solid states were mixed simultaneously.

-Foolish sinners.

“Rian! Now!”

The Black Chains blocking the path pulled out black chains from their cloaks.

At the same time, Shirone and Rian leaped upward, with Rian climbing the wall.

Boom!

Looking down, they saw the black chains embedded in the wall in a complex pattern like a spider’s web.

-Unpurified ones, face your trial.

As something wriggled along the chains and was absorbed, the eyes of the people forming the wall widened.

“Gasp! Gasp…”

A moment later.

“Ahhh!”

The entire wall vibrated as immense demonic energy was absorbed into the Black Chains.

-Capture the sinners!

The roar from below made Rian’s face contort as he sprinted vertically.

“Grrr!”

The Wailing Wall was so high that even running at near-flight speed, the top was not in sight.

-Yahweh! Why do you disturb hell?

Meanwhile, Shirone, who had fled to the opposite wall, dodged the chains of the Black Chains as he flew up.

Reaching the top of the wall faster than , Shirone landed on the swampy ground.

People were half-buried, and tiny humans continued to fall from the black cloud in the sky.

Twenty-four Black Chains, following Shirone with a gust of wind, floated in the air.

‘Light of Agape!’

As Shirone’s eyes widened, a brilliant sphere of light exploded above his head.

“Ahhh!”

When the light disappeared, a massive, shallow crater had formed around Shirone.

‘It’s not enough.’

The human wall was crushed as if melted by heat, but the effect was small compared to demons.

Because it wasn’t pure demonic energy.

‘And the Black Chains…’

The Black Chains, clearly empowered by hidden code, remained unscathed, wrapped in black chains.

“Yahweh.”

The Black Chains that spoke retracted their chains and spread their liquid-like cloaks wide.

A skeletal figure holding a scythe appeared.

“In this world where everyone rejects you, where do you wander?”

“I came to meet the manager of this world. If you let me pass easily, things won’t get complicated.”

“…Why?”

“To eliminate the demonic realm in reality. The code you created is killing countless humans.”

The Black Chain lowered its head.

“Your words always sound plausible. But Yahweh, do you know? Human death is…”

Numerous Black Chains arrived and spread out, surrounding Shirone.

-Our life.

The movements of the Black Chains, hidden under their black cloaks, were completely beyond the laws of physics.

‘Chaos.’

They were incalculable.

Watching the fluttering cloaks, it seemed as if space itself was rippling around them.

-I will purify you.

As the black chains shot out with a slippery sound, Shirone’s body twisted.

-What?

Hundreds of chains missed their mark, merely grazing past Shirone.

‘Quantum movement.’

The algorithm used by the world’s strongest magician was as bizarre as a hidden code.

-Do not be complacent. He is Yahweh.

The Black Chains circled around, wrapping Shirone in black chains.

At the same time, those on the rooftop embedded chains and activated the purification code.

-Uoooooo!

They intended to neutralize Shirone’s Agape by combining it with the impure emotions of humans.

-It’s coming! It’s coming!

As the Black Chains, thrilled by Yahweh’s love, looked up, they saw the Hand of God.

The moment the giant sphere of light vibrated, Shirone’s voice echoed from within the chains.

“Photon Cannon.”

A vertical flash struck the right rooftop of the Wailing Wall.

“Noooo! Noooo!”

As the outer wall swelled like a balloon, the people forming the wall wore expressions of horror.

“No! No…!”

Finally, with an explosion, light burst from over 3,000 holes.
