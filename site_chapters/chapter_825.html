Chapter 825: Anger (1)

Hearing his heart pounding, Ogent looked back at Leard.

"Hahaha! This looks fun!"

Seeing Leard swinging his wooden sword noisily in preparation for the duel, a surge of murderous intent rose within him.

'Don't kill him?'

If Ogent expressed his feelings through violence, Leard would be reduced to dust.

But he still wanted to hit him.

'What a coward.'

Realizing his own murderous intent, Ogent gave a bitter smile.

Damian spoke.

"Ogent, I'm sorry."

Ogent didn't know what he was apologizing for.

"If I hadn't taken you in as my son, this wouldn't have happened. But... it couldn't be helped."

Time couldn't be turned back.

"Father, there's nothing for you to be sorry about." Although every word made Ogent angry, it was true that Damian had saved his life.

That's why his anger grew even stronger.

Without saying anything, Ogent walked to the dueling ground and grabbed his wooden sword.

'Just for this...'

Having never fought anyone seriously before, Ogent didn't know his exact skill level.

But he didn't think of himself as a frog in a well.

Although he had never fought a person, he could cut down aquatic creatures, something no other human could do.

"I'd like to go easy on you, but..."

Leard said, tapping his shoulder with the wooden sword.

"A true warrior isn't afraid of injuries. What can a piece of wood do? Let's go all out."

'Foolish.'

To someone who had reached a certain level, it was common sense, but Ogent couldn't help but laugh.

'You can kill a person with a single chopstick.'

"Show the power of the river clan!"

Despite the awkward situation, Smile confidently declared Ogent’s victory.

'Is she clueless or just pure...'

No, Smile had always been like that.

Unlike Ogent, who examined all the risks before doing anything, she was always fearless.

'That's why I...'

Ogent erased his thoughts and extended his wooden sword.

"Let's begin."

As he declared the start, Leard's previously smiling face turned frighteningly calm.

'He's not a weakling.'

The unique aura known as the giant's strength was emanating from Leard's body.

Of course, only Ogent’s senses could perceive it.

"Ha!"

Leard charged first, and Ogent turned his body belatedly.

"Wow..."

The spectators were quickly captivated by Leard's unexpectedly powerful swordsmanship.

Even Smile, who had always believed Ogent was the best, widened her eyes at the speed of the sword.

Ogent, the only one whose expression didn't change, calmly deflected the attacks.

'He's a strong man.'

He didn't want to belittle him as weak.

He was certainly skilled enough to represent the mountain clan and adept at handling the giant's strength.

'So, how strong am I?'

The reason he felt chills and a creepy sensation was because he confirmed a different dimension of martial arts.

'Can there be such a difference?'

In a sense that couldn't be compared numerically, Leard's movements were vividly clear in a completely different dimension of perception.

'This isn't about dodging.'

Almost like foresight, Leard's every move was being individually analyzed.

Ogent’s senses, which could walk ten steps in a day, had reached such a level.

With just 1% of his nerves, he could handle Leard, while the remaining 99% of his nerves were thinking.

'This man will become Smile's husband.' The woman he had never let go of in his heart since the day they first met was leaving.

His insides boiled.

"Yaaah!"

As Leard made a vertical slash, Ogent twisted his body and took an upward striking stance.

'Rather...'

If he struck upward here, Leard's neck would break, but he hurriedly stopped his movement.

Leard, who hadn't been attacked once by Ogent, glared at him.

"If you're going to do it, do it properly, you coward."

He didn't know why Ogent wasn't attacking, but it was clear he was mocking him.

"Let's stop. There's no point in continuing."

Their whispers didn't spread outside, but everyone fell silent at the subtle atmosphere.

"No point? This is a duel to determine the superiority of the mountain clan and the river clan. Are you still a warrior?"

"I'm not part of the river clan."

It was obvious from the color of his hair that Ogent was the chieftain's adopted son.

'What is this guy?'

Despite his skill in easily deflecting Leard's fierce attacks, he had an extremely timid and nervous personality.

'Even if he's not blood-related, does he treat his brother-in-law like this?'

A possibility flashed through his mind.

"Could it be..."

Leard, who had been watching Smile, turned his gaze back to Ogent’s face.

His originally pale face had lost all color.

"You perverted bastard!"

At the sudden outburst, both the river clan and the mountain clan looked bewildered.

Only Smile was just watching.

"I'll beat you to death, you bastard!" Leard, who already considered Smile his own, swung his wooden sword in a fit of rage.

But at that moment, the person most enraged was Ogent.

'I'll kill him!' The anger born from humiliation blew away his reason, and his sword swung with tremendous speed.

It was impossible to distinguish with the naked eye, but Leard couldn't move even 1% of his body.

Hold back.

As he heard the voice of his heart, countless thoughts quickly flashed through his mind.

'If I kill him...'

He would become a murderer.

How would Smile look at him? How sad would his father be? Could he endure everyone's criticism?

'Hold back! Please hold back!'

Thousands of thoughts telling him not to do it quickly put the brakes on his swiftly moving sword.

"Aaah!"

As a strong wind blew, the wooden sword stopped abruptly.

"Ugh!"

At the moment when Leard, who didn't even know how he had reached it, stiffened with the wooden sword in front of his eyes.

Rumble!

The trees in the forest behind him were all cut down and began to fall in the same direction.

"What, what is that?"

Ogent didn't know either.

'What is this?'

Although his senses had reached the peak of his physical body, it was the first time he had experienced things being cut without being cut.

'Something inside me has erupted.'

Law.

While he was thinking about the still undefined concept, Leard lost his reason.

"You bastard!"

The wooden sword, swung with the intent to kill, twisted bizarrely in front of Ogent’s face and then burst apart.

"Brother!"

People thought Ogent had been hit directly, and Smile stood up in surprise.

"What... are you?"

Only Leard, who felt the bizarreness beyond human comprehension, knew.

'Greatness.'

The anger in his heart sublimated into reason, affecting the real world.

It was a divine transcendence possible only for the highest giants.

"I'll be leaving now."

Throwing the wooden sword to the ground, Ogent bowed deeply to Damian and left.

Watching his son walk away, Damian sighed.

'Is he running away again?' Even if it meant ruining his daughter's wedding, he hoped his son would vent his anger at least once.

'Well, that's you.'

If he could run away forever, living a life of enduring everything was also a way of life.

"I'll go to him."

As Smile tried to follow Ogent, Damian grabbed her wrist.

"Don't go."

"Why? He's my brother."

Damian couldn't bring himself to explain.

But Smile, as if she didn't care, shook off his hand and turned away.

"Everyone's a coward."

She was frustrated, not knowing what they were afraid of.

Ogent, who had entered the deep mountains where Smile couldn't find him, calmed his mind through meditation.

'Slowly. More slowly.'

He didn't have the courage to face Smile.

'Forget it.'

He would leave tonight.

As always, he would run away from the fear he couldn't face.

'I see.'

The level he reached through the duel with Leard flooded in through realization.

'Ah.'

As if his brain had opened, endless thoughts flowed in without him being conscious of it.

Ogent’s body floated up on its own, and his hands rose while he was in a lotus position.

'I have transcended the physical body.' It was the awakening of a genius.

'Yes, this is enough.'

For him, the sword was not a means to achieve something but an escape from unhappiness.

'This is enough.'

Without realizing it, tears flowed as he was in a state of selflessness, and when he opened his eyes, the day had already changed.

"I need to pack."

The reason he didn't have the courage to meet Smile was that she had probably spent the night with Leard.

Although they were still strangers until the wedding, from what he had observed, most first nights were spent on the wedding night.

His heart felt like it was being torn apart, but he tried to ignore such thoughts and was about to descend the mountain.

"Brother." Smile was waiting in a corner of the forest.

"Smile."

His voice sounded pathetic even to himself.

"How did you know?"

Smile sighed and pointed around.

All the trees in the forest were bent to the point of breaking due to the law of Maha.

"This is..."

"No, I don't care about that."

Her tone was angry, unlike usual.

"Tell me what happened." Had he been caught?

"What?"

"You know it's not something to ask me, right? Now it's your turn to talk."

Ogent desperately tried to make excuses.

"I'm sorry. I'm not feeling well today. Tell Leard I'm sorry too. And the wedding must..."

"Brother, should I just not get married?"

Ogent's face hardened.

"W-What are you saying, all of a sudden? You can't cancel a wedding that determines the fate of the clan like this."

Smile, who was speaking words she didn't mean, pressed on.

"Do you know what I did last night?" The only thought in his mind was that he had to run away.

"Of course..."

"Nothing happened."

Smile took a deep breath and spoke, watching Ogent's expression change.

"Do you really have nothing to say to me?" It seemed like she was giving him one last chance.

'Will there be another chance for me?' The certainty that he could only change things now struck his mind.

'I have to confess. Even my swordsmanship can't protect me anymore.'

There would be no higher level than this.

"Smile."

Ogent grabbed Smile's shoulders.

"Marry Leard."

Maybe Leard had judged people well.

'I'm a coward.'

He was a pathetic guy who couldn't do anything from the start.

"Get married and live happily. That's what will make the clan and father happy. If you don't get married, everyone will be unhappy."

"Why make it so complicated? Just take responsibility for everything."

Ogent gave a sad smile.

'How can you say such wonderful things?'

Smile, who was like a great river flowing quietly but encompassing everything, was always an object of admiration.

"Take care, Smile. I will now..."

Ogent's head snapped around.

"What is it?"

Wooooooong.

The air vibrated as a clear sound wave wrapped around the entire mountain and moved.

'Vibration?'

It was the unique resonance of an angel that anyone living in Purgatory would know.
