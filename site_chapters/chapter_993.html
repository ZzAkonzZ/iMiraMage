Chapter 993: Karma Chain (3)

Galliant Island.

When the Ultima System Extraction Team arrived at Mount Toa, Chief Mahatu of the Kergo tribe came out to greet them.

"Welcome, Shirone."

"Yes. Have you been well? I hate to say this as soon as I arrive, but we'll be relying on you for a while."

"Relying on us? It's an honor for Kergo."

Mahatu led them underground to a mysterious temple.

It was a familiar scene for Miro and Gaold.

"The Kergo tribe. It's a fortress, but... is it really safe?"

In response to Sein's concern, Mahatu said, "There is a separate shelter underground. My direct subordinates will guard it 24 hours a day."

Since they had to go through Drimo, the team members would inevitably fall into an unguarded sleep.

This was why Shirone had set up a base on the island.

'We can't be active while sleeping. I want to avoid overlapping simultaneous events.'

Mahatu emphasized again, "Leave it to us. I will protect you with the honor of Kergo and my life."

Crawling on the ground, Arius said, "Dreamwalkers can infiltrate dreams. Through Drimo, we will directly enter Ymir's REM domain, the sixth deep layer. But getting out is another matter. We can't set up a door."

Divers implant keywords into the target and use them to set up doors.

"This is also a form of magic. We can't implant keywords into Ymir, who is outside the galaxy. Even if we go, we would die instantly."

Miro asked, "Then how do we get out?"

"If you really plan to come back alive, I'd advise you to stop now."

"Just answer."

When Miro kicked him in the butt, Arius fell forward and hit his chin on the floor.

"We'll have to ascend from the depths. We'll exit Drimo from Ymir's dream. But it's quite difficult because once we reach the deep layer, the upper layers become complete chaos. It's because the core of the ego is under attack."

Kangnan rested her chin on her hand.

"We can't avoid combat. In other words, we have to break through those egoists?"

"Hahaha!"

Arius burst into laughter and then quickly cleared his throat.

"Sorry. It was just too absurd. Anyway, think carefully. Ymir is the strongest even in reality. But now we're entering a world where Ymir is the master."

The atmosphere grew heavy.

"I don't want to instill despair. Our team includes Gakseon, Parkae, and Gaold. What I want to say is, let's not choose to fight and win until we're about to die. Once we enter Ymir's mind, we'll gain a lot of information. I'll find a way out somehow."

As soon as Arius finished explaining, the group entered a bunker deep within Mount Toa.

There were prepared beds and several days' worth of food and water piled in a corner.

Mahatu said, "Once the door is closed, no one can open it until you send a signal, forever. Even 100 years from now, the descendants of Kergo will guard this door."

It was a grand statement, but no one knew what would happen.

"Thank you. We'll be back."

With Shirone's farewell, Mahatu closed the door, and complete darkness enveloped them.

Shirone cast a lighting spell and pointed to the beds.

"Let's begin. It's best to plan specific strategies once we're inside Ymir's mind."

As the six of them each took a bed and lay down, Arius cast a sleep spell.

Shirone slowly opened his eyes.

He was in a cabin that seemed to have been abandoned for a long time, with moonlight seeping through the walls.

Focusing his senses through lucid dreaming, he felt something sitting in the chair in the center of the cabin.

Cold wind, the smell of old dust.

A thin girl in white rags was wandering around the cabin with her face hidden by her hair.

'A nightmare.'

He would probably be trapped in this cabin with the nameless girl until this section ended.

An ordinary person would have been paralyzed with fear, but Shirone calmly spoke.

"Monga."

The satellite of Luber and the dream architect.

"No time for games."

The cabin door creaked open, and a boy with a pale face and wide eyes entered.

"Sorry. But it's not a game. Using nightmares is the fastest way."

Shirone and Monga turned their gaze to follow the girl passing between them.

"What do you mean?"

"It doesn't mean anything. That's the nature of nightmares. In short, it just has to be terrifying."

Monga lifted the black hair covering the girl's face.

A face distorted as if melted by heat was revealed.

"Nightmares process negative energy. Events unfold in a way that you absolutely don't want to experience. In reality, everyone would want the girl's face to be pretty, and it would be, but..."

"Underlying that is a psychology that fears the worst-case scenario."

"Exactly. The important reason is that the Dreamwalker plans to implant a nightmare into Ymir. Even if he's the master of the dream, if he's surrounded by negative energy, everything will attack Ymir. That will allow our team to move."

Shirone blinked and fell into thought.

"I understand it's effective... but is there really a situation where Ymir feels fear?"

"We don't know. But since it uses internal energy, it's not impossible. Actually, what worries me more is what happens when the nightmare unfolds."

It would be on a different level from the nightmares of ordinary people.

"Let's go out for now. By now, the others should have arrived following their nightmares."

Shirone stood up from the chair and looked back at the girl before leaving.

As he stared at her standing barefoot, he slowly lifted her hair.

A beautiful face was revealed.

When the girl smiled, showing her white teeth, Shirone smiled back and patted her head.

"Everything will be alright."

At the edge of the wasteland absorbing the moonlight, Miro and the others were waiting.

Archipelago of the Ten Kingdoms.

The earthquake caused by Legion Commander Gamagin triggered a tsunami, submerging most of the island.

Shirone, aboard a sailing ship carrying refugees, looked out at the sea from the deck.

'The Kingdom of Dionas.'

It was the least affected country, but it would take several months to cross the windless zone.

"Messiah."

The Poison Dragon, Poine, approached Shirone.

"According to Kaios, the tectonic plates are still shaking. If a tsunami occurs, the people on the ship won't be safe."

"It's not a one-time event. This is serious." The demon realm is another system implanted in reality, making it impossible to stop.

'As long as the purification of hell isn't complete...'

While Shirone was deep in thought, the sailors moved blankets and food onto the deck.

"Everyone, take your turn! Supplies are limited, so we'll distribute only the allotted amount. If anyone breaks the rules, we don't know what we'll do!"

The reason for the threat was that surviving in isolation at sea was a brutal affair.

"...Food."

People with lifeless eyes began to gather around the supplies, momentarily showing signs of life.

"Step back!"

At that moment, a burly knight drew his sword.

"How dare commoners touch this! According to the kingdom's law, Minister Desika will be served first."

All eyes turned to Desika, the Minister of the Interior of the Kingdom of Rosarion, who was resting with a cold expression.

"Move! Move aside!"

The knight, who had driven the people away, gathered thick blankets and plenty of food for the minister.

The sailors, watching this, looked grim, but they couldn't confront the minister's knight.

"We have a problem. That scrawny man took one-fifth of the bread."

There hadn't been enough time to load supplies, so the food each person received was far from enough for a meal.

"At least he has to eat his fill. Well, it's only natural for living beings."

Poine's eyes glinted with a deadly light that couldn't be ignored.

"What will you do? Just give the order, and this old man will give a stern lesson..."

"Let's go."

Poine tilted her head.

"What?"

"We need to get our share too, bread and water."

Waiting at the end of the line, Shirone received his turn to gather supplies.

As expected, the bread was insufficient, and all he got was half a bottle of water and a shabby blanket.

Those who didn't get any food were angry, but the knight's sword was still more frightening than hunger.

Poine sat next to Shirone.

"You must have a lot on your mind, Messiah. In my opinion, 'extraction' is also an option."

Removing factors that hinder unity could be one way.

Shirone said, "Desika is not just. But to perform an extraction, we need a more fundamental standard. If everyone here were Desika, could they act differently? Humans can't be unified by principles or rules alone."

"The important thing is the heart..."

Poine held back her words, but the light in her narrow eyes grew even more chilling.

"Drink slowly."

When Shirone turned his gaze, he saw a little girl drinking all her water for the day.

"Oh dear. She must have drunk seawater. It's unbearable pain for children."

As expected, the girl, unable to quench her thirst, licked the last drop and cried out.

"Mom, I'm thirsty! I'm dying of thirst!"

The mother, who hadn't even touched the water, was even more tormented by her child's suffering.

"Water, water..."

As people's eyes turned to the minister, Desika slowly turned his head away.

'No water. Food can be obtained by the knights, but water is life at sea.'

At that moment, Shirone gestured and called out.

"Child, come here."

The girl, seeing the water, ran over as if entranced, and Shirone handed her the open bottle.

"Drink this."

The girl tilted her head back and emptied the bottle in an instant.

"Do you feel better now?"

"Yes."

When the girl lowered her head in embarrassment, Poine handed her his water bottle.

"Take this to your mother. But from now on, you must drink sparingly. Understand?"

The girl hesitated but eventually accepted reality, unable to ignore her family's suffering.

"Thank you."

A heavy silence fell over the deck.

A man across from them, seemingly determined, stood up and approached Shirone, offering his water.

"Here. Have a sip."

The man, receiving Shirone's gaze, scratched his nose awkwardly.

"I can't give you much, but we have to help each other. Drink. Don't feel burdened."

As if a wall had crumbled, people finally began to notice those around them.

"Here... let's share this."

A small piece of bread was split in half and handed to those who hadn't received any food.

"Grandmother."

A young man handed Poine his water bottle.

"Take this. You're old, and you won't last the day without a sip."

The gaze of the Poison Dragon, known as the most ruthless among the Twelve Apostles, pierced the water bottle.

To a dragon, it was just a drop of water.

But her smile carried a warmth that hadn't been there before.

"Thank you, young man."

Taking a sip of the lukewarm water, Poine turned her gaze to Desika.

'The standard for extraction.'

She finally understood the Messiah's words.
