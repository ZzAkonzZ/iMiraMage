Chapter 757:

“What is that woman?”

As the military police moved to surround her, the commander raised a hand to stop them.

“Hold.”

He felt something strange.

And feeling something strange meant that the commander’s senses were on a much higher level than the average soldier’s.

What the hell is this?

He felt like vomiting—and once he realized that, the dizziness became unbearable.

“What are you doing?! Hurry up and get them moving! Hup! Hup!”

Hearing the commotion from the Beast King Unit, he slowly turned his head with a grim expression.

It was a shocking sight.

“W-Why are they acting like this?”

The hybrid hounds, enhanced through countless Garas modifications, were fiercer than wild beasts and programmed to obey their handlers without question.

“Move! I said move!”

Even when whipped with a leather strap hung with metal studs, they simply stared into the distance.

As if desperately refusing to acknowledge the woman before them.

They’re afraid.

The nauseating aura the commander had felt—something unknown—was radiating from her.

“The dogs are better than the humans.”

As Minerva approached, the hybrid hounds slunk off to corners of the forest, as if suddenly remembering urgent business.

Their behavior resembled the way handlers avoided provoking the monsters in the Garas storage cells.

“Urgh!”

As Minerva passed by, the commander finally couldn’t hold it in and vomited bile.

“C-Commander?”

His subordinates looked confused, but the commander didn’t envy them for not feeling the same fear.

I survived because I threw up.

In three more minutes, the rest of them would drop dead—crushed by her aura, without even understanding why.

“Come here, my lovely puppies.”

To be caught meant death.

The hybrid hounds instinctively understood this and began pretending to sniff flowers.

One venomous hound tried to inch away but was quickly caught by her hand.

Even with its powerful legs, it didn’t think to run. As her touch landed, it buckled and knelt.

A reaper.

How many lives must one take to carry such an aura?

Her beauty, her witch’s attire…

Mirac Minerva.

The commander realized there would be no mercy.

“Oh my.”

The hound, trembling uncontrollably with its hind legs flattened, began to leak hot urine onto the ground.

“Are you scared? Of me?”

Whimper… whiiiimper…

Its eyes, nose, and mouth wept like it had aged twenty years, and one by one the military police and Beast King Unit collapsed with whites showing in their eyes.

“Let’s see…”

Minerva walked to the dog’s rear, bent down, and glanced at the urine pooling beneath it.

“A male.”

YELP! YIIIIK!

The hybrid hound gave one last, shrill cry.

“Die.”

As if a switch had been flipped, the dog’s eyes rolled back and it slumped lifelessly to the side.

“M-My god…”

Propped against a tree, Jacey stared in disbelief at the bodies falling all around her.

The Beast King soldiers, the military police—coughing blood and dying—and the hybrid hounds convulsing in death throes.

“Even if you’re from the Ivory Tower…”

The sole surviving officer gasped and glared at Minerva.

“It’s too late to stop it now. Under Supreme Leader Igor’s grace, the Republic of North Aymond will rule the world.”

“Sure.”

Minerva snapped her fingers.

The man’s soul left his body.

“Go tell the afterlife all about your world domination plans.”

Still panting, Jacey asked cautiously:

“Are… are you a resident of the Ivory Tower?”

Minerva pulled a pipe from her broom handle, lit it, and placed it between her lips.

Is she ignoring me?

Jacey tried again.

“If you are, you have to strike North Aymond now! If not, the Garas will overrun the world!”

Minerva exhaled smoke with a weary expression.

“I know.”

Then, slowly walking toward Jacey, she knelt to eye level and suddenly slapped her across the face.

SMACK—Jacey’s head turned with the blow, and she stared in disbelief before snapping back:

“Why did you hit me?! I can’t even move!”

Minerva hated humans.

If a person thought, “If I say it this way, I’ll get the response I want,” that alone was enough to disgust her.

I’d rather roll around with a dog.

Still, she didn’t show it.

“Sorry. I thought maybe this would help clear your head.”

Jacey bit her lip in frustration but had no intention of provoking a witch who had just subdued monstrous hounds with her aura alone.

“I know who you are. The Grand Witch—Mirac Minerva.”

Most of the Five Stars of the Ivory Tower were shrouded in mystery, but Minerva had made the most dazzling debut in the tower’s history.

“What are you going to do now? Are you going to go to Igor and demand he stop the experiments? Or…”

“I’ll handle it.”

To be honest, she hadn’t decided yet—but once she got to the capital and met the Supreme Leader, something would be resolved.

“Please take me with you.”

Minerva’s eyes chilled.

“…And why should I?”

“Because I know something you don’t. If you go to the capital as you are now, you’re going to walk straight into a trap.”

Minerva’s laughter echoed through the forest.

“Hey, little miss. Oh, sorry—you’re actually younger than me. Though let’s be honest, I look way younger.”

“…”

“Anyway, if you really knew what kind of life the Grand Witch Minerva has lived, you wouldn’t dare say the word trap in front of me.”

There was a deathly chill in the air.

“You think if your mouth still works, you can say whatever you want—even if your limbs don’t. That’s what you people are. Keep pushing it, and you’ll join those corpses.”

“I want…”

Her voice cracked with tears.

“I want to kill them! Those damned bastards from the North who killed my husband! I want to kill every last one of them!”

Jacey pleaded, her face twisted in grief.

“Please take me with you! I have nothing left! If I can kill even one more, I’ll gladly give my soul—!”

Rage.

It was the only human emotion Minerva didn’t find repulsive—the purest form of feeling.

“…Tell me what you know.”

Jacey snapped back to focus.

“The military police captain said there were eyes in places we couldn’t see. The North is using the Underworld. That’s the only explanation for how they discovered my identity.”

“Or maybe you just messed up.”

“How dare you say that? I’ve mastered every espionage magic out there!”

No matter how she shouted, her words bounced off Minerva like air.

“…Fine. So you’re saying there’s a chance Igor is in league with the demons.”

Even if it wasn’t a complete disaster, it was definitely unpleasant news.

“I’ll take you with me.”

Minerva took a deep drag of her cigarette, gathered the smoke between her lips, and whispered softly.

“Open your mouth.”

As if entranced, Jacey parted her lips, and Minerva leaned in and kissed her.

Ah…

The scent of the smoke that entered through her lungs was like burning herbs, and Jacey slipped into a rapture she had never experienced in her life.

Detoxification Magic – The Witch’s Breath.

She lost consciousness for a moment, but it lasted only an instant.

“There. You should be able to move now.”

She felt nerves awaken from the tips of her toes, and her muscles filled with strength as if nothing had happened.

Jacey rose awkwardly to her feet, at a loss for words.

“U-Um…”

It wasn’t the kiss that was the problem.

It was the fact that her body had experienced intense pleasure—something she had never imagined possible—that left her unable to lift her head.

“Don’t sleep with a witch.”

There were countless people whose lives were ruined after being enchanted by a witch’s sweetness, especially when it came to Minerva.

“…Because I hate humans.”

Leaving the dazed Jacey behind, Minerva focused her mana into her broom.

With a puff, the shabby wooden stick transformed into a two-meter-long staff fitted with a propulsion engine at the rear.

This was Jet—the pinnacle of human engineering, created through the wisdom of the Great Star in exchange for her giving up Lawslayer.

As it was officially registered with the Ivory Tower, it could only be considered a legitimate mode of transportation.

“Get on. We’ll stop by the military facility before heading to the capital.”

Minerva gently mounted the hovering Jet, sitting gracefully with her thigh perched on its side.

“Ah, yes.”

Known as the fastest object in the world and featured in history books, the Jet made Jacey swallow nervously as she sat on the back.

“Um… should I press or hold onto something? What if I fall off…”

“No need.”

The Jet glowed faintly violet, and as if she’d been molded into a smooth frame, Jacey’s body held firmly in place without effort.

“Here we go.”

The moment Minerva spoke, the Jet’s propulsion engine roared with an explosive blaze.

“Kyahhhh!”

Even though she barely felt the inertia, the rapidly approaching sky made Jacey scream.

It’s too fast!

The Jet looped through the sky once, then accelerated in earnest—within just four seconds, a sonic boom cracked through the air.

It had hit Mach speed.

With a blank expression, Baseto stood in place. Seeing him like that, Verdi’s mother covered her mouth with tears in her eyes.

“Honey…”

What had he gone through to end up like this?

“Those bastards…”

Baseto looked dazed, his eyes swollen from crying for who knew how long.

“I need to bring him back. He’s going to die at this rate.”

As Verdi’s mother stepped forward in distress, Shirone spoke coldly.

“Don’t move.”

She flinched and froze in place.

“He seems safe for now. Let’s stay calm and watch. If we cause a disturbance, the number of victims will only grow.”

The spirit of compassion meant that Shirone could not let others die just to save one person.

Something’s definitely off.

But the main reason Shirone still hadn’t acted was something else entirely.

There are too many demons.

Viewing the Underworld through the senses of bats, he could see demons patrolling in pairs.

But this place is outside the spirit zone…?

The Underworld shares spatial coordinates with the real world, but since the temples had opened, they tended to keep their distance.

Deciding he couldn’t take everyone along, Shirone turned to Albas and said:

“Please protect Verdi and her mother. I’ll go after those people and bring Baseto back.”

“But how? There’s nowhere to hide from here on. This is the main supply route.”

A vast open space stretched out, wide enough for ten wagons to pass side by side.

“In terms of space, yes.”

Albas tilted his head in confusion, but Shirone figured there was no point trying to explain.

Choeni Bardo.

Combining bat vision and dimensional shift, Shirone’s figure vanished as the real and Underworld overlaid.

“No way…”

Albas looked around in disbelief.

“Where did he go?”

Of course, Shirone was still right beside them.

After confirming the positions of the demons, he gently patted Verdi’s head.

“Just wait a little. I promise I’ll bring your dad back.”

Verdi suddenly knew what to say.

“Where did he go? Easy.”

What was this swelling feeling in her chest?

“Of course, he went to save my dad.”
