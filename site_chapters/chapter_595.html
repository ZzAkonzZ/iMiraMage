Chapter 595:

Yolga’s face turned pale as she looked at the lifeless child in her arms.

“What…!”

Everyone turned their shocked faces to see Lucan, with one arm crushed, leaning against the wall.

“You are?”

Yolga recognized Lucan, but his demeanor was so different from before that she could hardly believe it was the same person.

His bloodshot eyes were unfocused, and his entire body was covered in blood.

“Heh, heh heh.”

He coughed up blood and collapsed against the wall, smearing it with blood.

“Now… I can go home.”

“Hexaaaa!”

Miro screamed and ran to retrieve the child’s body, but it was too late to save him after being struck by the chain sickle.

As Miro turned her head in shock, Shirone stood there, equally pale.

Hexa was dead.

‘Then what about me?’

Looking at his palm, Shirone saw his once colorful skin turning gray.

He was dying.

The colorless hue confirmed it, and Shirone felt dizzy and staggered.

“Dying. I’m…”

“Get a grip!”

Miro ran over and grabbed Shirone’s shoulders.

“It’s not over yet!”

“But I’m dead. I…”

“Listen carefully. The closed loop can’t separate cause and effect. For the event that just happened to conclude, the timeline has to make a full circle.”

Hearing this, Shirone snapped back to his senses.

‘The event can be changed.’

“Go and move Istas. Yolga and I must not meet. Got it?”

Shirone nodded immediately and took off.

As she watched him go, Miro glared at Yolga.

“Are you satisfied now?”

If asked if she was satisfied, the result wasn’t disappointing.

Yet, the question remained about Lucan’s appearance.

“How was he still alive?”

“The events are overlapping.”

Mustang said.

“New events are rushing in before the existing ones complete their cycle. It’s a sign that space is collapsing.”

If the event where Lucan didn’t die was coming in faster, the same could be true for Hexa.

“Nothing is over yet.”

Yolga finally turned her sharp gaze to Miro.

“Damn it! What’s going on?”

Lycan descended the stairs, avoiding the advancing Mars troops.

Yet Mars was everywhere.

‘This is completely different from what Fermi said.’

As Istas repeatedly changed its structure, his path became increasingly tangled.

“Who is that guy?”

When Mars Unit 4 blocked his way, Lycan immediately darted into a side passage.

‘I’d like to fight, but…’

It was pointless.

Most importantly, he had the "Last Scream of the Yeager Family."

A one-time-use object.

If he was in danger, the object’s ability would activate, and he would lose 10 billion gold before he could replicate it.

‘This thing is a burden.’

Normally a noble item that saves its owner’s life, it was now a hindrance to Lycan.

‘Almost there!’

Lycan sprinted at full speed towards the end of the corridor.

It was the warehouse with the vault Fermi had designated.

Failure, again and again.

In the complexly moving warehouses of Istas, Shirone encountered numerous events but couldn’t reach the space he wanted.

“There he is! Get him!”

“Be careful! He’s strong!”

Mars’ captain Nickel, wielding the kingdom’s standard longsword, charged.

His skill was top-notch, and each thrust of his sword aimed for Shirone’s neck.

‘Burst!’

As the light barrier exploded, Mars’ troops were pushed back equally.

The Photon Cannon fired, and the corridor was lit up with the scattering movement of the flashes.

“Got him!”

As Nickel, who had persistently pursued Shirone, swung his sword, two tentacles grabbed the blade.

“Grr!”

Then, two more tentacles struck his abdomen directly.

Nickel’s eyes widened, and blood spurted from his mouth, causing his troops to pale.

“Captain!”

‘I don’t have time for this…’

Shirone desperately gathered his mental strength.

“There’s no time!”

Elysion broke the boundary of the Spirit Zone, and the entire corridor was entangled in the flashes of the Photon Cannon.

“Aaaah!”

Dodging the screams of the troops, Shirone turned and cast the spell again.

As the timeline changed, Mars’ troops disappeared.

“Quickly! Quickly!”

Shirone had already bypassed the space seven times, but the timeline was too complex, and no method could prevent Hexa’s death.

‘The problem is the man with the chain sickle. Miro definitely succeeded in her mission. It got tangled in an event I didn’t perceive.’

As he finished thinking, he saw the control room in the distance.

Location: Istas Warehouse No. 9

‘There it is.’

As he opened the warehouse door, Anchal suddenly appeared.

“Shirone?”

“Anchal!”

Shirone looked around and ran to her.

“Have you been waiting here all this time?”

“No, I just arrived. What happened?”

Shirone bit his lip and walked to the control device.

“I failed. The events keep changing, and I can’t stop them.”

“What are you talking about?”

As time passed, Shirone grew more anxious.

When the closed loop’s time completed a cycle, the event of Hexa’s death would conclude, and then everything would be over.

“Damn it! What should I do? What should I…”

He had already activated the device seven times but couldn’t overcome the event of Hexa’s death.

‘I need to attach the Supernatural Psychic Research Society to Room 36. Then bypass Room 11 and…’

Failure.

Mars was already waiting there.

‘Fine, let’s attach Room 55 above. Then take a big detour…’

Anchal approached and pulled Shirone’s shoulder.

“What are you doing? You said it’s dangerous to twist the events.”

“We’re all going to die anyway!”

Shirone was frustrated.

“We have to reverse it. I…”

“Tell me what happened. I haven’t encountered any events yet.”

“It’s my mistake. I shouldn’t have let Yolga meet Miro from the beginning.”

“What?”

“Hexa. I died. Soon I’ll disappear too. You have to save me before that.”

As Istas stopped operating, Shirone turned and spoke.

“Help me! This way!”

Anchal still didn’t understand but had no choice but to follow.

“Damn it! What’s going on?”

This was the eighth attempt.

He couldn’t determine the exact time, but Shirone had a gut feeling this was the last chance.

Event Coordinates

Time: 12:58 AM

Location: Istas Warehouse No. 65

As the space became more tangled, the events of the closed loop continued to overlap, and Lucan was shocked to see himself dying with his lower body crushed.

“What, what is this?”

Lucan, leaning against the wall, was equally shocked.

“Am I dreaming? Grr!”

Blood poured from Lucan’s lower abdomen.

It was clearly himself, but he felt no kinship with the destroyed form.

‘This is hell.’

Lucan realized only one thing.

“I have to get out.”

“Oh? A survivor…”

As the warehouse door opened, Yolga and her comrades entered.

“No, not a survivor.”

Mustang stopped Yolga.

Lucan with his lower body crushed and another Lucan with his right arm crushed were facing each other.

“What’s going on?”

“The events are overlapping. It’s related to Istas’ changes.”

“Damn you, Yolga!”

The fallen Lucan cursed.

“It’s all your fault! You did this to us!”

As always, Yolga bowed her head.

“I’m sorry. I have no excuse.”

As Lucan died, the Lucan with the crushed right arm approached Yolga.

“What’s going on? Why did I die?”

“…”

“Answer me! Why did you bring us to this horrible place?”

There was no way Yolga could know.

But her insight, defying countless times and variables, reached a blind conclusion.

“Do you want to leave this place?”

“Don’t talk nonsense! Once you’re in, you can’t get out!”

“There is a way.”

Lucan, who was ready to fight, suddenly stopped.

“There’s a way…?”

After thinking for a moment, Yolga nodded confidently.

“Due to some variable, you escaped the event of death. So from now on, follow me. And…”

Yolga closed her eyes tightly.

Prajna-Break Maria.

“When the opportunity arises, do what you must.”

That was the natural order.

Event Coordinates

Time: 1:32 AM

Location: Istas Warehouse No. 13

‘Finally arrived!’

As he opened the warehouse door, Miro, who had wiped out the Mars troops, was holding Hexa and panting.

“Who are you?”

Miro, full of vigilance, didn’t have time for Shirone to explain everything.

“It’s a long story! We have to go now!”

“What are you talking about?”

“Goffin’s door! If we don’t get there faster than any event, Hexa will die!”

She was already wondering why Hexa’s presence was fading.

“So it was you! Why did you come to Istas? Don’t you know where this is?”

Anchal spoke.

“We came because we know. The situation is serious. We come from a world that has experienced three resets. We need information from the upper echelons.”

Miro immediately understood.

“Enough! Lead the way quickly! There’s no time!”

Shirone immediately turned and led Miro and Anchal towards the Supernatural Psychic Research Society.

Since he had manipulated the warehouse, he knew the way perfectly.

Thanks to encountering numerous events, he was able to avoid the areas where Mars’ troops were stationed.

‘This action will twist the events again.’

According to the original events, Miro would have had to fight more battles to reach the Supernatural Psychic Research Society, but by guiding her, Shirone changed the events once more.

“We’ve arrived!”

Event Coordinates

Time: 1:40 AM

Location: Supernatural Psychic Research Society

Shirone arrived eight minutes earlier than the previous event and looked around.

Since he couldn’t reverse the event where he met Yolga, she would inevitably arrive here.

However, by arriving earlier, he could prevent her from contacting Miro.

‘It worked. Success!’

Miro, who had entered the research society, looked at the activated Goffin’s door and said,

“From now on, we’ll go to another space through the dimensional door.”

Miro held Hexa even tighter.

“What’s in the other space?”

“A sacrifice. It’s fair to call it a scapegoat.”

She didn’t want to make excuses.

That was Goffin’s will.

“To save the world, someone has to be sacrificed. And beyond this door is the sacrificial lamb for cosmic initialization.”

That was why Miro couldn’t tell Yolga the truth.

‘Yolga could never do it. I have to.’

Only an individual can sacrifice for the whole.

If the mission succeeded, today’s events would be forgotten, but her life would still unfold as a series of sacrifices.

Miro turned to Shirone in front of the door.

“You’re Hexa, right?”

As time passed, Shirone’s body was becoming transparent, proving it.

“Yes.”

“Do you want to see it? You have the right.”

“I’ll go too.”

When Anchal spoke, Miro nodded indifferently and headed towards Goffin’s door.

As Miro disappeared through the barrier of light, Shirone glanced at Anchal and then pushed himself through Goffin’s door.

His retinas filled with white light, and then the night scenery unfolded… In the distance, he heard the cries of mountain birds.
