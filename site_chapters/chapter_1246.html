Chapter 1246: A Terrible Truth (1)

The Church of Satan.

Seina charged at Shirone, cutting through the garden as blood poured from the sky like rain.

“Demonic Sword Astasia.”

What should have been a holy sword was now wrapped in dark red demonic energy.

“Seina! Snap out of it!”

It wasn’t just brainwashing or hypnosis. The fact that her heart had tipped toward evil was undeniable.

“I’m sorry, Shirone.”

Dark energy blazed from her body like fire.

‘She’s stronger.’

The power now radiating from her was beyond anything her divine energy had ever displayed. Shirone grit his teeth.

‘Gyorgy… you bastard.’

“I stabbed Maximus, the one who repented. I have nowhere to return to.”

“It was a mistake.”

It was just the result of Gultan’s psychological manipulation.

“No.” Seina shook her head.

“It was the opposite. I wanted to kill Maximus. What stopped me was appearances—fear—the shell of being a holy knight. That’s the truth. The human heart wants to be righteous… but also wants to destroy everything. No one’s heart is ever permanently one-sided.”

Gyorgy added, “It’s useless. Just as your convictions are strong, so are Seina’s. Though, knowing you, I’m sure you’ll push through. Go ahead, if you want. But you’ll die.”

The Spear of Demonflow would strike.

“This is the ability I gave her. Askar Covenant, Chapter 7, Verse 2. The effect is... it boosts the target’s ability to the limits of their imagination, at the cost of my own life.”

A classic trait of dark-attribute powers.

“Don’t worry, it won’t destroy the universe. No one can imagine that. Askar used Satan’s temptations to test the human heart. When granted an infinite source of money, how far would a human go?”

It was like a hidden code on par with Infernal Prison.

“That’s the extent of human imagination. Fantasies about owning the universe—if made real, they wouldn’t even know how to spend it. The subjects of Askar’s experiments showed remarkably consistent results. Want to guess?”

“They bought everything.”

“Exactly. Put differently, they could only buy what others were willing to sell. A few rare humans tried to act beyond that framework, but even they failed due to lack of imagination.”

As with all of Satan’s temptations, those who woke from the dream were dragged straight to hell.

“These are the kinds of things written in the Book of Askar. Of course, you and I could imagine much more... but this ability can’t be used on oneself. The imagination must come from Seina.”

Shirone looked at her again.

‘If it’s within the limits of an average human’s imagination, then maybe “defeating all who can be defeated.”’

Even that would make her nearly invincible, but there were those in this world who had transcended the limits of imagination entirely.

“What are you thinking…?”

Shirone himself had transcended, which made Gyorgy’s choice all the more puzzling.

‘Of all the powers, why pick Askar 7:2?’

Was this truly Catacomb’s strongest card in the battle against Yahweh?

“What are you doubting? You can’t attack Seina, can you? If there’s even a moment of hesitation, the Spear of Demonflow will pierce you. And that’ll be our win.”

“That’s a lie. Seina can’t kill me. You know that better than anyone. So what’s your real motive? Why did you bother capturing her in the first place? There had to be more efficient ways to kill me.”

“There aren’t.” Gyorgy shook his head.

“Everyone who’s fought you has tried their best. But no one’s ever succeeded. When it comes to efficiency versus efficiency, your record is undefeated. But I know you. I am your demon. You can’t attack Seina. That’s why this is the strongest method… and yet…”

And yet…

“Now, in the face of the world’s end… does it even matter?”

At this point, there was something Gyorgy wanted to understand more than winning.

‘Yahweh’s heart. The heart Yahweh cast away.’

“Shirone!”

Nade shouted, and Shirone moved—just in time to dodge the Spear of Demonflow, which smashed the space where he’d stood.

“I’ll block her!”

Eden jumped in front of Shirone, standing firm.

‘Their strategy is to wait for an opening in Shirone’s defense. I’ll stop at least one strike, even if it costs my life.’

With the pride of a Yora.

As both sides moved simultaneously, Seina swung her sword.

It was clearly the strongest strike imaginable by a human. But Shirone spotted a gap.

‘There!’

Gyorgy gave a bitter smile.

‘Invincible.’

If Catacomb had made a mistake, it was underestimating Shirone’s rate of growth.

‘You process moves like this with intuition now? Yeah… you really are the strongest in this world.’

Once, he had been Shirone too.

—Why did you abandon me?

When he was first born from Yahweh’s demon, this was the first thought that came to mind.

—If Yahweh is right, why do I exist? If we were wrong, shouldn’t we have never existed at all?

Can existence itself be a mistake?

“You’ll win.”

They both saw beyond good and evil. But unlike Yahweh, Gyorgy had the opposite nature.

‘Yahweh… this is my final card.’

As Shirone’s Agape began to wash over Seina, Gyorgy prepared for oblivion.

At that moment, the Spear of Demonflow struck Eden.

“Aghhhh!”

As the dark energy crawled over her barrier and filled her with terror, she grit her teeth.

“Oh Lord Yora.”

She had vowed never to be defeated by evil again after succumbing to Havitz’s corruption.

“Grant me strength!”

Giving it her all, the spear exploded—blasting her body backward.

“Eden!”

Her friends cried out as she tumbled violently across the ground. But Shirone didn’t turn around.

“I’ll purify her.”

The moment he pushed the demonic energy out of Seina’s body, Gyorgy would also vanish.

Light gathered in Shirone’s palm, flaring into an overwhelming radiance. Gyorgy closed his eyes.

‘Yes, kill me. Erase evil from this world.’

If something had no right to exist, then vanishing was only fitting.

‘But I’ve won, Yahweh.’

Even Ultima couldn’t reach this far.

“...Huh?”

Suddenly, the light of Agape vanished. Everyone stopped fighting and turned to look.

Shirone stood still, fingers resting on the forehead of a kneeling Seina—deep in thought.

“What are you doing?” Nade shouted.

“Hurry up and—”

But then he saw it.

Clear tears were streaming down Shirone’s cheek.

“Shi… Shirone?”

The Legacy Dream had come to an end.

Ymir’s Deep Layer One.

Shirone’s eyelids slowly lifted after experiencing the entire lifetime of Yorahan and Armand.

So that’s how it was.

A dream named Yor.

Even now, Yorahan’s dream was being passed down through Dreemo to the followers of the Yor religion.

A vast forgiveness.

It was the only path by which Good and Evil, once separated from Lilith, could be reunited.

I was the one who needed to forgive.

Even if everyone else threw away their burdens like trash, even if they all passed them off to one another...

Yorahan did it.

Even through pain far worse than death, he bloomed the most beautiful flower this world had ever seen.

Isn’t that right, Armand? The woman who became a sword to protect Yorahan’s heart offered no reply.

—There will be one chance.

Shirone now faintly understood what Armand had meant during their final encounter.

Thank you. It had been an honor that she chose him.

“I won’t back down anymore.”

Rising to his feet, Shirone’s gaze had changed completely. He surveyed the depths of Ymir’s subconscious.

As a thunderous roar echoed from beyond the horizon, Shirone looked up at the black hole.

There...

Within it lay the only remaining Ultima in this world.

Somewhere in the Pyramid of Truth, Canis clutched his head and screamed.

“Aaaaagh! No! This can’t be!”

Within the Wave of In, Canis could have denied the possibility that Arin was his sister—but...

I can’t do that. If I do...

He would lose his heart.

But even if he held on like this, all that remained ahead was a life of despair.

“Grrrgh!”

“Canis! You have to endure! I can’t lose you!”

“A... Arin...”

She had been with him since his very first memories of life—his younger sister?

Turning his head, Canis shed tears.

“...I love you.”

Arin had realized it through her Awakening.

Canis, unable to choose any life, was planning to take his own.

“I know.”

She pulled him into a tight embrace.

“Let’s go together. Let’s fight until the very end. We won’t bow to fate.”

Harvist, who had been given the role of ending their lives, wrapped them both in his long arms.

—Is this really okay?

Canis nodded silently, and as Harvist’s body began to vibrate in preparation for its explosion—

“It’s not over yet.”

A voice came from the darkness.

“Mr. Ares?”

Zulu, who had been traveling with them, approached Canis and handed over the fluid of the Ironclad Beetle, Amoros.

“Drink. It should help you hold on for a while.”

The liquid looked revolting—but Canis had already resolved to die, so it didn’t bother him at all.

“...Huh?”

It was a long shot, but the Amoros extract worked with unbelievable effect.

“The Wave of In is receding... how?”

Ares explained.

“They say it’s a creature that lived in the ancient pyramid. I’m glad it worked. For now, let’s find Shirone.”

“No need for that,” Zulu said, pointing to the opposite darkness.

Moments later, Shirone appeared, carrying Glenn on his back.

A look of relief spread across Ares’s face.

“You made it. You actually found us.”

“I wouldn’t say that. It’s more like Miss Lucia led us here.”

“What are you talking about?”

Lucia, who followed behind Shirone, examined the pyramid’s interior and said,

“My guess was right. We don’t have much time. We need to leave immediately.”

Supported by Arin, Canis asked,

“Leave? To where?”

They were in the Pyramid of Truth—a place that stripped away not only space and time but even the direction of one’s life.

Lucia turned back and said,

“To meet God.”

She took the lead, guiding them through the maze-like corridors without hesitation.

Arin asked, “Don’t tell me... you know the way?”

“No,” Lucia replied, turning a corner.

“I don’t know the way at all. I’m just... going wherever my heart leads me.”

Shirone added,

“It’s no coincidence we all ended up in this pyramid. From the start, God wanted Lucia to come here. Which means, we don’t even need to know where God is.”

Ares muttered,

“So wherever Lucia believes God is... that’s where God will be. But why? Why does God want her?”

“Me...?“

It came to her like fate.

“I think I can summon God. Probably.”

Silence followed as the group marched on, turning corner after corner.

And finally, they arrived.

“This is it.”

The top of the pyramid—the final chamber they thought they’d never find.

“God is in there.”

Lucia pointed toward an altar, upon which sat a strange device—like a vacuum tube, coursing with electricity.
