Chapter 1116: A Grand Dream (4)

‘The first day is a probing battle.’

The officials headed to their respective quarters.

‘Only one country can become the leadership council. No country will propose an alliance right now.’

Even if an allied country wins the vote, the issue of deciding the leadership council remains.

‘To establish the priority among allied countries, the balance of power must tilt to some extent.’

At this point, no country was willing to extend a hand.

As the thoughts of each country grew complex, Uorin entered the room and shouted.

“What are you doing right now?!”

Kido was watching, and Havitz was lying on the bed, blinking.

“What?”

“You brought up Shirone. Did you forget the deal? The condition for handing over the world…”

“You get Shirone.”

Havitz sat up abruptly.

“It’s a good strategy. If Shirone accepts my deal, it’s good for you too.”

“You can’t win his heart that way. Stick to the original plan. Stop deviating.”

“You’re the one deviating.”

“What?”

Havitz pointed at Uorin.

“The power of History Search. If Kashan becomes the leadership council, you can unite humanity under Ultima.”

That was why Uorin was confident Shirone would come to her.

“So what? I said I’d give you the power once I achieve what I want.”

Even among the lineage of Mitochondrial Eve, Uorin was the only one with foresight.

When she looked at something, the path it would take in the future appeared as lines of light.

The apple she was looking at had faint light flowing along its surface.

After a moment, the light in the shape of a hand connected with the apple’s light and dropped the apple.

“It’s not a big deal.”

Uorin turned her gaze at Havitz’s words.

“Seeing future movements. That much can be predicted.”

The reason foresight was powerful.

“The true value is revealed when you close your eyes. That’s the secret you wanted to hide.”

When Uorin saw nothing, the timeline could expand infinitely.

‘Because all signals are connected.’

Havitz’s fingertip, the table it touched, the floor connected to the table, the ground connected to the floor.

If the paths of all buildings, humans, creatures, and plants built on that ground were revealed as lines of light.

“You end up controlling history. Manipulating the variables within it.”

Havitz picked up the apple.

“So, what do you think?”

The golden timeline swayed massively.

“If I drop this apple here, what happens 200 kilometers away?”

The apple rolled on the floor.

Kido would pick it up, and by tomorrow it would rot, and the cleaner would collect it.

Of course, the apple would end up in the trash, becoming food for insects…

‘When the timeline is accelerated endlessly.’

Changes would occur in the colony where the insects lived, resulting in biological changes in 10,000 units…

“Stop.”

Uorin stopped thinking.

The foresight expanded the more you delved into it, eventually overloading the brain.

“Impressive.”

Even without Uorin explaining, Havitz saw everything with the divine frequency.

“My trivial action of dropping an apple changes the history of insects. It’s like being a god.”

Uorin wrinkled her nose because those insects could become humans.

“Seeing with eyes has limits. History Search’s range is too vast. At best…”

Finding Ra Enemy.

“No, no. There are no limits to the power. It’s because your brain is human.”

Havitz pondered for a moment.

“I heard there’s still a Gaian brain…”

Uorin’s eyes flared with killing intent, and hot anger rose in her chest.

“What are you trying to say?”

“Don’t forget why I kept you alive. If I tried to take it by force, you’d kill yourself, so I had no choice but to go along with your will…”

It was a power he desperately wanted.

“If foresight passes to Shirone, I might feel anger for the first time.”

Havitz headed for the door.

“My method might be better. Think about it.”

If he crossed into the Kashan area, there would be chaos, but there was no way to stop him.

As the door closed, Kido spoke.

“I don’t like it. It was an impossible deal from the start. He wants your power too much and is just using you. If this continues…”

“Shut up.”

Kido’s eyes hardened.

“Get a grip! Even if you create Ultima, Shirone already loves someone else!”

“Kido, please.”

Uorin slowly raised her head.

“Please… shut up.”

As evening approached, Arachne’s envoy delivered letters to each country.

“A banquet. How luxurious.”

Lupist placed the letter on the table, and Shirone looked down to read it.

“Most will attend.”

“Yes, the behind-the-scenes work that can’t be done in the meeting will start now. No one will miss it to gauge the atmosphere. The problem is…”

Lupist pointed to the last sentence of the letter.

“To ensure an enjoyable banquet, we invite entertainers from each country to brighten the event. On the surface, it might seem like a cultural competition, but…”

Albino spoke.

“The nighttime politics are starting. Arachne will use their beauty tactics in earnest. I wonder how effective it will be. Why don’t you experience it to gauge how much information might leak from other countries?”

Receiving no answer, he shrugged.

“I can’t do it at my age…”

“I’ll decide based on the situation. More importantly, the problem is our entertainers. I’d like to save them if possible.”

He didn’t say he would protect them.

“If there’s a malicious intent, I want to refuse. Do we really have to go that far?”

No one agreed with Shirone’s words.

‘Could they do worse?’

It wasn’t a matter of liking or disliking.

If everyone did it, Tormia had to compete.

‘But ignoring Shirone’s wishes isn’t good either. This is tricky.’

Lupist chose a compromise.

"It would be proper to ask for the performers’ group's opinion first. Shirone, could you go and relay this to the leader?"

“Yes.”

Shirone thought it was better to persuade them directly.

“A banquet?”

After arriving at the performers’ lodgings, Shirone explained the contents of the letter to Ozent Reina.

“It probably won’t be a pleasant event. It’ll be full of greedy people.”

“That’s likely,” Reina nodded.

“All right. I’ll prepare and head out.”

“What?” Shirone was surprised. She had assumed Reina would decline.

“Are you sure? You know what kind of event this will be.”

“Well, we can’t avoid going altogether, can we? We’re fighting for Tormia too, so we have to endure such things. They’ll obviously focus on Maya, but if things go awry, I’ll step in and handle it.”

When Shirone’s concern didn’t fade, Reina smiled and patted his shoulder.

“Don’t worry. Situations like this are common in the royal palace. Trust me. But the problem is…”

Reina glanced down the hallway.

“I’m not sure how Kaiden will act. You know, if his hand moves uncontrollably…”

“That’s true.”

It was clear the banquet hall could become a mess if Kaiden lost control.

Reina and Shirone visited Kaiden’s room to explain the situation.

“So, we’d like you to stay in your room for a while…”

“No.”

Kaiden’s response was firm.

“I can’t let Maya go alone. I must protect her, no matter what. Please let me go.”

“No matter what you say…”

Kaiden knew full well he couldn’t control the symptoms that caused his hand to move against his will.

“I won’t bring a sword,” he added.

“This is a delicate event. A small mistake could tarnish Tormia’s reputation. It might be better if…”

“Please let me go with her.”

As Reina turned back, Maya stood among the performers.

“If Kaiden doesn’t go with me, then I won’t go either,” she declared.

Shirone stepped in to reassure her. “Don’t worry too much. I’ll be at the banquet too, so nothing will happen.”

Though touched by Shirone’s offer, Maya shook her head.

“Thank you, but… I want to go with Kaiden. I won’t feel at ease otherwise.”

Maya.

Kaiden’s expression faltered. He wasn’t so clueless as to misunderstand her feelings.

She’s trying so hard.

"Getting the performers' group's opinion should come first. Shirone, could you go and relay this to the leader?"

“Yes.”

Shirone agreed it would be better to persuade them personally.

“A banquet?”

After reaching the performers’ lodgings, Shirone relayed the contents of the letter to Ozent Reina.

“It’s probably not going to be a pleasant gathering. It’ll be full of greedy people.”

“That’s true,” Reina nodded.

“All right. I’ll prepare and head out.”

“What?” Shirone was surprised, having expected her to decline.

“Are you sure? You know what kind of event this will be.”

“Well, we can’t just refuse to go, can we? We’re also fighting for Tormia, so this much we’ll have to tolerate. They’ll clearly be interested in Maya, but if things go south, I’ll step in and take control.”

When Shirone still looked uncertain, Reina smiled and patted his shoulder.

“Don’t worry. These situations are common at the palace. Trust your sister to handle it. The only problem is…”

She glanced toward the hallway.

“I’m not sure how Kaiden will behave. You know, if his hand starts moving uncontrollably…”

“That’s true.”

It was easy to imagine the banquet hall turning into a disaster.

Reina and Shirone approached Kaiden in his room to explain the situation.

“So, we’d like you to stay here for a while…”

“No.”

Kaiden’s response was firm.

“I can’t let Maya go alone. I must protect her. Please let me go.”

“No matter what you say…”

Kaiden knew better than anyone that he couldn’t fully control the symptoms that made his hand move on its own.

“I won’t bring a sword,” he added.

“This is a difficult event. Even a small mistake could ruin Tormia’s reputation. It might be better if…”

“Please, let me go with her.”

As Reina turned back, she saw Maya standing among the performers.

“If Kaiden doesn’t go with me, then I won’t go either,” Maya declared.

Shirone stepped in to reassure her. “You don’t have to worry so much. I’ll be at the banquet too. Nothing will happen.”

Although Maya was touched by Shirone’s offer, she shook her head.

“No, thank you, Shirone… but I want to go with Kaiden. I won’t feel safe otherwise.”

Maya.

Kaiden froze, his expression blank. Of course, he wasn’t so oblivious as to misunderstand her feelings.

She’s trying her best.

Maya was desperately struggling to fulfill her promise to forget about Shirone.

Kaiden stepped forward.

“I swear I won’t make any mistakes. Please, allow me to go.”

His resolve was unwavering as he glared coldly at his right arm.

You’re part of my body, so you know what will happen. If you mess up this time, I’ll cut you off.

His right arm didn’t react.

When they arrived at the banquet hall, about 200 people were mingling and chatting.

The performers, surrounded by prominent figures, were visibly tense.

“Tormia’s turn will be last. Performers, please enjoy yourselves until you’re called.”

The fact that Kaiden’s fate had been pushed to the final ten-minute slot indicated the situation’s complexity.

How sharp of them.

This wasn’t a time to act carelessly.

As the alcohol flowed, the once-cordial atmosphere began to heat up, and certain individuals stood out.

“Ha ha! That’s right! Rubian wine is the best, don’t you think?”

It was Marcus, the prince of the Iron Kingdom—a notorious womanizer in his early thirties, openly ogling the women in the room.

Even mid-conversation, his gaze landed on Maya, and he stopped talking.

“Well, well.”

Most people knew Maya due to her past affiliation with Valkyrie’s performers’ troupe.

“A famous guest, I see.” Marcus rose from his seat, grabbed another glass of wine, and approached her.

“You must be Maya the Enchantress.”

His voice was smooth, but his drunken, glassy eyes were menacing.

“Yes, that’s me. It’s an honor to meet you,” Maya replied politely, extending her hand.

Marcus’s fingers coiled around hers like a snake.

“Oh, such soft skin…”

Just as he was about to make a crude comment, he noticed Kaiden glaring at him fiercely.

“And who might you be?”

Maya, having anticipated this, calmly explained. “He’s my bodyguard. Kaiden, introduce yourself.”

“Bodyguard?”

What Maya hadn’t expected was that this was not an environment where common sense prevailed.

“This is insulting. Is this the level of Tormia’s manners? A performer’s bodyguard daring to glare at a prince?”

Arguing right and wrong was pointless.

If Kaiden attacked Marcus, the Iron Kingdom would immediately become an enemy.

Hold it in, Kaiden. If Tormia antagonizes Iron now, other nations will only laugh at us.

The cold reality.

With the eyes of officials from various nations watching, Kaiden had to make a decision.

He couldn’t let Maya down, so he lowered his head.

“My apologies for my rudeness…”

And at that moment, Kaiden realized in horror that his right hand was moving on its own, hurling a fist upward with incredible speed.
