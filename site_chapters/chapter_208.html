Chapter 208: The Truth of Heaven (2)

The handcuffs had a pattern similar to Hena's, which Shirone had seen in Nors Shelter, but they seemed to be fixed in space and did not move, and even magic couldn't be activated.

They probably wouldn't have used such methods just because they were afraid of humans.

Amy thought of a frog caught in a lab.

The reason they tie up the limbs before dissecting them is not because they’re afraid of frogs.

The thought gave her goosebumps.

At the very least, it meant that what awaited them was something against which it was natural to struggle.

Tess and Arin also remained silent, perhaps thinking the same thing.

As if the current tense atmosphere would last forever if no sound was made.

The only small comfort was that there was no drone on Tess's wrist.

Upon arriving in Zebul, Tess had managed to launch a drone without Ikasa noticing.

It was the ingenuity of someone trained in espionage.

'What happened? Why can't I see anything?'

But her efforts were of no use at the moment.

The drone was able to guide Shirone and his group to arrive as quickly as possible, but nothing appeared on her retinal screen.

She didn't know that the space itself was isolated by the Meca System's functions.

Kariel was checking a machine about 70 meters away from the globular cluster.

It was a huge pillar that reached the ceiling, and thousands of panels were emitting various colored lights.

Kariel floated ten meters in the air, arms crossed, watching the screen.

The screen was filled with complex symbols that Amy and her friends could not understand.

Amy observed Kariel's halo.

It was much larger and clearer than Ikasa's.

Diagrams and formulas appeared and disappeared like afterimages inside the halo, and each time they did, the machine's screen changed rapidly.

- System check complete. Initiating the Elixir of Life. Please input the molecular structure of the genetic material.

A chilling female voice came from the ceiling.

The machine's voice was impossible to analyze, even for Arin. Trying to use mental communication with Kariel would have been madness.

Kariel, who had been staring at the screen for a while, flew towards the women.

They could hear low-pitched vibrations like a contrabass.

It was a young man. He was two and a half meters tall and was wearing white robes. His halo was so bright it seemed to punch a hole through space.

Amy mustered up her courage and glared at him.

If something had to happen, she intended to be the first to face it. She didn't want to imagine Tess or Arin suffering first in this situation.

"Aren't you ashamed to call yourself an archangel? Aren't you going to take off these handcuffs?"

"What a base, human-like thought. I have restrained you to fulfill my duties as an archangel."

Arin, who was staring intently at Kariel, bit her lip.

This was the first time she had seen such a scene.

All she could see was a vibrating string. The vibrations were shaking in a complex manner, forming shapes, but even those were unclear.

Kariel existed purely as probabilities.

Amy snapped again.

"Duty? What do you plan to do to us?"

"Come to think of it, I forgot to introduce myself. I am Archangel Kariel. I am tasked by Ra to carry out the Elixir of Life in occupied territories. I will now administer the Elixir of Life to you as well. It is an honorable mission, so you should accept it gladly."

"That's ridiculous! What kind of nonsense are you talking about while tying up a person! Release us now!"

Amy shook her body. The handcuffs were fastened at waist height, so all she could do was stomp her feet like a child throwing a tantrum.

Tess, mulling over Kariel's words, asked back as if wondering.

“Wait a minute. Occupied territory? Does that mean Heaven attacks other worlds?"

Kariel was lost in thought. The law is always right. But to persuade humans, a different approach was needed.

"I am merely conveying Ra's will. God loves humans and desires their eternal existence. Your world will soon submit to Ra’s will as well.”

Tess was gripped by fearful thoughts.

The idea she had just conceived was that Ra was the tyrant of the universe. It occurred to her that the subjects of Heaven were also residents of an occupied territory somewhere.

Amy screamed.

“We saw the Elixir of Life! Is that how you love humans? You’re no different from a perverted murderer!”

“You don’t understand. Humans confuse love with power. Don’t think that being loved by someone is some great ability. Ra cherishes you simply because you are beings who can give names."

Amy recalled the book of lifespans. Fairies control the lifespans of subjects through names.

But isn't a name something anyone can give? What significance does giving names have to Ra?

Tess retorted sarcastically,

"So, what do you plan to do with us? Are you going to show your love by whipping us now that we're handcuffed?"

“You have an important mission.”

“Mission? What mission?”

“It is to conceive my child.”

Amy, Tess, and Arin's faces turned red at the same time. The idea was so horrifying and infuriating that it made their hair stand on end.

Amy couldn't control the trembling of her jaw and screamed,

“You, you pervert! Just try touching my body. I won't let you get away with it!"

“What a pathetic reaction. Don’t worry. I’m just transmitting angel information to your bodies.”

Tess was even more annoyed by the words. It was like the precise language of perverts who insisted on expressing things accurately.

“In short, you want to have a disgusting time with us?”

Kariel showed a displeased expression.

"Human expressions are so vulgar. The Elixir of Life is a highly advanced art that only I, among the archangels, can perform. Do not compare it to the reproduction of lowly humans.”

- The preparation of the Elixir of Life is complete. Information accumulation rate: 100%. Would you like to proceed with the replication?

At that moment, a female voice was heard from the ceiling.

Kariel flew upward as if no words were needed.

"Replicate."

- Replicating genetic material. Estimated time: 2 minutes and 30 seconds.

Kariel's halo expanded into a ring over 20 meters in diameter.

When he brought his index and middle fingers together, a bead-sized light was born at his fingertips.

Although small in size, its intensity was enough to illuminate the entire Great Hall of World.

Kariel returned the halo to its original form and came back to the women.

At the tip of the finger, where the light had disappeared, a violet diamond-shaped glass panel was created.

"This panel contains near-infinite life information. Do you understand? In order to create a human, we need information on all life forms that existed before humans.”

"I don't care! Whatever it is, we won't do it!"

Amy screamed. She was already so angry that she was about to die, but the explanation only made her even more irritated.

However, Kariel seemed to have considerable pride in the Elixir of Life and continued his explanation without an audience.

"The information of an angel is delicate and complex. This is why women who have already given birth have a significantly lower success rate for bearing Nephilim. In that sense, you are fortunate. You have the honor of carrying the offspring of a great angel within you."

“Nephilim…What are you going to do by giving birth to them?”

"They will be sent to other worlds. In this case, it will be your world."

"To convert the humans there and offer them to God?"

Kariel closed his mouth at the pointed question.

In the end, that was it. He made the women of the occupied territory give birth to Nephilim, educated the humans there, and then led them to God.

Tess realized that the Nephilim were some kind of spies.

The fact that they were free from the law meant just that.

Because they would never impose their country's laws on combatants hiding in an enemy country.

Kariel held out the panel floating at his fingertips.

“Don’t worry. It's only about transferring information, so you won’t feel ashamed.”

When the glass panel is attached to the forehead, glass spikes protrude and become embedded in the brain like roots.

Through these roots, the angel's information penetrates and creates life from the woman's body.

Kariel brought the glass panel to the forehead of each of the three women as if measuring if it would fit.

All their foreheads were smooth and beautiful, seemingly suitable for embedding the roots.

"Hmm, very well. Shall I let you decide? Who will be the first to receive this glorious art?"

Three women shouted at the same time.

“I’ll go first!”

The women looked at each other, as if they hadn't expected the reaction. The fact that they were fighting together gave them renewed courage.

Kariel was pleased.

To make the Elixir of Life successful, the angel's information needed to be precise, but the role of the host was also significant.

The success rate was roughly one in thirty, and failure would result in a fatal brain shock.

However, the stronger the mental and physical condition, the higher the chances of success.

In that respect, all three women were excellent choices.

- The replication of genetic material is complete.

As a female voice came down from the ceiling, Kariel raised his hand.

Panels of identical shape were created on the left and right of the vertically floating glass panel.

"Sisters with a strong bond. Since I can’t tell which is better, I’ll plant them at the same time.”

The three panels left Kariel's hand and floated toward the women.

Amy and her group were gripped by uncontrollable rage. They finally understood.

Archangel Kariel was the worst kind of pervert.

Rian and Canis arrived at Zebul.

As soon as they crossed the city walls, they saw the landscape of Zebul, with countless discs spreading outward. It was like seeing a city in the sky built on a lotus flower.

As expected from the outside, the interior of Zebul was a dimensional maze.

The way they jumped from space to space was too complex for human reasoning to calculate.

There must have been some rules, but the two had no time to think.

They walked only according to the coordinates, tracing the memories that Peophe had given them.

After going to the same place several times, Canis was able to find the portal to Ingris.

"Let's part ways here. Even if we know the path, finding it is another matter. It would be better if at least one person gets there first.”

“Is that allright? If we can't disable the Meca System, there's no point in me going first. If there's an enemy in Ingris, it might be more effective to deal with it together."

Canis looked back at the portal.

There were no special devices visible, but according to the memories Peope had given, this was definitely Ingris.

Since they hadn't encountered anyone so far, the probability of an enemies in Ingris were slim.

However, if there was an enemy, it would be at least as strong as Mara.

In that respect, it might be better to move together as Rian said.

But Canis did not change his mind.

He didn't know how long it would take to disable the Meca System. Wasting manpower was not a good idea under any circumstances.

“Nothing is certain anyway. There’s no guarantee that it’ll be easier if we both go. Let's each do what we can. That's the best option right now."

Rian was convinced. After organizing the Signa and Exud he received from Lena, he headed east of Zebul.

Canis took a deep breath and walked onto the platform.

There was nothing special he needed to do. As soon as he placed his feet on the coordinates, a crack in the floor opened up and light exploded with a bang.

He closed my eyes once and opened them again, and the scenery had changed.

Angel's Archive Ingris.

Unlike the warm feeling contained in the word ‘archive’, it was a mechanical landscape without any trace of humanity.

The paths were laid out in a cross shape with the central control unit at the center, and the walls were covered with vertically standing rectangular panels.

Harvest appeared, looking displeased.

To him, who sensed the world through vibrations, Ingris was a space filled with vibrations without a single blank spot.

Canis looked around to see if there were any enemies.

Perhaps he had to look every corner, but so far he haven't seen anything notable.
