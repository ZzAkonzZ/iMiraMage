Chapter 702:

"Wow, this is really exciting!"

Dangling from Kaidera's sharp talons, Kido marveled at the breathtaking view of the city sprawling a kilometer below.

"Kido, be careful. You’re afraid of heights, remember?"

"Heh, if I fall, you’ll catch me, right?"

Everything was a new experience for Kido, but Shirone, eyelids heavy with sleep, was far from impressed.

"Kido, I want to sleep. Come back up already."

Like a skilled rock climber, Kido scaled Kaidera's body with ease and returned to his seat.

"Already calling it a day?"

"We’re flying all day. I’ve seen enough. I’m just going to nap for a bit."

As Shirone lay down beneath Kaidera’s spine, Kido claimed the cockpit seat that Shirone had vacated.

"Now, let’s see…"

Shielding his eyes from the sun, Kido took in the endless horizon, feeling as though the scenery was pulling him in.

"The world really is vast."

Someday, he wanted to see where the earth met the sky.

When Shirone woke from his nap, Kido was still sitting in the cockpit, captivated by the view.

"Still staring at the scenery?"

"Yeah, just taking it all in. Who knows when I’ll get to see this again?"

While Rian stretched and got up, Shirone crawled along Kaidera’s back toward Kido.

"Where are we now?"

"We left the city a while ago. It’s been nothing but desert for the past 30 minutes."

It was the Akad Desert, the largest desert in the world.

"Hey, look over there. People."

A caravan of about 200 people was swiftly crossing a dune.

As if sensing the movement, Ratusa lowered its altitude, revealing traders in black robes and turbans riding strange, fluffy creatures with unusually long legs. Despite the rugged terrain, the animals showed no signs of fatigue.

"They’re called Balthans," Shirone explained. "They’re specially adapted to the desert environment and can survive for seven days without water."

"But can they really cross the whole desert on those?"

"The Akad Desert has many tribes, and they probably know where the oases are."

The merchants, noticing Kaidera, glanced skyward and immediately unsheathed their crescent-shaped blades.

"Be careful! It’s a predator bird!"

It wasn’t uncommon for Kaidera, succumbing to hunger, to attack desert tribes.

"Run faster! Hurry!"

Over 200 Balthans picked up speed, kicking up massive clouds of sand.

Kido tilted his head. "Why are they panicking so much?"

"They’re probably scared of us. Let’s just ascend and leave them be."

Unless one was as powerful as an archmage, Kaidera—a third-tier beast—was a terrifying predator.

"We can’t outrun it! Attack!"

In desperation, the traders fired arrows into the sky. The projectiles fell short, arcing harmlessly down.

"Would you look at that? They’re trying to fight back," Kido said, standing up and grabbing Ratusa’s reins.

"What are you doing? Just let it go."

"Have you ever seen a goblin let an insult slide? They took a shot at us; we should show them what we can do."

"They can’t even hit us."

"Don’t worry. I’m just going to scare them a little. Let’s go, Ratusa! Show them how fast you are!"

Ratusa, however, was indifferent to the idea. For a creature capable of flying 7,000 kilometers in a day, engaging with humans on the ground seemed trivial.

"Hey! Move it! What, you’re ignoring me now?"

An idea sparked in Kido’s mind.

"Ah, I get it. You’re scared of the arrows, aren’t you?"

Ratusa’s eyes narrowed, its pride seemingly wounded.

"Ha! A so-called predator bird that’s actually a coward. Just a fast chicken after all, huh?"

With a piercing cry, Ratusa dove straight down, skimming over the sand dunes.

The merchants, terrified, raised their bows.

"It’s coming! Shoot!"

Among the hail of arrows, some ice dagger spells from mages mixed in, but none so much as scratched Kaidera’s hide.

"Kido! Slow down a bit!"

Watching the ground blur beneath them, Shirone finally grasped just how fast Kaidera was.

"Aaaah! Get out of the way!"

Just before impact, Kaidera pulled up, sending the merchants sprawling in a cloud of sand.

"Ugh! What the hell was that?"

Covered in dust, the bewildered traders looked skyward as Kido pumped his fist in victory.

"Hah! How’s that? Think twice before messing with us next time!"

Shirone sighed deeply.

"I’ve always wondered—why do goblins enjoy bothering others so much?"

Kido grinned. "Because it’s fun, of course. Goblins don’t live as complicatedly as humans do. Even dying isn’t such a big deal. So why would someone else’s trouble bother me?"

"But you understand humans, don’t you?"

"Sure, but understanding doesn’t mean I feel the same way." Kido shrugged. "Who knows? Maybe if I eat enough humans, I’ll start thinking like one someday."

It was the most inhuman thing Shirone had ever heard.

Two hours later, Shirone rubbed his growling stomach.

"I’m starving. Let’s land and grab something to eat."

Though they could eat mid-flight, the prolonged flying had left his stomach queasy.

"Ratusa, let’s rest over there."

Pointing to an oasis about four kilometers away, Shirone watched as Kaidera circled to scout for danger.

"It’s been well-trained," he thought.

As they landed and moved to find shade, they noticed a broken-down carriage partially hidden behind some cacti.

"What’s that?"

It looked like it had been abandoned for a long time.

Rian approached and pulled back the tarp covering the wagon bed.

"… Shirone, you might want to see this."

Inside the wagon, a corpse lay slumped over, its neck bent at an unnatural angle.

On the other side of the carriage, Kido called out, "There’s someone here too. Well, was."

Leaning against the carriage was a woman’s corpse. On her lap lay a girl’s skeletal remains, her head barely held together.

When Kido turned the girl’s head, it detached and rolled to the ground.

"Ugh!" Startled, Kido shifted his attention to the woman’s body.

"Hmm."

He tore off a piece of flesh still clinging to the skull and popped it into his mouth.

"Bleh! Not edible."

Digging into her belongings, he pulled out a small pouch and shook it. Two copper coins fell out.

"…"

Pocketing the coins, Kido turned to Shirone.

"Find anything?"

"Not much. The meat’s gone bad, and there were only two coins."

Shirone and Kido clearly had different ideas about what "searching" meant.

"I found this notebook in the wagon," Shirone said, opening it.

Rian and Kido leaned over to take a look. The text was written in Kashan’s language, resembling a diary.

"Rian, can you read this?"

Most nobles were educated in at least one of the Triumvirate languages—Kashan, Gustave, or Jinchun.

"I can hold a conversation, but reading? Not really."

"I see. I’ve heard Kashan is a popular choice for Torimia nobles."

"It was for me, too."

"…"

Shirone sighed. It was time to rely on the Ultima System.

"I’ll translate it."

As Shirone activated the system, the essence of the text entered his mind.

"Looks like they were a family fleeing a tribal war. The exact date isn’t clear, but it says the daughter suffered from heatstroke."

"Diseases like that are common in the desert. But wouldn’t a desert tribe have at least some fever medicine?"

"I don’t know. Maybe they didn’t have time to grab it while fleeing?"

Rian continued inspecting the carriage.

"There are no signs of an attack. Could it really just be heatstroke?"

Shirone pointed to the final page of the notebook.

"It says this: 'The desert god comes, swallowing all into the void.'"

Kido gaped, his mouth hanging open.

"What the hell does that mean? Did they die of despair or something?"

"It could’ve been suicide," Rian suggested.

Shirone nodded in agreement.

"The mother and daughter lying side by side bothers me. Maybe the husband went mad… or it was some aftereffect of the heatstroke."

Kido’s gaze suddenly shifted to the edge of the oasis.

"Shirone, something’s coming."

As someone attuned to the laws of the earth, he could feel it before anyone else. Simultaneously, Kaidera stood up, its movements tense and filled with hostility.

"There’s a lot of them! And they’re incredibly fast!"

As Kido finished speaking, the far-off desert shimmered like a mirage. Soon, the entire horizon was consumed by a massive swarm of King Scorpions.

"Ahhh! What are those things?!"

The sight of two-meter-long desert-colored scorpions swarming in the thousands was enough to make anyone’s heart stop.

"They’re not targeting us. It’s a mass migration. Could there have been an earthquake somewhere?"

Kido turned toward Kaidera.

"Does it matter? Let’s just get out of here!"

"We can’t! We need to stop them here!" Shirone said urgently.

"Why?!"

"If they keep going, they might run into the merchants," Shirone explained.

Given the swarm’s scale and speed, the chances of the merchants avoiding them were slim.

"Damn it!"

Grabbing his spear, Kido moved to Shirone’s side.

"And what’s your plan? Take on all of them? There are thousands of them!"

"Exactly, Kido."

Shirone kicked off the ground, launching himself forward.

"Humans are complicated like that."

Rian followed after him, leaving Kido momentarily stunned before muttering under his breath.

"Screw it, why not?"

Diving into the swarm of King Scorpions, Kido curled into a defensive position.

"Let’s make it worth our time!"

Two hours later.

Over a thousand King Scorpion corpses lay scattered around the oasis, their grotesque remains littering the sand.

"Whoa! Whoa!"

As Shirone’s group finished their battle and left, a band of forty desert raiders riding Omek arrived at the scene.

If Balthans were the horses of the desert, Omeks were its hyenas—long-maned beasts with wolf-like faces and razor-sharp fangs. While their carnivorous nature made feeding them difficult, their agility far surpassed that of Balthans.

"… Did a god descend here or something?"

The Maga Raiders, a notorious gang known as the strongest in the Akad Desert, were left speechless by the scene before them.

"Sub-leader, if the King Scorpions are moving north, does that mean…?"

"Yes. Noskarta is coming."

The desert god, Noskarta.

Due to the Akad Desert’s geography, spanning both north and south of the equator, the temperature differences caused massive atmospheric pressure shifts. Noskarta was the name given to the rare equatorial storm, a colossal sand tsunami carrying unimaginable volumes of sand.

"The northern dunes show no signs of movement. It seems to have gone south."

"Damn it. The only place it could reach before Noskarta hits would be Vanguard, and even that’s pushing it."

The fact that the King Scorpions had traveled this far north meant Noskarta had already crossed the equator.

"Does it matter? Let’s head north ourselves before it catches us."

Just as the subordinate turned to leave, the sub-leader spotted a figure racing toward them on an Omek.

"It’s the leader! Everyone, stand at attention!"

The raiders, who had been inspecting the scorpion corpses, stiffened and turned to face their leader as her Omek came to a halt.

"Leader."

She was a woman who had joined the band a mere month ago. Within an hour of her arrival, she had overthrown the sub-leader and claimed the top position.

No one objected.

Without her, they wouldn’t have even dared to dream of ruling the vast Akad Desert.

"She’s not human. She’s a beast… no, the god of beasts," the former sub-leader had said after her defeat.

"… Someone was here."

The leader’s voice, low and animalistic, sent chills down their spines. Her sharp instincts were unnerving.

"Yes. It looks like they wiped out everything in a short amount of time."

Descending from her Omek, the leader slung her bladed halberd over her shoulder. Her tattered clothes revealed glimpses of her body, but none of the raiders dared to stare.

"I smell the scent of yaksha."

She was Paknyeo, ranked 9th among the Council of Ten, currently leading the Maga Raiders under orders from Ra Enemy to carry out a mysterious mission.

"Two of them, no less."
