Chapter 1118: Depths of the Abyss (2)

Gaold had a dream.

Flying rapidly over an endless sea, he was clearly in a state of agony.

His hair had turned white, and blood-tears flowed from his eyes, which were now just white.

'Air Press!'

A massive black monster, 2,000 times larger than a deep-sea whale, roared and writhed.

The demon Leviathan.

The strongest and worst creature existing in Ymir's mind, in the second layer of the abyss.

Kraaaang!

A single roar created a tsunami hundreds of meters high, causing Sein to grimace.

'Sun and Moon Halo.'

Two halos interlocked like gears, seemingly reducing the noise.

Kraaaang!

But another roar shattered them into pieces.

"Damn…"

With the suppressing force gone, Leviathan twisted its body like an unbridled horse.

Every time it struck the sea, shockwaves erupted, and the deluge obscured the group's view.

"Hiyaaa!"

With Miro's shout, the incarnation of Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara appeared, parting the water curtain.

Thousands of giant palms struck Leviathan's body.

Kieee!

How long had it been since they heard the monster's scream?

'It's no use. It has no effect.'

But that was all; Leviathan soared again without a scratch.

Shirone panted heavily.

'How long have we been fighting?'

It was hard to estimate real-world time as they had delved too deep and vast.

But judging by the number of engagements with Leviathan…

'It feels like six months.'

The total power was enough to devastate a real-world planet, but Leviathan remained unbroken.

'Because it's a dream.'

But even so, Shirone's group had reached the limits of their mental strength.

"Is this Ymir?"

They couldn't impact his mind.

The biggest problem was that no attack could frustrate Leviathan.

Luber flew over.

"Master, our strength is depleted. No, I should say our mental strength. If we drag this out any longer, we have no chance."

Six months with the demon was probably the maximum duration they could fight without rest.

"…Understood."

The resolve was quick.

'We have to try, whether it works or not. If we fail this time, it's really over.'

The only way was…

'The power of the outside world.'

Since Luber's object, "Zhuangzi's Butterfly," activated, Shirone's thinking had changed.

'From a god's perspective.'

The realization he had reached was a level the real-world Shirone had not yet attained.

'We need power that surpasses infinity.'

Yahweh's mind is infinite, but why isn't his power infinite?

Because the world is closed.

'Cause and effect are just logic created by time. If you set the direction of time to negative…

The result becomes the cause, and the cause becomes the result again.

"Infinity circulates infinitely." It's infinite.

When Shirone's Hand of God spread its palm, particles of light compressed.

"Krrrr!"

As cause and effect bit each other's tails and circulated, Shirone's body began to change.

Luber's eyes widened.

"That's…"

Shirone's body started to unravel like a thread unwinding from a spool.

'More, more!'

As cause and effect circulated, the unraveling form became grotesque, and eventually, his waist twisted like a pretzel.

"Aaaah!"

"Master! No!"

As Luber shouted, a black line began to wrap around the light of the Hand of God.

Above the extraordinary law where light and darkness coexisted simultaneously.

"Oh…"

The color of the Photon Cannon, born with a roar, was grotesquely mottled.

Like ink splattered on a sphere of light, the distorted colors were the unknown of the outside world.

'A world where cause and effect are reversed.'

Luber's expression hardened, and his gaze at Shirone turned cold.

"The outside world is…"

Shirone grabbed Leviathan's tail, which was heading towards Gangnan and Arius, and flew.

"Hiyaaa!" As he swung his arm, a massive flash extended and buried itself in Leviathan's side.

"Kraa…"

Another scream erupted, but this time the sound wave was sucked into Leviathan.

In the side, collapsed hundreds of meters deep, the black-and-white Photon Cannon caused a chaotic rotation.

As Leviathan's massive maw opened wide, the group looked horrified.

"Damn it…"

Before Gaold could finish his sentence, the demon's roar, expelled with all its life, erupted.

Graaang!

Every time the massive body writhed, the world shook, and cracks appeared on its black skin.

'It's going to explode.'

The light seeping from the cracks was abnormal enough for anyone to feel it.

"It's working!"

Arius shouted.

"We've conveyed shock to Leviathan. Finally, the first layer of Ymir's mind…!"

Before he could finish, the whole world was engulfed in white light.

"Gulp! Gulp!"

Arius suddenly choked on seawater rushing into his mouth and flailed.

'Are we falling?'

No, the sea was rising.

The explosion's light filling the sky gradually shrank as the water rose.

'It's okay. We're going to Ymir now.'

How long had they waited?

"Gah! Gah!"

In the darkness where no light penetrated, Arius flailed his limbs.

'When will we arrive?'

They were endlessly sinking.

The water pressure, or more precisely, Ymir's mind, was crushing them.

"Grrr…"

As the air in his lungs escaped, Arius's gaping jaw stiffened with cold.

Meanwhile, Luber searched for Shirone.

'There he is.'

Yahweh could withstand Ymir's mind, but Shirone wasn't moving.

'Is it affecting him?'

It was hard to guess his mental state after repeated reversals of thought.

'He'll die like this.'

As Luber looked at Shirone sinking into the darkness, he made up his mind, his eyes shining.

'The barrier of dreams.'

The Depths of the Abyss (2)

It was a forbidden act, but Luber had no choice if he wanted to save Shirone.

As Luber's body merged with the water, the sea's color began to turn a deep purple.

'Please be safe!'

As his consciousness quickly faded, Gaold searched for Miro.

'Where is she?'

Even without understanding the theory of dreams, his body sensed his current state.

"Grrr!"

A human body can't hold its breath indefinitely, but this was the world of dreams.

However, the physical parameters were converting into mental strain, destroying Gaold's mind.

'I won't give up. Miro…'

At that moment, he felt Miro approaching from the depths of the abyss.

"Grrrk! Grrrk!"

Finally reaching Miro, a cold hand wrapped around Gaold's neck. A voice like an echo whispered in his ear. Gaold.

Awakening from his sleep, Gaold blinked, recalling the dream he had just experienced.

Sunlight streamed through the window, and though there was a lot of dust, the smell was comforting.

"Honey, wake up."

Slowly turning his gaze, he saw Gangnan carrying a basket of food into the kitchen.

"Oh."

That's right.

'Gangnan is my wife.'

Then… who was the woman who hugged and whispered to him in the dream?

"That dream again?"

Gangnan asked while chopping vegetables.

"Yes."

The reason her voice was hoarse was that she, too, had the same dream every night.

It was always the same.

Waking from the dream, they would walk together, eat, and sleep together.

'When I fall asleep again.'

The dreadful battlefield would become reality.

"Phew."

Gaold took a deep breath.

'It's just a nightmare.'

Yes, a nightmare.

The self-replicating mutants that tormented him so much in the dream didn't exist here.

'It doesn't hurt.'

That alone was enough reason to live in this world, Gaold thought.

"What happened to us?"

He couldn't help but ask.

"That again?"

"I don't know what's real. Sometimes it feels strange, doesn't it? This place. If this is really reality…"

"Stop it!"

Gangnan shouted.

"It's just a dream! I don't care who you love in the dream! But this is too much!"

Their confusion stemmed from not knowing the cause.

How they came to live here, why they fell in love, why they had nightmares.

"Sorry."

Gaold got out of bed and hugged Gangnan.

"I was too sensitive. Yes, it's just a dream. Now that we're awake, let's start a happy day."

"…Eat."

Gangnan, pouting, handed him the soup, and Gaold moved to the table.

'It's not strange.'

Having no cause, that's normal, right?

'But it is strange.' A feeling of missing something.

'Time.'

They had lived in this house for a long time, but it felt like they had just started today. They had nightmares every day, but it felt like the first time.

"Let's go out."

His wife's food was delicious, and the sense of relief was something he could never experience in the dream.

Gaold put down his utensils.

"Let's get some fresh air. It's been a while since we went shopping."

Had he ever earned money?

"Sure, sounds good."

Gangnan's smile was comforting, even though the lukewarm, uneasy feeling remained.

Outside, people dressed as refugees walked between the skeletal high-rise buildings.

Swoosh. Swoosh.

The sound of waves could be heard from somewhere, but no one knew its source.

"Cheap! Cheap!"

The market was bustling with stalls, and most of the faces were familiar.

Gaold felt relieved.

"How much is this?"

Gangnan asked, interested in a necklace with a wolf's tooth on a leather cord.

"It's 200 Pisk, but I'll give it to you for 150. It's a very good item."

"Honey, I want this one."

At that moment, a large dog came running from the other side of the market.

Woof! Woof!

"Arius! Wait!"

As Gaold turned his head, he saw a dog with bandaged eyes running towards him.

"Damn…"

Gaold pushed Gangnan aside and blocked the dog, which crashed into his abdomen.

"Damn it! What is this?"

Grimacing under the dog's weight, a wet tongue licked his chin.

"Arius! Are you okay?"

At the sound of a woman's voice, the dog turned back, and Gaold stood up with an angry expression.

"Kid! You can't let your dog run around like… huh?"

Gaold stopped mid-sentence.

A young girl, about eight years old, was hugging the dog's neck.

It was a common sight, but Gaold's heart pounded wildly.

"Miro?"

"Huh? How do you know my name, mister?"

Confused, Gaold looked back and forth between Gangnan and Miro, lost in thought.

'That's the name of the woman from the dream. But… she's a child?'

Gangnan must have felt it too.

"Kid."

As she was about to ask something serious, a tall man approached.

"What's going on?"

Gaold realized that the man's appearance was identical to the one he called Sein in the dream.

"Oh, the dog ran over here."

Sein assessed the chaotic scene and bowed his head.

"It seems my daughter made a mistake. Arius is blind. He's usually calm."

Woof! Woof!

Before he could finish, Arius started barking at Gaold.

"Hey, what's wrong today?"

Grrr! Grrr!

Arius bit Miro's sleeve and pulled her towards Gaold with all his might.

"You really… huh?"

Frowning, Miro saw tears flowing from under Arius's bandages.

Grrr! Grrr!

As if he had to go to that man, Arius desperately pulled his owner.

"Arius…"

Finally sensing something, Miro slowly turned her head to look up at Gaold.

"Who are you, mister?"

"Kid."

Gaold asked.

"Have you ever had a strange dream?"
