Chapter 671:

"Fire! It’s coming from the Leaf Circus!"

Shagall, who had been shopping in the market, threw down the bags in his hands and sprinted toward the circus tents.

"Those worthless bastards!"

Though the Märchen Kingdom was vast and the circus toured everywhere, this particular town—where Shagall had first met Raiden—was the one place he absolutely loathed.

Not only had the trio of troublemakers who used to harass the circus grown into gang leaders, but they also constantly eyed Tia with predatory intent.

‘No!’

With his extraordinary athleticism, Shagall scaled the hill in an instant, but the flames were already raging high.

‘If anything’s happened to Tia…’

He shook his head.

‘Just be alive!’

Even outside the tent, there were corpses. The fact that Raiden, the quick-draw swordsman, had broken his vow not to kill was, in a twisted way, hopeful.

"Director! Tia!"

He flung the tent flap open—and stepped into a crimson hellscape.

"T-Tia…"

Among the dozens of corpses, Shagall immediately spotted her.

Tia’s body was covered in stab wounds, and nearby, the three vile gang leaders lay dead with their tongues lolling out.

Everyone was dead.

Everyone—except Raiden, the quick-draw swordsman, who sat atop a large crate, his head bowed.

If not for the fact that his legs were slowly swinging, one might’ve thought he was a corpse too.

His face was drenched in blood, and his eyes were utterly lifeless.

"Ra Enemy…"

"Director… why…?"

Did he kill Tia too?

"Director!"

As Shagall shouted, Raiden slowly lifted his head as if regaining consciousness.

"Ra Enemy!"

He leapt down from the crate and charged at Shagall. As if drawn by the wind, daggers rose into his hands by themselves.

Dozens of blades shot toward Shagall’s vitals, and instinctively, he mirrored the same technique.

The ownership of the daggers shifted back and forth in real-time, and master and student unleashed an overwhelming display of skill.

As Raiden once called him a genius, Shagall, now wielding seven layers of Schema, had already surpassed his teacher.

When full control of the daggers finally shifted to Shagall, Raiden's body was punctured by dozens of blades.

"Haa! Haa!"

He didn’t even know what had just happened.

Only the terror remained—Tia was dead, and he had killed the man who killed her.

"Ughhhh!"

As he descended into madness, Raiden spoke.

"Forgive me."

Shagall's head snapped up.

"Why did you kill her? Why—?!"

"Forgive me."

Once his personal creed, the phrase was now an empty, meaningless farewell left by his dying master.

"Why—?!"

While everyone else was battling Igor’s terror, Shirone stood alone in a dark space.

Fear is a feeling that arises either from imagining what hasn’t happened or being unable to turn away from what already has.

But with a KAR (Cognitive Awareness Ratio) level of nearly 90%, neither applied to Shirone.

That’s why the one who appeared before him was fear itself—Igor.

"Kar, de sumahm?"

Are you the only one?

Through the Ultima System, the meaning behind Igor’s words came through.

"You can’t overpower me. Just let us go."

"No."

Igor raised his spear.

"You are not the only one."

The spear of blue flame flew and crashed into where Shirone had been standing moments before, sending blue shockwaves rippling outward in concentric circles.

‘That would’ve been bad if it hit.’

Shirone teleported out in a wide arc and responded with a flurry of Photon Cannons. Though his blasts pierced Igor’s form, the body dissolved like smoke and instantly reassembled.

‘Fear doesn’t disappear just because you ignore it, huh?’

Igor now held a new spear in his hand, and as he swung it, a curtain of blue flames filled Shirone’s vision.

‘So that’s how you want to play it!’

Shirone soared into the air and summoned the Avatar of the Archangel, unleashing the Judgment of Light.

Beams of radiance clashed with spears of blue flame.

The relentless bombardment of holy punishment shattered Igor again and again—but his body always regenerated.

‘How do I destroy him?’

His right arm, the first to regenerate, hurled more and more spears.

‘Sibulsangpokmae!’

As Shirone dodged the thousands of incoming spears, Igor tilted his head slightly.

"You’ve realized a bit."

"What?"

With a twang, Igor’s energy strands vibrated, and then he multiplied to fill the horizon.

"Fear exists within you!"

Igor’s domain overflowed with spears of blue flame.

With nowhere left to dodge, one spear struck Shirone directly.

"Grgh!"

As the electric shock spread through his body, Igor’s will of fear bypassed Shirone’s 90% KAR and struck the remaining 10%—his humanity.

And what emerged was, shockingly—

‘What is this…?’

—a memory of Shirone’s own death from long, long ago.

‘Ikael?’

In Araboth, where Anke Ra resided, Ikael knelt, and countless angels rebuked her.

There was no sound—because the one holding the memory couldn’t hear.

Shirone had never seen her cry like that before.

Not because of the scolding.

For a grand archangel, even death shouldn’t have inspired fear.

‘Why are you crying?’

Her sobbing face held not celestial majesty, but painfully human emotion.

It was maternal love.

Tears poured from Shirone’s eyes.

‘Why are you crying!’

Though the memory’s subject was unmistakably Shirone himself, it was not a memory stored in his mind.

‘Kariel!’

The angel who tormented Shirone in Heaven now looked down on him with disgust before turning away.

Suddenly, the thought crossed his mind:

‘What state… am I in?’

What state could prevent him from hearing, leave all of his vision exposed, and even keep his mouth shut?

Soon, the Three-Horned Mara loomed into view.

Its red, lizard-like face, slitted nostrils spitting fire—yet Shirone felt nothing.

‘What… am I?’

He couldn’t see himself by any means.

As the Mara raised its massive axe with both arms, Shirone realized:

‘This is my death?’

A death he had never experienced in life now came flooding in as memory—and fear began to surge.

‘I’m going to die!’

The Mara’s axe came crashing down, ending Shirone’s life.

At the same time, the body pierced by Igor’s spear convulsed and unleashed a storm of electric energy.

"UAAAAAAHHH!"

Fear!

Igor’s terror ability had recreated a death from a past life Shirone had never known—forcing him to experience it.

This was exactly what Ra Enemy desired, and now, his event had finally begun to infiltrate Shirone’s mind.

—Got you at last!

Just as the sensory memories were trying to alter Shirone’s past, protrusions extended from Armand’s robe.

Minor World Possession!

All of the sensory memories were blocked, and Shirone’s body collapsed into a dark space where a small torch flickered.

“Huuh... huff...!”

Still trembling from the lingering fear, he heard the scraping sound of a blade being sharpened.

As he wiped away his tears and lifted his head, he saw a woman whose fangs protruded beyond her lips, sharpening a sword against a whetstone.

“What a pity, my master. Truly a pity.”

‘Master?’

The warmth of the torchlight and the return of sensation brought a wave of relief.

“Who are you?”

He had a guess, but couldn’t help asking.

“I’m sharpening my blade.”

It wasn’t really an answer. Shirone took a step toward her, only to stop in his tracks.

Only her face was lit by the torch; the rest of her body was hidden in shadow—a grotesque amalgamation of creatures stretching dozens of meters.

Among the disturbing forms, Shirone recognized familiar things:

The True Demon Parasite Galtomic, the Shelled Mineral Beast Ringer, a clinging Akamae, and even the unconscious fallen angel Ikasa—all melded into one.

“I can be anyone.”

When she raised her torso, her face alone rose ten meters high. The only part of her that retained human form, her right arm, pointed a sword toward him.

“Countless beings have possessed and violated me. You’re no different, Shirone.”

It was a spiritual assimilation, but Shirone still held control of his consciousness.

If it had been a full human-to-human merge, he couldn’t begin to imagine the agony.

“But I don’t mind. I was prepared for this. That’s why I accepted it when you stuffed all those things into my body.”

“Th-that’s…”

“It’s fine. It’s fate. But this, too, is fate.”

“What do you mean?”

“I only call someone ‘Master’ once in my life—when it’s time to claim their body and soul.”

Shirone slowly stepped back.

“You mean… you’re going to kill me?”

“There’s nowhere to run. I am this place—and so are you.”

Strangely enough, it made sense.

“Then tell me why.”

“You gave in to fear. Every master I’ve ever had has.”

“I haven’t given in! I can still fight!”

“Even if I let you go now, you’ll die anyway, Shirone.”

The woman gave a faint, regretful smile before revealing her killing intent and rushing toward him.

“It was brief, but I enjoyed our time together.”

Realizing her sincerity, Shirone fired Photon Cannons—but she deflected them all with ease.

“It’s no use. You and I are one.”

With Timeburst Perception, Shirone sensed both one second ahead and one second behind—but in all moments, she was there.

‘She’s sharing my time exactly.’

A second ago, and a second from now, her blade would fall precisely on his crown and split him in two.

Before that absolute death, taking place within unified perception, Shirone couldn’t even move.

‘So this is the end.’

There wasn’t even room for denial, so acceptance came easily.

Arian Shirone.

In the moment when the phrase carved recklessly onto her body glowed, another name etched inside it rose to mind.

‘I can’t die like this!’

Because Arian Shirone existed, the concept of Carmis Amy could be realized.

‘Because I exist, Armand exists.’

When the concept corresponding to Law Number 2 struck his mind along the path of insight, a flash of lightning tore through the darkness.

“……”

The woman’s sword passed straight through Shirone—and without showing any emotion, she slowly turned back to look at him.

“So it was I who was cut…”

The Blade of Perception.

Shirone and Armand, unified by the Ultima System, had been split apart—into existence and non-existence—by a single cut.

The woman who had pierced through Shirone walked over to the torch and began sharpening her blade once more.

“Go. Looks like you’ll live a little longer.”

“But I don’t know how to leave.”

“Liar. You already know, don’t you?”

Shirone realized the reason he had hesitated to leave.

“Um… thank you. And I’m sorry.”

He meant it.

“I’m not a being. I’m a concept.”

The woman turned her head and gave him a gentle smile.

“I’ll swallow everything you are. Grow stronger.”

Asking her identity felt like it would wound her, so Shirone simply bowed his head and turned away.

Outside this place, unspeakable terror awaited—but he no longer feared it.

‘Wait for me!’

Shirone’s determined gaze pierced the thin veil toward the Lord of Terror.

Pakji — the Seventh Sense.
