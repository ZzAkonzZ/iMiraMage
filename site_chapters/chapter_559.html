Chapter 559:

"Shirone…?"

The sound of sobbing filled the room.

Amy couldn't say anything as she watched Shirone cry bitterly.

"Amy."

Shirone's chin trembled as he spoke.

"Feope is dead."

It was a heart-wrenching revelation for Amy as well.

She remembered the small, cute fairy who had helped Shirone, Rian, and Tess in heaven.

And knowing that Feope was dead meant Shirone had indeed been to heaven.

"She died because of me. She used up her lifespan to save me…"

He couldn't tell Miro, Iruki, or Nade because just thinking about it was unbearable.

But Amy knew Feope.

"I went to find Ikael, and she was in my hands…"

Recalling Feope's horribly withered form, Shirone's hands began to tremble.

He could still vividly feel her frail weight.

"She said she loved me… but she was too small… I…"

Amy closed her eyes gently.

'This is why I didn't want to hear it.'

Because she knew Shirone wouldn't leave a farewell letter and disappear without a reason.

"I was so angry that I used Valhalla Action… but after that, I don't remember…"

"It's okay, Shirone. You don't need to bring up painful memories."

Tears dripped from Shirone's bowed face, and his voice came out in sobs.

"Feope…"

Amy felt a mix of sadness and anger.

'You idiot, I'm the one who wants to cry.'

As Amy, sitting on the bed, pulled Shirone's shoulder, his face fell into her chest.

Shirone burst into tears.

As his voice echoed in Amy's chest, the iron lock that had been closed began to open slightly.

"Ha, really…"

Patting Shirone's back, Amy looked up at the ceiling.

'Just this once. If it happens again, I won't be shaken.'

And so, Amy buried Shirone's sorrow in her heart.

The next morning.

Shirone headed to Istas as soon as he woke up.

His eyes were swollen from crying too much the night before, but there was something more embarrassing.

"Ah, why did I do that?"

His heart had pounded when he heard Amy's firm words, and from that moment, he had just felt sorrowful.

'But thanks to that, we reconciled.'

He didn't feel bad.

Thinking of Feope still broke his heart, but having someone to share his sorrow with was a relief.

Sometimes, you live on by crying.

'I'm feeling nervous for no reason.'

Arriving at Istas, Shirone frowned as he recalled the supernatural sense he felt yesterday.

Istas.

A multi-purpose complex structure made up of 89 warehouses.

Every prestigious school had such a facility, but Istas at Alpheas Magic School was special both historically and functionally.

'Goffin's Gate is here.'

Sein had founded the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Group, but they hadn't created the gate.

'It was probably an incident that happened back then.'

Lost in thought, Shirone arrived at the warehouse where Istas's entrance was located.

'I need to investigate before lunchtime.'

Until now, he had activated Time Explosion in the lab, but this time he planned to go directly to where he heard the sounds.

"The sounds came from the ceiling…"

Calculating the coordinates through the master equation, Shirone headed for the stairs.

'Hmm, this is the place.'

He was scared to do it alone, but investigating a bit was the least he could do for his friends who were helping him.

'Time Explosion.'

The incarnation of the Archangel surged as if it would explode and then was absorbed into his body, and the hallucinations began.

"Captain! We can't hold them off!"

"We're going to die anyway! Target the child!"

Running towards the source of the sound, Shirone stopped with a grimace at the sight before him.

"What is this?"

Bodies, broken and scattered along the stairs, most armed with weapons, some even mages.

Shirone examined the gruesome state of the bodies closely.

"This is…"

The weapons bore the insignia of the Kingdom of Tormia.

'Why are kingdom soldiers here?'

"Surround them! Block the passage!"

Hearing the sound from upstairs, Shirone quickly climbed the stairs.

As if time and space had shifted, the line of bodies abruptly ended at the warehouse boundary.

Confirming his current coordinates with the ceiling markings, Shirone stopped, staring at the back of a woman standing in the center of the intersection.

"Miro?"

He didn't know what year this scene was from, but recognizing Miro, who hadn't aged in nearly 20 years, was easy.

Miro, facing the soldiers in front, slowly turned her head.

"Who are you?"

'Can she hear my voice?'

It wasn't like a hallucination.

He thought he might actually be at the scene of an incident.

"Is it really… Miro?"

Miro turned back to the soldiers.

"You must be a student. Go back. This isn't a place for you."

A moment later, she muttered to herself.

"Well, it's already a captured time."

"What? What does that mean…"

As Shirone tried to follow Miro, who was charging at the soldiers, a group of soldiers rushed in from the right hallway.

"Who's that?"

"He looks like a student! What should we do?"

"A student? How did a student get in here?"

The captain, pondering, glared with murderous intent.

"Kill him! There's no way out anyway!"

"Take him down!"

The soldiers' movements suddenly accelerated. They were elite soldiers, masters of Schema.

As they charged with eye techniques, Shirone's body felt like it was caught in a net thrown from all directions.

"Ugh!"

The first thought that came to mind was whether their swords would actually cut him.

Given that he could talk to Miro, it didn't seem impossible, but it still didn't feel like reality.

'How foolish!'

It wasn't a matter of whether it was possible or not; he had to dodge.

Overcoming the eye techniques with the mental strength of Diamond Body, Shirone used teleportation, and numerous swords and spears slashed through where he had been.

"Tch! So he is a magic school student?"

"He's still a kid! Finish him quickly!"

Blades flew from all directions towards the spot where the flash of teleportation had landed.

'Damn…!'

As the longsword, a common weapon of Tormia, aimed for his neck, Shirone's eyes flashed.

'Time Explosion!'

Suddenly, Shirone's head jerked back, and the blade narrowly missed his throat.

"What the…?"

If time were expressed as space, Shirone's range of activity was much broader than that of someone perfectly confined in a mold.

But because it was time, it was bizarre, and even the trained elite soldiers couldn't help but be confused.

"It's just magic! Kill him!"

At the captain's shout, the soldiers' killing intent surged like blades.

"Ugh!"

Cornered, Shirone leaned against the wall, glaring at the soldiers closing in on him in a semicircle.

The captain, breaking through the encirclement, charged with lightning speed, swinging his sword.

"Try dodging this!"

As the sword light slashed in all directions, the smell of death hit Shirone's senses.

'I can't dodge!'

"Die!"

As Shirone deactivated Time Explosion, the blade that had been right in front of him suddenly disappeared.

"Gasp!"

But from his perspective, it felt as if the sword had pierced him, and he instinctively held his breath.

"……"

The hallway was just silent.

"Gasp! Gasp!"

Breathing heavily, Shirone slid down the wall.

'I almost died.'

He couldn't be sure, but the feeling was incredibly vivid.

'What did I just see?'

One thing was certain: he should never use incarnation techniques here.

Meanwhile, about 70 meters away from where Shirone was resting, voices were quietly exchanging words.

"What's with him? He's going crazy by himself."

"Is he insane?"

"He's probably training. He did teleport and all."

The voices were faint, and their owners were not visible.

Having regained his composure, Shirone headed back to the research group.

'I'll wait until lunchtime and ask the others…'

In an instant, Shirone's eyes flashed.

'…I need to see.'

He had sensed the presence of intruders through his heightened senses after the battle.

'Who are they?'

Pretending not to notice, Shirone continued walking, but when the intruders didn't reveal themselves, he changed direction away from the research group.

'They're still following. I'm definitely the target. In that case…'

In the middle of the hallway, where the warehouses were connected in a line, Shirone stopped.

"Come out."

There was no response.

"I know you're there. If you don't come out, I'll attack."

Counting to three in his head, Shirone suddenly expanded his Spirit Zone to locate the enemies.

But it seemed they had anticipated this, as three transparent cloaks peeled off the wall like wallpaper, revealing their wearers.

"Damn! We've been caught! Run!"

Shirone, who had prepared a powerful Photon Cannon, watched the retreating figures with a dumbfounded expression.

They were all familiar faces.

"They're advanced class students?"

Even as they quickly fled down the hallway, Shirone regained his composure and deactivated his magic.

Instead, he carefully noted the ceiling markings, the color of the iron doors, and the number of intersections.

"Let's see, the coordinates here are…"

"Damn it! Who messed up?"

As the girl complained, the two boys running beside her pointed at each other.

"It must have been you!"

"No, it was you. My hiding skills are way better than yours…"

"Eek!"

The girl screamed as if she had seen a ghost, and the two boys stopped abruptly.

"H-how?"

Shirone was already blocking their path.

"How did you catch up so quickly?"

Shirone sighed as he approached.

"Catch up? You barely moved 10 meters according to the coordinates."

When they didn't respond, Shirone asked, "So, Shuamin, Alt, Gerein. What are you doing here?"

They were juniors from Class Five who had taken integrated classes with Shirone in the advanced class.

Especially Shuamin, who had shown off her Spirit Zone skills by counting coins when Shirone had first climbed over the Alpheas Magic School wall at twelve.

She had been younger than Shirone then, but now she was a girl, shrugging her shoulders slyly.

"What do you mean? We're here to get teaching materials for the teacher, of course."

Shirone chuckled at the innocent lie.

"Really? Then I can just ask the teacher?"

Alt's lips twitched nervously.

"Haha, are graduation class students so free these days? Do you really have time to check with the advanced class?"

"Yes, I do. I'm refusing the graduation class evaluation, so I have plenty of time."

As their faces turned sour, Shirone approached.

"Tell me the truth. Why are you here?"

Shuamin and her group exchanged glances and then simultaneously reached out their hands towards Shirone.

"Huh?"

"Wind Cutter!"

They suddenly cast attack magic, which was surprising, but Shirone's body reacted reflexively.

'Radiance.'

As the sphere of light flashed, the wind blades were crushed and dispersed into the air.

Of course, Wind Cutter was an impressive spell.

But at the advanced class level, it was impossible to break through Shirone's defensive magic.
