Chapter 871: All That Conflict (1)

In a situation where the entire excavation team was silently pressuring her, Dorothy honestly confessed.

"Yes, that's how it happened."

Whenever they reminisced about their school days over drinks, Dorothy would always mention Iruki.

Knowing her feelings, no one criticized her, but they had to separate personal feelings from work.

"I know we have no choice but to accept it."

Kanis said.

"But if this is overturned because of personal feelings, I at least want an apology from Iruki."

Sabina twisted her lips.

"Do we really need to be so persistent? Let's just resolve this ourselves."

"I joined the team not for money but for the relics. This kind of compensation won't do."

Everyone values different things, so Sabina couldn't stop him.

"Kanis, let's just back down." Arin said.

"After all, we're all old schoolmates. Iruki must have a lot on his mind too."

Kanis was more surprised than disappointed.

"Why the sudden change? Even putting emotions aside, it's the right thing to do."

Arin looked at Dorothy.

"You know what's going on with Iruki, don't you?"

With everyone's eyes on her, Dorothy's lips drooped.

"Iruki... might die." Closer frowned.

"What are you talking about?"

"I don't know the details either. But if he continues to overload his brain like this, he could end up brain dead..."

Kanis said.

"Overdrive."

He remembered the blue electricity that used to flash in Iruki's eyes during their magic school days.

Arin sighed.

"Iruki is unique even among savants. I always thought he wouldn't last long. If it's true, he shouldn't be on the battlefield anymore. He needs rest."

Closer snorted.

"Is that his personality? Besides, he's the Chief Commander leading the Holy War. Overload is inevitable."

Sabina smiled bitterly.

"Still, you're amazing. Knowing that and still wanting to meet him?"

Dorothy lowered her head.

"It's not something I should say as a team leader, but can't we help Iruki? I'm asking you."

"Kanis."

When Arin turned around, Kanis was openly showing his displeasure.

"...What an idiot." Fighting for humanity while destroying your own brain?

"Fine."

Kanis raised his hands in surrender.

"Let's do whatever she asks. We just need to leave here, right?"

A voice came from outside the temporary building.

"No. It would be troublesome if you left."

When Iruki opened the door and entered, everyone looked surprised.

"Iruki..."

Iruki quietly accepted their sympathetic gazes and smirked.

"I knew it would be like this."

He didn't want to be treated like a corpse already.

"Don't look at me like that. My brain is still fine. It's not like I want to die."

Kanis snapped back.

"Who cares? Anyway, what do you mean it would be troublesome if we left?"

"I mean it literally. You need to stay here and help me with my work."

That was why he revealed the secret.

"This is ridiculous."

"I'm sorry for disrupting your dreams." Kanis stopped as he was about to stand up.

"Honestly, fighting demons alone is overwhelming. I desperately need help. There's no one better than old schoolmates. I can trust you guys."

If they could take over even one part of her burden, Iruki's brain would be much more at ease.

"Tsk, you should have said so from the start."

Kanis sat back down with a sulky expression, and Arin smiled bashfully.

Sabina asked,

"Alright, since it's come to this, we'll do our best to help. What are we mining?"

"It's radioactive material for elemental bombs. A partner company will arrive soon. Of course, you'll be in charge. As long as we meet our target quantity, you can protect the dwarf relics."

Kanis joined the conversation.

"How urgent is this?"

"Nade is already designing the production line through his company. We'll get cooperation from Ainka Alchemy."

"Ainka? Richard's father?" Richard from the Golden Wheel graduated in the same class as Shirone.

"No. Richard is now the owner. His father Marto is in charge of the superhuman project in Valkyrie Weapon Development Department."

"This is almost like a reunion."

It meant that Iruki was cornered that much.

"I'm returning to the Holy War. I'll focus all my efforts on stopping Hell's army. I can't afford any other thoughts. I'll leave this place to you guys."

Dorothy asked,

"What about progress reports?"

"Nade will come down soon once the production line stabilizes. Discuss details with him." Sabina propped her chin with both hands.

"Everyone's working hard."

Their efforts would be medicine extending Iruki's life.

Closer shouted,

"Alright, let's do it! Let's turn this mountain range upside down!"

During their school days, a fiery passion was kindled in everyone’s hearts as they reminisced about their rivalries.

Havitz's army was currently cutting straight through the center of the central continent, crossing the Kingdom of Ferris.

"Go! Time is power!"

Before gathering the scattered demons from around the world, Balkan intended to push forward as much as possible.

Amid the Valkyrie soldiers being trampled by demons, Havitz stood out the most.

"Panting heavily while on horseback, an army of ten thousand lay before his eyes.

Yet no one knew that Havitz was approaching.

Even the royal guards did not move until he reached the commander.

"Hear this, proud army of Valkyrie!"

As the commander shouted, Havitz’s sword sliced through his neck.

"We… ugh. We are…"

Why couldn’t he speak?

The royal guards turned around with puzzled expressions, soon shouting in shock as they realized what happened.

"Commander!"

His head had already fallen and was rolling on the ground, but the commander’s expression seemed oblivious to it all.

"Advance..."

His life was cut short.

"What happened? Who did this!"

As one of the guards raised his head, he saw a burst of red flame.

As the light dissipated, Havitz on horseback was revealed.

"It’s the Emperor! Kill him!"

The arrival of this influential figure made them forget even the commander's death as all the guards charged at him.

At the same time, a red swirl emerged around Havitz, revealing the incarnation of Satan.

Incarnation magic? "What is this?"

The knights lost sight of Havitz and halted their charge as suddenly seven of their heads fell.

"What? What?"

The shock was profound; none of the slain realized what had happened to them.

"What is this! What in the world!"

"Tomi…"

One of the soldier’s colleagues, terrified, said.

"Your, your face…"

"Huh?"

It seemed as if Havitz’s sword had sliced from the top of his head to his left cheekbone.

"What about me?"

He was unaware that half of his field of vision should have disappeared, which was terrifying.

"You, you’re dead..."

Before he could finish, the guard with the missing face flipped his eyes back in death.

"Ah, ahhhh!"

Another guard screamed in horror and madness, unaware that his lower body had been severed.

From a distance, the noises from Valkyrie’s army reached Balkan, who smirked.

"Havitz has awakened."

It wasn’t invisibility or an illusion.

"The incident itself does not exist."

Operating outside the laws, those within the laws could not perceive him.

Even if someone broke a finger, they would not feel that anything was wrong with their finger.

"That is Satan."

The very embodiment of chaos.

Havitz, detached from hell's army, rampaged through Valkyrie’s forces.

"Linked."

Swallowing the woman's milk, he had realized a fragment of this world's crucial secret.

"If I set my mind to it, I could connect with anything."

"Ah! This is hell! This is hell!"

Havitz leaped off his running horse and started slashing wildly at the soldiers.

"You! Your, your head..."

As Havitz continued to slash at a soldier’s head until he tired, he suddenly knelt and closed his eyes.

"I'm sleepy."

Infinitely free.

As he woke and slowly stood up, all the soldiers turned to look.

"Over there!"

Havitz, now visible to their perception, watched indifferently as a large army charged at him with spears.

"If connected, even death…"

A spear thrust through Havitz, protruding from his back.

"Is just another form of life."

A soldier shouted in disbelief.

"I got him! I killed him!"

As hell's great army surged forward, Valkyrie’s forces were swept away, and Balkan found Havitz lying on the ground, bleeding from the spear wound.

"Zetaro!"

Zetaro, officially a medic, quickly turned his horse's head towards Balkan.

"Why! What’s going on...?"

He too was shocked upon seeing Havitz.

"Havitz! Damn it! What is hell’s army doing!"

"I already sent them. I did."

Despite significant blood loss, Havitz’s face showed no fear.

"This is tricky. Natasha, can you pull out this spear?"

Natasha approached.

"Oh? It’s pierced by a spear?"

Nonchalantly, she grabbed and pulled out the spear, causing sparks to fly from Havitz's eyes.

"That’s it. Let's start the first aid." Watching Zetaro pull out tools from his bag, Havitz asked hoarsely.

"Doctor?"

"Yes?"

"Do you have a way to save me?" As Zetaro turned his shaky gaze slowly to Havitz, he swallowed hard.

"Expecting a lot."

Havitz’s eyes sparkled brightly.

"I'm a jokester. This has to be funny."

Zetaro, rummaging in his bag, steadied his voice and said,

"Well, first, the method of emergency treatment is…"

Suddenly, madness took over Zetaro’s eyes, and he stabbed Havitz's abdomen wildly with a scalpel.

"How would I knowaaaah!" As the scalpel plunged in repeatedly, Havitz grimaced in pain and burst into laughter.

"Phahaha! Phahahahaha!"

"Aah! Aaaaah!"

Caught up in his act, Zetaro was grabbed by Balkan and thrown backwards.

"You madman! What are you doing!"

Blood gushed from Havitz’s slashed abdomen.

"Havitz! Snap out of it!"

As Balkan confirmed Havitz had lost consciousness, he bared his teeth and glared at Zetaro.

"You..."

"Oh, come on, Havitz likes it."

Balkan sighed.

"Let's retreat. Treating Havitz is the priority."

Since Havitz’s awakening, Balkan had been unable to predict his judgment.

"Damn, we’re stuck here now." For the first time, hell's army halted its march.
