Chapter 521: The Nameless Swordsman (5)

The battle had momentarily subsided.

Dozens of people had already died, and the bandits were in a state of near-madness, but the incident where Rian cut through the fireball was astonishing enough to extinguish their fighting spirit in an instant.

The wrath of the Yaksha transcends the law and manifests in action.

Commonly known as Axing.

Among them, Deny was Rian's unique technique that broke the opponent's will with his own.

In summary, it is a thesis that endlessly overlaps the law with the feedback of 'If you must do it, you must do it,' denying all the principles of the world.

"How did you… use magic…?"

Breeze still couldn't face reality.

Of course, a fireball combined with omniscience and omnipotence could be cut with a sword within the Spirit Zone, acting like real fire.

But the problem was not that the fire was cut, but that the magic itself had disappeared.

The magic suddenly vanished as if a candle had been blown out, which was not a normal phenomenon even to the bandits who were laymen.

"Is that so…?"

Faycon of the Red Spear stood up.

He still couldn't understand the result before his eyes, but if he expanded the category to a strange event, it wasn't entirely unimaginable.

On the battlefield, countless rumors circulate that cannot distinguish between truth and falsehood, and among them are the occasional stories of swordsmen with transcendent abilities that cannot be realized by Schema.

"I wondered what you believed in to attack the stronghold at such a young age."

Faycon gripped the red spear leaning against the stone chair.

"Kill him with all your might. He is a different kind of human from the swordsmen we know."

Following the commander's words, the officers lined up on either side drew their weapons and glared at Rian with fierce eyes.

"Let's go! Full attack!"

As Faycon swung his spear and jumped off the platform, the bandits, who had been stunned, regained their senses and charged at Rian.

Rian looked at the enemies with a calm gaze.

'Don't get attached.'

Rian put all his will into moment-to-moment judgments, and each time the greatsword swung, the enemies were cut down.

It was a mechanical severance, like cutting paper with a ruler and a knife.

And that was the unique swordsmanship that made Rian known as the Knight of Maha.

As the number of bandits fell below fifty, Rian began to gain momentum for the first time.

How unreasonable is it for one life to take seventy lives?

The bandits trembled in fear, feeling their hearts stop just by meeting Rian's eyes.

"Come here! You brat!"

Faycon charged at Rian, swinging the red spear.

The moment the sword and spear clashed, Rian's mind, which had been in a trance, returned to reality.

The sensation of colliding with an iron wall, not a person, transmitted through his fingertips.

'This is Faycon.'

Even without the red spear, Faycon's skill was outstanding.

The order of the technique tree was strength, nerves, mitochondria.

In particular, the base strength enhancement was multiplied in efficiency through a technique called folding.

"Haha! Is this all? Try rampaging like before!"

Rian was repeatedly pushed back by Faycon's spear techniques, which came in without giving him a moment's rest.

If it were one-on-one, he might have found a way, but fighting a master while surrounded by bandits was impossible.

Moreover, the officers assisting Faycon were far beyond the strength of ordinary people.

'Grandfather, how did you come up with this?'

As Rian fought with all his might, the image of Ogent Clump naturally came to mind.

When he was living as the youngest son of the family, under the devoted care of his parents and siblings, he thought that subjugating bandits wouldn't be difficult, but experiencing it firsthand made him realize how indulgent he had been.

'Still young.'

Faycon narrowed the distance with shining eyes.

He wavered greatly whenever a crisis approached.

Of course, he wanted to give points for eventually overcoming the crisis, but it was clear at a glance that he lacked experience.

'He's just stubborn, but his sense is too lacking. How did he reach this level? He should have died long ago.'

That was Faycon's diagnosis, having lived in the world of swords for many years.

"Ugh!"

As the blood-stained spear rushed in to sever his lifeline, Rian swung the greatsword widely.

It was a counterattack that would have worked at any other time, but Faycon had perfectly anticipated Rian's breathing with just a short duel.

'Dodge…'

The greatsword brushed past Faycon's nose.

'Stab.'

Bang!

The spearhead, twisted and thrust, pierced Rian's side about the size of a fist and passed through.

'Huh?'

Faycon's eyebrows slightly raised as he had aimed for the heart.

"Ugh!"

Rian's pupils rolled up as he gritted his teeth and endured the pain, and the afterimage of the incarnation appeared again.

Faycon, uncharacteristically for a veteran, was flustered.

It wouldn't actually be visible.

If some energy emanating from Rian passed through the brain and formed a special image, there would be no spatial limit.

Enormity.

He didn't know what the largest thing in the world was, but if there was, it would be this, baring its teeth.

At the same time, a sound like noodles being pulled from water was heard, and black lines sprang from Rian's side, quickly restoring the empty space.

'The wound is regenerating?'

It was a strange phenomenon that couldn't be explained by Schema.

"Ugh!"

Rian charged with his eyes rolled back.

Different from refined anger, boiling emotions exploded, making it impossible to control his body.

The nature of the emotion was belligerence.

The desire to fight, to face stronger opponents, led Rian's sword.

-Smile. Smile.

"Now's the chance! Kill him!"

If he had raised his firepower while ignoring the preservation of his body, his defense would inevitably be sloppy.

'No, this isn't just sloppy.'

He was attacking like a person without a life.

"Stab! Just stab!"

They could kill him now.

But in the face of the explosive momentum like a god of war, only Faycon among the bandits could thrust his sword.

"You fools!"

The remaining number was twenty-two.

If they lost more here, it would truly be irreversible.

Faycon, who had grasped Rian's intention to target the mage Breeze, closed the distance using his subordinates as sacrifices.

As Faycon's spear continued to pierce Rian's body with brilliant spear techniques, hallucinations were heard again.

-Smile. Smile.

Hearing the voice like the sound of wind swirling at the bottom of a deep valley, Rian regained consciousness.

'I'm scared. So scared, Shirone.'

Why was this happening to him?

Even when his right arm regenerated after eating Ymir's arm, and when he fought countless battles with the in hand, he didn't hear hallucinations.

One day, he started hearing them.

It was probably when he first suffered a near-fatal injury.

But the problem was, Clump, who had heard the same hallucination, had only experienced it once in his life.

So why wouldn't these hallucinations leave him alone?

The only thing he knew for sure was that this wouldn't last forever.

Rian gripped the greatsword tightly.

'It doesn't matter. As long as I can fight now!'

Bang!

Faycon, who blocked Rian's attack, grimaced at the shock that felt like his entire skeleton was being crushed.

"Heh!"

A laugh of fear leaked out.

"Hehehehe!"

Clang!

As he pressed his body close to the spear shaft and held on, Rian's charge stopped.

Between the crossed weapons, Faycon revealed a face of madness.

"Do you want to carve your name into history? Huh? Do you think it will be that easy?"

Who started it first?

The person who did the crazy thing of trying to make a name for himself by defeating the bandit gang.

"It's over. Your future is to become a corpse and be food for the birds."

Faycon turned his head and shouted.

"What are you doing! Finish him off!"

No one answered.

'No way…'

Feeling uneasy, Faycon hurriedly looked around.

No one was standing.

In three hours, Rian had cut down all 120 bandits.

'Maybe this guy…'

Might make a name for himself in history.

As he turned his head with that thought, Rian spoke.

"Die."

Ah, I'm going to die.

The overwhelming power in his voice made Faycon think so naturally.

"This…"

As Faycon tried to leave his last words, Rian's arm slowly rose.

Seeing the completely empty torso, Faycon thought he might survive again.

'If I stab the heart…'

But at that moment, he realized.

Not only his body but even his tongue, which was trying to speak, wasn't moving.

The gap in time due to the transcendent relative speed.

In reality, Rian's arm was moving faster than Faycon's tongue.

The two arms with Deny moved down several times faster than they had risen.

Bang!

The greatsword struck the ground, and with a shudder as if the world was splitting, Faycon's body was split in two.

"Ugh!"

Rian grimaced as he endured the shock of pushing his body to the limit.

As his muscles twisted, it felt like he was transforming into another creature.

"Whew."

It took Rian about five minutes to lift his waist.

"I'll be bedridden for two days."

He had survived, and with this, he had completely fulfilled his responsibility to the Sylvia family.

"I won't waver anymore."

It was a fight closer to death than life, but for Rian, who learned from real battles, it was a great achievement.

"How lucky. I didn't expect to find you here."

Rian turned his head at the voice coming from the entrance of the mountain stronghold.

"Who are you?"

A tall man in black iron armor was looking over the corpses with a sly gaze.

Judging by the scribe standing behind him with a pen and paper, he was clearly a noble on a knightly quest.

However, he seemed too old to be undertaking such a quest now.

"Are you the Knight of Maha? I've heard the rumors."

"Knight of Maha?"

Rian tilted his head at the unfamiliar title, but the man, unfazed, drew a long sword like a key.

The sword was also made of black iron and adorned with sophisticated patterns.

"As rumored, you're a sly man. But the sight before my eyes is undeniable proof."

As the man's eyes, with eyelashes like flower petals, gleamed with murderous intent, Rian's body reacted instinctively.

He was a master Rian had never met before.

"Subjugating bandits, huh? I used to do that whenever I was bored in my youth. Now I miss it."

Seeing no reaction from Rian, the man, Lambda, swung his sword and took his stance again.

"What are you doing? Come on, attack. Don't tell me you're waiting to die without knowing what to do?"

It was common for duels to occur during knightly quests.

However, Rian had just annihilated 120 bandits, and the effects of Deny had not completely worn off.

"Of course, I know."

Rian smiled and turned to Lambda.

The two swordsmen faced each other from a distance of 20 meters.

Although it was much farther than the reach of their swords, considering the speed of Schema's movement, it was not a distance to be underestimated.

"Come. I'll expose your true form."

As Lambda thought this, Rian's figure disappeared.

In the realm of thought, only the infinitely extending horizontal flash was visible.

It was as if Lambda's three-dimensional body had been cut into two dimensions, with his waist, wrist, and the black iron sword severed at the same hardness, falling to the ground.

"……"

The scribe stood with his mouth agape, unable to write a single word on the paper.

As he slowly turned around, he saw Rian, holding the greatsword, descending the mountain.

Bang!

Rian's muscles from shoulder to wrist burst, exposing the bone, but his fingers still gripped the greatsword.

-Smile. Smile.

Feeling the muscles in his right arm reconnecting, Rian moved out of the scribe's sight.

He would become stronger.

He would reach the end of the world.

"That's where your place is, right, Shirone?"

Only after Rian completely disappeared did the scribe swallow the saliva that had filled his mouth.

His master of ten years had died, but instead of grief, he felt a surge of admiration.

"The rumors… were true."

The trembling pen tip slowly brushed across the paper.

The Knight of Maha.
