Chapter 1159:

Son Yu-jeong said nothing.

She didn’t find Mortasinger disgusting, but she wasn’t sure how to react.

"So, you fell into Hell too? Hah! I knew it! Join the Church of Satan already!"

Son Yu-jeong finally spoke.

"… Are you okay? I mean, it’s not really my place to say this, but you don’t look good.”

The room fell silent.

Could she remain unaffected after hearing such an honest assessment from a close friend?

"… I don’t look good?”

Mortasinger’s expression twisted as she clamped a harsh grip on Son Yu-jeong’s shoulder.

"What the hell are you saying? Are you jealous of me too?! You hate that I’m beautiful, don’t you?!"

Even though she had some control over Hidden Codes, she was clearly speaking from the heart.

"Tell me the truth! How do I look?"

It was the last shred of reason she had left.

The logic of a magician, who had once understood the ways of the world, was barely keeping her delusion in check.

"Hurry up… answer me!"

As tears welled up in one of her twisted eyes, Son Yu-jeong suddenly understood.

Regret.

She was crying out for help.

Without showing any emotion, Son Yu-jeong slowly stepped back, retreating behind her companions.

Mortasinger sniffled.

"How… how could you do this to me?"

"I’m sorry."

As soon as Son Yu-jeong deceived Yahweh, Golden Eyes of Fire blazed to life in her pupils.

"KIIIIIIIIIIII!"

Skipping even the preparatory stance, she swung her two Ruyi Bangs, shattering the floor beneath her.

"Move!"

Shirone and the others threw themselves away from the blast.

Etella, who had jumped without thinking of her landing, felt a presence blocking her from behind.

"You…"

She turned to see Shagal, staring at her with a conflicted expression.

Then—

BOOM!

An explosion erupted from the wall.

A massive hole had been blown open.

Outside, soaring through the sky, Son Yu-jeong was holding onto Mortasinger.

"Let me go! What the hell are you doing?!"

"I’m getting you out of here. Run away. Go anywhere but here."

"What about you?"

"My Golden Shackles are still intact. I bought enough time to save you, but Yahweh will catch me."

As Mortasinger landed on the ground, she shook her head.

"No… I belong in the Church of Sa—"

"GO!"

Baring her monkey fangs, Son Yu-jeong let out a roar, making Mortasinger flinch.

"Get a grip! Do you seriously think your face is beautiful right now?!"

"I… I…"

At first, she had doubted herself, but surrounded by fellow believers, she had forgotten what was real.

Son Yu-jeong grabbed her shoulders.

"That place is a nest of lunatics, but you are not one of them. You were way smarter than me. The old you would have found a way to fix this. And you… you really were beautiful."

No matter what trouble they caused, Son Yu-jeong had always stood up for Mortasinger like an older sister.

"R-really? I… I’m not beautiful?"

How many times had she brainwashed herself to the point that she couldn’t even tell anymore?

Richella cut in.

"Well, not that I have anything down there anymore, but let me just say this—"

"Shut up."

Son Yu-jeong cut him off and spoke again.

"Leave. I’ll handle things here."

"No! I’m going with you! I can’t survive here on my own!"

"I already told you. You can’t. Yahweh is a damn pervert. He’ll find any excuse to strangle me—and he enjoys it."

Son Yu-jeong irritably rubbed the golden choker around her neck, then suddenly froze.

"… Huh?"

Taking a Hell Prison candidate and helping them escape was no different from aiding Yahweh’s enemies.

Why aren’t my Golden Shackles tightening?

"I’m fine?"

Son Yu-jeong was certain.

He didn’t recite the Golden Incantation… But why?

Richella spoke.

"Are we leaving or not? I don’t care either way, but I don’t want to stay here."

Even though Agents were surrounding them, Son Yu-jeong was still lost in thought.

Lethe asked.

"Is it okay to just leave them be?"

If Yahweh recited the incantation, Son Yu-jeong would be dragged back immediately.

"It’s her choice."

Shirone didn’t want to interfere.

"Even if she’s reckless, she chose to fight for her friend. And that much is genuine."

Lethe nodded.

"Alright then. Let’s get to the point. How about you accept my proposal now?"

Shirone couldn’t stubbornly refuse anymore.

Jin Seong Eum escaping through Hell Prison to reach reality is impossible. But…

There was still something nagging at him, something he couldn’t shake off.

The Flesh Brain… What am I missing?

Rian drew his great sword.

"Shirone, look."

From the 13th-floor stairway, a group clad in black robes was descending.

"Hell Prison."

The 1 o’clock seat of Pride smirked.

"Long time no see, Yahweh."

Lethe’s brows furrowed slightly.

Hell Prison doesn’t obey my orders… Yet they’re stepping forward against Yahweh?

There was only one reason Shirone couldn’t kill Havitz—

Because such an event had never happened before.

Yahweh, who had purged Demons for the sake of humanity, could not treat Havitz the same way he had treated Wizard.

A direct fight means Hell Prison has no chance of winning. And yet, they’re choosing to face me now…

It was for Satan, of course.

Havitz… No, Satan—just how badly is he losing in reality?

As Miracle Stream radiated from Shirone’s body, Mupa’s face contorted.

"Grrrrrr…!"

Even from the worshippers stationed at the entrance, a wave of hostility surged forward.

Shirone spoke.

"Whatever you’re planning, go ahead and try."

"Satan’s temptations are worthless to me."

That much was true.

Which was why Hell Prison’s strategy was always to tempt those around Yahweh instead.

Rian, Etella, Shagal…

It was an effective card, and among them, Rian was not lacking in strength compared to Yahweh.

‘Just one clean hit is all I need.’

The ten remaining members of Hell Prison, excluding the two who had perished, activated their Hidden Codes against Rian.

Etella clenched her fists.

“We need to fight too.”

At that moment, the Chains of Taiji rippled, and the strength drained from her body.

“Gah!”

By the time she came to her senses, Shagal was already carrying her, sprinting toward the exit.

“Stop! Put me down!”

Without responding, Shagal smashed through the doors of the 10th floor and dashed down the corridor.

As a loud explosion erupted from where Shirone was, Etella struggled in his arms.

“Let me go! I said, let me go!”

Shagal only put her down after making several long detours through the maze-like hallways.

“What the hell is wrong with you?!”

The truth was, Etella understood.

The reason she couldn’t resist Shagal within the intertwined karma of the Chains of Taiji—

“We don’t need to fight.”

It was because he had done it for her.

“Don’t get the wrong idea. Do you think I’ll be happy about this? Have you already forgotten what you did to me?”

The fact that she had no choice but to be dragged along with evil made her heart ache.

“I hate that I can’t even die. Don’t get your hopes up. There’s nothing you want from me that you’ll get.”

She hadn’t said anything cruel, but her feelings were obvious.

Shagal spoke without filtering his emotions.

“Yeah? All that talk about righteousness, and this is all you amount to? Whatever. I never cared anyway.”

‘Filthy bastard.’

Etella felt her blood boil, and once again, a sharp pain struck her abdomen.

“Ahh…”

Shagal turned away coldly.

“Come on. What we need is a way to break these chains. If we sever this cursed connection, neither of us has to suffer anymore.”

She wanted to smack the back of his head as he walked away, but she forced herself to stay calm.

‘I need to keep my composure.’

The pain in her stomach subsided, but she knew continuing to clash with Shagal would be dangerous.

“W-who are you people?”

As they turned a corner, a worshipper of the Church of Satan flinched and assumed a defensive stance.

“Perfect. Since you live here, you must know the layout well. Answer my questions from now on. If you lie…”

Illusions flickered around Shagal, and in an instant, a swift dagger materialized in his hand.

“You die.”

“... Kekekeke.”

The worshipper laughed, raising both hands. A spear over two meters long formed in the air.

“A mere dagger? My Hidden Code—”

Before he could finish, Shagal grabbed his forehead and slammed him into the wall.

Using the rebound, he smashed the back of the man’s head against the wall seven times in rapid succession.

The cultist, now half-unconscious, trembled as he felt the cold touch of a blade against his throat.

“Hiiiik! P-please… spare me…”

“This is your last chance. Where is the department that manages karma?”

“Hnnngh!”

The terrified cultist’s lips quivered violently.

Karma Management Department

In a room filled with massive monitors, the echoes of battle rang out.

“This bastard… Urgh!”

In just three minutes, the disturbance ended. The twenty agents who had been stationed there vanished without a trace.

“This is getting annoying.”

Etella watched as Shagal moved toward the monitors.

‘He’s gotten stronger.’

Perhaps ever since he slaughtered the agents at the entrance of the System Control Branch.

‘The power of Taiji is making him stronger. In other words… he’s becoming more and more evil.’

“If I can just learn what I was… that’s all I need. I don’t care about karma or anything else.”

Shagal called for her.

“Come here. I don’t know how to work this thing.”

Curious about the truth behind their manipulated fate, Etella approached eagerly.

“These machines are biological. Since it seems to use blood, I think you need to insert your blood here.”

Without hesitation, Shagal lifted his dagger and slashed his own wrist.

As his blood dripped onto the screen, text in the language of demons appeared.

They couldn’t interpret it, but they didn’t need to—because what came next was self-explanatory.

“This is…?”

A scene of a traveling circus performance played.

Shagal immediately recognized it.

“… Ah.”

Although he wasn’t in the video, he instinctively understood the situation.

“… Mother.”

A fierce-looking woman, bearing a striking resemblance to Shagal, performed acrobatics as the crowd applauded.

‘The origin. It’s showing the reason for Shagal’s existence.’

As the screen flickered like a fast-forwarded memory, a strange scent filled the air.

‘Shagal’s memories…’

Scents carried information from the past.

They didn’t need the visuals anymore—they could absorb Shagal’s entire history in an instant.

Shagal’s mother had fallen for the son of a wealthy noble who had come to watch their circus.

It couldn’t be called love, because the moment she became pregnant, she was abandoned.

"Waah! Waah!"

In a filthy, rotting room where no one cared for him, Shagal was born.

About three months later, his mother took him to a secluded alley.

“Haa… Haa…”

Staring at the frail baby, her face twisted with cruelty.

“Useless.”

Like tossing away an object, she hurled Shagal onto the ground.

“Heh… Heheheh…”

Lying there, Shagal laughed as if he were watching an amusing performance.

‘Yeah, this is just how I am.’

It was far from a pleasant sight, but having fallen into Hell, it was almost comforting in a twisted way.

His mother turned her back on him and walked away—but then suddenly stopped.

The sound of a baby crying.

She hesitated for a long time before finally biting her lip and running back to him.

"Live well."

Taking a few coins from her pocket, she tucked them into his blanket and disappeared forever.

"… Hic. Hic, hic… Hic, hic…!"

What the hell is a baby supposed to do with some coins?!

‘What is this?! What the hell is this, you damn bitch?!’

Shagal, laughing with his mouth while tears poured from his eyes, trembled.

Etella, watching him, felt the weight in her chest grow heavier.

‘Poor thing.’

Time passed. Eventually, an orphanage took Shagal in. And then, someone came to take him away.

“Wha—?”

Both Shagal and Etella were stunned.

"No way. This can’t be real."

Etella’s master, the very man Shagal had personally killed with his own hands—

Raphael of the Karsis Monastery.
