Chapter 488:

"Dawn is breaking."

Amidst the chaotic battle, Shirone sensed the sky brightening.

As Armand's pupils contracted to take in as much light as possible, the terrified expressions of the Kergo warriors became vividly clear.

"Kill! Don't retreat!"

Even the face of the Kergo chieftain shouting those words was pale with fear.

They had honed their Skima through centuries of heretic hunting, but Shirone's use of Elysion was truly an unknown realm.

As the boundaries of the Spirit Zone disappeared, dozens of photon cannons swept through the space, left and right.

The enemies' blades couldn't cut through Armand, and the tentacles continuously absorbed the corpses, giving Shirone more energy.

And finally, when the multi-explosion of Berserk occurred, grotesque screams filled the air.

Dozens of light sources flared up, crushing the entire Kergo hunting party.

Shirone, left alone, was filled with sorrow.

He had come with dozens of reasons to wage war, but with each life extinguished, those reasons also vanished, leaving him unsure of what he was fighting for.

"I'm scared."

As a mage and a member of Gaold's team, he hadn't come to heaven without this level of resolve.

What truly frightened him was his own numbness, feeling no emotion despite his resolve.

"Rian."

Shirone's previously calm face suddenly contorted as tears streamed down.

"I'm so scared, Rian."

"There he is! Chase him! Don't let him escape!"

The heretic hunters pursued him again.

As long as Shirone carried the scent of blood, they would follow him to the ends of the earth.

As a Kergo warrior swiftly approached and swung his crescent blade vertically, Akamai's eye activated Antithesis.

Before the movement even stopped, Armand's tentacle burst the enemy's face.

The ensuing battle was another one-sided slaughter.

Kuzan's metabolic ability prevented Shirone from feeling fatigue.

Ringer's armor prevented wounds, and Kensei's absolute restoration prevented injuries.

Amidst all these enhancements, the Ultima System embedded magic into every space perceived by Direct Perception.

Akamai subdued the enemies, Galtomic's magic amplification maximized power, and approaching enemies were sliced by the tentacle blades.

'Damn it! He's a monster! Not human!'

The pursuers, who had thought of Shirone as merely a symbol of light from Zone 73, trembled in fear at his god-like prowess.

'Elysion!'

As Shirone's eyes, piercing the world with Direct Perception, flashed, countless photon cannons emerged from the ground, all shooting skyward.

It was like a massive burst of light beams rising in clusters.

Adapting to attacks from below was extremely difficult for humans with eyes on their heads, and the enemies lost their lives without even approaching Shirone.

After another battle ended, a wave of poignant sorrow washed over him.

"Feope."

Shirone placed his hand on his chest to check Feope's condition.

Her life force hadn't ceased, but it was significantly weaker.

That faint life force spurred Shirone's spirit.

"I have to get to Araboth."

Shirone's robe, darkened by the blood of many, quickly absorbed the blood and converted it into energy.

With that power, Shirone pressed forward.

As he neared his destination, the number of pursuers dwindled significantly.

It might have been due to the sacred aura emanating from Araboth, but he also thought something significant might have happened in other regions besides Shehakim.

Finally, when the walls of Araboth came into view, Shirone cast a teleportation spell and stopped at a specific point.

'This is the first point.'

Sein had designated three points where God's punishment could be cast, and Shirone had learned the descent trajectory accordingly.

Casting the spell here would destroy heaven.

But Shirone ignored the point and walked on.

Unless special tactical instructions were given, God's punishment was to be cast at the point closest to Araboth.

But not past noon.

If Plu were here, the judgment might have been better, but the result would be the same.

'I will meet Ikael. Just wait a little longer, Feope.'

Shirone immediately leaped over the walls of Araboth.

He was the first to reach Araboth among Gaold's team and the rebels.

At the second point, Shirone paused for a moment.

Even though he was close enough to see the spire where Anke Ra resided, no one stopped him.

The place where Shirone finally encountered someone was beyond the third point, just 2 kilometers from Araboth.

With the spire behind her, the Archangel of Amplification, Ikael, was waiting for Shirone.

"You've finally come, Shirone."

How many near-death experiences had he survived?

Her dry, lifeless eyes conveyed no emotion.

Despite facing Ikael's clear hostility, Shirone's steps didn't falter.

"I have a request."

"I refuse. You and I are enemies. If you come any closer, I will have to kill you. No…"

Ikael's body tilted slightly.

She had a clear reason to block out her emotions.

"I will kill you regardless."

With a bang, the air exploded, and Ikael's figure vanished.

She reappeared right behind where Shirone had been standing.

As Ikael's fist, amplified in speed, cut through the air and stopped, her brow furrowed.

Shirone was now standing 10 meters away from Ikael.

Above his head, Ikasa's Judicial Halo, Valhalla Action, was spinning.

Although demoted to a fallen angel and most of her power restrained, the ability of the Judicial Halo was the same as the unique power possessed by other angels.

Seeing Valhalla Action, Ikael naturally thought of the Ataraxia linkage she had taught.

'To wield two angelic judicial powers…'

It was impossible for anyone in heaven, and thus Shirone had to die.

Having made her decision, Ikael lunged again.

Even with Armand's enhanced nervous system, her movements were so fast that only afterimages were visible.

With another bang, the air exploded as Ikael thrust her fist.

"……"

But once again, Shirone had vanished.

Ikasa's Valhalla Action consumed all the time required for the cause in exchange for preceding the result.

Giving Shirone time was clearly her judgment.

But she didn't consider it a mistake because she believed this time, he couldn't possibly evade.

'How did he see it?'

Ikael slowly turned her head to see Shirone, who had moved behind her.

Armand's tentacles fluttered, ready for battle.

But what surprised her was what she couldn't see.

Shirone's mental focal point had disappeared.

And it was clearly the ability of Gaia, vividly remembered in her mind.

"Elysion?"

Unconsciously muttering, Ikael grimaced in pain.

"Ugh!"

Even though she had organized her thoughts, another sensation was trying to connect with the forgotten past.

'I must not remember.'

That was Anke Ra's will.

Quickly regaining her composure, she spoke with a cold gaze.

"You've become much stronger. In just a few days."

Perceiving with the Ultima System and evading with Valhalla Action.

It was certainly possible, but Ikael thought it was foolish.

"Do you think you can face me with just that?"

He could only evade once.

If she attacked continuously, Valhalla Action's nature would prevent him from evading.

In fact, there was no need for that.

Knowing Shirone's level, she could move so fast that even Direct Perception couldn't detect it.

When Shirone took out Feope, Ikael's gaze turned to the emaciated fairy.

"She's still alive. You can revive her."

Feope was a familiar name to Ikael.

She was the fairy who had brought Shirone to heaven for the first time.

But now it was wartime, and Feope was a heretic who had helped Shirone.

"Don't be mistaken, Shirone. You can't make any deals with me. Can you survive this time…"

"I know you can kill me."

Clang!

A magic circle of Valhalla Action appeared above Shirone's head.

"Archangel Ikael is the strongest being in heaven."

"…What are you trying to say?"

Shirone looked up at the magic circle above his head and spoke.

"Valhalla Action reverses cause and effect. It means any action can immediately reach its result…"

Ikael's voice grew harsh.

"Are you mocking the archangel?"

All the angelic judicial powers were in Ikael's mind.

She was the source of all judicial powers.

"That's why I'm explaining. Once the result is reached, it can't be reversed."

Shirone turned his gaze back to Ikael.

"This Valhalla Action has various spells. I'll amplify a laser with Ataraxia and shoot it into the sky. Then, at an altitude of 6,000 kilometers, I'll cast a new spell using God's particles to compress mass. That will create a light spear with a mass of 10 tons by this planet's standards."

Ikael's face began to contort.

"And that spear will crash precisely into Araboth."

"Shirone…"

It was possible.

Ikael, who existed before Uriel's destruction and Kariel's creation, knew that.

The biggest problem was Valhalla Action.

Shirone's explanation was lengthy, but with Valhalla Action, it would all happen in an instant.

"I'm sorry for using the magic you taught me in this way. I can't tell you the point. You could predict and kill me before I cast the spell. But in the end, you won't be able to stop me. So…"

Shirone held out Feope with a desperate look.

"Save this fairy. She ended up like this to save me. Let me do what I can."

Ikael shook her head as if shaking off her internal conflict.

Feope wasn't the issue.

If Shirone activated God's punishment, there wouldn't be many who could survive in heaven.

No, even an archangel might be annihilated if hit directly, given the expected power.

"If you cast that spell, do you think you will survive? The cost of Valhalla Action will leave you unable to move for a very long time. Doing such a thing will also cost you your life."

"Don't you understand yet?"

As Shirone approached, Ikael stepped back to maintain distance.

In terms of power, she was unmatched by any being in heaven, but currently, all the choices were Shirone's.

"I have a mission to destroy heaven. But if you save Feope, I'll abandon that mission. So…"

At a closer distance, Shirone's steps came to a halt.

"Whatever decision you make, it doesn't matter if I die."

Ikael's nose wrinkled.

All the circumstances and situations were perfectly clear in her mind, yet she couldn't make any decision.

"……"

Boom. Boom.

In the long silence, the sounds of war echoed from the skies of the First to the Sixth Heaven.
