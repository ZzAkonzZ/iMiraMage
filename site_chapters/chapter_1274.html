Even the avatar of the Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara was reduced to black charcoal, delivering unbearable pain to Miro.

‘What is this?’

Everything moved according to his whims.

If Nane fought by the standard of emptiness, then Havitz was the very embodiment of total and utter wrongness.

“What are you doing? Aren’t you supposed to be the righteous one?”

As Miro lifted her head, Havitz had already appeared before her.

“The big bad villain is right here,” he said, mocking her with outstretched arms and a wiggling waist, his silhouette faceless.

“Yaaah!”

The avatar of the Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara unleashed a furious barrage of blows, but only pain followed.

Miro gritted her teeth and fell back.

“Haaah…”

Her hands trembled as smoke rose from them, and all she could think was how much it hurt.

‘How am I supposed to win this?’

“What’s wrong?”

Satan’s mouth split open unnaturally.

“If you won’t fight, should I just toy with you?”

“You—!”

Fire lit up in Miro’s eyes again.

“You bastard!”

The furious avatar of the Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara struck Satan with thirty million hits.

IZ IZ IZ TZ TZ IZ I

It was a miracle born of fractured time, but all that came in return was pain just as intense.

“Aaaaaaaah!”

Miro screamed uncontrollably.

“Puhahaha! What was that? That was your ultimate move? You’re just like any other human!” She didn’t even notice that Satan’s voice had gradually shifted to that of a child.

‘How humiliating.’

Why couldn’t she win?

‘They say it’s the pinnacle of good versus the pinnacle of evil.’

If each defined the other, then shouldn’t they at least be able to fight like emptiness and love?

On her knees, Miro shouted:

“Why! Why are you doing this!”

Even to that question, completely lacking in context, Havitz had only one answer.

“Because it’s fun.”

His body began to grow, quickly reaching dozens of meters in height. He raised one foot.

“This is gigantification.”

Miro’s face went pale at the Satan’s ability to manipulate the world at will.

“I’ll leave your mouth intact.”

Havitz brought his foot down.

“I want to hear you beg for death.”

In that moment, a tremendous atmospheric pressure—as if the sky itself were falling—crushed him.

RUMBLE RUMBLE RUMBLE RUMBLE!

“Huh?”

He tried to withstand it, but it felt impossible.

“Wh-what?”

Such overwhelming scale.

As Satan hastily shrunk and hunched down, the entire land was crushed.

BOOM

The demons within range were squashed flat. Only Havitz remained standing.

“Grrr…”

Gaold was walking in slowly.

“Your stellar energy is strong, I’ll give you that.”

If this had been a planet with normal durability, this entire area would already be a sea of lava.

In the next moment, the celestial guitar sound in the sky shifted into heavy metal.

“Ah…”

Maybe it was just coincidence, but Miro instinctively thought of the man who had just arrived on the battlefield.

“Gaold.”

As he passed by Miro, he cast a cold glance.

“You were gonna leave me behind?”

“No, I—”

Seeing Miro flustered for the first time made Gaold chuckle as he moved forward.

“Leave it to me.”

Havitz growled.

“You dare surprise me? Try something again and I’ll burn you alive. You’ll suffer eternal torment.”

“Eternal torment? Fire?”

Gaold’s teeth clenched tightly.

“Perfect.”

His lips split into a grin, and the essence of the Great Infernal Hell transformed the entire landscape.

“That’s my specialty.”

FWOOOOSH!

Havitz silently watched as fire lit up at the ends of his fingers and toes.

“Huh? What is…”

Suddenly, searing pain.

“AAAAAGH! IT HURTS! Agh…!”

Gaold’s fist, charged with the power of Air Press, sent Satan flying.

With each step Gaold took forward, hundreds of demons were crushed. Satan charged again.

“I’m not going to suffer alone!”

Seeing him spread out mid-air as he flew in, Gaold’s face twisted.

“Kuh, hahaha… what a fool.”

‘Pain, you see…’

The unprecedented Air Press crushed the ground.

‘…is not something you can share.’

Friction from the earth compressed over 10 meters triggered a massive explosion of fire.

BOOOOOOOM!

Even as the flames surged toward her, Miro was lost in thought.

‘Why?’

Why was the pinnacle of evil being struck down by someone not even at the pinnacle of good?

The Supreme Judge of Theraphos once said:

—Evil cannot be defeated alone.

‘And also…’

—Victory is the methodology of evil. When good, evil, emptiness, and love all intertwine, humanity will face its final decision. Until then, we must endure.

The flames suddenly stopped just in front of her.

“Huh?”

Miro turned her head to see Sein, Kangnan, Xing, and Zulu walking toward her.

“You guys…”

Sein reached out a hand.

“Finally found you. Though not you, exactly—Satan, I mean.”

Even Sein’s pointed joke went unanswered as Miro looked past them.

The human army was charging in.
