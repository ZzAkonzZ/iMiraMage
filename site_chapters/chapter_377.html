Chapter 377: One Step Further (2)

Shirone lowered his gaze to the evaluation sheet. The master difficulty levels of numerous items were listed one after another.

'I was an amateur.'

He thought he had reached a professional level in some aspects through real-world experience. He had certainly seen effective results.

But that wasn't the issue.

Whether the Photon Cannon was faster and stronger than other types, whether someone could beat someone else, or whether Ataraxia could be imitated.

The association was conveying through the evaluation sheet that if he couldn't achieve this level, he was just an amateur who had luckily obtained a special specialty.

-Your balance is really off.

It felt like he could hear Flu's voice.

Jonah, the top graduate of the Royal Magic School and a certified 8th-grade magician.

Among the countless 8th-grade magicians in the world, Flu held a special position.

She was a person with a lot of affection but was strict and unreserved when it came to magic.

Now he understood what kind of eyes she had looked at him with when they first met.

Unless he conquered all 20 items on the evaluation sheet, he was still an amateur in her eyes.

'Senior, strangely, I miss you today.'

Imagining Flu shuddering and being startled brought a smile to his face.

"Have you made your decision? Whatever you choose, I will respect your judgment."

If he changed his major, the difficulty would be acceptable.

He had learned all the basic specialties, so he might be able to earn points more easily depending on his efforts.

But having already experienced the world after graduation through the Magic Association, Shirone realized it wouldn't mean anything.

"I'll go with this. The divine particle. Please register it as my major."

Collie didn't forget to ask once more.

"Are you sure? Once the evaluation starts, you can't change it."

"Yes, I'm sure. Even if I don't get high scores, I won't regret it."

No regrets.

This is my magic, and this is my life.

"Understood. Then I'll register it as such. Good luck."

"Yes. I'll do my best."

Shirone bowed politely and left Collie's office.

Iruki and Nade were waiting in the hallway.

Iruki, ranked 22nd in the senior class, had just finished his interview, and Nade, ranked 30th, still had to wait longer.

Nade asked with a worried expression.

"How was it? Do you think you can manage?"

A bitter smile replaced a hundred words.

Iruki, who shared the same struggles, waved his crumpled evaluation sheet.

"The master difficulty for the explosive power item is 50 kilobusters in 20 seconds. In other words, they want me to blow up a fruit shop every 20 seconds."

Nade didn't think it was someone else's problem.

Shirone was an Unlocker, but his major, electrical magic, was as difficult as explosive magic.

"Are the association guys crazy? Do they think that's possible at a student level?"

Shirone felt the same frustration.

"Maybe. But if anyone could pass, it wouldn't be a differentiator, so we can't blame them."

"There's a big difference between the 9th level and the master level. I might be able to manage 9 points. But Shirone, you seem surprisingly calm. Are you confident?"

Shirone shook his head.

"No. I'm not confident at all. But it's something I have to do."

Iruki and Nade agreed in silence.

As Shirone said, it's something they have to do.

How could they aim to be the best magician without mastering their major?

'Of course, I won't do it,' Nade thought to himself.

The morning of the official start of the senior class schedule dawned.

The first evaluation of the first week was interpersonal combat, where 30 students were divided into 15 pairs to compete against each other.

The location was the second training ground behind the senior class building, a massive 120-square-meter combat arena.

The senior class students gathered in the training ground by class and lined up to wait for the interpersonal combat evaluation teacher.

Shirone stood between Nade and Iruki.

There was occasional conversation among classmates, but overall, the atmosphere was still awkward.

The evaluation teacher arrived on time.

He was a teacher in his mid-50s with graying hair and sharp eyes.

He was known for not speaking about anything other than the evaluation.

"Starting today, the interpersonal combat evaluation begins. Class Two already knows, but I'll briefly go over it for Class Three. According to the Kingdom's Education Law, the synchronization rate for the second training ground is set at 80%. Of course, it will be 100% for the graduation exam."

Even at 80%, it wasn't reassuring.

Considering the superhuman abilities of magic, even 80% power was not something one could withstand if hit directly.

Shirone had also fought Dante at a 50% synchronization rate, but it had been an intense battle that hardly felt like a simulation.

"Weapons are not allowed. However, embodied weapons are permitted."

The students of Class Three tilted their heads, and the model student Boyle raised his hand.

"I have a question. What is the criterion for embodiment?"

"The clear criterion is whether it can be removed. Weapons that cannot be removed are considered embodied. Even if they can be removed, if it would impair bodily functions, the association considers them embodied."

According to the teacher, Shirone couldn't use Armand.

On the other hand, Canis could fight with Harvest. It couldn't be removed.

Another person who came to Shirone's mind was Richard from Fermi's group.

Known as Richard the Alchemist, he had modified 35% of his body with alchemy. While the devices could be attached and detached, his body couldn't function normally without them, so they were considered embodied.

If Shirone cut off his right arm and attached Armand, he could use the Golden Armor in the graduation exam, but he had no intention of doing that.

Some of Class Three's students looked displeased.

Even though they knew they couldn't do such a crazy thing themselves, it felt unfair that others modified their bodies.

Boyle looked at Canis and said.

"Good for you, passing."

Boyle's resentment was understandable. As someone who had to use slots for summoning magic, he envied Canis, who could handle magical creatures without being constrained by slots.

'Don't worry. I can handle someone like you without Harvest.'

Canis didn't even bother to respond.

After the Arcane incident, everyone knew about Harvest's existence.

But only Shirone and Amy knew the true nature of the magical creature.

It's best to save your trump card for the right moment.

Unless it was a critical situation, Harvest wouldn't have a chance to shine.

Of course, Canis didn't intend to entrust his future entirely to the magical creature.

'This time, I'll win, Shirone. If you think Harvest is the end, you're mistaken.'

When the questions ended, the teacher proceeded with the evaluation.

"Now, we will conduct the interpersonal combat in order. The combat time is 10 minutes. Wear the second training ground bracelet and wait for your turn."

Except for the evaluation group and the waiting group, students could move freely around the training ground.

It was a different atmosphere from the advanced class, which emphasized discipline and principles.

Shirone found a spot with his friends where they could see the training ground well. He unfolded his schedule and said.

"My opponent today is Dorothy. What about you, Iruki?"

"Huh? Oh, it's someone named Binder."

Shirone turned his head to the opposite side of the second training ground and looked at Binder, who was doing warm-up exercises.

He belonged to Class Two and had been seen at the senior class events, but he wasn't a memorable student.

He had light brown hair, wore glasses, and had a sparse beard on his chin and under his nose.

Since he hadn't stood out in last year's graduation exam, Iruki was confident of his victory.

"Well, I guess I can reserve 3 points for this."

"Don't be overconfident. We haven't had a single match yet. Things might change once you're in there."

"I've considered that. My calculations show he's an opponent with no variables."

Shirone was worried about Iruki's overconfidence.

As Flu said, it's foolish to predict the outcome in advance, and battles aren't decided by numbers alone.

'Well, I can't imagine Iruki being nervous either.'

In any case, today's main match for them wasn't Shirone or Iruki.

"By the way, Nade, are you okay?"

Shirone asked, and Nade shrugged.

"Well, I'll give it a try."

His opponent today was none other than Fermi.

Although he had failed the graduation exam, he was a mysterious student who never missed the top spot in the final evaluations.

When Shirone first heard about Fermi, he could only make vague analyses, but now that he understood the significance of rankings in the senior class, he couldn't help but worry.

Other students seemed to have the same thought, as they kept an eye on Fermi's behavior.

At that moment, Fermi, sensing their gaze, turned his head and smiled, waving at Nade, his opponent for the day.

"Is that guy provoking me?"

"No, I think he was just saying hello."

Nade turned to Shirone with a face that looked ready to devour him.

"That's a provocation! Since when did he know me well enough to act familiar? Watch closely. Today, I'll expose his skills completely."

Shirone looked at the training ground where the evaluation was taking place and realized it was his turn. He stood up.

"Alright, I need to get ready."

When Shirone entered the second training ground, Dorothy approached, dragging her tin doll.

The tin doll made a clattering noise as it scraped the floor with each step.

Dorothy approached right up to Shirone's face. Her face was small, and her glasses were large, making her look like she was wearing a mask for a masquerade.

Her expressionless face and unfocused eyes served as a major shield, making it impossible to read her emotions.

'What should I do? Should I greet her?'

The waiting process for the interpersonal combat was the same as during the advanced class's second training ground duel.

After confirming the second training ground bracelet in the center of the training ground and returning to their positions, a 10-minute timer would start, and the second training ground would activate.

There was no need to greet each other, so turning away coldly could be a form of psychological warfare.

However, in the first week's evaluation, where friend and foe were not yet clearly identified, erasing humanity might not be the best approach.

Moreover, it didn't suit Shirone's personality.

"Nice to meet you. Since this is an important evaluation for both of us, let's do our best."

"Do you want to call it a draw?"

Dorothy's sudden proposal made Shirone immediately ask back.

"Huh? What did you say?"

"Let's call it a draw. Then I'll do something you want."

Before he could analyze the meaning of her words, his face turned red. It might be nothing, but if it was a girl saying it, it was different.

"W-what do you mean?"

"Just what I said. I'll do anything you want. Anyway, you think it's disadvantageous to show your skills from the beginning, right? So let's each take 1 point."

Shirone didn't intend to join in any collusion, but it was important to weigh the pros and cons of any proposal, so he thought about it.

Her proposal certainly had potential uses. It could create variables at important times.

'If she really agrees to my terms.'

If it was a draw, they would each get 1 point. He couldn't tell if the value of the 2 points he would forgo by assuming a win was included in her proposal.

He wanted to win the first match if possible.

A good start would give him confidence for future evaluations and realistically give him an advantage in the ranking competition.

"No, I'll decline. I want to compete fairly. So you should do your best too."

Dorothy still didn't show any emotion. Then, as if understanding, she nodded and turned around.

"Then it can't be helped. Let's do our best."

Shirone stared blankly at Dorothy's back as she dragged her tin doll back to her position.

If it was psychological warfare, it had worked perfectly.

He was really quite disturbed.

'Get a grip. Nothing happened. Just fight as you originally planned.'

When Shirone reached the waiting area, the lights on the second training ground scoreboard changed from green to yellow to red, signaling the start.

Shirone bent his knees slightly to be ready for a quick offense or defense.

As the machine beeped and the light on the second training ground bracelet turned on, Dorothy still didn't move, holding her tin doll.

Shirone equipped the teleportation specialty and raised his omnipotence, leaning forward.

'I'll attack!'

At the same time, Dorothy turned her body and said.

"I lost."

Shirone, who had hurriedly canceled his magic, stumbled forward.

Barely managing to stay upright, he looked ahead to see Dorothy already leaving the training ground.
