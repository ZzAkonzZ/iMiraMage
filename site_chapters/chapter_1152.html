Chapter 1152: 12 o’clock, 12 Nations (3)

The Jincheon Empire sector was blocked off with an unprecedented level of security.

"Your Majesty!"

Jingang, who had been struck by the Moon Kingdom's curse, collapsed and vomited blood before he could reach his bed.

"What happened!"

Dozens of physicians examined his condition and came to the same conclusion.

"His energy has weakened. This is not a normal pulse. He has fallen victim to the enemy's sorcery."

"Are you saying that! The world's strongest Jincheon has fallen to mere sorcery! And you call yourselves…!"

"Enough."

Jingang rose from his bed.

"Your Majesty, you must rest." Those struck by the curse could have their metabolism disrupted and, in severe cases, could be possessed by evil energy.

"I said I'm fine."

Although Jingang's face was pale, his eyes were intense.

"Sorcery… such a vile act."

Since Jincheon's roots were in the Moon Kingdom, their sorcery was similar in many ways.

"Sorcery?"

The officials' faces turned red.

"It's the Moon Kingdom! Only Moonryong could commit such a shameless act!"

Jingang calmly walked over and sat in a chair.

'Why couldn't we stop it?'

Although not as much as the Moon Kingdom, Jincheon also had plenty of sorcery experts.

'They pierced through all the sorcery defenses and hit me. It's impossible with just human power.'

It must have been the work of the other world.

"Your Majesty."

Anchal from the Jincheon Space Agency bowed her head at Jingang's feet.

"Please kill me."

As the head of the department preparing to intercept the other world, she could not avoid responsibility.

Jingang did not blame her.

"Report."

"We have confirmed abnormal demonic activity in a specific region of Hell. It is estimated to have occurred about an hour ago, and the Jincheon Space Agency confirmed it just seven minutes ago."

"Hmm."

Since Jinseong was connected to Gwangcheon, Jincheon could investigate the other world.

Of course, it was all mathematical data, like observing planets in other galaxies.

'There are technical limitations with just Gwangcheon. Rather, it should be considered impressive that it was detected in 53 minutes.'

Anyway, one hour.

'I don't know if it was pre-arranged, but I can't shake the feeling that it was rushed. Also, if it's the other world…

Havitz cannot be excluded.

'In other words, it is the result of Satan's impulsive nature. A cornered rat bites the cat.'

Jingang spoke.

"The enemy drew their sword first, so we must respond accordingly. Anchal, you must do it."

"…Yes."

Although she was the director of the Jincheon Space Agency, she was also the strongest spy with a demonic eye when she removed her eyepatch.

The physician said.

"Your Majesty, please rest now. We will find a way to break the curse."

"No. There's no need."

Jingang rose from his seat, drew the emperor's sword, and looked around.

He took a deep breath, but the resentment did not leave him and instead twisted his insides.

"Cough!"

As he vomited blood again and staggered, the physicians jumped up in surprise.

"Your Majesty!"

Jingang pointed his sword to block their approach.

"Do not come."

A ghost?

Jingang's face twisted grotesquely, and the face of a demon flickered like an afterimage.

"Hehehe. Hehehehe."

"Your Majesty…"

"Moonryong, you think you can kill me?"

Blood tears flowed straight down from his eyes as he clutched his chest.

'My daughter is in Hell. In eternal suffering with no escape or end…

A father's heart…

"I am a ghost! My heart is Hell!"

The shimmering heat around Jingang filled the room like thick smoke.

Anchal’s single eye widened.

'Extreme self-control.'

It seemed to smell like rotting flesh, and the ghost's scream continued as if it would tear apart.

"I will devour everything! Whether demon or god, I will chew up every bone fragment!"

As the veins on Jingang's face bulged, the officials cried out in tears.

"Your Majesty, please calm down! You must not harm your body! Please…!"

Anchal stopped the officials.

"Wait!"

"What are you saying, Director? At this rate, His Majesty will be in danger!"

"Just wait… a moment."

Beyond the staggering Jingang, who had reached the state of extreme self-control, the face of a ghost appeared.

'It is suffering.'

The strongest curse, combined with the grudge of a maiden and the demonic energy of Hell, was wailing.

Anchal shouted.

"Your Majesty, overcome it! It makes no sense for the emperor of Jincheon to fall to a ghost!"

"Director!"

"His Majesty's judgment is correct. If he cannot control his mind, the ghost's resentment will only grow stronger."

"Then shouldn't we stop it now?"

"Resentment is an emotion that cannot be endured without blaming someone. Therefore, a ghost that cannot release its resentment will seek a new target. The first to suffer will be…

Anchal turned to the officials.

"The one who cast the curse, the shaman. Depending on the situation at the time of the sorcery, it may vary, but it doesn't end there. The ghost, whose resentment is at its peak, will harm everyone present at the time until its resentment is released."

The officials finally realized.

"Could it be?"

"Yes. During my espionage activities, the astrologers of the Moon Kingdom called this phenomenon…"

Anchal said.

"Reverse killing."

Around Midnight.

Seventeen thousand fairies were stationed at the entrance of the forest known as the Green Ocean.

Deep inside that forest, beyond the reach of human footsteps, lay the homeland of the elves.

“This is troublesome.”

A fairy with iridescent wings was 1.5 times larger than the others.

Of course, compared to humans, its physique was still small, but its aura was as immense as a towering mountain.

This was Crown, the Fairy King.

He was the supreme ruler among the 72 ranks of fairies, the sole sovereign, and the only male fairy.

The fairies knew:

"For as long as water has filled the lakes, for as long as the wind has blown through the valleys, for as long as fire has roared within the lava, Lord Crown has stood at the highest place."

Now that even Ikael had abandoned heaven, it was only natural that he sought to become king himself.

A senior officer of the 72 ranks reported.

“Lord Crown, the scouting party has arrived.”

Fairies in cute camouflage uniforms with fierce makeup flew over to Crown.

“We have located the elves' stronghold. They have fortified their defenses, but we can guide you there immediately.”

Crown rested his chin on his hand.

“Hm…”

Though he was as beautiful as a young boy, the weight of an ancient existence had settled into his posture.

"We chased them and drove them to this point. But elves in the forest are on another level."

Moreover, this was the largest forest in the world—an impregnable fortress from a fairy’s perspective.

"The abilities of the Fire Tribe and their Small World Creation are troublesome. We must be prepared for complete annihilation."

At that moment, a deafening roar exploded in the sky.

“An ambush?!”

As all the fairies turned their eyes skyward, a flash of light landed before them.

“Ah…”

A towering archangel, overwhelming even the fairies, stood with arms crossed.

Uriel.

No other angel possessed the authority of Ikael, but Uriel was a different matter.

“O Archangel of Destruction.”

To avoid destruction, Crown immediately descended to the ground and bowed his head.

The supreme essence within him stirred with displeasure.

"For such a prideful archangel to seek us out… Even Ikael has abandoned heaven."

Uriel's words shattered Crown’s doubts.

“I need your help.”

“…What?”

A request rather than a command—something unprecedented in the history of heaven.

"What is happening?"

As 17,000 fairies cast skeptical gazes upon him, Uriel waited for an answer.

The darkest hour of the night.

In the Heaven’s Village—the truth the Vatican had desperately tried to conceal—Shirone and his companions waited.

“Mika.”

Melkidu does not exist.

There had to be a reason why the Catacomb, led by Giorgi, had kidnapped Seina.

“Ramirism is the world’s largest religion. And Satanism grows its power in its shadow. But what happens if that shadow disappears? Satanism will be wiped out.”

This was Iruki’s opinion.

It was a plausible theory, but Shirone couldn’t shake an uneasy feeling.

"Is that really all there is to it?"

Perhaps it was because Giorgi was a Ma (a sorcerer).

“Mika.”

Melkidu does not exist.

Every thirty minutes, Shirone summoned Mika to check whether Melkidu had materialized.

"Mika only extracts information based on specific keywords. It cannot detect things automatically."

When not summoned, Mika was nothing more than an unnamed apparition.

By dawn, Shirone finally spoke.

“Mika.”

Melkidu has materialized.

Shirone shot up to his feet, and both Iruki, Neid, and Eden instinctively reacted.

“You found it?”

Shirone asked Mika.

"Where is it?"

It is here.

"What do you mean?"

No matter how much he looked around, Heaven’s Village showed no visible changes.

As of the current moment, Melkidu exists simultaneously in all locations within the phase space.

"Then… that means someone has entered?"

Since the time tracking became possible, a total of four people worldwide have entered.

Shirone felt it instantly.

"Something they have… that I do not."

Dark energy.

Mika, can you copy and transmit the psychological state of those who entered Melkidu?

If his hypothesis was correct…

Psychological value judgments are not possible. However, brainwave copying is possible.

"That’s enough."

The moment he finished speaking, someone’s brainwaves overlapped with Shirone’s.

“Ghh…”

Of course, having identical brainwaves didn’t mean he could reconstruct their experiences.

What Shirone felt was a murky desire—overshadowed by an even greater fear.

“Shirone, look…”

Neid pointed ahead.

A pitch-black space billowed with gray smoke.

“That must be Melkidu. It has revealed itself in response to my dark energy.”

Eden asked, “How did you do it?”

“…Murder. Someone just committed a killing.” That was the brainwave he had received.

His friends couldn’t fully grasp it, but Shirone moved forward instead of explaining.

“Let’s go. We’ll understand once we get there.”

Shirone’s group stepped into the engulfing black space.

Beyond the boundary, silhouettes of humans ran through the thick mist.

"Criminals from all over the world."

With each breath of the mist, voices echoed in their minds.

Someone, help me! I don’t want to go to prison!

It was a mistake! I didn’t mean to kill them! I was just… I was just so angry… I was so furious that…

They’ll never catch me. I’ll kill more. More women, more pleasure…

They grimaced as they walked for about five minutes, until at last, the gray mist lifted.

A dense forest stretched before them.

“A momentary impulse.”

A woman’s voice rang out.

“Or was it years of suppressed desire that led you to commit an irreversible act?”

Shirone and his companions pressed their backs together, scanning their surroundings.

“Where are you? Show yourself!”

“No, that’s not it.”

Based on his experience in the Labyrinth of Andre, Shirone had a guess about what Melkidu truly was.

“No one knows how long it’s existed, but originally, it must have been a space that simply absorbed dark energy. Then, one by one, criminals who had committed heinous acts began to gather here.”

“They created the system?”

“Exactly. It became the safest hiding place for those who should have faced society’s judgment.”

The woman spoke again.

“Those who have crossed the forbidden line.”

“Welcome to the starting point of Melkidu.”
