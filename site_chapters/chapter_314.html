Chapter 314: The Wheel of Causality (6)

The instinctive thought was to get out of there.

To activate the Diamond Armor, he needed to grasp the sword. But Armand's hilt was too far away.

'Direct Control...!'

Armand, responding to Zion's mental wave, began to slide out of its sheath with a watery sound. But before it could reach Zion's hand, Uorin swiftly grabbed the hilt with her left hand.

Zion glared at Uorin with hostility.

This wasn't the sister he knew. Not only had she grabbed Armand's hilt without looking, but she was also holding the sword in place with a strength that defied explanation.

Uorin slowly moved the arm holding Armand in a reverse grip. Zion commanded the sword again, but Armand only vibrated in place, unable to break free.

As the blade approached Zion's neck, the vibration grew louder. Uorin's wrist, visible outside her sleeve, was as slender as that of an ordinary girl. He had never heard of her training in swordsmanship.

'How does she have this strength?'

Uorin's face remained calm, showing no sign of exertion.

But in her mind, a powerful force of will was at work. In heaven, this technique was called Incarnation. In this world, swordsmen referred to it as Divine Transcendence.

Uorin slowly moved the sword toward Zion's neck. Even with Divine Transcendence, it would be ineffective without physical support, but Zion's weak mental strength posed no problem.

"You are not worthy to be the master of this sword."

As Armand moved against his will, Zion's mind grew hazy.

Finally, the blade touched his neck. The vibration intensified, cutting into his skin.

"Cancel the contract. Otherwise, your head will be severed."

The blade felt icy cold where it touched, then suddenly burned like fire, making him scream involuntarily.

"Cancel the contract!"

At Zion's shout, Armand's vibration ceased.

Only then did Uorin release Zion's neck and sheath Armand.

"This sword is too precious for you. I'll put it to good use."

Zion, slumped against the wall, rubbed his neck where her hand had left a mark.

"Who... who are you?"

"You would do well to heed my words, mortal."

Leaving those words behind, Uorin exited the cell.

The door was open, but Zion couldn't move. Only after she disappeared did he realize the reality of his situation.

He must do nothing. Until she released him, he would rot in the dungeon forever.

Overwhelmed by despair, tears streamed down his face. He slowly collapsed to the side, curling up in a corner and trembling.

Uorin sheathed Armand and left the dungeon.

The combat-ready silver sheath had been custom-made by a craftsman. Gold was the most stylish, but she had excluded it for practical reasons.

"I hope he likes it."

As she exited the castle gate, all traces of Teraje disappeared from Uorin's face.

She reverted to a fresh-faced 14-year-old girl, smiling brightly as she ran toward the carriage.

"Shirone!"

Shirone, who was loading luggage onto the carriage, turned his head. Uorin was waving as she approached. For a moment, a nerve in his brain seemed to pass through a certain point, but it was just an empty space. After blinking a few times, he smiled lightly. He had severed ties with Orkamp, but he wanted to see her again.

He had heard that Teraje's personal guard, the Wind Spirit, had taken him when he lost consciousness. It was clear Uorin had helped. Without the she had given him, he would have been dead from Zenogger's strike.

"Are you leaving now?"

"Yes. Thank you for everything. I felt bad about leaving without saying goodbye."

"Hehe, no need for formalities between us. We'll see each other again, right?"

Shirone scratched his eyebrow awkwardly.

He had been in critical condition until recently, but fortunately, his mind was mostly intact. If he continued his life as a mage, Uorin would be an unavoidable presence.

But having just gained the freedom he longed for, he hoped not to get entangled again.

"Are you still upset with me?"

"It's not that. But we're too different."

Uorin smiled bitterly.

Anyone who had experienced what he did at the castle would feel the same. But hearing it from someone she had hoped would be her future husband was disheartening.

"Still... you consider me a friend, right?"

"Of course. No, you're my savior. You saved my life."

"Then that's enough. Don't worry. I won't bother you again."

Shirone realized the futility of words. Uorin had taught him that. But he appreciated her consideration.

"Take care."

"Oh! And this..."

Uorin handed him the sword she had been carrying.

It was unmistakably Armand. Shirone stared at it, puzzled, as Uorin explained.

"I'm giving it to you as a gift. Zion won't be out of the dungeon for a long time."

He had heard from Reina that Zion was alive. He didn't know the details, but Teraje had personally imprisoned him for disgracing their bloodline.

Armand shone with a tempting light, just as it had in Zion's gallery. But Shirone hesitated to accept it. He didn't want to owe the royal family again.

Amy understood his feelings. Just hearing the names Teraje or Kazra gave her a headache. But this was an S-class object. Only a fool would refuse it.

"Shirone, it's rude to refuse a gift."

When Shirone looked at her with half-closed eyes, Amy stuck out her tongue and stepped back.

Uorin, as if to reassure him, pushed the sword into his hands.

"It's okay to accept it. It's a token of appreciation from the Kingdom of Kazra. You went through a lot because of the castle's situation. Don't think of it as a debt."

Uorin was sincere this time. It wasn't a debt. She considered it a dowry of sorts.

With such words, it was hard to resist. But it was still an excessive gift.

S-class objects were rare even among royalty. And Armand wasn't just for show; it was specialized for combat. He couldn't even guess its market value.

"I don't know if I should accept it. I'm not a swordsman. It should go to someone who needs it more."

"Hehe, you don't know Armand's true value yet. Just make a contract. If you don't like it, you can always cancel."

Shirone finally took Armand. But having never dealt with a contract object, he didn't know how to proceed.

"So, how do I do this?"

"Hold the sword with both hands, stand it vertically, and say, 'I, the great mage Shirone, command Armand to obey me.'"

Shirone looked at Uorin incredulously. She smiled sheepishly.

"Just say 'Contract.'"

Shirone drew Armand and said, "Contract." The jewel glowed, and a majestic vibration spread through his palm.

"Is the contract done? I don't feel anything."

He knew that shared consciousness with its user. But he felt no different than before.

"It's because you haven't activated Armand yet. Direct Control requires copying the user's brain with Diamond Armor. Say 'Diamond Armor.'"

Amy, who had seen Diamond Armor in action, swallowed nervously.

The blade opened into a skeletal frame, enveloping Shirone's face. Unlike Zion, who transformed instantly, it took time for a first-time user.

The metal frame spiraled along his muscle lines. Once the frame was complete, a thin needle entered Shirone's central nervous system.

"Gasp!"

-Scanning new user's brain. Analyzing. Specializing in mental activity. Activating linguistic cognitive abilities.

A grand voice echoed in his mind, as if transmitted through a golden crown. The frame tightened around his body, and organic material flowed out.

Amy realized Armand was transforming into a robe, not armor.

Light bone-like gauntlets formed on his wrists, and the organic material turned into a hood covering his face. Finally, a cloak spread out like a stream of water.

-Activating neural network. Detecting focal points. Relaxing beta waves. Maximizing theta waves. Searching for time division sections. Usable. Suggesting materialization. Approved. Creating exobrain. Searching for conceptual term. External.

A pressure was felt on the leather of his right gauntlet. Looking at his palm, he saw a convex glass hemisphere embedded there. Light emanated from it, forming a glassy substance in the air.

A completed amethyst orb quickly circled around Shirone. It was an external brain, specialized for his time division ability.

"Wow..."

Everyone watching Shirone was wide-eyed.

With the hood on, Shirone looked like a perfect professional mage. The organic material forming the robe was desert-colored, not flashy, but it made him look stronger.

Unaware of his changed appearance, Shirone was amazed by the internal changes. His physical abilities had increased, but his mind felt refreshingly clear. It was at least twice as invigorating as his best condition.

Most of all, he loved the external brain. It was a duplicate of his own brain, so he still had only one slot, but his time division speed could increase dramatically. It was almost like having a double Spirit Zone when using passive magic.

"This is incredible..."

Uorin nodded with a satisfied smile.

"Armand enhances the user's strengths and compensates for weaknesses. Now deactivate Diamond Armor and try Direct Control."

When Shirone thought of deactivating, the Diamond Armor disassembled.

Armand, now a sleek sword, flew through the air at Shirone's command. Having achieved mental integration through the Diamond Armor, the shared consciousness felt natural.

Armand flew low and entered its sheath on the ground. Then it flew to Shirone's side, attaching itself like a belt. This was Armand's way of ensuring it didn't burden the mage’s body with any weight.

"Thank you. But no matter how I think about it, this is too much. It's an S-class object."

"Oh, brother, you really are something. But that's why I like you."

Uorin tested him slightly, but Shirone didn't seem to catch any special meaning.

Maybe it's too soon? But someday... She thought, as she held Shirone's arm to ease his burden.

"It's okay. Objects like this need to meet the right owner to be truly effective. Please take good care of it. See you next time."

With that, Uorin turned and walked away. She knew Shirone's personality well enough to understand that if she didn't leave decisively, he wouldn't be able to depart for hours.

Shirone felt grateful for Uorin's consideration and got into the carriage.

As the horses neighed and the carriage started moving, Shirone's group cheered.

Going home felt like a dream.

For a while, the carriage was lively with chatter. They gossiped about the Kazra nobles and speculated about .

After an hour, the carriage, furnished like a home, fell into a peaceful silence.
