Chapter 1227:

"Xing."

Great Star called out, but Xing gave no response.

"This is your last chance. Soon…"

"I know."

Just as Great Star forced the words out, the doors split cleanly in half and fell away.

Baal, Commander of the 1st Legion of Hell’s Army, stepped through.

"What a beautiful sight."

His brazen entrance made Umji and Yangji furrow their brows.

‘How?’

Even Laskajou of the Human Safety Enforcement Bureau had to risk his life just to bypass the self-detonation circuit on that very door.

Yangji gritted her teeth.

"He cut through Law. He’s gotten too strong."

Even Umji had to admit it—on a physical level, Baal might now be able to surpass even Satan.

Baal approached.

"My blade can now slice through the world itself. All thanks to humans. A bit disappointing, really, to see it end like this."

He could also sense that the Laws of Great Star and Xing were perfectly entangled—neither could move.

"Great Star. Star of Intelligence."

Even as Baal drew closer, Great Star couldn't move a single finger.

Xing bit her lip.

‘Please…’

In a gamble for the final moment of humanity, there could be no external intervention—

‘Please, someone… anyone, convince me.’

In that moment, the woman with the world’s strongest resolve… hoped for the first time in her life to be persuaded.

But no such person existed. And in the end, she couldn’t break free from her restraint.

Baal raised his sword.

"Evil has triumphed."

At that instant, the floor bulged and a soft, slime-like figure shot upward.

Great Star’s eyes sharpened.

‘Mimeon!’

A 1-Star satellite of the Human Safety Enforcement Bureau, gifted with the ability to liquefy her body into slime.

Unlike Shirone, who had battled through demon lines, she had slowly ascended the tower in a straight path.

‘I’ll sever his neck.’

Her leg morphed into a blade and swung toward Baal—but struck nothing but air.

"What…?"

Before she could grasp the situation, a diagonal flash sliced across her body.

"No!"

Great Star shouted as Mimeon’s body, split in two, writhed in separate spaces.

"Khhh…!"

Though she tried to hold her form by liquefying her body, Baal’s strike was on an entirely different level from anything she’d ever experienced.

‘I can’t regenerate… he cut through my ability. If this keeps up, I’ll regress into a single-cell organism…’

She could only maintain her brain cells for a few more minutes.

"Don’t worry," Baal said, raising his sword as he approached.

"I’m not into torture."

As Mimeon’s head was cleanly severed, Great Star bit her lip and shed silent tears.

"How pitiful… is this humanity’s final gasp?"

Baal shook his sword to flick off the blood, then turned and walked toward Great Star—

But Xing’s expression suddenly froze.

"Huh?"

Mimeon’s liquified remains began to rise again. Baal noticed it too and twisted his body in alarm.

"Still holding on? Damn…!"

The slime whipped forward at incredible speed and slammed Baal into the opposite wall.

"Urgh…!"

Even the strongest demon struggled to withstand the blow.

"You…"

Clutching his throbbing chest, Baal glared at the writhing slime.

"You’re not human."

The slime slowly began to take shape— but it wasn’t Mimeon.

It became the form of a man.

Great Star’s eyes widened in shock.

"Argones…"

The Father of All Living Creatures, and one of the Administrators.

Elven Forest.

The Green Ocean was completely engulfed in darkness due to the Black Dragon Abyris's breath.

‘So it endures, after all?’

The White Dragon Asriker sensed Uriel approaching through the abyss.

“…Wouldn’t it be better to rest? Fighting within the Shadow of the Abyss is meaningless.”

But Uriel did not stop walking.

He had taken hits from the combined breaths of the Fire Dragon, Black Dragon, and White Dragon, and was in poor condition—

“I will destroy.”

—but his fury was just as great.

“At the end of this war…”

Uriel suddenly stopped in his tracks.

“Do you truly believe there is victory in this?”

He didn’t even know what action he was preparing, but it was likely something utterly destructive.

“God has rejected the angels. Even if Ikael forced it through, there’s no reason to follow now.”

“This is my fight.”

“Heh…”

Asriker’s gaze grew wistful.

“A fight, huh… Now that I think about it, we’ve fought for quite a long time. I think I understand you a little now.”

“Understand what?”

“Let’s see… maybe that you're the most human of us?”

“There were eight archangels born from the original concepts. But you're the only one who doesn’t follow your core concept.”

“Me?”

“The leader of the White Order may be Ikael, but that’s only in conceptual hierarchy. You're the only archangel capable of obliterating an angelic commander.”

Uriel didn’t deny it.

“The thoughts of someone who can destroy anything must be simple… yet you carry so much conflict. Maybe it’s because there’s something you can’t destroy.”

“You mean Ikael?”

“No.”

Asriker’s eyes deepened.

“Longing.”

From the fairy laboratory where Crown resided, a terrible scream erupted.

“Kyaaaa!”

Then silence.

“Hm…”

An elven woman, captured earlier in the day, now stood before a mirror with a dry, doll-like expression.

Even the reflective fairy who had crafted the mirror trembled in disbelief.

“This…”

Her voice cracked, and she cleared her throat.

“Is this me?”

She muttered in a stern tone that didn’t suit her beautiful appearance and awkwardly ran her hands over her body.

The elven man who had been captured alongside her asked,

“You… who are you?”

She slowly turned and replied,

“Crown.”

They had suspected it, but hearing it from her own lips brought tears to his eyes.

‘I’m sorry I couldn’t protect you, Erin.’

On the surface, it had been a simple experiment.

Just drill a small hole in the elf’s skull and transplant Crown’s brain.

An experiment impossible even with human technology had succeeded solely thanks to Crown’s power.

“Not bad.”

Looking down at her hands, Erin—no, Crown—spoke with satisfaction.

“Becoming an elf, I mean.”

“You monster! Do you even understand what you’ve done?! You’ve defied the laws of nature—urk!”

As the fairy nearby activated a restraint spell, Crown—still in Erin’s body—raised a hand.

“It’s fine. Let him go.”

“But Crown, this one—”

“I want to test something.”

Even though the body had changed, Crown was still the king, and the fairies obeyed.

The freed elf stood and declared,

“Give Erin back.”

“If that’s your wish, I’ll consider it. But only if you defeat me.”

“What…?”

“Don’t understand? There's only one reason to break through the species barrier: to become a superior being. If I’m weaker than I was as a fairy, then this elf’s intellect and body are useless.”

“Fine. I’ll beat you at anything.”

Saving Erin was important, but in this wartime situation, he couldn’t bear the insult to his race.

“I’ll match you. Choose your weapon.”

“A bow.”

When Crown gave the order, a fairy brought a bow.

“It’s simple. Hit me anywhere—just once—and you win. You can’t aim for the head anyway.”

A shot to the head would kill Erin as well.

“Fine.”

The distance was only 4 meters.

At that range, even a mosquito would be an easy target for the man—let alone an elf.

‘The shoulder…’

He focused on Crown’s right shoulder.

‘No, not the shoulder.’

He nocked three arrows at once, drew his bow, and aimed for her forehead.

‘I’m sorry.’

This was the only chance to kill Crown.

‘Wind Art: Aruore.’ The arrows imbued with the spirit of wind flew at Mach speed, and the sonic boom followed.

“Crown!!”

By the time the fairies shouted in alarm, it was already over—but the result was not what they expected.

“…What?”

Erin stood by the tent entrance, having ripped off the elf’s head from the neck with her bare hands.

“If it’s this easy, the experiment’s not even worth doing.”

“Gghrk… gghrk…”

The elven man’s mouth opened and closed silently.

More terrifying than death itself was the fear of the extinction of the elf race.

‘This thing is neither fairy nor elf.’

It was a monster.

‘Wind Magic: Eios… even though it’s basic magic, it exceeded the elf’s limits.’

Even more than the elf fairy Enox—no, even the angels wouldn’t be able to subdue Crown now.

The greatest issue: the body he now inhabited was that of Erin, whom the elves loved and cherished.

‘This… isn’t Erin. We can’t be fooled.’

If the elves accepted Crown into their sanctuary without knowing the truth, it would lead to annihilation.

“E… Enox…”

Unable to even scream at the end, the man shed tears of blood and died.

“Tch.”

Tossing aside the head, Erin twisted her expression.

“Too quick. I meant to dodge at the last second, but I got bored and just killed him.”

The fairies didn’t even dare to breathe.

“In any case, the implant was successful. Compared to the body of a beast, this is unmatched. Far superior even to a human’s.”

It was the highest-grade body obtainable on this planet.

“C-congratulations… on your success.”

“Heh.”

A portion of the pineal gland between the elf’s left and right brain was removed, and Crown’s brain was implanted.

Normally, the subject’s immune system would react with shock—but…

“I can handle it.”

Born with the essence of Perfection, Crown instantly took control of the target system.

He called this state “boarding.”

“…Strange.”

Erin’s gaze deepened.

“Is this… life?”

No one had ever observed life from outside life before.

What had he seen?

The curious fairies listened closely, but he was lost in thought.

“Well, I like it. I’ll ride in this body for now. At least until a stronger one appears.”

“Um, Crown… what should we do with the body?”

Crown’s old body, its skull open, lay still on a tray atop a table.

The fairies couldn’t bring themselves to approach it.

Crown looked at his former body—one that had been his just ten minutes ago—with cold detachment.

‘What defines me?’

The body? The mind? Thoughts? Memories?

‘Those are thoughts only I can have because I am still myself. If I were no longer me, even those things…’

…would be meaningless.

‘What defines me…’

…is only the unique concept that I am myself—the Idea descending from that transcendent place.

‘The missing link…’

The moment I board myself, the Idea disappears—but Crown saw it clearly.

‘And so I…’

The Awakened One (Illuminati).

Crown grabbed the corpse on the table, stuffed its upper half into his mouth, and bit down.

“Hiik!”

Crunch. Crunch.

As the sound of bones being crushed filled the air, the fairies trembled, and he murmured:

“I am not this.”

Now it was simply a source of nourishment.

“Wait for my orders. We are going to the elven sanctuary.”

“What will you do there?”

And if the reason for feeding was reproduction—

“Domination.”

Crown spread his arms wide.

“I will become king. My descendants shall flourish upon this land and rule the humans from behind the scenes.”

It was the moment the ruler of the world began to change.
