Chapter 812: Age of Chaos (4)

"Now it begins!"

Benoff, who had been testing the firmness of Rian's guard, launched a full-scale attack.

Unlike jabs, his punches felt like hammers crashing down, causing Rian's upper body to sink into the ring.

'Skima.'

It was quite advanced.

'Can't he take it easy?'

Even as he thought this, Benoff's punches continued to rain down on his guard like a storm.

'It's like an iron wall.'

Even if he had set up his toughest subordinates, their bones would have broken under such an assault, and he would have had to write a report.

"Doesn't matter!"

Benoff's leg came up to head height.

"I'll just break it!"

As the impact hit from the side, Rian's body staggered along the line.

'The guard is open.'

Sensing the opening, Benoff charged in, but Rian's real fight was just beginning.

'It's starting to heat up.' The numerous practical techniques he had mastered fighting demons were hidden deep within him.

'Let's see what you've got.'

As their punches exchanged, the sound echoed through the security headquarters like hitting a leather water bag.

"Wow, he's holding his own against the captain." The fierce exchange of punches, with no hesitation, left everyone in awe.

"His technique is excellent. He's not allowing any clean hits."

Heavy punches brushed past his shoulders, and every time he lowered his stance, the attacks sliced through the air.

"Uho! Uho! Uho!"

Benoff pushed in, repeatedly jabbing Rian's side with left hooks.

"Ugh!"

As the dull pain surged, a right straight came towards his face.

Instinctively twisting his body, Rian grabbed Benoff's arm and attempted a throw.

Benoff flipped over and landed with a thud, sprawled out.

"You bastard...!"

As he raised his upper body, Rian moved behind him and locked in a triangle choke.

"Alright, let's do this..."

Benoff bounced his upper body, escaping the choke and tackling Rian.

"This is it!"

For the next two minutes, numerous ground techniques were exchanged as the two giants rolled on the floor.

'Damn! How strong is he?'

Despite daily endurance training, Benoff's muscles began to cramp just two minutes into the fight with Rian.

"Give up. Fighting in a fatigued state is dangerous."

"Hahaha! You rookie!"

Benoff's muscles bulged.

"Did you think this was a game?"

Using all his strength, he broke free from the hold, and Rian wrapped his arms around Benoff's waist.

'Idiot! Your face is exposed!'

As Benoff twisted his waist to throw an elbow, his legs suddenly lifted off the ground.

"Huh?"

As Rian's upper body arched backward, the guards closed their eyes tightly.

'German suplex! It's over!'

Though the impact should have been enough to break his neck, there was no sound of a crash.

"What, what is this?"

The guards opened their eyes in shock.

"Ugh... you, you..."

Benoff's head was hovering about a hand's width above the ring floor.

"Is that even a possible position for a human?"

Stopping mid-German suplex was beyond mere physical strength.

"...How is this possible?"

With his hips pointing to the sky, Benoff asked.

"It's the conviction of a swordsman."

It was the power of will to hold that position.

"Conviction?"

Lost in thought, Benoff's face briefly twisted before he broke into a bright smile.

"I lost. Let's end this."

Benoff rolled backward and stood up, while Rian straightened his upper body from the bent position.

Watching the incredible process, Benoff spoke.

"How do you feel? Doesn't it feel good to sweat a bit?"

"Yes, thanks to you."

It had been a long time since he relieved stress.

"Since I sweated, I need to wash up. I heard you just washed, but want to join me?"

"Sure. You can never have too many baths."

It was a lesson he had learned here.

The next morning.

Comparing the security guard's bed to the underground pits of the desert would be rude, but Rian's eyes opened early at dawn.

As he stepped outside, guards he hadn't seen before were sitting around, having finished their early morning shift.

"You're up early. Did you rest well?"

Security Chief Benoff had no concept of day or night.

"Yes. I need to leave early."

"You said you were going back home. Is there anything else you need to do?"

"No, not really..."

He was just curious about his family's well-being.

"I see. I'm sorry, but I need to ask you for a favor. Since we're connected by fate, I was hoping you could help me."

"The serial murder case, right?"

"Since you've heard, this will be quick. Follow me, and we'll talk on the way."

As they headed to the morgue, Benoff recounted the same story the guard had told him yesterday.

"Ordinary people are not allowed in, but I trust you, so I'll show you. Here it is."

As the coroner stepped aside, Benoff pulled out a handle that looked like a cabinet.

"Hmm."

Rian's face twisted.

"Do you have a sense of it?"

The corpse, completely shriveled from blood loss, looked like a mummy, with its mouth wide open.

It was hard to tell if it was from fear or ecstasy.

"Vampire."

Benoff nodded.

"It's a strong possibility. In fact, vampire attacks have been reported in Jive before. But this time, it's unusual. They don't usually drain this much blood."

Rian also found it abnormal.

"Even if vampires feed on blood, there's a limit to their stomach capacity, right? That's why it's ambiguous. It could be a human pretending to be a vampire."

"If it's a human, they're definitely a pervert."

"Exactly. The royal palace is starting to realize the seriousness. But I want to solve this with my own hands. Not for glory, but because such a bastard needs to be destroyed."

"It could be a vampire. A glutton who needs a lot of blood."

He wasn't denying it.

"I'm considering both possibilities. If it's really a vampire, things will escalate. If it's a pureblood vampire, the security team won't be enough. We'll need the elite 'Shinjang' unit."

Rian understood why Benoff was asking for his help.

'A vampire...'

He had nothing else to do but go home, and vampires were also demons.

"Understood. I'll help if I can."

Benoff patted Rian's shoulder.

"Haha! I knew you would. I'll set up a temporary position for you in the department. Wait here. You won't be sitting at a desk anyway."

Rian unpacked again and waited for a few hours in the spot Benoff had designated.

After lunch, he was called.

"Get in the carriage. We need to go somewhere urgently."

Benoff's face looked worse than in the morning, but Rian got into the carriage without complaint.

They arrived at a mansion so grand that it was obvious the owner was wealthy.

It wasn't as large as the Ozente estate, but it was too big to be in the middle of Jive's capital.

"Where is this?"

"This is the Bark family, the top rank in Jive. When I reported the Knight of Maha, they contacted me."

"Is it related to the vampire?"

He hated being dragged into political matters.

"It could be, or it might not be..."

The butler opened the door.

"Welcome. The master has been informed. The young lady is waiting."

Inside the mansion, a teenage girl was standing with her arms crossed, surrounded by knights.

"Oh, come on! I need to go shopping today! Why can't I go out?"

It was Irene Bark, the only daughter of the family head.

"Miss, you know about the recent gruesome incidents. You should refrain from going out..."

"But how can I not go out even during the day? Dad is too much!"

"Today is special because of certain circumstances."

The butler, who had turned to Rian and Benoff, bowed to Irene again and introduced them.

"This is the Knight of Maha, Rian Ogent, who will be guarding you from today. He is from the best family in swordsmanship..."

"Enough! My current knights are sufficient! How much more do you want to suffocate me?"

Rian hadn't expected this situation either.

"You want me to guard a civilian?"

Benoff scratched his head awkwardly.

"Strictly speaking, she's not a civilian. She's the daughter of the royal palace minister. Her mother is an advisor in the economic department."

Even so, it didn't make sense to him.

"An anonymous warning letter arrived the other day. It warned about a vampire. We don't know if it's a prank, but we can't ignore it."

That was why he had been hesitant to speak.

"Please understand my situation. I'll form a task force to catch the culprit. Just stay with her for now."

Hearing the conversation, Irene spoke with a displeased expression.

"So, what, I'm being rejected now? This is ridiculous. Hey, do you know who I am?"

"The minister's daughter."

Irene snorted.

"At least you know. But I don't let strange men in. And I hate big guys. You're already disqualified from the audition!"

"Audition?"

The knights behind Irene were all handsome and well-built.

"Hey, kid. First, I'm not a citizen of Jive. Second, having a good body doesn't mean you're strong. Lastly, I'm not an old man; I'm your brother."

"Hahaha! He calls himself brother. How old are you?"

Rian confidently replied.

"Twenty-two."

"Ew, you're so old." He didn't want to be here for other reasons.

"Hey, Knight of Maha. If you don't want to do it, just leave. The young lady has enough of us."

A knight in a sharp suit waved his hand dismissively.

Being the minister's daughter, getting noticed here would set him up for life.

'More importantly...'

If he caught her eye, it would be a jackpot.

"No, it's fine."

Irene took a step forward.

"I'll hire you. We're short on manpower. Protect our Tonas."

"Who's Tonas?"

Irene shouted towards the outside of the mansion.

"Tonas! Come here!"

A black-haired Doberman ran over with its tongue hanging out and jumped into Irene's arms.

"This is ridiculous."

As Benoff rubbed his forehead, Irene pointed confidently at Rian.

"Protect him!"

Rian was firm.

"No. Apologize for your rudeness and accept my protection, or I'll just leave."

"Hmph! Who do you think you are? You're here because my dad ordered you, right?"

"You don't want to die either."

Irene flinched at Rian's cold gaze.

"Your father called me because there aren't enough people to deal with the vampire. You know that, so you're just gathering these pretty boys to hold out." Most of the vampire's victims were beautiful.

"W-What..."

While Irene rolled her eyes, the knights behind her gathered around.

"Hey, musclehead. You've been pissing me off since earlier. Do you think being handsome adds anything?"

"Step aside, small fry."

"...Miss, can we take the audition instead?"

Irene quickly regained her composure and shouted.

"Fine! Show him what you've got!"

As the knights rubbed their fists and took a step forward, Rian's eyes widened.

Divine Transcendence - Mind Strike.

The mind strikes first.

-Mind Strike! Mind Strike! Mind Strike! Mind Strike! Mind Strike! Mind Strike! Mind Strike! Mind Strike! Mind Strike! Mind Strike! Mind Strike! Mind Strike!

There was no need to take action.

"What are you doing? Quickly, get that arrogant man..."

As Irene frowned and looked back, she was stunned and left her mouth agape.

"Huh..."

The knights, feeling as if they had been struck by dozens of punches, were all kneeling and sweating profusely.
